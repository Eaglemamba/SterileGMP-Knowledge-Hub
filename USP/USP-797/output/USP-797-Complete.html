1. Introduction and Scope | 簡介與範圍

原文內容 Original Text

This chapter describes the minimum standards to be followed for the preparation of compounded sterile preparations (CSPs) for human and animal drugs. Sterile compounding is defined as combining, admixing, diluting, pooling, reconstituting, repackaging, or otherwise altering a drug product or bulk drug substance to create a sterile preparation.

The requirements in this chapter must be followed to minimize harm, including death, to human and animal patients that could result from 1) microbial contamination [nonsterility], 2) excessive bacterial endotoxins, 3) variability from the intended strength of correct ingredients, 4) physical and chemical incompatibilities, 5) chemical and physical contaminants, and/or 6) use of ingredients of inappropriate quality.

Aseptic techniques, processes, and procedures must be followed for preparing any sterile medication. Processes and procedures must be in place to minimize the potential for contact with nonsterile surfaces, introduction of particulate matter or biological fluids, and mix-ups with other products or CSPs.

The use of technologies, techniques, materials, and procedures other than those described in this chapter is not prohibited as long as they are noninferior to those described herein and validated for the intended purpose (e.g., Validation of Alternative Microbiological Methods ⟨1223⟩ and Validation of Compendial Procedures ⟨1225⟩).

1.1 Scope

1.1.1 CSPs affected: The requirements in this chapter must be met to ensure the sterility of any CSP. Although the list below is not exhaustive, the following must be sterile:

  • Injections, including infusions
  • Irrigations for internal body cavities (i.e., any space that does not normally communicate with the environment outside of the body, such as the bladder cavity or peritoneal cavity). [Note—Irrigations for the mouth, rectal cavity, and sinus cavity are not required to be sterile.]
  • Ophthalmic dosage forms
  • Aqueous preparations for pulmonary inhalation. [Note—Nasal dosage forms intended for local application are not required to be sterile.]
  • Baths and soaks for live organs and tissues
  • Implants

1.1.2 Specific practices — Allergenic extracts: Licensed allergenic extracts are mixed and diluted to prepare prescription sets for administration to patients. A prescription set is a vial or set of vials of premixed licensed allergenic extracts for subcutaneous immunotherapy that have been diluted with an appropriate diluent for an individual patient. Because of certain characteristics of allergenic extracts and allergy practice, preparation of allergenic extract prescription sets is not subject to all of the requirements in this chapter.

Blood-derived and other biological materials: When compounding activities require the manipulation of a patient's blood-derived or other biological material (e.g., autologous serum), the manipulations must be clearly separated from other compounding activities and equipment used in CSP preparation activities, and they must be controlled by specific standard operating procedures (SOPs) to avoid any cross-contamination.

Hazardous drugs: Handling of sterile hazardous drugs (HDs) must additionally comply with Hazardous Drugs—Handling in Healthcare Settings ⟨800⟩.

Repackaging: Repackaging of a sterile product or preparation from its original container into another container must be performed in accordance with the requirements in this chapter.

Sterile radiopharmaceuticals: Compounding of radiopharmaceuticals is not required to meet the standards of this chapter as they are subject to the requirements in Radiopharmaceuticals—Preparation, Compounding, Dispensing, and Repackaging ⟨825⟩.

1.1.3 Personnel and settings affected: This chapter describes the minimum requirements that apply to all persons who prepare CSPs and all places where CSPs are prepared. This includes but is not limited to pharmacists, technicians, nurses, physicians, veterinarians, dentists, naturopaths, and chiropractors in all places including but not limited to hospitals and other healthcare institutions, medical and surgical patient treatment sites, infusion facilities, pharmacies, and physician or veterinarian practice sites.

The compounding facility must designate one or more individuals (i.e., the designated person(s)) to be responsible and accountable for the performance and operation of the facility and personnel in the preparation of CSPs and for performing other functions as described in this chapter.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為什麼需要 USP ⟨797⟩？

Compounded Sterile Preparation, CSP (調配無菌製劑)：與工廠大量生產的藥品不同，CSP 是藥房或醫療機構根據病患個別需求現場調配的無菌藥劑。因為沒有經過完整的商業製造驗證流程，其污染風險本質上更高。

本章列出六大患者危害來源：(1) 微生物污染、(2) 過量細菌內毒素、(3) 含量偏差、(4) 物化不相容、(5) 化學/物理污染物、(6) 劣質原料。這六點直接對應到 FDA 歷年召回事件中的主要原因。

比喻說明：調配藥局 vs 製藥工廠

想像一下：工廠製造的瓶裝礦泉水（conventionally manufactured product）有完整的滅菌驗證和品管體系；而 CSP 比較像是廚師在廚房現場製作的特調飲料——同樣需要衛生規範，但環境和驗證深度不同。

USP ⟨797⟩ 就是設定這個「廚房」的最低衛生標準，讓調配出的製劑足夠安全。

重點提示：Designated Person 的法律責任

Designated Person（指定人員）不只是一個頭銜，而是具有法律問責性的職位。在 FDA 的 483 觀察和警告信中，designated person 的缺失或失職是常見缺失項目。

對 CDMO 而言：必須在 SOP 中明確定義 designated person 的職責範圍，並有書面指派記錄。

實務應用：哪些製劑必須無菌？

快速判斷規則——凡是進入「正常情況下不與外界溝通的體腔」或「直接進入血液循環」的，都必須無菌：

  • 注射劑、輸液 → 無菌
  • 眼科製劑 → 無菌
  • 肺部吸入水溶液 → 無菌
  • 膀胱沖洗液 → 無菌（但口腔沖洗液不需要）
  • 植入物 → 無菌

1.2 Administration | 給藥 & 1.3 Immediate-Use CSPs | 即時使用CSP

原文內容 Original Text

1.2 Administration

For the purposes of this chapter, "administration" means the direct application of a sterile product or preparation to a single patient by injecting, infusing, or otherwise providing a sterile product or preparation in its final form.

Administration of medication is out of the scope of this chapter. Standard precautions such as the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) safe injection practices apply to administration. See ⟨800⟩ for additional recommendations for the administration of hazardous drugs.

1.3 Immediate-Use CSPs

When all of the following conditions are met, compounding of CSPs for direct and immediate administration is not subject to the requirements for Category 1, Category 2, or Category 3 CSPs:

  1. Aseptic techniques, processes, and procedures are followed, and written SOPs are in place to minimize the potential for contact with nonsterile surfaces, introduction of particulate matter or biological fluids, and mix-ups with other conventionally manufactured products or CSPs.
  2. Personnel are trained and demonstrate competency in aseptic processes as they relate to assigned tasks and the facility's SOPs.
  3. The preparation is performed in accordance with evidence-based information for physical and chemical compatibility of the drugs (e.g., approved labeling, stability and compatibility studies).
  4. The preparation involves not more than 3 different sterile products.
  5. Any unused starting component from a single-dose container must be discarded after preparation is complete. Single-dose containers must not be used for more than one patient.
  6. Administration begins within 4 h following the start of preparation. If administration has not begun within 4 h following the start of preparation, it must be promptly, appropriately, and safely discarded.
  7. Unless directly administered by the person who prepared it or administration is witnessed by the preparer, the CSP must be labeled with the names and amounts of all active ingredients, the name or initials of the person who prepared the preparation, and the 4-h time period within which administration must begin.

Handling of sterile hazardous drugs (HDs) must additionally comply with ⟨800⟩.

1.4 Preparation Per Approved Labeling

Compounding does not include mixing, reconstituting, or other such acts that are performed in accordance with directions contained in approved labeling or supplemental materials provided by the product's manufacturer.

Preparing a conventionally manufactured sterile product in accordance with the directions in the manufacturer's approved labeling is out of scope of this chapter only if:

  1. The product is prepared as a single dose for an individual patient; and
  2. The approved labeling includes information for the diluent, the resultant strength, the container closure system, and storage time.

Proprietary bag and vial systems: Docking and activation of proprietary bag and vial systems in accordance with the manufacturer's labeling for immediate administration to an individual patient is not considered compounding and may be performed outside of an ISO Class 5 environment.

Docking of the proprietary bag and vial systems for future activation and administration is considered compounding and must be performed in an ISO Class 5 environment in accordance with this chapter, with the exception of 14. Establishing Beyond-Use Dates. Beyond-use dates (BUDs) for proprietary bag and vial systems must not be longer than those specified in the manufacturer's labeling.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Immediate-Use CSP 的豁免邏輯

Immediate-Use CSP (即時使用CSP)：當臨床急需而無法符合正規 Category 1/2/3 要求時，可在滿足7個嚴格條件下調配的製劑。

豁免的核心原因是時間窗口極短（4小時內給藥），污染後增殖的風險遠低於有 BUD 的製劑。但請注意：這不是「隨便做」的許可證，仍需書面 SOP 和人員訓練。

關鍵數字：Immediate-Use 的硬性限制

最多混合成分數量：≤ 3 種不同無菌產品
給藥開始時限：   製備後 ≤ 4 小時
單次劑量容器：   不得供多位病患使用
未使用殘餘物：   製備完成後立即丟棄

重點提示：常見誤解 — 「按仿單稀釋」算不算調配？

1.4 節明確排除了按核准仿單操作的重配行為，但有兩個先決條件：(1) 為單一病患配製；(2) 仿單已涵蓋稀釋劑、濃度、容器和儲存時間。

若仿單缺少上述任一項資訊，該操作就落入 ⟨797⟩ 範疇。實務上這常是稽查缺失的灰色地帶。

比喻：袋瓶系統的兩種情境

想像一個咖啡膠囊機：直接按鈕沖泡給客人喝（立即使用）= 不需特殊清潔室。但若事先把膠囊插入機器，留待稍後使用（future activation）= 就等同於提前調配，需要符合無菌環境要求。

Proprietary bag and vial system 的邏輯完全相同。

1.5 CSP Categories | CSP 分類系統

原文內容 Original Text

This chapter distinguishes three categories of CSPs: Category 1, Category 2, and Category 3, primarily based on the state of environmental control under which they are compounded, the probability for microbial growth during the time they will be stored, and the time period within which they must be used.

Category 1 CSPs are compounded under the least controlled environmental conditions and therefore are assigned a BUD of 12 h or less at controlled room temperature or 24 h or less when refrigerated, if compounded in accordance with all of the applicable requirements for Category 1 CSPs in this chapter.

Category 2 CSPs require more environmental controls and testing than Category 1 CSPs and may be assigned a BUD of greater than 12 h at controlled room temperature or more than 24 h if refrigerated, but not exceed the limits established in Table 13 (see 14. Establishing Beyond-Use Dates), if compounded in accordance with all of the applicable requirements for Category 2 CSPs in this chapter.

Category 3 CSPs undergo sterility testing, supplemented by endotoxin testing when applicable, and have more requirements than Category 2 CSPs for personnel qualification, use of sterile garb, use of sporicidal disinfectants, frequency of environmental monitoring, and stability determination. Category 3 CSPs may be assigned longer BUDs than those set for Category 2 CSPs but not exceeding the limits in Table 14 (see 14. Establishing Beyond-Use Dates), if compounded in accordance with all applicable requirements for Category 3 CSPs in this chapter.

The requirements that are not specifically described as applicable to Category 1, Category 2, or Category 3, are applicable to the compounding of all CSPs unless the CSP is otherwise described in 1.1 Scope.

Category 1, Category 2, and Category 3 CSPs can be compounded by using only sterile starting ingredients, or by using some or all nonsterile starting ingredients. If all components used to compound are sterile from the start, the sterility of the components must be maintained during compounding to produce a CSP.

If one or more of the starting components being used to compound is not sterile, the sterility of the compounded preparation must be achieved through a sterilization process (e.g., terminal sterilization in the final sealed container) or sterilizing filtration, and then sterility must be maintained if the CSP is subsequently manipulated. When compounding with nonsterile starting components, supplies, or equipment, the quality of the components, the effectiveness of the sterilization step, and bacterial endotoxin mitigation strategies are critical to achieving a sterile preparation that is free from excessive bacterial endotoxins.

核心概念：三分類系統的設計邏輯

分類決定因素：環境控制程度 × 儲存時間風險 × 微生物增殖概率。三個類別呈金字塔形：Category 1 門檻最低但 BUD 最短；Category 3 要求最多但可獲得最長使用期限。

重點提示：非無菌起始原料的內毒素風險

使用非無菌起始原料時，即使通過過濾滅菌，細菌內毒素（革蘭氏陰性菌細胞壁碎片）不會被 0.22 µm 濾膜去除。因此必須有額外的去熱原策略（如適當沖洗或選用通過 LAL 測試的原料）。

這是 Category 2/3 CSP 從非無菌原料調配時最常見的合規陷阱。

2. Personnel Training and Evaluation | 人員訓練與評估

原文內容 Original Text

All personnel who compound or have direct oversight of compounding personnel must be initially trained and qualified by demonstrating knowledge and competency in compounding CSPs according to the requirements in this section before being allowed to perform their job functions independently. Designated person(s) are responsible for creating and implementing a training program for personnel and for ensuring that compounders, personnel who have direct oversight of compounders, and personnel who perform restocking or cleaning and disinfection duties are initially trained and qualified by demonstrating knowledge and competency in maintaining the quality of the sterile compounding environment before being allowed to perform their job functions independently.

Personnel who compound or have direct oversight of compounding personnel must complete training initially and at least every 12 months in appropriate sterile compounding principles and practices. Personnel compounding only immediate-use CSPs must complete training as required by the facility's SOPs.

Each compounding facility must develop a written training program that describes the required training, the frequency of training, and the process for evaluating the performance of individuals who compound, have direct oversight of compounding personnel, perform in-process checks, final verification, and dispensing of CSPs.

2.1 Demonstrating Knowledge and Competency of Core Skills

Before beginning to compound CSPs independently, personnel must complete training and be able to demonstrate knowledge of principles and competency of skills in at least the following areas:

  • Hand hygiene
  • Garbing
  • Cleaning and disinfection
  • Calculations, measuring, and mixing
  • Aseptic technique
  • Achieving and/or maintaining sterility (and apyrogenicity if compounding with nonsterile components)
  • Use of equipment
  • Documentation of the compounding process (e.g., master formulation and compounding records)
  • Principles of high-efficiency particulate air (HEPA)-filtered unidirectional airflow within the ISO Class 5 area
  • Proper use of PECs
  • Principles of movement of materials and personnel within the compounding area

2.2 Demonstrating Competency in Garbing and Hand Hygiene

Before beginning to compound Category 1, Category 2, or Category 3 CSPs, personnel must successfully complete an initial garbing competency evaluation no fewer than 3 separate times. The 3 successful completions must be in succession—failure of any of the 3 initial garbing competency evaluations requires repeat testing until personnel successfully complete 3 evaluations in a row.

The garbing competency evaluation consists of a visual observation and gloved fingertip and thumb sampling (GFT) of both hands (see Box 1).

Gloved fingertip and thumb sampling after garbing, but before applying sterile 70% IPA to gloves, must be performed on donned sterile gloves on both hands in a classified area or segregated compounding area (SCA).

After the initial garbing competency evaluations, compounding personnel must successfully complete the garbing competency:

  • At least one time every 6 months for personnel compounding Category 1 and Category 2 CSPs
  • At least one time every 3 months for personnel compounding Category 3 CSPs
  • Every 12 months for personnel with direct oversight (non-compounding)
Box 1. Gloved Fingertip and Thumb Sampling Procedures
Use one sampling media device (e.g., plates, paddles, or slides) per hand, containing general microbial growth agar (e.g., trypticase soy agar [TSA]) supplemented with neutralizing additives (e.g., lecithin and polysorbate 80). Label each media device with a personnel identifier, right or left hand, and the date and time of sampling. Do not apply sterile 70% isopropyl alcohol (IPA) to gloves immediately before touching the media device because this could cause a false-negative result. Collect samples from all gloved fingertips and thumbs from both hands by rolling fingertip pads and thumb pad over the agar surface. Incubate at 30°–35° for ≥48 h then at 20°–25° for ≥5 additional days. Record the number of cfu per hand.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為何 GFT 用「兩隻手總和」而非單手？

Gloved Fingertip and Thumb Sampling, GFT (戴手套指尖採樣)：以 TSA 培養基接觸戴手套的雙手指尖，評估更衣過程是否維持手套無菌性的測試。

USP 將兩隻手的菌落計算合計，原因是即使只有一隻手污染，在操作時仍可能交叉感染另一隻手或污染 CSP。合計計算比單手更嚴格，更符合實際風險邏輯。

GFT 關鍵數字總覽

初始合格標準：連續 3 次通過（任一次失敗需重新計算）
更衣後：兩手合計 = 0 cfu（任何菌落即為失敗）
Media-fill 後：兩手合計 ≤ 3 cfu 為通過

持續訓練頻率：
  Category 1 & 2 人員 → 每 6 個月 1 次
  Category 3 人員     → 每 3 個月 1 次
  監督人員（不操作）  → 每 12 個月 1 次

重點提示：最嚴格規定 — 更衣後 GFT 為 >0 即失敗

更衣後的 GFT 動作水準是 >0，意即任何菌落計數都代表失敗。這比 media-fill 後的 >3 標準嚴格得多。

邏輯：更衣過程本身應完全無菌；media-fill 則是模擬實際操作，允許微量但極低的風險。

比喻：手術室的刷手標準

GFT 就像外科醫師術前刷手後的無菌確認。外科團隊不會在完成刷手後、進入手術室前，就讓手接觸非無菌物品。同樣地，藥師在完成更衣程序後，必須確保手套指尖「零污染」——這是進入 CSP 調配區前的最後一道防線。

實務應用：文件記錄要求

失敗記錄必須包含：(1) 受評人員姓名、(2) 評估日期和時間、(3) 培養基廠商/效期/批號、(4) 各培養週期起始溫度、(5) 培養日期、(6) 結果、(7) 觀察者及讀板人身份。

任何失敗必須有矯正行動記錄。此記錄不只是合規文件，也是人員能力的長期評估依據。

2.3 Competency Testing in Aseptic Manipulation | 無菌操作能力測試

原文內容 Original Text

Before beginning to compound Category 1, Category 2, or Category 3 CSPs independently, personnel must successfully complete an aseptic manipulation competency evaluation. The aseptic manipulation competency evaluation consists of a visual observation, media-fill testing, followed by a gloved fingertip and thumb sampling on both hands, and surface sampling of the direct compounding area.

For personnel compounding Category 1 and Category 2 CSPs, the aseptic manipulation competency must occur initially and at least every 6 months thereafter. For personnel compounding Category 3 CSPs, the aseptic manipulation competency must occur initially and at least every 3 months thereafter. Personnel who have direct oversight of compounding personnel must complete an aseptic manipulation competency evaluation annually.

When performing a media-fill test, simulate the most difficult and challenging aseptic compounding procedures encountered by the person replacing all the components used in the CSPs with soybean–casein digest media. The simulation must capture elements that could potentially affect the sterility of the CSP including but not limited to:

  • Factors associated with the length of the process that can pose contamination risk (e.g., operator fatigue, quality of equipment)
  • Number of aseptic additions or transfers
  • Number, type, and complexity of manipulations
  • Number of personnel in the buffer room or SCA

Failure is indicated by visible turbidity or other visual manifestations of growth in the media in one or more container closure unit(s) on or before the end of the incubation period.

核心概念：Media-Fill Test 的設計原理

Media-Fill Test (培養基充填測試)：以微生物培養基取代實際藥品，模擬最困難的調配程序。如果過程中有任何污染，培養基會呈現混濁，提供直接可見的證據。

關鍵設計原則：「必須模擬最困難的程序」——若藥師通常調配含多次針頭刺穿的複雜配方，media-fill 就必須模擬相同難度，而非僅用簡單的單次操作。

比喻：Media-Fill 如同飛行模擬器

飛行員在執行真實飛行前，必須在模擬機中通過最惡劣天氣的測試。Media-fill 就是調配人員的「飛行模擬器」——用培養基代替真實藥品，在相同的操作環境和壓力下測試無菌技術。

若模擬機測試失敗（培養基混濁），就不應讓此人進行真實操作（調配患者用藥）。

重點提示：三層測試的邏輯順序

完整的無菌操作能力評估包含三層：

  • ①視覺觀察：評估員目視確認操作行為是否正確
  • ②Media-fill：實際模擬操作，用培養基檢驗污染
  • ③GFT + 表面採樣：Media-fill 完成後立即採樣，確認殘餘手套污染狀況

任何一層失敗 = 整體評估失敗。三層同時成立才能獨立操作。

Media-Fill 培養條件

培養條件（必須兩種溫度）：
  30°–35°：最少 7 天（細菌優化條件）
  20°–25°：最少 7 天（黴菌/酵母菌優化條件）
  → 共最少 14 天培養週期

判定標準：
  任何容器在 14 天前出現混濁 = FAIL
  所有容器均清澈（plus 陽性對照生長） = PASS

實務應用：非無菌原料的 Media-Fill 設計

當調配從非無菌原料（如 API 粉末）開始的 Category 2/3 CSP 時，media-fill 必須用「非無菌大豆酪蛋白消化培養基粉末」配製 3% 溶液，模擬從粉末溶解到過濾滅菌的完整流程。此時必須包含陽性對照組以確認培養基仍有促生長能力。

3. Personal Hygiene and Garbing | 個人衛生與更衣

原文內容 Original Text

Personal hygiene and garbing are essential to maintain microbial control of the environment. Most microorganisms detected in cleanrooms are transferred from individuals. Squamous cells are normally shed from the human body at a rate of 106 or more per hour, and those skin particles are covered with microorganisms.

Individuals that may have a higher risk of contaminating the CSP and the environment (e.g., personnel with rashes, recent tattoos, oozing sores, conjunctivitis, or active respiratory infections) must report these conditions to the designated person(s).

3.1 Personnel Preparation

Before entering a compounding area, individuals must at a minimum:

  • Remove personal outer garments (e.g., bandanas, coats, hats, jackets, sweaters, vests)
  • Remove all cosmetics because they shed flakes and particles
  • Remove all hand, wrist, and other exposed jewelry, including piercings that could interfere with the effectiveness of garbing
  • Not wear earbuds or headphones
  • Not bring electronic devices that are not necessary for compounding or other required tasks into the compounding area
  • Keep nails clean and neatly trimmed to minimize particle shedding and avoid glove punctures. Nail products (e.g., polish, artificial nails, and extenders) must not be worn
  • Wipe eyeglasses, if worn

Food (including mints, gum, etc.) and drinks must not enter anterooms, buffer rooms, or segregated compounding areas.

3.2 Hand Hygiene

Box 3. Hand Washing Procedures
1. Clean underneath fingernails under warm running water using a disposable nail cleaner.
2. Wash hands and forearms up to the elbows with soap and water for at least 30 s.
3. Dry hands and forearms up to the elbows completely with low-lint disposable towels or wipers.

Brushes must not be used for hand hygiene. Hand dryers must not be used. Disposable soap containers must not be refilled or topped off.

Box 4. Hand Sanitizing Procedures
Apply an alcohol-based hand rub to dry skin. Apply product to one hand and rub hands together, covering all surfaces of hands and fingers, until hands are dry. Allow hands to dry thoroughly before donning sterile gloves.

3.3 Garbing Requirements

The minimum garbing requirements for preparing Category 1 and Category 2 CSPs include:

  • Low-lint garment with sleeves that fit snugly around the wrists and an enclosed neck (e.g., gown or coverall)
  • Low-lint covers for shoes
  • Low-lint cover for head that covers the hair and ears, and if applicable, cover for facial hair
  • Low-lint face mask
  • Sterile powder-free gloves

For Category 3 CSPs, the following additional garbing requirements must be met continuously in the buffer room:

  1. No exposed skin in the buffer room (face and neck must be covered)
  2. All low-lint outer garb must be sterile, including sterile sleeves over gauntlet sleeves when a RABS is used
  3. Disposable garbing items must not be reused; laundered garb must not be reused without being laundered and resterilized with a validated cycle
  4. SOPs must describe disinfection procedures for reusing goggles, respirators, and other reusable equipment

Gloves: Gloves must be sterile and powder free. Application of sterile 70% IPA to gloves must occur immediately before compounding and regularly throughout the compounding process. All gloves must be inspected for holes, punctures, or tears and must be replaced immediately if such defects are detected.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：人體是最大的微生物來源

每小時脫落超過 100 萬個鱗狀細胞——每個細胞都附著微生物。這就是為什麼更衣程序是無菌製造中最關鍵的污染控制環節之一。

Garbing (更衣)：覆蓋人體以減少皮膚脫屑和微生物散佈的防護程序。目的不是保護人，而是保護 CSP 不被人污染。

比喻：手術室的外科洗手服

手術室裡的更衣規定有相同邏輯：外科醫師換上刷手服、戴手術帽、戴口罩，是為了防止身上的微生物落入開放的手術傷口，而非為了個人保護。

無菌調配室的更衣程序本質相同——「穿對的衣服，是為了保護病人」。

重點提示：Category 3 的更衣是針對整個緩衝室，不只針對調配當天

Category 3 的額外更衣要求（無露出皮膚、全無菌外衣）適用於進入 Category 3 緩衝室的所有人員，無論當天是否調配 Category 3 CSP。

這意味著：若你的設施在同一緩衝室混合調配 Category 2 和 Category 3，所有進入此緩衝室的人都必須符合 Category 3 更衣標準——這顯著提高了運作成本。

Category 1 & 2:
  低纖維防護衣（袖口密合、覆頸）
  鞋套、頭套（含耳部）、口罩
  無菌無粉手套

  + 臉部和頸部必須覆蓋（無外露皮膚）

  + 洗滌衣物需再次滅菌驗證後方可使用

重點提示：指甲和化妝品的禁止規定

指甲油、人工指甲、指甲延長片 → 全部禁止。原因：(1) 可能含有微粒脫落，(2) 破壞手套的密合性，(3) 人工指甲下方容易藏菌。

化妝品（粉底、腮紅等）同樣禁止，因為會產生粉塵微粒。這是許多調配設施稽查時的常見缺失。

實務應用：更衣順序的 SOP 設計

更衣順序必須由設施自行決定並寫入 SOP，但需符合「從乾淨到污染」的方向原則。典型順序：手部清潔 → 鞋套 → 頭套 → 口罩 → 防護衣 → 手部消毒 → 戴手套。

水槽位置會影響洗手和更衣的順序——水槽在前室外部 vs 前室內部，SOP 的程序不同，需明確說明。

4.1.2 Design requirements: Facilities used for compounding CSPs must be designed so that air quality improves with movement through separate operational areas to the PEC. Classified areas include anterooms, buffer rooms, and PECs.

  • Anterooms providing access only to positive-pressure buffer rooms must meet at least ISO Class 8

  • Category 1, 2, and 3 CSPs must be compounded in ISO Class 5 or better PEC
  • If compounding only Category 1 CSPs, the PEC may be placed in an unclassified SCA

4.2 Facility Design and Environmental Controls

The cleanroom suite should be maintained at a temperature of 20° or cooler and a relative humidity of 60% or below. Temperature and humidity must be monitored in each room each day that compounding is performed and documented at least once daily. Free-standing air conditioners, humidifiers, and dehumidifiers must not be used within the classified area or the SCA.

4.2.1 Types of SECs and design — Cleanroom suite: The ISO-classified anteroom and buffer room must be separated from the surrounding unclassified areas by fixed walls and doors. The classified rooms must be equipped with a pressure-differential monitoring system. Air supplied to the cleanroom suite must be introduced through HEPA filters in the ceiling.

The anteroom must have a line of demarcation to separate the clean side from the dirty side. Airlocks and interlocking doors may be used to facilitate better control of air balance. If a pass-through chamber is used, both doors must never be opened at the same time. Tacky mats must not be placed within ISO-classified areas.

Segregated compounding area (SCA): A PEC may be located within an unclassified area without an anteroom or buffer room. Only Category 1 CSPs may be compounded in an SCA. The SCA must be located away from unsealed windows, doors that connect to the outdoors, and traffic flow. The area within 1 m of the PEC should be dedicated only for sterile compounding.

4.2.2 The CSP compounding environment: The PEC must be certified to meet ISO Class 5 or better conditions during dynamic operating conditions. Unidirectional airflow must be maintained in the PEC. HEPA-filtered air must be supplied at a velocity sufficient to sweep particles away from critical sites.

Laminar airflow system (LAFS): An LAFS provides an ISO Class 5 or better environment. The unidirectional HEPA-filtered airflow within the LAFS helps protect the direct compounding area (DCA) from process-generated contamination.

4.2.4 Air Exchange Requirements

Air changes per hour (ACPH) requirements for non-HD sterile compounding areas:

  • ISO Class 7 rooms: ≥30 total HEPA-filtered ACPH, with at least 15 ACPH from HVAC through ceiling HEPA filters. The HEPA-filtered air from the PEC, when added to HVAC-supplied air, must increase total to ≥30 ACPH.

4.2.5 Pressure Differentials

A minimum differential positive pressure of 0.020-inch water column is required between adjacent ISO-classified areas (e.g., between buffer room and anteroom). The pressure differential between the anteroom and the unclassified area must not be less than 0.020-inch water column. A pressure differential monitoring device must be used to continuously monitor and document pressure differentials at least daily on days when compounding occurs.

  • Category 3 的放置：Pharmaceutical Isolator 只需 ISO Class 8 室，其他 PEC 需要 ISO Class 7 + 8 套組

比喻：CAI vs Pharmaceutical Isolator — 手套箱 vs 隔離艙

CAI 像是高級手套箱（glove box）：有手套、有 HEPA，但沒有自動去污系統。

Pharmaceutical Isolator 更像太空站的艙室：有完整的傳遞口（RTP）、自動去污系統（如 VHP），是更高層次的隔離。注意：USP 明確規定 CAI ≠ Pharmaceutical Isolator。

ACPH 計算邏輯

ISO Class 7 緩衝室：
  總 ACPH ≥ 30
  其中 HVAC 供給 ≥ 15 ACPH
  PEC 貢獻 = 總量 − HVAC量

ISO Class 8 前室：
  總 ACPH ≥ 20
  其中 HVAC 供給 ≥ 15 ACPH

舉例：緩衝室 HVAC 提供 15 ACPH
→ PEC 必須至少再提供 15 ACPH
→ 若 PEC 關閉，等效 ACPH 降為 15 → 違規
→ 因此 PEC 不得在非維護時間關閉！

重點提示：IVLFZ 的高難度設計

USP 原文特別注記：動態煙霧測試已顯示 IVLFZ 很難在動態作業條件下維持單向氣流。這是一個隱含的警告，表示 USP 雖不禁止 IVLFZ，但知道其設計具有挑戰性。

實務建議：若資金允許，選擇傳統 LAFW 或 CAI 通常比 IVLFZ 更容易通過煙霧測試認證。

實務應用：Pharmaceutical Isolator 的 Category 2/3 優勢

對需要調配 Category 2/3 CSP 的設施，Pharmaceutical Isolator 有一個重要優勢：只需要 ISO Class 8 室（不需要前室）。這可以節省相當可觀的設施建設成本。

代價是 Pharmaceutical Isolator 本身購置和維護成本較高，需要做總擁有成本（TCO）分析。

5. Certification and Recertification | 認證與再認證

原文內容 Original Text

Before a compounding area is used to compound CSPs, it must be independently certified using the requirements in this chapter and manufacturer specifications. Certification indicates that the compounding area is meeting its design and air quality specifications.

Certification of classified areas including the PEC must be performed initially, and recertification must be performed at least every 6 months and must include:

  1. Airflow testing: Determines acceptability of air velocity, room air exchange rate, and room pressure differential. The ACPH from HVAC, ACPH contributed from the PEC, and the total ACPH must be documented on the certification report.
  2. HEPA filter integrity testing: HEPA filters must be leak tested at the factory and then leak tested again after installation and as part of recertification.
  3. Total particle count testing: Must be performed under dynamic operating conditions using calibrated electronic equipment.
  4. Dynamic airflow smoke pattern test: Must be performed for each PEC during dynamic operating conditions to demonstrate unidirectional airflow and sweeping action over and away from the preparation(s).

Classified areas must additionally be recertified if there are changes to the area such as redesign, construction, replacement or relocation of any PEC, or alteration in the configuration of the room that could affect airflow or air quality.

All certification and recertification records must be reviewed by the designated person(s). The number of personnel present in each PEC and SEC during total particle-count tests and dynamic airflow smoke-pattern tests must be documented. Records must be maintained in accordance with 20. Documentation.

A corrective action plan must be implemented and documented in response to any out-of-range results. Data collected in response to corrective actions must be reviewed to confirm effectiveness.

5.1 Total Airborne Particle Sampling

Total airborne particle count testing must be conducted in all classified areas during dynamic operating conditions at least every 6 months. Measurements of total airborne particles must be taken in each PEC at locations where there is greatest risk to the exposed CSPs, containers, and closures.

核心概念：認證的四個測試項目

每次認證/再認證必須包含「四合一」測試：

  1. 氣流測試 (Airflow Testing)：測量風速、ACPH、壓差
  2. HEPA 濾芯完整性測試：確認無洩漏點
  3. 總微粒計數 (Total Particle Count)：動態條件下測 ≥0.5µm 微粒數量
  4. 動態煙霧測試 (Dynamic Smoke Pattern Test)：視覺確認單向氣流覆蓋

每6個月一次，並在任何可能影響氣流的變更後立即重新認證。

比喻：像汽車定期年檢

認證就像汽車年檢——你的車每年必須通過煞車、排放、安全燈等多項測試。即使看起來沒問題，法規仍要求定期驗證。

同理，即使調配室「感覺正常」，每6個月的認證仍是強制的，因為 HEPA 可能有微小洩漏、壓差感測器可能漂移，只有測試才能確認。

重點提示：動態 vs 靜態煙霧測試

USP 要求的是動態 (Dynamic) 煙霧測試——在人員存在且模擬操作的條件下進行。靜態測試（無人、無操作）不能充分代表實際調配時的氣流狀況。

煙霧測試的目的：確認單向氣流能「掃過」(sweep) 直接調配區 (DCA)，而不是在某處形成渦流（eddies）或停滯區（dead zones）。

認證頻率規則

必須認證的時機：
  ✓ 初始使用前
  ✓ 至少每 6 個月一次
  ✓ 設施改建或重新設計後
  ✓ 任何 PEC 更換或移位後
  ✓ 任何可能影響氣流的房間改變後

認證報告必須包含：
  HVAC 的 ACPH + PEC 貢獻的 ACPH + 總 ACPH
  測試期間 PEC/SEC 中的人員數量

實務應用：搬移設備即觸發重新認證

這是設施管理中常被忽略的規定：任何 PEC 的位置改變（即使只是稍微移動以便清潔），理論上都應重新進行動態煙霧測試，因為設備位置影響氣流模式。實務上建議在 SOP 中設定明確的「允許位移範圍」，超出即觸發重新認證。

原文內容 Original Text

An effective microbiological air and surface monitoring program provides information on the environmental quality of the compounding area. It identifies environmental quality trends over time, identifies potential routes of contamination, and allows for implementation of corrective actions to minimize the risk of CSP contamination.

6.1 General Monitoring Requirements

The microbiological air and surface monitoring program must include 1) viable impact volumetric airborne particulate sampling and 2) surface sampling.

Microbiological air and/or surface monitoring must be conducted in all classified areas during dynamic operating conditions. In addition to specific sampling frequencies, sampling must be performed:

  • In conjunction with the certification of new facilities and equipment
  • After any servicing of facilities or equipment

重點提示：表面採樣的時機選擇

表面採樣必須在調配結束後、清潔前進行。這個時機設計得非常聰明：它反映的是調配過程中累積的真實污染程度，而非清潔後的結果。

若在清潔後採樣，結果會人為偏低，無法真實評估調配過程中的污染狀態。

比喻：像在廚房打烊前檢查

想像一個餐廳的衛生檢查：最好的時間是晚餐高峰後、清潔前。此時砧板、爐台、台面上的污染程度最能反映廚房在工作時的實際衛生狀況。

若等到清潔後才檢查，已看不到真實的污染——只能看到清潔效果。無菌調配環境的表面採樣邏輯完全相同。

這是 Category 3 運作成本顯著高於 Category 2 的直接原因之一，必須在定價中充分反映。

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：清潔 → 消毒 → 殺孢子 的三步邏輯

這三個步驟不可互換，有嚴格的邏輯順序：

  1. 清潔 (Cleaning)：用清潔劑物理去除有機物和殘留藥物。有機物存在會降低消毒劑效果。
  2. 消毒 (Disinfecting)：用 EPA 認可消毒劑殺滅細菌、黴菌、病毒。
  3. 殺孢子 (Sporicidal)：殺滅細菌芽孢（最具抵抗力的微生物形態）。

若跳過清潔直接消毒，有機污物會保護微生物免受消毒劑的殺滅。

比喻：三步驟如同餐廳餐具洗滌

第一步：用清潔劑去除食物殘留（清潔）。第二步：放入洗碗機高溫殺菌（消毒）。第三步（對手術器械）：用高壓蒸汽滅菌芽孢（殺孢子）。

若直接跳到第二步而不清潔，食物殘留就是微生物的保護盾。同樣地，無菌調配室的表面必須先清潔，再消毒，再殺孢子。

重點提示：殺孢子劑頻率的 Category 差異

PEC 內部的殺孢子劑施用頻率：

  • Category 1/2：每月一次
  • Category 3：每週一次

這個差距（4倍）對設施的清潔成本和排程影響顯著。殺孢子劑通常比普通消毒劑更具腐蝕性，接觸時間更長（有時需要 10+ 分鐘），會增加設備磨損和調配停機時間。

PEC 內部清潔的 70% IPA 規則

調配開始前：
  → 在清潔消毒後施用 70% IPA（去除殘留）
  → 立即在調配前再次施用 70% IPA

調配過程中：
  → 若調配 ≤ 30 分鐘：每 30 分鐘施用一次
  → 若調配 > 30 分鐘：不中斷，結束後立即消毒

重要：70% IPA 必須是無菌的（Sterile 70% IPA）
      必須讓表面完全乾燥後才開始調配

重點提示：「接觸時間」是關鍵

消毒劑和殺孢子劑都有「最短接觸時間 (minimum contact time/dwell time)」的要求。噴上去立刻擦掉等於沒消毒。必須讓消毒劑在表面保持足夠時間，讓活性成分充分作用。

SOP 中必須明確規定每種消毒劑的接觸時間，且人員必須知道並遵守。

8. Introducing Items into the SEC and PEC

8.1 Introducing Items into the SEC: Before any item is introduced into the clean side of anteroom(s), placed into pass-through chamber(s), or brought into the SCA, it must be wiped with a sporicidal disinfectant, EPA-registered disinfectant, or sterile 70% IPA using low-lint wipers by personnel wearing gloves. All packages must be reinspected to detect container breaks, looseness of the cap, and deviation from expected appearance.

9.3.4 Component handling and storage: Temperature must be monitored at least once daily. Components with "not for pharmaceutical use," "not for injectable use," "not for human use" or equivalent statements must not be used.

10. Sterilization and Depyrogenation | 滅菌與去熱原

原文內容 Original Text

When selecting the sterilization method for CSPs prepared from one or more nonsterile starting components or using nonsterile supplies or devices, personnel must take into consideration the nature of the component(s), their physical and chemical properties, and the intended container closure system. The sterilization method used must sterilize the CSP without degrading its physical and chemical stability or the packaging integrity.

The following must be considered when selecting an appropriate sterilization method:

  • Terminal sterilization (e.g., steam, dry heat, or irradiation) is the preferred method unless the specific CSP or container closure system cannot tolerate terminal sterilization
  • Steam sterilization is not an option if moisture, pressure, or the temperatures used would degrade the CSP or if there is insufficient moisture to sterilize the CSP within the final, sealed container closure system
  • Filtration may not be an option for some compounded preparations, for example preparations with suspended drug particles or emulsions with a significant droplet size

CSPs that are terminally sterilized must use a process intended to achieve a probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU) of 10−6. [This is also called the sterility assurance level (SAL).] A PNSU of 10−6 is equivalent to a probability that 1 unit in a million is nonsterile. A PNSU value cannot be applied to CSPs that are aseptically filled following sterilization by filtration.

Injectable compounded preparations that contain nonsterile components or that come into contact with nonsterile devices during any phase of the compounding procedure must be sterilized within 6 h after completing the preparation to minimize the generation of bacterial endotoxins in CSPs.

10.1 Depyrogenation

Dry heat depyrogenation must be used to render glassware, metal, and other thermostable containers and components pyrogen free. Depyrogenation processes typically operate at approximately 170°–400°, depending on the exposure time (e.g., 250° for 30 min).

The effectiveness of the dry heat depyrogenation cycle must be established initially and verified annually using endotoxin challenge vials (ECVs) to demonstrate that the cycle achieves a ≥3-log reduction in endotoxins. Must be re-established if there are changes to the cycle (load conditions, duration, or temperature).

10.2 Sterilization by Filtration

Sterilizing filters must be sterile, depyrogenated, have a nominal pore size of 0.22 µm or smaller, and be appropriate for pharmaceutical use. Sterilizing filters must be certified by the manufacturer to retain at least 107 microorganisms of a strain of Brevundimonas diminuta per square centimeter of upstream filter surface area.

Filter units used to sterilize CSPs must be subjected to the manufacturers' recommended integrity testing, such as a post-use bubble point test. If multiple filters are required, each must pass an integrity test.

When CSPs are known to contain excessive particulate matter, a prefiltration step using a filter of larger nominal pore size (e.g., 1.2 µm) must be performed before the sterilizing filter. CSPs filtered through a failed integrity test filter must be discarded or, after investigation, re-filtered not more than one additional time.

10.3 Sterilization by Steam Heat

Autoclaving is the preferred method for terminal sterilization of aqueous CSPs in their final, sealed container closure system. To achieve sterility: all materials must be directly exposed to steam under adequate pressure for the required time (e.g., 20–60 min at 121° saturated steam under 15 psi). The effectiveness must be verified and documented with each sterilization run using appropriate biological indicators (spores of Geobacillus stearothermophilus).

10.4 Sterilization by Dry Heat

Dry heat requires higher temperatures and longer exposure times than steam. Usually performed at 160° or higher. Heated air must be evenly distributed throughout the chamber (typically by an air blower). The effectiveness must be verified using biological indicators (spores of Bacillus atrophaeus ATCC 9372).

重點提示：PNSU 不適用過濾滅菌

PNSU 10⁻⁶ 是終端滅菌的概率保證指標，基於已知的初始微生物負荷（bioburden）計算。過濾滅菌的原理是物理截留而非殺滅，無法以同樣邏輯計算非無菌概率。

因此，過濾滅菌後以無菌充填方式分裝的 CSP，其無菌保證是透過過濾效率驗證（Brevundimonas 測試）+ 整個無菌製程的控制，而非 PNSU 數值。

比喻：終端滅菌 vs 過濾滅菌

終端滅菌就像「把食物完全密封後再放入高壓鍋蒸煮」——在最終密封狀態下完成消毒，之後不再有再污染機會。

過濾滅菌則像「先用超細濾網過濾食物液體，再裝入容器」——濾網必須完整（氣泡點測試確認），裝填過程必須完全無菌（無菌充填）。後者對操作環境的要求更高。

11. Master Formulation and Compounding Records

11.1 Master Formulation Record (MFR): A master formulation record (MFR) is a detailed record of procedures that describes how the CSP is to be prepared. An MFR must be created for all CSPs prepared from nonsterile ingredient(s) or CSPs prepared for more than one patient.

Box 9. Master Formulation Records must include at least:
• Name, strength or activity, and dosage form of the CSP
• Identities and amounts of all ingredients; relevant characteristics of components (e.g., particle size, salt form, purity grade, solubility)
• Type and size of container closure system(s)
• Complete instructions for preparing the CSP, including equipment, supplies, description of compounding steps, and any special precautions
• Physical description of the final CSP
• BUD and storage requirements
• Reference source to support the stability of the CSP
• Quality control (QC) procedures (e.g., pH testing, filter integrity testing)
• Other information as needed to ensure repeatability (e.g., adjusting pH and tonicity; sterilization method)

11.2 Compounding Record (CR): A CR documents the compounding of each CSP. A CR must be created for all Category 1, Category 2, and Category 3 CSPs, and also for immediate-use CSPs prepared for more than one patient.

Box 10. Compounding Records must include at least:
• Name, strength or activity, and dosage form of the CSP
• Date and time of preparation
• Assigned internal identification number (e.g., prescription, order, or lot number)
• Method to identify individuals involved in the compounding process and individuals verifying the final CSP
• Name of each component; vendor, lot number, and expiration date (for multi-patient or nonsterile-starting CSPs)
• Weight or volume and strength or activity of each component
• Total quantity compounded; final yield (containers/units)
• Assigned BUD and storage requirements
• Results of QC procedures (visual inspection, filter integrity testing, pH testing)

12. Release Inspections and Testing

12.1 Visual Inspection: At completion of compounding, before release, the CSP must be visually inspected to confirm: physical appearance is as expected (free of visible particulates, discoloration, or defects), label matches the prescription or medication order, container closure integrity (no leakage, cracks, improper seals).

12.2 Sterility Testing: Sterility testing is not required for Category 1 CSPs. For Category 2 CSPs assigned a BUD that requires sterility testing (see Table 13) and all Category 3 CSPs, testing must be performed according to ⟨71⟩. Maximum batch size for all CSPs requiring sterility testing is limited to 250 final yield units.

For batches of 1–39 CSPs, sterility testing must be performed on 10% of the number of CSPs prepared, rounded up to the next whole number.

12.3 Bacterial Endotoxins Testing: Category 1 injectable CSPs do not require endotoxin testing. Category 2 injectable CSPs compounded from one or more nonsterile component(s) with a BUD requiring sterility testing and all Category 3 injectable CSPs compounded from nonsterile components must be tested for bacterial endotoxins (see ⟨85⟩). Category 2 injectable CSPs from nonsterile components with a BUD not requiring sterility testing should be tested for endotoxins.

13. Labeling

All labeling must comply with laws and regulations of the applicable regulatory jurisdiction. The label on each immediate container must prominently and legibly display:

  • Assigned internal identification number (barcode, prescription, order, or lot number)
  • Active ingredient(s) and their amounts, activities, or concentrations
  • Storage conditions if other than controlled room temperature
  • BUD
  • Dosage form
  • Total amount or volume
  • Single-dose or multiple-dose statement (when space permits)

The labeling must also display: route(s) of administration, special handling instructions, warning statements, and compounding facility name and contact information if the CSP is sent outside of the facility.

核心概念：MFR vs CR — 配方書 vs 生產記錄

14.1 Terminology

The BUD is determined from the date and time that preparation of the CSP is initiated. The BUD is not intended to limit the time during which the CSP is administered. BUDs and expiration dates are not the same.

Term Definition Applicability
BUD Date (or hour and date) after which a CSP must not be used. Determined from date and time that preparation is initiated. All CSPs
Expiration date Time during which a product can be expected to meet requirements of a USP–NF monograph or maintain expected quality under specified storage conditions. Conventionally manufactured products, APIs, added substances

Table 11. Summary of Terms

14.2 Parameters to Consider in Establishing a BUD

BUDs for CSPs must be established based on sterility maintenance factors including:

  • Conditions of the environment in which the CSP is prepared
  • Aseptic processing and sterilization method
  • Starting components (sterile or nonsterile)
  • Whether or not sterility testing is performed
  • Storage conditions (packaging and temperature)

Terminal sterilization allows for longer BUDs than aseptic processing because it provides reasonable assurance of sterility. A longer BUD is permitted for aseptically processed CSPs from sterile starting components than from nonsterile components. Sterility testing permits longer BUDs because it provides additional assurance.

14.4.3 Stability Requirements: The BUD assigned to a Category 3 CSP must be supported by stability data obtained using a stability-indicating analytical method that can distinguish the active ingredient from its degradants and impurities (e.g., by forced degradation studies) and quantify the amount of the active ingredient. A multiple-dose CSP must be prepared as a Category 2 or Category 3 CSP. An aqueous multiple-dose CSP must pass antimicrobial effectiveness testing in accordance with ⟨51⟩. 導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：BUD 的五個決定因素

BUD 由五個因素共同決定，每一個都會縮短或延長可允許的使用期限：

  1. 製備環境（Category）：SCA vs Cleanroom Suite
  2. 滅菌方法：終端滅菌 > 過濾滅菌（無菌充填）
  3. 起始原料：全無菌 > 含非無菌成分
  4. 滅菌測試：通過後可獲得更長 BUD
  5. 儲存條件：冷凍 > 冷藏 > 室溫

BUD 決策快速查閱

Category 1（最短）：
  室溫 ≤ 12h；冷藏 ≤ 24h

Category 2（依條件）：
  最短：無菌充填+非無菌原料+無ST+室溫 = 1天
  最長：終端滅菌+ST通過+冷凍 = 90天

Category 3（最長）：
  最短：無菌充填+ST通過+室溫 = 60天
  最長：終端滅菌+ST通過+冷凍 = 180天

  ★ Cat 3 還需要：
    穩定性數據（穩定性指示方法）
    每次調配均需ST + 內毒素測試

重點提示：BUD 不累加原則

CSP 可能在不同溫度下依序儲存（例如：先冷凍，解凍後冷藏，最終室溫給藥）。但 BUD 不能累加！

一旦 CSP 被置於需要較短 BUD 的儲存條件（如室溫），就必須在那個較短的時間內使用。例：Category 2 無菌充填+全無菌起始原料 CSP，冷凍45天後轉室溫，室溫 BUD 僅為 4 天（不是 45+10+4 天）。

比喻：BUD vs Expiration Date

廠商效期（Expiration Date）就像原廠蘋果汁的保存期限——是在工廠密封、完整測試後標示的。

BUD 就像把蘋果汁開瓶後裝入另一個容器——從開始製備的那一刻起計算，因為原廠保護已不再完整，環境污染的風險從此時起開始累積。

實務應用：Category 3 的商業價值

Category 3 CSP 最長可達 180 天冷凍 BUD（終端滅菌+滅菌測試）。這使大批量調配和集中配送成為可能，大幅提升效率。

然而代價是顯著的：每批次均需滅菌測試（約 14 天出結果）、需要穩定性數據支撐、每週環境監測等。必須計算每個 CSP 的全部成本後，才能判斷 Category 3 是否有商業意義。

15–16. Use of Conventionally Manufactured Products & CSPs as Components | 商業產品與CSP作為原料

原文內容 Original Text

15. Use of Conventionally Manufactured Products as Components

15.1 Use of Conventionally Manufactured Single-Dose Containers: A conventionally manufactured single-dose container is a container closure system that holds a sterile product for parenteral administration that is not required to meet antimicrobial effectiveness testing requirements.

If a single-dose vial is entered or punctured only in an ISO Class 5 or cleaner air, it may be used up to 12 h after initial entry or puncture as long as the labeled storage requirements during that 12-h period are maintained. Opened single-dose ampules must not be stored for any time period.

15.2 Use of Conventionally Manufactured Multiple-Dose Containers: Once initially entering or puncturing the multiple-dose container, the multiple-dose container must not be used for more than 28 days unless otherwise specified by the manufacturer on the labeling.

15.3 Use of Conventionally Manufactured Pharmacy Bulk Packages: A pharmacy bulk package must be used according to the manufacturer's labeling. The pharmacy bulk package must be entered or punctured only in an ISO Class 5 PEC.

16. Use of CSPs as Components

When a CSP is used as a component, care must be taken to minimize the risk of contamination of both the starting component CSP and the final CSP(s).

16.1 Use of Compounded Multiple-Dose CSPs: After a multiple-dose CSP is initially entered or punctured, the multiple-dose CSP must not be used for longer than the assigned BUD or 28 days, whichever is shorter.

16.2 Use of Compounded Single-Dose CSPs and CSP Stock Solutions: When entered in ISO Class 5 or cleaner air, the component CSP may be used for sterile compounding for up to 12 h or its assigned BUD, whichever is shorter. Any remainder must be discarded.

17. SOPs

Facilities that prepare CSPs must develop SOPs for the compounding process and other support activities. SOPs must include the types of CSPs prepared (Category 1, Category 2, Category 3). A designated person(s) must ensure that SOPs are appropriate and implemented, including ensuring that personnel demonstrate competency in performing every procedure that relates to their job function.

SOPs must be reviewed initially and at least every 12 months by the designated person(s) to ensure that they reflect current practices. Any changes must be documented. Revisions must be communicated to all personnel involved, and personnel should document acknowledgment of the communication.

All compounding personnel must be trained to:

  • Recognize potential problems, deviations, failures, or errors associated with preparing a CSP
  • Report any problems, deviations, failures, or errors to the designated person(s)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：開封後使用期限的邏輯

商業製造產品開封後的使用期限是基於：容器被穿刺或打開後，無菌屏障即被破壞，微生物污染風險從這一刻開始。

  • 單次劑量瓶（在 ISO 5 下穿刺）：12 小時
  • 安瓿（已開）：不得儲存任何時間 → 立即使用
  • 多次劑量瓶（含防腐劑）：28 天（或廠商標示）

重點提示：安瓿開封後的「零儲存」規定

開封的單次劑量安瓿（glass ampule）必須立即使用，不得儲存任何時間。這比玻璃瓶（vial）嚴格得多——原因是安瓿開封後形成完全開放的開口，無任何再密封機制，微生物和微粒污染風險極高。

若調配需要安瓿的內容物，必須在無菌條件下立即使用，剩餘部分立即丟棄。

開封後使用期限快速對照

商業產品：
  單次劑量瓶（ISO 5 穿刺） → 最多 12 小時
  安瓿（開封後）           → 零儲存，立即使用
  多次劑量瓶               → 最多 28 天（或廠商標示）
  藥房批量包裝             → 依廠商標示

CSP 組件：
  多次劑量 CSP（已開）     → BUD 或 28 天（較短者）
  單次劑量/儲備液（ISO 5） → BUD 或 12 小時（較短者）

比喻：SOP 如同作業標準說明書

想像一家連鎖餐廳：每個分店的廚師都遵循同一套詳細的烹飪 SOP——食材用量、料理順序、安全溫度、衛生要求。即使每天的廚師不同，出餐品質仍然一致。

調配設施的 SOP 作用相同：確保不同人員在不同班次中，都能以一致且合規的方式調配 CSP。

18. Quality Assurance and Quality Control | 品質保證與品質管制

原文內容 Original Text

Quality Assurance (QA) is a system of procedures, activities, and oversight that ensures that the compounding process consistently meets quality standards. Quality Control (QC) is the sampling, testing, and documentation of results that, taken together, ensure that specifications have been met before release of the CSP.

A facility's QA and QC programs must be formally established and documented in the facility's SOPs. Designated person(s) must ensure that the facility has formal, written QA and QC programs that establish a system of:

  1. Adherence to procedures
  2. Prevention and detection of errors and other quality problems
  3. Evaluation of complaints and adverse events
  4. Appropriate investigations and corrective actions

The overall QA and QC program must be reviewed at least once every 12 months by the designated person(s). The results of the review must be documented.

18.1 Notification About and Recall of Out-of-Specification Dispensed CSPs

If a CSP is dispensed or administered before release testing results are known, the facility must have procedures to:

  • Immediately notify the prescriber of a failure with the potential to cause patient harm
  • Recall any unused dispensed CSPs and quarantine any stock remaining in the pharmacy
  • Investigate if other lots are affected and recall if necessary

An SOP for recall must contain:

  • Procedures to determine the severity of the problem and the urgency for the recall
  • Procedures to determine the distribution of any affected CSP, including date and quantity
  • Procedures to identify patients who have received the CSP
  • Procedures for disposal and documentation of the recalled CSP
  • Procedures to investigate and document the reason for failure

The recall must be reported to appropriate regulatory bodies as required by laws and regulations of the applicable regulatory jurisdiction.

18.2 Complaint Handling

Compounding facilities must develop and implement SOPs for handling complaints. A designated person(s) must review all complaints to determine whether the complaint indicates a potential quality problem. A readily retrievable written or electronic record of each complaint must be kept, containing the name of the complainant or unique identifier, date received, nature of the complaint, and the response. A CSP returned in connection with a complaint must be quarantined until destroyed after completion of the investigation.

18.3 Adverse Event Reporting

Adverse events potentially associated with the quality of CSPs must be reported in accordance with the facility's SOPs and all laws and regulations of the applicable regulatory jurisdiction. If the investigation reveals a quality problem likely to affect other patients, those patients and prescribers must be informed.

19. CSP Handling, Storage, Packaging, Shipping, and Transport

19.1 Handling and Storing CSPs: Temperature must be monitored each day, either manually or by a continuous recording device. Temperature monitoring devices must be verified for accuracy at least every 12 months. When a CSP has been exposed to temperature excursions outside storage limits, a designated person(s) must determine whether the CSP retains its integrity or quality. If this cannot be determined, it must be discarded.

19.2 Packaging: Packaging materials should protect CSPs from damage, leakage, contamination, degradation, and adsorption. If the CSP is sensitive to light, light-resistant packaging materials must be used.

19.3 Shipping and Transporting: When shipping or transporting CSPs that require special handling, personnel must include specific handling instructions on the exterior of the container.

核心概念：QA vs QC — 系統 vs 測試

QA (Quality Assurance, 品質保證)：預防性的系統性活動，確保整個調配過程符合標準。QA 是「確保不會出錯的體制」。

QC (Quality Control, 品質管制)：透過採樣、測試和記錄，在放行前確認規格已達到。QC 是「確認這批次沒有出錯的驗證」。

類比：QA 是餐廳的食安體系（培訓、清潔規範、驗收標準）；QC 是每道菜出餐前的試嚐確認。

重點提示：召回 SOP 的設計必須包含患者追蹤機制

召回 SOP 中最關鍵但常被忽略的要素：必須能識別哪些患者已經接受了該 CSP。這需要完整的配發記錄追蹤系統。

若記錄不完整，一旦發生召回，無法通知受影響患者，將面臨重大監管和法律責任。這是調配設施需要強健的電子記錄系統的核心原因之一。

比喻：CSP 召回如同汽車召回

汽車製造商召回問題車型的流程：(1) 確認問題嚴重性、(2) 識別所有受影響車輛和車主、(3) 通知車主、(4) 提供補救措施、(5) 記錄和向監管機構報告。

CSP 召回程序完全相同邏輯，只是「車主」換成「患者」，「車輛 VIN」換成「CSP 批號/處方編號」。

QA/QC 年度審查要求

QA/QC 程序年度審查要求：
  頻率：至少每 12 個月一次
  執行者：Designated Person(s)
  結果：必須記錄
  必要時：採取適當行動

SOP 年度審查要求：
  頻率：至少每 12 個月一次
  更改時：記錄、通知相關人員
  人員應確認收到通知

投訴記錄必須包含：
  • 投訴者姓名或唯一識別碼
  • 接到投訴日期
  • 投訴性質
  • 回應措施
  • 調查結果和後續

實務應用：溫度偏差的決策流程

當發現 CSP 儲存溫度偏差時，designated person 必須根據以下步驟決策：

  1. 確認偏差幅度和持續時間
  2. 查閱藥物穩定性文獻或廠商資料
  3. 若能確認 CSP 仍符合品質要求 → 記錄後放行或繼續使用
  4. 若無法確認 → 必須丟棄，不得猶豫

每次偏差都必須記錄，即使決定繼續使用。

20–21. Documentation & Compounding Allergenic Extracts | 文件管理與過敏原萃取物調配

原文內容 Original Text

20. Documentation

All facilities where CSPs are prepared must have and maintain written or electronic documentation demonstrating compliance with this chapter. Documentation must include at a minimum:

  • Personnel training, competency assessments, and qualification records including corrective actions for any failures
  • Certification reports, including corrective actions for any failures
  • Environmental air and surface monitoring procedures and results
  • Equipment records (calibration, verification, and maintenance reports)
  • Receipt of components
  • SOPs, MFRs (if required), and CRs (if required)
  • Release inspection and testing records
  • Information related to complaints and adverse events including corrective actions taken
  • Results of investigations and corrective actions

All required documentation for a particular CSP (e.g., MFR, CR, and release inspection and testing results) must be readily retrievable for at least 2 years after preparation or as required by laws and regulations of the applicable regulatory jurisdiction or accrediting organization(s), whichever is longer.

21. Compounding Allergenic Extracts

Licensed allergenic extracts are mixed and diluted into prescription sets for an individual patient.

21.1 Personnel Qualifications: A designated person with training and expertise in allergen immunotherapy is responsible for ensuring that personnel are trained, evaluated, and supervised. Annual personnel training and competency must be documented. Before independent compounding is allowed, all compounders must successfully complete GFT no fewer than 3 separate times. After initial evaluation, GFT must be completed at least every 12 months.

21.2 Personnel Hygiene and Garbing for Allergenic Extracts: Minimum garb requirements include: low-lint garment with enclosed neck, low-lint disposable head cover, face mask, sterile powder-free gloves. Throughout the process, sterile 70% IPA must be applied to gloves and allowed to dry.

21.3 Facilities for Allergenic Extracts: The compounding process must occur in an ISO Class 5 PEC or in a dedicated allergenic extract compounding area (AECA). The AECA must be located away from unsealed windows, doors to the outdoors, and traffic flow. Carpet is not allowed in the AECA. If used, a visible perimeter must define the AECA; access during compounding must be restricted to authorized personnel.

21.4 Cleaning and Disinfecting for Allergenic Extracts: In a PEC, all interior surfaces must be cleaned and disinfected each day of use before compounding begins. Apply sterile 70% IPA to the horizontal work surface between each prescription set.

21.5 BUDs for Allergenic Extract Prescription Sets: The BUD must be no later than the earliest expiration date of any allergenic extract or any diluent that is part of the prescription set, and the BUD must not exceed 1 year from the date the prescription set is mixed or diluted.

21.6 Labeling for Allergenic Extracts: Label must display: patient name, type and fractional dilution of each vial with corresponding vial number, BUD, storage conditions.

21.8 Documentation for Allergenic Extracts: Documentation must include: SOPs, personnel training records, certification reports of the PEC (if used), temperature logs for refrigerators, CRs for individual prescription sets, complaints and adverse events records.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：文件保存的「2年規則」

所有 CSP 相關文件必須保存至少 2 年，但若法規或認可機構要求更長，則以更長者為準。

這意味著設施必須建立系統性的文件管理：無論是紙本或電子，都必須易於查閱（readily retrievable）。在稽查時，能否在幾分鐘內調出2年前的特定批次記錄，是衡量文件管理成熟度的重要指標。

核心概念：過敏原萃取物的特殊豁免

Section 21 是 ⟨797⟩ 中少數具有顯著豁免的特殊案例。過敏原萃取物調配不需要符合一般 Category 1/2/3 的所有要求，但有嚴格的前提條件：

  1. 必須使用已許可的商業無菌過敏原產品
  2. 只涉及無菌針頭/注射器穿刺和液體轉移
  3. 不涉及化學合成或非無菌原料

重點提示：AECA — 過敏原調配的替代空間

AECA（Allergenic Extract Compounding Area）是專為過敏原調配設計的非分類空間，類似 SCA 的概念但更寬鬆。AECA 不需要達到任何 ISO 等級，但必須有可見的周界（visible perimeter），且調配期間禁止其他活動進行。

這項豁免的存在反映了現實：大多數過敏科診所無法在短期內建造無菌調配室，但仍需在合規框架內為患者調配個人化的免疫治療注射液。

比喻：文件是品質系統的「記憶」

如果品質系統沒有文件，就像一家沒有記帳的企業——你不知道昨天賺了多少，更無法向稅務機關證明。

CSP 的文件管理也是如此：若沒有完整記錄，一旦患者出現不良反應，無法追溯是哪一批次的 CSP 出了問題，也無法證明設施按照 ⟨797⟩ 規定操作。

文件保存期限要求

最低要求：製備後 2 年
例外：若法規或認可機構要求更長 → 以更長者為準

常見情況：
  ACHC/PCAB 認可機構可能要求更長保存期
  某些州法規也可能要求 3 年以上

過敏原萃取物 BUD 計算：
  最長 = 混合/稀釋日期起算 1 年
  或所有成分的最短效期（哪個早）

實務應用：電子記錄系統的合規要求

電子記錄和電子簽名可以滿足 ⟨797⟩ 的文件要求，前提是：(1) 可隨時調取；(2) 有適當的備份機制；(3) 有審計追蹤（audit trail）防止擅自修改。

電子系統的稽查缺失通常集中在：無備份、可輕易修改記錄（沒有 audit trail）、或舊記錄在系統更新後無法讀取。

Glossary — Selected Key Terms | 詞彙表精選

原文內容 Original Text — Key Definitions

Aseptic processing: A method by which separate, sterile components (e.g., drugs, containers, or closures) are brought together under conditions that maintain their sterility. The components can either be purchased as sterile or, when starting with nonsterile components, can be separately sterilized prior to combining (e.g., by membrane filtration or by autoclave).

Beyond-use date (BUD): The date, or hour and the date, after which a CSP must not be used, stored, or transported. The date is determined from the date and time the preparation is compounded.

Classified area: An area that maintains an air quality classification based on the ISO standards required in this chapter.

Compounded sterile preparation (CSP): A preparation intended to be sterile that is created by combining, admixing, diluting, pooling, reconstituting, repackaging, or otherwise altering a drug product or bulk drug substance.

Critical site: A location that includes any component or fluid pathway surfaces (e.g., vial septa, injection ports, and beakers) or openings (e.g., opened ampules and needle hubs) that are exposed and at risk of direct contact with air, moisture, or touch contamination.

Designated person(s): One or more individuals assigned to be responsible and accountable for the performance and operation of the facility and personnel as related to the preparation of CSPs.

Direct compounding area (DCA): A critical area within the ISO Class 5 PEC where critical sites are exposed to unidirectional HEPA-filtered air, also known as first air.

Dynamic airflow smoke pattern test: A PEC test in which a visible source of smoke is used to observe air patterns within the unidirectional space under dynamic operating conditions.

Dynamic operating conditions: Conditions in the compounding area in which operating personnel are present and simulating or performing compounding. The conditions should reflect the largest number of personnel and highest complexity of compounding expected during routine operations.

First air: The air exiting the HEPA filter in a unidirectional air stream.

Pharmaceutical isolator: An enclosure that provides HEPA-filtered ISO Class 5 unidirectional air operated at a continuously higher pressure than its surrounding environment and is decontaminated using an automated system. It uses only decontaminated interfaces or rapid transfer ports for materials transfer. [Note—A CAI or CACI is NOT a pharmaceutical isolator.]

Primary engineering control (PEC): A device or zone that provides an ISO Class 5 air quality environment for sterile compounding.

Probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU): The probability of an item being nonsterile after it has been exposed to a verified sterilization process. A PNSU value can only be applied to terminal sterilization. [Also called the sterility assurance level (SAL).]

Secondary engineering control (SEC): The area where the PEC is placed (e.g., a cleanroom suite or an SCA). It incorporates specific design and operational parameters required to minimize the risk of contamination within the compounding area.

Sterility assurance level (SAL): See the entry for Probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU).

Terminal sterilization: The application of a lethal process (e.g., steam, dry heat, irradiation) to sealed containers for the purpose of achieving a predetermined PNSU of greater than 10−6.

核心概念：詞彙表的合規意義

USP ⟨797⟩ 的詞彙表不只是定義清單，而是具有法律效力的標準用語。使用精確的術語有三個實務重要性：

  1. 稽查溝通：稽查員和業者對同一術語有相同理解，避免誤解
  2. SOP 撰寫：使用官方術語確保 SOP 符合監管語言
  3. 爭議解決：當法規解釋有爭議時，詞彙表提供基準定義

重點提示：三個容易混淆的術語對比

CAI ≠ Pharmaceutical Isolator（本章明確聲明兩者不同）

  • CAI：無自動去污系統，手動進出
  • Pharmaceutical Isolator：必須有自動去污系統（如 VHP）和 RTP，需要 ISO Class 8 室

DCA vs PEC：

  • PEC 是整個設備（如整個 LAFW 工作台）
  • DCA 是 PEC 內部最關鍵的直接調配區域，暴露於「第一道氣流 (first air)」

比喻：First Air — 最乾淨的空氣

HEPA 過濾器就像淨水器的濾芯：直接從濾芯流出的水（first air）是最純凈的，尚未接觸任何其他表面。調配人員必須確保 CSP 的關鍵部位（critical sites）只暴露在 first air 中，而不是接觸過設備表面的「二手空氣」。

這就是「不要用身體阻擋 first air 流向 critical site」規則的根本依據。

實務應用：動態 vs 靜態操作條件的稽查意義

「Dynamic operating conditions」要求在人員存在、模擬最複雜操作的條件下進行測試。這意味著：

  • 認證測試必須在代表最壞情況的條件下進行
  • 環境監測採樣必須在動態條件下進行
  • 若設施在「沒人、沒操作」時進行測試，結果會被認為不具代表性
