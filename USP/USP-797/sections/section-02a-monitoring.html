6. Microbiological Air and Surface Monitoring | 微生物空氣與表面監測

原文內容 Original Text

An effective microbiological air and surface monitoring program provides information on the environmental quality of the compounding area. It identifies environmental quality trends over time, identifies potential routes of contamination, and allows for implementation of corrective actions to minimize the risk of CSP contamination.

6.1 General Monitoring Requirements

The microbiological air and surface monitoring program must include 1) viable impact volumetric airborne particulate sampling and 2) surface sampling.

Microbiological air and/or surface monitoring must be conducted in all classified areas during dynamic operating conditions. In addition to specific sampling frequencies, sampling must be performed:

  • In conjunction with the certification of new facilities and equipment
  • After any servicing of facilities or equipment
  • In response to identified problems (e.g., positive growth in sterility tests of CSPs)
  • In response to identified trends (e.g., repeated positive GFT results, failed media fill testing)
  • In response to changes that could impact the sterile compounding environment (e.g., change in cleaning agents)

Air sampling must be conducted during dynamic operating conditions and surface sampling should be performed at the end of a compounding activity or shift but before the area has been cleaned and disinfected.

6.2 Monitoring Air Quality for Viable Airborne Particles

6.2.1 Viable air sampling — timing and locations: Volumetric active air sampling using an impaction air sampler must be conducted in each classified area [ISO Class 5 PEC and ISO Class 7 and 8 rooms] during dynamic operating conditions:

  • For Category 1 and 2 CSPs: at least every 6 months
  • For Category 3 CSPs: within 30 days prior to commencement and at least monthly thereafter
Box 5. Active Air Sampling Procedures for Viable Airborne Monitoring
1. Follow manufacturer's instructions for impaction air sampler.
2. Test at least 1 cubic meter (1000 L) of air from each location.
3. Retrieve media device, cover it; invert plates to prevent condensate drop.
4. Incubate at 30°–35° for ≥48 h, examine, record cfu/m³.
5. Then incubate at 20°–25° for ≥5 additional days, examine, record cfu/m³.
6. Alternative: use two media devices per location incubated concurrently at different temperatures.

Table 7. Action Levels for Viable Airborne Particle Air Sampling

6.3 Monitoring Surfaces for Viable Particles

6.3.1 Surface sampling — timing and locations:

Samples should be taken from:

  • Equipment contained within the PEC
  • Staging or work area(s) near the PEC
  • Frequently touched surfaces

Surface sampling frequency:

  • For Category 1 and 2 CSPs: at least monthly
  • For Category 3 CSPs: prior to assigning BUD longer than Table 13 limits, and at least weekly on a regularly scheduled basis; additionally within the PEC at end of each batch before cleaning

Table 8. Action Levels for Surface Sampling

6.3.3 Surface sampling data evaluation: If levels exceed Table 8 action levels, an attempt must be made to identify any microorganism recovered to the genus level (see &langle;1113&rangle;) with the assistance of a microbiologist. The corrective action plan must be dependent on the cfu count and the microorganism recovered.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：EM 的「三個為什麼」

環境監測 (Environmental Monitoring, EM) 的三大目的：

  1. 趨勢識別：單次結果不一定有意義，但連續數據揭示系統性問題（如某個工作台持續偏高）
  2. 污染路徑識別：哪個位置的菌落計數總是偏高？可能是清潔死角或人流問題
  3. 矯正行動觸發：超過 Action Level 時必須調查，而非忽略

EM 行動水準完整對照表

空氣採樣 (cfu/m³):
  ISO Class 5 (PEC)     → Action Level: >1
  ISO Class 7 (緩衝室)  → Action Level: >10
  ISO Class 8 (前室)    → Action Level: >100

表面採樣 (cfu/媒體裝置):
  ISO Class 5 (PEC)     → Action Level: >3
  ISO Class 7 (緩衝室)  → Action Level: >5
  ISO Class 8 (前室)    → Action Level: >50

採樣頻率：
  Cat 1/2 空氣採樣：每 6 個月
  Cat 3 空氣採樣：每月（調配前30天內）
  Cat 1/2 表面採樣：每月
  Cat 3 表面採樣：每週 + 每批次後

重點提示：表面採樣的時機選擇

表面採樣必須在調配結束後、清潔前進行。這個時機設計得非常聰明：它反映的是調配過程中累積的真實污染程度，而非清潔後的結果。

若在清潔後採樣，結果會人為偏低，無法真實評估調配過程中的污染狀態。

比喻：像在廚房打烊前檢查

想像一個餐廳的衛生檢查：最好的時間是晚餐高峰後、清潔前。此時砧板、爐台、台面上的污染程度最能反映廚房在工作時的實際衛生狀況。

若等到清潔後才檢查，已看不到真實的污染——只能看到清潔效果。無菌調配環境的表面採樣邏輯完全相同。

實務應用：Category 3 的每週表面採樣成本

Category 3 CSP 要求每週表面採樣——這相當於 Category 1/2 月頻率的四倍。加上每批次後的 PEC 採樣，如果設施每週調配5批次 Category 3 CSP，等於每週至少6次表面採樣記錄。

這是 Category 3 運作成本顯著高於 Category 2 的直接原因之一，必須在定價中充分反映。

7. Cleaning, Disinfecting, and Applying Sporicidal Disinfectants | 清潔、消毒與殺孢子劑

原文內容 Original Text

Surfaces in classified areas used to prepare Category 1, Category 2, and Category 3 CSPs must be: Cleaned, Disinfected, Sporicidal disinfectants applied — according to the frequencies described in Table 10.

Additionally, in a PEC, sterile 70% IPA must be applied after cleaning and disinfecting (or after one-step disinfectant cleaner or sporicidal disinfectant application) to remove any residue. Sterile 70% IPA must also be applied immediately before initiating compounding. During the compounding process, sterile 70% IPA must be applied to the horizontal work surface at least every 30 min if the compounding process takes 30 min or less. If the compounding process takes more than 30 min, compounding must not be disrupted, and the work surface must be disinfected immediately after compounding.

Table 9. Purpose of Cleaning, Disinfecting, and Sporicidal Disinfectants

Surfaces must be cleaned prior to being disinfected with an EPA-registered disinfectant unless an EPA-registered one-step disinfectant cleaner is used. A sporicidal disinfectant must also be applied. After the disinfectant or sporicidal disinfectant is applied, the agent must be allowed to dwell for the minimum contact time specified by the manufacturer.

Cleaning must be performed in the direction of clean to dirty areas. The same floor mop may be used in both the buffer and anteroom, but only in that order.

Table 10 Summary. Minimum Frequency for Cleaning/Disinfecting in Classified Areas

7.1 Agents and Supplies

Cleaning and disinfecting agents must be selected and used with careful consideration of compatibilities, effectiveness, and user safety. Cleaning, disinfecting and sporicidal agents used within the PEC must be sterile. When diluting concentrated agents for use in the PEC, sterile water must be used.

All cleaning and disinfecting supplies (wipers, sponges, pads, and mop heads) must be low lint. Supplies used in the PEC must be sterile with the exception of tool handles and holders, which must be cleaned and disinfected prior to use.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：清潔 → 消毒 → 殺孢子 的三步邏輯

這三個步驟不可互換，有嚴格的邏輯順序：

  1. 清潔 (Cleaning)：用清潔劑物理去除有機物和殘留藥物。有機物存在會降低消毒劑效果。
  2. 消毒 (Disinfecting)：用 EPA 認可消毒劑殺滅細菌、黴菌、病毒。
  3. 殺孢子 (Sporicidal)：殺滅細菌芽孢（最具抵抗力的微生物形態）。

若跳過清潔直接消毒，有機污物會保護微生物免受消毒劑的殺滅。

比喻：三步驟如同餐廳餐具洗滌

第一步：用清潔劑去除食物殘留（清潔）。第二步：放入洗碗機高溫殺菌（消毒）。第三步（對手術器械）：用高壓蒸汽滅菌芽孢（殺孢子）。

若直接跳到第二步而不清潔，食物殘留就是微生物的保護盾。同樣地，無菌調配室的表面必須先清潔，再消毒，再殺孢子。

重點提示：殺孢子劑頻率的 Category 差異

PEC 內部的殺孢子劑施用頻率：

  • Category 1/2：每月一次
  • Category 3：每週一次

這個差距（4倍）對設施的清潔成本和排程影響顯著。殺孢子劑通常比普通消毒劑更具腐蝕性，接觸時間更長（有時需要 10+ 分鐘），會增加設備磨損和調配停機時間。

PEC 內部清潔的 70% IPA 規則

調配開始前：
  → 在清潔消毒後施用 70% IPA（去除殘留）
  → 立即在調配前再次施用 70% IPA

調配過程中：
  → 若調配 ≤ 30 分鐘：每 30 分鐘施用一次
  → 若調配 > 30 分鐘：不中斷，結束後立即消毒

重要：70% IPA 必須是無菌的（Sterile 70% IPA）
      必須讓表面完全乾燥後才開始調配

重點提示：「接觸時間」是關鍵

消毒劑和殺孢子劑都有「最短接觸時間 (minimum contact time/dwell time)」的要求。噴上去立刻擦掉等於沒消毒。必須讓消毒劑在表面保持足夠時間，讓活性成分充分作用。

SOP 中必須明確規定每種消毒劑的接觸時間，且人員必須知道並遵守。

8–9. Introducing Items into SEC/PEC & Equipment | 物品引入與設備管理

原文內容 Original Text

8. Introducing Items into the SEC and PEC

8.1 Introducing Items into the SEC: Before any item is introduced into the clean side of anteroom(s), placed into pass-through chamber(s), or brought into the SCA, it must be wiped with a sporicidal disinfectant, EPA-registered disinfectant, or sterile 70% IPA using low-lint wipers by personnel wearing gloves. If an EPA-registered disinfectant or sporicidal disinfectant is used, the agent must be allowed to dwell for the minimum contact time.

8.2 Introducing Items into the PEC: Just before any item is introduced into the PEC, it must be wiped with sterile 70% IPA using sterile low-lint wipers and allowed to dry before use. When sterile items are received in sealed containers designed to keep them sterile until opening, the sterile items may be removed from the covering as the supplies are introduced into the ISO Class 5 PEC without the need to wipe.

8.3 Use of Sterile 70% IPA on Critical Sites within the PEC: Critical sites (e.g., vial stoppers, ampule necks, and intravenous bag septums) must be wiped with sterile 70% IPA in the PEC to provide both chemical and mechanical actions to remove contaminants. The sterile 70% IPA must be allowed to dry before personnel enter or puncture stoppers and septums or break the necks of ampules.

9. Equipment, Supplies, and Components

9.1 Equipment: Equipment used in compounding CSPs (e.g., automated compounding devices [ACDs] and balances) should be of suitable composition such that the surfaces that contact components are not reactive or sorptive. Equipment that must be brought into classified areas must be wiped with a sporicidal disinfectant, EPA-registered disinfectant, or sterile 70% IPA.

ACDs (Automated Compounding Devices): Before using ACDs, compounding personnel must conduct an accuracy assessment before the first use and again each day the equipment is used to compound CSPs. Compounding personnel must maintain a daily record of the accuracy measurements on days the equipment is in use.

9.2 Supplies: Supplies (e.g., beakers, utensils, needles, syringes, filters, and tubing sets) should be of suitable composition such that the surfaces that contact components are not reactive or sorptive. Supplies in direct contact with the CSP must be sterile and depyrogenated.

9.3 Components:

9.3.1 Component selection: Conventionally manufactured sterile products should be used when available and appropriate for the intended CSP. When APIs are used: must comply with USP–NF monograph, must have a COA, must be manufactured by an FDA-registered facility (in the US).

9.3.2 Component receipt: Upon receipt, external packaging must be examined for evidence of deterioration. Any component found to be of unacceptable quality must be promptly rejected, clearly labeled as rejected, and segregated. The date of receipt must be clearly marked on each API package that lacks a vendor expiration date. Packages lacking a vendor's expiration date must be assigned a conservative expiration date, not to exceed 1 year after receipt.

9.3.3 Component evaluation before use: Compounding personnel must ascertain before use that components are of correct identity, appropriate quality, within expiry date, and stored under appropriate conditions. All packages must be reinspected to detect container breaks, looseness of the cap, and deviation from expected appearance.

9.3.4 Component handling and storage: Temperature must be monitored at least once daily. Components with "not for pharmaceutical use," "not for injectable use," "not for human use" or equivalent statements must not be used.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：物品進入分類區的「逐級消毒」原則

物品從外部進入無菌調配環境需要「逐級升級」消毒處理：

  • 進入 SEC 前室（clean side）：EPA 消毒劑 / 殺孢子劑 / 70% IPA（任選其一，保持接觸時間）
  • 進入 PEC：無菌 70% IPA（更嚴格——必須是無菌等級）
  • PEC 內關鍵部位：仍需擦拭（如針劑瓶口、安瓿頸部）

每次進階一層，都需要更嚴格的處理，因為越接近 CSP，污染影響越直接。

比喻：手術室的消毒分區

手術器械從倉庫到手術台的過程：外包裝擦拭 → 通過清潔室 → 開包進入無菌台 → 外科醫師直接操作。每個環節的潔淨要求都更嚴格。

無菌調配的物品管理邏輯完全相同——從外到內，每一層都要更嚴格。

重點提示：Critical Site 的定義與重要性

Critical Site (關鍵部位)：任何直接接觸空氣、可能被觸碰的開口或流路表面，包括：

  • 注射瓶塞（vial stoppers）
  • 安瓿頸部（ampule necks）
  • 靜脈袋注射隔膜（IV bag septums）

70% IPA 必須完全乾燥後才能穿刺。濕的 IPA 可能影響藥品，也可能使玻璃滑手導致安全事故。

實務應用：1年內使用期限的原料管理

對於沒有廠商效期的 API 和輔料，必須在收貨時標記收貨日期，並指定不超過1年的保存期限。這在院外調配藥局中尤為重要，因為有些散裝原料可能缺乏明確效期。

SOP 必須包含這一規定，並建立追蹤系統（如 FEFO——先到期先使用）。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 空氣 EM 動作水準：ISO 5 >1 cfu/m³；ISO 7 >10 cfu/m³；ISO 8 >100 cfu/m³
  • 表面 EM 動作水準：ISO 5 >3 cfu；ISO 7 >5 cfu；ISO 8 >50 cfu
  • Cat 3 EM 頻率：空氣採樣每月；表面採樣每週 + 每批次後（PEC）
  • 清潔順序：清潔（去污）→ 消毒（殺菌）→ 殺孢子劑（殺芽孢）；每步驟後必須達到接觸時間
  • 無效期原料：收貨日標記，不超過 1 年保存期；嚴禁使用「非藥用」或「非注射用」標示原料
