Introduction & Scope — 導論與適用範圍

原文內容 Original Text

Microbiologically controlled environments are used for a variety of purposes within the healthcare industry. This general information chapter provides information and recommendations for environments where the risk of microbial contamination is controlled through aseptic processing. Products manufactured in such environments include pharmaceutical sterile products, bulk sterile drug substances, sterile intermediates, excipients, and, in certain cases, medical devices.

Aseptic processing environments are far more critical in terms of patient risk than controlled environments used for other manufacturing operations — for example, equipment and component preparation, limited bioburden control of nonsterile products, and processing of terminally sterilized products.

In this chapter, the type of aseptic processing is differentiated by the presence or absence of human operators. An advanced aseptic process is one in which direct intervention with open product containers or exposed product contact surfaces by operators wearing conventional cleanroom garments is not required and never permitted.

The guidance provided in this chapter and the monitoring parameters given for microbiological evaluation should be applied only to clean rooms, restricted-access barrier systems (RABS), and isolators used for aseptic processing. ISO-classified environments used for other purposes are not required to meet the levels of contamination control required for aseptically produced sterile products.

"The terms aseptic and sterile are not synonymous. Sterile means having a complete absence of viable microorganisms. In contemporary aseptic healthcare-product manufacturing, aseptic describes the process for handling sterilized materials in a controlled environment designed to maintain microbial contamination at levels known to present minimal risk."

In any environment where human operators are present, microbial contamination at some level is inevitable. Even the most cautious clean-room environment design and operation will not eliminate the shedding of microorganisms if human operators are present. Thus, an expectation of zero contamination at all locations during every aseptic processing operation is technically not possible and thus is unrealistic.

There are no means to demonstrate that an aseptic processing environment and the product-contact surfaces within that environment are sterile. Although manufacturers should review environmental monitoring results frequently to ensure that the facility operates in a validated state of control, monitoring results can neither prove nor disprove sterility.

Environmental monitoring is usually performed by personnel and thus requires operator intervention. As a result, environmental monitoring can both increase the risk of contamination and also give false-positive results. Thus, intensive monitoring is unwarranted, particularly in the ISO 5 environments that are used in the most critical zones of aseptic processing.

The numerical values for air, surface, and personnel monitoring included in this chapter are not intended to represent limits or specifications but are strictly informational. Because of the variety of microbiological sampling equipment and methods, it is not scientifically reasonable to suggest that the attainment of these values guarantees microbial control or that excursions beyond values in this chapter indicate a loss of control.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Aseptic vs Sterile

這是 USP ⟨1116⟩ 開宗明義最重要的區分：

  • Sterile（無菌） — 完全沒有活的微生物，是一個「狀態」的描述
  • Aseptic（無菌操作） — 是一個「過程」的描述，透過排除微生物來防止汙染

在實際製藥環境中，aseptic processing 不可能達到真正的 sterile 狀態 — 因為有人在場就一定會釋放微生物。這就是為什麼 USP 強調「零汙染期望在技術上不可行」。

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) Section 4.3 也明確表達相同觀點，並要求在 CCS（Contamination Control Strategy）中納入此風險認知。

比喻說明：手術室 vs 實驗室

想像一間手術室：即使使用最先進的空氣過濾系統和最嚴格的消毒程序，只要有外科醫師和護理人員在場，環境就不可能是「完全無菌」的。手術室的目標不是達到零微生物，而是將汙染風險控制在不會對病人造成感染的水準。

無菌充填室也一樣 — 目標是「風險可接受」而非「絕對無菌」。這個認知對設計 EM 計畫至關重要：我們監測的目的是確認「控制狀態」，不是「證明無菌」。

重點提示：EM 數值的法律定位

USP ⟨1116⟩ 是 Informational Chapter（資訊性章節），不同於 USP ⟨71⟩（Sterility Tests）這類有法律約束力的 Official Chapter。然而：

  • FDA 在 Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004) 中直接引用 ⟨1116⟩ 的概念
  • EU GMP Annex 1 Table 7/8 的 alert/action limits 與 ⟨1116⟩ 的理念一致
  • PDA TR13（Fundamentals of an EM Program）以 ⟨1116⟩ 為科學基礎
  • 每次 FDA/EMA 查廠，EM program 都是必查項目

所以實務上，雖然 ⟨1116⟩ 的數值不是「法定限值」，但你的 EM 計畫如果與其建議嚴重偏離，稽查員會要求你提出科學理據。

重點提示：監測本身的矛盾

USP 提出一個深刻的悖論：環境監測會增加汙染風險。因為：

  • 取樣需要人員進入潔淨區域（增加微生物釋放）
  • 取樣器材的放置和操作可能干擾氣流
  • 接觸取樣可能造成假陽性

所以 ⟨1116⟩ 明確表示「在 ISO 5 區域，密集監測是不必要的」— 這與很多人的直覺相反。在 CDMO 中，這是設計 EM 計畫時需要與客戶和 QA 團隊溝通的重要議題。

Advanced Aseptic Technologies — 先進無菌技術

原文內容 Original Text

Advanced aseptic technologies can be defined as those that do not rely on the direct intervention of human operators during processing. At present, technologies such as isolators, blow/fill/seal, and closed RABS (designs that are never opened during setup or operation) may be considered advanced aseptic technologies, provided that direct intervention by gowned personnel is disallowed during processing.

Isolators and closed RABS effectively separate the operator from the critical aseptic processing environment. Because these systems substantially reduce contamination risk, their microbiological control levels are higher than those of conventional clean rooms that have comparable particulate air classification level, for example, ISO 5.

Some users have chosen to operate RABS in a manner that allows open, direct human intervention. In an open operational state, these systems are more similar in operation to conventional clean rooms and therefore cannot be considered advanced aseptic processing systems.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：技術階層與汙染風險

USP 建立了一個清楚的無菌技術階層（從最低到最高汙染風險）：

  1. Isolator — 最佳分離，自動去汙染，人員完全隔離
  2. Closed RABS — 操作中不開啟，僅使用手套/半身衣操作
  3. Open RABS — 允許開放式人員介入，等同傳統潔淨室
  4. Conventional Cleanroom — 人員在 ISO 5 區域直接操作

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) Section 4.18–4.22 對 RABS 和 Isolator 有更詳細的定義，並明確要求新設施優先考慮 separative technologies。

比喻說明：廚房防蟲的不同策略

傳統潔淨室就像一個開放式廚房 — 再怎麼打掃、再怎麼穿乾淨制服，廚師身上還是會帶進灰塵和微生物。

Isolator 則像把食物放在密封的玻璃櫃裡 — 透過手套從外面操作。即使廚房不是 100% 乾淨，食物被汙染的風險大幅降低。

Open RABS 就像有門的櫃子 — 如果你偶爾把門打開伸手進去，它的保護效果就和沒有櫃子差不多了。

重點提示：CDMO 技術選型

對 CDMO 的直接影響：

  • 使用 Isolator/Closed RABS 的產線，EM 要求更嚴格（contamination recovery rate <0.1%），但因為人員隔離更好，實際汙染率極低
  • 傳統潔淨室的 EM 要求相對「寬鬆」（ISO 5 <1%），但實際操作更依賴人員紀律
  • FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance 鼓勵使用 isolator 技術
  • White Raven（歐洲 CDMO）的 SA25 filling machine 就是 Isolator-based 設計

Clean Room Classification for Aseptic Processing Environments — 無菌操作環境的潔淨室分級

原文內容 Original Text

The design and construction of clean rooms and controlled environments are covered in ISO 14644 series. This standard defines the performance of a clean environment with respect to the concentration of total particulates per unit volume.

Pharmaceutical manufacturers are concerned with nonviable particulate contamination in injectable products. Unlike microbial contamination in which experimental data suggest that humans are the only significant source, nonviable particulates can arise both from humans and from processing equipment.

The argument that if fewer total particulates are present in a clean room, it is less likely that airborne microorganisms will be present is true only if human operators are the source of particulate matter.

Specifications for air changes per hour and air velocities are not included in ISO 14644. Typically:

  • ISO 8 / Class 100,000: minimum 20 air changes per hour
  • ISO 7 / Class 10,000: more than 50 air changes per hour
  • ISO 5 / Class 100: more than 100 air changes per hour

Manufacturers should maintain a predominantly unidirectional flow of air (either vertical or horizontal) in a staffed Class 5 clean room environment, particularly when products, containers, and closures are exposed.

核心概念：粒子數 vs 微生物數

USP 提出一個經常被誤解的觀點：非活性粒子數與微生物汙染之間的關係不是直接的。

  • 人員是微生物汙染的唯一顯著來源
  • 設備是非活性粒子的另一個重要來源
  • 因此「粒子數低就表示微生物少」只在人員是粒子主要來源時才成立

這就是為什麼 EM 計畫必須同時包含非活性粒子監測和微生物監測兩個部分 — 它們提供不同的資訊。

比喻說明：換氣次數與排風扇

換氣次數（ACH）就像房間裡的排風扇功率：

  • ISO 8（20 ACH）— 普通浴室排風扇，慢慢帶走濕氣
  • ISO 7（50 ACH）— 油煙機，快速排除廚房油煙
  • ISO 5（100+ ACH）— 工業級排氣系統，幾乎是持續吹拂的清潔氣流

重點不只是換氣頻率，而是氣流方向 — ISO 5 區域需要 unidirectional flow（單向氣流），就像風從天花板往地板吹，把汙染物帶離產品表面。

Importance of a Microbiological Evaluation Program — 微生物評估計畫的重要性

原文內容 Original Text

Monitoring of total particulate count in controlled environments, even with the use of electronic instrumentation on a continuous basis, does not provide information on the microbiological content of the environment. The basic limitation of particulate counters is that they measure particles of 0.5 µm or larger. While airborne microorganisms are not free-floating or single cells, they frequently associate with particles of 10–20 µm.

Microbial monitoring cannot and need not identify and quantify all microbial contaminants in these controlled environments. Microbiological monitoring of a clean room is technically a semiquantitative exercise, because a truly quantitative evaluation of the environment is not possible, given the limitations in sampling equipment.

No microbiological sampling plan can prove the absence of microbial contamination, even when no viable contamination is recovered. The absence of growth on a microbiological sample means only that growth was not discovered; it does not mean that the environment is free of contamination.

Routine microbial monitoring should provide sufficient information to demonstrate that the aseptic processing environment is operating in an adequate state of control. The real value of a microbiological monitoring program lies in its ability to confirm consistent, high-quality environmental conditions at all times.

Environmental microbial monitoring and analysis of data by qualified personnel can assist in ensuring that a suitable state of control is maintained. The environment should be sampled during normal operations to allow the collection of meaningful, process-related data.

核心概念：EM 的真正價值

USP 用了大量篇幅解釋 EM 的限制和真正用途，這些觀點是理解整個 ⟨1116⟩ 的基石：

  • EM 是半定量的 — 我們無法精確量化環境中的微生物數量
  • 零回收不等於無汙染 — 只是低於偵測極限
  • EM 不能證明無菌 — 它只能確認「控制狀態」的一致性
  • 趨勢比單一結果更重要 — 監測的價值在於偵測變化

Cross-reference：PDA TR13 Section 3.0 和 PDA TR88 都強調相同的哲學 — EM 是 CCS 的一個「確認工具」，不是品質保證的替代品。

比喻說明：體溫計的隱喻

EM 就像量體溫 — 體溫正常不代表你沒有任何疾病（可能有病但還沒發燒），體溫升高也不一定代表你感染了（可能剛運動完）。

但如果你每天量體溫，連續三天都比平常高 0.5°C，這個「趨勢」就值得注意了。EM 的價值也在這裡 — 不是單一數據點，而是持續的模式和趨勢。

重點提示：取樣時機

USP 明確要求在正常生產操作期間取樣 — 材料在現場、製程進行中、所有人員在位。這與某些工廠在「最佳條件」下取樣（如人員最少、設備靜止時）的做法相矛盾。

對 CDMO 的啟示：如果你的 EM 數據都是在非生產狀態下取得的，FDA 查廠員可能會質疑這些數據是否反映真實的生產風險。

Sampling Airborne Microorganisms — 空氣微生物取樣

原文內容 Original Text

Among the most commonly used tools for monitoring aseptic environments are impaction and centrifugal samplers. A number of commercially available samplers are listed for informational purposes. The selection, appropriateness, and adequacy of using any particular sampler are the responsibility of the user.

Slit-to-Agar Air Sampler (STA)

The unit is powered by an attached source of controllable vacuum. The air intake is obtained through a standardized slit below which is placed a slowly revolving Petri dish that contains a nutrient agar. Airborne particles that have sufficient mass impact the agar surface, and viable organisms are allowed to grow. A remote air intake is often used to minimize disturbance of unidirectional airflow.

Sieve Impactor

This apparatus consists of a container designed to accommodate a Petri dish that contains a nutrient agar. The cover of the unit is perforated with openings of a predetermined size. A vacuum pump draws a known volume of air through the cover, and airborne particles that contain microorganisms impact the agar medium in the Petri dish. Some samplers feature a cascaded series of sieves that contain perforations of decreasing size, allowing determination of the size range distribution of particulates that contain viable microorganisms.

Centrifugal Sampler

The unit consists of a propeller or turbine that pulls a known volume of air into the unit and then propels the air outward to impact on a tangentially placed nutrient agar strip set on a flexible plastic base.

Sterilizable Microbiological Atrium

The unit is a variant of the single-stage sieve impactor. The unit's cover contains uniformly spaced orifices approximately 0.25 inch in size. The base of the unit accommodates one Petri dish containing a nutrient agar. A vacuum pump controls the movement of air through the unit, and a multiple-unit control center as well as a remote sampling probe are available.

Surface Air System Sampler (SAS)

This integrated unit consists of an entry section that accommodates an agar contact plate. Immediately behind the contact plate is a motor and turbine that pulls air through the unit's perforated cover over the agar contact plate and beyond the motor, where it is exhausted.

Gelatin Filter Sampler

The unit consists of a vacuum pump with an extension hose terminating in a filter holder that can be located remotely in the critical space. The filter consists of random fibers of gelatin capable of retaining airborne microorganisms. After a specified exposure time, the filter is aseptically removed and dissolved in an appropriate diluent and then plated on an appropriate agar medium to estimate its microbial content.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：主動式空氣取樣器分類

USP 列出六種主動式空氣取樣器，可以依照「收集原理」分為三大類：

  • 衝擊式（Impaction） — STA、Sieve Impactor、SAS：利用慣性將微生物衝擊到培養基表面
  • 離心式（Centrifugal） — Centrifugal Sampler：利用離心力將微粒甩向培養基
  • 過濾式（Filtration） — Gelatin Filter：將空氣通過明膠濾膜捕捉微生物

每種方法的回收效率不同，USP 特別強調「不應假設不同方法取相同體積的空氣會產生相似的回收率」。這對 CDMO 的 method validation 有重大意義。

比喻說明：捕魚的不同方法

想像你要估算一條河裡有多少魚。你可以用刺網（衝擊式 — 魚撞上去被捕獲）、旋轉水車（離心式 — 水流帶魚到收集區）、或篩網（過濾式 — 水通過但魚留下來）。

每種方法能捕到的魚種和數量不同 — 大魚容易被刺網捕到，小魚可能從刺網的洞逃走但被篩網攔住。所以用不同方法得到的「魚數」不能直接比較。空氣取樣也一樣：不同型號的取樣器對同一環境給出的 cfu 數據可能相差 5–10 倍。

重點提示：CDMO 實務選型考量

  • STA（Slit-to-Agar）：可提供時間分辨資訊（旋轉盤），適合追蹤汙染事件的時間點，但體積大不適合 Isolator 內部

Settling Plates — 沉降板

原文內容 Original Text

This method is still widely used as a simple and inexpensive way to qualitatively assess the environments over prolonged exposure times. Published data indicate that settling plates, when exposed for 4- to 5-hour periods, can provide a limit of detection for a suitable evaluation of the aseptic environment.

Settling plates may be particularly useful in critical areas where active sampling could be intrusive and a hazard to the aseptic operation.

One of the major drawbacks of mechanical air samplers is the limited sample size of air being tested. When the microbial level in the air of a controlled environment is expected to contain extremely low levels of contamination per unit volume, at least 1 cubic meter of air should be tested in order to maximize sensitivity. Typically, slit-to-agar devices have an 80-L/min sampling capacity. If 1 cubic meter of air were tested, then it would require an exposure time of 15 min.

It may be necessary to use sampling times in excess of 15 min to obtain a representative environmental sample. Although some samplers are reported to have high sampling volumes, consideration should be given to the potential for disruption of the airflow patterns in any critical area and to the creation of turbulence.

Technicians may wish to use remote sampling systems in order to minimize potential risks resulting from intervention by environmental samplers in critical zones. Regardless of the type of sampler used, analysts must determine that the extra tubing needed for a remote probe does not reduce the method's sensitivity to such an extent that detection of low levels of contamination becomes unlikely or even impossible.

核心概念：Settle Plates 的獨特價值

沉降板（Settle Plates）是最古老也最簡單的空氣微生物取樣方法，但 USP 特別指出它有一個關鍵優勢：

  • 非侵入性 — 不需要設備、不需要電源、不會干擾氣流
  • 長時間暴露 — 4–5 小時的連續監測，增加偵測低濃度汙染的機會
  • 在 ISO 5 critical zone 特別適用 — 因為主動式取樣器在此區域可能造成更多問題

Surface Sampling — 表面取樣

原文內容 Original Text

Another component of the microbial-control program in controlled environments is surface sampling of equipment, facilities, and personnel. The standardization of surface sampling methods and procedures has not been as widely addressed in the pharmaceutical industry as has the standardization of air-sampling procedures.

Contact plates filled with nutrient agar are used for sampling regular or flat surfaces and are directly incubated for the appropriate time and temperature for recovery of viable organisms. Specialized agar can be used for the recovery of organisms that have specific growth requirements. Microbial estimates are reported per contact plate.

The swabbing method can be used to supplement contact plates for sampling of irregular surfaces, especially irregular surfaces of equipment. The area that will be swabbed is defined with a sterile template of appropriate size. In general, it is in the range of 24–30 cm². After sample collection the swab is placed in an appropriate diluent or transport medium and is plated onto the desired nutrient agar. The microbial estimates are reported per swab of defined sampling area.

Surface monitoring is used as an environmental assessment tool in all types of classified environments. In ISO 5 environments for aseptic processing, surface monitoring is generally performed beside critical areas and surfaces. Component hoppers and feed chutes that contact sterile surfaces on closures and filling needles can be tested for microbial contamination.

Surface monitoring on surfaces that directly contact sterile parts or product should be done only after production operations are completed. Surface sampling is not a sterility test and should not be a criterion for the release or rejection of product. Because these samples must be taken aseptically by personnel, it is difficult to establish with certainty that any contamination recovered is product related.

Microbiological Control Parameters — 微生物控制參數

原文內容 Original Text

Since the early 1980s, manufacturers have established alert and action levels for environmental monitoring. In recent years the numerical difference between alert and action levels has become quite small, especially in ISO 5 environments.

Growth and recovery in microbiological assays have normal variability in the range of ±0.5 log10. Studies on active microbiological air samplers indicate that variability of as high as tenfold is possible among commonly used sampling devices.

"As a result of this inherent variability and indeterminate sampling error, the supposed differences between, for example, an alert level of 1 cfu and an action level of 3 cfu are not analytically significant. Treating differences that are within expected, and therefore, normal ranges as numerically different is not scientifically valid and can result in unwarranted activities."

In a practical sense, numerical values that vary by as much as five- to tenfold may not be significantly different. Because of the limited accuracy and precision of microbial growth and recovery assays, analysts can consider the frequency with which contamination is detected rather than absolute numbers of cfu detected in any single sample.

Mean contamination recovery rates should be determined for each clean room environment, and changes in contamination recovery rate at a given site or within a given room may indicate the need for corrective action. Within the ISO 5 critical zone, airborne and surface contamination recovery rates of 1% or less should be attainable with current methods.

Contamination recovery rates for closed RABS and isolator systems should be significantly lower still and can be expected to be <0.1%, on the basis of published monitoring results.

Contamination observed at multiple sites in an environment within a single sampling period may indicate increased risk to product and should be carefully evaluated. The appearance of contamination nearly simultaneously at multiple sites could also arise from poor sampling technique, so careful review is in order before drawing conclusions about potential loss of control.

Resampling an environment several days after contamination is of little value, because the conditions during one sampling occasion may not be accurately duplicated during another.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為何「頻率」比「數量」重要

這是 USP ⟨1116⟩ 最具革命性的觀點之一。傳統做法是設定 alert level（如 1 cfu）和 action level（如 3 cfu），然後根據單次取樣的 cfu 數來判斷。但 USP 指出：

  • 微生物檢測的固有變異性為 ±0.5 log（即 3–5 倍）
  • 不同取樣器之間的變異性可達 10 倍
  • 因此 1 cfu 和 3 cfu 之間的差異在分析上不具統計意義

USP 建議改用汙染回收率（Contamination Recovery Rate）— 也就是「有多少百分比的樣品檢出任何汙染」。這是一個基於頻率的指標，比單次 cfu 計數更有科學意義。

比喻說明：量體溫 vs 發燒頻率

傳統的 alert/action level 就像每次量體溫：37.0°C 是正常，37.5°C 是 alert，38.0°C 是 action。但如果溫度計本身的精確度只有 ±0.5°C，那 37.5°C 和 38.0°C 的讀數根本沒有統計上的區別。

USP 的做法更像是追蹤「這個月有幾天量到超過 37.5°C」— 如果以前每月只有 1 天超標，現在變成每月 5 天，那才是真正有意義的警訊。這就是 contamination recovery rate 的核心思維。

重點提示：USP vs Annex 1 的哲學差異

這裡存在一個 CDMO 必須理解的重要差異：

  • EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) — 仍然使用傳統的 alert/action levels（Table 7/8），以 cfu 數為基礎（Grade A: action = 1 cfu for air, settle plate, contact plate）
  • USP ⟨1116⟩ — 主張這些數值在科學上不夠嚴謹，應改用頻率導向的 contamination recovery rate

CDMO 的務實做法：兩套標準都必須遵循。保留 Annex 1 的 cfu-based limits 以滿足法規要求，同時疊加 USP 的 recovery rate trending 以提供更深層的科學洞察。

核心概念：Table 3 的正確解讀方式

Table 3 是 USP ⟨1116⟩ 最重要的表格之一。注意它的數值表達方式是百分比，不是 cfu：

  • ISO 5 的 <1% 意味著：每 100 個樣品中，不超過 1 個有任何微生物生長
  • 這是一個頻率指標，與 Annex 1 Table 7/8 的 cfu 限值是完全不同的概念
  • Isolator/Closed RABS 的 <0.1% 意味著每 1000 個樣品中不超過 1 個有生長

比喻說明：學校的出席率標準

Significant Excursions & Data Interpretation — 顯著偏差與數據解讀

原文內容 Original Text

Significant Excursions

Excursions beyond approximately 15 cfu recovered from a single ISO 5 sample, whether from airborne, surface, or personnel sources, should happen very infrequently. When such ISO 5 excursions do occur, they may be indicative of a significant loss of control when they occur within the ISO 5 critical zone in close proximity to product and components. Thus, any ISO 5 excursion >15 cfu should prompt a careful and thorough investigation.

A key consideration for an abnormally high number of recovered colonies is whether this incident is isolated or can be correlated with other recoveries. Microbiologists should review recovery rates for at least two weeks before the incident of abnormally high recovery so that they can be aware of other recoveries that might indicate an unusual pattern.

Microbiologists should carefully consider all recoveries, including those that are in the more typical range of 1–5 cfu. The identity of the organisms recovered is an important factor in the conduct of this investigation.

"It is never wise to suggest a root cause for which there is no solid scientific evidence. Also, there should be an awareness of the variability of microbial analysis. Realistically, there is no scientific reason to treat a recovery of 25 cfu as statistically different from a recovery of 15 cfu."

Further Considerations About Data Interpretation

In the high-quality environments required for aseptic processing, detection frequency typically is low. In the most critical areas within an aseptic processing operation, it is expected that less than 1% of the samples will yield any recoverable contamination.

The microbiologist responsible for environmental control or sterility assurance should not take this to mean that the environmental quality approaches sterility. The sensitivity of any microbial sampling system in absolute terms is not known. In environmental monitoring, a result of zero means only that the result is below the limit of detection of the analytical system.

Sterility assurance is best accomplished by a focus on human-borne contamination and the facility design features that best mitigate risk from this contamination. Greatest risk mitigation can be attained by reducing or eliminating human interventions through proper equipment design and by providing sufficient air exchanges per hour for the intended personnel population of the facility.

Environmental monitoring cannot prove or disprove in absolute terms the sterility of a lot of product. Environmental monitoring can only assure those responsible for a process that a production system is in a consistent, validated state of control.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：15 cfu 門檻 — 唯一的數字標準

USP ⟨1116⟩ 雖然全文都在說「不要過度依賴 cfu 數值」，但在這裡提出了一個例外 — 15 cfu 作為 ISO 5 環境的顯著偏差門檻。這是因為：

  • 在 ISO 5 環境中，正常回收通常是 0–5 cfu
  • 15 cfu 代表至少 3 倍以上的偏離（超出 ±0.5 log 的正常變異範圍）
  • 但 USP 同時提醒：15 cfu 和 25 cfu 在統計上可能沒有顯著差異

所以 15 cfu 不是一個精確的界線，而是一個「應該開始認真調查」的觸發點。

比喻說明：地震的「有感地震」門檻

15 cfu 門檻就像地震學中的「有感地震」定義。規模 3.0 以下的地震幾乎感覺不到（正常的 0–5 cfu），規模 4.0 的地震可以感覺到但通常無害（15 cfu — 需要注意但不一定有損害）。

你不會因為一次規模 4.0 的地震就關閉整座城市（拒收產品），但你應該檢查建築結構是否有損壞（調查 EM 系統是否失控）。同樣地，USP 提醒我們：規模 4.0 和 4.5 的地震在實際影響上可能沒有太大區別（15 cfu vs 25 cfu）。

重點提示：調查的科學誠實原則

USP 提出一個非常重要的科學倫理原則：不要在沒有證據的情況下臆測根本原因。這在 CDMO 的調查實務中極為重要：

  • 單一的、不相關的偏差事件 → 通常無法找到確切原因 — 這是可以接受的結論
  • 應回顧事件前至少兩週的數據，尋找模式或關聯
  • 微生物鑑定是調查的重要工具 — 菌種身分可以暗示汙染來源
  • 避免「為了關閉調查而編造原因」的做法 — FDA 對此非常敏感

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 Section 9.33–9.36 對 OOS/OOT 環境監測結果的調查也有類似要求，但更強調 batch-related impact assessment。

重點提示：EM 的真正角色定位

USP 在這裡做了一個極為清晰的總結：

  • EM 不能證明或否定任何一批產品的無菌性
  • EM 能做的是確認生產系統處於一致的、經驗證的受控狀態
  • 真正的無菌保證來自設施設計（減少人員介入）和製程控制（validated processes）
  • EM 結果為零只代表「低於偵測極限」，不代表環境無菌

這個定位對 CDMO 尤其重要：當客戶要求用 EM 數據來「證明」產品無菌性時，你需要能夠清晰地解釋 EM 的科學定位和限制。

Personnel as the Primary Contamination Source — 人員：主要汙染源

原文內容 Original Text

The only significant sources of microbial contamination in aseptic environments are the personnel. Because operators disperse contamination and because the ultimate objective in aseptic processing is to reduce end-user risk, only healthy individuals should be permitted access to controlled environments.

Individuals who are ill must not be allowed to enter an aseptic processing environment, even one that employs advanced aseptic technologies such as isolators, blow/fill/seal, or closed RABS.

The importance of good personal hygiene and a careful attention to detail in aseptic gowning cannot be overemphasized. Gowning requirements differ depending on the use of the controlled environment and the specifics of the gowning system itself. Aseptic processing environments require the use of sterilized gowns with the best available filtration properties.

The fullest possible skin coverage is desirable, and sleeve covers or tape should be considered to minimize leaks at the critical glove–sleeve junction. Exposed skin should never be visible in conventional clean rooms under any conditions.

Once employees are properly gowned, they must be careful to maintain the integrity of their gloves, masks, and other gown materials at all times. Operators who work with isolator systems are not required to wear sterilized clean-room gowns, but inadequate aseptic technique and employee-borne contamination are the principal hazards to safe aseptic operations in isolators, as well as RABS, and in conventional clean rooms.

Glove-and-sleeve assemblies can develop leaks that can allow the mechanical transfer of microorganisms to the product. A second glove, worn either under or over the primary isolator/RABS glove, can provide an additional level of safety against glove leaks or can act as a hygienic measure.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：人員是唯一顯著汙染源

USP ⟨1116⟩ 做了一個非常明確的聲明：在無菌環境中，人是唯一顯著的微生物來源。這個觀點直接影響整個 EM 計畫的設計邏輯：

  • 取樣地點 → 應聚焦於人員活動區域和人員接觸的表面
  • 取樣頻率 → 人員密度越高的區域，取樣頻率應越高
  • 矯正行動 → 當汙染增加時，首先審查人員操作和更衣
  • 風險降低 → 最有效的方法就是減少人員介入（Isolator/RABS）

比喻說明：潔淨室裡的「人型蒲公英」

想像每位操作員都是一朵會走路的蒲公英 — 不管穿了多少層防護衣，每走一步、每個動作都會釋放看不見的「種子」（微生物和微粒）。USP 的研究顯示，即使穿著正確的無菌衣，操作員仍然會持續地向環境釋放微生物。

穿衣技術好壞就像蒲公英外面套了不同密度的塑膠袋 — 好的無菌衣（高過濾效率）可以攔住大部分種子，但不可能做到 100%。手套和袖口的接合處就像袋子的開口 — 那裡是最容易「漏種子」的地方。

重點提示：手套完整性 — CDMO 的關鍵痛點

USP 特別提到 glove-and-sleeve assemblies 的洩漏風險。在 CDMO 實務中：

  • Isolator 手套 — 必須定期做完整性測試（glove integrity testing），Annex 1 Section 4.20 要求每次使用前和使用後都要測試
  • 雙層手套策略 — USP 建議的「第二層手套」在許多 CDMO 已是標準做法
  • 手套更換頻率 — 傳統潔淨室中，手套應在每次介入（intervention）後消毒或更換
  • 袖口密封 — 袖套或膠帶（sleeve covers or tape）密封手套–袖口接合處是最佳實務

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 Section 7.14 對更衣有非常詳細的要求，包括 visual inspection 和 periodic requalification。

Training of Personnel — 人員教育訓練

原文內容 Original Text

Training of all personnel working in controlled environments is critical. This training is equally important for personnel responsible for the microbial monitoring program, because contamination of the clean working area could inadvertently occur during microbial sampling.

In highly automated operations, monitoring personnel may be the employees who have the most direct contact with the critical surfaces and zones within the processing area. Microbiological sampling has the potential to contribute to microbial contamination caused by inappropriate sampling techniques or by placing personnel in or near the critical zone.

A formal training program is required to minimize this risk. This training should be documented for all personnel who enter controlled environments. Interventions should always be minimized, including those required for monitoring activities; but when interventions cannot be avoided, they must be conducted with aseptic technique that approaches perfection as closely as possible.

Management of the facility must ensure that personnel involved in operations in clean rooms and advanced aseptic processing environments are well versed in relevant microbiological principles. The training should include:

  • Basic principles of aseptic technique and the relationship of manufacturing and handling procedures to potential sources of product contamination
  • Basic principles of microbiology, microbial physiology, disinfection and sanitation, media selection and preparation, taxonomy, and sterilization (for supervisors and auditors)
  • Knowledge of applicable standard operating procedures, especially those relating to corrective measures
  • Understanding of contamination control principles and individual responsibilities with respect to GMPs
  • Training in conducting investigations and in analyzing data

核心概念：EM 人員的悖論角色

USP 指出一個深刻的悖論：在高度自動化的生產線中，EM 取樣人員反而可能是與關鍵區域接觸最多的人。這意味著：

  • 取樣人員的無菌技術要求不亞於（甚至高於）生產操作員

Investigation of Excursions & Corrective Actions — 偏差調查與矯正行動

原文內容 Original Text

When contamination recovery rates increase from an established norm, process and operational investigation should take place. Investigations will differ depending on the type and processing of the product manufactured.

Investigation should include a review of:

  • Area maintenance documentation
  • Sanitization/decontamination documentation
  • The occurrence of nonroutine events
  • Inherent physical or operational parameters, such as changes in environmental temperature and relative humidity
  • The training status of personnel

In closed RABS and isolator systems, the loss of glove integrity or the accidental introduction of material that has not been decontaminated are among the most probable causes of detectable microbial contamination.

Following the investigation, actions should be taken to correct or eliminate the most probable causes of contamination. Because of the relative rarity of contamination events in modern facilities, the investigation often proves inconclusive.

When corrective actions are undertaken, they may include:

  • Reinforcement of personnel training to emphasize acceptable gowning and aseptic techniques
  • Additional microbiological sampling at increased frequency (but this may not be appropriate during aseptic processing because intrusive or overly intensive sampling may entail an increased contamination risk)
  • Additional sanitization, use of different sanitizing agents
  • Identification of the microbial contaminant and its possible source
"In any aseptic environment, conventional or advanced, the investigation and the rationale for the course of action chosen as a result of the investigation must be carefully and comprehensively documented."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：調查的觸發點與流程

USP 定義了調查的觸發條件和系統化流程：

  • 觸發點 — 汙染回收率偏離已建立的基線（不是單次偏差）
  • 調查範圍 — 五大領域同步審查（設備維護、消毒、非例行事件、環境參數、人員訓練）
  • 結論 — 可以是「inconclusive」— 這是科學上可接受的
  • 文件 — 調查過程和理由必須「carefully and comprehensively documented」

比喻說明：偵探辦案的科學態度

EM 偏差調查就像偵探辦案。當犯罪率（recovery rate）從基線上升時，你開始調查。你審查監視器錄影（維護記錄）、檢查門鎖（消毒記錄）、詢問鄰居（非例行事件）、檢查天氣變化（溫濕度）、審查警衛名單（人員訓練）。

有時候你能找到犯人（根本原因），但很多時候案件會成為 cold case（inconclusive）。USP 的重點是：說「我們找不到原因」比「我們猜測原因是 X」更有科學誠信。好的偵探不會為了結案率而冤枉無辜的嫌疑人。

重點提示：矯正行動中的悖論

USP 指出一個容易被忽略的矛盾：

  • 當偏差發生時，常見的反射性反應是「增加取樣頻率」
  • 但 USP 警告：在無菌操作過程中增加取樣 → 可能增加汙染風險
  • 更明智的做法是：在下一次 process simulation（media fill）期間進行額外監測
  • 或者將額外監測安排在非生產時段

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 Section 9.35 要求偏差調查必須評估對受影響批次的影響（batch impact assessment），這比 USP 的要求更嚴格。PDA TR13 Section 12 有完整的調查決策樹可供參考。

Establishment of Sampling Plan and Sites — 取樣計畫與地點建立

原文內容 Original Text

During initial startup or commissioning of a clean room or other controlled environment, specific locations for air and surface sampling should be determined. Locations considered should include those in proximity of the exposed product, containers, closures, and product contact surfaces.

In aseptic processing, the area in which containers, closures, and product are exposed to the environment is often called the critical zone — the critical zone is always ISO 5. For aseptic operations the entire critical zone should be treated as a sterile field.

A nonsterile object, including the gloved hands of clean room personnel or an RABS/isolator glove, should never be brought into contact with a sterile product, container closure, filling station, or conveying equipment before or during aseptic processing operations.

The frequency of sampling depends on the manufacturing process conducted within an environment. The user should conduct a prospective risk analysis and develop a rationale for the sampling locations and frequencies for each controlled environment.

The classification of a clean room helps establish control levels, but that does not imply that all rooms of the same classification should have the same control levels and the same frequency of monitoring.

"Oversampling can be as deleterious to contamination control as undersampling, and careful consideration of risk and reduction of contamination sources can guide the sampling intensity."

比喻說明：安保等級的差異化

想像兩棟銀行大樓。第一棟是開放式櫃台（傳統潔淨室），客人可以直接走到櫃員面前 — 你需要每個窗口都裝監視器，每班都有警衛巡邏。第二棟是全自動 ATM 大廳（Isolator），人員被厚重的防彈玻璃隔開 — 你仍然需要監控，但頻率可以大幅降低，因為搶劫風險本身就小得多。

USP 的觀點是：把第一棟銀行的安保規格原封不動搬到第二棟，不但浪費資源，而且警衛的頻繁巡邏反而可能打擾 ATM 的正常運作（取樣干擾氣流和操作）。

重點提示：「過度取樣」的風險

USP 直接引用的一句話值得每位 CDMO 品質主管銘記：「過度取樣對汙染控制的危害與取樣不足一樣大」。具體來說：

  • 每次取樣都需要人員進入或靠近 critical zone → 增加汙染風險
  • 取樣設備本身可能干擾氣流模式
  • 過多的數據點可能產生更多的 false positives → 觸發不必要的調查
  • 調查本身消耗大量 QA 資源，可能分散對真正風險的注意力

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 Section 9.21 要求 EM plan 基於 risk assessment，但 Table 7/8 給出的具體 limits 通常推動較高頻率的取樣。CDMO 需要在兩種哲學之間找到平衡。

Selection of Sample Sites — 取樣地點選擇

原文內容 Original Text

Microbiological sampling sites are best selected with consideration of human activity during manufacturing operations. Careful observation and mapping of the clean room during the qualification phase can provide useful information concerning the movement and positioning of personnel.

The location and movement of personnel within the clean room correlate with contamination risk to the environment. Sample sites should be selected so that they evaluate the impact of personnel movement and work within the area, particularly interventions and manipulations within the critical zone.

The most likely route of contamination is airborne, so the samples most critical to risk assessment are those that relate to airborne contamination near exposed sterile materials.

Other areas of concern are entry points where equipment and materials move from areas of lower classification to those of higher classification. Areas within and around doors and airlocks should be included in the monitoring scheme.

It is customary to sample walls and floors, and indeed sampling at these locations can provide information about the effectiveness of the sanitization program. Sampling at these locations can take place relatively infrequently, because contamination there is unlikely to affect product. Operators should never touch floors and walls, so mechanical transmission of contamination from these surfaces to critical areas where product is exposed should not occur.

On the basis of long-term observations, manufacturers may increase or decrease sampling at a given location or eliminate a sampling location altogether.

核心概念：人員活動導向的取樣策略

USP 明確拒絕「均勻網格」取樣法（那是 ISO 14644 用於粒子分級的方法）。對微生物取樣，USP 要求基於人員活動模式來選點：

  • 最高優先 — 人員在 critical zone 進行介入操作的位置（如填充站、加塞站）
  • 高優先 — 人員出入口、氣閘、物料傳遞窗口
  • 中優先 — 人員經常走動的路徑
  • 低優先 — 牆壁和地板（僅用於評估消毒效果）

在 CDMO 的 qualification 階段，應進行人員動態觀察（personnel mapping），記錄操作員的移動路線和停留點，以此為 EM 取樣點選擇提供科學依據。

原文內容 Original Text

Selection of Growth Media

A general microbiological growth medium such as soybean–casein digest medium (SCDM) is suitable for environmental monitoring in most cases because it supports the growth of a wide range of bacteria, yeast, and molds. This medium can be supplemented with additives to overcome or to minimize the effects of sanitizing agents or of antibiotics.

Manufacturers should consider the specific detection of yeasts and molds. If necessary, general mycological media such as Sabouraud's, modified Sabouraud's, or inhibitory mold agar can be used.

In general, monitoring for strict anaerobes is not performed, because these organisms are unlikely to survive in ambient air. However, micro-aerophilic organisms may be observed in aseptic processing.

Selection of Culture Conditions

Typically, for general microbiological growth media such as SCDM, incubation temperatures in the ranges of approximately 20°–35° have been used with an incubation time of not less than 72 hours. Longer incubation times may be considered when contaminants are known to be slow growing.

For many mesophilic organisms, recovery is possible over a range of approximately 20°. In the absence of confirmatory evidence, microbiologists may incubate a single plate at both a low and a higher temperature. Incubating at the lower temperature first may compromise the recovery of Gram-positive cocci that are important because they are often associated with humans.

Contamination should not be introduced into a manufacturing clean room as a result of using contaminated sampling media or equipment. Wherever possible, sampling media and their wrappings should be terminally sterilized by moist heat, radiation, or other suitable means.

核心概念：培養基與培養條件的科學考量

USP 對培養條件的建議看似簡單，但暗含幾個關鍵的科學考量：

  • SCDM（TSA）是首選通用培養基 — 因為它能回收細菌、酵母菌和黴菌
  • Sabouraud's agar用於專門偵測真菌 — 在某些設施中可能需要併行使用
  • 培養溫度的選擇影響回收的菌種：低溫（20–25°C）有利於黴菌，高溫（30–35°C）有利於細菌
  • 培養時間至少 72 小時，但遲緩生長菌可能需要更久

重要警示：先在低溫培養可能損害人員來源的 Gram-positive cocci（如 Staphylococcus spp.）的回收 — 這對人員是主要汙染源的環境非常重要。

比喻說明：釣魚的餌料選擇

培養基就像釣魚的餌料。TSA/SCDM 是「萬用餌」— 大部分魚都能釣到，但不是每種魚都會上鉤。Sabouraud's agar 就像「活蟲餌」— 專門吸引特定種類的魚（黴菌）。

培養溫度就像你決定在深水還是淺水釣魚。如果你先在淺水（低溫）放一段時間再拿到深水（高溫），某些深水魚（Gram-positive cocci）可能因為一開始的環境不適而死掉了。所以選擇培養條件的順序很重要。

重點提示：培養基本身的汙染風險

USP 特別強調一個容易被忽視的風險：取樣培養基本身可能帶入汙染。CDMO 必須確保：

  • 培養基優先使用最終滅菌（moist heat 或 radiation） — 避免使用無菌操作製備的培養基
  • 如果必須使用無菌製備的培養基 → 必須進行 pre-incubation 和 visual inspection
  • 培養基的包裝和運送方式也需要管控
  • 培養基供應商需要定期 qualification

Conclusion — 結論

原文內容 Original Text

Environmental monitoring is one of several key elements required in order to ensure that an aseptic processing area is maintained in an adequate level of control. Monitoring is a qualitative exercise, and even in the most critical applications such as aseptic processing, conclusions regarding lot acceptability should not be made on the basis of environmental sampling results alone.

Environments that are essentially free of human operators generally have low initial contamination rates and maintain low levels of microbial contamination. Human-scale clean rooms present a very different picture.

"Studies conclusively show that operators, even when carefully and correctly gowned, continuously slough microorganisms into the environment. Therefore, it is unreasonable to assume that samples producing no colonies, even in the critical zone or on critical surfaces, will always be observed."

Periodic excursions are a fact of life in human-scale clean rooms, but the contamination recovery rate, particularly in ISO 5 environments used for aseptic processing, should be consistently low.

Clean-room operators must strive to maintain suitable environmental quality and must work toward continuous improvement of personnel operations and environmental control. In general, fewer personnel involved in aseptic processing and monitoring, along with reduction in interventions, reduces risk from microbial contamination.

核心概念：USP ⟨1116⟩ 的五大核心訊息總結

整份 USP ⟨1116⟩ 可以濃縮為五個核心訊息：

  1. 人是唯一顯著汙染源 — EM 計畫應聚焦於人員活動
  2. EM 是定性工具 — 無法證明無菌，只能確認控制狀態
  3. 頻率比數量重要 — 用 contamination recovery rate 而非單次 cfu
  4. 過度監測有害 — 取樣本身增加汙染風險
  5. 持續改進 — 減少人員、減少介入 = 減少風險

比喻說明：城市的安全管理哲學

USP ⟨1116⟩ 的結論就像一位智慧的市長總結城市安全：

「我們城市的犯罪率不可能為零（periodic excursions are a fact of life），因為只要有人就有風險。但我們可以透過：減少夜間人流（fewer personnel）、減少不必要的巡邏干擾（reduction in interventions）、安裝更好的防盜系統（advanced aseptic technologies）來持續降低犯罪率。」

「我們的監控攝影系統（EM program）不能 100% 防止犯罪（cannot prove sterility），但它能告訴我們城市是否處於正常的安全狀態（validated state of control）。」

作為 CDMO 的營運主管，USP ⟨1116⟩ 給你的最大啟示是：

  • 投資技術升級 — Isolator/RABS 不只是降低汙染風險，還減少 EM 負擔（Table 2 的頻率差異）
  • 投資人員培訓 — 因為人是唯一顯著汙染源，培訓的 ROI 最高
  • 科學化 EM 計畫 — 用 recovery rate trending 取代單純的 pass/fail，提供更深入的品質洞察
  • 風險導向思維 — 取樣計畫應 tailored to facility，不是 copy-paste 其他公司的 SOP
  • 與客戶和稽查機構的溝通 — 能夠解釋 EM 的科學定位和限制，比盲目增加取樣更有說服力
