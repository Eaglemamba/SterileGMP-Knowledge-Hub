Personnel as the Primary Contamination Source — 人員：主要汙染源

原文內容 Original Text

The only significant sources of microbial contamination in aseptic environments are the personnel. Because operators disperse contamination and because the ultimate objective in aseptic processing is to reduce end-user risk, only healthy individuals should be permitted access to controlled environments.

Individuals who are ill must not be allowed to enter an aseptic processing environment, even one that employs advanced aseptic technologies such as isolators, blow/fill/seal, or closed RABS.

The importance of good personal hygiene and a careful attention to detail in aseptic gowning cannot be overemphasized. Gowning requirements differ depending on the use of the controlled environment and the specifics of the gowning system itself. Aseptic processing environments require the use of sterilized gowns with the best available filtration properties.

The fullest possible skin coverage is desirable, and sleeve covers or tape should be considered to minimize leaks at the critical glove–sleeve junction. Exposed skin should never be visible in conventional clean rooms under any conditions.

Once employees are properly gowned, they must be careful to maintain the integrity of their gloves, masks, and other gown materials at all times. Operators who work with isolator systems are not required to wear sterilized clean-room gowns, but inadequate aseptic technique and employee-borne contamination are the principal hazards to safe aseptic operations in isolators, as well as RABS, and in conventional clean rooms.

Glove-and-sleeve assemblies can develop leaks that can allow the mechanical transfer of microorganisms to the product. A second glove, worn either under or over the primary isolator/RABS glove, can provide an additional level of safety against glove leaks or can act as a hygienic measure.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：人員是唯一顯著汙染源

USP 〈1116〉 做了一個非常明確的聲明：在無菌環境中，人是唯一顯著的微生物來源。這個觀點直接影響整個 EM 計畫的設計邏輯：

  • 取樣地點 → 應聚焦於人員活動區域和人員接觸的表面
  • 取樣頻率 → 人員密度越高的區域，取樣頻率應越高
  • 矯正行動 → 當汙染增加時，首先審查人員操作和更衣
  • 風險降低 → 最有效的方法就是減少人員介入（Isolator/RABS）

比喻說明：潔淨室裡的「人型蒲公英」

想像每位操作員都是一朵會走路的蒲公英 — 不管穿了多少層防護衣，每走一步、每個動作都會釋放看不見的「種子」（微生物和微粒）。USP 的研究顯示，即使穿著正確的無菌衣，操作員仍然會持續地向環境釋放微生物。

穿衣技術好壞就像蒲公英外面套了不同密度的塑膠袋 — 好的無菌衣（高過濾效率）可以攔住大部分種子，但不可能做到 100%。手套和袖口的接合處就像袋子的開口 — 那裡是最容易「漏種子」的地方。

重點提示：手套完整性 — CDMO 的關鍵痛點

USP 特別提到 glove-and-sleeve assemblies 的洩漏風險。在 CDMO 實務中：

  • Isolator 手套 — 必須定期做完整性測試（glove integrity testing），Annex 1 Section 4.20 要求每次使用前和使用後都要測試
  • 雙層手套策略 — USP 建議的「第二層手套」在許多 CDMO 已是標準做法
  • 手套更換頻率 — 傳統潔淨室中，手套應在每次介入（intervention）後消毒或更換
  • 袖口密封 — 袖套或膠帶（sleeve covers or tape）密封手套–袖口接合處是最佳實務

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 Section 7.14 對更衣有非常詳細的要求，包括 visual inspection 和 periodic requalification。

Training of Personnel — 人員教育訓練

原文內容 Original Text

Training of all personnel working in controlled environments is critical. This training is equally important for personnel responsible for the microbial monitoring program, because contamination of the clean working area could inadvertently occur during microbial sampling.

In highly automated operations, monitoring personnel may be the employees who have the most direct contact with the critical surfaces and zones within the processing area. Microbiological sampling has the potential to contribute to microbial contamination caused by inappropriate sampling techniques or by placing personnel in or near the critical zone.

A formal training program is required to minimize this risk. This training should be documented for all personnel who enter controlled environments. Interventions should always be minimized, including those required for monitoring activities; but when interventions cannot be avoided, they must be conducted with aseptic technique that approaches perfection as closely as possible.

Management of the facility must ensure that personnel involved in operations in clean rooms and advanced aseptic processing environments are well versed in relevant microbiological principles. The training should include:

  • Basic principles of aseptic technique and the relationship of manufacturing and handling procedures to potential sources of product contamination
  • Basic principles of microbiology, microbial physiology, disinfection and sanitation, media selection and preparation, taxonomy, and sterilization (for supervisors and auditors)
  • Knowledge of applicable standard operating procedures, especially those relating to corrective measures
  • Understanding of contamination control principles and individual responsibilities with respect to GMPs
  • Training in conducting investigations and in analyzing data

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：EM 人員的悖論角色

USP 指出一個深刻的悖論：在高度自動化的生產線中，EM 取樣人員反而可能是與關鍵區域接觸最多的人。這意味著：

  • 取樣人員的無菌技術要求不亞於（甚至高於）生產操作員
  • 取樣行為本身就是一種「介入」（intervention），必須被管理
  • 過度取樣會增加汙染風險 — 這就是為什麼 USP 反對「intensive monitoring」

USP 要求取樣人員的無菌技術必須「approaches perfection as closely as possible」— 這是整份文件中用詞最強烈的要求之一。

比喻說明：醫院感控的查房悖論

感控護理師每天巡房檢查感染控制 — 但如果她自己沒有做好手部衛生，她的巡房反而可能在不同病房之間傳播感染。同樣地，EM 取樣人員如果技術不好，他們進入 ISO 5 區域取樣的行為本身就可能造成汙染，而且這些汙染還會被記錄為「環境汙染」，導致錯誤的調查方向。

重點提示：CDMO 培訓計畫的分層設計

USP 暗示了一個分層培訓架構，CDMO 應據此設計培訓矩陣：

人員層級培訓重點評估方式

生產操作員無菌技術、介入管理、汙染源認知Media fill 參與 + 手指尖取樣
EM 取樣人員取樣技術、培養基處理、數據記錄、無菌技術模擬取樣考核 + aseptic technique 評估

Investigation of Excursions & Corrective Actions — 偏差調查與矯正行動

原文內容 Original Text

When contamination recovery rates increase from an established norm, process and operational investigation should take place. Investigations will differ depending on the type and processing of the product manufactured.

Investigation should include a review of:

  • Area maintenance documentation
  • Sanitization/decontamination documentation
  • The occurrence of nonroutine events
  • Inherent physical or operational parameters, such as changes in environmental temperature and relative humidity
  • The training status of personnel

In closed RABS and isolator systems, the loss of glove integrity or the accidental introduction of material that has not been decontaminated are among the most probable causes of detectable microbial contamination.

Following the investigation, actions should be taken to correct or eliminate the most probable causes of contamination. Because of the relative rarity of contamination events in modern facilities, the investigation often proves inconclusive.

When corrective actions are undertaken, they may include:

  • Reinforcement of personnel training to emphasize acceptable gowning and aseptic techniques
  • Additional microbiological sampling at increased frequency (but this may not be appropriate during aseptic processing because intrusive or overly intensive sampling may entail an increased contamination risk)
  • Additional sanitization, use of different sanitizing agents
  • Identification of the microbial contaminant and its possible source
"In any aseptic environment, conventional or advanced, the investigation and the rationale for the course of action chosen as a result of the investigation must be carefully and comprehensively documented."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：調查的觸發點與流程

USP 定義了調查的觸發條件和系統化流程：

  • 觸發點 — 汙染回收率偏離已建立的基線（不是單次偏差）
  • 調查範圍 — 五大領域同步審查（設備維護、消毒、非例行事件、環境參數、人員訓練）
  • 結論 — 可以是「inconclusive」— 這是科學上可接受的
  • 文件 — 調查過程和理由必須「carefully and comprehensively documented」

比喻說明：偵探辦案的科學態度

EM 偏差調查就像偵探辦案。當犯罪率（recovery rate）從基線上升時，你開始調查。你審查監視器錄影（維護記錄）、檢查門鎖（消毒記錄）、詢問鄰居（非例行事件）、檢查天氣變化（溫濕度）、審查警衛名單（人員訓練）。

有時候你能找到犯人（根本原因），但很多時候案件會成為 cold case（inconclusive）。USP 的重點是：說「我們找不到原因」比「我們猜測原因是 X」更有科學誠信。好的偵探不會為了結案率而冤枉無辜的嫌疑人。

重點提示：矯正行動中的悖論

USP 指出一個容易被忽略的矛盾：

  • 當偏差發生時，常見的反射性反應是「增加取樣頻率」
  • 但 USP 警告：在無菌操作過程中增加取樣 → 可能增加汙染風險
  • 更明智的做法是：在下一次 process simulation（media fill）期間進行額外監測
  • 或者將額外監測安排在非生產時段

Cross-reference：EU GMP Annex 1 Section 9.35 要求偏差調查必須評估對受影響批次的影響（batch impact assessment），這比 USP 的要求更嚴格。PDA TR13 Section 12 有完整的調查決策樹可供參考。
