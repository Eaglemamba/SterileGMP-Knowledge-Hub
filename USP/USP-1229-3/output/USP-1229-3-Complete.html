Introduction — 緒論

Original Text 原文內容

Monitoring of in-process bioburden of pharmaceutical components and products is an essential element of the overall contamination-control program for appropriate sterilization process control. Bioburden monitoring should be designed for the recovery of a broad range of microorganisms that are likely to be present in the material being processed. Sterilization processes are implemented in order to eliminate bioburden in materials and the products, ensuring both adequate process control and end-user safety.

Bioburden is a potential risk to the patient not only because the sterilization process might not be completely effective, but also post-processing because of the possible presence of residual materials such as allergens, endotoxins, and exotoxins. It may also have adverse impact on product quality and stability. Therefore, although bioburden may be confidently killed by destructive sterilization processes or removed by retentive processes (filtration), as summarized in the ensuing sections, its proliferation before sterilization should be avoided.

Process controls and cleaning, sanitization, and disinfection programs provide active means for the control of bioburden population and support the sampling, enumeration, and characterization of bioburden necessary to ensure that sterilization processes are effective.

"Monitoring of in-process bioburden of pharmaceutical components and products is an essential element of the overall contamination-control program for appropriate sterilization process control."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Bioburden（生物負載）：指產品、原料或製程中間品在滅菌前所含有的活微生物總量。這不是一個理論數字，而是透過實際取樣和培養方法回收到的可培養微生物數量。

  • 為何監測？ — 滅菌製程的設計必須基於「你知道要殺死什麼」。如果不了解滅菌前的微生物負載，就無法證明滅菌製程有足夠的安全餘裕
  • 殘留風險 — 即使微生物被殺死，其殘留物（內毒素、外毒素、過敏原）仍可能危害患者。因此控制滅菌前的生物負載水平同樣重要

重點提示 CDMO 實務觀點

生物負載是滅菌驗證的輸入參數：在 CDMO 的無菌充填製程中，pre-sterilization bioburden 數據直接影響滅菌週期的設計。例如，濕熱滅菌的 F0 值計算、除菌過濾的挑戰測試設計，都需要以實際生物負載數據作為基礎。

三層防護邏輯：USP 1229.3 的核心邏輯是 — (1) 控制生物負載不要增殖、(2) 監測並了解生物負載的量和種類、(3) 設計滅菌製程來消除它。這三層缺一不可。

Destructive Processes — 破壞性滅菌製程

Original Text 原文內容

Destructive sterilization processes, e.g., moist and dry heat sterilization, are developed and validated to kill microorganisms. It is critically important to the quality control aspects of the sterilization program to fully understand the bioburden. The microorganisms that are most resistant to widely used destructive processes are spores of certain Gram-positive bacteria (note that mold spores are less resistant to these destructive processes than are bacterial spores). As a result of the resistance properties of these organisms, some species are commonly used as biological indicators for the evaluation of sterilization process performance. In their vegetative state, microorganisms that form spores do not exhibit extraordinary resistance to sterilization processes.

Processes that rely on ionizing radiation are an exception to typical resistance profiles because some non-spore-forming microorganisms exhibit higher resistance than do spore formers. Bioburden within materials and products subjected to radiation processes is evaluated as part of dose setting activities and is explained in the Radiation section that follows.

The sterilization process, the development of a thorough understanding of the process, and its microbiological impact are major elements in confirming that an acceptable level of sterility assurance is established. Understanding the inactivation kinetics of the typical bioburden as well as other potential bioburden microorganisms is critical to successful implementation. The new process should have a documented risk assessment outlining its monitoring and control aspects. The establishment of a new sterilization process requires the evaluation of the suggested biological indicator resistance (1,2).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

破壞性滅菌（Destructive Sterilization）：透過物理或化學手段直接殺死微生物的滅菌方式，包括：

  • 濕熱滅菌（Moist Heat） — 高壓蒸汽滅菌，最常見的終端滅菌方式
  • 乾熱滅菌（Dry Heat） — 除熱原（Depyrogenation）常用，如隧道式烘箱
  • 輻射滅菌（Radiation） — 主要用於醫療器材，藥品較少使用

Biological Indicator（生物指示劑，BI）：含有已知數量、已知抗性的微生物孢子的標準品，用於驗證滅菌製程的殺菌效力。常見的 BI 包括：

  • Geobacillus stearothermophilus — 用於濕熱滅菌驗證
  • Bacillus atrophaeus — 用於乾熱滅菌和 EtO 滅菌驗證

比喻說明 Analogy

消防演習的邏輯：生物指示劑就像消防演習中的「模擬火災」— 你用已知難度的挑戰來測試你的消防系統（滅菌製程）是否足夠強大。如果連最難撲滅的火（最耐熱的孢子）都能被成功消滅，那日常的火源（一般生物負載）自然也不成問題。

重點提示 Key Notes

輻射滅菌的例外：一般規則是「細菌孢子最耐滅菌」，但輻射滅菌是例外。某些非孢子形成菌（如 Deinococcus radiodurans）對輻射的抗性反而高於孢子形成菌。這就是為什麼輻射滅菌的劑量設定必須基於實際產品的生物負載數據，而非僅依賴 BI。

風險評估要求：原文強調新滅菌製程需要文件化的風險評估（Documented Risk Assessment）。在 CDMO 環境中，這通常意味著需要一份正式的 FMEA 或類似文件，涵蓋監測和控制的各個面向。

Retentive Processes — 截留性滅菌製程

Original Text 原文內容

The microorganisms least likely to be retained by a sterilizing filtration process are those that are potentially smaller than the smallest pores in the filter matrix. Although size exclusion is an important factor, filters do not retain microorganisms solely by sieve retention. Adsorption, wherein microorganisms are retained within the filter matrix by entrapment or electrostatic forces, also is an important retention mechanism. The control of prefiltration bioburden is an important risk-mitigation factor in retentive processes, and this is particularly true when adsorption may be a significant retention mechanism. Bioburden removal capability therefore depends on the size and number of the bioburden microorganisms, the pore size distribution of the filter, the properties of the solution being filtered, and the filtration process parameters.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

截留性滅菌（Retentive Processes）：即除菌過濾（Sterilizing Filtration），透過物理截留方式從液體中去除微生物，而非殺死它們。

兩種截留機制：

  • 篩除截留（Sieve Retention / Size Exclusion） — 微生物體積大於濾膜孔徑而被物理性攔截
  • 吸附截留（Adsorption） — 微生物因靜電力或物理纏繞而被截留在濾膜基質內部

這個區分非常重要：如果截留主要依賴吸附機制，那麼高生物負載可能使吸附位點飽和，導致微生物穿透。這就是為什麼控制前過濾生物負載是除菌過濾風險控制的關鍵。

重點提示 CDMO 實務連結

與 PDA TR 26 的關聯：USP 1229.3 此處的內容與 PDA TR 26（Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids）高度相關。在 CDMO 的無菌充填線上，除菌過濾是最後一道微生物屏障。前過濾生物負載的水平直接影響：

  • 除菌過濾器的使用壽命和通量
  • 細菌截留驗證（Bacterial Retention Validation）的挑戰水平設計
  • 過濾後產品的內毒素風險評估
Monitoring and Sampling — 監測與取樣

Original Text 原文內容

Statistical and analytical limitations complicate the evaluation of bioburden from both liquid and solid materials. Many products are inherently antimicrobial, and some formulations contain antimicrobial preservatives, both of which can limit bioburden recovery. Products that are outside of the pH range of approximately 4–9, are strongly hypertonic or strongly hypotonic, or have low water activity, may reduce the level of recoverable microorganisms.

The collection of multiple samples from the same bulk often gives varying results due to temporal and spatial differences in microbial distribution. Samples from varied components and different formulations will give different results because of different nutritional and cultural requirements, environmental stress to microorganisms, sampling methods, sample size, sampling pattern, species heterogeneity, and shifting microbial populations. Because of the technical challenges associated with bioburden analysis, results will be dependent upon sample-specific and method-specific variables when attempting to recover, grow, or enumerate organisms. Sampling frequency and maximum time delay between sampling and testing should be established based on previous data.

Presterilization bioburden analysis should be conducted on samples that are representative of materials produced during routine preparation and processing. Sampling frequency should be established based on previous data, known variability, batch size, material, process, and environmental influences. Bioburden should be recovered and enumerated from samples that are representative of the process material. When the material is stored in separate containers, analysts should consider testing multiple or composite samples. Bioburden evaluation should focus on microorganisms that represent a greater concern in the sterilization process. Total count methods should consider the properties of both the product under evaluation and the characteristics of the process that may affect recovery. Cultivation methods, diluents, and media selection must be based on past experience with the manufacturing process and test material (3). The methods listed in Microbial Enumeration Tests 〈61〉 and Mycoplasma Tests 〈63〉 may be appropriate for bioburden evaluation, although they may require modification to the methods and qualifications in order to meet the requirements of specific test materials.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

取樣的挑戰：生物負載分析不像化學分析那麼精確，原因包括：

  • 產品本身的抑菌性 — 許多藥品配方具有抗菌活性（如 pH 極端值、防腐劑），會殺死或抑制取樣中的微生物，導致回收率偏低
  • 微生物分佈不均勻 — 同一批次的不同取樣點可能得到差異很大的結果
  • 方法依賴性 — 不同的培養基、溫度、時間會回收到不同的微生物群

代表性取樣的重要性：取樣必須能反映常規生產條件下的實際情況。取樣頻率應基於歷史數據、已知變異性、批量大小、物料特性和製程條件來決定。

重點提示 Key Notes

USP 〈61〉 與 〈63〉 的角色：

  • USP 〈61〉 Microbial Enumeration Tests — 提供標準微生物計數方法（TAMC/TYMC），但可能需要針對特定產品進行方法適用性修改
  • USP 〈63〉 Mycoplasma Tests — 針對支原體的檢測方法，在生物製品中特別重要

CDMO 實務：每個新產品轉移到 CDMO 時，都需要在驗證階段建立該產品特定的生物負載取樣和檢測方法。不能假設所有產品都能用同一套標準方法。

比喻說明 Analogy

水質檢測的變異性：想像你從一條河流中取水樣來檢測水質。上游、中游、下游的結果可能完全不同；早上和下午的結果也可能不同；用不同的檢測試劑盒可能得到不同結果。生物負載取樣面臨完全相同的挑戰 — 這就是為什麼需要建立基於歷史數據的取樣策略，而不能隨意取樣。

Bioburden Screening — 生物負載篩選

Original Text 原文內容

Materials and products that are to be sterilized should be examined to determine the level of bioburden in the article. Determining the microbial population level is an essential element in ensuring process efficacy. For lethal processes other than radiation, the presence of gram-positive spore-formers represent the greatest potential for survival. The potential resistance of the bioburden to the specific sterilization process is an important consideration and can be evaluated by a screening process. Isolates that are identified as gram-negative rods have the potential to cause high levels of endotoxin. Figure 1 illustrates one possible bioburden screening program.

Destructive Processes (except Radiation)

Microorganisms can be recovered after exposure to heat screening or shock at 100°C, which eliminates vegetative cells and also triggers the germination process in spore-forming bacteria (see Methods for Determination of Resistance of Microorganisms to Sterilization Processes 〈56〉) (4). Heat shock treatment is an effective means to reduce the amount of bioburden microorganisms to be evaluated because spore-formers are the most resistant to all lethal processes other than radiation. Heat screening eliminates vegetative cells that lack resistance to sterilization and encourages germination, thus making it easier to isolate spore formers.

Published reports compare the resistance of recovered microorganisms to that of predefined bioindicators for many common (4,5) sterilization processes. Analysts should evaluate the isolated spore-forming bacteria to determine their resistance D-value to specific lethal sterilization processes and thus to ensure the validity of the sterilization method. The available data describe the lack of resistance of medically and environmentally isolated bacteria, mold, and yeast species to the more commonly used sterilization methods, so analysts may find only limited value in repeating these resistance studies.

Radiation

Sterilization by radiation processes is based on the bioburden in the presterilized material. Analysts commonly use bioburden-based sterilization processes as described in ANSI/AAMI/ISO 11137 (6). To ensure consistent sterility assurance, lot-to-lot variability of bioburden must be controlled. Data should be collected and analyzed for raw materials, intermediates, and/or products to ensure process control. ANSI/AAMI/ISO 11137 provides detailed information about radiation sterilization practices, including cycle development, validation approaches, and dose-auditing methodologies including expectations for initial and periodic bioburden assessment. ANSI/AAMI/ISO 11737 provides guidance for establishing methods to estimate bioburden levels on medical devices prior to irradiation (7).

Sterilizing Filtration

Bioburden screening for sterilizing filtration processes not yet subjected to formal validation can be performed by passing the material through a 0.45-µm-rated filter and examining the filtrate for viable microorganisms. Only those microorganisms that pass the 0.45-µm filter are of interest because they present the greatest potential challenge to the sterilizing filtration process. They should be evaluated against the upstream bioburden. Bioburden of greatest concern includes Pseudomonas, Brevundimonas, Ralstonia, and Mycoplasma.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 D-Value 與滅菌週期設計

D-Value（D 值）：在特定滅菌條件下，將微生物數量減少 90%（即 1 個 log）所需的時間或劑量。這是連結生物負載數據與滅菌週期設計的核心參數。

實務流程：

  1. 取樣測定生物負載總量（Bioburden Count）
  2. 熱篩篩選出孢子形成菌
  3. 測定分離菌株的 D-value
  4. 與生物指示劑的 D-value 比較
  5. 確認滅菌週期設計的安全餘裕

重點提示 除菌過濾的篩選策略

0.45 µm 前過濾法：這是一個巧妙的篩選邏輯 — 先用較大孔徑的濾膜過濾，只有能通過 0.45 µm 的微生物才是除菌過濾（0.2 µm）的真正挑戰。

關注菌種：Pseudomonas（假單胞菌）、Brevundimonas（短波單胞菌）、Ralstonia（雷爾斯通菌）和 Mycoplasma（支原體）— 這些都是體積較小、可能穿透除菌級濾膜的微生物。其中 Brevundimonas diminuta 正是 ASTM F838 的標準挑戰菌。

比喻說明 Analogy

安檢系統的分級篩選：機場安檢不會對每位旅客做同等程度的檢查。先用金屬探測門（熱篩/0.45 µm 前過濾）篩出可能有風險的對象，再對這些對象進行詳細檢查（D-value 測定/菌種鑑定）。這種分級策略既有效率又能確保安全。

Identification — 菌種鑑定

Original Text 原文內容

Recovered microorganisms can be identified to an appropriate level using the methods described in Microbial Characterization, Identification, and Strain Typing 〈1113〉. Only those microorganisms that present a potential risk to the product or the patient require identification to the species level or beyond. For a detailed discussion of the methods used to grow and identify microorganisms, see 〈1113〉.

It is not necessary for the purposes of evaluating presterilization bioburden to identify all isolates to the species level, although this can be helpful in some investigations. Analysts must conduct all evaluative work with pure cultures, and they must apply normal microbiology laboratory procedures for the selection and maintenance of pure cultures.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

適當水平的鑑定（Appropriate Level）：USP 的務實立場是 — 不需要對所有分離菌進行到種（Species）或株（Strain）的鑑定。只有以下情況才需要深度鑑定：

  • 該微生物對產品品質構成潛在風險
  • 該微生物對患者安全構成潛在風險
  • 偏差調查需要追溯污染來源

USP 〈1113〉：提供微生物鑑定的標準方法指引，包括表型鑑定（Phenotypic）、基因型鑑定（Genotypic）和分子分型（Strain Typing）等方法。

重點提示 CDMO 實務觀點

何時需要種級鑑定？在 CDMO 環境中，以下情況通常需要進行到種級（Species Level）的鑑定：

  • 生物負載超出既定限度（Excursion Investigation）
  • 發現疑似令人擔憂的微生物（Objectionable Organisms）
  • 環境監測中出現異常趨勢
  • 客戶或法規機構特別要求

日常的生物負載監測通常只需要到屬（Genus）或形態學分類（革蘭氏染色結果 + 形態描述）即可。

Bioburden Control — 生物負載控制

Original Text 原文內容

A typical bioburden-control program includes review and analysis of potential sources of contamination as well as sound process design and preventive and monitoring measures. The microbiological contamination-control program should be developed to identify and control bioburden and to assess product risk based on a formal assessment of risk modalities. The bioburden risk assessment should result in the establishment of critical control points and should include consideration of the following elements:

  • Microbiological attributes of materials before sterilization and the manufacturing process used for the materials (if applicable)
  • Inherent antimicrobial properties of the materials
  • Time limits for process execution
  • Water activity of the material
  • Environmental conditions within the facility
  • Equipment design and cleaning
  • Sanitization, decontamination, and other active microbial control processes (such as prefiltration, temperature, pH, osmolarity, etc.)

Controlling the bioburden of materials and products to be sterilized will ensure conformance to the levels required by the sterilization process validation. Additionally, controlling the bioburden levels of the items to be sterilized assures that residuals (e.g., allergens, endotoxins, and exotoxins) from that population will also be controlled. This is important because direct detection of these materials is challenging.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 與滅菌驗證的連結

關鍵邏輯鏈：生物負載控制 → 滅菌驗證 → 無菌保證

滅菌製程驗證時設定的參數（如 F0 值、輻射劑量、過濾挑戰水平）都是基於一定的生物負載假設。如果日常生產中的生物負載超出這些假設的範圍，滅菌製程的有效性就無法保證。因此，生物負載控制是滅菌驗證持續有效的先決條件。

內毒素的間接控制：原文特別指出，控制生物負載同時也控制了內毒素和外毒素的水平。這是因為革蘭氏陰性菌是主要的內毒素來源，而內毒素的直接檢測在某些基質中非常困難。控制源頭（微生物數量）比檢測終點（內毒素濃度）更為有效。

核心概念解析 Control Program Elements

生物負載控制計畫的七大要素：

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary

  • 生物負載監測的定位：不是獨立的檢測項目，而是整個滅菌製程控制策略的核心輸入。生物負載數據決定了滅菌週期的設計和驗證方法
  • 破壞性 vs 截留性：破壞性滅菌（濕熱/乾熱）最關注孢子形成菌的抗性；截留性滅菌（除菌過濾）最關注能穿透 0.45 µm 濾膜的小型菌。輻射滅菌則是特例，抗性規律不同
  • 取樣的挑戰：生物負載分析受產品特性、取樣方法、培養條件等多重因素影響，必須基於歷史數據建立科學的取樣策略
  • 篩選分級：熱篩（Heat Shock）篩選孢子形成菌、0.45 µm 過濾篩選小型菌 — 目標是識別「對特定滅菌製程構成最大風險」的微生物
  • 鑑定的務實原則：不需要對所有分離菌做到種級鑑定，但對產品或患者有潛在風險的微生物必須深入鑑定
  • 控制計畫的七大要素：涵蓋物料屬性、製程設計、環境控制、清潔消毒等全方位的生物負載管控措施
  • CDMO 核心訊息：Pre-sterilization bioburden 是滅菌週期驗證的基礎假設。維持生物負載在驗證範圍內，是確保每批產品無菌保證水平的前提
