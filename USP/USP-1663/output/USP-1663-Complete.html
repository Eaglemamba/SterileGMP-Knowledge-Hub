Section 1 — Purpose, Key Terms & Scope | 目的、關鍵術語與適用範圍

原文內容 Original Text

PURPOSE

This general information chapter presents a framework for the design, justification, and execution of an extractables assessment for pharmaceutical packaging and delivery systems. The chapter establishes critical dimensions of an extractables assessment and discusses practical and technical aspects of each dimension.

Although intended to be helpful and generally applicable, the chapter is for informational purposes and does not establish specific extraction conditions, analytical procedures, or mandatory extractables specifications and acceptance criteria for particular packaging and delivery systems or dosage forms.

Designing an individual extractables assessment is a process that balances sound science, prudent resource allocation, and effective risk management. Achieving this balance is the responsibility and obligation of the drug product manufacturer.

KEY TERMS (Selected)

Packaging System (also referred to as a container–closure system): The sum of packaging components and materials that together contain and protect the product.

Extractables: Organic and inorganic chemical entities that are released from a pharmaceutical packaging/delivery system, packaging component, or packaging material of construction and into an extraction solvent under laboratory conditions. Extractables themselves, or substances derived from extractables, have the potential to leach into a drug product under normal conditions of storage and use and thus become leachables.

Leachables: Foreign organic and inorganic chemical entities that are present in a packaged drug product because they have leached into the packaged drug product from a packaging/delivery system under normal conditions of storage and use or during accelerated drug product stability studies. Leachables are typically a subset of extractables or are derived from extractables.

Migrants: Also foreign chemical entities in the packaged drug product, but differentiated from leachables in that migrants accumulate in the drug product after crossing a physical barrier (e.g., primary and secondary packaging). Thus migrants are derived from secondary and tertiary packaging and ancillary components.

Safety Concern Threshold (SCT): The threshold below which a leachable has a dose so low that it presents negligible safety concerns from carcinogenic and noncarcinogenic toxic effects.

Analytical Evaluation Threshold (AET): The threshold at or above which a leachable should be characterized and reported for toxicological assessment. The AET can be mathematically derived from the SCT based on dosing parameters of the drug product.

SCOPE

The scientific principles and best-practices are intended to apply to any extractables assessment or extraction study of pharmaceutical packaging/delivery systems, packaging components, or materials of construction. Applications include:

  • Characterization, selection, and qualification of materials of construction, packaging components, or packaging/delivery systems
  • Establishing leachables–extractables correlations
  • Simulating worst-case drug product leachables profiles
  • Materials of construction and functional components of manufacturing equipment (e.g., filters, tubing, tanks)
  • Materials of construction for the medical device component of a combination product

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — E&L 三角關係

USP 1663 建立了可萃取物評估的基礎框架。理解三個核心概念之間的關係至關重要：

  • Extractables（可萃取物）：在實驗室強化條件下從包裝材料中被「逼出」的化學物質。
  • Leachables（可滲出物）：在實際儲存與使用條件下真正進入藥品的物質。Leachables 是 Extractables 的子集。
  • Migrants（遷移物）：穿越物理屏障（如外包裝）進入藥品的物質，來源是二、三次包裝。

關鍵邏輯：Extractables → 潛在的 Leachables；研究 Extractables 是為了預測 Leachables 的最壞情況。

比喻說明 — 擠海綿的邏輯

可以把包裝材料想像成一塊吸滿化學物質的海綿。

Extractables 研究就像「用力擰乾海綿」——在實驗室裡用溶劑、高溫等強化條件，把所有可能釋出的物質都逼出來，得到一份完整清單。

Leachables 研究則是觀察海綿在正常使用時（放入藥品中儲存2年）會自然滲出多少物質。

邏輯：先知道最壞情況（擰乾），再評估正常情況（自然滲出）是否在安全範圍內。

重點提示 — 章節性質與法規地位

USP 1663 是資訊性（informational）章節，不是強制規範章節（如 USP 661, 661.1, 661.2）。它提供科學框架與最佳實務，但不規定具體的萃取條件或接受標準。

這意味著設計個別評估方案是藥品製造商的責任，需要根據科學、法規要求與風險管理三者取得平衡。

SCT 與 AET 的邏輯關係

SCT（安全關切閾值）是毒理學概念，基於患者暴露量（µg/day）定義。低於此值不需進行致癌與非致癌毒性評估。

AET（分析評估閾值）是實驗室操作概念，由 SCT 換算而來（考慮劑量參數），以可測量的濃度單位（µg/mL）表示。AET 告訴分析人員：哪些化學物質需要被定性鑑定並報告給毒理學家。

簡言之：SCT 是終點目標，AET 是實驗室操作起點。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一個新型 CDO 客戶帶來一種用於靜脈注射藥品的橡膠塞，你的 E&L 評估應從哪一個 USP 章節開始？為什麼？
  2. Extractables 與 Migrants 最根本的差異是什麼？這對你的萃取研究設計有什麼影響？
  3. 如果某物質被偵測到但低於 AET，需要進行安全評估嗎？為什麼？

實務應用 — CDMO 材料資格確認

在 CDMO fill-finish 作業中，當客戶帶來新的容器封閉系統（如預灌注射器的橡膠活塞），USP 1663 框架提供了系統性評估步驟：

  1. 要求供應商提供材料配方（composition data）作為 Extractables 研究的先驗知識
  2. 根據藥品的 pH、溶劑特性設計模擬萃取介質
  3. 與 USP 1664 連結，確認萃取物評估的結果能用於預測實際藥品中的 Leachables

這個流程直接支持 CDMO 的 Change Control 程序和包裝材料供應商稽核。

Section 2 — Background Information & Uses of Extractables Assessment | 背景資訊與評估用途

原文內容 Original Text

BACKGROUND INFORMATION

During the course of manufacturing, packaging, storage, distribution, and administration; dosage forms and their constituents can contact components and materials of construction of manufacturing and packaging equipment, and primary and secondary packaging components and systems. Leachables, which can include both organic and inorganic chemical entities with wide chemical diversity, are of concern due to their potential safety risk to patients and potential compatibility risks for the drug product (e.g., drug substance interaction/degradation, pH change, appearance change, particle formation, protein aggregation/structure change, etc.).

In order to assess these risks and manage the potential issues posed by leachables, it is necessary to know the identities and the levels to which leachables will accumulate in the finished drug product over its shelf-life. These two pieces of information can be used to establish the magnitude of patient exposure (dose) and therefore the safety risk posed by an individual leachable, as well as the likelihood of any compatibility issues involving the drug product.

Uses of Extractables Assessments

Extractables assessments can be used to:

  • Characterize packaging/delivery systems, packaging components, combination product medical device components, manufacturing components, and their various materials of construction
  • Facilitate the timely development of safe and effective dosage form packaging/delivery systems, manufacturing systems and processes by assisting in the selection of components and materials of construction
  • Understand the effects of various manufacturing processes (e.g., sterilization) on packaging components and their potential leachables
  • Establish the worst-case potential leachables profile in a manner which facilitates leachables studies, the development of leachables specifications and acceptance criteria (should these be required), and the safety evaluation/qualification of potential and actual leachables
  • Establish the worst-case potential leachables profile in a manner which facilitates the safety evaluation/qualification of probable leachables when it is not scientifically possible to determine actual leachables
  • Facilitate the assessment of patient exposure to chemical entities resulting from direct contact between a patient's body tissue(s) and a packaging or combination product medical device component
  • Facilitate the establishment of qualitative and quantitative leachables–extractables correlations
  • Facilitate the development of extractables specifications and acceptance criteria (if required) for packaging components and materials of construction
  • Facilitate investigations into the origin(s) of identified leachables whose presence causes quality and/or safety issues (such as out-of-specification results) for a marketed product

In these ways, extractables assessments can support Quality by Design (QbD) principles for the development and manufacture of pharmaceutical packaging/delivery systems and drug products.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 為什麼需要 Extractables 評估？

藥品與包裝材料的接觸是不可避免的物理現實。從製造、填充、滅菌到儲存，藥品全程都與包裝材料接觸。問題不是「會不會有物質遷移」，而是「遷移了多少，是否安全」。

Extractables 評估的根本目的：建立一份可能滲入藥品的化學物質清單，並評估其安全性。這份清單必須在藥品真正上市前完成，而非等到問題出現後才調查。

比喻說明 — 藥品相容性像一場化學婚姻

藥品與包裝的關係就像一場長達 2–3 年的「化學婚姻」。在這段時間裡，雙方的化學成分會相互影響：

  • 包裝材料的塑化劑可能滲入藥液
  • 藥液的 pH 可能加速橡膠老化
  • 滅菌過程可能改變材料的化學特性，產生新的降解產物

Extractables 評估就是婚前協議——提前了解雙方的「化學個性」，預測可能發生的問題。

重點提示 — 九大用途中最關鍵的三項

從 CDMO 實務角度，以下三項用途最常見：

  1. 材料選擇與資格確認：評估新供應商的包裝材料，在客戶製程轉移（tech transfer）時確認相容性
  2. 建立 L-E 相關性（Leachables-Extractables Correlation）：連結萃取物研究與藥品穩定性試驗中的滲出物數據
  3. OOS 調查：當穩定性試驗出現未知雜質峰時，用萃取物評估結果追溯來源

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的客戶的 injection vial 在 18 個月穩定性試驗中出現了一個 unknown impurity peak。請描述如何利用 extractables assessment 的結果進行 OOS 調查。
  2. 為什麼「滅菌」（sterilization）被特別列為需要評估其對包裝材料影響的製程？請舉出一個具體的化學變化例子。

實務應用 — CDMO 的 QbD 整合

將 Extractables 評估整合進 QbD 流程：

  • Design Space 定義：包裝材料的 Extractables profile 是產品設計空間的一部分，應在 QTPP（目標產品品質概況）中明確定義
  • Risk Assessment：用 FMEA 評估各包裝組件的 Leachables 風險等級
  • Control Strategy：建立基於 Extractables 數據的包裝材料放行規格，作為 Leachables 監控的替代方法

這個整合方式可以大幅減少上市後包裝相關的 OOS 事件。

Section 3 — Generating the Extract | 萃取物的生成

原文內容 Original Text

GENERATING THE EXTRACT — General Concepts

Extractables are derived from a variety of sources and exhibit extensive chemical diversity. Primary sources of extractables include:

  • Chemical additives in individual elastomeric/polymeric packaging components and raw materials, including impurities in these additives
  • Chemical entities and additives that are present in packaging components composed of glass and metals
  • Entities related to the dissolution of the packaging component itself (e.g., iron extracted from a stainless steel material, silicon extracted from glass)
  • Monomers and higher molecular weight oligomers derived from incomplete polymerization
  • Migrants from secondary and tertiary packaging components, such as inks, label adhesives, and volatiles from cardboard shipping containers, plastic storage bags, and other shipping aids such as wooden pallets
  • Surface residues, such as heavy oils and degreasing agents on metal canisters and containers
  • Chemical substances on the surfaces of component fabrication machinery (mold release agents, antistatic and antislip agents)

The means by which an extraction process is accomplished are reflected in the juxtaposition of several experimental parameters:

  • The chemical nature of the extracting media
  • The time duration of the extraction process
  • The temperature and pressure at which the extraction is performed
  • The stoichiometry of the extraction process (extracted surface area per unit volume of extracting solution)
  • The mechanism or process by which the extraction is accomplished

Chemical Nature of the Extracting Medium

Of all the parameters involved in generating the extract, the extracting medium is the most critical because it is the extracting medium that accomplishes the extraction, and all other parameters merely facilitate the extraction.

Strategically, if the purpose of a particular extraction study is to simulate a worst-case leachables profile, then the ideal situation is for the extracting solvent to have a similar or greater propensity to extract substances as the formulation. Therefore, the most logical tactic for this simulation study is to use the formulation itself as the extracting medium.

If the use of the drug product as the extracting solvent is not feasible, then the drug product vehicle, or placebo, could be used as an effective extracting medium.

Large-volume parenteral plastic bag (pH 7.2)Water (pH 5.2), Water (pH 9.5), Isopropanol:water (50:50)

Extraction Time and Temperature

Extraction time and temperature are critical factors. The combination of extraction time and temperature establishes the magnitude of the driving force and the degree to which equilibrium is actually achieved. In a simulating extraction study, the purpose of elevated temperature is to increase the extraction rate, so that a short experimental time may simulate longer leaching times.

Because extraction is a diffusion process, the relationship between the diffusion rate and temperature can be expressed empirically by the Arrhenius equation. Extractables profiles should be monitored for equilibrium or the attainment of asymptotic levels of extractables.

Extraction Stoichiometry

Extraction stoichiometry considers the physical mass and/or surface area of the test article relative to the volume of the extracting medium. Stoichiometry can be manipulated to facilitate production of a more concentrated extract.

In summary, extraction stoichiometry can be based on:

  • The known chemical ingredients in a component or material
  • Safety-based thresholds for drug product leachables
  • The known or determined sensitivities of analytical instrumentation

Mechanism of Extraction — Extraction Technique

Common laboratory extraction techniques include:

  • Maceration (solvent soaking): test article allowed to soak in organic or aqueous extracting solvent at temperatures below the solvent's boiling point
  • Reflux: test article immersed in boiling solvent
  • Soxhlet: test article in the "thimble" of a Soxhlet apparatus; extraction solvent is redistilled repeatedly
  • Sealed vessel: test article and extracting solvent sealed inside a container capable of withstanding elevated temperatures and pressures, placed into a laboratory autoclave
  • Instrument-based solvent extraction: pressurized fluid extraction, microwave-assisted extraction, supercritical fluid extraction
  • Sonication: test article and extracting solvent in a glass container partly immersed in ultrasonic bath

For volatile organic compounds, extraction techniques include headspace analysis (HD/GC), direct thermal desorption (TD/GC), and thermogravimetric analysis (TGA/GC).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 萃取設計的優先順序

USP 1663 明確指出萃取介質（extracting medium）是最關鍵的參數，其他參數（時間、溫度、化學計量比）都是「輔助性」的。

選擇萃取介質的邏輯優先順序：

  1. 第一選擇：用真實藥品配方（formulation itself）作為萃取介質
  2. 第二選擇：用安慰劑（placebo/vehicle）作為萃取介質
  3. 第三選擇：用模擬溶劑（simulating solvents）——需要科學論證

高風險劑型（注射劑、吸入劑）應使用多種極性不同的溶劑（如 Table 1 所示的三種溶劑組合），以捕獲不同化學多樣性的可萃取物。

比喻說明 — Arrhenius 加速老化

提高萃取溫度就像「快轉電影」——讓 2 年的滲出過程在幾天內完成。

Arrhenius 方程式告訴我們：溫度每升高 10°C，反應速率大約加倍（Q10 效應）。因此，在 40°C 下萃取 3 天，相當於在 25°C 下萃取約 12 天。

但有一個重要警告：溫度不能過高，以免萃取條件本身改變了萃取物的化學性質（如某些有機物的熱分解），造成假陽性結果。

公式與概念 — 化學計量比計算邏輯

目標：萃取液中 AET 的濃度 ≥ 5 ng/µL（GC/MS 典型分析需求）

例：設計一個用於 5 mL 靜脈注射瓶橡膠塞的萃取研究
• 藥品體積：5 mL/瓶
• AET：0.1 µg/mL（基於 SCT 計算）
• 每瓶最大可能 leachable 量：0.1 µg/mL × 5 mL = 0.5 µg/瓶

為確保萃取液濃度 ≥ 0.5 µg/瓶（即 ≥ 5 ng/µL in 100 mL 萃取液）：
→ 萃取 20 個橡膠塞在 100 mL 溶劑中
→ 濃縮因子 = 20倍（比實際藥品接觸條件更嚴格）

這個邏輯確保萃取研究的靈敏度足以檢測出任何高於 AET 的化學物質。

重點提示 — 非溶劑萃取的應用場景

在以下情況必須考慮非溶劑萃取（headspace/TD/TGA）方法：

  • 乾粉吸入劑（DPI）膠囊：揮發性物質可能從膠囊材料遷移入粉末
  • 口服固體藥品：標籤黏膠中的揮發性有機物可能穿透塑膠瓶
  • 低密度聚乙烯容器的吸入溶液：木棧板中的揮發性物質可能透過塑膠容器遷移

CDMO 在評估客戶的包裝系統時，不應只考慮液態萃取，也需要評估揮發性物質的遷移風險。

在實際的 CDMO 客戶服務中，萃取研究設計的決策流程：

  1. 了解藥品特性：pH、有機溶劑含量、注射劑或吸入劑？（決定溶劑選擇）
  2. 確認評估目的：材料特性表徵（material characterization）還是模擬 Leachables 最壞情況？（決定萃取強度）

  4. 選擇萃取技術：固態或彈性體材料用浸泡/回流；揮發性物質評估用 headspace
Section 4 — Characterizing the Extract | 萃取物的表徵

原文內容 Original Text

CHARACTERIZING THE EXTRACT — Objectives and Challenges

Once an extract has been generated, the next objective is to perform a thorough chemical characterization of the extract. The extract characterization phase of the extraction study must enable the realization of the overall goals of the extractables assessment.

The practical objective of extract characterization must therefore be an exercise of due diligence in the discovery, identification, and quantitation to a reasonable degree of scientific certainty of all individual extractable chemical entities present in an extract above a specified level or threshold.

1. SCOUTING

The most useful analytical techniques in a scouting exercise provide information regarding bulk chemical properties of organic and/or inorganic chemical entities present in an extract, which can be used to guide extractables discovery, identification, and quantitation. Scouting analysis is not capable in and of itself of realizing the practical objective of extract characterization.

Examples of scouting utility:

  • Significant levels of nonvolatile residue (NVR by gravimetric analysis) could suggest significant inorganic chemical entities
  • Significant UV absorbance suggests organic entities with UV chromophores (e.g., phenolic antioxidants)
  • Characteristic features in an FTIR spectrum provide more detailed insights into chemical classes of organic extractables
  • For aqueous extracts, total organic carbon (TOC) provides a measure of total organic extractables

2. DISCOVERY

The process of discovery involves detecting instrumental responses from individual organic and inorganic extractables proportional to their levels within the extract. Trace organic analysis typically involves:

  • GC (Gas Chromatography): enormous separating capability for volatile and semi-volatile organic compounds
  • HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography): most applicable to semi-volatile and relatively nonvolatile organic compounds

GC and HPLC are complementary and orthogonal for the significant chemical diversity of extractables. Inorganic extractables (trace elements and metals) are typically discovered, identified, and quantitated by atomic emission spectroscopy.

3. IDENTIFICATION

Identification of an extractable can be accomplished either by structural analysis or qualitative analysis. The analytical techniques most applicable involve the combination of chromatography with mass spectrometry: GC/MS and LC/MS.

Individual extractables identifications are designated in the categories of Confirmed, Confident, or Tentative:

  • Tentative: data consistent with a class of molecule only (e.g., fragmentation behavior only)
  • Confident: tentative identification bolstered by additional confirmatory information (e.g., molecular weight, elemental composition)
  • Confirmed: only via mass spectral and retention time match with an authentic reference compound

For inorganic extractables: elemental analysis provides element-specific identifications and quantitations, but not the chemical speciation of the extractable. For example, the safety impact of sulfur as elemental sulfur may be different than that of sulfur as sulfate.

4. QUANTITATION

Quantitation is typically based on the instrumental response of an individual extractable relative to an authentic reference compound. Calibration is accomplished by analysis of authentic reference compounds (external standards). One or more internal standards can also be included to increase accuracy and precision.

Preparation of Extracts for Analysis

A good "rule of thumb" is that in order to accomplish a complete structural analysis of an unknown extractable, a GC/MS requires approximately 5 ng injected into the instrument, suggesting a concentration of 5 ng/µL or 5 µg/mL.

If the analyte concentration is insufficient, the following parameters can be optimized:

  • Extraction stoichiometry (extract more material or use more extracting solvent)
  • Extraction conditions (higher temperatures, longer times, more aggressive techniques)
  • Extract processing (concentration of the extract)

核心概念解析 — 四步驟表徵流程

萃取物的完整表徵分為四個步驟，各有不同的分析工具和目的：

  1. Scouting（偵察）：用非特異性分析（UV、FTIR、NVR、TOC）獲取整體化學特性，指引後續分析方向。可選但有幫助。
  2. Discovery（發現）：用 GC 和 HPLC 分離並偵測個別化學物質的存在（儀器響應信號）。
  3. Identification（鑑定）：用 GC/MS 或 LC/MS 確認化學結構。分三個置信水準：Tentative、Confident、Confirmed。
  4. Quantitation（定量）：測量每個化學物質的濃度，與 AET 比較。

重要：僅完成 Discovery 不代表完成評估目標。最終目標必須包含各化學物質的定性鑑定（至少 Confident 水準）和定量數據。

比喻說明 — 鑑定置信水準就像法庭證據標準

三種鑑定水準的置信度類比法律標準：

  • Tentative（推定）：只有間接證據，如「質譜碎裂模式符合某一類化合物」。就像法庭上的「情況證據」——可能是這個人，但無法確定。
  • Confident（可信）：有分子量確認、元素組成等多個佐證，但沒有標準品比對。就像有「多個目擊者」但沒有 DNA 證據。
  • Confirmed（確認）：與真實標準品（authentic reference compound）的質譜和保留時間完全匹配。就像 DNA 比對——確鑿無疑。

法規通常要求超過 AET 的物質達到至少 Confident 水準的鑑定，Confirmed 是最高標準，適用於需要安全評估的物質。

重點提示 — GC 與 LC 的互補性

GC 和 HPLC 在 Extractables 分析中是互補的，必須同時使用以獲得完整的 extractables profile：

  • GC 優勢：揮發性與半揮發性有機物（抗氧化劑、塑化劑、殘留單體）；與 MS 聯用可搜索質譜庫
  • HPLC 優勢：非揮發性有機物（大分子添加劑、聚合物低聚物）；適合直接分析水性萃取液

只用 GC/MS 會漏掉高沸點/非揮發性物質；只用 HPLC 會漏掉揮發性物質。高品質的 extractables 評估必須兩者並用。

無機元素萃取物的特殊挑戰

無機萃取物（金屬元素）的分析有一個獨特挑戰：原子光譜分析（AAS、ICP-AES、ICP/MS）只能告訴你「存在多少硫」，但無法告訴你硫是以何種形式存在：

  • 元素硫（S₈）— 相對低毒
  • 硫酸鹽（SO₄²⁻）— 通常可接受
  • 2-巰基苯并噻唑（2-MBT）— 具有毒性問題，是 OINDP 的特殊案例化合物

這意味著對重要的無機萃取物，可能需要額外的化學形態分析（speciation analysis）才能完成安全評估。

  2. 某個萃取液在 UV 掃描中顯示強吸收峰，但 GC/MS 和 HPLC/MS 都沒有發現任何化合物。可能的原因是什麼？應如何追查？

實務應用 — CDMO 分析能力建設

根據 USP 1663 的框架，CDMO 的 E&L 分析平台應具備以下核心能力：

  • GC/MS（必備）：用於揮發性與半揮發性有機萃取物的發現與鑑定
  • LC/MS or LC/UV（必備）：用於非揮發性有機萃取物
  • ICP/MS or ICP-AES（必備）：用於無機元素萃取物
  • Headspace GC/MS（強烈建議）：用於揮發性有機物，尤其是吸入劑或乾粉劑的評估
  • NMR（附加能力）：提供結構確認，補充 MS 數據

沒有這些分析能力的 CDMO 應與認可的第三方實驗室建立合作，在合約中明確分析責任分工。

SUMMARY — Assessing the Completeness of an Extractables Assessment

The completeness of an extractables assessment can only be judged against the overall goals of the assessment. For example, an extractables assessment accomplished solely for materials characterization might include one extracting solvent, one extraction technique, and one set of extraction conditions; along with a materials-based threshold (e.g., 10 ppm w/w). Such an extractables assessment might be considered complete if all extractables above the defined threshold were identified to the confident level (defined above) and quantitated.

For an extractables assessment designed to establish a rigorous leachables–extractables correlation for a high risk drug product, where a challenging safety threshold might apply (e.g., 0.15 µg/day), good scientific practice and due diligence requires the following:

Generation of extracts should be accomplished with:

  • Multiple solvents or extracting media with varying extracting power based on the known extracting power of the drug product vehicle
  • Multiple and complementary extraction techniques, including those with the capability for volatiles analysis
  • Extraction conditions that allow equilibrium to be achieved

Characterization of extracts should use:

  • Multiple and complementary analytical techniques
  • Careful sample preparation, keeping the analytical technique(s) in mind
  • A systematic process for identification and quantitation of extractables

In this case, the extractables assessment might be considered complete if all extractables above the defined threshold were identified to at least the confident level, quantitated, and correlated both qualitatively and quantitatively with drug product leachables data (if available) and the known ingredients in the packaging system, packaging component(s), or material(s) of construction.

Example Extractables Profiles — Materials Characterization

Extractables profiles (GC/MS and HPLC/UV) from a hexane Soxhlet extract of a cyclic olefin copolymer (COC) material of construction. COC materials are used in the fabrication of pre-filled syringes, vials for small-volume parenterals, and bags for large-volume parenterals.

The extracts were generated by subjecting approximately 5 g of suitably sized material to 16 hours of Soxhlet extraction with 125 mL of hexane, adjusted relative to an extractables identification threshold of 10 ppm (µg/g).

The major peak in the HPLC/UV extractables profile above the materials-based threshold was the known additive (antioxidant) Irganox 1010 (Peak 2).

Key lesson: GC/MS and HPLC/UV are complementary and orthogonal, illustrating the concept that multiple analytical methods are required to typically elucidate the complete extractable profile.

核心概念解析 — 完整性是相對的，取決於目的

USP 1663 的重要哲學：評估的「完整性」沒有絕對標準，只有相對於目的的標準。

兩個極端情境的對比：

  • 材料特性表徵（低風險目的）：1種溶劑 + 1種技術 + 材料基準值（10 ppm）→ 達到 Confident 鑑定水準即完整
  • 高風險藥品的 L-E 相關性建立（高風險目的）：多種溶劑 + 多種互補技術 + 揮發物分析 + 達到平衡條件 + SCT/AET 基準值 → 所有超過閾值物質達到 Confident 鑑定水準且與 Leachables 數據建立相關性

CDMO 在接受客戶委託 E&L 評估前，必須先確認：這個評估的目的是什麼？目的決定了評估的範圍和深度。

比喻說明 — COC 材料案例的重要啟示

USP 1663 用 Cyclic Olefin Copolymer（COC）材料的實際案例說明了一個關鍵事實：

GC/MS（偵測揮發性物質）和 HPLC/UV（偵測非揮發性物質）在同一個萃取液中各自發現了不同的化合物。如果只跑 GC/MS，主要的抗氧化劑 Irganox 1010 可能被漏掉；如果只跑 HPLC/UV，揮發性的小分子添加劑可能被忽視。

這個案例就像拼圖：每種分析技術只能看到拼圖的一部分，必須組合起來才能看見完整圖像。這就是為什麼 USP 1663 要求使用「multiple and complementary analytical techniques」。

實務應用 — CDMO E&L 評估範圍確認清單

在開始任何 extractables 評估前，CDMO 應與客戶確認以下關鍵決策點：

  3. 適用閾值：SCT = 0.15 µg/day（OINDP）或其他劑型的對應閾值？
