Section 1 — Purpose, Key Terms & Background | 目的、關鍵術語與背景

原文內容 Original Text

PURPOSE

This general chapter presents a framework for the design, justification, and implementation of assessments for drug product leachables derived from pharmaceutical packaging and delivery systems. A scientifically sound leachables assessment is important to manufacturers and their various suppliers primarily as a means of establishing the suitability for use of pharmaceutical packaging/delivery systems, as leachables can potentially affect drug product efficacy, safety, and quality.

Designing an individual leachables assessment is a process that strikes a balance between sound science, prudent resource allocation, and effective risk management with an emphasis on patient safety and product quality.

KEY TERMS (Selected)

Leachables: Foreign organic and inorganic chemical entities that are present in a packaged drug product because they have leached into the packaged drug product from a packaging/delivery system, packaging component, or packaging material of construction under normal conditions of storage and use or during accelerated drug product stability studies. Leachables are typically a subset of extractables or are derived from extractables.

Threshold of Toxicological Concern (TTC): A level of exposure for all chemicals, whether or not there is specific toxicity data, below which there would be no appreciable risk to human health. The TTC approach is a form of risk characterization in which uncertainties arising from the use of data on other compounds are balanced against the low level of exposure.

Safety Concern Threshold (SCT): The threshold below which a leachable would have a dose so low as to present negligible safety concerns from carcinogenic and noncarcinogenic toxic effects. For OINDP: SCT = 0.15 µg/day TDI.

Qualification Threshold (QT): The threshold below which a given noncarcinogenic leachable is not considered for safety qualification (toxicological assessments) unless the leachable presents structure-activity-relationship (SAR) concerns. For OINDP: QT = 5 µg/day TDI.

Analytical Evaluation Threshold (AET): The threshold at or above which a leachable should be characterized and reported for toxicological assessment. The AET can be mathematically derived from the SCT based on dosing parameters of the drug product.

Leachables Studies: Laboratory investigations into the qualitative and quantitative nature of a particular leachables profile(s) over the proposed shelf-life of a particular drug product.

BACKGROUND

Management of leachables is important to pharmaceutical and biotechnology/biologic product manufacturers and regulatory authorities because certain leachables above specific concentrations can present safety concerns for patients and/or compatibility issues for drug product formulations. During the 1980s, the U.S. FDA began to formally address leachables in drug products after findings of patient sensitivity induced by leachables and other potential safety concerns. Since then, management of both extractables and leachables for packaging systems and final drug products has become an important part of pharmaceutical development and regulatory submissions.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — USP 1664 vs 1663 的定位

USP 1663 和 1664 是姐妹章節，共同構成 E&L 評估的完整框架：

  • USP 1663：如何研究「包裝材料本身」（實驗室萃取條件下）→ Extractables 評估
  • USP 1664：如何研究「真實藥品中的滲出物」（正常儲存/使用條件下）→ Leachables 評估

關鍵連結：1664 的 Leachables 研究通常以 1663 的 Extractables 評估結果作為先驗知識（知道哪些物質可能滲出）。

USP 1664 的版本更新（Official as of 01-Dec-2024）比 1663 更新，反映了業界對 Leachables 管理科學認知的進展。

核心概念解析 — 三層安全閾值體系

USP 1664 定義了三個相關但不同的安全閾值（以 OINDP 為例）：

  • TTC（毒理學關切閾值）：來自 EMA，用於評估基因毒性雜質，1.5 µg/day（風險因子 10⁻⁵）。TTC 是整個閾值體系的理論基礎。
  • SCT（安全關切閾值）= 0.15 µg/day：PQRI 為 OINDP 提出，比 TTC 更嚴格（風險因子 10⁻⁶）。低於 SCT 不需要進行安全評估。
  • QT（資格確認閾值）= 5 µg/day：低於 QT 且無致癌結構警告的物質，不需要化合物特異性安全評估。

注意：SCT 和 QT 是「評估閾值」，不是「控制限」或「安全限」——超過這些閾值意味著需要進行評估，不代表不安全。

比喻說明 — 風險矩陣的直觀理解

Table 1 的風險矩陣把劑型按兩個維度分類：

  • 縱軸：路徑關切度（吸入 > 注射 > 局部）——物質越直接進入人體，風險越高
  • 橫軸：藥品與包裝的相互作用可能性——液體比固體更容易萃取包裝中的物質

最高風險位在左上角（吸入氣霧劑）：藥品直接進入肺部，配方含有機溶劑且長期使用。最低風險在右下角（口服固體）。

CDMO 應根據這個矩陣確認客戶藥品的風險等級，進而決定 Leachables 評估的嚴格程度。

重點提示 — 「低風險」不等於零風險

USP 1664 特別指出：即使是低風險劑型（口服片劑、外用粉末），仍存在 Leachables 問題的可能。

真實案例：標籤黏膠中的化學添加劑可能透過塑膠初級包裝遷移，出現在口服固體藥品的雜質圖譜中，造成 OOS 結果。

結論：雖然低風險劑型不需要常規 Leachables 研究作為上市申請的一部分，但 CDMO 仍應在風險評估中考慮這種可能性，特別是在調查 OOS 結果時。

Section 2 — Concepts: Safety Thresholds & Information Sharing | 安全閾值概念與資訊共享

原文內容 Original Text

CONCEPTS — General Concepts for Leachables Assessment

Leachables studies can be used within the context of an overall leachables assessment to:

  • Facilitate the timely development of safe and effective dosage form packaging/delivery systems by assisting in the selection of components and materials of construction
  • Facilitate the establishment of qualitative and quantitative leachables–extractables correlations in drug products
  • Establish the worst-case drug product leachables profile in a manner that facilitates the development of drug product leachables specifications and acceptance criteria
  • Identify trends in drug product leachables accumulation levels over the shelf-life of a particular drug product
  • Facilitate change-control processes for drug product packaging/delivery systems
  • Facilitate investigations into the origin(s) of identified leachables whose presence causes out-of-specification (OOS) results for a marketed drug product

Safety Thresholds

Safety thresholds allow for a science- and risk-based determination of acceptable levels of leachables. Because safety thresholds are derived from exposure data, they are considered in terms of units of exposure, such as Total Daily Intake (TDI). Any safety threshold must be converted into units of concentration (e.g., µg/mL) so that it can be applied as an analytical threshold in the laboratory.

Examples of safety thresholds:

  • TTC (EMA): 1.5 µg/day TDI for genotoxic impurities, using an excess cancer risk factor of 10⁻⁵
  • PQRI SCT for OINDP: 0.15 µg/day TDI (risk factor 10⁻⁶). Below the SCT, identification and safety evaluation of leachables generally would not be required
  • PQRI QT for OINDP: 5 µg/day TDI. Below the QT, leachables without structure alerts for carcinogenicity or irritation would not require compound-specific safety risk assessment

Note that neither the SCT nor the QT is a control threshold or safety-driven limit. Rather, they are leachables evaluation thresholds.

Special case compounds for OINDP requiring lower thresholds based on specific analytical capabilities: polyaromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs/PNAs), N-nitrosamines, and 2-mercaptobenzothiazole (see USP 〈1664.1〉).

Information Sharing

To successfully manage leachables throughout the drug product lifecycle, it is critical to establish close and regular communication among stakeholders: chemists, toxicologists, packaging engineers, manufacturing operations, procurement, etc. With respect to leachables, communication between the analytical chemist and toxicologist is critical.

Information sharing between packaging component manufacturers/suppliers and drug product developers/manufacturers is also important in order to guide packaging component and materials of construction selection, provide knowledge of potential extractables and leachables, and facilitate leachables–extractables correlations via knowledge of packaging component chemical compositions.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — SCT 的邏輯根源

為什麼 PQRI 為 OINDP 選擇 0.15 µg/day？這個數字的推導邏輯：

  1. EMA 的 TTC 方法：1.5 µg/day，使用 10⁻⁵ 的癌症風險因子（即每 100,000 人中增加 1 例）
  2. PQRI 認為 OINDP 需要更嚴格的標準，因為：（a）藥物直接進入肺部等敏感器官，（b）可能是終身用藥，（c）Leachables 通常是工業化學品，與藥物活性成分無關
  3. 因此採用 10⁻⁶ 的風險因子（每 1,000,000 人中增加 1 例），得到 SCT = 1.5 × (10⁻⁶/10⁻⁵) = 0.15 µg/day

這個邏輯同樣適用於其他高風險劑型——注射劑的 SCT 值可能也在類似範圍，但需要根據具體劑型的暴露模式計算。

比喻說明 — 三層閾值就像交通燈

用交通燈比喻三個閾值對 Leachable 的處理決策：

  • 低於 SCT（0.15 µg/day）= 綠燈：物質存在但量極少，不需要鑑定，不需要安全評估。繼續前進。
  • SCT 到 QT 之間（0.15–5 µg/day）= 黃燈：需要進行化學鑑定（確認是什麼物質），但如果沒有結構警告（SAR concerns），不需要進行個別的毒理學評估。謹慎前進。
  • 高於 QT（>5 µg/day）= 紅燈：需要完整的化學鑑定 + 化合物特異性毒理學評估。停下來評估。

記住：這些「燈號」都是評估決策，不是「安全/不安全」的判斷。即使超過 QT，如果毒理學評估顯示安全，仍可接受。

重點提示 — 特殊案例化合物（OINDP）

以下三類物質在 OINDP 中屬於「特殊案例」，因毒性特別高，需要比 SCT 更低的閾值：

  • PAHs/PNAs（多環芳烴）：具有致癌性，廣泛存在於橡膠硫化製品中。閾值基於 GC/MS 的偵測能力設定。
  • N-亞硝胺：強致癌物，在橡膠固化過程中可能形成，也是近年 NDMA/NDEA 問題的相關物質類別。
  • 2-巰基苯并噻唑（2-MBT）：橡膠促進劑的降解產物，具有毒性。

CDMO 評估含橡膠組件（橡膠塞、矽膠管）的包裝系統時，必須特別針對這三類物質建立方法並設定更低的 AET。

  2. 為什麼資訊共享（供應商提供材料配方）對 Leachables 評估的效率如此重要？如果供應商拒絕提供配方信息，有什麼替代方案？

實務應用 — CDMO 供應商稽核的 E&L 要素

在對包裝材料供應商進行稽核時，USP 1664 的資訊共享要求可以轉化為具體稽核項目：

  • 供應商能提供材料完整配方（包括添加劑種類與比例）嗎？
  • 供應商有進行過 Extractables 研究並提供報告嗎？
  • 供應商的製造過程（固化、滅菌、成型）有可能引入特殊案例化合物嗎？
  • 材料變更通知（Change Notification）程序是否完善？任何影響 Leachables profile 的變更都必須通知客戶。

無法提供這些信息的供應商應被列為「高風險供應商」，需要 CDMO 自行承擔更完整的 Extractables 評估責任。

Section 3 — Leachables Study Design | 滲出物研究設計

原文內容 Original Text

LEACHABLES STUDY DESIGN

Although leachables studies may be accomplished at any time during the drug product development/manufacturing lifecycle, leachables studies are especially relevant during late stage product development or during formal product stability assessment. Ideally, leachables assessment is conducted as follows:

  • The assessment is performed on the actual drug product and not simulations thereof
  • The assessment is performed with the actual packaging and delivery system in the form it will be commercialized, not with a prototype or on system components
  • The related extractables assessments are accomplished on the same lots of packaging components used to manufacture the drug product lots on which the leachables assessments are performed
  • The assessment is performed on a product manufactured under conditions that reflect the actual commercial processes; leachables studies may include accelerated storage conditions, but they cannot be limited to accelerated conditions and must include real-time assessment

Development Stage Leachables Studies

Leachables studies can also be performed early in the drug product development process (e.g., preclinical stage) in order to facilitate the selection of packaging components. Such leachables studies are particularly useful for certain "high-risk" dosage forms where selecting appropriate packaging components and materials of construction is critical.

For high-risk dosage forms, leachables characterization is recommended for any drug product batches that are used as test articles in any definitive toxicology or clinical studies.

For low-risk dosage forms (e.g., solid orals, topical powders) leachables studies conducted throughout development might be appropriate in order to assess, and thereby avoid, problems with packaging systems that might appear either in later stage development or marketed product.

Design Considerations

The design of a particular leachables study requires:

  1. Information on identities and maximum possible accumulation levels of all potential leachables from the packaging component manufacturers
  2. Consideration of chemical diversity and physical/chemical properties of potential leachables (polarity, volatility, solubility)
  3. Assessment of nature of formulation contact: direct or indirect; transient or continuous
  4. A rigorous leachables assessment considers leachables from sources other than primary packaging, including necessary secondary packaging and, in certain situations, tertiary packaging
  5. Various characteristics of the drug product formulation must also be considered (physical state, changes in state during production or use)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 研究設計的四個理想條件

USP 1664 列出的四個理想條件，定義了什麼是「高品質」的 Leachables 研究：

  1. 真實藥品（非安慰劑）：因為有些 Leachables 只在活性成分存在時才形成（如化學反應產物）
  2. 商業化最終包裝形式：原型包裝可能與商業化包裝的 Leachables profile 不同
  3. 相同批次的包材：確保 Extractables 研究和 Leachables 研究使用相同來源，這是建立 L-E 相關性的前提
  4. 必須包括實時（real-time）評估：加速穩定性（如 40°C/75%RH）可以幫助早期預測，但不能替代 25°C/60%RH 的長期穩定性研究

在實際 CDMO 操作中，這四個條件往往很難同時滿足（成本、時間限制），需要與客戶進行科學的風險-效益討論。

比喻說明 — Leachables 研究就像婚後生活觀察

繼續 USP 1663 的「化學婚姻」比喻：

Extractables 研究（USP 1663）：婚前協議——在實驗室受控條件下，強迫了解雙方的「化學個性」。

Leachables 研究（USP 1664）：婚後真實生活——觀察兩個「化學個性」在真實相處 2–3 年後，實際發生了哪些化學反應，哪些物質真正滲入了藥品。

婚前調查再完整，也不能保證婚後完全沒有意外。Extractables 評估提供預測，Leachables 研究提供確認。

重點提示 — 包材批次一致性的重要性

「相同批次包材」的要求容易被忽視，但它是建立有效 L-E 相關性的基礎：

案例：如果 Extractables 研究用的是 Batch A 橡膠塞，但 Leachables 研究用的是 Batch B 橡膠塞，而 Batch B 的配方有微小差異，那麼：

  • Extractables 結果可能無法解釋 Leachables 中出現的某個物質
  • L-E 相關性無法建立
  • 監管機構可能要求重新研究

實務建議：在填充生產批次時同步保留若干包材樣品，用於後續的 Extractables 確認研究。

  2. 為什麼加速穩定性條件（40°C/75%RH）的 Leachables 研究不能完全替代實時研究（25°C/60%RH）？請從化學動力學角度解釋。

實務應用 — CDMO Change Control 中的 Leachables 評估

USP 1664 特別強調：任何導致患者接觸到不同 Leachables profile 的變更，都需要進行 Leachables 研究作為 Change Control 的一部分。

在 CDMO 日常操作中，以下變更應觸發 Leachables 評估：

  • 橡膠塞或瓶子供應商變更（即使規格相同）
  • 藥品配方中 pH 或溶劑比例的調整
  • 終端滅菌條件的改變（如從乾熱改為濕熱，或調整 F0 值）
  • 填充線設備更換（接觸藥品的材料可能不同）

建立一個「Leachables Impact Assessment」的 SOP，作為 CDMO 變更評估程序的標準步驟之一。

Section 4 — Leachables Characterization & AET Calculation | 滲出物表徵與 AET 計算

原文內容 Original Text

LEACHABLES CHARACTERIZATION

The primary goal of any leachables study is leachables characterization; i.e., the discovery, identification, and quantitation of leachables present in a particular drug product. Analytical methods for leachables characterization are developed based on:

  • The nature of the drug product matrix
  • The identities and possible accumulation levels of potential leachables
  • The required sensitivity based on an adopted leachables evaluation threshold and the capabilities of the analytical methods employed

Unlike a typical drug product impurity method where target analytes are related to the drug substance, leachables have a wide chemical diversity and can come from various sources in the packaging/delivery system. This presents a significant challenge for trace analysis, especially in the case of organic leachables identification.

Analytical Thresholds

The starting action in leachables method development is to establish the level at which the method must perform — the analytical threshold. An example of a safety-based threshold is the SCT as established for OINDP (0.15 µg/day). In order to define the SCT in terms that facilitate laboratory analysis, it must be converted from units of exposure (µg/day) to units of concentration (e.g., µg/mL). The resulting analytically useful threshold is termed the Analytical Evaluation Threshold (AET).

General formula for converting SCT to AET:

AET (µg/container) = (0.15 µg/day ÷ doses/day) × (labeled doses/container)

For liquid dosage forms:

AET (µg/mL) = AET (µg/container) ÷ mL/container

For solid dosage forms:

AET (µg/g) = AET (µg/container) ÷ g/container

Analytical Method Requirements

Analytical method requirements are based on the determined AET and information on potential leachables obtained from extractables assessments. Since leachables are typically a subset of extractables, analytical methods for leachables characterization can often be based on those used for extractables characterization.

Any analytical method for leachables used for:

  • Drug product stability studies in support of product registration
  • Establishing leachables–extractables correlations for high-risk dosage forms
  • Development of leachables specifications and acceptance criteria

must be subject to complete validation using industry-accepted validation practices.

Preparing the Drug Product for Analysis — Sample Preparation

Sample preparation approaches by dosage form:

  • Aqueous dosage forms (inhalation solutions, parenterals, ophthalmics): liquid-liquid extraction with water-immiscible organic solvents (dichloromethane, hexane, petroleum ether); pH manipulation to enhance extraction of weakly acidic or basic leachables
  • Solid dosage forms (solid orals, inhalation powders, lyophilized powders): dissolve in aqueous solution, apply liquid-liquid extraction; or headspace GC for volatiles
  • Oral liquid dosage forms: dilute in aqueous solution, apply liquid-liquid extraction
  • Cream and ointment dosage forms: dissolve in aqueous solution, filter, apply liquid-liquid extraction
  • Nonaqueous vehicles (MDI with organic propellants): see USP 〈1664.1〉

Analytical Techniques

The most useful analytical techniques for discovery, identification, and quantitation of organic leachables: GC/MS and HPLC (LC)/MS. Headspace sampling can also be interfaced with GC/MS to address volatile compounds.

Analytical methods for leachables should be capable of characterizing both target and new (or unspecified) leachables. Structural analysis of leachables should be accomplished with a systematic process identical to that described in USP 〈1663〉 for extractables.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

公式與計算 — AET 計算完整示例

情境：評估一個吸入溶液（OINDP）的橡膠瓶塞
• 每日使用劑量：4 次吸入/天
• 每瓶標示劑量：200 次吸入
• 每瓶體積：25 mL
• SCT（OINDP）：0.15 µg/day

步驟 1：計算每容器的 AET
AET (µg/容器) = (0.15 µg/day ÷ 4 doses/day) × (200 doses/容器)
             = 0.0375 × 200
             = 7.5 µg/容器

步驟 2：換算為濃度（液態劑型）
AET (µg/mL) = 7.5 µg/容器 ÷ 25 mL/容器
            = 0.3 µg/mL

結論：分析方法必須能夠偵測並定量
      濃度 ≥ 0.3 µg/mL 的任何未知 Leachable

這個 AET 值直接決定了分析方法的最低偵測限（LOQ）要求，是方法開發的起點。

核心概念解析 — 為什麼 Leachables 分析比藥品雜質分析更困難？

傳統藥品雜質分析（ICH Q3B）的邏輯：雜質來自藥物合成，化學結構與活性成分相關，可以預測並準備標準品。

Leachables 分析的困難：

  • 化學多樣性極高：從揮發性有機物到金屬元素，涵蓋幾乎所有化學類別
  • 藥品基質干擾：活性成分、賦形劑的濃度遠高於 Leachables，造成分析干擾
  • 無法預先確定所有目標物：需要掃描式（non-targeted）分析方法
  • 標準品可能無法獲得：某些結構複雜的萃取物可能沒有商業標準品

這些挑戰解釋了為什麼「完整的」Leachables 研究在技術上是困難的，以及為什麼 AET 是一個務實的妥協點。

比喻說明 — 方法驗證就像校準秤

為什麼 Leachables 分析方法必須完整驗證？

想像你在稱量黃金（Leachables 濃度）。如果你的秤（分析方法）沒有經過校準（驗證），你報告的數字就沒有意義——你不知道 0.3 µg/mL 這個讀數是真實的 0.3，還是因為方法不準確而給出的錯誤數字。

USP 1664 要求用於法規申請的方法必須完整驗證，意味著：

  • 精確度（Accuracy）：你的秤能正確讀數嗎？
  • 精密度（Precision）：重複測量的結果一致嗎？
  • 特異性（Specificity）：你稱的真的是黃金，不是其他金屬嗎？
  • 偵測限/定量限（LOD/LOQ）：你能稱出多小的量？必須 ≤ AET。

重點提示 — 樣品前處理的設計邏輯

樣品前處理（Sample Preparation）的設計原則：創造一個讓分析技術能「看見」Leachables 的樣品形態，同時盡量去除藥品基質的干擾。

實際操作提示：

  • 加速穩定性樣品（40°C/75%RH，3個月）可以用於方法開發，因為它含有更高濃度的 Leachables，更容易建立方法並評估回收率
  • 對含有機溶劑的藥品（如 MDI），樣品前處理必須保留揮發性組分，否則會低估揮發性 Leachables
  • 使用 spike recovery（加標回收率）評估樣品前處理方法，確保 Leachables 不在處理過程中損失

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 計算：某注射藥品（aqueous, pH 6.5），每日最多注射 2 次，每瓶體積 10 mL，標示 10 次注射。使用 SCT = 0.15 µg/day，計算此藥品的 AET（µg/mL）。
  2. 驗證一個 Leachables 定量方法時，你選擇的空白基質（blank matrix）必須具備什麼特性？為什麼不能直接用超純水作為空白基質？

實務應用 — CDMO Leachables 方法開發策略

在承接客戶委託的 Leachables 研究時，CDMO 實驗室應遵循以下策略：

  1. 確定 AET：根據客戶藥品的劑型、每日劑量和容器體積計算
  2. 獲取 Extractables 數據：向客戶或包材供應商索取已有的 Extractables 報告，作為目標化合物清單的基礎
  3. 選擇分析平台：GC/MS（揮發性） + LC/MS（非揮發性） + ICP/MS（元素）是標準組合
  4. 開發樣品前處理方法：用加標回收率評估，確保回收率在 70%–130% 範圍內
  5. 執行全面驗證：準確度、精密度、LOQ（≤ AET）、特異性、耐受性
  6. 執行方法系統適用性：每次使用分析方法前確認系統正常運作

原文內容 Original Text

ESTABLISHING A LEACHABLES–EXTRACTABLES CORRELATION

A leachables–extractables correlation is established when actual drug product leachables can be linked either qualitatively or quantitatively with extractables from corresponding extractables assessments. Leachables–extractables correlations are important for:

  • Justifying the use of routine extractables release tests of packaging components as an alternative to leachables testing during stability studies for high-risk drug products
  • Establishing the source of a leachable producing an OOS result for a low-risk drug product
  • Change control
  • Ongoing quality control

Qualitative correlation: A leachable is linked either directly or indirectly to an extractable. Example: hexadecanoic acid in leachables profile directly linked to hexadecanoic acid in extractables profile; the ethyl ester of hexadecanoic acid can be indirectly linked to hexadecanoic acid in extractables profile (when ethanol is a formulation constituent and esterification is possible during storage).

Quantitative correlation: The quantity of any individual leachable over the shelf-life must be mathematically related to the quantity of the corresponding extractable in its source. The quantity of the leachable should be less than or equal to the quantity of the corresponding extractable.

Example of quantitative correlation:

  • BHT in drug product formulation: 5 µg/mL
  • BHT extracted from primary packaging component: 300 µg/component
  • Drug product volume: 50 mL per component
  • BHT extracted: 300 µg; BHT leached: 250 µg (5 µg/mL × 50 mL)
  • Conclusion: quantitative correlation established; 50 µg unaccounted for (likely not leached from component)

CONSIDERATIONS IN DEVELOPING LEACHABLES SPECIFICATIONS

One means of developing specifications: testing a minimum of three drug product batches to determine leachables levels. After thorough chemical and safety evaluation, acceptance criteria can be established consistent with:

  1. Qualitative and/or quantitative results of leachables studies
  2. Capabilities of the drug product's manufacturing process
  3. Potential safety, compatibility, and/or drug product quality impact of the leachables

Leachables specifications should be applicable to a product during all stages of its shelf-life, including release and at end of shelf-life. This is the case since leachables accumulate over the entire shelf-life of a drug product.

A typical leachables specification could include:

  • Quantitative end of shelf-life limits for target leachables (apply over the entire shelf-life)
  • A quantitative limit for "unspecified" (previously unidentified and uncorrelated) leachables

SIMULATION STUDIES

Occasions may arise in which it is not analytically feasible to successfully discover and identify all actual leachables in a drug product leachables study (e.g., due to challenging thresholds). In such cases, the activities of discovery and identification of probable leachables are accomplished in an extraction study using a simulating solvent.

Key design differences between a simulation study and a drug product leachables study:

  1. The drug product formulation has been replaced with a simulating solvent that mimics the formulation
  2. The conditions of contact have been accelerated to increase concentrations of probable leachables
  3. The test article can be either the complete packaging system or separate components

Key rule: For a correlation to be valid, each leachable concentration in the drug product must be less than or equal to the corresponding extractable concentration in the simulated extract (accounting for uncertainty and justifiable "exaggeration factors").

INORGANIC (ELEMENTAL) LEACHABLES

Testing of plastic packaging systems and their materials of construction will establish extractable elemental impurities relevant to a particular packaging system. The results should be used to establish those elemental impurities that should be monitored as targeted elemental leachables in the drug product. Atomic spectroscopy does not establish the compound form of the detected element — the form (e.g., sulfur as elemental sulfur vs. sulfate vs. 2-mercaptobenzothiazole) may have a marked effect on safety impact.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — L-E 相關性的實際價值

建立 L-E 相關性的最重要實用價值：用包材放行測試（Extractables 測試）替代昂貴的藥品穩定性 Leachables 測試。

邏輯：如果我們能證明「每當包材批次的 Extractables 在控制水準，藥品中的 Leachables 也必然在安全水準」，那麼我們就不需要每批藥品都做完整的 Leachables 穩定性研究——只需要在包材放行時測試 Extractables 即可。

這對高風險 CDMO 客戶（如吸入劑、注射劑）的 QC 策略具有重大的成本和時間影響。沒有 L-E 相關性，每批藥品都需要長達 2 年的穩定性追蹤。

公式與計算 — 定量 L-E 相關性的驗證邏輯

BHT 的 L-E 相關性驗證（USP 1664 範例）：

已知條件：
• 藥品中 BHT 濃度（最高穩定性時間點）：5 µg/mL
• 藥品每容器體積：50 mL
• 同批次包材 Extractables 研究中 BHT：300 µg/組件
• 每容器使用組件數：1個

計算：
• BHT 作為 Leachable 的總量：5 × 50 = 250 µg
• BHT 作為 Extractable 的總量：300 µg

驗證：250 µg (leached) ≤ 300 µg (extracted) ✓
→ 定量相關性成立！

意義：從包材萃取到的 BHT 多於藥品中實際出現的量
（50 µg 的差額可能是未滲出或降解消失）
→ Extractables 是 Leachables 的保守估計 ✓

比喻說明 — Simulation Study 就像食品模擬試驗

Simulation Study 的概念在食品包裝法規（如 21 CFR 177）中早就存在：

食品法規允許用「食品模擬液」（如水、乙酸、乙醇、橄欖油）替代真實食品進行包材相容性測試——因為直接用真實食品（如一桶義大利麵醬）做測試既昂貴又複雜。

USP 1664 的 Simulation Study 邏輯相同：用化學特性與藥品配方相似的模擬溶劑（simulating solvent）替代真實藥品，進行 Extractables 研究，再將結果用於預測 Leachables。

關鍵要求：模擬溶劑的「萃取能力」必須≥真實藥品，確保模擬研究是保守估計（過估）而非低估。

重點提示 — Leachables 規格的特殊性

Leachables 規格與普通藥品規格（如含量、雜質）有一個重要區別：

Leachables 在整個貨架期都在累積，因此規格必須適用於貨架期的所有時間點，包括上市時（release）和貨架期末（end of shelf-life）。

這意味著：

  • 上市放行的 Leachables 濃度通常低於貨架期末——因此規格必須以「貨架期末」的最高預期濃度為基準設定
  • 在制定接受標準時，需要考慮穩定性趨勢（是否線性累積？是否在某個時間點趨於平衡？）

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 如果藥品穩定性研究在 18 個月時發現某 Leachable 的濃度超過了 Extractables 研究估計的最大可能累積量，這意味著什麼？可能的原因有哪些？應如何處理？
  2. 某藥品 Simulation Study 顯示模擬萃取物中某物質的濃度為 2 µg/mL，而 AET 為 0.5 µg/mL。是否可以直接用 Simulation Study 的結果宣稱「Leachables 符合要求」？為什麼？

實務應用 — CDMO 建立 L-E 相關性資料庫

對於承接多個類似藥品（如同一包材供應商的注射劑 fill-finish 業務）的 CDMO，建立系統性的 L-E 相關性資料庫具有巨大的長期價值：

  • 前期投資：對標準包材組合（如特定供應商的橡膠塞 + 玻璃瓶）建立完整的 Extractables 數據庫
  • 長期回報：新客戶使用相同包材時，可以調用歷史數據，大幅縮短 Extractables 評估的時間和成本
  • L-E 相關性應用：一旦建立了特定包材在特定配方類型下的 L-E 相關性，可以用包材 Extractables 放行規格替代昂貴的藥品穩定性 Leachables 測試
  • 監管槓桿：完善的 L-E 相關性數據可以在 NDA/ANDA 審查中大幅提升包裝章節的審查信心

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary

  • USP 1664 的定位：1663 的姐妹章節，從 Extractables（包材研究）進入 Leachables（藥品研究），是 E&L 評估框架的第二步。
  • 三層安全閾值：TTC（1.5 µg/day）→ SCT（0.15 µg/day，OINDP）→ QT（5 µg/day，OINDP）；均為評估閾值，非控制限。
  • AET 計算：SCT ÷ doses/day × labeled doses/container，再除以容器體積得到 µg/mL 或 µg/g。AET 決定分析方法的最低 LOQ 要求。
  • 研究設計四原則：真實藥品、商業化包裝、相同批次包材、必須包括實時評估。
  • L-E 相關性的價值：成立後可以用包材 Extractables 放行測試替代藥品 Leachables 穩定性測試，是 CDMO 降低 QC 成本的重要工具。
  • Simulation Study：當直接 Leachables 分析技術上不可行時，用模擬溶劑進行 Extractables 研究代替，條件是模擬溶劑的萃取能力必須 ≥ 真實藥品。
  • 規格制定：基於至少三批次數據，兼顧科學（L-E 相關性）、生產能力和安全性；規格必須適用於整個貨架期（包括 end of shelf-life）。
  • 特殊案例化合物（OINDP）：PAHs、N-亞硝胺、2-MBT 需要更低的閾值，CDMO 評估橡膠組件時必須特別關注。
