Background and Scope

Original Text

This general information chapter provides an overview of the concepts and principles involved in sterilization (by various modes) of compendial articles that must be sterile. It includes information about supportive sterilization processes utilized in their preparation.1 More detailed recommendations are presented in specific information chapters for each sterilization mode:

  • ⟨1229.1⟩ Steam Sterilization by Direct Contact
  • ⟨1229.2⟩ Moist Heat Sterilization of Aqueous Liquids
  • ⟨1229.3⟩ Monitoring of Bioburden
  • ⟨1229.4⟩ Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids
  • ⟨1229.5⟩ Biological Indicators for Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.6⟩ Liquid-Phase Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.7⟩ Gaseous Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.8⟩ Dry Heat Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.9⟩ Physicochemical Integrators and Indicators for Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.10⟩ Radiation Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.11⟩ Vapor Phase Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.12⟩ New Sterilization Methods
  • ⟨1229.13⟩ Sterilization-in-Place
  • ⟨1229.14⟩ Sterilization Cycle Development
  • ⟨1229.15⟩ Sterilizing Filtration of Gases
  • ⟨1229.16⟩ Prion Inactivation
  • ⟨1229.17⟩ Mycoplasma Sterilization
  • ⟨1229.18⟩ Viral Clearance Methods

In the strictest definition of sterility, a specimen is deemed sterile only when there is a complete absence of viable microorganisms (bacteria, yeasts, and molds), but sterility cannot be demonstrated with respect to compendial articles and other items because of the inherent limitations of the current test (see Sterility Tests ⟨71⟩). Sterility, therefore, is defined in probabilistic terms that establish an acceptable level of risk. Sterility can be accomplished only by the use of a validated sterilization process under appropriate current good manufacturing practices and cannot be demonstrated by reliance on sterility testing alone.

The basic principles for control of sterilization processes, including method development, validation, and ongoing assurance, are as follows:

  1. Sterilization process development that includes evaluation of the stability and compatibility of materials, container integrity, expected presterilization bioburden, equipment method control parameters, etc.
  2. Identification of sterilization process parameters that preserve the inherent properties of the materials yet inactivate or remove microorganisms.
  3. Demonstration that the sterilization process and equipment are capable of operating within the prescribed parameters and corresponding to independent measurements of the critical parameters.
  4. Performance of replicate studies that represent the operational range of the equipment and employ actual or simulated product. The use of biological indicators for correlation between the measured physical parameters, and the expected lethality is recommended wherever possible.
  5. Maintenance and monitoring of the validated process during routine operation.
  6. Assurance that the bioburden (number and type) of the materials is acceptable and is maintained within predetermined limits during routine operation.
"Sterility can be accomplished only by the use of a validated sterilization process under appropriate current good manufacturing practices and cannot be demonstrated by reliance on sterility testing alone."

導師解析

核心架構：USP ⟨1229⟩ 的定位

你正在閱讀的是整個 USP 滅菌系列的「母章」(Parent Chapter)。想一個問題：為什麼 USP 需要一個總論性的章節，而不是直接寫各種滅菌方法的細節？

答案在於原則的統一性。無論你用的是蒸汽、乾熱、輻射還是過濾，滅菌驗證的根本邏輯是相同的：

  • 了解你的產品能承受什麼條件
  • 了解你要殺滅的微生物有多頑強
  • 用物理測量和生物指示劑來雙重確認製程效力
  • 持續監控確保驗證狀態不退化

⟨1229⟩ 建立這些通則，18 個子章節則針對各模式提供具體指引。

為什麼「無菌」是機率概念？

想像你有一批 100 萬支注射劑。你能一支一支打開來檢查有沒有活菌嗎？不行 — 因為打開檢查這個動作本身就破壞了無菌狀態。

這就是為什麼 USP 說無菌「不能被證明」(cannot be demonstrated) — 你永遠不可能檢驗每一個單位。你只能透過製程驗證來建立信心：我的製程有多大的「把握」能殺死所有微生物？這個把握程度就是 SAL。

CDMO 實務觀點

六大原則的審計意義：原文列出的六點基本原則，實際上就是 FDA/EMA 稽查員在查驗滅菌製程時的檢查清單。作為 CDMO，你面對不同客戶的不同產品時，這六點的執行深度會有差異：

  • 金屬/玻璃組件 → 產品穩定性評估（第1點）較簡單
  • 生物製劑溶液 → 材料相容性（第2點）是關鍵挑戰
  • 多產品共用設備 → 生物負載監控（第6點）必須特別嚴謹

Establishing and Justifying Sterilization Processes — SAL & PNSU

Original Text

Articles intended to be sterile must attain a ≤10−6 probability of a nonsterile unit, i.e., less than or equal to one chance in one million that viable bioburden microorganisms are present. [Note — This is also called the sterility assurance level. The term probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU) is used throughout this chapter because it is descriptive and substantially easier to understand.] This PNSU can be accomplished by balancing the method effect on the materials and the destruction of the bioburden (see Figure 1). Three methods are currently in use: overkill, bioburden/biological indicator, and bioburden-based methods. These methods are described in greater detail below. Regardless of the method chosen, the objective is a maximum PNSU of ≤10−6 for the bioburden.

An overkill method is the simplest method to establish but has the greatest impact on materials. The bioburden-based method requires the most method control but subjects the materials to the least stressful conditions. Confidence in the process's lethality is the same, regardless of the method utilized.

Figure 1. A basic comparison of various validation methods.

For items that are essentially unaffected by the sterilization process, the overkill method is preferred because of its simplicity. Overkill sterilization can be defined as a method in which the destruction of a high concentration of a resistant microorganism supports the destruction of reasonably anticipated bioburden present in routine processing. That objective can be demonstrated by attaining any of the following: a defined minimum lethality; a defined set of method conditions; or confirmation of minimum log reduction of a resistant biological indicator.

If articles could be damaged by extended exposure to the sterilization process, it may not be feasible to employ an overkill method. In these instances, demonstrating the effectiveness of the sterilization cycle requires not only information about the delivered method conditions but also knowledge about resistance and control of the population of the materials' bioburden. This method is widely used for the terminal sterilization of heat-labile solutions and laboratory media. This sterilization strategy is variously called the bioburden/biological indicator or combination based method and is defined thus:

"Bioburden/biological indicator based sterilization is an approach in which the incomplete destruction (or destruction of a modest population) of a resistant biological indicator can be used to demonstrate the capability of the method to reliably destroy the bioburden present."

The bioburden-based method is used when material stability is limited or when there are no suitable biological indicator microorganisms available to use with the sterilizing process. Customarily, radiation sterilization is validated using the bioburden-based method. The bioburden-based method can be defined as:

"A method in which bioburden samples from the material are routinely evaluated for resistance to the sterilization process and may be utilized to demonstrate the effectiveness of the process. Routine monitoring of the bioburden population, and its resistance to the sterilization process is mandatory."

Filter sterilization of liquids and gases differs from other sterilization modes because filtration relies on removal of microorganisms from the fluid rather than inactivation by chemical or physical means.

導師解析

三大驗證策略 — 核心比較

USP 在這裡描述的三種方法，本質上是一個光譜：從「對微生物最嚴厲、對產品最粗暴」到「對微生物剛好夠用、對產品最溫和」。

方法 對產品衝擊 對 Bioburden 控制要求 典型應用
Overkill 最高 最低 金屬、玻璃、耐熱組件
Bioburden/BI 中等 中等 大/小容量注射劑、培養基
Bioburden-based 最低 最高 輻射滅菌品項

關鍵洞察：無論選擇哪種方法，最終目標一致 — PNSU ≤10−6。差別只在於你用什麼方式來「證明」你達到了這個目標。

考試比喻：三種證明方式

想像你要證明一個學生的數學能力足以通過考試（= 達到 SAL 10−6）：

  • Overkill 方法：讓學生做一份超級困難的模擬考（高濃度抗性 BI），全部答對。如果連這種魔王題都能通過，正式考試（bioburden）當然不在話下。簡單暴力，但學生可能被操到崩潰（產品受損）。
  • Bioburden/BI 方法：用中等難度的模擬考，加上對學生平時成績的了解，來推斷他能通過正式考試。需要更多資訊佐證，但不會把學生操壞。
  • Bioburden-based 方法：直接研究正式考試的題目難度和範圍，再對照學生的實力。不需要模擬考（不用 BI），但你必須非常了解考題（持續監控 bioburden）。

CDMO 決策場景

過濾滅菌的特殊地位：注意原文最後一段 — 過濾滅菌是「移除」而非「殺滅」微生物，本質上與其他方法不同。這對 CDMO 無菌充填工廠尤其重要：

  • 你的多數生物製劑客戶產品都是用過濾滅菌 + 無菌充填，不是終端滅菌
  • 過濾滅菌的驗證邏輯不適用 overkill/bioburden 光譜，而是參照 ⟨1229.4⟩
  • 但預滅菌的設備和容器仍然適用本章的原則 — 例如用 overkill 蒸汽滅菌你的灌裝設備

D-Value and Microbial Resistance

Original Text

The D-value is the time (customarily in minutes) or radiation dose (customarily in kiloGrays) required to reduce the microbial population by 90% or 1 log cycle (i.e., to a surviving fraction of 1/10) and must be associated with the specific lethal conditions at which it was determined (see Figure 2). For steam and dry heat, the D-value is a function of temperature. In gas sterilization (ethylene oxide, chlorine dioxide, or ozone), D-values are a function of the chemical concentration, relative humidity, and temperature. Similarly, for liquid chemical sterilization the D-value is a function of the temperature and sterilant concentration. [Note — Determining the D-value for vapor (condensing) systems such as hydrogen peroxide, hydrogen peroxide plasma, and peracetic acid is complex because of the biphasic nature of the materials. Radiation and filtration sterilization are validated using unique methods. These processes are validated by methods that differ from those in this introductory chapter.]

Figure 2. Graphical representation of D-value.

The D-value is not an inherent attribute of the microbe only, so the influence of other factors such as substrate, matrix, recovery media, and test methodology must be considered in D-value determination. The resistance of a biological indicator is defined for the indicator as a system. Accurate assessment and comparison between D-values requires standardization of test methods. To properly evaluate the effectiveness of a sterilization process, analysts must evaluate the resistance of the bioburden experimentally or via a literature search.

導師解析

D-value 的數學意義

D-value 是滅菌動力學的基礎單位。用最簡單的方式理解：

起始菌數：10&sup6; (1,000,000)
經過 1 個 D-value：10&sup5; (100,000) — 減少 90%
經過 2 個 D-value：10&sup4; (10,000)
經過 6 個 D-value：10° (1) — 理論上剩 1 隻
經過 12 個 D-value：10&supmin;&sup6; — 達到 SAL 目標

所以如果你的 BI 起始菌數是 106，你需要 12 個 D-value 才能達到 PNSU ≤10−6。

D-value 不是微生物的「固有屬性」

這是一個經常被誤解的重點。同一株 Geobacillus stearothermophilus 孢子，在不同條件下可以有不同的 D-value：

  • 基質效應 — 在水溶液中 vs. 在蛋白質基質中，抗性可能差異很大
  • 回收培養基 — 用什麼培養基來計數存活菌，也會影響結果
  • 測試方法 — 浸沒法 vs. 載片法，結果未必一致

所以在比較 D-value 時，你必須確認測試條件是否標準化 — 否則就像比較不同單位的數字。

特殊滅菌模式的 D-value 挑戰

注意原文的括號提示 — 過氧化氫蒸氣（VHP）、過氧化氫電漿等系統的 D-value 很難確定，因為它們是雙相系統（蒸氣 + 冷凝液同時存在）。如果你的 CDMO 使用 VHP 來消毒隔離器，驗證策略不能簡單套用蒸汽滅菌的 D-value 概念 — 需要參考 ⟨1229.11⟩。

Death Curve Regions and Biological/Physical Data

Original Text

The death curve for microorganisms subjected to a sterilization process is comprised of three distinct regions (see Figure 3):

  1. Survivor curve region — Where viable microorganisms can be recovered and counted to determine the slope of the death curve. Using survivor counts in short exposure periods, the first section of the death curve can be drawn to where the population is approximately 10 cfu.
  2. Fraction negative region — Where replicate studies with multiple biological indicators are used to estimate the slope. This can extend the demonstrable portion of the death curve to approximately 10−2 to 10−3.
  3. Estimated region — Where the death rate curve established by either the survivor curve method or fraction negative method is extrapolated to the desired PNSU. Below 10−3 the death curve is assumed to be linear and is depicted in Figure 3 by the dashed line beyond the point assuming that the death of microorganisms continues at the same rate.

Figure 3. Death curve showing the various regions.

As stated earlier, the goal of the sterilization process is inactivation of the bioburden without adversely affecting product quality attributes. Demonstration of the lethality of a sterilization process under routine operation relies on differences in the relative resistance and population of the bioburden relative to the biological indicator (see Figure 4). Where the overkill method is used, bioburden controls can be less rigorous.

Figure 4. Relative resistance and population of typical biological indicator and bioburden microorganisms.

Validation of sterilization processes links physical measurements with biological indicator performance to establish method lethality. Knowledge of the method's effectiveness coupled with bioburden controls on the materials under processing and information on bioburden resistance allow determination of the probability of a nonsterile unit.

The biological indicator, when used, is a convenient means to simplify the sterilization validation effort. Biological indicators customarily are bacterial spores that have established resistance to the sterilization process under evaluation. When supplied as spores (on a substrate or as a suspension) with known initial population and resistance, their response to the method can be correlated to the measured physical conditions. Spores are preferable as biological indicators because their resistance and population are predictable and stable when they are handled, stored, and used as recommended. Additional details about biological indicators can be found in Biological Indicators for Sterilization ⟨1229.5⟩.

The spores can be placed in the sterilization load in locations where physical parameter measurements such as temperature or gas concentration cannot be easily obtained (e.g., within needle lumens, syringes, and ampules) or where measurement may alter the delivered conditions (e.g., sampling of the lethal gas). The biological indicator provides a means to directly assess the sterilizing effect of the method in a manner not possible by physical measurements. The lethality-based physical measurement and biological inactivation data from a sterilization process should be in reasonable agreement. When this is not the case, an investigation should be considered.

導師解析

微生物死亡曲線的三個區域 — 為什麼重要？

理解這三個區域，就理解了滅菌驗證的科學基礎與認知極限：

  • Survivor Curve 區（可直接計數區） — 你能真正「看到」存活菌的區域。數據最可靠。
  • Fraction Negative 區（統計推斷區） — 已經看不到存活菌了，但通過多重重複實驗中「有幾個長菌/有幾個沒長」來推斷斜率。可信度中等。
  • Estimated 區（外推假設區） — 純粹的數學外推。假設死亡曲線持續保持線性。這裡就是我們「相信」SAL 達到 10−6 的依據 — 但本質上是信仰加上數學，不是直接觀測。

這就是為什麼法規說「無菌不能被證明，只能被保證」— 因為 SAL 10−6 的那個區域，從來沒有人真正測量到過。

物理 + 生物 = 雙保險

USP 為什麼要同時使用物理測量和生物指示劑？想像你開車：

  • 物理測量（溫度探針、壓力感測器）= 你的儀表板。告訴你引擎轉速多少、時速多少。
  • 生物指示劑（BI）= 實際跑出來的距離。證明你真的走了那麼遠。

如果儀表板顯示時速 100 公里、跑了 1 小時，但 GPS 顯示你只走了 50 公里 — 這就是「not in reasonable agreement」，需要調查。

BI 放置位置的 CDMO 實務

原文提到 BI 可以放在「物理測量無法到達的位置」— 針頭管腔、注射器內、安瓿瓶內。這對 CDMO 的設備滅菌特別重要：

  • 灌裝針頭：管腔內部是最難被蒸汽穿透的位置，也是 BI 應該放置的位置
  • 不鏽鋼管路：彎頭、死角處的凝結水排除是關鍵
  • 隔離器內部：VHP 分布可能不均勻，BI 幫助驗證最難到達的角落

每次客戶的產品配置不同（不同大小的西林瓶、不同類型的瓶塞），BI 的放置策略可能都需要重新評估。

思考練習

  1. 如果你的滅菌鍋驗證中，溫度探針數據顯示 F0 ≥ 15 分鐘，但 BI 有一支呈現陽性（長菌），你的調查方向會是什麼？
  2. 為什麼死亡曲線在 10−3 以下「假設為線性」這個假設如此重要？如果死亡曲線實際上出現「拖尾」(tailing)，對 SAL 的計算有什麼影響？

  • USP ⟨1229⟩ 是滅菌系列的母章，統領 18 個子章節，涵蓋從蒸汽到輻射到過濾的所有滅菌模式
