Introduction — 緒論

Original Text

Sterilization processes are divided broadly into two categories: destruction of microorganisms and their physical removal from the material to be sterilized. Autoclaving is an example of the former, and sterilizing filtration is an example of the latter. The physical removal of microorganisms depends on the bioburden of the solution to be filtered, the properties of the solution, the filtration conditions, and the filter itself.

Sterilizing filtration is a process that can be validated to consistently yield filtrates that are sterile, as defined in Sterilization of Compendial Articles ⟨1229⟩. This chapter provides an overview of (1) various factors that affect the filtration process, (2) the filter integrity test and when to perform it, (3) prefiltration bioburden control, (4) responsibilities of the filter manufacturer and user, and (5) troubleshooting the filtration process.

Multiple factors contribute to the effectiveness of any sterilizing filtration process. These include the type and number of microorganisms, the properties of the liquid, the filter design and membrane polymer, and the filtration process parameters. Properties of the liquid that influence filtration effectiveness include its chemistry, viscosity, surface tension, pH, osmolarity, ionic strength, and temperature, as well as the presence of insoluble materials. Aspects of the filter that affect the filtration include effective filter area, nominal pore size, pore-size distribution, membrane thickness, porosity, membrane polymer, filter pleat density, nonwoven support layers, electrostatic charge, and the hydrophilic nature of the filter membrane. The filtration process parameters that influence microbial retention include temperature, flow rate, volume, filtration time, differential pressure, and pressure pulsations.

Additionally, effective sterilizing filtration depends on (1) the production controls and quality assurance systems used by the filter manufacturer, (2) the qualification and validation studies conducted by, or for, the filter user, (3) effective controls to ensure that prefiltration bioburden and operating parameters remain within validated ranges, and (4) filter integrity. Filter users should ensure that the filtrate remains sterile by using validated sterilization processes for the filtration assembly and all downstream manufacturing equipment and effective aseptic handling of the sterilized materials. Filter users should carefully consider placement of the filter (e.g., proximity to the filling line or hold tank) to minimize the possibility of postfiltration contamination.

"Sterilizing filtration is a process that can be validated to consistently yield filtrates that are sterile."

核心概念 Key Concepts

除菌過濾 vs 滅菌（Sterilizing Filtration vs. Sterilization by Destruction）：

  • 破壞型（Destruction） — 高壓蒸汽滅菌（Autoclaving）、乾熱、輻射等方法殺死微生物
  • 去除型（Physical Removal） — 除菌過濾透過濾膜將微生物截留，不殺死它們

對生物製劑（biologics）而言，蛋白質藥物無法承受高溫滅菌，因此除菌過濾是唯一可行的最終滅菌方式 — 這就是為什麼它被稱為 "THE method for aseptic fill-finish of biologics"。

CDMO 實務觀點

濾膜位置（Filter Placement）至關重要：USP 明確提到濾膜應盡量靠近灌裝線或保存槽。在 CDMO 環境中，這直接影響無菌保證水平（SAL）：

  • 濾膜距灌裝針越近，後過濾污染風險越低
  • 但也意味著需要在 Grade A 環境中操作，增加 PUPSIT 等完整性測試的挑戰
  • 每個客戶產品的製程驗證需個別考量濾膜的最佳位置

Sterilizing-Grade Filters — 除菌級濾膜定義

Original Text

A sterilizing-grade filter is one that is capable of retaining a minimum 1 × 107 cfu of B. diminuta (ATCC 19146) per square centimeter of effective filter area when tested in accordance with ASTM F838-05 (2013), Standard Test Method for Determining Bacterial Retention of Membrane Filters Utilized for Liquid Filtration.

The designation "sterilizing grade" implies a sterilizing action only if other conditions are met, including the integrity test specification established by the filter manufacturer and validated by the user (see Validation, below).

Sterilizing-grade filters typically are microporous membranes that have nominal pore-size ratings of about 0.2 µm. These membranes are fabricated with various materials, have relatively narrow pore-size distributions, and can be integrity tested. The integrity test results can be correlated with microbial retention. Membrane filters that have a nominal pore size of 0.45 µm can be validated to produce sterile filtrates under some conditions; for example, some liquids require high differential pressures to achieve useful flow rates, and these pressures are not suitable for use with 0.2-µm-rated filters. When manufacturers use 0.45-µm filters, they should ensure particularly stringent control of presterilization bioburden.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

B. diminuta 挑戰的黃金標準

Brevundimonas diminuta (ATCC 19146) 是全球公認的除菌級濾膜挑戰微生物：

  • 直徑約 0.3 µm — 是已知最小的可培養細菌之一
  • 挑戰水平：107 CFU/cm² — 每平方公分一千萬個菌
  • 通過標準：濾出液必須完全無菌（零 CFU 通過）

這個定義是除菌過濾驗證的基石，也是 PDA TR26 的核心參考。

0.2 µm vs. 0.22 µm — 有什麼差別？

USP 使用 "about 0.2 µm" 的說法。實務上，不同供應商標示為 0.2 µm 或 0.22 µm，兩者功能上等效，都必須通過 ASTM F838 的 B. diminuta 挑戰測試。

0.45 µm 的例外情境：USP 允許在特定條件下使用 0.45 µm 濾膜（如高黏度液體需要高壓差推動），但要求特別嚴格的前過濾生物負載控制。在 CDMO 實務中，除非客戶有特殊需求且提供完整的科學理由，否則一律使用 0.2 µm。

比喻說明

0.2 µm 除菌級濾膜就像機場安檢的標準 — 「除菌級」不僅僅是一個孔徑規格，而是一整套認證體系：濾膜要通過 ASTM F838 測試（類似機場安檢設備的型式認證），還需要每次使用時的完整性測試（類似每天開機前的安檢設備校準確認）。只有兩者都通過，才能宣稱「除菌級」。

Retention Mechanisms — 截留機制

Original Text

Microporous membranes remove microorganisms from the liquid by two primary mechanisms: sieve retention, which relies on physical blockage of particles that are larger than the pores they encounter, and adsorption, which is a charge-related phenomenon whereby particles are bound to the membrane surfaces. Both mechanisms should be considered during the development, qualification, and validation of sterilizing filtration processes.

Sieve retention, for the most part, is independent of filtration conditions and the microbial challenge level, but the composition of the liquid, temperature, and filtration conditions can affect sieve retention under certain conditions.

Adsorption is a charge-related phenomenon that is influenced by the composition of the membrane, the properties of the filtered liquid, the filtration conditions, and the number and type of microorganisms present in the liquid.

The number and type of microorganisms and other particles present in the material to be filtered can affect retention. Sieve retention implies that every microorganism is larger than the largest pore in the filter. Microorganisms and filter pores are not uniform in size, and some microorganisms may be smaller than the largest pores. Even if a microorganism is smaller than the pore it encounters, it may be retained if a site is available and the filtration conditions are conducive to adsorption. Retention probability is related to the number of microorganisms in the upstream bioburden.

  • 這是 PDA TR26 Section 6.0 細菌截留驗證章節的核心邏輯
Factors That Affect Retention — 影響截留的因素

Original Text

Nature of Pores

Microporous membranes consist of a polymer matrix that is penetrated by interstices commonly referred to as pores. Compared with those in depth filters, the pores in microporous membranes have a relatively narrow size distribution. The size, number, and shape of the pores determine the filter's retention capabilities. With the exception of the pores in track-etched filter membranes, pores are not cylindrical; they are made up of a series of pseudopolyhedral structures with varying internal diameters.

The pore size, pore-size distribution, and membrane porosity are a function of the manufacturing process. Careful design and control of that process are necessary to ensure that the resulting membrane has the desired integrity test value, microbial retention capability, and uniformity.

Nature of Microorganisms

Microorganisms have a variety of shapes and sizes. If the microorganism encounters a membrane pore that is smaller than its smallest diameter, the microorganism likely will be captured by sieve retention. If, however, the microorganism is smaller than the pore it encounters, it may be retained by adsorption if the residence time, electrostatic charge, pH, fluid chemistry, and membrane material are conducive to adsorption.

Some genera of microorganisms are deformable, so that at high-pressure differentials or flow rates a microorganism may be forced through a filter pore that is only slightly smaller than the organism. Mollicute bacteria lack cell walls and thus are small and pliable enough to pass through filter membranes under certain conditions.

Grow-through (i.e., passage of microorganisms through a filter as a function of time) may result from one or more scenarios. The microorganism may penetrate as it multiplies: a parent cell divides into two smaller daughter cells that negotiate pore passageways. Penetration can occur with time, because the increasing number of microorganisms overwhelms the few larger pores that are encountered. However, because of the limited time periods typically involved, the controls on bioburden, and the often limited availability of nutrients, grow-through is considered a rare phenomenon in pharmaceutical processes.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

孔隙不是圓柱形

一個關鍵的觀念修正：濾膜的孔隙並非整齊的圓柱管道（除了 track-etched 膜），而是由不規則的多面體結構（pseudopolyhedral structures）組成的彎曲通道。這意味著：

  • 「0.2 µm」是名義孔徑（nominal pore size），不是精確的物理尺寸
  • 實際的截留能力取決於孔徑分佈的整體特性
  • 這也是為什麼完整性測試（Integrity Test）比單純的孔徑量測更能預測微生物截留能力

三種微生物穿透風險

  1. 變形穿透（Deformation）：在高壓差下，某些微生物可被擠壓變形通過孔隙 — 這是為什麼驗證要使用製程壓差條件
  2. Mollicute（支原體類）穿透：缺乏細胞壁的支原體類微生物體積小且柔軟，可能穿過 0.2 µm 濾膜 — 這是為什麼生物製劑製程常使用 0.1 µm 前過濾
  3. 生長穿透（Grow-through）：微生物在濾膜上繁殖後，子代細胞可能穿過孔隙 — 這強調了控制過濾時間和前過濾生物負載的重要性

在 CDMO 環境中，這三種風險都必須在風險評估中被記錄和處理。

Original Text (continued)

Composition and Structure of the Filter Matrix

Several factors related to the filter matrix can affect microbial retention. These include the material from which the filter is made, the pore size and pore-size distribution, whether the membrane is isotropic or anisotropic (i.e., whether membrane pore structure is uniform from face to face or "tapers" from one face to the other), membrane thickness, and whether the filter consists of single or multiple layers.

The membrane material is especially important if adsorption is a significant mechanism. For example, polyamide exhibits stronger microbial adsorption than does cellulose ester.

Microporous membranes that have a relatively wide pore-size distribution are less likely to retain microorganisms, particularly at high challenge levels, than membranes of comparable pore-size ratings with narrower pore-size distributions.

Thicker membranes generally are more retentive than thinner membranes of the same type and pore-size rating. Multi-layered membrane filters exhibit a higher probability of retention than do single-layered membrane filters of the same thickness because the probability of a large pore in one layer being congruent to a large pore in the adjoining layer is negligible.

Composition of the Filtered Solution

The composition of the filtered solution can adversely affect the membrane material if an incompatibility exists, causing damage to the membrane and affecting both retention and physical integrity. In addition, if adsorption is a significant retention mechanism, then solution properties such as pH and the presence of surfactants become important.

Surface charge and ionic strength are important variables. Bacterial and membrane surfaces in aqueous media are negatively charged, resulting in repulsion. High ionic strength allows the surfaces to close to the point where hydrophobic adsorption can occur. Also, high ionic strength can draw water out of the cell, reducing its size, which may lower the probability of retention. Surfactants in concentrations as low as 0.05% have been shown to inhibit adsorption and decrease the retention of latex spheres.

Filtration Conditions

Differential pressure, flow rate, and temperature are among the factors that can affect microbial retention. Hydraulic shock should be avoided, not only because it affects pressure differential and flow rate but also because it can damage the filter. Flow rate is proportional to differential pressure, and higher flow rates reduce adsorption because the contact time is reduced. Microbial retention may be reduced at higher temperatures when these result in higher flow rates due to decreased solution viscosity.

界面活性劑的警告 — 0.05% 的教訓

USP 特別引用了一個關鍵數據：僅 0.05% 的界面活性劑就能抑制吸附機制。對 CDMO 而言，許多生物製劑配方中含有 Polysorbate 80（Tween 80）等界面活性劑作為穩定劑，濃度通常在 0.01%–0.1%。

這意味著你的細菌截留驗證必須使用含有界面活性劑的實際產品配方（或模擬液），而非單純的緩衝液。這也是 PDA TR26 要求使用 "process fluid" 進行驗證的原因。

水錘效應（Hydraulic Shock）的製程風險

USP 特別警告避免水錘效應。在 CDMO 灌裝環境中，以下情況可能產生水錘：

  • 泵浦突然啟停（特別是柱塞泵/蠕動泵）
  • 閥門快速開關
  • 管線中的氣泡突然釋放

水錘不僅降低截留效率，更可能直接損壞濾膜 — 這正是後過濾完整性測試（Post-use Integrity Test）存在的原因之一。

Filter Efficacy: Log Reduction Value — 過濾效能：對數降低值

Original Text

Filtration efficacy can be defined in terms of a log reduction value, which is the logarithm of the quotient produced by dividing the upstream challenge population by the recovered downstream population.

The log reduction value is influenced by the number and size of the challenge microorganisms, the filter design and membrane polymer, the filtration process parameters, and the properties of the solution.

To determine the specific log reduction value of a filter, the challenge test should permit some passage of the test microorganism through the filter in order to produce a denominator. Sterilizing-grade membrane filters should not permit passage of the specified challenge microorganisms for that filter rating. For this reason the log reduction value of sterilizing-grade filters is described as equal to or greater than the log of the challenge population.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

LRV 公式

LRV = log10 (upstream challenge / downstream recovery)

Example:
  Upstream challenge = 1 x 10^7 CFU/cm2
  Downstream recovery = 0 CFU
  LRV ≥ 7 (reported as "≥ 7" since denominator is zero)

對除菌級濾膜而言，LRV 永遠報告為 "≥ log(challenge)" — 因為合格的除菌濾膜不允許任何挑戰菌通過，所以無法計算出精確的 LRV 值。

LRV 與無菌保證水平（SAL）的關係

LRV ≥ 7 聽起來很高，但要注意這是每 cm² 膜面積的值。一個標準 10 吋濾芯（cartridge）的有效過濾面積約 0.7 m²（7,000 cm²），所以總挑戰水平為 107 × 7,000 = 7 × 1010 CFU。

在正常製程中，前過濾生物負載通常 < 10 CFU/100 mL — 這意味著實際操作中的安全裕度極大。這也說明了為什麼 USP 要求嚴格的前過濾生物負載控制。

Section Summary — 本節重點回顧

  • 除菌過濾是生物製劑無菌充填的核心製程，屬於「物理去除」而非「破壞」微生物的方法
  • 除菌級濾膜定義：能截留 107 CFU/cm² B. diminuta（ATCC 19146），經 ASTM F838 測試，標準孔徑為 0.2 µm
  • 兩大截留機制：篩分截留（sieve retention）可靠但受限於孔徑；吸附截留（adsorption）提供額外保護但受溶液組成影響
  • 影響截留的四大因素：孔隙結構、微生物特性、溶液組成（界面活性劑 ≥ 0.05% 即可抑制吸附）、過濾條件（壓差/流速/溫度）
  • LRV ≥ 7 是除菌級濾膜的基準，但實際無菌保證依賴整體系統：前過濾控制 + 濾膜驗證 + 完整性測試 + 無菌操作

Validation — 驗證

Original Text

Microbial retention in sterilizing filtration relies on a combination of sieve retention and adsorption. Validation of sterilizing filtration therefore requires determination of the effect of the liquid on the filter, determination of the effect of the filter on the liquid, and demonstration that the filter can consistently yield sterile solutions under the intended conditions of use.

The liquid to be filtered can affect the pore structure of the membrane, can have electrostatic properties different from the standard challenge suspension used to establish integrity test specifications, and can change the size and shape of the challenge microorganisms. Factors that should be considered when developing a sterilizing-filtration validation protocol include the surface tension, pH, temperature, and osmolarity of the liquid to be filtered; the compatibility of the material or solution components with the filter itself; the pressures, flow rates, and hydraulic shock likely to be encountered; and the maximum filtration time and volume to be filtered. The effect of sterilization (steam, radiation, or gas) on the filter's retention capability also should be considered.

B. diminuta (ATCC 19146) is used as the challenge organism unless it is not viable in the liquid to be filtered. Viability studies should be used to confirm that the liquid has no adverse effects on the challenge organism. If the challenge organism is viable in the liquid to be filtered, the liquid should be inoculated to achieve a challenge level of 1 × 107 cfu/cm², and the filtrate should be evaluated for the presence of the challenge organism.

If B. diminuta is not viable in the liquid, several options are available: (1) modify the liquid to ensure the viability of the challenge organism (e.g., adjust pH or remove the bactericidal component), (2) reduce the exposure time to ensure that the challenge organism remains viable, or (3) change the challenge organism from B. diminuta to one that has been isolated from the liquid to be filtered.

Three different lots of filter membranes should be used for the microbial retention studies. The membranes should have preuse integrity test values that are near the filter manufacturer's specification in order to minimize the possibility that production filters will fail to meet the integrity test value established during the validation exercise.

The sterilization process for the filter, its housing, and associated equipment should be validated. The filter should be sterilized within its housing rather than relying on aseptic assembly following sterilization of the filtration system components. The assembled filtration apparatus can be steam sterilized in an autoclave, using a vacuum cycle, with particular attention to the orientation and wrapping of the housing and any associated tubing to allow condensate drainage and steam penetration. Validated steam sterilization-in-place (SIP) cycles also can be used.

Finally, the scale of the validation approach should be considered. Two approaches are common: (1) a small section of membrane material, typically a 47-mm disk, to validate the microbial retention capability of the membrane; (2) the intended filter configuration, typically a 10-inch cartridge in its housing, to validate the filter system performance in addition to the retention capability. Analysts should consider the volume of the test liquid and operational considerations when choosing which approach to use.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

驗證三要素

USP 要求的除菌過濾驗證必須回答三個問題：

  1. 液體對濾膜的影響 — 相容性測試（Compatibility）：藥液是否會溶解、膨脹或損壞膜材？
  2. 濾膜對液體的影響 — 可萃取物/浸出物（Extractables/Leachables）：濾膜是否會釋放物質到藥液中？
  3. 微生物截留能力 — 細菌挑戰測試（Bacterial Challenge）：在製程條件下能否持續產生無菌濾出液？

B. diminuta 不存活時的三種替代方案

許多生物製劑（特別是含防腐劑或極端 pH 的產品）會殺死 B. diminuta。USP 提供三種解決方案，在 CDMO 環境中的選擇優先順序：

  1. 修改液體（最常用） — 調整 pH 或去除殺菌成分，使 B. diminuta 存活。需科學合理化
  2. 縮短暴露時間 — 減少挑戰菌與藥液接觸時間
  3. 更換挑戰菌（最後手段） — 使用從藥液中分離的微生物作為替代挑戰菌

PDA TR26 對這些替代方案有更詳細的指導，應交叉參考。

三批次 + 邊界規格

3 lots of filter membranes：USP 要求使用三個不同批次的濾膜進行驗證，且這些濾膜的完整性測試值應接近（near）供應商規格下限。這個「near」非常關鍵 — 它確保驗證結果代表「最差情況」（worst-case），而非「最佳情況」。

47-mm disk vs. 10-inch cartridge：

  • 47-mm disk 驗證膜材本身的截留能力，成本低，操作簡便
  • 10-inch cartridge 驗證完整系統（含密封、褶皺、支撐層），更接近實際製程
  • CDMO 最佳實務：先用 disk 確認膜材適用性，再用 cartridge 確認系統性能

濾膜滅菌 — 必須在殼體內

USP 明確要求濾膜應在殼體（housing）內進行滅菌，而非分開滅菌後無菌組裝。這是因為：

  • 無菌組裝存在污染風險
  • 濾芯安裝密封的確認更可靠
  • SIP（Steam-In-Place）是 CDMO 環境中的標準做法
Integrity Testing Principles and Methods — 完整性測試原理與方法

Original Text

Integrity testing can be used to show that a filter has the correct pore-size rating, is installed properly in its housing, and has not been damaged by the process used to sterilize it. These integrity test methods generally rely on detecting gas flow caused by pressure differential across a wetted membrane.

A filter that successfully passes an integrity test based on specifications developed during an effective sterilizing filtration validation study is capable of producing a sterile filtrate.

Integrity test methods for microporous membrane filters include bubble point, diffusive flow, and pressure hold. Membrane type and size and the particular filtration process influence the choice of an appropriate integrity test method. The sensitivity of the bubble point test decreases with increasing filter area because of increasing diffusive flow.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

完整性測試的三重目的

USP 將完整性測試的價值歸納為三項確認：

  1. 正確的孔徑等級 — 確認安裝的確實是 0.2 µm 而非 0.45 µm
  2. 正確安裝 — 確認 O-ring 密封完好、濾芯就位
  3. 未被滅菌損壞 — 確認 SIP 或 autoclave 未破壞濾膜結構

核心原理：所有方法都基於偵測氣體穿過潤濕（wetted）濾膜的行為。潤濕是前提 — 乾燥的濾膜無法進行有效的完整性測試。

Bubble Point — 泡點測試

The bubble point occurs when a gas displaces a wetting liquid from the largest membrane pores, resulting in bulk gas flow through those pores. The flow is evidenced by a steady stream of bubbles through a column of water on the downstream side of the membrane.

The bubble point relates to the pore size of the membrane and the contact angle that the wetting liquid makes with the pore wall. Bubble point is defined as:

P = 4 × γ × cosΘ / D
P = pressure to evacuate the pore
γ = surface tension
Θ = angle of wetting
D = pore diameter

Microbial retention and bubble point correlate: numerous studies have demonstrated that when the microbial reduction ratio is plotted against the bubble point, a line of constant slope results.

The actual bubble point is independent of the membrane surface area, but diffusive flow through high-surface-area filters can mask the true bubble point. The bubble point test is easy to perform on small- to medium-scale filters, the test time is short, and temperature effects are minor. Manual bubble point testing requires manipulation of the downstream side of the filter and is subjective.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

泡點公式解析

P = 4γcosΘ / D

關鍵關係：
  孔徑 D 越小 → 泡點壓力 P 越高
  表面張力 γ 越高 → 泡點壓力 P 越高
  潤濕角 Θ 越小（越親水）→ cosΘ 越大 → P 越高

這就是為什麼 0.2 µm 濾膜的泡點高於 0.45 µm — 孔徑小，需要更高壓力才能將潤濕液從孔隙中排出。

泡點測試的限制

大面積濾膜的難題：在多支濾芯組合的大面積過濾系統中，大量的擴散流（diffusive flow）會遮蔽真正的泡點轉折。這就是為什麼大系統通常使用擴散流測試而非泡點測試。

手動 vs. 自動：手動泡點測試需要觀察下游側的氣泡，具主觀性且需要操作下游管路（增加污染風險）。現代 CDMO 幾乎全面使用自動完整性測試儀（如 Sartorius Sartocheck, Pall Palltronic）。

Diffusive Flow — 擴散流測試

In diffusive flow testing a wetted filter provides a liquid layer across which gas can flow by means of diffusion. Diffusive flow is measured directly at constant pressure.

Diffusive flow is proportional to the differential pressure of the test gas, the diffusivity of the test gas in the wetting liquid, the thickness (depth) of the wetting liquid, the porosity (void volume) of the membrane, and the effective filtration area. Diffusive flow is defined by Fick's Law of Diffusion:

N = [D × H × (p1 − p2)] / (L × ρ)
N = permeation rate (moles of gas per unit time)
D = diffusivity of the gas in the liquid
H = solubility coefficient of the gas
p1 − p2 = transmembrane pressure
L = thickness of liquid in the membrane
ρ = void volume (porosity) of the membrane

Unlike the bubble point test, diffusive flow testing measures gas flow through all the wetted pores, and thus diffusive flow does not correlate directly with microbial retention. However, bacterial challenge tests with a series of filters that have decreasing diffusion rates show that a gas diffusion rate exists below which sterile filtrates are obtained.

Diffusive flow testing is highly sensitive, especially for higher-surface-area membranes. Larger pores or flaws can be detected by a thinning of the liquid layer and correspondingly higher diffusive flow rates. Diffusive flow testing is useful for membranes with small pore sizes (e.g., 0.1 µm and smaller). This test also is highly sensitive to temperature.

溫度敏感性的實務影響

擴散流測試對溫度非常敏感。在 CDMO 環境中，SIP 後的濾膜溫度可能尚未完全降至環境溫度。若在濾膜仍偏熱時進行測試，擴散流會偏高，可能導致假失敗（false failure）。最佳實務是確保測試前濾膜溫度穩定至 ±2°C 以內。

Pressure Hold — 壓力保持測試

The pressure hold integrity test is a variation of the diffusive flow test. The wetted membrane provides a liquid layer across which gas can flow by means of diffusion. The gas flow is proportional to differential pressure and is measured by pressure decay on the upstream side of the membrane.

The rate of pressure decay is influenced by upstream volume of the particular holder-filter combination, valve placement, and tubing volume. Temperature should remain constant because of the relationship between temperature and pressure defined by the ideal gas law.

Conversion of pressure decay test results to diffusive flow values allows correlation with microbial retention. Diffusive flow as it relates to pressure decay is shown as:

Df = [(p1 × Vu) / (p0 × t)] × ln[p1 / (p1 − Δp)]
Df = diffusion
p1 = starting test pressure
Vu = upstream volume of filter system
p0 = atmospheric pressure
t = test time
Δp = p1 − p2 (pressure drop)

The advantages and disadvantages of the pressure-hold test are similar to those of the diffusive flow test. The pressure-hold test has the additional abilities of revealing imperfections in the assembly and sealing of the housing and filter seating and avoiding downstream manipulation. Its disadvantages are that it is strongly influenced by temperature and that accurate measurement of the upstream volume is required.

壓力保持公式解析

Df = [(p1 × Vu) / (p0 × t)] × ln[p1/(p1 - Δp)]

關鍵變數：
  Vu = 上游體積（必須準確量測）
  t = 測試時間（通常 5-10 分鐘）
  Δp = 壓降值 — 越小越好

壓力保持測試的本質是把擴散流轉換為壓降來量測 — 不需要在下游側量測流量，降低了操作複雜度和污染風險。

壓力保持測試的獨特優勢

相較於直接擴散流測試，壓力保持測試有兩個關鍵優勢：

  1. 偵測組裝缺陷：除了濾膜本身，還能偵測殼體密封、O-ring 損壞、濾芯未正確就位等問題
  2. 避免下游操作：無需在下游側連接流量計，減少對已滅菌下游管路的干預 — 這在 PUPSIT 場景中尤為重要

三種測試方法總結比較

方法原理最佳適用主要限制
泡點最大孔排液壓力小面積、快速確認大面積失靈、主觀性

在 CDMO 灌裝環境中，壓力保持和擴散流是最常用的方法，通常由自動完整性測試儀執行。

When to Test Integrity — 完整性測試時機（PUPSIT 議題）

Original Text

The decision not to perform preuse, presterilization integrity testing should be based on a formal risk assessment.

Preuse, poststerilization integrity testing (PUPSIT) may create an unnecessary risk for microbial contamination of the filter and associated downstream tubing and equipment. Preuse, poststerilization integrity testing is unnecessary if effective validation studies have demonstrated that the process for sterilizing the filter does not affect the integrity test value of the filter.

Postfiltration integrity testing should be conducted to ensure that the filter was not damaged during the filtration process.

USP 的立場偏保守（可以不做 PUPSIT），但 EU GMP Annex 1 (2023) 明確要求 PUPSIT。作為服務全球客戶的 CDMO：

  • 歐洲市場產品：必須執行 PUPSIT，或提供科學合理的替代方案並獲得稽查機構認可
  • 美國市場產品：可基於風險評估決定是否執行，但後過濾完整性測試（post-use）是不可或缺的
  • 最佳實務：許多 CDMO 選擇全面實施 PUPSIT，使用自動化測試儀最小化操作風險，以滿足最嚴格的法規要求

後過濾完整性測試 — 唯一的「必須」

無論對 PUPSIT 持何種立場，USP 明確要求後過濾完整性測試（post-use integrity test）是必須執行的。這是回溯性確認：在整個過濾過程中，濾膜始終保持完整。如果後過濾測試失敗，該批次的無菌性即受到質疑，通常需要進行偏差調查。

Prefiltration Bioburden Control — 前過濾生物負載控制

Original Text

The bioburden removal capability depends on the available filter retention capacity, which is a function of the inherent bioburden load present in the entire volume of the liquid to be filtered and the effective filter surface area. Studies have demonstrated that microbial retention in sterilizing filtration is a function of the upstream bioburden.

Also, nonviable particulate matter that may be present in the solution can influence the retentive capacity of the filter. Therefore, the prefiltration bioburden and particulate levels of the solution should be minimized and controlled before the final sterilizing filtration step.

Various filter configurations and processes can be used to control the bioburden and nonviable particulate levels presented to the final, sterilizing-grade filter. One configuration is a multifilter arrangement that consists of two sterilizing-grade filters (or a bioburden reduction filter followed by a sterilizing-grade filter) connected in series (Figure 2).

Another configuration appropriate for prefiltration bioburden control uses two filtration steps separated in time: the liquid is sterile-filtered into a sterilized tank, where it is then held before a final, sterilizing filtration (Figure 3).

When the process results in a consistently low and controlled prefiltration bioburden, use of a single sterilizing-grade filter is appropriate.

EU GMP Annex 1 (2023) 強烈建議在最終除菌過濾前設置冗餘過濾。在 CDMO 環境中：

  • 標準配置：上游 0.2 µm 或 0.45 µm 前過濾 + 最終 0.2 µm 除菌過濾
  • 前過濾的目的：降低最終濾膜的生物負載和微粒負擔，延長使用壽命，降低堵塞風險

Responsibilities — 職責分工

Original Text

Filter Manufacturer

The filter manufacturer is responsible for ensuring that the filter production process has been validated and is well controlled and that the sterilizing-grade filters meet the requirements of ASTM F838-05 (2013). The filter manufacturer determines the integrity test specification for the filters, usually adding a safety factor. The filter manufacturer conducts extractable and leachable studies to ensure that the filter does not release objectionable levels of these materials. The filter manufacturer conducts cleanliness tests to assure that the filter does not adversely affect the USP particulate requirements. The filter manufacturer provides technical support and troubleshooting advice.

Filter User

The filter user is responsible for establishing microbial retention at the validated integrity test value, establishing microbial retention in the liquid to be filtered, and validating the use and sterilization of the filter and housing. The filter user is responsible for determining that the filter is not additive or extractive to the extent that the filtered liquid is adversely affected.

Troubleshooting — 疑難排解

Original Text

Failure of a filter to pass an integrity test may mean that the filter is damaged, improperly sealed in the housing, incompletely wetted, or nonintegral. It also could mean that the filter is incorrectly labeled (e.g., has the wrong pore size) or that the integrity test apparatus has been improperly set up or calibrated.

The cause of an integrity test failure can be determined by evaluating the test setup, test parameters, wetting fluid, and wetting procedure; ensuring that the system is leak-free and the temperature is constant; and ensuring that the test equipment has been properly calibrated.

If the cause of the failure cannot be determined, analysts can rewet the filter and repeat the integrity test, increasing flush time, flush volume, and pressure differential. It may be beneficial to use a lower surface tension reference fluid (e.g., 70% isopropyl alcohol). If the filter fails the integrity test using the reference fluid, it should be considered nonintegral. In addition, the filter can be returned to the manufacturer for a full analysis to further elucidate the cause of the integrity test failure.

完整性測試失敗的排查流程

USP 提供了一個系統性的排查邏輯，CDMO 應建立對應的 SOP：

  1. Step 1 — 確認測試條件：設備校準？測試參數正確？溫度穩定？
  2. Step 2 — 確認潤濕：濾膜是否完全潤濕？沖洗量是否足夠？
  3. Step 3 — 確認系統密封：殼體密封？管線無洩漏？
  4. Step 4 — 重新潤濕並重測：增加沖洗時間、體積和壓差
  5. Step 5 — 使用參考液：改用低表面張力液體（如 70% IPA）重測
  6. Step 6 — 判定不合格：若參考液測試仍失敗，則濾膜確認為不合格
  7. Step 7 — 根本原因分析：可將濾膜退回供應商進行完整分析

潤濕不完全 — 最常見的假失敗原因

在 CDMO 日常操作中，完整性測試失敗最常見的原因是濾膜潤濕不完全，特別是：

  • 疏水性膜材（如 PVDF）需要更長的潤濕時間
  • SIP 後殘留的蒸汽可能造成局部乾燥
  • 大型多支濾芯系統中，部分濾芯可能未均勻潤濕

建立標準化的潤濕程序（包括最小沖洗量和流速）是預防假失敗的最有效措施。
