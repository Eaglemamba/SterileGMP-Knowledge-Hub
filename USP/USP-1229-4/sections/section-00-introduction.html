USP ⟨1229.4⟩ Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids

Section 0: Introduction — Filter Selection, Retention Mechanisms & Factors Affecting Retention
General Chapter • Official as of 01-May-2018 • Pages 1–3

Learning Objectives — 本節學習目標

  • Understand the definition and scope of sterilizing filtration — physical removal, not destruction, of microorganisms
  • Recognize 0.2 µm as the standard pore-size rating and understand the B. diminuta challenge (107 CFU/cm²) per ASTM F838
  • Distinguish sieve retention vs. adsorptive retention and their practical implications
  • Identify factors affecting filter retention: pore structure, microbial properties, solution composition, and filtration conditions
  • Understand filter-solution compatibility and extractables considerations from the filter manufacturer
Introduction — 緒論

Original Text

Sterilization processes are divided broadly into two categories: destruction of microorganisms and their physical removal from the material to be sterilized. Autoclaving is an example of the former, and sterilizing filtration is an example of the latter. The physical removal of microorganisms depends on the bioburden of the solution to be filtered, the properties of the solution, the filtration conditions, and the filter itself.

Sterilizing filtration is a process that can be validated to consistently yield filtrates that are sterile, as defined in Sterilization of Compendial Articles ⟨1229⟩. This chapter provides an overview of (1) various factors that affect the filtration process, (2) the filter integrity test and when to perform it, (3) prefiltration bioburden control, (4) responsibilities of the filter manufacturer and user, and (5) troubleshooting the filtration process.

Multiple factors contribute to the effectiveness of any sterilizing filtration process. These include the type and number of microorganisms, the properties of the liquid, the filter design and membrane polymer, and the filtration process parameters. Properties of the liquid that influence filtration effectiveness include its chemistry, viscosity, surface tension, pH, osmolarity, ionic strength, and temperature, as well as the presence of insoluble materials. Aspects of the filter that affect the filtration include effective filter area, nominal pore size, pore-size distribution, membrane thickness, porosity, membrane polymer, filter pleat density, nonwoven support layers, electrostatic charge, and the hydrophilic nature of the filter membrane. The filtration process parameters that influence microbial retention include temperature, flow rate, volume, filtration time, differential pressure, and pressure pulsations.

Additionally, effective sterilizing filtration depends on (1) the production controls and quality assurance systems used by the filter manufacturer, (2) the qualification and validation studies conducted by, or for, the filter user, (3) effective controls to ensure that prefiltration bioburden and operating parameters remain within validated ranges, and (4) filter integrity. Filter users should ensure that the filtrate remains sterile by using validated sterilization processes for the filtration assembly and all downstream manufacturing equipment and effective aseptic handling of the sterilized materials. Filter users should carefully consider placement of the filter (e.g., proximity to the filling line or hold tank) to minimize the possibility of postfiltration contamination.

"Sterilizing filtration is a process that can be validated to consistently yield filtrates that are sterile."

核心概念 Key Concepts

除菌過濾 vs 滅菌（Sterilizing Filtration vs. Sterilization by Destruction）：

  • 破壞型（Destruction） — 高壓蒸汽滅菌（Autoclaving）、乾熱、輻射等方法殺死微生物
  • 去除型（Physical Removal） — 除菌過濾透過濾膜將微生物截留，不殺死它們

對生物製劑（biologics）而言，蛋白質藥物無法承受高溫滅菌，因此除菌過濾是唯一可行的最終滅菌方式 — 這就是為什麼它被稱為 "THE method for aseptic fill-finish of biologics"。

CDMO 實務觀點

濾膜位置（Filter Placement）至關重要：USP 明確提到濾膜應盡量靠近灌裝線或保存槽。在 CDMO 環境中，這直接影響無菌保證水平（SAL）：

  • 濾膜距灌裝針越近，後過濾污染風險越低
  • 但也意味著需要在 Grade A 環境中操作，增加 PUPSIT 等完整性測試的挑戰
  • 每個客戶產品的製程驗證需個別考量濾膜的最佳位置

Sterilizing-Grade Filters — 除菌級濾膜定義

Original Text

A sterilizing-grade filter is one that is capable of retaining a minimum 1 × 107 cfu of B. diminuta (ATCC 19146) per square centimeter of effective filter area when tested in accordance with ASTM F838-05 (2013), Standard Test Method for Determining Bacterial Retention of Membrane Filters Utilized for Liquid Filtration.

The designation "sterilizing grade" implies a sterilizing action only if other conditions are met, including the integrity test specification established by the filter manufacturer and validated by the user (see Validation, below).

Sterilizing-grade filters typically are microporous membranes that have nominal pore-size ratings of about 0.2 µm. These membranes are fabricated with various materials, have relatively narrow pore-size distributions, and can be integrity tested. The integrity test results can be correlated with microbial retention. Membrane filters that have a nominal pore size of 0.45 µm can be validated to produce sterile filtrates under some conditions; for example, some liquids require high differential pressures to achieve useful flow rates, and these pressures are not suitable for use with 0.2-µm-rated filters. When manufacturers use 0.45-µm filters, they should ensure particularly stringent control of presterilization bioburden.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

B. diminuta 挑戰的黃金標準

Brevundimonas diminuta (ATCC 19146) 是全球公認的除菌級濾膜挑戰微生物：

  • 直徑約 0.3 µm — 是已知最小的可培養細菌之一
  • 挑戰水平：107 CFU/cm² — 每平方公分一千萬個菌
  • 通過標準：濾出液必須完全無菌（零 CFU 通過）

這個定義是除菌過濾驗證的基石，也是 PDA TR26 的核心參考。

0.2 µm vs. 0.22 µm — 有什麼差別？

USP 使用 "about 0.2 µm" 的說法。實務上，不同供應商標示為 0.2 µm 或 0.22 µm，兩者功能上等效，都必須通過 ASTM F838 的 B. diminuta 挑戰測試。

0.45 µm 的例外情境：USP 允許在特定條件下使用 0.45 µm 濾膜（如高黏度液體需要高壓差推動），但要求特別嚴格的前過濾生物負載控制。在 CDMO 實務中，除非客戶有特殊需求且提供完整的科學理由，否則一律使用 0.2 µm。

比喻說明

0.2 µm 除菌級濾膜就像機場安檢的標準 — 「除菌級」不僅僅是一個孔徑規格，而是一整套認證體系：濾膜要通過 ASTM F838 測試（類似機場安檢設備的型式認證），還需要每次使用時的完整性測試（類似每天開機前的安檢設備校準確認）。只有兩者都通過，才能宣稱「除菌級」。

Retention Mechanisms — 截留機制

Original Text

Microporous membranes remove microorganisms from the liquid by two primary mechanisms: sieve retention, which relies on physical blockage of particles that are larger than the pores they encounter, and adsorption, which is a charge-related phenomenon whereby particles are bound to the membrane surfaces. Both mechanisms should be considered during the development, qualification, and validation of sterilizing filtration processes.

Sieve retention, for the most part, is independent of filtration conditions and the microbial challenge level, but the composition of the liquid, temperature, and filtration conditions can affect sieve retention under certain conditions.

Adsorption is a charge-related phenomenon that is influenced by the composition of the membrane, the properties of the filtered liquid, the filtration conditions, and the number and type of microorganisms present in the liquid.

The number and type of microorganisms and other particles present in the material to be filtered can affect retention. Sieve retention implies that every microorganism is larger than the largest pore in the filter. Microorganisms and filter pores are not uniform in size, and some microorganisms may be smaller than the largest pores. Even if a microorganism is smaller than the pore it encounters, it may be retained if a site is available and the filtration conditions are conducive to adsorption. Retention probability is related to the number of microorganisms in the upstream bioburden.

為什麼這對驗證很重要？

當 USP 說「兩種機制都應在驗證中被考量」，這意味著你的細菌截留驗證（Bacterial Retention Validation）必須使用實際產品液（或盡可能接近的替代液），而非單純的水。因為：

  • 某些藥液中的界面活性劑（surfactant）會抑制吸附機制
  • 如果截留主要靠吸附，換了不同配方可能就失效
  • 這是 PDA TR26 Section 6.0 細菌截留驗證章節的核心邏輯
Factors That Affect Retention — 影響截留的因素

Original Text

Nature of Pores

Microporous membranes consist of a polymer matrix that is penetrated by interstices commonly referred to as pores. Compared with those in depth filters, the pores in microporous membranes have a relatively narrow size distribution. The size, number, and shape of the pores determine the filter's retention capabilities. With the exception of the pores in track-etched filter membranes, pores are not cylindrical; they are made up of a series of pseudopolyhedral structures with varying internal diameters.

The pore size, pore-size distribution, and membrane porosity are a function of the manufacturing process. Careful design and control of that process are necessary to ensure that the resulting membrane has the desired integrity test value, microbial retention capability, and uniformity.

Nature of Microorganisms

Microorganisms have a variety of shapes and sizes. If the microorganism encounters a membrane pore that is smaller than its smallest diameter, the microorganism likely will be captured by sieve retention. If, however, the microorganism is smaller than the pore it encounters, it may be retained by adsorption if the residence time, electrostatic charge, pH, fluid chemistry, and membrane material are conducive to adsorption.

Some genera of microorganisms are deformable, so that at high-pressure differentials or flow rates a microorganism may be forced through a filter pore that is only slightly smaller than the organism. Mollicute bacteria lack cell walls and thus are small and pliable enough to pass through filter membranes under certain conditions.

Grow-through (i.e., passage of microorganisms through a filter as a function of time) may result from one or more scenarios. The microorganism may penetrate as it multiplies: a parent cell divides into two smaller daughter cells that negotiate pore passageways. Penetration can occur with time, because the increasing number of microorganisms overwhelms the few larger pores that are encountered. However, because of the limited time periods typically involved, the controls on bioburden, and the often limited availability of nutrients, grow-through is considered a rare phenomenon in pharmaceutical processes.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

孔隙不是圓柱形

一個關鍵的觀念修正：濾膜的孔隙並非整齊的圓柱管道（除了 track-etched 膜），而是由不規則的多面體結構（pseudopolyhedral structures）組成的彎曲通道。這意味著：

  • 「0.2 µm」是名義孔徑（nominal pore size），不是精確的物理尺寸
  • 實際的截留能力取決於孔徑分佈的整體特性
  • 這也是為什麼完整性測試（Integrity Test）比單純的孔徑量測更能預測微生物截留能力

三種微生物穿透風險

  1. 變形穿透（Deformation）：在高壓差下，某些微生物可被擠壓變形通過孔隙 — 這是為什麼驗證要使用製程壓差條件
  2. Mollicute（支原體類）穿透：缺乏細胞壁的支原體類微生物體積小且柔軟，可能穿過 0.2 µm 濾膜 — 這是為什麼生物製劑製程常使用 0.1 µm 前過濾
  3. 生長穿透（Grow-through）：微生物在濾膜上繁殖後，子代細胞可能穿過孔隙 — 這強調了控制過濾時間和前過濾生物負載的重要性

在 CDMO 環境中，這三種風險都必須在風險評估中被記錄和處理。

Original Text (continued)

Composition and Structure of the Filter Matrix

Several factors related to the filter matrix can affect microbial retention. These include the material from which the filter is made, the pore size and pore-size distribution, whether the membrane is isotropic or anisotropic (i.e., whether membrane pore structure is uniform from face to face or "tapers" from one face to the other), membrane thickness, and whether the filter consists of single or multiple layers.

The membrane material is especially important if adsorption is a significant mechanism. For example, polyamide exhibits stronger microbial adsorption than does cellulose ester.

Microporous membranes that have a relatively wide pore-size distribution are less likely to retain microorganisms, particularly at high challenge levels, than membranes of comparable pore-size ratings with narrower pore-size distributions.

Thicker membranes generally are more retentive than thinner membranes of the same type and pore-size rating. Multi-layered membrane filters exhibit a higher probability of retention than do single-layered membrane filters of the same thickness because the probability of a large pore in one layer being congruent to a large pore in the adjoining layer is negligible.

Composition of the Filtered Solution

The composition of the filtered solution can adversely affect the membrane material if an incompatibility exists, causing damage to the membrane and affecting both retention and physical integrity. In addition, if adsorption is a significant retention mechanism, then solution properties such as pH and the presence of surfactants become important.

Surface charge and ionic strength are important variables. Bacterial and membrane surfaces in aqueous media are negatively charged, resulting in repulsion. High ionic strength allows the surfaces to close to the point where hydrophobic adsorption can occur. Also, high ionic strength can draw water out of the cell, reducing its size, which may lower the probability of retention. Surfactants in concentrations as low as 0.05% have been shown to inhibit adsorption and decrease the retention of latex spheres.

Filtration Conditions

Differential pressure, flow rate, and temperature are among the factors that can affect microbial retention. Hydraulic shock should be avoided, not only because it affects pressure differential and flow rate but also because it can damage the filter. Flow rate is proportional to differential pressure, and higher flow rates reduce adsorption because the contact time is reduced. Microbial retention may be reduced at higher temperatures when these result in higher flow rates due to decreased solution viscosity.

界面活性劑的警告 — 0.05% 的教訓

USP 特別引用了一個關鍵數據：僅 0.05% 的界面活性劑就能抑制吸附機制。對 CDMO 而言，許多生物製劑配方中含有 Polysorbate 80（Tween 80）等界面活性劑作為穩定劑，濃度通常在 0.01%–0.1%。

這意味著你的細菌截留驗證必須使用含有界面活性劑的實際產品配方（或模擬液），而非單純的緩衝液。這也是 PDA TR26 要求使用 "process fluid" 進行驗證的原因。

水錘效應（Hydraulic Shock）的製程風險

USP 特別警告避免水錘效應。在 CDMO 灌裝環境中，以下情況可能產生水錘：

  • 泵浦突然啟停（特別是柱塞泵/蠕動泵）
  • 閥門快速開關
  • 管線中的氣泡突然釋放

水錘不僅降低截留效率，更可能直接損壞濾膜 — 這正是後過濾完整性測試（Post-use Integrity Test）存在的原因之一。

Filter Efficacy: Log Reduction Value — 過濾效能：對數降低值

Original Text

Filtration efficacy can be defined in terms of a log reduction value, which is the logarithm of the quotient produced by dividing the upstream challenge population by the recovered downstream population.

The log reduction value is influenced by the number and size of the challenge microorganisms, the filter design and membrane polymer, the filtration process parameters, and the properties of the solution.

To determine the specific log reduction value of a filter, the challenge test should permit some passage of the test microorganism through the filter in order to produce a denominator. Sterilizing-grade membrane filters should not permit passage of the specified challenge microorganisms for that filter rating. For this reason the log reduction value of sterilizing-grade filters is described as equal to or greater than the log of the challenge population.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

LRV 公式

LRV = log10 (upstream challenge / downstream recovery)

Example:
  Upstream challenge = 1 x 10^7 CFU/cm2
  Downstream recovery = 0 CFU
  LRV ≥ 7 (reported as "≥ 7" since denominator is zero)

對除菌級濾膜而言，LRV 永遠報告為 "≥ log(challenge)" — 因為合格的除菌濾膜不允許任何挑戰菌通過，所以無法計算出精確的 LRV 值。

LRV 與無菌保證水平（SAL）的關係

LRV ≥ 7 聽起來很高，但要注意這是每 cm² 膜面積的值。一個標準 10 吋濾芯（cartridge）的有效過濾面積約 0.7 m²（7,000 cm²），所以總挑戰水平為 107 × 7,000 = 7 × 1010 CFU。

在正常製程中，前過濾生物負載通常 < 10 CFU/100 mL — 這意味著實際操作中的安全裕度極大。這也說明了為什麼 USP 要求嚴格的前過濾生物負載控制。

Section Summary — 本節重點回顧

  • 除菌過濾是生物製劑無菌充填的核心製程，屬於「物理去除」而非「破壞」微生物的方法
  • 除菌級濾膜定義：能截留 107 CFU/cm² B. diminuta（ATCC 19146），經 ASTM F838 測試，標準孔徑為 0.2 µm
  • 兩大截留機制：篩分截留（sieve retention）可靠但受限於孔徑；吸附截留（adsorption）提供額外保護但受溶液組成影響
  • 影響截留的四大因素：孔隙結構、微生物特性、溶液組成（界面活性劑 ≥ 0.05% 即可抑制吸附）、過濾條件（壓差/流速/溫度）
  • LRV ≥ 7 是除菌級濾膜的基準，但實際無菌保證依賴整體系統：前過濾控制 + 濾膜驗證 + 完整性測試 + 無菌操作
