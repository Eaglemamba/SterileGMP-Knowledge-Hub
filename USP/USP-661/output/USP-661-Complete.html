0.1 Introduction & Purpose | 章節目的與背景

原文內容 Original Text

〈661〉 PLASTIC PACKAGING SYSTEMS AND THEIR MATERIALS OF CONSTRUCTION

(If the option of early adoption of Plastic Materials of Construction 〈661.1〉 and Plastic Packaging Systems for Pharmaceutical Use 〈661.2〉 is not used prior to December 1, 2025, the requirements under this chapter will apply.)

INTRODUCTION

The purpose of this chapter is to provide standards for plastic articles (materials, components, and systems) used to package medical articles (pharmaceuticals, biologics, and dietary supplements). Definitions that apply to this chapter are provided in Packaging and Storage Requirements 〈659〉. Standards and tests for the functional properties of containers and their components are provided in Containers—Performance Testing 〈671〉.

Plastics are composed of a mixture of homologous polymers, having a range of molecular weights. Plastics may contain other substances such as residues from the polymerization process and additives such as plasticizers, stabilizers, antioxidants, pigments, and lubricants.

Plastic articles are identified and characterized by infrared (IR) spectroscopy and differential scanning calorimetry (DSC). Standards are provided in this chapter for the identification and characterization of the different types of plastic, and the test procedures are provided at the end of the chapter. Furthermore, plastics are characterized to establish that they are suited for their intended use.

Plastics and their additives, regardless of their risk and application, must meet the requirements for food contact as provided in Title 21 of the US Code of Federal Regulations (CFR). Ingredients added to these items must conform to the requirements in the applicable sections of 21 CFR Part 174—Indirect Food Additives: General, or have been evaluated by the FDA and determined to be acceptable substances for the intended use.

Physicochemical tests are performed on plastics used in high risk applications (inhalation, parenteral preparation, and ophthalmics) to characterize the extracted components and identify possible migrants. To this end, resin-specific extraction tests are provided in this chapter for polyethylene, polypropylene, polyethylene terephthalate, and polyethylene terephthalate G used in such high risk applications. Test all other plastics as directed for Physicochemical Tests. Conduct the Buffering Capacity test only when the containers are intended to hold a liquid product.

Physicochemical Tests are not required for articles used in low risk applications (those intended for oral or topical administration). The degree or extent of biological testing is also dependent on the intended use of the plastic article and the degree of risk that extracted substances could adversely impact the efficacy of the compendial article.

Plastic articles used for high risk products, such as those intended for inhalation, parenteral preparation, and ophthalmics, are tested using Biological Tests. Plastic articles used for low risk products, such as those intended for oral or topical administration, do not require Biological Tests.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Plastic Articles (塑料物件)：在 USP 〈661〉 中涵蓋三個層次：
1. Materials（材料）：原始樹脂，如 PE、PP、PET
2. Components（組件）：成型後的個別零件，如瓶身、瓶蓋
3. Systems（系統）：完整的容器密封系統

理解這三層次很重要，因為測試要求可能因層次不同而有所差異。〈661.1〉專注 Materials，〈661.2〉專注 Systems，而現行 〈661〉 則整合三者。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像塑料包裝就像餐廳的餐具選擇：材料是製作餐盤的陶瓷或玻璃原料，組件是成品餐盤本身，系統則是完整的餐具組合（盤、碗、杯、蓋）。

高風險應用（注射劑）就像醫院手術室使用的器皿：必須通過嚴格的化學與生物相容性測試。低風險應用（口服錠劑）就像一般餐廳餐具：有基本衛生標準，但不需達到手術室等級。

重點提示 Key Notes

風險分層原則（Risk-based Approach）：

  • 高風險（High Risk）：吸入劑、注射劑、眼用製劑 → 需要 Physicochemical Tests + Biological Tests
  • 低風險（Low Risk）：口服、外用製劑 → 豁免 Physicochemical Tests 及 Biological Tests

這是監管效率的體現：不需要對每瓶維他命 C 錠劑的塑料瓶做注射劑等級的測試。但若包裝改變了藥物的療效或安全性，就必須提升測試等級。

章節框架解析 Chapter Architecture

USP 〈661〉 是一個「傘型章節」，其下有三個姊妹章節：

  • 〈661.1〉 Plastic Materials of Construction — 材料層次測試
  • 〈661.2〉 Plastic Packaging Systems for Pharmaceutical Use — 系統層次測試
  • 〈1661〉 Evaluation of Plastic Packaging Systems — 指導性資訊章節

自 2025 年 12 月 1 日起，廠商可選擇採用 〈661.1〉/〈661.2〉 替代現行 〈661〉。若未早期採用，〈661〉 仍為有效標準。

0.2 Polyethylene Containers | 聚乙烯容器

原文內容 Original Text

POLYETHYLENE CONTAINERS

Scope

The standards and tests provided in this section characterize containers and components, produced from either low-density polyethylene (LDPE) or high-density polyethylene (HDPE) of either homopolymer or copolymer resins that are interchangeably suitable for packaging dry oral dosage forms not meant for constitution into solution.

All polyethylene components are subject to testing by IR spectroscopy and DSC. Where stability studies have been performed to establish the expiration date of a particular dosage form in the appropriate polyethylene container, then any other polyethylene container meeting these requirements may be similarly used to package such a dosage form, provided that the appropriate stability programs are expanded to include the alternative container.

Background

High-density and low-density polyethylene are long-chain polymers synthesized under controlled conditions of heat and pressure, with the aid of catalysts from NLT 85.0% ethylene and NLT 95.0% total olefins. Other olefin ingredients that are most frequently used are butene, hexene, and propylene.

High-density polyethylene has a density between 0.941 and 0.965 g/cm³. Low-density polyethylene has a density between 0.850 and 0.940 g/cm³. Other properties that may affect the suitability of polyethylene include modulus of elasticity, melt index, environmental stress crack resistance, and degree of crystallinity after molding.

High-Density Polyethylene — Identification Tests

Infrared spectroscopy: Proceed as directed for Multiple Internal Reflectance. The corrected spectrum of the specimen exhibits major absorption bands only at the same wavelengths as the spectrum of USP High-Density Polyethylene RS.

Differential scanning calorimetry: Proceed as directed for Thermal Analysis. The melting peak temperature obtained from the thermal analysis curve of the specimen does not differ from that of the Reference Standard by more than 6.0°.

HDPE — Heavy Metals and Nonvolatile Residue

For each 20.0 mL of Extracting medium, the portion shall be 60 cm², regardless of thickness.

Nonvolatile residue limits:

  • Water at 70°: NMT 12.0 mg
  • Alcohol at 70°: NMT 75.0 mg
  • Hexanes at 50°: NMT 100.0 mg

Low-Density Polyethylene — Identification & Residue Tests

DSC: melting peak temperature shall not differ from USP Low-Density Polyethylene RS by more than 8.0°.

Nonvolatile residue limits:

  • Water at 70°: NMT 12.0 mg
  • Alcohol at 70°: NMT 75.0 mg
  • Hexanes at 50°: NMT 350.0 mg

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

HDPE vs LDPE（高密度聚乙烯 vs 低密度聚乙烯）：

密度差異源自分子鏈的分支程度。HDPE 分支少，鏈排列緊密 → 密度高（0.941–0.965 g/cm³）、剛性強、阻隔性佳。LDPE 分支多 → 密度低（0.850–0.940 g/cm³）、柔軟有彈性，適合擠壓瓶。

在藥品包裝中，HDPE 常見於藥錠瓶（如 OTC 藥品），LDPE 常見於滴眼液瓶、軟管。

數值比較 Specification Comparison

殘留物限值對比 (Nonvolatile Residue Limits):

萃取介質         HDPE限值    LDPE限值
────────────────────────────────
水（70°）         ≤12.0 mg    ≤12.0 mg
酒精（70°）       ≤75.0 mg    ≤75.0 mg
正己烷（50°）    ≤100.0 mg   ≤350.0 mg

關鍵差異：LDPE 在正己烷的限值是 HDPE 的 3.5 倍，
反映 LDPE 因結構疏鬆，對非極性溶劑的萃取量更大。

比喻說明 Analogy

HDPE 的分子結構就像整齊排列的竹籤（直鏈），密度高，外來物質難以滲入。LDPE 則像一堆彎曲的鐵絲（有分支），空隙多，溶劑萃取出的成分也相對較多。

正因如此，LDPE 在正己烷（非極性有機溶劑）的殘留限值比 HDPE 寬鬆 3.5 倍，因為 USP 認知到這是 LDPE 材料的固有特性。

重點提示 Key Notes

互換性原則（Interchangeability）：當穩定性試驗已建立某一 PE 容器的有效期後，符合相同規格的其他 PE 容器可互換使用，但前提是：必須將替代容器納入穩定性計畫擴充評估。

這對 CDMO 客戶服務非常實用：當原定容器短缺時，可快速切換到等效容器，但必須有數據支撐。

  2. 一個藥廠欲將某口服固體製劑從 HDPE 容器更換為 LDPE 容器，需要做哪些評估工作？
0.3 Polypropylene Containers | 聚丙烯容器

原文內容 Original Text

POLYPROPYLENE CONTAINERS

Scope

The standards and tests provided in this section characterize polypropylene containers, produced from either homopolymers or copolymers, that are interchangeably suitable for packaging dry solid and liquid oral dosage forms.

Background

Propylene polymers are long-chain polymers synthesized from propylene or propylene and other olefins under controlled conditions of heat and pressure, with the aid of catalysts. Examples of other olefins most commonly used include ethylene and butene. The propylene polymers, the ingredients used to manufacture the propylene polymers, and the ingredients used in the fabrication of the containers conform to the applicable sections of 21 CFR.

Factors such as plastic composition, processing and cleaning procedures, contacting media, inks, adhesives, absorption, adsorption and permeability of preservatives, and conditions of storage may also affect the suitability of a plastic for a specific use.

Polypropylene has a distinctive IR spectrum and possesses characteristic thermal properties. It has a density between 0.880 and 0.913 g/cm³. The permeation properties of molded polypropylene containers may be altered when reground polymer is incorporated, depending on the proportion of regrind in the final product.

Other properties that may affect the suitability of polypropylene: oxygen and moisture permeability, modulus of elasticity, melt flow index, environmental stress crack resistance, and degree of crystallinity after molding.

Identification Tests

Infrared Spectroscopy: The corrected spectrum exhibits major absorption bands only at the same wavelengths as USP Homopolymer Polypropylene RS or copolymer polypropylene standard.

Differential Scanning Calorimetry: The melting peak temperature does not differ from that of USP Homopolymer Polypropylene RS by more than 12.0°.

Heavy Metals and Nonvolatile Residue

For each 20 mL of Extracting medium the portion shall be 60 cm², regardless of thickness.

Nonvolatile residue limits:

  • Water at 70°: NMT 10.0 mg
  • Alcohol at 70°: NMT 60.0 mg
  • Hexanes at 50°: NMT 225.0 mg
[Note—Hexanes and alcohol are flammable. When evaporating these solvents, use a current of air with the water bath; when drying the residue, use an explosion-proof oven.]

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Polypropylene (聚丙烯，PP)：由丙烯（propylene）單體聚合而成，密度 0.880–0.913 g/cm³，比 PE 更輕，化學穩定性優異，熱穩定性高（可耐受高壓蒸汽滅菌）。

PP 的 DSC 熔點容許偏差（12.0°）比 HDPE（6.0°）和 LDPE（8.0°）都大，因為 PP 的結晶性受製程影響較大（均聚物 vs 共聚物間差異較顯著）。

重點提示 Key Notes

再研磨料（Regrind）的影響：加入再研磨料（回收加工的 PP）會改變容器的滲透特性，影響阻隔性。USP 明確提醒這一點，因為部分製造商會用再研磨料降低成本，但必須確認不影響材料規格的符合性。

這與 GMP 的材料一致性要求直接相關：若更換供應商或調整再研磨料比例，需重新驗證。

比喻說明 Analogy

PP 容器就像高壓鍋的密封圈：能承受高溫高壓，化學惰性好，既可用於乾燥固體（密封藥錠），也可用於液體口服製劑。

「再研磨料」的效果就像在新鮮麵糰中混入剩餘麵糰：比例少時影響不大，但比例過高時質地（物理屏障特性）就可能改變。

材料密度速查 Density Reference

塑料類型      密度範圍（g/cm³）   DSC容許偏差
──────────────────────────────────
HDPE          0.941–0.965         ±6.0°
LDPE          0.850–0.940         ±8.0°
PP            0.880–0.913         ±12.0°
PET           1.3–1.4             ±4.0°
PETG          ~1.27               ±6.0°

密度越高，通常分子排列越緊密，阻隔性越佳。

0.4 PET and PETG Containers | 聚對苯二甲酸乙二酯容器

原文內容 Original Text

POLYETHYLENE TEREPHTHALATE BOTTLES AND POLYETHYLENE TEREPHTHALATE G CONTAINERS

Scope

The standards and tests provided in this section characterize polyethylene terephthalate (PET) and polyethylene terephthalate G (PETG) bottles that are interchangeably suitable for packaging liquid oral dosage forms.

Background — PET

PET resins are long-chain crystalline polymers prepared by the condensation of ethylene glycol with dimethyl terephthalate or terephthalic acid. PET copolymer resins may also contain a small amount of either isophthalic acid (NMT 3 mol%) or 1,4-cyclohexanedimethanol (NMT 5 mol%).

PET and PET copolymer resins exhibit a large degree of order in their molecular structure. Characteristic properties: glass transition temperature ~76°, melting temperature ~250°. Density between 1.3 and 1.4 g/cm³; minimum intrinsic viscosity 0.7 dL/g (corresponds to number average molecular weight of ~23,000 Da).

Background — PETG

PETG resins are prepared by condensation of ethylene glycol with dimethyl terephthalate or terephthalic acid and 15–34 mol% of 1,4-cyclohexanedimethanol (CHDM). PETG resins are clear, amorphous polymers, having a glass transition temperature of about 81° and no crystalline melting point as determined by DSC. Density ~1.27 g/cm³; minimum intrinsic viscosity 0.65 dL/g (~16,000 Da).

PET and PETG resins do not contain any plasticizers, processing aids, or antioxidants. Colorants, if used, do not migrate into the contained liquid.

Colorant Extraction Test

Fill 2 test bottles: 50% alcohol for PET; 25% alcohol for PETG. Incubate at 49° for 10 days. Determine absorbances at the wavelength of maximum absorbance: the absorbance values are less than 0.01 for both test solutions.

Extracting Media for PET/PETG

  • Purified Water
  • 50% alcohol (for PET) / 25% alcohol (for PETG)
  • n-Heptane

General procedure: fill bottles to 90% nominal capacity; incubate at 49° for 10 days.

Key Analyte Limits

  • Heavy metals: NMT 1 ppm in extract
  • Total terephthaloyl moieties: NMT 0.150 absorbance (≤1 ppm) in alcohol and n-heptane extracts
  • Ethylene glycol: NMT 1 ppm in Purified Water extract
  • Colorant extraction: absorbance <0.01

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

PET vs PETG 的結構差異：

PET（聚對苯二甲酸乙二酯）是半結晶性聚合物，具有明確的熔點（~250°）和玻璃轉化溫度（Tg ~76°）。透明度佳，廣泛用於飲料瓶（可口可樂瓶即為 PET）。

PETG 在結構中引入大量 CHDM（15–34 mol%），破壞了結晶性，成為無定形透明聚合物，無結晶熔點，Tg ~81°。PETG 更易於加工成複雜形狀，透明度更高，但熱穩定性略低於 PET。

比喻說明 Analogy

PET 就像普通玻璃窗（有結晶結構，清透但稍帶色澤）；PETG 則像光學玻璃（無定形，透明度更高，更容易切割成複雜形狀）。兩者都透明，但 PETG 的加工彈性更大，適合藥廠需要特殊瓶型的情況。

重點提示 Key Notes

三種特定分析物的意義：

  • Total Terephthaloyl Moieties（對苯二甲酸基團）：PET/PETG 降解或水解的直接產物，是聚合物完整性的指標
  • Ethylene Glycol（乙二醇）：PET/PETG 合成原料之一，過量殘留具潛在毒性
  • Colorant Extraction（色素萃取）：確保任何添加色素不會遷移進入藥品

這三項測試是 PET/PETG 特有的，反映其化學組成的特殊性。萃取介質的不同（水、醇、正庚烷）模擬不同的食品/藥品基質環境。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 環境中，客戶可能要求使用 PET 或 PETG 容器包裝液體口服製劑（如糖漿、口服液）。評估要點：

  • 確認容器供應商提供符合 USP 〈661〉 的 CoA（Certificate of Analysis）
  • 若產品含有酒精溶劑，注意使用對應的萃取介質（PET 用 50% 醇，PETG 用 25% 醇）
  • 評估標籤/印刷油墨的色素遷移風險
  • 高溫儲存條件（如熱帶地區市場）需考慮 Tg（~76–81°）是否有實際影響

  2. PET 和 PETG 瓶使用不同濃度酒精（50% vs 25%）作為萃取介質，原因是什麼？
  3. Intrinsic viscosity（特性黏度）0.7 dL/g 對應分子量 ~23,000 Da，這個數值如何影響 PET 的機械強度？
0.5 New Scope (Dec 2025 Text) | 2025年12月更新範圍

原文內容 Original Text

INTRODUCTION (Official 1-Dec-2025)

Packaging systems used for drug products (pharmaceuticals, biologics) are defined in Packaging and Storage Requirements 〈659〉 and such systems may be constructed from plastic materials and components. Plastics are made of polymers, which can have a range of molecular weights and may contain other substances such as residues from the polymerization process, plasticizers, stabilizers, antioxidants, pigments, and lubricants.

Drug products will be in direct contact with the primary packaging component and may result in an interaction between the product and the packaging system. Such an interaction should not adversely affect the drug product, and this will be facilitated by the use of well-characterized plastic materials of construction in components and packaging systems and by the appropriate testing of packaging systems.

SCOPE (Official 1-Dec-2025)

Establishing the suitability of plastic packaging systems for drug products involves multiple tests and testing procedures, as briefly outlined below:

  • Material screening—characterization of a packaging system's materials of construction to evaluate ingredients as probable extractables and potential leachables. Such a characterization facilitates the identification of materials that are suitable for use in packaging systems.
  • Controlled extraction (simulation) study—worst-case controlled extraction (simulation) study to determine the extent to which extractables may become probable leachables (for additional information, see Assessment of Extractables Associated with Pharmaceutical Packaging/Delivery Systems 〈1663〉).
  • Product assessment—actual-case measurement of confirmed leachables in the drug product in the pharmaceutical packaging/delivery system intended for the commercial market (for additional information, see Assessment of Drug Product Leachables Associated with Pharmaceutical Packaging/Delivery Systems 〈1664〉).

Additionally, information provided by the vendor(s) of plastic packaging systems and their associated materials or components of construction can facilitate suitability for use assessments.

The testing of materials of construction used in packaging systems is addressed in Plastic Materials of Construction 〈661.1〉. A product's packaging component or system is deemed suited for its intended use, if it meets the requirements in 〈661.2〉.

For more information on the scope of, applicability of, and other topics related to the 〈661〉 suite of general chapters, see Evaluation of Plastic Packaging Systems for Pharmaceutical Use and Their Materials of Construction 〈1661〉.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

三層次評估框架（Three-tier Evaluation Framework）：

2025年12月新版 〈661〉 引入了更現代化的 E&L（Extractables and Leachables，可萃取物與可浸出物）評估框架：

  • Material Screening（材料篩選）：識別材料中的潛在 Extractables → 可能成為 Leachables 的物質
  • Controlled Extraction Study（控制萃取研究）：最壞情況下的萃取，確定 Extractables 的範圍（參見 〈1663〉）
  • Product Assessment（產品評估）：實際商業條件下測量 Leachables（參見 〈1664〉）

比喻說明 Analogy

這三層次就像食品安全檢驗的三道關卡：材料篩選是「原料檢驗」（確認食材安全），控制萃取研究是「烹飪模擬測試」（最惡劣條件下會釋出什麼），產品評估是「最終產品上市前的成品檢驗」（實際食品中真正含有什麼）。

重點提示 Key Notes

Extractables vs Leachables 的核心區別：

  • Extractables（可萃取物）：在極端/加速條件下從包裝材料中萃取出的物質，代表最壞情況的「潛在威脅清單」
  • Leachables（可浸出物）：在實際儲存條件下真正遷移進入藥品的物質，是真實的患者暴露風險

監管機構（FDA、EMA）的審查重點是 Leachables，但 Extractables 研究是預測 Leachables 的必要前提。ICH Q3E（E&L 指導原則）對此有詳細說明。

實務應用 Practical Application

對於 CDMO 而言，新版 〈661〉 框架意味著容器密封系統（Container-Closure System, CCS）的資質評估要更系統化：

  • 向供應商索取 Material Screening 報告（含 Extractables profile）
  • 依據劑型和給藥途徑決定是否需要執行 Controlled Extraction Study
  • 注射劑/吸入劑必須完成 Product Assessment（實際產品中的 Leachables 測定）
  • 善用 〈661.1〉/〈661.2〉 的數據可能作為 〈661〉 測試的替代方案

1.1 Multiple Internal Reflectance (IR Spectroscopy) | 多重內部反射 IR 光譜

原文內容 Original Text

TEST METHODS

Multiple Internal Reflectance

Apparatus: Use an IR spectrophotometer capable of correcting for the blank spectrum and equipped with a multiple internal reflectance accessory.

Specimen Preparation: Cut 2 flat sections representative of the average wall thickness of the container, and trim them as necessary to obtain segments that are convenient for mounting in the multiple internal reflectance accessory. Taking care to avoid scratching the surfaces, wipe the specimens with dry paper or, if necessary, clean them with a soft cloth dampened with methanol, and permit them to dry. Securely mount the specimens ensuring adequate surface contact. Prior to mounting the specimens on the plate, they may be compressed to thin uniform films by exposing them to temperatures of about 177° under high pressures (15,000 psi or more).

General Procedure: Place the mounted specimen sections within the multiple internal reflectance accessory, and place the assembly in the specimen beam of the IR spectrophotometer. Adjust the specimen position and mirrors within the accessory to permit maximum light transmission of the unattenuated reference beam. Determine the IR spectrum from 3500 to 600 cm⁻¹ for polyethylene and polypropylene and from 4000 to 400 cm⁻¹ for PET and PETG.

"The corrected spectrum of the specimen exhibits major absorption bands only at the same wavelengths as the spectrum of the USP Reference Standard."

Scanning Ranges by Resin Type

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Multiple Internal Reflectance (MIR，多重內部反射)，又稱 ATR-FTIR：這是一種表面分析技術，IR 光線在樣品與晶體界面多次反射（通常 2–25 次），累積吸收信號，使薄層材料也能獲得足夠強度的光譜。

與傳統透射 IR 相比，MIR 不需要將樣品壓成薄膜，操作更方便，是固體塑料容器鑑別的首選方法。

PET/PETG 使用更寬的掃描範圍（4000–400 cm⁻¹ vs 3500–600 cm⁻¹），因為其苯環結構和酯鍵特徵吸收峰部分落在 600–400 cm⁻¹ 的低頻區域。

比喻說明 Analogy

IR 光譜就像每種塑料的「分子指紋」：每種化學鍵（C-H、C=O、O-H 等）在特定波數吸收 IR 光，產生獨特的吸收峰圖案。就像每個人的指紋不同，每種聚合物的 IR 光譜也是唯一的。

USP 參考標準品（Reference Standard, RS）就是「標準指紋庫」，測試樣品的光譜必須與 RS 的主要吸收峰位置一致，才能確認材料身份。

重點提示 Key Notes

樣品製備的關鍵細節：

  • 切取「代表平均壁厚」的截面，確保測試的代表性
  • 避免刮傷表面（表面損傷影響 MIR 的接觸品質）
  • 可用甲醇輕拭清潔，但需充分乾燥
  • 需要時可在 177° / 15,000 psi 以上壓成薄膜，以確保與晶體的密切接觸

「Major absorption bands only at the same wavelengths」—注意是「主要吸收帶」在相同波長，允許次要峰的微小差異（不同批次/供應商可能略有不同）。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼 PET/PETG 的 IR 掃描範圍需要延伸到 400 cm⁻¹，而 PE/PP 只需要到 600 cm⁻¹？
  2. 如果 IR 光譜與 USP RS 的吸收帶位置不一致，可能的原因有哪些？樣品製備問題、材料問題、還是儀器問題？
  3. 177° / 15,000 psi 壓膜的目的是什麼？在什麼情況下需要這個步驟？
1.2 Thermal Analysis (DSC) | 熱分析（差示掃描量熱法）

原文內容 Original Text

Thermal Analysis

General Procedure: Cut a section weighing about 12 mg, and place it in the test specimen pan. [Note—Intimate contact between the pan and the thermocouple is essential for reproducible results.] Determine thermal analysis curve under nitrogen, using the heating and cooling conditions as specified for the resin type and using equipment capable of performing the determinations as specified under Thermal Analysis 〈891〉.

For Polyethylene

Determine the thermal analysis curve under nitrogen at temperatures between 40° and 200° at a heating rate between 2° and 10°/min followed by cooling at a rate between 2° and 10°/min to 40°.

For Polypropylene

Determine the thermal analysis curve under nitrogen at temperatures ranging from ambient to 30° above the melting point. Maintain the temperature for 10 min, then cool to 50° below the peak crystallization temperature at a rate of 10° to 20°/min.

For Polyethylene Terephthalate

Heat the specimen from room temperature to 280° at a heating rate of about 20°/min. Hold the specimen at 280° for 1 min. Quickly cool the specimen to room temperature, and reheat it to 280° at a heating rate of about 5°/min.

For Polyethylene Terephthalate G

Heat the specimen from room temperature to 120° at a heating rate of about 20°/min. Hold the specimen at 120° for 1 min. Quickly cool the specimen to room temperature, and reheat it to 120° at a heating rate of about 10°/min.

DSC Melting Point Tolerance Summary

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC，差示掃描量熱法)：測量材料在升溫或降溫過程中吸放熱量的變化，可識別：

  • 熔點（Tm）：結晶性聚合物特有，如 HDPE、LDPE、PP、PET 均有明確的熔融峰
  • 玻璃轉化溫度（Tg）：無定形或半晶材料在 Tg 以下為玻璃態，以上為橡膠態
  • 結晶化溫度（Tc）：降溫時結晶形成的放熱峰

PETG 因無定形結構，沒有熔點峰，這本身就是鑑別的特徵之一。

DSC 程序對比表 DSC Protocol Comparison

樹脂    溫度範圍         加熱速率    特殊步驟
──────────────────────────────────────────
PE      40–200°C        2–10°/min  加熱+冷卻循環
PP      RT → Tm+30°C   可變        保溫10min，再快速冷卻
PET     RT → 280°C     20°/min初  淬冷後以5°/min再掃
                                    （消除熱歷程影響）
PETG    RT → 120°C     20°/min初  淬冷後以10°/min再掃
                                    （Tg確認，無熔點峰）

注：PET 兩步驟掃描的目的是先消除製程熱歷程，
再以慢速掃描獲得精確的 Tg 和結晶特性。

比喻說明 Analogy

DSC 就像用溫度計觀察一塊巧克力從固體變液體的過程：純黑巧克力（HDPE 類比）有一個明確的熔化溫度；白巧克力（PETG 類比）因成分複雜，沒有明確的熔點，只是逐漸軟化（玻璃轉化）。

PET 測試前先「淬冷」（快速冷卻）再重新掃描，就像先把巧克力重新融化再冷卻，消除之前的熱處理歷程，確保測試的重現性。

重點提示 Key Notes

為什麼在氮氣（N₂）下測試？氮氣是惰性氣體，防止樣品在高溫下氧化，確保測試結果反映的是材料本身的熱性質，而非熱氧化降解行為。這對於 PP（易受氧化）和 PET（高溫下可能水解）尤為重要。

12 mg 樣品量：太多 → 傳熱不均勻，峰形寬；太少 → 信號太弱。12 mg 是在靈敏度和傳熱均勻性之間的平衡點。

各樹脂容許偏差的意義：PET 的 ±4.0° 最嚴格（分子規整性高，熔點應很精確）；PP 的 ±12.0° 最寬鬆（均聚/共聚 PP 間差異大）。

1.3 Physicochemical Tests — Testing Parameters | 物理化學試驗 — 測試參數

原文內容 Original Text

Physicochemical Tests

The following tests, designed to determine physical and chemical properties of plastics and their extracts, are based on the extraction of the plastic material, and it is essential that the designated amount of the plastic be used. Also, the specified surface area must be available for extraction at the designated temperature.

Testing Parameters

Extracting medium: Unless otherwise directed in a specific test below, use Purified Water as the Extracting medium, maintained at a temperature of 70° during the extraction of the Sample preparation.

Blank: Use Purified Water where a blank is specified in the tests that follow.

Apparatus: Use a water bath and the Extraction Containers as described in Biological Reactivity Tests, In Vivo 〈88〉, Classification of Plastics, Apparatus. Proceed as directed in the first paragraph of Classification of Plastics, Preparation of Apparatus. [Note—The containers and equipment need not be sterile.]

Sample preparation: From a homogeneous plastic specimen, use a portion, for each 20.0 mL of Extracting medium, equivalent to 120 cm² total surface area (both sides combined), and subdivide into strips approximately 3 mm in width and as near to 5 cm in length as is practical. Transfer the subdivided sample to a glass-stoppered, 250-mL graduated cylinder of Type I glass, and add about 150 mL of Purified Water. Agitate for about 30 s, drain off and discard the liquid, and repeat with a second washing.

Sample preparation extract: Transfer the prepared Sample preparation to a suitable extraction flask, and add the required amount of Extracting medium. Extract by heating in a water bath at the temperature specified for the Extracting medium for 24 h. Cool, but not below 20°. Pipet 20 mL of the prepared extract into a suitable container. [Note—Use this portion in the test for Buffering Capacity.] Immediately decant the remaining extract into a suitably cleansed container, and seal.

Key Parameters Summary

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Physicochemical Tests（物理化學試驗）的設計邏輯：這些試驗的核心思路是「模擬最壞情況的萃取（worst-case extraction）」，以評估塑料容器在使用條件下可能釋放到藥品中的化學物質。

關鍵設計要素：

  • 標準化表面積（120 cm²/20 mL）：確保不同形狀容器的可比性
  • 70° 萃取溫度：加速萃取，模擬更嚴苛的條件（比室溫嚴格，但不像高壓蒸汽滅菌那樣極端）
  • 24h 萃取時間：達到近平衡狀態
  • 預洗兩次：去除表面污染，確保測試結果代表材料本身的萃取物

比喻說明 Analogy

這個萃取程序就像泡茶：茶葉（塑料樣品）在熱水（萃取介質）中浸泡一定時間，測量泡出來的成分。

表面積標準化（120 cm²/20 mL）就像規定「每克茶葉對應多少毫升水」，確保不同批次茶葉（不同形狀的容器）的比較具有意義。

預洗兩次，就像正式泡茶前先用熱水「喚醒茶葉」，去除表面灰塵（去除製造過程中附著的表面污染物）。

重點提示 Key Notes

「120 cm² 雙面合計」的理解：塑料薄片（條狀）同時具有兩個面（正面 + 背面），120 cm² 是兩面的合計面積，因此實際單面面積為 60 cm²。

換算：若容器壁厚均勻，120 cm² 雙面面積大約相當於 10 cm × 6 cm 的薄片（雙面 = 120 cm²）。

「不冷卻至 20° 以下」：萃取物必須保持液態才能操作，冷卻過低可能導致某些高分子量萃取物沉澱，影響後續測試結果。

樣品量計算 Sample Amount Calculation

標準萃取設置：
  萃取介質體積：20.0 mL（基準）
  所需表面積：120 cm²（雙面）

若需要 100 mL 萃取介質：
  100 mL ÷ 20 mL × 120 cm² = 600 cm²（雙面）
  即單面 300 cm² 的塑料樣品

試條尺寸：3 mm × 5 cm = 0.3 cm × 5 cm = 1.5 cm²/條（雙面 = 3 cm²/條）
→ 100 mL 萃取需要：600 cm² ÷ 3 cm² = 200 條試條

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼預洗（pre-washing）必須進行兩次？一次是否足夠？USP 的規定有何科學依據？
  2. 若萃取溫度從 70° 提高到 121°（高壓蒸汽滅菌），萃取物的組成和量會有何變化？這與注射劑最終滅菌包裝的評估有何關聯？
1.4 Nonvolatile Residue, Heavy Metals & Buffering Capacity | 非揮發殘留物、重金屬與緩衝容量

原文內容 Original Text

Nonvolatile Residue

Transfer, in suitable portions, 50.0 mL of the Sample preparation extract to a suitable, tared crucible (preferably a fused-silica crucible that has been acid-cleaned), and evaporate the volatile matter on a steam bath. Similarly evaporate 50.0 mL of the Blank in a second crucible.

[Note—If an oily residue is expected, inspect the crucible repeatedly during the evaporation and drying period, and reduce the amount of heat if the oil tends to creep along the walls of the crucible.] Dry at 105° for 1 h: the difference between the amounts obtained from the Sample preparation extract and the Blank is NMT 15 mg.

Residue on Ignition 〈281〉

[Note—It is not necessary to perform this test when the Nonvolatile Residue test result is NMT 5 mg.] Proceed with the residues obtained from the Sample preparation extract and from the Blank: the difference between the amounts of residue on ignition is NMT 5 mg.

Heavy Metals

Lead nitrate stock solution: Dissolve 159.8 mg of lead nitrate in 100 mL of water to which has been added 1 mL of nitric acid, then dilute with water to 1000 mL.

Standard lead solution: On the day of use, dilute 10.0 mL of Lead nitrate stock solution with water to 100.0 mL. Each milliliter of Standard lead solution contains the equivalent of 10 µg of lead. A comparison solution prepared on the basis of 100 µL of Standard lead solution per gram of substance contains the equivalent of 1 ppm of lead.

pH 3.5 acetate buffer: Dissolve 25.0 g of ammonium acetate in 25 mL of water, and add 38.0 mL of 6 N hydrochloric acid. Adjust, if necessary, to a pH of 3.5, dilute with water to 100 mL.

Procedure: Pipet 20 mL of the Sample preparation extract into one of two matched 50-mL color-comparison tubes. Adjust to pH 3.0–4.0. Into the second tube pipet 2 mL of Standard lead solution, and add 20 mL of the Blank. To each tube add 1.2 mL of thioacetamide–glycerin base TS and 2 mL of pH 3.5 acetate buffer, dilute with water to 50 mL: any brown color produced within 10 min in the tube containing the Sample preparation extract is NMT that in the tube containing the Standard lead solution (1 ppm in extract).

Buffering Capacity

Titrate the previously collected 20-mL portion of the Sample preparation extract potentiometrically to a pH of 7.0, using either 0.010 N hydrochloric acid or 0.010 N sodium hydroxide, as required. Treat a 20.0-mL portion of the Blank similarly: if the same titrant was required for both, the difference between the two volumes is NMT 10.0 mL; and if acid was required for either the Sample preparation extract or the Blank and alkali for the other, the total of the two volumes required is 10.0 mL.

"Conduct the Buffering Capacity test only when the containers are intended to hold a liquid product."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

三項關鍵試驗的意義：

1. Nonvolatile Residue（非揮發殘留物）：量化萃取物中的不揮發性物質總量，間接反映塑料中可遷移化學成分的總量（包括添加劑、殘留單體等）。限值 ≤15 mg（per 50 mL），相對於 20 mL 萃取介質 × 120 cm² 表面積。

2. Heavy Metals（重金屬）：使用比色法（硫乙醯胺–甘油）檢測鉛及其他可形成硫化物沉澱的重金屬。限值 ≤1 ppm（以鉛計）。

3. Buffering Capacity（緩衝容量）：測量萃取物改變 pH 的能力，確保塑料不會顯著影響液體藥品的 pH。僅適用於液體製劑容器。

重點提示 Key Notes

Nonvolatile Residue vs Residue on Ignition 的差異：

  • Nonvolatile Residue（105°）：含有有機物 + 無機物，是所有非揮發性萃取物的總和
  • Residue on Ignition（灼燒殘留）：在 Nonvolatile Residue 的基礎上進一步高溫灼燒，去除有機成分，只剩無機灰分（金屬鹽、催化劑殘留等）

當 Nonvolatile Residue ≤5 mg 時免做 Residue on Ignition，因為此時灰分含量極低，測試意義不大。

比喻說明 Analogy

重金屬比色法就像水質快速測試盒：加入顯色試劑後，若水中含有超標的重金屬，顏色就會加深。「any brown color produced... is NMT that in the tube containing the Standard lead solution」就是「樣品色深 ≤ 標準液色深」的直觀比較。

緩衝容量測試就像檢查水的「抗 pH 變化能力」：若萃取液明顯緩衝（需要大量酸或鹼才能到 pH 7.0），說明塑料釋放了具有緩衝能力的成分（如碳酸鹽、磷酸鹽或有機酸），可能干擾藥品的 pH。

重金屬標準液計算 Heavy Metals Standard Calculation

鉛標準液製備：
  原液：159.8 mg Pb(NO₃)₂ / 1000 mL = 0.1598 mg/mL Pb
  稀釋：取 10.0 mL → 100.0 mL（稀釋10倍）
  = 0.01598 mg/mL = ~10 µg/mL Pb

測試中加入 2 mL 標準液 + 20 mL Blank → 50 mL
Pb濃度 = 2 mL × 10 µg/mL ÷ 50 mL = 0.4 µg/mL

樣品側：20 mL 萃取液 → 50 mL
若樣品顏色 ≤ 標準管 → 樣品 Pb ≤ 標準管 Pb 濃度
即萃取液中 Pb ≤ 1 ppm（以 20 mL 萃取液計算）

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 接收新塑料容器供應商時，Physicochemical Tests 是資質評估（Qualification）的核心：

  • 向供應商索取 USP 〈661〉 完整測試報告，確認非揮發殘留物、重金屬、緩衝容量均符合規格
  • 注意不同萃取介質（水、醇、正己烷）的測試結果，有機萃取物（醇/己烷）的殘留通常比水萃取高，需全面評估
  • 液體製劑容器特別需要 Buffering Capacity 測試，以免塑料影響藥品緩衝體系（如磷酸緩衝注射液）
  • 重金屬限值 ≤1 ppm 對比注射劑的重金屬限量（ICH Q3D），需確認容器貢獻不超過容許日攝入量（PDE）的限額

  1. Buffering Capacity 測試中，若樣品萃取液需要加入酸（HCl）才能到 pH 7.0，而空白需要加入鹼（NaOH），則兩者體積的合計不得超過多少 mL？這個限制的意義是什麼？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • IR 光譜（Multiple Internal Reflectance）是塑料鑑別的主要工具，各樹脂與 USP RS 比較主要吸收帶位置；PET/PETG 需掃描至 400 cm⁻¹
  • DSC（差示掃描量熱法）透過熔點確認樹脂身份；各樹脂的加熱/冷卻程序不同，容許偏差從 ±4°（PET）到 ±12°（PP）不等
  • Physicochemical Tests 標準萃取條件：Purified Water at 70°，120 cm²（雙面）/ 20 mL，萃取 24h，預洗兩次
  • 三項核心測試：Nonvolatile Residue（≤15 mg/50 mL）、Heavy Metals（≤1 ppm as Pb）、Buffering Capacity（差值 ≤10.0 mL）
  • Buffering Capacity 僅在容器用於盛裝液體產品時進行，其意義在於確保包裝材料不干擾藥品的 pH 系統
  • PET/PETG 額外進行：色素萃取（吸光度 <0.01）、Total Terephthaloyl Moieties（≤1 ppm）、Ethylene Glycol（≤1 ppm）

2.1 Biological Tests — Overview & Regulatory Context | 生物試驗概述與法規背景

原文內容 Original Text

Biological Tests

The in vitro biological tests are performed according to the procedures set forth under Biological Reactivity Tests, In Vitro 〈87〉.

Materials that do not meet the requirements of the in vitro tests are not suitable for containers for drug products.

Plastic articles used for high risk products, such as those intended for inhalation, parenteral preparation, and ophthalmics, are tested using Biological Tests. Plastic articles used for low risk products, such as those intended for oral or topical administration, do not require Biological Tests.

"Biological reactivity testing in support of plastic packaging materials, components, and systems used for final pharmaceutical product packaging/delivery systems (drugs and drug/device combination products) provides baseline information and will often not be sufficient to assess the final suitability for use expectations of regulatory authorities. Thus, it is important to work with the appropriate regulatory authority for guidance regarding a product specific application."

Risk-Based Application of Biological Testing

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

生物試驗（Biological Tests）的目的：物理化學試驗（Section 1）量化的是萃取物的「量」，而生物試驗評估的是這些萃取物對「生命系統」的影響，即細胞毒性（cytotoxicity）、組織反應性（tissue reactivity）和系統毒性（systemic toxicity）。

即使一個容器通過了物理化學試驗，仍可能含有低濃度但高毒性的物質（如某些殘留催化劑），只有生物試驗才能發現這類問題。

重點提示 Key Notes

「必要但不充分」原則：USP 明確指出，生物反應性測試「往往不足以評估監管機構對最終適用性的期望」。這意味著：

  • USP 〈87〉/〈88〉 合格 = 基礎門檻（baseline），不是終點
  • 對於注射劑、吸入劑等高風險產品，監管機構（FDA、EMA）通常還要求完整的 E&L 評估
  • 藥品上市申請（NDA/BLA/MAA）的包裝相容性章節需要超越 USP 〈87〉/〈88〉 的數據支撐

「與相應監管機構諮詢」的建議，是 USP 罕見的直接提醒：標準本身不夠，請去找監管機構確認。

比喻說明 Analogy

物理化學試驗就像檢查一個廚師的刀具是否生鏽（量化問題）；生物試驗則是讓廚師用這把刀切食材後，測試食材是否對人體有毒性（功能影響）。

「生物試驗是必要但不充分的」：就像通過了基礎食品安全測試的廚房，還需要衛生局的現場稽查才能拿到營業執照。USP 〈87〉/〈88〉 是「衛生局測試」，監管機構的具體要求是「營業執照」。

  3. 口服製劑的 PE 容器不需要生物試驗，但如果該口服製劑包含兒科患者（0-2歲），是否應考慮額外的評估？理由是什麼？
2.2 Biological Reactivity Tests In Vitro — 〈87〉 | 體外生物反應性試驗

原文內容 Original Text

Biological Reactivity Tests, In Vitro 〈87〉 — Referenced Procedures

USP 〈661〉 references the in vitro biological tests as performed according to the procedures set forth under Biological Reactivity Tests, In Vitro 〈87〉. This chapter covers three assay types:

1. Elution Test (Elution-based Cytotoxicity)

Extracts of the test article are prepared and added to a mammalian cell culture. The elution test involves adding extract directly to cell culture medium and observing cell response. Results are scored on a reactivity scale:

  • Grade 0: No reactivity
  • Grade 1: Slight reactivity (subtle morphological changes)
  • Grade 2: Mild reactivity (not more than 20% of cells show reactive changes)
  • Grade 3: Moderate reactivity (up to 50% of cells affected)
  • Grade 4: Severe reactivity (more than 50% cells destroyed)

Materials pass the elution test if the reactivity is Grade 2 or less (NMT mild reactivity).

2. Agar Diffusion Test

The agar diffusion test (also called agar overlay method) places the test article directly on a cell monolayer covered with a thin layer of agar. Any cytotoxic extractables will diffuse through the agar and cause a zone of cell lysis. The lysis zone is measured and scored.

3. MEM Elution Test

The MEM Elution Test (Minimum Essential Medium Elution Test) is an extract-based cytotoxicity assay using L-929 mouse fibroblast cells. Test sample extracts are prepared in complete cell culture medium (MEM) at 37° for 24 h. The extract is then applied to the cell culture.

Acceptance Criteria Summary

"Materials that do not meet the requirements of the in vitro tests are not suitable for containers for drug products."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

三種體外細胞毒性試驗的差異：

  • Elution Test（溶出試驗）：先製備萃取液，再用萃取液處理細胞。評估的是溶出成分的毒性，對水溶性毒素較靈敏
  • Agar Diffusion Test（瓊脂擴散試驗）：直接接觸法，材料放在細胞上方，毒素通過瓊脂層擴散至細胞。對脂溶性毒素較靈敏（可直接從材料表面釋出）
  • MEM Elution Test：使用完整培養基（含血清）作為萃取介質，更接近生理環境，L-929 細胞是 ISO 10993 標準細胞系

三種方法各有優缺點，通常至少需要完成其中一種（依製造商/監管要求選擇）。

比喻說明 Analogy

Elution Test 就像「先把食物浸泡在水中 24 小時，再用這杯水澆花，看花是否枯萎」——間接評估水溶性毒素。

Agar Diffusion Test 則像「直接把可疑物品放在花旁邊，看花是否因揮發物或接觸而受影響」——直接接觸評估。

Grade 4 反應（>50% 細胞破壞）就像整片花圃全部枯死——材料明顯不適合。Grade 0–2 則像花只是微微打蔫，仍在可接受範圍。

重點提示 Key Notes

L-929 細胞的意義：L-929 是 NCTC Clone 929 小鼠皮下結締組織細胞，是 ISO 10993（醫療器材生物相容性評估）和 USP 生物試驗的標準細胞系。選用原因：

  • 繁殖穩定，批次間一致性高
  • 對細胞毒性物質靈敏
  • 全球實驗室廣泛使用，數據可比

「不符合 = 不適用」：這是 USP 中少有的強制性語言。若體外試驗不通過，該材料在法規層面被視為不適合用於藥品容器，必須更換材料，不能以其他測試「補救」。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 的供應商資質評估（Supplier Qualification）中，生物試驗的策略性考量：

  • 優先向供應商索取 〈87〉 三種試驗的測試報告，確認合格
  • 若供應商僅提供 ISO 10993-5（細胞毒性）報告，需確認使用的細胞株和接受標準與 USP 〈87〉 兼容
  • 對於注射劑，客戶 CMC 文件通常還需要 〈88〉 體內試驗數據（見 Section 2.3）
  • 新材料或新製程（如增加 UV 穩定劑）應重新評估生物相容性，不能直接沿用舊材料的測試結果

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 如果一種塑料在 Elution Test 得到 Grade 2（Mild reactivity），這個材料是否可以用於注射劑容器？說明判斷依據。
  2. Agar Diffusion Test 和 Elution Test 各自對哪類毒素更靈敏（水溶性 vs 脂溶性）？為什麼 ISO 10993 建議同時做多種方法？

2.3 Biological Reactivity Tests In Vivo — 〈88〉 Classification | 體內生物反應性試驗與塑料分類

原文內容 Original Text

Biological Reactivity Tests, In Vivo 〈88〉 — Plastics Classification

USP 〈88〉 provides the Classification of Plastics based on their performance in a series of in vivo biological reactivity tests. This classification (Class I through Class VI) is used as a measure of the biocompatibility of plastic materials.

USP Plastics Classification System (Class I–VI)

In Vivo Test Methods (〈88〉)

  • Systemic Injection Test: Inject extract (saline and cottonseed oil) intraperitoneally or intravenously into mice; observe for 72 h for systemic reactions.
  • Intracutaneous Test: Inject extract intradermally into rabbits; observe for 72 h for local tissue reactions (erythema, edema).
  • Implantation Test: Implant test article strips subcutaneously or intramuscularly in rabbits; observe for macroscopic and histologic tissue reactions at defined time points (7, 14, 30 days).

Extracting Media for In Vivo Tests

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

USP Plastics Class I–VI 的邏輯：分類越高（Class VI），測試越嚴格，適用範圍越廣。Class VI 是「超級認證」——通過了最嚴格的全套測試，幾乎可以用於任何應用（包括注射劑包裝和長期植入物）。

重要理解：Class VI 不是法規要求，而是材料供應商提供給客戶的「信心保證」。多數藥用注射劑包裝材料應至少達到 Class VI 標準，這也是採購規格中常見的要求（"USP Class VI compliant"）。

重點提示 Key Notes

體內試驗 vs 體外試驗的定位：

  • 體外（In Vitro，〈87〉）：快速篩選，成本低，不需要動物，是 USP 〈661〉 高風險應用的最低要求
  • 體內（In Vivo，〈88〉）：更全面的系統性和局部組織反應評估，Class VI 認定需要完整的體內試驗套組

FDA 的 Container Closure System Guidance（2000年）要求注射劑包裝的生物相容性評估應包括體外和體內數據，且 USP 〈87〉/〈88〉 合格是基礎，但監管審查還考量 E&L 研究結果。

比喻說明 Analogy

USP Class I–VI 就像建築材料的耐火等級：Class I 是一般辦公室（低風險），Class VI 是核電站的防護材料（最高風險，最嚴格測試）。

採購藥用包裝容器要求「USP Class VI」，就像建造醫院時要求「最高防火等級材料」——不是法律強制，但是業界最佳實踐，也是客戶/監管機構的預期。
