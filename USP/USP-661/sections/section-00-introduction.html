0.1 Introduction & Purpose | 章節目的與背景

原文內容 Original Text

〈661〉 PLASTIC PACKAGING SYSTEMS AND THEIR MATERIALS OF CONSTRUCTION

(If the option of early adoption of Plastic Materials of Construction 〈661.1〉 and Plastic Packaging Systems for Pharmaceutical Use 〈661.2〉 is not used prior to December 1, 2025, the requirements under this chapter will apply.)

INTRODUCTION

The purpose of this chapter is to provide standards for plastic articles (materials, components, and systems) used to package medical articles (pharmaceuticals, biologics, and dietary supplements). Definitions that apply to this chapter are provided in Packaging and Storage Requirements 〈659〉. Standards and tests for the functional properties of containers and their components are provided in Containers—Performance Testing 〈671〉.

Plastics are composed of a mixture of homologous polymers, having a range of molecular weights. Plastics may contain other substances such as residues from the polymerization process and additives such as plasticizers, stabilizers, antioxidants, pigments, and lubricants.

Plastic articles are identified and characterized by infrared (IR) spectroscopy and differential scanning calorimetry (DSC). Standards are provided in this chapter for the identification and characterization of the different types of plastic, and the test procedures are provided at the end of the chapter. Furthermore, plastics are characterized to establish that they are suited for their intended use.

Plastics and their additives, regardless of their risk and application, must meet the requirements for food contact as provided in Title 21 of the US Code of Federal Regulations (CFR). Ingredients added to these items must conform to the requirements in the applicable sections of 21 CFR Part 174—Indirect Food Additives: General, or have been evaluated by the FDA and determined to be acceptable substances for the intended use.

Physicochemical tests are performed on plastics used in high risk applications (inhalation, parenteral preparation, and ophthalmics) to characterize the extracted components and identify possible migrants. To this end, resin-specific extraction tests are provided in this chapter for polyethylene, polypropylene, polyethylene terephthalate, and polyethylene terephthalate G used in such high risk applications. Test all other plastics as directed for Physicochemical Tests. Conduct the Buffering Capacity test only when the containers are intended to hold a liquid product.

Physicochemical Tests are not required for articles used in low risk applications (those intended for oral or topical administration). The degree or extent of biological testing is also dependent on the intended use of the plastic article and the degree of risk that extracted substances could adversely impact the efficacy of the compendial article.

Plastic articles used for high risk products, such as those intended for inhalation, parenteral preparation, and ophthalmics, are tested using Biological Tests. Plastic articles used for low risk products, such as those intended for oral or topical administration, do not require Biological Tests.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Plastic Articles (塑料物件)：在 USP 〈661〉 中涵蓋三個層次：
1. Materials（材料）：原始樹脂，如 PE、PP、PET
2. Components（組件）：成型後的個別零件，如瓶身、瓶蓋
3. Systems（系統）：完整的容器密封系統

理解這三層次很重要，因為測試要求可能因層次不同而有所差異。〈661.1〉專注 Materials，〈661.2〉專注 Systems，而現行 〈661〉 則整合三者。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像塑料包裝就像餐廳的餐具選擇：材料是製作餐盤的陶瓷或玻璃原料，組件是成品餐盤本身，系統則是完整的餐具組合（盤、碗、杯、蓋）。

高風險應用（注射劑）就像醫院手術室使用的器皿：必須通過嚴格的化學與生物相容性測試。低風險應用（口服錠劑）就像一般餐廳餐具：有基本衛生標準，但不需達到手術室等級。

重點提示 Key Notes

風險分層原則（Risk-based Approach）：

  • 高風險（High Risk）：吸入劑、注射劑、眼用製劑 → 需要 Physicochemical Tests + Biological Tests
  • 低風險（Low Risk）：口服、外用製劑 → 豁免 Physicochemical Tests 及 Biological Tests

這是監管效率的體現：不需要對每瓶維他命 C 錠劑的塑料瓶做注射劑等級的測試。但若包裝改變了藥物的療效或安全性，就必須提升測試等級。

章節框架解析 Chapter Architecture

USP 〈661〉 是一個「傘型章節」，其下有三個姊妹章節：

  • 〈661.1〉 Plastic Materials of Construction — 材料層次測試
  • 〈661.2〉 Plastic Packaging Systems for Pharmaceutical Use — 系統層次測試
  • 〈1661〉 Evaluation of Plastic Packaging Systems — 指導性資訊章節

自 2025 年 12 月 1 日起，廠商可選擇採用 〈661.1〉/〈661.2〉 替代現行 〈661〉。若未早期採用，〈661〉 仍為有效標準。

0.2 Polyethylene Containers | 聚乙烯容器

原文內容 Original Text

POLYETHYLENE CONTAINERS

Scope

The standards and tests provided in this section characterize containers and components, produced from either low-density polyethylene (LDPE) or high-density polyethylene (HDPE) of either homopolymer or copolymer resins that are interchangeably suitable for packaging dry oral dosage forms not meant for constitution into solution.

All polyethylene components are subject to testing by IR spectroscopy and DSC. Where stability studies have been performed to establish the expiration date of a particular dosage form in the appropriate polyethylene container, then any other polyethylene container meeting these requirements may be similarly used to package such a dosage form, provided that the appropriate stability programs are expanded to include the alternative container.

Background

High-density and low-density polyethylene are long-chain polymers synthesized under controlled conditions of heat and pressure, with the aid of catalysts from NLT 85.0% ethylene and NLT 95.0% total olefins. Other olefin ingredients that are most frequently used are butene, hexene, and propylene.

High-density polyethylene has a density between 0.941 and 0.965 g/cm³. Low-density polyethylene has a density between 0.850 and 0.940 g/cm³. Other properties that may affect the suitability of polyethylene include modulus of elasticity, melt index, environmental stress crack resistance, and degree of crystallinity after molding.

High-Density Polyethylene — Identification Tests

Infrared spectroscopy: Proceed as directed for Multiple Internal Reflectance. The corrected spectrum of the specimen exhibits major absorption bands only at the same wavelengths as the spectrum of USP High-Density Polyethylene RS.

Differential scanning calorimetry: Proceed as directed for Thermal Analysis. The melting peak temperature obtained from the thermal analysis curve of the specimen does not differ from that of the Reference Standard by more than 6.0°.

HDPE — Heavy Metals and Nonvolatile Residue

For each 20.0 mL of Extracting medium, the portion shall be 60 cm², regardless of thickness.

Nonvolatile residue limits:

  • Water at 70°: NMT 12.0 mg
  • Alcohol at 70°: NMT 75.0 mg
  • Hexanes at 50°: NMT 100.0 mg

Low-Density Polyethylene — Identification & Residue Tests

DSC: melting peak temperature shall not differ from USP Low-Density Polyethylene RS by more than 8.0°.

Nonvolatile residue limits:

  • Water at 70°: NMT 12.0 mg
  • Alcohol at 70°: NMT 75.0 mg
  • Hexanes at 50°: NMT 350.0 mg

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

HDPE vs LDPE（高密度聚乙烯 vs 低密度聚乙烯）：

密度差異源自分子鏈的分支程度。HDPE 分支少，鏈排列緊密 → 密度高（0.941–0.965 g/cm³）、剛性強、阻隔性佳。LDPE 分支多 → 密度低（0.850–0.940 g/cm³）、柔軟有彈性，適合擠壓瓶。

在藥品包裝中，HDPE 常見於藥錠瓶（如 OTC 藥品），LDPE 常見於滴眼液瓶、軟管。

數值比較 Specification Comparison

殘留物限值對比 (Nonvolatile Residue Limits):

萃取介質         HDPE限值    LDPE限值
────────────────────────────────
水（70°）         ≤12.0 mg    ≤12.0 mg
酒精（70°）       ≤75.0 mg    ≤75.0 mg
正己烷（50°）    ≤100.0 mg   ≤350.0 mg

關鍵差異：LDPE 在正己烷的限值是 HDPE 的 3.5 倍，
反映 LDPE 因結構疏鬆，對非極性溶劑的萃取量更大。

比喻說明 Analogy

HDPE 的分子結構就像整齊排列的竹籤（直鏈），密度高，外來物質難以滲入。LDPE 則像一堆彎曲的鐵絲（有分支），空隙多，溶劑萃取出的成分也相對較多。

正因如此，LDPE 在正己烷（非極性有機溶劑）的殘留限值比 HDPE 寬鬆 3.5 倍，因為 USP 認知到這是 LDPE 材料的固有特性。

重點提示 Key Notes

互換性原則（Interchangeability）：當穩定性試驗已建立某一 PE 容器的有效期後，符合相同規格的其他 PE 容器可互換使用，但前提是：必須將替代容器納入穩定性計畫擴充評估。

這對 CDMO 客戶服務非常實用：當原定容器短缺時，可快速切換到等效容器，但必須有數據支撐。

  2. 一個藥廠欲將某口服固體製劑從 HDPE 容器更換為 LDPE 容器，需要做哪些評估工作？
0.3 Polypropylene Containers | 聚丙烯容器

原文內容 Original Text

POLYPROPYLENE CONTAINERS

Scope

The standards and tests provided in this section characterize polypropylene containers, produced from either homopolymers or copolymers, that are interchangeably suitable for packaging dry solid and liquid oral dosage forms.

Background

Propylene polymers are long-chain polymers synthesized from propylene or propylene and other olefins under controlled conditions of heat and pressure, with the aid of catalysts. Examples of other olefins most commonly used include ethylene and butene. The propylene polymers, the ingredients used to manufacture the propylene polymers, and the ingredients used in the fabrication of the containers conform to the applicable sections of 21 CFR.

Factors such as plastic composition, processing and cleaning procedures, contacting media, inks, adhesives, absorption, adsorption and permeability of preservatives, and conditions of storage may also affect the suitability of a plastic for a specific use.

Polypropylene has a distinctive IR spectrum and possesses characteristic thermal properties. It has a density between 0.880 and 0.913 g/cm³. The permeation properties of molded polypropylene containers may be altered when reground polymer is incorporated, depending on the proportion of regrind in the final product.

Other properties that may affect the suitability of polypropylene: oxygen and moisture permeability, modulus of elasticity, melt flow index, environmental stress crack resistance, and degree of crystallinity after molding.

Identification Tests

Infrared Spectroscopy: The corrected spectrum exhibits major absorption bands only at the same wavelengths as USP Homopolymer Polypropylene RS or copolymer polypropylene standard.

Differential Scanning Calorimetry: The melting peak temperature does not differ from that of USP Homopolymer Polypropylene RS by more than 12.0°.

Heavy Metals and Nonvolatile Residue

For each 20 mL of Extracting medium the portion shall be 60 cm², regardless of thickness.

Nonvolatile residue limits:

  • Water at 70°: NMT 10.0 mg
  • Alcohol at 70°: NMT 60.0 mg
  • Hexanes at 50°: NMT 225.0 mg
[Note—Hexanes and alcohol are flammable. When evaporating these solvents, use a current of air with the water bath; when drying the residue, use an explosion-proof oven.]

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Polypropylene (聚丙烯，PP)：由丙烯（propylene）單體聚合而成，密度 0.880–0.913 g/cm³，比 PE 更輕，化學穩定性優異，熱穩定性高（可耐受高壓蒸汽滅菌）。

PP 的 DSC 熔點容許偏差（12.0°）比 HDPE（6.0°）和 LDPE（8.0°）都大，因為 PP 的結晶性受製程影響較大（均聚物 vs 共聚物間差異較顯著）。

重點提示 Key Notes

再研磨料（Regrind）的影響：加入再研磨料（回收加工的 PP）會改變容器的滲透特性，影響阻隔性。USP 明確提醒這一點，因為部分製造商會用再研磨料降低成本，但必須確認不影響材料規格的符合性。

這與 GMP 的材料一致性要求直接相關：若更換供應商或調整再研磨料比例，需重新驗證。

比喻說明 Analogy

PP 容器就像高壓鍋的密封圈：能承受高溫高壓，化學惰性好，既可用於乾燥固體（密封藥錠），也可用於液體口服製劑。

「再研磨料」的效果就像在新鮮麵糰中混入剩餘麵糰：比例少時影響不大，但比例過高時質地（物理屏障特性）就可能改變。

材料密度速查 Density Reference

塑料類型      密度範圍（g/cm³）   DSC容許偏差
──────────────────────────────────
HDPE          0.941–0.965         ±6.0°
LDPE          0.850–0.940         ±8.0°
PP            0.880–0.913         ±12.0°
PET           1.3–1.4             ±4.0°
PETG          ~1.27               ±6.0°

密度越高，通常分子排列越緊密，阻隔性越佳。

0.4 PET and PETG Containers | 聚對苯二甲酸乙二酯容器

原文內容 Original Text

POLYETHYLENE TEREPHTHALATE BOTTLES AND POLYETHYLENE TEREPHTHALATE G CONTAINERS

Scope

The standards and tests provided in this section characterize polyethylene terephthalate (PET) and polyethylene terephthalate G (PETG) bottles that are interchangeably suitable for packaging liquid oral dosage forms.

Background — PET

PET resins are long-chain crystalline polymers prepared by the condensation of ethylene glycol with dimethyl terephthalate or terephthalic acid. PET copolymer resins may also contain a small amount of either isophthalic acid (NMT 3 mol%) or 1,4-cyclohexanedimethanol (NMT 5 mol%).

PET and PET copolymer resins exhibit a large degree of order in their molecular structure. Characteristic properties: glass transition temperature ~76°, melting temperature ~250°. Density between 1.3 and 1.4 g/cm³; minimum intrinsic viscosity 0.7 dL/g (corresponds to number average molecular weight of ~23,000 Da).

Background — PETG

PETG resins are prepared by condensation of ethylene glycol with dimethyl terephthalate or terephthalic acid and 15–34 mol% of 1,4-cyclohexanedimethanol (CHDM). PETG resins are clear, amorphous polymers, having a glass transition temperature of about 81° and no crystalline melting point as determined by DSC. Density ~1.27 g/cm³; minimum intrinsic viscosity 0.65 dL/g (~16,000 Da).

PET and PETG resins do not contain any plasticizers, processing aids, or antioxidants. Colorants, if used, do not migrate into the contained liquid.

Colorant Extraction Test

Fill 2 test bottles: 50% alcohol for PET; 25% alcohol for PETG. Incubate at 49° for 10 days. Determine absorbances at the wavelength of maximum absorbance: the absorbance values are less than 0.01 for both test solutions.

Extracting Media for PET/PETG

  • Purified Water
  • 50% alcohol (for PET) / 25% alcohol (for PETG)
  • n-Heptane

General procedure: fill bottles to 90% nominal capacity; incubate at 49° for 10 days.

Key Analyte Limits

  • Heavy metals: NMT 1 ppm in extract
  • Total terephthaloyl moieties: NMT 0.150 absorbance (≤1 ppm) in alcohol and n-heptane extracts
  • Ethylene glycol: NMT 1 ppm in Purified Water extract
  • Colorant extraction: absorbance <0.01

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

PET vs PETG 的結構差異：

PET（聚對苯二甲酸乙二酯）是半結晶性聚合物，具有明確的熔點（~250°）和玻璃轉化溫度（Tg ~76°）。透明度佳，廣泛用於飲料瓶（可口可樂瓶即為 PET）。

PETG 在結構中引入大量 CHDM（15–34 mol%），破壞了結晶性，成為無定形透明聚合物，無結晶熔點，Tg ~81°。PETG 更易於加工成複雜形狀，透明度更高，但熱穩定性略低於 PET。

比喻說明 Analogy

PET 就像普通玻璃窗（有結晶結構，清透但稍帶色澤）；PETG 則像光學玻璃（無定形，透明度更高，更容易切割成複雜形狀）。兩者都透明，但 PETG 的加工彈性更大，適合藥廠需要特殊瓶型的情況。

重點提示 Key Notes

三種特定分析物的意義：

  • Total Terephthaloyl Moieties（對苯二甲酸基團）：PET/PETG 降解或水解的直接產物，是聚合物完整性的指標
  • Ethylene Glycol（乙二醇）：PET/PETG 合成原料之一，過量殘留具潛在毒性
  • Colorant Extraction（色素萃取）：確保任何添加色素不會遷移進入藥品

這三項測試是 PET/PETG 特有的，反映其化學組成的特殊性。萃取介質的不同（水、醇、正庚烷）模擬不同的食品/藥品基質環境。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 環境中，客戶可能要求使用 PET 或 PETG 容器包裝液體口服製劑（如糖漿、口服液）。評估要點：

  • 確認容器供應商提供符合 USP 〈661〉 的 CoA（Certificate of Analysis）
  • 若產品含有酒精溶劑，注意使用對應的萃取介質（PET 用 50% 醇，PETG 用 25% 醇）
  • 評估標籤/印刷油墨的色素遷移風險
  • 高溫儲存條件（如熱帶地區市場）需考慮 Tg（~76–81°）是否有實際影響

  2. PET 和 PETG 瓶使用不同濃度酒精（50% vs 25%）作為萃取介質，原因是什麼？

0.5 New Scope (Dec 2025 Text) | 2025年12月更新範圍

原文內容 Original Text

INTRODUCTION (Official 1-Dec-2025)

Packaging systems used for drug products (pharmaceuticals, biologics) are defined in Packaging and Storage Requirements 〈659〉 and such systems may be constructed from plastic materials and components. Plastics are made of polymers, which can have a range of molecular weights and may contain other substances such as residues from the polymerization process, plasticizers, stabilizers, antioxidants, pigments, and lubricants.

Drug products will be in direct contact with the primary packaging component and may result in an interaction between the product and the packaging system. Such an interaction should not adversely affect the drug product, and this will be facilitated by the use of well-characterized plastic materials of construction in components and packaging systems and by the appropriate testing of packaging systems.

SCOPE (Official 1-Dec-2025)

Establishing the suitability of plastic packaging systems for drug products involves multiple tests and testing procedures, as briefly outlined below:

  • Material screening—characterization of a packaging system's materials of construction to evaluate ingredients as probable extractables and potential leachables. Such a characterization facilitates the identification of materials that are suitable for use in packaging systems.
  • Controlled extraction (simulation) study—worst-case controlled extraction (simulation) study to determine the extent to which extractables may become probable leachables (for additional information, see Assessment of Extractables Associated with Pharmaceutical Packaging/Delivery Systems 〈1663〉).
  • Product assessment—actual-case measurement of confirmed leachables in the drug product in the pharmaceutical packaging/delivery system intended for the commercial market (for additional information, see Assessment of Drug Product Leachables Associated with Pharmaceutical Packaging/Delivery Systems 〈1664〉).

Additionally, information provided by the vendor(s) of plastic packaging systems and their associated materials or components of construction can facilitate suitability for use assessments.

The testing of materials of construction used in packaging systems is addressed in Plastic Materials of Construction 〈661.1〉. A product's packaging component or system is deemed suited for its intended use, if it meets the requirements in 〈661.2〉.

For more information on the scope of, applicability of, and other topics related to the 〈661〉 suite of general chapters, see Evaluation of Plastic Packaging Systems for Pharmaceutical Use and Their Materials of Construction 〈1661〉.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

三層次評估框架（Three-tier Evaluation Framework）：

2025年12月新版 〈661〉 引入了更現代化的 E&L（Extractables and Leachables，可萃取物與可浸出物）評估框架：

  • Material Screening（材料篩選）：識別材料中的潛在 Extractables → 可能成為 Leachables 的物質
  • Controlled Extraction Study（控制萃取研究）：最壞情況下的萃取，確定 Extractables 的範圍（參見 〈1663〉）
  • Product Assessment（產品評估）：實際商業條件下測量 Leachables（參見 〈1664〉）

比喻說明 Analogy

這三層次就像食品安全檢驗的三道關卡：材料篩選是「原料檢驗」（確認食材安全），控制萃取研究是「烹飪模擬測試」（最惡劣條件下會釋出什麼），產品評估是「最終產品上市前的成品檢驗」（實際食品中真正含有什麼）。

重點提示 Key Notes

Extractables vs Leachables 的核心區別：

  • Extractables（可萃取物）：在極端/加速條件下從包裝材料中萃取出的物質，代表最壞情況的「潛在威脅清單」
  • Leachables（可浸出物）：在實際儲存條件下真正遷移進入藥品的物質，是真實的患者暴露風險

監管機構（FDA、EMA）的審查重點是 Leachables，但 Extractables 研究是預測 Leachables 的必要前提。ICH Q3E（E&L 指導原則）對此有詳細說明。

實務應用 Practical Application

對於 CDMO 而言，新版 〈661〉 框架意味著容器密封系統（Container-Closure System, CCS）的資質評估要更系統化：

  • 向供應商索取 Material Screening 報告（含 Extractables profile）
  • 依據劑型和給藥途徑決定是否需要執行 Controlled Extraction Study
  • 注射劑/吸入劑必須完成 Product Assessment（實際產品中的 Leachables 測定）
  • 善用 〈661.1〉/〈661.2〉 的數據可能作為 〈661〉 測試的替代方案

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • USP 〈661〉 提供塑料包裝材料與系統的測試標準，適用於藥品、生物製品與膳食補充品包裝
  • 風險分層原則：高風險應用（注射劑、吸入劑、眼用製劑）需要物理化學試驗 + 生物試驗；低風險應用（口服、外用）可豁免
  • 主要塑料類型：HDPE（0.941–0.965 g/cm³）、LDPE（0.850–0.940 g/cm³）、PP（0.880–0.913 g/cm³）、PET（1.3–1.4 g/cm³）、PETG（~1.27 g/cm³）
  • 鑑別方法：IR 光譜（Multiple Internal Reflectance）+ DSC（熔點確認），各樹脂均有 USP 參考標準品（RS）比對
  • 2025年12月更新引入三層次評估框架：Material Screening → Controlled Extraction Study → Product Assessment，與現代 E&L 評估體系接軌
  • 所有塑料及添加劑均需符合 21 CFR 食品接觸要求，這是藥用包裝材料的基礎合規門檻
