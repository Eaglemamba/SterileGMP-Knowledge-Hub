1. Introduction | 簡介

原文內容 Original Text

This chapter provides guidance on the integrity assurance of nonporous packages intended for sterile pharmaceutical products. Background instruction is provided on the topics of leaks, leakage rate, and package sealing/closure mechanisms. Explanation is given as to how packages that conform to specified leakage limits help to ensure the contained product meets and maintains sterility and relevant physicochemical specifications. The integration of package integrity assurance as a key component of the entire product life cycle is stressed.

The term product–package refers to the container–closure system plus the product contents. The container–closure system consists of the primary packaging components, those components that are or may be in intimate contact with the product, as well as secondary packaging components vital to ensuring correct package assembly, for example, the aluminum cap used to seal a stoppered vial package.

Suitable container–closure systems adequately store and protect the contained pharmaceutical product. Thus, sterile product–package integrity is the ability of a sterile product container–closure system to keep product contents in, while keeping detrimental environmental contaminants out.

Specifically, leaks of concern for sterile product–packages include the following three categories described in Table 1. In other words, the leaks of concern for a given product–package are a function of the degree of package protection demanded by the product to ensure that all relevant product physicochemical and microbiological quality attributes are met through product expiry and use.

Leaks of Concern Product Quality Risks Posed by Leaks
Capable of allowing entry of microorganisms Failure of product sterility quality attribute
Capable of allowing escape of the product dosage form or allowing entry of external liquid or solid matter Failure of relevant product physicochemical quality attributes
Capable of allowing change in gas headspace content. For example, loss of headspace inert gases (e.g., nitrogen), loss of headspace vacuum, and/or entry of gases (e.g., oxygen, water vapor, air). Failure of relevant product physicochemical quality attributes and/or hindrance of product access by the end-user

Package integrity is synonymous with container–closure integrity; these terms are used interchangeably throughout this chapter. In the past, to say a sterile product–package has container–closure integrity commonly meant that the package either had passed or was capable of passing a microbiological challenge test. This guidance chapter defines the concept of container–closure integrity more broadly, encompassing the absence of all package leaks that risk product quality.

By this definition, a package is considered to have integrity if it allows no leakage greater than the product–package maximum allowable leakage limit. In other words, the largest and smallest leaks of concern are absent.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Package Integrity（包裝完整性）：這不只是「瓶子沒有裂」這麼簡單。USP 的定義涵蓋三種風險：微生物進入、內容物外漏或汙染物進入、以及頭部空間氣體組成改變。三者缺一不可。

Product–Package（產品–包裝）：指的是容器密封系統加上產品內容物的整體。一個完整的無菌注射劑包裝包括：玻璃瓶 + 橡膠塞 + 鋁蓋 + 藥液 + 頭部空間氣體，任何一環出問題都會破壞整體完整性。

三類問題洩漏：

  • 微生物侵入 → 無菌性失敗（最嚴重）
  • 產品外漏或外物進入 → 理化品質失敗
  • 頭部空間氣體改變 → 穩定性或使用性失敗

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你在高級餐廳點了一瓶名貴紅酒。「包裝完整性」就像這瓶酒的軟木塞系統：它必須讓酒液不外漏（液體封閉）、讓空氣無法進入導致氧化（氣體封閉）、讓細菌無法汙染（微生物封閉）。如果軟木塞輕微受損，即便酒液沒有漏出，酒的品質也可能早已劣化——這正是「頭部空間完整性」的概念。

重點提示 Key Notes

歷史觀念的轉變：過去業界常把「容器密封完整性」等同於「通過微生物挑戰試驗」。USP 1207 擴大了這個定義——即使沒有微生物問題，只要有任何影響產品品質的洩漏，就不符合完整性要求。

這對 CDMO 的影響：你的 CCI（Container-Closure Integrity，容器密封完整性）驗證策略必須考慮所有三種洩漏風險，而不只是做微生物侵入試驗就算數。

2. Scope | 適用範圍

原文內容 Original Text

The package integrity leak tests and seal quality tests outlined in this chapter apply to the testing of both large and small volume container–closure systems for sterile pharmaceutical dosage forms. Such package systems include, but are not limited to, the following examples:

  • Vials or bottles closed with elastomeric closures or screw-thread caps
  • Form-fill-seal plastic or glass ampules
  • Syringes or cartridges
  • Flexible bags or pouches
  • Packages for some drug/device combination products (e.g., packages cased inside autoinjectors)

Outside the chapter scope are packaging systems and processing equipment used in the preparation, storage, and manufacture of sterile pharmaceutical products. Examples include containers for active pharmaceutical ingredients and containers for product intermediate or final bulk product.

Outside the chapter scope are packages used for sterile diagnostic products or medical devices, and some packages used for sterile drug/device combination products.

Outside the chapter scope are products having a primary package composed of porous barrier package materials, i.e., materials designed with pores or openings to allow volumetric air flow while preventing airborne microbial contamination.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Porous vs. Nonporous：本章只討論非多孔（nonporous）包裝。多孔材料（如 Tyvek 袋，常用於醫療器材的包裝）允許空氣流通但阻止微生物，這類包裝的完整性評估是不同的概念。

比喻說明 Analogy

好比餐廳的設備管理規範：本章只管「送到客人桌上的餐具（最終包裝）」，不管後廚的儲存容器（製程中間物）。即使後廚的鍋子有小洞，那是另一套管理規範的問題，不是本章要處理的。

重點提示 Key Notes

對 CDMO 而言，重要的是明確區分哪些包裝屬於本章規範：成品西林瓶、安瓿、注射器、卡式管、藥袋，以及某些藥械組合產品的包裝（如自動注射器中的注射器）均在範疇之內。在設計驗證計畫時，先確認包裝類型是否適用，再選擇相應的測試方法。

3. Leaks and Leakage Rate | 洩漏與洩漏速率

原文內容 Original Text

Leaks are commonly conceptualized as holes of a defined diameter, or channels of distinctive diameter and length, although leaks that occur naturally are generally complex, multicavity tortuous paths and are rarely uniform in size or shape. Even artificially created leaks such as laser-drilled defects (also called holes) used for leak-test method development and validation are irregular in size, shape, and depth.

Gaseous leakage rate is a measure of the rate of gas flow (in mass or volume units) that passes through a leak path under specific conditions of temperature and the concentration or pressure differential across the barrier wall. Therefore, gaseous leakage rate has dimensions of pressure multiplied by volume, divided by time. The international standard SI nomenclature is pascal cubic meter per second (Pa · m³ · s⁻¹).

Unintentional leaks in packages are often detected or sized using gas as a tracer element. Given a situation in which a tracer gas partial pressure difference exists across the package barrier wall and no absolute pressure difference exists, gaseous leakage is predominantly diffusional in nature as the tracer gas moves from a region of higher concentration to a region of lower concentration.

Given the situation in which an absolute pressure difference exists across the package barrier wall, gas leakage through package gaps is more rapid (flux being primarily convective) as gas moves from the higher pressure region to one of lower pressure.

Liquid leakage rate is a measure of the volume of liquid that moves through a leak path as a function of time under specified conditions of temperature and absolute differential pressure across the barrier wall. Liquid movement through a leak path occurs only when leak size/shape, package materials of construction, the absence of leak obstruction, tracer liquid composition, and test parameters all work together.

Leakage differs from permeation. Leakage is the unintentional entry or escape of matter (solids, liquids, or gases) through a breach in a package wall or through a gap between package components. In the context of this chapter, permeation is the passage of fluid (e.g., gas) into, through, and out of a nonporous package wall. Permeation, not leakage, occurs when only a small fraction of molecules is able to move through a barrier by way of any one hole.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

洩漏的真實樣貌：現實中的洩漏不像教科書圖示的完美圓孔，而是複雜的曲折路徑（tortuous path，曲折路徑）。這解釋了為什麼同一個「名義孔徑（nominal diameter）」的缺陷，在不同測試條件下可能給出不同結果。

氣體洩漏速率的兩種機制：

  • 擴散（Diffusion）：無壓差，靠濃度梯度驅動（如氦氣從高濃度往低濃度移動）
  • 對流（Convection）：有壓差，氣體從高壓往低壓快速流動（更容易被儀器偵測）

公式與計算 Formula

氣體洩漏速率 (Gaseous Leakage Rate)
單位：Pa · m³ · s⁻¹（SI單位）
常用單位：mbar · L · s⁻¹ 或 std · cm³ · s⁻¹

換算參考：
1 Pa · m³ · s⁻¹ = 10 mbar · L · s⁻¹
標準條件：1 atm 差壓、乾燥空氣、25°C

洩漏 vs. 滲透：
洩漏 (Leakage) = 通過缺陷/間隙的體積流動
滲透 (Permeation) = 分子穿越完整包裝壁的緩慢傳輸
兩者量級不同，測試方法也不同

比喻說明 Analogy

「洩漏 vs. 滲透」就像水壩與海綿的差別：水壩破了一個洞（洩漏），水從洞口大量湧出；海綿沒有破洞，但水分子緩慢地從材料本身滲過（滲透）。對於包裝材料而言，我們擔心的是「洩漏」——那個讓微生物和雜質通過的真實缺陷。

重點提示 Key Notes

對於超低溫儲存（≤−80°C）產品：彈性橡膠塞在極低溫下可能失去彈性，導致氣體侵入。在升溫過程中，被捕集的氣體會造成容器內壓升高。這個特殊情境提醒我們，洩漏速率的評估必須涵蓋產品的整個生命週期，包括儲存、運輸及使用的所有溫度條件。

4. Closure Type and Mechanics | 密封類型與機制

原文內容 Original Text

An understanding of closure mechanics makes it possible to better characterize, monitor, and test packages for integrity. This section discusses various closure systems and how each type functions to ensure package integrity.

4.1 Physically Mated Closures | 物理配合密封

Closure can be achieved by the close physical mating of two surfaces that often are dissimilar in material composition. Examples include the interference fit of a plunger inserted in a syringe barrel, the compression fit of an elastomeric closure capped onto a vial finish surface, and the application force of a screw-cap torqued onto a bottle. Physically mated surfaces are not bonded together; therefore, a tiny gap exists even between well-closed components.

Nevertheless, when properly designed and assembled, closure systems fit together such that liquid leakage (and microbial ingress) is prevented and gas migration is limited. Regarding screw-thread closures such as those used for ophthalmic-product dropper bottles, the barrier to leakage is afforded by inner cap surfaces pressing against the package opening, in some cases aided by a secondary gasket or plug. The winding path afforded by the threads of a screw-cap does not provide an optimal barrier to gas or liquid leakage, or to microbial ingress in the event of liquid presence in the cap threads.

4.2 Physicochemically Bonded Closures | 物理化學鍵結密封

Seals are formed when two similar or dissimilar surfaces are physicochemically bonded together. One example is the formation and sealing of contiguous containers from a single material (e.g., glass or plastic ampules). In other form, fill, and seal systems, sheets of material are mated by means of a heat or ultrasonic welding process. Two dissimilar materials may be joined using an intermediate bonding material. Fully fused seals inherently block liquid leakage and microbial ingress; however, gaseous leakage and permeation may occur.

4.3 Multiple-Dose Package Closures | 多劑量包裝密封

Multiple-dose package closures are designed to allow product access while limiting microbial ingress and product leakage between doses. For example, elastomeric closures for parenteral products requiring needle-puncture access are designed to offer reseal protection against microbial ingress and product leakage. Some multiple-dose ophthalmic dosage form package closures are designed with filters, plugs, or other mechanical means that allow the product to be dispensed while restricting microbial ingress and product leakage.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

三種密封類型比較：

  • 物理配合（Physically Mated）：靠機械力壓緊，存在微小間隙，完整性依賴組件尺寸公差與組裝品質。例：西林瓶 + 橡膠塞 + 鋁蓋
  • 物理化學鍵結（Physicochemically Bonded）：材料融合在一起，本質上更好的液體和微生物屏障，但仍可能有氣體洩漏。例：玻璃安瓿、BFS（Blow-Fill-Seal）容器
  • 多劑量密封（Multiple-Dose）：設計上允許反覆刺穿後恢復密封（reseal），是折衷設計——方便使用 vs. 維持完整性

比喻說明 Analogy

想像三種飲料包裝：橡皮塞西林瓶像螺旋蓋的礦泉水（物理配合，靠壓力密封）；安瓿像用火封口的玻璃管（物理化學鍵結，熔合後本質封閉）；多劑量藥瓶像啤酒桶的出酒龍頭（多劑量，每次使用後必須自動「恢復密封」）。不同設計有不同的完整性挑戰。

重點提示 Key Notes

螺旋蓋的警示：USP 明確指出，螺旋蓋的螺紋「winding path（曲折路徑）」並非最佳的氣體、液體或微生物屏障。如果螺旋蓋沒有輔助墊片或塞子，其密封可靠性低於橡膠塞西林瓶。在設計眼藥水瓶等產品的完整性測試時，需要特別注意這一點。

多劑量包裝的特殊規定：多劑量包裝在「使用中（in-use）」需要單獨建立 in-use maximum allowable leakage limit（使用中最大允許洩漏限值），因為使用過程本身會反覆破壞密封。

實務應用 Practical Application

作為 CDMO，你可能同時生產：①單劑量安瓿（物理化學鍵結）；②橡膠塞西林瓶（物理配合）；③多劑量眼藥水瓶（多劑量密封）。每種包裝的完整性驗證策略都應針對其密封機制設計——安瓿的 CCI 測試著重熔封品質；西林瓶的測試關注橡膠塞壓縮力和鋁蓋封口品質；多劑量瓶還需額外的「反覆穿刺後密封性」驗證。

5. Product–Package Quality Requirements and the Maximum Allowable Leakage Limit | 產品–包裝品質要求與最大允許洩漏限值

原文內容 Original Text

As noted in Introduction, package integrity is necessary to maintain product critical quality attributes within physicochemical label-claim specifications and to ensure product sterility until time of use. Most package types display very low but definite gaseous leakage flow through the gap that exists even between well-fitted closures. Therefore, it is not practical to require that packages be absolutely leak-free. Rather, it is the significance of leakage in relation to product quality that needs to be considered. In other words, the package should not permit leakage beyond the product's maximum allowable leakage limit.

5.1 Sterility and Product Formulation Content must be Preserved; Gas Headspace Content Preservation is not Required

This category includes product–packages for which the maximum allowable leakage limit correlates to the prevention of product formulation escape, or product contamination by external liquid or solid matter or by microorganisms, while gas headspace preservation (i.e., ambient pressure air) is of no concern.

A key study series found orifice leaks of approximately 0.1 µm in nominal diameter placed in rigid packages demonstrate a small risk of aqueous liquid passage, while orifice leaks as small as approximately 0.3 µm in nominal diameter first demonstrate some risk of microbial ingress by liquid challenge. Given this body of evidence, a maximum allowable leakage limit of less than 6 × 10⁻⁶ mbar · L/s (measured by helium mass spectrometry in the vacuum mode) can be adopted for products in this category packaged in rigid container–closure systems.

5.2 Sterility, Product Formulation Content, and Gas Headspace Content must be Preserved

The second category includes product–packages for which the package must preserve the gas headspace content or absolute pressure in addition to preventing product formulation escape and contamination. Preservation of headspace content and/or pressure is needed to maintain product stability within physicochemical specification limits, and/or to aid end-user product access. Thus, the maximum allowable leakage limit for such products is likely more stringent than that described in the first category.

5.3 Sterility must be Preserved; Product Access is Required (Multiple-Dose)

This subcategory represents a quality requirement applicable only to multiple-dose product–packages. Once the product–package has reached the end-user and the closure has been activated or otherwise compromised to allow dosage access and delivery, the in-use maximum allowable leakage limit is defined in terms of microbial ingress and product loss prevention between and during dosage access.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Maximum Allowable Leakage Limit（最大允許洩漏限值，MALL）：這是一個科學與風險決策的結果，而非固定標準。它取決於：

  • 產品對微生物侵入的敏感性（有無防腐劑？）
  • 頭部空間氣體是否需要保護（凍乾品、氧敏感品）
  • 包裝材料和幾何形狀
  • 產品生命週期（儲存、運輸、使用條件）

三類產品品質要求：

  • 類別 1：保護無菌性 + 液體完整性（頭部空間不重要）→ MALL ≤ 6×10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s
  • 類別 2：以上 + 頭部空間保護 → 更嚴格的 MALL
  • 類別 3（多劑量）：以上 + 使用中重複密封 → 需要 in-use MALL

公式與計算 Formula

類別 1 的 MALL 基準（剛性容器）：
MALL < 6 × 10⁻⁶ mbar · L/s
（氦質譜法真空模式測量）

對應的孔徑意義：
0.1 µm 孔徑 → 低風險液體洩漏
0.3 µm 孔徑 → 開始出現微生物侵入風險
< 6×10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s → 微生物侵入概率 < 0.10

類別 2 的 MALL：
更嚴格，由頭部空間允許變化量反推
（每種產品需個別建立）

重點提示 Key Notes

「絕對不洩漏」是不現實的：即使是完美組裝的西林瓶，橡膠塞和瓶頸之間也會有極微小的氣體通道。USP 的務實做法是確認洩漏速率低於 MALL，而非追求零洩漏。這是科學導向的監管思維。

超低溫儲存的特殊考量：在 ≤ −80°C 儲存的產品，橡膠塞可能暫時失去密封性導致氣體侵入。升溫後內壓升高。即使這個產品「不需要」保護頭部空間，超低溫儲存也可能把它升級為「類別 2」的考量。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 接受新品委託時，應在早期 CMC 會議中確認產品屬於哪一類洩漏風險類別：①是否需要保護頭部空間氣體（凍乾品、含 O₂ 敏感成分）？②是否為多劑量包裝？③儲存溫度是否涉及超低溫？這些問題的答案直接決定完整性測試方法的選擇和驗證要求的嚴格程度。

6. Inherent Package Integrity and the Package Integrity Profile | 固有包裝完整性與包裝完整性檔案

原文內容 Original Text

Inherent package integrity is the leakage characteristic of a well-assembled container–closure system using no-defect package components. Inherent package integrity is first determined during product–package development and qualification and is a measure of the leak tightness of a container–closure system, given anticipated variables of material composition, dimension, processing, and assembly.

Confirmation that the inherent package integrity conforms to the maximum allowable leakage limit is the first step in product–package integrity verification. Verification of package integrity continues throughout the product life cycle. The package integrity profile is an ongoing database of product life cycle package leak and seal quality test results. This profile provides information regarding package integrity given operative variations in package component design and material; package assembly and processing; and product storage, distribution, and stability.

In summary, a product–package system having integrity is one in which the inherent package integrity conforms to the required product–package maximum allowable leakage limit. Further, the package integrity profile database operates as a risk management tool to ensure that finished product container–closure systems are intact, able to block microbial ingress, restrict loss of product contents including critical headspace gases, and prevent entry of detrimental gases or other substances, thus ensuring that the product meets all relevant physicochemical and microbiological label-claim specifications through expiry and final end-use.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Glossary | 詞彙表（節錄重要定義）

原文內容 Original Text

Deterministic leak test method: A deterministic leak test method is one in which the leakage event being detected or measured is based on phenomena that follow a predictable chain of events. In addition, the measure of leak detection is based on physicochemical technologies that are readily controlled and monitored, yielding objective quantitative data.

Probabilistic leak test method: A probabilistic leak test method is the converse of a deterministic leak test method, being stochastic in nature. Probabilistic tests rely on a series of sequential and/or simultaneous events, each associated with random outcomes described by probability distributions. Thus, the findings are associated with uncertainties that necessitate large sample sizes and rigorous test-condition controls to obtain meaningful results.

Detection limit: The leak test detection limit is a measure of test method sensitivity. The detection limit is the smallest leakage rate (or leak size) that a leak test method can reliably detect. Also called limit of detection.

Positive control: A positive control is a package with a known, intentional defect. Positive controls used for leak test method development and validation studies should duplicate study negative controls in terms of materials of construction, package assembly, and component processing.

Negative control: A negative control is a package with no known leak. Negative controls used for leak test method development and validation studies represent packages optimally assembled using normally processed components.

核心概念：確定性 vs. 概率性方法

Deterministic（確定性）方法：基於物理化學原理，每次給出客觀定量數值（如壓力衰減值、氦氣洩漏速率）。可控性強、重現性好，是 FDA 和業界推薦的首選。

Overview: Deterministic Test Methods Framework | 確定性方法框架概述

原文內容 Original Text

Package integrity tests are leak test methods. A leak test is a method that detects the presence of (and in some cases, the magnitude or location of) a package breach or gap.

Integrity test methods vary not only in their application and detection limit, but also in terms of detection range, precision, and specificity. No one test is appropriate for all packages or for all leak testing applications. Selection criteria for package integrity test methods, as well as method comparison aids, are presented to guide the user in the selection process.

Leak tests, even many commonly recognized industry standard testing approaches, require optimization and validation for each product–package application. A science- and risk-based approach may allow some tests to be leveraged for broader application under certain circumstances.

"A deterministic leak test method is one in which the leakage event being detected or measured is based on phenomena that follow a predictable chain of events. In addition, the measure of leak detection is based on physicochemical technologies that are readily controlled and monitored, yielding objective quantitative data." — USP 〈1207〉 Glossary

The key attributes of deterministic methods that distinguish them from probabilistic methods:

  • Based on predictable physical or chemical phenomena
  • Yield objective, quantitative data (a number, not a pass/fail observation)
  • Readily controlled and monitored instrumentation
  • Can be directly correlated to leakage rate or leak size
  • Amenable to statistical analysis and trending
  • Do not rely on random events (e.g., microbial growth)

Deterministic methods are preferred by FDA because they provide a quantitative measurement that can be directly compared to the established maximum allowable leakage limit (MALL). The FDA Container Closure Integrity guidance (2023) explicitly supports the move toward deterministic methods over traditional probabilistic approaches such as dye ingress and microbial ingress testing.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

確定性 vs. 概率性方法的本質差別：

確定性（Deterministic）方法：每次測試的結果都依循可預測的物理規律。例如，你施加一個真空，觀察壓力回升速度——如果有洩漏，壓力就會回升，這是必然的物理現象。儀器給出的是一個壓力讀數（定量數值）。

概率性（Probabilistic）方法：結果依賴一系列隨機事件。例如染料浸入試驗，染料是否進入取決於染料濃度、浸泡時間、毛細管效應、操作者判斷等多種隨機因素。最終的「通過/不通過」帶有主觀性。

重點提示 Key Notes — FDA 的立場

FDA 在 2023 年更新的容器密封完整性指引中，明確傾向確定性方法。監管機構的邏輯很清楚：確定性方法給出可量化數據（如 1.2×10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s），可以直接與已建立的 MALL 比較，而概率性方法的「沒有染料進入」只是一個主觀觀察。

對 CDMO 的實務影響：如果你的產品仍在用染料浸入法作為主要 CCI 測試，應該認真考慮遷移到確定性方法，並準備好遷移驗證（method transfer/bridging study）的計畫。

比喻說明 Analogy

確定性方法就像用數位血壓計量血壓——每次都給出一個精確數字（120/80 mmHg），可以與正常值直接比較。概率性方法像中醫把脈——有經驗的醫師可以判斷，但結果因人而異、難以量化。現代監管要求的是「數位血壓計」式的客觀數據。

1.1 Vacuum Decay / Pressure Decay Methods | 真空衰減法 / 壓力衰減法

原文內容 Original Text

Vacuum decay and pressure decay methods are among the most widely used deterministic leak test approaches for pharmaceutical packaging. These methods are referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉 as physicochemical leak test technologies.

Vacuum Decay (真空衰減法): In vacuum decay testing, the test package is placed in a sealed test chamber, and a vacuum is drawn. If the package has a leak, gas from inside the package will escape into the test chamber, causing the chamber vacuum (absolute pressure) to rise. The rate of pressure increase is measured and correlated to the leakage rate. Vacuum decay instruments can detect leaks in vials, bottles, blister packs, flexible pouches, and other package types.

Pressure Decay (壓力衰減法): In pressure decay testing, the test package headspace (or the test chamber) is pressurized, and the rate of pressure decrease is monitored. A leak will cause the pressure to decay more rapidly. This method is applicable to packages where internal pressurization is feasible.

Key performance characteristics:

  • Sensitivity: typically capable of detecting leaks in the range of 10⁻³ to 10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s depending on instrument design and package type
  • Nondestructive: packages can be returned to the batch after testing
  • 100% inspection capability in automated inline systems
  • Results are quantitative (pressure change per unit time)
  • Requires calibration with positive controls of known leak size
  • May be challenging for highly flexible or deformable packages

The vacuum decay method is widely used for vials closed with elastomeric stoppers and aluminum crimp caps and is amenable to high-speed automated 100% inspection on filling lines. It is a deterministic method: the pressure reading is a direct physical measurement, not dependent on a secondary reaction or biological response.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

真空衰減法的工作原理：

想像把西林瓶放入一個密封箱，然後抽真空。如果瓶子密封完好，裡面的氣體跑不出來，箱子的真空保持穩定。如果瓶子有洩漏，裡面的氣體會慢慢漏進箱子，導致真空度下降（絕對壓力上升）——儀器偵測到這個壓力變化，就判定洩漏。

靈敏度範圍：不同儀器設計的靈敏度差異很大。高端設備可達 10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s 量級，可以偵測比 MALL 更小的洩漏。

公式與計算 Formula

真空衰減法的基本原理：

測試步驟：
1. 將包裝放入密封測試室
2. 抽真空至目標壓力（如 1–10 mbar 絕對壓力）
3. 隔離測試室，監測壓力變化 ΔP/Δt

洩漏速率估算：
Q (mbar·L/s) ≈ ΔP(mbar) × V_chamber(L) / Δt(s)

校準要求：
- 使用已知孔徑的正對照（positive control）
- 建立壓力變化率與洩漏速率的相關曲線
- 系統適用性驗證（system suitability）在每次測試前確認

靈敏度範圍（典型值）：
- 高端儀器：~10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s
- 常規設備：~10⁻³ to 10⁻⁴ mbar·L/s

重點提示 Key Notes

彈性包裝的挑戰：真空衰減法對剛性容器（vials, cartridges）效果最好。對於軟袋（flexible bags）、泡罩包裝（blister packs）等有彈性的包裝，真空施加時包裝本身會變形，導致壓力讀數受到干擾（包裝收縮產生假陽性）。這種情況需要特殊的夾具設計或選用其他方法。

生產線整合：真空衰減法可整合到自動化充填封蓋生產線，實現 100% 線上檢測，是凍乾西林瓶和注射劑生產中最常見的 CCI 測試方式之一。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 的生產驗證中，真空衰減法常用作西林瓶批放行的 CCI 測試。驗證計畫應包括：①靈敏度評估（最小可檢測洩漏）；②特異性（是否有假陽性，例如殘留水分影響）；③樣品代表性（不同充填量、不同橡膠塞批次）；④ 100% 線上檢測的系統適用性規程。

1.2 High Voltage Leak Detection (HVLD) | 高壓電洩漏檢測法

原文內容 Original Text

High Voltage Leak Detection (HVLD, 高壓電洩漏檢測) is a deterministic, nondestructive integrity testing method based on electrochemical principles. HVLD is referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉 as one of the key physicochemical integrity test technologies.

Operating principle: HVLD applies a high voltage (typically in the range of 1–15 kV) across the package wall. When the package contains an aqueous, conductive liquid, the conductive fluid and the ground path outside the container create an electrical circuit. If a pinhole or defect in the container wall or seal area is present, the electrical resistance drops dramatically, and the instrument detects an increase in current flow. The change in electrical signal indicates a leak.

Key features and application range:

  • Highly sensitive: capable of detecting pinholes in glass or plastic walls as small as a few micrometers
  • Requires the product to be electrically conductive (aqueous formulations with ionic conductivity)
  • Not applicable to non-conductive products (oils, organic solvents, gas-only headspace)
  • Suitable for glass vials, plastic containers (PFS, blow-fill-seal), cartridges
  • Nondestructive; packages can return to the production stream
  • Can detect defects in both the container body and the closure/seal region
  • High-speed testing compatible with automated inspection systems

HVLD is particularly valued for its ability to detect very small pinholes in the container wall itself — a defect type that some other methods may miss if they focus only on the closure area. For lyophilized products, HVLD may be limited because the reconstituted cake is not electrically conductive during the test.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

HVLD 的工作原理：把一個充滿導電液體的玻璃瓶想像成電池中的電解質。當你在瓶子外壁施加高電壓時，如果瓶壁完好，電流無法穿透。一旦有針孔缺陷，導電液體透過缺陷形成電流通路，電阻驟降、電流飆升——儀器偵測到這個電信號異常就判定洩漏。

關鍵限制條件：產品必須具有電導性（ionic conductivity，離子導電性）。這是 HVLD 最重要的適用前提——凍乾粉、油性製劑、非水溶液通常不適用。

比喻說明 Analogy

HVLD 就像在泳池水面施加電壓——如果水裡沒有任何連通外界的破口，電流被絕緣材料（玻璃壁）阻斷。一旦玻璃壁有針孔，導電的水與外部形成迴路，就像保險絲短路一樣立即被偵測。這也說明為什麼純水（去離子水）或油性製劑難以用 HVLD 測試——沒有足夠的離子導電性，即使有破口也不會形成有效的電流迴路。

重點提示 Key Notes

HVLD 的獨特優勢：HVLD 是少數能直接偵測容器壁針孔（而非只偵測密封區域）的方法。玻璃瓶在製造過程中可能存在極細微的氣泡或薄弱點，這類缺陷在真空衰減法中可能因洩漏量極小而漏網，但 HVLD 對幾微米的針孔仍有靈敏度。

電壓選擇：電壓需要根據容器壁厚度和材質優化。過低的電壓會降低靈敏度，過高的電壓可能損傷容器或影響產品（需要驗證電壓對產品無影響）。

1.3 Headspace Analysis | 頭部空間分析法

原文內容 Original Text

Headspace Analysis (頭部空間分析) is a deterministic leak test method that monitors the composition or pressure of gas in the headspace of a container as an indicator of package integrity. It is particularly valuable for packages where headspace gas content is a critical quality attribute, and is referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉.

Two primary forms of headspace analysis are used in pharmaceutical CCI:

  • Headspace Gas Analysis (Laser-Based / Near-Infrared Spectroscopy): Uses tunable diode laser absorption spectroscopy (TDLAS, 可調二極體雷射吸收光譜) or near-infrared (NIR) spectroscopy to measure the concentration of specific gases (e.g., O₂, CO₂, H₂O vapor) in the headspace of a sealed container without opening it. A leak will manifest as a change in headspace gas composition over time (e.g., rising O₂ in a nitrogen-purged vial).
  • Headspace Pressure Analysis: Monitors the absolute pressure within a sealed container. For containers sealed under vacuum or low-pressure conditions (e.g., lyophilized vials), loss of vacuum over time indicates a leak.

Key advantages and applications:

  • Truly nondestructive and noninvasive: the laser passes through the glass wall without contact
  • Can be performed at multiple time points throughout shelf life (stability program)
  • Directly detects the product quality attribute of concern (headspace composition)
  • Compatible with high-speed 100% inline inspection systems
  • TDLAS provides quantitative O₂ or CO₂ concentration data
  • Can detect very slow leaks that only manifest over time (chronic leak detection)

Headspace analysis is especially appropriate for lyophilized products sealed under vacuum, oxygen-sensitive biologics sealed under nitrogen, and any product where headspace preservation is a Category 2 requirement per USP 〈1207〉.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

頭部空間分析的核心邏輯：這個方法的獨特之處在於它直接量測的是「產品品質」而不只是「包裝缺陷」。對於一個充氮保護的生物製劑，氧氣濃度上升不只代表有洩漏，更直接代表產品的氧化風險。這讓測試結果與臨床品質屬性（CQA）直接掛鉤。

TDLAS 技術：可調二極體雷射吸收光譜（TDLAS）利用特定氣體分子對特定波長雷射的吸收特性來定量。O₂ 在特定近紅外波長有強吸收峰。雷射穿透玻璃瓶後，根據吸收量計算出頭部空間氧氣濃度——全程非接觸，不需要刺破瓶塞。

比喻說明 Analogy

TDLAS 就像隔著玻璃用紅外線溫度計量體溫——不需要接觸，雷射穿過玻璃直接量到目標物質的特性。相比之下，傳統的頭部空間氣體分析需要插針取樣，是破壞性的。TDLAS 讓你可以在整個穩定性研究期間對同一支西林瓶反覆測量，追蹤氧氣濃度的時間趨勢。

重點提示 Key Notes

穩定性研究中的連續監測：頭部空間分析特別適合在穩定性研究中使用，因為它可以非破壞性地在同一批次上重複測量（0、3、6、12、24 個月）。如果 O₂ 濃度在某個時間點突然上升，可以及時發現「慢速洩漏（chronic leak）」——這種洩漏在生產時的 100% 檢測中可能因洩漏速率極慢而未被發現。

凍乾品的特殊應用：凍乾西林瓶通常在部分真空下密封（如 100 mbar 絕對壓力），定期進行頭部空間壓力監測是評估密封品質的標準做法。FDA 在檢查中常問及凍乾品的 CCI 策略，TDLAS 或壓力監測是監管認可的確定性方法。

1.4 Mass Extraction Method | 質量萃取法

原文內容 Original Text

The Mass Extraction method (質量萃取法) is a deterministic vacuum-based leak test technique referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉. It is related to vacuum decay but provides a more direct measurement of gas flow rather than inferring leakage from pressure change alone.

Operating principle: The test package is placed in a sealed test chamber, and a vacuum is applied. A sensitive mass flow sensor (or equivalent) directly measures the quantity of gas extracted from the package into the test chamber as a function of time. Unlike vacuum decay which measures the rise in chamber pressure, mass extraction measures the actual gas volume removed.

Advantages compared to vacuum decay:

  • More directly quantitative: measures actual gas volume extracted, not inferred pressure change
  • Can be more precise for packages with very large headspace volumes where pressure changes are small
  • Less susceptible to interference from gas solubility differences in the product formulation
  • Provides a direct leakage rate measurement (volume per time)

Applications:

  • Suitable for vials, ampoules, bottles with significant headspace
  • Can be adapted for flexible packages with appropriate tooling
  • Used in laboratory development and method validation studies
  • May be challenging for packages with very small headspace volumes

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

質量萃取 vs. 真空衰減：兩者都是「負壓驅動」的方法，差異在於量測的物理量：真空衰減量測的是壓力變化率（△P/△t）；質量萃取直接量測從包裝中萃出的氣體質量或體積。對於大體積頭部空間的包裝（如大輸液袋），微小洩漏引起的壓力變化可能很小（測量不精確），但氣體萃取量的直接計量則更可靠。

比喻說明 Analogy

真空衰減就像用壓力計測量輪胎氣壓隨時間的下降；質量萃取就像直接測量從輪胎中抽出了多少氣體分子。後者在輪胎體積很大（壓力變化不明顯）時更精確——例如你要檢測一個巨大的儲氣槽是否有微小洩漏。

重點提示 Key Notes

質量萃取法在大體積靜脈輸液袋（如 500 mL 生理鹽水）的 CCI 測試中有明顯優勢，因為大袋子的少量氣體洩漏對壓力的影響很小，但直接量測萃取出的氣體量則更靈敏。在選擇測試方法時，包裝體積（Volume）是重要的決策因素。

1.5 Tracer Gas Methods — Helium Mass Spectrometry | 示蹤氣體法——氦質譜法

原文內容 Original Text

Tracer gas leak test methods (示蹤氣體法) use a specific gas as a tracer to detect the presence and/or measure the rate of leakage. The most sensitive and widely used tracer gas method in pharmaceutical package integrity testing is Helium Mass Spectrometry (氦質譜法), referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉.

Helium mass spectrometry operating modes:

  • Vacuum mode (accumulation mode): The package, previously filled or backfilled with helium, is placed in a sealed vacuum test chamber. The chamber is evacuated, and a mass spectrometer detects and quantifies helium gas that leaks from the package into the test chamber. This is the most sensitive mode.
  • Sniffer mode (detector probe mode): A portable mass spectrometer "sniffer" probe is passed over the exterior of a helium-pressurized package. Escaping helium is detected at the probe. Less sensitive than vacuum mode, but useful for locating the leak site.

Critical specifications:

  • Sensitivity: helium mass spectrometry in vacuum mode is capable of detecting leaks as small as 10⁻⁹ to 10⁻¹⁰ mbar·L/s — orders of magnitude more sensitive than vacuum decay
  • Quantitative: provides a direct helium leakage rate in mbar·L/s
  • The MALL of < 6 × 10⁻⁶ mbar·L/s for Category 1 products (per USP 〈1207〉) was established using helium mass spectrometry as the reference measurement technique
  • Requires packages to be filled or backfilled with helium before testing (or tested in a helium atmosphere)
  • Destructive if packages must be filled with helium gas (product replacement may be needed) — however, can be nondestructive if packages are tested in a helium-filled test chamber without internal modification

Helium mass spectrometry serves as the reference standard for package leak testing, against which other test methods are calibrated and compared during method development and validation.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

為什麼選氦氣作示蹤氣體？

  • 氦（He）是最小的惰性氣體分子（原子序 2），穿透任何微小孔隙的能力最強
  • 大氣中氦含量極低（約 5 ppm），背景值低、信噪比高
  • 化學惰性，不與產品反應，不影響安全性
  • 質譜儀對氦有極高靈敏度

2.1 Probabilistic Test Methods Overview | 概率性方法概述

原文內容 Original Text

"A probabilistic leak test method is the converse of a deterministic leak test method, being stochastic in nature. Probabilistic tests rely on a series of sequential and/or simultaneous events, each associated with random outcomes described by probability distributions. Thus, the findings are associated with uncertainties that necessitate large sample sizes and rigorous test-condition controls to obtain meaningful results. Typically, sample size and test condition rigor are inversely related to leak size." — USP 〈1207〉 Glossary

Probabilistic methods share several defining characteristics that distinguish them from deterministic approaches:

  • Results are qualitative or semi-quantitative (pass/fail based on observation, not a direct physical measurement)
  • Outcomes depend on multiple random events occurring in sequence (e.g., liquid must enter the defect, traverse the tortuous path, and be visible)
  • Require large sample sizes to achieve statistical confidence — the smaller the leak, the larger the sample size needed
  • Test conditions must be tightly controlled to minimize variability, yet variability cannot be eliminated
  • Cannot be directly correlated to a specific leakage rate in mbar·L/s without extensive correlation studies
  • Results may vary between operators, laboratories, and test runs

Despite their limitations, probabilistic methods have a long history of use in pharmaceutical package integrity testing and remain in use today, particularly where deterministic alternatives have not been fully qualified. However, the current FDA guidance (2023) and industry consensus (PDA TR27, USP 〈1207〉) clearly advocate for transitioning to deterministic methods wherever feasible.

The three primary probabilistic methods covered in USP 〈1207.2〉 are:

  • Dye Ingress Test (染料浸入試驗)
  • Microbial Ingress / Microbiological Challenge Test (微生物浸入試驗)
  • Bubble Emission Test (氣泡發射試驗)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

「隨機（Stochastic）」的深層含義：概率性方法的結果不是單一物理測量的直接輸出，而是多個隨機事件連鎖的結果。以染料浸入法為例，測試結果取決於：①缺陷孔的大小和形狀；②毛細管效應的物理條件；③染料的表面張力和黏度；④浸泡時間；⑤染料濃度；⑥操作者的視覺判斷能力。任何一個因素的隨機變化都會影響結果。

「樣本量需求」的實務衝擊：對於接近偵測極限的小缺陷，概率性方法可能需要數百個樣本才能得到統計上有意義的結論。這在方法開發和驗證中是巨大的資源消耗。

重點提示 Key Notes

歷史背景：染料浸入法和微生物挑戰試驗在製藥行業已使用了幾十年，是許多公司驗證文件的歷史基礎。但 USP 1207 和 FDA 2023 指引的訊息很清楚：「歷史悠久」不等於「當前最佳實踐」。如果你的 CCI 驗證文件只依賴傳統概率性方法，在 FDA 檢查或 NDA/BLA 審查中可能面臨質疑。

比喻說明 Analogy

概率性方法像傳統天氣預報（只說「明天可能下雨」，成功率 70%）；確定性方法像現代氣象儀器（直接量測氣溫、濕度、氣壓，給出具體數值預報）。前者依賴經驗和統計，後者基於物理測量。監管機構現在要求的是「氣象儀器式」的客觀數據，而不是「老農預測式」的模糊判斷。

2.2 Dye Ingress Test | 染料浸入試驗

原文內容 Original Text

The Dye Ingress Test (染料浸入試驗) is a probabilistic, typically destructive package integrity test referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉. It has been one of the most widely used traditional CCI tests in the pharmaceutical industry.

Operating principle: The test package (typically filled with water, buffer, or the actual product) is immersed in a solution of a visible dye (most commonly methylene blue or tartrazine at a specified concentration) under conditions of differential pressure (either vacuum or elevated external pressure). After the immersion period, the packages are removed, the exterior is cleaned, the packages are opened, and the solution inside is inspected for dye color.

Test parameters requiring control:

  • Dye type, concentration, and solution properties (surface tension, viscosity)
  • Applied pressure differential (vacuum or positive external pressure)
  • Duration of immersion
  • Temperature of the test solution
  • Visual inspection method (visual or spectrophotometric analysis for quantitation)

Limitations of dye ingress:

  • Typically destructive: packages cannot be released after the test
  • Cannot detect very small leaks (below the capillary flow threshold for the dye under test conditions)
  • Results are qualitative: colored = fail, not colored = pass; cannot quantify leakage rate
  • Sensitive to test conditions: different pressures, times, and dye concentrations can give different results for the same package
  • Challenging for opaque or colored containers, or products with naturally colored formulations
  • Cannot be directly converted to a leakage rate in mbar·L/s without a correlation study using a reference method (e.g., helium mass spectrometry)

Despite these limitations, dye ingress may still be used as a supporting technique during method development, as a visual reference in validation studies, or in situations where no validated deterministic alternative exists yet. If used, it must be properly validated with documented positive and negative controls.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

染料浸入法的核心問題——毛細管作用閾值：染料液體要進入一個微小缺陷，必須克服表面張力。非常小的孔隙（< 0.1–0.3 µm）下，毛細管排斥力可能大於浸入驅動力，染料根本進不去。這意味著染料浸入法對於最小的「問題洩漏」（正好在 MALL 附近的洩漏）可能完全沒有偵測能力——而這些洩漏恰恰是最需要偵測的。

破壞性檢測的商業影響：由於染料浸入是破壞性測試，測過的產品不能放行，只能做抽樣檢測。這意味著你不能做 100% 批放行，而只能依靠統計推斷，本身就帶有不確定性。

比喻說明 Analogy

染料浸入法像用藍色墨水測試雨衣是否防水：把雨衣浸在藍色墨水裡，看裡面有沒有變藍。問題是：①測試後雨衣不能賣了（破壞性）；②極細微的針孔可能因表面張力而阻止墨水進入（漏網）；③「有藍色還是沒藍色」的判斷依賴人眼（主觀性）。現代汽車工廠檢測車門密封用超聲波掃描，而不是潑油漆看滲漏——這就是從概率性走向確定性的轉變。

重點提示 Key Notes

染料浸入法的「合規生存空間」：FDA 不是說「立即禁止使用染料浸入法」，而是說「應轉移到確定性方法」。在過渡期間，如果你仍在使用染料浸入法，需要：①有完整的驗證（方法驗證報告）；②有明確的正負對照；③有方法相關研究（correlation study）建立染料法結果與 MALL 的關係；④有清晰的遷移計畫，說明何時完成確定性方法的驗證。

2.3 Microbial Ingress / Microbiological Challenge Test | 微生物浸入試驗

原文內容 Original Text

The Microbiological Challenge Test (微生物挑戰試驗), also called microbial ingress test, is a probabilistic package integrity test that evaluates whether microorganisms can enter a sealed package under the test conditions. It is referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉 and historically has been considered the "gold standard" for CCI testing of sterile products.

Operating principle: The test package (filled with growth-supportive media such as tryptic soy broth, TSB) is immersed in a suspension of a specified challenge microorganism (typically a small bacteria such as Serratia marcescens or Brevundimonas diminuta) in an aqueous challenge medium. After the challenge period, the packages are incubated and inspected for turbidity (growth) as evidence of microbial ingress.

"A microbiological challenge test is a package leak test whereby package integrity is evaluated by exposing containers filled with growth-supportive media to microorganisms suspended in submersion media (a liquid-borne challenge test). Leakage is evidenced by the subsequent growth of the challenge microorganisms in the package contents." — USP 〈1207〉 Glossary

Critical limitations and USP's assessment:

  • Large sample sizes required: because microbial ingress is inherently probabilistic (a single cell must enter, survive, and grow), large numbers of test units are needed to achieve statistical confidence, especially at small leak sizes
  • Cannot quantify leakage rate: a positive result (turbid = growth) confirms ingress occurred but cannot say at what leakage rate
  • Destructive: packages must be opened or discarded after testing
  • Test conditions (microbial challenge concentration, immersion conditions, incubation conditions) significantly affect results
  • At orifice diameters smaller than ~0.3 µm, the probability of microbial ingress becomes very low even with large sample sizes — meaning the test may pass a package that has a concerning leak
  • Historically misused as the sole definition of "sterile package integrity" — USP now explicitly broadens the definition

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

為什麼微生物挑戰試驗不再是「黃金標準」？

這個方法的最大問題是：它只能評估「微生物侵入」這一個風險維度，而 USP 1207 現在定義的包裝完整性涵蓋三個維度（微生物侵入、產品外漏/汙染物進入、頭部空間氣體改變）。即使一個包裝「通過」了微生物挑戰試驗，它仍可能因頭部空間氧氣升高而導致藥品降解——這個真實的品質風險被微生物試驗完全忽略了。

另一個問題是0.3 µm 的「盲點」：比0.3 µm 小的洩漏孔，即使真的存在，微生物也幾乎無法通過，所以微生物試驗會「通過」這種包裝——但這個孔對氣體洩漏來說已經是 MALL 的邊界了。

比喻說明 Analogy

用微生物挑戰試驗評估「包裝完整性」，就像用「有沒有老鼠進來」評估家裡的防蟲措施——老鼠進不來，不代表螞蟻進不來，不代表水汽進不來，不代表你的食物不會腐壞。微生物挑戰試驗只問一個問題（細菌進不進來），而現代 CCI 評估需要回答三個問題（菌、液體/固體汙染物、氣體）。

重點提示 Key Notes

樣本量計算的監管挑戰：在 FDA 審查中，審查員常常要求看微生物挑戰試驗的樣本量計算（sample size justification）。如果你用了 30 個樣本，但統計上需要 200 個才能達到 95% 置信區間（P < 0.05），你的驗證數據可能被認為不足。確定性方法沒有這個問題，因為它給出的是可直接量化的數字，不依賴統計推斷。

實務應用 Practical Application

如果你的 CDMO 客戶要求在 NDA/BLA 申報中用微生物挑戰試驗作為主要 CCI 方法，你需要主動建議：①補充確定性方法的驗證資料；②至少進行相關性研究，建立微生物試驗結果與洩漏速率的關聯；③在文件中說明微生物試驗的局限性並提供科學理由；④制定確定性方法的遷移計畫。這不只是法規合規，也是保護客戶的商業投資。

2.4 Bubble Emission Test | 氣泡發射試驗

原文內容 Original Text

The Bubble Emission Test (氣泡發射試驗) is a probabilistic, visual leak test method based on detecting gas bubbles escaping from a pressurized package when submerged in or wetted by a liquid. It is referenced in USP 〈1207.2〉.

Operating principle: The test package is pressurized (internally) or placed under differential pressure, then submerged in a liquid (water, surfactant solution, or a designated test fluid). Gas leaking from the package through any breach forms visible bubbles. The location and rate of bubble formation are observed visually.

Variants:

  • Submersion method: Package is fully submerged in a liquid bath, and bubbles are observed on the package exterior
  • Wet method / trace method: Specific areas of the package are wetted with test fluid, and localized bubble formation is observed

Key characteristics:

  • Can provide information on leak location as well as detection
  • Qualitative: results are based on visual observation of bubbles — subjective and operator-dependent
  • Not suitable for small leaks: very small leaks may produce bubbles too small to see, or below the bubble formation threshold
  • Cannot quantify leakage rate
  • Potentially destructive (liquid exposure may damage some products)
  • Limited to packages that can be pressurized with gas
  • Primarily used in R&D, troubleshooting (OOS investigations), and method development — not typically suitable for routine 100% batch testing

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

氣泡試驗的最佳使用場景：雖然氣泡試驗靈敏度有限且主觀性強，但它有一個獨特優點：可以直接看到洩漏的位置（哪個地方冒泡，洩漏就在哪裡）。這讓它在以下場景非常有用：

  • OOS 根本原因分析（RCA）：當其他方法偵測到洩漏但無法定位時，氣泡試驗可以找出具體位置
  • 初步篩選/可行性評估：在方法開發早期，快速判斷某種缺陷類型是否產生可觀察的洩漏
  • 工廠模擬缺陷驗證：確認人工製作的缺陷（如雷射鑽孔）確實存在並連通

比喻說明 Analogy

氣泡試驗就像用肥皂水找輪胎漏氣點——只要氣泡在哪裡冒出來，洩漏就在那裡。簡單直觀，但有兩個問題：很小的洩漏可能產生的氣泡太微小難以肉眼看見，而且測試後輪胎表面被弄濕了。這個方法用來「找在哪裡漏」很好用，但不能用來判斷「漏了多少」。

重點提示 Key Notes

在 FDA 483 觀察意見（Form 483）中，CCI 相關的常見問題包括：①驗證方法靈敏度不足；②缺乏與 MALL 的相關性研究；③概率性方法樣本量不足；④操作者培訓記錄缺失。氣泡試驗因為高度依賴操作者目視判斷，特別容易受到這類質疑。

2.5 Method Validation Requirements | 方法驗證要求

原文內容 Original Text

Package integrity leak test method validation is a process that demonstrates the suitability of a given leak test method for a specific product–package application. Validation is required for both deterministic and probabilistic methods, though the validation approaches differ. Key validation parameters for CCI methods include:

  • Accuracy (準確性): The method's ability to correctly differentiate packages that leak above the detection limit from those that do not leak. Measured by the rate of false positives and false negatives using positive controls (packages with known, characterized defects) and negative controls (optimally assembled, defect-free packages).
  • Sensitivity / Detection Limit (靈敏度 / 偵測極限): The smallest leakage rate (or defect size) that the method can reliably detect. The method's detection limit must be at or below the established MALL.
  • Specificity (特異性): The method's ability to distinguish leaking from non-leaking packages despite interfering factors (e.g., package surface condensation, product residue, environmental noise).
  • Precision (精密度): The method's ability to yield reliable, repeatable data. Includes repeatability (same operator, same day), intermediate precision (multiple operators, multiple days), and reproducibility (multiple sites).
  • Linearity / Quantitation Limit: For quantitative methods — the ability to yield results proportional to leak size, and the smallest leak size that can be quantified with acceptable accuracy and precision.
  • Robustness (耐用性): The method's ability to accurately identify leaking vs. non-leaking packages despite small but deliberate variations in test parameters.

System suitability: A system suitability test should be performed prior to each test run to verify that the instrument and method are performing correctly. System suitability typically involves testing a positive control and a negative control to verify that the method can detect the target leak size and does not produce false positives.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

CCI 方法驗證的核心邏輯：驗證的目的是回答兩個問題：①這個方法夠靈敏嗎（能偵測到 MALL 附近的洩漏）？②這個方法夠準確嗎（不會漏掉真正有問題的包裝，也不會誤判好的包裝）？

關鍵驗證項目的實際意義：

  • 準確性（Accuracy）：用已知孔徑的正對照和完美包裝的負對照，計算假陽性（false positive）和假陰性（false negative）率
  • 靈敏度（Sensitivity）：方法的偵測極限必須 ≤ MALL
  • 精密度（Precision）：重複測試同一個洩漏，結果要一致（確定性方法通常比概率性方法好很多）
  • 特異性（Specificity）：排除干擾因素，如玻璃瓶表面的水珠不會誤觸發警報

公式與計算 Formula

CCI 方法驗證核心指標計算：

False Positive Rate (誤拒率) =
  False Positives / (True Negatives + False Positives)

False Negative Rate (誤放率，更危險) =
  False Negatives / (True Positives + False Negatives)

對 CCI 而言，False Negative Rate 更關鍵：
→ 漏掉一個洩漏的包裝 = 可能有不合格產品流入市場

檢測能力（Detection Power）：
用已知洩漏速率的 positive controls 測試，
確認方法在 MALL 處有充分靈敏度（通常要求 ≥ 95% 偵測率）

System Suitability（系統適用性）：
每次測試前必須確認：
- Positive control 判定為不合格（方法有靈敏度）
- Negative control 判定為合格（方法無假陽性）

重點提示 Key Notes

假陰性（False Negative）是最危險的錯誤：假陽性浪費產品（好的包裝被誤判為洩漏），但假陰性更嚴重（有洩漏的包裝被判定合格，流入市場）。CCI 方法驗證必須特別關注偵測極限，確保方法不會「漏掉」 MALL 附近的洩漏。

正對照的選擇：正對照必須真實模擬生產中可能出現的缺陷。雷射鑽孔（laser-drilled holes）是常用的人工缺陷，因為孔徑可以精確控制和標定（通常用氦質譜法驗證其洩漏速率）。但要注意：雷射孔是近乎圓形的直通孔，而真實洩漏是曲折路徑——兩者行為可能有差異，需要進行 type defect（型態缺陷）研究。

  2. 什麼是「Robustness（耐用性）」驗證？請舉出三個你會故意改變的測試參數，來評估真空衰減法的耐用性。
2.6 Method Transfer Considerations | 方法轉移考量

原文內容 Original Text

Method transfer (方法轉移) in the context of CCI testing refers to the process of extending a validated test method to a new product–package application, or of transferring a validated method from one testing site to another. The extent of validation work required for a method transfer depends on the degree of similarity between the new application and the original validated application.

Key considerations for method transfer:

  • Package similarity: If the new product–package has the same container and closure type, same material of construction, and same size range as the originally validated package, the transfer validation effort may be reduced (leveraging existing validation with limited bridging studies).
  • Product differences: Changes in product formulation (viscosity, conductivity, color) may affect the performance of the test method and require re-evaluation.
  • MALL similarity: If the MALL for the new product–package is less stringent than the MALL for the original application, and the test was already validated at the more stringent level, transfer may be straightforward.
  • Instrument equivalency: If the transfer is to a different instrument of the same type (e.g., vacuum decay instrument from Vendor A to Vendor B), instrument qualification (IQ, OQ, PQ) and comparative performance testing against the original instrument are required.
  • Regulatory strategy: For methods used in approved applications (NDA, BLA, ANDA), changes to the test method or transfer to a new site may require a prior approval supplement (PAS) or CBE-30 notification depending on the nature of the change.

A science- and risk-based approach to method transfer allows some simplification of the validation package when the differences between the original and new application are minimal and well-documented. However, the core validation parameters (detection limit, accuracy at MALL) must always be demonstrated for the specific product–package being tested.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

  • 產品導電性變了嗎？（影響 HVLD 的靈敏度）
  • 容器壁厚變了嗎？（影響 HVLD 的電壓設定）
  • 頭部空間體積變了嗎？（影響真空衰減的壓力讀數）
  • MALL 更嚴格了嗎？（需要確認方法仍夠靈敏）

重點提示 Key Notes

CDMO 的方法轉移挑戰：CDMO 常常需要為來自不同客戶的多個產品驗證類似的 CCI 方法。可以建立一個「通用驗證框架（platform validation）」，以最嚴格的條件（最小的 MALL，最具挑戰性的包裝）為基礎驗證，然後用橋接研究（bridging study）將方法延伸到較不嚴格的應用場景。這樣可以顯著減少每個產品的驗證工作量，但需要有充分的科學理由支持。

法規申報影響：如果方法轉移涉及已批准的藥品，必須評估是否需要提交補充申請（supplement）。通常情況下，同廠同設備的方法擴展（line extension）風險最低；跨廠或換設備供應商需要更多文件支持。

2.7 Regulatory Expectations — Transition from Probabilistic to Deterministic | 監管期望：從概率性方法向確定性方法的轉移

原文內容 Original Text

The current regulatory environment strongly encourages the transition from probabilistic CCI test methods to deterministic alternatives. Key regulatory positions include:

  • FDA Container Closure Integrity Testing Guidance (2023): States that deterministic methods are preferred for package integrity testing because they provide objective, quantitative data that can be directly compared to the MALL. The guidance acknowledges that probabilistic methods may still be used but requires robust justification and validation when deterministic alternatives exist.
  • USP 〈1207〉 framework: Defines both deterministic and probabilistic methods but provides a clear framework that ties CCI to the MALL and encourages quantitative, science-based approaches.
  • PDA Technical Report No. 27 (2013, revised): Provides detailed guidance on sterility testing and CCI, recommending deterministic methods as the current state of the science.
  • ICH Q8/Q9/Q10 framework: Although not specific to CCI, the Quality by Design (QbD) and risk-based approach principles support using the most scientifically rigorous test methods available.

When probabilistic methods are still being used, the following must be demonstrated:

  • Method is fully validated for the specific product–package system
  • Positive controls are well-characterized with documented leak sizes
  • A correlation study has been performed to link the qualitative result to the MALL
  • Sample sizes are statistically justified
  • A transition plan to deterministic methods is in place, or a scientific justification for continuing to use probabilistic methods is documented

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

  • 短期（0–12 個月）：盤點所有現有 CCI 方法；確認哪些是概率性方法；確認哪些方法有完整驗證文件和 MALL 相關性研究
  • 中期（1–3 年）：優先對新產品採用確定性方法；為高風險產品（生物製劑、凍乾品）啟動方法轉換計畫
  • 長期：建立確定性方法平台（如自動化真空衰減 + TDLAS），作為所有新產品的預設 CCI 策略

  • 概率性方法（Dye Ingress、Microbial Ingress、Bubble Emission）結果受多種隨機因素影響，帶有固有不確定性，需要大樣本量，且通常無法直接量化洩漏速率
  • 染料浸入法：破壞性、定性（通過/失敗）、靈敏度有毛細管閾值限制，對最小的問題洩漏可能失效；微生物挑戰試驗：只評估微生物侵入一個風險維度，且在 < 0.3 µm 洩漏孔時靈敏度迅速下降
  • CCI 方法驗證必須涵蓋準確性、靈敏度/偵測極限（必須 ≤ MALL）、特異性、精密度和耐用性；假陰性（False Negative）是最危險的錯誤
  • 方法轉移時需評估包裝相似性、產品差異、MALL 嚴格程度變化，並通過橋接研究確認等效性；法規申報影響必須提前評估
  • FDA 2023 指引和 USP 1207 明確優先確定性方法；CDMO 應建立現有概率性方法的完整驗證（包括 MALL 相關性研究）並制定向確定性方法遷移的計畫
