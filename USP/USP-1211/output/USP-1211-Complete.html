Introduction — 緒論

原文內容 Original Text

This informational chapter provides general information on the concepts and principles involved in the preparation of materials that must be sterile. Within the strictest definition of sterility, an item is deemed sterile only when it contains no viable microorganisms. However, this textual definition cannot be applied to actual items labeled as sterile because of irresolvable limitations in testing. Sterility cannot be demonstrated without the destructive testing of every sterile unit.

In a real sense, microbiological safety is achieved through the implementation of interrelated controls that in combination provide confidence that the items are suitable for use as labeled. It is the controls that provide the desired assurance from microbiological risk rather than the results of any in-process or finished goods testing. The verification of safety of products labeled sterile is generally known as “sterility assurance” and that nomenclature will be used throughout this chapter.

“It is the controls that provide the desired assurance from microbiological risk rather than the results of any in-process or finished goods testing.”

The establishment of an effective sterility assurance program requires information about the material to be sterilized. An initial determination should be made regarding the potential for terminal sterilization of the material in its primary container applying the principles defined in Sterilization of Compendial Articles 〈1229〉. As described, the appropriate process provides a balance between conditions that are lethal to potential bioburden present in/on the item and those that preserve its essential quality attributes.

Depending upon the results of that determination, sterility of the item may be achieved by either aseptic processing or terminal sterilization. The potential for a process that relies on both the protective measures inherent to aseptic processing and the lethal nature of terminal sterilization may offer advantages.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 無菌的悖論

無菌（Sterility）的絕對定義：一個物品「不含任何有生命力的微生物」。這是一個全有或全無（all-or-nothing）的概念。

根本矛盾：要證明一個物品是無菌的，唯一的方法是把它打開、培養、測試 — 但這個動作本身就破壞了無菌狀態。你無法在不破壞產品的前提下證明它是無菌的。

因此：USP 明確表示，無菌保證不是來自「測試結果」，而是來自「整體控制系統」— 包括設施設計、設備、人員、製程控制、環境監控等所有要素的綜合表現。

比喻說明

機場安全類比：一個機場能否確保沒有危險物品上飛機？你無法逐一檢查每位旅客身上的每個細胞。但透過 X 光機、金屬探測門、行李掃描、安全人員巡邏、旅客身份驗證等多層控制，你可以將風險降到極低水準。

「無菌保證」就是這個道理 — 不是靠最後的一次測試來「證明」安全，而是靠整個系統的每一層防護來「確保」安全。

重點提示 — USP〈1211〉的定位

Informational Chapter（資訊性章節）：USP 章節編號在〈1000〉以上的屬於資訊性章節，不具有強制性法規效力。但〈1211〉是所有無菌製造的概念基礎，被 FDA、EMA 等監管機構廣泛引用。

串連功能：〈1211〉就像一張路線圖，將無菌試驗〈71〉、滅菌方法〈1229〉系列、環境監控〈1116〉、去熱原〈1228〉等章節的核心概念整合為一個完整的無菌保證架構。

Sterility Assurance Level (SAL) — 無菌保證水準

原文內容 Original Text

Terminal Sterilization

Terminally sterilized products are the lowest risk category of sterile pharmaceutical products. Unlike products aseptically manufactured under conditions designed to prevent microbial ingress, terminally sterilized products are subjected to a sterilization process that imparts a quantifiable safety level. Terminal sterilization processes achieve this by delivering measurable physical conditions that correspond to microbial lethality.

For terminally sterilized products, sterility assurance is defined in terms of the probability of nonsterility (PNS), or the probability of the terminal sterilization process generating a nonsterile unit (PNSU). Terminal sterilization processes must achieve a consistent validated performance of a PNSU of ≤10−6 (a probability of NMT 1 nonsterile unit in 1 million units produced).

The convention by which terminal sterilization cycles are developed and validated ensures that the actual PNSU is typically much lower (better) than the minimum standard of <10−6.

Aseptic Processing

There are a substantial number of sterile products that cannot be terminally sterilized because of adverse impact on the product/package’s essential properties and must be prepared by aseptic processing. Aseptic processes are designed to prevent the introduction of viable microorganisms into/onto separately sterilized materials during their assembly into a sealed sterile package.

Regardless of process scale, all of the individually sterilized materials must be protected from contamination from the point of sterilization through closure of the primary package. This is accomplished through adherence to the principles described below in which an ISO 5 condition is maintained when materials are exposed to the environment.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — SAL 10−6

Sterility Assurance Level（SAL）：終端滅菌製程的安全水準以「非無菌機率」（PNSU）來定量表達。SAL 10−6 表示每一百萬個單位中，最多只有一個非無菌單位的機率。

  • SAL 10−6 = 百萬分之一的非無菌機率
  • 這是終端滅菌的「最低門檻」— 實務上驗證過的製程通常遠優於此
  • SAL 的概念僅適用於終端滅菌製程，無菌製程（Aseptic Processing）無法以此方式量化

核心概念 — 無菌製程的 ISO 5 要求

ISO 5 環境：無菌製程中，所有暴露在環境中的已滅菌物料必須處於 ISO 5 等級的潔淨環境下。ISO 5 的含義是每立方公尺空氣中 ≥0.5 μm 的粒子數不超過 3,520 個。

這個要求確保了從滅菌完成到密封封裝之間的整個「暴露窗口」都有最高等級的環境保護。

Post-Aseptic Processing Terminal Sterilization — 無菌製程後終端滅菌

原文內容 Original Text

An aseptic process followed by a terminal sterilization process provides superior control over the presterilization bioburden, such that the subsequent sterilization process can be designed with less overall lethality, thereby making it possible to substantially extend the use of terminal sterilization to products with greater sensitivity to the applied energy of the process.

From a patient safety perspective, this approach has the following distinct advantages:

  • An adventitious contaminant introduced during aseptic processing is easily killed by the terminal sterilization step, reducing the extent of in-process environmental monitoring performed
  • Bioburden controls for the terminal process are simplified because all units have been aseptically filled
  • Where a product is made using either process alone, the limitations of each (no terminal lethal component in aseptic, more degradation in terminal sterilization) would persist
  • Where bioburden is controlled through aseptic processing, terminal sterilization can be applicable at lower lethality levels

The focus must be on the ability of the process to kill bioburden organisms rather than biological indicators (see 〈1229〉 and Moist Heat Sterilization of Aqueous Liquids 〈1229.2〉).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 混合策略的優勢

Aseptic + Terminal = 最佳方案？USP〈1211〉提出了一個進階策略：先用無菌製程充填，再用溫和的終端滅菌處理。這個組合解決了兩種方法各自的弱點：

  • 無菌製程的弱點：無法殺死意外引入的污染微生物
  • 終端滅菌的弱點：標準滅菌條件（如 121°C / 15 min）可能破壞熱敏感產品
  • 混合方案：因為無菌充填已將生物負載控制到極低水準，終端滅菌只需要很溫和的條件即可達到 SAL 10−6

比喻說明

雙重安全鎖：想像一間金庫。第一道門（無菌製程）已經排除了 99.99% 的入侵者。第二道門（終端滅菌）只需要一個普通的鎖就夠了，因為能闖過第一道門的人已經極少。如果只靠第二道門，你就需要一個超級重型鎖（高溫長時間滅菌），但這可能會損壞金庫裡的物品（產品品質劣化）。

重點提示 — CDMO 實務應用

策略價值：對 CDMO 而言，這個混合策略代表可以將終端滅菌的適用範圍擴展到更多產品類型。關鍵在於：

  • 產品穩定性研究必須確認溫和滅菌條件不會影響產品品質
  • 需要對生物負載（bioburden）來源進行全面評估，而非僅依賴生物指示劑（BI）
  • 這個策略在 EU Annex 1（2023）中也得到認可，監管趨勢是鼓勵更多使用終端滅菌
Quality by Design in Sterile Manufacturing — 品質源於設計

原文內容 Original Text

Consideration of these principles adapts the Quality by Design (QbD) approach widely adopted in regulatory standards. Using QbD concepts in sterile operations is markedly different from the applications in the typical formulation, pharmaceutical, chemical, or biological synthesis process.

The establishment of direct linkage between a monitored condition and process outcome with respect to sterile manufacturing is statistically difficult and analytically uncertain. The situation with respect to the definition of physical design elements is similar. Given the great variation in sterile product manufacturing with respect to scale, configuration, and complexity, it follows that the design alternatives and operating practices must also be flexible.

Thus, the recommendations provided in this chapter are entirely non-numeric, because there are no ready means with which to demonstrate the suitability of specific values. Instead, QbD for sterile processing should be driven toward a singular goal of optimizing contamination control with a particular focus on the microbial risk impact of personnel.

Recognition that operating personnel are the most significant contributors of microbiological risk leads to design preferences and operating principles:

  • The use of automation technology—to reduce or eliminate personnel interventions and thus personnel-borne contamination
  • The use of separative technologies—to eliminate, to the extent technically possible, human sourced contamination

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — QbD 在無菌製造的獨特性

為什麼無菌製造的 QbD 不同？在一般製藥製程中，你可以建立「輸入參數」與「輸出品質」之間的直接數學關係（如攪拌速度 vs 粒徑分布）。但在無菌製造中：

  • 你無法建立「環境微粒數 = X」→「產品無菌機率 = Y」這樣的直接關聯
  • 環境監控數據都是「快照」— 只代表採樣那一刻的狀態
  • 微生物污染是隨機、低機率事件，統計分析困難

因此 USP〈1211〉明確表示：本章所有建議都是「非數值性」的 — 不會給你一個具體的微粒限值或 CFU 上限，而是提供設計原則和控制策略。

重點提示 — 人員是最大風險源

USP〈1211〉反覆強調的核心訊息：在無菌製造中，人是最大的微生物污染來源。這個認知直接驅動了兩大設計原則：

  • 自動化（Automation）：減少人員介入的需求
  • 隔離技術（Separative Technologies）：如隔離器（Isolator）、RABS，將人與無菌區域物理隔離

這與 EU Annex 1（2023）的方向完全一致 — 新法規明確偏好隔離器和 RABS 而非傳統開放式潔淨室。

Containers and Closures — 容器與封蓋

原文內容 Original Text

The container and closure for a sterile formulation are integral parts of the sterile product. The container materials provide essential protection to the product throughout its shelf life and are chosen to minimize interaction with, preserve the quality attributes of, and facilitate dispensing of the sterile product.

The container materials should be readily sterilizable, either separately prior to filling and/or together with the formulation in a terminal process. Containers and closures should be selected for:

  • Reliability of container–closure integrity over the shelf life
  • Absence of interaction with formulation materials
  • Ease of handling in the processing environment and during administration
  • Tolerance of variation in equipment and other components
  • Cleanliness including freedom from particulates and absence of leachable or extractable chemicals
  • Compatibility with the product
  • Control over endotoxin content (where appropriate)
  • Protection of components prior to use (where appropriate)
  • Compatibility with preparation, sterilization, and depyrogenation processes (where appropriate)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 容器封蓋系統的雙重角色

容器封蓋系統不僅是「裝藥品的瓶子」，它是無菌產品的一部分。它必須同時滿足：

  • 保護功能：在整個貨架壽命期間維持無菌狀態（Container-Closure Integrity, CCI）
  • 惰性要求：不與藥品發生化學反應（可溶出物/可提取物 E&L）
  • 製程相容性：能夠承受滅菌和去熱原處理的條件

重點提示 — CDMO 容器選擇策略

對 CDMO 而言，容器選擇是一個需要平衡多方需求的決策：

  • 客戶指定 vs 內部標準化：不同客戶可能指定不同的容器供應商，但 CDMO 應建立經過驗證的標準容器組合
  • CCI 驗證：必須涵蓋整個貨架壽命，包括運輸振動、溫度變化等挑戰條件
  • E&L 評估：需要在產品開發早期就開始，避免後期發現不相容問題
Decontamination — 去污染

原文內容 Original Text

Decontamination is a broadly defined term used to describe a variety of processes that reduce microbial populations without an expectation for total kill. It is not a substitute for sterilization; a sterilization process should be used wherever possible.

Decontamination processes are ordinarily separated into two major categories based upon their effectiveness against spore-forming microorganisms:

  • Sporicidal treatments — used in critical applications such as isolator decontamination, air-lock/pass-throughs. Their toxicity to personnel and sometimes corrosive chemistry may preclude their exclusive use
  • Non-sporicidal agents — have fewer safety and material impact concerns; the occasional use of a sporicidal agent is required to control spore populations

Applications for decontamination include:

  • Decontamination of controlled environments and non-product contact surfaces
  • Decontamination of items upon transition into an environment of higher classification
  • Periodic decontamination of operator gloves during processing
  • Decontamination of product contact surfaces (large equipment manually sanitized in addition to sterilization)
  • Re-decontamination of sterilized equipment after aseptic assembly or intervention

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 去污染 vs 滅菌

USP〈1211〉明確區分了兩個經常被混淆的概念：

關鍵原則：能滅菌就不要只做去污染。去污染是「不得已的備案」，不是「偷懶的選擇」。

重點提示 — 殺芽孢劑的實務考量

殺芽孢劑（如 VHP、過氧乙酸）雖然效力強，但有兩個限制：

  • 人員毒性：操作人員需要適當防護或等待殘留濃度降低
  • 材質腐蝕：長期使用可能損害設備表面

因此實務上採用「輪替策略」— 以非殺芽孢劑為常規使用，定期（如每週一次）使用殺芽孢劑來控制芽孢菌群。

Equipment — 設備

原文內容 Original Text

Equipment used for sterile product manufacturing varies in its impact on the manufacturing process and on product quality and should have several important characteristics:

  • Operate reliably and produce products of consistent quality
  • Not adversely impact essential product quality attributes
  • Be easily cleanable and sterilizable, as necessary
  • Minimize human intervention during set-up and operation through such features as physical separation, automation, and robotics
  • Be tolerant of variations in container–closure materials
  • Designed to minimize product exposure to the background environment

USP〈1211〉classifies equipment into three impact categories:

  • Direct contact equipment — items in direct contact with drug substance, drug product, raw materials, and primary packaging (e.g., mixing vessels, piping, filters, filling pumps, lyophilizer shelves). Product contact surfaces require validated cleaning and sterilization procedures including dirty, clean, and sterile hold times.
  • Indirect contact equipment — electro-mechanical elements (non-product contact) of filling machines, stoppering machines, and sterilizers that can affect fill weight, particle size, moisture level, and other CQAs.
  • Other equipment — conveyors, turntables, balances, air samplers, carts. Influence is largely on the environments.

其他設備 — 基本要求

直接接觸設備的清潔和滅菌驗證必須包含三個保持時間：髒污保持時間（dirty hold）、清潔保持時間（clean hold）、無菌保持時間（sterile hold）。

重點提示 — 拋棄式設備的興起

USP〈1211〉提到「適當考慮材質相容性後，可以使用拋棄式設備（Single-Use Disposable Equipment）」。這反映了產業趨勢：

  • 拋棄式設備消除了清潔驗證和交叉污染的風險
  • 特別適合 CDMO 多產品、小批量的營運模式
  • 但需要進行可提取物/可溶出物（E&L）評估
Facilities — 設施

原文內容 Original Text

The core activities for sterile product manufacture are carried out in classified environments operating in conformance with the ISO 14644 series of standards. A pressure cascade descending from the more critical areas to less critical is commonplace.

Human operators within ISO 14644 classified cleanrooms used in aseptic processing are the greatest risk to product safety; therefore, no single risk mitigation factor in aseptic processing is more important than minimizing risk emanating from gowned operators.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 從開放到封閉的進化路徑

USP〈1211〉的 Table 1 揭示了無菌製造技術的進化趨勢 — 從傳統潔淨室到封閉系統，人與產品之間的隔離程度不斷提升：

  • 傳統潔淨室：人在 ISO 5 區域內操作，風險最高
  • Open RABS：有物理屏障但可打開，中等隔離
  • Closed RABS：更高程度隔離，手套操作
  • 隔離器：完全隔離，背景環境可降至 CNC
  • 封閉系統：無需製程環境分級，最高隔離

注意：背景環境等級隨著隔離程度提升而降低。這就是隔離技術的核心價值 — 降低對背景環境的依賴。

比喻說明

太空人 vs 實驗室：傳統潔淨室就像穿著防護衣在開放實驗室工作 — 你的防護衣再好，人體散發的微粒仍會影響周圍環境。隔離器就像太空艙 — 太空人完全在密封環境中操作，外面的環境好壞不影響內部。封閉系統更進一步 — 連太空人都不需要了，全自動運行。

重點提示 — RABS 中途開啟的陷阱

USP〈1211〉明確指出：製程中途開啟的 RABS 應被視為傳統潔淨室。這是一個經常被忽略的規則 — 如果你的 RABS 設計允許中途開啟進行人工介入，它就失去了隔離技術的優勢，必須按照開放式潔淨室的標準進行環境控制和監控。

  3. USP 說「人是最大的微生物風險源」。在你的工廠中，有哪些具體的人員介入操作可以透過自動化來消除？
Materials — 物料

原文內容 Original Text

The preparation of sterile products encompasses a wide range of materials including active pharmaceutical ingredients (small and large molecules), excipients, solvents (usually water), process gases and processing aids, all of which contribute to the microbiological quality attributes of the product.

Controlling bioburden in materials and formulated product is a critical aspect of sterility assurance. The microbiological attributes of materials should be controlled through adequate supplier controls, shipping, receipt, sampling, handling, and storage. These controls should be commensurate with the microbiological risks to process controls and sterile product safety.

“Laboratory results should not be utilized to rationalize inadequate process controls on the part of the supplier.”

Risk assessment should consider the origin of the material (e.g., fermentation, chemical synthesis, biologically derived, enzymatic, semi-synthetic synthesis, natural origin). Materials of biological origin may have higher inherent microbiological risk. Materials may have inherent physicochemical properties that mitigate microbiological risks (e.g., low water activity, extreme pH, inherent antimicrobial properties) or increase microbiological risks (e.g., aqueous solution, growth supportive nutrients).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 生物負載控制鏈

USP〈1211〉建立了一個從供應商到成品的完整生物負載控制鏈：

  • 供應商控制：原料的微生物品質從源頭管理
  • 運輸與接收：確保運輸條件不導致微生物增殖
  • 取樣與檢驗：依據 USP〈61〉/〈62〉進行微生物限度檢查
  • 儲存管理：控制溫度、濕度等條件防止微生物生長

重點提示 — 檢驗結果不能為供應商的失控辯護

USP 的這句話直指一個常見問題：有些公司在收到微生物超標的原料時，會用「最終產品檢驗合格」來合理化接受。USP 明確否定這種做法 — 供應商的製程控制是獨立的品質要求，不能用下游的檢驗結果來取代。

對 CDMO 而言，這意味著需要建立健全的供應商稽核和合格供應商清單（Approved Supplier List）制度。

Sterilization & Parametric Release — 滅菌與參數放行

原文內容 Original Text

The most effective means for the control of microbial population is sterilization, a process that either kills or removes viable microorganisms. In the production of sterile products, sterilization processes are used to prevent microbial contamination.

Terminal sterilization processes that reproducibly destroy microorganisms in the final product container are the preferred means for the production of sterile products. Sterile products that cannot be terminally sterilized rely on individual sterilization processes (e.g., steam, radiation, gas, filtration) for the various materials that comprise an aseptically processed sterile product.

Terminal sterilization can rely on parametric release practices to obviate the need for sterility testing (see 〈1222〉).

“It should be recognized that parametric release is the default mode of sterile product release.”

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 參數放行是預設模式

USP〈1211〉做出了一個重要聲明：參數放行（Parametric Release）是終端滅菌產品的預設放行模式。這意味著：

  • 對於終端滅菌產品，理想情況下不需要進行無菌試驗〈71〉
  • 放行依據是滅菌製程的物理參數（溫度、時間、壓力等）是否在驗證的範圍內
  • 前提是滅菌製程已經過充分驗證，且有持續的過程監控

這是一個理念上的重大轉變 — 從「靠測試放行」轉向「靠製程控制放行」。

重點提示 — 參數放行的實施門檻

雖然 USP 將參數放行定位為「預設模式」，但在實務中，很多市場（特別是某些亞太地區法規市場）對參數放行的核准門檻較高。CDMO 在評估是否實施參數放行時需要考慮：

  • 所有目標法規市場是否都接受參數放行
  • 滅菌製程的驗證是否足夠全面
  • 持續監控系統是否可靠
  • 變更控制是否完善

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 容器封蓋系統：是無菌產品不可分割的一部分，選擇標準涵蓋 CCI、材質相容性、E&L、內毒素控制等九項要素
  • 去污染 ≠ 滅菌：去污染僅降低微生物數量，不期待完全殺滅。能滅菌的場景不應只做去污染
  • 設備三層級：直接接觸（最嚴格）→ 間接接觸 → 其他設備，驗證要求依層級遞減
  • 製造環境進化：傳統潔淨室 → RABS → 隔離器 → 封閉系統，人與產品的隔離程度持續提升
  • 人員風險：USP 再次強調人是最大微生物風險源，驅動自動化和隔離技術的採用
  • 物料控制：從供應商到儲存的全鏈條生物負載管理，檢驗結果不能為供應商失控辯護
  • 參數放行：USP 定位為終端滅菌的「預設放行模式」— 從「靠測試」轉向「靠製程控制」

Monitoring — 環境監控

原文內容 Original Text

Environmental monitoring is employed to qualitatively assess the effectiveness of the design and operational controls to provide suitable facility hygiene. It is neither a substitute for good facility, equipment, and process design, nor compensation for deficient practices and behavior.

There are inherent limitations with all forms of viable and non-viable monitoring in terms of sample size, sample location, and recovery capability that preclude their use as anything more than an indication that a facility is operating within an acceptable state of control.

Monitoring provides only a snapshot in time of the actual environmental conditions and excessive sampling due to its potentially intrusive nature can actually impair product safety or generate counts unrelated to process performance by increasing activity proximate to the critical zone.

“Environmental control can be measured only by the monitoring performed. Satisfactory monitoring performance is the result of proper design and operation as described in this chapter and not a means to establish that condition.”

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 環境監控的本質

USP〈1211〉對環境監控的定位非常明確：它是一個「評估指標」而不是「品質保證工具」。這個區別至關重要：

  • 環境監控合格 ≠ 產品一定是無菌的
  • 環境監控不合格 ≠ 產品一定是非無菌的
  • 好的監控結果是好的設計和操作的「結果」，不是它們的「替代品」

比喻說明

溫度計類比：環境監控就像你家裡的溫度計。溫度計顯示 22°C 不是因為溫度計本身在控制溫度，而是因為冷氣系統設計和運作良好。如果冷氣壞了，你把溫度計拿掉也不會讓房間變涼。而且溫度計只能告訴你它所在位置、那一刻的溫度 — 房間另一個角落的溫度可能完全不同。

重點提示 — 過度監控的反效果

USP 提出了一個常被忽略的警告：過度取樣反而可能降低產品安全性。原因是：

  • 每次取樣都是一次「介入操作」— 人員需要接近關鍵區域
  • 取樣動作本身可能擾動氣流或引入污染
  • 過多的取樣活動增加了關鍵區域周邊的人員活動量

這對 CDMO 的環境監控方案設計有直接指導意義 — 取樣點的數量和頻率需要基於風險評估，而不是「越多越好」。

Viable & Non-Viable Monitoring — 可培養與非可培養監控

原文內容 Original Text

Viable Monitoring

Viable monitoring consists of detecting and estimating the level of culturable microorganisms in the air, on surfaces, and on personnel. Sampling locations are defined following a risk assessment and sampling is executed by trained operators using a variety of methods including:

  • Active air sampling
  • Passive air sampling
  • Viable particle counting using fluorescence technology
  • Contact-plate sampling of surfaces, gloves, and gowns
  • Swabbing of surfaces
  • Personnel monitoring

Non-Viable Monitoring

Non-viable monitoring measures the number and size of particulates present in the air. It can be used to initially classify the cleanroom in accordance with ISO 14644-1 and to assess routine manufacturing conditions. When used for the purposes of monitoring, it can be performed under static conditions (no activity) and/or dynamic conditions (routine operation).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 兩類監控的互補關係

注意 USP 提到了螢光技術可培養粒子計數（Viable Particle Counting using Fluorescence Technology）— 這是一個新興技術，能提供接近即時的微生物偵測結果，正在被越來越多的先進設施採用。

Media Fills / Aseptic Process Simulations — 培養基充填 / 無菌製程模擬

原文內容 Original Text

Process simulations are exercises in which the performance of an aseptic activity is evaluated using a sterile growth medium. The medium can be directly substituted for the product or added to it.

Aseptic process simulations are typically performed before the introduction of new or revised process components (e.g., products, facilities, equipment, personnel, containers and closures, and processes) and periodically thereafter.

Process simulations should be fully representative of processing conditions and activities utilized during routine production.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — Media Fill 的設計原則

Media Fill（培養基充填試驗）是無菌製程獨有的驗證工具，其核心原則：

  • 代表性（Representative）：必須模擬所有常規生產條件和活動
  • 挑戰性（Challenging）：應涵蓋最差情況（worst-case）條件
  • 時機：新製程上線前 + 變更後 + 定期執行
  • 觸發事件：新產品、新設施、新設備、新人員、新容器封蓋、新製程

重點提示 — CDMO 的 Media Fill 挑戰

對 CDMO 而言，Media Fill 方案設計比單一產品工廠更複雜：

  • 多產品適用性：如何設計一個 Media Fill 方案能涵蓋多種產品的充填條件？
  • 容器多樣性：不同客戶使用不同規格的小瓶、注射器、卡匣，每種都需要驗證
  • 批量差異：小批量產品的 Media Fill 充填數量如何確定？
  • 人員輪替：所有可能參與無菌操作的人員都需要通過 Media Fill 資格認定

USP 強調「完全代表常規生產條件」— 這意味著 Media Fill 必須包含所有常規和可預見的糾正性介入操作。

Sterility Testing — 無菌試驗

原文內容 Original Text

The sterility test is a harmonized compendial test. It must be understood that while execution of the test is required for the release of sterile products where parametric release has not been approved, it cannot prove the sterility of the materials tested.

“It should be recognized that parametric release is the default mode of sterile product release.”

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 無菌試驗的根本局限

USP〈1211〉對無菌試驗〈71〉的評價是清醒而務實的：

  • 統計局限：無菌試驗通常只檢測每批 20 個樣品。一批 10,000 瓶的產品，即使有 1% 的污染率（100 瓶污染），20 個樣品全部通過的機率仍有 81.8%
  • 方法局限：培養基不能培養所有微生物種類；14 天培養期可能不足以讓所有微生物生長
  • 法規要求：儘管有這些局限，無菌試驗仍是未核准參數放行的產品的法定放行要求

重點提示 — 不要過度依賴無菌試驗

USP 的訊息非常明確：無菌試驗是必要的法規要求，但它的價值在於「偵測嚴重失控」而非「確認無菌」。真正的無菌保證來自整個製造系統的設計和控制。

這就是為什麼 FDA 稽查員不會只看你的無菌試驗結果 — 他們會檢查你的設施設計、環境監控趨勢、人員訓練記錄、介入操作程序、Media Fill 結果等整體表現。

Personnel — 人員

原文內容 Original Text

Personnel play an essential role in the preparation of sterile products. The essential activities they perform include cleaning, assembly, equipment operation, material transfer, environmental monitoring, and decontamination. While personnel are often necessary for the performance of these activities, the contamination derived from them must be prevented from entering the production materials before and after sterilization.

The personnel involved in the preparation of sterile products must:

  • Understand the principles of microbiology, sterilization/depyrogenation, aseptic processing, and contamination control
  • Be proficient and diligent in gowning practices. Personnel required to wear aseptic gowning should periodically demonstrate their ability to properly gown
  • Adhere to proper aseptic technique during all aseptic activities even when performed in a RABS or isolator
  • Be familiar with and adhere to standard operating procedures
  • Practice good personal hygiene to minimize contamination potential
  • Be trained in the proper and safe operation of necessary equipment
  • Be monitored microbiologically after performing aseptic operations

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 人員管理的七大支柱

USP〈1211〉列出了無菌操作人員的七項要求，可以歸納為三個層面：

  • 知識層面：理解微生物學、滅菌、無菌製程、污染控制原理
  • 技能層面：更衣熟練、無菌技術嫺熟、設備操作合格
  • 行為層面：遵守 SOP、良好個人衛生、接受微生物監控

特別值得注意的是：即使在 RABS 或隔離器中操作，仍需要遵守無菌技術。隔離屏障不是放鬆操作標準的理由。

重點提示 — CDMO 的人員資格挑戰

CDMO 面臨的獨特人員管理問題：

  • 高流動率：合約員工和臨時人員的訓練時間有限
  • 多產品操作：人員需要了解不同產品的特殊要求
  • 連續資格維持：定期更衣資格認定和 Media Fill 參與 — 資格失效意味著不能進入無菌區操作
Procedures & Interventions — 程序與介入操作

原文內容 Original Text

There are two types of interventions associated with the aseptic production of sterile drug products:

  • Inherent interventions — activities that are an integral part of the aseptic process and performed during every batch (e.g., set-up, replenishment of components, weight and volume checks, environmental monitoring)
  • Corrective interventions — activities that correct problems and might not be performed during every batch (e.g., stopper jams, broken glass, defective seals, liquid leaks, mechanical failures)

Each intervention should be covered by written procedures. The following concepts should be considered:

  1. Interventions must be recognized as increasing the risk of contamination and are to be avoided or designed out of the process to the extent possible
  2. Procedures should be critically reviewed to eliminate and/or simplify aseptic processes by reducing the frequency of inherent interventions
  3. Interventions should be designed for minimal risk of contaminating sterile and nonsterile materials
  4. All interventions should be performed using sterilized tools whenever possible
  5. Intervention procedures should be established in detail for all inherent interventions, and more broadly for corrective interventions
  6. Interventions should be incorporated in periodic media fills to evaluate the aseptic practices of the operators

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 介入操作管理的六原則

USP〈1211〉對介入操作的管理提出了系統性的六條原則，可以總結為一個遞進的控制策略：

  • 第一優先：消除 — 能不介入就不介入（透過設計消除）
  • 第二優先：簡化 — 無法消除的，降低頻率和複雜度
  • 第三優先：標準化 — 必須執行的，建立詳細的書面程序
  • 第四優先：驗證 — 在 Media Fill 中模擬所有介入操作

重點提示 — 「沒有程序的介入不得執行」

USP 原文明確指出：“No intervention should be permitted for which there is not a defined procedure.” 這是一個容易被忽略但極其重要的要求：

  • 每一個介入操作 — 不論是常規的還是糾正性的 — 都必須有書面程序
  • 操作員不能「即興發揮」來解決問題
  • 如果遇到沒有既定程序的異常情況，應該停止生產並諮詢 QA

對 CDMO 而言，這意味著介入操作程序清單必須涵蓋所有可預見的場景，並定期更新。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 盤點你工廠目前的常規介入操作（inherent interventions）。有哪些可以透過設備升級或自動化來消除？
  2. 你的 Media Fill 方案是否涵蓋了所有常規和糾正性介入操作？如果不是，缺少了哪些？
  3. 如果操作員在充填過程中遇到了 SOP 中未涵蓋的異常情況，你的工廠目前的處理流程是什麼？是否符合 USP 的要求？
Utilities — 公用系統

原文內容 Original Text

The manufacture of sterile products requires utilities that can have a substantial impact on the final product. Some utilities become an integral part of the formulated product (e.g., Water for Injection, Nitrogen) and appropriate design of the production and distribution system is essential.

Utilities included in the product, in direct product contact, and in the preparation of equipment, containers, closures, and other items must meet the requirements defined in the appropriate USP–NF monograph.

Essential practices to maintain controlled status of the utility systems include: calibration, change control, corrective and preventive maintenance, and ongoing process control.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 公用系統的影響層級

USP〈1211〉將公用系統依影響程度分為三類：

  • 成為產品一部分：WFI（注射用水）、氮氣 — 最嚴格要求，必須符合藥典專論
  • 直接接觸但不成為產品：純化水、潔淨蒸汽 — 用於清潔和去污染
  • 間接影響：真空、冷卻水 — 設備運作必需，但不接觸產品

維持公用系統受控狀態的四個要素：校正（Calibration）、變更控制（Change Control）、預防性維護（CAPA/PM）、持續過程控制（Ongoing Process Control）。

Summary — 總結

原文內容 Original Text

The safety of products labeled sterile requires that their critical quality attributes consistently meet specifications. Sterility is the most essential quality attribute. Sterility is an unqualified concept in which an item is devoid of living microorganisms capable of reproduction.

Monitoring of all types, environmental (viable and non-viable; air, surface, and personnel), media fills, and sterility tests are forms of microbiological analysis that have been historically employed as proof of “sterility”. These assessment tools cannot provide definitive evidence of either “sterility” or “nonsterility”, because the means to confirm either of those conditions non-destructively is not scientifically possible.

“Confidence in sterile product manufacturing is realizable only by a holistic approach in which all of the supportive elements of the operations are given due consideration and emphasis.”

The absolute nature of sterility presents the practitioner with an inherent paradox — there is no ready means to demonstrate sterility of an item in the absolute sense regardless of the means used to provide it.

The “sterility” of any item is definitively established by the process controls summarized in this chapter rather than any form of monitoring or sampling. For terminally sterilized products, greater weight can be placed on the sterilization process utilized than on any form of testing. Confidence in aseptic processing is a result of sound design, reliable equipment, quality materials, effective procedures, and personnel proficiency rather than through sampling dependent sterility testing, microbiological monitoring, and process simulation.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — USP〈1211〉的終極訊息

整個章節的結論可以濃縮為一句話：無菌保證來自於對所有支援要素的整體性管理，而非任何形式的測試或監控。

USP 在總結中最後一次強調了這個層級關係：

Process Controls 製程控制 — 最高權重
↓
Design + Equipment + Materials + Procedures + Personnel
↓
Monitoring + Media Fills + Sterility Testing — 輔助評估

比喻說明

考試 vs 教育品質：學生考試成績好不代表教育品質好 — 可能只是考題簡單。教育品質的保證來自課程設計、教師能力、學習環境、教材品質等整體系統。考試只是一個「快照」— 它能偵測嚴重問題，但無法證明教育品質優秀。

同理，無菌試驗通過不代表產品真的無菌。無菌保證來自設施設計、設備可靠性、物料品質、程序有效性、人員能力這個完整的系統。

重點提示 — 對稽查的實務意義

USP〈1211〉的 Summary 為 FDA、EMA 等監管機構的查廠邏輯提供了理論基礎。理解這個邏輯對 CDMO 準備稽查至關重要：

  • 稽查員不會因為你的無菌試驗全部通過就認為你的工廠沒問題
  • 他們會從設施設計開始，逐一檢查設備、人員、程序、環境監控等每個環節
  • 一個設計缺陷即使沒有導致監控異常，仍然是一個需要改善的發現
  • 核心問題永遠是：「你的整體控制系統能否持續提供無菌保證？」

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. USP 說環境監控、Media Fill 和無菌試驗都「無法提供無菌或非無菌的確定性證據」。如果這些工具的價值有限，為什麼法規仍然要求執行它們？
  2. 在你的 CDMO 工廠中，如果今天要進行一次全面的「無菌保證系統健康檢查」，你會按照 USP〈1211〉的哪些要素來建立檢查清單？
  3. USP〈1211〉說終端滅菌產品可以「將更大的權重放在滅菌製程上而非任何形式的測試」。對於你的無菌充填產品，相對應的「最大權重」應該放在哪裡？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 環境監控的定位：評估設計和操作效能的工具，不是品質保證手段。好的監控結果是好的系統的「結果」而非「原因」
  • 過度監控的風險：過多的取樣活動反而可能增加關鍵區域的污染風險
  • 可培養 vs 非可培養：兩類監控互補 — 前者偵測微生物但結果遲延，後者即時但無法區分生物性粒子
  • Media Fill 三原則：代表性、挑戰性、定期執行。必須涵蓋所有介入操作
  • 無菌試驗的局限：統計學上無法透過少量樣品證明一批產品的無菌性。它是法規要求，但不是品質保證的核心
  • 人員管理：七項要求涵蓋知識、技能、行為三個層面。即使在 RABS/隔離器中仍須遵守無菌技術
  • 介入操作：消除 > 簡化 > 標準化 > 驗證的遞進策略。沒有程序的介入不得執行
  • 終極結論：無菌保證 = 整體系統控制，不是任何單一測試的結果
