Introduction — 導論

Original Text 原文內容

The proper development and implementation of a sterilization process requires a number of important sequential steps to ensure that an appropriate sterilization process results in materials and products that are both microbiologically safe and suitable for their intended use.

Sterilization technologies that rely on heat, ionizing radiation, and chemicals often have the potential to alter the physicochemical properties of materials to which they are applied. In some cases, sterilization could either leave toxic residues on materials that were sterilized or result in impurities (i.e., leachables) being more readily released into products that contact them. Thus, it is necessary to consider sterilization not only as a means of eliminating microorganisms but also as a process that has the potential to change materials in a manner that impacts usefulness, safety, or both.

A balance between the microbial lethality of a sterilization process and other aspects of patient safety must be established. A robust sterilization process that satisfies both is the desired objective of the cycle development activities.

"A balance between the microbial lethality of a sterilization process and other aspects of patient safety must be established."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 Key Concept

滅菌週期開發的雙重目標：USP &langle;1229.14&rangle; 開宗明義提出滅菌開發必須同時滿足兩個目標：

  • 微生物安全（Microbiological Safety） — 確保滅菌後產品達到所需的無菌保證水準（SAL）
  • 產品適用性（Suitability for Intended Use） — 確保滅菌過程不會損壞產品品質或留下有害殘留物

這兩個目標有時是衝突的：更強的滅菌條件能提高微生物安全，但可能損害產品。週期開發的藝術就在於找到最佳平衡點。

重點提示 Key Notes

CDMO 實務觀點：在 CDMO 環境中，每接到一個新客戶產品都可能需要開發或調整滅菌週期。你無法用「一個週期打天下」的方式處理所有產品。蛋白質藥物、小分子藥物、醫療器材各有不同的熱敏感性。開發前必須充分了解產品的關鍵品質屬性（CQA）和材料特性。

Leachables（可溶出物）：滅菌可能促使容器/包裝材料釋放化學物質進入產品。這在預充填注射器、塑膠包材中特別重要，也是 E&L（Extractables & Leachables）研究的核心議題。

Process Screening — 製程篩選

Original Text 原文內容

Radiation: The methods for establishment of radiation sterilization are described in detail in ISO 11137, and adherence to the most recent version of that standard is recommended.

Sterilizing Filtration: Filtration sterilization processes rely on exclusion of microorganisms and differ substantially from those that rely on lethality (see Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids &langle;1229.4&rangle;). Process development for filtration must consider many factors, such as adverse effects of the fluid on the filter (e.g., chemical compatibility) and of the filter on the fluid (e.g., extractables and leachables), as well as adsorption of fluid components. Physical interactions such as pressure differential, hydraulic shock, flow rate, temperature, and filtration capacity must also be considered.

Other Sterilization Methods: The first step in the development of a sterilization cycle is exposing the materials to the sterilization process and evaluating the impact that the process has on them. Initially, this determination can be a general screening process in which gross changes in appearance may be sufficient to confirm incompatibility.

Where appearance is unchanged, the material should be evaluated for changes to its physical and chemical attributes. If the results are marginal, reductions in those parameters that most impact lethality and/or material effects may be beneficial. Tests conducted over a range of process lethality that would result in microbiological destruction should be performed.

The following conditions are merely examples for consideration when evaluating materials for compatibility with the sterilization processes indicated:

  1. Moist heat — at conditions sufficient to reliably kill the expected bioburden (see &langle;1229.1&rangle; and &langle;1229.2&rangle;)
  2. Dry heat — check for the lowest melting point of materials and process at 25° below the lowest melting temperature (see &langle;1229.8&rangle;)
  3. Liquids, gases, and vapors — at conditions sufficient to reliably kill the expected bioburden (see &langle;1229.6&rangle;, &langle;1229.7&rangle;, and &langle;1229.11&rangle;)

The selected sterilization method should be the process that has the least impact on the essential quality attributes of the material and delivers lethality sufficient to exceed the desired minimum probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 篩選原則

乾熱的 25°C 安全裕度：原文特別提到乾熱製程應在材料最低熔點以下 25°C 操作。這是一個實務性的安全裕度（Safety Margin）。例如：某塑膠組件熔點為 200°C，則乾熱除熱原（Depyrogenation）製程溫度應 ≤ 175°C。

PNSU 的首次出現：這裡首次提到 PNSU（Probability of a Nonsterile Unit），這是整個滅菌驗證的核心指標。藥典要求終端滅菌產品的 PNSU ≤ 10−6，即每一百萬單位中不超過一個非無菌單位。

比喻說明 Analogy

選鞋的過程：製程篩選就像幫產品「選鞋」— 你不會直接買最貴的鞋（最強的滅菌條件），而是先看腳的尺寸（產品特性）、穿著場合（預期用途）、材質耐受度（材料相容性），然後選一雙既合腳又耐穿的鞋（平衡致死性與產品保護）。

Bioburden Evaluation — 生物負載評估

Original Text 原文內容

In parallel with the process described above, an assessment of the bioburden present in or on the items to be sterilized should be conducted (see Monitoring of Bioburden &langle;1229.3&rangle;). The initial evaluation should include an estimation of bioburden population and resistance. A control strategy for periodic bioburden monitoring for resistance and population should be instituted regardless of the results of the initial study.

Bioburden Resistance: For the remaining sterilization processes, the execution of a boil test (100° for 10 min) can serve as a basic screen for spore-forming microorganisms. The absence of survivors in a boil test greatly simplifies the sterilization cycle development as the sterilizing conditions for non-spore-formers are less stressful on the production materials. If microorganisms are determined to survive the boil test, their resistance to the chosen sterilization process should be established. The boil test allows for estimation of a D value for a moist heat process.

Bioburden Population: If no microorganisms are recovered or only non-spore-formers are recovered, an estimation of bioburden population can be utilized to expedite selection of the appropriate process duration. As spore-forming bacteria are ubiquitous, the potential presence of spores in the bioburden should be given consideration.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 生物負載兩大維度

生物負載評估有兩個關鍵維度，缺一不可：

  • 族群數量（Population） — 滅菌前有多少微生物？用 N0 表示。數量越多，所需滅菌時間越長
  • 抗性（Resistance） — 這些微生物有多耐熱/耐化學品？用 D 值表示。抗性越高，所需條件越嚴苛

公式解析 D Value (Decimal Reduction Time)

D value = 在特定溫度下，使微生物數量減少 90%（1 個 log）所需的時間（分鐘）

例：D₁₂₁ = 1.5 min
→ 在 121°C 下，每 1.5 分鐘微生物數量減少到原來的 1/10

起始 10⁶ CFU：
  1.5 min → 10⁵
  3.0 min → 10⁴
  4.5 min → 10³
  6.0 min → 10²
  7.5 min → 10¹
  9.0 min → 10⁰ (1 CFU)
  → 繼續加熱才能達到 PNSU 10⁻⁶

D 值越大，微生物越耐熱，需要越長的滅菌時間。

重點提示 Boil Test 的角色

Boil Test（沸騰試驗）：100°C 加熱 10 分鐘 — 這是一個快速篩檢。營養型細菌（Non-spore-formers）會被殺死，芽孢菌（Spore-formers）則存活。

結果判讀：

  • 無存活菌 → 生物負載主要是營養型細菌 → 滅菌條件可以較溫和 → 對產品衝擊較小
  • 有存活菌 → 含芽孢菌 → 需建立其 D 值 → 滅菌條件需更嚴格

CDMO 實務：原料藥來源不同，生物負載差異極大。API 原料、賦形劑、包材各有不同的微生物風險。每個新產品轉入時都應重新評估。

Estimation of Process Duration — 製程時間估算

Original Text 原文內容

The minimum duration of the sterilization dwell period can be determined once the sterilizing method has been selected and the bioburden (as determined or assumed) information has been obtained. This is accomplished by rearranging Equation 1, used to estimate the PNSU (see Sterilization of Compendial Articles &langle;1229&rangle;).

Equation 1 — PNSU Estimation:

log NU = −F/D + log N0

Rearrangement of the equation and solving for F results in Equation 2:

F = D (log N0 − log NU)

Where:

  • NU = assumed PNSU (NMT 1 positive unit in 106 units)
  • F = dwell time of the sterilization process at a defined condition expressed in minutes
  • D = estimated D value of the bioburden in minutes
  • N0 = estimated maximum population of the bioburden

The calculated F value represents the minimum duration of the process dwell period to reduce the assumed initial microbial population to less than the desired PNSU. Safety factors can be added to the assumed D value of the bioburden, the minimum desired PNSU, and the bioburden population estimate.

The F value is equivalent to F0 for moist heat, FH for dry heat, or process dwell time L for other sterilization processes and is typically expressed in minutes.

Depending on the F value (or process dwell time) selected, the validation approach for sterilization can be determined. The preferred, but not required, method is the use of an overkill approach (see &langle;1229&rangle;) where the F value allows for complete inactivation of a resistant biological indicator. Where material limitations restrict the use of lengthy sterilizing conditions, the bioburden/biological indicator or bioburden methods may be more appropriate.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

公式解析 PNSU & F Value Calculation

Equation 1 — PNSU 估算公式：
  log N_U = −F / D + log N₀

Equation 2 — 解出 F（最低滅菌時間）：
  F = D × (log N₀ − log N_U)

各變數意義：
  N₀  = 初始生物負載（CFU）
  N_U = 目標 PNSU（通常 = 10⁻⁶）
  D   = 生物負載的 D 值（min）
  F   = 所需滅菌時間（min）

計算範例 Worked Example

假設條件：
  N₀ = 100 CFU (10²)
  D  = 1.5 min (生物負載 D₁₂₁ 值)
  目標 PNSU = 10⁻⁶

F = D × (log N₀ − log N_U)
F = 1.5 × (log 10² − log 10⁻⁶)
F = 1.5 × (2 − (−6))
F = 1.5 × 8
F = 12 min

→ 在 121°C 下至少需要 12 分鐘的保溫時間
→ 這是 F₀ = 12 的意義

安全裕度（Safety Factor）：實務上會在 D 值、N0、或 PNSU 上加入安全裕度。例如將 D 值假設為實測值的 2 倍，或將 N0 假設為實測值的 10 倍。

重點提示 驗證方法選擇

三種驗證方法（Validation Approach）：

  • Overkill Approach（過殺法） — 首選。F0 ≥ 12 min，可完全滅活高抗性 BI（如 G. stearothermophilus，D121 ≥ 1.5 min，106 孢子）。對材料要求最高
  • Bioburden/BI Approach — 當產品無法承受 Overkill 條件時使用。需同時考慮實際生物負載和 BI 的滅活數據
  • Bioburden Approach（純生物負載法） — 最溫和。僅基於實際生物負載數據設計週期。需要更嚴格的生物負載監控

CDMO 策略：對於耐熱產品（如不銹鋼器具、玻璃瓶），優先採用 Overkill — 驗證最簡單、文件最少、監管機構最認可。對於熱敏感產品（如蛋白質藥物），則需採用 Bioburden/BI 方法，並加強日常生物負載監控。

Formal Material Evaluation — 正式材料評估

Original Text 原文內容

Once the appropriate sterilization process has been chosen, the effects of that process on the materials should be reconfirmed. The requirements for this can vary from simple physical examinations for materials expected to be largely unaffected by the sterilization process, such as stainless steel or glass with steam, to comprehensive stability examinations for formulated products in their final container-closure configuration.

The criteria for these studies should be objective wherever possible. It is useful to do a literature search for materials about which there is minimal experience, and to search and discuss potential material effects with suppliers.

The preparation and sterilization of the test units for these formal studies can be conducted in a laboratory setting and must be fully documented.

When sterilizing using chemical agents, the sterilized materials should be tested for the presence of residual sterilant and its known degradation by-products. Post-sterilization cycle aeration or other treatments may be necessary to reduce these to acceptable levels.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 化學滅菌殘留

EO（環氧乙烷）殘留：使用氣體或化學滅菌時，殘留滅菌劑是重大安全議題。例如 EO 滅菌後，產品上的 EO 殘留量和 ECH（Ethylene Chlorohydrin）副產物必須符合 ISO 10993-7 限量標準。Post-sterilization aeration（滅菌後通氣）是標準程序。

CDMO 實務：接到新產品時，材料相容性研究是技術轉移（Tech Transfer）階段的關鍵活動。客戶通常會提供初步數據，但 CDMO 應在自己的設備和條件下重新驗證。這些研究可以在實驗室規模進行，但必須完整記錄（fully documented）— 這是 GMP 的基本要求。

Biological Indicator Selection — 生物指示劑選擇

Original Text 原文內容

A biological indicator should be selected that is appropriate for the chosen sterilization process and its duration, as well as the intended validation approach (see Biological Indicators for Sterilization &langle;1229.5&rangle;).

The chosen biological indicator provides a means to measure the lethality of the process through the selection of biological indicator population and resistance.

The sterilization process duration should not be adapted to accommodate the specifics of any biological indicator.

"The sterilization process duration should not be adapted to accommodate the specifics of any biological indicator."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 BI 選擇原則

BI 的角色：生物指示劑是滅菌驗證的「量尺」— 它用來量測製程是否達到所需的致死性，而不是用來決定製程條件。

關鍵原則：「製程不應遷就 BI」— 這是 USP 非常明確的立場。意思是：

  • 先根據產品和生物負載決定滅菌條件
  • 再選擇適合的 BI 來驗證該條件的致死性
  • 如果 BI 在該條件下無法被完全滅活，應選擇抗性較低的 BI，而非延長製程時間

重點提示 常用 BI

CDMO 注意事項：BI 的 D 值和孢子數量必須有供應商提供的 Certificate of Analysis（CoA）。不同批號的 BI 可能有不同的 D 值 — 驗證報告中應記錄所用 BI 的具體批號和特性。

Container and Item Mapping — 容器與品項熱分佈

Original Text 原文內容

Studies should be performed to determine the location within the materials to be sterilized that is least likely to achieve sterilizing conditions. The mapping can be conducted using thermocouples, dosimeters, biological indicators, and/or chemical indicators placed within the materials.

The materials to be sterilized should be wrapped or packaged and oriented as intended for routine use of the sterilization process. The wrapping and packaging materials and methods should be controlled to ensure reproducibility.

It is essential when conducting these studies that the introduction of the measuring devices be accomplished in a manner that does not alter (positively or negatively) the penetration of the sterilant through any wrapping and packaging or through the material itself.

If the materials to be sterilized have simple geometry, with minimal internal volume, and are sterilized with other more complex items, mapping of the simpler materials can be omitted. Interpolation of volume, density, dimension, and mass may be possible to reduce the extent of studies required. Mapping studies should be documented for future reference.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 Cold Spot 識別

Container/Item Mapping 的目的：找到容器或品項內部的「最冷點」（Cold Spot）或「最難到達點」— 即滅菌條件最不容易達到的位置。如果最冷點都能達標，其他位置自然合格。

測量工具選擇：

  • 熱電偶（Thermocouples） — 濕熱/乾熱滅菌的溫度分佈
  • 劑量計（Dosimeters） — 輻射滅菌的劑量分佈
  • BI / CI — 輔助驗證致死性達到各位置

重點提示 測量不能改變結果

非侵入性原則：USP 特別強調：放置測量裝置時不能改變滅菌劑的穿透路徑。例如：

  • 在密封包裝上穿孔放入熱電偶時，穿孔本身可能成為蒸汽進入的捷徑 — 這會讓測量結果偏樂觀
  • 在瓶中放入大型溫度探頭可能排擠液體，改變熱傳導特性

Interpolation（內插法）：USP 允許對簡單幾何形狀的物品使用內插法減少研究範圍。例如：已驗證 100 mL 和 500 mL 瓶的 Cold Spot，可以合理推估 250 mL 瓶的行為。但此推估必須有科學依據並記錄在案。

Load Mapping — 裝載熱分佈

Original Text 原文內容

The initial use of a sterilization process should include confirmation that the sterilization conditions can be delivered throughout the entire load. Additional guidance on this, and the subsequent aspect of sterilization validation and process control, can be found in other chapters within the &langle;1229&rangle; family of chapters.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 CDMO Re-validation Triggers

何時需要重新驗證（Re-validation）？

  • 新產品或新容器尺寸引入（新的 Container Mapping 需求）
  • 裝載模式（Loading Pattern）變更
  • 滅菌器設備維修或重大修改
  • 生物負載趨勢出現異常（更高的族群或抗性）
  • 容器封閉系統變更（可能影響蒸汽穿透）
  • 定期再驗證（通常每年一次）

對 CDMO 而言，新客戶產品轉入幾乎必然觸發 Container Mapping 和可能的 Load Mapping 更新。
