Section 1 — Introduction 簡介

原文內容 Original Text

1. INTRODUCTION

Water is widely used as a raw material, inactive ingredient, medicinal vehicle, and solvent in the processing, formulation, and manufacture of pharmaceutical products (dosage forms), active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs), API intermediates, compendial articles, and analytical reagents as well as in cleaning applications.

This informational chapter on pharmaceutical water topics includes some of the chemical and microbiological concerns unique to water and its preparation and uses. The chapter provides information about water quality attributes (that may or may not be included within a water monograph) and processing techniques that can be used to improve water quality. It also discusses water system validation and gives a description of minimum water quality standards that should be considered when selecting a water source including sampling and system controls.

This chapter is intended to be educational, and the user should also refer to existing regulations or guidelines that cover US and international [ICH or WHO] good manufacturing practice (GMP) issues. This chapter is not, and should not be considered, an all-inclusive document on pharmaceutical waters.

It is the user's responsibility to ensure that:

  1. The selection of the type and specifications of water is appropriate for its intended use.
  2. Water production and quality meet applicable governmental regulations and guidance.
  3. The pharmacopeial specifications for the types of water used in monographed articles are met.
  4. Water used in the preparation of reagents for analysis or the performance of required tests meets USP requirements.

2. SOURCE WATER CONSIDERATIONS

Source water is the water that enters the facility. The origin of this source water can be from natural surface waters like rivers and reservoirs, deep-bed well waters, sea waters, or some combination of these, potentially including multiple locations of each type of source water.

It is the responsibility of the users of any source water to ensure that the water used in the production of drug substances (or API), as well as water for indirect drug product contact or for purification system feed water purposes meets, at a minimum, drinking (potable) water standards as defined by the requirements of the National Primary Drinking Water Regulations (NPDWR) (40 CFR 141) issued by the US EPA or the drinking water regulations of the European Union (EU) or Japan, or the WHO drinking water guidelines.

Where water supplies are from regulated water utility companies, less stringent monitoring may be possible because the attributes may be tested regularly and ensured by the supplier. Water being withdrawn from a nonregulated supply should be sampled and tested appropriately at a suitable frequency that takes into account local environmental and seasonal changes and other quality fluctuations.

The use of water complying with one of these designated drinking waters as a source water allows water pretreatment systems to only be challenged to remove small quantities of potentially difficult-to-remove chemicals. Control of objectionable chemical contaminants at the source water stage eliminates the need to specifically test for some of them (e.g., trihalomethanes and elemental impurities) after the water has been further purified.

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 無菌製劑廠房設計時，原水品質監控應納入水系統驗證計畫（Water System Validation Plan）。建議至少每季採樣一次市政水，分析微生物計數、重金屬、TOC、導電度等關鍵指標，並記錄於水系統日誌中，以供 FDA 查廠時展示對原水變異的主動管理能力。

3. WATERS USED FOR PHARMACEUTICAL MANUFACTURING AND TESTING PURPOSES

There are many different grades of water used for pharmaceutical purposes. Several are described in USP monographs that specify uses, acceptable methods of preparation, and quality attributes. These waters can be divided into two general types:

  • Bulk waters — typically produced on-site where they are used
  • Sterile waters — produced, packaged, and sterilized to preserve microbial quality throughout their packaged shelf life

3.1.1 PURIFIED WATER

Purified Water (PW) is used as an excipient in the production of nonparenteral preparations and in other pharmaceutical applications, such as the cleaning of nonparenteral product-contact components and equipment. Unless otherwise specified, Purified Water also is to be used as the minimum water quality for all tests and assays in which "water" is indicated.

The minimal quality of source water for the production of Purified Water is drinking water. This source water may be purified using unit operations that include deionization, distillation, ion exchange, reverse osmosis, filtration, or other suitable purification procedures. Purified Water must meet the requirements for ionic and organic chemical purity and must be protected from microbial contamination.

Purified Water systems that function under ambient conditions are particularly susceptible to the establishment of biofilms of microorganisms, which can be the source of undesirable levels of viable microorganisms or endotoxins in the water. These ambient Purified Water systems require frequent sanitization and microbiological monitoring.

3.1.2 WATER FOR INJECTION

Water for Injection (WFI) is used as an excipient in the production of parenteral and other preparations where product endotoxin content must be controlled, and in other pharmaceutical applications, such as the cleaning of certain equipment and parenteral product-contact components.

The minimal quality of source water for the production of WFI is drinking water. This source water may be treated to render it suitable for subsequent final purification steps, such as distillation (or whatever other validated process is used, according to the monograph). The finished water must meet all of the chemical requirements specified in the monograph, as well as an additional bacterial endotoxin specification. Because endotoxins are produced by the kinds of microorganisms that are prone to inhabit water systems, the equipment and procedures used by the system to purify, store, and distribute WFI should be designed to control microbial contamination and must be designed to remove incoming endotoxins from the source water.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 水的分類

製藥用水的分類邏輯：

  • Bulk Monographed Waters（散裝藥典水）：現場生產、即時使用的高品質水，包括 PW、WFI、WFH、Pure Steam
  • Sterile Monographed Waters（無菌藥典水）：預先包裝並滅菌的水，如 Sterile PW、Sterile WFI、BWFI 等
  • Non-Monographed Waters（非藥典水）：有特殊用途描述的水，無正式規格，如 Deionized Water、Distilled Water 等

比喻說明 Analogy — WFI 的嚴格性

WFI 和 PW 的關係，就像「手術室用水」和「病房清潔用水」的差異。兩者都要乾淨，但手術室用水多了一個絕對不能有的東西：內毒素（endotoxin）。內毒素即使在水很純的情況下也可能存在（因為它是細菌的殘骸），所以 WFI 系統必須從設計上主動移除它，光靠化學純度測試是不夠的。

重點提示 Key Notes — PW 的 Biofilm 風險

USP 特別強調：常溫（ambient）PW 系統比 WFI 系統更容易形成生物膜（biofilm）。這是因為 PW 系統通常不需要熱水循環（hot loop），無法用溫度抑制微生物生長。因此 PW 系統的消毒頻率和微生物監控要求更高，且監控數據必須根據歷史趨勢設定 Alert Level 和 Action Level。

3.1.3 WATER FOR HEMODIALYSIS

Water for Hemodialysis (WFH) is used for hemodialysis applications, primarily the dilution of hemodialysis concentrate solutions. The minimal quality of source water is drinking water. Water for Hemodialysis has been further purified to reduce chemical and microbiological components, and it is produced and used on site. This water contains no added antimicrobial agents, and it is not intended for injection. Water for Hemodialysis must meet all of the chemical requirements specified in the monograph as well as an additional bacterial endotoxin specification. The microbial limits attribute for this water is unique among the "bulk" water monographs but is justified on the basis of this water's specific application, which has microbial content requirements related to its safe use.

3.1.4 PURE STEAM

Pure Steam (also sometimes referred to as "clean steam") is used where the steam or its condensate would directly contact official articles or article-contact surfaces, such as during their preparation, sterilization, or cleaning where no subsequent processing step is used to remove any impurity residues. These Pure Steam applications include, but are not limited to, porous load sterilization processes, product or cleaning solutions heated by direct steam injection, or humidification of processes where steam injection is used to control the humidity inside processing vessels.

The minimal quality of source water for the production of Pure Steam is drinking water which has been suitably treated. The water is then vaporized with suitable mist elimination and distributed under pressure. If an official article is exposed to Pure Steam and it is intended for parenteral use or other applications where the pyrogenic content must be controlled, the Pure Steam must additionally meet the specification for Bacterial Endotoxins Test 〈85〉.

Note that lower-purity "plant steam" may be used in the following applications: 1) for steam sterilization of nonproduct-contact nonporous loads, 2) for general cleaning of nonproduct-contact equipment, 3) as a nonproduct-contact heat-exchange medium.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 血液透析用水的特殊性

Water for Hemodialysis（WFH）是所有散裝水中唯一有明確微生物限度（microbial limit）規格的水。這是因為透析療程中，患者的血液直接透過薄膜與透析液接觸，微生物污染可能直接引起全身性感染。因此 WFH 的品質標準接近 WFI，但又稍有不同（不要求注射用途）。

Pure Steam vs Plant Steam — 關鍵區別

這是查廠常見的考點：

  • Pure Steam（純蒸汽）：接觸產品或產品接觸面時使用。必須符合藥典規格，冷凝液需通過 WFI 化學測試（有時還需通過 〈85〉 endotoxin 測試）
  • Plant Steam（工廠蒸汽）：僅用於非產品接觸設備的滅菌或加熱，通常含有防腐添加劑（anticorrosion additives），不得接觸產品

誤用 Plant Steam 接觸產品是嚴重的 GMP 違規，可能導致抑菌劑或腐蝕添加劑污染產品。

比喻說明 Analogy — Pure Steam 的重要性

Pure Steam 就像「食品級不鏽鋼」和「工業用不鏽鋼」的差異：外表看起來都是蒸汽，但工廠蒸汽裡可能添加了防鏽劑（就像工業鋼材含有對食品有害的成分）。無菌製劑廠的高壓蒸汽滅菌器（autoclave）必須使用 Pure Steam，確保滅菌後的器材不帶有有害殘留物。

實務應用 Practical Application

在無菌 CDMO 的設施設計中，Pure Steam 系統通常與 WFI 系統共用純化設備（同等品質的水源），並單獨設置蒸汽產生器（pure steam generator）。Pure Steam 冷凝液的定期測試（至少每年一次）是 FDA 查廠的檢查重點，測試項目包含：不揮發性殘留物（non-volatile residue）、導電度、pH、endotoxin（若用於 parenteral 相關用途）。

3.2 Sterile Monographed Waters

The following monographed waters are packaged forms of either Purified Water or Water for Injection that have been sterilized to preserve their microbiological properties. In general, these sterile waters may be used in a variety of applications in lieu of the bulk forms of water from which they were derived. However, a substantial difference exists between the acceptance criteria for the chemical purities of these bulk waters versus sterile waters. The specifications for sterile waters differ from those of bulk waters to accommodate a wide variety of packaging types, properties, volumes, and uses. As a result, the inorganic and organic impurity specifications are not equivalent for bulk and packaged waters. The packaging materials and elastomeric closures are the primary sources of these impurities, which tend to increase over the shelf life of these packaged articles.

3.2.1 STERILE PURIFIED WATER

Sterile Purified Water is Purified Water packaged and rendered sterile. It can be used in the preparation of nonparenteral compendial dosage forms or in analytical applications requiring Purified Water where access to a validated Purified Water system is not practical, or only a relatively small quantity is needed.

3.2.2 STERILE WATER FOR INJECTION

Sterile Water for Injection is Water for Injection packaged and rendered sterile. It is used for extemporaneous prescription compounding and as a sterile diluent for parenteral products. Water used for dilution or reconstitution of parenteral drug products must meet the requirements of Sterile Water for Injection. Sterile Water for Injection is packaged in single-dose containers not larger than 1 L.

3.2.3 BACTERIOSTATIC WATER FOR INJECTION

Bacteriostatic Water for Injection (BWFI) is Water for Injection packaged and rendered sterile, to which has been added one or more suitable antimicrobial preservatives. It is intended to be used as a diluent in the preparation of parenteral products, most typically for multi-dose products that require repeated content withdrawals. It may be packaged in single-dose or multiple-dose containers not larger than 30 mL.

3.2.4 STERILE WATER FOR IRRIGATION

Sterile Water for Irrigation is Water for Injection packaged and sterilized in single-dose containers that may be larger than 1 L and allow rapid delivery of their contents. Because of its usage, Sterile Water for Irrigation is not required to meet Particulate Matter in Injections 〈788〉.

3.2.5 STERILE WATER FOR INHALATION

Sterile Water for Inhalation is Water for Injection that is packaged and rendered sterile and is intended for use in inhalators and in the preparation of inhalation solutions. This monograph has no requirement to meet 〈788〉; it carries a less stringent specification for bacterial endotoxins than Sterile Water for Injection, and therefore is not suitable for parenteral applications.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 無菌水的特殊性

無菌水（Sterile Monographed Waters）和散裝水（Bulk Waters）最大的差別在於：無菌水的化學純度標準較寬鬆，因為包裝材料會釋放有機物和無機物到水中，且這種釋放會隨時間增加。因此，不能假設無菌水和散裝水同等純淨。

五種無菌水的比較：

  • Sterile PW：非注射用，PW 來源
  • Sterile WFI：注射稀釋，≤1 L 單劑量
  • BWFI：含防腐劑，多劑量用，≤30 mL
  • Sterile Water for Irrigation：>1 L 容器，不要求 〈788〉顆粒物
  • Sterile Water for Inhalation：吸入用，endotoxin 要求較寬

重點提示 Key Notes — 包裝容器的影響

無菌水的化學規格寬鬆的根本原因：玻璃容器會釋放鈉離子（影響導電度），塑膠容器會釋放有機物（影響 TOC）。USP 允許這些浸出物存在，但設定了相應的容器體積相關規格（見 Section 00b 的 TOC 容器體積表）。這就是為什麼在分析實驗室，如果要做精確的離子色譜（Ion Chromatography），應該使用現場生產的 PW，而非從瓶裝無菌水取樣。

比喻說明 Analogy — BWFI 的防腐劑

BWFI 含有防腐劑（通常是苯甲醇 benzyl alcohol 或苯扎氯銨 benzalkonium chloride），就像「含防腐劑的生理食鹽水」。多劑量瓶每次打開都有污染風險，防腐劑的作用是防止微生物在多次使用間繁殖。但正因如此，BWFI 不適用於不能接受防腐劑的情況（如新生兒注射），這是 BWFI 的應用限制。

3.3 Drinking Water

Drinking water is a non-monographed water that can be referred to as "potable water" (meaning drinkable or fit to drink), "National Primary Drinking Water", "primary drinking water", or "US EPA Drinking Water". Except where a singular drinking water specification is stated (such as the US EPA NPDWR, as cited in 40 CFR Part 141), this water may be purified by various methods.

3.4 Other Non-Monographed Waters

Both General Notices and Reagents, Indicators, and Solutions clearly state that where the term "water" is indicated for use in analyses without grammatical qualification or other specification, the quality of the water must be Purified Water. However, numerous qualifications do exist.

3.4.5 DEIONIZED WATER

Deionized water is water that has had dissolved ions removed by an ion-exchange process. This process can remove ionic (mostly inorganic) chemical contaminants but does not effectively remove non-ionic organics or pyrogens/endotoxins. It should not be confused with water of established monograph quality.

3.4.8 HIGH-PURITY WATER

High-purity water is used as a reagent, as a solvent for reagent preparation, and for test apparatus cleaning in which less stringent water purity would not be considered acceptable. For assays that require low-conductivity water (e.g., Ion Chromatography 〈1065〉 and Plasma Spectrochemistry—Theory and Practice 〈1730〉), a conductivity value of NMT 0.0556 µS/cm (NLT 18.0 Mohm-cm) may be appropriate.

3.4.11 WATER FOR BACTERIAL ENDOTOXINS TEST

Water for Bacterial Endotoxins Test (BET) is prepared by the user as needed using Purified Water or Water for Injection that meets the specifications in the BET chapter. The water is prepared fresh, heated to 70° for NLT 30 min, and cooled immediately before use, or it may be purchased as Sterile Water for Injection that is labeled as endotoxin-free or LAL Reagent Water.

重點提示 Key Notes — BET 用水的特殊要求

細菌內毒素測試（BET/LAL test）用水是所有水中最嚴格的——不僅要化學純，還必須確定無內毒素干擾（endotoxin-free）。製備方式：PW 或 WFI 加熱至 70°C 持續 30 分鐘以降解內毒素，或直接購買已驗證無內毒素的 Sterile WFI（LAL Reagent Water）。錯誤使用含內毒素的水做 BET 測試，會導致偽陰性結果，這是嚴重的品質失誤。

比喻說明 Analogy — 去離子水的誤解

很多人以為「去離子水」（DI water）就等於「純水」或「WFI」，這是錯誤的。去離子只移除了離子性污染物，但無法移除有機物、微生物和內毒素。就像「過濾掉鹽巴的海水」不等於「礦泉水」——去除鹽分只是純化的一個步驟，不是全部。

原文內容 Original Text

4. VALIDATION AND QUALIFICATION OF WATER PURIFICATION, STORAGE, AND DISTRIBUTION SYSTEMS

4.1 Validation Requirement

Purified Water and Water for Injection systems must be validated to reliably and consistently produce and distribute water of acceptable chemical and microbiological quality. Validation demonstrates that the water system performs as intended, within the defined design criteria and operating conditions.

4.2 Validation Approach

The validation protocol should be based on the boundaries of the water system and the critical water quality and process attributes needed to maintain consistent performance. The system boundary may stop at the point of use or may include the water transfer process.

4.2.1 VALIDATION ELEMENTS

Validation is accomplished through the use of a structured, documented process. The phases of this process include:

  • Design Qualification (DQ) — verifies that the proposed design meets the user requirements
  • Installation Qualification (IQ) — verifies that the system is installed per design specifications
  • Operational Qualification (OQ) — verifies that the system operates as designed within specified ranges
  • Performance Qualification (PQ) — demonstrates that the system consistently produces water meeting quality specifications under production conditions
  • Validation Maintenance — ongoing activities to maintain validated state

Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT), Site Acceptance Testing (SAT), and commissioning testing may supplement qualification tests for IQ or OQ.

4.2.4 OPERATIONAL QUALIFICATION

The OQ phase consisting of tests and inspections to verify that the equipment, system alerts, and controls are operating reliably and that appropriate Alert and Action Levels are established. During this phase of validation specific testing is performed for alarms, verifying control sequences, equipment functional checks, and verification of operating ranges. Standard operating procedures (SOPs) for all aspects of water system operation, maintenance, water use, water sampling, and testing should be in place and operator training completed.

4.2.5 PERFORMANCE QUALIFICATION

The prospective PQ stage considers two aspects of the water system: critical process parameters and critical water attribute parameters. These are evaluated in parallel by monitoring the water quality and demonstrating acceptable quality attributes while demonstrating control of the process parameters. The initial PQ stage may result in refinement of process parameters to yield appropriate water quality. This PQ stage includes an increased frequency of monitoring for approximately 2–4 weeks, or sufficient time to generate adequate data to demonstrate that water meeting the appropriate quality attributes is produced and distributed.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 水系統驗證生命週期

水系統驗證遵循標準的製藥驗證生命週期模型，通常分為三個主要階段：

  • 設計階段：DQ（設計確認）+ URS（使用者需求規格）
  • 安裝/操作確認：IQ（安裝確認）+ OQ（操作確認）
  • 性能確認：PQ Phase 1 → Phase 2 → Phase 3（長期監控）

  • Phase 1（2–4 週）：強化監控，建立基準線，確認系統能穩定生產
  • Phase 2（再 2–4 週）：繼續監控，跨越各種操作狀態（如消毒後）
  • Phase 3（1 年以上）：降低監控頻率至例行水準，包含季節性變異

比喻說明 Analogy — PQ 的三階段

PQ 三階段就像新進員工的「試用期管理」：Phase 1 是密集觀察（每天報到），Phase 2 是常規監督（每週 check-in），Phase 3 是正式轉正（按季度考核）。水系統也一樣——剛啟動時必須密集監控，確認系統穩定後才能降低監控頻率，但從不停止監控。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 新廠房啟動時，水系統驗證通常是商業批次生產的先決條件（prerequisite）。常見的計畫錯誤是低估 PQ Phase 1 的時間——USP 要求「至少 2–4 週，且需涵蓋系統的各種操作狀態」。若新廠在冬季完成安裝並開始 PQ，必須確認 PQ Phase 3 能涵蓋夏季（季節性微生物負荷高峰），否則需要補充驗證數據。

Section 4.3 — Operational Use: Monitoring, Maintenance & Change Control 操作維護與變更控制

原文內容 Original Text

4.3 Operational Use

4.3.1 MONITORING

After successful completion of the validation process, a water system enters its operational use phase. During this phase, the system must be continuously monitored to ensure that it remains in a state of control. Monitoring programs should include both in-process (PC) monitoring and quality control (QC) monitoring. The frequency and locations of sampling should be sufficient to generate meaningful data for trending and system control purposes.

4.3.2 VALIDATION MAINTENANCE

Maintaining the validated state of the water system is an ongoing activity. Validation maintenance activities include routine maintenance, preventive maintenance, calibration of instrumentation, corrective maintenance, sanitization activities, and periodic system review. A robust preventive maintenance program is essential to maintaining consistent water system performance.

4.3.3 CHANGE CONTROL

Any change to the water system that could affect its performance must be controlled through a formal change control process. Changes to be considered include physical changes to equipment and piping, changes to operating parameters, software changes, and changes to operating or maintenance procedures. The change control process should include an impact assessment to determine the extent of requalification needed.

4.3.4 PERIODIC REVIEW

The water system qualification, maintenance history, calibration records, quality and process data, issues with the unit operations and any process variability, change control, and other validation maintenance data should be assessed periodically to determine impact on the state of control. The review may result in adjustments to operating or sanitization processes, calibration or maintenance plans, or monitoring plans. This may also result in additional testing or repeating certain qualification tasks (e.g., re-qualification).

Section 5 — Design and Operation of PW and WFI Systems 系統設計與操作

原文內容 Original Text

5. DESIGN AND OPERATION OF PURIFIED WATER AND WATER FOR INJECTION SYSTEMS

The design, installation, and operation of systems to produce Purified Water and Water for Injection include similar components, control techniques, and procedures. The quality attributes of the two waters differ in their bioburden expectation, the presence of a bacterial endotoxin requirement for Water for Injection, and in their methods of preparation.

Production of pharmaceutical water involves sequential unit operations (processing steps) that address specific water quality attributes and protect the operation of subsequent unit operations in the production train.

5.1 Unit Operations Considerations

5.1.2 Activated Carbon: The chief operating concerns regarding activated carbon beds include the propensity to support bacterial growth, the potential for hydraulic channeling, the organic adsorption capacity, and insufficient contact time. Control measures may involve monitoring water flow rates and differential pressures, sanitizing with hot water or steam, backwashing, and frequent replacement of the carbon bed. Alternative technologies to activated carbon beds can be used to avoid their microbial challenges. These include disinfectant-neutralizing chemical additives and intense ultraviolet (UV) light for removal of chlorine, and regenerable organic scavenging deionizing resins for removal of organics.

5.1.6 Deionization: Concerns for all forms of deionization units include microbial and endotoxin control, chemical additive impact on resins and membranes, and loss, degradation, and fouling of resin. Control measures may include continuous recirculation loops, effluent microbial control by UV light, conductivity monitoring, resin testing, and frequent regeneration.

5.1.7 Reverse Osmosis: Reverse osmosis (RO) units use semipermeable membranes. Many factors, including pH, temperature, source water hardness, permeate and reject flow rate, and differential pressure across the membrane, affect the selectivity and effectiveness of this permeation. For most source waters, a single stage of RO is usually not enough to meet Purified Water conductivity specifications. A second pass of this permeate water through another RO stage usually achieves the necessary performance.

5.1.9 Microbial-Retentive Filtration: Filters rated as 0.2 to 0.22 µm may not provide absolute microbial retention in water systems because of the "pass-through" phenomenon resulting from tiny cells or less cell "stickiness," or perhaps by a "grow-through" phenomenon. Nevertheless, microbial retention success in water systems has been reported with the use of filters rated as 0.2 or 0.1 µm.

5.1.11 Distillation: Methods of control may involve the following: preliminary steps to remove dissolved carbon dioxide and other volatile or noncondensable impurities; reliable mist elimination to minimize feed water droplet entrainment; on-line conductivity sensing with automated diversion to waste to prevent unacceptable water upon still start-up or malfunction.

5.1.12 Storage Tanks: Storage tanks are included in water distribution systems to optimize processing equipment capacity. Areas of concern include microbial growth or corrosion due to irregular or incomplete sanitization and microbial contamination from unalarmed rupture disk failures caused by condensate-occluded vent filters. Control considerations may include the use of closed tanks with smooth interiors, spray balls on recirculating loop returns, and the use of heated, jacketed/insulated tanks.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 單元操作流程

典型 PW/WFI 系統的單元操作序列（由前到後）：

  • 原水 → 預過濾（Prefiltration）
  • → 活性碳（Activated Carbon）— 去除氯

  • → 蒸餾（Distillation）— WFI 傳統路線
  • → 儲槽（Storage Tank）→ 配水系統（Distribution Loop）

重點提示 Key Notes — 活性碳的微生物風險

活性碳床（Activated Carbon Bed）是水系統中最容易形成生物膜的單元，因為它提供了大表面積且能吸附微生物賴以生長的有機物（包括它所吸附的氯也被移除，消除了抑菌效果）。USP 建議：以熱水或蒸汽消毒，或考慮使用替代技術（UV 去氯 + 有機掃除型離子交換樹脂）來規避這個風險。

重點提示 Key Notes — RO 膜的限制

單段 RO 通常無法達到 PW 的導電度規格（≤1.3 µS/cm），這是因為 RO 的拒絕率（rejection rate）約 95–99%，取決於操作條件。工廠通常採用「雙段 RO（double-pass RO）」或「RO + EDI（電去離子）」來確保達標。注意：RO 不能有效去除所有 VOCs（揮發性有機物），若原水含有特殊有機物污染，需要在 RO 前後加裝活性碳或 UV/H₂O₂ 系統。

比喻說明 Analogy — 儲槽的呼吸器

水系統儲槽的排氣過濾器（vent filter）就像人的「鼻子」——儲槽水位上下波動時，必須允許空氣進出。但這個「鼻子」必須有過濾功能（0.2 µm 親水疏水膜），防止外界微生物進入。若排氣過濾器被蒸汽冷凝水阻塞，儲槽就「無法呼吸」，嚴重時可能導致槽體壓扁變形（負壓崩塌）。這就是為什麼儲槽排氣過濾器需要定期更換和熱追蹤（heat tracing）。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 的水系統設計審查（design review）時，以下幾點是常見的疏漏：(1) 活性碳床缺少取樣口或缺少消毒連接；(2) 儲槽缺少噴球（spray ball）或噴球覆蓋不完整；(3) 排氣過濾器缺少壓差監控或無破盤報警（rupture disk alarm）；(4) 死角（dead leg）長度超過 6D（管徑的6倍），違反 ISPE Baseline Guide 要求。

Section 5.2–5.4 — Materials, Sanitization & Operation 材料選擇、消毒與操作控制

原文內容 Original Text

5.2 Installation, Materials of Construction, and Component Selection

Materials of construction should be selected to be compatible with control measures such as sanitizing, cleaning, or passivation. Temperature rating is a critical factor in choosing appropriate materials because surfaces may be required to handle elevated operating and sanitization temperatures. The finish on metallic materials such as stainless steel, whether it is a refined mill finish, polished to a specific grit, or an electropolished treatment, should complement the system design and provide satisfactory corrosion and microbial activity resistance. Auxiliary equipment and fittings that require seals, gaskets, diaphragms, filter media, and membranes should exclude materials that permit the possibility of extractables, shedding, and microbial activity.

5.3 Sanitization

5.3.1 Thermal Sanitization: Validation of thermal methods should include a heat distribution study to demonstrate that sanitization temperatures are achieved throughout the system, including the body of use point valves; sampling ports; instrument side branches; and fittings, couplings, and adapters, relying on water convection and thermal conduction through system materials for heat transfer to wetted surfaces.

5.3.2 Chemical Sanitization: Oxidizing chemicals commonly used for sanitization of water systems include ozone (O₃), hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂), peracetic acid (PAA), and chlorine compounds. It is important to note that microorganisms in a well-developed biofilm can be extremely difficult to kill, even by using aggressive oxidizing chemicals. Validation of chemical sanitization requires demonstration of adequate chemical concentrations throughout the system, exposure to all wetted surfaces including the body of use point valves, and complete removal of the sanitizing agent from the system at the completion of treatment.

5.3.3 UV Sanitization: In-line UV light at a wavelength of 254 nm also can be used to continuously "sanitize" only the water circulating in the system, but these devices must be properly sized for the water flow. Such devices inactivate a high percentage (but not 100%) of microorganisms that flow through the device but cannot be used to directly control existing biofilm upstream or downstream of the device.

5.4 Operation, Maintenance, and Control

5.4.2 Process Monitoring Program: The process monitoring program should detect any developing adverse water quality trends early enough to allow for corrective action prior to exceeding alert levels, action levels, or specifications. This proactive approach to system control relies on trending of monitoring data.

5.4.3 Routine Microbial Control: Effective microbial control of water systems relies on a multifaceted program that includes appropriate system design, operation, and maintenance; routine sanitization; and microbial monitoring and trending.

Section 7 — Chemical Evaluations 化學評估

原文內容 Original Text

7. CHEMICAL EVALUATIONS

7.1 Chemical Tests for Bulk Waters

In 1996, USP moved away from traditional chemical attribute tests, switching to contemporary analytical technologies for the bulk waters Purified Water and Water for Injection. The intent was to upgrade the analytical technologies without tightening the quality requirements. The two contemporary analytical technologies employed were Total Organic Carbon (TOC) and conductivity.

The TOC test replaced the test for Oxidizable Substances that primarily targeted organic contaminants. A multi-staged conductivity test that detects ionic (mostly inorganic) contaminants replaced all of the inorganic chemical tests (i.e., Ammonia, Calcium, Carbon Dioxide, Chloride, Sulfate).

Replacing the heavy metals attribute was considered unnecessary because: 1) the source water specifications for individual heavy metals were tighter than the approximate limit of detection of the Heavy Metals test; 2) contemporary water system construction materials do not leach heavy metal contaminants; and 3) test results for this attribute have uniformly been negative.

The three-stage conductivity test (Stage 1: temperature-dependent limit; Stage 2: pH-based assessment; Stage 3: ion-specific calculation) provides a comprehensive evaluation of ionic purity. Stage 1 is the in-process on-line test. If Stage 1 fails, Stage 2 and Stage 3 are performed in the laboratory.

7.2 Chemical Tests for Sterile Waters

Packaged/sterile waters present a particular dilemma relative to the attributes of conductivity and TOC. The package itself is the major source of chemicals (inorganics and organics) that leach over time into the packaged water. Because Sterile Water for Injection is Water for Injection that has already met its monographed requirements for low conductivity and TOC, the Sterile Water for Injection attributes of conductivity and TOC reveal more about the packaging leachables than they do about the water's original purity.

7.2.1 CONDUCTIVITY FOR STERILE WATERS

To better control the ionic packaging leachables, USP 〈645〉 is divided into two sections. The first, Bulk Water, applies to Purified Water, Water for Injection, Water for Hemodialysis, and Pure Steam, and includes the three-stage conductivity testing instructions and specifications. The second, Sterile Water, applies to Sterile Purified Water, Sterile Water for Injection, Sterile Water for Inhalation, and Sterile Water for Irrigation.

7.4 Elemental Impurities in Pharmaceutical Waters

Water that meets US EPA NPDWR or WHO Drinking Water Guidelines that has been purified by conventional technologies used to produce Water for Injection can comply with 〈232〉 for parenterals. Source water that meets drinking water standards has maximum contaminant levels for lead, mercury, cadmium, and arsenic that are NMT 10 times (1-log) higher than the EI limits for parenterals. The purification technologies needed to produce Water for Injection that reduce the impurities by a factor of 100 to 1000 will assure compliance with 〈232〉.

6. SAMPLING

The testing of water samples from a water system is critical to the ongoing control of the system and assessment of the quality of the water being used. If improperly collected, a sample could yield a test result that is unrepresentative of the sample's purpose. This could lead to inaction when remediation is needed or to unnecessary remediation when none is necessary. Therefore, properly collecting water samples, understanding their purpose, and establishing appropriate water system sampling plans are essential to water QC and system control.

6.1 Purposes and Procedures

The data from water testing are generally used for one of two purposes:

  • Process Control (PC) Sampling — reflects the quality of the water within the distribution system, coming from the purification system, or between its purification steps
  • Quality Control (QC) Sampling — used for release or quality control of the water being drawn from the system for some application or use

In-line/on-line testing avoids the exogenous contamination potential of grab samples that could lead to artifactually variable data trends and incorrect decisions on system performance, maintenance, and utilized water.

6.1.1 PROCESS CONTROL SAMPLING

Because PC sampling is intended to reflect the quality of the water behind the valve and within the distribution system, coming from the purification system, or between its purification steps, efforts should be made to avoid contaminating the water as it is drawn from the system so that its test results accurately reflect the water quality within the system at that location. If microbial testing is needed for PC purposes, the sampling valve should have a properly installed, sanitary design that uses vigorous flushing procedures prior to sample collection to remove microorganisms that may have colonized the sampling valve since the last use.

6.2 Attributes and Sampling Locations

Once the water is in the distribution system, the compendial attributes of importance typically include at least conductivity, TOC, and microbial count. In Water for Injection systems and other systems or system locations where bacterial endotoxin control is important, endotoxin is also assayed.

6.2.1 CHEMICAL ATTRIBUTES

Dissolved chemical contaminants detected by conductivity or TOC testing tend to be uniformly distributed in the water throughout the water system. However, there are exceptions where localized chemical contamination sources can occur, such as from a coolant-leaking heat exchanger in a sub-loop, or at a point of use, or within a dead leg. Chemical attributes are candidates for on-line testing at fixed strategic locations within the distribution system, and are generally reflective of the same chemical quality at all locations and points of use within the distribution system.

6.2.2 MICROBIAL ATTRIBUTES

The same uniformity scenario cannot be assumed for microbial attributes. Planktonic organisms in a water sample could have originated from biofilms in the purification or distribution systems releasing more or less uniform levels of planktonic organisms into the circulating water, or from localized colonization spots that can be highly variable in their microorganism release rates. Because of this non-uniformity, comprehensive microbial sampling from all distribution system use-point locations is more valuable than comprehensive chemical sampling.

6.3 Validation Sampling Plans

During PQ, sampling plans should include all use points and key unit operations at a frequency sufficient to generate trend data within the validation period. The typical three-phase PQ plan involves: Phase 1 (intensive sampling at all locations, approximately 2–4 weeks), Phase 2 (reduced intensity but still comprehensive, approximately 2–4 weeks), and Phase 3 (routine monitoring plan, approximately 1 year covering seasonal variation).

6.4.1 SOURCE WATER SAMPLING

Because potable water is typically delivered to the facility under pressure from a potable water distribution grid, the quality of the water delivered to the facility may have changed depending on a number of factors, including distance from the input source, duration of travel within the piping, and condition of the piping in that potable water distribution grid. Based on a risk assessment, it may be prudent to verify full compliance with regulations using water collected from sample ports prior to the pretreatment system.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — PC vs QC 取樣的根本差異

水系統取樣有兩個截然不同的目的：

  • PC 取樣（製程控制）：了解系統內部狀況，目的是「調整系統」。取樣點在閥門後方（inside the system），避免外部污染，反映真實水質

重點提示 Key Notes — 化學 vs 微生物屬性的均勻性差異

這是 USP 〈1231〉 中一個非常重要的概念：化學屬性（導電度、TOC）在整個系統中相對均勻，因此可以在循環回路的關鍵點用在線儀器代表全系統的化學品質。但微生物屬性非常不均勻——不同管路位置可能因 biofilm 發育程度不同而有顯著差異。因此，微生物監控必須覆蓋所有使用點，不能像化學監控一樣「選代表點」。

比喻說明 Analogy — 微生物的不均勻性

水系統的微生物分佈就像城市的「食物攤販」——某些角落（dead legs、低流速處）可能聚集了很多攤販（biofilm），而其他地方（高流速主管）幾乎沒有。如果只在「中央廣場」取樣（循環回路回水端），你永遠發現不了「偏僻角落」的污染。這就是為什麼所有使用點都需要定期取樣。

8. MICROBIAL EVALUATIONS

8.1 Microorganism Types

8.1.2 Bacteria:

8.1.2.1 Gram-positive bacteria: Gram-positive bacteria are common in a pharmaceutical manufacturing environment but not in water systems. This is because they are generally not suited to surviving in a liquid environment that has the chemical purity of a pharmaceutical-grade water system. Although gram-positive bacteria can be detected in pharmaceutical water samples, their recovery is often associated with faulty aseptic technique during sampling or testing, or associated with exogenous contamination sources.

8.1.2.2 Gram-negative bacteria: These types of bacteria are found in soil, water, plants, and animals. Gram-negative bacteria are relevant to pharmaceutical manufacturers, primarily due to their production of endotoxins as well as their ability to populate water systems and other wet environments as biofilms. Some gram-negative bacteria prefer aquatic habitats and tend to colonize water systems.

8.2 Biofilm Formation in Water Systems

Common microorganisms recovered from water system samples include gram-negative bacteria from the genera Pseudomonas, Ralstonia, Burkholderia, Stenotrophomonas, Comamonas, Methylobacterium, and many other types of Pseudomonas-like organisms known collectively as pseudomonads. These types of microbes tend to colonize all water system distribution and purification system surfaces including activated carbon beds, deionizing resin beds, reverse osmosis systems, membrane filtration modules, connecting piping, hoses, and valves.

The three-dimensional structure of a well-developed biofilm, as well as the biofilm's creation and release of small, motile "pioneer cells" for further colonization, are facilitated through gene expression modulating "quorum sensing" chemicals released in tiny amounts by individual biofilm cells and concentrated to a functional level within this same EPS (extracellular polymeric substance) matrix. So, the EPS matrix of biofilms is primarily responsible for the biofilm's success in colonizing and proliferating in very low nutrient-containing high-purity water systems.

Some of the biofilm pseudomonads are opportunistic human pathogens and may possess resistance to commonly used pharmaceutical product preservatives, particularly when embedded in EPS matrix flocs sheared from water system biofilms.

8.3 Microorganism Sources

8.3.1 Exogenous Contamination: Exogenous contamination originates outside the water system and can enter through any opening, including: (1) source water; (2) sampling points; (3) poorly installed or maintained fittings; (4) air drawn through tank vents; (5) points of use (e.g., hoses and fittings that are repeatedly attached and detached).

8.3.2 Endogenous Contamination: Once microorganisms are established in the water system, they become a source of endogenous contamination. Endogenous contamination typically involves organisms that are adapted to the oligotrophic (nutrient-poor) environment of a pharmaceutical water system. Another source of endogenous microbial contamination is the distribution system itself. Microorganisms can colonize pipe surfaces, rough welds, misaligned flanges, valves, and dead legs, where they proliferate and form biofilms.

8.4 Endotoxin

Bacterial endotoxin is a lipopolysaccharide (LPS) that is a component of the outer cell membrane of gram-negative bacteria. Endotoxins may occur as collections of LPS molecules associated with living microorganisms, fragments of dead microorganisms, the EPS matrix surrounding biofilm bacteria, or free molecular clusters or micelles. The monomeric form of the endotoxin molecule does not exist in high-purity water because of the molecule's amphipathic nature.

8.4.2 REMOVAL AND CONTROL

To control endotoxin levels in water systems, it is important to control all potential sources of contamination with gram-negative bacteria as well as free endotoxin in the water. Examples of endotoxin removal steps in a water purification train include reverse osmosis, deionization, ultrafilters, distillation, and endotoxin-adsorptive filters.

製藥水系統中最重要的微生物學概念：

  • Pseudomonads 是最常見的水系統菌：Pseudomonas、Ralstonia 等是已適應純水環境的「oligotroph（寡養型微生物）」

Biofilm 的生命週期與控制策略

Biofilm 形成的四個階段：

  1. 初始黏附（Reversible Attachment）：浮游菌接觸管壁表面
  2. 不可逆黏附（Irreversible Attachment）：細菌開始分泌 EPS
  3. 成熟 Biofilm：三維立體結構，Quorum Sensing 協調，極難殺滅
  4. 分散（Dispersion）：釋放「先驅細胞」（pioneer cells）到水流中，繼續擴散

控制策略：在 Stage 2（不可逆黏附）之前介入最有效——高流速、定期消毒、無死角設計。

重點提示 Key Notes — Endotoxin 的特殊性

理解內毒素（Endotoxin/LPS）的幾個關鍵特性：

  • 耐熱性強：普通 121°C 高壓蒸汽滅菌無法破壞 endotoxin（需 250°C 乾熱 30 min 或 180°C 2–4 h）
  • 消毒不等於去除：殺死細菌後，endotoxin 仍留在水中（甚至增加，因為細菌裂解釋放 LPS）
  • 蒸餾是最佳去除方式：endotoxin 分子量大（分子量 >1000 Da），不會蒸發到蒸汽中，蒸餾可有效去除（3–4 log）
  • 膜過濾的限制：0.2 µm 過濾器不能去除 endotoxin（因為 endotoxin 聚集成膠束，大小不一定），UF（10 kDa）才能有效截留

比喻說明 Analogy — Biofilm 的 Quorum Sensing

Biofilm 的 Quorum Sensing 就像細菌的「人口計數」系統——每個細菌持續分泌微量的「信號分子」，當這些分子累積到足夠濃度（表示「人口夠多了」），所有細菌同時啟動 biofilm 基因程式，開始大量製造 EPS 保護層。這就像一個城市等到足夠人口才開始建設「城牆」（EPS）——一旦城牆建好，外來的消毒劑就很難滲入。這解釋了為什麼「早期介入」（在 biofilm 成熟前消毒）遠比「事後補救」更有效。

9. ALERT AND ACTION LEVELS AND SPECIFICATIONS

9.1 Introduction

Establishment of Alert and Action Levels for any manufacturing process facilitates appropriate and timely control. In the case of a pharmaceutical water system, the key PC parameters can be specific chemical, physical, and microbiological attributes of the water produced. Typically, most chemical attributes can be determined in real time or in the lab within a few minutes after sample collection. Obtaining timely microbial data is more challenging, often taking several days. This limits the ability to control microbial attributes in a timely manner, and therefore requires a more challenging evaluation of the test results and conservative implementation of PC levels.

9.4 Defining Alert and Action Levels and Specifications

When establishing Alert and Action Levels and Specifications, a two- or three-tier approach is typically used. In a three-tier approach, the typical structure is to establish in-process controls using "Alert Level", "Action Level", and "Specifications".

9.4.1 ALERT LEVEL

Alert Level — a measurement or parameter should be derived from the normal operating range of the water system. Specifically, Alert Levels are based on the historical operating performance under production conditions, and then are established at levels that are just beyond the majority of the normal historical data. The Alert Level for a parameter is often a single value or a range of values, such as:

  • Higher than typical conductivity or TOC
  • Higher than typical microbial count
  • Higher than typical endotoxin level
  • Low temperature during thermal sanitization

When an Alert Level is exceeded, the response is usually an investigation to determine the cause of the adverse trend and, if the cause can be identified, correction of that cause. The investigation is performed to understand the event and to take early corrective action before the trend worsens and before Action Levels or Specifications are exceeded.

9.4.2 ACTION LEVEL

Action Level — a level that, when exceeded, requires immediate corrective action. The term "corrective action" is intended to encompass one or more actions, such as: halting the use of the water, increasing monitoring, conducting a root cause investigation, issuing a change control to adjust alert and action levels, and/or performing sanitization.

9.4.4 SPECIFICATIONS

Specifications (or Limits) — set based on direct potential product and/or process impact and they represent the suitability for use of the water. The various bulk water monograph specifications are examples of water Specifications. When a Specification is exceeded, the water does not meet requirements for use in the pharmaceutical process or product for which it is intended. A water OOS (out-of-specification) result requires a formal investigation.

The investigation may need to consider the following potential impact:

  • All products or processes that used the non-conforming water
  • Physical and chemical properties of the finished product where the water was used that could mitigate the OOS attribute
  • Product administration routes and potentially sensitive/susceptible users

9.4.3 SPECIAL ALERT AND ACTION LEVEL SITUATIONS

In new or significantly altered water systems, where there is limited or no historical data from which to derive trends, it is common to establish initial Alert and Action Levels based on equipment design capabilities. As the system ages, a steady state microbiome (microorganism types and levels) may develop due to the collective effects of system design, source water, maintenance, and operation. This established or mature microbiome may be higher than the one detected when the water system was new. Therefore, there is cause for the impurity levels to increase over this maturation period and eventually stabilize.

9.4.5 SOURCE WATER CONTROL

A reasonable maximum bacterial Action Level for source water is 500 cfu/mL. This number is derived from US EPA NPDWR where it is used as an Action Level for the water authority. Considering the potential concern about objectionable microorganisms and chemical contaminants in the source water, contacting the water provider about the problem should be an immediate first step.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 三層控制架構

比喻說明 Analogy — 三層標準的比喻

可以用「交通燈系統」比喻三層控制架構：

重點提示 Key Notes — OOS 水的影響評估

水系統 OOS 事件的調查是最複雜的 QC 調查之一，因為水被用於多個批次和多種製程。調查必須回答：哪些產品批次可能受影響？受影響的程度如何？受影響批次的處置方式？影響評估需要跨部門協作（QC + QA + 生產），並可能需要通知客戶（CDMO 情況）或監管機構（重大偏差）。

重點提示 Key Notes — 新系統的 Alert/Action Level 設定

新水系統剛啟動時（前 12 個月），微生物計數往往呈現「成熟化趨勢」——從初始的極低計數，逐漸建立穩定的 microbiome。因此，新系統的 Alert Level 不應以系統第一個月的完美數據為基礎，否則在系統成熟後會造成大量誤報（false alert）。建議：Phase 1/2 PQ 完成後，基於第一年的完整數據重新調整 Alert/Action Level，並記錄在 Annual Review 中。
