Introduction — 緒論

Original Text

Radiation sterilization utilizes the lethal effect of various forms of radiation as a means of microbial destruction. Ionizing radiation (gamma, x-ray, or beam) sterilization is used extensively for the sterilization of medical devices and for a variety of other materials and products. Nonionizing sterilization methods such as microwave, infrared, x-ray, and ultraviolet light may be useful but have more restricted application, and are outside the scope of this chapter. This chapter provides an overview of sterilization using ionizing radiation and its validation, including dose setting, material compatibility, and dose verification.

The effects of radiation on materials can be substantial and are a major consideration when manufacturers select radiation as a processing method. The advantages of sterilization by irradiation include simplicity, absence of mechanical complexity, reproducibility, and overall efficiency. In fact, radiation sterilization is unique because the basis of control essentially is the absorbed radiation dose, which can be precisely measured.

Methods used to establish appropriate radiation doses to achieve the desired sterility assurance level are defined in ISO 11137-1 Sterilization of Health Care Products; ISO 11137-2 Sterilization of Health Care Products—Radiation—Part 2: Establishing the Sterilization Dose; and ISO TS 13004:2013 Sterilization of Health Care Products—Radiation—Substantiation of Selected Sterilization Dose: Method VDmaxSD. These methods include Method 1, Method 2A, Method 2B, and Method VDmax, which differ in the specific testing scheme and the number of articles that are needed for testing and are based on certain assumptions about bioburden.

The use of a biological indicator is inappropriate during radiation sterilization validation because (a) there are accurate correlations between dose measurement and microbial destruction for a wide range of microorganisms and (b) the established dose setting methods are based on the material’s bioburden in its natural state. These correlations have been developed by the medical device industry and provide a direct methodology for process control.

Dosimetry plays a central role in radiation sterilization and serves as a direct means for affirming process lethality. The radiation dose measured in kGy (formerly MRads) is directly related to the lethal effects of the radiation on microorganisms. The measured dose has the same utility as F0 in steam sterilization. Routine process control for radiation sterilization is provided by one or more reference dosimeters on the exterior of the packages (after dosimeters have been correlated during validation with dose measurement inside the package). The robustness and reliability of the absorbed dose of the article to be sterilized can support parametric release, as described in Terminally Sterilized Pharmaceutical Products—Parametric Release 〈1222〉, for many items.

“Radiation sterilization is unique because the basis of control essentially is the absorbed radiation dose, which can be precisely measured.”

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

輻射滅菌的獨特優勢：與蒸汽滅菌（需要控制溫度、壓力、時間）或濐滅滅菌（需要控制濃度、溫度、濕度、時間）相比，輻射滅菌的控制參數本質上就是一個：吸收劑量（absorbed dose）。這個劑量可以被精確測量，這讓輻射滅菌成為所有滅菌方法中「最可量化」的一種。

kGy（千戈瑞）：輻射吸收劑量的國際單位。1 kGy = 1000 J/kg。舊單位 MRad 已不再使用（1 MRad = 10 kGy）。

與 F0 的類比：在蒸汽滅菌中，F0 是衡量製程致死性的核心指標；在輻射滅菌中，kGy 劑量扮演完全相同的角色。兩者都是可測量的物理參數，可直接關聯到微生物殺滅效果。

重點提示 Key Notes

生物指示劑不適用：這是輻射滅菌與蒸汽滅菌的重大差異。蒸汽滅菌驗證會使用生物指示劑（BI）來確認製程致死性，但輻射滅菌不需要，因為：

  • 劑量計（dosimeters）提供的物理測量比生物系統更可靠、更可重現
  • 劑量與微生物殺滅之間已有精確的關聯性數據
  • 劑量設定方法基於材料的自然狀態生物負載，而非人工指示菌

參數放行（Parametric Release）：由於劑量測量的高可靠性，輻射滅菌產品可以基於製程參數（而非無菌試驗結果）進行放行。這在 USP 〈1222〉 中有明確描述。

CDMO 實務應用

RTU 容器與拚棄式組件：對於使用預填裝注射筒（PFS）、RTU 瓶、RTU tub 系統的 CDMO，這些組件通常由供應商以輻射滅菌（通常是 gamma）進行最終滅菌。CDMO 需要：

  • 驗證供應商的滅菌證書（CoS）是否符合 ISO 11137 要求
  • 確認最小與最大劑量範圍與材料相容性數據
  • 在穩定性試驗中納入輻射曝露的影響評估
Gamma Sterilization — γ 射線滅菌

Original Text

Gamma sterilization entails the use of a specifically designed facility where items to be sterilized are exposed to a Co60 radiation source in a manner that ensures uniform dosing. Highly penetrating photons (gamma rays) are emitted from Co60 as it decays to Ni60. The half-life for this isotope is 5.27 years, which means that over the course of each year the source loses about 12% of its radioactivity. This steady reduction in radioactivity requires that radiation process operators adjust their process controls (typically exposure time) to maintain the established dose required. Periodically, additional Co60 is required to maintain practical throughput.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Co60 射源：鎅60 是最常用的 gamma 射源。它衰變為鎳60（Ni60）時釋放高能量 gamma 光子。關鍵特性：

  • 穿透力極強：gamma 射線可穿透厚密包裝和大型棟板貨物，不需解包
  • 半衰期 5.27 年：每年大約損失 12% 的放射性，必須定期調整曝露時間或補充射源
  • 連續照射：物品通過輸送系統繞行射源，接受連續劑量照射

重點提示 Key Notes

對 CDMO 的意義：大多數 RTU 容器供應商（如 Schott、Gerresheimer、SCHOTT Pharma）使用 gamma 滅菌來處理預填裝玻璃瓶、注射筒和 tub 系統。CDMO 在接收這些組件時，應確認供應商的滅菌證書中包含：實際照射劑量範圍、劑量計類型、及該批次的劑量圖參考資料。

X-Ray Sterilization — X 射線滅菌

Original Text

X-ray sterilizers generate highly penetrative photons similar to the gamma photons from Co60 irradiators. X-ray photons are generated when accelerated electrons impact a target such as tantalum. These systems rely on scanning of materials with x-ray photons in order to sterilize them. Properly maintained, these systems are able to deliver a constant dose over time. No local radioactive source is required for x-ray sterilization systems.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

X 射線 vs Gamma：X 射線滅菌生成的光子與 gamma 射線物理特性相似（都是高穿透性光子），但來源完全不同：

  • Gamma：來自放射性同位素 Co60 的核衰變
  • X-ray：來自加速電子擊中錦（tantalum）等金屬靶材，是電機產生的

主要優勢：不需要本地放射源，可隨時開關；劑量不會隨時間衰減；可以提供與 gamma 相近的高穿透力。

E-Beam Sterilization — 電子束滅菌

Original Text

Electron beam systems rely on scanning of objects with focused electrons to sterilize the items within a defined radiation field. Properly maintained and controlled, these systems deliver a constant dose, so there is no change in dose with respect to time. The principal advantages of electron beam sterilization are a much higher dose rate and the absence of a localized radioactive source. These systems can be installed and operated by the end user.

Electron beam penetration is substantially less than that obtained with photons, and therefore dose mapping is critical to ensure that items of varying density and complexity are properly sterilized. Because of the high dose rates used with electron beam sterilization, some materials can experience significantly higher temperatures than the same materials would experience in Co60 irradiation.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

電子束的特色：

  • 高劑量率：處理速度極快，產品曝露時間以秒計（gamma 以小時計）
  • 低穿透力：電子穿透能力遠低於光子，只適合薄型或低密度物品
  • 溫度效應：高劑量率可能導致材料升溫，需評估熱敏感材料的相容性
  • 無放射源：可由終端使用者自行安裝與操作

重點提示 Key Notes

劑量分佈圖更為關鍵：由於電子束穿透力有限，物品密度與幾何形狀的變化會顯著影響劑量分佈。因此，劑量分佈圖（dose mapping）對電子束滅菌而言比 gamma 更加重要。

Validation of Radiation Sterilization — 輻射滅菌驗證

Original Text

Cycle development for radiation requires the identification of an appropriate radiation dose for the objects and confirmation that the dose does not adversely affect the material’s essential quality attributes. In other words, analysts should identify the minimum sterilization dose as well as the maximum dose the material can withstand without negative effects. With this information analysts can set the dose for a specific radiation sterilization application.

Dose setting or dose establishment typically is achieved by following one of the ISO methods. These are Method 1, Method 2A, Method 2B, and Method VDmax. The choice of the most appropriate method depends chiefly on production batch size, knowledge of the normal bioburden, and the material’s sensitivity to radiation. Dose mapping plays an important role through the cycle development and dose-setting exercise.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

劑量窗口（Dose Window）：輻射滅菌驗證的核心目標是確定一個「劑量窗口」：

  • 最小劑量（Dmin）：足以達成目標 SAL（通常 10−6）的最低劑量
  • 最大劑量（Dmax）：材料可承受而不影響品質屬性的最高劑量

Dmax / Dmin 比值越大，製程窗口越寬，操作靈活度越高。

重點提示 Key Notes

選擇劑量設定方法的三個關鍵因素：

  1. 批次大小：大批量生產適合 Method 1；小批量可考慮 VDmax
  2. 生物負載知識：能枝舉生物負載時用 Method 1；不能時用 Method 2
  3. 材料對輻射的敏感度：敏感材料需要更精確的劑量控制，適合 Method 1 的精細方法
Dose Setting — 劑量設定

Original Text

Method 1 is based upon the assignment and verification of a sterilization dose based on a microbial population. The resistance of the microbial population is not determined, and dose setting is based on a standard radiation resistance assigned to the microbial population, derived from data obtained from medical device manufacturers and from the literature. This analysis assumes that the distribution of standard resistance represents a more severe challenge than the natural microbial population on the material to be sterilized. A verification dose study should confirm the relative resistance assumption.

The VDmax method is similar to Method 1 (it requires both bioburden and dose verification testing) but relies on bioburden ranges (e.g., <1000 CFUs per item for a 25-kGy sterilization dose and, for example, 0.1–1.5 CFUs per item for a 15-kGy sterilization dose).

The more complex Method 2 does not require the enumeration of the microbial population for the purpose of setting the sterilization dose (although it is required for routine monitoring and control) but uses a series of incremental dose exposures to establish a dose at which approximately 1 out of 100 samples irradiated at that dose will be nonsterile. This is not the sterilization dose, but it provides the basis to determine the sterilization dose by extrapolation from this information.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 Key Notes

Method 2 的獨特邏輯：Method 2 不直接測定生物負載，而是通過遞增劑量實驗找到「100 個樣品中約 1 個未達無菌」的劑量點。然後從這個點向上外推，計算出能達成 PNSU 10−6 的滅菌劑量。這是一種「由下而上」的方法，與 Method 1 的「從已知生物負載出發」方向相反。

核心概念解析 Dose Setting Methods

25 kGy 的由來：許多人認為 25 kGy 是「標準」滅菌劑量，但實際上它是基於 VDmax 方法對生物負載 <1000 CFU 的物品所設定的劑量。如果生物負載更低，可能只需要 15 kGy。這不是一個「通用數字」，而是基於特定條件的計算結果。

Material Compatibility — 材料相容性

Original Text

Once the required dose level has been established, the maximum dose should be established. Analysts typically establish the maximum dose by evaluating the highest likely dose that might be seen during the sterilization process, adding a safety factor, and evaluating the item for immediate and long-term effects of the radiation exposure. Some materials may appear unchanged initially, and the effects may become evident only over time.

The evaluation should consider all of the materials exposed to the radiation processing, especially the drug product and its primary container. Product stability, safety, and functionality should be confirmed over the product’s intended use period.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

材料相容性評估的三個層次：

  1. 立即效應：照射後立即檢測的變化（外觀、顏色、氣味、機械強度）
  2. 長期效應：經過儲存後才顯現的變化（脆化、黃變、射出物）
  3. 安全因子：Dmax 應超過製程中可能出現的最高劑量，加上適當的安全裕量

CDMO 實務應用

RTU 容器與 SUS 組件的材料相容性：

  • 玻璃容器（Type I Borosilicate）：對輻射極為穩定，通常無須擔心材料相容性
  • 塑膠組件（如袋膜、管路、連接器）：輻射可能導致高分子鏈段裂（脆化）或交聯（變硬），以及產生可萃取物（E&L）
  • 橡膠塞（stoppers, plungers）：輻射可能影響彈性、密封性與萃取物檢測結果
  • 藥品本身：如果輻射滅菌應用於最終產品，必須評估藥品穩定性
Dose Verification — 劑量驗證

Original Text

The methods for cycle development and dose setting rely on the bioburden approach. Analysts use defined presterilization bioburden controls and periodic evaluation of the process effects on the bioburden to maintain cycle efficacy. Establishing the required dose for microbial destruction during cycle development uses the bioburden’s natural resistance; analysts then extrapolate the dose-setting algorithms to establish a dose that is capable of delivering a probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU) of 1 × 10−6.

Figure 1 illustrates VDmax dose-setting outcomes: higher bioburden populations with higher radiation resistance require greater sterilization doses, while lower bioburden with lower resistance can be sterilized at reduced doses.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

生物負載方法（Bioburden Approach）：輻射滅菌的劑量設定完全基於產品上的自然微生物汙染，而非人工指示菌。這是因為：

  • 輻射對不同微生物的殺滅力已有大量研究數據
  • 自然生物負載的輻射抗性通常低於指示菌假設值
  • 驗證劑量研究會確認這個假設是否成立

PNSU 10−6：即每百萬個滅菌單位中最多允許一個未達無菌。這與蒸汽滅菌的 SAL 10−6 目標相同。

比喻說明 Analogy

Figure 1 的意義：想像兩座城堡。一座有 1000 個士兵（高生物負載）且皆穿重甲（高輻射抗性），需要強大的攻城部隊（高劑量）。另一座只有 1 個哨兵（低生物負載）且無甲（低抗性），小型巡邏隊（低劑量）即可拿下。這就是為什麼生物負載與抗性共同決定所需的滅菌劑量。

Validation Activities — 驗證活動

Original Text

Equipment Qualification

The use of gamma sterilization requires initial and periodic assessment of equipment controls and parameters necessary to establish the system’s capability. Sterilization systems that deliver directed beams or rays have controls for scan speed, source intensity, and system timers. The other elements of radiation sterilization equipment largely are related to material transport and are easily qualified. Qualification of safety controls, devices, and software is required.

Empty Chamber Dose Mapping

This optional exercise entails mapping the target area for radiation dose in the absence of a load and is a possible means to evaluate a focused beam or ray system. It provides a baseline of performance that may be useful over time.

Load Dose Mapping

The arrangement of items in irradiation containers, carriers, or pallets is an essential part of the initial validation exercise. The goal of the mapping is to define the distribution of a dose throughout the load items and establish a configuration that minimizes dose variation across the materials. The items are mapped using multiple dosimeters positioned internally and externally. Identification of maximum dose location is important in evaluating the effects of the radiation on the load items. The location of minimum and maximum dose can be identified from the dose mapping data for monitoring in routine sterilization of materials.

Biological Indicators

The use of biological indicators for radiation sterilization is not indicated because the physical and dosimetric measurements employed are more reliable, reproducible, and robust than biological systems.

Dosimetry

Process control for radiation sterilization relies heavily on dosimetry for both initial development and ongoing verification. For guidance on the selection and use of a dosimetry system for use in radiation sterilization refer to ASTM E2628 Practice for Dosimetry in Radiation Processing. The dosimeters and the instruments used with them should be calibrated according to ISO/ASTM 51261 Practice for Calibration of Routine Dosimetry Systems for Radiation Processing.

Process Confirmation

The core of the validation activity is the confirmation of acceptable lethality using dosimeters that are positioned across the material as it is processed through the radiation-sterilizing equipment. Proof of sterilization cycle efficacy is provided in replicate studies in which the dosimetry results correspond to the required minimum value for sterility assurance and demonstrate that the maximum value has not been exceeded.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 Key Notes

劑量分佈圖（Dose Mapping）的關鍵輸出：

  • Dmin 位置：放置常規監控劑量計的地方，確認最低劑量達標
  • Dmax 位置：評估材料相容性的基礎，確認最高劑量不超過材料耐受限
  • 劑量均勻性比（DUR）：Dmax/Dmin 比值，越接近 1.0 越好，表示劑量分佈均勻

劑量計標準體系：ASTM E2628 提供劑量計系統選擇指南；ISO/ASTM 51261 規範校準要求。常用劑量計類型包括丙氨酸劑量計、薄膜劑量計等。

CDMO 實務應用

對於接收 RTU tub 與 SUS 組件的 CDMO：雖然滅菌由供應商執行，但 CDMO 應確認供應商的驗證文件包括：

  • 完整的劑量分佈圖數據與 DUR 值
  • 劑量計校準記錄與可追溯性
  • 批次放行證明上的實際照射劑量範圍
  • 生物負載監控與定期劑量審計記錄
Routine Process Control — 常規製程控制

Original Text

Radiation sterilization should be subject to formal controls that maintain the validated status. The practices outlined in General Principles of Sterilization of Compendial Articles 〈1229〉 provide the general requirements appropriate for all sterilization systems. This is accomplished by a number of related practices that are essential for the continued use of the process over an extended period of time. The practices that are essential to maintain validated status for radiation include training, calibration, physical measurements, bioburden monitoring, change control, preventive maintenance, and periodic dose audits.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

維持驗證狀態的七大實務：

  1. Training 培訓：操作人員能力確認與持續教育
  2. Calibration 校準：劑量計與相關儀器的定期校準
  3. Physical Measurements 物理測量：製程參數的常規記錄與審查
  4. Bioburden Monitoring 生物負載監控：定期確認產品的預滅菌微生物水平未超出劑量設定假設
  5. Change Control 變更控制：任何裝載配置、材料或製程變更的管理
  6. Preventive Maintenance 預防性維護：設備的定期維護計畫
  7. Periodic Dose Audits 定期劑量審計：通常每季或每年重新驗證劑量設定是否仍然有效

Chapter Summary / 全章重點回顧

  • Radiation Sterilization uses ionizing radiation (gamma, e-beam, X-ray) to destroy microorganisms; control is based on the precisely measurable absorbed dose (kGy), making it the most quantifiable sterilization method / 輻射滅菌以吸收劑量為唯一控制參數，精確可測量，是最可量化的滅菌方法
  • Three modalities offer different trade-offs: gamma (high penetration, isotope source), X-ray (high penetration, electrical), e-beam (highest dose rate, limited penetration) / 三種方式各有優勢：gamma 高穿透、X-ray 無放射源、e-beam 極快速度
  • Dose setting follows ISO 11137 methods (1, 2A, 2B, VDmax), chosen based on batch size, bioburden knowledge, and material sensitivity / 劑量設定依據 ISO 11137 方法，需考慮批量大小、生物負載與材料敏感度
  • Biological indicators are not used — dosimetry provides more reliable, reproducible process control than biological systems / 不使用生物指示劑，劑量計提供更可靠的製程控制
  • Material compatibility must assess both immediate and long-term radiation effects on drug product and primary container / 材料相容性需評估立即與長期輻射效應，含藥品與主要容器
  • Dose mapping identifies Dmin and Dmax locations for routine monitoring; the dose uniformity ratio (DUR) characterizes load configuration quality / 劑量分佈圖確定 Dmin、Dmax 位置與劑量均勻性
  • Routine control requires training, calibration, bioburden monitoring, change control, preventive maintenance, and periodic dose audits / 常規控制需含培訓、校準、生物負載監控、變更控制、預防維護與定期劑量審計
  • CDMO relevance: RTU tubs and SUS components are typically gamma-sterilized by suppliers; CDMO must verify supplier certificates, dose ranges, and material compatibility data / CDMO 應驗證供應商的輻射滅菌證書、劑量範圍與材料相容性數據
