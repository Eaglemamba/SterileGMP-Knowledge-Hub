Introduction — 緒論

Original Text 原文內容

Steam sterilization of aqueous liquids (including both suspensions and emulsions with mixing), also known as sterilization of nonporous loads, is the method of choice for aqueous parenteral products, in-process aqueous liquids, laboratory media, and biological waste materials.

This type of sterilization is accomplished primarily in closed containers. During steam sterilization by direct contact (also called steam sterilization of parts, hard goods, or porous items) the steam in the chamber directly contacts the surface of load items to effect sterilization (see ⟨1229.1⟩). In contrast, sterilization of liquids in containers is accomplished by application of heat to the container, heating of the container wall, and ultimately heating of the internal liquid volume. This can be accomplished using steam, superheated water, and air in various combinations.

Some aqueous liquids are susceptible to over-processing that could render them unfit for their intended use. Manufacturers should consider the influence of these differences when they establish a suitable process.

During the sterilization of liquid-filled containers, differential pressures between the interior of the containers and the sterilization chamber may potentially impact container integrity. Air over-pressure is used to minimize the pressure differential between the container and the sterilizer to protect the integrity of the container, especially prefilled syringes and plastic containers.

Before sterilization of product containers, manufacturers should consider the potential adverse consequences of excess heat on the materials. In order to ensure sterility as well as functionality, the process definition and validation method used must incorporate both lower and upper temperature and time limits on the process conditions.

When the overkill method can be used for sterilization of sealed liquid containers, it is the preferred method and is described in ⟨1229.1⟩. When product quality attributes can be impaired by excessive heat, the sterilization process should use less time or a lower temperature to minimize the adverse effect on the materials. Sterilization time–temperature or F0 conditions (F0 is defined as the equivalent sterilization time relative to a base temperature of 121°) include both lower (sterility-related) and upper (stability-related) limits.1

Manufacturers commonly employ the bioburden/biological indicator (BB/BI) or bioburden methods when constraints on the material's ability to withstand the process require the use of less aggressive conditions. This approach requires appropriate controls on presterilization bioburden and/or product-related D-values in conjunction with bioindicators of lower spore count or resistance to ensure sterilization.

1 Degradation kinetics may differ from those of microbial kill, and F0 values may not be sufficient to fully evaluate “worst case” effects.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Key Concepts 核心概念

Direct Contact vs. Liquid Sterilization（直接接觸 vs. 液體滅菌）：

  • ⟨1229.1⟩ Direct Contact — 蒸汽直接接觸物品表面（如工具、零件、濾芯）。熱傳遞快速且均勻。
  • ⟨1229.2⟩ Liquid in Containers — 熱量必須穿過容器壁才能加熱內部液體。存在熱傳遞延遲（thermal lag），容器越大、液體體積越多，達到滅菌溫度的時間越長。

F0（F-zero）：以 121°C 為基準溫度、z-value = 10°C 為標準，將實際不同溫度下的滅菌時間換算為等效的 121°C 暴露時間。F0 是衡量濕熱滅菌製程殺菌能力的通用指標。

Air Over-Pressure（空氣過壓）：滅菌過程中容器內部液體受熱膨脹，產生內壓。若不施加外部空氣壓力來平衡，預充填注射器或軟性塑膠容器可能變形或破裂。這是終端滅菌設備設計的關鍵參數。

Key Notes 重點提示

雙重限制 — 滅菌 vs. 穩定性：終端滅菌的獨特挑戰在於必須同時滿足兩個方向的要求：

  • 下限（Lower limit） — 確保足夠的 F0 來殺滅微生物（≥8 min for BB/BI method）
  • 上限（Upper limit） — 避免過多熱量損害產品品質（降解、變色、效價損失）

這就是為什麼終端滅菌的溫度控制窗口（process window）往往比直接蒸汽接觸滅菌窄得多。

Analogy 比喻說明

烤箱 vs. 隔水加熱：

⟨1229.1⟩ 的直接蒸汽接觸就像烤箱烤餅乾 — 熱空氣直接接觸食物表面，溫度均勻且快速。而 ⟨1229.2⟩ 的液體滅菌就像隔水加熱巧克力 — 你必須先加熱外層的水，再透過碗壁傳熱到巧克力。外層溫度太高會燒焦碗邊的巧克力（over-processing），太低又融不了中心（under-processing）。

Terminal Sterilization of Products — 產品終端滅菌

Original Text 原文內容

The maintenance of product attributes may require the use of sterilizing conditions that are less aggressive and sterilization equipment, cycles, and validation methods adapted to these more restricted circumstances. The substantial variations in equipment designs and methods for terminal sterilization preclude a singular description of a typical cycle.

All terminal sterilizers heat the load, but they accomplish this in varying ways: saturated steam; steam–air mixtures, steam–air–water mixtures, and superheated water. Air over-pressure for maintenance of container integrity and cooling containers and water for heating/cooling of the load may be present depending upon the autoclave size, throughput expectations, and container.

CDMO Decision Point CDMO 決策點

Terminal Sterilization vs. Aseptic Processing 終端滅菌 vs. 無菌製程：

法規機構（FDA, EMA, PIC/S Annex 1）明確偏好終端滅菌，因為它在最終容器中進行滅菌，提供更高的無菌保證水準（SAL）。然而，許多生物製劑、蛋白質藥物無法承受 121°C 的熱處理。

CDMO 在承接新產品時，必須先評估：產品是否能耐受終端滅菌？如果不能，才選擇無菌充填（aseptic fill-finish）。這個決策直接影響設備投資、廠房設計和法規申報策略。

In-Process Fluids — 製程中液體

Original Text 原文內容

In-process fluids are used for pH adjustment, dilution to a specified volume, lubrication, and other purposes. In many instances these liquids are sterilized in conjunction with items that must be sterilized by direct steam contact, and the sterilization process must ensure that all items are adequately sterilized.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Mixed Load Challenge 混合裝載的挑戰

當製程中液體與硬物件（如不鏽鋼零件、矽膠管）一起滅菌時，存在一個根本衝突：液體加熱慢（thermal lag）而硬物件加熱快。滅菌循環必須確保液體容器的冷點（cold spot）達到足夠溫度，同時不會過度加熱硬物件。

在 CDMO 無菌充填線上，WFI（注射用水）、緩衝液等製程中液體通常會單獨滅菌，避免與其他物品混裝的驗證複雜性。

Laboratory Media — 實驗室培養基

Original Text 原文內容

Laboratory media often are sterilized in standard steam autoclaves with minor adaptations. Provision for slow exhaust (to reduce stress on container integrity and minimize boil over) and jacket cooling can help improve the basic steam sterilizer design and operation to better accommodate the materials.

The sterilization process may be specific for media containers or a combination of both liquid-filled containers and hard goods. Manufacturers should be aware of the potential for under- and over-processing across the load and must consider container size, container contents, and position.

When liquid-filled containers are combined in the same load with hard goods, manufacturers must consider the unique concerns of each to ensure all items are properly sterilized. Because laboratory media are considered self-indicating with respect to sterility, the use of internal biological indicators during validation is not required.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Self-Indicating Media 自我指示培養基

為什麼培養基不需要生物指示劑（BI）？

培養基的功能本身就是培養微生物。如果滅菌不完全，殘留的微生物會在培養基上生長，導致培養基混濁或產生菌落 — 這就是「自我指示」。因此，培養基滅菌驗證可以直接透過觀察培養基本身是否有微生物生長來確認滅菌效果。

Slow Exhaust 慢排汽的原因

滅菌完成後如果快速降壓排汽，液體中的溶解氣體會突然釋放（如同搖晃後打開汽水瓶），導致培養基沸騰溢出、容器破裂。慢排汽讓壓力緩慢降低，液體溫度逐漸下降到沸點以下後再解除壓力。

Biowaste Sterilization — 生物廢棄物滅菌

Original Text 原文內容

The sterilization of biowaste in sealed containers from laboratory or production use is similar to parts sterilization. The process is defined to ensure a minimum time–temperature exposure or attainment of a specified F0 value throughout all items of the load.

Depending on the potential contaminants present, the autoclave design may incorporate condensate collection/sterilization or sterilizable exhaust filters to ensure that pathogens are adequately contained. Because the objective of biowaste sterilization is to render the materials safe for contact and disposal, the overkill method described in ⟨1229.1⟩ is employed.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Why Overkill for Biowaste 為什麼生物廢棄物用過殺法

生物廢棄物不需要擔心「品質損害」— 目的只是確保安全處置。因此可以使用最嚴格的過殺法（overkill），提供最高的安全保障。滅菌設備還需要特殊設計（冷凝液收集、可滅菌排氣過濾器），防止病原體在排汽過程中逸出。

在 CDMO 設施中，生物廢棄物滅菌是 BSL-2 或更高等級操作的必要環節，直接影響人員安全和環境保護。

Bioburden/Biological Indicator (BB/BI) Method — 生物負載/生物指示劑法

Original Text 原文內容

Application of the BB/BI method requires a thorough understanding of the bioburden type, population, and resistance typically present in the presterilized product-filled container. The method relies on substantial differences between moist heat resistance and the population of the bioburden present and the biological indicator used during validation.

BB/BI is a method in which the incomplete destruction (or destruction of a modest population) of a resistant biological indicator can be used to demonstrate the capability of the process to reliably destroy any bioburden. This is accomplished using detailed knowledge of the BB and BI populations and their relative resistance.

Typical BB microorganisms have only minimal resistance in comparison to BIs, and this can be confirmed by heat screening of BB isolates. The BB population is controlled by filtration steps for the fluid, process time limits, environmental controls, gowning systems, and other means.

The conventional BIs for terminal sterilization using the BB/BI method are Clostridium sporogenes ATCC 7955 and Bacillus subtilis ATCC 5230, although other strains can be used. The use of Geobacillus stearothermophilus for terminal sterilization is uncommon with the BB/BI method because the organism’s strong resistance to moist heat makes it poorly suited for this application.

Confirmation of an acceptable probability of a nonsterile unit (PNSU) can be accomplished using physical measurements and BI response (which define the lethality of the process) in conjunction with processing limits for the BB population and resistance (which define the N0 and D-value). D-value is the time (customarily in minutes) required to reduce the microbial population by 90% or 1 log10 cycle (i.e., to a surviving fraction of 1/10) and must be associated with the specific lethal conditions at which it was determined. For example, D121 is the D-value at 121°. Articles intended to be sterile must attain a ≤10−6 PNSU, i.e., less than or equal to 1 chance in 1 million that viable bioburden microorganisms are present.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Key Concepts 核心概念

BB/BI Method 的邏輯：這個方法的核心思維是 — 如果我們能在驗證中證明，一個比自然生物負載（BB）耐熱得多的生物指示劑（BI）被有效殺滅（或大幅減少），那麼實際製程中的自然微生物必然也會被消滅。

D-value（D 值）：在特定溫度下，將微生物數量降低 90%（1 個 log）所需的時間。D 值越大，微生物越耐熱。

  • 典型自然生物負載的 D121 ≈ 0.005 min（極低耐熱性）
  • BB/BI 法用的 BI（如 C. sporogenes）D121 ≈ 0.5 min
  • 過殺法用的 BI（G. stearothermophilus）D121 ≈ 1.5–3.0 min

PNSU（非無菌單位機率）：必須 ≤10−6，即每一百萬個容器中最多允許一個有活微生物的機率。這是藥典對無菌產品的基本要求。

Why Not G. stearothermophilus? 為什麼不用嗜熱脂肪芽孢桿菌？

G. stearothermophilus 的耐熱性太強（D121 約 1.5–3.0 min），如果用它做 BB/BI 法的 BI，要達到有意義的殺滅需要過高的 F0，這反而會損害產品品質 — 違背了 BB/BI 法「減少熱輸入」的初衷。

它更適合用於過殺法（overkill），因為過殺法不考慮產品耐熱性限制。

PNSU Equation — PNSU 計算公式

The PNSU can be determined from Equation 1:

Equation 1: PNSU Calculation

log Nu = −F / D + log N0

Where:

  • Nu = PNSU (probability of a nonsterile unit)
  • F = F0-value of the process (lethality)
  • D = D-value of the natural bioburden
  • N0 = bioburden population per container

Formula Explained 公式解析

Formula Breakdown 公式拆解

這個公式的意義：滅菌後殘留的微生物對數值 = −(殺菌力/耐熱性) + 初始數量

換句話說：起始微生物越多（N0 大）或微生物越耐熱（D 大），需要越多的殺菌力（F）才能達到目標 PNSU。

Table 1: PNSU Calculation Examples

Parameter Validation (BI) Routine Usage (BB)
F0 8.0 min 8.0 min
D121 0.5 min (BI) 0.005 min (bioburden)
N0 106 (BI) 102 (bioburden)
PNSU 10−10 10−1598

Table Interpretation 數據解讀

Why This Table Matters 這張表為什麼重要

Validation scenario（驗證情境）：

用 106 個高耐熱 BI（D121 = 0.5 min）挑戰 F0 = 8 min 的製程，PNSU = 10−10。雖然未達到 overkill 的 10−12，但仍遠低於 10−6 的要求。

Routine scenario（日常生產情境）：

自然生物負載只有 100 個/容器，且耐熱性極低（D121 = 0.005 min），同樣的 F0 = 8 min 製程產生的 PNSU = 10−1598 — 一個天文數字級別的安全餘裕。

這就是 BB/BI 法的精髓：用較溫和的條件（F0 = 8 min 而非 overkill 的 15+ min）就能對自然生物負載提供極高的安全保障，前提是你必須持續監控生物負載的數量和耐熱性。

Original Text 原文內容

Determining the resistance of the bioburden is accomplished using a heat-screening process during which a pure culture (a laboratory culture containing a single species of organism spores with minimal vegetative cells) is boiled at 100° for various periods. If the bioburden microorganism is viable after exposure, its resistance at 121° can be estimated for use in the PNSU calculation (see ⟨56⟩ Methods for Determination of Resistance of Microorganisms to Sterilization Processes).

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Heat Screening Process 熱篩檢流程

熱篩檢是一個實用的快速篩選方法：將生物負載的純培養物在 100°C 下煮沸不同時間（例如 5, 10, 15, 30 分鐘），然後檢查是否仍有存活。

  • 如果在 100°C 煮沸 10 分鐘後全部死亡 → 耐熱性很低，PNSU 計算結果會非常安全
  • 如果有存活 → 需要進一步測定精確的 D121 值，並評估是否影響製程安全

這是 BB/BI 法中「持續監控生物負載耐熱性」的核心工具之一。

Bioburden Method — 純生物負載法

Original Text 原文內容

In most respects the BB method is similar to the BB/BI indicator method. The difference lies in the isolation and characterization of the most-resistant bioburden microorganism. The worst-case isolate is used as the biological indicator in the evaluation of the process. For use in this manner, it must be cultured to produce a suitable challenge population. When this method is used, the bioburden of each process cycle must be closely controlled with respect to population and must be monitored for resistance.

Sterilization Cycle Control & F0 Calculation — 滅菌循環控制與 F0 計算

Original Text 原文內容

Process equipment for terminal sterilization typically is controlled by calibrated and pressure sensors in/on the chamber/equipment. During the exposure portions of the cycle, attainment of a minimum dwell time at a predefined temperature is used to support process lethality.

Cycle lethality for terminal sterilization customarily is measured using F0, which is defined as an actual exposure time at a variable process temperature that is equivalent to an exposure at 121° based on an ideal microorganism with a z-value of 10°. This can include lethality delivered during the heat-up and cooling phases of the sterilization process.

A z-value is defined as the number of degrees of temperature change necessary to change the D-value by a factor of 10. The F0 approach is used to evaluate to a single standard sterilization processes that are operated at varying temperature conditions.

The process lethality at temperatures other than 121° can be calculated to determine lethality equivalent to that provided at 121°. Sterilizer control systems for terminal sterilization deliver conditions within a predefined time–temperature or F0 range to avoid over-processing.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Key Concepts 核心概念

z-value（z 值）：使 D-value 變化 10 倍所需的溫度變化度數。對於濕熱滅菌，標準 z-value = 10°C。這意味著：

  • 溫度從 121°C 升高到 131°C → 殺菌速度加快 10 倍
  • 溫度從 121°C 降低到 111°C → 殺菌速度減慢 10 倍

Heat-up & Cooling Lethality（升溫和降溫殺菌貢獻）：F0 不只計算恆溫保持階段的殺菌力。升溫到達 100°C 以上時已開始有殺菌作用，降溫過程中液體仍處於高溫也在繼續殺菌。這些都要算入總 F0。

F0 Equation — F0 計算公式

Equation 2: F0 Calculation

F0 = ∫ 10(T − 121)/10 dt  ≈  Σ 10(T − 121)/10 · Δt

Where:

  • t = time (minutes)
  • T = temperature (°C) at time t
  • 121 = reference temperature (°C)
  • 10 = z-value (°C)

Summing the instantaneous lethality contributions over the entire sterilization process allows the calculation of the overall process lethality or F0 delivered over the course of the entire process at varying conditions.

Formula Explained 公式解析

Practical Interpretation 實務解讀

Lethality Rate（瞬時殺菌率）：10(T−121)/10

  • At T = 121°C: 100 = 1.0 — 每分鐘提供 1 分鐘的 F0（基準）
  • At T = 118°C: 10−0.3 = 0.50 — 每分鐘只提供 0.5 分鐘的 F0
  • At T = 124°C: 100.3 = 2.0 — 每分鐘提供 2 分鐘的 F0
  • At T = 115°C: 10−0.6 = 0.25 — 每分鐘只提供 0.25 分鐘的 F0

實務上，現代滅菌器的控制系統每隔 1–6 秒記錄一次溫度，自動計算即時 F0 累積值。當達到設定的 F0 目標（如 8.0 min），系統可自動進入冷卻階段。

F0 ≥8 min: The BB/BI Standard BB/BI 法的標準

USP ⟨1229.2⟩ 的 PNSU 計算範例以 F0 = 8.0 min 為基準。這是 BB/BI 法的典型最低要求。相比之下，overkill 法通常要求 F0 ≥15 min（對 106 個 D121 = 1.0 min 的 BI 達到 ≥12 log 殺滅）。

對於熱敏感產品，F0 = 8 min 已足夠提供 ≥10−6 PNSU，同時將熱輸入降至最低。

Validation of Liquid-Filled Container Sterilization — 液體充填容器滅菌驗證

Original Text 原文內容

The preferred method for steam sterilization is the overkill method as defined in ⟨1229⟩ and ⟨1229.1⟩. However, when the processed materials are susceptible to damage by moist heat at the overkill conditions, the BB/BI method is better suited because it results in reduced heat input while affording the same degree of process efficacy but with different controls.

The validation requirements for the BB/BI and BB methods are more rigorous than those associated with the overkill method. Although the overkill method can be confidently used without detailed consideration of the presterilization bioburden, application of the BB/BI and BB methods require continued monitoring and control of the bioburden, specifically the population and resistance.

Validation Elements 驗證要素

Effect of the Sterilization Process: A preliminary determination of the liquid and the container–closure system’s ability to withstand the expected sterilizing conditions should be made during product development. This can be accomplished by sterilization at conditions slightly in excess of the maximum expected and evaluating the effect on the material.

Equipment Qualification: Equipment qualification is a predefined program that confirms the equipment has been properly installed and that it operates as intended. Qualification of the sterilizing equipment provides a baseline for preventive maintenance and change control.

Empty Chamber Temperature Distribution: The dual considerations of sterility and stability commonly associated with sterilization of liquids require that equal attention be paid to potential under- and over-processing of the load. For this reason the temperature gradient across the sterilizer may require substantially tighter control than that expected in sterilization by direct contact.

Validation Analysis 驗證解析

Empty Chamber Distribution 空腔溫度分佈

終端滅菌的空腔溫度分佈研究比直接蒸汽接觸滅菌要求更嚴格。原因：

  • ⟨1229.1⟩ 直接接觸 — 允許 ±1°C 的溫差（因為過殺法有足夠的安全餘裕）
  • ⟨1229.2⟩ 液體滅菌 — 可能需要 ±0.5°C 甚至更窄的溫差，因為製程窗口（process window）較窄

溫度分佈不均會導致：冷區不夠 F0（滅菌不足），熱區過多 F0（產品降解）。

Biological Indicators 生物指示劑

The selection of a BI must be considered carefully because of the balance that must be maintained between attaining sterilization and maintaining the sterilized material’s essential quality attributes. The biological challenge is either directly inoculated into a liquid-filled container or is introduced via self-contained units provided there is adequate correlation between their resistance and the resistance that would occur in the process fluid.

The liquid can be either the product or a surrogate fluid. The resistance of the indicator in the product (and surrogate fluid, where used) must be known. The surrogate’s physical properties should approximate those of the product.

Liquid D-Value Determination

Determination of the thermal resistance (D-value and z-value) for the biological indicator in the liquid is required. This must be performed in a Biological Indicator Evaluation Resistometer (BIER) in replicate. The thermal resistance of each BI lot in the liquid should be determined.

BI Selection 生物指示劑選擇

Direct Inoculation vs. Self-Contained 直接接種 vs. 自含式

  • Direct inoculation（直接接種）：將 BI 孢子直接加入產品液體中。優點：最能代表實際滅菌條件。缺點：需要從液體中回收孢子來判定結果。
  • Self-contained units（自含式 BI）：BI 封裝在小容器中，放入產品容器旁邊。優點：操作方便。缺點：必須證明自含式 BI 在其載體中的耐熱性與在產品液體中的耐熱性有良好的相關性。

BIER Testing BIER 測試

BIER（生物指示劑評估耐熱器）是一種精確控制溫度的設備，專門用於測定 BI 的 D-value。關鍵要求：

  • 必須在產品液體（或替代液體）中測定 D-value — 因為液體成分可能影響 BI 的耐熱性
  • 每批次 BI 都需要測定 — 批次間的 D-value 可能有差異
  • 必須做重複試驗（replicate）以確保數據可靠

Container Mapping 容器映射

Liquid-fill containers with volumes greater than or equal to 100 mL should be mapped to determine internal cold spots. The mapping should be performed with product containers oriented as they would be within the load. The temperature probes should be introduced into the containers using methods that maintain container integrity.

Smaller containers (less than 100 mL) have fewer discernible cold spots, the importance of which is reduced as container size decreases. Smaller containers can be monitored with temperature probes secured to their exterior. The “cold spot” should be considered a “region” and not a single point in the container.

Load Positioning and Mapping 裝載定位與映射

A fixed loading position within the sterilizer may be necessary for proper sterilization to ensure uniformity of heating and cooling in routine use. Load-mapping studies should be performed to determine the coldest and hottest locations within the load.

Validation of variable-size load patterns is accomplished using a bracketing approach for which success with maximum and minimum loads (avoiding both under- and over-processing) establishes the acceptability of intermediate-size loads.

In product sterilization, only a single-size container with a single product lot is processed concurrently.

Cold spot = region, not a point — 冷點應該被理解為一個「區域」而非精確的「點」。在大容量容器中，冷區通常位於幾何中心或底部（因為熱從外壁向內傳遞，中心最後受熱）。

Bracketing Approach 括弧法

驗證最大裝載量和最小裝載量兩個極端，如果都通過（F0 在可接受範圍內），則認為中間的任何裝載量也是可接受的。

  • Maximum load 最大裝載 — 驗證最難加熱的情境（cold spot 的最差條件）
  • Minimum load 最小裝載 — 驗證最容易過熱的情境（hot spot 的最差條件）

注意：終端滅菌的每次裝載只能處理單一容器規格、單一產品批次。不能像實驗室滅菌器那樣混裝不同產品。

Heat Penetration & Microbiological Challenge 熱穿透與微生物挑戰

The core of the validation activity is confirmation of acceptable heat penetration using temperature measurements and microbial challenge inactivation. Temperature probes and biological indicators are placed within the load at worst-case locations (e.g., the coldest portions of the loaded chamber).

Introduction of the thermocouples must not alter the integrity of the container. Biological challenges are placed in containers adjacent to those that contain heat penetration probes (or the same unit with external temperature measurement).

Proof of cycle efficacy is provided by replicate studies in which the BIs perform as expected and the physical measurements correspond to the expected values of time and temperature or F0. If the microbial and physical measurements do not correlate, an investigation is in order, and corrective action must be taken to rectify the discrepancy.

Samples from the hottest regions of the load are used for evaluation of material stability and quality.

Analysis 解析

Dual Sampling Strategy 雙重取樣策略

終端滅菌驗證的取樣邏輯：

  • Cold spots（冷點） → 放置溫度探針 + BI → 驗證滅菌效果（最差條件是否達到足夠 F0）
  • Hot spots（熱點） → 取樣做品質穩定性測試 → 驗證產品完整性（最嚴苛加熱條件下產品是否受損）

這兩個面向缺一不可 — 只測冷點可能忽略產品降解問題，只測熱點可能忽略滅菌不足風險。

Correlation Requirement 關聯性要求

物理測量（溫度/F0）和生物測量（BI 殺滅結果）必須一致。如果 F0 顯示足夠但 BI 仍存活，或反之 — 代表有未知因素影響滅菌效果，必須啟動偏差調查。

常見原因：探針定位不當、空氣殘留導致局部冷點、BI 載體影響耐熱性。

Product Quality & Stability Evaluation 產品品質與穩定性評估

Manufacturers must conduct ongoing evaluation of the product’s ability to withstand the routine sterilizing conditions. The evaluation should encompass the essential quality attributes with attention focused on known and potential new impurities and those materials that receive the most heat input.

Manufacturers also should evaluate appearance, other physical properties, and container–closure integrity as part of this effort. For microbiological media, the ability of the media to meet growth promotion and other requirements is required as indicated in the appropriate test chapters (e.g., ⟨61⟩, ⟨62⟩, and ⟨71⟩).

Analysis 解析

Container-Closure Integrity (CCI) 容器密封完整性

終端滅菌對容器密封系統的影響必須評估。高溫和壓力變化可能導致：

  • 橡膠瓶塞軟化或變形
  • 鋁蓋壓鍊鬆動
  • 塑膠容器永久變形
  • 預充填注射器活塞移位

CCI 測試（如真空衰減法、高壓放電法）應在滅菌前後都進行，確認滅菌製程不會破壞密封性。

Routine Process Control — 例行製程管控

Original Text 原文內容

All sterilization processes should be subject to formalized practices that maintain them in a controlled state. The practices outlined in ⟨1229⟩ include the general requirements appropriate for all sterilization systems. This is accomplished by a number of related practices that are essential for continued use of the process over an extended period of time.

The practices include: calibration, physical measurements, physicochemical integrators, indicators for sterilization, monitoring of bioburden, ongoing process control, change control, preventive maintenance, periodic reassessment, and training.

The use of parametric release is common in the terminal sterilization of finished product containers. This subject is addressed in Terminally Sterilized Pharmaceutical Products—Parametric Release ⟨1222⟩.

Tutorial Commentary 導師解析

Parametric Release 參數放行

Parametric release（參數放行）是終端滅菌最具效率的放行模式：不需要逐批做無菌試驗（sterility test），而是根據滅菌製程的物理參數（溫度、時間、F0）來判定產品是否無菌。

前提條件：

  • 完善的滅菌製程驗證（通常需要 overkill 或嚴格的 BB/BI 驗證）
  • 持續的設備校準和維護
  • 變更管控系統
  • 法規當局的核准（需要在申請書中明確提出）

參數放行的優勢：避免無菌試驗的統計局限性（USP ⟨71⟩ 的取樣量只能檢出約 10% 污染率的批次）。

Chapter Summary 本章重點回顧

  • Scope: USP ⟨1229.2⟩ covers moist heat sterilization of aqueous liquids in sealed containers — terminal sterilization of parenteral products, in-process fluids, laboratory media, and biowaste. 範圍涵蓋密封容器中水性液體的濕熱滅菌。
  • Key Difference from ⟨1229.1⟩: Heat must penetrate through the container wall to reach the liquid — creating thermal lag, differential pressure concerns, and the need for air over-pressure. 與 ⟨1229.1⟩ 的關鍵差異：熱量需穿透容器壁，產生熱延遲和壓差問題。
  • Dual Limits: Terminal sterilization of liquids requires both a lower F0 limit (sterility) and an upper F0 limit (product stability). 雙重限制：F0 下限確保無菌，上限保護產品穩定性。
  • Three Approaches: Overkill (preferred, F0 ≥15 min), BB/BI (F0 ≥8 min, heat-sensitive products), and Bioburden-only (most rigorous monitoring). 三種方法各有適用場景。
  • F0 Calculation: F0 = Σ 10(T−121)/10 · Δt — integrates lethality across heat-up, hold, and cool-down. F0 涵蓋升溫、恆溫保持和降溫全程的殺菌貢獻。
  • PNSU: log Nu = −F/D + log N0 — must achieve ≤10−6. BB/BI method at F0 = 8 min provides PNSU = 10−1598 for typical bioburden. PNSU 必須 ≤10−6。
  • Validation: Container mapping (≥100 mL internal, <100 mL external), load positioning (bracketing approach), heat penetration + BI challenge at cold spots, stability testing at hot spots. 驗證要素包括容器映射、裝載定位、熱穿透和穩定性測試。
  • Parametric Release: Physical parameters (F0, temperature, time) replace sterility testing for routine batch release — requires regulatory approval. 參數放行以物理參數取代無菌試驗。
  • CDMO Context: Terminal sterilization is preferred by regulators but limited to heat-stable products. The decision between terminal sterilization and aseptic processing fundamentally shapes facility design and regulatory strategy. CDMO 必須在產品開發早期做出此決策。
