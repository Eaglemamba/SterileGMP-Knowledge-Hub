Introduction & Legal Basis 簡介與法規基礎

Original Text 原文內容

INTRODUCTION

Test procedures for assessment of the quality levels of pharmaceutical articles are subject to various requirements. According to Section 501 of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act, assays and specifications in monographs of the USP–NF constitute legal standards. The Current Good Manufacturing Practice regulations [21 CFR 211.194(a)] require that test methods, which are used for assessing compliance of pharmaceutical articles with established specifications, must meet proper standards of accuracy and reliability. Also, according to these regulations [21 CFR 211.194(a)(2)], users of analytical methods described in USP–NF are not required to validate the accuracy and reliability of these methods, but merely verify their suitability under actual conditions of use. Recognizing the legal status of USP and NF standards, it is essential, therefore, that proposals for adoption of new or revised compendial analytical procedures be supported by sufficient laboratory data to document their validity.

The text of this information chapter harmonizes, to the extent possible, with the International Council for Harmonisation (ICH) tripartite guideline Validation of Analytical Procedures and the Methodology extension text, which are concerned with analytical procedures included as part of registration applications submitted within the EC, Japan, and the USA.

SUBMISSIONS TO THE COMPENDIA

Submissions to the compendia for new or revised analytical procedures should contain sufficient information to enable members of the USP Council of Experts and its Expert Committees to evaluate the relative merit of proposed procedures. In most cases, evaluations involve assessment of the clarity and completeness of the description of the analytical procedures, determination of the need for the procedures, and documentation that they have been appropriately validated. In most cases a submission will consist of the following sections:

  • Rationale: Identify the need for the procedure and describe the capability of the specific procedure proposed and why it is preferred.
  • Proposed Analytical Procedure: Complete description sufficiently detailed to enable persons "skilled in the art" to replicate it, including all important operational parameters, preparation of reagents, system suitability tests, blanks, and explicit formulas.
  • Data Elements: Thorough documentation of the validation of the analytical procedure, including summaries of experimental data and calculations substantiating each of the applicable analytical performance characteristics.

Accuracy 準確性

Original Text 原文內容

ACCURACY

Definition: The accuracy of an analytical procedure is the closeness of test results obtained by that procedure to the true value. The accuracy of an analytical procedure should be established across its range. [Note on terminology: The definition of accuracy in this chapter and ICH Q2 corresponds to unbiasedness only. In ISO, "accuracy" combines the concepts of unbiasedness (termed "trueness") and precision.]

Determination: In the case of the assay of a drug substance, accuracy may be determined by application of the analytical procedure to an analyte of known purity (e.g., a Reference Standard) or by comparison of the results of the procedure with those of a second, well-characterized procedure.

In the case of the assay of a drug in a formulated product, accuracy may be determined by application of the analytical procedure to synthetic mixtures of the drug product components to which known amounts of analyte have been added within the range of the procedure. If it is not possible to obtain samples of all drug product components, it may be acceptable either to add known quantities of the analyte to the drug product (i.e., "to spike") or to compare results with those of a second, well-characterized procedure.

In the case of quantitative analysis of impurities, accuracy should be assessed on samples spiked with known amounts of impurities. Where it is not possible to obtain samples of certain impurities or degradation products, results should be compared with those obtained by an independent procedure. In the absence of other information, it may be necessary to calculate the amount of an impurity based on comparison of its response to that of the drug substance (relative response factor).

Accuracy is calculated as the percentage of recovery by the assay of the known added amount of analyte in the sample, or as the difference between the mean and the accepted true value, together with confidence intervals.

The ICH documents recommend that accuracy should be assessed using a minimum of nine determinations over a minimum of three concentration levels, covering the specified range (i.e., three concentrations and three replicates of each concentration).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

準確性的計算方式：

  • 回收率（Recovery %）：最常用方式 = (測量值/已知添加量) × 100%
  • 偏差（Bias）：測量均值與真值的差異，搭配信賴區間表示
  • ICH最低要求：至少3個濃度水準，每個水準3份重複 = 最少9個測定值

比喻說明 Analogy

準確性就像射箭時箭落在靶心的距離——箭群平均落在靶心附近，就叫「準確」。精確性（Precision）則是箭群聚不聚攏（重現性）。可以有高精確性但低準確性（箭群很密集但偏離靶心），也可以有高準確性但低精確性（平均在靶心但散得很開）。兩者都重要，代表不同的誤差來源。

重點提示 Key Notes

加標回收（Spiking）是主要手段，但有限制：

  • 原料藥確效：用已知純度的參考標準品
  • 製劑確效：向空白製劑基質中添加已知量原料藥
  • 雜質確效：向樣品中添加已知量雜質標準品；若無法取得，使用相對響應因子計算
  • Spike需在分析方法的測定範圍內進行，不能超出線性範圍

Precision, Specificity, Detection & Quantitation Limits 精確性、特異性、偵測限與定量限

Original Text 原文內容

PRECISION

Definition: The precision of an analytical procedure is the degree of agreement among individual test results when the procedure is applied repeatedly to multiple samplings of a homogeneous sample. Precision is usually expressed as the standard deviation or relative standard deviation (coefficient of variation). Precision may be a measure of either the degree of reproducibility or of repeatability:

  • Repeatability: Use of the analytical procedure within a laboratory over a short period of time using the same analyst with the same equipment.
  • Intermediate precision (Ruggedness): Within-laboratory variation, as on different days, or with different analysts or equipment within the same laboratory.
  • Reproducibility: Use of the analytical procedure in different laboratories, as in a collaborative study.

ICH Determination: Repeatability should be assessed using a minimum of nine determinations covering the specified range (i.e., three concentrations and three replicates of each concentration) or using a minimum of six determinations at 100% of the test concentration.

SPECIFICITY

Definition: The ability to assess unequivocally the analyte in the presence of components that may be expected to be present, such as impurities, degradation products, and matrix components. Lack of specificity of an individual analytical procedure may be compensated by other supporting analytical procedures.

  • Identification tests: Ensure the identity of the analyte.
  • Purity tests: Accurate statement of the content of impurities (e.g., related substances test, heavy metals, organic volatile impurities).
  • Assays: Provide an exact result on the content or potency of the analyte in a sample.

For chromatographic procedures, representative chromatograms should be presented to demonstrate selectivity, and peaks should be appropriately labeled. Peak purity tests (e.g., using diode array or mass spectrometry) may be useful.

DETECTION LIMIT

Definition: The lowest amount of analyte in a sample that can be detected, but not necessarily quantitated, under the stated experimental conditions. The detection limit is usually expressed as the concentration of analyte in the sample.

For instrumental procedures exhibiting background noise, typically acceptable signal-to-noise ratios are 2:1 or 3:1.

QUANTITATION LIMIT

Definition: The lowest amount of analyte in a sample that can be determined with acceptable Precision and Accuracy under the stated experimental conditions. Characteristic of quantitative assays for low levels of compounds in sample matrices, such as impurities in bulk drug substances and degradation products in finished pharmaceuticals.

For instrumental procedures exhibiting background noise, a typically acceptable signal-to-noise ratio is 10:1.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

精確性的三個層次 Three Levels of Precision

  • 重現性（Repeatability）：同一分析師、同一設備、同一天——最嚴格控制條件下的精確性
  • 中間精確性（Intermediate Precision / Ruggedness）：不同天、不同分析師或不同設備——反映日常使用條件
  • 再現性（Reproducibility）：不同實驗室——用於合作研究、方法轉移評估

對製藥QC而言，中間精確性最關鍵——反映了「在不同輪班、不同批次校正設備下，方法是否穩定」。

偵測限 vs. 定量限的比喻 DL vs. QL Analogy

把分析師想像成聽診器聽心音的醫生：偵測限是他「能聽到有什麼聲音」的最低閾值——可以確認「有/無」；定量限是他能「準確描述心跳速率」的最低閾值——能給出可靠的數字。偵測限用於限量試驗（pass/fail），定量限用於含量測定。信噪比：DL用2:1或3:1，QL用10:1。

特異性確效實作 Specificity Validation Practice

  • 鑑別試驗：測試含分析物的樣品（應陽性）+ 不含分析物但含結構相似物的樣品（應陰性）
  • 雜質試驗（色譜法）：加標已知雜質，確認定量準確；用DAD或MS確認峰純度
  • 含量測定：加標雜質或賦形劑，確認分析結果不受干擾
  • 強制降解試驗（Forced Degradation）：光照、熱、酸/鹼/氧化應激——確認方法能分離降解產物

關鍵指標公式 Key Formulas

精確性（%RSD）：
  %RSD = (標準差 / 均值) × 100

偵測限（信噪比法）：
  LOD = 3.3 × σ / S
  （σ = 基線噪聲標準差，S = 校正曲線斜率）

定量限（信噪比法）：
  LOQ = 10 × σ / S

可接受信噪比：
  LOD：S/N ≥ 2:1 或 3:1
  LOQ：S/N ≥ 10:1

Linearity, Range, Robustness & System Suitability 線性、範圍、穩健性與系統適用性

Original Text 原文內容

LINEARITY AND RANGE

Definition of linearity: The linearity of an analytical procedure is its ability to elicit test results that are directly, or by a well-defined mathematical transformation, proportional to the concentration of analyte in samples within a given range. Possible transformations may include log, square root, or reciprocal. If linearity is not attainable, a nonlinear model may be used.

Definition of range: The range of an analytical procedure is the interval between the upper and lower levels of analyte (including these levels) that have been demonstrated to be determined with a suitable level of precision, accuracy, and linearity using the procedure as written.

Determination: Linearity should be established initially by visual examination of a plot of signals as a function of analyte concentration. The correlation coefficient, y-intercept, slope of the regression line, and residual sum of squares should be submitted. ICH recommends a minimum of five concentrations for linearity.

ICH minimum specified ranges:

  • Assay of a drug substance or finished product: 80% to 120% of test concentration
  • Determination of an impurity: 50% to 120% of acceptance criterion
  • Content uniformity: minimum 70%–130% of test concentration
  • Dissolution testing: ±20% over the specified range

ROBUSTNESS

Definition: The robustness of an analytical procedure is a measure of its capacity to remain unaffected by small but deliberate variations in procedural parameters listed in the procedure documentation and provides an indication of its suitability during normal usage. Robustness may be determined during development of the analytical procedure.

SYSTEM SUITABILITY

If measurements are susceptible to variations in analytical conditions, these should be suitably controlled, or a precautionary statement should be included in the procedure. One consequence of the evaluation of Robustness and ruggedness should be that a series of system suitability parameters is established to ensure that the validity of the analytical procedure is maintained whenever used.

System suitability tests are based on the concept that the equipment, electronics, analytical operations, and samples to be analyzed constitute an integral system that can be evaluated as such. They are especially important in the case of chromatographic procedures. Submissions to the USP should make note of the requirements in Chromatography 〈621〉, System Suitability.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

線性確效的實作重點 Linearity Validation Practice

  • 最少5個濃度點（ICH建議），涵蓋測定範圍
  • 報告項目：相關係數(r²)、y截距、斜率、殘差平方和
  • 視覺檢查優先：先繪圖肉眼確認，再做統計回歸
  • 接受標準：r² 通常要求 ≥0.999（含量測定）；截距應接近零
  • 粒徑分析例外——不適用傳統線性定義，用濃度範圍不影響粒徑分佈結果來定義

穩健性的比喻 Robustness Analogy

穩健性就像測試一張食譜能不能應對「現實廚房」的小變化——烤箱溫度差5度、麵粉品牌換了、攪拌時間多30秒，成品還能達標嗎？對HPLC方法而言，就是測試：pH差0.2、有機溶劑比例差5%、柱溫差5°C、流速差0.1 mL/min——系統適用性（resolution、tailing factor）是否還合格？

系統適用性 vs. 穩健性的關係 System Suitability vs. Robustness

穩健性（Robustness）：方法開發/確效階段進行，目的是找出關鍵參數，設定合理的操作範圍。

系統適用性（System Suitability）：每次分析前運行，確認當次分析系統狀態符合要求。穩健性確效結果告訴你「系統適用性指標應設多嚴格」。兩者是因果關係。

各類方法的線性範圍要求 Range Requirements by Category

含量測定（原料藥/製劑）：
  80% ~ 120%（相對於試驗濃度）

雜質測定：
  50% ~ 120%（相對於接受標準）

含量均勻度：
  70% ~ 130%（相對於試驗濃度）

溶出度試驗：
  規格範圍 ± 20%
  例：緩控釋 30%~90%，則範圍為 10%~110%

實務應用 Practical Application

在CDMO場景中，方法轉移（Method Transfer）時通常需要確認中間精確性和特異性（在接收方實驗室的基質條件下）。若轉移協議要求完整確效，需要全套8個特性；若只是「使用已驗證方法」，則依<1226>進行驗證即可。建議在方法轉移協議（MTA）中明確界定確效vs.驗證的範疇，避免做了不必要的確效工作或少做了必要的驗證。

Validation Categories & Data Elements 確效類別與資料要素

Original Text 原文內容

DATA ELEMENTS REQUIRED FOR VALIDATION

This chapter covers the most common categories of tests for which validation data should be required. These categories are:

  • CATEGORY I: Analytical procedures for quantitation of major components of bulk drug substances or active ingredients (including preservatives) in finished pharmaceutical products.
  • CATEGORY II: Analytical procedures for determination of impurities in bulk drug substances or degradation compounds in finished pharmaceutical products. These procedures include quantitative assays and limit tests.
  • CATEGORY III: Analytical procedures for determination of performance characteristics (e.g., dissolution, drug release, and others).
  • CATEGORY IV: Identification tests.

a = May be required, depending on the nature of the specific test.

Already established general procedures (e.g., titrimetric determination of water or bacterial endotoxins) should be verified to establish their suitability for use, such as their accuracy (and absence of possible interference) when used for a new product or raw material.

The validity of an analytical procedure can be verified only by laboratory studies. Therefore, documentation of the successful completion of such studies is a basic requirement for determining whether a procedure is suitable for its intended application(s). Current compendial procedures are also subject to regulations that require demonstration of suitability under actual conditions of use (see Verification of Compendial Procedures 〈1226〉).

類別選擇邏輯 Category Selection Logic

確效類別的選擇就像選擇建築工程的安全規範：建一棟家用小屋（鑑別試驗/Category IV）只需符合基本安全要求，不需要地震工程評估；建一棟高層辦公樓（含量測定/Category I）則需要完整的結構分析、材料測試和荷載驗算。

Category IV（鑑別試驗）的特殊性 Category IV Notes

鑑別試驗不需要準確性、精確性、偵測限、定量限和線性——因為結果只是「符合/不符合」，不是定量數值。但特異性是必須確效的——必須證明方法能區分出目標分析物，而不會對相似化合物給出假陽性。

  • 法規地位：USP藥典方法具法律效力；藥廠使用時只需驗證（<1226>），提交USP時才需完整確效
  • 與ICH Q2協調：美國、歐盟、日本採用相同框架，確效資料可相互支持

  • 穩健性：方法開發/確效時進行，結果決定系統適用性指標的嚴格程度
