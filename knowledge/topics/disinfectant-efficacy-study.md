# Disinfectant Efficacy Study — Comprehensive Summary Report

## 1. Purpose & Regulatory Basis

A sound cleaning and sanitization program is required for controlled environments used in the manufacture of pharmaceutical articles to prevent microbial contamination. Regulatory agencies worldwide (U.S. FDA, EMEA, Health Canada, TGA) expect manufacturers to internally qualify antimicrobial chemical agents prior to use, and this qualification is routinely reviewed during GMP inspections. `[USP <1072> Introduction; PDA TR70 §4.1–§4.2]`

The qualification testing is **distinctly different from EPA/EU registration testing** performed by the manufacturer. In-house qualification is performed at lower bioburden levels, with decreased contact times, and on actual facility surface substrates. `[PDA TR70 §3.0]`

---

## 2. Types of Antimicrobial Agents & Contact Times

Three categories of agents are qualified in a typical pharmaceutical facility. `[PDA TR70 §5.2; USP <1072> Table 2]`

| Agent Type | Target | Max Contact Time | Common Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sanitizer | Vegetative microorganisms (low level) | 30 sec – 10 min | 70% IPA, 70% ethanol |
| Disinfectant | Vegetative microorganisms (not spores) | ≤ 10 min | QAC (quaternary ammonium), phenolics |
| Sporicide | All microorganisms including spores | ≤ 10 min | Sodium hypochlorite (bleach), H₂O₂, H₂O₂/PAA mixture |

> A typical biopharmaceutical or pharmaceutical facility qualifies a total of **three agents** (one of each type). `[PDA TR70 §5.2]`

### USP Classification by Chemical Entity `[USP <1072> Table 2]`

| Chemical Entity | Classification | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Aldehydes | Sporicidal agent | 2% Glutaraldehyde |
| Alcohols | General purpose disinfectant, antiseptic | 70% IPA, 70% ethanol |
| Chlorine / sodium hypochlorite | Sporicidal agent | 0.5% Sodium hypochlorite |
| Hydrogen peroxide | Vapor-phase sterilant; liquid sporicidal agent | 10–25% solution |
| Peracetic acid | Liquid sterilant; vapor-phase sterilant | 0.2% Peracetic acid |
| Quaternary ammonium compounds | General purpose disinfectant, antiseptic | Benzalkonium chloride |
| Phenolics | General purpose disinfectant | Chlorocresol 500 µg/g |

## 3. Resistance Hierarchy of Microorganisms

The order of resistance to chemical disinfectants (most to least resistant): `[USP <1072> Table 1]`

1. Bacterial spores (*Bacillus subtilis*, *Clostridium sporogenes*)
2. Mycobacteria (*M. tuberculosis*)
3. Non-lipid-coated viruses (poliovirus, rhinovirus)
4. Fungal spores and vegetative molds/yeasts (*Trichophyton*, *Candida*)
5. Vegetative bacteria (*Pseudomonas aeruginosa*, *Staphylococcus aureus*, *Salmonella*)
6. Lipid-coated viruses (herpes simplex, hepatitis B, HIV)

## 4. Factors Affecting Disinfectant Effectiveness

The effectiveness depends on: `[USP <1072> Classification of Disinfectants]`

- Intrinsic biocidal activity of the agent
- Concentration of the disinfectant (and concentration exponent, *n*)
- Contact time
- Nature and porosity of the surface
- Water hardness used for dilution
- Amount of organic material (soil load) present
- Type and number of microorganisms present

**Concentration Exponent (*n*) Practical Implication:** `[USP <1072> Table 3]`
An agent with a high *n* (e.g., phenol: *n* = 6) loses activity rapidly upon dilution; a 3× dilution yields a 729-fold reduction in activity. Agents with low *n* (e.g., H₂O₂, hypochlorite: *n* = 0.5) are more dilution-tolerant.

## 5. Supplier & Agent Qualification

Before performing efficacy testing, the supplier and agent must be qualified. A satisfactory audit, qualification testing, and a clearly defined Certificate of Analysis (CoA) are required. `[PDA TR70 §5.0]`

**Required information for each agent:** `[PDA TR70 §5.1]`

- Description of packaging, label, and container type
- Description of ingredients and concentrations
- Lot or batch number
- Efficacy testing results
- Irradiation or other sterilization verification certification (if applicable)
- Safety data sheet (SDS)
- Disposal information

**Requalification triggers:** changes in formulation, packaging, or manufacturing site. `[PDA TR70 §5.0]`

---

## 6. Qualification Testing — Overview

Qualification testing must include **both laboratory and in-situ (in-use) testing**: `[PDA TR70 §5.1]`

1. Chemical analysis of active ingredients
2. **Microbial efficacy testing** — in-suspension and/or carrier (coupon) surface studies
3. In-use (field) testing to validate the combination of agent + operator + approved procedure

Testing should use agents **close to or beyond their stated in-use expiration date**, and should be done in **replicate on multiple lots** where applicable. `[PDA TR70 §5.1]`

## 7. Method A — In-Suspension Study

Use for: initial agent screening; demonstrating efficacy against suspended organisms in liquid systems (tanks, bioreactors, holding vessels). `[PDA TR70 §5.2.1]`

> In-suspension studies **cannot replace carrier/coupon surface studies** for determining performance on cleanroom surfaces.

### Organism Panel `[PDA TR70 §5.2.1]`
- **6–10 microorganisms** including bacteria, yeast, and mold
- **Preferred:** environmental isolates recovered from the facility
- **Acceptable fallback:** ATCC cultures representing facility isolate types (until facility isolates are obtained)
- Organisms chosen must match agent type (e.g., sporicides must include spore-forming organisms)

### Step-by-Step Procedure `[PDA TR70 §5.2.1]`

1. Prepare a fresh culture of each organism to a known CFU/mL concentration
2. Transfer a small volume of culture into a sterile preparation of the chemical agent and mix (recommended inoculum: **10³–10⁴ CFU/mL**)
3. Allow mixture to sit for the specified contact time
4. Filter the entire solution; rinse membrane three times with an appropriate neutralizing agent
5. Plate filter onto TSA (Trypticase Soy Agar) or equivalent; incubate
6. Prepare a **positive control** (replace agent with saline; follow same method; recover 10–300 CFU/plate)
7. Prepare a **negative control** (no inoculum added; no CFU expected)
8. Calculate log reduction: compare CFU in inoculum vs. CFU recovered from agent-exposed inoculum

## 8. Method B — Carrier Surface Study (Coupon Study)

**Primary validation method** — required to demonstrate efficacy on actual facility surface materials. `[PDA TR70 §5.2.2]`

### Surface Selection

Select surfaces representing **worst-case porosity or most difficult to clean**: `[PDA TR70 §5.2.2]`

- Stainless steel (critical due to proximity to manufacturing operations)
- Plastic, plastic bags, glass
- Vinyl curtains, polycarbonates
- Floor materials: terrazzo, epoxy, vinyl, laminate
- Wall materials: painted epoxy, polysubstrates

If coated surfaces (e.g., cleanroom paint, epoxy) are selected, coat them on non-linting or non-absorbent backing.

### Coupon Specifications `[PDA TR70 §5.2.2]`

- Size: **≤ 1.5 inches × 1.5 inches (38 mm × 38 mm)**
- Smaller coupons fit into standard test tubes without manipulation
- **≥ 3 replicates** per organism per condition
- Decontaminate coupons prior to use; confirm no residual antimicrobial agent remains

### Organism Panel

Same as in-suspension studies: 6–10 organisms; environmental isolates preferred. `[PDA TR70 §5.2.2]`

### Total Kill Method (Procedure) `[PDA TR70 §5.2.2]`

1. Prepare fresh culture to known CFU/mL concentration
2. Transfer **10³–10⁵ CFU** onto the carrier surface
3. Allow to dry to specified time (simulates surface bioburden)
4. Apply disinfectant to coupon surface; maintain specified contact time
5. Neutralize with validated neutralizing agent; elute surface organisms
6. Filter and plate; incubate
7. Calculate log reduction vs. positive control

## 9. Neutralizer Validation

Must be performed **before** the efficacy study. Two validations are required: `[PDA TR70 §5.2, Neutralization section]`

| Validation | Method | Acceptance |
|---|---|---|
| **Toxicity test** — confirm neutralizer does not harm organisms | Add inoculum (≤100 CFU) directly to neutralizer; plate and count | CFU recovery must be comparable to positive control (no agent present) |
| **Neutralization efficacy** — confirm neutralizer stops disinfectant activity | Follow study method; add inoculum **after** neutralization step | CFU growth must be demonstrated; no inhibition |

Common neutralizing agents for specific disinfectants are listed in PDA TR70 Table 5.2.1-1.

## 10. Acceptance Criteria

### Pharmaceutical / Biotech Industry Standard `[PDA TR70 Appendix VI]`

Pharmaceutical and biotech operations typically look for the following log reductions at **10⁴ inoculum level** on hard surfaces:

| Microorganism Category | Required Log Reduction |
|---|---|
| Vegetative bacteria on hard surfaces | **≥ 3 log** |
| Spore-forming bacteria on hard, non-porous surfaces | **≥ 2 log** |

> The specific acceptance criteria should be guided by worst-case environmental monitoring data from the facility. `[PDA TR70 Appendix VI]`

### EU EN Standard Framework `[PDA TR70 Appendix VII]`

EU tests follow a three-phase evaluation:

| Phase | Description |
|---|---|
| Phase 1 | Basic suspension test; no contact time specified; no interfering substances |
| Phase 2 Step 1 | Suspension test with defined contact time; BSA added (0.3% clean / 3.0% dirty conditions) |
| Phase 2 Step 2 | Surface/carrier tests simulating practical conditions |
| Phase 3 | Field trial (in-situ) studies |

## 11. In-Use (In-Situ) Testing

A **field study** that validates the combined effectiveness of the disinfecting agent, the trained operators, and the approved SOPs. Required in addition to laboratory studies. `[PDA TR70 §2.0 Definitions; §5.1]`

## 13. Inspection Readiness Checklist

Key components reviewed during regulatory inspections: `[PDA TR70 §4.2]`

- [ ] Qualification of suppliers and agents (CoA, audit, efficacy data)
- [ ] Documented cleaning and disinfection methodologies

- [ ] Process for sterile filtering antimicrobial agents (for aseptic areas)
- [ ] Sterilization and storage of agents and cleaning equipment
- [ ] In-use expiration dating
- [ ] Rotation program for agents
- [ ] Training and qualifications of personnel
- [ ] Documented contact times (wetted periods)
- [ ] Method for addressing chemical residuals
- [ ] Hold times for cleaned/disinfected and soiled areas
- [ ] Documentation and records
