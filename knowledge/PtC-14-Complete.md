## How can a risk assessment be created for an ATMP facility?

                

ATMP 設施風險評估如何建立？ |  Recommendation + Rationale (p10–p13)

            

        

        

            
            

                
                

                    

### Recommendation

                    

The manufacture of any product, including an ATMP, necessarily entails some degree of risk. The degree of risk often correlates with such factors as the number of manual interventions, the type of environment in which those interventions occur, and the open or closed nature of the process.

                    

For example, autologous therapies produced in small batches (small-scale) often have a higher risk per unit produced when manual manipulations are numerous. The facility design element of a formal facility risk assessment should always be included as part of the Design Review process in the organization, but it is even more important when processes have varying levels of risk.

                    

Based on the process design principles and nature of the work to be performed in the facility, the organization can define the risk profile of the facility and work to mitigate those risks through appropriate design controls associated with the equipment (e.g., isolators, BSCs) and appropriate process improvements (e.g., single-use systems, closed processing, automation, improved intervention design).

                    

As part of the same facility risk assessment, design controls can also be considered to manage risks related to biological hazards.

                

                
                

                    

## How does the ATMP risk assessment determine the biosafety level requirement?

                

ATMP 風險評估如何決定生物安全等級（BSL）需求？ |  p14

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Risk Group Classification (WHO / FDA)

                    

A list of biological materials, including GMOs, should be maintained and reviewed regularly against local requirements as part of a risk management program. The list serves as a basis for the organization to determine the risk group classification of each material, and the risk group is determined by the hazard posed by the biological agents and toxins (if any) used in the manufacturing process.

                    

Although risk group classification depends on localities, the definition is generally in line with those from the World Health Organization (WHO) and U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA):

                    

                        
- Risk Group 1 (no or low individual and community risk): A microorganism that is unlikely to cause human or animal disease.

                        
- Risk Group 2 (moderate individual risk, low community risk): A pathogen that can cause human or animal disease but is unlikely to be a serious hazard to laboratory personnel, the community, livestock, or the environment. Laboratory exposures may cause serious infection, but effective treatment and preventive measures are available, and the risk of the spread of infection is limited.

                        
- Risk Group 3 (high individual risk, low community risk): A pathogen that usually causes serious human or animal diseases but does not ordinarily spread from one infected individual to another. Effective treatment and preventive measures are available *(not considered in this document)*.

                        
- Risk Group 4 (high individual and community risk): A pathogen that usually causes serious human or animal disease and that can be readily transmitted from one individual to another, directly or indirectly. Effective treatment and preventive measures are not usually available *(not considered in this document)*.

#### 地區差異的重要性

                        

PtC-14 特別強調：「風險群組分類取決於各地（localities）」。實務上，同一種病毒載體在不同國家可能有不同的 BSL 要求：

                        

                            
- **美國**：NIH 指南 + CDC/USDA 法規

                            
- **歐盟**：EU Directive 2000/54/EC（生物因子工作者保護）

                            
- **台灣**：衛福部疾管署的病原體分類表

                            
- **日本**：厚生勞動省的感染症法規

                        

                        

CDMO 若服務全球客戶，必須以最嚴格的適用法規為基準設計設施。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：風險群組就像食品安全等級

                        

RG 1 → 一般食品（洗手即可）

                        

RG 2 → 生肉（需要砧板隔離、特定刀具、烹調至熟）

                        

RG 3 → 致病菌培養（需要專業實驗室隔離）

                        

RG 4 → 高致死性病原體（需最高等級生物安全實驗室）

                        

大部分的 ATMP 病毒載體屬於 RG 2，相當於「生肉等級」——需要謹慎操作，但有標準的防護程序。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Determining BSL Requirements & Heightened Risk Situations

                    

Upon understanding the risk group classification of the materials involved in the product, the organization should consult with the local environmental health and safety regulatory bodies and the inspecting regulatory bodies of the countries in which the product is licensed to determine the BSL requirements for the manufacturing facility.

                    

The organization can then determine the controls required to manage the risk using facility risk assessment. Typically, risk group classification and control requirements are heightened for the following situations:

                    

                        
- Novel biological materials (e.g., new gene insertion or deletion, novel virus)

                        
- Biological material that is replication competent as opposed to biological material known to be no longer capable of replication

                        
- Viral vectors and their risk to the operator and cross-contamination

                    

                    

The organization should also consider the requirements related to the use of GMOs. Many countries maintain specific facility and procedural requirements for facility-handling of GMOs, and the containment requirements are typically applied in addition to the typical pharmaceutical facility cleanliness requirements.

                    

Risk management principles should be applied when determining the control points. The risk assessment and mitigations should aim to minimize the risk to as low as is reasonably practical.

                

                

                    

### BSL 判定流程與三大加嚴情境

                    

                        

#### BSL 需求判定流程（Decision Process）

                        

```

步驟 1：建立生物物料清單
  ↓ （包含所有原料、中間物、病毒載體、廢棄物）

步驟 2：對每種物料進行風險群組分類
  ↓ （參照 WHO、FDA、當地主管機關）

步驟 3：識別加嚴情況
  ↓ 是否為新型（Novel）生物物料？
  ↓ 是否具複製能力（RCV）？
  ↓ 是否為病毒載體？
  → 若任一「是」→ 考慮提升風險等級

步驟 4：諮詢主管機關
  ↓ 環境衛生主管機關（EH&S）
  ↓ 藥品審查機關（FDA/EMA/各國）

步驟 5：確定 BSL 等級
  ↓ BSL-1 / BSL-2（PtC-14 範疇）

步驟 6：設計設施控制措施
  ↓ 設施硬體、程序、個人防護

步驟 7：定期審查（物料清單更新時）
                        
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 三大加嚴情境解析

                        

**1. 新型生物物料（Novel biological materials）**

                        

全新的基因插入或新型病毒無前例可循，無法引用已有的安全數據。此時風險群組往往暫時上調一個等級，直到累積足夠的安全數據。

                        

**2. 複製能力（Replication Competency）**

                        

RCV（Replication-Competent Virus）vs. Non-RCV 是最重要的 BSL 決定因子之一：

                        

                            
- Non-RCV（複製缺陷型）：僅能感染一次，不能在體內複製傳播，風險較低

                            
- RCV（複製型）：可在人體複製，存在傳播風險，須提高 BSL 要求

                            
- 製程中必須有 RCV 篩查（RCA assay），確認載體為 Non-RCV

                        

                        

**3. 病毒載體的雙重風險**

                        

病毒載體同時存在兩個風險維度：

                        

                            
- **操作員感染風險**：載體若攜帶治療基因，意外感染員工後可能引起非預期基因表達

                            
- **交叉污染風險**：環境中殘留的病毒載體可能污染其他批次或其他產品

                        

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### BSL-1 vs. BSL-2 — Facility Requirements Overview

                    

PtC-14 focuses on facilities operating at BSL-1 and BSL-2, which cover the majority of ATMP manufacturing operations. The following provides a comparative overview based on WHO Laboratory Biosafety Manual and EU Directive 2000/54/EC requirements:

                    

**BSL-1 (Risk Group 1 agents):**

                    

                        
- Standard microbiological practices apply

                        
- No special primary or secondary barriers required beyond standard laboratory design

                        
- Handwashing sink required

                        
- Personal protective equipment: laboratory coat, gloves, eye protection

                        
- No specific HVAC requirements beyond general ventilation

                        
- Waste decontamination not required before disposal (though good practice)

                    

                    

**BSL-2 (Risk Group 2 agents):**

                    

                        
- BSL-1 practices plus enhanced controls

                        
- Access restricted to trained, authorized personnel

                        
- Biohazard signage required on all access points

                        
- Class II Biological Safety Cabinet (BSC) required for aerosol-generating procedures

                        
- Autoclave available within facility or building

                        
- Decontamination of all infectious materials before disposal

                        
- HVAC: inward directional airflow (negative pressure) recommended

                        
- Emergency eyewash and safety shower available

                        
- Defined emergency and spill response procedures

### GMO 法規疊加與 ALARP 原則

                    

                        

#### GMO 法規疊加在 GMP 之上（雙重合規要求）

                        

這是 ATMP CDMO 必須理解的關鍵點：GMO 遏制法規與 GMP 法規是**並行且疊加**的，不能互相替代：

                        

                            
- **GMP 法規**要求：產品品質保證、無菌保證、文件完整性

                            
- **GMO 法規**要求：設施遏制等級、廢棄物去活化、環境釋放許可

                            
- 兩套要求必須同時滿足，且有時互相衝突（如前述正負壓問題）

                        

                        

監管機關對 ATMP 設施的稽查可能同時包含 GMP 稽查員和生物安全稽查員，必須對兩個框架都有答案。

                    

                    

                        

#### ALARP 原則在 ATMP 設施中的應用

                        

**ALARP = As Low As Reasonably Practicable（盡可能合理降低）**

                        

這個原則來自職業安全領域，PtC-14 將其引入 ATMP 設施風險管理：

                        

                            
- **不是**"零風險"（不可能）

                            
- **不是**最低技術可能的風險（可能成本過高）

                            
- **是**在合理成本和技術可行範圍內的最低風險

                        

                        

實務應用：當工程改善（如升級 BSL-2 至 BSL-3 設施）的成本遠超過額外的安全收益時，ALARP 允許保留在 BSL-2，但需要更強的程序控制和 PPE 補償。

                    

                    

                        

#### 生物物料清單的動態維護框架

                        

```

生物物料清單（Living Document）
├── 原材料
│   ├── 人類來源（起始細胞、血漿）→ 外來因子風險

├── 製程物質
│   ├── 病毒載體（LVV/AAV/AdV）→ RG 分類

```

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：定期審查生物物料清單

                        

就像餐廳每年更新食材供應商名單與食品安全認證——不是一次性工作，而是持續維護的活文件。當新的食材（新型病毒載體）或新的供應商（新的細胞株來源）引入時，就必須重新評估整個清單的風險。

                    

                    

                        

#### CDMO 最終情境：從零開始的 BSL 判定

                        

一個製藥公司找上你的 CDMO，要委託製造一種「新型 AAV-X」基因療法——基於全新血清型（未在 WHO 風險群組清單中），攜帶治療人類罕見代謝疾病的基因，設計為 Non-RCV。

                        

請依 Q1.2 的框架：(1) 這個載體應如何進行初步 BSL 分類？(2) 你需要諮詢哪些主管機關？(3) 設施上你需要哪些額外控制措施？(4) 生物物料清單上如何記錄這個新物質？

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Integration: Risk Assessment → BSL → Facility Design

                    

The Q1.1 and Q1.2 recommendations work together as an integrated framework. The facility risk assessment (Q1.1) identifies all risks across six categories — material, facility, multiproduct, cleaning, airflow, external environment — while the BSL determination (Q1.2) specifically addresses the biological hazard dimension that is unique to ATMP manufacturing.

                    

The integrated outcome is a Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) that simultaneously addresses:

                    

                        
- Operator safety through appropriate BSL containment design

                        
- Patient safety through aseptic processing and cross-contamination prevention

                        
- Product quality through environmental monitoring and process controls

                        
- Regulatory compliance through documented risk assessment and BSL determination

                        
- Business flexibility through scalable and multi-product capable facility design

                    

                    

This integrated approach aligns with ICH Q9 quality risk management principles, applying systematic risk identification, analysis, evaluation, and control across the full spectrum of ATMP manufacturing hazards.

#### 污染控制策略（CCS）的 ATMP 特殊元素

                        

相比一般注射劑的 CCS，ATMP 設施的 CCS 必須額外包含：

                        

                            
- **生物遏制層（Biocontainment tier）**：BSL 設施要求、BSC、隔離器

                            
- **GMO 管理層（GMO management tier）**：廢棄物去活化、環境監測（GMO 面向）、GMO 溢灑應變

                            
- **身份鏈保護層（Chain of identity tier）**：（對自體療法）從採集到輸注全程可追溯性

                            
- **操作員健康監測（Occupational health layer）**：病毒暴露後健康追蹤、疫苗狀況確認

## Q2.1 — Process Flow Design: Contamination & Cross-Contamination Prevention

                

如何設計製程流程，以降低外來汙染、交叉汙染並防止作業錯誤？ | Pages 15–18

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Recommendation — Contamination Vectors

                    

Facility design plays an essential role in minimizing contamination, cross-contamination, and prevention of errors. The possible risk factors should be evaluated and defined in the CCS (Contamination Control Strategy) document.

                    

Contamination and cross-contamination occur through several different vectors, including:

                    

**Physical**

                    

                        
1. **Contaminated packaging or containers** — Packaging formats of raw or starting materials that cannot be adequately decontaminated upon transfer.

                        
2. **Mechanical transfers** — Spillages, improperly sealed transfer devices, or risks such as the "ring of concern" — an area on a rapid transfer port (RTP) that cannot be adequately disinfected and may be exposed during transfer procedures.

                        
3. **Personnel** — Contamination from non-process-dedicated clothing regimes.

                        
4. **Waste processes.**

                        
5. **Airborne sources** — Aerosols, failure of air-handling units in the Grade A environment, or volatile constituents of contaminated waste.

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### CCS — 汙染控制策略文件

                        

**CCS（Contamination Control Strategy）**是 ATMP 設施設計的核心文件，強制要求你在設施設計階段就識別並記錄所有汙染風險。

                        

EU GMP Annex 1 規定 CCS 必須貫穿整個產品生命週期，不只是一次性評估。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：餐廳廚房分區

                        

把 ATMP 設施想像成一間米其林餐廳廚房：

                        

                            
- **生食區 = BSL 高風險區**（病毒載體生產室）

                            
- **熟食區 = 無菌填充區**（Grade A/B）

                            
- **出菜口 = MAL 物料氣鎖**

                            
- **廚師更衣室 = PAL 人員氣鎖**

                        

                        

核心原則：食材（物料）、廚師（人員）、廚餘（廢棄物）絕對不能走同一條路。

                    

                    

                        

#### "Ring of Concern" — 轉移端口的高風險區

                        

RTP（Rapid Transfer Port，快速轉移端口）連接隔離器與外界。「Ring of Concern」是端口環狀接觸面，無法被有效消毒——如果操作不當，轉移時這個區域可能直接暴露，成為汙染進入路徑。

                        

**實務對策：**使用 VPHP 艙室，或將 RTP 轉移改設計為在 MAL 內執行並加自動表面去汙。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Containment Strategy — Inside-Out Principle

                    

To minimize these risks, a containment strategy should be developed from the inside-out (from critical operations to less-critical operations) based on a risk assessment.

                    

Based on the primary protection measures, a secondary means of protection should be planned, such as the PAL (Personnel Air Lock) and MAL (Material Air Lock) pressure cascades — from production room to air lock and from air lock to corridor.

                    

Personnel, product, sample, and waste paths should be considered to avoid possible contamination and minimize cross-contamination risk.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 由內而外的防護設計邏輯

                        

「由內而外（Inside-Out）」的設計思維：

                        

                            
- **最內層**：Grade A 核心製程區（最高保護需求）

                            
- **次外層**：Grade B/C 背景環境

                            
- **氣鎖層**：PAL / MAL（隔離與緩衝）

                            
- **最外層**：走廊、支援區域

                        

                        

先確定最核心操作的風險，再向外逐層設計防護——而不是從外部環境往內設計。

                    

                    

                        

#### 四流分離矩陣 / Four-Flow Separation Matrix

                        

```

流向      進入路徑     離開路徑     是否可共用
─────────────────────────────────────────
人員流    PAL 入口     PAL 出口     禁止與物料共用
物料流    MAL 物料入   MAL 物料出   禁止與廢棄物共用
樣品流    專用容器     獨立取樣口   嚴格標識
廢棄物    廢棄物 MAL   滅活後清除   禁止與清潔物料同時通過
```

                        

規則：四流不相交。設計時畫出流程圖，確保無交叉點。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Important Considerations (1–4): Closed Process & Material Handling

                    

                        
1. Is the overall process closed throughout the whole procedure? Any process which can be performed closed should be designed and executed closed. Items required within the Grade A space should be sterilized. Intrinsic sterile connectors should be used when possible.

                        
2. How are transfers in and out of the BSC or isolator performed? Use of RTPs (Rapid Transfer Ports) or MALs with automated surface decontamination is recommended.

                        
3. Isolators or BSCs should use appropriate technologies like RTPs for transferring sterilized or disinfected material to reduce the risk of physical transfer of contaminants.

                        
4. How is the pharmaceutical product handled inside the BSC or isolator?
                            

                                
    - First-air protection for any open-handled processes.

                                
    - Greatest possible separation of personnel from the process through use of barriers and sterile implements.

                            

                        

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### First-Air Protection — 第一氣流保護原則

                        

在 BSC（生物安全櫃）或 Isolator 中，**「第一氣流」**指 HEPA 過濾後直接吹出、尚未接觸任何表面或人員的潔淨空氣。

                        

原則：開放式操作（open manipulation）必須在第一氣流正下方進行，任何物件或手部都不得遮蔽這個氣流路徑。

                        

違反此原則 = Grade A 環境保護失效。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：醫院無菌換藥

                        

護理師在換藥時，不會把手伸到無菌包上方（會遮蔽第一氣流）。ATMP 製程也一樣：操作員的手、工具、包裝材料的移動路徑都必須避免「遮蔽」從 HEPA 下來的第一道氣流。

                    

                    

                        

#### Intrinsic Sterile Connectors 的重要性

                        

每一次無菌連接（aseptic connection）都是汙染機會。**固有無菌連接器（intrinsic sterile connectors）**讓連接動作本身維持無菌狀態，減少暴露風險。

                        

應盡量減少不同管路的無菌連接次數——每增加一個連接點，就增加一個 CCI（容器密封完整性）失效風險。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Important Considerations (5–9): Packaging, Waste, Spills

                    

                        
1. Can the packaging material be externally contaminated before leaving the isolator? Use VPHP (Vapor Phase Hydrogen Peroxide) or other appropriate decontamination air locks, or RTPs, to transfer material to a safe area.

                        
2. What are the methods to manage pharmaceutical product residues or waste?
                            

                                
    - Waste should be handled in a contained manner through a separate pathway from product or product samples.

                                
    - If the product exit is not separate from waste exit, removal-of-waste should be performed at a dedicated time separate from product manufacture.

                            

                        

                        
3. How will the processing area be cleaned to eliminate cross-contamination risk? Any inactivation step of residual pharmaceutical product should be performed before cleaning.

                        
4. Are single-use closed systems used? Contamination can occur at interfaces (e.g., single-use bioreactors, single-use bags) during additions, sampling, or dismantling. Tightness/integrity should be tested and validated.

                        
5. How will process leaks and spills be handled?
                            

                                
    - Containment measures to stop and prevent spread.

                                
    - Inactivation/decontamination steps to protect product and packaging.

                                
    - Procedures for gowning contaminated by spills.

                                
    - Deviation and investigation procedures.

                            

                        

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### VPHP — 氣態過氧化氫去汙

                        

**VPHP（Vapor Phase Hydrogen Peroxide）**是目前 ATMP 設施最常用的整室去汙方式。其優點：

                        

                            
- 可對整個房間或氣鎖進行 6-log 滅活

                            
- 能滲透角落、縫隙等難以擦拭的區域

                            
- 不留殘留物（分解成水和氧）

                        

                        

設施設計時必須確認材質相容性（金屬、塑膠、電子設備的耐 VPHP 性）。

                    

                    

                        

#### 先滅活，再清潔 — 必須牢記的順序

                        

**錯誤順序：**先清潔 → 再滅活（清潔已將病毒擴散到更大面積）

                        

**正確順序：**先滅活（原位灭活病毒）→ 再清潔（移除殘留物）

                        

這個順序對 ATMP（特別是病毒載體產品）尤其關鍵，一旦弄錯可能造成整個設施的生物汙染事件。

                    

                    

                        

### Warehouse & Kitting Requirements

                    

To reduce the risk of intrinsic particulate contamination and maintain low mold in support areas, kitting of consumables should be performed in, at minimum, an ISO-8 cleanroom or controlled non-classified area to prevent introduction of particulate matter from the warehouse.

                    

Warehouses should be maintained free of cardboard and paperboard by transferring materials into totes prior to storage. This lower-particulate warehouse for materials destined for Grade B cell-processing suites should be separated from the main warehouse where non-product-contact items are stored, ideally with air-wash curtains.

                    

**Rationale:** An appropriate closed process, with a cleaning and inactivation strategy, minimizes the chance of extraneous contamination and cross-contamination. Well-documented procedures and training also contribute to prevention.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### Kitting — 為什麼倉庫也需要潔淨控制？

                        

**Kitting**（備料整備）指在物料進入高等級潔淨室前，先在較低等級的受控環境中進行分裝、去除外包裝等準備工作。

                        

紙箱（cardboard）是黴菌孢子和微粒汙染的主要來源。如果紙箱直接進入倉庫，每次取料動作都可能揚起大量微粒，最終進入生產區域。

                    

                    

                        

#### Air-Wash Curtain — 氣簾的功能

                        

Air-Wash Curtain（氣簾）在倉庫分區界面產生連續氣流屏障：

                        

                            
- 阻擋外部微粒和孢子進入低微粒倉庫區

                            
- 不需要實體隔牆，方便推車和叉車通行

                            
- 搭配物料「去紙箱→裝入 tote（儲物桶）」程序共同使用

                        

                        

這是一個低成本、高效益的汙染控制措施，在 CDMO 多品種生產環境中尤其重要。

                    

                    

                        

## Q2.2 — Open vs. Closed Systems: Product & Operator Protection

                

開放式與密閉式系統的選擇：如何實現產品保護與操作員保護的雙重目標？ | Pages 18–19

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Open System

                    

An open system is one where the material is exposed to the manufacturing environment for even a short time. Due to this exposure, the environment is required to be of Grade A cleanliness.

                    

The manufacturing environment is a total sum of events that occur during the process — any particulate, bioburden, pyrogenic, or viral contamination present in the area has the potential to contaminate the product. This is why environmental controls and monitoring, operator movement, effectiveness of material transfers, and sanitization steps are of key importance.

                    

Open systems are often used for early-phase/proof-of-concept trials when the required manipulations are still being defined and refined.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 開放系統的三層含義

                        

**開放系統（Open System）**不只是指「蓋子打開的容器」，更廣泛指：

                        

                            
- 產品/物料在製程任何步驟中暴露於周圍環境

                            
- 暴露時間可以非常短（即使只有幾秒）

                            
- 一旦暴露，環境中的任何汙染都可能進入產品

                        

                        

要求：開放操作必須在 Grade A 環境下執行（BSC 下方第一氣流區或 Isolator 內部）。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：戶外開刀 vs. 手術室

                        

開放系統 = 在醫院走廊做傷口換藥（風險高，環境不可控）。Grade A BSC = 在正壓手術室換藥（環境已被控制到最低風險）。閉合系統 = 用預充填注射器給藥（完全不暴露環境，最安全）。

                    

                    

                        

#### 早期開發與商業化的系統選擇

                        

開放系統多用於**早期/概念驗證（PoC）試驗**，原因是開發初期操作步驟尚未定型、需要靈活調整。但進入商業化生產時，應盡量將所有可關閉的步驟改為密閉系統，以降低批次失敗風險。

                        

這是 CDMO 承接早期客戶時需要特別告知的技術升級路徑。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Closed System

                    

A closed system is hermetically sealed from the manufacturing environment, inside which the product pathway is considered sterile. Examples include single-use systems that have been gamma-irradiated using intrinsic sterile connectors.

                    

Systems are only considered closed when they have been integrity-tested and demonstrated to be hermetically sealed. Fully closed systems can be deployed in a Grade C environment if adequate control measures are implemented.

                    

### Hybrid System

                    

Hybrid processes — such as those requiring an open addition into a closed system, or that are open during initial assembly but closed thereafter — are required to be performed within:

                    

                        
- Grade A cleanroom with Grade B background when using a BSC

                        
- Grade D background when using a closed isolator (depending on CCS requirements)

                    

                    

The increased cleanliness requirement is due to the fact that hybrid processes may create the opportunity for exposure of the product pathway to the environment.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 三種系統類型與環境等級要求對照

                        
                            
                                
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                
| 系統類型 | 背景環境等級 | 操作設備 | 主要風險 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
                            
                            
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                
| 開放系統 | Grade A (with Grade B background) | BSC | 直接暴露環境汙染 |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                
| 混合系統 | Grade A + Grade B（BSC）或 Grade D（Isolator） | BSC 或 Closed Isolator | 開放/密閉介面時的短暫暴露 |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                
| 密閉系統 | Grade C（需有充分控制措施） | 密閉隔離器或 SUS | 介面洩漏、完整性失效 |
                            
                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 密閉性驗證 — Integrity Testing 是前提

                        

一個系統不能「自稱」是密閉的，必須：

                        

                            
- 通過完整性測試（壓力測試、染料侵入試驗等）

                            
- 證明在操作條件下維持密封性

                            
- 供應商認證 + 必要時進行內部測試

                        

                        

只有通過測試、被「證明」密封的系統，才能適用 Grade C 環境的較低要求。

                    

                    

                        

#### 系統選擇決策樹 / Decision Tree

                        

```

製程步驟有物料暴露嗎？
├── 否（全程密閉）→ 密閉系統 → 至少 Grade C
├── 部分（組裝/添加時開放）→ 混合系統
│   ├── 使用 BSC → Grade A + Grade B 背景
│   └── 使用 Closed Isolator → Grade D 背景
└── 是（製程中持續開放）→ 開放系統 → Grade A BSC + Grade B 背景
```

                    

                    

                        

## Q2.3 — Pressure Cascade Concepts: Containment & Classification

                

正壓、負壓、氣泡與水槽壓力概念：ATMP 設施如何同時保護操作員與產品？ | Pages 19–21

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Regulatory Background

                    

The leading regulatory standards for aseptic manufacturing include:

                    

                        
- **FDA Guidance for Industry:** Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing — cGMP

                        
- **EU GMP Annex 1:** Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products (EudraLex Volume 4)

                    

                    

These guidances state that pressure differentials for supporting clean areas must be maintained to keep sterile processing areas free from any contaminant (viable or nonviable).

                    

EU GMP Annex 1 states that a positive pressure differential of a minimum of 10 Pascals should be maintained between each adjacent space.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 壓差梯度的基本原理

                        

潔淨室使用**正壓**的邏輯非常直觀：

                        

                            
- 高壓區的空氣只會向外流

                            
- 外部「不潔淨」的空氣無法逆流進入

                            
- 因此，從最潔淨到最不潔淨形成「壓力階梯」

                        

                        

EU GMP Annex 1 要求相鄰空間的最小壓差為 **10 Pa**。實務上通常設計為 10–15 Pa 以提供安全餘裕。

                    

                    

                        

#### ATMP 特殊挑戰：正壓 vs. BSL 負壓的矛盾

                        

傳統無菌製程 = **正壓保護產品**（潔淨空氣向外吹）

                        

BSL-2 病毒載體生產 = **負壓保護環境/操作員**（含病毒氣溶膠往內抽）

                        

**矛盾：**你不能同時對同一個房間既正壓又負壓。

                        

**解法：**使用「Bubble + Sink 組合設計」——見下方 formula-box。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Pressure Concepts: Bubble, Sink, and Cascade

                    

Cleanrooms are designed to maintain positive pressure, ensuring filtered air always flows from the cleanest to less-clean spaces — a pressure cascade.

                    

In a multichambered cleanroom, the cleanest room is kept at the highest pressure. Air locks can act as a "bubble" (higher pressure than both adjacent rooms) or as a "sink" (lower pressure than both adjacent rooms).

                    

For potent products or viral vectors, the pressure concept should be adapted to prevent spreading into surrounding areas using negative and positive adjacent areas.

                    

The BSC is an open system; therefore the pressure differential cannot be regulated in the same way as an isolator. Instead, the surrounding environment is controlled with respect to the air locks and corridors.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### Bubble vs. Sink 氣鎖設計圖解

                        

```

【Bubble 氣泡式氣鎖】— 適用於多產品設施防交叉汙染
  走廊 (低壓) ← 氣鎖 (最高壓) → 生產室 (中壓)
  空氣從氣鎖向兩側流出，阻止任何方向的汙染進入氣鎖

【Sink 水槽式氣鎖】— 適用於高效力或病毒性產品的圍堵
  走廊 (中壓) → 氣鎖 (最低壓) ← 生產室 (高效力產品區)
  空氣從兩側流向氣鎖，防止生產室內的高效力物質溢出

【BSC 的壓力配置】— BSC 本身是開放系統，無法直接加壓
  BSC 周圍 Grade B 房間（正壓）
  ↑ BSC 層流頭（向下正壓氣流）
  → 廢氣經 HEPA 過濾後排出（BSC 下排風可設計為負壓抽排）
```

                        

決策原則：**高效力/病毒性產品 → 優先用 Sink 防止外洩；多產品設施防交叉汙染 → 優先用 Bubble**

                    

                    

                        

#### BSC 壓力管理的特殊性

                        

生物安全櫃（BSC）因為是「開放」接口的，無法像隔離器一樣在內部設定壓差。BSC 的保護機制依賴：

                        

                            
- **向下氣流（Downflow）**：HEPA 過濾空氣向下吹，在工作區形成正壓層

                            
- **前方進氣口（Inflow）**：負壓抽取前方空氣，阻止氣溶膠逸出

                            
- **周圍房間正壓**：BSC 外的 Grade B 環境提供額外保護層

                        

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### MAL/PAL Design Requirements & Suite Fumigation

                    

MALs and PALs should be designed with interlocks that prohibit opening both doors at once. They should also have an integrated timer that enforces the required time to ensure at least 4–5 room air-changes occur within the MAL to reduce particles introduced into the higher-classified side.

                    

Cleanroom suites should be designed for VPHP (Vapor Phase Hydrogen Peroxide) or ClO₂ (Chlorine Dioxide) compatibility. For product changeover (different vectors) and for RCV (Replication-Competent Virus) to non-RCV, whole-suite fumigation should be carried out periodically.

                    

RCV is a crucial safety concern in gene therapy. Vectors are engineered to be replication-defective, but there is a risk that the virus could revert to a RCV during manufacturing due to recombination events. If this happens, the RCV could replicate uncontrollably in the patient's body.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 氣鎖聯鎖與換氣次數要求

                        

**聯鎖（Interlock）**確保氣鎖兩側門不能同時打開——這是維持壓差的物理保障。

                        

**4–5 次換氣（Air Changes）**的計時要求：空氣需要足夠的時間置換，才能把從低等級端帶入的微粒稀釋到可接受水平，然後才能打開通往高等級端的門。

                        

這個設計要求必須在 MAL 設備規格中明確，並在驗證時確認計時器功能。

                    

                    

                        

#### RCV — 基因治療最嚴重的安全風險之一

                        

**RCV（Replication-Competent Virus，複製能力病毒）**是基因治療領域的關鍵安全議題：

                        

                            
- 載體病毒被設計為「複製缺陷型」（只能感染一次，不能繁殖）

                            
- 但在製造過程中，基因重組（recombination）可能使其恢復複製能力

                            
- 如果含 RCV 的產品注射到病人體內，病毒可能在體內無限複製，造成嚴重傷害

                        

                        

**設施含義：**換產品（不同病毒載體）前，必須進行整室燻蒸（whole-suite fumigation），以確保沒有 RCV 殘留。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：RCV vs. 複製缺陷型病毒

                        

想像一支 USB 隨身碟（病毒載體）被設計成「唯讀模式」——只能把基因藥物「複製」到細胞中，但自己不能「複製自身」。RCV 就像 USB 的唯讀鎖被解除了，變成一個會自我複製的病毒——這就是為什麼必須嚴格監控並通過整室燻蒸清除每一批產品的殘留。

                    

                    

                        

## Q2.4 — Cleanroom, HVAC, Air Locks & Corridors for Viral Vector Handling

                

病毒載體 ATMP 設施的硬體設計：潔淨室、HVAC、氣鎖與走廊的完整考量 | Pages 22–24

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Design Principles

                    

When designing a production site for ATMPs, essential design considerations include:

                    

                        
- Fulfilling a minimum of Grade D background (closed isolator) or Grade B background (BSC) with appropriate graded air locks.

                        
- Designing all cleanrooms to be easily cleaned and disinfected; as some ATMPs require BSL-1 or higher, rooms should accommodate specific cleaning/inactivation procedures required by the specific BSL.

                        
- Designing all equipment and room features to be hygienic and chemically compatible with cleaning, sanitizing, and inactivation agents used. Chemical compatibility should be considered early in the design stage.

                        
- Installing appropriate decontamination/deactivation methods (e.g., autoclaves) based on the required BSL, to deactivate/decontaminate waste or leftover raw materials/products before leaving the area.

                        
- Consider locating emergency response equipment (e.g., emergency shower, eye shower) outside the cleanroom, while ensuring there are no open drains within classified environments.

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 化學相容性 — 設計初期就要考慮

                        

ATMP 設施使用的消毒/去汙劑種類繁多（VPHP、ClO₂、次氯酸鈉、IPA 等），每種對材料的腐蝕性不同。在設計初期就必須確認：

                        

                            
- 牆面塗料、地板材質、密封條的耐化學性

                            
- 設備表面（不鏽鋼等級、塗層）

                            
- 電子元件、感測器的防護等級

                            
- 管道接頭和密封材質

                        

                        

如果設計時忽略，事後改造成本極高，且可能影響設施認證時間表。

                    

                    

                        

#### 潔淨室內禁止開放排水

                        

「無開放排水口（no open drains）」在 GMP 潔淨室中是基本要求：

                        

                            
- 開放排水口是微生物（特別是黴菌）滋生的溫床

                            
- 在 BSL-2 區域，排水可能含有病毒性廢液，必須先灭活

                            
- 緊急淋浴/洗眼站必須設置在潔淨室外，但位置需確保人員能快速到達

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：醫院手術室的設計邏輯

                        

ATMP 潔淨室的設計要求與醫院手術室非常相似：

                        

                            
- **易清潔表面**（圓角設計，沒有縫隙積塵）= 無菌手術台

                            
- **高效空氣過濾**（HEPA）= 手術室的層流頂棚

                            
- **無排水口** = 手術室地板防積水防污染

                            
- **緊急洗眼站在室外** = 急救設備不得影響無菌環境

                        

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### HVAC Requirements

                    

                        
- Design the HVAC system appropriate for the product potency and/or the required BSL.

                        
- Use HEPA filtration to obtain the required cleanroom classification.

                        
- Design the air supply for each space to promote appropriate air pressure differentials between rooms of varying classification or required operations (for maintenance of cleanliness or containment).

                        
- Design HEPA filter installation and replacement so they can be easily maintained in a way that minimizes cross-contamination and permits periodic integrity testing.

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### HVAC 設計的三個關鍵維度

                        
                            
                                
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                
| 維度 | 一般無菌設施 | ATMP/病毒載體設施 |
| --- | --- | --- |
                            
                            
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 過濾等級 | HEPA H14（Grade A/B） | 同上，且需雙層 HEPA（BSL-2 區域排氣） |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 壓差方向 | 正壓（保護產品） | 依 BSL 需求可能需負壓或 Bubble/Sink 組合 |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 換氣次數 | Grade B: 20–40 ACH；Grade A: 單向流 | BSL-2：需滿足 BSL 換氣要求，通常 ≥10 ACH |
                            
                        

                    

                    

                        

#### HEPA 過濾器的完整性測試

                        

HEPA 過濾器需定期進行 **DOP/PAO 完整性測試**（Dispersed Oil Particulate 或 Polyalphaolefin 測試），確認沒有洩漏點。

                        

在 ATMP（特別是 BSL-2）設施中，HEPA 過濾器的更換和測試是高風險操作：操作人員可能接觸到含有活性病毒的過濾器。設計時必須考慮「Bag-in/Bag-out」(BIBO) 過濾器更換機制，讓過濾器可以在不暴露操作人員的前提下完成更換。

                    

                    

                        

### Air Lock Requirements

                    

                        
- Position air locks to separate spaces of different classifications and/or avoid spreading contaminants in adjacent areas.

                        
- Design PALs and changing rooms so that exiting operators do not contaminate entering operators — providing separate routes of entry and exit.

                        
- Separate MALs from PALs.

                        
- Design MALs to allow for decontamination procedures within their environment, permitting the movement of materials from a lower grade to the higher grade.

                        
- Restrict personnel from crossing through a MAL to enter the processing cleanrooms. Instead, design a procedure for personnel to deliver material to one side of the air lock and, after decontamination, retrieve it from the other side.

                        
- Allow waste materials to pass through MALs/PALs only when clean materials or personnel are not present.

                        
- Separate waste flow from personnel and material based on potency and BSL requirements.

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### PAL vs. MAL 設計要點對比

                        
                            
                                
                                    
                                    
                                    
                                
| 項目 | PAL 人員氣鎖 | MAL 物料氣鎖 |
| --- | --- | --- |
                            
                            
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 主要功能 | 人員更衣/潔淨度轉換 | 物料表面消毒/轉移 |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 人員通行 | 允許 | 禁止（人員不得穿越 MAL） |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                
| 是否可合併 | 禁止合併，必須分開設置 |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 進出方向 | 進出必須分開（防止出方污染入方） | 單向物料流或雙門聯鎖 |
                                
                                    ****
                                    
                                    
                                
| 廢棄物 | 不得與人員同時通過 | 不得與清潔物料同時通過 |
                            
                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 人員不得穿越 MAL — 為什麼這個規則如此重要？

                        

MAL 是為物料表面消毒設計的，其內部會進行噴霧消毒、VPHP 處理等程序。如果人員穿越 MAL：

                        

                            
- 人員可能暴露於消毒劑

                            
- 人員的服裝可能帶入生物汙染

                            
- 消毒程序被人員通行干擾而失效

                        

                        

正確做法：人員把物料放在 MAL 一側，自行離開，讓 MAL 完成消毒，然後由另一側的人員（或同一人繞道通過 PAL）取出物料。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：核電廠的物料傳遞盒

                        

核電廠管制區外的人員需要將物品傳遞進去時，使用「通道箱」：物品放進去，外門關閉，輻射消毒，內門開啟，管制區內人員取出。人員本身絕不穿越這個通道箱。ATMP 設施的 MAL 邏輯完全一樣。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Corridor Design

                    

                        
- Plot personnel, material, samples, and waste flows throughout the facility to ensure corridors promote unidirectional flow where possible (cleanest to "dirtiest") and avoid risks from intersecting flow paths.

                        
- Design the facility to employ clean corridors with positive pressure from the corridor to the MAL and PAL, or MAL and PAL with positive pressure to the corridor (air locks as bubbles), if the product is highly potent or if multiproduct manufacture will occur.

                        
- Use air locks as sinks when containment of potent products is the primary concern, based on the CCS.

                    

                    

**Rationale:** The design of ATMP facilities requires careful understanding of all personnel, material, waste, and sample flows. The adjacent rooms, corridors, air locks, and HVAC all play a part in ensuring product is free from contamination and providing operator safety. Additional safety requirements should be based on risk assessment and captured within the CCS.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 走廊壓力設計選擇框架

                        

```

【情境 A】多產品設施，防止產品間交叉汙染
  走廊（高壓）→ MAL（正壓氣泡）→ 各生產室
  走廊保持最高壓，任何產品的洩漏都被走廊正壓推回生產室

【情境 B】高效力/病毒性產品，防止外洩到環境
  走廊（中壓）→ MAL（負壓水槽）← 生產室（高效力，低壓）
  生產室維持相對低壓，任何洩漏都被吸入 MAL，不向走廊散佈

【情境 C】BSC 設施的典型配置
  走廊（中壓 15 Pa）→ PAL/MAL（+10 Pa）→ Grade B 生產室（+5 Pa）
  BSC 本身層流頭維持 Grade A 環境
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 單向流走廊設計原則

                        

理想的走廊設計應避免流程「交叉」：

                        

                            
- 清潔物料進入方向 ≠ 廢棄物出去方向

                            
- 已更衣人員走廊 ≠ 去汙人員走廊

                            
- 如無法完全分開，至少設計時間隔離（不同時段通行）

                        

                        

設施設計審查時，繪製「流向衝突矩陣」是識別潛在問題的實用工具。

                    

                    

                        

## Q2.5 — External Environmental Risks: GMO & Highly Potent Materials

                

製造設施以外的環境風險：鄰近設施、醫院、居家環境中的 GMO 與高效力物質管控 | Page 24–25

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Recommendation — External Environmental Risks

                    

Highly potent materials and GMOs are considered major environmental risk factors in facility design since there are often additional local regulatory requirements to prevent unwanted release to the environment.

                    

The organization should:

                    

                        
- Consult local regulatory requirements, including assessing all biological materials handled in the facility against such regulations, including GMOs where appropriate.

                        
- Work with the point-of-administration facility (e.g., hospital) to determine any containment control requirements (e.g., administration precautions, patient waste, hospital waste).

                        
- Engage in a discussion with local regulatory authorities — a discussion is recommended since ATMP technology is developing rapidly, and novel technology may result in novel products with novel risks.

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### GMO 法規框架 — 製造商的責任範圍延伸至院外

                        

**GMO（Genetically Modified Organisms，基因改造生物）**在許多國家受到專門的環境法規管制，不只是醫藥法規：

                        

                            
- **台灣**：受《基因改造生物田間試驗管理辦法》及環保署相關規範管轄

                            
- **EU**：受 Directive 2001/18/EC（基因改造生物蓄意釋放）管制

                            
- **美國**：NIH Guidelines for Research Involving Recombinant DNA Molecules

                        

                        

製造商需在設施設計階段就識別哪些物料屬於 GMO，並確認相應法規要求。

                    

                    

                        

#### 與醫院合作的必要性

                        

ATMP 的「產品生命週期」不在工廠結束，而是延伸到：

                        

                            
- **醫院給藥室**：解凍、準備、給藥操作的人員防護

                            
- **病房**：給藥後 30 天的病患廢棄物處理

                            
- **居家**：病患出院後家人的接觸預防措施

                        

                        

製造商有責任提供醫院和病患家屬充分的操作指引，這不只是服務，而是法規合規要求。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Healthcare Setting & Home Environment

                    

Research laboratories manage biosafety risk through the oversight of an Institutional Biosafety Committee (IBC). The use of ATMPs with biosafety concerns in healthcare settings has resulted in the extension of the IBC's role into patient administration settings, which is not within the traditional purview of conventional IBCs.

                    

For gene therapy products in healthcare facilities:

                    

                        
- A contact precaution over a 30-day period post-product administration, including waste handling, is recommended.

                        
- When the patient has returned home, families are recommended to take similar precautions for the same duration.

                        
- Disinfection and cleaning procedures, including relevant disinfectant contact time, must be shown appropriate for the organisms involved within the post-administration setting (e.g., the home).

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### IBC — 機構生物安全委員會的角色延伸

                        

**IBC（Institutional Biosafety Committee）**傳統上負責研究實驗室的生物安全監督。但 ATMP 進入臨床後，其角色已延伸到：

                        

                            
- 醫院給藥環境的生物安全評估

                            
- 病患術後廢棄物（尿液、糞便、血液）的處理規範

                            
- 給藥 30 天內的醫療人員個人防護規範

                        

                        

這對許多醫院而言是全新挑戰——傳統 IBC 不熟悉基因治療產品的風險。ATMP 製造商需主動提供支持文件。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：放射性藥物的居家管控類比

                        

核醫科使用的放射性藥物有標準的「居家輻射防護指引」——病患治療後 X 天內：不能與嬰幼兒同睡、廁所使用後需沖水兩次等。

                        

ATMP 的「30 天接觸預防措施」概念類似，但現階段執行框架仍在發展中。ATMP 製造商必須主動填補這個指引空缺。

                    

                    

                        

#### 消毒劑接觸時間 — 家庭環境的挑戰

                        

在醫院可以強制執行標準消毒程序，但病患返家後，消毒劑和接觸時間的執行完全依賴病患/家屬的理解和配合：

                        

                            
- 選用的消毒劑必須是家庭可購得的普通商品

                            
- 接觸時間需足夠殺滅目標病毒，但也不能太複雜以至於家屬無法執行

                            
- 說明書必須以清楚的非專業語言呈現

                        

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Healthcare Personnel Training Challenges

                    

One particular risk in healthcare settings is related to handling competency. Healthcare settings tend to include:

                    

                        
- Use of floating staff where the personnel assigned are not always involved in one particular activity.

                        
- Training management for healthcare personnel (e.g., physician, nurse) is managed differently than for production workers. Use of a Learning Management System (LMS) may not be common in a healthcare setting, making tracking specific training competency difficult.

                    

                    

**Rationale:** Safety concerns for highly potent materials or GMOs must be addressed regardless of the setting, to protect patients, healthcare workers, home caregivers, and others who may come into contact with the patient. The evolving nature of ATMPs means that the systems designed to control these extended risks are not fully mature. Therefore, it is incumbent upon the manufacturer to provide appropriate guidance for the necessary controls.

                

                

                    

### 中文解析

                    

                        

#### 浮動人員制度的訓練風險

                        

醫院廣泛使用「浮動（floating）」制度，讓護理師根據需求分配到不同病房。這對 ATMP 給藥管理造成特殊挑戰：

                        

                            
- ATMP 給藥後的接觸預防知識不是每位護理師的常規訓練

                            
- 無 LMS 系統難以追蹤誰受過訓練

                            
- 換班時的交接可能導致關鍵防護措施被遺漏

                        

                        

這是 ATMP 上市後（post-marketing）設施風險的一部分，製造商和醫院必須共同解決。

                    

                    

                        

#### 製造商的法規責任延伸

                        

PtC-14 的最後一句話意義深遠：

                        

*"it is incumbent upon the manufacturer to provide appropriate guidance for the necessary controls."*

                        

**製造商必須主動提供**給醫院和居家環境的操控指引，這不是可選的服務，而是 ATMP 監管合規的組成部分。設施設計時就應將這個「院外風險管理」納入整體 CCS 文件架構。

#### 決策優先層級在 Topic 2 中的體現

                        

回顧本頁頂部的決策優先層級，在設施設計中每個層級的具體體現：

                        

                            
- **1. 操作員安全（BSL）**：負壓設計（Sink）、BSC 下排氣、BIBO 過濾器更換、Spill SOP

                            
- **2. 病患安全（無菌/交叉汙染）**：四流分離、MAL/PAL 設計、整室燻蒸、RCV 管控

                            
- **3. 產品品質**：First-air protection、密閉系統優先、HVAC 完整性、ISO 等級維持

                            
- **4. 法規合規**：CCS 文件化、EU Annex 1 壓差要求、GMO 法規、IBC 監督

                            
- **5. 商業彈性**：多套件設計、可切換的 Bubble/Sink 壓力配置、混合系統支持早期客戶

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### Topic 2 的核心洞察

                        

ATMP 設施設計與傳統無菌設施最大的差異在於：**雙向防護的矛盾**——傳統設施只需要保護產品（向外吹），ATMP 設施同時需要保護操作員（向內抽）。

                        

解決這個矛盾的工具是：Isolator（密閉隔離器）、Bubble/Sink 壓力配置組合、以及 CCS 文件框架——這三者構成 ATMP 設施設計的核心。

## Are there any specific requirements for designing utilities in an ATMP facility?

                

PDA PtC-14, p25–28 | Topic 3: Utilities — General Principles

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

                    

There are no specific design requirements for utilities used for the manufacture of ATMPs compared to traditional small-molecule or large-molecule manufacture. However, specific requirements pertaining to containment and BSL facilities may apply according to local and international regulations.

                    

EU GMP Annex 1 and other suggested readings provide recommendations of utilities for product contact. Although the design requirements are generally consistent with other types of manufacture, the following good principles apply to clean utilities:

                    

                        "The design requirements are generally consistent with other types of manufacture — but containment and BSL requirements add a critical overlay."
                          
  
— PDA PtC-14, Topic 3 Preamble
                    

                    

The six clean utility systems addressed in Q3.1 are:

                    

                        
1. Gaseous disinfection system

                        
2. Carbon dioxide (CO₂) generation and distribution

                        
3. Purified water generation and distribution

                        
4. Water for Injection (WFI) generation and distribution

                        
5. Liquid nitrogen (LN₂) generation and distribution

                        
6. Nitrogen (N₂) distribution

                    

                

                
                

                    

### 解說：為什麼 ATMP 公用系統設計有其獨特性？

                    

                        

#### 核心原則：基礎相同，但加了一層 BSL 防護

                        

PDA PtC-14 明確指出：ATMP 的公用系統設計**沒有全新的特殊要求**，其 GMP 基礎與傳統小分子、大分子製藥一致。

                        

差異在於：ATMP 涉及活細胞、病毒載體、GMO，因此**生物安全等級（BSL）**要求在所有公用系統設計上疊加了一層「圍堵邏輯」。

                        

                            
- 傳統藥廠：公用系統主要服務產品品質與無菌保障

                            
- ATMP 廠房：公用系統**同時**必須維持 BSL 圍堵完整性

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：醫院隔離病房的通風系統

                        

一般病房與隔離病房（如 COVID-19 負壓病房）都需要暖通空調（HVAC）。但隔離病房的 HVAC 多了一項核心要求：**負壓設計確保污染物不外洩**。

                        

ATMP 廠房的公用系統邏輯完全相同——基礎 GMP 規範不變，BSL 圍堵要求是額外疊加的防護層。

                    

                    

                        

#### 決策優先序：公用系統穿牆穿板的核心原則

                        

每一條管線、每一個感測器、每一個電源插座穿過潔淨室牆壁或 BSL 圍堵邊界時，都必須先問：

                        

**#1 此穿透點是否維持了 BSL 圍堵完整性？**（操作人員安全）

                        

如果不能，設計必須修改，無論成本為何。

                    

                    

                        

#### 思考題 1

                        

一家傳統 mAb 廠要轉型生產 CAR-T 細胞療法。工廠主任說：「我們的 WFI 和 Clean Steam 系統已符合 EU GMP Annex 1，應該不用改了。」

                        

這個說法哪裡正確？哪裡可能有遺漏？（提示：BSL 邊界的管線穿透點是否已評估？）

## Gaseous Disinfection System

                

PDA PtC-14, p25 | Risk-based commissioning, airtightness validation, residual removal

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

### Gaseous Disinfection — Key Requirements

                    

**a.** A risk-based commissioning approach for gaseous disinfection systems should be applied.

                    

**b.** Airtightness of the space needs to be demonstrated if gaseous disinfection is designed into an ATMP facility to decontaminate the facility/rooms. All surfaces and/or penetrations (e.g., sensors, electrical sockets) and openings that cannot be sealed in routine operation (e.g., the gap around the door, leak-tight isolation dampers in the HVAC duct) should be adequately sealed to prevent the escape of the decontaminating agent(s).

                    

**c.** The performance of the gaseous-disinfection system should be demonstrated through validation and appropriate Environmental Monitoring Performance Qualification.

                    

**d.** The residual level of disinfectant post-disinfection process should be validated using trace-limit-detection methods to demonstrate that the residuals are removed to levels that will have no adverse impact on the manufactured pharmaceutical product.

                    

**e.** Pipes should be marked with gas identity signs and should indicate the flow direction.

                

                
                

                    

### 氣態去污系統 — ATMP 廠房關鍵解析

                    

                        

#### 什麼是氣態去污系統？

                        

氣態去污（Gaseous Disinfection / Biodecontamination）是指使用氣態或蒸氣形式的殺菌劑對封閉空間進行滅菌/去污，最常見的試劑為：

                        

                            
- **VHP（Vaporized Hydrogen Peroxide，汽化過氧化氫）**：最廣泛使用

                            
- **氯二氧化物（ClO₂）**：適用於大空間

                            
- **甲醛氣體**：已逐漸被淘汰（毒性考量）

                        

                        

在 ATMP 廠房，氣態去污特別重要，因為處理活病毒或 GMO 後，**整個房間**可能需要徹底去污，而非僅表面清潔。

                    

                    

                        

#### 關鍵警告：氣密性（Airtightness）是 BSL 圍堵的命脈

                        

要點 (b) 特別強調：所有穿透點——包括感測器、電源插座、門縫、HVAC 隔離閘門——都必須在氣態去污期間被有效密封。

                        

這直接對應決策優先序 **#1（操作人員安全）**：若 VHP 或 ClO₂ 外洩到走廊，等於 BSL 圍堵失效，操作人員面臨雙重風險（化學品暴露 + 生物材料外洩）。

                    

                    

                        

#### 氣態去污驗證的三個關鍵量化指標

                        

```

1. 氣密性測試（Airtightness / Room Integrity Test）
   - 通常使用壓力衰減法（Pressure Decay Test）
   - 驗收標準：典型值 < 0.5%/hr 壓力衰減（視試劑種類而定）
   - BSL 2+ 設施可能要求更嚴格的標準

2. 去污效果確認（Biodecontamination Efficacy）
   - 使用生物指示劑（BI），通常為枯草桿菌芽孢（Geobacillus stearothermophilus）
   - 目標：≥ 6-log reduction（D-value × 週期次數）

3. 殘留量驗證（Residual Level Validation）
   - VHP 殘留量分析（trace-limit-detection method）
   - 驗收標準：對產品無不良影響的殘留濃度
   - 通常 < 1 ppm VHP（視產品敏感度而定）
                        
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：熱氣球充氣前的氣密性檢查

                        

氣態去污就像在氣密空間裡充入有殺菌效果的「氣體」。就像熱氣球充氣前需要確認無破洞，ATMP 廠房在進行 VHP 去污前，必須先驗證整個房間的氣密性。

                        

差別在於：熱氣球漏氣只是飛不起來，ATMP 廠房氣密失效可能導致 BSL 病原體或有毒試劑外洩，後果截然不同。

                    

                    

                        

#### 思考題 2

                        

設施工程師在 BSL-2 ATMP 生產間安裝了一個新的溫溼度感測器，將探頭穿過牆板固定，但未對穿牆孔進行封堵處理。

                        

在下次 VHP 去污週期中可能發生什麼問題？應如何預防？

## Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) Generation and Distribution System

                

PDA PtC-14, p25–26 | Cell culture supply, asphyxiation risk, O₂ monitoring

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

### CO₂ System — Key Requirements

                    

**a.** Any CO₂ distribution lines installed should be tested to confirm that the microbial and particulate levels are suitable for the intended use. The quality classification of the distribution line generally depends on the intended use (e.g., the classification of the air space within the equipment for which the CO₂ is used).

                    

**b.** The design of the distribution system should consider the pressure requirements of the intended use including any possible future expansion needs. Appropriate pressure regulation should be included in the design if equipment with different pressure requirements is connected to the distribution system.

                    

**c.** The design should include the necessary controls to provide a continuous supply of CO₂ gas (e.g., source tank switchover) at the appropriate flow rate and such features should be commissioned and/or qualified.

                    

**d.** Branched-distribution system designs serving different processes should consider how cross-contamination risk through the piping can be prevented.

                    

**e.** Pipes must be marked with CO₂ signs and should indicate the flow direction.

                    

**f.** An oxygen (O₂) sensor with visual and audible alarm should be installed to notify personnel of a low-oxygen condition. Personnel should also be equipped with personal O₂ sensors. A buddy system and/or man-down alarms should also be considered.

                    

**g.** A risk-based approach should be used for the qualification.

                

                
                

                    

### CO₂ 系統 — 細胞培養的「生命線」

                    

                        

#### 為什麼 ATMP 廠房特別需要 CO₂ 系統？

                        

CO₂ 在 ATMP 製造中的主要用途是細胞培養（Cell Culture）。哺乳動物細胞（包括 T 細胞、NK 細胞、iPSC）的培養環境需要：

                        

                            
- 溫度：37°C（模擬體溫）

                            
- **CO₂ 濃度：5%（維持培養液 pH 值在 7.2–7.4）**

                            
- 濕度：高濕度（防止培養液蒸發）

                        

                        

傳統小分子藥廠幾乎不需要 CO₂ 系統；ATMP 廠房的 CO₂ 供應一旦中斷，細胞可能在數小時內因 pH 值失控而死亡，導致批次報廢。

                    

                    

                        

#### 生命安全警告：CO₂ 窒息風險（Asphyxiation Risk）

                        

CO₂ 是無色無味的氣體，在封閉空間中濃度升高時，操作人員在察覺前可能已喪失行動能力。

                        

要點 (f) 要求的三重防護：

                        

                            
- **房間 O₂ 監測系統**：固定式感測器，設視覺 + 聽覺警報

                            
- **個人 O₂ 感測器**：操作人員隨身攜帶

                            
- **夥伴制度（Buddy System）/ 倒地警報（Man-Down Alarm）**：若人員失去回應，觸發緊急救援

                        

                        

這三重防護對應決策優先序 **#1（操作人員安全）**，屬於不可妥協的強制要求。

                    

                    

                        

#### CO₂ 系統品質分類原則

                        

```

CO₂ 品質分類取決於接觸點的環境分級：

使用場景              CO₂ 管線品質要求
─────────────────────────────────────────
CO₂ 進入 ISO 5 層流罩內部  →  需達到同等潔淨室標準
CO₂ 進入 ISO 7 培養箱      →  符合 ISO 7 微粒/微生物要求
CO₂ 用於非產品接觸用途     →  一般工業級可能可接受
(如 HVAC 壓力平衡用途)

關鍵原則：CO₂ 管線品質 ≥ 其進入的最嚴格環境分級
                        
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：餐廳廚房的天然氣供應

                        

想像一家餐廳的天然氣系統：

                        

                            
- 需要**持續供氣**（備用桶切換 = Source Tank Switchover）

                            
- 不同爐具有**不同壓力需求**（需要壓力調節）

                            
- 天然氣有**爆炸/窒息風險**（需要洩漏偵測）

                        

                        

CO₂ 系統的邏輯完全相同，只是廚房換成了細胞培養間，天然氣換成了 CO₂，後果從廚房爆炸換成了細胞死亡 + 人員窒息。

                    

                    

                        

#### 思考題 3

                        

一個 CAR-T 細胞製造設施有兩條獨立生產線：Line A（自體）和 Line B（異體）。設施工程師建議共用一條 CO₂ 主幹管，再分支到各個培養箱，以節省建設成本。

                        

從要點 (d)「交叉污染風險」角度評估：這個設計有何潛在問題？需要哪些工程控制措施？

## Purified Water (PW) Generation and Distribution System

                

PDA PtC-14, p26 | Biofilm prevention, disinfectant preparation, pressure differential

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

### Purified Water — Key Requirements

                    

**a.** Any purified water generation and distribution system should be qualified and commissioned for the intended use. Routine sampling of both the generation and distribution system should be conducted to ensure the ongoing quality of the purified water.

                    

**b.** The use of purified water in cleaning activities should consider the impact of water quality on any surface-disinfectant preparation and on the ability to maintain room classification, or WFI should be considered as an alternative.

                    

**c.** The design and maintenance (e.g., temperature, flow rate, routine disinfection) of the distribution system should ensure no biofilm formation.

                    

**d.** Distribution systems serving different processes or different equipment within the process should ensure that the process, including the cleaning process, operates at a lower pressure than the purified distribution system to prevent a cross-contamination risk through the piping, or the valve design should prevent any possible mixing (e.g., double-block and bleed).

                    

**e.** Pipes must be marked with "Purified Water" signs and should indicate the flow direction.

                

                
                

                    

### 純化水系統 — ATMP 廠房的清潔水策略

                    

                        

#### 純化水（Purified Water, PW）的定義與 ATMP 適用範圍

                        

純化水依 Ph. Eur. / USP 標準生產，主要用途：

                        

                            
- 清潔用水（設備表面清洗）

                            
- 配製非注射用消毒劑（如 70% IPA、次氯酸鈉溶液）

                            
- 非直接產品接觸用途

                        

                        

要點 (b) 提出一個重要的 ATMP 特有考量：若純化水水質不穩定，可能影響所配製消毒劑的**效力**（硬度、pH 值影響），從而危及 BSL 環境維持能力。在這種情況下，應考慮**改用 WFI** 來配製消毒劑。

                    

                    

                        

#### ATMP 廠房純化水的特殊挑戰：生物膜（Biofilm）

                        

傳統藥廠已有成熟的純化水生物膜控制措施（高溫循環、定期臭氧消毒等），但 ATMP 廠房面臨額外風險：

                        

                            
- 若設施規模較小（如 autologous ATMP 設施），水系統使用頻率可能較低

                            
- 低流速期間生物膜形成風險上升

                            
- ATMP 免疫療法患者往往免疫力極度低下（化療後），對水中微生物污染**零容忍**

                        

                        

要點 (c) 的「無生物膜」要求在 ATMP 廠房中等同決策優先序 **#2（患者安全）**的直接要求。

                    

                    

                        

#### 壓力差防交叉污染原則（Pressure Differential Principle）

                        

```

雙塊洩放閥（Double-Block and Bleed, DBB）設計：

高壓端（PW 主管）
       |
    [Block Valve 1]  ← 正常關閉狀態
       |
    [Bleed Valve]    ← 任何洩漏都從此排放到安全位置
       |
    [Block Valve 2]  ← 正常關閉狀態
       |
低壓端（製程設備 / 清潔用途）

原則：設備側壓力 < PW 分配管壓力
      即使閥門失效，污染不能逆流進入主管

替代方案：獨立管路設計（無共用連接）
                        
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：城市自來水系統的逆流防止器

                        

城市自來水管網在接入用水戶時，會安裝「逆流防止閥」（Backflow Preventer），確保自來水廠水壓始終高於用戶端，防止污染物從用戶端逆流回水廠。

                        

ATMP 廠房 PW 系統的 DBB 設計原理完全相同：確保潔淨水源永遠不被製程端污染，方向只能是「水源 → 製程」，永不逆流。

## Water for Injection (WFI) Generation and Distribution System

                

PDA PtC-14, p26 | Distillation requirement, product-contact validation, biofilm prevention

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

### WFI System — Key Requirements

                    

**a.** Any WFI generation and distribution system should be qualified and validated to consistently produce water meeting the specification as it is intended to be used. Sampling locations close to the intended points of use should be defined according to the Control of Contamination Strategy (CCS). Routine sampling of both the generation and distribution system should be conducted to ensure the ongoing quality of the WFI.

                    

**b.** WFI should be produced by distillation or by a purification process that is equivalent to distillation if WFI is used for direct product-contact purposes.

                    

**c.** The design and maintenance (e.g., temperature, flow rate, routine disinfection) of the distribution system should ensure no biofilm formation.

                    

**d.** Distribution systems serving different processes or different equipment within the process should ensure that the process, including the cleaning process, operates at a lower pressure than the WFI distribution system to prevent cross-contamination risk through the piping, or the valve design should prevent any possible mixing (e.g., double block and bleed).

                    

**e.** Pipes must be marked with "WFI" signs and should indicate the flow direction.

                

                
                

                    

### 注射用水系統 — ATMP 最高水質要求

                    

                        

#### WFI 在 ATMP 製造中的用途

                        

注射用水（Water for Injection, WFI）是 ATMP 製造中用於直接產品接觸的水質等級，包括：

                        

                            
- 最終配製（Final Formulation）：稀釋細胞懸浮液

                            
- 緩衝液配製（Buffer Preparation）

                            
- 無菌清洗（Final Rinse after CIP）

                            
- 蒸汽滅菌鍋進水（若使用純蒸汽）

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### WFI vs. 純化水：ATMP 廠房的選擇準則

                        
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 用途 | 建議水質 | 理由 |
| --- | --- | --- |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 直接接觸最終產品 | WFI 必須 | 內毒素、微生物最高管控 |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 配製注射用緩衝液 | WFI 必須 | 產品接觸等同直接接觸 |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 設備最終清洗淋洗 | WFI 建議 | 防止殘留水中內毒素污染 |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 配製消毒劑（非直接產品接觸） | PW 可能可接受 | 需進行風險評估 |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 設備初洗、環境清潔 | PW 通常足夠 | 非產品接觸用途 |
                        

                    

                    

                        

#### CCS（污染控制策略）與 WFI 取樣點設計

                        

要點 (a) 特別提到依據 **CCS** 定義取樣位置，這是 EU GMP Annex 1（2022 版）的核心概念。

                        

CCS 要求不能只在 WFI 產生機出口取樣，必須在**最接近實際使用點**（Point of Use）的位置取樣，因為分配管線本身也可能是污染源。

                        

在 ATMP 小批量生產中，WFI 系統可能規模較小，取樣點設計尤其重要，因為使用頻率低的支管更容易累積微生物。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：醫院直飲水系統與手術室用水

                        

醫院走廊的飲水機（純化水等級）和手術室的無菌生理食鹽水（WFI 等級），使用不同的水質標準，因為後者直接進入患者體內。

                        

ATMP 廠房的邏輯相同——「用到哪裡、就要達到那裡的水質」，這是 CCS 導向設計的核心精神。

                    

                    

                        

#### 思考題 4

                        

一家新建的 ATMP CDMO 設施，由於空間限制，工程師提議：WFI 和純化水共用同一條分配管的最後一段，在使用點前才分叉。為了節省成本，兩個管路共用一個取樣口。

## Liquid Nitrogen (LN₂) Generation and Distribution System

                

PDA PtC-14, p26–27 | Ultra-low temperature storage, cryogenic burn risk, cell bank backup

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

### Liquid Nitrogen — Key Requirements

                    

**a.** Installation of a liquid nitrogen (LN₂) generation and distribution system should consider the asphyxiation risk. A room O₂-level monitoring system with visual and audible alarms should be available, and personnel should also be equipped with O₂ personal-monitoring instruments. A buddy system and/or man-down alarms should also be considered. Means to detect and isolate any leak in the system should be available, including PPE for routine use and emergency-rescue purposes.

                    

**b.** LN₂ is extremely cold and contact to bare skin causes severe frostbite. Signage should be posted throughout the space. PPE-donning stations should be prominently positioned within the floor plan. First-aid stations should also be available for accidental exposures.

                    

**c.** The materials that require storage in LN₂ (ultra-low temperature storage) are commonly the most critical materials in the process (e.g., cell or viral banks). Therefore, it is common in designs to consider the inclusion of back-up equipment (e.g., LN₂ freezers) in case of equipment failure. The back-up equipment should be qualified for use and subject to routine maintenance so it is ready for deployment.

                    

**d.** Piping for distributing LN₂ should be adequately insulated both due to the cold-burn risk and to avoid condensation on external surfaces.

                    

**e.** Pipes should be marked with "LN₂" signs and should indicate the flow direction.

                    

**f.** Due to the risk of testing LN₂ and its stability, LN₂ quality through the distribution system is typically confirmed by the use of a Certificate of Compliance or Certificate of Analysis provided by LN₂ supplier.

                    

**g.** Design should include controls to provide continuous supply of LN₂ as required (e.g., source-tank switchover), and such features should be commissioned and/or qualified accordingly.

                

                
                

                    

### 液態氮系統 — ATMP 最關鍵的儲存介質

                    

                        

#### 為什麼 LN₂ 對 ATMP 如此關鍵？

                        

液態氮（Liquid Nitrogen, LN₂，沸點 -196°C）是 ATMP 製造中儲存以下最高價值物料的介質：

                        

                            
- **細胞庫（Cell Banks）**：Master Cell Bank / Working Cell Bank，是整個產品線的源頭

                            
- **病毒庫（Viral Banks）**：基因療法產品的病毒載體種子庫

                            
- **患者細胞（Patient Cells）**：自體 CAR-T 治療的起始原料，不可再生

                        

                        

要點 (c) 明確指出：這些是「製程中最關鍵的物料」，因此必須考慮備援設備。這在決策優先序中同時涉及 **#2（患者安全）**和 **#3（產品品質）**。

                    

                    

                        

#### 雙重危害：窒息（Asphyxiation）+ 低溫灼傷（Cryogenic Burn）

                        

LN₂ 系統是 ATMP 廠房中最危險的公用系統之一，具有兩種截然不同的危害機制：

                        

**危害 1：窒息風險**

                        

                            
- LN₂ 氣化後體積膨脹約 700 倍，迅速排擠空間中的 O₂

                            
- O₂ 濃度 < 19.5%：危險警戒；< 16%：失去判斷力；< 10%：立即喪失意識

                            
- 整個過程可能在數分鐘內發生，且無明顯預警感覺

                        

                        

**危害 2：低溫灼傷（凍傷）**

                        

                            
- -196°C 的 LN₂ 接觸皮膚，0.1 秒內造成嚴重凍傷

                            
- 液氮噴濺可能造成眼睛永久性損傷

                            
- PPE 穿戴站和急救站必須在平面圖中醒目標示

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### LN₂ 備援系統設計考量

                        

```

備援設計的三個層級：

Level 1：備用儲槽（Backup Dewar / Tank）
  - 確保主儲槽耗盡前可切換
  - 供應切換需經 Commissioning 驗證
  - 通常需要額外的樓板荷重設計

Level 2：備用 LN₂ 冷凍箱（Backup LN₂ Freezer）
  - 主設備故障時，立即將關鍵物料轉移
  - 備用設備同樣需要 IQ/OQ/PQ 確效
  - 需定期演練轉移程序（SOP）

Level 3：異地儲存（Off-site Storage）
  - 細胞銀行和病毒銀行通常應在兩個物理分離的地點備份
  - 這是行業最佳實務，但超出 Q3.1 的範圍

注意：備援設計增加了：
  + 設施佔地面積（Floor Footprint）
  + 資本支出（CapEx）
  + LN₂ 供應管路複雜度
                        
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：醫院加護病房的不斷電系統（UPS）

                        

加護病房的維生設備（呼吸器、心臟監護儀）絕不能因停電而中斷，因此醫院設計了 UPS + 發電機雙重備援。

                        

ATMP 廠房的 LN₂ 備援邏輯完全相同：細胞庫就是患者的「維生設備」，一旦 LN₂ 供應中斷而無備援，患者可能失去唯一的治療機會，後果不可逆。

一家自體 CAR-T 細胞療法的 CDMO，計劃在新設施中儲存 50 名患者的白血球分離產品（每位患者樣本不可複製）。

## Nitrogen (N₂) Distribution System

                

PDA PtC-14, p27 | Inert overlay, sterile filtration, asphyxiation risk, classification

            

        

        

            

                
                

                    

### Nitrogen (N₂) — Key Requirements

                    

**a.** Any nitrogen (N₂) distribution line installed should be classified. The classification of the distribution line generally depends on the intended use (e.g., classified environment within the equipment where the N₂ is used, criticality of the material in contact with the N₂ gas). Examples of N₂ use include, but are not limited to, N₂ overlay in the product container head space and N₂ overlaying in the bulk-material containers (i.e., displacement of oxygen to reduce oxidation of materials and/or maintain the stability of raw materials).

                    

**b.** Installation of an N₂ generation and distribution system should consider the asphyxiation risk. An O₂-level monitoring system room with visual and audible alarms should be available, and personnel should also be equipped with O₂ personal-monitoring instruments. Personnel in the room, or intending to enter the room, should be trained to exit (or not enter) the room when the alarm is active. A buddy system and/or man-down alarms should also be considered. The means to detect and isolate any leak in the system should be available, including the availability of PPE for emergency-rescue purposes.

                    

**c.** Appropriately qualified source and terminal sterile filtration should be considered if N₂ is used for product-contact purposes.

                    

**d.** Distribution systems serving different processes or different equipment within the process should ensure that the design prevents the risk of cross-contamination.

                    

**e.** Pipes must be marked with "N₂" signs and should indicate the flow direction.

                

                
                

                    

### 氮氣系統 — 惰性保護與產品穩定性

                    

                        

#### N₂ 在 ATMP 製造中的兩種主要用途

                        

**用途 1：惰性覆蓋（Inert Overlay / Blanket）**

                        

                            
- 在產品容器（藥瓶、袋）頭部空間覆蓋 N₂，排除 O₂

                            
- 防止產品（特別是細胞、蛋白質、脂質奈米顆粒）被氧化

                            
- 維持原料（如敏感性緩衝液試劑）的穩定性

                        

                        

**用途 2：管路吹掃（Purging）**

                        

                            
- 在灌裝前用 N₂ 吹掃管路，排除空氣中的 O₂ 和水分

                            
- 在維修後恢復設備前確認管路潔淨

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### N₂ 的窒息風險與 CO₂ 的差異

                        

N₂ 和 CO₂ 都有窒息風險，但機制稍有不同：

                        
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 特性 | N₂（氮氣） | CO₂（二氧化碳） |
| --- | --- | --- |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 比重 | 約與空氣相當（0.97） | 重於空氣（1.53） |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 在空間中的行為 | 均勻擴散 | 沉積在低處（坑道、地板） |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 警告感覺 | 完全無警告（純窒息） | 高濃度時有刺激感 |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 危險程度 | 極高（無預警） | 高（低處尤其危險） |
                        

                        

N₂ 的無警告特性使其在所有窒息性氣體中被認為是最危險的，操作人員可能在毫無感覺的情況下喪失意識。

                    

                    

                        

#### N₂ 無菌過濾設計：Source vs. Terminal 過濾

                        

```

當 N₂ 直接接觸產品時，需要雙重過濾保障：

供應端過濾（Source Filtration）：
  位置：N₂ 供應站（Gas Manifold）出口
  功能：去除來自氣源的微粒和微生物
  確效：依使用點的環境分級要求確效

末端過濾（Terminal Filtration）：
  位置：最接近使用點（Point of Use）
  功能：確保進入產品接觸區域的 N₂ 達到無菌標準
  確效：0.22 μm 除菌過濾，需進行完整性測試

為何需要兩道過濾？
  Source 過濾保護管線不受外部污染
  Terminal 過濾是最後一道防線，防止管線內部污染
  兩道過濾共同確保到達產品的 N₂ 是無菌的
                        
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：手術室醫療氣體系統

                        

手術室天花板上的醫療氣體終端（O₂、N₂O、壓縮空氣）每一個接頭都有顏色標示和防錯設計，就像 Q3.1(e) 要求的管路標示一樣。

                        

手術室的 O₂ 若被 N₂ 污染，患者有立即生命危險。ATMP 廠房的 N₂ 若被生物污染物逆流污染，可能危及後續患者的產品安全性。標示的目的都是防止「接錯管子」的災難性後果。

### 比較表解讀 — ATMP 公用系統設計的四個共通原則

                    

                        

#### 原則 1：所有公用系統都需要「分類」決策

                        

Q3.1 中每個公用系統都強調依「預期用途（Intended Use）」分類。這是一個貫穿整個文件的核心原則：

                        

                            
- CO₂ 進入 ISO 5 環境 = 需達到 ISO 5 品質

                            
- N₂ 直接接觸產品 = 需無菌過濾

                            
- WFI 用於最終配製 = 最嚴格的驗證要求

                        

                        

這與 EU GMP Annex 1 的「基於風險的方法」（Risk-Based Approach）完全一致。

                    

                    

                        

#### 原則 2：氣體系統 = 窒息風險的三重防護

                        

CO₂、LN₂、N₂ 三個系統都需要完全相同的三重安全防護架構：

                        

                            
1. 固定式房間 O₂ 監測（視覺 + 聽覺雙重警報）

                            
2. 個人攜帶式 O₂ 感測器

                            
3. 夥伴制度（Buddy System）/ 倒地警報（Man-Down Alarm）

                        

                        

這三重防護源自同一個決策優先序 **#1（操作人員安全）**，在任何 ATMP 設施設計中都是不可妥協的硬性要求。

                    

                    

                        

#### 原則 3：防交叉污染 = 壓力差或雙塊洩放閥

                        

PW、WFI、CO₂、N₂ 系統都強調了交叉污染風險。預防方法有兩種：

                        

                            
- **壓力差法**：分配主管壓力 > 製程設備壓力，污染不能逆流

                            
- **閥門設計法**：雙塊洩放閥（DBB），即使一個閥門失效，也有第二道防護

                        

                        

在 ATMP 廠房，交叉污染的後果可能包括批次間患者細胞混淆，屬於決策優先序 **#2（患者安全）**的最高風險類別。

                    

                    

                        

#### 原則 4：管路標示是 GMP 的基礎，也是安全的第一道防線

                        

Q3.1 中所有六個公用系統都要求管路標示流向。這看似基礎，但在 ATMP 廠房中特別重要：

                        

                            
- 多種高風險氣體（CO₂、N₂、LN₂）同時存在於同一設施

                            
- 接錯管路可能導致窒息事故或產品污染

                            
- 維護人員在緊急狀況下需要快速識別管路類型

## CDMO ATMP Facility Planning Scenarios

                

Practical application of Q3.1 utility design principles in real CDMO contexts

            

        

        

            
            

                

### CDMO Scenario 1：從傳統生技廠轉型 ATMP CDMO

                

**情境背景：**一家台灣 CDMO 目前主要生產重組蛋白和 mAb，計劃新增 CAR-T 細胞治療的代工服務。現有廠房已有完整的 WFI、純化水、CDA（Clean Dry Air）系統，通過 EU GMP 認證。

                

**需要新增或改造哪些公用系統？**

                

                    
- **必須新增：CO₂ 系統** — 傳統蛋白質生產不需要 CO₂，CAR-T 細胞培養需要 5% CO₂ 恆定供應。需要安裝 CO₂ 分配管、房間 O₂ 監測、個人感測器。

                    
- **必須新增：LN₂ 系統** — 患者白血球分離產品（Apheresis）和培養後的 CAR-T 細胞需要 LN₂ 長期保存。必須同時規劃備援儲存系統。

                    
- **需要評估升級：氣態去污系統** — 若 BSL-2 設施需要 VHP 或 ClO₂ 去污，需進行氣密性測試，並確認所有穿牆穿板點的密封性。

                    
- **現有系統可能需要確認：N₂ 管路品質分類** — 若用於細胞產品的惰性覆蓋，需確認是否已安裝末端無菌過濾。

                    
- **WFI 系統可能可沿用** — 但需依 CCS 重新評估新增生產區域的取樣點佈局。

                

            

            

                

### CDMO Scenario 2：設計新建 ATMP 多功能設施（Multi-modal Facility）

                

**情境背景：**一家新創 ATMP CDMO 計劃建設一個多功能設施，同時支援：Line A（自體 CAR-T，BSL-2）、Line B（AAV 基因療法，BSL-2）、Line C（異體 iPSC 衍生 NK 細胞，BSL-1）。

                

**公用系統設計的關鍵決策點：**

                

                    
- **CO₂ 系統是否共用主幹管？**  
Q3.1 要點 (d) 警告分支系統的交叉污染風險。自體 vs. 異體生產線的 CO₂ 管路應考慮獨立佈置，或在每個取用點安裝防回流閥，確保 Line A 患者細胞不會通過 CO₂ 管路污染 Line B。

                    
- **LN₂ 備援如何設計？**  
三條生產線共用一個 LN₂ 儲存系統還是各自獨立？共用節省成本，但 Line A 自體細胞（不可複製）的風險等級遠高於 Line B/C，建議 Line A 配備獨立備援儲槽。

                    
- **氣態去污系統的 BSL 邊界管理：**  
三條生產線的 BSL 邊界不同，VHP 去污週期需要分別設計。BSL-2 的 Line A/B 需要更嚴格的氣密性要求，Line C 可能採用不同的去污策略。

                    
- **WFI vs. PW 分配管路的壓力設計：**  
多條生產線共用 WFI 主管時，DBB 設計必須確保每條支管的製程壓力低於主管壓力，這需要在設計階段就由設施工程師和工藝開發團隊共同確認壓力需求。

                

            

            

                

### CDMO Scenario 3：法規稽查準備 — 公用系統的常見 FDA/EMA 檢查重點

                

**基於 Q3.1 原則，稽查員最可能關注的問題：**

                

                    
- **WFI 取樣點是否依 CCS 設計？**  
最常見的缺陷：只在 WFI 產生機出口取樣，未在最接近使用點的位置取樣。稽查員會要求看到 CCS 文件，並核對實際取樣點位置與圖面的一致性。

                    
- **LN₂ 備援設備是否已完成確效（Qualification）？**  
許多設施購置了備用 LN₂ 冷凍箱，但從未進行 IQ/OQ/PQ，因此在緊急情況下備援設備可能是「未確效狀態」，無法合規使用。

                    
- **氣態去污殘留物驗證是否完整？**  
稽查員會要求看到 VHP 殘留量分析的驗證報告，確認殘留水平對產品無不良影響。若缺少 Trace-Limit Detection 方法的驗證，這是典型的 483 觀察項目。

                    
- **O₂ 監測系統的驗證狀態？**  
CO₂、LN₂、N₂ 房間的 O₂ 監測系統是安全關鍵設備（Safety-Critical Equipment），必須有定期校驗記錄和警報測試記錄。稽查員會核對設備校驗日誌。

                    
- **所有穿牆管線是否有密封記錄？**  
尤其是氣態去污設施的所有穿透點。稽查員可能要求現場確認或查看密封材料的防火/防化學品相容性認證。

### 總結：Q3.1 的核心設計思維

                    

                        

#### 一句話總結 Q3.1

                        

ATMP 公用系統設計的**基礎**與傳統藥廠相同（GMP 標準），但有兩個額外疊加層：

                        

                            
1. **BSL 圍堵完整性**：所有公用系統的穿透點、分配設計都必須維持 BSL 邊界

                            
2. **活細胞/病毒的特殊脆弱性**：CO₂ 中斷 = 細胞死亡；LN₂ 失效 = 無可挽救的損失

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### ATMP CDMO 在公用系統設計上最容易犯的三個錯誤

                        

                            
1. **低估 CO₂ 和 N₂ 的窒息風險**：很多設施只安裝了固定式感測器，忘記要求操作人員攜帶個人感測器，違反 Q3.1 三重防護要求。

                            
2. **未驗證備援 LN₂ 設備**：買了備用冷凍箱但從未確效，緊急時無法合規使用，使備援形同虛設。

                            
3. **CCS 取樣點設計不足**：WFI 取樣只在產生機出口，不在使用點，無法發現分配管中的生物膜問題。

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 最終比喻：ATMP 公用系統 = 手術室的維生系統

                        

手術室有三個不能失效的系統：

                        

                            
- 醫療氣體（O₂、麻醉氣體）→ 對應 CO₂/N₂/LN₂

                            
- 無菌用水（手術沖洗液）→ 對應 WFI

                            
- 負壓通風（防止外部污染進入）→ 對應氣態去污 + BSL 圍堵

                        

                        

每一個系統失效都可能直接危及患者生命。ATMP 廠房的公用系統也是如此——它們不只是「基礎設施」，它們是**患者治療成功的保障**。

## Q4.1 — Biosafety Cabinet (BSC) Selection for ATMP Manufacturing

                

PDA PtC-14 p29–p33 | BSC Classes, Airflow, Grade Requirements, Operational Constraints

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Recommendation — BSC Overview

                    

A BSC is flexible in its use, although it typically does not have the same levels of integrated control and monitoring systems as an isolator. Instead, it relies on operators to manually decontaminate the Grade A space, maintain it through good aseptic practice, and appropriately monitor the environment.

                    

This increased reliance upon the operator means that operations should be carefully planned with an understanding of the barrier system limitations of the BSC and should incorporate appropriate risk mitigations to safeguard the aseptic operations.

                    

According to EU GMP Annex 1, the BSC is **not classified as barrier technology**.

                    

The room environment where the BSC is located should support the maintenance of the Grade A environment within the operational zone. This dictates the need for a Grade B environment to surround the BSC.

                    

BSCs are widely used in ATMP manufacturing as they are easy to install and maintain. They generally occupy a modest footprint that permits highly flexible operations. BSCs allow access to the inner working zone via an open sash.

                    

*Note: BSC Class III is the sole exception to the open-sash design.*

                

                

                    

## Q4.2 — Isolator Selection for ATMP Manufacturing

                

PDA PtC-14 p33–p36 | Open vs. Closed Design, VHP, MAL/RTP, CFD, Pressure Management

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Recommendation — Isolator Overview

                    

An isolator typically has integrated monitoring of the Grade A environment using viable and non-viable (total particulate) monitoring in addition to air-velocity and pressure monitoring. This provides the ability to control and monitor the environment while the isolator is in use.

                    

Isolators have the capability of undergoing an automatic decontamination cycle as they can be fully closed from the external environment. This ability during operations also provides effective separation from operators who can only interact with the interior through glove ports. Maintenance of Grade A conditions within an isolator is made simpler through this separation.

                    

Isolators are considered **closed barriers** to the surrounding environment and may be placed in a Grade C or Grade D environment based on the CCS and market requirements.

                    

In EU GMP Annex 1:

                    

                        
- **Closed isolators** require a background of Grade D or higher

                        
- **Open isolators** with small openings for continuous/semicontinuous infeed/outfeed require a Grade C background

                    

                    

The isolator system provides a Grade A work zone and should be equipped with an integrated, automated, and validated decontamination system (e.g., vaporized or nebulized H₂O₂).

                

                

                    

### 隔離器核心優勢解析

                    

                        

#### 隔離器 vs BSC：監控能力的本質差異

                        

BSC 依靠人員的肉眼判斷和程序紀律來維持 Grade A；隔離器則透過整合式感測器（粒子計數器、壓差計、風速計）**即時自動**監控 Grade A 狀態。

                        

任何異常（壓力降低、粒子超標）隔離器立即警報——而在 BSC 中，操作員可能在操作結束、採樣數據回來後才知道已發生污染。

                    

                    

                        

#### Grade D 背景的戰略意義

                        

封閉隔離器最低只需 Grade D 背景，這對 CDMO 來說是顯著的成本優勢：

                        

                            
- Grade D 房間：更衣要求低、EM 頻率低、訓練負擔低

                            
- Grade B 房間（BSC 需求）：相當於無菌核心區域的環境級別，建設與運營成本大幅增加

                        

                        

長期來看，隔離器的高 CapEx（設備費用）可被低 OpEx（Grade D 背景、少量更衣、少量 EM）抵消。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Closed Isolator Design — Figure 4.2-1

                    

                        **Figure 4.2-1 — Closed Isolator with Material Air Lock, Centrifuge, and Incubator (textual description):** The figure shows a modular closed isolator system. The main central chamber is a large rectangular enclosure with glove ports mounted on the front glass panel — operators interact with contents exclusively through these integrated gloves. To the left of the main chamber is a **Material Air Lock (MAL)**: a smaller interlocked antechamber where incoming materials undergo a validated VHP/VPHP decontamination cycle before the inner door opens to the main chamber. To the right, or integrated within the isolator frame, are ancillary process modules: a **centrifuge** chamber (sealed, connected via pass-through) and an **incubator** module (temperature-controlled compartment with glove access). All chambers are interconnected while maintaining Grade A throughout, enabling a continuous closed workflow without breaking containment.
                    

                    

During operation, the operator's only access to an isolator is through the gloves mounted on the glass panels. The quality of the gloves, set up, and design of the workspace are important and offer flexibility. Working with gloves through the barrier might be more challenging and could require enhanced training.

                    

According to EU GMP Annex 1, isolators are classified as a barrier technology. Open or closed isolators may be attached to adjacent chambers, permitting the closed transfer of materials in and out via a MAL (Material Air Lock).

                    

The VPHP used in the MAL decontamination cycle might pose a risk to the transferred material and should be carefully assessed.

                    

Additional process technologies — such as an incubator, centrifuge, or robotic system — can be attached and integrated within an isolator system to provide a closed and continuous working environment.

                

                

                    

### MAL 設計與 VHP 風險

                    

                        

#### 比喻：潛水艇的氣閘室（Airlock）

                        

潛水艇在水下要放人出去時，有一個「氣閘室」：先讓人進去，密封內門，把水灌進來，讓壓力平衡後再開外門。MAL 的邏輯完全相同——只是把「水」換成「VHP 去污劑」：

                        

                            
1. 物料進入 MAL，關閉外門

                            
2. MAL 內充入 VHP，完成去污循環

                            
3. 通風排除 VHP

                            
4. 開啟內門，物料進入隔離器 Grade A 區

                        

                        

全程隔離器主腔從未對外開放——Grade A 完整保持。

                    

                    

                        

#### VHP 對物料的潛在傷害

                        

VHP（汽化過氧化氫）是強氧化劑，對以下材料可能有傷害：

                        

                            
- 含天然橡膠或矽膠的耗材（可能降解）

                            
- 活細胞或組織（必須評估殘留 VHP 濃度）

                            
- 某些金屬表面（長期暴露可能氧化）

                        

                        

實務上需要做相容性研究（compatibility study）和殘留量驗證，確保去污完成後 VHP 已通風至安全水準，才能開啟內門。

                    

                    

                        

#### 整合設備的戰略價值

                        

將離心機、培養箱整合在隔離器內，等於把細胞治療的多個製程步驟（分離、培養、分裝）整合在一個封閉 Grade A 環境中，無需在不同設備間反覆轉移——每次轉移都是污染風險。對 CAR-T 等高價值 ATMP 而言，這種封閉連續工作流程是品質與效率的雙重保障。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Leak Rate & Integrity Testing — Figure 4.2-2

                    

                        **Figure 4.2-2 — An Example of a Closed Isolator System for ATMP Manufacturing (textual description):** The figure shows a complete closed isolator installation in a cleanroom background. The main body is a stainless-steel and glass enclosure mounted on a service frame. Multiple glove ports (typically 4–8 depending on working width) are visible on the front panel. A control panel with touch screen and alarm indicators is integrated on the side or top. HEPA supply filters are visible in the top plenum. Exhaust connections lead to the building ductwork. An attached MAL on one end allows material introduction. All seams and connections are sealed and leak-tested to the specified maximum rate. The overall system is designed to be both GMP-compliant and ergonomically accessible for the operators working through gloves.
                    

                    

The permissible maximum leak rate of the isolator should account for the criticality of the manufactured product.

                    

**Recommendation:** leak rate should be a maximum of 5% volume rate per hour (typical capability of today's systems: 3–5%).

                    

A routine monitoring program should include:

                    

                        
- Frequent leak-testing of the isolator

                        
- Integrity-testing of the glove/sleeve system

                    

                    

Supporting data from leak-testing should align with segregation policies and compliance requirements.

                

                

                    

### 洩漏率管理與風險意義

                    

                        

#### 洩漏率計算框架

                        

```
最大洩漏率 = 5% v/h（體積/小時）
典型實際值 = 3–5% v/h

若隔離器體積 = 200 L
最大允許洩漏量 = 200 L × 5% = 10 L/h

洩漏方向的雙重風險：
- 外漏（exfiltration）：產品/病毒載體外洩 → 操作員暴露風險
- 內滲（infiltration）：外部污染物進入 → 無菌保障失效
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 手套完整性測試：最常被忽略的關鍵點

                        

手套是隔離器最薄弱的環節——它們每天被拉扯、摩擦，針孔破損幾乎不可見。如果手套破裂：

                        

                            
- 正壓隔離器：產品暴露於外部空氣（污染風險）

                            
- 負壓隔離器（含毒性 ATMP）：操作員暴露於產品（安全風險）

                        

                        

因此 PDA 建議**定期**做手套完整性測試（壓力衰減法或染料滲透法），頻率由 QRM 決定，通常每批次前或每週至少一次。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### CFD Studies & Safety Filters — Figures 4.2-3 & 4.2-4

                    

                        **Figure 4.2-3 — Computational Fluid Dynamics Studies (textual description):** A CFD simulation visualization showing airflow streamlines and particle trajectory within an isolator chamber. Color gradients (blue = low velocity, red = high velocity) map the airflow field. Streamlines show air descending from the supply HEPA in the top plenum, sweeping across the work surface, and being drawn toward the return grilles and air-return ducts. Particle trajectories (shown as dots or arrows) demonstrate how aerosols generated at the work surface are swept away from the product and captured by return-duct safety filters rather than escaping to the surrounding environment. The simulation identifies any dead zones (stagnant air pockets) where aerosols might accumulate — these must be eliminated in the final design.
                    

                    

                        **Figure 4.2-4 — Safety Filter Before the Air-return Ducts (textual description):** A cross-sectional schematic showing the air-return pathway from the isolator chamber. At the junction where exhaust air from the chamber enters the building ductwork, a high-efficiency safety filter (HEPA or ULPA) is installed inside the isolator frame, upstream of the building ductwork connection. This filter captures any biological or particulate hazards before exhaust air enters the general building HVAC. For highly potent or biologically active ATMPs (e.g., viral vectors, gene therapy products), the diagram indicates that additional HEPA filters — a double-HEPA configuration — may be required to ensure no particles or aerosols escape the contained environment.
                    

                    

CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) studies are powerful tools to predict the spreading of particles and aerosols. Particles and aerosols should be transferred to air-return duct filters in the fastest and shortest pathway possible.

                    

The design process should use CFD to demonstrate control of airflow towards the safety filters within the air-return ducts. The criticality of the ATMP should always drive the necessary design attributes for both tightness (leaks) and airflow controls.

                    

For potent products, additional HEPA filters should be fitted before the air-return ducts to avoid spreading particles and aerosols outside the isolator chamber.

                

                

                    

### CFD 與安全過濾的重要性

                    

                        

#### 比喻：CFD 就像在設計排煙系統前先做風洞測試

                        

建築師設計辦公室通風時，會用電腦模擬氣流，找出哪些角落會有死角（氣流不流動）、哪些地方風速過高會造成不適。CFD 在隔離器設計中做的是同樣的事——只是「空氣」變成「含病毒載體的氣溶膠」，後果從「不舒適」變成「操作員感染」或「產品污染」。

                        

良好的 CFD 設計應確保：所有氣溶膠從產生點到安全過濾器的路徑**最短、最快**，不允許任何迂迴繞過過濾器的路徑存在。

                    

                    

                        

#### 雙重 HEPA 的應用場景

                        

標準隔離器：單一 HEPA 安全過濾器即可。

                        

含高效價病毒載體（如 AAV、慢病毒）的 ATMP：考慮雙層 HEPA（double HEPA）設計——第一層捕獲絕大多數粒子，第二層作為最終屏障。這與 BSL-2+ 或 BSL-3 生物安全設施的排氣處理邏輯相同。

                        

關鍵監管觀點：安全過濾器的完整性測試（filter integrity test，如 DOP/PAO test）應納入隔離器定期維護計畫。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Operating Considerations of Isolators

                    

                        
- Provides a controlled closed environment to protect the product and the operator

                        
- Surface decontamination with VPHP (vaporized hydrogen peroxide) or other decontamination agent systems is automated and validated

                        
- Controlled material transfer in and out via RTPs (Rapid Transfer Ports) or MALs with automated, validated decontamination cycles

                        
- Isolation of the pharmaceutical product within a small, closed environment that avoids spreading highly potent pharmaceutical products to surrounding areas

                        
- Higher BSL requirements can be accommodated due to the closed environment, automated decontamination, and the possibility of inactivation

                        
- More sustainable due to lower surrounding cleanroom environment (Grade D): less room EM, less gowning, and less related training

                    

                    

### Constraints of Isolators

                    

                        
- Isolators require careful design to ensure that all required aspects of the process can be executed through the designated glove ports and that all required material transfers can occur through designated RTPs or MALs

                        
- Isolators may, in some circumstances, be less flexible in terms of significant process or equipment changes

                        
- Isolators require routine leak-testing of the chamber(s) and integrity-testing of the gloves, sleeves, and glove ports to ensure that the Grade A environment is maintained

                    

                

                

                    

### 雙重壓力挑戰與 CDMO 選型思考

                    

                        

#### 關鍵矛盾：正壓 vs 負壓的兩難

                        

對於 ATMP 隔離器，壓力設計面臨根本性矛盾：

                        

                            
- **正壓（Positive Pressure）：**保護產品，防止外部污染物滲入（傳統無菌製藥的標準做法）

                            
- **負壓（Negative Pressure）：**保護操作員和環境，防止含有病毒載體或細胞毒性物質的氣溶膠外洩（生物安全要求）

                        

                        

ATMP 同時需要兩者——這是 BSC 和隔離器設計中最複雜的工程挑戰之一。解決方案通常是**壓力梯度設計**（如核心腔室正壓，廢氣路徑負壓）或雙重 HEPA 安全過濾。

                    

                    

                        

#### 隔離器壓力管理框架

                        
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 情境 | 壓力設計 | 主要保護對象 |
| --- | --- | --- |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 傳統無菌製藥 | 正壓 | 產品（防污染） |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| 含高效價病毒載體 | 負壓或雙重 HEPA | 操作員 + 環境 |
                            
                                
                                
                                
                            
| ATMP（兩者均需） | 壓力梯度設計 | 產品 + 操作員 |
                        

                    

                    

                        

#### CDMO ATMP 設備選型決策框架

                        

在評估為新 ATMP 客戶選用 BSC 或隔離器時，依以下順序思考：

                        

                            
1. **BSL 等級**：產品的生物安全等級是什麼？是否需要 BSL-2 以上圍堵？→ 決定是否必須使用封閉隔離器

                            
2. **批量大小與製程複雜度**：是單次小批量臨床試驗用，還是商業化生產？→ 影響 CapEx vs OpEx 判斷

                            
3. **製程彈性需求**：未來 2–3 年製程是否會顯著變化？→ BSC 更靈活，隔離器需前期精確規劃

                            
4. **監管市場**：主要服務 EU（EU GMP Annex 1 壓力大）還是 US FDA？→ 影響對 Barrier Technology 的要求

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 思考題

                        

1. 如果客戶要求在同一台隔離器中完成 T 細胞擴增（需要培養箱）和最終分裝，你需要評估哪些設計整合要素？

                        

2. 隔離器的 Grade D 背景環境要求，如何具體影響 CDMO 的廠房佈局規劃和環境監測計畫？

                        

3. VPHP 去污循環結束後，如何驗證 MAL 內的 VHP 殘留量已降到對活細胞產品安全的水準？

### Q 4.3. What should be considered when comparing biosafety cabinet and isolator design?

            

#### Recommendation

            

The design of an ATMP facility needs to be focused first considering its containment. The design of the containment and its background environmental classification are key considerations that will drive all other design elements (see Table 4.3-1). The choice of containment measures will be based on the existing facility design constraints, size and layout of the facility, new build budgets, and other company activities (see Table 4.3-2).

            

Regulators are increasingly pushing industry for containment measures similar to isolators, even if processing is not performed within an isolator.

            

                "According to EU GMP Annex 1, the BSC is not classified as a barrier technology, whereas isolators are."

### 解說：背景等級與無菌保證

            

                

#### 背景等級差異：Grade B vs. Grade D

                

這是 BSC 與 Isolator 最根本的差異之一。BSC 需要 Grade B 背景房間，而 Isolator 只需 Grade D（最低要求）。

                

                    
- **Grade B 房間**：需要更嚴格的 EM 監控、更複雜的更衣程序、更高的 HVAC 成本

                    
- **Grade D 房間**：較寬鬆，大幅降低設施長期營運負擔

                

            

            

                

#### 監管立場明確：BSC 是高風險設備

                

PDA 在此直接引用監管機構的觀點：BSC 被視為「高風險」設備，原因是操作員身體（手臂、手套）進入 Grade A 區域，成為最大污染風險來源。

                

EU GMP Annex 1 明確不將 BSC 列為屏障技術（barrier technology），這在法規定位上是根本性的差異。

            

            

                

#### 比喻：門是否鎖上的差別

                

BSC 就像一扇有缺口的門——裡面雖然乾淨，但人的手臂必須穿過缺口操作，沒有真正的「完全隔絕」。Isolator 則像是完全密封的手套箱，操作員只能透過固定的手套接觸內部。兩者的風險等級本質不同。

            

        

    

    
    

        

            

### Operational Comparison of Biosafety Cabinets and Isolators

            

BSCs and isolators are typically equipped with a filter of at least a HEPA classification. The type of HEPA filter installed is expected, at a minimum, to have particulate removal of **99.99%** when tested, in accordance with internationally acceptable standards. The filtration capability of HEPA filters in BSCs and isolators should provide a Grade A operating work zone.

            

Since a BSC is operated via an open sash compared with the closed operating mode of an isolator, there is a greater need to manage particulates and contaminants through good aseptic practice. Products in the work zone of the BSC may be exposed to particulates from gowning worn by personnel since personnel are required to manipulate the products directly via gloved hands.

            

In contrast, an isolator provides a closed environment where personnel indirectly access the work zone through integrated gloves. The use of gloves attached to the isolator may make product-handling inconvenient (i.e., due to the loss of some physical dexterity) in an isolator compared to a BSC. However, indirect access not only protects the product and process but also protects the operator as there is no risk of transfer of contamination to the operator's gowning.

            

Production within isolators also reduces the gowning requirement for operators since they may be gowned in accordance with the requirements of the background environment. This means operators working with isolators may be gowned for Grade D (or C) operations, while operators working with BSC must gown for Grade A operations.

            

Material transfers in a BSC are performed with the open sash, as one operator transfers items to the operator working in the BSC. Transfer technologies such as RTPs can provide lower-risk transfers. Isolators use transfer systems like MALs and/or RTPs to provide a safe and rapid transfer after a vaporized or sprayed decontamination agent cycle.

        

        

            

### 解說：操作比較重點

            

                

#### HEPA 過濾：兩者共同基礎

                

BSC 與 Isolator 都以 HEPA 濾網作為 Grade A 環境的基礎（去除效率 ≥ 99.99%）。這是必要條件，不是區分兩者的關鍵因素。

            

            

                

#### 更衣等級的實際影響

                

使用 BSC 的操作員需要穿著 Grade A 更衣（無菌手術衣、兩層手套、護面具等），使用 Isolator 的操作員只需 Grade D 更衣（基本工作服）。

                

**實際影響**：Grade A 更衣每次花費可達數十美元，且需要密集的訓練與環境監控，長期累積成本極高。

            

            

                

#### 物料轉移：高風險節點

                

BSC 的物料轉移是最高風險操作——需要打開 sash，由助手遞送物料，同時維持無菌狀態。這需要兩名操作員協作，且每次轉移都是污染風險節點。

                

Isolator 透過 MAL（物料氣閘）或 RTP（快速轉移埠）執行 VPHP 滅菌後再轉入，風險大幅降低，但需要評估 H₂O₂ 對物料的影響。

- 更衣成本大幅降低

                    
- 更衣訓練與驗證工作量減少

                    
- 整體每批次製造成本在高產量時遠低於 BSC

                

            

        

    

    
    

        

            

### Figure 4.3-5: Isolator and BSC Environment Options — Pressure Cascade and Bubble in Air Locks

            

                

[Figure 4.3-5 — Schematic illustration: not reproduced from source PDF]

                

**Pressure Cascade (for BSC in Grade B):** The surrounding Grade B cleanroom maintains a positive pressure cascade relative to adjacent lower-grade zones. Air flows outward from the Grade A zone inside the BSC, through the Grade B room, and then to lower-classified areas. This cascading pressure gradient prevents lower-grade air from entering the critical zone.

                

**Bubble Concept (for Isolator in Grade D):** The isolator functions as a self-contained pressure "bubble" within the Grade D background. The isolator interior is maintained at a defined positive pressure relative to the surrounding Grade D room. The air lock (MAL) between the Grade D environment and the isolator interior is purged with VPHP during material transfers, maintaining the integrity of the Grade A bubble.

                

                    
- Pressure cascade: Sequential positive-to-negative pressure gradient across room grades (B → C → D)

                    
- Pressure bubble: Isolator as an independent high-pressure zone within a lower-grade background

                    
- Air lock / MAL: Interlocked chamber between Grade D and isolator interior; decontaminated before each transfer

                

            

        

        

            

### 解說：壓力梯度與氣閘設計

            

                

#### 兩種壓力控制策略

                

**BSC 使用壓力梯度（Cascade）**：整個設施從 Grade A → B → C → D 形成連續壓差，每個等級比外圍高 10-15 Pa，防止污染氣流逆向進入。這需要整個設施的 HVAC 精密協調。

                

**Isolator 使用壓力泡泡（Bubble）**：Isolator 本身就是一個獨立的高壓氣泡，不依賴整個設施的壓差系統。Grade D 背景室相對簡單，Isolator 內部自行維持正壓。

            

            

                

#### MAL（物料氣閘）的關鍵角色

                

MAL 是 Isolator 系統中最關鍵的設計元件之一。每次物料進入 Isolator 之前，MAL 必須執行完整的 VPHP 除菌循環（通常 30-60 分鐘），確保不破壞 Isolator 內部的 Grade A 無菌完整性。

                

設計重點：MAL 的連鎖機制（interlock）必須防止兩側艙門同時開啟，類似太空船氣閘的設計邏輯。

### Decision Framework: When to Choose BSC vs. Isolator

            

#### Autologous vs. Allogeneic Considerations

            

**Autologous ATMPs** (patient-specific) typically involve:

            

                
- Small batch sizes (one patient = one batch)

                
- High product diversity and process variability

                
- Frequent rapid changeovers between different patient materials

                
- Shorter manufacturing campaigns

            

            

**Allogeneic ATMPs** typically involve:

            

                
- Larger batch sizes from a single donor source

                
- More standardized, repetitive processes

                
- Longer manufacturing campaigns

                
- Commercial-scale throughput potential

            

            

#### Phase-Appropriate Approach

            

**Clinical phase (Phase I/II):** BSC may be pragmatic given low batch frequency, limited capital, and need for rapid process adjustments. Regulatory agencies generally accept BSC at early clinical stages if appropriate risk mitigation is in place.

            

**Late clinical / Commercial phase:** Regulators increasingly expect isolator-level containment for licensed products. The investment in isolator infrastructure becomes justified by the higher production volumes and regulatory expectation for robust sterility assurance.

        

        

            

### 解說：決策框架

            

                

#### Autologous vs. Allogeneic：不同的設備邏輯

                

**自體（Autologous）ATMP**：每個病患一批，批次極小、換線頻繁、流程可能每次不同。BSC 的靈活性在這裡有優勢，Isolator 的複雜換線程序可能反而降低效率。

                

**異體（Allogeneic）ATMP**：一個細胞株可服務多個病患，批次較大、流程標準化、可重複。這是 Isolator 的主場——固定投資攤提到每個批次，ROI 更佳。

            

            

                

#### 法規期望的演進：何時必須升級？

                

目前監管機構的立場：

                

                    
- Phase I/II：BSC 可接受（附加充分的風險緩解措施）

                    
- Phase III 申請上市：開始要求更高的無菌保證，Isolator 漸成標準

                    
- 商業化許可：Annex 1 精神下，Isolator 已成事實上的行業標準

                

                

務實建議：即使現在使用 BSC，也應在設施設計階段預留未來安裝 Isolator 的空間與 HVAC 能力。

#### 思考練習：Q4.3 核心問題

        

            
1. 為何 EU GMP Annex 1 將 BSC 和 Isolator 歸為不同類別？這對 ATMP 製造的法規路徑有何影響？

            
2. 一家公司在 Phase II 臨床試驗中使用 BSC，計畫商業化後升級為 Isolator。在設施設計階段應預留哪些基礎設施？

## Q5.1 — What is important to consider in a mock-up for biosafety cabinets and isolators?

                

BSC 與隔離器模擬設備的重要考量 | pp. 43–45

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Recommendation — PDA Guidance

                    

A mock-up for the BSC or isolator at the vendor's site should be built to ensure that all reasonably practicable steps have been taken to ensure good aseptic practice when performing the necessary motions for the process, ergonomics for the operators, and the ability to perform the required maintenance.

                    

With an isolator system, the vendor normally performs a mock-up with wood, cardboard, or similar rigid materials because, after the isolator system is installed, the glove position can only be changed with significant effort and cost. With BSCs, the designs are normally standard and vary only with the size of the BSC.

                    

The mock-up exercise should consider the following key elements:

                    

                        
- Working position — seated vs. standing

                        
- Positions of all equipment within the Grade A area

                        
- Positions of all components and materials (hangers, trays, stands)

                        
- Position of process waste

                        
- Location of product-contact tools continuously bathed in first air

                        
- Process sequence/flow and all required manipulations and interventions

                        
- Location of critical surfaces relative to first air and operator movements

                        
- Locations of continuous viable and nonviable sampling in Grade A

                        
- Operator hand/arm/glove movements — hand must not pass over critical surfaces

                        
- Manipulations tested by both right- and left-handed individuals, varying heights

                        
- Frequency and duration of operations; weight/force for each action

                        
- Setup and performance of cleaning and disinfection actions

                    

                

                

                    

## Q5.2 — Differences in chamber leak-integrity testing between isolator and BSC?

                

隔離器與 BSC 腔體洩漏完整性測試頻率的差異 | pp. 46–47

            

        

        

            

                

                    

### Recommendation — Isolator Leak Testing

                    

For isolators, the leak test should be performed frequently with the interval established based on the frequency of intended operations in an appropriate risk assessment.

                    

The leak-testing frequency for isolators should not be limited to periodic evaluation, but rather should include the following situations:

                    

                        
- After changing gloves and sleeves (documented in the glove change management protocol)

                        
- Following any maintenance or repair that has breached the sealed structure

                        
- Supporting failure investigations, especially those involving viral and/or bioburden contaminations

                    

                    

PDA Points to Consider for the Aseptic Processing of Sterile Pharmaceutical Products in Isolators (2020) recommends that leak-testing be performed at defined periods, at a minimum, at the beginning and end of each batch or campaign and following any intervention that would affect the integrity of the unit.

                    

The testing procedures are described in Annex E.3 of ISO EN 14644-7. Methods include:

                    

                        
- Oxygen method (only for negative pressure)

                        
- Pressure-change method

                        
- Parjo method (very rare; mainly atomic industries)

                        
- Constant-pressure method (ISO 10648-2)

                        
- Tracer gas method (PDA TR No. 34)

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解說 — 隔離器洩漏測試

                    

                        

#### 為什麼隔離器需要高頻率洩漏測試？

                        

隔離器是一個封閉系統（closed system）：其無菌性依賴於腔體的物理完整性。一旦任何部位洩漏，外部微生物或粒子就能進入 Grade A 環境，直接危及產品無菌性。因此，每次可能影響完整性的操作後，都必須重新確認。

                        

隔離器洩漏測試的觸發時機：

                        

                            
- **常規**：每批次開始前和結束後

                            
- **維護後**：任何開蓋、更換零件、維修後

                            
- **手套更換後**：手套是最常見的完整性薄弱點

                            
- **調查支援**：發生不明微生物污染或病毒污染時

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 主要測試方法簡介

                        

```
壓力變化法 (Pressure-change method):
  - 原理：將腔體加壓（正壓隔離器）或抽真空（負壓），
    在規定時間內觀察壓力變化量
  - 接受標準：通常壓力變化 ≤ 0.25% / min（依 ISO 14644-7）
  - 優點：不需額外試劑，操作簡單，可頻繁執行

氧氣法 (Oxygen method — 負壓隔離器):
  - 原理：監測腔體內氧濃度變化，外部空氣滲入時
    O₂ 濃度上升，提示洩漏

示蹤氣體法 (Tracer gas method):
  - 原理：腔體內釋放示蹤氣體（如 He），外部偵測器
    測量是否有氣體逸出
  - 優點：高靈敏度，可定位洩漏點
  - 缺點：成本較高，需要專用設備
```

                    

                

            

            

                

                    

### BSC — Containment Testing vs. Leak Test

                    

As BSCs are an open system, a leak test cannot be performed before each batch as is done with an isolator system. A containment test method could be used instead of the leak test.

                    

This testing is typically performed:

                    

                        
- Upon first commissioning and validation for use

                        
- Repeated periodically (e.g., annually or biannually) to check continued performance

                        
- When required for significant investigations into the system's operation

                    

                    

Containment test methods referenced include the KI-Discus test and the segregation test (described further in Q5.3).

                

                

                    

### 中文解說 — BSC 的根本差異

                    

                        

#### 開放系統 vs. 封閉系統 — 根本上的不同

                        

BSC（生物安全櫃）是**開放系統**：前方有一個開口（sash opening），操作員的手臂從此進入操作。這意味著它在物理上**不可能完全密封**，因此傳統壓力保持洩漏測試無法適用。

                        

BSC 的防護原理是**氣流**，而非物理屏障：

                        

                            
- 向下的 HEPA 過濾氣流保護產品免受粒子污染

                            
- 向內的氣流（inflow）防止內部危險物質逸出到操作員區域

                        

                        

因此，BSC 的「完整性」測試，測的是**氣流的有效性**，而非物理密封性。

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
                            
                                ****
                                  

                                
                                  

                            
| 隔離器 | 洩漏完整性測試(壓力保持等) | 每批次前後 | 手套更換、維修、污染調查後 |
                            
                                ****
                                  

                                
                                  

                            
| BSC | 氣流展示 / 含蛋量測試(NSF/ANSI 49 煙霧研究) | 每年至少一次 | 初始確效、重大調查、定期驗證 |

## Q5.3 — Similarities and differences in airflow visualization (smoke study) between BSC and isolator?

                

BSC 與隔離器氣流可視化（煙霧研究）的異同 | pp. 47–50

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Airflow Visualization — Common Principles

                    

The airflow visualization, or smoke study, is a method used to aid in the evaluation of the CGMP-required airflow generated by a BSC, isolator system, or containment enclosure.

                    

Common criteria include:

                    

                        
- Unidirectional airflow in Grade A areas

                        
- Airflow around the apertures between Grade A and lower-grade areas

                    

                    

### Isolator — Airflow Visualization Objectives

                    

For isolator systems, airflow is designed to ensure that first air from the HEPA filter flows over the sterile product. The objective is to verify that airflows create a "sweeping action over and away from the product" under dynamic conditions (FDA, EU GMP Annex 1, PIC/S Annex 1, WHO TRS 1044 - Annex 2).

                    

The study visualizes flow at:

                    

                        
- Downflow across the chamber/work zone at the working position

                        
- Airflow with sweeping action over and away from the product

                        
- Airflow around large objects that may generate turbulence

                        
- Airflow during manual operations, interventions, and transfers

                        
- Airflow during processing of open units — no turbulence enters open units

                        
- Airflow at apertures — flow from critical area outward without reflux or entrainment

                    

                    

Studies performed at rest AND in operation, including during interventions. Air must not ingress from lower-grade to higher-grade zones.

                

                

                    

## Q5.4 — What methods are used to maintain facility cascade/pressure stability?

                

維持設施壓力梯度/壓力穩定性的方法 | pp. 52–53

            

        

        

            
            

                

                    

### Recommendation — Pressure Measurement Methods

                    

Pressure cascades can be measured using two methods:

                    

**Method 1 — Direct Differential Measurement**

                    

Directly measure the pressure difference between two adjacent areas where common areas exist. It is recommended that individual tubing be used between each high- and low-pressure sample-point location (although a manifold is possible but more complex). Advantage: a direct response to a pressure cascade change of state.

                    

**Method 2 — Absolute Pressure with Calculation**

                    

Measure the absolute pressure of each space and calculate the differential. Advantage: typically uses fewer sensors and provides a wider view of pressure comparison across the facility. Disadvantage: wider error range as two sensors are required for each differential calculation. In either case, a loss of local pressure would be identified.

### 中文解說 — 壓力量測方法

                    

                        

#### 壓力梯度（Pressure Cascade）的基本原理

                        

潔淨室設施的壓力梯度設計使空氣永遠從高等級區（更潔淨）流向低等級區（較不潔淨），防止交叉污染。

                        

                            
- **正壓泡泡（Positive bubble）**：核心無菌區相對周圍環境為正壓，確保任何洩漏方向都是從無菌區向外，而非向內

                            
- **負壓水槽（Negative sink）**：危害物質處理區相對周圍為負壓，確保危害物質不逸散到鄰近區域

                            
- **壓力梯度（Cascade）**：多個房間形成依次遞減的壓力序列，創造「單向逃逸路徑」

                        

                    

                    

                        

#### 兩種量測方法比較

                        

```
方法一：直接差壓量測
  測量：相鄰兩空間之間的直接壓差 (ΔP)
  公式：ΔP = P_高壓區 - P_低壓區 （由單一感測器直接測量）
  優點：反應速度快，直接反映壓差狀態
  缺點：每個相鄰對需要一個獨立感測器

  典型接受標準：
  Grade B vs. C：≥ 10–15 Pa
  Grade C vs. D：≥ 10–15 Pa
  Grade D vs. 外部：≥ 5–10 Pa

方法二：絕對壓力計算差壓
  測量：每個空間的絕對壓力 P_abs
  公式：ΔP_calculated = P_abs(A) - P_abs(B)
  優點：感測器數量少，可監測多房間的壓力整體狀況
  缺點：兩個感測器各有誤差，計算差壓的誤差為兩者之和
         → 對於小差壓（如 10 Pa）的量測，誤差影響較大
```

                    

                    

                        

#### 圖 5.4-1 解析 — 隔離器腔體與供氣 HEPA 上方的差壓量測

                        

此圖展示了隔離器系統中兩個關鍵壓力量測點：

                        

                            
- **P1（HEPA 上方）**：空氣在通過供氣 HEPA 濾器之前的靜壓，反映送風系統的驅動壓力

                            
- **P2（隔離器腔體內）**：過濾後的潔淨空氣進入工作腔體的靜壓

                        

                        

量測 P1 - P2 的差值（即 HEPA 的壓降），可以監測 HEPA 濾器的阻塞狀態——壓降逐漸升高代表 HEPA 負載增加，需要更換。這同時也是隔離器腔體維持設計壓力狀態的關鍵監測參數。

                    

                

            

            
            

                

                    

### Alarm Response and Pressure Control

                    

A suitable measurement interval for pressure readings should provide a balance between:

                    

                        
- Small variances caused by internal fluctuations within a controlled space

                        
- Loss of control parameters

                        
- Enabling the averaging of results accurately over longer periods

                    

                    

The process and use of the cleanrooms (entries, exits, frequency of use, movements) should be used to determine measurement and recording frequency as this may be different throughout each zone.

                    

If a pressure loss is observed:

                    

                        
- The air-handling unit (AHU) should react immediately to balance the pressure loss

                        
- Pressure loss should be recorded

                        
- An alarm should signal

                        
- The reason for the pressure loss must be investigated

                        
- Alarm signals should be timed to avoid signaling for routine operational events (entries and exits)

                    

                    

Manual operation in BSCs and isolators, or the opening of a door at a PAL (Personnel Air Lock) or MAL (Material Air Lock), are the most critical factors influencing air stability. Fast movements should be avoided.

                    

Alarm delays should be minimal (less than 3 minutes) and should be justified as part of the CCS (Contamination Control Strategy).

                    

For cleanrooms: air locks with door interlocks on all doors and pressure stabilization are recommended to avoid pressure fluctuations. Disruption of the differential pressure between air locks and adjacent cleanrooms will disrupt the function as:

                    

                        
- Pressure sink — negative relative to surrounding spaces

                        
- Air bubble — positive relative to surrounding spaces

                        
- Pressure cascade — positive on one side and negative on the other

                    

                

                

                    

### 中文解說 — 警報設計與壓力控制策略

                    

                        

#### 為什麼壓力穩定對 ATMP 尤為重要？

                        

ATMP 設施往往需要同時處理：

                        

                            
- **正壓無菌區**：保護細胞產品（高活性藥物）免受環境污染

                            
- **負壓含量區**：防止病毒載體（腺病毒、慢病毒）逸出傷害操作員

                        

                        

這兩種區域共存於同一設施，壓力管理系統必須確保它們的壓差在任何操作狀態（包括門的開啟、人員進出）下都能維持。一旦壓力梯度崩潰，可能同時危及**操作員安全（#1）**和**產品無菌性（#2）**。

                    

                    

                        

#### 警報延遲設計的合理性

                        

門的開啟必然造成短暫壓力波動。如果警報在每次門開啟的瞬間立刻觸發，操作員會因「警報疲勞（alarm fatigue）」而忽略真正的壓力失控事件。

                        

因此，警報延遲（alarm delay）的設計邏輯：

                        

                            
- 延遲 = 允許正常操作引起的短暫波動消散的時間

                            
- PDA PtC-14 設定上限：**延遲 < 3 分鐘**

                            
- 延遲設定必須以風險評估支撐，並納入 CCS（污染控制策略）文件

                        

                        

若超過 3 分鐘延遲，從壓力失控到警報觸發之間的時間視窗過長，可能已在無菌區域發生難以偵測的污染事件。

                    

                    

                        

#### 比喻：大樓防火門的壓差系統

                        

高樓火災時，逃生梯間通常維持正壓，防止煙霧進入。這個加壓系統設計必須在火災警報觸發後幾秒內啟動，且必須承受人員頻繁開門帶來的壓力衝擊。

                        

ATMP 潔淨室壓力控制與此原理相同：需要快速響應（fast-acting pressure control dampers）、允許短暫波動（門的開關）、但不能容忍持續壓力失控。

                    

                    

                        

#### 壓力控制系統設計框架

                        

```
CCS（污染控制策略）壓力參數典型設定：

  正常操作範圍（Action level 前）：
    目標差壓：15 Pa（Grade B vs. Grade C）
    警告限度（Alert limit）：10 Pa
    行動限度（Action limit）：<8 Pa（持續超過警報延遲時間）

  警報延遲時間：
    建議：30 秒 ~ 120 秒（依門的開關頻率調整）
    上限：< 3 分鐘（PDA PtC-14 要求）

  AHU 響應時間：
    壓力偏差偵測後 → AHU 調整 → 壓力恢復
    要求：AHU 響應時間 < 警報延遲時間（確保自動恢復）

  氣鎖門聯鎖（Door interlock）：
    同一時間只能開啟一扇門
    → 防止壓力梯度在人員進出時完全崩潰
```

#### Topic 5 — Supplementary Technical Information (pp. 42–55) — THIS SECTION

                

                    

                        

**Q5.1 Mock-Ups:** Three types — computerized (design phase), VR (mid-design training), physical (final qualification). Involves actual operators; requires right/left-handed, varying heights. Physical mock-up must replicate actual force/weight. Retained for ongoing operator training and new process validation.

                    

                    

                        

**Q5.2 Leak Integrity:** Isolators (closed system) — pressure-hold or tracer gas, minimum per batch. BSC (open system) — containment testing (KI-Discus, segregation test) cannot be performed per-batch; typically annually or at commissioning. Fundamental difference driven by open vs. closed system architecture.

                    

                    

                        

**Q5.3 Smoke Studies:** Isolator objective — product protection (sweeping action). BSC objective — operator protection (inward air curtain, no escape). Both require at-rest and in-operation testing. Documentation: multi-angle video, personnel count, activity list. Quantitative containment: KI-Discus (EN 12469), segregation test (ISO 14644-3).

                    

                    

                        

**Q5.4 Pressure Stability:** Two methods — direct differential (faster response) vs. absolute with calculation (fewer sensors, wider error). AHU must respond immediately to pressure loss. Alarm delay <3 min (CCS requirement). Air lock door interlocks essential. PAL/MAL opening is the most critical disturbance factor.

Key Takeaway for ATMP CDMO Operations

            

                PDA PtC-14 establishes that the core tension in ATMP facility design is the simultaneous requirement for
                **operator containment** (BSL-driven negative pressure) and
                **product sterility assurance** (GMP-driven positive pressure).
                Every technical decision — BSC vs. isolator selection, mock-up thoroughness, leak test frequency, smoke study scope,
                and pressure control system design — must be resolved against the same decision hierarchy:
                operator safety first, patient safety second. The supplementary topics in Topic 5 provide the practical
                qualification and operational tools that make this dual objective achievable in a licensed ATMP manufacturing environment.
            

            

                PDA PtC-14 的核心命題：ATMP 設施設計必須同時實現操作員含量保護（BSL 驅動）與產品無菌保證（GMP 驅動）。
                所有技術決策均服從同一決策層次：操作員安全優先，患者安全其次。
