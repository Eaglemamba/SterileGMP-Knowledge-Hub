INTRODUCTION

Electrical conductivity in water is a measure of the ion-facilitated electron flow through it. Water molecules dissociate into ions as a function of pH and temperature and result in a very predictable conductivity. Some gases, most notably carbon dioxide, readily dissolve in water and interact to form ions, which predictably affect conductivity also.

Water conductivity is also affected by the presence of extraneous ions. The extraneous ions used in modeling the conductivity specifications are the chloride and ammonia ions. The conductivity of the ubiquitous chloride ion (at the theoretical endpoint concentration of 0.47 ppm) and the ammonium ion (at the limit of 0.3 ppm) represents a major portion of the allowed water ionic impurity level.

The procedure in the section Bulk Water is specified for measuring the conductivity of: Purified Water, Water for Injection, Water for Hemodialysis, condensate of Pure Steam.
The procedure in the section Sterile Water is specified for measuring conductivity of: Sterile Purified Water, Sterile Water for Injection, Sterile Water for Inhalation, Sterile Water for Irrigation.

INSTRUMENT SPECIFICATIONS AND OPERATING PARAMETERS

conductivity, κ (S/cm) = Θ (cm-1)/R (Ω)
where Θ = cell constant, R = resistance

Cell Constant:
- Must be known within ±2%
- Verified directly with solution of known conductivity, OR
- Indirectly by comparing to sensor of known cell constant

Resistance Measurement:
- Calibrate using precision resistors traceable to NIST (accurate to ±0.1%)
- Measured conductivity must be within ±0.1 µS/cm of calculated value
- Minimum resolution: 0.1 µS/cm on lowest range

System Verification:
- Two non-temperature-compensated conductivity values should be within ±5% of each other
- Two sensors should be positioned to measure same water sample at same temperature

Temperature Compensation:
- Stage 1 Bulk Water: non-temperature-compensated measurements required
- Other conductivity tests at 25°: may use temperature-compensated or non-compensated
- Temperature accuracy must be ±2°

BULK WATER

Three-stage test method for: Purified Water, Water for Injection, Water for Hemodialysis, condensate of Pure Steam.

STAGE 1 (online or offline):
1. Determine temperature and non-temperature-compensated conductivity
2. From Table 1, find next lower temperature value — that is the limit
   [Note: Do not interpolate]
3. If measured conductivity ≤ table value: passes. If higher: proceed to Stage 2.

STAGE 2 (offline, equilibrated):
4. Transfer to suitable container, stir. Maintain at 25 ± 1°C.
   Vigorously agitate while observing conductivity.
   When change in conductivity < 0.1 µS/cm per 5 min, note conductivity.
   [Conductivity measurements may be temperature-compensated to 25° or non-compensated]
5. If conductivity ≤ 2.1 µS/cm: passes. If > 2.1 µS/cm: proceed to Stage 3.

STAGE 3 (pH test):
6. Perform within ~5 min of Stage 2. Maintain at 25 ± 1°.
   Add saturated KCl solution (0.3 mL per 100 mL specimen).
   Determine pH to nearest 0.1 pH unit per USP <791>.
7. Refer to Table 2 for conductivity limit at measured pH.
   If measured conductivity (from step 4) ≤ table value: passes.
   If measured conductivity > table value OR pH outside 5.0-7.0: fails.

STERILE WATER

Applies to: Sterile Purified Water, Sterile Water for Injection, Sterile Water for Inhalation, Sterile Water for Irrigation.

Procedure:
- Obtain sample; vigorously agitate package before opening
- Transfer to suitable container, stir, maintain at 25 ± 1°C
- Agitate while observing conductivity; when change < 0.1 µS/cm per 5 min, note conductivity

Acceptance Criteria:
- Containers ≤ 10 mL nominal volume: NMT 25 µS/cm
- Containers > 10 mL nominal volume: NMT 5 µS/cm
