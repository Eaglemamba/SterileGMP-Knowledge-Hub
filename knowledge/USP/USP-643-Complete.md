# USP General Chapter 〰643〱: Total Organic Carbon

USP General Chapter <643> TOTAL ORGANIC CARBON
Status: Currently Official on 12-Feb-2025
Official Date: Official as of 01-Sep-2021
Document Type: General Chapter

INTRODUCTION

Total organic carbon (TOC) is an indirect measure of organic molecules present in pharmaceutical waters measured as carbon. Organic molecules are introduced into the water from the source water, from purification and distribution system materials, from biofilm growing in the system, and from the packaging of sterile and nonsterile waters. TOC also can be used as a process control attribute to monitor the performance of unit operations comprising the purification and distribution system. A TOC measurement is not a replacement test for endotoxin or microbiological control. Although there can be a qualitative relationship between a food source (TOC) and microbiological activity, there is no direct numerical correlation.

A number of acceptable methods exist for analyzing TOC. This chapter does not endorse, limit, or prevent any technologies from being used, but this chapter provides guidance on how to qualify these analytical technologies as well as how to interpret instrument results for use as a limit test.

Apparatuses commonly used to determine TOC in water for pharmaceutical use have in common the objective of oxidizing the organic molecules in the water to produce carbon dioxide followed by the measurement of the amount of carbon dioxide produced. All technologies must discriminate between the inorganic carbon and the carbon dioxide generated from the oxidation of organic molecules in the sample.

PROCEDURES

1. BULK WATER
Applies to: Purified Water, Water for Injection, Water for Hemodialysis, condensate of Pure Steam.

### 1.1 Instrumentation requirements:

- On-line or off-line calibrated instrument
- Manufacturer's specified limit of detection of 0.05 mg/L (0.05 ppm) or lower of carbon

### 1.2 Reagent water: TOC level NMT 0.10 mg/L

### 1.3 Container preparation: Use scrupulously cleaned labware; final rinse with Reagent water

1.4 System suitability solution: USP 1,4-Benzoquinone RS in Reagent water, 0.75 mg/L (0.50 mg/L carbon)

1.5 Standard solution: USP Sucrose RS in Reagent water, 1.19 mg/L sucrose (0.50 mg/L carbon)

### 1.6 Reagent water control: Suitable quantity of Reagent water from same preparation time

### 1.7 Water sample: On-line or off-line sample reflecting quality of water used

### 1.9 Limit response:

rL = rS - rW
rS = instrument response to Standard solution
rW = instrument response to Reagent water control
Limit response of 0.50 mg/L of carbon

### 1.10 System suitability:

rC = rSS - rW
rE = 100 x (rC / rL)
System is suitable if rE is NLT 85% and NMT 115%

### 1.11 Procedure: Water sample meets requirements if rU is NMT rL

2. STERILE WATER
Applies to: Sterile Water for Injection, Sterile Purified Water, Sterile Water for Irrigation, Sterile Water for Inhalation.

TOC Limits Based on Container Volume (Table 1):
- ≤5 mL: Limit 1 = 32.00 mg/L carbon; Limit 2 = 48.00 mg/L carbon
- >5 and ≤100 mL: Limit 1 = 24.00 mg/L carbon; Limit 2 = 36.00 mg/L carbon
- >100 mL: Limit 1 = 8.00 mg/L carbon; Limit 2 = 12.00 mg/L carbon

### 2.1 Instrument requirements: Limit of detection 0.10 mg/L (0.10 ppm) or lower

### 2.2 Reagent water: TOC level NMT 0.50 mg/L

### 2.3 System suitability solution: USP 1,4-Benzoquinone RS per Table 2:

- ≤5 mL: 72.00 mg/L benzoquinone (48.00 mg/L carbon)
- >5 and ≤100 mL: 54.00 mg/L benzoquinone (36.00 mg/L carbon)
- >100 mL: 18.00 mg/L benzoquinone (12.00 mg/L carbon)

2.4 Standard solution: USP Sucrose RS per Table 3 (concentrations vary by container volume and Limit 1/2)

### 2.6 Water sample: Vigorously agitate packages before opening to homogenize TOC residues

### 2.8 System suitability:

rE = 100 x [(rSS - rW)/(rS - rW)]
System is suitable if rE is NLT 85% and NMT 115%

### 2.9 Procedure (10-step decision tree):

1. Measure TOC of Water sample; record rU
2. Compare rU to corrected rL1 (Limit 1) for appropriate container volume
3. If rU ≤ rL1: meets requirements, test complete
4. If rU > rL1: compare rU to rL2 (Limit 2)
5. If rU > rL2: does not meet requirements, test complete
6. If rL1 < rU ≤ rL2: identify and quantify individual organic impurities >0.20 mg/L carbon
7. If no impurities >0.20 mg/L carbon: meets requirements
8. If impurities >0.20 mg/L carbon exist: evaluate for safety
9. If safety evaluation: safe → meets requirements
10. If safety evaluation: impacts patient safety → does not meet requirements
