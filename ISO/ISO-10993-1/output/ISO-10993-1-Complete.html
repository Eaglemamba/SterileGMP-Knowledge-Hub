導師解析：前言

核心概念：ISO/TC 194 是什麼？

ISO/TC 194 是負責制定醫療器材生物學評估標準的技術委員會，全名「Biological and clinical evaluation of medical devices」。由全球各國標準機構的專家代表組成，確保標準具備國際一致性。

ISO 10993 第五版（2018）是目前最新版本，相較於 2009 年第四版，最重要的改變是：

  • 從「必須做的測試（X）」改為「需考慮的終點（E）」—— 這是從規定性（prescriptive）到風險導向（risk-based）的重大思維轉變
  • 新增「短暫接觸器材」的評估指引，對 CDMO 的一次性接觸設備評估尤為重要
  • 新增奈米材料評估要求，因應藥物遞送技術的進步

生活類比：從「食譜」到「烹飪原則」

想像一下，舊版 ISO 10993 像是一本固定食譜：「鹽放兩茶匙、糖放三湯匙」，照著做就對了。

新版 2018 則像是烹飪學校的教學原則：「根據你的食材特性、烹調方式、食用對象，自行判斷調味量。」

這對 CDMO 的意義是：與其說「這個橡膠塞需要做哪 12 項測試」，不如說「評估這個橡膠塞與藥液的接觸風險，再決定需要哪些數據」。數據可來自現有文獻、材料供應商憑證，不一定要全部重新做實驗。

法規重點注意

第五版將 Annex A 的標記從「X（應執行的測試）」改為「E（應考慮的終點）」，代表監管機構（FDA、EMA）不再接受「我按照 Annex A 的 X 都做了」作為充分理由，而是要求企業說明「為何這些終點與我的產品相關或不相關」。

CDMO 支持客戶審計時，應熟悉這個轉變，幫助客戶準備以風險為基礎的生物學評估報告（Biological Evaluation Report, BER）。

Introduction 導言

The primary aim of this document is the protection of humans from potential biological risks arising from the use of medical devices. It is compiled from numerous International and national standards and guidelines concerning the biological evaluation of medical devices. It is intended to describe the biological evaluation of medical devices within a risk management process, as part of the overall evaluation and development of each medical device.

This approach combines the review and evaluation of existing data from all sources with, where necessary, the selection and application of additional tests, thus enabling a full evaluation to be made of the biological responses to each medical device, relevant to its safety in use.

The term "medical device" is wide-ranging and, at one extreme, consists of a single material, which can exist in more than one physical form, and at the other extreme, of a medical device consisting of numerous components made of more than one material.

This document addresses the determination of the biological response to medical devices, mostly in a general way, rather than in a specific device-type situation. Thus, for a complete biological evaluation, it classifies medical devices according to the nature and duration of their anticipated contact with human tissues when in use and indicates, in a matrix, the biological endpoints that are thought to be relevant in the consideration of each medical device category.

The range of biological hazards is wide and complex. The biological response to a constituent material alone cannot be considered in isolation from the overall medical device design. Thus, in designing a medical device, the choice of the best material with respect to its biocompatibility might result in a less functional medical device, biocompatibility being only one of a number of characteristics to be considered in making that choice. Where a material is intended to interact with tissue in order to perform its function, the biological evaluation needs to address this.

Biological responses that are regarded as adverse, caused by a material in one application, might not be regarded as such in a different situation. Biological testing is based upon, among other things, in vitro and ex vivo test methods and upon animal models, so that the anticipated behaviour when a medical device is used in humans can be judged only with caution, as it cannot be unequivocally concluded that the same biological response will also occur in this species.

In addition, differences in the manner of response to the same material among individuals indicate that some patients can have adverse reactions, even to well-established materials.

The primary role of this document is to serve as a framework in which to plan a biological evaluation. A secondary role is to utilize scientific advances in our understanding of basic mechanisms, to minimize the number and exposure of test animals by giving preference to in vitro models and to chemical, physical, morphological, and topographical characterization testing, in situations where these methods yield equally relevant information to that obtained from in vivo models.

It is not intended that this document provide a rigid set of test methods, including pass/fail criteria, as this might result in either an unnecessary constraint on the development and use of novel medical devices, or a false sense of security in the general use of medical devices. Where a particular application warrants it, experts in the product or in the area of application concerned can choose to establish specific tests and criteria, described in a product-specific vertical standard.

ISO 10993 series is intended for use by professionals, appropriately qualified by training and experience, who are able to interpret its requirements and judge the outcome of the evaluation for each medical device, taking into consideration all the factors relevant to the medical device, its intended use and the current knowledge of the medical device provided by review of the scientific literature and previous clinical experience.

Informative Annex A contains a table that is generally helpful in identifying endpoints recommended in the biocompatibility evaluation of medical devices, according to their category of body contact and duration of clinical exposure. Informative Annex B contains guidance for the application of the risk management process to medical devices which encompasses biological evaluation.

導師解析：導言

核心概念：生物評估的目的與框架邏輯

ISO 10993-1 的核心目的是保護人體免受醫療器材帶來的生物風險。整個標準的邏輯架構是：

  • Step 1：蒐集並審查現有所有來源的數據（文獻、材料憑證、歷史測試結果）
  • Step 2：識別數據缺口（Gap Analysis）
  • Step 3：只在必要時選擇並執行額外測試
  • Step 4：整合所有數據進行生物安全性評估

關鍵洞察：現有數據可取代新測試。這對 CDMO 意義重大——如果供應商能提供完整的材料生物學數據，客戶的 BER 可以不需要重新做動物試驗。

核心概念：生物相容性不能脫離整體設計

導言特別強調：「材料的生物反應不能單獨考量，必須結合整體器材設計。」這意味著：

  • 同一種材料在不同應用情境下，生物反應可能完全不同
  • 最生物相容的材料，未必是功能最佳的設計選擇
  • In vitro 和動物模型的數據，外推到人體使用時須謹慎解讀

對 CDMO 的啟示：容器密封系統的橡膠塞或玻璃材質，其生物相容性評估必須結合具體藥品、給藥途徑與接觸時間，不能僅憑材料本身的通用憑證。

生活類比：醫療器材生物評估 vs. 食品安全審查

想像政府審查一種新包裝材料（如食品接觸塑膠袋）：

舊思維：「做完這 10 項毒理學測試，全部 Pass 就批准。」

新思維（ISO 10993-1 精神）：「先問：這個袋子裝什麼食物？在什麼溫度下使用？接觸多久？消費者是嬰兒還是成人？根據這些風險因素，判斷哪些測試是必要的，哪些現有文獻數據已經足夠回答安全性問題。」

這正是 Risk-Based Approach 的核心：問題驅動測試，而非測試清單驅動決策。

法規重點：動物試驗最小化原則

導言明確指出，本標準的次要目標是盡量減少動物測試，優先選用：

  • In vitro 細胞測試方法
  • 化學、物理、型態學表徵測試
  • 現有文獻與歷史數據

這與 FDA 2016 年的 Biocompatibility Guidance（Use of International Standard ISO 10993-1）以及 EU MDR/IVDR 的要求一致。監管機構普遍期望申請者在 BER 中先論證為何需要動物試驗，而非直接執行。

CDMO 若能幫助客戶提供充分的化學表徵數據（如 E&L 研究），往往可避免不必要的動物試驗，同時縮短上市時程。

1 Scope 適用範圍

This document specifies:

  • the general principles governing the biological evaluation of medical devices within a risk management process;
  • the general categorization of medical devices based on the nature and duration of their contact with the body;
  • the evaluation of existing relevant data from all sources;
  • the identification of gaps in the available data set on the basis of a risk analysis;
  • the identification of additional data sets necessary to analyse the biological safety of the medical device;
  • the assessment of the biological safety of the medical device.

This document applies to evaluation of materials and medical devices that are expected to have direct or indirect contact with:

  • the patient's body during intended use;
  • the user's body, if the medical device is intended for protection (e.g., surgical gloves, masks and others).

This document is applicable to biological evaluation of all types of medical devices including active, non-active, implantable and non-implantable medical devices.

This document also gives guidelines for the assessment of biological hazards arising from:

  • risks, such as changes to the medical device over time, as a part of the overall biological safety assessment;
  • breakage of a medical device or medical device component which exposes body tissue to new or novel materials.

Other parts of ISO 10993 cover specific aspects of biological assessments and related tests. Device-specific or product standards address mechanical testing.

This document excludes hazards related to bacteria, moulds, yeasts, viruses, transmissible spongiform encephalopathy (TSE) agents and other pathogens.

導師解析：第 1 節 適用範圍

核心概念：ISO 10993-1 的六大規範對象

Scope 明確指出本標準規範六件事：（1）風險管理框架下的生物評估原則，（2）器材分類方法，（3）現有數據審查，（4）數據缺口分析，（5）識別補充數據需求，（6）生物安全性評估結論。

這六點本質上就是「生物評估報告（BER）」的寫作大綱。客戶提交給 FDA 或 EMA 的 BER，必須覆蓋這六個面向。

核心概念：直接接觸 vs. 間接接觸

直接接觸（Direct Contact）：器材本身物理上碰觸到人體組織。例：外科手術縫線、注射針頭。

間接接觸（Indirect Contact）：液體或氣體流過器材後，再接觸人體組織。例：靜脈輸液套管（IV tubing）、氧氣面罩呼吸管路。

CDMO 關鍵應用：玻璃瓶+橡膠塞容器密封系統屬於「間接接觸」——藥液流過容器內壁後注入人體，容器本身不直接接觸組織。這個分類決定了後續採用哪個評估矩陣（Annex A）。

生活類比：什麼「不在」ISO 10993-1 範圍內？

ISO 10993-1 只管「材料毒性」相關的生物風險。如果你的顧慮是：「這個設備會不會被細菌污染？」或「這個針頭會不會傳播病毒？」——那是微生物學和感染控制的問題，不屬於 ISO 10993-1 的範疇。

就像汽車安全法規管「車體結構安全」，但不管「駕駛是否帶病上路」——兩者都重要，但是不同標準管不同風險。

TSE（傳播性海綿狀腦病，如狂牛病）被明確排除，有其他專屬的 ISO 22442 系列標準處理動物源性材料的風險。

法規重點：CDMO 容器密封系統的適用性

很多 CDMO 和客戶的疑問是：「藥品包裝（vials、stoppers、syringes、bags）需要做 ISO 10993 評估嗎？」

答案是：是的，尤其是組合產品或注射劑型。依據 FDA 2016 年 ISO 10993-1 指引及 EU MDR 附件 I，任何與藥品直接或間接接觸、最終進入人體的包裝材料，都需要生物學風險評估。

實際操作上，CDMO 的 E&L（Extractables & Leachables）研究數據是客戶 BER 的核心輸入，而 BER 的框架依據就是 ISO 10993-1。

導師解析：第 2 節 規範性引用文件

核心概念：ISO 10993 是一個「系列標準（Standard Series）」

ISO 10993-1 是整個系列的主框架（Master Framework），就像公司的最高管理政策。其他 Parts 則是各專項實作細則：

  • Part 5（細胞毒性）：最常被引用的入門測試，幾乎所有器材都需要
  • Part 10（致敏性/刺激性）：皮膚接觸器材的關鍵
  • Part 11（全身毒性）：長期植入物的重要終點
  • Part 17（可萃取物容許限量）：E&L 研究的法規計算基礎
  • Part 18（化學表徵）：材料化學身份確認，常替代部分生物測試
  • ISO 14971：風險管理總框架，ISO 10993-1 的風險評估邏輯在此基礎上建立

生活類比：法典 vs. 技術手冊

把 ISO 10993 系列想像成一套「食品法規體系」：

  • ISO 10993-1 = 食品安全法（總綱，確立風險評估原則）
  • ISO 10993-5 = 農藥殘留檢測方法標準
  • ISO 10993-17 = 各類污染物的最高容許量標準
  • ISO 10993-18 = 食品成分化學鑑定方法
  • ISO 14971 = 食品風險分析框架（如 HACCP 的法律依據）

每個 Part 可以單獨引用，但都必須在 Part 1 的整體框架下解讀。

CDMO 最重要的幾個 Parts

對於藥品容器密封系統（Container Closure System, CCS），最常引用的 Parts 是：

  • Part 5：細胞毒性（橡膠塞、玻璃材料的基礎測試）
  • Part 10：皮膚致敏性（表面接觸材料，如注射部位皮膚刺激）
  • Part 12：樣品製備方法（萃取條件標準化，影響所有測試結果可比性）
  • Part 17：可瀝出物容許限量（E&L 研究的毒理學安全界限計算）
  • Part 18：化學表徵（替代部分生物測試的關鍵工具）

注意：ISO 10993-2 要求在進行動物試驗時遵循動物福祉規範，這也是為什麼企業要盡量以 in vitro 和化學表徵方法替代動物試驗。

3.4 chemical constituent 化學成分

any synthetic or natural substance that is used in a process for manufacturing materials (3.12) and/or medical devices (3.14), including the base material(s), additives (antioxidants, UV stabilizers, color additives, dyes, etc.), and processing aids (solvents, lubricants, antifoaming agents, etc.)

3.5 data set 數據集

information, such as physical and/or chemical characterization, toxicity data, etc. from a variety of sources necessary to characterize the biological response to a medical device (3.14)

3.6 direct contact 直接接觸

medical device (3.14) or medical device component that comes into physical contact with body tissue

3.7 externally communicating medical device 外部連通型醫療器材

medical device (3.14) or medical device component that has been subjected to all manufacturing processes for the "to be marketed" medical device including packaging and if applicable, sterilization

3.9 geometry 幾何形狀

shape and relative arrangement of the parts of the medical device (3.14)

Also referred to as device configuration.

3.10 implant 植入物

medical device (3.14) which is intended to be totally introduced into the human body or to replace an epithelial surface or the surface of the eye by means of clinical intervention and which is intended to remain in place after the procedure

導師解析：第 3 節 術語與定義

核心概念：為什麼「生物相容性」的定義令人驚訝？

3.1 Biocompatibility（生物相容性）的官方定義是：「在特定應用中，以適當的宿主反應執行功能的能力。」

注意這個定義沒有說「不造成任何傷害」或「對人體完全無毒」，而是說「適當的宿主反應（appropriate host response）」。

這意味著：某些器材引發輕度局部炎症反應，可能是「適當的」——因為炎症是傷口癒合的必要過程。生物相容性是情境依賴的（context-dependent），不是材料的絕對特性。

例如：骨科植入物的骨整合（osseointegration）就是利用骨組織對鈦金屬的特定反應，這種反應在適當情境下是「相容的」。

核心概念：風險三角形——危害、風險、安全

ISO 10993-1 定義了一組相互關聯的風險術語：

  • 3.23 毒理學危害（Toxicological Hazard）：材料有可能造成傷害的潛力（固有屬性）
  • 3.24 毒理學風險（Toxicological Risk）：在特定暴露量下，實際發生傷害的概率
  • 3.2 生物風險（Biological Risk）：發生傷害的概率 × 傷害嚴重程度
  • 3.3 生物安全（Biological Safety）：生物風險在可接受範圍內的狀態

關鍵公式：危害（Hazard）≠ 風險（Risk）。氰化物（Hazard 很高）但如果劑量極低、接觸時間極短，Risk 可能可接受。

核心概念：直接 vs. 間接接觸的三個分類器

ISO 10993-1 將接觸類型分為三種，與後續的 Annex A 評估矩陣直接對應：

  • 3.6 直接接觸（Direct Contact）：器材本體物理接觸人體組織。例：骨科板、縫線
  • 3.11 間接接觸（Indirect Contact）：液體或氣體流過器材後接觸人體。例：輸液管、藥品容器
  • 3.16 非接觸（Non-contacting）：與人體組織既無直接也無間接接觸。例：體外診斷儀器外殼

對 CDMO 的關鍵意義：注射劑的玻璃瓶+橡膠塞是「間接接觸」器材——藥液流過容器後注入人體，容器不直接接觸組織，但可溶出物（leachables）可通過藥液轉移到人體，因此仍需進行生物評估。

生活類比：毒理學危害 vs. 風險——水的例子

水（H₂O）在極端情況下也可以「有危害」——如果你一次喝下 10 公升水，可能導致水中毒（低鈉血症）。但水的「風險」對大多數人來說極低，因為正常飲用量遠低於危害閾值。

同樣邏輯用於橡膠塞：即使橡膠配方中含有某種潛在毒性助劑，只要可溶出物進入藥液的量遠低於毒理學閾值（TTC），生物風險就是可接受的。

這就是為什麼 E&L 研究要計算「患者每日最大暴露量（Daily Patient Exposure）」，並與毒理學閾值（3.25）比較，而不是直接判斷「有沒有這種物質」。

核心概念：Final Product（最終產品）的重要性

3.8 Final Product 定義為「已完成所有製造流程（包括包裝與滅菌）的產品」。

這個定義在 CDMO 語境下極為重要：生物評估必須針對最終上市狀態的產品，而非製造中間態。

實際影響：

  • 如果橡膠塞在壓蓋前進行了額外清洗或矽化處理，生物測試必須使用清洗/矽化後的最終狀態樣品
  • 如果產品需要輻射滅菌，滅菌後的材料可能與滅菌前有不同的化學特性（如聚合物降解），生物評估必須反映這個變化
  • 使用滅菌前的材料數據來支持滅菌後產品的安全性，是監管機構常見的審查要點

法規重點：接觸時間分類的三個門檻（CDMO 必知）

雖然接觸時間的完整定義在 Section 5.3，但 3.26 Transitory Contact（短暫接觸）已在術語中定義，為理解分類奠定基礎。三個時間門檻是：

  • 短暫性（Transitory）：< 24 小時 — 例：單次使用注射器、靜脈留置針
  • 長期性（Prolonged）：24 小時 – 30 天 — 例：短期植入物、多日留置導管
  • 持久性（Permanent）：> 30 天 — 例：骨科植入物、心臟瓣膜

CDMO 容器密封系統的接觸時間分類取決於：藥品是單次注射（短暫）、多日連續輸注（長期），還是植入式緩釋系統（持久）。接觸時間類別直接決定 Annex A 中需要考慮的生物學終點數量——持久性接觸需要考慮更多終點（如慢性毒性、致癌性）。

法規重點：Risk Analysis vs. Risk Assessment vs. Risk Management

這三個術語在審計中常被混用，但 ISO 10993-1 有精確區別（均來自 ISO 14971）：

  • 3.18 Risk Analysis（風險分析）：識別危害 + 估算風險（定量/定性）→ 輸出：風險矩陣
  • 3.19 Risk Assessment（風險評估）：風險分析 + 風險評價（判斷可接受性）→ 輸出：是否可接受的結論
  • 3.21 Risk Management（風險管理）：分析、評估、控制、監控的完整系統管理 → 輸出：風險管理文件檔案（Risk Management File）

在客戶審計中，稽核員會問：「你的 CCS 生物評估是 Risk Analysis 還是 Risk Assessment？」答案應該是 Risk Assessment（包含 Analysis 和 Evaluation 兩個步驟），最終納入整體器材或藥品的 Risk Management 體系。

核心概念：Toxicological Threshold（毒理學閾值）——E&L 研究的計算基礎

3.25 Toxicological Threshold 定義了多種閾值類型：

  • TI（Tolerable Intake，可耐受攝入量）：長期每日可接受的物質攝入量
  • TE（Tolerable Exposure，可耐受暴露量）：特定暴露情境下的上限
  • AL（Allowable Limit，允許限量）：ISO 10993-17 計算的器材專屬上限
  • TTC（Threshold of Toxicological Concern，毒理學關注閾值）：無完整毒理學數據時的預防性保守估算值（通常 1.5 µg/day）

這些閾值是 E&L 研究中判斷可溶出物是否「安全」的數學基準。CDMO 在協助客戶準備 ICH Q3E（Extractables & Leachables）相關文件時，必須熟悉這些概念。

重點回顧與 CDMO 實踐指引

本節最重要的三個認知轉變

1. 生物相容性 ≠ 固定測試清單
ISO 10993-1 2018 版已明確從「必做測試（X）」改為「需考慮終點（E）」。CDMO 在支持客戶準備 BER 時，要協助論證「為什麼選擇或不選擇某些測試」，而非機械地執行清單。

2. 現有數據 > 新測試
標準明確鼓勵優先使用現有文獻、材料憑證、供應商數據，只有在確認有數據缺口時才執行新測試。CDMO 維護完整的材料技術檔案，可顯著降低客戶的 BER 工作量。

3. 接觸時間分類是評估矩陣的入口
短暫（<24h）vs. 長期（24h–30d）vs. 持久（>30d）的分類，決定了 Annex A 中需要考慮的生物終點數量。CDMO 需要了解客戶藥品的用藥週期，才能提供正確的材料評估支持。

CDMO 審計準備：常見問題清單

當客戶或稽核員對 CCS 材料提出生物相容性質疑時，CDMO 應能回答：

  • 這個材料的接觸類型是直接還是間接接觸？
  • 對應的接觸時間分類是哪一類？
  • 現有哪些生物學數據（供應商 CoC、已有測試報告）？
  • 是否有數據缺口需要補充測試？
  • E&L 研究中的可溶出物是否低於毒理學閾值（TTC/AL/TI）？
  • 生物評估報告（BER）是否涵蓋了所有相關 Annex A 終點的討論？

最後類比：ISO 10993-1 就像「醫療器材的建築法規」

建築法規不規定你的房子必須長什麼樣子，但規定了結構安全原則：地震承載、防火分區、逃生動線。具體設計由建築師根據建築類型、使用需求、地理環境來決定。

ISO 10993-1 也是如此：不規定你必須做哪 12 項測試，而是建立了生物安全的評估框架：接觸類型分類、風險評估流程、測試選擇邏輯。具體測試由專業人員根據器材特性、使用情境、現有數據來決定。

CDMO 的角色就像「結構工程師」——提供材料技術數據，支持「建築師（客戶法規部門）」做出符合「建築法規（ISO 10993-1）」的設計決策。

4 General Principles Applying to Biological Evaluation of Medical Devices

4.1 — Evaluation as Part of Risk Management

The biological evaluation of any material or medical device intended for use in humans shall form part of a structured biological evaluation plan within a risk management process in accordance with ISO 14971:2007, Annex I, as given in Figure 1 of this document. This risk management process involves identification of biological hazards, estimation of the associated biological risks, and determination of their acceptability.

The biological evaluation shall be planned, carried out, and documented by knowledgeable and experienced professionals. The risk management plan should identify aspects of the biological evaluation requiring specific technical competencies and shall identify the person(s) responsible for the biological evaluation.

The evaluation shall include documented, informed consideration of advantages/disadvantages and relevance of:

  • a) Medical device configuration (e.g. size, geometry, surface properties) and a listing of a medical device's materials of construction (qualitative) and where necessary, the proportion and amount (mass) of each material in the medical device (quantitative).
  • b) The physical and chemical characteristics of the various materials of construction and their composition.
  • c) Any history of clinical use or human exposure data. Note: Previous regulatory approval history can be relevant.
  • d) Any existing toxicology and other biological safety data on product and component materials, breakdown products and metabolites.
  • e) Test procedures.

Evaluation can include both a review of relevant existing preclinical and clinical data and actual testing. Such an evaluation might result in the conclusion that no testing is needed if the material has a demonstrable safe history of use in a specified role and physical form that is equivalent to that of the medical device under design. Testing is usually not necessary when sufficient information is already available to perform a risk assessment of the material and/or the medical device.

4.2 — Material Selection: Fitness for Purpose First

In the selection of materials to be used in the medical device manufacture, the first consideration shall be fitness for purpose with regard to characteristics and properties of the material, which include chemical, toxicological, physical, electrical, morphological and mechanical properties.

4.3 — Scope of Evaluation: All Relevant Factors

The following shall be taken into account for their relevance to the overall biological evaluation of the medical device:

  • a) The material(s) of construction (all direct and indirect tissue contacting materials)
  • b) Intended additives, process contaminants and residues (e.g. testing for ethylene oxide sterilization residuals per ISO 10993-7)
  • c) Packaging materials that directly or indirectly contact the medical device can transfer chemicals to the medical device and then indirectly to the patient or clinician
  • d) Leachable substances (see ISO 10993-17 and ISO 10993-18)
  • e) Degradation products (see ISO 10993-9, ISO 10993-13, 10993-14, and 10993-15)
  • f) Other components and their interactions in the final product
  • g) The performance and characteristics of the final product
  • h) Physical characteristics of the final product, including porosity, particle size, shape and surface morphology

Description of medical device chemical constituents and consideration of material characterization including chemical characterization (see ISO 10993-18) shall precede any biological testing. Chemical characterization with an appropriate toxicological threshold can be used to determine if further testing is needed.

Both local and systemic effects shall be considered for risk evaluation. Medical devices that contain, generate, or are composed of nanomaterials can pose specific challenges to the biological evaluation due to their potentially unique properties (see ISO/TR 10993-22).

4.4 — Categorization Drives Gap Analysis

The biological evaluation shall commence with categorization of medical devices (see Clause 6). Assessment of the information already available then enables a gap analysis to facilitate the selection of appropriate tests. The rigour necessary in the biological evaluation is principally determined by the nature, degree, frequency and duration of the exposure and the hazards identified for the medical device or material.

Testing is usually not necessary when sufficient information is already available to perform a risk assessment of the material and/or the medical device. For example, biological testing is usually not necessary if material characterization demonstrates equivalence to a previously assessed medical device or material with established safety.

The interpretation of the data shall take into account the chemical composition of the materials, including the conditions of exposure as well as the nature, degree, frequency and duration of exposure of the body to the medical device or its constituents.

4.5 — Hazard Identification: All Known Possible Hazards

All known possible biological hazards shall be taken into account for every material and final product, but this does not imply that testing for all possible hazards will be necessary or practical. Test results cannot guarantee freedom from potential biological hazards, thus biological investigations shall be followed by careful observations for unexpected adverse reactions or events in humans during clinical use.

The range of possible biological hazards is wide and can include short-term effects such as acute toxicity, irritation to the skin, eye and mucosal surfaces, haemolysis and thrombogenicity, as well as long-term or specific toxic effects such as subchronic and chronic toxic effects, sensitization resulting in allergy, genotoxicity, carcinogenicity (tumorigenicity) and effects on reproduction or development, including teratogenicity.

4.6 — In Vitro Preferred Over In Vivo

If testing is needed, selection of any in vitro or in vivo tests (see Annex A) shall be based on intended use. In vitro test methods, which are appropriately validated, reasonably and practically available, reliable and reproducible, shall be considered for use in preference to in vivo tests (see ISO 10993-2). Whenever in vivo tests are indicated by findings of the initial risk assessment, use of appropriate in vitro screening, if available, shall be considered before in vivo tests are commenced.

A rationale for the testing strategy, as well as for test selection, shall be provided. Test data, complete to the extent that an independent analysis could be made, shall be evaluated by competent, informed professionals, and shall be retained.

4.7–4.8 — Lifecycle and Reusable Devices

4.7: The biological safety of a medical device shall be evaluated by the manufacturer over the whole life-cycle of a medical device.

4.8: For re-usable medical devices, biological safety shall be evaluated for the maximum number of validated processing cycles by the manufacturer.

4.9 — Triggers for Re-evaluation

The biological risk assessment of materials or final products shall be re-evaluated if any of the following occur:

  • a) Any change in the source or in the specification of the materials used in the manufacture of the product
  • b) Any change in the formulation, processing, primary packaging or sterilization of the product
  • c) Any change in the manufacturer's instructions or expectations concerning storage, e.g. changes in shelf life and/or transport
  • d) Any change in the intended use of the product
  • e) Any evidence that the product can produce adverse biological effects when used in humans
4.10–4.11 — Existing Data and Historical Products

4.10: The biological evaluation shall take into account preclinical tests, clinical investigations, post-market experience from similar medical devices or materials, and other relevant information.

4.11: This document shall not be used to mandate re-testing of historical products assessed previously using the appropriate edition of this document at the time of the assessment. Nevertheless, compliance to this new edition shall be shown, by providing a justification for omission of further testing. However, if any of the changes described in Clause 4.9 occur, an evaluation of the biologic risks related to the change shall be performed using the current version of this standard.

第四節解析 — 一般原則

核心概念：生物評估是「流程」，不是「一張測試清單」

ISO 10993-1 最根本的邏輯：生物評估不是拿到器材就去做一批標準試驗。它是一個嵌入在 ISO 14971 風險管理框架之中的結構化流程。

這個流程的起點是「我們已知什麼？」，而不是「我們需要做哪些測試？」這個思維轉換非常重要——現有資料（材料規格、毒理文獻、供應商 CoA）可以取代全新的動物試驗。

  • 有歷史安全使用記錄 → 可能不需要新測試
  • 材料化學特性已充分建立 → 毒理閾值評估代替動物實驗
  • 等效於已評估器材 → 引用既有資料

類比：藥品杜撰對照 vs. 文獻引用

想像一位毒理學家不是每個藥物都從頭做 LD50 試驗——如果化學物質的毒理資料庫已足夠充分，他會先做文獻回顧，只在資料缺口無法填補時才啟動新試驗。

ISO 10993-1 的生物評估邏輯完全相同：先找資料，再補缺口，最後才是新測試。

CDMO 實務關聯：客戶提交的生物風險評估

在藥品 CDMO 的日常工作中，客戶（Sponsor/MAH）會在技術轉移包中附上容器/封閉系統的生物評估文件，這份文件的根據就是 ISO 10993-1。

CDMO QA 在審查這些文件時，應重點確認：

  • 評估是否由「有資格的專業人員」執行並簽署（4.1 要求）
  • 是否列出所有接觸材料（玻璃瓶、橡膠塞、鋁蓋、包材）
  • 是否說明不需要某項測試的理由（waiver justification）
  • 4.9 觸發條件：若 CDMO 更換供應商或改變一級包材，必須通知客戶重新評估

4.3 — 評估範圍：不只是器材本體

許多人只想到器材本身，但 4.3 列出的 a~h 項目提醒我們，評估範圍包含：

  • 包材：一級包材接觸到器材/藥品，可能間接傳遞化學物質給病人
  • 加工助劑殘留：EtO 滅菌殘留（ISO 10993-7）、潤滑劑等
  • 降解產物：長期使用或高溫環境下的材料降解
  • 奈米材料：特殊挑戰，另有 ISO/TR 10993-22 規範

在無菌充填 CDMO，橡膠塞的矽油塗層、玻璃瓶的脫層問題都在此評估範圍之內。

4.5 — 測試結果不等於「零風險保證」

標準明確指出：test results cannot guarantee freedom from potential biological hazards。這意味著生物評估是持續進行的上市後監察（post-market surveillance）的一部分，而非一次性的批准行為。

對 CDMO 的啟示：即使容器系統已通過所有生物相容性測試，上市後若出現不預期的不良反應，仍需重新評估（4.9e）。

4.9 重新評估觸發點 — CDMO 場景對照

  1. 更換橡膠塞供應商 → 觸發 4.9a，需重新評估
  2. 更改滅菌方式（gamma → EtO） → 觸發 4.9b
  3. 延長產品效期 → 觸發 4.9c（儲存條件評估）
  4. 藥品從靜脈注射改為肌肉注射 → 觸發 4.9d（接觸部位改變）
  5. 有病人回報疑似器材相關不良反應 → 觸發 4.9e

5 Categorization of Medical Devices

5.1 General

Medical devices shall be categorized according to the nature and duration of body contact as specified in 5.2 and 5.3. The categorization of medical devices facilitates selection of appropriate data sets (see informative Annex A).

The evaluation of any medical device that does not fall into one of the categories specified shall follow the general principles contained in this document. Certain medical devices might fall into more than one body contact or duration category, in which case evaluation appropriate to each category shall be carried out.

Example: For medical devices that include both an implanted component and a delivery system used only during a surgical procedure to place the medical device, the implant should be assessed separately from the delivery system.

5.2 Categorization by Nature of Body Contact

5.2.1 Non-contacting Medical Devices

These include medical devices (or components) that have neither direct nor indirect contact with the body and where biocompatibility information would not be necessary. Diagnostic software, an in vitro diagnostic device and a blood-collection tube are examples of non-contact devices.

5.2.2 Surface-contacting Medical Devices

These include medical devices in contact with the following:

  • a) Skin — Medical devices that contact intact skin surfaces only. Examples: Electrodes, external prostheses, fixation tapes, compression bandages and monitors of various types.
  • b) Mucosal membranes — Medical devices that contact intact mucosal membranes. Examples: Contact lenses, urinary catheters, intravaginal and intra-intestinal devices, endotracheal tubes, bronchoscopes, some dental prostheses.
  • c) Breached or compromised surfaces — Medical devices that contact breached or otherwise compromised body surfaces. Examples: Dressings or healing devices and occlusive patches for ulcers, burns and granulation tissue.

5.2.3 Externally Communicating Medical Devices

Externally communicating medical devices shall be categorized according to their contact with the following application sites:

  • a) Blood path, indirect — Medical devices or components that do not necessarily directly contact the blood path but serve as conduits to deliver fluids into the vascular system. Examples: Solution administration sets, extension sets, transfer sets and blood administration sets.
  • b) Tissue/bone/dentin — Medical devices that contact tissue, bone or pulp/dentin systems. Examples: Laparoscopes, arthroscopes, draining systems, dental filling materials and skin staples.
  • c) Circulating blood — Medical devices that contact circulating blood. Examples: Intravascular catheters, temporary pacemaker electrodes, oxygenators, extracorporeal oxygenator tubing, dialysers, haemoadsorbents and immunoadsorbents.

5.2.4 Implant Medical Devices

Implant medical devices shall be categorized according to their contact with the following application sites:

  • a) Tissue/bone — Medical devices principally contacting bone (e.g. orthopaedic pins, plates, replacement joints, bone cements, intra-osseous devices) or contacting tissue and tissue fluid (e.g. pacemakers, drug supply devices, breast implants, artificial larynxes, ligation clips, intrauterine devices that do not achieve their primary function by chemical activity).
  • b) Blood — Medical devices principally contacting circulating blood in the cardiovascular system. Examples: Pacemaker electrodes, artificial arteriovenous fistulae, heart valves, vascular grafts, internal drug-delivery catheters, ventricular assist devices.

5.3 Categorization by Duration of Contact

5.3.1 Contact Duration Categories

Medical devices shall be categorized according to the anticipated duration of contact as follows:

  • a) Limited exposure (A) — medical devices whose cumulative sum of single, multiple or repeated duration of contact is up to 24 h.
  • b) Prolonged exposure (B) — medical devices whose cumulative sum of single, multiple or repeated contact time is likely to exceed 24 h but not exceed 30 d.
  • c) Long-term exposure (C) — medical devices whose cumulative sum of single, multiple or repeated contact time exceeds 30 d.

5.3.2 Transitory-contacting Medical Devices

Some medical devices with limited exposure (A) have very brief/transitory contact with the body (e.g. lancets, hypodermic needles, capillary tubes that are used for less than one minute). These generally would not require testing to address biocompatibility.

However, for products made with materials such as coatings or lubricants that could be left in contact with body tissues after the medical device is removed, it is possible that a more detailed biocompatibility assessment will be necessary. Cumulative use should also be considered.

5.3.3 Medical Devices with Multiple Contact Duration Categories

If a material or medical device can be placed in more than one duration category, the more rigorous testing and/or evaluation considerations shall apply. With expected or intended multiple exposures to a device, the decision into which category a medical device is placed shall take into account the potential cumulative effect, bearing in mind the period of time over which these exposures occur.

If a medical device is intended to change during its lifetime, such as those that are polymerized and/or degraded in situ, the evaluation shall consider all the different device states. For example, for an absorbable glue intended to polymerize in situ, the different device states would include starting components, intermediate reaction products, the fully polymerized material and degradation products.

第五節解析 — 醫療器材分類

為什麼分類是生物評估的入口？

ISO 10993-1 把分類放在評估流程的最前端（4.4 明確規定 evaluation shall commence with categorization），因為分類決定了後續 Annex A 的測試端點清單。

邏輯鏈：分類 → 測試端點矩陣 → 缺口分析 → 必要測試清單

錯誤分類會導致評估不足（under-evaluation）或過度測試（over-testing），前者有安全風險，後者浪費資源和動物。

類比：航空乘客安檢的分級

想像機場安檢：持本國護照的國內線旅客（非接觸類）只需一般安檢；持外國護照搭乘國際線（植入型，長期接觸）則需更嚴格的審查。

器材與人體接觸越深、時間越長，生物相容性審查就越嚴格——這是 ISO 10993-1 分類系統的核心邏輯。

CDMO 填充生產的容器分類思考

在無菌藥品製造中，容器/封閉系統的分類取決於藥品的最終給藥方式：

  • 玻璃藥瓶（完整皮膚外部）：非接觸類 — 不需生物相容性評估
  • 玻璃藥瓶（藥品供靜脈注射）：器材本身視為「Blood path, indirect」（間接血路接觸） — 需要評估，但透過 CoA 和既有資料常可支持
  • 預填式注射器（Prefilled Syringe）：接觸循環血液 — 評估要求最嚴格，特別是注射器的矽油（silicone oil）和橡膠塞成分
  • 植入式緩釋器材：組織/骨骼植入，長期暴露（C類）— 最高風險等級，需完整測試

「累積接觸時間」的重要性 — 不是單次使用

5.3 特別強調是「cumulative sum」（累計總和），不是單次使用時間。這對一次性器材的重複使用場景特別重要。

例如：透析患者每週使用血液透析器 3 次，每次 4 小時。單次 4 小時屬 A 類（有限暴露），但若考慮累積暴露，則必須以更高的分類（B 或 C）來評估。

CDMO 實務：如果客戶的藥品是需要長期重複注射的（如生物製劑每月注射），則注射容器的材料評估必須考量累積效應。

類比：皮膚接觸 vs. 直接接觸血液的差異

想像手術室中的兩種手套：

  • 手術室外牆的橡膠手柄（接觸完整皮膚） → 表面接觸，A 類風險
  • 外科手術手套（接觸開放傷口） → 破損/受損表面接觸，風險大幅提升
  • 血液體外循環管路（直接接觸循環血液） → 最高風險，需要完整血液相容性測試

這個類比說明了為什麼 5.2 的分類從皮膚到血液，風險依次遞增。

分類練習 — CDMO 常見容器/封閉系統

  1. Type I 玻璃瓶 + 鹵丁基橡膠塞（供 IV 輸液）
    → 接觸性質：Blood path, indirect | 時間：有限暴露（A）（單次輸液 <24h）
  2. 預填充注射器（BD Uniject, insulin pen）
    → 接觸性質：Blood path, indirect（皮下）或 Circulating blood（靜脈）| 時間視頻率而定
  3. 鋁製鋁蓋（未接觸藥品）
    → 接觸性質：非接觸類（間接包材），一般不需單獨生物評估
  4. 植入式藥物緩釋晶片
    → 接觸性質：Tissue/bone 植入 | 時間：長期暴露（C）→ 最嚴格評估

6 Biological Evaluation Process

6.1 Physical and Chemical Information for Biological Risk Analysis

Figure 1 indicates how the general steps in the physical and/or chemical characterization process link to the overall biological evaluation decision points.

Gathering physical and chemical information on the medical device or component is a crucial first step in the biological evaluation and its associated process of material characterization. These data should be sufficient to answer the first two rows of questions in the Figure 1 flow chart. The extent of physical and/or chemical characterization required depends on what is known about the material formulation, what nonclinical and clinical safety and toxicological data exist, and on the nature and duration of body contact with the medical device.

At a minimum, the characterization shall address the constituent chemicals of the medical device and possible residual process aids or additives used in its manufacture. Material characterization, if performed, shall be conducted in accordance with ISO 10993-18. For nanomaterials see ISO/TR 10993-22.

If the combination of all materials, chemicals and processes has an established history of safe use in the intended application, and the physical properties have not changed, then further characterization and additional data sets may not be necessary. In this case the rationale shall be documented.

For device extractables and leachables that have sufficient toxicological data relevant to the expected exposure (quantity, route and frequency), further testing need not be required. For medical devices that have known leachable chemical mixtures, potential interactions between the leachables should be considered.

Where the potential for degradation exists under the conditions of manufacture, sterilization, transport, storage, and use of the medical device, the presence and nature of degradation products shall be characterized in accordance with ISO 10993-9, ISO 10993-13, ISO 10993-14, and ISO 10993-15, as applicable.

6.2 Gap Analysis and Selection of Biological Endpoints for Assessment

Assess available information and compare to the data set(s) needed to assess the biological safety of the medical device (see Clause 4, Annexes A and C). Identify additional reasonably and practicably obtained data or testing needed to complete the data sets required to perform the risk assessment.

Characterize the data gap and determine its significance both to the assessment of the biological endpoint (Annex A) and to the overall biological risk assessment. Identify options for data sets that would address the data gap.

NOTE: For example, pharmacopeial plastics' testing is typically conducted on raw materials, whereas ISO 10993 evaluates the medical device in its final stage. Therefore, data from such pharmacopeial testing is not sufficient for the final medical device without appropriate justification.

The results of the risk analysis of identified chemicals can lead to the conclusion that additional material characterization is necessary. Appropriate extraction testing can be used to estimate the degree of clinical exposure to the chemical constituent (see ISO 10993-18). The acceptability of the level of estimated leachables shall be established by comparing the amount of each compound extracted from the medical device to its respective relevant toxicological threshold as developed in accordance with ISO 10993-17.

Protection of humans is the primary goal of this document; a secondary goal is to ensure animal welfare and to minimize the number and exposure of the test animals. ISO 10993-2 applies to any in vivo testing being considered. Additional testing shall not be carried out where:

  • 1) results are available from relevant studies that have been carried out previously, or
  • 2) the existing non-clinical and clinical data, including history of safe use, meet the requirements of biological evaluation and therefore further animal testing would be unethical.

6.3 Biological Testing

6.3.1 General

In addition to the general principles given in Clause 4, the following shall apply when biological testing of medical devices is considered necessary:

  • a) Testing shall be performed on the final medical device, or representative samples processed in the same manner as the final device (including sterilization, if needed).
  • b) The choice of test procedures shall take into account: (1) nature, degree, duration, frequency and conditions of exposure; (2) chemical and physical nature of the final device; (3) toxicological activity of chemicals in the formulation; (4) that certain biological tests are not justifiable where leachable chemicals have been excluded or have acceptable toxicity profiles; (5) the ratio of device surface area to recipient body size; (6) existing information from literature and previous experience; (7) sensitivity and specificity of the test.
  • c) If extracts of the medical devices are prepared, the solvents and conditions of extraction used should be appropriate to the nature and use of the final product and shall be prepared in accordance with ISO 10993-12. Whenever possible, extraction conditions should represent at a minimum an exaggeration of use conditions.
  • d) Positive and negative controls should be used where appropriate.

The test methods used shall be sensitive, precise and accurate. When biological testing is conducted, it shall be carried out in accordance with good laboratory practices (ISO/IEC 17025 or equivalent). Test methods should be reproducible (intralaboratory) as well as repeatable (interlaboratory) and robust.

第六節解析 — 生物評估流程

六步驟生物評估流程（基於 Figure 1 精神）

ISO 10993-1 Section 6 描述的流程可以整理為六個步驟：

  • Step 1：物理/化學特性表徵（6.1）— 收集材料組成、工藝殘留、降解產物等資料
  • Step 2：缺口分析（6.2）— 比較已有資料 vs. Annex A 所需資料集，識別缺口
  • Step 3：選擇評估端點（6.2）— 根據分類結果決定需評估哪些生物學端點
  • Step 4：執行測試或引用現有資料（6.3）— 優先使用體外試驗，最小化動物試驗
  • Step 5：解讀資料（Section 7）— 毒理專家進行整合評估
  • Step 6：撰寫生物評估報告 — 文件化所有決策邏輯和結論

6.2 缺口分析（Gap Analysis）— 最關鍵的一步

缺口分析是生物評估流程的核心，也是最容易被忽視的步驟。它的邏輯是：

  • 先列出 Annex A 要求的資料集（根據器材分類）
  • 再盤點手上已有的資料（供應商文件、文獻、歷史測試）
  • 比對兩者的差距 → 這就是「缺口」
  • 決定如何填補缺口：追加資料？ 新測試？ 風險評估豁免？

重要提醒：藥典（BP/USP）的塑膠材料測試是針對原料，ISO 10993 評估的是最終器材狀態。藥典合格不代表可以直接替代 ISO 10993 測試，仍需說明理由（justification）。

類比：健康檢查的分項檢驗

缺口分析就像醫生排健康檢查項目：不是所有人都需要做全套項目。

  • 20 歲健康男性：基本血液、尿液分析（有限接觸，低風險）
  • 50 歲有家族史：加做腸鏡、心臟超音波（更長接觸，更高風險）
  • 已有症狀：針對性深度檢查（已知問題需填補）

ISO 10993 的邏輯：器材接觸越深、越久，需要評估的端點越多，但有舊資料就不需要重做。

E&L（Extractables & Leachables）在 CDMO 的核心地位

對於藥品 CDMO，6.1 提到的「extractables and leachables」是最直接相關的評估內容。

Extractables（可萃取物）：在極端實驗室條件下，從容器/封閉系統中可以萃取出的化學物質。這是最大可能的暴露量估算。

Leachables（可瀝出物）：在實際使用條件下（正常儲存、溫度、時間），實際遷移進入藥品的物質。這是病人實際暴露量。

評估流程：Extractables 研究 → 識別關鍵化合物 → 預測 Leachables → 與毒理閾值（TTC / SCT）比較 → 決定是否需要進一步測試。

監管標準：ICH Q3D、ISO 10993-17（毒理閾值）、ISO 10993-18（化學表徵）。

6.3.1 — 測試樣品必須是「最終器材」

這是許多廠商容易犯錯的地方：6.3.1a 明確要求測試樣品必須是最終器材狀態，包括滅菌後。

常見錯誤：

  • 用原料橡膠做測試，但實際產品橡膠已硫化、塗矽油 → 不符合要求
  • 滅菌前的樣品測試，未考慮 EtO 殘留或 gamma 輻射對材料的影響 → 需重新測試
  • 縮小版模型替代實際器材 → 需確認表面積/體重比的合理性（6.3.1b.5）

測試端點的風險分層邏輯 — CDMO 應用

  1. 所有藥品容器系統都需考慮：細胞毒性（Cytotoxicity）、刺激性（Irritation）
  2. 靜脈注射用途額外需要：血液相容性（Haemocompatibility）、熱原性（Pyrogenicity）
  3. 長期使用或植入額外需要：亞急性/慢性毒性、致癌性風險評估
  4. 可降解材料需要：降解試驗（Degradation）、毒物動力學（Toxicokinetics）
  5. 免疫原性未知材料：免疫毒性（Immunotoxicology）

7 Interpretation of Biological Evaluation Data and Overall Biological Risk Assessment

Expert assessors who have the necessary knowledge and experience shall determine and document:

  1. a) The strategy and planned content for the biological evaluation of the medical device
  2. b) The criteria for determining the acceptability of the material for the intended purpose, in line with the risk management plan
  3. c) The adequacy of the material characterization
  4. d) The rationale for selection and/or waiving of tests
  5. e) The interpretation of existing data and results of testing
  6. f) The need for any additional data to complete the biological evaluation
  7. g) Overall biological safety conclusions for the medical device
Informative Annex A gives the general endpoints that should be considered for each device contact type and duration category in a biological risk assessment.

第七節解析 — 評估資料解讀與風險評估

「生物評估報告」的核心要素

Section 7 的重點不是「做了哪些測試」，而是「有資格的人對所有資料做出了有文件記錄的判斷」。輸出文件稱為 Biological Evaluation Report（BER），它必須包含：

  • 評估策略與範圍（a）
  • 可接受性標準（b）— 量化或定性的安全閾值
  • 材料表徵充分性聲明（c）
  • 每項測試選做或豁免的理由（d）— 這是稽核重點
  • 對現有資料的解讀（e）
  • 是否仍有資料缺口（f）
  • 整體生物安全結論（g）— 明確聲明材料是否適合預期用途

誰可以撰寫 BER？資格要求

標準要求 expert assessors — 在實際應用中，這通常意味著：

  • 具有毒理學背景（toxicologist，理想上是 ERT/DABT 等認證）
  • 了解 ISO 10993 系列標準的技術人員
  • 有器材生物安全評估實際經驗

對 CDMO QA 的啟示：CDMO 不需要自己的毒理學家，但需要能審查客戶提交的 BER 是否符合此框架要求。如果 BER 缺少 d 項（豁免理由）或 g 項（整體結論），應要求補件。

類比：法院判決 vs. 證據收集

Section 6 的測試過程就像收集法庭證據，而 Section 7 是法官基於所有證據做出最終裁決。

BER 就是這份判決書：它整合了所有化學資料、測試結果、文獻引用，並由「法官」（毒理學專家）寫下有理由的結論。

如果稽核員看到 BER 只有測試數據表格而沒有專家解讀，這就等於只有「物證清單」沒有「判決書」——這不符合 ISO 10993-1 的要求。

CDMO QA 審查客戶 BER 的重點清單

  1. 簽署人資格是否載明（toxicologist / qualified assessor）？
  2. 是否列出所有接觸材料（包含 CDMO 使用的容器/包材型號）？
  3. 每個測試豁免項目是否有書面理由？
  4. E&L 研究結果是否已與毒理閾值（TTC/SCT）比對？
  5. 整體安全結論是否明確——是「適合預期用途（fit for intended purpose）」的正面聲明？
  6. BER 版本是否涵蓋目前在 CDMO 使用的容器/封閉系統版本（非舊版器材）？
  7. 若 CDMO 更換過任何包材規格，客戶是否已更新 BER（對應 4.9 觸發條件）？

總結：Section 4-7 的邏輯鏈

完整的生物評估思路：

  • Section 4：確立生物評估是風險管理的一部分，現有資料優先，專家主導
  • Section 5：分類器材 → 接觸性質（表面/外部通訊/植入）× 接觸時間（A/B/C）
  • Section 6：收集物化資料 → 缺口分析 → 測試或引用資料（15個端點，按需選用）
  • Section 7：毒理專家整合所有資料，形成有文件記錄的 BER，並聲明整體安全結論

這四個 Section 共同構成了 ISO 10993-1 的骨幹，後面各 Part（10993-2 至 10993-20）都是在執行具體的測試方法，而此框架定義了「為什麼選這個測試」的決策邏輯。

A.1 解讀：框架 vs. 清單

核心概念：評估框架，不是測試清單

Table A.1 最常被誤用的方式是：把它當作「必做測試的勾選表」。ISO 10993-1:2018 的立場恰恰相反——它是一份端點評估框架（endpoint evaluation framework）。

意思是：看到 E，你需要評估這個端點，但評估的方式可以是：

  • 查閱現有文獻或毒理資料庫
  • 引用同材質已核准器械的安全資料
  • 做新的測試（當既有資料不足時）
  • 撰寫正當理由說明為何不需新測試

這是一個重大的哲學轉變——從「規定測試」到「風險推理」。

比喻：健康檢查的「項目選單」

把 Table A.1 想像成一家診所的健康檢查項目選單。醫生會根據你的年齡、性別、病史，勾選哪些項目「應該評估」（E）。但不是每個人都要做每一項檢查——有些人已有近期的血液報告（既有資料），就不需要再抽血。

X 則像是「基本資料」（血壓、身高體重）——每次看診都需要，是所有後續評估的前提條件。

X 與 E 的本質差異

  • X（物理/化學資訊）：永遠需要，是所有生物評估的先決條件。你必須先知道材料是什麼，才能評估它對人體有什麼影響。這包含材料組成、分子量、可萃取物（extractables）、可滲出物（leachables）資料。
  • E（待評估端點）：需要評估，但不代表需要做體外或體內試驗。評估者必須主動說明「此端點我已評估完畢，結論是……」，而非沉默略過。
  • 空白欄位：對於特定分類與接觸時間，該端點通常不需要評估。但如有特殊情況（如器械材料含已知致癌物），仍需在風險評估中說明。

新穎材料的特殊規則

若器械使用全新材料（novel materials）——即從未用於醫療器械、無毒理文獻可查——則 Table A.1 標記 E 的端點只是最低要求，評估者可能需要增加額外端點。

這是標準對創新材料的謹慎立場：資料空白不等於安全，要主動設計評估方案。

Physical & Chemical Information All device categories, all contact types, all durations

Irritation / Intracutaneous Reactivity Externally communicating devices — indirect blood path, long-term duration

Components with long-term indirect contact with blood (e.g. infusion systems) can introduce irritants into the body/systemic circulation that should be addressed as part of the biological risk assessment.

Material-mediated Pyrogenicity Breached/compromised surface contact — all durations

Extractables/leachables can be introduced to the systemic circulation through breached or compromised surfaces, and therefore both material-mediated pyrogenicity and acute systemic toxicity should be considered.

Material-mediated Pyrogenicity Externally communicating & implant devices — all contact types, all durations

Extractables/leachables can be introduced to the systemic circulation, lymphatic system, and/or cerebrospinal fluid, and therefore material-mediated pyrogenicity should be considered.

Acute Systemic Toxicity Mucosal membrane contact — prolonged and long-term contact; tissue/bone/dentin — limited duration; implant tissue/bone — limited duration

Extractables/leachables can be introduced via mucosal membranes and to the systemic circulation, lymphatic system, and/or cerebrospinal fluid, and therefore acute systemic toxicity should be considered.

Subacute Toxicity All medical device types with prolonged and long-term contact

For medical devices/components with more than 24 hours use, extractables/leachables can be introduced to the systemic circulation, lymphatic system, and/or cerebrospinal fluid, and therefore subacute toxicity should be considered.

Subchronic & Chronic Toxicity All medical device types with long-term contact (>30 days)

For medical devices/components with at least 30 day use, extractables/leachables can be introduced to the systemic circulation, lymphatic system, and/or cerebrospinal fluid, and therefore subchronic and chronic toxicity should be considered.

Implantation Effects Mucosal membrane & breached/compromised surface — prolonged and long-term contact

For medical devices/components with this type of contact, local and systemic effects from implantation should be considered. For medical devices/components where repeat use could change the category from limited to prolonged or long-term duration, information on potential for accumulation of chemicals in the tissue can be used to inform whether implantation testing should be considered.

Implantation Effects Externally communicating devices — indirect blood path, long-term duration

Components with long-term indirect contact with blood (e.g. infusion systems) can introduce extractables/leachables into the blood stream that could impact the inflammatory response to direct contacting components of the medical device (if applicable). If literature is available to address systemic toxicity of all extractables/leachables, and there is no direct contacting component, it is possible that implantation assessment will not be needed for this category.

Genotoxicity Externally communicating devices — circulating blood, limited duration; implant devices — blood contact, limited duration

For medical devices/components used in extracorporeal circuits, extractables/leachables can be introduced into the blood stream and remain after the medical device is removed, and therefore genotoxicity should be considered.

Carcinogenicity Surface medical devices — breached/compromised surface, long-term; all externally communicating & implant devices — long-term duration

Extractables/leachables can be introduced into systemic circulation, lymphatic system, and/or cerebrospinal fluid, and therefore carcinogenicity should be addressed as part of the biological risk assessment.

Table A.1 在 CDMO 的應用解讀

比喻：Table A.1 是「端點菜單」，不是「強制點餐」

想像你是餐廳客人，Table A.1 給你一張菜單，標注哪些菜「建議點」（E）。但你不需要每道菜都點——如果你已經吃過某道菜（有現成資料），就不用再點。重點是你要說明你為什麼沒點，而不是沉默跳過。

X 欄則是「進門前必須結帳」——是前提條件，不是選擇。

CDMO 實務：常見器械分類對照

  1. 玻璃瓶（vials）+ 橡膠塞：藥液從不直接接觸血液，而是通過護士/患者注射進入體內。分類視藥品類型而定：靜脈注射藥品 → 間接血液路徑接觸（Indirect blood path）；注射用水 → 同上；外用製劑 → 表面接觸（完整皮膚或黏膜）。
  2. 預充注射器（prefilled syringes）：針頭直接接觸血管 → 循環血液直接接觸（Circulating blood contact）。接觸時間通常 <24 h（A 類）。這意味著需要評估血液相容性（Hemocompatibility）端點。
  3. 輸液袋 + 輸液管：液體流經管路後進入靜脈 → 間接血液路徑（Indirect blood path）。接觸時間可能達數小時到數天（B 類）。需評估材料致熱原性（Material-mediated pyrogenicity）。
  4. 組合產品（combination products）：藥品+器械一體，器械部分的生物相容性仍須依 ISO 10993-1 評估。CDMO 有責任協助客戶判斷分類。

A.2 的深層意義：2018 版本的哲學轉變

2009 版 (第四版) 的 Table A.1 端點較少，且隱含「E = 需要做測試」的思維。2018 版做了三項重大轉變：

  • 新增端點：物理/化學資訊、材料致熱原性、急性全身毒性等成為明確要求，反映對 E&L（可萃取物/可滲出物）全身毒性風險的更深認識。
  • 語言轉變：「X = 需要測試」改為「E = 需要評估端點」，明確區分「評估」與「測試」。
  • 路徑多元化：評估可透過現有資料、文獻、或正當理由達成，不必每次都做新的動物實驗或體外試驗。

材料致熱原性（Material-mediated Pyrogenicity）：為何重要？

傳統的 LAL 試驗（Limulus Amebocyte Lysate）針對的是細菌內毒素（endotoxins），無法偵測到來自材料本身的非細菌熱原物質。

2018 版 ISO 10993-1 特別新增此端點，要求評估器械材料（如橡膠塞、塑膠管、矽膠）本身是否會在體內引發發熱反應。這對 CDMO 特別重要：客戶若使用新供應商的包材，即使批批通過 LAL 試驗，仍可能有材料致熱原性風險。

評估方式：可使用MAT（Monocyte Activation Test）或進行萃取物熱原試驗。

累積毒性 → 慢性毒性 + 致癌性的邏輯鏈

A.2 的核心邏輯：接觸時間越長 → 可滲出物進入全身循環越多 → 累積風險越高 → 需要評估更多端點。

  • <24 h：關注直接局部反應（細胞毒性、致敏性、刺激性）
  • 24 h–30 d：新增急性全身毒性、亞急性毒性
  • >30 d：追加慢性毒性、致癌性、生殖毒性、降解評估

這個邏輯鏈解釋了為什麼長期植入物（如人工關節、心臟瓣膜）需要最完整的生物相容性評估包。

風險評估實務：如何對應 E 欄位？

  1. 確認器械分類（表面/外部連通/植入）及接觸部位。
  2. 確認接觸時間（A/B/C）。
  3. 找出 Table A.1 對應行的所有 E 欄位。
  4. 對每個 E 端點，搜尋現有材料資料（如供應商 CoA、REACH SVHC 清單、同類器械上市資料）。
  5. 若現有資料充足 → 撰寫端點評估結論，不需新測試。
  6. 若資料不足 → 設計測試方案（參閱 ISO 10993 系列對應部分）。
  7. 所有判斷（評估或豁免）都需記錄在生物風險評估報告中。

A.2 各端點新增的核心理由（速查）

核心原則：E&L 進入人體後怎麼走？

A.2 的每條理由都遵循同一邏輯：可萃取物/可滲出物（Extractables/Leachables, E&L）可以透過以下途徑進入人體——

  • 黏膜（mucosal membranes）→ 全身循環
  • 受損皮膚（breached surface）→ 全身循環
  • 血液路徑（indirect/circulating blood）→ 全身循環
  • 淋巴系統、脊髓液（lymphatic / cerebrospinal fluid）

一旦進入全身循環，就可能造成：急性毒性、亞慢性毒性、基因毒性、致癌性……端點清單隨接觸時間與途徑逐步擴大。

外部迴路（Extracorporeal Circuits）的特殊風險

血液透析機、ECMO（體外膜氧合）、體外循環機（CPB）等器械屬於「循環血液直接接觸」類別，且在移除器械後 E&L 仍殘留在血液中。

Annex B (Informative) — Guidance on the Conduct of Biological Evaluation within a Risk Management Process

B.1 Background Information

B.1.1 General

This annex provides guidance on conduct of biological evaluation of medical devices according to the requirements of this document. Although this document provides a general framework for biological evaluation of medical devices, more detailed guidance can be helpful in the practical application of this document. As a result, this Annex was developed to provide such guidance to users.

Biological evaluation is a design verification activity which is set in the context of broader risk management processes. Therefore this annex includes guidance on the application of this document in the context of risk management processes conducted according to the requirements of ISO 14971.

As scientific knowledge advances our understanding of the basic mechanisms of tissue responses, the way biological evaluation is carried out can also change, moving towards an evaluation based upon review of relevant established scientific data and upon physical and chemical characterization and testing, with testing only being carried out where these are required to fill gaps in our understanding.

This document specifies a framework in which to plan a biological evaluation which minimizes the number and exposure of test animals by giving preference to identifying chemical constituent testing and in vitro models in situations where these methods yield equally relevant information to that obtained from in vivo models.

When judging the applicability of the guidance in this annex, applicable regulatory requirements and regulatory guidance should be considered. An organization can voluntarily incorporate guidance from this annex, wholly or in part, into its risk management process.

B.1.2 Relationship with Other Standards and Regulatory Requirements

The relationship between this document, this annex and the standards for biological evaluation of medical devices and general risk management is summarized as follows:

  • This annex provides guidance on the application of this document;
  • Biological evaluation is a component of risk management and this Annex includes guidance on the application of ISO 14971 to the conduct of biological evaluation.

This annex does not add to, or otherwise change, the requirements of this document. This annex does not include requirements to be used as the basis of regulatory inspection or certification assessment activities.

中文解析 — Annex B 總覽與定位

Annex B 是什麼？

如果說 ISO 10993-1 本文是「What（要做什麼）」，那 Annex B 就是「How（怎麼做）」。它把整份標準的框架翻譯成一套可操作的風險管理作業流程，是整個 ISO 10993-1 最有實務價值的章節。

關鍵定位：Annex B 是 informative（資訊性），不是 normative（規範性）。也就是說，它不能被用來作為法規稽核或認證評估的直接依據，但卻是理解整套系統最重要的參考文件。

類比：設計驗證活動 vs. 品質管制

生物評估是「設計驗證活動」（design verification activity）。這個定位非常重要 —— 它意味著生物安全性的評估應該在產品設計階段就開始，而不是等到生產後才做。

類比：就像結構工程師在設計建築時就要算承重，而不是蓋好之後再去測試它會不會塌。CDMO 的角色就是在 tech transfer 的起點，就要問客戶：「你的生物風險評估做到哪一步了？」

科學進化趨勢：減少動物實驗

Annex B 明確指出一個重要趨勢：生物評估正在朝向「以化學表徵與文獻審查為主、動物實驗為輔」的方向演進。當 in vitro 方法能提供同等相關資訊時，應優先採用。

這對 CDMO 的實務意義：建立完整的材料表徵資料庫，有機會減少高成本、長週期的動物測試，加速開發時程。

與 ISO 14971 的關係

Annex B 的整個架構都建立在 ISO 14971（醫療器材風險管理）之上。生物評估不是獨立存在的 —— 它是整體風險管理計劃（Risk Management Plan, RMP）的一個子集。

換言之：Biological Evaluation Plan = RMP 的一部分，而不是一份獨立文件。

B.2 Biological Evaluation as a Risk Management Practice

B.2.1 General

B.2 and B.3 describe a continuous process by which a manufacturer can identify the biological hazards associated with medical devices, estimate and evaluate the risks, control these risks, and monitor the effectiveness of the control. Appropriate protection of the patient should be achieved by implementing a biological evaluation plan which includes, as an essential element, the weighing of the risks and benefits of medical devices.

Consideration of biological risk is only one aspect of the risk assessment of a medical device, which should consider all aspects of risk. For example, it can be possible that the most biologically safe material available can have unacceptable mechanical strength, in which case it would be necessary to consider if an alternate stronger material is of acceptable biological safety.

Material selection and risk analysis are integral components of the design process for medical devices. In line with ISO 13485 and ISO 14971, criteria to define the acceptable biological risk should be established at the start of the design process.

Because starting material, formulation and processing variations including packaging, transportation and aging could impact final product biocompatibility, these considerations should also be incorporated into the risk assessment.

Key steps in hazard identification (material characterization):

  • Define and characterize each material, including suitable alternative materials;
  • Identify hazards in materials, additives, processing aids, etc.;
  • Identify the potential effect of downstream processing (e.g. chemical interactions, final product sterilization) on chemicals present in final product;
  • Identify the chemicals that could be released during product use (e.g. intermediate or final degradation products);
  • Estimate exposure (total or clinically available amounts);
  • Review toxicology and other biological safety data (published/available).

Information on biological safety to be reviewed includes:

  • Toxicology data on relevant component materials/compounds;
  • Information on prior use of relevant component materials/compounds;
  • Data from biological tests.

If it can be concluded from existing data that risks are acceptable, then no additional testing is needed to support biological safety. When existing data are insufficient, additional information should be obtained. The purpose of testing is to obtain additional data which can assist in reaching a conclusion.

The evaluation should be documented in a report that indicates the identity and significance of all relevant evidence and highlights the scientific basis of the overall conclusions in an accurate, clear and transparent manner.

B.2.2 The Biological Evaluation Plan

ISO 14971:2007, 3.4, requires that risk management activities be planned in advance. Since biological evaluation is a risk management activity, a Biological Evaluation Plan is required, and this forms part of the Risk Management Plan.

It is emphasized that simply planning to conduct testing against all of the aspects of biocompatibility identified in Annex A does not meet the requirements of ISO 14971 or this document.

The biological evaluation plan should be drawn up by a knowledgeable and experienced team and include as a minimum:

  • Arrangements for gathering of applicable information from the published literature (including information sources and search strategies), in-house and supplier data and other sources in order to conduct risk analysis;
  • Arrangements for conducting the evaluation, including the requirement for any specific technical competencies relevant to the specific medical device application;
  • Arrangements for review and approval of the plan as part of the overall design control process;
  • Arrangements for review of the final conclusions of the evaluation and the approval of any additional testing required;
  • Arrangements for the final review and approval of the outcomes of the biological risk assessment, including the risk control measures applied and the documentation of any residual risks and the disclosure of residual risks through means such as product labelling.

B.3 Guidance on Risk Management

B.3.1 Risk Assessment

B.3.1.1 Introduction

Risk assessment is the combination of the processes of risk analysis in which risks are identified and estimated, and risk evaluation in which risks are evaluated to identify those which require mitigation (risk control).

B.3.1.2 Risk Analysis

Risk analysis is the process of identifying the specific hazards and assessing their significance. In a biological evaluation, one important consideration is the potential toxicity of materials components and their route of exposure. Another important consideration is how physical properties could affect the biological response.

Risk analysis begins with identification and characterization of the indirect and direct tissue-contacting materials and components of the medical device. This should be done based on the final form of the medical device in its manufactured state, taking into account the presence of any manufacturing additives, processing aids or other potential contaminants such as sterilant residues.

Physical and chemical material properties relevant to biological safety include:

  • Wear, load, fatigue (especially in load-bearing devices such as total joint prostheses — associated production of particulates or materials degradation);
  • Friction and associated irritation (e.g. in applications such as catheters);
  • Interactions between material combinations (chemical interactions), e.g. galvanic corrosion, abrasion;
  • Heat (e.g. thermal degradation or thermally induced material changes);
  • Manufacturing processes (e.g. internal stresses promoting environmental stress cracking, morphological changes, or degradation);
  • Environmental interactions (e.g. endoscope in stomach acids, dressings in external environment, UV-light, detergents, decontamination and sterilization);
  • Electricity (e.g. short circuits, degradation, heating, muscle stimulation);
  • Potential interactions between components;
  • Effect of physical form, e.g. particulates, including nanomaterials;
  • Processing; transportation and aging.

Materials information can be obtained through review of literature, vendor data, in-house data or comparison with existing products on the market where the manufacturing processes and formulations are known and the same as in the medical device under evaluation.

B.3.1.3 Risk Estimation

From a chemical toxicity perspective, risk estimation includes consideration of the anticipated exposure, e.g. the bioavailability of leachable or soluble components (see ISO 10993-17). From a material property perspective, risk estimation also includes exposure that might be anticipated due to the use of the medical device.

Risk is typically estimated by assigning values to the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm. In general toxicological terms, likelihood can be estimated from knowledge of the actual availability of toxic components and the known dose response in relevant tissue(s).

If insufficient information is available from published literature, in-house data and documented clinical history, risk estimation can require conduct of chemical or physical characterization or biological testing to estimate or quantify the hazards which cannot be satisfactorily determined from prior knowledge.

The amount of data required for risk analysis and the depth of the analysis will vary with the intended use and are dependent upon the nature and duration of tissue contact. Data requirements are usually less stringent for materials with indirect patient contact.

B.3.1.4 Risk Evaluation

Risk evaluation builds upon the risk analysis, taking the next step of evaluating the risks defined in the risk analysis for their significance and identifying requirements and opportunities for mitigation (risk control). It should be realized that for a full evaluation the whole medical device should be taken into consideration including all its components.

Biocompatibility can only be demonstrated for a particular material in relation to a defined set of circumstances, which include the purpose for which it is used and the tissues with which it comes into contact.

Of particular importance is consideration of any history of clinical use or human exposure data from relevant similar applications. For example, clinical studies showing a final product is a non-irritant might be useful in justifying omission of animal irritation studies.

It is critical for the integrity of a biological risk evaluation that it should be conducted by assessors with the necessary knowledge and expertise to determine the appropriate strategy for the evaluation.

B.3.2 Risk Control

Risk control is the process of identifying and implementing measures to reduce risks. Examples of possible strategies include:

  • Design changes to avoid more hazardous exposure routes or reduce exposure time;
  • Design changes to optimize geometric surface properties to minimize areas where low blood flow could result in thrombus formation;
  • Design changes to avoid medical device failures (e.g. particulation, or coating delamination) that could result in adverse biological responses;
  • Reduction of toxicity by means of reformulation or materials change;
  • Changes to production processes to reduce or eliminate hazardous residues or process additives.

It is emphasized that conducting animal testing for risk reduction should only be considered after all alternative courses of action (review of prior knowledge, chemical or physical characterization, in vitro evaluation or alternatives means of risk mitigation) have been exhausted.

B.3.3 Evaluation of Residual Risk Acceptability

Following risk analysis and evaluation and the implementation of risk controls, it is necessary to review the findings of these preceding activities and to document the residual risk and to decide on any further disclosure of such residual risks, for example through appropriate labelling, cautions or warnings.

B.3.4 Post-Production Monitoring

The processes of risk assessment are based upon human judgement using the available information, supplemented by biological testing where required. This assessment should be updated as needed with new information that becomes available from post market monitoring of medical device performance and safety in actual clinical use. Monitoring should include both trends in adverse events associated with the specific medical device, plus new information which arises in relation to other relevant similar medical devices or materials. Monitoring should also include ongoing review of relevant scientific literature.

ISO 14971 vs. ISO 10993-1：風險管理映射

風險分析起點：最終製造態（Final Form）

B.3.1.2 強調一個關鍵細節：風險分析必須基於「醫療器材在製造完成狀態下的最終形式」，不是材料原料的狀態。

CDMO 類比：你不是在評估空瓶子的安全性，而是評估裝有藥液、經過滅菌、在包裝材料保護下、歷經運輸老化之後的完整產品。這意味著滅菌殘留物（sterilant residues）、包裝材料的 extractables，都必須納入考量。

B.3.1.3 風險估算：E&L 的核心地位

風險估算中最關鍵的化學毒性評估路徑是：

  • Extractables：在最嚴苛萃取條件下，材料能釋放什麼？（潛在上限）
  • Leachables：在實際使用條件下，材料真正釋放多少？（臨床實際暴露量）
  • Bioavailability：釋放的物質有多少被患者吸收進入全身循環？

這三個數字串聯起來，就是 Dose（劑量），然後才能與 Tolerable Intake (TI) 比較，得出風險估算結論。

CDMO 關鍵連結：容器密封系統資格確認

你的容器密封系統（Container-Closure System）資格確認研究 —— 包括 E&L 測試、相容性測試 —— 直接為 B.3.1.3 的風險估算步驟提供輸入資料。

換言之：CCS 驗證不是獨立的品管活動，它是生物風險評估的數據來源之一。未來向客戶解釋為什麼要做 E&L 研究時，可以用這個框架說明其法規必要性。

生物相容性的「情境依賴性」

B.3.1.4 說得很清楚：生物相容性只能在「特定情境下」對特定材料加以論證，這個情境包括用途和接觸組織。

類比：同一種矽膠，用在皮膚接觸的醫療貼片上，和用在長期植入的骨科器材上，需要的生物安全性數據完全不同。不能拿皮膚接觸的數據去支持長期植入的應用。

對 CDMO 來說，這意味著：客戶提供的材料安全數據是否「情境相符」，必須仔細審查。

風險控制的優先順序（3R 精神）

B.3.2 明確規定風險控制的優先順序：

  1. 設計變更（Design change）—— 最優先
  2. 重新配方 / 材料替換（Reformulation / Material substitution）
  3. in vitro 測試提供更準確的風險估算
  4. 警語 / 禁忌症標示（Warnings / Contraindications）
  5. 動物測試 —— 最後手段，且只在其他方法都窮盡後才考慮

這個順序體現了 3R 原則（Replace / Reduce / Refine）的精神。

上市後監測：生物評估不是一次性活動

B.3.4 強調：生物評估是持續性的。上市後必須持續監測：

  1. 本產品的不良事件趨勢
  2. 同類材料或類似器材的相關新資訊
  3. 最新科學文獻的持續回顧

這個要求與 MDR/IVDR 的 Post-Market Clinical Follow-up (PMCF) 要求直接對應，也是 FDA 510(k) 上市後要求的一部分。

B.4 Guidance on Specific Aspects of Biological Evaluation

B.4.1 Material Characterization

B.4.1.1 Chemical Characterization

From a practical perspective, chemical characterization data are most useful in a biological assessment when:

  • Issues of a proprietary nature can be resolved;
  • Only one or a small number of chemical constituents are changed in a medical device;
  • Toxicity data are readily available for the chemical constituent(s);
  • Extraction/analytical chemistry studies are easily conducted.

B.4.1.2 Use of Chemical Characterization Data in a Biological Evaluation

There are several clauses in this document that ask the user to conduct a chemical characterization of the medical device undergoing the biological evaluation. From a hazard identification standpoint, information on the compounds released from the medical device can be useful in selecting appropriate biological evaluation tests. Chemical characterization data can also be useful for risk estimation — if data are available on the rate at which a compound is released from the medical device under conditions that simulate the in-use environment, and if sufficient data are available to derive a relevant toxicological threshold, then it is possible to compare the dose received to the relevant threshold or limit in assessing likelihood of adverse effects.

B.4.1.3 Proprietary Materials Formulations

Where the necessary data (e.g. complete formulation data) are not available to a manufacturer because of confidentiality of proprietary information, enquiries should be made with the materials supplier as to the availability of materials biological evaluations which can be relevant to the proposed application. In some cases it is possible to manage confidentiality of proprietary formulations by means of separate lodgement by the manufacturer of biological evaluation data with an independent assessor or regulatory agency (known as a "Master File" in some jurisdictions).

B.4.1.4 Physical Characterization

For particulates and nanomaterials physical characterization is necessary, as described for nanomaterials in ISO/TR 10993-22. In addition, physical form (e.g. geometry, particle size, porosity, surface texture) can have a substantial effect on biological interactions with the medical device and can impact safety. Examples include:

  • Evaluation of geometry on blood flow and haemocompatibility;
  • Evaluation of porosity on tissue ingrowth;
  • Evaluation of release of wear particles on local and distant tissue responses;
  • Evaluation of surface texture (topography) on cell adhesion, phenotypic expression and growth.

B.4.1.5 Effects of Manufacturing Processes

It is important to consider the effect of manufacturing conditions on materials as well as the use of additives or presence of contaminants. Materials testing should have been carried out on materials test samples which have been processed (including sterilization if applicable) in equivalent ways to the materials included in the final medical device. Particular aspects which should be considered include:

  • Processes which can cause either bulk or surface changes in materials properties (e.g. moulding, surface treatment, welding or machining);
  • Intended additives or processing aids, such as catalysts, antioxidants, pigments, surface treatments;
  • Potential process contaminants (e.g. cleaning/disinfection/sterilization agents, mould release agents, cutting fluids and particles, lubricants);
  • Degradation during manufacturing and processing, clinical use and storage;
  • Potential process residuals of chemicals and additives.

B.4.2 Collection of Existing Data

Before a data gap analysis can be conducted, the extent of relevant data should be determined, including:

  • Toxicology data on component materials or constituent or other relevant compounds (Annex C);
  • Existing biological safety data on component materials or products;
  • Data on history of clinical use or human exposure.

B.4.3 Device Testing Considerations

B.4.3.1 Tiered Approaches to Biological Testing

When it is found to be necessary to conduct additional testing to gather further data, a tiered approach should be taken. Testing should begin with chemical and physical characterization and in vitro screens. The results of the characterization and testing should be reviewed before proceeding to animal testing.

B.4.3.2 When to Do Long-Term Testing

The need to conduct long-term testing requires specific consideration and justification. Factors that can contribute to a justification for not carrying out long-term testing:

  • Exposure quantity (i.e. total device/material mass per patient);
  • Time;
  • Bioavailability.

Situations likely to indicate a need for long-term testing:

  • The quantity of material present and the length of exposure indicate that long-term toxicological effects could be of concern;
  • Constituent compounds are known to be, or considered likely to be, toxic;
  • There are insufficient prior data for the material concerned in equivalent long-term applications;
  • There are specific chemical reasons (e.g. particular molecular structures of concern) indicating particular chronic toxicological concerns;
  • Shorter term screens (e.g. genotoxicity screens) indicate potential for concern;
  • There are known concerns regarding biostability for the particular class of material of interest.

B.4.3.3 In Vitro System pH and Osmolality Compensation for Absorbable Materials

Polymeric, metallic, or ceramic materials that are intended to be absorbed will release soluble components or degradation products. If the release rate is sufficiently rapid, elevated concentrations of released products could alter the pH and/or osmolality of an in vitro test system. It is possible that adjustment of the pH and/or osmolality of an in vitro test system will be necessary to maintain physiologically relevant conditions. Results from both the standard assay and adjusted assay should be compared, as some modifications can mask important considerations.

中文解析 — 材料表徵與測試策略

化學表徵的戰略價值

B.4.1.1 列出化學表徵最「物超所值」的使用情境。對 CDMO 而言，最常見的場景是：客戶改了一個原料成分，此時化學表徵可以幫助我們「聚焦」——只需評估那個被改變的成分的生物安全性，而不需要重做所有測試。

這大幅降低了變更管理（Change Control）的成本和時間。

專有配方的解法：Master File

B.4.1.3 提到一個很實用的機制：當供應商的配方是機密時，可以讓供應商把生物評估數據直接提交給主管機關（以 Master File 形式），由 CDMO 或藥廠在法規申請中引用，監管機關可以保密審查。

這個機制在 FDA 稱為 Drug Master File（DMF），在歐盟稱為 European Pharmacopoeia Master File（EDMF）。雖然 ISO 10993-1 講的是醫療器材語境，但概念是相通的。

製造製程對生物相容性的影響：CDMO 必須重視

B.4.1.5 是對 CDMO 最直接相關的條款之一。它說：測試樣品的處理方式必須與最終產品的材料處理方式「等效」，包括滅菌。

實務含義：

  • 如果你用 EtO 滅菌，生物相容性測試的樣品也必須經過 EtO 滅菌後再測
  • 如果換了滅菌方式（如從 EtO 改為 gamma），需要重新評估
  • 清潔劑殘留、模具脫模劑殘留都是潛在污染物，必須納入考量

奈米材料的特殊注意事項

B.4.1.4 指出奈米材料（nanomaterials）需要特別的物理表徵，因為次微米尺寸的材料可能表現出與大尺寸相同材料截然不同的生物行為。這意味著不能直接外推大尺寸材料的安全數據到奈米材料。

對 CDMO：如果你的製程涉及奈米粒子藥物製劑（如 LNP for mRNA vaccines），這個條款直接適用。

分層測試策略（Tiered Approach）

B.4.3.1 規定了測試的正確順序，必須遵守這個優先級：

  1. 物理與化學表徵（Physical & Chemical Characterization）
  2. 體外測試 / in vitro 篩選
  3. 動物測試（必須先完成前兩步並評估結果，才能進行）

直接跳到動物測試是不符合 ISO 10993-1 要求的，也違反 3R 原則。監管機關越來越嚴格審查這個順序的遵從性。

長期測試的決策邏輯

B.4.3.2 給出了「不需要」做長期測試的理由框架：如果你能證明暴露量極低（低於毒理學閾值），暴露時間短，且生物利用度低，就可以有理由豁免長期測試。

這個邏輯就是所謂的 Risk-based Justification —— 用數據論證為什麼不需要測試，而不是隨意跳過測試。

反之，如果材料有已知的毒性結構特徵（如特定的 genotoxic 官能基），或 genotoxicity 篩選顯示有問題，就必須做長期測試。

B.4.4 Biological Safety Assessment Methods

B.4.4.1 Use of Clinically Relevant Data for a Risk Assessment

A judgement on whether there are sufficient clinically relevant data for a risk assessment can be based on several factors, including whether all materials used in the medical device have a long history of safe use in the same application. Where the materials in the final medical device are chemically identical (taking into consideration both formulation and processing) to those used in existing medical devices, the nature of exposure is the same, and clinical information from targeted analyses is available for relevant biocompatibility endpoints, then a materials characterization-based approach to risk assessment can be justified to assess biological safety.

B.4.4.2 What Constitutes "Sufficient Toxicology Data"

Although it is possible to identify a number of chemical compounds released from a medical device in a chemical characterization scheme, it is likely that toxicity data will not be available for some compounds by the clinically relevant route of exposure. Methods are available to conduct a route-to-route extrapolation of dose, including PBPK modelling, but these approaches should be used with caution and portal-of-entry effects should be taken into consideration.

Caution is required in interpreting effects observed in in vitro tests at very high dose levels relative to the actual exposure in clinical use.

B.4.4.3 Determining the Acceptability of the Level of Leachable (Allowable Limit) According to ISO 10993-17

Risk characterization involves a comparison of the dose of the compound received by the patient or clinician to the "safe" dose or Tolerable Intake (TI) value for that compound. If dose/TI ratio is > 1, then there is an increased likelihood for adverse effects to occur. However, the dose/TI ratio should not be thought of as a "bright line" value — the greater the value of the dose/TI ratio, the greater the likelihood of adverse effects; however it is important to also take into consideration such factors as severity of adverse effects, the pharmacokinetics of the compound, and whether default or conservative assumptions were used to derive the TI.

B.4.4.4 Thresholds of Toxicological Concern (TTC)

When considering the presence in a material of potentially toxic components which are present at low concentrations and a tolerable intake (TI) cannot be derived from the literature, consideration should be given to the concept of a "threshold of toxicological concern". It is possible to establish by reference to the known toxic effects of the substance in question that the substance is present in sufficiently low amounts to not present a significant risk.

B.4.4.5 Guidance on Mixtures in Risk Assessment

ISO 10993-17 notes that patients or clinicians are rarely exposed to only one residue at a time. It is more likely that exposure occurs to multiple compounds released from the medical device. This co-exposure to multiple compounds has the potential to increase or decrease the toxicity of a constituent of the mixture. If compounds are structurally similar, they could result in an additive toxicological effect. For compounds that are structurally dissimilar, it is unknown whether chemicals might have an additive or inhibitory toxicological effect.

B.4.5 General Guidance on Changes and Documentation

B.4.5.1 Changes Which Can Require Re-evaluation of Biological Safety

Conventional medical device design practices require that a risk assessment be revisited when a design change occurs. Typical changes that could alter the biological performance of a material or final medical device include:

  • Processing (e.g. sterilization, cleaning, surface treatment, welding, injection moulding, primary packaging);
  • Material source (e.g. new vendor, new facility);
  • Material specification (e.g. wider tolerances, new specification);
  • Formulation (e.g. new materials, new additives, change in tolerances);
  • Storage conditions (e.g. longer shelf-life, wider tolerances, new transportation conditions);
  • Biological environment (i.e. change in clinical use).

The scope of re-evaluation should be appropriate to the nature of the change and should focus on the specific materials changed, the nature and use of the medical device and the potential interactions.

B.4.5.2 Good Laboratory Practice

Any testing to support a biological evaluation is expected to be an integral part of a manufacturer's quality management system and is therefore subject to the same requirements for validation and traceability as any quality control test. Quality systems controls applicable to non-clinical testing are known as Good Laboratory Practice (GLP). GLP studies are carried out to defined quality standards in laboratories that are accredited in line with an internationally implemented governmental scheme, typically compliant to ISO/IEC 17025 or an equivalent standard.

B.4.5.3 Biocompatibility Evaluation Documentation

Documentation for a biocompatibility evaluation should include, to the extent feasible and necessary:

  • A general description or drawing of the medical device;
  • Quantitative information on the material composition/formulations for all device components with direct or indirect contact;
  • Description of processing conditions that could introduce manufacturing contaminants;
  • A review of available toxicity and prior use data relevant to each medical device component;
  • Reports of biological tests;
  • An assessment of the data;
  • A statement confirming the risk analysis and risk controls have been completed.

The information collected should be incorporated in the medical device design documentation as part of the process of design control (ISO 13485:2016, Clause 7) and should also form part of the risk management file (ISO 14971:2007).

中文解析 — 安全評估方法與變更管理

「足夠的臨床數據」怎麼判斷？

B.4.4.1 提供了一個強有力的工具：如果一種材料在相同的應用中有長期安全使用歷史（且配方和製程完全相同），這就可以作為風險評估的充分依據，不需要重新做大量測試。

這就是 History of Safe Use 論證策略。對 CDMO 特別有價值的場景：客戶使用了業界已有幾十年使用歷史的標準輔料（如 polysorbate 80），可以援引文獻和臨床記錄來支持生物安全性，而無需做全套新測試。

Dose/TI 比值：不是硬性截止線

B.4.4.3 澄清了一個常見誤解：Dose/TI > 1 並不自動意味著「不合格」——它只是表示「風險升高的可能性」。評估人員必須同時考量：

  • 毒性反應的嚴重程度
  • 化合物的藥動學特性
  • 萃取條件是否保守（worst case）
  • TI 計算中使用的假設是否過於保守

這個彈性空間是有意設計的 —— 監管邏輯不是「有就不行」，而是「超出閾值需要更詳細的論證」。

混合物毒性：現實比模型複雜

B.4.4.5 承認了生物評估中最棘手的問題之一：患者同時暴露於多種 leachables，而不是單一化合物。

現實情況：目前業界主要採用 additive toxicity（加成毒性） 假設來處理混合物，即假設各化合物的效應可以直接加總。這是保守但務實的方法。ISO 10993-17 Annex B 有更詳細的混合物評估方法指引。

什麼樣的「變更」需要重做生物評估？

B.4.5.1 的變更清單對 CDMO 的 Change Control 程序直接相關：

  • 換供應商 → 需要重評
  • 換滅菌方式 → 需要重評
  • 改包裝材料 → 需要重評
  • 延長保質期 → 可能需要重評（更長的老化時間可能改變 leachables 量）
  • 臨床使用方式改變 → 需要重評

重評的範圍不需要是全套 —— 只需聚焦在「被改變的部分」以及「可能受影響的生物安全性端點」。

GLP 要求：什麼情況下必須用 GLP 實驗室？

B.4.5.2 要求生物評估測試必須符合 GLP，實驗室應通過 ISO/IEC 17025 認證。

實務含義：

  1. 委外測試時，必須確認 CRO 有適當的 GLP/GCP 認證
  2. 測試報告必須包含驗證和可追溯性記錄
  3. 數據的完整性（Data Integrity）要求同樣適用於生物評估

生物相容性評估文件：完整的文件包

B.4.5.3 列出的文件清單，就是監管機關在審查技術文件（Technical File）或 510(k) 時會查的內容。缺少任何一項都可能導致資訊補充要求（Deficiency Letter）。

最容易被忽略的項目：「確認風險分析和風險控制已完成的聲明」—— 這是一份正式的結案聲明，必須由具備資質的評估人員簽署。

Annex C (Informative) — Suggested Procedure for Literature Review

C.1 Introduction

A review and evaluation of the literature is essential for justification and planning of any biological evaluation of a material or a medical device. The aim of such a review is to determine scientific background for the biological evaluation. It also provides essential information for assessing risks/benefits and achieving the ethical conduct of the planned evaluation as required by ISO 10993-2.

Such a literature review can be helpful to assess whether the relevant data available in the literature are sufficient to demonstrate biological safety of the medical device in question without the need to generate further data from actual testing, or to conclude that the available data are not sufficient.

Performing a literature review is a scientific activity that should be done with rigour and objectivity, and should allow verification by third parties.

C.2 Methodology

C.2.1 General

Prior to performing the literature review, a plan should be established for the identification, selection, collation and review of all available studies/data. This plan should be documented and based on recognized practices for systematic review of the scientific literature.

C.2.2 Objectives

The objective(s) of the literature review should be clearly defined. The types of study that are relevant to these objectives should be specified, taking into account any already well-established knowledge of the material or medical device.

C.2.3 Selection Criteria for Documents

The criteria for selecting or excluding data should be defined with an appropriate rationale. Published data should be taken from recognized scientific publications. Preference may be given to GLP over non-GLP data. All available unpublished relevant data should also be taken into account in order to avoid publication bias. All data should be referenced.

The literature review should state the sources of literature and data, and the extent of the searches of databases or other compilation of information.

C.2.4 Assessment of Documents

A literature review should clearly assess the quality of the documents and the extent to which the literature relates to the specific characteristics and features of the material or medical device under consideration, taking into account the intended use of the medical device.

The following should be considered:

  • Similarity of the material or medical device in the selected documents to the medical device under consideration based on technology, critical performance, design and principles of operation;
  • The relevance of the particular experimental animals used in the selected studies for the biological evaluation of the material or medical device under consideration;
  • Conditions of use of the material or medical device in the selected documents and the intended use of the medical device in question.

C.2.5 Critical Evaluation of the Literature

The literature review should make an assessment of the significance and weight of studies of different designs and between published and unpublished data. Factors include:

  • Whether the author's conclusions are substantiated by the available data;
  • Whether the literature reflects the current medical practice and state of the art technologies;
  • Whether references are taken from recognized scientific publications reported in peer reviewed journals;
  • The extent to which the published literature is the outcome of a study/studies that have followed scientific principles.

The literature review should contain a critical evaluation of the literature. A review is then completed as it relates to the medical device in question, and a structured report of the review should be written, consisting of:

  • A short description of the material or medical device including its intended use;
  • An analysis of all the selected literature and data, both favourable and unfavourable;
  • A critical evaluation of the hazards, associated risks and appropriate safety measures;
  • A description of the methods of weighting the different papers (particular attention to repeated publications by the same authors to avoid over-weighting);
  • A list of publications appropriately cross-referenced in the evaluation;
  • A conclusion with a justification, making it clear how the objectives of the literature review have been met and how any gaps in the evidence have been identified;
  • The signature(s) of the reviewer(s) and date.

中文解析 — 系統性文獻審查程序

文獻審查的雙重功能

Annex C 強調文獻審查有兩個核心目的：

  • 替代測試：如果現有文獻已足以支持生物安全性論證，就不需要再做新的動物或體外測試
  • 識別數據缺口：如果文獻不足，則指出哪些缺口需要通過新測試來填補

對 CDMO 的實務價值：一份好的文獻審查報告可以省下大量時間和成本，因為它可能讓你有理由跳過某些測試，同時向監管機關展示你做了嚴謹的科學評估。

文獻審查是「科學活動」，不是「搜尋練習」

C.1 特別強調：文獻審查必須以「嚴謹性和客觀性」進行，且必須能夠被第三方驗證。

類比：就像一篇 systematic review 論文，需要有預先定義的搜尋策略、納入/排除標準、數據萃取方法，最終結論必須由數據支持，而不是研究者的主觀印象。

常見錯誤：把 Google Scholar 的隨機搜尋結果彙整成「文獻審查」。監管機關現在對這份文件的要求越來越接近 clinical evidence systematic review 的標準。

文獻審查計劃：事前定義，事後記錄

C.2.1 要求在執行前就建立計劃，必須文件化：

  1. 搜尋資料庫（PubMed、EMBASE、Cochrane、監管數據庫）
  2. 搜尋關鍵字和策略（MeSH terms + free text）
  3. 時間範圍（通常近 10 年為主）
  4. 語言限制（英文為主，重要的可包含其他語言）
  5. 納入標準（Study type? Sample size? Relevance?）
  6. 排除標準（與排除理由）

關鍵資料庫清單（來自 Bibliography）

ISO 10993-1 的 Bibliography 直接引用了以下數據庫，說明這些是業界認可的文獻來源：

  • PubMed — US National Library of Medicine（最廣泛使用）
  • EMBASE — Elsevier（歐洲藥學/毒理學文獻強項）
  • BIOSIS Previews — 生命科學文獻
  • SciFinder — 化學結構搜尋（美國化學學會）
  • IPCS (WHO) — 國際化學品安全規範
  • IRIS (EPA) — 美國環保署毒理學數據庫
  • ATSDR ToxGuides — 有毒物質與疾病登錄署

數據品質加權：GLP > Non-GLP

C.2.3 指出可以優先採用 GLP 數據，但不排除 non-GLP 數據 —— 後者必須說明你如何評估其可靠性。

C.2.5 提出的加權方法特別提醒：同一作者的重複發表可能造成過度加權（over-weighting）。這在某些材料的文獻中很常見 —— 某個研究組可能發表了多篇基於同一數據集的論文。

文獻審查報告必備要素（C.2.5）

  1. 器材/材料簡要描述（含預期用途）
  2. 所有選入文獻的分析（包括不利數據，不能只選對自己有利的）
  3. 危害、風險與安全措施的批判性評估
  4. 不同論文的加權方法說明
  5. 文獻引用清單（可交叉引用）
  6. 結論與論證（說明目標如何達成、哪些缺口已識別）
  7. 審查者簽名 + 日期（不可缺少！）

第 2 點「包括不利數據」至關重要：監管機關會看你有沒有刻意忽略不支持你結論的文獻。

OECD Guidelines for the Testing of Chemicals Section 4（健康效應）是業界進行毒理學測試時最廣泛引用的方法學標準。當 ISO 10993-x 系列指定某個測試「應按照 OECD 指南進行」，指的就是這套文件。

對 CRO 選擇：委外測試時，確認 CRO 的測試方法符合 OECD 相關指南，是品質保證的基本要求。

總結：Annex B + C 的核心價值

如果整本 ISO 10993-1 只能讀兩個附錄，就讀 Annex B 和 Annex C。

Annex B 告訴你怎麼把風險管理框架落地成可操作的評估流程；Annex C 告訴你怎麼把文獻審查做到符合監管要求的科學水準。這兩份附錄加在一起，就是一份完整的「生物評估作業手冊」。

對 CDMO 的最終啟示：生物評估不是一個「一次性的測試清單」，而是一個貫穿產品生命週期的持續性風險管理活動。從 tech transfer 的第一天，到上市後的持續監測，每一個環節都在 Annex B 的框架之內。
