4 General Principles Applying to Biological Evaluation of Medical Devices

4.1 — Evaluation as Part of Risk Management

The biological evaluation of any material or medical device intended for use in humans shall form part of a structured biological evaluation plan within a risk management process in accordance with ISO 14971:2007, Annex I, as given in Figure 1 of this document. This risk management process involves identification of biological hazards, estimation of the associated biological risks, and determination of their acceptability.

The biological evaluation shall be planned, carried out, and documented by knowledgeable and experienced professionals. The risk management plan should identify aspects of the biological evaluation requiring specific technical competencies and shall identify the person(s) responsible for the biological evaluation.

The evaluation shall include documented, informed consideration of advantages/disadvantages and relevance of:

  • a) Medical device configuration (e.g. size, geometry, surface properties) and a listing of a medical device's materials of construction (qualitative) and where necessary, the proportion and amount (mass) of each material in the medical device (quantitative).
  • b) The physical and chemical characteristics of the various materials of construction and their composition.
  • c) Any history of clinical use or human exposure data. Note: Previous regulatory approval history can be relevant.
  • d) Any existing toxicology and other biological safety data on product and component materials, breakdown products and metabolites.
  • e) Test procedures.

Evaluation can include both a review of relevant existing preclinical and clinical data and actual testing. Such an evaluation might result in the conclusion that no testing is needed if the material has a demonstrable safe history of use in a specified role and physical form that is equivalent to that of the medical device under design. Testing is usually not necessary when sufficient information is already available to perform a risk assessment of the material and/or the medical device.

4.2 — Material Selection: Fitness for Purpose First

In the selection of materials to be used in the medical device manufacture, the first consideration shall be fitness for purpose with regard to characteristics and properties of the material, which include chemical, toxicological, physical, electrical, morphological and mechanical properties.

4.3 — Scope of Evaluation: All Relevant Factors

The following shall be taken into account for their relevance to the overall biological evaluation of the medical device:

  • a) The material(s) of construction (all direct and indirect tissue contacting materials)
  • b) Intended additives, process contaminants and residues (e.g. testing for ethylene oxide sterilization residuals per ISO 10993-7)
  • c) Packaging materials that directly or indirectly contact the medical device can transfer chemicals to the medical device and then indirectly to the patient or clinician
  • d) Leachable substances (see ISO 10993-17 and ISO 10993-18)
  • e) Degradation products (see ISO 10993-9, ISO 10993-13, 10993-14, and 10993-15)
  • f) Other components and their interactions in the final product
  • g) The performance and characteristics of the final product
  • h) Physical characteristics of the final product, including porosity, particle size, shape and surface morphology

Description of medical device chemical constituents and consideration of material characterization including chemical characterization (see ISO 10993-18) shall precede any biological testing. Chemical characterization with an appropriate toxicological threshold can be used to determine if further testing is needed.

Both local and systemic effects shall be considered for risk evaluation. Medical devices that contain, generate, or are composed of nanomaterials can pose specific challenges to the biological evaluation due to their potentially unique properties (see ISO/TR 10993-22).

4.4 — Categorization Drives Gap Analysis

The biological evaluation shall commence with categorization of medical devices (see Clause 6). Assessment of the information already available then enables a gap analysis to facilitate the selection of appropriate tests. The rigour necessary in the biological evaluation is principally determined by the nature, degree, frequency and duration of the exposure and the hazards identified for the medical device or material.

Testing is usually not necessary when sufficient information is already available to perform a risk assessment of the material and/or the medical device. For example, biological testing is usually not necessary if material characterization demonstrates equivalence to a previously assessed medical device or material with established safety.

The interpretation of the data shall take into account the chemical composition of the materials, including the conditions of exposure as well as the nature, degree, frequency and duration of exposure of the body to the medical device or its constituents.

4.5 — Hazard Identification: All Known Possible Hazards

All known possible biological hazards shall be taken into account for every material and final product, but this does not imply that testing for all possible hazards will be necessary or practical. Test results cannot guarantee freedom from potential biological hazards, thus biological investigations shall be followed by careful observations for unexpected adverse reactions or events in humans during clinical use.

The range of possible biological hazards is wide and can include short-term effects such as acute toxicity, irritation to the skin, eye and mucosal surfaces, haemolysis and thrombogenicity, as well as long-term or specific toxic effects such as subchronic and chronic toxic effects, sensitization resulting in allergy, genotoxicity, carcinogenicity (tumorigenicity) and effects on reproduction or development, including teratogenicity.

4.6 — In Vitro Preferred Over In Vivo

If testing is needed, selection of any in vitro or in vivo tests (see Annex A) shall be based on intended use. In vitro test methods, which are appropriately validated, reasonably and practically available, reliable and reproducible, shall be considered for use in preference to in vivo tests (see ISO 10993-2). Whenever in vivo tests are indicated by findings of the initial risk assessment, use of appropriate in vitro screening, if available, shall be considered before in vivo tests are commenced.

A rationale for the testing strategy, as well as for test selection, shall be provided. Test data, complete to the extent that an independent analysis could be made, shall be evaluated by competent, informed professionals, and shall be retained.

4.7–4.8 — Lifecycle and Reusable Devices

4.7: The biological safety of a medical device shall be evaluated by the manufacturer over the whole life-cycle of a medical device.

4.8: For re-usable medical devices, biological safety shall be evaluated for the maximum number of validated processing cycles by the manufacturer.

4.9 — Triggers for Re-evaluation

The biological risk assessment of materials or final products shall be re-evaluated if any of the following occur:

  • a) Any change in the source or in the specification of the materials used in the manufacture of the product
  • b) Any change in the formulation, processing, primary packaging or sterilization of the product
  • c) Any change in the manufacturer's instructions or expectations concerning storage, e.g. changes in shelf life and/or transport
  • d) Any change in the intended use of the product
  • e) Any evidence that the product can produce adverse biological effects when used in humans
4.10–4.11 — Existing Data and Historical Products

4.10: The biological evaluation shall take into account preclinical tests, clinical investigations, post-market experience from similar medical devices or materials, and other relevant information.

4.11: This document shall not be used to mandate re-testing of historical products assessed previously using the appropriate edition of this document at the time of the assessment. Nevertheless, compliance to this new edition shall be shown, by providing a justification for omission of further testing. However, if any of the changes described in Clause 4.9 occur, an evaluation of the biologic risks related to the change shall be performed using the current version of this standard.

第四節解析 — 一般原則

核心概念：生物評估是「流程」，不是「一張測試清單」

ISO 10993-1 最根本的邏輯：生物評估不是拿到器材就去做一批標準試驗。它是一個嵌入在 ISO 14971 風險管理框架之中的結構化流程。

這個流程的起點是「我們已知什麼？」，而不是「我們需要做哪些測試？」這個思維轉換非常重要——現有資料（材料規格、毒理文獻、供應商 CoA）可以取代全新的動物試驗。

  • 有歷史安全使用記錄 → 可能不需要新測試
  • 材料化學特性已充分建立 → 毒理閾值評估代替動物實驗
  • 等效於已評估器材 → 引用既有資料

類比：藥品杜撰對照 vs. 文獻引用

想像一位毒理學家不是每個藥物都從頭做 LD50 試驗——如果化學物質的毒理資料庫已足夠充分，他會先做文獻回顧，只在資料缺口無法填補時才啟動新試驗。

ISO 10993-1 的生物評估邏輯完全相同：先找資料，再補缺口，最後才是新測試。

CDMO 實務關聯：客戶提交的生物風險評估

在藥品 CDMO 的日常工作中，客戶（Sponsor/MAH）會在技術轉移包中附上容器/封閉系統的生物評估文件，這份文件的根據就是 ISO 10993-1。

CDMO QA 在審查這些文件時，應重點確認：

  • 評估是否由「有資格的專業人員」執行並簽署（4.1 要求）
  • 是否列出所有接觸材料（玻璃瓶、橡膠塞、鋁蓋、包材）
  • 是否說明不需要某項測試的理由（waiver justification）
  • 4.9 觸發條件：若 CDMO 更換供應商或改變一級包材，必須通知客戶重新評估

4.3 — 評估範圍：不只是器材本體

許多人只想到器材本身，但 4.3 列出的 a~h 項目提醒我們，評估範圍包含：

  • 包材：一級包材接觸到器材/藥品，可能間接傳遞化學物質給病人
  • 加工助劑殘留：EtO 滅菌殘留（ISO 10993-7）、潤滑劑等
  • 降解產物：長期使用或高溫環境下的材料降解
  • 奈米材料：特殊挑戰，另有 ISO/TR 10993-22 規範

在無菌充填 CDMO，橡膠塞的矽油塗層、玻璃瓶的脫層問題都在此評估範圍之內。

4.5 — 測試結果不等於「零風險保證」

標準明確指出：test results cannot guarantee freedom from potential biological hazards。這意味著生物評估是持續進行的上市後監察（post-market surveillance）的一部分，而非一次性的批准行為。

對 CDMO 的啟示：即使容器系統已通過所有生物相容性測試，上市後若出現不預期的不良反應，仍需重新評估（4.9e）。

4.9 重新評估觸發點 — CDMO 場景對照

  1. 更換橡膠塞供應商 → 觸發 4.9a，需重新評估
  2. 更改滅菌方式（gamma → EtO） → 觸發 4.9b
  3. 延長產品效期 → 觸發 4.9c（儲存條件評估）
  4. 藥品從靜脈注射改為肌肉注射 → 觸發 4.9d（接觸部位改變）
  5. 有病人回報疑似器材相關不良反應 → 觸發 4.9e

5 Categorization of Medical Devices

5.1 General

Medical devices shall be categorized according to the nature and duration of body contact as specified in 5.2 and 5.3. The categorization of medical devices facilitates selection of appropriate data sets (see informative Annex A).

The evaluation of any medical device that does not fall into one of the categories specified shall follow the general principles contained in this document. Certain medical devices might fall into more than one body contact or duration category, in which case evaluation appropriate to each category shall be carried out.

Example: For medical devices that include both an implanted component and a delivery system used only during a surgical procedure to place the medical device, the implant should be assessed separately from the delivery system.

5.2 Categorization by Nature of Body Contact

5.2.1 Non-contacting Medical Devices

These include medical devices (or components) that have neither direct nor indirect contact with the body and where biocompatibility information would not be necessary. Diagnostic software, an in vitro diagnostic device and a blood-collection tube are examples of non-contact devices.

5.2.2 Surface-contacting Medical Devices

These include medical devices in contact with the following:

  • a) Skin — Medical devices that contact intact skin surfaces only. Examples: Electrodes, external prostheses, fixation tapes, compression bandages and monitors of various types.
  • b) Mucosal membranes — Medical devices that contact intact mucosal membranes. Examples: Contact lenses, urinary catheters, intravaginal and intra-intestinal devices, endotracheal tubes, bronchoscopes, some dental prostheses.
  • c) Breached or compromised surfaces — Medical devices that contact breached or otherwise compromised body surfaces. Examples: Dressings or healing devices and occlusive patches for ulcers, burns and granulation tissue.

5.2.3 Externally Communicating Medical Devices

Externally communicating medical devices shall be categorized according to their contact with the following application sites:

  • a) Blood path, indirect — Medical devices or components that do not necessarily directly contact the blood path but serve as conduits to deliver fluids into the vascular system. Examples: Solution administration sets, extension sets, transfer sets and blood administration sets.
  • b) Tissue/bone/dentin — Medical devices that contact tissue, bone or pulp/dentin systems. Examples: Laparoscopes, arthroscopes, draining systems, dental filling materials and skin staples.
  • c) Circulating blood — Medical devices that contact circulating blood. Examples: Intravascular catheters, temporary pacemaker electrodes, oxygenators, extracorporeal oxygenator tubing, dialysers, haemoadsorbents and immunoadsorbents.

5.2.4 Implant Medical Devices

Implant medical devices shall be categorized according to their contact with the following application sites:

  • a) Tissue/bone — Medical devices principally contacting bone (e.g. orthopaedic pins, plates, replacement joints, bone cements, intra-osseous devices) or contacting tissue and tissue fluid (e.g. pacemakers, drug supply devices, breast implants, artificial larynxes, ligation clips, intrauterine devices that do not achieve their primary function by chemical activity).
  • b) Blood — Medical devices principally contacting circulating blood in the cardiovascular system. Examples: Pacemaker electrodes, artificial arteriovenous fistulae, heart valves, vascular grafts, internal drug-delivery catheters, ventricular assist devices.

5.3 Categorization by Duration of Contact

5.3.1 Contact Duration Categories

Medical devices shall be categorized according to the anticipated duration of contact as follows:

  • a) Limited exposure (A) — medical devices whose cumulative sum of single, multiple or repeated duration of contact is up to 24 h.
  • b) Prolonged exposure (B) — medical devices whose cumulative sum of single, multiple or repeated contact time is likely to exceed 24 h but not exceed 30 d.
  • c) Long-term exposure (C) — medical devices whose cumulative sum of single, multiple or repeated contact time exceeds 30 d.

5.3.2 Transitory-contacting Medical Devices

Some medical devices with limited exposure (A) have very brief/transitory contact with the body (e.g. lancets, hypodermic needles, capillary tubes that are used for less than one minute). These generally would not require testing to address biocompatibility.

However, for products made with materials such as coatings or lubricants that could be left in contact with body tissues after the medical device is removed, it is possible that a more detailed biocompatibility assessment will be necessary. Cumulative use should also be considered.

5.3.3 Medical Devices with Multiple Contact Duration Categories

If a material or medical device can be placed in more than one duration category, the more rigorous testing and/or evaluation considerations shall apply. With expected or intended multiple exposures to a device, the decision into which category a medical device is placed shall take into account the potential cumulative effect, bearing in mind the period of time over which these exposures occur.

If a medical device is intended to change during its lifetime, such as those that are polymerized and/or degraded in situ, the evaluation shall consider all the different device states. For example, for an absorbable glue intended to polymerize in situ, the different device states would include starting components, intermediate reaction products, the fully polymerized material and degradation products.

第五節解析 — 醫療器材分類

為什麼分類是生物評估的入口？

ISO 10993-1 把分類放在評估流程的最前端（4.4 明確規定 evaluation shall commence with categorization），因為分類決定了後續 Annex A 的測試端點清單。

邏輯鏈：分類 → 測試端點矩陣 → 缺口分析 → 必要測試清單

錯誤分類會導致評估不足（under-evaluation）或過度測試（over-testing），前者有安全風險，後者浪費資源和動物。

類比：航空乘客安檢的分級

想像機場安檢：持本國護照的國內線旅客（非接觸類）只需一般安檢；持外國護照搭乘國際線（植入型，長期接觸）則需更嚴格的審查。

器材與人體接觸越深、時間越長，生物相容性審查就越嚴格——這是 ISO 10993-1 分類系統的核心邏輯。

CDMO 填充生產的容器分類思考

在無菌藥品製造中，容器/封閉系統的分類取決於藥品的最終給藥方式：

  • 玻璃藥瓶（完整皮膚外部）：非接觸類 — 不需生物相容性評估
  • 玻璃藥瓶（藥品供靜脈注射）：器材本身視為「Blood path, indirect」（間接血路接觸） — 需要評估，但透過 CoA 和既有資料常可支持
  • 預填式注射器（Prefilled Syringe）：接觸循環血液 — 評估要求最嚴格，特別是注射器的矽油（silicone oil）和橡膠塞成分
  • 植入式緩釋器材：組織/骨骼植入，長期暴露（C類）— 最高風險等級，需完整測試

「累積接觸時間」的重要性 — 不是單次使用

5.3 特別強調是「cumulative sum」（累計總和），不是單次使用時間。這對一次性器材的重複使用場景特別重要。

例如：透析患者每週使用血液透析器 3 次，每次 4 小時。單次 4 小時屬 A 類（有限暴露），但若考慮累積暴露，則必須以更高的分類（B 或 C）來評估。

CDMO 實務：如果客戶的藥品是需要長期重複注射的（如生物製劑每月注射），則注射容器的材料評估必須考量累積效應。

類比：皮膚接觸 vs. 直接接觸血液的差異

想像手術室中的兩種手套：

  • 手術室外牆的橡膠手柄（接觸完整皮膚） → 表面接觸，A 類風險
  • 外科手術手套（接觸開放傷口） → 破損/受損表面接觸，風險大幅提升
  • 血液體外循環管路（直接接觸循環血液） → 最高風險，需要完整血液相容性測試

這個類比說明了為什麼 5.2 的分類從皮膚到血液，風險依次遞增。

分類練習 — CDMO 常見容器/封閉系統

  1. Type I 玻璃瓶 + 鹵丁基橡膠塞（供 IV 輸液）
    → 接觸性質：Blood path, indirect | 時間：有限暴露（A）（單次輸液 <24h）
  2. 預填充注射器（BD Uniject, insulin pen）
    → 接觸性質：Blood path, indirect（皮下）或 Circulating blood（靜脈）| 時間視頻率而定
  3. 鋁製鋁蓋（未接觸藥品）
    → 接觸性質：非接觸類（間接包材），一般不需單獨生物評估
  4. 植入式藥物緩釋晶片
    → 接觸性質：Tissue/bone 植入 | 時間：長期暴露（C）→ 最嚴格評估

6 Biological Evaluation Process

6.1 Physical and Chemical Information for Biological Risk Analysis

Figure 1 indicates how the general steps in the physical and/or chemical characterization process link to the overall biological evaluation decision points.

Gathering physical and chemical information on the medical device or component is a crucial first step in the biological evaluation and its associated process of material characterization. These data should be sufficient to answer the first two rows of questions in the Figure 1 flow chart. The extent of physical and/or chemical characterization required depends on what is known about the material formulation, what nonclinical and clinical safety and toxicological data exist, and on the nature and duration of body contact with the medical device.

At a minimum, the characterization shall address the constituent chemicals of the medical device and possible residual process aids or additives used in its manufacture. Material characterization, if performed, shall be conducted in accordance with ISO 10993-18. For nanomaterials see ISO/TR 10993-22.

If the combination of all materials, chemicals and processes has an established history of safe use in the intended application, and the physical properties have not changed, then further characterization and additional data sets may not be necessary. In this case the rationale shall be documented.

For device extractables and leachables that have sufficient toxicological data relevant to the expected exposure (quantity, route and frequency), further testing need not be required. For medical devices that have known leachable chemical mixtures, potential interactions between the leachables should be considered.

Where the potential for degradation exists under the conditions of manufacture, sterilization, transport, storage, and use of the medical device, the presence and nature of degradation products shall be characterized in accordance with ISO 10993-9, ISO 10993-13, ISO 10993-14, and ISO 10993-15, as applicable.

6.2 Gap Analysis and Selection of Biological Endpoints for Assessment

Assess available information and compare to the data set(s) needed to assess the biological safety of the medical device (see Clause 4, Annexes A and C). Identify additional reasonably and practicably obtained data or testing needed to complete the data sets required to perform the risk assessment.

Characterize the data gap and determine its significance both to the assessment of the biological endpoint (Annex A) and to the overall biological risk assessment. Identify options for data sets that would address the data gap.

NOTE: For example, pharmacopeial plastics' testing is typically conducted on raw materials, whereas ISO 10993 evaluates the medical device in its final stage. Therefore, data from such pharmacopeial testing is not sufficient for the final medical device without appropriate justification.

The results of the risk analysis of identified chemicals can lead to the conclusion that additional material characterization is necessary. Appropriate extraction testing can be used to estimate the degree of clinical exposure to the chemical constituent (see ISO 10993-18). The acceptability of the level of estimated leachables shall be established by comparing the amount of each compound extracted from the medical device to its respective relevant toxicological threshold as developed in accordance with ISO 10993-17.

Protection of humans is the primary goal of this document; a secondary goal is to ensure animal welfare and to minimize the number and exposure of the test animals. ISO 10993-2 applies to any in vivo testing being considered. Additional testing shall not be carried out where:

  • 1) results are available from relevant studies that have been carried out previously, or
  • 2) the existing non-clinical and clinical data, including history of safe use, meet the requirements of biological evaluation and therefore further animal testing would be unethical.

6.3 Biological Testing

6.3.1 General

In addition to the general principles given in Clause 4, the following shall apply when biological testing of medical devices is considered necessary:

  • a) Testing shall be performed on the final medical device, or representative samples processed in the same manner as the final device (including sterilization, if needed).
  • b) The choice of test procedures shall take into account: (1) nature, degree, duration, frequency and conditions of exposure; (2) chemical and physical nature of the final device; (3) toxicological activity of chemicals in the formulation; (4) that certain biological tests are not justifiable where leachable chemicals have been excluded or have acceptable toxicity profiles; (5) the ratio of device surface area to recipient body size; (6) existing information from literature and previous experience; (7) sensitivity and specificity of the test.
  • c) If extracts of the medical devices are prepared, the solvents and conditions of extraction used should be appropriate to the nature and use of the final product and shall be prepared in accordance with ISO 10993-12. Whenever possible, extraction conditions should represent at a minimum an exaggeration of use conditions.
  • d) Positive and negative controls should be used where appropriate.

The test methods used shall be sensitive, precise and accurate. When biological testing is conducted, it shall be carried out in accordance with good laboratory practices (ISO/IEC 17025 or equivalent). Test methods should be reproducible (intralaboratory) as well as repeatable (interlaboratory) and robust.

6.3.2 Testing for Evaluation — Endpoint Summary

Testing for the evaluations specified in 6.3.2.1 to 6.3.2.15 shall be considered and carried out where necessary to complete the data sets needed for the biological evaluation. Where the existing data are adequate, additional testing is not required.

6.3.2.7 Subacute/subchronic toxicity ISO 10993-11 Effects from exposures 24 h to 10% of animal lifespan (~13 wks in rats)
6.3.2.8 Chronic toxicity ISO 10993-11 Effects from major portion of animal lifespan (~6 months in rats)
6.3.2.9 Implantation effects ISO 10993-6 Local pathological effects at gross and microscopic level in living tissue
6.3.2.10 Genotoxicity ISO 10993-3 Gene mutations, chromosomal changes, DNA toxicity — battery of tests
6.3.2.11 Carcinogenicity ISO 10993-3 Risk assessment approach; in vivo tests rare, typically risk-based
6.3.2.12 Reproductive/developmental toxicity ISO 10993-3 Reproductive function, embryonic development (teratogenicity)
6.3.2.13 Degradation ISO 10993-9, -13, -14, -15 Required for absorbable or potentially degrading devices
6.3.2.14 Toxicokinetics ISO 10993-16 ADME of chemicals — absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion
6.3.2.15 Immunotoxicology ISO/TS 10993-20 Immunotoxic potential of device materials

第六節解析 — 生物評估流程

六步驟生物評估流程（基於 Figure 1 精神）

ISO 10993-1 Section 6 描述的流程可以整理為六個步驟：

  • Step 1：物理/化學特性表徵（6.1）— 收集材料組成、工藝殘留、降解產物等資料
  • Step 2：缺口分析（6.2）— 比較已有資料 vs. Annex A 所需資料集，識別缺口
  • Step 3：選擇評估端點（6.2）— 根據分類結果決定需評估哪些生物學端點
  • Step 4：執行測試或引用現有資料（6.3）— 優先使用體外試驗，最小化動物試驗
  • Step 5：解讀資料（Section 7）— 毒理專家進行整合評估
  • Step 6：撰寫生物評估報告 — 文件化所有決策邏輯和結論

6.2 缺口分析（Gap Analysis）— 最關鍵的一步

缺口分析是生物評估流程的核心，也是最容易被忽視的步驟。它的邏輯是：

  • 先列出 Annex A 要求的資料集（根據器材分類）
  • 再盤點手上已有的資料（供應商文件、文獻、歷史測試）
  • 比對兩者的差距 → 這就是「缺口」
  • 決定如何填補缺口：追加資料？ 新測試？ 風險評估豁免？

重要提醒：藥典（BP/USP）的塑膠材料測試是針對原料，ISO 10993 評估的是最終器材狀態。藥典合格不代表可以直接替代 ISO 10993 測試，仍需說明理由（justification）。

類比：健康檢查的分項檢驗

缺口分析就像醫生排健康檢查項目：不是所有人都需要做全套項目。

  • 20 歲健康男性：基本血液、尿液分析（有限接觸，低風險）
  • 50 歲有家族史：加做腸鏡、心臟超音波（更長接觸，更高風險）
  • 已有症狀：針對性深度檢查（已知問題需填補）

ISO 10993 的邏輯：器材接觸越深、越久，需要評估的端點越多，但有舊資料就不需要重做。

E&L（Extractables & Leachables）在 CDMO 的核心地位

對於藥品 CDMO，6.1 提到的「extractables and leachables」是最直接相關的評估內容。

Extractables（可萃取物）：在極端實驗室條件下，從容器/封閉系統中可以萃取出的化學物質。這是最大可能的暴露量估算。

Leachables（可瀝出物）：在實際使用條件下（正常儲存、溫度、時間），實際遷移進入藥品的物質。這是病人實際暴露量。

評估流程：Extractables 研究 → 識別關鍵化合物 → 預測 Leachables → 與毒理閾值（TTC / SCT）比較 → 決定是否需要進一步測試。

監管標準：ICH Q3D、ISO 10993-17（毒理閾值）、ISO 10993-18（化學表徵）。

6.3.1 — 測試樣品必須是「最終器材」

這是許多廠商容易犯錯的地方：6.3.1a 明確要求測試樣品必須是最終器材狀態，包括滅菌後。

常見錯誤：

  • 用原料橡膠做測試，但實際產品橡膠已硫化、塗矽油 → 不符合要求
  • 滅菌前的樣品測試，未考慮 EtO 殘留或 gamma 輻射對材料的影響 → 需重新測試
  • 縮小版模型替代實際器材 → 需確認表面積/體重比的合理性（6.3.1b.5）

測試端點的風險分層邏輯 — CDMO 應用

  1. 所有藥品容器系統都需考慮：細胞毒性（Cytotoxicity）、刺激性（Irritation）
  2. 靜脈注射用途額外需要：血液相容性（Haemocompatibility）、熱原性（Pyrogenicity）
  3. 長期使用或植入額外需要：亞急性/慢性毒性、致癌性風險評估
  4. 可降解材料需要：降解試驗（Degradation）、毒物動力學（Toxicokinetics）
  5. 免疫原性未知材料：免疫毒性（Immunotoxicology）

7 Interpretation of Biological Evaluation Data and Overall Biological Risk Assessment

Expert assessors who have the necessary knowledge and experience shall determine and document:

  1. a) The strategy and planned content for the biological evaluation of the medical device
  2. b) The criteria for determining the acceptability of the material for the intended purpose, in line with the risk management plan
  3. c) The adequacy of the material characterization
  4. d) The rationale for selection and/or waiving of tests
  5. e) The interpretation of existing data and results of testing
  6. f) The need for any additional data to complete the biological evaluation
  7. g) Overall biological safety conclusions for the medical device
Informative Annex A gives the general endpoints that should be considered for each device contact type and duration category in a biological risk assessment.

第七節解析 — 評估資料解讀與風險評估

「生物評估報告」的核心要素

Section 7 的重點不是「做了哪些測試」，而是「有資格的人對所有資料做出了有文件記錄的判斷」。輸出文件稱為 Biological Evaluation Report（BER），它必須包含：

  • 評估策略與範圍（a）
  • 可接受性標準（b）— 量化或定性的安全閾值
  • 材料表徵充分性聲明（c）
  • 每項測試選做或豁免的理由（d）— 這是稽核重點
  • 對現有資料的解讀（e）
  • 是否仍有資料缺口（f）
  • 整體生物安全結論（g）— 明確聲明材料是否適合預期用途

誰可以撰寫 BER？資格要求

標準要求 expert assessors — 在實際應用中，這通常意味著：

  • 具有毒理學背景（toxicologist，理想上是 ERT/DABT 等認證）
  • 了解 ISO 10993 系列標準的技術人員
  • 有器材生物安全評估實際經驗

對 CDMO QA 的啟示：CDMO 不需要自己的毒理學家，但需要能審查客戶提交的 BER 是否符合此框架要求。如果 BER 缺少 d 項（豁免理由）或 g 項（整體結論），應要求補件。

類比：法院判決 vs. 證據收集

Section 6 的測試過程就像收集法庭證據，而 Section 7 是法官基於所有證據做出最終裁決。

BER 就是這份判決書：它整合了所有化學資料、測試結果、文獻引用，並由「法官」（毒理學專家）寫下有理由的結論。

如果稽核員看到 BER 只有測試數據表格而沒有專家解讀，這就等於只有「物證清單」沒有「判決書」——這不符合 ISO 10993-1 的要求。

CDMO QA 審查客戶 BER 的重點清單

  1. 簽署人資格是否載明（toxicologist / qualified assessor）？
  2. 是否列出所有接觸材料（包含 CDMO 使用的容器/包材型號）？
  3. 每個測試豁免項目是否有書面理由？
  4. E&L 研究結果是否已與毒理閾值（TTC/SCT）比對？
  5. 整體安全結論是否明確——是「適合預期用途（fit for intended purpose）」的正面聲明？
  6. BER 版本是否涵蓋目前在 CDMO 使用的容器/封閉系統版本（非舊版器材）？
  7. 若 CDMO 更換過任何包材規格，客戶是否已更新 BER（對應 4.9 觸發條件）？

總結：Section 4-7 的邏輯鏈

完整的生物評估思路：

  • Section 4：確立生物評估是風險管理的一部分，現有資料優先，專家主導
  • Section 5：分類器材 → 接觸性質（表面/外部通訊/植入）× 接觸時間（A/B/C）
  • Section 6：收集物化資料 → 缺口分析 → 測試或引用資料（15個端點，按需選用）
  • Section 7：毒理專家整合所有資料，形成有文件記錄的 BER，並聲明整體安全結論

這四個 Section 共同構成了 ISO 10993-1 的骨幹，後面各 Part（10993-2 至 10993-20）都是在執行具體的測試方法，而此框架定義了「為什麼選這個測試」的決策邏輯。
