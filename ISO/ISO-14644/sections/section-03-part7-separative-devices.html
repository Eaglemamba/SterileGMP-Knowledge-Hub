Introduction — 導言

Why Separative Devices? The Separation Continuum Concept

Original Text

In the spirit of the generic requirements of an International Standard, the term separative devices was developed by Technical Committee ISO/TC 209 to encompass the wide continuum of configurations from open unrestricted air overspill to wholly contained systems. Common terms-of-trade, such as clean air hoods, gloveboxes, isolators and mini-environments, have different meanings depending on the specific industry.

Difficulties experienced in the manufacture and handling of certain products or materials have driven the development of separative devices. These difficulties include:

  • Product sensitivity to particles, chemicals, gases or microorganisms
  • Operator sensitivity to the process materials or byproducts
  • Both product and operator sensitivity

Separative devices provide assured protection in varying levels by utilising physical or dynamic barriers, or both, to create separation between operation and operator. Certain processes may require special atmospheres to prevent degradation or explosions. Some systems may be capable of providing 100% recirculation of the contained atmosphere to allow inert gas operation or biodecontamination with reactive gases.

Usually people do not work directly inside the separative-device environment during production. These separative devices may be movable or fixed, and used for transport, transfer and process. The product or process, or both, are manipulated remotely with access devices either manually, with protection by barrier technology such as wall-integrated personal interface systems (e.g. gloves, gauntlets, half-suits), or mechanically with robotic handling systems.

Air cleanliness definitions and test methods covered in ISO 14644-1, 14644-2 and 14644-3 generally apply within separative devices. In applications with biological contamination requirements, ISO 14698-1 and 14698-2 will apply. However, some applications can have special requirements for monitoring because of extreme conditions that may be encountered.

Transfer devices to move material in and out of separative devices form an important portion of this part of ISO 14644. Mini-environments are often used in the electronics industry with transport containers, called boxes or pods, to provide very clean process conditions.

From a unifying conceptual standpoint, a continuum of separation exists between the operation and the operator, ranging from totally open to totally enclosed systems depending on the application. Similarly, a continuum exists for containment.

導師解析

為什麼需要隔離設備？三大驅動力

ISO 14644-7 的開篇直接點出隔離設備發展的三大現實驅動力：

  • 產品保護（Product Protection）：產品對微粒、化學品、氣體或微生物敏感——典型如無菌注射劑需要 ISO 5 Grade A 環境
  • 人員保護（Operator Protection）：操作人員對製程物料或副產品敏感——典型如高效活性藥物（HPAPI）、細胞毒性藥物需要負壓防護
  • 雙向保護（Dual Protection）：產品與人員均需保護——最嚴格的應用場景，如放射性藥物製備

理解這三個驅動力，就理解了為什麼隔離設備設計會有如此大的差異。

比喻說明：「隔離連續體」如同防護衣的等級

想像一個化學家需要處理危險物質：

  • 只戴手套 = 開放式層流罩（unrestricted air overspill）
  • 戴護目鏡+手套 = 受限氣流設備
  • 穿實驗衣+手套+護目鏡 = 半開放式設備
  • 穿全身防護服 = 手套箱（glovebox）
  • 在獨立密封艙內操作 = 全封閉隔離器（isolator）

防護等級越高，對外部干擾的抵抗越強，但操作靈活性越低、設備成本越高。這就是「連續體」的精髓——沒有唯一正確答案，只有根據風險的最優選擇。

法規連結：EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 屏障技術

EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 第 8 節「Barrier Technology」明確區分 RABS 與隔離器：

  • RABS（Restricted Access Barrier System）：物理屏障限制進出，但與背景環境（Grade B）有空氣聯通
  • 隔離器（Isolator）：完全物理隔離，內部可進行化學去污染（VHP），可降低背景環境要求至 Grade C 甚至 D

Annex 1 第 8.2 條：「Where isolators are used as an alternative to a conventional Grade A/B environment, the background environment for an isolator used for aseptic processing should be at least Grade D.」

這意味著隔離器的應用不僅是技術選擇，更是監管認可的環境降級路徑。

Original Text

1 Scope

This part of ISO 14644 specifies the minimum requirements for the design, construction, installation, test and approval of separative devices, in those respects where they differ from cleanrooms as described in ISO 14644-4 and 14644-5.

The application of this part of ISO 14644 takes into account the following limitations:

  • User requirements are as agreed by customer and supplier
  • Application-specific requirements are not addressed
  • Specific processes to be accommodated in the separative-device installation are not specified
  • Fire, safety and other regulatory matters are not considered specifically; where appropriate, national and local regulations apply

This part of ISO 14644 is not applicable to full-suits.

重要限制：「客戶與供應商協議」原則

ISO 14644-7 反覆強調「由客戶與供應商協議確定」，這是標準設計的刻意選擇。原因在於：隔離設備的應用極其多樣（製藥、核工業、電子、生物技術……），標準不可能規定所有具體數值。

在實務中，這意味著：

  • 採購隔離器時，必須準備詳細的 User Requirement Specification (URS)
  • 供應商資格確認（Supplier Qualification）非常重要
  • 「未規定的」參數需要雙方明確協議，否則驗收時容易產生爭議

標準定位：ISO 14644-7 與其他標準的關係

ISO 14644-7 是對 ISO 14644-4（設計與建造）和 14644-5（操作）的補充，專門涵蓋潔淨室中「不同於一般潔淨室」的隔離設備部分。

Section 3 — Terms and Definitions 術語定義

18 key terms — the vocabulary of separative device technology

Original Text

3.1 access device

Device for manipulation of processes, tools or products within the separative device.

3.2 action level

Level set by the user in the context of controlled environments, which, when exceeded, requires immediate intervention, including the investigation of cause, and corrective action.

3.3 alert level

Level set by the user in the context of controlled environments, giving early warning of a drift from normal conditions, which, when exceeded, should result in increased attention to the process.

3.4 barrier

Means employed to provide separation.

3.5 breach velocity

Velocity through an aperture sufficient to prevent movement of matter in the direction opposite to the flow.

3.6 containment

State achieved by separative devices with high degree of separation between operator and operation.

3.7 decontamination

Reduction of unwanted matter to a defined level.

3.8 gauntlet

One-piece glove covering the full arm-length.

3.9 glove

<of separative devices> Component of an access device that maintains an effective barrier while enabling the hands of the operator to enter the enclosed volume of a separative device.

3.10 glove port

Attachment site for gloves, sleeves and gauntlets.

3.11 glove sleeve system

Multi-component access device that maintains an effective barrier while enabling replacement of the sleeve piece, connecting cuff piece and glove.

3.12 half-suit

Access device that maintains an effective barrier while enabling the head, trunk and arms of the operator to enter the working space of the separative device.

3.13 hourly leak rate (Rh)

Ratio of the hourly leakage q of the containment enclosure under normal working conditions (pressure and temperature) to the volume V of the said containment enclosure.

NOTE: It is expressed in reciprocal hours (h⁻¹). [ISO 10648-2:1994]

3.14 leak

<of separative devices> Defect revealed by testing under a pressure differential after corrections for atmospheric conditions.

3.15 pressure integrity

Capability to provide a quantifiable pressure leakage rate repeatable under test conditions.

3.16 separation descriptor [Aa:Bb]

Numerical abbreviation summarizing the difference in cleanliness classification between two areas as ensured by a separative device under specified test conditions, where:

  • A = the ISO class inside the device
  • a = the particle size at which A is measured
  • B = the ISO class outside the device
  • b = the particle size at which B is measured

3.17 separative device

Equipment utilizing constructional and dynamic means to create assured levels of separation between the inside and outside of a defined volume.

NOTE: Some industry-specific examples are clean air hoods, containment enclosures, gloveboxes, isolators and mini-environments.

3.18 transfer device

Mechanism to effect movement of material into or out of separative devices while minimizing ingress or egress of unwanted matter.

導師解析

關鍵公式：Separation Descriptor [Aa:Bb]

這是 ISO 14644-7 最重要的概念工具之一：

格式：[Aa:Bb]
A = 設備內部 ISO 等級
a = 測量粒徑（微米）
B = 設備外部 ISO 等級
b = 測量粒徑（微米）

範例：[ISO5(0.5):ISO7(0.5)]
意義：設備內部 ISO Class 5（0.5 µm），
      設備外部 ISO Class 7（0.5 µm）

這個描述符告訴你：
- 這台設備維持內外 2 個等級的差距
- 一個 Grade A 填充隔離器通常有 [ISO5:ISO8] 的分隔能力

注意：Separation Descriptor 是「在指定測試條件下」的描述，不等同於持續性保證。

關鍵公式：Hourly Leak Rate Rh

Rh = q / V

Rh = 每小時洩漏率（h⁻¹，倒數小時）
q  = 每小時洩漏量（m³/h）
V  = 隔離設備體積（m³）

ISO 10648-2 四級分類：
Class 1：Rh ≤ 5×10⁻⁴ h⁻¹ → 高壓力完整性（High）
Class 2：Rh < 2.5×10⁻³ h⁻¹ → 中壓力完整性（Medium）
Class 3：Rh < 10⁻² h⁻¹ → 低壓力完整性（Low）
Class 4：Rh < 10⁻¹ h⁻¹ → 最低等級

實例計算：
隔離器體積 = 0.5 m³
測試 1 小時後壓力變化 = 5 Pa（在 1000 Pa 基礎上）
洩漏率 Rh ≈ 5/1000 = 5×10⁻³ h⁻¹（屬 Class 2）

Alert Level vs Action Level：監控管理的兩道防線

  • Alert Level（警示水準）：超出後發出早期預警，需「增加關注」——類似黃色警戒，不需立即停止生產，但需記錄並調查趨勢
  • Action Level（行動水準）：超出後需「立即介入、調查原因、採取糾正措施」——類似紅色警戒，可能需要停止生產

EU GMP Annex 1 對 Alert/Action Level 有更詳細的規定，與本標準一致。CDMO 在起草 EM（環境監測）計劃時必須明確設定兩個水準。

導師解析

監管重點：條款 e — 危害分析方法

標準特別列舉了 HACCP、HAZOP、FMEA、FTA 作為適用的危害分析方法。在製藥行業中：

  • FMEA（失效模式與影響分析）：最常用於隔離器設計風險評估，特別是評估各個密封點、手套、傳遞口的失效可能性
  • HAZOP（危害與可操作性分析）：適合用於評估氣流系統、壓力控制系統的偏差影響
  • ICH Q9（Quality Risk Management）是製藥界進行上述分析的主要指導框架

EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 第 3 節明確要求對無菌製程使用 CCS（污染控制策略），隔離器的危害分析是 CCS 的組成部分。

CDMO 實務應用：隔離器採購的 URS 要點

當 CDMO 客戶要求 Grade A 無菌充填，你在起草 URS 時，第 4 條清單提示你需要明確的關鍵點：

  • 分離描述符（h）：通常指定 [ISO5:ISO7] 或 [ISO5:ISO8]，取決於背景環境
  • 操作狀態（g）：是否需要驗證 As-Built、At-Rest、Operational 三個狀態？（EU GMP 要求都要驗證）
  • 壓差監控（i）：正壓還是負壓？壓差值設定（通常 ±15-50 Pa）？連續報警？
  • 洩漏率（j）：若為 HPAPI，需明確要求 Class 1（Rh ≤ 5×10⁻⁴ h⁻¹）
  • 物料進出模式（n）：指定 RTP 型號、傳遞艙規格、消毒隧道要求

Section 5 — Design and Construction 設計與建造

19 design requirements for separative devices

Original Text

5 Design and Construction

5.1 Design shall include capability to support qualification and to comply with regulatory requirements.

5.2 Separative-device design shall provide the process, the operator or third party with protection against contamination appropriate to the operation being performed.

5.3 Consideration shall be given to separation means (see Annex A). The separation descriptor, where applicable, shall be taken into account. The risk of concentrated leaks should be addressed.

5.4 Consideration shall be given to malfunction, procedures and ancillary systems involved with the separative-device application (see Annex B).

5.5 Consideration shall be given to access devices and transfer devices (see Annexes C and D).

5.6 Separative devices shall be ergonomically designed for easy access to all internal surfaces and work areas, and with respect to the process undertaken.

5.7 Access devices shall be of the minimum size and number consistent with operation, cleaning and maintenance.

5.8 Consideration shall be given to differential operating pressure, including excursions.

5.9 Hourly leak rate, when applicable, shall be taken into account. The rigidity or flexibility of the separative device shall be taken into account if quantified leak rates are required.

5.10 External influences, such as air flow, vibration and pressure differences, shall be considered to avoid adverse effects on integrity and function.

5.11 Where appropriate, hazard analysis shall be performed.

5.12 Provision for cleaning or decontamination, including possible disposal of the device or its components, shall form part of the design criteria.

5.13 Built-in test facilities and appropriate alarms shall be included.

5.14 Transfer device(s) shall be appropriate to process and routine operation.

5.15 Filtration shall be appropriate for application.

5.16 Volumetric flow rate shall be appropriate for application.

5.17 Exhaust effluents shall undergo treatment where appropriate.

5.18 Whenever possible, items requiring maintenance shall be external to the separative device.

5.19 Materials used in the construction of separative devices, including sealing materials, fans, ventilation systems, piping and associated fittings, shall be chemically and mechanically compatible with the intended processes, process materials, application and decontamination methods. Protection against corrosion and degradation during prolonged use shall be considered. Heat and fire resistant construction materials shall be considered when appropriate. Where appropriate, materials used shall be checked for thermal characteristics, sorption and out-gassing properties. Materials selected for viewing panels shall be tested and proven to remain transparent and resistant to changes that would prevent clear visibility.

導師解析

設計原則的核心邏輯：五大主軸

第 5 條的 19 個設計要求可以歸納為五大主軸：

  • 可驗證性（Qualification）：5.1 — 設計必須支持 IQ/OQ/PQ 驗證，這是製藥應用中的根本要求
  • 保護功能（Protection）：5.2, 5.3 — 根據應用類型提供適當的分離保護
  • 人體工學（Ergonomics）：5.6, 5.7 — 最小化進入孔數量，但確保操作可達性
  • 材料相容性（Compatibility）：5.19 — VHP 去污染時對材料的要求尤為關鍵
  • 可維護性（Maintainability）：5.12, 5.18 — 清潔/去污染設計，維護件外置

設計重點：5.7 最小化進入孔——為什麼重要？

「Access devices shall be of the minimum size and number」——這句話看似簡單，但有深刻的工程含義：

  • 每個手套口都是潛在的洩漏點（leak path）
  • 手套口越多，完整性測試越複雜，失效概率越高
  • EU GMP Annex 1：「Glove integrity testing shall be performed on a risk-based frequency」

設計審核時，應質疑：「這個手套口是否必要？是否有機器人自動化的可能？」

  • a) gauntlets
  • b) glove systems (e.g. sleeve, cuff-ring and glove)
  • c) half-suits and similar devices that allow extended reach
  • d) remote manipulator

Where full-suits are used, reference should be made to appropriate standards. Where possible, consideration should be given to alternative manipulation devices that minimize the number of holes pierced through the structure of the separative device.

6.2.2 Gauntlets, glove systems, half-suits

6.2.2.1 When using gauntlets, glove systems and half-suits, these types of flexible-membrane access device systems shall be designed and constructed to allow for glove change without breaching the separative device. These systems are unlikely to maintain molecular containment, therefore alternative systems should be considered for applications requiring molecular containment.

6.2.2.2 Glove ports and glove cuff rings devices shall be designed for ease of change, integrity testing and security of operation.

6.2.2.3 The following selection criteria shall be considered in choosing gauntlet, glove sleeve and half-suit system materials:

  • a) materials and tools to be handled within the separative device
  • b) temperature limitations of the glove materials
  • c) acceptable permeability
  • d) chemical resistance or mechanical strength, or both
  • e) sorption and desorption of chemicals
  • f) known shelf and service lives of glove material
  • g) differential pressures, including transient excursions
  • h) operations to be performed

6.2.3 Remote manipulation

Remote-handling systems consist of mechanical or servo links between an operator's hands and arms to a mechanical manipulation system within separative devices designed for specific applications.

6.3 Robotic handling

Robotic handling consists of automated systems designed to manipulate materials in a separative device following a process sequence for specific applications.

導師解析

傳遞裝置的核心原則：不能成為弱點

7.1 的「不得降低隔離設備性能」是黃金原則。傳遞裝置是隔離設備最常見的完整性弱點，因為它必須週期性地「開放」以允許物料進出。設計挑戰是：在開放時期如何維持最大程度的分離？

7.2 的另一個關鍵原則：傳遞裝置的洩漏率不得大於隔離設備本體的洩漏率。這意味著：

  • 如果隔離器需要 Class 1（Rh ≤ 5×10⁻⁴），其 RTP 也必須達到同等或更好的洩漏率
  • 不能用低等級的傳遞口「降級」一台高完整性的隔離器

傳遞裝置選擇標準：與 Annex D 對應的分類

傳遞裝置洩漏要求：
傳遞裝置 Rh(transfer) ≤ 隔離器 Rh(isolator)

Annex D 傳遞裝置分類（詳見 Annex D 解析）：
A 類（A1, A2）= 最低隔離性：氣流可自由通過
B 類（B1, B2）= 中等隔離性：雙門密封，可抽真空
C 類（C1, C2）= 帶 HEPA 過濾：濾除顆粒但非完全封閉
D 類（D1, D2）= 雙 HEPA 過濾 + 時間延遲
E 類 = 可滅菌/消毒處理
F 類 = 快速傳遞系統（RTP/Alpha-Beta 口）

選擇法則：分離等級越高的隔離器，需要越高等級的傳遞裝置。

7.3 故障安全設計：電力失效時必須鎖定

「Fail-safe」原則在製藥隔離器設計中至關重要：

  • 電力失效時，電子聯鎖必須默認「鎖閉」（fail-locked）狀態
  • 這防止了電力故障時操作人員無意識地開啟傳遞口，破壞隔離完整性
  • UPS（不斷電系統）設計也需要考慮：停電後有多少時間維持正常壓差？

設計審核（Design Review）時應確認：SIL（安全完整性等級）要求是否已定義？失效模式分析是否覆蓋了停電場景？

Original Text

9.1 General

9.1.1 Selection of test procedures depends upon location, design, configuration and application of the separative device.

9.1.2 If air supply and exhaust systems are an integral part of the separative device, these systems shall also be tested.

9.1.3 In some situations, the air cleanliness in the separative device is not measurable by ISO 14644-1. Therefore alternative test procedures are required. EXAMPLE 1: Testing of molecular contamination. EXAMPLE 2: Testing by particle surface contamination.

When appropriate, the airflow through one open glove port shall be measured by placing an anemometer at the centre of the glove port. The velocity shall be agreed between customer and supplier (guidance value: 0.5 m/s).

9.3 Operating differential pressure

9.3.1 The differential pressure shall be tested in the at-rest and operational states.

9.3.2 When devices depend on differential pressure, the differential pressure should be continuously monitored and alarmed.

9.4 Leak testing

9.4.1 When appropriate, a leak test shall be performed. Guidance is given in Annexes E and F. NOTE: Integrity testing on some separative devices that operate close to atmosphere pressure (less than 1000 Pa) requires detailed procedures and sensitive test equipment.

9.4.2 When appropriate, an induction leak test shall be performed. NOTE: Induction leaks can occur when the velocity across an orifice creates a pressure depression and induces a reverse flow through the orifice (Venturi effect). Devices that operate at low differential pressures may be compromised by induction leakage. Similarly, devices that utilise over pressure or flow may be at risk from induction leakage when operating under transient volume changes such as glove entry or withdrawal.

9.5.1 Testing shall be conducted in accordance with ISO 14644-1, 14644-2, 14698-1 and 14698-2.

9.5.3 The following recommendations for testing are given:

  • a) half-suit/glove testing: 1) on commissioning, 2) prior to and after completion of work, 3) after glove/glove sleeve changes
  • b) pressure testing: 1) on commissioning, 2) after any airflow or filter-pressure parameter changes, 3) after maintenance affecting the separative device envelope or pressure control devices

手套突破測試（Glove Breach Test）：9.2 詳解

測試方法：
在開放的手套口中央放置風速計（anemometer）
測量通過開口的氣流速度

指導值：≥ 0.5 m/s（最低保護速度）

物理含義：
正壓隔離器（無菌充填用）：
  - 內部正壓 → 空氣從內部向外流出
  - 手套破裂時，正壓氣流提供「突破速度」保護
  - 防止外部環境（微粒/微生物）進入隔離器內部

負壓隔離器（HPAPI 含毒用）：
  - 內部負壓 → 空氣從外部流入
  - 手套破裂時，負壓確保毒性物質不外洩
  - 保護操作人員不接觸有毒藥物

0.5 m/s 的依據：
研究表明，0.5 m/s 的面速度可有效阻止
絕大多數微粒的反向流動（ISO 14644-4 也引用此值）

9.3 壓差監控：連續監控的要求

9.3.2 的「should be continuously monitored and alarmed」對製藥無菌應用而言實際上是強制性的：

  • EU GMP Annex 1：隔離器的壓差必須連續監控並設置警報，且需要記錄趨勢數據
  • 典型設定：正壓隔離器 +15 Pa（可範圍 +10 至 +30 Pa）；負壓隔離器 -15 Pa
  • Alert Level：通常設為目標值 ±5 Pa，觸發時增加觀察
  • Action Level：通常設為目標值 ±10 Pa，觸發時需立即評估是否停止生產
  • 在兩個狀態下測試（9.3.1）：靜止（At-rest）和操作（Operational）狀態的壓差可能不同，均需記錄和確認

感應洩漏（Induction Leak）——容易被忽視的風險

9.4.2 的 NOTE 解釋了「感應洩漏」（Venturi 效應）：

  • 原理：高速氣流通過小孔時產生局部負壓，反而將外部空氣「吸入」設備
  • 觸發條件：插入或抽出手套時，會導致隔離器內部短暫的體積/壓力變化
  • 危險性：對低差壓的設備（如某些 RABS）尤其危險，一瞬間的反向流動可帶入外部微粒

這解釋了為什麼手套插入技術（operator training）非常重要：應緩慢、穩定地插入，避免快速動作導致壓力衝擊。

THE conceptual framework — from open LAF to fully enclosed isolator

Original Text

A separative device utilises physical means, aerodynamic means, or both, to create improved levels of separation between the inside and outside of a defined volume. Physical separation means include both rigid and flexible barriers. Aerodynamic means include air/gas flow with or without filtration.

Generally, the assurance of maintaining separation increases with the degree of rigidity of the physical separation. Two measures of this separation are:

  • The separation descriptor [Aa:Bb] — a convenient measure when hourly leak rate is not appropriate
  • The hourly leak rate (Rh) — ISO 10648-2 provides a four-level classification system

ISO 10648-2 classification is generally applied to devices with rigid physical barriers.

Dual mode separative devices usually have a large degree of physical separation in their design, and may be capable of either open or contained atmosphere operation during specific periods of their operation.

Both dynamic and static conditions should be specified with regard to:

  • b) hourly leak rate or separation descriptor, or both
  • c) material ingress (transfer devices)
  • d) material egress (transfer devices)

B.1 General

B.1.1 It is normal to protect the extract or exhaust system by an internally-fitted safe change filter.

B.1.2 Over-pressure in a separative device can be avoided by the use of an oil-filled pressure-relief device. The discharge of the pressure-relief device is connected to the exhaust gas system.

The three main gases in general use:

  • a) nitrogen (most cost-effective)
  • b) helium
  • c) argon

B.3.3 Active gases

Active gases, e.g. ozone, hydrogen peroxide, chlorine dioxide, peracetic acid and steam, may be used for decontamination purposes.

Single-pass gas systems provide flow of gas through the separative devices without recirculation. Gases from bottled or stored systems should be reduced in pressure before admittance to a flow regulator.

安全更換過濾器（Safe Change Filter）——含毒設備的關鍵設計

B.1.1 提到的「safe change filter」（袋進袋出過濾器 / Bag-in Bag-out, BIBO）是含毒隔離器設計的核心要素：

  • HEPA 過濾器在更換時本身已被污染（尤其是 HPAPI 應用）
  • BIBO 設計允許操作員在不接觸過濾器表面的情況下將其密封在袋中取出
  • 必須在 HPAPI（含毒）隔離器的排氣系統上設置，這是監管要求

標準罕見地使用了「can kill by asphyxiation（可致窒息死亡）」這樣強烈的措辭。這是認真的安全警告：

  • 氮氣（N₂）是無色無味的，洩漏時人員無法感知
  • 當環境氧氣濃度降至 16% 以下時，人員會出現認知障礙；降至 6% 以下時可迅速致命
  • 設計要求：惰性氣體設備房間必須安裝氧氣濃度監測警報系統
  • 操作要求：進入含有惰性氣體系統的房間前，必須確認氧氣濃度正常

Annex C (Informative) — Access Devices 進入裝置詳解

Original Text

C.1 Scope

This annex is limited to gloves, gauntlets, glove sleeve systems and half-suits. Gloves tend to form the weakest link in the pressure integrity of a separative device. Operator and product protection is limited by choice of glove system and glove material.

C.2 Glove materials

Common glove materials include:

  • a) Latex (natural rubber): Great flexibility and good mechanical properties. However, not impermeable to gas, perishes in ozone, no flame resistance, poor resistance to esters, acids and bases. Potential for life-threatening allergic reactions should be considered.
  • b) Polychloroprene (Neoprene): Good resistance to oils and greases, self-extinguishing, resistant to ozone, UV, concentrated acids/bases and strong oxidising agents. Unsuitable for hydrocarbons, halogens and esters.
  • c) Nitrile rubber: Good resistance to solvents, aliphatic hydrocarbons and hydroxyl compounds.
  • d) Poly(vinyl chloride) (PVC): Good electrical properties and chemical resistance. Certain elasticity.
  • e) Chlorosulfonated polyethylene: Very good resistance to H₂O₂, white colour allows visual inspection.

C.3 Sandwich/multi-layer gloves

C.3.1 Polychloroprene base + butyl rubber layer + outer polychloroprene: improved gas impermeability due to butyl layer.

C.3.2 For strong oxidising agents: chlorosulfonated polyethylene coating.

C.3.3 For oils, lubricants, solvents: fluoroelastomer terpolymer coating.

C.3.4 PVC loaded with lead: ionizing-radiation-shielding film (delicate handling required, used as inner glove).

C.4 Glove size

If several operators use the same device, the size of the largest hand is chosen. Typical sleeve lengths: 700 mm, 750 mm, 800 mm. Glove shapes: ambidextrous, left-hand, right-hand.

C.5 Available thickness

Varying thicknesses selected as a function of tactile requirement, permeability, chemical resistance, mechanical strength and wear resistance.

C.6 Glove ports

C.6.2 Glove ports may have a glove-port bung facility — a removable item that provides high integrity seal when a glove is not in use.

C.6.3.1 Glove replacement using a glove-port bung: step-by-step procedure involving O-ring, gaiter extrusion, and careful over-glove placement to avoid breaching integrity.

C.6.3.2 Sleeve and glove replacement without using bungs: using cuff rings and the double-groove system on the glove port to minimise breach risk. Key steps include: fitting new sleeve with cuff ring and glove → carefully moving old sleeve hem → replacing O-ring and metal clamp.

C.7 Sleeves and gloves

C.7.2 Glove change procedure: using the cuff ring system, it is possible to change gloves while minimising breach risk. The glove-change system should be practised on a regular basis to ensure operator competence.

C.8 Half-suits

C.8.1 Normally double-lined suit from welded PVC with rigid acrylic vision panel. Attached to separative device for vertical access.

C.8.2 Double lining allows pressurisation between linings for positive-pressure applications (preventing suit "clamping" on operator). Single-skin for negative-pressure.

C.8.3 Half-suits should incorporate suspension points to minimise weight burden beyond ergonomic limits.

導師解析

C.1 的核心聲明：手套是完整性最薄弱環節

「Gloves tend to form the weakest link in the pressure integrity」——這是整個 Annex C 的主題。

實務含義：

  • 隔離器的整體完整性受限於其手套的完整性
  • 即使設備本體是 Class 1 壓力完整性，若手套滲透率高，分子級封閉就無法實現（見 6.2.2.1）
  • 這就是為什麼手套材料選擇、測試頻率、更換程序在 GMP 文件系統中占有重要地位

  • 不能「暴露」內部（無菌區）到外部環境
  • 必須以「由外到內」的包裹方式處理舊手套
  • 新舊手套的交替必須確保始終有「屏障」存在
  • 整個過程必須有標準操作程序並定期演練（C.7.2 明確要求「regular practice」）

這與 EU GMP Annex 1 對培訓和資格確認的要求直接對應：操作員必須展示手套更換的能力。

  1. 你公司的隔離器使用 Neoprene 手套，但新引進的 HPAPI 原料對氯丁橡膠有滲透性問題。請根據 C.2-C.3 的材料清單，推薦更換方案，並說明選擇依據。
  2. C.3.4 提到了含鉛 PVC 手套用於放射性防護，但說明「這類手套需要精心操作」。請推測其脆弱性原因，以及在核醫藥生產中的操作注意事項。

EU GMP Annex 1 要求手套材料選擇必須有文件記錄的風險評估，包括與產品和去污染方法的相容性。

This annex provides examples of transfer devices referred to in 7.2. These diagrams are intended to be illustrative examples only and are not normative design specifications.

D.2 A1 transfer device

When operated in accordance with a validated transfer procedure, air can flow freely through the A1 transfer device between the background environment and the separative-device environment when the door is open.

When operated in accordance with a validated transfer procedure, air flows freely through the A2 device out of the separative device environment (dynamic holes, mouse holes).

D.4 B1 transfer device

When operated with a correct sequence or interlocked transfer procedure, the B1 device does not permit the direct passage of air between environments. However, air from each environment can be trapped and then released into the other environment.

EXAMPLES: Double-door sealed transfer chambers, bagging ports, telescopic waste ports, simple docking devices.

D.5 B2 transfer device

The B2 device has double sealed doors and facilities that permit the purging and evacuation of the transfer device to ensure compatibility of environments before breaching the interconnection to the separative-device environment. Evacuation gases require safe disposal. NOTE: Evacuation may not be possible with liquid transfer.

D.6 C1 transfer device

Doors and HEPA filters. When used in a positive-pressure device, operated correctly, does not allow unfiltered air from background to reach the separative device, but may allow unfiltered air from inside to reach the background. NOT suitable for negative-pressure devices or where operator/third-party protection is required. EXAMPLE: Single-filtered transfer chambers.

D.7 C2 transfer device

Doors and HEPA filters. Used in negative-pressure separative devices. Correctly operated, prevents unfiltered air from background to device (passes under work surface and exits via exhaust). Not appropriate for positive-pressure devices. EXAMPLE: Single-filtered transfer chambers.

D.8 D1 transfer device

Doors and HEPA filters on both sides. Operated correctly, neither unfiltered air from background reaches device nor vice versa. EXAMPLES: Double-filter transfer chambers, or separative devices used as a transfer device.

D.9 D2 transfer device

D1 transfer device fitted with interlocked and time-delayed ingress/egress control which, when operated with a valid transfer procedure, creates a period to allow surface decontamination to minimise contamination transference.

D.10 E transfer device

Subject to sanitation together with its contents before being opened into other areas that have been subject to sanitation. EXAMPLES: Gassable/autoclavable transfer devices, permanently connected autoclaves, certain transfer separative devices and docking devices.

D.11 F transfer device

Docks and seals onto a separative device. Commonly used as a transport container. May have disconnects for air bleed. EXAMPLES: Rapid transfer systems, standard mechanical interfaces, split valve connections (Alpha-Beta ports/RTP).

導師解析

比喻：F 型 RTP（Alpha-Beta 口）如同太空站氣閘

F 型傳遞裝置（快速傳遞口 RTP，也稱 Alpha-Beta port）的工作原理：

  • Alpha（母口）：永久安裝在隔離器本體上，有密封機構
  • Beta（公口）：安裝在容器/傳遞包裝上，可分離
  • 對接過程：Beta 口靠近 Alpha 口 → 兩個密封面對接 → 同步開啟內部門 → 物料在「零暴露」條件下傳入

就像太空站的氣閘：對接艙（太空船）與空間站（隔離器）對接，開啟內部門，太空人（物料）在不暴露於外太空（外部環境）的情況下轉移。

RTP 的外部接觸面（Beta 口外表面）可在對接前使用 VHP 噴霧或酒精消毒。

C1 的嚴重警告：不可用於含毒負壓設備

D.6 明確指出「C1 transfer devices are NOT suitable for negative-pressure separative devices」且「NOT recommended where operator and third-party protection is required」。

原因：C1 的 HEPA 是單側的（隔離器側），在負壓模式下，外部（背景環境）的未過濾空氣會直接進入隔離器——這對含毒藥物是災難性的，因為毒性物質可能通過背景環境空氣向外洩漏。

設計審查時必須確認：HPAPI 隔離器使用 C2（負壓）或更高等級的傳遞裝置，絕不使用 C1。

CDMO 實務：如何選擇傳遞裝置類型

根據應用場景的傳遞裝置選擇指南：

  • 無菌充填隔離器（正壓）：日常物料進出 → B1 或 D1；容器/包裝進出 → F 型 RTP
  • HPAPI 充填隔離器（負壓）：物料進出 → B1 或 C2；廢棄物出 → A1（bag-out）；特殊 → E 型（可消毒）
  • 核醫藥製備熱室：物料進出 → B2（需惰性氣體相容）；廢棄物 → E 型（需消毒）
  • 電子微環境：晶圓傳遞 → F 型（SMIF/FOUP pod 介面）

練習思考

  1. 一台正壓無菌充填隔離器（B 型）目前使用 B1 傳遞艙。客戶要求升級到 RTP（F 型）。請說明升級的技術優勢和 URS 中需要新增的規格。
  2. D.9 的 D2 傳遞裝置包含「時間延遲」功能，允許表面去污染。請設計一個 D2 傳遞艙的操作程序，包括進物料、等待時間設定和驗證方法。

Annex F (Informative) — Parjo Leak Test Method Parjo 洩漏測試法

Quick, non-intrusive leak rate assessment for devices operating near atmospheric pressure

Original Text

F.1 Background

Parjo is the name given to a method for the leak rate assessment of separative devices operating at pressures close to atmospheric. The method was originated by and named after K. Parkinson and W. F. Jones. This test provides a (relatively) quick and versatile method of determining leak rates. It can be used on contaminated devices providing the pressure tapping is suitably protected, thus avoiding lengthy down times — there are no intrusive test instruments. The short duration of the test tends to reduce effects of changes in temperature and atmospheric pressure. It is a sensitive test for minor leaks.

F.2 Principle

The Parjo method uses a pressure-sensitive detergent film (meniscus) injected into a glass tube of known dimensions and a reference vessel of known volume. With stable conditions and the device isolated at test pressure, any change in separative-device pressure is reflected by movement of the piston (meniscus) towards the lower pressure. This movement represents a volume change.

Keeping observations short (no more than 5 min), temperature and barometric variations can be ignored.

F.3 Equipment

F.3.2 Equipment list:

  • Parjo tube Type A (glass tube of known bore)
  • Metric scale (clip on)
  • Spring clips
  • Rubber stopper (bored for Parjo tube, 19mm or 21mm)
  • Clear glass bottle — volume 2500 cm³
  • Propipette rubber bulb with 3 valves
  • Rubber tubing (6mm bore)
  • U-tube manometer or capsule gauge
  • Stop watch
  • Needle valve (for introducing controlled leak)
  • Pressure/vacuum source
  • Bubble solution: 50/50 % household liquid soap and clean tap water

F.4 Procedure

F.4.2 Key steps:

  • Seal device and set test pressure: +1000 Pa or as specified
  • Allow ~30 min for temperature equilibration
  • Inject bubble into Parjo tube via propipette to form meniscus
  • Observe meniscus movement and record time (3-5 min observation window)
  • If device is negative pressure and leaking: bubble moves towards reference vessel
  • If device is positive pressure and leaking: bubble moves away from reference vessel
  • Measure deflection d (cm) over time t (min) and calculate Rh

F.5 Calculation of results

F.5.2 Formula:

Rh = (Ap × d × 60) / (Vr × t)

Where:

  • Ap = cross-sectional area of Parjo tube = 0.127 cm² (for 4mm bore tube)
  • d = deflection of meniscus (cm)
  • Vr = volume of reference bottle (cm³) — typically 2500 cm³ or 2700 cm³
  • t = time (min)

F.5.3 Examples:

  • 0.8 cm deflection in 5 min, 2500 cm³ bottle: Rh = 0.127 × 0.8 × 60 / (2500 × 5) = 4.88 × 10⁻⁴ h⁻¹
  • 1.0 cm deflection in 5 min, 2700 cm³ bottle: Rh = 0.127 × 1 × 60 / (2700 × 5) = 5.64 × 10⁻⁴ h⁻¹
  • 1.5 cm deflection in 3 min, 2700 cm³ bottle: Rh = 0.127 × 1.5 × 60 / (2700 × 3) = 1.41 × 10⁻³ h⁻¹

F.5.4 Test certification

If no detectable deflection, introduce a controlled leak within acceptable limits (via needle valve) to prove the test method is working. State on certificate: "actual leak rate does not exceed the introduced leak and is thus acceptable."

導師解析

Parjo 公式計算實例

Parjo 公式：
Rh = (Ap × d × 60) / (Vr × t)

已知參數（標準裝置）：
- Ap = 0.127 cm²（4mm 管徑）
- Vr = 2500 cm³（標準玻璃瓶）

實例 1：觀察到 0.8 cm 漂移，用時 5 分鐘
Rh = (0.127 × 0.8 × 60) / (2500 × 5)
   = 6.096 / 12500
   = 4.88 × 10⁻⁴ h⁻¹
   → Class 1 邊界值（≤ 5×10⁻⁴）✓

實例 2：觀察到 1.0 cm 漂移，用時 5 分鐘（2700 cm³ 瓶）
Rh = (0.127 × 1.0 × 60) / (2700 × 5)
   = 7.62 / 13500
   = 5.64 × 10⁻⁴ h⁻¹
   → 超過 Class 1 限值，屬 Class 2 ✗

解讀：
Rh 越小 = 洩漏越少 = 完整性越好
Class 1 隔離器的 Rh 應 ≤ 5×10⁻⁴ h⁻¹

Parjo 測試的操作技巧與常見問題

關鍵技巧：

  • 玻璃瓶選擇：必須是透明玻璃瓶（不可用有色瓶），便於觀察氣泡
  • 肥皂水配製：50/50 優質液態皂 + 自來水，廉價洗潔精會殘留影響液膜
  • 溫度平衡：安裝後至少等待 30 分鐘，讓所有部件達到相同溫度
  • 次生氣泡：取讀數前確保 Parjo 管兩端沒有次生氣泡，否則會干擾結果

F.4.2 的智慧：「沒有可見洩漏也要引入受控洩漏」

若設備洩漏率低於測試方法的檢測限，標準允許引入一個小的受控洩漏（針閥），驗證測試方法在工作。這防止了「測試方法本身失效」卻誤以為設備完好的情況。

CDMO 實務：何時選用 Parjo 法？

Parjo 法特別適合以下場景：

  • 生產中週期性監測：每批次前後的快速完整性確認（不需要 1 小時停機）
  • 維護後快速確認：隔離器維護（如手套更換）後的快速洩漏確認
  • 污染設備的測試：含毒 HPAPI 隔離器不需要暴露測試人員給污染物
  • IQ/OQ 驗收測試：快速確認安裝後的基準洩漏率

RABS vs 隔離器：技術選擇的監管考量

EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 沒有直接禁止 RABS，但明顯傾向隔離器：

  • Annex 1 §8.2：「The use of isolators offers the greatest protection.」
  • 新建設施的趨勢：監管機構在 PAI（Pre-Approval Inspection）中越來越期望看到隔離器，特別是新產品
  • 現有設施：有效的 RABS + Grade B 背景環境 + 嚴格的 EM 程序仍然可以接受

CDMO 在做技術路線選擇時，應考慮：未來主要市場的監管傾向（FDA、EMA、NMPA 的立場可能不同）。

综合練習思考

  1. 你的 CDMO 正在評估兩個方案：方案 A = 封閉 RABS + Grade B 環境；方案 B = 隔離器 + Grade C 環境。根據 ISO 14644-7 的框架，列出你評估報告的核心比較指標（至少 8 個維度）。
  2. EU GMP Annex 1 §8.17 要求手套完整性測試「基於風險評估的頻率」。請為一台用於高毒性 HPAPI（OEB Band 5，OEL < 0.1 µg/m³）充填的負壓隔離器起草手套完整性測試的風險評估，確定適當的測試頻率。
  3. 一家客戶將原來在傳統 Grade B 環境填充的標準小分子藥物遷移到新隔離器系統（Grade C 背景）。根據 ISO 14644-7 第 4 條的要求，你需要重新評估和文件記錄哪些內容？是否需要重新進行培地充填（Media Fill）驗證？
