Introduction — The Case for Systematic Symbol Validation

Why symbols need a disciplined development process | Pages i–v

Introduction

The ISO 15223 series of International Standards addresses symbols that can be used to convey information that is essential for the safe and proper use of medical devices. As such, in most regulatory domains the symbols are required to be presented with the device. The information can be required to be presented on the device itself, as part of the label, or provided with the device.

Many countries require that their own language be used to present textual information with medical devices. This presents problems to device manufacturers and users. Faced with the requirement to produce labelling in a number of different languages, manufacturers might have to increase the size of the package or label, thus potentially increasing packaging waste, or compressing the information, thus compromising legibility. Users presented with devices labelled in a number of different languages can experience confusion and delay in locating the needed information in an appropriate language. ISO 15223-1 proposes solutions to these problems through the use of internationally recognized symbols, with precisely defined meanings, that are independent of language.

While compiling the symbols presented in ISO 15223-1, it was recognised that a systematic methodology for the development and presentation of symbols was needed. ISO/TC 210 began by formulating a "best practices" document, Guide to the development and registration of symbols for use in the labelling of medical devices.

When this guide was circulated to interested parties, a number of regulatory authorities were of the opinion that they would have greater confidence in the use of symbols to replace text if the best practices set out in the Guide were expressed as normative requirements in a standards document. Some of the best practices for symbols development and usage have been translated into normative requirements in ISO 15223.

Much of the information required on a medical device itself, as part of the label, or provided with the device constitutes information for safety within an integrated approach to risk management. As with any risk control measure, the manufacturer needs to verify the effectiveness of the information for safety before it can be accepted. The use of standardized symbols agreed by consensus on an international basis can address the confusion that users can experience when presented with labelling in a number of different languages.

However, the proliferation of symbols without control and harmonization is undesirable and detracts from the effectiveness of using symbols to convey information for safety. In addition, some users and regulatory authorities have concerns that the unrestricted use of symbols without validation can represent a hazard.

This part of ISO 15223 includes methods for validating those candidate symbols being proposed for inclusion in ISO 15223-1. It can also be used by manufacturers and regulators for validating symbols for use with medical devices, where suitable symbols are not standardized.

"When the processes detailed in this part of ISO 15223 have been carried out, the probability of misinterpretation of symbols accepted in ISO 15223-1 is reduced."

This document was prepared by ISO/TC 210 to influence the quality of symbols developed for use in labelling by establishing a process that addresses the need to ensure quality of symbols accepted in ISO 15223-1 by:

  • establishing need;
  • providing guidance on development of symbols;
  • carrying out testing to make sure that the candidate symbol is suitable for adoption and use.

為什麼需要符號驗證？

問題根源：多語言標籤的困境

全球醫療器材貿易面臨一個根本矛盾：各國要求本國語言標籤，但製造商無法無限擴大包裝。符號（symbols）是突破語言障礙的關鍵工具——但未經驗證的符號本身就是風險。

  • 包裝空間有限 → 文字壓縮 → 可讀性下降
  • 多語言並排 → 使用者找不到母語資訊 → 誤用風險
  • 符號若無統一定義 → 各國解讀不同 → 危害

比喻：從「最佳實踐指南」到「規範性標準」

想像一家餐廳的食品安全守則：一開始是廚房內部的「最佳做法手冊」，後來衛生局介入，要求將這些做法寫進法規。ISO 15223-2的誕生路徑完全相同：

ISO/TC 210的最佳實踐指南 → 監管機構認為指導性不夠 → 轉為具約束力的「規範性要求」（normative requirements）

核心原則：符號 = 風險控制措施

ISO 15223-2引入了一個重要的思維框架：標籤上的符號不僅是「設計元素」，而是風險控制措施（risk control measure）。這意味著：

  • 符號必須「有效」才能被接受 → 需要驗證
  • 驗證邏輯與ISO 14971風險管理完全對接
  • 未驗證的符號 = 未確效的風險控制 = 監管不合規

CDMO實際意義

對於CDMO而言，這段Introduction傳達了一個重要訊號：當您為客戶的產品設計標籤，使用非ISO 15223-1標準符號時，您正在承擔未驗證的風險控制措施責任。ISO 15223-2提供了您需要的驗證框架。

Clause 1 — Scope

What this standard covers and the presumption of acceptable residual risk

1 Scope

This part of ISO 15223 specifies a process for developing, selecting and validating symbols for inclusion in ISO 15223-1.

The purpose of this part of ISO 15223 is to ensure that symbols included in ISO 15223-1 are readily understood by the target group.

"If the symbol validation process detailed in this part of ISO 15223 has been complied with, then the residual risks, as defined in ISO 14971 and IEC 62366, associated with the usability of a medical device symbol are presumed to be acceptable, unless there is objective evidence to the contrary."

This part of ISO 15223 is not restricted to symbols intended to meet regulatory requirements or specified in regulatory guidelines on labelling.

Dual Application

The standard serves two audiences:

  1. ISO/TC 210 Working Groups — to evaluate and validate symbols proposed for inclusion in ISO 15223-1 (the standardized symbol library)
  2. Manufacturers and regulators — to validate non-standard symbols where no suitable standardized symbol exists

標準化符號 vs. 自訂符號：使用決策樹

兩類使用者，一套流程

ISO 15223-2的Scope明確了兩類使用場景：

  • 場景A：向ISO提交新符號，申請納入ISO 15223-1標準符號庫 → 必須走完完整的ISO/TC 210評審流程
  • 場景B：製造商自行使用非標準符號（因為ISO 15223-1沒有合適選項）→ 可以參照本標準的驗證方法自行執行

「presumed acceptable」的法律意涵

Scope中最重要的句子是「residual risks... are presumed to be acceptable」。這是一個法律層面的「presumption」（推定）：

  • 符合ISO 15223-2流程 → 監管機構推定殘餘風險可接受
  • 但「unless there is objective evidence to the contrary」→ 若有客觀證據顯示符號仍被誤解，推定不成立
  • 這與ISO 14971的「風險可接受性判斷」框架完全一致

實務含義：遵循ISO 15223-2的驗證，在法規審查時有「安全港」保護；但不代表永遠免責。

CDMO決策框架

面對標籤符號選擇時，CDMO應依序問：

  1. ISO 15223-1有這個符號嗎？→ 有：直接使用，無需驗證
  2. ISO 7000 / IEC 60417有嗎？→ 有：考慮採用，可能需要適用性評估
  3. 完全沒有？→ 必須走ISO 15223-2驗證流程，才能在法規層面站得住腳

標準體系關係圖解

ISO 15223系列的分工

把ISO 15223系列想成一家餐廳的「菜單管理系統」：

  • ISO 15223-1 = 菜單（已核准可使用的符號清單）
  • ISO 15223-2 = 新菜品審核流程（如何把新符號納入菜單）

  • IEC 80416-1 = 菜品呈現規範（符號的視覺設計規則）

IEC 62366與ISO 15223-2的關係

雖然IEC 62366（可用性工程）列在Bibliography而非Normative References，但它與ISO 15223-2的關係極為密切：

  • ISO 15223-2提供符號驗證的具體方法
  • 遵循ISO 15223-2可視為滿足IEC 62366中符號相關可用性要求的一種方式
  • Scope明確引用IEC 62366的「residual risks」定義，兩者互相呼應

實務上，QA/RA審查符號時，應同時對照IEC 62366的使用情景分析（use scenario analysis）。

Clause 3 — Terms and Definitions

11 key terms that define the vocabulary of symbol validation

3 Terms and Definitions

3.1 Appropriateness Ranking Test

Procedure for ranking candidate symbols according to their considered appropriateness for representing a particular meaning.

3.2 Associative Strength Test

Procedure for comparing the strength of an association between a candidate symbol and several possible meanings.

3.3 Characteristic Information

Information that represents the property or properties of a symbol.

3.4 Comprehension Test

Procedure for quantifying the degree of understanding by the target group of the candidate symbol.

NOTE: Adapted from ISO 9186-1:2007, definition 3.1.

3.5 Description

Normative text that defines the purpose, the application and the use of the symbol.

NOTE: Adapted from IEC 80416-1:2008, definition 3.2.

3.6 Symbol Concept

Diagrammatic representation of a candidate symbol which conveys the essential elements of the symbol but which has not yet been produced as a symbol original on a pattern template.

3.7 Symbol Original

Drawing of a graphical symbol, including the corner markings, prepared in accordance with IEC 80416-1 and, where appropriate, ISO 80416-2.

NOTE: Adapted from IEC 80416-1:2008, definition 3.8.

3.8 Symbol Used in Medical Device Labelling

Graphic representation appearing on the label and/or associated documentation of a medical device which communicates characteristic information without the supplier or receiver of the information relying on knowledge of the language of a particular nation or people.

NOTE: The symbol can be an abstract pictorial or graphic representation, or one that uses familiar objects including alphanumeric characters.

3.9 Title

Unique name by which a graphical symbol is identified and spoken of.

NOTE: Adapted from IEC 80416-1:2008, definition 3.9.

3.10 Target Group

User population characterized by such factors as age, gender, education, occupation, cultural background, experience and training, and physical ability when relevant.

3.11 Usability

Characteristic of the symbol which establishes effectiveness, efficiency, ease of user learning and user satisfaction.

NOTE: Adapted from IEC 62366.

術語解析：符號驗證的概念地圖

三個層次的符號形態

理解符號開發流程，首先要掌握三個術語之間的演進關係：

  • Symbol Concept（符號概念）：草圖階段，傳達核心想法，不需要正式格式
  • Symbol Original（符號原稿）：按IEC 80416-1製作的正式圖形，包含角標記和基本模板，是提交ISO登記的法定格式
  • Symbol Used in Labelling（標籤用符號）：最終出現在產品上的版本，可以有不同尺寸但意義不變

Target Group的重要性

「目標族群」的定義直接影響驗證設計：

  • 醫療專業人員 vs. 一般消費者 → 測試樣本不同
  • 已接受培訓者 vs. 無培訓者 → 理解閾值可能不同
  • 不同文化背景 → 某些符號在特定文化有特殊含義（如顏色、方向）

CDMO的產品（如預填充注射器）的目標族群通常包括護理人員和有時患者自行注射，需要同時測試兩組。

Clause 4 — Principles for Identification and Development of New Symbols

Establishing need; horizontal vs. restricted application symbols

4 Principles for Identification and Development of New Symbols

4.1 Identifying the Need for a Symbol

When identifying the need for a symbol, the following elements shall be considered:

  1. the benefits derived from using a symbol;
  2. the intended target group:
    • their training and knowledge of, and experience with, the medical device(s) where the candidate symbol is intended to be used;
    • their general medical knowledge.

4.2 Symbols with Horizontal Applications

The development of symbols with horizontal applications to a wide range of medical devices in a number of geographic or regulatory areas should be encouraged when there is a clear and significant need for a symbol. This is particularly true when these symbols are used to meet regulatory requirements. Such symbols shall be considered prime candidates for inclusion in ISO 15223-1.

4.3 Symbols for Use within a Restricted Range of Device Types

The development of symbols for use within a restricted range of device types or within specific geographic or regulatory areas should be encouraged only if the need is clear and significant. The process of identifying the need should take account of the target group and the arrangements foreseen for publicizing the new symbols.

Such symbols can be prime candidates for inclusion in standards for particular kinds of medical devices. However, they are not prime candidates for inclusion in ISO 15223-1. When, in the opinion of ISO/TC 210, there is sufficient international interest in a particular symbol of this type, the symbol may be included in ISO 15223-1 and any geographic or other limitations on its use shall be indicated.

符號開發第一步：建立需求正當性

需求識別的商業邏輯

Clause 4.1要求評估「符號的好處」，這實際上是一個成本效益分析：

  • 好處：減少多語言標籤成本、降低誤用風險、符合法規要求
  • 成本：設計費用、驗證測試費用、提交ISO流程費用
  • 決策標準：好處必須明確（clear）且顯著（significant）

這防止了符號的「無謂增生」——如果文字就夠用，就不應該創造新符號。

CDMO場景：什麼情況需要新符號？

典型觸發情境：

  • 客戶產品有特殊儲存要求（如「避免震動」），ISO 15223-1無對應符號
  • 產品進入多個需要本語言標籤的市場，包裝空間不足
  • 監管機構要求使用符號替代文字以節省空間
  • 產品目標族群識字率低，符號比文字更有效

確認需求後，下一步是查現有符號庫（Clause 7.1），避免重複創造。

Clause 5 — Process for Selecting and Validating Symbols for ISO 15223-1

Two-stage evaluation; Option 1 (register first) vs. Option 2 (test first)

5.1 General

Symbols presented to ISO/TC 210 for inclusion in ISO 15223-1 shall be developed, selected and validated using the process described in this clause.

Proposals for symbols to be included in ISO 15223-1 shall be submitted to ISO/TC 210/WG 3 via the ISO/TC 210 Secretariat at: symbolsinfo@aami.org. Any person or group may propose a symbol.

5.2 Initial Evaluation

Symbols shall be submitted to ISO/TC 210/WG 3 with at least the information required by items a) to g) of Annex A for initial evaluation. If the symbol is an existing symbol or based on existing symbology, relevant parts of the requirements in Annex A may be provided by reference to published documents.

ISO/TC 210/WG 3 will undertake an initial evaluation and provide:

  • an assessment of the symbol proposal;
  • an opinion as to whether further development of the symbol is recommended;
  • if recommended, advice on whether symbol registration should be sought via ISO/TC 145/SC 3 or IEC/SC 3C.

5.3 Second Evaluation — Option 1 (Register First, Then Test)

The proposer submits documentation to ISO/TC 145/SC 3 or IEC/SC 3C for registration. After registration is approved, the proposer carries out the safety tests described in Clauses 8 and 9.

Key steps under Option 1:

  1. Prepare and submit registration documentation + Annex A items a–g to ISO/TC 210 Secretariat
  2. ISO/TC 210 submits to ISO/TC 145/SC 3 or IEC/SC 3C for registration
  3. If approved for registration: carry out tests (Clauses 8–9), submit results (Annex A item i)
  4. If not approved: proposer may still proceed with testing if they wish to continue
  5. ISO/TC 210/WG 3 undertakes second evaluation based on test results
  6. If acceptance criteria met: submitted for adoption in ISO 15223-1 through normal ISO voting
  7. Adopted symbol published in ISO 15223-1

5.3 Second Evaluation — Option 2 (Test First, Then Register)

The proposer carries out all safety tests first, then submits for registration simultaneously with the test results.

  1. Prepare registration documentation AND carry out tests (Clauses 8–9); submit test results + Annex A items a–g and i to ISO/TC 210 Secretariat
  2. ISO/TC 210/WG 3 second evaluation based on test results
  3. If criteria met and not yet registered: submit to registrar for registration
  4. If registration approved: submit for adoption in ISO 15223-1 through normal voting
  5. Adopted symbol published in ISO 15223-1

兩條路徑的策略選擇

比喻：藥品申請的類似結構

兩條路徑類似藥品開發中的選擇：

  • Option 1 = 先取得臨床試驗許可（IND），再做臨床試驗
  • Option 2 = 先在研究層面完成試驗數據，再正式申請許可

兩條路都能到達終點，只是風險分配不同。

初始評審：提交的最低要求

Annex A items a–g是初始評審的最低門檻：

  • a) 需求識別 + 風險分析結果
  • b) 符號的標題和描述
  • c) 目標族群 + 預期器材 + 法規市場
  • d) 相似意義的現有符號
  • e) 提案者身份和聯絡資料
  • f) 符號原稿（在基本模板上）
  • g) 特殊考量（如方向性）

Items h（登記狀態）和i（測試結果）不是初始評審必要條件，但若已有應提交。

製造商自用符號的「簡化路徑」

Clause 5是針對提交ISO的流程。製造商自行開發非標準符號（不提交ISO）時，只需要：

  • 執行Clause 6的風險分類
  • 執行Clause 7的概念開發（包括查現有符號庫）
  • 執行Clause 8的評估（依風險等級選擇測試類型）
  • 確認Clause 9的接受標準
  • 記錄全部過程（Annex A格式可作參考）

Clause 6 — Classification of Risk

Risk analysis for symbol substitution; no/low vs. moderate/high safety relevance

6 Classification of Risk

A risk analysis evaluating the substitution of text with a symbol shall be performed.

The risk analysis shall pay particular attention to the following:

  1. the severity of harm that is likely to occur if the intended meaning of the symbol is misunderstood;
  2. the risk(s) associated with the presence on the medical device label of text and its translation into multiple languages;
  3. the possibility that the language of the user might not be present on the label.

As part of the risk analysis, the information associated with the candidate symbol shall be classified as one of the following:

  1. No to low safety relevance.
    EXAMPLE: Catalogue number (ISO 15223-1:2007, symbol 5.15); Fragile, handle with care (ISO 15223-1:2007, symbol 5.5).
  2. Moderate to high safety relevance.
    EXAMPLE: Do not resterilize (ISO 15223-1:2007, symbol 5.25); Use by date (ISO 15223-1:2007, symbol 5.12).

The results of the risk analysis shall be documented in the proposal for the adoption of a symbol into ISO 15223-1 (see item a) of Annex A).

NOTE 1: When considering the possible consequences of a misunderstanding, those proposing a new symbol need to focus on the typical use of the candidate symbol. Subclause 4.2 of ISO 15223-1:2007 requires that a manufacturer determine that no additional risk is incurred by use of a symbol in that part of ISO 15223 in a particular situation, or that appropriate strategies to control the risk are adopted.

NOTE 2: Additional information regarding risk analysis is available in ISO 14971.

風險分類：決定測試深度的關鍵步驟

67%接受標準的由來 — 與ISO 14971的聯結

ISO 15223-2本身並未在Clause 6直接規定67%閾值，但這個數字來自ISO 9186-1（Clause 8-9所引用的測試標準）。其背後邏輯：

  • 67% = 2/3的目標族群能正確理解
  • 這是「can be considered acceptable」的最低門檻
  • 在ISO 14971框架下：若2/3以上理解正確，殘餘誤解風險被認為可接受
  • 對於中高風險符號，接受標準由提案者基於風險分析結果設定，可能高於67%

實際上，「勿再滅菌」這類符號誤解可能導致感染，其接受標準通常遠高於67%。

比喻：風險分類的直覺理解

想像兩個場景：

  • 低風險：超市貨架上「易碎」標誌被誤解 → 最壞結果是商品損壞
  • 高風險：無菌預填充注射器上「單次使用」符號被誤解 → 可能導致感染或劑量錯誤

風險等級直接決定驗證力度：前者「比比看哪個更好理解」就夠了；後者需要嚴格的量化測試。

CDMO風險分類實例

  • 低風險 批號標識、製造商標誌
  • 中等 儲存溫度範圍（誤解可能影響藥效）
  • 高風險 勿冷凍、無菌標識、單次使用（誤解可能直接危害患者）

Clause 7 — Concept Development

Search existing symbol databases first; design principles for symbol originals

7 Concept Development

7.1 Existence of Other Symbols

During the symbol development process, existing symbol sources shall be reviewed to establish whether symbol(s) that address the need identified in 4.1 already exist. This review should include at least:

  • ISO 15223-1
  • ISO 7000
  • ISO 7010
  • IEC 60417-DB
  • IEC/TR 60878
  • EN 980

NOTE 1: Existing basic design elements can be used as the basis for new symbols.

Standards produced by technical committees with expertise in the areas of technology relevant to the proposed new symbol(s) should also be reviewed.

NOTE 2: The IEC/ISO database can be an aid in identifying existing symbols with overlapping meaning, and should be consulted early in the development process.

7.2 Symbol Design

This stage involves both the generation of trial designs and the collection of design elements in use.

Symbols considered for initial assessment of suitability need only exist in concept form. However, once trial designs have been selected for further testing, a more formal design, following the methods of IEC 80416-1, is advisable. The basic pattern to guide design symbol graphics appears in B.2.

It is useful to agree on a title and description before committing designs to paper. Useful guidance on design issues is set out in ISO/TR 7239. Principles for the application of arrows in symbols are found in ISO 80416-2.

In addition, IEC 80416-3 provides guidelines on the application of symbols in order to maintain visual clarity and overall consistency when such symbols are applied. This includes such important concepts as the proper way to use the general prohibition symbol (see Annex D of ISO 15223-1:2007).

Care should be taken to avoid ambiguity or confusion arising from changes in orientation of the symbol design. If the meaning of the symbol is dependent on orientation, for example where arrows identify specific directions, this shall be stated in the description.

Consideration shall also be given to the legibility of the symbol, for example when reproduced in a small print size or when positioned near other, possibly related, symbols.

概念開發：先查、再設計

「先查」的強制性要求

Clause 7.1使用「shall be reviewed」——這是強制性的。在設計任何新符號之前，必須查閱至少六個現有符號庫：

  1. ISO 15223-1：醫療器材符號的主要標準（首選）
  2. ISO 7000：設備用圖形符號通用庫
  3. ISO 7010：安全顏色和安全標誌
  4. IEC 60417-DB：設備用圖形符號的IEC資料庫
  5. IEC/TR 60878：醫療電氣設備用圖形符號
  6. EN 980：歐盟醫療器材標籤符號標準

未先查詢而設計新符號，可能浪費資源，也可能與現有符號衝突。

設計原則：先定義，再繪圖

Clause 7.2的關鍵建議：「先同意標題和描述，再動手設計」。這個順序很重要：

  • 先定義：這個符號叫什麼名字？用一句話描述其用途和應用範圍？
  • 再設計：依據定義，哪種圖形最能傳達這個意思？
  • 避免「設計師偏好」主導，應由「意思定義」主導

就像寫法規文件先寫定義，再寫要求條款。

方向性和可讀性的設計要點

  • 方向性：若符號含箭頭或方向性元素（如「上方」），必須在description中明確說明，因為符號旋轉後可能改變意思
  • 小尺寸可讀性：醫療器材包裝空間有限，符號可能印刷極小，設計時需確保細節清晰
  • 近鄰效應：當多個符號排列在一起時，不應相互干擾或產生新的誤讀
  • 禁止符號：「禁止」疊加（圓圈斜線）的使用方式有嚴格規定（見ISO 15223-1:2007 Annex D）

Clause 8 — Evaluation Methods

Four evaluation types: appropriateness ranking, comprehension, memory, and usability testing

8 Evaluation

8.1 Testing Early Symbol Concepts

When developing a new symbol where no prior model exists, evaluation should result in a progressive reduction of multiple concepts to a preferred candidate symbol.

At the initial stage, there can be more versions of a symbol than can reasonably be tested for comprehension. The most appropriate candidates for further testing may be selected using a comprehensibility judgement test.

A comprehensibility judgement test is intended to answer the question: "Which of these symbols best represents a given meaning?"

A simple form of a comprehensibility judgement test is the "appropriateness ranking test". In this test, a sample of the target group places the candidate symbols in order of merit according to their considered appropriateness for representing a particular meaning.

In this method, a sample of the target group is given a meaning that they will be asked to associate with the candidate symbols. The candidate symbols are presented to the sample for ranking according to their appropriateness. If there are only a few candidate symbols, they can be embedded within a group of similar symbols in order to provide more choice.

The top two or three symbols that best represent the given meaning are selected for further evaluation. Candidate symbols that are rarely selected are eliminated from further consideration.

Clause 7 of ISO 9186-1:2007 describes a more detailed method that may be used for testing the comprehensibility of candidate symbols with either printed material or computer screen presentation.

8.2 Comprehension Testing

Comprehension testing quantifies the degree of understanding of candidate symbols by the target group.

Comprehension testing is intended to answer the questions: "What do you think this means?" or "What action would you take in response to this symbol?"

A simple form of a comprehension test is the "associative strength test". A candidate symbol can have more than one interpretation. The associative strength test can be used to show if there is an appropriate association between the candidate symbol and its intended meaning.

In this method, each candidate symbol is presented to a sample of the target group who are asked to rank from low to high the association between the candidate symbol and several possible meanings. It is not advisable to use a symbol with weak association as that symbol is likely to be misinterpreted.

Clause 6 of ISO 9186-1:2007 describes a more detailed method that may be used for testing comprehension of candidate symbols.

8.3 Memory Testing

It is rare that everyone in the target group understands a new symbol's meaning on first exposure. Some learning can be involved. Some symbols are easily learned while others require significant effort to memorize.

In memory testing, subjects are taught the meaning of the symbols, then their mastery of the symbols and their meaning is demonstrated. The ease of learning can indicate whether a symbol is intuitive and can be inferred by the time it takes the subject to learn the meaning of the symbol. Retention is then tested after a period of time has elapsed by asking the subjects to recall the meaning of the symbols.

8.4 Usability Testing

Usability testing is the method of systematically observing and recording representative subjects' understanding of symbols in the context of use. It might be appropriate to employ usability testing in situations where the symbol:

  1. has high safety implications,
  2. has a large and varied target group, or
  3. has implications for the use of the device.

Usability testing should be executed systematically and controlled for sources of bias and unreliability. It can verify that the symbol usability meets the usability objective.

NOTE: Context is an important factor in learning and understanding the meaning of symbols. The symbol needs to be assessed in context because the context could have significant effects on the understanding and use of symbols. A given symbol might be meaningless when seen by itself but becomes obvious when seen in the context where it is intended to be used. Likewise, a given symbol on one device may have a different meaning on another product, which could cause confusion.

四種評估方法：功能與時序

評估流程的進程邏輯

1
概念篩選（8.1）：適切性排序測試
目的：從5-10個草圖概念中篩選出2-3個最佳候選
問題：「哪個圖最能代表這個意思？」
2
理解性量化（8.2）：理解性測試（開放式）
目的：量化目標族群對候選符號的理解程度
問題：「您認為這個符號代表什麼意思？」
3
記憶保留（8.3）：記憶測試（中高風險符號）
目的：評估符號是否容易記住，學習後能否保留
流程：教導意思 → 間隔一段時間 → 測試回憶
4
情境可用性（8.4）：可用性測試（高安全相關性）
目的：在實際使用情境下觀察符號理解和行動反應
關鍵：必須在「context」（使用情境）中測試

理解性測試的關鍵設計要點

Clause 8.2的comprehension testing是驗證流程的核心。設計測試時需注意：

  • 開放式提問：不給選項，讓受試者自由描述理解（避免猜測）
  • 評分標準：預先定義哪些答案算「正確理解」
  • 負面測試：有無危險的誤解？（如把「勿冷凍」誤解為「需冷凍」）
  • 樣本代表性：必須涵蓋目標族群的各種特徵（年齡、教育、文化背景）

「情境效應」的重要性（8.4 NOTE）

Clause 8.4的NOTE揭示了一個重要設計原則：

  • 同一個符號，單獨看可能不知道意思
  • 在產品包裝上，配合其他資訊，意思可能一目了然
  • 反過來，在不同產品上，同一符號可能有不同解讀

實務含義：測試必須在接近實際使用的環境中進行，不能只給白紙上一個孤立符號。

IEC 62366（可用性工程）與Clause 8的關係

IEC 62366要求對醫療器材的使用者介面進行可用性工程研究。標籤符號是「使用者介面」的一部分，因此：

  • IEC 62366的形成性評估（formative evaluation）對應ISO 15223-2的Clause 8.1-8.2
  • IEC 62366的總結性評估（summative evaluation）對應ISO 15223-2的Clause 8.4
  • 兩個標準的測試可以整合執行，共享受試者和測試情境
  • 在技術文件（Technical Documentation）中，兩個標準的結果都應被記錄

Clause 9 — Acceptance Criteria

The critical section: what passing looks like for no/low vs. moderate/high safety relevance symbols

9 Acceptance Criteria

9.1 General

Much of the information required on or with a medical device is information for safety within an integrated approach to risk management. The criticality of that information depends on the nature of its application. For example, the "sterile" symbol usually conveys a significant safety message because there is a risk of contamination. On the other hand, the date of manufacture might convey significant information for safety only under particular circumstances.

As with any risk control measure, the effectiveness of a candidate symbol for conveying information for safety shall be validated in accordance with 9.2 or 9.3, as relevant, before it can be accepted in ISO 15223-1.

In the early stages of testing, comparative evaluation is sufficient because the purpose of the testing is to select the best candidates from an initial set of design concepts. Absolute criteria are advisable later in the process to avoid the adoption of a poor symbol merely by virtue of its being tested against even worse alternatives.

9.2 Symbols with No to Low Safety Relevance

When a symbol is intended to convey information with no to low safety relevance, the preferred candidate may be compared against established symbols to validate its association with the intended meaning. The association with the intended meaning shall be at least as strong as with symbols of comparable safety relevance that have already been accepted in ISO 15223-1.

A simple form of comparison is the "associative strength test". In this method, the candidate symbol(s) is/are presented to a sample of the target group along with symbols drawn from ISO 15223-1. Those participating in the test are asked to choose an intended meaning for each symbol, from a list of possibilities. As part of the report, the proposer shall indicate which symbols from ISO 15223-1 were chosen for the associative strength test.

NOTE 1: The list of possible meanings should be longer than the list of symbols to avoid arriving at an answer by process of elimination.

Candidate symbol(s) with no to low safety relevance which receive(s) an associative strength score that is at least equal to the score received by the symbols already in ISO 15223-1 can be considered for inclusion in ISO 15223-1.

9.3 Symbols with Moderate to High Safety Relevance

Candidate symbols with moderate to high safety relevance shall undergo comprehension testing (8.2), memory testing (8.3) and usability testing (8.4).

The acceptance criteria for these tests shall be determined by the proposer and shall be based on the results of the risk analysis in Clause 6. This criterion shall be established prior to the start of the testing and shall be documented in the proposal for the adoption of a symbol in ISO 15223-1 (see item i) of Annex A).

「比較法」的邏輯陷阱

Clause 9.1提出了一個重要警告：早期階段用「比較」就夠了，但後期必須用「絕對標準」。原因：

  • 如果所有候選符號都很差，「最好的那個」仍然可能是差符號
  • 絕對標準（absolute criteria）防止「矮子裡面選將軍」
  • 這就是為什麼ISO 9186-1設定了67%這個絕對門檻

接受標準「預先設定」的重要性

Clause 9.3強調接受標準必須「prior to the start of testing」（測試前）設定。這是科學嚴謹性的基本要求：

  • 防止「看到結果後再設標準」的確認偏誤
  • 類似臨床試驗的「Pre-specified primary endpoint」
  • 在ISO/TC 210的評審中，事後修改標準會損害提案公信力
  • 對CDMO而言：這個標準也應記錄在設計確效計畫（DVP）中

「意義清單必須比符號多」（9.2 NOTE 1）

如果你有5個符號，給受試者5個意思讓他們配對，他們可能靠「刪去法」猜到答案，而不是真正理解符號。

正確做法：給10個意思，讓受試者從中選一個最符合每個符號的——這才能測試真實理解。

這個原則在設計任何理解性測試問卷時都適用。

CDMO自訂符號的驗證記錄要求

即使不提交ISO，CDMO執行ISO 15223-2驗證時，應保留以下記錄：

  • 需求識別文件（為何需要此符號）
  • 現有符號庫查詢記錄（至少6個來源）
  • 風險分類文件（連結ISO 14971風險管理檔案）
  • 目標族群定義
  • 預先設定的接受標準
  • 測試計畫和受試者招募記錄
  • 原始測試數據和評分
  • 測試結果摘要和結論

Annex B (Normative) — ISO/TC 145/SC 3 Registration Process

The basic pattern from IEC 80416-1 shall be used as the basis for the creation of a symbol original. It is used as a tool for the design of a symbol original to ensure a balanced visual impression between graphical symbols.

The registration process steps include:

  1. TC or SC completes the symbol proposal form (including EPS file) and emails it to ISO/TC 145/SC 3. The secretary reviews for completeness. Completed forms are posted for member review for: content compliance, ISO/IEC 80416 compliance, TC liaison, and symbol duplication.
  2. ISO/TC 145/SC 3 compiles comments and votes. Approved originals are assigned preliminary registration numbers.
  3. TC or SC is notified of preliminary registration numbers and may submit revised proposals.
  4. TC or SC Secretariat ballots the DIS of approved symbols through the normal ISO process.
  5. Registration numbers are assigned to approved symbols by ISO/TC 145/SC 3 Secretariat.
  6. The proposing TC or SC Secretariat ballots the FDIS with symbols containing final registration numbers.

The IEC/SC 3C proposal form (downloadable from http://sc3c.iec.ch/) uses the basic pattern from IEC 80416-1:2008. Required fields include: English and French title, product type, description, notes, main field of application (general vs. restricted), user types (general public vs. specialist), purpose and justification, relevant documents, and symbol drawing.

Annex A：提交清單的實務解讀

九個提交項目的分層結構

Annex A的九個項目可以分三組理解：

  • 項目a-g（初始評審必備）：符號的身份、需求、設計、目標族群——建立符號的「基本面」
  • 項目h（登記狀態，有則提交）：符號是否已在ISO或IEC資料庫登記
  • 項目i（第二輪評審必備）：測試結果——這是最終接受的關鍵依據

ISO vs. IEC登記路徑的選擇

Annex B（ISO/TC 145/SC 3）和Annex C（IEC/SC 3C）代表兩條不同的符號登記路徑：

  • ISO/TC 145/SC 3：適合一般用途圖形符號（ISO 7000系列）
  • IEC/SC 3C：適合電氣設備相關符號（IEC 60417系列）

哪條路更適合醫療器材符號？通常ISO路徑，因為ISO 15223-1屬於ISO體系。但如果符號涉及電氣安全（如設備的電源開關），IEC路徑更合適。ISO/TC 210/WG 3在初始評審時會給建議。

製造商自用：Annex A作為內部文件模板

即使不提交ISO，Annex A的九個項目是極佳的內部文件框架。CDMO可以基於此建立「符號驗證技術文件（Symbol Validation Technical File）」，在法規審查時提供：

  • 需求背景（對應Annex A items a, b, c）
  • 現有符號查詢記錄（對應 item d）
  • 符號設計文件（對應 items e, f, g）
  • 驗證測試計畫和結果（對應 item i）

這個技術文件可直接納入MDR/510(k)技術文件的「標籤」章節。

案例研究：CDMO為生物製劑預填充注射器新增「勿冷凍」符號

背景：客戶的單克隆抗體預填充注射器必須2-8°C冷藏，但絕對不能冷凍（凍結會導致蛋白質聚集，失效）。ISO 15223-1現有「冷藏（2°C-8°C）」符號，但無「勿冷凍」符號。

走完ISO 15223-2流程：

  1. 需求識別（Clause 4.1）：目標族群包括護理人員和患者自行注射；分發鏈含冷鏈，但部分地區急診室有冷凍庫，誤放可能性真實存在
  2. 查詢現有庫（Clause 7.1）：ISO 15223-1、ISO 7000、IEC 60417均無「勿冷凍」符號；EN 980有，但僅適用歐盟
  3. 風險分類（Clause 6）：凍結後蛋白質聚集 → 給藥後可能引發免疫反應 → 中高安全相關性
  4. 概念設計（Clause 7.2）：設計三個候選符號（雪花+禁止符號的不同組合）；確立title：「Do not freeze」；description：「Symbol indicating that the medical device shall not be exposed to freezing temperatures」
  5. 早期篩選（Clause 8.1）：30名護理人員進行適切性排序，選出最佳2個候選
  6. 理解性測試（Clause 8.2）：n=50（25名護理人員+25名患者），開放式問卷；預設接受標準：≥80%正確理解（因為中高安全相關性）
  7. 記憶測試（Clause 8.3）：培訓後2週再測，保留率>75%為通過
  8. 可用性測試（Clause 8.4）：在模擬護理站情境中，觀察受試者看到符號後的實際操作行為
  9. 結果：候選符號B通過，理解率83%，記憶保留率78%；記錄全部結果，納入器材技術文件

練習問題（5道）

  1. ISO 15223-2的Scope規定，若遵循本標準的驗證流程，殘餘風險被「推定可接受」——但有一個例外條件，請說明這個例外條件是什麼，並舉例說明何種情況下推定不成立。
  2. 比較Appropriateness Ranking Test和Comprehension Test的核心差異：各自回答什麼問題？各自在流程的哪個階段使用？各自的結果如何決定下一步行動？
  3. 一個新設計的「避免電磁干擾」符號被分類為「中高安全相關性」（因為誤解可能影響植入式心律調節器患者）。依據ISO 15223-2，這個符號需要通過哪些具體測試？接受標準應如何設定，由誰設定，在什麼時間點設定？
  4. 為什麼ISO 15223-2要求提供的「意義清單」必須比被測試的符號數量多（Clause 9.2 NOTE 1）？如果不遵守這個原則，測試結果會有什麼偏差？
  5. 一家CDMO正在為客戶產品開發一個新的「單次使用，生物危害廢棄」組合符號。請說明：（a）為什麼應優先使用ISO 15223-1的現有符號組合；（b）若必須開發新符號，需要查閱哪些符號庫；（c）這個符號應屬於哪個風險等級，理由是什麼。
