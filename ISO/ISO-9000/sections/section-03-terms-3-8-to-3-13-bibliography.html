3.8

Terms Related to Data, Information and Document

資料、資訊與文件相關術語 — The backbone of pharmaceutical documentation and data integrity

ISO 9000 Definitions — Clause 3.8

3.8.1 data 資料

Facts about an object (3.6.1).

Raw, unprocessed facts. Not yet meaningful on their own.

3.8.2 information 資訊

Meaningful data (3.8.1).

Data that has been processed, organized, or given context to make it useful for decision-making.

3.8.3 objective evidence 客觀證據

Data (3.8.1) supporting the existence or verity of something.

Note 1: Can be obtained through observation, measurement, test, or other means.
Note 2: For audit purposes, generally consists of records, statements of fact, or other information which are relevant to the audit criteria and verifiable.

3.8.4 information system 資訊系統

Network of communication channels used within an organization (3.2.1).

Specific to a quality management system context.

3.8.5 document 文件

Information (3.8.2) and the medium on which it is contained.

Examples: record, specification, quality manual, quality plan, procedure document, drawing, report, standard.
Note 1: Medium can be paper, magnetic, electronic or optical disc, photograph, or master sample.
Note 2: A set of documents, for example specifications and records, is frequently called "documentation".

3.8.6 documented information 文件化資訊

Information required to be controlled and maintained by an organization (3.2.1) and the medium on which it is contained.

Note 1: Can be in any format and media and from any source.
Note 2: Can refer to: (a) the management system and its related processes; (b) information created for the organization to operate (documentation); (c) evidence of results achieved (records).
Note 3: This is one of the Annex SL common core definitions.

3.8.7 specification 規格/規範

Document (3.8.5) stating requirements (3.6.4).

Examples: quality manual, quality plan, technical drawing, procedure document, work instruction.
Note 1: Can relate to activities (procedure, process, test spec) or products/services (product spec, drawing).

3.8.8 quality manual 品質手冊

Specification (3.8.7) for the quality management system (3.5.4) of an organization (3.2.1).

Note 1: Quality manuals can vary in detail and format to suit the size and complexity of an individual organization.

3.8.9 quality plan 品質計畫

Specification (3.8.7) of the procedures (3.4.5) and associated resources to be applied when and by whom to a specific object (3.6.1).

Note 1: These procedures generally include those referring to QMS processes and to product/service realization processes.
Note 2: A quality plan often references parts of the quality manual or procedure documents.
Note 3: A quality plan is generally one of the results of quality planning.

3.8.10 record 記錄

Document (3.8.5) stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed.

Note 1: Records can be used to formalize traceability and to provide evidence of preventive action, corrective action, and verification.
Note 2: Generally records need not be under revision control.

3.8.11 project management plan 專案管理計畫

Document (3.8.5) specifying what is necessary to meet the objectives (3.7.1) of a project (3.4.2).

Note 1: Should include or refer to the project's quality plan. [SOURCE: ISO 10006:2003]

3.8.12 verification 驗證

Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence (3.8.3), that specified requirements (3.6.4) have been fulfilled.

Note 1: The objective evidence needed for a verification can be the result of an inspection or other forms of determination.
Note 2: Activities for verification are sometimes called "qualification".
Note 3: The word "verified" designates the corresponding status.

3.8.13 validation 確認

Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence (3.8.3), that the requirements (3.6.4) for a specific intended use or application have been fulfilled.

Note 1: The objective evidence needed for a validation is the result of a test or other form of determination.
Note 2: The word "validated" designates the corresponding status.
Note 3: Use conditions for validation can be real or simulated.

3.8.14 configuration status accounting 組態狀態紀錄

Formalized recording and reporting of product configuration information (3.6.8), the status of proposed changes and the status of the implementation of approved changes.

[SOURCE: ISO 10007:2003]

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

"Documented Information" — The 2015 Unification 文件化資訊：2015年的整合

Before ISO 9001:2015, QMS standards distinguished between two types of controlled items:

  • Documents — procedures, SOPs, work instructions, forms (controlled for content)
  • Records — completed forms, batch records, logs (controlled for retention and retrieval)

ISO 9001:2015 merged both under the single term "documented information" — defined here in 3.8.6. The standard now uses two phrases:

  • "Maintain documented information" = what you used to call documents/procedures
  • "Retain documented information" = what you used to call records

Impact on pharma QMS: Your old SOP that says "the operator shall prepare a record" still works — but strictly speaking, ISO 9001:2015 language would say "the operator shall retain documented information as evidence." Most pharma companies kept their existing terminology since FDA regulations (21 CFR Part 211) still say "records."

Record → ALCOA+ Connection 記錄與ALCOA+的連結

ISO 9000's definition of "record" (3.8.10) — "document stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed" — maps directly to FDA's ALCOA+ data integrity principle:

  • Attributable — who created the record
  • Legible — readable throughout its retention period
  • Contemporaneous — recorded at the time of the activity
  • Original — first capture of data
  • Accurate — truthful representation of events
  • + Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available

ISO 9000 gives you the vocabulary foundation; FDA's ALCOA+ adds the quality attributes that make that evidence trustworthy.

Verification vs. Validation — The Pharma View 驗證確認的製藥視角

ISO 9000 draws a clear distinction:

  • Verification (3.8.12) — Did we build the product right? Are specified requirements met? → IQ/OQ in pharma validation
  • Validation (3.8.13) — Did we build the right product? Does it work for its intended use? → PQ, process validation, cleaning validation

A clean room HVAC system might be verified to meet design specifications (air changes per hour, temperature range), but it's only validated when demonstrated to consistently maintain ISO 5 conditions during actual aseptic filling operations.

Objective Evidence in Pharma Audits 稽核中的客觀證據

ISO 9000 defines objective evidence (3.8.3) as data "supporting the existence or verity of something" obtained through observation, measurement, test, or other means. In a pharma CDMO audit context:

  • Batch records = objective evidence of process execution
  • Environmental monitoring logs = objective evidence of controlled conditions
  • Calibration certificates = objective evidence of measurement equipment fitness
  • Training records = objective evidence of personnel competence

When an FDA investigator requests "evidence that your process consistently produces sterile product," they are asking for objective evidence in the ISO 9000 sense.

3.9

Terms Related to Customer

顧客相關術語 — Who is the customer in pharma CDMO? Closer than you think.

ISO 9000 Definitions — Clause 3.9

3.9.1 feedback 回饋

Opinions, comments and expressions of interest in a product (3.7.6), a service (3.7.7) or a complaints-handling process (3.4.1).

Context: <customer satisfaction>. [SOURCE: ISO 10002:2014, modified]

3.9.2 customer satisfaction 顧客滿意度

Customer's perception of the degree to which the customer's expectations have been fulfilled.

Note 1: Customer's expectation may not be known to the organization, or even to the customer, until the product or service is delivered.
Note 2: Complaints are a common indicator of low customer satisfaction but their absence does not necessarily imply high customer satisfaction.
Note 3: Even when customer requirements have been agreed and fulfilled, this does not necessarily ensure high customer satisfaction. [SOURCE: ISO 10004:2012, modified]

3.9.3 complaint 投訴/抱怨

Expression of dissatisfaction made to an organization (3.2.1), related to its product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7), or the complaints-handling process (3.4.1) itself, where a response or resolution is explicitly or implicitly expected.

[SOURCE: ISO 10002:2014, modified]

3.9.4 customer service 顧客服務

Interaction of the organization (3.2.1) with the customer (3.2.4) throughout the life cycle of a product (3.7.6) or a service (3.7.7).

[SOURCE: ISO 10002:2014, modified]

3.9.5 customer satisfaction code of conduct 顧客滿意行為準則

Promises made to customers (3.2.4) by an organization (3.2.1) concerning its behaviour, aimed at enhanced customer satisfaction (3.9.2) and related provisions.

Note 1: Related provisions can include objectives, conditions, limitations, contact information, complaints handling procedures. [SOURCE: ISO 10001:2007, modified]

3.9.6 dispute 爭議

Disagreement, arising from a complaint (3.9.3), submitted to a DRP-provider (dispute resolution process provider).

Note 1: Some organizations allow their customers to express dissatisfaction to a DRP-provider in the first instance. [SOURCE: ISO 10003:2007, modified]

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Who Is the Customer in Pharma CDMO? 誰是製藥CDMO的顧客？

ISO 9000 defines "customer" (3.2.4) as a person or organization that could or does receive a product or service. In a pharmaceutical CDMO context, there are multiple layers:

Patient 病患 — Ultimate End Customer
▲ receives treatment
Pharma Company 藥廠 — Direct Customer (NDA/MAA holder)
▲ contracts manufacturing
CDMO — Manufacturer/Provider

The direct customer (pharma company) defines the specifications, holds the regulatory dossier, and evaluates the CDMO's quality system. But the ultimate customer (patient) defines what "quality" actually means — efficacy, safety, and purity.

This is why pharma QMS must satisfy both: contractual requirements from the pharma client AND regulatory/compendial requirements protecting the patient.

Customer Complaints in Pharma QMS 藥品投訴管理

ISO 9000's "complaint" (3.9.3) maps to pharmaceutical complaint handling requirements:

  • 21 CFR 211.198 — written procedures for handling complaints, investigations required for potential product defects
  • EU GMP Chapter 8 — complaints and product recalls, QP notification
  • ICH Q10 — complaint handling as a knowledge management tool

Key pharma additions beyond ISO: complaints must be evaluated for whether they represent a product defect requiring recall or field alert report (FDA FAR). In ISO terms: a complaint triggers a determination (3.11.1) of nonconformity (3.6.9) and may require corrective action (3.12.2).

Customer Satisfaction ≠ Regulatory Compliance 顧客滿意 ≠ 法規合規

ISO 9000 Note 3 to 3.9.2 warns that "even when customer requirements have been agreed and fulfilled, this does not necessarily ensure high customer satisfaction." The reverse is also true in pharma:

A CDMO can satisfy all customer contractual requirements (batch yield, timeline, cost) while failing to meet regulatory requirements (sterility assurance, process validation). Both dimensions must be managed. This is why the Quality Agreement between CDMO and pharma client exists — to align what the customer wants with what the regulator requires.

3.10

Terms Related to Characteristic

特性相關術語 — Traceability as a quality characteristic: three types every pharma professional must know

ISO 9000 Definitions — Clause 3.10

3.10.1 characteristic 特性

Distinguishing feature.

Note 1: Can be inherent or assigned.
Note 2: Can be qualitative or quantitative.
Note 3: Various classes: physical (mechanical, electrical, chemical, biological); sensory (smell, touch, taste, sight, hearing); behavioural (courtesy, honesty, veracity); temporal (punctuality, reliability, availability, continuity); ergonomic; functional.

3.10.2 quality characteristic 品質特性

Inherent characteristic (3.10.1) of an object (3.6.1) related to a requirement (3.6.4).

Note 1: Inherent means existing in something, especially as a permanent characteristic.
Note 2: A characteristic assigned to an object (e.g. the price of an object) is not a quality characteristic of that object.

3.10.3 human factor 人為因素

Characteristic (3.10.1) of a person having an impact on an object (3.6.1) under consideration.

Note 1: Characteristics can be physical, cognitive or social.
Note 2: Human factors can have a significant impact on a management system (3.5.3).

3.10.4 competence 能力/職能

Ability to apply knowledge and skills to achieve intended results.

Note 1: Demonstrated competence is sometimes referred to as qualification.
Note 2: This is one of the Annex SL common core definitions.

3.10.5 metrological characteristic 計量特性

Characteristic (3.10.1) which can influence the results of measurement (3.11.4).

Note 1: Measuring equipment usually has several metrological characteristics.
Note 2: Metrological characteristics can be the subject of calibration.

3.10.6 configuration 組態

Interrelated functional and physical characteristics (3.10.1) of a product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) defined in product configuration information (3.6.8).

[SOURCE: ISO 10007:2003, modified — The term "service" has been included]

3.10.7 configuration baseline 組態基線

Approved product configuration information (3.6.8) that establishes the characteristics (3.10.1) of a product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) at a point in time that serves as reference for activities throughout the life cycle.

[SOURCE: ISO 10007:2003, modified]

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Traceability in Pharmaceutical Manufacturing 製藥製造中的追溯性

While ISO 9000 does not define "traceability" in Clause 3.10 directly (it appears in Clause 3.6.13 as the ability to trace history, application, or location of an object), the characteristics-based framework here underpins three types of traceability critical to pharma:

  • Material Traceability 物料追溯
    Raw material lot → intermediate → finished product. Required by FDA 21 CFR 211.184 (component labeling), EU GMP Part II, ICH Q7.
  • Equipment Traceability 設備追溯
    Each piece of equipment used in production linked to the batch record — equipment ID, last calibration, last cleaning, operator. ISO 9000 "metrological characteristic" (3.10.5) is the formal name for what you calibrate.
  • Process Traceability 製程追溯
    Process parameters (temperature, pressure, time), operator identity, and timestamps linked to each unit operation within a batch. This is what makes batch record review possible.

Inherent vs. Assigned Characteristics 固有特性與賦予特性

ISO 9000 Note 1 to 3.10.1 distinguishes inherent from assigned characteristics. In pharma:

  • Inherent quality characteristics of a drug product: potency, purity, sterility, particle size, dissolution rate — these exist in the product itself
  • Assigned characteristics: expiry date, batch number, price, storage temperature label — these are assigned by the manufacturer or regulator

Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs, per ICH Q8) are inherent quality characteristics whose variability has a significant impact on product quality. This maps exactly to ISO 9000's "quality characteristic" (3.10.2) — inherent characteristics related to requirements.

Human Factor in Aseptic Processing 無菌製程中的人為因素

ISO 9000 3.10.3 defines "human factor" as a characteristic of a person having an impact on an object under consideration. Note 2 states these can significantly impact a management system.

In aseptic fill-finish, human factors are the #1 source of contamination risk. Gowning technique, behaviour in the cleanroom, proximity to the fill zone — all are human factor characteristics that directly impact sterility assurance. This is why:

  • PIC/S Annex 1 requires personnel to minimize cleanroom interventions
  • Aseptic Process Simulations (media fills) specifically test human factor impact
  • Personnel qualification (ISO 9000: "competence" 3.10.4) is a regulatory requirement, not just an HR process
3.11

Terms Related to Determination

判定相關術語 — Monitoring vs. measurement vs. inspection vs. test: four subtly different activities in pharma QC

ISO 9000 Definitions — Clause 3.11

3.11.1 determination 判定

Activity to find out one or more characteristics (3.10.1) and their characteristic values.

3.11.2 review 審查

Determination (3.11.1) of the suitability, adequacy or effectiveness (3.7.11) of an object (3.6.1) to achieve established objectives (3.7.1).

Examples: management review, design and development review, review of customer requirements, review of corrective action, peer review.
Note 1: Review can also include the determination of efficiency (3.7.10).

3.11.3 monitoring 監測

Determining (3.11.1) the status of a system (3.5.1), a process (3.4.1), a product (3.7.6), a service (3.7.7), or an activity.

Note 1: For the determination of status there can be a need to check, supervise or critically observe.
Note 2: Monitoring is generally a determination of the status of an object, carried out at different stages or at different times.
Note 3: This is one of the Annex SL common core definitions.

3.11.4 measurement 量測

Process (3.4.1) to determine a value.

Note 1: According to ISO 3534-2, the value determined is generally the value of a quantity.
Note 2: This is one of the Annex SL common core definitions.

3.11.5 measurement process 量測過程

Set of operations to determine the value of a quantity.

3.11.6 measuring equipment 量測設備

Measuring instrument, software, measurement standard, reference material or auxiliary apparatus or combination thereof necessary to realize a measurement process (3.11.5).

3.11.7 inspection 查驗/檢驗

Determination (3.11.1) of conformity (3.6.11) to specified requirements (3.6.4).

Note 1: If the result of an inspection shows conformity, it can be used for purposes of verification (3.8.12).
Note 2: The result of an inspection can show conformity or nonconformity or a degree of conformity.

3.11.8 test 測試

Determination (3.11.1) according to requirements (3.6.4) for a specific intended use or application.

Note 1: If the result of a test shows conformity, it can be used for purposes of validation (3.8.13).

3.11.9 progress evaluation 進度評估

Assessment of progress made on achievement of the project objectives (3.7.1).

Context: <project management>. [SOURCE: ISO 10006:2003, modified]

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Monitoring vs. Measurement: The Critical Difference 監測與量測的關鍵差異

ISO 9000 3.11.3 notes that monitoring is "carried out at different stages or at different times" — implying ongoing, repeated observation. Measurement (3.11.4) is a single process to determine a specific value.

Pharma application:

  • Cleanroom temperature monitoring — continuous logging via calibrated sensors = monitoring. The system automatically checks status at each time point.
  • Airborne particle count measurement — discrete sampling event at defined locations per ISO 14644-1 = measurement. You get a specific number (e.g., 3,520 particles/m³ ≥0.5 µm).

Inspection adds judgment: a microbiologist reading a settle plate is not just measuring colony count — they are inspecting (making a conformity determination: does this result exceed the alert or action limit?).

Inspection Outcome: Conformity or Nonconformity? 查驗結果：符合或不符合？

ISO 9000 Note 2 to 3.11.7 states: "The result of an inspection can show conformity or nonconformity or a degree of conformity." This third option — degree of conformity — is important in pharma visual inspection:

  • Critical defect = nonconformity → reject
  • Major defect = reduced conformity → may trigger concession (3.12.5) or disposition decision
  • Minor defect / cosmetic = lower degree of conformity → may be acceptable per specification

AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) sampling explicitly acknowledges this: you accept a lot not because all units are conforming, but because the degree of conformity across the lot meets the acceptance criteria.

Review in Quality Management Context 品質管理中的審查

ISO 9000's "review" (3.11.2) is a determination of suitability, adequacy, or effectiveness. In pharma, this applies to multiple formal review activities:

  • Management Review — ISO 9001 Clause 9.3, covers QMS performance, audit results, customer feedback
  • Annual Product Review (APR) — FDA 21 CFR 211.180(e), EU GMP Chapter 1 — periodic review of product data to identify trends
  • Batch Record Review — QP/QA review before batch release — suitability check of all documentation
  • Change Control Review — adequacy determination before implementing changes

Four Determination Activities — Pharma QC Mapping 四種判定活動的製藥品管對應

ISO 9000 defines four determination activities that pharma QC teams use daily, but rarely distinguish formally:

ISO Term What it Is Pharma Example
Monitoring 監測 Continual status check at different stages/times Environmental monitoring (EM), temperature logging, online pH measurement during fermentation
Measurement 量測 Process to determine a specific quantitative value Particle count (ISO 14644), fill volume check, container closure integrity test pressure
Inspection 查驗 Conformity determination by observation + judgment Visual inspection of vials (cosmetic defects, stopper seating), incoming material inspection, AQL sampling
Test 測試 Determination for a specific intended use Sterility test, potency assay, dissolution test — results used for batch release (validation-level evidence)
3.12

Terms Related to Action

措施相關術語 — CAPA in pharma maps directly to these ISO 9000 definitions

ISO 9000 Definitions — Clause 3.12

3.12.1 preventive action 預防措施

Action to eliminate the cause of a potential nonconformity (3.6.9) or other potential undesirable situation.

Note 1: There can be more than one cause for a potential nonconformity.
Note 2: Preventive action is taken to prevent occurrence whereas corrective action (3.12.2) is taken to prevent recurrence.

3.12.2 corrective action 矯正措施

Action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity (3.6.9) and to prevent recurrence.

Note 1: There can be more than one cause for a nonconformity.
Note 2: Corrective action is taken to prevent recurrence whereas preventive action (3.12.1) is taken to prevent occurrence.
Note 3: This is one of the Annex SL common core definitions.

3.12.3 correction 修正

Action to eliminate a detected nonconformity (3.6.9).

Note 1: A correction can be made in advance of, in conjunction with, or after a corrective action (3.12.2).
Note 2: A correction can be, for example, rework (3.12.8) or regrade (3.12.4).

3.12.4 regrade 重新分級

Alteration of the grade (3.6.3) of a nonconforming (3.6.9) product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) in order to make it conform to requirements (3.6.4) differing from the initial requirements.

3.12.5 concession 特許/讓步

Permission to use or release (3.12.7) a product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) that does not conform to specified requirements (3.6.4).

Note 1: A concession is generally limited to the delivery of products and services that have nonconforming (3.6.9) characteristics within specified limits, given for a limited quantity or period of time, and for a specific use.

3.12.6 deviation permit 偏差許可

Permission to depart from the originally specified requirements (3.6.4) of a product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) prior to its realization.

Note 1: A deviation permit is generally given for a limited quantity or period of time, and for a specific use.

3.12.7 release 放行

Permission to proceed to the next stage of a process (3.4.1) or the next process.

Note 1: In English, in the context of software and documents, the word "release" is frequently used to refer to a version of the software or the document itself.

3.12.8 rework 重工

Action on a nonconforming (3.6.9) product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) to make it conform to the requirements (3.6.4).

Note 1: Rework can affect or change parts of the nonconforming product or service.

3.12.9 repair 修復

Action on a nonconforming (3.6.9) product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) to make it acceptable for the intended use.

Note 1: A successful repair does not necessarily make the product or service conform to the requirements. It can be that in conjunction with a repair a concession (3.12.5) is required.
Note 2: Repair includes remedial action taken on a previously conforming product or service to restore it for use, for example as part of maintenance.
Note 3: Repair can affect or change parts of the nonconforming product or service.

3.12.10 scrap 報廢

Action on a nonconforming (3.6.9) product (3.7.6) or service (3.7.7) to preclude its originally intended use.

Examples: Recycling, destruction.
Note 1: In a nonconforming service situation, use is precluded by discontinuing the service.

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Why Preventive Action Disappeared from ISO 9001:2015 預防措施為何從ISO 9001:2015消失？

ISO 9000:2015 still defines "preventive action" (3.12.1) — but ISO 9001:2015 no longer has a dedicated clause for it (unlike ISO 9001:2008 Clause 8.5.3).

The reason: ISO 9001:2015 introduced risk-based thinking (Clause 6.1 — Actions to address risks and opportunities). The philosophy is that all of QMS planning is essentially preventive action — if you identify and address risks before they materialize as nonconformities, you never need a reactive preventive action process.

For pharma teams using ICH Q9 (Quality Risk Management), this is familiar territory. Risk assessments, FMEAs, and process hazard analyses are all proactive measures to prevent potential nonconformities — exactly what ISO 9000 means by "preventive action."

Practical note: Pharma QMS that have a "Preventive Action" SOP for compliance with older standards can retain it — the concept is still valid, just restructured within risk management framework.

Correction vs. Corrective Action — A Critical Distinction 修正與矯正措施的關鍵區別

ISO 9000 makes a distinction that pharma deviations and CAPAs often blur:

  • Correction (3.12.3) — Fix the specific instance. Discard the OOS batch. Re-test a sample. Reclean equipment. The nonconformity is addressed, but the cause is not yet resolved.
  • Corrective Action (3.12.2) — Eliminate the root cause so it won't recur. Retrain the operator. Change the SOP. Upgrade the instrument.

A batch record is evidence of both: the OOS result triggers a correction (product quarantine), then a corrective action investigation determines root cause and implements systemic changes. FDA 483 observations most commonly cite failures to distinguish these — organizations correct problems without addressing root causes.

Nonconforming Product Dispositions 不符合品的處置選項

ISO 9000 defines a hierarchy of responses to nonconforming products that pharma dispositions mirror:

  • Rework (3.12.8) — Reprocess to make it conform to original spec. In pharma: refilter, reprocess, re-blend within validated parameters. Requires a disposition decision by QA.
  • Repair (3.12.9) — Make acceptable for intended use but not necessarily conforming. In pharma: limited application (more common in medical devices). A "repaired" drug product still needs conformity to specifications.
  • Concession (3.12.5) — Use as-is despite nonconformity. In pharma: requires regulatory notification if it affects safety/efficacy. Very rare for finished drug product; more common for raw materials within pre-agreed limits.
  • Regrade (3.12.4) — Change grade/class. In pharma: downgrade API from pharmaceutical to research grade.
  • Scrap (3.12.10) — Destroy. In pharma: batch destruction, typically by incineration for controlled substances.

CAPA System Maps Exactly to ISO 9000 Definitions CAPA系統完全對應ISO 9000定義

Pharmaceutical CAPA (Corrective Action and Preventive Action) maps directly to ISO 9000 action terms:

CAPA ComponentISO 9000 TermDescription
Immediate Action (containment) Correction (3.12.3) Stop the problem now — quarantine the batch, recall, halt production
Corrective Action (CA) Corrective Action (3.12.2) Eliminate the root cause so this nonconformity does not recur
Preventive Action (PA) Preventive Action (3.12.1) Eliminate causes of potential nonconformities before they occur

FDA 21 CFR 820.100 (medical devices) and ICH Q10 explicitly require CAPA systems. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 10.2 covers corrective action but does NOT have a separate clause for preventive action — by design, as explained below.

ISO 9000 Definitions — Clause 3.13

3.13.1 audit 稽核

Systematic, independent and documented process (3.4.1) for obtaining objective evidence (3.8.3) and evaluating it objectively to determine the extent to which the audit criteria (3.13.7) are fulfilled.

Note 1: Fundamental elements include the determination of the conformity of an object according to a procedure, carried out by personnel not being responsible for the object audited.
Note 2: Can be internal (first party) or external (second or third party), combined or joint.
Note 3: Internal audits are conducted by or on behalf of the organization itself for management review and other internal purposes.
Note 4: External audits include second-party audits (conducted by parties having an interest in the organization, e.g. customers) and third-party audits (independent auditing organizations, e.g. certification bodies or regulatory agencies).
Note 5: This is one of the Annex SL common core definitions.

3.13.2 combined audit 合併稽核

Audit (3.13.1) carried out together at a single auditee (3.13.12) on two or more management systems (3.5.3).

3.13.3 joint audit 聯合稽核

Audit (3.13.1) carried out at a single auditee (3.13.12) by two or more auditing organizations (3.2.1).

3.13.4 audit programme 稽核計畫（年度）

Set of one or more audits (3.13.1) planned for a specific time frame and directed towards a specific purpose.

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.5 audit scope 稽核範圍

Extent and boundaries of an audit (3.13.1).

Note 1: The audit scope generally includes a description of the physical locations, organizational units, activities and processes (3.4.1). [SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.6 audit plan 稽核計畫（個別）

Description of the activities and arrangements for an audit (3.13.1).

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011]

3.13.7 audit criteria 稽核標準

Set of policies (3.5.8), procedures (3.4.5) or requirements (3.6.4) used as a reference against which objective evidence (3.8.3) is compared.

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified — "audit evidence" replaced by "objective evidence"]

3.13.8 audit evidence 稽核證據

Records, statements of fact or other information, which are relevant to the audit criteria (3.13.7) and verifiable.

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.9 audit findings 稽核發現

Results of the evaluation of the collected audit evidence (3.13.8) against audit criteria (3.13.7).

Note 1: Audit findings indicate conformity (3.6.11) or nonconformity (3.6.9).
Note 2: Can lead to identification of opportunities for improvement or recording good practices.
Note 3: If audit criteria are selected from statutory or regulatory requirements, the audit finding can be called compliance or non-compliance. [SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.10 audit conclusion 稽核結論

Outcome of an audit (3.13.1), after consideration of the audit objectives and all audit findings (3.13.9).

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011]

3.13.11 audit client 稽核委託方

Organization (3.2.1) or person requesting an audit (3.13.1).

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.12 auditee 受稽核方

Organization (3.2.1) being audited.

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011]

3.13.13 guide 嚮導/陪同人員

Person appointed by the auditee (3.13.12) to assist the audit team (3.13.14).

Context: <audit>. [SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011]

3.13.14 audit team 稽核小組

One or more persons conducting an audit (3.13.1), supported if needed by technical experts (3.13.16).

Note 1: One auditor (3.13.15) of the audit team is appointed as the audit team leader.
Note 2: The audit team can include auditors-in-training. [SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.15 auditor 稽核員

Person who conducts an audit (3.13.1).

[SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011]

3.13.16 technical expert 技術專家

Person who provides specific knowledge or expertise to the audit team (3.13.14).

Note 1: Specific knowledge or expertise relates to the organization, the process or activity to be audited, or language or culture.
Note 2: A technical expert does not act as an auditor in the audit team. [SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

3.13.17 observer 觀察員

Person who accompanies the audit team (3.13.14) but does not act as an auditor (3.13.15).

Note 1: An observer can be a member of the auditee (3.13.12), a regulator or other interested party who witnesses the audit (3.13.1). [SOURCE: ISO 19011:2011, modified]

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Three Types of Audit in Pharma Context 製藥中的三種稽核類型

ISO 9000 Note 2 to 3.13.1 distinguishes internal (first party), and external (second or third party) audits:

  • First-party (internal) audits — Self-inspections per EU GMP Chapter 9; internal QMS audits per ISO 9001 Clause 9.2. Conducted by the organization on itself. Independence ensured by auditors not auditing their own work.
  • Second-party audits — Customer audits of suppliers. In pharma: pharma company auditing the CDMO; CDMO auditing its raw material suppliers. Basis for Vendor Qualification programs per ICH Q10.
  • Third-party audits — Regulatory inspections (FDA, EMA, MHRA, PMDA) and certification audits (ISO 9001 certification body). In pharma, third-party audits determine commercial viability — a Warning Letter or OAI classification can halt product supply.

Audit Programme vs. Audit Plan — Scope Hierarchy 稽核計畫層次

ISO 9000 uses two distinct terms that pharma quality teams sometimes conflate:

  • Audit programme (3.13.4) — The annual schedule: "In 2026, we will conduct 12 internal audits and 5 supplier audits." This is the strategic-level plan across the year.
  • Audit plan (3.13.6) — The specific agenda for a single audit event: "On 15 April, we will audit Sterile Manufacturing (8:00–12:00), QC Lab (13:00–15:00), and Documentation (15:00–17:00)." This is tactical.
  • Audit scope (3.13.5) — The boundaries of a specific audit: "This audit covers sterile manufacturing suite A, batch records from Q1 2026, and related SOPs."

When preparing for an FDA Pre-Approval Inspection (PAI), you need all three levels: the programme (when), the plan (the day's schedule), and the scope (which process, which product, which time period).

Audit Findings → CAPA Trigger 稽核發現觸發CAPA

ISO 9000 defines audit findings (3.13.9) as the result of evaluating audit evidence against audit criteria. Findings can indicate either conformity or nonconformity. This directly maps to pharma quality system linkage:

  • An internal audit finding of nonconformity → triggers a CAPA (corrective action 3.12.2)
  • An external customer audit finding → may require formal CAPA response within agreed timeframe (typically 30 days for major findings)
  • An FDA 483 observation (audit finding) → requires a written response within 15 business days; significant findings may escalate to Warning Letter
  • An audit finding of opportunity for improvement (ISO Note 2 to 3.13.9) → may trigger a preventive action or quality improvement initiative, not necessarily a CAPA

The audit conclusion (3.13.10) integrates all findings and audit objectives to render an overall judgment — the audit team leader decides whether the QMS is effective overall, regardless of individual findings.

Pharma Context 製藥產業關聯

Which ISO 9000 Bibliography Standards Matter Most in Pharma? 哪些參考標準對製藥最重要？

The bibliography of ISO 9000:2015 points to a broader ecosystem. For pharmaceutical professionals:

  • ISO 19011:2011 (audit guidelines) — foundational for internal audit programs. Supplier auditor qualification should align with ISO 19011 competency framework.
  • ISO 10012:2003 (measurement management) — directly relevant to metrology, calibration management, and measurement uncertainty — all critical for pharmaceutical analytical instruments.
  • ISO 31000 (risk management) — complements ICH Q9. The ISO 31000 risk management framework is more generic, while ICH Q9 is pharmaceutical-specific. Many CDMOs use both together.
  • ISO 10002:2014 (complaints handling) — the detailed operational standard behind ISO 9000's complaint definitions. Pharma complaint SOPs can reference this for best-practice structure.
  • ISO 10015 (training guidelines) — supports competence management for GMP personnel qualification.

ISO 9000 vs. ISO 9001 — Vocabulary vs. Requirements ISO 9000與ISO 9001：術語vs.要求

The relationship between these two standards is critical to understand:

  • ISO 9000:2015 — Defines the vocabulary (terms and definitions) and fundamentals (principles and rationale). It tells you what things mean.
  • ISO 9001:2015 — States the requirements for a quality management system. It tells you what you must do. Uses the definitions from ISO 9000.

When ISO 9001 says "the organization shall maintain documented information" — the meaning of "documented information" is defined in ISO 9000 Clause 3.8.6. You cannot fully interpret ISO 9001 requirements without ISO 9000 definitions.

For pharma companies seeking ISO 9001 certification alongside GMP compliance, ISO 9000 is the dictionary you use when reading the ISO 9001 rulebook.

ISO 19011 and Pharmaceutical Audit Programs ISO 19011與製藥稽核計畫

ISO 9000 Clause 3.13 audit terms are largely sourced from ISO 19011:2011 (Guidelines for auditing management systems). ISO 19011 provides detailed guidance on:

  • Managing an audit programme (annual schedule, objectives, resources)
  • Conducting an audit (opening meeting, document review, on-site activities, sampling)
  • Competence and evaluation of auditors

Pharmaceutical companies that want to formalize their supplier qualification audit programs often use ISO 19011 as the methodological framework, then apply pharmaceutical-specific audit criteria (21 CFR 210/211, EU GMP, ICH Q10, technical standards) as the audit criteria (3.13.7).
