Foreword 前言

ISO 9000:2015 — Fourth Edition | ISO/TC 176 SC 1

Original Text — Foreword

ISO 9000 is part of the ISO 9000 family of standards, which is prepared by Technical Committee ISO/TC 176, Subcommittee SC 1 (Concepts and terminology). This fourth edition cancels and replaces the third edition (ISO 9000:2005), which has been technically revised.

The ISO 9000 family consists of three core standards:

  • ISO 9000:2015 — Fundamentals and Vocabulary (this document): defines terms and provides the conceptual framework
  • ISO 9001:2015 — Requirements: specifies QMS requirements organisations must meet
  • ISO 9004:2018 — Quality of an organisation — Guidance to achieve sustained success

Key changes from the 2005 third edition include alignment with the seven quality management principles and structural alignment with Annex SL (the common high-level structure shared across ISO management system standards such as ISO 14001 and ISO 45001).

What is Annex SL?

Annex SL (now replaced by ISO/IEC Directives, Annex L) defines a common High-Level Structure for all ISO management system standards. It enables integrated management systems — a single audit covering ISO 9001 (quality), ISO 14001 (environment), ISO 45001 (OH&S) and ISO 50001 (energy) simultaneously.

解析 Commentary

ISO 9000家族：三本書的分工

可以這樣理解：

  • ISO 9000 = 哲學書。告訴你「為什麼」要這樣做品質管理，定義所有重要詞彙。
  • ISO 9001 = 法規書。告訴你「必須」做哪些事才能通過認證。
  • ISO 9004 = 進階書。告訴你如何超越合規，達到持續卓越。

很多人跳過ISO 9000直接讀ISO 9001，就像跳過物理學概念直接背公式——考試或許能過，但真正遇到問題時無法靈活應用。

第四版的重要升級

2015年修訂版最重要的兩個改變：

  • 7大原則正式確立：從2005年的8原則精簡整合為7原則，邏輯更清晰。
  • Annex SL對齊：與ISO 14001、ISO 45001等管理系統標準採用相同骨架，便於企業整合稽查。製藥CDMO可以一次稽查同時驗證QMS、EHS、能源管理系統。

Introduction 導言

Purpose and scope of ISO 9000:2015

Original Text — Introduction

This International Standard provides the fundamental concepts, principles and vocabulary for quality management systems (QMS) and provides the foundation for other QMS standards. It is intended to help the user to understand the fundamental concepts, principles and vocabulary of quality management, in order to be able to effectively and efficiently implement a QMS and realize value from other QMS standards.

This International Standard proposes a well-defined QMS, based on a framework that integrates established fundamental concepts, principles, processes and resources related to quality, in order to help organizations realize their objectives. It is applicable to all organizations, regardless of size, complexity or business model.

Its aim is to increase an organization's awareness of its duties and commitment in fulfilling the needs and expectations of its customers and interested parties, and in achieving satisfaction with its products and services.

"This International Standard contains seven quality management principles supporting the fundamental concepts... for each quality management principle, there is a statement, a rationale, key benefits, and possible actions."

解析 Commentary

ISO 9000的核心定位：「為什麼」的哲學

ISO 9001告訴你要做什麼（What），而ISO 9000告訴你為什麼要這樣做（Why）。理解ISO 9000的人，在實施ISO 9001時能做出更好的判斷——尤其在面對標準條文語意不明時。

例如：ISO 9001要求「確定組織背景」，但不說明如何做。理解ISO 9000第2.2.3節（Context of Organization）的概念，你才知道內部因素（文化、能力）與外部因素（法規、市場）各應如何分析。

CDMO的適用性

ISO 9000強調「適用於所有組織，不論規模、複雜度或商業模式」——這對CDMO尤其重要。CDMO同時服務多個客戶（多品項），同時接受多個法規機構（FDA、EMA、PMDA）的稽查。ISO 9000的概念框架幫助CDMO建立一套能同時滿足所有要求的思維方式，而非針對每個客戶或法規機構各自應付。

Clause 1: Scope 第1節：適用範圍

Who is ISO 9000 for?

Original Text — Clause 1

This International Standard describes the fundamental concepts and principles of quality management which are universally applicable to:

  • Organizations seeking sustained success through implementation of a QMS
  • Customers seeking confidence in an organization's ability to consistently provide conforming products and services
  • Organizations seeking confidence in their supply chain
  • Organizations and interested parties seeking to improve communication through a common understanding of QM vocabulary
  • Organizations performing conformity assessments against ISO 9001 requirements
  • Providers of training, assessment or advice in quality management
  • Developers of related standards

This International Standard specifies the terms and definitions that apply to all quality management and QMS standards developed by ISO/TC 176.

解析 Commentary

製藥產業對應七類使用者

  • 製藥企業/CDMO → 尋求持續成功，建立QMS
  • 客戶（品牌藥廠） → 對CDMO的能力建立信心
  • CDMO對其供應商 → 供應鏈品質信心（API供應商、包材廠）
  • 跨部門溝通 → QA、生產、研發使用共同語言（什麼是「不符合」、「矯正措施」）
  • ISO 9001稽查員 → 了解標準基礎
  • QA顧問/培訓師 → 教學基礎
  • 法規機構/標準制定者 → ICH、WHO參考

詞彙統一的重要性

在FDA 483觀察項中，常見問題之一是不同部門對同一詞彙理解不同。例如「矯正措施」(corrective action) vs「預防措施」(preventive action)——ISO 9000在第3節提供了精確定義，確保全公司用同一語言溝通。

2.1–2.2: General & Fundamental Concepts 基本概念

Quality | QMS | Context | Interested Parties | Support

2.1 General — Why Concepts Matter

The quality management concepts and principles described in this International Standard give the organization the capacity to meet challenges presented by an environment that is profoundly different from recent decades — characterized by accelerated change, globalization of markets and the emergence of knowledge as a principal resource.

The impact of quality extends beyond customer satisfaction: it can also have a direct impact on the organization's reputation. Society has become better educated and more demanding, making interested parties increasingly more influential.

"All concepts, principles and their interrelationships should be seen as a whole and not in isolation of each other. No individual concept or principle is more important than another. At any one time, finding the right balance in application is critical."

解析 Commentary

「整體性」的重要提醒

ISO 9000明確警告：7大原則必須整體看待，不能只挑一兩個實施。這在製藥稽查中很常見：有些公司客戶導向做得很好（P1），但流程管理薄弱（P4），導致批次記錄異常率高。或者有強大的改善系統（P5 CAPA），但領導層不足（P2），導致改善措施無法落實。

在CDMO的環境下，知識（knowledge）確實是主要資源——製程知識、法規知識、客戶需求理解——都需要有系統地管理。

2.2.1 Quality 品質

An organization focused on quality promotes a culture that results in the behaviour, attitudes, activities and processes that deliver value through fulfilling the needs and expectations of customers and other relevant interested parties.

The quality of an organization's products and services is determined by the ability to satisfy customers and the intended and unintended impact on relevant interested parties.

The quality of products and services includes not only their intended function and performance, but also their perceived value and benefit to the customer.

品質的製藥定義延伸

注意「intended and unintended impact」——製藥領域，unintended impact包含：副作用風險、交叉污染、環境影響。ICH Q8/Q9/Q10框架中的「Quality by Design」正是回應這個要求：在設計階段就預見所有潛在影響，而非靠終端測試發現問題。

「perceived value」在CDMO語境下：客戶付錢買的不只是合格的批次，還有批次記錄的清晰度、溝通的即時性、供貨的穩定性——這些都是「感知價值」。

2.2.2 Quality Management System (QMS) 品質管理系統

A QMS comprises activities by which the organization identifies its objectives and determines the processes and resources required to achieve desired results.

The QMS manages the interacting processes and resources required to provide value and realize results for relevant interested parties.

The QMS enables top management to optimize the use of resources considering the long and short term consequences of their decisions.

A QMS provides the means to identify actions to address intended and unintended consequences in providing products and services.

QMS ≠ 文件系統

很多人把QMS等同於SOP文件庫——這是最常見的誤解。ISO 9000說得很清楚：QMS是「識別目標、決定過程與資源以達成結果」的活動集合。文件是支撐QMS的工具，不是QMS本身。

在CDMO：一個有效的QMS讓管理層能夠決定：哪條生產線優先排程、哪個供應商值得長期合作、哪個改善項目優先投資——這些都是QMS對資源優化的具體表現。

2.2.3 Context of an Organization 組織環境

Understanding the context of the organization is a process. This process determines factors which influence the organization's purpose, objectives and sustainability. It considers:

  • Internal factors: values, culture, knowledge and performance of the organization
  • External factors: legal, technological, competitive, market, cultural, social and economic environments

Examples of the ways in which an organization's purpose can be expressed include its vision, mission, policies and objectives.

CDMO的組織環境分析（SWOT對應）

內部因素： 現有設施能力（無菌灌裝線數量）、人員能力水準、現有QMS成熟度、企業文化（是否鼓勵問題通報）。

外部因素： FDA 21 CFR 210/211法規要求、EMA GMP指引、客戶合約規定、競爭對手的技術能力、原材料市場供應穩定性。

這對應ISO 9001:2015的第4.1條——理解ISO 9000的概念，才能更有意義地完成這個分析，而非只是填一張SWOT表格應付稽查員。

2.2.4 Interested Parties 利益相關方

The concept of interested parties extends beyond a focus solely on the customer. The relevant interested parties are those that provide significant risk to organizational sustainability if their needs and expectations are not met.

Organizations attract, capture and retain the support of the relevant interested parties they depend upon for their success.

CDMO的利益相關方全圖

  • 客戶（品牌藥廠）：需要合規、準時、符合成本的製造服務
  • 法規機構（FDA、EMA、PMDA）：需要GMP合規
  • 最終患者：需要安全有效的藥品
  • 員工：需要安全工作環境與職業發展
  • 供應商：需要公平合約與穩定採購
  • 股東/投資人：需要財務回報與聲譽維護
  • 社區/環境：需要負責任的廢棄物管理

並非所有利益相關方同等重要——ISO 9000的關鍵詞是「relevant」（相關的）。組織需判斷哪些方的需求若不滿足，將對可持續性造成重大風險。

2.2.5 Support 支持

Top management support of the QMS and engagement of people enables:

  • Provision of adequate human and other resources
  • Monitoring of processes and results
  • Determining and evaluating of risks and opportunities
  • Implementing appropriate actions

2.2.5.2 People: Performance is dependent upon how people behave within the system in which they work. People become engaged and aligned through a common understanding of the quality policy.

2.2.5.3 Competence: A QMS is most effective when all employees understand and apply the skills, training, education and experience needed to perform their roles.

2.2.5.4 Awareness: Awareness is attained when people understand their responsibilities and how their actions contribute to the achievement of the organization's objectives.

2.2.5.5 Communication: Planned and effective internal and external communication enhances people's engagement and understanding of context, customer needs, and the QMS.

「支持」是QMS的基礎建設

這5個子概念（資源、人員、能力、意識、溝通）在ISO 9001:2015第7節中都有對應要求。理解這些概念的邏輯鏈：

Top management提供資源 → 選擇具備能力的人員 → 建立意識（知道自己工作的意義）→ 透過溝通保持對齊。

在製藥CDMO的稽查觀察中，「能力」(competence)的定義至關重要：FDA期望看到education + training + experience的完整組合，而非只有完成訓練記錄。能力要有「驗證」，例如操作員實際執行無菌技術的評估記錄。

意識 vs 訓練：關鍵差異

很多組織混淆「完成訓練」與「建立意識」。ISO 9000的定義是：意識=理解自己的責任+知道自己的行為如何影響組織目標。完成訓練簽名不代表有意識。一個無菌填充操作員若不理解為什麼要做LVP（低風險行為訓練），其操作風險就高。

解析 Commentary

為什麼從8原則精簡到7原則？

ISO 9000:2005有8大原則。2015年修訂時，「系統方法管理」(System approach to management) 被合併進「過程方法」(Process approach)，因為兩者概念高度重疊。這體現了ISO 9000的整體觀：組織是一個相互關聯的系統，過程方法本身就是系統思維的體現。

PDCA與ICH Q10的關係

ICH Q10 Pharmaceutical Quality System（製藥品質系統）在結構上與PDCA高度對應：

  • Plan → ICH Q10: Process Performance and Product Quality Monitoring System
  • Do → ICH Q10: Corrective Action and Preventive Action (CAPA) implementation
  • Check → ICH Q10: Management Review
  • Act → ICH Q10: Change Management System

理解PDCA讓你看懂ICH Q10的底層邏輯，而非把它當作另一堆合規要求。

風險思維：不是獨立工具，是思維方式

ISO 9000明確說明：風險思維不是一個獨立的過程或工具（不像FMEA那樣的獨立方法），而是貫穿於整個管理系統的思維方式。每個決策、每個過程設計都應該包含「如果出問題會怎樣」的考量。這與ICH Q9（品質風險管理）的精神完全一致。

P1

2.3.1 Customer Focus 顧客導向

Primary focus: meet requirements and strive to exceed expectations

1
Customer Focus
顧客導向
Statement: The primary focus of quality management is to meet customer requirements and to strive to exceed customer expectations.

Rationale: Sustained success is achieved when an organization attracts and retains the confidence of customers and other relevant interested parties. Every aspect of customer interaction provides an opportunity to create more value.

Key Benefits:

  • Increased customer value and satisfaction
  • Improved customer loyalty
  • Enhanced repeat business
  • Enhanced reputation of the organization
  • Expanded customer base
  • Increased revenue and market share

Possible Actions:

  • Recognize direct and indirect customers as those who receive value
  • Understand customers' current and future needs and expectations
  • Link organizational objectives to customer needs
  • Communicate customer needs throughout the organization
  • Plan, design, develop, produce, deliver and support products/services to meet customer needs
  • Measure and monitor customer satisfaction and take appropriate actions
  • Actively manage relationships with customers to achieve sustained success

解析 Commentary

CDMO的顧客導向：患者是最終顧客

CDMO的直接客戶是品牌藥廠，但間接客戶是最終患者。ISO 9000對「顧客」的定義包含direct（直接）和indirect（間接）——這意味著CDMO在做決策時必須考慮最終患者安全，而非只滿足品牌藥廠的商業需求。

實例：品牌藥廠可能要求縮短交期，但若這會影響批次品質，CDMO有責任以患者安全為由提出異議。這就是「顧客導向」的真正意涵——超越眼前交易，建立長期信任。

「超越期望」的製藥解讀

在製藥CDMO，超越客戶期望的具體表現：

  • 主動預警原材料供應風險（而非等客戶問）
  • 批次記錄品質超過最低要求（清晰度、完整性）
  • 提供製程優化建議（而非只執行客戶的規格）
  • 出現偏差時主動溝通，不等客戶發現

常見失敗模式

組織只看短期業績KPI（今季出貨量），忽略長期客戶關係管理。在製藥，這等同於：只看批次通過率，不看客戶對整體服務品質的滿意度——導致合約不續簽。

P2

2.3.2 Leadership 領導力

Unity of purpose and direction; creating conditions for engagement

2
Leadership
領導力
Statement: Leaders at all levels establish unity of purpose and direction and create conditions in which people are engaged in achieving the organization's quality objectives.

Rationale: Creation of unity of purpose and engagement enables an organization to align its strategies, policies, processes and resources to achieve its objectives.

Key Benefits:

  • Increased effectiveness and efficiency in meeting quality objectives
  • Better coordination of the organization's processes
  • Improved communication between levels and functions
  • Development and improvement of the capability to deliver desired results

Possible Actions:

  • Communicate the organization's mission, vision, strategy, policies and processes
  • Create and sustain shared values, fairness and ethical models for behaviour
  • Establish a culture of trust and integrity
  • Encourage an organization-wide commitment to quality
  • Ensure leaders at all levels are positive examples
  • Provide people with required resources, training and authority to act with accountability
  • Inspire, encourage and recognize the contribution of people

解析 Commentary

QP（合格人士）制度：領導力的法規化體現

歐盟GMP框架中，Qualified Person（QP）是代表最高管理層對每批藥品品質做出認證的關鍵角色。這完美體現了「領導力」原則——QP的權威不可被商業壓力凌駕，這需要高層真正建立「品質文化」，而非只是名義上的承諾。

若高層只在稽查時才表現重視品質，員工就會學習「稽查模式」——平時走捷徑，稽查前才趕緊補文件。這正是領導力失效的典型表現。

「各級別領導」的重要性

ISO 9000強調「leaders at all levels」——不只是CEO或QA總監，還包括現場主管、班長。在無菌填充操作中，現場主管（班長）的行為直接影響操作員是否願意通報潛在問題。如果班長文化是「能過就過」，再好的QMS文件都是空話。

P3

2.3.3 Engagement of People 人員參與

Competent, empowered and engaged people at all levels

3
Engagement of People
人員參與
Statement: Competent, empowered and engaged people at all levels throughout the organization are essential to enhance the organization's capability to create and deliver value.

Rationale: Recognition, empowerment and enhancement of competence facilitate the engagement of people in achieving the organization's quality objectives.

Key Benefits:

  • Improved understanding of quality objectives and increased motivation
  • Enhanced involvement in improvement activities
  • Enhanced personal development, initiatives and creativity
  • Enhanced people satisfaction
  • Enhanced trust and collaboration throughout the organization
  • Increased attention to shared values and culture

Possible Actions:

  • Communicate to promote understanding of individual contribution
  • Promote collaboration throughout the organization
  • Facilitate open discussion and sharing of knowledge and experience
  • Empower people to determine constraints to performance and take initiatives without fear
  • Recognize and acknowledge people's contribution, learning and improvement
  • Enable self-evaluation of performance against personal objectives
  • Conduct surveys to assess people's satisfaction

解析 Commentary

跨職能團隊：人員參與的最佳實踐

在製藥CDMO，人員參與的最高形式是有效的跨職能團隊：QA、生產、研發、供應鏈共同處理偏差調查、CAPA設計、新產品導入。若每個部門只各自為政，問題的根本原因往往找不到（例如：生產說是設備問題，QC說是原料問題，QA說是SOP問題——只有跨部門合作才能找到真正原因）。

「無懼環境」(without fear) 的關鍵性

ISO 9000特別提到empowering people to take initiatives without fear。這對應FDA的「品質文化」概念：員工若害怕通報問題會被責罰，就會選擇沉默——導致小問題變成大事故。ISO 9000把這個心理安全環境定義為品質管理的核心，而非只是HR議題。

P4

2.3.4 Process Approach 過程方法

Consistent and predictable results through interrelated processes managed as a system

4
Process Approach
過程方法
Statement: Consistent and predictable results are achieved more effectively and efficiently when activities are understood and managed as interrelated processes that function as a coherent system.

Rationale: The QMS consists of interrelated processes. Understanding how results are produced by this system enables an organization to optimize the system and its performance.

Key Benefits:

  • Enhanced ability to focus effort on key processes and opportunities for improvement
  • Consistent and predictable outcomes through a system of aligned processes
  • Optimized performance through effective process management, efficient use of resources and reduced cross-functional barriers
  • Enabling confidence to interested parties related to consistency, effectiveness and efficiency

Possible Actions:

  • Define objectives of the system and processes necessary to achieve them
  • Establish authority, responsibility and accountability for managing processes
  • Understand the organization's capabilities and determine resource constraints prior to action
  • Determine process interdependencies and analyse the effect of modifications on the system as a whole
  • Manage risks which can affect outputs of the processes and overall outcomes of the QMS

解析 Commentary

Process vs. Procedure — FDA稽查中的關鍵區別

Procedure（程序）：描述「如何做某件事」的文件指引（SOP）。

Process（過程）：將輸入轉化為輸出的一組相互關聯的活動，包含其輸入、輸出、資源、控制點、績效指標。

FDA 483常見觀察：「操作員依照SOP操作，但仍發生問題」——原因往往是SOP（procedure）正確，但過程設計（process design）有缺陷，例如輸入材料的品質控制點不足，或過程之間的交接介面定義不清楚。

ISO 9000的「過程方法」要求你理解過程，不只是遵循程序。

ICH Q10：製藥品質系統 = 過程方法的最佳實踐

ICH Q10定義的四個主要過程：
(1) 過程績效與產品品質監控系統
(2) 矯正措施與預防措施系統（CAPA）
(3) 變更管理系統
(4) 管理審查

這四個過程相互關聯：監控系統發現問題→觸發CAPA→CAPA可能引發變更→變更在管理審查中評估。理解過程間的輸入輸出關係，才能設計有效的QMS。

2.3.5 Improvement 改善

Successful organizations have an ongoing focus on improvement

5
Improvement
改善
Statement: Successful organizations have an ongoing focus on improvement.

Rationale: Improvement is essential for an organization to maintain current levels of performance, to react to changes in its internal and external conditions and to create new opportunities.

Key Benefits:

  • Improved process performance, organizational capability and customer satisfaction
  • Enhanced focus on root cause investigation and determination, followed by prevention and corrective actions
  • Enhanced ability to anticipate and react to internal and external risks and opportunities
  • Enhanced consideration of both incremental and breakthrough improvement
  • Improved use of learning for improvement
  • Enhanced drive for innovation

  • Educate and train people on basic tools and methodologies

  • Recognize and acknowledge improvement

解析 Commentary

CAPA系統：改善原則的法規實踐

Corrective Action and Preventive Action（CAPA）是製藥GMP對「改善」原則的法規化要求。21 CFR 820.100（美國QSR）和EU GMP第1章均要求有效的CAPA系統。

ISO 9000強調兩類改善：
Incremental（漸進式）：CAPA處理偏差，持續降低不符合率
Breakthrough（突破式）：製程重新設計、PAT（製程分析技術）導入

只有漸進式改善而無突破性創新，組織最終會停滯。只追求突破而不做好日常改善，基礎不穩。兩者必須平衡。

「改善」的最大陷阱：CAPA的形式化

在許多製藥公司，CAPA系統變成「關閉偏差記錄」的工具，而非真正改善的引擎。常見徵兆：CAPA的根本原因分析只寫「人員訓練不足」，改善措施只有「重新訓練」——下次同類問題照樣發生。真正的改善需要系統性根因分析（5 Whys, Fishbone），而非症狀處理。

Evidence-Based Decision Making

Statement: Decisions based on the analysis and evaluation of data and information are more likely to produce desired results.

Rationale: Decision-making can be a complex process and it always involves some uncertainty. Facts, evidence and data analysis lead to greater objectivity and confidence in decision making.

Key Benefits:

  • Improved decision making processes
  • Improved assessment of process performance and ability to achieve objectives
  • Improved operational effectiveness and efficiency
  • Increased ability to review, challenge and change opinions and decisions
  • Increased ability to demonstrate the effectiveness of past decisions

Possible Actions:

  • Make all data needed available to the relevant people
  • Ensure that data and information are sufficiently accurate, reliable and secure
  • Analyse and evaluate data and information using suitable methods
  • Ensure people are competent to analyse and evaluate data as needed
  • Make decisions and take actions based on evidence, balanced with experience and intuition

解析 Commentary

OOS調查：以事實決策的最高要求

Out-of-Specification（OOS）調查是製藥以事實決策最典型的應用。FDA Guidance for Industry (OOS, 2006) 要求：所有OOS結果必須完整調查，不能因為「可能是實驗室錯誤」就直接拒絕結果（invalidation必須有充分科學依據）。

每個拒批或放行決定，都必須有數據支撐，不能依賴「感覺這批應該沒問題」的直覺。

重要：「平衡經驗與直覺」

ISO 9000特別提到決策要「balanced with experience and intuition」——這不是說直覺比數據重要，而是承認人類判斷在複雜決策中的作用。在製藥，資深QA的「直覺」（基於多年經驗的模式識別）可以在數據不完整時幫助識別問題，但最終決策仍需數據支持。ISO 9000在此提供了更現實的平衡觀點，而非單純的「data-only」主義。

數據品質的前提

以事實決策的前提是「data integrity」（資料完整性）——ALCOA+ 原則（Attributable, Legible, Contemporaneous, Original, Accurate, plus Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available）。若原始數據有問題，所有基於數據的決策都是建立在沙上的城堡。這也是FDA近年對數據完整性稽查力度顯著加強的背景。

供應商資格審查計畫：關係管理的法規實踐

FDA 21 CFR 211.68/211.80 以及EU GMP第5章均要求組織對供應商進行資格確認和定期評估。這不只是「挑最便宜的供應商」，而是建立長期戰略夥伴關係：

  • 初始資格：稽查、問卷、樣品測試
  • 持續評估：定期再稽查、批次品質數據分析
  • 改善合作：與供應商共同解決品質問題（聯合CAPA）
  • 長期vs短期：不因短期成本壓力輕易更換已驗證的優質供應商

CDMO在關係管理上有雙重身份：
對上游供應商（API廠、包材廠）是客戶 → 需要評估和管理供應商關係
對下游品牌藥廠是服務提供者 → 需要維護客戶關係

這兩個方向的關係管理都必須做好，才能保證整個供應鏈的品質穩定。任何一個環節的關係管理失效，都會影響最終患者收到的藥品品質。

2.4 Developing the QMS 發展品質管理系統

QMS Model | QMS Development | Integration with other standards

Original Text — 2.4

2.4.1 QMS Model

Organizations share many characteristics with humans as a living and learning social organism. Both are adaptive and comprise interacting systems, processes and activities. An organization's QMS model recognizes that not all systems, processes and activities can be predetermined; therefore it needs to be flexible and adaptable within the complexities of the organizational context.

The outputs from one process can be the inputs into other processes and are interlinked into the overall network. Although often appearing to be comprised of similar processes, each organization and its QMS is unique.

Processes have interrelated activities with inputs to deliver outputs. People collaborate within a process to carry out their daily activities.

2.4.2 Development of a QMS

A QMS is a dynamic system that evolves over time through periods of improvement. Every organization has quality management activities, whether formally planned or not.

A formal QMS provides a framework for planning, executing, monitoring and improving the performance of quality management activities. The QMS does not need to be complicated; rather it needs to accurately reflect the needs of the organization.

QMS planning is not a singular event, rather it is an ongoing process. Auditing is a means of evaluating the effectiveness of the QMS, in order to identify risks and to determine the fulfilment of requirements.

2.4.3 QMS Standards, Other Management Systems and Excellence Models

Standards related to quality management (ISO 9001), environmental management (ISO 14001) and energy management (ISO 50001), as well as other management standards and organizational excellence models, have addressed common principles. The various parts of an organization's management system can be integrated as a single management system.

The organization can perform an integrated audit of its management system against the requirements of multiple International Standards, such as ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO/IEC 27001 and ISO 50001.

解析 Commentary

「活的有機體」比喻

ISO 9000將組織比作「living and learning social organism」——這個比喻深刻：就像生物體會適應環境，組織的QMS也必須隨內外部環境變化而調整。僵化、一成不變的QMS會像不能適應環境的物種一樣被淘汰。

製藥CDMO的QMS必須能夠快速適應：新客戶的要求、新法規的實施、新技術的導入、疫情等突發事件——同時保持核心品質控制不動搖。

「不複雜但準確反映需求」

這句話對中小型CDMO非常重要。很多公司誤以為QMS越複雜、SOP越多就越好。ISO 9000明確說：QMS不需要複雜，只需要準確反映組織的真實需求和運作方式。過度複雜的QMS反而讓員工無法遵從，增加不符合的風險。

整合管理系統（IMS）的效益

對製藥CDMO，整合ISO 9001（品質）+ ISO 14001（環境）+ ISO 45001（職安衛）的實際效益：

  • 一套稽查而非三套稽查，節省時間與成本
  • 同一支持系統（文件管理、訓練、內部稽查）服務多個標準
  • 避免各系統之間的矛盾和重複
  • 向客戶和法規機構展示系統性的管理能力

稽查的真正目的

ISO 9000說稽查是「評估QMS有效性、識別風險、確定要求履行」的手段——不是懲罰工具，不是形式性合規檢查。有效的內部稽查應該是QMS改善的驅動力，發現問題是好事，代表系統正在正常運作。這個思維轉變對建立健康的品質文化至關重要。

Commentary & Pharma Application  |  解析與製藥應用

Top Management in Pharma: QP vs. ISO 9000 定義

ISO 9000 defines "top management" functionally — whoever has authority over the QMS scope, can delegate, and provides resources. In pharmaceutical manufacturing, this typically maps to the Site Director or VP of Operations.

However, pharma has a uniquely regulated role with no direct ISO equivalent: the Qualified Person (QP) under EU GMP Annex 16. The QP holds legal authority to certify batch release — authority that cannot be fully delegated upward or downward. This creates a dual-authority structure:

  • ISO "top management" → Site Director (business + QMS authority)
  • Regulatory "top authority" → QP (batch release authority, personal legal liability)

In ISO audits, the question "who is top management?" matters because ISO 9001 Clause 5 places specific obligations (leadership, commitment, policy) on top management. Ensure roles are clearly documented.

Involvement vs. Engagement — 為什麼這個差異重要？

Think of it this way: a bystander who watches a fire drill is involved (present, taking part). A fire warden who leads the drill, checks headcounts, and reports findings is engaged (contributing, driving outcomes).

In pharma quality culture, ISO 9001:2015 explicitly requires engagement of people — not just their physical presence in training sessions. This shift is deliberate: the 2015 revision moved away from "procedure compliance" toward genuine participation and ownership.

For GMP purposes: an operator who signs a batch record is involved. An operator who flags a process deviation before it becomes an OOS result is engaged.

Commentary & Pharma Application  |  解析與製藥應用

"Interested Party" in Pharma — Who Counts? 利害關係人：製藥業的完整清單

ISO 9000's definition of interested party is deliberately broad: anyone who can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves affected by your decisions. For a pharma CDMO, this includes:

  • Regulatory authorities — FDA, EMA, PMDA, TFDA; can shut down manufacturing
  • Marketing Authorization Holder (MAH) — client who owns the product license
  • Patients — ultimate end-users; cannot perceive a problem until harm occurs
  • Healthcare providers — prescribers, pharmacists who depend on product quality
  • Internal workforce — operators, QC analysts exposed to risk
  • Investors / shareholders — exposed to recall, enforcement, reputational risk
  • Society / environment — affected by pharmaceutical waste, water discharge

ISO 9001:2015 Clause 4.2 requires identifying and understanding the needs of relevant interested parties — a more expansive obligation than ICH Q10's "customer focus."

Customer vs. Patient — 顧客與病患的差異

In a CDMO business model, the customer (3.2.4) is the MAH who pays for manufacturing. But the end-user — the patient — is not the paying customer.

ISO 9000 captures this complexity: "consumer, client, end-user, retailer... beneficiary" are all potential customers depending on context. In pharma: the MAH is the direct customer; the patient is the end-user; the healthcare provider is an intermediary. All three have different "requirements" (3.6.4) the QMS must satisfy.

This distinction matters enormously in QMS audits. When a non-conformance harms a patient who never directly contracted with you, regulatory liability still applies — because the patient is a recognized interested party.

Provider vs. External Provider — CDMO Context 外部提供者在合約製造中的應用

ISO 9000 distinguishes provider (3.2.5) from external provider (3.2.6). A CDMO is an external provider to the MAH. Within the CDMO, raw material suppliers, contract testing labs, and qualified subcontractors are also external providers.

ISO 9001:2015 Clause 8.4 places specific controls on externally provided processes, products and services. This directly maps to GMP Annex 7 (outsourced activities) — both require qualifying and monitoring external providers as part of the QMS.

Context of the Organization — PESTLE Analysis 組織環境分析

Clause 4.1 of ISO 9001:2015 requires identifying internal and external issues. Common frameworks used to map "context of the organization" include PESTLE (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental). For pharma:

  • Political: FDA enforcement climate, import alerts
  • Legal: country-specific GMP legislation, data integrity requirements
  • Technological: PAT adoption, continuous manufacturing trends
  • Social: patient access, drug shortage risks

KEY: QA vs. QC — The Most Misunderstood Distinction in Pharma 製藥界最常混淆的概念

ISO 9000 provides precise, unambiguous definitions:

  • QA (3.3.6) = Part of quality management focused on providing confidence that requirements will be fulfilled — prospective, systemic, preventive.
  • QC (3.3.7) = Part of quality management focused on fulfilling quality requirements — operational, testing, verification of actual results.

Pharma example:

  • QA writes the SOP for sterility testing, validates the test method, and audits that the lab follows it → providing confidence the system works.
  • QC runs the sterility test on each batch, reads results, and reports pass/fail → fulfilling the test requirement on each unit.

A clean room that is built and validated correctly (QA activity) is different from the environmental monitoring swab taken today (QC activity). Both are needed. Neither replaces the other.

Quality Management as the Umbrella 品質管理是大傘

Think of quality management (3.3.4) as an umbrella with four spokes:

  • Planning (3.3.5) — deciding where to go / 策劃目標
  • Assurance (3.3.6) — checking you have the right map / 提供信心
  • Control (3.3.7) — navigating correctly at each step / 執行確認
  • Improvement (3.3.8) — making the next trip better / 持續改善

All four are parts of quality management — not alternatives to each other. In pharma QMS organization charts, QA and QC departments handle different spokes of the same umbrella.

Continual vs. Continuous — ISO Uses "Continual" Deliberately 持續 vs. 連續：ISO的有意選擇

"Continual improvement" (3.3.2) means recurring cycles — improvement activities happen repeatedly over time but may have gaps between cycles. The PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle is the classic model.

"Continuous" would imply uninterrupted, non-stop activity — like a continuous manufacturing process running 24/7 without pause. ISO 9001 deliberately uses "continual" because:

  • Improvement projects have start and end points (CAPA closure, change control completion)
  • Between improvement cycles, the system may be stable (not actively improving every second)
  • "Continuous" is reserved for process descriptions in chemistry/engineering contexts

GMP auditors sometimes confuse these terms. The correct ISO 9001 language is always "continual improvement."

Change Control in Pharma — 3.3.10 Mapped to GMP 變更管制對照

ISO 9000's "change control" (3.3.10) aligns directly with GMP change control systems. In both frameworks:

  • Formal approval precedes any change to a controlled output
  • Product configuration information (specifications, methods, processes) requires documented control
  • Configuration management provides the systematic framework

For pharma: change control applies to master batch records, validated processes, analytical methods, equipment qualifications, and facility configurations — all elements of "product configuration information" in ISO 9000 terms.

Commentary & Pharma Application  |  解析與製藥應用

Process Model: Input → Transformation → Output 過程模型

ISO 9000's process definition establishes the fundamental model used throughout ISO 9001 and all ISO management system standards:

  • Inputs — typically outputs of preceding processes (materials, information, energy)
  • Activities — the interrelated, value-adding work that transforms inputs
  • Outputs — intended result: product, service, or other output
  • Controls — procedures, specifications, regulations that govern the transformation
  • Resources — people, equipment, infrastructure, environment

In aseptic fill-finish, a single manufacturing process (e.g., vial filling) has inputs (sterile bulk drug product, stopper, vial), activities (filling, stoppering, capping), controls (SOP, process parameters, environmental conditions), resources (isolator, filling machine, operators), and outputs (filled vials ready for inspection).

Special Process — 無法完全驗證輸出的過程

Note 5 to process (3.4.1) introduces the concept of a "special process": one where conformity of the resulting output cannot be readily or economically validated.

In GMP, this concept maps directly to aseptic processing: you cannot 100% test sterility of every vial without destroying it. Process validation (APS, media fills) provides the evidence that the process reliably produces sterile product — even though the output cannot be fully verified after-the-fact.

Special processes require heightened controls: qualified operators, validated processes, controlled environments, and continuous monitoring — all elements of PDA TR22 and Annex 1.

Outsource (3.4.6) — Scope Boundary 外包的範圍邊界

ISO 9000 clarifies a critical boundary: the external organization performing outsourced work is outside the QMS scope, but the function or process itself remains inside the scope.

Real-world pharma translation: if a CDMO outsources analytical testing to a CRO, the CRO is not part of the CDMO's QMS. But the testing activity — its accuracy, turnaround, OOS handling — is very much within the CDMO's QMS responsibility. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 8.4 and GMP Annex 7 both require the organization to control outsourced activities as if they were internal.

Procedure (3.4.5) — Documented or Not? 程序：可以不書面化？

ISO 9000 explicitly notes that procedures can be documented or undocumented. ISO 9001:2015 no longer mandates the "six mandatory procedures" required by the 2008 version. Instead, it requires "documented information" where needed to support operations.

In contrast, GMP regulations (EU Annex 1, FDA Aseptic Guidance) still require extensive written SOPs. The practical takeaway: ISO 9001 sets a minimum floor; pharma GMP requirements set a higher bar. A pharma company's QMS must satisfy both — and GMP typically wins on procedural documentation requirements.

QMS — Not Just the Manual 品質管理系統不等於品質手冊

A common misconception: the QMS is the quality manual, or the set of SOPs. ISO 9000 defines QMS (3.5.4) as "part of a management system with regard to quality" — i.e., all the interrelated elements (structure, roles, processes, documentation, resources) that work together to achieve quality.

The quality manual is one documented information artifact within the QMS. The QMS itself encompasses:

  • Organizational structure and responsibilities
  • All processes from design to delivery
  • Infrastructure, work environment, resources
  • Documented information (policies, procedures, records)
  • Monitoring, measurement, audit, and improvement activities

When an FDA inspector asks "show me your QMS," they want to see the whole system in operation — not just a folder of SOPs.

Work Environment (3.5.5) in Pharma — Beyond Temperature 工作環境不只是溫度

ISO 9000 explicitly includes psychological and social factors in work environment: recognition schemes, occupational stress, ergonomics. This is more holistic than GMP's typical focus on physical environment (temperature, humidity, particle count, lighting).

Why does this matter? Operator error is a leading cause of GMP deviations. An overworked, stressed, or unrecognized operator in an aseptic area is a contamination risk factor. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 7.1.4 explicitly requires managing the environment to support conforming products — and that includes the human environment.

Metrological Confirmation (3.5.6) — Calibration Lifecycle 計量確認：校準的全過程

Metrological confirmation is more than a calibration sticker. ISO 9000 defines it as a set of operations including:

  • Calibration or verification of the instrument
  • Adjustment or repair if out of tolerance
  • Subsequent recalibration after adjustment
  • Comparison with metrological requirements for the intended use
  • Required sealing and labelling

Key distinction: metrological requirements (the instrument's accuracy specification) are distinct from product requirements (the spec on the product being measured). A pH meter calibrated to ±0.05 pH may or may not be fit for a process requiring pH ±0.1 — the metrological confirmation process determines fitness for intended use.

Policy → Vision → Mission → Strategy 方針、願景、使命、策略的層次

ISO 9000 provides definitions for organizational direction concepts that are often loosely used:

  • Mission (3.5.11) — Why we exist / 我們存在的理由
  • Vision (3.5.10) — Where we aspire to be / 我們想成為什麼
  • Strategy (3.5.12) — The plan to get there / 實現願景的計畫
  • Policy (3.5.8) — Top management's stated intentions within the QMS / 高層對品質的承諾聲明

In pharma quality culture, quality policy must be aligned with the mission and vision — not treated as a standalone compliance document. A CDMO whose mission is "delivering safe medicines reliably" should have a quality policy that explicitly supports that mission.

Nonconformity vs. Defect — Legal Distinction Matters 不符合 vs. 缺陷：法律意義的差異

ISO 9000 draws a sharp distinction:

  • Nonconformity (3.6.9) — Non-fulfilment of any requirement. A broad term: a missing SOP signature, a temperature excursion, a failed sterility test — all are nonconformities.
  • Defect (3.6.10) — Nonconformity related to an intended or specified use. Narrower, and carries legal liability implications: product/service liability, recall, patient harm.

In pharma: a visible particle in an injectable is both a nonconformity (fails specification) and a defect (fails intended use — safe injection). A process that runs 2°C above the validated range may be a nonconformity without necessarily being a defect — depending on risk assessment of impact on product quality.

Why it matters: product liability claims hinge on whether a product had a "defect." ISO 9000 aligns the vocabulary with legal frameworks.

Statutory vs. Regulatory Requirement — Two Layers 法定要求 vs. 法規要求

ISO 9000 distinguishes these carefully:

  • Statutory requirement (3.6.6) — Set by a legislative body (parliament, congress). Example: the US Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act; EU Directive 2001/83/EC on medicinal products.
  • Regulatory requirement (3.6.7) — Set by an authority mandated by that legislative body. Example: FDA's 21 CFR Parts 210/211 (Current GMP regulations); EMA guidance; WHO GMP guidelines.

The law creates the framework; the regulatory authority writes the technical rules. Both are obligatory. ISO 9001 Clause 8.2.2 requires capturing both types of applicable requirements when determining product and service requirements.

Quality Definition — "Degree to Which" 品質的程度化定義

ISO 9000's definition of quality (3.6.2) is deliberately graduated: "degree to which a set of inherent characteristics fulfils requirements."

This means quality is not binary (good/bad) but a continuum. A product can have high quality (fully meets all requirements), adequate quality (meets critical requirements but has minor deviations), or poor quality (fails key requirements).

In pharma: a drug product's quality encompasses safety (no harmful impurities), efficacy (correct potency), and purity (correct identity, absence of contamination). Each characteristic contributes to the overall "degree" of quality fulfillment.

Traceability in Pharma — GMP Alignment 可追溯性的GMP應用

ISO 9000's definition of traceability (3.6.13) covers three dimensions for products:

  • Origin of materials and parts → raw material certificates, supplier lot numbers, chain of custody
  • Processing history → batch records, equipment logs, environmental monitoring records, operator IDs
  • Distribution and location after delivery → distribution records, serialization, cold chain documentation

EU Falsified Medicines Directive and US Drug Supply Chain Security Act (DSCSA) requirements align directly with this three-dimensional traceability model — extending it to the serialization and patient-level tracking of finished medicines.

Requirement (3.6.4) — Stated, Implied, or Obligatory 要求的三種形式

ISO 9000 identifies three types of requirements that together define what the QMS must satisfy:

  • Stated — Explicitly documented in contracts, specifications, regulatory filings
  • Generally implied — Common practice or custom that both parties take for granted (e.g. a drug product must not contain gross particulate contamination even if not specified in every contract)
  • Obligatory — Legal / regulatory requirements that apply regardless of what the contract says

Note 5's addition is particularly significant: to achieve high customer satisfaction, you may need to fulfil expectations that are neither stated, implied, nor obligatory. This is the basis for "customer delight" programs and proactive quality improvement beyond minimum compliance.

Risk = Effect of Uncertainty — Including Positive Risk 風險包含正面偏差（機會）

ISO 9000's definition of risk (3.7.9) is strikingly different from everyday usage. Key points:

  • Risk = "effect of uncertainty" — it is an effect, not the source of uncertainty itself
  • Effects can be positive or negative. A positive deviation from expected outcome is an opportunity
  • ISO 9001:2015 explicitly requires organizations to address both risks and opportunities (Clause 6.1)

Contrast with ICH Q9: ICH Q9 (pharmaceutical risk management) focuses primarily on threats to patient safety — harm, hazard, probability of harm, severity of harm. ICH Q9 risk = potential for negative outcomes.

ISO 9000's broader definition captures both sides: a new analytical technology might present uncertainty (risk), but if outcomes exceed expectations, it's an opportunity. Risk-based thinking in ISO 9001:2015 is explicitly bidirectional.

ISO 9000 distinguishes:

  • Output (3.7.5) — Generic result of any process
  • Product (3.7.6) — Output that can be produced without direct customer transaction; tangible dominant
  • Service (3.7.7) — Output requiring at least one activity performed with the customer; intangible dominant

For a CDMO: manufactured drug product = product (tangible, produced without patient interaction). Contract manufacturing services = service (requires interaction with the MAH customer throughout — tech transfer, batch review, release discussion). The same CDMO provides both products and services simultaneously.

This distinction matters for ISO 9001:2015 Clause 8.2 (Requirements for products and services) — different controls apply to each.

Effectiveness vs. Efficiency — 有效性 vs. 效率

A critical distinction often collapsed in practice:

  • Effectiveness (3.7.11) = Did we achieve the planned results? (Doing the right things) / 計畫的事做到了嗎？
  • Efficiency (3.7.10) = What was the resource cost of achieving those results? (Doing things right) / 用了多少資源去做？

Example: A CAPA that successfully prevents recurrence of a GMP deviation is effective. If it took 200 person-hours to close, it may not be efficient. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 9.1 requires monitoring both: KPIs for quality system performance should capture both dimensions.

For pharma quality culture: you can have high effectiveness with low efficiency (gold-plating CAPAs) or high efficiency with low effectiveness (rushing to close CAPAs without fixing root causes). The ideal is both.

Quality Objectives — SMART Requirements in ISO 9001 品質目標的設定

ISO 9000 defines quality objectives (3.7.2) as objectives related to quality, consistent with the quality policy. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 6.2 adds specific requirements for quality objectives — they must be:

  • Consistent with the quality policy
  • Measurable (where practicable)
  • Taking into account applicable requirements
  • Relevant to conformity of products/services and enhancement of customer satisfaction
  • Monitored, communicated, and updated as appropriate

For pharma sites: typical quality objectives include batch right-first-time rates, OOS rates, deviation closure timeliness, CAPA effectiveness, audit findings per cycle. These must cascade from quality policy to process-level targets.

Performance (3.7.8) — Measurable Results Across All Dimensions 績效：可量測的結果

ISO 9000's definition of performance as "measurable result" is deliberately broad — it applies to management activities, processes, products, services, systems, or organizations. This underpins ISO 9001:2015's performance evaluation requirements (Clause 9):

  • Monitoring and measurement of processes (process performance)
  • Customer satisfaction measurement (customer-facing performance)
  • Internal audit findings (system performance)
  • Management review inputs (organizational performance)

For pharma quality dashboards: process capability (Cpk), yield, OOS rate, deviation rate, and complaint rate are all "performance" data in ISO 9000 terms — measurable results that reflect the quality management system's effectiveness.

Key Takeaways for GMP Practitioners  |  GMP從業者重點摘要

QA/QC Distinction — Use ISO Language in Audits

When regulators or ISO auditors ask "what is your quality assurance?" they expect you to describe system-level activities that provide confidence in quality — not just describe the QA department. Use ISO 9000's precise language: QA = providing confidence; QC = fulfilling requirements.

Risk Language — Context Matters

In ICH Q9 discussions (pharmaceutical risk management, FMEA, hazard analysis), "risk" means threat to patient safety — probability × severity. In ISO 9001:2015 discussions (QMS risk-based thinking, Clause 6.1), "risk" includes opportunities. Clarify which framework applies before discussing "risk management."

The CDMO Dual Identity

A CDMO simultaneously operates as:

  • Organization (3.2.1) with its own QMS, policies, objectives
  • External provider (3.2.6) to the MAH customer
  • Customer (3.2.4) to its raw material suppliers
  • Provider of both products (3.7.6) and services (3.7.7)

ISO 9000's organizational framework captures all these roles simultaneously — which is why ISO 9001 certification is relevant for CDMOs beyond GMP compliance alone.

Effectiveness vs. Efficiency in CAPA

Apply both metrics to CAPA management:

  • Effectiveness: Did the CAPA prevent recurrence? (measure: repeat deviation rate)
  • Efficiency: How many person-hours / days to close? (measure: CAPA cycle time)

A quality system that is only effective but not efficient is costly. One that is only efficient but not effective creates repeat deviations. Both metrics should appear in management review.

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

"Documented Information" — The 2015 Unification 文件化資訊：2015年的整合

Before ISO 9001:2015, QMS standards distinguished between two types of controlled items:

  • Documents — procedures, SOPs, work instructions, forms (controlled for content)
  • Records — completed forms, batch records, logs (controlled for retention and retrieval)

ISO 9001:2015 merged both under the single term "documented information" — defined here in 3.8.6. The standard now uses two phrases:

  • "Maintain documented information" = what you used to call documents/procedures
  • "Retain documented information" = what you used to call records

Impact on pharma QMS: Your old SOP that says "the operator shall prepare a record" still works — but strictly speaking, ISO 9001:2015 language would say "the operator shall retain documented information as evidence." Most pharma companies kept their existing terminology since FDA regulations (21 CFR Part 211) still say "records."

Record → ALCOA+ Connection 記錄與ALCOA+的連結

ISO 9000's definition of "record" (3.8.10) — "document stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed" — maps directly to FDA's ALCOA+ data integrity principle:

  • Attributable — who created the record
  • Legible — readable throughout its retention period
  • Contemporaneous — recorded at the time of the activity
  • Original — first capture of data
  • Accurate — truthful representation of events
  • + Complete, Consistent, Enduring, Available

ISO 9000 gives you the vocabulary foundation; FDA's ALCOA+ adds the quality attributes that make that evidence trustworthy.

Verification vs. Validation — The Pharma View 驗證確認的製藥視角

ISO 9000 draws a clear distinction:

  • Verification (3.8.12) — Did we build the product right? Are specified requirements met? → IQ/OQ in pharma validation
  • Validation (3.8.13) — Did we build the right product? Does it work for its intended use? → PQ, process validation, cleaning validation

A clean room HVAC system might be verified to meet design specifications (air changes per hour, temperature range), but it's only validated when demonstrated to consistently maintain ISO 5 conditions during actual aseptic filling operations.

Objective Evidence in Pharma Audits 稽核中的客觀證據

ISO 9000 defines objective evidence (3.8.3) as data "supporting the existence or verity of something" obtained through observation, measurement, test, or other means. In a pharma CDMO audit context:

  • Batch records = objective evidence of process execution
  • Environmental monitoring logs = objective evidence of controlled conditions
  • Calibration certificates = objective evidence of measurement equipment fitness
  • Training records = objective evidence of personnel competence

When an FDA investigator requests "evidence that your process consistently produces sterile product," they are asking for objective evidence in the ISO 9000 sense.

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Who Is the Customer in Pharma CDMO? 誰是製藥CDMO的顧客？

ISO 9000 defines "customer" (3.2.4) as a person or organization that could or does receive a product or service. In a pharmaceutical CDMO context, there are multiple layers:

Patient 病患 — Ultimate End Customer
▲ receives treatment
Pharma Company 藥廠 — Direct Customer (NDA/MAA holder)
▲ contracts manufacturing
CDMO — Manufacturer/Provider

The direct customer (pharma company) defines the specifications, holds the regulatory dossier, and evaluates the CDMO's quality system. But the ultimate customer (patient) defines what "quality" actually means — efficacy, safety, and purity.

This is why pharma QMS must satisfy both: contractual requirements from the pharma client AND regulatory/compendial requirements protecting the patient.

Customer Complaints in Pharma QMS 藥品投訴管理

ISO 9000's "complaint" (3.9.3) maps to pharmaceutical complaint handling requirements:

  • 21 CFR 211.198 — written procedures for handling complaints, investigations required for potential product defects
  • EU GMP Chapter 8 — complaints and product recalls, QP notification
  • ICH Q10 — complaint handling as a knowledge management tool

Key pharma additions beyond ISO: complaints must be evaluated for whether they represent a product defect requiring recall or field alert report (FDA FAR). In ISO terms: a complaint triggers a determination (3.11.1) of nonconformity (3.6.9) and may require corrective action (3.12.2).

Customer Satisfaction ≠ Regulatory Compliance 顧客滿意 ≠ 法規合規

ISO 9000 Note 3 to 3.9.2 warns that "even when customer requirements have been agreed and fulfilled, this does not necessarily ensure high customer satisfaction." The reverse is also true in pharma:

A CDMO can satisfy all customer contractual requirements (batch yield, timeline, cost) while failing to meet regulatory requirements (sterility assurance, process validation). Both dimensions must be managed. This is why the Quality Agreement between CDMO and pharma client exists — to align what the customer wants with what the regulator requires.

Pharma Commentary 製藥產業解析

Traceability in Pharmaceutical Manufacturing 製藥製造中的追溯性

While ISO 9000 does not define "traceability" in Clause 3.10 directly (it appears in Clause 3.6.13 as the ability to trace history, application, or location of an object), the characteristics-based framework here underpins three types of traceability critical to pharma:

  • Material Traceability 物料追溯
    Raw material lot → intermediate → finished product. Required by FDA 21 CFR 211.184 (component labeling), EU GMP Part II, ICH Q7.
  • Equipment Traceability 設備追溯
    Each piece of equipment used in production linked to the batch record — equipment ID, last calibration, last cleaning, operator. ISO 9000 "metrological characteristic" (3.10.5) is the formal name for what you calibrate.
  • Process Traceability 製程追溯
    Process parameters (temperature, pressure, time), operator identity, and timestamps linked to each unit operation within a batch. This is what makes batch record review possible.

Inherent vs. Assigned Characteristics 固有特性與賦予特性

ISO 9000 Note 1 to 3.10.1 distinguishes inherent from assigned characteristics. In pharma:

  • Inherent quality characteristics of a drug product: potency, purity, sterility, particle size, dissolution rate — these exist in the product itself
  • Assigned characteristics: expiry date, batch number, price, storage temperature label — these are assigned by the manufacturer or regulator

Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs, per ICH Q8) are inherent quality characteristics whose variability has a significant impact on product quality. This maps exactly to ISO 9000's "quality characteristic" (3.10.2) — inherent characteristics related to requirements.

Human Factor in Aseptic Processing 無菌製程中的人為因素

ISO 9000 3.10.3 defines "human factor" as a characteristic of a person having an impact on an object under consideration. Note 2 states these can significantly impact a management system.

In aseptic fill-finish, human factors are the #1 source of contamination risk. Gowning technique, behaviour in the cleanroom, proximity to the fill zone — all are human factor characteristics that directly impact sterility assurance. This is why:

  • PIC/S Annex 1 requires personnel to minimize cleanroom interventions
  • Aseptic Process Simulations (media fills) specifically test human factor impact
  • Personnel qualification (ISO 9000: "competence" 3.10.4) is a regulatory requirement, not just an HR process

Monitoring vs. Measurement: The Critical Difference 監測與量測的關鍵差異

ISO 9000 3.11.3 notes that monitoring is "carried out at different stages or at different times" — implying ongoing, repeated observation. Measurement (3.11.4) is a single process to determine a specific value.

Pharma application:

  • Cleanroom temperature monitoring — continuous logging via calibrated sensors = monitoring. The system automatically checks status at each time point.
  • Airborne particle count measurement — discrete sampling event at defined locations per ISO 14644-1 = measurement. You get a specific number (e.g., 3,520 particles/m³ ≥0.5 µm).

Inspection adds judgment: a microbiologist reading a settle plate is not just measuring colony count — they are inspecting (making a conformity determination: does this result exceed the alert or action limit?).

Inspection Outcome: Conformity or Nonconformity? 查驗結果：符合或不符合？

ISO 9000 Note 2 to 3.11.7 states: "The result of an inspection can show conformity or nonconformity or a degree of conformity." This third option — degree of conformity — is important in pharma visual inspection:

  • Critical defect = nonconformity → reject
  • Major defect = reduced conformity → may trigger concession (3.12.5) or disposition decision
  • Minor defect / cosmetic = lower degree of conformity → may be acceptable per specification

AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) sampling explicitly acknowledges this: you accept a lot not because all units are conforming, but because the degree of conformity across the lot meets the acceptance criteria.

Review in Quality Management Context 品質管理中的審查

ISO 9000's "review" (3.11.2) is a determination of suitability, adequacy, or effectiveness. In pharma, this applies to multiple formal review activities:

  • Management Review — ISO 9001 Clause 9.3, covers QMS performance, audit results, customer feedback
  • Annual Product Review (APR) — FDA 21 CFR 211.180(e), EU GMP Chapter 1 — periodic review of product data to identify trends
  • Batch Record Review — QP/QA review before batch release — suitability check of all documentation
  • Change Control Review — adequacy determination before implementing changes

CAPA System Maps Exactly to ISO 9000 Definitions CAPA系統完全對應ISO 9000定義

Pharmaceutical CAPA (Corrective Action and Preventive Action) maps directly to ISO 9000 action terms:

CAPA ComponentISO 9000 TermDescription
Immediate Action (containment) Correction (3.12.3) Stop the problem now — quarantine the batch, recall, halt production
Corrective Action (CA) Corrective Action (3.12.2) Eliminate the root cause so this nonconformity does not recur
Preventive Action (PA) Preventive Action (3.12.1) Eliminate causes of potential nonconformities before they occur

FDA 21 CFR 820.100 (medical devices) and ICH Q10 explicitly require CAPA systems. ISO 9001:2015 Clause 10.2 covers corrective action but does NOT have a separate clause for preventive action — by design, as explained below.

Why Preventive Action Disappeared from ISO 9001:2015 預防措施為何從ISO 9001:2015消失？

ISO 9000:2015 still defines "preventive action" (3.12.1) — but ISO 9001:2015 no longer has a dedicated clause for it (unlike ISO 9001:2008 Clause 8.5.3).

The reason: ISO 9001:2015 introduced risk-based thinking (Clause 6.1 — Actions to address risks and opportunities). The philosophy is that all of QMS planning is essentially preventive action — if you identify and address risks before they materialize as nonconformities, you never need a reactive preventive action process.

For pharma teams using ICH Q9 (Quality Risk Management), this is familiar territory. Risk assessments, FMEAs, and process hazard analyses are all proactive measures to prevent potential nonconformities — exactly what ISO 9000 means by "preventive action."

Practical note: Pharma QMS that have a "Preventive Action" SOP for compliance with older standards can retain it — the concept is still valid, just restructured within risk management framework.

Correction vs. Corrective Action — A Critical Distinction 修正與矯正措施的關鍵區別

ISO 9000 makes a distinction that pharma deviations and CAPAs often blur:

  • Correction (3.12.3) — Fix the specific instance. Discard the OOS batch. Re-test a sample. Reclean equipment. The nonconformity is addressed, but the cause is not yet resolved.
  • Corrective Action (3.12.2) — Eliminate the root cause so it won't recur. Retrain the operator. Change the SOP. Upgrade the instrument.

A batch record is evidence of both: the OOS result triggers a correction (product quarantine), then a corrective action investigation determines root cause and implements systemic changes. FDA 483 observations most commonly cite failures to distinguish these — organizations correct problems without addressing root causes.

Nonconforming Product Dispositions 不符合品的處置選項

ISO 9000 defines a hierarchy of responses to nonconforming products that pharma dispositions mirror:

  • Rework (3.12.8) — Reprocess to make it conform to original spec. In pharma: refilter, reprocess, re-blend within validated parameters. Requires a disposition decision by QA.
  • Repair (3.12.9) — Make acceptable for intended use but not necessarily conforming. In pharma: limited application (more common in medical devices). A "repaired" drug product still needs conformity to specifications.
  • Concession (3.12.5) — Use as-is despite nonconformity. In pharma: requires regulatory notification if it affects safety/efficacy. Very rare for finished drug product; more common for raw materials within pre-agreed limits.
  • Regrade (3.12.4) — Change grade/class. In pharma: downgrade API from pharmaceutical to research grade.
  • Scrap (3.12.10) — Destroy. In pharma: batch destruction, typically by incineration for controlled substances.

Three Types of Audit in Pharma Context 製藥中的三種稽核類型

ISO 9000 Note 2 to 3.13.1 distinguishes internal (first party), and external (second or third party) audits:

  • First-party (internal) audits — Self-inspections per EU GMP Chapter 9; internal QMS audits per ISO 9001 Clause 9.2. Conducted by the organization on itself. Independence ensured by auditors not auditing their own work.
  • Second-party audits — Customer audits of suppliers. In pharma: pharma company auditing the CDMO; CDMO auditing its raw material suppliers. Basis for Vendor Qualification programs per ICH Q10.
  • Third-party audits — Regulatory inspections (FDA, EMA, MHRA, PMDA) and certification audits (ISO 9001 certification body). In pharma, third-party audits determine commercial viability — a Warning Letter or OAI classification can halt product supply.

Audit Programme vs. Audit Plan — Scope Hierarchy 稽核計畫層次

ISO 9000 uses two distinct terms that pharma quality teams sometimes conflate:

  • Audit programme (3.13.4) — The annual schedule: "In 2026, we will conduct 12 internal audits and 5 supplier audits." This is the strategic-level plan across the year.
  • Audit plan (3.13.6) — The specific agenda for a single audit event: "On 15 April, we will audit Sterile Manufacturing (8:00–12:00), QC Lab (13:00–15:00), and Documentation (15:00–17:00)." This is tactical.
  • Audit scope (3.13.5) — The boundaries of a specific audit: "This audit covers sterile manufacturing suite A, batch records from Q1 2026, and related SOPs."

When preparing for an FDA Pre-Approval Inspection (PAI), you need all three levels: the programme (when), the plan (the day's schedule), and the scope (which process, which product, which time period).

Audit Findings → CAPA Trigger 稽核發現觸發CAPA

ISO 9000 defines audit findings (3.13.9) as the result of evaluating audit evidence against audit criteria. Findings can indicate either conformity or nonconformity. This directly maps to pharma quality system linkage:

  • An internal audit finding of nonconformity → triggers a CAPA (corrective action 3.12.2)
  • An external customer audit finding → may require formal CAPA response within agreed timeframe (typically 30 days for major findings)
  • An FDA 483 observation (audit finding) → requires a written response within 15 business days; significant findings may escalate to Warning Letter
  • An audit finding of opportunity for improvement (ISO Note 2 to 3.13.9) → may trigger a preventive action or quality improvement initiative, not necessarily a CAPA

The audit conclusion (3.13.10) integrates all findings and audit objectives to render an overall judgment — the audit team leader decides whether the QMS is effective overall, regardless of individual findings.
