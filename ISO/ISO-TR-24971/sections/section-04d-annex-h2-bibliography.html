H.2.6 Identification of IVD Sequences of Events Leading to Hazardous Situations
識別導致危險情況的IVD事件序列

H.2.6.1 General Considerations

Identification and analysis of reasonably foreseeable sequences or combinations of events that can lead to a hazardous situation is necessary to estimate the probability that harm would occur. Understanding these activities and events also helps the manufacturer select risk controls and identify opportunities for risk reduction.

The specific sequences to be analysed depend on the particular IVD medical device and its intended use. The manufacturer should consider the knowledge, skills, and abilities of intended users, the use environment, and any events and circumstances that could increase or decrease risk.

H.2.6.2 Description of the Sequence of Events

Risk scenarios for IVD medical devices typically involve longer sequences of events than for other medical devices. They include activities not under the direct control of the manufacturer, such as those performed by medical laboratory users and clinical decision-making by clinicians who ordered the examination results.

The description should start with the initiating event (typically the cause of the hazard being analysed) and progress logically through foreseeable decisions and actions of device users, ending with clinical decisions and actions leading to each identified foreseeable outcome.

Three categories of events define the sequence:

  • a) Events under control of the manufacturer — quality management system failures; design and development faults; device labelling errors; manufacturing and supplier management issues; distribution, servicing, or training failures
  • b) Events under control of the user of the IVD medical device — selection, collection, processing and storage of patient samples; system installation and setup; calibration activities and metrological traceability; quality assurance (QC, proficiency testing, delta checks); communication with clinicians; contingency and recovery planning
  • c) Events under control of the clinician — receipt and review of examination result; comparison to expected values; request for confirmatory examinations; clinical diagnosis; therapy initiation or withholding
NOTE: Clinical decision making is specifically excluded from the scope of ISO 14971:2019. This refers to clinical decisions whether to use a particular medical device or IVD medical device — not to clinical decisions affected by incorrect or delayed IVD results.

Three-Layer Event Sequence

關鍵概念：IVD風險不止於儀器故障

一般醫療器械的風險分析可能只需考慮設備本身的故障模式。但IVD的風險序列橫跨三個控制域，製造商只能直接管控第一層。

  • 第一層（製造商控制）：設計缺陷、標籤錯誤、製程不穩定 — 製造商完全責任
  • 第二層（實驗室/使用者控制）：樣品採集、QC操作、結果回報 — 製造商透過標籤與培訓間接影響
  • 第三層（臨床醫師控制）：診斷決策、治療選擇 — 超出ISO 14971管轄範圍

CDMO視角：若你為IVD試劑盒做代工，你的風險分析必須延伸到「錯誤結果傳到醫師手中」的那一刻，即使實驗室操作不在你的直接控制之下。

類比：血糖試片的完整風險鏈

想像你製造家用血糖試片。風險鏈如下：

  1. 試片批次校準值偏高（製造商控制層）
  2. 患者在過期後使用試片（使用者控制層）
  3. 血糖顯示偏高，患者注射額外胰島素（臨床決策層）
  4. 患者陷入低血糖昏迷（傷害 Harm）

在這條鏈中，製造商的風險控制著力點主要在第1步，但也必須透過IFU警告、效期標示與校準訓練延伸影響第2步。

實驗室獨立驗錯機制的重要性

H.2.6.1 特別提醒：醫學實驗室有流程設計用來偵測錯誤，但隨機性故障（sporadic random failures）特別難被偵測。如果一個錯誤結果看起來「合理」，有經驗的臨床醫師也可能接受它並據此做出治療決定。

對CDMO而言：這意味著即使實驗室QC流程存在，也不能作為減少製造端風險的理由。製造品質本身就是第一道防線。

H.2.7 Estimation of the Probability of Occurrence of Harm
傷害發生概率之估算

H.2.7.1 General Considerations

The risk analysis should consider the entire sequence of events. The probability of a patient being harmed is the combined probability of each event in the sequence. For short sequences (e.g., self-testing, point-of-care devices), a straightforward cross-functional team approach using expert judgment is appropriate. For complex sequences with extended chains of events, segmenting the analysis at the hazardous situation makes more efficient use of expert resources.

H.2.7.2 The P1 × P2 Approach

For complex scenarios where a sequence of events extends from an incorrect result beyond the medical laboratory to a clinician's decisions and actions, the approach illustrated in Figure C.1 of ISO 14971:2019 can be divided into two probability segments:

  • P1 = probability that the hazardous situation would occur (i.e., incorrect result reported to the clinician). Estimated by experts familiar with device design, construction, use, and the laboratory environment.
  • P2 = probability that harm would result from that hazardous situation. Estimated by experts familiar with the medical use of IVD results, using clinical judgment, adverse event data, and medical literature.
  • Overall probability of harm = P1 × P2

This approach allows efficient division of a complex analysis between technical experts (P1) and clinical experts (P2), and enables updates when either segment changes independently (e.g., design change affecting P1 only).

Why Split the Probability Estimation?

P1×P2：讓正確的人估算正確的部分

一個IVD錯誤結果到最終造成病患傷害，需要穿越兩個截然不同的知識領域：

  • P1（技術領域）：儀器工程師、試劑化學師、QC人員 — 他們了解設備如何失效
  • P2（臨床領域）：臨床醫師、醫學顧問 — 他們了解錯誤結果在臨床上的後果

讓同一個跨功能團隊評估整條鏈是低效且不準確的。P1×P2的拆分讓每個專家只評估自己有能力判斷的部分。

類比：葡萄糖分析儀案例（圖H.1）

血液葡萄糖分析儀因校準值指定錯誤，導致偏高結果被報告給臨床醫師：

  • P1估算：技術團隊評估校準值偏離的頻率、QC能否截獲、系統警報的有效性
  • 危險情況定義：「錯誤結果被報告給臨床醫師」這一刻
  • P2估算：臨床顧問評估醫師接受偏高血糖結果的概率、是否會調整胰島素劑量
  • 最終傷害概率 = P1 × P2（兩個獨立專家評估的乘積）

Q2（QC攔截能力）是最關鍵的P1問題：若常規QC材料能偵測到批次偏差，P1大幅降低；若故障是間歇性的（如電子元件接觸不良），QC幾乎無法攔截，P1偏高。

Q5（臨床醫師識別能力）是最關鍵的P2問題：若錯誤結果在生理上「合理」（例如糖尿病患者血糖偏高），醫師不會質疑；若結果明顯荒謬（例如健康人血鉀9.0 mmol/L），醫師通常會要求重測，P2降低。

H.3 Risk Control for IVD Medical Devices
IVD醫療器械的風險控制

H.3.1 General

Since the decisions and actions of clinicians are largely beyond any reasonable means of control by the manufacturer, safety activities should focus on reducing the probability of events under the control of the manufacturer. Risk control measures are primarily directed at reducing probability P1.

H.3.2 Inherently Safe Design and Manufacture

Risks are generally reduced by lowering the probability that incorrect results will be reported or that clinically significant delays will occur. Examples of design features controlling accuracy and reliability include:

  • Trueness of calibrator values (e.g., traceability to recognized reference standards; per ISO 17511)
  • Measurement uncertainty (precision of measuring system)
  • Analytical specificity of IVD reagents (optimized components)
  • Detection limit / quantitation limit (improved measurement technology)
  • Reliability of the instrument (minimize hardware/software failures)
  • Discrimination between positive and negative samples (robust cut-off values)
  • Elimination of mistake-prone steps (automation, mistake-proofing)
  • Component version traceability and positive sample identification (bar-coding)
  • Software functionality (coding standards)
  • Reduced reagent or calibrator variability (lot-to-lot specifications)
  • Stability of reagents, calibrators, or control materials (microbiological control)

Examples of detection features in the IVD medical device or reagent kit:

  • Liquid level sensors (detect "short draws")
  • Fault detection systems (spectrophotometer drift, inadequate temperature control)
  • Sample quality checks (haemolysis, icterus, lipemia)
  • Built-in controls to verify correct calibrator or reagent lots (bar codes)
  • Alarms and error messages for fault conditions and recovery procedures
  • Incoming inspections of supplied components; in-process and final-product acceptance testing

H.3.3 Protective Measures Incorporated in the Manufacturing Process

Standards with potential relevance to IVD risk control include:

  • ISO 15197 — self-monitoring blood glucose devices
  • ISO 17593 — oral anticoagulation monitoring (requires validated training program)
  • ISO 17511 — metrological traceability of IVD calibrator values
  • ISO 17822-1 — nucleic acid-based detection systems
  • ISO 20776 — antimicrobial susceptibility testing
  • IEC 61010-2-101 — safety requirements for IVD equipment
  • IEC 80001-1 — risk management for networked medical devices

H.3.4 Information for Safety

Information for safety provided to IVD users can prevent occurrence of a hazard or hazardous situation, provided that: (1) it instructs users what actions to take or avoid; (2) intended users are capable of following the instructions; (3) it can be reasonably expected that they will follow them. Examples include warnings and instructions addressing:

  • Chemical or biological hazards associated with the device
  • Contraindicated medical conditions or clinical applications
  • Sample collection, storage, and preparation
  • Identification of inappropriate sample types
  • Interfering substances detectable by the user (e.g., visible haemolysis)
  • Incompatible system components and accessories
  • Validated measuring intervals and dilution instructions
  • Biological reference intervals and medical decision points
  • Validated cleaning methods for reusable items
  • Backup and recovery in case of system failure

H.3.6 User Education and Training

For complex user interfaces, difficult examination procedures, or critical clinical applications, information for safety can take the form of training and education programs. ISO 17593 (oral anticoagulation monitoring) specifically requires the manufacturer to provide a validated training program for clinicians and an education program for patients using these devices at home.

教學解析：IVD風險控制三層次
Three Tiers of IVD Risk Control

風險控制優先層次（同一般醫療器械，但IVD特色鮮明）

ISO 14971的風險控制層次對IVD完全適用，但每一層的具體措施有IVD的獨特性：

  • 第一層：固有安全設計 — 試劑配方的分析特異性、校準值的計量溯源性、儀器的內建偵錯系統。這些措施直接消除或降低P1。
  • 第二層：保護措施 — 液位感應器、樣品品質偵測（溶血偵測）、條碼核對系統。這些是製造端的工程防護，獨立於用戶行為。
  • 第三層：安全資訊 — IFU中的警告、干擾物質清單、樣品類型限制。只有當前兩層無法完全控制風險時才使用。

注意：H.3.4特別提醒，建議實驗室用戶執行的QC檢測、確認性測試或緊急值通知，屬於「安全資訊」，而非「保護措施」。

CDMO IVD代工的四大製造風險

在CDMO廠房中，IVD試劑盒的製造風險直接對應到H.3.2的設計特性：

  • 試劑穩定性：配方或儲存條件不當 → 批次效期縮短 → 校準值漂移 → 患者結果偏差
  • 分裝精準度：填充量偏差 → 反應物比例異常 → 假陰性/假陽性
  • 容器完整性：密封不良 → 水分滲入 → 試劑降解 → 靈敏度下降
  • 污染控制：交叉污染 → 特異性下降 → 假陽性（尤其核酸試劑）

這四項都直接影響P1，是CDMO的核心品質控制點。

計量溯源性（Metrological Traceability）：IVD準確性的基礎

H.3.2和H.3.5多次強調計量溯源性。ISO 17511定義了建立IVD校準值溯源到高階參考物質的流程。JCTLM（Joint Committee for Traceability in Laboratory Medicine）維護一個受認可的參考測量系統數據庫。

對CDMO：若你代工的IVD試劑使用了客戶指定的校準品，你需要確認該校準品是否已建立符合ISO 17511的溯源鏈。這是風險分析中「trueness of calibrator values」的文件基礎。

安全資訊的侷限：資訊不一定到達臨床醫師

H.3.6末尾指出三個安全資訊的現實限制：

  • 不同地區用戶的能力和使用環境差異極大
  • 品質控制與品質保證的實踐在全球各地並不統一
  • 提供給實驗室用戶的禁忌症和干擾藥物資訊，不一定能到達開具檢驗單的臨床醫師

這意味著「提供安全資訊」的風險控制效力有上限，不能過度依賴，必須優先依靠設計端的固有安全措施。

H.4–H.6 Benefit-Risk Analysis, Residual Risk Disclosure & Post-Production
效益-風險分析、殘餘風險揭露與上市後管理

H.4 Benefit-Risk Analysis

Clause 7.4 provides guidance for performing a benefit-risk analysis for IVD medical devices. Where it is not possible to describe the benefits of an IVD directly, surrogate endpoints can be established. Examples include:

  • Ability to identify a specific disease
  • Provide diagnosis at different stages of a disease
  • Predict future disease onset
  • Identify patients likely to respond to a given therapy

H.5 Disclosure of Residual Risks

Disclosure of residual risks in the accompanying documentation of an IVD medical device can take different forms:

  • Performance specifications ("claims") — defining the analytically and clinically verified boundaries of device performance
  • Limitations of the risk control or examination procedure — situations where the device might not perform as intended (interfering substances, specific patient populations, values outside measuring interval, unvalidated sample types)
  • Potential causes of hazardous situations that could not be eliminated by the manufacturer

Per ISO 18113-1, analytical and clinical limitations must be disclosed in the accompanying documentation. Results of performance evaluations conducted in actual or simulated use conditions can be summarized therein.

Some events considered general laboratory knowledge (e.g., equipment occasional failure, contingency planning requirements) need not be exhaustively listed, to avoid overwhelming users with unnecessary details.

H.6 Production and Post-Production Activities

Establishing an effective post-production monitoring system for IVD medical devices is challenging because reliable data on the actual frequency of hazardous situations and harm can be difficult to obtain. Monitoring use errors and device failures is more straightforward since this feedback can be provided directly by device users.

Recommended sources for post-production surveillance:

  • Internal: Production data from release testing, value assignment activities, stability monitoring, product failure investigations
  • External: External QC and proficiency testing programs, user performance evaluations, instrument installation and servicing activities
  • Adverse event databases: Regulatory authority databases, public institute reports, national medical laboratory associations, medical literature

H.6.2 specifically notes the importance of monitoring analytical performance data. A classification and coding scheme for anticipated harms, hazardous situations, and their causes can facilitate harm-based prioritization of investigations for complaint handling and post-market analysis.

教學解析：效益-風險、揭露與上市後監控
Benefit-Risk, Disclosure & PMS

IVD的「效益」如何量化？

一般治療性器械的效益可以是「降低死亡率X%」或「縮短住院天數Y天」。但IVD的效益通常是間接的——它提供資訊，讓醫師做出更好的決策。

H.4因此引入「替代終點（surrogate endpoints）」概念：

  • 直接效益難以量化時：改用「偵測特定疾病的能力（靈敏度/特異性）」作為代理指標
  • 範例：一個HbA1c測試的效益可以用「正確診斷糖尿病的概率」來表述，而不需要追蹤到「患者5年後的糖尿病控制結果」

這對監管申請中的效益宣稱寫法有直接影響，EU IVDR 2017/746要求明確陳述臨床效益。

殘餘風險揭露 vs. 安全資訊：區別何在？

H.5.1特別強調：殘餘風險的揭露是在安全資訊之外額外進行的。

  • 安全資訊（IFU中的警告）：「避免使用溶血樣品」→ 這是告訴用戶如何操作以降低風險
  • 殘餘風險揭露（規格聲明中的限制）：「血紅素濃度>10 g/L時結果可能偏低20%」→ 這是告訴用戶即使正確操作，仍有哪些性能邊界

對CDMO：在代工產品的技術文件審查中，確認客戶已區分這兩類文件，並確保殘餘風險揭露與風險管理檔案中的殘餘風險評估結果一致。

IVD上市後監控的特殊挑戰

H.6.3揭示一個IVD特有的困境：醫學實驗室通常不知道一個回報的結果是錯誤的，除非後來收到臨床醫師的投訴。也就是說，「沒有投訴」不等於「沒有問題」。

解決策略：

  • 參加外部能力測試（proficiency testing programs）— 可以客觀評估系統性偏差
  • 主動監控類似產品的不良事件資料庫
  • 建立傷害分類與編碼系統，在接收到投訴時快速判斷嚴重性

H.7 Examples of Risk Scenarios for IVD Medical Devices
IVD風險場景案例

H.7.2 Automated Medical Laboratory Analyser: Incorrect Examination Result

Scenario: A clinician orders an IVD examination from the central medical laboratory. The result from the analyser is incorrect and reported to the clinician. Sequence of events:

  1. Initiating event occurs (device fault or use error)
  2. IVD produces a clinically incorrect examination result (hazard)
  3. Device user fails to detect the incorrect result or its cause
  4. Device user reports the incorrect result to the clinician (hazardous situation)
  5. Clinician does not identify the result as incorrect
  6. Incorrect result misleads clinician to a misdiagnosis
  7. Clinician inappropriately intervenes / does not intervene
  8. Patient is injured by the clinician's intervention/non-intervention (harm)

The hazardous situation is defined as the point when the incorrect result is received by the clinician, separating the analytical segment (P1) from the clinical segment (P2). This definition enables efficient parallel analysis by technical and clinical expert teams.

H.7.3 Personal (Self-Testing) Device: Incorrect Classification of Glycaemic Status

Scenario: A patient with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus monitors blood glucose and self-administers anti-glycaemic drug when results indicate hyperglycaemia. Although actually hypoglycaemic, the device gave an elevated result, causing the patient to become further hypoglycaemic. Sequence of events:

  1. Initiating event (device fault or use error)
  2. Personal IVD produces clinically incorrect glucose result (hazard)
  3. Patient does not identify the result as incorrect
  4. Incorrect result misleads patient to inappropriate therapeutic decision (hazardous situation)
  5. Patient administers anti-glycaemic therapy
  6. Patient becomes significantly hypoglycaemic (harm)

In this case, there is no practical advantage to segmenting the sequence of events. The entire analysis can be performed efficiently by a single cross-functional team of technical and medical specialists.

H.7.4 Point-of-Care IVD Device: Critical Result Delayed

Scenario: A patient suspected of internal injuries is treated in an urgent care facility. The IVD device displayed an error message, and the result was unavailable when needed for emergency intervention decision. Sequence of events:

  1. Initiating event (device fault or use error)
  2. IVD fails to produce a clinically necessary examination result (hazard)
  3. Device user cannot repeat examination within required timeframe
  4. Result not available to clinician to support intervention decision (hazardous situation)
  5. Clinician takes critical decision/action without benefit of examination result
  6. Clinician's decision/action is not appropriate for patient's condition
  7. Patient is injured by clinician's action/inaction (harm)

The hazardous situation is defined as the event when the expected result was not received at the time it was needed. The risk analysis can consider analysing the entire sequence as a whole or dividing into segments based on suitability for an objective analysis.

  • 急診POC試劑：除了準確性，你還需要確保可靠性和結果時效性（availability）— 延誤本身就是一種傷害機制

實務應用：CDMO代工IVD時的風險分析檢查清單

  1. 確認客戶已定義預期用途（analyte種類、樣品類型、使用者族群、臨床情境：篩檢/診斷/監測）
  2. 識別三層事件序列中，哪些事件受你的製造品質直接影響（對應P1）
  3. 確認試劑穩定性研究、分裝精準度驗證、容器完整性測試已納入設計輸出
  4. 確認計量溯源性文件（校準品→參考標準→JCTLM数据库）已建立
  5. 確認上市後監控計劃包含外部能力測試（proficiency testing）的定期回顧
  6. 確認殘餘風險揭露文件與風險管理檔案中的殘餘風險評估結果一致

場景比較：為何分析方法不同？

關鍵差異在於臨床決策鏈的複雜度：中央實驗室涉及實驗室-臨床醫師兩層，自我測試只有患者一層，POC介於兩者之間。

CDMO的核心優勢：跨標準整合能力

參考書目中的40個標準反映了一個事實：現代醫療器械的風險管理不是單一標準能夠覆蓋的。對CDMO而言，能夠整合這些標準的能力本身就是競爭優勢。

具體體現在客戶選擇CDMO時的評估問題：

  • 「你們的生產流程是否已整合ISO 14971的風險管理要求？」（ISO 13485 Cl.7.1驗證）
  • 「你們的IVD試劑穩定性研究是否符合ISO 23640？」（書目[40]）
  • 「對於預充填注射系統，你們如何管理針具安全和劑量準確性的風險？」（ISO 11608-1，書目[23]）

從監管送審角度看標準互連

以一個CDMO代工的combination product（如預充填抗體注射器）為例，完整的技術文件需要：

  • ISO 14971 風險管理文件 — 整體架構（必須）
  • ISO 10993-1 生物評估報告 — 接觸材料的生物相容性（必須）
  • ISO 11608-1 測試報告 — 針具系統性能驗證（必須）
  • IEC 62366-1 可用性工程文件 — 自動注射筆的使用錯誤分析（需要）
  • IEC 62304 軟體生命週期文件 — 如含電子劑量追蹤功能（如適用）

每一份文件都是ISO 14971風險管理文件的輸入或輸出，構成一個互相驗證的技術文件包。EU MDR Annex II和FDA PMA/510(k)審查官都會核查這些文件的一致性。

CDMO風險管理成熟度評估問題

  1. 你的FMEA（IEC 60812）輸出是否已連結到ISO 14971的風險評估表格？
  2. 軟體的SFMEA（IEC 62304/IEC 62366-1）是否作為獨立輸入進入整體風險管理文件？
  3. 生物相容性評估（ISO 10993-1）的危害識別是否出現在Annex A風險清單中？
  4. 你的PMS流程（ISO/TR 20416）是否有觸發機制，將新發現的危害反饋到風險管理文件更新中？
  5. 對於IVD產品，校準品的計量溯源性（ISO 17511）是否已在風險分析的「calibrator value assignment」環節中被引用？

ISO/TR 24971書目的邊界提醒

書目中有幾個值得注意的缺口：

  • EU MDR 2017/745 / IVDR 2017/746 沒有直接引用（因為ISO TR是國際標準，不引用特定地區法規）。但在實務上，這兩個法規是在歐洲使用ISO 14971的主要驅動力。
  • FDA 21 CFR 820 (QSR) / 21 CFR 803 (MDR) 同樣缺席。美國的設計管制要求與ISO 13485高度兼容，但仍有細微差異。
  • ICH Q9（品質風險管理） 未引用 — 但對CDMO同時生產藥品和器械的情境，ICH Q9與ISO 14971的對照理解是必要的。

換言之：ISO/TR 24971給你技術框架，但法規合規要求你同時掌握地區性法規。

五個不能忘的IVD風險管理原則

  1. 風險序列橫跨三層：製造商、實驗室、臨床醫師 — 製造商只能直接管控第一層，但必須分析整條鏈。
  2. P1×P2分段估算：讓技術專家估算P1（錯誤產生的概率），讓臨床專家估算P2（錯誤導致傷害的概率），兩者相乘得最終風險。
  3. 危險情況的精確定義決定分析效率：對中央實驗室IVD，「錯誤結果傳到臨床醫師」是分段點；對自我測試，整條鏈由單一團隊分析。
  4. 計量溯源性是IVD準確性的基石：ISO 17511的溯源鏈確保你的校準品值可追溯到國際參考標準，這是降低P1最有效的技術手段之一。
  5. 標準是一個互連生態系統：ISO 14971是中心，ISO 13485是QMS框架，ISO 10993-1是生物安全輸入，ISO 11608-1是注射系統輸入，IEC 62304是軟體輸入 — 它們共同構成一個完整的技術文件包。

給CDMO同仁的行動建議

學完ISO/TR 24971全部Annex H和Bibliography，最重要的行動點是：

  • 建立自己公司的「標準矩陣」：哪些標準適用於你代工的哪類產品？對應哪個法規要求？負責人是誰？
  • 為IVD代工建立專用FMEA模板：明確包含三層事件序列（製造商/實驗室/臨床），並標注P1與P2的分界點。
  • 將ISO 23640穩定性研究與風險管理文件掛鉤：試劑穩定性數據不只是放行測試，它是風險分析中「reagent degradation」危害的降低措施的驗證證據。
