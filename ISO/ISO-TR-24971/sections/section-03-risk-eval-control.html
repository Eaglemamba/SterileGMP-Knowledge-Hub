Section 6 — Risk Evaluation

ISO 14971:2019 describes the process of risk evaluation for risk management. The standard, however, does not specify levels of acceptable risk. The criteria for acceptability are based on the manufacturer's policy for risk management and are documented in the risk management plan.

During risk evaluation, the manufacturer compares the estimated risk with the criteria for acceptability and determines if those criteria are met or not. If the criteria are met, the risk is documented as acceptable and the process continues. If the criteria are not met, the manufacturer must proceed to risk control.

"The criteria for acceptability are based on the policy for risk management and are documented in the risk management plan." — ISO/TR 24971:2020, Clause 6

See Annex C of ISO/TR 24971:2020 for further guidance and examples of applying criteria for acceptability in risk evaluation.

第6節 — 風險評估：核心概念

兩種結果，二選一

風險評估只有兩種結果：

  • 可接受（Acceptable）：記錄結論，繼續下一步。
  • 不可接受（Unacceptable）：必須進入第7節的風險控制流程，不能停在這裡。

關鍵原則：標準必須在看到結果前就先設定

可接受性標準（acceptability criteria）必須在風險評估之前就已寫入風險管理計畫，不能在看到風險估計值之後才「回頭定義」什麼叫可接受。這是法規稽查的常見查核點。

CDMO 實務情境

當供應商通知原料規格變更（material change），你需要重新估計新原料的風險，然後對照風險管理計畫中已定義的標準進行評估。若超出標準，必須啟動風險控制，不能以「歷史上沒問題」為由接受。

Section 7 — Risk Control

7.1.1 Risk Control Option Analysis — Medical Device Design

Several options exist to reduce risks associated with a medical device. These can be used alone or in combination. The order of priority is important, as emphasised in ISO 14971:2019:

a) Inherently Safe Design

Making the design and manufacturing process inherently safe by:

  • Eliminating a particular hazard (e.g., designing rounded edges to eliminate sharp-edge injury risk; using a manually operated pump instead of an electrical pump to eliminate electric shock hazard)
  • Reducing the probability of occurrence of harm (e.g., using cleanroom technologies or sterilization to reduce microbial contamination; identity management to reduce unauthorised data access)
  • Reducing the severity of the harm (e.g., using a low-power, low-speed motor to reduce crush injury severity; using voltage below 42 V to reduce electric shock severity)

b) Protective Measures

Taking protective measures by:

  • Preventing the occurrence of a hazardous situation (e.g., automatic cut-off valves, guards for moving parts, inspection/testing in manufacturing to detect non-conforming products)
  • Preventing a hazardous situation from leading to harm (e.g., visual or acoustic alarms to alert the user)

c) Information for Safety

Providing information for safety by:

  • Placing warnings on the medical device
  • Including contra-indications in the accompanying documentation
  • Providing instructions to support correct use and to avoid use error
  • Providing instructions for use of personal protective equipment
  • Providing training to users on how to use the medical device correctly
  • Providing instructions relating to installation and maintenance during the device lifetime

Options a) to c) are listed in descending order of priority with regard to their generally recognised effectiveness in reducing risk. The manufacturer should take this order into account before deciding on the most appropriate combination of measures.

7.1.2 Risk Control for Manufacturing Processes

Deviations or errors in manufacturing processes can compromise the safety of medical devices, for example by:

  • Introducing hazardous residues or particulates
  • Affecting critical physical or chemical properties (surface coating, tensile strength, resistance to ageing, homogeneity, etc.)
  • Exceeding critical tolerances
  • Insufficient process control, leading to mix-up of gas lines during assembly
  • Impairing the integrity of welding, gluing, or bonding of components

Inherently safe manufacture eliminates the particular hazard from the manufacturing process and ensures the hazard is not present in the medical device. Protective measures in the manufacturing process, such as inspection and/or testing, can detect non-conformities and prevent distribution of affected devices.

Techniques such as FMEA (Annex B.5) and HACCP (Annex B.7) can be useful for analysing critical steps in manufacturing and distribution processes. Risk control must also consider outsourced processes (purchased products, components and services) and other lifecycle phases (storage, distribution, installation, servicing, decommissioning and disposal).

7.1.3 Standards and Risk Control

International standards can generally be considered to represent the state of the art. By applying a standard, the manufacturer can simplify the task of analysing residual risks, but it is emphasised that the standard might not address all risks associated with a specific medical device. The manufacturer can presume that, in the absence of objective evidence to the contrary, meeting the requirements of relevant standards results in risks being reduced to an acceptable level.

7.2 Implementation of Risk Control Measures

ISO 14971:2019 requires implementation of risk control measures, verification of implementation, and verification of the effectiveness of those measures. The risk management plan specifies how these two distinct verification activities will be carried out.

Verification of implementation of risk control measures in the medical device design can be obtained from design documentation. Verification of the effectiveness of the risk control measures can require testing of individual measures or testing the medical device. Testing with users can provide useful information supporting verification of effectiveness — for example usability testing (IEC 62366-1), clinical investigation (ISO 14155) or clinical performance studies (ISO 20916).

Verification of implementation of risk control measures in the manufacturing process can be done by checking the process specifications. Verification of the effectiveness of risk control measures in the manufacturing process can be done by qualification of the manufacturing process, such as validation, inspection method qualification, or other appropriate means.

7.3 Residual Risk Evaluation

Residual risks are evaluated by the same method and with the same criteria for acceptability as the initial risks. Residual risk is either acceptable or unacceptable. Where unacceptable, further risk control options should be investigated. If further risk control is not practicable, a benefit-risk analysis may be performed.

7.4 Benefit-Risk Analysis

7.4.1 General

ISO 14971:2019 allows the manufacturer to perform a benefit-risk analysis for those risks that are not judged acceptable using the risk management criteria established in the plan and for which further risk control is not practicable. The benefit-risk analysis is used to determine if the residual risk is outweighed by the expected benefits of the intended use of the medical device.

Benefit-risk analyses cannot be used to weigh risks against business or economic advantages (i.e., for business decision making only). The decision as to whether risks are outweighed by benefits is essentially a matter of judgment by experienced and knowledgeable individuals, usually a multidisciplinary team comprising medical, clinical and application experts.

7.4.2 Benefit Estimation

The benefits arising from a medical device are related to the likelihood and extent of improvement of health expected from its use. Benefits can be described in terms of positive impact on clinical outcome, the patient's quality of life, outcomes related to diagnosis, or a positive impact on public health.

Benefits can be estimated from knowledge of: the performance expected during clinical use; the clinical outcome expected from that performance; benefits resulting from the use of similar medical devices; and factors relevant to the benefit and to other diagnosis or treatment options.

The following aspects should be taken into account: the type of benefit expected for the patient; the magnitude of the expected benefit; the probability that the patient will experience the expected benefits; and the duration of the expected effects.

7.4.3 Criteria for Benefit-Risk Analysis

Those involved in making benefit-risk judgments have a responsibility to understand and take into account the technical, regulatory, economic and sociological context of their decisions. Since this type of analysis is highly product-specific, further guidance of a general nature is not possible. Requirements specified by standards addressing specific products or risks can be presumed to be consistent with an acceptable level of residual risk, especially where the use of those standards is sanctioned by the prevailing regulatory system.

7.4.4 Benefit-Risk Comparison

A direct comparison of benefit and risk is complicated and should take the following into account:

  • Characterization of the disease or condition of the intended patient
  • The uncertainty of benefit and risk data (initially, a literature search can provide insight into the balance between benefit and risk)
  • Production and post-production information for similar medical devices already on the market
  • The generally acknowledged state of the art
  • A comparison of the benefits of the medical device under development with the benefits of similar devices available on the market
  • A comparison of the residual risks of the device under development with the residual risks of similar devices on the market

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to record the results of a benefit-risk analysis in the risk management file, including the rationale for the conclusion reached.

7.4.5 Examples of Benefit-Risk Analyses

  • Example 1: Burns can occur where the return electrode of a high-frequency surgery device is improperly attached. Although the probability is minimized by conformance to the relevant product standard, the benefit of using the device outweighs the residual risk of burns.
  • Example 2: X-rays are potentially harmful, but the clinical effectiveness of diagnostic imaging almost always justifies its use. Standards exist to minimize radiation exposure; where a new application is developed and existing standards are not applicable, the manufacturer verifies that the benefit-risk results are at least as favourable as for alternative treatments.
  • Example 3: Some cochlear implant components cannot easily be replaced once implanted. The residual risk of failure is weighed against the risk of potential hearing impairment and the expected benefit. The conclusion depends on estimated reliability and the confidence in those reliability estimates.

7.5 Risks Arising from Risk Control Measures

Implementing a risk control measure to reduce one risk can introduce new risks or increase other risks, including those previously evaluated to be acceptable. For example, elimination of a use-related risk in the user interface can restrict the user's flexibility in controlling the device. A software change to control one particular risk can unintentionally undermine another risk control measure embedded in the software architecture. The manufacturer reviews these effects to ensure those risks are still acceptable.

7.6 Completeness of Risk Control

ISO 14971:2019 requires that risks from all identified hazardous situations are considered and that all risk control activities are completed. This can be achieved by maintaining a list of all hazards and hazardous situations and the associated risk management activities. The list can be checked to ensure that risks from all identified hazardous situations have been considered and that no hazardous situations are overlooked. The results of this activity are documented in the risk management file.

第7節 — 風險控制：三層次優先序

三層次優先順序（降序排列）

  • 第一優先：設計本質安全（Inherently Safe Design）
    消除或減少危害本身——從根本解決問題。例：將銳角設計為圓角；改用手動幫浦取代電動幫浦。
  • 第二優先：防護措施（Protective Measures）
    防止危險情境發生，或防止危險情境導致傷害。例：安全閥、自動斷電、IPC 檢測、EM 監控。
  • 第三優先：安全資訊（Information for Safety）
    標籤警語、使用說明書（IFU）、操作培訓。

黃金法則：不能只靠標籤

標籤（labeling）是最後手段，不是第一選擇。若一個風險可以透過設計消除，就不能用貼警語來替代。法規稽查員會特別審查：「你為何沒有在設計層面控制這個風險，而僅依賴標籤？」

CDMO 製造場景：防護措施的典型例子

無菌填充工廠的大多數過程控制（IPC、環境監測、滅菌驗證）屬於防護措施（第二層）。它們降低傷害發生的機率，但並未消除危害本身（微生物仍然存在於環境中）。

若要達到「設計本質安全（第一層）」，則需要徹底重新設計——例如改用隔離器（isolator）技術，將操作人員完全隔離在無菌空間之外。

7.2 實施驗證 vs. 效果驗證

ISO 14971 要求兩種截然不同的驗證：

  • 實施驗證（Verification of Implementation）：這個措施有沒有被正確落實？（查設計文件或製程規格）
  • 效果驗證（Verification of Effectiveness）：這個措施有沒有達到預期的風險降低效果？（可能需要測試、臨床研究或可用性測試）

兩者都必須有文件記錄，缺一不可。

7.3 殘餘風險評估：用同一把尺量

風險控制實施後，剩下的殘餘風險要用與最初風險評估相同的標準和方法再評估一次。這不是重新打分，是再次對照同一把尺——若仍不可接受，必須繼續尋找可行的控制措施，或進行效益-風險分析。

7.4 效益-風險分析：最後的防線

效益-風險分析只有在「進一步風險控制不可行」時才能使用，且不能用來掩護商業決策。

效益評估的四個維度：

  • 效益的類型（救命性 vs. 診斷輔助）
  • 效益的幅度（改善程度有多大）
  • 患者實際獲得效益的機率
  • 效益的持續時間

7.5 警覺：控制措施可能引入新風險

新增一個風險控制措施可能會降低某個風險，但同時引入新的風險，或使原本可接受的風險變得不可接受。每次新增控制措施後，必須重新評估整體風險狀況。這在軟體設計中尤為常見——修一個 bug 可能破壞另一個安全機制。

記憶口訣：ABC 三層控制

  1. A — 設計（Architecture）：從根本消除危害
  2. B — 屏障（Barrier）：防護措施，阻止危害成真
  3. C — 溝通（Communication）：安全資訊，最後防線

先 A 再 B 再 C，不能跳過前面直接到 C。

Section 8 — Evaluation of Overall Residual Risk

8.1 General Considerations

ISO 14971:2019 requires that the overall residual risk be evaluated in relation to the benefits of the intended use of the medical device. Both the criteria for acceptability of the overall residual risk and the method of evaluation must be included in the risk management plan.

The evaluation of overall residual risk is the point where risk is viewed from a broad perspective. All identified hazardous situations have been evaluated and all risks have been reduced to an acceptable level or have been accepted based upon a benefit-risk analysis. Now, the manufacturer considers if the overall residual risks associated with the intended use as a whole satisfy the criteria for acceptability of overall residual risk. This consideration takes into account the contributions of all risks together in relation to the benefits of the medical device. This step is particularly important for complex medical devices and for devices with a large number of individual risks.

The evaluation of overall residual risk is a challenging task that cannot be achieved by simply adding all individual risks numerically. The difficulty arises because:

  • Each probability of occurrence of harm is related to a different hazardous situation with different severity and can be related to different harms
  • Probabilities are often known with different degrees of uncertainty — some from historical data or testing, others from expert judgment, and some (such as probability of a software failure) cannot be estimated precisely
  • It is not possible to combine the severities of individual harms within the broad categories usually employed in risk analysis

Furthermore, the criteria for acceptability of the overall residual risk can be different from the criteria for acceptability of individual risks. Criteria for individual risks usually include limits for the probability of occurrence of harms with a particular severity. Criteria for the overall residual risk are often based on additional elements, such as the benefits of the intended use of the medical device.

There is no preferred way for evaluating the overall residual risk. The manufacturer is responsible for determining an appropriate method. The evaluation must be performed by persons with the knowledge, experience and authority to perform such tasks, and should involve application specialists with knowledge of and clinical experience with the device.

8.2 Inputs and Other Considerations

The evaluation of overall residual risk can take several inputs and considerations into account:

  • a) Event sequences: Different sequences of events can lead to different hazardous situations and risks, each contributing to the overall residual risk. Event Tree Analysis (ETA) can be a suitable method for analysing these sequences.
  • b) Common harm sources: A particular harm can originate from different hazardous situations. In such cases, Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) can be a suitable method for estimating the combined probability of occurrence.
  • c) Conflicting requirements: Risk control measures that are appropriate for independent individual risks could result in conflicting requirements, which can increase the overall residual risk. For example, an instruction to never leave an unconscious patient unattended could conflict with an instruction to stand behind a protective screen during X-ray imaging.
  • d) Warning overload: A warning considered on its own could provide adequate risk reduction for an individual risk. However, too many warnings can confuse the user and can reduce the effect of individual warnings. An analysis might be needed to determine if there is an over-reliance on warnings.
  • e) Instructions inconsistency: A comprehensive review of all operating instructions might reveal that instructions are inconsistent or too difficult to follow, which can also have an impact on overall residual risk.
  • f) Clinical data: Results of design validation, usability studies, clinical evaluations and clinical investigations can provide useful information about the overall residual risk.
  • g) All individual risk analyses should be taken into account.
  • h) Trade-offs: When there have been trade-offs between risks, the impact on the overall residual risk should be analysed with extra care.

8.3 Possible Approaches

The method to evaluate the overall residual risk can include the following approaches:

  • a) Benefit-weighting: The benefits related to the intended use of the medical device are weighed against the overall residual risk. Benefits are described by their magnitude, probability of experience within the intended patient population, and duration/frequency.
  • b) Visual representation: Each individual residual risk can be shown in a chart or matrix (e.g., risk matrices in Figures 3 and 4), giving a graphic view of the distribution of risks. If many risks are in the higher severity or probability regions, the overall residual risk may not be acceptable even if each individual risk has been judged acceptable.
  • c) Comparison with similar devices: The manufacturer can compare the medical device under consideration to similar devices available on the market, taking account of differences in intended use and reviewing up-to-date information on complaints and adverse events of similar devices.
  • d) Expert review: The manufacturer can use experts to support the evaluation. These experts should come from a variety of disciplines — including clinical or application experience and knowledge of similar medical devices — and should have an appropriate level of independence from the design team.
  • e) Further investigation: Even though all individual risks should have been controlled and judged acceptable, it could be appropriate that some are investigated further as a result of the overall evaluation (e.g., if many risks are close to being not acceptable).
  • f) Interdependent risk controls: Risk control measures should be verified for effectiveness not only individually but also in combination with other measures. FTA or ETA can be useful tools to discover relationships between risks and measures.

第8節 — 整體殘餘風險評估

個別可接受 ≠ 整體可接受

這是 Section 8 最重要的核心概念：每一個單獨的殘餘風險都通過了評估，不代表所有風險加總後的整體殘餘風險也是可接受的。

類比：一棟大樓裡每個房間的消防設備都合格，但若走道設計有問題導致整棟樓疏散動線混亂，整體安全仍然不足。

為什麼不能直接數字加總？

  • 每個危害情境對應不同的嚴重度（severity）與發生機率，不在同一基準上。
  • 機率的不確定程度各不相同（軟體失效的機率往往無法精確估計）。
  • 在風險分析常用的寬泛分類下，各傷害的嚴重度無法合併計算。

ISO 10993-1 的銜接

對於涉及材料與生物相容性的風險，ISO 10993-1 的生物學評估報告（Biological Evaluation Report, BER）是整體殘餘風險評估的重要輸入文件。BER 提供材料相關殘餘風險的量化或定性結論，整合進第8節的整體評估。

整體評估的四種方法工具

  • 效益加權法：以整體效益對照整體殘餘風險——最常用的敘述性方法。
  • 視覺化矩陣法：將所有殘餘風險標繪於風險矩陣中，觀察整體分佈。
  • 對標類似器材法：與市場上類似器材的上市後投訴、不良事件資料比較。
  • 專家審查法：跨學科專家團隊（含臨床專家）進行獨立評估。

過多警語的悖論

8.2(d) 提出一個微妙但重要的觀點：若在個別風險控制中大量使用警語，每個警語單獨看都有效，但警語太多反而會讓使用者感到困惑、降低每一條警語的實際效果，進而提高整體殘餘風險。這呼應了 7.6 節的「過度依賴標籤」問題。

實務提醒：記錄評估理由

ISO 14971:2019 要求將整體殘餘風險評估結果記入風險管理文件（risk management file），且強烈建議附上接受理由（rationale）。稽查時，「整體殘餘風險可接受」這個結論必須有具體理由支撐，而不是一句「所有個別風險均已評估為可接受」。

Section 9 — Risk Management Review

ISO 14971:2019 requires that the final results of the risk management process be reviewed to ensure that:

  • The risk management plan has been appropriately executed
  • The overall residual risk is acceptable
  • Appropriate methods are in place to collect and review relevant production and post-production information

The review is performed after implementation of all risk control measures but prior to commercial release of the medical device. The risk management report provides the summary of this review and is included in the risk management file.

There can be a need to revise or update the risk management process if new information becomes available — for example during the production and post-production phases. The manufacturer determines when subsequent reviews of the execution of the risk management plan and updates of the risk management report are performed — for example, after a major change in the design of the medical device.

"The review of the execution of the risk management process plan is not to be confused with the review of the suitability of the risk management process at planned intervals by top management." — ISO/TR 24971:2020, Clause 9

The process plan is related to the risk management life cycle of one type of medical device (or family). The review of the suitability of the process is related to the effectiveness of the risk management process and how this is implemented across the organisation.

第9節 — 風險管理審查

上市前的最後把關

風險管理審查在所有風險控制措施均實施完畢之後、商業放行之前進行。這是一個里程碑式的審查，確認：

  • 風險管理計畫（risk management plan）已被正確執行
  • 整體殘餘風險可接受
  • 上市後資訊收集機制已就位

兩種審查，不要混淆

ISO/TR 24971 特別澄清：

  • 風險管理計畫執行審查（Clause 9）：針對特定器材（或器材家族）的風險管理計畫執行情況進行審查。
  • 管理階層審查（Clause 4.2.3）：由最高管理層定期審查整個風險管理流程是否持續有效——屬於QMS層面的審查。

兩者目的不同，不能相互取代。

觸發式審查：CDMO 變更控制的直接銜接

以下事件必須觸發（trigger）風險管理審查：

  • 設計變更（design change）
  • 製造流程變更（process change）
  • 上市後數據發現新問題（post-market signal）
  • 投訴趨勢（complaint trends）
  • 近似事件（near-miss events）

CDMO 的變更控制（Change Control）記錄應直接連結到風險管理審查的觸發記錄——任何影響產品品質的製造變更都必須評估其對風險管理的影響。

Section 10 — Production and Post-Production Activities

10.1 General

Monitoring of production and post-production information is the critical step that enables manufacturers to close the risk management feedback loop and to make risk management a continuous lifecycle process. During this phase, information is collected from many different sources, reviewed for relevance to safety, and where appropriate, fed back into earlier phases of the lifecycle process to maintain the safety of the medical device.

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to establish a system to actively collect and review information about the medical device that could be related to safety. The activities necessary to establish this system are recorded in the risk management plan (see Clause 4.4.8). The production and post-production activities can be part of a post-market surveillance (PMS) system. See ISO/TR 20416 for more guidance on post-market surveillance.

NOTE: This phase is aligned with the relevant parts of Clauses 7 and 8 of ISO 13485:2016.

10.2 Information Collection

Information relevant to the safety of a medical device can come from a variety of sources. Sources typically include general feedback from users, distributors, service personnel and training personnel. The information can be related to harm that has occurred or to hazardous situations that occurred without harm. Activities also involve reaching out to stakeholders to obtain specific information and insight using methods such as customer surveys, expert user groups (focus groups) and manufacturer-sponsored tracking/implant registries. Publicly available information such as clinical literature, incident reports and adverse event databases is also included.

Post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) studies are carried out following market approval to enhance the clinical evidence for the safety and performance of a medical device when it is used in accordance with its intended use. See ISO 14155 for requirements on clinical investigations and GHTF/SG5/N4:2010 for further guidance on PMCF studies.

The information collected does not necessarily have to directly relate to the manufacturer's medical device. Other devices with similar intended uses, similar principles of operation, or similar hazards can yield useful information about the risks associated with the device.

10.3 Information Review

The collected information is reviewed to determine if it is relevant to safety. The following questions can help in this review:

  • Is the intended use still valid?
  • Are the anticipated hazards still the hazards actually achieved?
  • Is there evidence of hazardous situations or harm not previously identified?
  • Are there occurrences of misuse which were previously not foreseen?
  • Is there an increasing trend of use for applications other than the intended use?
  • Does the frequency of occurrence of a particular hazardous situation or harm suggest that the probability of occurrence of harm was underestimated?
  • Does the reported harm indicate that the severity of harm was underestimated?
  • Is there evidence that the residual risk control measures are not effective?
  • Does the evaluation of the overall residual risk accurately represent the actual market experience?
  • Are there changes in the generally acknowledged state of the art?
  • Are there indications that the criteria for acceptability should be adjusted?

The information review can lead to several possible outcomes:

  • The hazard and hazardous situation were correctly identified. The risk was adequately assessed and remains acceptable. → No further action needed.
  • The hazard and hazardous situation were correctly identified, but the risk has increased and is no longer acceptable. → Further action is required.
  • The hazard or hazardous situation was not identified previously. → Further action is required.
  • The generally acknowledged state of the art or the benefits for the medical device have changed. → Further action is required.

Statistical techniques should be considered to assist in the processing of data, such as trend analysis, predictive reliability engineering techniques (e.g., Weibull analysis), and reliability evaluation. See ISO/TR 10017 for further guidance on the selection and use of statistical techniques.

10.4 Actions

If collected information is reviewed and determined to be relevant to safety, several actions are required by ISO 14971:2019. If a new hazard or hazardous situation is identified that was not previously recognised, the associated risk is assessed and controlled where appropriate, following the steps of ISO 14971:2019 Clauses 5 to 7. Results are recorded in the risk management file.

If a risk has become no longer acceptable, an update of the risk assessment is necessary. The impact of the collected information on previously implemented risk control measures is evaluated to see if those measures are still effective and sufficient. Results of this evaluation should be considered as an input for modification of the risk control process. If appropriate, the steps of ISO 14971:2019 Clauses 5 to 7 are repeated and new/additional risk control measures are implemented. The updated risk assessment and the implemented measures are recorded in the risk management file.

The manufacturer should also consider if actions are needed to address medical devices that are:

  • Already distributed (beyond the manufacturer's control) — correction or removal from market could be necessary
  • Already manufactured but not yet distributed (still under manufacturer's control) — containment and correction could be necessary
  • To be manufactured in the future — modification of the design, manufacturing processes, or servicing instructions could be necessary

For devices on the market, the manufacturer should consider whether urgent information should be communicated to users, patients and other stakeholders as an interim measure (e.g., as an advisory notice as described in ISO 13485:2016 Clause 8.3). The degree of urgency in this communication should be commensurate with the degree of risk, because the speed of these actions contributes to their effectiveness. Decisions and actions taken are recorded in the risk management file.

The results of the information review can indicate that the risk management process is insufficient or inadequate. Therefore, ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to evaluate the impact of the collected information on previously implemented activities, to see which activities should be improved. The results of this evaluation are communicated to top management, who will take these results as input into the planned reviews of the suitability of the risk management process (see Clause 4.2.3).

第10節 — 生產與上市後活動

為何 Section 10 是整個風險管理的閉環關鍵？

ISO 14971 把風險管理定義為產品生命週期全程（life cycle）的活動，而不僅僅是設計階段的一次性工作。Section 10 就是閉環的機制：上市後收集的真實世界數據，必須反饋回到 Clauses 5–7（風險分析、評估、控制），形成持續改善的循環。

上市後監控的主要資訊管道

  • 投訴處理（Complaint Handling）：使用者回饋的問題，按嚴重度、器材家族、客戶類型分類分析。
  • 不良事件報告（Vigilance Reports）：法規層面的強制通報，含類似器材的公開資料。
  • 上市後臨床追蹤（PMCF）：上市後設計的臨床研究，強化安全性與性能的臨床證據。
  • 上市後監控（PMS）系統：主動收集所有相關資訊的系統化框架（參照 ISO/TR 20416）。

CDMO 的 APR/PQR 就是 Section 10 的核心輸入

年度產品品質審查（Annual Product Review / Product Quality Review）是 CDMO 對製造商履行 Section 10 義務最重要的貢獻之一：

  • 製程監控數據（IPC 趨勢、偏差率）
  • 無菌測試結果（sterility test results）
  • 環境監控數據（Environmental Monitoring, EM）
  • 在製品（in-process）測試結果統計
  • 供應商績效評估

這些數據若顯示製程品質下降，必須評估其對風險管理的影響，並觸發必要的更新。

10.3 資訊審查的核心問題

在審查上市後資訊時，最關鍵的三個問題是：

  • 我們對危害的識別是否仍然完整、正確？
  • 我們對風險機率和嚴重度的估計是否仍然準確？
  • 我們的風險控制措施在真實世界中是否仍然有效？

任何一個問題的答案從「是」變成「否」，都需要採取後續行動。

10.4 三類已製造器材的處置策略

一旦確認問題，必須分別處理三種不同狀態的器材：

  • 已在市場流通：考慮召回（recall）、Field Safety Corrective Action（FSCA）、發布安全性通知（advisory notice）。
  • 已製造未上市（在倉庫中）：隔離並評估是否需要返工或銷毀（containment）。
  • 尚未製造：修改設計或製程，防止問題繼續發生。

Section 10 行動決策流程圖

  1. 收集上市後資訊（生產數據、投訴、文獻、PMCF）
  2. 審查資訊是否與安全相關

  6. 將評估結果回報最高管理層，輸入 QMS 管理審查（Clause 4.2.3）

Integrated Summary: Sections 6–10 in the Risk Management Lifecycle

Sections 6 through 10 of ISO/TR 24971:2020 represent the operational core of the risk management process under ISO 14971:2019. Together, they form a continuous improvement loop:

總結：Section 6–10 的系統邏輯

風險管理不是一次性審核，是持續循環

ISO 14971 的設計邏輯是：從設計到上市後的整個生命週期，風險管理必須持續運作。Section 6–10 的連鎖關係如下：

  • Section 6：評估——符合標準嗎？
  • Section 7：不符合就控制——用正確的優先順序。
  • Section 8：控制後再看整體——不能只看個別。
  • Section 9：上市前最後審查——確認所有環節都做了。
  • Section 10：上市後持續監控——閉環回到前面各節。

CDMO 角色定位

作為製造服務提供商，CDMO 的主要貢獻集中在：

  • Section 7.1.2：製造過程的風險控制（IPC、EM、滅菌驗證）
  • Section 9：變更控制觸發審查
  • Section 10：APR/PQR 提供上市後製造數據

器材製造商（MAH/NDA holder）負責整體風險管理決策，CDMO 負責提供製造層面的可靠數據輸入，讓製造商能做出正確判斷。
