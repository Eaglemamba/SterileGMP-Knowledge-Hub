Introduction

This document provides guidance to manufacturers in the development, implementation and maintenance of a risk management process for medical devices that aims to meet the requirements of ISO 14971:2019. It provides guidance on the application of ISO 14971:2019 for a wide variety of medical devices. These include active, non-active, implantable, and non-implantable medical devices, software as a medical device, and in vitro diagnostic medical devices.

The clauses and subclauses in this document have the same structure and numbering as the clauses and subclauses of ISO 14971:2019, to facilitate the use of this guidance in applying the requirements of the standard. Further division into subclauses is applied where considered useful. The informative annexes contain additional guidance on specific aspects of risk management. The guidance consists of the clauses of ISO/TR 24971:2013 and some of the informative annexes of ISO 14971:2007, which are merged, restructured, technically revised, and supplemented with additional guidance.

Annex H was prepared in cooperation with Technical Committee ISO/TC 212, Clinical laboratory testing and in vitro diagnostic test systems.

This document describes approaches that manufacturers can use to develop, implement and maintain a risk management process conforming to ISO 14971:2019. Alternative approaches can also satisfy the requirements of ISO 14971:2019.

When judging the applicability of the guidance in this document, one should consider the nature of the medical device to which it will apply, how and by whom these medical devices are used, and the applicable regulatory requirements.

1 Scope

This document provides guidance on the development, implementation and maintenance of a risk management system for medical devices according to ISO 14971:2019.

The risk management system can be part of a quality management system, for example one that is based on ISO 13485:2016, but this is not required by ISO 14971:2019. Some requirements in ISO 13485:2016 (Clause 8 on product realization and 8.2.1 on feedback during monitoring and measurement) are related to risk management and can be fulfilled by applying ISO 14971:2019. See also the ISO Handbook: Medical devices — Application of risk management to medical devices.

第 1 條：範圍解讀

核心訊息：指引，不是要求

本條明確指出這份文件的用途：提供建立、實施和維護醫療器材風險管理系統的指引（guidance），依據 ISO 14971:2019。

一個重要的澄清：風險管理系統可以整合進 QMS（如 ISO 13485）中，但 ISO 14971:2019 並不要求這樣做。這兩個系統是互補的，不是互相依賴的。

  • 8.2.1（監測與量測中的回饋）— 與上市後監測直接相關

Key Terms from ISO 14971:2019 (applied throughout this TR)

Term 1 harm 傷害

Physical injury or damage to the health of people, or damage to property or the environment.

身體傷害、健康損害，或財產/環境的損壞。

Term 2 hazard 危害

Potential source of harm.

可能導致傷害的潛在來源。

Term 3 hazardous situation 危險情況

Circumstance in which people, property or the environment are exposed to one or more hazards.

人員、財產或環境暴露於一個或多個危害的情境。

Term 4 risk 風險

Combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm.

傷害發生的可能性與傷害嚴重程度的組合。

Term 5 residual risk 剩餘風險

Risk remaining after risk control measures have been taken.

採取風險控制措施後仍存在的風險。

Term 6 risk management file 風險管理文件

Set of records and other documents that are produced by risk management activities.

由風險管理活動所產生的記錄與文件的集合體。

Term 7 risk control 風險控制

Process in which decisions are made and measures implemented by which risks are reduced to, or maintained within, specified levels.

制定決策並實施措施，以將風險降至或維持在規定水準的過程。

Term 8 intended use / intended purpose 預期用途

Use for which a product, process or service is intended according to the specifications, instructions and information provided by the manufacturer.

依照製造商提供的規格、說明和資訊，產品、程序或服務所預期的使用方式。

第 3 條：術語解讀

術語體系：全部繼承自 ISO 14971:2019

本 TR 不重新定義術語，完全採用 ISO 14971:2019 的定義。這是有意為之的設計——確保 TR 的指引與標準在語言上完全一致。

這些術語的重要性不在於死記，而在於理解它們之間的邏輯鏈：

危害（Hazard）→ 危險情況（Hazardous Situation）→ 傷害（Harm）→ 風險（Risk）

風險邏輯鏈的生活類比

以無菌注射器為例：

  • 危害：針頭刺穿能力（本質上存在的物理性質）
  • 危險情況：護理師在移除針頭保護帽時，針頭朝向自己的手
  • 傷害：針刺傷導致感染
  • 風險= 傷害可能性（高？低？）× 傷害嚴重程度（輕微？嚴重？）

風險管理的核心工作就是：識別所有危害 → 分析危險情況 → 估算風險 → 採取控制措施。

「剩餘風險」的重要性

風險不可能被完全消除。「剩餘風險」是在所有控制措施實施後仍然存在的風險。ISO 14971 要求製造商：

  • 評估每個剩餘風險是否可接受
  • 評估整體剩餘風險（所有風險加總後的整體評估）
  • 與預期效益進行效益—風險分析

這個概念在 CDMO 審計中非常重要：客戶的風險管理報告必須明確記錄剩餘風險已被評估為可接受。

術語衍生來源

NOTE 指出這些術語盡量來自 ISO/IEC Guide 63:2019（專為醫療器材領域開發的術語指南）。這確保了整個醫療器材標準體系內術語的一致性——從 ISO 14971、ISO 13485 到 ISO 10993，用的都是同一套語言。

4 General Requirements for Risk Management System

4.1 Risk Management Process

ISO 14971:2019 requires that the top management establishes, implements, documents and maintains an ongoing risk management process throughout the life cycle of the medical device. The required elements in this process and the responsibilities of the manufacturer are given in ISO 14971:2019 and explained in further detail in this document.

4.1 風險管理程序解讀

四個關鍵動詞：建立、實施、記錄、維護

ISO 14971:2019 對風險管理程序的要求濃縮在四個動詞中：

  • 建立（Establishes）：設計並訂立程序架構
  • 實施（Implements）：實際執行，不只是紙面文件
  • 記錄（Documents）：留下可追溯的書面證據
  • 維護（Maintains）：持續更新，跟隨產品生命週期

這四個要求都是高層管理（top management）的責任，無法完全委派下去。

「持續進行」的重要性

使用 ongoing（持續進行）這個詞是刻意的。風險管理不是一次性的設計階段活動——它貫穿醫療器材的整個生命週期，從概念設計到最終除役報廢。

即使產品已上市，也必須持續收集上市後資訊，並定期審查是否影響原有的風險評估結論。

4.2 Management Responsibilities

4.2.1 Top Management Commitment

Top management has the responsibility to establish and maintain an effective risk management process. It is important to note the emphasis on top management in ISO 14971:2019. Top management has the power to assign authorities and responsibilities, to set priorities and to provide resources within the organization. Commitment at the highest level of the organization is essential for the risk management process to be effective.

If the organization consists of separate entities, for example business units or divisions, then top management can refer to those individuals who direct and control the entity implementing the risk management process. Each entity can have its own risk management process (and its own quality management system).

4.2.2 Policy for Establishing Criteria for Risk Acceptability

ISO 14971:2019 requires top management to define and document the policy for establishing criteria for risk acceptability. Annex C provides detailed guidance on how to define such a policy and which elements should be included, such as applicable regulations, relevant international standards, the generally acknowledged state of the art, and known stakeholder concerns. Annex C also explains the relation between the policy and the criteria for risk acceptability and how these criteria are used in risk control and risk evaluation.

The policy can allow specific criteria for each type of medical device (or family). This can depend on the characteristics of the medical device and its intended use (including the intended patient population). ISO 14971:2019 requires that the policy provides guidelines on how to establish the criteria for acceptability of the overall residual risk.

4.2.3 Suitability of the Risk Management Process

ISO 14971:2019 requires top management to review the suitability of the risk management process at planned intervals. The review of the suitability is a high-level review of the risk management process and can include reviewing the following aspects, for example:

  • the effectiveness of the implemented risk management procedures;
  • the adequacy of the criteria for risk acceptability, which can imply the need for an adaptation of the criteria for acceptability for specific medical devices; and
  • the effectiveness of the feedback loop of the production and post-production information (see 10.4).

4.2 管理責任解讀

4.2.1 高層承諾的真正意涵

強調 top management 的用意在於：風險管理不只是工程師或 RA 部門的事。高層擁有三個工程師沒有的權力：

  • 分配授權：決定誰有權做哪些風險決策
  • 設定優先順序：在資源有限時決定先處理哪些風險
  • 提供資源：人力、時間、預算

如果高層不真正承諾，風險管理就會淪為形式上的文件作業。

分組織的情況（CDMO 特別適用）

文件特別提到：如果組織分為多個事業單位（如 CDMO 同時服務多個客戶），每個實體可以有自己的風險管理程序。

這對 CDMO 的實務含義是：你為客戶 A 的產品建立的風險管理程序，可以與客戶 B 的完全獨立。高層管理可以在事業單位層級（而非整個公司層級）承擔這份責任。

4.2.2 風險可接受性政策：設定遊戲規則

在開始任何風險評估之前，高層必須先制定一個政策，說明什麼樣的風險算是「可接受」的。這個政策需要考慮：

  • 適用的法規要求（如 EU MDR, FDA 21 CFR）
  • 相關國際標準
  • 業界公認的技術水準（state of the art）
  • 已知的利害關係人關切（患者、醫護人員等）

關鍵原則：先制定政策，再開始風險評估。不能讓評估結果影響政策的制定——這會形成循環論證。

4.2.3 定期審查程序適用性

這是個常被忽略的要求：高層必須定期審查風險管理程序本身是否仍然適用。這不是審查某個產品的風險，而是審查整個程序架構是否有效。

特別要檢查：上市後回饋機制（feedback loop）是否真的在運作？收集到的資訊有沒有流回到風險評估？這是 4.2.3 的核心問題。

4.3 人員能力解讀

跨功能團隊：風險管理不是一個人的工作

Table 1 列出的人員清單告訴我們：一個完整的風險管理活動需要來自至少 8-10 個不同功能的專業人員。這說明風險管理是一個跨功能的協作活動，而不是 RA 部門獨力完成的文件工作。

CDMO 情境中的人員配置

在 CDMO 的技術移轉或生產支援情境中，你的團隊天然地覆蓋了 Table 1 的幾個關鍵角色：

  • Operations：你的製造工程師了解無菌灌裝流程的危害
  • Supply-chain management：你的採購團隊了解原料來源風險
  • Packaging, storage, handling：你的包裝工程師了解容器密封完整性風險
  • Quality assurance：你的 QA 負責確保風險控制措施的記錄與驗證

當客戶的風險管理計畫中指定了某些製造流程的風險控制措施，CDMO 必須確認自己的 SOP 確實覆蓋了這些措施，並有能力提供「objective evidence」（客觀證據）。

外部顧問的使用

文件允許使用外部顧問，但有一個關鍵要求：必須記錄：（1）所需能力的要求，（2）顧問確實具備這些能力的客觀證據。

這在稽核時常被忽略：公司雇用了顧問，但沒有留存顧問的資歷證明文件。

風險管理專家培訓的 11 個主題

文件還提供了風險管理專家教育訓練的建議主題清單，涵蓋從概念（機率與統計）到實務（驗證控制措施效果）的完整能力要求。這可作為你組織內部風險管理培訓課程設計的參考框架。

4.4 Risk Management Plan

4.4.1 General

The risk management plan describes the scope of the medical device risk management activities, the responsibilities and authorities of those involved, the criteria for risk acceptability, the production and post-production information to be collected and reviewed for the risk management, and all activities that are carried out during the entire product life cycle. The plan can be a separate document, or it can be integrated with other documentation, e.g. quality management system documentation. It can be self-contained or it can reference other documents, such as planning of clinical, biological or usability evaluations or planning of risk management activities.

The plan is a "living document" that will be reviewed and updated throughout the life cycle of the medical device as new information becomes available. The risk management information should be collected on a continuous basis, even after the last medical device is sold and placed on the market. ISO 14971:2019 requires that changes to the plan be recorded in the risk management file.

The extent of planned risk management activities and the level of detail of the plan should be commensurate with the level of risk associated with the medical device. The requirements in ISO 14971:2019 are the minimum requirements for a risk management plan. Manufacturers can include other items such as time-schedule, tools, or a rationale for the choice of specific risk analysis acceptability criteria.

4.4.2 Scope of the Risk Management Plan

The scope identifies and describes the medical device and the life cycle phases for which each element of the plan is applicable.

Some of the elements of the risk management process plan can apply to the product realization phase (design, development and production of the medical device). Other elements can apply to the production and post-production phase (such as installation, use, maintenance, decommissioning and disposal of the medical device).

4.4.3 Assignment of Responsibilities and Authorities

The risk management plan identifies the personnel or functions with responsibility for the execution of specific risk management activities related to risk management (see Table 1). In addition, the risk management plan identifies the individuals with appropriate authority to review and approve safety decisions and actions. This can entail assignment of personnel familiar with the unique characteristics of the medical device (or family) and their possible relevance to the life cycle. This assignment can be included in a resource allocation matrix defined for the specific phase and the activities covered in the scope of the plan.

4.4.4 Requirements for Review of Risk Management Activities

The risk management plan details how and when the risk management activities will be reviewed for a specific medical device (or family). This should include the review method, the responsible individuals or functions, who is required to participate in the review, and how the review results are managed. The results of the review of planned risk management activities will be consolidated in the risk management report (see Clause 9). The requirements for the review of risk management activities can be part of other quality system review requirements, such as design and development review.

4.4.5 Criteria for Risk Acceptability

Criteria for risk acceptability are established according to the manufacturer's policy for determining acceptable risk. This includes criteria for situations where the probability of occurrence of harm cannot be estimated, in which case the criteria for acceptability can be based on the severity of harm alone.

The criteria can be common for categories of similar medical devices (or families).

It is important to establish the criteria for acceptability before starting the risk assessment. Otherwise, the results of the risk assessment could influence the decision when establishing the criteria. See Annex C for further guidance and examples of criteria that are derived from the policy and applied in risk evaluation.

4.4.6 Method to Evaluate Overall Residual Risk and Criteria for Acceptability

The method to evaluate the overall residual risk and the criteria for its acceptability are derived from the manufacturer's policy for establishing criteria for risk acceptability. ISO 14971:2019 requires that the method and the criteria be stated in the risk management plan for the particular medical device under development. Some inputs for and considerations on the evaluation of overall residual risk are listed in Clause 8.

4.4.7 Verification Activities

The risk management plan specifies how the two verification activities required per 7.2 of ISO 14971:2019 are carried out. The risk management plan can detail the verification activities explicitly or by reference to other plans.

Verification of implementation of risk control measures can be part of design review, approval of specifications, design and development verification in a quality management system, or other activities in a quality management system.

Verification of the effectiveness of risk control measures can be part of design and development validation in a quality management system. It can require the collection of clinical data, usability studies, etc., as part of design and development validation in a quality management system.

4.4.8 Activities Related to Collection and Review of Production and Post-Production Information

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to establish a system to actively collect and review safety information about the medical device in the production and post-production phases and to review this information for relevance to risk management. Thus, it is important that the risk management plan includes the activities necessary to establish this system. Manufacturers should understand that the information to be collected can be voluminous and comes from many disparate sources. Consequently, robust processes should be used to analyse the information and to identify trends that could otherwise go undiscovered, so that appropriate conclusions and actions can be taken. Statistical techniques should be considered to assist in the processing of the collected data.

The system to actively collect and review information includes monitoring and receiving feedback such as complaints and adverse event reports. In addition, the system should include active solicitation of feedback from users and collection of other relevant information. The manufacturer should consider the extent of these activities and determine which activities are appropriate for the particular medical device.

For example, limited monitoring might be sufficient for medical devices with a long history of use and well understood risks. For medical devices involving novel treatments (for example new intended uses) or innovative technologies and possibly with less understood risks, more elaborate monitoring including post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) studies could be warranted to understand the issues that can arise in the actual use of the device. Further guidance is provided in Clause 10.

The method for collecting production and post-production information can be part of established quality management system procedures (see for example 8.2 of ISO 13485:2016). While a reference to an existing procedure can be sufficient in some cases, any requirements specific to the risk management under consideration should be documented in the risk management plan. Details of the monitoring activities and any planned PMCF studies should also be specified in the risk management plan.

The frequency of review of the collected information should be commensurate with the severity and can also depend on the number of medical devices on the market, the number of incidents reported and the frequency of reported harm. The collection and review should continue during the expected lifetime of the medical device.

4.4 風險管理計畫解讀

4.4.1 計畫的七大要素

一份完整的風險管理計畫必須涵蓋：

  • 範圍：針對哪個產品的哪些生命週期階段
  • 責任與授權：誰負責哪些風險管理活動
  • 可接受性標準：什麼樣的風險可被接受
  • 上市後資訊計畫：收集哪些資訊、如何審查
  • 所有活動清單：整個生命週期中的每項風險管理工作
  • 驗證活動：如何驗證風險控制措施的實施與有效性
  • 整體剩餘風險評估方法：如何評估加總後的剩餘風險

計畫是一份「活文件」（living document）——必須持續更新，即使最後一台設備已售出。

「活文件」的類比

把風險管理計畫想像成一個飛行任務手冊。飛機在設計時有初版手冊，但每次發現新問題（飛行事故報告、系統改版、新的監管要求），手冊就要更新。手冊不會因為飛機出廠就停止修訂——只要飛機還在飛，手冊就要持續維護。

同理，醫療器材只要還在市場上使用，風險管理計畫就必須持續更新，反映最新的安全資訊。

4.4.2 計畫範圍的生命週期視角

計畫範圍要涵蓋兩個不同階段：

  • 產品實現階段：設計、開發、生產
  • 生產與上市後階段：安裝、使用、維護、除役、廢棄

CDMO 主要影響的是「生產」這一段，但必須了解自己的製造活動對整個生命週期風險的影響。例如：滅菌工藝的變更可能影響除役後的廢棄物處理安全性。

4.4.5 先訂標準，再做評估——關鍵順序

這是最容易犯的錯誤之一：先做風險評估，看到某個風險值「太高」，才回頭訂一個剛好讓它「可接受」的標準。

ISO/TR 24971 明確指出：必須在開始風險評估之前訂立可接受性標準。這樣才能確保標準是基於客觀考量（法規、業界水準、利害關係人），而非受到評估結果的反向影響。

稽核員在審查風險管理文件時，一定會查看計畫的生效日期與風險評估的完成日期——標準應早於評估。

4.4.7 兩種驗證：實施 vs. 有效性

ISO 14971:2019 第 7.2 條要求兩種不同的驗證活動：

  • 實施驗證：確認風險控制措施確實被落實（例如：確認設計規格已包含過壓保護裝置）
  • 有效性驗證：確認控制措施確實有效地降低了風險（例如：測試過壓保護裝置在預期情況下確實動作）

在 CDMO 環境中，「實施驗證」通常對應的是工廠驗收測試（FAT）或工藝驗證；「有效性驗證」則可能需要臨床數據或使用性研究的支持。

4.4.8 上市後資訊系統：規模要與風險相稱

這個條款的核心邏輯：監測強度應與風險高低成正比。

  • 低風險、成熟技術：較簡單的監測可能就足夠
  • 高風險、新型技術：可能需要 PMCF（上市後臨床追蹤）研究

CDMO 特別注意：製造工藝的任何重大變更（如滅菌方法、容器密封系統、配方改變）都可能要求重新評估現有的上市後監測計畫，並通知客戶更新其風險管理文件。

實務檢核清單：審查客戶風險管理計畫時

  1. 計畫是否列明了本次技術移轉涉及的生命週期階段？
  2. CDMO 的製造職責是否已明確列入責任矩陣？
  3. 可接受性標準的訂立日期是否早於風險評估的完成日期？
  4. 計畫中是否包含了容器密封完整性（CCIT）和無菌工藝相關的風險控制措施？
  5. 上市後資訊收集計畫是否說明了 CDMO 需要提供哪些製造偏差資訊？
  6. 整體剩餘風險的評估方法是否已明確說明？

4.5 Risk Management File

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to establish and maintain a risk management file, which contains records and other documents created during risk management activities for the medical device throughout its life cycle from initial conception until final decommissioning and disposal. The individual clauses in ISO 14971:2019 specify what records and related documents are to be maintained as part of the risk management process. The risk management file should provide the information necessary for the review of the risk management file at any phase in the medical device's life cycle.

The risk management file can be structured and organized for one type of medical device or for a family. It is important that the risk management records can be assembled in a timely fashion throughout the life cycle of the medical device, as the information could be used during post-production to support other activities and decision making, for example during review of production and post-production information, evaluation of the effect of a change to the medical device, or during audits.

The risk management file is a logical construct. It is not necessary that the manufacturer's records physically contain all the required records and related documents. The records and related documents can be part of files required by other systems such as the quality management system. The records and related documents can exist in any format or media (hard copy, electronic, etc.).

ISO 14971:2019 requires traceability for each identified hazard to the risk estimation, risk evaluation, risk control, implementation and verification of risk control measures, and the evaluation of residual risk.

Traceability is a requirement to prove that all identified hazards have been completely addressed in the risk management file. A traceability tool can be used to provide an index to each document in the risk management file providing information on the identified hazards. Such an index can be useful in the management of knowledge concerning the identified hazards. This index could be used in later activities such as the evaluation of overall residual risk and the review of production and post-production information. Traceability should be updated as new information becomes available and when the medical device is changed. See Annex G for guidance on building a risk management file for medical devices that were designed without using ISO 14971:2019.

4.5 風險管理文件解讀

Risk Management File：不是一本書，是一個系統

文件明確說明：Risk Management File 是一個邏輯概念（logical construct），不需要把所有記錄都集中在同一個物理位置。這些文件可以：

  • 分散在 QMS 文件、DHF、技術文件等不同系統中
  • 以任何格式存在（紙本、電子、混合）
  • 跨越整個產品生命週期（從初始概念到最終除役）

重要的是：當需要時（如稽核、產品變更評估），這些文件能夠及時被組裝起來。

可追溯性（Traceability）：Risk Management File 的脊梁

ISO 14971:2019 要求對每個識別出的危害（hazard）建立完整的可追溯鏈：

危害識別 → 風險估算 → 風險評估 → 風險控制 → 控制措施驗證 → 剩餘風險評估

這條鏈的每一個環節都必須有記錄，且前後可以互相追溯。如果某個危害無法追溯到最終的剩餘風險評估，稽核員就會認為這個危害「沒有被完全處理」。

可追溯性工具的實務應用

業界通常使用「Risk Traceability Matrix」（風險可追溯矩陣）——一個類似 Excel 的表格，將每個危害 ID 連結到對應的分析、控制和驗證文件。

想像這個矩陣就像供應鏈中的「批次追蹤記錄」：從原料來源到最終產品，每個步驟都有記錄且可以回溯。Risk Management File 的可追溯矩陣就是風險管理版本的批次追蹤。

CDMO 的 Risk Management File 責任

當客戶將產品移轉給 CDMO 生產時，CDMO 成為風險管理系統的一部分。具體而言：

  • CDMO 的製造工藝風險（如灌裝重量偏差、容器密封完整性失敗）應被納入客戶的 Risk Management File
  • CDMO 進行的工藝變更（如更換關鍵耗材、調整滅菌參數）可能觸發客戶風險管理文件的更新要求
  • CDMO 的偏差記錄和 CAPA 可能成為客戶上市後監測系統的輸入來源

在技術移轉協議（Technology Transfer Agreement）中，明確規定哪些製造資訊需要回報給客戶的風險管理系統，是保護雙方的最佳做法。

Risk Management File 快速健康檢查

  1. 是否有一個索引（traceability matrix）連結所有危害識別到最終剩餘風險評估？
  2. 文件是否涵蓋從設計到預期除役的完整生命週期？
  3. 所有風險控制措施是否都有「實施驗證」和「有效性驗證」的記錄？
  4. 是否有書面程序說明如何處理新資訊（如客訴、不良事件）對現有風險評估的影響？
  5. 風險管理報告（Risk Management Report）是否存在並已核准？
  6. 任何產品或工藝變更是否觸發了風險管理文件的相應更新？

5.1 Risk Analysis Process

The risk analysis process consists of the following steps, which are explained in further detail in the next subclauses:

  • description of the intended use of the medical device and identification of the reasonably foreseeable misuse associated with safety;
  • identification of the characteristics of the medical device that are related to safety;
  • identification of hazards and hazardous situations associated with the medical device;
  • risk estimation for each identified hazardous situation.

導師解析：風險分析流程概覽

核心概念：風險分析是 ISO 14971 的骨幹

整個 ISO 14971 風險管理框架的核心邏輯，是先搞清楚「這個器材要做什麼、給誰用、在哪裡用」，再系統性地問「什麼可能出錯」，最後量化「出錯了有多嚴重、機率多高」。

這四個步驟是有先後順序的：你必須先清楚地定義預期用途，才能識別相關危害；必須先識別危害，才能估計風險。跳步驟是法規審查失敗的常見原因。

5.2 Intended Use and Reasonably Foreseeable Misuse

The intended use should take into account information such as:

  • the intended medical indication, e.g. treatment or diagnosis of type 2 diabetes mellitus, cardiovascular disease, bone fracture, infertility;
  • patient population, e.g. age groups (adults, children, adolescent, elderly), gender (male, female), or disease state;
  • part of the body or type of tissue interacted with, e.g. leg or arm;
  • user profile, e.g. patient, lay person, health care provider;
  • use environment, e.g. home, hospital, intensive care unit; and
  • operating principle, e.g. mechanical piston driven syringe, X-ray imaging, MR imaging, subcutaneous drug delivery.

Reasonably foreseeable misuse — use error is defined as use of the medical device in a way not intended by the manufacturer, but which can result from readily predictable human behaviour. This can relate to reasonably foreseeable misuse (slip, lapse or mistake), intentional acts of misuse, and intentional use of the medical device for other (medical) applications than intended by the manufacturer. Cases of reasonably foreseeable misuse can be identified during design and development by an analysis of simulated use, for example by applying a usability engineering process, or during the manufacturer's post-production phase by an analysis of actual use. Reasonably foreseeable misuse can be identified throughout the life cycle of a medical device, including iterations of design activities, during which the ability to anticipate potential misuse progressively increases.

The usability engineering process can help to determine whether a particular misuse is reasonably foreseeable or not, for example by observation during usability testing. The usability test might reveal that users could routinely use the medical device in a manner that is not according to the manufacturer's instructions. This misuse can occur due to poor working culture, inadequate perception, limited knowledge of the consequences, or because operating instructions are not clear.

EXAMPLE: A single-use medical device is designed to be used only once, but it is reasonably foreseeable that some users might attempt to reuse the medical device. Therefore, warnings against reuse and indications of the possible harm resulting from reuse were included in the accompanying documentation. Application of usability engineering according to IEC 62366-1 demonstrated that this information for safety would be effective, i.e. users would know the correct use and understand the risks of reusing the medical device. However, the usability evaluation also showed that some users are likely to disregard this information and intentionally reuse the medical device. Intentional reuse can be considered abnormal use, which is beyond the scope of the usability engineering process, because the risks associated with this misuse cannot be controlled in the user interface (see 3.1 and 3.26 of IEC 62366-1:2015). Since this behaviour can be considered a risk control measure, the risks from such reuse are analysed in the risk management process and evaluated against the criteria for acceptability according to ISO 14971:2019. It could be necessary to implement risk control measures outside the user interface.

導師解析：預期用途與合理可預見的誤用

核心概念：「預期用途」必須被明確文件化

ISO 14971 要求製造商必須書面記錄預期用途，包含六個面向：醫療適應症、病患族群、接觸部位、使用者類型、使用環境、操作原理。

這不是形式主義——每一個面向都會影響後續的危害識別。例如，同樣是注射器：

  • 「由醫護人員操作」vs.「病患自行注射」→ 使用錯誤的類型完全不同
  • 「兒科用途」→ 劑量誤差的嚴重性更高
  • 「居家使用」vs.「ICU 使用」→ 環境條件和監督程度不同

生活類比：剪刀的預期用途與合理誤用

設計一把剪刀的預期用途是剪紙，但必須考慮有人可能用來剪電線（合理誤用）。

「合理可預見的誤用」的關鍵字是「可預見」——不是故意破壞（sabotage），而是日常生活中真實可能發生的使用偏差。一個人剪紙時手指不小心被割到，這是可預見的；有人故意用剪刀攻擊他人，這超出可預見誤用的範疇。

對醫療器材而言：錯誤病人、錯誤劑量、錯誤給藥途徑——這些都是必須分析的合理誤用情境。

CDMO 實務關聯：填充製程對預期用途的影響

CDMO 的核心責任是「不改變客戶產品的預期用途與安全性」。然而，製程變更可能在不知不覺中影響安全特性：

  • 滅菌方式改變（如從濕熱滅菌改為輻射滅菌）→ 可能改變容器材料的浸出物（leachables）特性 → 影響預期用途場景下的安全性
  • 橡膠塞供應商更換 → 新批次的生物相容性數據可能不同 → 需要重新評估風險
  • 充填量調整 → 濃度變化 → 可能超出原始預期用途的安全範圍

任何製程變更都應觸發風險管理的重新審視，確認不影響原始預期用途的安全保證。

法規重點：IEC 62366-1 使用性工程

ISO/TR 24971 引用 IEC 62366-1（使用性工程標準），表示風險分析不能只是紙上作業——必須透過使用性測試（simulated use testing）來驗證「合理可預見的誤用」清單是否完整。

在法規審查中，FDA 和 EU MDR 都會要求提供使用性測試報告，作為風險分析的支持證據。若 CDMO 代工的是組合產品（combination product），這點尤為重要。

5.3 Identification of Characteristics Related to Safety

It is important to identify the characteristics of the medical device that could affect safety. These characteristics can be qualitative or quantitative and can be bound by certain limits. The questions in Annex A cover many aspects of hazards and can assist in identifying the characteristics related to safety. For every question, it is indicated which factors should be considered in further detail, with the ultimate goal of identifying all hazards and hazardous situations associated with the medical device.

The list of questions in Annex A should not be used as a checklist. It can also be helpful to review available information and literature, including adverse event reports, for similar medical devices.

A manufacturer can identify the performance or the functions of the medical device that are necessary to achieve its intended use or that could affect safety, and consider whether any hazardous situations could occur, if any of these functions did not perform properly.

Examples of characteristics related to safety that the questions in Annex A address include:

  • energy outputs (electrical, thermal, radiation, mechanical)
  • substances or materials that come into contact with patients or users
  • sterility and biocompatibility requirements
  • software and electronic control characteristics
  • human factors and ergonomic characteristics
  • environmental conditions of use
  • performance and reliability characteristics

導師解析：安全相關特性識別

核心概念：Annex A 是問題清單，不是勾選清單

ISO/TR 24971 特別強調：Annex A 的問題清單「不應作為勾選清單使用」（should not be used as a checklist）。這是一個重要的思維提醒——答案是「YES/NO」的勾選作業，會讓人停止深入思考。

正確的使用方式是：對每個問題，思考「如果這個特性出現問題，會導致什麼危害？危害在什麼情境下轉變為危險情境？損害的嚴重性和發生機率如何？」

CDMO 製程中的三大安全特性

1. 容器密封完整性（Container-Closure Integrity, CCI）

玻璃瓶、注射器、安瓿的密封性是藥品無菌性保障的核心安全特性。任何密封失效都可能導致微生物污染，危害病患。

2. 浸出物（Leachables from rubber stoppers / closures）

橡膠塞、隔膜、柱塞中的化學物質遷移到藥液中，是典型的化學危害。不同的橡膠配方、不同的藥液 pH 值、不同的儲存溫度都會影響浸出物的種類與濃度。

3. 微粒污染（Particulate contamination）

可見微粒和不可見微粒（次可見微粒）都是安全特性——靜脈注射藥品中的微粒可能導致血管栓塞、肉芽腫炎症等嚴重損害。

思考框架：功能與安全的雙重視角

ISO/TR 24971 建議從兩個方向識別安全特性：

  • 正面識別：哪些功能是達成預期用途所必要的？（如果這些功能失效，會怎樣？）
  • 負面識別：哪些特性可能在正常功能運作時仍產生危害？（如 AED 的電擊本身就是危害源，但也是治療的必要手段）

5.4 Identification of Hazards and Hazardous Situations

5.4.1 Hazards

A hazard is a potential source of harm. Depending on the specific situation, hazards can have different origins/natures. Examples of hazards are electricity, moving parts, infectious bacteria, chemicals, gases, sharp edges, high currents, temperature, and ionising radiation.

Hazards associated with the intended use can be deduced from the reasonably foreseeable misuse and safety characteristics as determined in 5.2 and 5.3.

Annex C of ISO 14971:2019 provides guidance that can help in identifying hazards and sequences of events that can lead to hazardous situations. Annex H provides similar guidance for IVD medical devices, where incorrect diagnostic information can lead to indirect harm to patients.

5.4.2 Hazardous Situations in General

Medical devices only cause harm if a sequence of events occurs that results in a hazardous situation, which then causes or leads to harm. Sequences of events can include a chronological series of causes and effects, as well as combinations of concurrent events. A hazardous situation occurs when people, property or the environment are exposed to one or more hazards.

Hazardous situations can arise even when there are no faults, i.e. in the normal condition for the medical device when it is performing as intended. Hazardous situations can be intrinsic aspects of certain therapies. For example, an automated external defibrillator (AED) delivers an electric shock to the patient as part of its normal operation. Similarly, wound cauterization involves the application of high energy to a wound site, and a scalpel has a sharp blade intended to make incisions.

Annex A provides guidance in the form of questions on the characteristics of a medical device that could affect safety. Those characteristics can help in identifying hazards and hazardous situations. Annex B provides guidance on several techniques that can support a risk analysis. Annex H provides specific guidance on identifying hazards and hazardous situations for IVD medical devices.

5.4.3 Hazardous Situations Resulting from Faults

In cases where a hazardous situation only occurs due to a fault, the probability of a fault occurring is not the same as the probability of the occurrence of harm. A fault can initiate a sequence of events but does not necessarily result in a hazardous situation. A hazardous situation does not always result in harm.

It is important to understand that there are generally two types of fault that can lead to a hazardous situation: random faults and systematic faults.

5.4.4 Hazardous Situations Resulting from Random Faults

Random faults are typically due to physical or chemical causes such as corrosion, contamination, thermal stress, and wear-out. For many random faults, a numerical value can be given for the probability that the fault will occur. Some examples of random faults are:

  • the failure of a part such as an integrated circuit in an electronic assembly;
  • the contamination of an IVD reagent leading to incorrect results;
  • the presence of an infectious or toxic substance in or on a medical device.

NOTE: A quantitative estimate can only be applied to biological hazards if sufficient information is known about the hazard and the circumstances affecting the probability of the hazardous situation occurring, for example in the use of sterility assurance levels.

5.4.5 Hazardous Situations Resulting from Systematic Faults

A systematic fault can be caused by an error in any activity. It will systematically give rise to a failure when some particular combination of inputs or environmental conditions arises, but will otherwise remain latent.

Errors leading to systematic faults can occur in any part of the medical device such as hardware and software in electro-mechanical medical devices. Systematic faults in labelling can lead to use errors for any medical device. These systematic faults can be introduced at any time during a medical device's development, manufacture or maintenance. Some examples of systematic faults are:

  • an incorrectly rated fuse fails to prevent a hazardous situation: the fuse rating could have been incorrectly specified during design;
  • a software database does not provide for the condition of full database: if the database is full, it is not clear what the software will do, with possible consequence that the system will simply replace existing data with new data;
  • a fluid, used during the production of a medical device, has a boiling point lower than body temperature: residues of the fluid can, in certain circumstances, be introduced into the blood, possibly leading to an embolism;
  • the antibody in a hepatitis assay does not detect some variants of the virus;
  • inadequately designed environmental control leads to contamination with a toxic substance or an infectious agent;
  • the user's manual is written so that if a maintenance routine is performed according to the instructions, the user could be injured (e.g. by a sharp probe).

The accurate estimation of the probability of occurrence of systematic faults is difficult. This is primarily for the following reasons.

  • The frequency of systematic faults is laborious to measure. Achieving a reasonable level of confidence in the result will not be possible without extensive data on systematic faults or parameters relevant to risk.
  • Consensus does not exist for a method to quantitatively estimate the probability of occurrence of systematic faults.

Because risk estimation is difficult in these circumstances, the manufacturer should not focus on estimating the probability of systematic faults but rather on implementing robust systems to prevent systematic faults which could lead to hazardous situations or harm.

5.4.6 Hazardous Situations Arising from Security Vulnerabilities

Security in this document includes cybersecurity and data and systems security. Security vulnerabilities can lead to loss of data, disclosure of personal health information, unauthorized access to patient harm, etc. Such situations can initiate sequences of events, which can ultimately lead to harm (patient injury or damage to property). For example:

  • loss of confidentiality can lead to the disclosure of personal health information;
  • loss of integrity can lead to incorrectly represented lab results or malfunction of the medical device;
  • loss of availability can prevent the use of critical functionality of a medical device or can stop the use of a medical device altogether.

See Annex F for further guidance on security.

導師解析：危害與危險情境識別

核心概念：三步鏈邏輯 — 危害 → 危險情境 → 損害

ISO 14971 的最關鍵概念之一是這條三步鏈：

  • 危害（Hazard）：潛在的損害來源，本身可能不造成任何問題。例如：橡膠塞中含有某種化學物質
  • 危險情境（Hazardous Situation）：人員、財產或環境暴露於危害的情境。例如：橡膠塞浸出物進入藥液，病患注射後體內達到有害濃度
  • 損害（Harm）：實際發生的損害。例如：病患發生肝毒性

重要認知：危害存在不等於危險情境發生；危險情境發生不等於損害一定出現。這三步之間的「連結機率」是風險估計的核心。

FMEA 與三步鏈的對應關係

對熟悉失效模式與影響分析（FMEA）的工程師而言，這個對應關係很有幫助：

生活類比：廚房刀具的三步鏈

危害：廚房刀具有鋒利的刀刃（本身只是一個潛在傷害源）

危險情境：孩童在無人監督的廚房中拿起刀具（人員暴露於危害）

損害：孩童手指被割傷（實際損害發生）

如果刀具鎖在刀架上（風險控制），危險情境的發生機率降低；如果廚房有大人在場（另一個風險控制），就算孩童拿起刀，也可能及時阻止損害。

隨機故障 vs. 系統性故障：策略完全不同

隨機故障（Random Faults）：由物理/化學原因引起，可用統計數據估計機率。例如：零件的 MTBF（平均故障間隔時間）。對策：可靠性設計、冗餘設計。

系統性故障（Systematic Faults）：由設計錯誤、製造錯誤或維護錯誤引起，在特定條件下必然重現，但難以用數字估計機率。對策：不應著重估計機率，而應著重預防——通過嚴格的設計控制、驗證、確認活動來消除。

ISO/TR 24971 特別強調：系統性故障的機率估計不可靠，因此製造商應聚焦於「預防系統性故障的根本原因」，而非試圖量化機率。

網絡安全危害：現代醫療器材的新風險維度

5.4.6 新增的網絡安全段落，反映 ISO 14971 對現代數位醫療器材的重要更新。CIA 三角（機密性 Confidentiality、完整性 Integrity、可用性 Availability）的任何一項失效，都可能間接導致病患傷害。

CDMO 關聯：具有嵌入式軟體的自動化填充機（如具備 MES/SCADA 連接的設備），其網絡安全漏洞必須納入風險分析——特別是在 EU MDR 及 FDA 指引日益重視網絡安全的背景下。

5.4.7 Sequences or Combinations of Events

The hazardous situation can be the result of a sequence or combinations of independent events. The probability of the hazardous situation occurring is then given by the product of the probabilities of occurrence of the independent events. A sequence of events can have branches leading to different hazardous situations, and different events can lead to the same hazardous situation.

The example below is for an electricity hazard related to an insulated wire inside a medical electrical device. There is a small probability that the insulation material is degraded and becomes damaged by cracks, and that the cracks lead to an exposed wire. The next possible events are that the user connects and turns on the medical device, and that (depending on choices in the user interface) the exposed wire now has line voltage. When the user subsequently opens the protective cover, the hazardous situation occurs, namely that the user is exposed to the line voltage of 220 V.

Figure 1 — Pictorial example (summarised):
Hazard: electricity — line voltage (220 V) of insulated wire beneath a cover

Events:
A. Insulation material damaged by cracks (PA = 0.01)
B. Insulation falls off the wire (PB = 0.10)
C. User connects and turns on the device (PC = 0.10)
D. User removes cover (PD = 0.10)

Hazardous situation: User exposed to line voltage
P1 = PA × PB × PC × PD = 1 × 10−5

Probability that user touches wire and experiences:
— discomfort (P2 = 0.10)
— burn (P2 = 0.01)
— death (P2 = 0.001)

It is emphasized that several scenarios can be relevant, not only those with the highest severity of harm or with the highest probability of occurrence of hazardous situations. Other scenarios can also be relevant. The manufacturer should consider what the best manner is to document the harm, describing one or more sequences of events that can lead to this harm and the different kinds of harm that can occur.

Information about medical devices on the market can be useful in estimating risk. Several approaches are commonly employed to estimate probabilities:

  • use of historical design and development data;
  • prediction of probabilities using analytical or simulation techniques;
  • use of experimental data;
  • reliability estimation;
  • post-production and production information;
  • use of expert judgment (an expert in this context can be a person competent on the basis of appropriate education, training, skills and experience; see ISO 13485).

Each of these approaches has strengths and weaknesses. Complementary approaches should be used to increase confidence in the results. Expert judgment should be supplemented with one or more of the other approaches wherever possible. When the other approaches cannot be used or are not sufficient, it might be necessary to rely solely on expert judgment.

導師解析：事件序列與機率乘積

核心概念：P₁ × P₂ = 損害發生總機率

ISO 14971 把損害的總機率分解為兩部分：

  • P₁：危險情境發生的機率（從初始事件到人員暴露）
  • P₂：暴露於危險情境後，實際發生損害的機率

這個分解的實用價值在於：有時候其中一個機率很容易估計，另一個很難——可以先估計容易的那個，對難以估計的那個保守地設為 1，再判斷最終風險是否可接受。

電線例子的深度解析

這個例子示範了事件鏈的核心邏輯：四個獨立事件必須全部發生，危險情境才出現。

P₁ = 0.01 × 0.10 × 0.10 × 0.10 = 1 × 10⁻⁵（十萬分之一）

這說明了：即使每個單獨事件的機率都不低（1%~10%），透過多重獨立保護層（Defense in Depth），整體危險情境的機率可以降到非常低的水準。

這也是 CDMO 無菌製程中「多重屏障策略」的邏輯基礎——ISO 8、ISO 7、RABS/隔離器、培養基模擬試驗，每一層都是獨立降低危險情境機率的手段。

機率估計的六種方法：各有優缺點

標準建議綜合使用多種方法，不要單獨依賴一種。在新產品開發初期，數據少，必須大量依賴專家判斷；在產品上市後，應以真實的上市後監測數據修正機率估計。

CDMO 在接受新客戶委託時的實務應用：客戶通常會要求 CDMO 對製程進行初步風險評估。若該藥物的劑型已有類似品種的歷史數據（如同類注射劑的微粒不合格率），應優先引用；若為全新劑型（如新型脂質奈米粒），則必須依賴專家判斷加上模擬試驗數據。

5.5 Risk Estimation

5.5.1 General

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to perform risk estimation. Various methods can be used to estimate risk. Those methods should examine, for example:

  • the circumstances in which a hazard is present;
  • the sequence of events leading to a hazardous situation;
  • the probability of a hazardous situation occurring;
  • the probability of a hazardous situation leading to harm;
  • the nature of the harm that could result.

Risk should be expressed in terms that facilitate decision making on acceptability and the need for risk control, for example, using severity and probability scales. In order to analyse risks, their components, i.e. probability and severity, should be analysed separately.

A risk chart such as that shown in Figure 2 shows the distribution of the estimated risks, which can be useful for later decision making. The risks (R₁, R₂, R₃, …) would be plotted on the chart as they are estimated. Risk matrices developed from this figure will be used in examples throughout this clause.

This does not imply that this method has general applicability to all risks; however, it can be useful in many instances. If a risk chart or matrix is used for ranking risks, the particular chart or matrix and the interpretation used should be justified for that application.

5.5.2 Probability

When sufficient data are available to estimate the probability of occurrence of harm with adequate confidence, a quantitative method should be used. Otherwise, a qualitative method based on expert judgment is preferable to a quantitative estimate with high uncertainty. An example of this situation is a new medical device where suitable quantitative data are not available until design validation or later when data become available. For a qualitative method, the manufacturer can describe a series of probability levels with descriptors appropriate for the medical device.

Although probability is a continuous variable, a number of discrete levels can be used in practice to simplify the analysis. The manufacturer decides how many probability levels are appropriate, based on the expected confidence in the estimates. A larger number of probability levels can be used when estimates are made with greater confidence. At least three levels should be identified to facilitate decision making. The levels can be descriptive and qualitative (e.g. not expected to occur during the lifetime of the medical device, likely to occur a few times, likely to occur frequently, etc.) or quantitative. Manufacturers should define the levels explicitly, so that there will be no confusion over what falls within each level. A particularly effective way is to assign ranges of non-overlapping numerical values to the discrete levels.

The definitions of the probability ranges can be the same or different for different product families. Scales for probability can include probability of occurrence per use, per device, per hour of use, or within a population, etc.

It is required to document the chosen probability levels or ranges and their descriptors in the risk management file for the particular medical device.

There are several factors that are important for estimating the probability of occurrence of harm. These include, but are not limited to, the following.

  • How often is a particular medical device used?
  • What is the lifetime of the medical device?
  • Who makes up the user and patient populations?
  • What is the number of users/patients?
  • How long and under what circumstances is the user/patient exposed?

Probability estimation encompasses the circumstances and the sequence of events from the occurrence of the initiating event through to the occurrence of the hazardous situation. The probability of occurrence of harm can be decomposed into a probability P₁ that a hazardous situation occurs (i.e. that persons are exposed to the hazard) and a probability P₂ that the hazardous situation leads to harm. See Figure C.1 in ISO 14971:2019. A decomposition into P₁ and P₂ can be useful to estimate the probability of occurrence of harm, but such decomposition is not mandatory.

When the probability of occurrence of harm is decomposed into P₁ and P₂, it could be the case that one of them can be estimated and the other not. In such cases, a conservative approach can be used by setting the unknown probability equal to 1. Such an approach can be useful when the estimated probability is either so low that the resulting risk becomes clearly insignificant or negligible, or so high that it is clear the resulting risk should be reduced.

5.5.3 Risks for Which Probability Cannot Be Estimated

Confidence in a risk estimate can be enhanced when a quantitative estimate of the probability of occurrence of harm is made on the basis of accurate and reliable data, or when a reasonable qualitative estimate is based on a consensus by qualified experts. However, this is not always achievable. For example, the probabilities of systematic faults, such as those discussed in 5.4.5, are difficult to estimate.

When the accuracy of the probability estimate is in doubt, it is often necessary to establish a broad range for the probability or to determine that it is no worse than some particular value.

Examples where probabilities are difficult to estimate include:

  • software failure;
  • exceptional misuse situations, such as sabotage or tampering with a medical device;
  • novel hazards that are poorly understood, e.g. imprecise knowledge of the infectivity of the causative agent of Bovine Spongiform Encephalopathy (BSE) prevents quantification of the risk of transmission;
  • certain toxicological hazards, such as genotoxic carcinogens and sensitizing agents, where it might not be possible to determine a threshold of exposure below which toxic effects do not occur.

When the probability of occurrence of harm cannot be estimated, it is necessary to evaluate the risk on the basis of the severity of harm alone. The risk control measures should focus on preventing the hazardous situation entirely or on preventing that the hazardous situation leads to harm. If this is not possible, the risk control measures should focus on reducing the severity of harm.

An inverse relationship can be presumed between the rigor of the processes used in design, development, manufacturing and maintenance and the probability of some systematic faults being introduced or remaining undetected. The required rigor of these risk control processes can be determined by taking account of the severity of the consequences of systematic faults and the effectiveness of risk control measures external to the medical device. The more severe the consequences are and the less effective the external measures, the more rigorous these processes should be.

5.5.4 Severity

To categorize the severity of the potential harm, the manufacturer should use descriptors appropriate for the medical device. Severity is, in reality, a continuum; however, in practice, the use of a discrete number of levels simplifies the analysis. In such cases, the manufacturer decides how many categories are appropriate and how they are to be defined. The severity levels should be descriptive and should not include any element of probability. See the examples in Table 2 and Table 4.

Severity levels are chosen and justified by the manufacturer based on the harm that could result for a particular medical device. The levels should be defined with sufficient specificity, so that the correct level of severity can be assigned to each harm identified in the risk analysis.

It is required to document the chosen severity levels or ranges and their descriptors in the risk management file for the particular medical device.

導師解析：風險估計

核心概念：風險 = 嚴重性 × 發生機率

風險估計的公式看似簡單：

Risk = Severity of Harm × Probability of Harm

但實際應用時有兩個重要原則：

  • 分開分析：嚴重性和發生機率必須獨立評估，不能混淆——嚴重性描述的是「如果損害發生了，有多嚴重」，不含機率成分
  • 文件化定義：所有的等級定義必須寫進風險管理檔案（Risk Management File），並對每個產品保持一致

CDMO 實務：新項目報價時的初步風險估計

當 CDMO 接受一個新客戶委託時，初步風險估計決定了哪些研究是必要的：

  • 高風險（嚴重性高 × 機率不低）：直接接觸藥液的橡膠塞必須進行完整的 E&L（萃取物與浸出物）研究；靜脈注射製劑需要全套微粒測試
  • 中風險：外包裝材料的化學物質僅間接接觸，可能只需要文獻審查和供應商 CoA
  • 低風險：非接觸材料（如外箱紙板）通常不需要生物相容性測試

這個初步風險估計也是 CDMO 與客戶在技術轉移階段達成共識的基礎，避免後期因安全特性遺漏而需要重新驗證。

保守設定未知機率為 1 的邏輯

想像你不知道某種新型藥物包裝材料的浸出物遷移速率（P₁ 未知），但你知道這種浸出物如果進入藥液、被靜脈注射的病患接受的話，發生嚴重副作用的機率（P₂）大約是 0.1%。

保守做法：設 P₁ = 1（假設遷移一定發生），計算最壞情況下的風險。如果最壞情況下的風險已在可接受範圍內，你就不需要花時間精確測量 P₁；如果最壞情況下的風險不可接受，那你就必須先測量 P₁，或者直接採取風險控制措施降低 P₂。

無法估計機率時的策略轉向

基因毒性致癌物（genotoxic carcinogens）是典型案例：科學界至今無法確定「安全暴露閾值」（threshold），因此無法用機率估計的方式處理風險。

ISO/TR 24971 指出，此時應改變策略：

  1. 第一優先：完全防止危險情境發生（如更換無基因毒性的材料）
  2. 第二優先：防止危險情境導致損害（如確保濃度遠低於 ICH Q3C/M7 的 TTC 限值）
  3. 最後手段：降低損害的嚴重性（如限制病患暴露頻率）

這個邏輯與 CDMO 的 E&L 合規策略高度一致——先選擇符合規範的原材料，再用分析測試確認殘留量在限值以內。

However, matrices with more than five levels can require significantly more data to be able to distinguish between the various levels and to avoid overlap of the levels. Rationales for the selection of matrices and their outcome scores should be documented. Note that matrices with three levels might not always be sufficiently accurate for adequate decision making. While the above examples were 3 × 3 and 5 × 5, there is no need that these matrices be balanced. For example, a 4 × 5 matrix could be appropriate for a given application.

核心概念：風險矩陣的兩個維度必須獨立定義

風險矩陣的橫軸是嚴重性（Severity），縱軸是發生機率（Probability）。兩個維度的等級定義必須：

  • 有明確書面定義：不能讓評估者自行解讀「中等嚴重性」的含義

Section 6 — Risk Evaluation

ISO 14971:2019 describes the process of risk evaluation for risk management. The standard, however, does not specify levels of acceptable risk. The criteria for acceptability are based on the manufacturer's policy for risk management and are documented in the risk management plan.

During risk evaluation, the manufacturer compares the estimated risk with the criteria for acceptability and determines if those criteria are met or not. If the criteria are met, the risk is documented as acceptable and the process continues. If the criteria are not met, the manufacturer must proceed to risk control.

"The criteria for acceptability are based on the policy for risk management and are documented in the risk management plan." — ISO/TR 24971:2020, Clause 6

See Annex C of ISO/TR 24971:2020 for further guidance and examples of applying criteria for acceptability in risk evaluation.

第6節 — 風險評估：核心概念

兩種結果，二選一

風險評估只有兩種結果：

  • 可接受（Acceptable）：記錄結論，繼續下一步。
  • 不可接受（Unacceptable）：必須進入第7節的風險控制流程，不能停在這裡。

關鍵原則：標準必須在看到結果前就先設定

可接受性標準（acceptability criteria）必須在風險評估之前就已寫入風險管理計畫，不能在看到風險估計值之後才「回頭定義」什麼叫可接受。這是法規稽查的常見查核點。

CDMO 實務情境

當供應商通知原料規格變更（material change），你需要重新估計新原料的風險，然後對照風險管理計畫中已定義的標準進行評估。若超出標準，必須啟動風險控制，不能以「歷史上沒問題」為由接受。

Section 7 — Risk Control

7.1.1 Risk Control Option Analysis — Medical Device Design

Several options exist to reduce risks associated with a medical device. These can be used alone or in combination. The order of priority is important, as emphasised in ISO 14971:2019:

a) Inherently Safe Design

Making the design and manufacturing process inherently safe by:

  • Eliminating a particular hazard (e.g., designing rounded edges to eliminate sharp-edge injury risk; using a manually operated pump instead of an electrical pump to eliminate electric shock hazard)
  • Reducing the probability of occurrence of harm (e.g., using cleanroom technologies or sterilization to reduce microbial contamination; identity management to reduce unauthorised data access)
  • Reducing the severity of the harm (e.g., using a low-power, low-speed motor to reduce crush injury severity; using voltage below 42 V to reduce electric shock severity)

b) Protective Measures

Taking protective measures by:

  • Preventing the occurrence of a hazardous situation (e.g., automatic cut-off valves, guards for moving parts, inspection/testing in manufacturing to detect non-conforming products)
  • Preventing a hazardous situation from leading to harm (e.g., visual or acoustic alarms to alert the user)

c) Information for Safety

Providing information for safety by:

  • Placing warnings on the medical device
  • Including contra-indications in the accompanying documentation
  • Providing instructions to support correct use and to avoid use error
  • Providing instructions for use of personal protective equipment
  • Providing training to users on how to use the medical device correctly
  • Providing instructions relating to installation and maintenance during the device lifetime

Options a) to c) are listed in descending order of priority with regard to their generally recognised effectiveness in reducing risk. The manufacturer should take this order into account before deciding on the most appropriate combination of measures.

7.1.2 Risk Control for Manufacturing Processes

Deviations or errors in manufacturing processes can compromise the safety of medical devices, for example by:

  • Introducing hazardous residues or particulates
  • Affecting critical physical or chemical properties (surface coating, tensile strength, resistance to ageing, homogeneity, etc.)
  • Exceeding critical tolerances
  • Insufficient process control, leading to mix-up of gas lines during assembly
  • Impairing the integrity of welding, gluing, or bonding of components

Inherently safe manufacture eliminates the particular hazard from the manufacturing process and ensures the hazard is not present in the medical device. Protective measures in the manufacturing process, such as inspection and/or testing, can detect non-conformities and prevent distribution of affected devices.

Techniques such as FMEA (Annex B.5) and HACCP (Annex B.7) can be useful for analysing critical steps in manufacturing and distribution processes. Risk control must also consider outsourced processes (purchased products, components and services) and other lifecycle phases (storage, distribution, installation, servicing, decommissioning and disposal).

7.1.3 Standards and Risk Control

International standards can generally be considered to represent the state of the art. By applying a standard, the manufacturer can simplify the task of analysing residual risks, but it is emphasised that the standard might not address all risks associated with a specific medical device. The manufacturer can presume that, in the absence of objective evidence to the contrary, meeting the requirements of relevant standards results in risks being reduced to an acceptable level.

7.2 Implementation of Risk Control Measures

ISO 14971:2019 requires implementation of risk control measures, verification of implementation, and verification of the effectiveness of those measures. The risk management plan specifies how these two distinct verification activities will be carried out.

Verification of implementation of risk control measures in the medical device design can be obtained from design documentation. Verification of the effectiveness of the risk control measures can require testing of individual measures or testing the medical device. Testing with users can provide useful information supporting verification of effectiveness — for example usability testing (IEC 62366-1), clinical investigation (ISO 14155) or clinical performance studies (ISO 20916).

Verification of implementation of risk control measures in the manufacturing process can be done by checking the process specifications. Verification of the effectiveness of risk control measures in the manufacturing process can be done by qualification of the manufacturing process, such as validation, inspection method qualification, or other appropriate means.

7.3 Residual Risk Evaluation

Residual risks are evaluated by the same method and with the same criteria for acceptability as the initial risks. Residual risk is either acceptable or unacceptable. Where unacceptable, further risk control options should be investigated. If further risk control is not practicable, a benefit-risk analysis may be performed.

7.4 Benefit-Risk Analysis

7.4.1 General

ISO 14971:2019 allows the manufacturer to perform a benefit-risk analysis for those risks that are not judged acceptable using the risk management criteria established in the plan and for which further risk control is not practicable. The benefit-risk analysis is used to determine if the residual risk is outweighed by the expected benefits of the intended use of the medical device.

Benefit-risk analyses cannot be used to weigh risks against business or economic advantages (i.e., for business decision making only). The decision as to whether risks are outweighed by benefits is essentially a matter of judgment by experienced and knowledgeable individuals, usually a multidisciplinary team comprising medical, clinical and application experts.

7.4.2 Benefit Estimation

The benefits arising from a medical device are related to the likelihood and extent of improvement of health expected from its use. Benefits can be described in terms of positive impact on clinical outcome, the patient's quality of life, outcomes related to diagnosis, or a positive impact on public health.

Benefits can be estimated from knowledge of: the performance expected during clinical use; the clinical outcome expected from that performance; benefits resulting from the use of similar medical devices; and factors relevant to the benefit and to other diagnosis or treatment options.

The following aspects should be taken into account: the type of benefit expected for the patient; the magnitude of the expected benefit; the probability that the patient will experience the expected benefits; and the duration of the expected effects.

7.4.3 Criteria for Benefit-Risk Analysis

Those involved in making benefit-risk judgments have a responsibility to understand and take into account the technical, regulatory, economic and sociological context of their decisions. Since this type of analysis is highly product-specific, further guidance of a general nature is not possible. Requirements specified by standards addressing specific products or risks can be presumed to be consistent with an acceptable level of residual risk, especially where the use of those standards is sanctioned by the prevailing regulatory system.

7.4.4 Benefit-Risk Comparison

A direct comparison of benefit and risk is complicated and should take the following into account:

  • Characterization of the disease or condition of the intended patient
  • The uncertainty of benefit and risk data (initially, a literature search can provide insight into the balance between benefit and risk)
  • Production and post-production information for similar medical devices already on the market
  • The generally acknowledged state of the art
  • A comparison of the benefits of the medical device under development with the benefits of similar devices available on the market
  • A comparison of the residual risks of the device under development with the residual risks of similar devices on the market

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to record the results of a benefit-risk analysis in the risk management file, including the rationale for the conclusion reached.

7.4.5 Examples of Benefit-Risk Analyses

  • Example 1: Burns can occur where the return electrode of a high-frequency surgery device is improperly attached. Although the probability is minimized by conformance to the relevant product standard, the benefit of using the device outweighs the residual risk of burns.
  • Example 2: X-rays are potentially harmful, but the clinical effectiveness of diagnostic imaging almost always justifies its use. Standards exist to minimize radiation exposure; where a new application is developed and existing standards are not applicable, the manufacturer verifies that the benefit-risk results are at least as favourable as for alternative treatments.
  • Example 3: Some cochlear implant components cannot easily be replaced once implanted. The residual risk of failure is weighed against the risk of potential hearing impairment and the expected benefit. The conclusion depends on estimated reliability and the confidence in those reliability estimates.

7.5 Risks Arising from Risk Control Measures

Implementing a risk control measure to reduce one risk can introduce new risks or increase other risks, including those previously evaluated to be acceptable. For example, elimination of a use-related risk in the user interface can restrict the user's flexibility in controlling the device. A software change to control one particular risk can unintentionally undermine another risk control measure embedded in the software architecture. The manufacturer reviews these effects to ensure those risks are still acceptable.

7.6 Completeness of Risk Control

ISO 14971:2019 requires that risks from all identified hazardous situations are considered and that all risk control activities are completed. This can be achieved by maintaining a list of all hazards and hazardous situations and the associated risk management activities. The list can be checked to ensure that risks from all identified hazardous situations have been considered and that no hazardous situations are overlooked. The results of this activity are documented in the risk management file.

第7節 — 風險控制：三層次優先序

三層次優先順序（降序排列）

  • 第一優先：設計本質安全（Inherently Safe Design）
    消除或減少危害本身——從根本解決問題。例：將銳角設計為圓角；改用手動幫浦取代電動幫浦。
  • 第二優先：防護措施（Protective Measures）
    防止危險情境發生，或防止危險情境導致傷害。例：安全閥、自動斷電、IPC 檢測、EM 監控。
  • 第三優先：安全資訊（Information for Safety）
    標籤警語、使用說明書（IFU）、操作培訓。

黃金法則：不能只靠標籤

標籤（labeling）是最後手段，不是第一選擇。若一個風險可以透過設計消除，就不能用貼警語來替代。法規稽查員會特別審查：「你為何沒有在設計層面控制這個風險，而僅依賴標籤？」

CDMO 製造場景：防護措施的典型例子

無菌填充工廠的大多數過程控制（IPC、環境監測、滅菌驗證）屬於防護措施（第二層）。它們降低傷害發生的機率，但並未消除危害本身（微生物仍然存在於環境中）。

若要達到「設計本質安全（第一層）」，則需要徹底重新設計——例如改用隔離器（isolator）技術，將操作人員完全隔離在無菌空間之外。

7.2 實施驗證 vs. 效果驗證

ISO 14971 要求兩種截然不同的驗證：

  • 實施驗證（Verification of Implementation）：這個措施有沒有被正確落實？（查設計文件或製程規格）
  • 效果驗證（Verification of Effectiveness）：這個措施有沒有達到預期的風險降低效果？（可能需要測試、臨床研究或可用性測試）

兩者都必須有文件記錄，缺一不可。

7.3 殘餘風險評估：用同一把尺量

風險控制實施後，剩下的殘餘風險要用與最初風險評估相同的標準和方法再評估一次。這不是重新打分，是再次對照同一把尺——若仍不可接受，必須繼續尋找可行的控制措施，或進行效益-風險分析。

7.4 效益-風險分析：最後的防線

效益-風險分析只有在「進一步風險控制不可行」時才能使用，且不能用來掩護商業決策。

效益評估的四個維度：

  • 效益的類型（救命性 vs. 診斷輔助）
  • 效益的幅度（改善程度有多大）
  • 患者實際獲得效益的機率
  • 效益的持續時間

7.5 警覺：控制措施可能引入新風險

新增一個風險控制措施可能會降低某個風險，但同時引入新的風險，或使原本可接受的風險變得不可接受。每次新增控制措施後，必須重新評估整體風險狀況。這在軟體設計中尤為常見——修一個 bug 可能破壞另一個安全機制。

記憶口訣：ABC 三層控制

  1. A — 設計（Architecture）：從根本消除危害
  2. B — 屏障（Barrier）：防護措施，阻止危害成真
  3. C — 溝通（Communication）：安全資訊，最後防線

先 A 再 B 再 C，不能跳過前面直接到 C。

Section 8 — Evaluation of Overall Residual Risk

8.1 General Considerations

ISO 14971:2019 requires that the overall residual risk be evaluated in relation to the benefits of the intended use of the medical device. Both the criteria for acceptability of the overall residual risk and the method of evaluation must be included in the risk management plan.

The evaluation of overall residual risk is the point where risk is viewed from a broad perspective. All identified hazardous situations have been evaluated and all risks have been reduced to an acceptable level or have been accepted based upon a benefit-risk analysis. Now, the manufacturer considers if the overall residual risks associated with the intended use as a whole satisfy the criteria for acceptability of overall residual risk. This consideration takes into account the contributions of all risks together in relation to the benefits of the medical device. This step is particularly important for complex medical devices and for devices with a large number of individual risks.

The evaluation of overall residual risk is a challenging task that cannot be achieved by simply adding all individual risks numerically. The difficulty arises because:

  • Each probability of occurrence of harm is related to a different hazardous situation with different severity and can be related to different harms
  • Probabilities are often known with different degrees of uncertainty — some from historical data or testing, others from expert judgment, and some (such as probability of a software failure) cannot be estimated precisely
  • It is not possible to combine the severities of individual harms within the broad categories usually employed in risk analysis

Furthermore, the criteria for acceptability of the overall residual risk can be different from the criteria for acceptability of individual risks. Criteria for individual risks usually include limits for the probability of occurrence of harms with a particular severity. Criteria for the overall residual risk are often based on additional elements, such as the benefits of the intended use of the medical device.

There is no preferred way for evaluating the overall residual risk. The manufacturer is responsible for determining an appropriate method. The evaluation must be performed by persons with the knowledge, experience and authority to perform such tasks, and should involve application specialists with knowledge of and clinical experience with the device.

8.2 Inputs and Other Considerations

The evaluation of overall residual risk can take several inputs and considerations into account:

  • a) Event sequences: Different sequences of events can lead to different hazardous situations and risks, each contributing to the overall residual risk. Event Tree Analysis (ETA) can be a suitable method for analysing these sequences.
  • b) Common harm sources: A particular harm can originate from different hazardous situations. In such cases, Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) can be a suitable method for estimating the combined probability of occurrence.
  • c) Conflicting requirements: Risk control measures that are appropriate for independent individual risks could result in conflicting requirements, which can increase the overall residual risk. For example, an instruction to never leave an unconscious patient unattended could conflict with an instruction to stand behind a protective screen during X-ray imaging.
  • d) Warning overload: A warning considered on its own could provide adequate risk reduction for an individual risk. However, too many warnings can confuse the user and can reduce the effect of individual warnings. An analysis might be needed to determine if there is an over-reliance on warnings.
  • e) Instructions inconsistency: A comprehensive review of all operating instructions might reveal that instructions are inconsistent or too difficult to follow, which can also have an impact on overall residual risk.
  • f) Clinical data: Results of design validation, usability studies, clinical evaluations and clinical investigations can provide useful information about the overall residual risk.
  • g) All individual risk analyses should be taken into account.
  • h) Trade-offs: When there have been trade-offs between risks, the impact on the overall residual risk should be analysed with extra care.

8.3 Possible Approaches

The method to evaluate the overall residual risk can include the following approaches:

  • a) Benefit-weighting: The benefits related to the intended use of the medical device are weighed against the overall residual risk. Benefits are described by their magnitude, probability of experience within the intended patient population, and duration/frequency.
  • b) Visual representation: Each individual residual risk can be shown in a chart or matrix (e.g., risk matrices in Figures 3 and 4), giving a graphic view of the distribution of risks. If many risks are in the higher severity or probability regions, the overall residual risk may not be acceptable even if each individual risk has been judged acceptable.
  • c) Comparison with similar devices: The manufacturer can compare the medical device under consideration to similar devices available on the market, taking account of differences in intended use and reviewing up-to-date information on complaints and adverse events of similar devices.
  • d) Expert review: The manufacturer can use experts to support the evaluation. These experts should come from a variety of disciplines — including clinical or application experience and knowledge of similar medical devices — and should have an appropriate level of independence from the design team.
  • e) Further investigation: Even though all individual risks should have been controlled and judged acceptable, it could be appropriate that some are investigated further as a result of the overall evaluation (e.g., if many risks are close to being not acceptable).
  • f) Interdependent risk controls: Risk control measures should be verified for effectiveness not only individually but also in combination with other measures. FTA or ETA can be useful tools to discover relationships between risks and measures.

第8節 — 整體殘餘風險評估

個別可接受 ≠ 整體可接受

這是 Section 8 最重要的核心概念：每一個單獨的殘餘風險都通過了評估，不代表所有風險加總後的整體殘餘風險也是可接受的。

類比：一棟大樓裡每個房間的消防設備都合格，但若走道設計有問題導致整棟樓疏散動線混亂，整體安全仍然不足。

為什麼不能直接數字加總？

  • 每個危害情境對應不同的嚴重度（severity）與發生機率，不在同一基準上。
  • 機率的不確定程度各不相同（軟體失效的機率往往無法精確估計）。
  • 在風險分析常用的寬泛分類下，各傷害的嚴重度無法合併計算。

ISO 10993-1 的銜接

對於涉及材料與生物相容性的風險，ISO 10993-1 的生物學評估報告（Biological Evaluation Report, BER）是整體殘餘風險評估的重要輸入文件。BER 提供材料相關殘餘風險的量化或定性結論，整合進第8節的整體評估。

整體評估的四種方法工具

  • 效益加權法：以整體效益對照整體殘餘風險——最常用的敘述性方法。
  • 視覺化矩陣法：將所有殘餘風險標繪於風險矩陣中，觀察整體分佈。
  • 對標類似器材法：與市場上類似器材的上市後投訴、不良事件資料比較。
  • 專家審查法：跨學科專家團隊（含臨床專家）進行獨立評估。

過多警語的悖論

8.2(d) 提出一個微妙但重要的觀點：若在個別風險控制中大量使用警語，每個警語單獨看都有效，但警語太多反而會讓使用者感到困惑、降低每一條警語的實際效果，進而提高整體殘餘風險。這呼應了 7.6 節的「過度依賴標籤」問題。

實務提醒：記錄評估理由

ISO 14971:2019 要求將整體殘餘風險評估結果記入風險管理文件（risk management file），且強烈建議附上接受理由（rationale）。稽查時，「整體殘餘風險可接受」這個結論必須有具體理由支撐，而不是一句「所有個別風險均已評估為可接受」。

  • The risk management plan has been appropriately executed
  • The overall residual risk is acceptable
  • Appropriate methods are in place to collect and review relevant production and post-production information

The review is performed after implementation of all risk control measures but prior to commercial release of the medical device. The risk management report provides the summary of this review and is included in the risk management file.

There can be a need to revise or update the risk management process if new information becomes available — for example during the production and post-production phases. The manufacturer determines when subsequent reviews of the execution of the risk management plan and updates of the risk management report are performed — for example, after a major change in the design of the medical device.

"The review of the execution of the risk management process plan is not to be confused with the review of the suitability of the risk management process at planned intervals by top management." — ISO/TR 24971:2020, Clause 9

The process plan is related to the risk management life cycle of one type of medical device (or family). The review of the suitability of the process is related to the effectiveness of the risk management process and how this is implemented across the organisation.

上市前的最後把關

風險管理審查在所有風險控制措施均實施完畢之後、商業放行之前進行。這是一個里程碑式的審查，確認：

  • 風險管理計畫（risk management plan）已被正確執行
  • 整體殘餘風險可接受
  • 上市後資訊收集機制已就位

兩種審查，不要混淆

ISO/TR 24971 特別澄清：

  • 風險管理計畫執行審查（Clause 9）：針對特定器材（或器材家族）的風險管理計畫執行情況進行審查。
  • 管理階層審查（Clause 4.2.3）：由最高管理層定期審查整個風險管理流程是否持續有效——屬於QMS層面的審查。

  • 製造流程變更（process change）
  • 上市後數據發現新問題（post-market signal）
  • 投訴趨勢（complaint trends）
  • 近似事件（near-miss events）

Section 10 — Production and Post-Production Activities

10.1 General

Monitoring of production and post-production information is the critical step that enables manufacturers to close the risk management feedback loop and to make risk management a continuous lifecycle process. During this phase, information is collected from many different sources, reviewed for relevance to safety, and where appropriate, fed back into earlier phases of the lifecycle process to maintain the safety of the medical device.

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to establish a system to actively collect and review information about the medical device that could be related to safety. The activities necessary to establish this system are recorded in the risk management plan (see Clause 4.4.8). The production and post-production activities can be part of a post-market surveillance (PMS) system. See ISO/TR 20416 for more guidance on post-market surveillance.

NOTE: This phase is aligned with the relevant parts of Clauses 7 and 8 of ISO 13485:2016.

10.2 Information Collection

Information relevant to the safety of a medical device can come from a variety of sources. Sources typically include general feedback from users, distributors, service personnel and training personnel. The information can be related to harm that has occurred or to hazardous situations that occurred without harm. Activities also involve reaching out to stakeholders to obtain specific information and insight using methods such as customer surveys, expert user groups (focus groups) and manufacturer-sponsored tracking/implant registries. Publicly available information such as clinical literature, incident reports and adverse event databases is also included.

Post-market clinical follow-up (PMCF) studies are carried out following market approval to enhance the clinical evidence for the safety and performance of a medical device when it is used in accordance with its intended use. See ISO 14155 for requirements on clinical investigations and GHTF/SG5/N4:2010 for further guidance on PMCF studies.

The information collected does not necessarily have to directly relate to the manufacturer's medical device. Other devices with similar intended uses, similar principles of operation, or similar hazards can yield useful information about the risks associated with the device.

10.3 Information Review

The collected information is reviewed to determine if it is relevant to safety. The following questions can help in this review:

  • Is the intended use still valid?
  • Are the anticipated hazards still the hazards actually achieved?
  • Is there evidence of hazardous situations or harm not previously identified?
  • Are there occurrences of misuse which were previously not foreseen?
  • Is there an increasing trend of use for applications other than the intended use?
  • Does the frequency of occurrence of a particular hazardous situation or harm suggest that the probability of occurrence of harm was underestimated?
  • Does the reported harm indicate that the severity of harm was underestimated?
  • Is there evidence that the residual risk control measures are not effective?
  • Does the evaluation of the overall residual risk accurately represent the actual market experience?
  • Are there changes in the generally acknowledged state of the art?
  • Are there indications that the criteria for acceptability should be adjusted?

The information review can lead to several possible outcomes:

  • The hazard and hazardous situation were correctly identified. The risk was adequately assessed and remains acceptable. → No further action needed.
  • The hazard and hazardous situation were correctly identified, but the risk has increased and is no longer acceptable. → Further action is required.
  • The hazard or hazardous situation was not identified previously. → Further action is required.
  • The generally acknowledged state of the art or the benefits for the medical device have changed. → Further action is required.

Statistical techniques should be considered to assist in the processing of data, such as trend analysis, predictive reliability engineering techniques (e.g., Weibull analysis), and reliability evaluation. See ISO/TR 10017 for further guidance on the selection and use of statistical techniques.

10.4 Actions

If collected information is reviewed and determined to be relevant to safety, several actions are required by ISO 14971:2019. If a new hazard or hazardous situation is identified that was not previously recognised, the associated risk is assessed and controlled where appropriate, following the steps of ISO 14971:2019 Clauses 5 to 7. Results are recorded in the risk management file.

If a risk has become no longer acceptable, an update of the risk assessment is necessary. The impact of the collected information on previously implemented risk control measures is evaluated to see if those measures are still effective and sufficient. Results of this evaluation should be considered as an input for modification of the risk control process. If appropriate, the steps of ISO 14971:2019 Clauses 5 to 7 are repeated and new/additional risk control measures are implemented. The updated risk assessment and the implemented measures are recorded in the risk management file.

The manufacturer should also consider if actions are needed to address medical devices that are:

  • Already distributed (beyond the manufacturer's control) — correction or removal from market could be necessary
  • Already manufactured but not yet distributed (still under manufacturer's control) — containment and correction could be necessary
  • To be manufactured in the future — modification of the design, manufacturing processes, or servicing instructions could be necessary

For devices on the market, the manufacturer should consider whether urgent information should be communicated to users, patients and other stakeholders as an interim measure (e.g., as an advisory notice as described in ISO 13485:2016 Clause 8.3). The degree of urgency in this communication should be commensurate with the degree of risk, because the speed of these actions contributes to their effectiveness. Decisions and actions taken are recorded in the risk management file.

The results of the information review can indicate that the risk management process is insufficient or inadequate. Therefore, ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to evaluate the impact of the collected information on previously implemented activities, to see which activities should be improved. The results of this evaluation are communicated to top management, who will take these results as input into the planned reviews of the suitability of the risk management process (see Clause 4.2.3).

第10節 — 生產與上市後活動

為何 Section 10 是整個風險管理的閉環關鍵？

ISO 14971 把風險管理定義為產品生命週期全程（life cycle）的活動，而不僅僅是設計階段的一次性工作。Section 10 就是閉環的機制：上市後收集的真實世界數據，必須反饋回到 Clauses 5–7（風險分析、評估、控制），形成持續改善的循環。

上市後監控的主要資訊管道

  • 投訴處理（Complaint Handling）：使用者回饋的問題，按嚴重度、器材家族、客戶類型分類分析。
  • 不良事件報告（Vigilance Reports）：法規層面的強制通報，含類似器材的公開資料。
  • 上市後臨床追蹤（PMCF）：上市後設計的臨床研究，強化安全性與性能的臨床證據。
  • 上市後監控（PMS）系統：主動收集所有相關資訊的系統化框架（參照 ISO/TR 20416）。

CDMO 的 APR/PQR 就是 Section 10 的核心輸入

年度產品品質審查（Annual Product Review / Product Quality Review）是 CDMO 對製造商履行 Section 10 義務最重要的貢獻之一：

  • 製程監控數據（IPC 趨勢、偏差率）
  • 無菌測試結果（sterility test results）
  • 環境監控數據（Environmental Monitoring, EM）
  • 在製品（in-process）測試結果統計
  • 供應商績效評估

這些數據若顯示製程品質下降，必須評估其對風險管理的影響，並觸發必要的更新。

10.3 資訊審查的核心問題

在審查上市後資訊時，最關鍵的三個問題是：

  • 我們對危害的識別是否仍然完整、正確？
  • 我們對風險機率和嚴重度的估計是否仍然準確？
  • 我們的風險控制措施在真實世界中是否仍然有效？

任何一個問題的答案從「是」變成「否」，都需要採取後續行動。

10.4 三類已製造器材的處置策略

一旦確認問題，必須分別處理三種不同狀態的器材：

  • 已在市場流通：考慮召回（recall）、Field Safety Corrective Action（FSCA）、發布安全性通知（advisory notice）。
  • 已製造未上市（在倉庫中）：隔離並評估是否需要返工或銷毀（containment）。
  • 尚未製造：修改設計或製程，防止問題繼續發生。

A.1 General — Purpose of the Checklist

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to identify those characteristics of the medical device that could affect safety. Consideration of the characteristics is seen as an essential step in identifying the hazards associated with the medical device.

One way of doing this is to ask a series of questions concerning the manufacture, intended users, medical device, and ultimate disposal of the medical device. If one asks these questions from the point of view of all the individuals involved (e.g. users, maintenance staff, patients, etc.), a more complete picture can emerge of the hazards that might exist.

The questions in A.2 can assist the reader in identifying all the characteristics of the medical devices that could affect safety. These lists are neither exhaustive nor representative of all medical devices, and the manufacturer is advised to add questions that can have applicability to the particular medical device, and to skip questions that are not relevant.

The manufacturer is also advised to consider each question not only on its own but also in relation to others. The manufacturer may further consult relevant clinical literature, applicable regulations, or the essential principles of safety and performance for medical devices in ISO 16142-1 or for in vitro diagnostic medical devices in ISO 16142-2.

A.2 Questions — The 37-Item Checklist

A.2.1–A.2.10: Intended Use, Design, and Materials

A.2.1 What is the intended use and how is the medical device to be used?

Factors include: the device's role (diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment, alleviation), indications for use, contra-indications, whether it sustains or supports life, and whether performance can be impacted by a security breach or unauthorized access.

A.2.2 Is the medical device intended to be implanted?

Factors include location of implantation, patient population characteristics (age, weight, physical activity), effect of ageing on implant performance, expected lifetime, reversibility of implantation, and whether the implant can be modified while implanted.

A.2.3 Is the medical device intended to be in contact with the patient or other persons?

Factors include the nature of the intended contact (surface contact, invasive contact, implantation) and the utilization period and frequency of contact.

A.2.4 What materials or components are utilized in the medical device or are used with, or are in contact with, the medical device?

Factors include: compatibility with relevant substances; compatibility with tissues or body fluids; whether characteristics relevant to safety are known; and whether the device is manufactured utilizing materials of animal origin (see ISO 10993-1 and the ISO 22442 series).

A.2.5 Is any energy delivered to or extracted from the patient?

Factors include the type of energy transferred, its control, quality, quantity, intensity and duration, and whether energy levels are higher than those currently used for similar devices.

A.2.6 Are substances delivered to or extracted from the patient?

Factors include whether the substance is delivered or extracted, whether it is a single substance or a range of substances, and the maximum and minimum transfer rates and how these are controlled.

A.2.7 Are biological materials processed by the device for subsequent reuse, transfusion or transplantation?

Factors include the type of process and substances processed (e.g. auto-transfusion, dialysis, blood component supply, or cell therapy processing).

A.2.8 Is the device supplied sterile by the user, or are other microbiological controls applicable?

Factors include: whether the device is intended for single use or reuse; packaging and shelf-life issues; limitation on reuse cycles; method of product sterilization; and the impact of other sterilization methods not intended by the manufacturer.

A.2.9 Is the medical device intended to be routinely cleaned and disinfected by the user?

Factors include the types of cleaning or disinfecting agents to be used and limitations on the number of cleaning cycles. Device design can influence the effectiveness of routine cleaning and disinfection, and the effect of cleaning and disinfecting agents on device performance should be considered.

A.2.10 Does the medical device modify the environment?

Factors include: temperature; humidity; atmospheric gas composition; pressure; light.

A.2.11–A.2.20: Measurements, Software, and Connectivity

A.2.11 Are measurements taken?

Factors include the variables measured, the accuracy and precision of measurement results, and whether the measurement apparatus or data can be compromised. The need for calibration and maintenance should also be considered.

A.2.12 Is the medical device intended to be interpretative?

Factors include whether conclusions are presented by the device from input or acquired data, the algorithms used, and confidence limits. Special attention should be given to unintended applications of the data or algorithm, as well as unauthorized manipulation or changes to algorithms and data.

A.2.13 Is the medical device intended for use in conjunction with other medical devices, medicines or other medical technologies?

Factors include identifying any other medical devices, medicines or technologies involved; potential problems associated with interactions (such as the device impacting the performance of other devices); and whether the patient follows the instructions for energy or substances.

A.2.14 Are there unwanted outputs of energy or substances?

Energy-related factors include: noise and vibration, heat, radiation (ionizing, non-ionizing, UV/visible/IR), contact temperatures, leakage currents, electric or magnetic fields. Substance-related factors include substances used in manufacturing, cleaning or testing having unwanted physiological effects if they remain in the product. Other factors include discharge of chemicals, waste products, and body fluids.

A.2.15 Is the medical device susceptible to environmental influences?

Factors include the operational, transport and storage environments: light, temperature, humidity, vibrations, spillage, susceptibility to variations in power and cooling supplies, and electromagnetic interference.

A.2.16 Does the medical device influence the environment?

Factors include: effects on power and cooling supplies; emission of toxic materials; generation of electromagnetic disturbance.

A.2.17 Does the medical device require consumables or accessories?

Factors include specifications for such consumables or accessories and any restrictions placed upon users in their selection.

A.2.18 Is planned maintenance or calibration necessary?

Factors include: whether maintenance or calibration are carried out by the user or specialist; whether special substances or equipment are needed; traceability of calibrator values to a higher order reference; how to determine when maintenance or recalibration is needed; how to verify the calibration is still acceptable.

A.2.19 Does the medical device contain software?

Factors include whether software is intended to be installed, verified, modified or exchanged by the user or by a specialist, and the authenticity of a software update.

A.2.20 Does the medical device allow access to information?

Factors include accessible Ethernet ports, USB ports, serial ports, and removable hard drives.

A.2.21–A.2.37: Lifecycle, Human Factors, and Autonomy

A.2.21 Does the medical device store data critical to patient care?

Factors include the possibility of data being modified or corrupted, unauthorized access to the data, and the consequences for the patient.

A.2.22 Does the medical device have a restricted shelf life?

Factors include whether the device can deteriorate over time, the impact of storage conditions and primary packaging, communication of the expiry date, possibility of use after expiry, and disposal of expired devices.

A.2.23 Are there any delayed or long-term use effects?

Factors include ergonomic and cumulative effects. Examples: pumps for saline that corrode over time, mechanical fatigue, loosening of straps and attachments, vibration effects, mechanical or material degradation.

A.2.24 To what mechanical forces will the medical device be subjected?

Factors include whether the forces to which the device will be subjected are under the control of the user or controlled by interaction with other persons.

A.2.25 What determines the lifetime of the medical device?

Factors include battery depletion, deterioration of materials, and failure of components due to ageing, wear, fatigue or repeated use. The availability of spare parts should also be considered.

A.2.26 Is the medical device intended for single use?

Factors include: whether the device self-destructs after use; whether it is obvious to the user that the device has been used.

A.2.27 Is safe decommissioning or disposal necessary?

Factors include waste products generated during disposal, proper sanitization (removal) of all sensitive data, and whether the device contains hazardous material (e.g. toxic chemical or biological agent) or recyclable material.

A.2.28 Does installation or use of the medical device require special training or special skills?

Factors include the complexity and novelty of the device and the knowledge, skills and ability of persons installing, maintaining or using it. This can include training, education, competency assessment, certification or qualification.

A.2.29 How will safety information be provided?

Factors include: whether information will be provided directly to the end user by the manufacturer or through third parties (installers, care providers, health care professionals, laboratory directors, pharmacists); whether commissioning and transfer to the end user can be carried out by people without the necessary skills; and whether re-training or re-certification is appropriate based on device type and expected lifetime.

A.2.30 Are new manufacturing processes established or introduced?

Factors include the application of new or innovative technology and changes in the scale of production. This can also involve changes in contract manufacturing, suppliers and vendors.

A.2.31 Is successful application dependent on the usability of the user interface?

This question includes seven sub-questions (A.2.31.1–A.2.31.9) covering: contribution of user interface design features to use error; distractions in the use environment; connecting parts or accessories; control interface design; display of information; menu-controlled devices; dependency on user knowledge and skills; use by persons with specific needs; and whether the interface can be used to initiate unauthorised actions.

A.2.32 Does the medical device include an alarm system?

Factors include the risk of false alarms, missing alarms, disconnected or unreliable remote alarm systems, and the user's ability to understand how the alarm system works (see IEC 60601-1-8 for guidance on alarm systems).

A.2.33 In what ways might the medical device be misused?

Factors include: incorrect use of connectors; disabling features or alarms; neglect of recommended maintenance; unauthorized access to the device or to the manufacturer's medical device functions.

A.2.34 Is the medical device intended to be mobile or portable?

Factors include the need for grips, handles, wheels or brakes, and the need for mechanical stability and durability.

A.2.35 Does the use of the medical device depend on essential performance?

Factors include the characteristics of output of life supporting medical devices or the operation of an alarm (see IEC 60601-1 for a discussion of essential performance of medical electrical equipment).

A.2.36 Does the medical device have a degree of autonomy?

Factors include: awareness of the user when the device with autonomy generates an error, alarm or failure; awareness when intervention in an autonomously performed action is required; the ability of the user to intervene in or abort an autonomous action; the ability of the user to select and perform proper corrective actions (see IEC TR 60601-4-1 for further guidance).

A.2.37 Does the medical device produce an output used as input in determining clinical action?

Factors include whether incorrect or delayed outputs from IVD medical devices can result in direct or indirect risks to patients, e.g. an incorrect diagnosis resulting in delayed or omitted therapy (see Annex H for guidance on IVD risks).

B.1 General — Overview of Techniques

This annex provides guidance on several techniques that can be used to support a risk analysis. Some techniques start with the possible harm and analyse the variety of events that can cause that harm. Other techniques start with an initiating event and analyse the subsequent sequence or combinations of events that could lead to harm.

It is emphasized that risk analysis is only one step of the risk management process described in ISO 14971:2019. Further, the techniques described in this annex do not address all elements of a hazardous situation, and only provide supporting information. For example, the identification of a risk analysis is not included in all of these techniques. These techniques are complementary, and it can be necessary to use more than one of them in order to support a thorough and complete risk analysis.

The following analysis techniques are discussed:

  • Preliminary Hazard Analysis (PHA) — used early in development when few design details are known
  • Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) and Event Tree Analysis (ETA) — especially useful in safety engineering and early development for hazardous situation identification and risk control prioritization
  • Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) — more appropriate as design matures and failure modes are better understood
  • Hazard and Operability Study (HAZOP) — used early in development to study deviations from intended performance
  • Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Point (HACCP) — used later in development to verify and optimize design concepts or changes

B.2 Preliminary Hazard Analysis (PHA)

PHA is an inductive method of analysis with the objective of identifying the hazards, hazardous situations, and events that can cause harm for a given activity, facility or system. It is most commonly carried out early in the development of a project when there is little information on design details or operating procedures and can often be a precursor to further studies.

In a Preliminary Hazard Analysis, a list of hazards and hazardous situations is formulated by considering characteristics such as:

  • Materials used or produced and their reactivity
  • Equipment used
  • Use environment
  • Layout
  • Interfaces among system components

The method is completed with:

  1. The identification of the probabilities that a hazardous situation occurs and the probabilities that a hazardous situation leads to harm
  2. The qualitative evaluation of the extent of possible harm
  3. The identification of possible risk control measures

The results obtained can be presented in different ways such as tables and trees.

PHA（初步危害分析）在CDMO的應用

PHA：新產品引進時的第一道篩網

當CDMO承接新的客戶產品時，在正式開始FMEA或工藝開發之前，PHA提供了一個快速的初步危害掃描框架。

應用場景：新型mRNA疫苗充填製程的初步危害分析

  • 材料：脂質奈米顆粒（LNP）在特定pH下的不穩定性
  • 設備：低溫充填設備（-20°C）的結冰危害
  • 環境：充填速度與剪切力對mRNA完整性的影響
  • 介面：充填系統與CIP/SIP系統的相容性危害

PHA vs. FMEA：時機差異

PHA適合在「設計規格尚未定案」時使用，而FMEA需要已知的組件設計和失效模式。在實際工作流程中：PHA → 危害識別 → FMEA → 失效模式評估 → HACCP → 製程控制點確認。

B.3 Fault Tree Analysis (FTA) — 故障樹分析

FTA is primarily a means of analysing identified hazards and starts from a postulated undesired consequence, i.e. a possible harm or hazardous situation, also called a "top event."

In a deductive manner, starting with the top event and asking "Why?", the possible causes or fault modes of the next lower functional system level causing the undesired consequence are identified. Following stepwise identification of undesirable system operation to successively lower system levels will lead to the desired system level, which is usually either the component fault mode or the lowest level at which risk control measures can be applied.

This will reveal the combinations most likely to lead to the postulated consequence. The results are represented pictorially in the form of a tree of fault modes. At each level in the tree, combinations of fault modes are described with logical operators (AND, OR, etc.). The fault modes identified in the tree can be events associated with hardware faults, human errors, or any other pertinent event. They are not limited to the single-fault condition.

FTA allows a systematic approach that is sufficiently flexible to allow analysis of a variety of factors, including human interactions. FTA is used in risk analysis as a tool to provide an estimate of fault probabilities and to identify single faults and common cause faults that result in hazardous situations.

The pictorial representation leads to an easy understanding of the system behaviour, but as the trees become large, processing of fault trees can require computer systems (see IEC 61025 for more information on the procedure for FTA).

FTA（故障樹分析）：由上而下找根源

FTA的核心邏輯：「為什麼會發生？」

FTA從一個已知的不良結果（頂端事件）開始，逐層向下追問根本原因。

邏輯閘的意義：

  • AND閘：多個原因同時發生才會導致上層事件 → 機率相乘，風險較低
  • OR閘：任何一個原因單獨發生就會導致上層事件 → 機率相加，風險較高

CDMO FTA實例：無菌充填失效

頂端事件（Top Event）：患者接受到非無菌藥品

向下展開（OR閘）：

  • 充填過程中發生微生物污染（OR）
  • 最終產品CCI失效（OR）
  • 滅菌製程失效

「充填過程中微生物污染」再向下展開（OR閘）：

  • B級環境超標（OR）
  • 操作員無菌技術失誤（OR）
  • 充填設備除菌過濾器失效

「除菌過濾器失效」再向下（AND閘）：

  • 過濾器破損 AND 完整性測試未執行或未通過驗收標準

透過AND閘的識別，我們知道過濾器完整性測試是防止這條路徑發生的關鍵控制點。

FTA的限制

當故障樹非常龐大時，手動分析變得困難，需要電腦輔助工具（如ReliaSoft、Isograph等）。FTA也僅分析已已識別的危害，因此必須與PHA或附錄A的危害識別配合使用。

B.4 Event Tree Analysis (ETA) — 事件樹分析

ETA is a causal analytical technique based on an analysis of a sequence of actions and events that can lead to a negative outcome. ETA uses the same logical and mathematical techniques as FTA. However, whereas FTA analyses how an undesirable top event can occur, ETA considers the impact of the failure of a particular component or item in the system, and works out the effect such a failure can have on the overall system and on the users and patients. ETA uses an inductive approach, whereas FTA is deductive.

The initiating event in an event tree will usually fall into one of the following four categories:

  1. Failure or unsafe conditions in the medical device
  2. Use errors
  3. Utility failures (such as loss of power or internet connectivity)
  4. Environmental conditions (such as temperature, humidity, altitude, weather)

The goal of ETA is to determine the probability of possible negative outcomes that can result from the selected initiating event and that can eventually lead to harm. It is necessary to use detailed information about a system to understand the sequence of events to construct the event tree diagram.

The event tree begins with the initiating event where consequences of this event follow in a binary (success/failure) manner. Each event creates a path in which a series of successes or failures will occur, where the overall probability of occurrence for that path can be estimated (see IEC 62502 for more information on the procedure for ETA).

ETA（事件樹分析）：由因推果的二元路徑

ETA vs. FTA：方向性的根本差異

ETA應用：電力中斷對無菌充填線的影響

觸發事件：無菌充填室電力中斷

事件樹二元展開：

  • UPS是否自動切換？
    • 成功 → 充填繼續，影響最小化
    • 失敗 → 繼續分析...
  • （UPS失敗後）HVAC是否維持正壓？
    • 成功 → 環境受控，但需評估暴露時間
    • 失敗 → 正壓失效，微生物污染風險
  • （HVAC失敗後）操作員是否即時執行隔離程序？
    • 成功 → 批次隔離待調查，損失可控
    • 失敗 → 污染批次可能繼續充填

ETA讓我們清楚看到，UPS和緊急應變SOP是防止電力中斷導致患者危害的關鍵防線。

B.5 Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) — 失效模式與效應分析

FMEA is a technique by which the consequences of an individual fault mode are systematically identified and evaluated. It is an inductive technique using the question "What happens if ... ?". Components are analysed one at a time, thus generally looking at a single-fault condition. This is done in a "bottom-up" mode, i.e. following the procedure to the next higher functional system level.

The FMEA is not restricted to a failure of a component's design but can also include:

  • Process FMEA — failures in the manufacturing and assembling of components
  • Use FMEA — the use or misuse of the product by the end user

FMEA can be extended to incorporate an investigation of the individual component fault modes, their probability of occurrence and detectability (only to the degree that detection will enable preventive measures in the context of ISO 14971:2019) and also the degree of severity of the consequences. In order to perform an FMEA, the construction of the medical device should be known in some detail.

Disadvantages of this technique can arise from difficulties in dealing with redundancies and the incorporation of repair or preventive maintenance actions, as well as its restriction to single-fault conditions (see IEC 60812 for more information on procedures for FMEA).

FMEA（失效模式與效應分析）：CDMO的核心工具

FMEA的三個關鍵參數

在ISO 14971框架下的FMEA通常評估三個維度（注意：ISO 14971本身不規定RPN計算，但業界常用）：

  • 嚴重性（Severity, S）：失效模式對患者造成傷害的嚴重程度（1–10分）
  • 發生率（Occurrence, O）：失效模式發生的概率（1–10分）
  • 可探測性（Detectability, D）：現有控制措施能否在造成傷害前探測到失效（1–10，分數越高越難探測）

RPN = S × O × D（風險優先數，Risk Priority Number）

重要提醒：ISO 14971:2019不要求RPN計算。RPN是FMEA工具（如IEC 60812）的概念，但在ISO 14971框架下，嚴重性（S）是最重要的維度——高嚴重性的風險即使發生率低，也必須採取風險控制措施。

CDMO FMEA實例：無菌充填線橡膠塞壓塞製程

三種FMEA類型在CDMO的應用

  • 設計FMEA（Design FMEA）：評估西林瓶/膠塞/針管的設計本身失效模式，適用於容器系統選型階段
  • 製程FMEA（Process FMEA）：評估充填、壓塞、軋蓋、凍乾等製造步驟的失效模式，是CDMO最常用的FMEA類型
  • 使用FMEA（Use FMEA）：評估藥品使用者（護士、患者）可能的使用錯誤，對自我注射器材尤為重要

B.6 Hazard and Operability Study (HAZOP) — 危害與可操作性研究

HAZOP is based on a theory that assumes that hazardous situations and harm are caused by design deviations or operational variations. HAZOP can be performed early in the development process when only the design and development inputs are defined. It is a systematic technique for identifying medical device design and operability problems.

It was originally developed for use in the chemical industry focusing on process deviations from design intent, but there are alternative applications for medical device developers.

HAZOP can be applied to:

  • The operation/function of the medical device itself (e.g. to the existing methods/processes used for the diagnosis, treatment or alleviation of disease as the "design intent")
  • A process used in the manufacture or maintenance/service of the medical device (e.g. sterilization) that can have significant impact on the function of the medical device

Two particular features of a HAZOP are:

  • It uses a team of people with expertise covering the design of the medical device and its application
  • Guide words are used to help identify deviations from normal use: ALL, NONE, NO/NOT, MORE/LESS THAN, AS WELL AS, PART OF, OTHER THAN

The objectives of the HAZOP technique are:

  • To produce a full description of the medical device and how it is intended to be used
  • To review systematically every part of the medical device in order to discover how deviations from normal operating conditions and the design can occur
  • To identify the consequences of such deviations and to decide whether these consequences can lead to hazardous situations or operability problems

When applied to the processes used to manufacture a medical device, the last objective is particularly useful in those cases where the device characteristics depend upon the manufacturing process (see IEC 61882 for more information on the procedure for HAZOP).

HAZOP（危害與可操作性研究）：用「引導詞」找偏差

HAZOP引導詞系統

HAZOP的核心在於將「引導詞」應用到每一個製程節點（Process Node），系統性地探索可能的偏差：

HAZOP在無菌充填製程的應用：滅菌節點分析

製程節點：蒸汽滅菌（高壓滅菌釜）

設計意圖：121°C / 15分鐘 / F₀ ≥ 12 min

  • MORE（溫度過高）→ 藥品降解，包材變形
  • LESS（溫度不足）→ 滅菌失敗，無菌保證水平（SAL）不達標
  • NO（不滅菌）→ 微生物污染，直接患者傷害
  • OTHER THAN（錯誤的滅菌程序）→ 使用了乾熱程序替代濕熱，效果不等效
  • MORE（時間過長）→ F₀值過高，藥品穩定性受損

HAZOP在此的優勢：強迫團隊系統性地審視「超出規格」和「低於規格」兩個方向的風險。

HAZOP的跨領域應用

HAZOP最初起源於化學工業的製程安全分析。在CDMO環境中，它特別適合分析：

  • 無菌充填線的流體傳輸系統（配液、過濾、充填）
  • CIP/SIP系統（清潔滅菌偏差）
  • 凍乾製程（各階段溫度/真空偏差）
  • 環境監控系統（壓差、溫濕度控制偏差）

B.7 Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Point (HACCP)

HACCP is a systematic approach to identify hazards and hazardous situations and to control and monitor the associated risks by focusing on the critical control points in a manufacturing process.

HACCP is based on the following seven principles:

  1. Conduct a hazard analysis (risk analysis) to identify hazards and hazardous situations
  2. Determine the critical control points
  3. Establish appropriate limits
  4. Monitor each critical control point
  5. Establish corrective and preventive actions (identify and implement risk control measures)
  6. Establish procedures for verification and record keeping
  7. Establish documentation and record keeping

Each medical device has its own intended use and life cycle that can be related to its intended use, reasonably foreseeable misuse, or its characteristics related to safety. Hazardous situations can be initiated by events during different phases in the manufacturing process, such as design, development, manufacturing, service, use, disposal, etc.

The heart of an effective HACCP system focuses on the continuing control and monitoring of the identified hazards and hazardous situations. The manufacturer demonstrates the effectiveness of the implemented risk control measures by establishing and documenting the flow diagram, the hazard analysis worksheet, and the critical control plan.

HACCP Documentation Tools

a) Process Flow Diagram

The purpose of the diagram is to provide a clear and simple description of the steps involved in the process. The diagram is necessary to the HACCP team in its subsequent work and can also serve as a future guide for others. The scope of the flow diagram should cover all the processing steps that are under the direct control of the manufacturer.

b) Hazard Analysis Worksheet

The worksheet contains the records of the hazard analysis (risk analysis) — including:

  • The identification and listing of steps in the process where hazards and hazardous situations of significance are present
  • The listing of all identified hazards (and hazardous situations) associated with each step and their significance
  • The listing of all risk control measures for each hazard (and hazardous situation)
  • The identification of all critical control points and their monitoring and controls

c) Critical Control Plan

The plan is based on the seven principles of HACCP and delineates the procedures to be followed to assure the control of a specific design, product, or process. The plan includes:

  • Identifying critical control points and appropriate limits
  • Monitoring and continuing control activities
  • Implementing and monitoring risk control measures
  • Verification activities and record keeping

HACCP（危害分析關鍵控制點）：製程控制的系統性保障

HACCP的起源與在製藥的轉化

HACCP起源於1960年代NASA的太空食品安全計畫，後廣泛用於食品工業。在製藥CDMO中，HACCP的邏輯與ICH Q10的製藥品質體系（PQS）高度契合：

  • CCP（關鍵控制點） ≈ CPP（關鍵製程參數, Critical Process Parameter）
  • 關鍵限度 ≈ 製程驗收標準（Process Acceptance Criteria）
  • 監控與糾偏 ≈ 即時製程監控（In-Process Monitoring）+ CAPA

CDMO應用：無菌充填製程的HACCP關鍵控制點

HACCP七原則在CDMO的操作步驟

  1. 危害分析：使用附錄A問題清單 + PHA識別所有潛在危害，確定顯著危害清單
  2. 確定CCP：使用判定樹（Decision Tree）判斷哪些步驟是CCP vs. 一般管制點（CP）
  3. 建立關鍵限度：基於科學（工藝開發數據）+ 法規（USP/Ph.Eur.藥典標準）
  4. 建立監控程序：誰監控？何時監控？如何監控？頻率？
  5. 建立糾偏措施：偏差發生時的即時處置程序（Deviation SOP）
  6. 驗證程序：製程驗證（PV）、持續製程確認（CPV）
  7. 文件記錄：批次記錄（BPR）、偏差報告、CAPA記錄

HACCP vs. FMEA：選擇哪一個？

在CDMO實踐中，這兩種工具通常並用，而非互相替代：

  • Process FMEA：適合分析每個製程步驟的失效模式，計算RPN，排定改善優先順序
  • HACCP：適合確定製程中的關鍵控制點，建立監控和驗證體系
  • 常見的工作流程：Process FMEA → 識別高風險步驟 → HACCP → 確立CCP並建立控制計畫

Annex A + Annex B Integration in Risk Management Workflow

Annex A and Annex B are designed to be used together within the ISO 14971:2019 risk management process. The typical workflow is:

  1. Step 1 — Annex A (Hazard Identification): Use the A.2 question checklist to systematically identify all potential hazards. Document the results in the hazard identification record.
  2. Step 2 — PHA (B.2): When design details are limited (early development), conduct a Preliminary Hazard Analysis to prioritize identified hazards.
  3. Step 3 — FTA/ETA (B.3/B.4): For complex systems or high-severity hazards, conduct FTA to understand fault combination pathways, and ETA to evaluate the consequences of initiating events.
  4. Step 4 — FMEA (B.5): As design matures, conduct FMEA at the component and process level. Use FMEA findings to identify risk control measures.
  5. Step 5 — HAZOP (B.6): For manufacturing processes (sterilization, filling, CIP/SIP), apply HAZOP with guide words to identify operational deviations.
  6. Step 6 — HACCP (B.7): Translate FMEA and HAZOP findings into a formal critical control plan with monitoring, limits, and verification activities.

Annex C — 風險可接受性中政策、準則、估算與評估之關係
Relation between the Policy, Criteria for Risk Acceptability, Risk Estimation and Risk Evaluation (p44–p46)

C.1–C.2 General & Risk Policy Framework

Annex C describes the relation between the manufacturer's policy for determining acceptable risk and the criteria for risk acceptability established from that policy. The policy sets a framework for developing criteria at the individual risk level and at the overall residual risk level.

C.2 Elements of the Risk Acceptability Policy

ISO 14971:2019 requires the policy for establishing acceptability criteria to be documented — typically within the quality management system. The policy need not reside within the risk management plan itself but must address:

  • Purpose — why the policy exists (e.g., to ensure a high level of safety consistent with stakeholder expectations)
  • Scope — to whom and when the policy applies (all persons involved in establishing, reviewing, and approving criteria in risk management plans)
  • Factors and considerations — applicable regulatory requirements; relevant international standards including approval/rejection limits; the generally acknowledged state of the art; validated stakeholder concerns
  • Approaches to risk control — e.g., ALARP (as low as reasonably practicable), ALARA (as low as reasonably achievable), or benefit-risk ratio preservation
  • Approval and review requirements — who approves, how frequently reviewed

The policy and its elements should be tailored to the specific needs of the organization and the particular type of medical device or process.

C.1–C.2 政策框架：從哲學到準則

四層邏輯鏈：Annex C 的核心是一條從上到下的邏輯鏈：

政策 → 準則 → 估算 → 評估

  1. 風險政策（Risk Policy）：由最高管理層制定，是「哲學層面」的聲明 — 我們相信什麼程度的風險是可接受的？
  2. 可接受準則（Criteria）：記錄於風險管理計畫中，將政策轉化為具體的數值或定性要求
  3. 風險估算（Estimation）：產出嚴重度 × 發生機率的數字或等級
  4. 風險評估（Evaluation）：將估算結果與準則比對，判斷是否可接受

CDMO 實務類比

在無菌充填製程中，你設定的「無菌保證水準（SAL）≤ 10⁻⁶」、「微粒限度：≥0.5µm <6000/container」、「內毒素限度：<0.25 EU/mL」——這些都是風險可接受準則。

常見錯誤：直接從藥典抄數字，沒有說明為什麼這個數字對這個產品/製程是合理的。ISO 14971 要求你論證（justify），不只是援引（cite）。

政策必須考量的因素包括：各上市市場的法規要求、相關國際標準的核准/拒絕限值、現行技術水準（state of the art），以及來自使用者、臨床醫師和主管機關的已確認利害相關者關切。

C.3 Criteria for Risk Acceptability

The criteria for risk acceptability are established based on the risk policy. They are recorded in the risk management plan. Specific criteria can be established for each device type (or family), or the same criteria can be applied across all devices — but the choice must be justified.

ISO 14971:2019 requires that criteria be established for the acceptability of:

  • Each individual risk
  • The overall residual risk (can use the same or different criteria)

When the probability of occurrence cannot be estimated, criteria may be based on severity alone.

C.4 Risk Control — Two Approaches

Risk control is the process by which decisions are made and measures implemented to reduce risks to, or maintain them within, specified levels.

Approach 1: Practicability

Technical practicability — the ability to reduce risk regardless of cost. Measures are NOT technically practicable if they:

  • Diminish the effectiveness of the device or compromise its intended use
  • Make the device overly complex, increasing use error or reducing benefit
  • Create alarm fatigue (multiple alarms causing confusion)
  • Include so many warnings/cautions that the user is hampered in operation
  • Communicate so many residual risks that users cannot distinguish important ones

Economic practicability — the ability to reduce risk without making the device an unsound economic proposition. Cost implications are considered only insofar as they impact the preservation, promotion, or improvement of human health. Economic practicability cannot be used as a rationale for accepting unnecessary risks.

Approach 2: Magnitude-Based (Three-Zone Model)

Residual risks are classified into three categories:

  • a) Unacceptable — magnitude exceeds the criteria for acceptability; must be redesigned or controlled
  • b) Insignificant/Negligible — so small that removing it adds no meaningful safety benefit
  • c) ALARP zone — between (a) and (b); further investigation required to determine if additional control is feasible

ALARP 的實務邏輯

ALARP（As Low As Reasonably Practicable，合理可行的最低風險）不是「盡力而為」，而是有嚴格的成本效益邏輯：

技術可行性 vs 實用性（Practicability vs Practicality）

這兩個英文詞的差異很重要：

  • Practicable（可行的）：技術上能做到、放入實踐中是可能的
  • Practical（實用的）：方便、好用

ISO 14971 問的是「技術可行性」，不是「是否方便」。你不能因為「加這道安全措施太麻煩」就省略它。

「成本太高」本身不能作為接受不必要風險的理由。只有當成本高到會讓設備失去可及性（availability），從而損害公共健康時，才可考量。這個邊界很高，不輕易觸發。

Annex D — 安全資訊與殘餘風險披露
Information for Safety and Information on Residual Risk (p47–p49)

D.1 General — Two Distinct Concepts

The purpose of Annex D is to clarify the difference between two distinct concepts that are often confused:

  • Information for safety — instructive; tells the user what to do or avoid to prevent harm
  • Disclosure of residual risk — descriptive; informs users about risks that remain after all controls so they can make informed decisions

D.2 Information for Safety

Information for safety is a risk control measure of last resort. ISO 14971:2019 establishes a hierarchy: (1) inherently safe design, (2) protective measures, (3) information for safety. Only after design features and protective measures have been maximized should information for safety be relied upon.

When residual risk can still cause harm despite other controls, instructions are required to tell users what actions to take or avoid. This information can be provided as:

  • Warnings and cautions (e.g., "Do not step on surface")
  • Contra-indications
  • Instructions for use (including installation, maintenance, and disposal)
  • Training materials
  • Warning labels on the device
  • Graphical user interface instructions

ISO 14971:2019 requires information for safety to be verified for effectiveness (e.g., by usability engineering studies) and to be traceable to the risk in the risk management file.

Key Considerations When Developing Information for Safety

  • Classify information based on the level of risk
  • Determine level of detail needed for clarity
  • Select appropriate location (warning label on device, IFU, user interface)
  • Choose appropriate wording, pictures, or symbols
  • Identify intended recipients (users, service personnel, installers, patients)
  • Select appropriate media (IFU, labels, UI messages, advisory notices)
  • Comply with applicable regulatory requirements (some markets prescribe exact text)

D.1–D.2 安全資訊：最後一道防線

ISO 14971 風險控制優先順序（重要！）

  1. 本質安全設計（Inherently safe design）：從設計根源消除危害或降低風險
  2. 保護措施（Protective measures）：防護罩、聯鎖裝置、警報系統等
  3. 安全資訊（Information for safety）：警告標示、使用說明、禁忌症等

安全資訊只能在前兩層已充分發揮後才使用。若一個公司將「警告標示」作為主要的風險控制手段，審查時這本身就是一個發現（finding）。

三個常見例子

正確用法："Warning: Do not use haemolyzed serum samples. These can interfere with the measurement and affect the accuracy of the result." — 這是在告訴使用者如何避免特定傷害，屬於安全資訊。

注意：太多警告標示本身也是一個風險（警告疲勞 / warning fatigue）。如果使用者因為警告太多而分不清哪個重要，這個設計在技術可行性上是有問題的。

CDMO 關聯：CoA、CoC 與技術文件

從 CDMO 的角度，向客戶提供的以下文件，部分具有「安全資訊」的性質：

  • CoA（分析證書）：記錄檢測結果，讓客戶判斷產品是否符合規格
  • CoC（符合性聲明）：聲明製程符合特定標準
  • 技術文件（Technical Package）：包含容器密封系統、滅菌確效、無菌保證水準等製程風險資訊

這些文件中關於已知限制或殘餘風險的披露，是 ISO 14971 框架下「安全資訊」概念的延伸。

法規觀點：標示錯誤是 FDA 召回主要原因之一

FDA 每年發布的 Class I/II 召回案例中，標示（labeling）錯誤或遺漏殘餘風險披露是最常見原因之一。「充分披露」的標準是：使用者需要的所有資訊，以安全且有效地使用設備。這個標準在法規審查中的門檻很高。

D.3 Disclosure of Residual Risk

Residual risk is the risk that remains after all risk control measures have been implemented. Residual risks can relate to possible side-effects or after-effects associated with use of a medical device.

ISO 14971:2019 requires the manufacturer to inform users about significant residual risks.

The disclosure is descriptive — it provides the user with information necessary to understand the risks associated with using the device, weigh those risks against the benefits, and make an informed clinical decision. The manufacturer examines the risk management file and determines what information users need to receive. Decisions on disclosure are recorded in the risk management file.

Considerations for Residual Risk Disclosure

  • Level of detail — sometimes a summary is more appropriate than raw data from the risk management file
  • Wording for clarity and understandability
  • Intended recipients (users, service personnel, patients)
  • Means and media (IFU, package insert, training)

The nature and extent of the information should be commensurate with the residual risk and with the knowledge and experience of the intended recipient.

D.3 Examples of Residual Risk Disclosure

  • Linear accelerators for tumours: residual risks include erythema or epilation from radiation therapy
  • MRI: patient may experience claustrophobia in enclosed space
  • Mechanical ventilation: complications including airway injury, alveolar damage, or pneumothorax
  • Lithotripsy: ~10% of patients have blood in urine or kidney pain; ~2% incur urinary tract infection
  • Ophthalmic surgical laser: swelling, inflammation, or pain; mild light sensitivity in 1% until 6 weeks post-surgery
  • ICD system: inappropriate shocks, dependence, depression, fear of shocks during daily activities

D.3 殘餘風險披露：讓使用者做知情決策

兩個概念的核心差異

臨床決策框架中的殘餘風險

殘餘風險披露在臨床決策中可能非常重要。以 ICD 為例：植入手術的決定必須在了解「不適當電擊」、「幽靈電擊（phantom shock）感知」等殘餘風險後做出，這些資訊影響患者的生活品質選擇。

披露的深度應與殘餘風險的性質和受眾的知識水準相稱（commensurate）——給臨床醫師的資訊可比給一般患者更詳細、更技術性。

CDMO 實務：充填製程的殘餘風險披露

  1. 容器密封完整性測試（CCI）通過後，仍存在統計性失敗率 → 應在技術文件中披露 AQL 或抽樣計畫的局限性
  2. 滅菌後的無菌保證水準（SAL 10⁻⁶）是機率性概念，不代表零風險 → 客戶使用說明應包含此說明
  3. 生物相容性評估中的殘餘萃取物/溶出物（E&L）風險 → 必要時在 CoA 或技術包中披露已知化學物質的可接受暴露限值評估結果

Annex E — 國際標準在風險管理中的角色
Role of International Standards in Risk Management (p50–p54)

E.1 General — Standards as Risk Management Evidence

International standards can play a significant role in medical device risk management by providing requirements for the safety of products and/or processes. They are developed by experts and are considered to represent the generally acknowledged state of the art.

When performing risk management, the manufacturer first considers the device being designed, its intended use, characteristics related to safety, and associated hazards and hazardous situations. The manufacturer can then select and apply product and process standards that contain specific requirements to assist in managing the risks associated with those hazards throughout the device life cycle.

"International standards, therefore, provide valuable information on acceptability that has been validated during a worldwide evaluation process, including multiple rounds of review, commenting and voting to reach international consensus."

For objective evidence that the requirements and compliance criteria of these standards are satisfied, the residual risks related to those hazards can be considered acceptable, unless there is evidence to the contrary (e.g., adverse event reports, product recalls, or complaints).

E.2 Use of International Product Safety Standards

Three scenarios are addressed:

(a) Standard Fully Specifies Requirements + Test Acceptance Criteria

Where a standard specifies requirements addressing particular hazards together with specific acceptance criteria, compliance establishes that residual risks have been reduced to acceptable levels — unless evidence to the contrary exists (e.g., IEC 60601-1 leakage current limits). The steps are:

  1. Identify safety characteristics; identify hazards and hazardous situations
  2. Identify which hazards and situations are completely covered by the standard
  3. Rely on the standard's requirements to demonstrate acceptable safety for those hazards
  4. Ensure device design specifications comply with the standard's risk control requirements
  5. Obtain verification from design documentation review; confirm device meets relevant requirements
  6. If requirements are met, associated residual risk is considered acceptable; document in risk management file

(b) Standard Provides Tests Without Specific Acceptance Criteria

Some standards specify tests related to known hazards but without test acceptance criteria (e.g., IEC 60601-2-16), or identify specific hazardous situations for risk management without further requirements (e.g., some clauses of IEC 60601-1). In these cases, the situation is more complex; manufacturers are encouraged to use the content of such standards in their hazard analysis of the particular device.

(c) Hazard Not Addressed in Any International Standard

Where a hazard is not specifically addressed in international product standards, the manufacturer addresses that hazard or hazardous situation in the risk management process, estimates and evaluates the risk, and if necessary controls it.

E.1–E.2 符合性推定與「現行技術水準」

符合性推定（Presumption of Conformity）

「協調標準（harmonized standard）」的使用賦予你一個重要的法律效力：如果你符合該標準的所有要求，就推定（presumed）你符合對應的法規基本要求（如 EU MDR Essential Requirements 或 FDA QSR）。

但這個推定有條件：

  • 必須符合標準的全部要求，不能只選擇有利的條款
  • 若有相反的證據（不良事件報告、召回），推定不成立
  • 偏離標準必須有明確記錄的理由（documented rationale）

三種情境的應對策略

現行技術水準（State of the Art）的實務意義

「現行技術水準」不是靜態的。它由國際標準、最佳實踐技術、已接受的科學研究、主管機關發布文件、以及類似產品的市場資訊共同定義。

當你偏離一個已建立的標準時，你必須論證為什麼你的替代方案仍符合或超越現行技術水準，而不只是說「我有不同的方法」。

CDMO 實務意義

在品質系統中引用並遵循 ISO 14971 和 ISO/TR 24971，對法規審查員提供了防禦性依據（defensibility）。若你的無菌充填工廠使用以下標準作為風險管理輸入：

  • ISO 14644-1（潔淨室分級）→ 符合即視為控制了微粒風險
  • ISO 13408-1（無菌充填設備）→ 符合即視為控制了無菌操作相關風險
  • ISO 11135（EO 滅菌）→ 符合即視為控制了滅菌有效性風險

這些是 Annex E(a) 場景的直接應用。偏離任何一個，都需要在 RM 文件中記錄理由。

E.3 International Process Standards and ISO 14971

International process standards work with ISO 14971 in one of two ways:

  • The process standard requires application of ISO 14971 as part of its implementation
  • The process standard is intended to be used in risk management

In either case, proper use requires attention to the interfaces between the standards to optimize inputs, outputs, and timing.

(a) IEC 62304 — Medical Device Software

IEC 62304 makes a normative reference to ISO 14971. Software development planning requirements are consistent with the risk management plan required by ISO 14971. The standard requires identification of software items that are contributing factors to hazardous situations, including software that could cause inappropriate treatment by providing misleading information.

(b) IEC 62366-1 — Usability Engineering

IEC 62366-1 and ISO 14971 run as two parallel, interconnected processes. Key interfaces:

  • Clause 5.1: use specification as input to ISO 14971 intended use determination
  • Clause 5.2: identification of user interface characteristics related to safety as input to risk analysis
  • Clause 5.3: identification of known/foreseeable hazards and hazardous situations related to use
  • Clause 5.9: summative evaluation on the final user interface as part of risk management

(c) ISO 10993-1 — Biological Evaluation

ISO 10993-1 is a guidance document for biological evaluation within a risk management process. It requires a biological risk analysis approach consistent with ISO 14971: identification of biological hazards, estimation and evaluation of risks, risk control, and monitoring. The biological evaluation should take account of physical/chemical characteristics, history of clinical use, and existing toxicology data. The conclusion is documented in the Biological Evaluation Report (BER), which becomes part of the risk management file. If overall residual risks are acceptable, no further risk control is needed; otherwise appropriate measures are taken.

(d) ISO 14155 — Clinical Investigation

ISO 14155 addresses good clinical practice for the design, conduct, recording, and reporting of pre- and post-market clinical investigations. It is relevant to the estimation of clinical risks and the assessment of the benefit-risk balance for medical devices.

E.3 流程標準與 ISO 14971 的介面管理

四個流程標準的 ISO 14971 介面

IEC 62304 的核心邏輯

軟體本身是一個特殊的「風險貢獻因素」：它不直接造成危害，但可以透過提供錯誤資訊（如誤導性的劑量計算）間接導致不適當的治療。這正是 IEC 62304 Clause 7 與 ISO 14971 的介面所在。

在 CDMO 環境中：充填設備的 PLC 程式、劑量控制演算法、環境監控系統的軟體——都需要評估是否是危害的貢獻因素。

ISO 10993-1 與 CDMO 的高度相關性

對 CDMO 而言，ISO 10993-1 最為直接相關，原因：

  • 容器密封系統（西林瓶、塞子、鋁蓋）的材料必須有生物相容性評估
  • BER 是提交 IND/NDA/MAA 時的必要文件
  • Annex B 提供了與 ISO 14971 一致的風險方法：
    1. 識別生物危害（hazard identification）
    2. 估算與評估風險（risk estimation and evaluation）
    3. 控制風險（risk control）
    4. 監控控制措施效果（monitoring）
  • 最終結論記錄在 BER 中，成為 RM 文件的一部分

系統整合思維：三個流程同時運行

在醫療器材開發中，以下三個流程必須同時整合，而不是串聯執行：

  1. ISO 14971（風險管理）— 主框架
  2. IEC 62366-1（可用性工程）— 使用相關危害的輸入和評估
  3. ISO 10993-1（生物評估）— 材料相關危害的輸入和評估

輸入、輸出和時間節點必須優化協調。常見錯誤：生物評估完成後才做 RM，導致大量補充文件工作。

Annex F — Guidance on Risks Related to Security / 附錄F：資安相關風險指引 (p55–59)

F.1 General — Security in the ISO 14971 Framework

The risk management process described in ISO 14971:2019 can be applied to risks associated with the security of medical devices. Risks related to data and systems security are specifically mentioned in the scope of ISO 14971:2019 to avoid any misunderstanding that a separate process would be needed to manage risks related to security of medical devices.

Breaches of data and systems security can lead to harm — for example, through loss of data, uncontrolled access to data, corruption or loss of diagnostic information, or corruption of software leading to malfunction of the device.

Security in this document includes cybersecurity and data and systems security.

A common misconception is that ISO 14971:2019 would only apply to health of people, disregarding that the definition of harm includes damage to property and the environment. This misconception is often discovered during discussions of security, where it is assumed that ISO 14971:2019 is restricted to risks related to the patient and user and would not cover risks related to security.

The definition of harm in ISO 14971:2019 applies to people, property, and the environment. For example, damage to an electronic health record (damage to property) can additionally result in incorrect diagnosis, which can lead to patient injury (damage to people).

F.1 資安與ISO 14971框架 — 中文說明

ISO 14971:2019的風險管理流程可直接套用於醫療器材的資訊安全（security）相關風險。標準明確將資料與系統安全納入適用範圍，以避免誤解認為需要另建一套獨立流程。

資安漏洞可造成危害（harm），例如：資料遺失、未授權存取、診斷資訊被竄改、軟體被破壞導致器材失效等。

關鍵概念：Safety vs. Security

Safety（產品安全）：關注最終結果 — 器材是否對人員、財產或環境造成危害。

Security（資訊安全）：關注過程 — 系統是否不受惡意或意外的hostile acts影響，維持機密性、完整性、可用性。

兩者關係：Security failure → 可能觸發 Safety hazard → 最終導致 harm。

ISO/IEC Guide 63建議「safety」不作形容詞使用（不說"safety risk"），而應說「risk related to safety」。

實例：胰島素注射幫浦（Insulin Pump）

情境：駭客透過開放的網路埠（vulnerability）入侵，篡改劑量設定資料（loss of data integrity）→ 幫浦輸注錯誤劑量（hazardous situation）→ 病患低血糖昏迷或死亡（harm）。

這是資安事件，但同時也是ISO 14971框架下需要管理的安全風險——兩套流程在此交會。

F.2 Terminology Used in Security Risk Management

Security risk management often employs different terminology than ISO 14971:2019. Nevertheless, correspondence exists between the terms used in security risk management and those used in ISO 14971:2019. The following key terms originate from IEC Guide 120 and AAMI TIR 57:

  • Security: A condition that results from the establishment and maintenance of protective measures that ensure a state of inviolability from hostile acts or influences (intentional or unintentional).
  • Threat: Potential for violation of security, which exists when there is a circumstance, capability, action, or event that could breach security and cause harm. — Threat corresponds to an event or sequence of events that can exploit a vulnerability, leading to a hazard (ISO 14971 term).
  • Vulnerability: Flaw or weakness in a system's design, implementation, or operation and management that could be exploited to violate the system's security policy. — Vulnerability can be seen as a type of event or circumstance (see Table C.2 in ISO 14971:2019).
  • Confidentiality: Property that information is not made available or disclosed to unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes.
  • Integrity: Property of accuracy and completeness.
  • Availability: Property of being accessible and usable upon demand by an authorized entity.

F.3–F.4 與ISO 14971的關係及資安風險管理特性

資安風險 vs. 一般安全風險：主要差異

發生機率的不同估算邏輯

一般安全風險：發生機率主要由設計、製造、材料選擇、公差等因素決定 — 可預測性高。

資安風險：發生機率受攻擊者動機、財務誘因、漏洞公開程度影響 — 難以預估。一旦漏洞資訊被公開在網路上，機率可能從「極罕見」瞬間變為「每次都會發生」。

嚴重度（Severity）的特殊考量

一般安全風險的嚴重度通常聚焦在直接身體傷害（輕傷→需醫療介入→永久損傷/死亡）。

資安風險的嚴重度則需考量CIA三要素的喪失程度，以及對器材功能的間接影響。

風控措施的「雙刃劍效應」

場景：為了防止駭客入侵自動體外心臟去顫器（AED），設計了密碼保護功能（資安控制措施）。

反效果：急救現場若忘記密碼，無法及時啟動 → 心臟驟停病患無法獲救（新的安全風險）。

結論：資安風控措施可能引入新的安全風險，反之亦然。ISO 14971要求必須評估風控措施所帶來的次生風險。

CDMO視角：資安風險與無菌製造

Fill-finish製程本身（灌裝、壓蓋、凍乾）通常不直接引入資安風險，但CDMO應了解：

  • 客戶產品若含有嵌入式軟體或連網功能（如智慧型注射裝置、自動注射器搭配app），則附錄F適用。
  • 當客戶要求CDMO製造combination product時，需確認器材部分是否已完成資安風險評估。
  • 製造控制系統（MES、SCADA）本身的資安，雖非ISO 14971範疇，但影響GMP合規（21 CFR Part 11 / GAMP 5）。

F.5 Prioritizing Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability

When evaluating security-related risks, the manufacturer ensures that the security priorities (confidentiality, integrity and availability) properly take the intended use of the medical device into account.

  • Integrity priority: For some applications, integrity of information is of high concern — e.g. changes in drug orders or medical data/images in a patient's medical record.
  • Confidentiality priority: In other instances, loss of confidentiality could be more important — e.g. disclosure of personal health information can create potential for blackmail. Also, reverse engineering of unencrypted design features could compromise operation of medical devices and result in patient injury.
  • Availability priority: Especially for life-supporting or life-saving devices, loss of availability or a reduction in effectiveness can be most important to the health of people — e.g. delay of diagnosis or treatment due to DDoS attack or ransomware.

These examples indicate that risks related to security can impact the patient's health, depending on the intended use of the device.

Annex G — Components and Devices Designed Without Using ISO 14971 / 附錄G：未依ISO 14971設計的器材處理 (p60–61)

G.1 General — The Legacy Device Challenge

This guidance assumes that the manufacturer has already established a risk management process compliant with ISO 14971:2019. It does not replace or eliminate any of the requirements in ISO 14971:2019 for a manufacturer, but recommends a way for the manufacturer to remediate deficiencies that might exist.

For various reasons, a manufacturer might not have followed all the requirements described in ISO 14971:2019 for each constituent component of a medical device — such as proprietary components, software components, subsystems of non-medical origin, or for devices already available on the market. In such cases, the medical device documentation could be limited and insufficient for the purpose of demonstrating compliance with ISO 14971:2019.

In most cases, however, a wealth of information about the medical device and its constituent components is available. For example, information on actual use could be acquired through a review of post-production data for the device or for similar devices on the market. Relevant reliability and production data and previously compiled manufacturer-related documentation could also be available.

This annex aims to provide manufacturers with guidance on how available information can be used to build an initial risk management file that can be maintained in the future.

G.1 舊型器材的挑戰 — 中文說明

Legacy device（舊型器材）是指在ISO 14971出現之前、或在現行版本之前已設計完成的醫療器材，其風險管理文件可能不完整甚至缺失。

真實場景：製造商繼承舊有產品線

某公司透過併購取得一款已上市十年的注射裝置，發現原廠風險管理文件付之闕如 — 僅有安規測試報告和少量抱怨記錄。

ISO/TR 24971附錄G的建議：不要從零開始，而是利用現有的上市後資料（post-market data）、可靠度資料、生產記錄，回溯建構初始風險管理文件（initial risk management file）。

CDMO視角：接手既有產品製造

當CDMO從客戶或另一CDMO接手既有（legacy）產品的製造時，應考慮：

  • 要求客戶提供完整的ISO 14971合規風險管理文件
  • 若文件不完整，協助進行gap assessment（差距分析）
  • 製造工藝變更（如設備、包材、製程參數）可能引入新的風險，需重新評估
  • 附錄G的七步驟方法可作為補救（remediation）的工作框架

「Medical device」定義提示

附錄G的「Medical device」包括其子系統、組件（含醫療及非醫療來源的軟體元件）。因此，即使只是一個第三方採購的零組件未依ISO 14971評估，整台器材的合規性也可能受影響。

G.2 Risk Management Plan for Legacy Devices

ISO 14971:2019 requires that all risk management activities be planned. In establishing a risk management plan for a legacy device, particular attention should be given to:

  1. Activities for the remaining phases of the lifecycle of the device (especially maintenance, decommissioning and disposal, where applicable);
  2. The assignment of responsibilities and authorities;
  3. Requirements for review of risk management activities from now on;
  4. The criteria for acceptability, including criteria for accepting risks when the probability of occurrence of harm cannot be estimated;
  5. A method to evaluate the overall residual risk and criteria for acceptability of the overall residual risk;
  6. Verification activities, both for existing risk control measures and for new risk control measures that are considered necessary;
  7. Post-production activities for the collection and review of production and post-production information, and how this information is used to determine if the risks are acceptable.

G.2 舊型器材風險管理計畫的重點

附錄G特別強調：建立合規的Risk Management Plan時，必須顧及「從現在起的剩餘生命週期」而非僅補充過去缺失的文件。

關鍵點 d)：無法估算發生機率時的處理

舊型器材的資料往往不足以統計計算發生機率（probability of occurrence）。ISO 14971允許在計畫中明訂當機率無法估算時的風險可接受準則（例如：以嚴重度作為主要判斷依據）。

這對legacy device審查非常重要：不能以「沒有資料」為由迴避風險評估。

關鍵點 e)：整體殘餘風險評估

除了個別風險的可接受性，ISO 14971要求評估overall residual risk（整體殘餘風險）。舊型器材常見的缺口就在這裡 — 只有個別元件的安規測試，缺乏整體風險評估。

關鍵點 g)：上市後資訊回饋

對於已上市的legacy device，上市後監視（post-market surveillance）資料（客訴、不良事件報告、競品資料）往往是建立初始風險管理文件的最重要資訊來源。

G.3 Building the Risk Management File — Seven Steps

Since the device was designed without using ISO 14971:2019, the risk management file should start building from available evidence. It is likely that some risk control measures have already been implemented but without recorded traceability to the hazards and hazardous situations associated with the device. The manufacturer can begin by identifying the solutions already adopted and then identifying the hazards and hazardous situations that are controlled by these solutions. These solutions are now considered risk control measures and are documented in the risk management file.

Such an approach to build an initial risk management file for a legacy device consists of the following steps:

  1. Document the intended use, reasonably foreseeable misuse, and safety-related characteristics. Safety characteristics can be derived from information about actual use gathered during the post-production phase. The questions in Annex A can be useful to determine the characteristics related to safety.
  2. Identify all solutions already adopted in the device that can be considered risk control measures.
  3. Identify all hazards and hazardous situations associated with the device and the possible harm that can result.
  4. Determine if any hazards or hazardous situations exist for which no risk control measure is implemented. For those, the manufacturer should estimate and evaluate the risk and apply ISO 14971:2019. For hazards and hazardous situations for which risk control measures are implemented, verify their effectiveness and estimate and evaluate the residual risk. For residual risks judged not acceptable, consider further risk control and apply ISO 14971:2019.
  5. Document traceability for each identified hazard and hazardous situation to the risk control measures. Elements include: identified hazards and hazardous situations; possible harm; risk control measures; verification of implementation and effectiveness; acceptability of residual risks.
  6. Evaluate the overall risk according to ISO 14971:2019 Clause 8.
  7. Review the execution of the risk management plan according to ISO 14971:2019 Clause 9. Document the result in a report.

The risk management file and other documents generated during these steps form the initial risk management file.

G.3 建立初始風險管理文件的七步驟

七步驟流程 — 實務操作指南

  1. 記錄預期用途與安全特性：從上市後資料（如客訴、ADE報告）萃取實際使用情況，參考附錄A問題清單辨識安全相關特性。
  2. 盤點既有的風險控制措施：器材上現有的設計特徵（如保護蓋、警報、使用限制）都可能是風控措施，只是從未被文件化為「risk control measure」。
  3. 識別所有危害與危險情況：從已知的hazards出發，推導可能的hazardous situations與harm。
  4. 找出未受控的危害：對沒有任何風控措施的危害，立即啟動ISO 14971評估流程；對有風控措施的，驗證其有效性並評估殘餘風險。
  5. 建立可追溯性矩陣：危害 → 危險情況 → 可能傷害 → 風控措施 → 驗證結果 → 殘餘風險可接受性，形成完整的traceability chain。
  6. 整體殘餘風險評估：依ISO 14971:2019 Clause 8進行整體評估，確保benefit-risk balance可接受。
  7. 風險管理報告審查：依ISO 14971:2019 Clause 9完成審查，撰寫Risk Management Report，與上述文件共同構成初始風險管理文件。

比喻：老屋翻修的結構安全評估

Legacy device就像購入一棟老屋 — 建築本身存在，但建築圖說可能殘缺，也未曾做過現代標準的結構安全評估。

正確做法不是拆掉重建，而是：先盤點現有結構（步驟1-2）→ 找出潛在問題（步驟3-4）→ 補強並記錄（步驟5）→ 整體評估是否安全可居（步驟6-7）。

Annex H (Part 1) — Guidance for In Vitro Diagnostic (IVD) Medical Devices / 附錄H前半：體外診斷器材特殊指引 (p62–70)

H.1 General — IVD Risk Management Context

H.1.1 Purpose and Scope

The purpose of this annex is to provide guidance for the application of particular aspects of ISO 14971:2019 to in vitro diagnostic (IVD) medical devices. This guidance is focused on the indirect risks to patients from incorrect or delayed in vitro diagnostic results, and is intended to supplement the general guidance provided throughout this document.

Throughout this annex, "clinician" is used as a general term to mean a healthcare provider who sees patients and who orders, interprets and acts upon IVD examination results.

H.1.2 Context for IVD Risk Management

Managing risks to patients can be challenging for manufacturers of IVD medical devices. These risks are indirect, often characterized by extended sequences of events that involve "competent intermediaries" — such as trained users who use IVD medical devices to perform IVD examinations, and clinicians who rely on the examination results.

ISO 15189 (international standard for quality and competence of medical laboratories) requires medical laboratories to control risks to patients. ISO 22367 is being developed to describe risk management for IVD medical devices for medical laboratories, based on the same principles as ISO 14971:2019.

When a manufacturer supplies an IVD medical device to a medical laboratory, any residual risks that could not be controlled through design or protective measures are transferred to the laboratory along with the accompanying documentation (including information for the laboratory to control those risks). The manufacturer also discloses any residual risks in the safety information, so that the laboratory director can evaluate and determine their acceptability.

Some IVD medical devices are intended for use by clinicians at the point of care, while self-testing devices are actually used by patients. The user's ability to control risks can be more limited, so point-of-care and self-testing devices must be designed with risk control measures appropriate for the (intended) users and use environment outside laboratories.

H.1 IVD器材風險管理背景 — 中文說明

什麼是IVD醫療器材？

IVD（In Vitro Diagnostic / 體外診斷器材）：用於對人體取得的樣本（血液、尿液、組織切片等）進行體外檢測的器材。

關鍵特點：檢測過程中器材不直接接觸病患。風險不來自「器材本身傷害病患」，而來自「錯誤的檢測結果導致錯誤的臨床決策」。

例如：血糖機、HIV快篩試劑、生化分析儀、PCR檢測系統、免疫組化染色試劑。

IVD風險的獨特性：間接傷害路徑

一般醫療器材（如手術刀、心律調節器）的風險路徑相對直接：器材故障 → 直接傷害病患。

IVD器材的風險路徑較長且間接：

檢測結果錯誤 → 臨床醫師誤判 → 錯誤治療或漏診 → 病患受害。

中間存在多個「有能力的中間人（competent intermediaries）」：實驗室技術員、臨床醫師。這些中間人的行為可以放大或緩衝IVD器材的風險。

CDMO視角：填充IVD試劑的特殊考量

CDMOs承接IVD試劑（如診斷用抗體試劑、PCR試劑）的灌裝時，應了解：

  • 客戶的風險管理文件必須涵蓋「錯誤結果」的風險路徑
  • 製造相關風險（如試劑批次不一致、交叉污染）直接影響分析性能（analytical performance），進而影響病患安全
  • IVD試劑的穩定性（stability）、標定值可追溯性（calibration traceability）是風險管理的關鍵控制點
  • 殘餘風險揭露（residual risk disclosure）需透過IFU（說明書）傳遞給實驗室使用者

H.2.1 Intended Use and Reasonably Foreseeable Misuse

H.2.1.1 Analytical and Clinical Use

Most IVD medical devices have two types of users to consider:

  • A user who performs all or part of an examination ("analytical use"); and
  • A clinician who receives, interprets and acts on the examination results ("clinical use").

In the case of IVD medical devices intended for self-testing, the patient can be the only user.

H.2.1.2 Device Description

Risk analysis for an IVD medical device begins with identifying and documenting a clear description of the IVD and its specific role in producing the examination result. Questions include:

  • Is the device used alone or in combination with other devices?
  • If standalone, is it automated (software, robotics)?
  • If part of a system, what is its role (sample collection, measuring instrument, software, reagents, calibrators, control materials, accessory)?
  • Does the device employ new or novel technology (e.g. for measurement, communication)?
  • Does the device employ digital information technology for documenting/transmitting examination results?
  • Do software applications provide diagnostic or treatment recommendations?
  • Does the IVD communicate with a device that immediately administers treatment based on the IVD result (e.g. blood glucose monitor communicating with an implanted insulin administration system)?

H.2.1 預期用途與可合理預見的誤用

IVD的雙重用途框架

分析用途 vs. 臨床用途

實例：血糖自我監測（SMBG）

分析用途：病患在家用採血針取血，滴在試紙上，等待結果 — 使用者就是病患。

臨床用途：數值用於決定胰島素劑量 — 使用者同時也是決策者（病患）。

風險分析必須考慮：若結果偏高（false high）→ 病患注射過多胰島素 → 低血糖昏迷；若結果偏低（false low）→ 胰島素不足 → 高血糖危機。

此場景中IVD結果直接驅動治療行動，風險影響最大。

H.2.1.3–H.2.1.4 Analytical & Clinical Use Details

H.2.1.3 Analytical Use

The intended use of the IVD includes the analyte(s) intended to be detected or measured; acceptable sample types; calibration, quality control and preventive maintenance activities; and the use environment. Key questions for analytical use:

  • What analyte is the device intended to measure or examine?
  • Will examination results be qualitative, semi-quantitative or quantitative?
  • What specimens can be analysed (serum, plasma, blood, urine, other body fluids, tissue)?
  • Do other substances potentially found in samples interfere with the analytical process?
  • Are there limitations for use in specific environments (medical lab, emergency room, ambulance, ICU, home)?
  • Who will perform examinations, and what training/qualifications are appropriate?

H.2.1.4 Clinical Use

The intended clinical use includes the medical conditions and patient populations for which examination results are used. Clinical use considerations:

  • How will IVD results be used in clinical decision making?
  • What are the medical decision points and degree of accuracy required?
  • Can clinicians recognize incorrect results (based on magnitude of error or consistency with other clinical information)?
  • What actions would the clinician take in the event of an abnormal or unexpected result?
  • What are the clinical significance of delayed results?
  • What are the potential adverse consequences of unnecessary medical intervention?

Clinical use categories include: diagnosis/treatment/prevention; measuring body constituents; monitoring therapeutic drug levels; determining safety of donated blood/organs; screening for markers; predicting effectiveness of therapeutics ("companion diagnostic"); predicting risk of disease development.

H.2.1.3–H.2.1.4 分析用途與臨床用途詳解

分析用途風險問題的核心

分析用途的風險分析需要回答：「什麼樣的分析錯誤，在什麼情況下，會導致臨床有害後果？」

干擾物質（interfering substances）是IVD分析用途風險的常見來源：

  • 溶血（hemolysis）干擾電解質、酶類測定
  • 高脂血症（lipemia）干擾光學法測定
  • 某些藥物干擾免疫測定（immunoassay）
  • 生物素（biotin）補充劑干擾多種免疫測定，曾導致心肌梗塞漏診的案例

臨床用途的風險分析重點

製造商可能不是臨床專家，但必須理解其IVD在臨床決策中的角色。重要問題：

  • 這是唯一的診斷依據嗎？還是與其他資訊結合使用？若為唯一依據，錯誤結果的風險更高。
  • 臨床醫師能察覺異常結果嗎？若結果雖錯誤但在臨床邏輯上說得通，醫師更可能接受錯誤結果。
  • 延遲結果有多嚴重？急診環境下，結果延遲數小時可能造成不可逆傷害。

Companion Diagnostic（伴隨診斷）的特殊風險

Companion diagnostic是預測特定藥物（如標靶治療、免疫療法）對特定病患有效性的IVD。

False negative → 病患被判定「不適合」該療法 → 失去有效治療機會（loss of therapeutic benefit）。

False positive → 病患接受實際無效的療法 → 延誤替代治療 + 藥物副作用（iatrogenic harm）。

這類IVD的風險分析需要腫瘤科臨床專家的深度參與。

H.2.2 Characteristics Related to Patient Safety

H.2.2.1 General

In addition to biological, chemical, electrical, mechanical and security characteristics in common with other medical devices (see Annex A), IVD medical devices have analytical performance and reliability characteristics that determine their suitability for intended clinical use. Some IVD medical devices can perform multiple examinations simultaneously, and their clinical performance can rely on interpretation of patterns of results (e.g. multiplex assays). Failure to meet a performance, reliability or communication requirement can initiate a sequence of events that might result in harm to a patient.

H.2.2.2 Performance Characteristics Related to Patient Safety

a) Quantitative examinations measure a quantity in a representative specimen. The required analytical performance depends on the medical application, but false high, false normal or false low results can affect diagnosis, cause inappropriate or delayed therapy, and lead to harm. Relevant performance characteristics include: trueness (bias, traceability); precision (repeatability, intermediate precision, reproducibility); analytical specificity; analytical sensitivity; detection limit; quantitation limit; measuring interval.

b) Semi-quantitative examinations provide a clinically useful approximation. Values are assigned on an ordinal scale or reported as a quantity limit. Examples: urine dipsticks, tablets detecting ketones, serological agglutination procedures, microscopic cell counts. Performance characteristics: analytical sensitivity, analytical specificity, detection limit, precision of measured signal values.

c) Qualitative examinations determine presence or absence of an analyte, reported as positive/negative/indeterminate. A positive result when analyte is absent (false positive) or a negative result when analyte is present (false negative) can affect diagnosis or treatment. Performance characteristics include: analytical sensitivity (fraction of true positives in samples containing analyte); analytical specificity (fraction of true negatives in samples not containing analyte); diagnostic sensitivity (fraction of true positives in patients with disease); diagnostic specificity (fraction of true negatives in patients without disease).

H.2.2 與病患安全相關的特性

IVD獨特的「安全特性」類別

ISO 14971附錄A的安全特性問題清單涵蓋一般醫療器材。IVD額外需要考量：

  • 分析性能特性（Analytical performance）：準確度、精密度、特異性、靈敏度等
  • 可靠度特性（Reliability）：系統故障時間、試劑穩定性
  • 數位資訊技術特性（Digital IT）：資料傳輸、結果報告的準確性

False Positive vs. False Negative — 風險的不對稱性

False negative（偽陰性）：病患有病卻被判為無病 → 漏診 → 疾病惡化、錯失治療黃金時機。

False positive（偽陽性）：病患無病卻被判為有病 → 過度診斷 → 不必要的治療（手術、化療）帶來的醫源性傷害（iatrogenic harm）及心理創傷。

兩種錯誤的harm性質不同，嚴重度和可接受標準也可能不同 — 風險分析必須分開評估。

H.2.2.3–H.2.2.4 Reliability & Digital IT Characteristics

H.2.2.3 Reliability Characteristics

When clinicians depend on IVD examination results for urgent medical decisions (e.g. emergency or intensive care settings), timely results can be as important as accurate results. Failure to produce a result when it is needed can delay necessary medical intervention. IVD reliability characteristics include:

  • System reliability (mean time between failures, mean time to failure)
  • Component compatibility (including versions and critical tolerances)
  • Software reliability (error-free operation)
  • Reagent or control stability
  • System usability (avoidance of use errors)

H.2.2.4 Digital Information Technology Characteristics

Correct identification of the patient and sample is clearly essential. Some examinations also require ancillary information about the patient, sample, or examination for proper interpretation. If an IVD is designed to collect, store and report such information, device characteristics leading to data corruption or alteration can contribute to misdiagnosis or inappropriate therapy.

Required ancillary patient information can include: correct patient name and sample identification; patient details (age, gender, population, genetic factors, medications, nutritional state); sample details (type, description, acquisition time); measurement details (procedure, units of measure, measurement uncertainty); application details (cut-off points, reference intervals).

Digital information technology characteristics that can affect patient safety include: wireless/wired connections; internet data transmission; interface with digital applications; applications emulating IVD results; embedded software applications; unshielded data transfer (ESD susceptibility); digital data storage susceptibility; disruption of connected devices.

H.2.2.3–H.2.2.4 可靠度與數位資訊技術特性

時效性（Timeliness）作為安全特性

在急診或加護環境中，「結果能否及時提供」與「結果是否準確」同樣重要。

情境：急性心肌梗塞病患入院，需快速troponin檢測結果以決定是否啟動心導管手術。若系統故障導致結果延遲2小時 → 黃金治療時窗喪失 → 心肌壞死範圍擴大。

因此，IVD器材的可靠度（reliability）本身就是一項安全特性，需納入風險分析。

輔助資訊錯誤的風險

IVD結果的解讀不僅依賴數值本身，還需要正確的輔助資訊（ancillary information）：

  • 病患識別碼錯誤 → 結果歸錯病患 → 甲的檢查結果用於乙的診療
  • 採樣時間錯誤 → 動態監測誤判趨勢
  • Cut-off值使用不當版本 → 定性判斷錯誤

這些資訊錯誤可能比數值本身的誤差更危險，因為臨床醫師往往不會質疑輔助資訊的準確性。

連網IVD器材的資安與安全交叉風險

現代IVD器材大量連網，與附錄F的資安風險在此交會：

  • 數據傳輸被攔截篡改 → 結果錯誤
  • 第三方應用程式仿冒IVD器材發送假結果
  • 軟體配置被惡意竄改 → 系統性錯誤結果（對所有樣本）
  • 智慧型設備OS更新導致應用程式失效 → 結果延遲或無法取得

連網IVD器材需同時遵循資安風險管理（附錄F）與IVD特殊風險管理（附錄H）的要求。

H.2.3 Known and Foreseeable Hazards

H.2.3.1 General Hazard Identification

From the standpoint of the patient, an IVD examination result would be considered a hazard if it could lead to (1) inappropriate medical intervention that can result in harm, or (2) lack of medical intervention necessary to prevent being harmed. General hazards include:

  • a) Incorrect examination result — For quantitative/semi-quantitative examinations, results are considered incorrect if the difference from a correct value exceeds the error limit required for the clinical application. For qualitative examinations (e.g. HIV, pregnancy), results are either correct, incorrect, or indeterminate.
  • b) Delayed examination result — A result is considered delayed if it is needed for a medical decision and the clinician does not receive it in time to support a critical therapeutic or intervention decision.
  • c) Incorrect information accompanying the result — The consequences of an error in ancillary information depend on how the information is used in clinical decision making, and whether the error could cause or contribute to harm.

In addition to hazard identification for the IVD device itself, hazard identification related to connectivity should be evaluated given the increasing use of connected IVD medical devices.

H.2.3.2 Hazards from Fault Conditions

Fault conditions potentially leading to hazards include:

  • Within-batch or batch-to-batch inconsistency (reagents, calibrators, controls)
  • Non-traceable value assignment (calibrators, proficiency materials)
  • Reagent non-specificity (interfering factors, antibodies)
  • Sample or reagent carryover (pipetting instruments)
  • Measurement imprecision (system-level)
  • Unstable materials (during transportation, storage or use)
  • System malfunctions (hardware, software, components, accessories)
  • Digital technology failures (software vulnerability, data transfer errors, ESD, connectivity loss)

H.2.3 已知及可預見的危害

IVD危害的三大類型

故障條件（Fault Conditions）中的CDMO製造風險

批次間不一致性（batch-to-batch inconsistency）直接與CDMO的製造過程相關：

  • 試劑原料批次差異 → 校正值需重新標定
  • 灌裝體積偏差 → 濃度偏差 → 訊號強度不穩定
  • 儲存溫度偏差（運輸鏈溫度失控）→ 試劑提前降解
  • 交叉污染（共用設備清潔不徹底）→ 特異性（specificity）下降

這些製造缺陷可直接轉化為IVD風險管理中的fault conditions，是CDMO品質控制直接影響病患安全的典型路徑。

H.2.3.3–H.2.3.5 Normal Use, Use Errors, and Reasonably Foreseeable Misuse

H.2.3.3 Hazards from Normal Use

Inherent limitations in technology can occasionally lead to general hazards to patients even when all warnings and instructions were followed, the device functioned as intended, and analytical performance met manufacturer's claims. Every examination result is subject to unavoidable sources of variability. Hazards in normal use can include:

  • Inherent false negative and false positive rates of qualitative examinations caused by the uncertainty of statistically assigned cut-off values
  • Measurement uncertainty associated with quantitative examination procedures (performance claims often represent 95% of results within medically defined target limits)
  • Misclassification as "abnormal" or outside "normal" reference interval (based on central 95% of a "normal" population study)
  • Influence of interfering substances (cross-reacting antibodies, certain drugs, biochemical metabolites, sample preparation materials)
  • Biological variability of the analyte itself (heterogeneity of natural proteins, population differences)
  • Chemical properties of the analyte (intrinsic instability, adhesiveness)
  • Variability of the patient sample matrix ("matrix effects")
  • Finite reliability of instrument components

NOTE: Medically defined performance requirements take into account the statistical distribution of examination results in the intended patient populations. The occurrence of a hazardous situation in normal use is considered an unavoidable contribution to the residual risk.

H.2.3.4 Hazards from Use Errors

Use errors in the medical laboratory: overlooking special requirements outside normal routine; performing operations out of sequence (pre-examination/post-examination processes); data entry errors (patient name, ID, birth date, gender). Use errors by patients in self-testing: applying insufficient sample volume; inserting reagent module improperly.

H.2.3.5 Hazards from Reasonably Foreseeable Misuse

Examples include: using device before reading IFU or completing training; collecting inappropriate sample type; reporting results for contraindicated clinical use; using self-testing device in critical care settings; using unverified third-party calibrators/reagents/controls; storing materials incorrectly; operating outside specified environmental conditions; disabling safety features; neglecting prescribed maintenance; malicious intent to create incorrect results.

H.2.3.3–H.2.3.5 正常使用、使用錯誤與可預見誤用

「正常使用下的危害」— 難以迴避的現實

重要觀念：即使器材完全正常運作，IVD的統計本質決定了某些「錯誤」不可避免：

  • 定性測試的cut-off值是統計設定的，必然存在inherent false positive/negative rate
  • 定量測試的precision specification通常涵蓋95%結果，表示5%的結果天然落於規格外
  • 參考區間（reference interval）基於95%「正常」族群，表示5%健康人的結果被標記為「異常」

這些情況在ISO 14971框架下屬於殘餘風險（residual risk），必須在風險管理報告中明確記錄，並透過IFU告知使用者。

使用錯誤 vs. 可預見誤用的區別

Use error（使用錯誤）：使用者本有意正確操作，但因器材設計或流程問題導致錯誤發生。例如：說明書不清晰導致步驟順序錯誤。

Reasonably foreseeable misuse（可合理預見的誤用）：使用者偏離預期用途，但這種偏離基於人類行為是可預測的。例如：自我檢測器材被用於急重症環境（因為「手邊只有這個」）。

兩者都需要在風險分析中識別，並透過設計措施或警告標示來減輕風險。

特別注意：第三方耗材的誤用風險

使用未經驗證的第三方校正品、試劑或控制品，是IVD領域最常見的可預見誤用之一。

CDMO或試劑製造商在IFU中必須明確說明：僅限使用指定耗材，並說明使用其他耗材的風險。監管機構（如FDA 510(k)、EU IVDR）的審查重點之一就是製造商如何應對此類誤用。

H.2.4 Identification of Potential Harms and Their Severity

ISO 14971:2019 requires manufacturers to estimate the risks associated with each identified hazardous situation, based on the probability of occurrence and the severity of possible harm. This requires manufacturers to identify the potential harms (e.g. injuries) to patients with sufficient specificity to assign appropriate severity values.

For some examinations, a single hazardous situation can result in several different harms representing a range of severities. Manufacturers should determine which harms to include in the risk analysis to ensure a high degree of protection of health and safety, and document the rationale. All harms judged reasonably likely to occur should be included. Other harms can be added to the risk analysis if post-production information shows they were experienced.

NOTE: Identifying potential harms for risk analysis and determining their severity and probability of occurrence requires an understanding of the clinical use of the IVD examination results. Participation of qualified medical experts in the risk analysis is essential.

Key questions to help identify and classify potential harms:

  • Is the intended use a major determinant of therapy for a serious condition? What harm might occur from misdiagnosis or inappropriate therapy?
  • Does the intended use involve detection of an infectious disease agent (e.g. hepatitis A or HIV)? Could a false negative result allow the infection to spread to others?
  • Is the intended use to detect an inherited condition (e.g. sickle cell disease, predisposition to Alzheimer's)? Could a false negative allow disease progression? Could a false positive lead to unnecessary intervention?
  • Is the intended use to predict drug or device effectiveness? Could a false negative cause loss of therapeutic benefit? Could a false positive have harmful consequences?
  • Is the intended use to screen transfusion or transplant donors? Could incorrect results cause disease transmission to recipients or rejection of functioning organ?
  • Is the intended use to monitor a critical body function? What harms might occur from an incorrect result?

H.2.4 潛在傷害的識別與嚴重度評估

臨床場景驅動的危害識別

IVD風險分析中最核心的挑戰：製造商需要像臨床醫師一樣思考 — 「如果這個結果是錯的，病患會怎樣？」

因此，ISO/TR 24971強調：具資格的醫療專家參與IVD風險分析是必要的（essential），而非可選的。

IVD風險分析實務步驟

  1. 明確器材的分析用途（analyte, specimen type, method）和臨床用途（適應症、病患族群）
  2. 邀請臨床專家（相關科別醫師）參與危害識別工作坊
  3. 對每一種可能的結果錯誤類型（偽陰性、偽陽性、延遲、數值錯誤）分別識別潛在危害
  4. 評估嚴重度時考量：多個possible harms（同一危險情況可能導致不同嚴重程度的危害），選取最可能且最嚴重者納入分析
  5. 確保殘餘風險資訊完整揭露於IFU，並透過警語（warnings）和注意事項（precautions）指導臨床使用

Key Takeaways — Annexes F, G & H (Part 1)

Annex F: Security Risks

  • ISO 14971's risk management process applies equally to security risks. No separate framework is needed.
  • Security (protecting information systems from hostile acts) is not the same level as safety (freedom from unacceptable harm), but security failures can trigger safety hazards.
  • The CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) maps to ISO 14971's harm framework. Each element's compromise can lead to patient harm depending on device intended use.
  • Security risk control measures can introduce new safety risks (and vice versa) — these interactions must be evaluated per ISO 14971.
  • Prioritization of CIA depends on intended use — there is no universal priority order.

Annex G: Legacy Devices

  • Many devices were designed before ISO 14971 or without following its requirements. This is a common gap in acquired products and outsourced manufacturing.
  • Available post-market data (complaints, incident reports, surveillance data) can form the basis for building an initial risk management file.
  • The seven-step retrospective approach enables manufacturers to systematically identify existing risk controls, document traceability, and address uncontrolled risks.

Annex H Part 1: IVD Devices

  • IVD risks to patients are indirect — wrong or delayed results lead to wrong clinical decisions, which lead to harm.
  • Analytical performance characteristics (trueness, precision, sensitivity, specificity) are safety characteristics under ISO 14971.
  • Three harm types: incorrect result, delayed result, incorrect ancillary information.
  • Normal use contains unavoidable residual risks due to statistical nature of IVD testing — these must be disclosed in safety information.
  • Qualified medical experts must participate in IVD risk analysis.

重點總結 — 附錄F、G及H前半

附錄F：資安風險

  • 資安風險無需另建流程 — ISO 14971的風險管理框架直接適用。
  • Security ≠ Safety，但security失效可導致safety hazard，兩者在ISO 14971框架下交會。
  • CIA三要素（保密性、完整性、可用性）的優先排序取決於器材的預期用途，無固定順序。
  • 資安控制措施可能引入新的安全風險（如密碼保護延誤急救）— 必須評估次生風險。

附錄G：舊型器材

  • Legacy device缺乏ISO 14971文件是常見現象，特別是併購取得的產品線或接手他廠的CMO製造。
  • 不需從零開始 — 利用上市後資料回溯建立初始風險管理文件。
  • 七步驟方法：記錄用途→盤點現有控制→識別危害→找出未受控危害→建立追溯矩陣→整體評估→審查報告。
  • CDMO接手既有產品時，應要求gap assessment以確認ISO 14971合規狀態。

附錄H前半：IVD器材

  • IVD的獨特性：風險來自「錯誤結果→錯誤決策→病患傷害」的間接路徑。
  • 分析性能特性（準確度、精密度、靈敏度、特異度）= 安全特性，需納入風險分析。
  • 三類危害：結果錯誤、結果延遲、輔助資訊錯誤。
  • 正常使用下的殘餘風險（統計性誤差）不可消除，必須透過IFU揭露。
  • 臨床醫療專家參與IVD風險分析是必要條件，不能僅靠工程師或法規人員完成。
  • CDMO填充IVD試劑時，批次一致性和試劑穩定性直接影響器材的分析性能安全特性。

H.2.6 Identification of IVD Sequences of Events Leading to Hazardous Situations
識別導致危險情況的IVD事件序列

H.2.6.1 General Considerations

Identification and analysis of reasonably foreseeable sequences or combinations of events that can lead to a hazardous situation is necessary to estimate the probability that harm would occur. Understanding these activities and events also helps the manufacturer select risk controls and identify opportunities for risk reduction.

The specific sequences to be analysed depend on the particular IVD medical device and its intended use. The manufacturer should consider the knowledge, skills, and abilities of intended users, the use environment, and any events and circumstances that could increase or decrease risk.

H.2.6.2 Description of the Sequence of Events

Risk scenarios for IVD medical devices typically involve longer sequences of events than for other medical devices. They include activities not under the direct control of the manufacturer, such as those performed by medical laboratory users and clinical decision-making by clinicians who ordered the examination results.

The description should start with the initiating event (typically the cause of the hazard being analysed) and progress logically through foreseeable decisions and actions of device users, ending with clinical decisions and actions leading to each identified foreseeable outcome.

Three categories of events define the sequence:

  • a) Events under control of the manufacturer — quality management system failures; design and development faults; device labelling errors; manufacturing and supplier management issues; distribution, servicing, or training failures
  • b) Events under control of the user of the IVD medical device — selection, collection, processing and storage of patient samples; system installation and setup; calibration activities and metrological traceability; quality assurance (QC, proficiency testing, delta checks); communication with clinicians; contingency and recovery planning
  • c) Events under control of the clinician — receipt and review of examination result; comparison to expected values; request for confirmatory examinations; clinical diagnosis; therapy initiation or withholding
NOTE: Clinical decision making is specifically excluded from the scope of ISO 14971:2019. This refers to clinical decisions whether to use a particular medical device or IVD medical device — not to clinical decisions affected by incorrect or delayed IVD results.

關鍵概念：IVD風險不止於儀器故障

一般醫療器械的風險分析可能只需考慮設備本身的故障模式。但IVD的風險序列橫跨三個控制域，製造商只能直接管控第一層。

  • 第一層（製造商控制）：設計缺陷、標籤錯誤、製程不穩定 — 製造商完全責任
  • 第二層（實驗室/使用者控制）：樣品採集、QC操作、結果回報 — 製造商透過標籤與培訓間接影響
  • 第三層（臨床醫師控制）：診斷決策、治療選擇 — 超出ISO 14971管轄範圍

CDMO視角：若你為IVD試劑盒做代工，你的風險分析必須延伸到「錯誤結果傳到醫師手中」的那一刻，即使實驗室操作不在你的直接控制之下。

類比：血糖試片的完整風險鏈

想像你製造家用血糖試片。風險鏈如下：

  1. 試片批次校準值偏高（製造商控制層）
  2. 患者在過期後使用試片（使用者控制層）
  3. 血糖顯示偏高，患者注射額外胰島素（臨床決策層）
  4. 患者陷入低血糖昏迷（傷害 Harm）

在這條鏈中，製造商的風險控制著力點主要在第1步，但也必須透過IFU警告、效期標示與校準訓練延伸影響第2步。

實驗室獨立驗錯機制的重要性

H.2.6.1 特別提醒：醫學實驗室有流程設計用來偵測錯誤，但隨機性故障（sporadic random failures）特別難被偵測。如果一個錯誤結果看起來「合理」，有經驗的臨床醫師也可能接受它並據此做出治療決定。

對CDMO而言：這意味著即使實驗室QC流程存在，也不能作為減少製造端風險的理由。製造品質本身就是第一道防線。

H.2.7 Estimation of the Probability of Occurrence of Harm
傷害發生概率之估算

H.2.7.1 General Considerations

The risk analysis should consider the entire sequence of events. The probability of a patient being harmed is the combined probability of each event in the sequence. For short sequences (e.g., self-testing, point-of-care devices), a straightforward cross-functional team approach using expert judgment is appropriate. For complex sequences with extended chains of events, segmenting the analysis at the hazardous situation makes more efficient use of expert resources.

H.2.7.2 The P1 × P2 Approach

For complex scenarios where a sequence of events extends from an incorrect result beyond the medical laboratory to a clinician's decisions and actions, the approach illustrated in Figure C.1 of ISO 14971:2019 can be divided into two probability segments:

  • P1 = probability that the hazardous situation would occur (i.e., incorrect result reported to the clinician). Estimated by experts familiar with device design, construction, use, and the laboratory environment.
  • P2 = probability that harm would result from that hazardous situation. Estimated by experts familiar with the medical use of IVD results, using clinical judgment, adverse event data, and medical literature.
  • Overall probability of harm = P1 × P2

This approach allows efficient division of a complex analysis between technical experts (P1) and clinical experts (P2), and enables updates when either segment changes independently (e.g., design change affecting P1 only).

H.3 Risk Control for IVD Medical Devices
IVD醫療器械的風險控制

H.3.1 General

Since the decisions and actions of clinicians are largely beyond any reasonable means of control by the manufacturer, safety activities should focus on reducing the probability of events under the control of the manufacturer. Risk control measures are primarily directed at reducing probability P1.

H.3.2 Inherently Safe Design and Manufacture

Risks are generally reduced by lowering the probability that incorrect results will be reported or that clinically significant delays will occur. Examples of design features controlling accuracy and reliability include:

  • Trueness of calibrator values (e.g., traceability to recognized reference standards; per ISO 17511)
  • Measurement uncertainty (precision of measuring system)
  • Analytical specificity of IVD reagents (optimized components)
  • Detection limit / quantitation limit (improved measurement technology)
  • Reliability of the instrument (minimize hardware/software failures)
  • Discrimination between positive and negative samples (robust cut-off values)
  • Elimination of mistake-prone steps (automation, mistake-proofing)
  • Component version traceability and positive sample identification (bar-coding)
  • Software functionality (coding standards)
  • Reduced reagent or calibrator variability (lot-to-lot specifications)
  • Stability of reagents, calibrators, or control materials (microbiological control)

Examples of detection features in the IVD medical device or reagent kit:

  • Liquid level sensors (detect "short draws")
  • Fault detection systems (spectrophotometer drift, inadequate temperature control)
  • Sample quality checks (haemolysis, icterus, lipemia)
  • Built-in controls to verify correct calibrator or reagent lots (bar codes)
  • Alarms and error messages for fault conditions and recovery procedures
  • Incoming inspections of supplied components; in-process and final-product acceptance testing

H.3.3 Protective Measures Incorporated in the Manufacturing Process

Standards with potential relevance to IVD risk control include:

  • ISO 15197 — self-monitoring blood glucose devices
  • ISO 17593 — oral anticoagulation monitoring (requires validated training program)
  • ISO 17511 — metrological traceability of IVD calibrator values
  • ISO 17822-1 — nucleic acid-based detection systems
  • ISO 20776 — antimicrobial susceptibility testing
  • IEC 61010-2-101 — safety requirements for IVD equipment
  • IEC 80001-1 — risk management for networked medical devices

H.3.4 Information for Safety

Information for safety provided to IVD users can prevent occurrence of a hazard or hazardous situation, provided that: (1) it instructs users what actions to take or avoid; (2) intended users are capable of following the instructions; (3) it can be reasonably expected that they will follow them. Examples include warnings and instructions addressing:

  • Chemical or biological hazards associated with the device
  • Contraindicated medical conditions or clinical applications
  • Sample collection, storage, and preparation
  • Identification of inappropriate sample types
  • Interfering substances detectable by the user (e.g., visible haemolysis)
  • Incompatible system components and accessories
  • Validated measuring intervals and dilution instructions
  • Biological reference intervals and medical decision points
  • Validated cleaning methods for reusable items
  • Backup and recovery in case of system failure

H.3.6 User Education and Training

For complex user interfaces, difficult examination procedures, or critical clinical applications, information for safety can take the form of training and education programs. ISO 17593 (oral anticoagulation monitoring) specifically requires the manufacturer to provide a validated training program for clinicians and an education program for patients using these devices at home.

Three Tiers of IVD Risk Control

風險控制優先層次（同一般醫療器械，但IVD特色鮮明）

ISO 14971的風險控制層次對IVD完全適用，但每一層的具體措施有IVD的獨特性：

  • 第一層：固有安全設計 — 試劑配方的分析特異性、校準值的計量溯源性、儀器的內建偵錯系統。這些措施直接消除或降低P1。
  • 第二層：保護措施 — 液位感應器、樣品品質偵測（溶血偵測）、條碼核對系統。這些是製造端的工程防護，獨立於用戶行為。
  • 第三層：安全資訊 — IFU中的警告、干擾物質清單、樣品類型限制。只有當前兩層無法完全控制風險時才使用。

注意：H.3.4特別提醒，建議實驗室用戶執行的QC檢測、確認性測試或緊急值通知，屬於「安全資訊」，而非「保護措施」。

CDMO IVD代工的四大製造風險

在CDMO廠房中，IVD試劑盒的製造風險直接對應到H.3.2的設計特性：

  • 試劑穩定性：配方或儲存條件不當 → 批次效期縮短 → 校準值漂移 → 患者結果偏差
  • 分裝精準度：填充量偏差 → 反應物比例異常 → 假陰性/假陽性
  • 容器完整性：密封不良 → 水分滲入 → 試劑降解 → 靈敏度下降
  • 污染控制：交叉污染 → 特異性下降 → 假陽性（尤其核酸試劑）

這四項都直接影響P1，是CDMO的核心品質控制點。

計量溯源性（Metrological Traceability）：IVD準確性的基礎

H.3.2和H.3.5多次強調計量溯源性。ISO 17511定義了建立IVD校準值溯源到高階參考物質的流程。JCTLM（Joint Committee for Traceability in Laboratory Medicine）維護一個受認可的參考測量系統數據庫。

對CDMO：若你代工的IVD試劑使用了客戶指定的校準品，你需要確認該校準品是否已建立符合ISO 17511的溯源鏈。這是風險分析中「trueness of calibrator values」的文件基礎。

安全資訊的侷限：資訊不一定到達臨床醫師

H.3.6末尾指出三個安全資訊的現實限制：

  • 不同地區用戶的能力和使用環境差異極大
  • 品質控制與品質保證的實踐在全球各地並不統一
  • 提供給實驗室用戶的禁忌症和干擾藥物資訊，不一定能到達開具檢驗單的臨床醫師

這意味著「提供安全資訊」的風險控制效力有上限，不能過度依賴，必須優先依靠設計端的固有安全措施。

H.4–H.6 Benefit-Risk Analysis, Residual Risk Disclosure & Post-Production
效益-風險分析、殘餘風險揭露與上市後管理

H.4 Benefit-Risk Analysis

Clause 7.4 provides guidance for performing a benefit-risk analysis for IVD medical devices. Where it is not possible to describe the benefits of an IVD directly, surrogate endpoints can be established. Examples include:

  • Ability to identify a specific disease
  • Provide diagnosis at different stages of a disease
  • Predict future disease onset
  • Identify patients likely to respond to a given therapy

H.5 Disclosure of Residual Risks

Disclosure of residual risks in the accompanying documentation of an IVD medical device can take different forms:

  • Performance specifications ("claims") — defining the analytically and clinically verified boundaries of device performance
  • Limitations of the risk control or examination procedure — situations where the device might not perform as intended (interfering substances, specific patient populations, values outside measuring interval, unvalidated sample types)
  • Potential causes of hazardous situations that could not be eliminated by the manufacturer

Per ISO 18113-1, analytical and clinical limitations must be disclosed in the accompanying documentation. Results of performance evaluations conducted in actual or simulated use conditions can be summarized therein.

Some events considered general laboratory knowledge (e.g., equipment occasional failure, contingency planning requirements) need not be exhaustively listed, to avoid overwhelming users with unnecessary details.

H.6 Production and Post-Production Activities

Establishing an effective post-production monitoring system for IVD medical devices is challenging because reliable data on the actual frequency of hazardous situations and harm can be difficult to obtain. Monitoring use errors and device failures is more straightforward since this feedback can be provided directly by device users.

Recommended sources for post-production surveillance:

  • Internal: Production data from release testing, value assignment activities, stability monitoring, product failure investigations
  • External: External QC and proficiency testing programs, user performance evaluations, instrument installation and servicing activities
  • Adverse event databases: Regulatory authority databases, public institute reports, national medical laboratory associations, medical literature

H.6.2 specifically notes the importance of monitoring analytical performance data. A classification and coding scheme for anticipated harms, hazardous situations, and their causes can facilitate harm-based prioritization of investigations for complaint handling and post-market analysis.

IVD的「效益」如何量化？

一般治療性器械的效益可以是「降低死亡率X%」或「縮短住院天數Y天」。但IVD的效益通常是間接的——它提供資訊，讓醫師做出更好的決策。

H.4因此引入「替代終點（surrogate endpoints）」概念：

  • 直接效益難以量化時：改用「偵測特定疾病的能力（靈敏度/特異性）」作為代理指標
  • 範例：一個HbA1c測試的效益可以用「正確診斷糖尿病的概率」來表述，而不需要追蹤到「患者5年後的糖尿病控制結果」

這對監管申請中的效益宣稱寫法有直接影響，EU IVDR 2017/746要求明確陳述臨床效益。

殘餘風險揭露 vs. 安全資訊：區別何在？

H.5.1特別強調：殘餘風險的揭露是在安全資訊之外額外進行的。

  • 安全資訊（IFU中的警告）：「避免使用溶血樣品」→ 這是告訴用戶如何操作以降低風險
  • 殘餘風險揭露（規格聲明中的限制）：「血紅素濃度>10 g/L時結果可能偏低20%」→ 這是告訴用戶即使正確操作，仍有哪些性能邊界

對CDMO：在代工產品的技術文件審查中，確認客戶已區分這兩類文件，並確保殘餘風險揭露與風險管理檔案中的殘餘風險評估結果一致。

IVD上市後監控的特殊挑戰

H.6.3揭示一個IVD特有的困境：醫學實驗室通常不知道一個回報的結果是錯誤的，除非後來收到臨床醫師的投訴。也就是說，「沒有投訴」不等於「沒有問題」。

解決策略：

  • 參加外部能力測試（proficiency testing programs）— 可以客觀評估系統性偏差
  • 主動監控類似產品的不良事件資料庫
  • 建立傷害分類與編碼系統，在接收到投訴時快速判斷嚴重性

H.7 Examples of Risk Scenarios for IVD Medical Devices
IVD風險場景案例

H.7.2 Automated Medical Laboratory Analyser: Incorrect Examination Result

Scenario: A clinician orders an IVD examination from the central medical laboratory. The result from the analyser is incorrect and reported to the clinician. Sequence of events:

  1. Initiating event occurs (device fault or use error)
  2. IVD produces a clinically incorrect examination result (hazard)
  3. Device user fails to detect the incorrect result or its cause
  4. Device user reports the incorrect result to the clinician (hazardous situation)
  5. Clinician does not identify the result as incorrect
  6. Incorrect result misleads clinician to a misdiagnosis
  7. Clinician inappropriately intervenes / does not intervene
  8. Patient is injured by the clinician's intervention/non-intervention (harm)

The hazardous situation is defined as the point when the incorrect result is received by the clinician, separating the analytical segment (P1) from the clinical segment (P2). This definition enables efficient parallel analysis by technical and clinical expert teams.

H.7.3 Personal (Self-Testing) Device: Incorrect Classification of Glycaemic Status

Scenario: A patient with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus monitors blood glucose and self-administers anti-glycaemic drug when results indicate hyperglycaemia. Although actually hypoglycaemic, the device gave an elevated result, causing the patient to become further hypoglycaemic. Sequence of events:

  1. Initiating event (device fault or use error)
  2. Personal IVD produces clinically incorrect glucose result (hazard)
  3. Patient does not identify the result as incorrect
  4. Incorrect result misleads patient to inappropriate therapeutic decision (hazardous situation)
  5. Patient administers anti-glycaemic therapy
  6. Patient becomes significantly hypoglycaemic (harm)

In this case, there is no practical advantage to segmenting the sequence of events. The entire analysis can be performed efficiently by a single cross-functional team of technical and medical specialists.

H.7.4 Point-of-Care IVD Device: Critical Result Delayed

Scenario: A patient suspected of internal injuries is treated in an urgent care facility. The IVD device displayed an error message, and the result was unavailable when needed for emergency intervention decision. Sequence of events:

  1. Initiating event (device fault or use error)
  2. IVD fails to produce a clinically necessary examination result (hazard)
  3. Device user cannot repeat examination within required timeframe
  4. Result not available to clinician to support intervention decision (hazardous situation)
  5. Clinician takes critical decision/action without benefit of examination result
  6. Clinician's decision/action is not appropriate for patient's condition
  7. Patient is injured by clinician's action/inaction (harm)

The hazardous situation is defined as the event when the expected result was not received at the time it was needed. The risk analysis can consider analysing the entire sequence as a whole or dividing into segments based on suitability for an objective analysis.

ISO/TR 24971書目的邊界提醒

書目中有幾個值得注意的缺口：

  • EU MDR 2017/745 / IVDR 2017/746 沒有直接引用（因為ISO TR是國際標準，不引用特定地區法規）。但在實務上，這兩個法規是在歐洲使用ISO 14971的主要驅動力。

五個不能忘的IVD風險管理原則

  1. 風險序列橫跨三層：製造商、實驗室、臨床醫師 — 製造商只能直接管控第一層，但必須分析整條鏈。
  2. P1×P2分段估算：讓技術專家估算P1（錯誤產生的概率），讓臨床專家估算P2（錯誤導致傷害的概率），兩者相乘得最終風險。
  3. 危險情況的精確定義決定分析效率：對中央實驗室IVD，「錯誤結果傳到臨床醫師」是分段點；對自我測試，整條鏈由單一團隊分析。
  4. 計量溯源性是IVD準確性的基石：ISO 17511的溯源鏈確保你的校準品值可追溯到國際參考標準，這是降低P1最有效的技術手段之一。
  5. 標準是一個互連生態系統：ISO 14971是中心，ISO 13485是QMS框架，ISO 10993-1是生物安全輸入，ISO 11608-1是注射系統輸入，IEC 62304是軟體輸入 — 它們共同構成一個完整的技術文件包。

給CDMO同仁的行動建議

學完ISO/TR 24971全部Annex H和Bibliography，最重要的行動點是：

  • 建立自己公司的「標準矩陣」：哪些標準適用於你代工的哪類產品？對應哪個法規要求？負責人是誰？
  • 為IVD代工建立專用FMEA模板：明確包含三層事件序列（製造商/實驗室/臨床），並標注P1與P2的分界點。
  • 將ISO 23640穩定性研究與風險管理文件掛鉤：試劑穩定性數據不只是放行測試，它是風險分析中「reagent degradation」危害的降低措施的驗證證據。
