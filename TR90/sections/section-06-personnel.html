8.0 Personnel Training and Qualification

Original Text

Before individuals can enter manufacturing areas, they should have received appropriate training in a standard curriculum for manufacturing personnel. The curriculum should be designed to the role the trainee will be expected to perform. Personnel training and qualification programs should include at a minimum an explanation of the underlying principles and rationale for the controls regarding:

  • Basic Microbiology (see Section 8.1)
  • Personnel, personnel flow, and associated requirements (see Section 5.2; Section 15.0, Table 6)
  • Material and waste flow (see Section 5.3; Section 15.0, Table 4)
  • Environmental controls (see Section 7.0)
  • Cleaning and Disinfection (see Section 5.1.4)
  • Process design (see Section 4.0)

A key element of a microbial contamination control strategy is a routinely scheduled, validated cleaning and disinfection process. The microbiology training program should encompass a clear explanation of the difference between cleaning and disinfection in that the former is purposed to remove physical and chemical residues and the latter is purposed to kill microorganisms. The training program should include an overview of the different cleaning and disinfectant agents and why each are used.

Basic microbiology, personal hygiene, gowning, and cleanroom behavior are core topics within a robust contamination control training program. Operators performing activities in the aseptic processing areas should have additional aseptic operator training and meet qualification requirements, including reviewing the airflow visualization studies. Table 8.0-1 highlights the mindset shift toward holistic CCS relating to personnel.

Table 8.0-1 — Holistic Approach to Personnel Considerations
Non-Holistic Approach Holistic Approach
Contamination cannot occur if SOP instructions and batch records are precisely followed by personnel Contamination can be prevented by understanding and reducing the specific hazards throughout the process, through a combination of strong CCS practices, quality culture, and continuous improvement

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Personnel Training and Qualification (人員訓練與資格確認)：這是 CCS 中針對「人」這個最大污染源的系統性管控機制。TR90 要求在人員進入製造區域之前，必須完成一套涵蓋微生物學、個人衛生、更衣、潔淨室行為等核心主題的標準課程。

Cleaning vs. Disinfection (清潔 vs. 消毒)：清潔的目的是去除物理和化學殘留物；消毒的目的是殺死微生物。訓練計畫必須清楚區分這兩個概念，因為許多操作員容易混淆。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像一家醫院的感染管控計畫 -- 在醫護人員進入手術室之前，他們不僅需要知道「怎麼做」(SOP)，更需要理解「為什麼這樣做」。一位只會機械式遵循步驟的護士，在遇到異常狀況時可能不知如何應變；而一位真正理解微生物傳播原理的護士，即使在非標準情境下也能做出正確判斷。這就是 Table 8.0-1 中「非全面性」與「全面性」方法的核心差異。

重點提示 Key Notes

思維轉變的關鍵：傳統觀念認為「只要精確遵循 SOP 和批次記錄，就不會發生污染」。但全面性方法認為，光遵循程序是不夠的 -- 人員必須理解整個製程中的具體危害，並結合 CCS 實務、品質文化和持續改善來預防污染。

訓練課程應「依角色設計」(designed to the role)，這意味著不同職位的人員接受不同深度的訓練，而非一體適用。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 環境中，由於客戶產品多樣、製程頻繁轉換，人員訓練的挑戰更大。一個有效的做法是建立「模組化訓練矩陣」：基礎模組 (微生物學、衛生、更衣) 為所有人員必修，而進階模組 (無菌操作、特定製程) 則依角色選配。這樣既確保全面覆蓋，又能靈活適應不同產品線的需求。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  2. 在你的工廠中，清潔 (cleaning) 和消毒 (disinfection) 的訓練是否有清楚區分？操作員能否正確解釋兩者的差異？

8.1 Basic Microbiology

Original Text

Microbiology is the science and study of microorganisms. Microorganisms can potentially impact pharmaceutical facilities, manufacturing processes, and finished drug products. An education program of basic microbiology should be integrated into the employee training that explains the various types of microorganisms (minimally, bacteria, yeasts, and molds). This would describe how microorganisms may cause an impact through contamination, including proliferation, and their potential to release toxins (including endotoxins), and why this can impact patient safety based on the types of finished drug products, patient population and the route of drug administration.

The program should explain the main sources of microorganisms, including people, utilities (e.g., water, gases), the environment, and raw materials. A comprehensive microbiology training program would also include a description of viruses and prions, their origins, their sources, and how and where they might impact the manufacturing process and finished drug product quality. This should also include any requirements for TSE and BSE statements for all materials that pose a potential risk of contamination from prions.

When employees understand the sources and potential consequences of contamination from microorganisms, they are more capable to prevent contaminations and build practices that support a proactive quality culture related to contamination control.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Basic Microbiology (基礎微生物學)：微生物學訓練的最低要求涵蓋三大類微生物：

  • Bacteria (細菌)：最常見的污染源，可快速增殖並釋放內毒素 (endotoxins)
  • Yeasts (酵母菌)：真菌的一種，常見於環境和人體
  • Molds (黴菌)：真菌的另一種，可透過孢子在空氣中傳播

進階訓練還應涵蓋 Viruses (病毒) 和 Prions (朊病毒)，後者與 TSE (傳染性海綿狀腦病) 和 BSE (牛海綿狀腦病) 相關。

比喻說明 Analogy

微生物學訓練就像教駕訓班學員認識各種道路危害 -- 你不能只告訴他們「紅燈停、綠燈行」，你還需要讓他們了解為什麼闖紅燈危險（交通事故的後果）、道路上有哪些潛在危險（行人、腳踏車、施工區），以及在不同情境下如何正確判斷。當操作員真正理解微生物的來源和危害，他們就會從「被動遵守規則」轉變為「主動預防污染」。

重點提示 Key Notes

四大微生物來源：People (人員)、Utilities (公用系統，如水、氣體)、Environment (環境)、Raw Materials (原料)。記住這四個來源是理解整個 CCS 的基礎。

Proactive Quality Culture (主動品質文化)：TR90 明確指出，當員工理解污染的來源和後果時，他們更有能力主動預防污染。這不只是知識傳授，而是文化建設。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼微生物學訓練需要涵蓋「給藥途徑」(route of drug administration) 和「病患族群」(patient population) 的概念？
  2. 在你的 CDMO 工廠中，是否有針對 TSE/BSE 風險的物料管理程序？這如何整合到訓練計畫中？

8.2 Personal Hygiene

Original Text

The training program should explain how people and their actions are recognized as one of the greatest sources of microbiological contamination within the pharmaceutical industry. Therefore, the program should stress the requirements for maintaining a high standard of personal hygiene and how to effectively perform handwashing. The topic of hygiene should list health conditions that would restrict personnel from operating in a cleanroom.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Personal Hygiene (個人衛生)：TR90 明確指出「人和他們的行為」是藥品產業中微生物污染的最大來源之一。訓練計畫必須涵蓋：

  • 維持高標準個人衛生的要求
  • 有效的 Handwashing (洗手) 技術
  • 限制人員進入潔淨室的 Health Conditions (健康狀況) 清單

比喻說明 Analogy

就像餐廳的廚師必須遵守嚴格的衛生標準一樣 -- 你不會希望一位感冒的廚師在開放式廚房裡為你準備壽司。同樣地，一位有傳染性疾病或皮膚傷口的操作員，在潔淨室中會成為微生物污染的活躍源頭。Health condition restrictions (健康狀況限制) 不是歧視，而是保護患者安全的必要措施。

重點提示 Key Notes

人是最大的污染源：這是整個 CCS 中最重要的認知之一。一個正常的人每分鐘可以釋放數千到數百萬個微粒，而不良的衛生習慣會大幅增加這個數字。因此，個人衛生訓練不是形式，而是污染控制的第一道防線。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠中有哪些健康狀況會限制人員進入潔淨室？這份清單是否涵蓋了皮膚病、呼吸道感染、腸胃疾病等？
  2. 洗手程序是否經過驗證？操作員是否定期接受洗手技術的評估？

8.3 Gowning

Original Text

Appropriate garments and the process of gowning provides a barrier between personnel and the cleanroom and, therefore, it should be covered in detail within a training program. The program should detail observing wear and tear, use of non-shedding materials, number of uses, frequency of changes, sterilization program, etc. A description of the types and layers of garments to be worn and an explanation of how gowning prevents contamination are necessary components. Personnel assigned to execute cleaning and disinfection SOPs should regown prior to conducting aseptic operations in critical areas. Importantly, instructions for the process of gowning that would permit successful qualification of personnel in gowning should be described explicitly.

For detailed gowning requirements, refer to EU GMP Annex 1 Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products and PDA Points to Consider for Aseptic Processing: Part 1 (2015).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Gowning (更衣)：更衣是在人員和潔淨室之間建立物理屏障的關鍵程序。訓練計畫應詳細涵蓋：

  • Wear and Tear (磨損檢查)：使用前檢查衣物完整性
  • Non-shedding Materials (不脫落材質)：確保衣物本身不會成為微粒來源
  • 使用次數與更換頻率：明確規定每件衣物的使用壽命
  • Sterilization Program (滅菌計畫)：衣物的滅菌週期與方法
  • 衣物層次與類型：不同潔淨度等級對應不同的穿著要求

比喻說明 Analogy

更衣程序就像太空人穿太空衣 -- 每一個步驟都有其意義，每一層都提供特定的保護功能。如果太空衣有一個小洞，就可能危及太空人的生命。同樣地，如果潔淨室衣物有破損或穿著不當，就可能讓微生物突破這道屏障，威脅到產品和患者的安全。

重點提示 Key Notes

清潔消毒後需重新更衣：TR90 特別強調，執行清潔和消毒 SOP 的人員在進行關鍵區域的無菌操作之前，必須重新更衣 (regown)。這是因為清潔消毒過程中衣物可能已被污染。

資格確認導向的訓練：更衣指導必須足夠明確，使人員能夠成功通過更衣資格確認。這意味著訓練不只是「示範」，而是要有清楚的步驟說明和評估標準。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠對更衣衣物的使用次數和洗滌/滅菌週期是否有明確規定？這些規定是否基於數據驗證？
  2. 執行清潔消毒的人員是否在進入無菌操作前重新更衣？如果沒有，這會帶來什麼風險？

8.3.1 Gowning Qualification

Original Text

Gowning qualification is the demonstration that a person is capable of consistently donning cleanroom garments and maintaining the barrier properties to ensure the exterior of the garment is not contaminated with microorganisms. Qualification should include multiple observations of the person donning the gown and taking multiple samples from the exterior of the garment after gowning to demonstrate the presence or absence of any microorganisms. Sample sites of the gown should be determined by a risk assessment that considers the specific operation. A documented rationale should explain the acceptance criteria for the personnel gowning qualification process. Personnel are prohibited from Grade A and Grade B cleanroom entry without having successfully completed gowning qualification.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Gowning Qualification (更衣資格確認)：這是一項正式的評估程序，用以證明人員能夠一致地、正確地穿戴潔淨室衣物並維持其屏障特性。關鍵要素包括：

  • Multiple Observations (多次觀察)：不是一次就通過，需要多次觀察確認一致性
  • Multiple Samples (多點取樣)：從衣物外表面多個位置取樣進行微生物檢測
  • Risk Assessment (風險評估)：取樣位置應基於特定操作的風險評估來決定
  • Acceptance Criteria (接受標準)：必須有書面記錄的合格標準及其理由

重點提示 Key Notes

Grade A 和 Grade B 的強制要求：TR90 明確規定，未成功完成更衣資格確認的人員「禁止」進入 Grade A 和 Grade B 潔淨室。這是一項不可妥協的要求。

取樣位置的科學依據：並非隨機取樣，而是根據風險評估決定。例如，前臂和胸部通常是高風險區域，因為它們最接近產品和關鍵操作面。

比喻說明 Analogy

更衣資格確認就像飛行員的技能檢定 -- 你不能只看一次降落就認定飛行員合格。考官需要多次觀察不同條件下的表現，確認其操作的一致性和可靠性。同樣地，更衣的一致性才是真正的品質保證 -- 一個人在壓力下、趕時間時是否仍然能正確更衣，這才是資格確認要驗證的能力。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠更衣資格確認要求多少次連續成功的觀察？取樣位置是否基於書面的風險評估？
  2. 如果一位資深操作員在更衣資格確認中失敗，你的程序中是否有明確的處置流程？

8.3.2 Gowning Disqualification and Requalification

Original Text

A documented program should also be established to provide the criteria that disqualifies personnel from cleanroom entry based on insufficient gowning technique and/or exceeding established microbial counts and/or trends from personnel monitoring. A matrix approach should be considered in developing the plan for disqualification, such as multiple alert-level excursions in a prescribed period, multiple action-level deviations in a prescribed period, or a combination of both. Operators can also be disqualified for observation of poor aseptic/cleanroom behavior.

If disqualified, a return to accessing cleanrooms would be contingent upon appropriate corrective actions being taken and successful completion of qualification (see Section 8.3.1). Additionally, requalification of gowning should be performed routinely on an annual basis or return from a long leave / extended absence for personnel to demonstrate their continued capability in gowning.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Disqualification (取消資格)：當人員的更衣技術不足或超出微生物計數標準時，應取消其潔淨室准入資格。TR90 建議使用 Matrix Approach (矩陣方法) 來建立取消資格的標準，例如：

  • 在特定期間內多次 Alert-level Excursions (警戒值偏離)
  • 在特定期間內多次 Action-level Deviations (行動值偏差)
  • 上述兩者的組合
  • 觀察到不良的無菌/潔淨室行為

Requalification (重新確認)：應定期（年度）執行，或在人員長假/長時間缺勤後執行。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像駕照的扣點制度 -- 不是一次違規就吊銷駕照，而是根據違規的嚴重程度和頻率來做累計判斷。Alert-level 就像輕微違規扣 1 點，Action-level 就像嚴重違規扣 3 點。當累計到一定程度，就暫時「吊銷」潔淨室准入資格，必須重新訓練和考核後才能恢復。年度重新確認則像定期體檢 -- 即使沒有問題，也需要定期確認能力是否維持。

重點提示 Key Notes

矩陣方法的優勢：使用矩陣方法可以避免「一刀切」的判斷 -- 單次偏離可能是偶發事件，但短期內多次偏離則可能反映系統性問題。這種分層次的評估方式更加科學合理。

恢復資格的前提：取消資格後，人員必須 (1) 採取適當的矯正措施，且 (2) 成功完成資格確認，才能恢復潔淨室准入權。兩個條件缺一不可。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 工廠中，由於人員流動性較高，建議建立一套電子化的人員資格追蹤系統，自動整合環境監控數據和人員監控結果。當觸發取消資格標準時，系統自動鎖定門禁權限並通知相關主管。這樣可以確保即時反應，避免人為疏忽導致未合格人員進入潔淨室。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠目前使用矩陣方法來評估人員取消資格嗎？矩陣中包含哪些觸發條件？
  2. 年度重新確認的執行率是否達到 100%？對於因排班或其他原因錯過年度確認的人員，如何處理？

8.4 Cleanroom Behavior

Original Text

To successfully prevent contamination in manufacturing areas, it is critical for operators to always observe appropriate cleanroom behaviors, including, but not limited to, the list below. It should be noted that these behaviors apply even when using barrier technologies:

  • Move carefully: Moving slowly and deliberately will prevent unnecessary air turbulence in a critical area thus minimizing the risk of particle-generation or shedding. The principle of slow, careful movement should be followed throughout the cleanroom, including awareness of engineering controls and locations of air returns.
  • Minimize conversation: Speaking should only occur when necessary and never in a critical area.
  • Maintain unidirectional airflow ("first air"): Unidirectional airflow is designed to protect sterile equipment surfaces, container-closures, and product. Disruption of the path of unidirectional flow air in the critical area can pose a risk to product sterility.
  • Perform interventions carefully: Using the correct tools and equipment and performing interventions in a manner that does not compromise sterility of the product ensures that unidirectional airflow over sterile product and sterile components will not be interrupted. When using a restricted access barrier system (RABS) system, barrier doors should be opened only when necessary and for the shortest time possible.
  • Position body: Keeping the entire body out of the path of the unidirectional airflow will prevent airflow from being blocked over critical surfaces. Personnel should not reach across open containers, exposed product, or components and product contact parts/surfaces.
  • Maintain proper gown control: Prior to and throughout aseptic operations, operators should not engage in any activity that poses an unreasonable contamination risk to the gown, such as unnecessary contact with walls, floors, and cleaned surfaces. After initial gowning, sterile gloves should be regularly sanitized or changed, as appropriate, to minimize the risk of contamination. If damage (e.g., hole, tear) is detected when donning the gown, the gown should be replaced immediately. If damage is detected during manufacturing, the operator should exit the cleanroom and regown.
  • Protect sterile parts: Parts should be staged and grouped for setup to allow for consistent setup order—from top to bottom, back to front—for the protection of all sterile parts. During setup, sterile product contact parts should remain protected until setup is completed. Between uses, sterile tools should be held under Grade A conditions and maintained in a manner that prevents contamination (e.g., placed in sterilized containers) or should be replaced. Parts should be sterilized together, whenever possible, to minimize aseptic connections during setup.
  • Minimize surface contact: Personnel should not come in direct contact with sterile product, containers, closures, or critical surfaces with any part of their gowns or gloves. Sterile tools should always be used, where possible.
  • Manage gloves: Regular glove sanitization techniques and frequency and re-gloving activities should always be followed. At a minimum, single gloves should be worn in supporting areas; sterile double-gloves should be worn in all conventional aseptic processing areas. A fresh pair of sterile gloves should be worn upon each entry to Grade A clean room. Gloves should be checked for integrity upon donning and prior to performing an intervention. If damage (e.g., hole, tear) is detected when donning the glove, the glove should immediately be replaced. If damage is detected during manufacturing, the operator should follow an established risk-based procedure to replace the glove while limiting any contamination hazard to the operation.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Cleanroom Behavior (潔淨室行為規範)：TR90 列出九大行為準則，每一項都直接關係到污染風險的控制。特別注意：這些行為要求即使在使用 Barrier Technologies (屏障技術，如 RABS 或 Isolator) 時也同樣適用。

核心原則可歸納為三大類：

  • 氣流保護：謹慎移動、維持 First Air (初始空氣)、正確身體定位
  • 屏障維護：管控更衣、管理手套、最小化表面接觸
  • 無菌保護：謹慎介入操作、保護無菌部件、最小化對話

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你在一個佈滿蜘蛛絲的房間裡行走 -- 每一個動作都必須緩慢而刻意，因為任何突然的動作都會擾動空氣，破壞那些脆弱的結構。在潔淨室中，你要保護的不是蜘蛛絲，而是單向氣流 (unidirectional airflow) 形成的「無菌空氣幕」。任何不必要的動作、說話、或不當的身體定位，都可能破壞這道保護屏障。

重點提示 Key Notes

First Air (初始空氣) 原則：這是無菌操作中最重要的概念之一 -- 從 HEPA 過濾器流出的空氣在接觸產品之前，不應接觸任何其他表面或障礙物。操作員的手、手臂或身體都不應阻擋 first air 到達關鍵表面的路徑。

手套管理的層次要求：

  • 支援區域：最低要求單層手套
  • 傳統無菌加工區域：無菌雙層手套
  • 每次進入 Grade A：更換全新的無菌手套

組裝順序原則：從上到下、從後到前 (top to bottom, back to front)，確保已組裝的無菌部件不會被後續操作污染。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 工廠中，由於操作員可能需要在不同產品線之間輪換，建議為每條產線製作一份「潔淨室行為速查卡」，標示該產線特定的氣流方向、空氣回風口位置、以及關鍵介入操作的正確姿勢。搭配定期的視頻回放訓練，讓操作員能「看到自己的動作」並自我改善。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠是否有明確記錄每個操作工位的 first air 方向和氣流路徑？操作員是否能準確指出這些位置？
  2. RABS 門板的開啟時間是否有監控和記錄？如何確保「僅在必要時開啟且時間最短」？
  3. 當手套在操作中發現破損時，你的風險評估程序是否涵蓋對已加工產品的影響評估？

8.4.1 Aseptic Operator Training and Qualification

Original Text

Aseptic processing depends on personnel operating in a manner that does not disturb airflow, minimizes the generation of particles, and does not introduce bioburden into the process through inappropriate handling of product contact equipment or components. Aseptic operator trainees should only be permitted to perform interventions upon successful participation with a process simulation. Participation in the process simulation requires that the operator perform a pre-identified set or subset of critical tasks that they would perform during batch production, and the result of the process simulation was successful. Where there are practical limitations or an aseptic process simulation is not required, operators can be qualified outside of an aseptic process simulation provided the qualification exercise is representative of worst-case process conditions.

After an operator has been initially qualified, the facility should schedule an annual refresher training program as well as participation in routine aseptic process simulation (i.e., media-fill study). Access to the aseptic processing area should be controlled (e.g., badge access, etc.) and a procedure should be established for disqualification and timely revocation of access.

Disqualification should result in retraining and requalification, reassignment, or permanent removal from the cleanroom. Personnel would be considered disqualified from performing in an aseptic filling process if they had participated in a media-fill study that resulted in the detection of positive (failing) units and the root cause is determined to be inappropriate aseptic behavior. Additionally, disqualification should be considered if personnel participate in a commercial production lot that does not meet the requirements for sterility testing and the root cause is determined to be inappropriate aseptic behavior.

In addition to training and qualification/disqualification, there should be regular oversight of personnel following established, written procedures and performing good cleanroom behaviors and aseptic technique during manufacturing operations. Routine oversight through an observation program of aseptic operators should form part of the aseptic-operator training and qualification.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Aseptic Operator Training and Qualification (無菌操作員訓練與資格確認)：這是比一般潔淨室人員更高層級的要求。關鍵要素：

  • Process Simulation (製程模擬)：操作員必須在培養基充填試驗 (media-fill study) 中成功執行其預定的關鍵任務，才能獲得資格
  • Worst-case Process Conditions (最壞情況製程條件)：當無法進行製程模擬時，資格確認練習必須代表最壞情況
  • Annual Refresher (年度複訓)：初始合格後需定期參加複訓和例行培養基充填
  • Access Control (准入控制)：使用門禁卡等方式控制無菌加工區域的進入

重點提示 Key Notes

取消資格的觸發條件：

  • 參與的培養基充填試驗檢出陽性（不合格）單元，且根因為不當的無菌行為
  • 參與的商業生產批次未通過無菌試驗，且根因為不當的無菌行為

取消資格後的處置選項：

  • 重新訓練和重新確認
  • 重新分配至其他崗位
  • 永久移除潔淨室准入權

注意：取消資格不是懲罰性的，而是風險管理的必要手段。重點在於根因分析和矯正措施。

比喻說明 Analogy

無菌操作員的資格確認就像外科醫師的考核 -- 你不能僅靠讀書和考試就上手術台，必須先在模擬手術中成功執行操作。Media-fill study 就是無菌操作員的「模擬手術」。如果在模擬中出現問題（長出微生物），那就意味著實際操作中也可能污染產品。年度的複訓和 media-fill 參與，則確保操作員的技能不會隨時間退化。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 環境中，Media-fill study 的排程是一項挑戰，因為每位操作員都需要參與，同時又不能影響商業生產排程。建議做法：

  • 建立「資格確認日曆」，提前 6 個月規劃所有人員的 media-fill 參與排程
  • 利用產品轉換或年度停機期間安排 media-fill
  • 確保每位操作員在 media-fill 中執行的任務與其日常工作完全一致
  • 將門禁系統與資格確認資料庫連結，過期的資格自動鎖定准入

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠是否要求所有無菌操作員每年參與 media-fill？如果操作員因排班原因錯過，如何處理？
  2. 當 media-fill 失敗時，你的根因調查程序是否能有效區分「操作員行為問題」和「設備/環境問題」？
  3. 操作員的門禁權限是否與資格確認狀態即時連動？

8.4.2 Aseptic Observer Program

Original Text

An aseptic-observer program is a structured process through which aseptic operators are trained to routinely assess and evaluate one another's performance and compliance to all aspects of training and qualification, especially with respect to cleanroom behaviors and aseptic practices. The aseptic-observer program not only identifies areas for improvement but also reinforces observed best practices. An aseptic-observer program can follow the seven simple steps illustrated in Figure 8.4.2-1.

Figure 8.4.2-1 — Aseptic-Operator's Observation 7-Step Program

Step 1: Qualify Operators
All personnel included in the aseptic-observer program should be fully qualified.

Step 2: Identify and Train Aseptic Observers
Aseptic-behavior observers are trained individuals with experience in aseptic behaviors and practices who are respected by the operators.

Step 3: Observe Aseptic Behaviors
Observers use a defined expectations list of behaviors as described in Section 8.4. Behaviors and interventions performed during manufacturing should be consistent with interventions qualified during aseptic process simulations.

Step 4: Report and Provide Feedback
Observers should immediately give feedback to operators on their behaviors as part of continuous improvement. Observations discussed with personnel and frontline management should include overall observations, identification of any observation trends, and potential corrective actions. Potential corrective actions might include refresher training, procedural revisions for clarification, equipment modifications, or use of tools.

Step 5: Improve Aseptic Processes and Behaviors
Barriers to improvement (e.g., process/line configuration, material availability, procedure) should be addressed, and corrective actions should be identified and implemented.

Step 6: Analyze and Trend Observations
Periodically, long-term trending should be performed to determine root cause and to proactively address the most frequent observations. Trend results should then be communicated to operators, management, and support personnel according to site practices. An overall risk assessment tool may also be used for this purpose.

Step 7: Maintain Program
Periodically, the observation program itself should be reviewed to determine if changes are needed to adapt to personnel, facility, and/or process changes. Modifications may include updates to the defined expectations list of behaviors (see Step #3), communication of results, data trending, or effectiveness of corrective actions.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Aseptic Observer Program (無菌觀察員計畫)：這是一種結構化的同儕評估程序，讓經過訓練的無菌操作員定期評估彼此的表現和合規性。這個計畫有雙重目的：

  • 識別需要改善的領域
  • 強化觀察到的最佳實踐

七步驟循環構成了一個完整的 PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) 改善迴路。

重點提示 Key Notes

七步驟計畫的核心邏輯：

  • Step 1-2 (建立基礎)：確保參與者和觀察員都是合格且受尊重的
  • Step 3-4 (觀察與回饋)：使用標準化的行為期望清單進行觀察，並即時給予回饋
  • Step 5-6 (改善與趨勢)：消除改善障礙，進行長期趨勢分析以發現系統性問題
  • Step 7 (維護計畫)：定期檢視計畫本身的有效性並調整

關鍵成功因素：觀察員必須是「受操作員尊重的人」(respected by the operators)。如果觀察員被視為「監視者」而非「同儕教練」，計畫將失去其文化建設的價值。

比喻說明 Analogy

無菌觀察員計畫就像運動隊伍中的「影片回顧」(film review) -- 教練和隊員一起觀看比賽錄影，不是為了指責誰犯了錯，而是為了識別可以改善的地方並強化好的表現。最好的球隊不僅有好的球員，更有持續互相學習和改善的文化。同樣地，最好的無菌操作團隊不僅有合格的個人，更有持續觀察、回饋和改善的機制。

實務應用 Practical Application

建立有效的無菌觀察員計畫的實務建議：

  • 數位化觀察表單：使用平板電腦上的電子表單取代紙本記錄，方便即時輸入和後續趨勢分析
  • 積極正面的回饋文化：觀察報告不僅記錄偏差，也記錄良好實踐，建立正向激勵
  • 與環境監控數據交叉分析：將觀察員報告與環境監控數據結合，識別人員行為與環境數據之間的關聯性
  • 季度趨勢報告：每季度彙整觀察數據，向管理層和操作團隊報告主要發現和改善進度

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠目前是否有正式的無菌觀察員計畫？如果有，觀察頻率和覆蓋率如何？
  2. 觀察員的選拔標準是什麼？是否確保他們「受操作員尊重」？
  3. 觀察結果是否定期進行趨勢分析？最常見的偏差類型是什麼？這些偏差是否與環境監控數據有相關性？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 人員是最大污染源：TR90 反覆強調人員及其行為是藥品製造中微生物污染的最大來源，因此人員訓練與資格確認是 CCS 的核心要素。
  • 六大必修課程：基礎微生物學、人員流向、物料與廢棄物流向、環境控制、清潔與消毒、製程設計 -- 這是所有製造人員進入產區前的最低訓練要求。
  • 四大核心主題：基礎微生物學、個人衛生、更衣、潔淨室行為 -- 這四項構成了污染控制訓練計畫的骨幹。
  • 更衣的完整生命週期管理：從資格確認 (multiple observations + multiple samples) 到取消資格 (matrix approach) 再到重新確認 (annual basis)，形成閉環管理。
  • 九大潔淨室行為準則：謹慎移動、最小化對話、維持 first air、謹慎介入、正確身體定位、管控更衣、保護無菌部件、最小化表面接觸、管理手套。
  • 無菌操作員的進階要求：必須在 media-fill 中成功執行關鍵任務才能獲得資格，年度複訓和 media-fill 參與為持續要求。
  • 無菌觀察員計畫的七步驟：從操作員資格確認到觀察、回饋、改善、趨勢分析，再到計畫本身的維護，形成持續改善的閉環。
  • 從「非全面性」到「全面性」思維轉變：不再認為「遵循 SOP 就夠了」，而是追求理解危害、品質文化建設和持續改善的全面性方法。
