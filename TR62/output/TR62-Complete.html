Introduction — Purpose, Scope & Background

導論 — 目的、範疇與背景 | p1–p2

Purpose & Goal of TR62

The purpose of this technical report is to outline methods and approaches for control and evaluation of aseptic processing operations for drug products/medicinal products which use all or partially manual procedures. The goal of aseptic processing is to prevent the contamination of sterile materials during their processing. The goal of evaluating any aseptic process is to demonstrate that aseptic processing can be achieved and maintained successfully under the specified operational configuration, activities, and conditions. These goals are the same for manual or automated aseptic operations, and for small-scale or large-scale operations.

TR62 的核心目的

PDA TR62 專門處理「手動無菌操作（Manual Aseptic Processing, MAP）」——即部分或全部依賴人員手動執行的無菌製造流程。

兩個核心目標：

  • 控制：在操作過程中防止汙染
  • 評估：透過驗證證明流程能持續維持無菌

無論是手動或自動、小規模或大規模，這兩個目標完全一致。

比喻：外科手術的無菌要求

想像一位外科醫師手術時，手術室環境控制、刷手流程、無菌手套——所有這些都是為了同一個目標：防止細菌進入病人體內。MAP 也是如此，只是對象是藥品而非人體。

How MAP Differs from Automated Operations

Manual aseptic processing (MAP) operations differ from automated operations in several ways. These differences pose unique operational and evaluation challenges not generally encountered with automated operations. These challenges must be considered thoroughly when designing the evaluation procedure or protocol for the MAP operation.

MAP involves a human operator performing, at a minimum, the container and/or closure movements. Some semi-automated equipment may be used, but the process is not fully automated. For this reason MAP relies heavily on individual operator's basic understanding of microbiology proficiency where personnel must be individually qualified.

The greatest source of microbial contamination during MAP is generally recognized to be the operational personnel and their activities. Aseptic processes that rely on manual operations are inherently subject to performance or procedural drift over time. Furthermore, reproducible human performance cannot be assumed, especially where there are significant time gaps between aseptic processing events.

The likelihood of human performance deviations or failures is linked to:

  • Complex aseptic processing tasks
  • The continuous span of time during which an operator carries out repetitive aseptic activities
  • The expected rate of activity
  • Change in personnel

關鍵警示：人員是最大汙染源

TR62 明確指出：MAP 中微生物汙染的最大來源是操作人員本身及其行為。這是手動操作與自動化操作最根本的差異。

自動化設備可以精確重複；人員則無法保證每次行為完全一致，尤其是：

  • 長時間重複性操作後疲勞
  • 兩次無菌作業之間間隔過長
  • 人員異動（訓練水準不同）

比喻：飛行員與自動駕駛

自動駕駛飛機能精確執行每個動作，但人類飛行員在長途飛行後可能因疲勞而反應遲鈍。MAP 也類似——人員操作受到時間、疲勞、注意力分散等影響，因此需要更嚴格的資格認證制度。

Scope & Applicability

This technical report has value for hospital and formulation pharmacies where manual aseptic processing may occur. The guidance provided in this report may be applicable to various operations, including: vaccine preparation, cell culture, gene therapy, IND/IMP manufacturing, clinical and commercial manufacturing, and pharmacy formulation and dispensing.

When manual aseptic processing of sterile dosage forms is required, special consideration must be given to sterility and verification of processing accuracy, as the administration of these products into the vascular and nervous system of human subjects poses the greatest risk of harm. As applicable, the 2004 FDA guidance on aseptic processing, EU GMP–Annex 1, Ph Eur 5.1.1, and USP Chapters <797> and <823> provide procedures and the requirements for:

  • Training of personnel involved in sterile preparation processes
  • Environmental control and monitoring requirements
  • Specifications for sterile and non-sterile ingredients and components
  • Release criteria for sterility and pyrogen testing

Compliance with these requirements is paramount to ensure the safety of human recipients.

TR62 的適用範圍

TR62 不只適用於商業製造廠，也涵蓋：

  • 醫院藥局的無菌配製
  • 疫苗製備
  • 細胞治療、基因治療
  • IND/IMP（試驗用藥）製造
  • 臨床與商業規模製造

監管框架 — 四大參考依據

  • FDA 2004：無菌加工指導文件（Aseptic Processing Guidance）
  • EU GMP Annex 1：無菌藥品製造（2022年更新）
  • USP <797>：藥局複方配製（無菌製劑）
  • USP <823>：放射性藥品製備

注射至血管或神經系統的產品風險最高，法規要求也最嚴格。

CDMO 應用情境

TWi Pharmaceuticals 承接客戶 IND/IMP 製造時，如果某一步驟（如 pH 調整、分裝移轉）需要手動進行，即受 TR62 規範。此時必須將 MAP 評估設計納入整體 APS 計劃，確保監管可接受性。

What TR62 Does NOT Cover

This report is not intended to address the brief, relatively infrequent, human interventions into an otherwise automated filling process. Examples include reach-ins to remove a toppled vial from the filling line or to obtain a container for a fill-weight check, aseptic connections made during set-up, corrective activities during line stoppages, and so on. Operator activities and interventions of this nature should be integrated into the aseptic process simulation (APS) of the respective automated processes and they will not be addressed further in this report.

The guidance provided in this report builds upon, and is intended to supplement, published guidance which is generally more focused on automated large-scale operations. In conclusion, for adequate evaluation of the manual aseptic process it is important that human factors, such as those described above, are fully accounted for in both the design of an MAP and also in the design of the APS program.

Some of these processes require aseptic steps subsequent to sterilization but prior to filling, such as volume or pH adjustments, which present their own contamination risks.

TR62 的邊界：哪些不在範圍內

TR62 不涵蓋自動化充填線上偶發性的人員介入，例如：

  • 撿起傾倒的西林瓶（reach-in）
  • 取樣進行充填重量檢查
  • 設備設置期間的無菌連接
  • 生產停線時的糾正性操作

這些介入應整合進對應自動化製程的 APS 設計中，而非獨立按 TR62 評估。

比喻：廚師 vs. 機器手臂

自動化充填線像機器手臂——偶爾需要廚師（人員）去撿掉落的食材，這是「介入」。但若整道菜從頭到尾都由廚師手工製作，那才是 MAP——每一個手部動作都需要被系統性地評估與驗證。

滅菌後的手動步驟也有風險

值得注意的是，TR62 特別提醒：即使在滅菌之後、充填之前，如果需要進行 pH 調整或體積調整等手動操作，這些步驟本身也會帶來汙染風險，必須納入 MAP 評估設計。

Glossary of Terms

術語定義 | p3–p5

Microorganisms & Sterility Terms

Aerobic Microorganism
A microorganism that utilizes oxygen as the final electron acceptor during metabolism; a microorganism that will grow primarily in the presence of oxygen. For the purpose of this report, this definition encompasses facultative anaerobes.

Anaerobic Microorganism
A microorganism that does not utilize oxygen as the final electron acceptor during metabolism; microorganism that will grow only in the absence of oxygen.

Bioburden
Total number of viable microorganisms on or in a health care product prior to sterilization.

Colony Forming Unit (CFU)
One or more microorganisms that produce a visible, discrete growth entity on a semi-solid, agar-based microbiological medium.

Sterile
Absence of life; usually refers to absence of viable microorganisms. Note: In practice, no such absolute statement regarding the absence of microorganisms can be proven (see "Sterilization").

Sterility Test
Test performed to determine if viable microorganisms are present.

Sterilization
Validated process used to render a product free of viable microorganisms. Note: In a sterilization process, the nature of microbiological death or reduction is described by an exponential function. Therefore, the number of microorganisms which survive a sterilization process can be expressed in terms of probability. While the probability may be reduced to a very low number, it can never be reduced to zero.

Positive Unit
Unit filled in an aseptic processing simulation that exhibits detectable microbial growth after incubation.

好氧菌 vs. 厭氧菌 — 為何都需關注

好氧菌（Aerobic）：需要氧氣才能生長，是環境監測中最常見的目標菌群，例如葡萄球菌、芽孢桿菌。

厭氧菌（Anaerobic）：在無氧環境中生長，如充填惰性氣體（N₂）保護的製品中，厭氧菌才是主要威脅。TR62 Section 8.6 會進一步討論 APS 中的厭氧測試。

兼性厭氧菌（Facultative anaerobes）：兩種環境均可生長，TR62 將其歸類於好氧菌定義下。

無菌（Sterile）的哲學

「無菌」在統計意義上永遠無法被絕對證明——滅菌過程只能將存活微生物的機率降低至極低（通常 SAL = 10⁻⁶），但不能歸零。這就是為何「無菌保證水準（SAL）」採用概率表達，而非「零」。

CFU — 環境監測的基本單位

CFU（菌落形成單位）是環境監測（EM）中用來計數微生物的基本單位。一個 CFU 不代表一個細菌，而是一個或多個細菌在培養基上形成可見菌落的單位。EM 結果以 CFU/m³（空氣）或 CFU/plate（沉降碟）報告。

Positive Unit（陽性單位）— APS 的核心判定標準

在 APS（培養基充填試驗）中，若任何充填單元在培養後出現微生物生長，即判定為「陽性單位」。這是 APS 最關鍵的失敗指標，必須立即啟動調查。

Aseptic Processing Core Terms

Aseptic Filling
The part of aseptic processing where a pre-sterilized product is filled and/or packaged into sterile containers and closed.

Aseptic Processing
Handling sterile materials in a controlled environment, in which the air supply, facility, materials, equipment and personnel are regulated to control microbial and particulate contamination to acceptable levels.

Aseptic Processing Area (APA)
Controlled environment, consisting of several zones, in which the air supply, facility, materials, equipment and personnel are regulated to control microbial and particulate contamination to acceptable levels.

Aseptic Processing Simulation (APS)
A means for establishing the capability of an aseptic process as performed using a growth medium. Note: Aseptic processing simulations are understood to be synonymous with media fills, simulated product fills, broth trials, broth fills, etc.

Campaign
A series of consecutive production batches manufactured without intervening cleaning and sterilization.

Compounding
A process wherein bulk drug substance is combined with one or more excipients and/or another bulk drug substance to produce a drug product.

Critical Area
An area designed to maintain sterility of sterile materials. Sterilized product, containers, closures, and equipment may be exposed in critical areas.

Environmental Monitoring Program
Defined documented program that describes the routine particulate and microbiological monitoring of processing and manufacturing areas. Note: The program should reference a corrective action plan in cases where action levels are exceeded.

Aseptic Processing 的五個管控要素

無菌加工（Aseptic Processing）的定義揭示了五個必須同步管控的要素：

  • Air supply：空氣供應（HEPA過濾、單向氣流）
  • Facility：廠房設施（潔淨室等級、壓差）
  • Materials：物料（已滅菌的容器、閉合件）
  • Equipment：設備（已清潔滅菌）
  • Personnel：人員（著裝、行為、資格）

APA（無菌加工區）由多個分區組成，管控要求隨分區等級遞增。

APS = Media Fill = 培養基充填試驗

APS 是驗證無菌加工能力的核心工具。用促進微生物生長的培養基（如大豆酪蛋白消化液體培養基，SCDM）代替實際藥液，模擬完整的生產操作，然後培養觀察是否有微生物生長。

TR62 的核心主題就是：如何針對 MAP 的特殊性（人員因素、操作複雜度）設計適當的 APS。

Campaign（活動批次）— 清潔週期的邊界

Campaign 是指連續生產批次之間未進行清潔與滅菌的系列批次。Campaign 長度是 MAP 製程時間限制的重要考量因素（詳見 TR62 Section 6.0）。Campaign 越長，汙染累積風險越高。

CDMO 應用：EM 計劃的關鍵要素

有效的環境監測計劃（EM Program）必須包含：

  • 取樣位置與頻率的書面定義
  • 行動水準（Action Level）超標時的 CAPA 程序
  • 警戒水準（Alert Level）趨勢分析
  • APS 期間的額外 EM 要求

Facility, Equipment & Air Quality Terms

Barrier System
A system of physical partitions that affords ISO 5 protection by partially separating its interior from the surrounding environment utilizing airflow.

Biological Safety Cabinet (BSC)
An enclosed, ventilated workspace with engineering controls designed to remove or minimize exposure to hazardous biological materials. A BSC is a principle device to provide containment of infectious splashes or aerosols generated by many microbiological procedures. BSCs are designed to provide personnel, environmental and product protection when appropriate practices and procedures are followed. A cabinet that is designed to protect the operator and the environment from the hazards of handling infected material and other dangerous biological materials.

First Air (First Work Location)
The work location first in the path of HEPA filtered air.

HEPA filter
High efficiency particulate air filter with minimum 0.3 µm particle retaining efficiency of 99.97 percent.

ISO 5
Environmental operating conditions defined in ISO 14644-1. Note: For total particulates, ISO 5 approximates the Class 100 description from the now obsolete U.S. Federal Standard 209, and is roughly comparable to Grade A as defined in European GMP Annex 1.

ISO 7
Environmental operating conditions defined in ISO 14644-1. Note: For total particulates, ISO 7 approximates Class 10,000 from the now obsolete Federal Standard 209.

ISO 8
Environmental operating conditions defined in ISO 14644-1. Note: For total particulates, ISO 8 approximates Class 100,000 from the now obsolete Federal Standard 209.

Unidirectional Air Flow Hood (UAFH)
A cabinet designed to protect materials from operator and environmental contamination. Also referred to as laminar air flow hood.

First Air — 最優先的操作位置原則

「First Air」指的是 HEPA 過濾後，空氣最先到達的位置。在此位置進行操作，受到人員、設備等汙染源干擾最少。

操作原則：無菌操作應盡量在 First Air 位置進行；任何阻擋氣流或將手伸過開口產品上方（shadowing）的動作都是高風險行為。

HEPA vs. ULPA — 過濾效率

HEPA（High Efficiency Particulate Air）過濾器對 ≥0.3 µm 粒子的過濾效率為 99.97%。部分高要求場所使用 ULPA（Ultra-Low Penetration Air），效率可達 99.999%。MAP 操作按 TR62 要求使用 HEPA 過濾的 ISO 5 環境。

比喻：BSC vs. LAFH — 方向相反的保護

LAF Hood（層流罩）的氣流從 HEPA 出發，向外吹，保護的是產品不被人員汙染。BSC 的氣流則將危險材料向內抽，保護的是操作人員不被材料感染。兩者目的截然相反，不可混用。

Isolators, RABS & Barrier Technology

Isolator, Closed
A decontaminated unit meeting ISO 5 conditions that provides uncompromised, continuous, isolation of its interior from the surrounding environment. Any air exchange with the surrounding environment takes place only through microbially retentive filters.

Isolator, Open
A decontaminated unit meeting ISO 5 conditions that provides uncompromised, continuous isolation of its interior from the surrounding environment. It may transfer air directly to the surrounding environment through openings (e.g., "mouseholes") that preclude the ingress of microbial contamination.

Restricted Access Barrier System (RABS)
RABS are aseptic processing systems (ISO 5) intended to substantially reduce human borne contamination within the aseptic environment where sterile product, containers, closures and equipment are exposed by the use of separative devices and defined mechanical features and operating procedures.

Mousehole — 開放式隔離器的關鍵設計

開放式隔離器的「mousehole」是允許物料傳遞的開口，其設計必須確保正壓氣流持續向外流出，防止外部微生物進入。這是開放式隔離器維持 ISO 5 環境的核心機制。

CDMO 選型考量

選擇 UAFH / RABS / 隔離器時，須考量：

  • 製程複雜度（手動操作步驟數量）
  • 產品毒性（高活性 API 需更高隔離等級）
  • 批次規模（小批量臨床製造 vs. 商業規模）
  • 監管預期（EU vs. FDA vs. 日本 PMDA）

Interventions, Human Factors & Operations

Human Factors
A science discipline that examines human psychological, social, physical, and biological characteristics to evaluate the design, operation, or use of products or systems for optimizing human performance, health, safety, and/or habitability. [Synonym: Ergonomics]

Intervention
An aseptic manipulation or activity that occurs within the critical area. This technical report regards interventions as either corrective or inherent.

Intervention, Corrective
An intervention that is performed to correct or adjust an aseptic process during its execution. Examples include such activities as: clearing component misfeed, adjusting sensors, and replacing equipment components.

Intervention, Inherent
An intervention that is an integral part of the aseptic process and is required for set-up or routine operation and/or monitoring, e.g., aseptic assembly, container replenishment, environmental sampling, etc. Inherent interventions are required by batch record, procedure, or work instruction for the proper conduct of the aseptic process.

Shift
Scheduled periods of work or production, usually less than 12 hours in length, staffed by alternating groups of workers.

Worst Case
A set of conditions encompassing upper and lower processing limits and circumstances, including those within standard operating procedures, that pose the greatest chance of process or product failure (when compared to ideal conditions). Such conditions do not necessarily induce product or process failure.

Validation
Establishing documented evidence that provides a high degree of assurance that a specific process will consistently produce a product meeting its predetermined specifications and quality attributes.

人因工程（Human Factors / Ergonomics）在 MAP 的重要性

TR62 特別強調人因工程，因為 MAP 的設計必須考量：

  • 心理因素：注意力持續時間、疲勞、壓力
  • 生理因素：手部靈活度、視線角度、穿著厚重無菌衣後的行動限制
  • 社交因素：班組溝通、人員異動

MAP 設計必須讓「正確操作」成為自然而然的選擇，而非依賴人員時刻保持完美。

固有介入 vs. 糾正性介入

固有介入（Inherent）：SOP 要求的、預期中會發生的操作，如：環境取樣、補充容器。必須在 APS 設計中完整模擬。

糾正性介入（Corrective）：為修正異常狀況而進行的臨時操作，如：清除卡料、更換感測器。每次發生都需記錄，APS 中也應模擬最壞情況。

Worst Case — APS 設計的核心原則

APS 必須在「最壞情況」條件下進行，包括：

  • 最長預期操作時間（upper limit）
  • 最多預期人員介入次數
  • 人員資格認證的邊界條件（如新進操作員）

注意：Worst Case 不代表一定要導致失敗，而是代表最接近失敗邊界的操作條件。

Minimum Environmental Requirements for MAP

The selection of an appropriate clean environment in which to perform the required manufacturing activities is critical to MAP operations. In general, the minimum requirement is an ISO 5 environment in which unidirectional air flow protection is provided to critical materials during the manufacturing procedures. A listing of selected air quality classification standards is shown in Table 3.0-1.

In practice, many different locations and equipment for MAP operations are possible, including:

  • A portion of a larger environment of the same class
  • A localized unidirectional air flow hood (also known as a laminar flow hood or LAFH) protecting a specific portion/area within a lower classified environment
  • A table mounted unidirectional air flow hood (with either horizontal or vertical air flow)
  • An isolator (open or closed)

ISO 5 — MAP 的最低門檻

TR62 明確規定：MAP 操作的最低環境要求是 ISO 5 等級，且必須提供單向氣流（Unidirectional Airflow）保護關鍵物料。

這個 ISO 5 環境可以透過以下方式實現：

  • 整體 ISO 5 潔淨室（如 Grade A 充填間）
  • 局部 UAFH/LAFH（在較低等級環境中提供局部 ISO 5）
  • 隔離器（Isolator）——開放式或密閉式

比喻：帳篷中的手術室

戰地醫院無法建造正式手術室，但可以在帳篷內設置一個小型無菌區域。UAFH 就像這個帳篷中的「無菌帳中帳」——在較低等級的背景環境中，為關鍵操作提供局部 ISO 5 保護。

水平 vs. 垂直氣流 — 選擇依據

垂直氣流（Vertical LAF）：氣流由上而下，適合大多數無菌操作；操作者的手部動作不易阻擋氣流進入關鍵區。

水平氣流（Horizontal LAF）：氣流從背板朝操作者方向流出，提供更直接的 First Air 保護，但操作者身體易成為阻擋物。

UAFH, Isolators, and BSC Operations

Each of the above is in current use in the industry for the conduct of MAP operations. In general, as MAP operations become more complex, a more sophisticated and complex operating environment will be required. The manual processes considered in this report are commonly carried out in either a unidirectional air flow hood or an isolator.

A unidirectional air flow hood (UAFH) is a cabinet designed to protect materials from operator and environmental contamination. All air supplied to the UAFH is High Efficiency Particulate Air (HEPA) filtered. In operation, all air flows from the UAFH, through the critical manufacturing areas, and exits to the surrounding environment.

An isolator is a decontaminated system, meeting ISO 5 conditions, which provides uncompromised and continuous isolation of its interior from the surrounding environment. A closed isolator only allows air exchanges with the surrounding environment through microbially retentive filters. An open isolator may transfer air directly to the surrounding environment through openings (commonly called "mouse holes") that preclude the ingress of microbial contamination. All these environments provide appropriate conditions for the execution of MAP operations which are considered critical zones.

Biological Safety Cabinets (BSC) are designed to protect operators and the external environment from infected or dangerous materials inside the cabinet. All air to and from the BSC is HEPA filtered before entering the cabinet or exhausting to the external environment. Some BSC designs provide some measure of protection to the materials being handled. Since air flows into the BSC from the surrounding environment, biosafety cabinets should be used only when worker safety from the material being handled is a meaningful concern. Worker safety must also be considered if the toxicity of the compound has not yet been determined.

UAFH 的氣流邏輯

UAFH 的氣流從 HEPA 出發，向外流出，形成正壓屏障，阻止外部汙染進入關鍵區。關鍵特點：

  • 全部供氣均經 HEPA 過濾
  • 氣流穿過關鍵製造區域後，排放至背景環境
  • 操作者必須避免遮擋（shadowing）First Air 氣流

隔離器的核心優勢：物理屏障 + 去汙染

隔離器不僅提供 ISO 5 空氣，還透過物理屏障將操作者與關鍵區完全分隔。每次使用前通常需要進行去汙染（如汽化過氧化氫，VHP）。

這使得隔離器對人員的依賴程度顯著低於 UAFH——降低了 MAP 中最大的風險來源。

BSC — 保護方向相反，使用場合明確

BSC 是為了保護操作人員免受危險生物材料感染，氣流方向與 LAF Hood 相反（從外部流入）。因此：

  • BSC 只應在需要保護操作者的情況下使用（如細胞治療、高毒性 API）
  • 若產品毒性未確定，也應考慮 BSC
  • BSC 不能被視為等同於 LAF Hood 的產品保護設備

Background Environment Requirements

The supporting clean environment outside the critical zone is typically ISO 7 when a UAFH or BSC is utilized for the manual process. The use of an isolator under appropriate risk-management procedures may relax the requirements for the surrounding area; most new manufacturing installations employ an ISO 8 background environment in accordance with the 2004 FDA aseptic processing guidance or European GMP Annex 1.

Open isolators are typically installed in ISO 8 (Table 3.0-1) environments in order to maintain ISO 5 conditions for material transfers and operations. The surrounding clean area is where the personnel performing the manual processes are located. Appropriate gowning facilities are required which are consistent with the background environment requirements. The execution of the MAP is ordinarily supported by various sterilization processes for the materials and equipment required, and these sterilization processes may also be located in the support areas.

The overall flow of the MAP facility, personnel and equipment is consistent with large-scale environments utilized for equipment-based aseptic filling, whether in a cleanroom or an isolator, but on a much smaller scale.

4.1 Personnel Training and Qualification

Because the greatest source of microbial contamination during MAP is generally recognized to be the operational personnel and their activities, rigorous training and qualification of all personnel who perform manual aseptic operations is essential. All personnel performing MAP must be individually trained and qualified to perform the specific aseptic tasks assigned to them.

Training programs for MAP personnel must address:

  • Fundamentals of microbiology and aseptic technique
  • The specific aseptic procedures required for the tasks being performed
  • Understanding of the critical nature of their role in maintaining product sterility
  • Proper gowning procedures consistent with the environment in which they work
  • Environmental monitoring requirements and how to respond when limits are exceeded
  • Contamination control principles and the consequences of failures

Personnel must demonstrate competency through both written assessments and practical demonstration before they are authorized to perform MAP operations. Qualification must be maintained through periodic re-qualification, particularly when there are significant gaps between aseptic processing events or when personnel have been absent for extended periods.

Because reproducible human performance cannot be assumed, individual qualification — rather than group training — is the standard for MAP. Each person must independently demonstrate the ability to perform aseptic operations to the required standard.

「個別認證」原則 — MAP vs. 一般訓練

一般訓練課程可以一次教導多人（group training）。但 MAP 要求每位人員個別認證（individual qualification）——因為人員行為的一致性無法假設。

這意味著：即使同一個教室、同一位講師、同一套 SOP 訓練出來的兩個人，也必須分別通過實際操作考核才能執行 MAP。

訓練計劃的六個核心要素

  • 微生物基礎：理解為何無菌如此重要
  • 具體程序：針對特定任務的 SOP 訓練
  • 角色重要性：個人行為對產品無菌性的影響
  • 著裝程序：符合環境等級要求
  • 環境監測：如何解讀並回應 EM 結果
  • 汙染控制後果：失敗的影響（患者安全）

再認證觸發條件

以下情況必須重新進行資格認證：

  • 長期未執行 MAP 操作後（具體時間間隔應由 SOP 定義）
  • 長期請假或停工復工後
  • 操作程序或設備有重大變更後
  • APS 或環境監測出現負面趨勢後

CDMO 實務：認證體系設計

在 CDMO 環境中，因為每個客戶產品的操作步驟可能不同，人員認證計劃應設計為：

  • 通用模組：無菌技術基礎、著裝——所有人員必修
  • 任務特定模組：依充填類型（小瓶、注射筒、袋裝等）分別認證
  • 設備特定模組：依 UAFH/RABS/隔離器類型分別認證

4.2 Gowning Qualification

Personnel who perform MAP in an ISO 5 environment or who enter the background environment supporting MAP operations must be qualified in gowning appropriate to that environment. Gowning qualification verifies that a person can consistently don and remove sterile garments without contaminating themselves, the garments, or the surrounding environment.

Gowning qualification typically involves:

  • Direct observation of the gowning process by a qualified trainer or assessor
  • Environmental sampling of the operator's gowned body surfaces (glove fingertip sampling, gown surface contact plates) immediately after gowning
  • Review of sampling results against specified acceptance criteria (typically zero CFU or very low limits for Grade A background)

The frequency of gowning re-qualification should be defined in the facility's quality system and should account for personnel performance history, the frequency with which the person performs MAP, and regulatory expectations for the target markets. Gowning re-qualification is typically conducted at defined intervals (e.g., semi-annually or annually) and whenever there are significant gaps in MAP activity.

For operations in isolators, where personnel interact with the critical zone through glove ports, the gowning requirements for the background environment are less demanding, but glove integrity and glove change procedures become critical qualification elements.

著裝資格認證的三個驗證方法

著裝資格認證（Gowning Qualification）不是只看人員「有沒有穿對」，而是要實際驗證著裝過程不會帶入微生物：

  • 目視觀察：由資深人員觀察完整著裝流程
  • 手套指尖取樣：著裝後立即對五指進行接觸培養，確認未帶入汙染
  • 著裝表面接觸碟：驗證無菌衣表面微生物符合接受標準

Grade A 背景環境的著裝接受標準

在 Grade B 背景環境（支援 Grade A 充填）中，手套指尖取樣的要求通常為：

  • 行動水準（Action Level）：1 CFU/手套（EU GMP 要求）
  • 警戒水準（Alert Level）：各設施依風險評估設定

這意味著著裝過程幾乎不允許任何微生物的帶入——要求極高。

比喻：外科手術的刷手程序

外科醫師在手術前刷手後，護士會確認其著裝符合無菌標準，才能進入手術室。MAP 的著裝資格認證就是這個驗證過程的 GMP 制度化版本——不是靠信任，而是靠採樣數據證明。

隔離器操作中的特殊著裝考量

在隔離器環境中，操作者透過手套口（glove ports）與關鍵區互動。此時：

  • 背景環境著裝要求相對較低（因有物理屏障）
  • 但手套完整性成為最關鍵的認證要素
  • 手套更換程序（glove change procedure）需要專項認證
  • 定期的手套洩漏測試也是資格維持的一部分

你的設施目前著裝資格認證的頻率是多少？如果有一位操作員半年內只執行了一次 MAP，你認為應該在下次操作前重新進行著裝資格認證嗎？TR62 對此有何指引？

4.3 Risk Management for Personnel

A formal risk management approach should be applied to the selection, training, qualification, and monitoring of personnel performing MAP. Human factors analysis can be used to identify and mitigate risks associated with the specific tasks being performed.

Key risk factors for MAP personnel include:

  • Frequency of MAP performance: Personnel who perform MAP infrequently are at higher risk of performance drift and should be subject to more frequent re-qualification and closer supervision.
  • Task complexity: More complex manual aseptic tasks carry higher contamination risk and require more rigorous qualification and ongoing oversight.
  • Duration of operations: Extended periods of repetitive manual work increase operator fatigue and the likelihood of lapses in aseptic technique.
  • Environmental monitoring history: Trends in EM data associated with specific operators or shifts should be analyzed and used to drive targeted training or qualification actions.
  • Personnel health and wellness: Personnel with conditions that may increase bioburden shedding (e.g., skin conditions, respiratory infections) should be excluded from critical MAP operations until resolved.

Risk management for MAP personnel should be a dynamic, ongoing process rather than a one-time assessment. Performance data from APS runs, environmental monitoring, and routine operations should all feed back into the risk management program to identify emerging concerns and drive continuous improvement.

4.4 Aseptic Handling Challenges

Manual aseptic operations present a set of handling challenges that are distinct from those encountered in automated processing. These challenges arise from the nature of human interaction with the aseptic environment and must be explicitly addressed in both the design of the MAP and the design of the APS program.

Key aseptic handling challenges in MAP include:

  • Working within restricted geometries: Unidirectional air flow hoods, RABS, and isolators impose physical constraints on operator movement and reach. Operators must understand how to position their hands and bodies to avoid obstructing critical airflow (shadowing) or contaminating exposed sterile surfaces.
  • Maintaining aseptic technique over extended periods: As operations extend in time, the risk of procedural lapses increases due to fatigue, boredom, or distraction. Shift length limits and appropriate pacing are important design considerations.
  • Performing aseptic connections and transfers: Aseptic connections — including the connection of tubing, bags, filters, and other components — represent critical moments in MAP where contamination risk is highest. Each connection type requires specific technique and should be validated through APS.
  • Maintaining sterility of open containers and closures: Open sterile containers and exposed closure surfaces must be protected from contamination at all times. This requires operators to understand and consistently apply first air principles.
  • Responding to unexpected events: Equipment malfunctions, component defects, or other unexpected events may require corrective interventions that were not anticipated in the original process design. Operators must be prepared to respond in a manner that preserves aseptic integrity, even under pressure.

These challenges must be explicitly addressed in the design of the APS program. The simulation must replicate the full range of planned and foreseeable activities, including those that represent the greatest challenge to aseptic technique.

五大手動無菌操作挑戰

TR62 識別了五個需要在 MAP 設計和 APS 中明確處理的核心挑戰：

  1. 受限空間作業：在 UAFH/RABS/隔離器的物理限制下操作
  2. 長時間維持無菌技術：疲勞導致的技術偏差
  3. 無菌連接與轉移：最高風險時刻
  4. 保護開口容器與閉合件：First Air 原則的持續應用
  5. 應對突發事件：在壓力下維持無菌完整性

Shadowing（遮蔽效應）— 最常見的技術缺失

Shadowing 是指操作者的手、手臂或身體遮擋了 HEPA 過濾後流向關鍵產品的氣流，使該區域喪失 ISO 5 保護。

常見的 shadowing 情況：

  • 手臂伸過開口小瓶上方（最危險）
  • 手持工具時阻擋氣流路徑
  • 在水平 LAF Hood 中身體阻擋從背板流出的氣流

APS 觀察員應特別記錄所有 shadowing 行為，並納入 APS 評估報告。

比喻：外科手術的「無菌場（Sterile Field）」概念

外科手術中，所有無菌物品必須置於「無菌場」內，任何未認證的物品（包括人員未著裝的手臂）接觸無菌場即構成汙染。MAP 的 First Air 原則正是這個概念在無菌充填環境中的延伸——保護氣流路徑就是保護無菌場。

無菌連接 — 最高風險操作時刻

每一次無菌連接（如管路連接、濾器安裝、物料轉移）都是 MAP 中汙染風險最高的時刻。每種連接類型需要：

  • 明確定義的操作技術（在 SOP 中書面規定）

5.0 Equipment, Components and Container/Closure

5.0 Equipment, Components and Container/Closure

The equipment, raw material components, containers, closures and other items required for MAP vary with the requirements of the process. These items are typically reduced in size or quantity, which allows them to be supplied to the processing environment in a sealed package after depyrogenation/sterilization.

The preparation methods for such sealed packages prior to depyrogenation/sterilization are similar to those associated with automated aseptic processing though on a smaller scale of operation.

The depyrogenation/sterilization methods for all of these items are required to be validated.

When sterile containers/closures are purchased from a commercial source, procedures should be in place and implemented to ensure the sterility of these items are maintained when introduced into an aseptic environment and used in production.

Where sterilization or depyrogenation of processes for non-product contact surfaces cannot be utilized, a validated sanitization process is required to be performed.

核心概念：MAP 設備的縮小化特性

手動無菌製程的設備比自動化系統小得多，可以整批放入密封包裝後進行除熱原 / 滅菌。這是 MAP 的主要優勢之一——彈性高、批量小。

  • 設備、原料、容器、封蓋均需在密封包裝狀態下完成滅菌

6.0 Process Time Limitations

製程時間限制

6.0 Process Time Limitations

Personnel time limitations with MAP are usually more important than with automated aseptic processes. In very short or nonrepetitive processes, such as an aseptic connection, time may be of little relevance (except as it may relate to material stability) and its impact lessened.

Where operators must perform repetitive tasks such as container filling/stoppering, egg harvesting (as in vaccine manufacturing) or similar tasks, the effect of fatigue must be considered in both routine operation and process simulation. The operator's aseptic technique may deteriorate with the passage of time.

A "worst-case" evaluation of fatigue would include a process simulation equal or greater in time duration to the longest period an operator might perform the task without interruption. Similarly, a process simulation conducted at the end of a normal day's production can evaluate the effect of fatigue on the operator's aseptic technique.

Time limits for holding dirty/clean/sterile equipment and sterile/depyrogenated components shall also be validated. It is also necessary to validate the maximum time allowed between formulation (addition of the first ingredient, which includes water) to the point of sterilization (filtration considering filter grow-through) and the validation of holding sterile bulk through usage.

Note: As part of the process risk assessment, personnel fatigue factors need to be considered, and where possible minimized/eliminated through improvement in ergonomics, an understanding of human factors, etc.

核心概念：時間限制的三個層次

  • 設備保存時間：髒設備、乾淨設備、已滅菌設備各自的保存時限，均需驗證
  • 製程時間：從配方第一個成分投料到過濾滅菌的最長允許時間（需考量 filter grow-through）

7.0 Design of Manual Aseptic Processes

手動無菌製程設計

7.0 Design of Manual Aseptic Processes — Introduction

Sterility assurance requires a holistic approach to the entire manufacturing process, and encompasses appropriate facility/equipment/process design, aseptic practice/validation and a well thought out risk assessment to mitigate contamination.

Considering the importance of personnel practices in MAP, the process should be designed to minimize the impact of personnel. General process design principles that will reduce the risk of contamination are outlined below.

中文解析

製程設計的核心哲學

TR62 的製程設計理念建立在一個前提上：人員是最大污染源，因此製程設計的首要目標是「最小化人員介入的影響」。這不是排除人，而是透過設計讓人的每一個動作都在受保護的條件下進行。

7.1 Manual Aseptic Process Design Principles in Unidirectional Air Flow Hoods (UAFH / LAF)

層流罩內手動無菌製程設計原則

7.1 Design Principles — UAFH / LAF

The list below elaborates on design principles for manual aseptic processing in Unidirectional Air Flow Hoods:

  • Adequate space to perform the work.
  • All exposed product and product-contacting components should continuously remain in First Air, i.e., the work location first in the path of HEPA-filtered air.
  • Aseptic manipulations should be made in First Air, not having passed over any other components or blocked by the operator's hands.
  • The operators should decontaminate or change their gloves on a frequent basis.
  • The operators should work as a team:
    • Primary operator: performs all tasks inside the ISO 5 environment.
    • Secondary operator: assists in introduction/removal of items from ISO 5, may assist primary with less critical tasks.
    • Support operator: activities exclusively in the surrounding environment.
  • The primary operator should wear sterile gloves and sleeves and never contact a non-sanitized or non-sterilized item.
  • The hands of the primary operator should remain in the ISO 5 environment at all times. (Exceptions: PET products or radioactive products.)
  • The secondary operator should put on sterile gloves/sleeves prior to any activity inside the ISO 5 environment, or in transfers of items to/from the primary operator.
  • Anytime the primary operator is required to leave the ISO 5 environment, gloves and sleeves (if appropriate) should either be changed or gloves should be re-sanitized prior to reentry.
  • Sterilized items should be introduced to the ISO 5 area by aseptic removal of the final wrap as it is being introduced.
  • Extra subassemblies and utensils should be sterilized and available for immediate use in the event a replacement is needed.
  • Sterile tools and utensils should be employed wherever possible to handle sterile materials, rather than direct contact with operator gloves. There should be sterile supports or hangers for tools inside the ISO 5 environment.
  • The process should be designed so that samples can be taken with minimal risk of contamination. When withdrawing samples, it is preferable to take all desired samples in a single step, then subdivide. Alternatively, the residual left post-production can be used as the test sample. Technologies such as sterile septum or connectors should be considered.
  • Wherever possible, materials being introduced should be pre-measured into a tightly sealed container prior to sterilization and addition.
  • Electrical equipment and controls pose a contamination risk and should be located outside the processing environment. If not possible, a second operator (not the primary) should adjust settings. Pay special attention to equipment which exhausts air (e.g., mixers, blenders).
  • Liquid transfers should be made using peristaltic pumps located outside the aseptic environment, rather than automatic pipettes (due to concerns regarding exhausted air). Containers can be pre-marked to indicate the amount of material to transfer.
  • Perform as much of the process inside the ISO 5 environment as possible to minimize removal and reentry of in-process materials.
  • When containers must be removed and later returned, they should be aseptically wrapped in a pre-sterilized covering, properly removed and discarded prior to reentry. Alternatively, the exterior can be re-sanitized.
  • Sanitize the operating environment when empty; sanitize each nonsterilizable item as it is first introduced. Do not introduce a large item into the environment in mid-process. Note: If the primary operator performs sanitization, sterile gloves/sleeves should be changed before aseptic manipulation.
  • Product contact surfaces shall be sterilized. Sterility should be maintained with protective layers which can be removed as materials are transitioned to cleaner environments.
  • Significant aseptic assembly in the processing environment should be avoided through the use of sterilized pre-assembled items.
  • Process steps not required to be aseptic should be performed outside the ISO 5 environment by other operator(s).
  • Once the process design has been established, it should be rehearsed several times and documented in air flow studies using all required items and placebo materials. The use of engineering runs to develop the process is strongly encouraged.
  • The manufacturing process should be documented in sufficient detail. The secondary or support operator(s) should complete the batch record.
  • Environmental monitoring practices should be non-intrusive. Air sampling during processing may be performed with specially designed equipment; surface monitoring should be performed using contact plates or swabs after processing has been completed.

First Air 原則：最核心的概念

First Air 是指從 HEPA 過濾器流出後、尚未接觸任何物體的初始氣流。產品、容器、封蓋必須始終暴露在 First Air 中，不能被任何物品或操作員手部遮擋。

違反 First Air 原則等同於將未過濾的空氣（可能含有微粒子）引入關鍵區域。

生活比喻：三人分工

想像一個外科手術團隊：主刀醫師（Primary operator）雙手始終在無菌區域內操作；刷手護士（Secondary operator）負責傳遞器械，必要時戴無菌手套；巡迴護士（Support operator）在非無菌區處理所有外部事務。三層分工確保主刀醫師不需要碰觸任何非無菌物品。

GMP 警示：蠕動泵 vs 自動移液管

液體轉移必須使用蠕動泵（泵體放在 ISO 5 外），而非自動移液管——因為自動移液管在操作時會向環境排出空氣，可能污染關鍵區域。這是很多早期 MAP 設施設計不當的地方。

GMP 警示：電氣設備排氣問題

攪拌器、混合器等設備在運轉時會排出熱氣或氣流，若放置在 ISO 5 區域內，排出的氣流會破壞單向氣流，造成微粒子污染。原則上電氣設備應放在 ISO 5 外部。

├── 任務: 物料引入/取出、協助次要任務
└── 著裝: 進入 ISO 5 前穿戴無菌手套

Support Operator (支援操作員)
├── 位置: 外圍環境 (ISO 7/8)
└── 任務: 批次紀錄、外部設備操作

7.2 Manual Aseptic Process Design Principles in Isolators and RABS

隔離器與限制進入屏障系統的製程設計原則

7.2 Design Principles — Isolators and RABS

The use of an isolator for manual aseptic processing requires some adaptation to the methods described above.

An isolator is sealed or is supplied with air through a microbially retentive filtration system (HEPA minimum) and may be reproducibly decontaminated. When closed it uses only decontaminated (where necessary) interfaces or Rapid Transfer Ports (RTPs) for materials transfer. When open it allows for the ingress and/or egress of materials through defined openings ("mouse holes") that have been designed and validated to preclude the transfer of contamination. Isolators can be used for aseptic processing activities, containment of potent compounds, or simultaneously for both asepsis and containment.

Restricted access barrier systems (RABS) vary with respect to their design. Where items are introduced using a rapid transfer port (RTP) their use resembles that of isolators. RABS lacking these features may have greater resemblance to manned clean rooms.

Regardless of the type of isolator or RABS system employed, it should be recognized that these are aseptic, not sterile, process enclosures. Therefore good aseptic technique must be used at all aseptic steps. First air principles should be adhered to where unidirectional flow is provided.

Key design and operational principles for isolators/RABS:

  • The enclosure should be decontaminated according to a validated procedure.
  • The operators should wear fresh sterile gloves prior to entering the enclosure gloves.
  • Sterile tools and utensils shall be employed rather than direct contact with the enclosure gloves. There should be sterile supports for tools inside the enclosure to minimize contact with horizontal surfaces.
  • All items and equipment should be introduced into the enclosure using validated decontamination/sterilization methods and/or RTPs.
  • It is preferable to perform as much of the process as possible inside the enclosure to minimize the removal and subsequent reentry of sterile items. When containers must be removed and returned, use validated decontamination/sterilization methods and/or RTPs, or aseptically wrapped pre-sterilized covering.
  • All exposed product and product-contacting components should always remain in First Air (if present). Aseptic manipulations should be made in First Air from the HEPA filters, not having passed over other components or blocked by enclosure gloves.
  • Significant aseptic assembly in the ISO 5 environment should be avoided through the use of sterilized pre-assembled items.
  • Extra subassemblies and utensils should be sterilized and available for immediate use.
  • Electrical equipment and controls should be located outside the processing enclosure if possible. If not possible, a second operator (not the primary) should adjust settings. Pay special attention to equipment which exhausts air.
  • Utilize secondary and support operator(s) to supply/remove items to/from the enclosed environment. Materials should be pre-measured into a tightly sealed container prior to sterilization and addition into the barrier.
  • Liquid transfers should be made using peristaltic pumps outside the enclosure rather than automatic pipettes.
  • Sampling: take all samples in a single step, then subdivide. Alternatively, use the residual post-production as the test sample.
  • Once the process design has been established, it should be rehearsed using all required items and placebo materials. Engineering runs to develop the process are strongly recommended.
  • The manufacturing process should be documented in sufficient detail.
  • Environmental monitoring should be non-intrusive. Air sampling during processing with specially designed equipment; surface monitoring by contact plates or swabs after processing.

生活比喻：隔離器是「太空艙」

隔離器就像太空站的氣密艙室：所有物品進出都必須透過特定的傳遞系統（RTPs），維持內外的屏障完整性。操作員透過厚手套（glove ports）或半身防護服進行操作，從未直接暴露在「外部」環境中。

重要認知：隔離器是「無菌」而非「滅菌」環境

TR62 明確指出：隔離器和 RABS 是 aseptic（無菌）製程封閉系統，而非 sterile（已滅菌）空間。這意味著內部始終存在微生物污染的可能性——除非每次使用前都完成驗證的除污程序，否則無法確保空間無菌性。因此，良好無菌技術在隔離器內同樣不能放鬆。

RTP（快速傳遞埠）的作用

RTP（Rapid Transfer Port）是隔離器物料進出的關鍵機構，設計為連接時形成密封屏障，防止外部污染進入。引入新物料時，外部容器的 RTP 蓋與隔離器的 RTP 座形成配對密封，開蓋後物料進入，全程不破壞無菌屏障。

GMP 警示：Hand Ports 與 Half-Suit 的選擇

手套埠（glove ports）是最常見的隔離器介面，但對於複雜操作，半身防護服（half-suit）提供更大的活動空間。選擇時需考量：操作複雜度、手套完整性檢測頻率、操作員訓練需求。半身防護服若密封失效，污染風險更高，需要更嚴格的監控。

CDMO 實務場景

在隔離器環境下進行臨床前細菌內毒素敏感製劑的 MAP 分裝時：(1) 每批生產前需完成 VHP 除污週期並確認殘留 H₂O₂ 濃度合格；(2) 所有玻璃器材、容器均透過 RTP 傳入；(3) 無法透過 RTP 傳入的大型設備，需在隔離器組裝前完成預滅菌並以驗證方式引入。工程試驗記錄需包含手套完整性測試結果。

8.0 Evaluation of Manual Aseptic Processing — Process Simulation

手動無菌製程評估 — 製程模擬

8.0 Introduction

The following are the elements of a MAP simulation that involve extensive participation of personnel in the aseptic process.

Details of study design provided in the APS reports on aseptic processing for either filling or sterile bulk production should be consulted for those activities that are essentially unchanged when manual procedures are employed.

中文解析

APS 在 MAP 中的核心地位

APS（Aseptic Process Simulation），即培養基充填，是驗證無菌製程可靠性的黃金標準。在 MAP 中，APS 同時承擔兩個功能：（1）驗證製程設計是否足夠安全；（2）驗證操作員個人的無菌技術是否達標。

TR62 以 TR22（Process Simulation for Aseptically Filled Products）為基礎，針對手動製程的特殊性加以調適。

生活比喻：模擬考試

APS 就像外科醫師的模擬手術考核：使用培養基（而非真正藥品）重現整個生產流程，如果培養基在孵育後出現微生物生長，代表操作中有某個環節污染了系統——這就是失敗，需要調查。

8.1

8.1 Simulation Design

模擬設計

8.1 Simulation Design

The development of a supporting rationale for the design of the manual aseptic process simulation is essential. The rationale must define the adaptations to the production process necessary for the execution of the simulation.

The smaller scale of the manual process lends itself to these adaptations and in many instances only minimal changes to the process are required.

In the event of a failure investigation, maintaining the sequence in which samples were filled during the APS can be beneficial in determining at what point contamination was introduced. Definition of these sample points should be included in the rationale.

The simulation rationale should be kept current with changes to process, products, components, or equipment that could impact the acceptability of the process.

8.1.1 Compositing/Assembly Activities

Compositing/assembly activities involve repetitive actions in which sterile materials in smaller amounts are pooled. Such practices are common in vaccine manufacturing in which the contents of incubated eggs are composited early in the formulation process.

Evaluation: For this category, adaptation of the validation methods for sterile filling and bulk materials may be appropriate for the simulation rationale.

8.1.2 Formulation/Compounding Activities

Formulation/compounding activities in a manual setting might use different equipment than automated processes due to the smaller scale of the operation. For example, laboratory glassware and utensils may be used to produce a sterile bulk formulation. The smaller scale of the operation may also mandate alternative transfer methods.

Evaluation: The methods utilized for sterile bulk materials may be appropriate in these processes.

8.1.3 Filling/Subdivision Activities (Including Lyophilization if Needed)

Filling/subdivision activities involve repetitive actions in which sterile materials are transferred from a bulk container into smaller containers, sealed and closed (or partially closed when lyophilization is planned). This practice is common in early development and early clinical stage manufacturing of sterile products, and in the manufacture of extremely small lot sizes.

Evaluation: These activities may use methods defined for automated filling.

8.1.4 Manual Manipulation Steps in Conjunction with Other Processes

Manual activities, such as sampling, aseptic connection, etc., are usually an integral part of other aseptic processes. As such there is no need to address them independently in the rationale for the study design.

Additional details on process-specific evaluation methods are to be provided in conjunction with each of the elements addressed within the overall process evaluation protocol/procedure.

8.2

8.2 Media Sterilization

培養基滅菌

8.2 Media Sterilization

The preparation of media for use in a MAP simulation is normally managed with few problems. If the media bulk container is small enough it can be sterilized in an autoclave prior to introduction into the process.

In other cases non-sterile bulk media may be filtered into the process in a manner similar to the drug product.

Where possible, sterilization methods, such as autoclaving or irradiating, should be combined with filtration to remove mycoplasma contamination from media components (for a more detailed discussion, see PDA Technical Report No. 22).

For compositing/assembly processes, suitable sterile materials to use in the process simulation may be unavailable. In such cases the choice may be to use the production materials themselves suitably sterilized/adapted (if possible and necessary) in the simulation, if they do not inhibit the growth of microorganisms.

For the APS each of the liquid containers containing sterile materials for the process should have their contents replaced with media.

中文解析

培養基的滅菌選項

  • 高壓滅菌（Autoclave）：小容量散裝培養基的首選，操作簡單
  • 過濾滅菌：模擬藥品過濾製程，同時去除培養基中的微粒
  • 輻照滅菌：可與過濾結合，特別用於去除黴漿菌污染

GMP 警示：黴漿菌污染的隱患

黴漿菌（Mycoplasma）是培養基中常見的潛在污染物，尤其在含動物源性成分的培養基（如 TSB 胰酪大豆肉湯）中。過濾滅菌（0.1 µm 膜）可有效去除黴漿菌，但需確認膜完整性。在生物製品 MAP 中，黴漿菌控制是合規重點。

GMP 警示：生產原料不能抑制微生物生長

某些合成/組裝製程（如疫苗）無法找到適合的無菌替代培養基，可能直接用生產原料代替。此時必須確認生產原料不含抑菌成分（如防腐劑、抗生素），否則 APS 結果無效——即使培養結果陰性，也不能代表製程是無菌的。

生活比喻

培養基就像微生物的「最佳餐廳」——專門設計讓任何細菌都能快速生長。如果培養基本身被污染，或者裡面有成分抑制細菌生長，那麼即使充填結果陰性，也不能相信這個結果。就像用變質的試紙做測試——結果毫無意義。

8.3

8.3 Frequency and Number of APS Runs

APS 執行頻率與次數

8.3 Frequency and Number of APS Runs

The recommendation for frequency and number of simulation studies (runs) for manual processing is that each operator (primary and secondary) should be qualified semi-annually (or in accordance with the applicable regulatory requirements).

Typically, three replicate APS studies are performed to initially qualify an operator and process. The combined duration and size of the APS runs will support both operator and process qualification.

Note: Operator qualification is critical since the operators are the primary means of production. More frequent studies may be required for infrequent manufacturing operations.

8.3.1 Duration of Runs

Simulation studies should meet or exceed the expected maximum duration of a single working session by a single operator.

8.3.2 Size of Runs

The size of the process simulation is largely dictated by the time period that a single operator would remain performing the same activity. The actual numbers of units produced in that time period should meet or exceed the production quantity that the operator(s) would normally handle in that time period.

As the operator is an integral part of the process and a key variable in MAP, it may be that each operator will have to perform the whole process simulation for the process to be considered valid especially where only small numbers are filled.

GMP 警示：每位操作員都需獨立完成

MAP 的特殊性在於：操作員本人就是製程的核心變數。因此，與自動化充填不同，MAP 的 APS 驗證是以操作員為單位的，而非僅以設備或製程為單位。每位 primary 和 secondary 操作員都需要獨立完成至少 3 次初次 APS，以及每半年的定期 APS。

生活比喻：半年一次的「駕照更新測驗」

MAP 操作員的 APS 資格認證類似飛行員的定期技術考核——不是考一次就永久有效，而是每半年需要重新確認技術水準。這是因為人類技術會因時間、疲勞、習慣而退化，需要定期再校準。

8.4 Observation of the Process Simulation

製程模擬的觀察

8.4 Observation of the Process Simulation

Considerations for observation of MAP simulations are consistent with observations of automated simulations, as described in PDA Technical Report No. 22:

The process simulation should be observed to assure that all planned activities are properly executed and represent an appropriate challenge to the process capability. Observation may also be used to augment aseptic conduct and technique training.

Observation should commence upon the initiation of the process simulation, including equipment set-up, and continue until the process simulation has completed. Observation of the simulation should be performed by individuals having the knowledge and competency to assess if operators have used proper aseptic conduct in their activities. These individuals should also be able to assess that aseptic interventions have been executed consistent with good practice so as to provide for realistic assessments of sterility control.

The process simulation observation should be documented and/or video recorded. The use of video recording of the APS allows activities to be reviewed in detail to assist with training or failure investigation.

中文解析

APS 觀察員的職責與資格

觀察員不是旁觀者，而是 APS 過程的關鍵記錄者和評估者。觀察員必須：

  • 具備無菌製程知識和能力，能判斷操作行為是否符合良好無菌技術
  • 從設備組裝開始全程在場，直到模擬結束
  • 能評估干預操作（interventions）是否以符合 GMP 的方式執行
  • 完整記錄所有觀察結果

GMP 警示：影像記錄的重要性

影像記錄不只是「留存資料」，而是失敗調查時的關鍵工具。當 APS 出現陽性結果（污染），影像記錄可以幫助確定：哪個時間點、哪個操作步驟、哪位操作員出現了偏差。沒有影像記錄，失敗調查幾乎只能依靠觀察員的主觀記憶，說服力大幅下降。

APS 觀察就像體育賽事的裁判——不只記錄比分，還需要評判每個動作是否符合規則。如果有爭議，影像回放（video replay）是最客觀的判斷依據。對 MAP 失敗調查而言，影像記錄的作用完全相同。

8.5

8.5 Media Fill Volume

培養基充填量

8.5 Media Fill Volume

In aseptic filling simulations the amount of media transferred during the simulation should be sufficient to wet the product contact surfaces of the container and be sufficient to detect growth.

In compositing simulations, the amount of media transferred should be identical to that normally handled in production to simulate the process duration more accurately.

In manual manufacturing simulations, the volumes of media and other fluids (which should all be replaced with media) should be identical to, or greater than that in the process to be simulated.

The goal is to assure that the sterile product components (e.g., containers, closures, and media) are exposed for the maximum length seen during routine manufacturing, thus assuring the simulation represents a comparable risk as routine processing.

中文解析

充填量的三個考量維度

  • 最低技術要求：足以潤濕容器所有產品接觸面，且足以支持微生物生長（通常 ≥ 1 mL / 瓶）
  • 製程代表性：充填量應等於或大於實際生產量，以確保模擬製程時間的代表性
  • 最大暴露時間：模擬應確保容器、封蓋、培養基的暴露時間達到生產中的最長狀況

GMP 警示：所有液體都應替換為培養基

在手動製程模擬中，用於沖洗、清洗或轉移的所有液體都應以培養基替代（不僅僅是最終充填液）。這確保整個製程的全部液體接觸表面都受到微生物挑戰，不遺漏任何潛在污染路徑。

充填量設定原則

充填量要求：
├── 最低: 足以潤濕所有產品接觸面
│         + 足以支持微生物生長
├── 模擬性: ≥ 生產批次的實際充填量
└── 目標: 暴露時間 = 生產最大暴露時間

注意: 所有製程液體（不只充填液）
       均應以培養基替代

8.6

8.6 Anaerobes / Inert Gassing

厭氧 / 惰性氣體充填

8.6 Anaerobes/Inert Gassing

The methods utilized for automated, machine-based aseptic processing can be adopted without change.

Air should be substituted for inert gases in all systems, except in those rare instances where an isolator providing true anaerobic conditions is utilized for the production process environment.

In such situations, the usual inert gas would be utilized and the appropriate media would generally be fluid thioglycollate media (FTM) or alternative FTM (if filtration of the media is necessary).

中文解析

為什麼用空氣替代惰性氣體？

生產時使用 N₂、Ar 等惰性氣體充填容器頂空，以防止藥品氧化。APS 時這些惰性氣體會創造低氧環境，抑制好氧菌的生長，可能導致 APS 假陰性結果。

因此：APS 時用空氣替代惰性氣體，確保好氧菌能在培養基中正常生長，使 APS 具備最大微生物挑戰性。

例外情況：真正的厭氧製程

少數產品（如某些厭氧菌製劑或特殊生物製品）需要在真正的厭氧隔離器中生產。此時 APS 應保留惰性氣體環境，並使用能在厭氧條件下生長的培養基（如 Fluid Thioglycollate Medium，FTM）。FTM 含有硫醇乙酸鹽，可清除氧氣，支持厭氧菌生長。

8.7

8.7 Environmental Monitoring

環境監控

8.7 Environmental Monitoring

Environmental monitoring for MAP uses methods generally the same as those for other highly controlled (ISO 5/7) environments. The same cautions exercised with monitoring in other systems apply to MAP as well.

Sampling activities must not introduce contamination into the environment or into the sample.

Since the environmental systems utilized for MAP are of smaller size, the monitoring methods must be chosen for their lack of impact on the environment.

A thorough understanding of the process should be used to ensure that areas of greatest risk are monitored at the appropriate frequency using the appropriate monitor.

中文解析

MAP 環境監控的特殊考量

MAP 設施比自動化設施小很多，這帶來環境監控的特殊挑戰：

  • 取樣設備可能因為佔用空間而干擾操作員或改變氣流
  • 取樣動作本身（如打開沉降盤）可能引入污染
  • 微粒子計數器的採樣管口若位置不當，會影響 First Air

解決方案：選擇非侵入式監控方法；主動取樣在製程中進行，表面取樣在製程後進行。

GMP 警示：監控的悖論

環境監控的目的是確認製程的無菌性，但監控行為本身也可能引入污染。這是一個典型的 GMP 悖論。TR62 的解決思路：使用「最高風險區域」識別來集中監控資源，並確保監控設備的設計和放置不會破壞待保護的環境。

8.10 Incubation Time / Temperature

孵育時間與溫度

8.10 Incubation Time/Temperature

The same incubation methods used for automated machine-based aseptic processing are appropriate for MAP and can be found in PDA Technical Report No. 22:

Prior to incubation, units should be inverted, and swirled or manipulated to ensure contact of the medium with all internal surfaces of the closure system before they are incubated.

Process simulation units should be incubated for a minimum of 14 days unless supported by another qualified duration/method.

The temperature chosen should be based upon its ability to recover microorganisms normally found environmentally or in the product bioburden. 促生長試驗的目的

促生長試驗（Growth Promotion Test, GPT）是驗證培養基仍具備支持微生物生長能力的測試：

8.13 Interpretation of Test Results

結果判讀

8.13 Interpretation of Test Results

Production lots produced by MAP are typically smaller than the current standard minimum simulation size of 5,000 units. Thus MAP simulations conducted in support of container filling must have a contamination frequency of zero (0) filled units. In compositing or formulation simulations, the simulated bulk material container(s) should be sterile.

Consistent with PDA Technical Report No. 22:

Regardless of the number of units filled during a process simulation or the number of positives allowed, the ultimate goal for the number of positives in any process simulation should be zero. A sterile product is, after all, one which contains no viable organisms.

There are, however, numerous technical problems in achieving this goal. Media and simulated product do not match real products perfectly in terms of their processing characteristics and microbiological growth-support properties. Media differ in many respects from the products they are intended to simulate; for example, there are differences in solubility, pH, filtration rates and filterability and viscosity.

With powdered products, the process simulation involves reconstituting powdered media or simulated product, introducing extra processing equipment or manipulation, with the inherent risk of contamination.

Since a microbiological medium is designed specifically to support or stimulate the growth of microorganisms, it is a more rigorous challenge than processed products, which often provide neutral and sometimes hostile microbial growth environments.

Although Technical Report No. 22 makes provision for some low number other than zero, in the case of manual aseptic processing this criterion must be set to zero due to the manual nature of the operation. The assumptions used for low numerical acceptance are not applicable for manual aseptic processing operations.

MAP APS 的黃金標準：零污染

生活比喻：零容忍的邏輯

想像用高度敏感的細菌培養板（而非真實食物）測試廚師的衛生習慣——如果連最容易讓細菌生長的環境都出現污染，那這個廚師的衛生習慣絕對有問題，不能用「只有一個污染點，其他都乾淨」來辯護。MAP 的零容忍邏輯完全相同。

9.0 Conclusion

Summary of manual aseptic process principles and key industry position | p21–p22

Original Text (English)

This technical report is one of the first attempts to address the subject of manual aseptic processing in a comprehensive manner. As such, it includes recommendations that may establish recommended practices in the absence of guidance from regulators or industry organizations.

Manufacturers that use manual aseptic processing to produce products used in patients must be aware of the uncertainties associated with a manufacturing process so heavily reliant on personnel performance.

In new installations, we strongly encourage the use of isolation technology to minimize the risk of microbial contamination from personnel involved in the manufacturing operations.

The operators are the primary means of production in MAP — and therefore the primary variable. Human performance is inherently variable, making process control and contamination prevention fundamentally different from automated aseptic processing.

The preceding sections of this report have addressed the full lifecycle of manual aseptic processing: regulatory context, facility and environment design, personnel training and qualification, equipment and materials considerations, aseptic technique, and process simulation (Aseptic Process Simulation / APS). Each of these elements must function together to maintain sterility assurance throughout manufacturing.

Key technical positions established in this report:

  • APS acceptance criterion for MAP: zero contaminated units — the statistical assumptions allowing low non-zero limits in automated filling do not apply to manual operations.
  • Operator qualification frequency: semi-annually for each operator (primary and secondary), with three initial replicates for new qualification.
  • Each operator must individually perform and pass APS — shared or grouped qualification is insufficient where operator variability is the key risk.
  • Isolation technology (RABS / isolators) is strongly preferred over open cleanroom environments for all new MAP installations.
  • Media fill simulation scope must cover the maximum production duration and replicate all critical interventions encountered during routine manufacturing.

中文解析與學習輔助

核心概念 — TR62 的歷史定位

TR62 是業界首份系統性處理手動無菌操作（MAP）的技術報告。在此之前，監管指引（如 FDA 2004 無菌指南）主要針對自動化設備，MAP 的相關規範存在空白。TR62 的角色是以業界共識填補這個空白，為監管機構尚未明文規範的情況提供建議實踐。

在監管環境中，此類共識文件具有重要地位：雖非法律強制，但在查廠時往往被視為業界現行最佳實踐的參考基準。

比喻 — MAP 就像外科手術

想像一台外科手術：無論手術室設備多先進，最終的無菌性在很大程度上取決於外科醫師的技術、紀律與即時判斷。同樣地，MAP 的無菌保證核心在「人」。

自動化無菌充填就像機械手臂輔助手術——人的影響降到最低；而 MAP 就像完全徒手操作——每一個動作都是變數。這正是 TR62 為何對 APS 採用比 TR22 更嚴格標準（零汙染）的根本原因。

最關鍵的合規要求

  • APS 零汙染標準不可妥協：MAP 的 APS 結果必須是零陽性，不接受任何統計性允許值。
  • 個別操作員各自合格：不能以「批次」方式通過 APS，每位操作員必須獨立完成並通過。
  • 半年一次再確認：已合格的操作員每半年須重新執行 APS，確保技術維持。
  • 新設施強烈建議隔離技術：若計畫新建 MAP 設施，TR62 明確建議採用隔離器或 RABS，而非傳統開放式潔淨室。

TR62 全文綜合摘要 — Sections 1–9 完整回顧

以下摘要涵蓋 PDA TR62 所有主要章節的核心內容，供學習後的自我測試與快速複習使用。

Section 1–2：範疇與背景

  • MAP 定義：人員直接參與開放無菌環境操作
  • 適用對象：小批量、特殊劑型、臨床試驗用製品
  • 法規背景：FDA 無菌指南、EU GMP Annex 1、USP <797>
  • MAP vs 自動化：人員是最大變數，無菌保證方法必須對應調整

Section 3：設施與環境

  • MAP 操作區必須維持 ISO 5（Class 100）環境
  • HEPA 過濾、單向氣流、正壓梯度為基本要求
  • RABS 與隔離器能提供額外的人員隔離屏障
  • 設施設計需考量人員動線、更衣流程與材料進出路徑

Section 4：人員訓練與資格確認

  • 所有接觸無菌區的人員必須完成正式無菌技術訓練
  • 訓練涵蓋：更衣程序、無菌操作行為、干預技術
  • 資格確認須通過 APS（媒體充填）——個別操作員各自測試
  • 持續訓練與再資格確認為維持合規的必要機制

Section 5：無菌技術

  • 第一氣流（First Air）原則：不得遮蔽 HEPA 出風口與暴露物品之間的氣流路徑
  • 禁止動作：快速移動、不必要說話、觸碰已滅菌表面
  • 正確干預手法：所有干預必須以無菌方式執行並記錄
  • PPE 要求：手套、口罩、無塵衣的正確穿戴順序

Section 6：設備與材料

  • 所有接觸產品的設備必須滅菌（SAL ≤ 10⁻⁶）
  • 容器與封蓋系統的選擇需考量手動操作的相容性
  • 滅菌方法：蒸氣滅菌（首選）、乾熱滅菌、輻射滅菌
  • 設備轉移入 ISO 5 區的流程必須驗證以防止汙染

Section 7：製程控制

  • 批次記錄（BPR）必須即時記錄所有關鍵操作步驟
  • 在線觀察員：建議指定獨立觀察員全程監控並記錄
  • 關鍵控制點（CCP）識別：哪些步驟若出錯將直接影響無菌性
  • 偏差處理：任何觀察到的無菌技術偏差必須立即記錄並評估

Section 8：無菌製程模擬（APS）

  • 媒體充填設計：必須涵蓋最長生產時間、最壞情況干預
  • 頻率：半年一次，初始三次重複 APS 通過後方可正式生產
  • 接受標準：MAP 的 APS 必須達到零汙染（不接受統計允許值）
  • 培養條件：最低 14 天，20–35°C，事前目視檢查去除缺陷品
  • 生長促進試驗：APS 後以藥典方法（USP <71>）確認培養基有效性

Section 9：結論與策略方向

  • TR62 是 MAP 領域首份系統性業界共識文件
  • 人員是 MAP 的核心變數，也是最大的無菌風險來源
  • 所有新 MAP 設施應優先考慮隔離技術（RABS/隔離器）
  • 製造商必須充分認識 MAP 製程的不確定性並建立相應控制體系
