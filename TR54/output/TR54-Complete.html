Foreword — 前言

Joint Initiative between IPEC Federation and PDA

In March 2018, the IPEC Federation and PDA announced their first joint initiative. Both associations signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on the development of a joint technical report on excipient good manufacturing practices risk assessment in response to input from their respective memberships.

Prior to the collaboration announcement, however, both parties were active on the topic of quality risk management. PDA published the PDA Technical Report No. 54 series. In March 2016, IPEC Europe published the How-To Document – A Guide to Support Manufacturing Authorization Holders (MAH) in their Compliance with the European Commission Guidelines on Risk Assessment for Excipients (2015/C 95/02). Subsequently, in May 2017, IPEC-Americas and IPEC Europe jointly published the Risk Assessment Guide for Excipient Makers, Users, and Distributors.

Both PDA and IPEC Federation believe that presenting a common approach to the legal, regulatory, and related issues concerning excipients is best done as "one voice." Both collaborators see significant potential benefit in leveraging the two organizations' expertise on excipients and drug product manufacture. This technical report will serve as a practical guidance intended for use with existing regulatory and industry standards. The authors expect that the document will enable Manufacturing Authorization Holders of drug product to either set up or benchmark their quality systems, and further establish or continue to collaborate with parties in their excipient supply chain.

This joint PDA-IPEC technical report extends the PDA Technical Report No. 54 series and provides guidance on risk assessments for excipients by presenting a model risk assessment, guidance on key elements, and a collection of actual examples from excipient users in the pharmaceutical industry.

1.0 Introduction — 導論

1.0 Introduction

Excipients are integrated into the drug product formulation for various reasons: to aid the drug product formulation, to serve as functional or nonfunctional ingredients, or to enable the drug to be released from the drug product in a particular, desired manner. The rationale for their use extends beyond the point of medical use to include maintenance of the safety or function of the drug product, even during storage. While good manufacturing practices (GMP) regulations clearly outline what is required for active pharmaceutical ingredients, only a basic framework exists for ascertaining appropriate GMP for excipients through risk assessment.

In recognition of the criticality of excipients in the production of drug products, the European Commission (EC) Falsified Medicines Directive (2011/62/EU) revised Article 46(f) of Directive 2001/83 to require manufacturing authorization holders to verify that any excipients used in a product are made according to appropriate GMP standards. The Falsified Medicines Directive also committed the EC to publishing Guidelines on the formalised risk assessment for ascertaining the appropriate GMP for excipients of medicinal products for human use (EC Guidelines), which were published on March 19, 2015. In 2018, PIC/S incorporated the same provisions for formalized risk assessment into a publication of the same name, extending the provisions to have global applicability.

In the U.S., the 2012 Food and Drug Administration Safety and Innovation Act (FDASIA) redefined "current good manufacturing practices" to include "the implementation of oversight and controls over the manufacture of drugs to ensure quality, including managing the risk of and establishing the safety of raw materials [and] materials used in the manufacturing of drugs," in addition to the final drug product. This change also emphasizes the criticality of excipients.

1.0 Introduction (continued) — MAH Accountability & Lifecycle

Although the accountability for risk assessment rests entirely with the manufacturing authorization holder, all parties in the supply chain are required to support the process by providing relevant information (e.g., Excipient Information Packages (EIP)) to the manufacturing authorization holder. The risk management process is applicable throughout the excipient's lifecycle, that is, from selection through termination of use, and remains the responsibility of the manufacturing authorization holder at each stage.

Over the course of an excipient's lifecycle, there will be a broad array of drivers for risk assessment and reassessment. Those drivers may include, among others, product deviations, market recalls, changes in excipient use, supply chain amendments, and adverse trends in quality. The manufacturing authorization holder is responsible for providing evidence that the risk management approach adopted is commensurate with the level of risk to ensure the safety, purity and other quality characteristics of the excipient in use.

To comply with the EC Guidelines and PIC/S publication, each excipient used in a drug product must be assessed for the risks that it poses to the product's quality, safety, and purity. From these risks, the manufacturing authorization holder must determine the quality standards required for the manufacture of the excipient. The manufacturing authorization holder should communicate these quality standards to the excipient supplier and seek the supplier's agreement. Any confirmed gaps in quality standards determined by the assessment must be appropriately mitigated, which may require the manufacturing authorization holder to take action on its own or through collaboration with the supplier. To ensure that risk assessments remain effective during the excipient's lifecycle, the manufacturing authorization holder must incorporate a periodic review into the excipient risk assessment process.

1.1 Purpose — 目的

1.1 Purpose

This report is based on the general principles of quality risk management (QRM) as outlined in ICH Quality Guideline Q9 Quality Risk Management. It has been developed to provide additional guidance on the excipient risk assessment process required by the EC Guidelines and incorporated into the PIC/S publication. In addition, TR 54-6 presents a model risk assessment and real-world examples from the pharmaceutical industry applicable to excipient use for all dosage forms to illustrate risk assessment as an excipient knowledge management solution.

In general, the model presented in this report is based on the risk ranking and filtering method as referenced in ICH Q9. It also addresses pertinent factors such as end-to-end supply chain, control strategy, and benefits and challenges, among others. TR 54-6 will also serve as a practical guidance intended for use in conjunction with existing regulatory and industry standards; however, it does not replace or alter existing statutory and regulatory requirements for assessing excipients for use in medicinal drug products.

Despite the clarity and guidance offered by the EC Guidelines, risk assessors for manufacturing authorization holders still face some challenges in implementing and benchmarking the excipient risk assessment process for two main reasons. First, quality systems applied during the manufacture of materials used as excipients are diverse and generally based on the material's intended use, which may not be primarily as a pharmaceutical excipient. Therefore, adjusting an excipient manufacturer's or supplier's quality systems to meet the pharmaceutical quality system outlined in the guidelines could prove challenging.

Second, an excipient may be used in different dosage forms by manufacturing authorization holders. One manufacturing authorization holder may use a single excipient in both tablets and injectables, which may result in different quality requirements applying to a single excipient from the same supplier. This also may lead to conflicting requirements for GMP.

中文解析：TR 54-6 的目的與兩大挑戰

核心概念：ICH Q9 風險排序與篩選法

TR 54-6 的風險評估模型基於 ICH Q9 的兩大工具：

  • Risk Ranking and Filtering (風險排序與篩選)：根據多個標準（給藥途徑、功能性、供應鏈複雜度等）對賦形劑進行評分，得出風險等級 (Low/Medium/High)
  • ICH Q9 一般原則：風險識別 → 風險分析 → 風險評估 → 風險控制 → 溝通 → 審查

TR 54-6 的創新：提供真實案例（來自製藥業賦形劑用戶），將抽象框架具體化。

兩大實務挑戰

挑戰一：賦形劑製造商的 GMP 多元性

許多賦形劑（如乙醇、丙二醇、蔗糖）的主要用途是食品、化妝品，其 GMP 標準（如 Food GMP、Cosmetics GMP）與藥品 GMP 要求差距很大。MAH 必須評估這些差距並決定是否可接受。

挑戰二：同一賦形劑用於不同劑型

例如：Polysorbate 80 同時用於口服膜衣錠和靜脈注射劑型。口服用途的 GMP 要求遠低於注射用途。同一家供應商可能收到兩份不同的 GMP 要求，產生衝突。

比喻：「知識管理解決方案」是什麼意思？

把賦形劑風險評估想成「建立一份供應商知識檔案」。每次評估後產生的文件（風險分數、供應商 GMP 等級、稽核記錄）累積成組織知識，下次評估或供應商變更時可快速參照，不必從零開始。

這就是 TR 54-6 所說的 excipient knowledge management solution。

思考練習

你的CDMO生產一款凍乾注射劑，使用蔗糖作為凍乾保護劑。同一批蔗糖供應商也供應你們的口服固體製劑工廠。按照 TR 54-6 的邏輯，注射劑和口服製劑對同一家供應商的 GMP 要求應該相同嗎？為什麼？

提示：不同。注射劑給藥途徑風險更高，因此對供應商的 GMP 要求更嚴格。同一家供應商可能需要滿足兩套不同的品質要求。

CDMO 實務應用

無菌填充 CDMO 面臨挑戰二的典型場景：客戶 A 用凍乾保護劑做注射劑，客戶 B 用相同賦形劑做口服錠。MAH 分別是客戶 A 和客戶 B，各自需要對同一供應商做風險評估，可能得出不同的 GMP 要求。CDMO 作為製造服務方，需要協助客戶（MAH）取得並整合這些評估資料。

1.2 Scope — 適用範圍

1.2 Scope

The information in TR 54-6 applies to all excipients used in drug products for human use at all stages of the product lifecycle. It assumes the excipient will be used for the drug product in the commercial supply phase; however, the manufacturing authorization holder may choose to apply this practical guidance in the clinical phase as well. That said, this technical report refrains from providing guidance to the manufacturing authorization holder regarding at which clinical phase it should be applied.

"The risk management process is applicable throughout the excipient's lifecycle, that is, from selection through termination of use, and remains the responsibility of the manufacturing authorization holder at each stage."

中文解析：適用範圍詳解

適用範圍三個維度

  • 賦形劑類型：所有人用藥品中的賦形劑（無例外）— 包括無菌注射劑、口服固體製劑、外用製劑、生物製劑輔料等
  • 產品生命週期階段：主要適用於商業供應階段 (Commercial Supply Phase)；臨床階段可選擇性適用，但本技術報告不規定具體從哪個臨床階段開始
  • 賦形劑生命週期：從選擇 (Selection) 到終止使用 (Termination) 的全過程

商業階段 vs. 臨床階段的實務差異

商業階段（本報告重點）：賦形劑供應鏈固定、供應量大、QMS 成熟，需要完整的正式風險評估文件。

臨床階段（可選擇適用）：供應鏈仍在建立中，賦形劑用量小，TR 54-6 不強制，但建議早期建立風險評估習慣，以便進入商業階段時能順利過渡。

重要提示：許多法規（如 EU FMD Article 46(f)）在商業藥品製造時才有強制性，臨床試驗材料有不同的監管路徑。

比喻：「適用範圍」如同保單條款

保單（TR 54-6）的承保範圍（Scope）是「所有人用藥品賦形劑」。就像汽車保險只保私家車，不一定保貨運車輛。知道邊界，才能正確引用。

臨床階段的賦形劑就像「試駕車輛」— 保單可以延伸覆蓋（MAH 自行決定），但不是默認保障。

思考練習

一個生物製劑 CDMO 正在為客戶生產 Phase 2 臨床試驗用注射劑。賦形劑包括：聚山梨醇酯 80、組胺酸緩衝液、蔗糖。根據 TR 54-6 的 Scope，CDMO 或 MAH 現在必須做賦形劑風險評估嗎？

流程圖的核心邏輯

中文解析 Commentary

核心概念：供應鏈角色的法規意義

TR54-6 的詞彙定義不是學術性的，而是直接決定你的供應商資格審查（Supplier Qualification）範圍。作為 MAH 或 CDMO，你需要明確判定每一個供應鏈參與者屬於哪種角色，因為不同角色對應不同的稽核要求與品質協議（Quality Agreement）義務。

最需要清楚區分的三組關係：

  • Original Manufacturer vs Distributor：後者取得法律所有權但不製造
  • Distributor vs Broker/Trader：前者有實體操作，後者完全沒有
  • Supplier（廣義）vs 以上所有角色：Supplier 是通用詞，但在本報告中不包含原廠

類比：供應鏈角色像房地產市場

想像輔料供應鏈像一個房地產市場：

  • Original Manufacturer = 建商（實際蓋房子的人）
  • Distributor = 房仲公司（買下房產、持有所有權、可進行裝修後再賣）
  • Broker/Trader = 純仲介（媒合買賣雙方、從不持有房產、不搬家具）
  • Haulier = 搬家公司（只負責從 A 到 B 的移動）
  • Warehousing Company = 倉儲業者（只提供儲存空間）
  • Shipping Agent = 報關行（港口清關、文件處理，有時也短暫持有貨物）
  • End User = 最終買家（使用房子的人，即藥廠）

關鍵差異：Broker 永遠不「碰」貨；Distributor 有法律所有權；Haulier 只是運輸。

重點提示：Broker 的特殊風險

Broker（仲介商）在風險評估中特別棘手，原因在於：

  • 他們沒有實體操作輔料——沒有倉庫、不搬運、不再包裝
  • 但他們控制文件流、可能影響可追溯性（Traceability）
  • 如果 Broker 不提供進入其品質系統的管道，你作為 End User 就無法追溯到 Original Manufacturer
  • 這直接影響 Supply Chain Profile（供應鏈剖析）的完整性——這是 Section 6.0 風險評估的基礎

實務結論：對 Broker 的 Supplier Qualification 重點在於文件透明度與品質協議，而非 GMP 廠房稽核。

Relabeling 與 Repackaging 的可追溯性風險

Relabeling：換貼新標籤。可追溯性只有在 Relabeler 提供品質系統存取管道時才能保證。

Repacking/Repackaging：換不同包裝。通常伴隨重新貼標，兩者在實務上常同時發生。

風險點：一旦貼上新標籤，如果沒有 CoA（Certificate of Analysis）鏈，就無法確認批次來源。CDMO 在採購非原廠包裝輔料時，必須要求完整的 CoA 追溯鏈直至原廠。

CDMO 應用場景

假設你的輔料供應商是一家台灣進口商（Distributor），他們從德國原廠採購後重新分裝成小包裝販售。在這個供應鏈裡：

  • 德國廠 = Original Manufacturer（需評估其 GMP 等級）
  • 台灣進口商 = Distributor（取得法律所有權、進行 Repacking）
  • 若台灣進口商只是媒合、貨從德國直送你的工廠 = Broker/Trader

這個區分決定你的 Supplier Questionnaire 要問哪些問題，以及是否需要到台灣進口商做現場稽核。

中文解析 Commentary

風險三層次：Risk → Risk Analysis → Risk Assessment

這三個術語在 ICH Q9 框架下有明確的階層關係，不能混用：

  • Risk（風險）= 危害發生的機率 × 嚴重性。這是一個數值或程度概念。
  • Risk Analysis（風險分析）= 針對已識別的危害，估算其風險大小。這是計算過程。
  • Risk Assessment（風險評估）= 更完整的系統性程序，包含：危害識別 → 風險分析 → 風險評價。這是整個框架，包含了 Risk Analysis。

TR54-6 做的正是 Risk Assessment：系統性評估輔料在供應鏈每個節點的危害，最終回答「這個輔料是否適合用於藥品？」

類比：CQA 與 QTPP 的關係

QTPP 是「理想的最終產品長什麼樣」——你希望這個注射劑的 pH、滲透壓、無菌性都符合什麼規格。

CQA 是「哪些特性必須受控以確保達到 QTPP」——例如輔料的粒徑分佈（影響溶解度）、水分含量（影響穩定性）。

TR54-6 的風險評估邏輯是：先確立 QTPP → 推導輔料的 CQA → 評估供應鏈是否會危害這些 CQA。

RPN 的計算與限制

RPN（Risk Priority Number）是量化風險的工具，常見公式為：

RPN = Severity × Probability × Detectability

TR54-6 在 Section 6.0 中使用 Risk Ranking and Filtering 方法，RPN 用於排序危害的優先順序。注意：RPN 本身的絕對數值不重要，重要的是相對排序與閾值設定——哪個 RPN 以上需要採取控制措施。

Residual Risk = 採取控制措施後，剩餘的 RPN 或風險程度。你的 QRM 程序需要定義可接受的 Residual Risk 水準。

Supply Chain 定義的起點與終點

TR54-6 對 Supply Chain 的定義有明確的時間邊界：

  • 起點：輔料離開原廠物料管理系統的控制範圍之後
  • 終點：End User 實際使用時

這意味著原廠內部的製造過程不在 TR54-6 供應鏈評估範圍內（原廠製造有其他 GMP 標準管轄）。TR54-6 關注的是輔料「出廠後到你手上」這段旅程中的所有風險節點。

3.0 Overall Strategy – A Holistic View

TR 54-6 provides guidance on quality risk management principles that can be used to assess the risks to the quality, safety, and function of an excipient in a drug product. The proposed generic model allows the user to answer the risk question: "Is the excipient fit for use in the drug product?" through the use of a formalized risk assessment.

The model (Section 6.0 and Figure 6.0-1) describes the steps involved in identifying whether an excipient is fit for use considering:

  • An assessment of its use and function for the excipient user
  • An assessment of the entire supply chain

In the model, the potential hazards from the excipient itself, as well as the supply chain, are ranked and evaluated using a risk ranking and filtering approach to evaluate the overall risk of harm to the drug product and, through that, to patient safety.

GMP Standards Referenced

Based on the results of the risk assessment, a pharmaceutical manufacturer can determine what level of GMP will be required. The widely accepted guides/standards for excipient GMPs include:

  • EXCiPACT™: International Pharmaceutical Excipients Certification Standards for Pharmaceutical Excipient Suppliers
  • NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363: Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) for Pharmaceutical Excipients
  • IPEC/PQG: The Joint Good Manufacturing Practices Guide for Pharmaceutical Excipients
  • USP: USP <1078> Good Manufacturing Practices for Bulk Pharmaceutical Excipients and <1059> Excipient Performance
  • Japan: Self-imposed Standards of Good Manufacturing Practices for Pharmaceutical Excipients

Collaboration and Documentation Requirements

As part of the risk assessment process, the manufacturer or MAH should engage the excipient manufacturer and suppliers to obtain specifics from the excipient manufacturer's own risk assessment. The complete supply chain profile must be considered for each excipient.

Any actions recommended as a result of the risk assessment must be discussed and agreed upon by the excipient manufacturer and the manufacturer of the drug product or MAH before being implemented, in case those actions may impact the supplier. The results should be documented in a quality agreement or other document.

Global vs. Local Risk Assessment

A global risk assessment of all excipients used by the drug product manufacturer or MAH will ensure a standardized assessment of all excipients in all drug products and provide a holistic view of the supply chain. This enables the excipient user to communicate with one voice to the excipient manufacturer — a single (worst case) risk assessment.

In a global risk assessment, risk review and improvements may be more difficult. This process could be shortened by instituting a global risk assessment that involves local ownership: each product or manufacturing site conducts a separate risk assessment, all results are collected in a single document repository, and local owners maintain responsibility for data collection, review, and approval workflows.

中文解析 Commentary

核心問題：輔料是否適合用於藥品？

TR54-6 的整體策略圍繞一個核心問題：「Is the excipient fit for use in the drug product?」。這不是一個是/否的問題，而是一個需要系統性評估兩個維度的問題：

  • 維度一：輔料本身的用途與功能——這個輔料的 CQA 是否符合製劑的 QTPP？
  • 維度二：供應鏈完整性——從原廠到你手上的每個節點是否引入了新的危害？

只有兩個維度都通過評估，才能判定輔料「適合使用」。這也解釋了為什麼 Broker（沒有實體操作）仍然需要評估——因為它影響供應鏈的文件可追溯性。

五大輔料 GMP 標準的定位

TR54-6 列出的五個標準並非互相競爭，而是各有適用場景：

  • EXCiPACT™：第三方認證體系，有稽核+認證機制，適合快速確認供應商資格
  • NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363：美國標準，ISO 格式，常見於北美市場
  • IPEC/PQG：歐洲導向，PQG（Pharmaceutical Quality Group）是英國 CQI 分支
  • USP <1078>：專注於散裝輔料製造，有法規強制性（美國 NDA/ANDA）
  • 日本自主標準：適用於日本市場輔料採購稽核

TR54-6 的風險評估結果決定你需要輔料供應商符合哪個等級的標準。高風險輔料需要更嚴格的 GMP（如 EXCiPACT 認證）；低風險輔料可能一般 GDP 即足夠。

類比：全球風險評估 vs 各地獨立評估

想像一家跨國連鎖餐廳（MAH）在全球 20 個城市使用同一款食鹽（輔料）：

  • 全球統一評估：總部做一份完整評估，所有分店套用。效率高，但更新時需要全球審批，流程慢。
  • 各地獨立評估：每個分店各自評估。彈性高，但可能造成「台北分店認為高風險、上海分店認為低風險」的矛盾。
  • TR54-6 推薦的混合模式：各地做評估（Local Ownership）→ 彙整到統一的文件庫（Single Repository）→ 對外以「一個聲音」溝通原廠。

這個模式的精髓是：用最壞情況（Worst Case）代表所有評估結果，避免同一個供應商收到矛盾的要求。

品質協議（Quality Agreement）的角色

Section 3.0 明確指出：風險評估建議的任何行動，必須在實施前與輔料製造商（或供應商）討論並達成共識，結果需記錄於品質協議或其他文件中。

這意味著：

  • 你不能單方面要求供應商增加測試項目而不知會對方
  • 任何變更（如抽樣計畫調整）需要雙方同意
  • 品質協議是風險控制措施的法律基礎文件

對 CDMO 而言，這通常意味著你的 QA 部門需要與客戶（MAH）及輔料供應商同時維持兩份品質協議，明確各方在輔料風險管理中的責任邊界。

CDMO 的全球評估策略設計

如果你的 CDMO 有多個製造廠址（如台灣廠 + 中國廠），且都使用同一批輔料供應商，TR54-6 的建議是：

  • 各廠址由本地 QA 負責人（Local Owner）維護各自的輔料風險評估
  • 所有評估彙整至集團層級的文件庫（如 QMS 平台）
  • 與供應商溝通時，以最嚴格的那份評估（Worst Case）作為集團統一立場
  • 避免台灣廠和中國廠向同一供應商傳遞不同的風險等級信號

3.0 Overall Strategy

Based on the results of the risk assessment, a pharmaceutical manufacturer can determine what level of GMP will be required. The guides and standards most widely accepted for excipient GMPs are:

  • EXCiPACT™: International Pharmaceutical Excipients Certification Standards for Pharmaceutical Excipient Suppliers
  • NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363: Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP) for Pharmaceutical Excipients
  • IPEC/PQG: The Joint Good Manufacturing Practices Guide for Pharmaceutical Excipients
  • USP: USP <1078> Good Manufacturing Practices for Bulk Pharmaceutical Excipients and <1059> Excipient Performance
  • Japan: Self-imposed Standards of Good Manufacturing Practices for Pharmaceutical Excipients

The pharmaceutical manufacturer may determine that some additional technical considerations beyond those in established GMP guides and standards may be needed. As part of the risk assessment process, the manufacturer or manufacturing authorization holder of the drug product should engage the excipient manufacturer and suppliers to obtain any specifics from the excipient manufacturer's own risk assessment process that the excipient manufacturer is willing to share. Additionally, the complete supply chain profile must be considered for each excipient.

Should the risk assessment uncover an issue requiring additional action, a control strategy should be put in place and the affected parties should be notified.

Before being implemented, any actions recommended as a result of the risk assessment must be discussed and agreed upon by the excipient manufacturer and the manufacturer of the drug product or manufacturing authorization holder, in case those actions may impact the supplier. The results of the discussion should be documented in a quality agreement or other document.

A global risk assessment of all excipients used by the drug product manufacturer or manufacturing authorization holder will ensure a standardized assessment of all excipients in all drug products and provide a holistic view of the supply chain. This enables the excipient user to provide the excipient manufacturer, with one voice, a single (worst case) risk assessment.

That approach is preferable to having multiple users within a single drug product manufacturer each communicating different assessment results to the excipient manufacturer.

In a global risk assessment, risk review and improvements of the risk assessment might be more difficult than they would be if separate risk assessments were completed for each drug product or by each site using the excipient. The review and approval workflows for a single global risk assessment may require more time and effort. However, this process could be shortened by instituting a global risk assessment that involves local ownership. In this scenario, the owner of each product or manufacturing site would conduct a separate risk assessment, but all risk assessments would be collected in a single document repository. The local owner would maintain responsibility for the data collection, review, and approval workflows, and for ensuring that updated information is included in the global risk assessment repository. The repository would serve as a knowledge base for all excipients, allowing the drug product manufacturer or manufacturing authorization holder to communicate its requirements to the excipient manufacturer with a single voice representing all local owners.

4.0 Supply Chain — End to End

Section 4.0 provides examples of supply chains, helps identify the various actors in the supply chain, and describes their typical roles. The subsections herein offer examples of the types of information and documentation that the various actors could provide. Additionally, it discusses some aspects of the potential risks that could be introduced by actors in the supply chain.

Supply chains have become more and more complex over the years. In the past, a manufacturer of pharmaceutical excipients often supplied directly to a drug product manufacturer. Today, in addition to the manufacturer of the excipient, the distributor, broker, and contract manufacturing organization (CMO) each plays a role in the supply chain, and each contributes to the risks to quality in one way or another. Each of these companies also could provide information on the excipient's manufacture, testing, and distribution that might be relevant for the risk assessment.

4.1 Types of Supply Chains and Risks Presented

Many materials used as excipients are not manufactured specifically for pharmaceutical use, but are intended for other applications such as food, cosmetics, or home care. As such, the direct supply of an excipient from the manufacturer to the end user may be unusual. In addition, the quantities of excipients used by the pharmaceutical end user may be only a fraction of the excipient manufacturer's overall volume; in fact, the original manufacturer may not have a business interest in supplying the pharmaceutical industry. In such cases, intermediaries often become involved, procuring larger quantities of the excipient from the original manufacturer, and then repackaging and relabeling it into quantities more suitable for pharmaceutical use. Thus, the supply chain between the excipient manufacturer and the pharmaceutical end user often is complex. The complexity is magnified for excipients that move internationally.

In both international and domestic supply chains, the point at which the legal ownership of the excipient transfers from one organization to another is a critical juncture. Each organization needs to have defined responsibilities for maintaining records and securing the quality and security of the excipient. In complex supply chains, legal ownership may transfer several times, for example from original manufacturer to distributor to repackager to end user.

In all cases, the end user needs to identify the intermediaries in the supply chain in order to properly assess the risks. This may be difficult, as some organizations in the supply chain may not want to disclose their sources, even including the original manufacturer of the excipient.

國際供應鏈的核心風險：當事方每批可能都不同

TR54-6 點出了國際供應鏈最難管控的特點：每一批貨的運輸公司、船運代理、倉儲設施都可能不同。

4.1.6 Manufacturer and Contract Manufacturing Organizations

When one or more CMOs are involved in the supply chain, it is critically important to clearly identify and understand which party or parties gather information and who owns the risk assessment.

The manufacturing authorization holder is ultimately responsible for the risk assessment. Depending on the quality agreement and relationship between the CMO and manufacturing authorization holder, there generally are three options for conducting the risk assessment:

  • The CMO might carry out the entire risk assessment, collecting the information about the use and function of the excipient in the final drug product. That risk assessment would be reviewed and approved by the manufacturing authorization holder.
  • The risk assessment might be carried out by the manufacturing authorization holder, incorporating information from the CMO.
  • The risk assessment might be carried out by the manufacturing authorization holder based on the risk assessment delivered by the CMO.

In any case, the roles and responsibilities for the CMO and manufacturing authorization holder must be clearly defined, including what actions will be taken if the risk assessments completed by the CMO and manufacturing authorization holder result in different outcomes.

Section 6.1 — Initiate Risk Management Activities and Identify Assessment Team

The risk assessment process begins with the identification of a need to qualify an excipient or to review an existing excipient risk assessment (e.g., at new product introduction, as a result of obtaining a second source, being notified by the supplier of major changes, or one of the numerous other drivers discussed below in Section 7.1).

A successful excipient risk assessment program requires a holistic approach encompassing knowledge of the excipient's use in the pharmaceutical dosage form as well as knowledge of the excipient's manufacturer and supply chain. The assessment team should include individuals from various functional areas, including subject matter experts (SME) from the drug product manufacturer who understand the excipient's use in the drug product, as well as individuals from the functions involved in oversight of the supply chain.

The risk assessment process might include participants with defined roles and responsibilities in the functional QRM areas and others, such as:

  • QRM — risk assessment facilitator knowledgeable about the methodology and tools
  • QRM experts — area or function leads for QRM or the QRM decision-makers
  • Quality Assurance — focused on use and function
  • Quality — focused on oversight activities related to supply chain (e.g., excipient manufacturer, supplier, transport)
  • Qualified / Responsible Person
  • Material Sciences
  • Quality Control
  • Regulatory Affairs
  • Technical / Operations
  • Sterility Assurance
  • Manufacturer or Supplier Auditing
  • Procurement
  • Outsourcing Operations — oversight activities
  • R&D
  • Engineering
  • Nonclinical Statistics
  • Legal
  • Manufacturing
  • Medical / Clinical
  • Safety and Pharmacology / Toxicology

Having a multidisciplinary team of individuals working collaboratively to gather and review data and assess the potential for risk is critical to ensuring that all risks are considered holistically. Section 6.2 — Identify Intrinsic Risk Factors

In identifying the excipient's intrinsic risk factors, numerous factors should be considered. One primary source is input from product and process development. The Quality Target Product Profile (QTPP) of the drug product(s) in which the excipient is to be used provides requirements for the drug product, and this forms the basis of information about the use and function of the excipient.

The manufacturing authorization holder assesses the drug product requirements to develop the drug product critical quality attributes (CQA) and to establish the foundation for drug product design and development. During the drug product's design and development, scientific studies are conducted and information is collected concerning the excipient, particularly about how it may interact with the product's other components.

Drug product development and process design studies, commercial manufacturing, and associated risk assessments for this product and any other products in which the excipient has been used, provide historical information about the excipient that can be used to assess its impact on the targeted drug product.

In addition, examples of parameters that can be considered as excipient attributes include:

  • Characteristics of excipient needed for use in the drug product
  • Manufacturer's excipient specifications
  • Factors learned from the manufacture of the excipients
  • Existence of multiple and/or global manufacturers
  • Lot-to-lot variability
  • Risk factors listed in the EC Guidelines of March 19, 2015, such as:
    • Transmissible spongiform encephalopathy (TSE)
    • Potential for viral contamination
    • Potential for microbiological or endotoxin/pyrogen contamination
    • Potential for any impurity originating from raw materials (e.g., aflatoxins, pesticides) or generated as part of the process (e.g., residual solvents and catalysts)
    • Sterility assurance for excipients claimed to be sterile
    • Potential for any impurities carried over from other processes, in the absence of dedicated equipment and/or facilities
    • Environmental control and storage/transportation conditions, including cold chain management if appropriate
    • Excipient stability
    • Packaging integrity evidence

Section 6.3 — Excipient Risk Analysis

The excipient risk analysis can be carried out in two steps. First, an initial excipient impact assessment can be conducted to provide input to the internal specification of the excipients, to identify intrinsic risk factors relating to the excipient's impact on the drug product. This assessment is used in the second step — the subsequent risk-scoring of the excipients.

Section 6.3.1 — Impact of Excipient on Each Type of Drug Product

After identification as described in Section 6.2, the excipient attributes are assessed for correlation to the CQAs of the drug product in which the excipient is to be used. The rationale of the impact must be explained. This step must be taken for each drug product or drug product family. Then, the results can be consolidated across products.

Initially, all potential risk factors (hazards) are identified and documented as excipient attributes. Then, CQAs are correlated to the pharmaceutical dosage form using the following notation:

ERPN 公式解析

ERPN（Excipient Risk Priority Number）是 TR54-6 的核心輸出指標：

ERPN = f(Impact × Probability)

ERPN = (RF_E1 + RF_E2 + RF_E3 + RF_E4 + RF_E5 + RF_E6 + RF_E7) / n

n = 風險因子數量（預設 7；可擴充）
每個 RF_E：1（低）、3（中）、5（高）
ERPN 範圍：1（最低風險）～ 5（最高風險）

注意：ERPN 採平均值而非加總，避免因增加因子數量而自動拉高分數。 若公司加入額外 RFEx，分母也相應增加。

公司也可為各 RFE 加入加權係數（Weight），以反映企業特定風險容忍度。 例如，注射劑工廠可給 RFE3（給藥途徑）更高的權重。

核心概念：七個風險因子的業務邏輯

  • RFE1 功能性：防腐劑 / 溶劑化劑是高風險，因為若品質不達標，藥效或安全性直接受損
  • RFE2 劑量攝入：只有「是否超過最大允許日劑量」的二元判斷（無中等）
  • RFE3 給藥途徑：注射 = 5，口服 = 1，這是最大的單一差異化因子
  • RFE4 污染潛力：動物源原料自動為高風險（TSE / 病毒）
  • RFE5 複雜性：複合輔料（如某些植物萃取物）若成分未被追蹤，視為高風險
  • RFE6 使用經驗：新輔料或有缺陷歷史的輔料 = 高風險
  • RFE7 包裝規格：需要再分包或精密稱量的輔料，增加操作失誤風險

6.4 Supply Chain Risk Analysis

A supply chain risk score (SCRPN) is created using a risk ranking system similar to that used for evaluating the excipient risk score. The ranking should consider input from the supplier, including knowledge from questionnaires, as well as knowledge gained through previous experience, audits, visits, and media coverage.

At a minimum, the following risk factors related to the supply chain (RFS) should be considered:

  • RFS1: Supply chain complexity — supplier, broker, manufacturer relationships; opportunity for fraud (See Section 4.1.)
  • RFS2: Prior knowledge of the supply chain — capability
  • RFS3: Organizational volatility
  • RFS4: Excipient manufacturer performance history — oversight knowledge such as customer complaints, changes, audits, trustworthiness, tailgate samples
  • RFS5: Packaging suitability — ability to protect excipient from fraud, moisture, heat, and similar elements
  • RFS6: QMS standard and certification

SCRPN 是什麼？

供應鏈風險評分（SCRPN, Supply Chain Risk Priority Number）是針對供應商端的風險量化工具，與前一節的 ERPN（賦形劑風險評分）形成「雙軌並行」的評估架構。

SCRPN 評估的核心問題是：這個供應鏈是否有能力穩定、誠實地交付符合品質要求的賦形劑？

  • 六項風險因子（RFS1–RFS6）
  • 每項評分：1（低）、3（中）、5（高）
  • 最終分數範圍：1～5

比喻：SCRPN 像「供應商健康檢查報告」

想像你在評估一家供應商，你不只看產品品質，你還要看：

  • RFS1（供應鏈複雜度）：貨是直接從工廠來的，還是中間轉了三手？每多一個中間商，就多一個偽造風險。
  • RFS2（過往知識）：你和這家廠商合作過嗎？有沒有抱怨或偏差紀錄？
  • RFS3（組織穩定性）：這家公司的關鍵人員流動率高嗎？高流動意味著品質知識流失。
  • RFS4（QMS 歷史表現）：查核有沒有重大缺失？CAPA 有沒有準時完成？
  • RFS5（包裝適合性）：包裝有防竄改設計嗎？能保護賦形劑免受潮濕或熱害嗎？
  • RFS6（QMS 標準與認證）：有通過第三方認證嗎？還是只有文件卻無實質執行？

SCRPN 計算公式

SCRPN = (RF_S1 + RF_S2 + RF_S3 + RF_S4 + RF_S5 + RF_S6) ÷ 6

範例：
  RF_S1 = 1（直接購買，無中間商）
  RF_S2 = 3（少量合作經驗）
  RF_S3 = 1（組織穩定）
  RF_S4 = 3（QMS 有小弱點）
  RF_S5 = 1（包裝通過驗證）
  RF_S6 = 1（有 EXCiPACT 認證）

SCRPN = (1+3+1+3+1+1) ÷ 6 = 10 ÷ 6 ≈ 1.67（偏低風險）

重點提示：可擴充、可加權

TR54 明確指出：這六項因子是最低要求（minimum），企業可以：

  • 新增針對特定賦形劑的額外風險因子（RFSx）
  • 對重要性較高的因子給予「加權（weighting）」
  • 隨時間和經驗持續優化評分標準

這反映 ICH Q9 的風險管理精神：彈性適用，而非一體適用。

6.5 Risk Evaluation

The excipient risk score and the supply chain risk score are then evaluated together using a risk matrix approach (Figure 6.5-1), based on the excipient risk score (ERPN) on one side and the supply chain risk score (SCRPN) on the other side, to create an overall risk score.

This final evaluation provides an overall view of the risk question: "Is the excipient fit for use?" based on the strength of the manufacturer's and supply chain's GMP (or capability to control risk) and the risk related to the excipient's use in a drug product. The higher the risk related to the excipient's use and function, the greater the need for risk-controlling capability in the supply chain.

Because risk acceptability and the need for risk mitigation can vary significantly from company to company, the matrix and the associated cut offs showing risk acceptability should be tailored to the individual company's risk tolerance based on the drug products it manufactures.

Two options for the matrix are described and illustrated in Section 6.5.1 and Section 6.5.2. To create the matrix using either option, the user first must understand all potential values for ERPN and SCRPN. When calculating ERPN and SCRPN, the result for each will be a number between 1 and 5 where a result of 1 is considered Low, and 5 considered High. In most cases, the ERPN will lie somewhere between those two extremes, and will not necessarily be an integer. Further, the scale is not linear and should not be treated as such.

6.5.2 Risk Matrix — Option 2: Calculating Final Risk Score

Option 2 for the risk matrix allows for additional customization by the manufacturing authorization holder. For instance, the manufacturing authorization holder may weight the factors according to its internal evaluation and controls. In addition, the manufacturing authorization holder may calculate and consider additional scores for other supply chain factors.

The final risk score is calculated by again using ERPN and SCRPN. However, in calculating the final risk score, the manufacturing authorization holder can apply different weights to the two factors, depending on the manufacturing authorization holder's needs. For instance, if the manufacturing authorization holder has reason to believe that the excipient risk score should be given greater consideration, the excipient risk score could be given double the weight of the supply chain risk score in the final calculation.

The final risk score calculation is shown below, where n is the sum of the weights in the numerator. If the excipient risk score is given a weight of 2 and the supply chain risk score is given the weight of 1, then n = 3.

Final Risk Score = [wt(Excipient Risk Score) + wt(Supply Chain Risk Score)] ÷ n

6.6 Implementation of Controls — Risk Mitigation

When the risk is found to be at an acceptable level, the risk assessment process is concluded. The identified controls should be documented, and the agreement with the manufacturer or supplier should ensure effective maintenance of those controls.

If additional actions are needed, Table 6.6-1 describes factors that should be considered. Depending on the specific excipient, other considerations might also need to be taken into account. Specific actions that might lower the residual risk can be found by deconstructing the OverallRPN and investigating the risk scores for both the supply chain and the excipient that are driving the overall risk score.

Table 6.6-1: Factors to Consider in Determining if Additional Actions are Needed

Control Category Examples of Actions
Additional Drug Product Manufacturer Controls
  • Additional receiving inspection or testing
  • Special storage to avoid certain risks (e.g., degradation)
  • Additional processing of the excipient (e.g., filtration, irradiation)
Additional Excipient Controls
  • Change of GMP standard applied
  • Changes to specifications
  • Changes to packaging
  • Change of testing (e.g., every pack or every lot to ensure identity and homogeneity)
  • Microbiological testing
  • Regular verification of supplier's Certificates of Analysis
  • Introduction of new testing
Additional Excipient Supplier Supply Chain Controls
  • Increased auditing by excipient user or third party
  • Changes to the excipient manufacturing process or controls
  • Higher demand for notification of changes
  • Increased oversight and control of shipments, tamper-proofing
Additional Excipient Manufacturing Controls
  • Increased effectiveness of cleaning (microbiological risk reduction)
  • Sanitization (microbiological risk reduction)
  • Enhanced filtration of excipient, e.g., Technically Unavoidable Particle Profile (TUPPS) risk reduction
  • Altered processing conditions (by-product reduction)
  • Exclusion of rework (reduced unknown impurity risks)
  • Use of dedicated equipment
  • Introduction or change of in-process controls

The level of existing controls may exceed actual requirements. In this case, a company may consider reducing efforts in one area to focus on areas of greater risk, such as increasing audit frequency for excipients with high overall risk scores and decreasing audit frequency for excipients with low overall risk scores.

Confirmation that the additional controls have been agreed upon and implemented should be obtained from the excipient manufacturer or supplier and the drug product manufacturer, and should be documented, for example, in supply and quality agreements. Once risk control measures are in place, the risk assessment should be reviewed and rescored to determine the residual risk acceptability.

If the OverallRPN is still unacceptable, further mitigation of the residual risks will be required. This cycle should be repeated until an acceptable residual risk has been obtained.

比喻：「解構 OverallRPN」就像查帳

當 OverallRPN 過高，TR54 說的「解構（deconstructing）」就像查帳一樣：把 ERPN 和 SCRPN 分別拆開，看是哪幾個風險因子在拉高分數。

例如：OverallRPN = 16，是因為 ERPN = 4（賦形劑是注射用的，高功能），SCRPN = 4（供應商有 Warning Letter，且無認證）。這時要優先改善供應鏈（因為那是可以快速影響 SCRPN 的地方），而不是試圖改變賦形劑本身的功能屬性。

「過度管控」也是問題

TR54 罕見地提醒：如果現有管控超出實際需求，可以考慮重新配置資源——把過度投入低風險賦形劑的精力，轉移到高風險賦形劑上。

這是基於風險的資源分配邏輯，也是 ICH Q9 的核心精神：GMP 不是越多越好，而是對的地方、對的力道。

文件化與循環迭代

完成風險緩解措施後，整個流程並未結束：

  1. 將措施記錄在供應與品質協議中（Supply & Quality Agreements）
  2. 重新對 ERPN 和 SCRPN 評分（Re-score）
  3. 計算新的 OverallRPN（殘餘風險）
  4. 若仍不可接受 → 重複循環

這個「PDCA 螺旋式改善」正是 ICH Q10 品質管理系統的實踐。

CDMO 實務情境：高風險賦形劑的處理

情境：某 CDMO 使用聚山梨醇酯 80 製造無菌凍乾注射劑，評估結果：

  • ERPN = 4.2（高功能影響、親水性凍乾製劑）
  • SCRPN = 3.8（供應商無 EXCiPACT 認證、有兩個中間商）
  • OverallRPN = 4.2 × 3.8 ≈ 16 → 不可接受（紅色）

CDMO 採取的行動（依 Table 6.6-1）：

  • 藥品製造商端：增加每批次的微生物限度試驗
  • 賦形劑規格端：要求供應商提供每批的粒徑分佈數據
  • 供應鏈端：要求直接向製造商採購（消除兩個中間商）；供應商需在 18 個月內取得 EXCiPACT 認證

結果：重新評分後 SCRPN 降至 1.8，OverallRPN ≈ 4.2 × 1.8 = 7.6 → 進入「中等」區，可接受並記錄理由。

6.6.1 General Considerations on the Appropriate GMP Required to Meet Risk Mitigation Level Determined by Risk Assessment

Figure 6.6.1-1 illustrates the extent and degree of risk mitigation that could be required based on the type of drug product and its use. This mitigation is defined by the excipient, how it is made, how it is supplied, why it is used, and how it functions in the drug product, as determined in the risk assessment.

The degree of mitigation — that is, the reduction in overall risk that is necessary to reach a low residual risk level — increases as the different definitions of GMP are applied. The list of QMS standards and GMP definitions provided here is illustrative of the principle and is designed to help identify the GMP definition that reduces the risk to the desired level.

The following spectrum illustrates the degree of risk mitigation needed, from lowest to highest:

EFfCI GMP for Cosmetic Ingredients

IPEC-PQG GMP Guide

中文解析：GMP 等級與風險緩解程度

核心概念：GMP 等級 = 風險緩解工具

TR54 把各種 GMP/QMS 標準視為「風險緩解的工具」——不同標準提供不同程度的保護：

  • 風險低的賦形劑 → 只需 ISO 9001 或食品 GMP 等基本水準
  • 風險中等的賦形劑 → EXCiPACT / IPEC-PQG GMP
  • 風險高的賦形劑（例如注射用） → EC GMP Part I，乃至 Annex 1（無菌製劑）

比喻：防護裝備等級

就像工廠的個人防護裝備（PPE）有不同等級：低風險作業穿基本防護，高風險化學作業穿全防護服。對賦形劑的 GMP 要求也一樣——不是所有賦形劑都需要 Annex 1 等級的控制，但無菌注射劑的賦形劑絕對不能只靠 ISO 9001。

這個框架讓 QA 人員在做供應商評估時有了具體的「晉升路徑」：如果供應商現在只有 ISO 9001，但賦形劑風險需要 EXCiPACT 等級，就可以明確要求供應商升級認證。

無菌賦形劑的特殊要求

光譜的最高端是 EC GMP Part I + Annex 1——這是針對無菌物料的標準。

對於 CDMO 而言，這意味著：如果某個賦形劑（例如注射用水、無菌溶媒）直接影響最終製劑的無菌性，供應商的 GMP 等級應當對應到無菌製造標準，而不只是一般藥品製造標準。

這在實務上很重要，因為它為與供應商的技術協商提供了有文件依據的要求依據，而非主觀判斷。

中文解析：GMP 標準適用性

認識主要標準體系

不同市場的賦形劑供應商可能持有不同的認證。TR54 在此提供了一個參考地圖：

  • EXCiPACT：最廣泛被認可的賦形劑專用 GMP 標準。ISO 9001 的附加要求，適用於歐洲市場。
  • NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363：美國版本，與 EXCiPACT 等效，適用於不採用 ISO 9001 的供應商。
  • IPEC-PQG GMP Guide：全球認可的賦形劑 GMP 指引，等效於 EXCiPACT 和 NSF 363。
  • ICH Q7 / EC GMP Part II：API 標準，有時用於高風險賦形劑。
  • ISO 9001：通用品質管理體系，GMP 細節可能不足，單獨使用時保護程度有限。

GMP 標準選擇決策樹

OverallRPN 高（>10）
→ 目標 GMP 等級至少為 EXCiPACT / NSF 363
→ 若賦形劑用於無菌製劑 → EC GMP Part I + Annex 1

OverallRPN 中等（4–10）
→ IPEC-PQG GMP Guide 或 EXCiPACT 可能足夠
→ 需記錄可接受的理由

OverallRPN 低（<4）
→ ISO 9001 基礎，搭配 EFfCI 或食品 GMP
→ 仍需在品質協議中明確規定要求

ISO 9001 單獨使用的局限

TR54 明確指出：ISO 9001 「所需的 GMP 細節可能缺失，因此風險緩解可能缺乏高度保證（lack a high degree of assurance）」。

這是很重要的警示：很多供應商以「我們有 ISO 9001 認證」作為品質保證。但對藥品賦形劑而言，這只是最低基礎，不能等同於 GMP 合規。

實務建議：在供應商選擇和稽核準則中，要明確區分「有 ISO 9001」和「有 EXCiPACT / NSF 363」，兩者的保護程度不同。

CDMO 實務情境：供應商 GMP 升級要求

假設你的 CDMO 為一家製藥客戶生產無菌注射劑，使用的賦形劑為注射用葡萄糖（Glucose for injection）：

  • OverallRPN 評估後為 14（高風險）
  • 目前供應商只有 ISO 9001，無賦形劑專用認證

依 TR54 邏輯，你可以要求供應商：

  1. 18 個月內取得 EXCiPACT 認證（或 NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363 等效）
  2. 在取得認證前，接受每年一次的客戶 GMP 稽核
  3. 在品質協議（Quality Agreement）中明訂上述要求

這樣的要求有 TR54 作為引用依據，在與供應商談判時更具說服力。

7.0 Ongoing Review — Formalized Risk Assessment Lifecycle

Internal procedures should define the periodic review and revision process and list the events that would trigger a reassessment, such as:

  • New product
  • New formulation of existing product
  • New manufacturer/supplier
  • New excipient
  • Change in notification from manufacturer/supplier
  • Change in supply chain
  • Change in GMP certification status or GMP status of manufacturer/supplier
  • Change to site
  • Geopolitical/socioeconomic/business changes
  • Regulatory changes
  • Pharmacopeial changes
  • Quality events, industry-wide (e.g., TSE)
  • Shifts/changes in trends
  • New safety information

When triggering events occur, both the event and the existing excipient risk assessment should be reviewed, and a decision should be made about the need to revise the excipient risk assessment based on the new knowledge. The process, decision, and rationale should all be documented. Updates to the risk assessment may lead to revised control decisions.

中文解說

核心概念：風險評估的生命週期

Section 7.0 強調：輔料風險評估不是一次性的工作，而是一個持續循環的生命週期管理機制。

ICH Q9 的 QRM 框架本就設計為循環模型——從啟動、識別、分析、評估、降低到接受，最終進入「Ongoing Review（持續審查）」階段，當觸發條件出現時，整個流程重新啟動。

Figure 7.0-1 呈現的是這個簡化週期：Initiate → Risk ID → Risk Analysis → Risk Evaluation → Risk Reduction → Risk Acceptance → Ongoing Review（回到 Initiate）。

比喻：汽車保養手冊

把風險評估的生命週期想成汽車保養排程：

  • 首次評估 = 出廠前的出廠檢驗
  • 定期審查 = 每年定保（例行重新確認）
  • 觸發事件 = 警示燈亮了、換了新輪胎品牌、長途旅行前的特別檢查
  • Ongoing Review = 不是等到壞了才修，而是主動監控狀態

重點是：保養不做一次就結束，供應鏈風險評估也一樣。

合規重點：觸發機制是主動與被動並用

觸發事件清單分兩類，是稽核常見考點：

  • 主動型（Proactive）：新產品、新供應商、法規變更、藥典更新——這些是可預見的，SOP 應設有計劃性審查排程。
  • 被動型（Reactive）：品質事件（如業界 TSE 事件）、供應鏈異常通報、安全性新資訊——這些需要即時回應機制。

法規稽核員常問：「你的 SOP 是否明確列出哪些觸發事件需要啟動重新評估？」缺少這份清單，等於風險管理沒有閉環。

思考練習：何時需要啟動重新評估？

請判斷以下情境，哪些應觸發輔料風險評估的重審：

  1. 供應商通知原料提純工藝有細微調整，但規格不變
  2. 歐洲藥典更新了某輔料的微生物限度標準
  3. 同行業發生了與你使用的輔料相關的汙染召回事件
  4. 公司行政部門更換了辦公室電話系統

答案：情境 1、2、3 均應觸發重審（分別對應：供應商通知變更、藥典變更、業界品質事件）。情境 4 與供應鏈無關，不適用。

8.0 Benefits and Value / QRM / Knowledge Management

A global excipient risk assessment throughout the drug product lifecycle delivers the following advantages:

  • Documented knowledge, in a structured manner, about the excipient supply chain
  • Ability to rapidly and effectively evaluate the requirements for new products that use existing or new excipients
  • Identification of supply chains that present lower risks and preferred supply chains with which to build relationships
  • Focus on specific risk mitigation oversight activities for both manufacturer or supplier and user
  • More detailed product knowledge and increased understanding of how the risks from excipients may affect product quality
  • Formal assessment and database of risk review triggers, such as major changes and critical investigations
  • Knowledge-based and proactive decision-making
  • Provides a common language and approach for clear communication with stakeholders throughout the supply chain
  • Opportunities for knowledge-sharing and comparative assessments between manufacturers or suppliers and end users

中文解說

核心概念：形式化風險評估的業務價值

Section 8.0 回答了一個關鍵問題：「為什麼要花這麼多工夫做正式的風險評估？」

答案是多層次的——不只是合規要求，而是切實的業務效益：

  • 知識管理：把對輔料供應鏈的判斷從個人腦袋轉移到結構化文件，組織知識得以留存。
  • 決策加速：新產品導入時，已有的評估資料庫讓團隊能快速判斷，而非從零開始。
  • 供應鏈分級：識別「優選供應商」，建立長期合作關係，降低短缺風險。
  • 共通語言：跨部門、跨公司溝通時有共同框架，減少誤解。

比喻：企業 CRM 系統

形式化風險評估對輔料供應鏈，就像 CRM（客戶關係管理）對業務團隊：

  • 沒有 CRM：客戶知識在每個業務員腦袋裡，人一離職就消失。
  • 有 CRM：客戶歷史、偏好、風險全部結構化記錄，新成員上手快。

同理，形式化輔料風險評估讓供應鏈知識成為組織資產，而非個人記憶。

CDMO 情境：供應商變更觸發重新評估

場景：你的公司為某客戶生產凍乾注射劑，所用的關鍵輔料（冷凍保護劑）原本由供應商 A 提供，現在客戶通知要改用供應商 B。

應觸發的動作：

  1. 依照 Section 7.1 觸發清單（「New manufacturer/supplier」），啟動輔料風險評估重審
  2. 重新評估供應商 B 的 GMP 狀態、供應鏈複雜度、TSE/BSE 風險
  3. 更新風險評估文件，必要時修訂控制決策（如：需要額外的 CoA 審查或供應商稽核）
  4. 記錄決策理由（Section 7.0 要求：process, decision, and rationale shall be documented）

這正是 Section 8.0 所述「Formal assessment and database of risk review triggers」帶來的價值：下次遇到類似情境，查閱資料庫即可，不必重頭摸索。

10.0 Annex I: Real World Examples of Risk Assessments

The generic model proposed in Section 6.0 was developed based on real-world risk assessment processes donated by several marketing authorization holders. Many risk assessment approaches used some form of risk ranking and filtering approach—conducting assessments in two separate streams that can then be combined in an overall risk score.

One stream would provide a risk profile for the excipient itself, based on its use in the finished product and any intrinsic risk factors. The second stream would generate a risk profile for the supply chain, including the excipient manufacturer, suppliers, brokers, and any other players, as well as the strength of their quality management systems (QMS). The two streams would then be combined into one risk evaluation to determine whether the overall risk is at an acceptable level or additional controls are needed.

"The generic model proposed in Section 6.0 was developed based on real-world risk assessment processes donated by several marketing authorization holders."

解說與應用 Commentary

核心概念：兩流評估架構

PDA TR54-6 的附錄一揭示了一個貫穿所有案例的共同模式：雙流評估（Two-Stream Assessment）。

  • 第一流 — 賦形劑風險：基於製劑功能、給藥途徑、原料來源（動物性/植物性/礦物性/合成）評估內在風險
  • 第二流 — 供應鏈風險：評估製造商、供應商、中間商的品質管理系統（QMS）強度
  • 合流決策：兩個分數合併，決定整體風險等級與所需控制措施

這個框架是後續六個案例理解的基礎。

比喻：飛機安全審查

想像飛機的安全審查：第一流就像評估飛機本身的設計風險（機型、用途、載重），第二流是評估航空公司的維護體系（公司規模、認證、維修記錄）。兩者合起來才能決定這趟航班的整體風險等級。

賦形劑風險評估也是如此：即使是同一種賦形劑，來自不同供應鏈的風險可能截然不同。

重點：本節的學習價值

這六個案例（U~Z）並非學術範例，而是真實的全球製藥公司在符合 EC Guidelines（2015 年歐盟指引）要求下所建立的系統。每個案例都代表一種不同的實施路徑，沒有絕對的「最佳做法」，只有最符合公司規模與複雜度的選擇。

10.1

Example 1: Company U

案例一：公司 U — 加權 RPN 矩陣分析法

10.1 Example 1: Company U

Company U is a global manufacturer of various pharmaceutical dosage forms. The company established a Microsoft® Excel® tool to be used as a corporate standard.

When multiple manufacturing sites use the same excipient provided by the same excipient manufacturing site, the risk assessments can be shared across all sites (with appropriate site-specific modifications to accommodate excipient usage, supply chain complexity, and other factors).

First, basic information about the excipient and the manufacturer is collected. Then, the collected information is consolidated to produce an overview.

For each section of the model, the parameters to evaluate are defined (see Figure 10.1-1), each parameter is described in detail, and possible scenarios are developed. The severity of each parameter is assigned a standard value. In conducting the evaluation, Company U determines the actual probability of each parameter.

The analysis is conducted as follows:

  • Excipient and excipient usage risk score — Each of the identified excipient and excipient use risk factors is evaluated based on the actual origin or usage of the excipient and the actual supply chain to determine the probability of risk (P). The predefined weight, or severity (S), is used to calculate the risk score for each parameter (P × S). Then, the overall excipient risk score is calculated as the average across the parameters.
  • Combined material risk score — A combined material risk score is calculated by adding the excipient risk score and the excipient usage risk score.
  • Manufacturer risk score — The list of risk factors to be considered is evaluated based on the actual performance and QMS of the manufacturer. The predefined weight (S) is used to calculate the risk score of each parameter (P × S). Then, the overall manufacturer risk score is calculated as the average across the parameters.
  • Gap analysis and actions — The combined material risk score and the manufacturer risk score are evaluated together using a risk matrix to identify the "gap level," as illustrated in Table 10.1-1. Where a gap exists between the manufacturer risk score and the combined material risk score, the gap must be evaluated to determine the appropriate remediation.

解說與應用 Commentary

此案例展示的核心方法：加權 RPN 矩陣

公司 U 採用機率 (P) × 嚴重性 (S) = 風險分數的加權計算方式，這是來自 ICH Q9 的 RPN（Risk Priority Number）概念。

  • P（Probability）：實際評估時，依賦形劑的真實來源與使用情境判定
  • S（Severity）：預先定義的標準加權值，確保跨批次評估的一致性
  • Gap Analysis Matrix：2×2 風險矩陣，將賦形劑組合風險與製造商風險交叉比對，得出需補救的落差等級

特別之處：多個製造廠可以共享同一份評估，再加入廠點特定修改，節省大量重複工作。

比喻：供應商評鑑+原料風險的交叉比對

想像公司採購部門評估一個零件供應商：

  • 第一欄：這個零件本身有多重要（高功能性 = 高賦形劑風險）
  • 第二欄：這家供應商的品質有多穩定（工廠認證差 = 高製造商風險）

重要零件 + 不穩定供應商 = 一定要採取行動。公司 U 的矩陣正是把這個直觀判斷系統化。

關鍵觀察：Excel 工具的優缺點

公司 U 使用 Excel 作為企業標準工具。這在中型公司很常見，優點是靈活易用、成本低，缺點是版本管控困難、難以自動觸發複審。後面的案例（公司 V、Z）都在討論如何用電子系統解決這個問題。

思考練習

在你的 CDMO 環境中，如果有五個製造廠都使用同一種乳糖（lactose）作為賦形劑，且都來自同一供應商——你會為每個廠分別做評估，還是採用公司 U 的「共享評估＋廠點修改」模式？哪個方式的 GMP 文件管控風險更低？

CDMO 應用建議

對於同一賦形劑跨多廠使用的情形，建議建立母版評估（Master Assessment）加廠點附件（Site Annex）的架構。母版由中央品質部門維護，廠點附件負責記錄廠點特定的使用量、給藥途徑差異與供應鏈路徑。

案例二：公司 V — 全球 SOP + 加權 RPN 三分法

10.2 Example 2: Company V

Company V is a global manufacturer of pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical solid oral dosage forms and lyophilized injectables. To a large extent, its products are manufactured through contract manufacturing organizations (CMOs).

The risk model (Figure 10.2-1) was created to meet the European Commission Guidelines on The Formalised Risk Assessment for Ascertaining the Appropriate GMP For Excipients of Medicinal Products For Human Use (EC Guidelines), and the risk factors to be considered reflect those in the guidelines (1). The model was implemented in a global SOP that applies for all sites manufacturing commercial products and, going forward, for new products, including biologics and other type of products.

This model was extended for products during their transfer into commercial production. The quality risk assessment tool is applied based on risk ranking and filtering, evaluating the risk factors in two groups:

  • Multiple criteria analysis
    • Function and use of excipient (→ Score Excipient)
    • Manufacturer (→ Score Manufacturer)
    • Suppliers (→ Score Supplier)
  • Risk score for each excipient (low, medium, or high)
    • Independent risk factors
    • Risk ranking and weighing
    • Overall weighing

The three single scores—Function and Use, Manufacturer, and Suppliers—were combined by multiplication into a weighted RPN score. Acceptability was evaluated based on the RPN value, and mitigation actions were considered according to the overall risk score (low, medium, or high).

Outcomes and Lessons Learned:

  • No major issues were found for existing products, though some minor changes in audit strategy and other controls were instituted as a result.

解說與應用 Commentary

此案例展示的核心方法：三維乘法 RPN

公司 V 比公司 U 多增加了一個維度——獨立評分供應商（Supplier），形成三分法：

  • Score Excipient（賦形劑功能與使用分數）
  • Score Manufacturer（製造商分數）
  • Score Supplier（供應商/中間商分數）

三者相乘（乘法 RPN），而非相加，能放大任何一個高風險維度的影響力——若任何一項分數極高，整體 RPN 會顯著上升，避免被其他低分項目平均掉。

此外，全球 SOP 標準化確保了跨 CMO 廠的一致執行。

比喻：供應鏈三角審查

如同評估一家餐廳的食品安全：

  • 食材本身的風險（Score Excipient）：生食 vs. 熟食
  • 農場/製造商的品質（Score Manufacturer）：認證有機農場 vs. 非認證
  • 物流/配銷商（Score Supplier）：冷鏈是否完整

三個環節任何一個出問題，食品安全風險都會倍增。公司 V 的乘法模型正是體現了這個道理。

重點觀察：Living Risk Assessment 的挑戰

公司 V 坦承「尚未如期望中那樣做到 Living Risk Assessment」——這是許多公司共同面對的痛點。初次建立評估不難，難在建立觸發複審的機制（製造商更換、品質事件、新供應商加入時自動提示），以及讓文件系統足夠靈活、不需要走完整的 GMP 審批流程才能更新。

思考練習

公司 V 採用「乘法 RPN」（三分相乘），公司 U 採用「加法再矩陣」。哪個方法對於 CDMO 的賦形劑評估更適合？想想你公司裡哪些賦形劑可能有「製造商分數很低但賦形劑本身風險很高」的組合——在加法模型和乘法模型中，這個組合會得出不同的優先順序嗎？

CDMO 應用建議

對於大量委外的 CDMO 環境，建議在全球 SOP 中明確指定單一聯絡窗口（One Point of Contact）負責每項賦形劑的資訊彙整——公司 V 正在討論這個改善方向。同時，評估系統應設計自動觸發條件（change control、supplier audit 結果），避免評估停留在靜態文件。

10.3 Example 3: Company W

Company W is a global manufacturer of pharmaceuticals and devices, primarily parenteral products.

As part of the material assessment, the company examines the correlation of impact between parameters (attributes) describing the excipient and the CQAs of the product in which it is an ingredient. An excipient assessment provides input to material specifications, as illustrated in Figure 10.3-1. In this way, the company evaluates the impact of the excipient on the finished drug products.

Attributes are derived by ideation from a defined list of potential risk sources. Attributes with impact are included on the specification, and limits or other controls are defined after analyzing the possible impacts of variation in the attribute. This method uses a qualitative assessment with a simple +/– score for impact combined with text rationales and references describing the underlying science. The impact is evaluated considering the amount of material to be used and its function in the formulation.

The result of the assessment is used to define the excipient's specification based on the specific use in a specific drug product. An assessment is made for each use in a drug product.

Supply Chain Assessment (in parallel):

解說與應用 Commentary

此案例展示的核心方法：CQA 連結的科學評估

公司 W 是六個案例中最以科學品質屬性（CQA）為核心的方法。其邏輯是：

  • 從潛在風險來源清單，藉由腦力激盪（Ideation）衍生出賦形劑的可評估屬性（Attributes）
  • 每個屬性以+/− 定性評分判斷是否影響成品 CQA
  • 有影響的屬性自動納入賦形劑規格（Specification），並設定允許限度
  • 每個藥品用途都做獨立評估（product-specific + use-specific）

這個方法直接與 ICH Q8/Q9 的品質源於設計（QbD）思維對接。

比喻：食品成分對健康指標的影響分析

想像評估一個食品配方的健康風險：

  • 鈉含量 → 影響血壓（CQA）？+（有影響）
  • 添加劑顏色 → 影響血壓？−（無影響）
  • 鈉含量 → 影響腎臟負荷？+（有影響）

有影響的項目（+）才需要設規格限制。公司 W 的 +/− 評估法讓科學理由透明化，比純數字評分更容易被監管機構理解。

重點觀察：最接近法規期望的方法

FDA 和 EMA 在審查賦形劑風險評估時，最希望看到的是「科學理由（scientific rationale）」而非單純的數字評分。公司 W 的定性 +/− 加文字依據（text rationale and references）的方法，在法規審查中往往最能說服審查員。缺點是工作量大——每個藥品用途都需獨立評估，難以規模化。

思考練習

你公司目前的賦形劑規格是如何制定的？有沒有明確的文件記錄「為什麼這個規格限度設在這裡」——也就是公司 W 所說的 text rationale？如果沒有，這在法規審查（如 EMA MAA）中可能是哪類缺失（observation）？

CDMO 應用建議

對於注射劑（parenteral）賦形劑，建議採用類似公司 W 的CQA 連結評估。實作上，可在賦形劑規格設定文件中增加「CQA Impact Table」，列出每個賦形劑屬性、對應的成品 CQA、影響判斷（+/−）及科學依據來源（ICH、USP、文獻）。這個表格可直接作為 dossier 的支持文件。

Example 4: Company X

案例四：公司 X — 產品品質經理主導的跨部門協作模式

10.4 Example 4: Company X

Company X expects its suppliers to comply with the IPEC-PQG Joint Good Manufacturing Practices Guide (2), EXCiPACT™ Standard (3), or NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363 (4). The excipient risk management process considered input from the manufacturer qualification process, audits, relationships, testing, and outside intelligence. The company also worked with its CMOs to align expectations between them and the manufacturing authorization holder and rationalized scores. The actual process is owned by the company's organizational product quality management unit and follows the model illustrated in Figure 10.4-1.

The product quality manager (PQM) is responsible for pulling together the information and subject matter experts from across Company X to provide the data needed to complete the spreadsheet. Much of the information comes from the company's audit team. The model allows for rationalization across materials and suppliers to ensure that scoring is consistent and can be readily justified.

Company X maintains an SOP that describes the overall process, including triggers for revisiting a risk assessment. Because the PQMs also are involved in the change control process for company materials, they have firsthand knowledge of any changes that would require a review of the excipient risk assessment. As part of this process, Company X has already made several revisions to some excipient risk assessments.

Initially, there was some resistance to the concept and process. Now that the system is in place, however, staff have noticed that it is easier to find information without going into several systems or speaking to several departments.

解說與應用 Commentary

此案例展示的核心方法：PQM 主導的跨部門整合

公司 X 的特色不在於評估工具本身，而在於組織架構設計：

  • 由產品品質經理（PQM）擔任資訊整合者，召集各領域 SME（稽核、測試、法規、CMO 關係管理）
  • PQM 同時參與變更管控（Change Control），因此能第一時間識別哪些變更需要重新評估
  • 評估結果允許跨物料、跨供應商的合理化比對（Rationalization），確保評分一致性
  • 明確的 SOP 包含觸發複審的條件（Triggers for Revisiting）

公司 X 的方法解決了「誰負責更新」這個最常見的組織痛點。

比喻：案件負責人制度（Case Manager）

公司 X 的 PQM 就像法律事務所的案件負責人——不是所有工作都自己做，而是負責匯集所有相關人員的資訊、做出最終判斷，並追蹤後續行動。這種「一個窗口」的設計，比各部門各自維護分散資訊的方式更有效率，也更不容易遺漏。

重點觀察：變革管理的成功故事

公司 X 坦承「初期遭遇抵抗（resistance）」，但系統建立後員工開始體會到益處——資訊更易查找。這是推行任何新 GMP 系統時典型的採用曲線（adoption curve）。關鍵成功因素：PQM 的角色設計讓評估系統與既有的 change control 流程有機結合，而非額外負擔。

另一個值得注意的點：公司 X 與其 CMO 協作，對齊期望並合理化評分——這對 CDMO 環境尤其重要。

思考練習

在你的組織中，誰應該扮演 PQM 的角色——品質部門？採購部門？法規部門？當供應商提出重大變更（賦形劑製造廠搬遷）時，現有的 change control 流程有沒有自動觸發賦形劑風險評估複審的機制？如果沒有，這個缺口如何填補？

CDMO 應用建議

建議在 CDMO 的賦形劑管理 SOP 中，明確定義觸發複審的條件清單，例如：供應商更換、製造廠遷址、稽核發現重大缺失、法規要求變更、品質事件（QE/CAPA）、產品配方修改等。並將這些觸發條件與 change control 或 supplier change notification 流程直接連結，確保評估是動態的（Living Document）。

Example 5: Company Y

案例五：公司 Y — 六準則加權工具與稽核頻率決策

10.5 Example 5: Company Y

The model illustrated in Figure 10.5-1 applies to finished products for human use that Company Y manufactures for European markets.

Company Y manufacturing sites completed risk ranking of each excipient from each manufacturer used at their sites.

A risk ranking tool is used to evaluate each excipient from each manufacturer. A weight of 1 (low), 3 (medium), or 10 (high) is assigned for each of six criteria and, from that, an overall risk ranking of the excipient (low, medium, or high) is calculated. Where the GMPs used to manufacture excipients are found to be inappropriate according to risk ranking, mitigation measures are implemented. A risk analysis tool for manufacturer audit frequency is then used to assess the risk and determine audit frequency.

Based on the outcome, a second risk assessment could be performed to determine if (1) additional mitigation measures should be implemented, or (2) use of the excipient from that specific manufacturer should be discontinued. If Company Y decides to continue to use the excipient after implementing additional mitigation measures to reduce the remaining risk to an acceptable level, the company documents this decision in a "statement to maintain business."

Company Y periodically reevaluates risk profiles during the review of a manufacturer's performance. A risk assessment is reevaluated in cases of a significant quality event, e.g., significant change by manufacturer, serious GMP deficiencies, or loss of GMP certification.

解說與應用 Commentary

此案例展示的核心方法：六準則加權 + 稽核頻率決策樹

公司 Y 的方法有兩個顯著特點：

1. 六準則加權工具（1/3/10 Scoring）：

  • 每個準則賦予 1（低）、3（中）、10（高）三個加權值
  • 六個準則的加總或加權平均決定整體風險等級
  • 此 1-3-10 標度比 1-5-10 或 1-2-3 更能放大高風險項目的影響

2. 稽核頻率決策工具（Audit Frequency Risk Tool）：

  • 風險等級直接連結稽核頻率，使資源分配有依據
  • 若第一次評估後仍有疑慮，進行第二次評估決定「繼續使用」或「停止使用」
  • 繼續使用高風險賦形劑時，須出具「維持業務聲明（Statement to Maintain Business）」

比喻：信用評分系統

公司 Y 的 1-3-10 評分類似銀行信用評分：

  • 大多數因素是低風險（1分），只有少數關鍵因素是高風險（10分）
  • 一個 10 分的負面因素（例如：失去 GMP 認證）會大幅拉高整體分數，讓風險等級跳升為「高」

「Statement to Maintain Business」則像銀行在向信用有疑慮的客戶放貸時附上的風險備忘錄——決策有理有據，且有文件記錄。

重點觀察：跨廠評分差異的處理

公司 Y 坦承不同廠點有時對同一賦形劑的評估結果不同。這反映了真實環境中的挑戰：不同廠點的採購路線、測試記錄、稽核經驗不同，導致主觀判斷有差異。公司 Y 的解決方案是：即使分數不同，在執行管控措施後，各廠最終達到一致的使用決策。這是務實的處理方式，也說明了評估結果「一致性」的要求應落在決策層面，而非數字層面。

思考練習

公司 Y 的模型「簡單易實施」，但允許廠點間評分有差異。在你的 CDMO 環境中，你更偏向「評分一致性（統一工具、統一標準）」還是「決策一致性（各廠可自行評分，但最終決策要一致）」？兩者在 GMP 合規性上有什麼不同的含義？

CDMO 應用建議

公司 Y 的稽核頻率決策工具是非常實用的資源分配工具，強烈建議 CDMO 採用。具體實施建議：

  • 高風險賦形劑製造商：每年稽核或書面問卷
  • 中風險：每兩年稽核
  • 低風險：每三至五年稽核或認證審查

並在 SOP 中規定：失去 GMP 認證、重大品質事件發生時，不論排程如何，立即啟動臨時稽核。

Example 6: Company Z

案例六：公司 Z — 中央化兩階段端到端評估體系

10.6 Example 6: Company Z

Company Z's model (Figure 10.6-1) was designed as a centralized process focused on highlighting the inherent risks and control strategies associated with an excipient, regardless of the manufacturer. The process is also designed to provide the user with the minimum quality standard expected for the manufacture of the excipient and its associated key risks. These risk and control strategies provide input for the gap analysis and, subsequently, to the quality risk assessment performed on supplier sites.

Company Z established a corporate procedure that outlines the requirements for the excipient risk assessment process and its linkages to the overarching supplier quality management process.

Figure 10.6-1
Risk Assessment Model for Company Z
(End-to-end two-phase model: Phase 1 — Central team determines inherent risk + control strategy; Phase 2 — Global supplier QM performs gap analysis per supplier site)
Figure 10.6-1: Risk Assessment Model for Company Z (p35–36)

The first part of the "end-to-end process excipient risk assessment" determines the control strategy and standards that are established by the centralized excipient risk assessment team. In the second part, the quality management group of the global supplier uses the output of the risk assessment to conduct individual supplier site quality risk assessments. The information from the individual supplier site quality risk assessments is shared with the formulation sites through a vendor assurance database system, highlighting any changes in supplier risk ranking via change control. The excipient risk assessments are reviewed periodically and any changes in inherent risk are brought to the attention of the global supplier's quality management team. In addition, any quality event that impacts the excipient quality, whether internal or external, results in a review of the initial excipient risk assessment.

After the need for an excipient risk assessment is identified, the centralized, cross-functional excipient risk assessment team performs the assessment, which focuses entirely on inherent risk. Supplier risks are not considered at this time. An electronic tool is used to perform the assessment, and the output is used to determine the appropriate quality level for the excipient and any relevant excipient control strategy.

Each supplier quality manager from Company Z's global supplier quality team then performs a gap analysis of the excipient supplier quality standard against an assessment of defined standards and excipient control strategies for supplied excipients. This identifies any gaps for the supplier. All gaps are challenged to ensure their validity and valid gaps are used as input, along with other supplier quality management intelligence, in performing the supplier site quality risk assessment. This final assessment generates a supplier risk ranking (low, medium, or high) which, in turn, determines the quality oversight to be applied.

The maintenance of the excipient risk assessment is managed under the periodic review process outlined in the corporate standard on excipient risk management. Quality events that impact excipient quality trigger an ad hoc excipient risk assessment review.

Company Z implemented this process in March 2016 in line with the EC Guidelines.

Key Benefits:

  • A key benefit of conducting the assessment is consistency in managing excipients and suppliers. The centralized approach provided a concise framework for consistent input into the initial steps of the process. The implementation formalized the process and improved the documentation of information, supporting existing supplier risk assessment processes.
  • Grouping excipient grades into categories aligned with the route of administration, origin, and function has reduced the number of assessments required.
  • Conducting a formalized assessment process provides clarity to sourcing partners, both internal and external.
  • The risk assessment acts as an effective standard for comparing and assessing supply chain risks.

解說與應用 Commentary

此案例展示的核心方法：兩階段端到端中央化體系

公司 Z 是六個案例中系統化程度最高的，其設計邏輯是：

第一階段（中央化）：固有風險評估

  • 中央跨職能團隊評估賦形劑固有風險（Inherent Risk）——不考慮任何特定供應商
  • 輸出：賦形劑品質標準（Quality Standard）+ 控制策略（Control Strategy）
  • 使用電子工具執行，確保一致性

第二階段（供應商層級）：差距分析

  • 各地供應商品質經理將固有風險控制策略對照各供應商實際情況做差距分析（Gap Analysis）
  • 有效差距作為供應商現場品質風險評估的輸入
  • 最終輸出：供應商風險排名（Low/Medium/High）→ 決定品質監督強度

資訊透過供應商保證資料庫系統（Vendor Assurance Database）傳遞給配方廠，change control 觸發排名更新。

比喻：中央廚房與分店品質管理

公司 Z 的模型像連鎖餐廳集團：

  • 中央廚房（總部）：定義食材標準與最低品質要求（固有風險評估）
  • 各分店（供應商品質經理）：對照中央標準評估各自的在地供應商是否符合（差距分析）
  • 總部資料庫：彙整各分店的供應商評估結果，總部即時掌握全局供應鏈風險

這種架構讓「標準一致」和「在地執行」兩者兼顧——中央制定遊戲規則，各地執行並回報。

重點觀察：分組策略降低評估數量

公司 Z 提到「依給藥途徑、來源與功能將賦形劑分組（Grouping into Categories）」，顯著減少了所需評估的數量。這是非常務實的做法——例如，所有「注射劑用緩衝鹽」可以共用一份固有風險評估框架，差別只在各自的供應商差距分析。這個分組策略對擁有大量賦形劑品項的 CDMO 尤其有價值。

思考練習

公司 Z 的中央化模型適合大型跨國集團，但對於中型 CDMO，「中央跨職能團隊」的人力是否支撐得起？如果你要設計一個縮小版的公司 Z 模型，哪些元素是不可省略的（中央化固有風險評估？電子工具？差距分析程序），哪些可以簡化？

CDMO 應用建議

公司 Z 的賦形劑分組策略是 CDMO 立即可用的成本效益工具。建議步驟：

  • 第一步：清點所有在用賦形劑，依給藥途徑（注射/口服/外用）+ 化學來源（動物性/植物性/合成）建立分組矩陣
  • 第二步：每個分組做一份通用固有風險評估框架（Generic Inherent Risk Assessment）
  • 第三步：各賦形劑在框架上只需填入供應商特定差距分析，大幅降低總評估工作量

此外，建立Vendor Assurance Database（可以是簡單的 SharePoint 清單或 ERP 模組）作為賦形劑風險排名的單一資料來源，確保生產廠點能即時查到最新的供應商風險狀態。
