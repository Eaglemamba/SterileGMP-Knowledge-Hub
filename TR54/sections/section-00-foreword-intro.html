Foreword — 前言

Joint Initiative between IPEC Federation and PDA

In March 2018, the IPEC Federation and PDA announced their first joint initiative. Both associations signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on the development of a joint technical report on excipient good manufacturing practices risk assessment in response to input from their respective memberships.

Prior to the collaboration announcement, however, both parties were active on the topic of quality risk management. PDA published the PDA Technical Report No. 54 series. In March 2016, IPEC Europe published the How-To Document – A Guide to Support Manufacturing Authorization Holders (MAH) in their Compliance with the European Commission Guidelines on Risk Assessment for Excipients (2015/C 95/02). Subsequently, in May 2017, IPEC-Americas and IPEC Europe jointly published the Risk Assessment Guide for Excipient Makers, Users, and Distributors.

Both PDA and IPEC Federation believe that presenting a common approach to the legal, regulatory, and related issues concerning excipients is best done as "one voice." Both collaborators see significant potential benefit in leveraging the two organizations' expertise on excipients and drug product manufacture. This technical report will serve as a practical guidance intended for use with existing regulatory and industry standards. The authors expect that the document will enable Manufacturing Authorization Holders of drug product to either set up or benchmark their quality systems, and further establish or continue to collaborate with parties in their excipient supply chain.

This joint PDA-IPEC technical report extends the PDA Technical Report No. 54 series and provides guidance on risk assessments for excipients by presenting a model risk assessment, guidance on key elements, and a collection of actual examples from excipient users in the pharmaceutical industry.

1.0 Introduction — 導論

1.0 Introduction

Excipients are integrated into the drug product formulation for various reasons: to aid the drug product formulation, to serve as functional or nonfunctional ingredients, or to enable the drug to be released from the drug product in a particular, desired manner. The rationale for their use extends beyond the point of medical use to include maintenance of the safety or function of the drug product, even during storage. While good manufacturing practices (GMP) regulations clearly outline what is required for active pharmaceutical ingredients, only a basic framework exists for ascertaining appropriate GMP for excipients through risk assessment.

In recognition of the criticality of excipients in the production of drug products, the European Commission (EC) Falsified Medicines Directive (2011/62/EU) revised Article 46(f) of Directive 2001/83 to require manufacturing authorization holders to verify that any excipients used in a product are made according to appropriate GMP standards. The Falsified Medicines Directive also committed the EC to publishing Guidelines on the formalised risk assessment for ascertaining the appropriate GMP for excipients of medicinal products for human use (EC Guidelines), which were published on March 19, 2015. In 2018, PIC/S incorporated the same provisions for formalized risk assessment into a publication of the same name, extending the provisions to have global applicability.

In the U.S., the 2012 Food and Drug Administration Safety and Innovation Act (FDASIA) redefined "current good manufacturing practices" to include "the implementation of oversight and controls over the manufacture of drugs to ensure quality, including managing the risk of and establishing the safety of raw materials [and] materials used in the manufacturing of drugs," in addition to the final drug product. This change also emphasizes the criticality of excipients.

賦形劑的三大功能角色

  • 功能性賦形劑 (Functional)：直接影響藥品性能，如緩釋劑型中的控釋聚合物、保護蛋白質穩定性的凍乾保護劑

1.0 Introduction (continued) — MAH Accountability & Lifecycle

Although the accountability for risk assessment rests entirely with the manufacturing authorization holder, all parties in the supply chain are required to support the process by providing relevant information (e.g., Excipient Information Packages (EIP)) to the manufacturing authorization holder. The risk management process is applicable throughout the excipient's lifecycle, that is, from selection through termination of use, and remains the responsibility of the manufacturing authorization holder at each stage.

Over the course of an excipient's lifecycle, there will be a broad array of drivers for risk assessment and reassessment. Those drivers may include, among others, product deviations, market recalls, changes in excipient use, supply chain amendments, and adverse trends in quality. The manufacturing authorization holder is responsible for providing evidence that the risk management approach adopted is commensurate with the level of risk to ensure the safety, purity and other quality characteristics of the excipient in use.

To comply with the EC Guidelines and PIC/S publication, each excipient used in a drug product must be assessed for the risks that it poses to the product's quality, safety, and purity. From these risks, the manufacturing authorization holder must determine the quality standards required for the manufacture of the excipient. The manufacturing authorization holder should communicate these quality standards to the excipient supplier and seek the supplier's agreement. Any confirmed gaps in quality standards determined by the assessment must be appropriately mitigated, which may require the manufacturing authorization holder to take action on its own or through collaboration with the supplier. To ensure that risk assessments remain effective during the excipient's lifecycle, the manufacturing authorization holder must incorporate a periodic review into the excipient risk assessment process.

核心原則：責任不可外包

風險評估的全部責任在 MAH 身上 — 即使賦形劑供應商、經銷商都有提供資訊，最終判斷和決策責任仍由 MAH 承擔。

供應鏈各方的義務：

  • 賦形劑製造商：提供 EIP（賦形劑資訊包）
  • 賦形劑供應商/經銷商：傳遞相關品質資訊
  • MAH：整合所有資訊，進行風險評估，做出決策

賦形劑生命週期的五個階段

選擇 (Selection)
↓
供應商評估與核准 (Qualification)
↓
商業供應監控 (Commercial Monitoring)
↓
定期審查 (Periodic Review)
↓
終止使用 (Termination)

每個階段 MAH 均負有風險管理責任。

風險重評估的觸發因素 (Drivers)

  • 產品偏差 (Product Deviations)
  • 市場召回 (Market Recalls)
  • 賦形劑用途變更（如新劑型、新給藥途徑）
  • 供應鏈變更（供應商轉廠、原料來源改變）
  • 品質不良趨勢 (Adverse Quality Trends)

關鍵字：commensurate（相稱的） — 風險管理方法的力度必須與風險等級相符，高風險賦形劑需要更嚴格的控制。

比喻：EIP 就像供應商的「健康檢查報告」

Excipient Information Package (EIP) 就像你去看醫生前填寫的健康問卷 — 供應商主動提供生產流程、品質系統、測試方法等資訊，讓 MAH（醫生）能夠做出風險判斷。

但最終診斷（風險評估結論）還是醫生的責任，不是問卷本身。

思考練習

你的CDMO使用一個已核准的賦形劑供應商 5 年了。突然該供應商的賦形劑出現外觀異常偏差（Batch deviation）。根據本節，這個事件屬於哪種風險重評估觸發因素？MAH 應採取什麼行動？

提示：觸發因素 = Product Deviation；行動包括：審查現有風險評估 → 判斷是否需要更新 → 與供應商溝通 → 若有缺口則要求改善或找替代供應商。

CDMO 實務應用

在無菌填充環境，賦形劑的週期性審查（Periodic Review）可與年度產品回顧 (APR/PQR) 整合。每年回顧時，同步確認賦形劑供應商的品質狀況是否有異常趨勢，若有則啟動風險重評估流程。

1.1 Purpose — 目的

1.1 Purpose

This report is based on the general principles of quality risk management (QRM) as outlined in ICH Quality Guideline Q9 Quality Risk Management. It has been developed to provide additional guidance on the excipient risk assessment process required by the EC Guidelines and incorporated into the PIC/S publication. In addition, TR 54-6 presents a model risk assessment and real-world examples from the pharmaceutical industry applicable to excipient use for all dosage forms to illustrate risk assessment as an excipient knowledge management solution.

In general, the model presented in this report is based on the risk ranking and filtering method as referenced in ICH Q9. It also addresses pertinent factors such as end-to-end supply chain, control strategy, and benefits and challenges, among others. TR 54-6 will also serve as a practical guidance intended for use in conjunction with existing regulatory and industry standards; however, it does not replace or alter existing statutory and regulatory requirements for assessing excipients for use in medicinal drug products.

Despite the clarity and guidance offered by the EC Guidelines, risk assessors for manufacturing authorization holders still face some challenges in implementing and benchmarking the excipient risk assessment process for two main reasons. First, quality systems applied during the manufacture of materials used as excipients are diverse and generally based on the material's intended use, which may not be primarily as a pharmaceutical excipient. Therefore, adjusting an excipient manufacturer's or supplier's quality systems to meet the pharmaceutical quality system outlined in the guidelines could prove challenging.

Second, an excipient may be used in different dosage forms by manufacturing authorization holders. One manufacturing authorization holder may use a single excipient in both tablets and injectables, which may result in different quality requirements applying to a single excipient from the same supplier. This also may lead to conflicting requirements for GMP.

中文解析：TR 54-6 的目的與兩大挑戰

核心概念：ICH Q9 風險排序與篩選法

TR 54-6 的風險評估模型基於 ICH Q9 的兩大工具：

  • Risk Ranking and Filtering (風險排序與篩選)：根據多個標準（給藥途徑、功能性、供應鏈複雜度等）對賦形劑進行評分，得出風險等級 (Low/Medium/High)
  • ICH Q9 一般原則：風險識別 → 風險分析 → 風險評估 → 風險控制 → 溝通 → 審查

TR 54-6 的創新：提供真實案例（來自製藥業賦形劑用戶），將抽象框架具體化。

兩大實務挑戰

挑戰一：賦形劑製造商的 GMP 多元性

許多賦形劑（如乙醇、丙二醇、蔗糖）的主要用途是食品、化妝品，其 GMP 標準（如 Food GMP、Cosmetics GMP）與藥品 GMP 要求差距很大。MAH 必須評估這些差距並決定是否可接受。

挑戰二：同一賦形劑用於不同劑型

例如：Polysorbate 80 同時用於口服膜衣錠和靜脈注射劑型。口服用途的 GMP 要求遠低於注射用途。同一家供應商可能收到兩份不同的 GMP 要求，產生衝突。

比喻：「知識管理解決方案」是什麼意思？

把賦形劑風險評估想成「建立一份供應商知識檔案」。每次評估後產生的文件（風險分數、供應商 GMP 等級、稽核記錄）累積成組織知識，下次評估或供應商變更時可快速參照，不必從零開始。

這就是 TR 54-6 所說的 excipient knowledge management solution。

思考練習

你的CDMO生產一款凍乾注射劑，使用蔗糖作為凍乾保護劑。同一批蔗糖供應商也供應你們的口服固體製劑工廠。按照 TR 54-6 的邏輯，注射劑和口服製劑對同一家供應商的 GMP 要求應該相同嗎？為什麼？

提示：不同。注射劑給藥途徑風險更高，因此對供應商的 GMP 要求更嚴格。同一家供應商可能需要滿足兩套不同的品質要求。

CDMO 實務應用

無菌填充 CDMO 面臨挑戰二的典型場景：客戶 A 用凍乾保護劑做注射劑，客戶 B 用相同賦形劑做口服錠。MAH 分別是客戶 A 和客戶 B，各自需要對同一供應商做風險評估，可能得出不同的 GMP 要求。CDMO 作為製造服務方，需要協助客戶（MAH）取得並整合這些評估資料。

1.2 Scope — 適用範圍

1.2 Scope

The information in TR 54-6 applies to all excipients used in drug products for human use at all stages of the product lifecycle. It assumes the excipient will be used for the drug product in the commercial supply phase; however, the manufacturing authorization holder may choose to apply this practical guidance in the clinical phase as well. That said, this technical report refrains from providing guidance to the manufacturing authorization holder regarding at which clinical phase it should be applied.

"The risk management process is applicable throughout the excipient's lifecycle, that is, from selection through termination of use, and remains the responsibility of the manufacturing authorization holder at each stage."

中文解析：適用範圍詳解

適用範圍三個維度

  • 賦形劑類型：所有人用藥品中的賦形劑（無例外）— 包括無菌注射劑、口服固體製劑、外用製劑、生物製劑輔料等
  • 產品生命週期階段：主要適用於商業供應階段 (Commercial Supply Phase)；臨床階段可選擇性適用，但本技術報告不規定具體從哪個臨床階段開始
  • 賦形劑生命週期：從選擇 (Selection) 到終止使用 (Termination) 的全過程

商業階段 vs. 臨床階段的實務差異

商業階段（本報告重點）：賦形劑供應鏈固定、供應量大、QMS 成熟，需要完整的正式風險評估文件。

臨床階段（可選擇適用）：供應鏈仍在建立中，賦形劑用量小，TR 54-6 不強制，但建議早期建立風險評估習慣，以便進入商業階段時能順利過渡。

重要提示：許多法規（如 EU FMD Article 46(f)）在商業藥品製造時才有強制性，臨床試驗材料有不同的監管路徑。

比喻：「適用範圍」如同保單條款

保單（TR 54-6）的承保範圍（Scope）是「所有人用藥品賦形劑」。就像汽車保險只保私家車，不一定保貨運車輛。知道邊界，才能正確引用。

臨床階段的賦形劑就像「試駕車輛」— 保單可以延伸覆蓋（MAH 自行決定），但不是默認保障。

思考練習

一個生物製劑 CDMO 正在為客戶生產 Phase 2 臨床試驗用注射劑。賦形劑包括：聚山梨醇酯 80、組胺酸緩衝液、蔗糖。根據 TR 54-6 的 Scope，CDMO 或 MAH 現在必須做賦形劑風險評估嗎？

提示：不強制（臨床階段），但 TR 54-6 允許 MAH 選擇提前適用。考量到日後 Phase 3 和商業化時需要完整評估，建議早期開始建立基礎文件。

CDMO 實務應用

TWi Pharmaceuticals 作為無菌填充 CDMO，所有商業批次製造均落在 TR 54-6 的強制範圍內。建議做法：

  • 在 Tech Transfer 階段即啟動賦形劑風險評估
  • 建立統一的賦形劑風險評估模板（參照 TR 54-6 的模型）
  • 對所有核准賦形劑供應商建立 EIP 收集流程
  • 將定期審查納入 APR/PQR 的年度計畫

Figure 1.0-1 — Iterative Risk Management Process (賦形劑風險管理迭代流程)

Figure 1.0-1: Identifying Whether an Excipient is Fit for Use

The figure illustrates the iterative, cyclical nature of the excipient risk management process as applied in supply chain management. Key elements include:

  • Central question: Is an Excipient fit for use?
  • Supplier/Excipient Identification — starting point
  • Risk Identification — driven by Route of Administration and Function of Excipient
  • Internal document assessment (Regulatory Dossier, Technical Documentation)
  • Excipient supplier assessment — covering QMS, Manufacturing, Supply Chain (suppliers information, quality/supply agreement, CAPA System/Change Management, complaint history/recalls, audit/inspection results)
  • Risk score rating — Low, Medium, High
  • Decision outcomes: Accept risk OR Risk reduction (for Unacceptable risk)
  • Control — Implementation of relevant Quality Practices (GMP/GDP required)
  • Review — Compliance Monitoring (KPI / Events / Audit)
  • Continuous loop back to Risk Assessment

Parties involved: Excipient User, Excipient Manufacturer.

中文解析：風險管理迭代流程圖

流程圖的核心邏輯

圖 1.0-1 呈現了一個循環迭代的風險管理模型，而非一次性評估。流程要點：

  1. 起點：供應商/賦形劑識別
  2. 雙軌評估：內部文件評估（法規檔案）+ 賦形劑供應商評估（品質系統）
  3. 風險評分：產出低/中/高風險等級
  4. 決策點：可接受 → 實施管控；不可接受 → 降低風險
  5. 持續監控：合規監控（KPI、事件、稽核）→ 回到風險評估

風險識別的兩大維度

根據圖 1.0-1，風險識別由兩個核心因素驅動：

  • 給藥途徑 (Route of Administration)：靜脈注射 > 肌肉注射 > 皮下注射 > 口服 > 外用，風險依序遞減
  • 賦形劑功能 (Function of Excipient)：功能性（直接影響療效/安全性）> 非功能性（輔助製造）

這兩個維度共同決定了風險評分的起始點。

比喻：風險管理就像開車的 GPS

GPS 不只是告訴你怎麼到達目的地（首次評估），它會持續更新路況（合規監控），遇到塞車（品質事件）就重新規劃路線（風險重評估）。TR 54-6 的迭代風險管理模型也是同樣的邏輯 — 持續、動態、響應式。

CDMO 實務：供應商評估涵蓋項目

依據圖 1.0-1，供應商評估需涵蓋：

  • 供應商資訊文件（DMF、CoA、CoC）
  • 品質/供應協議 (Quality & Supply Agreement)
  • CAPA 系統與變更管理記錄
  • 投訴歷史與召回記錄
  • 稽核/查驗結果（內部稽核或第三方）

這些正是 MAH 對賦形劑供應商進行年度供應商回顧 (Annual Supplier Review) 時需要收集的資料。

本章重點總結 — Section Summary

  • 聯合倡議：PDA + IPEC Federation 於 2018 年 3 月簽署 MOU，以「一個聲音」統一賦形劑 GMP 風險評估方法，TR 54-6 是此倡議的核心成果。
  • 四大法規驅動力：EU FMD 2011/62/EU (Article 46(f)) → EC Guidelines 2015 → PIC/S 2018 → FDA FDASIA 2012，共同將賦形劑 GMP 風險評估納入全球監管要求。
  • MAH 的不可轉讓責任：風險評估責任完全在 MAH，貫穿賦形劑整個生命週期（選擇到終止使用），供應鏈各方提供資訊支持但不承擔最終責任。
  • TR 54-6 的方法論基礎：以 ICH Q9 的風險排序與篩選法為核心，提供模型風險評估 + 真實案例，定位為補充指南，不取代現行法規要求。
  • 兩大實務挑戰：(1) 賦形劑製造商的 GMP 多元性（非藥用主業）；(2) 同一賦形劑用於不同劑型導致衝突的 GMP 要求。
  • 適用範圍：所有人用藥品賦形劑，商業供應階段為強制適用；臨床階段為 MAH 自行決定，但 TR 54-6 不規定具體介入時機。
  • 迭代流程：圖 1.0-1 強調風險管理是動態循環（識別 → 評估 → 控制 → 審查 → 再評估），並非一次性活動。
