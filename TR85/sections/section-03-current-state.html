3.0 Current State of Visual Inspection for Particles

原文內容 Original Text

A review of recalls of injectable products over the last ten years provides a useful measure of the current state of manufacturing and particle control. Figure 3.0-1 shows this information for product regulated by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA). The major contributors to these recalls are lack of sterility/sterility assurance and particles.

Particle recalls alone in Figure 3.0-2 shows a notable upward trend in 2013 with a peak in 2014. This was driven by an increased occurrence of both glass and metal particles. This resulted in greater awareness by both manufacturers and regulators and contributed to the higher number of particle recalls in following years. This situation was exacerbated by the nonspecific requirements for particles found in the global pharmacopeias but improved with the official publication of USP <790> in 2014 (6). At present, annual recalls for particles have returned to historical levels, but still account for a significant amount of product removed from the market and does not consider lots rejected while still within the control of the manufacturer.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Particle Control (微粒控制)：指在藥品製造過程中，從原材料、包裝元件到最終產品的整個鏈條中，對可見及不可見微粒進行監測、預防和消除的系統性策略。微粒控制是注射劑品質保證的核心要素之一。

USP <790> Visible Particulates in Injections (注射劑中可見微粒)：2014年正式生效的美國藥典章節，為注射劑可見微粒檢測建立了明確的要求和接受標準，填補了此前藥典中對可見微粒規範不夠具體的缺口。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你經營一家高級餐廳，如果客人在菜餚中發現頭髮或碎片，不僅要退菜（召回），還會影響餐廳聲譽。注射劑中的微粒問題就像餐廳中的異物問題 -- FDA 的召回數據就是「客訴記錄」，反映了整個產業的品質管控水準。2013-2014年的高峰就像一段時間內餐廳業界集體爆出異物事件，促使整個行業提高標準。

重點提示 Key Notes

召回數據僅代表「冰山一角」-- 報告明確指出，這些數據不包括在製造商控制範圍內就被拒絕的批次。實際上，微粒問題造成的經濟損失遠大於公開召回數據所顯示的。對 CDMO 而言，每一次因微粒問題導致的批次拒絕都意味著直接的產能損失和客戶信任受損。

2014年的召回高峰揭示了兩個關鍵問題：(1) 玻璃和金屬微粒是主要污染源，(2) 藥典要求的模糊性使得產業缺乏統一標準。USP <790> 的發布是重要里程碑，但仍不足以覆蓋包裝元件的檢測需求。

3.1 Patient Risk Assessment

原文內容 Original Text

All parenteral drug preparations contain particulate matter. Particles ultimately administered to a patient may originate from the primary packaging components or may enter through the manufacturing process and during administration from the syringe or infusion set apparatus. Therefore, the potential patient particulate exposure burden derives from the sum of these sources.

The clinical implications of visible particulate matter in injectables have been reviewed in several recent publications (7,8). Historically the presence of unintended visible particulate matter has been regarded as a quality defect which can impact safety resulting in additional batch-related actions, including batch reinspection, rejection, or recall. Visible product defects such as cracked or leaking containers or product discoloration often point to a product quality and possible safety issue. While the presence of a rare or isolated visible particle induces a similar instinctive response, the potential safety implication requires a comprehensive and contextually-based evaluation.

Patient exposure to particles occurs across a size continuum from submicron to millimeters, while risk assessments are generally conducted on particles reported as visible or subvisible components and not the aggregated risk of both subgroups. Assessing the potential risk that patient exposure to particles might present requires identifying the source of the particle; determining particle-specific attributes such as size, number, and composition; and ascertaining the intended use including the route and frequency of administration and relevant patient population characteristics (9). Any impact to the final product's benefit-risk profile that might result from the presence of particulate matter arises from the combination of these elements and calculated once the reference product and characteristics of the intended recipient are known.

In all instances of injections, the size of the particle is a restricting attribute. The particle must be small enough (or nonrigid) to pass through the bore of the needle (i.e., the particle must be less than the internal diameter of the needle in at least two dimensions). Detection probability increases with size such that larger particles are more likely to be identified with a detection probability (under ideal conditions) typically exceeding 95% for particles that are 200 µm and larger (2). Consequently, the product is either rejected during manufacture or found during dose preparation and discarded. There have been no reports identifying patient harm from randomly administered visible particulate matter in pharmaceutical preparations (8). Therefore, particles that are ultimately injected will be at or below the limit of detection of the qualified manufacturing environment with most of those particles being subvisible.

In most instances, the greatest risk to patients receiving injectable medicines is related to a lack of product sterility. For this reason alone, a robust inspection program is not sufficient; but rather, a broad range of other visible attributes is assessed along with laboratory testing of microbiological, chemical, and physical parameters. These are based on statistical sampling plans to assess the risk of elevated bioburden or other related harms in the batch. The risks to product sterility may be mitigated through processing decisions such as qualified component and product terminal sterilization processes (e.g., moist or dry heat, irradiation, or gaseous or vapor treatments). While the presence of a single particle is unlikely to provide a sufficient substrate to support microbiologic proliferation, multiple units identified with extrinsic particles should raise an alarm since the source is likely from a nonsterile environment. Appropriately qualified depyrogenation and autoclaving processes preclude the introduction of bioburden through primary packaging components.

Inert particles such as those from elastomers or glass generally pose little concern when introduced into a patient's subcutaneous tissue or muscle because they are typically sterile or sanitized. However, the particles could potentially stimulate a local immunologic reaction consisting of macrophages and foreign body giant cells at the interface of the particulate material (e.g., a granuloma), but it is likely that such a reaction would remain asymptomatic (10). More commonly, complications from subcutaneous and intramuscular medications arise from the irritating properties of the drug product and are often drug-specific but generally have a minimal impact (11).

Intravenous (IV) infusion of particles may induce phlebitis due to the particles causing direct traumatic irritation to the vein or from chemical damage by undissolved particles (12). Recent observational studies have also described instances in select patient cohorts where morbidity has been reduced through the use of infusion filters, suggesting that particles in the infusate were responsible for the difference in outcomes (13). Other studies have failed to corroborate these findings in additional patient populations (14).

A particle introduced intravenously that exceeds the internal diameter of a pulmonary capillary (7–12 µm) will lodge in that capillary or a more proximal pulmonary arteriole. While clinical outcomes are generally benign, such matter introduced into the systemic circulation might stimulate a local immunologic reaction (e.g., a granuloma) in the lung (15). These isolated, small parenchymal lesions generally remain asymptomatic. In addition, as demonstrated in animal studies, the massive infusion of particulates has been accompanied by histologic evidence of injury to pulmonary capillary endothelial cells (16), microscopic thrombi in the pulmonary capillaries (17), pulmonary microscopic granulomata (18), and hepatic inflammatory effects (19). An important distinction to note is that most of the studies above infused massive amounts of subvisible particles. While an all-inclusive review of the clinical implications of particulate infusions is beyond the intended scope of this technical report, the potential exists for the intravascular infusion of particulate matter to cause pathophysiologic injury depending upon the size and quantity of the particles, and, therefore, should be included as an element in any clinical risk assessment.

"In summary, particulate matter will be present in every injectable dose. The risk of visible particles in injections to a patient is low from products manufactured and inspected under appropriately qualified current Good Manufacturing Practices conditions (20,21). Their origin, composition, size, numbers, and characteristics of the intended patient population are all important factors to weigh when assessing the clinical import from exposure."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Particulate Exposure Burden (微粒暴露負擔)：患者最終接收到的微粒總量，來源包括包裝元件（玻璃瓶、橡膠塞）、製造過程，以及給藥時的注射器或輸液裝置。風險評估必須考慮所有這些來源的累積效應。

Benefit-Risk Profile (效益風險評估)：評估微粒對產品安全性的影響時，必須綜合考量微粒來源、大小、數量、成分，以及給藥途徑、頻率和目標患者群體特徵。不能僅看單一因素做出判斷。

Detection Probability (檢測機率)：微粒被目視檢測發現的概率與其尺寸正相關。在理想條件下，200 µm 以上的微粒檢測機率通常超過95%。這意味著最終注入患者體內的微粒，絕大多數是在檢測閾值以下的亞可見微粒。

Extrinsic Particles (外來微粒)：來自非產品或包裝本身的外部環境微粒。如果多個單位中都發現外來微粒，應視為重大警訊，因為其來源很可能是非無菌環境，暗示無菌保證存在漏洞。

比喻說明 Analogy

微粒風險評估就像評估食物中的異物風險。一根頭髮掉進湯裡（皮下注射中的惰性微粒）雖然不衛生，但通常不會造成嚴重健康問題。然而，如果碎玻璃混入食物（靜脈注射中的大顆粒微粒），可能會造成直接的物理傷害。而如果發現廚房裡多道菜都出現異物（多個單位出現外來微粒），就不是偶發事件，而是衛生管控系統出了問題 -- 這正是為什麼 TR85 強調要區分隨機單一微粒與系統性污染的重要性。

重點提示 Key Notes

關鍵結論：目前沒有任何報告證實「隨機給予的可見微粒」曾對患者造成傷害。這個重要事實為整個風險評估框架提供了基礎 -- 微粒風險雖然存在，但在合格的 cGMP 條件下製造和檢測的產品，其可見微粒對患者的風險是低的。

給藥途徑的風險分級：

  • 皮下/肌肉注射：惰性微粒（如玻璃、橡膠）通常已經過滅菌，風險較低，最多可能引起局部肉芽腫反應，通常無症狀
  • 靜脈輸注：風險最高。微粒可能引起靜脈炎、阻塞肺毛細血管（內徑7-12 µm），動物實驗顯示大量微粒可造成肺部和肝臟損傷

無菌保證仍是第一優先：報告再次強調，對注射劑患者而言，最大的風險仍然是產品的無菌性問題，而非微粒本身。這與 PDA 系列報告一貫的「決策層級」一致。

公式與計算 Formula

患者微粒暴露風險 = f(來源, 大小, 數量, 成分, 給藥途徑, 給藥頻率, 患者族群)

關鍵尺寸閾值：
  - 200 µm：目視檢測機率 > 95%（理想條件）
  - 100 µm：可見微粒與亞可見微粒的分界線
  - 7-12 µm：肺毛細血管內徑，IV給藥的關鍵截留尺寸
  - 10 µm：藥典亞可見微粒測試的起始尺寸

  微粒必須在至少兩個維度上 < 針頭內徑 才能通過

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 情境：你的客戶要求你為一種需要高頻靜脈輸注的生物製劑進行充填。根據本節的風險評估框架，你應該：

  • 將微粒控制策略的嚴格程度提升至最高等級（IV給藥 + 高頻 = 累積暴露風險最高）
  • 優先選擇低微粒釋放的包裝元件（如經過表面處理的橡膠塞、低脫片率的玻璃瓶）
  • 建立完整的微粒來源鑑定能力（forensic particle characterization），以便在出現微粒問題時快速追溯根因
  • 與客戶共同完成微粒風險評估，將結果納入產品的效益風險檔案

3.2 Existing Test Methods and Limitations

原文內容 Original Text

Visual inspection of primary packaging components prior to use is hindered by four factors: the lack of a standardized industry inspection method for particle detection; the diversity of potential particle defects introduced through the manufacturing process; decreased visual detection capabilities on isolated or empty components; and the probabilistic nature of particle detection (3). Visual inspection of the packaging components is generally conducted by sampling and not inspection of every piece (100% inspection), thereby limiting the information necessary to construct an adequate library of the type and number of particles the process produces. Detection of particles by MVI, SAVI, or AVI is a cumulative function of the particles' visible attributes including particle size, shape, color, density, and reflectivity. Particle detection can also be more difficult in the empty components because the particles cannot be put in motion as with particles in a liquid product (22).

The absence of standardized methods or visible particle acceptance criteria leaves it to each supplier and pharmaceutical manufacturer to establish these requirements for each product supplied. While regulatory guidance documents and industry publications may provide useful perspectives, the absence of a clearly articulated standard results in manufacturers subsequently adopting a hybrid approach using various portions of the published pharmacopeial (6,23) and International Standards Organization (ISO) methods (24). Identifying the limitations of test methods adapted for the evaluation of primary packaging components provides the opportunity to develop component-specific methods that would serve as a common standard for supplier-customer quality consideration.

Understanding the particle attributes is essential for each manufacturer when developing and adopting appropriate test methods, characterizing particle defect attributes, defining achievable detection capabilities, and assessing the potential impact of particles to the product's benefit-risk profile. The use of standard forensic methods of particle characterization (22) can be beneficial to inform the clinical risk assessment and direct allocation of resources to critical visual inspection and process control strategies. Additionally, the forensic datasets are important components to identify potential sources of particle ingress or generation, variability in raw material components, and monitor the stability of manufacturing processes (4).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

MVI / SAVI / AVI (目視檢測方法三大類)：

  • MVI (Manual Visual Inspection，人工目視檢測)：由訓練合格的檢驗員在標準化條件下以肉眼檢查
  • SAVI (Semi-Automated Visual Inspection，半自動目視檢測)：機器輔助旋轉/照明，但最終由人員判定
  • AVI (Automated Visual Inspection，自動目視檢測)：完全由機器視覺系統完成檢測和判定

Forensic Particle Characterization (微粒鑑識分析)：使用顯微鏡、光譜分析等方法，鑑定微粒的來源、成分和形態。這是建立微粒知識庫和追溯污染根因的基礎工具。

Probabilistic Nature of Detection (檢測的機率特性)：微粒檢測不是「有或無」的確定性結果，而是受微粒大小、形狀、顏色、密度、反射率等多重屬性影響的機率事件。同一顆微粒在不同條件下可能被檢出或遺漏。

比喻說明 Analogy

包裝元件的微粒檢測就像在空房間裡找灰塵 -- 比在一杯水裡找懸浮物困難得多。水中的微粒可以搖晃後觀察運動軌跡，但空的玻璃瓶或橡膠塞上的微粒是靜止的、可能黏附在表面，檢測難度大幅增加。這就是為什麼現有的藥典方法（設計給充填後的液體產品）在應用於空的包裝元件時效果有限。

重點提示 Key Notes

包裝元件檢測的四大障礙：

  1. 缺乏標準化方法 -- 沒有業界統一的包裝元件微粒檢測標準
  2. 缺陷多樣性 -- 製造過程可能產生各種不同類型的微粒缺陷
  3. 空元件檢測困難 -- 微粒無法像液體中那樣被驅動運動
  4. 檢測本身的機率性 -- 無法保證100%檢出率

這四個障礙構成了 TR85 存在的核心理由 -- 正是因為現有方法不足，才需要開發增強型測試方法。

3.2.1 ISO Test Method 8871-3

原文內容 Original Text

ISO 8871-3 Elastomeric parts for parenterals and for devices for pharmaceutical use — Part 3: Determination of released-particle count (24) provides analytical methods for the extraction of loose particulates from the surface of elastomeric components used in pharmaceutical packaging applications. The ISO standard is split into subvisible particle extraction using light extinction (or light obscuration (LO)) and visible particle extraction followed by membrane filtration and microscopic examination (MM) for particle detection. Once particle extraction is complete, quantification is performed with an optical microscope and a calibrated reticle at approximately 50x magnification. Particle counts can be conducted either manually or via a validated automated microscopic method. Both the particle extraction and the counting technique are considered optimized in evaluating elastomer-associated visible particles. However, the method would require some level of enhancement for visible particles (i.e., particles >100 µm and fibers >300 µm as defined in this report) through the design of a reticle capable of differentiating particles from fibers and identifying a shaker for use in Europe that is comparable to that specified in ISO 8871-3. Additionally, further concern exists regarding the sensitivity of the method based on the surface area/number of stoppers sampled. Currently, there is no similar ISO method for particle extraction or characterization for parenteral container inspection.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

ISO 8871-3：專門針對注射劑用橡膠元件（如瓶塞、柱塞）表面釋放微粒的定量分析方法。包含兩個主要分析路徑：

  • LO (Light Obscuration，光阻法)：用於亞可見微粒（通常 ≥10 µm），透過雷射感測微粒對光的遮擋來計數
  • MM (Microscopic Method，顯微鏡法)：用於可見微粒，通過膜過濾收集後在50倍光學顯微鏡下使用校準標尺計數

Calibrated Reticle (校準標尺)：置入顯微鏡目鏡中的刻度尺，用於測量微粒尺寸。TR85 指出需要改良設計，使其能區分微粒（particles）和纖維（fibers）。

比喻說明 Analogy

ISO 8871-3 的方法就像「洗衣服後檢查洗衣水」-- 先把橡膠塞放入溶液中搖洗（萃取），把表面鬆散的微粒沖下來，然後用濾紙過濾（膜過濾），最後在放大鏡下數濾紙上有多少顆粒（顯微鏡計數）。這個方法對橡膠元件效果不錯，但如果要檢查玻璃瓶，目前還沒有類似的「標準洗滌檢查法」。

重點提示 Key Notes

ISO 8871-3 的三個關鍵侷限：

  1. 可見微粒的增強需求：對於 >100 µm 的微粒和 >300 µm 的纖維，需要改良標尺設計才能有效區分
  2. 地區性設備差異：歐洲需要找到與 ISO 8871-3 指定震盪器等效的設備
  3. 取樣靈敏度：對於不同表面積/數量的橡膠塞，取樣策略的靈敏度是否足夠仍有疑慮

最大缺口：目前完全沒有類似的 ISO 方法可用於玻璃容器的微粒萃取和鑑定。這是 TR85 要解決的核心問題之一。

3.2.2 U.S. Pharmacopeia Methods

原文內容 Original Text

USP Chapters <788> and <790> are generally used to provide analytical method guidance for packaging component particle assessment. USP <788> Particulate Matter in Injections (23) provides for two test methods for the detection of particles ≥ 10 µm in size: LO and MM. The methods do not appear suitable for elastomeric component particles since surface particle extraction methods are not outlined, and the sample preparation and counting techniques do not appear applicable for particles >100 µm. Likewise, the applicability of these methods in the evaluation of glass-related components hold limited utility for visible particles. While nonfixed particles in glass components might be identified using these methods, the diversity of particle morphology and container design significantly impact the sensitivity and applicability of these methods to glass components.

USP <790> Visible Particulates in Injections (6) became official in 2014. Chapter <790>, along with Chapter <1790> Visual Inspection of Injections (1) established requirements and provides supporting information to be used along with 100% inspection during the manufacturing process to demonstrate that the process has produced a batch that is "essentially free" of visible particulates.

Table 3.2.2-1 provides a comparison of current inspection conditions and acceptance criteria for visible particles contained in the USP, EP, and JP. The chapters describe the manual inspection environment (lighting, background, and handling methods) that many component manufacturers have adapted for the inspection of empty containers and loose components. However, particle detection can also be more difficult in these components because the particles cannot be put in motion as with particles in a liquid product and the broad range of potential manufacturing defects generated by glass primary packaging suppliers. Additionally, these chapters are intended for filled-finished product and do not include defined test methods with clear requirements and specifications directly applicable to the individual packaging components used to manufacture injectable products. Therefore, the need for packaging component visual inspection guidance, which is currently lacking in the pharmacopeias, is critical to help characterize risk associated with these components and their impact on overall product quality assurance.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

USP <788> (注射劑中微粒物質)：針對亞可見微粒（≥10 µm）的定量測試方法，使用 LO 和 MM 兩種技術。但此方法未包含從包裝元件表面萃取微粒的步驟，且對 >100 µm 的可見微粒不適用。

USP <790> + <1790> (可見微粒檢測體系)：<790> 是強制性章節，規定了「essentially free（基本無可見微粒）」的要求及 AQL=0.65% 的統計接受標準；<1790> 是參考性章節，提供100%目視檢測的支援資訊。兩者結合構成了美國藥典的可見微粒管控框架。

"Essentially Free" (基本無可見微粒)：USP <790> 使用的關鍵術語，承認完全零微粒是不現實的，但要求透過合格的檢測程序證明批次達到可接受的品質水準。

比喻說明 Analogy

USP <790> 的「essentially free」概念，就像餐廳宣稱「本廚房基本無過敏原污染」-- 不是說絕對零污染（在現實中不可能），而是說已經採取了所有合理措施來最小化風險，並且能用統計數據證明。AQL=0.65% 就是那個統計證據 -- 相當於說「每檢查150個產品，允許不超過1個有缺陷」。

圖表解讀 Table Analysis

Table 3.2.2-1 的重要觀察：

  • 三大藥典的檢測條件高度一致：照度（2,000-3,750 lux）、時間（10秒，5秒白底+5秒黑底）、背景（黑白）完全相同，這反映了國際協調的成果
  • 接受標準卻各不相同：USP 最具體（AQL=0.65%），EP 最寬泛（"clear and practically particle-free"，無數字標準），JP 居中。這種差異正是造成供應商和製藥企業溝通困難的根源
  • JP 對塑膠容器有特殊要求：建議8,000-10,000 lux的更高照度，反映了塑膠容器因透明度和反射特性不同而需要調整的實務考量

核心缺口：所有這些章節都是為「充填後成品」設計的，沒有直接適用於「空包裝元件」的測試方法和規格。這是整個章節的核心訊息。

重點提示 Key Notes

對 CDMO 而言，理解 USP <790> 與包裝元件檢測之間的缺口至關重要。許多元件供應商借用了 <790> 的檢測環境條件（照度、背景、時間）來檢查空容器和元件，但這是一種「變通做法」而非「標準做法」。TR85 的價值正在於填補這個缺口，提供專門為包裝元件設計的增強型檢測方法。

3.2.3 European Pharmacopeia Methods

原文內容 Original Text

Two EP chapters govern subvisible and visible particles in sterile injectable products; EP 2.9.19 Particulate contamination: sub-visible particles (25) and EP 2.9.20 Particulate contamination: visible particles (26), respectively. The chapter on subvisible particles is harmonized with similar chapters found in the USP and JP, describing the same methods and containing the same acceptance criteria.

Chapter 2.9.20 describes the same inspection conditions as found in the USP and JP but does not provide a numeric acceptance criterion.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

EP 2.9.19 / 2.9.20 (歐洲藥典微粒章節)：

  • EP 2.9.19（亞可見微粒）：與 USP <788> 和 JP 6.07 完全協調一致，使用相同的方法和接受標準
  • EP 2.9.20（可見微粒）：檢測條件與 USP/JP 一致，但關鍵差異在於沒有提供數字化的接受標準

Pharmacopeial Harmonization (藥典協調)：三大藥典在亞可見微粒方面已實現協調，但在可見微粒的接受標準方面仍存在分歧。這種「半協調」狀態是產業面臨的現實挑戰。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像三個國家的交通法規 -- 大家都同意要有紅綠燈（檢測條件一致），也同意小車的速限標準（亞可見微粒已協調），但對大車應該跑多快卻各有不同看法（可見微粒接受標準不一致）。對跨國營運的 CDMO 而言，必須滿足最嚴格的那個標準。

重點提示 Key Notes

EP 不提供數字接受標準意味著什麼？在實務中，這讓製造商需要自行建立內部標準，並在驗證文件中證明其合理性。這增加了法規審查的不確定性，因為不同的審查員可能對「clear and practically particle-free」有不同的解讀。對 CDMO 來說，這意味著歐洲市場的客戶可能需要更多的溝通和文件來達成品質協議。

3.2.4 Japanese Pharmacopeia Methods

原文內容 Original Text

Like the USP and EP, the JP provides two chapters to address subvisible and visible particles. These are JP 6.07 Insoluble Particulate Matter Test for Injections (27) for subvisible particles and JP 6.06 Foreign Insoluble Matter Test for Injections (28) for visible particles. The subvisible particle test is also harmonized with the USP and EP while the test for visible particles has harmonized inspection conditions, again without a numeric acceptance criterion.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

JP 6.06 / 6.07 (日本藥典微粒章節)：

  • JP 6.07（亞可見微粒）：與 USP/EP 完全協調
  • JP 6.06（可見微粒）：檢測條件協調，但同樣缺乏數字接受標準（與 EP 類似）

值得注意的是，JP 對塑膠容器建議使用更高的照明強度（8,000-10,000 lux），這是三大藥典中唯一的差異化要求。

比喻說明 Analogy

三大藥典的現狀就像三家評鑑機構 -- 都使用相同的評分表格（檢測條件），但在「及格線」的定義上，只有一家（USP）給出了明確的數字（AQL=0.65%），另外兩家都只說「看起來夠乾淨就好」。這對想要同時取得三家認證的企業（跨國 CDMO）來說是一大挑戰。

重點提示 Key Notes

三大藥典比較總結：

  • 完全協調：亞可見微粒方法和標準（USP <788> = EP 2.9.19 = JP 6.07）
  • 部分協調：可見微粒檢測條件（照度、時間、背景一致）
  • 未協調：可見微粒接受標準（僅 USP 有 AQL 數字標準）
  • 完全缺失：包裝元件（空容器、橡膠塞）的專用檢測方法和標準

這個「從協調到缺失」的梯度，清楚地定位了 TR85 要解決的問題空間。

3.3 Conclusion

原文內容 Original Text

The observations documented in this report are intended to result in a standard test method. Such standardization is needed to produce reliable and comparable test results. The benefits of such a standard include clearer communication between supplier and users of pharmaceutical packaging components. It also permits setting meaningful limits and acceptance criteria for receipt and use of these important packaging components. Such limits would better align with the expectations for the finished products they are intended to protect and ultimately lead to more reliable supply of particle free sterile medicinal products.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Standard Test Method (標準化測試方法)：TR85 的最終目標是建立一個被業界廣泛接受的標準方法，使得不同實驗室、不同公司之間的測試結果具有可比性和可重現性。這是品質體系中「測量系統一致性」的基本要求。

Meaningful Limits (有意義的限度)：不是任意設定的數字，而是基於科學數據、風險評估和製造能力所建立的合理接受標準。這些標準需要與最終產品的品質期望相一致。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像供應商和藥廠之間的品質溝通 -- 如果雙方用的「尺」不一樣（不同的檢測方法），即使量出來的數字相同也無法比較。TR85 要做的就是給大家一把「統一的尺」，這樣供應商說「合格」，藥廠也能認同「合格」，不會出現雞同鴨講的情況。

重點提示 Key Notes

TR85 的價值鏈：

標準化測試方法 → 可靠且可比較的結果 → 清晰的供應商-客戶溝通 → 有意義的限度和接受標準 → 與成品品質期望對齊 → 更可靠的無微粒無菌藥品供應

這個邏輯鏈展示了 TR85 從「技術方法」到「商業價值」再到「患者安全」的完整影響路徑。對 CDMO 而言，採用 TR85 方法不僅是技術升級，更是提升供應鏈管理能力和客戶信任的戰略投資。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 行動計畫：基於第3章的分析，CDMO 應考慮以下行動：

  • 短期：盤點目前使用的包裝元件微粒檢測方法，識別與 TR85 建議方法的差距
  • 中期：建立微粒鑑識分析能力（SEM/EDS、FTIR等），為風險評估提供數據支持
  • 長期：將 TR85 方法整合到供應商品質協議和來料檢驗程序中，建立產業領先的微粒控制體系

  2. 「有意義的限度」（meaningful limits）應該如何設定？是基於「技術可達性」還是「患者風險」？兩者之間如何平衡？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 召回趨勢：FDA 2010-2019年的召回數據顯示，無菌保證和微粒是注射劑召回的兩大主因。2013-2014年因玻璃和金屬微粒出現召回高峰，USP <790> 的發布（2014）有助於改善此情況
  • 患者風險框架：微粒風險取決於來源、大小、數量、成分、給藥途徑和患者特徵的綜合評估。在合格cGMP條件下，可見微粒對患者的風險是低的。靜脈給藥的微粒風險高於皮下/肌肉注射
  • 檢測方法侷限：ISO 8871-3 僅適用於橡膠元件；USP/EP/JP 方法是為充填後成品設計的，不直接適用於空包裝元件。三大藥典在可見微粒接受標準上未完全協調
  • 標準化需求：TR85 的核心目標是建立包裝元件專用的標準化微粒測試方法，實現供應商-藥廠之間的可靠溝通和有意義的品質標準
  • 決策層級強化：即使在討論微粒風險時，無菌保證（Sterility Assurance）仍然是第一優先。多個單位出現外來微粒是無菌風險的警訊信號
