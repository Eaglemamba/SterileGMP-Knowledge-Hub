Scope — 範疇

Scope

The methods employed in the manufacture of Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products (ATMPs) are a critical factor in shaping the appropriate regulatory control. ATMPs can be defined therefore largely by reference to their method of manufacture.

For example, for gene therapy ATMPs, genetic modifications can be obtained through a variety of methods (e.g. viral & non-viral vectors, mRNA, ex vivo and in vivo genome-editing tools). The genetically modified cells can be of human origin (autologous or allogeneic) or of animal origin (xenogeneic cells), either primary or established cell lines. In a medicinal product, the genetically modified cells or gene therapy products can be presented alone or combined with medical devices.

This annex provides additional and specific guidance on the full range of ATMPs (as defined in the glossary) and the active substances that are used in their manufacture. This annex applies both to investigational ATMPs and market-authorised ATMPs. It can also be applied to ATMP manufacturing in hospital settings and for compassionate use programs, where authorised by national law.

This annex is divided into two main parts:

  • Part A: supplementary guidance and alternative provisions on the manufacture of ATMPs, from control over seed lots and cell banks through to finishing activities and testing.
  • Part B: further guidance on selected types of ATMPs and its substances.

範疇解析

ATMP 三大類型快速識別

  • 基因治療產品 (Gene Therapy ATMP)：透過病毒/非病毒載體、mRNA、基因編輯工具引入基因修飾
  • 細胞治療產品 (Somatic Cell Therapy ATMP)：人類細胞（自體/異體）經培養後回輸
  • 組織工程產品 (Tissue Engineered ATMP)：含有工程化細胞或組織，旨在再生/修復/替代組織

製造方法決定監管路徑

ATMP 的特殊之處在於「製造方法」本身就是產品定義的核心。CAR-T 的「從病人取血 → 病毒轉導 → 回輸」這個製程，決定了它必須遵循什麼法規。

CDMO 接案時第一個問題應是：「這個產品的製造方法讓它落在哪個 ATMP 分類？」分類決定法規義務。

適用範圍重要邊界

Annex 2A 同時適用於：臨床試驗用 ATMP（investigational）和上市後商業 ATMP（market-authorised）。在國家法規允許下，也可延伸至醫院自製和慈悲用藥場景。台灣 CDMO 為全球藥廠代工時，需確認適用 PIC/S 會員國的具體國家法規解讀。

Application of This Annex — 本附錄的適用方式

Application of This Annex

This annex, along with several other annexes of the Guide to GMP, provides guidance which supplements that in Part I: Basic Requirements for Medicinal Products and in Part II: Basic Requirements for active pharmaceutical ingredients of the PIC/S GMP Guide. This annex is not a stand-alone document and should be applied in conjunction with PIC/S GMP guidelines and annexes.

Where due to the nature of the product or technical necessities, specific guidance is provided in this annex, compliance with this annex is expected and takes precedence over other sections in the PIC/S GMP Guide unless there are good reasons for not doing so with documented sound scientific rationale applied using QRM principles.

In certain cases, other national laws may be applicable to the starting materials for ATMPs. For example:

  • (a) Tissues and cells used as starting materials of ATMPs may be subject to other national legislation that cover donation, procurement, testing, processing, preservation, storage and distribution.
  • (b) For blood or blood components used as starting materials for ATMPs, national legislation may provide the technical requirements for the selection of donors and the collection and testing of blood and blood components.

The manufacturing process for ATMPs is product-specific and different design approaches are possible. The appropriate application of GMP should be described, justified in the Clinical Trial Application (CTA) or Marketing Authorisation (MA), and in accordance with national law.

The manufacture and control of genetically modified organisms also needs to comply with other local, national or regional requirements. Appropriate containment should be established and maintained in facilities where any genetically modified organism is handled.

適用邏輯解析

法規層次優先順序

當 Annex 2A 對 ATMP 有特定規定時，優先於 PIC/S GMP Part I / Part II 的一般要求。但如有充分科學依據（以 QRM 原則支持並有文件記錄），可允許偏離。這給了 CDMO 合理的彈性空間，但代價是需要強健的風險評估文件。

起始原料的雙重法規義務

ATMP 起始原料（特別是人類組織/細胞、血液成分）除 GMP 外，還受捐贈、採購、測試等人體組織法規管轄。台灣 CDMO 如果涉及使用從海外引進的人類細胞，需確認進口許可及台灣《人體器官移植條例》相關規範。

GMP 與生物安全的雙軌合規

處理基因改造生物體（GMO）時，必須同時滿足 GMP 要求和生物安全等級（Biological Safety Level, BSL）要求。這兩者可能在某些設計上產生衝突（例如負壓艙對無菌保障的影響），Annex 2A 的 Section 3.7 對此有具體規定。

CDMO 決策點

接受 ATMP 委託製造前，合規評估清單應包含：(1) 確認產品分類 (2) 識別國家層級的起始原料法規義務 (3) 確認廠區現有 BSL 等級是否足夠 (4) 在 CTA/MA 文件中充分說明 GMP 適用策略。

GMP 強度「由早到晚遞增」原則

製程越接近最終產品，GMP 要求越嚴格。早期步驟（如細菌庫建立、質體製造）可適用「GMP 原則」而非完整 GMP；最終充填步驟則必須完全合規。這對 CDMO 的質量體系範圍界定（QMS scope）有直接影響。

Principle — 製造原則

Principle

The manufacture of ATMPs involves certain specific considerations arising from the nature of the products and the processes. The ways in which biological medicinal products are manufactured, controlled and administered make some particular precautions necessary.

Since materials and processing conditions used in manufacturing processes are designed to provide conditions for the growth of specific cells and microorganisms, this provides an opportunity for extraneous microbial contaminants (e.g. bacteria, fungi) to grow. In addition, some products may be limited in their ability to withstand a wide range of purification techniques, particularly those designed to inactivate or remove adventitious viral contaminants.

The design of the processes, equipment, facilities, utilities, the conditions of preparation and addition of buffers and reagents, sampling and training of the operators are key considerations to minimise such contamination events (i.e. engineering and technical controls). In addition, manufacturing processes need to be well designed and controlled so as not to add further variability to the product.

For biological materials that cannot be sterilized (e.g. by filtration), processing must be conducted aseptically to minimise the introduction of contaminants.

ATMPs require a combination of unique biological methods and standard physico-chemical assays for their Quality Control (QC). For many cell-based products, there is variability introduced through the starting materials that cannot be overcome by the manufacturing process or In-Process Controls (IPCs).

Adequate control of the starting and raw materials, well defined characterisation of the ATMP active substance and ATMP drug product release testing form the crucial part of the QC. Controls should take into consideration the intrinsic variability of the biological material needed for ATMP manufacturing. A robust manufacturing process is therefore crucial and in-process controls take on a particular importance in the manufacture of biological active substances and medicinal products.

ATMP 製造核心挑戰

ATMP 與傳統小分子的根本差異

傳統小分子藥品：原料藥結構明確，可用多種分析方法表徵，最終可滅菌。

ATMP：(1) 活細胞是「活的工廠」，培養條件同時適合雜菌生長；(2) 許多 ATMP 無法最終滅菌，必須無菌製造；(3) 起始原料（病患細胞）的生物學變異性無法被製程完全消除，這是 ATMP QC 最大的挑戰。

IPC 的特殊地位

傳統藥品中，製程中控制 (IPC) 是輔助工具；ATMP 中，IPC 往往是唯一能及時干預的手段。因為最終放行測試可能在產品交付前才能完成（特別是自體 ATMPs）。CDMO 必須在 IPC 設計上投入更多資源，而非依賴最終測試把關。

工程控制優先於程序控制

Annex 2A 強調「engineering and technical controls」——這意味著設計封閉系統、隔離器、適當的廠房氣流設計，比依賴人員操作規程更重要。這對 CDMO 資本支出決策有重要意義：投資硬體設施通常比訓練人員程序更能降低長期風險。

ATMP 污染風險公式

污染風險 = f(培養條件暴露時間 × 開放面積 × 操作複雜度)
                                 ÷ (工程控制強度 × 人員訓練水準)

降低風險的首要策略是縮短分子，即縮短暴露時間、縮小開放面積，並增強工程控制。

Chapter 1 — Pharmaceutical Quality System (品質管理系統)

Pharmaceutical Quality System & Quality Risk Management

1.1 ATMPs are not sold or supplied before an Authorised Person has certified that each production batch has been produced and controlled in accordance with the requirements of the CTA, MA and any other regulations relevant to the production, control and release of medicinal products as applicable. Special provisions apply for the supply of products that have a two-step release process (described in Section 6.14) or such that do not meet release specifications where there is no alternative treatment available (described in Sections 6.11 to 6.13).

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 1.4, xv)

1.2 GMP applies to the lifecycle stages from the manufacture of investigational ATMP, technology transfer, and commercial manufacturing through to product discontinuation. The biological processes may display inherent variability, so that the range and nature of by-products may be variable. As a result, Quality Risk Management (QRM) principles as detailed in Annex 20 are particularly important for this class of medicinal products and should be used to develop their control strategy across all stages of development and manufacturing steps to minimise variability and to reduce the opportunity for contamination and cross-contamination.

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 1.2)

品質管理系統重點

授權放行人 (Authorised Person) 的 ATMP 特殊義務

傳統藥品的 QP（歐洲）/ AP（台灣）放行是基於批次記錄和規格符合性。ATMP 增加了兩個特殊場景：

  • 兩步驟放行 (two-step release)：如 autologous CAR-T，在交付醫院時還需要補充最終品質確認
  • 不符規格緊急放行：當病患無其他治療選項時，不符規格產品也可能在 Competent Authority 批准下例外供應——這是 ATMP 獨有的人道主義彈性

QRM 在 ATMP 生命週期的全程應用

1.2 條明確要求 QRM 從臨床試驗製造貫穿至商業化、技術轉移直至產品停產。這意味著 CDMO 在承接技術轉移時，不能只接收 SOP，還需接收原研發廠的完整風險評估記錄，並在新製造地重新驗證風險控制措施的有效性。

生物製程的固有變異性管理

「biological processes may display inherent variability」——這句話的實踐意義是：ATMP 的 Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs) 和 Critical Process Parameters (CPPs) 設定必須容納合理的生物學變異範圍，而不是像小分子那樣追求極窄公差。CDMO 的製程驗證策略需要相應調整。

Chapter 2 — Personnel (人員)

Personnel Requirements

2.1 The health status of personnel should be taken into consideration for product safety. Personnel (including those concerned with cleaning, maintenance or quality control) employed in areas where ATMP active substances and products are manufactured and tested should receive training, and periodic retraining, specific to the products manufactured and to the duties assigned to them, including any specific safety measures to protect product, personnel and the environment.

2.2 Any changes in the health status of personnel, which could adversely affect the quality of the product, should prevent work in the production area. Health monitoring of staff should be commensurate with the risk; medical advice should be sought for personnel involved with hazardous organisms. General consideration should be given to Occupational Health & Safety (OH&S) for personnel involved with hazardous substances as required by national law.

2.3 Every person entering the manufacturing areas should wear clean protective garments appropriate to the operations to be carried out.

Where required to minimise the opportunity for cross-contamination, restrictions on the movement of all personnel (including QC, maintenance and cleaning personnel) should be controlled based on QRM principles.

In general, personnel should not pass from areas of exposure to live micro-organisms, genetically modified organisms, toxins or animals to areas where other products, inactivated products or different organisms are handled. If such route is unavoidable, a Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) based on QRM principles should be applied.

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 2.18)

ATMP 人員管理特點

雙向保護概念

ATMP 人員管理是雙向的：

  • 保護產品免於人員污染：人員健康狀態（如病毒感染）可能污染細胞培養基
  • 保護人員免於產品危害：病毒載體、基因改造生物體對人員構成潛在生物安全風險

這在傳統無菌製造中不存在——操作工作站人員通常只需保護產品，不需要特別的自身生物防護。

人員流動是污染控制的關鍵變數

2.3 條的核心要求：接觸「活微生物、GMO、毒素、動物」的人員，不得直接進入其他產品區域。這在多產品 CDMO 廠房中是高風險設計點。如果人員動線不可避免地交叉，必須有基於 QRM 的 CCS（污染控制策略）文件支撐。

實務設計建議

多產品 ATMP CDMO 的人員管理最佳實務：(1) 在平面設計時就規劃單向動線；(2) 建立「區域專屬人員」制度，避免人員在高風險區與低風險區之間流動；(3) 在 OH&S 計畫中納入定期血清學監測（如涉及特定病毒載體）。

稽核常見查核點

稽核員在人員章節通常查核：培訓記錄是否涵蓋 ATMP 特定安全措施？健康監測頻率是否與風險等級相符？更衣程序是否區分不同產品區域？人員動線圖是否與 CCS 文件一致？

Chapter 3 — Premises: Production Areas (廠房：生產區域)

Production Areas

3.1 Cross-contamination should be prevented for all products by appropriate design and operation of manufacturing facilities. The measures to prevent cross-contamination should be commensurate with the risks to product quality. QRM principles should be used to assess and control the risks.

Depending on the level of risk presented by some ATMPs, it may be necessary to dedicate premises and equipment for manufacturing and/or packaging operations to control the risk. Segregated production areas should be used for the manufacture of ATMPs presenting a risk that cannot be adequately controlled by operational and/or technical measures.

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 3.6)

3.2 Concurrent production of two or more different ATMPs/batches in the same area might be permitted where justified under QRM principles. For example:

  • (a) More than one closed isolator in the same room at the same time is acceptable, provided appropriate mitigation measures are taken.
  • (b) When more than one isolator is used to process different viral vectors in the same room, there should be 100% air exhaustion (no recirculation). In case of concurrent production of viral vectors, closed, separate and unidirectional waste handling is required.
  • (c) More than one biosafety cabinet (BSC) in the same room is only acceptable if effective technical and organisational measures are implemented to separate the activities.
  • (d) Multiple closed systems in the same area are permitted if their closed state can be demonstrated.

3.3 The measures and procedures necessary for containment (i.e. for environment and operator safety) should not conflict with those for product quality.

3.4 Special precautions should be taken for manufacturing activities involving infectious viral vectors (e.g. oncolytic viruses, replication competent vectors) that should be segregated based on a documented CCS and QRM principles. Simultaneous incubation and/or storage of replication competent vectors/products, or infected materials/products, with other materials/products is not acceptable.

廠房設計核心決策

隔離層級決策框架

廠房隔離要求由風險等級決定，由低到高：

  • Level 1：操作/技術控制即可（如 closed isolators 共用空間）
  • Level 2：需要 segregated production area（隔離生產區）
  • Level 3：需要 dedicated facility（專用廠房）

判斷依據：QRM 結果 + CCS 文件 + 國家法規要求。

3.2(b) 的病毒載體同區生產紅線

多種病毒載體同區同時生產時，100% 新鮮風（無循環）是硬性要求，這對 HVAC 系統是重大設計挑戰。台灣 CDMO 如計劃在同一廠房處理多種病毒（如 AAV、慢病毒），必須在建廠初期就規劃獨立的排氣系統，而非後期改造。

3.4 的絕對禁止：複製型載體的同時儲存/孵育

「replication competent vectors」（有複製能力的載體）與其他物料不得同時孵育或儲存，這是無條件禁止（"not acceptable"），沒有 QRM 豁免空間。設計儲存系統時必須確保物理隔離。

生物安全 vs. 無菌保障的矛盾

3.3 條提醒：生物安全（保護人員和環境）和產品品質（無菌保障）有時會相互衝突。例如，為了生物安全使用負壓艙，可能影響潔淨室的正壓維持。這個矛盾必須在 CCS 中明確處理，不能只顧其一。

Chapter 3 — Premises: Environmental Control & Air Classification (環境控制與潔淨度分級)

Environmental Control and Air Classification

3.5 Air handling units should be designed, constructed and maintained to minimise the risk of cross-contamination between different manufacturing areas and may need to be specific for an area. Consideration, based on QRM principles, should be given to the use of single pass air systems.

3.6 If materials (such as culture media and buffers) have to be measured or weighed during the production process, small stocks may be kept in the production area for a specified duration based on defined criteria.

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 3.13)

3.7 Positive pressure areas should be used to process sterile products, but negative pressure in specific areas at the point of exposure of pathogens is acceptable for containment reasons. Where negative pressure areas or BSCs are used for aseptic processing, they should be surrounded by a positive pressure clean zone of appropriate Grade. These pressure cascades should be clearly defined and continuously monitored with appropriate alarm settings as defined by Annex 1.

3.8 Air vent filters that are directly linked to the sterility of the product should be hydrophobic, monitored during use and validated for their scheduled life span with integrity testing at appropriate intervals. For short shelf life ATMPs, where microbiological quality tests are not available at the time of batch release prior to medical product administration, the filter integrity should be considered in the CCS as an additional risk factor.

3.9 Drainage systems must be designed so that effluents can be effectively neutralised or decontaminated to minimise the risk of cross-contamination. They must comply with national law to minimize the risk of contamination of the external environment according to the risk associated with the biohazardous nature of waste materials.

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 3.11)

3.10 The degree of environmental control should be adapted to the product and the production step, bearing in mind the potential level of contamination of the starting materials and the risks to the product. The microbiological environmental monitoring programme should be supplemented by methods to detect the presence of specific microorganisms (e.g. host organism, yeasts, moulds, anaerobes) where indicated by QRM principles.

3.11 Where processes are not closed and there is exposure of the product to the immediate room environment without a subsequent microbial inactivation process, appropriate environmental conditions should be applied. For aseptic manipulations, parameters in line with Annex 1 (i.e. Grade A with Grade B background) should be applied.

3.12 Only in exceptional circumstances when an appropriate manufacturing environment is not available, a less stringent environment than that specified in Section 3.11 may be acceptable for processes that are not closed where approved by the Competent Authority and in accordance with CTA or MA. However, this option should be considered exceptional and applicable only if the product is intended to treat a life-threatening condition where no alternative therapeutic options exist.

3.13 For closed systems, a lower classified area than Grade A in background Grade B might be acceptable based on the outcome of a QRM assessment:

  • (a) Processing inside single use sterile disposable kits, or using closed, automated manufacturing platform or incubation in closed flasks, bags or fermenters in Grade C may be acceptable if adequate control measures are implemented.
  • (b) If the closed system can be shown to remain integral throughout the entire usage, a background of Grade D might be acceptable.

3.14 In exceptional circumstances, it is permissible to perform a manufacturing step in premises not under direct control of the ATMP manufacturer or MAH (including placing equipment in hospital wards or theatre) where approved by the Competent Authority. Responsibilities of each party should be defined in written technical agreements.

環境控制策略解析

ATMP 潔淨度分級決策樹

開放製程（open process）：無後續微生物滅活步驟

  • 無菌操作 → Grade A + Grade B 背景（與 Annex 1 一致）
  • 例外：無其他治療選項的威脅生命疾病 → 可向主管機關申請豁免

封閉製程（closed process）：QRM 評估決定

  • 封閉但定期開啟 → Grade C 可接受
  • 全程保持密封完整性 → Grade D 可能可接受

3.8 短效期 ATMP 的過濾器風險

這是 ATMP 特有的難題：autologous CAR-T 等產品可能在無菌測試結果出爐前就已注射給病患。此時，「通氣過濾器是否完整」變成唯一可在放行前確認的間接無菌指標。CCS 必須包含此項風險的緩解措施，例如：每批次前後均進行完整性測試、強化操作員訓練、設定更嚴格的壓差監測閾值。

3.9 廢水處理是監管稽查熱點

生物危害廢水（如含病毒的培養廢液）的排放系統設計，必須同時滿足 GMP 和環境保護法規。台灣若設立 ATMP CDMO，需提前諮詢環保署和農委會關於 GMO 廢水的特殊排放標準。

3.14 「院區製造」的合規架構

某些 ATMP（特別是個人化療法）可能需要在醫院手術室完成最後一個製造步驟（如細胞回輸前的最終準備）。此時設備雖在醫院，但必須：(1) 主管機關事先批准；(2) 製造商與醫院之間有書面技術協議；(3) 在醫院中的操作維持驗證狀態。這對 CDMO 的品質合約管理能力是重大挑戰。

廠房設計稽查準備要點

稽查員在廠房章節重點查核：(1) 壓差設計圖及連續監測記錄；(2) 單程通風（single pass）的設計依據文件；(3) 封閉系統的完整性驗證記錄；(4) 負壓艙（如使用）如何確保無菌保障不被影響；(5) 廢水消毒系統的驗證報告。

Chapter 3 — Equipment (設備)

Equipment Requirements

3.15 Production equipment should not present any hazard to the products. The parts of the production equipment that come into contact with the product must not be reactive, additive or absorptive to such an extent that it will affect the quality of the product.

If single use systems (SUS) (i.e. disposable systems) are used, the manufacturer should take into account and verify the impact on the product from extractables, leachables, insoluble particulate and insoluble matter derived from such systems. Annex 1 regarding provisions for single use systems should be considered.

(Replaces PIC/S GMP Guide Part I Section 3.39)

3.16 Where required to minimise the risk of cross-contamination, restrictions on the movement of equipment should be applied. In general, equipment should not be moved from high-risk areas to other areas, or between high-risk areas (e.g. equipment used for the handling of cells from infected donors or the handling of oncolytic viruses). Where relocation is unavoidable, the risk should be assessed in line with QRM principles, mitigated and monitored.

3.17 The design of equipment used during handling of live organisms and cells, including those for sampling, should be considered to prevent any contamination during processing.

3.18 Primary containment should be designed and periodically tested to ensure the prevention of escape of biological agents into the immediate working environment.

3.19 Electronic systems used to support manufacturing must be qualified in accordance with Annex 11 and 15. Any analytical testing performed on materials not used in manufacturing but that support bioinformatics informing the manufacturing process (e.g. patient gene sequencing) should be validated. Such analytical equipment is expected to be qualified prior to use.

設備管理特殊要求

SUS (Single Use Systems) 的 E&L 評估義務

一次性使用系統 (SUS) 在 ATMP 中極為普遍（生物反應器袋、管路、過濾器），因為它消除了清洗驗證需求並降低交叉污染風險。但 3.15 條要求評估：

  • Extractables（可萃取物）：在溶劑/強烈條件下可能從材料遷移的化學物質
  • Leachables（可浸出物）：在實際使用條件下遷移至產品的物質
  • 不溶性微粒：可能對細胞治療產品造成毒性

3.18 一次性圍阻設備的定期測試義務

Primary containment（初級圍阻）包括手套箱、生物安全艙的密封系統。「定期測試」這個要求意味著需要建立測試頻率的 SOP，並保存記錄作為稽查文件。頻率應基於 QRM 評估，考慮設備使用強度和生物風險等級。

3.19 病患基因定序的設備資格

個人化 ATMP（如 autologous gene therapy）的製造通常從病患基因定序開始，以決定治療策略。3.19 條要求：這類「支持製造決策的生物資訊分析」設備和軟體也需要驗證和資格確認，即使這些設備本身不直接接觸藥品。這對 CDMO 的數位系統驗證範疇有重要意義。

設備稽查準備清單

ATMP 設備稽查關鍵文件：(1) SUS 供應商的 E&L 研究報告（含 AET 評估）；(2) SUS 完整性測試記錄（使用前後）；(3) Primary containment 定期測試記錄；(4) 生物資訊系統的 Annex 11 資格確認文件；(5) 設備移動風險評估記錄（如有）。

Chapter 4 — Documentation: Specifications & Traceability (文件管理：規格與可追溯性)

Documentation Requirements

Specifications

4.1 Specifications for ATMP starting and raw materials may need additional documentation on the source, origin, distribution chain, method of manufacture, and controls applied, to assure an appropriate level of control and oversight including their microbiological quality.

4.2 Some products may require specific definition of what materials constitute a batch. For autologous and donor-matched situations, the manufactured product should be viewed as a batch.

Traceability

4.3 Where human cells or tissues are used, full traceability is required from starting and raw materials, including all substances coming into contact with the cells or tissues through to confirmation of the receipt of the products at the point of use whilst maintaining the privacy of individuals and confidentiality of health-related information, according to national legislation.

4.4 For starting materials of human origin, the identification of the supplier and the anatomical environment from which the cells/tissues/virus originates (or, as appropriate, the identification of the cell-line, master cell bank, seed lot) should also be described.

4.5 A system that enables the bidirectional tracking of cells/tissues contained in ATMPs from the point of donation, through manufacturing, to the delivery of the finished product to the recipient should be created. This system can be manual or automated. It should be used throughout the manufacturing lifecycle to include clinical trial and commercial batches.

4.6 Traceability records should be kept as an auditable document and unequivocally linked to the relevant batch record. The storage system should ensure that traceability data allow for easy access, in case of an adverse reaction from the patient.

4.7 Traceability records for cellular and tissue-based products and for any personalized ATMP must be retained 30 years after the expiry date of the product unless otherwise specified in the MA/CTA or national law. Particular care should be taken to maintain the traceability of products for special use cases, such as donor-matched cells.

4.8 When xenogeneic cells are used as starting materials for ATMPs, information permitting the identification of the donor animal should be kept for 30 years unless otherwise specified in the MA/CTA or national legislation.

文件管理重點解析

「一病人一批次」的批次定義革命

傳統藥品的批次定義是「同一次製造過程中均一的一批材料」。ATMP 的 4.2 條要求：對於 autologous（自體）和 donor-matched（配對捐贈者）的產品，「那個病人的那次製造就是一個批次」。這從根本上改變了：

  • 批次記錄的設計（每個病患一份完整記錄）
  • 批次放行的工作量（批次可能多達每週數十個）
  • LIMS 系統的設計需求

雙向追溯 (bidirectional tracking) 的系統需求

4.5 條要求的雙向追溯意味著：

  • 正向追溯：從捐贈者 → 製造 → 病患（發現供應鏈問題時追蹤影響範圍）
  • 反向追溯：從病患不良反應 → 回溯製造批次 → 回到捐贈者（不良事件調查）

這要求資料系統在整個製造生命週期中保持完整的資料鏈，且在不良反應發生時能「快速」取用。

30年保存規則的實務挑戰

30年的追溯記錄保存是個系統挑戰：(1) 電子系統的長期資料完整性（軟體升級、格式遷移）；(2) 保存成本；(3) 個人資料保護法（GDPR 等）與強制保存義務的衝突。台灣 CDMO 需在 QMS 中建立專門的長期記錄保存政策，並評估資料主權與法規義務的平衡。

異種移植細胞（xenogeneic cells）的額外義務

使用豬或其他動物細胞作為起始原料時，不只要追蹤人類捐贈者，還要能識別供體動物（donor animal）。這觸及動物福利記錄、SPF（specific pathogen free）農場認證等額外的供應鏈文件需求。

CDMO 文件系統設計建議

為 ATMP 設計文件管理系統時需考慮：(1) 是否採用電子批次記錄系統（eBR）以應對高批次頻率；(2) 追溯資料庫需能存取個人健康資訊但同時加密保護；(3) 建立 30年資料生命週期管理計畫（包含媒體遷移策略）；(4) 設計系統能在不良反應發生後的 24 小時內提供完整追溯鏈。

Part A 第一部分總結 — Key Takeaways for CDMO Operations

  • 風險驅動架構：Annex 2A 全篇以 QRM 為核心，廠房分級、人員管理、設備選用、文件要求都基於風險評估，而非固定規則。CDMO 必須建立強健的 QRM 流程，並保存決策依據文件。
  • 封閉系統是 ATMP 的關鍵競爭力：3.13 條允許封閉系統在 Grade C 甚至 Grade D 背景下操作，意味著高度自動化的封閉製程可以降低廠房建設成本，同時提高污染控制能力——這是 ATMP CDMO 資本投資的重要考量方向。

Chapter 5（續）— 起始物料管理：供應商、人體血液/組織與種子批/細胞庫

5.23–5.28 Starting Material Controls & Supplier Management

The quality requirements established by the manufacturer in the MA or CTA for materials classified as critical during QRM process (according to PRP profile) should be discussed and agreed with the suppliers during the product life cycle. Appropriate aspects of the production, testing and control, including handling, labelling, packaging and distribution requirements, complaints, recalls and rejection procedures should be documented in a formal quality agreement.

Particular attention should be paid to avoiding contamination and to minimising the variability of the materials. Specifications related to the product (such as those in pharmacopoeial monographs, CTA, or MA), will dictate whether and to what stage substances and materials can have a defined level of bioburden or need to be sterile.

For products where final sterilisation is not possible and the ability to remove microbial by-products is limited, the controls required for the quality of materials and on the aseptic manufacturing process assume greater importance.

The selection, qualification, approval and maintenance of suppliers of starting materials, raw materials and materials that come in direct contact with the products during manufacture and storage (e.g. single use systems) together with their purchase and acceptance should be documented as part of the pharmaceutical quality system.

解析：起始物料管理的 ATMP 特殊性

PRP（Product Risk Profile）驅動的供應商管理

PRP 是 ATMP 特有概念：依 QRM 評估將每種起始物料定義為低/中/高風險，決定 GMP 控制深度。高風險物料（如細胞來源的原材料）需要正式品質協議；低風險物料的要求可以相應降低。

CDMO 操作意涵：客戶帶來 ATMP 專案時，必須提供每種起始物料的風險評估文件，CDMO 再依此建立供應商審核計畫。

無法終端滅菌 → 無菌製程更重要

ATMP 通常含有活細胞或對熱敏感的生物分子，不能像小分子藥物一樣進行終端滅菌。這意味著整個製程的無菌保障（aseptic assurance）成為產品安全的核心支柱，而不是最後的「補救措施」。

比喻：Single Use Systems 的角色

單次性使用系統（一次性袋子、管路、反應槽）在 ATMP 中幾乎不可或缺——它們大幅降低交叉污染風險。但 GMP 要求這些系統的供應商也需被納入合格供應商管理，就像傳統不鏽鋼設備的維護一樣嚴謹。

5.29 Human Blood, Tissues and Cells Used as Starting Materials

The donation, procurement and testing of human blood, tissues and cells used as starting materials for ATMPs should be in accordance with the applicable national law.

  • (a) Supply sites must hold appropriate approvals from the Competent Authority(ies) which should be verified as part of supplier management.
  • (b) For cell therapies, the maintenance of the aseptic processing from time of procurement of cells through manufacturing and administration back into the patient should be ensured.
  • (c) Where such human cells or tissues are imported, they must meet equivalent national standards of quality and safety.
  • (d) Processing at blood or tissue establishments is permissible only if authorised by national law (e.g. the material would be otherwise compromised and processing involves only minimal manipulation).
  • (e) Blood, tissue and cells are released by the Responsible Person (RP) in the blood or tissue establishment before shipment to the ATMP manufacturer.
  • (f) A technical agreement clearly defining the responsibilities should be in place between all involved parties (e.g. manufacturers, tissue establishment, sponsors, MAH).
  • (g) The transport of blood, tissues and cells to the manufacturing site must be controlled by a written agreement between the responsible parties.
  • (h) Continuation of traceability requirements started at tissue establishments through to the recipient(s), and vice versa.

解析：人體組織作為起始物料

「Chain of Custody」的法規意義

人體血液與組織在法律上受「捐血/組織捐贈法規」監管，與一般化學原料完全不同。供應商不只是商業廠商，而是持有國家主管機關許可的「血液庫/組織庫」。ATMP 製造商必須驗證這些機構的許可狀態。

RP（Responsible Person）的角色

組織庫的 RP 相當於製造端的 Authorised Person（AP）。組織庫 RP 放行後，起始物料才能離開組織庫進入 ATMP 製程。這是雙重放行架構——組織庫 RP 放行原料，ATMP 廠的 AP 最終放行成品。

CDMO 實務：接收人體組織的標準程序

當客戶的自體細胞（autologous cells）由醫院採集後送到 CDMO：

  • 確認是否有組織庫 RP 的放行文件
  • 確認運輸條件（冷鏈溫度記錄）是否符合書面協議
  • 在成品放行前確保捐者身份與收受者 ID 的可追溯鏈完整
  • 進口組織需符合本地等效法規標準

5.30–5.38 Seed Lot and Cell Bank System

A system of master and working virus seed lots and/or cell banks is recommended if the production of allogeneic ATMP involves cell culture or propagation.

5.31 The number of generations (doublings, passages) between the seed lot or cell bank, the active substance and finished product should be consistent with specifications in the MA or CTA.

5.32 Establishment of seed lots and cell banks, including master and working generations, as well as maintenance and storage, should be performed under appropriate GMP conditions. During the establishment of the seed lot and cell bank, no other living or infectious material should be handled simultaneously in the same area or by the same persons.

5.33 Following establishment, quarantine and release procedures should be followed. This should include adequate characterisation and testing for contaminants. Evidence of the stability and recovery of the seeds and banks should be documented.

5.34 Seed lots and cell banks should be stored and used in such a way as to minimise the risks of contamination (e.g. stored in the vapour phase of liquid nitrogen in sealed containers).

5.35 Cell based ATMPs are often generated from a cell stock obtained from limited number of passages. In contrast with the two-tiered system of Master and Working cell banks, the number of production runs from a cell stock is limited. Cell stock changes should be addressed in the MA/CTA.

5.36–5.38 Storage containers should be sealed, clearly labelled and kept at an appropriate temperature. A stock inventory must be kept. The storage temperature and, where used, the liquid nitrogen levels should be continuously monitored. It is desirable to split stocks and to store the split stocks at different locations to minimise the risks of total loss.

MCB / WCB 兩層系統的邏輯

Chapter 6 — 品質管制（Quality Control）

6.1–6.8 General QC Requirements & Sampling

6.1 In-process controls have a greater importance in ensuring the consistency of the quality of ATMPs than for conventional products. In-process control testing should be performed at appropriate stages of production to control those conditions that are important for the quality of the finished product.

6.2 The head of quality control is responsible for control of ATMP active substances, starting materials, raw materials and other materials such as primary packaging materials and any other material in direct contact with the product during manufacture as well as medical devices that are used in combined ATMPs. In case of autologous products or allogeneic products in a donor-matched scenario, the match between the origin of the starting material and the recipient should be verified.

6.3 Samples should be representative of the batch of materials or products. Certain types of cells (e.g. autologous cells used in ATMPs) may be available in limited quantities and, where allowed in the CTA or MA, a modified testing and sample retention strategy may be developed and documented.

6.4 Sample containers should bear a label indicating the contents, with the batch number, the date of sampling and the containers from which samples have been drawn.

6.5 In line with requirements of Annex 19, a reference sample of a batch of starting material, raw materials, packaging material and finished product should be drawn. It is acknowledged that drawing reference samples may not always be feasible due to scarcity of the materials or limited size of the batches (e.g. autologous products, allogeneic products in a matched donor scenario, products for ultra-rare diseases, and products for use in first-in-man clinical trials).

6.6 Samples of the starting materials should generally be kept for two years after the batch release. It is justified not to keep reference samples of the cells/tissues used as starting materials in the case of autologous ATMPs and certain allogeneic ATMPs (i.e. matched donor scenario). For cases where the starting material is an established cell bank system, there is no need to keep cell bank vials specifically for the purpose of reference samples.

6.7 A sample of a fully packaged unit (retention sample) should be kept per batch for at least one year after the expiry date. A retention sample is, however, not expected in the case of autologous products or allogeneic products, where justified (e.g. in a matched donor scenario).

6.8 Shorter retention periods might be justified based on the stability and shelf life of the product. In cases of short shelf life, the manufacturer should consider if the retention of the sample under conditions that prolong the shelf life (such as cryopreservation) is representative for the intended purpose.

解析：ATMP QC 的特殊挑戰

「In-process controls 更重要」的理由

傳統製劑（如注射劑）可以在成品完成後進行完整的品質放行測試，但 ATMP 往往因為：

  • 成品量極少（自體治療，一批=一位患者）
  • 效期極短（數小時到數天）
  • 最終測試結果回報前產品已需給藥

因此製程中的控制成為保障品質的核心，而非最終放行測試。

自體產品的身份確認義務

QC 主管的責任清單中明確包含：確認起始物料的來源患者與最終接受者一致。這是 ATMP 特有的「身份比對」要求，一般藥廠不需要做這件事。錯誤給藥（wrong patient）在細胞治療中是潛在致命事件。

比喻：參考樣品的「豁免邏輯」

Annex 19 要求每批保留參考樣品。但對自體 ATMP，整批就是一位患者的治療劑量，保留樣品意味著患者少一份藥。法規承認這個現實，允許在 CTA/MA 中說明並豁免保留，或以替代方式（例如批次記錄照片）替代。

CDMO 實務：樣品策略設計

  • 在 CTA/MA 階段與客戶及主管機關確認參考樣品/留樣策略
  • 若保留低溫樣品，確認該樣品在凍存條件下仍具代表性（無菌測試適用，但細胞活性特性不適用）
  • 細胞庫 vial 不需另為參考樣品保留，但其他起始物料通常需保留 2 年

6.9 On-going Stability Programme

The protocol for the on-going stability programme can be different from that of the initial long term stability study as submitted in the MA dossier provided that this is justified and documented in the protocol (e.g. the frequency of testing, or when updating to ICH/VICH recommendations).

Stability studies on the reconstituted and thawed product are performed during product development and need not be monitored on an on-going basis.

The use of surrogate materials (i.e. material derived from healthy volunteers) or alternative scientifically sound approaches are acceptable in case of autologous products (or matched donor scenario) where the entire batch needs to be administered to the patient.

解析：持續性穩定性試驗

Surrogate Materials（替代物料）的使用

自體 ATMP 因整批需給藥，無法保留樣品進行持續穩定性試驗。法規允許使用來自健康捐者的「替代物料」模擬產品行為——這是 ATMP 特有的彈性，需在 MA/CTA 中說明並論證科學合理性。

解凍後穩定性的處理

解凍後穩定性（post-thaw stability）在產品開發期間完成研究即可，不需要納入持續性穩定性監控計畫。這是對現實操作困難的務實讓步。

Chapter 6（續）— 批次放行：標準放行與分散式製造

6.10–6.14 Release — General & Short Shelf Life ATMPs

6.10 In general, batches of ATMPs should only be released for sale or supply to the market after certification by an Authorised Person. The batch release specifications are not limited to analytical results. The Authorised Person should assess the quality of each batch considering processing records, results from environmental monitoring, monitoring of process parameters, analytical results and all deviations.

6.11 Where authorised by national law, the administration of a product that does not meet the release specification might be performed under exceptional circumstances (such as when there is no alternative treatment available that would provide the same therapeutic outcome and the administration of the failed products could be lifesaving).

6.12 In cases where product does not meet release specification, the responsibility and the decision of the patient treatment are solely of the treating physician. The Authorised Person, the MAH and/or the Sponsor should consider:

  • (a) Batch manufacturing records should clearly state that the batch has failed the release specifications.
  • (b) The MAH should provide its evaluation of the risks of product administration; however, the decision is solely the physician's.
  • (c) The Authorised Person should report the supply to the relevant Competent Authorities.

6.13 Individual instances that do not meet release specifications may be addressed through lot-by-lot release programmes and specific case-by-case, risk-based assessments, where such programs exist within national law.

6.14 For ATMPs with a short shelf life, where established analytical tests might not permit batch certification prior to product administration, alternative methods of obtaining equivalent data should be considered (e.g. rapid microbiological methods). Subject to approval from the Competent Authority, batch certification of short shelf life products performed prior to completion of all product quality control is permitted.

  • (a) A suitable control strategy must be in place, built on enhanced understanding of the product and process performance.
  • (b) The procedure for batch certification should provide an exact and detailed description of the entire release procedure.
  • (c) The procedure may be carried out in two or more stages: initial certification followed by final certification after completion of remaining tests.
  • (d) Increased reliance on process validation should be considered as supporting data for batch release in absence of a complete analytical results panel.
  • (e) A continuous assessment of the effectiveness of the pharmaceutical quality system must be in place.

解析：ATMP 批次放行的倫理與法規張力

AP 的放行責任：超越純分析數據

ATMP 的批次認證不只看 CoA（Certificate of Analysis）。AP 必須整合：製程記錄、環境監控趨勢、工藝參數偏差、所有 OOS 事件。這要求 AP 具備更廣的判斷能力，而非單純審查分析報告。

「Failed Batch + Life-Threatening」的灰色地帶

6.11/6.12 是法規中罕見的「倫理條款」：當 ATMP 批次不合格，但不用藥患者可能死亡時，允許醫師在充分知情後決定是否給藥。此時 AP 的責任是：清楚標示批次失敗、提供風險評估、向主管機關通報——但最終決定交給醫師，不在 GMP Annex 的管轄範疇內。

短效期放行的兩階段策略

效期只有幾小時的細胞治療產品，傳統 14 天無菌測試根本無法在給藥前完成。法規允許「分階段認證」：

  • 第一階段：初步認證（基於製程記錄、EM 結果、已完成的快速測試）→ AP 初簽
  • 第二階段：完整認證（補充最終測試結果後）→ AP 最終確認

此模式需主管機關批准，且必須在 SOP 中詳細描述整個程序與責任分工。

CDMO 實務：短效期放行 SOP 設計要點

  • 明確定義哪些測試結果必須在初步認證前取得
  • 定義 OOS 結果出現時通知臨床人員的程序（含時間窗口）
  • 製程驗證數據必須足夠強健，能在測試完整性不足時作為支持依據
  • 持續評估 QPS 有效性，包含趨勢分析

6.15–6.16 Batch Release in Decentralised / Point of Care Manufacturing

6.15 In exceptional circumstances where approved by the Competent Authority, manufacturing of the ATMP may take place in sites close to the patient (e.g. ATMPs with short shelf life, clinical advantage of using fresh cells). This includes manufacturing models where partial manufacturing occurs at a central site and finishing occurs at a local site. National law might require GMP-manufacturing authorisations and/or authorisations for the procurement and/or manufacture of blood, cells and tissues.

6.16 The batch certification and release process becomes particularly important in decentralised systems. The following conditions need to be respected:

  • (a) A "responsible site" should be identified, responsible for oversight of decentralised sites. It must:
    • have availability of an Authorised Person;
    • ensure those involved in batch certification are adequately qualified and trained;
    • perform audits to confirm compliance;
    • ensure written contract/technical agreement between responsible and decentralised sites; and
    • ensure written arrangements for timely reporting of quality defects and deviations.
  • (b) The Authorised Person has ultimate responsibility for batch certification (responsibility cannot be delegated). However, when permitted by national law, the Authorised Person may delegate release to trained and qualified personnel at the decentralised site for exceptional situations (e.g. life-threatening cases or off-hours). A detailed algorithm must determine when local release is permissible. The Authorised Person reviews all releases that have occurred at a decentralised site within an appropriately justified timeframe.

解析：分散式製造的監管架構

Point of Care 製造的監管邏輯

部分 ATMP（特別是新鮮細胞製劑）在醫院現場製備後立即給藥，傳統「中央工廠 → 配送 → 給藥」模式不適用。PIC/S 允許分散式製造，但要求：

  • 有一個「負責任基地」（responsible site）承擔整體 GMP 監管
  • 分散現場的放行人員受 AP 監督，不能完全獨立運作
  • AP 的批次認證責任不可轉讓，只能「委託」部分動作

「責任不可轉讓」原則

即使在醫院現場由受訓人員操作放行，AP 仍須在合理時間內審查所有現場放行決定。若任何現場批次有問題，AP 必須能啟動回收或警示程序。

比喻：連鎖餐廳的品質管理

分散式製造就像連鎖餐廳：中央廚房（responsible site）提供食材規格和 SOP，各分店（decentralised sites）按程序操作。若某分店出問題，中央廚房負責調查、矯正、決定是否暫停。AP 是總公司的品質長，不在現場但負全責。

Chapter 7 — 外包活動（Outsourced Activities）

7.1 Outsourcing to Non-GMP Licensed Third Parties

Collection of starting materials and highly specialised testing in the jurisdictions that are subject to licensing (e.g. karyotype testing, exome sequencing) can be outsourced to non-GMP licensed third party, as allowed by national law, provided:

  • (a) there is a rationale and a justification in the quality system;
  • (b) the contract giver takes responsibility to ensure that the contract acceptor demonstrates an appropriate level of GMP commensurate to the risk to the product and the activities performed using the principles of Annex 20; and
  • (c) that proportionate qualifications/validations as appropriate are conducted (with reference to Annex 15 and Annex 20) to demonstrate that the activities are not detrimental to the quality of the product manufactured.

解析：ATMP 特有的外包彈性

為何允許委外給非 GMP 認證機構？

核型分析（karyotype testing）、外顯子組定序（exome sequencing）等高度專業的基因/染色體檢測通常由學術機構或專業臨床實驗室執行，這些機構未必持有 GMP 製造許可。法規承認此現實，允許委外，但合約委託方（ATMP 製造商或 MAH）仍負有監管責任。

Contract Giver 的責任不轉讓

委外不代表責任轉出。ATMP 製造商必須：評估外包機構的能力→確認其 GMP 水準與風險相稱→進行適當資格確認。這與傳統原料委外的管理邏輯一致，只是允許非全 GMP 認證的情形。

CDMO 實務：委外測試的管理

  • 建立合格外包實驗室清單，記錄評估依據
  • 技術協議明確規定測試方法、結果回傳時效、OOS 處理程序
  • 定期審核（audit）外包實驗室的品質表現
  • 確認外包測試活動不影響最終 ATMP 的品質

Chapter 8 — 投訴與產品回收（Complaints & Product Recall）

8.1–8.3 Product Recalls and Other Risk-Reducing Actions

8.1 If additional donor (human or animal) health information becomes available after procurement, which affects product quality, a 'look-back' procedure needs to be initiated. This involves an analysis of the risk(s) and of the need for corrective or preventive measures.

8.2 In addition to recalls, other risk-reducing actions may be considered to manage the risks presented by quality defects, such as the transmission of appropriate information to healthcare professionals which may be important for:

  • (a) a single batch product (e.g. autologous ATMP where the entire batch has been administered), or
  • (b) products where patient treatment interruption presents a higher risk than continued use of the recalled product.

In such cases, the MAH/manufacturer needs to provide information to the treating physician and to the Competent Authority. Quality defect notifications, pharmacovigilance signals and other notifications should also be sent as set in national law.

8.3 Consideration should be given to performing mock recall or mock transmission of appropriate information to healthcare professionals. Such evaluations should extend to both within office-hour situations as well as out-of-office hour situations. For authorised products, a yearly frequency is recommended unless otherwise justified.

解析：ATMP 回收的特殊複雜性

Look-back 程序：捐者資訊後追

傳統藥品的回收是「產品 → 市場」方向的物流問題。ATMP 增加了「捐者 → 製品 → 患者」的雙向追蹤。若捐者在採集後被診斷出傳染病，所有使用該捐者材料製備的 ATMP 都需要啟動 look-back，評估已給藥患者的風險並採取措施。

「無法回收」的現實：自體 ATMP

自體 ATMP 的整批已全數給藥給唯一患者，傳統意義上的產品回收無法執行。法規因此提供替代方案：向醫療人員通報質量缺陷資訊，讓醫師評估後續臨床監控需求。這是一種「風險溝通」而非「實物召回」。

Mock Recall 的重要性

ATMP 的供應鏈涉及組織庫、CDMO、醫院等多個環節，且患者通常病情嚴重、時間緊迫。若從未演練召回，真正需要時程序混亂的後果嚴重。建議每年進行模擬演練，且要覆蓋正常上班時間與非上班時間（凌晨接到通報如何處理？）。

Part B — 特定產品類型（Specific Guidance on Selected Product Types）

B1. Animal Sourced Products

This guidance applies to animal materials, which includes materials from establishments such as abattoirs. Since the supply chains can be extensive and complex, controls based on QRM principles need to be applied.

B1.1 Monitoring programmes should be in place for animal disease that is of concern to human health. Organisations should take into account reports from trustworthy sources on national disease prevalence (e.g. World Organisation for Animal Health — OIE).

B1.2 Control measures for starting and raw materials at establishments such as abattoirs should include appropriate elements of a Quality Management System. Xenogeneic starting material should comply with other national laws.

B1.3 Control measures for starting or raw materials should prevent interventions which may affect the quality of materials during their progression through the manufacturing and supply chain. This includes the movement of material between sites of initial collection, partial and final purification(s), storage sites, hubs, consolidators and brokers.

B1.4 Regular audits of the starting or raw material supplier should be undertaken which verify compliance with controls at the different stages of manufacture.

B1.5 Cells, tissues and organs intended for the manufacture of xenogeneic cell based medicinal products should be obtained only from animals that have been bred in captivity (barrier facility) specifically for this purpose. Under no circumstances should cells, tissues and organs from wild animals or from abattoirs be used.

解析：動物來源物料的特殊管控

Xenogeneic（異種）材料的定義

Xenogeneic = 來自不同物種（通常是動物）的細胞/組織。異種細胞療法（如豬胰島細胞移植）屬此類。這類產品的風險包含動物病原體跨種傳播（zoonosis），因此管控要求更嚴格，甚至需要特別法規核准。

屠宰場材料明確禁止

用於異種細胞治療的動物組織不得來自屠宰場（abattoir）或野生動物。必須使用在封閉屏障設施（barrier facility）中專門飼養的動物，且其健康狀態需連續監控記錄。這是 B1 最核心的操作禁止事項。

OIE 疾病監控的實際應用

世界動物衛生組織（OIE）定期發布各國動物疾病疫情報告。ATMP 製造商必須訂閱並追蹤相關報告——如果供應國爆發人畜共通傳染病（如口蹄疫、禽流感），可能影響動物來源物料的使用決策。

B2. Gene Therapy Medicinal Products (GTMPs)

There are several types of gene therapy products. Synthetic GTMPs are within the scope of the guidance in this section. For cell-based gene therapy products, some aspects of Section B3 may also be applicable.

B2.1 The manufacture and testing of GTMPs raises specific issues regarding safety and quality of the final product and safety issues for recipients and staff. A risk-based approach for operator, environment and patient safety and the implementation of controls based on the biological hazard class should be applied.

B2.2 A description of the production of viral and non-viral vectors, nucleic acids (e.g. plasmids, linear DNA, mRNA, siRNA) and genetically modified cells should be available in sufficient detail to ensure the traceability of the products from the starting material to the finished product.

B2.3 The following considerations apply to the ex-vivo gene transfer to recipient cells:

  • (a) Traceability requirements must be maintained.
  • (b) There should be a clear batch definition, from cell source to final product container(s).
  • (c) For products that utilise non-biological means to deliver the gene, their physico-chemical properties should be documented and tested.
  • (d) Although the vector used for the manipulation of the cell will not be part of the final product, all early processes in the manufacture of viral vectors are considered critical and their quality needs to be under control.

B2.4–B2.5 Manufacture of Viral Vectors and Plasmids under "principles of GMP": Annex 2A and elements of Part II of the PIC/S GMP Guide can be considered for the manufacturing of viral vectors and plasmids where appropriate. The ATMP manufacturer is responsible for appropriate quality of the viral vectors and plasmids used as starting materials.

B2.6 If the manufacturing of the vectors is outsourced, the ATMP manufacturer should assess the risk presented by the vector to the quality and safety of the ATMP and thereby select a suitable vector supplier. The vector manufacturer should be part of the ATMP manufacturer's vendor qualification programme.

解析：基因治療產品的製造要點

GTMP 的製造鏈複雜性

基因治療產品的製造通常分三層：

質粒製備（Plasmid Production）
▼
病毒載體生產（Viral Vector Mfg）
▼
細胞轉導 / 成品製備（DP）

每一層都有各自的 GMP 要求深度，法規允許早期階段（質粒、載體）適用「GMP 原則」而非完整 GMP。

「GMP Principles」vs. 完整 GMP

病毒載體製備若非最終產品，各國法規可能不要求完整 GMP 許可。但 Annex 2A 要求：製造商仍須確保過程控制、異常調查、變更控制的核心 GMP 元素到位。這個灰色地帶需要製造商主動風險評估並文件化。

生物安全等級（BSL）的影響

能複製的病毒載體（replication-competent）BSL 等級高於不能複製的（replication-incompetent）。BSL 等級決定廠房設計（負壓、廢氣過濾）、人員防護、廢棄物處理要求。CDMO 在評估 GTMP 項目前必須確認設施 BSL 許可範圍。

CDMO 接收 GTMP 項目的查核清單

  • 確認病毒載體類型及 BSL 等級，對照本廠設施許可
  • 要求客戶提供載體製造商的供應商資格文件
  • 確認質粒 → 載體 → 成品的可追溯性文件設計
  • 若載體委外，審查技術協議涵蓋的 GMP 要求範圍
  • 評估員工生物安全培訓需求

B3. Somatic Cell Therapy, Tissue Engineered Products & Combined ATMPs

For genetically modified cell-based products that are not classified as GTMPs, some aspects of guidance in Section B2 may be applicable.

B3.1 In the manufacture of such products involving human or xenogeneic cells special attention should be given to traceability requirements and definition of a batch.

B3.2 Authorised sources of cellular products, bio-molecules, bio-materials, scaffolds, matrices, and other substances that are licensed medicinal products or medical devices should be used where available.

B3.3 During the life cycle of the product where devices, including custom-made devices, are incorporated as part of the product, an appropriate Quality Agreement should be made between manufacturer and device suppliers to assure consistent quality of the device.

解析：細胞療法、組織工程與複合型 ATMP

Scaffold（支架）的 GMP 地位

組織工程產品的 scaffold 可能是醫療器材（Medical Device）或生物材料。法規要求優先使用已獲授權的 scaffold 產品。若需要客製化支架，必須與裝置供應商簽訂品質協議，明確雙方在 GMP 符合性上的責任。

Combined ATMP 的監管交叉

Combined ATMP 同時涉及藥品（ATMP 細胞部分）和醫療器材（整合裝置）的監管。製造商必須同時符合兩套要求，並在技術文件中清楚說明各部分的符合性路徑。

比喻：客製化裝置的品質協議

若 scaffold 是根據患者解剖結構客製的 3D 列印支架，它是「客製醫療器材」，沒有標準產品規格可參照。品質協議需要定義：每批 scaffold 的驗收標準是什麼、如何確保批次間一致性、不良事件如何通報。這類產品的 QA 管理需要跨領域協作。

術語彙編（Common Glossary to Annex 2A and 2B）

Glossary — Definitions

The Glossary in the main GMP Guide applies also to Annex 2A & B. Entries below are only included where the terms require further explanation within the ATMP context.

術語解析：CDMO 操作情境應用

MCB / WCB / WVS 系統——CDMO 的實際意義

當客戶帶來 ATMP 項目，最早需要確認的問題之一就是：「你的 MCB/WCB 建立了嗎？」

  • MCB 尚未建立：CDMO 可能需要協助客戶進行 MCB 建立，這屬於 GMP 範圍內的高度關鍵活動
  • MCB 已建立，在其他廠：需要技術移轉協議，確認 MCB 特性資料完整
  • WCB 是生產用細胞庫：每批生產使用 WCB 而非 MCB，確保 MCB 的完整性

Vector 與 Viral Vector 的層級關係

Vector 是廣義傳遞工具（包括質粒、脂質體、病毒）；Viral vector 是特指病毒來源的版本。臨床上常用的 viral vector 類型：

  • Lentivirus：可整合至基因組，用於 CAR-T 等
  • AAV（腺相關病毒）：多為 replication-incompetent，安全性高
  • Adenovirus：不整合，瞬時表現

CDMO 的設施 BSL 許可決定了可接收哪些 viral vector 類型。

Scaffold vs. Raw Material vs. Medical Device

Scaffold 的法規身份可能是原料、醫療器材、或兩者的組合，取決於其在產品中的作用方式。若 scaffold 是 combined ATMP 的「不可分割組成部分」，則整個產品按 combined ATMP 監管，scaffold 部分需同時符合醫療器材要求。這影響 CDMO 如何設計供應商管理和品質協議。

Somatic Cells 的三種來源類型

三種類型的 GMP 要求、可追溯性深度、免疫考量各不相同，CDMO 在接案前必須明確確認。

SPF 動物與 Barrier Facility

SPF（無特定病原體）動物就像「潔淨室中的動物」。它們生活在封閉環境，飼養人員不接觸外界動物群體。製造商必須持有這些動物健康監測的記錄，且可隨時供主管機關查驗。這是生物製藥最基本的生物安全控制之一。

QTPP vs. CQA vs. CPP：三者關係

這三個 ICH Q8R2 術語構成 ATMP 品質管理的邏輯架構：

  • QTPP：「我希望這個藥品達到什麼品質標準？」（目標設定）
  • CQA：「哪些品質屬性決定了是否達到 QTPP？」（關鍵特性）
  • CPP：「哪些製程參數影響 CQA？」（製程控制點）

ATMP 的 QTPP 必須在 MA/CTA 中定義，並指導整個控制策略設計。

Zoonosis 風險與 CDMO 的實際影響

異種材料的 zoonosis 風險是監管機關的高度關注點。CDMO 接收含動物來源材料的 ATMP 項目時，需要評估：

  • 動物健康監控記錄的完整性
  • 動物相關病原體（豬內源性反轉錄病毒 PERV 等）的病毒清除驗證
  • 員工個人防護與健康監控計畫
  • 廢棄物的特殊處置要求（GMO 含有的生物廢棄物）

本節重點回顧

  • QC 的核心挑戰：ATMP 因樣品稀缺、效期短、自體產品的獨特性，傳統 QC 要求（參考樣品、留樣、穩定性測試）均有對應的科學合理豁免路徑，需在 CTA/MA 中事先規劃並獲得主管機關同意。
  • 短效期放行：兩階段批次認證（initial + final certification）是 ATMP 的重要創新機制，允許 AP 在測試完整結果取得前初步放行，前提是有強健的製程驗證數據和完整的 SOP 支撐。
  • 分散式製造：Point-of-care 模式的核心是「responsible site」概念——AP 的認證責任不可轉讓，但可建立監督框架讓分散現場在特定情境下代為放行，AP 事後審查。
  • Look-back 程序：捐者來源資訊在採集後仍可影響已製備或已給藥的 ATMP，製造商必須有完整的雙向追蹤能力和相應的風險評估/通報機制。
  • Part B 特定類型：動物來源材料需 OIE 疾病監控 + 屠宰場材料禁用；GTMP 需釐清 GMP vs. GMP Principles 的適用範圍；細胞/組織工程產品強調 scaffold 的供應商品質協議；combined ATMP 需同時符合藥品和器材監管。
  • 術語應用：MCB/WCB 兩層架構、viral vector 的複製能力分類、somatic cells 的三種來源、QTPP/CQA/CPP 的邏輯關係——這些是 CDMO 在接收 ATMP 項目時評估可行性的基礎語言。

SCOPE — 適用範圍

Scope

The methods employed in the manufacture of biological active substances and biological medicinal products for human use are a critical factor in shaping the appropriate regulatory control. Biological active substances and medicinal products can be defined largely by reference to their method of manufacture.

This annex provides guidance on the full range of active substances and medicinal products defined as biological with the exception of Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products (ATMPs). Manufacturers of ATMPs should refer to PIC/S Annex 2A.

This annex is divided into two main parts:

  • Part A — supplementary guidance on the manufacture of biological active substances and medicinal products, from control over seed lots and cell banks through to finishing activities and testing.
  • Part B — further guidance on selected types of biological active substances and medicinal products.

This annex, along with several other annexes of the PIC/S Guide to GMP, provides guidance which supplements that in Part I and in Part II of the Guide.

There are two aspects to the scope of this annex:

  • Stage of manufacture — for biological active substances to the point immediately prior to their being rendered sterile, the primary guidance source is Part II. Guidance for subsequent manufacturing steps of biological products is covered in Part I.
  • Type of product — this annex provides guidance on the full range of medicinal products defined as biological with the exception of ATMPs.

The level of GMP increases in detail from early to later steps in the manufacture of biological active substances but GMP principles should always be adhered to. The inclusion of some early steps of manufacture within the scope of this Annex does not imply that those steps will be routinely subject to inspection by the authorities.

Antibiotics are not defined as biological medicinal products, however where biological stages of manufacture occur, guidance in this Annex may be used.

Guidance for medicinal products derived from fractionated human blood or plasma is covered in Annex 14 and for non-transgenic plant products in Annex 7.

Additionally, the manufacture and control of genetically modified organisms needs to comply with local and national requirements. Appropriate containment should be established and maintained. Advice should be obtained according to national legislation to establish and maintain the appropriate Biological Safety Level.

範圍解析

Annex 2B vs. 2A — 兩者分工

Annex 2A：ATMP（先進治療醫療產品）— 基因療法、細胞療法、組織工程

Annex 2B：所有其他生物製品 — 疫苗、單株抗體、重組蛋白、免疫血清、過敏原等

台灣 CDMO 若承接 CAR-T 或基因療法委託，需適用 Annex 2A；承接傳統疫苗或 mAb 則適用本 Annex 2B。

比喻：GMP 是一條漸進的坡道

生物製程的早期步驟（如初始發酵培養）就像在坡道底部——GMP 原則要遵守，但細節要求較寬鬆，且主管機關通常不會主動查廠。

越靠近最終滅菌前的步驟，坡道越陡——環境管制、文件要求、測試頻率全面升高，對應到 Part I 的要求。

重要分界：滅菌前後

滅菌前的生物活性物質製造：主要參考 Part II（API GMP）

滅菌後的後續步驟：適用 Part I（製劑 GMP）

這個分界點在 CDMO 廠的廠房設計和系統建置上非常重要——需要清楚定義在哪個步驟之後，文件、環控、人員訓練要全面切換到更高標準。

基因改造生物體（GMO）的額外法規層

在台灣生物製程廠製造重組蛋白或基因工程疫苗時，除了 GMP 要求，還需符合生物安全等級（Biosafety Level）的規定，兩者不得衝突。

建立 GMO 設施時需同時評估：GMP 潔淨等級要求 vs. 生物安全封鎖需求，找到兩者都符合的設計方案。

PRINCIPLE — 製造原則

Principle

The manufacture of biological active substances and medicinal products involves certain specific considerations arising from the nature of the products and the processes. Unlike conventional medicinal products manufactured using chemical and physical techniques capable of a high degree of consistency, the manufacture of biologicals involves biological processes and materials, such as cultivation of cells or extraction from living organisms.

These biological processes may display inherent variability, so that the range and nature of by-products may be variable. As a result, quality risk management (QRM) principles are particularly important for this class of materials and should be used to develop the control strategy across all stages of manufacture so as to minimise variability and to reduce the opportunity for contamination and cross-contamination.

Since materials and processing conditions used in cultivation processes are designed to provide conditions for the growth of specific cells and microorganisms, this provides extraneous microbial contaminants the opportunity to grow. In addition, some products may be limited in their ability to withstand a wide range of purification techniques particularly those designed to inactivate or remove adventitious viral contaminants.

Specifications related to products will dictate whether and to what stage substances and materials can have a defined level of bioburden or need to be sterile. Manufacturing must be consistent with other specifications set out in the CTA or MA (e.g. number of generations between the seed lot or cell bank).

Control usually involves biological analytical techniques, which typically have a greater variability than physico-chemical determinations. A robust manufacturing process is therefore crucial and in-process controls take on a particular importance.

Biological medicinal products which incorporate human tissues or cells must comply with national requirements for the coding, processing, preservation, storage and distribution of human tissues and cells.

Biological active substances and medicinal products must comply with the applicable national guidance on minimising the risk of transmitting animal spongiform encephalopathy (TSE) agents via human and veterinary medicinal products.

製造原則解析

為什麼生物製品比化學藥品「難管」？

化學藥品：合成反應遵循固定的化學方程式，批次間變異相對小，物化分析方法精確。

生物製品：活細胞本身就有自然變異，培養條件的微小差異（pH、溶氧、溫度）都可能影響蛋白質折疊、糖基化模式，導致批次間產品特性差異。

比喻：生物製程是「養活生物」不是「燒化學品」

化學合成就像按照食譜煮菜——原料加對、溫度控制好，成品基本一致。

細胞培養更像養一群挑剔的生物——環境稍有不對勁，它們就產生不一樣的代謝產物。這就是為什麼 QRM 在生物製程中特別重要：你需要主動識別、評估每個變異來源的風險。

生物製程的汙染雙重挑戰

挑戰一：培養基設計為促進目標細胞生長，同樣也為雜菌提供了溫床

挑戰二：部分產品無法承受強力去除病毒的純化步驟（如高溫、極端 pH），因此防護設計在前端更加重要

CDMO 對應策略：密閉系統設計 + 嚴格的環境監控 + 驗證的病毒去除步驟（如過濾、色層分析）。

In-Process Control 的特殊地位

化學藥品可以等最終產品出來再做一次全面測試；但生物製品的中間品往往無法長期保存，且某些產品的最終放行測試本身耗時長（如細菌培養）。

因此，在製程中每個關鍵步驟取樣測試（IPC），是確保生物藥品品質的核心手段，而非補救措施。

TSE 風險管控

使用動物源性原料（牛血清、胰蛋白酶等）時，需評估 TSE（海綿狀腦病，如狂牛病）傳播風險。

台灣 CDMO 採購這類原料時，需要供應商的 TSE 合規文件，並優先選用非動物源或重組來源替代品。

Part A — 人員與廠房設備

Part A: General Guidance — Personnel (Clauses 1–4)

1. Personnel employed in areas where biological active substances and products are manufactured and tested should receive training, and periodic retraining, specific to the products manufactured and to their work, including any specific security measures to protect product, personnel and the environment.

2. The health status of personnel should be taken into consideration for product safety. Where necessary, personnel engaged in production, maintenance, testing and animal care should be vaccinated with appropriate specific vaccines and have regular health checks.

3. Any changes in the health status of personnel, which could adversely affect the quality of the product, should preclude work in the production area and appropriate records kept. Production of BCG vaccine and tuberculin products should be restricted to staff who are carefully monitored by regular checks of immunological status or chest X-ray.

4. Restrictions on the movement of all personnel (including QC, maintenance and cleaning staff) should be controlled on the basis of QRM principles. In general, personnel should not pass from areas where exposure to live micro-organisms, genetically modified organisms, toxins or animals to areas where other products, inactivated products or different organisms are handled.

Premises and Equipment (Clauses 5–18)

5. The degree of environmental control of particulate and microbial contamination should be adapted to the active substance, intermediate or finished product and the production step. The environmental monitoring programme should be supplemented by methods to detect the presence of specific microorganisms (host organism, yeasts, moulds, anaerobes) where indicated by the QRM process.

6. Manufacturing and storage facilities, processes and environmental classifications should be designed to prevent extraneous contamination. Where processes are not closed and there is exposure of the product to the immediate room environment, QRM-based control measures should be put in place, including engineering and environmental controls. These principles should take into account guidance from Annex 1.

7. Dedicated production areas should be used for the handling of live cells. Dedicated production area should be used for the manufacture of pathogenic organisms (Biosafety Level 3 or 4).

8. Manufacture in a multi-product facility may be acceptable where considerations include: (a) knowledge of key characteristics of all cells/organisms/adventitious agents; (b) health status of donors for small batches; (c) live organisms prevented from entering non-related areas; (d) validated cleaning and decontamination; (e) environmental monitoring specific for the micro-organism; (f) products moved safely to prevent contamination; (g) campaign based manufacturing.

11. Air handling units should be designed to minimise cross-contamination. Consideration should be given to the use of single pass air systems.

12. Positive pressure areas for sterile products; negative pressure acceptable at point of exposure of pathogens. These pressure cascades should be clearly defined and continuously monitored with appropriate alarm settings.

15. Clean in place (CIP) and steam in place (SIP) systems should be used where possible. Valves on fermentation vessels should be completely steam sterilisable.

16. Air vent filters should be hydrophobic and validated for their scheduled life span with integrity testing at appropriate intervals.

17. Drainage systems must be designed so that effluents can be effectively neutralised or decontaminated.

人員與廠房解析

生物製程人員訓練的三個特殊面向

  • 產品特異性訓練：製造 BCG 疫苗的人員，需了解結核菌的感染風險與防護措施，與製造一般重組蛋白的人員訓練內容完全不同
  • 健康監控：如有免疫系統疾病或近期接受免疫抑制治療的員工，不能進入某些高風險區域
  • 動線管制：從活菌區走到滅活產品區，即使是維修人員也必須有明確的換裝或清潔程序

比喻：多產品廠就像共用廚房

在同一廚房做海鮮和做花生製品，都需要：嚴格的清潔驗證（證明沒有殘留），分開的器具或有序的使用時間（campaign 製造），以及清楚的標示和隔離。

生物製程的挑戰更大：活菌可能形成孢子、病毒可能在環境中存活——清潔驗證必須針對具體的微生物特性進行。

正壓 vs. 負壓的設計邏輯

正壓區（保護產品）：無菌填充區、關鍵製造區 → 防止外部汙染進入

負壓區（保護人員和環境）：病原體操作區 → 防止危險微生物外洩

CDMO 廠若同時有「無菌製造」和「病原體操作」需求，廠房設計必須仔細規劃壓差梯度，確保兩個目標都能達成且不衝突。

CIP/SIP 在發酵設備的重要性

CIP（就地清洗）：不拆管路，用清洗液循環清洗，驗證清洗效果（TOC、生物負載）

SIP（就地蒸氣滅菌）：發酵槽閥門需能承受蒸氣滅菌，確保滅菌後的密封性

空氣過濾器（疏水性 PTFE 材質）需定期做完整性測試（泡點或壓力持留測試），防止因過濾器失效導致整批發酵汙染。

多產品廠的 QRM 決策框架

台灣 CDMO 評估是否接受新產品（如在現有 mAb 廠添加腺病毒載體疫苗）時，需評估：

  • 病毒的致病性等級（Biosafety Level）
  • 現有清潔方法對腺病毒的殺滅效果是否已驗證
  • 是否需要獨立的 HVAC 系統
  • Campaign 安排與相鄰產品的交叉汙染風險
  • 環境監控方法是否需要增加腺病毒特異性偵測

Part A — 動物、文件與生產

Animals (Clauses 19–25)

19. Animal species used in manufacture fall into two categories: (a) live groups/herds/flocks (e.g. horses for immunosera, goats for transgenic products); (b) materials derived post-mortem from abattoirs (e.g. sheep/pigs for enzymes and hormones). Animals may also be used in QC assays (pyrogenicity, potency).

20. In addition to TSE regulations, adventitious agents (zoonotic diseases, diseases of source animals) should be monitored by an ongoing health programme. A look-back procedure should be in place to assess the suitability of materials from animals that have become ill. Withdrawal periods for therapeutic agents used to treat source/donor animals must be documented.

21. Particular care should be taken to prevent and monitor infections. Measures include sourcing, facilities, husbandry, biosecurity procedures, testing regimes, control of bedding and feed materials. This is of special relevance to specified pathogen free (SPF) animals.

22. For transgenic animals, traceability should be maintained from source animals through to production animals.

Documentation (Clauses 26–28)

26. Starting and raw materials may need additional documentation on source, origin, distribution chain, method of manufacture and controls applied to assure appropriate control of their microbiological quality.

27. Some product types may require specific definition of what constitutes a batch, particularly for cells.

28. Where human cell or tissue donors are used, full traceability is required through to confirmation of receipt at point of use. Traceability records must be retained for 30 years after the expiry date of the medicinal product, while maintaining donor confidentiality.

Production (Clauses 29–30)

29. Steps to increase process robustness, reducing process variability and enhancing reproducibility should be reassessed during Product Quality Reviews.

30. Particular attention should be paid to the control strategy to prevent or minimise the occurrence of unwanted bioburden and associated metabolites and endotoxins. For products from cells and tissues where production batches are frequently small, cross-contamination risks between cell preparations from different donors must be controlled.

動物、文件與生產解析

動物作為原料的特殊風險管理

使用活體動物（馬、羊）生產免疫血清時，動物本身就是原料來源。Annex 2B 要求的「回溯程序（look-back）」確保：若某匹馬後來被確認患有某種疾病，之前採集的血清批次都能追溯到並評估風險。

SPF 動物（無特定病原體）需要符合藥典專論要求，標準更高——相當於「有出生證明的有機動物」。

人類組織供體：30 年追溯義務

這是生物製品中最嚴格的追溯要求之一。使用人類組織或細胞製造的生物製品，從供體到最終患者用藥的完整紀錄，必須保存至產品有效期後 30 年。

實務上，CDMO 必須與客戶（MAH）明確在技術協議中規定：誰負責保管哪段追溯記錄，且如何在轉廠或公司易手時確保記錄不遺失。

比喻：細胞製品的「批次」定義難題

一般固體製劑的「批次」很直觀——同一鍋混合的片劑。

但細胞製品呢？來自不同供體的細胞能混在一起算一批嗎？通常不行（尤其是異體細胞治療）——每個供體的細胞可能就是一個獨立的「批次」。

Annex 2B 要求事先定義清楚，而不是等查廠時才解釋。

控制策略涵蓋內毒素和生物負載

培養基（溫暖、富含養分）特別容易孳生細菌並釋放內毒素（LPS）。即使細菌被後續步驟殺死，內毒素仍可能殘留在產品中，引發患者的發熱反應。

CDMO 的控制策略需包含：培養基除菌過濾、原料內毒素限度測試、製程中定期的生物負載監控，以及最終產品的內毒素測試。

CDMO 實務：小批次細胞產品的交叉汙染防護

承接多個病患的同種異體細胞製品時，同一天可能有多個供體來源的細胞同時在廠內處理。需要的措施：

  • 嚴格的隔離操作（不同隔離器或生物安全操作台）
  • 清晰的標示和確認程序（兩人確認制）
  • 不同供體的細胞在不同時間段操作（時間隔離）
  • 每個供體批次的完整鏈追溯記錄

Part A — 起始原料與原材料

Starting and Raw Materials (Clauses 31–38)

31. The source, origin and suitability of biological starting and raw materials (e.g. cryoprotectants, feeder cells, reagents, culture media, buffers, serum, enzymes, cytokines, growth factors) should be clearly defined. Where tests take a long time, it may be permissible to process starting materials before results are available, but the risk should be assessed under QRM principles. Release of finished product is conditional on satisfactory results.

32. The risk of contamination during passage along the supply chain must be assessed, with particular emphasis on TSE. Materials that come into direct contact with manufacturing equipment or the product (media used in media fill experiments, lubricants) must also be taken into account.

33. The preparation of solutions, buffers and other additions should be based on the principles and guidance contained in the appropriate sections of Annex 1. Controls for quality of starting/raw materials and aseptic manufacturing assume greater importance particularly for products where final sterilisation is not possible.

34. Where sterilisation of starting and raw materials is required, it should be carried out where possible by heat. Where necessary, other methods may also be used (irradiation, filtration).

35. Reduction in bioburden associated with procurement of living tissues and cells may require the use of antibiotics at early manufacturing stages. This should be avoided, but where necessary their use should be justified and they should be removed from the manufacturing process at the stage specified in the CTA or MA.

36. The donation, procurement and testing of human tissues and cells used as starting materials should be in accordance with national law. Traceability must be maintained from the donor to the batch of finished medicinal product. Requirements include:

  • (a) Supply sites must hold appropriate approvals from national competent authorities.
  • (b) Imported human cells/tissues must meet equivalent national standards; traceability and adverse reaction notification requirements apply.
  • (c) Processing of cells/tissues may be conducted at tissue establishments.
  • (d) Tissues and cells released by the Responsible Person (RP) in tissue establishment before shipment; test results must be available to manufacturer.
  • (e) Transport must be controlled by a written agreement; documentary evidence of storage/transport conditions required.
  • (f) Traceability from tissue establishments through to recipients must be maintained.
  • (g) A technical agreement should be in place between all responsible parties.

38. Where human or animal cells are used as feeder cells, appropriate controls over sourcing, testing, transport and storage should be in place.

起始原料解析

「先用後驗」（Use Before Test）的 QRM 評估

某些測試（如無菌測試需 14 天）的等待時間，若等待結果才投料，會大幅延誤生產。Annex 2B 允許在測試結果尚未出來時先投入生產，但：

  • 必須事先以 QRM 評估風險（若測試失敗，影響哪些批次？）
  • 最終放行以測試結果為條件（conditional release）
  • 必須有隔離和追溯機制，確保不合格原料的批次能被完整拉回

無法最終滅菌產品的原料管制加倍嚴格

對於不能最終滅菌的生物製品（大多數蛋白質藥物、活菌疫苗），原料的品質就是最終產品安全性的第一道防線。

Annex 1（無菌製造）的原則同樣適用於緩衝液、培養基的配製，即使在發酵階段——這代表無菌過濾、環境監控、潔淨度要求都必須提前介入。

比喻：抗生素使用是「最後手段」

在活組織採集過程中，有時難以完全避免細菌汙染，所以允許在早期步驟使用抗生素降低生物負載。

但抗生素必須在指定步驟「洗掉」——就像廚師可以用消毒劑清洗食材，但不能讓消毒劑殘留在端上桌的菜裡。若患者對青黴素過敏，殘留的抗生素可能引發嚴重反應。

人類組織採集：三重責任分工

組織庫（Tissue Establishment）：負責供體評估、採集、初步測試，Responsible Person 批准出貨

製造商（CDMO）：接收後適用完整 GMP，繼續追溯記錄

技術協議：明確界定每個環節的責任，包括出現問題時的通報義務

若組織庫在出貨後才收到供體健康資訊（如後續確診感染），必須立即通知製造商評估影響。

CDMO 供應鏈稽核重點

承接人類組織製品委託時，對組織庫的稽核應涵蓋：

  • 主管機關批准文件（Tissue Establishment 認可）
  • Responsible Person 的資格與職責
  • 供體篩選與測試的 SOP
  • 冷鏈運輸驗證記錄
  • 追溯系統的完整性（從供體到批號的連結）
  • 不良反應通報程序

Part A — 種子批次與細胞庫系統

Seed Lot and Cell Bank System (Clauses 39–47)

39. To prevent the unwanted drift of properties which might ensue from repeated subcultures or multiple generations, production of biological medicinal substances and products obtained by microbial culture, cell culture or propagation in embryos and animals should be based on a system of master and working virus seed lots and/or cell banks.

40. The number of generations (doublings, passages) between the seed lot or cell bank, the biological active substance and the finished product should be consistent with specifications in the CTA or MA.

41. Establishment of seed lots and cell banks, including master and working generations, should be performed under appropriate GMP conditions. This should include an appropriately controlled environment to protect the seed lot/cell bank and the personnel handling it. During establishment, no other living or infectious material should be handled simultaneously in the same area or by the same persons. For all stages prior to the establishment of the master seed or cell bank generation, principles of GMP may be applied. Documentation should be available to support traceability for all pre-master bank stages.

42. Following establishment, quarantine and release procedures should be followed. This should include adequate characterisation and testing for contaminants. On-going suitability should be demonstrated by consistency of characteristics and quality of successive product batches. Evidence of stability and recovery should be documented.

43. Seed lots and cell banks should be stored and used to minimise risks of contamination or alteration (e.g. stored in the vapour phase of liquid nitrogen in sealed containers). Storage of different seeds/cells in the same area must prevent mix-up and take into account infectious nature of materials.

45. Storage containers should be sealed, clearly labelled and kept at appropriate temperature. A stock inventory must be kept. Storage temperature should be recorded continuously; liquid nitrogen level monitored. Deviations and corrective actions must be recorded.

46. It is desirable to split stocks and store at different locations to minimise risk of total loss. Controls at such locations should provide the same assurances.

47. Storage and handling conditions should be managed according to the same procedures. Once containers are removed from the seed lot/cell bank management system, containers should not be returned to stock.

MCB 建立前 vs. 建立後：GMP 要求的分水嶺

MCB 建立前（研發早期克隆篩選）：可套用 GMP 原則（principles），不強制完整 GMP。但文件仍需完整，以支持後續追溯。

MCB 建立後：必須在完整 GMP 條件下操作——環境管制、文件、偏差管理一樣都不能少。

這個分水嶺在 CDMO 承接新分子委託時非常重要——客戶轉移 MCB 時，需確認原建立條件是否符合要求。

比喻：MCB 是「種源」，WCB 是「種苗」

農業比喻：MCB 是種源中心保管的珍貴原種，WCB 是農民每季從種源中心取出的種苗。每季都從種源補充種苗，確保農作物的遺傳穩定性，不會因為多代繁殖而退化。

生物製程中「代數限制」（passages limit）就是確保細胞不會隨著繁殖代數增加而產生不可控的基因漂移。

液態氮氣相儲存：為什麼是氣相而非液相？

傳統方法將樣品直接浸入液態氮（液相儲存），但這可能造成交叉汙染——若某個安瓿有細微裂縫，液態氮會滲入，取出復溫時可能洩出並汙染其他樣品。

氣相儲存（vapour phase）讓樣品懸浮在氮氣蒸氣中而非直接接觸液氮，降低交叉汙染風險。溫度約 -150°C 至 -190°C，足以長期保存生物活性。

備份分存（Split Stock）：災難防護的必要措施

MCB 只有一份保存在同一地點，若發生火災、停電、液氮外洩，整條產品線就此消失。

Annex 2B 建議分存在不同地點——理想情況是不同城市甚至不同國家的合格儲存設施。對 CDMO 而言，這是風險管理的基本動作。

MCB/WCB 管理查核要點

  • 庫存清單是否持續更新（包含每次使用紀錄）
  • 溫度監控是否連續且有警報設置
  • 液氮液位是否定期監控
  • 偏差（如停電事件）是否有完整調查和 CAPA
  • 取出安瓿後是否有「不退還」政策的執行記錄
  • 備份分存設施的管制標準是否等同主庫

Part A — 操作原則與品質管制

Operating Principles (Clauses 48–65)

48. Change management should, on a periodic basis, take into account cumulative effects of changes on quality, safety and efficacy of the finished product.

49. Critical operational parameters, or other input parameters which affect product quality, need to be identified, validated, documented and shown to be maintained within requirements.

50. A control strategy for the entry of articles and materials into production areas should be based on QRM principles. For aseptic processes, heat stable articles should enter through a double-ended autoclave or oven. Heat labile articles should enter through an air lock with interlocked doors and effective surface sanitisation.

51. The growth promoting properties of culture media should be demonstrated suitable for its intended use. If possible, media should be sterilized in situ. In-line sterilizing filters for routine addition of gases, media, acids, alkalis, anti-foaming agents to fermenters should be used where possible.

52. Addition of materials or cultures to fermenters and other vessels, and sampling, should be carried out under carefully controlled conditions to prevent contamination. Correct connection of vessels should be verified.

53. Continuous monitoring of some production processes (e.g. fermentation) may be necessary; such data should form part of the batch record. Where continuous culture is used, special consideration should be given to QC requirements.

54. Centrifugation and blending of products can lead to aerosol formation; containment of such activities to minimise cross-contamination is necessary.

55. Accidental spillages, especially of live organisms, must be dealt with quickly and safely. Qualified decontamination measures should be available for each organism or groups of related organisms.

57. In cases where a virus inactivation or removal process is performed during manufacture, measures should be taken to avoid the risk of recontamination of treated products by non-treated products.

58. For products inactivated by addition of a reagent (e.g. micro-organisms during vaccine manufacture), the process should ensure complete inactivation of live organism. In addition to thorough mixing of culture and inactivant, consideration should be given to contact of all product-contact surfaces and, where required, transfer to a second vessel.

59. For chromatography equipment and matrices, QRM principles should be used to devise the control strategy when used in campaign manufacture and in multi-product environments. Re-use of the same matrix at different stages of processing is discouraged. Acceptance criteria, operating conditions, regeneration methods, life span and sanitization/sterilisation methods of columns should be defined.

61. There should be a system to assure the integrity and closure of containers after filling where final products or intermediates represent a special risk. Filling and packaging operations need procedures to maintain the product within specified limits (e.g. time and/or temperature).

62. Activities in handling vials containing live biological agents must prevent contamination of other products or egress of live agents into the work or external environment.

63. Care should be taken in preparation, printing, storage and application of labels. For autologous products, the unique patient identifier and "for autologous use only" should be indicated on the outer packaging.

64. The compatibility of labels with ultra-low storage temperatures should be verified where such temperatures are used.

65. Where donor information becomes available after procurement which affects product quality, it should be taken into account in recall procedures.

Quality Control (Clauses 66–70)

66. In-process controls have greater importance in ensuring the consistency of biological active substances and medicinal products than for conventional products. In-process control testing should be performed at appropriate stages of production.

67. Where intermediates can be stored for extended periods, consideration should be given to inclusion of batches made from materials held for their maximum in-process periods in the on-going stability programme.

69. For cellular products, sterility tests should be conducted on antibiotic-free cultures of cells or cell banks to provide evidence for absence of bacterial and fungal contamination.

70. For biological medicinal products with a short shelf life (period not permitting release when sterility testing results are provided after 14 days or less), a suitable control strategy must be in place. Such controls need enhanced understanding of product and process performance. The procedure for batch certification and release may be carried out in two stages:

  • (a) Assessment by designated person(s) of batch processing records and environmental monitoring results for initial certification by the Responsible Person.
  • (b) Assessment of final analytical tests for final certification by the Authorised Person. Procedures for out-of-specification results, including liaison with clinical staff, must be in place.

操作原則與品質管制解析

累積變更影響評估

生物製程的「累積效應」問題比化學製藥更顯著：單一變更（如更換培養基供應商）可能影響不大，但同期進行的多個微小變更疊加起來，可能對產品品質產生顯著影響。

Annex 2B 要求在 PQR（產品品質審查）中定期評估累積變更——這不只是合規要求，也是維護生物製程穩定性的好工具。

發酵製程的連續監控

不同於固體製劑批次，發酵過程是動態的——pH、溫度、溶氧、糖濃度等參數隨時間變化。連續監控這些參數並將數據納入批次紀錄，是追溯製程異常的重要手段。

現代的 Process Analytical Technology（PAT）和 SCADA 系統可自動記錄這些數據，大幅減少人工抄錄錯誤。

比喻：病毒滅活的「兩室隔離」

就像急診室處置完傳染病患者後，需要徹底消毒才能接待下一位病患。病毒滅活步驟完成後，必須確保滅活後的產品不會接觸任何尚未處理的活性材料。

「轉移到第二個容器」的做法，就是物理隔離滅活前後的材料——產品接觸面的殘留活性病毒風險，靠空間隔離徹底消除。

短效期產品：兩階段放行（Two-Stage Release）

某些生物製品（如細胞治療、放射性藥品）的保存期限只有數天，傳統的先完成所有測試再放行根本行不通。

解決方案：兩階段放行——

  • 第一階段（Responsible Person）：評估製程數據、環境監控結果，初步認可
  • 第二階段（Authorised Person）：最終測試完成後，補充完整放行

前提是整個製程必須有高度的參數理解和驗證，才能支撐「提前放行」的信心。

色層分析管柱：多產品廠的關鍵管制點

在多產品 CDMO 廠，蛋白質 A 管柱（Protein A column）用於多個 mAb 產品的純化是常見安排。需要管制的要素：

  • 每個管柱的使用壽命（循環次數）和再生方法
  • 不同產品間的清潔驗證（是否允許跨產品使用？）
  • 管柱效能衰退的接受標準（如板高、不對稱因子）
  • 廢棄管柱的處理程序

Annex 2B 明確不鼓勵在不同純化步驟間重複使用同一管柱——每個步驟應有專屬管柱。

自體治療產品的標示要求

「For autologous use only（僅供自體使用）」不只是文字規定——這條標示是防止悲劇的最後防線。

自體細胞治療中，患者 A 的細胞若誤用於患者 B，不僅無效，還可能引發免疫排斥。標示、標籤相容性（超低溫下不脫落）、雙人確認程序缺一不可。

Part B — 動物源性產品與過敏原

B1. Animal Sourced Products

Since the supply chains can be extensive and complex, controls based on QRM principles need to be applied. Documentation to demonstrate supply chain traceability and clear roles of participants, typically including a sufficiently detailed process map, should be in place.

B1.1. Monitoring programmes should be in place for animal disease of concern to human health. Organisations should take into account reports from trustworthy sources (e.g. World Organisation for Animal Health, OIE) on national disease prevalence. This should be supplemented by health monitoring at national and local levels, including source farms/feedlots and controls during transport to abattoirs.

B1.2. Where abattoirs are used, they should operate to stringent standards. Account should be taken of reports from national regulatory organisations verifying compliance with food safety, veterinary and plant health legislation.

B1.3. Control measures for starting or raw materials at abattoirs should include appropriate elements of a Quality Management System to assure operator training, materials traceability, control and consistency. These measures may be drawn from sources outside PIC/S GMP but should provide equivalent levels of control.

B1.4. Controls should prevent interventions affecting material quality, or at least provide evidence of such activities, during progression through the manufacturing and supply chain, including movement between collection, purification, storage sites, hubs, consolidators and brokers. Breaches must be recorded, investigated, and actions taken.

B1.5. Regular audits of starting/raw material suppliers should verify compliance. Issues must be investigated appropriately. Systems must ensure effective corrective and preventive actions.

B2. Allergen Products

Materials may be manufactured by extraction from natural sources or by recombinant DNA technology.

B2.1. Source materials should be described in sufficient detail to ensure consistency in supply (common and scientific name, origin, nature, contaminant limits, collection method). Those derived from animals should be from healthy sources. Appropriate biosecurity controls for colonies (e.g. mites, animals) used for allergen extraction. Allergen products should be stored under defined conditions to minimise deterioration.

B2.2. Production process steps including pre-treatment, extraction, filtration, dialysis, concentration or freeze-drying should be described in detail and validated.

B2.3. The modification processes to manufacture modified allergen extracts (allergoids, conjugates) should be described. Intermediates should be identified and controlled.

B2.4. Allergen extract mixtures should be prepared from individual extracts from single source materials. Each individual extract should be considered as one active substance.

動物源性產品與過敏原解析

動物源性原料的「延伸供應鏈」管理

豬胰蛋白酶的供應鏈可能是：農場 → 屠宰場 → 初步處理商 → 冷鏈運輸 → 中間商 → 原料供應商 → CDMO。

每個環節都可能引入品質風險。製程地圖（process map）需要詳細到可以追溯每一個環節的責任方，才能在問題發生時快速定位根因。

OIE 動物疾病監控的實務應用

OIE（世界動物衛生組織）定期發布各國動物疾病報告。台灣 CDMO 採購豬或牛源性原料時，需定期檢視供應國的 OIE 報告，評估口蹄疫、非洲豬瘟、BSE 等疫情對供應鏈的影響。

若供應國爆發動物疫情，應觸發供應商審核和原料替代方案評估，而非等到原料測試失敗才行動。

比喻：過敏原每種萃取物 = 一種獨立活性成分

一瓶過敏原免疫治療藥水可能含有塵蟎、貓毛、花粉等多種成分。Annex 2B 要求每種成分的萃取物分開製備和管制，最後才混合。

這就像調雞尾酒——每種酒都要分開品質管制，最後才依配方混合。若其中一種萃取物不合格，只需捨棄那種，不影響其他成分。

屠宰場稽核 — CDMO 的挑戰

屠宰場不受 GMP 監管，但 Annex 2B 要求其控制措施提供「同等水準」的保證。CDMO 稽核屠宰場時的評估重點：

  • 動物衛生檢驗程序（獸醫師資格）
  • 動物識別和追溯系統
  • 採集過程的衛生管制
  • 冷鏈管理（從採集到出貨的溫度記錄）
  • 異常動物的隔離和處理程序

Part B — 免疫血清、疫苗與重組產品

B3. Animal Immunosera Products

B3.1. Particular care should be exercised on the control of antigens of biological origin to assure their quality, consistency and freedom from adventitious agents. Preparation of materials used to immunise source animals (antigens, hapten carriers, adjuvants, stabilising agents) and storage prior to immunisation should be in accordance with documented procedures.

B3.2. The immunisation, test bleed and harvest bleed schedules should conform to those approved in the CTA or MA.

B3.3. Manufacturing conditions for preparation of antibody sub-fragments (Fab or F(ab')₂) and any further modifications must be in accordance with validated and approved parameters.

B4. Vaccines

B4.1. Where eggs are used, the health status of all source flocks used in production of eggs (whether specified pathogen free or healthy flocks) should be assured.

B4.2. The integrity of containers used to store intermediate products and the hold times must be validated.

B4.3. Vessels containing inactivated products should not be opened or sampled in areas containing live biological agents.

B4.4. The sequence of addition of active ingredients, adjuvants and excipients during formulation must be in compliance with specifications.

B4.5. Where organisms with a higher biological safety level (e.g. pandemic vaccine strains) are to be used, appropriate containment arrangements must be in place. The approval of such arrangements should be obtained from the appropriate national authority(ies).

B5. Recombinant Products

B5.1. Process conditions during cell growth, protein expression and purification must be maintained within validated parameters to assure a consistent product with a defined range of impurities within the capability of the process to reduce to acceptable levels. The type of cell used may require increased measures to assure freedom from viruses. For production involving multiple harvests, the period of continuous cultivation should be within specified limits.

B5.2. The purification processes to remove unwanted host cell proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, viruses and other impurities should be within defined validated limits.

免疫血清、疫苗與重組產品解析

免疫血清製造：動物就是「反應器」

用馬製造抗毒血清的流程：將毒素（如蛇毒）注射入馬體 → 馬產生抗體 → 採集血液 → 純化抗體（可進一步製備 Fab 片段降低過敏風險）。

免疫時間表和採血時間表必須按照 CTA/MA 核准的方案執行——過早採血抗體效價不夠，過晚可能影響動物健康。

疫苗製造的關鍵隔離原則

活病毒 vs. 滅活病毒的嚴格隔離：裝有滅活疫苗的容器，絕對不能在仍有活性病原體的區域開啟取樣。

這一條看似基本，卻在現實操作中容易因為流程設計不當而出錯——例如，在病毒滅活後、換瓶分裝前，樣品需要在哪裡取樣？分析人員是否需要進入仍有活毒的區域？這些都需要在 SOP 中明確規定。

比喻：大流行疫苗菌株 = 特許危險品

製造 H5N1 禽流感疫苗時，廠商必須使用實際的高致病性病毒株。這就像為了研究核能安全而需要接觸放射性材料——需要特別許可、特殊設施和嚴格的安全程序。

主管機關的批准文件必須在廠內隨時可供查驗，稽查員會核對設施的生物安全等級是否與使用的病毒株相符。

重組蛋白純化：雜質去除的三個維度

  • 製程相關雜質：宿主細胞蛋白（HCP）、宿主細胞 DNA、殘留培養基成分
  • 產品相關雜質：聚集體、片段、錯誤折疊的蛋白質
  • 病毒安全性：清除/滅活病毒的步驟需要驗證（如低 pH 滅活 + 奈米過濾）

每個純化步驟都需要驗證其去除能力，並設定明確的接受標準。多次收穫（multiple harvests）需特別注意累積代數不超過規格限制。

重組蛋白 CDMO 的製程驗證重點

承接 mAb 或重組疫苗委託，製程驗證需涵蓋：

  • 細胞生長曲線和蛋白質表現量的批次一致性
  • 各純化步驟的 HCP/DNA 去除率（對數清除值）
  • 病毒安全性驗證（spike study）
  • 培養時間/代數的上限驗證
  • 多次收穫的批次間一致性

B6. Monoclonal Antibody Products

B6.1. Monoclonal antibodies may be manufactured from murine hybridomas, human hybridomas, or by recombinant DNA technology. Control measures appropriate to the different source cells (including feeder cells) and materials used to establish the hybridoma/cell line should be in place. Freedom from viruses should be given particular emphasis. Data from products generated by the same manufacturing technology platform may be acceptable to demonstrate suitability.

B6.2. Criteria to be monitored at the end of a production cycle and for early termination of production cycles should be verified to be within approved limits.

B6.3. Manufacturing conditions for preparation of antibody sub-fragments (Fab, F(ab')₂, scFv) and any further modifications (radio labelling, conjugation, chemical linking) must be in accordance with validated parameters.

B7. Transgenic Animal Products

Consistency of starting material from a transgenic source is likely to be more problematic than for non-transgenic biotechnology sources. Consequently, there is an increased requirement to demonstrate batch-to-batch consistency of product in all respects.

B7.1. A range of species may be used to produce biological medicinal products, which may be expressed into body fluids (e.g. milk) for collection and purification. Animals should be clearly and uniquely identified and backup identification arrangements should be in place.

B7.2. Housing and care arrangements should minimise the exposure of animals to pathogenic and zoonotic agents. Appropriate measures to protect the external environment should be established. A health-monitoring programme should be established and all results documented.

B7.3. The genealogy of the founder animals through to production animals must be documented. Materials from different transgenic lines should not be mixed.

B7.4. Harvest conditions should be in accordance with CTA or MA conditions. Harvest schedule and conditions under which animals may be removed from production should follow approved procedures and acceptance limits.

B8. Transgenic Plant Products

B8.1. Additional measures may be required to prevent contamination of master and working transgenic banks by extraneous plant materials and relevant adventitious agents. The stability of the gene within defined generation numbers should be monitored.

B8.2. Plants should be clearly and uniquely identified; the presence of key plant features, including health status, across the crop should be verified at defined intervals to assure consistency of yield between crops.

B8.3. Security arrangements should minimise exposure to microbiological contamination and cross-contamination with non-related plants. Measures should prevent pesticides and fertilisers from contaminating the product. A monitoring programme should be established.

B8.4. Conditions for plant removal from production should be defined. Acceptance limits should be set for materials (e.g. host proteins) that may interfere with the purification process.

B8.5. Environmental conditions (temperature, rain) which may affect quality attributes and yield from planting through cultivation to harvest and interim storage should be documented. Principles in documents such as Good Agricultural and Collection Practice guidelines should be taken into account.

單株抗體、基因轉殖動植物解析

mAb 製造平台：病毒安全性的三重保護

mAb 通常用中國倉鼠卵巢（CHO）細胞或融合瘤（hybridoma）細胞製造，這些細胞本身可能帶有內源性逆轉錄病毒（endogenous retroviruses）。

三重防護策略：

  • 原材料（培養基、血清）病毒測試
  • 製造過程的病毒去除步驟（低 pH、nanofiltration）
  • 最終產品的病毒檢測

「同一技術平台的數據可接受」（B6.1）意味著若某企業的 CHO 平台已建立完整病毒清除數據，新產品可部分借用，縮短開發時間。

比喻：基因轉殖山羊是「活體工廠」

想像一個表達人類凝血因子 IX 的基因轉殖山羊群——工廠的「生產設備」就是這些山羊，牠們的乳汁含有目標蛋白質。

挑戰：山羊是活生生的動物，不像不鏽鋼發酵槽可以精確控制環境。每隻山羊的遺傳背景（genealogy）必須從創始動物完整記錄，確保所有生產動物都來自同一轉殖株，不同轉殖株的乳汁不能混合。

基因轉殖植物製藥：開放田地的獨特挑戰

在開放田地種植基因轉殖產藥作物，面臨的風險包括：

  • 花粉飄散造成基因汙染鄰近農田
  • 農藥/肥料殘留
  • 天候影響（乾旱、颱風）導致批次間一致性差
  • 害蟲和微生物汙染

這些是 Annex 2B 要求必須監控和記錄的環境條件，同時參考 GACP（良好農業採集規範）指引。

ADC（抗體藥物結合物）的特殊考量

B6.3 提到的「進一步修飾」（conjugation、chemical linking）正是 ADC 的製造步驟：將細胞毒性小分子連接到 mAb 上。

這個步驟涉及高毒性化合物的操作，需要同時符合：mAb GMP（Annex 2B）+ 高毒性化合物操作安全（工業安全規定）+ 結合物驗證（連接比例、均一性）。台灣 CDMO 發展 ADC 能力時，需規劃整合性的廠房和 SOP 架構。

mAb 生產週期終止的決策標準

B6.2 提到「生產週期終止標準」——CDMO 應在與客戶的技術協議中明確定義：

  • 活細胞密度（VCD）下降到何值時終止
  • 細胞活力（Viability）低於多少時終止
  • 代謝副產品（乳酸、氨）超過何濃度時終止
  • 發現微生物汙染時的立即終止程序
  • 提前終止批次的文件和客戶通知要求
