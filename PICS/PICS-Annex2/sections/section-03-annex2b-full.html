SCOPE — 適用範圍

Scope

The methods employed in the manufacture of biological active substances and biological medicinal products for human use are a critical factor in shaping the appropriate regulatory control. Biological active substances and medicinal products can be defined largely by reference to their method of manufacture.

This annex provides guidance on the full range of active substances and medicinal products defined as biological with the exception of Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products (ATMPs). Manufacturers of ATMPs should refer to PIC/S Annex 2A.

This annex is divided into two main parts:

  • Part A — supplementary guidance on the manufacture of biological active substances and medicinal products, from control over seed lots and cell banks through to finishing activities and testing.
  • Part B — further guidance on selected types of biological active substances and medicinal products.

This annex, along with several other annexes of the PIC/S Guide to GMP, provides guidance which supplements that in Part I and in Part II of the Guide.

There are two aspects to the scope of this annex:

  • Stage of manufacture — for biological active substances to the point immediately prior to their being rendered sterile, the primary guidance source is Part II. Guidance for subsequent manufacturing steps of biological products is covered in Part I.
  • Type of product — this annex provides guidance on the full range of medicinal products defined as biological with the exception of ATMPs.

The level of GMP increases in detail from early to later steps in the manufacture of biological active substances but GMP principles should always be adhered to. The inclusion of some early steps of manufacture within the scope of this Annex does not imply that those steps will be routinely subject to inspection by the authorities.

Antibiotics are not defined as biological medicinal products, however where biological stages of manufacture occur, guidance in this Annex may be used.

Guidance for medicinal products derived from fractionated human blood or plasma is covered in Annex 14 and for non-transgenic plant products in Annex 7.

Additionally, the manufacture and control of genetically modified organisms needs to comply with local and national requirements. Appropriate containment should be established and maintained. Advice should be obtained according to national legislation to establish and maintain the appropriate Biological Safety Level.

範圍解析

Annex 2B vs. 2A — 兩者分工

Annex 2A：ATMP（先進治療醫療產品）— 基因療法、細胞療法、組織工程

Annex 2B：所有其他生物製品 — 疫苗、單株抗體、重組蛋白、免疫血清、過敏原等

台灣 CDMO 若承接 CAR-T 或基因療法委託，需適用 Annex 2A；承接傳統疫苗或 mAb 則適用本 Annex 2B。

比喻：GMP 是一條漸進的坡道

生物製程的早期步驟（如初始發酵培養）就像在坡道底部——GMP 原則要遵守，但細節要求較寬鬆，且主管機關通常不會主動查廠。

越靠近最終滅菌前的步驟，坡道越陡——環境管制、文件要求、測試頻率全面升高，對應到 Part I 的要求。

重要分界：滅菌前後

滅菌前的生物活性物質製造：主要參考 Part II（API GMP）

滅菌後的後續步驟：適用 Part I（製劑 GMP）

這個分界點在 CDMO 廠的廠房設計和系統建置上非常重要——需要清楚定義在哪個步驟之後，文件、環控、人員訓練要全面切換到更高標準。

基因改造生物體（GMO）的額外法規層

在台灣生物製程廠製造重組蛋白或基因工程疫苗時，除了 GMP 要求，還需符合生物安全等級（Biosafety Level）的規定，兩者不得衝突。

建立 GMO 設施時需同時評估：GMP 潔淨等級要求 vs. 生物安全封鎖需求，找到兩者都符合的設計方案。

早期步驟「不列入常規查廠」的含義

實務意義：即使早期發酵步驟在理論上受 Annex 2B 規範，稽查員通常聚焦在後端純化、填充和放行測試。但文件必須齊全，以備不時之需。

PRINCIPLE — 製造原則

Principle

The manufacture of biological active substances and medicinal products involves certain specific considerations arising from the nature of the products and the processes. Unlike conventional medicinal products manufactured using chemical and physical techniques capable of a high degree of consistency, the manufacture of biologicals involves biological processes and materials, such as cultivation of cells or extraction from living organisms.

These biological processes may display inherent variability, so that the range and nature of by-products may be variable. As a result, quality risk management (QRM) principles are particularly important for this class of materials and should be used to develop the control strategy across all stages of manufacture so as to minimise variability and to reduce the opportunity for contamination and cross-contamination.

Since materials and processing conditions used in cultivation processes are designed to provide conditions for the growth of specific cells and microorganisms, this provides extraneous microbial contaminants the opportunity to grow. In addition, some products may be limited in their ability to withstand a wide range of purification techniques particularly those designed to inactivate or remove adventitious viral contaminants.

Specifications related to products will dictate whether and to what stage substances and materials can have a defined level of bioburden or need to be sterile. Manufacturing must be consistent with other specifications set out in the CTA or MA (e.g. number of generations between the seed lot or cell bank).

Control usually involves biological analytical techniques, which typically have a greater variability than physico-chemical determinations. A robust manufacturing process is therefore crucial and in-process controls take on a particular importance.

Biological medicinal products which incorporate human tissues or cells must comply with national requirements for the coding, processing, preservation, storage and distribution of human tissues and cells.

Biological active substances and medicinal products must comply with the applicable national guidance on minimising the risk of transmitting animal spongiform encephalopathy (TSE) agents via human and veterinary medicinal products.

製造原則解析

為什麼生物製品比化學藥品「難管」？

化學藥品：合成反應遵循固定的化學方程式，批次間變異相對小，物化分析方法精確。

生物製品：活細胞本身就有自然變異，培養條件的微小差異（pH、溶氧、溫度）都可能影響蛋白質折疊、糖基化模式，導致批次間產品特性差異。

比喻：生物製程是「養活生物」不是「燒化學品」

化學合成就像按照食譜煮菜——原料加對、溫度控制好，成品基本一致。

細胞培養更像養一群挑剔的生物——環境稍有不對勁，它們就產生不一樣的代謝產物。這就是為什麼 QRM 在生物製程中特別重要：你需要主動識別、評估每個變異來源的風險。

生物製程的汙染雙重挑戰

挑戰一：培養基設計為促進目標細胞生長，同樣也為雜菌提供了溫床

挑戰二：部分產品無法承受強力去除病毒的純化步驟（如高溫、極端 pH），因此防護設計在前端更加重要

CDMO 對應策略：密閉系統設計 + 嚴格的環境監控 + 驗證的病毒去除步驟（如過濾、色層分析）。

In-Process Control 的特殊地位

化學藥品可以等最終產品出來再做一次全面測試；但生物製品的中間品往往無法長期保存，且某些產品的最終放行測試本身耗時長（如細菌培養）。

因此，在製程中每個關鍵步驟取樣測試（IPC），是確保生物藥品品質的核心手段，而非補救措施。

TSE 風險管控

使用動物源性原料（牛血清、胰蛋白酶等）時，需評估 TSE（海綿狀腦病，如狂牛病）傳播風險。

台灣 CDMO 採購這類原料時，需要供應商的 TSE 合規文件，並優先選用非動物源或重組來源替代品。

Part A — 人員與廠房設備

Part A: General Guidance — Personnel (Clauses 1–4)

1. Personnel employed in areas where biological active substances and products are manufactured and tested should receive training, and periodic retraining, specific to the products manufactured and to their work, including any specific security measures to protect product, personnel and the environment.

2. The health status of personnel should be taken into consideration for product safety. Where necessary, personnel engaged in production, maintenance, testing and animal care should be vaccinated with appropriate specific vaccines and have regular health checks.

3. Any changes in the health status of personnel, which could adversely affect the quality of the product, should preclude work in the production area and appropriate records kept. Production of BCG vaccine and tuberculin products should be restricted to staff who are carefully monitored by regular checks of immunological status or chest X-ray.

4. Restrictions on the movement of all personnel (including QC, maintenance and cleaning staff) should be controlled on the basis of QRM principles. In general, personnel should not pass from areas where exposure to live micro-organisms, genetically modified organisms, toxins or animals to areas where other products, inactivated products or different organisms are handled.

Premises and Equipment (Clauses 5–18)

5. The degree of environmental control of particulate and microbial contamination should be adapted to the active substance, intermediate or finished product and the production step. The environmental monitoring programme should be supplemented by methods to detect the presence of specific microorganisms (host organism, yeasts, moulds, anaerobes) where indicated by the QRM process.

6. Manufacturing and storage facilities, processes and environmental classifications should be designed to prevent extraneous contamination. Where processes are not closed and there is exposure of the product to the immediate room environment, QRM-based control measures should be put in place, including engineering and environmental controls. These principles should take into account guidance from Annex 1.

7. Dedicated production areas should be used for the handling of live cells. Dedicated production area should be used for the manufacture of pathogenic organisms (Biosafety Level 3 or 4).

8. Manufacture in a multi-product facility may be acceptable where considerations include: (a) knowledge of key characteristics of all cells/organisms/adventitious agents; (b) health status of donors for small batches; (c) live organisms prevented from entering non-related areas; (d) validated cleaning and decontamination; (e) environmental monitoring specific for the micro-organism; (f) products moved safely to prevent contamination; (g) campaign based manufacturing.

11. Air handling units should be designed to minimise cross-contamination. Consideration should be given to the use of single pass air systems.

12. Positive pressure areas for sterile products; negative pressure acceptable at point of exposure of pathogens. These pressure cascades should be clearly defined and continuously monitored with appropriate alarm settings.

15. Clean in place (CIP) and steam in place (SIP) systems should be used where possible. Valves on fermentation vessels should be completely steam sterilisable.

16. Air vent filters should be hydrophobic and validated for their scheduled life span with integrity testing at appropriate intervals.

17. Drainage systems must be designed so that effluents can be effectively neutralised or decontaminated.

人員與廠房解析

生物製程人員訓練的三個特殊面向

  • 產品特異性訓練：製造 BCG 疫苗的人員，需了解結核菌的感染風險與防護措施，與製造一般重組蛋白的人員訓練內容完全不同
  • 健康監控：如有免疫系統疾病或近期接受免疫抑制治療的員工，不能進入某些高風險區域
  • 動線管制：從活菌區走到滅活產品區，即使是維修人員也必須有明確的換裝或清潔程序

比喻：多產品廠就像共用廚房

在同一廚房做海鮮和做花生製品，都需要：嚴格的清潔驗證（證明沒有殘留），分開的器具或有序的使用時間（campaign 製造），以及清楚的標示和隔離。

生物製程的挑戰更大：活菌可能形成孢子、病毒可能在環境中存活——清潔驗證必須針對具體的微生物特性進行。

正壓 vs. 負壓的設計邏輯

正壓區（保護產品）：無菌填充區、關鍵製造區 → 防止外部汙染進入

負壓區（保護人員和環境）：病原體操作區 → 防止危險微生物外洩

CDMO 廠若同時有「無菌製造」和「病原體操作」需求，廠房設計必須仔細規劃壓差梯度，確保兩個目標都能達成且不衝突。

CIP/SIP 在發酵設備的重要性

CIP（就地清洗）：不拆管路，用清洗液循環清洗，驗證清洗效果（TOC、生物負載）

SIP（就地蒸氣滅菌）：發酵槽閥門需能承受蒸氣滅菌，確保滅菌後的密封性

空氣過濾器（疏水性 PTFE 材質）需定期做完整性測試（泡點或壓力持留測試），防止因過濾器失效導致整批發酵汙染。

多產品廠的 QRM 決策框架

台灣 CDMO 評估是否接受新產品（如在現有 mAb 廠添加腺病毒載體疫苗）時，需評估：

  • 病毒的致病性等級（Biosafety Level）
  • 現有清潔方法對腺病毒的殺滅效果是否已驗證
  • 是否需要獨立的 HVAC 系統
  • Campaign 安排與相鄰產品的交叉汙染風險
  • 環境監控方法是否需要增加腺病毒特異性偵測

Part A — 動物、文件與生產

Animals (Clauses 19–25)

19. Animal species used in manufacture fall into two categories: (a) live groups/herds/flocks (e.g. horses for immunosera, goats for transgenic products); (b) materials derived post-mortem from abattoirs (e.g. sheep/pigs for enzymes and hormones). Animals may also be used in QC assays (pyrogenicity, potency).

20. In addition to TSE regulations, adventitious agents (zoonotic diseases, diseases of source animals) should be monitored by an ongoing health programme. A look-back procedure should be in place to assess the suitability of materials from animals that have become ill. Withdrawal periods for therapeutic agents used to treat source/donor animals must be documented.

21. Particular care should be taken to prevent and monitor infections. Measures include sourcing, facilities, husbandry, biosecurity procedures, testing regimes, control of bedding and feed materials. This is of special relevance to specified pathogen free (SPF) animals.

22. For transgenic animals, traceability should be maintained from source animals through to production animals.

Documentation (Clauses 26–28)

26. Starting and raw materials may need additional documentation on source, origin, distribution chain, method of manufacture and controls applied to assure appropriate control of their microbiological quality.

27. Some product types may require specific definition of what constitutes a batch, particularly for cells.

28. Where human cell or tissue donors are used, full traceability is required through to confirmation of receipt at point of use. Traceability records must be retained for 30 years after the expiry date of the medicinal product, while maintaining donor confidentiality.

Production (Clauses 29–30)

29. Steps to increase process robustness, reducing process variability and enhancing reproducibility should be reassessed during Product Quality Reviews.

30. Particular attention should be paid to the control strategy to prevent or minimise the occurrence of unwanted bioburden and associated metabolites and endotoxins. For products from cells and tissues where production batches are frequently small, cross-contamination risks between cell preparations from different donors must be controlled.

動物、文件與生產解析

動物作為原料的特殊風險管理

使用活體動物（馬、羊）生產免疫血清時，動物本身就是原料來源。Annex 2B 要求的「回溯程序（look-back）」確保：若某匹馬後來被確認患有某種疾病，之前採集的血清批次都能追溯到並評估風險。

SPF 動物（無特定病原體）需要符合藥典專論要求，標準更高——相當於「有出生證明的有機動物」。

人類組織供體：30 年追溯義務

這是生物製品中最嚴格的追溯要求之一。使用人類組織或細胞製造的生物製品，從供體到最終患者用藥的完整紀錄，必須保存至產品有效期後 30 年。

實務上，CDMO 必須與客戶（MAH）明確在技術協議中規定：誰負責保管哪段追溯記錄，且如何在轉廠或公司易手時確保記錄不遺失。

比喻：細胞製品的「批次」定義難題

一般固體製劑的「批次」很直觀——同一鍋混合的片劑。

但細胞製品呢？來自不同供體的細胞能混在一起算一批嗎？通常不行（尤其是異體細胞治療）——每個供體的細胞可能就是一個獨立的「批次」。

Annex 2B 要求事先定義清楚，而不是等查廠時才解釋。

控制策略涵蓋內毒素和生物負載

培養基（溫暖、富含養分）特別容易孳生細菌並釋放內毒素（LPS）。即使細菌被後續步驟殺死，內毒素仍可能殘留在產品中，引發患者的發熱反應。

CDMO 的控制策略需包含：培養基除菌過濾、原料內毒素限度測試、製程中定期的生物負載監控，以及最終產品的內毒素測試。

CDMO 實務：小批次細胞產品的交叉汙染防護

承接多個病患的同種異體細胞製品時，同一天可能有多個供體來源的細胞同時在廠內處理。需要的措施：

  • 嚴格的隔離操作（不同隔離器或生物安全操作台）
  • 清晰的標示和確認程序（兩人確認制）
  • 不同供體的細胞在不同時間段操作（時間隔離）
  • 每個供體批次的完整鏈追溯記錄

Part A — 起始原料與原材料

Starting and Raw Materials (Clauses 31–38)

31. The source, origin and suitability of biological starting and raw materials (e.g. cryoprotectants, feeder cells, reagents, culture media, buffers, serum, enzymes, cytokines, growth factors) should be clearly defined. Where tests take a long time, it may be permissible to process starting materials before results are available, but the risk should be assessed under QRM principles. Release of finished product is conditional on satisfactory results.

32. The risk of contamination during passage along the supply chain must be assessed, with particular emphasis on TSE. Materials that come into direct contact with manufacturing equipment or the product (media used in media fill experiments, lubricants) must also be taken into account.

33. The preparation of solutions, buffers and other additions should be based on the principles and guidance contained in the appropriate sections of Annex 1. Controls for quality of starting/raw materials and aseptic manufacturing assume greater importance particularly for products where final sterilisation is not possible.

34. Where sterilisation of starting and raw materials is required, it should be carried out where possible by heat. Where necessary, other methods may also be used (irradiation, filtration).

35. Reduction in bioburden associated with procurement of living tissues and cells may require the use of antibiotics at early manufacturing stages. This should be avoided, but where necessary their use should be justified and they should be removed from the manufacturing process at the stage specified in the CTA or MA.

36. The donation, procurement and testing of human tissues and cells used as starting materials should be in accordance with national law. Traceability must be maintained from the donor to the batch of finished medicinal product. Requirements include:

  • (a) Supply sites must hold appropriate approvals from national competent authorities.
  • (b) Imported human cells/tissues must meet equivalent national standards; traceability and adverse reaction notification requirements apply.
  • (c) Processing of cells/tissues may be conducted at tissue establishments.
  • (d) Tissues and cells released by the Responsible Person (RP) in tissue establishment before shipment; test results must be available to manufacturer.
  • (e) Transport must be controlled by a written agreement; documentary evidence of storage/transport conditions required.
  • (f) Traceability from tissue establishments through to recipients must be maintained.
  • (g) A technical agreement should be in place between all responsible parties.

38. Where human or animal cells are used as feeder cells, appropriate controls over sourcing, testing, transport and storage should be in place.

起始原料解析

「先用後驗」（Use Before Test）的 QRM 評估

某些測試（如無菌測試需 14 天）的等待時間，若等待結果才投料，會大幅延誤生產。Annex 2B 允許在測試結果尚未出來時先投入生產，但：

  • 必須事先以 QRM 評估風險（若測試失敗，影響哪些批次？）
  • 最終放行以測試結果為條件（conditional release）
  • 必須有隔離和追溯機制，確保不合格原料的批次能被完整拉回

無法最終滅菌產品的原料管制加倍嚴格

對於不能最終滅菌的生物製品（大多數蛋白質藥物、活菌疫苗），原料的品質就是最終產品安全性的第一道防線。

Annex 1（無菌製造）的原則同樣適用於緩衝液、培養基的配製，即使在發酵階段——這代表無菌過濾、環境監控、潔淨度要求都必須提前介入。

比喻：抗生素使用是「最後手段」

在活組織採集過程中，有時難以完全避免細菌汙染，所以允許在早期步驟使用抗生素降低生物負載。

但抗生素必須在指定步驟「洗掉」——就像廚師可以用消毒劑清洗食材，但不能讓消毒劑殘留在端上桌的菜裡。若患者對青黴素過敏，殘留的抗生素可能引發嚴重反應。

人類組織採集：三重責任分工

組織庫（Tissue Establishment）：負責供體評估、採集、初步測試，Responsible Person 批准出貨

製造商（CDMO）：接收後適用完整 GMP，繼續追溯記錄

技術協議：明確界定每個環節的責任，包括出現問題時的通報義務

若組織庫在出貨後才收到供體健康資訊（如後續確診感染），必須立即通知製造商評估影響。

CDMO 供應鏈稽核重點

承接人類組織製品委託時，對組織庫的稽核應涵蓋：

  • 主管機關批准文件（Tissue Establishment 認可）
  • Responsible Person 的資格與職責
  • 供體篩選與測試的 SOP
  • 冷鏈運輸驗證記錄
  • 追溯系統的完整性（從供體到批號的連結）
  • 不良反應通報程序

Part A — 種子批次與細胞庫系統

Seed Lot and Cell Bank System (Clauses 39–47)

39. To prevent the unwanted drift of properties which might ensue from repeated subcultures or multiple generations, production of biological medicinal substances and products obtained by microbial culture, cell culture or propagation in embryos and animals should be based on a system of master and working virus seed lots and/or cell banks.

40. The number of generations (doublings, passages) between the seed lot or cell bank, the biological active substance and the finished product should be consistent with specifications in the CTA or MA.

41. Establishment of seed lots and cell banks, including master and working generations, should be performed under appropriate GMP conditions. This should include an appropriately controlled environment to protect the seed lot/cell bank and the personnel handling it. During establishment, no other living or infectious material should be handled simultaneously in the same area or by the same persons. For all stages prior to the establishment of the master seed or cell bank generation, principles of GMP may be applied. Documentation should be available to support traceability for all pre-master bank stages.

42. Following establishment, quarantine and release procedures should be followed. This should include adequate characterisation and testing for contaminants. On-going suitability should be demonstrated by consistency of characteristics and quality of successive product batches. Evidence of stability and recovery should be documented.

43. Seed lots and cell banks should be stored and used to minimise risks of contamination or alteration (e.g. stored in the vapour phase of liquid nitrogen in sealed containers). Storage of different seeds/cells in the same area must prevent mix-up and take into account infectious nature of materials.

45. Storage containers should be sealed, clearly labelled and kept at appropriate temperature. A stock inventory must be kept. Storage temperature should be recorded continuously; liquid nitrogen level monitored. Deviations and corrective actions must be recorded.

46. It is desirable to split stocks and store at different locations to minimise risk of total loss. Controls at such locations should provide the same assurances.

47. Storage and handling conditions should be managed according to the same procedures. Once containers are removed from the seed lot/cell bank management system, containers should not be returned to stock.

種子批次與細胞庫解析

雙層庫存系統的設計邏輯

Master Cell Bank (MCB) / Master Seed Lot (MSL)
主細胞庫 / 主種子批次
完整 GMP 建立、最嚴格管制、不輕易動用
↓ 衍生
Working Cell Bank (WCB) / Working Seed Lot (WSL)
工作細胞庫 / 工作種子批次

↓ 培養至指定代數
Production Culture → 批次生產
代數需符合 CTA/MA 規格

MCB 建立前 vs. 建立後：GMP 要求的分水嶺

MCB 建立前（研發早期克隆篩選）：可套用 GMP 原則（principles），不強制完整 GMP。但文件仍需完整，以支持後續追溯。

MCB 建立後：必須在完整 GMP 條件下操作——環境管制、文件、偏差管理一樣都不能少。

這個分水嶺在 CDMO 承接新分子委託時非常重要——客戶轉移 MCB 時，需確認原建立條件是否符合要求。

比喻：MCB 是「種源」，WCB 是「種苗」

農業比喻：MCB 是種源中心保管的珍貴原種，WCB 是農民每季從種源中心取出的種苗。每季都從種源補充種苗，確保農作物的遺傳穩定性，不會因為多代繁殖而退化。

生物製程中「代數限制」（passages limit）就是確保細胞不會隨著繁殖代數增加而產生不可控的基因漂移。

液態氮氣相儲存：為什麼是氣相而非液相？

傳統方法將樣品直接浸入液態氮（液相儲存），但這可能造成交叉汙染——若某個安瓿有細微裂縫，液態氮會滲入，取出復溫時可能洩出並汙染其他樣品。

氣相儲存（vapour phase）讓樣品懸浮在氮氣蒸氣中而非直接接觸液氮，降低交叉汙染風險。溫度約 -150°C 至 -190°C，足以長期保存生物活性。

備份分存（Split Stock）：災難防護的必要措施

MCB 只有一份保存在同一地點，若發生火災、停電、液氮外洩，整條產品線就此消失。

Annex 2B 建議分存在不同地點——理想情況是不同城市甚至不同國家的合格儲存設施。對 CDMO 而言，這是風險管理的基本動作。

MCB/WCB 管理查核要點

  • 庫存清單是否持續更新（包含每次使用紀錄）
  • 溫度監控是否連續且有警報設置
  • 液氮液位是否定期監控
  • 偏差（如停電事件）是否有完整調查和 CAPA
  • 取出安瓿後是否有「不退還」政策的執行記錄
  • 備份分存設施的管制標準是否等同主庫

Part A — 操作原則與品質管制

Operating Principles (Clauses 48–65)

48. Change management should, on a periodic basis, take into account cumulative effects of changes on quality, safety and efficacy of the finished product.

49. Critical operational parameters, or other input parameters which affect product quality, need to be identified, validated, documented and shown to be maintained within requirements.

50. A control strategy for the entry of articles and materials into production areas should be based on QRM principles. For aseptic processes, heat stable articles should enter through a double-ended autoclave or oven. Heat labile articles should enter through an air lock with interlocked doors and effective surface sanitisation.

51. The growth promoting properties of culture media should be demonstrated suitable for its intended use. If possible, media should be sterilized in situ. In-line sterilizing filters for routine addition of gases, media, acids, alkalis, anti-foaming agents to fermenters should be used where possible.

52. Addition of materials or cultures to fermenters and other vessels, and sampling, should be carried out under carefully controlled conditions to prevent contamination. Correct connection of vessels should be verified.

53. Continuous monitoring of some production processes (e.g. fermentation) may be necessary; such data should form part of the batch record. Where continuous culture is used, special consideration should be given to QC requirements.

54. Centrifugation and blending of products can lead to aerosol formation; containment of such activities to minimise cross-contamination is necessary.

55. Accidental spillages, especially of live organisms, must be dealt with quickly and safely. Qualified decontamination measures should be available for each organism or groups of related organisms.

57. In cases where a virus inactivation or removal process is performed during manufacture, measures should be taken to avoid the risk of recontamination of treated products by non-treated products.

58. For products inactivated by addition of a reagent (e.g. micro-organisms during vaccine manufacture), the process should ensure complete inactivation of live organism. In addition to thorough mixing of culture and inactivant, consideration should be given to contact of all product-contact surfaces and, where required, transfer to a second vessel.

59. For chromatography equipment and matrices, QRM principles should be used to devise the control strategy when used in campaign manufacture and in multi-product environments. Re-use of the same matrix at different stages of processing is discouraged. Acceptance criteria, operating conditions, regeneration methods, life span and sanitization/sterilisation methods of columns should be defined.

61. There should be a system to assure the integrity and closure of containers after filling where final products or intermediates represent a special risk. Filling and packaging operations need procedures to maintain the product within specified limits (e.g. time and/or temperature).

62. Activities in handling vials containing live biological agents must prevent contamination of other products or egress of live agents into the work or external environment.

63. Care should be taken in preparation, printing, storage and application of labels. For autologous products, the unique patient identifier and "for autologous use only" should be indicated on the outer packaging.

64. The compatibility of labels with ultra-low storage temperatures should be verified where such temperatures are used.

65. Where donor information becomes available after procurement which affects product quality, it should be taken into account in recall procedures.

Quality Control (Clauses 66–70)

66. In-process controls have greater importance in ensuring the consistency of biological active substances and medicinal products than for conventional products. In-process control testing should be performed at appropriate stages of production.

67. Where intermediates can be stored for extended periods, consideration should be given to inclusion of batches made from materials held for their maximum in-process periods in the on-going stability programme.

69. For cellular products, sterility tests should be conducted on antibiotic-free cultures of cells or cell banks to provide evidence for absence of bacterial and fungal contamination.

70. For biological medicinal products with a short shelf life (period not permitting release when sterility testing results are provided after 14 days or less), a suitable control strategy must be in place. Such controls need enhanced understanding of product and process performance. The procedure for batch certification and release may be carried out in two stages:

  • (a) Assessment by designated person(s) of batch processing records and environmental monitoring results for initial certification by the Responsible Person.
  • (b) Assessment of final analytical tests for final certification by the Authorised Person. Procedures for out-of-specification results, including liaison with clinical staff, must be in place.

操作原則與品質管制解析

累積變更影響評估

生物製程的「累積效應」問題比化學製藥更顯著：單一變更（如更換培養基供應商）可能影響不大，但同期進行的多個微小變更疊加起來，可能對產品品質產生顯著影響。

Annex 2B 要求在 PQR（產品品質審查）中定期評估累積變更——這不只是合規要求，也是維護生物製程穩定性的好工具。

發酵製程的連續監控

不同於固體製劑批次，發酵過程是動態的——pH、溫度、溶氧、糖濃度等參數隨時間變化。連續監控這些參數並將數據納入批次紀錄，是追溯製程異常的重要手段。

現代的 Process Analytical Technology（PAT）和 SCADA 系統可自動記錄這些數據，大幅減少人工抄錄錯誤。

比喻：病毒滅活的「兩室隔離」

就像急診室處置完傳染病患者後，需要徹底消毒才能接待下一位病患。病毒滅活步驟完成後，必須確保滅活後的產品不會接觸任何尚未處理的活性材料。

「轉移到第二個容器」的做法，就是物理隔離滅活前後的材料——產品接觸面的殘留活性病毒風險，靠空間隔離徹底消除。

短效期產品：兩階段放行（Two-Stage Release）

某些生物製品（如細胞治療、放射性藥品）的保存期限只有數天，傳統的先完成所有測試再放行根本行不通。

解決方案：兩階段放行——

  • 第一階段（Responsible Person）：評估製程數據、環境監控結果，初步認可
  • 第二階段（Authorised Person）：最終測試完成後，補充完整放行

前提是整個製程必須有高度的參數理解和驗證，才能支撐「提前放行」的信心。

色層分析管柱：多產品廠的關鍵管制點

在多產品 CDMO 廠，蛋白質 A 管柱（Protein A column）用於多個 mAb 產品的純化是常見安排。需要管制的要素：

  • 每個管柱的使用壽命（循環次數）和再生方法
  • 不同產品間的清潔驗證（是否允許跨產品使用？）
  • 管柱效能衰退的接受標準（如板高、不對稱因子）
  • 廢棄管柱的處理程序

Annex 2B 明確不鼓勵在不同純化步驟間重複使用同一管柱——每個步驟應有專屬管柱。

自體治療產品的標示要求

「For autologous use only（僅供自體使用）」不只是文字規定——這條標示是防止悲劇的最後防線。

自體細胞治療中，患者 A 的細胞若誤用於患者 B，不僅無效，還可能引發免疫排斥。標示、標籤相容性（超低溫下不脫落）、雙人確認程序缺一不可。

Part B — 動物源性產品與過敏原

B1. Animal Sourced Products

Since the supply chains can be extensive and complex, controls based on QRM principles need to be applied. Documentation to demonstrate supply chain traceability and clear roles of participants, typically including a sufficiently detailed process map, should be in place.

B1.1. Monitoring programmes should be in place for animal disease of concern to human health. Organisations should take into account reports from trustworthy sources (e.g. World Organisation for Animal Health, OIE) on national disease prevalence. This should be supplemented by health monitoring at national and local levels, including source farms/feedlots and controls during transport to abattoirs.

B1.2. Where abattoirs are used, they should operate to stringent standards. Account should be taken of reports from national regulatory organisations verifying compliance with food safety, veterinary and plant health legislation.

B1.3. Control measures for starting or raw materials at abattoirs should include appropriate elements of a Quality Management System to assure operator training, materials traceability, control and consistency. These measures may be drawn from sources outside PIC/S GMP but should provide equivalent levels of control.

B1.4. Controls should prevent interventions affecting material quality, or at least provide evidence of such activities, during progression through the manufacturing and supply chain, including movement between collection, purification, storage sites, hubs, consolidators and brokers. Breaches must be recorded, investigated, and actions taken.

B1.5. Regular audits of starting/raw material suppliers should verify compliance. Issues must be investigated appropriately. Systems must ensure effective corrective and preventive actions.

B2. Allergen Products

Materials may be manufactured by extraction from natural sources or by recombinant DNA technology.

B2.1. Source materials should be described in sufficient detail to ensure consistency in supply (common and scientific name, origin, nature, contaminant limits, collection method). Those derived from animals should be from healthy sources. Appropriate biosecurity controls for colonies (e.g. mites, animals) used for allergen extraction. Allergen products should be stored under defined conditions to minimise deterioration.

B2.2. Production process steps including pre-treatment, extraction, filtration, dialysis, concentration or freeze-drying should be described in detail and validated.

B2.3. The modification processes to manufacture modified allergen extracts (allergoids, conjugates) should be described. Intermediates should be identified and controlled.

B2.4. Allergen extract mixtures should be prepared from individual extracts from single source materials. Each individual extract should be considered as one active substance.

動物源性產品與過敏原解析

動物源性原料的「延伸供應鏈」管理

豬胰蛋白酶的供應鏈可能是：農場 → 屠宰場 → 初步處理商 → 冷鏈運輸 → 中間商 → 原料供應商 → CDMO。

每個環節都可能引入品質風險。製程地圖（process map）需要詳細到可以追溯每一個環節的責任方，才能在問題發生時快速定位根因。

OIE 動物疾病監控的實務應用

OIE（世界動物衛生組織）定期發布各國動物疾病報告。台灣 CDMO 採購豬或牛源性原料時，需定期檢視供應國的 OIE 報告，評估口蹄疫、非洲豬瘟、BSE 等疫情對供應鏈的影響。

若供應國爆發動物疫情，應觸發供應商審核和原料替代方案評估，而非等到原料測試失敗才行動。

比喻：過敏原每種萃取物 = 一種獨立活性成分

一瓶過敏原免疫治療藥水可能含有塵蟎、貓毛、花粉等多種成分。Annex 2B 要求每種成分的萃取物分開製備和管制，最後才混合。

這就像調雞尾酒——每種酒都要分開品質管制，最後才依配方混合。若其中一種萃取物不合格，只需捨棄那種，不影響其他成分。

屠宰場稽核 — CDMO 的挑戰

屠宰場不受 GMP 監管，但 Annex 2B 要求其控制措施提供「同等水準」的保證。CDMO 稽核屠宰場時的評估重點：

  • 動物衛生檢驗程序（獸醫師資格）
  • 動物識別和追溯系統
  • 採集過程的衛生管制
  • 冷鏈管理（從採集到出貨的溫度記錄）
  • 異常動物的隔離和處理程序

Part B — 免疫血清、疫苗與重組產品

B3. Animal Immunosera Products

B3.1. Particular care should be exercised on the control of antigens of biological origin to assure their quality, consistency and freedom from adventitious agents. Preparation of materials used to immunise source animals (antigens, hapten carriers, adjuvants, stabilising agents) and storage prior to immunisation should be in accordance with documented procedures.

B3.2. The immunisation, test bleed and harvest bleed schedules should conform to those approved in the CTA or MA.

B3.3. Manufacturing conditions for preparation of antibody sub-fragments (Fab or F(ab')₂) and any further modifications must be in accordance with validated and approved parameters.

B4. Vaccines

B4.1. Where eggs are used, the health status of all source flocks used in production of eggs (whether specified pathogen free or healthy flocks) should be assured.

B4.2. The integrity of containers used to store intermediate products and the hold times must be validated.

B4.3. Vessels containing inactivated products should not be opened or sampled in areas containing live biological agents.

B4.4. The sequence of addition of active ingredients, adjuvants and excipients during formulation must be in compliance with specifications.

B4.5. Where organisms with a higher biological safety level (e.g. pandemic vaccine strains) are to be used, appropriate containment arrangements must be in place. The approval of such arrangements should be obtained from the appropriate national authority(ies).

B5. Recombinant Products

B5.1. Process conditions during cell growth, protein expression and purification must be maintained within validated parameters to assure a consistent product with a defined range of impurities within the capability of the process to reduce to acceptable levels. The type of cell used may require increased measures to assure freedom from viruses. For production involving multiple harvests, the period of continuous cultivation should be within specified limits.

B5.2. The purification processes to remove unwanted host cell proteins, nucleic acids, carbohydrates, viruses and other impurities should be within defined validated limits.

免疫血清、疫苗與重組產品解析

免疫血清製造：動物就是「反應器」

用馬製造抗毒血清的流程：將毒素（如蛇毒）注射入馬體 → 馬產生抗體 → 採集血液 → 純化抗體（可進一步製備 Fab 片段降低過敏風險）。

免疫時間表和採血時間表必須按照 CTA/MA 核准的方案執行——過早採血抗體效價不夠，過晚可能影響動物健康。

疫苗製造的關鍵隔離原則

活病毒 vs. 滅活病毒的嚴格隔離：裝有滅活疫苗的容器，絕對不能在仍有活性病原體的區域開啟取樣。

這一條看似基本，卻在現實操作中容易因為流程設計不當而出錯——例如，在病毒滅活後、換瓶分裝前，樣品需要在哪裡取樣？分析人員是否需要進入仍有活毒的區域？這些都需要在 SOP 中明確規定。

比喻：大流行疫苗菌株 = 特許危險品

製造 H5N1 禽流感疫苗時，廠商必須使用實際的高致病性病毒株。這就像為了研究核能安全而需要接觸放射性材料——需要特別許可、特殊設施和嚴格的安全程序。

主管機關的批准文件必須在廠內隨時可供查驗，稽查員會核對設施的生物安全等級是否與使用的病毒株相符。

重組蛋白純化：雜質去除的三個維度

  • 製程相關雜質：宿主細胞蛋白（HCP）、宿主細胞 DNA、殘留培養基成分
  • 產品相關雜質：聚集體、片段、錯誤折疊的蛋白質
  • 病毒安全性：清除/滅活病毒的步驟需要驗證（如低 pH 滅活 + 奈米過濾）

每個純化步驟都需要驗證其去除能力，並設定明確的接受標準。多次收穫（multiple harvests）需特別注意累積代數不超過規格限制。

重組蛋白 CDMO 的製程驗證重點

承接 mAb 或重組疫苗委託，製程驗證需涵蓋：

  • 細胞生長曲線和蛋白質表現量的批次一致性
  • 各純化步驟的 HCP/DNA 去除率（對數清除值）
  • 病毒安全性驗證（spike study）
  • 培養時間/代數的上限驗證
  • 多次收穫的批次間一致性

Part B — 單株抗體、基因轉殖動物與植物

B6. Monoclonal Antibody Products

B6.1. Monoclonal antibodies may be manufactured from murine hybridomas, human hybridomas, or by recombinant DNA technology. Control measures appropriate to the different source cells (including feeder cells) and materials used to establish the hybridoma/cell line should be in place. Freedom from viruses should be given particular emphasis. Data from products generated by the same manufacturing technology platform may be acceptable to demonstrate suitability.

B6.2. Criteria to be monitored at the end of a production cycle and for early termination of production cycles should be verified to be within approved limits.

B6.3. Manufacturing conditions for preparation of antibody sub-fragments (Fab, F(ab')₂, scFv) and any further modifications (radio labelling, conjugation, chemical linking) must be in accordance with validated parameters.

B7. Transgenic Animal Products

Consistency of starting material from a transgenic source is likely to be more problematic than for non-transgenic biotechnology sources. Consequently, there is an increased requirement to demonstrate batch-to-batch consistency of product in all respects.

B7.1. A range of species may be used to produce biological medicinal products, which may be expressed into body fluids (e.g. milk) for collection and purification. Animals should be clearly and uniquely identified and backup identification arrangements should be in place.

B7.2. Housing and care arrangements should minimise the exposure of animals to pathogenic and zoonotic agents. Appropriate measures to protect the external environment should be established. A health-monitoring programme should be established and all results documented.

B7.3. The genealogy of the founder animals through to production animals must be documented. Materials from different transgenic lines should not be mixed.

B7.4. Harvest conditions should be in accordance with CTA or MA conditions. Harvest schedule and conditions under which animals may be removed from production should follow approved procedures and acceptance limits.

B8. Transgenic Plant Products

B8.1. Additional measures may be required to prevent contamination of master and working transgenic banks by extraneous plant materials and relevant adventitious agents. The stability of the gene within defined generation numbers should be monitored.

B8.2. Plants should be clearly and uniquely identified; the presence of key plant features, including health status, across the crop should be verified at defined intervals to assure consistency of yield between crops.

B8.3. Security arrangements should minimise exposure to microbiological contamination and cross-contamination with non-related plants. Measures should prevent pesticides and fertilisers from contaminating the product. A monitoring programme should be established.

B8.4. Conditions for plant removal from production should be defined. Acceptance limits should be set for materials (e.g. host proteins) that may interfere with the purification process.

B8.5. Environmental conditions (temperature, rain) which may affect quality attributes and yield from planting through cultivation to harvest and interim storage should be documented. Principles in documents such as Good Agricultural and Collection Practice guidelines should be taken into account.

單株抗體、基因轉殖動植物解析

mAb 製造平台：病毒安全性的三重保護

mAb 通常用中國倉鼠卵巢（CHO）細胞或融合瘤（hybridoma）細胞製造，這些細胞本身可能帶有內源性逆轉錄病毒（endogenous retroviruses）。

三重防護策略：

  • 原材料（培養基、血清）病毒測試
  • 製造過程的病毒去除步驟（低 pH、nanofiltration）
  • 最終產品的病毒檢測

「同一技術平台的數據可接受」（B6.1）意味著若某企業的 CHO 平台已建立完整病毒清除數據，新產品可部分借用，縮短開發時間。

比喻：基因轉殖山羊是「活體工廠」

想像一個表達人類凝血因子 IX 的基因轉殖山羊群——工廠的「生產設備」就是這些山羊，牠們的乳汁含有目標蛋白質。

挑戰：山羊是活生生的動物，不像不鏽鋼發酵槽可以精確控制環境。每隻山羊的遺傳背景（genealogy）必須從創始動物完整記錄，確保所有生產動物都來自同一轉殖株，不同轉殖株的乳汁不能混合。

基因轉殖植物製藥：開放田地的獨特挑戰

在開放田地種植基因轉殖產藥作物，面臨的風險包括：

  • 花粉飄散造成基因汙染鄰近農田
  • 農藥/肥料殘留
  • 天候影響（乾旱、颱風）導致批次間一致性差
  • 害蟲和微生物汙染

這些是 Annex 2B 要求必須監控和記錄的環境條件，同時參考 GACP（良好農業採集規範）指引。

ADC（抗體藥物結合物）的特殊考量

B6.3 提到的「進一步修飾」（conjugation、chemical linking）正是 ADC 的製造步驟：將細胞毒性小分子連接到 mAb 上。

這個步驟涉及高毒性化合物的操作，需要同時符合：mAb GMP（Annex 2B）+ 高毒性化合物操作安全（工業安全規定）+ 結合物驗證（連接比例、均一性）。台灣 CDMO 發展 ADC 能力時，需規劃整合性的廠房和 SOP 架構。

mAb 生產週期終止的決策標準

B6.2 提到「生產週期終止標準」——CDMO 應在與客戶的技術協議中明確定義：

  • 活細胞密度（VCD）下降到何值時終止
  • 細胞活力（Viability）低於多少時終止
  • 代謝副產品（乳酸、氨）超過何濃度時終止
  • 發現微生物汙染時的立即終止程序
  • 提前終止批次的文件和客戶通知要求

本節學習重點回顧

  • 範圍判斷：Annex 2B 涵蓋所有非 ATMP 生物製品；Table 1 的七大產品類型幫助你定位在哪個製造步驟需要完整 GMP vs. 僅套用 GMP 原則——滅菌前以 Part II 為主，滅菌後適用 Part I。
  • MCB/WCB 雙層系統：MCB 在完整 GMP 下建立，是產品生命週期的生物起點；代數限制需符合 CTA/MA；備份分存在不同地點是災難防護的基本措施；取出的安瓿不得退還。
  • 製造操作核心：連續監控發酵數據入批次紀錄；病毒滅活前後嚴格隔離；色層分析管柱需有使用壽命和接受標準；短效期產品需兩階段放行策略。
  • Part B 特定產品：疫苗的活毒/滅活毒嚴格分區；mAb 需強調病毒安全性平台數據；基因轉殖動植物面臨批次間一致性挑戰，需完整世代追溯；ADC 修飾步驟需整合毒性安全管制。
