4.1–4.4 潔淨室基礎與等級概覽 General Premises & Grade Overview

原文內容 Original Text

4.1 The manufacture of sterile products should be carried out in appropriate cleanrooms, entry to which should be through change rooms that act as airlocks for personnel and airlocks for equipment and materials. Cleanrooms and change rooms should be maintained to an appropriate cleanliness standard and supplied with air which has passed through filters of an appropriate efficiency. Controls and monitoring should be scientifically justified and should effectively evaluate the state of environmental conditions of cleanrooms, airlocks and pass-through hatches.

4.2 The various operations of component preparation, product preparation and filling should be carried out with appropriate technical and operational separation measures within the cleanroom or facility to prevent mix up and contamination.

4.3 Restricted Access Barrier Systems (RABS) or isolators are beneficial in assuring required conditions and minimizing microbial contamination associated with direct human interventions in the critical zone. Their use should be considered in the CCS. Any alternative approaches to the use of RABS or isolators should be justified.

4.4 For the manufacture of sterile products there are four grades of cleanroom/zone:

Grade A The critical zone for high-risk operations (e.g. aseptic processing line, filling zone, stopper bowl, open primary packaging or for making aseptic connections under the protection of first air). Normally, such conditions are provided by a localised airflow protection, such as unidirectional airflow workstations within RABS or isolators. The maintenance of unidirectional airflow should be demonstrated and qualified across the whole of the grade A area. Direct intervention into the grade A area by operators should be minimized by premises, equipment, process and procedural design.
Grade B For aseptic preparation and filling, this is the background cleanroom for grade A (where it is not an isolator). Air pressure differences should be continuously monitored. Cleanrooms of lower grade than grade B can be considered where isolator technology is used (see paragraph 4.20).
Grade C and D These are cleanrooms used for carrying out less critical stages in the manufacture of aseptically filled sterile products or as a background for isolators. They can also be used for the preparation/filling of terminally sterilised products.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 四個潔淨室等級

Grade A（A 級）：最高等級，直接接觸產品的關鍵區域。充填區、分裝區、暴露的一次包材均屬此類。必須維持單向氣流（UDAF）並使用 RABS 或 Isolator 提供屏障保護。

Grade B（B 級）：A 級的背景環境（非 Isolator 情況下）。是 A 級潔淨室周圍的支援環境。壓差需連續監控。

Grade C（C 級）：次要無菌操作的支援環境，例如配藥室、溶液過濾前製備。也可作為 Closed Isolator 的背景環境。

Grade D（D 級）：最低分級，用於初步準備工作或終端滅菌產品的充填背景。也可作為 Closed Isolator 的最低背景要求。

比喻說明 手術室層層防護

想像一間頂級手術室的設計：

  • A 級 = 手術刀接觸傷口的無菌區域（最嚴格，完全隔絕）
  • B 級 = 手術室本體（手術人員穿無菌衣的空間）
  • C 級 = 刷手間、前室（準備工作在此完成）
  • D 級 = 手術走廊（支援物料在此暫存）

每進入一層，都需要換裝、消毒、通過氣閘——這正是 Annex 1 的設計邏輯。

重點提示 RABS vs Isolator 的策略選擇

4.3 條款的重要性在於：RABS 或 Isolator 的使用已不再只是選項，而是 CCS 中必須考慮的技術手段。 若選擇不使用，必須在 CCS 中明確說明替代方案及合理性。

2022 版 Annex 1 的精神是「以工程控制取代人為控制」，RABS 和 Isolator 正是這一理念的體現。任何能減少人員直接干預 Grade A 區域的技術，都是監管機構所鼓勵的方向。

實務應用 CDMO 廠房規劃策略

對 CDMO 而言，廠房等級設計直接影響客戶品項的可接受性。當接受高風險生物製品（如活病毒疫苗）時，可能需要負壓 Isolator 來同時保護產品與操作人員。選擇 Isolator 而非 RABS 的決策，雖然前期投資較高，但可將背景環境從 Grade B 降至 Grade D（甚至 Grade C），大幅節省 HVAC 系統的運作成本，提升 TCO（總擁有成本）競爭力。

4.5–4.11 廠房物理設計與物料傳遞 Physical Design & Material Transfer

原文內容 Original Text

4.5 In cleanrooms and critical zones, all exposed surfaces should be smooth, impervious and unbroken in order to minimize the shedding or accumulation of particles or micro-organisms.

4.6 To reduce accumulation of dust and to facilitate cleaning there should be no recesses that are difficult to clean effectively, therefore projecting ledges, shelves, cupboards and equipment should be kept to a minimum. Doors should be designed to avoid recesses that cannot be cleaned. Sliding doors may be undesirable for this reason.

4.7 Materials used in cleanrooms, both in the construction of the room and for items used within the room, should be selected to minimize generation of particles and to permit the repeated application of cleaning, disinfectant and sporicidal agents where used.

4.8 Ceilings should be designed and sealed to prevent contamination from the space above them.

4.9 Sinks and drains should be prohibited in the grade A and grade B areas. In other cleanrooms, air breaks should be fitted between the machine or sink and the drains. Floor drains in lower grade cleanrooms should be fitted with traps or water seals designed to prevent back flow and should be regularly cleaned, disinfected and maintained.

4.10 The transfer of equipment and materials into and out of the cleanrooms and critical zones is one of the greatest potential sources of contamination. Any activities with the potential to compromise the cleanliness of cleanrooms or the critical zone should be assessed and if they cannot be eliminated, appropriate controls should be implemented.

4.11 The transfer of materials, equipment, and components into the grade A or B areas should be carried out via a unidirectional process. Where possible, items should be sterilised and passed into these areas through double-ended sterilisers (e.g. through a double-door autoclave or depyrogenation oven/tunnel) sealed into the wall. Where sterilisation upon transfer is not possible, a procedure which achieves the same objective of not introducing contamination should be validated and implemented, (e.g. using an effective transfer disinfection process, rapid transfer systems for isolators or, for gaseous or liquid materials, a bacteria-retentive filter). The removal of items from the grade A and B areas should be carried out via a separate unidirectional process. If this is not possible, time-based separation of movement (incoming/exiting material) by procedure should be considered.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 物理設計的五大原則

  • 平滑無縫（4.5）：表面不能有微裂縫或孔隙，否則微生物在清洗後仍會殘留在縫隙中繁殖。
  • 無死角（4.6）：所有突出結構（ledge、shelf）都是清潔死角，因此潔淨室設計要「極簡主義」。推拉門的軌道也是污染源，因此不推薦。
  • 耐化學品（4.7）：清潔劑、消毒劑、孢子殺滅劑會反覆使用，材料必須能承受這些強效化學物質而不老化或釋放粒子。
  • 天花板密封（4.8）：吊頂上方通常是管線和纖維，若未妥善密封，塵埃會從天花板縫隙落入潔淨室。
  • 禁止排水（4.9）：A/B 級禁止設置水槽和排水溝——這些是微生物（尤其是革蘭陰性菌）的溫床。

核心概念解析 物料傳遞的三種方式

1. 雙門滅菌設備（Double-ended steriliser）：如雙門高壓滅菌釜（autoclave）或去熱原隧道烘箱，嵌入牆壁。物料從 Grade C 一側裝入，滅菌後從 Grade B 一側取出。物料傳遞同時完成滅菌——最理想的方式。

2. 快速傳遞口（Rapid Transfer Port, RTP）：又稱 Alpha-Beta 快速傳遞口，專為 Isolator 設計。Alpha port 安裝在 Isolator 壁上，Beta port 安裝在容器上。兩者對接時形成無菌密封界面，容器打開進入 Isolator 內部而不暴露於外部環境。

3. 時間序分離（Time-based separation）：當無法設計單向流時，利用程序規定「物料進入」與「物料移出」不能同時進行，以時間區隔降低交叉污染風險。

重點提示 單向流原則的精髓

4.11 條款強調「unidirectional process（單向流程）」——物料只能單向流動，進入和移出必須是不同路徑或不同時間。這不只是氣流的單向，也包括物流動線的單向。

思考：為什麼廢棄物（如用過的棉籤、環境監測樣本）也需要「via a separate unidirectional process」移出？因為這些物品表面可能已受污染，若與新進物料混用同一通道，有將污染帶入的風險。

比喻說明 機場安全管制

想像機場的安全管制：

  • 旅客（物料）從一般區進入登機門（Grade A），必須經過安全檢查（消毒/滅菌）
  • 行李（設備）通過 X 光機（雙門滅菌設備）的安全通道進入
  • 已登機的旅客（用過的物料/廢棄物）從另一個出口離開，不能反向通過安檢區
  • 快速傳遞口（RTP）就像貴賓通道——設計精巧，讓特定物品無縫進入而不干擾整體安全

4.12–4.17 氣閘設計、壓力梯度與監控 Airlock Design, Pressure Cascades & Monitoring

原文內容 Original Text

4.12 Airlocks should be designed and used to provide physical separation and to minimize microbial and particle contamination of the different areas and should be present for material and personnel moving between different grades. Wherever possible, airlocks used for personnel movement should be separated from those used for material movement. Airlocks should be flushed effectively with filtered air to ensure that the grade of the cleanroom is maintained. The final stage of the airlock should, in the "at rest" state, be of the same cleanliness grade (viable and total particle) as the cleanroom into which it leads. Where the CCS indicates that the risk of contamination is high, separate change rooms for entering and leaving production areas should be used. Airlocks should be designed as follows:

  • i. Personnel airlocks: Areas of increasing cleanliness used for entry of personnel (e.g. from the grade D area to the grade C area to the grade B area). In general hand washing facilities should be provided only in the first stage of the changing room and not be present in changing rooms directly accessing the grade B area.
  • ii. Material airlocks: used for materials and equipment transfer. Only materials and equipment that have been included on an approved list and assessed during validation of the transfer process, should be transferred into the grade A or grade B areas via an airlock or pass-through hatches. Equipment and materials (intended for use in the grade A area) should be protected when transiting through the grade B area. Pass-through hatches should be designed to protect the higher-grade environment, for example by effective flushing with an active filtered air supply. The movement of material or equipment from lower grade or unclassified area to higher grade clean areas should be subject to cleaning and disinfection commensurate with the risk and in line with the CCS.

4.13 For pass-through hatches and airlocks, the entry and exit doors should not be opened simultaneously. For airlocks leading to the grade A and grade B areas, an interlocking system should be used. For airlocks leading to grade C and D areas, a visual and/or audible warning system should be operated as a minimum.

4.14 Cleanrooms should be supplied with a filtered air supply that maintains a positive pressure and/or an airflow relative to the background environment of a lower grade under all operational conditions. Adjacent rooms of different grades should have an air pressure difference of a minimum of 10 Pascals (guidance value). The recommendations regarding air supplies and pressures may need to be modified where it is necessary to contain certain materials (e.g. pathogenic, highly toxic or radioactive products). Decontamination of facilities and treatment of air leaving a clean area may be necessary for some operations.

4.15 Airflow patterns within cleanrooms and zones should be visualised to demonstrate that there is no ingress from lower grade to higher grade areas. Where unidirectional airflow is required, visualisation studies should be performed. Video recordings of the airflow patterns should be retained. The outcome of the air visualisation studies should be documented and considered when establishing the facility's environmental monitoring programme. Airflow pattern studies should be performed both at rest and in operation (e.g. simulating operator interventions).

4.16 Indicators of air pressure differences should be fitted between cleanrooms. Air pressure differences identified as critical should be continuously monitored and recorded. A warning system should be in place to instantly indicate and warn operators of any failure in the air supply or reduction of air pressure differences. The warning signal should not be overridden without assessment. Where alarm delays are set, these should be assessed and justified within the CCS.

4.17 Facilities should be designed to permit observation of production activities from outside the grade A and B areas (e.g. through the provision of windows or remote cameras with a full view of the area and processes to allow observation and supervision without entry).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 氣閘設計原則

氣閘（Airlock/氣閘）是不同等級潔淨室之間的過渡空間，其設計核心是「緩衝」——讓人員或物料在進入高等級區域前，先在一個中間空間完成換裝、消毒，並讓環境自行恢復。

關鍵要求：氣閘的最後一道門（連接高等級潔淨室的那一側）在靜止態下，必須達到與其連接的潔淨室相同的潔淨度等級。換句話說，進入 Grade B 的更衣室最後一間，靜止態必須達到 Grade B 標準。

人員氣閘 vs 物料氣閘：兩者應盡量分開。洗手設施只設在更衣室的第一道（最低等級）入口，Grade B 前更衣室內不應有水槽（再次呼應 4.9 條款）。

壓力梯度規範 Pressure Cascade

壓力梯度方向（正壓模式）：
Grade A > Grade B > Grade C > Grade D > 走廊/外部

最低壓差要求：
相鄰不同等級房間之間 ≥ 10 Pa（指導值）

典型設計示例：
  Grade A    : +45 Pa（相對於外部參考點）
  Grade B    : +35 Pa
  Grade C    : +25 Pa
  Grade D    : +15 Pa
  更衣室/走廊: +5 Pa
  外部        :  0 Pa（參考基準）

注意：上述數值僅為示例，實際設計
需根據 HVAC 工程計算和 CCS 確定。

重點提示 警報系統的嚴格要求

4.16 條款特別強調：壓差警報不得在未評估的情況下被忽略（override）。這在實務中意義重大——操作人員不能因為「趕時間」或「一時誤觸」就取消警報而繼續操作。

Alarm delay（警報延遲設定）也需要在 CCS 中說明理由。例如，開門時壓差短暫下降是正常的，設定 30 秒的延遲是合理的，但若無書面說明則不合規。

4.17 條款（觀察窗/遠端攝影機）的核心邏輯是：主管和 QA 應能在不進入 Grade A/B 的情況下監督生產活動，減少人員進入帶來的污染風險。這是「以工程取代人為干預」的體現。

比喻說明 壓力梯度如同瀑布

想像一系列從高到低的水池（瀑布）：水（空氣）永遠從高處流向低處。

  • Grade A 是最高的水池，水（潔淨空氣）不斷從上方（HEPA 過濾器）注入，並向外溢出到 Grade B
  • Grade B 的水位比 Grade C 高，Grade C 比 Grade D 高
  • 任何外部污染物（髒水）都只會從低水位流向更低水位，永遠無法往上爬
  • 如果某個水池的水位異常下降（壓差警報），就說明可能有洩漏或 HVAC 問題，必須立即處理

實務應用 特殊產品的壓力設計

對於處理高毒性或放射性藥物（如 ADC 藥物抗體藥物偶聯物、放射性藥物）的設施，4.14 條款允許使用負壓設計——此時保護方向反轉，外部空氣流向內部，防止危險物質外洩。

在 CDMO 環境下，如果同一廠房同時生產普通無菌製品（需正壓）和高毒性製品（需負壓），通常需要獨立的 HVAC 系統和物理隔離，這是廠房設計的重大挑戰，也是 CDMO 差異化定價的依據之一。

4.18–4.22 屏障技術 Barrier Technologies: Isolators & RABS

原文內容 Original Text

Barrier Technologies

4.18 Isolators or RABS, which are different technologies, and the associated processes, should be designed to provide protection through separation of the grade A environment from the environment of the surrounding room. The hazards introduced from entry or removal of items during processing should be minimized and supported by high capability transfer technologies or validated systems that robustly prevent contamination.

4.19 The design of the technology and processes used should ensure appropriate conditions are maintained in the critical zone to protect the exposed product during operations.

i. Isolators:

  • a. Open isolators: should ensure grade A conditions with first air protection in the critical zone and unidirectional airflow that sweeps over and away from exposed products during processing.
  • b. Closed isolators: should ensure grade A conditions with adequate protection for exposed products. Airflow may not be fully unidirectional in closed isolators where simple operations are conducted. However, any turbulent airflow should not increase risk of contamination. Where processing lines are included, grade A conditions should be ensured with first air protection and unidirectional airflow.
  • c. Negative pressure isolators: should only be used when containment of the product is considered essential (e.g. radiopharmaceutical products) and specialized risk control measures should be applied to ensure the critical zone is not compromised.

ii. RABS:

The design of RABS should ensure grade A conditions with unidirectional airflow and first air protection in the critical zone. A positive airflow from the critical zone to the supporting background environment should be maintained.

4.20 The background environment for isolators or RABS should ensure the risk of transfer of contamination is minimized.

i. Isolators:

  • a. The background environment for open isolators should generally correspond to a minimum of grade C. The background for closed isolators should correspond to a minimum of grade D. The decision on the background classification should be based on risk assessment and justified in the CCS.
  • b. Key considerations when performing the risk assessment for the CCS of an isolator should include: the bio-decontamination programme, the extent of automation, the impact of glove manipulations that may potentially compromise 'first air' protection, the impact of potential loss of barrier/glove integrity, transfer mechanisms used, and activities such as set-up or maintenance that may require the doors to be opened prior to the final bio-decontamination of the isolator.
  • c. Airflow pattern studies should be performed at the interfaces of open isolators to demonstrate the absence of air ingress.

ii. RABS:

The background environment for RABS used for aseptic processing should correspond to a minimum of grade B and airflow pattern studies should be performed to demonstrate the absence of air ingress during interventions, including door openings if applicable.

4.21 The materials used for glove systems (for both isolators and RABS) should be demonstrated to have appropriate mechanical and chemical resistance. The frequency of glove replacement should be defined within the CCS.

i. Isolators: Leak testing of the glove system should be performed using a methodology demonstrated to be suitable for the task and criticality. The testing should be performed at defined intervals — generally at a minimum frequency of the beginning and end of each batch or campaign. Glove integrity monitoring should include a visual inspection associated with each use and following any manipulation that may affect the integrity of the system.

ii. RABS: For RABS, gloves used in the grade A area should be sterilised before installation and sterilised or effectively bio-decontaminated by a validated method prior to each manufacturing campaign. If exposed to the background environment during operation, disinfection using an approved methodology following each exposure should be completed. Gloves should be visually examined with each use, and integrity testing should be performed at periodic intervals.

4.22 Decontamination methods (cleaning and bio-decontamination, and where applicable inactivation for biological materials) should be appropriately defined and controlled. The cleaning process prior to the bio-decontamination step is essential.

i. For isolators: The bio-decontamination process of the interior should be automated, validated and controlled within defined cycle parameters and should include a sporicidal agent in a suitable form (e.g. gaseous or vaporized form). Gloves should be appropriately extended with fingers separated to ensure contact with the agent.

ii. For RABS: The sporicidal disinfection should include the routine application of a sporicidal agent using a method that has been validated and demonstrated to robustly include all areas of the interior surfaces.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Isolator vs RABS 關鍵差異

Annex 1 明確指出兩者是「不同技術（different technologies）」，不可互換。核心差異在於屏障的完整性和背景環境要求：

特性Isolator（隔離器）RABS（限制進出屏障系統）
屏障類型完全封閉或半封閉物理屏障硬質屏障 + 手套埠
最低背景環境開放式: Grade C
密閉式: Grade D		Grade B

核心概念解析 開放式 vs 密閉式 Isolator

開放式 Isolator（Open Isolator）：有氣體入口/出口，與外界有受控的氣流交換。背景最低需 Grade C。在出口處需有過濾器防止污染物進入。

密閉式 Isolator（Closed Isolator）：完全密封，無持續氣流交換。背景最低只需 Grade D。常用於小批量或非連續操作（如放射性藥物配製）。

負壓 Isolator（Negative Pressure Isolator）：僅用於需要「containment（遏制）」的情況，如放射性藥物或高毒性物質。內部相對外部為負壓，防止危險物質逸出。

比喻說明 太空站 vs 無塵室

Isolator = 太空站：完全與外界隔絕，有完整的生命保障系統（bio-decontamination），太空人（操作人員）透過特殊介面（手套埠/RTP）與內部互動，進入前需完整的「氣閘程序（airlock procedure）」。

RABS = 高規格無塵室中的手術區：在嚴格控制的背景環境（Grade B）中，透過硬質屏障和手套埠操作，但屏障並非完全密封——它依賴正壓氣流從 Grade A 向外推進來維持保護。開門時需暫停操作並重新驗證。

重點提示 手套完整性測試的重要性

4.21 條款對手套系統有嚴格要求，因為手套是屏障技術中最薄弱的環節——它同時需要：

  • 機械強度（不易破裂）
  • 化學相容性（不被 VHP、消毒劑侵蝕）
  • 無菌性（Isolator 手套需能承受 bio-decontamination 循環）

Isolator 手套的洩漏測試必須在每批次開始和結束時進行（最低頻率）。每次使用後還需目視檢查。這是因為即便通過了開批測試，操作過程中仍可能因操作不當而造成細微破損。

公式與計算 VHP 生物去污染的 6-log 殺滅要求

Isolator bio-decontamination（生物去污染）目標：

標準要求：≥ 6-log reduction（6 個對數值減少）
例如：初始孢子負載 = 10^6 個
       去污染後目標: ≤ 1 個孢子
       即：SAL (Sterility Assurance Level) ≤ 10^-6

常用孢子殺滅劑形式：
• VHP（Vapour-phase Hydrogen Peroxide，氣相過氧化氫）
• HPO（Hydrogen Peroxide Liquid）
• Peracetic acid（過乙酸蒸氣）

驗證指標（Biological Indicator, BI）：
常用 Geobacillus stearothermophilus 孢子
在暴露面積最大/最難到達的位置放置

實務應用 CDMO 選擇 Isolator 的決策框架

對於 CDMO 在接受新產品時，是否應要求客戶的生產線配置 Isolator，需考量：

  • 產品特性：高毒性（需負壓 Isolator）、生物製品（需完整 bio-decontamination）
  • 批量大小：小批量（密閉式 Isolator 更具靈活性）、大批量（開放式 Isolator 或 RABS）
  • 廠房等級節省：Closed Isolator 可讓背景降至 Grade D，節省大量 HVAC 成本
  • 驗證複雜性：Isolator 的 bio-decontamination 驗證需要額外的時間和資源

原文內容 Original Text

Cleanroom and clean air equipment qualification

4.23 Cleanrooms and clean air equipment such as unidirectional airflow units (UDAFs), RABS and isolators, used for the manufacture of sterile products, should be qualified according to the required characteristics of the environment.

4.24 Cleanrooms and clean air equipment should be qualified using methodology in accordance with the requirements of Annex 15. Cleanroom qualification (including classification) should be clearly differentiated from operational environmental monitoring.

4.25 Cleanroom and clean air equipment qualification is the overall process of assessing the level of compliance of a classified cleanroom or clean air equipment with its intended use. As part of the qualification requirements of Annex 15, the qualification of cleanrooms and clean air equipment should include (where relevant):

  • i. installed filter system leakage and integrity testing,
  • ii. airflow tests — volume and velocity,
  • iii. air pressure difference test,
  • iv. airflow direction test and visualisation,
  • v. microbial airborne and surface contamination,
  • vi. temperature measurement test,
  • vii. relative humidity test,
  • viii. recovery test,
  • ix. containment leak test.

Reference for the qualification of the cleanrooms and clean air equipment can be found in the ISO 14644 series of standards.

4.26 Cleanroom classification is part of the cleanroom qualification and is a method of assessing the level of air cleanliness against a specification for a cleanroom or clean air equipment by measuring the total particle concentration. Classification activities should be scheduled to avoid any impact on process or product quality. Initial classification should be performed during simulated operations and reclassification performed during simulated operations or during aseptic process simulation (APS).

4.27 For cleanroom classification, the total of particles equal to or greater than 0.5 and 5 µm should be measured. This measurement should be performed both at rest and in simulated operations in accordance with the limits specified in Table 1.

(a) Classification including 5µm particles may be considered where indicated by the CCS or historical trends.
(b) For grade D, in operation limits are not predetermined. The manufacturer should establish in operation limits based on a risk assessment and routine data where applicable.

4.28 For classification of the cleanroom, the minimum number of sampling locations and their positioning can be found in ISO 14644 Part 1. For the aseptic processing area and the background environment (the grade A and grade B areas, respectively), additional sample locations should be considered and all critical processing areas such as the point of fill and container closure feeder bowls should be evaluated.

4.29 Cleanroom classification should be carried out in the "at rest" and "in operation" states.

  • i. "At rest" state: the condition whereby the installation of all the utilities is complete including any functioning HVAC, with the main manufacturing equipment installed as specified but not operating and without personnel present in the room.
  • ii. "In operation" state: the condition where the installation of the cleanroom is complete, the HVAC system fully operational, equipment installed and functioning in the manufacturer's defined operating mode with the maximum number of personnel present performing or simulating routine operational work.
  • iii. The total particle limits given in Table 1 above for the "at rest" state should be achieved after a "clean up" period on completion of operations and line clearance/cleaning activities. The "clean up" period (guidance value of less than 20 minutes) should be determined during the qualification of the rooms.

4.30 The speed of air supplied by unidirectional airflow systems should be clearly justified in the qualification protocol. Unidirectional airflow systems should provide a homogeneous air speed in a range of 0.36 – 0.54 m/s (guidance value) at the working position, unless otherwise scientifically justified in the CCS. Airflow visualization studies should correlate with the air speed measurement.

  • i. cleanroom classification (total particle concentration),
  • ii. integrity test of final filters,
  • iii. airflow volume measurement,
  • iv. verification of air pressure difference between rooms, and
  • v. air velocity test (Note: For grade B, C and D the air velocity test should be performed according to a risk assessment documented as part of the CCS. However, it is required for filling zones supplied with unidirectional airflow. For grades with non-unidirectional airflow, a measurement of recovery testing should replace velocity testing).
The maximum time interval for requalification of grade A & B areas, is 6 months.
The maximum time interval for requalification of grade C & D areas, is 12 months.

Appropriate requalification should also be carried out following remedial action or after changes to equipment, facility or processes. Examples of changes to be considered include:

  • i. interruption of air movement which affects the operation of the installation,
  • ii. change in the design of the cleanroom or of the operational setting parameters of the HVAC system,
  • iii. special maintenance which affects the operation of the installation (e.g. change of final filters).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Qualification vs Environmental Monitoring

4.24 條款強調必須明確區分兩個概念：

潔淨室資格確認（Qualification）：是一次性（或定期）的正式評估，證明潔淨室符合設計規格。結果記錄在 Qualification Report 中，屬於 GMP 文件體系的一部分。

環境監控（Environmental Monitoring, EM）：是生產過程中的日常監控，確認環境持續符合要求。EM 結果用於趨勢分析，不能替代 Qualification。

簡言之：Qualification 問的是「這個房間能達到 Grade B 嗎？」EM 問的是「今天的生產中，Grade B 有沒有維持？」

重點提示 Table 1 關鍵數據解讀

Grade A 的特殊性：靜止態和運作態的粒子限度相同（0.5 µm = 3,520/m³），這意味著 Grade A 必須在有人員操作時也維持靜止態的潔淨度——這正是 UDAF 和 RABS/Isolator 屏障存在的理由。

Grade B 的顯著差異：靜止態 3,520（與 Grade A 相同），但運作態允許到 352,000——差了 100 倍！這說明人員進入 Grade B 後，即使穿著潔淨室服裝，仍會帶入大量粒子。

Grade D 的「Not predetermined」：運作態粒子限度不預設，由製造商根據風險評估自訂。這給了廠方靈活性，但也要求有充足的數據支撐。

核心概念解析 潔淨室資格確認的九項測試

4.25 條款列出的九項測試，每一項都有其目的：

  • 濾器洩漏/完整性（i）：確認 HEPA 濾器無破損（通常用 PAO/DOP 測試）
  • 氣流量/風速（ii）：確認換氣次數和送風量符合設計
  • 壓差（iii）：確認各房間之間的壓力梯度符合要求（≥10 Pa）
  • 氣流方向/可視化（iv）：確認氣流方向正確，無回流（用煙霧或乾冰霧測試）
  • 微生物污染（v）：在靜止態和運作態測試微生物含量（Table 2）
  • 溫度（vi）：確認室溫符合工藝要求（通常 20±2°C 或 18-22°C）
  • 相對濕度（vii）：通常 <50% RH，防止細菌繁殖
  • 恢復測試（viii）：測量從污染狀態恢復到 at rest 標準的時間（應 <20 分鐘）
  • 圍護結構洩漏測試（ix）：特別適用於 Isolator，測試整個圍護結構的密封性

比喻說明 新車出廠檢驗 vs 定期保養

把潔淨室想像成一輛汽車：

  • 初次資格確認（IQ/OQ/PQ） = 新車出廠前的完整品質檢驗——每個零件都要測試是否符合規格
  • 定期再確認（Requalification） = 定期保養——A/B 級每 6 個月、C/D 級每 12 個月
  • 環境監控（EM） = 每天的儀表板警示燈——不能替代保養，但可以即時發現問題
  • 變更後再確認 = 重大修理後的重新驗車——換了 HEPA 濾器就要重新測試濾器完整性

實務應用 CDMO 的再確認計畫管理

對 CDMO 而言，同時管理多個客戶品項的生產線，再確認計畫的排程至關重要：

  • Grade A/B 每 6 個月的再確認視窗需要提前規劃，避免與關鍵批次生產衝突
  • 再確認通常需要 2-5 天（取決於潔淨室規模和測試項目），期間生產線暫停
  • APS（無菌製程模擬）可以與再確認同步進行（4.26 條款），提高效率
  • 若有 OOC（Out of Compliance）事件，需立即進行再確認——這可能打亂生產計畫，影響客戶交期

4.33–4.36 消毒程序 Disinfection

原文內容 Original Text

Disinfection

4.33 The disinfection of cleanrooms is particularly important. They should be cleaned and disinfected thoroughly in accordance with a written programme. For disinfection to be effective, prior cleaning to remove surface contamination should be performed. Cleaning programmes should effectively remove disinfectant residues. More than one type of disinfecting agent should be employed to ensure that where they have different modes of action, their combined usage is effective against bacteria and fungi. Disinfection should include the periodic use of a sporicidal agent. Monitoring should be undertaken regularly in order to assess the effectiveness of the disinfection programme and to detect changes in types of microbial flora (e.g. organisms resistant to the disinfection regime currently in use).

4.34 The disinfection process should be validated. Validation studies should demonstrate the suitability and effectiveness of disinfectants in the specific manner in which they are used and on the type of surface material, or representative material if justified, and should support the in-use expiry periods of prepared solutions.

4.35 Disinfectants and detergents used in grade A and grade B areas should be sterile prior to use. Disinfectants used in grade C and D may also be required to be sterile where determined in the CCS. Where the disinfectants and detergents are diluted/prepared by the sterile product manufacturer, this should be done in a manner to prevent contamination and they should be monitored for microbial contamination. Dilutions should be kept in previously cleaned containers (and sterilized where applicable) and should only be stored for the defined period. If the disinfectants and detergents are supplied "ready-made" then results from certificates of analysis or conformance can be accepted subject to successful completion of the appropriate vendor qualification.

4.36 Where fumigation or vapour disinfection (e.g. Vapour-phase Hydrogen Peroxide) of cleanrooms and associated surfaces are used, the effectiveness of any fumigation agent and dispersion system should be understood and validated.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 消毒的三個層次

Annex 1 的消毒要求分為三個遞進層次：

第一層：清潔（Cleaning）——先物理移除表面污垢、有機物和消毒劑殘留。若此步驟不充分，後續消毒劑無法有效接觸表面微生物（有機物會「保護」細菌）。

第二層：消毒（Disinfection）——用化學試劑殺死或使微生物失活。必須輪換使用不同作用機制的消毒劑，防止耐藥性的產生。

第三層：殺孢子（Sporicide）——定期使用能殺死孢子形成菌的試劑（如次氯酸鈉、過氧化氫）。普通消毒劑對芽孢無效，這是 Annex 1 明確要求定期使用孢子殺滅劑的原因。

重點提示 消毒劑輪換的必要性

4.33 條款要求使用「不只一種消毒劑」，原因是：

  • 不同消毒劑有不同的作用機制（氧化、去脂、滲透等），輪換使用可以防止微生物產生抗藥性
  • 單一消毒劑長期使用會造成環境中的菌種選擇壓力，最終出現「對現有消毒方案有抵抗力的菌株（resistant organisms）」
  • 環境監控的微生物趨勢分析（EM trending）是偵測消毒效果下降的關鍵工具——如果持續監測到相同菌種，可能代表消毒程序有漏洞

重點提示 Grade A/B 消毒劑必須無菌

4.35 條款的核心邏輯：你不能用一個「髒的」消毒劑去消毒最潔淨的區域。如果消毒劑本身受到微生物污染，施用後反而會將污染帶入 Grade A/B 區域。

這代表：

  • 用於 Grade A/B 的消毒劑需要過濾（0.22 µm）或其他滅菌方式
  • 必須在無菌條件下分裝，並在有效期限內使用
  • 廠商提供的「ready-made」消毒劑需有 Certificate of Analysis 和已通過的供應商資格確認

比喻說明 消毒如同多重門鎖

想像你要保護一個重要文件：

  • 清潔（Cleaning） = 先確認辦公桌上沒有雜物遮擋，才能上鎖
  • 消毒（Disinfection） = 第一把鎖（殺滅一般細菌和真菌）
  • 孢子殺滅劑（Sporicide） = 第二把鎖（殺滅最頑固的芽孢菌）
  • VHP 燻蒸（4.36） = 整個保險箱重新上保全密碼（用於 Isolator 或整個潔淨室的全面生物去污染）

每一把鎖對應不同的威脅——只有全部到位，才能確保「無菌保證（Sterility Assurance）」。

實務應用 消毒程序驗證的實務挑戰

4.34 條款要求對消毒程序進行驗證。在 CDMO 環境中，這意味著：

  • 驗證必須在「代表性的表面材料」上進行（不銹鋼、環氧樹脂地板、PVC 軟牆等）
  • 「in-use expiry（使用效期）」需要有數據支撐——稀釋後的消毒劑放置多久仍然有效？
  • 如果 CDMO 接受了新的表面塗料或建材（如廠房翻新），可能需要重新驗證消毒程序
  • 微生物抗藥性監測是消毒程序管理的長期工作，需要建立歷史數據庫

  • 等級分類：A/B/C/D 四個等級構成無菌製造的環境框架。Grade A 在靜止態和運作態都必須維持相同的粒子標準（3,520/m³ @ 0.5 µm），這正是 UDAF 和屏障技術存在的必要性。
  • 屏障技術：Isolator（隔離器）和 RABS（限制進出屏障系統）的使用必須在 CCS 中考慮。Isolator 提供更高的無菌保證，但需要 bio-decontamination 驗證；RABS 必須在 Grade B 背景下操作。
  • 氣閘與壓差：氣閘必須單向使用，Grade A/B 氣閘需聯鎖系統。相鄰不同等級房間的最低壓差為 10 Pa；壓差需連續監控，警報不得在未評估情況下被忽略。
  • 氣流可視化：UDAF 系統的風速為 0.36–0.54 m/s（指導值），必須進行靜止態和運作態的氣流可視化研究，並保留影片記錄。
  • 物料傳遞：進出 Grade A/B 的物料必須遵循單向流程，優先使用雙門滅菌設備或 RTP；無法滅菌時須有驗證的消毒程序替代。
  • 再確認頻率：Grade A/B 最長每 6 個月再確認一次；Grade C/D 最長每 12 個月一次。包括粒子分類、濾器完整性、氣流量、壓差和風速（共五項最低要求）。
  • 消毒要求：清潔先於消毒；必須使用多種不同機制的消毒劑輪換使用；定期使用孢子殺滅劑；Grade A/B 使用的消毒劑須為無菌；消毒程序必須驗證。
