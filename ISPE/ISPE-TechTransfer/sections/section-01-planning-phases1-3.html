Chapter 2 — Technology Transfer: Planning Considerations and Success Criteria｜第二章：技術轉移規劃考量與成功標準
2.1 Introduction｜導論

原文內容 Original Text

This chapter provides guidance on what to consider for a transfer, including establishing success criteria, prior to the formal formation of the full technology transfer team. Guidance provided is applicable to drug substance, drug product, and analytical procedure technology transfers.

A technology transfer typically starts with an agreement (referred to as a charter in Chapter 3) normally formalized or on a contractual basis between a sending unit and a receiving unit for the transfer of, e.g., a developed process or a commercial process or an analytical method.

The success of a technology transfer project will be largely dependent upon the skill and performance of individuals assigned to the project from both the sending unit and the receiving unit. It is, therefore, critical that a clear objective for any technology transfer project be developed. It is also critical that a project team comprised of individuals from both the sending unit and the receiving unit is established and that there is a precise understanding of each team member's role and responsibility prior to initiation of the technology transfer project.

"Skill alone will not ensure a successful technology transfer project. Once a project has been identified and a team chosen, a clear and realistic project implementation plan is required to guide the project, manage expectations, and handle the inevitable deviations and changes that may present themselves during implementation."

Along with that plan comes the need to consider the relationship between the various tasks associated with a successful technology transfer. It should be clearly understood that each technology transfer assignment is unique; therefore, it is impossible to provide a generic technology transfer plan.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Sending Unit（送出單位）：持有製程或分析方法的一方，通常是研發單位、原廠，或另一個生產廠。

Receiving Unit（接收單位）：承接並執行該製程的一方，在 CDMO 情境下，通常就是我們自己。

Success Criteria（成功標準）：技術轉移在「什麼條件下」算成功的預先定義。本章的核心論點是：這些標準必須在轉移開始前定義，而非事後補充。

比喻說明 Analogy

技術轉移就像廚師交接食譜給另一家餐廳。光有食譜不夠——接手的廚師需要知道這道菜的「標準」：顧客吃到什麼味道算成功？廚房設備不同怎麼辦？如果到出餐後才爭論標準，雙方都會痛苦。

重點提示 Key Notes

本指南明確指出，每個技術轉移都是獨一無二的，不存在通用計畫範本。這對 CDMO 特別重要——不能用同一套 SOP 套用所有客戶的轉移案。每個專案都需要客製化的計畫。

  2. 在您目前的 CDMO 操作中，是否有清楚界定送出單位與接收單位各自的責任範圍？
2.2 Technology Transfer Planning Considerations｜技術轉移規劃考量

原文內容 Original Text

In the early planning phase, a charter may not be fully developed yet. In this case, the team should document, at a minimum, the major deliverables and assumptions not expected to change. Examples include:

  • High level program deliverables such as delivery of material to clinical trials or early planning (e.g., for analytical methods)
  • Agency submission strategies
  • Site licensure milestones
  • Budget
  • Mechanism for handling changes, e.g., in scope or budget

As stated earlier, technology transfer can be considered a transfer of knowledge using well founded project management principles.

2.2.1 Developing an Early Agreement Between the Sending and Receiving Units

An agreement (referred to as a charter in Chapter 3) is typically high level and developed with input from sending and receiving unit Subject Matter Experts (SMEs); input is rarely from the full technology transfer team. Securing an early agreement, sometimes referred to as site selection, may be necessary to release the full resources required for detailed planning and execution.

It is increasingly common that early stages with long lead times are initiated prior to formation of the entire team. It is also often necessary to make an early assessment of HSE aspects; an example is a facility that is not capable of handling particular compounds from an HSE standpoint.

By its nature, a technology transfer requires effective management of incomplete information; consideration of information and SME availability is required to determine appropriate planning timelines.

2.2.2 Sending Unit and Receiving Unit Working Closely

Technology transfer planning should be initiated as early as possible. Roles and responsibilities for sending and receiving units should be defined early and updated frequently in the early planning stages. Effective planning and diligent tracking of potential issues can help to expedite technology transfer projects and prevent delays.

A plan created by collaboration between sending and receiving units can help to unify the technology transfer team and to ensure that there is agreement on deliverables, success criteria, and ownership of the transfer at different stages. Project management tools such as the RACI matrix can be useful to formalize roles.

RACI: R = Responsible (實際執行者), A = Accountable (最終責任者), C = Consulted (需徵詢意見者), I = Informed (需告知結果者)

The relationship within the technology transfer team should be actively maintained. Partnership between sending/receiving project managers and accountable functional area leads is key to actively managing the flow of known and emerging process, method, and site capability knowledge.

It is important to understand the critical path timetable and ensure that the technology transfer planning and execution timelines are consistent with the critical path timing. Communicating an unrealistic timeline to management could result in the team's acceptance of undesirable risks in order to meet their commitment.

People to people interaction should be encouraged, especially between counterparts in the sending unit and receiving unit. This is especially important where different cultures exist between sending and receiving units.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

RACI Matrix（責任分配矩陣）：一種專案管理工具，用於釐清每項任務誰負責執行（R）、誰有最終問責（A）、誰需要被諮詢（C）、誰需要被告知（I）。在技術轉移中，送出單位與接收單位各自的 RACI 必須書面化，避免「以為對方會做」的灰色地帶。

Critical Path（關鍵路徑）：專案中影響最終完成日期的最長任務鏈。任何在關鍵路徑上的任務若延誤，整個轉移就會延誤。

比喻說明 Analogy

RACI 矩陣就像餐廳的 HACCP 計畫一樣——每個控制點都要指定「誰量、誰記錄、誰核准、誰被通知」。技術轉移若沒有 RACI，就像廚房裡大家都以為別人會量油溫，最後沒人量。

重點提示 Key Notes

HSE 要早評估：健康、安全、環境（Health, Safety and Environment）的評估必須在計畫初期就完成，因為可能需要長前置期（long lead time）的設施改造或許可申請。CDMO 若接收含毒性化合物或特殊物質的專案，這個評估往往決定是否能承接。

時程務實性：指南特別警告——對管理層報告不切實際的時程，會讓團隊被迫接受不應接受的風險。這在 CDMO 的商業壓力下尤其重要。

2.2.3–2.2.4 Roles, Responsibilities & Effective Knowledge Transfer｜角色責任與有效知識移轉

原文內容 Original Text

2.2.3 Clear Understanding of Roles and Responsibilities

A clear understanding of roles and responsibilities for team members, from both the sending and receiving units, serves to minimize redundancies and oversights. Knowing what is expected by other team members can increase accountability and help to minimize confusion and conflicts.

Additional recommendations include:

  • Performing an assessment of the team capabilities relative to the skills required to complete tasks for which team members are accountable
  • Defining decision rights (i.e., who will make the decision regarding particular aspects or differences of view)
  • Defining clear communication routes both within the team and external to the team using, e.g., meetings, shared network or other project sites, meeting minutes
  • Documenting roles and responsibilities with appropriate project management tools such as a RACI matrix

2.2.4 Effective Knowledge Transfer

It is very important that both sending and receiving units discuss and agree the knowledge to be transferred — scientific, regulatory, operational (including tacit knowledge), business, and cultural.

Scientific aspects include:

  • Process description, rationale, history, flow diagrams, unit operations, scale, and material transfers
  • Understanding what is important to control for both drug substance and drug product manufacture (see ICH Q8(R2)) including impact on packaging
  • Ensuring CQAs are understood by both sending and receiving units
  • Analytical technology required to measure process performance (HPLC equipment, columns, etc.)
  • Transferring the control strategies and operationalizing it — note: even for legacy products, regulators are increasingly expecting a QTPP, CQAs and control strategies to be in place
  • Being clear on CPPs and material attributes for each unit operation; design space if used
  • Multivariate approaches (design space, PAT tools, real time release testing, continuous manufacturing)
  • Potential sources of variation

Regulatory aspects include:

  • Compliance with regulatory requirements in both the operating country and the supply country (individual countries may have their own variation guidelines)
  • Some countries require additional bioequivalence studies
  • Validation carried out appropriately including lifecycle approach
  • Adequacy of the QMS/Pharmaceutical Quality System (ICH Q10)
  • Checking no regulatory changes in progress that could impact implementation

Operational aspects (including tacit knowledge) include:

  • Facility and utility requirements; facility fit
  • Operator training and/or qualification requirements
  • Equipment principles that may differ (mixers, dryers using different engineering principles)
  • Developing new manufacturing instructions and SOPs
  • HSE requirements (containment, operator protection)
  • Differences in shifts, automation, PAT tools, scale, batch sizes, process flows, sampling techniques
  • Understanding tacit knowledge — knowledge not explicitly documented but shows actual practices (e.g., scalability, vessel charging, analyst technique)
  • Long term planning for raw materials (import license restrictions)
  • Operators from sending unit working at receiving site (knowledge transfer, visa requirements, QA, HSE concerns)

Business aspects include:

  • Importance of the product being transferred (profitability may differ between units)
  • Determining who pays for what (new equipment, training)
  • Capital responsibility — who invests, who owns the asset, depreciation

Cultural aspects: Gaining understanding of a company or country's culture can significantly contribute to success. Some cultures may be procedural, hierarchical, unstructured, or relationship dependent. Language differences in SOPs and operator communication are also a factor. (Refer to ISPE Cultural Excellence Report [13].)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Tacit Knowledge（默會知識）：指「做得出來但說不清楚」的隱性操作知識。例如，攪拌槳的進料方式、分析師實際如何執行樣品前處理——這些資訊在 SOP 中往往付之闕如。指南明確要求將這類知識文件化，這是技術轉移中最難但最重要的一步。

QTPP / CQA / CPP：
QTPP（Target Product Quality Profile，目標產品品質概況）：最終產品應具備的品質標準
CQA（Critical Quality Attribute，關鍵品質屬性）：直接影響安全性、有效性的品質特性
CPP（Critical Process Parameter，關鍵製程參數）：對 CQA 有顯著影響的製程參數

比喻說明 Analogy

默會知識就像頂級廚師的「手感」——書上寫「攪拌至適當稠度」，但沒人告訴你那個稠度是什麼感覺。技術轉移若只傳遞 SOP 而不傳遞這種手感，接收廚房（CDMO）的第一批產品往往失敗。最好的解法是讓送出單位的廚師親自來廚房示範幾次。

重點提示 Key Notes

五大知識類型：指南將需要轉移的知識分為五類——科學、法規、操作（含默會知識）、商業、文化。CDMO 在接收新案時，往往專注在科學和操作層面，容易忽略法規合規差異（不同國家的送件策略）和文化差異（客戶來自不同管理文化）。

法規層面的隱形地雷：指南特別提醒，legacy product（老產品）的客戶也被監管機構要求提供 QTPP 和 CQA 文件。CDMO 在承接老產品轉移時，若客戶無法提供這些文件，可能需要協助重建，這必須計入報價和時程。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 接收單位在評估新轉移案時，應建立一份標準的知識查核清單，涵蓋以上五類。特別要確認：（1）送出單位是否能提供設計空間文件；（2）分析方法是否使用相同設備平台（HPLC 品牌、管柱型號）；（3）該產品在哪些市場銷售，各市場的法規要求是否一致。這些問題若在商業談判階段就提出，可避免後期巨大的補救成本。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 您是否曾在技術轉移後才發現送出單位有重要的「默會知識」未被傳遞？如何在下次避免？
  2. 文化差異如何影響 CDMO 與客戶（送出單位）之間的溝通效率？
2.2.5–2.2.7 Information Package, QRM & Stage Gates｜資訊套件、品質風險管理與階段閘門

原文內容 Original Text

2.2.5 Information Package

A sufficiently complete technology transfer package of information should be developed to meet the requirements of the technology transfer. Refer to Chapter 3, Chapter 8, and Chapter 13 for additional information.

2.2.6 Quality Risk Management

QRM is integral to technology transfer and should follow ICH Q9 principles (see Figure 2.1) or similar. It is important that teams are rigorous in their application of Q9 and spend sufficient time during the initiation phase to ensure full understanding by both sending and receiving units of the purpose of the QRM exercise.

Once the initiation phase is completed, a detailed risk assessment should be carried out (e.g., using tools such as cause and effect matrices, Ishikawa (fishbone) diagrams, gap analysis, and FMEA). After agreement on acceptable and unacceptable risks, then the unacceptable risks should be mitigated to an acceptable level and then controlled through a defined risk mitigation plan.

QRM is an iterative activity. All technology transfer projects require risk assessment. Existing risk assessments may need to be adapted to acknowledge changes in risk during the technology transfer. It is helpful to have a QRM plan if there will be multiple risk assessments. Note that there can be several types of risk assessment — for the project, the process, the product, the equipment, etc. Normally, the project manager would own the project risk assessments and the technical team would own the technical risk assessments.

2.2.7 Phases and Stage Gate Approach

The technology transfer project may be approached in phases (stages). Documentation of the project should include the important activities for each phase, e.g., reviews, deliverables, and projected timelines. Refer to Chapter 3 for the six phases of technology transfer.

Throughout the project, stage gate reviews should be used to ensure that all critical items from the current project phase have been completed satisfactorily before moving on to subsequent phases. This approach helps to ensure critical project items are completed on time and that later phases of technology transfer are not put at risk. Stage gate reviews should be attended by stakeholders and the technology transfer team.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

QRM（Quality Risk Management，品質風險管理）：根據 ICH Q9，QRM 的核心流程包括：風險識別 → 風險分析 → 風險評估 → 風險控制（降低或接受）→ 風險回顧。在技術轉移中，QRM 是識別送出單位與接收單位差異的正式工具。

Stage Gate（階段閘門）：每個階段結束時的正式審查點，確認所有關鍵交付物完成後才放行進入下一階段。這防止「問題堆積到後期才爆發」的常見情況。

2.3 Technology Transfer Success Criteria｜技術轉移成功標準

原文內容 Original Text

Technology transfer is considered successful if the receiving unit can consistently produce material through the qualified process and demonstrate that quality attributes have been maintained through process monitoring and analysis of the material produced.

"Simply stated, technology transfer can be considered successful if the receiving unit can routinely reproduce the transferred product, process, or method against a predefined set of specifications as agreed upon with the sending unit and/or development unit."

A comparability exercise may be completed as an assessment of sending versus receiving process/method performance. Interim success criteria are developed to track progress toward these goals. Success is ultimately demonstrated by regulatory agency licensure in the desired region(s). In addition, business performance parameters are established to balance risk versus cost, including resources.

Evaluation of the success of a technology transfer should be based on a pre-established set of criteria, which are evaluated as the project progresses. These criteria may be used to track progress of the transfer into subsequent phases and hence manage risk.

Success criteria usually take into account the business situation and product requirements, for example:

  • Stage within the product lifecycle
  • Availability of product and process knowledge
  • Experience of the sending units and receiving units
  • Number of sites involved
  • Whether it is a transfer between internal sites or to an external site

From a business perspective, the success of a technology transfer is based on efficiently meeting the requirements of product quality, patient safety, and efficacy, and business requirements. Business requirements may include:

  • Cost, including capital cost, expense and Cost of Goods Manufactured (COGM) targets
  • Capacity/volume
  • Equipment and facility capabilities
  • Time frames
  • Risk management
  • Regulatory requirements or strategy, including meeting existing commitments
  • Supply chain strategy

Success criteria can take several forms and may be dependent on the technology being transferred and the phase of development. The criteria should be measurable and achievable. Success criteria may include:

  • Evaluation of the technology performance
  • Adherence to the project timeline
  • Adherence to the budget

Specific success criteria should be documented in the technology transfer plan. Main aspects may include:

  • Process performance — comparing in-process data (either traditional or PAT generated) with historical data
  • Product quality that meets pre-established criteria (data sets for comparability analyses)
  • Confirmation that the control strategy is executable
  • Confirmation that the product control strategy from the sending unit has been successfully delivered by the receiving unit, including meeting requirements of product CQAs and specifications
  • Process performance as expected based on historical data:
    • Large molecule examples: cell growth rates, titers, step yields, chromatogram profiles, impurity clearance
    • Small molecule examples: impurity levels (solvents, elemental impurities), quality attributes (dissolution), material attributes (particle size distribution), process parameters (compression force), process yield, and throughput
  • Analytical method transfer and equivalency
  • Successful routine operation over an agreed number of batches
  • Successful routine operation of facility and equipment (operability, labor, OEE)
  • Training of staff
  • HSE objectives achieved
  • Documentation fully and satisfactorily completed
  • Regulatory inspection outcomes and regulatory submission on time
  • Improvement of process robustness and efficiency (exact 1:1 transfers are rare)

Consideration should also be made to establish longer term criteria (e.g., during process validation Stage 3) following closure of the technology transfer report. Technology transfer studies may fail to predict variance inherent in longer term evaluations. Success criteria could include permitting natural material attribute variance or intentional material attribute variance.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 三維成功標準

ISPE 將技術轉移成功標準分為三個維度：

  • 技術層面（Technical）：製程性能是否符合規格？分析方法是否等效？
  • 法規層面（Regulatory）：是否取得目標市場的法規核准？法規提交是否準時？
  • 商業層面（Business）：成本（COGM）是否達標？時程是否符合？設備利用率（OEE）是否改善？

三個維度缺一不可。技術上成功但商業上虧損，或技術上成功但法規上被拒，都不算真正的成功。

比喻說明 Analogy

成功標準就像奧運競賽的評分規則——必須在比賽「開始前」公告，否則裁判（和選手）都不知道往哪個方向努力。技術轉移若等到最後才定義「什麼叫成功」，等同於比賽結束後才宣布評分標準，必然引發爭議。

重點提示 Key Notes

可量化且可達成：成功標準必須是 measurable（可量化）且 achievable（可達成）的。「製程穩定」不是成功標準；「三批連續生產的 API 回收率 ≥ 95%，RSD < 2%」才是。

「一比一完美複製」是神話：指南明確指出，exact 1:1 transfers are rare。接收單位幾乎必然需要調整某些參數以適應自身設備。這意味著成功標準應允許合理的製程優化空間，而非要求零偏差。

長期標準不容忽視：短期 TT 報告關閉後，Stage 3 製程驗證期間的批次間變異可能比 TT 階段大。建議在 TT 計畫中就預先規劃長期監控指標。

├─ 製程性能 vs. 歷史數據
│   ├─ 大分子：cell growth rates, titers, yields
│   └─ 小分子：impurities, dissolution, particle size

└─ 員工訓練完成

實務應用 Practical Application

Chapter 3 — Technology Transfer Project Phases (Phases 1–3)｜第三章：技術轉移專案階段（第1-3階段）

原文內容 Original Text

This chapter provides a description for each of the six phases of a technology transfer. It is intended to be generic and describes general practices that apply to all transfers regardless of the technology being transferred. To note, at the time of publication, it is increasingly common for phases to overlap and/or be executed in parallel. The same principles used to manage and control a traditionally phased technology transfer can be leveraged for transfer with overlapping phases. The key principle in either case is to identify critical dependencies and control phase execution accordingly.

Figure 3.2 shows the six phases of technology transfer: Phase 1 — Form Transfer Team and Develop Charter Phase 2 — Consolidate Knowledge for Transfer and Develop TT Proposal Phase 3 — Identify Risks, Conduct Risk Assessments, Develop TT Plan Phase 4 — Execute Technology Transfer (covered in other chapters) Phase 5 — Confirm Technology Transfer Phase 6 — Close Technology Transfer

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

六階段框架：ISPE 將技術轉移分為六個有序但可能重疊的階段。本節涵蓋前三個規劃階段（Phase 1-3）。重要認知：指南承認在現實中各階段可能重疊執行（parallel execution），這反映了業界對縮短上市時間的壓力。關鍵原則是識別關鍵依存關係，而非強求線性順序。

重點提示 Key Notes

對 CDMO 而言，Phase 1-3 決定了整個專案是否「有一個好的開始」。這三個規劃階段若執行草率，Phase 4（執行）必然問題叢生——通常表現為：批次失敗、偏差處理爆炸、客戶投訴、成本超支。規劃投入的時間永遠比修復失誤的時間便宜。

Phase 1 (3.1) — Form Transfer Team and Develop Charter｜建立轉移團隊與制定章程

原文內容 Original Text

The initiation phase of technology transfer should establish the team, strategy, and goals of a project. Sending and receiving units should establish an integrated team of the required SMEs from the functional areas involved in the transfer. This team should develop a technology transfer charter to capture the goals, milestones, and roles and responsibilities for the project.

3.1.1 Establishing a Team

A formal technology transfer team should be established. The team structure, roles, and responsibilities should be identified. Team structure should include leadership from both sending units and receiving units, along with SMEs from the relevant functional areas.

At the start of the technology transfer program, key stakeholders, technical leads, and project managers should be identified. An overall project manager is usually responsible for delivery of the technology transfer and should coordinate activities such as finance, quality/regulatory, and materials management.

Technical leads are usually responsible for ensuring that project managers have the technical information to enable delivery of the technology transfer. Depending upon the complexity of the project, a technical lead and project manager may be the same person. If the lead serves this dual role, it is important to ensure that project management responsibilities cannot be neglected when competing priorities arise.

The core team should include representation from both the sending and receiving, as appropriate. Examples of functions represented in the core team include:

  • Analytical sciences / Analytical/Quality Control (QC)
  • Engineering
  • HSE and sustainability
  • Late stage development
  • Manufacturing operations / Manufacturing technical
  • Material management / Planning
  • Process development
  • Project management
  • QA
  • R&D
  • Regulatory affairs
  • Supply chain

Project stakeholders (e.g., senior management, budget managers) provide oversight and guidance. Examples of stakeholder functions: Finance, Manufacturing, Process development, Program management (commercial), Quality, R&D, Regulatory affairs, Technical services.

Technology transfer team leads and project stakeholders should define the appropriate governance and controls, generally covering:

  • Scope of the project
  • Timelines and key milestones
  • Resources and budget
  • Success criteria
  • Reporting and escalation structure
  • Key decision gates/approval process
  • Change and risk management processes
  • Quality management

Communication between the members of the team can be more effective when team members:

  • From both units have a similar level of knowledge and experience of the techniques involved
  • Have good team working skills
  • Have the time to ensure that actions from risk/gap assessment are progressed
  • Define mutually accepted means of communication and collaboration (face-to-face kickoffs + regular teleconferences)
  • Acknowledge and manage cultural differences

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Project Manager vs. Technical Lead：這兩個角色在技術轉移中有不同職責。PM 管交付、時程、資源協調；Technical Lead 管技術正確性。指南提醒，若由同一人兼任，當技術問題緊急時，PM 職責容易被排擠——這是很常見的失敗模式。

Core Team vs. Stakeholders：核心團隊（Core Team）是日常執行者，利害關係人（Stakeholders）是監督和提供資源的高層。兩者都必須在 Phase 1 就識別，確保資源和決策支持。

比喻說明 Analogy

技術轉移團隊就像一場外科手術：主刀醫師（Technical Lead）專注技術執行；麻醉師、護士、協調員各司其職；手術室主任（Project Manager）確保整個流程順暢。如果主刀醫師還要兼任協調員，手術品質必然下降。

重點提示 Key Notes

QA 的角色：請注意，QA 被列在核心團隊（core team），而非只是利害關係人。這意味著 QA 應在 Phase 1 就深度參與，而非在 Phase 4 執行時才被動審查文件。早期 QA 參與可以預防大量後期的合規問題。

Regulatory Affairs 同在核心團隊：技術轉移的法規策略（哪些變更需要申請，哪些可以年度報告）必須從 Phase 1 就確定方向，不能等到快驗證完才詢問 RA 團隊。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 內部應建立「技術轉移 RACI 模板」，明確在每個階段，QA、RA、工程、分析等各部門各自的 Responsible / Accountable 角色。建議在每個新案的 Kick-off Meeting 上正式確認這份 RACI，並記錄在 meeting minutes 中。這份文件之後可作為爭議仲裁的基礎。

3.1.2 Developing a Charter｜制定技術轉移章程

原文內容 Original Text

The technology transfer team may develop a technology transfer charter. The charter can include:

  • Assumptions and constraints
  • Sites impacted (sending and receiving) — scope including overview of the project and business drivers; items out of scope are equally important to document
  • Objectives and technical success criteria (see Chapter 2) — e.g., number of batches required, anticipated yield, successful registration and launch
  • Roles and responsibilities — clear understanding of expectations for each leadership role, deliverables for functional areas (documentation, testing, disposition, stability)
  • Technology transfer team — who is within the team from each unit, sub-team requirements, meeting frequency

    • Manufacturing plan and sampling requirements

  • Regulatory strategy
  • Change control procedure
  • Technology transfer completions and formal transfer to clinical and/or commercial operations

However, if the sending unit is, for example, in production, utilizing methods transferred, under regulatory agency review, etc., then sending to receiving reporting will be equally important."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — Charter 的本質

Charter（章程）：是技術轉移啟動的正式協議文件，通常是高層次的「合作框架」，而非詳細的執行計畫。Charter 確立的是「Why（為何轉移）、Who（誰負責什麼）、What（轉移範疇）、When（關鍵里程碑）」，而非技術細節的「How」。

Charter vs. TT Proposal vs. TT Plan：
Charter = 高層次框架協議（1-5頁，供高層簽署）
TT Proposal = 詳細技術要求文件（覆蓋 Phase 2）
TT Plan = 逐項執行計畫（覆蓋 Phase 3）

比喻說明 Analogy

Charter 就像是建築工程的「合約書」，TT Proposal 是「設計圖」，TT Plan 是「施工日誌」。三者功能不同，不能互相替代。很多 CDMO 犯的錯誤是把所有東西塞進一份「TT Protocol」，結果既不夠高層也不夠細緻，合約談不清楚，技術也沒說明白。

重點提示 Key Notes

「Out of Scope」同樣重要：指南特別強調，不在轉移範疇內的事項同樣需要書面化在 Charter 中。這防止日後客戶（送出單位）要求「這個不也應該包含在轉移裡嗎？」——明確的 out-of-scope 是 CDMO 防範範疇蔓延（scope creep）的重要工具。

偏差雙向回報：注意指南要求，偏差（deviation）不只由接收單位向送出單位報告，若送出單位也在使用同一套方法或製程，則反向報告也同樣重要。CDMO 應在 Quality Agreement 和 Charter 中明確規定這個雙向溝通機制。

Phase 2 (3.2) — Consolidate Knowledge & Develop Technology Transfer Proposal｜整合知識與制定技術轉移提案

原文內容 Original Text

3.2.1 Technology Transfer Proposal

A technology transfer proposal may be useful to define, facilitate review, and garner agreement of incomplete elements of the charter. In addition, prior to developing a detailed execution plan, a proposal can be used to develop detailed requirements and acceptance criteria for either key, or yet undefined, aspects of the plan.

The items listed below are recommended for inclusion in the technology transfer proposal:

  • HSE required assessments and known impacts
  • Overview of project and business drivers
  • List of team members and management/budget approvers (stakeholders)
  • Broad objectives and deliverables — projected clinical manufacture (successful number of runs), agreed process upgrades, successful registration
  • High level timeline and resources needed — divided into phases with "approval to proceed" at each phase end; realistic timeline (not assuming 100% success); significant capital requirements and estimated FTE; responsibility for funding
  • Issue resolution pathways and governance, including escalation
  • Validation strategy
  • Regulatory strategy — product/process comparability, stability, process validation; regulatory agency meetings and filing dates
  • Assumptions — raw material availability, successful analytics transfer, acceptable process changes, data system compatibility, facility fit, site utilities
  • Potential risks — FTE competition, unfavorable clinical/regulatory outcomes, gaps in process understanding, inability to mitigate facility fit, limitations in analytical support, comparability issues
  • Identification of information needed for the technology transfer package
  • Opportunities for receiving unit personnel to observe the process at the sending unit (tacit knowledge)
  • Requirements for disposition, storage, shipping, and forward processing

Note: Where a drug substance or drug product has received a marketing authorization, the regulatory strategy may be a key contributory factor in the strategic intent of the project.

Both the sending unit and the receiving unit should review and agree on the technology transfer proposal.

3.2.2 Technology Transfer Package

The technology transfer package should be organized efficiently to provide easy access to necessary information. It should also be comprehensive, so that a receiving unit can become self-sufficient in running a process or analytical procedure.

Basic requirements for the technology transfer package include:

  • HSE related requirements — prioritize HSE impact assessments early (long lead activities such as facility modification, regional licenses, training/monitoring programs)
  • Business requirements
  • Product specific requirements, particularly CQAs and control strategy and how these will be delivered at the receiving unit
"Note: It is not the role of technology transfer to identify quality attributes and process parameters and their relationships; this is the basis of the control strategy which is typically defined during process development. The control strategy is required to be available to the receiving unit to provide the scientific rationale which supports the manufacturing process and supporting analytical methods."
  • Process flow diagrams, unit operations, parameters and material attributes, design space (where used), process trends, detailed historical data (including sequence and justification of any process changes), risk assessments, identified CPPs and CQAs
  • Facilities capabilities assessment relative to the ability to implement and consistently execute requirements of the control strategy

The technology transfer team should review the completed technology transfer package and request SME input, as appropriate, to ensure that all relevant knowledge is captured. The team should also summarize the available knowledge and conduct a high level technical risk assessment.

The technology transfer team should ensure that the receiving unit understands the information available and can obtain clarifications. The knowledge transfer should take into account the level of expertise at both units.

For transfers to an external partner or CMO, the transferring organization should determine what kinds of intellectual property it would consider to be confidential and determine how to redact documents appropriately.

The technology transfer package information will be used by team members to work on a detailed process description and list of requirements for the receiving unit to execute the process. The team should then work through this list as part of a gap analysis, to identify, assign scores (if needed), and propose mitigation activities for gaps. The information gathered from the process description and gap analysis should be incorporated into a detailed technology transfer plan.

核心概念解析 — TT Package 的「食譜」本質

Technology Transfer Package（技術轉移套件）：這是讓接收單位能夠獨立執行製程的完整知識集合。必要的核心內容包括：

  • 製程描述與歷史（含所有製程變更的理由）
  • CPP 設定值與可接受範圍
  • CQA 規格與管控策略
  • 分析方法及設備規格
  • 已知失效模式（known failure modes）
  • 設計空間（如有）
  • 設施能力評估

若任何一項缺失，接收單位就像拿到一份缺頁食譜——可能做出來，但必然存在隱患。

比喻說明 Analogy

TT Package 就像是特許加盟連鎖店（franchise）給加盟主的「完整操作手冊」。手冊必須詳細到讓新加盟主即使從未接受過親自訓練，也能做出與總部相同水準的產品。若手冊缺少某個秘密醬料的配方，那個加盟店的出品必然不達標。

重點提示 Key Notes

TT 不是用來「定義」CQA 的：指南明確提醒，技術轉移的目的是「傳遞」控制策略，而非「建立」它。CQA 和製程參數之間的關係應在製程開發階段就已確定。若 CDMO 承接的案子，送出單位連 CQA 都說不清楚，這是一個嚴重的紅旗——代表送出單位的製程理解本身就不充分。

IP 機密性管理：對外部 CMO（即 CDMO）的轉移，送出單位需要決定哪些資訊屬於機密 IP，並進行適當的文件脫敏（redact）。CDMO 應在合約中明確要求獲得「足夠執行製程的完整資訊」，同時尊重客戶的 IP 保護需求。必要時，以技術諮詢服務費取代文件交付。

TT Package 核心內容清單

必備（Must Have）
├─ 製程描述 + 製程歷史（含變更理由）
├─ CPP 設定值、範圍、設計空間
├─ CQA 規格 + 控制策略科學根據
├─ 分析方法 + 設備規格（HPLC 型號等）
├─ 已知失效模式（risk register）
└─ HSE 要求與特殊操作說明

應備（Should Have）
├─ 供應商資訊（原料、試劑）
├─ 製程流程圖（PFD）
├─ 設施能力評估報告
└─ 前次驗證/確認摘要

建議備（Nice to Have）
├─ 操作技巧說明（tacit knowledge 文件化）
└─ 歷史批次數據（APR/PQR 摘要）

Phase 3 (3.3) — Identify Risks, Conduct Risk Assessments & Develop TT Plan｜識別風險、執行風險評估與制定轉移計畫

原文內容 Original Text

Following approval of a proposal for the technology transfer and exchange of the technology transfer package, the transfer team should perform risk assessments on identified gaps between the process or procedure requirements and the receiving unit capabilities and practices.

The technology transfer package can be used by both units to initiate risk assessments and begin developing the technology transfer plan for process or method implementation, along with the information, data, or work that will be required to ensure the technology can be successfully executed at the receiving unit. These assessments should compare the process or procedure history with the receiving unit capabilities and operations to identify gaps or misalignments that will require modifications.

In addition, the receiving unit should perform assessments of the following:

  • Process/method performance and validation packages
  • Raw materials/reagents for supply risk
  • Impact of incoming technology
  • Impact of changes needed as part of the implementation

This planning phase concludes once the sending and receiving units have completed the required assessments and have initiated plans to either remediate identified risks or receive risk acceptance from an appropriate governance.

Planning and execution of a technology transfer should include a comprehensive risk assessment process (i.e., risk identification, risk analysis, and risk evaluation as per ICH Q9), followed by risk control (i.e., risk reduction and risk acceptance) and development of a detailed action plan to mitigate risks.

"As part of a technology transfer, QRM is the documented procedure by which differences between sending unit and receiving unit can be identified and managed. Risk assessment is the process by which the criticalities of these differences are qualitatively or quantitatively estimated and prioritized. Once key gaps that require mitigation of risk are determined, a specific action plan for each gap should be defined and performed as part of the technology transfer execution."

The aim of QRM is to lower risks to acceptable levels. Where this is not possible or readily achievable, the risks are presented to the stakeholders for their approval to proceed "at risk" to the process qualification stage. Any differences between the sending unit and the receiving unit have the potential to increase risk to the receiving unit's ability to demonstrate product realization, cause delays, increase technology transfer costs, and have negative business impacts.

Sufficient time should be provided before technology transfer execution to identify and address differences. The sending unit and the receiving unit should perform these activities to help to improve knowledge transfer. These activities can promote teambuilding and improve future communication during later stages of a technology transfer.

It is usually preferable to address issues prior to the process qualification exercise, as opposed to during. For instance, if a process change is required due to conditions/constraints at the receiving unit, risk could be better defined and potentially mitigated by conducting studies in the small scale process model to examine the effects of the change.

It is advised that risk identification, risk analysis, and mitigation be conducted as early as possible since some risk mitigation activities may have long lead times and thus could significantly delay technology transfer. Although site selection is not discussed in this Guide, facility fit and site expertise are important factors to consider as they can have significant impact on the timeline and costs.

This risk assessment should be used to define the overall transfer approach.

3.3.1 Identifying Risks

As noted in Section 1.2, effective knowledge management (including tacit knowledge) is critical for effective risk management. Teams are advised to critically review information and knowledge utilized in risk identification, e.g., gaps in development reports.

Identification of risks is typically performed by appropriate SMEs and ad hoc members from the sending unit and the receiving unit (i.e., process/technical experts, manufacturing, quality, facility and process engineers, automation, materials/procurement, HSE, analytical, Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls (CMC) regulatory, etc.).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — Gap Analysis 的邏輯

Gap Analysis（差距分析）：系統性比較送出單位製程要求（what is needed）與接收單位現有能力（what is available）之間的差距。差距可能來自：

  • 設備差距：接收單位使用不同廠牌/型號的設備，其操作原理可能不同
  • 原料差距：同一原料可能來自不同供應商，規格略有差異
  • 分析平台差距：HPLC 系統、管柱型號、軟體版本不同
  • 法規歷史差距：接收單位可能沒有送出單位的法規核准歷史
  • 人員能力差距：接收單位人員對特定技術的熟悉程度

比喻說明 Analogy

Phase 3 的 Gap Analysis 就像搬家前的「家具清點」：你在新家有什麼？你需要把什麼從舊家搬過去？什麼需要新買？哪些舊東西在新家放不下？這個清點工作必須在搬家前（Phase 3）完成，而非搬進去後才發現床根本進不了門（Phase 4 批次失敗）。

風險類別框架 Risk Category Framework

技術風險（Technical Risk）
├─ 製程穩健性（process robustness）
├─ 設備等效性（equipment equivalency）
├─ 原料相容性（raw material compatibility）
└─ 分析方法轉移等效性

組織風險（Organizational Risk）
├─ 知識流失（knowledge loss / staff turnover）
├─ 人員能力缺口
└─ 文化差異造成的溝通障礙

法規風險（Regulatory Risk）
├─ 變更申請要求（variation filing）
├─ 可比性數據需求
└─ 不同市場的法規差異

時程風險（Timeline Risk）
├─ 長前置期活動（long lead items）
├─ 關鍵路徑延誤
└─ 資源競爭（FTE competition）

重點提示 Key Notes

「先做小規模研究」的黃金原則：若接收單位因設備或條件限制需要製程變更，指南建議在小規模模型中先研究變更的影響，而非直接在 GMP 批次中實驗。這在 CDMO 的無菌充填轉移中特別重要——灌裝參數調整應先在工程批（engineering run）中驗證。

「At Risk」決策：若某些風險無法在執行前完全緩解，必須由適當的治理層級（governance）正式批准「帶風險進行」（proceed at risk）。這個決策必須書面記錄，不能是口頭協議。CDMO 在接受此類「at risk」決策時，應確保客戶（送出單位高層）也簽署確認，明確風險由誰承擔。

長前置期活動要提早識別：某些風險緩解行動本身就需要很長時間（如設備採購、法規申請、原料供應商轉換）。若到 Phase 3 才發現這些需求，可能已無法在既定時程內完成。Phase 2 結束時就應初步識別這些長前置期項目。

實務應用 Practical Application — CDMO 接收單位視角

CDMO 在執行 Phase 3 時，建議從四個維度系統性評估風險：

  • 技術維度：比對設備規格表、確認製程參數範圍是否在接收設備的操作能力內
  • 分析維度：確認每個分析方法所需的設備型號、試劑、管柱是否與接收單位一致
  • 法規維度：確認在目標市場，此次設備/地點變更是否觸發變更申請，預估審批時間
  • 人員維度：評估操作團隊對此技術的熟悉程度，制定培訓計畫

每個識別的差距應在 Risk Register 中記錄：差距描述、嚴重性評分（S）、發生機率（O）、可偵測性（D）、計算 RPN，以及提議的緩解行動和負責人/完成時限。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 在無菌充填製程的技術轉移中，哪類風險（技術、組織、法規、時程）通常是最難提前識別的？
  2. 若送出單位同意「帶風險進行」，CDMO 應如何在合約中保護自己，確保風險責任清晰？
  3. 長前置期活動（long lead items）的典型例子有哪些？如何在 Phase 2 就提早識別？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 成功標準三維度：技術（製程性能、方法等效）+ 法規（可比性數據、提交準時）+ 商業（COGM、時程、OEE）— 必須在轉移開始前書面定義，不能事後才評定。
  • 文件層次：Charter（高層協議，含 out-of-scope 條款）→ TT Proposal（詳細要求）→ TT Plan（逐項執行計畫）。三者功能不同，混用會導致範疇蔓延。
  • TT Package 是轉移成敗的關鍵：必須涵蓋製程歷史、CPP/CQA、控制策略、分析方法、已知失效模式、設施評估。任何缺失都是潛在的批次失敗種子。
  • Gap Analysis 系統化：從設備、原料供應商、分析平台、法規歷史四個維度比對差距，每個差距在 Risk Register 中記錄嚴重性、機率、可偵測性和緩解行動。
  • 長前置期項目提早識別：Phase 3 中任何緩解行動若需要超過 4 週的準備時間，應立即列為高優先級並啟動，否則將成為整個時程的瓶頸。
  • QA 與 RA 從 Phase 1 就深度參與：而非等到執行後才審查，是縮短整體 TT 週期、避免後期補救的關鍵實踐。
