9.5.1 Biologics Case Study #1 — Transfer of a Phase 2 mAb Drug Substance 9.5.1 生物製劑案例 #1 — Phase 2 mAb 原料藥轉移

原文內容 Original Text

ISPE Tech Transfer | p104–106

The first two case studies are interrelated in that they involve two products, one CMO company, and three CMO sites. The first case study describes a product in early phase 2 development that held significant clinical potential to move to a pivotal study. With the advent of breakthrough therapy designation and accelerated product development, the timelines for process optimization and the timeframe for gaining manufacturing experience to achieve a robust and reproducible manufacturing process is significantly reduced. Therefore, the process was transferred from the client's internal early phase manufacturing site to a US-based commercial CMO (site A) fairly early in clinical development.

This CMO was selected for the following reasons:

  • Commercial experience
  • Appropriate scale – 2000 L single-use bioreactors (SUB)
  • Available capacity – manufacturing slots available with ~12 months lead time
  • Good compliance history – FDA inspected as well as multiple other regulatory agency inspections
  • Technical skill/capability
  • Cost competitive

The client's internal manufacturing train was 250 L and 1,000 L, so transfer and scale-up to a 2,000 L scale was deemed low risk. However, manufacturing at the client site was performed with a low productivity cell line. Development of a new higher productivity cell line and establishment of a new Master Cell Bank (MCB) was nearing completion at the start of the technology transfer. Limited development work was performed at the client site and technology transfer was initiated prior to having completed full process development. Utilization of a Research Cell Bank (RCB) for initiation of technology transfer was also required since the MCB was not available yet.

Changes were made to the downstream process to enable facility fit and to improve process efficiency. A focused development and optimization approach leveraging CMO expertise and experience was taken for the downstream process to enable timely transfer of the process to GMP manufacturing. Changes in the downstream process included addition of a pH treatment step, change in depth filters, change in sequence of operations (Sartoclear® P filtration step moved from post to pre-SP-Sepharose® column chromatography), change in viral filter, and removal of a dilution step prior to loading the SP-Sepharose® column. An engineering run was performed to shake down the process and ensure process changes were effective and successfully scaled up to the manufacturing scale (2,000 L).

  • Spirit of partnership and flexibility; commitment to improve and resolve issues

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Breakthrough Therapy Designation（突破性療法指定）：FDA 給予臨床意義重大藥品的加速審核通道。後果是：開發時程壓縮，製程還沒完全確認就得啟動技術轉移。這對 CMO 而言是常態挑戰。

Research Cell Bank (RCB) vs. Master Cell Bank (MCB)：RCB 是研究用細胞庫，尚未完全符合 GMP 要求；MCB 是正式 GMP 細胞庫。TT 理論上應以 MCB 啟動，但 MCB 未就緒時只能先用 RCB，並需加強測試確認等效性。

Facility Fit（設施適配）：讓送出方的製程適應接收方的設備配置，包括設備尺寸、操作順序、現有耗材類型。不是「複製」製程，而是「移植」製程。

比喻說明 Analogy

這像是一位主廚（client）還在研發菜單（製程開發未完成），就把食譜送到新餐廳（CMO）去練習。新廚師不但要學食譜，還要同步改良食譜讓它適合新廚房的爐灶尺寸（facility fit）。這種「邊飛邊換引擎」的模式風險極高，需要雙方都有極強的技術能力和溝通機制。

重點提示 Key Notes

問題一：製程發展未完成就轉移。這是最高頻的生物製劑 TT 失敗起點。本案的解法是在 CMO 端同步進行製程優化——前提是 CMO 必須有足夠的製程開發能力。選 CMO 時「process development capability」不是加分項，是必要條件。

問題二：EU 病毒清除合規缺口。CMO 有 FDA 認證但缺 EU QP 認證，直接影響歐洲市場的藥品申請。多市場申請時，合規資格必須在 CMO 選擇階段就確認，事後補救代價極高。

問題三：批次放行流程不熟悉。客戶不了解 CMO 的批次審查機制，導致延遲。解法是靠 CMO 的靈活性，而非流程設計。這是一個「人治」解法，長期有風險。

CDMO 實務應用 Practical Application

若你的 CDMO 接到一個「breakthrough therapy」mAb 案件，製程開發尚未完成：

  • 立刻確認：MCB 預計完成日期，若延誤，RCB 的補充測試項目有哪些？
  • 設置「製程鎖定（process lock）」里程碑——在哪個節點之後不再接受送出方的改動請求？
  • 確認 CMO 端是否有 Ambr® 或類似高通量篩選系統，能夠承接優化工作？
  • 確認 EU QP 合規狀況——若客戶有歐洲市場計畫，此項必須在合約前確認。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼用 RCB 而非 MCB 開始 TT 工作是一種風險？需要哪些額外測試來降低此風險？
  2. 本案的 15 個月時程達成了嗎？有延誤嗎？延誤的根本原因是什麼？
  3. 缺乏 Joint Steering Committee 在早期造成了什麼問題？你的 CDMO 是否有類似的治理結構？
9.5.2 Biologics Case Study #2 — Late Clinical Phase mAb Transfer from One CMO to Another 9.5.2 生物製劑案例 #2 — 晚期臨床階段 mAb 從一家 CMO 轉移至另一家

原文內容 Original Text

ISPE Tech Transfer | p107–109

The second case study involves the mAb intermediate component of an antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) in late stage clinical development. Technology transfer was driven by the need to move the mAb manufacture from the originally intended commercial CMO because of that company's decision to decommission the bioreactors that were planned for commercial manufacture of the product. In addition, there was no viable alternative available at the original CMO, so identification and selection of a new CMO was required.

Based on the prior experience with the CMO (site A) described in Biologics Case Study #1, another site in that CMO's network (site B) was selected for the following reasons:

  • Although site A was originally approached based on prior positive experience, capacity constraints indicated they could not meet the required ~12 month timeline. The client was directed to site B, a new facility in the CMO's network that had capacity and could accommodate the timeline.
  • Although the distance to site B (outside the US) was considered a potential challenge, the proximity of a CMO development site in the US (site C) was deemed a compensating attribute. Supporting optimization and small scale studies would be performed at site C.
  • CMO site B's host country offered financial incentive to perform pharmaceutical R&D activities in country.
  • Having multiple products with a single CMO was anticipated to provide efficiencies from a contractual, quality, and overall management perspective. However, there is also an associated risk with depending on a single CMO; this risk was mitigated by the fact that the CMO had a global footprint with multiple manufacturing sites in different countries.

At the time of initiating the technology transfer activities, the client was still in the process of establishing a new cell line suitable to meet regulatory expectations for commercialization. Utilization of a RCB for initiation of technology transfer was required because the MCB was unavailable due to delays in testing and challenges from differences in requirements for the receipt of new cell lines at the various CMO sites. Implementation of a modified filtration step following low pH viral inactivation was also required. However, due to time constraints, optimization prior to manufacturing was not possible. Based on the CMO's experience with the filter, it was considered low risk, and it was agreed that optimization could occur at a later date. Finally, a new bulk drug substance container was introduced to accommodate a change in storage temperature from 2–8°C to ≤ -65°C.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Antibody-Drug Conjugate (ADC，抗體藥物複合物)：由 mAb 與細胞毒性小分子藥物透過連接子（linker）結合而成。製造上分兩段：mAb 生產（本案）+ 偶聯（conjugation）。因此 mAb 的轉移成功直接影響整個 ADC 計畫。

Person-in-Place（現場駐點人員）：客戶派駐在 CMO 現場的技術代表，可即時溝通、解決問題。本案明確指出其價值巨大。在跨國轉移中，這幾乎是必要的，尤其在首批 GMP 生產前。

CMO 整合後的「文化差異」風險：多個 CMO 透過併購形成網路，各站點的 SOP、文化、質量體系尚未統一，是一個真實但常被低估的風險。

比喻說明 Analogy

「單一 CMO 網路」的承諾，就像連鎖餐廳承諾「每家分店都一樣」。但若這個連鎖品牌是最近收購了十家不同風格的獨立餐廳，廚師的訓練、食材供應商、甚至點餐系統都不同，吃起來的體驗就會有天壤之別。客戶需要主動了解每個「分店」的實際狀況，而不是只看品牌名稱。

重點提示 Key Notes

國際原料運輸的隱形風險：一個在美國國內完全無限制運輸的培養基成分，跨國運輸時可能被歸類為「危險品」，需要特殊包裝、文件、甚至航空禁運。這類供應鏈風險必須在 CMO 選定後立即進行全面評估，不能等到製造時才發現。

容器儲存溫度變更（2–8°C → ≤-65°C）：看似是小改動，實際上牽涉容器相容性、穩定性數據、運輸驗證，以及下游 ADC 偶聯製程的接收條件。每一個步驟都必須有數據支持。本案以廠商白皮書加短期穩定性研究作為依據，是務實但需記錄充分風險評估的做法。

多站點 CMO 網路風險評估框架

多 CMO 站點風險 =
  (各站點的技術熟悉度差異)
+ (SOP/QMS 整合完成度)
+ (跨國法規與供應鏈複雜度)
+ (溝通時區與語言障礙)

緩解措施：
  Person-in-place + 明確的跨站點溝通結構
  + 獨立的技術子團隊（upstream / downstream / analytical）

CDMO 實務應用 Practical Application

若你是接收方 CMO（site B），首次與客戶合作且需在 12 個月內完成首批 GMP 批次：

  • 第一步：確認所有原料的進口許可與運輸分類（尤其是有機溶劑或特殊培養基成分）
  • 第二步：評估自身設備與客戶製程的落差（無離心機？使用單次性層析系統？），先做小規模模擬確認可行性
  • 第三步：建議客戶派遣 Person-in-Place，至少在 GMP 前的 Engineering Run 期間全程駐守
  • 第四步：確認客戶的 QA 在首批 GMP 生產前就深度參與，避免批次放行時才發現質量體系認知差距

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 本案選擇 CMO site B（而非 site A）的主要原因是什麼？其中哪個原因事後來看是最關鍵的？
  2. 「單一 CMO 網路」的商業優勢與風險各是什麼？如何在合約中設計 mitigation？
  3. 媒體成分被列為「危險品」這個問題，在 TT 流程的哪個階段應該被識別出來？
9.5.3 Biologics Case Study #3 — Buffer Formulation Discrepancy in Protein A Purification 9.5.3 生物製劑案例 #3 — Protein A 純化中緩衝液配方差異

原文內容 Original Text

ISPE Tech Transfer | p109–110

An mAb purification process was developed at a small biotechnology company and transferred to a CMO for evaluation at laboratory scale, prior to scale-up to a non-GMP pilot and first GMP clinical batch. The process consisted of a Protein A chromatography step, low pH virus inactivation, and pH adjustment followed by cation exchange and anion exchange chromatography steps.

At the sending unit, the product was eluted from Protein A with acetate buffer pH 3.2 and the product eluted from the column within the required range for the viral inactivation step. The eluate was pH adjusted to pH 5.0 for the cation exchange step using 1 part product to 4 parts titrant. Prior to manufacture of the first GMP batch, a laboratory scale and pilot scale evaluation was performed at the receiving unit. During the laboratory scale evaluation at the receiving unit, two issues arose with the Protein A step.

A root cause analysis (Ishikawa diagram) was performed on the Protein A step. A number of items were highlighted for further investigation between the two units, relating to elution buffer and titrant preparation. During the transfer, only the names of the buffers were shared and not the preparation details. Two key differences were identified:

  • At the sending unit, the elution buffer and the titrant were prepared using the trihydrate salt, compared to use of the anhydrous salt at the receiving unit.
  • At the sending unit, the elution buffer was titrated with hydrochloric acid, compared to titration with acetic acid at the receiving unit, which resulted in much higher buffering capacity.

Result: Formulation of the elution buffer with the trihydrate salt and adjustment of the acetate concentration resulted in the eluted product pH achieving the required pH range for viral inactivation. Preparation of the titrant with the trihydrate salt and adjustment of the acetate concentration resulted in the expected volume of titrant being added for pH adjustment to pH 5.0.

What Worked:

  • Good communication between technical SMEs at sending and receiving units: the unexpected performance of the Protein A step and potential causes were flagged for the receiving unit, so details of buffer and titrant preparation could be compared quickly and adjusted as the laboratory scale evaluation was ongoing
  • Formulation of the elution buffer facilitated preparation at scale in the GMP facility

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Trihydrate vs. Anhydrous Salt（三水合物 vs. 無水鹽）：同一種化學物質的兩種形態。三水合物含有結晶水，相同「克數」的三水合物含有較少的實際活性成分。若配方以「mg/mL」為準，用錯形態會導致緩衝能力（buffering capacity）不同，pH 行為因此出現偏差。

低 pH 病毒滅活（Low pH Viral Inactivation）：生物製劑的重要病毒清除步驟。Protein A 洗脫的 eluate 通常 pH 在 3–4 之間，需維持一定時間以確保病毒被滅活。若 pH 偏低，可能增加蛋白質變性風險；若偏高，病毒滅活效果可能不足。pH 控制精確性是這一步的核心。

Ishikawa Diagram（石川圖，魚骨圖）：結構化根本原因分析工具，系統性列出可能導致問題的各類原因（人、機、料、法、環境）。是 GMP 偏差調查的標準工具之一。

比喻說明 Analogy

這個案例就像兩個廚師都說「加一茶匙鹽」，但一個用的是粗鹽（trihydrate，顆粒大，一茶匙的重量少），另一個用的是細鹽（anhydrous，顆粒密，一茶匙的重量多）。食譜上只寫「一茶匙鹽」，沒有說明是哪種鹽，結果味道完全不同。這就是「只傳遞緩衝液名稱，不傳遞配製細節」的危險。

重點提示 Key Notes

這是最常見、也最容易預防的 TT 失敗模式之一：緩衝液配製細節未納入轉移文件。正確的 TT 文件應包含：鹽的化學形態（trihydrate / anhydrous）、滴定酸的種類、pH 調整操作順序、水質要求（WFI vs. purified water）。僅傳遞「緩衝液名稱」是不完整的技術轉移。

低 pH 滅活的 pH 範圍必須有安全邊際：本案 pH 比預期更低，雖仍在病毒滅活範圍內，但已接近可能造成產品損傷的區域。這種「剛好在範圍內」的情況，在驗證時必須確認操作空間（design space）足夠寬裕。

緩衝液轉移的完整文件清單

最低要求（必傳）：
  1. 緩衝液名稱及目標 pH / 濃度
  2. 鹽類的化學形態（trihydrate vs. anhydrous）
  3. 滴定所用酸/鹼的種類與濃度
  4. 配製順序（先加哪種成分）
  5. 水質規格（注射用水 WFI / 純化水）
  6. 過濾規格（0.2 μm，使用前 / 使用後）
  7. 貯存溫度與有效期限

推薦附加（降低誤解風險）：
  - 配製實例（以具體批量計算）
  - 已執行配製批次的 pH 實測範圍
  - 代表性緩衝曲線（titration curve）

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若你是接收方 QC，在接到送出方的緩衝液配方後，應該主動確認哪些資訊？
  2. 為什麼用鹽酸（HCl）滴定和用醋酸（acetic acid）滴定的緩衝能力不同？這對 Protein A 洗脫步驟有什麼影響？
  3. 這個問題在實驗室規模就被發現，如果直接進行 GMP 生產才發現，後果會是什麼？
9.5.4 Biologics Case Study #4 — Transfer of a Challenging IgM Molecule 9.5.4 生物製劑案例 #4 — 高難度 IgM 分子轉移

原文內容 Original Text

ISPE Tech Transfer | p110–112

This case study relates to transferring a process from a small biotechnology company with process development capabilities to a CMO with process development, scale-up, and GMP capabilities. A good relationship had been established between the two parties from collaborations on previous process transfers, process development, and GMP batch manufacture.

In this case study, the product was a very large, challenging antibody molecule (immunoglobulin M, IgM) which could not be purified using resins already available at the CMO. The plan was to evaluate the process at laboratory scale and scale-up to non-GMP pilot scale before manufacturing a GMP batch.

As there were no batch records or development reports available from the sending unit, the process was transferred using a transfer document developed at the CMO. In addition to process details, this transfer document included a request for information from the sending unit on product stability at high or low pH, any tendency to aggregate, evidence of instability in particular buffers, solubility issues at high or low conductivity, and highest concentration that the product has been demonstrated to be stable. In addition, SOPs at the CMO were outlined in this transfer document, including flow rate ranges (based on equipment available), resin cleaning conditions, and generic hold times. This helped to align procedures at both sites.

The process transferred to the CMO included a Protein A step, anion exchange (AEX) chromatography, and ultraviolet (UV) treatment. Final formulation development was ongoing during the transfer. Initially, the process was evaluated at 10 L bioreactor scale at the CMO, then scaled up and evaluated in two non-GMP pilot batches. SMEs from the sending unit were present during key processing steps at the receiving unit to be able to discuss, advise, and help resolve technical issues real time.

Result: The purification process was evaluated over a 12-month timeframe, at laboratory and non-GMP pilot scale. The final batch was manufactured with the revised, three chromatography step process. However, although product quality and impurity levels in drug substance were acceptable, the cumulative recovery was <10%. Consequently, cost of goods was excessive. At this point, the program was placed on hold by the client, due to other more promising molecules in their pipeline and the process was never transferred to GMP manufacturing.

What Worked:

  • Throughout the technology transfer process, both the sending and receiving units gained a huge amount of experience and maintained a good working relationship
  • Technical competence at both units — data compiled real time for sharing and discussing; SMEs worked together to resolve issues real time
  • New technologies were implemented which required support from vendors

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

IgM（免疫球蛋白 M）：分子量約為 IgG（mAb）的 5–10 倍，無法用標準 Protein A 親和力層析（因 Protein A 主要針對 IgG Fc 區域）純化。製程開發難度遠高於一般 mAb，每個步驟都充滿未知數。

Cumulative Recovery（累積回收率）：多個純化步驟的回收率連乘積。若每步 70%，三步後：70% × 70% × 70% = 34%。本案 <10% 意味著某些步驟回收率極低，導致成本效益無法成立。

UV Treatment for Virus Reduction（UV 病毒清除）：當分子太大無法通過 20 nm 奈米過濾膜（nanofiltration）時的替代病毒清除方案。但 UV 對大分子蛋白質可能促進聚集體（aggregates）形成，需要精確控制劑量和操作條件。

比喻說明 Analogy

IgM 的製程轉移就像嘗試用做西餐的廚房設備（標準 CMO 層析管柱）來做傳統糕點（IgM）。工具本來就不合用，每一步都要臨時找新方法、借新設備、改流程。做出來的糕點雖然「可以吃」（品質合格），但損耗太大（回收率 <10%），一塊糕點的成本是正常的 10 倍。老闆（客戶）最後決定換一道菜（其他管線分子）。這是一個無論技術上多努力，商業上仍然失敗的案例。

重點提示 Key Notes

沒有批次記錄就啟動轉移：這是本案最大的根本問題。送出方沒有批次記錄和開發報告，接收方必須自行開發轉移文件框架，要求送出方填寫關鍵產品知識（pH 穩定性、聚集傾向、溶解度等）。這個做法雖然彌補了部分缺口，但轉移過來的製程基本上是一個「待完成」的製程。

UV 病毒清除步驟在轉移前未驗證：送出方只是「建議」使用 UV，沒有任何實驗數據。這意味著接收方要在 CMO 端從零開發一個全新的病毒清除步驟，並且需要在非 GMP 規模進行評估（因為需要大量物料）。這是一個典型的「不完整製程轉移」。

<10% 回收率的商業意義：以 mAb 商業規模計算，若正常製程回收率為 60%，改善空間在 50 倍以上。低回收率直接推高 Cost of Goods (COGS)，在競爭性產品定價環境中幾乎無法立足。技術可行性 ≠ 商業可行性。

CDMO 實務應用 Practical Application

接到一個「沒有批次記錄」的非標準分子（如 IgM、双特異性抗體）詢價時：

  • 立即要求送出方填寫「分子特性問卷」：pH / 濃度穩定性、聚集傾向、特殊溶解度需求、已知製程問題
  • 評估自身是否有相應的設備（特殊填料、UV 設備、UF/DF 能力）
  • 在合約中明確區分：「技術轉移」vs.「製程開發代工」——後者風險更高、費用應更高
  • 設定「go/no-go」里程碑，以回收率、雜質去除率等指標為觸發點，避免無止境投入資源

  2. 如果你是 CMO 的項目主管，在哪個里程碑點應該向客戶提出「此製程可能無商業可行性」的警示？
  3. 「CMO 開發轉移文件，要求送出方填寫分子知識」這種做法有什麼優缺點？

ISPE Tech Transfer | p112

For the final case study, the process was transferred from one CMO where the process had been developed and manufactured to a second CMO with the intent of scaling up the process, due to increased material requirements beyond the equipment capabilities of the first CMO.

A SharePoint site was set up between the biotech company and the second CMO as a way of exchanging confidential information, including process development reports, executed batch records, in-process testing, drug substance testing, and trend analysis of the GMP batches for upstream and downstream processes. During the laboratory scale evaluation at the receiving unit, an issue arose on the second chromatography step in the purification process.

Table 9.9: Challenges and Resolutions

An initial investigation was performed. Process step details were compared between the batch records from the sending unit, the process description generated at the receiving unit, and data recorded in the laboratory notebook. Initial root causes considered were related to buffer pH and conductivity specifications as the ranges transferred were broad. However, all buffers prepared at the second CMO were within the required ranges.

Investigation resulted in an error in chromatography resin type used. The correct resin had been specified, yet the wrong resin was dispensed. A documentation and verification gap allowed an incorrect resin to be used without detection.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Letter of Intent（意向書）先啟動工作：在正式合約簽署前，以意向書為依據開始實驗工作。這在時間緊迫的生物製劑 TT 中相當普遍，但需注意：若後續談判破裂，雙方的費用歸屬和知識產權歸屬可能產生爭議。合約談判應與技術工作並行，不能無限期拖延。

Wrong Resin Used（使用錯誤填料）：正確填料規格已記載在製程文件中，但實驗室領料時發出了錯誤的填料，且沒有被檢查出來。這是一個純粹的操作和文件管控失敗，而非技術問題。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像醫院藥局開出了正確的藥名，但藥師從貨架上拿錯了藥瓶，因為沒有雙人確認機制，直接發給了護理師。病人吃錯藥不是因為醫生開錯處方，而是執行環節缺少了驗證步驟。在 GMP 生產中，任何物料的使用都需要「兩人確認 + 標籤驗證」作為防呆機制。

重點提示 Key Notes

「時程壓力」是最容易觸發程序跳步的因素：本案在收到技術轉移文件後 1 個月就開始實驗，10 週後就進行 pilot 批次。這種速度下，材料確認、程序審查容易被壓縮。「填料用錯了」這件事，在時間充裕的情況下很可能會在實驗前的 SOP 審查就被發現。

TT 文件的「傳遞 → 轉化 → 執行」三層驗證：本案的問題發生在「執行」層：正確規格已在文件中，但實際操作時沒有驗證領料是否符合規格。完整的 TT 體系需要確保每一層都有獨立的查核機制，包括：(1) 文件是否完整傳遞；(2) 接收方是否正確理解並轉化為本地 SOP；(3) 執行時是否按 SOP 操作並驗證物料。

CDMO 實務應用 Practical Application

針對「使用錯誤填料」這類執行層錯誤，CDMO 應建立：

  • 物料雙人確認制度：層析填料、培養基、緩衝液成分，在使用前需由第二人對照 SOP 驗證批號、規格、外觀。
  • 填料登記冊（resin log）：記錄每批填料的使用歷史、清洗次數、性能測試結果，確保正確追蹤。
  • 「超緊時程」紅旗機制：當 TT 計畫時程極度壓縮時，QA 應啟動強化監督模式——增加實驗前審查會議，確保速度不犧牲程序完整性。

9.6 Conclusions: Lessons Learned from Biologics Technology Transfer 9.6 結論：生物製劑技術轉移經驗教訓

原文內容 Original Text

ISPE Tech Transfer | Appendix 2 Synthesis

The five biologics case studies presented in this appendix — spanning Phase 2 mAb transfers, CMO-to-CMO late-stage transfers, purification process evaluations, and complex molecule development — collectively illustrate the unique complexity of biologics technology transfer compared to small molecule manufacturing.

Across all five case studies, several universal themes emerge:

Theme 1: Cell line and cell bank availability is a recurrent critical path item. In Case Studies #1 and #2, the unavailability of the Master Cell Bank (MCB) at the start of transfer required use of a Research Cell Bank (RCB), adding risk and requiring enhanced testing. Cell line readiness — including third-party cell bank testing timelines — must be factored into every biologic TT project plan as a potential critical path constraint.

Theme 2: Process development completion is prerequisite to successful transfer. Multiple case studies describe transfers initiated before process development was complete. While sometimes unavoidable under breakthrough therapy or accelerated development timelines, this approach requires a CMO partner with strong in-house development capability and a clear, contractually defined boundary between "technology transfer" and "process development."

Theme 3: Documentation detail — not just document titles — must be transferred. Case Study #3 demonstrates that transferring buffer names without preparation details led to a reproducible but unexpected pH result. Complete transfer packages must include chemical forms of salts, titration reagent identities, preparation sequences, water quality specifications, and working concentration ranges.

Theme 4: Facility fit and equipment equivalence require proactive small-scale modeling. All case studies involve some degree of facility fit work — changes in scale, equipment type, or process sequence. The successful transfers used small-scale modeling at a development site (site C in Case Study #2) to confirm process feasibility before committing to full-scale GMP manufacturing.

Theme 5: Communication infrastructure is as important as technical capability. Across every case study, the "What Worked" section highlights dedicated project management, regular cross-functional meetings (core team + SME sub-teams), documented action items, and — particularly for international transfers — person-in-place representation. These are not "soft" factors; they are structural enablers of transfer success.

Theme 6: Commercial feasibility must be assessed alongside technical feasibility. Case Study #4 demonstrates that a technically successful process development effort (achieving acceptable product quality) can still result in program termination if cumulative recovery is insufficient to support commercial cost-of-goods. Go/no-go criteria based on yield and COGS should be defined at project initiation.

Theme 7: Regulatory compliance scope must be confirmed before CMO selection. Case Study #1 reveals a gap in EU viral clearance certification that was not identified until late in the transfer. For products targeting multiple markets, regulatory compliance coverage (FDA, EMA, PMDA, etc.) must be a CMO selection criterion, not an afterthought.

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 案例 #1（Phase 2 mAb）：製程未完成就轉移，MCB 未就緒用 RCB，EU 合規缺口——這三個問題是生物製劑 TT 的高頻風險，需要 CMO 具備強大的製程開發能力與靈活的 QA 體系。
  • 案例 #2（CMO-to-CMO ADC 中間體）：跨國多站點 CMO 網路的隱性風險——整合程度不足、國際原料供應鏈、地區法規差異，Person-in-Place 是跨國轉移的關鍵成功因素。
  • 案例 #3（緩衝液配製差異）：「只傳遞名稱、不傳遞細節」是導致 Protein A 步驟 pH 異常的根本原因。完整的緩衝液 TT 文件必須包含鹽的化學形態和滴定試劑種類。
  • 案例 #4（IgM 困難分子）：技術上可以成功，商業上不可行。<10% 的累積回收率導致 COGS 過高，程序被叫停。TT 計畫必須設定商業可行性的 go/no-go 里程碑。
  • 案例 #5（融合蛋白 CMO-to-CMO）：時程壓力下的操作層缺失——使用錯誤層析填料。速度不能犧牲物料管控程序；Letter of Intent 先啟動的模式需要配套完善的文件確認機制。
  • 共同結論：生物製劑 TT 的成功需要「技術能力 + 文件完整性 + 溝通基礎設施 + 商業判斷」四者兼備。任何一個維度的缺失，都可能導致整個計畫失敗。
