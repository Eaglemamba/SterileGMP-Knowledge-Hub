3.4 Operational Readiness — 操作準備度

原文內容 Original Text ISPE TT 3rd Ed.

3.4 Operational Readiness

The goal of the operational readiness phase is to achieve a state of readiness required to move into the qualification phase, as described in Section 3.5. Both the process and methods are required to be in a state of control prior to entering qualification phase. A critical review of the operational readiness should be performed and formally documented.

Analytical methods are required to be qualified prior to full scale process qualification in order to be able to measure process performance. In some cases, method qualification may be concurrent with process qualification; in this case, the risk to the overall qualification risk must be documented and accepted by management.

Closure of actions defined by gap analysis and corresponding risk assessments drive the requirements to complete this phase.

Operational readiness activities required may include:

  • Additional process or method development
  • Equipment installation, modification, or re-validation
  • General commissioning, qualification and validations as needed (refer to ISPE Baseline® Guide: Commissioning and Qualification)
  • Engineering runs
  • Procurement
  • Training runs or training of staff

Having identified gaps and risks, then subsequently prioritizing activities, technology transfer operational readiness may include the following:

  • Facility modifications identified in risk identification/gap analysis, including appropriate equipment validations
  • Testing of full scale manufacturing equipment to ensure proper function, e.g., mixing studies, buffer or water runs, cleaning studies, etc.

In the case of cleaning, it is recommended that a comprehensive review of the requirements (e.g., soil types, required cleanability limits, and methods of detection) are compiled into a planning document. It is highly likely that changes in equipment type, scale-up, and process optimization implemented during the transfer will require new cleaning and monitoring methods, sample types, and corresponding analytical method qualifications.

As noted in Sections 2.2 and 3.3.1, effective knowledge management is crucial throughout all the previous phases. Operation readiness requires generation of documentation at the receiving unit required to implement and control changes necessary for implementation of the transferred process and/or methods. Diligence in document review and reconciliation in this phase is of paramount importance prior to proceeding to the qualification phase.

Examples of required documentation are as follows:

  • Flow chart of process steps
    • Quantities of all materials and stoichiometry of the starting materials, intermediates, and reagents
    • Specific processing conditions (e.g., times, temperatures, and pressures)
    • Sequence of activities (e.g., order of addition of reagents)
    • Expected theoretical yield and output weight/volume of product
    • CPPs, i.e., factors impacting product quality
    • Permitted tolerance ranges for key parameters (e.g., yield impacting parameters)
    • Detail of other materials and by-products generated and their fate
    • Mass balance information
  • Development and approval of receiving unit manufacturing instruction documents (batch records, SOPs, automation recipes, etc.)
    • A clearly defined structure for review and approval of the manufacturing documents should be agreed upon by the sending unit and receiving unit, including expectations for duration and number of reviews. A well-organized system for tracking and managing edits throughout the process should be used by the teams.
  • Implementation of small scale laboratory models at receiving unit
    • As part of the transfer, small scale models of some or all the unit operations may be transferred to the receiving unit. These can be used for process training before large scale runs begin, for additional process development work identified in the risk identification/gap analysis, and can also be used for efficient troubleshooting during the initial large scale runs.
    • A suggested practice is to use the small scale model in a "satellite" mode alongside the initial large scale runs with the same buffers or media. This can help to troubleshoot problems that may be observed at scale by providing a control for some of the parameters.
    • An overall assessment comparing the small scale runs to the production runs should be documented or referenced in a campaign summary report.
  • Process training
    • Training should be conducted by SMEs prior to the start of the GMP campaign. It may be helpful to have some elements of this training led by personnel from the sending unit where they may have more extensive process experience. It also may be useful to have personnel from the sending unit as support staff during operation of first full scale batch.
  • Models
    • Various models can be used by companies to develop pharmaceutical products. The rigor and documentation associated with the technical transfer of such models should be commensurate with the complexity and risk associated with their implementation and intended use.
    • Where models are established only for the purpose of understanding a process and its unit operations, it is usually sufficient to transfer the model as part of the knowledge base for the product, as these models are not part of ongoing control decisions during production. Where models are established as elements of a control strategy for ongoing production, consideration should be given to scale, sampling, and instrument dependencies as applicable.
    • In addition, procedures to calibrate, verify, maintain, if applicable validate, and update models should be established, as appropriate to the nature of the model being used.
  • Creation of real time processing models and data trending template
  • Identification of in-process controls for monitoring
  • Cleaning requirements per process step, including required analysis and forward processing criteria
  • Generation of sample plan

In a GMP environment, some of these activities may require additional risk assessments and change controls as a part of the activity.

Additionally, the operational readiness phase of the technology transfer project will include the generation of the documentation and/or electronic systems that will be used to execute the technology at the receiving unit. This activity should be supported through the information and deliverables that were included as a part of the technology transfer package generated in the pre-transfer planning phase.

The operational readiness phase of the technology transfer is complete when the process and analytical procedures are qualified. The sending and receiving units should be confident in the receiving unit's ability to successfully execute analytical procedures or manufacturing processes.

Following successful completion of the technology transfer operational readiness phase, the project will move into the process/procedure qualification phase of the project.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：操作準備度的五大支柱

ISPE 將 Operational Readiness（操作準備度）定義為「進入確認階段前必須達到的就緒狀態」。實務上，這可以分解為五個缺一不可的支柱：

  • 設備（Equipment）：安裝、確認（IQ/OQ/PQ）全部完成，清潔驗證已有計畫
  • 人員（Personnel）：製程 SME 完成培訓，GMP 記錄能力通過評估
  • 物料（Materials）：原料、耗材、標準品均已取得並合格，供應鏈就緒
  • 程序（Procedures）：批次記錄、SOP、自動化配方全部發布且經雙方審核
  • 法規（Regulatory）：必要的申請變更已提交，監管時間軸已確認

五個支柱全部亮綠燈才能啟動第一批次——這是最多延誤發生的地方。

比喻說明 Analogy

操作準備度就像醫院開刀房的「清單確認」流程：在開刀前，必須確認麻醉機（設備）可用、外科團隊（人員）到位、血袋（物料）準備好、手術程序（SOP）已核准、術前家屬同意書（法規文件）已簽妥。任何一項缺失，手術就不能開始——無論外科醫師多有經驗。技術轉移的第一批 GMP 批次，正是這台「手術」。

重點提示：為什麼大多數 TT 延誤發生在 Phase 4？

工程試驗（Engineering Runs）暴露真正的差距，而在 Phase 3 的風險識別中往往低估以下問題：

  • 清潔驗證方法在新設備幾何形狀下失效，需要重新開發分析方法
  • 原料資格（Vendor qualification）因供應商審核週期長而拖延
  • 批次記錄（BPR）的雙方審核輪次比預期多出 2-3 輪
  • 自動化配方在新平台移植後出現參數偏移，需要額外確認

給 CDMO 的警示：合約時程通常低估 Phase 4 的 buffer，這是最常見的商業糾紛來源。

實務應用：小規模衛星模型的 CDMO 戰略價值

ISPE 建議將小規模實驗室模型以「衛星模式」（satellite mode）與大規模生產同步運行。對 CDMO 而言，這個策略帶來三個競爭優勢：

  • 快速除錯：大規模批次出現異常時，可立即用小規模模型重現並隔離變數，不必等待下一批次
  • 知識留存：即使客戶撤出，CDMO 保有可執行的小規模模型，是未來再轉移的技術資產
  • 法規支援：衛星實驗室數據可作為 scale-down model 的確認依據，支持 FDA/EMA 的申請文件

重點提示：分析方法確認的時序風險

ISPE 明確指出：分析方法確認（Method Qualification）必須先於製程確認（Process Qualification）完成。若允許並行（concurrent），管理層必須正式接受並記錄此風險。

為何這是風險？若製程批次在方法尚未確認時啟動，萬一方法失效，批次結果將無法判讀——GMP 批次白跑，時間與成本雙輸。這正是清潔驗證分析方法轉移常被低估的陷阱。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若某 CDMO 在 Phase 4 發現設備清潔方法不適用於新型設備，最佳的風險處理路徑為何？是開始小規模清潔效果研究，還是直接要求客戶提供替代方法？
  2. 「衛星模式小規模模型」的數據需要多少才足以支持 Scale-down Model qualification？ICH Q5E 對生物製劑的可比性研究有何要求？
  3. 批次記錄的雙方審核輪次在合約中通常如何界定？超出輪次時，費用與時間責任應如何分配？
3.5 Process (Procedure) Qualification — 製程（程序）確認

原文內容 Original Text ISPE TT 3rd Ed.

3.5 Process (Procedure) Qualification

As noted in Section 3.3, a documented review of all readiness criteria and transfer requirements in the plan should be completed in a gated review with management oversight prior to proceeding to the process (procedure) qualification phase.

This phase may be executed through a demonstration of the technology at the receiving unit and a comparison of the process and product and data generated to ensure it aligns with expectations. This activity may be performed as a part of a formal qualification or validation exercise, in which case the data generated may be useful for supporting the regulatory approval processes.

The risk assessment from the technology transfer planning phases should be signed off and documented in accordance with the QMSs for both the sending and receiving units prior to proceeding to process (procedure) qualification. For further information, see ICH Q9 and the ISPE Guide Series: Product Quality Lifecycle Implementation (PQLI®). As previously noted in Section 3.3, risk assessments are most effective when utilized in phase gate reviews.

This phase should involve completion of the assessment of the data and product produced as a part of the transfer execution and identification of required mitigation plans to remediate any issues observed during qualification. The sending unit and receiving unit should define a plan for routine support of the technology at the receiving unit.

Execution of a qualification protocol should not begin until the protocol has been reviewed and approved by all appropriate departments, including the appropriate quality units. Any departures from the protocol should be made according to established quality procedures.

A report documenting and assessing adherence to the written qualification protocol should be prepared in a timely manner after the completion of the protocol. This report should:

  • Discuss and cross-reference all aspects of the protocol.
  • Summarize data collected and analyze the data, as specified by the protocol.
  • Evaluate any unexpected observations and additional data not specified in the protocol.
  • Summarize and discuss all non-conformances, such as deviations, aberrant test results, or other information that has bearing on the validity of the analytical procedure/process.
  • Describe in sufficient detail any corrective actions or changes that should be made to existing procedures and controls.
  • State a clear conclusion as to whether the data indicates the analytical procedure met the conditions established in the protocol and whether the analytical procedure is considered to be in a state of control. If not, the report should state what should be accomplished before such a conclusion can be reached.
  • Include all appropriate department and quality units review and approvals.
  • Provide the basis for continued process monitoring and control strategies.

It is recommended that a statistician or person with adequate training in statistical techniques help develop the data collection plan and any statistical methods and procedures used in measuring and evaluating the process or analytical method. If the process or analytical method qualification does not meet the acceptance criteria, the cause of the failure should be investigated and addressed before repeating the exercise.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Engineering Runs vs. PPQ 批次

這是 Phase 5 最重要的概念區別：

特性 Engineering Runs PPQ 批次
GMP 狀態 非正式，可失敗 正式 GMP，不得隨意廢棄
法規用途 學習與調整 作為法規申請的核心證據
數量 視需要而定 通常最少 3 批（FDA 建議）
偏差處理 記錄即可 每個偏差均需完整調查
放行 不放行市場 合格時可放行市場

PPQ（Process Performance Qualification）（製程性能確認）是 FDA Process Validation Guidance 的第二階段，也是法規核准的關鍵依據。

比喻說明 Analogy

Engineering Runs 就像廚師在正式開幕前的「試菜日」——可以試錯、調整配方，不對顧客收費。PPQ 批次則像是餐廳的「開幕首日」——每道菜都要完美，主廚（QA）必須簽名，失敗紀錄會留在衛生稽查檔案中，而且這批菜（產品）是要出售給病患的。兩者的失敗代價截然不同。

分析方法可比性的統計框架

分析可比性 (Analytical Comparability) 核心問題：
接收單位的分析方法，能否產生與送出單位
「統計上等效」的結果？

驗證層次（依 ICH Q2(R1) 邏輯）：
1. 精確度比較 (Precision Comparison)
   RSD(接收) vs RSD(送出) — F-test or equivalence test

2. 準確度比較 (Accuracy/Bias Comparison)
   Mean bias = |μ接收 - μ送出| / μ送出 × 100%
   通常接受標準: bias < 2% (small molecule)
                       bias < 5% (bioassay)

3. 平台比較 (Platform Comparison)
   n 樣品在雙方實驗室同步分析 → paired t-test 或
   Bland-Altman plot — 視覺化系統偏差

4. 不確定度評估 (Measurement Uncertainty)
   EU Annex 15 / USP <1033> 要求量化不確定度

統計支援不可或缺：ISPE 明確建議由具備統計背景的人員設計數據收集計畫。

重點提示：Concurrent Validation 的法規態度

在某些情境（如罕見疾病、緊急需求），FDA 允許「同步驗證」（Concurrent Validation）——即在確認批次完成全部分析前即放行批次。但這要求：

  • 在申請檔案（BLA/NDA/ANDA）中明確說明理由
  • 有強健的即時放行測試（RTRT）控制策略
  • 後續確認結果必須追溯補回並形成報告

ISPE 在此強調：無論採用哪種策略，風險評估必須由管理層正式書面接受。CDMO 若接受此要求，必須在合約中明確保留撤回批次的權利。

實務應用：PPQ 報告的「六大必備」

ISPE 列出 PPQ 報告必須涵蓋的六個要素，對 CDMO 而言，這同時也是交付客戶的核心文件框架：

  • 全面對應協議（cross-reference all aspects of protocol）
  • 數據摘要與統計分析（summarize and analyze data）
  • 未預期觀察的評估（unexpected observations）
  • 不符合項完整清單（non-conformances, deviations）
  • 糾正行動說明（corrective actions）
  • 明確結論聲明（clear conclusion）

這六項缺一不可；FDA 483 觀察中，「PPQ 報告結論不明確」是常見警告項目。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若 PPQ 第一批次出現一個 Critical Deviation（例如溫度超出規格），是否可以繼續執行第二批次，還是必須先完成 CAPA？ISPE 與 FDA 的立場各為何？
  2. 在生物製劑的技術轉移中，PPQ 批次通常需要幾批才足夠？「足夠」的判斷標準是什麼？
  3. 若接收單位的分析數據顯示系統偏差（systematic bias），但仍在規格內，這算轉移成功嗎？如何說服監管機構？
3.6 Finalize Transfer and Perform Review — 完成轉移與執行審查

原文內容 Original Text ISPE TT 3rd Ed.

3.6 Finalize Transfer and Perform Review

The final actions of the project should be focused on performing a review (sometimes called an after action review) to collect specific knowledge from the technology transfer. This should consider how each stage in the transfer went, how effective the communication and team work were, and whether any issues experienced during the qualification exercises were related to the effectiveness of the knowledge transfer.

The review may identify the need for a remediation plan and any ongoing support required from the sending unit. Where appropriate, key knowledge from the review should be shared with the teams involved in the technology transfer.

The complete evaluation of the success of the technology transfer through analysis of data or product (e.g., unexpected variability) generated as a part of the transfer execution will involve the sending and receiving units evaluating the performance of the technology, as well as the laboratory or manufacturing facility, and determining if any corrective action is required prior to declaring the transfer successful.

3.6.1 Planning for Transition of Support and Disengagement of Sending Unit

This can potentially include accountabilities and responsibilities for process or method improvements identified as a part of the transfer or through lifecycle management. There should be a clear transfer of responsibility to the receiving unit.

Agreement should be defined regarding the intended level of continuing support and involvement from the sending unit members of the technology transfer team.

Examples of aspects that should be considered for ongoing operations include:

  • The receiving unit will operate the specified process.
  • A change control procedure should be in place at the receiving unit.
  • Process deviations should be noted and evaluated for communication with the sending unit.
  • Process changes (continuous process improvement/lifecycle management) may be needed and initiated by either the sending or receiving unit. The initiated change must have prior agreement and be performed through a change control procedure. In some cases, process changes may require re-validation of the process. Global change control (e.g., when multiple sites are making the same product) is outside the scope of this document.
  • Process monitoring should be performed by the receiving unit. Any anomalous process trends should be evaluated for communication with the sending unit. If the product is manufactured at multiple sites, regular manufacturing review meetings should be conducted to troubleshoot processing problems between sites.

3.6.2 Support of Regulatory Activities

Post-transfer activities can include support of regulatory inspections or questions as a result of submissions associated with the transfer. Members from either the sending or receiving unit may be needed to provide assistance with regulatory activities by authoring documentation for filings or participating in site inspections.

3.6.3 Lessons Learned

An ongoing review during the transfer and/or a review at the conclusion of transfer phases that captures best practice and shared lessons learned should be performed. This should be documented and accessible to other teams from a continuous improvement perspective.

Where lessons learned are not captured during the execution of the transfer, a lessons learned meeting should be held as soon as possible after the completion of the process qualification. The meeting should provide an opportunity to review the technology transfer project and overall GMP performance. At a minimum, the meeting should involve members of the technology transfer team and focus on the successes and challenges faced throughout the project. It may be valuable to break out into specific areas that need to be addressed, for example:

  • Process
  • Documents
  • Analytics
  • Quality
  • Project management

The output from the meeting should be documented and shared with the technology transfer team.

The sending and receiving units should formally document lessons learned and determine future actions that would improve the success and efficiency of transfer projects or product commercialization.

Teams should strive to distinguish knowledge that is known (e.g., CQAs, CPPs, formal reports, Annual Product Review summaries, SOPs, etc.) from that which can be described as tacit (i.e., the knowledge that may be crucial for a successful transfer but is not actually written down.) Such tacit knowledge might include:

  • Historical knowledge
  • Informal knowledge
  • The way an operator or a laboratory analyst performs certain steps
  • Details of the order of operations, e.g. powder addition rates in the preparation of intermediates
  • The unusual operation of items of equipment
  • The actual way an operation is carried out (rather than just what the SOP says)
  • Ranges within which operations proven to be effective
  • Interventions
  • Other (non-critical) quality attributes or process parameters that might have an impact on, for example, ease of operation rather than directly impact product quality
  • Converting data into knowledge (e.g., data points can be plotted on a control chart and the knowledge is gained by assessing trends, limits, robustness, etc.)
  • Use of work arounds (which, in practice, should be built into formal procedures)
  • Taking learning from new events that arise (e.g., ensuring CAPAs do deliver the prevention as well as the correction)

It should be noted that ICH Q10 recommends knowledge to be managed over the whole product lifecycle.

核心概念：「轉移完成」的法律與操作定義

Phase 6 要回答一個核心問題：誰有權宣告轉移成功，以及依據什麼標準？

ISPE 建議轉移完成標準應包含：

  • 技術層面：PPQ 批次全部符合預設接受標準，無未關閉的 Critical OOS
  • 文件層面：技術轉移包（TTP）完整移交，知識轉移文件化
  • 組織層面：接收單位正式接管責任，變更控制已移交
  • 法規層面：相關申請（NDA/BLA Supplement 或 Site Change）已提交或獲批

關鍵教訓：若憲章（Charter）中未明確定義「誰」宣告完成，當雙方意見不一致時，商業糾紛不可避免。

比喻說明 Analogy

Phase 6 就像房屋交屋流程：不是工程完工就算完成，而是要有完整的「驗屋報告」（After Action Review）、明確的「交屋清單」（缺陷清單與責任分配）、以及「過戶登記」（法律責任轉移）。少了任何一個環節，房子雖然蓋好了，法律上還沒「交到你手上」。技術轉移亦然——PPQ 通過，不等於法律上「轉移完成」。

重點提示：送出方解除義務的三個條件

ISPE 3.6.1 強調：送出方（Sending Unit）的脫離（Disengagement）需要有明確合約支撐，且必須滿足三個前提：

  • 技術責任轉移：接收方已有獨立的製程改善與偏差處理能力
  • 變更控制機制就位：接收方已建立 Change Control 程序，且雙方對未來製程改變的溝通義務有書面共識
  • 監測責任移交：製程監測（Process Monitoring）數據的異常通報義務已明確定義

CDMO 警示：「技術轉移完成後，客戶還有問題找上門」是常見爭議。合約中必須明定 Phase 6 後的技術支援費率與義務上限。

核心概念：顯性知識 vs. 隱性知識

ISPE 在 3.6.3 提出了最重要的知識管理挑戰——隱性知識（Tacit Knowledge）的轉移。

顯性知識（Explicit Knowledge）可以寫在文件裡：CQA 清單、CPP 範圍、SOP、驗證報告。

隱性知識（Tacit Knowledge）在 SOP 裡找不到，但對成功至關重要，例如：

  • 「這台設備的攪拌軸有輕微振動，所以轉速要設在 85% 而非 100%」
  • 「粉末加料速率要在最後 5 分鐘降低，否則溶解度不一致」
  • 「分析師在取樣時習慣等 30 秒穩定期，但 SOP 只寫立即取樣」

這些知識若未轉移，PPQ 可能通過，但後續生產批次的良率會系統性低於送出方水準。

Lessons Learned 會議的結構化框架

Lessons Learned 五維分析框架：

維度          | 關鍵問題
-------------|----------------------------------
製程(Process)| 哪些製程步驟轉移最困難？為何？
文件(Docs)   | 哪些文件品質不足？審核效率如何？
分析(Analytics)| 方法轉移是否準時？哪些指標偏移？
品質(Quality)| OOS / Deviation 發生根本原因？
專案管理(PM) | 時程偏差發生在哪個 Phase？為何？

區別觀察（Observation）vs. 教訓（Lesson Learned）：
- 觀察 = 發現問題但無行動 → 下次仍會重演
- 教訓 = 發現問題 + 優先排序 + 行動計畫 + 追蹤監控

實務應用：CDMO 的 Phase 6 合約策略

技術轉移完成後，CDMO 通常面臨客戶要求的兩大「後轉移義務」：

  • 法規稽查支援（3.6.2）：客戶申請獲批前的 FDA/EMA 回應。CDMO 必須在合約中明定支援範圍（幾小時內回應？誰負責撰寫回應文件？費用如何計算？）
  • 配方/製程更新：若原廠在轉移後更新製程，CDMO 是否有義務重新確認？費用歸誰？

建議在 TT Charter 中明確加入：「Phase 6 完成後，超出範疇的技術支援應按 [費率表] 另行計費，客戶有義務提供最新製程更新資訊，延遲提供者不得主張 CDMO 違約。」

重點提示：ICH Q10 的知識管理要求

ISPE 引用 ICH Q10 第 1.6.1 節的重要聲明：知識管理必須涵蓋從開發到商業生命週期直至停產的全程。技術轉移活動本身就是知識管理的一環。這表示：

  • 轉移過程中產生的數據和知識屬於產品知識，必須系統性保存
  • ICH Q10 合規性要求 CDMO 建立知識管理系統（不只是文件管理系統）
  • Annual Product Review（APR）必須能追溯至 TT 期間建立的基準數據

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若 Lessons Learned 會議發現某個製程風險在 Phase 3 就被識別，但因為優先順序低而未採取行動，最終在 PPQ 造成偏差——這屬於「教訓」還是「觀察」？下一步行動應為何？
  2. 送出方 SME 在 Phase 6 完成後三個月離職，帶走了大量隱性知識。接收單位此時應如何應對？這是誰的責任？合約中應如何預防？
  3. 客戶在技術轉移完成後提出要求：因為原廠更新了一個關鍵原料的規格，需要 CDMO 重新驗證。這屬於 TT 合約範圍內還是範圍外？你的論點為何？
3.6.3.1 Transfer of Ownership and Closure — 所有權移交與結案

原文內容 Original Text ISPE TT 3rd Ed.

3.6.3.1 Transfer of Ownership and Closure

Upon completion of technology transfer, a final assessment of the remaining risks should be conducted and documented as part of technology transfer close-out. A review should be conducted by the technical transfer team in order to document and agree that all critical issues have been resolved, knowledge transfer is documented, and any outstanding items have been assigned for owners to resolve.

Successful technology transfer should ensure that all high risks have been addressed and justification has been documented as to why some residual risks are acceptable and agreed by the receiving unit. At the time, any additional support that is required should be agreed to by the receiving unit.

An important step to improve future technology transfers is for the receiving unit to provide feedback to the sending unit. Depending on the cycle times of technology transfer, production schedules, and regulatory agency filings, this feedback may occur during the transfer, throughout PPQ and during regulatory agency driven events such as filings and inspections.

ICH Q10, Section 1.6.1: "Product and process knowledge should be managed from development through the commercial life of the product up to and including product discontinuation. For example, development activities using scientific approaches provide knowledge for product and process understanding. Knowledge management is a systematic approach to acquiring, analysing, storing and disseminating information related to products, manufacturing processes and components. Sources of knowledge include, but are not limited to prior knowledge (public domain or internally documented); pharmaceutical development studies; technology transfer activities; process validation studies over the product lifecycle; manufacturing experience; innovation; continual improvement; and change management activities."

The following ISPE concept papers provide useful supporting information and refer to technology transfer and knowledge management:

  • ISPE Concept Paper: Implementing Knowledge Management in Bioprocess
  • ISPE Concept Paper: The Role of Process Capability in Monitoring Product Quality

The ISPE Cultural Excellence Report also has some indicators regarding the importance of knowledge.

It is important for the team to distinguish between observations and true lessons learned. The key distinction is the effectiveness and sustainability of the actions taken related to the observation. The following points are offered as a tool for teams to distinguish lessons learned from observations:

  • Lessons learned could include observation, prioritization, and an action and monitoring plan.
  • Observations without actions can result in reoccurrence of the respective event. Depending on the impact of reoccurrence, CAPAs may be developed and tracked in a company's QMS.
  • Collect "work arounds" can be short term solutions but are often an unsustainable means to maintain performance.

Communication of the observations to the appropriate stakeholders, particularly to the receiving unit, should be confirmed prior to conclusion of the technology transfer project. In addition, it is recommended that the receiving unit manage a decision process for which observations warrant action plans and tracking.

Lessons learned documentation may be in addition to corrective actions captured and tracked through a QMS.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：結案的三道門檻

ISPE 3.6.3.1 定義了技術轉移正式結案（Close-out）的三個必要條件：

  1. 所有 Critical Issue 已解決：轉移團隊書面確認，無懸而未決的高風險項目
  2. 殘餘風險有書面理由：不是「沒有風險」，而是「殘餘風險已被接收方知情同意接受」
  3. 未完成項目已有責任人：Outstanding items 不能無主，必須指定擁有者與完成期限

缺少任何一項，技術轉移在法律與 GMP 意義上均不算完成。

比喻說明 Analogy

結案就像建築工程的「竣工驗收」：不是所有問題都解決了才驗收，而是所有重大缺陷必須修正，次要缺陷允許在約定期限內補正，並由承包方書面列清單、載明責任。若沒有正式驗收程序，日後業主與承包方的糾紛就只能靠記憶與口頭說法——這在 GMP 世界中是完全不可接受的。

重點提示：殘餘風險的「知情接受」原則

ISPE 的用字很精確：「justification has been documented as to why some residual risks are acceptable and agreed by the receiving unit」。這有兩層含義：

  • 可接受性有理由（Justified）：不能只寫「風險低」，要說明為什麼低、有何數據支持
  • 接收方知情同意（Agreed）：接收方 QA 和管理層必須書面簽名確認接受，否則未來出事時責任歸屬不清

這正是 ICH Q9 風險管理中「風險溝通（Risk Communication）」的核心要求。

技術轉移結案清單（Close-out Checklist）

Phase 6 正式結案必備文件清單：

[ ] PPQ 報告 — 已核准，結論明確
[ ] 方法可比性報告 — 已完成
[ ] 殘餘風險評估 — 接收方已書面簽認
[ ] 技術轉移包（TTP）— 移交完整
[ ] Outstanding Items 清單 — 責任人已指定
[ ] Lessons Learned 文件 — 已分發
[ ] 後續支援協議 — 合約已更新
[ ] 法規申請狀態 — 時間軸已確認
[ ] 知識轉移文件 — 包含隱性知識清單
[ ] 接收方回饋記錄 — 已收集並分析

重點提示：Work-Arounds 的危險性

ISPE 特別警告「work arounds（變通做法）」——那些「大家都知道但 SOP 裡沒寫」的非正式操作方式。這些通常是：

  • 短期有效但長期不可持續的解決方案
  • 新進員工不知道，老員工視為理所當然的「潛規則」
  • 若沒有納入正式程序，當關鍵人員離職時即形成知識斷層

對 CDMO 而言：在 Lessons Learned 會議後，必須系統性地將所有已知的 work-arounds 轉化為正式的 SOP 修訂，這是防止下一批次失敗的最直接行動。

實務應用：接收方的回饋如何改善未來 TT

ISPE 3.6.3.1 強調接收方必須向送出方提供回饋，且回饋時點不只在結案時，而是貫穿整個 TT 週期（包括 PPQ 期間與法規稽查期間）。對 CDMO 而言，建立系統性的回饋機制帶來兩個商業價值：

  • 內部學習：每次 TT 的 Lessons Learned 累積成 CDMO 的機構智慧，縮短下一次同類轉移的時間
  • 客戶關係：主動分享 Lessons Learned 報告（適當保密後）展現透明度，建立長期合作信任

白鴉（White Raven）等已成熟的歐洲 CDMO 之所以能縮短 TT 週期，部分原因正是他們擁有系統化的 Lessons Learned 資料庫，避免重複犯錯。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若接收方在 Phase 6 結案後發現某個 Work-Around 未被納入 SOP，但這個做法已在 PPQ 批次中使用，此時 CDMO 應如何處理？是否需要啟動偏差調查？
  2. ICH Q10 Section 1.6.1 提到知識管理的「六大來源」——請列舉這六個來源，並說明技術轉移是其中的哪個環節。
  3. 若 CDMO 在 Lessons Learned 中識別出送出方 TTP 文件品質低劣是本次 TT 延誤的主因，這個觀察如何轉化為「教訓」？具體的行動計畫應包含什麼？

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary

  • Phase 4 — 操作準備度（Operational Readiness）是技術轉移延誤的高發地帶：設備、人員、物料、程序、法規五大支柱必須全部就緒，才能啟動第一個 GMP 批次；分析方法確認應先於製程確認。
  • Engineering Runs vs. PPQ：工程試驗是非正式學習，PPQ 批次是正式 GMP 法規證據，兩者在偏差處理、批次放行和法規意涵上截然不同；PPQ 報告的六大必備要素缺一不可。
  • 分析方法可比性不只是「在規格內」，而是統計上可証明接收方的分析方法與送出方等效（精確度、準確度、系統偏差三維驗證，需統計背景人員設計）。
  • Phase 6 的轉移完成定義必須在 Charter 中事先明確——包括誰宣告完成、依據何種技術/文件/法規標準；殘餘風險必須由接收方知情書面接受。
  • 隱性知識（Tacit Knowledge）是最難轉移且最常被低估的風險：operator 的習慣操作、設備的非常規行為、有效的操作範圍——這些在 SOP 找不到，卻對生產成功至關重要。
  • CDMO 合約戰略：Phase 6 後的持續技術支援義務、法規稽查協助費率、製程更新責任，必須在合約中明確界定，避免商業糾紛。
