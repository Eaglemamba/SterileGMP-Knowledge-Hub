導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：查核表 = 知識差距分析工具

附錄一的查核表不只是「收文清單」，更是技術轉移啟動時最重要的差距分析（Gap Analysis）工具。正確使用方式是在技術轉移啟動後立即填寫，為每一項目標記：

  • Available（已備齊）：文件完整，可直接移交
  • Partial（部分具備）：文件存在但不完整，需補充
  • Missing（缺失）：文件尚未建立，需要重新產生

這份差距分析的輸出直接驅動風險登錄冊（Risk Register）的建立——「Missing」項目通常就是高風險項目，需要立即分配資源處理。

比喻說明：搬家前的庫存清點

想像你要把一間運轉中的廚房（原廠）搬到另一個地方（接收廠）繼續營業。這張查核表就像是「搬家前的廚房庫存清點表」：鍋子、食譜、食材供應商聯絡方式、健康許可證……每一樣都要逐一確認是否帶走。少了任何一項，新廚房就無法在第一天正常開門。

大分子（生物製劑）多了「細胞株來源（Cell Bank）」這一行——這就像廚房獨門配方的「酵母菌種」，沒有它，整個生產就無從開始。

重點提示：大分子 vs. 小分子的關鍵差異

仔細比對查核表，大分子（生物製劑）專屬項目揭示了生物製劑TT的核心複雜性：

  • Cell Bank / Origin of Cell Bank：MCB（主要細胞庫）與WCB（工作細胞庫）的特性描述文件，是生物製劑製程的起點
  • Control Strategy：大分子列為必要，小分子未標記——因生物製劑的製程複雜度使控制策略成為TT成敗關鍵
  • Waste/Vent Characterization：生物製劑廢棄物（含活性生物物質）需特別處理，小分子則無此強制要求

小分子則多了物理化學特性：粒徑（Particle Size）、多形體（Polymorphs）、溶解度（Solubility）、毒理數據（Toxicology）、生體可用率（Bioavailability）——這些是固態製劑和化學合成製程特有的考量。

CDMO實務應用：簽約前必備清單

作為CDMO，在與生物製劑客戶簽署技術轉移協議前，以下文件屬於「不可或缺（Non-negotiable）」：

  • Cell Bank Origin Documentation（細胞株起源文件）
  • Process Description + CPPs with ranges（含參數範圍的製程描述）
  • Current Specifications & CQAs（現行規格與關鍵品質屬性）
  • 至少3批歷史批次數據（Historical Batch Data）
  • Stability Data（穩定性數據，至少初步結果）

若客戶無法提供上述文件，CDMO應在合約中明確界定「客戶責任（Client Obligation）」並連結時程里程碑，避免因文件缺失造成TT延誤而引發商業糾紛。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若客戶無法提供「Development Run Data」，這對你的工程批（Engineering Run）規劃有何影響？你會如何在風險登錄冊中記錄此差距？
  2. 「Control Strategy」僅在大分子欄位標記 x，但小分子是否也應有控制策略？查核表的設計邏輯是什麼？
  3. 「Extractable/Leachable/Adsorption Data」在大小分子都標記，但生物製劑為何對此項更敏感？請從產品CQA角度分析。

ISPE Tech Transfer

9   Appendix 2 – Case Studies: Biologics

9.1   Summary

This chapter presents case studies that illustrate key technology transfer concepts presented in this Guide, with an emphasis on utilizing CMOs.

The key components of any technology transfer are the technical challenges and effective program management. With any technology transfer program, technical challenges can arise that could subsequently impact the planned timelines, process performance, and product quality. These challenges should be addressed in a collaborative way between the sending and receiving units to minimize delays in the program and frustrations arising between sites.

In this chapter, five case studies are presented, the first two from a client perspective and the subsequent three from a CMO perspective. These examples demonstrate a range of challenges which occurred during technology transfer programs and how they were subsequently resolved.

The molecules in these case studies are primarily monoclonal antibodies (mAbs) but the lessons learned are generally applicable across all molecular classes. Manufacturing processes for mAbs have become relatively well-defined and are often based on platform technologies that can be effectively leveraged; there is a generally broad knowledge base. In addition, the advent of single-use technologies is facilitating transfers by minimizing changes in equipment and operating parameters.

The amount of drug substance manufacturing experience that smaller companies typically have is modest, often at different scales and with limited development history and process knowledge. Transfers represent the opportunity to right-size a process and implement process improvements with an eye to minimizing changes that might impact product comparability. Technology transfers also potentially offer an opportunity to assess new technologies, such as continuous manufacturing and PAT, if the CMO has these capabilities.

9.2   Main Challenges

One of the main challenges for successful technology transfer utilizing a contract manufacturer is selecting the right CMO and developing a good partnership. From an operational perspective, this involves ensuring capacity (i.e., having manufacturing slots available within an acceptable time frame) and ensuring appropriate process and analytical capabilities necessary for project success. Demand for manufacturing slots will vary over time, but demand for capacity continues to grow and is a potential constraint that is a priority planning consideration. Recent experience with these case studies has resulted in successful transfers (i.e., completion of first GMP batch in approximately 12 months).

Another main challenge for a small to mid-size company executing technology transfer is how to deal with a lack of development history or process knowledge, either due to limited manufacturing experience or insufficient process development. Performing a gap assessment prior to initiating the transfer and then sharing this assessment with the CMO at initiation (to gain alignment and ensure transparency) can be critical for success. Early involvement of manufacturing teams during technology transfer and development phases is also critical for successful implementation of the process at scale.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為何以mAb為主角？

本附錄選擇單株抗體（mAb）作為主要案例，原因有三：

  • 市場主流：mAb佔全球生物製劑市值超過一半，CDMO最常遇到此類品項
  • 平台製程成熟：CHO細胞培養 → 蛋白A親和層析 → 病毒去活化 → 精製層析的平台路徑已建立，CDMO可快速評估接收可行性
  • 一次性技術普及：Single-use bioreactor/filtration等技術大幅降低了設備配置差異，減少TT中的設備兼容性風險

mAb的經驗雖可類推至其他生物製劑，但融合蛋白（fusion proteins）、ADC（抗體藥物結合物）、細胞基因治療（CGT）等分子則有各自獨特的挑戰，不能完全套用。

重點提示：「12個月完成第一批GMP批」的含義

文中提到成功TT的基準是「約12個月內完成第一批GMP批次」。這個數字代表從CMO選定到GMP生產完成。對CDMO業務開發而言，這是客戶最常問的問題之一。

影響這12個月的關鍵因素：

  • 文件完整性（缺文件 = 延誤最大來源）
  • 長前置期物料（如一次性袋組、層析介質、細胞培養基）的採購時間
  • CDMO的製程工程師是否熟悉客戶的平台製程
  • 監管策略（工程批是否需要ICH Q5E可比性研究）

比喻說明：小公司TT困境

小到中型生物技術公司常面臨的情況：臨床前/Phase I期的製程是由學術實驗室或小型CRO完成，批量小（1–10L）、記錄不完整，現在要轉移到2,000L商業規模。

這就像一位廚師在家裡用小鍋試驗出一道菜，現在要複製給工廠大量生產——不僅食譜要完整，還要考慮熱傳導、混合效率、原料批次差異……原先在小鍋裡自然成立的條件，大鍋裡可能完全失效。

這就是為什麼「gap assessment前置」如此重要：早點發現差距，才能在合約中明確定義誰來填補、費用如何分攤。

  2. 「gap assessment prior to initiating the transfer」與「gap assessment during transfer」有何不同？分別在哪個情境下使用？

9.3   Overview of Technology Transfer Phases

As described in Chapter 3, there are six phases of technology transfer:

  1. Project Initiation: Form technology transfer team and develop charter
  2. Knowledge Transfer: Consolidate knowledge for transfer and agree on high level technology transfer proposal
  3. Project Delivery Planning: Identify risks, conduct risk assessments, and develop technology transfer plan
  4. Operational Readiness
  5. Project Implementation: Process (procedure) qualification
  6. Project Close-Out: Finalize technology transfer and perform review

The approach presented in this chapter takes a modified approach that has been successfully utilized by small to mid-sized companies. The three general stages, as defined in this chapter from the client perspective, are:

  1. CMO Selection
  2. Project Initiation
  3. Project Execution

In relation to the six phases presented in Chapter 3: CMO selection precedes establishment of a technology transfer team but is a necessary precursor for outsourcing.

  • Project initiation incorporates aspects of phases 1 to 3.
  • Project execution includes aspects of phase 3 as well as phases 4 and 5. Phase 6 can range in scope from completion of a pilot or engineering batch to submission of a regulatory filing as well as a lessons learned exercise between the client and the CMO.

From the CMO's perspective, stage gates can be defined within the detailed project plan per their procedures to ensure that activities (such as document transfer, preparation of GMP documentation, etc.) occur in a timely manner to support manufacture. This is also a good example of the need for a small to mid-sized company to have flexible and adaptable operational and quality systems. Typically, they will not have formal technology transfer procedures and it will be advantageous and efficient to utilize and leverage the CMO's procedures, recognizing that these will likely differ between different CMOs.

9.3.1   CMO Selection

The CMO selection process is initiated by developing a list of candidates based on industry knowledge and experience that meet baseline criteria, including scale, capability, and compliance history. A detailed Request for Proposal (RFP) should be developed that includes an appropriate amount of process detail, analytical method descriptions, the product specification, a bill of materials, and requests for any development work. Target dates for major deliverables should also be included, such as any requirements to have toxicology or clinical studies initiated by a certain date or if there are any commitments to achieve a target date for an investigational new drug filing.

Adequate process detail is necessary to ensure that the proposals that are received in response to the RFP are comparable and permit an appropriate comparison. It is also important to initiate the CMO selection process with adequate time for refining responses to the RFP. In general, there might be at least one to two cycles of proposal refinement that will allow an accurate comparison between CMOs and facilitate contract negotiation. In addition, an appropriately detailed RFP permits the CMO to properly assess their ability to run the process and be successful within project timeline expectations.

For example, an RFP can be structured as follows:

  • 1 Introduction — Background, Objectives, Response to RFP
  • 2 Scope of Services Requested — Technology Transfer, Analytical Services, Engineering and Clinical Batches, PPQ Batches, Process Validation, Stability, Additional Services
  • 3 Supporting Information — development reports, batch records (if available), PFDs, specifications, test methods, bill of materials, etc.

For the first case study, this approach started with engagement of six candidate CMOs. The proposals received were reviewed and a head to head analysis was performed on work scope, cost, and timing. From this assessment, two lead candidates were identified and due diligence audits were performed, resulting in a lead and a backup CMO.

9.3.2   Project Initiation

Once the CMO is selected, contract and quality agreement negotiations are immediately initiated, utilizing CMO templates for efficiency. A project kickoff meeting should be scheduled as soon as possible at the CMO and is critical for establishing the personal connections and lines of communication.

The composition of the client project team may vary, but will typically include a project leader, project manager, process and analytical SMEs, a CMC regulatory representative, and a quality representative. The CMO project team will be comparable and complementary.

Establishment of personal connections between these team members and their counterparts at the CMO are invaluable in anticipation that there will be challenges during the technology transfer and manufacturing that will require good working relationships to ensure appropriate and timely resolution. Face-to-face meetings are critical for establishing relationships and lines of communication.

9.3.3   Project Execution

The third aspect of project initiation is document transfer and sharing of the QTPP, CQAs, and CPPs that define the control strategy. These documents are important because they:

  • Define the intended use and product quality profile
  • Ensure that technology transfer process development/optimization begins with the end in mind and that client and CMO goals and expectations are aligned
  • Define those product characteristics or CQAs that must be monitored and controlled during technology transfer

Project execution follows a standard course of events. The following is an overview of activities undertaken in both case studies:

  • Process assessment and identification of the need for process development/optimization for facility fit
  • Identification and immediate ordering of long lead time materials/consumables
  • Small scale/development studies, as appropriate
  • Method transfer and qualification, as appropriate
  • Development of GMP documentation, including batch records, test methods, specification, etc.
  • Pilot scale run, if needed
  • Engineering run, if needed
  • GMP run(s)
  • Analytical comparability
  • Regulatory submission

核心概念：三階段 vs. 六階段框架的調和

本節將ISPE六階段框架（教科書模型）與小型生技公司實際使用的三階段模型（實務模型）對應起來：

  • CMO Selection（遴選）→ 先於Phase 1，是外包的前提
  • Project Initiation（啟動）→ 涵蓋Phases 1–3（組隊、知識整合、風險識別）
  • Project Execution（執行）→ 涵蓋Phases 3–5（OQ、PQ、PPQ）

這種「壓縮框架」在小公司常見，因為沒有足夠的專職TT人員執行完整六階段的儀式性流程。CDMO必須理解客戶可能沒有正式的TT SOP，並主動以自身程序引導流程。

重點提示：Stage Gates的作用

Stage Gate（階段關卡）是CDMO保護自身和客戶的機制——只有前一個里程碑確認完成，才允許進入下一階段。例如：

  • 文件傳輸 Gate：文件收齊並審核通過 → 才開始編寫批次記錄
  • 方法轉移 Gate：分析方法資格確認 → 才開始工程批
  • 工程批 Gate：製程數據審查通過 → 才進入GMP批

Stage Gate能早期發現問題，避免在昂貴的GMP批才發現分析方法根本無法用於接收廠設備。

比喻說明：面對面會議為何不可省略？

文中特別強調「Face-to-face meetings are critical」。在視訊會議盛行的時代，這個建議仍然成立，原因是：技術轉移過程中不可避免地會出現問題（偏差、批次失敗、客戶投訴）。

這就像急診室的醫護團隊——平常你可以用電話溝通，但在緊急情況下，面對面的默契、信任感與即時決策速度是電話無法取代的。TT啟動會議就是在「真正的危機還沒來之前」，先建立這種信任基礎。

CDMO實務應用：Long Lead Time物料管理

「Identification and immediate ordering of long lead time materials/consumables」是執行階段的第二個行動項目，時序上比多數人預期的要早。

生物製劑TT中常見的長前置期物料：

  • 蛋白A層析介質（MabSelect Resin）：前置期6–12週
  • 一次性生物反應器袋組（SU Bioreactor Bag）：8–16週
  • 細胞培養基（Basal + Feed Media）：如為客製配方可達12週
  • 病毒過濾器（Virus Filter）：6–10週

若啟動會議後才開始詢價採購，這些物料的到位時間很可能成為工程批的瓶頸。最佳實務是在RFP階段即要求客戶提供BOM，CDMO收到後立即評估哪些屬於長前置期物料。

ISPE Tech Transfer

9.4   Risk Management

Although there is a defined process for CMO selection that plays a key role in reducing risk, no formal risk assessment (technical, operational, or business) was performed by the client for initiating technology transfer in these case studies. Client SME experience working with CMOs and due diligence audits were used to informally assess risk. However, formal risk assessments are performed by the client as part of change control and are subsequently formally introduced through collaboratively performed FMEAs as the project advances toward pivotal study or PPQ.

The requirements of the selection process that reduce risk are as follows:

  • Commercial experience
  • Appropriate scale (current and future)
  • Capacity (availability of manufacturing slots)
  • Demonstrated ability to expand capacity/capabilities
  • Compliance history
  • Technical skill/experience

These requirements were met through the selection process that included a combination of the following:

  • Proposal submission based on the detailed RFP
  • Site visit
  • Due diligence audit
  • Professional experience
"This could generally be considered a standard practice for small companies."

A key component of the proposal is an adequately detailed project plan that is utilized by both client and CMO project managers to manage timelines and inherently manage risk associated with those activities by identifying critical path activities. Capacity and technical experience play important roles in meeting deadlines and ensuring that the ultimate goal of the technology transfer, production of the first GMP batch, is successful and on time.

Once a contract is signed, the project is initiated with a face-to-face team meeting at the CMO. The purpose of this meeting is to formally present the project goals and objectives and initiate relationships between SMEs and other project team members. Document transfer will also occur around this time, preferably before the kickoff meeting, but as soon as possible. The scope of documentation may include executed batch record(s), test methods and qualification reports, specifications, bill of materials, development reports, etc.

Risk is further managed throughout the technology transfer process with regular project meetings to facilitate communication. This will include weekly teleconferences and face-to-face meetings at critical junctures. Information can also be shared through new and evolving technologies that can provide real time operational data as the process moves into manufacturing. Effective communication is critical throughout the process; regular interaction between SMEs is encouraged with the important caveat that project leaders and/or project managers are involved to ensure coordination and appropriate follow up.

Formal risk management tools will subsequently be used to support process characterization and process validation, but a quantitative risk assessment for a small company may have limited utility and the process relies more on involvement by more senior and experienced personnel.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：生物製劑TT的風險管理現實

本節揭示了一個重要的實務真相：小型生技公司在TT啟動階段通常不做正式風險評估。風險管理的手段主要是：

  • 非正式層面：SME個人經驗 + due diligence audit（盡職調查審計）
  • 正式層面：在Change Control框架下執行，以及接近PPQ時進行FMEA

這並不代表這種做法最優，而是小公司資源有限的現實。CDMO應理解客戶可能沒有完整的風險文件，需要主動協助建立。

重點提示：「量化風險評估對小公司效用有限」

文中直白地說：「a quantitative risk assessment for a small company may have limited utility」。這句話值得深思——傳統的ICH Q9 RPN評分（嚴重性 × 發生率 × 可偵測性）在數據不足的情況下，評分本身可能缺乏可信度。

在這種情況下，「有經驗的資深人員的判斷」往往比填滿一份RPN表格更有價值。監管機構對此也有共識：對新分子、新製程，質化風險評估加上專家判斷是被接受的方法。

關鍵是：無論用哪種方式，都要有文件記錄——哪怕只是「依據SME判斷，此項目風險可接受，理由如下」也比沒有記錄好。

比喻說明：Critical Path = 登山的關鍵路線

「Critical path activities」就是整個TT專案中，如果任何一個延誤，整個專案就無法按時完成的活動鏈。

就像登山隊要在日落前攻頂，必須識別哪條路線上有哪些必須在幾點前通過的節點。如果第一個節點就延誤了，後面再快也無法補回來。

在生物製劑TT中，典型的critical path包含：細胞株特性確認 → 長前置期物料到位 → 方法轉移完成 → 工程批執行 → 數據審查 → GMP批。任何一個環節阻塞，第一批GMP批的日期就會直接後移。

CDMO實務應用：Weekly Teleconference最佳實務

文中提到「weekly teleconferences」作為持續風險管理的機制。有效的每週例行會議結構：

  • Action Item Review（5分鐘）：確認上週行動項目完成狀態
  • Critical Path Status（10分鐘）：關鍵路徑活動是否如期進行
  • Issues & Risks（10分鐘）：新出現的問題，責任人和預計解決日期
  • Next Week Plan（5分鐘）：下週重要里程碑預告

關鍵原則：PM必須在場（確保follow-up），技術SME之間的討論不應繞過PM直接做決策，所有決策需有會議紀錄存檔以支持未來的查核與調查。

ISPE Tech Transfer

9.5   Technology Transfer Case Studies

The first two case studies present specific technical, operational, and project management challenges that were faced from a client perspective during the technology transfer of two products, how these challenges were resolved, and what the outcome was for each.

The following three case studies are presented from the CMO perspective to illustrate technical challenges arising during technology transfer from small and mid-sized companies.

"The molecules in these case studies are primarily monoclonal antibodies (mAbs) but the lessons learned are generally applicable across all molecular classes."

Key themes covered across the five case studies:

  • Selection of the right CMO and managing the partnership
  • Dealing with limited development history or process knowledge
  • Managing technical challenges through collaborative problem-solving
  • Ensuring product comparability across the transfer
  • Effective program management and communication

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：雙視角的學習設計

ISPE本附錄刻意安排了客戶視角（案例1–2）與CDMO視角（案例3–5）的雙面設計。這並非偶然，而是要讓讀者理解同一個技術轉移事件，在雙方眼中的感受和優先順序可能完全不同：

  • 客戶視角：關注的是時程、成本控制、產品可比性、法規路徑
  • CDMO視角：關注的是製程可行性、設備配置、文件完整性、責任邊界

理解對方的視角是成功TT夥伴關係的基礎。

本教學文件（section-06a）涵蓋至Section 9.5的概述性文字（p103結束）。五個個別案例研究的完整內容（9.5.1–9.5.5）收錄於下一個文件（section-06b）。
