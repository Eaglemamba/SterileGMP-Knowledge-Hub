9.1 Sustainability in the Context of Biopharmaceutical Facility Design

Although the processing chemicals used as buffers can be a challenge to the environment and the risk of non-deactivated viruses should be given careful consideration in regard to disposal, biopharmaceutical drug substances can be considered sustainable, as they are cultured in a growth medium which promotes their growth through the use of enhanced natural means.

Sustainable facility design involves:

  • Designing layouts that are spatially efficient.
  • Designing layouts that consider passive solar design in the placing and orientation of individual blocks of the facility on the site to gain maximum advantage/ecological benefit of the climatic location.
  • Insulating the facility to ensure that expenditure on energy is kept to a minimum.
  • The minimization of heat loss and heat gain through an efficient building envelope should be balanced against the need for cooling load reduction considerations in pharmaceutical facilities.
  • Correct site orientation and sun screening to minimize heat gain for cooling and to maximize heat gain for heating.
  • Commissioning and monitoring the facility for ongoing energy savings.
  • Ensuring that the facility operation and maintenance is efficient by design.
  • Providing renewable energy sources as a strategy including sources that recycle or return energy to the community.
  • Using process heat sources rather than exhausting them.
  • Minimizing water consumption and considering grey water recycling.
  • Working in conjunction with the process to provide all of the above to the benefit of the stakeholders, company, personnel, and public at large.

Each of the above activities may be considered as a sustainable design basis or criterion when placed in the context of building designs that consider:

  • Adaptability and flexibility in use
  • The potential for re-use, given full life cycle/design life assessment from implementation to demolition

Closed processing can provide opportunities for a significant reduction in:

  • Energy use and consumption (by reductions in Grade)
  • Once through air requirements
  • Facility response
  • Facility and utility size

The use of modular continuous processing lines and the potential both for closed processing and the greater use of disposables could allow facility size reduction and adaptability and flexibility in the use of a facility. This is likely to provide both an environmental benefit and an operational cost benefit through reduced local taxes and reduced facility energy usage.

Simpler manufacturing formats for retrofitting available commercial industrial buildings may provide a cost benefit, when compared to new bespoke facility designs expected of open, batch type processing.

An ISPE Good Practice Guide on Sustainability [67] is in development and is intended to provide overall guidance on sustainability and related topic areas.

Sustainable facility design can provide significant contributions to sustainability in the following areas:

  • Application of green chemistry
  • Reduction in carbon and/or total greenhouse gas emissions (metric tons CO2 equivalent), downward trend in annual figures
  • Process intensification, reduced waste, improved yield
  • Waste minimization (e.g., reduced water usage), reduced total waste, downward trend in annual figures
  • Energy reduction (in GJ), downward trend in annual figures
  • Reduction in solvent usage (tons), reduction VOC emissions (tons), downward trend in annual figures
  • Reduction in waste water COD (mg/l)
  • Areas where policies have been instituted
  • Verification of a system, e.g., ISO 14001 [68]
  • Certification using an agreed environmental assessment method/system to meet a specified target
  • Performing a life cycle assessment using an agreed assessment methodology

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 永續性 (Sustainability) 的定義

永續設計 (Sustainable Facility Design)：設施在滿足當前製造需求的同時，將對環境的長期負面影響降至最低。在生物製藥廠，這並非選擇題，而是在法規壓力、碳稅、ESG 報告要求下的必答題。

重要的是，生物製藥的原料（生物培養基）本身就比化學合成更接近自然，因此其本質較具永續潛力——前提是設施設計也同步跟上。

關鍵指標：

  • 碳排放量（公噸 CO₂ 當量）
  • 能耗（GJ / 年）
  • 廢水 COD（mg/L）
  • 溶劑用量（噸 / 年）

比喻說明 — 永續藥廠 = 醫院節能改造

想像一家醫院在不降低病患安全前提下推動節能——不能關掉手術室空調，但可以優化走廊照明、回收廢熱加溫熱水，以及在辦公區域使用自然採光。

生物製藥廠的永續設計邏輯完全相同：無菌保證區域（Grade A/B）絕不妥協，但其他支援區域大有節能空間。封閉製程可讓核心區域降等（Grade C → CNC+），等同讓「手術室」縮小範圍，其餘空間就可以用普通節能建築標準設計。

重點提示 — 封閉製程是永續最強槓桿

本節最關鍵的洞察是：Closed Processing（封閉製程）是降低環境等級的技術前提，而環境等級降低直接轉化為能源與建設成本的大幅節省。

一個 Grade A/B 的無塵室每平方米每年能耗，可能是普通辦公室的 50–100 倍（主要來自 HVAC 的超高換氣率）。若封閉製程允許將某操作降至 Grade C 或 CNC+，等同於在該區域節省數十倍的能耗，同時減少設施面積、降低運維成本。

這也是 Section 10.2 風險導向分級方法論的核心價值所在。

實務應用 — CDMO 的 ESG 競爭力

大型製藥客戶（Big Pharma）在選擇 CDMO 夥伴時，ESG 評分已成為採購標準之一。具備以下永續設施特徵的 CDMO，在競標時有明顯優勢：

  • ISO 14001 環境管理認證
  • 生命週期評估（LCA）報告
  • 模組化/可彈性調整的廠房設計（支援多客戶、快速換產）
  • 封閉製程比例高（間接證明污染控制能力強，同時低能耗）

永續設計指標框架

能源強度指標 = 年總能耗 (GJ) / 產量 (kg API)

水耗指標 = 年用水量 (m³) / 批次數

廢棄物強度 = 年廢棄物總量 (kg) / 產量 (kg API)

碳排放 = 直接排放 (Scope 1)
       + 電力間接排放 (Scope 2)
       + 供應鏈排放 (Scope 3)

這些指標應逐年追蹤並呈現「下降趨勢（downward trend）」，以滿足 ISO 14001 持續改善要求。

10.1 Mechanical Information

10.1.1 Aide Mémoire 07121104 Classifications

Grade F: a classified space that satisfies the German Aide Mémoire 07121104 [69] airborne viable microorganisms < 500 cfu/m³.

Grade E: a classified space that satisfies the German Aide Mémoire 07121104 [69] airborne viable microorganisms < 250 cfu/m³.

10.1.2 PIC/S and EMA Application of ISO 14644

Grade D: a classified space that satisfies EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-8 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "at-rest" state only and airborne viable microorganisms < 200 cfu/m³.

Grade C: a classified space that satisfies EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-8 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state and meet ISO-7 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "at-rest" state (with a transition between states of 15 to 20 minutes) and airborne viable microorganisms < 100 cfu/m³.

Grade B: a classified space that satisfies EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-7 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state and meet ISO-5 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "at-rest" state (with a transition between states of 15 to 20 minutes) and airborne viable microorganisms < 10 cfu/m³.

Grade A: a classified space that satisfies EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-5 measured via airborne 0.5 μm particulate and ISO-4.8 measured via airborne 5.0 μm particulate in the "in-operation" and "at-rest" states and airborne viable microorganisms < 1 cfu/m³. These spaces are normally unidirectional flow with an air velocity of 0.20 to 0.45 m/s.

10.1.3 Revised ISPE Grades

Grade 8: a classified space that satisfies US FDA requirements for ISO-8 measured via airborne 0.5 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state, as well as EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-8 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state and meet ISO-7 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "at-rest" state (with a transition between states of 15 to 20 minutes) and airborne viable microorganisms < 100 cfu/m³.

Grade 7: a classified space that satisfies US FDA requirements for ISO-7 measured via airborne 0.5 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state, as well as EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-7 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state and meet ISO-5 measured via airborne 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particulate in the "at-rest" state (with a transition between states of 15 to 20 minutes) and airborne viable microorganisms < 10 cfu/m³.

Grade 5: a classified space that satisfies US FDA requirements for ISO-5 measured via airborne 0.5 μm particulate in the "in-operation" state, as well as EMA and PIC/S requirements to meet ISO-5 measured via airborne 0.5 μm particulate and ISO-4.8 measured via airborne 5.0 μm particulate in the "in-operation" and "at-rest" states, and airborne viable microorganisms < 1 cfu/m³. These spaces are normally unidirectional flow with an air velocity of 0.20 to 0.45 m/s.

Controlled Not Classified (CNC): areas where HVAC systems are specifically designed to reduce airborne contaminants below the level of the ambient environment and both temperature and Relative Humidity (RH) are controlled more tightly than in the ambient environment. Claims for environmental control in these areas are related to the design of the system and installation qualification is common. No claim is made or qualified for the specific control of particulate. Typical systems will have heating, cooling and filtration meeting MERV 13 or better. These areas are sometimes referred to as "pharmaceutical" or "clean" areas within pharmaceutical facilities.

Controlled Not Classified with Local Monitoring (CNC+): areas where HVAC systems are specifically designed to reduce airborne contaminants below the level and both temperature and RH are controlled more tightly than in the ambient environment. Claims for environmental control in these areas are related to both the design of the system and system performance, installation qualification and operational qualification is common. These areas are typically qualified to meet ISO 8 requirements at rest only to control temperature and humidity within a specified band and are monitored for viable particulate during operation to provide background information for investigations and to assure the adequacy of layers of closure. These areas are generally aligned with PIC/S designation "Grade D" and airborne viable microorganisms < 200 cfu/m³.

Temperature Controlled: areas where HVAC systems are specifically designed to control both temperature and (where applicable) Relative Humidity (RH) more tightly than in the ambient environment. Temperature and RH are typically qualified in these areas and temperature mapping is expected. This designation is typically found in warehouse spaces, cold rooms, logistics.

核心概念 — 環境等級分類體系完整對照

本節是整本指南的技術詞彙表，特別重要的是 ISPE 統合了三大法規體系（FDA / EMA / PIC/S）的等級定義。以下是完整對照：

重點提示 — CNC vs CNC+ 的關鍵差異

CNC 和 CNC+ 是本指南特有的創新等級概念，也是 CDMO 設計閉合製程區域時最常用到的兩個等級：

  • CNC（Controlled Not Classified）：只有溫濕度控制＋MERV 13 過濾，無微粒計數要求。適用於完全密閉、無產品暴露的操作區。
  • CNC+（帶本地監測）：增加了 ISO 8 靜態驗證＋運作中微生物監測。雖不是「正式分級」，但有 OQ 記錄和持續監控，作為封閉製程中最後一道背景保證。

從成本角度：Grade 8 換氣率約 20–30 ACH，CNC+ 可低至 15–20 ACH，CNC 更可低至 6–10 ACH。每 ACH 的差異在大型設施中，每年電費差距可達百萬台幣以上。

比喻說明 — 環境等級 = 防護口罩的等級選擇

就像選擇防護口罩：外科口罩（Grade D/CNC+）→ N95（Grade C/Grade 8）→ PAPR（Grade B/Grade 7）→ 全身正壓防護衣（Grade A/Grade 5）。

重點不是「越貴越好」，而是依危險程度（暴露風險）選擇最匹配的防護等級——這正是 10.2 節風險導向分級的核心邏輯。如果你的操作本身是「密閉的」（等同於沒有飛沫傳播），那麼連外科口罩都不需要——這就是 CNC 等級的意義。

Gaseous Decontamination: one of a number of technologies that disseminate a biocidal gas to produce a minimum 3 log (and as much as 6 log) reduction in viable organisms in the environment. These technologies diffuse at the molecular level and can pass through a HEPA filter.

Fogging Decontamination: one of a number of technologies that disseminate a biocidal agent as very small particle size to produce a minimum 3 log (and as much as 6 log) reduction in viable organisms in the environment. These technologies cannot pass through a HEPA filter efficiently (greater than 20% loss).

Containment: equipment, systems or construction intended to prevent the escape of products, materials or organisms.

Biological Containment: an organized system of equipment, and construction intended to prevent the escape of organisms. Biological containment is usually identified according to the biological safety designation of the organism(s) being contained.

Containment Boundary: the imaginary line in the construction of systems or facilities on which the various components of a containment barrier are aligned. Spaces within this boundary are normally considered as contaminated with the contained material or organism.

Containment Barrier: construction that is impervious or highly resistant to the passage of micro-organisms. Solid materials make up the majority of containment barriers, but HEPA and ULPA filters are also considered containment barriers.

Global Recirculation: re-use (recirculation) of conditioned air leaving a room to serve any room of similar type or function.

Local Recirculation: re-use (recirculation) of conditioned air leaving a room only to serve that room or suite of rooms with similar biological and contamination risks.

N+1 Redundancy: a system where the failure of any single major component will not result in a reduction in capacity below the design requirement.

XX% N+1 Redundancy: a system where the failure of any single major component will result in XX% of the design required capacity remaining.

Decontamination Capable HVAC: an HVAC system that is arranged to recirculate gaseous decontamination agent for effective decontamination of a space and the air system(s) serving it.

Decontamination Compatible HVAC: an HVAC system where exposed components are resistant to gaseous decontamination agents.

Decontamination Capable Exhaust: an exhaust system that is arranged to allow recirculation of gaseous decontamination agent for effective decontamination of filter and fans as well as exhaust of decontamination agent from a served space.

Pre and Post-Viral Separation (Viral+ and Viral−): the separation of pre-viral and post-viral product or upstream (bioreaction and harvest) from downstream (purification) is not precise. Most processes utilize multiple process steps to reduce the concentration of organisms or DNA/RNA from the culture, creating the final product. In the case of vaccines, the viral contaminants may never be removed. The convention is to separate air systems that serve these two open processing areas to reduce the chance of contamination of the final product; however, this may not be scientifically supportable. Similar to earlier evaluations, the ability of the product or organisms to become airborne should be considered. This separation is affected by process closure, the achievement of closure obviates the need for HVAC separation.

Multiple Efficiency Reporting Value (MERV): the ASHRAE Standard 52.2 [70] method of testing filter efficiency by challenging filters with particles of multiple sizes and integrating the efficiency into a single number rating. The higher the MERV number the more efficient the filter. MERV 14/15 is approximately equivalent to a 95% efficient filter.

Air Change Rate (Ventilation Rate): the volume of air supplied to a room (per hour) divided by the volume of the room. This is a purely numerical evaluation used as a rule of thumb in cleanroom design.

Particle Generation Rate: the number of particles of a target size released into a room (per hour) by processes, people, or in the supply air.

Dilution Ventilation: Reduction in airborne contamination via mixing of clean incoming air with contaminated air within the room and removal of an equivalent amount to exhaust or recirculation via treatment (e.g. filtration).

Displacement Ventilation: reduction in airborne contamination via "plug flow" of clean incoming air forcing contaminated air within the room to exhaust or recirculation via treatment (e.g., filtration).

Ventilation Efficiency (Effectiveness): the ability of an HVAC system to dilute contaminants when compared to ideal dilution. A ventilation effectiveness of 1.0 indicates that a room is able to dilute contaminants as effectively as is indicated by an ideal dilution calculation. Displacement ventilation may have an effectiveness as high as 3.0 to 4.0.

Recovery: a test defined in ISO 14644-3 [63] intended to show a cleanroom's ability to "cleanup" from an operational particulate specification to a lower (cleaner) classification.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 氣態除汙 vs 霧化除汙

氣態除汙（Gaseous Decontamination，如 VHP/H₂O₂ 氣體）：分子層級擴散，可通過 HEPA 過濾器，因此可對整個送風系統進行去汙（含管道和過濾器本體）。這是 BSL-2/3 生物廠房或有病毒暴露風險的生物製藥廠使用的主流技術。

霧化除汙（Fogging，如過氧化氫霧化）：粒子較大，無法有效穿過 HEPA 過濾器（損失 >20%），僅能對房間本體去汙，不能覆蓋 HVAC 系統。

選擇時機判斷：若需要同時對房間和 HVAC 去汙（如隔離器驗證後的整體再清潔），必須選擇氣態技術。

核心概念 — Viral+ / Viral− 分區原理

生物製藥生產分為「病毒清除前（Viral+）」和「病毒清除後（Viral−）」兩個區域：

  • Viral+ 區域：上游製程（細胞培養、收穫），含有活性病毒和細胞的空間
  • Viral− 區域：下游純化後，理論上病毒已被去除或滅活

傳統做法是將兩者的 HVAC 系統完全分離，防止 Viral+ 空氣污染最終產品。但關鍵洞察是：若製程本身是封閉的，這種 HVAC 分離就沒有科學必要性——封閉系統內的病毒不會變成空氣污染物。這再次強調了「過程封閉性」對設施設計的根本影響。

HVAC 設計關鍵參數計算

換氣率 ACH = 送風量 (m³/h) ÷ 房間體積 (m³)

通風效率比較：
  稀釋通風（亂流）效率 ≈ 1.0
  置換通風（單向流）效率 ≈ 3.0–4.0

含義：
  置換通風達到相同清潔效果，
  只需稀釋通風 1/3 的換氣次數
  → 重大節能潛力

MERV 對應關係：
  MERV 13 ≈ 75–90% 效率 (≥1μm)
  MERV 14 ≈ 90–95% 效率
  HEPA   ≈ 99.97% 效率 (≥0.3μm)

重點提示 — N+1 備援的實際含義

N+1 備援（N+1 Redundancy）是 GMP 廠房設計的基本安全原則：任一主要元件故障，系統總容量仍維持 100%。

XX% N+1 備援則是較實際的版本：例如「50% N+1 備援」表示任一元件故障後，系統仍維持 50% 容量——對非無菌製造區域可能可接受，但對 Grade A/B 空間通常需要 100% N+1。

CDMO 設計時的商業考量：N+1 備援增加初始建設成本，但可防止因設備故障導致的批次失敗（每批生物製藥價值可達數千萬台幣）。需用 ROI 模型評估最適備援策略。

10.2 Methods of Environmental Classification through Risk Assessment

In a risk-based approach to area classification, increasing exposure risk (based on process sensitivity, bio-safety, or the presence of downstream mitigations) should be considered in light of established layers of protection, in order to determine the appropriate space classification for an operation. There are numerous methods to do this analysis. Tables 10.1 and 10.2 are an example of one possible approach to risk assessment.

In stage 1, the risk of contamination is determined based on the stage of the process and the inherent risk to contamination of the process. The risk may derive from a lack of isolation from the ambient environment, high energy in adjacent processes (high risk of aerosol contaminants), the presence of growth media in the process, or the inherent fragility of the organism or protein. The product of these two factors yields an exposure risk ranking (given here on a 1 to 5 scale of Low, Med-Low, Medium, Med-High, and High).

In the second step, this exposure risk is adjusted by evaluating the layers of protection available to shield the process from contamination. The more layers of protection available to prevent contamination the less critical the ambient environment is to product quality. The risk of failure of the protection should be included in this evaluation; for example, a silicone tube has a higher risk of failure than a stainless steel tube; therefore, the layers of protection it provides would be less.

This example equates the levels of protection, approximately, with log reduction of contaminants provided by that layer. This concept is leveraged from sterile processing where log reductions in contamination are the standard for rating process closure. This concept is also well aligned with the environmental classifications, where each succeeding lower classification number is one order of magnitude cleaner than its predecessor. This example equates the five levels of risk with five classifications (Grade 5, Grade 7, Grade 8, CNC+, and CNC) as discussed in the following section.

Notes:
  1. A fully hermetic system should provide > 4 log reduction in contaminants or 4 layers of protection.
  2. An open micro-environment can typically provide a > 1 to 2 log reduction in contaminants, or 1 to 2 layers of protection.
  3. A closed micro-environment can provide a > 3 log reduction in contaminants, or 3 layers of protection.
  4. The log reductions proposed herein are derived from sterile and containment equipment performance.
  5. The risk of failure should be included in the evaluation (e.g., stainless steel piping would rank as greater protection than a plastic tube).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 風險導向分級的邏輯框架

這是本指南最具實踐價值的工具之一。傳統方法是「照本宣科」套用法規等級；風險導向方法則要求：

最終所需環境等級 = f（暴露風險, 防護層數）

核心邏輯：
• 暴露風險高 → 需要更嚴格的環境等級
• 防護層數多 → 可以降低對環境等級的要求
• 兩者相互抵消，得出最低必要等級

這種方法讓設施設計者有科學依據去主張「我的製程雖然是高風險步驟，但因為有4層保護，所以只需要 CNC 等級環境」——這在監管溝通中是強有力的論據。

比喻說明 — 風險分級 = 防災等級評估

想像建築結構工程師評估一棟建築需要多強的防震規格：地震風險等級（暴露風險）× 結構防護措施（防護層數）= 所需建築規範等級。

台北市（高地震區）+ 純木造結構（0 層防護）→ 需要最嚴格規範（Grade 5 等效）。
台北市（高地震區）+ 鋼骨+隔震墊+制震器（4 層防護）→ 標準規範即可（CNC 等效）。
宜蘭外海（低地震區）+ 任何結構 → 寬鬆規範（CNC）。

關鍵洞察：不是地點危險就必然要最貴的設計——防護措施可以「對沖」風險。

重點提示 — 理解 Table 10.2 的閱讀方式

Table 10.2 是一個「查表工具」：

  1. 先從 Table 10.1 確定暴露風險等級（High / Med-High / Medium / Med-Low / Low）
  2. 再數算有多少層防護（0 = 完全開放；4 = 完全密封系統）
  3. 查表得出最低所需環境等級

關鍵結論：即使是「High」暴露風險（如開放式生物反應器進料），只要有 4 層保護，環境等級可低至 CNC——而非直覺上的 Grade 5。這是節省建設成本的關鍵槓桿。

反之，「Low」風險但零防護（如完全暴露的純化操作），只需 CNC——說明這個步驟本身就不需要高等級環境。

防護層數的對數等效計算

防護等效關係（近似）：
  0 層防護 → 完全暴露（0 log 減少）
  1-2 層  → 開放微環境（RABS等）
             1–2 log 污染減少
  3 層    → 封閉微環境（隔離器等）
             >3 log 污染減少
  4 層    → 完全密封系統
             >4 log 污染減少

材料可靠性影響（Note 5）：
  不鏽鋼管路 > 矽膠管路
  （不鏽鋼失效率低 → 保護層「信用值」更高）

環境等級間距 = 1 個數量級：
  Grade 5 (ISO 5) → Grade 7 (ISO 7) 約 100x 差異
  Grade 7 (ISO 7) → Grade 8 (ISO 8) 約 10x 差異
  → 每升一個等級 ≈ 1 log 更乾淨

實務應用 — CDMO 的分級效益計算

以一個 500 m² 的純化操作區為例：

  • 傳統保守做法（Grade C / Grade 8）：HVAC 換氣率 20–30 ACH，建設成本高 +30%，年電費高 +200萬
  • 封閉製程 + 風險評估（CNC+）：換氣率 15 ACH，建設成本降低，年電費節省 200萬以上
  • 完全密封系統（CNC）：換氣率 6–10 ACH，最大節省

15 年廠房生命週期內，正確的風險導向分級可節省 3,000萬台幣以上的能源和建設成本，同時完全符合法規要求。這份文件（Appendix 1, Section 10.2）就是你向客戶或監管機構解釋為何選擇較低等級的技術正當性文件。

10.2.1 Other Approaches to Risk Assessment

FMEA is one of the most popular methods of risk assessment and prioritization. Most companies have a standard approach (evaluating the probability, impact and detectability of an environmental control failure) that is fully applicable to this type of assessment. Given the consistency of the factors that must be considered, as discussed in the preceding, an approach with a fixed taxonomy can be used to promote a consistent evaluation of risk and resultant environmental controls. The following is an example of one such approach and method.

Figure 10.1: Example Environmental Risk Assessment Figure
[Risk assessment tool figure referenced in source — shows a multi-factor FMEA framework for environmental risk evaluation]

The above tool is used in a multi-step process when performing an environmental risk assessment to determine area biosafety and cleanliness classifications:

Step 1:

Look at every room or function in the facility to consider the risk associated with the activities in that room.

Step 2:

Look at every activity and sub activity (e.g., charging, processing, discharging) in the room.

Step 3:

Look at every activity and sub-activity and assign a risk associated with its sensitivity to the room environment. Consider the level of energy, the level of openness, the sensitivity of the process itself (presence of growth media, hand manipulation, etc.)

Look for the presence of organisms requiring biosafety.

Examine both the impact of contamination as well as the probability of contamination at that step.

Step 4:

Look at the layers of protection against the above sensitivity to the room environment.

Step 5:

Look at both the impact of contamination at the stage in the process and the presence of downstream processing that can further mitigate the environmental risk.

Step 6:

Take the product of the preceding factors and determine a level of environmental control required. Gowning may be a factor included in this determination.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — FMEA 在環境分級中的應用

FMEA（Failure Mode and Effects Analysis，失效模式與效應分析）是品質風險管理（QRM）的核心工具，ICH Q9 推薦使用。將其應用於環境分級時，三個評分維度為：

  • 嚴重性（Severity）：若環境控制失效，對產品品質的衝擊有多大？
  • 發生率（Probability）：環境控制失效的可能性有多高？
  • 可偵測性（Detectability）：若發生，能否及時發現並採取措施？

三者相乘得出 RPN（風險優先數），RPN 高者需要更嚴格的環境控制；RPN 低者可降低等級要求。

比喻說明 — 六步驟如同「安全審查清單」

這六個步驟就像航空公司飛行員的飛行前清單（Pre-flight Checklist）——每一步都有明確目的，必須依序執行，不能跳過：

  • 步驟 1–2：把「飛行計畫」分解到每個細節（每個房間的每個操作）
  • 步驟 3：評估每個細節的「危險性」（能量大小、開放程度、生長培養基存在）
  • 步驟 4–5：盤點「安全設備」（防護層數、下游去除步驟）
  • 步驟 6：根據以上得出「本次飛行需要幾名空服員」（所需環境等級）

關鍵是：每個房間、每個操作步驟都要獨立評估——一個大房間裡可能同時有需要 Grade 8 的操作和只需 CNC 的操作。

重點提示 — 步驟 5 中「下游緩解」的重要性

步驟 5 中「下游製程的緩解作用（downstream mitigation）」是一個常被忽視但極有價值的因素：

如果某個可能被污染的操作，其下游有多道去除步驟（例如：Protein A 純化 + 病毒去除過濾 + UF/DF），那麼即使在這個步驟引入了少量環境污染，後續製程也能清除它——因此環境要求可以降低。

反之，若某步驟是「最後一道關卡」（如最終配製充填），下游沒有任何過濾去除，環境要求就必須最嚴格。

這正是為何 API 步驟（最終填充前）在 Table 10.1 中被標示為「High」暴露風險的原因。

六步驟決策框架總結

Step 1: 列出所有房間/功能區
         ↓
Step 2: 分解每個房間內的每個操作
         （充填、加料、排放等子活動）
         ↓
Step 3: 評估每個操作的暴露敏感性
         · 能量水平（aerosol 生成風險）
         · 開放程度
         · 生長培養基的存在
         · 生物安全等級要求
         · 污染衝擊 × 污染概率
         ↓
Step 4: 計算防護層數
         （密封容器、隔離器、封閉管路等）
         ↓
Step 5: 評估下游緩解措施
         （後續純化步驟能否清除污染）
         ↓
Step 6: 查 Table 10.2
         → 確定最低所需環境等級
         （+ 考量人員更衣要求）

實務應用 — 如何將這份報告用於監管溝通

在 FDA Pre-approval Inspection (PAI) 或 EMA GMP 稽查中，若稽查員質疑某區域的環境等級選擇，這套六步驟 FMEA 文件就是你的技術防禦工事：

  • 文件化每個操作的暴露風險評分（附理由）
  • 文件化防護層數計算（含材料可靠性分析）
  • 文件化下游緩解措施清單
  • 最終等級選擇的邏輯鏈（從 Table 10.1 → Table 10.2 → 選定等級）

這份文件一旦在設計階段完成並獲批，就成為你設施設計的「科學正當性依據（Scientific Justification）」，在後續所有監管溝通中都可以引用。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 永續設計的核心槓桿是封閉製程（Closed Processing）：封閉製程允許降低環境等級，等級降低直接轉化為數十倍的能耗節省和大幅降低建設成本，同時改善 ESG 表現。
  • ISPE 統合了三大法規體系（FDA / EMA / PIC/S）的等級定義：Grade 5 = Grade A, Grade 7 = Grade B, Grade 8 = Grade C, CNC+ ≈ Grade D。掌握這個對應表，可以在跨法規溝通時避免混亂。
  • CNC 和 CNC+ 是生物製藥封閉製程最常用的環境等級：它們不是「次等」的無等級空間，而是有完整 IQ/OQ 文件支持的設計控制空間，在閉合製程設計中具有重大成本優勢。
  • 風險導向分級的兩步驟邏輯：Step 1 從製程階段和污染敏感性確定暴露風險等級；Step 2 根據防護層數（0–4層）折算出最低所需環境等級，查 Table 10.2 得出答案。
  • FMEA 六步驟是監管溝通的技術防禦工事：完整的六步驟文件讓低於傳統保守等級的設計選擇有科學正當性，在 FDA PAI 和 EMA 稽查中可作為強力支持文件。
  • 下游緩解（Downstream Mitigation）是降低等級的重要論據：若某操作後有多道純化去除步驟，環境要求可合理降低；反之，「最終關卡」操作的環境要求必須最嚴格。
