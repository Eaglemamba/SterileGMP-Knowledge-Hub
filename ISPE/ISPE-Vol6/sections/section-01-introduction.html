1.1 Background 背景

原文內容 Original Text

This second edition of the ISPE Baseline® Guide: Biopharmaceutical Manufacturing Facilities intends to further reinforce the concepts described in the first edition of the Guide, provide examples of how these concepts can be put into practice, and detail the value and benefits of the approach described.

The biopharmaceutical industry has adapted to reflect available technologies, products with more focused patient populations, regulatory conditions, and continued pressures on costs, while maintaining high quality standards. This edition of the Guide develops concepts to reflect how these changes affect biopharmaceutical manufacturing facilities without sacrificing product quality, by reducing risk and enhancing the manufacturing control strategy.

As the relevant technology has evolved, the implementation of closed systems has become more accepted and routine; the Guide emphasizes the use of closed systems as an approach to mitigating risks associated with the production of biopharmaceuticals.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 為何需要專屬的生物製藥設施指南？

生物製藥 (Biopharmaceutical) 是指以活的細胞或生物體為基礎所生產的治療性藥物。相較於傳統化學合成的小分子藥物 (small molecule drug)，生物製藥具有以下根本差異：

  • 分子量極大：小分子藥物分子量通常小於 500 Da；生物製藥如單株抗體 (MAb) 分子量高達 150,000 Da — 大了 300 倍以上
  • 結構複雜：具有一、二、三、四級蛋白質結構，對剪切力 (shear force)、溫度、pH 極為敏感
  • 由活細胞製造：製程涉及細胞培養、發酵，無法用傳統有機化學合成路徑重現
  • 污染類型不同：除了化學污染外，還需面對微生物污染 (microbial contamination) 和病毒污染 (viral contamination) 的獨特挑戰

這些根本差異使得小分子製藥的設施設計原則無法直接套用，因此 ISPE 才編寫本專屬指南。

比喻說明 — 廚房 vs. 手術室

如果把小分子製藥比喻成「烹飪」（有明確食譜、化學反應可重現），那麼生物製藥更像是「培育一個珍稀的活體培養物」。你不只要控制溫度和食材，還要確保整個環境中沒有任何外來的微生物「闖入者」——因為一旦培養物被污染，整個批次就報廢了。這就是為什麼設施設計必須從根本上不同。

重點提示 — 密閉系統是本指南的核心哲學

背景段落最後一句話極為重要：「the Guide emphasizes the use of closed systems as an approach to mitigating risks」。這不是一個建議，而是整本指南所有後續設計決策的核心哲學前提。理解「為什麼要密閉」，才能理解後面各章節中每一個設計選擇的邏輯。

練習思考

  1. 為什麼生物製藥生產成本遠高於傳統小分子藥物？這對設施設計有什麼影響？
  2. 「closed systems 的實施已成為常規」這句話說明了什麼產業趨勢？這對於還在使用傳統開放式廠房的 CDMO 意味著什麼競爭壓力？
1.2 Scope 適用範圍

原文內容 Original Text

The ISPE Baseline® Guide: Biopharmaceutical Manufacturing Facilities (Second Edition) applies to new facilities for the development and manufacture of biopharmaceutical drug substances (or Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs)). This Guide applies to clinical and commercial cGMP production facilities.

Closed systems and closed processing is the central concept throughout this Guide. Closing a bioprocess impacts area classifications, layouts, and how a facility is operated. This Guide is intended to support the development of decisions which allow compliant and cost effective design of biopharmaceutical manufacturing facilities.

Types of drug substances considered in this Guide include:

  • Protein therapeutics
  • Synthetic proteins
  • Polypeptides
  • Monoclonal antibodies
  • Vaccines1
  • Biopharmaceutical drug substances isolated from tissues or body fluids
  • Transgenic biopharmaceutical drug substances
  • Gene therapy biopharmaceutical drug substances
  • Stem cells

1 Note: additional design criteria could be required for specific classes of biopharmaceutical drug substances (e.g., live vaccines). Concepts in this Guide can apply to various scales of operation from single digit liters to tens of thousands of liters.

The facility designs and processes described in this Guide are intended to acknowledge guidelines, such as ICH Guidance for Industry Q7 GMP for APIs [1], PIC/S Guide to GMP for Medicinal Products Part II [2], and ICH Q11 [3], and meet requirements, such as Eudralex Volume 4 GMP Part II Basic Requirements for Active Substances used as Starting Materials [4], and to US GMPs, e.g., 21 CFR Part 211 [5].

The intended audience for this Guide includes:

  • Professionals involved in the design, construction, qualification, and operation of biopharmaceutical manufacturing facilities
  • Regulatory and quality personnel involved in evaluating technical decisions associated with biopharmaceutical manufacturing facility design

The concepts in this Guide can be applied to other facilities, such as those for blood fractionation, although additional information may be required (e.g., information found in EMA Annex 14 for Blood Fractionation [6] and/or CFDA Annex 4: Blood Products) [7].

This Guide is not intended to be used in the retrofit or evaluation of older facilities; however, the execution of these concepts and strategies can be evaluated and implemented as it benefits production in a major modification to an existing facility.

This Guide is not intended as a replacement for cGMP regulations. This Guide is intended to support options and practices to achieve cGMP compliance.

The processes and facility designs described in this Guide do not apply to filling and/or packaging of sterile drug product.

The appropriate regulatory agencies should be consulted before advancing into detailed design and before starting major construction activities.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 本指南的適用對象與法規框架

藥物原料 (Drug Substance / API) vs. 藥品 (Drug Product)：這個區別至關重要。本指南僅涵蓋 Drug Substance 的生產設施，不包含後續的充填包裝 (Fill & Finish) 作業。Fill & Finish 適用的是 EU GMP Annex 1（無菌藥品製造）等另一套規範。

適用法規的層次架構：

  • 美國：21 CFR Part 211（藥品 cGMP）；API 製造通常參照 ICH Q7
  • 歐盟：EudraLex Vol.4 GMP Part II（API 製造要求）
  • ICH：Q7（API GMP）、Q11（化學合成 & 生技 API 開發）
  • PIC/S：Guide to GMP Part II（與 EU GMP Part II 高度一致）

產品類型一覽 — 為什麼這份清單很重要

本指南涵蓋的產品類型從蛋白質到幹細胞，跨度極大。每種產品對製造環境的要求可能不同：

  • Monoclonal antibodies (MAbs，單株抗體)：目前生物製藥市場最大類別，哺乳類細胞培養（CHO cells）為主
  • Vaccines（疫苗）：可能需要額外的 biosafety 分級考量（活疫苗尤其嚴格）
  • Gene therapy（基因治療）：新興領域，含病毒載體（AAV, lentivirus），需特殊的密閉和生物安全設計
  • Stem cells（幹細胞）：最高複雜度，通常在小規模、高度受控環境中生產

比喻說明 — 製程密閉就像密封真空包裝流水線

想像一個生產食品的工廠。傳統的「開放式廚房」讓工人可以隨時伸手操作，但也讓空氣中的灰塵、細菌都有機會落入食品中。這就是「開放式系統 (Open System)」的概念。

相較之下，現代的「密封真空包裝流水線」從食材進入到產品出廠，整個過程都在封閉管線中進行，外界環境完全無法進入。這就是「密閉系統 (Closed System)」的概念。

本指南的核心訊息是：當你能讓製程「密閉」，你就可以降低周圍環境（廠房潔淨度等級）的要求——因為環境已經不是風險的主要來源了。這直接影響廠房建設成本。

重點提示 — 三個重要的「不適用」

指南明確說明了三個不在範疇內的情況，這在實際工作中容易混淆：

  1. 不適用於舊廠房改造評估（但如有重大修繕，可部分採用）
  2. 不取代 cGMP 法規（法規優先，指南是輔助合規的工具）
  3. 不適用於無菌藥品的充填/包裝（Fill & Finish 適用 Annex 1 等其他指南）

對於一個 CDMO 而言，如果你同時承接 Drug Substance 生產和 Fill & Finish，需要清楚知道哪個廠房的哪個區域應該引用哪份法規指引。

實務應用 Practical Application

一個 CDMO 正在規劃一個新廠，客戶的管線包含 MAb（用於癌症治療）和一個新型 AAV 基因治療載體。從本章範疇，你需要注意：

  • 兩者都在本指南範疇內（都是生物製藥 API）
  • AAV 屬於基因治療類別，需要額外的生物安全設計考量
  • 如果你的工廠後面還要執行充填，那充填區需要另外引用 Annex 1 設計
  • 在開始詳細設計之前，必須先諮詢主管機關（FDA/EMA）

練習思考

  1. 本指南明確排除 Fill & Finish 作業。請思考：Drug Substance 生產的最後一個步驟（例如配方調配 formulation 或無菌過濾）和 Fill & Finish 的邊界在哪裡？這個邊界對廠房設計有什麼影響？
  2. 本指南同時符合 US GMPs (21 CFR 211) 和 EU GMP (EudraLex Vol.4)。請思考：在實際設計中，如果兩者有衝突，應如何處理？
1.3 Key Concepts and Terms 核心概念與術語

原文內容 Original Text

Several key concepts and terms are defined in this section of the Guide. Definitions for other terms used in this Guide are provided in the Glossary (see Appendix 3). When these terms are used in the context of this Guide, these definitions should be used.

This Guide discusses the concepts of closed processing and the potential impact of closing a biopharmaceutical drug substance manufacturing process on facility design. Critical Process Parameters (CPPs) and Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs) should be understood before defining process closure and other strategies. Critical process conditions should be understood and facility design should include critical design features.

Aseptic Operation

Operations that are devoid of measurable (detectable) bioburden. Aseptic operations generally require sterilization of the environment, equipment and process solutions to achieve the sterile state prior to use. Use of Biosafety Cabinets (BSCs) and laminar flow hoods are useful when aseptic open operations are required.

Axenic State

A culture that includes the intended living organism, but is free of all other contaminating organisms.

Bioburden-Free

A condition where bioburden is not detectable. Bioreactors used in cell culture or fermentation operations should be sterile prior to use for consistent and reproducible performance. Biosafety Cabinets (BSCs), Restricted Access Barriers (RABs) and isolators should provide an environment free of bioburden for consistent and reproducible aseptic operations. Sterile product manufacturing testing and qualification guidelines are by necessity more stringent and prescriptive than those for active pharmaceutical ingredient manufacturing and therefore are not required.

Bioprocessing

Bioprocessing is the attainment of medicinal products from living organisms. Includes all operations used in the manufacture of biologics including upstream processes such as inoculum preparation, cell culture/fermentation processes, harvest and clarification processes such as sedimentation and filtration processes, downstream processing such as chromatography and filtration processes. Bioprocessing also may include other aqueous processes, such as plasma fractionation and chemically synthesized drug manufacturing performed in non-bacteriostatic solvents.

Fill finish operations are not included in this group; however, many of the concepts presented would still apply.

Briefly Exposed

Open processes containing process and/or product components that are rendered closed by means of an appropriate closing process. Examples of briefly exposed processes include open buffer or media preparations where the solution is "briefly" exposed to the environment prior to closing by sterile filtration and/or thermal sanitization. For solution preparation, it is important that the interval between formulation and sanitization be defined and validated.

Briefly exposed operations may be performed in Controlled Not Classified (CNC) (see Chapter 4) or low bioburden environments if measures used to close the process are appropriate to mitigate risk of contamination from the environment. Pre-closure processing and hold periods and conditions must be carefully monitored and validated. The premise is that in-process (in-line) sterile filtration or thermal sterilization is more effective (and more appropriate) than a classified environment in mitigating the risk of contamination from the environment.

Local protected environments may be used for open processes. Examples include BSCs, Unidirectional Down Flow Hoods (UDFHs) or Unidirectional Horizontal Flow Hoods, isolators and RABs. A formal risk assessment (see ICH Q9) is required to fully evaluate the appropriateness and quality of the environment used for bioprocess operations.

Carryover

Contaminants detected in process streams arising from insufficient removal of contaminating components from previous manufacturing steps or batches. Maximum allowable carryover residues should be estimated and meet defined criteria.

Closed System

A process system that is designed and operated such that the product is never exposed to the surrounding environment. Additions to and draws from closed systems must be performed in a completely closed fashion. Sterile filters may be used to provide effective barriers from contaminants in the environment. A system is closed (or isolated from the environment) when the risk of contamination to the product or process cannot be mitigated by housing the operation in a bioburden-free or particulate-free environment.

"In Quality Risk Management (QRM) verification, the environment does not represent a critical aspect of a closed process... In a closed system, the probability of detecting a contaminant from the environment within the processing period is less than the process acceptance criteria."

A closed process is one that has been validated to show that there are sufficient layers of protection to mitigate the risk of contamination from the environment. Examples of closed systems include sterile single use bags supplied with integrated aseptic connection devices.

Critical Quality Attribute (CQA)

Physical, chemical, biological or microbiological properties or characteristics that should be within an appropriate limit, range, or distribution to ensure the desired product quality (ICH Q8(R2)).

Critical Process Parameter (CPP)

A measurable input (input material attribute or operating parameter) or output (process state variable or output material attribute) of a process step that must be controlled to achieve the desired product quality and process consistency.

Crossover (a type of Cross Contamination)

Contamination of a system by components or contaminants found in a neighboring system. Crossover typically occurs with open processes sharing environments.

Functionally Closed

Functionally Closed systems may be opened but are "rendered closed" by a cleaning, sanitization and/or sterilization process that is appropriate or consistent with the process requirements, whether sterile, aseptic or low bioburden. These systems shall remain closed during production within the system. Examples include process vessels that may be cleaned in place (CIP) and steamed in place (SIP) between uses.

Low Bioburden (Operation)

Process where a limited and controlled level of measurable bioburden is acceptable under specific conditions. (e.g., WFI, chromatography operations).

Module

Modules in the biopharmaceutical design context may consist of:

  • Integrated process lines (individual continuous bioprocessing modules)
  • Total integrated facility sections, including building, process, services and utilities elements
  • Process or building services/HVAC/utilities skids or repetitive items
  • Building sectional modules
  • Modular process or building elements

Open Process

A process that is exposed to the environment and therefore requires environmental conditions to mitigate the risk of contamination from the environment. Under QRM verification, the environment within the RABs and/or BSCs represents a critical aspect of the open process. Open sterile and aseptic operations must be performed in an environment where the probability of contamination is acceptably low. Open bioburden-controlled processing may be performed in a Grade 7 or 8 environment as appropriate for the unit operation.

Process Contact Surface

Surfaces of piping, components, equipment or systems that may or may not be exposed to product, but may contain solutions that are potentially exposed to product or product intermediates.

Product Contact Surface

Surfaces of piping, components, equipment or systems that are exposed to product. When systems used in multiple products, batches or process steps are reused, their product contact surfaces require cleaning and sanitization to reduce bioburden and the potential for carryover and crossover.

Sterile

Devoid of life. Zero bioburden. Free of bioburden.

Sterilization

Form of sanitization that results in a bioburden-free (sterile) condition. The different sterilization methods used in bioprocessing generally include thermal sterilization, sterile filtration (0.2 µm or better), chemical sterilization (e.g., VHP, chlorine dioxide, ozone) or irradiation (e.g., gamma, UV) to achieve the sterile state.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 無菌操作 (Aseptic Operation) vs. 純淨狀態 (Axenic State)

這兩個詞常被混淆，但有重要區別：

Aseptic Operation（無菌操作）：關注的是「操作環境與物品中沒有可測到的微生物負荷」。重點在於操作過程的無菌維持——包括滅菌環境、滅菌設備、滅菌液體的搭配使用。

Axenic State（純淨狀態）：專門用於細胞/微生物培養領域，指「培養物中只有目標生物體，沒有其他污染性微生物」。例如一個 CHO 細胞培養物處於 axenic state，表示裡面只有 CHO 細胞，沒有細菌、真菌等外來污染物。

生物製藥的上游製程（細胞培養）必須維持 axenic state；這是確保產品一致性和安全性的基礎。一旦 axenic state 被破壞（即發生「染菌 contamination」），整批細胞培養就必須報廢。

核心概念解析 — 無微生物負荷 (Bioburden-Free) vs. 低微生物負荷 (Low Bioburden)

這兩個條件代表不同的嚴格程度，適用於製程的不同階段：

  • Bioburden-Free（無微生物負荷）：完全不可偵測到任何微生物。生物反應器在使用前必須達到此狀態（滅菌）。等同於「無菌 (Sterile)」狀態。
  • Low Bioburden（低微生物負荷）：允許有限且受控的微生物存在。例如層析純化步驟，其本質上無法完全滅菌，但透過管理措施將微生物負荷控制在可接受範圍內。

正確區分這兩個概念對於設施設計至關重要：不同區域需要不同的環境控制等級。

核心概念解析 — 密閉系統 (Closed System) 與開放式系統 (Open System) 的核心差異

這是整本指南最重要的概念，值得深入理解：

Closed System（密閉系統）的判斷標準：「產品永遠不暴露於周圍環境中」且「經過驗證（validated）證明污染風險已被充分控制」。關鍵在於：環境不是密閉系統的關鍵因素——這意味著密閉系統可以放在較低等級的潔淨室中，甚至是非潔淨區域。

Open Process（開放式製程）：產品直接暴露於環境，因此環境品質（潔淨度等級、微生物控制）成為關鍵控制因素。

Functionally Closed（功能性密閉）：介於兩者之間的重要概念。系統物理上可以開啟（例如 CIP/SIP 後重新使用的設備），但透過適當的清潔消毒/滅菌流程使其「等效密閉」。

比喻說明 — 開放式廚房 vs. 密封真空包裝流水線

想像兩種不同的食品加工場景：

開放式廚房（Open System）：廚師在開放空間中切菜、烹調，食材暴露在空氣中。為了保持食品安全，廚房必須維持高度整潔、溫濕度控制、人員衛生管理——這就像是開放式製程需要嚴格的潔淨室環境。

密封真空包裝流水線（Closed System）：從原料進入到成品輸出，整個過程都在封閉管線和容器中進行，沒有任何暴露點。生產車間不需要是無塵室——因為產品根本不會接觸到環境。這就是密閉系統的邏輯。

設計決策的關鍵：密閉系統可以讓你把廠房設計在較低環境等級的空間，省下大量的潔淨室建設成本。這就是 ISPE Vol.6 指南強調「process closure」的商業邏輯所在。

概念框架 — 污染風險的決定因素

污染風險 = f(製程開放程度, 環境品質, 持續時間)

密閉系統：製程開放程度 → 0
  → 環境品質的重要性降低
  → 可使用較低等級潔淨室 (CNC 或 Grade 7/8)
  → 廠房建設成本降低

開放式系統：製程開放程度 > 0
  → 環境品質成為關鍵控制點
  → 需要較高等級潔淨室 (Grade 5/6/7)
  → 廠房建設成本較高

QRM 驗證原則：
  密閉系統 → 環境不是關鍵因素 (no-impact)
  開放式系統 → 環境是關鍵因素 (direct/indirect impact)

重點提示 — CPP 和 CQA 是製程密閉策略的前提

指南特別強調：在定義「製程密閉 (process closure)」策略之前，必須先充分理解 CPP（關鍵製程參數）和 CQA（關鍵品質屬性）。這個順序非常重要：

  1. 首先定義 CQA — 什麼是這個產品的關鍵品質特性？
  2. 接著識別 CPP — 哪些製程參數直接影響 CQA？
  3. 然後評估污染風險 — 環境污染如何影響 CPP 和 CQA？
  4. 最後決定密閉策略 — 對哪些步驟需要密閉？需要什麼等級的密閉？

這個邏輯鏈條體現了 QbD（Quality by Design）的精髓：設計決策必須以科學理解為基礎。

重點提示 — Briefly Exposed 的重要性

「短暫暴露 (Briefly Exposed)」是一個在實務中極為重要的中間態概念。配製緩衝液或培養基時，溶液不可避免地會短暫暴露於環境中，但隨後通過無菌過濾或熱滅菌「關閉」製程。

關鍵設計要求：從配製到密閉的時間間隔必須被定義和驗證。這個時間窗口的長短直接影響：你需要什麼等級的製備環境（例如是否需要潔淨室），以及如何監控和驗證這個步驟。

重要洞察：指南指出，在線（in-line）無菌過濾或熱滅菌比廠房潔淨等級更有效地控制污染風險。這是一個反直覺但重要的設計原則。

實務應用 — CDMO 多產品廠房的 Crossover 風險

對於承接多個客戶不同產品的 CDMO，Crossover（交叉污染的一種）是重大風險。根據定義，Crossover 發生在開放式製程共享環境時，或當一個系統的密閉完整性被破壞時。

防止 Crossover 的設計策略：

  • 最佳：盡量使用密閉系統 → 物理上隔絕交叉污染的可能
  • 次之：為不同產品分配獨立的潔淨室或廠房分區
  • 驗證：即使是密閉系統，也需驗證密閉的完整性，確保無洩漏

練習思考

  1. 一個生物反應器在批次生產後，需要 CIP 清洗和 SIP 蒸汽滅菌，然後再用於下一批。這個系統屬於「Closed」、「Functionally Closed」、還是「Open」？為什麼？
  2. 如果一個層析步驟（chromatography）在操作時需要連接管線和採樣，這使它成為「Open Process」嗎？如何才能讓它達到「Functionally Closed」的標準？
  3. 「低微生物負荷 (Low Bioburden)」和「無菌 (Sterile)」的根本區別是什麼？在製程設計中，什麼情況下可以接受 Low Bioburden，什麼情況下必須達到 Sterile？

1.4.1 Synopsis of Guide Chapters

Chapter 2: Regulatory
Chapter 2 provides an overview of the regulatory requirements for facility design and summarizes the sources that influence both regulators and industry best practices.

Chapter 3: Risk Management
Chapter 3 provides an overview of several approaches to risk management that may be applied to biopharmaceutical facilities.

Chapter 4: Process Closure
Closing a bioprocess impacts area classifications, layouts, and how a facility is operated. This chapter helps to define what a closed process is, and through the use of risk assessment tools, indicate how closure can be demonstrated. Examples of typical closed processes are given, as well as how some open processes can be closed.

Chapter 5: Operations
This chapter describes trends in the operation of biopharmaceutical facilities. Facilities are operating under diverse requirements — often they are multi-product facilities requiring short product campaigns and efficient and thorough changeover between products. Higher titers and products focused on smaller markets allow for reduced scale and increased use of single-use technology.

Chapter 6: Facility Layouts
Chapter 6 provides guidelines and discussions for the design of integrated cGMP facilities. This is in the context of regulatory, process, production, and technological factors that significantly influence the layout and flow of a production facility.

Chapter 7: Architectural Design
As the biopharmaceutical industry matures, there is an emerging pattern to many of the processes, equipment, infrastructure, and facilities. The use of modular process skid and facility concepts allows for faster and less costly projects. Chapter 7 discusses master planning which continues to be important as organizations plan their future and allow for growth opportunities against changing product portfolios and market demands.

Chapter 8: Mechanical and Electrical Systems
This chapter reviews requirements with a focus on the unique balancing of risks required for biopharmaceutical and vaccine facilities. The chapter discusses how mechanical systems are focused on environmental control to manage the conflicting risks of general product contamination, cross-contamination, biosafety, and biosecurity. The chapter also discusses how electrical systems can further support the mitigation of risk through support of mechanical systems in emergency situations.

Chapter 9: Sustainability
Carbon footprint, green, environmentally friendly, LEED, and BREEAM are now familiar terms, but this is still a changing area with new requirements and legislation across the globe. This chapter provides background and links to some of the latest trends in this area and how they affect biopharmaceutical thinking.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 各章之間的邏輯關聯

本指南的章節結構並非隨機排列，而是遵循一個由「基礎原則」到「具體設計」的邏輯層次：

  • 第1章（導論）：建立概念框架 — 為什麼需要不同的設施？核心術語是什麼？
  • 第2章（法規）：確立合規義務 — 必須滿足哪些要求？
  • 第3章（風險管理）：提供決策工具 — 如何評估和量化風險？
  • 第4章（製程密閉）：核心設計哲學 — 如何決定哪些製程需要密閉？
  • 第5章（營運）：應用背景 — 現代生物製藥廠房如何運作？
  • 第6章（設施佈局）：實體設計 — 如何整合所有因素設計廠房佈局？
  • 第7-8章（建築/機電）：工程細節 — 具體的建築和設備系統如何設計？
  • 第9章（永續性）：前瞻思維 — 如何兼顧環保要求？

比喻說明 — 建造一棟生物製藥廠就像蓋一棟醫院

你不會在不了解病患需求（CQA）和醫療流程（製程）的情況下開始設計醫院的建築平面圖。你需要先知道：要服務什麼類型的病人？有哪些感染控制要求？如何讓潔淨區和污染區分流？本指南的章節順序就是這個「從需求到設計」的邏輯流程。

重點提示 — 第3章和第4章是核心

對於設施設計工程師和品質人員來說，第3章（風險管理）和第4章（製程密閉）是最重要的兩章。它們回答了最關鍵的問題：

  • 第3章：如何系統性地評估污染風險？（工具：FMEA、HAZOP、HACCP、ICH Q9）
  • 第4章：基於風險評估結果，如何決定哪些步驟需要密閉，以及對應的環境分級？

這兩章的結論直接決定了你的廠房需要多少 ISO 5/6/7/8 潔淨室，從而直接影響廠房建設成本。

重點提示 — 第5章提到的趨勢對 CDMO 的意義

第5章提到幾個重要的產業趨勢：

  • 多產品設施 (Multi-product facilities)：為了降低成本，越來越多工廠需要在同一設施中生產多個不同客戶的產品，這對於防止交叉污染的設計要求極高
  • 更高的效價 (Higher titers)：製程改進使得每批可以生產更多產品，意味著可以用更小規模的設施服務更多患者
  • 一次性使用技術 (Single-use technology)：有助於降低交叉污染風險、減少清潔驗證工作量

實務應用 — 如何使用本指南作為決策工具

在實際的廠房設計項目中，建議按以下流程使用本指南：

  1. 第1章：確認你的產品類型是否在範疇內
  2. 第2章：識別適用的法規要求（哪個市場？FDA/EMA？）
  3. 第3章：選擇適當的風險評估方法（FMEA/HAZOP/HACCP）
  4. 第4章：基於風險評估決定哪些製程需要密閉，決定環境分級
  5. 第6章：根據製程密閉策略設計廠房佈局和流向
  6. 第7-8章：具體建築和機電設計

練習思考

  1. 如果一個 CDMO 同時有早期臨床（Phase 1/2）和商業化生產的業務，指南第5章提到的「產品聚焦患者群體」這個趨勢意味著什麼？對工廠設計有什麼彈性要求？
  2. 第9章談到永續性（LEED、BREEAM）。在生物製藥廠房設計中，潔淨室的能耗極高（大量的空調換氣）。製程密閉策略如何幫助降低能耗，同時符合永續性目標？

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary

  • 生物製藥的本質差異決定了設施設計必須採用不同的原則：活體細胞製造、分子量大、對污染高度敏感，需要比傳統小分子藥物更嚴格的製程控制
  • 製程密閉 (Process Closure) 是本指南的核心哲學：密閉系統可以降低對周圍環境的依賴，從而在不犧牲產品品質的前提下降低廠房建設和運營成本
  • 六個核心術語構成理解全書的基礎：Aseptic Operation（無菌操作）、Axenic State（純淨狀態）、Bioburden-Free（無微生物負荷）、Closed System（密閉系統）、Functionally Closed（功能性密閉）、Open Process（開放式製程）
  • 本指南適用於 Drug Substance 的生產設施，不適用於 Fill & Finish（充填包裝）；同時兼顧 21 CFR Part 211（US）和 EudraLex Vol.4 GMP Part II（EU）等主要法規
  • CPP 和 CQA 的理解是製程密閉決策的前提：必須先知道什麼對產品品質最重要，才能科學地決定哪些製程環節需要密閉保護
  • 指南章節遵循邏輯層次：從法規基礎→風險評估工具→製程密閉策略→設施佈局→工程細節，每一層建立在前一層的基礎上
