8.1 Introduction — 導論

原文內容 Original Text

Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing, and Fire Protection (MEP) systems should be designed to meet local codes and regulations and to satisfy both good engineering practice and business needs.

The utilities needed for biopharmaceutical facilities are common to many manufacturing facilities, and include:

  • Lighting
  • Power
  • Cooling water
  • Process water
  • Fire protection
  • Compressed air
  • Other gases

The design of HVAC and other supporting systems specific to biopharmaceutical facilities relies on the evaluation of factors pertaining to the risk of product contamination or adulteration by the system or the ambient environment. In addition, the design should consider the sometimes conflicting risks to worker or public health from the product or process.

The factors to be evaluated may be divided into two groups:

  1. Those risk factors that add to the risk of product contamination
  2. Those protection factors that provide layers of protection against contamination

The combination of risk and protection factors determines the design of the facility. Example risk and protection factors include:

  • Product Risk Factors:
    • Kinetic energy of the process (ability to produce aerosols)
    • Biocontainment (related to pathogenic or hazardous nature of organisms or genetic material)
    • Biosafety (related to biocontainment with regard to operator exposure)
    • Hazardous and flammable material handling
    • Product potency
    • Material sensitivity to molecular contaminants
    • Material ability to support microbial growth
  • Business Risk Factors:
    • Resource conservation and sustainability
    • Product value
    • Product medical criticality
  • Protection Factors:
    • Process closure
    • Process containment (use of micro environments)
    • Downstream processing (virus reduction and removal of other impurities and contaminants)
    • Pre and post-viral separation
    • Gowning
    • Classification
    • General arrangement and physical construction

A risk management approach is usually applied to these factors and may be formal, informal (or both). Risk mitigation approaches should be concomitant with the level of identified risk and the acceptable level of operator exposure. The risk to product quality should be assessed, appropriate mitigations designed, and the risks tracked. These tasks may be performed by the MEP engineering team.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

MEP (Mechanical, Electrical, Plumbing and Fire Protection) 機械電氣管線防火系統：這四個子系統合稱 MEP，是廠房建築工程的骨幹。在生物製藥廠中，MEP 不只是「讓建築能用」的基礎設施，更直接影響產品品質、操作員安全，以及生物圍堵的完整性。

HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning) 暖通空調系統：在所有 MEP 系統中，HVAC 對生物製藥最為關鍵，因為它同時影響：產品微生物污染風險、操作員生物安全暴露、以及對外部環境的生物圍堵。

風險因子 vs 保護因子的雙向框架：ISPE Vol.6 的設計哲學是先識別每個製程步驟的風險來源，再疊加對應的保護層，最終決定空間等級與 HVAC 需求。這與 ICH Q9 品質風險管理的架構完全一致。

比喻說明 Analogy

設計生物製藥廠的 HVAC，就像設計一棟醫院的感染控制系統——既要防止外部病原體進入手術室（保護產品），也要防止醫院裡的病菌擴散到走廊（生物圍堵），還得避免醫護人員被患者的傳染病感染（生物安全）。三個目標有時相互衝突，需要整合式風險評估。

重點提示 Key Notes

生物製藥 vs 傳統製藥的關鍵差異：傳統製藥 HVAC 設計的主要目標是「保護產品不受環境污染」；生物製藥多了兩個獨特挑戰——生物圍堵（biocontainment）防止活生物體外洩危害公眾，以及生物安全（biosafety）防止操作員被製程中的病原體感染。這使得 HVAC 設計必須同時滿足三個有時相互矛盾的目標。

8.2 Mechanical Systems and Environmental Controls — 機械系統與環境控制

原文內容 Original Text

Each step in the manufacture of a biopharmaceutical drug substance should be evaluated for risk and protection factors to determine the type of mechanical systems and environmental controls required to assure both a safe product and a safe work environment.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

本節的關鍵邏輯是：逐步驟評估（step-by-step assessment）。不同製程步驟的風險輪廓差異極大——細胞培養（上游）的主要風險是外部污染進入，而病毒去活化步驟（下游）的主要風險是活病毒外洩。因此，同一棟廠房的不同區域，可能需要截然不同的 HVAC 策略。

8.2.1 Applying Risk Tools to Classification and HVAC Design 8.2.1 將風險工具應用於分級與暖通空調設計

原文內容 Original Text

HVAC design can impact basic parameters of the work environment and emissions to the outside, including:

  • Temperature
  • Pressure
  • Moisture content
  • Particulate content
  • Molecular (contaminant) content

The capabilities of an HVAC system can impact product contamination risk factors. Risk factors also can impact the need for the HVAC system to enhance or provide environmental parameters to support product quality.

An assessment of the impact of the environment on the process is required in order to understand the environmental control requirements of each space within a facility appropriately. During this risk assessment, product risk factors specific to each stage of a process should be considered and the effect on HVAC design should be identified.

8.2.1.1 Kinetic Energy

Process steps which add significant energy to a culture or product present an increased risk of generating aerosols and cross-contaminating other products or increasing operator exposure. Examples of processes and equipment that, if breached, may allow product to become airborne include positive pressure and pumped transfers (for chromatography, filtration, or movement of material), centrifugation, and pressurized reactor vessels. Typical mitigations in areas of high aerosol risk include increasing air exchange rate to dilute aerosols in the space and enhanced filtration to remove aerosols from the airstream.

8.2.1.2 Biocontainment (Related to Hazardous or Pathogenic Nature of Organisms or Genetic Material)

Biocontainment requires the prevention of the escape of airborne organisms from a space to protect public health. When assessing the biocontainment risk, the probability of the material becoming airborne should be considered. Liquids or solids will need added energy (e.g., by pumping) to allow the material to become airborne.

The need for biocontainment can drive risk mitigation approaches such as:

  • Primary process containment or closure
  • Secondary containment isolation or facilities
  • Treatment of the exhaust air from a space (e.g., scrubbing, chemical or thermal deactivation, or filtration)
  • Negative pressurization of spaces to prevent accidental emissions
  • Terminal filtration of supply air to prevent emission through the supply ductwork in the case of fan failure
  • Dedicated air handling equipment

Filtration is a frequently used treatment for exhaust air with single and double HEPA filters used to help provide assurance of containment. A single properly installed HEPA filter can provide a greater than 4 log reduction in airborne pathogens. The need for biocontainment also may drive redundancy in HVAC systems to assure that pressure reversals and flow stoppages do not occur.

Biocontainment should not drive decisions relating to the use of recirculation or once-through ventilation.

8.2.1.3 Biosafety (Similar to Biocontainment — With Regard to Operator Exposure)

Biosafety requires the containment of airborne organisms within a space. The need for biosafety can drive risk mitigation approaches such as:

  • Secondary containment isolation or facilities
  • Treatment of the recirculated air from a space (e.g., filtration)
  • Negative pressurization of spaces to prevent accidental emissions
  • Terminal filtration of supply air to prevent emission through supply ductwork in the case of fan failure
  • Dedicated air handling equipment
  • Once-through ventilation

Filtration is a frequently used treatment for recirculated air with single and double HEPA filters used to help provide assurance of reduction of airborne pathogens. A single properly installed HEPA filter can provide a greater than 4 log reduction in airborne pathogens. Where isolators are used, the need for biocontainment also may drive redundancy in HVAC system, to assure that primary containment is preserved.

When assessing this risk of potential airborne contaminants, the probability of the material becoming airborne should be considered. Liquids or solids will need energy added (e.g., by pumping) to allow the material to become airborne.

The quantitative reduction in potential airborne contaminants provided by filtration should be evaluated as part of the risk assessment. At high levels of biosafety (e.g., BSL-3 and 4) once-through ventilation is frequently used. Although HEPA filters are effective against microorganisms, increasing levels of biosafety discourages the use of recirculation, favoring once-through ventilation.

8.2.1.4 Hazardous and Flammable Material Handling

Hazardous or flammable material handling requires dilution of the hazard within a space and containment of airborne hazards to prevent fugitive emissions from escaping a space. Hazardous or flammable material handling can drive risk mitigation approaches such as:

  • Primary process containment closure
  • Treatment of the exhaust air from a space (e.g., scrubbing, adsorption, thermal oxidation, or filtration)
  • Negative pressurization of spaces to prevent accidental emissions
  • Redundancy to assure pressurization in all conditions
  • Lower Explosive Limit (LEL) detection
  • Deflagration containment and venting

Fume scrubbing or carbon adsorption may be used to treat exhaust air. Hazardous or flammable material handling also may drive redundancy in HVAC systems to assure that dilution of contaminants and pressurization are not lost.

The use of hazardous or flammable materials usually discourages the use of recirculation, favoring once-through ventilation. When assessing this risk, the actual probability of the material becoming airborne should be considered. Flammable liquids and some other hazardous materials may have a high vapor pressure, emitting vapors without added energy; some liquids or solids will need to have energy added (e.g., by pumping) to allow the material to become airborne.

8.2.1.5 Product Potency

The handling of potent products has the same impact as hazardous material handling; however, the actual concentration of the potent product at the particular stage of the process should be considered. The potency of biological products usually increases as they move downstream through purification. The potency of an API is usually reduced during formulation, as it is mixed with excipients.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

動能（Kinetic Energy）與氣溶膠（Aerosol）風險：任何對培養液或產品施加機械能的操作（離心、加壓傳輸、攪拌等），都可能將液滴霧化為氣溶膠。氣溶膠比液滴更危險，因為它能懸浮在空氣中長時間漂移，穿越房間甚至進入其他空間。高動能區域的 HVAC 設計核心是：加大換氣次數（ACH）稀釋濃度 + 強化排氣過濾。

生物圍堵（Biocontainment）vs 生物安全（Biosafety）的細微差異：

  • Biocontainment（生物圍堵）：防止活性生物體/基因物質從設施內逃逸到外部環境，保護公眾安全。重點是「向外圍堵」，通常需要負壓。
  • Biosafety（生物安全）：防止活性生物體在設施內暴露給操作員，保護員工安全。重點是「向內圍堵」，同樣需要負壓。
  • 兩者都指向負壓設計，但控制的邊界和目標不同。

比喻說明 Analogy

雙層 HEPA 排氣過濾 = 核電廠排氣過濾的製藥版本：核電廠為防止輻射微粒外洩，排氣管道裝設多道過濾器；BSL-2+ 以上的生物製藥廠排氣系統，也裝設單層或雙層 HEPA 過濾器，確保含有活性微生物的空氣在排出前已被充分清潔。一道 HEPA 即可提供 >4 log（99.99%）的病原體去除率，是非常強的保護屏障。

一次通過氣流（Single-pass / once-through air）：就像醫院手術室——空氣進來用一次就排掉，不循環回去。這是高等級生物安全區域（BSL-3, 4）的標準做法，代價是能耗極高，因為每一立方米空氣都需要重新加熱或冷卻。

重點提示 Key Notes

LEL（爆炸下限）偵測的重要性：生物製藥下游純化普遍使用大量乙醇（Ethanol）和異丙醇（IPA）作為層析管柱的沖提液（eluent）。這些有機溶劑的蒸氣在特定濃度範圍內即會形成爆炸混合物。LEL 偵測器是強制性的安全設備，一旦偵測到超過 10~20% LEL 就需啟動警報和排氣應急程序。

產品效能（Potency）沿製程遞增：生物製藥的有效成分在下游純化過程中被濃縮，效能遞增。這意味著下游製程空間對空氣的潛在暴露危害更高——即使少量氣溶膠也可能造成操作員嚴重暴露，需要更嚴格的圍堵措施。

原文內容 Original Text

8.2.1.6 Pre- and Post-Viral Separation

Processes normally use several process steps to reduce the concentration of organisms or DNA/RNA and "virus-like particles" from a culture. In the case of vaccines, the pathogens may not be completely removed, but an acceptable level of inactivation may be achieved by a similar series of steps. The continuous nature of these processes may make "pre-viral" and "post-viral" steps difficult to determine precisely; however, a point in the process where virus reduction is adequate to justify separation should be identified.

Air systems that serve upstream open processing areas (e.g., bioreactor and harvest) are usually separated from downstream processes (e.g., purification) at the point at which an acceptable reduction is achieved. Air systems are usually separated with the intent of reducing the chance of contamination of the final product; however, this may not be scientifically supportable.

Using closed processing during pre- and post-viral separation can minimize the need to use HVAC systems for separation of these process stages.

The ability of the product or organisms to become airborne and the ability of treatment steps to remove viruses or virus-like particles should be considered.

8.2.1.7 Product Sensitivity to Molecular Contaminants

Open processes may be susceptible to ambient contamination by particulate or molecular contaminants, such as copper or zinc, which can denature products. This may impact HVAC material and filtration selections.

8.2.1.8 Product Ability to Support Microbial Growth

The greater the ability of the product or culture to sustain microbial growth, the greater the risk and associated drive toward classified space or process closure. A frequently used mitigation, required to protect product, is supply filtration with single HEPA filtration providing a very high level of protection.

8.2.1.9 Process Closure

The more layers of closure (separation from the room environment) a process has, the less impact the room environment normally has on product quality. This can drive a reduction in the:

  • Classification of spaces
  • Required contamination and cross contamination control
  • Need for exhaust of any hazardous gases, vapors, or dusts
  • Once-thru air supply

Process closure also can provide primary containment to address issues of biocontainment and biosafety.

8.2.1.10 Downstream Processing

Bioprocesses typically move from a clean or sterile media to an aseptic culture to a harvested solution that is contaminated with cellular material and other effluvia. These processes isolate desirable proteins and discard undesirable proteins and organisms from a culture. Downstream stages of processing, which are closer to the final API state are typically more sensitive to contamination than are early stages, see Figure 8.1.

[Figure 8.1: Sensitivity of Contamination to Processing Stage — referenced in source, image not available]

8.2.1.11 Gowning

The level of gowning can have a significant impact on room contamination and particulate/microbial performance. Personnel can be significant contributors to space contamination, due to the shedding of both particulate and viable organisms.

The higher the level of gowning, the lower the demand for diluting airflow to offset the particulate and bioburden from operators. This becomes increasingly important as the space classification increases. For further information on gowning and its relationship to space classification, see the ISPE Good Practice Guide: HVAC [58].

8.2.1.12 Classification

Manufacturing environments should be controlled in a manner appropriate to the process and product being handled. Where the risk of product quality being impacted by the ambient environment is unacceptable; the environment is typically identified as "high risk" or "critical" and an enhanced level of environmental control is usually required.

Classification of critical environments in biotech and vaccine facilities according to airborne particulate concentration, as outlined in ISO 14644-1 and ISO 14644-2, is intended to reduce this risk to product quality. While there is no direct correlation between total particulate count and microbiological purity, it is generally accepted that reductions in airborne particulate should result in reductions in airborne colony forming units.

Regulations further enhance these particulate requirements by setting limits for the expected and tested airborne particle count and airborne microbial counts in sterile product facilities. Although there is no specific requirement for formal classification of spaces for non-sterile product according to ISO 14644-1, the latest draft of the EudraLex Annex 2 suggests that the principles of space classification outlined in Annex 1 (for sterile product) should be applied to biological product production as appropriate. This is also consistent with historical and risk-based practices which have typically employed classified spaces for both sterile and non-sterile therapeutic protein and vaccine manufacture.

Process closure (see Chapter 3 of this Guide) also can be a significant driver of environmental classification. Generally speaking, the fewer layers of closure a process has, the greater the need to perform it in a classified space. The relationship between process risk of contamination and layers of protection (e.g., from process closure) requires a flexible, multi-dimensional, approach to classification of spaces. For further information, see Appendix 1.

Historical approaches to area classification were usually more prescriptive.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

病毒分離點（Pre/Post-Viral Separation Point）：生物製藥製程中，有一個關鍵的「病毒分離點」——在這個點之前，製程液含有活性病毒或病毒樣粒子（來自細胞培養）；之後，通過病毒去活化/去除步驟，病毒濃度降至可接受水平。HVAC 系統設計需要識別這個分離點，並在該點前後使用不同的空調系統，防止含病毒的上游空氣污染已「清潔」的下游產品。

製程密閉性（Process Closure）的槓桿效應：這是本章最重要的設計原則之一。製程密閉程度每提升一個層次（例如從開放製程升級到密閉管路），就可以相應降低環境分級要求——這意味著可以降低 HVAC 複雜度、減少潔淨室面積、大幅節省能源和建設成本。生物製藥廠廣泛採用單次使用（SU）生物反應器和密閉傳輸系統，部分原因正是為了利用這個槓桿。

ISO 14644-1 分級系統：以每立方公尺空氣中的微粒濃度定義 ISO Class 1 至 9，數字越小越潔淨。監管層面，EU GMP 使用 Grade A/B/C/D，PIC/S 系統近似，FDA 使用 ISO 5/6/7/8，而 ISPE 提出了 Grade 5-8 + CNC+ / CNC / UC 的統一橋接系統。

比喻說明 Analogy

病毒分離點 = 污水處理廠的特殊版本：城市污水處理廠在排放前必須殺滅病菌；生物製藥廠的病毒去活化步驟就是製程中的「殺菌站」。在「殺菌站」上游的空調系統，必須與下游的完全隔離——這就像污水管道絕對不能與飲用水管道相連。

換衣程度 vs 換氣量的取捨：就像廚師戴帽戴手套能大幅減少食物落菌風險一樣，操作員穿著更嚴密的無塵衣，對環境排放的微粒和微生物會顯著減少，從而讓 HVAC 系統不需要那麼高的換氣次數（ACH）來稀釋污染。高級別分級空間的換衣規程嚴格，正是「用換衣換取低 ACH」的工程取捨。

重點提示 Key Notes

EudraLex Annex 2 對非無菌生物製品的分級要求：傳統上，ISO 14644-1 的分級系統主要針對無菌產品。但 Annex 2 的最新草案指出，即使是非無菌生物製品的生產，也應參照 Annex 1（無菌產品）的分級原則執行。這對疫苗和重組蛋白製造商影響深遠，意味著更高的合規投入。

下游製程對污染更敏感：直覺上可能認為「越到最後越乾淨」，但 Figure 8.1 顯示的邏輯是：越接近最終 API 狀態，產品的純化程度越高，但也越沒有緩衝能力——一旦污染，沒有後續的去除步驟可以補救。因此下游區域（純化、配製）雖然不再含有活性病毒，但對環境污染的敏感度反而更高。

實務應用 Practical Application

一家 CDMO 正在設計一棟新的生物製藥廠（生產治療用 mAb），評估是否採用全密閉單次使用（SU）生物反應器系統（相比傳統開放式不鏽鋼生物反應器）：

  • 製程密閉性提升的好處：SU 系統幾乎消除了人工操作與環境空氣的接觸點，可將培養區（上游）從 ISO 7（Grade B）降至 ISO 8（Grade C），大幅節省 HVAC 設計複雜度與能耗
  • HVAC 成本節省：ISO 7 要求 30-60 ACH，ISO 8 僅需 15-20 ACH——換氣量減半即代表 AHU 機組容量、管道尺寸、能耗均可顯著縮減
  • 建議：採用 SU 系統並結合密閉傳輸，可實現分級降等（classification downgrade），是現代生物製藥廠設計的主流策略

The greater airflow in cleaner spaces may be expressed in terms of the ventilation rate (or Air Change (AC) rate). While this measure has been dropped by IEST and others, the USP cites air change rates from 20 AC/hr for ISO-8 to 100 AC/hr for ISO-5. This approach is also embodied in the FDA 2004 Sterile Guide, which contains a suggestion that 20 AC/hr should be adequate for ISO-8 and suggests that cleaner spaces should have higher ventilation rates.

However, considering only the ventilation rate neglects the correction for the mixing factor (K). The mixing factor is a measure of room design performance. In addition, considering only the air change rate in the design of cleanrooms fails to account for the following fundamental factors:

  • Contaminant Sources:
    • Occupants (people): Population, Gowning, Activity level
    • Process: Equipment, Materials
    • Contamination Ingress: Through facility fabric (cracks in walls, ceilings, etc.), Through doorways
  • Configuration:
    • Architecture
    • HVAC system layout
    • Room distribution
  • Engineering Controls and Mitigations:
    • Local exhaust ventilation at generation points
    • Containment of generated particles
    • Room performance (ventilation effectiveness)
    • Pressurization/direction of airflow
    • Supply filtration
    • Airlocking

When using the ventilation rate principal to set air volumes (flowrates) in classified spaces, airflow should be increased as the classification becomes more stringent. However, the air volume required should be adjusted to accommodate the factors above. Sophisticated cleanroom airflow calculators and Computational Fluid Dynamic (CFD) modeling can help designers to achieve effective and efficient designs that relate directly to the process design and architectural configuration.

8.2.1.13 Resource Conservation and Sustainability Targets

As much as 60% of the energy consumed by an average pharmaceutical facility is dedicated to the HVAC systems. Optimizing the selected critical parameters and acceptance criteria for HVAC can provide a significant reduction in both cost and greenhouse gas emissions.

The design of highly efficient systems, employing a few core principles, can lead to further reductions and achievement of sustainability goals. The core principles for energy reduction include:

  • Classification of a space should be based on real risk. Over-classifying spaces should be avoided.
  • Over-filtering should be avoided. A single HEPA filter can provide a greater than 4 log reduction in airborne contaminants.
  • Where operations are not continuous, an idle mode should be considered to reduce airflow (and possibly relax temperature requirements) whenever an area is idle or when no open operations are present.
  • Consideration of advanced control techniques such as discharge air dewpoint control and adaptive static pressure reset.
  • Consideration of use of recirculated air wherever practical.
  • Consideration of heat recovery from exhaust streams.
  • Consideration of use of once-through air systems for heat rejection during winter months.
  • Consideration of utilizing local recirculation systems separate from primary pressurization air to allow recirculation to be shut down during idle periods.

8.2.1.14 Product Value

As product value increases, business drivers may push design toward a higher level of space classification to minimize the risk of a lost batch due to environmental contamination or due to an inconclusive investigation into potential contamination.

8.2.1.15 Product Medical Criticality

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

能源消耗中 HVAC 佔 60% 的含義：製藥廠是能源密集型設施，而其中 HVAC 是最大的單一能源消耗者。這直接轉化為巨大的運營成本（OpEx）和碳排放。在廠房設計生命週期內，過度分類（over-classification）造成的累積能源浪費可能遠超建設成本節省。

「待機模式（Idle Mode）」的概念：當潔淨室沒有開放操作進行時（如換班、假日），可以臨時降低換氣次數（通常降至全速的 20-50%），待恢復生產前再升回至全速，並以 Recovery 測試確認達到規格。這一技術稱為「setback mode」或「idle mode」，是現代 HVAC 節能設計的核心策略之一。

生物安全等級（BSL）與 HVAC 要求的遞進關係：從 BSL-1 到 BSL-4，壓力、排氣過濾、循環方式、氣鎖要求全面遞增：

  • BSL-1/GLSP：正壓，允許循環，無特殊過濾
  • BSL-2：負壓，允許循環，氣鎖「建議」
  • BSL-2+：負壓，HEPA 過濾（選配），局部循環，氣鎖「建議」
  • BSL-3：強負壓，強制 HEPA，100% 新鮮風（不循環），多道氣鎖
  • BSL-4：全封閉負壓，雙層 HEPA，100% 新鮮風，多道強制氣鎖

比喻說明 Analogy

「不要過度分類」= 開飯館不需要手術室等級的無菌廚房：如果廚師的食材不需要在滅菌條件下處理，強制使用 ISO 5 潔淨廚房只是浪費電費。同理，如果一個生物製藥製程步驟使用完全密閉的設備，將其所在空間分級為 ISO 7 而非 ISO 8，只是白白多花換氣費用——這就是 ISPE 強調「基於真實風險分級」的本意。

HVAC 的 Idle Mode = 辦公室的空調下班自動降溫：現代智慧大樓在下班後自動降低空調設定點，節省夜間能耗；潔淨室的 Idle Mode 邏輯相同——無人作業時降低換氣量，節省大量電費，只需確保「開工前恢復到規格狀態」即可。

大多數生物製藥廠的 BSL 等級是 GLSP 或 BSL-1/2：雖然 BSL-3 和 BSL-4 在學術文獻和監管指南中常被討論，但商業生物製藥（mAb、疫苗）製造廠絕大多數在 GLSP（適宜大規模生產的生物體）或 BSL-1/BSL-2 條件下操作。BSL-2+ 適用於含有條件致病性元素的製程，BSL-3/4 在商業規模製藥中非常罕見。理解 Table 8.4 有助於在設計新廠時與監管機構討論設施適當性。

8.3 Fire Protection — 防火系統

原文內容 Original Text

Attributes of fire protection systems specific to biopharmaceutical and vaccine facilities include:

  • Use of cleanable sprinkler heads that are compatible with cleaning agents. Sealing of concealed heads should be considered for reducing risk of accidental release and increasing the capacity for cleaning. These heads can also minimize air leakage.
  • Use of pre-action systems to minimize the risk of accidental release in cleanrooms and biosafety areas.
  • Collection of sprinkler discharge may be required in manufacturing and biosafety areas. The collected discharge may require abatement prior to discharge.
  • Dry chemical extinguishing systems are typically only needed where volatile organic solvents are used.
  • Clean agent extinguishing systems (gaseous suppression) may also be used in high risk/high value areas, to assure prevention of contamination of product by the fire extinguishing agent:
    • Clean agent extinguishing systems normally require HVAC isolation, room seal testing. Any means of egress and notifications should be considered. Appropriately qualified personnel should be consulted when employing these systems.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Pre-action 消防系統（預作動系統）：傳統濕式灑水系統在灑水頭熔解時立即噴水。Pre-action 系統需要兩個觸發條件才噴水（通常是煙霧偵測 + 高溫熔斷），大幅降低「誤噴」的機率。在潔淨室或生物安全區域，誤噴水會造成：(1) 批次損失（水污染）；(2) 廠房停機清潔（Recovery 驗證需時數天）；(3) 生物安全事件（若含活性生物體的液體被噴灑散布）。因此 pre-action 是生物製藥廠的標準選擇。

Clean agent（潔淨滅火劑）系統：使用氣態化學物質（如 FM-200、Novec 1230）滅火，不留殘留物，不污染產品。適用於高價值設備間（電腦機房、精密分析儀器區、高價值 API 存儲區）。

灑水排放的生物安全隱患：如果消防灑水系統在 BSL-2 以上的區域激活，噴出的水會混入含活性微生物的液體，產生的廢水必須當作生物危害廢液（biological waste）處理，需要滅活後才能排放——這就是「abatement prior to discharge」的含義。

比喻說明 Analogy

Pre-action 消防系統 = 雙重認證的保險箱：普通灑水頭像一把鎖，溫度到了就開；Pre-action 系統像一個需要密碼 + 鑰匙的保險箱——必須兩個條件同時滿足才啟動，大幅降低誤觸發機率。在動輒數百萬美元一批的生物製藥生產中，避免誤噴的代價絕對合理。

乾粉滅火系統的侷限性：乾粉是最便宜的滅火方式，但粉末殘留會污染 ISO 7/8 以上的潔淨室，需要極長時間清潔復原，因此只用於有大量有機溶劑（如乙醇槽）的倉儲或處理區域，而非生產潔淨室內。

重點提示 Key Notes

乙醇（Ethanol）和 IPA 在生物製藥下游的廣泛應用：蛋白質 A 管柱（Protein A chromatography）用水性緩衝液；但反相色譜（RP-HPLC）、病毒滅活（低 pH 加溶劑）、以及設備 70% IPA 消毒等步驟，都大量使用可燃有機溶劑。下游純化車間（DSP area）必須進行防爆設計評估（ATEX / hazardous area classification），包含 LEL 偵測器、防爆電氣設備、以及必要時的乾粉或 clean agent 滅火系統。

原文內容 Original Text

Attributes of electrical systems specific to biopharmaceutical and vaccine facilities include:

  • Cleanroom lighting that is fully sealed from the room side may be required for manufacturing areas. Top access should be considered to minimize the risk of contamination of these rooms.
  • Cleanroom phones and intercoms that are fully sealed from the room side are recommended for manufacturing areas.
  • It may be beneficial to seal all conduits and penetrations from the room side in manufacturing areas.
  • Within biosafety level 2+, 3, and 4 areas conduits and penetrations from the room side in manufacturing areas should be sealed.
  • Biopharmaceutical processes are typically wet and the use of NEMA 3R, 4, and 4X closures may be beneficial within manufacturing areas.
  • Within biosafety areas, NEMA 4X wiring devices and liquid tight conduit are recommended.
  • Backup power should be considered for all biological product facilities. Loss of power to bioreactors and processing equipment for more than a brief period can be catastrophic and result in the loss of batches. This is generally a business issue, rather than a GMP issue, but in cases of medical necessity the loss can be more than financial.
  • When evaluating the need for backup power, the need for HVAC during periods of power loss to assure environmental control should be considered. Qualification of the period that the facility is capable of maintaining conditions without active HVAC systems and a study of the sequencing of the restart of all systems upon the application of emergency power are recommended.
  • The transition back from emergency power to normal power also should be evaluated. Standard transfer switches make an "open" transition from normal power to emergency power and back, requiring equipment to restart in both cases. It may be advisable to make the transition back to normal power a manual transition (rather than automatic) in order to minimize impact. Similarly, "closed" transition equipment, which is capable of transferring back to normal power without a loss of current for more than a few milliseconds, can be considered to minimize this impact. Paralleling equipment can be considered, which would allow a facility to transition to backup power and back to normal power without loss of current, in a planned outage, offers the greatest flexibility, but at the greatest cost.
  • Uninterruptable power supplies (UPS) should be provided for critical control systems and equipment. The loss of data from process equipment can result in lost batches. While it is advisable to put environmental monitoring systems on uninterruptable power; it is not usual to put environmental equipment on these systems as the inrush current needed to start motors presents a challenge to these systems. In addition, environmental control (HVAC) systems can usually withstand a short shut-down period.
  • Wiring means and methods within biopharmaceutical facilities vary, but the electrical services within production areas should be liquid tight and capable of withstanding the cleaning products normally used in these environments.
  • Coated rigid conduit, stainless steel conduit and PVC conduit can be used, along with "liquid tight" flexible conduits. NEMA 4 and 4X enclosures may be used in wet areas. NEMA 12 may be used for powder handling or other process areas. NEMA 3R is not generally recommended because of its incapability of withstanding water from all directions.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

備用電力系統的三個層次：

  • UPS（不斷電系統，Uninterruptible Power Supply）：以電池維持數分鐘至 30 分鐘的供電，專為防止短暫停電造成控制系統數據丟失和設備誤動作。UPS 無法驅動大型馬達（如 HVAC 風機），只用於控制器、儀表、數據記錄系統。
  • 緊急發電機（Emergency Generator）：燃油發電機在停電後約 10-30 秒內啟動，可驅動大型設備（HVAC、冷凍機、攪拌器）。從停電到發電機接管的空窗期，需要 UPS 橋接。
  • Paralleling Equipment（並聯切換設備）：最高等級解決方案，允許在有計劃的電力切換（如年度電力維修）時，不中斷供電完成從正常電源到備用電源的切換。成本最高，但對 24/7 不間斷生產的 CDMO 至關重要。

NEMA 防護等級（電氣設備防護標準）：

  • NEMA 3R：防雨、有限防水，不能承受從所有方向噴水，不推薦用於生物製藥濕製程區域
  • NEMA 4：防噴水（任何方向），適用於一般製造區
  • NEMA 4X：防噴水 + 防腐蝕（不鏽鋼），BSL-2+ 以上必用，能承受強力清洗劑
  • NEMA 12：防粉塵，適用於粉末處理區域（如原料藥稱量間）

比喻說明 Analogy

細胞培養停電 = 細胞的「窒息」事件：動物細胞培養（如 CHO 細胞培養 mAb）在 37°C、5% CO₂ 的精密控制環境中生長，代謝速率極高。一旦停電造成攪拌停止、pH 控制失效、氣體供應中斷，細胞在數十分鐘內就開始死亡，製程批次無法挽救。一個 2000L 生物反應器的 mAb 批次，市值可能超過 500 萬美元——UPS 和緊急發電機的投資相比之下微不足道。

「Open」vs「Closed」轉換 = 騎腳踏車換速的差異：「Open transition」（開路轉換）就像腳踏車換速時必須短暫停止踩踏，設備需要重新啟動；「Closed transition」（閉路轉換）就像無縫換速系統，動力不中斷。在細胞培養期間，即使幾秒鐘的電力中斷也可能造成控制系統重啟，導致批次數據記錄空缺，觸發偏差調查。

重點提示 Key Notes

停電後的重啟順序（Restart Sequencing）至關重要：緊急電力恢復後，系統必須按正確順序重啟——通常是：UPS 系統優先（已持續供電）→ HVAC 啟動（建立壓差和環境控制）→ 製程設備（確認環境達標後）→ 控制系統確認數據完整性。若順序錯誤（例如在 HVAC 恢復前啟動生物反應器），可能違反無菌生產的 GMP 要求，導致批次被拒絕放行。

環境監控系統（EMS）需要 UPS：EMS（含在線粒子計數器、溫濕度傳感器、壓差計）應接 UPS，以確保停電期間的環境數據記錄不中斷——這是 GMP 批次記錄完整性的要求。但環境「控制」設備（HVAC 風機等）因啟動電流太大，不適合接 UPS，由緊急發電機供電。

公式與計算 Formula

備用電力規劃評估框架：

關鍵負載分類：
Level 1（UPS 必需）：
  - 製程控制系統（DCS/PLC）
  - 環境監控系統（EMS）
  - 生命安全系統（緊急照明、疏散系統）
  - 關鍵數據記錄設備

Level 2（發電機 10-30 秒內接管）：
  - HVAC 系統（環境控制）
  - 生物反應器攪拌/控制
  - 冷凍/冷藏系統（-80°C 超低溫、2-8°C）
  - 廢水處理/生物滅活系統

Level 3（可接受短暫中斷）：
  - 一般照明
  - 辦公設備
  - 非關鍵製造設備

UPS 容量估算（簡化）：
UPS 容量（kVA）= 總關鍵負載（kW）/ 功率因數（≈0.9） × 安全係數（1.25）
持續時間 = 電池容量（kWh）/ 實際負載（kW）

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 停電風險評估情境：一家 CDMO 的 mAb 生產廠（3 台 2000L 生物反應器，每批市值 $3M USD）計畫評估備用電力投資：

  • UPS 系統（$50K 投資）：保護控制系統，防止停電造成批次數據記錄空缺和偏差報告；保護 EMS 數據連續性，維護 GMP 合規
  • 緊急發電機（$300K 投資）：保護所有 Level 2 設備，防止停電超過 30 秒造成的批次損失；每次停電事件的潛在損失（3 批 × $3M = $9M）遠高於設備投資
  • Closed Transition 並聯切換（$800K 追加投資）：消除計劃性電力維護期間的生產中斷，對年產 50+ 批次的大型 CDMO 可在 2 年內回收投資
  • 結論：對於高價值生物製藥 CDMO，UPS + 發電機是最低限度投資，Closed transition 是長期效益最佳的選項

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary

  • MEP 設計哲學：生物製藥廠的機械系統設計需同時滿足三個目標——保護產品品質（產品污染控制）、保護員工安全（生物安全/Biosafety）、保護公眾健康（生物圍堵/Biocontainment），三者有時相互衝突，必須通過系統性風險評估（結合風險因子與保護因子）進行多維度整合設計
  • HVAC 核心設計原則：空間分級應基於真實風險（非過度分類）；製程密閉性（Process Closure）是降低分級要求最有效的設計槓桿；換氣次數（ACH）是設計起點而非規範，需結合 CFD 模擬和混合因子（K）校正；BSL 等級決定排氣過濾（HEPA 要求）和氣流策略（循環 vs 一次通過）
  • 分級系統對照：ISPE Grade 5/6/7/8 + CNC+/CNC/UC 系統橋接了 ISO 14644-1、EU GMP（A/B/C/D）和 FDA（ISO 5-8）的不同監管框架；Grade 7 對應 ISO 7 / EU Grade B，30-60 ACH，HEPA 過濾；Grade 8 對應 ISO 8 / EU Grade C，15-60 ACH
  • 防火特殊考量：Pre-action 消防系統是潔淨室和生物安全區域的標準；灑水排放在含活性微生物的區域必須進行生物去活化處理（abatement）；下游純化區的乙醇/IPA 使用需要 LEL 偵測和防爆設計；高價值區域可選用 Clean agent 氣體滅火系統
  • 電氣與備用電力：細胞培養批次無法承受停電（停電 = 批次損失）——UPS 保護控制系統和 EMS 數據記錄，緊急發電機保護 HVAC 和製程設備，Closed Transition 切換設備消除計劃性停電的影響；所有 BSL-2+ 區域的電氣設備必須使用 NEMA 4X 防腐蝕防水規格，配管必須液密封堵
