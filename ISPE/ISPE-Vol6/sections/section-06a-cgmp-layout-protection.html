6.1 Introduction — 導論

原文內容 Original Text

This chapter focuses on the facility aspects of product protection and specific requirements of CPPs and product CQAs. Facility layouts can be diverse while still protecting the product; this chapter discusses reasons behind layout options to help to facilitate the preferred project solution.

The result of the design phase should be a layout that focuses on protecting the product while also addressing key project drivers, such as:

  • CPPs and product CQAs
  • Production processes, scale, and technologies
  • Product protection and segregation approaches
  • Facility and process flows
  • Equipment and facility cleaning and finishes
  • cGMP requirements for Clinical Phase 2 and Phase 3 through commercial production
  • Vaccine facilities and biosafety requirements
  • Product and process changes over time, including:
    • Flexibility
    • Adaptability
    • Expandability
  • Regulatory requirements
  • Operational requirements
  • Code and life safety aspects
  • Business and economic factors

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 廠房設計的出發點

CPPs（關鍵製程參數）與CQAs（關鍵品質屬性）是廠房設計的最高指導原則。每一個廠房佈局決策——房間數量、氣流方向、走廊配置——都應追溯到「這樣設計如何保護產品的關鍵品質屬性？」這個問題。

設計階段的成果不是一套圖紙，而是一個保護策略的實體化。廠房的牆壁、門、壓力梯度，都是保護 CQAs 的工具。

比喻說明 — 廚房設計決定料理品質

想像一家高級餐廳的廚房設計。主廚（CPP/CQA）決定了廚房需要：隔開生熟食區（隔離）、專用刀具架（防交叉汙染）、冷藏溫度監控（環境管控）。廚房設計必須服務於料理品質，而非反過來讓廚師遷就廚房格局。

生物製藥廠房設計亦同：產品特性決定廠房設計，而非廠房決定產品製程。

重點提示 — 彈性、適應性、擴充性

ISPE 明確將 Flexibility（彈性）、Adaptability（適應性）、Expandability（擴充性） 列為關鍵設計驅動因素。這對 CDMO 尤為重要：

  • 彈性：同一廠房可生產不同客戶的產品
  • 適應性：製程改變時廠房能以低成本調整
  • 擴充性：從臨床批次擴大到商業化生產無需另建廠房

  2. 疫苗設施為何需要特別考慮「生物安全要求」？這與傳統小分子藥物有何不同？
6.2 Production Capacity, Process, Scale and Technologies — 產能、製程、規模與技術

原文內容 Original Text

Business and marketing factors can establish desired production capacities for each product produced in a facility. Capacities are converted into a production definition, beginning with:

  • Block Flow Diagrams (BFDs)
  • Process Flow Diagrams (PFDs)
  • A process description
  • An equipment list
  • A preliminary material balance

Capacities also define the process scale, the process technology, and the degree of process exposure or closure. This production definition forms the basis for a facility design. Process adjacency diagrams are created at this stage to begin to graphically understand the interrelation of the process steps.

Production capacities can be produced at different scales with scale defined as a bioreactor or fermenter size. An example of the same production capacity at different scales is a single 12,000 liter bioreactor or six 2,000 liter bioreactors. Each approach has pros and cons from a scale up, regulatory and a business perspective and each production scale has a very different impact on a facility layout. Recently, the industry has been trending toward increasingly larger production scales for Monoclonal Antibody (MAb) production. Processing improvements have increased the production yield from bioreactors, allowing for the same capacity to be produced from smaller bioreactors.

Scales for Monoclonal Antibody (MAb) production may be described as:

  • Large scale production typically ranges from 10,000 liters to 30,000 liters. Large scale processes can be stacked in a vertical configuration to assist with gravity fed processing, drains, CIP return lines, and utility distribution.
  • Medium scale production typically ranges from 4,000 liters to 8,000 liters. Medium scale processes are typically either stacked in a vertical configuration or configured in a horizontal arrangement.
  • Small scale production is typically up to 2,000 liters. Small scale processes are typically configured in a horizontal arrangement.

核心概念 — 從產能需求到廠房設計的推導鏈

ISPE 提出了一個完整的設計推導邏輯：

商業目標（年產量）→ BFD/PFD → 設備清單 → 物料衡算 → 廠房佈局

關鍵洞見：製程密閉程度（degree of closure）在此階段就必須決定，因為它直接決定了後續所需的潔淨室等級和房間數量。密閉程度越高，所需的受管控空間越少，廠房可以設計得更簡單、更便宜。

規模比較 Scale Comparison

相同總產能 = 12,000 L 的兩種方案：

方案 A：1 × 12,000 L 生物反應器
  → 高樓層廠房（垂直堆疊）
  → 需要強結構支撐與重型起重設備
  → 適合商業化大規模生產

方案 B：6 × 2,000 L 生物反應器
  → 水平排列廠房（單層或低樓層）
  → 可搭配一次性耗材（SU）
  → 更靈活，適合多產品 CDMO

規模分類：
  大規模：10,000 - 30,000 L（垂直堆疊）
  中規模：4,000 - 8,000 L（垂直或水平）
  小規模：≤2,000 L（水平排列）

比喻說明 — 一台大型釀酒槽 vs. 多台小型釀酒槽

想像一個啤酒廠：一台 12,000 公升的大釀酒槽需要高天花板廠房、特殊起重設備和強化地板；六台 2,000 公升的小槽則可在普通廠房水平排列，更靈活但管理成本較高。

生物製藥廠房面臨相同的取捨：大規模單一設備 vs. 小規模多線並行，兩者的廠房設計方式截然不同。

重點提示 — MAb 產業趨勢

ISPE 指出業界趨勢是 MAb 生產規模持續增大，但同時細胞培養效價（titer）的提升讓同等產能可用更小的生物反應器達成。這代表：

  • 新廠設計需考慮未來工藝改進後的彈性
  • 預留 CNC（Controlled Not Classified）空間比固定潔淨室更具未來彈性
  • 一次性耗材（Single-Use）技術搭配小規模生物反應器是 CDMO 首選策略

原文內容 — 垂直堆疊與小規模水平設計

Production processes that are vertically stacked can result in a facility with many processing levels. Three main processing levels:

  • The highest floor (L3) can contain weigh/dispense and media and buffer preparation.
  • The floor below (L2) can house the seed reactors and buffer hold.
  • The lowest production level (L1) can house the production bioreactors, harvest, purification, and bulk fill.

These three levels will generally sit on a utility level below L1 and have a HVAC penthouse above level L3, resulting in a five level building. Large scale processing typically consists of fixed stainless steel vessels with stainless steel distribution piping that is cleaned with CIP/SIP systems.

Figure 6.2: Large Scale Vertical Stack-up Adjacency Diagram
[圖示：大規模垂直堆疊廠房各層功能分佈示意圖]

Figure 6.2: Large Scale Vertical Stack-up Adjacency Diagram

Other large scale stack-up diagrams are shown in Chapter 4, Figures 4.2 and 4.3.

Small scale bioreactors have smaller volume demands for liquids. Fixed stainless steel piping may be replaced by flexible hoses and tubing. The length of hoses and tubing between process equipment should be minimized. This can significantly change a layout, e.g., for buffer hold. A tube can be dropped from buffer hold to purification on a level below. However, where the hold volume is small, it may be preferable to place the hold vessel next to its use point either in the purification room or just outside the purification room. The tubing can be passed through the wall, allowing a considerably shorter tube to be used.

At smaller scale, stainless steel bioreactors, mix, and hold vessels can be replaced with disposable technology, typically referred to as biocontainers or "bags."

核心概念 — 垂直堆疊廠房的五層結構

大規模生物製藥廠房的垂直堆疊邏輯遵循重力輔助製程流動原則：

  • 最高層（L3）：秤重/分裝、培養基與緩衝液配製 → 液體往下流
  • 中層（L2）：種菌培養鏈（seed train）、緩衝液暫存
  • 生產層（L1）：生物反應器、收穫、純化、原料藥充填
  • 公用設施層（地下）：CIP/SIP、純水、壓縮空氣
  • 頂層（HVAC penthouse）：空調機組

這種五層設計雖然充分利用重力，但建設成本高且未來改造困難。

比喻說明 — 種菌培養鏈如同工廠育苗室

種菌培養鏈（Seed Train）就像農場的育苗溫室：從少量種子（工作細胞庫 Working Cell Bank）開始，在溫控環境中逐步擴大培養量，最終移入生產溫室（生產生物反應器）。

在廠房設計上，種菌培養鏈通常位於生產反應器的上一樓層或相鄰房間，確保接種操作路徑最短、汙染風險最低。

重點提示 — 一次性耗材（SU）的空間優勢

小規模廠房使用一次性耗材（Single-Use / SU 技術）的核心優勢在於大幅減少廠房佔地面積：

  • 無需固定不鏽鋼管路 → 減少管路間距要求
  • 緩衝液儲袋（Biocontainer bag）可直接掛在儲存架上 → 大幅縮減緩衝液暫存室面積
  • 整個製程可壓縮至單一生產樓層

核心概念 — In-Line Dilution（在線稀釋）

在線稀釋（In-line dilution）是一種革命性的緩衝液管理策略。傳統上，層析純化需要大量不同濃度的緩衝液，每種都需要一個大型儲槽和獨立房間。在線稀釋改為：

  • 僅儲存高濃度緩衝液原液（concentrate）
  • 在管路中即時按比例混合純水稀釋
  • 直接輸送到層析柱使用點

結果：緩衝液儲存空間可縮減 60–80%，廠房面積大幅縮小。

面積節省估算 Footprint Reduction Estimate

緩衝液儲存方案比較（以下游純化為例）：

傳統不鏽鋼儲槽方案：
  典型緩衝液種類：8-12種
  每種需獨立儲槽 + 操作平台
  → 緩衝液儲存區面積：~500-800 m²

一次性耗材（SU bags）方案：
  12-pack bin holder, 12 × 2,500 L = 30,000 L
  → 面積縮減：約 40-60%

  僅需高濃度原液儲存
  → 面積縮減：可達 70-80%

結論：技術選擇直接決定廠房投資金額

比喻說明 — 飲料濃縮液 vs. 整箱飲料

傳統緩衝液儲存如同在倉庫放滿一箱箱已調好的飲料；在線稀釋如同只存放濃縮果汁，臨用前加水稀釋。前者需要巨大倉庫，後者只需一個小架子。

對 CDMO 來說，在線稀釋技術不只節省空間，還降低了緩衝液批次間的差異性，有助於減少純化步驟的變異。

實務應用 — CDMO 技術選擇建議

對於臨床階段（Phase 1–2）的 CDMO 設施：

  • 推薦選擇 SU bags + in-line dilution：初期投資低、廠房面積小、換產快速
  • 避免大規模固定不鏽鋼系統：初期客戶需求不確定，大型固定設備靈活性差
  • 廠房設計應預留未來轉換為不鏽鋼的可能性（管路連接點、地板荷重）

6.3 Product Protection and Segregation Applications — 產品保護與隔離應用

原文內容 Original Text

Sources of product contamination include:

  • Environmental contamination
  • Crossover contamination
  • Carryover contamination
  • Raw material contamination

For further information, see Chapter 4 of this Guide. Preventing product contamination may be achieved by segregating sources of product contamination from the product. An exposed product should have environmental protection, such as a classified cleanroom environment. Preventing crossover contamination between two exposed products is typically achieved by physical barriers. Cleaning methods should be sufficient to prevent carryover contamination. Preventing contaminated raw materials from entering the process can be assisted by proper material testing.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 — 生物製藥中的雙重壓力

生物製藥廠房設計面臨兩個相互競爭的保護需求：

  • 保護產品（Product Protection）：防止環境微生物汙染產品 → 需要潔淨室（正壓、過濾空氣）
  • 保護人員/環境（Containment）：防止活性生物製劑外洩 → 需要圍堵設計（負壓、廢氣處理）

決定哪個方向優先的關鍵槓桿：製程密閉程度（Process Closure Level）。密閉製程可以同時解決兩個問題。

  2. 若一個生物製藥製程是完全密閉的，是否仍需要潔淨室？請說明理由。

核心概念 — 四種污染來源的對策矩陣

污染來源中文主要對策
Environmental環境污染潔淨室分級、空氣過濾
Crossover交叉污染物理隔離（獨立房間）
Carryover殘留污染清潔驗證（Cleaning Validation）

理解這四種污染來源，才能為每種設計出正確的防護策略，而非對所有情境都採用最嚴格的潔淨室等級。

6.3.1 Segregation Concepts — 隔離概念

原文內容 Original Text

Preventing product contamination by environmental and crossover sources of product contamination can have a significant impact on facility design. These sources of product contamination can be addressed using primary segregation and secondary segregation.

  • Primary segregation focuses on the facility design aspects required to protect an exposed product.
  • Secondary segregation focuses on non-critical facility aspects which result from the use of closed processing.

Both primary and secondary segregation consist of four basic segregation concepts:

  1. Physical (or spatial) segregation
  2. Environmental (including local environmental protection) segregation
  3. Chronological (temporal or time based) segregation
  4. Procedural (or SOP based) segregation

These segregation concepts apply to cGMP facility design, utility design, and process design.

6.3.1.1 Physical Segregation

Physical segregation involves a physical barrier which can prevent both environmental and crossover contamination. The space inside a closed vessel is physically segregated from the space outside the vessel.

In a closed system, non-exposed product is physically segregated from the room environment. Process closure is an application of physical segregation.

In open systems, exposed products require both a physical segregation and environmental protection from other open processes to prevent crossover contamination. Physical segregation for open processing is typically achieved by using separate rooms. This can result in dividing open process steps into a series of separate processing cleanrooms. These cleanrooms are surrounded by airlocks, and use the concept of nesting and facility flows that are designed and arranged to reduce the environmental challenge to the cleanroom.

Example facility applications of physical segregation include:

  • Open Processing: An open processing pre-viral reduction step and an open processing post viral reduction step requiring two separate rooms.
  • Closed Processing:
    • Closed processing pre-viral reduction and closed processing post-viral reduction steps located in the same room
    • Upstream multi-product and multi-host concurrent production in separate closed bioreactors located in the same room
    • Downstream multi-product concurrent production in separate closed equipment located in the same room
    • Closed upstream processing and closed downstream processing located in the same room
    • Closed processing in vessels, piping, bags, and tubes is an application of spatial segregation
    • A product transfer pipe and a separate waste pipe both located in the same pipe rack
    • A closed product vessel and a closed waste container following the same facility flow path and located in the same elevator

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — 主要隔離 vs. 次要隔離

Primary Segregation（主要隔離）：針對暴露產品的保護。當產品與房間空氣直接接觸時，必須透過物理隔離（獨立房間）和環境隔離（潔淨室分級）來保護。

Secondary Segregation（次要隔離）：針對密閉製程的管理。產品已在密閉設備中，隔離的重點轉為防混淆（mix-up）和廢棄物管理。

關鍵洞見：引入密閉技術後，主要隔離的需求大幅降低，次要隔離成為主角。

比喻說明 — 隔離如同醫院隔離病房分級

醫院對不同傳染性疾病採用不同隔離等級：

  • 一般病房（CNC）：普通病人，無特殊隔離需求（如密閉製程）
  • 半隔離病房（Grade 8）：需防護但傳染性較低（如部分暴露製程）
  • 負壓隔離病房（Grade 7/5）：高度傳染性病人（如開放暴露的關鍵製程步驟）

關鍵在於：不是每個病人都需要負壓隔離。同樣地，不是每個製程步驟都需要 Grade 5 潔淨室。

重點提示 — 物理隔離的關鍵應用

物理隔離最重要的應用是病毒削減步驟（viral reduction）的前後隔離：

  • 開放製程：病毒削減前後必須在獨立房間進行，防止已清除病毒的產品被再次污染
  • 密閉製程：病毒削減前後可在同一房間進行，因為物理隔離在設備層面已完成

這個區別在病毒性生物安全評估（Viral Safety Assessment）中極為重要，直接影響法規審查結論。

原文內容 — 環境、時間與程序隔離

6.3.1.2 Environmental Segregation

Environmental segregation is used for open systems. It uses clean air to protect exposed product and product contact surfaces that cannot be functionally closed. A typical application is a cleanroom. The level of the room environment relates to the degree and the criticality of the product or surface exposure. An example cleanroom level is Grade 8, where the entire room is designed to meet the Grade 8 requirements. This is also referred to as the room background classification.

A local protected process (also referred to as local environmental protection) is a sub-set of environmental segregation. It is designed to provide a higher level of clean air protection for a specific purpose within the cleanroom background.

Example facility applications of environmental segregation include:

  • A cleanroom
  • A biosafety cabinet used as local protection in inoculum preparation
  • A local protection enclosure constructed around a fixed process step
  • A portable local protection enclosure moved as needed to a POU

6.3.1.3 Temporal Segregation

Temporal or time based segregation can be applied to both open and closed processing, and also can be a factor in maintaining control of materials. Time based segregation involves using the same physical area for more than one function, but where activities are separated by time, e.g., campaigned manufacturing. Two open processing production lines occupying the same room and operating concurrently will be at risk from crossover contamination. Separating the processing activities by time (i.e., campaigning) can mitigate the risk.

Closed processing can involve concurrent processing or campaigned processing of several products.

Example facility applications of temporal segregation include:

  • Campaigned production for either open or closed processing
  • An enclosed, but not closed waste container (e.g., trash in a tied garbage bag) following the same "facility flow" path as personnel and product and using the same elevator, but the waste travel is non-concurrent with the other flows. This could be scheduled for the third shift or another convenient time.
  • Bi-directional gowning scheduled for non-concurrent gowning and de-gowning. The two functions occupy the same space, but are separated by time.

6.3.1.4 Procedural Segregation

Procedural or SOP based segregation is typically applied to preventing mix-ups, but it also has open processing or exposed surface aspects. An example is manual equipment cleaning in a washroom with many components from different products being handled in the same area concurrently, but segregated by procedures. This would involve using the same area for different open functions (possibly handled concurrently), but using procedures to prevent equipment contamination.

Example facility applications of procedural segregation include:

  • Preventing mix-ups
  • Washing components and equipment, while exposed to the room environment
  • A closed process that briefly exposes product to the room environment that needs special SOPs to protect the product. Operator action may be needed when the process closure technology does not address all aspects of a functionally closed system.

比喻說明 — 壓力梯度如同氣壓迷宮

環境隔離中的壓力梯度（Pressure Cascade）設計就像一個氣壓迷宮：空氣永遠從高壓流向低壓。將潔淨區設計為高壓，走廊次之，外部最低，汙染物就無法逆流進入潔淨區。

但要注意：生物製藥廠房有時需要負壓圍堵（containment）以防止活性生物製劑外洩，此時壓力方向與普通潔淨室相反。這是設計時需特別注意的雙重需求。

重點提示 — Campaign 製造的 GMP 含義

Campaign 製造（Campaigned Manufacturing）是時間隔離最重要的應用。多產品共用廠房時，同一房間在不同時間段分別生產不同產品，中間需要：

  • 徹底清潔與清潔驗證（Cleaning Validation）
  • 環境監控（EM）確認交叉污染水平
  • 文件確認前後批次的隔離

密閉製程可以實現同一房間多產品同時並行生產，不需要 Campaign，這是密閉系統最大的商業優勢之一。

實務應用 — CDMO 多產品廠房的隔離策略選擇

CDMO 通常同時承接多個客戶的訂單。選擇合適的隔離策略至關重要：

  • 開放製程：必須採用時間隔離（Campaign）+ 清潔驗證，成本高、彈性低
  • 密閉製程：可採用物理隔離（設備層面）+ 同時並行生產，大幅提升廠房產能利用率
  • 程序隔離：在洗滌間、廢棄物管理等支援區域廣泛應用，成本最低但風險管理責任最重

6.3.3 Application of Physical Segregation Concepts — 物理隔離概念的應用

原文內容 Original Text

Following technology improvements, most biopharmaceutical process equipment is able to provide a functionally closed system. These closed systems allow for CNC room environments. Closed equipment next to other closed equipment are physically segregated from each other. Closed equipment can be grouped into a single large processing room, which may be called a "ballroom" or a "dance floor." Where process steps are open, they will require a classified environment. This transformation can provide a significant benefit for retrofitting an existing facility, as well as for planning a new facility.

核心概念 — Ballroom 概念（舞廳概念）

Ballroom（或 Dance Floor）是生物製藥廠房設計中的革命性概念：

  • 將所有密閉製程設備集中在一個大型開放空間中
  • 整個空間降為 CNC 等級（無需潔淨室認證）
  • 不同設備之間的物理隔離在設備層面完成，而非在房間層面
  • 少數開放操作步驟（如柱填充）則在 Ballroom 旁邊的小型潔淨室中進行，並設置氣閘（Airlock）連接

Ballroom 最大優點：廠房靈活性極高，可根據訂單需求重新配置設備。

比喻說明 — 從格子間辦公室到開放式辦公空間

傳統的多房間廠房（圖 6.7）就像 1990 年代的格子間辦公室：每個人被隔牆圍住，溝通效率低，空間利用率差。

Ballroom 概念（圖 6.8）就像現代的開放式辦公空間：移除隔牆後，同樣的面積容納更多工作站，協作更靈活。密閉設備的外殼就是每個「工作站」的隱形隔牆。

重點提示 — 既有廠房改造的策略路徑

ISPE 明確指出密閉化改造可以釋放既有廠房的產能，避免新建廠房：

  1. 評估現有設備密閉性：無法密閉的設備阻礙 CNC 降級
  2. 逐步引入密閉技術：優先從高頻率操作的設備開始（如收穫離心機引入 CIP/SIP）
  3. 分階段拆除隔牆：從相鄰兩間開始合并，逐步擴大
  4. 驗證新廠房配置：重新做 URS/DQ/OQ/PQ 認證

The options presented are considered viable and each has benefits and disadvantages. The intent is to align product exposure with the appropriate facility design response.

注：圖中僅顯示製程房間，未顯示 PALs 和 MALs

Figure 6.9: Room Division Case Studies — from fully open (Case 1) to nearly fully closed (Case 6)

Figure 6.9 lists typical biopharmaceutical process steps in the left column. The remaining six columns are the six case studies. The black boxed areas around functions are proposed rooms for physical segregations. The cases are arranged from open processing on the left to almost all functionally closed processing on the right.

Case Descriptions:

Case 1: This is a facility concept with all process steps significantly exposed to the room environment. Biotechnology facility designed as a series of Grade 7 rooms with Grade 5 local protection zones above the critical areas of exposed product, e.g., inoculum or cell culture, seed, and production using open cell stacks in lieu of closed bioreactors.

Case 2: Application of closure technology on vessels — technology applied to fermenters, bioreactors, and media and buffer hold tanks. These closures add a level of physical segregation — may not be fully closed systems. The room environments reduce one level to Grade 8, but not to CNC. Seed and production reactors, and media and buffer hold tanks combined into a single room. Remaining areas are Grade 7.

Case 3: Closure methods added to most process steps. This closure provides an increased level of product protection from the room environment, resulting in a shift to Grade 8 environments. Closure confidence may not be high, so individual rooms retained for physical segregation to prevent cross-over contamination. The facility benefits from reduced room environments — essentially a Grade 8 production facility. Inoculum preparation remains at Grade 7. Cell manipulations follow aseptic processing guidelines in local protection Grade 5 Biosafety Cabinets (BSCs) with Grade 5 requiring a Grade 7 room environment background. Bulk fill is similar, with pooling under a Grade 5 BSC requiring a Grade 7 room environment.

Case 4: Inoculum preparation redefined from aseptic "processing" to aseptic "techniques." Manipulations are under a Grade 5 "quality" BSC which does not require a Grade 7 room background environment. Inoculum preparation and bulk fill reduced to Grade 8. Seed and production bioreactors functionally closed and room is CNC. Harvest (centrifuges) becomes functionally closed and is CNC, but is kept in a separate room because it is manually cleaned in an exposed manner and because of acoustic concerns. Pre and post viral processing equipment are moved into the same room. Pre and post physical room segregation is mitigated by redefining the viral free step as the final UFDF step located in bulk fill. Purification becomes a single room and reduces to Grade 8. Media and buffer hold are closed and room class drops to CNC. Media and buffer preparations are open and Grade 8, but are combined into the same room with dedicated equipment and possible separate local protection zones.

Case 5: New harvest (centrifuge) equipment incorporates CIP/SIP technology and is now within acceptable acoustic (decibel) limits. Harvest joins the bioreactors in a production ballroom. Media and buffer preparation incorporate 0.2 µm filtration en route to hold vessels. This filtration step functionally closes the open preparation process to a closed state at hold. The preparation room environment drops to CNC. Following a risk assessment the downstream processes can follow the upstream example and combine into a single processing room at CNC. Column packing is not closed in this diagram. In this case example, column packing is a separate room with a classified environment accessed from the CNC ballroom.

Case 6: The upstream ballroom and the downstream ballroom are combined into a single process room. Media and buffer prep are combined with their hold vessels. Inoculum preparation is de-classed to CNC with monitoring, which can be designed to the European Grade D. Inoculum cell manipulations are not a closed process. They are in a local protection device (BSC) and the room environment needs to be assessed for the proper background level. A closed processing option for inoculum is a glove box which could be placed in the CNC ballroom adjacent to the seed reactors, but a glove box may be considered not user friendly. An option for column packing (or any other non-fully closed process) is in lieu of locating it in a separate cleanroom is to keep it on the CNC production floor with local protection devices protecting the area of concern.

"Through an independent cost analysis, the case 1 facility was approximately 40% more expensive to construct and operate than the case 6 facility."

Segregating the product from the room environment increases control over the product while reducing the facility size, complexity, and cost. The facility can be a non-critical aspect system of the product (for CNC areas) and the facility is removed from the product protection equation.

重點提示 — Case 4 的兩個關鍵突破

突破一：接種準備的重新定義

將接種準備（inoculum preparation）從「無菌製程（aseptic processing）」重新定義為「無菌技術（aseptic technique）」，這是概念上的重大轉變：

  • 「無菌製程」需要 Grade 7 背景環境的 BSC
  • 「無菌技術」的「Quality BSC」不需要 Grade 7 背景，可在 Grade 8 房間操作
  • 此重新定義需要充分的科學論證和法規溝通

突破二：病毒前後步驟合室

將「病毒去除完成」的定義點移到最終 UF/DF 步驟，讓純化的所有步驟可以在同一房間進行。

重點提示 — Case 5 的離心機問題

收穫步驟的離心機在之前案例中一直獨立成房，原因有二：

  1. 清潔方式：離心機需要手動暴露清洗（無 CIP），無法在 CNC 房間進行
  2. 噪音（Acoustic）問題：高速離心機噪音超過可接受水平（dB 限制）

Case 5 中，新型離心機配備了 CIP/SIP 技術，同時改善了噪音問題，才得以加入 Ballroom。這提示設備技術進步是推動廠房設計升級的重要驅動力。

40% 成本差異的來源分析

Case 1 vs Case 6 成本差異約 40%，來源：

建設成本差異：
  潔淨室面積大幅減少     → -15~20%
  HVAC 系統規模縮減      → -8~12%
  PAL/MAL 數量減少       → -3~5%

年度運營成本差異：
  更衣消耗品（無塵服等）→ -30~40%
  環境監測（EM）頻次     → -40~60%
  設施維護（過濾器等）   → -20~30%
  能源消耗（HVAC）       → -20~30%

人員效率提升：
  更衣時間減少           → 生產時間增加 10~15%
  物料流動簡化           → 操作效率提升

比喻說明 — 六個案例如同餐廳廚房的演進

想像一家餐廳廚房的演進：

  • Case 1：完全開放廚房，每道菜都需要廚師穿特製防護服，每個料理台之間都有玻璃隔牆
  • Case 3-4：大多數食材都有真空包裝，開包裝和擺盤時需要局部保護
  • Case 6：幾乎所有食材都密封包裝到最後才開，整個廚房只是一個通風良好的大空間，效率最高、衛生管理最容易

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念 — Ballroom 的完整設計要素

一個完整的 Ballroom 設施包含：

  • 核心 Ballroom（CNC）：所有密閉製程設備（生物反應器、純化設備、緩衝液系統）
  • 衛星潔淨室（Grade 8）：無法密閉的步驟，如柱填充（column packing），通過氣閘連接到 Ballroom
  • 設備清洗室：獨立區域，可為 CNC 或 Grade 8（取決於清洗後的設備用途）
  • 更衣設施（最小化）：CNC 區域更衣要求大幅低於潔淨室，節省空間

重點提示 — 一次性耗材與不鏽鋼均相容

ISPE 特別強調 Ballroom 概念不依賴特定設備材質，一次性耗材（SU）和不鏽鋼設備都能在 Ballroom 概念下運作，前提是設備必須達到功能密閉（functionally closed）狀態。

這對於正在考慮從 SU 技術過渡到大規模不鏽鋼的 CDMO 是重要信息：廠房本身不需要重建，只需確保新設備的密閉性。

比喻說明 — Ballroom 如同現代化的無人工廠

現代汽車工廠的生產線是開放式的大空間，不同工序的機械手臂在同一廠房中並行工作，靠機器人精準操作實現隔離，而不是靠牆壁。

Ballroom 概念正是如此：密閉設備的外殼就是「機器人手臂的精準邊界」，房間只是一個提供基礎環境管控（溫濕度、換氣）的外殼，不再是 GMP 隔離的主要工具。

實務應用 — CDMO 選擇 Ballroom 設計的商業考量

對於規劃新建或改造廠房的 CDMO：

  • 初始投資：Ballroom 廠房建設成本比多房間設計低 20-40%
  • 運營彈性：同一空間可快速重新配置設備，應對不同客戶需求
  • 擴充性：只需增加設備，不需新建隔牆和潔淨室認證
  • 監管風險：需要完整的 CCS（污染控制策略）和完善的 SU 管理程序
  • 法規文件：URS 必須清楚描述密閉性要求，MAA/BLA 中需論證 CNC 的合理性

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary — Chapter 6A Key Takeaways

  • 廠房設計的推導邏輯：從商業產能目標 → BFD/PFD → 製程密閉程度決策 → 隔離策略 → 廠房佈局，每一步都有明確的邏輯聯繫。
  • 規模對廠房的影響：大規模（>10,000 L）傾向垂直堆疊五層設計；小規模（≤2,000 L）使用一次性耗材，水平排列於單一生產層，可大幅縮減廠房面積。
  • 四種隔離概念：物理、環境、時間、程序隔離各有其適用場景。密閉製程最大的設計紅利是將物理隔離從「房間層面」下移到「設備層面」，使廠房從多個潔淨室演進為單一 CNC Ballroom。
  • 六個案例的核心訊息：Case 1（全開放 Grade 7）到 Case 6（全密閉 CNC Ballroom）的建設與運營成本差距約 40%。密閉程度越高，廠房越簡單、越便宜、越靈活。
  • CNC 概念的重要性：CNC 不是「沒有環境管控」，而是「有管控但不符合 ISO 粒子計數分級」。對於完全密閉製程，CNC 是合理且法規接受的空間等級。
  • 雙重保護壓力：生物製藥廠房面對「保護產品（潔淨室設計）」和「保護人員/環境（圍堵設計）」兩個競爭需求。製程密閉是同時滿足兩者的最佳策略。
  • 在線稀釋 + SU 技術：緩衝液在線稀釋可將緩衝液儲存空間縮減 60-80%，結合一次性耗材可進一步壓縮廠房面積，是現代 CDMO 的首選技術組合。
