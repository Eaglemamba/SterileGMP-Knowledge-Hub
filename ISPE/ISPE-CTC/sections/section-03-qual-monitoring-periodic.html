Chapter 7: Qualification / 第七章：確效

7.0 When Is Qualification Required? / 何時需要確效？

Systems that have the potential to impact product quality require Qualification. This includes systems used to store GMP raw materials, intermediates, and finished products.

Where individual gel packs are verified before use (confirming system performance/adequate pack conditioning time), the systems used for their conditioning and storage may be categorised as engineering systems (i.e., commissioned systems).

The decision to Commission only versus Commission and Qualify is a judgment based on a comprehensive understanding of the product, process, and the nature of the system by appropriately qualified personnel. Refer to the ISPE Baseline® Guide: Commissioning and Qualification.

The traditional approach uses separate IQ and OQ protocols to provide documented evidence that installation and operation meet the user requirements/system specifications after commissioning. IQ and OQ may be combined into a single document.

Typically, a system Performance Qualification (PQ) is not required; any operational testing needed may be addressed within OQ. Where product-specific or loading-specific evidence is necessary, PQ is applied.

中文解說：確效的觸發條件

GMP 系統分類原則

ISPE 將受控溫度設備分為兩類：

  • 工程系統（Commissioned）：對產品品質影響間接，例如用於調節凝膠包溫度的冷房。試車（Commissioning）即可，不強制執行 IQ/OQ。
  • 確效系統（Qualified）：直接存放 GMP 原料、中間體或成品者。必須完成 IQ + OQ（PQ 視風險決定）。

類比：汽車出廠驗收 vs 年度保養

IQ = 新車交付時驗收（硬體規格符合訂單）；OQ = 試駕測試（操控在規格範圍內）；PQ = 實際載貨測試（滿載後性能達標）。試車（Commissioning）= 工廠組裝後的內部品管，為 IQ 提供基礎文件。

CDMO 現場提示

多產品 CDMO 中，同一台 2–8°C 冷藏箱可能同時存放生物製劑原液與成品。此類設備必須確效，且應在 PQ 中涵蓋代表性裝載配置，避免因產品切換導致溫度分布改變。

7.1 Installation Qualification (IQ) / 安裝確效

IQ should include:

  • A record of the major components — model numbers, serial numbers, supplier details
  • Confirmation that calibration has been conducted as required by the specifications
  • Confirmation that the specified utilities have been correctly connected — electrical supply, drainage, alarm cabling
  • Reference to the User Requirement Specification (URS) to confirm that the installed equipment matches the agreed specification
  • Listing of all critical instruments and their calibration due dates
  • As-built drawings or photographs showing equipment layout, probe locations, and utility connections

IQ may leverage commissioning documentation (FAT/SAT records, vendor certificates) provided the documents are referenced, controlled, and meet GMP traceability requirements.

7.2 Operational Qualification (OQ) / 運行確效

OQ testing provides evidence that the system temperature ranges meet specifications during simulated operations. Key OQ requirements:

  • Empty chamber mapping — conducted for no less than 24 hours; sensors placed as defined in the test strategy; protocol must include a diagram showing sensor placement
  • Temperature study on the specified load — also no less than 24 hours
  • Open door testing (where applicable) — simulates routine access; results used to justify alarm delay settings
  • Power loss / temperature rise testing (where applicable) — how long until temperature exceedance after power failure
  • Quality-related alarm testing — confirm alert and action alarms trigger at set points
  • For liquid nitrogen or CO₂ freezers: confirm that the level of LN₂/CO₂ is maintained at set-point
  • Worst-case bracket approach: test at extreme conditions that cover all normal operating scenarios

最差情況括號法 (Worst-Case Bracket Approach)

在 OQ 中，選取最不利的操作條件組合（例如：空腔 + 夏季環境 + 最高預期開門頻率），若在此條件下仍符合規格，則可覆蓋一般操作情境，無需逐一測試每種組合。

7.3 Performance Qualification (PQ) / 性能確效

For CTC systems, ISPE states that a system PQ is typically not required. Any operational testing required may be addressed in OQ. However, PQ may be warranted when:

  • Product-specific evidence is needed (e.g., temperature-sensitive biologics with narrow stability ranges)
  • Representative loading configurations must be demonstrated for multiple product types
  • Regulatory requirements or customer contracts explicitly call for PQ documentation
  • The system stores particularly high-value or critical intermediates where product loss risk is significant

Where PQ is conducted:

  • Use representative loading patterns — not necessarily full load, but covering the range of expected usage
  • Conduct loaded chamber mapping with thermal mass representative of actual product containers
  • Document the relationship between PQ results and the URS acceptance criteria
  • PQ protocols and reports follow the same GMP documentation structure as IQ/OQ

7.4 Science and Risk-Based Approach / 科學與風險導向方法

ISPE promotes a science and risk-based approach to CTC qualification. The core concept is based on defining a test strategy and conducting testing once, with "Qualification" confirming that there is documented evidence that installation and operation meet specifications.

This documentation can be built through engineering commissioning documents, installation verification, and operational verification documented through commissioning / FAT / SAT. These activities become qualification evidence when they are:

  • Conducted per an approved protocol with pre-defined acceptance criteria
  • Reviewed and approved by Quality
  • Traceable and version-controlled
  • Cross-referenced explicitly in the IQ/OQ documents

Examples of the science-based approach are provided in Appendix 5 of the ISPE CTC Guide. The key principle: test once, document robustly, qualify efficiently.

8.0 Independent Monitoring Principle / 獨立監測原則

It is considered good practice to use an independent system to monitor critical parameters. Calibration of monitoring probes can be performed without impacting system operation. The location of probes is determined during mapping.

Monitoring systems used in practice include:

  • Conventional chart recorders
  • Datalogging chart recorders
  • Building Management Systems (BMS) / Building Automation Systems (BAS)
  • Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems

The choice depends on the facility, the number of CTCs to monitor, and regulatory expectations. BMS systems used for GMP monitoring must comply with data integrity requirements, including audit trails and access control.

8.1 Temperature Sensor Types / 溫度感測器類型

Three main types are used in GMP monitoring:

1. Thermocouples (熱電偶)
Operate via the Seebeck effect — a temperature gradient across a conductor generates a voltage. Two dissimilar metals form a circuit where the voltage difference is measurable (1–70 µV/°C for standard combinations). Cables and joints can be prone to damage. A common protocol allowance is up to 1% sensor failure without invalidating the test run, provided failed sensors are not in critical regions.

2. Resistance Temperature Detectors (RTDs) and Thermistors (熱電阻)
Based on resistance change with temperature. RTDs use metal (resistance increases with temperature); thermistors use ceramic material (resistance decreases with temperature). Measured using a Wheatstone bridge with a small current. High-quality RTDs may last >8 years in accelerated life tests. Wiring configurations (3-wire, 4-wire) minimise cable resistance effects.

RTD accuracy classes (IEC 751):

  • Class A: ± 0.15°C at 0°C (± 0.27°F at 32°F)
  • Class B: ± 0.30°C at 0°C (± 0.54°F at 32°F)

3. Chemical Indicators (化學型指示劑)
Chemicals that change colour with temperature. Used only to record minimum/maximum temperatures. Not considered accurate; no time record associated with temperature change. Not suitable for continuous GMP monitoring.

選型決策原則

對於 2–8°C 冷藏設備：

  • 接受範圍窄（僅 6°C 跨度），應選用 RTD Class A（±0.15°C），確保感測器精準度不佔用過多接受範圍餘裕
  • 熱電偶適合廣域測繪（±1.5°C 精度對 2–8°C 範圍仍屬偏大），需搭配高精度的參考傳感器進行現場校準
  • 化學型指示劑僅適用於貨物隨附的最低/最高溫度確認，不可用於設備確效或持續監測

8.2–8.3 Number and Location of Monitoring Probes / 探針數量與位置

Monitoring sensors should be placed where stored product will experience the maximum and minimum temperatures and, where applicable, more extreme humidity during normal CTC operation. At-risk (worst-case) locations are identified during mapping.

General sizing guidance:

  • For units <2 m³: one monitoring sensor in the worst-case location (typically the hottest location for a below-ambient unit) may be acceptable. A single sensor provides robust detection of operational or performance issues.
  • For larger systems or those with multiple cooling units: more sensors are justified to ensure adequate coverage of positions where stored product experiences maximum and minimum temperatures (and/or humidity).

Permanent sensor placement considerations:

  • Risk-based worst-case storage locations from mapping results
  • Single vs. dual temperature control unit placement
  • Volume of controlled space; density of materials and obstructions
  • High/low storage locations (warm air rises)
  • Independent heat sources (production equipment) within the CTC
  • Door areas and ingress/egress locations
  • Frequency of personnel and forklift ingress/egress during working hours
  • Operations outside working hours (lower traffic, different thermal dynamics)
  • Air conditioning of loading area — humidity and frost on evaporator coils
  • Sensors must not interfere with product movement or storage, and must be positioned in multiple geometric planes

The mapping results and data analysis are summarised in a report recommending optimum sensor locations for approval by the Quality Unit and System Owner.

8.4 Thermal Dampening Fluids / 熱阻尼液

Monitoring probes may have alarm delay times or be placed in thermal dampening fluids to avoid nuisance alarms from short-term temperature variances due to loading/unloading.

Air Probes with time delays:

  • Delay times must be justified as having no product impact
  • Must consider the monitoring system sample frequency: if product has an acceptable excursion limit of 30 minutes and the system samples every 15 minutes, every alarm is potentially an OOS situation
  • Standard approach: alarm delay of 15 minutes is commonly used as a default for units with a wide range of operational loads

Probes in thermal dampening fluid:

  • The dampening fluid must simulate the storage of product in primary containers
  • The fluid must react to CTC temperature changes quicker than the primary container/packaging
  • Justification must consider: container size and material of construction; fluid selection (glycol, glycerin, Paratherm™); location of probe within the container
  • Probe must be suitable for immersion; procedures confirm correct liquid level and probe positioning
  • For a variety of containers, a conservative (worst-case smallest container) approach guides the probe medium design
  • Account for both primary containers and secondary packaging thermal mass

15分鐘延遲設定的科學依據

ISPE 解釋 15 分鐘標準延遲的合理性：

  • 低溫設備開門後，密度較高的冷空氣會從腔體底部流出，被溫暖環境空氣取代，降溫需要時間
  • 統一延遲設定（15 min）可降低因設備個別設定不一致而導致的錯誤風險
  • 15 分鐘內的短暫溫升通常不代表系統異常，超過 15 分鐘則可能指向設備問題或人員忘關門
  • 警示：若產品穩定性限制（stability limit）接受時間 < 15 分鐘，此標準設定不適用，需客製化評估

8.5 Alarm Set Points / 警報設定值

Good practice is to define a clear alarm strategy for the CTC with two distinct categories:

8.5.1 Engineering Alarms (工程警報)

Generated by the temperature conditioning system and its control system. Include: system fault, high/low pressure, high/low temperature, temperature sensor fault (open/closed circuit). These require an engineering action.

Temperature alarm set points are defined based on system "as commissioned" performance — typically just outside the ± 3 standard deviation range to avoid nuisance alarms while ensuring early detection of potential problems.

Rationale: for a normal distribution, 99.7% of results lie within ±3σ. Using ±3σ set points creates very few false alarms while reliably detecting operational drift.

8.5.2 Quality Alarms (品質警報)

Alarms from the independent monitoring system, configured based on the defined temperature storage limits. Two levels are typically defined:

  • Alert (警示)：Tighter than the specification; triggers investigation. Typically ±1°C inside the specification band.
  • Action/Alarm (行動警報)：At or just inside the specification limits; triggers immediate action and investigation.

Example: For a 2–8°C unit with ±0.5°C sensor calibration accuracy:

  • Alert limits: 3°C and 7°C (37°F and 45°F)
  • Action limits: 2°C and 8°C (36°F and 46°F)

Engineering Alarm Set Point Calculation / 工程警報計算

Based on ≥24 hours of operational data at steady state:

Upper engineering alarm = Mean + 3σ
Lower engineering alarm = Mean − 3σ

Example: If the steady-state mean is 4.5°C and σ = 0.3°C:
Upper engineering alarm = 4.5 + 3(0.3) = 5.4°C
Lower engineering alarm = 4.5 − 3(0.3) = 3.6°C

This means engineering alarms are narrower than quality alerts, providing the earliest possible warning before product risk occurs.

Chapter 9: Periodic Evaluation & Frequency of Thermal Mapping Studies / 第九章：週期性評估與熱測繪頻率

9.0 Periodic Assessment Framework / 週期性評估框架

CTCs should be periodically assessed to determine the need for re-validation (re-mapping). Control systems, equipment, monitoring, and procedures should be part of this periodic evaluation to ensure the unit still operates in a verified/qualified manner.

The evaluation should include, but is not limited to, a review of:

  • Changes made to the system
  • Changes made to storage locations (rack/bin configuration)
  • Calibration history
  • Equipment use logs
  • Deviation investigations
  • Maintenance and repair history
  • Temperature mapping studies
  • Operational procedures
  • Existing verification/qualification documentation
  • Ambient temperature profiles (seasonal extremes)

Data collected from CTCs equipped with multiple permanently installed monitoring probes may be an acceptable substitute for requalification temperature mapping studies, provided the probes have a demonstrated history of successful operation including calibration and maintenance.

週期性評估 vs 重新測繪的決策邏輯

兩種不同的評估行動

Periodic assessment（週期性評估）：文件審查型——回顧校準記錄、警報歷史、SOP 變更、偏差、維護記錄。是每次週期性評估的最低要求，不一定觸發重新測繪。

Re-mapping（重新測繪）：實地測量型——重新在腔體中佈置溫度探針，運行 ≥24 小時，產生新的溫度分布數據。由 risk assessment 或觸發事件決定是否需要。

類比：健康檢查 vs 手術

週期性評估如同每年的健康檢查（血液報告、X 光），是例行性的。重新測繪如同精密外科手術前的術前評估——只有在有臨床指徵（觸發條件）時才執行，但頻率和指徵應提前在書面中定義清楚。

持續監測數據替代重新測繪的前提條件

  • 探針已安裝於測繪確定的最差情況位置
  • 探針具有完整的校準歷史（持續無 OOT / OOS）
  • 維護記錄完整，無未解釋的數據缺口
  • 監測系統本身已通過 CSV（電腦化系統驗證）
  • 替代方案需在 VMP（驗證主計畫）中明確定義

9.1 Re-mapping Triggers / 重新測繪觸發條件

Mapping exercises should be repeated according to risk assessment results or whenever significant modifications are made to the facility or temperature-controlling equipment.

System / Equipment triggers:

  • Equipment repair affecting temperature performance (e.g., compressor replacement, refrigerant recharge)
  • Relocation of the CTC to a different area
  • HVAC changes affecting the space surrounding the CTC
  • Changes to racking, bin configuration, or storage organisation patterns
  • Addition or removal of de-stratification fans
  • Changes to door configuration or door seals

Performance / Trend triggers:

  • Increasing frequency of temperature alerts or alarms (negative trend)
  • Temperature deviations outside specification
  • Seasonal extremes exceeding historical data range
  • Monitoring data showing increased variability or drift

Regulatory / Administrative triggers:

  • Regulatory inspection observations or internal audit findings
  • Number of SOP changes beyond a threshold defined in the VMP
  • Number of change controls to the area, temperature control system, or monitoring system
  • Scheduled requalification frequency defined in the VMP (e.g., every 3 years)

9.2 Risk-Based Frequency / 風險導向頻率

A Risk Assessment is recommended to determine the frequency of periodic review and the need for repeating mapping. The frequency should be aligned with the site's Validation Master Plan (VMP) and based on risk factors including:

  • Local regulations and regulatory expectations for maintaining a validated state
  • Regulatory inspection observations and internal audit observations
  • History of performance in maintaining the desired temperature range
  • Number of temperature deviations (number of alerts and alarms)
  • Number of SOP changes
  • Number of change controls to the area, temperature control system, or monitoring system

Typical industry practice:

  • Remapping frequency: every 3 to 5 years — for standard storage units with stable performance history
  • Critical product storage: annually — for high-risk, temperature-sensitive, or high-value products
  • The rationale for the chosen frequency must be documented for the facility and/or equipment

Risk factors that increase frequency:

  • High exposure to environmental extremes (refrigerated storage directly exposed to seasonal fluctuations)
  • Narrow operating range (e.g., -20°C ± 5°C vs. 15–25°C)
  • High product value or supply-chain criticality
  • Old equipment with degrading performance history

The storage of high-value and/or temperature-sensitive products may warrant more frequent re-verification/re-qualification. Maintaining product availability in the supply chain is a critical quality and customer-focused consideration.

9.3 Continuous Performance Verification / 持續性能驗證

An alternative to periodic re-mapping: continuous performance verification using a sufficient number of permanently installed monitoring sensors. This strategy has become more feasible due to the reduced cost of radio transmitter sensors.

How it works:

  • Additional sensors are installed beyond the minimum monitoring requirement, placed at positions that would detect performance degradation from any direction (unit cooler failure, structural degradation, loading pattern changes)
  • Storage locations are formally defined and any revisions addressed through change control, allowing reassessment of probe number/location
  • The ongoing monitoring data provides continuous evidence that the system operates within specification

Design requirements for continuous verification:

  • The number and location of sensors must be determined based on ability to detect any impact of the conditioning system performance and system construction issues
  • Sensors must cover all zones where a degradation event would be detectable — including corners, near unit coolers, door areas, and stratification zones
  • For a walk-in unit in use, mapping may be performed with the unit loaded, using sensor locations from the initial mapping where possible

ISPE provides Figures 9.1–9.4 as examples of cold room sensor layout designs for both initial mapping (sparse sensors) and continuous verification (denser sensor arrays).

9.4 Handling Excursion Documentation / 偏差文件處理

A point person or department should be formally identified to coordinate the response to temperature excursions outside specified limits.

Excursion response protocol elements:

  • Immediate containment — assess whether product has been compromised; initiate product quarantine if needed
  • Root cause investigation — equipment failure, human error, systemic issue, or environmental factor
  • Impact assessment — review product's temperature stability data; determine if the excursion duration × magnitude is within the product's known stability budget
  • Deviation report — formal GMP documentation of the event, investigation, and disposition decision
  • CAPA (Corrective and Preventive Actions) — address root cause to prevent recurrence
  • Trend review — incorporate excursion data into the next periodic evaluation to assess whether re-mapping is warranted

Walk-in freezer / refrigerator specific checks (periodic maintenance):

  • Envelope inspection: door seals, moisture/ice buildup as indicator of air leakage
  • Alarm history review for negative performance trends
  • Verify refrigeration set point is being maintained (e.g., compressor suction pressure)
  • Confirm coils are cleaned, fan operation checked, control valves and thermal expansion valves verified, defrost settings and operation verified

Core Message / 核心訊息

Integrating Chapters 7, 8, and 9 / 整合第七、八、九章

"Qualification proves a snapshot; monitoring proves the state over time — both are required to demonstrate ongoing control."

「確效證明的是某一時刻的快照；監測證明的是隨時間的持續狀態——兩者都是展示持續控制的必要條件。」

Why Both Are Required / 為何兩者缺一不可

Qualification (IQ/OQ/PQ) establishes that a CTC was installed correctly and did perform within specification under defined test conditions — typically a 24-hour or defined-duration snapshot. It answers the question: "Is this system capable?"

Continuous monitoring establishes that the system continues to perform within specification across every hour of every operational day, under real-world loading patterns, seasonal temperature swings, door-opening events, and equipment aging. It answers: "Is this system actually in control right now?"

Periodic evaluation bridges the two — it reviews monitoring data trends, maintenance history, and change history to determine whether the qualification status remains valid, or whether a new mapping study is needed to re-establish the snapshot.

In the ISPE framework, a CTC is considered to be in a state of control only when all three elements are in place:

  1. Valid, current qualification documentation (IQ + OQ)
  2. Functioning continuous monitoring with calibrated probes in worst-case locations
  3. Regular periodic evaluation with documented risk assessment for re-mapping decisions

對於承接多客戶、多產品的 CDMO，這三要素構成了「溫控設備生命週期管理」的核心：

  • 確效：在設備安裝、重大變更後，用受控測試證明設備有能力達到規格
  • 監測：每天 24 小時、每週 7 天，持續確認設備仍在控制狀態，保護所有客戶的產品
