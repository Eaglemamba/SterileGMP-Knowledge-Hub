9	 Periodic Review
9.1	
Introduction
There is a regulatory requirement to maintain qualified systems in a validated state, i.e., the qualification documents 
remain valid and the system remains suitable for the intended purpose (refer to Chapter 15 for the regulatory basis). 
Quality systems are in place to maintain that status and should be subject to internal audit to ensure robustness. 
These quality systems are:
•	
Change control
•	
Maintenance
•	
Calibration
•	
Deviation
It is regulatory expectation that there is also a periodic review process to ensure that systems can be considered 
qualified (refer to Chapter 15 for the regulatory basis).
This chapter describes an approach for periodic review where the frequency of review or requalification is established 
based on risk, the current level of process understanding and process performance, and regulatory requirements.
9.1.1	
Rationale
Certain systems have regulatory expectations to confirm ongoing system performance through periodic verification; 
such systems include sterilizing autoclaves, depyrogenation tunnels/ovens, and equipment with a Sterilize-in-Place 
(SIP) functionality (e.g., freeze dryer). Table 9.1 summarizes examples of periodic verification frequencies for systems 
with regulatory expectations.

For other types of systems, organizations have traditionally used an annual detailed review of the change history, 
maintenance, and operational records to provide this confirmation. However, this approach has typically provided 
a small return when compared to the considerable investment to conduct the review process. In addition, there is 
concern that, due to the scale of the effort involved, there is a significant risk that important performance signals may 
be missed.
This Guide proposes applying a science and risk-based approach to periodic review:
•	
For a critical utilities system (such as HVAC, clean compressed air, pharmaceutical water and steam systems) 
routine monitoring includes:
-	
Regular reviews of the results
-	
Regular reporting of system performance trends, preventative maintenance and calibration metrics
-	
Regular check of qualification drawings (typically biannually) and of system tagging (typically annually)
	
If routine monitoring is performed, then no additional review is considered necessary. The monitoring confirms 
system performance and the drawing verification confirms that documentation is up-to-date.
•	
For other systems (e.g., manufacturing systems), the relative potential risk to product quality for different system 
types is evaluated and the frequency of review is defined based on the risk.

This proposed approach is a triage process that looks for any unusual performance signals at a high level. If none 
are observed, then the review is complete. If there are signals observed, then a deeper review is conducted that may 
result in detailed investigation and testing.
9.1.2	
Principles/Concepts
This periodic review approach is most efficient if the organization/site has mature quality systems and the associated 
quality system performance metrics. These metrics could include:
•	
Monitoring of maintenance to ensure scheduled activities are not delayed beyond the permitted calendar window
-	
Monitoring of breakdown/unplanned maintenance activities for frequency and recurrence of specific issues
•	
Monitoring of calibration to ensure any scheduled checks/activities are not delayed beyond the permitted 
calendar window
•	
Robust change control system with categorization of changes
•	
Robust deviation system with categorization of deviations
•	
Robust review of computer system audit trails and security at procedurally controlled intervals including:
-	
Confirming users are not using generic names or shared accounts
-	
Ensuring that there are no redundant users or obsolete users, e.g. for staff who have changed roles or left 
the organization (i.e., good user access management)
-	
Reviewing the audit trail of administrator actions, ensuring a quality system record justifies each action, and 
listing the audit trails reviewed
-	
Reviewing the audit trail of other user category actions, ensuring a quality system record justifies the actions 
(if applicable), and listing the audit trails reviewed
-	
Reviewing the audit trail for changes to critical configuration settings identified in the data integrity 
assessment, ensuring a quality system record justifies the changes, and listing the audit trails reviewed
-	
Verifying that audit trails reviews were performed as dictated in the system administrator SOP and 
operational SOP
-	
Making sure the Quality Unit reviews the audit trail as verifier
-	
Verifying backup and archiving of GMP critical data
The use of these performance metrics can minimize the amount of effort required to obtain the information for the 
periodic review.
Where these mature quality systems are not in place, the same principles can be used, but the process may not be 
as efficient.

9.2	
Periodic Review Approach
The periodic review approach proposed in this Guide consists of two phases:
•	
Phase 1: System Categorization and Schedule Assignment
•	
Phase 2: Periodic Review Execution
-	
Initial Assessment (Tier A)
-	
Second Level Assessment (Tier B)
-	
Third Level Assessment (Tier C)
It is strongly recommended that the period review process be led by an individual who has a broad range of 
experience.
The Tier B and Tier C reviews should be conducted by a small team where necessary. This small team should 
consist of individuals who are knowledgeable in the system operating, maintenance, and calibration history; these 
are typically the system owner, maintenance, and a Quality Unit representative. The review should be approved, at a 
minimum, by the system owner and the Quality Unit. This approach is intended to provide an efficient process, where 
the overall review is accomplished in a timely manner.

9.2.1	
Prerequisites
The required prerequisites for the periodic review process include:
•	
List of validated manufacturing systems
•	
Copy of relevant current regulations (i.e., those for the markets the facility supplies)
•	
For each system:
-	
As-built or as-is record drawing (qualification record drawing)
-	
Change system metrics or system change history
-	
Maintenance and calibration system metrics or system maintenance and calibration history
-	
Deviation system metrics or deviations related to the system performance (i.e., where a root cause 
investigation has highlighted equipment performance as a root cause)
-	
System validation/qualification documents
-	
Risk assessment (if available)
9.3	
Phase 1: System Categorization and Schedule Assignment
The systems are categorized based on the potential to impact product quality. Typically, due to in-process testing, 
systems used for early stages of manufacture are considered lower risk that those used for stages closer to the 
finished product.
The category defines the periodic review frequency schedule. These frequencies should be based on SME 
determination, considering the potential for the system to impact product quality. Systems that have requirements 
defined in the relevant local regulations and/or regulatory guidance must be managed to meet those requirements.

9.4	
Phase 2: Periodic Review Execution
Using the frequency schedules established in Phase 1 (System Categorization and Schedule Assignment), the 
periodic reviews can be performed using a three-tier approach which involves reviewing the following four quality 
systems:
•	
GMP compliance expectations
•	
Change control system
•	
Maintenance/calibration system
•	
Deviation system
The three-tier approach is summarized as follows:
•	
An initial assessment (Tier A) is done at a high level. If this initial assessment shows any signs that the system is 
not operating consistently, then the assessment progresses to the next level; if not, then the review is considered 
complete.
•	
The second level assessment (Tier B) is more detailed. If this assessment shows that there are any issues, then 
the assessment progresses to the next level. If this assessment concludes that the system is still performing 
satisfactorily, then the review is complete.
•	
The third level assessment (Tier C) involves defining and completing the actions required to determine the 
system to be suitable for the intended purpose, with the necessary supporting documentation updated as 
necessary.
The assessment period is the time period since the previous periodic review or since the original qualification.
Refer to Chapter 25 (Appendix 11) for an example periodic review form. Refer to Chapter 26 (Appendix 12) for 
information regarding periodic review of Controlled Temperature Chambers (CTCs), including an approach designed 
to address the system-specific issues.
Note: When a periodic review program is initially introduced, it is typical to cover the systems in each schedule class 
within the associated timeframe. For example, for systems with a two-year review period, the initial review of all 
systems in that schedule class can be completed in the first two years.

9.4.1	
Initial Assessment (Tier A)
To perform the initial assessment, the records from the four quality systems are reviewed to identify any concerns 
related to the qualified state of the system being assessed.
GMP Compliance Expectations (Tier A)
	
If there is an organization/site quality system that monitors changes in regulatory requirements/expectations and 
proactively addresses changes within the organization/site, then this category does not need to be reviewed as 
part of periodic review.
If this system is not in place, then the Quality Unit should:
•	
Review changes in the relevant regulatory regulations and guidances for the assessment period, with 
the current scope of qualification reviewed to ensure it meets any changes in the requirements where 
necessary.
•	
Review related audit observations and determine if there are any changes in regulatory expectations.
Change Control System (Tier A)
For the system being assessed:
•	
Perform a query in the change control system database for the assessment period
•	
Review the query results and determine if there have been any reportable change requests with qualification 
impact that can affect the system quality attributes
	
Note: Change records that have been determined to be low risk, and thus are not reportable to regulatory 
agencies, do not need to be reviewed as part of the periodic review process.
Maintenance/Calibration System (Tier A)
For the system being assessed:
•	
Perform a query in the maintenance management system for the assessment period
•	
Determine if there were repeated corrective maintenance and/or calibration records
Deviation System (Tier A)
For the system being assessed:
•	
Perform a query of the deviations for the assessment period
•	
Review the query results for deviations with potential product quality impact where the root cause has been 
attributed to the system being assessed
	
Note: Deviations that have already been determined to have little or no risk to product quality do not need to be 
reviewed as part of the periodic review process.

After this initial assessment of each quality system:
•	
If any concerns (regarding the potential impact to the qualified state) within a quality system are identified, then a 
second level assessment needs to be performed. Note that the scope of the second level assessment is limited 
to the quality system(s) of concern.
•	
If no concerns are identified, then the assessment for that quality system is complete.
9.4.2	
Second Level Assessment (Tier B)
The second level assessment should only be performed on each quality system category that was determined during 
the initial assessment to require further assessment. All second level assessment activities should be performed by 
representatives (SMEs) from the same departments as the initial assessment team.
GMP Compliance Expectations (Tier B)
Using the information from the initial assessment:
•	
Evaluate the impact of the regulatory changes identified in the initial assessment
•	
In the judgment of the assessment team, determine if the changes present concerns about the qualified 
state of the system
Change Control System (Tier B)
Using the information from the initial assessment:
•	
Evaluate the changes identified in the initial assessment
•	
In the judgment of the assessment team, determine if the number and/or type of changes identified present 
concerns about the qualified state of the system
Maintenance/Calibration System (Tier B)
Using the information from the initial assessment:
•	
Review the maintenance and calibration records identified in the initial assessment (including corrective 
maintenance)
•	
In the judgment of the assessment team, determine if the records present concerns about the qualified state 
of the system
Deviation System (Tier B)
Using the information from the initial assessment:
•	
Review the deviations with potential product impact identified in the initial assessment
•	
In the judgment of the assessment team, determine if the deviations indicate that the system performance is 
not consistent and thus may not be performing in a qualified state
