4.1 Design of Generation of Compressed Air Systems — 壓縮空氣系統生成設計

原文內容 Original Text

For process applications, compressed air systems should include:

  • a supply side (generation): including compressors and air treatment
  • a demand side: including distribution and storage systems, connecting the gas to the process application

The supply side and the demand side of compressed air systems should be integrated and should be considered as a complete system to maximize overall efficiency and ensure the required air quality is delivered at the specified flow rates and pressures required.

This section of the Guide focuses on the supply (generation) side of the compressed air system. The supply side of the system should continuously provide clean, dry air at the correct pressure, quality, and flow.

The design of the supply side of compressed air systems should consider:

  • compressors
  • filtration
  • air receivers
  • dryers
  • system control

Possible improvements to a system's operating efficiency should also be detailed, to help improve sustainability and to reduce operating costs.

A single generation system may be used for both instrument (or facility) air and process air; however, additional criteria may need to be met in order to achieve quality requirements for process air. Entire systems can be designed to meet the additional criteria for process air. Alternatively, further treatment to meet these additional criteria can be given only to air in a process branch.

4.1.1 Arrangement of Supply Side Components

Air discharged from a compressor at pressure will usually be saturated and contain particulate (usually with a particle size of under 10 µm, i.e., at sizes below the typical size limit of an inlet air filter). If the compressor is a lubricated compressor, the discharge air can also contain oil as a vapor or particulate. Typically, oil content in the discharge of a lubricated compressor is around 3 ppm. Oil removal filters (coalescing type) should be included in the sequence if lubricated compressors are used.

When using a desiccant type dryer, the typical arrangement of equipment is:

  1. compressor
  2. general purpose filter (If placed after a lubricated compressor, this filter will need coalescing properties.)
  3. optional "wet" receiver for control storage (The wet receiver provides purge air when a desiccant tower regenerates and prevents an artificial pressure drop in the compressor.)
  4. desiccant dryer
  5. particulate filter (Desiccant beds can move during use, generating dust which can escape from the desiccant tower.)
  6. dry air receiver (This receiver is responsible for controlling the system pressure during periods where there is a high demand from a distribution system. This receiver provides "capacitance" or stored energy to control pressure fluctuations.)
  7. optional system pressure controller (This device is responsible for controlling the pressure in a system and can optimize the capacitance in a dry air receiver.)

Redundancy of filters or dryer with transfer valves may be necessary to allow maintenance to be performed safely while a system is kept operational.

"Monitoring differential pressure across a filter for pressure build-up or spikes may not be appropriate, as a collapsed or ruptured element could yield a very low to no pressure differential, and coalescing filters may not show an increase in differential pressure at the end of their useful life."

Filter effectiveness is dependent on maintenance and conditions. Higher temperatures reduce effective filtration limits. Manufacturers usually rate a filter's efficiency at 70°F (or 20°C). In addition, higher oil carryover will load a filter more rapidly.

高油量、高溫度都會加速濾心失效——廠務需將此納入維護頻率的修正因子。

4.2–4.3 Equipment Selection & Compressors — 設備選型與壓縮機

原文內容 Original Text

4.2 Equipment Selection

Equipment selection should start with a review of user requirements. Typically, a specific volume flow rate or mass flow and pressure are required by a process.

4.3 Compressors

There are two basic types of compressors:

  1. positive-displacement
  2. dynamic

In positive-displacement type compressors, a given quantity of air is squeezed or mechanically reduced to create pressure. The volumetric flow rate is directly proportional to the size and speed of the compressor.

Dynamic compressors induce velocity using impellers and then convert the energy of the air motion into pressure using scrolls or diffusers.

4.3.1 Positive Displacement — Types

Positive displacement compressors include:

  • reciprocating or piston type compressors
  • oil free rotary scroll compressors
  • oil (or lubricant) injected rotary screw or rotary vane compressors
  • oil (or lubricant) free rotary screw compressors

4.3.2 Large Water Cooled Piston Type Compressors (50–500 hp / 37–400 kW)

Double-acting reciprocating compressors use both sides of the piston for air compression. These compressors may be single- or multi-stage, depending on discharge pressure and size. They can be made for pressures from 50 psig, but are primarily used for high pressure (600 psig/40 barg) applications, such as plastic blow molding. They may rarely be used for general process air pressures in pharmaceutical applications (i.e., 80 to 150 psig/5.5 to 10 barg). Large piston compressors usually require large foundations to minimize vibration and expensive wearable components, but remain among the most efficient compressors at approximately 19 to 20 bhp/100 cfm at 100 psig. Generally, piston type compressors have been replaced by rotary screw compressors.

4.3.3 Small Air Cooled Piston and Scroll Type Compressors (to 20 hp/15 kW)

The air cooled piston type compressors come in splash lubrication and pressure lubricated as well as oil-less designs. Scroll compressors are oil free by design, although their wearing parts may discharge some carbon fines, which can be removed with filtration. Laboratories and small pharmaceutical manufacturing may use a small air cooled piston type compressor or rotary scroll.

4.3.4 Rotary Screw or Rotary Vane – Lubricant or Oil Injected Compressors (5–600 hp)

The most common type of oil injected rotary compressor is the single stage helical-twin, screw-type (also known as rotary screw or helical-lobe). Rotary screw compressors usually have a lower initial cost, more compact size, and lower weight than large piston compressors. They are also considered relatively simple to maintain.

Oil injected rotary compressors typically use cleaner and longer life synthetic lubricants, including:

  • diesters
  • polyglycols
  • polyalphaolefins
  • polyol esters
  • silicon-based lubricants

The lubricant has three purposes: lubrication, sealing, and cooling. Discharge air normally contains 2 to 5 ppm of entrained lubricant; therefore, coalescing filters should be used. Other considerations include the cost of lubricant, separation elements, oil-water separation systems for condensate traps.

容積式壓縮機 (Positive Displacement)：靠機械結構「擠壓」固定量的空氣，輸出流量與轉速成正比，壓力穩定。分活塞式、螺旋式、旋片式。

動態式壓縮機 (Dynamic)：靠高速葉輪加速氣體，再透過擴散器將速度能轉換為壓力能，代表為離心式 (Centrifugal)。適合大流量場合，本質上無油。

比喻說明 Analogy

容積式壓縮機像是針筒——你推活塞，固定體積的空氣被壓縮。動態式壓縮機像是電風扇——靠旋轉加速空氣，高速後再讓它減速升壓。針筒式壓力穩定但流量受機械尺寸限制；電風扇式則特別適合大量氣體。

重點提示 Key Notes — 油潤式壓縮機的風險

油潤式螺旋壓縮機出口空氣含 2–5 ppm 潤滑油。對一般工業用途這可接受，但製藥廠如果用於 product-contact 區域，這是不可接受的污染源。

即使加裝聚結過濾器，仍存在殘留油霧風險，且過濾器一旦失效，油就直接進入製程。這也是為什麼 無油式壓縮機 (Oil-Free) 已成為製藥壓縮空氣的行業標準。

  ≈ 19–20 bhp / 100 cfm @ 100 psig
（目前仍是效率最高的類型之一）

  初始成本低、體積小、易維護
  但效率略低於大型活塞式

選型原則：
  流量需求 → 選機型大小
  壓力需求 → 選單/多段設計

4.3.5–4.3.7 Oil-Free & Dynamic Compressors — 無油螺旋式與動態式壓縮機

原文內容 Original Text

4.3.5 Lubricant-Free Rotary Screw Compressors (25–1250 hp / 18–900 kW)

"Lubricant free" refers to no lubricant in the compression area or passages. Lubricant may still be used to lubricate gearing, but it is kept separate from the air compression section by seals. Two types are available:

  1. dry
  2. water-injected

In the dry-type of lubricant-free rotary screw compressor, there is no injected fluid to remove the heat of compression. The lack of a sealing fluid also typically requires higher rotation speeds, timing gears, and a high temperature coating.

Single-stage lubricant-free rotary screw compressors can operate up to 50 psig. Two stages of compression may also be used, with an intercooler between the stages and an aftercooler after the second stage. Two-stage lubricant-free rotary screw compressors can achieve up to 150 psig.

In water-injected lubricant-free rotary screw compressors, similar timing gear construction is used, but water is injected into the compression chamber to act as a seal and to remove the heat of compression. This allows pressures in the 100 to 150 psig range to be achieved in a single stage. Typically, water lubricated compressors are below 100 kW. Consideration should be given to the potential risk of microbial contamination in water lubricated systems.

4.3.6 Dynamic Compressors

Dynamic compressors raise the pressure of air or gas by producing energy as air motion and converting it to pressure. Dynamic compressors include centrifugal and axial types and normally produce lubricant free air.

4.3.7 Centrifugal Air Compressors (200–20,000 hp / 150–15,000 kW)

Centrifugal air compressors may:

  • have two to four stages for pressures in the 100 to 150 psig range
  • range from around 300 to more than 100,000 cfm, but generally occur in the 1,200 to 5,000 cfm range

These compressors can provide lubricant-free air delivery, as there is no lubricant in the compression chambers. Lubrication for speed increasing gears and the hydrodynamic shaft bearings is separated from the compression chambers using shaft seals. Bearings have no moving parts as the pinion (high speed shaft) rides on a thin film of oil; therefore, built in shaft vibration monitoring is normally used to record operational trends and to protect the equipment.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

乾式無油螺旋 (Dry Oil-Free Screw)：壓縮腔完全無液體，依靠精密塗層與高速齒輪同步運轉，熱管理靠多段冷卻器處理。造價高，但空氣品質最純淨。

水噴射式 (Water-Injected)：用水取代潤滑油作為密封與冷卻介質，好處是可以單段達到更高壓力，但需管理水質與微生物風險。

離心式 (Centrifugal)：適合超大流量，本質上無油（軸承油與壓縮腔隔離），適合需要大量製程壓縮空氣的大型工廠。

重點提示 Key Notes — 水噴射式的微生物風險

水噴射式壓縮機雖然「無油」，但水本身是微生物的培養基。如果水循環系統管控不嚴，可能導致壓縮空氣受微生物汙染，對製藥廠而言是嚴重的 CQA 風險。

需要：定期更換循環水、監測微生物、確保後端過濾器設計涵蓋此風險。

比喻說明 Analogy

離心式壓縮機就像工廠的大型鍋爐房——體積大、設備貴、一旦裝好就穩定地供應大量能源。適合大型製藥廠或 CDMO 園區有多棟廠房需要大流量供氣的場合。小型多批次的 CDMO 反而更適合用較靈活的無油螺旋式，方便按需啟停。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：若您的廠區同時供應多條無菌充填線（隔離器加壓、塞子振動碗、氣動閥），壓縮空氣屬於 product-contact 輔助氣體類別。選擇無油乾式螺旋壓縮機 + 後端終端過濾，是目前最符合監管期望的組合，也是 EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 所強調的污染控制策略 (CCS) 的一部分。

4.4 Compressed Air System Controls — 壓縮空氣系統控制

原文內容 Original Text

Compressed air system controls usually have two purposes:

  1. to meet the requirements of the demand side of a compressed air system
  2. to monitor selected parameters, providing alarms and interlocks to protect the equipment from premature failure

Controls can affect efficiency, life, and reliability of compressed air systems.

The simplest system is to use inlet valve throttling to regulate the compressor's capacity. Throttling the intake valve all the way to 0% capacity will still have system power consumption circa 70% of the full load power. Also, compressor operation can become unstable; typically the operating range is 50 to 60% of the maximum capacity.

The system discharge can be throttled – this is not an efficient process and is rarely used.

System speed control may be used. This approach works for all compressor types, and improves efficiency. The operating range will vary depending on the system type; the cost of the variable speed drive can be significant for larger units.

The simplest form of control is stop start; improved efficiency can be from unloading the compressor, letting it idle, usually with a time limit on the idle run time.

More complex control systems can monitor system pressure and vary the compressor speed to maintain a constant pressure, monitoring usage to optimize the start and run times.

During system design the control options should be carefully investigated to select a system that will provide the optimum life cycle cost.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

進氣節流 (Inlet Valve Throttling)：最簡單的容量調節方式，但效率差——即使關到 0% 輸出，仍消耗約 70% 的滿載電力。

變頻驅動 (Variable Speed Drive, VSD)：根據實際需求調整馬達轉速，效率最佳，是現代大型系統的主流控制方式。初始成本高但長期節電效果顯著。

卸載怠速 (Unload-Idle)：在需求低谷時讓壓縮機空轉（不輸出氣體），比頻繁啟停對設備的損耗更小。

公式與計算 — 控制方式耗電比較

進氣節流 (Inlet Throttling)：
  0% 輸出 → 仍耗 ~70% 滿載電力
  有效調節範圍：50–60% 最大容量

變頻控制 (VSD)：
  輸出正比於轉速
  20% 輸出 → 約 20–30% 電力消耗
  （節能效果顯著）

生命週期成本 (Life Cycle Cost, LCC) 考量：
  VSD 初始成本高 + 節電收益
  vs. 節流閥 低初始成本 + 長期高電費

重點提示 Key Notes

控制系統的選擇直接影響廠務的年度能源帳單。製藥廠的壓縮空氣系統通常 24/7 運行，VSD 在需求波動大的廠區（如多批次 CDMO）節省的電費往往在 2–3 年內可回收設備投資差額。

4.5 Air Treatment & ISO 8573-1 Quality Classes — 空氣處理與品質等級

原文內容 Original Text

Treating compressed air to remove contaminants such as dirt (viable and non-viable particulate), lubricant, and water, may use equipment such as:

  • compressor aftercoolers
  • filters
  • separators
  • dryers
  • air receivers
  • traps
  • automatic drains

A commonly recognized standard is ISO 8573-1:2010 which identifies three classes of contaminants:

  1. particulate
  2. water
  3. oil/oil vapor

ISO 8573-1:2010 does not differentiate viable and non-viable particulates. Therefore, when using ISO 8573-1:2010 to define purity class of compressed air, further definition may be required in the User Requirements.

Measurements relate to standard conditions of 20°C, 1 bar absolute pressure.

4.5.1 Air Inlet Filters

Air inlet filters protect compressors from atmospheric airborne particles. Air inlet filters do not determine final air quality. Provided the air quality will not compromise the compressor reliability, it may be more cost effective to treat the air downstream of the compressor.

4.5.2 Intercooler and Aftercoolers

Intercoolers and aftercoolers are heat exchangers designed to remove heat of compression, to improve system efficiency. Moisture separators following an intercooler or aftercooler separate liquids entrained in the air or gas.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

ISO 8573-1:2010 是壓縮空氣品質的國際標準，定義三類污染物的七個等級（Class 0–6）：

  • 微粒 (Particulate)：依顆粒大小與濃度分級
  • 水分 (Water)：以壓力露點 (Pressure Dew Point, PDP) 表示
  • 油/油霧 (Oil & Vapor)：以 mg/m³ 表示

重要限制：ISO 8573-1 不區分活性（有微生物）與非活性微粒。製藥廠必須在 URS（使用者需求規格）中額外定義微生物限度。

重點提示 Key Notes — 製藥廠的品質目標

製藥製程空氣通常目標 ISO Class 1–2（油含量 ≤ 0.01–0.1 mg/m³），並搭配微生物規格（如 <1 CFU/m³ 或滅菌級 0.2 µm 過濾後無菌）。

Class 0 表示「用戶自訂、比 Class 1 更嚴格」，適用於最高品質要求的場合（如直接接觸無菌產品的壓縮空氣）。

比喻說明 Analogy

ISO 8573 的品質等級就像飲用水標準：Class 6 是工業冷卻水（能用就行），Class 1 是瓶裝礦泉水，Class 0 是注射用水級別。你的製程需要哪個等級，取決於空氣與產品的接觸程度——越靠近產品，要求越嚴格。

4.6 Dryers — 乾燥機類型比較

原文內容 Original Text

Once air leaves an aftercooler and moisture separator, its moisture content should have been reduced significantly; however, it may still be saturated. The temperature at which this moisture condenses is called the dewpoint. The dewpoint may be measured at line pressure or at atmospheric pressure.

Dryers are usually rated on pressure dewpoint. Dryer design should anticipate worst case operating condition based on the maximum allowable dewpoint. Types of dryers include:

  1. regenerative-desiccant dryer
  2. heat-of-compression dryer
  3. refrigerant dryer

4.6.1 Regenerative-Desiccant Dryers

Regenerative-desiccant dryers normally use a porous desiccant that adsorbs moisture. Desiccants include: silica gel, activated alumina, molecular sieves.

Normally, the desiccant resides in a twin tower. Compressed air to be dried flows through one tower, while the desiccant in the other is being regenerated. There are three types:

  1. Heatless dryer: requires about 15% of the dryer's capacity to be used for purge air, but normally has lowest initial cost.
  2. Heated dryer: reduces purge to 7 or 8%, but may cost more than a heatless dryer.
  3. Blower purge dryer: can reduce purge to 0 to 2%, but may cost more than a heated dryer.

Regenerative-desiccant-type dryers are commonly used in pharmaceutical manufacturing applications for process air, as they have a dewpoint rating more than 70°F (21°C) lower than refrigerant dryers (-40°F / -40°C dewpoint at standard inlet conditions).

4.6.2 Heat-of-Compression Dryers

Heat-of-compression dryers are regenerative desiccant dryers that use the heat generated during compression to achieve desiccant regeneration. They represent an efficient means for drying to −40°F (−40°C) dewpoint using either no or only small amounts of purge air for regeneration. Both require hot air from lubricant free rotary or centrifugal compressors.

Single-Vessel: A rotating desiccant drum directs air into two separate air streams. Where load is variable, so is the dewpoint. Heat-of-compression dryers are unlikely to provide −40°F (−40°C) constant dewpoint.

Twin-Tower: This dryer can be designed for constant dewpoint with varying loads. It has low to no purge air and repeatable dewpoint.

4.6.3 Refrigerant Dryers

Refrigerant dryers have low initial and operating costs, but are not frequently used in pharmaceutical applications for process air, as they usually require a much lower dewpoint. Refrigerated dryers provide a dewpoint in the 38°F to 50°F (3°C to 10°C) range.

Where process air system generation is combined with instrument air systems, it would not be unusual to use a refrigerated air dryer for the main generation system and a smaller desiccant dryer for the process air branch requirements.

露點 (Dew Point)：空氣中的水蒸氣在某溫度下開始凝結為液態水——這個溫度即為露點。露點越低，代表空氣越乾燥。

比喻說明 Analogy

冷凍式乾燥機就像冰箱裡的除濕盒——能讓空氣變乾一點，但到冬天（低溫環境）就沒效了。吸附式乾燥機則像矽膠乾燥劑包——不管什麼溫度都能持續吸濕，而且可以加熱再生重複使用。製藥廠需要全年穩定的超低露點，吸附式因此成為首選。

重點提示 Key Notes — 吹掃損耗

無加熱型吸附乾燥機 (Heatless) 需要消耗 15% 的乾燥空氣來吹掃再生另一塔。對壓縮空氣系統而言，這 15% 就是白白損失的能源。

外加熱型可減至 7–8%，鼓風吹掃型減至 0–2%，但設備成本逐步上升。選型時需做生命週期成本 (LCC) 分析，而非只看初始設備報價。

熱壓縮式 (Heat-of-Compression) 單容器型的露點會隨負荷變化而漂移——不適合需要穩定露點的 GMP 製程。應優先選用雙塔型以獲得穩定露點。

公式與計算 — 吹掃損耗對系統容量的影響

系統設計壓縮機容量必須涵蓋吹掃損耗：

若製程需求 = 1,000 cfm 乾燥空氣

壓縮機需輸出 = 1,000 / (1 - 0.15)
             = 1,000 / 0.85
             ≈ 1,176 cfm 總輸出

→ 多設計 17.6% 的壓縮機容量來補償吹掃損耗

換用鼓風吹掃型（2% 吹掃）：
壓縮機需輸出 = 1,000 / 0.98 ≈ 1,020 cfm
→ 節省顯著！

4.7 Compressed Air Filters — 壓縮空氣過濾器

原文內容 Original Text

Compressed air filters downstream of the air compressor are normally required for the removal of contaminants. Types of filter available include:

  • particulate filters to remove solid particles
  • coalescing filters to remove hydrocarbon droplets, moisture droplets, and particulates
  • adsorbent filters to remove hydrocarbon vapors and other aromatics (odors and tastes)

A particulate filter may be recommended after a desiccant-type dryer to remove desiccant dust. A coalescing-type filter may be recommended before a desiccant-type dryer to prevent fouling of the desiccant bed if a lubricant injected compressor is used upstream.

Filter grades of 0.45 µm or 0.2 µm may be employed at POUs (Point of Use).

When reviewing filters for their applicability, particle size removal ratings (e.g., 0.2 µm) and the filter efficiency rating (e.g., 99.97% efficient) should be reviewed. These ratings are normally based on defined inlet conditions, usually 70°F (21°C) at 100 psig.

A typical sequence of filters for a system is:

  1. Particulate Filter: typically 1 µm to 3 µm for removing rust, pipe scale, metal oxides, and desiccant particles
  2. High Temperature Particulate Filter: Temperature spikes can occur when using a heated desiccant dryer. Similar ratings to standard particulates apply, but can withstand higher operating temperatures to 350°F to 450°F (176.7°C to 232.2°C).
  3. Coalescing Filters: Liquid droplet removal capacity is typically 0.001 ppm by weight and aerosol size rated at 0.01 µm.
  4. Final Filter: A 0.2 µm liquid rated cartridge filter (down to 0.003 µm absolute rated for particles in gases) may be used as a final filter to obtain "sterile grade" compressed air. These filter cartridges are typically sanitary style to minimize the risk of bypass and incorporate a hydrophobic non-volatile membrane as the filter media.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

過濾器種類三大類：

  • 微粒過濾器 (Particulate Filter)：去除固態微粒（鐵鏽、管垢、吸附劑粉塵），通常 1–3 µm 評級。
  • 聚結過濾器 (Coalescing Filter)：讓細小油霧或水霧液滴聚合成大液滴後流出。聚結效率可達 0.01 µm 液霧。
  • 吸附過濾器 (Adsorbent/Activated Carbon Filter)：去除油蒸氣等氣態碳氫化合物，通常以活性碳為介質。

使用點過濾器 (POU Filter)：0.2 µm 疏水性膜過濾器，用於最終滅菌級壓縮空氣，防止微生物穿透。

比喻說明 Analogy

壓縮空氣過濾鏈路就像城市自來水系統：先是粗濾網擋掉大顆粒，再是沉澱池去除懸浮物，再是活性碳濾除異味，最後才是 0.2 µm 超濾膜確保飲用安全。每一層都有它的任務，缺一不可，順序也不能錯。

重點提示 Key Notes

加熱型吸附乾燥機在塔切換瞬間，會有短暫的溫度峰值（Heat Spike）。普通聚結過濾器可能因高溫而失效，必須在此位置使用高溫型微粒過濾器（耐 176–232°C）。

最終 0.2 µm 滅菌級過濾器的設計要點：使用衛生型殼體以防止旁路；濾膜材質必須為疏水性（不吸水），確保壓縮空氣通暢流過而水不會被攔截造成壓降過大。

實務應用 Practical Application

典型 CDMO 無菌充填線的壓縮空氣過濾配置（由上游到使用點）：

  1. 壓縮機出口 → 聚結預過濾（去除油霧，保護吸附乾燥機）
  2. 吸附乾燥機出口 → 1–3 µm 微粒過濾（去除吸附劑粉塵）
  3. 分配管網入口 → 活性碳吸附過濾（去除殘留碳氫蒸氣）
  4. 使用點 (POU) → 0.2 µm 疏水性滅菌級過濾（無菌空氣進入填充區）

4.8 Air Receivers — 空氣儲槽

原文內容 Original Text

Receivers can be used to provide surge capacity to meet peak process needs and minimize changes in system pressure during periods of peak demand.

Compressed air systems require a receiver. When demand peaks are short and limited (usually less than 60 to 90 seconds), a large air receiver may allow a smaller air compressor to be specified. Additional benefits include the:

  • improved system reliability, as the load cycles on the compressor are reduced
  • increased efficiency, as the likelihood of another compressor starting is minimized

A desiccant dryer's purge can be the temporary push to peak demand. Given the cyclical nature of purge, it creates a temporary artificial demand on the compressor for a brief period but can be invisible to the compressor controls with a wet air receiver (i.e., an air receiver placed before a purge type desiccant dryer).

A receiver placed downstream of air treatment equipment can be used to manage pressure variation in a distribution system.

"A rule of thumb used for sizing a receiver is to use a unit with a capacity of 6 to 10 times the compressor peak capacity (free air delivered/second)."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

溼空氣儲槽 (Wet Receiver)：位於壓縮機與乾燥機之間，緩衝乾燥機再生吹掃造成的瞬間壓降，讓壓縮機「看不見」吹掃需求，避免頻繁啟動。

乾空氣儲槽 (Dry Receiver)：位於乾燥機下游，作為緩衝儲能，穩定配管網路的壓力波動，應對短時間的高峰需求。

電容量 (Capacitance)：本文用這個物理學類比描述儲槽的「緩衝能量」——就像電容在電路中平滑電壓波動。

公式與計算 — 儲槽尺寸估算

儲槽選型經驗法則：
  容積 = 6–10 × 壓縮機峰值輸出 (FAD/秒)

範例：
  壓縮機輸出 = 50 cfm = 0.83 cf/s
  建議儲槽容積 = 6 × 0.83 至 10 × 0.83
               = 5 至 8.3 立方英尺 (cf)
               ≈ 140 至 235 公升

注意：
  高峰需求超過 60–90 秒 → 需要更大儲槽
  或升級更大壓縮機容量

重點提示 Key Notes

空氣儲槽是壓力容器，受各國法規管制（美國 ASME Boiler & Pressure Vessel Code，歐洲 PED）。必須定期進行檢驗（壓力測試、外觀檢查）並納入廠務保養計畫。

儲槽底部會積累冷凝水（因空氣在儲存中降溫），必須設置適當的排放機制（traps / drains），否則水分會進入下游系統。

4.9 Traps and Drains — 冷凝液疏水閥與排水

原文內容 Original Text

Traps or drains allow the removal of condensate from the compressed air system to protect the equipment being supplied by the system from the effects of a non-compressible fluid. Automatic condensate traps are used to conserve energy by preventing the loss of air through open petcocks and valves.

Methods to drain condensate include:

  1. mechanical traps
  2. solenoid actuated drain valves
  3. zero air-loss traps with reservoirs
  4. manual

4.9.1 Mechanical Traps

These are usually float-type mechanical traps with inverted buckets as a secondary design feature. Float-type traps are designed to open only as increasing condensate raises the water level and consequently, a float. Before the water is fully evacuated, the float reseals the trap, to prevent waste of air.

These traps can easily be blocked by buildup of particulate in the condensate. The inspection and maintenance of these traps should be incorporated into a maintenance program.

4.9.2 Solenoid Actuated Drain Valves

Solenoid-actuated drain valves are electrically operated and employ a timed sequence for opening and closing. Concerns include:

  • The open interval duration may be inadequate to evacuate all condensate.
  • The closed interval may be too long creating a back up of condensate into the compression stages.

The settings on these units should be revised depending on seasonal variation in outside air supplied to a system.

4.9.3 Zero-Air-Loss Traps with Reservoirs

Zero-air-loss traps can improve efficiency of a compressed air system. Types include:

  1. solenoid actuated with level sensor
  2. pneumatically actuated air cylinder, which opens a ball valve through a linkage to expel the condensate in the reservoir to the low-level point

Zero-air-loss traps combine the efficiency of no air loss and reduced maintenance with larger orifices, typically preventing the build-up of particulate in the trap. Strainers are recommended for all traps to prevent the likelihood of sediment blocking the passage of condensate.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

冷凝液 (Condensate)：壓縮空氣降溫後析出的液態水（可能含有油分），必須定期排放，否則進入設備會造成腐蝕、微生物滋生，甚至損壞氣動元件（液體不可壓縮）。

零氣損疏水閥 (Zero Air-Loss Trap)：最高效的排水方式——先將冷凝液蓄積在小型儲存腔，達到液位後才開閥排放，不讓壓縮空氣同時逸出。

比喻說明 Analogy

浮球式疏水閥就像廁所的浮球水箱：水位高時開閥排放，水排完浮球下降關閥。問題是若浮球機構裡積了雜質，就可能卡死不動作。電磁閥式定時排水則像定時澆花器——不管有沒有水都按時開關，在夏天（空氣含水量高）可能不夠用，冬天則可能浪費大量空氣。

重點提示 Key Notes

電磁閥式疏水閥的定時設定需要隨季節調整！台灣夏季濕度高，空氣含水量是冬季的 3–5 倍，冷凝液生成量大幅增加。固定不變的開閥間隔可能導致冷凝液滿溢，或者白白放掉大量壓縮空氣。建議納入每季廠務維保確認項目。

油水混合的冷凝液屬工業廢水，不能直接排入水溝——必須經過油水分離系統處理，這是環保法規的要求，也是稽查重點之一。

4.10 Designing for Redundancy — 備援設計（N+1 策略）

原文內容 Original Text

The design of a compressed air system should consider the implications of loss of supply to the process. The N+1 redundancy strategy — where N is the number of compressors required to meet the demand, plus one standby unit — is a common approach to ensure continuity of supply.

Key redundancy design considerations include:

  • Standby compressors should be capable of being brought online automatically or with minimal manual intervention in the event of a primary compressor failure.
  • Redundancy of filters or dryer with transfer valves may be necessary to allow maintenance to be performed safely while a system is kept operational.
  • Control systems should be configured to monitor system pressure and automatically switch to standby equipment when the primary unit fails or drops below a set pressure threshold.
  • Planned maintenance of one compressor should be possible without interrupting the supply to the process (i.e., the remaining compressors can carry the full process load).

The level of redundancy should be proportional to the criticality of the application. For critical pharmaceutical manufacturing processes where loss of compressed air supply could lead to batch loss, equipment damage, or patient safety risk, a higher level of redundancy is justified.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

N+1 備援策略：N 台壓縮機足夠應付正常需求，額外再加 1 台備用機。若任一台故障或進行維護，其餘機台仍可滿足完整製程需求。

類似邏輯也應用於：乾燥機（並聯雙套，帶切換閥）、主要過濾器（並聯旁通設計）。

比喻說明 Analogy

N+1 就像飛機的雙引擎設計——平常兩台引擎各出一半力，一台故障時另一台能獨力維持飛行。製藥廠的壓縮空氣也是這樣：不能為了省錢買單台大機，一旦故障整個製程停擺，那批次損失的代價往往比備援設備更貴。

公式與計算 — 備援投資的 ROI 分析

備援壓縮機成本：$150,000–300,000 USD（典型）

壓縮空氣故障造成的批次損失：
  無菌充填批次 × 損失風險（批均值）
  = 例如：2批 × $500,000/批 = $1,000,000

年化故障機率（未備援）：
  假設 MTBF = 5年 → 故障機率 20%/年

預期年損失 = $1,000,000 × 20% = $200,000/年

投資回收期 ≈ $250,000 / $200,000 ≈ 1.25 年

結論：備援壓縮機通常 1–2 年內回收投資

重點提示 Key Notes — CDMO 的備援邏輯

對 CDMO 而言，壓縮空氣故障的影響不只是一批產品，而是：

  • 隔離器失壓 → 無菌環境立即失效 → 整條線需要重新確認
  • 塞子振動碗停止 → 充填線中斷 → 部分充填批次可能需廢棄
  • 氣動門/閥無法動作 → 人員無法安全進出生產區
  • 客戶關係損失 → CDMO 的聲譽和合約續簽

這些連鎖影響讓 N+1 備援的商業論據非常清晰——這不只是技術問題，而是 CDMO 業務連續性 (Business Continuity) 的核心保障。

實務應用 Practical Application

建議操作：為您廠的壓縮空氣系統繪製一份「失效模式地圖」：

  1. 列出所有依賴壓縮空氣的關鍵設備（隔離器、充填機、氣動閥等）
  2. 評估各設備失去壓縮空氣後的影響程度（批次損失、安全風險、合規風險）
  3. 對最高風險節點，確認是否有足夠的備援（N+1、緊急氮氣備援、儲槽緩衝時間）
  4. 在變更管理 (Change Control) 中，記錄備援設計的合理性依據

本章重點回顧 Chapter Summary

  • 系統架構：壓縮空氣系統分供給側（壓縮機＋處理設備）與需求側（配管＋POU），需作為整體系統設計，而非各自獨立考量。
  • 無油壓縮機：製藥製程空氣的行業標準。油潤式壓縮機出口含 2–5 ppm 油分，即使加裝聚結過濾器仍有殘留風險，不適合 product-contact 應用。
  • 乾燥機選型：吸附式再生乾燥機可達 −40°C 以下壓力露點，是製藥製程空氣的首選；冷凍式僅能達 +3 至 +10°C，適用於儀器空氣。
  • 過濾鏈路：從 1–3 µm 粗濾到最終 0.2 µm 疏水性滅菌級過濾，每一層都有其不可替代的角色；溫度峰值位置需使用高溫型過濾器。
  • 儲槽功能：溼空氣儲槽緩衝乾燥機吹掃需求，乾空氣儲槽穩定配管壓力；選型以壓縮機峰值容量的 6–10 倍為參考。
  • 冷凝液管理：零氣損疏水閥效率最高；電磁閥式需隨季節調整設定；油水冷凝液須經油水分離後才能排放。
  • N+1 備援：對 CDMO 而言，壓縮空氣故障的連鎖損失（批次報廢、隔離器失壓、客戶信任）通常遠超備援設備的投資成本，備援是商業連續性的核心保障。
