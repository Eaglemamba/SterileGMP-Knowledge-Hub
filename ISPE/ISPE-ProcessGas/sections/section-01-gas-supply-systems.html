3.1.1 Compressed Gases

Gases under pressure are commonly referred to as compressed gases. A compressed gas means: A gas or mixture of gases having, in a container, an absolute pressure exceeding 40 psi at 70°F (21.1°C); or a gas or mixture of gases having, in a container, an absolute pressure exceeding 104 psi at 130°F (54.4°C) regardless of the pressure at 70°F (21.1°C); or a liquid having a vapor pressure exceeding 40 psi at 100°F (37.8°C) as determined by ASTM D-323-72.

Compressed gases may be divided into two categories depending upon their inherent physical state under pressure and their range of boiling points:

  • non-liquefied gases
  • liquefied gases

Non-Liquefied Gases
Non-liquefied gases are elements, or a combination of elements, that have boiling points lower than -130°F (-90°C). Examples include nitrogen, oxygen, and argon. When specific elements become liquefied at extremely low temperatures they are referred to as cryogenic liquids.

Liquefied Gases
Liquefied gases are those elements or compounds that become liquid in their containers at ambient temperatures and at specified pressures from 25 psig (172 kPa) to 1500 psig (10,340 kPa). These gases have boiling points in the range of -130°F (-90°C) to 68°F (20°C).

Carbon dioxide is an example of a liquefied gas that also has unique physical characteristics in that it may exist as a gas, liquid, or solid simultaneously at its triple point temperature of -69.9°F (-56.6°C) and at a pressure of 60.4 psig (416 kPa).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

壓縮氣體 (Compressed Gas): 凡容器內絕對壓力在 21°C 時超過 40 psi，即屬壓縮氣體。這個定義決定了儲存、運輸和法規要求的類別。

非液化氣體 vs 液化氣體:

  • 非液化氣體（如 N₂、O₂、Ar）沸點極低（低於 -90°C），在常溫容器中維持氣態。
  • 液化氣體（如 CO₂、NH₃）沸點接近常溫，在加壓容器中以液態存在。

低溫液體 (Cryogenic Liquid): 非液化氣體在極低溫下液化的狀態，如液態氮（LN₂）、液態氧（LOX）。這是工廠大量用氣的主要採購形式。

比喻說明 Analogy

把壓縮氣體想成瓶裝水：液化氣體像礦泉水（液體裝在瓶裡，開瓶才揮發）；非液化氣體像泡打粉（固有氣態，高壓塞進鋼瓶）。CO₂ 更特別——它像乾冰，能同時以固、液、氣三態共存（三相點），打開時直接昇華。

重點提示 Key Notes

CO₂ 的三相點（-56.6°C, 416 kPa）在製藥超臨界流體萃取（Supercritical Fluid Extraction, SFE）中極為重要。CO₂ 超過臨界溫度（31°C）與臨界壓力（7.38 MPa）後，成為超臨界流體，兼具氣體的擴散性與液體的溶解力，廣泛用於天然物萃取純化。

3.2.2 Process Gas Sources and Manufacturing Considerations

Nitrogen, oxygen, and argon are naturally occurring elements. The earth's atmosphere is composed of approximately 78.09% nitrogen; 20.95% oxygen; and 0.93% argon by volume at sea level. Oxygen supports combustion. Nitrogen and argon are considered to be chemically inert.

Nitrogen, oxygen, and argon are most commonly manufactured by fractional distillation of liquefied air. A manufacturing facility that processes liquefied air is commonly referred to as an air-separation unit (ASU). Other manufacturing methods include electrolysis, molecular sieve separation, and chemical reactions.

The purity of the processed gases by the distillation method typically meets or exceeds minimum purities stated in the current published pharmacopoeial monographs, see Table 3.2.

*Nitrogen purity includes rare gases

Liquefied atmospheric gases may be delivered to users and cylinder packaging operations in specialized vacuum-insulated trailers. Cylinder packaging operations process the gases into returnable liquid containers and high pressure cylinders of various sizes.

Carbon dioxide occurs naturally in the atmosphere in small quantities, typically between 150 to 300 ppm. Carbon dioxide feedstock is pre-purified from a variety of sources, including chemical refining processes, ammonia production, ethyl alcohol production, fermentation processes, and coal-powered electrical plant combustion.

Carbon dioxide purity levels are typically greater than 99.5% and meet or exceed those required by the current pharmacopoeial monographs. Liquid carbon dioxide is normally delivered in specialized insulated trailers.

大氣分離廠 (Air-Separation Unit, ASU): 工業上最主要的製氮、製氧方式。將空氣液化後利用沸點差異（N₂: -195.8°C vs O₂: -183.0°C）分餾，可同時獲得高純度 N₂、O₂、Ar。

CO₂ 的特殊來源: CO₂ 多來自化學生產的副產品（如氨廠排放氣），因此其雜質輪廓（impurity profile）與大氣分離產品不同，採購時需特別審核 CoA。

注意表 3.2 的數據：典型低溫蒸餾所得氮氣純度為 99.998%，遠高於藥典要求的 99.0%（USP）或 99.5%（EP）。這意味著商業供應氣體通常已超規，但 CDMO 仍需要在 URS（用戶需求規格）中明確指定純度等級，並索取 CoA 或 CoC 作為每批放行的依據。

CO₂ 因來源多元（發酵、燃燒、化工），其微量雜質（如苯、HCN）尤其需要管控，特別是用於 pH 調控的生物製程中。

比喻說明 Analogy

大氣分離廠就像一座「空氣精煉廠」——把空氣這鍋混合湯，透過精準的溫度控制，把不同沸點的成分一層層分開倒出來，就像用蒸餾塔把酒精和水分開一樣。最純的 N₂ 從最冷的塔頂取出，O₂ 從稍暖的位置抽取，Ar 則夾在中間。

3.3 Process Gases: Typical Applications 典型應用

原文內容 Original Text ISPE

The biopharmaceutical and life science industries use compressed gases in a wide range of applications including:

  • pneumatic systems
  • chemical synthesis
  • controlled atmospheres and blanketing
  • cell cultivation
  • chilling/freezing
  • material extraction

Nitrogen is most commonly used as an inert gas for the conditioning of pharmaceutical products in storage and transportation. In specific applications, argon or carbon dioxide may be used instead of nitrogen. These gases (nitrogen, argon, and carbon dioxide) are also used during the manufacture of APIs and in the production of finished pharmaceutical products.

Gases such as oxygen, compressed air, and carbon dioxide are often used for cultivation of mammalian cells and for fermentation.

In fermentation and cell culturing processes, to improve productivity, cell growth intensity and viability, modern aeration concepts with enriched oxygen up to 100% in the inlet air may be applied. The technical implementation is usually done by mixing of gaseous oxygen and air from the usual pressure line by means of special devices, followed by sterilizing gas filtration in front of the bioreactor.

Filtration of oxygen is a significant safety concern: it leads to accelerated oxidation and corrosion of plastics as well as spontaneous combustion. Because thinner filter components such as non-woven support and drainage layers are much more vulnerable to oxidation, it is recommended to use filters that contain more oxidation resistant materials (e.g., polyaramide nonwoven layers) instead of the relatively fast degradable and flammable polypropylene non-wovens found in typical air sterilizing PTFE membrane filters.

Beside degradation effects, oxygen flow builds up static charges on filter media surfaces that can lead to spontaneous discharging with the risk of ignition of degraded organic traces and flammable filter components, followed by combustion and fire generation inside pipes and filter housings.

Blanketing with an inert gas, such as nitrogen, is commonly used in chemical processes, primarily to minimize the risk of fire, dust explosions, and other explosions. It is also used to prevent undesired reactions with atmospheric oxygen. Blanketing is a method for constantly maintaining a protective layer of gas on top of APIs or pharmaceutical solution. The inert gas replaces humid air or explosive vapor of solvents in the headspace of contained batches.

Nitrogen is also used as a blanket in bio-reactors for oxygen replacement for anaerobic fermentation processes. On Water for Injection (WFI) or Purified Water, nitrogen blanketing may be used to reduce changes in conductivity associated with dissolved carbon dioxide.

Oxygen is commonly used to enrich air and to enhance oxidation. In biological processes, it can also be used to increase the rate of the process, e.g., fermentation or in the cultivation of mammalian cells.

Carbon dioxide is used with incubators, in the production of biopharmaceuticals and in the cultivation of mammalian cells.

The storage of biological materials at very low temperatures is known as cryo-preservation. Cryo-preservation is used to ensure the survival of living cells and organisms. The cells can be stored either submerged in liquid nitrogen or in contact with the cold vapor phase of nitrogen.

Supercritical Fluid Extraction (SFE) refers to a process in which a Supercritical Fluid (SF) is used as a solvent. The most widely used SF gas is carbon dioxide (CO₂).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

氣體覆蓋 / 惰化 (Blanketing/Inerting): 在液面上方維持惰性氣體層，防止氧化、防爆、防潮。常見於 API 儲槽、WFI 儲罐頭空間管控。

吹掃 (Purging): 以惰性氣體置換管線或容器中的空氣，確保無殘留氧氣，常用於充填前的管路預處理。

冷凍保存 (Cryo-preservation): 細胞浸泡在液態氮（-196°C）中或接觸其冷蒸氣相，維持長期存活，是 CGT（細胞基因治療）產品的關鍵操作。

超臨界萃取 (SFE): CO₂ 超越臨界點後（31°C，73.8 bar）兼具氣液雙重特性，溶解力強且無有機溶劑殘留，是製藥萃取純化的綠色技術。

重點提示 Key Notes — 氧氣過濾的特殊風險

這是許多工程師忽略的安全盲點：當氧氣濃度提高到 100% 時，標準 PTFE + 聚丙烯（PP）非織布濾膜會面臨：

  • 加速氧化降解：PP 纖維在純氧環境快速脆化
  • 靜電積累：氣流摩擦產生靜電，可能引燃有機殘留物
  • 自燃風險：過濾器內部可能起火

因此，高濃度氧氣過濾必須使用含聚芳醯胺（polyaramide）非織布的特殊濾膜，並可能需要主管機關特殊批准。

比喻說明 Analogy

氮氣覆蓋（N₂ blanketing）就像在食物保存袋裡抽真空後充入惰性氣體——超市的「氣調包裝」正是這個原理，防止食物氧化變質。製藥廠的 API 儲槽也一樣，用 N₂ 取代頭空間空氣，防止藥物成分降解。

而細胞凍存在液態氮中，就像把生物「按下暫停鍵」——細胞在 -196°C 下代謝幾乎停止，解凍後仍可恢復活性，是 CAR-T、幹細胞等 CGT 產品的生命線。

實務應用 CDMO Application

針對不同操作選擇氣體的決策框架：

  • 無菌充填頭空間惰化 → N₂（最常見，成本低，惰性）
  • 高敏感性產品（如蛋白質）氧化防護 → Ar（比空氣重，覆蓋效果更好）
  • 哺乳動物細胞培養 pH 控制 → CO₂（與碳酸鹽緩衝液作用調節 pH）
  • 發酵充氧提升效率 → O₂（富氧曝氣，但須使用特殊耐氧濾膜）
  • 氣動設備動力（無需藥典等級） → 壓縮空氣（符合 ISO 8573 即可）

3.4 Applicable Regulations, Standards, and Codes 適用法規、標準及規範

原文內容 Original Text ISPE

Gas suppliers can describe the quality of gases as either "industrial" or "pharmaceutical." Gas should be purchased according to an agreed specification and delivered with a Certificate of Conformity (CoC) or a Certificate of Analysis (CoA), depending on the criticality of the gas.

Gas production normally follows Good Engineering Practices (GEPs). Gas is not a medicinal product and does not need to be produced following Good Manufacturing Practices (GMPs).

3.4.1 Process Aids

ICH Q7 "GMP Guide for Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients," Chapter 20 Glossary, defines process aids as materials, excluding solvents, used as an aid in the manufacture of an intermediate or API that do not themselves participate in a chemical or biological reaction (e.g., filter aid, activated carbon, etc).

ICH Q7, Chapter 7 (Materials Management) states:

"Materials should be purchased against an agreed specification, from a supplier or suppliers approved by the quality unit(s)."
"If the supplier of a critical material is not the manufacturer of that material, the name and address of that manufacturer should be known by the intermediate and/or API manufacturer."

When used in API production, inert process gases (e.g., nitrogen) would typically fall into the category of "process aid" rather than raw material. When used in production of finished pharmaceuticals, nitrogen would typically be defined as an inactive ingredient.

ICH Q7 (chapter 7.3) also suggests that at least one test to verify the identity of each batch of material should be conducted, with the exception of processing aids where a supplier's Certificate of Analysis can be used in place of performing other tests, provided that the manufacturer has a system in place to evaluate suppliers.

3.4.2 Components

As defined in US FDA Title 21 CFR Part 210.3, a component means any ingredient intended for use in the manufacture of a drug product, including those that may not appear in such drug product; and an inactive ingredient means any component other than an active ingredient.

Because of the special containers and characteristics, gases can be difficult to analyze on site, and the sec. 211.84 (d)(2) suggests that in lieu of testing for identity and conformity, a report of analysis may be accepted from the supplier of a component, provided that at least one specific identity test is conducted on such component by the manufacturer, and provided that the manufacturer establishes the reliability of the supplier's analyses through appropriate validation of the supplier's test results at appropriate intervals.

3.4.3 Gas Related Regulations

The FDA's quality system-based inspections have a risk-management approach and focus on operating systems. The Compliance Program Guidance Manual 7356.002 says that the materials system includes measures and activities to control finished products, components, including water or gases, that are incorporated into the product. Areas to be covered during inspection include:

  • identification and inventory of components
  • at least one specific identity test conducted on each lot of each component
  • testing or validation of the supplier's test results for components
  • water and process gas supply, design, maintenance, validation, and operation

The PIC/S Aide-Memoire — Inspection of Utilities (PI 009-3) provides guidance for GMP inspectors including useful checklists for HVAC, water, steam, and gas systems.

Other applicable regulations, standards, and codes include:

  • European Pharmacopoeia – Gas Monographs: Air (Medicinal), Synthetic Air (Medicinal), Argon, Carbon Dioxide, Nitrogen, Nitrogen (Low Oxygen), Oxygen
  • USP/NF – Gas Monographs: Medical Air, Carbon Dioxide, Nitrogen, Oxygen
  • EIGA – Comparison of European, US & Japanese Pharmacopoeia Monographs for Medicinal Gases (Document 152/08/E)
  • ASME BPE-2009 – Bioprocessing Equipment
  • Health Canada – Guide-0031: GMP for Medical Gases
  • NFPA 50 – Bulk Oxygen System at Consumer Sites
  • NFPA 55 – Storage, Use, and Handling of Compressed Gases and Cryogenic Fluids
  • Compressed Gas Association (CGA) Standards: G-4 (Oxygen), G-6 (CO₂), G-10.1 (N₂), G-11.1 (Ar), P-1, P-2, P-12 (Cryogenic Liquids), P-18 (Bulk Inert Gas)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

CoC vs CoA 的差異：

  • CoA（分析報告）：包含實際測試數據（如純度、露點、含油量），適用於高關鍵性氣體（直接接觸產品）。
  • CoC（符合性聲明）：供應商聲明符合約定規格，未必包含實測數據，適用於低關鍵性氣體（如氣動設備用空氣）。

製程助劑 vs 非活性成分 vs 成分：

  • N₂ 用於 API 製造中的惰化 → ICH Q7 定義為製程助劑
  • N₂ 用於最終製劑頭空間 → 21 CFR 定義為成分/非活性成分
  • 法律分類不同，GMP 控制要求也不同！

重點提示 Key Notes — 稽查重點

FDA 稽查官依據 Compliance Program 7356.002 檢查氣體系統時，會特別關注：

  • 是否每批氣體都有至少一項鑑別試驗（identity test）
  • 供應商的 CoA 可靠性是否定期驗證（supplier qualification）
  • 氣體分配系統是否有設計文件、維護記錄、確效文件
  • 若使用 CoA 代替自行測試，是否有完整的供應商評估系統

這些都應體現在 CDMO 的供應商管理程序（SOP）和成分控制程序中。

比喻說明 Analogy

氣體的法規分類就像餐廳食材的管理：製程助劑（N₂ 在 API 製造中）像廚房用的清潔劑——不進食物，但需要有合格認證；而成分/非活性成分（N₂ 在充填頭空間）像包裝食物的保護氣——直接進入最終產品，必須符合食品級規格，並附帶詳細分析報告。

法規層次架構 Regulatory Hierarchy

氣體用於 API 製造（ICH Q7 管轄）
  ├── 製程助劑：不參與反應
  │   → CoA 可代替自行測試
  │   → 但需有供應商評估系統
  └── 需有規格說明 + QU 核准供應商

氣體用於成品藥製造（21 CFR Part 211 管轄）
  ├── 成分 / 非活性成分
  │   → 需至少一項鑑別試驗
  │   → 可接受供應商 CoA
  │   → 但需定期驗證 CoA 可靠性
  └── 分配系統需設計、維護、確效

3.5 Safe Handling and Storage of Gases 氣體安全操作與儲存

原文內容 Original Text ISPE

3.5.1 Source Gas Distribution and Storage

Argon, nitrogen, and oxygen are typically supplied to the customer by one or more of the following means:

  • on-site liquid storage vessel
  • transportable liquid containers
  • high-pressure cylinders and manifolds
  • gas pipeline
  • gas generation systems

Storage Vessels
Storage vessels for liquid argon, nitrogen, and oxygen are commercially available in capacities ranging from 350 to 13,000 US gallons (1,325 to 49,210 liters). The storage vessels may be vertical, spherical, or horizontal. Liquid storage vessels have three basic components:

  1. inner pressure vessel
  2. outer vessel
  3. insulation

Inner Pressure Vessel
Usually made of stainless steel or other materials with favorable strength at cryogenic temperatures. Typically designed to sustain a maximum allowable working pressure of 250 psig (1,724 kPa). Equipped with dual pressure relief valves and rupture disks to protect against over-pressurization.

Rupture disks are thin metal membranes designed to rupture at a prescribed pressure above the pressure relief valve setting. Unlike pressure relief valves, rupture disks, once activated, do not close — the pressure and entire contents of the tank may be released.

Outer Vessel
Made of carbon steel or stainless steel. Retains the insulation and a vacuum around the inner pressure vessel. Equipped with a vacuum rupture disk to protect the outer vessel from pressurized leaks into the insulation space.

Insulation
The space between the inner and outer vessel contains several inches of insulating material maintained in a vacuum to reduce heat transfer and reduce the boil-off of stored liquid.

Vaporizing Systems
Most user applications use gases in their gaseous state. The stored liquid is converted to gas through the use of a vaporizing system, which may utilize heat from ambient air, hot water, or steam.

Frost or ice will frequently build up on the fins of the ambient air vaporizer system during gas use. Typically, icing should not exceed three-quarters of the vaporizer's length. Excessive ice build-up can reduce the vaporizer efficiency.

Carbon Dioxide Storage Tank Installations
Carbon dioxide is normally maintained in its liquid phase by an integral refrigeration system and pressure control system. Bulk carbon dioxide storage tanks are available in capacities up to approximately 50 tons.

Typically, gas suppliers are responsible for the bulk gas storage tanks and related controls. Customer responsibility normally begins after the vaporizing system and at the outlet of the gas supply valve or regulator.

3.5.2 Transportable Liquid Containers

Small quantities of liquid gases may be supplied in portable or transportable containers. Liquid containers are usually double-walled and vacuum insulated, and normally set to a specified gas service pressure with safety relief devices.

General rules for handling and storing liquid containers include:

  • Rough handling or subjecting containers to shocks, drops, or impact should be avoided.
  • Containers should be moved using carts and equipment specifically designed for the purpose.
  • Containers should be kept vertical and upright.
  • Containers should be stored in a well-ventilated area, preferably out-of-doors.
  • Regulators, hoses, and equipment should have been designed to withstand the gas service pressures.
  • Containers and connections should be properly labeled and tagged.
  • Gas/liquid supply valves should be opened slowly.
  • During periods of heavy gas use, the outer surface of the container may frost. Reduce withdrawal rate; do not apply heat to the container.
  • Connections should not be fitted forcibly.
  • Adaptors should not be used to circumvent gas connection standards.
  • Containers should not be lifted by protection rings or top-works.

3.5.3 Transportable Gas Cylinders

Small quantities of compressed gas or gas mixtures may be supplied in high pressure cylinders in a variety of sizes up to 330 ft³ (10 m³). Cylinders may be of steel or aluminum construction and should be equipped with a valve, gas-specific outlet connection, and a pressure relief device. Once activated, pressure relief devices do not reset and the cylinder contents will be lost.

High pressure cylinder handling and storage recommendations include:

  • Reference should be made to Material Safety Data Sheets (MSDSs) for important gas product safety information.
  • Cylinders should be moved using handcarts and equipment specifically designed for the purpose.
  • Cylinders should be protected from being knocked or damaged.
  • Cylinders should be secured in place when in use.
  • Cylinder caps should be kept in place when not in use.
  • "Full" cylinders should be segregated from "empty" cylinders.
  • Cylinders should be segregated by hazard class.
  • Flow/pressure regulating devices should be used to withdraw cylinder contents.
  • Cylinder valves should be closed tightly when a cylinder is not in use.
  • Cylinder markings, labels, and tags should be kept complete and unmodified.
  • Cylinders should not be lifted by valves or protection caps.
  • Cylinders should not be used as rollers for moving equipment.
  • Cylinders should not be stored near heaters, open flames, or sparks.
  • Cylinders should not be placed where they may become part of an electric circuit.
  • When welding, an arc should not be struck against a cylinder.

3.5.4 Gas Safety Considerations

Oxygen-Deficient Atmospheres
The primary hazard of the inert gases argon, nitrogen, and carbon dioxide is their ability to displace oxygen in the air and create an oxygen-deficient environment. An oxygen-deficient atmosphere is defined as oxygen concentration in air of less than 19.5%.

An oxygen-deficient atmosphere is a serious physiological hazard. Depending on the degree of deficiency, effects on personnel can vary from physiological changes to illness or death by asphyxiation. Exposure can also cause sudden unconsciousness, preventing personnel from helping or protecting themselves.

Where the potential exists for an oxygen-deficient atmosphere, oxygen-level monitors should be installed to alert personnel before entering the controlled area.

Compression fittings and connections in gas distribution systems should be minimized to reduce potential sources of gas leaks. Pressure relief devices should be piped outside a facility to a point of safe discharge.

Oxygen-Enriched Atmospheres
An oxygen-enriched atmosphere is defined as oxygen concentration in air greater than 23.5%. An enriched oxygen atmosphere can cause materials that will burn in air to do so more vigorously. Hydrocarbons and oils may react violently and explosively when in contact with oxygen or oxygen-enriched atmospheres.

Liquid nitrogen and liquid argon storage vessels, and liquid piping systems, should have appropriate insulation. Without appropriate insulation, ambient air may condense on extremely cold surfaces forming liquid air. Liquid air can contain up to 50% oxygen; evaporation can increase the oxygen concentration to over 80%.

Cryogenic Liquid and Cold Temperature Hazards
The safe use of liquid argon, nitrogen, and oxygen requires knowledge of how materials change their behavior at cryogenic temperatures. Three common strength characteristics altered by low temperatures are:

  1. Ductility: the ability of a material to permanently change its shape or stretch without fracturing. Carbon steel loses ductility at low temperatures.
  2. Tensile Strength: the resistance of a material to tearing. The tensile strength of many metals increases at low temperatures.
  3. Impact Strength: the ability to resist impact without shattering. Rubber and plastic are reduced so much at low temperatures that they can shatter like glass.

Some stainless steels, copper, nickel alloys, and aluminum do not lose significant strength at cryogenic temperatures. Materials used in gaseous service may not be suitable for handling or storing the liquid phase.

Training and Safety Programs
General safety recommendations for handling liquid gas products include:

  • Liquid nitrogen and liquid argon should be handled only by personnel instructed in the properties of the material and in the proper procedures for handling it.
  • Piping in which liquid nitrogen or liquid argon could be trapped between two valves should be equipped with pressure relief valves piped to properly designed vents.
  • Liquid nitrogen or liquid argon should be stored in open and well-ventilated areas.

重點提示 Key Notes — 儲槽安全設計

液態低溫儲槽的雙重防護設計至關重要：

  • 壓力洩放閥（Pressure Relief Valve）：超壓時自動開啟，降壓後自動關閉，可重複使用。
  • 爆破片（Rupture Disk）：在更高壓力時動作，作為最後防線，但一旦破裂，儲槽內容物全部洩出，無法重置。

這兩者的差異在 FAT/SAT 確效中必須驗證，且爆破片的放空管必須導至戶外安全位置，不得在室內洩壓。

重點提示 Key Notes — 缺氧環境的致命性

N₂、Ar、CO₂ 都是無色無味的惰性氣體——人體無法感知其濃度！當環境 O₂ 濃度：

  • 19.5% 以下：法規定義缺氧，需警報
  • 16%：頭暈、判斷力下降
  • 12%：失去意識
  • 6% 以下：幾分鐘內死亡

特別危險之處：人可能在毫無預警的情況下突然昏倒，無法自救。封閉空間作業（confined space entry）的氧氣監控是 GMP 設施的安全底線。

比喻說明 Analogy

低溫儲槽的三層結構（內膽 + 真空層 + 外殼）就像一個超大型「不鏽鋼保溫杯」——真空隔熱層防止熱傳導，讓 -196°C 的液態氮能在常溫環境中長期保存。但如果真空層破壞（outer vessel leak），外面的暖空氣會迅速傳熱，造成液體快速蒸發（boil-off），壓力急升，就是爆破片的設計用途。

而 LOX 外洩後接觸冷管路，空氣中的水氣凝結成「液態空氣」，其中 O₂ 濃度可達 50-80%——相當於在管路周圍創造了一個富氧引爆環境，極度危險。

  • 用量：日耗量 < 50 Nm³ → 鋼瓶；50-500 Nm³ → 液態容器；> 500 Nm³ → 液態儲槽或現場製氣

  • 供應連續性：生產線 24/7 運轉 → 液態儲槽 + 自動補貨監控系統

3.6.1 Gas Specifications

Source gas specifications should be user-defined. Where a gas is used as an excipient, process aid, or as a component in drug manufacturing process, users should evaluate the potential impact the gas might have on the finished product. To evaluate the impact, a risk assessment is recommended.

Mitigating risk may be accomplished through one or more of the following actions:

  • source gas specifications
  • detailed work instructions
  • training
  • system engineering controls
  • equipment maintenance programs
  • change-control procedures

There are financial benefits gained from using one of the "standard" specifications from a supplier — the requirements should be evaluated against these.

Source Gas Purity
Fractional distillation and other source gas manufacturing processes produce high purity liquefied gas products. End-users are encouraged to consider the following in developing their gas product specifications.

Purity classification is usually expressed as a quality code (e.g., 4.8). In the quality code notation, the number before the point represents the number of nines and the last number indicates the last decimal (i.e., 4.8 = 99.998%, and 6.0 = 99.9999%).

There are two methods for determining the purity of a gas:

  1. direct measurement using specifically designed analytical instruments
  2. measuring minor components of interest and subtracting from 100%

Both the measurement uncertainty characteristics of instruments and the analytical method used can affect the accuracy of purity measurements. Users should identify minor components of concern in a source gas specification. Minor components of concern should be determined by the specific gas application and relevant CQAs (identified through a process risk assessment).

*Nitrogen purity includes rare gases

Water Content in Source Gases
Water content in the source gases is usually very low and inherent to the source gas manufacturing processes.

Purging of gas delivery hoses during product transfer at user sites should maintain low-level moisture specifications. New or re-commissioned gas distribution systems should be thoroughly dried and their installation qualified. Prior to utilizing the gas from such systems, users should verify acceptable moisture content levels.

Gas distribution systems should be maintained leak-free and under positive gas pressure to prevent contamination by atmospheric air.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

露點（Dew Point）的意義： 露點是氣體中水蒸氣開始凝結的溫度。露點越低，氣體越乾燥。-65°C 露點代表極低含水量，確保氣體不會在管路或產品接觸面帶入水分。

URS 中氣體規格的關鍵參數（依 ISO 8573）：

  • 固體顆粒：按粒徑分 Class 1-9
  • 水分（露點）：Class 1（-70°C）到 Class 9（+10°C）
  • 含油量：Class 0（<0.01 mg/m³）到 Class 4（>5 mg/m³）

微量關注成分（Minor Components of Concern）： 不同製程的關注雜質不同：CO 影響細胞活力；THC 影響產品安全性；H₂O 影響穩定性；O₂（在 N₂ 中）影響氧化敏感品。

URS 氣體規格撰寫框架

製程氣體 URS 規格項目（以無菌充填用 N₂ 為例）：

1. 純度（Purity）：≥ 99.998%（Grade 4.8）
2. 露點（Dew Point）：≤ -65°C（at delivery point）
3. 含油量（Oil Content）：≤ 0.01 mg/m³（ISO 8573 Class 0）
4. 顆粒（Particulates）：ISO 8573 Class 1
   （≥ 0.1 μm → ≤ 20,000 /m³）
5. O₂ 殘留（O₂ Residual）：≤ 10 ppm（對氧化敏感品）
6. CO 殘留：≤ 1 ppm
7. 微生物：≤ 1 CFU/m³（可選，依接觸風險）

文件要求：
- 每批 CoA（含實測值）
- 供應商定期稽核紀錄
- 分配系統確效文件（IQ/OQ/PQ）

重點提示 Key Notes — 風險評估驅動規格

氣體規格不應「一刀切」——必須依風險評估結果客製化。考量因素：

  • 接觸程度：直接接觸產品 vs 間接接觸 vs 不接觸（公用系統）
  • 產品 CQA：氧化敏感性、顆粒污染風險、微生物污染風險
  • 操作類型：惰化頭空間（允許微量 O₂）vs 吹掃 vs 氣動傳動（不接觸）
  • 法規要求：USP/EP 藥典規格是最低門檻，CQA 導向的規格可能更嚴

ISPE 明確建議：採用供應商「標準規格」（standard specifications）可降低成本，但必須評估其是否滿足使用者的 CQA 需求。

比喻說明 Analogy

訂定氣體規格就像為嬰兒配方奶選擇水源——普通自來水（工業等級氣體）可以洗車，礦泉水（藥典等級）可以喝，但嬰兒配方需要的是通過特定純度規格的無菌水（URS 客製化規格）。過度規格是浪費成本，規格不足是品質風險，平衡點由風險評估決定。

氣體分配系統保持正壓就像血管系統——血液（氣體）流動時保持正壓，確保外界汙染物（空氣）不能逆滲入，一旦壓力消失（管路洩漏），汙染就可能侵入。
