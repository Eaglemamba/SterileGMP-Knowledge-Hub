2.1 Regulatory Guidance / 法規指引

Original Text (ISPE Vol. 3)

2.1.1 Global Regulations and Guidance

Regulatory expectations for sterile drug products are defined throughout global regulations and guidance documents, including:

  • US FDA issued Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing – Current Good Manufacturing Practice, which provides the FDA current thinking and is in addition to the Code of Federal Regulations (CFR).
  • EU Annex 1 Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products.
  • PIC/S, WHO and other regulatory bodies defined sterile product GMPs, which are mirrored after Annex 1.

In addition to regulations specific to sterile products, regions may also provide specific guidelines for "specialty" drug products, such as hormones, sensitizing compounds, biological preparations (including live viruses and spore formers), radiopharmaceuticals, and highly hazardous products.

The design concepts and philosophies presented in this Guide are intended to comply with the GMPs of most of the major global markets, including those regulated by the US FDA, EMA, WHO, and countries following PIC/S.

At the start of a project, all markets where the resulting manufactured products could be sold should be defined. The regulations for each region should be researched and understood, as there are both subtle and substantial differences between global market regulations.

Revisions to GMPs are made on a reoccurring basis. Regulated companies should investigate and understand all proposed changes and published standards by the governing bodies. Consideration should also be given to the evolving expectations of inspectors.

2.1.2 Current Guidance and Practices

Regulated companies should ensure that they are following the most recently published regulations and guidance documents. Regulatory guidance may follow and incorporate best practices that have already been adopted by industry.

Since the first edition of this ISPE Baseline Guide was issued in January 1999, there have been several new and revised regulatory guidance documents. Some examples of changes include:

  • Cleanroom design related to barrier technology and BFS / downgrading the background environment requirements
  • Gowning and movement in the aseptic processing area
  • Optimizing manual interventions with proper operator techniques and visualized airflow pattern analysis
  • Airflow velocity measurements (see Chapter 5)
  • Establishment and monitoring of differential pressures (DP) between areas of different classifications
  • Maintaining partially stoppered vials under ISO 5/Grade A conditions at all times until stopper is fully inserted and crimped
教學解說 / 實務觀點

法規架構總覽

核心概念：全球無菌製造法規三大支柱

無菌製造設施的設計必須同時滿足多個市場的法規要求，三大核心文件為：

  • 歐盟GMP附件一（EU GMP Annex 1）：全球最嚴格的無菌製造法規，2022年修訂版大幅強化污染控制策略（CCS）要求
  • FDA無菌製程指引（FDA Aseptic Guidance）：美國市場的核心要求，針對無菌製程提供具體操作指南
  • PIC/S：多數亞太國家（如台灣、新加坡、日本）採 PIC/S 體系，內容與歐盟 Annex 1 高度對齊

重點提示：法規區域差異

各國法規之間存在「微妙與重大」的差異。例如，美國 FDA 稽查員可能會期望看到歐盟 Annex 1 的要求在美國工廠中被採用。這種「交叉期望」趨勢在全球化稽查中越來越常見。

比喻：「最嚴標準」原則

想像不同國家的法規就像不同的考試：美國考數學、歐盟考物理、PIC/S考化學。作為全球供應商，你不能只準備一科——必須每科都達到最高標準。

法規演變與最佳實務

重點變化：從1999年至今的關鍵演進

ISPE指南列出的變化反映了行業趨勢：

  • 屏障技術普及：從傳統潔淨室轉向 RABS/隔離器
  • 人員管理強化：更嚴格的穿衣和移動管控
  • 氣流視覺化：強調煙霧測試與氣流模式分析
  • 半塞瓶管控：部分塞塞的瓶子必須始終保持在 ISO 5/Grade A 條件下

CDMO 實務應用

作為 CDMO，我們服務多個客戶，產品可能銷售到不同市場。因此：

  • 設施設計應採用「最嚴標準」原則，同時滿足 FDA + EU Annex 1 + PIC/S
  • 建立法規情報追蹤機制，定期監控各市場法規變化
  • 客戶引進時，首先確認目標市場，據此決定設施需求

2.2 Product Requirements / 產品需求

Original Text (ISPE Vol. 3)

2.2.1 Product Profile/Characteristics

Sterile products require rigorous control of potential contamination which may take the form of particulates, microorganisms, and endotoxins. There are two primary methods for producing sterile products:

  • Terminal sterilization
  • Aseptic processing

Generally, regulatory agencies have stated that, where possible (i.e., product is not affected or compromised by the terminal sterilization process), parenteral products should be terminally sterilized. Products should be developed from the outset to be terminally sterilized. Where this is not feasible without damaging the product, alternative processes, such as aseptic processing, can be employed.

The choice between the two processing routes has significant influence on:

  • Facility layout including process/production flow
  • Level of environmental classification
  • HVAC design
  • Subsequent Environmental Monitoring (EM)

In addition to the aseptic processing methodology, the equipment technology selected for contamination control (isolator, RABS, or BFS) or the use of conventional cleanroom technology also has a significant impact on planning, facility layout, equipment arrangements, area classifications, and HVAC system design.

Several key aspects of the products themselves need to be considered at an early stage, including whether the product:

  • Is a liquid, a suspension, an emulsion, a powder, semi-solid, or freeze-dried
  • Supports microbiological growth
  • Is potent, toxic, and/or radioactive
  • Is adversely impacted by exposure to light, oxygen, temperature, humidity/moisture
  • Is volatile
  • Is biologically active (e.g., live vaccines)

Additional product and process aspects influencing facility design include:

  • Presentation (vial, ampoule, syringes, bags, etc.)
  • Scale or capacity required
  • Cross-contamination potential in multi-product/multi-purpose facilities
  • Use of ready-to-use and single-use components and technology

2.2.2 Process Definition

The product(s) final form can influence processing conditions, equipment selection, and facility design. The facility layout is affected by:

  • Size of the product presentation
  • Capacity and throughput required
  • Number and variety of presentations to be processed
  • Potential flexibility to add or adjust presentations and products in the future

2.2.2.1 Product Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs)

CQAs are those physical, chemical, biological, or microbiological properties or characteristics that should be within an appropriate limit, range, or distribution to ensure the desired product quality. These can include:

  • Purity, Composition, Identity
  • Biological activity, Potency
  • Sterility and endotoxin limits
  • Particulates

2.2.2.2 Critical Process Parameters (CPPs)

CPPs include input settings/controls or output readings from process equipment or monitoring devices. These can include:

  • Temperature and the associated rate of change
  • Time, Flow rate and velocities
  • Pressure, pH, Conductivity

2.2.2.3 Critical Process Steps

Critical process steps should be defined for all product types. For aseptically manufactured products, the most critical steps are those during product transfers and when sterilized product and container/closure are exposed to the atmosphere or a surface. Examples include:

  • Sampling, weighing, and dispensing of materials
  • Preparation, sterilization, and depyrogenation of containers and closures
  • Aseptic connections and transfers (under ISO 5/Grade A)
  • Formulation and sterile filtration
  • Filling and primary sealing
  • Transfers to lyophilizers, loading/unloading, and final capping
  • Storage and transfer of sterilized equipment and components
  • Cleaning and sterilization of product contact equipment
教學解說 / 實務觀點

終端滅菌 vs. 無菌製程

比喻：餐廳食品安全

終端滅菌就像在廚房完成所有備料後，把整道菜放進烘箱高溫殺菌。即使備料過程不夠完美，最後的高溫處理可以補救。

無菌製程則像做壽司——每一個步驟都必須在極度清潔的環境下完成，沒有最後的「高溫補救」機會。

產品特性對設施的影響

重點：六大產品特性決定設施需求

設計階段必須提前考慮的產品特性：

  • 劑型：液體、懸浮液、粉末、凍乾等，直接影響設備選擇與空間配置
  • 微生物生長支持性：影響持有時間與環境控制要求
  • 毒性/放射性：需要專用設施或額外防護措施
  • 環境敏感度：光、氧、溫度、濕度敏感則需特殊設計
  • 揮發性：需要密閉系統與排氣控制
  • 生物活性：活病毒疫苗需專用設施

CQA、CPP 與關鍵製程步驟

核心概念：CQA 與 CPP 的關係

CQA（關鍵品質屬性）是產品必須滿足的品質標準（如無菌、微粒數、內毒素）。

CPP（關鍵製程參數）是影響 CQA 的製程條件（如溫度、時間、壓力）。

設施設計的目的就是確保能夠可靠地控制 CPP，進而達到 CQA 的要求。

ICH Q8/Q10 邏輯鏈

產品品質 = f(CQA)
CQA = f(CPP)
CPP = f(設施設計 + 製程控制)

∴ 設施設計 → CPP 控制 → CQA 達標 → 產品品質

重點：無菌製程八大關鍵步驟

以下每個步驟都可能引入污染，必須在設計階段就納入風險評估：

  1. 稱量與配料
  2. 容器/蓋子滅菌與除熱原
  3. 無菌連接與轉移
  4. 配方與無菌過濾
  5. 填裝與初級密封
  6. 凍乾機轉移與最終加蓋
  7. 滅菌設備儲存與轉移
  8. 清潔與滅菌

CDMO 實務應用

對於 CDMO 的多產品設施，產品需求分析特別重要：

  • 需要考慮「最惡情境產品」來設計設施（例如：最毒的、最敏感的、最難清潔的）
  • 凍乾產品需預留凍乾機空間，影響整體廠房布局
  • 單一使用技術（Single-Use）可大幅減少換線清潔驗證負擔
  • 未來彈性：預留增加新劑型或包裝的空間

2.3 Risk Assessment / 風險評估

Original Text (ISPE Vol. 3)

2.3 Risk Assessment

The US FDA GMP for the 21st Century initiative and global GMPs, such as ICH Q9, along with science and risk-based approaches, are fundamental to ensuring that products of the highest possible quality are manufactured. Sterile product manufacturing is recognized as requiring special steps to mitigate risk to product (particularly by bio-contamination); therefore, adequate controls should be established to minimize particulate and microbial ingress.

Risk assessments, using tools such as Failure Mode, Effects, and Criticality Analysis (FMECA), Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP), or other methods, are encouraged to ensure that product manufacturing risks are systematically understood, assessed, and controlled.

Reference should be made to Annex II within ICH Q9, which gives specific examples of quality risk management. ICH Q9 Annex II.4 covers risk management for Facilities, Equipment, and Utilities under the following headings:

  • Design of facility/equipment
  • Hygiene aspects in facilities
  • Qualification of facility/equipment/utilities
  • Cleaning of equipment and environmental control
  • Calibration/preventative maintenance
  • Computer systems and computer-controlled equipment

Risk assessments should be led by subject matter experts or a diversified team who understand the process and product critical aspects. In aseptic processing, risk assessments are performed for individual processes or production lines to identify hazards to product quality.

Risk is defined as the combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm. Risk, when expressed in terms of Risk Priority Numbers (RPN), is a function of Severity, Probability of Occurrence, and Detectability. From this determination, mitigation and control options are developed to minimize risk to the product. Such options could involve engineering solutions, procedural solutions, or a combination of both.

教學解說 / 實務觀點

風險評估在設施設計中的角色

核心概念：ICH Q9 風險管理架構

ICH Q9 提供了系統性的風險管理方法，應用於製藥設施設計時包含六大領域：

  • 設施/設備設計：如潔淨室分區、氣流方向
  • 衛生面向：如表面材質、清潔可及性
  • 確認活動：IQ/OQ/PQ 的測試範圍
  • 清潔與環境控制：清潔驗證與環境監控
  • 校準/預防維護：關鍵儀器的校準頻率
  • 電腦化系統：SCADA、BMS 等控制系統

RPN 風險優先數 計算公式

RPN = 嚴重度 (S) x 發生機率 (O) x 可偵測性 (D)

其中：
  S (Severity)     = 1–10（危害嚴重程度）
  O (Occurrence)   = 1–10（發生機率）
  D (Detectability)= 1–10（不可偵測程度，10最難發現）

RPN 越高，需要優先處理。
超過門檻值的 RPN 必須制定緩解措施。

比喻：風險評估就像買保險

想像你在評估一棟房子的風險：

  • 嚴重度：如果房子着火，損失多大？
  • 發生機率：這個地區多常發生火災？
  • 可偵測性：有沒有煙霧警報器能及早發現？

製藥設施的風險評估邀輯完全相同——評估每個風險點，然後決定需要多少「保險」（工程或程序控制）。

重點：工程解決方案優先於程序解決方案

ISPE 強調：應優先考慮「工程解決方案」（Engineered Solutions）而非依賴「程序解決方案」（Procedural Solutions）。

例如：

  • 工程解決：安裝隔離器阻隔人員與產品接觸
  • 程序解決：SOP 要求人員穿戴無菌手套

工程解決更可靠，因為不依賴人員執行的一致性。

練習思考

在一條新的無菌填裝線設計中，如果你發現「人員在填裝區的介入頻率」的 RPN 超過門檻值，你會建議哪種緩解措施？

  • A. 加強人員無菌技術培訓（程序解決）
  • B. 導入 RABS 或隔離器減少介入（工程解決）
  • C. 增加環境監控頻率（可偵測性改善）

最佳策略通常是 B 優先，輔以 A 和 C。

2.4 Risk Mitigation / "Quality by Design" / 風險緩解與「品質源於設計」

Original Text (ISPE Vol. 3)

2.4 Risk Mitigation / "Quality by Design"

Pharmaceutical production facilities should be designed to promote and encourage good manufacturing processes. For such a design to occur, the risks associated with producing a quality product should be identified so they can be mitigated either by engineered solutions or by procedural solutions.

Engineered solutions should be considered before relying on procedures, wherever possible.

A "Quality by Design" process focuses on the operational or "future state" of the facility, and seeks to identify and mitigate risks that may occur during production. Solutions are defined and incorporated into the design and verified in the installed asset to ensure the facility produces quality products based on understanding the production requirements.

教學解說 / 實務觀點

QbD 在設施設計中的應用

核心概念：Quality by Design（品質源於設計）

QbD 的核心理念是：品質不是檢驗出來的，而是設計進去的。應用於設施設計時：

  • 理解生產需求：先明確要生產什麼、怎麼生產
  • 辨識風險：哪些生產步驟可能影響品質
  • 納入設計：將緩解措施融入設施設計
  • 驗證確認：通過確認活動驗證設計意圖已實現

比喻：蓋房子 vs. 消防安全

想像你在蓋一棟大樓：

  • 傳統做法：先蓋好，然後檢查是否符合消防法規，不符合就改
  • QbD 做法：從設計階段就把消防通道、灑水系統、防火材料融入建築設計

QbD 在製藥設施中就是後者——從一開始就把品質控制設計進設施中。

CDMO 實務應用

CDMO 設施的 QbD 特別挑戰：

  • 必須同時滿足多個客戶的品質需求，設計應基於最嚴格的需求
  • 彈性設計很重要：今天生產蛋白質藥物，明天可能是細胞治療
  • 設施生命週期內的法規變化需納入考量（如 Annex 1 修訂）
  • 設計階段的投資可以避免生產階段的巨額失敗成本

2.5 Contamination Control Approaches / 污染控制方法

Original Text (ISPE Vol. 3)

2.5 Contamination Control Approaches

The prevention of product contamination is the single largest objective in sterile product facilities. Eliminating product contamination is paramount, as every contaminated container has the potential to severely harm a patient.

Contamination is defined as the undesired introduction of impurities of a chemical or microbiological nature, or of foreign matter, into a raw material, intermediate, API, or product during production, sampling, packaging or repackaging, storage or transport.

Contamination can occur in two ways:

  • Environmental contamination results from the ingress of contaminants (microbiological, particulate, or foreign matter) from the surrounding production areas or even from outside environments.
  • Cross-contamination is contamination of a starting material, intermediate product, or finished product with another starting material or product during production. Cross-contamination results when APIs from one product find their way into another product.

2.5.1 Sources of Contamination

Particulate/foreign matter and bio-contamination usually occur in one of two ways:

  • Through mechanical transfer (e.g., via personnel, materials, or equipment)
  • Via airborne contaminants

Sources of cross-contamination include four primary routes: Mix-Up, Retention, Mechanical Transfer, and Airborne Transfer.

2.5.2 Contamination Control

Once sources of contamination have been identified, controls or procedures can be established. Most contamination can be controlled through measures such as:

  • Nested zones of protection including barrier technology around the most critical areas
  • Selecting closed processes and associated control systems
  • Removing sources of contamination
  • Use of pre-sterilized closed container systems and single-use aseptic connectors
  • Proper control of personnel, material, equipment and waste flow
  • Design and implementation of effective cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization procedures
  • Personnel gowning and hygiene
  • Employee training and control of the process environment

"Nested manufacturing zones" is the concept where critical aseptic zones are at the heart of the facility and are surrounded by lesser zones according to criticality and cleanliness. As materials and personnel pass from less critical to higher criticality areas, an improvement step occurs.

2.5.3 Approaches to Environmental Contamination Control

This Guide recognizes three approaches in increasing levels of separation effectiveness:

  1. Conventional cleanroom technology (batch operations and substantially open processing lines most vulnerable to contamination)
  2. RABS (Restricted Access Barrier Systems)
  3. Isolator technology

This edition recommends the use of barrier technology (isolators and RABS) for aseptic processing in new or renovated facilities. Barrier technology provides engineered separation and control rather than relying on the aseptic technique of an individual.

Effective environmental control requires:

  • Control of air filtration
  • Determination of airflow patterns
  • Control of temperature and humidity
  • Control of internally generated contamination by dilution or displacement
  • Segregation of zones by physical separation, pressurization, and/or airflow direction control

2.5.4 Cross-Contamination

Avoidance of cross-contamination to prevent carryover of one product into another must be considered. Under GMP, manufacturers may set cross-contamination limits on a substance-by-substance basis. In some forms, several products may pose a significant risk if they contaminate other products — for these products, separate production facilities, air handling equipment, and process equipment may be necessary.

教學解說 / 實務觀點

污染控制：無菌設施的核心任務

核心概念：兩種污染類型

  • 環境污染（Environmental Contamination）：來自周圍環境的微粒、微生物或異物入侵
  • 交叉污染（Cross-Contamination）：一個產品的成分混入另一個產品

每一個受污染的容器都可能嚴重傷害患者——這是無菌製造的最基本信念。

巢狀製造區概念

核心概念：Nested Manufacturing Zones（巢狀製造區）

無菌設施的空間配置遵循「由外到內、逐層淨化」的原則：

外部環境 / Unclassified
▼
Grade D / ISO 8（支援區）
▼
Grade C / ISO 7（準備區）
▼
Grade B / ISO 5（背景區）
▼
Grade A / ISO 5（填裝區 — 核心）

每一層轉換都需要「改善步驟」：物料清潔、人員更衣、氣壓階梯等。

三大污染控制技術

比喻：手術室的無菌層級

想像三種手術室設置：

  • 傳統潔淨室：醫生在潔淨房間裡直接操作，全靠手套和無菌技術
  • RABS：醫生透過一層透明屏障操作，可透過手套孔介入
  • 隔離器：醫生透過完全密封的手套箱操作，與產品完全隔離

隔離等級越高，對人員無菌技術的依賴越低。

重點：有效環境控制的五大要素

  1. 空氣過濾控制：HEPA 過濾器，確保空氣潔淨度
  2. 氣流模式確定：單向氣流（UAF）在關鍵區域提供保護
  3. 溫濕度控制：影響微生物生長和產品穩定性
  4. 內部污染稀釋/排除：通過換氣次數控制
  5. 區域隔離：物理分隔、壓差控制、氣流方向控制

CDMO 實務應用：多產品設施的污染控制

對於 CDMO 的多產品設施，污染控制是最大挑戰：

  • 環境污染：推薦採用隔離器或 RABS，降低對人員無菌技術的依賴
  • 交叉污染：應建立基於風險的清潔驗證策略，特別是高毒性/高效產品
  • 專用設施：對於細胞治療、活病毒疫苗等高風險產品，可能需要專用生產區域
  • 單一使用技術：大幅減少交叉污染風險，特別適合多產品切換

練習思考

你的 CDMO 設施需要同時生產一個高效致敏藥物和一個普通注射劑。你會如何設計設施布局？

  • 考慮：空氣處理系統是否需要獨立？
  • 考慮：人員、物料流向如何設計？
  • 考慮：清潔驗證策略如何制定？
  • 考慮：是否需要專用生產線？

Section Summary / 章節總結

Key Takeaways
  1. Regulatory Landscape: Facility design must satisfy multiple global GMP standards simultaneously (FDA, EU Annex 1, PIC/S). Cross-referencing expectations between markets is increasingly common during inspections.
  2. Terminal vs. Aseptic: Terminal sterilization is preferred when product stability permits. The choice fundamentally shapes facility classification, HVAC, layout, and environmental monitoring requirements.
  3. CQA-CPP-Facility Chain: Product CQAs drive CPPs, which in turn drive facility and equipment design requirements. This chain must be documented from design phase onward (ICH Q8/Q10).
  4. Risk-Based Design (ICH Q9): Use structured risk assessment tools (FMECA, HACCP) to identify and prioritize risks. Engineered solutions should always be preferred over procedural controls.
  5. Quality by Design: Build quality into the facility from the design phase rather than testing it in afterward. Focus on the operational "future state" of the facility.
  6. Contamination Control Hierarchy: Isolators > RABS > conventional cleanrooms. New facilities should adopt barrier technology. The "nested zones" concept with improvement steps at each transition is fundamental.
  7. Cross-Contamination: Multi-product facilities must address four contamination routes (mix-up, retention, mechanical, airborne) through both engineering and procedural controls.
重點回顧
  1. 法規架構：設施設計必須同時滿足多個市場的 GMP 要求。稽查員越來越頻繁地採用「交叉期望」標準。
  2. 終端滅菌 vs. 無菌製程：終端滅菌是法規首選。選擇哪種路徑決定了設施的根本設計方向。
  3. CQA-CPP-設施鏈：產品品質屬性驅動製程參數，製程參數驅動設施設計。必須從設計階段開始文件化。
  4. 風險導向設計：使用 FMECA、HACCP 等工具系統性評估風險。工程解決方案始終優先於程序解決方案。
  5. 品質源於設計：從設計階段就將品質控制融入設施，而非事後檢測。
  6. 污染控制階層：隔離器 > RABS > 傳統潔淨室。新建設施應採用屏障技術。「巢狀區域」概念是基本原則。
  7. 交叉污染：多產品設施必須針對四大路徑（混淆、殘留、機械轉移、氣傳）建立控制措施。
