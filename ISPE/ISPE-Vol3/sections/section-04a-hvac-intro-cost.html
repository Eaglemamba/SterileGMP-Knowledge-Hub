Introduction — Scope & Role of HVAC

5.1.1 Scope

This chapter deals with creating a suitable environment for the processing of sterile products, including sterile bulks and terminally sterilized products. Biopharmaceutical bulk facilities are covered in the ISPE Baseline Guide: Biopharmaceutical Manufacturing Facilities (Second Edition), but further HVAC details for classified manufacturing space are considered in this Guide.

ISPE has chosen a new harmonized nomenclature for space classification that bridges the differing cleanliness classifications, focusing on the US FDA guidance, EU Annex 1, ISO 14644-1, and ISPE legacy grading systems (see Table 5.3).

For further information, refer to the ISPE Good Practice Guide: Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning, ISPE Baseline Guide: Biopharmaceutical Manufacturing Facilities (Second Edition), and Chapter 11 of this Guide.

5.1.1 範疇

本章探討如何為無菌產品（包括無菌原料藥與最終滅菌產品）的製造創建適當環境。生物製藥原料藥設施已在ISPE生物製藥設施指南中涵蓋，但有關分級製造空間的HVAC細節在本指南中進一步討論。

Key Point / 重點

ISPE採用新的協調命名法來統一各地潔淨室分級：結合美國FDA指南、歐盟Annex 1、ISO 14644-1以及ISPE舊版分級系統（詳見Table 5.3）。

詳細資訊請參閱ISPE HVAC良好實務指南、ISPE生物製藥設施指南以及本指南第11章。

5.1.2 The Role of HVAC

The role of HVAC in sterile product facilities is primarily to ensure that the processing environment does not negatively impact product quality. The product and process should be understood, to assess the level of environmental control necessary to reliably make product. The environmental control provided by HVAC typically impacts a very limited number of Critical Process Parameters (CPPs). The critical environmental parameters associated with sterile product quality can include:

  • Airborne particulates (both viable and non-viable, which may contaminate the product)
  • Temperature (which may affect product stability and operator comfort/cleanliness)
  • Humidity (which impacts microbial growth, operator comfort and cleanliness, static electricity, and may impact some products)
  • Relative pressurization (which may impact the ingress and egress of airborne gases and particles)
  • Gaseous contaminants and oxygen concentration (which may impact operator health/safety)
  • Airborne product aerosols (which may impact operator health/safety and cross-contamination risk)

The control of airborne particulates is usually associated with HVAC for sterile manufacturing. HVAC can also indirectly affect surface contamination by limiting the deposition of airborne particulates, but it has limited use in removing deposited (surface) contaminants, due to the high velocities typically required to overcome surface attractions.

Advanced aseptic processing techniques (such as isolators, BFS, single-use technologies, etc.) have influenced the importance of airborne particulate control required in facilities. These advanced technologies are intended to provide enhanced separation of the processing environment from the room environment (and personnel). This separation improves sterility assurance and decreases the impact of room conditions on product quality. Therefore, lower space classifications and HVAC environmental controls may be required, depending on the level of separation provided.

5.1.2 HVAC的角色

HVAC（暖通空調）在無菌製藥設施中的主要角色是確保製造環境不會對產品品質產生負面影響。必須充分理解產品與製程，以評估可靠製造所需的環境控制等級。HVAC提供的環境控制通常僅影響少數關鍵製程參數（CPPs）。

Six Critical Environmental Parameters / 六大關鍵環境參數

  • 空氣微粒（活性與非活性，可能污染產品）
  • 溫度（影響產品穩定性與操作員舒適度）
  • 濕度（影響微生物生長、靜電與部分產品）
  • 相對壓力（影響空氣與微粒的進出）
  • 氣態污染物與氧濃度（影響操作員健康）
  • 產品氣溶（影響健康與交叉污染風險）

Analogy / 比喻說明

想像HVAC系統如同潔淨室的「呼吸系統」——它持續供應潔淨空氣並排出污染物，維持室內環境的「健康」。但HVAC無法清除表面污染，因為需要極高風速（3,000-5,000 ft/min）才能克服表面附著力。

Advanced Technology Impact / 先進技術影響

隔離器、BFS、一次性技術等先進無菌製程技術可提供製程環境與房間環境的增強隔離。此隔離提升無菌保證並降低房間條件對產品的影響，因此可能允許較低的空間分級與HVAC控制要求。

Temperature & Humidity Impact on Product

The temperature and humidity of the room can impact the product in four ways:

  • The comfort of operators present in the room can be affected, and therefore impact the number of particles (viable) shed by them. A high degree of protective gowning is usually employed to minimize contamination from operators, but cooler room temperatures and lower relative humidity may be needed to keep operators comfortable with this added gowning.
  • Heat sensitive products (especially large molecules) may need to be processed or stored at temperatures well below those required for comfort.
  • Hygroscopic (or moisture reactive) products may need to be processed at lower relative humidity than is required for health and safety.
  • The temperature and relative humidity of a space significantly impact the growth of microorganisms and can impact the hygiene of a facility.

溫濕度對產品的四種影響

Four Impact Pathways / 四大影響路徑

  • 操作員舒適度：溫度過高會增加操作員釋放的微粒數量。較低溫度與濕度可幫助穿著厚重防護服的人員保持舒適。
  • 熱敏感產品：特別是大分子藥物，可能需要在遠低於舒適溫度的條件下製造或儲存。
  • 吸濕性產品：可能需要在低於健康與安全要求的相對濕度下製造。
  • 微生物控制：溫度與相對濕度顯著影響微生物生長與設施衛生。
5.1.3

HVAC and Particulate Control for Product Quality

Pages 109–111

5.1.3 HVAC and Particulate Control for Product Quality

HVAC systems control particulates by applying filtration of varying efficiencies to incoming and recirculating airstreams. For the most critical environments (ISO 5/Grade A, ISO 6, ISO 7/Grade B, ISO 8/Grade C), FDA guidance suggests that one layer of HEPA filtration of at least 99.97% efficiency at 0.3 μm particle diameter should be applied (similar to EU filter designation H13). While not required, HEPA filters with 99.99% efficiency (similar to EU filter designation H14) or Ultra Low Penetration Air (ULPA) filters may also be applied to provide enhanced particulate matter control and to comply with integrity testing to a limit of 0.01% penetration as suggested by the FDA.

This filter is typically fitted at the boundary to the room (terminal) to separate the HVAC system from the critical environment, although it is not stipulated in regulations.

EU filter designation F9/MERV 14-15 filtration may achieve ISO 8/Grade C classification at particle sizes ≥ 0.5 μm (depending on room internal particle generation), making this level of filtration suitable for lower class environments (such as Grade D or CNC) or for pre-filtration of air prior to final HEPA filtration.

Regulated companies may employ two or more HEPA filters in series in the most critical environments. This practice may be justified in cases where highly potent products may be present in recirculated air.

Exhaust and return grilles are generally not fitted with filters, but these may be added to protect ductwork and the outside environment where an aerosol of a highly potent or sensitizing product is present or for biosafety level (BSL) above BSL-2.

5.1.3 HVAC與微粒控制

HEPA Filtration Hierarchy / HEPA過濾層級

  • HEPA 99.97%（H13）：FDA建議的最低要求，適用於ISO 5至ISO 8等級
  • HEPA 99.99%（H14）：提供增強微粒控制，符合0.01%穿透率的完整性測試要求
  • ULPA過濾器：用於需要更高潔淨度的關鍵應用
  • F9/MERV 14-15：可達到ISO 8（≥ 0.5 μm），適用於較低分級或預過濾

Terminal Filter Placement / 終端過濾器位置

HEPA過濾器通常安裝在房間邊界（終端位置），將HVAC系統與關鍵環境分隔。雖然法規未強制要求此配置，但終端過濾器被強烈建議用於ISO 7/Grade B及更高等級，並常見於ISO 8/Grade C系統。

Dual HEPA in Series / 雙重HEPA串聯

在最關鍵環境中可採用兩個或更多HEPA過濾器串聯，特別是當高效產品可能存在於回風中時。但ISPE HVAC指南建議使用MERV 13/14作為替代方案。

Particles in the Critical Area

Particles generated inside the critical area, where sterile product or materials are exposed, may be composed of:

  • The product itself
  • Particles from the process equipment
  • Particles from containers/closures
  • Particles from operators

The difference between particles from these different sources should be understood. Particles of product are not a contaminant and steps should be taken to exclude these during space qualification or environmental monitoring. Particles generated by equipment, materials, or personnel are of concern.

The HVAC system can control particulates within the critical area by providing a flow of clean, nearly particle free air to displace these potential contaminants and sweep them out of the critical area. This unidirectional flow of air from the source, through the critical zone, to a less clean area, and without recirculation back into the critical zone should maintain ISO 5/Grade A conditions and establish LP/GAAS zones.

Particles generated outside the critical zone (i.e., in the room) should be kept out of the critical zone, especially viable particles from operators. This is generally accomplished by use of displacing unidirectional flow and physical barriers. The concentration of particulate within areas outside the critical zone is typically controlled by dilution of airborne particulate with a flow of clean, nearly particle free air.

關鍵區域內的微粒

Four Particle Sources / 四大微粒來源

  • 產品本身：不被視為污染物，應在監測時排除
  • 製程設備：需關注的污染來源
  • 容器/密封件：需關注的污染來源
  • 操作員：最大微粒污染來源

Two Control Strategies / 兩大控制策略

策略一：單向氣流置換——在關鍵區域（ISO 5/Grade A）內，使用單向氣流（UAF）從HEPA過濾器經過關鍵區域掃向較不潔淨的區域，將污染物掀出。

策略二：稀釋混合——在關鍵區域外（ISO 7/Grade B、ISO 8/Grade C），通過大量潔淨空氣稀釋微粒濃度。稀釋計算應以微粒產生率（「源強度」）為基礎。

Air Change Rate (ACR)

Traditionally, the industry has used the Air Change Rate (ACR), expressed in Air Changes per Hour (ACH) to specify the desired diluting airflow. The target ACR has historically been correlated to a specific cleanliness level; however, industry standards such as ISO 14664-4 and IEST-RP-CC012 no longer provide minimum ACH tables and are shifting towards a scientific evaluation utilizing dilution calculations and computer modeling.

While FDA guidance states that a value of 20 ACH is typically acceptable for ISO 8 (0.5 μm, "in operation"), this Guide recommends using ACH as a planning tool and suggests using the following parameters as acceptance criteria for cleanrooms: room particulate, viable concentration, or other empirical measures of room performance (such as recovery, as outlined in ISO 14644-3).

換氣次數（ACR / ACH）

Industry Shift / 行業轉變

傳統作法是以換氣次數（ACH）來規定稀釋氣流，並將其與特定潔淨等級關聯。但ISO 14644-4及IEST-RP-CC012等標準已不再提供最低ACH表格，轉向基於科學的稀釋計算與電腦建模。

Recommended Approach / 建議作法

FDA建議ISO 8採用20 ACH作為參考值，但本指南建議將ACH僅作為規劃工具，實際驗收標準應採用：

  • 房間微粒計數
  • 活性微生物濃度
  • 其他經驗性性能指標（如恢復測試）

Maintaining Environmental Classification

5.1.4 Maintaining Environmental Classification

The airborne particulate concentration in a room is the result of a balance of competing factors:

Particles entering the room:

  • Internal activities (particle generation into the space from personnel and processes)
  • Particles entering at the interfaces to outside spaces
  • Particles entering through the HVAC system

Particles retained within the room:

  • Containment devices
  • Adhesion or deposition of airborne particles on surfaces

Particles leaving the room:

  • Returns
  • Effectiveness of airflow patterns in the room and/or unidirectional flow
  • Exhaust of particles from their source

Quantity of air diluting the particles

A good design should include specific controls to address all of these factors:

  • Equipment should be designed for minimal particulate release into the cleanroom. Particle generating components should be fully enclosed and provided with filtered ventilation or remotely mounted outside the clean space.
  • Personnel should be properly gowned in a properly ventilated gowning room.
  • Particles entering at the interfaces should be controlled by air pressure differences, mass flow across openings, or airlocks and pass-through boxes.
  • Particles entering through HVAC should be appropriately filtered.
  • Wherever possible, ensure that generated particles cannot be disseminated through the room by use of containment barriers and exhaust.
  • Ensure rapid removal of particles from the room via evenly distributed low returns, effective airflow patterns, exhaust at source, and adequate dilution air quantity.

In ISO 7/Grade B and ISO 8/Grade C rooms, dilution of airborne particles using high airflow rates of clean supply air is common. Within these rooms, areas of unidirectional flow (e.g., UFH) can be used to create conventional ISO 5/Grade A areas or areas of LP/GAAS.

Historically, unidirectional air has been introduced at nominally 0.45 m/s (90 ft/min) ± 20%, although other velocities (as low as 0.20 m/s / 40 ft/min) may create more favorable airflow patterns. The velocity employed should be periodically verified as consistent with velocities found at the time of airflow visualization.

5.1.4 維持環境分級

Particle Balance Model / 微粒平衡模型

房間內的微粒濃度是多個競爭因素平衡的結果：

  • 進入：人員與製程產生、外部界面滲透、HVAC送風
  • 滞留：容納装置、表面沉積與附著
  • 離開：回風口、氣流模式效率、源頭排氣
  • 稀釋：空氣量的稀釋效果

Design Control Principles / 設計控制原則

  • 設備應將微粒產生部件完全封閉或配置過濾通風
  • 人員應在適當通風的更衣室正確穿著
  • 界面控制採用壓差、質量流或氣閘/傳遞箱
  • HVAC送風適當過濾
  • 使用容納屏障與排氣防止微粒擴散
  • 均勻分佈的回風口、有效氣流模式、充足稀釋空氣量

UAF Velocity / 單向氣流速度

歷史上單向氣流速度為 0.45 m/s（90 ft/min）± 20%，但較低速度（低至0.20 m/s / 40 ft/min）可能產生更佳的氣流模式。速度應定期驗證，通常與過濾器完整性測試（FIT）同步進行。

5.1.5

Environmental Classification and Monitoring: Occupancy States

Pages 111–113

"In Operation" State

Both the US FDA and EU require that sterile manufacturing cleanrooms should be designed and tested to ensure proper cleanliness while "in operation". Operational, or "in operation", is defined in ISO 14644-1, Section 3.3 as "agreed condition where the cleanroom or clean zone is functioning in the specified manner, with equipment operating and with the specified number of personnel present".

It is recommended that pharmaceutical "in operation" (also referred to as "dynamic") complies with this ISO definition, with HVAC in operation, including local protection devices, with product or simulated product present, with process equipment running, and the specified number of operating and support personnel performing appropriate tasks.

Regulations require that monitoring of the "in operation" particle counts be continuous during aseptic operations. EU regulations require that this be undertaken with a fixed system within Grade A space. The FDA prefers this approach for ISO 5, but allows for portable equipment. The ISO 7/Grade B space surrounding ISO 5/Grade A space should also be monitored "in operation".

「運行中」狀態

"In Operation" Definition / 「運行中」定義

依據ISO 14644-1第3.3節，「運行中」是指潔淨室或潔淨區在規定方式下運作，設備運轉中，且規定人數在場。

製藥「運行中」建議包含：HVAC運行、局部保護裝置啟用、產品或模擬產品在場、製程設備運轉、操作與支援人員執行適當任務。

Monitoring Requirements / 監測要求

  • EU：Grade A空間需採用固定系統連續監測「運行中」微粒計數
  • FDA：ISO 5優先固定系統，但允許使用攜帶式設備
  • ISO 7/Grade B周圍空間也應在「運行中」進行監測

"At Rest" State

In addition to designing for "in operation" conditions, design should account for an "at rest" datum condition to meet EU requirements. "At rest" is defined in ISO 14644-1, Section 3.3 as "condition where the cleanroom or clean zone is complete with equipment installed and operating in a manner agreed upon, but with no personnel present".

Pharmaceutical "at rest" (also referred to as "static") is monitored with HVAC in operation, including local protection devices where these impact room cleanliness, with no product or personnel present, and with process equipment not running.

The FDA 2004 guidance suggests that "as-built" conditions be measured initially as reference data, and it is considered good practice to periodically verify these conditions (e.g., at any major change to HVAC or room configuration, or annually to biennially).

Where "at rest" testing is undertaken, it is important that a "clean up" (also called a "purge") be allowed after the sampling personnel leave the space, before samples are collected. A period of at least 5 minutes should be allowed, with a delay of up to 15–20 minutes being fully appropriate.

Purge Time / 淨化時間

進行「靜止態」測試時，採樣人員離開後必須等待「淨化」期：至少5分鐘，最長15-20分鐘均為適當。此期間HVAC持續運行以掃除人員產生的微粒。

5.1.5.1 Recovery

EU Annex 1 specifies airborne particulate limits for rooms in both the "in operation" and "at rest" states. Recovery testing for classified spaces is needed to prove that this requirement has been satisfied. The speed with which the airborne particle concentration of a classified space (ISO 7/Grade B or ISO 8/Grade C) recovers from "in use" concentration to "at rest" concentration depends on the effectiveness of ventilation (dilution efficiency) and on the air changes.

There are two approaches to the measurement of recovery:

Method 1 — In-operation monitoring transition:

  • Once equilibrium is achieved, operations are ceased and the room is vacated while counting remains ongoing.
  • The recovery time is recorded as the time required to get from "in operation" particulate concentration to the appropriate "at rest" limit.
  • This method does not ensure the facility can recover from elevated particulate concentrations and does not test HVAC performance alone.

Method 2 — ISO 14644-3 recovery test:

  • The particulate concentration is raised to, or above, the "in operation" limit, often using a challenge agent.
  • The injection is ceased and the room is allowed to recover as much as 3-log.
  • Target recovery: 1-log for Grade C, 2-log for Grade B.
  • This method provides more data, covers a broader range of concentrations, and challenges HVAC at upset levels.

The term "recovery" and "air change" do not apply to UAF spaces (usually ISO 5/Grade A) where unidirectional airflow naturally creates extremely large air change rates and very rapid removal of particles.

5.1.5.1 恢復測試

項目 方法一：運行轉靜止 方法二：ISO 14644-3
起始條件 實際運行平衡後 使用挑戰劑提高濃度

UAF Exception / UAF例外

「恢復」與「換氣次數」的概念不適用於單向氣流（UAF）空間（通常為ISO 5/Grade A）。UAF天生創造極大的換氣次數與極快的微粒去除速度，無需單獨測試恢復。

5.1.5.2 HVAC Risk Assessment

Before an HVAC risk assessment can be performed, process and product parameters should be defined. A successful risk assessment exercise should reduce high risks to an acceptable level through redesign or, if necessary, through procedures.

Generally, the risks to be considered when specifying area classifications include: the risk inherent in the product being produced (sterile products are high risk) and the risk that the background environment can impact the product quality or patient health.

Two approaches to risk assessment are very well suited to HVAC and environmental control:

  • Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP): This seven-step process focuses on the critical parameters and the monitoring of control points. In the case of HVAC, these control points are typically room temperature, room humidity, room DP, room filter integrity, room airflow, and room airborne contamination (viable and non-viable).
  • Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA): This approach provides a score for risks and allows easy assessment of the impact of failures. A fixed taxonomy (HAZOP) can easily be used as HVAC can only impact temperature, humidity, pressure and airborne particulate.

Risk assessment should address expected failure and transitional states (e.g., loss of power, loss of controls, shut down, restart, etc.). The expected conditions for pressurization, cleanliness and other critical parameters should be planned and tested.

5.1.5.2 HVAC風險評估

Two Risk Assessment Methods / 兩大風險評估方法

  • HACCP（危害分析與關鍵控制點）：七步驟法，專注於關鍵參數與控制點監測。HVAC的控制點通常為溫度、濕度、壓差、過濾器完整性、氣流與微粒監測。
  • FMEA（失效模式與效應分析）：提供風險評分，易於評估失效影響。可配合HAZOP固定分類法使用，因HVAC僅影響溫度、濕度、壓力與微粒四項參數。

Failure & Transitional States / 失效與過渡狀態

風險評估必須涵蓋預期的失效與過渡狀態，例如斷電、控制失效、關機、重啟等。應規劃並測試各狀態下的壓差、潔淨度與其他關鍵參數。

Critical Control Points in HVAC Risk Assessment

Typical critical parameter/control points where a risk assessment might indicate that drug product quality could be affected include:

  • Room temperature monitoring system
  • Room humidity monitoring system
  • Room DP monitoring system
  • Environmental monitoring for total and viable airborne particulate
  • Continuous airborne particulate monitoring system (expected for ISO 5/Grade A, optional for lower grades). Note: Automated continuous particle monitoring systems provide extensive relevant data and are generally more reliable than manual monitoring.
  • Periodic final HEPA filter integrity (as proven by regular scheduled filter integrity testing)
  • Periodic verification of airflow and/or the airflow monitoring system
  • Periodic verification of ISO 5/Grade A filter face velocity and air velocity proximate to work height. Note: velocity near work height will not be the same as velocity near the filter face.
  • Periodic verification of airflow patterns in ISO 5/Grade A (unidirectional) and general room airflow patterns in ISO 7/Grade B or at interfaces, airlocks, pass-throughs, etc.

Items which only indirectly affect the process environment — and therefore have no direct patient safety and product quality impact — usually include AHU pre-filters, fans, coils, humidifiers, dehumidifiers, ductwork, chilled water, and steam.

HVAC風險評估的關鍵控制點

Nine Critical Control Points / 九大關鍵控制點

  1. 房間溫度監測系統
  2. 房間濕度監測系統
  3. 房間壓差（DP）監測系統
  4. 總微粒與活性微粒環境監測
  5. 連續微粒監測系統（ISO 5/Grade A必要）
  6. 定期HEPA過濾器完整性測試
  7. 定期驗證氣流與監測系統
  8. 定期驗證過濾器表面速度與工作高度風速
  9. 定期驗證氣流模式（ISO 5單向流與房間氣流）

Direct vs. Indirect Impact / 直接與間接影響

僅間接影響製程環境的項目（無直接安全/品質影響）通常包括：AHU預過濾器、風扇、盤管、加濕器、除濕器、風管、冷凍水與蒸汽。這些列為GEP範疇而非關鍵控制點。

5.1.6

Local Protection and Grade A Air Supply (LP/GAAS)

Pages 114–118

Background and Definition

The terms "Local Protection" and "Grade A Air Supply" have appeared in several regulatory documents. In 2004, the FDA guidance introduced the terms "Local Class 100 (ISO 5) protection" and "local protection". In 2008, the EU GMP Annex 1 introduced "Grade A air supply". In 2010, PIC/S provided a definition and noted that Grade A air supply is one of the most significant changes in Annex 1.

The terms Local Protection (LP) and Grade A Air Supply (GAAS) should be considered as equivalent, and are used to indicate any instance where a localized HEPA filtered air supply is used to reduce the risk of total or viable particulate contamination of critical surfaces within a specified working zone.

LP/GAAS is generally configured with terminal HEPA filtration proximate to the work zone (above or to one side, as appropriate to the risks) in order to ensure ISO 5 particulate levels. This engineering control can be applied to reduce risk in any background classification (including Grade D, ISO 8/Grade C, and ISO 7/Grade B cleanroom areas).

A well-designed LP/GAAS involves proper configuration of the extract to ensure a flushing flow of clean air through the area of concern. In certain cases, additional engineering controls (such as enclosures or RABS with glove ports) can be reasonably employed to enhance the air quality within the work zone.

背景與定義

LP/GAAS Definition / LP/GAAS定義

局部保護（LP）與A級空氣供應（GAAS）應視為等價，指任何使用局部HEPA過濾空氣供應來降低指定工作區域內關鍵表面微粒污染風險的情況。

配置要點：

  • 終端HEPA過濾器靠近工作區（上方或側方）
  • 確保ISO 5微粒等級
  • 適用於任何背景分級（Grade D至ISO 7/Grade B）
  • 適當配置回風口以確保潔淨空氣沖洗流

Analogy / 比喻

LP/GAAS如同手術室中的局部無菌燈光——即使整個房間不是最高等級，但在關鍵操作區域提供最潔淨的空氣保護。

  • 清洗機卸貨：減少卸貨與轉運時的微粒污染

Environmental Monitoring for LP/GAAS

Application of LP/GAAS is straightforward from an HVAC design perspective. However, establishing target "at rest" or "in operation" limits for these areas is often overlooked during facility design, which can cause considerable difficulty and delays during qualification.

LP/GAAS zones should be differentiated from ISO 5/Grade A zones or conditions, as these limits may not be applicable.

Principles for establishing LP/GAAS EM limits:

  • Monitoring/testing should include measurement of total and viable particulates proximate to the work zone.
  • "At rest" monitoring/testing is primarily useful as a pre-check of overall system performance.
  • LP/GAAS air supply should generally meet ISO 5 requirements for 0.5 μm and 5.0 μm particles, when measured 100–150 mm below the filter face, under all conditions.
  • A well-designed LP/GAAS should achieve a 1-log reduction in total particulates below the "in operation" background limit.
  • Care should be taken for viable limits where LP/GAAS is used in classified areas below ISO 7/Grade B.
  • For initial qualification, viable particulate limits 1/2 to 1/3 of background environment limits are recommended.

LP/GAAS環境監測

Common Pitfall / 常見陷阱

LP/GAAS區域的「靜止態」或「運行中」限值經常在設施設計階段被忽略，導致確認時出現重大困難與延誤。應在設計初期即建立LP/GAAS的EM限值。

EM Limit Principles / EM限值原則

  • LP/GAAS與ISO 5/Grade A應明確區分，限值可能不同
  • 空氣供應應在過濾器面下100-150 mm處滿足ISO 5
  • 良好設計應實現背景「運行中」限值的1-log降低
  • 初始確認時活性微粒限值建議為背景限值的1/2至1/3

5.2

Cost Considerations

Pages 118–120

5.2.1 Capital Costs

HVAC systems for sterile manufacturing are expensive and can represent a significant proportion of the total facility cost. The capital cost of a system can vary greatly and is dependent upon the decisions made throughout the design stages. The main factors that influence HVAC cost include:

  • Use of isolators and other microenvironments: The use of isolators may reduce room classification requirements, leading to smaller and lower cost HVAC systems. Other microenvironments (such as RABS and LP/GAAS) can reduce the particulate challenge to critical zones, also reducing HVAC system sizing. Process closure and closed filling have a similar impact.
  • Size of aseptic processing area: This should be optimized, without compromising material flow and product quality. HVAC size may be optimized correspondingly.
  • Redundancy: The need for redundant HVAC equipment is best addressed via a failure mode risk analysis. It is often unnecessary to duplicate all HVAC plant items.
  • Simplicity: Overly elaborate design solutions are more expensive and may have a greater risk of failure.
  • Integration: Process room layouts, gowning room design, material transfers and process equipment design can all impact HVAC design.

5.2.1 資本成本

Five Capital Cost Drivers / 五大資本成本驅動因素

  • 隔離器與微環境：採用隔離器可降低房間分級要求，減小HVAC系統規模與成本。RABS、LP/GAAS、封閉製程同樣有助
  • 無菌區域大小：應在不影響物料流與品質的前提下優化面積
  • 冗餘設計：通過失效模式風險分析決定，通常無需完全複製
  • 簡單性：過度複雜的設計更貴且故障風險更高
  • 整合性：製程布局、更衣室、物料轉運與設備設計都影響HVAC

5.2.2 Operating Costs

The HVAC system design affects the operating costs of the manufacturing facility, particularly as continuous operation of HVAC is normally required. The preceding capital cost considerations may be outweighed by operating costs if a lifecycle approach to costing is undertaken.

Important energy considerations/approaches in the design process include:

  • Optimizing the size and occupancy of classified space to minimize airflow needed
  • Optimizing airflow through modeling and/or dilution calculations
  • Designing room air distribution for the most effective ventilation practical
  • Designing rooms to minimize excess leakage
  • Optimizing temperature and humidity requirements (widest acceptable ranges)
  • Treating outside air separately from recirculated air, especially for humidity control
  • Providing energy recovery on exhaust streams:
    • Enthalpy or heat wheel (low cross-contamination concern)
    • Air to air energy recovery (greater concern)
    • Heat pipes and runaround (serious concern)
  • Optimizing differential pressures
  • Optimizing filtration to minimize total pressure drop and maximize HEPA filter life
  • Recirculation of air (global or primary/secondary systems)
  • Advanced airside control strategies:
    • Airflow setback during idle periods
    • Occupancy sensing airflow setback
    • AHU supply static pressure setback
    • Discharge air temperature setback
    • Tracking VAV control of primary airflow
    • Adaptive airflow control (monitoring particulate and adjusting airflow)
  • Commissioning according to GEP
  • Utilizing common sized HEPA filters to reduce spares inventory
  • Design for ease of maintenance and testing

5.2.2 營運成本

Lifecycle Cost Perspective / 生命週期成本觀點

HVAC需要持續運行以維持潔淨度。資本成本的節省可能被運營成本抵消，因此應採用生命週期成本法進行總體評估。

Energy Optimization Strategies / 能源優化策略

  • 空間優化：最小化分級區域面積與人員數量
  • 氣流建模：以科學計算取代固定ACH標準
  • 熱回收：依交叉污染風險選擇轉輪、空對空或熱管方案
  • 壓差優化：避免過度壓差與過大流量差
  • 過濾優化：最小化總壓降，最大化HEPA壽命
  • 先進控制：閒置氣流調降、在場感應、VAV控制、自適應氣流控制

Warning: HVAC Shutdown / 警告：HVAC關機

強烈不建議為節能而定期關閉HVAC系統，因為這可能導致環境控制喪失並增加產品污染風險。數小時或數天的關機通常需要大量清潔、消毒與監測工作才能恢復生產。

5.3

Sources of Particulate Contamination

Pages 120–122

5.3.1 Internal Sources

Non-viable (microbiologically inert) particles may be dust, smoke, plastic, and metal debris from process or HVAC equipment, synthetic clothing fibers, makeup, hair, skin flakes, etc. Non-viable particles may also be active product from another area within a multi-product facility, which is a cross-contamination concern. Among and upon non-viable particles, there can be viable (living) organisms, such as spores, bacteria, and viruses. HEPA (and optional higher quality ULPA) filters can effectively remove more than 99.97% of these particles from the HVAC air supply.

Typical sources from inside the classified space include:

  • Personnel
  • Process equipment
  • Contamination entering through gaps in construction and cycling of doors
  • Contamination on items entering the space
  • Shedding from surfaces and cleaning materials or agent residue
  • Contamination from facility systems and HVAC ductwork downstream of final HEPA filters
  • Product particulate contamination from air returning from other product processing areas

Personnel: People are the greatest source of particle contamination, and the level of contamination they add depends on gowning and how they perform their tasks. Interventions (people inserting themselves into the aseptic process) are a common source of risk. The number and activity level of personnel, coupled with their gowning, is one of the most significant factors challenging the cleanroom environment.

The impact of gowning is often much greater than the impact of increasing dilution airflow (air change rate). The use of barrier technology (RABS or isolators) minimizes or eliminates interventions into the ISO 5/Grade A environment.

Process and Equipment: Contaminants released from equipment are mostly non-viable if equipment was properly cleaned and stored. Cleaning activities may release large quantities of particles. Single-use equipment can reduce the particulate associated with cleaning. Airborne product itself may become a cross-contaminant of another product.

Particles generated within the HVAC system should be virtually eliminated by HEPA filters. Terminal filters (located at the point where air enters the room) are preferable, and are strongly recommended for rooms classified ISO 7/Grade B or higher and commonly applied in ISO 8/Grade C.

5.3.1 內部來源

Internal Contamination Sources / 內部污染來源

  • 人員：最大的微粒污染來源。或為非活性（皮屑、衣物纖維）或活性（細菌、霉菌）
  • 製程設備：如正確清潔儲存，主要釋放非活性微粒
  • 建築縫隙與門循環：外部污染的入侵途徑
  • 進入物品上的污染：物料與零件攜帶的微粒
  • 表面脫落與清潔殘留：清潔活動可能釋放大量微粒
  • HEPA過濾器下游的風管：終端過濾器後的污染來源
  • 其他製程區域的回風：交叉污染風險

Personnel = #1 Risk / 人員＝首要風險

人員是最大的微粒污染來源。穿著的影響通常遠大於增加換氣次數（稀釋氣流）的效果。使用屏障技術（RABS或隔離器）可最大程度減少或消除人員對ISO 5/Grade A環境的干預。

Single-Use Advantage / 一次性優勢

一次性設備可減少與清潔相關的微粒，因為清潔活動本身會釋放大量微粒。這是一次性技術除了避免交叉污染外的另一項優勢。

5.3.2 External Sources

A positive room differential pressure relative to surrounding areas helps to exclude external contaminants, reducing infiltration from more contaminated spaces through cracks in the room fabric and doors. Where rooms of different air quality classifications are joined by a doorway, an airlock should be used to ensure that at least one door in the potential contamination path is closed, thus maintaining DP between the spaces.

In tightly constructed envelopes (e.g., panelized or modular wall systems), the airflow necessary to generate differential pressure across the envelope may be very small. By contrast, some stick-built walls and suspended ceiling systems may consume significant airflow to maintain pressurization.

A negative room differential pressure may be required to help contain highly potent or sensitizing compounds, live pathogen, or other high-risk materials. In these cases, surrounding positive pressurized clean spaces are most preferable, commonly achieved using airlocks and clean corridors.

Where isolators are employed to provide both a barrier and containment of potent/pathogenic compounds, "bubble" or "sink" airlock zones may be employed as part of the isolator design.

Particles entering the HVAC system, such as from outdoor (fresh) air used for room pressurization and operator health, are usually removed in the HVAC air filtration system, with the location and performance of the final HEPA filter being important to assuring removal.

5.3.2 外部來源

Pressure Cascade / 壓力梯度

正壓差可防止外部污染物透過縫隙與門長射入。不同分級區域之間應使用氣閘，確保污染路徑中至少一道門關閉。

Construction Type Matters / 建築類型影響

  • 模組化牆板系統：緊密建造，產生壓差所需氣流極小
  • 傳統建造與懸吊天花板：可能消耗大量氣流來維持加壓，並可能成為顯著的微粒來源

Negative Pressure Cases / 負壓案例

處理高效藥物、致敏化合物或活性病原體時，可能需要負壓房間以防止泄漏。此時周圍空間應為正壓潔淨區域，通常通過氣閘與潔淨走廊實現。隔離器可採用「泡泡」或「沉陷」式氣閘區設計。

Section Summary / 章節總結

Key Takeaways from Sections 5.1–5.4

Summary of Key Concepts

5.1 Introduction

  • HVAC is the backbone of environmental control in sterile manufacturing facilities, primarily controlling airborne particulates, temperature, humidity, and pressure differentials.
  • Advanced aseptic technologies (isolators, BFS, single-use) can reduce room classification requirements by providing enhanced separation.
  • HEPA filtration (99.97% at 0.3 μm minimum) is the primary tool for airborne particulate control; ULPA filters may be used for enhanced performance.
  • Air Change Rate (ACH) should be used as a planning tool, not an acceptance criterion; empirical measures (particle counts, recovery tests) are preferred.

5.1.4–5.1.6 Classification, Risk, and LP/GAAS

  • Room particulate concentration is a balance of particles entering, retained within, and leaving the room, diluted by clean air supply.
  • Three occupancy states: "In Operation" (dynamic), "At Rest" (static), and "As-Built".
  • Recovery testing proves the transition from "in operation" to "at rest" conditions; two methods available (operational transition vs. ISO 14644-3 challenge).
  • HACCP and FMEA are the recommended risk assessment approaches for HVAC systems.
  • LP/GAAS provides localized HEPA-filtered air protection in any background classification, from Grade D to ISO 7/Grade B.

5.2–5.4 Cost, Contamination, and Standards

  • HVAC capital costs are driven by isolator use, area sizing, redundancy, simplicity, and integration with other facility systems.
  • Operating costs can be optimized through energy recovery, airflow modeling, pressure optimization, advanced control strategies, and lifecycle costing.
  • Personnel are the greatest internal source of particulate contamination; gowning impact exceeds dilution airflow impact.
  • Positive pressure differentials exclude external contaminants; airlocks preserve classification between differing zones.
  • Table 5.3 provides a globally harmonized classification bridging US FDA, EU/PIC/S, and ISO 14644-1 nomenclatures.

5.1 導論

  • 先進無菌技術可降低房間分級要求
  • HEPA過濾（0.3 μm處至少99.97%效率）為主要微粒控制工具
  • ACH應作為規劃工具而非驗收標準

  • 微粒濃度是進入、滞留、離開與稀釋的平衡結果
  • 三種占用狀態：運行中、靜止態、空置態
  • HACCP與FMEA為HVAC推薦風險評估方法
  • LP/GAAS可在任何背景分級下提供局部HEPA過濾保護

  • 營運成本可通過熱回收、氣流建模、壓差優化與先進控制策略優化
  • 人員是最大微粒來源，穿著影響超過稀釋氣流
