3.2.4 Sterile Filtration

Original Text

Sterile filtration is a mode of sterilization for solutions that can be used when the solution cannot be subjected to terminal sterilization in its final container/closure system. The process provides a defined reduction in the microbiological load of the feed solution and is intended to render the solution sterile. Sterile filtration has a limited effect on endotoxin reduction, so it is necessary to ensure that the upstream solution has a low bioburden to minimize the formation of endotoxins.

Solutions sterilized by filtration are subsequently processed aseptically by formulation and/or filling operations. The final filtration should occur as close as possible to the point of formulation or filling.

Any aqueous solution destined for terminal sterilization should also be pre-filtered, preferably through a sterilizing grade filter, prior to the terminal sterilization step to remove both bioburden and particulate.

The filtration train and associated vessels and piping network can be prepared in three ways:

  • Aseptically Assembled Multi-Use Systems: Associated with older facilities. Components are cleaned, rinsed, autoclaved, and carefully assembled within ISO 5/Grade A environment using full aseptic handling techniques. Can be closed (no exposure) or open (limited exposure). Declining use as older equipment is replaced.
  • Sterilize-in-Place (SIP) Multi-Use Systems: Usually preferred over aseptically assembled systems, often applied to larger scale operations. Vessels, pipes, and components are cleaned and assembled under clean conditions (ISO 7/Grade B), then the closed system is SIP using clean/pure steam. Alternatively, systems can be CIP and SIP in ISO 8/Grade C.
  • Pre-Sterilized Single-Use Systems: Assembled under clean conditions, placed in protective barrier (e.g., double bagged), sterilized via gamma irradiation. Transferred to ISO 5/Grade A filling area when needed. Equipped with proprietary aseptic connection devices. Benefits include elimination of cleaning/cleaning validation, elimination of SIP, advantages for potent compounds, and ability to stockpile inventory.

In general, reusable product contacting equipment should be product dedicated to reduce cross-contamination risk. If not possible, multi-product contact equipment should be supported by risk assessment and a robust cleaning validation program.

Key design considerations for sterile filtration systems:

  • Sterile filtration should be used only when terminal sterilization cannot be applied.
  • Interaction between sterilizing filter and product should be evaluated (extractables/leachables, chemical compatibility, adsorption).
  • A sterilizing filter should be validated for microbial retention using appropriate challenge microorganisms under worst case processing conditions.
  • Filtration through two sterilizing grade filters in series (redundant filtration) may be appropriate to reduce chance of batch sterilization failure.
  • Integrity of sterilizing filter membrane should be confirmed both prior to and following use by validated test methods (bubble point, diffusion/forward flow, or water intrusion).
  • Sterile vent filters should be checked for integrity post-use and, as a risk mitigation step, pre-use.
  • For pressure rated systems, a pressure hold/decay test should be applied to the assembled system prior to sterilization.
  • Where drain lines are connected, barrier arrangements including valves and air breaks should be provided to minimize back siphoning risk.
Note: The risk assessment for pre-use integrity testing should include whether testing is performed prior to or following sterilization of the filter. EU Annex 1 recommends testing prior to sterilization to avoid non-detection of a compromised filter element. However, this operation has the potential to compromise sterility of the downstream product pathway. Proper system design with redundant filter elements and pre-sterilization integrity testing can minimize both risks.

導師解析

核心概念：無菌過濾的三大系統組態

製藥產業中，無菌過濾系統可分為三大類：

  1. 無菌組裝式多用系統（舊式）：各元件分別清洗、滅菌後，在 ISO 5/Grade A 環境下手動組裝。風險高，正逐步淘汰。
  2. SIP 多用系統（主流）：組裝後封閉系統直接通入純蒸汽進行就地滅菌，無需打開連接點，大幅降低人為污染風險。
  3. 預滅菌單次使用系統（趨勢）：免洗免 SIP，伽馬射線滅菌後直接使用。特別適合高效藥物（potent compounds）及 CDMO 多品項切換。

選擇依據：生產規模、產品數量、廠房新舊及法規市場要求。

重點提示：冗餘過濾與完整性測試

冗餘過濾（Redundant Filtration）是在過濾路徑中串聯兩個滅菌級濾膜，當其中一個膜失效時，另一個仍能提供無菌保障。這是無菌製程的安全網。

濾膜完整性測試是法規強制要求：

  • 使用前測試（Pre-use）：確認濾膜安裝正確且無瑕疵
  • 使用後測試（Post-use）：確認過濾過程中濾膜未受損
  • 測試方法：泡點測試（Bubble Point）、擴散流（Forward Flow）、水侵入（Water Intrusion）

EU Annex 1 建議在滅菌前進行完整性測試，但這可能破壞下游無菌路徑的滅菌狀態——因此需要透過冗餘設計來兼顧兩個風險。

比喻說明：無菌過濾如同淨水系統

想像家用淨水器有三種安裝方式：(1) 每次使用前拆開清洗再組裝；(2) 安裝好後用蒸汽消毒，不用拆開；(3) 直接買全新密封的濾芯插上去用完即丟。

製藥無菌過濾的三種系統也是相同邏輯——從費工高風險的手動組裝，到免洗免消毒的一次性系統，技術演進方向就是「減少人為操作、降低污染風險」。

CDMO 實務觀點

CDMO 需處理多種不同產品，單次使用系統的優勢特別明顯：

  • 免除跨產品清潔驗證的巨大負擔
  • 消除殘留交叉污染風險
  • 可預先建立庫存，縮短換線時間
  • 特別適合小批量、高價值的生物製劑生產
Bulk Product Holding / 散裝產品靜置保存

Original Text

Following compounding, and prior to any filtration process, a solution should be held under conditions designed to protect the solution from contamination from the surrounding environment. The maximum time that a solution can be held under these conditions without risk of increased bioburden should be validated. It is recommended that solutions proceed through a filtration step as soon as possible following compounding to minimize contamination risk. Sustained validated hold periods should only be used by exception. Bioburden sampling of the unfiltered bulk should occur after any holding period, prior to filtration, as a process control step.

In some circumstances, it is necessary to sterilize a solution by filtration into a holding tank, hold it as sterile bulk, and then feed the filling machine from the holding tank without further filtration. If product is held in this way, it should be verified that product integrity is not compromised during the maximum holding time. The holding tank should be maintained at an overpressure with continuous monitoring to ensure that no ingress from the surrounding environment is possible, unless there are safety or product stability considerations which prevent this. This approach may also be used as a pre-filtration or bioburden reduction step where the product is filtered a second time during the filling process.

導師解析

重點提示：Hold Time 驗證是法規稽查重點

散裝藥液的「保存時間限制」是製程驗證中經常被稽查員關注的項目：

  • 過濾前保存（Pre-filtration hold）：驗證在最長保存時間內，生物負載不會超過可接受限度
  • 過濾後無菌保存（Post-filtration sterile hold）：持續正壓監控，確保外部微生物無法進入
  • 生物負載取樣必須在保存期結束後、過濾前進行——這是製程控制步驟

原則是「儘快過濾」，延長保存只能作為例外情況使用。

比喻說明：正壓保護如同氣球防塵

無菌散裝儲罐維持正壓，就像一個充飽氣的氣球——因為內部壓力大於外部，空氣只會從裡面往外跑，灰塵和細菌無法「逆流」進入。一旦正壓消失，就像氣球洩了氣，外部污染物就能輕易侵入。

Container Preparation / 容器準備

Original Text

Container preparation involves the cleaning, sterilization, and depyrogenation (applicable for parenteral products) of the empty product containers. There are four forms of contamination which container preparation processes should control:

  • Bioburden: Viable microbiological counts, as quantified in Colony Forming Units (CFU)
  • Endotoxin: Pyrogenic cell wall material resulting from growth and degradation of gram negative microorganisms
  • Extraneous Particulates: Solid particulate matter, sometimes resulting from container manufacturing, packing, and staging processes (e.g., glass fragments)
  • Extraneous Chemicals: Contamination with chemicals that are not part of the product formulation

Washing and rinsing processes should be designed to effectively remove extraneous particulates and chemicals. Such processes should also be capable of significantly reducing any bioburden or endotoxin contamination. Any remaining bioburden is then inactivated and endotoxins degraded by subjecting the containers to dry heat depyrogenation. For containers that cannot be depyrogenated (e.g., plastic), endotoxin reduction is dependent upon the washing and rinsing process.

Washing Machine Design Considerations:

  • Alarms for low/high pressure and low/high temperature
  • Use of WFI for the final wash step
  • Easy draining, cleaning, and drying of equipment and pipes, with appropriate gradients and drains
  • Whether the washing machine is equipped with an ultrasonic bath
  • Post-wash drying with air passed through a sterilizing grade filter
  • Protection of washed components prior to depyrogenation phase
  • Sampling points to aid in qualification/validation
  • Air breaks at washer drain to prevent back flow

Depyrogenation Tunnel Design:

Modern depyrogenation tunnels are designed with UAF and should be provided with heat-up, dwell, and cool-down zones. The combination of residence time and set point temperature, commonly 250°C (482°F) to 350°C (662°F), in the dwell zone should achieve the required degree of depyrogenation. Residence time is typically controlled by belt speed.

Containers should exit the tunnel via a cooling zone to reach a sufficiently low temperature to avoid affecting either the product when filled, or adversely deflecting the protecting UAF over the exit conveyor.

Depyrogenation tunnel design considerations:

  • Filter quality and construction of gaskets for high temperature air filters (non-shedding at higher than expected use temperatures)
  • Alarms and data recording for critical measurements (temperature, air speed, belt speed, pressure between cooling zone and surrounding area)
  • Design of building HVAC system to accommodate sensible heat emission to surrounding cleanroom
  • Sterilization and performance needs of the cooling zone
  • ISO 5/Grade A environment in the cooling zone
  • Integration with upstream and downstream equipment

Ready-to-Use Containers: Pre-sterilized and pre-depyrogenated containers reduce complexity of facility design. Advantageous for small batch/low volume operations. Supplied in wrapped tub/nest configuration interfacing with loading/unwrapping system. Each batch accompanied by Certificate of Conformance/Analysis.

導師解析

核心概念：容器準備的四大污染控制

注射劑容器在使用前必須控制四類污染：

  1. 生物負載（Bioburden）：活的微生物，以 CFU 計量
  2. 內毒素（Endotoxin）：革蘭氏陰性菌的細胞壁碎片，即使細菌死了，內毒素仍會引發發熱反應
  3. 外來微粒（Particulates）：玻璃碎片、纖維等固體微粒
  4. 外來化學物質（Chemicals）：非產品配方的化學殘留

清洗去除微粒和化學物質，去熱原隧道用高溫破壞內毒素並滅菌——兩步缺一不可。

重點提示：去熱原隧道的三區設計

去熱原隧道分為三個功能區域：

  • 升溫區（Heat-up Zone）：容器從室溫逐漸加熱
  • 恆溫區（Dwell Zone）：維持 250–350°C，停留足夠時間以達到至少 3-log 內毒素降低
  • 冷卻區（Cool-down Zone）：必須是 ISO 5/Grade A 環境，冷卻至不影響產品的溫度

隧道的壓差平衡至關重要——必須在充填室（ISO 5）和洗瓶室（ISO 8）之間維持正確的壓差梯度。

比喻說明：去熱原隧道像烤箱輸送帶

想像一條連續式烤箱輸送帶：玻璃瓶從一端進入，先經過預熱段，然後在超高溫段被「烤」到 250–350°C（這個溫度足以摧毀所有內毒素），最後通過冷卻段降溫。整個過程全自動、連續運行，瓶子排隊通過就像生產線上的產品一樣。

CDMO 實務：即用型容器 (RTU) 的趨勢

預洗預滅菌的即用型容器（RTU, Ready-to-Use）正在迅速改變 CDMO 設施設計：

  • 免除洗瓶機和去熱原隧道的資本投資
  • 大幅減少潔淨室面積需求
  • 降低公用工程（WFI、壓縮空氣、HVAC 散熱）的負荷
  • 特別適合小批量、高價值產品及臨床試驗階段

但需注意：必須稽核供應商的品質系統，每批需附合格證明（Certificate of Conformance）。

Closure Preparation / 膠塞準備

Original Text

As the closure (e.g., stopper) will be in direct contact with the product at some time during storage/handling/use, it should be sterile, free from foreign contaminants, and essentially free of endotoxins.

Manufacturers should determine the nature and extent of contaminants normally found on closures when received from a supplier. The washing process should be qualified to remove these contaminants, including endotoxins, to an acceptable level.

Washers should use hydraulic or mechanical agitation to dislodge attached particulate matter and to remove such debris without re-deposition onto another portion of the load. WFI should be used for rinsing closures. A cleaning agent or detergent wash may be used for endotoxin load reduction. Use of a cleaning agent or detergent may impact stopper machineability. Siliconization may also be required, in limited instances, for machineability.

Washing should be followed by sterilization and drying. This should be achieved by minimizing the time that closures are held in the wet condition, so that they can be sterilized and dried in a nominal time period, without intermediate handling. For lyophilized products, the final moisture content of the stoppers should be considered when designing and validating a sterilization process.

Ready-to-use (e.g., pre-sterilized) or ready-to-sterilize (e.g., pre-washed) closures may be used with the same benefits and considerations discussed for containers.

導師解析

核心概念：膠塞處理的完整流程

膠塞（Stopper）直接接觸產品，品質要求極高：

  1. 清洗：以水力或機械攪動去除微粒，避免再沉積（re-deposition）
  2. 最終沖洗：必須使用 WFI（注射用水）
  3. 滅菌與乾燥：清洗後儘快完成，避免濕態長時間保存滋生微生物

重點提示：凍乾產品的膠塞水分控制

對於凍乾產品，膠塞的殘留水分至關重要。滅菌後膠塞中殘留的水分可能在凍乾過程中釋放，影響產品的最終含水量和穩定性。因此，膠塞滅菌製程的設計必須同時考慮滅菌效力和乾燥效果。

這是凍乾產品特有的驗證挑戰：需要證明膠塞水分不會影響產品 CQA（關鍵品質屬性）。

即用型膠塞（RTU Closures）

與即用型容器相同的趨勢，預洗預滅菌的膠塞正在被廣泛採用：

  • Ready-to-Sterilize（預洗型）：供應商完成清洗，用戶只需滅菌
  • Ready-to-Use（即用型）：供應商完成清洗和滅菌，用戶直接使用

對 CDMO 而言，這進一步簡化了設施需求並縮短了換線時間。

Filling and Sealing / 充填與密封

Original Text

During filling, the product is dosed into containers and then sealed. For aseptically processed products, the containers, closures, and product should be sterile prior to filling. Filling is a critical operation, particularly for an aseptically filled product, as it often is the only operation following sterile filtration in which the product and product contact surfaces are exposed to an open environment. Aseptic filling should be performed within an ISO 5/Grade A environment.

Liquid filling is usually achieved by one of three methods:

  • Fixed volume stroke piston pumps (e.g., liquid sealed and rolling diaphragm)
  • Time-pressure system: Controlled overpressure applied to a pilot filling vessel, opened to filling needles for a fixed time. Combination of opening time and overpressure control the fill volume.
  • Peristaltic pumps with flexible tubing

A system with automatic adjustment of the filling volume via feedback from a statistical or 100% (non-destructive) in-process check-weighing system is recommended.

After filling, the vial headspace may be purged with sterile nitrogen or other qualified non-reactive gas to displace atmospheric gases to pre-defined limits. This technique is commonly used for products sensitive to oxygen.

Design considerations for aseptic filling machines:

  • The machine should be sited within an ISO 5/Grade A RABS or isolator to minimize operator contact with product, product contact surfaces, and containers/closures.
  • Container closure contact surfaces should be constructed of stainless steel, of suitable design and finish to withstand cleaning and sterilization.
  • Design should allow introduction of sterile container-closure components into the critical zone without compromising sterile integrity.
  • Design should permit easy access for cleaning and disinfection with absence of crevices or niches where contamination may accumulate. Threaded fittings should be avoided.
  • Equipment should be suitable for delivering product into the container with fill accuracy assurance.
  • Moving parts and lubricating fluids within ISO 5/Grade A should be minimized. Lubricants should be evaluated and purchased sterile or sterilized (e.g., gamma irradiation).
  • Design for easy changeover between batches and container/closure configurations.
  • IPC samples should be removable or testable (check-weigh) without interrupting line operation.
  • Critical area should support optimal UAF pattern to prevent contamination.
  • RABS doors and machine guards should prevent particulate contamination when manipulated. Access doors should be interlocked to automatically shut down filling operations when opened.
  • Equipment should be installed to allow inherent intervention and maintenance from outside critical zones.
  • Design with consideration to operator ergonomics for line setup and corrective intervention.
  • Design for ease of setup (tool-less), to minimize post-sterilization assembly activities and eliminate manual contacting of sterile processing contact surfaces.
  • Operator interface should be restricted, preferably using glove port access.
  • Stopper hoppers should be designed to prevent particulate contamination. 316L stainless steel or non-shedding, robust materials recommended.
  • Stopper bowls and delivery chutes that cannot be SIP should be easily demountable for autoclaving. If closed isolator technology is used with VHP decontamination, this can also decontaminate indirect product contact parts. A thorough cleaning step should be applied to remove potential silicone layers from vibrating siliconized stoppers.
  • Barrier enclosure should permit transfer of sterilized components while maintaining ISO 5/Grade A continuity.
  • Transport belts or cassette/tray loading systems should be protected under ISO 5/Grade A UAF until vials are loaded in the lyophilizer.
  • Filling and sealing of potent/hazardous compounds may require special air exhaust or particulate capture systems for operator safety.

For lyophilized products, stoppers are not fully seated to enable solvent escape during sublimation and desorption. The stoppering tool height should be pre-adjusted for consistent stopper positioning.

Glass ampoules should be sealed by application of heat (gas flame jet or laser) under ISO 5/Grade A conditions. Note: Sealing of ampoules generates high levels of particulates, removed via active exhaust/vacuum utilities with non-return systems.

導師解析

核心概念：充填是無菌製程的最高風險環節

充填是產品在無菌過濾後唯一暴露在開放環境中的操作。這就是為什麼：

  • 必須在 ISO 5/Grade A 環境下進行
  • 必須使用 RABS 或隔離器
  • 必須最大限度減少人員介入
  • 任何中斷（intervention）都必須被記錄和評估

重點提示：三種充填方式的比較

充填方式 原理 適用場景
活塞泵 固定容積衝程 高精度、大多數液體產品
時間-壓力系統 定壓定時計量 黏度穩定的產品
蠕動泵 軟管擠壓 單次使用路徑、生物製劑

無論哪種方式，都建議配備自動秤重反饋系統（check-weighing），實現充填量的即時監控和調整。

設計要點清單：充填機的 16 項關鍵考量

本節列出的充填機設計要求可歸納為五大原則：

  1. 隔離原則：RABS/隔離器隔離人員、手套埠操作、門互鎖
  2. 可清潔原則：無縫隙、無螺紋連接、316L 不鏽鋼、可拆卸高壓滅菌
  3. 單向氣流原則：最佳 UAF 氣流模式、微粒排出設計
  4. 快速換線原則：免工具設計、最少組裝步驟、預滅菌組件
  5. 製程控制原則：IPC 取樣不中斷、秤重系統、氮氣置換

比喻說明：充填機像手術室中的手術台

充填機所在的 ISO 5 環境就像手術室裡的無菌區：手術刀（充填針）、手術台（充填工位）和所有接觸組織的器械（產品接觸面）都必須滅菌。外科醫生透過手套操作（glove port），護士從外部傳遞器械（barrier transfer），整個過程中空調系統維持正壓和單向氣流以排除微粒。

凍乾產品的膠塞半插設計

凍乾產品的膠塞在充填後不會完全壓入瓶口，而是「半插」（partially seated）。這是因為凍乾過程中，溶劑需要從瓶口逸出（昇華和解吸階段）。凍乾完成後，凍乾機的層板下壓將膠塞完全壓入瓶中。

從充填機到凍乾機之間，這些半插膠塞的瓶子必須全程在 ISO 5/Grade A 環境下受保護——這是設施佈局設計的關鍵考量。

Blow, Fill, and Seal (BFS) Technology / 吹瓶-充填-密封技術

Original Text

An alternative technique to filling in a pre-formed container is to use BFS technology. BFS technology is the integration of plastic blow molding and aseptic filling on a single machine where the container is created by the machine just prior to filling. Use of a BFS process generally replaces the steps for container and stopper preparation.

The BFS process consists of the following steps:

  1. Dry heat extrusion of plastic into a parison (i.e., molten tube)
  2. Forming of the container in the mold
  3. Filling the container with product
  4. Insertion of a pre-sterilized component, if applicable
  5. Sealing the container
  6. Trimming the container and conveyance to the inspection area

Advantages of BFS:

  • Exposure of container to environment is very short compared to conventional filling
  • Capable of continuously filling for extended durations (>120 hours) for large batch sizes
  • Reduced requirements for adjacent manufacturing support space
  • Fully automated with restricted access design, minimizing operator presence
  • Wide variety of container designs and sizes (0.25 mL to 1 L or larger)

Limitations and special considerations:

  • May not be compatible with heat or oxygen sensitive products. Plastic extrusion temperatures generally exceed 150°C and most plastics are gas permeable.
  • Requires special operator training.
  • Output speed slower than conventional fill lines.
  • Risk of leachables from plastic and/or secondary packaging materials.

Two machine design types:

  • Open Parison (Shuttle Type): Molds move between parison formation zone and aseptic fill zone. Hot or ultrasonic knife cuts parison each cycle. More flexible for product changeover, suited for smaller batches, only type capable of component insertion.
  • Closed Parison (Rotary/Reciprocating): Molds indexed beneath parison formation zone. Filling nozzles integrated with parison head. Higher output capacity. Larger footprint. Significantly reduced plastic waste stream.

Facility design considerations for BFS:

  • Ventilation must cope with particles generated by the filling machine and heat load from extrusion area. A grey side/white side approach may improve mechanical access and heat distribution.
  • Multiple plastic types available (low/high density polyethylene, polypropylene). Polypropylene and special grades of polyethylene amenable to terminal sterilization.
  • Extrusion process should be evaluated for sterilizing polymer and reducing endotoxin.
  • Best suited for high volume products with large batch sizes and long filling durations.
  • Continuous waste stream of plastic needs to be transferred out of manufacturing space.
  • Storage space for raw plastic granulate should be allocated.
  • Care should be taken to ensure upstream process does not promote microbiological growth in bulk product, as fill duration is usually longer than ordinary filling machines.
  • Sealed containers should be leak tested following deflashing (removal of excess plastic). Typical technology: vacuum decay and high voltage. Some markets require 100% testing.
  • Consideration for downstream processing needs (ampoule separation, overwrapping).

導師解析

核心概念：BFS 一體成型的革命性設計

BFS 技術將三個傳統上分離的步驟整合在一台機器上：

  1. 吹瓶（Blow）：塑膠粒經高溫擠出成為管狀（parison），再吹模成型為容器
  2. 充填（Fill）：成型後立即充填產品
  3. 密封（Seal）：充填後立即熱封密封

整個過程中，容器暴露在環境中的時間極短，大幅降低污染風險。

重點提示：兩種 BFS 機型的比較

特性 開放式（Shuttle） 封閉式（Rotary）
設計 模具在型胚區和充填區之間移動 模具在型胚區下方旋轉
產能 較低 較高（無穿梭時間）
換線彈性 高，適合小批量 較低
組件插入 可以 不可以
塑膠廢料 較多 顯著減少
佔地面積 較小 較大（需更高樓高）

比喻說明：BFS 像自動包裝冰棒的機器

傳統充填像是先買好空瓶、洗瓶、消毒、再灌裝液體、再蓋蓋子——每一步都有污染風險。BFS 就像自動冰棒包裝機：塑膠管加熱融化、吹成容器形狀、立刻灌入液體、瞬間熱封——從無到有一氣呵成，容器幾乎沒有機會接觸外界環境。

設施設計挑戰：BFS 的「灰區/白區」概念

BFS 機器的擠出區產生大量熱量和塑膠微粒。解決方案是將機器分為兩側：

  • 白區（White Side）：充填和成型區域，維持潔淨環境
  • 灰區（Grey Side）：機械系統區域，便於維護和散熱

這種設計透過實體牆將兩個區域分隔，既保護了無菌充填區，又方便了設備維護。HVAC 系統必須能夠應對擠出區產生的巨大熱負荷。

CDMO 實務：BFS 的定位

BFS 最適合大批量、長時間連續充填的產品（如眼藥水、吸入劑、小容量注射液）。對 CDMO 而言：

  • 高初期投資但營運成本低
  • 免除容器和膠塞準備的整條生產線
  • 需要注意可萃取物/浸出物（E&L）的評估
  • 不適合需要凍乾的產品或對溫度/氧氣極敏感的生物製劑
3.2.5 Lyophilization / 凍乾（冷凍乾燥）

Original Text

The lyophilization process, also known as freeze drying, is completed to enhance product stability in the dry state.

Lyophilization consists of three distinct phases:

  1. Freezing
  2. Sublimation or primary drying
  3. Desorption or secondary drying

The total sequence of operations (15 steps):

  1. Pre-use cleaning of chamber
  2. Sterilization of chamber
  3. Shelf cooling
  4. System leak testing
  5. Loading
  6. Freezing
  7. Sublimation/primary drying
  8. Desorption/secondary drying
  9. Backfill
  10. Stoppering
  11. Aeration
  12. Unloading
  13. Defrosting
  14. Post-use cleaning/CIP
  15. Filter integrity testing

Loading Systems: Systems vary from manual tray-by-tray loading, through semi-automatic loading, to fully automatic loading (with simultaneous insertion of pre-loaded group of trays or sequential layer-by-layer loading with automatic tray lifter). Automated loading devices represent the method of choice. The system should ensure that the ISO 5/Grade A environment where partially stoppered vials are exposed is fully protected from the surrounding cleanroom. Barrier technology should be considered for operator segregation during loading.

Selection of loading/unloading method should consider:

  • Type of barrier technology selected
  • Lyophilizer/batch size
  • Lyophilizer configuration (single side load/unload or pass-through type)
  • Number of lyophilizers to be installed
  • Loading with frames or frameless loading

Lyophilizer specification data requirements:

  • Ability to effectively clean and sterilize the chamber and condenser on a routine basis
  • Detailed process cycle for each product: product type, density, eutectic/glass transition point, time and temperatures for all phases, vacuum level, chamber stoppering pressure, and vapor flux
  • Product container data (size, filling quantity)
  • Maximum ice load/condenser capacity, temperature, and surface area
  • Freezing rate of the product
  • System leak rate (typically validated via aseptic process simulation studies)
  • Batch size

A lyophilizer may be the bottleneck of the production chain, due to the extended time required for freeze drying. Selection of lyophilizer size, based on batch sizes, product mix, and expected line usage, is fundamental to overall production yield. Two or more freeze dryers may be required to fully utilize the capacity of a filling line.

Care should be taken to optimize the lyophilization cycle early in the development process, due to the large impact on facility production capacity.

Additional specification data:

  • Type of refrigeration system (compressors or liquid nitrogen)
  • Process control system and parameters to be controlled/monitored
  • Number and type of doors (single or pass-through)
  • Door closing system
  • Cleaning and sterilization requirements
  • Use of a capper dedicated to lyophilizer products, to avoid impact on processing of liquid products

Cleaning and Sterilization: Required between each batch. Most common medium is moist heat.

Advantages of moist heat: easily available, easy to monitor, highly penetrating, easy to distribute uniformly, non-hazardous if traces remain.

Disadvantages of moist heat: need to reach high temperatures and pressures with saturated steam, time required for cooling, potential thermo-mechanical stress on system leak rate, potential effect of residual moisture on leak testing.

VHP for lyophilizer decontamination has been applied and validated for small and commercial scale units. Particularly suitable for laboratory scale units or retrofit to older commercial units not pressure rated. To date, VHP decontamination has seen limited commercial scale application.

All moving parts should be correctly sterilized with sterility maintained during all process steps (e.g., sterile bellows enclosing stoppering pistons), including effective sterilization of shelf support columns, rams, gas lines, condenser, and the system used to seal containers.

The lyophilizer construction will be in a state of mechanical stress due to the wide range of temperatures and pressures during operation. This may increase the risk of leakage over time, emphasizing the importance of an effective preventative maintenance and equipment inspection program.

導師解析

核心概念：凍乾的三階段與 15 步操作序列

凍乾的核心目的是提高產品在乾燥狀態下的穩定性。三個關鍵階段：

  1. 冷凍（Freezing）：將產品降溫至共熔點/玻璃轉化溫度以下，使水分結冰
  2. 昇華/初級乾燥（Primary Drying）：在真空條件下，冰直接昇華為水蒸氣，被冷凝器捕捉
  3. 解吸/二級乾燥（Secondary Drying）：移除結合水，進一步降低含水量至目標值

但完整的操作序列遠不止這三步——從清洗、滅菌、冷卻、洩漏測試到裝載、凍乾、出料、除霜、清洗，共 15 個步驟。

重點提示：凍乾機是產線瓶頸

凍乾是整條無菌充填線中最耗時的環節，一個凍乾週期可能需要 24–72 小時甚至更長。因此：

  • 凍乾機大小的選擇直接決定整條產線的產出效率
  • 通常需要 2 台或更多凍乾機來匹配一條充填線的產能
  • 計算產能時必須考慮完整週期時間（含清洗、滅菌、除霜、維護保養）
  • 凍乾製程優化應在開發早期完成，因為對產能影響巨大

比喻說明：凍乾機像太空級冷凍乾燥機

想像一台巨型太空食品乾燥機：先將食物急速冷凍，然後在極低壓的真空中，冰晶不經過液態直接「蒸發」（昇華）。最後的產品像是一塊海綿狀的乾燥餅，加水後可以恢復原狀。不同的是，製藥用凍乾機必須在無菌條件下操作，而且每批之間必須完全清洗和滅菌。

進出料系統：從手動到全自動的演進

凍乾機的裝載（loading）和卸載（unloading）是無菌製程中的高風險環節：

  • 手動裝載：操作員逐盤放入，風險最高
  • 半自動裝載：部分自動化，減少人為操作
  • 全自動裝載：自動托盤預裝 + 同步送入，或逐層堆疊 + 自動升降器

全自動系統是首選，尤其當同一套自動化系統能服務多台凍乾機時，產能利用率最高。

凍乾機規格選定的關鍵參數

正確規格化凍乾機需要的製程數據包括：

參數類別 具體項目
產品特性 類型、密度、共熔點/玻璃轉化溫度、水蒸氣通量
製程參數 各階段溫度與時間、真空度、壓封壓力
容器數據 容器尺寸、充填量
冷凝器 最大冰負荷/容量、溫度、表面積
系統完整性 系統洩漏率（經無菌模擬驗證）
產能 批量大小、產品組合

重點提示：凍乾機的清洗與滅菌挑戰

濕熱滅菌（蒸汽）是最常用的方法，但有其挑戰：

  • 需要達到高溫高壓——凍乾機的設計必須能承受壓力容器等級
  • 冷卻耗時——增加了批間轉換時間
  • 熱膨脹-收縮的機械應力可能導致密封墊劣化，增加洩漏風險
  • 殘留水分可能影響後續的洩漏率測試

VHP 除污是新興替代方案，特別適合無法承受壓力的舊式設備或小型實驗室設備，但商業規模應用仍然有限。

CDMO 實務：凍乾機是最昂貴的單一設備

對 CDMO 而言，凍乾機的投資決策是最具策略性的設備選擇之一：

  • 一台商業級凍乾機的成本可達數百萬美元
  • 配置錯誤（太大或太小）直接影響數年的營收
  • Pass-through 設計（穿透式）可分隔裝載側和卸載側的潔淨等級
  • 自動裝卸系統的投資回收期通常在 2–3 年內
  • 凍乾週期優化對產能影響極大——縮短 10% 的週期時間可能增加 10% 以上的年產能

練習思考

  1. 一條充填線的充填速度是每小時 10,000 瓶，凍乾週期需要 48 小時，每台凍乾機可裝載 50,000 瓶。若要維持充填線不停機，至少需要幾台凍乾機？
  2. 為什麼 ISPE 建議在開發早期就優化凍乾製程？這與設施設計有什麼關係？
  3. VHP 除污為什麼特別適合舊式凍乾機？它的限制是什麼？
3.2.6 Container Closure Systems / 容器密封系統

Original Text

There are various container closure systems routinely used for small volume parenteral drugs and biologics, including ampoules, BFS containers, vials, syringes and cartridges. These containers are sealed either by fusion (ampoules and BFS containers) or through use of an elastomeric closure (syringes, cartridges, and vials). In the case of vials or cartridges, the elastomeric seal may be combined with an aluminum or plastic cap/crimp seal for final package sealing.

The objective of any container/closure system is to maintain product quality attributes of sterility and physiochemical stability through expiry. Container closure systems should be fully validated to ensure that these objectives are met; that is, the container closure system has the necessary Container Closure Integrity (CCI).

Development and subsequent production of primary packaging having adequate CCI requires a lifecycle approach that includes the following phases:

  • Package development, package processing and assembly validation
  • Product manufacturing
  • Commercial product shelf life stability assessments

Package development includes selection of appropriate and compatible primary packaging components that, when assembled or sealed under defined operating conditions, provide the necessary CCI. CPPs, as required to ensure CCI, should be defined.

In the product manufacturing phase, CPPs are measured and controlled to ensure consistent product quality. Several test methods are suitable for in-process (statistical) or 100% basis inspection.

In the final lifecycle phase, commercial product shelf life stability assessments are performed to monitor package performance over the shelf life of the product.

Note: Each specific container/closure system behaves differently and should be validated separately. For example, independent testing should be performed for each combination of two different stoppers (different formulation and/or configuration) with a single vial.

Containers closed by fusion (ampoules and BFS containers) comprise less complex packaging systems since a single material is used to form and seal the container.

Regulatory requirements for inspection:

  • EU Annex 1: "Containers closed by fusion, e.g., glass or plastic ampoules should be subject to 100% integrity testing. Samples of other containers should be checked for integrity according to appropriate procedures." Also: "Containers sealed under vacuum should be tested for maintenance of that vacuum after an appropriate, pre-determined period."
  • US FDA: "A container closure system that permits penetration of microorganisms is unsuitable for a sterile product. Any damaged or defective units should be detected, and removed, during inspection of the final sealed product."

導師解析

核心概念：容器密封完整性（CCI）生命週期

CCI 不是一次性的測試，而是一個貫穿產品生命週期的驗證體系：

  1. 開發階段：選擇相容的容器/膠塞/鋁蓋組合，定義 CPP，進行初始 CCI 測試
  2. 生產階段：監控和控制 CPP（如壓蓋力、膠塞壓縮量），進行統計取樣或 100% 檢查
  3. 安定性階段：長期穩定性試驗中監控密封完整性是否隨時間劣化

每一種容器/膠塞組合都需要獨立驗證——不同的膠塞配方或規格搭配同一種瓶子，CCI 表現可能完全不同。

重點提示：密封方式的兩大類別

  • 熔合密封（Fusion）：安瓿瓶和 BFS 容器——單一材料成型密封，系統較簡單，但 EU Annex 1 要求 100% 完整性檢查
  • 彈性體密封（Elastomeric）：西林瓶、預填充注射器、卡式瓶——使用膠塞 + 鋁蓋壓封，涉及多個組件的介面，系統較複雜

美國 FDA 和歐盟 EMA 均認為：西林瓶在鋁蓋壓封完成前，密封系統不算完整。這一立場直接影響了壓蓋區的環境等級要求。

比喻說明：CCI 如同保溫瓶的密封

容器密封完整性就像保溫瓶的蓋子——不管保溫瓶做得多好，如果蓋子沒蓋緊，熱量就會散失（無菌性喪失）。CCI 的生命週期方法就是確保：蓋子選得對（開發）、每次都蓋好（生產）、放久了也不會鬆（穩定性）。

導師解析

核心概念：CCIT 方法的三層應用

CCI 測試方法按應用場景分為三層：

  1. 開發階段測試：所有方法都適用——建立密封完整性的基線數據和接受標準
  2. 製程中測試：只有非破壞性方法（RSF 例外）適用——取樣監控
  3. 100% 在線檢查：僅少數非破壞性方法（HVLD、HSA、真空/壓力衰減、氣體電離）適用——全數檢查

重點提示：關鍵 CCIT 方法解析

PAT（製程分析技術）的應用趨勢：透過持續監控 CPP 來降低或取消抽樣檢查需求。

實務選擇指南

選擇 CCIT 方法時需考慮：

  • 容器類型（安瓿、西林瓶、預充填注射器各適合不同方法）
  • 產品特性（導電性、頂空要求、穩定性）
  • 設備通量（是否需要匹配充填線速度）
  • 法規市場要求（部分市場要求 100% 檢查）
  • 洩漏偵測靈敏度（不同方法的偵測下限不同）
Vial Capping/Crimping / 西林瓶壓蓋/壓封

Original Text

For vials, two cases frequently arise where transfer of the container and final sealing is performed:

  • Liquid vials: Aseptically filled and fully stoppered in ISO 5/Grade A environment, subsequently transferred out of aseptic zone for capping.
  • Lyophilized vials: Aseptically filled and partially stoppered on filling equipment, transferred to lyophilizer and loaded onto shelves. After lyophilization, containers are fully stoppered by collapsing shelves, then unloaded for capping.

Application of the crimp seal can be performed in the aseptic zone using sterilized caps or as a clean operation outside the aseptic core.

The US FDA and EMA do not consider the container closure system to be fully integral until the application of the crimp seal is complete. EU Annex 1 states: "The container closure system for aseptically filled vials is not fully integral until the aluminum cap has been crimped into place on the stoppered vial."

Therefore, additional measures to safeguard product sterility are required if capping is performed outside the aseptic zone:

  • Use of LP/GAAS (Laminar Protection / Grade A Air Supply) to safeguard the product from exit of aseptic zone until crimp seal is complete.
  • Use of raised/missing stopper detection systems for inspection prior to crimp seal. Non-conforming containers should be rejected prior to capping.

Raised/missing stopper detection systems: Can be laser or camera-based. Should detect skewed stoppers and evenly lifted stoppers by inspecting from at least 2 directions (typically 90° apart). Maximum allowable gap should be determined for each container/closure combination using suitable method (e.g., helium leak testing, HSA). More sensitive systems detect and measure the gap between container neck flange and bottom of stopper with 0.1mm resolution.

Recent PAT developments for capping:

  • Pneumatic application of pre-capping compression (centrally adjustable, continuous monitoring and alarming)
  • Compressive force confirmation via strain gauge
  • Monitoring of container rotation during capping (to detect slip)
  • Residual seal force testing (destructive for flip-off type seals)
  • Visual crimp inspection via camera-based systems (real-time quality monitoring)

Aluminum seal application steps:

  1. Seal placement on container by neck draw-off (cap chute) or pick-and-place mechanism
  2. Pre-sealing stopper compression via pneumatic or spring-loaded spindle
  3. Roll bottom edge of seal under container neck flange to maintain compressive force

Crimping mechanisms:

  • Jaw type crimping (smaller seals, lower speed)
  • Container rotation against fixed outer rail or inner disk
  • Container rotation about fixed idle roller(s)

For multi-head capping machines, controls should detect consecutive faults on a per-spindle basis. Sample sets should correspond to a defined capping spindle sequence.

During capping, mechanical working of the seal generates particulates. Proper airflow design should provide unidirectional flow to sweep particles away from critical zones. Particle generation may also cause cap discoloration, particularly with white flip seals.

導師解析

核心概念：壓蓋前的密封完整性風險

法規立場非常明確：西林瓶在鋁蓋壓封完成前，不被視為密封完整。這意味著：

  • 從充填區到壓蓋區之間的轉運過程中，產品處於「未完全密封」狀態
  • 必須使用 LP/GAAS（層流保護/Grade A 空氣供應）保護產品
  • 必須在壓蓋前進行膠塞偵測（raised/missing stopper detection）
  • 不合格品必須在壓蓋前被剔除

重點提示：膠塞偵測系統的精度要求

膠塞偵測系統必須能夠偵測：

  • 歪斜膠塞（Skewed stopper）：一側有縫隙、另一側正常
  • 整體上浮膠塞（Evenly lifted stopper）：膠塞均勻抬高
  • 必須從至少 2 個方向（通常 90° 夾角）進行檢查
  • 最靈敏的系統可偵測到 0.1mm 的間隙

每種容器/膠塞組合的「最大允許間隙」都必須透過適當方法（如氦氣洩漏測試、HSA）獨立確定。

壓蓋 PAT 技術五大進展

  1. 氣動預壓縮：取代彈簧，可集中調整、持續監控和警報
  2. 應變計確認：壓蓋過程中即時確認壓縮力
  3. 旋轉監測：偵測壓封時容器是否打滑（導致密封不良）
  4. 殘留密封力（RSF）測試：監控壓封後的殘餘壓縮力
  5. 視覺壓封檢查：攝像機即時檢查壓封外觀品質

這些 PAT 應用正在將壓蓋從「事後檢查」轉變為「即時控制」的製程。

重點提示：多頭壓蓋機的按主軸監控

多頭壓蓋機中，每個壓蓋頭（spindle）可能產生不同的缺陷模式。因此：

  • 控制系統必須能夠按單個主軸追蹤連續缺陷（如主軸 #1 連續出現 3 次缺陷）
  • 取樣組必須對應定義的主軸順序（如 1–12 號頭依序取樣）
  • 這與充填機按單個充填頭取樣的邏輯相同

比喻說明：壓蓋如同開瓶的逆向操作

想像用開瓶器開啤酒——壓蓋就是這個動作的逆向過程。鋁蓋被放在膠塞上方，先施加向下壓力確保膠塞緊貼瓶口，然後滾輪將鋁蓋邊緣捲入瓶頸凸緣下方鎖定。如果壓蓋時瓶子打滑（rotation），就像用開瓶器時瓶蓋歪了——密封就會失敗。

微粒管理：壓蓋區的特殊挑戰

壓蓋過程中金屬碾壓會產生大量微粒。如果壓蓋在 ISO 5/Grade A 環境內進行，微粒管理尤為重要：

  • 單向氣流設計必須將微粒從關鍵區域吹走
  • 需要調整風速、回風口位置，可能使用 RABS/隔離器內部隔板
  • 白色翻蓋特別容易因微粒而變色——需考慮蓋子顏色選擇和分選碗清潔頻率

練習思考

  1. 為什麼 EU Annex 1 認為西林瓶在壓蓋完成前不算「密封完整」？如果膠塞已經完全壓入，為什麼還不夠？
  2. 一台 12 頭壓蓋機中，3 號和 7 號頭各出現了 2 次連續缺陷。根據「按主軸監控」原則，你會採取什麼行動？
  3. 比較在無菌區內壓蓋和在無菌區外壓蓋的優缺點——各需要哪些額外措施？
