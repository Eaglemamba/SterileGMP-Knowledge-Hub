2.6

Area Classification Definitions

Harmonized designations, particle limits, microbial limits — p27–p30

Harmonized Classification System

The area classification nomenclature in this Guide uses established terminology required by many regulatory agencies worldwide. It allows the user to present their facility design using the terminology and requirements with which regulators are familiar.

There are key criteria that define the classification of a process area:

  • Whether the specification relates to the "in operation", "at rest", or both conditions
  • Microbiological limits
  • Total air particulate limits

The airborne particle size limits should be clearly defined when classifying an area by airborne particles. The assessment technique and microbial limits should be clearly defined when classifying the microbiological cleanliness of an area.

When reference is made to Cleanliness Grades A through D, note that these integrate "at rest" and "in operation" conditions with two particle sizes (0.5 µm and 5.0 µm) and microbial levels.

Although there are many similarities, regulatory requirements are not 100% consistent globally. Regulated companies should define requirements that account for the criteria specified for all markets where their products will be sold.

Table 2.2 shows harmonized designations and their correlation with the Class/Grade, particle size and count, and microbial limits of US FDA, EU Annex 1, and PIC/S guidelines. These three guidelines were chosen because many guidance documents written by agencies around the world are either based on the US FDA or EU Annex 1 or have adopted PIC/S as a global standard.

調和分級系統

核心概念：三大分級體系對照

全球無菌製造有三大分級體系，實務上必須熟悉並交叉對照：

  • EU/PIC/S：Grade A / B / C / D（A/B/C/D級潔淨區）
  • ISO 14644-1：ISO 5 / 7 / 8（ISO 5/7/8級）
  • 舊版 US FDA：Class 100 / 10,000 / 100,000

重點提示：靜態 vs 動態的關鍵區別

靜態（At Rest）：設備安裝完成、HVAC運作中，但無人員在場、無生產活動。

動態（In Operation）：正常生產運作中，人員在場、設備運轉。

EU Annex 1 定義兩種條件下的粒子限值；US FDA 則以動態條件為主。這是歐美法規的核心差異。

比喻說明：餐廳衛生檢查

想像衛生局檢查餐廳：「靜態」就像開店前檢查廚房是否乾淨；「動態」則是營業高峰期檢查，看廚師操作中能否維持衛生標準。無菌製造的「動態」標準永遠比「靜態」更難達成，因為人員是最大的污染源。

Key Notes on Harmonization

  • The US FDA does not have an area classification equivalent to Grade D. Grade D is ISO 8 "at rest" only, and US FDA classifications are based on "in operation" conditions.
  • The "Controlled Not Classified" (CNC) designation is increasingly popular. It originated in biologics facilities for spaces that are access controlled and cleaner than general HVAC areas, but for which no formal classification claim is made.
  • A change of grade is typically associated with a change in the status of personnel or materials—usually achieved through clothing changes, cleaning/decontamination processes, or physical separation (RABS, isolators, UAF hoods).
  • For Grade A, there is no ISO 14644-1 classification for particles ≥5.0 µm at or below ISO 5. The EU Annex 1 limit of 20 particles/m³ has no ISO equivalent.

調和重點補充

實務陷阱：Grade D 的歐美差異

US FDA 沒有 Grade D 對應等級。當設計面向全球市場的設施時，Grade D 製造區可能需要以 ISO 8「動態」呈報給FDA。這對設施設計有直接影響：原本在歐洲可以用 Grade D 的工序，面向美國市場時可能需要升級到 ISO 8/Grade C。

CNC：控制但不分級區域

CNC（Controlled Not Classified）是近年越來越流行的概念，尤其在生物製品設施。這些區域有門禁管控、比一般HVAC更乾淨，但不做正式的潔淨等級聲明。可以用於檢驗、終端滅菌等支援區域。

2.6.3

Area Classification for Typical Process Stages

Environmental classifications per process step — p30–p33

Bioburden Control Strategy

The control of bioburden in aseptic processing and terminal sterilization should minimize the bioburden challenge to the sterilization process and ensure appropriate control of endotoxins that will not be inactivated or removed by the sterilization process.

Pre-sterilization bioburdens are controlled by deployment of an appropriately controlled immediate and surrounding process environment. For example, the air classification at the filling stage of terminally sterilized products may be controlled to a lesser standard than required for aseptic processing.

Different process steps normally require different environmental classifications. It is normal practice to cascade air quality from higher quality levels to lower quality levels (e.g., from ISO 5/Grade A critical areas to ISO 7/Grade B or ISO 8/Grade C areas).

Facilities designed for potent or biological products (viral or spore forming) may be required to place production areas within environments that are negative to the surrounding areas. A risk assessment should determine the risk of environmental exposure versus the risk of contaminating the product.

生物負荷控制策略

空氣質量級聯原則

無菌製造設施的空氣質量應從高到低級聯：

  • 核心區（ISO 5/Grade A）→ 背景環境（ISO 7/Grade B）→ 支援區（ISO 8/Grade C）→ 一般控制區（Grade D/CNC）

每一層級聯都通過壓差、氣流方向和物理屏障來維持。

特殊情況：高活性/生物製品的壓差反轉

一般無菌製造是正壓（內高外低）保護產品，但高活性藥物或病毒製品可能需要負壓（內低外高）以保護人員和外部環境。這需要風險評估來平衡產品保護與操作人員安全。

表格重點解讀

比喻：開放式 vs 封閉式製程的保護等級

想像在戶外煉菜（開放製程）與在密封壓力鍋內煉菜（封閉製程）的差別。戶外需要更嚴格的防污染措施（遮蔽、潔淨環境），而壓力鍋本身就是屏障。同理，隔離器內的 ISO 5 環境允許背景降到 ISO 8/Grade C。

LP/GAAS 實務應用

LP/GAAS（局部保護/A級送風）是一種彈性的保護措施，不是完整的 ISO 5 環境，但提供過濾氣流來保護產品暴露點。常見於等級較低的區域（如 Grade C/D）中的關鍵操作點。

2.7

Hazard and Operator Protection

Containment, potent compounds, risk vs hazard — p33

Operator Protection Requirements

In addition to the design requirements for product protection, it is critical that engineers consider protection of the operator and the room environment, particularly if the product is potentially harmful if inhaled.

The key requirement for operator protection is to understand the difference between hazard and risk. The potential risk to the operator should be understood and appropriate mitigation to manage the level of risk should be defined.

A risk analysis should be performed to assess the potential for harm and an operator protection plan should be defined. Engineered approaches are preferred, because they typically remove the operator from the harmful situation. Examples include:

  • Utilizing containment isolators for potent compounds
  • Automating the movement of open containers, such as in lyophilization loading

The use of Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) should be employed only in breach situations, or as a secondary means of protection.

操作人員防護要求

危害（Hazard）vs 風險（Risk）

危害：物質本身的固有危險性（如高活性藥物的毒性）。

風險：危害造成傷害的可能性，考慮暴露途徑和防護措施。

工程控制（如隔離器、自動化）優於 PPE，因為工程控制從根源消除暴露，而 PPE 只是最後一道防線。

比喻：防護階層

就像處理危險化學品：最好的做法是封閉系統（工程控制），其次是通風櫥（管理控制），PPE 是最後手段。無菌製造中，封閉式隔離器同時保護產品和操作人員。

思考練習

您的設施要生產一種高活性藥物（OEL < 1 µg/m³）的無菌注射劑。如何同時滿足產品保護（正壓）和操作人員保護（負壓）的衝突需求？

  • 提示：考慮隔離器 + 氣鎖設計
2.8

Open/Closed Processing

Open vs closed process categories, environmental requirements — p33–p35

2.8.1 Open Processing / 開放製程

Definition and Examples

Open processing is a process condition when the product, materials, or container/closure surfaces are exposed to the immediate process environment at a stage/time when such exposure could influence the quality or purity of the product.

Examples of open processes include:

  • Open cleaned equipment post-sterilization
  • Loading and unloading an item or process equipment
  • Aseptic assembly of process equipment
  • Filling open product containers such as ampoules, vials, or syringes
  • Transporting a partially secure vial (uncapped stoppered vial)

When open aseptic processing is employed, there is a requirement to control the immediate and surrounding process environment (typically to ISO 5/Grade A).

Open Aseptic Processing Using RABS

Exposed product and containers should be protected under UAF, to maintain at least an ISO 5/Grade A environment with a background room classification of ISO 7/Grade B. Appropriate materials, equipment, and services should enter the ISO 5/Grade A and ISO 7/Grade B environments using appropriate decontamination steps. Personnel should be gowned appropriately.

Open Aseptic Processing Using Isolators

Isolators are decontaminated internally, typically using VHP or similar. No access is permitted other than via controlled alpha-beta docking ports and integrity-tested glove ports. The background room for a globally compliant facility should be ISO 8/Grade C. A Grade D environment classified as ISO 8 "in operation" as well as "at rest" is also possible.

定義與範例

開放製程的核心定義

當產品、物料或容器/封蓋表面暴露於即時製程環境，且此暴露可能影響產品品質或純度時，即為開放製程。開放製程需要更高的環境分級保護。

比喻：手術室與手術機器人

RABS 像是傳統手術室：外科醫師穿戴無菌衣進入，外部環境必須非常乾淨（ISO 7/Grade B）。隔離器像是手術機器人：醫師在外面遠端操控，外部環境要求可以降低（ISO 8/Grade C）。

RABS vs 隔離器的背景環境差異

項目 RABS 隔離器
關鍵區（產品暴露） ISO 5/Grade A ISO 5/Grade A
背景環境 ISO 7/Grade B ISO 8/Grade C
人員介入 有限開門介入 僅透過手套口
滅菌方式 手動清潔/消毒 自動VHP滅菌

2.8.2 Closed Processing / 封閉製程

Definition and Examples

Closed processing is a process condition when the product, materials, critical components, or container/closure surfaces are contained and separated from the immediate process environment within closed/sealed process equipment or enclosure.

Examples of closed processes include:

  • Closed sterile (post-sterilized-in-place) vessels
  • Closed material additions through canisters and alpha-beta ports
  • Closed sterilized (post-sterilized-in-place) pipework transporting product or materials
  • Closed single-use components/containers

When closed processing is employed, minimal controls are required for the immediate processing environment, provided that the integrity of the system is ensured through equipment design and operation and that there is appropriate monitoring to provide evidence for maintained integrity. A minimum of Grade D (qualified as ISO 8 "in operation" for US FDA) should be considered for such areas.

定義與範例

封閉製程的核心優勢

封閉製程的最大優點是大幅降低對環境的依賴：

  • 背景環境可降至 Grade D（最低要求）
  • 減少人員着裝要求
  • 降低 HVAC 系統建造和營運成本
  • 減少環境監測負擔

但前提是：系統完整性必須通過設計和監控來證明。

實務應用：開放 vs 封閉決策流程

在設計新製程時，報問自己：

  1. 產品在此步驟是否暴露於環境？
  2. 能否通過工程手段實現封閉？
  3. 封閉系統的完整性能否被監控與證明？

如果三個問題的答案都是「是」，封閉製程是較佳選擇；否則需要開放製程的高等級環境保護。

2.9

Integrated Facility Design

Zoning, material/personnel flow, segregation, lifecycle cost — p35–p36

Manufacturing Process Flow

The manufacturing process includes a sequence of manufacturing and work-in-progress storage steps en route to the creation of the finished product. This embraces unit process operations such as:

  • Weighing of components
  • Milling, mixing, formulation
  • Filtering
  • Transport of sterilized materials and components
  • Filling into containers
  • Transport of partially stoppered vials
  • Lyophilization
  • BFS (Blow-Fill-Seal)
  • Sterilizing, sealing, coding

The manufacturing process is normally supported by other functions close by: utilities, warehousing, inspection areas, offices, and laboratories.

Contamination Control Through Design

The design of each element of the manufacturing facility should contribute to minimizing product contamination risk. Contamination may be minimized by methods such as:

  • A clothing changing regime for personnel (separate gowning and de-gowning)
  • Pre-treatment of components and container/closures
  • Air filtration, airflow direction, and room pressurization
  • Personnel flow and material flow control

For personnel and materials to move from one area to another while maintaining protection for the product, engineers should consider the facility as a whole rather than as isolated parts.

Facility up-time should be optimized and it should be possible to perform maintenance and repair efficiently, especially if complex technology is employed—for example, by minimizing the need for interventions into the aseptic area.

The lifecycle cost of facilities, and not just the initial cost, should be considered. A higher initial cost using better materials may mean less operating and maintenance costs, resulting in a lower lifecycle cost.

製造製程流程與整合設計

整合設計的四大核心原則

  1. 人流與物流分離：人員和物料應有獨立的進入路徑，避免交叉污染。
  2. 潔淨度級聯：從低潔淨區到高潔淨區，逐步提升，每一層都有相應的控制措施。
  3. 設施可用性最佳化：維護區域應能夠在不影響無菌區域的情況下進入。
  4. 生命週期成本考量：不能只看初始建造成本，要考慮長期營運、維護、能源等總成本。

比喻：機場的動線設計

無菌製造設施的人流/物流設計就像機場：出境與入境分離、行李與乘客走不同通道、安檢區作為級聯關卡。同理，潔淨物料和髒污物料絕不應共用通道，人員進入無菌區前必須經過更衣階段（就像安檢）。

生命週期成本實例

一個使用較貴但耐久的316L不鏽鋼表面的設施，初始投資可能高出20-30%，但由於減少清潔、維修和停機時間，10年總成本可能反而更低。ISPE強調：不要只比較建造成本，要比較生命週期總成本。

思考練習：設施動線設計

如果您要設計一個新的無菌灌裝設施，請考慮以下動線：

  1. 原料從倉庫到配製區的路徑？
  2. 滅菌後的容器如何進入灌裝區？
  3. 人員從辦公區到 Grade B 的更衣階段？
  4. 維護人員如何不進入無菌區即可維修設備？
2.10

Other Considerations

Fire protection, emissions, noise, health & safety — p36–p37

Additional Facility Design Considerations

Additional considerations for facility design, which are outside the specific scope of this guide, include:

  • Means of escape — Emergency exit routes must be designed to allow rapid evacuation without compromising cleanroom integrity during normal operations.
  • Fire protection — Fire suppression systems must be compatible with cleanroom environments and pharmaceutical products.
  • Emissions — Air handling and exhaust systems must meet environmental regulations for particulate and chemical emissions.
  • Noise control — HVAC systems and production equipment should meet occupational noise exposure limits.
  • Health and safety — General occupational health and safety requirements must be integrated into the facility design.

其他設計考量

實務觀點：這些「非核心」要素往往是禁區痛點

這些項目雖然不在本指南的主要範圍內，但在實際工程設計中經常造成問題：

  • 消防：潔淨室內的灑水系統是污染源，需要特殊設計（如預作用式系統）。
  • 逃生通道：緊急出口不能使潔淨室壓差系統失效，但必須滿足建築法規。
  • 噪音：HVAC系統特別是高級別HEPA風機的噪音可能超過85dB。

比喻：買房時的「隱藏成本」

就像買房不能只看房價，還要考慮物業費、停車、社區安全等。無菌設施設計也一樣：消防、逃生、噪音控制這些「隱藏」需求如果在設計後期才發現，改造成本會非常高。

Section Summary / 章節總結

Key takeaways from Sections 2.6–2.10

Key Takeaways

  1. Harmonized Classification: Three parallel systems exist globally (EU Grade A-D, ISO 5/7/8, old US Class 100/10K/100K). Facilities serving multiple markets must design to the most conservative requirement across all applicable regulations.
  2. At Rest vs In Operation: EU Annex 1 defines limits for both conditions; US FDA is primarily "in operation." This distinction affects classification strategy, especially for Grade D areas.
  3. CNC Designation: "Controlled Not Classified" areas are increasingly common for support functions where formal classification is not required but access control and basic cleanliness are maintained.
  4. Operator Protection: Engineered controls (containment, isolators, automation) are preferred over PPE. The distinction between hazard and risk is fundamental to designing appropriate protection.
  5. Open vs Closed Processing: Open processes require ISO 5/Grade A at the point of exposure; closed processes can operate with Grade D background. The choice has significant cost and design implications.
  6. Isolator Advantage: Isolators allow background environment reduction from ISO 7/Grade B to ISO 8/Grade C while maintaining ISO 5/Grade A at the critical zone.
  7. Integrated Design: Personnel flow, material flow, zoning, and segregation must be designed holistically. The facility is a system, not a collection of isolated rooms.
  8. Lifecycle Cost: Higher initial investment in quality materials and design may yield lower total cost over the facility lifecycle through reduced maintenance, downtime, and energy consumption.

關鍵要點

  1. 調和分級：三大體系並行（EU Grade A-D、ISO 5/7/8、舊版 US Class）。面向多國市場時，必須採用最嚴格標準。
  2. 靜態與動態：EU 定義兩種條件，US以動態為主。Grade D 在歐美間的差異是設計陷阱。
  3. CNC 區域：控制但不分級，適用於檢驗、終端滅菌等支援區域。
  4. 人員防護：工程控制 > 管理控制 > PPE，這是防護層次的黃金原則。
  5. 開放與封閉：封閉製程可大幅降低背景環境要求，但必須證明系統完整性。
  6. 隔離器優勢：背景從 ISO 7 降至 ISO 8，關鍵區維持 ISO 5。
  7. 整合設計：人流、物流、分區、隔離必須整體考慮，設施是系統而非孤立房間。
  8. 生命週期成本：高品質初始投資可能帶來更低的總擁有成本。
