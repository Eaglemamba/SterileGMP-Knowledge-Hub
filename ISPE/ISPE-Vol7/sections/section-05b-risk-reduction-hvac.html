HVAC and Environmental Control — 概論

Three levels of proximity: Room → Local → Equipment Enclosure | p98-p99

9.6 HVAC and Environmental Control

The key requirement of life science HVAC is to assure a suitable environment for the product and process with regard to temperature, humidity, and most importantly, airborne particulate, molecular, and viable cleanliness. This requirement is in addition to the usual industrial HVAC requirements of making workers comfortable and protecting them from hazardous airborne fumes, vapors, aerosols, and dust.

Often, the need to control hazardous compounds complicates environmental control as cleanliness and containment may require conflicting pressurization schemes.

As product potency increases, the need for control of airborne product/API also increases. The effectiveness of HVAC related contamination controls improve as proximity to the product increases. Three levels of increasing proximity are considered:

  • Room Level HVAC — dilutes airborne contaminants by entrainment in air streams, extracted from the room. Susceptible to behavior, technique, and operational failures.
  • Local Ventilation — "near field" effect by placing exhaust and/or supply close to emission sources. Higher particulate concentration at source enables more efficient filtration or exhaust disposal.
  • Equipment Level Ventilation — e.g., isolator, RABS, vented balance enclosure, or closed equipment. Combines physical barriers with HVAC to control particulate buildup within the enclosure. Closing a process reduces the size of the critical environment, making the surrounding room a support area.

This triumvirate of control technologies can provide significant safety and product protection benefits, individually and in concert. They can safely handle highly hazardous materials with very low OELs.

At HVAC equipment level, removal of undesirable airborne contaminants is achieved through exhaust (disposal) or depth filtration. Filters also serve as an enhanced boundary protecting against unintended backflow or migration of API dust through duct systems upon loss of HVAC. Terminal filters at air inlets and outlets of processing rooms are therefore common.

解說：三層 HVAC 控制概念

三層控制距離原則

HVAC 控制效能與距離成反比。越靠近污染源，控制越有效：

  • Room Level（房間層）：靠大量換氣稀釋，效果依賴人員行為與操作技術，是最脆弱的一層。
  • Local Ventilation（局部層）：LEV（局部排氣通風）在污染源附近直接捕集，比房間換氣更高效，捕集的是高濃度空氣。
  • Equipment Level（設備層）：隔離器、RABS 等將製程完全包覆，把「潔淨區」縮小到最小範圍，周圍環境降為支援區。

比喻：廚房油煙機

把炒鍋油煙想成 API 粉塵。
房間換氣 = 開窗讓風吹散 → 效果差
LEV = 油煙機抽直接在鍋上方 → 效果好
密閉設備 = 在密閉蒸汽爐裡烹調 → 幾乎零逸散

生命科學 HVAC 設計就是讓每一層互補，形成縱深防禦。

重點提示：潔淨度 vs 密閉度的矛盾

高效能藥品廠常見矛盾：
- 產品保護需要正壓（防外部污染進入）
- 人員和環境保護需要負壓（防 API 逸出）
設計時需根據產品毒性（OEL/HBEL）決定哪個優先，並採用氣鎖隔離兩種需求。

Once-Through versus Recirculation

When is 100% exhaust required for hazardous compounds? | p99-p100

9.6.1 Once-Through versus Recirculation

Recirculated HVAC systems offer energy savings and operational cost benefits over once-through systems. The air recirculated from manufacturing areas is often cleaner and much closer to the desired temperature and humidity conditions than fresh air.

However, recirculation of air from powder handling spaces, especially in multiproduct concurrent manufacturing facilities, can convey airborne product particles to other areas and may maintain aerosols in suspension, depending on particle size and room air patterns.

Regulations suggest that if air is to be recirculated, when potentially contaminated with airborne product particles, it should be adequately treated. The definition of adequate treatment is amenable to a risk-based approach, as described in Section 9.6.2.

解說：單通 vs 循環系統的決策邏輯

核心概念

單通系統（Once-Through / 100% Exhaust）：供氣全部來自新鮮外氣，排氣全部排至室外（經處理）。無再循環，交叉污染風險最低，但能耗極高。

循環系統（Recirculation）：部分或全部回風再使用，節能，但需要過濾處理以移除 API 粒子。

法規核心要求

EU GMP Annex 15 及多國 GMP 規定：多品種共用設施中，若空氣循環使用，必須「充分處理」（adequately treated）。「充分」的定義由風險評估決定（見 9.6.2）。

對於高毒性化合物（低 OEL / HBEL），「充分處理」通常意味著：
→ 雙重 HEPA 過濾，或
→ 100% 排氣（完全不循環）

比喻：辦公室空調 vs 化學實驗室抽風

一般辦公室空調把排氣循環再用是合理的（無毒性氣體）。但化學實驗室通風櫃的排氣一定要 100% 排到室外，不能循環回辦公室。API 粉塵處理邏輯相同：毒性越高，越傾向 100% 排氣。

9.6.2

Quantitating the Risk of Airborne Cross-Contamination

9.6.2 Quantitating the Risk of Airborne Cross-Contamination

(Advanced Topic)

Control of cross-contamination is primarily a matter of understanding the mass of "product A" which might be carried into "product B". For contaminants spread through the air, the process involves:

  • Understanding the potential maximum mass of airborne product that may be produced
  • Determining how much of this mass could be delivered to other spaces
  • Evaluating upset cases (e.g., spills) and potential failures of abatement equipment (failure sensitivity evaluation)

The method is similar to the approach used by Industrial Hygienists for worker safety: the actual quantity of product aerosolized during a working day is measured, and correction factors for PPE are applied.

A quantitative evaluation provides:

  • More robust understanding of contamination risks and appropriate controls
  • Verification measures through facility life
  • Data for investigations, event response planning, and critical management tasks
  • Assurance that resources are commensurate with risk — neither inadequate nor wasteful

Contamination Sources and Potency

The potency of the product and the potential maximum concentration of airborne product drive the need for more rigorous abatement. The highest particle concentration air streams — such as from dust collectors, especially when handling potent products — require the most stringent abatement and may not be candidates for recirculation.

Results from Environmental Health and Safety studies are generally expressed in µg/m³, which couples well with the quantitative method outlined here. To determine appropriate filtration, fractional removal efficiencies across twelve particle size ranges from standards such as ANSI/ASHRAE 52.2, EN 1822, EN 779, or ISO 29463-1 are used.

Filter Efficiency Examples

Table 9.2/9.3/9.4 (source tables) demonstrate mass reduction through filtration stages:

A properly installed and maintained MERV 15 (~95% ASHRAE) filter can provide a 3 log reduction in contaminants. A MERV 11 (~50% ASHRAE) gives a 1.5 log (20:1) reduction.

Large Particles — Greater Cross-Contamination Risk

While small particles (<10 µm) are of interest in worker safety (greater respirability), large particles (>10 µm) pose greater cross-contamination risk for two reasons:

  1. In any heterogeneous particle dispersion, the greatest mass resides in large particles. A 10 µ particle is ~10,000 times heavier than a 0.5 µ particle and 1,000,000 times heavier than a 0.1 µ particle. At example specific gravity, it takes 15,000,000 of 0.5 µ particles to deliver 1 µg; only 2,000 of 10 µ particles are needed.
  2. Lower buoyancy of large particles (higher settling rate) makes them more likely to contaminate product by falling out of an airstream as velocity decreases in a production room.

This is good news for filtration: even moderate efficiency filters are quite effective at removing large particles.

Failure Analysis Considerations

Analysis must always consider failure causes including:

  1. Filter and/or frame leakage
  2. Spills and other upsets which cause an excess of airborne API

解說：空氣傳播交叉污染量化方法

核心計算公式：空氣傳播污染質量評估

量化方法的核心邏輯分三步：

步驟 1：確定污染源的空氣中 API 濃度
  來源：EHS 監測數據，單位 µg/m³
  → 代入粒徑分布（各粒徑質量佔比）

步驟 2：計算過濾效率的保護因子
  各粒徑殘留質量 = 總質量 × (1 - 過濾效率%)
  → 參考 ANSI/ASHRAE 52.2 或 EN 1822 分效率數據
  → 對 12 個粒徑區間逐一計算
  → 加總得到過濾後空氣總殘留質量

步驟 3：驗證殘留濃度 ≤ 可接受限度
  接受限度 = HBEL / ADE（基於健康的暴露限值）
  → 若超標：升級過濾或改為 100% 排氣

關鍵公式（粒子質量）：

粒子體積 = (4/3) × π × r³
粒子質量 = 粒子體積 × 比重 (density)
  （常用比重：0.6–1.0 g/cm³）

粒徑效應：
  10 µm 粒子質量 ≈ 5.2×10⁻⁴ µg
  0.5 µm 粒子質量 ≈ 6.5×10⁻⁸ µg
  質量比 = 10µm / 0.5µm ≈ 8,000倍

關鍵變數說明

  • Total Mass（各粒徑總質量）：來自 EHS 監測，單位 µg/m³
  • Fractional Filter Efficiency（分效率）：各粒徑的過濾效率，來自 MERV 或 EN 標準測試
  • Fractional Mass After Filtration：= Total Mass × (1 - Efficiency)
  • MERV（Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value）：美國 ASHRAE 過濾器分級標準
  • EN 1822：歐洲 HEPA/ULPA 過濾器標準（對應 H13/H14）

重要洞察：大粒子是交叉污染主要威脅

直覺上可能以為小粒子（可飄浮更遠）風險更高，但實際上：

  • 大粒子（>10 µm）含有的質量遠遠大於小粒子（體積與半徑三次方成正比）
  • 大粒子沉降速度快，更容易落入開放容器或製程中
  • 中等效率過濾器（MERV 11-13）對大粒子已有很好的捕集效果

這意味著：交叉污染風險控制靠中等效率過濾即可有效管理，無需全部裝 HEPA（除非 API 毒性極高）。

比喻：沙塵 vs 花粉

沙塵粒子大但重，飄不遠就落地，但一旦落在食物上就帶來大量質量（污染）。花粉雖細小但質量輕，飄很遠但每顆攜帶的污染量極少。API 粉塵的邏輯與此相同：大粒子才是交叉污染的主要質量來源。

與工業衛生學方法的連結

本方法源自工業衛生師（Industrial Hygienist）用於工人安全的評估方法，差別在於：

  • 工業衛生：保護工人，關注吸入小粒子（<10 µm）
  • 交叉污染控制：保護產品，關注大粒子（>10 µm）質量轉移
  • 兩者均用 µg/m³ 表達，數據可共用

思考練習

假設 EHS 監測數據顯示某粉塵處理間空氣中 API 濃度為 50 µg/m³，產品 HBEL = 0.5 µg/day，員工每日吸入量假設 10 m³：

  • 每日吸入 API 質量 = 50 × 10 = 500 µg/day → 超過 HBEL 1000 倍
  • 需要過濾效率達到 99.9%（3 log）以上才能降至可接受水準
  • → 建議：MERV 15 或 HEPA，或 100% 排氣

Directional Airflow and Pressure Regimens

Positive vs negative pressure, pressure cascades, airlock configurations | p103-p105

9.6.3 Directional Airflow and Pressure Regimens

In order to control the migration of product aerosols from one room or area to another, it is important to manage the airflow between spaces. Two approaches are possible:

Approach 1 — High Mass Airflow Entrainment: Entrain the aerosol in a large mass of air, typically moving at 0.2 m/s (40 fpm) or greater. Typically used in open downflow booths and similar devices, but not preferred for rooms due to large and rapid changes in airflow when doors are opened or closed (typically 400 m³/h (800 cfm)).

Approach 2 — Controlled Direction with Adequate Velocity (preferred): Control the direction of airflow and assure adequate velocity to transport particles, typically in the range of 0.5 to 1.0 m/s (100 to 200 fpm). Two sub-approaches:

  • Maintain a fixed offset between air entering and leaving via the HVAC system (fixed balance or airflow tracking)
  • Measure and control room pressurization, typically in steps of 10 to 15 Pa (0.4 to 0.6 in. W.G.), but no lower than 5 Pa (0.02 in. W.G.). High-pressure difference across doors results in high velocities at leak points, on the order of 3 to 3.5 m/s (600 to 700 fpm).

Pressure Cascade Design Principles

The pressure cascade and direction of airflow should be designed to be protective of product, personnel, and environment:

  • To prevent environmental contaminants from entering: production area positive with respect to surrounding environment
  • However, a positive manufacturing space can spread product aerosols to other products and personnel; therefore, open product handling areas should be negative with respect to other GxP areas (e.g., the GxP corridor)

Airlock Configurations

Single door configurations between production room and corridor are challenged when the door is open. For greater protection, airlocks with interlocked doors are used. Three configurations (Figure 9.7🔍):

  • Simple Pressure Cascade: Corridor (positive) → Production (negative). Effective for API containment.
  • Sink-type Airlock: Process room positive to airlock AND corridor positive to airlock. Airlock is the lowest pressure (sink).
  • Bubble-type Airlock: Airlock is positive with respect to both corridor and process room. Testing has shown greater emission potential with this configuration — generally not preferred for hazardous compounds.

解說：定向氣流與壓力梯度設計

壓力梯度基本原則

氣流從高壓流向低壓。壓力梯度設計的目標：讓「乾淨空氣」永遠往「可能被污染的區域」流，污染源區域維持負壓以防逸散。

  • 正壓（Positive Pressure）：防外部污染進入（保護產品）
  • 負壓（Negative Pressure）：防 API 逸出（保護人員和其他產品）

對於高毒性 API 製造，通常以負壓為主，防止有害物質向外擴散。

壓力差設計數值

建議壓差（相鄰房間）：10–15 Pa
最低可接受壓差：5 Pa（低於此值難以維持方向性氣流）

門縫氣流速度（在壓差作用下）：
  3.0–3.5 m/s (600–700 fpm)
  → 足以攜帶並阻止粒子逆向移動

空氣流速（大面積掃流）：
  ≥ 0.2 m/s (40 fpm)：用於降流罩
  0.5–1.0 m/s (100–200 fpm)：用於定向控制

矛盾困境：正壓 vs 負壓

製造廠常遇到的設計矛盾：

  • 產品保護 → 製造間正壓（防外部微生物污染）
  • 人員 / 環境保護 → 製造間負壓（防 API 粉塵外洩）

解決方案：在製造間與走廊之間插入氣鎖（Airlock），形成壓力緩衝區，走廊保持正壓，製造間保持負壓，兩者不直接相通。

比喻：病房隔離室

傳染病隔離室採用負壓（防止病毒外洩）；無塵室採用正壓（防止外部粒子進入）。API 製造間兼具兩種需求，氣鎖就是讓兩種需求共存的「緩衝帶」。

Containment Related Technologies

9.7 Containment Related Technologies

Containment related technologies are typically used to make a process step, or a process a closed system, keeping the product and the room environment separate. Employing containment related technologies can reduce the risk of cross-contamination by airborne or mechanical transfer routes.

9.7.1 Selection of Appropriate Containment Systems

The equipment selection process should result in individual equipment items or systems identified which give high confidence that the design target/performance expectation will be met. Success depends on an early understanding of the full range of activities, including normal operation, sampling, and cleaning, shared with the full team including supplier(s) and integrators.

Planned and breakdown maintenance activities should be discussed at an early stage. Requirements for activities such as sampling should be questioned, as they may present significant difficulties in effective containment design and may present an exposure risk that outweighs other benefits.

Information Required for Successful Equipment Selection

  • Detail of all activities to be undertaken including normal operation, sampling, and cleaning
  • Appreciation of the rate of movement of particles or the energy with which they may be released
  • Variety of containers in and out of the equipment
  • Way waste and contaminated equipment will be removed
  • Need for aids for heavy, awkward, or restricted space lifting
  • Operators' understanding of risks and how behavior affects containment performance — behavior has the greatest impact on local extract and enclosures and least impact on closed systems and isolators
  • Ergonomics of activities, especially repetitive actions, access requirements, working position, load characteristics, and operator stature range
  • Knowledge of containment or emission performance of equipment in similar applications
  • How containment performance may change with time due to ineffective cleaning and/or wear
  • Understanding of the problem by the design team
  • Potential for powder to ignite or support a dust cloud explosion — contained systems have smaller volumes and low air change rates creating risk of explosive concentrations; nitrogen inerting may be required
  • To dissipate electrostatic buildup, all metal parts should be grounded (earthed), including elements docked and insulated by plastic wear elements

解說：密閉系統選擇層級

密閉系統選擇層級（依 OEL/HBEL 分級）

密閉技術的選擇遵循「能量控制層級」原則：先考慮工程控制（密閉）而非依賴行政控制（SOP）或 PPE。以下為從低到高的密閉層級：

開放作業（Open Processing）
OEL > 100 µg/m³ | 適合低毒性化合物
↓ 毒性增加
局部排氣通風（LEV / Downflow Booth）
OEL 10–100 µg/m³ | 近場捕集，半開放
↓
密閉容器 / 密封設備（Closed Vessels / Systems）
OEL 1–10 µg/m³ | 降低逸散，人工干預點受控
↓
手套箱 / 隔離器（Glovebox / Isolator）
OEL < 1 µg/m³ | 完全物理隔離，最高等級

人員行為對不同密閉技術的影響

關鍵洞察：密閉程度越低，操作人員行為對實際密閉效果的影響越大。

  • 開放操作 / LEV：操作技術、站位、移動速度對密閉效能影響最大
  • 半密閉（手套箱外接）：中等依賴人員行為
  • 隔離器 / 完全密閉：人員行為影響最小，設備本身提供保障

→ 高毒性化合物不應依賴人員行為作為主要控制手段

比喻：口罩 vs 防護艙

開放作業 + PPE 就像戴口罩防病毒，效果取決於佩戴方法。隔離器就像把病毒放在完全密封的防護艙裡處理，效果幾乎不依賴人員技術。API 越毒，越需要靠近「防護艙」端的解決方案。

爆炸風險：常被忽略的設計考量

密閉系統的副作用：體積小、換氣率低，可能形成爆炸性粉塵濃度（Dust Explosion Limit）。設計時必須：

  • 評估 API 粉塵的爆炸風險（MEC：Minimum Explosive Concentration）
  • 若有風險：通入氮氣（Nitrogen Inerting）降低氧氣濃度
  • 所有金屬部件需接地，防止靜電火花引燃

採樣設計挑戰

密閉系統設計中最困難的環節之一是「採樣」（Sampling）。文件強調應主動質疑採樣是否必要——有時採樣的暴露風險遠大於獲得的品質資訊價值，應在工廠設計初期即納入討論，而非事後補救。

Personal Protective Equipment and Gowning

PPE vs RPE distinction, roles, and cross-contamination risk from mechanical transfer | p106-p108

9.8 Personal Protective Equipment and Gowning

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) refers to gowns, gloves, booties, hair covers, hoods, respiratory protection, face masks, etc.

Respiratory Protective Equipment (RPE) covers devices designed only to protect the mouth and nose.

The distinction is important because PPE, without RPE, is recommended for protecting the product from contamination and cross-contamination as well as for dermal protection for the operators at all times.

Whenever RPE is used, it is important to consider its use, storage, maintenance, and decontamination, and whether or not it could lead to a risk of cross-contamination from mechanical transfer.

PPE is Normally Worn to:

  • Help limit mechanical transfer of materials
  • Provide appropriate outer clothing to protect the product, e.g., made of non-shedding material
  • Provide a psychological and physical indication of transfer from the outside environment into a GxP facility where specific procedures, rules of conduct, and action apply
  • Provide dermal and chemical protection to the operator so that any drug exposure related effects are minimized

Risk Acceptance Requirement (Section 10 cross-reference)

Where risk reduction strategies (including PPE, containment, HVAC) are implemented, the manufacturer must demonstrate to all stakeholders that strategies are effective in maintaining cross-contamination and worker exposure at or below acceptable limits. This requires demonstration that:

  • Procedures and detection devices are in place to minimize mix-up potential even in accident or error scenarios
  • Any residues on product contact and non-product contact surfaces fall within health-based limits, in support of cleaning processes
  • Flow of personnel, product, material, and HVAC are designed to minimize potential for mechanical transfer
  • Any product that may escape the process is maintained below levels that could impact cross-contamination and worker safety

解說：PPE / RPE 的角色與限制

RPE 的隱藏風險：機械轉移

RPE 本身可能成為交叉污染載體！

若 RPE 在高 API 濃度環境中使用後，未妥善儲存/去污就帶到其他區域，RPE 表面殘留的 API 會造成機械轉移。因此：

  • RPE 的使用、儲存、維護、去污程序必須文件化
  • 高毒性環境的 RPE 應視為危險廢棄物，不可帶出
  • 建議在更衣間/氣鎖區域脫除 RPE，而非帶至走廊

PPE 的四個功能（按重要性）

  1. 限制機械轉移：防止人員將 API 粉塵帶到其他區域（保護其他產品）
  2. 保護產品：不脫屑材質的防護衣防止人員粒子污染產品（雙向保護）
  3. 心理 / 行為標誌：穿上特定防護衣代表進入 GxP 環境，強化人員的合規意識
  4. 皮膚化學防護：防止 API 的皮膚吸收（dermal exposure），對潛在毒性化合物尤為重要

比喻：廚師帽的雙重意義

餐廳廚師的帽子（防脫髮）：
- 保護食物不被頭髮污染（保護產品）
- 防止廚師吸入食物蒸汽（保護人員）
API 製造的 PPE 同樣具有雙向保護功能，但在有毒藥品環境中，人員保護的需求更為迫切，RPE 的選擇必須基於定量風險評估。

PPE 是最後一道防線，不是第一道

依照 ISPE Vol.7 的風險降低層級邏輯（第 9 章整體框架）：

  • 優先：工程控制（密閉設備、隔離器、LEV）
  • 其次：行政控制（SOP、人員限制、更衣程序）
  • 最後：PPE / RPE（個人防護）

過度依賴 PPE 而不解決工程層面的問題，是 GMP 稽查常見缺失。

與其他章節的整合：風險接受

第 9 章（風險降低）的所有措施——HVAC 設計、密閉系統、PPE——最終都要回到第 10 章（Risk Acceptance）：製造商需向所有利害相關人證明這些措施有效地將：

  • 交叉污染維持在健康基礎限值以下（HBEL/ADE）
  • 工人暴露維持在 OEL 以下

這需要監控數據、驗證記錄，以及偏差調查機制的整體配合。

Chapter 9 (Part B) 綜合總結

HVAC、密閉技術、PPE 三者的整合應用邏輯

Key Takeaways — English Summary

  • Once-Through vs Recirculation: Recirculation is permitted with adequate treatment; for potent APIs (low OEL), double HEPA or 100% exhaust is required. Dust collectors handling potent products are not candidates for recirculation.
  • Quantitative CC Risk: Large particles (>10 µm) carry the greatest mass and pose the greatest cross-contamination risk. MERV 11 provides ~1.5 log reduction; MERV 15 provides ~3 log. Filtration efficiency data by particle size range is available from ASHRAE 52.2 and EN 1822.
  • Pressure Regimens: Typical steps of 10–15 Pa; minimum 5 Pa. Sink-type airlocks are preferred for potent API areas. Bubble-type airlocks show higher emission potential and are generally not recommended for hazardous compounds.
  • Containment Hierarchy: Open → LEV/Downflow Booth → Closed Vessels → Gloveboxes → Isolators. Higher containment levels reduce dependence on operator behavior.
  • PPE is the last line of defense: Engineering controls (closed systems, HVAC) take priority. RPE must be managed to prevent becoming a cross-contamination vector via mechanical transfer.
