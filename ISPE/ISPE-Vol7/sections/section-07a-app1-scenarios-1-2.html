14 Appendix 1 – Risk-MaPP Application Examples

14.1 Introduction

The examples provided in this Guide are intended to illustrate four scenarios showing the application of the Risk-MaPP tools. Examples were selected to illustrate suboptimal facility and process configurations. This is intended to demonstrate how a well conducted Quality Risk Management Plan (QRMP) can be used.

None of the examples should be seen as setting a precedent, nor are they a benchmark of an acceptable standard. The examples are intended simply to illustrate that when science and risk-based approaches are applied diligently, facilities and processes that seem to present unacceptable levels of patient risk can consistently deliver product within defined margins for patient safety.

If the risk assessment is for a new product being brought into an existing facility, the QRMP should be part of the change control process.

There are four scenarios which can be summarized as:

  • Scenario 1: Obviously Acceptable
  • Scenario 2: Apparently Acceptable (but was not)
  • Scenario 3: Apparently Unacceptable (but was)
  • Scenario 4: Existing facility with a large product portfolio

To make comparisons easier, examples 1 to 3 use the same basic facility layout, processes, and existing product range. The API being introduced varies between each scenario.

The ADEs were set by a qualified toxicologist.

The compounds being introduced in each scenario are: Scenario 1 — Another Anti-hypertensive; Scenario 2 — Anti-neoplastic; Scenario 3 — Sex hormone.

A steering committee was set up of the stakeholders including management, legal, regulatory, quality, operations, validation and cleaning specialists, engineering and EHS, and with significant input from toxicology.

The first task of the committee was to conduct a risk analysis, beginning with the logic diagram (see Section 14.6) followed by the use of risk management tools. All stakeholders took part in these exercises.

Outcomes are not as may be perceived by a superficial look.

14.1 附錄導論

附錄的核心目的

本附錄提供四個真實情境案例，示範如何將 Risk-MaPP 工具實際應用於多產品製造設施中的新產品引入決策。重點在於說明：表面看起來有問題的設施，透過嚴謹的 QRMP 分析，可能仍可安全生產；反之，看似沒問題的情況，可能隱藏著關鍵風險。

重要聲明

這些案例不是「標準答案」或可引用的先例。每個設施情況不同，不可直接套用。案例的價值在於展示思考過程，而非提供固定結論。

四個情境概覽

  • 情境一（明顯可接受）：引入相似類型的抗高血壓藥，風險輪廓相近，評估過程直接清晰。
  • 情境二（表面可接受，但實際不可）：引入抗腫瘤藥，最終因清潔驗證瓶頸而放棄。
  • 情境三（表面不可接受，但實際可以）：引入性荷爾蒙，透過工程控制最終獲批。
  • 情境四：現有設施多產品組合的全面評估。

如何閱讀這些案例

情境一至三使用同一個基礎設施（Kingswood, CT），只有引入的新 API 不同。這個設計讓讀者能直接比較：同一個廠房，引入不同風險等級的化合物，決策路徑如何分岐。把它想成「同一個廚房，做不同料理」——設備一樣，但某些食材的交叉污染風險就是不一樣。

Logic Diagram 的作用

Logic Diagram（第 14.6 節）是整個評估流程的導引工具，類似決策樹。評估團隊從第一個問題出發，逐步回答每個節點，最終得出是否需要隔離設施，或是否可以透過工程控制解決問題。每個情境的評估都從這裡開始。

CDMO 操作要點

當客戶要求導入新產品時，根據此框架，QRMP 應納入變更控制（Change Control）程序中正式執行，而不是個案討論。跨部門指導委員會（含毒理、EHS、法規、生產、清潔驗證）的組建是成功評估的前提條件。

14.2 情境一：明顯可接受

情境一 — Obviously Acceptable

情境設定：為何「明顯可接受」？

業主希望將一個新的抗高血壓藥 Antitense™ 引入現有設施。此設施已有一個相同治療類別的抗高血壓藥在生產中（Anti-hypertensive A，ADE = 0.5 mg/day）。新產品的 ADE 為 0.3 mg/day，略低於現有品，但屬於同一數量級，且現有清潔流程已被驗證能控制類似風險。

關鍵數據解讀：ADE 比較

Antitense™ 的 ADE = 0.3 mg/day，計算如下：

  • 臨床最低治療劑量：10 mg/day
  • 個體間差異調整因子（AFH）：10
  • 外推至無效應劑量的調整因子（AFL）：3
  • ADE = 10 mg ÷ 10 ÷ 3 = 0.33 mg/day ≈ 0.3 mg/day

與現有 Anti-hypertensive A（ADE = 0.5）相比，Antitense™ 只是稍微更嚴格，並未跨越一個數量級的門檻。

設施條件：重要背景

此情境中的設施使用空氣循環（recirculation）系統，這在後續情境二中會是一個問題點。情境一之所以能接受，部分原因是引入的化合物風險等級與現有品相當，不需要特殊隔離設計。

設施特點：單一通道設計、共用走廊、同時最多加工兩種化合物。

Risk Assessment

Risk Identification — Hazard Identification and Dose-Response Assessment

A review of all available data on each API (e.g., data included in the Clinical Investigators Brochure or New Drug Application or the scientific literature) was completed to identify all possible adverse effects. For each compound, the critical effect (the first adverse effect observed at the low end of the dose-response curve) was identified.

The ADE value was derived by dividing the no effect level for the critical endpoint by appropriate adjustment (uncertainty or safety) factors. The ADE was used to derive product contact surface cleaning target values. Additional calculations were made to reflect batch size, product contact equipment surface area, and maximum daily dose of the next product, to arrive at the final surface target value or rinse concentration.

Establishment of ADE for Antitense™

An ADE value of 0.3 mg/day was established for Antitense™. The ADE was based on the low clinical dose of 10 mg and incorporates appropriate adjustment factors: interindividual variability (AFH = 10) and extrapolation to a no effect level (AFL = 3).

Risk Analysis

A review of monitoring data for the existing products and processes was completed to ensure the facility can safely manufacture the products.

Risk Evaluation

The analysis quickly showed that the new compound posed less risk than some compounds already in manufacture, for which data existed showing that they posed an acceptably low level of risk to patient or products.

風險評估解析

ADE 推導邏輯（關鍵概念）

ADE（Acceptable Daily Exposure，每日可接受暴露量）是整個 Risk-MaPP 評估的基石。其推導流程：

  1. 文獻回顧：找出化合物所有不良反應
  2. 識別「關鍵效應」（critical effect）：劑量-反應曲線低端的第一個不良效應
  3. 找出該效應的無不良效應劑量（NOAEL 或等效值）
  4. 除以適當的調整因子（AFH、AFL 等）得出 ADE
  5. 用 ADE 推算清潔驗證目標值（cleaning limits）

思考框架：比較風險，而非絕對風險

風險評估的關鍵洞察：不是問「這個新化合物安不安全？」而是問「這個新化合物的風險，比我們現在已在生產的化合物更高嗎？」。如果現有系統已成功控制 Anti-hypertensive A（ADE 0.5），那麼 ADE 0.3 的 Antitense™ 只需驗證清潔效果稍微更嚴格即可，不需要重新設計設施。

Risk Evaluation 的速捷判斷

「The analysis quickly showed that the new compound posed less risk than some compounds already in manufacture」——這句話是情境一成立的核心。現有監測資料已顯示設施可控制更高挑戰性的化合物，新品的 ADE 雖稍低，但整體風險輪廓未超出現有能力範圍。

Summary — Scenario 1

The new anti-hypertensive has an ADE that is slightly lower than the current anti-hypertensive agent which is manufactured under existing QRMP No. 170129. The new anti-hypertensive can be cleaned to acceptable levels using the current cleaning protocols and methods. The introduction of this new anti-hypertensive poses no additional risk of patient exposure.

情境一：CDMO 結論與應用啟示

為何評估「快速」完成？

情境一之所以稱為「Obviously Acceptable」，是因為：

  • 新品與現有品屬同一藥理類別（抗高血壓），毒理輪廓相似
  • 新品 ADE（0.3 mg/day）與現有品（0.5 mg/day）在同一數量級
  • 現有清潔流程已被歷史數據驗證能達到更嚴格的要求
  • 現有監測數據庫充足，不需要額外的暴露評估

Logic Diagram 路徑（情境一）

Q1
新 API 的風險等級是否超過現有產品？
答：否（ADE 相似，同類藥物）
Q2
現有清潔系統能否達到新的清潔限值？
答：是（歷史數據顯示現有程序可達）
結論
可引入，無需設施改造。納入現有 QRMP 範疇，更新清潔驗證目標值即可。

CDMO 實務啟示

對 CDMO 而言，情境一代表最理想的新產品引入情況：客戶帶來的新品與現有產品在毒理學上可比較，且設施已有相應的控制能力。關鍵文件工作：更新 QRMP 以納入新品，更新清潔驗證計劃（cleaning validation plan）以反映略微更嚴格的目標值，並確認現有 SOP 覆蓋新品的所有製程步驟。

14.3 Scenario 2 (Apparently Acceptable (but was NOT))

Scenario 2 — Apparently Acceptable (but was NOT)

In this example, the owner wishes to introduce an anti-neoplastic agent into the facility. The owner has an excellent history of compliance with the agencies that cover the territories in which the products are sold.

After the FMEA review, the final version of a QRMP summary was produced to show how ICH Q9 had been applied to minimize or prevent risk to product/patient.

Quality Risk Management Plan for Technology Transfer and Commercialization of Anti-neoplastic Agent C

Overview

The document covers the introduction of anti-neoplastic Agent C into an existing multiproduct manufacturing facility. A formalized risk management approach was applied to the Kingswood, CT manufacturing facility. The assessment supported the development of a QRMP addressing: antacid, anti-hypertensive, anti-neoplastic, and NSAID.

Table 14.3: Scenario 2 – ADE Values for Active Ingredients

The ADEs were set by a qualified toxicologist.

Facility Description

The facility under review is a single corridor facility with access from a GMP gowning area for personnel and a material airlock to a warehouse. Each of the unit processes is located off the common corridor. Air is supplied on a once through basis. Up to two compounds are processed at one time. See Figure 14.2.

Segregation Philosophy

Following the FMEA analysis, changes were made to the facility to protect the existing products from cross-contamination by the proposed anti-neoplastic. The philosophy behind these changes:

  • The processes were closed by the retrofitting of engineering control methods. API handling for sampling and subdivision takes place in an isolator for anti-neoplastic Agent C. Closed transfer using bins and split butterfly valves (SBV) was introduced for all transfers for all compounds.
  • Fan assisted safe change room boundary filtration introduced to reduce the risk of fugitive API distribution by HVAC system.
  • Additional gowning: disposable coveralls, bootees, and hair nets in rooms where anti-neoplastic Agent C is being processed. Gowning removed at room boundary.
  • Access to process rooms controlled by electronic verification. Only correct materials and personnel allowed to enter.
  • Cleaning bay divided into clean and dirty sides with a pass through for equipment. Gowning to prevent cross-contamination for dirty side personnel.
  • Anti-neoplastic Agent C equipment subjected to a closed in place clean before leaving the process room.
  • All walls, floors, and ceilings of hard washable coved construction.
  • Material flows controlled by electronic verification (bar code readers) allowing separation in time between material transits in the corridor. Movement of more than one compound at a time with a 1-minute dwell is enforced.

14.3 情境二：表面可接受（但實際不可）

情境二 — Apparently Acceptable (but was NOT)

情境設定：為何「表面可接受」？

業主希望引入抗腫瘤藥 Anti-neoplastic Agent C，一種烷化劑。初步看來，業主已:

  • 在設施中大量投入工程控制（隔離器、密閉傳輸、獨立空調）
  • 建立完整的 SOP 體系（進出人員管制、清潔程序等）
  • 使用一次性通風（once-through）以避免循環污染

光看工程控制清單，似乎已做到位。問題出在哪裡？

關鍵差異：ADE 數量級的跳躍

Anti-neoplastic Agent C 的 ADE = 0.001 mg/day（即 1 μg/day），遠低於設施現有化合物：

  • Antacid：50 mg/day（高出 50,000 倍）
  • Anti-hypertensive：0.5 mg/day（高出 500 倍）
  • NSAID：40 mg/day（高出 40,000 倍）

這個數量級的差距，意味著清潔限值的要求將比現有設備歷史表現嚴格得多。

為何 ADE 如此低？

Anti-neoplastic Agent C 是一種直接作用型基因毒性致癌物（direct acting genotoxic material）。其毒理特性：

  • 烷化劑，干擾 DNA 複製與 RNA 轉錄
  • 動物試驗：低至 2.5 mg/kg/day 即產生發育毒性
  • 損傷成年大鼠精子 DNA
  • 多種動物中增加惡性腫瘤發生率
  • 人類研究確認具致癌性

ADE 推算基於癌症風險外推，1 μg/day 對應 1 × 10⁻⁵ 的額外癌症風險（即百萬分之十的超額風險）。

「Once-Through」vs「循環」通風的意義

情境一設施使用空氣循環（recirculation），對於情境二這樣的高毒性化合物是不可接受的風險。業主在情境二中已切換為一次通過式（once-through）通風——這是正確的工程決策，確保空氣中的微粒不會在設施內循環。

工程控制架構（情境二特有）

情境二中導入的控制措施比情境一更全面，包括：

  • 隔離器（Isolator）：取樣、稱重在隔離器內進行
  • 密閉傳輸：Split Butterfly Valve（SBV）、Rapid Transfer Port（RTP）
  • 房間邊界 HEPA 過濾：風扇輔助安全換氣，防止逸散 API 進入 HVAC
  • 電子門禁：條碼讀取器控制人員與物料流動，走廊內強制 1 分鐘間隔
  • 就地密閉清洗（CIP）：設備離開製程室前先進行密閉式清潔

Process and Manufacturing Equipment Description

The range of operations and equipment used includes:

  • Sampling of incoming API in an isolator; sample sent to QA lab in a closed transfer device — Rapid Transfer Port (RTP).
  • Weighing to batch weight in an isolator; closed transfer in a bin with a Split Butterfly Valve (SBV).
  • High shear granulation and wet milling prior to charging to a fluid bed dryer. The active is layered into the granulator; heel retention is very high, requiring manual scraping for yield. Process conducted in an isolator; transfer in a closed bin.
  • Discharge into the fluid bed dryer bowl in an isolator. Fluid bed dryer is a closed operation: vacuum discharge to an isolator with a hammer type mill; collection in a weigh hopper; vacuum transfer to a bin via SBV.
  • Bin blend or bin to V-Shell transfer using transfer valves and bins.
  • Lubricant added via transfer valve on side arm of V-shell or via charge using SBV to a bin.
  • V-Cone® discharged into SBV bins or blending bin used without transfer. Bins tested for blend uniformity using PAT.
  • Products that are tableted passed to compression with the bin connected via SBV.
  • Products B and C are coated; A is not. Data shows no risk of exposure from coating.
  • Products are bottle or blister packed.

Following the steps in the logic diagram (see Section 14.6), manufacture of the compound was possible.

Personnel access into the process rooms is controlled by card reader. Personnel are trained in gown/de-gown procedures. Personnel who clean equipment put on wet gear and boots at the designated position.

製程設備：密閉系統設計要點

密閉系統的設計邏輯

整個製程設計圍繞一個核心原則：在任何可能產生逸散 API 的節點，全面實施密閉操作。重要設備節點：

  • 隔離器（Isolator）：取樣、稱重、造粒
  • Split Butterfly Valve（SBV）：所有物料傳輸密閉接口
  • Rapid Transfer Port（RTP）：實驗室樣品傳遞
  • 流化床乾燥器：密閉操作、真空排料
  • V-Blender（V 型混合機）：物料進出均使用 SBV

Logic Diagram 路徑（情境二——初步評估）

Q：需要隔離？
Anti-neoplastic ADE = 0.001 mg/day，屬高活性化合物（HPAPI）
→ 需要工程控制
Q：工程控制夠用？
已安裝隔離器、密閉傳輸、once-through 通風、門禁
→ 表面上看似充足

→ 此處發現問題

HVAC

Figure 14.5🔍 shows the pressure cascade and airflow direction in the rooms. The flow is always away from the corridor and toward the rear wall. Supply is through HEPA filters; exhaust via roughing and HEPA filters to protect the ductwork. The MALs and PALs create lock chambers with door operation delay to allow the airlock to be purged. The system is once through — no risk of recirculating particulate.

Exhausts are protected by redundant HEPA filtration (two passes through 99.997% filters with pressure differentials indicated and alarmed). Routine challenge testing is performed.

For product to cross-contaminate another product it must: escape the process; pass through airlocks against low velocity airflow; travel into the corridor; enter another airlock; enter another room; enter the other process.

Key SOPs Brought About by FMEA Analysis

  • Access control procedures for personnel and materials into process areas
  • Cleaning procedures and gowning
  • Health-based cleaning protocol
  • Material handling procedures
  • Gowning/de-gowning procedures
  • HVAC performance monitoring and filter replacement
  • Procedures to cover closed operation
  • Sampling and subdivision protocols
  • Handling and storage of anti-neoplastic Agent C
  • Upset recovery protocols

HVAC 與 SOP 系統解析

多重氣流屏障設計

情境二的 HVAC 設計展示了「縱深防禦（defence in depth）」原則：即使製程室發生逸散事件，污染物仍需突破多個屏障才能進入走廊或其他製程室：

  1. 製程室本身負壓（相對走廊）
  2. 門廳（airlock）的吹掃延遲
  3. 走廊正壓（相對製程室）
  4. 排氣雙重 HEPA 過濾（99.997% × 2）
  5. 一次通過式通風（無循環風險）

SOPs 的 FMEA 驅動設計

值得注意的是：SOP 清單中包含「Upset recovery protocols」（異常恢復程序）。這表明評估團隊認識到，即使在正常操作時系統可控，但當發生設備異常（如密封失效、溢漏）時，需要預定義的應對程序。此類「最壞情境」SOP 是 HPAPI 設施的必要文件。

「表面可接受」的陷阱

此時，若只看工程控制清單——隔離器、SBV、HEPA、電子門禁——的確看起來很完整。但 Risk-MaPP 的力量在於不允許評估在此止步。FMEA 強制團隊逐一檢視每個製程步驟的殘留風險，其中隱藏的炸彈在下一步才被發現。

Risk Assessment — Scenario 2

Establishment of ADE for Anti-neoplastic Agent C

Anti-neoplastic Agent C is an alkylating agent used in the treatment of numerous myeloproliferative and lymphoproliferative disorders and solid malignancies. It disrupts nucleic acid function by interfering with DNA replication and transcription of RNA. The recommended oral dosage ranges from 1 to 5 mg/kg/day for adults or 10 to 15 mg/kg IV every 7 to 10 days.

Anti-neoplastic Agent C is considered moderately toxic orally and produced adverse developmental effects in animals at dosages as low as 2.5 mg/kg/day. It was shown to damage DNA in spermatozoa from adult male rats and increased the incidence of malignant tumors by several routes of administration. The compound has been shown to be a human carcinogen.

The critical endpoint for deriving a health-based value is cancer. Because it is a direct acting genotoxic material, the ADE was derived by extrapolating cancer risks from animal and human tumor data. An ADE of 1 μg/day was calculated corresponding to a 1 × 10⁻⁵ excess cancer risk.

Risk Analysis — Plausible Pathways

Mix-up: Bar code reading control of all equipment and materials; separation of clean and dirty equipment by room; tagging of clean and dirty equipment.

Retention on Product Contact Surfaces: Swab monitoring of cleaning performance against health-based limits; visually clean inspection; routine monitoring shows consistent performance well below the health-based limit — except where the FMEA identified the blender issue.

Mechanical Transfer: Critical transfers are closed; all equipment and materials wiped down or bagged before leaving the process room; tack mats used at room boundaries.

Airborne Transfer: Processes occur in rooms protected by airlocks; corridor positive relative to process rooms; redundant HEPA filtration on exhausts.

Cleaning Review

Cleaning Process Development activities:

  • Cleanability assessments
  • Cleaning agent(s) selection
  • Design of cleaning processes
  • FMEAs for each cleaning process
  • Modification of cleaning processes based on FMEA results

Cleaning Risk Evaluation activities:

  • Collection of swab sample data on each cleaning procedure
  • Process capability evaluations of swab sample data
  • FMEAs of overall cleaning program to identify high risk procedures
  • Improvement/optimization of high risk procedures

Cleaning Process Review: a cleanability assessment was performed on the product and the product was found to be easily cleaned by the existing processes. The cleanability rank was a 2, with a rank of 4 for the hardest to clean product. Current cleaning agents were found to be appropriate for solubilizing and suspending the product.

風險評估深度解析

致癌物的 ADE 推算：無閾值模型

Anti-neoplastic Agent C 屬於直接作用型基因毒性物質（direct acting genotoxic），不同於一般毒性物質使用 NOAEL ÷ 調整因子的方法。基因毒性致癌物通常採用線性無閾值外推（linear no-threshold extrapolation）：

  • 從動物或人類腫瘤數據推算癌症風險
  • 選定可接受的超額癌症風險水準（此案為 1 × 10⁻⁵）
  • 反推出對應的每日暴露量 = ADE = 1 μg/day

這解釋了為何 ADE 比普通化合物低了 3 個數量級。

四條暴露途徑的評估架構

Risk-MaPP 要求評估所有「合理可能的暴露途徑（plausible pathways）」：

  • Mix-up（混藥）：標籤錯誤、設備誤置 → 條碼管制
  • Retention on product contact surfaces（殘留）：清洗不乾淨 → 清潔驗證
  • Mechanical transfer（機械傳遞）：設備移動帶出污染 → 密閉系統
  • Airborne transfer（氣載傳遞）：揚塵或氣流帶出 → HVAC 設計

清潔可行性：初步令人放心

注意：清潔可行性評估（cleanability rank = 2，最難清潔品為 4）顯示 Agent C 相對容易清洗，清潔劑也適用。這讓評估團隊最初以為清潔不是問題。但這只是化合物本質的清潔性，尚未考慮設備的幾何形狀與表面積帶來的挑戰。

Ppk 分析：從「數量」到「限值」的挑戰

想像清潔驗證就像射靶：現有設備一直射在靶心附近（Ppk 高），清潔表現穩定。但引入 Agent C 後，靶心的位置（清潔限值）移動到了之前從未達到的極小值。即使你的射擊精準度（製程能力）沒有改變，靶心太遠就是射不中。

Risk Evaluation — The Critical Finding

New Product Review

The new anti-neoplastic product was evaluated against the existing program for its compatibility with the cleaning processes and its ADE was assessed against the existing swab sampling database for the facility.

The FMEA analysis of cleaning showed a significant risk occurring in the V-blender which, due to its significant surface area, required a level of residue significantly below the historically achievable results.

Cleaning Risk Evaluation — Ppk Analysis

The swab data for all cleaning processes were found to have high Process Performance Indices (Ppk) with undetectable levels Defects per Million Opportunities (ppm) — with the exception of the blender.

The Ppk for the blender was 0.34 with the potential for Defects per Million Opportunities of 14,994. This evaluation indicated that the current cleaning process for the blender is not satisfactory to safely clean the new product.

In order to introduce the new product into the facility, the cleaning process for the blender would need to be improved or a new blender would have to be purchased.

Risk Control

A conclusion was made that the risks to patients with the existing manufacturing process was unacceptably high. This was due to the potential carryover of residuals of Anti-neoplastic Agent C to other products from the V-blender.

A Design of Experiment (DoE) was performed to improve the Blender Cleaning Process. Cleaning agent concentration, cleaning time, cleaning temperature, and rinse time were evaluated. The new data indicate that the blender cannot be effectively cleaned to safe levels for the new product. The introduction of a dedicated blender was not possible and so the proposal was abandoned and the anti-neoplastic will not be manufactured at this site.

Risk Communication

No further communication on the proposed introduction of Anti-neoplastic Agent C is required because the proposal was rejected.

Summary — Scenario 2

The FMEA analysis of cleaning showed a significant risk occurring in the V-blender which due to its significant surface area required a level of residue significantly below the historically achievable results. Since the other processes required a V-blender and no space was available for a dedicated blender, the owner decided not to manufacture Anti-neoplastic Agent C.

情境二：關鍵發現與教訓

核心發現：V-Blender 成為阻斷因素

整個情境二的轉折點在於 FMEA 清潔評估的量化數據：

  • 除 V-Blender 以外，所有設備的清潔 Ppk 值高，ppm（缺陷率）趨近於零
  • V-Blender 的 Ppk = 0.34（正常標準為 Ppk ≥ 1.33 表示能力充足）
  • 對應的缺陷率 DPM = 14,994 ppm（即每百萬次清潔中有近 1.5 萬次無法達標）
  • 問題根源：V-Blender 表面積大，對 ADE = 1 μg/day 的化合物，清潔限值極低，遠超現有能力

Ppk 數值的意義

製程能力指數（Ppk）代表製程實際輸出相對於規格限值的能力：

  • Ppk ≥ 1.33：製程能力良好（cGMP 通常要求）
  • Ppk = 1.00：邊界值，約 0.27% 的失敗率
  • Ppk = 0.34：嚴重不足，幾乎 1.5% 的批次清潔後殘留超標

換算成患者風險：每 67 批（≈1000 ppm ÷ 14.994）就可能有一批清潔失敗，導致下一個產品被致癌物污染。

DoE 嘗試失敗：邊界在哪裡？

評估團隊沒有輕易放棄。他們進行了正式的實驗設計（DoE），系統性地優化：

  • 清潔劑濃度
  • 清潔時間
  • 清潔溫度
  • 沖洗時間

DoE 結論：即使在最佳化條件下，V-Blender 就是無法被清潔至足夠安全的水準。這是設備幾何形狀（大表面積）與物理化學限制共同決定的結果，非 SOP 可以克服。

最終決定：拒絕引入

解決方案本可以是購買專用 V-Blender（dedication）——但廠房無法容納。在無法解決設備問題的情況下，業主做出了正確的決策：拒絕在此設施生產 Anti-neoplastic Agent C。

這本身是一個重要的監管信號：Risk-MaPP 流程的價值不在於「讓所有產品都能生產」，而在於提供科學依據拒絕無法安全生產的品項。

Logic Diagram 完整路徑（情境二）

步驟 1
ADE = 1 μg/day → 屬高活性物質（HPAPI）
→ 需要工程控制 / FMEA
步驟 2
導入隔離器、SBV、once-through 通風 → 氣載、機械傳遞途徑受控
→ 表面上可以繼續
步驟 3
清潔 FMEA：V-Blender 表面積大，清潔限值超出歷史能力（Ppk = 0.34）
→ 關鍵風險識別：清潔無法達標
步驟 4
DoE 嘗試優化 → 失敗；購買專用 Blender → 廠房無空間
→ 最終結論：無法引入，拒絕提案

CDMO 核心教訓

對 CDMO 最重要的啟示：

  • 工程控制完備，不等於清潔驗證必然可達。兩者需要獨立評估。
  • 清潔驗證的挑戰不只是「能不能洗乾淨」，更要看設備幾何形狀（表面積）是否允許達到所需的清潔限值。
  • Ppk 分析是清潔風險量化的有效工具——不要只看「合格/不合格」，要看製程能力指數是否足夠應對新品的嚴格限值。
  • 能夠拒絕客戶要求（在科學依據下），本身就是 Risk-MaPP 系統發揮價值的體現。
  • 在報價或技術轉移評估早期，應先查清設施中最大表面積設備的歷史清潔 Ppk，再評估能否引入高活性化合物。

Cross-Scenario Comparison: Scenarios 1 vs. 2

情境一 vs. 情境二：比較總結

決定性差異：數量級跳躍觸發不同評估路徑

兩個情境使用同一個設施，評估結果截然相反。關鍵在於：

  • 情境一：新 ADE（0.3）與現有最低 ADE（0.5）在同一數量級，現有系統可覆蓋。
  • 情境二：新 ADE（0.001）低了兩到三個數量級，觸發完全不同的工程控制要求，且進一步發現清潔設備的物理限制無法克服。

本章的核心訊息

不要以「看起來有嚴格工程控制 = 可以引入」作為評估終點。Risk-MaPP 要求你系統性地走完所有路徑——特別是清潔驗證的製程能力量化分析——才能得出真正基於科學的結論。

給監管審查員的信號

情境二最終的主動拒絕（self-rejection）本身就是品質文化成熟度的體現。監管機構最希望看到的是：企業能在問題變成缺陷前，用科學方法找到邊界，並做出正確決策——不論那個決策是否有利於商業利益。
