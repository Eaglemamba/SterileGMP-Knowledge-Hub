繁體中文解說 — 核心監管文件

最重要文件：EMA HBEL 指引（參考文獻 #6）

EMA/CHMP/CVMP/SWP/169430/2012 是本指引最核心的監管參照。此指引確立了在共用設施中以 健康暴露限值（HBEL）取代傳統 1/1000 劑量標準的法規框架。2014年正式生效後，全球製藥廠須重新評估清潔驗證的接受限值基礎。

ICH Q9 & Q10（#1 & #8）— 品質風險管理雙柱

ICH Q9（2005）建立了藥品品質風險管理的系統化框架，定義 FMEA、HAZOP、FTA 等工具的應用原則，是本 Vol.7 指引的理論基礎。

ICH Q10（2008）確立藥品品質系統（PQS）概念，將 QRM 嵌入產品生命週期管理，與 Vol.7 的 CAPA 及風險回顧機制直接銜接。

EU GMP EudraLex Vol.4（#4, #5, #10, #11）

第3章（廠房設備）與第5章（生產）2015年修訂版明確要求製造商採用 HBEL/ADE 作為交叉污染控制的科學依據，取代過去「可見殘留」標準。

附件2（生物製品）與附件15（確效）定義了生物活性物質製造與清潔驗證的特殊要求。

21 CFR Part 211（#13）— FDA cGMP 核心法規

美國 FDA 現行良好製造規範，涵蓋廠房設備、人員、製程管控、成品測試等完整要求。Vol.7 中所有風險管理措施均以符合此法規為前提。

ICH M7（#7）— DNA 反應性雜質

針對基因毒性雜質引入毒理學關注門檻（TTC）概念：1.5 μg/day 終生暴露為基準。在多產品共用設施中，若前一批次產品含基因毒性雜質，清潔驗證限值需特別嚴格。

FDA Process Validation Guidance（#26）

16.1 Acronyms and Abbreviations

繁體中文解說 — 重點縮寫詳解

HBEL — Health-Based Exposure Limit｜健康暴露限值

雖然 HBEL 本身未列入縮寫表，但為本指引最核心概念。HBEL 是以 ADE（Acceptable Daily Exposure）或 PDE（Permitted Daily Exposure）為基礎計算出的暴露限值，用於共用設施交叉污染風險評估，取代傳統 1/1000 最小治療劑量或 10 ppm 可見殘留標準。

ADE vs PDE — 兩個核心暴露限值

ADE（可接受每日暴露量）：由 ISPE 風險管理社群推廣，適用於多路徑暴露評估（包括口服、吸入、皮膚）。終生暴露不產生不良效應的劑量。

PDE（允許每日暴露量）：EMA HBEL 指引與 ICH Q3C 採用的術語。計算邏輯與 ADE 相同，但命名來自監管文件。

實務上：ADE ≈ PDE，兩者常互換使用，計算公式相同。

MACO — Maximum Allowable Carryover｜最大允許殘留轉移量

MACO 未列入本縮寫表，但為清潔驗證最重要計算結果。公式：

MACO (mg) = ADE (mg/day) × Batch size of next product (g) / Maximum daily dose of next product (g/day)

此結果轉換為表面殘留限值（μg/cm²），指導清潔驗證取樣標準。

NOAEL / NOEL / LOAEL — 毒理學劑量描述符

  • NOAEL（無可觀察不良效應劑量）：動物實驗中未觀察到不良效應的最高劑量，是 ADE 計算的主要起始點（Point of Departure, PoD）
  • NOEL（無可觀察效應劑量）：比 NOAEL 更嚴格，連適應性效應都未出現
  • LOAEL（最低可觀察不良效應劑量）：當 NOAEL 不可得時作為替代 PoD，通常需額外增加不確定係數（UF = 3–10）

RPN — Risk Priority Number｜風險優先數

FMEA 中核心量化指標：RPN = 嚴重性（S）× 發生頻率（O）× 可偵測性（D），各項 1–10 分，RPN 範圍 1–1000。Vol.7 附錄1案例中：RPN < 125 可接受；125–343 需降低風險；> 343 不可接受，須停止作業。

CPP / CQA / CPV — 製程生命週期三角

  • CPP（關鍵製程參數）：必須被監控和控制的製程變數
  • CQA（關鍵品質屬性）：必須符合規格的產品特性
  • CPV（持續製程驗證）：商業化生產期間的持續監控，對應 FDA 2011年製程驗證指引

FMEA / HAZOP / FTA — QRM 工具三劍客

  • FMEA（失效模式與效應分析）：前瞻性，適合設備/製程設計審查，計算 RPN
  • HAZOP（危害與可操作性分析）：關鍵字驅動（More/Less/No/As Well As），適合連續製程和管道系統
  • FTA（故障樹分析）：後推性分析，從頂事件（不良結果）向下找根本原因

p170–p176 | 45項定義，涵蓋 ADE、OEB、PDE、MACO、ALARP 等核心術語

16.2 Definitions of Terms

As used in this Guide, derived from multiple sources: FDA, other ISPE documents, ICH, EFPIA Resource Document (Assessing Facilities Needs, 14 November 2005), EU EudraLex Volume 4, and various WHO documents.

Acceptable Daily Exposure (ADE)

A dose that is unlikely to cause an adverse effect if an individual is exposed, by any route, at or below this dose every day for a lifetime. By definition the ADE is intended to be protective of all subpopulations and by all routes of administration. Establishment of the ADE value is an important first step in a risk assessment and the inherent assumptions should be consistent with how it is applied within the product mix in a given facility.

Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API)

Any substance, or mixture of substances intended to be used in the manufacture of a drug (medicinal) product and that, when used in the production of a drug, becomes an active ingredient of the drug product. Such substances are intended to furnish pharmacological activity or other direct effect in the diagnosis, cure, mitigation, treatment, or prevention of disease, or to affect the structure and function of the body.

Antineoplastic (see Cytostatic and Cytotoxic)

Chemotherapeutic agent that down regulates growth or kills cancer cells. May be classified as cytotoxic or cytostatic.

Aerosol

Suspension of fine solid or liquid particles (e.g., dust, fiber, fog, fume, mist, smoke) in a gas (i.e., typically in air).

Barrier

Facility/room/area, process, equipment, or isolation devices (e.g., glovebox) designed to achieve API or product containment.

Beta-lactam Antibiotics

Penicillins, cephalosporins, monobactams, and carbapenems, other APIs containing beta-lactam rings, beta-lactamase inhibitors with beta-lactam rings (e.g., clavulanic acid, and sulbactam and tazobactam).

Bulk

Any product that has completed all processing stages up to, but not including, final packaging.

Campaign

The manufacture of more than one product in a facility, with strict adherence to accepted cleaning procedures between these products. The products may be run in the same equipment, but not at the same time.

Carry Over

The residue or accumulated product on product contact surfaces that is retained after cleaning and may be available to enter the next product processed with the same product contact surfaces (i.e. it is carried-over to the next product in production). See definition of Retention.

Clean Zone

An area with defined environmental control of particulate and microbial contamination, constructed and used in such a way as to reduce the introduction, generation, and retention of contaminants within the area.

Closed Process

A process step (or system) where the product is not exposed to the immediate room environment (and vice versa). It is the manufacturer's responsibility to define and prove closure for a process step.

Contained Area

Specific manufacturing space constructed and equipped with appropriate air handling and filtration and operated to prevent contamination of the external work environment.

Containment

State achieved by enclosures with separation between manufacturing environment and manufacturing operation. The action of confining a biological agent or other entity within a defined space.

Contaminant

Any particulate, molecular, non-particulate, or biological entity that can adversely affect the product or process.

Contamination

The undesired introduction of impurities of a chemical or microbiological nature, or of foreign matter, into or onto a raw material, intermediate, or API during production, sampling, packaging or repackaging, storage, or transport.

Controlled Area

Facility space with restricted access and operated to restrict introduction of potential contamination, consequences of accidental release of airborne contaminants.

Cross-Contamination

Contamination of a starting material, intermediate product or finished product with another starting material or product during production.

Cytostatic (see Antineoplastic)

Chemotherapeutic agent capable of impairing cell proliferation.

Cytotoxic (see Antineoplastic)

Chemotherapeutic agent intended to control or kill cancer cells, which may act as an indiscriminate poison preventing cell reproduction or growth and causing death of cancerous and potentially healthy cells.

Decontamination

Process that reduces, detoxifies, and/or eliminates contaminated substances to a predetermined acceptance level.

Dedicated Area

Separate area, which may comprise one or more rooms within a multipurpose facility with its own air locked personnel/material accesses, dedicated to a specific product or product range.

Dedicated Building/Facility

Physically separate or segregated building with its own personnel/material accesses and HVAC systems, dedicated to process steps associated with a specific product or product.

Dedicated Change Part

An equipment change part (e.g., sieve, filter, etc.) that is dedicated to a specific product or product range, and is identified accordingly.

Dedicated Equipment

Equipment that is dedicated to a specific product or product range, and is identified accordingly.

Dedicated Suite

One or more rooms within a multipurpose facility or dedicated area that is dedicated to a specific product or product range, and is identified accordingly.

Detectability

Ability to discover or determine the existence, presence, or fact of a hazard.

Drug (a.k.a., API)

Chemical compound that may be used on, or administered to, humans as an aid in the diagnosis, treatment, cure, mitigation, or prevention of disease or other abnormal conditions.

Drug Product

A finished dosage form, for example, tablet, capsule, solution, etc., that contains one or more APIs generally, but not necessarily, in association with inactive ingredients. The term also includes a finished dosage form which does not contain an API but is intended to be used as a placebo.

Excipient (USP)

Components of a finished medicinal drug product other than the API. They are included in the formulation to facilitate manufacture, enhance stability, control release of API from the product, assist in product identification, or enhance other product characteristics.

Genotoxic

Substances which damage or modify DNA.

GxP Regulation

The underlying international pharmaceutical requirements, such as those set forth in the US FD&C Act, US PHS Act, FDA regulations, EU Directives, Japanese regulations, or other applicable national legislation or regulations under which a company operates. These include, but are not limited to: Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP), Good Clinical Practice (GCP), Good Laboratory Practice (GLP), Good Distribution Practice (GDP), Good Quality Practice (GQP), Good Pharmacovigilance Practice, Medical Device Regulations, and PDMA regulations.

Hazard

Inherent pharmacologic and toxicologic property of a substance that adversely affects the health of people, environment, process or product.

Hazardous Substance

A substance which by reason of being explosive, flammable, toxic, poisonous, corrosive, oxidizing, irritant or otherwise harmful, is likely to cause injury.

Health Hazard

Classification of a chemical for which there is statistically significant scientific data that acute or chronic adverse health effects may occur in exposed persons. Examples include: cardiovascular toxins, carcinogens, toxic or highly toxic, developmental and reproductive toxins, immunotoxic agents, hepatotoxins, nephrotoxins, and neurotoxins.

Highly Hazardous Drugs

Genotoxic compounds that are known to be, or highly likely to be, carcinogenic to humans, compounds that can produce reproductive and/or developmental effects at low dosages, and compounds that can produce serious target organ toxicity or other significant adverse effects at low dosages.

Holistic

Considering the whole system or process and its interdependencies rather than addressing separate parts in isolation.

Intermediate

A material produced during steps of the processing of an API that undergoes further molecular change or purification before it becomes an API. Intermediates may or may not be isolated.

Manufacturing Process

All manufacturing and storage steps in the creation of the finished product from the weighing of components through the storing, packaging, and labeling of the finished product, including, but not limited to, the following: mixing, granulating, milling, molding, formulating, lyophilizing, tableting, encapsulating, coating, sterilizing, and filling.

Mix-up

The contamination, at unsafe levels, of one product with another via inadequate plant and process design or human error.

Multiproduct Facility

A facility that supports production of two or more products, either in a campaigned or concurrent manner.

Mutagenic

Capable of inducing inheritable effect either in vitro and/or in vivo at the genetic (i.e., DNA or chromosomal) level.

No-Observed-Adverse-Effect Level (NOAEL)

The dose in a study which produces no adverse effects.

Occupational Exposure Band (OEB)

A range of airborne concentrations, usually spanning an order of magnitude, corresponding to a category assigned to a compound based on its inherent toxicological and pharmacological properties. The OEB also corresponds to control strategies, known in advance, to control airborne levels within the range of concentrations associated with the band.

Occupational Exposure Limit (OEL)

A health-based airborne concentration for a conventional 8-hour workday and a 40-hour workweek, to which it is believed that nearly all workers may be repeatedly exposed, day after day, for a working lifetime without adverse effect.

Open System/Process

A system that fails to meet one or more of the requirements that set the criteria for a closed system.

Permitted Daily Exposure (PDE)

A substance specific dose that is unlikely to cause an adverse effect if an individual is exposed at or below this dose every day for a lifetime.

Pharmaceutical Product

Substance or combination of APIs and excipients, or preservatives which has a therapeutic, prophylactic, or diagnostic purpose, or is intended to modify physiological functions, and is presented in a dosage form suitable for administration to the patient.

Physical Segregation

The separation of materials, spaces, or operations by means of physical barriers to prevent their mixing or overlap.

Plausible Pathways

A reasonable route of potential exposure.

Point-of-Departure (PoD)

The dose in animals or humans which serves as the starting point for derivation of the ADE value. This is the dose associated with the critical effect, which is the most sensitive clinically significant adverse effect observed that is relevant to humans.

Process Boundary

The process equipment and the interface, through which it is charged, discharged, sampled, intervened, and recovered.

Product Changeover

The program by which a processing area is cleared of supplies and components used in the manufacture of a previous product and then readied for production of a new product. This often includes parts changeover and/or special cleaning to eliminate cross-contamination.

Product Life Cycle

All phases in the life of the product from the initial development through marketing until the product's discontinuation.

Primary Containment

The first level of containment, consisting of the inside portion of that container which comes into immediate contact on its inner surface with the material being contained.

Quality Risk Management (QRM)

A systematic process for the assessment, control, communication and review of risks to the quality of the drug product across the product life cycle.

Quality System

The sum of all aspects of a system that implements quality policy and ensures that quality objectives are met.

Retention

The residue, or accumulated product, on product contact surfaces that can carryover from one product to another in the same equipment used in a sequential or campaign manner.

Risk

Likelihood of an identified harmful consequence as a function of exposure to a hazard.

Risk Metrics

A collection of quality, compliance, and/or performance measurements which help management measure and control the risks associated with operating within a regulated industry. Also known as dashboards.

Secondary Containment

Level of containment that is external to, and separate from, primary containment.

Self Contained Area

Premises which provide complete and total separation of all aspects of an operation including personnel and equipment movement, with well established procedures, controls, and monitoring. This includes physical barriers as well as separate air handling systems, but does not necessarily imply two distinct and separate buildings.

Sensitizer

A compound that can cause immediate (IgE mediated) or delayed (cell-mediated) hypersensitivity reactions.

Segregation

The provision of facilities which, although dedicated to a particular use, can be within the same building as other operations. A fully segregated area within a common building is one where there is a continuous floor to roof wall. The wall should be sealed at all break points where common services breach the wall.

Severity

A measure of the seriousness of possible consequences of a hazard.

Teratogenicity

The occurrence of behavioral or structural malformations in a developing foetus when a substance is administered during pregnancy.

Vaccine

Antigens prepared from modified organisms that elicit immune response (antibody production) to protect a person or animal from a disease agent.

Wipe Limit

A surface limit applied to external equipment and facility surfaces for the purpose of evaluating worker exposures. Any use of wipe sample results should be done with caution, within the context of a risk assessment for cross-contamination based on the underlying ADE value.

繁體中文解說 — 核心定義中文對照與詮釋

ADE（可接受每日暴露量） — 最重要定義

核心意涵：ADE 是終生暴露的安全劑量，保護所有次族群（老人、兒童、孕婦、病患），適用所有暴露途徑。

計算公式：

ADE = (NOAEL × 體重調整 × 暴露頻率調整) / (不確定係數 UF1×UF2×...×UFn)

實務應用：ADE 值決定清潔驗證 MACO 限值，也是 OEB 分級的依據。製藥廠在引入新 API 時，毒理學家必須先建立 ADE。

PDE（允許每日暴露量） — 與 ADE 的關係

PDE 源自 ICH Q3C（殘留溶劑指引），後被 EMA HBEL 指引採用為共用設施交叉污染控制的標準術語。計算邏輯與 ADE 完全相同，差別僅在命名慣例：

  • ISPE / RiskMAPP 社群 → 使用 ADE
  • EMA HBEL 指引、ICH Q3C → 使用 PDE
  • 實務上：ADE = PDE

OEB（職業暴露分級帶） — 早期控制策略工具

在尚未取得完整毒理數據時，OEB 依據已知藥理/毒理特性將化合物初步分為 5 個等級（OEB 1 最低危害 → OEB 5 最高危害），並與對應的工程控制策略掛鉤：

  • OEB 1–2：開放製程，一般通風即可
  • OEB 3：局部排氣通風（LEV）
  • OEB 4：完全封閉設備、隔離手套箱
  • OEB 5：專用設施、嚴格隔離、負壓環境

MACO（最大允許殘留轉移量） — 清潔驗證核心

雖然 MACO 未出現於定義表中，但是本指引清潔驗證的核心計算值。MACO 計算需要 ADE/PDE 值，並考慮後續批次的最大批量與最大每日劑量。

MACO → 換算為表面殘留限值 → 對比清潔驗證拭抹樣品結果 → 決定清潔是否合格。

ALARP（盡可能低） — 風險管理原則

ALARP（As Low As Reasonably Practicable）雖未列入本縮寫表，但為 Vol.7 風險控制決策的指導原則。其意涵：在技術可行且經濟合理的前提下，將風險降至盡可能低的水準。不等於「零風險」，而是在風險與成本間取得合理平衡。

PoD（起始點）— ADE 計算的出發點

PoD 是毒理學家進行 ADE 推算的關鍵起點：

  • 優先選用 NOAEL（動物或人體研究）
  • 若無 NOAEL，使用 LOAEL + 額外 UF（3–10×）
  • 現代方法：BMD（基準劑量）建模取代 NOAEL，提供更精確的統計 PoD

PoD 選擇直接影響 ADE 大小，是整個 HBEL 評估中最需要毒理學專業判斷的步驟。

Retention vs Carry Over — 兩個容易混淆的定義

Retention（殘留）：產品接觸面上的殘留量，是靜態概念，指清潔後仍附著在設備表面的物質。

Carry Over（帶入）：Retention 的動態結果，指殘留物在下一批次生產時被帶入產品的量。Carry Over = Retention × 接觸因子。

清潔驗證的目標是確保 Carry Over ≤ MACO。

Highly Hazardous Drugs — 高危害藥物三大類別

本定義涵蓋三類高危害化合物，需要最嚴格的 OEB 5 控制措施：

  • 基因毒性致癌物：已知或高可能性對人類致癌（如鉑類化合物）
  • 生殖/發育毒性物質：低劑量即可產生生殖或發育毒性（如沙利竇邁類）
  • 嚴重靶器官毒性物質：低劑量產生嚴重不良效應（如細胞毒性抗癌藥）

Quality Risk Management (QRM) — Vol.7 的核心框架

QRM 的四步驟循環：

  • 評估（Assessment）：識別危害、分析暴露途徑、評估風險大小
  • 控制（Control）：工程控制（隔離、HVAC）、管理控制（SOP、CAPA）
  • 溝通（Communication）：向管理層和監管機構報告風險狀態
  • 回顧（Review）：定期審查（至少每年一次），產品組合或製程變更時立即觸發

Wipe Limit — 表面殘留監控的謹慎解讀

指引特別提醒：拭抹結果應在交叉污染風險評估的脈絡下謹慎解讀，不可脫離 ADE 值單獨使用。拭抹效率（回收率）因物質、表面材質、拭抹技術而異，實際表面殘留量可能被低估或高估 2–10 倍，需納入不確定性評估。
