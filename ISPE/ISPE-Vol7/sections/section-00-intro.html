Preface（前言）

Original Text

The use of multiproduct facilities for the production of pharmaceutical products is necessary to allow products to be brought to patients in a timely and cost-effective manner. Doing this safely requires an understanding of the products, the facilities, the processes, and the equipment and the risks posed by this combination. The major risk of concern within shared facilities is the risk of cross-contamination.

New GMP guidance for the EU came into effect in 2015 that requires a risk management process to determine if products can be safely manufactured in shared facilities. This new GMP requirement left industry unsure how to perform and document these risk assessments.

This Baseline® Guide provides a scientific risk-based approach based on ICH Q9 to manage the risk of cross-contamination to maintain product quality and operator safety. This allows the selection of the appropriate risk control strategies to be implemented on a case-by-case basis to maintain an appropriate balance between assurance of product quality and worker safety. This Guide also provides examples of risk assessments and the summary report.

This second edition of the Guide is updated to include the new EU GMP requirements as well as additional information for cleaning, HVAC, and examples to assist the reader.

During the development of the first edition of this document, the FDA and EMA were actively involved and other regulatory agencies reviewed the document prior to publication. For the second edition there were discussions with the EMA to ensure that the Guide did not contradict the new GMP requirements and again other regulatory agencies, including the FDA, reviewed the document prior to publication.

Advanced Topics Notice: Advanced Topics are noted and represent topics that require subject matter experts to address. These issues should not be addressed by those that are not familiar with the topic or do not have sufficient experience to understand how to apply the concepts appropriately.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

Risk-MaPP 的存在理由

Risk-MaPP = Risk-Based Manufacture of Pharmaceutical Products。這本指引解決一個真實的產業痛點：

  • 藥廠為節省成本，同一設施生產多種藥品（共用廠房）
  • 2015 年 EU GMP 更新要求必須做風險評估，但沒有告訴業者「怎麼做」
  • Risk-MaPP 就是填補這個空白的實務指南

核心問題只有一個：「在共用廠房製造藥品時，交叉污染的風險是否可接受？」

比喻：共用廚房的食物過敏風險

想像一家餐廳同一個廚房既做花生菜餚也做無過敏原餐點。如何確保花生不污染其他食物？你需要：評估花生的危害程度 → 分析污染途徑 → 設定清潔標準 → 確認殘留量低於安全閾值。

Risk-MaPP 做的就是把這個邏輯系統化、科學化、文件化，適用於製藥的多產品生產。

第二版 vs 第一版：主要變化

  • 納入 EU GMP Chapter 3 & 5 新要求（2015 年生效）
  • 整合 EMA HBEL Guideline（2014 年 11 月發布）
  • 新增清潔、HVAC 章節內容與更新範例
  • FDA 與 EMA 均參與審閱，確保不牴觸新法規

CDMO 實務思考

作為無菌充填的 CDMO，你的廠房同時生產多個客戶的產品——這正是 Risk-MaPP 最直接的應用場景。當客戶稽核問：「你如何確保我的產品不被其他產品污染？」Risk-MaPP 提供了完整的回答框架。

This ISPE Baseline® Guide: Risk-Based Manufacture of Pharmaceutical Products (Risk-MaPP) provides a scientific risk-based approach based on ICH Q9 to manage the risk of cross-contamination to maintain product quality and operator safety. This allows the selection of the appropriate risk control strategies to be implemented on a case by case basis to maintain an appropriate balance between assurance of product quality and worker safety.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

Risk-MaPP 的雙重目標

開篇一句話點出了 Risk-MaPP 的本質：它同時服務兩個對象——

  • 病患安全：確保產品不因交叉污染而影響療效或安全性（GMP 視角）
  • 員工安全：確保操作人員不因接觸高活性藥物而受害（工業衛生視角）

這兩個目標有時會有張力，Risk-MaPP 的價值在於提供一個系統化的方法來同時滿足兩者。

1.1 Scope of this Guide（指引範圍）

Original Text

When considering multiproduct facilities, to satisfy regulatory requirements risk management processes are necessary to determine and document reasonable and acceptable risk. This Guide provides a process that allows manufacturers to assess risk and determine where control strategies are necessary to meet acceptable limits for cross-contamination. The control strategies to manage risk can vary from administrative to full dedication or segregation.

This Guide is intended to provide professionals in the pharmaceutical industry with a consistent approach on setting acceptable limits to assess the potential of cross-contamination causing an undue risk to patient safety.

It should be noted that the use of the principles in this Guide assumes the following:

  1. There is a basic understanding of the hazard spectrum of compounds and containment fundamentals
  2. Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) are part of the team for the quality risk management process
  3. The integrity of the data used to support the quality risk management process is of high quality and meets regulatory expectations
  4. Risk management principles are not used to justify bad design or engineering practices

The principles described in this Guide can be applied equally to large and small molecular weight APIs, pre-clinical and clinical materials, and commercially marketed products in all dosage forms.

The term "controls" as used throughout this Guide encompasses all types of controls including:

  • Process controls
  • Operational controls
  • The full range of engineering controls
  • Procedural controls

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

適用範圍：比你想的更廣

Risk-MaPP 的適用對象涵蓋：

  • 大分子量 API（生物製劑）與小分子量 API（化學合成藥）
  • 臨床前、臨床試驗材料與上市商業化產品
  • 所有劑型（注射劑、口服固體、外用等）
  • 任何設施規模，從小型研發設施到大型商業生產廠

關鍵前提：這不是給初學者的手冊——第 2 點明確要求 SME 參與。

「Controls」的四個層次

注意本指引對「controls（管制措施）」的廣義定義——這是一個從最輕到最重的連續體：

  • 程序性管制（最輕）：SOP、工作指引、訓練
  • 操作管制：排程隔離、campaign 生產
  • 工程管制：設備設計、HVAC、密閉系統
  • 完全專用/隔離（最重）：獨立廠房、專用設備

Risk-MaPP 的核心貢獻：讓企業能夠根據風險高低選擇適當層次，而非一律採用最嚴格措施。

比喻：駕駛安全等級

一個危險化學品運輸公司的風險管理：低風險貨物（程序控制：注意路標）→ 中風險（操作控制：特定時段行駛）→ 高風險（工程控制：密閉罐車）→ 極高風險（完全隔離：專用道路）。Risk-MaPP 就是幫你決定每種藥品應該用哪個等級的「運輸方式」。

無菌充填 CDMO 的實踐問題

你的廠房同時承接一般抗生素、荷爾蒙藥物、和普通小分子藥品的充填作業。問：

  • 這三類產品應分別採用哪個層次的 controls？
  • 若客戶要求引入一個新的細胞毒性藥物，觸發哪些評估步驟？
  • SME 組成應包含哪些專業背景的人？
1.2 Background（背景）

Original Text

The science of toxicology recognizes the principle of a continuum of hazard associated with all compounds, even within individual classes of compounds. Zero risk is considered scientifically unachievable and not necessary. Some classes of compounds, that have historically included hormones, cytotoxic compounds, genotoxic compounds, pathogenic organisms, and veterinary products, have traditionally been placed in dedicated and segregated facilities to manage the risk. Sensitizers such as Beta-Lactam antibiotics have received particular scrutiny due to the perceived severity of potential allergic reactions.

Dedicated facilities are required for manufacturing active substances and medicinal products with a high sensitizing potential for which scientific data does not support an acceptable level of exposure or the risk associated with the handling of the product at the facility cannot be adequately controlled by organizational or technical measures.

The ICH in their Q9 Quality Risk Management document state: "The manufacturing and use of a drug product, including its components, necessarily entail some degree of risk."

The FDA also has acknowledged: "Although medicinal products are required to be safe, safety does not mean zero risk. A safe product is one that has reasonable risks, given the magnitude of the benefits expected and the alternative available."

The EMA acknowledged: "the concept of 'zero risk' does not apply to medicinal products. The licensing of medicinal products needs to be assessed in the context of the benefit/risk balance concept."

When processing compounds in a multiproduct facility the risk of cross-contamination should be controlled so that products do not have any cross-contamination above established safe levels.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

「零風險」的科學破除

這段背景提出了 Risk-MaPP 最重要的哲學前提：零風險是科學上無法實現的目標。ICH Q9、FDA、EMA 三大機構均一致認同這個立場。

這個前提有實際意義：它允許藥廠用「風險可接受」而非「風險為零」作為決策標準。如果你的稽核員堅持要求「零殘留」，這個概念就是你的科學依據。

高危害藥物的歷史分類

這段列出了傳統上需要專用設施的化合物類型，理解這個分類的歷史背景很重要：

  • 細胞毒性/遺傳毒性藥物：癌症化療藥，低劑量即有效/有毒
  • 荷爾蒙（尤其是性荷爾蒙）：微量即可影響體內平衡
  • Beta-Lactam 抗生素：特殊案例——問題不在毒性，而在致敏性（不可用 HBEL 計算，必須專用廠房）
  • 獸藥：非人體用藥，交叉污染至人用藥風險獨特

注意：Risk-MaPP 的方法論不適用於高致敏性物質（如 Beta-Lactam），那類必須直接專用廠房。

比喻：食品業的過敏原管理

花生過敏者對花生的反應類似 Beta-Lactam 的致敏性——即使是極微量殘留也可能引發嚴重反應（過敏性休克）。這類物質不能用「可接受殘留量」來管理，只能徹底隔離。Risk-MaPP 的風險量化方法適用於「多少量會有多少毒性效果」可以計算的化合物，不適用於「任何量都可能觸發免疫過激反應」的致敏劑。

1.3 Reasons for Revisions（修訂原因）

Original Text

Since the original publication of this Guide there were some significant changes in regulations and application of regulations. These include:

  1. The updated EU GMPs relative to managing the risk of cross-contamination:
    • Chapter 3 was updated to allow the use of quality risk management principles to determine if products can be produced in shared facilities.
    • Chapter 5 was also updated to incorporate quality risk management principles and require toxicological assessments which refers to the EMA guideline.
  2. The EMA issued "Guideline on setting health based exposure limits for use in risk identification in the manufacture of different medicinal products in shared facilities" to provide guidance on how to set health-based limits. This guideline also states "PDE and ADE are essentially synonymous".
  3. ICH issued M7 "Assessment and Control of DNA Reactive (Mutagenic) Impurities" which sets a Threshold of Toxicological Concern (TTC) for mutagenic impurities.
  4. The adoption by US and EU regulators of a "life cycle" approach to process validation incorporating stages of process design, process qualification, and continued process verification.
  5. Industry began implementing risk-based approaches to managing the risk of cross-contamination and sharing some lessons learned.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

五大修訂驅動力的時間軸

第一版（2010 年）→ 第二版（2017 年），7 年間的法規環境劇變：

  • 2014 年 8 月：EU GMP Chapter 3 & 5 更新，正式要求毒理學評估
  • 2014 年 11 月：EMA HBEL Guideline 發布，定義 PDE = ADE
  • 2014 年：ICH M7 發布，設定基因毒性雜質的 TTC 概念
  • 2015 年：EU GMP Annex 15 更新，清潔驗證限值需基於毒理學評估
  • 2011 年起：FDA Process Validation Guideline 推動生命週期方法

關鍵等號：PDE = ADE

這個等式非常重要：

  • PDE（Permitted Daily Exposure）= EMA 用語，源自 ICH Q3C 殘留溶劑概念
  • ADE（Acceptable Daily Exposure）= ISPE/Risk-MaPP 用語，業界工業衛生傳統

兩者計算方法本質上相同——都基於毒理學數據，考慮 NOAEL、不確定因子、體重調整、給藥途徑等。EMA 在 2014 年指引中正式聲明兩者「本質上同義」，統一了業界術語。

實務提示：在文件中，使用 PDE/ADE 都可以被接受，但最好保持一致，並說明與另一術語的關係。

生命週期驗證方法的重要轉變

第 4 點的清潔驗證生命週期概念影響深遠：

  • 舊觀念：清潔驗證 = 三批次測試，通過後完成
  • 新觀念：清潔驗證 = 持續的過程控制，需要：
    設計階段（了解殘留行為）→ 確認階段（驗證清潔有效性）→ 持續驗證（監控日常清潔效果）

這個概念與 Risk-MaPP 高度契合：風險管理不是一次性評估，而是持續的生命週期活動。

Chapter 2: Concepts and Regulatory Philosophy（第二章：概念與法規哲學）

Original Text

The current situation is that there exists some variation between regulatory authorities in the interpretation of commensurate control strategies associated with pharmaceutical manufacturing. Worldwide, regulators are beginning to support the use of risk-based approaches to determine the acceptable level of controls required for the compliant manufacture of highly hazardous compounds within multiproduct facilities, but there are still several regions that have not embraced a risk-based approach.

2.1 EudraLex – EU GMP Updates（歐盟 GMP 更新）

Original Text

In August of 2014 the European Commission updated Chapters 3 and 5 to provide improved guidance on managing the risk of cross-contamination. These changes encourage the use of risk management principles to manage the risk of cross-contamination. Both chapters reference a toxicological evaluation as described in the EMA "Guideline on setting health based exposure limits."

Section 3.6 has updated guidance on when dedicated facilities are required:

"Dedicated facilities are required for manufacturing when a medicinal product presents a risk because:
i. the risk cannot be adequately controlled by operational and/or technical measures,
ii. scientific data from the toxicological evaluation does not support a controllable risk (e.g. allergenic potential from highly sensitising materials such as beta lactams) or
iii. relevant residue limits, derived from the toxicological evaluation, cannot be satisfactorily determined by a validated analytical method."

Section 5.20 states: "A Quality Risk Management process, which includes a potency and toxicological evaluation, should be used to assess and control the cross-contamination risks presented by the products manufactured. Factors including; facility/equipment design and use, personnel and material flow, microbiological controls, physico-chemical characteristics of the active substance, process characteristics, cleaning processes and analytical capabilities relative to the relevant limits established from the evaluation of the products should also be taken into account."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

必須專用廠房的三種情況

EU GMP 3.6 列出的三個觸發點非常清晰，也是稽核員的檢查清單：

  1. 技術/操作措施不足：即使使用所有工程控制手段，風險仍無法充分控制
  2. 毒理學數據不支持：最典型的就是 Beta-Lactam 的致敏性——科學無法設定「安全殘留量」
  3. 無法驗證的分析方法：如果連殘留量都無法被可靠地偵測，就無法確認清潔有效性

注意：這三點是「必須」（required）專用，不是「建議」。

實務應用：Section 5.20 的稽核意涵

Section 5.20 是 EU GMP 查廠時最常被引用的條文。稽核員會問：

  • 你的 QRM 流程是否包含毒效學評估？（ADE/PDE 報告在哪？）
  • 設施/設備設計、人員/物料流向是否在風險評估中被考慮？
  • 你的分析方法是否足夠靈敏到偵測到相關殘留限值？
  • 是否有定期審查機制確保管制措施持續有效？

2.2 EMA HBEL Guideline（EMA 健康暴露限值指引）

Original Text

The EMA introduced this guideline in November 2014. This guideline introduces the Permitted Daily Exposure (PDE) to determine a substance specific dose that is unlikely to cause an adverse effect if an individual is exposed at or below this dose every day for a lifetime. This is an extension of the PDE introduced in ICH Q3C(R6) for residual solvents.

This guideline outlines the process used to develop a substance specific PDE and a suggested summary document. The EMA document also states that the PDE and ADE are essentially synonymous. This guidance document is applicable to API manufacture as well as finished dosage manufacture.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

PDE 的概念起源與擴展

PDE 概念的發展歷程：

  • ICH Q3C（1997）：PDE 首次用於藥品中殘留溶劑的安全限值
  • ICH Q3D（2014）：擴展 PDE 概念至元素雜質
  • EMA HBEL（2014）：進一步擴展至藥品活性成分在共用設施中的交叉污染控制

PDE 定義的關鍵詞：「一生每天暴露於此劑量或以下，不可能引起不良效應」。注意「終生」（lifetime）和「任何途徑」（any route）兩個重要假設。

PDE/ADE 計算框架（概覽）

PDE = NOAEL × 體重調整係數 / (F1 × F2 × F3 × F4 × F5)

  • NOAEL：無觀察到不良效應劑量（來自毒理學研究）
  • F1：種間差異（動物→人，通常 ×5 或 ×12）
  • F2：個體間差異（一般 ×10）
  • F3：研究期限（短期 vs. 長期研究）
  • F4：嚴重/不可逆毒性調整
  • F5：其他不確定性

計算 PDE 需要具備毒理學專業背景的 SME。不建議非專業人員自行計算。

適用範圍：API 製造 + 製劑生產

EMA HBEL 指引明確涵蓋原料藥（API）製造廠和製劑廠（finished dosage）兩個層次。這意味著：

  • API 供應商做多種 API 共用設備時，需要 HBEL
  • 製劑廠做多產品充填時，同樣需要 HBEL
  • CDMO 同時承接 API 合成和製劑業務，兩個場景都要評估
2.3 EU GMP Annex 2 – Biological Medicinal Products（生物藥品製造）

Original Text

Annex 2 states "Unlike conventional medicinal products, which are manufactured using chemical and physical techniques capable of a high degree of consistency, the manufacture of biological active substances and medicinal products involves biological processes and materials, such as cultivation of cells or extraction from living organisms. These biological processes may display inherent variability, so that the range and nature of by-products may be variable. As a result, quality risk management (QRM) principles are particularly important for this class of materials and should be used to develop the control strategy across all stages of manufacture so as to minimise variability and to reduce the opportunity for contamination and cross-contamination."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

生物藥品的特殊挑戰

Annex 2 提醒為什麼生物製品更需要 QRM 思維：

  • 固有可變性：生物製程（細胞培養、發酵）天然就有批次間差異，副產物組成不固定
  • 複雜性：蛋白質、多醣等大分子的特性遠比小分子複雜，難以用單一化學指標評估風險
  • 交叉污染的特殊形式：生物藥的交叉污染可能包括宿主細胞蛋白、DNA、病毒等

Risk-MaPP 原則可以應用於生物製品，但 Annex 2 的引用是提醒：評估時需要考慮生物製品的獨特複雜性。

CDMO 無菌充填的實際含義

若你的 CDMO 同時承接生物製劑（如單株抗體）和小分子注射劑的充填，需要特別注意：

  • 生物製劑的 ADE 計算需要不同的毒理學考量（效價、免疫原性等）
  • 清潔驗證需考慮大分子蛋白質的清潔困難性
  • 分析方法需能區分並偵測生物製劑特有的殘留物

Original Text

The cleaning validation section of Annex 15 (as well as other sections) was updated in 2015. The Annex states that "Limits for the carryover of product residues should be based on a toxicological evaluation." and the EMA guideline on setting health-based exposure limits is referenced. PIC/S Annex 15 adopted the same language in a recent update.

2.5 US FDA 21 CFR 211.42（美國 FDA 法規要求）

Original Text

The US FDA regulation that often is cited when there are concerns with cross-contamination is 21 CFR 211.42 that states "There shall be separate or defined areas or such other control systems for the firm's operations as are necessary to prevent contamination..."

During the planning meetings for the original ISPE Risk-MaPP Baseline® Guide published in 2010, the FDA specifically requested that this Guide:

  • Provide an approach to identify highly hazardous drugs
  • Provide a risk management/assessment model that gives a clear view on how to address the controls to comply with 21 CFR 211.42(c)
  • Discuss how the approach fits into cleaning validation

2.5.1 Approach to Identify Highly Hazardous Drugs

The original intention of this Guide was to provide a method to identify highly hazardous compounds. However, during the development of the first edition, it became apparent that an approach to identify acceptable risk in the manufacture of pharmaceutical products was more appropriate.

Health-based limits should be developed by toxicologists from available toxicological and pharmacological data. This Guide refers to this health-based limit as the Acceptable Daily Exposure (ADE) value. The ADE value represents a dose that is unlikely to cause an adverse effect if an individual is exposed, by any route, at or below this dose every day for a lifetime.

This Guide is predicated on some key concepts:

  • Hazard: the inherent property of a compound to produce harm.
  • Exposure: contact with the hazard; dependent on the control systems used.
  • Risk: the probability of occurrence of harm at a given level of exposure, reflecting severity of harm.

2.5.2 Risk Management Model for 21 CFR 211.42(c)

21 CFR 211.42(c) states that "Operations shall be performed within specifically defined areas of adequate size. There shall be separate or defined areas or such other control systems for the firm's operations as are necessary to prevent contamination or mixups..."

2.5.3 How the Approach Fits into Cleaning Validation

Residues from a compound which remain after cleaning on equipment or other product contact surfaces may pose a risk to patient safety. Cleaning procedures should be developed to minimize the risk from residues. To reduce risk, cleaning procedures should be capable of reducing residues to, or below, a predetermined safe level based on the risk assessment.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

21 CFR 211.42 的解讀重點

這條法規的關鍵在於：「separate or defined areas or such other control systems」。

「or such other control systems」是 Risk-MaPP 存在的法律空間——FDA 的條文本身已允許用「其他管制系統」替代「實體隔離」。Risk-MaPP 提供的就是這個「other control systems」的科學依據和文件架構。

危害 × 暴露 = 風險：三角關係

Risk-MaPP 的核心風險公式：

  • 危害（Hazard）：化合物的固有屬性，不能改變（ADE 是量化指標）
  • 暴露（Exposure）：接觸危害的程度，可以透過管制措施降低
  • 風險（Risk）= 危害嚴重性 × 暴露發生機率

結論：因為危害（化合物毒性）無法改變，降低風險的唯一手段是降低暴露。這就是所有管制措施的理論基礎。

「高危害藥物辨識」的概念轉變

值得注意的思維轉變：第一版原本想提供「高危害藥物清單」，但最終改為提供「可接受風險評估框架」。

原因：

  • 「高危害」是相對概念，取決於管控能力
  • 貼標籤（cytotoxic、potent）容易引發情緒化決策
  • 科學的做法是：設定 ADE → 評估暴露 → 計算風險 → 決定管控等級

清潔驗證整合框架

Risk-MaPP 與清潔驗證的整合邏輯：

  1. 取得化合物 ADE（毒理學評估）
  2. 根據批量大小、設備接觸面積、後續產品最小劑量計算清潔限值（swab 或 rinse 限值）
  3. 確認分析方法靈敏度足以偵測該限值
  4. 驗證清潔程序可將殘留量降至限值以下
  5. 持續監控（生命週期方法）
2.5.4 Regulatory Environment（法規環境演進）

Original Text

From 2003 through 2015, ICH working groups produced several significant documents: ICH Q3C(R6), Q3D, Q7A, Q8(R2), Q9, Q10, Q11, Q12 and ICH M7. These documents have been or are being adopted by the ICH parties (US, Japan, and EU) and used worldwide.

Key milestones:

  • ICH Q3C(R6): Introduced the PDE concept for residual solvents
  • ICH Q3D: Clarifies PDE limits for elemental impurities
  • ICH Q7 (section 4.4): Dedicated production areas for highly sensitizing materials (penicillins, cephalosporins) and consideration for high pharmacological activity compounds
  • ICH Q8(R2): Introduced Quality by Design — "quality should be built into a product with a thorough understanding"
  • ICH Q10: Effective pharmaceutical quality system throughout the product life cycle
  • ICH M7: Mutagenic impurities — limit of 1 in 100,000 excess cancer risk

In 2002, the FDA announced its Pharmaceutical cGMPs for the 21st Century – A Risk-Based Approach initiative. In 2007, it was renamed to "Pharmaceutical Quality for the 21st Century" to capture the larger issue of product quality.

In 2011 the FDA Process Validation Guideline states quality product is defined as the absence of unacceptable adulteration due to cross-contamination with other products produced in a shared facility.

The FDA OPQ key concepts applied to cross-contamination:

  1. Risk is a function of hazard and exposure
  2. There is a continuum of hazard — no distinct lines between low and high hazard
  3. Pharmacological and toxicological descriptions (NOAEL, ADE) should be used — not subjective terms like "potent"
  4. A consistent, robust, science risk-based approach across the hazard continuum
  5. Health-based exposure limits should not be artificially reduced
  6. GxP is primary; IH (worker safety) also must be addressed holistically

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

「不得人為降低 ADE」的重要警示

第 6 點（健康暴露限值不應人為降低）是業界常見錯誤的直接反應：

錯誤做法：毒理學家算出 ADE = 1 mg/day，管理層說「太高了，改成 0.1 mg/day 更安全」。

問題：如果 0.1 mg/day 是正確的科學限值，那原本的毒理學評估有誤；如果 1 mg/day 是科學正確的，人為降低到 0.1 mg/day 的唯一效果是讓不必要的「不符合規定」更容易發生，然後需要解釋「為什麼超出一個不必要嚴格的限值是可接受的」——這在邏輯上是自相矛盾的。

「避免情緒化標籤」的文化意義

第 3 點批評「cytotoxic」「potent」等主觀標籤——這是對舊文化的挑戰。在製藥業，「這是細胞毒性藥物，必須最高等級防護」是一種自動觸發的決策模式，不需要科學計算。Risk-MaPP 說：不對。你需要算 ADE、評估暴露、才能決定管控等級。這個轉變需要組織文化的改變。

2.6 Overview of the Risk Management Approach（風險管理方法概覽）

Original Text

A risk management approach should be applied to the handling of pharmaceutical products. This should be a holistically defined process to formally identify hazards and understand risks to assist decision making for the implementation of appropriate approaches for risk control. Although this process should be a formal part of an organization's quality system, the level of effort and detail for the risk management process can, and should, vary based on the perceived risks which are based on hazard, including severity and probability of occurrence.

The risk assessment process should be used to derive criteria that can assist in decision making and control strategies to manage the risks to the patient. The risk assessment should be a transparent evaluation of all data relevant to hazard identification, assessment of potency (dose response) and severity, exposure assessment, and characterization of risk.

ICH Q9 outlines a quality risk management process that is iterative and consists of the identification of the hazards and the analysis and evaluation of risks associated with exposure to those hazards, as well as control strategies to manage the risks.

這個原則很實用：

2.7 Use of Health-Based Exposure Limits（健康暴露限值的應用）

Original Text

An API represents a hazard, as it is capable of producing biological effects (e.g., pharmacological or toxicological). The likelihood that these effects will occur depends on:

  • The dose-response relationship
  • The amount absorbed as determined by the route, level, and duration of exposure

The ADE takes these into consideration and reflects a safe dose which incorporates a margin of safety. The main premise of this Guide is that if exposure to an API is kept below a prespecified level that is determined to be safe, this would indicate low risk for adverse health effects.

A number of different terms have been used to describe health-based limits:

  • PDE — Permitted Daily Exposure (ICH Q3C, Q3D; EMA 2014)
  • ADE — Acceptable Daily Exposure (ISPE Risk-MaPP; industrial hygiene)
  • ADI — Acceptable Daily Intake (food additives)
  • TI — Tolerable Intake
  • RfD / RfC — Reference Doses/Concentrations (US EPA)
  • OEL — Occupational Exposure Limits (OSHA PELs, ACGIH TLVs, EC SCOEL IOELVs)

For carcinogens: a different risk-based approach — ICH M7 uses 1 in 100,000 excess cancer risk for mutagenic impurities.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

致癌物的特殊處理：線性無閾值模型

一般毒性化合物使用「閾值模型」（NOAEL → 安全係數 → ADE）；但對基因毒性/致突變物質（mutagens），使用不同的「線性無閾值模型」：

  • ICH M7 設定 1/100,000（十萬分之一）終生超額癌症風險
  • TTC（毒理學關注閾值）= 1.5 μg/day（當數據不足時的保守預設值）
  • 這個數字比一般 ADE 通常要嚴格得多

比喻：速限的制定原理

ADE 就像道路速限——不是「越慢越安全」就越好。速限是根據道路設計、事故統計、使用者特性等數據科學制定的「平衡點」。人為將 ADE 降低十倍，就像把安全速限 60 km/h 改為 6 km/h——雖然更「保守」，但不必要的嚴格且不符合科學。

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

GMP 視角 vs. 工業衛生視角：為何兩者都重要

這兩個視角在製藥廠中代表兩個不同的職能部門：

  • GMP/品質部門：關注病患安全，產品殘留，清潔驗證
  • 工業衛生/HSE 部門：關注員工安全，工作環境暴露，PPE

問題：這兩個部門使用不同的標準體系，有時會產生矛盾。例如：某藥品 OEL = 0.01 mg/m³（工業衛生認為極危險）但 ADE = 5 mg/day（GMP 認為殘留風險很低）。如何整合？

OEL vs. ADE 的數值關係

兩者可以相互換算但不能直接比較：

  • OEL（mg/m³）需要乘以呼吸量（約 10 m³/8小時工作日）才能得到每日攝入量
  • ADE（mg/day）是更通用的基礎，可以轉換成任何暴露場景的限值
  • 通常：ADE < OEL 轉換後的日劑量（因為病患保護需要更嚴格）
  • 但也有例外：某些藥物對工人的吸入途徑特別危險（職業性哮喘），OEL 可能更嚴格

比喻：工廠消防安全 vs. 生產安全的協調

消防部門說：「這個化學品儲存量超過消防規定，必須減少」；生產部門說：「我們需要這個量才能保持生產連續性」。兩者都對，但需要一個整合方案。Risk-MaPP 的 IH-GMP 平衡就是同樣的整合思維——不能只顧一方而忽略另一方。

CDMO 組織架構啟示

這個章節對 CDMO 組織架構有直接啟示：

  • Risk-MaPP 風險評估團隊應同時包含 GMP/品質代表和工業衛生/HSE 代表
  • 新產品引入評估流程應同時觸發 GMP 風險評估（交叉污染）和 IH 暴露評估（員工安全）
  • 兩者都認為「可接受」才能啟動生產，不能只過一方的審核
  • 如果兩者結論衝突（例如 GMP 說 OK 但 IH 說需要更高防護），需要有明確的決策程序

  • Risk-MaPP 使命：為共用廠房的交叉污染風險管理提供科學的、基於 ICH Q9 的系統化方法，同時保護病患（GMP）和員工（IH）
