醫療器材區域的 HVAC 設計考量 | p246-p247

16.1 Introduction

The regulations regarding medical devices provide opportunity for risk assessment to define different requirements for the associated HVAC systems.

There are three classifications of device according to the FDA:

  • Class I – those subject to the least regulatory control, as they present minimal potential for harm to the user, hence are subject to “general controls.” Class I devices include elastic bandages, examination gloves, and hand held surgical instruments. They are subject to 21 CFR Part 820.
  • Class II – Class II devices are devices subject to special controls, i.e., those for which general controls alone are insufficient to assure safety and effectiveness, and existing methods are available to provide such assurances. Examples of such products include powered wheelchairs, infusion pumps, and surgical drapes. Again, HVAC requirements are relatively simple.
  • Class III – this is the most stringent regulatory category. Class III devices are those for which insufficient information exists to assure safety and effectiveness solely through general or special controls. Class III devices are usually those that support or sustain human life, including heart valves, silicone gel filled breast implants, and implanted cerebella stimulators.

The FDA Medical Device Quality Systems Manual makes the following statements: “A controlled environment is, to various degrees, an integral part of most production facilities. Some environmental factors to be considered are lighting, ventilation, temperature, humidity, pressure, particulates, and static electricity. Section 820.70 (c), Environmental Control, of the QS regulation, is considered by the FDA as a ‘discretionary’ requirement: that is, the degree of environmental control to be maintained should be consistent with the intended use of the device and details of how to achieve this control are left to the manufacturer to decide.”

It also makes the statement that “General air conditioning is not normally regarded as an environmental control; however, changes in temperature and lighting can have an adverse effect on employee performance and, in turn, on assuring that the device is properly assembled, inspected, and tested.”

This Guide suggests that for each operation, the manufacturer should analyze the operations to identify the controls needed for the finished device to meet the specifications and be fit for use. Many Class I and Class II devices may require only comfort HVAC or at best, CNC. Although the manufacture of some Class III devices may start in an environment similar to a machine shop (with comfort HVAC at best), at some point, they become functionally implantable, requiring CNC or classified spaces, depending on sterility of the device.

The guidance provided in the FDA “Guidance for Industry for Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing – Current Good Manufacturing Practice” often is used as an environmental specification in the absence of any specific guidance for Medical Devices.

教學解析

FDA 醫療器材三級分類與 HVAC 需求

FDA 將醫療器材依風險等級分為三類，HVAC 需求隨風險等級遞增：

  • Class I（第一類）：彈性繃帶、檢查手套等低風險產品 → 一般舒適空調（Comfort HVAC）即可
  • Class II（第二類）：電動輪椅、輸液幫浦等中風險產品 → HVAC 要求相對簡單
  • Class III（第三類）：心臟瓣膜、植入式裝置等高風險產品 → 可能需要 CNC（Controlled Not Classified，受控非分級）或分級潔淨空間

比喻說明：器材分類如同料理衛生等級

想像三種不同的料理情境：

  • Class I 像是包裝外帶咖啡 — 只需基本衛生就行
  • Class II 像是餐廳廚房 — 需要適當的通風排煙設備
  • Class III 像是手術室等級的無菌餐食（給移植患者吃的）— 需要最嚴格的潔淨環境

醫療器材越是「進入人體」，HVAC 環境要求就越高。

關鍵法規觀念：「裁量性要求」

FDA 的 21 CFR 820.70(c) 環境控制條款被視為「裁量性要求」（discretionary requirement），意思是環境控制的程度由製造商自行決定，但必須與器材的預期用途一致。

這代表：沒有一個固定的「器材 HVAC 標準」可以套用。製造商必須進行風險評估（Risk Assessment），根據產品用途來定義合理的 HVAC 需求。

CDMO 實務應用

在同時承接藥品與醫療器材製造的 CDMO 中，常見的挑戰是：客戶（器材製造商）無法提供明確的 HVAC 規格。此時常見做法是引用 FDA 無菌藥品 GMP 指引作為環境標準，這雖然保守但安全。

對於 Class III 植入式器材（如人工關節），在組裝過程中從「機加工」階段轉換到「可植入」階段時，HVAC 等級需要相應升級。設計時應規劃清楚的等級轉換區域與氣鎖。

16.2 Clean Workstations for Medical Devices

As medical devices commonly do not include potent compounds (with the exception of combination devices) horizontal flow clean workstations may be employed to protect product without endangering operators. Horizontal flow workstations are preferred as they present the lowest risk of operator contamination of components which are being assembled for implantation.

水平層流工作站（Horizontal Flow Clean Workstation）

醫療器材通常不含高活性化合物（除了複合型器材），因此可以使用水平層流工作站：

  • 氣流方向：HEPA 過濾後的潔淨空氣從後方水平吹向操作者方向
  • 優點：提供產品保護，且組裝植入式元件時，操作者汙染產品的風險最低

17.1 Equations Used in HVAC and their Derivation

HVAC 系統常用方程式與推導 | p248-p253

17.1.1 Room DP (Differential Pressure)

Air, like any gas, expands as it is heated and contracts (becomes more dense) as it is cooled. For every 5°F (3°C) that air is heated, it expands (becomes less dense) by approximately 1%.

The ideal gas law states that the pressure (P) and the volume (V) of a gas are proportional to its temperature (T). If a gas (in HVAC, the gas is air) is heated, it wants to expand to a larger volume, but if it is constrained in a fixed volume container, its pressure will increase and the air becomes more dense.

PV = N Ru T

Where Ru is the universal gas constant and N the mass of the gas in moles. Since Ru is constant, and N is usually fixed for a particular situation, the equation reduces to:

PV is proportional to T

Bernoulli’s Equation for fluid dynamics also plays a role in HVAC.

P/ρ + V²/2 + gh = constant

Where g is the Earth’s gravitational constant, h is elevation, and ρ (rho) is the density of air. In HVAC, air density is affected by altitude above sea level, but for practical HVAC applications (assuming the manufacturing building is less than 10 stories high), air density and elevation are essentially constant. Thus, Bernoulli’s Equation applied to HVAC is:

P/ρ + V²/2 = constant

Where the subscripts 1 and 2 imply two different points along an airflow path:

(P2 – P1) is proportional to (V1)² – (V2)²

From this, it can be deduced that pressure is proportional to the square of the airflow velocity, i.e., to double the velocity of airflow between two points along a fixed path, the pressure difference between them must be quadrupled. In HVAC, V2 often is zero (it is the air inside a space at zero velocity). If zero velocity air is to be accelerated into an opening to move it to another location or under a door to pressurize a room, according to Bernoulli:

P2 – P1 is proportional to (V1)²

This can be used in calculating the velocity of air flowing through the cracks around a door at a given DP between two rooms. Further manipulation yields the simplified method:

VP ≈ P2 – P1

Where VP is the velocity pressure of the airflow within the door crack. This equation also can be used to calculate the velocity inside the crack (from ASHRAE):

V = 4005 × √VP

Where:

  • V is in feet/minute
  • VP is in inches water gauge (1 inch wg = 254 Pascals)

4005 is a constant derived for air at roughly standard temperature (68°F) and at sea level.

This method ignores “duct” entry loss and the pressure recovery that offsets it, such that the resultant calculated air velocity will be more than is required to create a pressure differential between two rooms. In this case, VP can be replaced with DP:

V = 4005 × √DP

For example, the VP of air flowing at 890 feet per minute is 0.05 inches wg or 12.5 Pa. The above equation implies that the air flowing through the cracks between two rooms at 0.05 inch DP has a velocity of 890 feet per minute.

Using the equation Q = V × A, where Q is cubic volume per time (CFM), V is velocity (ft/min), and A is area (sq ft):

An open 20 square foot door with air flowing through it at 890 ft/min (to maintain 0.05 inch DP) requires airflow of 17,800 CFM. This may be more than the capacity of the entire HVAC system and could provide a robust justification for airlocks.

A closed “pharmaceutical grade” door typically has a crack area of 0.3 square feet or less, needing less than 300 CFM to maintain 0.05 inch wg across the closed door. A specific leakage rate should not be assumed without knowing about the type of door to be used.

核心公式推導：壓差與風速的關係

理想氣體定律：PV = NRuT
  （壓力 × 體積 = 莫耳數 × 氣體常數 × 溫度）

Bernoulli 方程式（HVAC 簡化版）：
  P/ρ + V²/2 = 常數

  ΔP ∝ V²  （壓差與風速的平方成正比）

門縫風速公式（ASHRAE）：
  V = 4005 × √DP
  V = 風速 (ft/min)
  DP = 壓差 (inch wg)
  4005 = 標準條件常數（68°F，海平面）

風量公式：
  Q = V × A
  Q = 風量 (CFM)
  A = 截面積 (ft²)

關鍵物理概念

  • 壓差與風速的二次方關係：風速加倍 → 壓差需要增加四倍。這就是為什麼維持大面積開口（如開門）的壓差極為困難。
  • 4005 常數：此常數適用於海平面標準條件。在高海拔地區（如丹佛，空氣密度約低 15%），此常數需要修正。

比喻說明：門縫壓差如同水壩洩洪

想像一個水壩（潔淨室）。水壩的高度差（壓差）驅動水流通過洩洪口（門縫）：

  • 門關閉時：洩洪口很小（0.3 ft²），只需少量水流（300 CFM）即可維持水位差
  • 門打開時：洩洪口巨大（20 ft²），需要超大流量（17,800 CFM）才能維持相同水位差 — 這通常超過整個系統的供水能力

這正是潔淨室需要氣鎖（Airlock）的物理原因！

實務要點：門的類型影響巨大

  • 緊密無菌室門（帶密封條）：在 10 Pa 壓差下洩漏量僅 50 CFM
  • 倉庫滑門（無密封）：洩漏量可超過 1,000 CFM
  • 設計空氣平衡時，必須知道將使用哪種類型的門 — 門封的小細節對空氣平衡有重大影響

17.1.2 Fans

A useful equation for fan power is:

HP is proportional to η × P × Q

Where HP is horsepower, η is fan efficiency at the operating point, P is fan pressure, and Q is fan airflow in cubic volume per time (such as CFM).

From the pressure equations, if airflow in an existing duct system needs to be doubled, the fan’s delivery pressure will need to be quadrupled, as well as having its airflow doubled; therefore, needing eight times the horsepower. It is considered better to slightly oversize an HVAC system’s fan and ductwork and not need all the horsepower installed, rather than run out of horsepower when the system cannot supply sufficient air to meet required room particulate levels.

教學解析

風機功率公式與三次方定律

HP = η × P × Q

風量加倍的影響：
  Q → 2Q      （風量加倍）
  P → 4P      （壓力增至 4 倍，因 ΔP ∝ V²）
  HP → 2 × 4 = 8 倍馬力

結論：風量加倍 → 馬力需增加 8 倍！

工程設計原則：寧大勿小

指引建議風機和風管應適度超大設計：寧可裝了多餘馬力不用，也不要系統風量不足。因為事後增加風量的成本極高 — 需要更換風機、改裝風管，甚至加裝局部 FFU 單元。

這是「設計裕度（Design Margin）」的典型應用。

17.1.3 Room Air Balance

A basic air balance equation is:

Air Volume in = Air Volume out

Supply + Infiltration = Return + Exhaust + Exfiltration

For a fixed volume (i.e., not a balloon), any air that enters the room has to leave the room. In a cleanroom, exfiltration is difficult to measure (it is the air flowing under the door and out the cracks in the wall), but it can be calculated. An air balance check should be performed on each fixed volume, including air handlers.

Note that air handled only inside a room, such as with a ceiling mounted FFU or local class 100 hood, does not actually leave the room or enter it; therefore, it does not affect the room’s air balance relative to the building. However, the FFU unit does add its air changes as well as filtered air supply volume to the room HVAC supply, and it will contribute to faster room recovery time and help reduce room airborne particle levels.

教學解析

空氣質量守恆（Air Mass Balance）

對任何固定體積的空間：

  • Supply（送風）：透過 AHU 和風管送入的空氣
  • Infiltration（滲入）：從較高壓力區域透過縫隙滲入的空氣
  • Return（回風）：透過回風口回到 AHU 的空氣
  • Exhaust（排風）：排出建築的空氣
  • Exfiltration（滲出）：透過門縫、牆縫滲出到較低壓力區域的空氣

FFU 不影響房間空氣平衡

天花板式 FFU（Fan Filter Unit，風機過濾單元）只在房間內部循環空氣，不引入或排出空氣，因此不影響房間與建築之間的空氣平衡。但 FFU 確實增加了房間內的有效換氣次數，有助於降低粒子水平和加快恢復時間。

17.1.4 Airborne Particle Levels

Another simplified equation deals with air particles per unit volume (C):

Cavg = Cs + PGR/Q

Where:

  • Cavg is average particles per cubic foot in the pressurized room
  • Cs is the particle concentration in the air supply (often negligible)
  • PGR is the steady state internal particle generation rate in particles per minute
  • Q is the supply airflow in cubic feet/minute, including contributions from in-room fan-HEPA units

With little air mixing (turbulence) in the room, localized values of C may be orders of magnitude more or less than Cavg. When a room is at rest and PGR approaches zero (assuming no particles leaking into the room), the equation above indicates that room counts will eventually approach the particle counts in the supply air.

Note that this equation ignores air changes and room volume. The value of Cavg will be the same regardless of room volume as long as the airflow (Q) and particle generation (PGR) are constant. Hence, the particle counts in a big room running a specific process will be the same as the particle counts in a small room running that identical process, as long as the Q and particle counts of the supply airflow are the same.

教學解析

粒子濃度穩態方程式

Cavg = Cs + PGR / Q

Cavg = 房間平均粒子濃度 (particles/ft³)
Cs   = 送風空氣粒子濃度（通常可忽略）
PGR  = 粒子產生速率 (particles/min)
Q    = 送風量 (CFM)

關鍵洞察：
  - Cavg 只與 Q 和 PGR 有關
  - 與房間大小無關！
  - 大房間與小房間跑同樣製程，
    只要 Q 和 PGR 相同，粒子濃度相同

比喻說明：粒子濃度如同水族箱的濁度

想像一個水族箱：魚不斷攪動沙子（PGR = 粒子產生），過濾器不斷淨化水（Q = 送風量）：

  • 過濾器流量越大（Q 增加），水越清
  • 魚越多/越活躍（PGR 增加），水越濁
  • 不管水族箱大小，只要魚的活動量和過濾器流量相同，濁度就相同

局部值可能與平均值差距極大

如果房間內空氣混合不良（紊流不足），局部粒子濃度可能比平均值高或低數個數量級。這就是為什麼送風口和回風口的位置配置如此重要 — 良好的空氣分佈才能確保整個房間均勻。

17.1.5 Recovery

Room volume is an element of the measurement of air changes in a room. The formula for air changes is:

AC/hr = 60 × Q / Volume

Where AC/hr is room air changes per hour, Q is CFM supply in cubic feet per minute, and Volume is the volume of the room in cubic feet.

From the two equations above it appears that AC/hr is merely an indicator of air change rates in the room and not directly associated with air quality (it is CFM that most determines steady state air quality). However, air changes per hour play two critical roles:

  1. Air changes need to be sufficiently high to assure sufficient turbulence in a room to achieve thorough mixing and dilution, such that particle counts are relatively the same throughout the room (except under local unidirectional hoods). This may require more than 10 (and usually more than 20) air changes per hour.
  2. Air changes per hour affect how quickly a room can recover from its in-use state to its at-rest state. European GMPs require this recovery to be 15 to 20 minutes.

The ISPE Baseline® Guide on Sterile Manufacturing Facilities uses the formula:

C(at rest) = (Cop – Cs) × e(-Nt) + Cs

Where Cop is in-operation particle count, N is number of air changes, t is time, and Cs is supply air particle counts (usually close to zero).

From the room recovery graph, a 100-fold recovery (from say C = 10,000/volume to C = 100/volume, typical of an EU Grade B room) would occur in less than 15 minutes with 20 air changes per hour. So, for cleaner classified rooms (EU grades B and C), a minimum starting point would be an HVAC supply CFM that creates 20 AC/hr although more Q may be necessary if internal PGR is high.

In addition to air changes, the thoroughness of airborne particle counts throughout a room depends on the configuration of air supply and return openings. A single supply outlet near a single return inlet would lead to cleaner air in the path between the two with poor mixing in other parts of the room. Such a room would show a slower recovery than a room with multiple well-distributed air supply outlets and low level returns.

教學解析

換氣次數與恢復時間公式

換氣次數：
  AC/hr = 60 × Q / Volume

恢復時間（指數衰減模型）：
  C(at rest) = (Cop - Cs) × e^(-Nt) + Cs

  Cop = 運轉中粒子計數
  Cs  = 送風粒子計數（≈0）
  N   = 換氣次數 (per hour)
  t   = 時間 (hours)

範例：EU Grade B 100 倍恢復
  從 10,000 → 100 particles
  20 AC/hr → 約 14 分鐘
  30 AC/hr → 約 9 分鐘

換氣次數的兩個關鍵角色

  1. 確保充分混合：至少 10 次/hr（通常需 20 次以上）才能保證房間各處粒子濃度均勻
  2. 決定恢復速度：EU GMP 要求恢復時間在 15-20 分鐘內，這直接決定了最低換氣次數

重要區別：穩態粒子濃度由 CFM（送風量）決定，而恢復速度由 AC/hr（換氣次數）決定。兩者數學上相關但概念不同。

送風口與回風口的配置影響恢復時間

即使換氣次數相同，送風口和回風口的佈局也會顯著影響恢復速度。單一送風口+單一回風口會造成「短路」— 兩者之間的路徑很乾淨，但房間其他區域混合不良、粒子偏高、恢復緩慢。

最佳做法：多個分散的送風口 + 低位回風口。

17.1.6 Cascaded HEPA Filters

A HEPA filter passes a percentage of upstream particles of the MPPS (Most Penetrating Particle Size — traditionally, particles at 0.3 microns). The MPPS of a modern HEPA may be closer to 0.15 to 0.25 micron. For a given particle size, the overall leakage of a series of HEPA filters in a supply air path is the product of the leakages for each of the filters:

Ltot = L1 × L2

For a pair of standard 99.97% HEPA filters (assuming 0.03% leakage at MPPS):

Ltot = 0.03% × 0.03% = 0.0009% leakage at MPPS

Therefore, placing two 99.97% HEPA filters in series with no particle sources between them creates a virtual 99.9991% HEPA filter at the MPPS.

In addition to the advantages in particle removal, there is an engineering advantage if the primary HEPA is at the air handler and the second HEPA filter is at the room (a terminal HEPA). The terminal filter will receive very little challenge, and therefore, its pressure drop increases so slowly that its flow is not reduced significantly over months or maybe years of service. Air balance of the system is easier, requiring less in-duct hardware, such as CV devices, and room pressure deviations because of decay of supply airflow are less likely.

Note: (according to EN1822) HEPA filters may be rated at MPPS. A HEPA rated 99.97% at 0.3 micron has less leakage for larger particles; it also should have less leakage for smaller particles. As bacteria and spores usually are much larger than 0.3 micron, and viruses are smaller than 0.3 micron, a 99.97% filter with MPPS at 0.3 micron is considered a suitable filter for pharmaceutical applications. Although HEPA filters capture 99.97% or better of particles at 0.3 micron, their true MPPS may be somewhat smaller with a capture rate lower than 99.97%. A HEPA filter rated at 99.97% at 0.3 micron may actually be as low as 99.9% at 0.1 to 0.2 micron MPPS. If viruses are a concern, the HEPA may be scanned to 99.99% or better using a smaller aerosol; ULPA filters may be advisable.

串聯 HEPA 洩漏計算

  Ltot = L1 × L2

範例（兩個 99.97% HEPA）：
  L1 = 0.03%，L2 = 0.03%
  Ltot = 0.0003 × 0.0003 = 0.0000009
       = 0.00009%
  等效效率 = 99.99991%

  傳統定義：0.3 μm
  現代 HEPA：0.15 - 0.25 μm
  ULPA：0.10 - 0.15 μm

串聯 HEPA 的雙重優勢

  1. 粒子去除能力：兩個 99.97% HEPA 串聯等同於一個 99.9991% 的虛擬超級過濾器
  2. 工程穩定性：
    • 主 HEPA 在 AHU 中承受大部分粒子挑戰
    • 末端 HEPA（Terminal HEPA）幾乎無負載，壓降增長極慢
    • 風量衰減極小 → 空氣平衡更穩定，數月甚至數年免維護
    • 減少對恆風量裝置（CV devices）的需求，降低系統複雜度

HEPA 效率的真相：MPPS 不等於 0.3 μm

99.97% 的效率是在 0.3 μm 測定的，但真正的 MPPS 可能在 0.1-0.2 μm 之間，此處的實際效率可能低至 99.9%。如果需要對病毒（通常小於 0.3 μm）進行防護，應考慮：

  • 使用更小氣溶膠（如熱產生 PAO）掃描至 99.99% 以上
  • 改用 ULPA 過濾器

17.3 HEPA Filter Arrangements

HEPA 過濾器佈置策略 | p254-p256

17.3.1 Air Filtration Arrangements

It is common practice for aseptic manufacturing facilities to recirculate air back to the air handler unit. Generally, this is good practice as it limits the particle load on the filters, reduces the cost of conditioning outdoor air, and optimizes control. However, there are other factors to account for:

  • potential for cross-contamination in multi-purpose facilities
  • accidental recirculation of product-contaminated air affecting operators or plant maintenance staff

These factors may be overcome by the use of return air filters. If the logic is that these are to capture airborne contamination; however, they need to be of the “safe change” type to protect maintenance personnel.

The environmental standards in the FDA Sterile Drug Products GMP identify a 0.5 μm particle size as the reference point. Only HEPA and ULPA filters have quantified performance ratings against a most penetrating particle size, usually sizes smaller than 0.5 μm. When looking at sub-micron particle reduction by filtration, only HEPA and ULPA filters should be considered as effective.

The location of HEPA filters within a system should be at points such that there is no opportunity for the air to become re-contaminated. The use of terminal supply (ceiling) HEPA filters is recommended for classification of Grade 7 and cleaner. They also have the additional advantage of maintaining the sealed envelope of the processing area should the supply fan fail.

Figure 17.2: A Possible Aseptic Area Filter Arrangement (diagram referenced in original text)

DOP and PAO (and Sodium Flame) penetration tests use a high concentration of particles of a known spectrum (usually in the range of 0.3 μm diameter). Efficiency results then can be related to specific size of airborne particle. Only filters tested by such methods have reliable data for this size of particle. Filters grades lower than HEPA are tested by averaging methods in which specific particle size efficiencies are not identified.

Note: European H14 air filters can pass a penetration scan test, but the purchaser must request that H13 filters do so.

教學解析

空氣過濾策略的核心考量

  • 空氣回風再利用：是常見且良好的做法 — 降低外氣處理成本，但在多品種設施中需注意交叉汙染風險
  • 回風過濾器：若用於捕捉產品粉塵，必須使用「安全更換型」（Safe Change / Bag-in Bag-out），保護維護人員免受暴露
  • 末端 HEPA 位置原則：HEPA 應安裝在空氣不會再被汙染的位置 — 因此推薦 Grade 7（ISO 7）及更高等級使用天花板末端 HEPA

末端 HEPA 的額外安全效益

末端天花板 HEPA 在送風機故障時仍能維持製造區的「密封外殼」效應 — 過濾器本身就是一道物理屏障，阻止管道中的未過濾空氣進入潔淨室。這是單純在 AHU 中安裝 HEPA 無法提供的保護。

H13 vs. H14 過濾器（EN 1822）

  • H14：必須通過穿透掃描測試（Penetration Scan Test），確保整個過濾面無局部洩漏
  • H13：預設不需通過掃描測試 — 但採購時可以特別要求
  • 藥廠通常要求所有 HEPA 在安裝後進行現場完整性測試（In-situ Integrity Test），無論是 H13 或 H14

17.3.2 Prefiltration for Terminal HEPAs

A single HEPA filter bank in normal circumstances is adequate to reduce supply particulate concentration below that of a reasonable “at rest” design classification. An important consequence of using a single HEPA bank is that the supply air 0.5 μm particle count is unlikely to be near zero. This could affect the calculations of air change volume flow rate to offset particulate gains, and in particular, recovery periods.

Another potential problem to be addressed if only terminal HEPA filters are used, is that of filter blinding that can result in reversed DPs putting environmental conditions at risk.

Figure 17.3: Example of the Effects of Terminal Filter Differential Blinding

Terminal filter blinding can result in design DPs being reversed. There are a number of possible solutions:

  • Install a main bank of HEPA filters.
  • Install a main bank of high efficiency non-HEPA filters (minimum MERV 13/F7 or better) in the AHU.
  • Employ active pressure or constant air volume control.
  • Frequently replace terminal HEPA filters.
  • Install airflow or pressure drop indicators to indicate filter performance.

The option to use high efficiency pre-filters in series with the HEPA has a number of advantages:

  • The effects of differential blinding will be minimized. The performance of the terminal filters will be maintained constant for years longer, balancing the ratio between supply and extracts, and in turn, DPs.
  • Because the bank of filters handles the main particulate load and acts as a prefilter in the AHU, only these will require regular replacement. This reduces replacement maintenance costs and downtime.

It is recommended that high efficiency non-HEPA pre-filtration be installed in all new or renovated facilities employing HEPA terminal filtration.

教學解析

過濾器阻塞（Filter Blinding）的連鎖效應

當末端 HEPA 過濾器逐漸阻塞時：

  1. 通過該過濾器的風量下降
  2. 該房間送風減少 → 壓力降低
  3. 其他房間的末端 HEPA 阻塞速率不同 → 不同房間風量比例改變
  4. 設計壓差可能被反轉 — 較低等級房間壓力反而高於較高等級房間！

這是一個嚴重的 GMP 風險。

預過濾設計建議（強烈推薦）

指引明確建議：所有新建或改造設施，若使用末端 HEPA，都應在 AHU 中安裝高效預過濾器（至少 MERV 13 / F7）。

好處包括：

  • 末端 HEPA 壽命延長數年，壓降變化極小
  • 差異化阻塞最小化 → 各房間風量比例穩定 → 壓差穩定
  • 只需定期更換 AHU 中的預過濾器 → 降低維護成本和停機時間
  • 減少對恆風量（CV）或主動壓力控制裝置的依賴

比喻說明：預過濾如同泳池的粗篩網

游泳池的過濾系統通常有粗篩網（攔截樹葉）和精濾器（過濾細小雜質）。如果沒有粗篩網，精濾器很快就會堵塞。HVAC 中的預過濾與末端 HEPA 正是這個關係 — 預過濾攔截大部分粒子，讓 HEPA 只處理最細小的粒子，大幅延長其使用壽命。

17.5 Additional Controls Information

控制系統補充資訊：致動器與控制閥 | p257-p260

17.5.1 Electrical/Electronic Actuators

The actuator uses a low voltage control signal to drive an electric motor; the units can be on/off or proportional. These systems are used where the speed of actuation can be slower, typical times for a valve to go from fully open to fully closed are in the range of 1 to 2 minutes.

Installation is simple, as all signals are by cable, e.g., control signal, power supply, and feedback, such as valve position or open/closed signals. The actuators can be supplied as fail open, fail closed, or with a manual override.

17.5.2 Pneumatic Actuators

An electronic control signal (from a stand-alone controller, BMS, or Process Controller) varies the output air pressure from a pneumatic controller, which is fed to a pneumatic actuator on the controlled component.

The system requires the use of an I/P (control signal to pneumatic) converter, an instrument quality air supply, and local tubing to the actuator. In order to get the best response time, the converter should be as close as possible to the actuator.

The units use air pressure in one direction with an opposing spring to return the controlled item to the fail position. The system is proportionally controlled. These units typically have a faster response time than an electric or electronic unit. The all-pneumatic system also is ideal for hazardous areas requiring intrinsically safe installations.

教學解析

Fail Position 的重要性

「失效位置」是 HVAC 安全設計的關鍵概念。當供電或供氣中斷時，閥門必須自動回到安全位置：

  • 冷卻盤管閥：通常設為 Fail Closed（失效關閉），避免過度冷卻
  • 加熱盤管閥：在寒冷氣候可能設為 Fail Open（失效開啟），防止盤管凍裂
  • 每個閥門的 Fail Position 都應在設計階段明確定義並記錄

兩種致動器（Actuator）的比較

17.5.3 Control Valves for Liquids

There are two types of control valve: the three port valve (mixing or diverting) and the two port valve (directly controls flow). The three-port valve was the industry standard; however, the use of two port valves with variable flow rate systems is becoming more common.

17.5.3.1 Valve Characteristic

The valve characteristic is the ratio of flow through the valve to the valve lift (opening) at a constant DP across the valve. There are three main types:

  • Fast opening – typically used for only on/off control
  • Linear – flow rate directly proportional to the amount it is open; commonly used for diverting applications
  • Equal percentage – flow rate has a logarithmic relation to the amount it is open; more commonly used in water flow and steam control applications

17.5.3.2 Flow Coefficient (Cv)

A simple flow coefficient calculation for liquids:

In English Units: Cv = gpm × (SG/dp)1/2

Where gpm = water flow (US gallons per minute), SG = specific gravity (1 for water), dp = pressure drop (psi)

In SI Units: Cv = 11.7 × CMH × (SG/dp)1/2

Where CMH = water flow (Cu.Meter/hour), dp = pressure drop (KPa)

Select a valve where the required Cv is in the 30 to 80% range of the stroke. A valve that is too small or too large would not have the ability to control the flow accurately, i.e., will not have adequate “authority.”

17.5.3.3 Valve Authority

Valve Authority is the percentage of total system pressure drop assigned to the valve. If the valve’s pressure drop is small in comparison to the pipe and heat exchanger losses, the valve will have less ability to control effectively.

教學解析

控制閥選型三要素

  1. 閥門特性（Characteristic）：根據應用選擇
    • 快開式：僅用於開/關控制
    • 線性式：流量與開度成正比 → 適合分流/混合應用
    • 等百分比式：流量與開度呈對數關係 → 適合蒸汽和水流控制
  2. 流量係數 Cv：選閥時所需 Cv 應在閥門行程的 30-80% 範圍內
  3. 閥門權限（Authority）：閥門壓降占系統總壓降的比例，比例太低則控制能力不足

Cv 計算公式

英制：Cv = gpm × √(SG / dp)
  gpm = 流量（美制加侖/分鐘）
  SG  = 比重（水 = 1）
  dp  = 閥門壓降（psi）

SI 制：Cv = 11.7 × CMH × √(SG / dp)
  CMH = 流量（立方米/小時）
  dp  = 閥門壓降（KPa）

選型規則：
  所需 Cv 應在閥門額定 Cv 的 30-80% 行程處
  閥門太小（< 管線尺寸一半）→ 控制粗糙
  閥門太大（≥ 管線尺寸）→ 控制不穩定

比喻說明：閥門權限如同水龍頭控制

想像你家的水龍頭（控制閥）和整條水管系統的水壓損失。如果水管本身的摩擦損失很大（比如管線很長、很細），即使你把水龍頭從全開轉到半開，出水量變化也不大 — 因為水龍頭的壓降只占總壓降的一小部分。

HVAC 控制閥的「權限」（Authority）就是同樣概念。閥門壓降至少要占系統總壓降的 30-50%，才能有效控制流量。

The purpose of temperature mapping helps confirm that the area as a whole remains within its defined limits and determine the locations representing temperature extremes within the area.

The second objective defines the locations for permanently installed sensors that provide data for the quality system of record for the area, the information from which the MKT (Mean Kinetic Temperature) is calculated (if required).

There are benefits to a smaller number of sensors for this purpose, while it is necessary to use a sufficient number of sensors to ensure that the mapping study is robust.

The method is based on the size of the chamber and the area potentially occupied by product. For chambers over 700 cubic feet (20 cubic meters), the standard suggests considering:

  • door openings
  • location of the cooling system
  • position of the control sensor

An approach that has been used with some success is to consider the area around each HVAC outlet as a zone and apply the sensor placement logic with a sensor adjacent to the temperature control sensor as well as others monitoring the supply and return temperatures.

For an area bounded by an external wall, the area requires mapping in the seasonal extremes in order to determine the influences of heating and cooling supply temperatures from the HVAC system, and the heating or cooling effect of the external walls and roof adjacent to the racking.

Mapping Considerations

  • Product temperatures – if upon delivery these are significantly different from defined storage conditions, the product may require monitoring.
  • Loading and unloading – will the product be at a different temperature, how much is loaded/unloaded at any one time, will there be significant ingress/egress of conditioned air?
  • Is there an outside door that can be left open or is it monitored to a defined maximum time?
  • Storage locations nearest the heating/cooling system inlet/outlet
  • Internal loads – lighting, equipment, and personnel
  • Conditions during minimum (empty) and full storage capacity

Example Plan

External Heat Gains and Losses: The major heat gains and losses to the cold room are due to the influences of the external environment with the most significant being through the walls and ceiling.

Internal Heat Gains and Losses:

  • Lighting – low energy fluorescent lamps, left on during the working day
  • Product – supplied at storage temperature, will not represent additional load
  • Equipment – stacking fork truck, a relatively small intermittent load
  • People – 2 people occasionally working in the area; heat gains are small and transient

HVAC: The room is conditioned through two registers with a common return air grille located at high level in the center of the room.

Mapping Sensor Placement: The areas considered during the mapping are only the areas where product will be stored. The proposed sensor locations are based on: considering the chamber as two zones with each zone supplied by a register; placing a temperature sensor at the lowest and highest storage point at each corner on the racking nearest the perimeter; and one sensor at the center at the midpoint level.

Load Testing: The unit will be mapped during peak summer and winter seasons, considering two load scenarios: empty chamber and one half of the warehouse kept empty with the other half filled with full boxes simulating the largest stored pallet (40″ × 48″ × 46″ tall).

Note that the HVAC has constant flow rate and with the room empty, airspeed will be low. When the room is not empty, the airflow will be at a higher speed, as the same volume of air must travel through a smaller area.

教學解析

溫度分佈驗證的雙重目的

  1. 確認區域整體合規：驗證整個儲存區域在定義限值內
  2. 決定永久感測器位置：找出溫度極值點，作為品質記錄系統中永久監測感測器的安裝位置，用於計算 MKT（Mean Kinetic Temperature，平均動力學溫度）

感測器佈局策略（NF X15-140 法國標準）

  • 小型腔室（≤ 2 m³）：最少數量的感測器放置在工作區角落
  • 中型腔室（≤ 20 m³）：增加感測器數量，覆蓋更多位置
  • 大型區域（> 20 m³）：以每個 HVAC 出風口為一個「區域」，在每區應用標準佈局
  • 必須在控制感測器附近放一個驗證感測器，以確認控制點的代表性

季節性測試的必要性

有外牆的區域必須在夏季和冬季極端條件下各做一次溫度分佈驗證，原因：

  • 夏季：外牆傳熱使靠牆區域偏熱 → HVAC 冷卻負載增加
  • 冬季：外牆散熱使靠牆區域偏冷 → 可能出現局部低溫
  • 結果可能截然不同，最高溫點和最低溫點的位置可能互換

CDMO 倉庫溫度驗證實務

對於藥品倉庫的溫度驗證，以下實務要點尤其重要：

  • 負載測試：空倉 vs. 滿倉的氣流模式完全不同 — 滿倉時貨架阻擋氣流，風速增加但分佈可能不均
  • 進出貨影響：大量進出貨時外門開啟，會大量引入未調節外氣 → 需評估最長開門時間
  • 產品溫度：冬天從室外運入的產品溫度可能遠低於儲存條件 → 需要額外的回溫監測

比喻說明：溫度驗證如同冰箱體檢

家用冰箱也有「溫度分佈」問題：門邊比較溫暖、後壁比較冷、上下層溫度不同。溫度驗證就像對冰箱做徹底體檢 — 找出最熱和最冷的點，確保所有儲存位置都在安全範圍內，然後在極端位置放一個溫度計長期監控。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 醫療器材 HVAC：FDA 三級分類（Class I/II/III）決定 HVAC 需求等級。環境控制是「裁量性要求」，由製造商透過風險評估自行定義。Class III 植入式器材可能需要分級潔淨空間，常參照 FDA 無菌藥品 GMP 作為環境標準
  • 九大潔淨室方程式：從理想氣體定律到串聯 HEPA 計算，這些公式構成 HVAC 設計的數學基礎。最實用的是 Q=VA（風量）、AC/hr=60Q/V（換氣次數）、Cavg=Cs+PGR/Q（粒子濃度）
  • 壓差與風速的二次方關係：風速加倍需壓差增四倍、馬力增八倍。這解釋了為什麼開門狀態難以維持壓差（需 17,800 CFM），也是氣鎖存在的物理基礎
  • 恢復時間優先：設計應以目標恢復時間反推換氣次數，而非直接套用固定值。20 AC/hr 可在 14 分鐘內完成 100 倍恢復（EU Grade B 要求）
  • 串聯 HEPA 雙重優勢：兩個 99.97% HEPA 串聯可達 99.9991% 效率，且 AHU 主 HEPA 承擔大部分負載使末端 HEPA 壓降穩定數年，簡化空氣平衡
  • 預過濾強烈建議：所有使用末端 HEPA 的設施應安裝高效預過濾（MERV 13/F7），防止差異化阻塞導致壓差反轉
  • 控制閥選型：需同時考慮閥門特性（快開/線性/等百分比）、Cv 流量係數（30-80% 行程範圍）和閥門權限（Authority）
  • 溫度驗證：雙重目的（確認區域合規 + 決定永久感測器位置），有外牆區域需在夏冬極端條件下各做一次，空倉與半滿倉各做一次負載測試
