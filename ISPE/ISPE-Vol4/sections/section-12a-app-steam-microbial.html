11.7 Clean Steam Generator - System Selection & Sizing 11.7 純蒸汽產生器 - 系統選擇與尺寸設計

原文內容 Original Text

A generator designed to deliver the maximum output at a relatively high clean steam pressure will deliver significantly more steam at the lowest pressure and vice versa.

This appears to be an advantage and often is; however, there can be problems if the performance is not well understood and is taken into consideration at the design stage and later during operation.

Example Calculation

A generator is sized to deliver 2000 lbs/hr (909 kgs/hr) clean steam at 60 psig (4.2 kgs/cm² gauge), utilizing plant steam at 120 psig (8.43 kgs/cm² gauge). Operating at a reduced level of 30 psig (2.1 kgs/cm² gauge) clean steam pressure and assuming 5 psi (0.35 kgs/cm²) pressure drop across the steam control valve:

  • Net steam pressure to evaporator = 120 - 5 = 115 psig (8.08 kgs/cm²), saturated temperature 347.3°F (175.17°C)
  • CS temperature @ 60 psig = 307.63°F (153.13°C)
  • CS temperature @ 30 psig = 274.46°F (134.7°C)
  • ΔT @ 60 psig = 347.3 - 307.63 = 39.67°F (22°C)
  • ΔT @ 30 psig = 347.3 - 274.6 = 72.7°F (40.5°C)

The amount of steam produced: W = U × A × ΔT

Where: U = heat transfer coefficient (Btu/hr/ft²/°F), A = CS evaporator surface area (ft²).

Since the heat transfer surface is fixed, and the change in the heat transfer coefficient is negligible, the amount of clean steam produced is directly proportional to the temperature difference.

CS @ 30 psig = (72.7 / 39.67) × 2000 = 3,665 lbs/hr (1666 kgs/hr), or 183% of that at 60 psig.

Total volume of steam @ 30 psig = 9.403 × 3665 = 34,462 ft³/hr, or 296% of that @ 60 psig. Since the steam passage areas including the separator have not changed, the velocity of steam increases approximately 300%.

If this increase is not anticipated and planned for at the design stage, there can be a potential for carryover of contaminants through the separator due to the higher steam velocity.

In addition to the high velocity and carryover considerations, operating at a temperature difference above 70°F (21°C) is not recommended without consulting the CS manufacturer. At high temperature difference, a heat transfer phenomenon called "dry wall condition" can occur — the steam is so much hotter than the water on the other side that the surface cannot be maintained wet, leaving behind a residue (scale). The problem is more of a concern when the CS feed water has relatively high TDS, such as with softened water.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：壓力差與產量的反比關係

潔淨蒸汽產生器的本質就是一個熱交換器：用廠蒸汽 (Plant Steam) 加熱進水，蒸發產生潔淨蒸汽。產量取決於兩者的溫差 (ΔT)。

當你降低潔淨蒸汽壓力時，CS 的飽和溫度降低，但廠蒸汽溫度不變，所以 ΔT 增大 → 產量上升。這聽起來很好，但隱含的風險是蒸汽體積暴增、流速飆升、分離器可能無法有效攔截雜質。

關鍵公式 Key Formula

W = U × A × ΔT

W = 蒸汽產量 (lbs/hr)
U = 傳熱係數 (Btu/hr/ft²/°F) -- 近似不變
A = 蒸發器面積 (ft²) -- 固定
ΔT = 廠蒸汽溫度 - CS 飽和溫度

結論：產量與 ΔT 成正比

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你用大火煮一鍋水，鍋子大小固定（A 不變）、火力不變（U 不變）。如果你把鍋蓋的壓力閥調鬆（降低 CS 壓力），水的沸點降低，火焰跟沸水的溫差變大，水蒸氣產生得更快。但鍋子的排氣孔沒有變大——蒸氣衝得太快，可能把鍋裡的湯汁（雜質）一起噴出來。這就是「carryover」的道理。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • ΔT > 70°F (21°C)：必須諮詢製造商，否則有 dry wall condition 風險——蒸發面過熱導致結垢 (scale)
  • 蒸汽速度增加 300%：分離器設計未考慮這種工況時，會導致內毒素和鹽類穿透 (carryover)
  • 高 TDS 進水更危險：軟化水的 TDS 較高，乾壁效應會加速結垢

11.7.1 Feed Water Pressure, Pump & Contact Surfaces 11.7.1 給水壓力、泵與接觸面

原文內容 Original Text

11.7.1.2 Feed Water Pressure and Pump

The feed pressure is typically 8-10 psi (0.5-0.7 kgs/cm²) above the clean steam pressure, which is typically 30-60 psig (2.1-4.2 kgs/cm² gauge), a feed pressure range of approximately 40-70 psig (2.8-4.9 kgs/cm² gauge). Most CS generators have outputs of less than 4,000 lbs/hr (1,816 kgs/hr). Many have outputs of less than 1,000 lbs/hr (450 kgs/hr). That is a feed rate of slightly more than 2 gpm (7.5 l/min).

The sanitary centrifugal feed pumps favored by the pharmaceutical industry will be operating near shutoff head at flow rates of less than 10 gpm (38 l/min), particularly with very small generators. Installing pump circulation loops complicates piping, and under certain scenarios may call for installing a heat exchanger to address temperature buildup. This adds undue complexity.

Consider the possibility of feeding the generator from an existing source with sufficient pressure. The dedicated pump, even at very low flow rate, is most likely the next best option.

11.7.1.3 Feed Water Contact Surfaces

It is common that users specify mechanical and electropolish and sanitary clamp connections for piping, heat exchangers, and vessels in contact with the feed water although such features are not necessary for achieving the desired clean steam quality.

Often, the reason given for such requirements is attributed to having the feed to the CS piped from the WFI or USP Purified Water loop. Such practices rule out the use of the less costly Simple CS Generator which otherwise may be acceptable.

Consider the economics of the proposed feed source at the selection stage. If WFI or USP Purified is the most logical source, consider the possibility of using an atmospheric break or sanitary backflow preventers so that the CS does not become an extension of the source loop.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

進水壓力設計邏輯

進水壓力 = CS 壓力 + 8~10 psi。這個「多出來的壓力」用來克服管路阻力並確保水能穩定進入蒸發器。如果壓力不足，進水會不穩定，蒸汽產量波動，嚴重時甚至導致乾燒。

大部分 CS 產生器的進水流量很小（2 gpm 左右），這對衛生型離心泵來說太接近關機揚程 (shutoff head)，操作在這個區域泵效率極低且容易過熱。

重點提示：別過度設計進水管路

ISPE 在這裡明確指出一個常見的過度設計陷阱：因為進水來自 WFI 或 PW 迴路，就要求所有進水管路都要 electropolish + sanitary clamp。

實際上，進水管路的表面處理規格不需要等同於分配系統的規格，因為水進入 CS 後會被蒸發——蒸汽品質取決於蒸發器和分離器的效能，而非進水管路的粗糙度。

建議：在進水管路與 WFI/PW 迴路之間裝設 atmospheric break 或 backflow preventer，避免 CS 成為分配迴路的「延伸」，這樣可以降低整個迴路的驗證負擔。

比喻說明 Analogy

就像你家的自來水管接到熱水器——熱水器入口管不需要跟淨水器出水管一樣的材質等級。水進入熱水器後會被加熱消毒，入口管的光潔度對最終熱水品質影響不大。但如果你不裝逆止閥，熱水器裡的水可能回流污染冷水系統。

11.7.2 Utilities Requirements 11.7.2 公用設施需求

原文內容 Original Text

Plant Steam Flow Rate, lbs/hr: Make provisions for 110-120% of the maximum expected clean steam production. Generators with feed heaters and low TDS feed water will use closer to the 110% rate, and those without feed heaters and with high TDS (high blowdown rate) will use closer to the 120% rate.

Plant Steam Pressure should be nominally 50-60 psig (3.5-4.2 kgs/cm² gauge) above the clean steam pressure.

Feed Water Rate: With very low TDS, DI, or equal water, the typical feed rate is 105%-110% of the CS production rate depending on the size of the generator. With higher TDS feed water, the blowdown rate is increased to lower the concentration of salts. The feed varies and often is 115%-120% of CS rate.

Electric Power: Other than controls, the only power requirement is for the feed/booster pump when used. Since almost all CS generators require relatively small flow rates, the power requirement is mainly dependent on the boost pressure. Motors will vary from 2-5 horsepower (1.5-3.75 kW).

Instrument Air: 80-100 psig (5.6-7.0 kgs/cm² gauge) instrument air is required for systems with air operated controls.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示：TDS 與排污率的關係

TDS（總溶解固體）越高，蒸發器內鹽類濃縮越快，就需要更頻繁地排污 (blowdown) 來避免結垢。排污越多，進水補充就越多。這就是為什麼高 TDS 進水需要 115-120% 的進水率，而低 TDS 只需 105-110%。

實務建議：在 URS 階段就要確認進水水質，因為它直接影響公用設施的規模設計。如果後期從 DI 水改為軟化水，所有公用設施計算都要重來。

11.7.3 Periodic Cleaning and Derouging 11.7.3 定期清潔與除鏽

原文內容 Original Text

It is common for CS generators and other stainless steel equipment used in high purity steam and water applications, to develop rouge or scale. Some of the cleaning chemicals can be very harsh and may etch the stainless surface. The extra cost on electropolishing becomes wasted after one harsh cleaning.

When contracting an outside service to perform the cleaning and any subsequent passivation, ensure chemicals used do not cause etching. It is common to avoid passivation with nitric acid if the surface is electropolished, using citric acid and ammonium citrate instead.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

Rouge（紅鏽）的本質

Rouge 是不鏽鋼在高溫純水/蒸汽環境中形成的氧化鐵沉積物，外觀呈紅棕色。在 WFI 和 CS 系統中非常常見，尤其是在 80°C 以上持續運作的設備。Rouge 不一定代表系統故障，但過度積累可能影響水質、堵塞過濾器。

重點提示：清洗化學品的選擇

  • 避免硝酸鈍化 (Nitric Acid Passivation)：如果表面已經電拋光 (EP)，硝酸可能侵蝕精細的表面結構，浪費先前的投資
  • 推薦替代方案：檸檬酸 (Citric Acid) + 檸檬酸銨 (Ammonium Citrate)，溫和但有效
  • 外包清洗時：必須要求供應商提供化學品清單，確認不會導致蝕刻 (etching)

比喻說明 Analogy

Rouge 就像不鏽鋼鍋具長期高溫使用後出現的彩虹色變色。你可以用溫和的清潔劑恢復，但如果用太強的酸性清潔劑（像是工業用鹽酸），反而會腐蝕鍋面。ISPE 在這裡提醒你：花大錢做的電拋光表面，一次粗暴的酸洗就前功盡棄。

11.7.4 Conductivity Monitoring 11.7.4 電導度監控

原文內容 Original Text

The conductivity of the CS condensate sample on a system with relatively high feed conductivity is an indication of carryover through the separator. Therefore, it serves as a good on-line warning that pyrogens may be present.

When the CS generator is operating on high purity DI feed water, the monitoring of CS sample conductivity or resistivity is meaningless; especially if the conductivity is lower than the limits set in the USP.

In order to verify that the separator system works as intended, it would have to be challenged. Sodium sulfite or other non-chloride-based salt can be injected in the feed to determine the corresponding product conductivity. Recommended salt concentration in the feed is 150-200 mg/l. Based on approximately 10% blowdown, the concentration inside the CS will be 1,500-2,000 mg/l.

The test is recommended after fieldwork or cleaning which may involve the disturbance of the separator system.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

電導度監測的邏輯

電導度（Conductivity）反映水中離子濃度。如果 CS 冷凝水的電導度升高，代表有溶解鹽類從蒸發器「穿過」分離器進入蒸汽——這也意味著內毒素 (pyrogen) 可能一起被帶過來。

但有個陷阱：如果你的進水本身就是高純度 DI 水（導電度極低），那 CS 冷凝水的導電度自然也很低，即使分離器壞了也測不出差異。這就是為什麼 ISPE 說「對 DI 進水的系統，導電度監測毫無意義」。

重點提示：分離器挑戰測試 (Challenge Test)

當導電度監測不可靠時，必須主動進行挑戰測試：

  • 注入亞硫酸鈉 (Sodium Sulfite)，不能用含氯鹽類（避免腐蝕不鏽鋼）
  • 進水中鹽濃度：150-200 mg/l
  • 以 10% 排污率計算，蒸發器內部濃度可達 1,500-2,000 mg/l
  • 測量 CS 冷凝水導電度，判斷分離器攔截效率
  • 何時做？每次維修、清洗或任何可能干擾分離器結構的操作之後

11.8.1 Bacteria in Pharmaceutical Water Systems 11.8.1 製藥用水系統中的細菌

原文內容 Original Text

11.8.1.1 Background

Various types of bacteria can be found in the feedwater to purified water systems. Among the ways that these bacteria can be classified is Gram positive or Gram negative, which is based on their retention of a dye after a staining and washing procedure. The results of the Gram test are dependent on cell wall structure, and therefore indicative of many other characteristics.

Most aquatic bacteria are Gram negative, and bacteria commonly found in purified water systems are generally Gram negative because Gram positives cannot thrive on extremely low nutrient levels. Gram negative bacteria are more heat sensitive than Gram positives, and cannot proliferate at temperatures above 60°C. However, the possibility of Gram positive organisms in the system cannot be completely ruled out.

Bacteria generally range from approximately 0.5 μm to 5 μm. The rod-shaped Pseudomonas diminuta, which is used for challenging sterilizing filters, has a minimum dimension of approximately 0.3 μm.

When Gram negative organisms are killed they release endotoxins; lipopolysaccharide molecules which cause a fever when injected into the bloodstream. Because they cause fever, these substances are termed "pyrogens", meaning generating heat. These substances also have been linked to much more serious reactions when introduced into the blood stream, including lethal septic shock.

Bacteria have a tendency to attach to surfaces and form biofilms. Biofilms form an external polysaccharide layer. This external film protects the bacteria from antimicrobial agents and makes them much more resistant to sanitization procedures. In addition, disinfection of the biofilm does not necessarily remove it from the surfaces. Since the dead biofilm provides nutrients and attachment points for new biofilm, its removal must be considered when developing a sanitization strategy.

Biofilms are difficult to detect since water samples are used to measure free floating (planktonic) bacteria, and these bacterial counts do not directly correlate to biofilm concentration. Prevention or removal of biofilms can be affected by the various continuous and periodic microbial control methods described in this chapter.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：水系統微生物學基礎

理解製藥用水微生物控制，需要掌握三個關鍵角色：

  • 革蘭氏陰性菌 (Gram-negative)：純水系統的主要「居民」，能在極低營養環境中存活。殺死它們會釋放內毒素——注射劑的頭號敵人
  • 內毒素 (Endotoxin/Pyrogen)：脂多醣分子，熱穩定，比細菌本身更難去除。即使細菌被殺死，內毒素仍然存在
  • 生物膜 (Biofilm)：細菌附著在管壁表面形成的「保護城堡」，消毒劑很難穿透

比喻說明：生物膜如同水管內的「青苔層」

你可以把 biofilm 想像成魚缸壁上的那層滑膩的綠色薄膜。用漂白水泡一下可以殺死表面的藻類，但那層黏膜還在。死掉的黏膜反而成為新一批藻類的「養分」和「腳架」。所以 ISPE 強調：消毒 (disinfection) 不等於清除 (removal)。有效的策略必須包含物理去除，而不僅僅是化學殺菌。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 60°C 是分水嶺：革蘭氏陰性菌無法在 60°C 以上繁殖，這就是為什麼 hot loop 設計在 75-85°C 運行
  • 浮游菌 ≠ 生物膜菌：取水樣測到的是浮游菌 (planktonic)，生物膜中的菌量可能遠高於取樣結果
  • Pseudomonas diminuta（0.3 μm）：這就是為什麼除菌過濾器標準挑戰用 0.22 μm 濾膜——要攔截最小的已知病原菌
  • 殺菌釋放內毒素的悖論：在分配系統中殺菌反而可能增加內毒素負荷，所以 WFI 系統強調在純化階段就去除細菌，而非依賴分配系統中的熱殺菌

CDMO 實務應用

在無菌充填的 CDMO 環境中，WFI 系統的生物膜管理是日常營運的核心挑戰之一。如果你的系統在維護後生物膜沒有被完全清除，即使取樣結果短期合格，幾天後菌量就會反彈。這就是為什麼許多 FDA 檢查員會特別關注：你的消毒 SOP 是否包含「清除」步驟，而不僅僅是「殺菌」。

11.8.1.2-1.3 Microbial Levels in PW and WFI 11.8.1.2-1.3 純化水與注射用水的微生物限度

原文內容 Original Text

11.8.1.2 Microbial Levels in USP Purified Water and Non-Compendial Water

Most techniques for measuring microbial populations in a water sample require a time delay of two to three days, and they do not yield an exact value. For this reason, the USP monograph for purified water does not contain a microbial limit. Instead, alert and action level guidelines must be established by the end user.

An action level of 100 CFU/ml is suggested in the USP, and the FDA Guide to Inspection of High Purity Water Systems states that any action level over 100 CFU/ml is unacceptable.

What may be more important than the total count is a continuous monitoring program that indicates trends before they become a problem. It also is important to identify the specific contaminants in addition to the total count. The water system should not add objectionable organisms to the final drug product. Objectionable organisms are defined in the FDA Inspection Guide as "any organisms that can cause infections when the drug product is used as directed, or any organisms capable of growth in the drug product."

Alert levels are set according to a statistical review of data to reveal those results that are above the normal operating range of the system. Action levels indicate that a problem exists and the problem must be investigated and an appropriate corrective action taken.

11.8.1.3 Microbial Limits in WFI

According to the USP, Water For Injection is "intended for use in the preparation of parenteral solutions." WFI is generally expected to be free of microorganisms. Since some microbial contamination may be encountered during sampling, an action limit of 10 CFU/100 ml is commonly specified.

The bacterial requirement which is explicitly stated on the monograph is for bacterial endotoxin. The USP endotoxin limit for WFI is 0.25 EU per ml.

Even bacteria which are destroyed quickly within the storage and distribution system will release endotoxin. Therefore, it is imperative that the treatment system removes bacteria before they get to the distribution system, rather than relying on protection measures within the distribution system (such as heat) to remove bacteria.

Although bacterial contamination in a WFI system will lead to the proliferation of endotoxin, it may be possible to pass an endotoxin limit, but not a bacterial limit so both tests should be performed.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重點提示 Key Notes

  • Alert Level ≠ Action Level：Alert 是「注意，趨勢不對了」；Action 是「必須調查並採取糾正措施」。兩者都不是 pass/fail，而是管理工具
  • 趨勢比單次結果更重要：一個持續接近 alert level 的系統，比偶爾超標一次更危險
  • 鑑定特定微生物：不是只看總菌數，還要鑑定菌種。不同菌種指向不同污染源（Staph = 取樣問題，Bacillus = 環境污染，Gram-negative rods = 水系統本身）
  • 殺菌不等於除毒素：WFI 系統中殺死細菌會釋放內毒素，所以必須在純化階段就除菌，而非依賴分配迴路的高溫殺菌

FDA 稽查重點

FDA 明確表示：PW 的 action level 超過 100 CFU/ml 是「不可接受的」。這意味著如果你的 SOP 把 action level 設在 200 CFU/ml，FDA 稽查員會直接開 observation。

此外，objectionable organisms 的定義是「能在藥品中生長，或在正常使用條件下引起感染的微生物」。即使總菌數合格，如果鑑定出 objectionable organism，仍然是嚴重的品質事件。

11.8.2 Testing and Documentation 11.8.2 測試與文件

原文內容 Original Text

11.8.2.1 General Testing Considerations

All use points in the distribution loop should be represented in a monitoring regimen. Testing of the water being supplied to the loop, prior to dilution in the distribution tank, provides important information on the efficacy of the purification system.

For WFI or other water with endotoxin limitations, LAL testing is typically performed concurrently with the heterotrophic plate count. The water system is typically tested daily with individual sample ports being rotated through to include each point over a specified period.

Sampling each point every week has generally been found acceptable by the FDA. With smaller systems, this may result in only one sample from the system on any day.

The sampling performed should simulate the production use of that water. For example, water used through a hose should be sampled through the same hose. The flush performed prior to sampling (if any) should reflect the flush used prior to production use.

The quantity of water used for analysis is a minimum of 1 ml for purified and non-compendial, and 100 ml for WFI. Larger quantities such as 100 ml for purified and 250 ml for WFI are recommended.

11.8.2.2 Testing Documentation

Sampling procedures should be delineated in an approved protocol (SOP). Testing methods also should be specified in an approved SOP. This would include the equipment and materials to be used, the procedures, growth promotion testing of the media, and negative controls.

Organisms in pharmaceutical water will have adapted to a low nutrient environment, and a standard growth media may shock the organisms and result in erroneously low counts. Therefore, a low nutrient media may be more appropriate for the testing.

For new or modified systems, sampling and testing should be completed as part of a Performance Qualification (PQ). The sampling regimen should include samples taken immediately prior to sanitization in order to exhibit worst case conditions.

11.8.2.3 Analysis of Results

The primary purpose of reviewing test results is to reveal OOS findings. The identification of the organisms found is an important part of the investigation since this will provide strong evidence of the source of contamination:

  • Staphylococcus: typical of sample handling problems
  • Bacillus: typical of environmental contamination of the sample port
  • Gram negative rods: typical of water borne contamination

原文內容 Original Text

11.8.3 Sterilization/Sanitization Designs

Various designs may be incorporated to facilitate heat sanitization of process water distribution systems. In this section, two designs for sanitization are considered:

  • Hot Pharmaceutical Water (80°C) System
  • Ambient Pharmaceutical Water System

11.8.4 Hot System Distribution Loop

In this system, the boundaries begin with the storage vessel and include pumps, distribution piping (all drops) and the return loop that terminates at the storage vessel.

The storage vessel should be a fully jacketed vessel with the capability of heating and cooling. Heating of the storage vessel is usually achieved with steam. Cooling, when required, is achieved with chilled or cooling tower water.

It has been found that a hot distillation system has a tendency to build heat when the effluent from the still is discharging into the tank. The effluent may be as high as 96°C. If the water is too hot, the distribution pump may cavitate or boiling will occur in the pump. In addition, the system may be more susceptible to "Rouge" when water is kept at temperatures exceeding 87°C.

A hot system is generally considered to be self sanitizing as long as the temperature of the circulating water is maintained between 75°C and 85°C.

When the system is down for maintenance or repairs, it will be necessary to sanitize the system. The method used is to flush the system, all sampling points, and all points of use with hot (80°C) water. Flushing with two to three volumes of the water in the distribution system should achieve sanitization.

The heating medium may be hot liquid or steam. It should be designed to heat the ambient process water from 15 to 80°C within a specified period (generally two to four hours).

As a safety note, all exposed piping and storage tank surfaces should be insulated to prevent accidental burning of personnel.

熱系統設計核心要點

熱水循環系統的最大優勢是「自消毒 (self-sanitizing)」——只要維持 75-85°C，革蘭氏陰性菌無法存活。但這個溫度窗口有兩個邊界：

  • 下限 75°C：低於此溫度，部分耐熱菌可能存活
  • 上限 85-87°C：超過此溫度，Rouge（紅鏽）加速形成，且泵可能因接近沸點而汽蝕 (cavitation)

重點提示：溫度管理的兩難

  • 蒸餾出水溫度可達 96°C：必須在儲罐設計中考慮冷卻能力，否則泵會汽蝕
  • Rouge 風險在 87°C 以上急升：需要在「殺菌效果」和「材料壽命」間取得平衡
  • 維護後重新消毒：使用等級不低於系統水質的 80°C 熱水，沖洗 2-3 倍系統容量
  • 升溫時間 2-4 小時：必須在 URS 中明確規定，影響夾套換熱器和蒸汽供應的設計

比喻說明 Analogy

熱水循環系統就像你家的中央暖氣——暖氣水在管路中持續循環保持高溫，細菌就像冬天試圖在暖氣管裡結冰的水滴，只要溫度夠高就不可能存在。但如果暖氣停了一段時間（維護停機），重新啟動時你需要先用最熱的水沖洗整個系統，確保每個角落都被燙過。

安全提醒

ISPE 特別強調：所有外露管路和儲罐表面必須保溫隔熱。80°C 的水接觸皮膚會造成嚴重燙傷。消毒週期中，所有使用點必須有人監控。這不僅是 GMP 要求，更是 EHS（環境健康安全）的基本責任。

11.8.5 Cold System Design 11.8.5 冷系統設計

原文內容 Original Text

The boundaries are the same as a hot system. A by-pass line may be provided to recirculate process water through the pretreatment train to avoid stagnant flow conditions.

Sanitization of a cold system may be accomplished using hot water using two stainless steel double tube sheet heat exchangers, one for cooling and one for heating.

The heat exchanger used for cooling is typically installed after the distribution pump(s). The heat exchanger used for heat sanitization is usually installed on the return loop just prior to the return to the storage tank.

One heat exchanger to heat and cool the process water is a viable alternative, but should be designed for the more extreme temperature range.

The heat exchanger for cooling may be cooled with cooling tower water, chilled, or domestic cold water. The cool down cycle should be designed to cool 80°C process water to approximately 15°C over a specified period (based on the operational needs). The initial cooling should be very gradual to prevent thermal shock.

冷系統消毒設計邏輯

11.8.6 Ozone System 11.8.6 臭氧系統

原文內容 Original Text

Ozone (O&sub3;) is a naturally occurring triatomic form of oxygen. O&sub3; is unstable at atmospheric temperatures and pressures, and decomposes readily into molecular oxygen (O&sub2;). There are no objectionable by-products or residues when water is disinfected with ozone.

Very low concentrations of ozone of the order of 0.1 to 0.2 mg/l have been shown to control microbiological growth to below 1 CFU/100ml. Due to the limited half life, ozone must be produced on-site.

11.8.6.1 Materials of Construction

Few organic materials are unaffected by ozone contact. Gaskets, piping, vessels, filters, and ion exchange resins may be subject to attack. PVC will be attacked by ozone, but PVDF and PTFE are not so vulnerable. Stainless steels that may be pitted by Chlorine resist ozone.

11.8.6.2 Comparisons with Chlorine

Chlorine is relatively stable in water and is not described by any half-life characteristic. Ozone has a relatively short half-life depending on temperature and pH. At neutral pH and 20°C, this can be two hours in high purity water.

Removal of chlorine and many of its bi-products are comparatively difficult to remove. Chlorine may convert many organic substances into derivatives that have been identified with carcinogenicity.

Ozone reverts to oxygen and/or carbon dioxide.

Chlorine requires diffusion through the cell walls of the organism. Tests indicate that chlorine required more than 400 hours at 0.1 mg/l to destroy 99% of 60,000 CFU of E. Coli. Ozone, when added to water using an efficient mixer, kills bacteria in seconds. At an equivalent CFU level, ozone will destroy more than 99% in less than one minute.

11.8.6.3 Ozone Generation

There are two commercial methods to manufacture ozone:

  • Corona discharge: uses an air/oxygen feed. Feed stream must be sufficiently low in nitrogen and moisture to avoid production of harmful levels of nitric acid. Pharmaceutical design typically employs a dryer and a molecular sieve.
  • Electrolytic generation: low conductivity water is used as the feed stream to a catalytic generation cell. Can be operated directly immersed in a side stream or produce gas for a conventional contactor. Typically produces lower contamination levels but more capital cost intensive.

11.8.6.4 Ozone System Installation

The optimum location for ozone injection is in the loop return, just before it re-enters the storage tank. To ensure ozone does not contaminate the pharmaceutical product, it may be removed shortly before the first "point of use."

Ozone destruction can be accomplished by: Catalytic, Thermal, UV, Chemical, and Time (half life in solution is 20 to 100 min).

Ozone monitoring is required: in solution prior to and following ozone destruction, return line to the storage tank, return ozone levels from loop piping during sanitation sequences, and ambient air surrounding the system.

11.8.7 UV Light 11.8.7 紫外光

原文內容 Original Text

11.8.7.1 Ultraviolet Lights

Flowing water may be exposed, at a controlled rate, to ultraviolet light waves. With proper maintenance, UV systems are simple and reliable, generating a high reduction in bacteria (99% plus) when operating at 254 nm wavelengths. However, the feedwater to a UV needs to be free of suspended solids which can "shadow" bacteria, preventing adequate UV contact.

Two different types of mercury vapor arc tubes generate UV light: low pressure and medium pressure. Low pressure is near a vacuum and generates a narrow wavelength band in the 254 nm range. Medium pressure arc tubes, near 1 atmosphere, generate wavelengths ranging from 180 to 365 nm. The peak germicidal efficiency for UV light is between 254 and 265 nm.

11.8.7.2 Design and Installation of UV Lights

Water from a storage tank can be recirculated through UV light systems so that the entire system volume is treated. The minimum recycle rate to maintain disinfection is between 4-6 times per hour.

Conventional UV units have a maximum temperature limitation of 120°F (48.9°C) and require special cooling arrangements to withstand temperatures up to 150°F (65.6°C). Medium pressure units can withstand much higher temperatures up to 212°F (100°C).

Pressure limitations also need to be recognized. Most UV units have an operating pressure limit of 100-120 psi.

Most UV units are installed in a horizontal position to eliminate air pockets.

The use of UV units does not negate the need for periodic sanitization of the system.

11.8.7.3 UV Light Dosage and Sizing Requirements

Proper UV dosage measured in milli-watt seconds per square centimeter (mW·s/cm²) at the chamber wall determines the performance. Each microorganism has been assigned a kill dose, known as D10 that produces a 1 log, or 90% kill rate.

E. Coli has a D10 of 3 mW·s/cm². To achieve a 3 log reduction, a dose of 6 mW·s/cm² is required. Most pharmaceutical water systems require at least a minimum of 30 mW·s/cm² dosage.

Factors involved in UV unit selection:

  • Flow-rate: units must be sized to handle the maximum flow possible
  • Water quality: UV absorbing compounds (iron, manganese, dissolved organics, turbidity) affect germicidal efficiency
  • Identification of microorganisms: high concentration or multiple organisms may dictate a larger UV unit
  • Temperature: low pressure units have specific temperature limitations; in very cold (5-10°C) or hot water (>45°C) the arc tube efficiency is greatly reduced
  • Recirculation rate: minimum 4 times per hour for the entire volume

11.8.7.4 Characteristics

Low-pressure units usually have multiple lamps in a chamber. Medium pressure units usually employ a single lamp approach due to the much higher output. The lamps are enclosed within quartz sleeves which contact the water. All other wetted parts should be manufactured from 316L SS. UV intensity and frequency should be monitored continuously and recorded.

The specified dosage should be obtained even at the end of lamp life (EOL), as all UV lamps degrade and lose germicidal efficiency over time. The units should be sized so that a safety margin is built in so that even at EOL, the minimum dose requirement is always exceeded.
