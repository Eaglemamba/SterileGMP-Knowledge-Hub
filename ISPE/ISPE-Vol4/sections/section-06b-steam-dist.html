Process & Operating Principle of a Sanitary CS Generator — 衛生型淨蒸汽發生器的製程與運作原理

Shell-and-tube evaporator, separator, and feedback control loop | pp. 100–101

Original Text (English) — pp. 100–101

Clean Steam is normally generated in a shell-and-tube heat exchanger evaporator. Feed water is introduced on one side of the tubes, while the heating medium is introduced on the other side. Heating of the feed water to above the boiling temperature causes the water to evaporate, producing steam. The heating medium does not come in direct contact with the feed water or with the clean steam, and is normally utility steam.

a) Operation

Clean Steam pressure is maintained by a feedback control loop, which modulates the supply steam control valve. The evaporator feed water is independently controlled using a level sensor and feed water pump.

b) Steam Supply

The utility steam supplied to the generator at typically 100–120 psig (7.0–8.5 kg/cm² gauge or 7.9–9.25 bar) must be at a higher pressure than the required clean steam pressure. The greater the differential between the utility steam pressure and the clean steam pressure, the higher the clean steam production rate. Utility steam pressure should be at least 30–40 psig higher than the clean steam pressure. Utility steam consumption will be approximately 10%–20% greater than the quantity of clean steam produced.

c) Clean Steam Pressure

Clean Steam pressure is selected by the user. Typical units are designed for pharmaceutical applications at 40–60 psig (3.75–5.1 bar).

d) Separator

Entrainment separators are normally designed to function over an optimum range of steam velocity. If the volume of steam increases substantially, carryover of endotoxins can occur. This condition can exist if the steam pressure differential significantly exceeds design conditions. The manufacturer should be consulted regarding the output of the generator at the highest possible pressure difference. An alarm and equipment shutdown is recommended.

e) Feed Water Level

The feed water level is controlled to protect against flooding of the evaporator and carryover of endotoxins by a high level alarm and subsequent shutdown. Evaporator level condition does not affect clean steam purity, but is an indication of insufficient feed water or excessive blow down.

A feed pump may be required if feed water supply pressure is inadequate. Depending on system design, a feed pressure of approximately 8–10 psig above the maximum expected Clean Steam pressure is required.

A sample cooler fitted with conductivity meter and alarms is often used to monitor Clean Steam condensate purity.

中文解說與學習重點

衛生型 CS 發生器架構

本質上是一個「淨版鍋爐」：以 utility steam 為熱源，在管殼式熱交換器中加熱純化進料水，產生淨蒸汽。關鍵設計原則：

  • 隔離原則：加熱介質與進料水/淨蒸汽「永不接觸」，避免污染交叉
  • 壓力差驅動：utility steam 須高於 CS 壓力 ≥30–40 psig，壓差越大、產量越高
  • 回饋控制：CS 壓力由控制閥自動調節 utility steam 進汽量

比喻：「雙層煮水壺」

想像一個雙層鍋：外層裝「高壓蒸汽」（utility steam），內層裝「純水」（feed water）。外層熱量透過金屬壁傳給內層，內層水沸騰產生「乾淨蒸汽」。內外層液體永遠不混合，確保淨蒸汽純度。

分離器 (Separator) 的關鍵角色

分離器不僅移除內毒素，還移除所有滴狀攜帶 (entrainment)。但它有「速度窗口」：蒸汽流速超過設計範圍時，內毒素 carryover 風險飆升。因此：

  • 必須設置高壓差報警與自動關機
  • 運轉時不得超過製造商設計壓差上限

關鍵數據整理

Utility Steam 壓力:      100–120 psig (7.9–9.25 bar)
CS 壓力 (典型):        40–60 psig (3.75–5.1 bar)
最低壓差:             ≥ 30–40 psig
Utility 消耗量:        CS 產量 × 1.10–1.20
Feed 水壓力:          CS 壓力 + 8–10 psig

思考練習

如果你的工廠 utility steam 壓力只有 80 psig，而淨蒸汽需求壓力為 50 psig，壓差僅 30 psig。這會帶來什麼問題？該如何改善？

Sizing the Clean Steam System — 淨蒸汽系統尺寸設計

Capacity calculation, diversity factor, generator selection | pp. 102–103

Original Text (English) — pp. 102–103

Figure 7-3 provides a systematic approach to sizing the Clean Steam system. The process involves identifying all use points, determining the peak demand for each, applying diversity factors, and selecting a generator with adequate capacity plus a safety margin.

7.8.1.1 CS Produced from a Simple Clean Steam Generator

There are applications where pyrogen free steam and sanitary construction features are not required, and at the same time, Utility Steam cannot be used. In such cases, it may be most economical to utilize a simple Clean Steam generator of the most economical design. Savings may be worthwhile when the elimination of the steam separator is combined with non-sanitary features such as:

  • Non-sanitary pipe and fittings
  • Non-sanitary instruments and valves
  • No polishing
  • Minimum controls

The elimination of the separator alone does not provide significant cost savings. It is important to remember that the separator’s function is more than removal of endotoxins. It removes entrainment, which includes all types of contaminants present in the feed water, except volatiles. Without an entrainment separator, impurities from the feed water may well be entrained in the steam and the moisture content can be much higher than in the standard entrained generator.

Thus the feed water purity becomes a critical factor in controlling the steam purity if entrainment is not incorporated in the design.

Independent sanitary entrainment devices are available for installation at, or close to, the point of use, and may be used with typical “Simple CS generators” as well as to control additional moisture build up due to heat losses in the distribution system of Sanitary CS Generators.

中文解說與學習重點

Sizing 基本步驟

  1. 列出所有使用點：滅菌器、凝水器、SIP 腳位等
  2. 計算尖峰需求：每個使用點的最大蒸汽消耗量
  3. 應用多樣性係數 (Diversity Factor)：不是所有使用點同時運作，典型 0.6–0.8
  4. 加安全裕量：通常 10%–20%
  5. 選型發生器：確保容量 ≥ 計算需求

比喻：「瀎水器」與「淨水器」

Simple CS Generator 就像一臺基本瀎水器：能燒沸水，但不能過濾雜質。如果水源本身夠乾淨，燒出來的蒸汽就沒問題。但如果水源含雜質，就需要「淨水器」（Sanitary CS Generator 的分離器）來確保蒸汽品質。

使用點獨立分離器 (Point-of-Use Separator)

配合 Simple CS Generator 的補救措施：在使用點前安裝獨立衛生級分離器。同樣可用於 Sanitary CS 系統中，補償配送管線熱損造成的額外水分。

7.8.2

Steam Condensate Sampling — 蒸汽凝結水取樣

Purity sampling (endotoxin, conductivity) and quality sampling (dryness, NCGs) | pp. 103–104

Original Text (English) — pp. 103–104

7.8.2.1 Purity Sampling

When required by the process, the steam purity shall be monitored through acceptable sampling techniques. A slipstream of the steam may be passed through a sample condenser/cooler, fitted with a sampling valve.

To ensure that the steam does not contribute to drug product contamination, sampling should be included during commissioning, as a good engineering practice, and/or prior to each time the steam is used.

If the sampling requirement is for endotoxin or pyrogen testing, the sample cooler, tubing and valve should be of sanitary construction.

Sample coolers can be fitted to the CS generator, or located in the distribution line, or at the use point (recommended location), or a combination thereof. It is common practice to fit sample coolers with conductivity monitors and alarms.

Endotoxin removal: The condensate sample from a Clean Steam generator with separator is expected to show 3–4 log10 level reduction in pyrogens compared to the level in the feed water.

7.8.2.2 Steam “Quality” Sampling

Steam “Quality” sampling may be employed to determine the level of saturation and non-condensable gases. This can be determined by applying a steam calorimeter and measuring the dryness or saturation level. A steam calorimeter measures the percentage by weight of steam in a mixture of steam and entrained water.

Purity sampling 就像量血壓：確認「成分」是否合格。Quality sampling 就像測體脂：確認「物理狀態」是否合格（蒸汽夠乾、沒有空氣）。兩者都需要，但目的不同。

實務思考

你的工廠在哪些位置安裝了 sample cooler？是在發生器出口、配送管線中、還是使用點？ISPE 推薦使用點，因為那才是蒸汽實際進入製程的狀態。

7.8.3

Materials of Construction — 材質規格

Stainless steel, titanium, and carbon steel applications | pp. 104–105

Original Text (English) — pp. 104–105

7.8.3.1 Materials for Sanitary and Simple CS Generators

Structural integrity and chemical compatibility with the contact fluid and its constituents are two of the more practical issues that drive construction material selection for CS systems.

The inherent corrosion potential forces CS manufacturers to consider relatively inert metals including stainless steel or titanium. Sanitary piping and valves, considered unnecessary for utility and simple CS generators, are often standard features for CS systems. The materials chosen should not contribute to contamination of the drug product.

Typical materials of construction:

  • Evaporator & separator — Shell, tubesheets, internals: 300 series S.S.
  • Evaporator tubes — 300 series or titanium, or other suitable alloy
  • Heat exchangers (feed heater, blow down, sample cooler) — 300 series
  • Piping — 300 series for water and CS; carbon steel for utility steam contact
  • Valves — 300 series and elastomers/diaphragms for water
  • Skid & structural — Carbon steel

7.8.3.2 Materials for Utility Steam Generation

Chemical compatibility with the utility boiler generated steam and the carried over feed water chemicals are required. Based on particulate levels and required steam purity, more than one filtration stage may be utilized. Acceptable materials must be relatively inert and may include SS or tin-coated copper.

中文解說與學習重點

材質選擇兩大原則

  • 結構強度：耐受蒸汽壓力與溫度
  • 化學相容性：不與淨蒸汽/凝結水反應、不污染藥品

淨蒸汽本身極具腐蝕性（高純水+高溫），因此必須使用耐腐蝕材料。

為什麼用鈰？

鈰合金在蒸發器管子中的優勢：耐腐蝕性比不鏽鋼更佳，尤其對氯化物的抗性。但成本較高，通常在進料水中可能含有微量氯化物的應用中才會選用。

7.8.4

Surface Finish — 表面處理

Mechanical polishing, electropolishing, passivation | p. 104

Original Text (English) — p. 104

Mechanical polishing (MP), electropolishing (EP), and passivation processes are implemented in some stainless CS systems. Chlorine and/or chlorides will damage the generator regardless of the finish.

The operating temperatures of these systems are more than sufficient for inhibiting microbiological growth. Therefore, MP is advocated for final finishing of mechanical welds, with mill finishes and final passivation to optimize the formation of the corrosion resistant chromium oxide barrier. Electropolishing will also optimize the chromium oxide barrier, and should be considered if passivation is not an option.

中文解說與學習重點

表面處理的目的

淨蒸汽系統的表面處理主要目的不是抑菌（因為運作溫度已足夠殺菌），而是：

  • 防腐蝕：通過形成 Cr₂O₃ 氧化鉻保護層
  • 處理焊接痕跡：MP 應用於機械焊接的最終處理

處理方式選擇

處理方式作用適用時機
Mill Finish + Passivation工廠原始表面 + 鈍化增強 Cr₂O₃首選方案
Mechanical Polish (MP)研磨平整焊接處焊接後處理
Electropolish (EP)電化學拋光，最佳化 Cr₂O₃當鈍化無法執行時

氯的致命威脅

無論表面處理多好，氯和氯化物都會摧毀發生器。這是整個 CS 系統最大的化學威脅，必須在進料水前處理階段徹底移除。

7.8.5

Pretreatment for CS Generators — 淨蒸汽發生器前處理

Scale, corrosion, and volatile carryover considerations | pp. 105–106

Original Text (English) — pp. 105–106

The feed water pretreatment for a CS generator is born from three separate and distinct considerations:

  1. Scale formation
  2. Corrosion
  3. Volatiles which carryover with the steam and may affect steam purity

7.8.5.1 Scale

Scale formation is a function of generator feed water chemistry, concentration (depends on blow down rate), and temperature. It is independent of design and make. Because scale inhibitors are not used, and because of the relatively high operating temperatures, the TDS of the feed water should be very low. Silica is of particular concern — most manufacturers stipulate less than 1 ppm; some go as high as 5 ppm. The feed water should have no measurable hardness. It is therefore common to use DI or reverse osmosis as pretreatment.

Using compendial water as feed is wasteful, unless the steam quantity is small and the cost of a dedicated feed water pretreatment system exceeds the cost of using compendial water.

Some manufacturers offer generators to operate on softened water. Usually, the rate of blow down is increased to maintain low concentration.

Note: If the TDS of the soft water is relatively high, soft scale (such as sodium scale and sludge) can form.

7.8.5.2 Corrosion

The most common cause of corrosion is free chlorine, not chlorides. Chlorine and chlorides, at any detectable level, are very detrimental to stainless steel. The higher the temperature and chlorine level, the more severe is the attack. Chlorine is known to migrate and concentrate in localized cells where the level can reach tens or hundreds of ppm.

Chlorine can be removed from the feed water by chemical injection of a reducing agent such as sodium bisulfate, or by passing the chlorinated water through carbon filters.

7.8.5.3 Volatiles

Dissolved gases and substances that are volatile at the operating temperature of the CS generator will carryover with the steam. If such substances are objectionable, they must be removed at the pretreatment stage. Ammonia and CO₂ are examples of volatile gases that will have an effect on the conductivity, such that a condensate sample may not meet USP requirements for Purified or WFI Water.

中文解說與學習重點

前處理三大課題

CS 發生器的進料水前處理必須同時解決三個問題，缺一不可：

  • 水垢 (Scale)：降低 TDS & 矽，零硬度
  • 腐蝕 (Corrosion)：移除游離氯
  • 揮發性物質 (Volatiles)：移除 NH₃、CO₂

水垢問題詳解

淨蒸汽發生器不能使用水垢抑制劑（因為會污染蒸汽），因此進料水本身必須非常乾淨：

  • TDS 要非常低（通常使用 DI 或 RO 水）
  • 矽含量 < 1 ppm（嚴格）至 < 5 ppm（寬鬆）
  • 硬度 = 0（無可測量硬度）

即使用軟化水，高 TDS 仍可能形成鈉垢和污泥。

氯的腐蝕機制 — 深層解說

氯的危害最大難點在於「局部濃縮」：即使進料水中氯含量很低（<1 ppm），氯會遷移到縫隙、死角處濃縮到數十至數百 ppm，造成劇烈局部腐蝕。解決方法：

  • 化學注入還原劑（如亞硫酸氫鈉）
  • 活性碳過濾

比喻：「食物過敏原」

對 CS 發生器而言，氯就像人體的過敏原 — 即使微量也能觸發嚴重反應。而揮發性物質（NH₃、CO₂）就像「食物氣味」，會跟著蒸汽一起走，影響最終產品的「味道」（電導度）。

實務檢查清單

  • 你的工廠 CS 發生器進料水是用什麼？DI？RO？還是軟化水？
  • 是否定期監測進料水的矽含量和游離氯？
  • 發生器是否有定期除垢與目視檢查的 SOP？
  • Blow down rate 是多少？是否足以控制濃縮倍數？

Cost Implications — 成本考量

Economics of pharmaceutical steam production | p. 106

Original Text (English) — p. 106

Determining the economics of pharmaceutical steam production is complex. Costs are quite predictable, but vary greatly depending on scale of operation, system design, actual usage, etc.

The total operating cost to produce pharmaceutical steams is obtained by adding the cost of feed water to the costs of pretreatment and final treatment (primary ion removal and polishing).

The type of pharmaceutical steam system design option selected is typically based on feed water TDS, silica and hardness levels, organic and colloidal content, as well as anticipated steam system utility costs (acid, caustic, salt, power, and source water). Consideration should also be given to maintenance requirements and available resources.

中文解說與學習重點

成本組成

藥用蒸汽的總成本 = 進料水成本 + 前處理成本 + 最終處理成本

  • 進料水：市政水/井水成本
  • 前處理：軟化、RO、DI 等設備與耗材
  • 最終處理：離子交換、拋光等
  • 運行成本：酸、鹼、鹽、電力、源水
  • 維護成本：定期除垢、鈍化、改管等

成本決策的關鍵

進料水水質越差，前處理成本越高。但用藥典級水（WFI）作為進料水通常浪費，除非蒸汽用量很小。最佳實務是：專用 RO/DI 系統供水給 CS 發生器，與 WFI 系統分開。

7.10

Steam “Quality” — 蒸汽「品質」（乾燥度）

Dryness fraction, saturation, and moisture sources | p. 106

Original Text (English) — p. 106

Steam quality is defined as the saturation percentage of steam to water or more explicitly, the ratio of the vapor mass to the mass of the steam mixture.

Dry Saturated Steam with minimum superheat is necessary for efficient steam sterilization.

Water can be generated and carried by steam within distribution systems in two ways:

  1. In suspension as moisture when the steam is not 100% saturated
  2. As condensate separated from the steam

Water vapor carried in suspension may be reduced by: adding more heat or raising the temperature, reducing the pressure, or adding a steam entrainment separator. Water moisture and condensate may be reduced by steam traps.

中文解說與學習重點

Steam Quality 的定義

蒸汽品質（乾燥度）= 蒸汽質量 ÷ （蒸汽質量 + 水質量）

  • 100% = 乾飽和蒸汽：理想狀態，滅菌效率最高
  • <100% = 濕蒸汽：攜帶水分，滅菌效率降低
  • >100% = 過熱蒸汽：熱交換效率差，也不利滅菌

公式：蒸汽乾燥度

Steam Quality (x) = m_steam / (m_steam + m_water)

其中：
  m_steam = 蒸汽質量
  m_water = 攜帶的水分質量

理想值：x ≥ 0.95 (即 ≥ 95% 乾燥度)

比喻：「濕毛巾烘衣服」

想像用毛巾擦乾衣服：如果毛巾本身是濕的（濕蒸汽），擦來擦去衣服也乾不了。只有乾毛巾（乾飽和蒸汽）才能有效地「擦乾」（滅菌）。但如果用烘乾機（過熱蒸汽），溫度太高反而可能傷衣料。

減少蒸汽中水分的方法

  • 提高溫度/熱量：讓攜帶水分蒸發
  • 降壓：蒸汽膨脹，相對飽和度上升
  • 安裝分離器：機械方式移除液滴
  • 安裝蒸汽陀 (Steam Trap)：排出已分離的凝結水
7.11

Distribution — 淨蒸汽配送系統

Materials, connections, traps, and general design practices | pp. 106–107

Original Text (English) — pp. 106–107

Distribution systems for clean steam follow the same good engineering practices commonly used for utility steam, with the exception that contact materials must be inert to the aggressive nature of clean steam.

Corrosion-resistant 304, 316, or 316L grade stainless steel “tubing” or solid-drawn “pipe” are commonly used. Surface finish is not critical due to the self-sanitizing nature of the clean steam. Mill finish or 180-grit mechanically polished pipe or tubing is sufficient. TIG Orbital welding and post-installation passivation is considered appropriate.

Piping must be designed to allow for thermal expansion and to drain condensate.

Note: Drains should have air breaks.

Sanitary clamps or pipe flanges are most commonly used where the piping must be broken, but welded connections are used as much as possible to eliminate maintenance costs and potential for leaks. Threaded connections may be suitable for instrumentation if positioned to drain condensate and remain hot.

Ball valves are commonly used for isolation because elastomeric diaphragms do not hold up well in this service. Where diaphragm valves are used, Teflon-encapsulated EPDM diaphragms give the best long-term performance.

Steam quality sampling may be determined during “commissioning” and consistency ensured based on the proper location and subsequent maintenance of traps, entrainment separators, and vents.

相同點：都遵循良好工程實務 — 排凝結水、排空氣、支撐管線、熱膨脹補償。

不同點：CS 配送必須全程使用耐腐蝕材料（304/316/316L SS），因為淨蒸汽極具腐蝕性。

為什麼用 Ball Valve 而不是 Diaphragm Valve？

想像在炎熱環境中使用橡膠手套與金屬工具：橡膠手套（橡膠隔膜）很快就會老化變脫，而金屬工具（金屬球閥）則能長期耐受高溫。CS 配送溫度高、腐蝕性強，橡膠隔膜壽命明顯縮短。

7.11.1

Line Sizing — 管徑選擇

Maximum velocity and erosion control | p. 107

Original Text (English) — p. 107

The steam distribution header should be sized for a maximum velocity of 7,200 feet per minute (120 ft/sec or 37 m/sec) to limit erosion and extend the life expectancy of the piping. Condensate line sizing should follow good engineering practices for utility condensate.

中文解說與學習重點

關鍵設計數據

蒸汽幹管最大流速:
  7,200 ft/min = 120 ft/sec = 37 m/sec

超過此速度 → 管壁侵蝕加速 → 管線壽命縮短

實務意義

這個速度限制是避免「高速侵蝕」的設計上限。在實際設計中：

  • 根據尖峰流量計算所需管徑
  • 確保在最大負荷時流速 ≤ 37 m/s
  • 凝結水管按一般 utility condensate 標準設計
7.11.2

Water Moisture Removal — 除濕/去水分

Pressure reduction, in-line separators | p. 107

Original Text (English) — p. 107

Water vapor forms in steam systems due to heat loss, causing a change in the liquid/vapor ratio or steam “quality”.

Steam may be dried of moisture by reducing the generated pressure just prior to the point of use to coincide with the steam temperature of saturation at the reduced pressure.

Moisture entrained in the steam can also be removed by installing an In-Line separator at the point of use, just prior to, or just after, the regulator. If the separator is located upstream of the regulator, the regulator should be protected from water damage (wire drawing) and impingement damage on the regulator diaphragms.

In-line separators are available in sizes from 1/2" to 6" (approx. 1 cm to 15 cm) and remove moisture with a series of baffles on which the suspended water droplets impinge and fall out by gravity to the drain, which must be piped to a trap. Separators have a separation efficiency of better than 99% in the removal of all liquid and solid entrainment exceeding 10 microns.

中文解說與學習重點

兩種除濕方法

  • 方法 1：降壓 — 在使用點前降低蒸汽壓力，讓蒸汽在新的壓力下達到飽和狀態，多餘的熱量讓攜帶水分蒸發
  • 方法 2：線內分離器 — 利用擋板 (baffles) 使水滴撞擊並靠重力落下，效率 >99%（>10 μm）

分離器安裝位置的註意事項

如果分離器安裝在調壓閥 (regulator) 上游，必須保護調壓閥免受：

  • Wire drawing：水流沖刷閥座造成的損傷
  • Impingement damage：水滴撞擊隔膜造成的損壞

因此許多設計將分離器放在調壓閥下游。

7.11.3

Condensate Removal — 凝結水排除

Steam traps, water hammer prevention, bacterial contamination | pp. 107–108

Original Text (English) — pp. 107–108

Condensate is the water that has separated from the liquid vapor mixture and forms in steam systems due to heat losses and natural separation effects. Lines should be designed to prevent the buildup of condensate to avoid dangerous water hammer and to eliminate potential cold spots where bacteria can grow.

Any untrapped vertical length of pipe will quickly fill with condensate. If this condensate is allowed to stand for sufficient time, it can cool and become a breeding ground for bacteria. This bacteria could possibly be entrained back into the main distribution header and contaminate use points downstream.

Worst case condensate removal locations should be sampled monthly for presence of bacteria.

The following practices are commonly employed:

  • Each line is adequately supported to avoid sagging and subsequent condensate accumulation.
  • Steam traps are installed at all points where condensate can collect (e.g., at least every 100 feet (30 meters) of line, upstream of control valves, at the bottom of vertical risers, etc.). Steam traps for clean steam service should be sanitary design and self-draining.
  • If the main distribution header is above the use points, the branches should be routed from the top of the header to avoid excessive condensate loads at the branch.
  • An alternative is to run the main distribution header below the use points. Then the branches can drain back to the main header, avoiding the need for additional traps.
  • The requirement to trap each branch can be waived for short drops from main headers to vessels that are in frequent use where sterilization and water hammer are not impacted.
中文解說與學習重點

凝結水的三大風險

  • 水錘 (Water Hammer)：凝結水被蒸汽推動撞擊管壁，可能造成管線破裂
  • 細菌滋生：冷卻的凝結水是微生物溫床，細菌可能被帶回主管
  • 滅菌失敗：冷點無法達到滅菌溫度

凝結水管理實務規則

規則內容
管線支撐避免垮陷，防止凝結水積累
Steam Trap 位置每30m、控制閥前、垂直升管底部
Trap 規格衛生級、自排式設計
支管走向幹管在上方 → 支管從頂部接出
備選方案幹管在下方 → 支管自然回流，減少 trap
豁免條件短落管 + 頻繁使用的設備
取樣最差位置每月採樣檢測細菌

比喻：「屋頂排水溝」

蒸汽管線的凝結水管理就像屋頂排水：如果排水溝垮陷或堵塞，水就會積在屋頂上，最終造成漏水（水錘）或發霉（細菌）。良好的斜度設計和定期清理（steam trap）是關鍵。

實務檢查

你的工廠的 CS 管線是幹管在上還是在下？支管是從幹管頂部還是側面接出？每個 steam trap 是否定期檢查（卡死的 trap 會排不出凝結水，洩漏的 trap 會排掉蒸汽）？

7.11.4

Non-Condensable Gas Removal — 不凝結氣體排除

Air as insulator, thermostatic traps, correct piping | p. 108

Original Text (English) — p. 108

Air and other non-condensable gases should be minimized from pharmaceutical steam systems. Since air acts as an insulator, incomplete sterilization can occur in the process. Air in a system offers a very effective barrier to the heat transfer which will lead to a reduced temperature at the surface of a tube, system component or process equipment.

Air can be discharged using steam traps, however excessive levels may slow down the discharge of condensate. The subcooled condensate can then lead to insufficient sterilization temperatures due to the excess water.

The removal of air can be achieved by placing thermostatic pharmaceutical steam traps with the inlet in the upward position. These should be placed in positions where air is prone to collect such as:

  • The terminal points of the main and large branches of the steam header
  • High points in tanks, reactors and sterilizers

In the case of air and condensate discharge at the bottom of large vessels, the air and condensate should be separated by correct piping practices.

中文解說與學習重點

空氣為什麼是滅菌的敵人？

空氣是優良的「隔熱層」，會在被滅菌表面形成氣袋，阻擋蒸汽接觸該表面。結果：

  • 該區域溫度達不到滅菌要求
  • 造成「冷點」，滅菌不完全
  • 嚴重時導致整個 SIP 失敗

NCG 排除策略

  • 設備：熱靜力式 (thermostatic) 藥用蒸汽陀，進口朝上安裝
  • 位置：幹管與大支管的末端、槽體/反應器/滅菌器的高點
  • 大容器底部：需將空氣與凝結水通過正確配管分開

比喻：「池塘的水面膜」

空氣在蒸汽系統中就像池塘表面的一層膜 — 看不見但會阻止熱量穿透。就像在澡缸裡放了一層保鲜膜，炎水的熱量就無法有效傳遞到被滅菌的物品。

7.11.5

Superheat — 過熱

Impact on sterilization efficiency | p. 108

Original Text (English) — p. 108

While higher-pressure steam can be used to compensate for superheat, the latent heat, or killing power of the potentially superheated steam is reduced at higher pressures; leading to increased sterilization cycles.

中文解說與學習重點

過熱蒸汽的問題

過熱蒸汽溫度高於該壓力下的飽和溫度，看似「更熱」，但實際上：

  • 潛熱 (Latent Heat) 減少：過熱蒸汽的熱交換主要靠顯熱，而非相變潛熱，效率較低
  • 滅菌時間增加：需要更長的滅菌週期
  • 為什麼高壓更糟：壓力越高，飽和蒸汽的潛熱越低，滅菌效率越差

比喻：「燒紅的鐵塊」vs「蒸籠」

過熱蒸汽就像用燒紅的鐵塊去蒸食物 — 雖然溫度很高，但熱量傳遞效率遠不如蒸籠（飽和蒸汽）。蒸籠靠的是蒸汽凝結釋放潛熱，這才是最高效的熱傳遞方式。

7.12

Four Examples of Correct Piping Practice — 四個正確配管實務範例

Practical installation guidance from Figure 7-4 | pp. 109–110

Original Text (English) — pp. 109–110

Figure 7-4 (Vessel Sterilization) and the four piping practice examples illustrate the correct configuration for clean steam distribution. These examples integrate the principles discussed in Sections 7.11.1 through 7.11.5:

Example 1: Branch Take-Off from Header

When the main distribution header is above the use points, branches should be taken from the top of the header. This prevents condensate in the header from flowing directly into the branch line. Each branch must be trapped at its lowest point.

Example 2: Header Below Use Points

An alternative design runs the main distribution header below the use points. Branches rise upward to the equipment, and condensate naturally drains back to the main header by gravity, reducing the need for individual branch traps.

Example 3: Vessel Bottom Drain

For large vessels, air and condensate must be discharged at the bottom. A properly designed piping arrangement separates the air discharge (via thermostatic trap with inlet up) from the condensate discharge (via standard steam trap). This prevents air pockets from impeding condensate drainage.

Example 4: Short Drop to Frequently Used Equipment

For short vertical drops from a main header to equipment that is used daily (e.g., a media storage tank sterilized daily), individual traps on the branch can be waived. The condensate built up in the vertical drop has limited time to cool, and is quickly eliminated by the trap at the bottom of the vessel when the block valve is opened. The vertical drop is sterilized daily with the vessel.

中文解說與學習重點

四個範例總覽

#情境核心原則
1幹管在上、使用點在下支管從頂部接出 + 底部裝 trap
2幹管在下、使用點在上凝結水自然回流，減少 trap 數量
3大容器底部排放分開排空氣與排凝結水的管路
4短落管至頻繁使用的設備可豁免支管 trap，每日滅菌即可

範例 1 詳解：支管從頂部接出

這是最常見的配置。如果支管從幹管側面或底部接出，幹管中的凝結水會直接流入支管，增加水錘與污染風險。從頂部接出，只有乾蒸汽進入支管。

範例 3 詳解：空氣與凝結水分離

大容器底部同時有空氣和凝結水需要排出。如果共用同一管路，空氣會堵塞 trap，導致凝結水排不出去。因此必須：

  • 熱靜力式 trap（進口朝上）負責排空氣
  • 標準 steam trap 負責排凝結水

範例 4 詳解：短落管豁免條件

只有在以下條件都滿足時才能豁免：

  • 落管距離短
  • 設備每天都在使用（如每日滅菌）
  • 凝結水量不足以影響水錘和滅菌
