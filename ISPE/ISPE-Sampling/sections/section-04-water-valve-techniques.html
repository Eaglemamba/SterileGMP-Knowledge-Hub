Sample Valve Design  |  取樣閥設計

2.4 Original Text (ISPE GPG 2016)

General Design Principles

Good sample valve design should not allow contamination of the sample itself and should take into account both the internal function and external operation of the valve. Design considerations should include the method of connection, the length of the flow path, water retention within the valve body upstream of the seat/seal, and the amount of water held within the exit path after the valve has been closed.

This water downstream of the valve is either trapped or held by capillary properties of water. Valve outlet configuration should allow for drainage while not obstructing the sampling process. Good sample valve outlet fitting design should not be so constructed as to facilitate water holdup in the valve resulting in microbial growth.

Any hardware added after the sample valve often poses a significant risk of contaminating samples by introducing extrinsic contaminants that are not representative of the actual water produced and delivered by the system.

Sample valves should also be suitable for use in the selected system such that they should be appropriate for the pressure, temperature, and flow of the system. Sample valves should also be selected based on the location within the system.

"Sample valves designed to sanitary requirements should be employed when feasible and appropriate. Sanitary diaphragm valves are a well-accepted standard within the pharmaceutical industry although other designs have been successfully employed."

Ball valves and other non-sanitary valves should be avoided and only implemented when design constraints (high pressure, the requirement to meter flow rate, or other constraints) necessitate their use. In these cases, additional microbial control consideration may be required.

中文詮釋 & CDMO 應用解析

設計核心原則解析

取樣閥設計的核心邏輯：閥門本身不能成為污染源。ISPE 特別提到三個設計陷阱：

  • 死角體積（Dead Volume）：閥座上游積存的水，是微生物滋生的溫床。每次取樣前必須充分沖洗以驅逐此積水。
  • 出口積水：閥門關閉後，出口路徑中殘留的水（受表面張力維持）會接觸外界空氣，即使微生物污染的風險極高。
  • 附加硬體：閥門後方的任何接頭、彎管、轉接器都是引入外源污染的潛在來源，應盡量精簡。

核心概念：衛生設計等級

製藥用水系統中，衛生設計等級隨系統位置而提高：

  • 進料水 / 前處理 → 可採工業級閥門（化學取樣）
  • 初級純化後段 → 衛生級連接推薦
  • 最終純化後（PW/WFI 分配系統）→ 衛生設計強制要求

CDMO 實務：閥門採購決策

選購取樣閥時，應同時考量：（1）是否有效排水（outlet 朝下）；（2）把手材質（高溫熱水系統避免不鏽鋼導熱把手）；（3）是否支援酒精注入（sanitizable valve）。安裝前應實際測試出水口是否能讓常用取樣容器接觸到。

Valve Outlet & Connection Configurations

The most common connection for sample valves in the sanitary portion of the system is via sanitary clamp, regardless of whether used for chemical or microbial sampling, although some may be welded or occasionally mounted via other specialty fitting.

Valve configurations vary widely and are available with alternate outlet connections ranging from hose barb, to sanitary clamp, to plain end. Common configurations include:

  • 1/2" clamp inlet with 1/2" barb outlet
  • 1/2" clamp inlet with 1/4" barb outlet
  • 3/4" to 2" clamp inlet with 1/2" or 1/4" barb outlet
  • 1/2" clamp inlet with barbed outlet with separate or onboard port for sanitant injection

Valves mounted onto tee fittings should always utilize short outlet fittings or fittings that incorporate a valve mount flush to the interior wall or into the flow mainstream.

Sample valve design should also take into account other issues such as water temperature and sample location within the pipe (near the sidewall or midstream). In heated systems, valve handles made of stainless steel will often conduct heat making the valve handle too hot to operate.

When sampling hot water and clean/pure steam it is often necessary to reduce the temperature for safety reasons. Cooling the water or condensing the steam reduces safety concerns and simplifies sampling but adds an inherent risk of microbial contamination.

出口配置與材質選擇重點

衛生夾頭（Tri-Clamp / Sanitary Clamp）

衛生夾頭是製藥分配系統的標準連接方式，提供：可拆卸清潔、無螺紋死角、快速組裝等優勢。微生物取樣閥應以此為標準連接形式。

比喻：出口朝上如同積水漏斗

想像一個漏斗倒過來放——水不但不會流出，還會積在頸部。取樣閥出口朝上的設計，就是相同邏輯：閥門關閉後，殘留水無法靠重力排出，成為微生物溫床。正確設計應使出口朝下，確保自然排水。

高溫系統特別注意

WFI 系統通常維持 80°C 以上循環。不鏽鋼閥門把手在此溫度下觸摸危險。應改用耐高溫塑膠（如 PVDF、PEEK）把手材質。O-ring 密封也應確認材質相容性（推薦 EPDM 或 PTFE encapsulated）。

CDMO 冷卻閥站設計建議

WFI / Pure Steam 取樣點應配備冷卻裝置（如蛇形冷卻管、板式熱交換器）。設備尺寸需足夠，且冷卻設備本身應定期滅菌，避免成為額外污染源。

Sampling Techniques  |  取樣技術

2.5.1–2.5.2 Representative Samples & Containers

2.5.1 Obtaining Representative Samples

The goal of any sampling program is to obtain samples that accurately reflect the contents of the larger system and/or to ensure water of suitable quality is used for manufacturing or other designated purposes. The vast majority of chemical constituents are uniformly distributed and relatively easy to sample with a minimal amount of rinsing and flushing.

Sampling technique, including flushing, that is representative of use should be performed, as bacterial and endotoxin contaminants will not be uniformly distributed because bacteria primarily adhere to surfaces in a system.

"The 1993 US FDA Guide to Inspection of High Purity Water Systems states that WFI should essentially be sterile and that the allowance of bacteria counts of 10 cfu per 100 ml are to account for sampling error."

2.5.2 Use of Appropriate Sample Containers

When taking samples, efforts should be taken to ensure that sampling containers do not introduce measurable amounts of the contaminant being monitored to the sample. This implies that several different sample containers may be required to sample any given location.

  • TOC: certified low-TOC containers — many containers will introduce extractables that increase TOC values
  • Bacteria: pre-sterilized containers
  • Endotoxin: depyrogenated or pyrogen-free containers (glass preferred, heat-treated ≥250°C for ≥30 min)
  • Conductivity: clean plastic containers (glass leaches sodium and silicate ions)

Sample containers should allow samples to be collected in an aseptic manner and minimize splashing and aeration upon collection. They should provide an adequate sealing mechanism and provide a means to record pertinent sample information.

中文詮釋 & 容器選擇關鍵點

代表性取樣的微生物挑戰

化學屬性（TOC、電導率）在水體中均勻分布，理論上在任何點取樣結果相同。但微生物與內毒素不均勻分布——它們附著在表面，尤其集中在管壁、閥門出口、接頭處形成的生物膜（biofilm）。

這就是為什麼 FDA 要求從實際 POU 取樣：你在分配迴路中間取樣，得到的微生物數據不能代表製程設備實際用到的水質。

容器材質選擇原則（三不一是）

  • 不添加（Non-additive）：不浸出被測物
  • 不反應（Non-reactive）：不與被測物發生化學反應
  • 不吸附（Non-absorptive）：不吸收被測物
  • 是：能提供無菌密封機制

常見錯誤 #1：容器混用

  • 用玻璃瓶做電導率取樣 → 矽酸鹽和鈉離子浸出 → 結果偏高
  • 用可塑膠袋做內毒素取樣 → 疏水表面吸附內毒素 → 結果偏低（假陰性）
  • 用普通塑膠瓶做 TOC → 有機物浸出 → TOC 偏高，OOS 觸發調查
  • 用組織培養瓶做微生物取樣 → 表面促細胞黏附塗層可能促進細菌黏附增殖

CO₂ 污染：電導率取樣的隱形殺手

大氣中的 CO₂ 溶入樣品水中形成碳酸（HCO₃⁻），提高電導率。即使快速裝滿容器也無法完全避免。這是為何離線電導率幾乎永遠高於在線讀數——不是系統變差，而是取樣過程引入的誤差。電導率趨勢管控應優先採用在線儀器。

2.5.3–2.5.9 Hoses, Training & Full Sampling Technique

2.5.3 When Hoses Are Involved

When the sample port is located in a tough to reach spot, hoses may be employed. However, hoses provide an opportunity to introduce contamination. If a hose is not disconnected and allowed to fully drain and dry between uses, bacteria may grow inside the hose that would introduce contamination to the sample. Hoses used for sampling should be installed immediately before samples are taken and then removed immediately afterward or within a qualified period.

2.5.4 Training

Personnel taking samples should be trained on proper sampling technique. The vast majority of variations in results can be traced back to variations in how samples are taken. Ideally, one person would be designated to take all samples and their technique would be regularly reviewed. Without rigorous training and continual review, variations in water quality data may reflect variations in sampling technique rather than actual variations in water quality.

2.5.7 Sample Point Preparation Before Taking Samples

Samplers should understand that preparation of the sample point for one type of analysis may introduce extrinsic contamination for a subsequent sample. Sample point preparation may involve flushing, spraying, disinfecting, or other treatments. Spraying and disinfecting a sample point with isopropyl alcohol (IPA) prior to taking a sample for bacterial testing could increase the value of a sample taken subsequently for TOC measurement.

Sampling order: Samples for bacteria and endotoxins should be taken last in the sequence; samples for chemical constituents, metals, and TOC should be taken first.

2.5.8 Sampling Technique — 10-Step Procedure

When sampling for process control or diagnostic purposes:

  1. Locate a proper size receptacle or drain to receive the water being flushed from the valve. The receptacle must be large enough.
  2. Slowly open the valve until it is fully open (or meets 8 FPS).
  3. Allow water to flow at full force for at least 30 seconds, then slowly close the valve to reduce flow to a comfortable rate.
  4. Collect samples for any required non-microbial constituents (conductivity, TOC) in appropriate containers.
  5. If spraying with IPA for bacteria/endotoxin, slowly close the sample valve.
  6. Spray the outlet of the sample valve with disinfectant (IPA).
  7. Wait 30 seconds.
  8. Repeat steps 2 to 3 and collect bacteria and endotoxin samples in appropriate containers.
  9. Close sample valve and post-treat if appropriate to remove or displace retained water in the outlet flow path.
  10. Ensure samples are labeled and handled/processed properly.

2.5.3 軟管的污染風險管理

常見錯誤 #2：軟管未拆除留置

軟管（hose）安裝後若不立即拆除，其內部殘水在室溫下數小時內即可形成明顯生物膜。下次取樣時，前面的沖洗量可能不足以驅逐軟管內部生長的細菌，導致取樣結果偏高，觸發 OOS 調查。

正確做法：取樣前安裝，取樣後立即拆除，並通過驗證建立最長可留置時間（qualified period）。

2.5.4 人員訓練是數據品質的根本

比喻：取樣員是系統的最後一道分析儀器

再好的 WFI 系統，如果取樣員不按程序執行，數據就是有缺陷的。取樣員的手法差異（沖洗時間、容器選擇、蓋子開啟方式），往往是水質數據變異的最大來源——而這一點很容易被誤判為系統問題。

取樣順序記憶法

正確順序：化學先，微生物後

  1. 電導率（Conductivity）
  2. TOC
  3. 金屬離子 / 其他化學屬性
  4. 微生物（Bioburden）——IPA 消毒後再取
  5. 細菌內毒素（Endotoxin）——最後取

原因：IPA 消毒噴灑後，若在 IPA 前取 TOC，則 IPA 殘留會拉高 TOC 結果。

常見錯誤 #3：QC 取樣複製製程操作

QC 取樣的目的是複製製程的實際用水方式，而非「取到最乾淨的水」。若製程使用 30 秒 8 fps 沖洗，QC 取樣也必須這樣沖洗。若製程不噴 IPA，取樣時也不噴。任何偏差都會讓 QC 數據失去代表性。

取樣後閥門後處理

取樣後出口殘水是微生物生長的溫床（2.5.9）。正確做法：

  • 噴酒精讓其揮發，驅逐殘水
  • 使用含消毒劑注入口的特殊閥門，封存消毒劑
  • 使用無菌壓縮空氣吹乾出口（封閉系統 POU）
  • 禁止蓋上潮濕的閥門蓋：積水 + 密封 = 細菌滋生

2.6 Original Text — Sample Handling

Offline Analysis Contamination Risks

If sample analysis is being performed by an in-line or on-line instrument or analyzer, then the sample fluid never leaves the closed confines of the water system en route to analysis. Where justifiable, having in-line or on-line instrumentation that can measure a homogeneously distributed attribute is generally preferable to collecting a sample and transporting it to an off-line analyzer in a remote laboratory.

Sampling for off-line analysis in the laboratory can affect the measured quality of a given attribute, exposing it to external contamination by any of several routes:

  • Through manual sampling procedures that could be poorly performed due to negligence, lack of training or inadequate procedural detail
  • From transfer between the closed confines of the water system and the sample container
  • Through contact of the inside of the sample container with the outside surface of a sample port
  • During its transport between the sample location and the laboratory
  • During storage prior to analysis
  • Through sample container opening and re-closure in the laboratory
  • During transport and/or restorage for repeat or investigative retest

Transport & Temperature Control

The transport of the samples to the laboratory for testing should be executed so that sampling container contents spillage or coolant seepage into samples does not occur. This may involve protective bagging of sample containers and/or the application of additional barriers (such as protective tape) to protect the sealing surfaces from leakage or seepage during transport.

Depending on the attributes of concern and its intended testing destination, temperature control may also be a consideration. This may involve the use of insulated containers, as well as the use of coolant materials such as waterless cold packs. The use of water and ice should be avoided since sloshing and seepage of this melting coolant into inadequately sealed sampling containers could occur. Similarly, the use of ultra-cold coolants should be avoided since the freezing of the sampled water in the container is usually contraindicated for most types of samples.

The duration and temperature of sample storage is a hotly debated issue, especially for attributes that could quantitatively change during that storage period.

2.6.1 Conductivity

Container cleanliness and atmospheric CO₂ are the two greatest risks. Sample containers should be filled as much as practical to minimize air entrapment in the headspace. As a best practice, samples for conductivity testing should not be refrigerated during storage while waiting testing, because it increases the time required to run the Stage 2 conductivity test. Sample testing should occur as soon as possible.

2.6.2 Total Organic Carbon (TOC)

A significant impact on TOC during sample storage can be from organic residues leaching from the container. For TOC samples only, if certified low TOC containers are used, the samples should be suitable for up to 2 to 3 days at room temperature or when refrigerated without suffering any substantial increase or decrease in TOC level. If reused sample containers are employed, testing within a few hours after collection is advised.

2.6.3 Microbial Count

The concern is that either microbial proliferation or microbial loss due to adsorption or starvation could occur if the elapsed time after sample collection is more than a few hours. Generally, refrigeration is recommended if testing cannot commence within about 2 hours of sample collection. Refer to USP <1231> or other relevant compendia for guidance regarding refrigerated hold times.

2.6.4 Bacterial Endotoxin

Because at least a portion of the endotoxin in a sample could be of a particulate nature, the same guidelines as for microbial testing should be followed for endotoxin test samples.

運輸溫度控制原則

冰水運輸的禁忌

ISPE 明確指出：禁用冰水（ice and water）混合物作為冷卻劑，因融化冰水可能滲入密封不完全的容器。應改用無水冷袋（waterless cold packs）加隔熱箱。同時，避免超低溫冷劑（如乾冰），防止樣品結凍。

CDMO 實驗室排程建議

  • 電導率：盡快檢測（不建議冷藏），當日上午取樣，當日上午分析
  • TOC：使用認證低 TOC 容器，最多 2-3 天緩衝；但務必在期限內安排
  • 微生物：2 小時內開始培養；否則立即冷藏（2~8°C），並記錄冷藏起始時間
  • 內毒素：同微生物保存條件，參考 USP <1231>

實際上，多屬性取樣日需要安排「取樣到實驗室」的時程，使最嚴格的項目（微生物）能在 2 小時內處理。

常見錯誤 #4：超時才送實驗室

最常見的偏差原因之一：取樣後樣品在現場滯留過久（等待其他取樣點、等待運輸人員）。這段時間的溫度、時間未被記錄，樣品鏈（chain of custody）出現空白，後續 OOS 調查時無法重建樣品狀態。

解決方案：在取樣 SOP 中規定最長樣品滯留時間，並在容器上貼取樣時間標籤，實驗室接收時強制核對。

電導率不冷藏的理由

為何電導率樣品「不冷藏」反而較好？

USP <645> Stage 2 電導率測試需要在特定溫度（通常 25°C）進行。若樣品從 2~8°C 冰箱取出，需等待回溫至室溫才能開始測試——增加等待時間，也增加更多 CO₂ 吸收機會。因此，對電導率測試而言，在室溫下盡快測試，反而比冷藏後回溫更合理。

2.6.6 Avoiding Extrinsic Contamination Through Closure Design

When a sample is collected, the ease and cleanliness of opening and closing the sample container should be considered. This activity occurs multiple times between sampling and eventual analysis in the laboratory. The process of opening and closing the container during sampling has specific precautions:

  • Placement of fingers near, or on, the sealing surfaces of the closure should be avoided to prevent a source of extrinsic sample contamination, even when the hands are gloved and alcohol sanitized
  • Fingers should be able to grasp and open the closure at a reasonable distance from any sealing surface
  • Sealing surfaces of the container should not contact the potentially contaminated external surfaces of the container or closure during opening and closing

2.6.7 Chain of Custody

Maintaining a chain of custody provides documented proof of where the samples are and have been and under what conditions, at what times, and handled by whom. This may be a significant consideration when there is an excursion in the value of one or more attributes. The sample's history could have a bearing on how to interpret the test results.

Chain of custody information needs to be proactively collected on every sample in order to have a retrospective interpretation. Chain of custody should also be considered for samples being sent to contract laboratories or sister sites within the same company for testing.

If the test results for samples become included in any legal disputes, chain of custody should be able to verify that the samples are legitimate and have maintained their integrity. The user may not know this in advance.

「不接觸密封面」原則

無論手套是否酒精消毒，手指都不應觸碰容器蓋子的密封面（sealing surface）。微生物取樣時，手套僅是「清潔」而非「無菌」。應選擇設計上可以在不觸碰密封面的情況下開蓋的容器，例如翻蓋式、螺旋蓋側握設計等。

2.7 Original Text — Parametric Release

Concept and Regulatory Basis

Releasing products in real time based on monitoring and control of manufacturing in process parameters critical to finished product quality has been a long-term goal for pharmaceutical water systems. This approach employs Process Analytical Technology (PAT) and Statistical Process Control (SPC) and should be properly validated.

Generally, these parameters should be continuously monitored, so that deviations from acceptable ranges can be detected and process adjustments made to maintain the acceptable finished product quality. Speed and timeliness of these analyses is crucial, which is why real time measurements are needed for real time product release.

The traditional and untimely alternative for release of pharmaceutical waters is to collect water samples and send them to a lab for testing prior to release, all by human mediated procedures.

2.7.1 Chemical Attributes — Real-Time Capability

Since 1996, with the changes made with USP 23rd Edition, 5th Supplement, USP has facilitated the use of:

  • On-line monitoring of TOC (USP <643>)
  • In-line monitoring of Conductivity (USP <645>)

The only provision is that the data from these on-line, in-line, and at-line instruments should be representative of those attributes as measured at the POU. Chemical attributes are homogeneously distributed hence the values measured at remote locations in a simple water distribution system usually are identical to the values measured at the POUs, except for the unavoidable issues associated with sample contamination during collection and off-line testing.

However, if opportunities for non-equivalence exist, due to complex system designs (with sub or parallel loops, heat exchangers, etc.), and potentially TOC contributing hardware, then additional sampling points may be required.

2.7.2 Microbiological Attributes — Limitations

Microbiological attributes of pharmaceutical water are not homogeneously distributed. Although microbiological quality is not a USP monograph requirement, suggested action levels are furnished in USP <1231> which reflect the original guidance established by the FDA for their inspectors in 1993.

Microbial counts in the water being used from POUs could vary significantly in the distribution system, as planktonic or free floating microorganisms originate from biofilms and biofilms are localized in the piping or hardware between the water distribution system and the POU. This localized phenomenon can cause rapid on-line microbial testing to be an inaccurate representation of the microbial quality of the water that is being used for manufacturing.

Similar concerns exist with bacterial endotoxin, as it is produced by the Gram-negative microorganisms that can inhabit the water distribution system, the water purification system, and may be present in the feed water.

中文詮釋 & 即時放行策略分析

參數式放行的核心邏輯

即時放行（RTR）的本質

傳統做法：取樣 → 送實驗室 → 等待結果（數小時到數天）→ 根據結果放行用水。RTR 目標：用連續在線測量取代離線取樣測試，即測即知，即時放行。

關鍵前提：在線測量數據必須與 POU 的離線結果等效，且需要驗證。這個等效性是 USP 允許 RTR 的核心條件。

化學屬性 vs. 微生物屬性的根本差異

比喻：均勻湯 vs. 黏土塊

TOC 和電導率在水中如同均勻攪拌的湯——任何一瓢取出的成分都相同。因此，在迴路任何一點監測，結果都能代表 POU 水質。

微生物和內毒素則如同水中漂浮的黏土塊（biofilm 碎片）——不均勻分布，聚集在局部。在迴路中間點的在線儀器，根本無法代表 POU 出口閥附近的真實微生物狀態。

USP 法規依據整理

  • USP <643>：TOC 測試方法（支援在線監測）
  • USP <645>：電導率測試方法（支援在線監測）
  • USP <1231>：製藥用水指南（微生物 action levels、hold times 等）
  • FDA 1993 Guide：高純度水系統檢查指南（WFI 微生物行動水準）

即時放行不適用微生物：法規風險

若廠商嘗試以迴路中央在線微生物儀替代 POU grab sampling 用於產品放行，此做法違反目前 USP <1231> 和 FDA 立場——因為：

  • 生物膜定位於 POU 附近管路，中央在線儀無法代表
  • POU 測試是法規要求的「release testing」基礎
  • 快速微生物方法（RMM）可替代傳統培養，但仍需從 POU grab sample

2.7.3 Grab Sampling vs. On-Line/At-Line Monitoring

Because of the potential for localized, outlet specific contamination, grab samples for microbial and endotoxin testing have been unequivocally accepted from POUs as reflective of the quality of the water being used at the POU. However, manual sampling and conventional testing approaches can lead to days of delay in data availability and potential for artificially influenced good or bad data.

If rapid on-line or at-line technologies were installed for microbial and endotoxin testing at a single point in the distribution system, the resulting data may be valuable for process control purposes, but could not be used for product release purposes. POU testing is required for release.

2.7.4 Rapid Microbial Detection Techniques

Rapid techniques can be discriminated as being either destructive (microbial cell is killed during analysis — cannot be speciated) or non-destructive (cells not killed — identification possible by culturing).

Rapid microbial detection techniques can also be discriminated by whether or not there is a need to amplify the number of original CFUs. The quickest techniques which can generate data in perhaps 1 to 2 hours usually involve destructive techniques. Many of the slower techniques are non-destructive but take longer (1 to 3 days) for data availability.

2.7.5 Rapid Endotoxin Detection Techniques

The detection of the miniscule levels of endotoxin in WFI can currently only be accomplished by the molecular amplification process involved with the bioassay employing Limulus Amebocyte Lysate (LAL) reagents. Analogs and chemical modifications of these reagents have allowed endotoxin to be quantitated photometrically in as little as 15 minutes. The LAL technology has been commercialized as a rapid portable version that can be easily employed at-line, making it a near real time at-line test, rivaling the test result turnaround time of some on-line TOC instruments.

2.7.6 Future of Real Time Release for Pharmaceutical Water

The localized formation of biofilm between the distribution loop outlets and the POUs has consigned the definitive microbial testing of the water used by manufacturing to be from that POU where the water is delivered to a process or piece of equipment.

From an on-line instrument perspective, and with the rapid advances occurring with instrumentation, real time release of the water for microbial attributes may become a more accepted practice in the relatively near future. For WFI systems where endotoxin levels are also an important attribute, the use of the portable at-line endotoxin test kit essentially adds endotoxin to the list of attributes that can be assured as passing in the finished water with an essentially real time test.

A 1 to 3 day delay in result availability is not suitable for a parametric or real time release approach, but currently emerging technologies may make real time release a reality.

中文詮釋 & 快速方法策略分析

快速微生物方法（RMM）的分類邏輯

破壞性 vs. 非破壞性方法

破壞性（Destructive）：測試過程殺死細菌細胞。優點：速度快（1-2 小時）。缺點：無法做菌種鑑定（speciation）。適用情境：製程管控（知道有沒有，不需要知道是哪種菌）。

非破壞性（Non-destructive）：不殺死細菌。優點：可進行後續培養和鑑定。缺點：仍需部分培養時間（1-3 天）。適用情境：OOS 調查（需要鑑定菌種）。

比喻：LAL 快速試劑如同「快篩試劑盒」

傳統 LAL 需要在實驗室進行，結果要數小時到隔天。可攜式 LAL 試劑盒讓你在現場 15 分鐘得到結果，就像新冠快篩試劑盒——仍需要 grab sample（從 POU 取樣），但結果等待時間大幅縮短，接近「即時放行」。

CDMO WFI 放行策略建議

目前最優化的 WFI 放行組合策略：

  • 化學屬性（TOC + Conductivity）→ 在線儀器持續監測 + 定期 POU 等效驗證 → 即時放行
  • 內毒素 → 可攜式 LAL 從 POU grab sample → 15 分鐘內結果 → 近即時放行
  • 微生物 → 快速 RMM（1-2 hr 破壞性）或傳統培養（2-5 天）→ 仍是最慢的瓶頸

製造排程需要考慮微生物結果最長等待時間，才能決定「先用水後補結果」的風險管理策略。

「在線生物儀器用於製程管控 vs. 產品放行」的重要區別

若在系統中央裝置在線微生物計數儀：

  • 製程管控（Process Control）：可以用，且有價值——監測趨勢、偵測系統異常
  • 產品放行（Product Release）：不能用——因為數據不能代表 POU 水質

這個區別在法規審查中非常關鍵，不可混淆。

Chapter 2.7 Summary — Key Takeaways for Real-Time Release  |  即時放行關鍵重點總結

  • Chemical RTR is established (since 1996): TOC (USP <643>) and Conductivity (USP <645>) support on-line/in-line release provided POU equivalence is validated.
  • Microbiological RTR is not yet achievable for release: Biofilm localization means POU grab sampling remains the regulatory gold standard. On-line microbial data is for process control only.
  • Endotoxin is approaching near-real-time: Portable at-line LAL (~15 min) from POU grab sample is a practical near-RTR solution for WFI systems.
  • Rapid microbial methods (RMM) reduce wait time but don't eliminate POU requirement: 1–2 hr destructive methods improve scheduling; 1–3 day non-destructive methods allow speciation.
  • The future is approaching: Emerging on-line technologies may eventually make microbial RTR from POU a reality, but current pharmacopoeial and regulatory frameworks do not yet support this.
