Foreword

Joint Initiative between IPEC Federation and PDA

In March 2018, the IPEC Federation and PDA announced their first joint initiative. Both associations signed a memorandum of understanding to collaborate on the development of a joint technical report on excipient good manufacturing practices risk assessment in response to input from their respective memberships.

Prior to the collaboration announcement, however, both parties were active on the topic of quality risk management. PDA published the PDA Technical Report No. 54 series. In March 2016, IPEC Europe published the How-To Document – A Guide to Support Manufacturing Authorization Holders (MAH) in their Compliance with the European Commission Guidelines on Risk Assessment for Excipients (2015/C 95/02). Subsequently, in May 2017, IPEC-Americas and IPEC Europe jointly published the Risk Assessment Guide for Excipient Makers, Users, and Distributors.

Both PDA and IPEC Federation believe that presenting a common approach to the legal, regulatory, and related issues concerning excipients is best done as "one voice." Both collaborators see significant potential benefit in leveraging the two organizations' expertise on excipients and drug product manufacture. This technical report will serve as a practical guidance intended for use with existing regulatory and industry standards. The authors expect that the document will enable Manufacturing Authorization Holders of drug product to either set up or benchmark their quality systems, and further establish or continue to collaborate with parties in their excipient supply chain.

This joint PDA-IPEC technical report extends the PDA Technical Report No. 54 series and provides guidance on risk assessments for excipients by presenting a model risk assessment, guidance on key elements, and a collection of actual examples from excipient users in the pharmaceutical industry.

Section 1.0 Introduction — 導論

1.0 Introduction

Excipients are integrated into the drug product formulation for various reasons: to aid the drug product formulation, to serve as functional or nonfunctional ingredients, or to enable the drug to be released from the drug product in a particular, desired manner. The rationale for their use extends beyond the point of medical use to include maintenance of the safety or function of the drug product, even during storage. While good manufacturing practices (GMP) regulations clearly outline what is required for active pharmaceutical ingredients, only a basic framework exists for ascertaining appropriate GMP for excipients through risk assessment.

In recognition of the criticality of excipients in the production of drug products, the European Commission (EC) Falsified Medicines Directive (2011/62/EU) revised Article 46(f) of Directive 2001/83 to require manufacturing authorization holders to verify that any excipients used in a product are made according to appropriate GMP standards. The Falsified Medicines Directive also committed the EC to publishing Guidelines on the formalised risk assessment for ascertaining the appropriate GMP for excipients of medicinal products for human use (EC Guidelines), which were published on March 19, 2015. In 2018, PIC/S incorporated the same provisions for formalized risk assessment into a publication of the same name, extending the provisions to have global applicability. In the U.S., the 2012 Food and Drug Administration Safety and Innovation Act redefined "current good manufacturing practices" to include "the implementation of oversight and controls over the manufacture of drugs to ensure quality, including managing the risk of and establishing the safety of raw materials [and] materials used in the manufacturing of drugs," in addition to the final drug product. This change also emphasizes the criticality of excipients.

Although the accountability for risk assessment rests entirely with the manufacturing authorization holder, all parties in the supply chain are required to support the process by providing relevant information (e.g., Excipient Information Packages) to the manufacturing authorization holder. The risk management process is applicable throughout the excipient's lifecycle, that is, from selection through termination of use, and remains the responsibility of the manufacturing authorization holder at each stage.

Over the course of an excipient's lifecycle, there will be a broad array of drivers for risk assessment and reassessment. Those drivers may include, among others, product deviations, market recalls, changes in excipient use, supply chain amendments, and adverse trends in quality. The manufacturing authorization holder is responsible for providing evidence that the risk management approach adopted is commensurate with the level of risk to ensure the safety, purity and other quality characteristics of the excipient in use.

To comply with the EC Guidelines and PIC/S publication, each excipient used in a drug product must be assessed for the risks that it poses to the product's quality, safety, and purity. From these risks, the manufacturing authorization holder must determine the quality standards required for the manufacture of the excipient. The manufacturing authorization holder should communicate these quality standards to the excipient supplier and seek the supplier's agreement. Any confirmed gaps in quality standards determined by the assessment must be appropriately mitigated, which may require the manufacturing authorization holder to take action on its own or through collaboration with the supplier. To ensure that risk assessments remain effective during the excipient's lifecycle, the manufacturing authorization holder must incorporate a periodic review into the excipient risk assessment process.

中文解說

核心概念：賦形劑 (Excipient)

賦形劑是藥品中除活性藥物成分（API）與包裝材料以外的所有成分。依據不同定義來源：

  • EC 定義：藥品中除活性成分及包裝材料以外的任何成分。
  • IPEC 定義：非 API、已進行適當安全性評估、並有意納入藥物傳遞系統的物質。

賦形劑的功能包含：協助配方成型、充當功能性或非功能性成分、控制藥物釋放，以及在儲存期間維持藥品的安全性與功能性。

核心概念：製造授權持有人 (Manufacturing Authorization Holder, MAH)

MAH 是持有製造授權、負責藥品生產的法人實體。在 TR54-6 的框架下，MAH 承擔賦形劑風險評估的全部責任，包括：

  • 主動評估每種賦形劑對產品品質、安全性與純度的風險。
  • 向賦形劑供應商溝通所需的品質標準。
  • 處理評估發現的任何 GMP 缺口。
  • 定期複審風險評估的有效性。

雖然供應鏈各方需配合提供資訊，但最終責任仍在 MAH 身上，不可轉嫁。

重要提示：TR54-6 不取代法規要求

TR54-6 的目的在於提供實務指引框架，協助 MAH 落實賦形劑風險評估。它：

  • 不取代或修改現有的法定及法規要求。
  • 與 EC Guidelines、PIC/S 出版物及 ICH Q9 QRM 原則相輔相成。
  • 提供模型風險評估與實際案例，作為實務參考。

換句話說，TR54-6 是業界的最佳實務工具書，幫助企業達到法規要求，但法規本身（EC Guidelines 等）才是法律義務的來源。

賦形劑風險評估的驅動因素

以下情況需啟動或重新進行賦形劑風險評估：

  • 產品偏差 (product deviations)
  • 市場召回 (market recalls)
  • 賦形劑用途變更 (changes in excipient use)
  • 供應鏈修改 (supply chain amendments)
  • 品質不良趨勢 (adverse trends in quality)

Section 1.1 Purpose — 目的

1.1 Purpose

This report is based on the general principles of quality risk management (QRM) as outlined in ICH Q9 Quality Risk Management. It has been developed to provide additional guidance on the excipient risk assessment process required by the EC Guidelines and incorporated into the PIC/S publication. In addition, TR 54-6 presents a model risk assessment and real-world examples from the pharmaceutical industry applicable to excipient use for all dosage forms to illustrate risk assessment as an excipient knowledge management solution. In general, the model presented in this report is based on the risk ranking and filtering method as referenced in ICH Q9. It also addresses pertinent factors such as end-to-end supply chain, control strategy, and benefits and challenges, among others. TR 54-6 will also serve as a practical guidance intended for use in conjunction with existing regulatory and industry standards; however, it does not replace or alter existing statutory and regulatory requirements for assessing excipients for use in medicinal drug products.

Despite the clarity and guidance offered by the EC Guidelines, risk assessors for manufacturing authorization holders still face some challenges in implementing and benchmarking the excipient risk assessment process for two main reasons:

  1. Diversity of quality systems: Quality systems applied during the manufacture of materials used as excipients are diverse and generally based on the material's intended use, which may not be primarily as a pharmaceutical excipient. Therefore, adjusting an excipient manufacturer's or supplier's quality systems to meet the pharmaceutical quality system outlined in the guidelines could prove challenging.
  2. Multiple dosage forms: An excipient may be used in different dosage forms by manufacturing authorization holders. One manufacturing authorization holder may use a single excipient in both tablets and injectables, which may result in different quality requirements applying to a single excipient from the same supplier. This also may lead to conflicting requirements for GMP.

TR54-6 的目的（Purpose）

TR54-6 建立於品質風險管理 (QRM) 的一般原則之上，具體依據為 ICH Q9。其核心目的為：

  • 提供 EC Guidelines 要求的賦形劑風險評估流程之額外指引。
  • 呈現一個模型風險評估（model risk assessment），適用於所有劑型。
  • 透過真實案例（Annex I, Sections 10.1–10.6），示範如何將風險評估作為知識管理的工具。
  • 採用 ICH Q9 的風險排序與篩選法（risk ranking and filtering method）作為評估基礎。

兩大實務挑戰

挑戰一：品質系統多元性

許多賦形劑並非專門為藥品用途製造，而是用於食品、化妝品或家用品等行業。這些製造商的品質系統設計並非以藥品 GMP 為首要考量，導致 MAH 要求其升級至藥品品質系統時面臨阻礙。

挑戰二：多劑型導致 GMP 要求衝突

同一種賦形劑可能同時被用於錠劑與注射劑。注射劑（非腸道製劑）的 GMP 要求遠高於口服固體劑型，但兩者可能都採購自同一個供應商，因此 MAH 內部可能對同一賦形劑產生相互矛盾的 GMP 要求，需要謹慎管理。

比喻：食品廠轉型醫材供應商

想像一家工廠主要生產食用澱粉（食品 GMP），但藥廠想要採購其澱粉作為片劑賦形劑。食品 GMP 與藥品 GMP 的要求有顯著差距——這正是 TR54-6 所說的「品質系統多元性挑戰」。MAH 必須透過風險評估，判斷此供應商的現有品質水平是否足夠，或需要要求其改進。

Section 1.2 Scope — 適用範圍

1.2 Scope

The information in TR 54-6 applies to all excipients used in drug products for human use at all stages of the product lifecycle. It assumes the excipient will be used for the drug product in the commercial supply phase; however, the manufacturing authorization holder may choose to apply this practical guidance in the clinical phase as well. That said, this technical report refrains from providing guidance to the manufacturing authorization holder regarding at which clinical phase it should be applied.

中文解說

適用範圍解析

TR54-6 的範圍具有以下特點：

  • 適用對象：所有用於人用藥品的賦形劑（不含獸藥）。
  • 生命週期：涵蓋產品生命週期的所有階段。
  • 主要假設：預設為商業化供應階段（commercial supply phase）使用。
  • 臨床階段：MAH 可選擇性地在臨床試驗階段應用，但 TR54-6 不規定應從哪個臨床試驗階段開始。

範圍的實務意涵

「所有賦形劑」意味著不論賦形劑是否具有複雜的藥理功能，只要被納入藥品配方，MAH 就必須對其進行風險評估。這包括常見的如微晶纖維素（MCC）、乳糖，以及較複雜的如具有緩釋功能的聚合物賦形劑。

TR54-6 不對動物用藥品（獸藥）提供直接指引，但其原則可以類比應用。

中文解說

為何精確定義術語如此重要？

在 GMP 環境中，術語的精確性直接影響責任歸屬與法律義務。以賦形劑供應鏈為例，以下四個角色的定義截然不同，其所承擔的 GMP 責任也相差甚遠：

  • 原始製造商 (Original Manufacturer)：將材料製造至可被指定為賦形劑階段的主體，GMP 責任最高。
  • 經銷商 (Distributor)：取得賦形劑法律所有權，負責分銷，需符合 GDP 要求。
  • 再包裝商 (Repackager)：將賦形劑重新包裝，可能改變標籤，引入額外的品質風險。
  • 仲介商 (Broker)：純粹的中間人，不進行實體處理（無倉儲、運輸、再包裝），僅促成買賣。

正確區分這些角色，才能正確評估供應鏈風險等級。

Section 2.1 的縮寫表雖然簡短，但每個縮寫都指向重要的監管或技術概念：

  • EC（European Commission）：歐盟委員會，發布 EC Guidelines (2015/C 95/02)，是本報告的主要法規依據之一。

  • GMP（Good Manufacturing Practices）：良好製造規範，是整份 TR54-6 的核心議題。
  • QMS（Quality Management System）：品質管理系統，供應商評估時需審查其 QMS 的成熟度。
  • QRM（Quality Risk Management）：品質風險管理，TR54-6 的整體方法論框架，依據 ICH Q9。

EXCiPACT 認證的意義

EXCiPACT 是全球第一個針對藥品賦形劑製造商與經銷商的國際認證計畫。取得 EXCiPACT 認證的供應商，代表其已通過獨立第三方稽核，符合製藥級 GMP/GDP 要求。

在 TR54-6 的風險評估框架中，供應商是否持有 EXCiPACT 認證是評估其品質水平的重要考量指標，通常有助於降低風險評分。

QTPP 與 CQA 的關係

品質目標產品概況 (QTPP) 是對藥品理想品質特性的前瞻性總結，定義了「最終藥品應達到什麼樣的品質標準」。從 QTPP 衍生出各個關鍵品質屬性 (CQA)，這些 CQA 反映了哪些特性對藥品安全性與療效至關重要。

在賦形劑風險評估中，CQA 幫助 MAH 判斷某種賦形劑對藥品 CQA 的影響程度，進而決定其風險等級。例如：用於注射劑的賦形劑若影響產品的滲透壓（CQA），其風險等級將高於僅作為填充劑的口服賦形劑。

實務應用：CDMO 情境分析

Context: CDMO Excipient Sourcing

In modern pharmaceutical manufacturing, Contract Development and Manufacturing Organizations (CDMOs) frequently source excipients from multiple global suppliers simultaneously. For example, a CDMO manufacturing both oral solid dosage forms (tablets) and parenteral (injectable) products for multiple innovator clients may use the same excipient — such as hydroxypropyl methylcellulose (HPMC) — sourced from three different suppliers in Asia, Europe, and the Americas.

Each supplier has a different supply chain configuration:

  • Supplier A: Direct supply from original manufacturer
  • Supplier B: Via a distributor who repackages into smaller quantities
  • Supplier C: Via a broker, with the original manufacturer unknown

TR 54-6 provides the framework for this CDMO to conduct a formalized risk assessment across all three supply chains, tailored to each dosage form's risk profile.

TR54-6 建議採用全球統一的風險評估方法，以「最壞案例」（worst case）評分代表所有劑型，向賦形劑製造商傳遞一致的品質要求，避免企業內部不同部門對同一供應商提出相互矛盾的要求。

3.0 Overall Strategy – A Holistic View

TR 54-6 provides guidance on quality risk management principles that can be used to assess the risks to the quality, safety, and function of an excipient in a drug product. The proposed generic model allows the user to answer the risk question "Is the excipient fit for use in the drug product?" through the use of a formalized risk assessment.

The model, as described in Section 6.0 and illustrated in Figure 6.0-1, describes the steps involved in identifying whether an excipient is fit for use, considering:

  • An assessment of the excipient's use and function for the excipient user
  • An assessment of the entire Supply Chain

In the model, the potential hazards from the excipient itself, as well as the supply chain, are ranked and evaluated using a risk ranking and filtering approach to evaluate the overall risk of harm to the drug product and, through that, to patient safety.

Based on the results of the risk assessment, a pharmaceutical manufacturer can determine what level of GMP will be required. The guides and standards most widely accepted for excipient GMPs include:

  • EXCiPACT: International Pharmaceutical Excipients Certification Standards for Pharmaceutical Excipient Suppliers
  • NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363: Good Manufacturing Practices for Pharmaceutical Excipients
  • IPEC/PQG: The Joint Good Manufacturing Practices Guide for Pharmaceutical Excipients
  • USP: <1078> GMP for Bulk Pharmaceutical Excipients and <1059> Excipient Performance
  • Japan: Self-imposed Standards of GMP for Pharmaceutical Excipients

Should the risk assessment uncover an issue requiring additional action, a control strategy should be put in place and the affected parties should be notified. Any actions recommended as a result of the risk assessment must be discussed and agreed upon by the excipient manufacturer and the drug product manufacturer or Manufacturing Authorization Holder, and the results documented in a quality agreement or other document.

A global risk assessment of all excipients used by the drug product manufacturer will ensure a standardized assessment across all excipients and drug products and provide a holistic view of the supply chain. This enables the excipient user to provide the excipient manufacturer, with one voice, a single (worst-case) risk assessment — preferable to multiple users within the same company communicating different results to the excipient manufacturer.

3.1 Risk Areas Beyond Quality

While the focus of the excipient risk assessment is quality, a company also should consider other aspects that can impact the excipient and, with this, product availability for the patient. These include:

  • Excipient availability
  • Business continuity risk
  • Environmental, socio-economic, health, safety, and geographic risks
  • General business data

While consideration of these risks is useful as part of an overall strategy, it is out of scope of this document and is not discussed further within TR 54-6.

核心概念：賦形劑風險評估的「全面視角」

TR54-6 的整體策略要求 MAH（藥品上市許可持有人）以「全面視角」（holistic view）評估賦形劑風險，而不僅僅是品質問題。這個全面視角涵蓋四個維度：

  • 品質（Quality）：GMP 合規性、生產標準、雜質控制
  • 安全供應連續性（Supply Continuity）：賦形劑可用性、業務連續性風險、替代供應商
  • 法規合規（Regulatory Compliance）：符合 EC 指引（2015）、PIC/S 規範、各國藥典要求
  • ESG 因素：環境（Environmental）、社會經濟（Socio-economic）、地理（Geographic）及職業健康安全（Health & Safety）風險

雖然 TR54-6 的正式範圍僅涵蓋品質風險，但 3.1 節明確提醒企業不應忽視其他風險維度——這些風險同樣會影響病患能否及時獲得治療。

重點提示：全球統一評估 vs. 分散評估

TR54-6 建議採用「全球統一風險評估」，以「一個聲音」向賦形劑製造商溝通要求。這個策略的好處是：

  • 避免同一公司的不同部門或廠區對同一賦形劑給出矛盾的風險評估結論
  • 確保所有藥品（使用同一賦形劑）的評估基準一致
  • 可建立知識庫（knowledge repository），供全球各廠址共享

實際操作上，可由各本地廠址（local owner）分別執行評估，但統一匯整至中央儲存庫，由本地所有者負責資料更新與審核。

GMP 標準選擇的依據

品質風險評估的結論，直接決定賦形劑製造商需符合的 GMP 等級。TR54-6 列出五套業界公認標準，其中最廣泛使用的是 EXCiPACT 認證方案——這是一個由非營利組織管理的全球賦形劑供應商認證計畫，適用於製造商與經銷商。風險越高的賦形劑（如注射劑用賦形劑），通常需要達到更嚴格的 GMP 要求。

4.0 Supply Chain — End to End

Section 4.0 provides examples of supply chains, helps identify the various actors in the supply chain, and describes their typical roles. Supply chains have become more and more complex over the years. In the past, a manufacturer of pharmaceutical excipients often supplied directly to a drug product manufacturer. Today, the Distributor, Broker, and CMO each play roles in the supply chain, each contributing to the risks to quality in one way or another.

Many materials used as excipients are not manufactured specifically for pharmaceutical use but are intended for other applications such as food, cosmetics, or home care. Intermediaries often become involved, procuring larger quantities from the original manufacturer and repackaging into quantities more suitable for pharmaceutical use. The complexity is magnified for excipients that move internationally.

In both international and domestic supply chains, the point at which legal ownership transfers from one organization to another is a critical juncture. In complex supply chains, legal ownership may transfer several times — for example: original manufacturer → distributor → repackager → end user.

4.1 Types of Supply Chains and Risks

4.1.1 Direct Supply from Manufacturer

Even when an excipient is supplied directly by a manufacturer to an end user, intermediaries may be involved. Logistics operations may be subcontracted, and one or more warehousing companies may be involved. Because all intermediaries are subcontracted by either the original manufacturer or the end user, they should have good information about how to safely transport the excipient. The supply chain illustrated in Figure 4.1.1-1 is one of the lowest-risk arrangements due to the small number of intermediaries and the degree of control that can be applied.

Figure 4.1.1-1: Direct supply from manufacturer — fewest intermediaries, lowest risk configuration

4.1.2 Supply via a Distributor

When an excipient moves from manufacturer to Distributor, the distributor becomes the legal owner and then transfers ownership to the end user, possibly after repackaging or relabeling. The distributor may have an agreement with the original manufacturer to market the excipient under the distributor's tradename. Usually in these cases the entire supply chain can be mapped with some certainty. This type of supply chain presents few more risks than direct supply; however, those risks may be magnified if the distributor engages in repacking or relabeling.

If the original manufacturer has not established agreements with distributors, it will be much harder to fully trace all parties in the supply chain, increasing the risks. The original manufacturer may also change its distributor, and a process for notification of such changes should be agreed between the parties.

The end user needs to understand the GMP controls applied by the Repackager in order to minimize the risks to excipient quality, and, if feasible, needs to specify appropriate measures in supply or quality agreements.

4.1.4 Supply Via Broker

A Broker usually connects the manufacturer with the customer without physically handling the excipient. The ability to map the supply chain in this scenario will depend on the degree of transparency provided by the broker. When communications are totally open, the supply chain can be traced and any risks assessed clearly — if the broker is fully transparent and the supplier is the original manufacturer, the risks may be as low as in the direct shipment scenario (Section 4.1.1). On the other hand, if the broker is less transparent, it may be very difficult to assess the risks.

An international supply chain usually involves many intermediaries. Unlike previous examples where the parties are relatively fixed, the parties to an international supply chain — including Hauliers, shipping agents, and ships — may vary from shipment to shipment. In these situations, quantifying the risks may be very difficult. If the intermediaries are fixed, however, suitable inspection controls can be applied at those points, reducing the uncertainty.

4.1.6 Manufacturer and Contract Manufacturing Organizations (CMO)

When one or more CMOs are involved in the supply chain, it is critically important to clearly identify and understand which party gathers information and who owns the risk assessment.

The Manufacturing Authorization Holder is ultimately responsible for the risk assessment. Depending on the quality agreement and relationship between the CMO and manufacturing authorization holder, there are generally three options:

  • The CMO carries out the entire risk assessment, collecting information about the use and function of the excipient in the final drug product — which is then reviewed and approved by the manufacturing authorization holder.
  • The risk assessment is carried out by the manufacturing authorization holder, incorporating information from the CMO.
  • The risk assessment is carried out by the manufacturing authorization holder based on the risk assessment delivered by the CMO.

In any case, the roles and responsibilities for the CMO and manufacturing authorization holder must be clearly defined, including what actions will be taken if the risk assessments completed by the CMO and manufacturing authorization holder result in different outcomes.

比喻：食品外送供應鏈類比

賦形劑供應鏈的六種類型，可類比為食物從農場到餐桌的不同路徑：

  • 4.1.1 直接供應：如農場直送到你家（農場→物流→消費者）——中間環節最少，品質最可追溯
  • 4.1.2 經由經銷商：農場→大型量販超市→消費者——超市取得所有權後再轉售，有一定的品質管控，但需確認超市（Distributor）有與農場簽訂品質協議
  • 4.1.3 經由重新包裝商：農場→超市→分裝公司（小包裝）→消費者——每次分裝都是汙染或混摻的風險點，需嚴格驗證分裝 GMP
  • 4.1.4 經由中介仲介（Broker）：仲介居中媒合農場與超市，本身不碰貨——若仲介不透明，無法確認貨物實際來源，風險難以量化
  • 4.1.5 國際出口：來自海外農場，途經多個港口、倉庫、船運公司——每個環節的人員都可能換，每批次路線都可能不同，風險最難管控
  • 4.1.6 CMO 介入：農場（賦形劑製造商）→委外加工廠（CMO）→藥廠——需明確誰負責賦形劑風險評估，誰對最終藥品品質負最終責任（始終是 MAH）

重點警示：供應鏈愈長，風險愈高

TR54-6 的核心訊息是：每增加一個供應鏈中間環節，就增加了以下風險：

  • 摻假（adulteration）：中間商可能混入劣質物料
  • 汙染（contamination）：每次分裝、重新包裝都是潛在汙染點
  • 文件斷鏈（documentation gaps）：原始製造商的 CoA、EIP 可能無法完整傳遞至終端用戶
  • 所有權轉移不明確：法律責任歸屬不清，發生問題時難以追究

因此，供應鏈愈複雜，MAH 就需要投入更多資源進行稽核、簽訂品質協議，並建立更嚴格的進料檢驗控制。

Broker 與 Distributor 的關鍵區別

Distributor（經銷商）：取得賦形劑的「法律所有權」，負責實體儲存、運輸，有時也進行分裝或重新標示。

Broker（仲介）：「不碰貨」——僅媒合買賣雙方，不實際持有或運輸賦形劑。風險主要來自於資訊透明度：若 Broker 不願透露原始製造商身分，MAH 幾乎無法評估供應鏈風險。

TR54-6 指出：若 Broker 完全透明且供貨來自原始製造商，其風險可與直接供應（4.1.1）相當；若不透明，風險評估極為困難。

CMO 場景下的風險評估所有權

當 CMO 介入時，TR54-6 強調：無論評估由誰執行，MAH 始終是最終責任人。三種模式的差異在於工作分工，而非責任分工——責任永遠在 MAH。

實際上，若 CMO 進行評估，MAH 仍需審查並核准，不能以「已交由 CMO 評估」為由推卸責任。品質協議（Quality Agreement）必須清楚規範：若 CMO 評估結果與 MAH 結論不一致時，如何處理。

  • 品質標準落差（Scenario 3）：製造商現行品質標準低於 MAH 要求，需決定是否加強監管、修改規格或更換供應商
  • 協議不完備（Scenario 4）：缺乏品質協議或保密協議，需立即建立法律框架以支持資訊交換
  • 商業關係薄弱（Scenario 5）：因採購量小，製造商配合度低，需善用 Distributor 關係或透過協議強制資訊提供義務
  • 稽核受限（Scenario 6）：無法執行現場稽核，可使用 EXCiPACT 等第三方認證作為替代方案

  1. 更新供應鏈地圖（Supply Chain Mapping）：識別新供應鏈中的所有中間環節（Broker 是否透明揭露原始製造商？）

  3. 資訊蒐集評估（Table 5.0-2🔍 Scenario 1）：確認 Broker 是否願意揭露供應鏈全貌，或是否存在不透明風險
  4. 品質協議修訂：在 CDMO 與 MAH 的品質協議中更新供應商批准清單（AVL）及相應風險評估結論
  5. 觸發重新評估（Section 7.1 Triggers）：供應鏈變更是風險評估重新審查的觸發事件，需正式記錄並獲 MAH 批准
  6. 考慮稽核：若 Broker 不允許稽核，評估是否使用 EXCiPACT 認證作為替代

A Model for Quality Risk Management for Excipients

The Complete Generic Risk Model — Figure 6.0-1 Overview

6.0 A Model for Quality Risk Management for Excipients

The TR 54-6 Task Force developed this QRM model by surveying industry practices, analyzing real-world company models shared by manufacturing authorization holders, and combining best practices. The model applies a risk ranking and filtering approach to answer the central risk question:

"Is the excipient fit for use in the drug product?"

Risk ranking and filtering involves breaking this question into as many components as needed to capture all relevant risk factors. The two main factors in the model are:

  • Intrinsic excipient risks — factors inherent to the excipient itself and its use in the drug product
  • Supply chain risks — factors related to the manufacturer, distributor, and transport of the excipient

These two factors are combined into a single relative risk score. Filters in the form of weighting factors or cut-offs support risk acceptance or mitigation decisions.

ICH Q9 QRM 框架如何應用於賦形劑

ICH Q9 定義了品質風險管理的通用原則。TR54-6 將此框架具體應用於賦形劑評估，形成以下七個循環階段：

► 啟動（Initiate） — 確認需要評估的賦形劑，組成評估小組
↓
► 風險識別（Risk ID） — 識別固有風險因素與供應鏈風險因素
↓
► 風險分析（Risk Analysis） — 計算賦形劑風險評分（ERPN）與供應鏈評分（SCRPN）

↓

此模型適用於賦形劑整個生命週期——從選用到停止使用，最終責任由藥品製造授權持有人（MAH）承擔。

重點提示：為何需要兩條風險路徑？

TR54-6 模型的獨特之處在於同時考慮賦形劑本身（固有因素）與供應鏈（外部因素）。一個固有風險高的賦形劑（例如：用於注射劑的多山梨酸酯 80），若供應鏈管控嚴密，整體風險可能可接受；反之，即使固有風險低，若供應鏈不透明，整體風險仍可能偏高。

兩條路徑的評分最終在風險矩陣中交叉，以決定是否需要採取額外行動。

6.1

Initiate Risk Management Activities and Identify Assessment Team

Who, when, and what triggers the excipient risk assessment process

6.1 Initiate Risk Management Activities

The risk assessment process begins with the identification of a need to qualify an excipient or to review an existing excipient risk assessment. Triggers include:

  • New product introduction
  • Obtaining a second source for an existing excipient
  • Notification from supplier of major changes
  • Other drivers discussed in Section 7.1 (ongoing review triggers)

The Assessment Team

A successful excipient risk assessment requires a holistic approach encompassing knowledge of the excipient's excipient function in the pharmaceutical dosage form, as well as knowledge of the manufacturer and supply chain. The team should include individuals from various functional areas. The risk assessment process may include participants with defined roles such as:

  • QRM facilitator — knowledgeable about methodology and tools
  • QRM experts — area or function leads for QRM or decision-makers
  • Quality Assurance — focused on excipient use and function
  • Quality — focused on supply chain oversight (excipient manufacturer, supplier, transport)
  • Qualified/responsible person
  • Material sciences, Quality control
  • Regulatory affairs, Technical/operations
  • Sterility assurance (critical for injectables and sterile products)
  • Manufacturer or supplier auditing
  • Procurement
  • R&D, Engineering
  • Nonclinical statistics, Legal, Manufacturing
  • Medical/clinical, Safety and pharmacology/toxicology

Having a multidisciplinary team working collaboratively to gather and review data is critical to ensuring that all risks are considered holistically. The excipient risk management process should be built into the company's existing processes and QTPP-based quality management system (QMS).

Documentation Required

The output of the initiation phase includes:

  • Identification and documentation of the assessment team with defined roles
  • A clear statement of the risk question to be answered
  • Documentation of the trigger that initiated the assessment
  • Scope of the assessment (which drug products or drug product families)
  • Timeline and resource plan for completing the assessment

6.2

Identify Intrinsic Risk Factors

Factors inherent to the excipient — chemical nature, origin, contamination potential, prior knowledge

6.2 Identify Intrinsic Risk Factors

In identifying the excipient's Intrinsic Risk Factors, numerous factors should be considered. The primary source is input from product and process development.

The QTPP (Quality Target Product Profile) of the drug product(s) in which the excipient is to be used provides requirements for the drug product and forms the basis of information about the excipient's use and function. During drug product design and development, scientific studies are conducted and information is collected about how the excipient may interact with other components of the product.

Excipient Attributes to Consider

Examples of parameters that can be considered as excipient attributes include:

  • Characteristics of excipient needed for use in the drug product
  • Manufacturer's excipient specifications
  • Factors learned from the manufacture of the excipient
  • Existence of multiple and/or global manufacturers
  • Lot-to-lot variability

EC Guidelines Risk Factors (March 19, 2015)

The EC Guidelines specify the following as key intrinsic risk factors that must be assessed:

  • TSE/BSE — Transmissible spongiform encephalopathy risk (especially animal-origin excipients)
  • Viral contamination — Potential for viral contamination from biological sources
  • Microbiological / endotoxin / pyrogen contamination — Risk of microbial growth or endotoxin carry-over
  • Process impurities — Residual solvents, catalysts, and other process-generated impurities carried over
  • Raw material impurities — Aflatoxins, pesticides from plant-origin excipients
  • Sterility assurance — For excipients claimed to be sterile
  • Cross-contamination — Impurities from other processes in shared facilities (absence of dedicated equipment)
  • Environmental control and storage/transport conditions — Including cold chain management if appropriate
  • Excipient stability — Degradation risk over time or under storage conditions
  • Packaging integrity — Evidence supporting container closure integrity

比喻：食品過敏原的固有風險邏輯

賦形劑的「固有風險」類似食品中的過敏原——其風險源於物質本身的特性，而非外在條件：

賦形劑的來源（動物、植物、礦物、合成）決定了其天然的固有風險等級。動物來源的賦形劑（明膠、乳糖）固有攜帶 TSE/BSE 風險；植物來源可能有農藥/黃麴毒素殘留；合成來源的主要風險是製程殘留物。

固有風險 vs. 供應鏈風險 — 核心區別

固有風險（Intrinsic Risk）是賦形劑本身帶來的風險，無論由誰生產、從哪裡購買，這些風險都存在。例如：

  • 注射用水（WFI）固有地需要嚴格無菌控制
  • 豬明膠固有地存在豬源 TSE 風險（對某些宗教或飲食限制的患者群體也有影響）

供應鏈風險（Supply Chain Risk）則是由供應商的管理能力、誠信度和供應複雜度所帶來的風險——這是可以透過選擇更好的供應商來降低的。

固有風險無法消除，只能透過CQA 管控和規格設定加以管理。

QTPP 與 CQA 在固有風險識別中的作用

QTPP（品質目標產品概況）定義了藥品「應達到什麼樣的品質」，而 CQA（關鍵品質屬性）則描述「哪些屬性對品質最重要」。

識別固有風險的起點正是這兩個文件：QTPP 告訴評估小組賦形劑需要做什麼（功能），CQA 告訴他們賦形劑的哪些屬性若失控將影響患者安全。

6.3.1 Impact of Excipient on Each Type of Drug Product

Correlating excipient attributes to CQAs by route of administration and dosage form

Excipient Impact Assessment — Overview

After identification of intrinsic risk factors (Section 6.2), excipient attributes are assessed for their correlation to the CQAs (Critical Quality Attributes) of the drug product in which the excipient is to be used. The rationale of any impact must be explained. This step is taken for each drug product or drug product family, and results can then be consolidated across products.

Initially, all potential risk factors (hazards) are identified and documented as excipient attributes. Then, CQAs are correlated to the pharmaceutical dosage form using a three-symbol notation:

  • +   Known Impact (documented effect on CQA)
  • –   No Impact (no indication of impact on CQA)
  • ?   Impact cannot be ruled out; more information may be needed

A "Compliance" column is also included near the CQAs so that compliance with pharmacopeial specifications can be ensured. This column allows assessors to force excipient attributes marked for compliance (e.g., a compendial specification the company will claim adherence to) further into the assessment and specification, regardless of actual CQA impact.

Handling of "?" Attributes

Only excipient attributes with a correlation marked "+" are brought forward as needed into the specification for further analysis. Attributes marked "?" should be further investigated to obtain data necessary to determine whether there is potential impact. If further data cannot be gathered, "?" will be treated as "+", taking a worst-case approach.

From Impact Assessment to Specification

Excipient attributes and impact assessments are used to carry out a qualitative failure analysis describing failure options and the effect of failure (consequences). This analysis is based on knowledge from development or literature. From the failure analysis, the excipient user gains understanding of allowed variation in attributes, and specification limits for each material attribute are proposed to mitigate risk. In this way, the excipient impact assessment is used to justify the excipient specification.

  • Prospective: The assessment provides input to one or more new specifications consistent with drug product needs

關鍵重點：為什麼注射劑和吸入製品風險最高？

人體有三道主要的防禦屏障：

  • 皮膚 — 外用製品接觸此屏障，大多數物質難以穿透
  • 胃腸道黏膜 + 肝臟首過代謝 — 口服製品中的有害物質在到達全身循環之前，經由肝臟代謝降解
  • 血腦屏障 — 保護中樞神經系統

Parenteral（注射）製品完全繞過了皮膚和胃腸道這兩道屏障，雜質或內毒素直接進入血流或組織。這就是為什麼注射劑的賦形劑風險被評定為最高等級（RFE3 = 5分）。

吸入製品繞過胃腸道，但肺部巨大的表面積（約 70 平方公尺）提供了高效吸收通道，因此評為中等風險。

影響評估的三符號邏輯

TR54-6 使用「+」、「–」、「?」三個符號來表示賦形劑屬性與 CQA 之間的關聯：

  • +（已知有影響）：此屬性必須納入賦形劑規格管控
  • –（無影響）：此屬性不需要納入規格，可從評估中移除
  • ?（無法排除影響）：必須進一步調查；若無法取得資料，採用最壞情況處理（視同「+」）

此方法確保規格制定是基於科學依據，而非僅靠慣例——這正是 ICH Q8 品質源於設計（QbD）的精神。

比喻：機場安檢的風險等級邏輯

影響評估的邏輯類似機場安檢依旅客分類調整檢查力度：

  • 注射劑 = VIP 外交包裹 → 最嚴格審查，任何疑點都不放過
  • 吸入製品 = 商務艙行李 → 詳細審查，重點關注微粒

Excipient Risk Profile — Overview

The second part of the excipient risk analysis is creating a Risk Profile (or ranking) using a question-based risk ranking and filtering system. At a minimum, the following factors related to the excipient and its use must be considered in developing the risk score:

  • Excipient attributes and relevant specification

  • Excipient complexity (composite)
  • Prior knowledge of and experience with excipient (known quality defects)
  • Standard packaging size of the excipient

Table 6.3.2-1🔍: Excipient Risk Calculation Tool

Each risk factor (RFE) is assigned a score of 1 (Low), 3 (Medium), or 5 (High).

ERPN Formula

The Risk Priority Number for the excipient (ERPN) is calculated as the sum of the risk scores for each of the seven identified risk factors (RFE), divided by the number of risk factors. The resulting ERPN will be in the range of 1 (Low) to 5 (High):

ERPN = (RFE1 + RFE2 + RFE3 + RFE4 + RFE5 + RFE6 + RFE7) ÷ 7

Note: Users would adjust the calculation as necessary to reflect additional risk factors identified, or to weight individual risk factors based on the company's analysis and risk tolerance. The resulting ERPN will then be used together with the supply chain risk score (SCRPN) in the risk evaluation matrix.

6.4 Supply Chain Risk Analysis

A Supply Chain Risk Score (SCRPN) is created using a risk ranking system similar to the Excipient Risk Profile (ERPN) described in Section 6.3.2. The ranking should draw on input from the supplier, including knowledge from questionnaires, previous experience, Audits, site visits, and media coverage.

Minimum Risk Factors for Supply Chain (RFS)

At a minimum, the following six risk factors must be considered when building the SCRPN:

  • RFS1 — Supply Chain Complexity: Supplier–broker–manufacturer relationships; opportunity for fraud; presence of repackaging or manipulation; regional security context.
  • RFS2 — Prior Knowledge of the Manufacturer: Track record, prior experience, delivery reliability, evidence of excipient variability, history of complaints.
  • RFS3 — Organizational Volatility: Stability of management; turnover in key quality and technical positions.
  • RFS4 — Excipient Manufacturer QMS Performance History: Regulatory observations, Audit responses, CAPA timeliness, consent decrees, warning letters.
  • RFS5 — Packaging Suitability: Scientific justification; tamper-evident seals; ability to protect against moisture, heat, fraud.
  • RFS6 — QMS Standards and Certification: Whether the supplier holds relevant certifications (e.g., ISO 9001, EXCiPACT), documentation systems, materials management, supplier qualification programs.
  • RFSx — Additional factors as needed for the specific excipient and its use in the final drug product.

Each RFS selection yields a value of 1 (Low), 3 (Medium), or 5 (High). The SCRPN is calculated as the arithmetic mean across all identified risk factors:

SCRPN Formula

SCRPN = (RF_S1 + RF_S2 + RF_S3 + RF_S4 + RF_S5 + RF_S6) / 6

Result: a number between 1 (Low) and 5 (High)
Note: scale is not linear — do not treat as such.

Users may adjust the calculation to reflect additional risk factors or to weight individual risk factors based on the company's analysis and risk tolerance (footnote 3 in TR54-6).

供應鏈風險分析 — 中文解說

核心概念：什麼因素讓供應商「高風險」？

SCRPN的設計邏輯是：當供應鏈的透明度越低、過去表現越差、QMS越薄弱，被假冒或品質失控的機率就越高。具體而言，以下特徵代表高風險：

  • 多層中間商（Broker）： 每增加一個中間環節，就多一次再包裝或摻偽的機會。直接向製造商採購是最安全的選項（RFS1 = 1）。
  • 無稽核記錄： 對製造商毫無了解，或曾有重大投訴而未解決（RFS2 = 5）。
  • 組織人員不穩定： 關鍵職位高流動率意味著SOP知識流失和品質文化薄弱（RFS3 = 5）。
  • 主動調查或警告信： 正式監管行動（consent decree, warning letter）直接代表QMS失效（RFS4 = 5）。
  • 無防竄改包裝： 藥用輔料在運輸過程中被替換或污染，無從察覺（RFS5 = 5）。
  • 無認證： 未持有任何ISO、EXCiPACT或同等認證（RFS6 = 3~5）。

反之，直接向歐美製造商採購、具EXCiPACT認證、CAPA及時完成的供應商，其SCRPN會接近1（低風險）。

GDP合規與供應鏈風險的關係

GDP（Good Distribution Practices，良好配送規範）要求藥品及輔料在運輸過程中維持品質。若供應商符合GDP，RFS5（包裝適合性）和RFS1（供應鏈複雜度）的風險分數均可降低。

TR54-6明確將GDP合規納入供應鏈風險評估的考量範疇，尤其對於需要溫控或防潮的輔料更為重要。

比喻：供應鏈風險就像食材的「產地履歷」

想像你在餐廳訂餐，廚師直接從認證農場進貨（RFS1 = 低）、認識農場主人20年沒有問題（RFS2 = 低）——這就像從原廠直採附EXCiPACT認證的輔料。

相反，若食材輾轉經過五個中間商、農場老闆每年換、包裝沒有防拆標籤——你會不會擔心食安？這正是高SCRPN的直觀含義。

藥用輔料的後果更嚴重：一旦用在靜脈注射液（IV）中，任何污染都可能直接危及生命。

6.5 Risk Evaluation

The excipient risk score (ERPN) and the supply chain risk score (SCRPN) are evaluated together using a Risk Matrix approach. The excipient risk score appears on one axis; the supply chain risk score on the other. The combination creates an overall risk score that answers the central QRM question: "Is the excipient fit for use?"

Both ERPN and SCRPN range between 1 (Low) and 5 (High). The scale is not linear and should not be treated as such. Because risk acceptability varies significantly from company to company, the matrix and its cut-offs should be tailored to the individual company's risk tolerance.

6.5.1 Risk Matrix — Option 1: Calculating the Combined Risk Score (OverallRPN)

In Option 1, the combined risk score is calculated by multiplying ERPN by SCRPN:

OverallRPN Formula (Option 1)

OverallRPN = ERPN × SCRPN

ERPN range:  1 (Low) to 5 (High)
SCRPN range: 1 (Low) to 5 (High)
OverallRPN range: 1 (minimum) to 25 (maximum)

6.5.2 Risk Matrix — Option 2: Calculating the Final Risk Score

Option 2 allows additional customization. The MAH may weight the ERPN and SCRPN differently based on its internal evaluation. For instance, if excipient-related risk deserves greater emphasis, it can be given double the weight of the supply chain risk score.

The formula uses user-assigned weights (wt) for each score; n is the sum of all weights in the numerator:

Final Risk Score Formula (Option 2)

Final Risk Score = [wt(ERPN) + wt(SCRPN)] / n

Where n = sum of all weights applied.

Example: wt(ERPN) = 2, wt(SCRPN) = 1 → n = 3
Final Risk Score = [(2 × ERPN) + (1 × SCRPN)] / 3

The MAH may also calculate individual scores for each role in the supply chain — for example, a separate risk score for the original manufacturer and another for each broker — and use these individual mean risk scores instead of a single SCRPN.

風險評估方法 — 中文解說

兩種風險計算方法並列比較

Option 1 (OverallRPN)：
  OverallRPN = ERPN × SCRPN
  結果範圍：1–25
  判斷門檻：
    > 10  → 不可接受（必須緩解）
    4–10  → 邊際可接受（應考慮緩解）
    < 4   → 可接受（無需額外行動）

Option 2 (Final Risk Score)：
  Final Risk Score =
    [wt(ERPN) + wt(SCRPN)] / n
  結果範圍：1–5（加權平均）
  判斷門檻：由公司依風險容忍度自訂

何時使用 Option 1？何時使用 Option 2？

Option 1（乘法/OverallRPN） 是較簡單且保守的方法。乘法的特性是「放大效應」：當ERPN和SCRPN都偏高時，OverallRPN會急遽上升（例如：ERPN=4，SCRPN=4 → OverallRPN=16，遠超過10的門檻）。這使得Option 1在同時存在高輔料風險和高供應鏈風險時，能夠有效「抓出」真正危險的組合。

Option 2（加權平均/Final Risk Score） 則提供更高的客製化彈性。若公司認為輔料本身的固有風險遠比供應鏈複雜度更重要（例如：用於注射劑的高毒性輔料），可以給ERPN更高的權重，讓評分更能反映實際判斷。

關鍵差異： Option 1結果在1–25之間，Option 2結果在1–5之間（加權後仍是平均值）。兩者的門檻值不同，不可混用。

乘法為何會「放大」風險？

以數學角度理解：若ERPN=3（中等），SCRPN=3（中等），OverallRPN=9——落在「邊際可接受」區。但若兩者都提高為4，OverallRPN=16——立刻跳到「不可接受」區。

這種非線性放大效果正是乘法公式的設計意圖：當兩個獨立的風險來源（輔料本身＋供應鏈）同時偏高時，整體風險遠不是簡單加總，而是倍增的。

比喻：OverallRPN 就像交通燈系統

可以把OverallRPN想像成供應商資格審查的「紅綠燈」：

  • 綠燈（Low，<4）： 正常監控即可。定期審閱CoA、年度回顧。
  • 黃燈（Medium，4–10）： 加強監控 + 考慮現場稽核。需書面說明為何接受。
  • 紅燈（High，>10）： 強制緩解措施。必須執行完整GMP稽核 + 額外測試，直到OverallRPN降低至可接受水準為止。

就像交通燈，紅燈並不代表「永遠不能用」，而是代表「在採取緩解行動之前，不得放行」。

6.6 Implementation of Controls — Risk Mitigation

When the risk is found to be at an acceptable level, the risk assessment process is concluded. The identified controls should be documented, and the agreement with the manufacturer or supplier should ensure effective maintenance of those controls.

If additional actions are needed, Table 6.6-1🔍 describes factors that should be considered. Specific actions that might lower the residual risk can be found by deconstructing the OverallRPN and investigating the risk scores for both the supply chain and the excipient that are driving the overall risk score.

The level of existing controls may exceed actual requirements. In such cases, a company may consider reducing efforts in lower-risk areas to focus on higher-risk ones — for example, increasing Audit frequency for high-OverallRPN excipients while decreasing it for low-OverallRPN excipients.

Confirmation that additional controls have been agreed upon and implemented should be obtained from both the excipient manufacturer/supplier and the drug product manufacturer, and documented in supply and quality agreements. After controls are implemented, the risk assessment must be reviewed and re-scored to determine the residual risk acceptability. If the OverallRPN remains unacceptable, further mitigation is required. This cycle continues until an acceptable residual risk level is reached.

風險緩解控制措施 — 中文解說

風險緩解的四個層面

TR54-6將緩解措施分為四個層面，每層面的責任主體不同：

  • 藥品製造商自身控制（Drug Product Manufacturer）： 例如進廠加測、特殊儲存、輔料再加工（過濾/輻照）。這是MAH可以直接決策的部分，不依賴供應商配合。
  • 輔料品質控制（Excipient Controls）： 要求供應商提升GMP標準、修改規格或增加測試項目（如每批次身份確認），直接影響CoA的可信度。
  • 供應鏈控制（Supply Chain Controls）： 增加第三方稽核頻率、強化變更通知機制、運輸監控。這是針對RFS1–RFS4的直接對策。
  • 製造過程控制（Manufacturing Controls）： 由輔料供應商引入，例如強化清潔驗證、使用專用設備、禁止重工（no rework）。這是針對輔料本身ERPN的對策。

「解構OverallRPN」的邏輯是：找出ERPN高還是SCRPN高，再針對較高的那端優先施加控制措施，而不是浪費資源在已足夠的低風險面向。

重點：緩解是一個迭代迴圈，不是一次性決策

TR54-6明確規定：實施緩解措施後，必須重新評分（re-score）。若OverallRPN仍超標，繼續緩解，直到達到可接受水準。這個「執行→重評→再執行」的迴圈體現了ICH Q9 QRM的核心精神：風險控制是動態管理過程。

所有已商定的控制措施都必須記錄於供貨協議（Supply Agreement）和品質協議（Quality Agreement）中，確保法律約束力。

應用情境：高OverallRPN時如何「解構」找對策

情境： 某輔料 OverallRPN = 18（>10，不可接受）

解構： ERPN = 3.6（輔料中等風險），SCRPN = 5（供應鏈高風險）

結論： 驅動因子是供應鏈，不是輔料本身。優先行動應針對SCRPN：

  • 進行第三方稽核（降低RFS4）
  • 要求供應商取得EXCiPACT認證（降低RFS6）
  • 新增防竄改包裝要求（降低RFS5）

實施後假設SCRPN降至3.0，新OverallRPN = 3.6 × 3.0 = 10.8（仍略超標）→ 繼續緩解，例如加入每批次身份測試，直到OverallRPN < 10。

6.6.1 General Considerations on Appropriate GMP Standards

Figure 6.6.1-1 in TR54-6 illustrates the extent and degree of risk mitigation required based on the type of drug product and its use. The degree of mitigation — the reduction in overall risk necessary to reach a low residual risk level — increases as progressively more stringent GMP Standards are applied.

The mitigation is defined by:

  • The excipient itself — what it is and how it is made
  • How the excipient is supplied
  • Why it is used and how it functions in the drug product
  • The drug product type (e.g., oral, topical, parenteral, sterile)

GMP標準梯度與決策邏輯 — 中文解說

EXCiPACT認證：製藥輔料專用GMP標準

EXCiPACT™是製藥業輔料專用的GMP認證計畫，可類比於ISO 9001——但ISO 9001是通用品質管理系統，而EXCiPACT是專門為製藥輔料製造商設計的進階標準。

重要特點：

  • EXCiPACT是ISO 9001的「附加件（Annex）」，不是獨立標準。廠商必須同時持有 ISO 9001 + EXCiPACT 認證。
  • EXCiPACT完全包含EC指引中的GMP原則，因此在供應鏈風險評估中，EXCiPACT認證可以直接降低RFS6（QMS標準）的風險分數至Low（1分）。
  • NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363與EXCiPACT實質等效，但是為不使用ISO 9001的廠商設計的替代路徑。
  • IPEC-PQG GMP Guide也與EXCiPACT等效——這三者（EXCiPACT、NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363、IPEC-PQG）代表同一層級的風險緩解能力。

類比： EXCiPACT之於輔料製造商，就像ISO 13485之於醫療器材廠商——都是在通用ISO 9001之上，針對特定監管要求的強化版本。

GMP標準決策邏輯：依劑型和風險選擇最低要求

理解GMP標準梯度的關鍵是：不是要求所有輔料都用最高標準，而是依據OverallRPN和藥品用途，找到「足夠」的標準。決策邏輯如下：

  • 低風險輔料 + 外用藥品（Topical）： ISO 9001或EFfCI GMP通常足夠。不需要藥品級GMP，成本更低。
  • 中風險輔料 + 口服固體製劑： EXCiPACT™或IPEC-PQG GMP Guide是標準選擇，代表製藥業對輔料的「最低期望」。
  • 高風險輔料 + 注射劑（Parenteral）： EC GMP Part II（ICH Q7）或更高。供應商實質上需具備API製造能力。
  • 高風險輔料 + 滅菌製劑（Sterile）： EC GMP Part I + Annex 1。這是最高要求，等同於藥品製造廠的無菌製造標準。

一個有趣的推論：若注射用輔料供應商僅持有ISO 9001而無EXCiPACT，依TR54-6邏輯，RFS6至少應評為Medium（3分），可能推高SCRPN，進而提高OverallRPN，觸發緩解要求。

比喻：GMP標準梯度就像「駕照級別」

ISO 9001相當於普通小客車駕照——可以開車，但不代表能開救護車或卡車。EXCiPACT是加開「專業車輛許可」，代表具備製藥級品質意識。ICH Q7是大型車駕照，適用於原料藥等複雜產品。EC GMP Annex 1則是飛機飛行執照——只有真正需要無菌製造能力的廠商才需要取得。

選錯駕照等級（GMP標準不足）不只是合規問題，更代表實際的品質保障缺口，直接反映在SCRPN的RFS6分數上。

應用情境：CDMO的IV山梨醇採購決策

情境： 一家CDMO正在使用山梨醇（Sorbitol）用於靜脈注射液（IV）配方。供應商是一家無稽核歷史的中間商（Broker），無EXCiPACT認證。

TR54-6要求MAH在使用前採取以下行動：

  1. 計算OverallRPN： IV用途代表高ERPN（藥品風險高）；無稽核歷史+Broker+無認證代表高SCRPN → OverallRPN很可能 > 10（不可接受）。
  2. 緩解供應鏈風險（降低SCRPN）：
    • 要求Broker提供原廠製造商的完整資訊並進行供應鏈追溯（降低RFS1）
    • 對原廠製造商進行第三方稽核或要求其取得EXCiPACT認證（降低RFS6）
    • 要求防竄改包裝和逐批次的身份確認測試（降低RFS5 + 輔料控制）
  3. 確認GMP標準需求： IV用途 → 至少需達EC GMP Part I水準；若為無菌製劑，需Part I + Annex 1。僅ISO 9001遠不足夠。
  4. 重新評分： 所有緩解措施落實並記錄於品質協議後，重新計算OverallRPN，確認降至可接受水準（<10，最好<4）再放行使用。

結論： TR54-6不允許「繞過」風險評估直接使用。任何高風險輔料在未完成緩解循環前，都不應投入生產。

7.0 Ongoing Review — Formalized Risk Assessment Lifecycle

持續回顧 — 正式風險評估生命週期

7.0 The Risk Assessment as a Living Document

Once the excipient risk assessment is formally in place, it will be used as living data and will contribute to a knowledge base through ongoing risk review. Figure 7.0-1 shows the overall generic risk model with respect to risk review only.

Risk Review generally will be conducted in two modes:

  • Periodic (monitoring-driven): Scheduled reassessment at defined intervals based on the excipient's risk tier
  • Event-driven (Trigger-based): Immediate reassessment whenever a qualifying event occurs

The lifecycle model mirrors the generic risk management cycle: Initiate → Risk ID → Risk Analysis → Risk Evaluation → Risk Reduction → Risk Acceptance → Communication → Risk Review → (loop back). This is not a one-time exercise; Lifecycle Management ensures the risk assessment remains current and actionable.

"The risk assessment… will be used as living data and will contribute to a knowledge base through ongoing risk review." — TR54-6, Section 7.0

核心概念：活文件與生命週期管理

什麼是「活文件」(Living Document)?

Lifecycle Management（生命週期管理）的核心觀念是：風險評估不是「做一次就結束」的靜態文件，而是持續更新的活文件。這與 ICH Q10《藥品品質系統》的哲學一致——品質應透過整個產品生命週期持續監控與改進。

可以把它想成汽車的維修記錄：你不只在買車時做一次檢查，每次更換零件、發生事故或達到里程碑時，都需要更新紀錄。賦形劑風險評估也是如此。

  • ICH Q9 QRM 框架要求持續監控風險控制的有效性
  • ICH Q10 PQS 框架要求建立知識管理系統，使風險評估能隨新知識演進
  • EU GMP Annex 15（查驗/驗證）也強調定期回顧是法規期望

比喻：汽車保險年度回顧

你的汽車保險不會永久不變——每年要回顧保費、保障範圍是否合適。當你「搬家」（新廠址）、「換新車」（新供應商）或「出了事故」（品質異常）時，你會立刻重新評估風險，而不是等到年度回顧。

TR54-6 的邏輯完全相同：定期回顧（如同年度保險審查）+ 觸發事件回顧（如同發生重大事件後立即重評）。

觸發因素：何時必須重新評估？

必須記住的六大關鍵觸發因素

這六類觸發因素是考試與實際工作中最常被測試的知識點：

  1. 供應商變更 — 新的製造商/供應商，或現有供應商的 GMP 認證狀態改變
  2. 規格變更 — 賦形劑規格或藥典標準的修訂（Pharmacopeial changes）
  3. 法規行動 — 主管機關的召回、警告信、法規要求更新
  4. 新安全資訊 — 出現新的毒理學資料或業界安全事件（如 TSE 疫情）
  5. 稽核發現 — 內部稽核或外部查廠發現供應商存在系統性缺陷
  6. 產品配方變更 — 現有產品的新配方，或全新產品使用現有賦形劑

任何一項觸發均強制要求重新評估，且過程、決策與理由必須文件化。

  2. 現有庫存評估 — 審查已驗收入庫的批次，判斷是否有品質疑慮
  3. 增強測試 — 對後續進貨批次實施強化放行測試，直至風險釐清
  4. 替代供應商資格確認 — 啟動備用供應商的 Supplier Qualification 程序
  5. 法規通知評估 — 評估是否需要通知藥品主管機關（視藥證要求而定）
  6. CAPA 更新 — 將此事件納入 CAPA 系統，並更新供應商評估記錄

8.0 Benefits and Value / QRM / Knowledge Management

效益與價值 / QRM / 知識管理

8.0 The Business Case for Formalized Excipient RA

A global excipient risk assessment throughout the drug product lifecycle delivers the following advantages:

  • Documented Knowledge Management: Structured knowledge about the excipient supply chain, building a growing institutional repository
  • Rapid new-product evaluation: Ability to rapidly and effectively evaluate the requirements for new products that use existing or new excipients
  • Preferred supply chain identification: Identification of supply chains that present lower risks and preferred supply chains with which to build long-term relationships
  • Focused oversight: Focus on specific risk mitigation oversight activities for both manufacturer or supplier and user — directing audit resources to where they matter most
  • Deeper product knowledge: More detailed product knowledge and increased understanding of how the risks from excipients may affect product quality
  • Formal trigger database: Formal assessment and database of risk review triggers, such as major changes and critical investigations (CAPA integration)
  • Knowledge-based decision-making: Proactive rather than reactive risk management — moving from crisis response to anticipatory control
  • Common language for stakeholders: Provides a common language and approach for clear communication with stakeholders throughout the supply chain
  • Knowledge-sharing opportunities: Opportunities for comparative assessments between manufacturers, suppliers, and end users
"A global excipient risk assessment throughout the drug product lifecycle delivers… knowledge-based and proactive decision-making." — TR54-6, Section 8.0

效益解析：為何值得投入？

知識管理 (Knowledge Management) 的本質

Knowledge Management 在此脈絡中的意義是：建立一個結構化的賦形劑風險資料庫，隨時間累積「機構智慧」，使每次決策都比上一次更有依據。

這個資料庫包含：

  • 供應商稽核歷史記錄（Supplier audit history）
  • 分析證書趨勢分析（CoA trend analysis）
  • 客訴記錄與根本原因分析（Complaint records & RCA）
  • 變更通知記錄（Change notification log）
  • 各批次風險評分變化趨勢

隨著時間累積，這個資料庫讓你能在幾小時內完成新產品的賦形劑風險評估，而非從零開始花費數週。這就是「主動式（proactive）」vs「被動式（reactive）」風險管理的差異。

比喻：信用評分系統

想像銀行的信用評分（Credit Score）系統：每次你的供應商完成一筆交易（準時交貨、CoA 合格）就累積「信用」；每次發生品質事件（批次拒絕、遲交）就扣分。長期累積後，你對每個供應商的「風險評分」越來越精準。

TR54-6 的 Knowledge Management 正是建立這樣一套供應商「信用歷史」——讓採購、品保、法規事務部門能用共同的數字語言溝通，而非各自憑直覺判斷。

法規合規價值：三大主管機關期望

正式化賦形劑風險評估直接回應以下法規要求：

  • EU GMP Annex 15（查驗/變更控制）：要求持續回顧與文件化，與 TR54-6 生命週期模型直接對應
  • EU EC Guidelines (2015)：明確要求對賦形劑供應商實施「正式化風險評估」以確定適用 GMP 等級，TR54-6 正是其實施指引
  • ICH Q9 QRM：要求風險管理為持續性活動，而非單次評估；TR54-6 的觸發機制正是此要求的操作化

在稽核中，能展示一套成熟的 Lifecycle Management 系統，代表法規成熟度（regulatory maturity）與品質文化的具體證明。

10.0   Annex I: Real World Examples of Risk Assessments

The generic model proposed in Section 6.0 was developed based on real-world risk assessment processes donated by several marketing authorization holders (MAHs). Many risk assessment approaches used some form of risk ranking and filtering approach — conducting assessments in two separate streams that can then be combined in an overall risk score.

One stream would provide a risk profile for the excipient itself, based on its use in the finished product and any intrinsic risk factors. The second stream would generate a risk profile for the supply chain, including the excipient manufacturer, suppliers, brokers, and any other players, as well as the strength of their quality management systems (QMS). The two streams would then be combined into one risk evaluation to determine whether the overall risk is at an acceptable level or additional controls are needed.

核心概念：雙流模型架構

TR54-6 通用模型的起源

Section 10 揭示了一個重要事實：TR54-6 的通用模型不是憑空設計的，而是從多家 MAH（上市許可持有人）實際使用中的 RA 流程歸納而來。這六個案例就是那些「原始模型」。

雙流模型的邏輯：

  • 第一流（Excipient Stream）：關注賦形劑本身 —— 它是什麼、用在什麼劑型、有何固有風險（毒性、微生物污染可能性、關鍵品質屬性 CQA 的影響）
  • 第二流（Supply Chain Stream）：關注供應商 —— 製造商 GMP 水準、供應鏈長度、中間商、品質管理系統 QMS 強度
  • 合流決策：兩流評分合併後，判斷整體風險是否可接受，或是否需要額外的 control strategy

為何六家公司模型各不相同？

每家公司的賦形劑組合、劑型、供應商基數、資源限制都不同。TR54-6 提供的是框架，不是單一答案。六個案例正是為了展示這種靈活性。

10.1   Example 1: Company U

Company U is a global manufacturer of various pharmaceutical dosage forms. The company established a Microsoft Excel tool to be used as a corporate standard.

When multiple manufacturing sites use the same excipient provided by the same excipient manufacturing site, the risk assessments can be shared across all sites (with appropriate site-specific modifications to accommodate excipient usage, supply chain complexity, and other factors).

First, basic information about the excipient and the manufacturer is collected. Then, the collected information is consolidated to produce an overview. For each section of the model, the parameters to evaluate are defined, each parameter is described in detail, and possible scenarios are developed. The severity of each parameter is assigned a standard value. In conducting the evaluation, Company U determines the actual probability of each parameter.

The analysis is conducted as follows:

  • Excipient and excipient usage risk score — Each identified risk factor is evaluated based on the actual origin or usage of the excipient and the actual supply chain to determine the probability of risk (P). The predefined weight, or severity (S), is used to calculate the risk score for each parameter (P × S). The overall excipient risk score is calculated as the average across the parameters.
  • Combined material risk score — A combined material risk score is calculated by adding the excipient risk score and the excipient usage risk score.
  • Manufacturer risk score — The list of risk factors is evaluated based on the actual performance and QMS of the manufacturer. The predefined weight (S) is used to calculate each parameter's risk score (P × S). The overall manufacturer risk score is the average across all parameters.
  • Gap analysis and actions — The combined material risk score and the manufacturer risk score are evaluated together using a risk matrix to identify the "gap level." Where a gap exists between the manufacturer risk score and the combined material risk score, the gap must be evaluated to determine the appropriate remediation.

學習導讀：Company U — 缺口分析矩陣法

核心概念：什麼是「Gap（缺口）」？

Company U 的創新在於它不只問「風險有多高？」，而是問「供應商的 GMP 能力與這個賦形劑所需的 GMP 水準之間，差了多少？」——這個差距就是 Gap。

Gap 分析的三種可能結果：

  • Gap = Low（低缺口）：供應商能力足以管理賦形劑風險 → 接受，維持現有管控
  • Gap = Medium（中缺口）：供應商能力略顯不足 → 輔導供應商提升，或加強稽核/測試頻率
  • Gap = High（高缺口）：供應商嚴重不足以管控高風險賦形劑 → 必須立即採取行動：(a) 要求 CAPA，(b) 強制開發備用供應商，或 (c) 更換供應商

比喻：Gap 分析就像「貸款審核」

想像銀行決定是否核貸：

  • 貸款金額（賦形劑風險） = 你想借的錢越多，要求越嚴格
  • 申請人信用（製造商 GMP 水準） = 你的財力是否足以還款
  • Gap = 財力不足以支撐貸款金額的程度

高風險賦形劑（高額貸款）配上 GMP 薄弱的供應商（信用不佳）= 最大缺口，銀行會拒貸或要求擔保。

Company U 的特色：共享 RA 機制

多個製造廠使用同一家供應商提供的同一種賦形劑時，RA 結果可以跨廠共享（加上廠址特定修改）。這大幅節省了多廠集團的評估資源，是企業標準化的最佳實踐。

10.2   Example 2: Company V

Company V is a global manufacturer of pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical solid oral dosage forms and lyophilized injectables. To a large extent, its products are manufactured through contract manufacturing organizations (CMOs).

The risk model was created to meet the European Commission Guidelines on the Formalised Risk Assessment for Ascertaining the Appropriate GMP for Excipients of Medicinal Products for Human Use (EC Guidelines), and the risk factors to be considered reflect those in the guidelines. The model was implemented in a global SOP that applies for all sites manufacturing commercial products and, going forward, for new products, including biologics.

This model was extended for products during their transfer into commercial production. The quality risk assessment tool is applied based on risk ranking and filtering, evaluating the risk factors in two groups:

  • Multiple criteria analysis
    • Function and use of excipient (→ Score Excipient)
    • Manufacturer (→ Score Manufacturer)
    • Suppliers (→ Score Supplier)
  • Risk score for each excipient (low, medium, or high)
    • Independent risk factors
    • Risk ranking and weighing
    • Overall weighing

The three single scores — Function and Use, Manufacturer, and Suppliers — were combined by multiplication into a weighted RPN score. Acceptability was evaluated based on the RPN value, and mitigation actions were considered according to the overall risk score (low, medium, or high).

[Figure 10.2-1🔍 — Three parallel scoring streams: (1) Function & Use of Excipient → Score Excipient; (2) Manufacturer assessment → Score Manufacturer; (3) Supplier assessment → Score Supplier. Key outcomes from implementation:

  • No major issues were found for existing products, though some minor changes in audit strategy and other controls were instituted as a result.
  • One benefit of performing the risk assessment was obtaining the collected knowledge and an overview of the supply chains.
  • Company V found that responsibilities for information-gathering need to be changed in the global SOP. Currently, the global support function gathers needed information from different stakeholders. Using one point of contact for each product is under discussion as an alternative.
  • While the model has been well accepted, it has not yet been used as a living risk assessment as much as would be liked.
  • The risk assessment is stored in the GMP documentation system. Software automatically triggers formal review and approval cycles globally. More flexibility to revise and update the risk assessment would be appreciated, especially in cases where the RA is completed before the drug product is commercially available. The company plans to introduce a new electronic tool to serve this purpose.

學習導讀：Company V — 乘法加權 RPN 模型

比喻：這就像「信用評分（FICO Score）」

美國的 FICO 信用評分模型將多個因素（付款記錄、欠款比例、信用年齡、新申請次數、信用組合）各自加權後，合成一個單一評分 (300-850)，用來預測違約風險。

Company V 的 RPN 模型做的是同樣的事：

  • Score Excipient = 這種賦形劑本身的風險權重
  • Score Manufacturer = 製造商的 GMP 能力評分
  • Score Supplier = 供應鏈（中間商/代理商）的評分
  • Weighted RPN = Score Excipient × Score Manufacturer × Score Supplier

乘法（而非加法）的好處：任何一項評分極高，最終 RPN 都會被拉高，不允許其他項的「好成績」掩蓋單一嚴重弱點。

Company V 實施後的真實挑戰

原文誠實記錄了幾個困難，這對學習者非常有價值：

  • 資訊蒐集責任不清：誰負責收集哪些資料？全球支援部門 vs. 各廠產品負責人，需要重新定義。
  • 活的 RA（Living RA）難以維持：RA 完成後放入文件系統就「凍結」了，未能持續更新反映供應鏈變化。
  • CMO 複雜性：大部分產品透過 CMO 製造，RA 必須涵蓋 CMO 使用的賦形劑來源，資訊取得難度更高。

這些挑戰在你的公司可能同樣存在。建議：建立 RA 觸發機制（供應商變更、重大品質事件）以確保 RA 的時效性。

EC Guidelines 連結

Company V 的模型明確對應 2015 年歐盟委員會指南（EC Guidelines on Formalised Risk Assessment for Excipients GMP）。這表示在歐洲上市的藥品，其賦形劑 RA 若採用 Company V 模型，可直接對應監管要求。

10.3   Example 3: Company W

Company W is a global manufacturer of pharmaceuticals and devices, primarily parenteral products.

As part of the material assessment, the company examines the correlation of impact between parameters (attributes) describing the excipient and the CQAs of the product in which it is an ingredient. An excipient assessment provides input to material specifications, as illustrated in Figure 10.3-1. In this way, the company evaluates the impact of the excipient on the finished drug products.

Attributes are derived by ideation from a defined list of potential risk sources. Attributes with impact are included on the specification, and limits or other controls are defined after analyzing the possible impacts of variation in the attribute. This method uses a qualitative assessment with a simple +/− score for impact combined with text rationales and references describing the underlying science. The impact is evaluated considering the amount of material to be used and its function in the formulation.

The result of the assessment is used to define the excipient's specification based on the specific use in a specific drug product. An assessment is made for each use in a drug product.

[Figure 10.3-1🔍 — Excipient attribute list → Ideation of potential risk sources → Attribute-to-CQA impact assessment (+/−) → Attributes with impact → Specification setting & limits; In parallel: Supply chain assessment (qualitative: impact / likelihood / controls) → Additional controls if risk identified]

An assessment of the supply chain is carried out in parallel with the material assessment. The supply chain — including manufacturer, retailers, brokers, and others — is evaluated using a predefined list of risk elements, brainstorming possible relevant issues in the specific supply chain for the particular excipient. 學習導讀：Company W — 定性 +/- 簡化矩陣法

Company W 的獨特之處

相較其他五家公司使用數值評分（如 P×S、RPN），Company W 選擇了純定性方法，只用 +/- 判斷每個屬性是否對 CQA 有影響。

為何 Company W 可以這樣做？

  • 主要產品是注射劑（parenteral products），對每一個屬性的科學理解已相當深入，定性判斷有紮實的科學文獻支撐
  • 「有/無影響」的二元判斷反而更清晰，避免了數值評分的主觀性誤差
  • 評估結果直接輸出到物料規格（material specification），目的是定義規格限度，而非單純判斷供應商風險等級

簡化矩陣：何時適用，何時不夠？

適合使用簡化 +/- 方法的情境：

  • 產品組合單純（如 Company W 以注射劑為主，劑型一致）
  • 科學知識成熟，屬性影響明確
  • RA 目的是定規格而非比較供應商

不適合的情境（需要更複雜模型）：

  • 賦形劑組合龐大，涵蓋多種劑型（固體口服、注射劑、外用）
  • 供應商眾多，需要排序決定稽核優先順序
  • 監管機構要求提供量化風險評分作為記錄

比喻：+/- 就像「航空安全核查表」

飛行員起飛前的核查表用的是 Yes/No，而非 1-10 分評分。對於已知風險明確的項目，二元確認往往比數值評分更快速、更不容易出現「分數膨脹」問題。Company W 的方法體現了相同邏輯。

10.4   Example 4: Company X

Company X expects its suppliers to comply with the IPEC-PQG Joint GMP Guide, EXCiPACT Standard, or NSF/IPEC/ANSI 363. The excipient risk management process considered input from the manufacturer qualification process, audits, relationships, testing, and outside intelligence. The company also worked with its CMOs to align expectations between them and the MAH and rationalized scores.

The actual process is owned by the company's organizational product quality management unit (PQM) and follows the model illustrated in Figure 10.4-1.

The product quality manager (PQM) is responsible for pulling together information and subject matter experts from across Company X to provide the data needed to complete the spreadsheet. Much of the information comes from the company's audit team. The model allows for rationalization across materials and suppliers to ensure that scoring is consistent and can be readily justified.

Company X maintains an SOP that describes the overall process, including triggers for revisiting a risk assessment. Because the PQMs also are involved in the change control process for company materials, they have firsthand knowledge of any changes that would require a review of the excipient risk assessment. As part of this process, Company X has already made several revisions to some excipient risk assessments.

Initially, there was some resistance to the concept and process. Now that the system is in place, however, staff have noticed that it is easier to find information without going into several systems or speaking to several departments.

Figure 10.4-1🔍: Risk Assessment Model for Company X

[Figure 10.4-1🔍 — Inputs: Manufacturer qualification process + Audits + Relationships + Testing + Outside intelligence → PQM consolidates → Spreadsheet-based scoring (Intrinsic excipient risk + Supply chain / manufacturer risk) → Combined risk score → Risk level → Control strategy; Change control process triggers RA review]

Key features of Company X's approach:

  • Combines input from multiple sources including audits, testing, outside intelligence, and inter-company relationships
  • Integrates with the CMO network — aligning MAH and CMO expectations and rationalizing scores across sites
  • Linked directly to the change control process, ensuring RA reviews are triggered by changes
  • Cross-material rationalization ensures consistency and auditability of scoring decisions

學習導讀：Company X — 混合式評分 + 跨廠理性化

Company X 的評分公式結構

Company X 的模型整合多個來源，評分結構可描述如下：

固有賦形劑風險分數
  = 賦形劑特性風險 (Intrinsic Risk)
  + 使用情境風險 (Usage Risk: 劑型/給藥途徑)

供應鏈風險分數
  = 製造商稽核結果
  + 製造商資格認定
  + 測試數據
  + 外部情報（行業動態、警告信等）
  + 關係品質（合作年數、透明度）

Combined Risk Score = 固有風險分數 + 供應鏈風險分數
→ 對應控制策略（低/中/高）

與通用 OverallRPN 的差異：Company X 不採用簡單 P×S×D 乘法，而是用加法模型並引入「外部情報」和「關係品質」這兩個通用模型中較少見的因素。這反映了其以 CMO 為主的運營模式——供應商關係本身就是風險信號。

「跨物料理性化（Cross-material rationalization）」是什麼？

想像公司有 200 種賦形劑，由 50 家供應商供應。如果每種賦形劑獨立評分，不同評估者對「相似情境」可能給出不同分數，形成不一致性。

Company X 的 PQM 負責確保：同一供應商、同一評分維度，在不同產品的 RA 中獲得相同或可解釋差異的分數。這就是「理性化」——讓評分系統具備內部一致性，能通過監管稽核。

變更控制 (Change Control) 作為 RA 觸發機制

Company X 將 RA 複審直接嵌入變更控制流程：只要有影響賦形劑的變更（供應商、規格、製程），PQM 自動知道並觸發 RA 審查。這解決了「活的 RA」維護難題，是一個值得學習的制度設計。

10.5   Example 5: Company Y

The model illustrated in Figure 10.5-1🔍 applies to finished products for human use that Company Y manufactures for European markets.

Company Y manufacturing sites completed risk ranking of each excipient from each manufacturer used at their sites.

A risk ranking tool is used to evaluate each excipient from each manufacturer. A weight of 1 (low), 3 (medium), or 10 (high) is assigned for each of six criteria and, from that, an overall risk ranking of the excipient (low, medium, or high) is calculated. Where the GMPs used to manufacture excipients are found to be inappropriate according to risk ranking, mitigation measures are implemented.

A risk analysis tool for manufacturer audit frequency is then used to assess the risk and determine audit frequency. Based on the outcome, a second risk assessment could be performed to determine if:

  1. Additional mitigation measures should be implemented, or
  2. Use of the excipient from that specific manufacturer should be discontinued.

If Company Y decides to continue to use the excipient after implementing additional mitigation measures to reduce the remaining risk to an acceptable level, the company documents this decision in a "statement to maintain business."

Company Y periodically reevaluates risk profiles during the review of a manufacturer's performance. A risk assessment is reevaluated in cases of a significant quality event, e.g., significant change by manufacturer, serious GMP deficiencies, or loss of GMP certification.

Figure 10.5-1🔍: Risk Assessment Model for Company Y

[Figure 10.5-1🔍 — Risk ranking tool (6 criteria, weights 1/3/10) → Overall excipient risk ranking (Low/Medium/High); If GMP inappropriate → Mitigation measures; Risk analysis for audit frequency → Audit schedule; Second RA: continue with additional mitigation (document "statement to maintain business") OR discontinue use; Periodic review + quality event trigger → RA reevaluation]

Implementation findings:

  • The model is simple to implement.
  • The result of a risk assessment sometimes varies between sites. However, after implementing a control strategy, the sites could reach the same decision to continue use of the excipient from that manufacturer.
  • Use of this model aids Company Y in implementation and modification of control measures to reduce risk to an acceptable level.

學習導讀：Company Y — 分層稽核頻率模型

應用情境：CDMO 適用 Company Y 的分層供應商資格模型

假設你是一家 CDMO（委託開發製造機構），為多個藥廠客戶製造注射劑產品。你的賦形劑供應商可以按 Company Y 的分層邏輯管理：

  • Tier 1（最高風險）：注射用賦形劑（注射用水、緩衝劑、防腐劑）+ 單一來源供應商 + 尚未 EXCiPACT 認證
    • 稽核頻率：每 12-18 個月一次現場稽核
    • 管控：每批次 CoA 審查 + 定期進廠測試
  • Tier 2（中等風險）：口服固體賦形劑 + 多個備用供應商 + 通藥典標準
    • 稽核頻率：每 2-3 年一次（可由問卷調查替代）
    • 管控：CoA 審查 + 抽樣測試
  • Tier 3（低風險）：外用/局部用賦形劑 + 知名跨國供應商 + 已有 ISO 認證
    • 稽核頻率：每 4-5 年或僅需問卷
    • 管控：CoA 審查即可

Company Y 的 1/3/10 加權系統正是支撐此分層決策的量化依據。

「Statement to Maintain Business」是什麼？

這是 Company Y 特有的文件，用於記錄「我們知道這個供應商有缺陷，但基於以下理由，在實施額外管控後仍繼續使用」的正式決定。

這份文件的重要性：

  • 提供監管稽查時的證明：決定是知情且有根據的，而非忽視風險
  • 記錄所有額外管控措施（CAPA 要求、增加測試等）
  • 設定複審時間點，避免風險決定永久化

類似概念在品質管理中稱為「risk acceptance with documented rationale」（有文件根據的風險接受）。

10.6   Example 6: Company Z

Company Z's model was designed as a centralized process focused on highlighting the inherent risks and control strategies associated with an excipient, regardless of the manufacturer. The process is also designed to provide the user with the minimum quality standard expected for the manufacture of the excipient and its associated key risks. These risk and control strategies provide input for the gap analysis and, subsequently, to the quality risk assessment performed on supplier sites.

Company Z established a corporate procedure that outlines the requirements for the excipient risk assessment process and its linkages to the overarching supplier quality management process.

Figure 10.6-1🔍: Risk Assessment Model for Company Z

[Figure 10.6-1🔍 — Centralized excipient RA team performs inherent risk assessment (excipient-level, manufacturer-independent) → Output: Quality standard + Control strategy; Each supplier quality manager performs gap analysis (excipient standard vs. actual supplier capability) → Gaps identified → Validated gaps + other supplier intelligence → Supplier site quality risk assessment → Supplier risk ranking (Low/Medium/High) → Quality oversight level; Vendor assurance database shares results to formulation sites; Change control → Update supplier risk ranking; Periodic review & quality events → Ad hoc RA review]

The first part of the "end-to-end process excipient risk assessment" determines the control strategy and standards that are established by the centralized excipient risk assessment team. In the second part, the quality management group of the global supplier uses the output of the risk assessment to conduct individual supplier site quality risk assessments. The information from the individual supplier site quality risk assessments is shared with the formulation sites through a vendor assurance database system, highlighting any changes in supplier risk ranking via change control.

After the need for an excipient RA is identified, the centralized, cross-functional team performs the assessment, which focuses entirely on inherent risk. Supplier risks are not considered at this time. An electronic tool is used, and the output is used to determine the appropriate quality level for the excipient and any relevant excipient control strategy.

Each supplier quality manager then performs a gap analysis of the excipient supplier quality standard against an assessment of defined standards and excipient control strategies for supplied excipients. This identifies any gaps for the supplier. All gaps are challenged to ensure their validity and valid gaps are used as input, along with other supplier quality management intelligence, in performing the supplier site quality risk assessment. This final assessment generates a supplier risk ranking (low, medium, or high) which, in turn, determines the quality oversight to be applied.

The maintenance of the excipient RA is managed under the periodic review process. Quality events that impact excipient quality trigger an ad hoc excipient RA review.

Company Z implemented this process in March 2016 in line with the EC Guidelines.

Key benefits observed:

  • Consistency in managing excipients and suppliers. The centralized approach provided a concise framework for consistent input into the initial steps of the process. Implementation formalized the process and improved the documentation of information, supporting existing supplier risk assessment processes.
  • Grouping excipient grades into categories aligned with the route of administration, origin, and function has reduced the number of assessments required.
  • Conducting a formalized assessment process provides clarity to sourcing partners, both internal and external.
  • The risk assessment acts as an effective standard for comparing and assessing supply chain risks.

學習導讀：Company Z — 集中式 + 端到端多層供應鏈 RA

核心概念：複雜多層供應鏈的風險管理

Company Z 的模型是六家公司中最複雜的，因為它處理的是真正的「多層供應鏈風險」問題——不只是「我的賦形劑供應商」，而是「供應商的供應商」甚至更上游的風險。

Company Z 的兩階段架構：

  • 第一階段（集中式固有風險評估）：跨職能的集中團隊評估賦形劑本身的固有風險，與供應商無關。輸出的是「這種賦形劑應達到的最低品質標準與控制策略」——這是整個供應商網絡的基準線（baseline）。
  • 第二階段（各供應商缺口分析）：每位供應商品質經理以第一階段的基準線為標準，對各自負責的供應商廠址進行缺口分析，得出各廠址的風險等級。

為何分兩階段？這避免了「供應商偏見」——評估賦形劑固有風險的人不應同時是評估特定供應商的人，否則可能因關係親近而低估供應商風險。

比喻：食品安全稽查制度

想像食品藥物管理局（FDA）稽查大型食品供應鏈：

  • 第一步：FDA 制定「生菜的最低安全標準」（固有風險基準）— 這是全國一致的，與農場無關
  • 第二步：各地稽查官員前往個別農場，對照標準做缺口分析
  • 結果：每個農場得到自己的風險等級，決定稽查頻率

Company Z 的「集中式 RA + 個別供應商缺口分析」邏輯完全相同。

「Vendor Assurance Database」的角色

這個數據庫是讓資訊在整個組織流通的關鍵：集中RA結果 → 供應商風險等級 → 自動通知各製劑廠。當供應商風險等級因變更控制而更新，所有相關廠址都能即時知道，確保整個供應鏈管理的一致性。

在大型跨國藥廠中，這類數字化供應商管理系統（如 Veeva Vault QMS、TrackWise）是落實「活的 RA」的技術基礎。

Summary: Comparing the Six Company Models

六家公司綜合分析：共同點與差異點

6家公司的共同點（ICH Q9 核心不變）

無論採用何種模型，六家公司都遵循相同的底層邏輯：

  1. 識別危害（Hazard Identification）：賦形劑有哪些固有風險？供應鏈有哪些脆弱點？
  2. 評估風險（Risk Assessment）：可能性（P/Likelihood）× 嚴重性（S/Severity）的組合評估
  3. 決定管控（Risk Control）：接受 / 加強管控 / 要求 CAPA / 更換供應商
  4. 溝通與審查（Review & Communication）：定期複審 + 觸發機制確保 RA 保持有效
  5. 注射劑永遠是最嚴格審查對象：無論使用哪種模型，parenteral 用途的賦形劑評分最高，管控最嚴

差異點：各公司的個性化選擇

  • 評分方式：數值評分 vs. 定性 +/-；加法 vs. 乘法
  • 組織結構：集中式 (Z) vs. 分散至各廠 (Y)
  • 整合深度：獨立 RA 工具 (W) vs. 嵌入品質體系 (X, Z)
  • 電子化程度：Excel (U) vs. 企業數據庫 (Z)
  • 供應鏈複雜度：直接供應商 (Y) vs. 多層供應鏈 (Z)

選擇哪種模型，應根據你公司的：賦形劑組合複雜度、供應商基數大小、可用資源、以及監管市場的要求。

練習思考題

  1. 比較題：Company U 和 Company Z 都使用了「gap analysis」這個詞，但兩者的 gap 定義和用途不同。請比較這兩個 gap 分析在流程中的位置和具體含義。
    （提示：U 的 gap 是矩陣輸出的最終決策；Z 的 gap 是中間步驟，輸入到供應商廠址 QRM）
  2. 情境選擇題：你是一家中型製藥公司的品質主管，產品組合包括口服固體製劑和一條注射劑生產線，共使用 80 種賦形劑，由 30 家供應商提供，其中 5 家是注射劑專用供應商。你的資源有限，無法建立像 Company Z 那樣的集中式團隊。請問你最適合參考哪一家或哪幾家公司的模型？請說明理由。
