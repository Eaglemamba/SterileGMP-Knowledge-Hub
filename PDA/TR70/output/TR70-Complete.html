1.0 Introduction

This technical report provides comprehensive information and suggested best practices as well as appropriate references to support guidance on cleaning and disinfection programs for aseptic manufacturing facilities.

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this document is to identify systematic elements that are essential to assuring an appropriate and compliant cleaning and disinfection program for aseptic and bioburden controlled manufacturing facilities and classified environments.

1.2 Scope

The document covers cleaning and disinfection within controlled and noncontrolled environments using chemical agents that reduce or destroy microorganisms. It provides guidance for non-product-contact surface cleaning and disinfection.

This document is not intended to fully address product-contact surface cleaning from a CIP (clean-in-place) or COP (clean-out-of-place) system, which is specifically addressed in:

  • PDA TR No. 29 (Revised 2012): Points to Consider for Cleaning Validation
  • PDA TR No. 49: Points to Consider for Biotechnology Cleaning Validation
This document should be considered as technical guidance; it is not intended to establish any mandatory or implied standard.

The technical report team consisted of cleaning and disinfection experts from global pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical companies, academia, and manufacturers of disinfection agents.

解讀與應用

TR70 的核心定位

TR70 是目前最完整的製藥無菌設施清潔與消毒實務指引之一。它不是法規本身，但其建議被全球監管機構在稽核時視為「業界最佳實務」的參考標準。

在 CDMO 環境中，每當稽核員詢問「你們如何選擇消毒劑？」，能夠引用 TR70 框架並展示系統性驗證文件，是通過稽核的關鍵。

範圍邊界：非產品接觸面

TR70 聚焦於地板、牆壁、天花板、設備外表面等非產品接觸面 (non-product-contact surfaces) 的清潔。

產品接觸面（如配液罐內壁、管路）另由 TR29 和 TR49 規範，在 TR70 學習時需留意這個邊界。

比喻：醫院與手術室的差異

想像 TR70 就像「手術室清潔 SOP 指南」——不是說如何清洗手術器械（那是 CIP/COP 的事），而是說如何消毒手術室的地面、門把、推車，讓環境維持在微生物可控範圍。

TR70 vs TR29 vs TR49 — 三份報告的分工

  • TR70：設施環境（牆壁、地板、設備外表面）
  • TR29：小分子藥品產品接觸面清潔驗證
  • TR49：生技藥品（大分子）清潔驗證

關鍵術語解析

三劑層次：最重要的區別

TR70 最核心的概念就是這三者的層次差異，稽核時常被問到：

Sanitizer（殺菌劑）

降低細菌數至安全水平（通常 3-log 減少）。不殺孢子。適用於低潔淨度區域或輔助步驟。

Disinfectant（消毒劑）

消滅活性形態微生物（vegetative organisms）。不殺孢子。Grade C/D 主要使用，Grade B 也使用。

Sporicide（殺孢子劑）

消滅所有活性微生物 及 孢子。Grade A/B 無菌環境必須定期使用，因為消毒劑無法消除孢子。

Log Reduction 實務計算

如果表面有 10,000 CFU（104）：

  • 1-log 減少 → 剩下 1,000 CFU
  • 3-log 減少 → 剩下 10 CFU（Sanitizer 標準）
  • 6-log 減少 → 剩下 0.01 CFU（滅菌標準，SAL = 10-6）

TR70 要求的 3-log 減少是 Sanitizer 的最低門檻；Sporicide 的要求通常更高。

Contact Time — 為什麼不能提早擦乾？

想像消毒劑是「浸泡殺蟲劑」——藥劑必須維持濕潤狀態一段時間才能完成作用。如果表面提早乾燥，接觸時間不足，即使用了正確藥劑，效果也無法保證。這是操作 SOP 中必須規定最短接觸時間，並在驗證時確認的原因。

APA 分區 vs. 藥劑選擇邏輯

Aseptic Processing Area (APA) 包含多個潔淨等級區域，EU GMP 以 Grade A/B/C/D 定義：

  • Grade A（充填區）：Sporicide 必須定期使用；消毒劑輪替使用
  • Grade B（背景環境）：Sporicide + Disinfectant 輪替
  • Grade C/D（支援區）：Disinfectant 為主，Sanitizer 可輔助

Transfer Disinfection（移入消毒）

當物料、設備或容器從低潔淨區移入高潔淨區前，必須進行表面消毒處理。這個程序需要經過驗證，確認接觸時間與消毒劑有效。是稽核中常見查核點。

VPHP（氣態過氧化氫）的特殊地位

VPHP 是目前 Grade A/B 隔離器 (Isolator) 和 RABS 生物去汙 (bio-decontamination) 的主要方式。TR70 將其列入術語，後續章節有深入討論。其作用機制包含孢子殺滅，屬於 Sporicide 層級。

3.0 Sanitizer, Disinfectant, and Sporicide Claims and Classifications

Sanitizers, disinfectants, and sporicides are chemical agents that reduce, eliminate, or destroy microorganisms. Registration testing is performed to meet requirements of organizations such as the:

  • U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
  • EU Biocidal Directive
  • Australian Therapeutics Goods Administration (TGA)
  • Health Canada

Registration testing addresses high-bioburden environments (hospitals, food processors, clinical laboratories). Label claims on commercial products are approved through this registration process.

Testing for use of these chemical agents in GMP manufacturing also requires testing prior to use. However, this testing is significantly different — the bioburden load within a cleanroom is much lower.

GMP-context testing (internal qualification) is performed with:

  • Lower bioburden levels (reflecting actual cleanroom conditions)
  • Decreased wet contact time periods
  • On varying substrates (floor, wall, stainless steel, etc.)

This internal qualification is an expectation of regulatory agencies worldwide (FDA, EMEA, and others) and may be reviewed during inspections.

Classification of Antimicrobial Chemical Agents

Note: The term "disinfectant" is often used as a general term. In TR70, the term antimicrobial chemical agent is used when referring collectively to sanitizers, disinfectants, and sporicides.

分類解析與實務應用

關鍵區別：商業登記驗證 vs. 製藥內部資格驗證

市面上消毒劑標示的有效性聲明（如「殺滅 99.9% 細菌」）是在高菌量環境下測試的結果（醫院、食品加工廠）。

製藥無菌廠的潔淨室菌量遠低於此，因此需要另行進行符合 GMP 實際條件的內部資格驗證 (in-house qualification)。這一點非常重要 — 不能直接引用廠商數據作為 GMP 依據。

常用藥劑的特性速覽

  • IPA 70%（酒精）：Disinfectant。速效、易揮發、無殘留。不殺孢子。Grade B/C/D 日常消毒主力。
  • QAC（四級銨）：Disinfectant。持效時間長，但有累積抗藥性疑慮，需與其他類輪替。
  • 次氯酸鈉（漂白水）：Sporicide 能力，但對金屬腐蝕性強，需控制濃度與接觸時間。
  • 過氧乙酸（PAA）：強效 Sporicide。廣用於 Grade A/B 環境及隔離器表面消毒。
  • VPHP：Sporicide，用於密閉空間整體生物去汙（隔離器、RABS）。需專業設備。

「輪替」策略的生態學邏輯

如果長期只用同一種藥劑，環境中的微生物會逐漸篩選出抗性菌株，就像抗生素抗藥性問題一樣。TR70 建議定期輪替不同機制的消毒劑，目的是防止任何單一菌種發展出耐受性。在 CDMO 實務中，通常每 1-3 個月輪替一次，並記錄在 EM 趨勢分析報告中。

基質 (Substrate) 相容性驗證

「在不鏽鋼上有效的藥劑」不一定對環氧樹脂地板或塑膠設備外殼有相同效果，也可能造成材料腐蝕或降解。

TR70 要求的內部資格驗證必須在實際使用的基質種類上進行測試，包括：不鏽鋼 (SS316L)、環氧樹脂地板、塗裝牆面、玻璃、聚碳酸酯等。

稽核準備思考題

如果稽核員問：「你們的 Grade B 環境為什麼選用 X 消毒劑？」

標準答案框架：

  1. 藥劑類別說明（Disinfectant / Sporicide）
  2. 針對哪些目標微生物（環境 EM 歷史分離株）
  3. 內部資格驗證 (in-house qualification) 數據支持
  4. 輪替策略說明
  5. Change control 記錄

4.0 Regulatory Expectations

4.1 Regulations and Guidance

References to cleaning and disinfection can be found in regulations from various organizations. Key citations:

21 CFR Part 211.42(c)(10)(v) — U.S. FDA (cGMP)

"A system for cleaning and disinfecting the room and equipment to produce aseptic conditions."

FDA Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing — cGMP (2004), Section X

"The suitability, efficacy, and limitations of sanitization agents and procedures should be assessed."

EU GMP Annex 1 — Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products

"In clean areas, all exposed surfaces should be smooth, impervious and unbroken in order to minimize the shedding or accumulation of particles or micro-organisms and to permit the repeated application of cleaning agents, and disinfectants where used."

PIC/S Guide to GMP — Part I Chapter 3: Premises and Equipment

"Premises and equipment must be located, designed, constructed, adapted and maintained to suit the operations to be carried out. Their layout and design must aim to minimize the risk of errors and permit effective cleaning and maintenance in order to avoid cross-contamination, build-up of dust or dirt and, in general, any adverse effect on the quality of products."

EU GMP Part 1 Chapter 5: Production

"Cross-contamination should be prevented by attention to design of the premises and equipment. This should be supported by effective and reproducible cleaning processes to control risk of cross-contamination."

USP <1072> Disinfectants and Antiseptics

"A sound cleaning and sanitization program is needed for controlled environments used in the manufacture of Pharmacopeial articles to prevent the microbial contamination of these articles."

ISO 14698 — Cleanrooms and Associated Controlled Environments: Biocontamination Control

"This part of ISO 14698 establishes the principles and basic methodology of a formal system of biocontamination control for assessing and controlling biocontamination when cleanroom technology is applied... specifies the methods required for monitoring risk zones in a consistent way and for applying control measures appropriate to the degree of risk involved."

ISO 13408-1 — Aseptic Processing of Health Care Products

Establishes general requirements for the development, validation, and routine control of aseptic processing for health care products.

These documents are general in nature, providing limited detail on how cleaning and disinfection are executed but conveying that these programs must be in place. The responsibility of proving effectiveness of agents remains with the individual firms.

4.2 Regulatory Inspections

Regulatory inspections review the cleaning and disinfection program as part of the broader GMP assessment. Firms must demonstrate documented rationale for agent selection, validated procedures, and ongoing monitoring data.

法規解析與稽核準備

監管框架全景

TR70 所引用的法規構成一個層次分明的監管框架：

最重要的監管原則：責任在廠商

TR70 明確指出：「證明所用藥劑有效性的責任，在於個別製藥廠商。」

這意味著不能只說「我們用市售消毒劑，廠商說有效」。必須提供：

  • 選擇依據（書面 rationale）
  • 在實際潔淨室條件下的資格驗證數據
  • 針對設施分離株的有效性確認
  • 持續監測趨勢（EM 數據）

FDA 2004 無菌指南的核心要求

該指南要求廠商評估消毒劑的三個面向：

  1. Suitability（適用性）：藥劑是否適合該環境、基質與目標微生物？
  2. Efficacy（有效性）：是否有數據證明在 GMP 條件下有效？
  3. Limitations（侷限性）：哪些情況下不適用？對哪些微生物效果有限？

稽核時，這三項通常體現在消毒劑 Qualification Report 中。

EU GMP Annex 1（2022 修訂版）的演進

TR70 引用的是舊版 Annex 1，但 2022 年 Annex 1 大幅更新，對消毒計畫的要求更為具體，包括：

  • 明確要求殺孢子劑定期使用（Grade A/B）
  • 要求記錄消毒劑選擇依據
  • 要求評估環境分離株的敏感性

在應用 TR70 指引時，應同步對照最新版 Annex 1 要求。

CDMO 稽核準備清單

  • 消毒劑選擇書面依據 (written rationale)
  • 各潔淨等級區域適用的藥劑清單
  • 每種藥劑的內部資格驗證報告 (qualification report)
  • 接觸時間、濃度、使用方法的 SOP
  • 藥劑輪替計畫 (rotation schedule)
  • EM 趨勢分析 — 顯示消毒程序有效性
  • Change control 記錄（藥劑更換時）
  • 人員培訓記錄 (in-use testing 含操作人員)

Section Summary — TR70 Section 1: Foundations

Key Concepts

  • TR70 governs non-product-contact surface cleaning and disinfection in aseptic manufacturing facilities
  • Three agent levels: Sanitizer (3-log vegetative reduction) → Disinfectant (eliminates vegetative organisms) → Sporicide (eliminates vegetative organisms + spores)
  • Commercial label claims are insufficient for GMP — in-house qualification at GMP-representative bioburden levels is required
  • Contact time must be validated and maintained (wet surface = active agent)
  • Agent rotation prevents resistance development in environmental isolates

核心重點摘要

  • TR70 規範非產品接觸面的清潔與消毒，不涵蓋 CIP/COP（詳見 TR29、TR49）
  • 殺菌劑 / 消毒劑 / 殺孢子劑三層次，等級越高適用越嚴格的潔淨區（Grade A 必須使用殺孢子劑）
  • 市售標示有效性不等於 GMP 有效性 — 必須進行符合潔淨室實際條件的內部資格驗證
  • 主要法規依據：21 CFR 211、FDA 無菌指南 2004、EU GMP Annex 1、USP <1072>
  • CDMO 稽核成功關鍵：書面選擇依據 + 驗證數據 + 輪替計畫 + EM 趨勢證明

學習目標 Learning Objectives

  1. 能說明新供應商資格認定的程序要求，包含稽核（audit）重點、分析証書（Certificate of Analysis, CoA）審查，以及配方或包裝變更時再確認（requalification）的觸發條件。

  3. 能識別效力測試中使用的標準測試菌株，包含 Staphylococcus aureus、Pseudomonas aeruginosa 與 Bacillus subtilis 孢子，並解釋為何孢子形成菌（spore-formers）對殺孢劑（sporicide）驗證至關重要。
  4. 能說明對數降低標準（log reduction criteria）：消毒劑（disinfectant）≥3-log 降低，以及此標準在設施生物負荷（bioburden）水準下的科學依據。
  5. 能運用中和劑驗證（neutralizing agent validation）的邏輯，解釋中和劑確認試驗的兩個方向（中和能力 + 菌株生長兼容性），並說明 in-use expiration dating（使用中有效期）對稀釋藥劑測試的實務影響。

5.0 Qualification of New Suppliers and Agents

New suppliers and new antimicrobial chemical agents for use in the disinfection program should be qualified prior to use following established procedures. A satisfactory audit, qualification testing, and a clearly defined Certificate of Analysis (CoA) are important aspects to be considered as part of the qualification. If changes occur in the agent's formulation, packaging, or manufacturing site, an evaluation should be performed to determine if requalification is required.

When choosing a new antimicrobial chemical agent, the following supplier documentation should be evaluated:

  • Product literature and technical data
  • Material compatibility and storage conditions
  • Expiration dating and efficacy data
  • Material safety information and disposal requirements
  • Sterility and sterilization information (if the product is provided sterile)
  • Packaging presentations

Depending on the specific use and experience with the supplier, an audit may need to be performed. Key audit focus areas include:

  • Environmental control and cleaning of the manufacturing area and equipment
  • Control and disinfection/sterilization of packaging containers
  • Documentation of production processing activities
  • For aseptically filled agents: environmental monitoring (EM) program data, including alert and action levels, trending, corrective actions, and use of neutralizing agents in EM media
  • For sterile-labeled agents: sterility testing data and sterilization process qualification
  • Water systems and quality of water used in manufacturing
  • Package or container integrity studies
  • For double- and triple-bagged containers: disinfection of filled container and overwrap integrity; where sterility is claimed for inner bags, sterilization process qualification
  • Handling and storage of finished product containers
  • Change control: customer notification of ingredient, process, or packaging changes (e.g., wrapping, irradiation, sterilization)

供應商資格認定概述

所有新的抗菌化學藥劑（antimicrobial chemical agents）在正式導入消毒程式之前，必須依照既定程序完成資格認定。核心要素包含三項：稽核（audit）結果滿意、資格確認測試（qualification testing）通過，以及有明確定義的分析証書（Certificate of Analysis, CoA）。

觸發再確認的情境

若供應商發生以下任一變更，必須評估是否需要重新進行資格確認（requalification）：

  • 藥劑配方（formulation）變更
  • 包裝形式（packaging）變更
  • 製造地點（manufacturing site）變更

這對 CDMO 而言是 GMP 稽核的高頻發現點。供應商若未主動通知變更，而設施未建立變更通知機制，即構成合規缺失。

稽核重點：無菌填充藥劑

若採購的消毒劑標示為無菌（sterile），稽核時需特別確認：

  • 供應商的 EM 程式是否包含警示與行動水準（alert & action levels）
  • EM 培養基是否使用適當的中和劑（neutralizing agents），以避免抑制菌落生長
  • 滅菌製程確認文件（sterilization process qualification）是否完整
  • 多層包裝（double/triple bag）的完整性研究（integrity studies）是否到位

比喻：CoA 就像食品標示

Certificate of Analysis（CoA）就像食品的成分標示 — 它告訴你「裡面有什麼、濃度是多少」。但 CoA 只是供應商的自我宣稱；資格確認測試（qualification testing）才是你自己驗證它真的有效的步驟。兩者缺一不可。

5.1 Qualification Testing

Qualification testing of a new antimicrobial chemical agent should include both laboratory and in-situ testing. Chemical analysis of the actives and microbial efficacy testing should be performed. Chemical analysis may be provided by the vendor or performed in-house by a qualified contract laboratory using the vendor's method.

Microbial efficacy testing — whether in suspension or in carrier studies — should be performed in-house or by a qualified contract testing laboratory. Critically, the antimicrobial agents used for testing should be close to or beyond their stated in-use expiration date. This must account for both ready-to-use and use-dilution prepared from concentrate expiry. Testing should be done in replicate on multiple lots of the agent where applicable.

Information supporting qualification includes:

  • Description of packaging, label, and container type
  • Description of ingredients and concentrations
  • Lot or batch number
  • Efficacy testing results
  • Irradiation or other sterilization verification certification
  • Safety data sheet (SDS) information
  • Disposal information

5.2 Efficacy Testing — Overview

Demonstrating antimicrobial kill is a function of: concentration of microorganisms, type of microorganisms, choice of agent, concentration of agent, porosity or texture of the surface, method of application, and contact time.

The agent should be effective against the normal microbial vegetative flora recovered from the facility. AOAC (Association of Official Analytical Chemists) guidelines suggest high inoculum levels; however, inoculum levels for facility testing should not exceed 105.

The three categories of antimicrobial agents are:

  • Sanitizers — minimal reduction in 30 seconds to 10 minutes; effective against low levels of vegetative microorganisms. Example: 70% isopropanol (IPA).
  • Disinfectants — higher efficacy than sanitizers; kill vegetative microorganisms but not spore-forming organisms. Examples: quaternary ammonium compounds (QACs), phenolics.
  • Sporicides — up to total kill of bacterial spore-formers and mold. Examples: bleach (sodium hypochlorite), hydrogen peroxide (H2O2), mixtures of H2O2 and peracetic acid.

In general, contact times in qualification studies should not exceed 120 seconds for alcohols and 10 minutes for disinfectants and sporicides. Typically, only three agents total (one of each class) are qualified within a biopharmaceutical or pharmaceutical facility.

資格確認測試與效力測試概述

關鍵要求：使用接近或超過有效期的藥劑測試

測試所使用的藥劑必須接近或已超過其 in-use expiration date（使用中有效期）。這代表最壞情況（worst case）：

  • 若藥劑在快過期時仍能達到 log reduction 標準，正常使用中一定也能達到
  • 需同時考量「即用型（ready-to-use）」與「由濃縮液稀釋（use dilution）」兩種型態的有效期
  • 多批次（multiple lots）測試可確認不同批次間的一致性

三類抗菌藥劑的核心差異

殺菌劑（disinfectant）無法殺滅孢子形成菌（spore-formers），這是 GMP 消毒程式中必須同時搭配殺孢劑使用的核心理由。

比喻：三層防守

消毒程式就像足球的三線防守：Sanitizer 清理日常低量汙染（前鋒壓迫）；Disinfectant 消滅正常植生型菌（中場攔截）；Sporicide 對付最頑強的孢子（最後一道防線）。缺少任一層，都有突破的風險。

CDMO 實務提醒：只需三種藥劑

歷史上曾認為需要輪換多種消毒劑以防止抗性發展，但這已不再是主流觀點（見 TR70 Section 11.0）。典型無菌製藥設施只需各認定一種 sanitizer、disinfectant、sporicide 即可。此觀點可協助簡化 validation 策略。

5.2.1 In-Suspension Studies

In-suspension studies may be used to quickly screen various chemical agents to determine which may be the most effective. However, these studies should not be considered a replacement for carrier/coupon surface studies in determining antimicrobial agent performance on clean room surfaces. They may also demonstrate an agent's efficacy in destroying suspended organisms in solutions (tanks, holding vessels, bioreactors).

A panel of six to ten microorganisms, including bacteria, yeast, and mold, should be used. Environmental isolates from the facility are preferred; ATCC cultures or internationally recognized culture collection cultures are acceptable until facility isolates can be obtained.

Method (one acceptable approach):

  1. Prepare a fresh culture of each organism to a known CFU/mL concentration.
  2. Transfer a small volume into a sterile preparation of the chemical agent and mix. Suggested inoculum: 103 to 104 CFU.
  3. Allow the mixture to sit for the specified contact (wetted) time.
  4. Filter the entire solution and rinse three times with an appropriate neutralizing agent. Plate the filter to a suitable medium (e.g., Trypticase Soy Agar, TSA) and incubate.
  5. Prepare a positive control: replace chemical agent with saline; serial dilutions to allow 10–300 CFU per plate.
  6. Prepare a negative control: follow the method but add no inoculum. No CFUs should be recovered.
  7. Calculate log reduction against each organism based on positive control CFUs versus recovered CFUs. Assess against pre-established acceptance criteria.

Neutralization agent validation must confirm: (1) organisms can grow in the presence of the neutralizing agent; (2) the neutralizing agent effectively neutralizes the chemical agent.

懸浮液效力試驗

懸浮液試驗的定位

懸浮液試驗（in-suspension studies）是篩選工具，不是最終驗證。它的優點是快速、操作簡單；但限制是：液態環境不能代表真實潔淨室表面（clean room surfaces）的殺菌效果。完整的效力驗證必須包含載體表面試驗（carrier surface studies）。

測試菌株選擇邏輯

菌株選擇優先順序：

  1. 設施環境分離株（facility environmental isolates） — 最能代表真實挑戰
  2. ATCC 標準株 — 在設施分離株取得前的替代選項

選用的菌株應與被測試的藥劑類別相符：

  • Sanitizer → 一般植生型菌（如 Staphylococcus aureus ATCC 6538、Pseudomonas aeruginosa ATCC 9027）
  • Disinfectant → 多種植生型菌 + 酵母菌 + 黴菌
  • Sporicide → 必須包含孢子形成菌（如 Bacillus subtilis 孢子 ATCC 6633）

Log Reduction 計算

Log Reduction = log₁₀(N₀) - log₁₀(N)

N₀ = 陽性對照組 CFU（初始菌量）
N  = 暴露於藥劑後回收之 CFU

範例：
  N₀ = 10,000 CFU (10⁴)
  N  = 10 CFU
  Log Reduction = 4 - 1 = 3-log

消毒劑合格標準：≥ 3-log reduction

中和劑驗證：兩個方向都要確認

中和劑（neutralizing agent）扮演「停止計時」的角色，讓藥劑在測試指定接觸時間後立即失效。驗證需雙向確認：

  • 方向一：中和能力 — 確認中和劑能有效終止藥劑活性（加入菌株後，中和劑 + 藥劑 + 菌株三者混合下，菌株仍能生長）
  • 方向二：菌株兼容性 — 確認中和劑本身不會抑制測試菌株生長（中和劑 + 菌株 vs. 純菌株對照，回收 CFU 應相當）

若只做單方向驗證，風險是：中和劑本身就有抑菌作用，導致假性低計數，誤判藥劑效力優異。

5.2.2 Carrier Surface Studies

Carrier surface studies provide verification of the ability of the antimicrobial chemical agent to reduce microorganism levels on the types of material surfaces present within the facility.

A variety of surfaces commonly found in the facility should be selected, representing worst-case porosity or most difficult to clean. These may include: stainless steel, plastic, plastic bags, glass, vinyl curtains, polycarbonates, terrazzo, epoxy, vinyl, laminate flooring, painted epoxy and polysubstrate wall materials. Surface selection should be based on criticality and risk of harboring contamination impacting the final product. Carrier size should not exceed 1.5 in × 1.5 in (38 mm × 38 mm). Three or more replicates are recommended due to test method variability.

As with in-suspension studies, a panel of six to ten microorganisms (bacteria, yeast, mold) should be used. Environmental isolates are preferred.

Method 1 — Total Kill Method:

  1. Prepare a fresh culture of each organism to a known CFU/mL.
  2. Transfer a sufficient number of microorganisms (103–105) onto the carrier surface.
  3. Allow the inoculum to thoroughly air dry. Apply the antimicrobial agent by spraying, wiping, or submerging the carrier. Allow contact for a defined time (e.g., 5–10 minutes).
  4. Submerge the carrier in a vessel containing neutralizing agent and appropriate growth medium (e.g., Trypticase Soy Broth, TSB).
  5. After a set time, transfer the carrier to a second vessel containing the same growth medium without neutralizing agent.
  6. Incubate both vessels at appropriate temperature for a suitable time.
  7. No growth in both vessels is required to demonstrate the required log reduction.
  8. Positive control: carriers submerged in saline and gently sonicated or scrubbed; serial dilutions plated to allow 10–100 CFU per plate using the same media type.
  9. Negative control: sterile carrier used; no CFUs expected.
  10. Calculate log reduction using CFUs from positive controls versus test results.

Method 2 — Enumeration Method:

  1. After exposure to the antimicrobial agent, place the carrier in a vessel containing neutralizing solution and gently sonicate to remove organisms.
  2. Filter the entire solution; plate the filter to TSA and incubate to assess survival level.

Alternatively, serial dilution using saline, followed by filtration and plating, or a pour/spread plate method may be used.

載體表面效力試驗

為何載體試驗比懸浮液試驗更重要

真實潔淨室的消毒是對「乾燥後殘留在表面的菌」進行作用，而非對液態懸浮菌。乾燥（air dry）的菌體對藥劑更具抵抗力，這就是為何 carrier surface studies 才是效力確認的黃金標準。

關鍵差異：載體試驗中，菌株先被乾燥（air dry）在材料表面，模擬真實污染情境，然後再噴灑或擦拭藥劑。

表面選擇的風險思維

選擇最難清潔（worst-case）的表面，通常是質地最粗糙或多孔的材料。不鏽鋼雖然相對容易清潔，但因為與製造操作接觸最密切（最高臨界性 criticality），仍必須納入測試。

CDMO 常見表面清單：

  • 不鏽鋼（填充機台、架台）
  • 塑膠袋（物料傳遞袋）
  • 玻璃（隔離器視窗）
  • 環氧地坪（epoxy floor）
  • 乙烯簾幕（vinyl curtains）

全滅法（Total Kill）vs. 計數法（Enumeration）

Total Kill Method 使用兩個培養瓶的邏輯：第一瓶含中和劑（停止藥劑作用後立即轉移菌）；第二瓶不含中和劑（確認載體本身無殘留活性藥劑干擾）。兩瓶均需無菌生長才算合格。

比喻：超音波浴的用途

計數法中的「超音波震盪（sonication）」就像用超音波牙刷清除縫隙中的汙垢 — 目的是將乾燥黏附在表面的菌完整轉移到液體中，讓計數結果準確，而不是讓菌停留在表面被低估。

GMP 稽核視角：CDMO 文件包（TWi 適用）

GMP 稽核員在查核消毒藥劑資格認定時，會要求看到以下文件：

  • 供應商稽核報告（supplier audit report）或問卷結果
  • 每批次 CoA 審查紀錄
  • In-suspension study 報告（含測試菌株清單、接受標準、實際結果）
  • Carrier surface study 報告（含表面清單、three replicates 結果）
  • 中和劑驗證報告（neutralizing agent validation）
  • In-use expiration dating 依據（接近或超過有效期的測試紀錄）
  • 測試批次號與生產批次號的追蹤紀錄

任何一項缺失，在 FDA 483 或 EMA 查廠中都可能成為觀察項（observation）。

PDA TR70 Section 5 — Supplier Qualification & Efficacy Testing

Key Takeaways

  • Supplier qualification is a prerequisite, not optional. A CoA alone is insufficient — audit findings, qualification testing, and change control mechanisms must all be in place before a new agent is introduced to the disinfection program.
  • Requalification triggers are change-driven. Formulation, packaging, or manufacturing site changes all require evaluation for requalification.
  • In-suspension studies are a screening tool only. They provide rapid comparative data but cannot replace carrier surface studies as the definitive efficacy confirmation method.
  • Sporicide validation requires spore-forming test organisms. Bacillus subtilis spores (ATCC 6633) are the critical test organism — disinfectants do not kill spores, so this distinction is non-negotiable.
  • Log reduction criterion for disinfectants: ≥ 3-log. Testing must use agents close to or beyond in-use expiration date (worst-case scenario), in replicate, across multiple lots.
  • Neutralizing agent validation is bidirectional: confirm (1) it neutralizes the chemical agent, and (2) it does not inhibit organism growth.
  • Three agents is the standard qualification target: one sanitizer, one disinfectant, one sporicide for a typical pharmaceutical facility.

核心重點整理

  • 供應商資格認定是強制前置步驟。光有 CoA 不夠 — 稽核結果、資格確認測試、變更控制機制三者缺一不可，才能導入新藥劑。
  • 再確認由變更觸發。配方、包裝或製造地點任一變更，都需評估是否需要 requalification。
  • 懸浮液試驗是篩選工具，不是最終驗證。提供快速比較數據，但無法取代載體表面試驗作為效力確認的最終依據。
  • 殺孢劑驗證必須包含孢子形成菌。Bacillus subtilis 孢子（ATCC 6633）是關鍵測試菌株 — 殺菌劑無法殺孢子，此區別在 GMP 上不可妥協。
  • 消毒劑合格標準：≥ 3-log reduction。測試必須使用接近或超過 in-use expiration date 的藥劑（最壞情境），多重複、多批次。
  • 中和劑驗證需雙向確認：（1）能有效中和藥劑；（2）不會抑制測試菌株生長。
  • 標準配置為三種藥劑：各一種 sanitizer、disinfectant、sporicide 即滿足典型製藥設施需求。

6.0 In-Use Expiration Dating（使用中有效期標示）

6.0 In-Use Expiration Dating

Sanitizers, disinfectants, and sporicides should be assessed to ensure their performance throughout their assigned in-use period. They should be stored for use no longer than the predefined period as specified by written procedures.

The expiration dating provided by the manufacturer relates to the expiration of a closed or "primary" container. Once the container (ready-to-use or concentrate) has been opened, the manufacturer's expiration date is no longer valid for active ingredient potency and sterility.

The two important points surrounding in-use expiration are:

  • Antimicrobial effectiveness: The length of time the solution retains its ability to destroy microorganisms, as evaluated during efficacy testing.
  • Container bioburden: For controlled areas, how long the container and its contents maintain an appropriate bioburden level.

This is determined by performing bioburden testing on samples taken from containers (spray bottles, squeeze bottles, etc.) used in the disinfection process at the end of their in-use period.

Key Regulatory Implication

Failure to label prepared disinfectant containers with preparation date and in-use expiry is a common FDA 483 observation. Written SOPs must define the maximum in-use period for each type of container and agent.

6.0 使用中有效期標示

消毒劑（Sanitizer）、殺菌劑（Disinfectant）與殺孢子劑（Sporicide）在使用期間必須持續評估其效能，且不得超過書面程序所規定的預設使用期限。

製造商提供的有效期限，僅適用於未開封的原裝容器（Primary Container）。一旦容器被開封（無論是即用型還是濃縮液），原有效期即不再適用於活性成分效力與無菌性保證。

使用中有效期（In-Use Expiration Dating）的兩個核心考量：

  • 消毒效力保持期：溶液在多長時間內仍能達到效力測試所驗證的殺滅能力。
  • 容器生物負荷控制：在受控區域中，容器及其內容物能在多長時間內維持適當的微生物負荷水準。

判定方法：在使用期末，對噴霧瓶、擠壓瓶等實際使用中的容器取樣，進行生物負荷測試（Bioburden Testing）。

CDMO 稽核實務提醒

未在配製好的消毒劑容器上標示配製日期與使用期限，是 FDA 483 最常見的觀察項目之一。書面 SOP 必須明確規定每種容器與藥劑的最大使用期限。TWi 所有配製消毒劑容器均須依此標示。

7.0 Control of the Environment（環境管控）

7.0 Overview — Contamination Control Framework

An effective cleaning and disinfection system starts by limiting the introduction of contamination into the facility. Stopping both viable and nonviable contamination from entering controlled areas is critical to achieving the desired environmental conditions.

On the scale of importance, the control over the introduction of contamination is the most critical concern in the entire cleaning and disinfection process. This begins with the cleanliness of all items transferred into the facility — components, personnel, carts, tanks, tools, and instruments.

A comprehensive list of every item entering the controlled area should be constructed, and each item evaluated to determine whether it can be effectively cleaned and disinfected (or sterilized). Items that cannot be appropriately cleaned must be replaced.

Three high-risk time periods when contamination entry can impact operations:

  1. After cleaning/disinfection, before manufacturing begins: Personnel and components enter the area and potentially shed particulates and microbes during setup.
  2. During manufacturing interventions: Inappropriate clean room behavior and poor aseptic technique can contaminate disinfected surfaces.
  3. After manufacturing, before cleaning: Personnel must maintain aseptic awareness to preserve environmental cleanliness.

Control must also address proliferation of viable contaminants already present — molds can penetrate surfaces and equipment attachment points, making elimination difficult. Gram-negative organisms can generate endotoxins from product-contact surfaces if not cleaned appropriately.

7.0 概述 — 污染管控架構

有效的清潔消毒系統，始於限制污染物進入設施。阻止有生命（viable）與非生命（nonviable）污染物進入受控區域，是達到預期環境條件的關鍵。

在整個清潔消毒流程中，污染物進入的管控是最重要的環節。這從進入設施的每一項物品的清潔度開始：物料、人員、推車、槽桶、工具、儀器，全部納入管控。

應建立進入受控區域的所有物品清單，逐一評估是否能被有效清潔、消毒或滅菌。無法適當清潔的物品，必須以可清潔的替代品取代。

三個污染風險最高的時間節點：

  1. 清潔消毒完成後、生產開始前：人員與物料進入區域時，可能在設置期間散落微粒與微生物。
  2. 生產干預期間：不當的潔淨室行為與差劣的無菌操作技術，可能污染已消毒表面。
  3. 生產結束後、清潔前：人員仍需保持無菌意識，防止環境受到污染。

管控還必須涵蓋對已存在污染物的增殖進行防制。黴菌可滲入設備接縫等難以觸及處，造成清除困難。革蘭陰性菌若未適當清潔，可在產品接觸表面產生內毒素（Endotoxins）。

7.1 Introduction of Clean Room Manufacturing Supplies

The design of the facility and procedures in place must assure prevention of contamination from the flow of components, drug products, containers, closures, labeling, and in-process materials through the building.

"Materials should be disinfected according to appropriate procedures or, when used in critical areas, rendered sterile by a suitable method." — U.S. FDA Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing (CGMPs)

Items entering the Aseptic Processing Area (APA) should be exposed to the highest level of decontamination the material can withstand. If an item can be physically sterilized, it must be.

Sterilization methods for items entering APA:

  • Autoclaving — most widely used; multiple outer wraps allow progressive de-layering as items move to cleaner grades.
  • Gamma irradiation — multiple wraps/barriers used, removed stepwise when passing to more stringent grades.
  • VPHP (Vaporized Hydrogen Peroxide) decontamination chambers — for items that cannot tolerate heat or radiation.
  • Manual sporicidal disinfection — spray or wipe with sporicidal agent as the minimum acceptable method for non-sterilizable items.
  • UV pass-through systems — can reduce bioburden but are NOT considered sterilization.

If an item cannot be disinfected before entering the clean room, it must not be introduced and a suitable alternative must be found. Personnel should not carry items through the gowning room.

Common clean room supplies requiring introduction procedures: mechanic's tools, carts, production supplies, handling implements, EM supplies, wipers, antimicrobial agents, filling equipment components, markers, pens, electronic equipment (meters, particle counters).

7.1 潔淨室製造耗材的引入管控

設施設計與程序必須確保，物料、藥品、容器、封蓋、標籤及中間品在廠房流轉過程中不引入污染。

「物料應依照適當程序消毒，或在關鍵區域使用時以適合的方法滅菌。」— 美國 FDA 工業指南：無菌加工藥品生產（cGMP）

進入無菌加工區（APA, Aseptic Processing Area）的物品，應接受該材質所能承受的最高等級去污處理。若物品能被實體滅菌，則必須滅菌。

進入 APA 物品的滅菌方法：

  • 高壓滅菌（Autoclaving）：最廣泛使用；多層外包裝可在物品進入更嚴格等級時逐層移除。
  • Gamma 輻射滅菌：多層包裝/屏障，逐步移除以進入更高潔淨等級。
  • VPHP（汽化過氧化氫）去污室：適用於無法承受高溫或輻射的物品。
  • 人工殺孢子消毒：以殺孢子劑噴灑或擦拭，為無法滅菌物品的最低可接受方式。
  • 紫外線（UV）穿越系統：可降低生物負荷，但不視為滅菌方法。

若物品進入潔淨室前無法被消毒，則不得引入，必須尋找合適替代品。人員不得攜帶物品通過更衣室。

常見需執行引入程序的潔淨室耗材：維修工具、推車、生產耗材、操作器具、EM 耗材、抹布、消毒藥劑、充填設備組件、筆記用具、電子設備（量測儀、粒子計數器）。

7.1.2 Introduction of Tanks, Vessels, Carts, and Equipment into the APA

The transfer of equipment into an aseptic system can introduce contamination and must be addressed accordingly. Ideally via autoclave or VPHP chamber, but commonly performed manually.

Special attention to cart wheels:

  • Wheels are high-risk — they contact floors outside the APA and carry significant contamination.
  • Manual process: First wipe with a particulate-free, non-shedding wipe to clean the wheel surface, then apply disinfectant to assure validated contact (dry) time.
  • VPHP chamber process: Wipe wheels with cleaning agent, then spray with antimicrobial agent before entering the chamber.

Why Wheels Matter

Cart wheels roll across non-classified corridors before entering the APA. Each rotation deposits floor contamination that is then carried into the clean room. Overlooking wheel disinfection is a frequent root cause identified in environmental excursion investigations.

7.1.2 槽桶、容器、推車與設備的引入管控

設備移入無菌加工系統的過程可能引入污染，必須妥善處理。理想方式為高壓滅菌或 VPHP 腔室處理，但實際上多以人工方式執行。

特別注意：推車輪子

  • 輪子是高風險部位，在進入 APA 前滾過非潔淨走廊，攜帶大量污染物。
  • 人工程序：先以無微粒、不脫落的抹布擦拭輪面清潔，再施用消毒劑確保達到驗證的接觸（乾燥）時間。
  • VPHP 腔室程序：先用清潔劑擦拭輪子，再噴灑抗微生物藥劑後進入腔室。

為何輪子格外重要

推車輪子在進入 APA 前，已滾過非潔淨等級走廊。每一圈轉動都在沉積地板污染物，隨後帶入潔淨室。忽視輪子消毒，是環境超標調查中頻繁發現的根本原因之一。

7.1.3 Introduction of Cleaning Supplies and Equipment into the APA

Cleaning supplies and equipment represent potential bioburden sources. Prior sterilization or disinfection is standard practice.

For Grade A (ISO 5) and adjacent Grade B (ISO 5/6) areas: Cleaning equipment (buckets, mops, mop handles, mop heads, sprayers, wipes, extensions) must be thoroughly cleaned and rendered sterile prior to use. Methods: steam sterilization or sterile single-use disposable systems.

Sterility of disinfectant solutions: Sanitizers, disinfectants, and sporicides can harbor resistant microorganisms. For Grade A/B (ISO 5/6) areas, solutions must be sterile-filtered or sterilized before use. Approaches:

  • Aseptic filtration at 0.2 µm of final-use dilution into pre-sterilized containers in the Grade A/B area.
  • Mixing with sterile unit-dose containers certified as sterile, using sterile WFI.
  • Purchasing ready-to-use or ready-to-mix sterile solutions from a vendor with appropriate sterility documentation.
  • Autoclaving the mixed solution (if formulation permits).

Container type matters: Aspirating containers (squeeze bottles, trigger sprayers, bulk containers opened and closed) introduce environmental contamination into the solution — 70% IPA bottles that aspirate have been found to harbor mold. Non-aspirating containers prevent contamination ingress and may be used until their validated expiration period.

Grade cascade rule: A solution used in a Grade A area may subsequently be used in Grade B, then Grade C, in that descending order. A solution used in Grade A cannot return to Grade A use unless kept under positive pressure. Discard open bucket contents after each cleaning operation.

7.1.3 清潔耗材與設備的引入管控

清潔耗材與設備本身即為潛在生物負荷來源，使用前滅菌或消毒應視為標準作業。

Grade A（ISO 5）與鄰近 Grade B（ISO 5/6）區域：桶子、拖把、拖把桿、拖把頭、噴霧器、抹布、延伸桿等清潔設備，使用前必須徹底清潔並滅菌。方法：蒸汽滅菌或無菌一次性拋棄式系統。

消毒溶液的無菌性要求：消毒劑本身可能含有耐藥微生物。用於 Grade A/B 區域的溶液，必須在使用前0.2 µm 過濾滅菌或滅菌處理。方法包括：

  • 在 Grade A/B 區域外，將最終使用濃度進行 0.2 µm 無菌過濾，注入置於 Grade A/B 區域的預滅菌容器。
  • 使用已驗證無菌的單劑量容器，以無菌注射用水（WFI）配製。
  • 向供應商採購有無菌性文件佐證的即用型或即配型無菌溶液。
  • 若配方允許，高壓滅菌已配製好的溶液。

容器類型的選擇：會吸入空氣的容器（擠壓瓶、扳機噴霧器、反覆開蓋的大容器）會將環境污染帶入溶液中——已有 70% IPA 瓶子因吸氣而含有黴菌的案例。不吸氣容器（Non-Aspirating Containers）可避免此問題，可使用至驗證的有效期限。

潔淨等級遞降使用原則：Grade A 使用後的溶液可降至 Grade B、再降至 Grade C 使用，但不得反向回到 Grade A，除非容器持續保持正壓防止污染回流。開放式桶系統的溶液，每次清潔操作完畢後應丟棄。

7.1.4 Introduction of Components into the APA

"Components" refers to items used directly in the manufacturing process — stoppers, plungers, vials, cartridges, and similar product-contact items.

Depyrogenation and sterilization are required for components that come into direct contact with the sterile product. A validated sterilization process must be used, and sterility must be maintained after entry into the APA through integration into the final product.

Methods: dry heat (for depyrogenation and sterilization) or validated washing and sterilization processes. For components sterilized outside the APA, multiple outer wraps, layers, or barriers allow appropriate disinfection before entering the Grade A (ISO 5) environment.

Refer to PDA Technical Report No. 3 (Revised 2013): Validation of Dry Heat Processes Used for Depyrogenation and Sterilization for additional guidance.

7.1.4 物料組件（Components）的引入管控

「組件（Components）」指直接用於生產製程的物品，包括膠塞（Stoppers）、柱塞（Plungers）、西林瓶（Vials）、卡匣（Cartridges）等產品接觸物品。

與無菌產品直接接觸的組件，須完成去熱原（Depyrogenation）與滅菌（Sterilization）。必須使用經過驗證的滅菌製程，且在進入 APA 後直至整合入最終產品的整個過程，均須維持無菌性。

方法：乾熱法（同時完成去熱原與滅菌）或驗證過的清洗滅菌程序。在 APA 外完成滅菌的組件，應使用多層外包裝，以便在進入 Grade A（ISO 5）環境前，一層一層消毒後移除。

詳細指引參閱：PDA Technical Report No. 3（2013 修訂版）：Validation of Dry Heat Processes Used for Depyrogenation and Sterilization。

7.2 Environmental Monitoring Data Analysis

Environmental monitoring (EM) data demonstrates the effectiveness of the microbial contamination control system, including the cleaning and disinfection program. Analysis considers:

  • Genus and species of organisms recovered
  • Number of isolates and their distribution within the facility
  • Trending history — comparison against historical performance

Normal pattern: When a facility is in control, data follows a predictable profile. Most common isolates are from personnel; fewer from air, soil, water, or liquid sources.

Analysis frequency: Formal documented trend analysis should be performed periodically. Evaluation of cleaning/disinfection program effectiveness and alert/action level adequacy should be performed at least annually. Results should be reported to site management and reviewed/documented by quality management.

Adverse trends and excursions: Any deviation from normal conditions triggers a signal. Actions are proportional to risk:

  • Alert level reached or atypical organism: heightened awareness notification.
  • Recurring action level exceedances: special cleaning/disinfection of the area.
  • Multiple sites or multiple occasions: full investigation.

Organism-specific responses: Vegetative organisms above action levels may require increased disinfection. Detection of spore-forming organisms above action level signals immediate response with sporicidal agents.

7.2 環境監測數據分析

環境監測（EM）數據用來證明微生物污染控制系統（包括清潔消毒程序）的有效性。分析重點包括：

  • 回收微生物的屬種（Genus & Species）鑑定
  • 分離株數量及其在設施內的分布
  • 趨勢追蹤——與歷史數據比較

正常狀態特徵：設施處於管控狀態時，數據呈現可預測的分布型態。最常見的分離株來自人員；來自空氣、土壤、水或液體來源的較少。

分析頻率：應定期進行正式、文件化的趨勢分析。清潔消毒程序有效性及警戒/行動限界適用性的評估，應至少每年執行一次。分析結果應呈報場址管理層，並由品質管理人員審查與文件化。

異常趨勢與偏差（Excursion）：任何偏離正常狀態的情況均需採取行動，行動力度與風險相稱：

  • 達到警戒限（Alert Level）或出現非典型菌：提高警覺通知。
  • 多次超過行動限（Action Level）：對該區域進行特別清潔消毒。
  • 多個監測點或多次發生：展開完整調查。

菌種特異性回應：超過行動限的營養細胞，可能需要加強消毒。偵測到超過行動限的孢子形成菌，則需立即以殺孢子劑進行處理。

7.3 Attaining and Selecting Environmental Isolates

Recoveries of microorganisms from environmental monitoring samples should be identified to genus and species level when exceeding alert or action levels, and periodically even when limits are not exceeded.

Organism identifications should be evaluated to determine the most frequently occurring organisms. Representative organisms should be preserved and included in the panel of organisms used in efficacy testing of antimicrobial chemical agents used in the facility.

8.0 In-Situ Field Studies（現場實地研究）

8.0 In-Situ Field Studies — Overview

The true test of the effectiveness of a cleaning and disinfection program is the monitoring data collected from the manufacturing area. Evaluation of in-situ data from a robust environmental monitoring program will verify that the program is capable of attaining and maintaining a level of cleanliness that minimizes the probability of contamination.

In-situ data types include:

  • Nonviable (total particulate) data
  • Viable data for surfaces and ambient air
  • Personnel monitoring data
  • Microbial identification of representative environmental isolates
  • Residual testing of surfaces
  • Product quality data (in-process bioburden and sterility testing)

Two approaches to conducting in-situ monitoring are commonly used:

  1. Environmental monitoring before and after start-up of a facility or area (Section 8.1)
  2. Environmental monitoring before and after cleaning during routine operation (Section 8.2)

Laboratory vs. Real-World Performance

Coupon studies in the lab use controlled inoculums on polished stainless steel — clean, flat, and reproducible. Real clean rooms have epoxy floors, acrylic windows, irregular equipment surfaces, and personnel interactions. In-situ studies bridge this gap, confirming that the program works under actual operating conditions with real surfaces and real operators.

8.0 現場實地研究 — 概述

清潔消毒程序有效性的真正考驗，是從生產區域收集的監測數據。評估來自健全環境監測計劃的現場（In-Situ）數據，能驗證程序是否能達到並維持所需的清潔度，將污染風險降至最低。

In-situ 數據類型包含：

  • 非活性微粒（Non-viable Particulate）數據
  • 表面與環境空氣的活菌（Viable）數據
  • 人員監測數據
  • 代表性環境分離株的微生物鑑定結果
  • 表面殘留測試（Residual Testing）
  • 產品品質數據（中間品生物負荷與無菌測試）

現場監測常用兩種方法：

  1. 設施或區域啟用前後的環境監測（第 8.1 節）
  2. 例行生產期間清潔前後的環境監測（第 8.2 節）

實驗室 vs. 真實生產環境

實驗室的測試片（Coupon）研究使用受控菌液、拋光不鏽鋼材質——表面光滑、條件穩定、結果可重現。但真實潔淨室有環氧樹脂地板、壓克力窗、不規則設備表面，還有人員干預。In-situ 研究彌合這個落差，確認程序在真實作業條件、真實表面材質、真實操作人員下同樣有效。這是清潔驗證資料包的核心支撐。

8.1 Environmental Monitoring Before and After Start-up of a Facility or Area

Applicable scenarios include: opening a new area, area shutdown due to adverse events, areas that have undergone significant modifications, or areas left idle for a significant period.

Facilities should have special start-up cleaning and disinfection programs for shutdowns or significant construction activities.

Standard approach — step-by-step EM after each stage:

  1. Initial cleaning: Remove gross soil (broom, vacuum) — e.g., after construction, to prepare the area for formal cleaning.
  2. Baseline monitoring: Increased viable monitoring of air and surfaces. Nonviable air monitoring. This establishes the pre-cleaning baseline for comparison.
  3. Cleaning and disinfection: Execute the full cleaning and disinfection program.
  4. Post-cleaning monitoring: After surfaces are dry, repeat increased viable surface monitoring and nonviable air monitoring.

Analysis: Compare before/after data. A robust program should demonstrate reduction of viable and nonviable counts and minimization of spore-forming or mold contaminants. If results do not demonstrate acceptable reduction, the program must be reviewed and modified.

8.1 設施或區域啟用前後的環境監測

適用情境：新區域開啟、因異常事件關閉的區域、已進行重大改造的區域，或閒置較長時間的區域。

針對停工或重大施工活動，設施應備有專屬的啟動清潔消毒程序（Start-up C&D Program）。

標準方法——每個階段後進行 EM：

  1. 初步清潔：移除粗大污垢（掃帚、吸塵器）——例如施工完成後，為後續正式清潔程序做準備。
  2. 基線監測：加強空氣與表面的活菌監測；同時進行非活性微粒監測。此步驟建立清潔前基線數據作為比較基準。
  3. 執行清潔消毒：執行完整的清潔消毒程序。
  4. 清潔後監測：表面乾燥後，重複加強的表面活菌監測與非活性微粒監測。

數據分析：比對清潔前後數據。健全的清潔消毒程序應能證明活菌與非活性微粒數量顯著下降，並最小化孢子形成菌或黴菌的存在。若結果未達可接受的降低水準，必須檢討並修改程序。

8.2 Environmental Monitoring Before and After Cleaning During Routine Operation

In this approach, a facility in routine manufacturing operation evaluates its ongoing cleaning and disinfection program effectiveness:

  1. Increased viable surface and air monitoring is performed after operations have occurred and just before cleaning and disinfection take place. Nonviable air monitoring is also performed.
  2. Cleaning and disinfection are performed.
  3. Increased monitoring is performed again after cleaning and disinfection are complete.

The data gathered before and after is analyzed. Expectations: reduction of viable and nonviable counts, minimization of spore-forming or mold contaminants. If results are unsatisfactory, the program must be reviewed and modified.

Additional guidance: PDA Technical Report No. 13 (Revised 2014): Fundamentals of an Environmental Monitoring Program.

CDMO Application — Cleaning Validation Package

In-situ studies (8.1 and 8.2) generate the critical field data that supports the cleaning validation package. Regulators expect documented before/after EM data that demonstrates cleaning effectiveness under actual production conditions, not just laboratory coupon studies.

8.2 例行生產期間清潔前後的環境監測

此方法針對正常生產運行中的設施，用於評估清潔消毒程序的持續有效性：

  1. 在生產操作完成後、清潔消毒執行前，加強表面與空氣的活菌監測，並進行非活性微粒監測。
  2. 執行清潔消毒程序。
  3. 清潔消毒完成後，再次進行加強監測。

分析清潔前後的數據。預期：活菌與非活性微粒數量下降，孢子形成菌或黴菌最小化。若結果未達要求，必須檢討並修改程序。

延伸參考：PDA Technical Report No. 13（2014 修訂版）：Fundamentals of an Environmental Monitoring Program。

CDMO 應用 — 清潔驗證資料包

8.1 與 8.2 的 in-situ 研究，產生支撐清潔驗證資料包（Cleaning Validation Package）所需的現場關鍵數據。法規機關期望看到文件化的清潔前後 EM 數據，以證明清潔程序在真實生產條件下的有效性，而不僅是實驗室測試片研究結果。

Key Takeaways (English)

  • In-Use Expiration Dating (Sec. 6.0): Once a disinfectant container is opened, the manufacturer's expiration date is void. In-use periods must be defined, and containers labeled with preparation date and expiry — a common 483 observation.
  • Environmental Control (Sec. 7.0): Contamination prevention begins before cleaning starts — at the point of item introduction. Every item entering the APA requires a documented decontamination procedure. The highest achievable level of decontamination is required.
  • Disinfectant solution sterility: Sanitizers and disinfectants for Grade A/B must be sterile-filtered (0.2 µm) or otherwise sterilized before use. Non-aspirating containers prevent contamination ingress and are preferred.
  • EM Data Analysis (Sec. 7.2–7.3): EM data must be trended periodically. Environmental isolates should be identified and used to update efficacy test panels, closing the loop between monitoring and disinfection qualification.
  • In-Situ Field Studies (Sec. 8.0): Before/after EM around cleaning events provides the field-level evidence that laboratory efficacy data alone cannot supply. Both start-up scenarios and routine operations must be covered. This data is central to cleaning validation packages.

9.0 Cleaning and Disinfection — Why Cleaning Comes First

Cleaning is a critical step in the cleaning and disinfection process because the buildup of antimicrobial chemical agent residues, product residues, particulates, and other contaminants can inhibit an antimicrobial chemical agent's efficacy.

By lessening the level of particulates, microbes, and residues on the surface, cleaning prepares the surfaces for disinfection because:

  • There are fewer organisms to destroy, as most have been removed from the area.
  • Obstructions blocking the chemical agent from contacting the organism are minimized.
  • Chemical interference that would reduce the stability and effectiveness of the active agents is removed.
  • Residual that can interfere with future disinfection, or dry and flake off into the environment, is lessened.

The key to disinfection in the clean room is keeping the surface wetted for a sufficient period of time for the chemical to accomplish its mode of action. Drying time is a variable that must be carefully evaluated — especially in areas where unidirectional airflow (UDAF) is present, as air movement tends to dry surfaces quickly.

Cleaning operations should be performed routinely, with frequency based on area classification, usage, risk, and visible cleanliness.

為何清潔必須在消毒前進行？

清潔（Cleaning）並非消毒（Disinfection）的替代步驟，而是前置必要條件。殘留的有機物（產品殘跡、微粒）會：

  • 保護微生物免受消毒劑接觸
  • 與消毒劑的活性成分發生化學干擾，降低殺菌效力
  • 乾燥後剝落，重新釋放至環境中

關鍵操作要點：接觸時間（Contact Time / Wet Time）

消毒劑需在表面保持濕潤狀態，才能讓活性成分滲透微生物細胞壁。Grade A/B 區域採用單向氣流（Unidirectional Airflow, UDAF），氣流速度快，表面乾燥極迅速——SOP 必須明確規定接觸時間，並考慮氣流對蒸發速率的影響。

清潔頻率的決定因素

  • 區域等級——等級越高，頻率越高
  • 使用強度——批次數量、換班次數
  • 風險評估——產品暴露程度
  • 目視清潔狀態——可見污染立即觸發清潔
A/B

Grade A / B Areas — Highest Rigor

Filling zones · LAF workstations · Background environments · Sterile tools and wipers

Grade A Areas — Most Critical Zones

Grade A is the highest classification, representing the local zone for high-risk operations such as filling, stoppering, and open product transfers. Grade A is typically provided by a Laminar Air Flow (LAF) unit or RABS/isolator unidirectional airflow workstation.

Cleaning requirements for Grade A:

  • Only sterile wipers (pre-wetted or dry) are used — non-woven, low-lint materials validated for use in the zone.
  • Cleaning tools (mop handles, buckets, squeegees) must be sterilized before entry into the Grade A/B suite.
  • Sporicide (e.g., hydrogen peroxide vapor, peracetic acid, chlorine dioxide) is routinely employed as the primary antimicrobial chemical agent. Sporicides eliminate bacterial and fungal spores, which neither sanitizers nor standard disinfectants achieve.
  • No floor mop is used directly within the Grade A zone itself; floor areas under LAF are typically wiped or cleaned by automated VHP/HPV cycles.
  • All cleaning actions follow unidirectional, overlapping stroke technique — clean to less-clean direction (top to bottom, inside to outside) to avoid spreading contamination.
  • Personnel performing Grade A cleaning must wear appropriate gowning (sterile gown, double gloves, face shield) and be qualified to work in the aseptic core.

Grade B Areas — Background to Grade A

Grade B is the background environment surrounding the Grade A zone during aseptic operations. Requirements are nearly as stringent as Grade A:

  • Sporicide used periodically (e.g., weekly or per campaign), with disinfectant rotation applied between sporicide applications.
  • Sterile or sanitized wipers required; low-lint materials.
  • Floors: mop system permitted, but mop heads must be dedicated to Grade B and either sterilized or replaced between uses.
  • Environmental monitoring (EM) frequency is highest — active air sampling every shift, settle plates during operations, surface contact plates after critical activities.
  • SOP must specify agent identity, concentration, contact time, wiper type, and cleaning sequence for each surface type.

Grade A / B 區域清潔要求

Grade A — 無菌製程核心區

Grade A 即 EU Annex 1 最高等級，對應 ISO Class 5。典型場所：灌裝工作站（Filling Zone）、層流罩（LAF, Laminar Air Flow）、RABS 或隔離器（Isolator）內部。

Grade A 清潔六大要件：

  1. 無菌抹布（Sterile Wipers）——必須使用已滅菌的低落絮（Low-lint）不織布抹布，不得使用一般紙巾或棉布
  2. 工具滅菌入場——所有清潔器具（拖把桿、水桶、刮水器）使用前必須完成滅菌，並記錄
  3. 孢子殺菌劑（Sporicide）——如汽化過氧化氫（VHP）、過氧乙酸（Peracetic Acid, PAA）、二氧化氯（ClO₂）。一般消毒劑無法殺滅芽孢，Grade A 必須定期使用孢子殺菌劑
  4. 不使用拖把直接拖地——LAF 下方地板通常以 VHP 氣化循環或人工無菌擦拭處理
  5. 單向重疊擦拭技術——由內向外、由上向下，每次擦拭重疊 30%，避免交叉污染
  6. 人員資格——執行 Grade A 清潔的人員需完成無菌區域更衣培訓與媒體充填（Media Fill）資格確認

比喻：外科手術刷手規程

Grade A 清潔就像外科刷手——每一個步驟、每一個方向都有嚴格規範，因為任何疏失都直接影響病患安全（產品無菌性）。「差不多」不存在於這個等級。

CDMO 實務：TWi 無菌灌裝廠

TWi 的 Grade A/B 區域（無菌灌裝線）屬最高風險等級，清潔 SOP 必須明確載明：藥劑名稱、有效濃度、接觸時間、抹布類型與批號、清潔順序及操作人員資格。稽核常見缺失之一：使用的消毒劑與區域等級不符（如 Grade A 僅使用一般消毒劑而未定期施用孢子殺菌劑）。

C/D

Grade C / D Areas — Secondary and General Support

Preparation areas · General support corridors · Sanitizer and disinfectant programs

Grade C Areas — Secondary Support Zones

Grade C areas are less critical support areas where less critical stages of the manufacturing process are carried out. Examples include solution preparation, component preparation, and filling of products for further sterilization.

Cleaning program requirements for Grade C:

  • Disinfectant rotation is the standard approach — at least two chemically distinct disinfectants (e.g., a quaternary ammonium compound and an alcohol, or an oxidizing agent) are alternated on a defined schedule to prevent the development of resistant organisms.
  • Sporicide applied periodically — typically monthly or quarterly, or following any adverse EM trend. The sporicide cycle is less frequent than in Grade B.
  • Mops are acceptable for floors; mop heads must be disposable or laundered/sterilized on a defined schedule.
  • Wipers do not require sterilization but should be low-lint and compatible with the chemical agents used.
  • Environmental monitoring at Grade C occurs on a risk basis — typically twice weekly for air and surfaces, but frequency is adjusted based on EM data trends.
  • Written procedures must document agent identity, concentration, preparation method, contact time, and application sequence.

Grade D Areas — General Support Areas

Grade D represents the general support environment for aseptic manufacturing — packaging, storage of containers before cleaning/sterilization, and personnel corridors.

Cleaning requirements for Grade D:

  • A sanitizer (e.g., 70% isopropyl alcohol, quaternary ammonium solution) is generally sufficient as the primary antimicrobial agent.
  • Routine disinfectant use is standard; periodic sporicide may still be applied (e.g., biannually) to control environmental bioburden.
  • Standard mop-and-bucket systems are acceptable; dedicated equipment per zone is still best practice.
  • Environmental monitoring is performed on a risk basis — less frequent than Grade C, typically weekly or monthly depending on operations.
  • Operational particulate counts in Grade D are not defined by EU Annex 1 and depend on the specific operation.

Grade C / D 區域清潔要求

Grade C — 二級支援區域

Grade C（對應 ISO Class 8 靜態）典型場所：溶液配製室（Solution Preparation）、元件準備室、部分灌裝前工序。雖非直接接觸最終無菌產品，仍屬受控環境，需要系統性的消毒計畫。

Grade C 清潔計畫重點：

  • 消毒劑輪替（Disinfectant Rotation）——定期交替使用兩種以上化學機制不同的消毒劑，防止耐藥性微生物產生（Resistance Development）
  • 定期施用孢子殺菌劑——通常每月或每季一次，或在環境監測（EM）趨勢出現異常時觸發
  • 拖把系統——可使用，但拖布頭需為一次性或依計畫滅菌/洗滌
  • 抹布——無需滅菌，但須低落絮（Low-lint）且與所用藥劑相容

消毒劑輪替的科學依據

長期使用單一消毒劑，環境中的耐受性微生物株（Tolerant Strains）可能逐漸累積。輪替使用不同作用機制的藥劑（如氧化劑 vs. 界面活性劑），可有效防止此現象，是 EU GMP 和 FDA 指南的共識要求。

Grade D — 一般支援區域

Grade D 包括：包裝室、容器滅菌前暫存區、更衣前室、物料傳遞走廊等。此等級的主要目標是維持基本的生物負荷控制，防止污染源進入高等級區域。

一般消毒劑（Sanitizer）即可滿足日常需求，但仍需書面 SOP 規定頻率與方法。

Application Methods — How Agents Are Applied

Spray-and-wipe · Mop · Soak / Immersion · Proper wiping technique

Spray-and-Wipe — Most Common Method

The spray-and-wipe technique is the most widely used method for applying cleaning and disinfecting agents to walls, equipment surfaces, and work surfaces in classified areas.

Procedure:

  1. Apply the agent to the surface via spray bottle or pre-wetted wiper, ensuring full coverage of the target area.
  2. Allow contact time as specified in the SOP — the surface must remain visibly wet for the required duration.
  3. Wipe in a unidirectional, overlapping stroke pattern: strokes must overlap by approximately 30% to ensure no area is missed.
  4. Work in a systematic direction — top to bottom, inside to outside (highest risk zone cleaned first to avoid re-contamination from cleaner zone).
  5. Discard the wiper after each surface or after a defined number of strokes — never re-dip a used wiper into a clean agent container.

Mop Systems — Floor Cleaning

Mopping is the standard method for floor cleaning in Grade B, C, and D areas. Key requirements:

  • Dedicated mop heads per area grade — never use a Grade C mop in Grade B.
  • Mop heads should be disposable (single-use) or laundered at high temperature and validated for efficacy.
  • Mop head must be pre-wetted or wetted at the point of use from a freshly prepared agent solution — never from stagnant water.
  • Use the same unidirectional, overlapping stroke technique on floors as for walls.
  • In Grade B, mop handle and bucket must be sterilized before entry.

Soak / Immersion — Small Parts and Tools

Soaking is used for small equipment components, accessories, and tools that can be fully immersed in a disinfectant solution. Requirements:

  • Agent concentration and contact time must be pre-validated for the specific material/surface.
  • Items must be pre-cleaned before immersion — residual soil reduces disinfectant efficacy.
  • The immersion solution should be freshly prepared; reuse of solutions must be limited by defined time or contamination threshold.
  • After soaking, items are rinsed (if required) with sterile or WFI water before introduction into classified areas.

施藥方式說明

三種主要施藥方式

  • 噴霧擦拭法（Spray-and-wipe）——最普遍，適用於牆面、設備外表面、工作台面
  • 拖把法（Mop）——用於地板，各等級均適用但規格要求不同
  • 浸泡法（Soak / Immersion）——適用於小型器具、零件，需完全浸沒

單向重疊擦拭技術（Unidirectional Overlapping Stroke）——最關鍵的操作細節

隨意的「來回擦拭」會將微生物從污染區帶回已清潔區，反而擴散污染。正確方式：

  1. 抹布從最高風險或最乾淨區域開始（Grade A 灌裝區入口優先）
  2. 每次擦拭為同一方向（如從左到右或從上到下）
  3. 每次新的擦拭行程重疊前一行程約 30%
  4. 到達邊界後，翻轉或更換抹布，禁止反向重擦
  5. 每個區域或每定義擦拭次數後丟棄抹布

比喻：粉刷牆面

好的粉刷技師會用重疊的橫向筆觸，由上到下均勻覆蓋，不留縫隙——清潔抹布操作原理完全相同。隨意畫圈或反覆來回，就像用同一支沾了舊漆的刷子，結果只是把污染均勻分散。

清潔程序步驟順序

  1. 穿戴符合等級要求的防護裝備（PPE / Gowning）
  2. 移除可動設備或物件，擴大清潔可及範圍
  3. 初步清掃（Initial Cleaning）——去除可見灰塵、碎屑、產品殘跡（此步驟使用清潔劑，非消毒劑）
  4. 施用消毒劑（Disinfectant）——噴霧覆蓋，確保全面濕潤，計時維持接觸時間
  5. 按 SOP 規定決定是否擦拭或讓藥劑自然揮發
  6. 依計畫施用孢子殺菌劑（Sporicide）——視等級頻率而定
  7. 清潔後環境監測（Post-cleaning EM）——確認清潔效果
  8. 記錄並簽署清潔日誌（Cleaning Log）

SOP 必備要素（稽核核查清單）

  • 藥劑名稱與製造商
  • 使用濃度（配製比例）
  • 接觸時間（Contact Time）
  • 抹布類型與規格（是否需要滅菌）
  • 清潔順序（由高等級到低等級，由上到下）
  • 拖把頭更換 / 滅菌頻率
  • 操作人員資格要求
  • 廢棄物處置方式

Cleaning Materials — Selection and Control

The choice of cleaning materials — mop heads, wipers, and buckets — directly affects the efficacy of a cleaning and disinfection program. Materials must be selected based on their physical properties, compatibility with chemical agents, and the cleanliness classification of the area in which they are used.

Wipers

Wipers are used throughout all cleanroom grades. Their primary function is to deliver mechanical action and carry chemical agents to the surface. Key selection criteria include:

  • Grade A/B (ISO 5): Sterile gamma-irradiated (γ-irradiated) wipers are required. Pre-saturated sterile wipers are preferred to ensure consistent chemical delivery and to eliminate the risk of contamination from the wiper itself.
  • Grade C/D: Sterile or non-sterile wipers may be used depending on risk assessment. Non-sterile wipers must still be low-particulate and low-linting.
  • Single-use vs. reusable: Single-use wipers eliminate cross-contamination risk between surfaces. Reusable wipers must be laundered and re-sterilized via a validated procedure before re-use.
  • Material: Polyester/cellulose blends are common. Fiber shedding and chemical compatibility with the intended cleaning agent must be evaluated.

Critical Note — Wiper Residues

Residues from chemical agents, loose fibers from wipers, and released wiper binders can become contamination sources on product-contact or near-product surfaces. Wiper type and chemistry must be part of the formal validation package.

Mop Heads

Mops are the primary tool for applying cleaning and disinfecting agents to walls and floors. Mop heads must be validated for compatibility with the disinfectants and sporicides used in the program. Key requirements include:

  • Mop heads used in Grade A/B areas must be sterile — typically γ-irradiated single-use heads or heads that have been laundered and re-sterilized by a validated process.
  • Reusable mop heads accumulate bioburden if not properly laundered. Validated laundering processes define wash temperature, detergent, rinse cycles, and re-sterilization method.
  • The wringing mechanism affects liquid retention: poor wringing leads to inadequate surface wetting and insufficient contact time for the disinfecting agent.
  • Mop handles and wringer buckets must also be included in the C&D program; they are potential sources of secondary contamination.

Buckets

Buckets used to prepare and carry cleaning solutions should be:

  • Dedicated to specific areas or grades to prevent cross-contamination.
  • Cleaned and disinfected after each use using the same validated procedures applied to other equipment in the area.
  • Inspected regularly for cracks, pitting, or residue buildup that can harbor microorganisms.
  • Properly labeled with the cleaning agent in use, concentration, and preparation date/time to ensure solution is not used beyond its validated in-use period.

清潔材料的選擇與管控

清潔材料（拖把頭 mop heads、擦拭布 wipers、水桶 buckets）的選用直接影響整個清潔消毒程序的效果。材料必須根據其物理特性、與化學藥劑的相容性，以及使用場所的潔淨等級來選擇。

擦拭布 Wipers

擦拭布用於所有潔淨室等級，主要功能是提供機械動作並將化學藥劑傳送至表面。選擇重點如下：

  • A/B 級 (ISO 5)：必須使用無菌伽瑪射線照射（γ-irradiated）擦拭布。預先浸潤的無菌擦拭布（pre-saturated sterile wipers）是首選，可確保藥劑量的一致性，並消除擦拭布本身的污染風險。
  • C/D 級：根據風險評估，可使用無菌或非無菌擦拭布。非無菌擦拭布仍須具備低微粒、低掉纖維特性。
  • 單次使用 vs. 可重複使用：單次使用擦拭布可消除表面間的交叉污染風險。可重複使用擦拭布在再次使用前必須按照驗證程序進行洗滌與重新滅菌（laundering and re-sterilization）。
  • 材質：聚酯/纖維素混紡布料常見。纖維脫落（fiber shedding）程度及與清潔藥劑的相容性必須經過評估。

關鍵注意事項 — 擦拭布殘留物

化學藥劑殘留物、擦拭布散落的游離纖維及黏合劑成分，都可能成為產品接觸面或鄰近產品表面的污染來源。擦拭布的種類與化學特性必須納入正式的驗證文件包中。

拖把頭 Mop Heads

拖把是將清潔劑和消毒劑施用於牆面和地板的主要工具。拖把頭必須針對程序中使用的消毒劑及孢子殺滅劑（sporicides）進行相容性驗證（compatibility validation）。主要要求：

  • 用於 A/B 級區域的拖把頭必須是無菌狀態——通常採用 γ-irradiated 的單次使用頭，或已經過驗證程序洗滌與重新滅菌的可重複使用頭。
  • 可重複使用的拖把頭若未正確洗滌，會累積生物負載（bioburden）。驗證洗滌程序需定義洗滌溫度、洗劑、沖洗週期及重新滅菌方式。
  • 擰乾（wringing）機制影響液體保留量：擰乾不足將導致表面潤濕不充分，消毒劑接觸時間（contact time）不足。
  • 拖把桿與絞桶（wringer buckets）也須納入 C&D 程序；它們是次要污染的潛在來源。

水桶 Buckets

用於配製和盛裝清潔溶液的水桶應：

  • 專屬於特定區域或等級，以防止交叉污染（cross-contamination）。
  • 每次使用後，以適用於該區域設備的相同驗證程序進行清潔和消毒。
  • 定期檢查裂縫、腐蝕凹坑或殘留積垢，此類缺陷易滋生微生物。
  • 正確標示使用中的清潔劑名稱、濃度及配製日期/時間，確保溶液不超過其已驗證的使用有效期。

9.4.1 Non-Product-Contact Equipment Surfaces

Non-product-contact equipment surfaces are those found in close proximity to product-contact surfaces. Because of their critical location, any cleaning chemical, sanitizer, disinfectant, sporicide, wiper, or other material used on these surfaces must not leave a residue that could be inadvertently transferred to a product-contact surface.

The cleaning sequence for non-product-contact surfaces is:

  1. Pre-cleaning: Remove past product spills, broken glass (from vials, syringes, ampoules), torn stoppers, damaged caps, and other foreign matter before attempting disinfection.
  2. Disinfection: Once pre-cleaning is complete, spray or wipe the surface with an efficacious disinfectant or sporicide and allow it to remain wetted for the specified contact time.
  3. IPA wipe-down: After the contact time, all surfaces should receive a wipe-down using 70% isopropyl alcohol (IPA) if a sporicide or disinfectant with residual properties was used.

9.4.2 Work Surfaces

Work surfaces — tables, carts, setup areas — may be near product or near components that come into contact with product. Precleaning before disinfection provides a higher level of disinfection efficacy. After cleaning, surfaces should be disinfected using either a disinfectant or a sporicide. A 70% IPA spray-down followed by a dry wipe may be required if the agent used leaves a residue. Frequency is normally daily but should be based on usage.

9.4.3 Nonstructural and Hard-to-Clean Surfaces

Structural surfaces (walls, ceilings, floors) along with filling equipment should be routinely cleaned and disinfected. Frequency should be based on environmental monitoring (EM) results and/or a risk-based analysis. Nonstructural surfaces require equal consideration, as they can contaminate the environment.

Surfaces are divided into two categories:

  • Routine nonstructural surfaces: Tanks, carts, countertops, racks, packaged supplies, storage bins, stairs, work surfaces, non-product-contact surfaces, monitors, gauges.
  • Hard-to-clean surfaces: Tops of doors, tracks, conveyors, phones, equipment feet and legs, underside of tanks/carts/equipment, wheels, exterior of tubing/pipes, incubators, refrigerators, cold rooms, tools (sterilization may be required).

HVAC and Air Handling Surfaces

HVAC components in cleanrooms — HEPA filters, air supply plenums, return air grilles, and ductwork — require a dedicated cleaning approach:

  • HEPA filters: Must not be wetted with cleaning or disinfecting agents under any circumstances. Accidental wetting creates a habitat for mold growth and can damage or compromise the filter matrix, leading to filter integrity failure.
  • Air supply plenums: Cleaned on a scheduled basis. Visual inspection for dust or particulate buildup informs frequency. Fogging or gassing agents may penetrate into plenum spaces but care must be taken to protect filter integrity.
  • Return air grilles: Wiped with a disinfectant-moistened wipe. Grilles collect particulates and must be cleaned with a frequency informed by EM trend data — adverse EM trends may trigger increased grille cleaning frequency.
  • Cleaning schedule tied to EM data: HVAC surface cleaning is not fixed at an arbitrary calendar interval. It is a dynamic schedule responsive to environmental monitoring trends, particularly elevated particle counts or microbial exceedances.

9.4.1 非產品接觸設備表面

非產品接觸設備表面（non-product-contact equipment surfaces）位於產品接觸面的鄰近區域，因其位置關鍵，所有在這些表面使用的清潔藥劑、消毒劑（disinfectant）、孢子殺滅劑（sporicide）、擦拭布或其他材料，均不得留下可能轉移至產品接觸面的殘留物。

非產品接觸表面的清潔流程：

  1. 預清潔（pre-cleaning）：在嘗試消毒之前，先移除過去的產品溢漏、玻璃碎片（來自瓶罐、針筒、安瓿）、破損橡膠塞、損毀蓋子及其他異物。
  2. 消毒（disinfection）：預清潔完成後，以有效的消毒劑或孢子殺滅劑噴灑或擦拭表面，並讓表面保持濕潤達到規定的接觸時間（contact time）。
  3. IPA 擦拭：接觸時間到達後，若使用含殘留特性的孢子殺滅劑或消毒劑，所有表面均應以 70% 異丙醇（isopropyl alcohol，IPA）進行擦拭去除殘留。

9.4.2 工作表面 Work Surfaces

工作台面、推車、設置區域等工作表面可能鄰近產品或產品接觸組件。在消毒前進行預清潔（precleaning）可提升消毒效果。清潔後，使用消毒劑或孢子殺滅劑消毒；若所用藥劑有殘留性，需使用 70% IPA 噴灑並乾布擦拭。頻率通常為每日，但應依實際使用情況調整。

9.4.3 非結構性及難以清潔的表面

結構性表面（牆壁、天花板、地板）及充填設備應定期進行清潔消毒，頻率應依環境監測（environmental monitoring，EM）結果及風險分析決定。非結構性表面（nonstructural surfaces）同樣需要關注，因為它們也可能污染潔淨室環境。

表面分為兩類：

  • 常規非結構性表面：儲槽（tanks）、推車（carts）、工作台面（countertops）、架子（racks）、已包裝物料、儲物箱（storage bins）、樓梯、工作表面、非產品接觸面、監測儀器、儀表。
  • 難以清潔的表面（hard-to-clean surfaces）：門頂、軌道（tracks）、輸送帶（conveyors）、電話、設備腳架與腿部、儲槽/推車/設備底部、輪子、管路（tubing/pipes）外表面、培養箱、冰箱、冷藏室、工具（可能需要滅菌）。

HVAC 空氣處理系統表面

潔淨室中的 HVAC 組件——HEPA 過濾器（HEPA filters）、送風靜壓箱（air supply plenums）、回風格柵（return air grilles）和風管——需要專屬的清潔方式：

  • HEPA 過濾器：任何情況下均不得以清潔或消毒藥劑潤濕。意外潤濕會為黴菌生長創造條件，並可能損壞過濾器基材，導致過濾器完整性（filter integrity）受損。
  • 送風靜壓箱：按計劃定期清潔。透過目視檢查粉塵或微粒積累情況來決定清潔頻率。噴霧（fogging）或氣體薰蒸（gassing）可能滲入靜壓箱空間，但須保護過濾器完整性。
  • 回風格柵：以浸有消毒劑的擦拭布擦拭。格柵會積累微粒，清潔頻率應根據 EM 趨勢數據決定——若 EM 出現不良趨勢，應提高格柵清潔頻率。
  • 清潔計劃與 EM 數據掛鉤：HVAC 表面清潔不固定於某一日曆時間表，而是動態回應環境監測趨勢——特別是微粒計數升高或微生物超標時。

核心概念：EM 數據驅動的清潔計劃

以「每月清潔一次」的固定計劃作為唯一依據是不夠的。GMP 要求清潔頻率應具備回應性——當 EM 趨勢顯示微粒或微生物數量上升時，這是強化 HVAC 表面清潔的訊號，而非等待下一個計劃週期。

Floors, Drains & Special Surfaces

Floor Cleaning Principles

Floors in classified areas are among the highest-bioburden surfaces due to gravity settling of particles and personnel foot traffic. Effective floor cleaning requires:

  • Directional mopping: Always mop from the cleanest area toward the dirtiest area, and from the highest-grade room toward lower-grade rooms. This prevents reintroduction of contamination to cleaner zones.
  • Mechanical action: Mopping provides the mechanical action required to dislodge adhered microorganisms and particulates. Spraying alone is insufficient for floors.
  • Liquid removal: After mopping with a cleaner, excess liquid and suspended contaminants must be removed using a sterile dry mop, a sterile dry wipe, or a HEPA-filtered wet vacuum. This prepares the surface for the disinfecting agent.
  • Disinfection contact time: After the floor surface dries, it should be sufficiently wetted with a sterile (Grade A/B) or sterile/non-sterile (Grade C/D) disinfecting agent. The disinfectant must remain in contact with the surface for the validated contact time.
  • Zone boundaries: Physical entry mats, tacky mats, and step-over barriers define zone boundaries and reduce particulate transfer between areas. These items must also be included in the C&D program.

Drains

Floor drains present unique microbiological challenges in classified areas:

  • Drains are inherently high-bioburden areas. Standing water, biofilm, and organic matter accumulate within drain traps and surrounding surfaces.
  • Drains in Grade C and D areas should be cleaned and disinfected regularly. Sporicide application to drain surfaces and interior trap areas is strongly recommended.
  • Drains in Grade A or B areas are generally not present (open drains are incompatible with aseptic processing). Where present, they must be sealed or capped when aseptic operations are underway.
  • Drain cleaning must be documented with frequency and agent defined in the C&D SOP. Results should be correlated with environmental monitoring data from nearby monitoring points.

Curtains and Pass-Through Areas

Cleanroom curtains present particular challenges due to their soft surface finish. When viewed microscopically, curtain surfaces are rougher than wall surfaces and provide more protection for dirt and microorganisms against cleaning and disinfecting agents.

  • Curtains should be cleaned first using a high-surfactant-based cleaner or 70% IPA applied via a mechanical wipe or mop action.
  • After cleaning, curtains should be disinfected with a low-residue disinfectant or sporicide (e.g., hydrogen peroxide or peracetic acid). Contact time must meet validated requirements (minimum 5 minutes).
  • If a medium- to high-residue agent (phenolic, quaternary ammonium, or bleach) is used, curtains must be subsequently wiped with 70% IPA and a dry wipe to remove residue. Residue transfer to critical areas or filling equipment must be prevented.
  • Curtains should be cleaned with greater frequency than wall surfaces due to higher personnel contact frequency.

地板清潔原則

潔淨室地板因重力沉降的微粒及人員走動，是生物負載（bioburden）最高的表面之一。有效的地板清潔需要：

  • 方向性拖地：始終從最乾淨的區域往最髒的區域拖，從較高等級的房間往較低等級的房間拖。這可防止污染物被帶回較乾淨的區域。
  • 機械動作：拖地提供了清除黏附微生物和微粒所需的機械動作，僅靠噴灑對地板是不夠的。
  • 液體移除：以清潔劑拖地後，必須用無菌乾拖把、無菌乾擦拭布或 HEPA 過濾濕式吸塵器移除多餘液體及懸浮污染物，以備接下來施用消毒劑。
  • 消毒接觸時間：地板表面乾燥後，應充分潤濕地塗布無菌（A/B 級）或無菌/非無菌（C/D 級）消毒劑，消毒劑必須與表面保持驗證要求的接觸時間。
  • 區域邊界：入口黏塵墊（tacky mats）和跨越屏障（step-over barriers）定義區域邊界，減少區域間的微粒轉移。這些物件也必須納入 C&D 程序。

排水口 Drains

排水口在潔淨室中存在獨特的微生物挑戰：

  • 排水口本身是高生物負載區域。積水、生物膜（biofilm）和有機物在排水彎管（drain traps）及周圍表面積聚。
  • C 級和 D 級區域的排水口應定期清潔和消毒。強烈建議對排水口表面及彎管內部施用孢子殺滅劑。
  • A 級或 B 級區域通常不設置排水口（開放式排水口與無菌製程不相容）。若存在，在無菌操作進行時必須密封或加蓋。
  • 排水口清潔必須有文件記錄，SOP 中應定義頻率和使用藥劑，結果應與附近監測點的環境監測數據相關聯。

門簾與傳遞窗區域 Curtains and Pass-Through Areas

潔淨室門簾（curtains）因其較軟的表面特性而面臨特殊挑戰。在顯微鏡下觀察，門簾表面比牆面更粗糙，為污垢和微生物提供了更多受清潔和消毒藥劑保護的藏匿空間。

  • 門簾應先以高界面活性劑基底清潔劑或 70% IPA，透過機械擦拭或拖地動作進行清潔。
  • 清潔後，以低殘留的消毒劑或孢子殺滅劑（如過氧化氫或過氧乙酸 peracetic acid）消毒。接觸時間必須達到驗證要求（最少 5 分鐘）。
  • 若使用中至高殘留藥劑（酚類 phenolic、四級銨化合物 quaternary ammonium 或漂白劑），門簾隨後必須用 70% IPA 和乾布擦拭以去除殘留物，以防止殘留物轉移至關鍵區域或充填設備。
  • 由於人員接觸頻率較高，門簾清潔頻率應高於牆面。

類比說明：排水口是污染的「陷阱」

想像排水口是一個持續接收各種污染物的「儲存庫」——每次地板清潔的污水、人員攜入的微生物、環境中沉降的微粒，最終都集中在排水彎管中。若不定期以孢子殺滅劑處理，排水口可能成為逆向污染的來源，在地板清潔後反而將微生物帶回環境。

Lyophilizer Cleaning & Disinfection / Corrosion Prevention

Lyophilizer Cleaning and Disinfection

Lyophilizers (freeze-dryers) represent one of the most critical pieces of equipment for C&D in aseptic fill-finish manufacturing. The interior chamber creates a complex environment for microbial contamination control due to its enclosed nature, condensation accumulation, and the use of cryogenic temperatures during operation.

Key C&D Requirements for Lyophilizers

  • Interior chamber cleaning after each cycle: The lyophilizer chamber must be cleaned after every production cycle. Product residues, stopper fragments, broken vials, and condensed moisture must be removed before the next loading.
  • Door gasket inspection: The door gasket (seal) must be inspected at each cleaning cycle for integrity. Cracks, cuts, or biological residue on the gasket can compromise chamber sterility and provide a harbor for contamination.
  • Shelf condensation management: Shelf surfaces accumulate condensation during the freeze and primary drying phases. After cycle completion, shelves must be wiped with a sterile wipe and cleaned before sporicide application. Standing moisture on shelf surfaces can dilute disinfectants and reduce efficacy.
  • Sporicide treatment: Sporicide treatment of the lyophilizer interior is often required — particularly following product campaigns, environmental monitoring excursions, or scheduled periodic decontamination. Common sporicides include 0.52% sodium hypochlorite, peracetic acid solutions, or vaporized hydrogen peroxide (VHP). VHP offers the advantage of gaseous penetration to all internal surfaces without manual wiping.
  • Condenser cleaning: The condenser accumulates ice during operation. After defrost, the condenser must be cleaned and disinfected. Condensate water must be drained and the condenser surface treated to prevent biofilm formation.

GMP Documentation Requirements

Lyophilizer C&D is a critical GMP activity. At manufacturing sites such as aseptic CDMO operations, all C&D activities on the lyophilizer must be:

  • Documented in the batch record (batch manufacturing record / BMR) for each production lot processed in that lyophilizer.
  • Linked to environmental monitoring results — EM samples from the lyophilizer chamber and loading area should be reviewed alongside the C&D record to confirm that cleaning was effective and the environment was in a state of control.
  • Executed according to approved SOPs with version control. Operators performing lyophilizer C&D must be trained and qualified on the procedure.
  • Subject to periodic validation review — lyophilizer C&D procedures require validation data demonstrating that sporicide contact time, agent concentration, and application method achieve log reduction targets for target organisms.

Regulatory Emphasis

FDA and EMA inspection findings frequently cite inadequate lyophilizer cleaning and disinfection as a contributor to environmental monitoring excursions and sterility assurance failures. The lyophilizer is a confined, low-temperature environment where moisture and residue can persist if C&D practices are not rigorously controlled.

凍乾機的清潔消毒

凍乾機（lyophilizer，即冷凍乾燥機）是無菌充填製程中最關鍵的 C&D 設備之一。其密閉腔體在冷凍操作過程中會積聚冷凝水，加上低溫環境的特殊性，使微生物污染控制面臨複雜挑戰。

凍乾機 C&D 的主要要求

  • 每個週期後進行腔體清潔：每次生產週期完成後必須清潔凍乾機腔體。在下一批次裝載前，必須移除產品殘留物、橡膠塞碎片、破損玻璃瓶和冷凝水。
  • 門墊圈（door gasket）檢查：每次清潔時必須檢查門密封墊圈的完整性。墊圈上的裂縫、切痕或生物殘留物會危害腔體無菌性，並提供污染藏匿的空間。
  • 棚架冷凝水管理（shelf condensation management）：棚架表面在冷凍和初級乾燥階段會積累冷凝水。週期完成後，棚架必須用無菌擦拭布擦拭清潔，再施用孢子殺滅劑。棚架表面的積水可能稀釋消毒劑並降低效力。
  • 孢子殺滅劑（sporicide）處理：凍乾機內部通常需要進行孢子殺滅劑處理——特別是在產品活動後、環境監測超標後，或例行定期去污染（periodic decontamination）時。常用孢子殺滅劑包括 0.52% 次氯酸鈉（sodium hypochlorite）、過氧乙酸（peracetic acid）溶液，或氣化過氧化氫（VHP，vaporized hydrogen peroxide）。VHP 的優勢在於以氣態滲透所有內部表面，無需手動擦拭。
  • 冷凝器清潔：冷凝器在運作中積累冰霜。除霜後必須清潔和消毒冷凝器，排出冷凝水，並對冷凝器表面進行處理以防止生物膜（biofilm）形成。

GMP 文件要求

凍乾機 C&D 是關鍵 GMP 活動。在如無菌 CDMO 的製造場所（例如 TWi），所有凍乾機 C&D 活動必須：

  • 定期接受驗證審查——凍乾機 C&D 程序需要驗證數據，證明孢子殺滅劑接觸時間、藥劑濃度和施用方式能對目標微生物達到對數降低（log reduction）目標。

Corrosion Prevention — Disinfectant Compatibility with Stainless Steel

Many disinfectants and sporicides used in aseptic manufacturing facilities are corrosive to stainless steel equipment surfaces when used incorrectly or in excess concentration. Understanding material compatibility is essential to protecting equipment longevity and preventing product contamination from corrosion byproducts.

High-Risk Disinfectants for Corrosion

  • Chlorine-based agents (sodium hypochlorite / bleach): These are highly effective sporicides but are among the most corrosive to 316L stainless steel, aluminum, and other metals. Pitting corrosion (localized corrosion creating small holes in the surface) is the primary failure mode. Risk increases with concentration, temperature, and contact time. Chlorine solutions must not be left on stainless steel surfaces beyond the validated contact time.
  • Peracetic acid (PAA): Effective sporicide; less corrosive than chlorine at typical use concentrations but still requires rinsing. Higher concentrations used in recirculating systems can accelerate corrosion of welds and surface imperfections.
  • Hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂): Generally lower corrosion risk than chlorine at standard concentrations. VHP applications at high concentrations may affect certain polymers and elastomers rather than stainless steel.

Compatibility Data and Rinsing Protocols

Formal material compatibility data must be obtained before introducing any new disinfectant or sporicide into the C&D program:

  • Compatibility testing should cover the actual materials of construction in the facility: grade of stainless steel, polymers, gasket materials, painted surfaces, anodized aluminum, and floor coatings.
  • Validated rinsing protocols must define the rinse agent (sterile water for injection / WFI, or purified water), rinse volume, number of rinse cycles, and acceptable residual limits for the disinfecting agent on the surface post-rinse.
  • Contact time limits must be established and enforced. Operators must not allow sporicides or chlorine-based agents to dry on stainless steel surfaces before rinsing.
  • Corrosion inspection should be a routine part of equipment maintenance and cleaning verification — visible pitting, discoloration, or surface roughening should trigger investigation and potentially a change in C&D protocol.

Practical Application — Chlorine Rinsing at Fill-Finish Sites

At aseptic fill-finish CDMO operations, sodium hypochlorite is commonly used for periodic sporicide treatment of lyophilizer chambers and equipment surfaces. The validated procedure must specify that surfaces are rinsed with WFI within a defined time window after the sporicide contact time. Failure to rinse within this window has been the root cause of stainless steel pitting in filling line equipment, leading to surface qualification failures and unplanned equipment replacements.

腐蝕預防——消毒劑與不銹鋼的相容性

無菌製造設施中使用的許多消毒劑和孢子殺滅劑，若使用不當或濃度過高，對不銹鋼設備表面具有腐蝕性（corrosive）。了解材料相容性（material compatibility）對於保護設備壽命和防止腐蝕副產品污染產品至關重要。

高腐蝕風險的消毒劑

  • 含氯製劑（chlorine-based agents）——次氯酸鈉（sodium hypochlorite）/漂白劑：這類藥劑孢子殺滅效果極佳，但對 316L 不銹鋼、鋁及其他金屬腐蝕性最強。點蝕（pitting corrosion，在表面形成小孔的局部腐蝕）是主要失效模式。風險隨濃度、溫度和接觸時間增加而升高。氯溶液不得在不銹鋼表面上的接觸時間超過已驗證的範圍。
  • 過氧乙酸（peracetic acid，PAA）：有效的孢子殺滅劑；在一般使用濃度下腐蝕性低於氯，但仍需沖洗。用於循環系統的高濃度過氧乙酸可加速焊縫（welds）和表面缺陷的腐蝕。
  • 過氧化氫（hydrogen peroxide，H₂O₂）：在標準濃度下腐蝕風險通常低於氯。高濃度 VHP 應用可能影響某些聚合物和彈性體，而非不銹鋼。

相容性數據與沖洗程序（Compatibility Data and Rinsing Protocols）

在 C&D 程序中引入任何新的消毒劑或孢子殺滅劑之前，必須取得正式的材料相容性數據：

  • 相容性測試應涵蓋設施中實際使用的建構材料：不銹鋼等級、聚合物、墊圈材料、塗漆表面、陽極氧化鋁（anodized aluminum）和地板塗層。
  • 驗證過的沖洗程序必須定義沖洗藥劑（無菌注射用水 WFI 或純化水 purified water）、沖洗體積、沖洗週期次數，以及沖洗後表面消毒劑殘留量的可接受限值。
  • 必須建立並執行接觸時間限制。操作人員不得讓孢子殺滅劑或含氯製劑在沖洗前乾燥於不銹鋼表面上。
  • 腐蝕檢查應作為設備維護和清潔驗證的常規部分——可見點蝕、變色或表面粗糙化應觸發調查，並可能導致 C&D 程序的變更。

實務應用 — 充填工廠的含氯藥劑沖洗

在無菌充填 CDMO（如 TWi）的作業中，次氯酸鈉（sodium hypochlorite）常用於凍乾機腔體和設備表面的定期孢子殺滅處理。驗證程序必須規定，在孢子殺滅劑接觸時間結束後的特定時間窗口內，必須以 WFI 沖洗表面。未能在此時間窗口內沖洗，已是充填線設備不銹鋼點蝕的根本原因，導致表面資格確認失敗和計劃外設備更換。

10.0 Frequency for Cleaning and Disinfection

10.0 Frequency for Cleaning and Disinfection

Selecting an appropriate cleaning and disinfection frequency for manufacturing facility surfaces — walls, ceilings, doors, non-product-contact equipment, and floors — is essential for maintaining effective contamination control. The industry has developed three main approaches:

Approach 1: Area Classification

Frequencies are set according to the stringency of the area classification. The most stringent area receives the most frequent cleaning. Example:

  • Grade A (ISO 5): Daily cleaning and disinfection
  • Grade C (ISO 7): Weekly
  • Grade D (ISO 8): Monthly

Limitation: Does not account for product contamination risk or type of manufacturing conducted.

Approach 2: Environmental Monitoring (EM) Data

Frequency is set based solely on trending EM data. This approach tends to be reactive and retrospective — it responds to existing contamination levels rather than proactively preventing them. More commonly used to justify reducing an already-established frequency based on sustained satisfactory results.

Approach 3: Risk-Based Model (Preferred)

Combines area classification and EM data while also considering product exposure risk, personnel intervention, and manufacturing type. This provides the greatest flexibility to tailor the program to the specific facility and process.

Key risk-based principles:

  • Frequency must be commensurate with the risk of product contamination. Open vs. closed processing and proximity to final sterilization steps are primary criteria.
  • Areas within the aseptic manufacturing boundary (Grade D to Grade C) should be cleaned more frequently than those outside.
  • ISO 8 areas adjacent and contiguous to open aseptic processing zones may need more frequent cleaning than standalone ISO 8 areas.
  • Product changeovers require cleaning per established procedures to prevent cross-contamination.
  • High-ingress locations (gowning airlocks, doors, floors) and areas supporting microbial growth warrant higher frequency.
  • Older buildings or difficult-to-clean layouts may warrant increased frequency.
  • Event-based triggers — such as EM action level excursions, HEPA filter failures, or facility shutdowns — require additional cleaning beyond routine schedules.
  • Routine EM data must be reviewed regularly to verify that the chosen frequency remains effective.

10.0 清潔與消毒頻率

為製造設施表面（牆壁、天花板、門、非接觸產品的設備及地板）選擇適當的清潔與消毒頻率，是維持有效污染控制的關鍵。業界發展出三種主要方法：

方法一：區域分類法（Area Classification）

根據潔淨區等級高低設定頻率，等級越嚴格，清潔頻率越高。範例：

  • Grade A（ISO 5）：每日清潔與消毒
  • Grade C（ISO 7）：每週
  • Grade D（ISO 8）：每月

限制：未考慮各區域的產品污染風險或製造類型差異。

方法二：環境監測數據法（EM Data）

完全依據 EM 趨勢數據決定頻率。此方法本質上是被動且回溯性的，通常用於在持續良好表現的基礎上，合理化降低已建立的清潔頻率，而非積極預防污染。

方法三：風險模型法（Risk-Based Model，推薦）

整合前兩種方法，並額外考量：產品暴露風險、人員干預程度、製造類型。靈活性最高，可針對特定設施設計量身定制的污染控制計劃。

核心風險原則：

  • 頻率必須與產品污染風險相稱。開放式 vs. 密閉製程，以及與最終滅菌步驟的距離，是首要選擇標準。
  • 無菌製造邊界內的區域（如 Grade D 至 Grade C 的傳遞路徑）需比邊界外更頻繁清潔。
  • 緊鄰開放無菌操作區的 ISO 8 區域，可能需要比獨立 ISO 8 區域更頻繁的清潔。
  • 每次換產（product changeover）皆需按 SOP 清潔，以防交叉污染。
  • 高污染入侵點（更衣氣閘、門、地板）及可能滋生微生物的區域，需提高頻率。
  • 建築老化或清潔困難的佈局，可能需提高頻率。
  • 事件觸發額外清潔：EM 行動水準超標、HEPA 過濾器故障、設施停機等。
  • EM 數據需定期審查，驗證所選頻率的持續有效性。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

在 TWi 的無菌充填作業中，風險模型法是最實用的選擇。對於 Grade A 充填區（ISO 5），每批次後的清潔與消毒是基本要求；Grade B（ISO 5/6 背景）每日清潔；Grade C 根據批次頻率設定（通常每週至少一次）。

當 EM 監測出現 Alert Level 超標時，必須立即啟動額外清潔程序，並在 CAPA 中記錄根本原因分析。這正是「事件觸發」頻率調整的典型應用。

11.0 Resistance and Rotation

11.0 Resistance and Rotation

For many years, the industry debated whether microorganisms can develop resistance to sanitizers, disinfectants, and sporicides — analogous to antibiotic resistance. The current scientific consensus from TR70 and USP <1072> is as follows:

"The development of microbial resistance to antibiotics is a well-described phenomenon. The development of microbial resistance [to disinfectants] is less likely, as disinfectants are more powerful biocidal agents than antibiotics and are applied in high concentrations against low populations of microorganisms, so the selective pressure for the development of resistance is less profound." — USP <1072>

Key reasons why true disinfectant resistance is unlikely in cleanrooms:

  • Antibiotic resistance typically requires modification or acquisition of a single gene. Disinfectants have multiple, simultaneous mechanisms of action against cellular physiology — multiple simultaneous mutations would be required in a short contact time (e.g., 5 minutes).
  • Cleanrooms maintain inherently low cell numbers, reducing the statistical probability of a resistance mutation surviving.

Evolution of Rotation Strategy

Historically, firms rotated between two or more disinfectants (e.g., QAC alternate with phenolic). This led to:

  • High residue buildup on surfaces
  • Subordinate (reduced) efficacy performance

Current best practice: Rotate a disinfectant with a sporicide. This is superior because sporicides address spore-forming organisms that standard disinfectants cannot reliably eliminate.

Special considerations:

  • Using a sporicide alone (without a disinfectant) is possible if efficacy is proven — but is discouraged due to the inherent corrosive nature of most sporicidal agents.
  • All rotation systems must be evaluated using area classification, EM data, and/or formal risk assessment.

11.0 抗藥性與輪替策略

業界長期爭論微生物是否會對消毒劑產生類似抗生素的抗藥性（Resistance）。TR70 和 USP <1072> 的現行科學共識是：

「微生物對消毒劑產生抗藥性的可能性低於抗生素，因為消毒劑是效力更強的殺菌劑，施用於低菌落數環境中，抗藥性的選擇壓力遠低於抗生素。」— USP <1072>

潔淨室環境不易產生真正抗藥性的主要原因：

  • 抗生素抗藥性只需單一基因突變；消毒劑對細胞生理有多重作用機制，短暫接觸時間內（如 5 分鐘）需要多個同步突變才能產生抗性，機率極低。
  • 潔淨室中菌落數極少，產生有效抗藥性突變的統計概率更低。

輪替策略的演進

歷史上，工廠採用「兩種或多種消毒劑交替輪換」（如 QAC 輪替酚類）。問題：

  • 表面殘留物累積嚴重
  • 整體殺菌效能下降

現行最佳實務：以消毒劑（Disinfectant）搭配孢子殺滅劑（Sporicide）進行輪替，因後者可有效消滅標準消毒劑無法殺滅的孢子型微生物。

比喻說明 — 輪替的意義

想像用同一種洗髮精洗頭很久後，效果似乎變差了——這不是真正的「抗藥性」，而是表面殘留物或效能下降的感知。

消毒劑輪替的真正價值不在於「防抗藥性」，而在於：(1) 確保不同類型微生物（含孢子）都被有效覆蓋；(2) 防止殘留物堆積；(3) 通過 EM 數據驗證輪替計劃的持續有效性。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

TWi 的清潔計劃必須有書面記錄的輪替時程，並附上選擇各藥劑的科學依據（rationale）。稽查員會要求查看：

  • 輪替 SOP 或程序
  • 各藥劑的有效性驗證數據（包含孢子殺滅劑的孢子試驗）
  • 與 EM 趨勢的定期關聯審查

若 EM 數據出現持續偏高趨勢，應評估是否需要增加孢子殺滅劑頻率或改變輪替設計。

12.0 Return from a Shutdown

12.0 Return from a Shutdown

A shutdown is a planned or unplanned stoppage of operations that is likely to compromise the environmental conditions in a classified area. Types include:

  • Planned: Preventive maintenance, construction activities, scheduled facility holidays
  • Unplanned: Unexpected power outages, HVAC failures, emergency stops

The extent and duration of a shutdown determines the level of viable and non-viable contamination introduced. Regardless of type, defined actions must be taken before operations resume.

Return-to-Control Criteria

  1. Verify control systems: Confirm that all HVAC/air handlers, pressure differentials, and environmental controls are functioning properly before beginning any cleaning.
  2. Pre-clean with detergent or water: Remove dirt, dust, or construction debris before applying any disinfectant. Disinfectants cannot effectively penetrate gross contamination.
  3. Disinfect per established procedures: Apply disinfectant according to qualified procedures to reduce bioburden to acceptable levels. All surfaces — walls, floors, and equipment surfaces — must be included.
  4. Use qualified, in-situ data where possible: The multistep cleaning and disinfection regimen should be supported by evidence demonstrating it is capable of bringing the facility back to a state of control.
  5. Monitor before resuming operations: Sample for viable and non-viable particulates after cleaning and disinfection is complete. Evaluate results to verify the area is within control limits before returning to use.

Key Principle

The return-to-use decision must be data-driven. Environmental monitoring results — not just visual inspection or adherence to a cleaning schedule — should confirm the area is back in a state of control.

12.0 停機後的復工程序

停機（Shutdown）是指計劃性或非計劃性的作業停止，可能危及潔淨區的環境狀態。類型包括：

  • 計劃性停機：預防性維護（PM）、建設活動、排定的廠區假期
  • 非計劃性停機：突發停電、HVAC 故障、緊急停線

停機的時長與範圍決定引入的活性與非活性污染水準。無論何種類型，復工前必須執行明確的行動程序。

復工受控標準（Return-to-Control Criteria）

  1. 確認控制系統正常運作：在啟動任何清潔作業前，確認 HVAC / 空調、壓差及所有環境控制系統已恢復正常。
  2. 先用清潔劑或水進行預清潔：移除停機期間積累的灰塵、污垢或建設殘屑。消毒劑無法有效穿透大量有機污染物。
  3. 按已建立的程序執行消毒：應用已驗證的消毒程序，使生物負荷降至可接受水準。必須涵蓋所有表面——牆壁、地板及設備表面。
  4. 盡可能採用現場（In-situ）數據支持：多步驟清潔與消毒方案應有數據佐證，證明其能將設施恢復至受控狀態。
  5. 恢復生產前進行環境監測：清潔消毒完成後，對活性和非活性微粒進行取樣。評估結果確認在控制限度內後，方可重新投入生產。

核心原則

復工決定必須以數據為依據。環境監測結果——而非僅憑目視檢查或清潔時程的遵守——才能確認區域已回到受控狀態。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

在 TWi，任何非計劃性停機（如停電或 HVAC 故障）都需要啟動偏差調查。復工程序必須在偏差報告中記錄，包括：停機原因、持續時間、採取的清潔消毒措施、EM 監測結果，以及批准復工的授權人員。

若停機期間發生建設或維修活動，預清潔步驟尤為關鍵——施工塵埃是孢子的潛在載體。

13.0 Hold Times for Cleaned Areas, Non-Product-Contact Equipment, and Utensils

13.0 Hold Times for Cleaned Areas

After an area or facility has been cleaned and disinfected, a maximum hold time must be established — the maximum allowable time between the completion of cleaning/disinfection and the resumption of production activities, during which the area is considered to remain in a "clean" state.

Hold Time Study Requirements

Studies must be performed to establish and validate the maximum hold time. Key elements:

  • Sampling timepoints: Viable and non-viable sampling is performed immediately after cleaning and disinfection, and again at or beyond the maximum proposed hold time limit (e.g., at 24h and 48h).
  • Controlled entry: Entrance into the area after cleaning should be limited to reduce artificial contamination during the study.
  • Representative conditions: The study should include the normal level of non-production activities that would typically occur in the area during the hold period (e.g., routine checks, maintenance pass-throughs).
  • Equipment and utensils: Hold times for non-product-contact equipment and utensils must also be established and validated. These materials must be stored in a manner that maintains their integrity for the duration of the established hold time.

Exceeding Hold Times

If the hold time is exceeded — for areas, non-product-contact equipment, or utensils — they must be cleaned and disinfected again before use. This is a non-negotiable requirement. Exceeding hold time without re-cleaning constitutes a potential GMP deviation.

Common Industry Hold Time Limits

While specific limits must be established for each facility based on its own validation data, common industry benchmarks include:

  • 24-hour hold time for Grade A/B areas after cleaning
  • 48-hour hold time for Grade C areas
  • 72-hour or weekly hold times for Grade D areas (depending on risk)

These are illustrative examples only — each site must validate its own limits.

13.0 清潔後保留時間

設施或區域完成清潔與消毒後，必須建立最大保留時間（Hold Time）——即清潔消毒完成後至恢復生產前，該區域仍被視為「清潔狀態」的最長允許時間。

Hold Time 研究要求

必須執行研究以建立並驗證最大保留時間。關鍵要素：

  • 取樣時間點：於清潔消毒後立即取樣，並在達到（或超過）建議最大時限時再次取樣（如 24h 和 48h）。
  • 限制入室：研究期間應限制人員進入清潔後區域，以避免人為引入污染影響結果。
  • 代表性條件：研究應模擬保留期間通常會發生的非生產活動（如例行檢查、維護通行），使研究條件具代表性。
  • 設備與器具：非接觸產品的設備和器具的 Hold Time 同樣需要建立和驗證。儲存方式必須確保在 Hold Time 期間維持清潔狀態的完整性。

超過 Hold Time 的處理

若區域、非接觸產品設備或器具超過已建立的 Hold Time，必須重新進行清潔與消毒，方可使用。這是不可妥協的要求。未重新清潔即繼續使用，構成潛在的 GMP 偏差（Deviation）。

業界常見 Hold Time 基準

雖然各設施必須依據自身驗證數據建立各自的限度，以下為常見的業界參考值：

  • Grade A/B 區域：清潔後 24 小時
  • Grade C 區域：清潔後 48 小時
  • Grade D 區域：72 小時或每週（視風險而定）

以上僅為示例參考值，各廠址必須驗證自己的限度。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

在 TWi，Hold Time 超標直接觸發偏差（Deviation）流程。操作員必須了解每個區域和每類設備的 Hold Time 限度，並在生產批次記錄（BPR）中記錄清潔時間。

稽查員重點檢查項目：

  • Hold Time 驗證研究報告是否存在且包含合適的取樣時間點
  • BPR 中是否記錄清潔完成時間及使用前確認
  • 超時偏差是否被正確記錄並完成 CAPA
14.0 Training

14.0 Training

Personnel must be properly trained on the SOPs governing the cleaning and disinfection program as well as the specific techniques used. Because most cleaning and disinfection processes are manual in nature and their outcome (contamination reduction) depends on execution quality, training is a critical factor in program success.

Training Scope — Required Topics

  • Basic microbiology — understanding what microorganisms are and how they behave in a controlled environment
  • Contamination sources and risks — personnel, materials, equipment, and environment as contamination vectors
  • Facility design and airflow — how cleanroom classifications and unidirectional airflow protect product
  • Gowning — correct gowning technique, order of gowning, and common errors
  • Clean room behavior and personal hygiene — movement, communication, and hygiene standards within classified areas
  • Basic environmental monitoring — what is being measured, why, and how to interpret results
  • Aspects of a cleaning program — objectives, agents, procedures, and frequency
  • Aspects of a disinfection program — agent selection, contact time, rotation rationale
  • Relevant SOPs — all procedures directly governing the individual's tasks

Trainer and Trainee Scope

Both the individuals performing cleaning and disinfection activities and their supervisors must be trained. Supervisors need sufficient knowledge to verify correct technique and recognize deviations.

Training Documentation and Requalification

  • Documentation: All training must be documented. Records typically include: trainee name, trainer name, training date, topic/SOP number, method (read, observed, performed), and assessment result.
  • Competency assessment: Training effectiveness should be evaluated through direct observation of technique and/or written testing.
  • Periodic re-administration: Training must be re-administered on a periodic basis. Annual requalification is the industry norm.
  • Change-triggered retraining: Any change to a cleaning or disinfection SOP, agent, or procedure requires retraining of affected personnel before implementation.

14.0 人員培訓

人員必須在清潔與消毒相關 SOP 及具體操作技術上接受適當培訓。由於大多數清潔消毒作業是手工操作，其效果（污染削減）完全取決於執行品質，培訓是計劃成功的關鍵因素。

培訓範疇 — 必要主題

  • 基礎微生物學：了解微生物的特性及其在潔淨環境中的行為
  • 污染來源與風險：人員、物料、設備和環境作為污染媒介的機制
  • 設施設計與氣流：潔淨區等級分類和單向氣流如何保護產品
  • 更衣（Gowning）：正確的更衣技術、更衣順序及常見錯誤
  • 潔淨室行為與個人衛生：潔淨區內的行動規範、溝通方式和個人衛生標準
  • 環境監測基礎：監測什麼、為何監測，以及如何解讀結果
  • 清潔計劃要素：目標、藥劑、程序和頻率
  • 消毒計劃要素：藥劑選擇、接觸時間、輪替原理
  • 相關 SOP：所有直接規範個人工作任務的程序文件

受訓人員範圍

執行清潔消毒作業的人員以及其主管均必須接受培訓。主管需具備足夠知識，以驗證技術正確性並識別偏差。

培訓文件與定期再資格認證

  • 文件記錄：所有培訓必須有文件記錄，通常包括：受訓者姓名、訓練者姓名、培訓日期、主題 / SOP 編號、方式（閱讀、觀察、實操），以及評估結果。
  • 能力評估（Competency Assessment）：應通過直接觀察操作技術和/或書面測驗評估培訓效果。
  • 定期再培訓：培訓必須定期重新進行，業界常規為每年再資格認證（Annual Requalification）。
  • 變更觸發再培訓：任何清潔消毒 SOP、藥劑或程序的變更，都需要在實施前對相關人員進行再培訓。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

稽查員幾乎必然審查操作員培訓記錄（Training Records）。在 TWi，需確保：

  • 所有清潔消毒相關 SOP 的 OJT（On-the-Job Training）記錄齊全，且由合格訓練者簽署
  • 年度再資格認證（Annual Requalification）已按時完成，無逾期缺口
  • SOP 修訂後，相關人員的補訓記錄完整（Change-Triggered Retraining）
  • 能力評估不只是「簽名確認閱讀」，而是包含觀察評估或書面測驗

培訓記錄的缺口（gaps）是 FDA 和 EMA 稽查中最常見的 483 觀察項目之一。

Section 5 Summary

This section covers the operational and programmatic elements that determine whether a cleaning and disinfection program sustains its effectiveness over time. The five topics are interconnected:

Key Takeaways

  • Frequency (10.0): The risk-based model is the industry best practice for selecting cleaning frequency. Frequency must be supported by ongoing EM data and adjusted when data trends indicate declining efficacy. Event-based triggers (EM excursions, shutdowns, HEPA failures) require additional cleaning beyond routine schedules.
  • Resistance & Rotation (11.0): True disinfectant resistance is scientifically unlikely in cleanrooms. The current best practice is rotating a disinfectant with a sporicide — not rotating multiple disinfectants. All rotation programs must be evaluated using EM data and risk assessment.
  • Shutdown (12.0): After any shutdown, HVAC must be verified first, followed by pre-cleaning with detergent, formal disinfection, and EM monitoring — in that order — before resuming operations.
  • Hold Times (13.0): Hold times for areas and equipment must be validated through EM studies. Exceeding a hold time without re-cleaning constitutes a GMP deviation.
  • Training (14.0): All cleaning and disinfection personnel and their supervisors must be trained on a defined curriculum. Competency must be assessed (not just documented). Annual requalification is required, and SOPs changes trigger retraining.

The Underlying Theme

A cleaning and disinfection program is not a static document — it is a living system. Frequency, agent selection, rotation schedules, hold times, and training records must all be reviewed regularly using EM data as the primary indicator of program effectiveness.

第五節 總結

本節涵蓋決定清潔消毒計劃能否持續有效運作的作業與管理要素。五個主題相互關聯：

核心要點

  • 頻率（10.0）：風險模型法是選擇清潔頻率的業界最佳實務。頻率必須由持續的 EM 數據支持，並在數據趨勢顯示效能下降時調整。事件觸發（EM 超標、停機、HEPA 故障）需要在例行時程外增加清潔。
  • 抗藥性與輪替（11.0）：在潔淨室中，消毒劑的真實抗藥性在科學上不太可能發生。目前最佳實務是以消毒劑搭配孢子殺滅劑輪替，而非多種消毒劑輪換。所有輪替計劃必須結合 EM 數據和風險評估進行驗證。
  • 停機復工（12.0）：任何停機後，必須依序完成：確認 HVAC 正常 → 清潔劑預清潔 → 正式消毒 → EM 監測，確認受控後方可復工。
  • 保留時間（13.0）：區域和設備的 Hold Time 必須通過 EM 研究驗證。超過 Hold Time 未重新清潔即使用，構成 GMP 偏差。
  • 培訓（14.0）：所有清潔消毒人員及其主管必須完成既定課程的培訓，能力評估不能僅靠書面簽名確認。每年再資格認證為必要要求，SOP 變更須觸發再培訓。

深層主題

清潔消毒計劃不是一份靜態文件，而是一個動態系統。頻率、藥劑選擇、輪替時程、保留時間和培訓記錄，都必須定期審查，以 EM 數據作為計劃有效性的主要指標。

15.0 Overview — Linking EM Excursions to Cleaning Failures

Cleaning and disinfection programs and practices that are not followed can result in unacceptable microbial levels in areas or on equipment within the facility. Investigations related to negative shifts, excursions, or trends in the Environmental Monitoring (EM) data should include a review of the cleaning and disinfection program.

Additional information on investigating EM excursions can be found in PDA Technical Report No. 13 (Revised 2014): Fundamentals of an Environmental Monitoring Program.

15.0 概覽——EM 超標與清潔失效的連結

清潔消毒程式若未確實執行，將導致設施內特定區域或設備表面的微生物計數超標。凡出現 環境監測（Environmental Monitoring, EM） 負向趨勢、超標（Excursion）或數據偏移，調查時必須納入「清潔消毒程式檢討」作為必要項目。

在調查可存活污染物時，菌種類型本身即是指向污染來源的重要線索：

菌種溯源速查

  • 革蘭氏陽性球菌 (Gram-positive cocci) / 非孢子桿菌：最可能來自人員（皮膚脫屑、呼吸道）。
  • 革蘭氏陽性桿菌 (Gram-positive rods) & 真菌 (Fungi)：最可能來自外部環境，包括空氣、土壤及設施夾層空間。
  • 革蘭氏陰性桿菌 (Gram-negative rods)：最可能與水或液體來源相關，例如排水、拖把桶、噴霧容器。

CDMO 實務提示

EM 陽性不代表產品一定受污染，但必須觸發調查。菌種比對是縮小根本原因範圍的第一步——若分離菌為革蘭氏陰性，應優先審查水系統、噴霧瓶管理與濕區清潔規程。

15.1 Investigation Areas — Cleaning Program Failures

When reviewing the cleaning and disinfection program as part of an investigation, areas that should be considered based on available data include but are not limited to:

Agent & Surface Factors
  • Antimicrobial chemical agent residue buildup and soil not adequately removed by cleaning, thus preventing adequate disinfection
  • Inappropriate application of antimicrobial chemical agents
  • Insufficient contact times on surfaces
  • Incorrect selection of agents in relation to the organisms found
  • Expired solution
  • Incorrectly prepared solutions
Equipment & Tool Factors
  • Inappropriate decontamination of components or bagging before transfer to the controlled area
  • Use of inadequate clean room tools
  • High bioburden or shedding from inappropriate cleaning apparatus
Procedural / Human Factors
  • Lack of adherence to established cleaning and disinfection procedures

15.1 調查重點領域——清潔程式失效原因

在調查過程中審查清潔消毒程式時，應依現有數據考量下列各面向（不限於此）：

消毒劑與表面因素
  • 殘留消毒劑積累或表面汙垢未被有效清除，導致後續消毒效能降低
  • 消毒劑施用方式不當（例如劑量不足、噴塗不均）
  • 表面接觸時間（Contact Time）不足，未達驗證值
  • 消毒劑選擇與分離菌種不匹配（例如對特定孢子無效）
  • 使用過期溶液
  • 稀釋配製錯誤（濃度不足或過高）
設備與清潔工具因素
  • 物料或包裝袋轉移進入受控區前未適當去污（Decontamination）
  • 使用不適合潔凈室環境的清潔工具
  • 清潔裝置本身生物負載過高或脫屑（Shedding）
程式 / 人為因素
  • 未遵守已建立的清潔消毒 SOP（Standard Operating Procedure）

根本原因分析（RCA）要問的關鍵問題

用錯藥劑？接觸時間不夠？技術不對？還是遺漏了某個區域？——系統性地逐一排除，才能找到真正的失效點。

15.2 Investigation Checklist Questions

Equipment Related

  • For area disinfection (floors, walls, countertops), is the disinfection equipment clean and dry before each use? In Grade A/B (ISO 5/6) areas, is all equipment including tanks and tubing sterilized before each use?
  • Is equipment stored properly in a clean area and covered until use?
  • For transfer disinfection, are spray containers either single use or properly cleaned before reuse?
  • Are carts used for transport properly disinfected, including the wheels?

Materials and Solutions Related

  • Is cleaning and disinfection performed starting from the cleanest area, progressing to the dirtiest?
  • Are disinfection efficacy studies available at the site, including methods and expiry dating, as well as any predominant site isolates?
  • Are antimicrobial chemical agents adequately rotated with use of a sporicide to maintain low levels of spore-forming organisms without causing surface erosion from overuse?
  • Is the agent applied such that surfaces remain wet for the required contact time, yet not so wet that puddles remain — which may allow non-fermenting gram-negative organisms to proliferate?
  • Are in-situ studies available demonstrating effectiveness of the site's restart disinfection program (after construction, power outage, or prolonged shutdown)?
  • Do certificates of analysis on agents show any changes?
  • If the agent is sterile-filtered in-house, do sterile filtration records and bioburden data reveal any issues?

Personnel / Training

  • Are accurate and complete SOPs in place and available?
  • Are operators trained and qualified on agent application, including contact time and residual removal?
  • Have agents been prepared properly?
  • Are personnel instructed not to enter disinfected areas until after the contact time has been exceeded?
  • Are construction areas properly segregated from in-service areas to prevent cross-contamination?
  • Are clean room staff trained in and exhibiting consistently good aseptic technique?

15.2 調查查核清單問題

設備相關

  • 地板、牆面、工作台等區域消毒前，消毒設備是否清潔乾燥？A/B 級（ISO 5/6）區域的罐體、管路是否在每次使用前經過滅菌（Sterilize）？
  • 設備儲存是否放置於清潔區域並加蓋保護至使用前？
  • 轉移消毒用噴霧瓶是否為一次性使用或使用前正確清潔？
  • 運輸推車（包括輪子）是否正確消毒？

物料與溶液相關

  • 清潔消毒是否從最潔淨區域朝最髒區域方向進行？
  • 現場是否備有消毒效能研究資料，涵蓋方法、有效期及主要場所分離菌種？
  • 消毒劑輪換計劃是否包含孢子殺滅劑（Sporicide），以控制孢子形成菌，同時避免過度使用造成表面侵蝕？
  • 施用時是否確保表面保持濕潤至驗證接觸時間，但不積水（積水可能使革蘭氏陰性非發酵菌大量繁殖）？
  • 現場是否備有「重啟消毒程式」的現場效能研究資料（如施工後、停電後或長期停產後）？
  • 消毒劑批次分析證書（Certificate of Analysis）是否出現規格或成分變化？
  • 若現場自行無菌過濾消毒劑，滅菌過濾記錄與生物負載數據是否顯示異常？

人員 / 訓練相關

  • SOP 是否完整、正確且易於取得？
  • 操作人員是否已受訓並取得消毒劑施用（含接觸時間及殘留去除）的資格認證（Qualification）？
  • 消毒劑是否按規定正確配製？
  • 是否明確告知人員：消毒後須等接觸時間結束才能進入？
  • 施工區是否與在用區域妥善隔離以防止交叉污染？
  • 潔凈室人員是否持續展現良好的無菌操作技術（Aseptic Technique）？

實務思考：調查三步驟

Step 1 — 菌種識別：確認分離菌種類型，推斷污染來源方向（人員 / 環境 / 水）。

Step 2 — 時序比對：比對 EM 超標日期與清潔紀錄、人員更動、施工活動、物料批次。

Step 3 — 現場驗證：對可疑區域進行 EM 加強採樣，確認消毒劑效能及接觸時間是否符合要求。

16.0 Conclusion — The Role of Cleaning and Disinfection

A robust contamination control system starts with controlling contamination from entering classified areas. Such a system stages items from the exterior environment to be cleaned, disinfected, and sterilized prior to entry. Without a system for controlling contamination from entering, loss of control of environmental conditions is very likely.

Cleaning complements the control system as the blockage of all contamination from entering is extremely difficult. Cleaning with a non-abrasive action and subsequent lifting of dirtied soils, liquids, particulates, and microbes prepares the surface to be characteristically lower in soil/residue and bioburden, making disinfection of what remains a simpler and more successful process.

The disinfection of areas utilizing a validated agent corrects the errors that have occurred during the control and cleaning process.

"Cleaning and disinfection of classified areas is not a preventative measure but rather a corrective action procedure done to equalize the failures of the control system."

Many pertinent details defined in this technical report combine together to help provide the opportunity for success, which is measured as consistent acceptable environmental conditions. Consistent control is the ultimate goal.

This technical report is not intended to replace any existing requirements or standards; it is a best practice reference document regarding the fundamentals of cleaning and disinfection.

16.0 結論——清潔消毒的系統定位

健全的污染控制系統從「防止污染進入分級區域」開始建立。這套系統要求所有來自外部環境的物品，在進入前必須完成清潔、消毒乃至滅菌的分段處理。若缺乏進入管控機制，環境條件失控的風險將極高。

清潔是污染控制的補充機制——因為要完全阻絕污染進入幾乎不可能。清潔的作用是以非磨損方式去除汙垢、液體、微粒及微生物，降低表面生物負載，讓後續消毒能以更低的起始菌數、更高的成功率完成任務。

消毒則是針對清潔後仍殘留的微生物，使用已驗證藥劑進行最終修正（Corrective Action）。

核心觀念重構

很多人誤以為清潔消毒是「預防措施」。TR70 明確指出：它是糾正性措施（Corrective Action Procedure）——是在污染控制系統已出現失效後，用來補救的程序。

因此，最終目標不是「清潔消毒做得完美」，而是「污染控制體系持續一致、環境條件穩定達標」。

本報告的定位

TR70 是最佳實務參考文件（Best Practice Reference Document），並非取代現行法規或標準。在 CDMO 應用中，應與客戶 Site Master File 及相關 GMP 要求並行使用。

Historical Overview

A disinfectant is a substance that kills microorganisms — bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens — on inanimate objects. People were routinely killing microorganisms long before they knew of their existence. Ancient Egyptians, Persians, and Chinese used wine, pine pitch, copper, silver, and mercury as early disinfectants — all toxic at higher concentrations but useful at lower dosages for preventing rot and spoilage.

The following timeline traces key milestones in the science of disinfection.

歷史概覽

消毒劑（Disinfectant）是指能在無生命物體表面殺滅微生物（細菌、病毒及其他致病菌）的物質。人類在尚未了解微生物存在之前，便已在實踐消毒行為。古埃及、波斯、中國人使用葡萄酒、松脂、銅、銀及汞作為早期消毒劑——高濃度時有毒，但低劑量下可有效防止腐敗。

Key Milestones — Disinfection Technology

~800 BC
Greek poet Homer records first intentional use of sulfur dioxide (SO₂) as a disinfectant / fumigant.
AD 500
Hindu physician Sushruta Samhita uses fumigation and disinfecting vapors. Venetian cargo ships fumigated to control disease.
Mid-1600s
Anton van Leeuwenhoek perfects the microscope — first person to view bacteria ("animalcules"). He discovered that pepper, vinegar, and other chemicals killed them, making him the first person to knowingly disinfect with a chemical substance.
~1600s
Sir Francis Bacon experiments with substances preventing putrefaction — astringents, acids, salt, sugar, lack of oxygen.
Mid-1700s
Sir John Pringle develops a coefficient table comparing antiseptic solutions, using salt as the standard. Possibly the first to call these chemicals "antiseptics."
~1770
Carl Wilhelm Scheele discovers chlorine. Calcium hypochlorite later used in hospitals, sewers, and stables. During WWI, 0.5% sodium hypochlorite & alkali used to disinfect wounds. Chloride salts still used widely in water treatment today.
1830
Tincture of iodine enters the US Pharmacopeia (USP); used to treat wounds during the Civil War.
1832
Carl Ludwig von Reichenbach discovers creosote (phenols from beech tar) — initially used as a deodorant. Alkylphenols from coal tar later emulsified with soap and marketed as Lysol.
~1860s
Louis Pasteur advocates heat to destroy invisible disease-causing microbes. Joseph Lister, inspired by Pasteur, uses carbolic acid (phenol) to prevent wound infection — a potent germicide that can kill spores, though toxic at full strength.
1879
Joseph Lawrence (inspired by Lister's talk in the US) develops Listerine. Robert Johnson (also inspired) selects phenols for surgical wound dressings — founding Johnson & Johnson.
Late 1800s
Robert Koch conclusively demonstrates that bacteria cause disease in live tissues — establishing the germ theory of disease and revolutionizing the scientific basis for disinfection.

Appendix II: Registration of Sanitizers and Disinfectants

Appendix II — Regulatory Registration Overview

Disinfectants and sanitizers used in pharmaceutical and food-drug manufacturing facilities must comply with applicable registration requirements. These requirements differ significantly between the US and EU regulatory frameworks.

United States — US EPA Registration

In the United States, antimicrobial pesticide products — including disinfectants and sanitizers used in drug manufacturing facilities — are regulated under the Federal Insecticide, Fungicide, and Rodenticide Act (FIFRA), administered by the US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).

  • All disinfectants used in pharmaceutical and food/drug manufacturing environments must hold a valid US EPA Registration Number.
  • The EPA registration process requires efficacy data demonstrating the product kills claimed organisms under labeled conditions, including contact time and concentration.
  • Manufacturers must provide the EPA-registered product label; claims must not exceed label specifications.
  • State-level registration may also be required in certain US states beyond federal EPA registration.

European Union — Biocidal Products Regulation (BPR)

In the EU, disinfectants used in aseptic manufacturing facilities are regulated under Regulation (EU) No 528/2012 — the Biocidal Products Regulation (BPR), administered at the EU level by the European Chemicals Agency (ECHA) with national competent authorities.

  • All biocidal active substances must be approved at the EU level before products containing them can be authorized in Member States.
  • Product Authorization (PA) is required in each Member State where the product will be placed on the market, unless Mutual Recognition procedures are used.
  • Product type classification (e.g., PT2 — disinfectants for private and public health areas) determines the scope and use claims of the authorization.
  • Transitional measures apply for existing products while active substance reviews are ongoing.

附錄 II——消毒劑法規登記制度概述

製藥及食品藥品製造設施所使用的消毒劑與衛生劑，必須符合各市場的登記要求。美國與歐盟的監管框架存在顯著差異。

美國——US EPA 農藥登記制度

在美國，用於藥品製造設施的消毒劑屬於「抗菌農藥產品（Antimicrobial Pesticide Products）」，受《聯邦殺蟲劑、殺菌劑及滅鼠劑法》（FIFRA）管轄，由美國環境保護署（US EPA）執行。

  • 所有用於製藥及食品藥品環境的消毒劑，必須持有有效的 US EPA 登記編號（EPA Registration Number）。
  • EPA 登記程序要求提供效能數據，證明產品在標籤規定的條件下（含接觸時間與濃度）能殺滅所聲稱的微生物。
  • 製造商必須提供 EPA 登記標籤；所有聲稱的用途不得超出標籤規定範圍。
  • 部分美國州別在聯邦 EPA 登記之外，還要求另行辦理州級登記。

歐盟——生物殺滅產品法規（BPR）

在歐盟，無菌製造設施所使用的消毒劑受第 528/2012 號法規——生物殺滅產品法規（Biocidal Products Regulation, BPR）管轄，由歐洲化學品管理局（ECHA）在歐盟層級推進，各會員國主管機關負責具體授權。

  • 所有生物殺滅活性物質（Active Substance）必須先在歐盟層級獲得批准，含有該物質的產品才能在會員國申請授權。
  • 產品授權（Product Authorization, PA）需在計劃上市的每個會員國分別取得，或透過互認程序（Mutual Recognition）簡化流程。
  • 產品類型（Product Type, PT）分類（如 PT2——私人及公共健康區域消毒劑）決定了授權範圍與使用聲稱。
  • 現行產品在活性物質審查期間，適用過渡性措施。

CDMO 合規提示

作為接受合約製造的 CDMO，TWi 必須確認其使用的每一種消毒劑，在每個生產發生的市場（美國、歐盟、台灣等）均取得合法登記或授權。消毒劑若未依法登記，在 FDA 或 EMA 查廠時可能被視為 cGMP 缺陷。

Appendix III: US Regulatory Overview

美國消毒劑監管框架：FDA、EPA、USDA  |  p54–p55

Appendix III — US Regulatory Landscape for Disinfectants

In the United States, multiple federal agencies have overlapping but distinct authority over disinfectants and sanitizers used in pharmaceutical manufacturing settings. Understanding which agency regulates which product type is essential for compliance.

Key US Compliance Points for Pharma Disinfectants

  • EPA-registered products must be used according to label directions — concentration, contact time, and application method must match label claims.
  • FDA 21 CFR Part 211.56 (Sanitation) requires facilities to establish a written sanitation program using EPA-registered disinfectants.
  • Disinfectant qualification studies (efficacy, in-situ testing) are required by FDA to demonstrate fitness for purpose in the specific facility environment.
  • Sporicide use rotation is an industry best practice recommended to prevent adaptation of spore-forming organisms.
  • For sterile drug product manufacturing, disinfectant solutions intended for use in Grade A/B areas must themselves be sterile (sterile-filtered or purchased sterile).

附錄 III——美國消毒劑監管框架

美國有多個聯邦機構對製藥設施消毒劑擁有交疊但有所區別的監管權限。了解「哪個機構管哪類產品」是合規的基本前提。

美國藥廠消毒劑合規要點

  • EPA 登記產品必須依標籤指示使用——濃度、接觸時間及施用方式均不得偏離標籤規定。
  • FDA 21 CFR Part 211.56（衛生管理）要求設施建立書面衛生程式，使用 EPA 登記消毒劑。
  • FDA 要求提供消毒劑資格確認研究（效能驗證、現場應用測試），以證明其在具體設施環境中的適用性。
  • 輪換使用殺孢子劑（Sporicide）是業界最佳實務，以防止孢子形成菌產生適應性。
  • 用於 A/B 級區域的消毒劑溶液本身必須是無菌的（無菌過濾製備或購買已滅菌產品）。

EPA vs. FDA：誰管哪個？

EPA 管「產品登記」——這瓶消毒劑能不能合法上市、聲稱哪些殺菌效果。

FDA 管「使用合規」——藥廠要能證明你用的消毒劑在你的設施、你的條件下有效，且有文件記錄。

兩者都要滿足，缺一不可。

Appendix IV: EU Regulatory Overview

Appendix IV — EU Regulatory Landscape for Disinfectants

The EU regulatory framework for disinfectants used in pharmaceutical aseptic manufacturing involves several overlapping regulatory instruments. Unlike the US system where EPA and FDA have clearly delineated roles, the EU system requires compliance with both the product-authorization pathway (BPR) and the GMP framework.

Key EU Compliance Points for Pharma Disinfectants

  • BPR authorization must be obtained in each Member State where the product is used (or via EU Mutual Recognition / Union Authorization for harmonized products).
  • EU GMP Annex 1 (2022 revision) significantly expanded Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) requirements — disinfectant programs must be part of the documented CCS.
  • Disinfectant qualification (in-use testing per EN standards or equivalent) is expected; contact time, temperature, and concentration must be validated for the specific surface and microbial challenge.
  • Sporicides must be used periodically in rotation to prevent spore-forming organism proliferation — now explicitly addressed in Annex 1.
  • Disinfectant solutions used in Grade A (ISO 5) and Grade B (ISO 6) environments must be sterile; this requirement is strictly enforced during EU GMP inspections.
  • Import and transfer of biocidal products across EU borders is governed by BPR; CDMO sites manufacturing for EU markets must verify their supply chain compliance.

附錄 IV——歐盟消毒劑監管框架

歐盟針對無菌製藥設施消毒劑的監管，涉及多個交疊的法規框架。與美國 EPA 和 FDA 職責相對明確不同，歐盟系統要求同時符合產品授權路徑（BPR）和 GMP 框架的要求。

歐盟藥廠消毒劑合規要點

  • BPR 授權須在每個使用市場的會員國分別取得（或透過互認程序 / 歐盟統一授權取得跨境效力）。
  • 2022 年修訂的 EU GMP 附件 1 大幅擴展了污染控制策略（CCS）要求——消毒劑計劃必須納入書面化的 CCS 文件。
  • 消毒劑資格確認（依 EN 標準或等效方法進行現場使用測試）是必要的；接觸時間、溫度及濃度須針對特定表面和微生物挑戰進行驗證。
  • 殺孢子劑（Sporicide）必須定期輪換使用，以防止孢子形成菌增殖——此要求在 2022 年附件 1 中已明確規定。
  • 用於 A 級（ISO 5）及 B 級（ISO 6）環境的消毒劑溶液必須為無菌；此要求在歐盟 GMP 查廠中嚴格執行。
  • 生物殺滅產品在歐盟各國之間的進口與流通受 BPR 管轄；為歐盟市場生產的 CDMO 廠點，必須確認其供應鏈合規性。

BPR vs. GMP 附件 1：雙軌合規

BPR 管「產品能否銷售使用」——消毒劑本身的上市授權，是否有活性物質批准、產品授權號。

GMP 附件 1 管「如何在 GMP 環境中使用」——效能驗證、輪換計劃、無菌製備、納入 CCS。

兩套要求必須同時滿足；通過 BPR 授權不自動符合 GMP 效能驗證要求。

Section 6a 學習摘要 — Investigations, Conclusion & Appendices I–IV

  • EM 調查框架：EM 超標必須觸發對清潔消毒程式的系統性調查；菌種識別是縮小根本原因範圍的第一步——革蘭氏陽性菌指向人員，革蘭氏陽性桿菌 / 真菌指向外部環境，革蘭氏陰性菌指向水源。
  • 調查查核清單：從設備管理（噴霧瓶、拖把、推車）、溶液管理（有效期、配製、接觸時間）到人員訓練（SOP 遵守、無菌技術），三大面向均需納入調查。
  • 清潔消毒的本質定位：TR70 明確指出清潔消毒是「糾正性措施」而非「預防性措施」——是在污染控制系統失效後的補救程序；一致、穩定的環境條件才是最終目標。
  • 消毒史里程碑：從荷馬時代的 SO₂ 薰蒸，到雷文霍克首次以化學物質刻意殺菌，到 19 世紀李斯特（Lister）確立消毒傷口原則、巴斯德 / 科赫建立微生物致病理論，奠定現代消毒科學基礎。
  • 美國 EPA 登記：藥廠使用的消毒劑受 FIFRA 管轄，須持有 EPA 登記號；FDA cGMP（21 CFR 211）另要求提供效能驗證文件。A/B 級消毒劑溶液本身須為無菌。
  • 歐盟 BPR + GMP 附件 1 雙軌合規：BPR 管消毒劑上市授權（以活性物質批准為前提，再取得各會員國產品授權）；GMP 附件 1（2022）要求消毒劑納入 CCS、完成效能驗證、定期輪換殺孢子劑，A/B 級用溶液須無菌。
  • CDMO 合規義務：在不同市場生產的 CDMO 必須確認每一種使用中的消毒劑，在各生產市場均取得合法登記或授權；登記缺失可能在 FDA / EMA 查廠中被認定為 cGMP 偏差。

Overview: How EPA Safety Labels Are Determined

Labeling requirements for antimicrobial products vary by country. For products registered in the United States, the EPA provides prescriptive precautionary label language. This language is determined by the results of six acute toxicity studies performed on the product formulation:

  • Acute oral toxicity — systemic toxicity via ingestion
  • Acute dermal toxicity — systemic toxicity via skin absorption
  • Acute inhalation toxicity (4-hour exposure) — systemic toxicity via breathing
  • Primary eye irritation — irritation or corrosion of ocular tissue
  • Primary skin irritation — tissue destruction or erythema
  • Dermal sensitization — potential for allergic contact dermatitis

With the exception of dermal sensitization, each study is assigned to a Toxicity Category (I–IV) based on study results. Category I is most hazardous; Category IV is least hazardous.

EPA 安全標示制度概述

美國 EPA 要求所有登記的抗菌產品（包括清潔劑、消毒劑、殺孢劑）在上市前完成六項急毒性研究，並根據結果將每項研究分入 毒性分類（Toxicity Category） I–IV：

  • 急性口服毒性（Acute oral toxicity）
  • 急性皮膚吸收毒性（Acute dermal toxicity）
  • 急性吸入毒性（Acute inhalation toxicity，4小時）
  • 原發性眼刺激（Primary eye irritation）
  • 原發性皮膚刺激（Primary skin irritation）
  • 皮膚致敏性（Dermal sensitization）

除致敏性外，其他五項均按結果分類。分類決定標籤上的警示語與 PPE 要求。Category I = 最危險；Category IV = 最低危害。

實務重點

供應商 SDS（Safety Data Sheet）必須反映 EPA 毒性分類。採購時應索取原廠 EPA 登記標籤，確認分類與 PPE 規格是否與 SOP 一致。

Appendix VI: AOAC Protocol Testing for Disinfectant Registration (Section 22.0)

Overview: AOAC and EPA Registration

Firms registering antimicrobial products are required by the U.S. EPA to submit detailed information including:

  • Formula and chemical/physical characteristics of the formulation
  • Effectiveness data supporting claims against specific microorganisms
  • Safety / toxicity data

The AOAC International (Association of Official Analytical Collaboration) develops standardized methods accepted by the EPA for this testing. While EPA registration requires these methods, they should not be considered a mandatory requirement for GMP operations. GMP facilities do not utilize AOAC protocols for their own antimicrobial effectiveness programs; however, verifying that a supplier holds U.S. EPA approval is part of the disinfectant selection process.

See www.eoma.aoac.org for specific AOAC methods.

EPA Label Claim Categories

The general efficacy label claims recognized by EPA are:

  • Sporicide (Cold Sterilant) — eliminates all forms of microbial life including fungi, viruses, bacteria, and spores. Spores are the most resistant; sporicide = cold sterilant in EPA terminology.
  • Disinfectant — eliminates or irreversibly inactivates infectious bacteria (not necessarily spores). Divided into: Hospital Use and General Use categories.
  • Fungicide — reduces microorganisms on inanimate surfaces to safe levels per public health codes.
  • Tuberculocide — destroys or irreversibly inactivates Mycobacterium tuberculosis in the inanimate environment.
  • Virucide — destroys or irreversibly inactivates viruses in the inanimate environment.

AOAC 測試體系概述

AOAC International（官方分析化學家協會）制定了一系列標準化微生物殺滅效力測試方法，是美國 EPA 接受的消毒劑登記核心工具。

AOAC 測試 ≠ GMP 效力驗證

重要區別：AOAC 測試是供應商取得 EPA 上市登記的工具，不等同於製藥廠的消毒劑驗證。GMP 設施仍需針對自身的環境分離株（site-specific isolates）進行獨立的效力確認測試（使用懸浮法或載體法）。

EPA 效力聲明分類（中文說明）

  • 殺孢劑 / 冷滅菌劑（Sporicide）：可殺滅所有微生物形態（含孢子）。在無菌製藥環境中，殺孢劑是最高等級的消毒要求，用於滅菌隔離器（isolator）或 VHP 循環等。
  • 消毒劑（Disinfectant）：殺滅營養型細菌，但不一定殺孢子。分醫院級（Hospital Use）與一般級（General Use）。醫院級需通過三菌株（S. choleraesuis、S. aureus、P. aeruginosa）測試。
  • 殺黴菌劑（Fungicide）：消滅或降低黴菌至安全水準。
  • 殺結核菌劑（Tuberculocide）：對結核分枝桿菌有效，代表對廣譜非典型分枝桿菌（如 M. chelonae）也有一定活性。
  • 殺病毒劑（Virucide）：對特定病毒有效；範疇廣（如 Adenovirus、Norovirus）。

  • Organisms: S. choleraesuis, S. aureus, and Pseudomonas aeruginosa ATCC 15442
  • Requirement: 60 carriers, three lots (one 60 days old), killing 59 out of 60 carriers per set.

  • Organism: Trichophyton mentagrophytes ATCC 9533

AOAC 測試方法詳解（中文）

AOAC Use-Dilution Method（使用稀釋法）

最常用的 EPA 登記測試之一。將標準菌株（S. aureus、S. choleraesuis、P. aeruginosa）接種於金屬載體（penicylinder），暴露於使用濃度的消毒劑一定時間後，轉移至培養基培養，計算殺滅率。

判定標準：60 個載體中至少 59 個無菌生長（≥ 98.3% 殺滅率）。

AOAC Sporicidal Test（殺孢子測試）

針對最難殺滅的芽孢形成菌（B. subtilis、C. sporogenes）設計。使用兩種截然不同的載體材質（陶瓷、絲縫線），模擬不同表面的殺孢效果。

要求：720 個載體（每批次 60 個 × 2 種載體 × 3 批次 × 2 菌株）全數無生長，標準極嚴格。

醫院級消毒劑的三菌株要求

醫院/醫療環境聲明需同時通過三種代表性菌株測試：

  • S. choleraesuis（革蘭氏陰性，代表腸桿菌科）
  • S. aureus（革蘭氏陽性，代表葡萄球菌科）
  • P. aeruginosa（革蘭氏陰性，最難殺滅的非發酵菌）

此三菌株涵蓋了臨床環境主要病原菌類型，也是製藥環境常見的環境監測菌種。

殺結核菌劑（Tuberculocide）的製藥意義

在製藥生產環境中，具有殺結核菌聲明的產品代表對廣譜分枝桿菌（Mycobacterium spp.）有效。環境監測中若發現 M. chelonae 或 M. gordonae 等非典型分枝桿菌，應優先選用具此聲明的消毒劑。

製藥環境的 AOAC 應用改良

AOAC 方法原為 EPA 登記設計，接種量通常為 106–108 CFU/mL。
製藥/生技公司通常將接種量降低至 104 CFU/mL（反映潔淨室較低的生物負荷），判定標準改為：

  • 營養型細菌（vegetative bacteria）：≥ 3-log 殺滅
  • 芽孢（spore-forming bacteria）：≥ 2-log 殺滅

此改良需在消毒劑驗證方案中明確文件化，說明偏離標準方法的理由。

批次穩定性要求

EPA 要求在測試的三批次中，有一批必須是 至少 60 天「舊」 的產品，以模擬供應鏈中產品效期的最差情況（worst-case shelf stability）。採購與 QA 在審核供應商資料時，須確認測試批次確實符合此要求。

European standard disinfection tests — EN 1276, EN 1650, EN 13697, EN 13704  |  pp. 61–62

EU Biocidal Products Regulation (BPR) and EN Tests

The EU Biocidal Products Regulation (BPR 98/8/EC) requires approval for all biocide products — including clean room disinfectants — sold in EU markets. The BPR replaces individual country registration systems for active ingredients.

A key component of BPR dossiers is the efficacy assessment. The EU Commission places EN (European Norm) test methods at the top of its preferred efficacy data list. EN tests follow a three-phase evaluation framework:

Three-Phase EN Evaluation Framework

Phase 1: Basic suspension test to determine bactericidal, fungicidal, or sporicidal activity. No specific contact time; no interfering substances required.

Phase 2: Tests for defined applications, split into two steps:

  • Step 1: Suspension test with specified contact time. Bovine serum albumin (BSA) added as an interfering substance — 0.3% for clean conditions, 3.0% for dirty conditions.
  • Step 2: Tests simulating practical conditions, e.g., surface/carrier tests.

Phase 3: Field trial tests (in-situ field studies).

Individual EN Test Standards

EN 1276:2009 — Chemical disinfectants and antiseptics.
Quantitative suspension test for bactericidal activity in food, industrial, domestic, and institutional areas (Phase 2, Step 1).

EN 1650:1998 — Chemical disinfectants and antiseptics.
Quantitative suspension test for fungicidal activity in food, industrial, domestic, and institutional areas (Phase 2, Step 1).

EN 13704:2002 — Chemical disinfectants.
Quantitative suspension test for sporicidal activity in food, industrial, domestic, and institutional areas (Phase 2, Step 1).

EN 13697:2001 — Chemical disinfectants and antiseptics.
Quantitative nonporous surface test for bactericidal or fungicidal activity in food, industrial, domestic, and institutional areas (Phase 2, Step 2).

歐盟生物殺滅劑法規（BPR）與 EN 測試體系

歐盟 生物殺滅劑產品法規（BPR, Biocidal Products Regulation 98/8/EC） 要求所有生物殺滅劑（包括潔淨室消毒劑）在歐盟市場上市前取得核准。EN 測試是歐盟核准流程中最受認可的效力測試體系。

EN 三段式評估架構

EN 測試從基礎到實際情境逐步驗證產品效力：

  • Phase 1（基礎懸浮測試）：確認基本殺菌/殺黴/殺孢活性，不要求特定接觸時間，不添加干擾物質（interfering substance）。
  • Phase 2 / Step 1（定量懸浮測試）：模擬實際使用條件，加入 牛血清白蛋白（BSA） 作為干擾物質：
    – 清潔條件（clean conditions）：0.3% BSA
    – 髒污條件（dirty conditions）：3.0% BSA
  • Phase 2 / Step 2（表面測試）：模擬實際表面（如不鏽鋼、玻璃）上的殺菌效果。
  • Phase 3（現場試驗）：in-situ 實際使用環境驗證。

主要 EN 測試標準中文說明

EN 1276：定量懸浮殺菌測試（Phase 2, Step 1）

最廣泛使用的 EN 殺菌測試。針對食品、工業、家庭及機構環境中的細菌殺滅效力進行定量評估。
判定標準：5 分鐘內對營養型細菌達到 5-log（99.999%）殺滅。

EN 1650：定量懸浮殺黴測試（Phase 2, Step 1）

與 EN 1276 平行設計，針對真菌（fungi）的效力。
判定標準：15 分鐘內達到 4-log 殺滅。

EN 13704：定量懸浮殺孢測試（Phase 2, Step 1）

針對細菌孢子（bacterial spores）的殺滅效力。孢子比營養型細菌更難殺滅，故接觸時間更長、要求 log 殺減標準較低。
判定標準：60 分鐘內達到 3-log 殺滅。

EN 13697：定量無孔表面測試（Phase 2, Step 2）

在不鏽鋼、玻璃等無孔硬質表面上（模擬潔淨室牆壁、桌面）評估殺菌或殺黴效力，更接近實際操作情境。
判定標準：細菌 5 分鐘 4-log 殺滅；黴菌 15 分鐘 3-log 殺滅。

潔淨室作業的實務含義

EN 測試中的 BSA 干擾物質（0.3% clean / 3.0% dirty）模擬了表面有有機物殘留的情況。在實際潔淨室環境中：
– 清潔步驟（cleaning）後再消毒 = 接近 clean conditions（0.3% BSA）
– 跳過清潔直接消毒 = dirty conditions（3.0% BSA）
這正是 GMP 要求「先清潔、再消毒」的科學依據之一。

Users' Protection — SDS 與安全操作

無論 AOAC 或 EN 體系，操作人員個人防護均為核心要求：

  • 稀釋消毒劑時：橡膠或丁腈手套 + 化學相容防護衣 + 橡膠圍裙
  • 消毒天花板時：防護帽/罩衫 + 密封型護目鏡（防止液體進入眼眶）
  • Fogging/gassing 作業：充分通風或呼吸裝置；揮發性成分（酒精、殺孢劑）降至 PEL 以下方可返回作業區

AOAC vs. EN Methods: Comparative Overview

Side-by-side analysis for CDMO supplier qualification  |  CDMO 供應商資格審核對照表

AOAC vs. EN: Key Differences

AOAC vs. EN 對照解讀（中文）

監管機構與適用市場

AOAC 方法服務於美國 EPA 的 FIFRA 法規框架；EN 方法服務於歐盟 BPR 法規框架。兩者均為產品在各自市場上市的必要條件，互不可代替。

測試設計邏輯差異

AOAC：以載體測試（carrier test）為主，模擬消毒劑接觸到帶菌表面（penicylinder = 醫療器材模型）後的殺菌效果，判定為通過/不通過的二元結果（59/60）。

EN：以定量懸浮測試（quantitative suspension）為主，同時使用標準化干擾物質（BSA），更系統性地模擬「清潔」與「髒污」兩種實際情況。

CDMO 供應商資格審核重點

同時供應美國與歐盟客戶的 CDMO（如 TWi）在審核消毒劑供應商時，應要求：

  • AOAC 測試數據 — 確認 EPA 登記聲明（殺孢、消毒、殺菌）
  • EN 測試數據 — 確認 BPR 法規符合性（EN 1276/EN 13697/EN 13704）
  • 雙重 標籤聲明範圍一致性 — 確保 AOAC 與 EN 聲明涵蓋相同的微生物類型

常見供應商資格缺口：聲稱「符合 GMP 要求」但僅提供其中一種測試數據，或 AOAC 聲明（如殺孢）與 EN 測試數據（無 EN 13704）不匹配。

比喻：AOAC vs. EN

AOAC 像是一張「美國駕照」——在美國可以開車上路（EPA 登記）；EN 像是「歐盟駕照」——在歐盟合法使用（BPR 登記）。持有美國駕照不能自動在歐盟使用，反之亦然。CDMO 在全球供應鏈中操作，需確保所用消毒劑「雙照俱全」。

Section Summary: Appendices V–VII

Key takeaways for aseptic manufacturing CDMO operations  |  無菌製藥 CDMO 核心要點

Summary: Appendices V–VII in Practice

Appendix V — EPA Safety Labeling

The EPA assigns antimicrobial products to Toxicity Categories I–IV based on six acute toxicity studies (oral, dermal, inhalation, eye irritation, skin irritation, dermal sensitization). Category I is most hazardous and requires the strongest precautionary language and PPE specifications on labels. GMP facilities must align their SOPs and PPE requirements to the EPA toxicity category of each disinfectant in use.

Appendix VI — AOAC Protocol Testing

AOAC methods (Use-Dilution, Sporicidal, Germicidal Spray, Fungicidal, Tuberculocidal) are the standardized U.S. EPA registration tools. They are not GMP validation requirements, but confirming supplier EPA registration (and the underlying AOAC data) is a necessary step in disinfectant selection. Pharma and biotech operations typically adapt AOAC test designs with lower inocula (104 CFU/mL) and quantitative log-reduction criteria against site-specific environmental isolates.

Appendix VII — EN Tests

EN tests operate in a three-phase framework (basic suspension → quantitative suspension with BSA → surface tests → field trials) and are the EU regulatory standard under BPR. EN 1276 (bacteria, 5-log/5 min), EN 1650 (fungi, 4-log/15 min), EN 13697 (surface, 4-log bacteria/3-log fungi), and EN 13704 (spores, 3-log/60 min) together cover the full spectrum of disinfection claims.

The CDMO Challenge

Facilities supplying both US and EU markets must verify that disinfectant suppliers provide both AOAC and EN test data. A mismatch between a claimed efficacy level and the supporting test standard is one of the most common supplier qualification gaps identified during audits.

章節總結：無菌製藥 CDMO 行動要點

附錄 V — EPA 安全標示

每種消毒劑的 EPA 毒性分類決定了標籤語言與最低 PPE 要求。CDMO 的 SOP 應明確列出每種消毒劑的毒性分類，並確保操作人員 PPE 配備符合最高分類要求。定期審查供應商標籤更新是品保職責的一部分。

附錄 VI — AOAC 測試

AOAC 測試是 EPA 登記工具，不是 GMP 驗證替代品。在供應商資格審核中，應確認：(1) EPA 登記號碼有效，(2) AOAC 測試涵蓋所需的聲明類型，(3) 測試批次包含 60 天以上的穩定性批次。對製藥廠自身的消毒劑驗證，需以 AOAC 方法為基礎，加入工廠環境分離株並調整接種量。

附錄 VII — EN 測試

EN 測試體系比 AOAC 更系統化地模擬實際使用條件（含干擾物質、表面測試）。EN 1276（殺菌）、EN 1650（殺黴）、EN 13697（表面）、EN 13704（殺孢）共同涵蓋全面的效力聲明需求。歐洲市場的消毒劑採購，必須確認供應商持有相關 EN 測試數據並符合 BPR 法規。

CDMO 全球市場挑戰

同時供應美國與歐盟市場的 CDMO：
美國 — 要求供應商 EPA 登記 + AOAC 測試數據
歐盟 — 要求 BPR 核准 + EN 測試數據
共同 — 工廠自身消毒劑驗證：使用場地特異分離株（site-specific isolates）進行懸浮或載體測試，以符合 FDA/EMA 的 GMP 要求

最常見稽查缺口：聲稱具有「殺孢效力」的產品，供應商僅提供殺菌（EN 1276/AOAC Use-Dilution）數據，缺乏殺孢（EN 13704/AOAC Sporicidal）數據支持。

Appendix VIII: Large-Scale Gassing or Fogging of Clean Rooms (Section 24.0)

p. 62–65 | Decontamination technologies overview

Overview & Chemical Agents

Vaporization or fogging of disinfectants for large-scale decontamination is being used or considered by many firms in the pharmaceutical industry. A significant amount of published data show the efficacy of this type of disinfectant system against vegetative bacteria, fungi, and bacterial spores.

Chemical agents commonly used for large-scale gassing/fogging include:

  • Paraformaldehyde
  • Peracetic acid / hydrogen peroxide
  • Phenols
  • Bleach (sodium hypochlorite)
  • Quaternary ammonia
  • Vapor phased hydrogen peroxide (VPHP / VHP)
  • Gaseous chlorine dioxide
  • Ozone

Critical Principle: Gassing Does Not Replace Cleaning

While gassing and fogging systems can provide excellent destruction of microorganisms present in contaminated areas, such systems do not clean surfaces. Therefore, cleaning is considered a mandatory and routine step in addition to gassing or fogging. Residues, particulates, foreign matter, and pyrogens are not removed by any gaseous decontamination process.

重點解析

大規模氣態去污 vs. 例行清潔/消毒

TR70 把大規模氣態去污視為「特殊事件工具」而非日常程序替代品：

  • 觸發情境：設施翻修後復歸使用、發生重大微生物汙染事件（positive EM event）、全廠停產後重啟
  • 補充關係：去污在清潔之前或之後都可進行，但不能取代機械性清潔（擦拭、拖地）
  • CDMO 應用：客戶切換、不同產品批次之間的廠房轉換，有時需要大規模 VHP 作為額外保障

比喻：核武 vs. 常規武器

VHP 大規模去污就像軍事上的「核選項」——威力強大，但不能取代日常的例行防護。廠房每天的擦拭消毒是常規武器；VHP 是萬不得已時才動用的重型武器。兩者角色不同，缺一不可。

氣態去污劑的分類邏輯

TR70 將這些技術分成兩大類：

  • 真氣態（gaseous）：VHP、ClO₂、臭氧、甲醛——氣體分子均勻分佈，穿透力強
  • 濕霧（wet fogging）：微液滴（10–25 µm）物理性覆蓋表面——依賴接觸而非氣體穿透

選擇哪一種取決於廠房結構、目標菌種、殘留要求及人員安全考量。

P

Paraformaldehyde Gassing

p. 63 | Legacy technology — declining use

Mechanism & Historical Use

Paraformaldehyde gas has been used as a large-scale clean room decontamination methodology for many years. It is a white, crystalline powder that, when heated, releases formaldehyde gas — the actual decontaminant. The gas is created inside the room by a pan and heating element, then spread by portable fans set up prior to gas generation.

Paraformaldehyde gas microbial destruction claims through the U.S. EPA are limited to what would be termed a high-level disinfectant, sanitizer, or fungicide. However, sporicidal reduction has been obtained in clean rooms by many firms worldwide.

"Paraformaldehyde is an older methodology that has been replaced by most GMP firms with current, more modern chemistries and systems. It leaves concerning residuals on all surfaces (as defined by FDA) and requires the utmost safety concerns for human health. For these reasons, this methodology is declining in use."

為何 GMP 廠逐漸淘汰甲醛熏蒸

甲醛熏蒸的核心問題

  • 表面殘留：甲醛會在所有接觸表面留下殘餘，FDA 對此有明確關切
  • 人員健康風險：甲醛為已知致癌物（IARC Group 1），操作風險極高
  • 法規壓力：歐盟及多國職安法規對甲醛暴露限值越來越嚴格
  • 更好的替代品已存在：VHP、ClO₂ 等技術已成熟，殘留問題更易管理

CDMO 實務提醒

若廠房 SOP 或歷史記錄仍保有甲醛熏蒸程序，應列入設施現代化改善計畫（facility modernization）優先項目。在新設施設計時，完全不應納入甲醛熏蒸選項。

W

Wet Droplet Fogging

p. 63–64 | Aerosol-based surface coating technology

Technology Description

Wet droplet fogging involves the generation of small liquid droplets (typically 10–25 microns in diameter) from a chemical agent placed into an airstream by a generator linked to a fogging device. Various fogging devices are placed strategically throughout the room; portable fans circulate the droplets.

Chemical agents commonly used include peracetic acid, hydrogen peroxide, sodium hypochlorite, phenols, and quaternary ammoniums — chosen based on the type of antimicrobial action required.

Key Operating Parameters

  • Goal: Lightly coat all surfaces with a thin but constant layer of chemical agent for an extended period
  • Fogging time: Typically 15 minutes to 1 hour
  • Release time: 15 minutes to 1 hour, or until all surfaces are dry
  • Efficacy range: Sanitization to sporicidal, depending on agent selected

This is considered a wet or aerosol process rather than a true gaseous process. Wetting of surfaces reduces decontamination times, dry times, and release times. With overuse and without manual cleaning procedures, residues can build up over time and corrosion can occur.

濕霧法實務解析

比喻：淋浴 vs. 蒸氣浴

濕霧法像「淋浴」——水霧打濕表面達到消毒目的；VHP 像「蒸氣浴」——水蒸氣無孔不入，連縫隙都能滲透。前者快速方便，後者穿透力更強但設置更複雜。

CDMO 操作注意事項

使用濕霧法需特別注意：

  • 操作人員在液霧濃度未降至容許暴露限值前，不得進入廠房
  • 過度使用且缺乏機械清潔，會造成 QAC 殘留累積（resistance selection pressure）
  • 每次噴霧結束後，所有未完全乾燥的表面應人工輔助擦乾，但需穿戴適當個人防護裝備
VHP

Vaporized Hydrogen Peroxide (VPHP / VHP)

p. 64 | Gold standard for pharmaceutical gaseous decontamination

Technology & Mechanism

The vapor of hydrogen peroxide is noncarcinogenic and breaks down to water and oxygen, eliminating the corrosive residuals inherent with traditional methods such as paraformaldehyde.

VPHP systems generate a low level of vaporized 35% hydrogen peroxide (250–1,200 ppm) into manufacturing areas through portable or fixed distribution systems. In the vapor phase, disinfection may require longer times compared to traditional methods, depending on:

  • Number of VPHP generators
  • Size of the area to be treated
  • Required contact and clearance times

The distribution of vapor within the room can be verified with hydrogen peroxide sensors or chemical indicators.

Operational Considerations

  • The VPHP process is classified as a disinfection step, not sterilization
  • Leaks to the external environment and clearance time must be tested and assessed
  • Air-handling systems may need to be shut off; doorways and return vents sealed
  • Release times: 2–4 hours, depending on area size and gassing duration

Isolator vs. Large-Scale Application

The effectiveness of VPHP has been well documented in isolator operations for many years. However, isolator operations are smaller, sealed, and have evacuation systems that can remove and scrub the volatile gases. The use of VPHP in a large-scale, open manufacturing environment has also been very successful and — unlike UV — is effective in the presence of shadowing.

Conclusive validation studies for large-scale VPHP are specific to the operation and setup. Each area should be assessed for effectiveness in its own validation study.

VPHP in the C&D Program Sequence

  • Before cleaning: VPHP = sanitization step → must be followed by mechanical wipe/mop
  • After cleaning: VPHP = final disinfection step → the preferred sequence in most GMP programs

VHP 深度解析

VHP（氣化過氧化氫）核心概念

VHP / VPHP (Vaporized Hydrogen Peroxide，氣化過氧化氫) 是目前製藥 GMP 環境中最主流的大規模氣態去污技術。

  • 作用原理：H₂O₂ 蒸氣釋放自由基（•OH），破壞細胞膜、DNA 及蛋白質
  • 殺菌能力：可達 6-log 殺滅芽孢（Geobacillus stearothermophilus），是無菌製造廠最重要的去污工具
  • 殘留優勢：最終分解為 H₂O + O₂，無有毒殘留，符合 FDA 對殘留的要求
  • 濃度範圍：250–1,200 ppm（視目標殺滅等級而定）

VHP 循環四階段（Cycle Phases）

1. 去濕（Dehumidification）
   → 降低相對濕度，防止 H₂O₂ 提前凝結

2. 充裝（Conditioning）
   → 開始注入 H₂O₂ 蒸氣，濃度逐漸上升

3. 暴露（Exposure）
   → 維持目標濃度（ppm）× 時間（min）
   → 生物指示劑（BI）置於最難達到的位置

4. 充氣復原（Aeration）
   → 通入過濾空氣，將 H₂O₂ 分解至安全濃度
   → 通常需 2–4 小時

CDMO 實務：TR70 與 PtC-12 的互補關係

TR70（本文件）提供整體 C&D 框架脈絡：VHP 在廠房層次的定位、適用場景、與清潔程序的關係。

PtC-12（RABS 及隔離器）則提供隔離器 VHP 去污的具體指引：循環開發、生物指示劑放置策略、D 值計算、參數化放行（parametric release）。

在 CDMO 日常運作中，設計隔離器 VHP SOP 時，應同時參照兩份文件——TR70 提供「為什麼」，PtC-12 提供「怎麼做」。

G

Gaseous Chlorine Dioxide (ClO₂) & Ozone Gas

p. 64–65 | Emerging alternatives for large-scale decontamination

Gaseous Chlorine Dioxide (ClO₂)

Gaseous chlorine dioxide is another available alternative for gassing. Of the methods discussed, it is most similar to paraformaldehyde or VPHP rather than wet droplet fogging, as it is a true gas product. Application to the area to be decontaminated is accomplished in much the same way as the VPHP gas process. Basic differences between the products relate to corrosion, residual, safety, and setup.

To disinfect a room, gaseous ClO₂ is precipitated into the area via a generator and dispersion heads. These systems have been employed in many industry settings and are now being considered as a possible alternative for GMP operations.

Ozone Gas

Ozone is made by adding high voltage to oxygen. The system uses a high concentration of ozone gas, integrating a gas generator to emit ozone to the area to be decontaminated. Design specifications for typical systems include:

  • Ozone concentration: 200 ppm or more
  • Relative humidity: 80% or more
  • Treatment time: Determined by area size, inherent bioburden, and obstructions

These systems have been employed in many industry settings and are now being considered as a possible alternative for GMP operations.

ClO₂ 與臭氧的定位

TR70 的視角：技術選擇框架

TR70 對 ClO₂ 和臭氧的描述較 VHP 簡略，暗示這兩種技術在 2015 年時仍處於「考慮中」（being considered）的階段。選擇大規模去污技術時，需從以下維度評估：

  • 腐蝕性（不鏽鋼、電子設備的兼容性）
  • 殘留風險（藥品接觸面、患者安全）
  • 人員安全（容許暴露限值 PEL）
  • 設置複雜度（發生器、散佈系統）
  • 驗證數據的可取得性
S

Safety Requirements & Validation Obligations

p. 65 | Non-negotiable principles for any gassing program

Safety Imperative

Whenever chemical agents are used for large-scale gassing or fogging of clean rooms, safety concerns must be addressed. All of the agents discussed can result in injury or death of personnel if proper precautions are not taken to ensure the containment of the chemical agent to the intended areas. For many of the agents discussed, residues that are left behind on product-contact surfaces are also a significant concern and must be evaluated.

Validation Requirement

Although these methods of decontamination are effective, they should not be used to replace a routine program for cleaning and disinfecting the clean room areas. If they are used as the standard practice, they should be validated to demonstrate their ability to achieve an appropriate level of bioburden reduction. This should be performed taking into consideration the material of construction present in the clean room areas.

Source Identification

It is also important to consider the source of the organisms, for gassing will only remove what is present and may leave behind moisture, allowing for further proliferation if the causative agents (e.g., leaks, contamination sources) have not been removed from the area.

安全與驗證：CDMO 必須遵守的底線

大規模去污的驗證最低要求

TR70 雖未詳細列出驗證方案，但以下要素是業界共識：

  • 生物指示劑（BI）：Geobacillus stearothermophilus 芽孢，≥10⁶ 初始菌數，目標 6-log 殺滅
  • 暴露地圖（exposure mapping）：在廠房最難到達位置（死角、設備後方、角落）放置 BI 和化學指示劑
  • 濃度驗證：使用 H₂O₂ 感測器或化學指示劑確認全廠房濃度均勻分佈
  • 材料相容性：確認廠房建材、設備表面可承受去污劑而無損壞
  • 殘留測試：確認去污後產品接觸面的殘留量符合規範
  • 洩漏測試：確認去污劑不會外洩至其他區域或走廊

根本原因分析先於去污

TR70 強調：大規模去污只能消滅「現有」的微生物，但如果污染源（如設施缺陷、人員操作問題、設備損壞）未被消除，去污後水分殘留反而可能造成更大規模的微生物增殖。

正確順序：找出並消除污染源 → 清潔 → 去污 → 環境監測確認。

思考練習：你的廠房 VHP 程序完整嗎？

  • VHP 循環是否有完整的驗證報告（含 BI 結果、暴露地圖）？
  • 操作 SOP 是否涵蓋人員安全、HVAC 封閉程序、釋放標準？
  • VHP 後是否有確認程序（EM sampling）驗證去污效果？
  • VHP 前的機械清潔是否有文件記錄？
  • 廠房建材的 VHP 相容性是否已評估？

關鍵參考文獻解析

核心監管文件層次（Ref. 3–8）

TR70 的監管框架建立在三個主要法規體系之上：

  • 美國：21 CFR 211.42(c)（設施要求）+ FDA 2004 無菌製造指引（最核心的 GMP 依據）
  • 歐盟：EU GMP Annex 1（無菌製藥製造）+ Chapter 3（廠房設備）+ Chapter 5（生產）——三份文件共同建構 EU GMP C&D 要求
  • 國際：PIC/S PE 009-11（各國互認的 GMP 基準）

注意：本 TR70 引用的是 2008 年版 EU GMP Annex 1。2022 年已發布重大修訂版，對 C&D 的要求更為明確，CDMO 應以新版為準。

USP <1072> 的地位（Ref. 9）

USP General Chapter <1072> 消毒劑與防腐劑 是美國藥典對消毒劑使用的指引性章節（非強制執行）。它提供：

  • 消毒劑分類與選用標準
  • 效力測試方法指引
  • 消毒劑輪換（rotation）的理論依據

CDMO 應在 C&D 程序文件中引用此章節作為支持性依據。

ISO 14698 的角色（Ref. 11）

ISO 14698 潔淨室生物污染控制 提供環境微生物監測的國際標準框架。TR70 引用此標準以確立：C&D 程序與 EM 程序（環境監測）必須作為一個整合系統運作。消毒效果的驗證，最終要反映在 EM 數據的改善上。

歷史文獻的意義（Ref. 16–19）

TR70 引用 Robert Koch 1881 年的消毒學著作和 1903 年 Rideal-Walker 標準化方法，顯示消毒科學的現代框架直接源自 19 世紀微生物學奠基者。這些引用不只是學術致敬——它們提醒我們：消毒的基本原理（接觸時間、濃度、菌種特異性）百年來沒有本質改變，只是材料和技術演進了。

EPA 登記架構（Ref. 20–23）

FIFRA、CFR Title 40 和 EPA Label Review Manual 共同構成美國消毒劑上市許可的法律框架。CDMO 需確認所用消毒劑均具有適當 EPA 登記號碼，且使用方法符合標籤說明（use as labeled）——這是 FDA 查廠時的常見查核點。
