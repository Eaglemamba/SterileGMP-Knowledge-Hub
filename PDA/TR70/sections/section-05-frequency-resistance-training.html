10.0 Frequency for Cleaning and Disinfection 10.0 清潔與消毒頻率

10.0 Frequency for Cleaning and Disinfection

Selecting an appropriate cleaning and disinfection frequency for manufacturing facility surfaces — walls, ceilings, doors, non-product-contact equipment, and floors — is essential for maintaining effective contamination control. The industry has developed three main approaches:

Approach 1: Area Classification

Frequencies are set according to the stringency of the area classification. The most stringent area receives the most frequent cleaning. Example:

  • Grade A (ISO 5): Daily cleaning and disinfection
  • Grade C (ISO 7): Weekly
  • Grade D (ISO 8): Monthly

Limitation: Does not account for product contamination risk or type of manufacturing conducted.

Approach 2: Environmental Monitoring (EM) Data

Frequency is set based solely on trending EM data. This approach tends to be reactive and retrospective — it responds to existing contamination levels rather than proactively preventing them. More commonly used to justify reducing an already-established frequency based on sustained satisfactory results.

Approach 3: Risk-Based Model (Preferred)

Combines area classification and EM data while also considering product exposure risk, personnel intervention, and manufacturing type. This provides the greatest flexibility to tailor the program to the specific facility and process.

Key risk-based principles:

  • Frequency must be commensurate with the risk of product contamination. Open vs. closed processing and proximity to final sterilization steps are primary criteria.
  • Areas within the aseptic manufacturing boundary (Grade D to Grade C) should be cleaned more frequently than those outside.
  • ISO 8 areas adjacent and contiguous to open aseptic processing zones may need more frequent cleaning than standalone ISO 8 areas.
  • Product changeovers require cleaning per established procedures to prevent cross-contamination.
  • High-ingress locations (gowning airlocks, doors, floors) and areas supporting microbial growth warrant higher frequency.
  • Older buildings or difficult-to-clean layouts may warrant increased frequency.
  • Event-based triggers — such as EM action level excursions, HEPA filter failures, or facility shutdowns — require additional cleaning beyond routine schedules.
  • Routine EM data must be reviewed regularly to verify that the chosen frequency remains effective.

10.0 清潔與消毒頻率

為製造設施表面（牆壁、天花板、門、非接觸產品的設備及地板）選擇適當的清潔與消毒頻率，是維持有效污染控制的關鍵。業界發展出三種主要方法：

方法一：區域分類法（Area Classification）

根據潔淨區等級高低設定頻率，等級越嚴格，清潔頻率越高。範例：

  • Grade A（ISO 5）：每日清潔與消毒
  • Grade C（ISO 7）：每週
  • Grade D（ISO 8）：每月

限制：未考慮各區域的產品污染風險或製造類型差異。

方法二：環境監測數據法（EM Data）

完全依據 EM 趨勢數據決定頻率。此方法本質上是被動且回溯性的，通常用於在持續良好表現的基礎上，合理化降低已建立的清潔頻率，而非積極預防污染。

方法三：風險模型法（Risk-Based Model，推薦）

整合前兩種方法，並額外考量：產品暴露風險、人員干預程度、製造類型。靈活性最高，可針對特定設施設計量身定制的污染控制計劃。

核心風險原則：

  • 頻率必須與產品污染風險相稱。開放式 vs. 密閉製程，以及與最終滅菌步驟的距離，是首要選擇標準。
  • 無菌製造邊界內的區域（如 Grade D 至 Grade C 的傳遞路徑）需比邊界外更頻繁清潔。
  • 緊鄰開放無菌操作區的 ISO 8 區域，可能需要比獨立 ISO 8 區域更頻繁的清潔。
  • 每次換產（product changeover）皆需按 SOP 清潔，以防交叉污染。
  • 高污染入侵點（更衣氣閘、門、地板）及可能滋生微生物的區域，需提高頻率。
  • 建築老化或清潔困難的佈局，可能需提高頻率。
  • 事件觸發額外清潔：EM 行動水準超標、HEPA 過濾器故障、設施停機等。
  • EM 數據需定期審查，驗證所選頻率的持續有效性。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

在 TWi 的無菌充填作業中，風險模型法是最實用的選擇。對於 Grade A 充填區（ISO 5），每批次後的清潔與消毒是基本要求；Grade B（ISO 5/6 背景）每日清潔；Grade C 根據批次頻率設定（通常每週至少一次）。

當 EM 監測出現 Alert Level 超標時，必須立即啟動額外清潔程序，並在 CAPA 中記錄根本原因分析。這正是「事件觸發」頻率調整的典型應用。

11.0 Resistance and Rotation 11.0 抗性與輪替

11.0 Resistance and Rotation

For many years, the industry debated whether microorganisms can develop resistance to sanitizers, disinfectants, and sporicides — analogous to antibiotic resistance. The current scientific consensus from TR70 and USP <1072> is as follows:

"The development of microbial resistance to antibiotics is a well-described phenomenon. The development of microbial resistance [to disinfectants] is less likely, as disinfectants are more powerful biocidal agents than antibiotics and are applied in high concentrations against low populations of microorganisms, so the selective pressure for the development of resistance is less profound." — USP <1072>

Key reasons why true disinfectant resistance is unlikely in cleanrooms:

  • Antibiotic resistance typically requires modification or acquisition of a single gene. Disinfectants have multiple, simultaneous mechanisms of action against cellular physiology — multiple simultaneous mutations would be required in a short contact time (e.g., 5 minutes).
  • Cleanrooms maintain inherently low cell numbers, reducing the statistical probability of a resistance mutation surviving.

Evolution of Rotation Strategy

Historically, firms rotated between two or more disinfectants (e.g., QAC alternate with phenolic). This led to:

  • High residue buildup on surfaces
  • Subordinate (reduced) efficacy performance

Current best practice: Rotate a disinfectant with a sporicide. This is superior because sporicides address spore-forming organisms that standard disinfectants cannot reliably eliminate.

Special considerations:

  • Using a sporicide alone (without a disinfectant) is possible if efficacy is proven — but is discouraged due to the inherent corrosive nature of most sporicidal agents.
  • All rotation systems must be evaluated using area classification, EM data, and/or formal risk assessment.

11.0 抗藥性與輪替策略

業界長期爭論微生物是否會對消毒劑產生類似抗生素的抗藥性（Resistance）。TR70 和 USP <1072> 的現行科學共識是：

「微生物對消毒劑產生抗藥性的可能性低於抗生素，因為消毒劑是效力更強的殺菌劑，施用於低菌落數環境中，抗藥性的選擇壓力遠低於抗生素。」— USP <1072>

潔淨室環境不易產生真正抗藥性的主要原因：

  • 抗生素抗藥性只需單一基因突變；消毒劑對細胞生理有多重作用機制，短暫接觸時間內（如 5 分鐘）需要多個同步突變才能產生抗性，機率極低。
  • 潔淨室中菌落數極少，產生有效抗藥性突變的統計概率更低。

輪替策略的演進

歷史上，工廠採用「兩種或多種消毒劑交替輪換」（如 QAC 輪替酚類）。問題：

  • 表面殘留物累積嚴重
  • 整體殺菌效能下降

現行最佳實務：以消毒劑（Disinfectant）搭配孢子殺滅劑（Sporicide）進行輪替，因後者可有效消滅標準消毒劑無法殺滅的孢子型微生物。

比喻說明 — 輪替的意義

想像用同一種洗髮精洗頭很久後，效果似乎變差了——這不是真正的「抗藥性」，而是表面殘留物或效能下降的感知。

消毒劑輪替的真正價值不在於「防抗藥性」，而在於：(1) 確保不同類型微生物（含孢子）都被有效覆蓋；(2) 防止殘留物堆積；(3) 通過 EM 數據驗證輪替計劃的持續有效性。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

TWi 的清潔計劃必須有書面記錄的輪替時程，並附上選擇各藥劑的科學依據（rationale）。稽查員會要求查看：

  • 輪替 SOP 或程序
  • 各藥劑的有效性驗證數據（包含孢子殺滅劑的孢子試驗）
  • 與 EM 趨勢的定期關聯審查

若 EM 數據出現持續偏高趨勢，應評估是否需要增加孢子殺滅劑頻率或改變輪替設計。

12.0 Return from a Shutdown 12.0 停工後復工

12.0 Return from a Shutdown

A shutdown is a planned or unplanned stoppage of operations that is likely to compromise the environmental conditions in a classified area. Types include:

  • Planned: Preventive maintenance, construction activities, scheduled facility holidays
  • Unplanned: Unexpected power outages, HVAC failures, emergency stops

The extent and duration of a shutdown determines the level of viable and non-viable contamination introduced. Regardless of type, defined actions must be taken before operations resume.

Return-to-Control Criteria

  1. Verify control systems: Confirm that all HVAC/air handlers, pressure differentials, and environmental controls are functioning properly before beginning any cleaning.
  2. Pre-clean with detergent or water: Remove dirt, dust, or construction debris before applying any disinfectant. Disinfectants cannot effectively penetrate gross contamination.
  3. Disinfect per established procedures: Apply disinfectant according to qualified procedures to reduce bioburden to acceptable levels. All surfaces — walls, floors, and equipment surfaces — must be included.
  4. Use qualified, in-situ data where possible: The multistep cleaning and disinfection regimen should be supported by evidence demonstrating it is capable of bringing the facility back to a state of control.
  5. Monitor before resuming operations: Sample for viable and non-viable particulates after cleaning and disinfection is complete. Evaluate results to verify the area is within control limits before returning to use.

Key Principle

The return-to-use decision must be data-driven. Environmental monitoring results — not just visual inspection or adherence to a cleaning schedule — should confirm the area is back in a state of control.

12.0 停機後的復工程序

停機（Shutdown）是指計劃性或非計劃性的作業停止，可能危及潔淨區的環境狀態。類型包括：

  • 計劃性停機：預防性維護（PM）、建設活動、排定的廠區假期
  • 非計劃性停機：突發停電、HVAC 故障、緊急停線

停機的時長與範圍決定引入的活性與非活性污染水準。無論何種類型，復工前必須執行明確的行動程序。

復工受控標準（Return-to-Control Criteria）

  1. 確認控制系統正常運作：在啟動任何清潔作業前，確認 HVAC / 空調、壓差及所有環境控制系統已恢復正常。
  2. 先用清潔劑或水進行預清潔：移除停機期間積累的灰塵、污垢或建設殘屑。消毒劑無法有效穿透大量有機污染物。
  3. 按已建立的程序執行消毒：應用已驗證的消毒程序，使生物負荷降至可接受水準。必須涵蓋所有表面——牆壁、地板及設備表面。
  4. 盡可能採用現場（In-situ）數據支持：多步驟清潔與消毒方案應有數據佐證，證明其能將設施恢復至受控狀態。
  5. 恢復生產前進行環境監測：清潔消毒完成後，對活性和非活性微粒進行取樣。評估結果確認在控制限度內後，方可重新投入生產。

核心原則

復工決定必須以數據為依據。環境監測結果——而非僅憑目視檢查或清潔時程的遵守——才能確認區域已回到受控狀態。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

在 TWi，任何非計劃性停機（如停電或 HVAC 故障）都需要啟動偏差調查。復工程序必須在偏差報告中記錄，包括：停機原因、持續時間、採取的清潔消毒措施、EM 監測結果，以及批准復工的授權人員。

若停機期間發生建設或維修活動，預清潔步驟尤為關鍵——施工塵埃是孢子的潛在載體。

13.0 Hold Times for Cleaned Areas, Non-Product-Contact Equipment, and Utensils 13.0 已清潔區域、非產品接觸設備及器具之保存時間

13.0 Hold Times for Cleaned Areas

After an area or facility has been cleaned and disinfected, a maximum hold time must be established — the maximum allowable time between the completion of cleaning/disinfection and the resumption of production activities, during which the area is considered to remain in a "clean" state.

Hold Time Study Requirements

Studies must be performed to establish and validate the maximum hold time. Key elements:

  • Sampling timepoints: Viable and non-viable sampling is performed immediately after cleaning and disinfection, and again at or beyond the maximum proposed hold time limit (e.g., at 24h and 48h).
  • Controlled entry: Entrance into the area after cleaning should be limited to reduce artificial contamination during the study.
  • Representative conditions: The study should include the normal level of non-production activities that would typically occur in the area during the hold period (e.g., routine checks, maintenance pass-throughs).
  • Equipment and utensils: Hold times for non-product-contact equipment and utensils must also be established and validated. These materials must be stored in a manner that maintains their integrity for the duration of the established hold time.

Exceeding Hold Times

If the hold time is exceeded — for areas, non-product-contact equipment, or utensils — they must be cleaned and disinfected again before use. This is a non-negotiable requirement. Exceeding hold time without re-cleaning constitutes a potential GMP deviation.

Common Industry Hold Time Limits

While specific limits must be established for each facility based on its own validation data, common industry benchmarks include:

  • 24-hour hold time for Grade A/B areas after cleaning
  • 48-hour hold time for Grade C areas
  • 72-hour or weekly hold times for Grade D areas (depending on risk)

These are illustrative examples only — each site must validate its own limits.

13.0 清潔後保留時間

設施或區域完成清潔與消毒後，必須建立最大保留時間（Hold Time）——即清潔消毒完成後至恢復生產前，該區域仍被視為「清潔狀態」的最長允許時間。

Hold Time 研究要求

必須執行研究以建立並驗證最大保留時間。關鍵要素：

  • 取樣時間點：於清潔消毒後立即取樣，並在達到（或超過）建議最大時限時再次取樣（如 24h 和 48h）。
  • 限制入室：研究期間應限制人員進入清潔後區域，以避免人為引入污染影響結果。
  • 代表性條件：研究應模擬保留期間通常會發生的非生產活動（如例行檢查、維護通行），使研究條件具代表性。
  • 設備與器具：非接觸產品的設備和器具的 Hold Time 同樣需要建立和驗證。儲存方式必須確保在 Hold Time 期間維持清潔狀態的完整性。

超過 Hold Time 的處理

若區域、非接觸產品設備或器具超過已建立的 Hold Time，必須重新進行清潔與消毒，方可使用。這是不可妥協的要求。未重新清潔即繼續使用，構成潛在的 GMP 偏差（Deviation）。

業界常見 Hold Time 基準

雖然各設施必須依據自身驗證數據建立各自的限度，以下為常見的業界參考值：

  • Grade A/B 區域：清潔後 24 小時
  • Grade C 區域：清潔後 48 小時
  • Grade D 區域：72 小時或每週（視風險而定）

以上僅為示例參考值，各廠址必須驗證自己的限度。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

在 TWi，Hold Time 超標直接觸發偏差（Deviation）流程。操作員必須了解每個區域和每類設備的 Hold Time 限度，並在生產批次記錄（BPR）中記錄清潔時間。

稽查員重點檢查項目：

  • Hold Time 驗證研究報告是否存在且包含合適的取樣時間點
  • BPR 中是否記錄清潔完成時間及使用前確認
  • 超時偏差是否被正確記錄並完成 CAPA
14.0 Training 14.0 教育訓練

14.0 Training

Personnel must be properly trained on the SOPs governing the cleaning and disinfection program as well as the specific techniques used. Because most cleaning and disinfection processes are manual in nature and their outcome (contamination reduction) depends on execution quality, training is a critical factor in program success.

Training Scope — Required Topics

  • Basic microbiology — understanding what microorganisms are and how they behave in a controlled environment
  • Contamination sources and risks — personnel, materials, equipment, and environment as contamination vectors
  • Facility design and airflow — how cleanroom classifications and unidirectional airflow protect product
  • Gowning — correct gowning technique, order of gowning, and common errors
  • Clean room behavior and personal hygiene — movement, communication, and hygiene standards within classified areas
  • Basic environmental monitoring — what is being measured, why, and how to interpret results
  • Aspects of a cleaning program — objectives, agents, procedures, and frequency
  • Aspects of a disinfection program — agent selection, contact time, rotation rationale
  • Relevant SOPs — all procedures directly governing the individual's tasks

Trainer and Trainee Scope

Both the individuals performing cleaning and disinfection activities and their supervisors must be trained. Supervisors need sufficient knowledge to verify correct technique and recognize deviations.

Training Documentation and Requalification

  • Documentation: All training must be documented. Records typically include: trainee name, trainer name, training date, topic/SOP number, method (read, observed, performed), and assessment result.
  • Competency assessment: Training effectiveness should be evaluated through direct observation of technique and/or written testing.
  • Periodic re-administration: Training must be re-administered on a periodic basis. Annual requalification is the industry norm.
  • Change-triggered retraining: Any change to a cleaning or disinfection SOP, agent, or procedure requires retraining of affected personnel before implementation.

14.0 人員培訓

人員必須在清潔與消毒相關 SOP 及具體操作技術上接受適當培訓。由於大多數清潔消毒作業是手工操作，其效果（污染削減）完全取決於執行品質，培訓是計劃成功的關鍵因素。

培訓範疇 — 必要主題

  • 基礎微生物學：了解微生物的特性及其在潔淨環境中的行為
  • 污染來源與風險：人員、物料、設備和環境作為污染媒介的機制
  • 設施設計與氣流：潔淨區等級分類和單向氣流如何保護產品
  • 更衣（Gowning）：正確的更衣技術、更衣順序及常見錯誤
  • 潔淨室行為與個人衛生：潔淨區內的行動規範、溝通方式和個人衛生標準
  • 環境監測基礎：監測什麼、為何監測，以及如何解讀結果
  • 清潔計劃要素：目標、藥劑、程序和頻率
  • 消毒計劃要素：藥劑選擇、接觸時間、輪替原理
  • 相關 SOP：所有直接規範個人工作任務的程序文件

受訓人員範圍

執行清潔消毒作業的人員以及其主管均必須接受培訓。主管需具備足夠知識，以驗證技術正確性並識別偏差。

培訓文件與定期再資格認證

  • 文件記錄：所有培訓必須有文件記錄，通常包括：受訓者姓名、訓練者姓名、培訓日期、主題 / SOP 編號、方式（閱讀、觀察、實操），以及評估結果。
  • 能力評估（Competency Assessment）：應通過直接觀察操作技術和/或書面測驗評估培訓效果。
  • 定期再培訓：培訓必須定期重新進行，業界常規為每年再資格認證（Annual Requalification）。
  • 變更觸發再培訓：任何清潔消毒 SOP、藥劑或程序的變更，都需要在實施前對相關人員進行再培訓。

CDMO 實務觀點 — TWi

稽查員幾乎必然審查操作員培訓記錄（Training Records）。在 TWi，需確保：

  • 所有清潔消毒相關 SOP 的 OJT（On-the-Job Training）記錄齊全，且由合格訓練者簽署
  • 年度再資格認證（Annual Requalification）已按時完成，無逾期缺口
  • SOP 修訂後，相關人員的補訓記錄完整（Change-Triggered Retraining）
  • 能力評估不只是「簽名確認閱讀」，而是包含觀察評估或書面測驗

培訓記錄的缺口（gaps）是 FDA 和 EMA 稽查中最常見的 483 觀察項目之一。

Section Summary / 本節總結

Section 5 Summary

This section covers the operational and programmatic elements that determine whether a cleaning and disinfection program sustains its effectiveness over time. The five topics are interconnected:

Key Takeaways

  • Frequency (10.0): The risk-based model is the industry best practice for selecting cleaning frequency. Frequency must be supported by ongoing EM data and adjusted when data trends indicate declining efficacy. Event-based triggers (EM excursions, shutdowns, HEPA failures) require additional cleaning beyond routine schedules.
  • Resistance & Rotation (11.0): True disinfectant resistance is scientifically unlikely in cleanrooms. The current best practice is rotating a disinfectant with a sporicide — not rotating multiple disinfectants. All rotation programs must be evaluated using EM data and risk assessment.
  • Shutdown (12.0): After any shutdown, HVAC must be verified first, followed by pre-cleaning with detergent, formal disinfection, and EM monitoring — in that order — before resuming operations.
  • Hold Times (13.0): Hold times for areas and equipment must be validated through EM studies. Exceeding a hold time without re-cleaning constitutes a GMP deviation.
  • Training (14.0): All cleaning and disinfection personnel and their supervisors must be trained on a defined curriculum. Competency must be assessed (not just documented). Annual requalification is required, and SOPs changes trigger retraining.

The Underlying Theme

A cleaning and disinfection program is not a static document — it is a living system. Frequency, agent selection, rotation schedules, hold times, and training records must all be reviewed regularly using EM data as the primary indicator of program effectiveness.

第五節 總結

本節涵蓋決定清潔消毒計劃能否持續有效運作的作業與管理要素。五個主題相互關聯：

核心要點

  • 頻率（10.0）：風險模型法是選擇清潔頻率的業界最佳實務。頻率必須由持續的 EM 數據支持，並在數據趨勢顯示效能下降時調整。事件觸發（EM 超標、停機、HEPA 故障）需要在例行時程外增加清潔。
  • 抗藥性與輪替（11.0）：在潔淨室中，消毒劑的真實抗藥性在科學上不太可能發生。目前最佳實務是以消毒劑搭配孢子殺滅劑輪替，而非多種消毒劑輪換。所有輪替計劃必須結合 EM 數據和風險評估進行驗證。
  • 停機復工（12.0）：任何停機後，必須依序完成：確認 HVAC 正常 → 清潔劑預清潔 → 正式消毒 → EM 監測，確認受控後方可復工。
  • 保留時間（13.0）：區域和設備的 Hold Time 必須通過 EM 研究驗證。超過 Hold Time 未重新清潔即使用，構成 GMP 偏差。
  • 培訓（14.0）：所有清潔消毒人員及其主管必須完成既定課程的培訓，能力評估不能僅靠書面簽名確認。每年再資格認證為必要要求，SOP 變更須觸發再培訓。

深層主題

清潔消毒計劃不是一份靜態文件，而是一個動態系統。頻率、藥劑選擇、輪替時程、保留時間和培訓記錄，都必須定期審查，以 EM 數據作為計劃有效性的主要指標。

思考練習 — CDMO 場景

假設 TWi 的 Grade B 充填室在一次計劃性 PM 停機後重新開放，但操作員發現上次 EM 取樣數據顯示某監測點出現 Alert Level。請思考：

  1. 復工前需要完成哪些步驟？（提示：HVAC 確認 + 加強消毒 + EM 監測）
  2. 若 Hold Time 已超過 24 小時，如何處理？
  3. 若負責執行消毒的操作員本月尚未完成年度再資格認證，是否可以放行？
