1. Introduction and Scope — 導論與範圍

Original Text (English)

This document is designed to communicate best practices and considerations and to encourage further dialog with industry, health authorities, and suppliers of technology and materials while taking into account the changes and needs of the modern, global, sterile, healthcare product manufacturing industry.

In 2001, PDA issued Technical Report No. 34: Design and Validation of Isolator Systems for the Manufacturing and Testing of Health Care Products. Since then, much has been learned by industry, and several important regulatory guidelines, scientific articles, and conference presentations have been published.

In an effort to address the impact of the knowledge gained, this task force was established to focus on important regulatory and technology updates impacting isolator design, validation, and operations for aseptic processing. Two primary types of isolators — open and closed — will be addressed in this Points to Consider (PtC) document.

Isolators provide superior environmental control compared with conventional grade A/B aseptic manufacturing operations and restricted access barrier systems (RABS), since isolators increase the sterility assurance of aseptically prepared products. This document is intended to support identification and use of modern technology; it does not represent a standard or regulatory guidance.

Aseptic processing within isolation systems physically separates the external environment from the aseptic processing line, minimizing personnel exposure. By design, isolators are decontaminated while closed, using validated methods (e.g., controlled application of vapor phase hydrogen peroxide). A traditional isolator enclosure consists of a shell, viewing window, glove/sleeve assemblies, supply and exhaust filters, lights, input and output openings (e.g., equipment door airlocks, rapid transfer ports), and various other connection points.

A properly designed positive pressure isolator, along with adequate procedures for its maintenance, monitoring, and control, provides great advantages over traditional aseptic processing of sterile dosage forms, offering fewer opportunities for microbial contamination during processing.

Science- and risk-based approaches should be used to obtain information needed to make decisions related to the evaluation, design, qualification, operation, monitoring, and interpretation of results from various studies of sterile product manufacturing processes. It should be recognized that a properly designed and operated isolator will have a lower contamination risk than a conventional aseptic process design.

Note: This PtC does not specifically address RABS, however, some of the recommendations may be applicable.

Note: In this document, "Qualification" refers to efforts to obtain documented evidence that items, systems, and processes are suitable for their intended purpose. "Validation" refers to efforts to obtain documented evidence that the process will perform effectively and reproducibly.

中文解說 Commentary

核心概念：為什麼需要這份文件？

PDA 在 2001 年已出版 TR34 作為隔離器設計的基礎指引。2020 年這份 PtC 是在行業知識累積、法規更新後的精煉版本——強調科學與風險導向，而非硬性規定標準。

文件定位：最佳實踐建議，而非法規強制要求。這讓製造商有靈活空間，但也要求你的設計決策有科學依據。

比喻：Isolator vs. RABS vs. 傳統無菌區

想像無菌保護是三層防護等級：

  • 傳統 Grade A/B：在潔淨房間裡小心行事，人員穿無菌衣，但空間是共享的。
  • RABS：裝了玻璃圍牆和固定手套的工作站，人員從外部介入受限制，但手套仍會接觸外部空氣。
  • Isolator：完全密封的太空艙——VHP 消毒後，內部環境與外界完全隔絕，人員只能透過手套操作。

Isolator 的優勢：物理隔絕 + 自動化消毒 = 最低污染風險。

CDMO 實務重點

在多產品 CDMO 環境中，Isolator 的優勢更加顯著：每個批次間的活性成分交叉污染風險可透過 VHP 循環徹底消除，不需要更換整個潔淨室環境。這對於高活性或高致敏性化合物尤其重要。

思考練習

你的廠房現在是哪種設計？如果是 RABS，你是否評估過切換到 Isolator 的成本效益——特別是在周圍房間等級降低（Grade D 取代 Grade B）帶來的節省？

2. Glossary — 術語表

Original Text (English)

The following definitions describe how terms are used specifically in this PtC document.

Term Definition
Biological Indicator (BI) Test systems that contain viable microorganisms with a defined resistance to a specific decontamination process. They help monitor whether the necessary conditions were met to kill a specified number of microorganisms for a given process.
Corrective Intervention (nonroutine) An intervention that is performed to correct or adjust an aseptic process during its execution. Examples include clearing component misfeeds, adjusting sensors, and replacing equipment parts.
Conventional Aseptic Filling Aseptic filling that occurs in an environment where one or more critical work areas are not contained in an isolator or RABS.
Inherent Intervention (routine) An intervention that is an integral part of the aseptic process and is required for setup or routine operation and/or monitoring (e.g., aseptic assembly, container replenishment, or environmental sampling). Required by batch record, procedure, or work instruction.
Critical Work/Processing Area Typically a Grade A/ISO 5 area where sterile product, direct product contact surfaces, or indirect product contact surfaces are or may be exposed to the environment.
Decontamination An action taken to render the surface of an item, environment, material, or component in the isolator incapable of microbiologically contaminating sterile product, product contact surfaces, or materials (also referred to as bio-decontamination).
Disinfection Process by which surface bioburden is reduced to a safe level or eliminated. Some disinfection agents are effective only against vegetative microbes, while others possess additional capability to effectively kill bacterial and fungal spores.
Intervention Activities, manipulations, and tasks performed in Grade A/ISO 5, or other critical processing areas during aseptic operations.
Isolator A contained, decontaminated environment meeting Grade A/ISO 5 conditions used for aseptic process manufacturing that provides an uncompromised, continuous isolation of its interior from the external environment. Once decontaminated by a validated cycle, an isolator prevents microbiological contamination of sterile products and product contact surfaces by enclosures and the supply of continuous, controlled overpressure of HEPA-filtered air.
Closed Isolator Systems Excludes external contamination from the isolator's interior by transfer of material via aseptic connection to auxiliary equipment, rather than using openings to the surrounding environment. Closed systems remain sealed throughout operations.
Open Isolator Systems Designed to allow for the continuous or semicontinuous ingress and/or egress of materials during operations through one or more openings. Openings are engineered (e.g., using continuous overpressure) to exclude the entry of external contamination into the isolator.
Product Contact Surfaces Direct: Stationary or placed surfaces within the isolator that directly contact sterile product (e.g., filling needles, hosing, valve interiors).
Indirect: Surfaces that contact items, materials, or components that will contact sterile product (e.g., stopper bowls and tracks).
Rapid Transfer Port (RTP) Interlocking systems for the transfer of sterilized materials into the isolator without the interchange of environment outside of the isolator, in order to maintain the sterility of the transferred materials and the decontaminated state of the isolator interior (e.g., Double DPTE®).
Restricted Access Barrier System (RABS) An area that includes one or more critical work areas that is fully or partially enclosed with rigid or semi-rigid walls, which restricts the access of aseptic processing personnel, via fixed gloves, to the critical work area during aseptic operations.
Unidirectional Flow An airflow moving in a single direction in a robust and uniform manner and at sufficient speed to reproducibly sweep particles away from the critical processing or testing area.
Vapor Hydrogen Peroxide (VHP) Agent for the decontamination of the interior of the isolator, including all exposed surfaces, fixed isolator glove exteriors, transfer systems, equipment, and certain supplies, materials, and parts, using a defined and validated gaseous mixture or micro-atomization of hydrogen peroxide over a defined and validated period of time, under defined and validated conditions.
中文解說 Commentary

術語架構：理解層次關係

這份術語表有一個隱含的邏輯層次：

  • 環境等級：Critical Work Area = Grade A/ISO 5（最高潔淨等級）
  • 保護方式：Isolator（完全密封）> RABS（半封閉）> Conventional（開放）
  • 消毒強度：Decontamination（殺孢子，滅菌級）> Disinfection（減少活菌，消毒級）

比喻：Decontamination vs. Disinfection

用餐廚房打比方：

  • Disinfection（消毒）= 用酒精擦桌子——殺死大部分細菌，但孢子可能殘留。日常清潔夠用。
  • Decontamination（生物除污）= VHP 燻蒸整個空間——連芽孢都消滅。這是 Isolator 每批次前必做的動作，確保起始狀態為微生物學上的「歸零」。

無菌充填操作需要的是 Decontamination，而非一般的 Disinfection。

重點：Inherent vs. Corrective Intervention

這個分類在 Media Fill 和製程風險評估中非常重要：

  • Inherent（例行）介入：在批次記錄中預先規定的動作，如補充容器、環境監測採樣。屬於「計畫內風險」，可透過設計最小化。
  • Corrective（矯正）介入：製程中出現問題才執行，如清除卡料、更換損壞零件。屬於「計畫外風險」，每次都需要評估對產品無菌性的衝擊。

Open vs. Closed Isolator — CDMO 選型考量

  • Open Isolator：適合連續或半連續充填線，物料可在操作中持續進出。利用正壓氣流阻擋外部污染進入——適用於大批量產品線。
  • Closed Isolator：整個操作過程完全密封，物料透過 RTP（如 DPTE 系統）無菌轉移進出。適合高活性化合物、細胞毒素藥物，或特別需要人員保護的場景。

CDMO 選型原則：有高效能化合物（HPAPI）的產品線 → 考慮 Closed Isolator；一般無菌小瓶充填 → Open Isolator 即可。

Topic 1: Isolator Design — Q1-1: Pressure Differential（壓差設計）

Original Text (English)

Q1-1: What should the pressure differential be between the isolator interior and the surrounding area?

Recommendation

When designing an isolator for aseptic processing, the pressure differential between the isolator inside and the outside environment must be positive while performing critical operations to prevent the reversal of flow. The pressure differential set point between the isolator internal environment and the background environment should generally be higher than 10 Pa, with a positive pressure differential between the two maintained and monitored at all times during operation.

In sterile facilities that employ conveyers to move components through isolators, material transfer openings (e.g., mouseholes) are often needed between environments. Mouseholes must be positively pressured with airflow from the cleaner space. The positive airflow direction must be demonstrated with airflow visualization studies.

Pressure both inside and outside the isolator should be monitored continuously and alarmed with the appropriate limits to provide an indication of deviation from acceptable pressure differential.

Rationale

Isolators rely on a physical partition that affords the aseptic processing area (Grade A/ISO 5) protection by separating it from the surrounding area. Therefore, the appropriate design of isolators must enable the isolator to achieve the intent of separating the internal environment from the external surroundings and maintaining an aseptic processing area within the isolator during manufacturing.

Critical design consideration includes the appropriate pressure differentials between the inside of an isolator and the outside environment. A positive pressure is required to maintain the aseptic environment inside the isolator and prevent air of lesser quality from the surrounding environment from entering the isolator.

Reference: U.S. FDA Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing — Current GMP, 2004.

中文解說 Commentary

核心概念：壓差是物理屏障的第一道防線

Isolator 的無菌保護有兩個機制：

  • 物理屏障：不鏽鋼外殼、手套、密封系統
  • 壓差屏障：內部高壓 → 外部低壓，空氣只能從內往外流

最低要求：>10 Pa 正壓差。這個數字來自 EU GMP Annex 1 的傳統潔淨室標準，在 Isolator 設計中被沿用為最低門檻。實務上許多設施設定 20–30 Pa 以提供更大的安全裕度。

比喻：壓差就像門縫的風向

想像你開著有冷氣的車，車窗微開。內部壓力略高於外部，所以車內冷氣往外吹，而不是外面的熱空氣吹進來。

Isolator 的正壓差原理完全一樣：只要內部壓力持續高於外部，外部的含菌空氣就物理上無法進入 Grade A 空間，即使在 mousehole（物料傳送開口）處也是如此。

Mousehole 設計的監控要求

連續充填線常需要 mousehole 讓容器或器材通過。這裡是壓差設計的弱點：

  • 必須確保氣流方向是「從 Isolator 內部往外吹」
  • 必須做 airflow visualization study（煙霧試驗）證明氣流方向正確
  • 壓差必須 24/7 持續監控並設報警上下限

確效文件重點：不只是靜態數據，要有動態操作中的壓差穩定性數據。

設計審查思考題

如果 Isolator 壓差在充填操作中瞬間跌落至 0 Pa（例如手套更換、RTP 操作），你的 SOP 如何應對？這個偏差需要 OOT 調查嗎？還是觸發 CAPA？

Topic 1: Isolator Design — Q1-2: Glove Design Considerations（手套設計）

Original Text (English)

Q1-2: What are the design considerations for isolator gloves?

Recommendation

Quality risk management principles should be used to identify and address special considerations for the safe and effective use of isolator gloves. Efforts should be made to minimize the need to use isolator gloves, including reducing, eliminating, or repositioning activities, manipulations, and interventions to reduce the impact or risk to process performance and product sterility.

A risk assessment should be used to identify, assess, and mitigate risk posed by the installation and use of isolator gloves in the aseptic operation. Factors to consider include:

  • Ergonomics to address personnel fatigue and safety
  • Diversity of personnel height and reach
  • Orientation and location of planned and anticipated activities, manipulations, and interventions
  • Proximity to critical work surfaces, transfer ports, and exposed sterile items, components, containers, and products
  • Blockage of HEPA airflow creating excessive turbulence in critical work areas
  • Blockage of airflow restricting adequate distribution of decontamination chemicals, vapors, or gases
  • Crowding of materials and glove ports
  • Excessive reaching or stretching of gloves
  • Weight and orientation of materials and items to be handled by gloved personnel
  • Ability to decontaminate and aerate gloves
  • Orientation of gloves during decontamination, aeration, and non-use
  • Proximity to sharp objects or surfaces that can pierce gloves
  • Ease of placement and donning of gloves
  • Position of environmental monitoring apparatus, instruments, and sampling sites
  • Need to perform tasks requiring dexterity (e.g., turning handles or fitments)
  • Thickness of glove material to address dexterity and resist puncture, cracking, or excessive wear
  • Ability to adequately seal gloves and glove ports
  • Ability to confirm or test integrity of gloves
  • Expiry date of gloves
  • Exposure to direct sunlight that can degrade gloves or glove seal material
  • Operators with sharp fingernails or jewelry that can pierce gloves
  • Safe position for storage of gloves in the isolator after or between operations

A review of the positioning should be performed using a simulation method. Modeling, rendering, 3-D model, virtual reality, mock-up, or any method that allows for demonstration and testing of glove-related procedures with different operators of diverse heights and arm length could be used.

Rationale

The performance of personnel-related activities, manipulations, and interventions in the isolator during aseptic operations can pose a risk to the sterility of the product or adversely affect it. The improper or incorrect positioning and composition of isolator gloves used to perform these activities can pose such a risk. Choosing the right glove material will affect dexterity, glove resistance, and chemical resistance of the glove.

Reference: Denk, R. et al. Isolator Surfaces and Contamination Risks to Personnel. PDA Letter 2017, Nov/Dec, 36–40.

核心概念：手套是 Isolator 的「弱點」

Isolator 的密封性幾乎完美，但手套是唯一讓人員直接與內部環境互動的介面，因此也是污染風險最集中的地方。

PDA 的建議邏輯很明確：最好的手套設計，是盡量不需要用手套——透過自動化、機器人手臂、重新設計物料流向來減少人員介入。

比喻：手套設計 = 航空駕駛艙人因工程

飛機駕駛艙的每個開關位置，都是依據人因工程設計——飛行員不需要伸長手臂、不會誤觸相鄰開關。Isolator 手套設計也應該用同樣的思維：

  • 操作員身高範圍？（P5 到 P95 的手臂長度差異）
  • 在手套限制下能否完成精細操作（如轉動配件）？
  • 手套的位置是否遮擋 HEPA 氣流，在 Grade A 區製造渦流？

這些問題要在設計階段用 mock-up 或 3D 模型模擬，不能等到設備到廠後才發現問題。

Topic 1: Isolator Design — Q1-3: Minimizing Intervention Risk（降低介入風險）

Original Text (English)

Q1-3: How should the isolator be designed to minimize risk posed by interventions?

Recommendation

Risk-based assessments should be used to evaluate the need for and design of human interventions, and interventions should be eliminated, reduced, or modified to reduce their relative risk where possible.

  • Interventions within the isolator should be designed to minimize those factors of the intervention that contribute to the risk of microbiological contamination, such as intervention duration, complexity, and proximity to exposed sterile product, components, and surfaces.
  • The isolator and process should be designed to allow for interventions and manipulations in the Grade A/ISO 5 space to adhere to proper aseptic technique.
  • Number and location of glove ports should be designed based on need for and ease of performance of interventions.
  • Activities that do not require Grade A/ISO 5 protection should be located outside the isolator or outside the area where sterile product, materials, and surfaces are exposed.
  • The use of robotics, automated process steps, and intervention-reducing technology should be considered to minimize interventions during setup, operations, and sampling.
  • Materials and equipment within the isolator should be positioned to avoid crowding and need for reaching over exposed sterile product, materials, and surfaces.

Rationale

Human interventions and manipulations within the isolator in the vicinity of exposed sterile product, materials, and surfaces can add the risk of microbiological contamination to sterile product. Where possible, the elimination or reduction of these activities through process design, equipment layout, technology, and automation can reduce risk. Where activities cannot be eliminated or reduced, process and equipment design that allows for proper aseptic technique and practices can reduce risk.

References: PDA TR34 (2001); FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004); USP <1208>; EU GMP Annex 1 Consultation Document (2017); PIC/S PI 032-2 (2010); PIC/S PI 014-3 (2007); ISO 14644-1:2015.

中文解說 Commentary

核心概念：介入風險的三個控制維度

Q1-3 提供了一個思考介入風險的框架，風險來自三個向量：

  • 時間（Duration）：介入持續越久，暴露風險越高 → 優化操作流程縮短時間
  • 複雜度（Complexity）：動作越複雜，失誤機率越高 → 簡化設計或使用工具輔助
  • 距離（Proximity）：越靠近暴露無菌表面，後果越嚴重 → 重新布局物料與設備位置

這三個維度都可以在設計階段介入——而不是等到問題發生後靠 SOP 補救。

比喻：外科手術室的設計邏輯

優秀的手術室設計，讓外科醫師不需要跨越無菌區域去拿器械，護士遞器械的路徑不會越過手術視野。

Isolator 的介入設計也應如此：充填站周圍的物料補充，能否設計成自動補給？環境監測採樣點，能否設計在不干擾充填操作的位置？這些都是 Q1-3 要求你在 DQ 階段就回答的問題。

自動化趨勢：機器人在 Isolator 中的應用

EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 明確鼓勵在無菌操作中採用機器人和自動化技術。Q1-3 的建議與這個方向一致：

  • 自動化裝配（如泵管安裝）
  • 機器人無菌採樣（EM active air sampling）
  • 自動化物料傳送（RTP + 機械手臂）

White Raven（競爭對手）的 SA25 充填機已整合這類技術——這是 CDMO 需要追趕的差距。

Section 1 總結 — Key Takeaways

  • Isolator 的核心優勢：物理隔絕 + VHP 自動消毒，提供比 RABS 和傳統無菌室更高的無菌保證水平，同時允許周圍環境降至 Grade D（ISO 8），大幅降低設施成本。
  • 壓差是第一道防線：正壓差 >10 Pa 是最低要求，須 24/7 連續監控並設報警。Mousehole 設計必須有 airflow visualization 數據支撐。

Q2-1: What should the classification of the room surrounding the isolator be?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Determination of the classification level should be risk-based, taking into consideration the risk of introducing contaminants during preparation of the isolator with open doors, as well as movement into and out of the isolator during operation.

Note: Current FDA guidance recommends a background environment of no less than ISO 8 in operation; current EU GMP Annex I recommends ISO 8 at rest (Grade D).

Rationale

When isolator systems are properly designed, contaminants are not exchanged between the interior of the isolator and the surrounding environment. Robust qualification of the isolator system provides assurance that the classified space within the isolator can be maintained.

Additional considerations for the surrounding room include maintaining a clean and organized space and controlled access. Temperature and humidity settings are generally for the comfort of the operators, but potential impact to decontamination processes of the surrounding environment must be considered.

The quality of the air inside the isolator is more critical to the process than the outside air. When the isolator functions properly and monitoring and alarm systems are integrated, the risk of the room air causing an issue to the process is minimal. Nonetheless, the space surrounding the isolator should be controlled and meet airborne particle limits for cleanrooms, as defined in ISO 14644, in order to provide a high degree of assurance that surrounding conditions and practices will not negatively affect the aseptic processes within the isolator.

Although Annex 1 requires only Grade D "at rest" determination of airborne particle count, it is common practice to apply at least the ISO 8 limit "in operation" for open and closed isolators with gloves. Higher risk operations may require more rigorous control. However, a lower Grade surrounding area/controlled environment may be acceptable for gloveless isolators that present no access during aseptic operation.

直覺比喻：為什麼隔離器外的房間還需要分級？

把隔離器想像成一個密封的魚缸。魚缸內的水質才是關鍵，但如果魚缸外的空氣充滿塵埃，每次開門換水時仍然存在污染風險。周圍環境分級，就是為那些「開蓋時刻」建立一道緩衝防護。

操作主任的視角：兩個情境的差異很重要

At rest（靜態）：設備就緒但未生產，人員不在場。EU 的 Grade D 要求是在這個狀態下量測的。

In operation（運作中）：生產進行中，人員活動增加粒子。FDA 要求的 ISO 8 是在這個更嚴苛的條件下符合。

實務上，許多廠房兩種情境都按 ISO 8 管控，以確保在任何狀態下都合規。法規查核時，inspector 通常會確認兩者都有量測記錄。

無手套隔離器的例外

完全密閉的無手套（gloveless）隔離器，在無菌操作期間不存在人員介入的開口，因此周圍環境要求可以相對寬鬆。這是設備設計直接影響廠房分級成本的典型案例。

Q2-2: Should the isolator fill zone have unidirectional flow?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Unidirectional airflow may not be required within an isolator fill zone where it has been demonstrated that nonunidirectional (i.e., turbulent) air does not present the risk of product contamination. In all cases, however, the airborne particulate limit should not exceed that for ISO 5. Likewise, the zone surrounding an isolator need not provide unidirectional air. In this zone, the airborne particulate limit should not exceed ISO 8 in operation.

Rationale

Airborne contamination is the major risk in the fill zone and other critical zones within an isolator, because the physical barrier provided by the isolator eliminates potential contamination directly from personnel. Air in the isolator is filtered through HEPA filters, providing the same degree of cleanliness whether or not flow is unidirectional. The purpose of unidirectional flow is to prevent the incursion of airborne contamination in the form of viable and nonviable particulates from outside the clean air stream.

In addition, all outside items introduced into the isolator are decontaminated, entering either through rapid transfer ports or a sterilizing tunnel. Nonsterile air incursion through isolator openings (e.g., an exit mousehole), is prevented by a positive pressure differential to the outside. The risk that surface contamination will become airborne in a turbulent air flow is obviated by effective chemical decontamination.

With proper risk analysis, an effective isolator decontamination process, and a rigorous aseptic fill challenge, turbulent air flow may be demonstrated to be acceptable in an aseptic fill isolator. ISO 5 is required in order to achieve an airborne particulate level equivalent to that prescribed for unidirectional airflow.

ISO 8 in operation is recommended for the area around an isolator to provide a controlled environment even though air exchange between this zone and the interior of the isolator is not possible.

中文導讀與詮釋

關鍵概念：為什麼隔離器可以允許湍流？

傳統無菌室裡，Grade A 區域必須使用單向流（Unidirectional Flow / UDAF），原因是要把人員帶來的微生物「沖走」，避免其漂向產品。

但隔離器的邏輯完全不同——人員已被物理屏障隔絕在外，VHP 去污程序已消滅表面微生物，所有進入物品都經過除菌。因此，氣流的主要任務從「趕走人帶來的污染」變成「維持 ISO 5 的粒子環境」。HEPA 過濾本身就能做到這點，不一定需要單向流的方向性。

比喻：「空氣品質」vs.「空氣方向」

單向流就像一陣固定方向的清潔風，把污染物吹走；湍流則像房間裡均勻循環的冷氣，只要空氣本身夠乾淨（ISO 5），污染物就不會積累。

隔離器的屏障讓後者成立——前提是 VHP 去污有效，且每次進出物料的界面（mousehole、RTP）都能維持正壓差。

法規審查時的重點：你能證明什麼？

選擇湍流設計，代表你必須在驗證報告中提供足夠證據：粒子計數符合 ISO 5、APS（Media Fill）結果合格、mousehole 正壓差持續受控。這些是 inspector 一定會看的項目。如果選單向流，舉證負擔相對較低，但設備成本也高。

Q2-3: Should airflow pattern visualization/demonstration be performed in an isolator?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Unidirectional airflow should first be verified by measuring air velocity 150–300 mm from the HEPA filter face as described in ISO Standard 14644-3. Once the airflow velocity distribution has been established, the airflow may be further verified by an airflow visualization or "smoke" study.

If nonunidirectional airflow is employed, only directional airflow at the boundaries of the isolator, e.g., mouse-hole exit, need be performed. Differential pressure between the isolator and the surrounding zone should be monitored continuously.

Rationale

For unidirectional isolators, the airflow velocity test will establish the airflow streamlines as delivered at the HEPA filter. Airflow visualization is used to ensure that any disruption to these streamlines, e.g., by a stationary object in the flow path, does not present a risk of contamination. This type of test may be extended to observe the effect of routine and expected non-routine glove-aided interventions. Where unidirectional flow is specified, "first-air" principles should be maintained.

Nonunidirectional isolators should be demonstrated with a high degree of assurance to not present a risk of product contamination. In such isolators, airflow will not necessarily follow defined streamlines and may be subject to random backflows and eddy currents. Visualization of these flows is of no value. On the other hand, measuring pressure differential satisfies this concern, but airflow visualization at the points determined by risk analysis to be vulnerable to incursion adds a high degree of assurance.

Air flow visualization studies should be performed at system qualification. In general, they do not have to be repeated except where a risk assessment has determined they are required, as in the case of a significant equipment change.

中文導讀與詮釋

氣流可視化研究（AVS / Smoke Study）是什麼？

用發煙裝置（煙霧發生器或商業煙棒）在隔離器內產生可見霧氣，再透過攝影或錄影記錄煙霧的流動路徑。目的是確認：

  • 單向流的「first-air」原則是否被遵守（乾淨氣流先接觸產品）
  • 手套操作介入是否造成危險的回流或渦流
  • mousehole 等界面是否維持正向氣流方向（空氣只出不進）

法規申報的關鍵：「in operation」才算數

FDA 強烈暗示 AVS 必須在「in operation」情境下執行，EU GMP Annex I 對 Grade A / ISO 5 環境更是明確要求。也就是說，只有靜態的煙霧照片是不夠的——必須有操作人員透過手套進行典型介入動作時的錄影記錄。

提醒：影片必須作為 GMP 文件保存，並在法規查核時可提供展示。

重複測試的門檻：什麼時候要重做 AVS？

初次驗證做過之後，通常不需要定期重做。但以下情況需透過 risk assessment 評估是否重做：重大設備改造（如 HEPA 換位）、隔離器佈局大幅更動、新增介入動作類型、或發生污染事件需要根因調查。

思考練習

你的廠房隔離器使用湍流設計，近期更換了一個位置偏移的 mousehole 密封墊。這種情況是否需要重做 AVS？用 risk assessment 的角度思考：mousehole 是「最脆弱的氣流界面」，答案應該是肯定的。

Q2-4: Should the isolator environment qualification runs (at rest and in operation) include the sample sites established according to ISO 14644-1 or can a reduced sampling plan be followed?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

For total particle count, the firm should use the methodology defined in ISO 14644-1. Because ISO 14644-1 only defines limits for total airborne particles, qualification of performance for viable counts should require a risk assessment to determine sampling methodology and location.

Rationale

ISO 14644-1 is the international consensus standard recognized by the European Union, PIC/S, and the U.S. FDA to define cleanroom classification and performance. ISO 14644-1 provides for a specific and well-defined sampling plan, including the minimum number of sample locations, sampling pattern, sample size, and methodology to interpret the data. By following the ISO standard, a firm may be highly confident in the isolator design and, upon recertification, continuing performance.

Qualification and recertification according to the ISO standard for total particle count provides assurance that pockets of potential contamination do not exist.

Q2-5: What techniques should be used to identify, track, and evaluate air flow during qualification and maintenance of the isolator?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

As with any pharmaceutical operation, a risk-based approach should be applied to isolators. Because airborne particulate presents a significant risk to pharmaceutical operations carried out within isolators, airflow visualization studies are often considered essential to assessing that risk. Video recording these studies provides even more value by allowing further analysis by more reviewers, as well as providing permanent documentation.

ISO 14644-3 Appendix B-7 describes various test methods applicable to airflow visualization, including: (a) tracer thread method, (b) tracer injection method, (c) airflow visualization method by image-processing techniques, and (d) airflow visualization method by the measurement of velocity distribution.

The tracer injection ("smoke" study) method is most frequently recommended for pharmaceutical isolators, for both unidirectional and nonunidirectional isolators. Aerosol fog covering a larger area to test unidirectional flow may be generated from deionized water or from commercial organic and inorganic solutions. Commercially available "candles" or "sticks" generate lesser quantities of visible fog and are usually limited to testing airflow direction at critical interfaces (e.g., mouseholes, transfer isolators).

Rationale

Evaluation of test results will depend upon the perceived risks. At critical isolator interfaces, such as mouseholes or transfer points, directional airflow can be visualized and recorded, adding evidence to air pressure differential measurements. Inadequate flow or even unacceptable reversals may be discovered and corrected before committing actual product to the isolator.

In operation, airflow visualization can help in the evaluation of the effect of normal movement (e.g., conveyed vials and stopper transport), as well as routine and nonroutine interventions. Particular attention should be paid to human activity through glove ports and the introduction of items through transfer ports. This can help distinguish between safe and unsafe technique and provide a valuable training aid, especially in video form.

Physical measurement, such as airflow velocity and pressure differential, provide important data regarding isolator operation. Experience has shown, however, these measurements alone may not be adequate unless correlated to actual experience. The FDA guidance on aseptic processing strongly implies that air visualization studies be conducted and emphasizes the "in operation" application of the test. EU GMP Annex I requires it in Grade A/ISO 5 environments.

Q3-1: How should personnel be gowned when working in the isolator or the background environment area during aseptic processing?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Depending on the classification of the surrounding area, it may be prudent to gown according to the company's Grade C/D gowning requirements. In addition, it is a good practice for operators working in isolator gloves to perform interventions or other activities inside the isolator to wear a separate set of sterile, cleanroom gloves. Street clothing should not be worn in the environment of production isolators to minimize the bioload in the room and in the isolator during cleaning, loading, and unloading.

For loading, unloading, and cleaning opened isolators, an additional hood, sterile goggles or glasses, and gloves, in addition to Grade C garments, should be considered to reduce the risk of particles and microbial load.

Rationale

The isolator provides a good physical barrier between the environment and the critical area; therefore, a Grade A/B garment is not required. Gloves worn underneath the isolator gloves will reduce the microbial load within the isolator gloves. Wearing sterile gloves provides an additional layer that can help to protect against potential ingress of microorganisms from the environment in the event of a pinhole in the isolator glove.

During the loading of the isolator, the aim is to keep the microbial load low. Skin flakes, hair, and particulates from operators may result in microbial or particulate contamination after the VHP-decontamination cycle. Operators should wear gloves to reduce the risk of passing oily substances/sebum onto the surface of the isolator as this could prevent the surface from being decontaminated.

隔離器 vs. 傳統無菌室：更衣需求比較

隔離器更衣簡化的邏輯

傳統無菌室裡，人員本身就是最大污染源，所以要用 Grade A/B 全身包覆來「封住人」。但隔離器把人和產品完全分開了——人在外面，產品在裡面。人員更衣的目的因此從「避免污染產品」轉變為「降低周圍環境的微生物負荷」，後者所需的保護等級自然低得多。

「裝載時刻」是最高風險的時間窗口

VHP 去污循環開始之前，隔離器必須開蓋裝入物料和組件。此時屏障失效，人員的皮屑、頭髮、皮脂直接暴露在隔離器內表面。油性皮脂特別危險——它會阻礙 VHP 的去污效果，形成微生物保護層。

因此，裝載前必須戴手套（避免皮脂污染），並考慮戴頭罩和護目鏡。這是一個即使更衣要求較低，仍需特別注意的操作節點。

稽核思考

如果 inspector 問你：「你的操作人員在進行隔離器裝載時，具體穿戴了哪些防護裝備，依據是什麼？」你的 SOP 是否明確規定了這個 risk-based 選擇，並且有文件化的 rationale？

Q3-2: Should operator gloves be disinfected prior to entering isolator gloves?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Sterile gloves should be disinfected before entering isolator gloves. Use of sterile 70% isopropanol or 70% ethanol is recommended, or another sanitizing/sporicidal agent can be used. The disinfectant can be applied by spraying or wiping.

Rationale

Disinfected gloves worn underneath isolator gloves will better reduce the microbial load within the isolator gloves than nondisinfected gloves. In the case of a pinhole in an isolator glove, a reduced microbial load on the inside of the isolator glove will reduce the chance of microbial ingression into the isolator.

中文導讀與詮釋

手套消毒的完整邏輯鏈

這是一個「多層防護」的典型設計，每一層都有其作用：

  1. 無菌內手套：降低人員皮膚微生物直接接觸隔離器手套內壁的風險
  2. IPA 70% 消毒：進一步殺滅殘留在無菌手套表面的微生物
  3. 隔離器外層手套：提供物理屏障，阻擋外部環境污染進入

這三層組合的目標：即使外層手套出現 pinhole，穿越漏點進入隔離器的微生物數量趨近於零。

為什麼是 70% 而不是 100%？

70% IPA/乙醇的殺菌效果優於純酒精，因為水分有助於蛋白質變性、促進滲透細胞壁。100% 酒精蒸發過快，來不及發揮作用。這是製藥消毒的基礎知識，但在法規查核中常被問到。

「pinhole 假設」是隔離器安全設計的核心思維

整個 Topic 3 的設計邏輯建立在一個假設上：如果手套出現肉眼不可見的微小漏點，我們的其他措施能否防止污染進入隔離器？ 這種「worst-case 假設」正是 PDA 推薦多層防護的理由，也是手套完整性測試（Topic 4）如此重要的原因。

Topic 4: Integrity Testing of Isolator and Gloves

Q4-1 ~ Q4-3 · Pages 18–21

Q4-1: What methods should be used for integrity testing of isolators and gloves?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Integrity testing of the isolator and gloves should be performed using visual, mechanical, and/or physical methods. Testing should be performed at defined periods — at a minimum, at the beginning and end of each batch or campaign and following any intervention that may affect the integrity of the unit.

Interventions that pose a risk to the integrity of the gloves or isolator should be avoided when possible. When they cannot be avoided, the integrity of the gloves should be visually inspected after such interventions.

Pressure decay is confirmed using a fully automated, validated, and calibrated instrument for pre- and post-batch integrity testing and periodic integrity testing. Visual inspection should precede and augment pressure-decay testing.

Rationale

Isolators have components (including gloves) constructed of various materials that can be prone to puncture. Glove integrity is critical to maintaining the aseptic environment within the isolator. Integrity issues during production runs, regardless of type, create a potential contamination risk. Process impact is the same whether the process is a development (i.e., media fills) or a regular production run.

Q4-2: What should the maintenance program be for gloves?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

Expectations and practices regarding inspection and replacement of glove systems can be documented within the maintenance program. This should be supported with a quality risk assessment that details the rationale for glove replacement frequency.

The use of a glove may not exceed the glove vendor's expiry date. Common replacement frequencies are between 6 and 12 months. Based on the required quality risk assessment, each glove may have a unique replacement frequency. Not all gloves in a system are required to be changed at the same frequency. Trending of glove performance is recommended, as this will ensure proactive identification of any developing patterns, such as type of damage or increased damage frequency to a particular glove location.

Gloves should not be used until failure. Frequency of preventive maintenance activities must be established via risk assessment with input from the vendor.

The quality risk assessment should include, but not be limited to:

  • Glove material of construction
  • Frequency of glove use
  • VHP contact time or concentration (delamination can occur due to prolonged VHP/decontaminant exposure)
  • Historical data for loss of integrity
  • How gloves are used (e.g., handling sharp-edged equipment, stretching, etc.)
  • Type of disinfectant used for inner disinfection of the gloves
  • Ability to clean residual product from the gloves

Rationale

Glove integrity is critical to product quality. A robust risk assessment including specific process parameters will provide the information needed for maintenance procedures. Site procedures should define maintenance and operational procedures based on risk. Some manufacturers offer the ability to replace gloves aseptically.

Risk Assessment 驅動的手套更換計畫

PDA 的核心立場是：沒有適用所有廠房的固定更換週期。每個隔離器、每個手套位置，都可能因使用強度和環境條件不同，而有不同的最佳更換頻率。

「不應等到失效才換」是 PM（預防性維護）的精髓

等到壓降測試失敗才換手套，代表該手套已在「已知失效」狀態下使用了一段時間——而在那段時間內，你不知道有沒有批次被污染。預防性更換的目的正是避免這種不確定性。

趨勢分析（trending）在這裡非常有價值：如果某個手套位置的壓降測試數值逐漸變差（但仍在 pass 範圍），提前換掉比等到 fail 更安全。

VHP 對手套材質的傷害：常被忽略的因素

長期暴露在高濃度 VHP 下，某些手套材質會出現「去層化（delamination）」——表層材料逐漸分離。這個過程是累積的，不會突然發生。因此，VHP 循環的次數和濃度必須納入 risk assessment，同樣重要的還有消毒劑種類（某些消毒劑對橡膠材質的腐蝕性更強）。

文件化挑戰

你的維護程序是否能回答以下問題：（1）每個手套位置的預定更換頻率是多少？（2）這個頻率的 risk assessment 依據是什麼？（3）最近一次更換記錄在哪裡？法規查核時，這三個問題都可能被詢問。

Q4-3: What is the response to a glove integrity failure?
Original English (PDA PtC-Isolators)

Recommendation

A procedure should detail the actions required when a glove failure is discovered. Physical failure of gloves should be treated as a deviation, and the associated investigation must be documented. The response and actions taken may vary, depending on when the failure is identified.

Scenario 1 — Before a production run: The run should not proceed until the failure is investigated and resolved.

Scenario 2 — During line setup or prior to isolator decontamination: The nonintegral glove should be replaced.

Scenario 3 — Subsequent to isolator decontamination, but prior to product exposure: The glove should be replaced and the decontamination cycle repeated. Conducting a full investigation may not be necessary if the previous post-batch physical integrity test and visual check passed. However, an investigation to determine the cause of the failure may be helpful for identifying actions required to prevent a repeat of the failure.

Scenario 4 — During/after a production run: An investigation should be conducted to determine the cause of the failure and the impact on the filled product. The investigation should consider the location of the glove, the activities performed by those wearing the glove, and the timing of integrity tests that passed. Prior to resuming production, the glove should be replaced and the conditions that caused the failure should be resolved.

Rationale

Quality risk management should be applied for each scenario and corrective action should be taken in order to minimize the risk of contamination. Each site should evaluate the specific processes and procedures used. It is not acceptable to use nonintegral gloves or gloves with pinhole leaks during media fills or manufacturing processes. Validation activities should ensure adherence and performance to aseptic processing requirements.

當手套失效發現於生產過程中或結束後，調查必須回答三個問題：

  1. 位置：失效手套在哪個位置？它距離產品暴露區域多近？

Topic 5 — Environmental Monitoring（環境監測）

Q5-1: EM 監測頻率應如何設定？

Original Text (English)

Q5-1: What should the environmental monitoring frequency be in an isolator?

Recommendation

A risk-based approach should be employed to determine the frequency of surface monitoring in an isolator as well as the frequency and duration of airborne monitoring. The approach is similar to that used for conventional filling lines and cleanrooms, although the results of the risk assessment may be different.

In some cases, the design of the isolator (e.g., gloveless isolators) may not allow the performance of some airborne monitoring methods, such as settling plates. Therefore, less intrusive methods may be advantageous.

Surface sampling of the isolator interior and equipment in the isolator (including gloves) should be conducted at the end of the production batch or campaign, but prior to decontaminating or opening the isolator. Surface sampling may also occur after an intervention or activity that requires decontamination of the isolator prior to resuming production.

Airborne monitoring should start prior to any aseptic processing activities performed in the decontaminated isolator, including any equipment or parts setup being performed after decontamination. Total particulate monitoring should be continuous over the duration of critical activities (i.e., when product or product contact surfaces are exposed). Airborne viables should be sampled in a manner that provides a representative record of air purity for the duration of the run while not introducing added risk to product.

Trending of environmental monitoring results should be analyzed regularly to assess contamination control performance.

Rationale

Although the interior of an isolator is decontaminated by a chemical agent, monitoring throughout the run is necessary to demonstrate continuing contamination control. This is particularly important as an isolator may allow for extended runs beyond those normally associated with more traditional lines. Monitoring frequency and duration must be carefully considered to optimize benefit while risk is managed. The contamination risk introduced by required sampling interventions, as well as for sample media, must be assessed.

Sampling of gloves and other surfaces at the end of the production run will provide adequate evidence of contamination while not introducing risk to product whether or not media is introduced (e.g., use of swabs wetted with buffer solution).

For airborne viables, sampling methodology should be selected from various alternatives (e.g., settling plates, active air samplers) to ensure adequately characterized air purity while limiting the necessary number and type of interventions. The daily or batch-wise monitoring of air is considered to be an adequate level of contamination control during campaign production/batch filling over several days.

Modern particulate-monitoring systems allow for continuous airborne monitoring for total particulate without introducing risk to product. Such systems should be employed wherever possible.

解說與評析（繁體中文）

核心邏輯：為什麼隔離器的 EM 頻率可以「相對降低」？

傳統 Grade A 潔淨室的 EM 之所以頻繁，是因為周圍環境（Grade B）仍存在人員、氣流擾動等污染源；每次干預都是高風險事件。

隔離器的情況截然不同：內部已經過 VHP 化學除污，外部操作員無法直接接觸產品。污染風險大幅降低，因此 EM 頻率可以透過風險評估來決定，不必照搬傳統標準。

表面採樣的時機：為什麼要「批次結束後、除污前」？

原則很直觀：你要取得生產過程的真實紀錄，而非除污後的乾淨表面。若先除污再採樣，數據就失去意義了。這和 FDA 2004 年無菌指引的立場一致。

例外情況：發生需要中途除污的介入（intervention）之後，也應補做採樣。

比喻：非活性 vs. 活性粒子監測

想像你在錄影（非活性：總顆粒數，連續監測）和定期做文化培養（活性：微生物，批次/每日採樣）。

現代粒子計數器可以全程 24 小時連續記錄，不需要介入——這就是「無干擾監測」的理想。活性微生物採樣則需要放置培養皿或使用 SAS/RCS 主動採樣器，每次介入都帶來額外風險，因此頻率應謹慎設計。

長時間連續生產（Campaign）的挑戰

隔離器的優勢之一是可以做 multi-day campaign（跨天連續批次），但這也意味著 EM 程序需要支持長時間操作。PDA 認為：每日或每批次的活性空氣監測即屬足夠。

這對你的 CDMO 作業有直接意涵：如果你的 campaign 跨 3 天，活性採樣可能每天做一次，而非每幾小時做一次。

Q5-2: 哪些表面應納入監測？

Original Text (English)

Q5-2: Which surfaces should be monitored in an isolator?

Recommendation

Risk-based approaches should be used to select surface monitoring locations in an isolator. Emphasis should be placed on product contact surfaces (i.e., surfaces that directly contact product or a primary filling component), although risk analysis may determine that not all direct contact surfaces need to be sampled. Other locations may also be chosen if they are considered to present significant risk to product or to confirm overall contamination control.

Gloves are of particular interest, because they are involved in setup and both routine and non-routine interventions. Filling needles are also important as they provide the most direct product contact and, potentially, the greatest risk.

Sampling of filling needles or other surfaces where product may have accumulated should be considered for the possibility of growth inhibition inherent to the product itself (i.e., preservative action). In this case, a location adjacent to the needles (e.g., the needle rack) that has not been exposed to product may be chosen.

Rationale

Besides the risk of product contact, determination of surface sampling locations may take into account other mitigating or exacerbating factors. For example, sampling of every contact surface may not be necessary if other samples are deemed to adequately represent the location in question. Risk factors to consider may include the proximity of the locations, if they are subjected to the same potential airflow disturbances, and if they are exposed to higher risk interventions.

Where interventions are necessary, sampling locations may be selected based on the associated risk. If these interventions are not routine, additional surface sampling may be required.

If a surface has been sterilized and not threatened by an intervention, it may not need to be sampled. Conversely, if a surface has been decontaminated but not sterilized, as may be the case with certain filling component-handling devices, surface monitoring would usually be performed.

解說與評析（繁體中文）

選點優先順序（風險由高到低）

  • 手套（Gloves）：每次介入都要經過手套——是污染最可能的入口，優先採樣。
  • 灌裝針（Filling needles）：與產品最直接接觸，風險最高。
  • 其他直接接觸面：可用風險評估判斷是否需要全數採樣。
  • 間接接觸面：如已滅菌且未受介入威脅，可豁免採樣。

含防腐劑產品的陷阱

如果你的產品含防腐劑（如 benzalkonium chloride），直接從灌裝針採樣可能導致假陰性——微生物根本長不起來。

解法：改採針架（needle rack）或其他沒有直接接觸產品的鄰近位置。這是一個需要在 EM 計畫中明確說明的技術決策。

「已滅菌 vs. 僅除污」的實務差異

已滅菌（如高壓滅菌後密封轉入）的表面，若沒有發生任何介入威脅，監測優先級可降低。但如果某設備只接受了 VHP 除污（非 steam sterilization），監測還是必要的——因為 VHP 的保證水平（SAL）與高壓滅菌不同。

介入風險分層

常規介入（routine intervention，如更換廢棄物袋）vs. 非常規介入（non-routine，如突發性修復）——後者風險更高，需要增加臨時採樣點。這個邏輯應寫入你的 EM 計畫 SOP 中。

Q5-3: 隔離器 EM 偏差與傳統產線的處理方式有何不同？

Original Text (English)

Q5-3: Should environmental monitoring excursions in isolators be treated differently compared to excursions in conventional filling lines?

Recommendation

Regarding batch disposition, environmental monitoring excursions in an isolator must be treated the same as excursions on a more traditional line. A deviation investigation should take into account all relevant information pertaining to the risk that product may be contaminated, and an appropriate decision made as to batch release.

Regarding system performance, environmental monitoring excursions in an isolator may raise deeper concerns beyond those for a more traditional line. Because an isolator is specifically designed and operated to ensure environmental control and eliminate contamination, the likelihood of environmental monitoring excursions should be significantly reduced. Therefore, environmental monitoring excursions that are detected in isolators are of heightened concern and should be investigated at a more detailed systemic level, taking into account the structure and function of the isolator itself, as well as the more traditional routes of contamination.

Rationale

An environmental excursion in a critical zone, as for an isolator, requires an investigation regardless of the technology employed. Environmental monitoring is a critical element of the sterility assurance program and an important measure associated with control in clean areas. Monitoring provides meaningful information about the quality of the environment and helps identify routes of contamination which, in turn, allows for corrective and preventative actions to ensure product safety.

Isolators are designed and qualified to separate product from sources of contamination. Any contamination detected inside an isolator must be considered a system failure and trigger respective investigation and corrective action. These include, but are not limited to, effectiveness of decontamination, air flow and pressurization, isolator integrity, glove integrity, and interventions.

If an isolator system failure is the root cause for the environmental recovery, then broader potential product impact (i.e., prior batches) may need to be considered, as well as a more complex corrective and preventive action.

解說與評析（繁體中文）

雙軌思考：批次放行 vs. 系統失效

PDA 在這裡劃了一條很重要的界線：

  • 批次放行決策：與傳統產線相同——需要偏差調查，根據污染風險做出判斷。一個陽性結果不自動否決批次，但必須調查。
  • 系統層面的意涵：比傳統產線更嚴重——隔離器本來就不應該有 excursion，因此一旦發生，代表某個系統屏障（VHP 除污、正壓、手套完整性）可能已失效。

為什麼隔離器的 excursion 訊號更強？

在 Grade B 開放環境中偶爾出現一個陽性，可能只是偶發性的空氣擾動。但在隔離器內部——一個被設計成幾乎不可能污染的封閉系統——出現陽性，幾乎必然代表系統性失效（systemic failure）。

這也意味著你需要追溯：這個失效點在多久之前就已存在？之前的批次是否也受影響？

調查清單（隔離器專屬）

  • VHP 除污循環效果是否確認？（生物指示劑結果）
  • 隔離器壓力差（正壓）是否全程維持？
  • 隔離器本體完整性（integrity test）是否合格？
  • 手套完整性是否在批次前後均經測試？
  • 有無非常規介入？轉移埠開關紀錄是否完整？

在開放廚房（傳統潔淨室）偶爾冒煙，可能只是烹飪過程的正常現象。但如果是防火保險庫（隔離器）內警報響起，那就是重大事件——你必須假設系統已被突破，而不能只看局部的煙。

Q5-5: 將 EM 培養皿轉移進出隔離器時，應注意哪些因素？

Original Text (English)

Q5-5: What factors should be taken into consideration when transferring environmental monitoring plates into and out of an isolator?

Recommendation

In general, rigorous procedures and precautions should be employed when transferring any sterile item into a critical (i.e., aseptic) zone. Transfer into an isolator, however, typically requires special procedures due to the isolator's built-in access restrictions.

Where the use of settling plates has been determined to be valuable, they may be contained within a sterile outer bag and moved from a transfer isolator through a rapid transfer port into a previously decontaminated isolator. Care should be taken to ensure that the media is not contaminated with microorganisms or chemical disinfectants during the transfer and handling.

Alternatively, plates within a nonsterile bag may be placed in the main isolator (or an external airlock) prior to decontamination. In this case, the bag is hung so that all external surfaces are decontaminated effectively. The bag material must be selected to avoid penetration by the decontaminating agent to preserve the growth promotion capability of the media. If a media additive is used to neutralize the decontaminant, its effectiveness in preserving media function must be demonstrated.

At the end of the batch, plates must be secured appropriately (e.g., in a sterilized bag) before they are removed from the isolator to avoid any risk of false positive results from contact with the surrounding environment.

Rationale

The design, confined space, and restrictive access of the isolator present unique challenges for the effective transfer of media plates into and out of the isolator. Improper handling and transfer may result in false positive or false negative results.

When it is necessary to use plates of nutrient media in an isolator, it is important that the transfer of the plates into and out of the isolator results in neither contamination of the media nor of the isolator.

  • 假陽性：取出時培養皿接觸外部環境（未適當密封），或帶入了不屬於生產過程的污染。

Q6-1: 間接接觸面應如何滅菌或除污？（四種方案）

Original Text (English)

Q6-1: How should indirect product contact surfaces be sterilized or decontaminated?

Recommendation

Equipment surfaces like stopper bowls and tracks are considered indirect product contact surfaces because they contact sterile components. These surfaces should not contaminate or pose an unacceptable risk of contaminating sterile product. To eliminate microbiological contamination, indirect product contact surfaces should be treated to achieve and maintain a state that does not contaminate the sterile components.

The predominant method for sterilization of indirect product contact surfaces outside of the isolator is moist heat/autoclave sterilization, although dry heat sterilization or irradiation may also be appropriate. Additionally, or as an alternative, the decontamination of these indirect product contact surfaces in the isolator can be largely achieved through or during the isolator decontamination process by methods such as VHP.

The approach for treating, transferring, handling, and installing indirect product contact equipment depends on the design of the isolator and facility. A risk assessment should be used to determine an effective approach for treatment of the indirect product contact equipment given those design considerations.

Approach 1: Equipment sterilized → aseptically transferred directly into isolator through Grade A/ISO 5

  • Equipment cleaned, wrapped, and autoclave-sterilized.
  • Transferred directly into decontaminated isolator through a transfer section or port connected to autoclave.
  • Precautions for moisture/heat ingress; aseptic unwrapping and assembly inside.
  • Manipulations must be within scope of process simulation (APS).

Approach 2: Equipment sterilized → transferred through classified environment → assembled → decontaminated

  • Equipment cleaned, wrapped, sterilized. Transferred into open isolator (classified background).
  • Run unidirectional flow during loading to create outward airflow to protect against ingress.
  • Remove overwrap; assemble using Good Aseptic Practices. Then run full VHP decontamination cycle.
  • Operators handling sterile components should wear additional hood, sterile goggles, and sterile gloves.

Approach 3: Equipment sterilized → transferred through non-Grade A → decontaminated while wrapped → assembled

  • Equipment cleaned, wrapped, sterilized. Validated decontamination cycle performed with equipment still wrapped.
  • Note: wrapped materials may absorb H₂O₂ and impact cycle; occluded surfaces must be considered.
  • After decontamination, wraps removed and parts assembled aseptically.

Approach 4: Large equipment — periodically sterilized via in-place decontamination cycle

  • For equipment too large to remove: clean with validated process, then decontaminate in place with validated VHP cycle.
  • Frequency of sterilization is risk-based.
  • Cleaning must remove silicone, oily residues, fingerprints that inhibit VHP effectiveness.
  • Bioburden of indirect contact surfaces must be controlled.
解說與評析（繁體中文）

名詞釐清：直接 vs. 間接接觸面

  • 直接接觸面（Direct contact）：灌裝針、stopper、container — 必須 steam sterilization（高壓蒸汽）。
  • 間接接觸面（Indirect contact）：stopper bowl、stopper track——接觸的是 stopper 而非藥液本身。可用 autoclave 或 VHP 處理。

這個區分是本題的核心：間接接觸面的滅菌要求略低，但必須確保它們不會污染直接接觸面。

四個方案的風險層級比較

方案主要滅菌方式輔助除污特殊風險
Approach 1Autoclave（主）無水分/熱帶入、手套操作風險
Approach 2Autoclave（主）VHP（次）開放隔離器時外部污染風險
Approach 3Autoclave（主）VHP（次，帶包裝）包裝吸 H₂O₂、遮蔽面除污不均
Approach 4VHP（原位）無 autoclave油脂殘留影響 VHP 效果

Approach 4 的關鍵限制：清潔第一

VHP 是強氧化劑，但它有「弱點」：油脂、矽膠、指紋都會在表面形成保護層，降低 VHP 對微生物的殺滅效果。因此，Approach 4 能否成功，完全取決於清潔步驟的品質。這也是為什麼清潔必須「驗證」，而非僅「確認」。

比喻：盔甲下的弱點

Autoclave 是「完整盔甲」——物理滲透，殺滅保證最高（SAL 10⁻⁶）。VHP 是「化學噴霧」——覆蓋廣但容易被油脂擋住。選方案時，要看你的武器能不能到達目標表面。

APS（Aseptic Process Simulation）涵蓋範疇

Approach 1 和 3 都明確要求：設備轉移和組裝的手套操作必須納入 APS（無菌工藝模擬）。這不只是文件要求——你的 media fill 設計必須真實再現這些步驟，否則驗證結果不具代表性。

Q6-2: 包裝材料完整性是否需要測試 VHP 滲透？

Original Text (English)

Q6-2: Should a test for packaging material integrity of load items be carried out with respect to potential for VHP ingress?

Recommendation

Packaging integrity should be qualified for items being decontaminated and transferred for subsequent processing (e.g., decontamination chamber). When items are placed in heat-sealed breather bags, it is important to validate that package integrity is not compromised during the heat-sealing process.

For VHP processes in isolators, the integrity of the packaging materials to prevent ingress, and their compatibility with VHP, should be tested prior to use. If a robust materials compatibility evaluation has been conducted, routine testing during the process should not be required.

Whether or not packaging material should be tested post-decontamination would depend on the decontamination process. Specifically, if a pressure differential is created across the exterior/interior of the package, it could potentially impart a force that creates integrity concerns.

Rationale

If packaging is not integral, sterility could be compromised because decontamination gasses could reach the inside of the package and impact materials.

解說與評析（繁體中文）

為什麼包裝完整性對 VHP 特別重要？

VHP（氣化過氧化氫）是一種氣體——它可以滲透密封不完整的包裝袋，進入並破壞內容物（培養基、stopper 表面、已滅菌器械等）。

這裡有一個雙向風險：

  • VHP 滲入：破壞無菌物品的無菌狀態或功能性。
  • 壓差造成完整性失效：若 VHP 循環中產生壓差，可能將袋材撐破或密封處剝離。

驗證一次 vs. 每次測試

PDA 的立場很務實：若已做過完整的材料相容性評估（materials compatibility evaluation），日常生產不需要每次都測試袋材完整性。一次驗證搞定，降低日常操作負擔。但這個前提是——驗證本身必須夠嚴謹，且覆蓋實際使用條件。

熱封袋的特殊風險

Breather bag（透氣袋，用於高壓滅菌）以熱封封口。熱封過程本身可能造成密封不完整（邊緣燒焦、未完全熔合）。驗證時必須測試：熱封後的袋材在 VHP 壓差條件下是否仍維持完整。

Q6-3: Stopper 如何從 Grade C/D 轉移進入 Grade A？

Original Text (English)

Q6-3: What approaches are recommended for transferring stoppers from outside Grade C or D to inside Grade A/ISO 5 — manual or automated rapid transfer system?

Recommendation

Approach A: Ready-to-use, sterilized stoppers packed in a bag connected to a rapid transfer port (RTP)

The packaging and sterilization process is typically performed by a supplier at a different location before being shipped to the pharmaceutical company. The ready-to-use stoppers are delivered in a second bag for protection of the primary packaging and for safe transfer from a lower to a higher cleanroom class.

Before connecting the beta port on the bag to the alpha port of the isolator RTP, the external surfaces of the ports can be cleaned with a sporicidal agent. After the ports are connected and locked, the RTP door can be opened. Good practice recommends having a protective sleeve on the stopper bag to avoid contact with the area of greatest potential for contamination.

During packaging, transport, storage, and debagging, either the primary and secondary package could be damaged and, thus, open to potential microbiological contamination. Therefore, inspecting the integrity of the bag prior to use is a good practice.

The RTP and the protective liner are opened and positioned manually. During this positioning process, the stoppers could potentially come into contact with the gloves, which should be avoided.

Approach B: Ready-to-sterilize stoppers packed in a Tyvek bag connected to an RTP

The stopper supplier washes and packages the stoppers, but the stoppers would be sterilized at the manufacturing site in an autoclave. The port system for the transfer must be prepared and handled in the same manner as for the ready-to-use stoppers. The distinction from the ready-to-use approach is that the sterilization would be performed in-house rather than at the supplier.

Approach C: In-house stopper processing with washing, sterilization, and docking of the process and transport container to the isolator for unloading

This approach prevents the risk of microbiological contamination from the transfer process associated with ready-to-use or ready-to-sterilize solutions. The stopper processing system is equipped with all the utilities needed to clean, siliconize, sterilize, and dry the stoppers. The process can be validated and performed in-house at the manufacturing site.

Sterilized stoppers remain in the pressurized container until the container is connected through a butterfly valve to an RTP beta port. The RTP beta port connects directly to the RTP alpha port on the isolator. The RTP alpha-beta port can be opened automatically without human intervention, which prevents any contamination risk by gloved operations. A transfer pipe is inserted in the alpha-beta port connection to cover the RTP ring of concern to prevent the stoppers from coming in contact with it. Once the container, valve, pipe, and ports are in place, the transfer valve is opened slowly to avoid an instantaneous release of pressure, and the stoppers are transferred to the stopper bowl.

Rationale

These three approaches are commonly used in aseptic processing to reduce the risk of contamination ingress when transferring stoppers using RTPs. The relative level of risk of each approach will depend on the capability of the manufacturing site and the processing vendor. Companies should assess and take into consideration the relative risks when deciding which approach to use.

解說與評析（繁體中文）

RTP（Rapid Transfer Port）是什麼？

RTP 是隔離器物料進出的專用轉移埠，採用 Alpha-Beta 雙埠設計：

  • Alpha port：固定在隔離器本體上，始終維持在無菌 Grade A 狀態。
  • Beta port：連接在要轉入的容器/袋子上，由外部操作員對接。

對接後，兩個埠的內表面形成一個「封閉通道」，Alpha 門打開後，外部環境永遠不會直接接觸隔離器內部。這是隔離器物料轉移的黃金標準。

三種方案的風險梯度

方案滅菌地點人工介入整體風險
A: RTU（現成滅菌）供應商手動定位，手套可能接觸 stopper中（依賴供應鏈）
B: RTS（自行高壓）製造廠同 A中（滅菌可控）
C: 全廠內處理製造廠（全程）自動對接，無手套介入最低

Approach A 的關鍵弱點：供應鏈完整性

RTU stopper 的滅菌是在供應商端完成的，到你工廠之前要經過包裝、運輸、儲存、入庫。每個環節都可能造成袋材損傷。使用前的袋材完整性目視檢查不是可選步驟，而是必要的品質控制。

Approach C 的技術亮點

全廠內處理的最大優勢：stopper 在加壓容器中保持無菌，直到自動 alpha-beta 對接完成。Alpha 門自動打開，無需手套操作——這完全排除了「手套觸碰 stopper」的風險，是目前污染風險最低的方案。缺點是設備投資大，需要現場 stopper 清洗/矽化/滅菌系統。

比喻：太空站對接艙口

Alpha-Beta 對接就像太空站的對接艙口——兩邊各自密封，對接時形成一個安全通道，宇航員（stopper）從容器移到太空站（隔離器）全程不接觸外太空（外部環境）。這個機械設計本身就是污染控制的一道屏障。

Q6-4: 預裝盤（tub）的注射器或小瓶如何除污並轉移進隔離器？

Original Text (English)

Q6-4: How should tubs of sterilized syringes or vials be decontaminated and transferred into the isolator?

Recommendation

Surface decontamination for ready-to-use items, like nested vials or syringes in tubs, has become more common in aseptic processing in recent years. These tubs are packed in a single- or double-liner for transport. For the tubs to be transferred into the isolator via pass-through with VHP or electronic beam (E-beam), the outside packaging (liners) are removed.

VHP Airlock:

For a VHP airlock, the tubs are positioned inside the airlock and then decontaminated on the outer surface using VHP. The VHP airlock is a batch unit for the safe transfer of tubs into a filling-line isolator; with this method, a 6 log reduction of surface contamination and a cycle time of less than 30 minutes can be reached. For prefilled pharmaceutical products that are sensitive to VHP, it is recommended that precautions be taken to avoid penetration of VHP inside the tub.

E-beam (Electronic Beam):

If the outside surfaces of the tubs are decontaminated by E-beam, they should be positioned in front of the E-beam and, then, automatically transferred inside the E-beam where the outer surface of the tubs are decontaminated with beta electrons. The E-beam is a fast and continuous decontamination system to transfer the tubs inside a filling-line isolator; with this system, a 6 log reduction (25 kGy) and capacity of six tubs per minute can be reached.

Alternate methods for transferring presterilized and bagged materials that do not involve VHP decontamination or E-beam may be considered. Where alternate methods are considered, a risk-based assessment should be performed to determine their capability and effectiveness. Any alternate method must be qualified and validated.

Rationale

Both the VHP airlock and E-beam technologies can be used and validated as an efficient outside decontamination process for tubs. The E-beam process has two advantages: it is continuous, as opposed to using a batch process, and it circumvents VHP penetrating the insides of vials and other types of containers. VHP airlock and E-beam technologies are currently the only automated technologies that can be validated and those that are considered fully GMP-compliant.

為什麼 E-beam 是更現代的選擇？

E-beam（電子束）用 beta 電子直接撞擊表面微生物，無需化學氣體，因此：

  • 不存在 VHP 滲入內部的風險——對玻璃瓶內乾燥無影響。
  • 連續作業，產能更高（6 tubs/min），與高速灌裝線更匹配。
  • 已有完整驗證框架，符合 GMP 要求。

何謂「外表面除污」？

重要概念：VHP 和 E-beam 的目標都是 tub 的外包裝表面（outer liner 去除後的 tub 外壁），而非內部的注射器或小瓶。內部的無菌狀態是由供應商的滅菌工藝保證的。轉移過程只是確保「外壁帶入隔離器時不會污染 Grade A 環境」。

比喻：食品外包裝消毒

就像無塵室食品工廠在食品進入作業區之前，先對外包裝噴酒精消毒——目的是讓「外部帶菌的包裝」不污染內部環境，而非重新滅菌食品本身。E-beam 就是更強版、更可驗證的外包裝消毒工具。

CDMO 選型建議

如果你的 CDMO 未來要處理 VHP 敏感藥品（如某些蛋白質製劑、含有機物的組合產品），E-beam 通道是值得投資的設備。批次 VHP airlock 雖然成本較低，但在連續高速灌裝場景下可能成為產能瓶頸。

Section 3 — 重點總結 Key Takeaways

  • EM 頻率可降低，但不可省略：隔離器的 VHP 除污降低了 EM 基線風險，允許採用比傳統 Grade A 更低的頻率——但連續非活性監測（粒子）仍為必要，活性採樣可每日或每批次執行。
  • 表面採樣選點三優先：手套 > 灌裝針 > 其他直接接觸面；已滅菌且未受威脅的表面可豁免；含防腐劑產品需改採鄰近替代位置。
  • EM excursion 雙重調查框架：批次放行邏輯與傳統相同（風險評估決定）；但系統層面調查更嚴格——任何隔離器內的陽性都視為系統失效，需追溯至 VHP、壓差、完整性、手套、介入等各屏障。
  • 培養皿轉移程序決定數據品質：轉入時防 VHP 滲透（袋材驗證、中和劑驗證）；轉出時防外部接觸（密封袋保護）；任何失誤均可能造成假陽性或假陰性。
  • 間接接觸面四方案：Autoclave 為主要滅菌方式；VHP 為補充除污；Approach 4 完全依賴 VHP 原位除污（大型設備），清潔品質是成敗關鍵。
  • 物料轉移黃金標準是 Alpha-Beta RTP：Stopper 轉移三方案中，全廠內處理（Approach C）的自動對接方案污染風險最低；RTU 方案依賴供應鏈完整性，使用前袋材目視檢查是必要步驟。
  • VHP vs. E-beam：兩者都能達到 6 log reduction；E-beam 連續、無 VHP 滲透風險，更適合 VHP 敏感品或高速灌裝線；VHP airlock 投資成本較低，適合批次生產場景。

Q7-1  |  Cleaning & Disinfecting Isolator Interiors Prior to Decontamination
Original Text — PDA PtC-Isolators 2020

Recommendation

The qualification or validation of the cleaning and disinfecting of nonproduct contact surfaces should be based on risk assessment. The cleanliness of nonproduct contact surfaces should be visually verified, at the least.

Cleaning

The decontamination process does not remove product or foreign material. The interior of the isolator should be cleaned to remove product residue and foreign material prior to the decontamination process.

The cleaning process should be documented in a procedure. The cleaning process should be reliable and adequate to remove levels of product residue and foreign material that can pose a risk to the quality of the next product or the performance of the decontamination process.

The cleaning process should be developed based on process and product requirements, the design of the isolator interior, and the surfaces to be cleaned. Potential considerations include:

  • Dedicated versus shared or multiproduct isolators
  • Complexity or variability of the cleaning process
  • Location of surfaces to be cleaned within the isolator
  • Activities performed at given locations within the isolator
  • Exposure of product to potentially contaminated environment or surfaces
  • Toxicity and potency of previous product
  • Sensitivity of next product to presence of previous product
  • Difficulty in removing product residue or foreign materials from isolator surfaces
  • Ability to visually or analytically detect presence of product residue or foreign material
  • Diversity of products handled or filled in the isolator
  • Effect of cleaning material residue on product or isolator interior
  • Material surface quality and cleanability
  • Effect of product or foreign material residue (including cleaning material residue) on decontamination process
  • Other controls in place to address the risk of contamination of product with previous product residue and foreign material

Disinfection

Since the effectiveness of the decontamination process may depend on the level of bioburden present, a disinfection process may establish a baseline prior to the decontamination of the isolator interior.

The disinfection process should be validated and documented in a procedure. The disinfection process should include limits on hold time prior to disinfection (time after end of isolator cleaning to initiation of disinfection process) and hold time after disinfection (time after completion of disinfection process to initiation of decontamination process).

A risk assessment should be used to determine the maximum level of bioburden allowable to assure effectiveness of the decontamination process and the method for disinfection. Risk-based considerations may include:

  • Complexity, variability, and effectiveness of the disinfection process
  • Complexity, variability, and effectiveness of the decontamination process
  • Location of surfaces to be disinfected within the isolator
  • Bioburden levels assessed through periodic surface monitoring of the opened isolator
  • Routine environmental monitoring program of the isolator
  • Environmental monitoring of environment surrounding the isolator
  • Ability to remove disinfectant from isolator interior and sensitivity of product to levels of disinfectant
  • Occurrence of any occluded surfaces from moving parts that will be exposed during manufacturing operations, e.g., conveyor belts

If the bioburden of the isolator interior is higher than the spore log reduction of the validated decontamination cycle, an additional disinfection step may be required after the cleaning process.

The design of the isolator interior should be adequate for the cleaning and disinfection process. Design considerations may include:

  • Materials of construction should be cleanable and resistant to cleaning and disinfectant chemicals, treatment, and process
  • Surfaces should be smooth and nonporous
  • Surfaces should drain properly and not pool or accumulate liquids
  • Surfaces close to exposed product or product contact surfaces should not have excessive or deep seams that can harbor product residue or foreign material
  • Critical spaces should be accessible to the cleaning procedure
  • Areas within the isolator that expose product or generate or harbor foreign material should be limited

Other Considerations

Precautions should be taken to avoid excessive exposure to foreign material and microbiological contamination when opening the isolator and exposing the interior to the surrounding environment. To reduce the risk of particulates and microbial load, in addition to Grade C garments, wearing an additional hood, sterile goggles or glasses, and gloves should be considered.

A quality risk document for the complete process within the isolator must define critical areas where product could be open and process operations are critical. The higher the risk to the product, the more detailed the cleaning and disinfection procedure needed.

Rationale

The objective of the isolator cleaning and disinfection procedure is to remove residues, foreign matter, and bioburden prior to decontamination of the isolator interior to ensure the effectiveness of the decontamination cycle and, thus, the sterility of product.

A risk-based approach to designing the cleaning, disinfection, and decontamination processes will help identify the conditions and variables that must be controlled. This approach will also help establish the procedures, parameters, and acceptance criteria needed to ensure that the isolator is in a condition that allows for an effective decontamination and manufacturing process.

解說與思考 — 繁體中文

核心觀念：清潔不等於除汙

這是很多工程師容易混淆的地方。清潔（Cleaning）是移除殘留物和異物；消毒（Disinfection）是降低活菌數（bioburden）；除汙（Decontamination）才是真正的孢子殺滅步驟（通常靠 VHP）。三步驟必須依序完成，缺一不可。

比喻：手術前的皮膚準備

想像外科醫師消毒切口前，一定先清除傷口的汙垢與碎屑——你不會把碘酒噴在泥土上就算消毒完成。VHP 也一樣：殘留的藥物粉末或油脂會在孢子和 VHP 之間形成「保護盾」，讓除汙效果大打折扣。

因此，「清潔驗證除汙」（cleaning validates decontamination）是這一章的核心邏輯——你的清潔程序要能確保 VHP 接觸到所有孢子，而不是接觸到殘留物的表面。

重點：Hold Time 的雙重管制

PDA 要求消毒程序中必須定義兩個時間窗口：

  • Hold time prior to disinfection：清潔結束 → 開始消毒的最長等待時間
  • Hold time after disinfection：消毒完成 → 開始 VHP 除汙的最長等待時間

這兩段時間若超過限制，bioburden 可能回升，讓消毒步驟失去意義。在 SOP 撰寫時，這兩個參數必須明確寫入並驗證。

CDMO 實務提醒：多產品共用隔離器

如果你的隔離器是 multiproduct（不同品項輪流使用），清潔程序必須針對「前一個產品的毒性與殘留風險」設計。毒性愈高的產品，清潔驗證標準愈嚴格。這直接關係到交叉汙染風險評估（cross-contamination risk assessment）的結果。

設計要點：隔離器內部材質

PDA 列出的設計考量，說白了就是「容易清潔、不藏汙納垢」：

  • 表面光滑無孔（smooth and nonporous）——粗糙表面藏孢子
  • 不積液（drain properly）——積液是 bioburden 溫床
  • 縫隙最小化——尤其靠近產品路徑的區域

如果設計本身有缺陷，再完美的清潔 SOP 也無法彌補。

思考練習

你的隔離器如果今天 bioburden 監測結果超過已驗證除汙週期的 spore log reduction 上限，下一步應該怎麼做？（提示：PDA 原文已經給了答案——additional disinfection step。）

Q7-2  |  Current Options for Isolator Interior Decontamination
Original Text — PDA PtC-Isolators 2020

Recommendation

The predominant decontamination agents currently being used for isolator interior decontamination are VHP and peracetic acid. Alternative agents and technologies could become available that may be considered for decontamination. A careful risk assessment should be performed to evaluate, select, develop, qualify, and validate agents, technologies, methods, and cycles based on equipment condition, process performance, product quality and sterility, and personnel safety.

The selection of the decontamination process, agent, and dispersion technology or procedure should take into consideration the:

  • Effectiveness of the decontamination process as applied to the condition, design, and use of the isolator
  • Production requirements
  • Potential impact of the agent and procedure on the isolator, product, process, and personnel
  • Corrosive or reactive properties of the agent and its impact on the equipment and materials present in the isolator
  • Potential impact of the agent on personnel and the external environment
  • History or familiarity with an agent or technology
  • Preference for an agent or technology at the user's site or location
  • Availability of alternative agents or technologies

VHP: Vapor Phase Hydrogen Peroxide

Decontamination agents are typically dispersed in two basic states: gaseous and vapor phase. VHP in the vapor phase may be used to decontaminate pharmaceutical isolators. VHP decontamination is an extremely potent, complex process due to its biphasic nature — both liquid and vapor (gas) phases are typically present in the chamber. The phase equilibrium is extremely sensitive to minor differences in temperature, VHP, and water concentration, making it virtually impossible to know the exact process physical parameters at any given time and location.

No matter what system is being used, the process fundamentals remain the same. VHP is distributed in the decontaminated chamber until enough VHP has been deposited (i.e., adsorbed/condensed) on the decontaminated surfaces to yield the targeted decontamination effect. Increasing the saturation of the VHP atmosphere increases the efficacy, because a large amount of liquid VHP is deposited on the surfaces.

Too much deposited VHP, however, can lead to long aeration and increasing material compatibility and VHP ingress concerns. Two main approaches for VHP vapor generation used in the industry are hot plate vaporization and fogging.

Hot Plate Vaporization

Hot plate vaporizers use heat to transform injected liquid aqueous VHP solution into hot vapor, which is then distributed within the chamber to be decontaminated. Hot plate vaporizers should be operated to ensure flash vaporization of injected VHP. An operating temperature too low or liquid dosing too high may result in VHP boiling that will affect the efficacy and may even lead to explosion. Hot vapor entering the chamber should be well distributed to prevent local hot spots or positions with too much VHP condensation.

When using remote vapor generators, premature cooling and condensation of generated VHP vapor inside the piping needs to be prevented before reaching the target destination.

Fogging Processes

Fogging processes are based on the generation of micron-sized droplets with enormous surface area, which facilitates natural vaporization without the need of additional heat. The vaporization of droplets continues until the maximum vapor saturation is reached and the fog is created.

Depending on the generated droplet size, fog is termed "dry" or "wet" by the industry. "Wet" fog is considered aerosol with droplet sizes larger than approximately 15–20 µm. Such particles settle and attach more easily to surfaces compared to smaller droplets — decontamination using "wet" fog is a combination of vapor- and liquid-deposited VHP.

Sprays with a droplet size below 15 µm are typically considered "dry." Small droplets experience minimal sedimentation and tend to bounce off surfaces without leaving liquid deposits. Adsorption of vapor phase VHP on surfaces is the main mechanism of action. Dry fogging provides fast surface decontamination and reduces aeration time due to short contact time of the VHP on surfaces.

Nozzles or nebulizers should be operated to allow enough space for the spray to develop and spread throughout the chamber; objects too close to the nozzle may receive an excessive deposit of VHP "wetting." The initial temperature and humidity should be considered for fogging processes as these determine how much VHP vapor can be generated by atomization.

Rationale

This recommendation primarily focuses on VHP because it is the predominant agent and technology used. Due to the complexity of the entire decontamination process, diversity of isolator designs, aseptic processes, products, components, and materials exposed in the isolator, a risk-based approach is recommended. It is essential to carefully evaluate, qualify, and validate the method and the equipment used to disperse and monitor the process.

三種除汙劑快速比較

除汙劑 業界地位 主要特性
VHP（汽化過氧化氫） 業界主流（2017 PDA 調查多數廠用 VHP） 兩相平衡複雜，溫濕度敏感，需完整 mapping
過氧乙酸（Peracetic Acid） 次要選項 腐蝕性較強，對金屬材質需評估相容性
二氧化氯（ClO₂） 替代技術，少數設施使用 穿透力強，但安全性與廢氣處理要求高

比喻：VHP 的「兩相平衡」是關鍵難點

想像浴室裡的水蒸氣：溫度高一點，鏡子不起霧；溫度低一點，整面鏡子都是水珠。VHP 也是如此——表面溫度決定「凝結量」，凝結量決定殺孢子效果。

這就是為什麼 PDA 說 VHP 除汙「幾乎不可能精確掌握某一時刻某一點的物理參數」——它是一個動態平衡的複雜過程，不是「噴一噴就好」的消毒。

熱板蒸發 vs. 霧化：兩種 VHP 產生方式

熱板蒸發（Flash Vaporization）：

  • 液態 H₂O₂ 注入熱板，瞬間汽化成乾燥高溫蒸氣
  • 優點：蒸氣乾燥，均勻分布
  • 風險：溫度不夠或注液過快 → 沸騰（非閃蒸）→ 效果差，甚至爆炸
  • 遠端產生器需防止管路中提前冷凝

霧化（Fogging）：

  • 不需加熱，靠超細噴霧（micron 級液滴）自然蒸發
  • Dry fog（<15 µm）：液滴不沉降，以蒸氣相吸附為主，曝氣時間短
  • Wet fog（>15–20 µm）：液滴沉降附著，液相 + 蒸氣相雙重作用
  • 風險：噴嘴附近物件可能過度濕潤

CDMO 實務重點：材質相容性評估不可省

VHP 具強氧化性，選定除汙劑後，必須確認隔離器內所有材質（橡膠手套、不銹鋼、塑膠件、電子元件）的相容性。如果因腐蝕導致隔離器元件降解，不只是設備問題，更是產品汙染風險。

監管視角：沒有通用標準，但需要完整驗證

PDA 明確指出，目前業界沒有統一的 VHP 機械設計或分佈系統標準（no industrial standard）。這意味著監管機構（FDA、EMA）期待每個廠自行建立並驗證其使用的系統——你的驗證文件本身就是你的「標準」。

Q7-3  |  Empty Isolator Mapping of Temperature and Humidity
Original Text — PDA PtC-Isolators 2020

Recommendation

Temperature mapping of isolators or decontamination chambers should be consistent with the requirements for decontamination. When using a decontamination agent that is in a gaseous or vapor phase, surface temperatures can influence the amount of localized condensation, which may impact the effectiveness of the chosen decontaminant. Ideally, surface temperatures are uniform, thus promoting homogeneous levels of surface condensate.

The intent of temperature and humidity mapping is to provide evidence that they are distributed evenly, ensuring a consistent distribution and lethality of the chemical decontaminant. When choosing mapping locations, things to consider include:

  • Energy sources (e.g., lights, fans, servos, squeales)
  • Movement of items during the decontamination cycle
  • Material properties (e.g., stainless versus polymer)
  • Placement of equipment, parts, shelving, platforms, or surfaces that can affect the flow of air or act as a heat sink
  • External room conditions
  • Attached process equipment, like autoclaves and lyophilizers, that could generate heat on the outer surfaces attached to the isolator
  • Loading items with temperatures different from the room (e.g., bottles directly from a freezer)

Infrared cameras can be used during initial assessments to determine the placement of temperature-sensing instruments. The reflective properties of the internal surface material, however, must be considered, because they could negatively impact findings. Based on this initial screening, infrared cameras may prove useful in identifying hot and cold spots, which could then be mapped with thermocouples. In some instances, however, equipment configurations or existing cold spots may mask the existence of hot spots.

Humidity mapping within the isolator should be considered if there are materials of construction that could cause localized variances in humidity.

Mappings could be performed during "empty" conditions, if the load items are not expected to influence temperature or humidity conditions. Mapping is expected to be performed under loaded conditions. The influence of load items on air flow and material of construction should be considered.

Rationale

Since the effectiveness of a gaseous or vapor phase process can be impacted by surface condensation levels, and that temperature plays an important role in determining the degree of condensation, temperature mapping is an important aspect of cycle qualification. If there are specific conditions that determine potential variations, humidity mapping should also be undertaken.

解說與思考 — 繁體中文

為什麼要先做空腔 mapping？

Mapping 的邏輯是：先了解空腔環境的溫濕度分布，才能預測 VHP 凝結量在哪裡多、在哪裡少。

如果某個角落比其他區域溫度低 2°C，VHP 就容易在那裡過度凝結，形成液滴——液相 VHP 雖然殺孢子力強，但過多時會延長曝氣時間，甚至傷害材質。反之，溫度較高的熱點，VHP 蒸氣不易凝結，殺孢子效果可能不足。

比喻：溫度 mapping 就像畫天氣地圖

你不能假設整個隔離器內部溫度均一，就像你不能假設整棟廠房的溫度一樣。必須先「量」才能知道哪裡是高壓（熱點）、哪裡是低壓（冷點），然後把 biological indicator（BI）放在最難殺到的地方——也就是「最差條件」位置。

重點：空腔 mapping ≠ 最終 mapping

PDA 說得很清楚：空腔 mapping 只是起點，最終的資格確認必須在負載條件（loaded conditions）下進行。原因是：

  • 不銹鋼零件的熱容量高（heat sink），會讓周圍局部溫度下降
  • 物件遮蔽了氣流，改變 VHP 分布
  • 從冷藏取出的瓶子（如直接從冷凍庫）會帶入冷點

空腔 mapping 的價值在於識別「固有熱點/冷點」，讓你知道應該把 thermocouple 和 BI 放在哪裡。

工具選擇：紅外線相機的局限性

PDA 提到可以用紅外線相機（IR camera）做初步掃描，但有個陷阱：不銹鋼表面反射率高，IR 讀值不準確。因此 IR 只能用於初步識別，最終仍需熱電偶（thermocouple）做精確量測。此外，現有的冷點可能遮蓋後方的熱點——所以不能只靠一次掃描就下定論。

濕度 mapping 的必要性

VHP 的汽液平衡除了受溫度影響，也受相對濕度（RH）影響。如果隔離器內有吸濕性材質（如某些塑膠或聚合物零件），局部濕度會改變，進而影響 VHP 的凝結行為。因此，當材質多樣性高時，濕度 mapping 不能省略。

思考練習

你的隔離器連接著一台凍乾機（lyophilizer），其外壁在除汙過程中會散熱。這對隔離器的 temperature mapping 有什麼影響？你會在哪裡放 thermocouple？

Q7-4  |  Conditions and Configurations During Decontamination Cycle Development
Original Text — PDA PtC-Isolators 2020

Recommendation

A risk assessment should be used to determine the "worst case" conditions and configurations to challenge during decontamination cycle development and validation.

The load configuration should be set up to avoid any materials potentially touching, which could lead to occluded surfaces where the decontamination agent could not reach.

Process, loading configuration, and load item variables, which could impact either the distribution or the inactivation rate of the decontamination agent, must be considered when determining worst-case conditions.

Clutter, piling, or placing materials that can occlude surfaces from contact with decontamination agent should be avoided, as should contamination of surfaces with substances, such as oil, grease, fingerprints, or silicone, that can interfere with or otherwise adversely affect the decontamination of those surfaces.

Conditions and Configurations to Consider

Decontamination Process

  • Environmental conditions (temperature, humidity range, and variation)
  • Fan or blower speed (fixed or variable) or isolators without a fan
  • Decontamination agent concentration, dose levels, and rates of application

Load Configuration

  • Minimum and maximum loads
  • Load placement and orientation
  • Characteristics of load materials
  • Exposure of material and load surfaces
  • Extension of gloves
  • Position of doors and openings

Material Properties

  • Compatibility of materials with the decontamination agent or process
  • Porosity of materials
  • Stacking or placement of materials
  • Substances or materials on a surface that could adversely affect the decontamination process or outcome
  • Wrapping or folding of wrapping materials that occlude surfaces from decontamination

Rationale

The intent of decontamination cycle development and validation is to ensure that the decontamination process is robust and provides the desired spore logarithmic reduction at conditions that create the greatest challenge for biological indicator (BI) inactivation or decontamination distribution, while remaining within the planned operating conditions.

The isolator environment and load characteristics play a crucial role in the effectiveness of the cycle. Effective surface decontamination requires the exposure of the surface to a sufficient quantity of the agent; consequently, many variables are involved.

For more information, PDA Technical Report No. 34 details considerations of several of the conditions listed above, and PDA Technical Report No. 51 explores the potential factors involved in BI manufacturing that can also impact the accuracy of data gathered during cycle validation.

解說與思考 — 繁體中文

「最差條件」的邏輯

除汙週期開發的核心問題是：「最難被殺到的孢子在哪裡？」 這個問題的答案就是你 BI 的放置位置，也是你「worst-case」負載配置的設計依據。

如果你在最差條件下能達到目標 spore log reduction（通常 ≥6-log），那麼在所有較佳條件下也一定能達到。這是驗證策略的核心邏輯。

比喻：最差條件就像壓力測試

銀行壓力測試不是在「正常市場」下測，而是在「最糟市場條件」下測。除汙週期驗證也一樣——你要在「最擠、最遮蔽、最難到達的負載配置」下放 BI，才能證明你的週期是真的強健（robust），而不是只在空腔或輕負載時才有效。

遮蔽表面（Occluded Surfaces）：最常見的失敗原因

實務上，除汙失敗最常見的原因就是「遮蔽」——VHP 氣體無法接觸到的表面。常見的遮蔽情況：

  • 零件緊靠在一起，接觸面無法接觸 VHP
  • 手套摺疊或捲曲，內表面無法曝露
  • 包材折疊後形成密閉空間
  • 大型不銹鋼零件背面（陰影區）

因此，負載配置規格（load configuration specification）必須固定並文件化——任何改變都需要重新評估是否影響 VHP 分布。

油脂、指紋、矽油：除汙的隱形殺手

PDA 特別提到 oil、grease、fingerprints、silicone 等表面汙染物，這些物質會在孢子和 VHP 之間形成物理屏障，大幅降低殺孢子效果。這就回應了 Q7-1 的清潔邏輯——在除汙前，所有表面必須清潔乾淨，尤其是手工操作時觸碰到的表面。

三層最差條件矩陣

層面 最差條件範例 影響機制
製程條件 高溫高濕（或低溫低濕）的邊界條件 影響 VHP 凝結量與分布
負載配置 最大負載 + 最密集堆疊 遮蔽表面最多，氣流最受限
材質特性 多孔材質（如紙張）+ 高密度不銹鋼 吸附 VHP 導致局部濃度降低

如果你的負載配置在生產中需要依批次調整（例如不同規格的零件組合），這對除汙週期驗證策略意味著什麼？你會設計幾個 worst-case 配置？

  • VHP 是業界主流，但沒有通用標準（Q7-2）：熱板蒸發與霧化是兩種主要 VHP 產生方式，各有其操作風險。VHP 的兩相平衡特性使製程控制複雜，每個廠必須自行建立並驗證其系統。
  • 溫濕度 mapping 是週期資格確認的必要步驟（Q7-3）：空腔 mapping 識別固有熱點/冷點；負載 mapping 才是最終標準。外接設備（凍乾機、高壓滅菌釜）與低溫物料都會影響隔離器內部的溫度分布。
  • 最差條件負載驗證（Q7-4）：遮蔽表面（occluded surfaces）是除汙失敗最常見原因。負載配置必須固定並文件化，任何改變都需重新評估。油脂、指紋等表面汙染物會干擾 VHP 效果，再次強調清潔的關鍵性。
  • 整合視角：清潔 → 消毒（bioburden 降低）→ 溫濕度 mapping → 最差條件負載驗證 → VHP 週期——這是一條環環相扣的驗證鏈，任一環節薄弱都會影響最終的無菌保證。

Q7-5 — Key Differences Between Cycle Development and Cycle Qualification

Original Text (pp. 47–48)

Recommendation

Decontamination cycle development and cycle qualification may involve similar equipment and process performance testing and monitoring. The objective is to develop and qualify the suitability, reliability, and performance of equipment, systems, and instrumentation supporting the decontamination process, as well as the ability or effectiveness of the decontamination agent to eliminate microorganisms.

Cycle Development

Cycle development establishes the conditions, parameters, and settings for an effective cycle. As such, cycle development may involve the adjustment of conditions, parameters, and settings to meet the objective of the effective cycle. Cycle development testing and monitoring may indicate the reaction of adjustment to the output of the cycle.

Cycle Qualification

Cycle qualification provides documented evidence of the reliability and effectiveness of the developed cycle. As such, cycle qualification involves testing the performance of the prescribed conditions, parameters, and settings of the cycle development that normally would not be changed during or after the cycle qualification. Cycle qualification testing and monitoring is designed to confirm that the cycle is performing to specifications. For a production cycle, an overage of decontaminating agent and/or exposure time is typically included.

The qualification or validation of the decontamination process should provide confidence that microbiological contamination of all surfaces within the isolator have been eliminated or reduced to a level that will not result in contamination of sterile product, materials, or surfaces. Decontamination cycles for critical areas used in aseptic processing are commonly validated to a 6 log reduction of the biological indicator. Lower levels of log reduction may be acceptable based on risk assessment.

Documentation

Since cycle development provides information needed to design and qualify the cycle, proper documentation is important; however, it may not require the same level of rigor and quality assurance scrutiny as cycle qualification documentation.

Cycle development protocols should be flexible, allowing for parameter adjustments as the result of a failure or an unexpected outcome. While triplicate runs are common for validation activities, a single confirmation run could be acceptable for cycle development.

The cycle qualification protocol should use findings and recommendations from cycle development to confirm the robustness and repeatability of the process. Triplicate runs are expected for the process qualification.

Rationale

A key difference between decontamination cycle development and cycle qualification is that the objective of cycle development is to design a decontamination cycle or procedure that achieves the intended result. The objective of cycle qualification is to provide evidence that the developed cycle can reliably perform to prescribed specifications and meet prescribed acceptance criteria.

Settings, load configurations, and operating conditions may change during cycle development to meet criteria, while they must remain constant and unchanging during qualification.

導讀與解析

核心區別一眼看清

這一題的答案其實是兩個字的差距：開發（Design）vs. 驗證（Evidence）。

  • Cycle Development（循環開發）：你在「探索地形」。哪裡難以到達？H₂O₂ 濃度要多高才夠？溫度、濕度的影響有多大？允許調整，允許失敗，允許從頭來過。
  • Cycle Qualification（循環確效）：你在「出考卷並要求滿分」。所有參數已鎖定，只允許確認，不允許修改。

比喻：食譜研發 vs. 廚師認證考試

開發就像廚師在廚房裡反覆嘗試一道菜，鹽多了可以減，火大了可以調，目的是找到最佳配方。

確效就像廚師認證考試當天，必須照著已定好的食譜做，考官在旁邊記錄每一步，過程不能臨時更改，三次都要端出一樣的成品。

實務關鍵：6 log reduction

無菌充填用 isolator 的除污循環，通常要驗到 6 log 滅活——也就是 10⁶ 個孢子要殺到零。這不只是慣例，更是業界基準線。若要採用較低的 log reduction，必須有完整的風險評估文件支撐，監管單位審查時會直接追問理由。

文件嚴謹度的不同期待

  • 開發階段：允許彈性協議（flexible protocol），單次確認執行即可，失敗可調整後重跑。
  • 確效階段：需要三次重複執行（triplicate runs），每次都必須符合預設的接受標準；若失敗，須啟動調查與 CAPA。

開發的文件是為了「設計出好循環」；確效的文件是為了「向監管單位證明好循環可以重複執行」。

思考一下

如果你的團隊在確效執行過程中，第二次跑出異常數據，你能直接修改參數後繼續第三次嗎？為什麼不行？這個問題的答案，就是確效與開發最重要的邊界線。

Q7-6 — Which BI Should Be Used? Requirements for Spore Count, D-Value

Original Text (pp. 49–50)

Recommendation

  • BI should be a suitably qualified organism (e.g., Geobacillus stearothermophilus) with not less than 10⁶ spores/carrier.
  • Microbial count recovery of incoming biological indicator control should be between 50% and 300%.
  • BIs should be handled and stored at temperatures and humidity conditions as specified by the vendor prior to use.
  • Carrier should be made of a material that is also commonly used in the isolator, typically stainless steel. Additional studies might be required if carrier materials differ significantly from stainless steel.
  • BI should consist of a monolayer of spores to guarantee direct contact of the VHP to the spores on the carrier.
  • BI should be packed in a material permeable for H₂O₂ and easy to handle in the lab.
  • D-value of the BI should be known and should be comparable from one validation to the next in order to make validations comparable and to see any shift in the efficacy of the VHP decontamination.

Different methods are available for determining the D-value. The D-value should be tested in a given isolator system, not just assigned to a BI lot. The D-value can be defined as the time required to kill 90% of the population, reduced by 1 log. A fractional approach may be used as an initial check at one location without a full load.

The D-value is usually determined in a routine place in the isolator, not a hard-to-reach surface. Some isolator manufacturers offer a service to pretest BIs prior to shipment, and to test for D-value and consistency within the lot. Maintaining one BI supplier and a consistent lot within cycle development and qualification may reduce variability.

Rationale

The high variability of BIs must be taken into account for development of a consistent and effective cycle, as the BI lot will obviously change during the life cycle of the line during requalification work. Although in-house D-value testing is not often performed, it provides valuable information and can ensure more successful requalification over time.

The value assigned by a vendor effectively represents the D-value obtained in their isolator under their cycle conditions. This will invariably differ compared to the D-value obtained in any other system. There is significant risk associated with using a D-value for a BI lot from a supplier as opposed to confirming it in your own isolator system prior to use. The D-value depends on the saturation of air with hydrogen peroxide and the consistency of the cycle over time.

導讀與解析

為何選 G. stearothermophilus？

Geobacillus stearothermophilus（嗜熱地芽孢桿菌）是 VHP 除污確效的標準挑戰菌。它的孢子對 H₂O₂ 具有高度抵抗性，成為「最壞情況」的代理指標。換句話說：如果這個菌都殺得乾淨，一般環境菌就更不用說了。

最低要求：每個 BI 載體上至少 10⁶ 個孢子（≥ 6 log），這樣才能在殺光之前觀察到對數遞減的動態。

D 值的真正意義與隱藏風險

D 值（Decimal Reduction Time）= 在特定條件下，殺死 90% 孢子所需的時間（即減少 1 log 所需時間）。

問題在於：供應商的 D 值是在他們的 isolator、他們的循環條件下測得的。換到你的設備，H₂O₂ 的分布、氣流、溫濕度完全不同，D 值可能差距甚大。

最佳實務：在你自己的 isolator 系統中進行 in-house D 值測試，而不是直接沿用供應商資料。

比喻：D 值就像料理時間表

供應商告訴你「這道菜在 200°C 烤 20 分鐘」，但那是用他們的烤箱測的。你的烤箱可能溫度不均，同樣設定實際上只有 180°C。你需要在自己的烤箱裡重新校準，不能直接套用別人的食譜。

批次間變異的管理策略

  • 孢子單層（Monolayer）：避免孢子堆疊（clumping），確保 VHP 能直接接觸每個孢子，防止孢子被外層遮蔽而形成「rogue spores」假陽性。
  • 回收率 50–300%：入廠 BI 品質控制的接受標準。回收率過低代表品質問題，過高則可能計數異常。
  • 固定供應商 + 固定批次：在開發和確效期間維持一致的 BI 來源，可大幅降低批次間變異對結果的影響。
  • 存活-死亡窗口（Survival-Kill Window）：同批次 BI 的一致性檢查工具——在不同暴露時間下，確認孢子由存活轉為死亡的轉折點是否一致。

實務提問

你的廠在換 BI 供應商時，是否有要求重新進行 in-house D 值測試？還是直接沿用新供應商的 CoA 數據？這兩者在監管審查時的風險，有實質差距。

Q7-7 — How Should Multiple BIs Be Used and Evaluated During VHP Cycle Development and Validation?

Original Text (pp. 51–52)

Recommendation

The exposure of multiple, usually three, BIs at given locations may be used to evaluate and qualify the effectiveness of the decontamination cycle.

BI Location and Placement

BIs should be placed in locations that will provide the best opportunity for identification of difficult-to-decontaminate surfaces. BI locations and placement should be determined through a risk assessment of the isolator, including the configuration of the isolator, the composition and positioning of nonproduct contact surfaces that may impact the effectiveness of the cycle, and the condition and distribution of decontamination agent.

Where difficult-to-decontaminate surfaces or locations are identified, placement of multiple (usually triplicate) BIs at individual locations during cycle development and qualification may provide information about the efficacy of the decontamination cycle.

BI Evaluation and Investigation

The logarithmic reduction of surface contamination may be determined through empirical evidence, mathematical formula, experiment, most probable number (MPN) of surviving spores, and/or data evaluation.

Where all BIs at a location are deactivated or killed, a more efficient decontamination effect and log reduction level may be assumed. Where one or more of the BIs are not deactivated, a less effective decontaminating cycle or log reduction level may be assumed.

The failure to deactivate one or more of the BIs at a given location should be investigated. The outcome of the investigation should indicate whether the growth is due to the cycle failure or to a BI-related issue.

The investigation should consider conditions that may adversely affect the decontamination cycle, such as:

  • Confirmation of indicator organism growth
  • BI lot or batch variation
  • BI D-value, population, and resistance
  • BI preparation and handling
  • BI spore clumping or occlusion (rogue spores)
  • BI placement configuration and location
  • Insufficient exposure to the decontamination agent
  • Decontamination cycle parameters
  • Decontamination agent concentration
  • Decontamination agent application or injection
  • Control and monitoring instrumentations and sensors
  • Temperature or humidity changes
  • Condition, bioburden, and cleanliness of the isolator interior
  • Effect of decontamination agent retention
  • Results of chemical indicator monitoring

If the outcome of the investigation indicates a decontamination cycle failure, the cause should be determined, a corrective action implemented, and validation studies repeated.

If the outcome of the investigation is that the cause is a problem with the BI, then the reason for the positive BI must be determined and documented. The investigation should include an analysis of the BI lot and historical performance to determine if clumping or occlusion of spores is possible.

Rationale

The deactivation of BIs during the decontamination cycle qualification provides further evidence that the decontaminating agents have contacted the surfaces to the extent needed to provide the decontaminated environment required for aseptic processing. However, the inactivation of all BIs may not be needed to demonstrate that the isolator has been decontaminated to a level that will not contribute contamination to product or product contact surfaces.

導讀與解析

多個 BI 的策略邏輯

為什麼每個位置要放三個（triplicate）BI？因為孢子的生死本身就有統計變異。一個 BI 的結果可能是 BI 本身的問題；三個一起才能判斷：

  • 三個都死→ 該位置除污效能充足
  • 一個沒死→ 可能是 BI 問題（孢子堆疊、rogue spore），需調查
  • 多個沒死→ 高度懷疑是循環本身的問題

佈點的風險導向思維

BI 不是隨機放的。要先做 isolator 的風險評估，找出「最難除污的位置」：

  • 死角、轉角、設備背面
  • VHP 氣流較弱的區域
  • 有陰影遮蔽的非產品接觸表面
  • 管線接頭、閥門周邊

這些位置才是真正的 worst-case locations。如果這些地方都能達到 6 log reduction，其他位置就不用太擔心了。

比喻：消防演習的灑水頭測試

多個 BI 的佈點就像在建築物裡測試灑水頭：你要刻意測試最遠的那個房間、有遮蔽的角落、管線末端。如果水壓到得了最難到的地方，整棟建築就沒問題了。

BI 陽性調查的分叉決策

BI 陽性（孢子沒死）= 啟動調查。調查有兩條路：

  • 路徑 A：循環失效→ 找出根本原因（RCA），執行 CAPA，重新執行確效。
  • 路徑 B：BI 本身問題→ 記錄原因（如孢子堆疊、處理不當），確認同批次歷史表現，排除後可繼續。

兩條路的出口完全不同，而且都必須有文件佐證——不能「感覺上是 BI 問題」就跳過調查。

CDMO 實務提醒

你的 isolator 確效佈點圖（BI placement map）是否已文件化並可重現？每次再確效時，BI 要放在完全一樣的位置。如果位置每次都不同，那不同批次之間的數據根本無法比較。

Q7-8 — What Effect Could Oil-Based HEPA-Filter Integrity Agents Have on the Decontamination Cycle?

Original Text (pp. 53)

Recommendation

HEPA-filter testing should be carried out as part of routine installation and operation qualification as well as requalification activities. The test method and potential impact on the decontamination process must be considered. Care should be taken to ensure that any residue from HEPA-filter testing that might be trapped on filters or surfaces can be removed and that any remaining residue will not compromise subsequent decontamination cycles. The equipment vendor (e.g., isolator or pass-through chamber) should be consulted for the appropriate test method and post-test activities.

If using an oil-based material to test the filters, ensure that the filter does not retain any of the material, because it could interact with the VHP in the system and negatively impact the efficacy of the decontamination process. A risk assessment of the system to be tested should include this consideration, and the timing of the HEPA-filter testing would, ideally, occur before requalification cycles.

Rationale

HEPA-filter testing is critical to ensure efficacy of the decontamination process, but it is also important to consider any potential contamination that could be introduced in the system by the procedures used to test the filters.

導讀與解析

問題的根源：油性介質 vs. VHP

HEPA 過濾器完整性測試（DOP/PAO test）傳統上使用 Emery 3004 等油性氣溶膠作為挑戰介質。問題在於：

  • 油性殘留物會吸附在 HEPA 濾材纖維上
  • H₂O₂（VHP）是強氧化劑，會與油性殘留物發生化學反應
  • 反應消耗了 H₂O₂，導致循環中實際可用的殺菌濃度降低
  • 最終結果：除污效能下降，BI 可能出現假陽性

時序管理是關鍵

PDA 的建議是：HEPA 完整性測試應在再確效循環之前執行（ideally before requalification cycles）。

這代表如果你的年度計劃是：先跑 VHP 確效，再做 HEPA 測試——這個順序需要重新評估。正確做法是：

  1. 執行 HEPA 完整性測試
  2. 確認殘留油性介質已清除或驗證不影響後續循環
  3. 再執行 VHP 除污確效

比喻：油汙染水源的消毒

就像在消毒游泳池之前，如果有人倒入一桶機油，氯氣就會先和機油反應，消耗殺菌劑。等你以為池水消毒完成，其實氯早就耗光了，根本沒有殺菌效果。HEPA 油性介質對 VHP 做的就是同樣的事。

風險評估必須涵蓋的面向

  • 使用的完整性測試介質是否為油性？（PAO / DOP vs. 水性替代品）
  • 殘留量是否可測量或可排除？
  • 廠商提供的清除程序是否有效？
  • HEPA 測試與 VHP 確效的時間間隔是否足夠？
  • 是否有替代方案（如使用水性完整性測試介質）可降低風險？

廠房實務提問

你的 isolator PM 計劃中，HEPA 完整性測試與 VHP 除污確效的順序是如何安排的？是否有書面的風險評估說明為何這樣排序，以及油性介質的殘留風險已被合理排除？

Q7-9 — Should Each Load Be Qualified During the Decontamination Cycle Qualification?

Original Text (p. 54)

Recommendation

Typically, production isolators do not have high-load variability. The variability of the load within the isolator should be minimized and controlled. If variability does exist, however, a risk-based approach should be used to qualify the decontamination of these loads.

The most conservative approach is to qualify each manufacturing cycle or load as a separate activity. Testing each load type would provide data to show that each approach is controlled and products are manufactured as expected. The requalification process should align with the original validation strategy. If the approach includes qualifying each presentation within the isolator, the requalification should mimic that approach.

It may become possible to choose alternate revalidation strategies, such as rotating loads, where all loads are qualified over time. This approach is supported by data collection and risk-based principles. That is only possible with data and strong monitoring processes to ensure control of the process.

Bracketing strategies (e.g., minimum and maximum loads) may also be employed with proper assessment.

Rationale

Strong process understanding is the best basis for determining validation requirements. Robust monitoring and aseptic manufacturing processes will add to the confidence that the manufacturing process is under a state of control. Decisions should be made based on the facilities process, control structure, and process understanding. Then, based on those decisions, validation programs should be initiated and followed. Decisions regarding the types of loads and frequency of revalidation should be included in the validation program.

導讀與解析

為什麼不能外推（Extrapolate）？

除污循環的效能，取決於 H₂O₂ 如何在 isolator 內擴散和接觸每一個表面。當你放入不同的器具、不同數量的藥瓶或不同尺寸的包材時，氣流路徑改變、陰影位置改變、H₂O₂ 分布也改變。

空腔確效 ≠ 帶負載確效。 兩者的 worst-case location 可能完全不同。

三種確效策略的選擇邏輯

策略 描述 適用條件
逐一確效
（最保守）		 每種裝載配置各自執行完整確效 負載差異大，監管風險高
括弧策略
（Bracketing）		 只確效最小量和最大量負載，中間的推斷覆蓋 有充分數據支持線性關係
輪換再確效
（Rotating）		 每次再確效輪換確效不同負載，長期覆蓋所有配置 有強大日常監控數據支撐

比喻：洗衣機的洗滌程式

你確效了洗一件白色棉質 T-shirt 的程式，不代表同樣設定能洗乾淨一件黑色羊毛毛衣加三件牛仔褲。負載的質量、密度、擺放方式都會影響水流與洗滌劑分布——除污循環對 H₂O₂ 分布的敏感性，也是同樣道理。

再確效策略要與原始確效一致

PDA 的立場非常清楚：再確效（requalification）的策略必須和原始確效保持一致。如果原始確效是針對每種負載個別驗的，那再確效也要維持同樣的框架——不能因為方便就降低標準，除非有充分的數據和風險評估支持策略的調整。

你的廠房現況

你的 isolator 目前有幾種不同的裝載配置（例如：10 mL 藥瓶滿載、2 mL 藥瓶半載、空腔）？每一種配置都有對應的確效記錄嗎？如果未來新增一種包材規格，是否有文件化的流程說明何時需要重新進行除污確效？

  • Q7-5 開發 vs. 確效的本質差異：開發是探索與設計，允許調整；確效是以固定參數提供可重複的書面證據，需三次重複執行。6 log reduction 是無菌充填用 isolator 的業界基準。
  • Q7-6 BI 選擇與 D 值管理：G. stearothermophilus 是 VHP 確效的標準選擇，孢子數 ≥ 10⁶，需為單層排列。D 值必須在自己的 isolator 系統中實測，不能直接沿用供應商資料——因為 D 值高度依賴系統條件，不同設備之間無法互換。
  • Q7-7 多個 BI 的佈點與調查：BI 佈點以風險評估為基礎，瞄準最難除污的 worst-case 位置，通常每處三個。陽性 BI 必須調查，且調查結論需明確區分是循環失效（重跑確效）還是 BI 本身問題（記錄後排除）。
  • Q7-8 HEPA 完整性測試的時序風險：油性完整性測試介質（如 Emery 3004）殘留於 HEPA 濾材時，會消耗 VHP 中的 H₂O₂，降低除污效能。正確做法是在 VHP 確效之前進行 HEPA 測試，並確認殘留已清除或不影響循環效能。
  • Q7-9 每種負載配置均需個別確效：不同裝載配置影響 H₂O₂ 分布，無法從一種配置外推到另一種。可選用逐一確效（最保守）、括弧策略或輪換再確效，但任何替代策略都必須有數據與風險評估支撐，且再確效方法必須與原始確效策略保持一致。

Q8-1: What are the special considerations for media fills in isolators?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

The same risk-based analysis and approach taken for aseptic process simulation are recommended for all aseptic processing technologies, including isolator systems. This paradigm is addressed in both PDA Aseptic Processing Points to Consider, Part 2, and PDA Technical Report No. 22: Process Simulations for Aseptically Filled Products.

Many of the considerations for planning and performing aseptic process simulations (APS) in isolator aseptic processes are the same or similar to those for conventional filling lines. Topic 8 addresses special considerations and the differences in approach to isolator APS.

Rationale

The highest risk for contamination of an aseptic process is based on the events that occur. The variability of the process (i.e., the level of human versus automated interventions), and the protection of the process (closed versus open) directly impacts the level of risk.

Many isolator applications still involve a number of interventions that may represent a risk if performed poorly. As such, generalizations cannot be made about the relative risk of a process in an isolator. Unique aseptic process characteristics or conditions that exist in the design and operation of the isolator, however, should be considered in planning and performing APS.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

核心前提：APS 邏輯不因技術而變

不管是傳統潔淨室、RABS 還是隔離器，APS 的基本邏輯都一樣：用風險評估設計、模擬真實製程、以污染率衡量結果。PDA TR-22 是這個領域的聖經，隔離器只是「場景不同」，原則不變。

隔離器的特殊性在哪裡？

隔離器有更強的屏障保護，但這不代表「免疫」於污染風險。關鍵在於：

  • 干預仍然存在——手套箱操作、RTP 傳遞、BI 放置等，每一個都是潛在污染事件
  • 空間受限——glove port 的操作比傳統潔淨室更難、更受約束
  • 去汙染週期——APS 前必須先執行 VHP 或過氧化氫去汙，這個步驟本身也需要在 APS 準備流程中被納入

比喻：APS 是「彩排」，不是「考試」

把 APS 想像成外科手術前的完整彩排——不只是學技術動作，而是在真實的劇場（隔離器）裡，用真實的道具（培養基、容器、transfer 裝置），演練所有可能的「意外」插曲。彩排夠真實，才能找出會在正式演出時出差錯的環節。

思考問題

你的隔離器填充線有哪些干預是「每次生產必有」的？這些干預都納入 APS 方案了嗎？去汙染週期在 APS 前有被執行、記錄嗎？

Q8-2: What are the special considerations for isolator APS study acceptance criteria?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

There should be no difference in the acceptance criteria between isolator and conventional or RABS aseptic filling processes. In all cases, the objective of the APS is to produce zero contaminated units, regardless of run size. Therefore, the target involving such simulations should be zero positive units, regardless of the technology or run size.

Since isolators provide a greater physical separation of personnel and environment from product and product contact surfaces than conventional lines, failures that involve lapses in aseptic technique or environmental monitoring excursions should be especially scrutinized to determine if they represent the failure of process, system design, or contamination control strategy.

Rationale

Process simulation contamination rates resulting in zero positive units especially should be achievable in well-designed and well-operated isolator filling lines. APS failures that result from environmental contaminants or lapses in aseptic technique should be rare. Therefore, positive units may indicate a process, system design, or contamination control strategy failure.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

零污染：不容妥協的絕對標準

驗收標準與傳統線一致——目標為 0 個污染單位。這不因隔離器的屏障優勢而放寬。PDA TR-22 的表述很清楚：5,000–10,000 units 的批次中，任何陽性都是 unacceptable。

隔離器失敗的訊號更嚴重

正因為隔離器提供了比傳統潔淨室更強的隔離，一旦出現陽性結果，意義完全不同。在傳統線，陽性可能來自環境波動；但在隔離器裡，陽性往往指向：

  • 設備設計缺陷（系統性問題）
  • 去汙染失敗或隔離器完整性破損
  • 操作員技術問題（glove 操作不當）
  • 傳遞裝置密封失效

這些原因每一個都比傳統線的「環境偶發波動」更嚴重，必須徹底調查。

比喻：防護更強，代表失守更嚴重

如果銀行金庫（隔離器）比一般辦公室（傳統潔淨室）的防盜系統強十倍，那麼金庫裡的東西被偷，一定不是門沒鎖——必然是有人從裡面開門，或者金庫本身設計有漏洞。失敗的調查深度應相應提高。

思考問題

如果你的隔離器 APS 出現 1 個陽性，你的 CAPA 流程第一步會查什麼？你的 SOP 是否有明確規定隔離器 APS 失敗調查的優先順序？

Q8-3: Which activities and interventions within the isolator should be included in the APS?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

Activities and interventions that may pose a risk to the aseptic process or sterile product should be included in the APS. Where activities or interventions do not represent a risk to the aseptic process or sterile product, there may be no need to include them. In general, the following types of activities do not necessarily need to be included in the APS challenge:

  • Activities that take place outside of the isolator that do not introduce contamination into the isolator interior
  • Automated processes with no human involvement that take place completely within the decontaminated isolator
  • Activities that are validated as part of other studies (e.g., decontamination cycle validation)
  • Activities that are completely automated with no associated manual interventions
  • Activities where the isolator provides a complete and maintained separation between the activity and the sterile product or product contact surfaces
  • Activities that do not bring operators into proximity of sterile product or product contact surfaces
  • Activities that are inherently low risk due to design controls

If these conditions or performance of these activities are determined to pose a risk to the aseptic process or sterile product, however, they may need to be included in the APS challenge.

Rationale

Activities and interventions that may pose a risk to the aseptic process or product sterility within the isolator, and are not otherwise validated, should be included in the APS regardless of the technology used. Where there is no risk to the aseptic process or sterile product posed by an activity, there is no scientific value to be gained from inclusion in the APS.

The objective of an APS is to assess the capability to perform a process including interventions under defined conditions to achieve units free from microbial contamination. Determining the criticality of interventions can be an effective way to identify steps to mitigate or reduce the risk of those interventions and improve the aseptic process.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

納入原則：「有風險才需要挑戰」

APS 的設計邏輯是風險驅動，不是「越多越好」。沒有風險的步驟納入 APS 只會增加複雜度，卻沒有科學意義。以下幾類可以不納入：

  • 隔離器外部作業，且不影響內部環境
  • 完全自動化、無人工介入的製程步驟
  • 已在其他驗證研究中單獨確認的活動（如去汙染週期驗證）
  • 設計控制已確保不會接觸無菌產品的操作

必須納入的干預類型

以下活動因涉及與無菌產品或關鍵表面的潛在接觸，應納入 APS：

  • 透過 glove port 進行的任何手工干預（更換零件、清理異常品、更換消耗品）
  • RTP / DPTE 傳遞材料進入隔離器的操作
  • 容器開封、組裝或移位等手動步驟
  • 環境監控取樣（沉降碟放置、表面取樣等）

比喻：只排練「容易出錯的橋段」

樂團彩排不會從頭到尾反覆演奏每一個音符——重點放在轉調困難的段落、獨奏接管的節點。APS 設計也一樣：把有限的彩排時間，花在最可能出錯的干預動作上。

思考問題

你的 APS 方案有沒有做過干預項目的風險分類（Critical / Major / Minor）？那些被排除在 APS 之外的活動，是否有文件化的風險評估為依據？

Q8-4: Should the connection, use, and unloading of transfer devices — RTP containers, DPTE bags, or alpha-beta ports — be included in the APS?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

If RTP and/or DPTE systems are used to transfer decontaminated or sterile materials into the isolator prior to or during aseptic filling operations, that transfer should be included in the aseptic process simulations.

The aseptic process simulation should not be the primary means by which the sterilization or the effectiveness of the RTP or DPTE systems is validated. The sterilization and integrity of these systems should be qualified prior to the performance of the aseptic process simulations.

Rationale

The transfer of decontaminated and sterile materials is an essential step in the aseptic process. Failure to properly transfer these materials or failure of the integrity of the systems may result in contamination of the material, the isolator environment, and the sterile product or sterile product contact surfaces.

The use of these systems, including any interventions associated with the setup of the system or performance of the transfer of materials, should be included in the APS, because there are interventions that can affect its effectiveness.

The aseptic process simulation is not sensitive enough to uncover flaws or failures in the design, integrity, or sterilization of the transfer system. Therefore, additional steps should be taken to qualify and validate these systems and the sterilization process.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

三種傳遞裝置，全部需要納入 APS

RTP（Rapid Transfer Port）容器、DPTE 袋（Double-Packaging Transfer Enclosure）、Alpha-Beta port——這些都是隔離器生產中傳遞滅菌材料的標準工具。只要在無菌灌裝前或期間使用，這些傳遞操作就必須被 APS 模擬。

重要邊界：APS 不是傳遞系統的驗證工具

APS 無法偵測 RTP/DPTE 本身的設計缺陷或滅菌失效——因為 Media Fill 對低生物負載過於不敏感。因此：

  • RTP/DPTE 的滅菌驗證，必須在 APS 之前完成獨立的驗證研究
  • APS 驗證的是「作業員正確操作這些裝置的能力」，而非裝置本身的滅菌效果
  • 外袋去汙染後的轉移操作（撕除外袋進入隔離器）是特別關鍵的干預，必須在 APS 中重現

比喻：驗門鎖，還是驗開門的人

RTP/DPTE 的滅菌驗證是在確認「鎖是好的」；APS 是在確認「操作員知道怎麼正確開鎖進門，不會讓汙染跟著進來」。兩件事都重要，但必須用不同的方法驗。

實務要點

APS 方案設計時，應明確列出哪些批次的 RTP/DPTE 傳遞操作被模擬、由哪位作業員操作、幾次。這些記錄是作業員資格認定的一部分。

Q8-5: Should participation in an APS be used to qualify aseptic filling operators working in isolators?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

It may not be necessary for aseptic processing personnel who are performing interventions in the isolator to be initially qualified through participation in an APS. If proficiency in aseptic technique can be demonstrated, whether by successful completion of other training and qualification activities involving media manipulation, personnel would not need to be qualified through APS.

The method for qualification of the aseptic processing personnel who perform interventions and activities within the decontaminated isolator should be risk-based. Emphasis should be placed on their knowing and demonstrating the ability to perform aseptic process tasks in the confined isolator space with limited movements, using good aseptic technique, practices, and principles.

Mere participation in one or more successful APS may not be enough to qualify aseptic processing personnel. They should be trained and evaluated to show they can perform these interventions and activities in the production or simulator isolator.

Aseptic processing personnel who do not perform interventions in the isolator do not have to participate in an APS unless their function and activities can introduce contamination into the isolator or otherwise adversely affect the aseptic process.

When participation in a successful APS is part of the personnel qualification procedure, failure of that APS should be investigated to determine if the cause of the APS failure was the result of personnel performance. If so, this failure may be a determining factor in disqualifying the person responsible.

Rationale

Qualification should ensure that aseptic processing area personnel have the proper training and knowledge for their respective functions. The critical aspects of qualification involve the demonstrated ability to understand and perform their job functions in an isolator space that may restrict movement. The APS is not sensitive enough to determine if aseptic processing personnel are able to reliably perform interventions and activities within the isolator.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

資格認定：風險導向，不是參加 APS 就算數

PDA 的立場很清楚：APS 不是人員資格認定的萬能工具。關鍵在於證明作業員能夠在受限空間（手套箱）內，正確執行無菌操作。初始資格認定可以透過：

  • 模擬隔離器的 Media Manipulation 練習
  • 生產用隔離器的技術評估
  • APS 參與（作為組合方式之一）

誰需要參加 APS？誰不需要？

需要：在隔離器內執行干預操作的人員（換消耗品、手工取樣等）

不一定需要：僅在隔離器外圍作業、不影響內部環境的人員

APS 失敗時：若調查後確認失敗原因與特定人員操作相關，可作為取消其資格的依據。這個規定讓人員資格認定具有真正的意義。

比喻：飛行員認證不只靠模擬飛行

飛行員資格認定包括：筆試、模擬艙訓練、帶飛觀察、獨飛評估。沒有人說「只要跟著完成一次 APS Media Fill 就合格」。APS 是完整認定計畫的一個環節，不是全部。

實務要點：頻率與週期

定期再認定的頻率與傳統線相同（通常每 6 個月一次）。特別要注意的是：隔離器手套箱的操作對身體姿勢、手腕角度、動作範圍有特殊要求，訓練方案應針對這些受限條件設計專項評估。

Q8-6: Does qualification on a conventional aseptic filling line APS qualify personnel on a similar isolator filling line?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

Participation in conventional aseptic filling lines would not qualify aseptic processing personnel on isolator lines. A history of successful qualification on and participation in conventional line APS, however, may provide useful evidence that the aseptic process personnel has sufficient knowledge and proficiency in aseptic technique to perform similar tasks in isolators.

Both PDA Points to Consider for Aseptic Processing, Part 1, and PDA TR-22 discuss the initial qualification of aseptic processing personnel. The Points to Consider document recommends that for initial qualification, in addition to other requirements, personnel demonstrate their proficiency by:

  1. Successfully completing a qualification test involving manual media manipulation not associated with an APS; or
  2. Successfully participating in an APS run in which they perform the same function(s) to the same extent as they will perform the function(s) during actual production, as applicable.

While participation in an APS on a conventional line would not necessarily satisfy the second option, it may be considered sufficient as "successfully completing a qualification test involving manual media manipulation" if the activities performed are similar and relevant to those performed in the isolator.

A risk assessment should be performed to help determine the similarity and relevance of activities performed during the conventional APS and if the participation in the conventional line media fill is useful and appropriate as a basis for operator qualification in the isolator.

Regardless of the outcome of the risk assessment, the operator should participate in an APS in the isolator as a part of periodic requalification.

Rationale

Because participation in an APS on a conventional filling line does not take into consideration many of the restrictions and movements associated with similar interventions in isolators, the aseptic processing personnel should demonstrate that they can perform those unique activities and job functions associated with the isolator operations.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

明確答案：傳統線資歷不能直接轉移

這是監管查核中常見的缺失來源。PDA 的立場很明確：在傳統潔淨室 APS 取得的資格，不能直接認定為隔離器作業員的資格。必須針對隔離器作業進行獨立的資格認定。

可以做什麼：風險評估的彈性空間

傳統線的 Media Fill 資歷並非毫無價值——透過風險評估，可以評估其與隔離器操作的相似性，可能作為「手動 Media Manipulation 技術測試」的證據之一。但無論如何，定期再認定必須在隔離器 APS 中進行，沒有例外。

比喻：汽車駕照不等於摩托車駕照

你有多年安全駕駛汽車的記錄，這證明你有基本的道路意識與操控紀律——但考摩托車駕照時，你仍然需要實際在摩托車上示範技術。隔離器 glove port 的操作限制，就像摩托車的重心掌控，必須用該設備直接驗證。

思考問題

你的廠有沒有作業員同時在傳統線和隔離器線作業？他們的資格認定計畫是分開管理的嗎？如果有共用的訓練模組，風險評估文件在哪裡？

Q8-7: Should the maximum number of operators be considered during the APS in the isolator?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

All activities performed in the isolator during the aseptic process should be included in the APS in the manner they are normally performed during production. If the number of operators is a variable, then a risk assessment should be used to help determine if the number of operators poses a risk to the sterility of product.

  • Where multiple interventions are being performed by multiple personnel at the same time in the isolator, those interventions should be performed or simulated during the APS.
  • Activities performed by personnel outside of the isolator that do not impact the isolator interior environment, sterility of the product, or the product contact surfaces would not necessarily need to be performed or simulated as part of the isolator APS. Consideration should be given, however, to the number of people and the level of activity occurring around the disinfection and transfer of materials into the isolator.
  • In some cases, the maximum number of operators performing interventions at the same time may represent worst-case conditions due to the level of activity, environmental bioburden, and potential for intervention-related contamination.
  • In some cases, fewer operators performing the same interventions may provide a higher risk of contamination from excessive activity and speed of movements.

Rationale

The design of the isolator and associated barrier systems should prevent or reduce the risk of operator contamination compared to conventional filling lines; however, the level of activity and movement within the isolator may pose a risk of contamination to the isolator interior environment, exposed sterile product, and product contact surfaces. Therefore, all activities and levels of activities that pose a risk to the sterility of the product should be included in the APS in the same manner they are performed during production.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

最差情況 = 同時最多人手伸進手套箱

隔離器的「最差情況」在人員這個維度，不是「外面站了多少人」，而是「同時有幾個 glove port 被使用、進行哪些干預」。這個配置必須在 APS 中被複製。

雙向風險：人多與人少都可能是最差情況

這是 PDA 一個微妙但重要的觀點：

  • 人多：同時多個干預 → 氣流擾動、生物負載增加
  • 人少：一人獨立操作 → 動作速度加快、一人兼顧多個任務 → 動作粗糙風險升高

因此，風險評估必須雙向考量，APS 設計應針對真正的最差情況，而非想當然的「人越多越危險」。

比喻：手術室中的協同操作

一台複雜手術可能同時有主刀、助手、器械護士在無菌區域活動。人多不一定不好，但每個人的動作都必須受過訓練、符合無菌操作規範。APS 的設計就是要模擬這種最大並發操作的狀態，確認整體配置下仍能維持無菌保障。

實務要點

APS 方案中應明確記錄：最大同時操作人數、各 glove port 分配的任務、模擬的干預項目清單。若生產時曾有特殊情況（如緊急干預），也應考慮是否在 APS 中模擬。

Q8-8: Should the APS be used to validate the isolator loading pattern?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

The configuration of equipment and materials in the isolator during the decontamination process should be established prior to the qualification and validation of the decontamination cycle. The APS should not be used to qualify or validate the decontamination cycle.

The conditions, configurations, design, and loading pattern within the isolator should be qualified and validated in separate studies prior to the performance of the APS. The validated cycle should then be used in preparation of the isolator for the APS.

Rationale

The APS is not sensitive enough to determine, demonstrate, or test the effectiveness and lethality of the decontamination cycle because there is no environmental or surface microbiological contaminant challenge. The effectiveness and lethality of the decontamination cycle is best proven during separate and specific validation studies that include the use of biological indicators.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

APS 不是去汙染週期驗證的工具

這個問題澄清了一個常見的認知混淆：APS 的 Media Fill 培養基對微生物污染的敏感度，完全不足以驗證 VHP/H₂O₂ 去汙染週期的殺菌效果。去汙染週期驗證必須使用生物指示劑（BI）在獨立研究中完成。

正確的驗證序列

隔離器驗證的正確時間順序：

  1. 確定設備與材料的加載型態（loading pattern）
  2. 進行去汙染週期開發（cycle development）
  3. 用 BI 完成去汙染週期驗證（cycle validation）
  4. 驗證後，以已驗證的週期準備 APS
  5. 執行 APS

APS 必須在「已驗證的環境下」執行，它本身不是驗證環境的工具。

比喻：先驗手術刀滅菌，再做手術評估

你不會用手術的結果（病人有沒有感染）來驗證高壓滅菌鍋的效能——那需要生物挑戰測試。同理，APS 是在驗證「操作過程」，不是在驗證「隔離器是否被正確去汙染」。

思考問題

你的 APS SOP 中，有沒有明確記載「使用已驗證的去汙染週期參數」？如果去汙染週期參數有任何變更（如加載型態改變），是否需要重新驗證後才能執行 APS？

Q8-9: What are the special considerations for setting the APS duration for multiple-shift and multiple-day campaign production in isolators?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

The duration of the APS should be sufficient to adequately challenge the aseptic process. Each company must determine appropriate rationale and approaches applicable to its unique operations by means of documented risk assessment and process simulation design.

  • The design of the APS study should include a risk-based assessment of conditions and activities that can adversely affect product sterility and are not otherwise addressed in other qualification and validation studies.
  • Where there are conditions and activities that occur as a result of multiple shifts and multiple-day operations, those activities should be considered for inclusion in the APS study.
  • The duration should be long enough to allow simulation of the predetermined interventions, as well as other intrinsic characteristics of the container and closure systems.
  • Because of the barrier properties of isolators, conditions and activities within the decontaminated isolator would likely present more risk than conditions outside. Shift changes that involve replacement of personnel outside the isolator would not necessarily require inclusion in the APS. Line clearance, removal, or transfer of materials into or within the decontaminated isolator during shift or batch changes, however, might require inclusion.
  • The APS should not be used to establish sterile product or material hold times, the cleanliness of the environment, the impact of human fatigue, or the capability of the sterilizing filter.
  • Manual aseptic filling or closing processes are highly dependent on the operator's individual performance. Therefore, full duration media fills are recommended for qualifying these processes.
  • The period that the isolator interior remains in a condition suitable for aseptic processing after decontamination should be established, qualified, and validated. If the isolator remains integral and the decontamination process is shown to be effective, there should be no need to further test the effectiveness of the process during full duration APSs.

Rationale

Contamination of an aseptic process is primarily a function of events rather than time. Therefore, the duration of the process simulation should be sufficient to assess the performance of those activities identified in a risk assessment as having the potential to introduce contamination. Good process design, including assessment of human factors, adherence to first-air principles, training, ergonomics, and scheduling of breaks, is a better tool for controlling potential effects of human fatigue than the use of extended media fills.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

核心邏輯：污染由「事件」決定，不是由「時間」決定

PDA 的立場打破了「APS 要跑多久才算充分」的時間迷思。重點是：在 APS 期間，所有被識別為高風險的干預事件都要被模擬到。時間長短服從於事件清單，不是硬性規定「必須等於最長生產時間」。

多班次換班：內外有別

這是隔離器 APS 設計的一個重要差異點：

  • 外部換班（只是外圍人員輪替，不進入隔離器）→ 不一定要納入 APS
  • 內部操作連動的換班（需要進入隔離器進行換批清場、傳遞材料）→ 必須考慮納入 APS

隔離器的屏障功能決定了評估重心：一切以「是否影響隔離器內部」為判斷基準。

APS 的「不適用清單」——四個不該用 APS 驗證的事

  • 無菌產品或物料的 hold time
  • 環境空氣潔淨度
  • 人員疲勞對操作的影響
  • 滅菌過濾器效能

這些都有專屬的驗證方法，APS 對這些問題的靈敏度不夠。

比喻：劇本決定彩排長度，不是時鐘

一出三幕戲的彩排，要確保每一個困難場景都被排練到，不是規定「彩排必須等於正式演出的時長」。APS 的持續時間邏輯相同——讓所有預定的干預場景都有機會被執行，才是目標。

Q8-10: What are the special considerations for the inclusion and rejection of units during the isolator APS?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

The same considerations for the inclusion or rejection of filled media units during a conventional fill line APS should apply to an isolator APS.

The decision to reject or incubate integral, filled units normally rejected because of an intervention should be based on a risk assessment of the intervention, proximity of the intervention to open units, positioning of units during the intervention, barrier properties of the isolator, and procedures and systems in place to identify and reject units.

The dosed design of and limited access to the isolator interior that present unique or additional challenges for the inclusion, identification, or removal of rejected units should be considered in the decision to incubate or reject intervention-related units.

Rationale

The incubation of nonintegral units will not provide any meaningful measure of the aseptic process, because these units represent neither acceptable production practices nor acceptable container-closure integrity. Other defective units that may routinely be rejected during commercial operations (e.g., cosmetic, particulate, and fill volume defects) should be incubated and included in the evaluation and overall determination of contamination rate. The inclusion of these units is considered meaningful since the routine rejection of these vials is not related to consideration of sterility assurance.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

納入 vs. 剔除：原則與傳統線相同

APS 單位的納入/剔除邏輯，在隔離器與傳統線之間沒有本質差異，但隔離器的密閉設計增加了辨識與移除被剔除單位的操作挑戰。

判斷框架：哪些單位必須孵育、哪些可以剔除？

應孵育（納入計算）：

  • 外觀缺陷（cosmetic defect）——不影響容器密封性，正常生產會被剔除但與無菌保障無關
  • 顆粒異物、裝量偏差等製程相關缺陷
  • 干預附近的完整單位（完整性未受損）

可剔除（不納入孵育）：

  • 非完整（nonintegral）單位——容器密封已破損，孵育無意義

比喻：考試卷必須全收，才能真正評量

如果考試老師只收「漂亮字跡」的試卷，成績分布就失去代表性。APS 的道理一樣——只有納入所有有效填充的單位（包括有外觀問題但密封完整的），才能真實反映製程的無菌保障能力。

隔離器的額外挑戰

隔離器密閉空間使得「在生產中途辨識並移除特定單位」比傳統線更困難。APS 方案應提前設計標記與移除程序，確保干預相關單位能被正確追蹤。

Q8-11: Should power failures that result in loss of airflow, differential pressure, or integrity of the isolator be simulated in an APS?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

Only acceptable interventions and process conditions should be challenged in the aseptic process simulation. Deviations, poor practices, and lapses in contamination control measures should not be qualified or shown to be acceptable by simulating them in an APS.

Rationale

One of the primary contamination control measures for the isolator is the maintenance of positive differential pressure to the outside environment. The loss of power and differential pressure is the loss of that primary contamination control measure. Therefore, contamination control is lost. Aseptic process simulations are not sensitive enough to determine if that loss of contamination control will adversely affect the sterility of the product.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

明確答案：不應該在 APS 中模擬停電

這個問題的答案非常直接。停電導致正壓喪失，等同於第一道污染控制屏障失效。APS 的設計前提是「在可接受的操作條件下」挑戰製程，停電屬於失控偏差，不是「可接受的操作條件」。

邏輯推論：在 APS 中模擬停電，不代表可以接受停電後繼續生產

如果有公司想透過「停電後 APS 沒有陽性」來證明停電後可以繼續生產，這個邏輯是錯誤的：

  • APS 的培養基對低生物負載不夠敏感
  • 一次 APS 通過，不能涵蓋所有可能的停電情境
  • 正確做法：停電後隔離器必須重新評估完整性、重新去汙染，這是 SOP 問題，不是 APS 問題

比喻：不要用賭注測試安全閥

鍋爐工廠不會用「在失壓狀態下繼續加熱，看結果是否爆炸」來驗證安全閥設計。停電是緊急情境，應有獨立的危機應對 SOP，而不是讓 APS 背書「停電後可以繼續跑」。

實務要點：停電 SOP 的必要性

隔離器停電後應有明確的書面程序：確認完整性、決策是否廢棄批次或重新去汙染、記錄偏差。這套流程不依賴 APS 來驗證，而是基於風險評估與設備的 FAT/SAT 確認。

Q8-12: What are the special considerations for determining worst-case conditions in an isolator APS?

Original Text (English)

Recommendation

Worst-case conditions or configurations are those within the range of normal operating conditions and configurations that challenge the process to provide the best opportunity to uncover process weaknesses and unaddressed process variability. "Worst case" does not mean creation of artificial conditions or environments that exceed allowed operating conditions and force a system failure.

  • Worst-case conditions and configurations should be assessed and determined on the basis of risk to process and product sterility.
  • Because of the barrier properties of isolators, the number of operators around the isolator may not represent a worst-case condition. However, the number of operators performing interventions within the decontaminated isolator at one time could be considered worst case. Fewer operators may also be considered as a worst case if it results in excessive glove movement, air flow turbulence and disruption, or less attention by those performing interventions.
  • The confinement of space, limitation of movement, position of isolator gloves, and proximity to exposed sterile product contact surfaces and materials should be considered in determining worst-case conditions for activities such as transfer, unwrapping, assembly, movement, and removal of materials, and environmental monitoring and sampling.
  • Where uncertainty exists related to which conditions represent the worst case, multiple versions of the conditions may need to be used during APS. In this case, it may not be necessary for all conditions and configurations to be used for all APS.

Rationale

The design, barrier properties, and gloved operation of isolators may provide worst-case conditions and configurations that are different from those identified in conventional lines. Companies should take the time to conduct thoughtful risk assessments to help determine which conditions and configurations represent the most useful challenge to the process and how to most effectively include them in the APS.

解說與應用（繁體中文）

最差情況的定義：正常操作範圍內的最高挑戰

Worst case ≠ 人為製造異常。最差情況是「在允許的操作範圍內，最能暴露製程弱點的條件組合」。這個定義排除了停電、密封失效等系統性失控情境——這些是偏差，不是最差操作條件。

隔離器特有的 Worst-Case 考量維度

隔離器與傳統線的 worst case 識別邏輯不同，需要特別關注：

  • glove port 數量與位置——最接近無菌區域的手套，產生氣流擾動的風險最高
  • 同時操作的干預數量——多人同時使用 glove port，或一人快速連續多個動作
  • 材料傳遞時的展開動作——在隔離器內拆開雙層包裝、組裝零件，空間限制使動作範圍減小但精準度要求更高
  • 延長的生產時間——多班次、多天連續生產，人員與環境的複合挑戰
  • 換班時的物料進出——見 Q8-9，內部操作連動的換班

比喻：壓力測試，不是破壞性測試

工程師測試橋梁承載力，用「設計載重的 1.5 倍」——這是合理的最差情況測試。他們不會開車把橋炸掉，然後說「看，橋塌了，所以我們驗證了最差情況」。APS 的 worst case 設計邏輯相同：在合理範圍內拉高挑戰強度，不是故意破壞系統。

思考問題

你的 APS 方案文件中，「worst-case rationale」這一節是怎麼寫的？有沒有明確說明為什麼這些條件被選為最差情況，而不是其他組合？這個理由能通過查核委員的審視嗎？

Q9-1  |  Aseptic Technique: Isolators vs. Conventional Fill Lines

Original Text — Recommendation & Rationale

Question: Is it necessary to use the same aseptic technique and practices when performing interventions in isolators and conventional fill lines?

Recommendation: Proper aseptic technique and good working principles and practices, as applied in conventional Grade A/ISO 5 cleanrooms, should also be followed in isolator operations where possible. Risk assessments should be used to provide information for making decisions related to the performance and control of interventions and procedures in the isolator.

The isolator equipment and processes should be designed to allow for proper aseptic technique and practices, where reasonably possible. Where equipment and processes cannot be designed in such a way, additional controls should be considered to mitigate risk of contamination, e.g., use of sterile devices.

Rationale: While isolators provide better protection and safety against microbial and particulate ingress from the environment than conventional cleanroom operations, proper aseptic technique and good aseptic practices provide additional assurance of quality and mitigate the risk of unforeseen variables that may adversely affect product quality.

The use of good aseptic practices and techniques as additional controls, where feasible and reasonable, to address unforeseen process variables and mitigate risks of contamination, process lapses, and errors, is prudent and provides a more reliable aseptic process.

中文解析與操作思維

核心原則：屏障減少風險，不取代無菌意識

這是 PDA 在整份 PtC-Isolators 文件中反覆強調的底層邏輯：隔離器提供的是物理屏障，使操作員與產品分離——但無菌操作的思維方式、手部動作的刻意與緩慢、「first air」概念，在隔離器中仍然完全適用。

換句話說：穿戴著手套伸進隔離器的那雙手，依然需要受過無菌技術訓練的人來控制。隔離器不會「自動補償」差勁的操作員。

比喻：外科手套不取代外科技術

外科醫生戴手套動手術，手套防止手部微生物污染傷口——但外科技術本身（精確切割、最小化組織損傷、無菌場域管理）仍然必要。隔離器對無菌操作的關係，正是如此。

CDMO 操作啟示

在多產品 CDMO 環境中，不同批次使用不同配方與組件，每次設定（setup）都涉及干預操作。訓練紀錄必須能展示操作員接受過隔離器特定的無菌技術訓練，而非只有一般 Grade A 潔淨室訓練。

APS（無菌工藝模擬）設計中，應納入最差情況下的干預場景，以驗證無菌技術的穩健性。

Q9-2  |  Interventions Disrupting Unidirectional Airflow (UDF)

Original Text — Recommendation & Rationale

Question: Should interventions that disrupt unidirectional airflow in the isolator in proximity to or above exposed sterile product, product contact surfaces, and indirect product contact surfaces be permitted?

Recommendation: Interventions performed in the decontaminated isolator during aseptic operations should be performed through decontaminated, integral isolator gloves or other means that isolates aseptic processing personnel from the sterile product, product contact surfaces, and isolator environment.

The isolator, the placement of isolator glove positions, and the aseptic operation should be designed to avoid or minimize the disruption of unidirectional airflow above or in the proximity of exposed sterile product or product contact surfaces.

A risk assessment should be performed to determine the level of risk for each specific intervention with regard to a potential contamination created by interventions, e.g., adjusting of filling nozzles or assembling stopper bowls.

If the risk assessment shows that there is low or no risk of contamination added by the intervention, then disrupted or turbulent airflow resulting from the intervention within a properly designed and maintained isolator may be acceptable.

The risk assessment should take into consideration the potential for contamination and the effectiveness of controls in place, including:

  • Design and qualification of the isolator and process
  • Demonstrating that air within the isolator is HEPA filtered and isolated from potential contamination
  • Decontamination of the isolator gloves and other surfaces that turbulent air might contact
  • Maintaining isolator gloves and other surfaces in a decontaminated state
  • Level of aseptic technique used during the intervention
  • Maintaining ISO 5 total particulate and Grade A/ISO 5 viable environmental levels before, during, and after interventions
  • Other risk factors

Where intervention risk is determined to be unacceptable, additional actions may be required, including removal or isolation of product, components, direct product contact surfaces, or indirect product contact surfaces.

Rationale: To function effectively, isolators and exposed critical areas are designed to be more compact than conventional cleanroom filling systems. These relatively closed areas may not demonstrate strict unidirectional airflow during interventions at all surfaces.

This may not represent an increase of contamination risk, though. Isolators provide a physical, rigid barrier between the pressurized, continuously controlled Grade A/ISO 5 interior and the external classified environment and operators. Properly designed, decontaminated, and operated isolators should minimize the impact of non-unidirectional airflow on product quality.

The interior of the isolator, including surfaces that may contact HEPA-filtered air, should be decontaminated, using a validated method, to the point where contact with such surfaces poses little or no risk of contaminating product. If performed properly, turbulent HEPA-filtered air may not pose the same risk as it could with conventional cleanroom applications, where similar surfaces are sanitized rather than decontaminated.

If the isolator is not properly designed, decontaminated, or operated, however, the disruption of airflow resulting in turbulence and contact with adjacent surfaces may pose an increased product contamination risk.

關鍵概念：UDF 被打破 ≠ 自動失敗

這題的答案出乎很多人意料：在隔離器中，干預操作造成氣流紊亂不必然是不可接受的——前提是風險評估支持此結論。

理由是：隔離器的內壁、手套、所有表面都已經過 VHP 去污驗證。當氣流打亂，接觸到的表面本身已經是去污狀態。這與傳統潔淨室本質上不同——潔淨室的表面只是消毒（sanitized），不是去污（decontaminated）。

兩個核心術語區分

  • Sanitized（消毒）：降低微生物數量，未達到滅菌水準，傳統潔淨室表面適用
  • Decontaminated（去污）：透過 VHP 等驗證方法，達到接近無菌的水準；隔離器內部表面適用

因此，隔離器中氣流擾動的風險，比傳統潔淨室低得多——前提是去污週期被正確執行並持續維持。

重點：哪些因素影響風險評估結論？

  • 干預發生時，產品是否暴露？是否在直接產品接觸表面上方？
  • 手套移動的頻率、速度、持續時間
  • 隔離器設計：手套位置相對於產品路徑的空間關係
  • 環境監測（EM）數據：干預前/中/後的 ISO 5 狀態是否維持？
  • APS 中是否模擬了此干預的最差情況？

操作思維：把手套視為「氣流擾動器」

每次手套伸入操作，都像把一塊石頭投入平靜水面——擾動不可避免，但若水本身是乾淨的（已去污的隔離器內部），擾動的影響就大幅降低。

CDMO 實務上的意涵：干預操作應被記錄、分類、納入 APS 設計，而不是任意允許或禁止。每個干預都需要有自己的風險評估文件。

Q9-3  |  Glove Contact with Product Contact Surfaces

Original Text — Recommendation & Rationale

Question: Should contact between decontaminated isolator gloves and direct or indirect product contact surfaces during setup or operations be permitted?

Recommendation: Isolator gloves, even if decontaminated, should not contact the part of the filling or sealing equipment that comes in contact with sterile product or components. Only items or materials that have been sterilized by a validated process should contact those product or product contact surfaces.

Rationale: Although the validated isolator glove decontamination process should be sufficient to render and maintain the glove exterior in a decontaminated state throughout the operation period of the isolator, the environment in the isolator is not sterile. Consequently, a low risk of contamination contacting the exterior of the gloves may remain.

The isolator equipment and aseptic process should be designed to avoid the need for contact between the glove and product contact surfaces during interventions and operations. Therefore, there should be no need to incur even a low additional risk of contamination from contact between gloves and product contact surfaces.

核心原則：去污手套 ≠ 無菌手套

這是整份 PtC-Isolators 中最重要的一個區分：手套透過 VHP 去污，但隔離器內部環境本身並非無菌（sterile）——它只是去污（decontaminated）的。

因此，去污手套觸碰去污表面是可接受的；但去污手套觸碰直接產品接觸表面（direct product contact surfaces）則不可接受，因為那些表面需要被滅菌物品所觸及。

什麼是「禁止接觸區（no-touch zones）」？

  • 灌裝針（filling needle）的產品出口端
  • 膠塞碗（stopper bowl）的內部接觸面
  • 產品路徑上的管線、連接件內側
  • 已滅菌容器（vial、syringe）的開口端

污染控制策略（Contamination Control Strategy, CCS）文件必須明確列出這些「禁止接觸區」，並在 SOP 和訓練材料中加以強調。

設計原則：讓接觸在設計上不可能發生

PDA 的邏輯是：既然去污手套不應接觸直接產品接觸表面，那麼隔離器的設計和操作程序就應該讓這種接觸在物理上或程序上變得「不必要」甚至「不可能」。

這意味著機器設定（machine setup）應由自動化完成，或在裝配時預先完成並以滅菌方式引入隔離器，而不是依賴操作員的手套接觸來完成關鍵設定步驟。

Q9-4  |  Unsterilized / Non-Decontaminated Materials in the Isolator

Original Text — Recommendation & Rationale

Question: Should surfaces or materials that have not been sterilized or decontaminated be exposed in the isolator interior during aseptic operations?

Recommendation: All materials and surfaces entering into or in the isolator that are or may be exposed to the isolator environment should be sterilized or decontaminated using validated and qualified procedures prior to use. They should remain in a decontaminated state throughout the aseptic operation.

  • Interventions that expose surfaces or transfer materials into the isolator that have not been sterilized or decontaminated should be avoided.
  • If an intervention results in the exposure of materials or surfaces that have not been properly sterilized or decontaminated and cannot be avoided, the risk and impact of these interventions on sterile product should be assessed.
  • If the risk assessment concludes that the intervention and exposure pose an unacceptably high level of risk to sterile product, additional actions or controls should be taken to eliminate or change these interventions to mitigate or reduce the risk of contamination, including steps to ensure that the intervention does not contaminate the isolator environment, isolator gloves, sterile product, or product contact surfaces.
  • If additional actions and controls do not adequately mitigate the risk, consideration should be given to stopping aseptic operations or segregating product. Operations should resume only after decontaminated conditions in the isolator have been reestablished.
  • If the mitigation actions include disinfection or decontamination of the exposed surfaces, isolator gloves, or materials, the relative risk and effectiveness of those disinfection or decontamination steps should be considered, and the process designed to minimize the potential risk of spreading or adding chemical contamination to the isolator environment, product, or product contact surfaces.
  • Spraying of chemical disinfectants in proximity to open product contact surfaces especially should be avoided.

Rationale: The effectiveness and operation of the isolator relies on the confidence that the interior of the isolator and its environment remain in a clean and decontaminated state. A primary contamination control strategy for achieving this condition is allowing only sterile or decontaminated surfaces, items, and materials to be present in the isolator. If surfaces, items, or materials are present or exposed that are not sterilized or decontaminated, then this primary contamination control strategy is not in place. Therefore, a higher level of contamination risk may be present.

中文解析與操作思維

隔離器的第一道防線：進入控制

隔離器的去污邏輯建立在一個前提上：隔離器內部的每一個物品都是已滅菌或已去污的。一旦有未處理的物品進入，整個「受控環境」的可信度就被破壞了。

這就像一個無菌房間——只要有一個人未穿隔離衣走進去，整個無菌保證就失效了。隔離器也是同樣邏輯：通道不是用來「快速傳東西」的，而是用來確保進入物品的污染控制狀態。

偏差應對的層級邏輯（Escalation Ladder）

  1. 優先：設計上避免未去污物品進入的需求
  2. 若無法避免：進行風險評估，評估對產品的衝擊
  3. 若風險不可接受：停止操作、隔離產品
  4. 恢復條件：重新建立隔離器的去污狀態（通常需重跑 VHP 週期）
  5. 緩解行動包含化學品噴灑時：必須避免在開放產品接觸表面附近使用，防止化學污染

CDMO 實務關鍵：pass-through 管理

CDMO 多產品環境中，pass-through（傳遞窗）的使用頻率很高。每一次物料傳入都必須有書面記錄：此物料是否已滅菌/去污？方法為何？有效期/保存條件是否維持？

這些記錄不只是法規要求，更是污染根因調查時的第一道追溯證據。批次記錄中的物料引入紀錄，應能完整追溯到 VHP 去污或滅菌批次。

為什麼禁止在開放產品接觸面附近噴消毒劑？

若在消毒動作中使用噴霧（如 IPA、雙氧水溶液），微液滴可能沉降到開放容器或填充針上，造成化學污染（chemical contamination）而非微生物污染。這種污染的後果同樣嚴重，且往往更難偵測。

Q9-5  |  Decontaminated vs. Sterilized Materials — Different Precautions?

Original Text — Recommendation & Rationale

Question: Should different precautions or steps be taken when handling decontaminated materials versus sterilized materials in the decontaminated isolator?

Recommendation: Generally, decontaminated materials may be transferred and handled in the same manner as sterilized material. Therefore, no special precautions need to be taken, beyond the following considerations:

  • Sterilized items, materials, and utensils may contact product and product contact surfaces, providing they remain in a sterile state. Additional precautions, actions, or controls may be needed if the decontaminated items, materials, parts, and utensils do contact sterile product or product contact surfaces.

  • The integrity, hold time, and conditions of wrapped or packaged materials being transferred into the isolator should be qualified through appropriate challenge studies, included in the APS, and verified throughout the aseptic operation.

Rationale: Both the decontamination and the sterilization processes should render items, materials, parts, and utensils free of microorganisms to the extent that they do not pose a risk and are incapable of causing microbiological contamination to sterile product, product contact surfaces, or the isolator environment. Precautions should be taken to ensure that decontaminated or sterilized items, materials, parts, and utensils are maintained and remain in such a decontaminated or sterile state throughout their use in the isolator.

一、隔離器的品質保證基礎：三位一體

整份 PtC-Isolators 文件的底層邏輯，可以歸納為三個相互依存的支柱：

  • 去污週期（Decontamination Cycle）：VHP 去污必須經過完整的開發、驗證、並在每次生產前確認。去污週期的有效性，是隔離器所有無菌保證的前提。一旦此前提失效，隔離器比傳統潔淨室更難補救——因為沒有「進去重新消毒」的空間。
  • 手套完整性程序（Glove Integrity Program）：手套是隔離器最脆弱的接口。破損的手套意味著分隔失效，整批風險立即上升。完整的手套完整性程序包括：使用前壓力測試、目視檢查、破損發現後的即時回應程序，以及 APS 中的手套破損模擬。
  • 操作員訓練（Operator Training）：隔離器不是「設定好就忘記」的技術（not a "set and forget" technology）。操作員需要理解 first air 概念、干預動作對氣流的影響、去污與滅菌的差異、以及各種偏差情境下的應對程序。訓練紀錄必須反映隔離器專屬技能，而非只有一般潔淨室訓練。

三、隔離器作為 CDMO 競爭優勢的戰略意義

從 Operations Director 的視角，隔離器技術對 CDMO 業務的戰略價值在於：

  • 多產品彈性：隔離器允許快速切換產品批次，不需要完整的 Grade A/B 重新建立。每次 VHP 週期後即可轉換，大幅縮短換批時間。
  • 降低基礎設施成本：隔離器內部維持 Grade A，但外部操作環境只需 Grade D 或 Grade C，大幅降低潔淨室建設與維護成本。
  • 減少穿衣負擔：操作員無需全套 Grade A/B 無菌服，降低人員培訓時間與不適感，提升操作效率。
  • 更高的無菌保證水準（SAL）：隔離器透過物理屏障 + VHP 去污，比傳統潔淨室提供更高的無菌保證，對高風險產品（如 ATMP、高活性藥物）尤為關鍵。
  • 法規認可的競爭力：EU Annex 1 (2022) 明確推薦隔離器作為無菌製造的優先選擇。具備隔離器能力的 CDMO，在法規合規性上具有顯著優先地位。
