1.0 Introduction

1.0 Introduction

The complexity of global post-approval change/variation (PAC) processes and the time required to receive their approval presents a challenge for industry when it is working to implement needed changes and operating with a focus on continual improvement. Industry benchmarking shows that receiving approval for a PAC requiring global approval can take between two and five years, and the time it takes from original submission can vary from six months to several years depending on the jurisdiction and review pathway.

For new products, where the amount of commercial process or product data at the time of the initial submission is limited, this timing can significantly impact the ability to implement necessary process improvements identified as more data becomes available. In specific cases, when a change is needed to ensure the product remains safe, efficacious, and of high quality or provides additional capacity based on high product demand, the global approval timing can result in drug product shortages.

The current regulatory pathway to implement change is highly complex. For each jurisdiction in which a product is licensed, the respective regulatory agency typically requires a notification, based on its risk reporting category, or it must preapprove certain PACs for certain products. Differences in country- and region-specific requirements for PAC filings, filing procedures, and associated timelines can often make simultaneous implementation of changes problematic, necessitating a product be tested or produced using different processes based on where it will be distributed.

These challenges were highlighted during the COVID-19 pandemic, as companies continued to manufacture lifesaving products despite the extreme strain on supply chains for raw materials, components, and manufacturing supplies. As alternative sources of supply or alternative materials were identified, the complexity of the global regulatory PAC process presented a significant challenge to product supply, highlighting the need for a harmonized approach to PAC implementation.

解說與思考 Commentary

核心概念：PAC 的全球困境

PAC（Post-Approval Change，上市後變更）是任何已取得藥品許可的製造商都必須面對的課題。全球批准最長需 2–5 年，這意味著一個緊急的製程改善，可能在問題發生後好幾年才能在所有市場正式實施。

對 CDMO 而言，這個時間差尤其關鍵：你必須在不同市場同時維持多個版本的製程，同步管理多套文件、驗證數據、以及不同批次的 QC 放行標準。

比喻：全球高速公路的紅綠燈

想像你要開車從台北到高雄，但每個城市的紅綠燈規則不一樣——有的地方綠燈 30 秒，有的地方需要先申請「變換車道許可」才能過彎。PAC 的全球申請就是這樣：同一個變更，美國 FDA 可能只需要 CBE-30（30天生效通知），但日本可能需要事前核准，而 WHO 又有另一套時程。結果是你在不同市場開著「不同版本的車」上路。

重點提示：COVID-19 的教訓

疫情是一個現實壓力測試。當原材料供應鏈斷裂、需要緊急替換供應商時，法規 PAC 流程的複雜性直接威脅藥品供應。這是 TR91 出版的直接背景之一——業界迫切需要一套更有彈性、更協調的工具。

1.1 Purpose

1.1 Purpose

This technical report expands on the latest concepts and tools for improving PAC management, as published in ICH Q12. It provides a practical guide for the pharmaceutical industry on how to implement PACs within the framework of an effective pharmaceutical quality system (PQS) and in alignment with ICH quality guidelines. It recognizes the challenges that the limited amount of global harmonization among regulators presents with respect to PACs and attempts to provide some insight on how industry can work with regulators to improve in this area. Though TR-91 aligns with ICH Q12, it also provides guidance for non-ICH regions. It suggests how to implement the tools described in ICH Q12 and provides examples of the concepts that can facilitate aligned industry understanding for PACs.

TR-91 highlights the benefits of some of the tools described in ICH Q12 that are already available in certain jurisdictions, specifically, PAC management protocols (PACMPs, or comparability protocols) and established conditions (ECs). These concepts were acceptable for use by several regulatory agencies prior to ICH Q12, that is, ECs and comparability protocols by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and comparability protocols by the European Medicines Agency (EMA) and WHO.

Section 10.0 (Appendix 1) and Section 11.0 (Appendix 2) provide examples of PACs common in industry; these are presented as representative frameworks for consideration, presented as hypothetical PACMPs (or comparability protocols) that can be leveraged for similar changes. These examples, although not all-inclusive, do offer insights into the types of documentation and the scientific justification that may be required for implementation. They are intended to be illustrative, but they do portray how negotiation with regulators, where existing requirements may be disproportionate to risk given either knowledge gained through development or process experience, could be demonstrated using a PACMP approach.

解說與思考 Commentary

核心概念：TR91 填補了什麼空缺？

ICH Q12 於 2019 年發布，建立了上市後變更管理的原則框架，引入了 ECs、PACMP 等概念。但 ICH Q12 是原則性文件，它告訴你「什麼是 Established Conditions」，卻不告訴你「如何在實際申請中設定 ECs」。

TR91 就是那本「操作手冊」——它把 ICH Q12 的概念翻譯成實務步驟，並附上假設性的 PACMP 案例，讓你看到真實的文件結構與科學論證邏輯。

比喻：法律條文 vs 法庭實務手冊

ICH Q12 好比法律條文——定義了規則與原則。TR91 好比法律從業者的實務手冊——告訴你在實際案例中怎麼寫狀紙、怎麼與法官（監管機構）談判、哪些證據最有說服力。兩者缺一不可。

重點提示：CDMO 的特殊地位

TR91 的對象是「製藥業界」，但 CDMO 扮演的角色與 MAH（Marketing Authorization Holder，上市許可持有人）不同。CDMO 執行變更，MAH 擁有申請文件。因此，TR91 中所有「如何與監管機構談判」的指引，在 CDMO 情境下必須翻譯成：如何支援你的客戶（MAH）準備完整的 PAC 申請包裝，以及在 QTA 中清楚規範誰負責哪個環節。

1.2 Scope

1.2 Scope

The information in this technical report focuses on chemistry, manufacturing, and controls (CMC) changes that can be applied to PACs for both small-molecule and large-molecule pharmaceutical products.

解說與思考 Commentary

核心概念：TR91 的範疇邊界

TR91 的範疇非常明確——只涵蓋 CMC 變更（化學、製造與品管），適用於小分子藥品（化學藥）與大分子藥品（生物製劑，如 mAb、蛋白質藥物）。

它不處理：

  • 臨床安全性或有效性的重新評估
  • 適應症擴充或新劑型的初始申請
  • 非 CMC 的標籤變更

換言之，TR91 的核心問題是：「製造方式或品管方式改變了，我如何向監管機構證明產品品質沒有下降？」

比喻：裝修已有建築 vs 蓋新房子

TR91 談的是「已核准產品的裝修工程」——更換設備、改善製程、更換原料供應商。它不談「蓋新房子」（新產品開發），也不談「改變房子用途」（適應症變更）。這個邊界的釐清，讓你在閱讀每一個章節時，都能問自己：「這個變更是 CMC 性質的嗎？」

重點提示：小分子與大分子的差異

雖然 TR91 同時適用於兩者，但大分子（biologics）的 PAC 通常更複雜：因為生物製劑的製程本身就定義了產品，任何製程變更都可能影響可比性（comparability）。這也是為什麼「Comparability Protocol」這個工具在生物製劑領域特別重要。

核心術語解析：三個最重要的概念

1. Established Conditions (ECs) — 已建立條件
ECs 是核准文件中被認定為「確保產品品質的關鍵描述」。任何對 ECs 的更動都需要向監管機構申報。理解 ECs 的範疇是 PAC 管理的起點——你必須先知道「什麼被鎖定了」，才能判斷一個變更是否需要申報。

2. PACMP（Post-Approval Change Management Protocol）— 上市後變更管理協定
PACMP 是一種「預先協議」機制：你在實際執行變更之前，先向監管機構提交一份計畫，說明你將如何評估這個變更。一旦協議被核准，後續執行時只需提交數據確認，大幅簡化流程。

3. Product Lifecycle Management (PLCM) — 產品生命週期管理文件
PLCM 文件是整個 PAC 管理架構的「地圖」，列出所有 ECs、變更的申報類別、以及已核准的 PACMP。對 MAH 來說，這是與監管機構溝通的主文件；對 CDMO 來說，這是確認你的職責範圍的重要依據。

比喻：ECs vs 非 ECs

把核准文件想像成一棟建築的「建照」。ECs 就像建照中明確載明的結構規格（牆厚、承重柱位置）——任何更動都必須重新申請許可。非 ECs 的部分就像室內裝潢——可以在一定範圍內自行更改，只需通知或備查。

PACMP 的價值在於：你提前告訴主管機關「我打算怎麼改牆」，取得預先認可。等你真正動工時，只需提交施工記錄，不需重新跑一次完整審查流程。

重點提示：FDA vs ICH 對 ECs 的定義差異

FDA 將 ECs 定義為「確保製程表現與產品品質的描述」，較著重功能性。ICH 則強調 ECs 是「具法律約束力的資訊」。實務上，這個差異意味著在不同市場，同一個製程描述可能被歸類為不同程度的 EC，因此需要分別評估申報類別。

縮寫速記：TR91 的核心語言

這份縮寫表是閱讀 TR91 全文的「解碼鑰匙」。以下幾組縮寫特別值得記憶：

  • EC / CPP / CQA：品質管理的三角關係——CQA 定義產品品質目標，CPP 是影響 CQA 的製程參數，EC 是需要在核准文件中申報的關鍵描述（可能包含也可能不包含 CPP）。
  • CBE（Changes Being Effected）：一種中等風險的申報途徑，先執行再申報（或申報同時執行），有 CBE-0 和 CBE-30 兩種。

比喻：PAC 申報的「速限分級」

把不同申報途徑想像成道路速限：

  • Prior Approval (PAS) = 高速公路施工 → 需要事前完整審查核准才能動工
  • CBE-30 = 省道改道 → 先通知主管機關，30 天後若無異議才能實施
  • CBE-0 / Notification = 巷道標線調整 → 通知即可，無需等待核准
  • Annual Report = 定期道路狀況回報 → 低風險變更，年底彙整申報即可

PACMP 的價值在於：事先與監管機構協商好「哪些變更走哪條道路」，避免每次變更都要重新判斷。

重點提示：ICH 的角色

ICH（人用藥品技術要求國際協調理事會）是 TR91 引用最多的標準制定機構。ICH Q12 是 PAC 管理的主要框架；ICH Q8/Q9/Q10 則提供了品質設計（QbD）、風險管理、與品質系統的基礎。TR91 大量引用這些 ICH 指引，因此對這些文件有基本認識是閱讀 TR91 的前提。

3.0 Background and Landscape for PACs — 背景與全球監管環境

3.0 Background and Landscape for PACs

The importance of PAC and the need to overcome these myriad challenges has led many international organizations, including the Parenteral Drug Association (PDA), to develop recommendations and proposals to address the situation:

  • PDA PAC iAM (Post Approval Change; Innovations of the Availability of Medicines) program has developed numerous papers, including this technical report.
  • The European Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations (EFPIA) and International Federation of Pharmaceutical Manufacturers and Associations (IFPMA) have issued position papers on PAC, calling for regulatory convergence and proposing a relevant framework.
  • One Voice of Quality for PAC, an initiative sponsored by the chief quality officers of more than 30 global pharmaceutical companies, has been developing risk-based recommendations to help reduce the global complexity associated with PACs.
  • The World Health Organization (WHO) has provided guidance, suggesting pathways and requirements for PACs.
  • The International Council for Harmonisation (ICH) has continued its push for harmonization by publishing Quality Guideline Q12: Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management, which focuses specifically on PACs, while building upon established ICH Quality Guidelines Q8–Q11.

The ICH Q12 guidance, which describes key concepts necessary to streamline the PAC process, is recognized as a significant step forward. In addition to the implementation of ICH Q12, there are areas where work is still needed to achieve full harmonization, including:

  • Agreement on the change categories among regulatory authorities
  • Harmonization on filing procedures and timelines within ICH regions
  • Inclusion of non-ICH regulatory authorities to converge on the application of these principles

By fully implementing ICH Quality Guidelines Q8–Q11, manufacturing firms can develop an effective internal PQS sufficient to optimize the management of PACs. Demonstrating the effectiveness of the PQS would allow a reduction in the regulatory reporting burden for changes with a lower level of risk, as they would be managed through a firm's PQS, by:

  • Ensuring ICH Q8–Q12 are implemented and leveraged as building blocks for PAC management
  • Working with regulators to drive consistent reporting categories
  • Developing a robust PQS system
  • Enhancing transparency and trust between companies and regulators to identify when a firm's PQS system is sufficient to allow certain types of lower-risk change to be implemented without reporting

In keeping with ICH Q10 and Q12 guidances and with a robust PQS system in place, PACs requiring prior approval by regulatory authorities would be limited to changes assessed as having the potential to have a major impact on product quality and/or patient safety. Minor-to-moderate changes would be made with notification to the regulatory authorities, and low-risk changes would be managed under a firm's PQS. 解說：全球 PAC 監管環境

核心概念：為什麼需要協調？

藥廠做一個變更（例如更換原料藥來源），在美國可能只需 CBE-30（30天通知），在歐盟卻需要 Type II（重大變更，事前核准），在日本又是另一套規定。同樣的風險，四套報告要求 — 這就是 PAC 全球複雜性的核心問題。

ICH Q12（2019年發布）是迄今最重要的框架，試圖把這些分歧拉近。但 Q12 只是起點，非終點。

比喻：護照通關 vs. 每國重辦簽證

試想你持有一本護照，在大多數國家可以快速通關，但每次都要重新辦理該國簽證 — 即使你的風險評估已做得很完整。現在的 PAC 世界就是這樣：每個市場都要「重新申報」，不承認其他地區的評估結果。

ICH Q12 的願景就是讓「品質護照」成立：如果你的 PQS 夠強、資料夠透明，主管機關應該信任你、讓你用更低的申報類別管理低風險變更。

重點：三層申報架構

  • 高風險 → 事前核准（Prior Approval / PAS / Type II）：先說、等待、再做
  • 中低風險 → 通知（Notification / CBE / Type IB）：做了之後告知
  • 低風險 → 完全由 PQS 管理：只在內部記錄，無需外部申報

這個三層架構是 TR91 整份報告的底層邏輯 — 每個後續章節都在探討「如何讓更多變更往低風險那一層移動」。

各地區申報類別對照（同一變更，不同要求）

同一個製程變更，在不同地區可能落在不同類別 — 這正是全球協調工作的難點。

4.0 Key Elements of a Pharmaceutical Quality System for PAC Management — PQS 五大核心要素

4.0 Key Elements of a Pharmaceutical Quality System for PAC Management

An effective PQS is the foundation upon which PAC management and implementation are established. PQS effectiveness in relation to risk-based change management is a core concept within the ICH Q12 framework. Several PQS elements are needed to establish effective PAC management:

  • Management review
  • A corrective action and preventive action (CAPA) program
  • A change management system
  • Process performance and product quality monitoring

Importantly, knowledge management and quality risk management are key enablers of a firm's PQS.

Having an established PQS that is executed effectively and consistently within a firm will provide confidence to the regulatory agency that changes are supported by data obtained through the application of patient-centric, scientific, risk-based principles. A well designed and effective PQS is an essential foundation for trust between regulatory authorities and industry that will facilitate harmonization on the categorization of changes. It also should allow more PACs to be managed under the firm's PQS without requiring prior review and approval by regulatory agencies.

When using contract manufacturing organizations (CMO) or contract development and manufacturing organizations (CDMO), a firm needs to ensure that appropriate contractual agreements are in place for PACs occurring at the CMO or CDMO and that they maintain an effective PQS. The same principles for lifecycle management and reporting responsibility apply.

解說：PQS 是 PAC 的地基

核心概念：PQS 不只是文件系統

PQS（製藥品質系統）在這裡的意義遠超過 SOP 和記錄管理。它是一套能夠「主動識別變更需求、評估風險、決定申報類別、執行變更、驗證效果」的完整管理架構。

ICH Q12 的核心主張：一家公司的 PQS 越成熟，主管機關就越願意讓它自主管理低風險變更 — 減少事前核准、增加 PQS 內部自理。這是「信任換取彈性」的交換邏輯。

比喻：飛行員執照分級

初學飛行員每次起飛都需要塔台核准；有儀器飛行證書（IFR）的飛行員可以在更多條件下自主判斷；有豐富記錄的機長甚至可以在特定情境下自主決策。

PQS 就是你的「飛行紀錄本」——越完整、越成熟，主管機關對你的信任度越高，賦予你的自主空間也越大。

CDMO 的特別責任

如果你是 CDMO（例如 TWi），你的客戶是 MAH（上市許可持有人），但製程發生在你的設施裡。這段話特別重要：

  • CDMO 必須維持有效 PQS，且與客戶的 PAC 責任合約有明確規範
  • 變更管理的生命週期責任（包括申報）不因外包而消失
  • 客戶的申報義務 + CDMO 的 PQS 能力，兩者缺一不可

思考練習

你們的 CDMO 合約中，對 PAC 的責任歸屬是否有明確條款？當製程在你們廠發生變更時，誰啟動 PAC 評估？誰決定申報類別？這個流程是否書面化？

4.1 Management Review — 管理層審查

4.1 Management Review

A firm's senior management is responsible for ensuring that appropriate systems and processes are in place to manage changes effectively. In accordance with ICH Quality Guideline Q10: Pharmaceutical Quality System, these responsibilities include:

  • Defining and communicating roles within the change management system
  • Ensuring timely and appropriate communication to address potential quality issues
  • Providing adequate resources to ensure that changes are made based on sound scientific and risk-based judgments
  • Maintaining awareness of the long-term product lifecycle management strategy and ensuring that appropriate controls are in place
  • Developing and fostering the desired quality culture at all levels throughout the firm to safeguard the patient's access to high-quality medicines

Quality culture is a set of behaviors, beliefs, and values of an enterprise that serve as a guide to decision-making. In the ideal state, a quality mindset and quality-focused behaviors are embedded into all functions of daily work and result in positive patient outcomes. In an organization with a well-established quality-culture environment, employees not only follow quality guidelines but consistently see others taking quality-focused actions, hear others talk about quality, and understand the importance of quality in everyday activities.

For sustained effectiveness, the PQS needs to be embedded alongside a firm's culture of quality for it to properly perform its function and be considered reliable. Management plays an important role in nurturing key elements of a mature quality culture, including a focus on continuous improvement, demonstration of leadership, communication of a common vision, and investment in employee development and education. Training is essential to ensure a proficient and knowledgeable quality-oriented workforce.

解說：管理層是 PAC 的守門人

核心概念：PAC 是品質系統活動，不只是法規合規

許多公司把 PAC 視為「法規部門的事」——只有在必須申報時才啟動。但 ICH Q10 的精神是：PAC 應該是管理層定期審查的議題，因為每一個變更都是產品生命週期管理的一部分。

管理層的責任具體包括：定義角色、分配資源、建立文化。特別是「文化」這一點，不是管理層說說而已 — 員工看到的日常行為才算數。

比喻：品質文化如同組織免疫系統

免疫系統不是在你生病時才啟動的 — 它平時就在運作，隨時識別異常並回應。品質文化也是如此：不是在稽查前緊急補文件，而是每個人在日常工作中都把「這個決定對病患安全有何影響？」當成反射性問題。

管理層的角色是讓這個「免疫系統」保持活化 — 通過榜樣、資源、教育訓練，而不只是 KPI 考核。

重點提示：管理審查與 PAC 的連結

你的年度管理審查（Management Review）是否包含以下 PAC 相關指標？

  • 本年度啟動的 PAC 數量與類型
  • PAC 申報時間是否符合各市場要求
  • PAC 有效性查核（Effectiveness Check）的結果
  • 因 PAC 衍生的 CAPA 追蹤狀況

如果這些指標不在管理審查中，PAC 就只是法規部門的例行工作，而非公司層級的品質承諾。

思考練習

上次你參加或主持管理審查時，有沒有討論到「我們的 PAC 管理有沒有比上一年度更有效率？」這個問題？如果沒有，是什麼原因？資料不足、沒有指定負責人，還是組織文化上這不被視為管理層該關心的事？

4.2 Corrective Action and Preventive Action (CAPA) Program — CAPA 系統

4.2 Corrective Action and Preventive Action Program

A properly functioning CAPA system identifies not only when actions are needed as a result of an event that has occurred, but also operates proactively to improve based on weaknesses identified prior to an event occurring. Ideally, the majority of all actions would be preventative. The CAPA system is often where the initial need for change is identified and where the PAC process begins. Having a strong CAPA program is a critical component of any PQS system, which will lead to better process understanding, process control, and determining when action is needed.

An effective CAPA program monitors and manages unintended risks and consequences of PACs for internal and outsourced operations. It should enable identification of root cause(s) so that appropriate actions can be taken to correct problems and prevent their recurrence. The program should also monitor and verify the effectiveness of any CAPAs associated with PAC initiatives.

解說：CAPA 是 PAC 的起點

核心概念：CAPA → 變更需求 → PAC

CAPA 系統是 PAC 流程最常見的發源地。以下是典型的觸發路徑：

  • 缺陷矯正：OOS（超標結果）調查 → 根本原因是製程不穩定 → CAPA 要求製程改善 → 觸發 PAC
  • 主動改善：趨勢分析發現製程漂移 → 預防性 CAPA → 啟動製程優化 PAC
  • 法規更新：主管機關發布新指引 → 現有方法不符合 → CAPA 要求方法驗證更新 → PAC

CAPA 不只是「出了問題才做」，理想狀態下大多數行動應該是「預防性」的 — 這正是成熟 PQS 的標誌。

比喻：CAPA 是品質系統的神經系統

神經系統接收全身訊號（痛覺、壓力、溫度），整合後輸出指令（縮手、調整姿勢）。CAPA 系統的功能類似：接收各種品質訊號（偏差、投訴、稽查觀察），整合根本原因分析，輸出系統性改善行動。

一個「只有反應，沒有預防」的 CAPA 系統，就像一個只有痛覺、沒有本體感覺的神經系統 — 等到痛了才知道出問題，已經太晚了。

重點：CAPA 與 PAC 的雙向關係

CAPA 不只觸發 PAC，也必須監控 PAC 本身帶來的非預期後果。一個變更實施後，如果監測發現品質指標惡化，必須立即啟動 CAPA — 這是 PAC 有效性查核的安全閥機制。

雙向流動：CAPA → PAC，以及 PAC 後監測 → CAPA。

思考練習

回顧你們過去12個月的 CAPA 清單：有多少 CAPA 最終觸發了 PAC 評估？這個比例是否合理？如果 CAPA 很多但 PAC 幾乎沒有，可能代表 CAPA 處理的都是表面問題，沒有觸及製程根本改善。

4.3 Change Management System — 變更管理系統

4.3 Change Management System

While most changes made have no impact on the regulatory dossier, the importance of having a system that appropriately evaluates changes and their impact on the manufacturing processes and product cannot be emphasized enough. The change process must be able to adequately evaluate the change, the level of risk to the process and product, and the need for a PAC to be filed. Tools, such as those described in ICH Q10 and Q12 and in the PIC/S Recommendation: How to Evaluate/Demonstrate the Effectiveness of a Pharmaceutical Quality System in relation to Risk-based Change Management, focus on the relevance of risk-based change management.

The PIC/S recommendation provides a checklist to evaluate the effectiveness of a firm's PQS in relation to risk-based change management and acknowledges that the level of effort and associated documentation should be commensurate with the level of risk the change entails. This, in turn, should be used to justify the categorization and filing requirements for that change. To ensure that the underlying risk level is well understood, the change management process needs to be robust enough to capture the impact of any proposed change. Therefore, it must support a knowledge-based assessment and review of the change, ensuring that the final scientific justification supports the ultimate filing decision to the applicable regulatory agency.

A structured, well-organized change management process is essential for a successful PAC process. It must cover:

  • Critical Quality Attributes (CQA)
  • Critical Process Parameters (CPP)
  • Product Lifecycle Management (PLCM)
  • Control strategy
  • Creating Established Conditions (ECs) as appropriate

Such an approach ensures that relevant quality aspects are considered, and possible quality impacts are thoroughly assessed, documented, and communicated to relevant stakeholders, including regulatory bodies.

解說：變更管理系統決定申報路徑

核心概念：變更分類決定一切

變更管理系統的最關鍵功能是「分類」——這個變更是否影響 CQA？是否涉及 CPP？是否改變了 Established Conditions（EC）？這些問題的答案直接決定申報類別。

典型的變更管理流程：

變更提案（Change Initiation）
↓
影響評估（Impact Assessment：CQA / CPP / EC）
↓
法規分類（Regulatory Classification）
↓
實施 → 驗證 → 有效性查核

比喻：建築許可申請

你要改建房子：換一扇窗（低風險）vs. 拆掉一面承重牆（高風險）。換窗只需內部記錄；拆牆需要建築師評估、結構工程師簽名、政府核可。

變更管理系統就是那個「判斷這個改動是換窗還是拆牆」的評估機制。問題在於：很多公司沒有清晰的判斷標準，導致所有變更都按最高等級申報（浪費資源），或者低估風險少報（監管風險）。

重點：Established Conditions (ECs) 的核心地位

ICH Q12 引入 EC（已建立條件）的概念：這些是法規申請中記載的製程/產品參數，一旦改變就需要申報。

有效的變更管理系統必須能清楚回答：「這個變更是否改變了任何 EC？」如果 EC 清單設計得好（只包含真正關鍵的參數），公司就能把更多低風險變更保留在 PQS 內部管理。

思考練習

你們公司的 CMS（Change Management System）在評估一個新的變更時，是否有結構化的問卷或決策樹來判斷是否影響 CQA/CPP/EC？這個判斷是由誰做出的（單人決定 vs. 跨部門審查）？文件記錄是否足以支撐未來稽查時的科學論證？

4.4 Process Performance and Product Quality Monitoring — 製程績效與產品品質監測

4.4 Process Performance and Product Quality Monitoring

A comprehensive program to monitor the manufacturing process performance and product quality should be in place at every manufacturing facility to ensure that the process continues to perform in a state of control and in keeping with the validated state. A sound monitoring plan allows a firm to establish the baseline process performance and detect early warnings of atypical process performance.

Having some process monitoring data before and after the change is an essential component of the overall qualification of any change. In essence, product and process monitoring results serve as the scientific basis for improvement of the manufacturing process and the control system. Typically, after a process change is put in place, monitoring frequency is increased to confirm that the process continues to perform as expected. For example, documentation of any deviations from trend limits and/or adverse trends with respect to process, in-process, release, and/or stability testing parameters should be carefully assessed in line with the quality system. These deviations should be investigated for root cause and evaluated to ensure the change has no adverse impact on product quality.

Effective change management is fundamental to ensuring that PACs are controlled in an environment characterized by data-driven, scientific, and risk-based assessments. In an effective change management environment, all aspects of the product and process are managed to ensure that there are no unintended consequences and that product quality and process performance are maintained in a state of control.

解說：APR/PQR 是 PAC 觸發器

核心概念：監測數據 = PAC 的科學依據

PPPQ（製程績效與產品品質）監測，也就是我們日常說的 APR（Annual Product Review）或 PQR（Product Quality Review），是 PAC 的兩個關鍵功能：

  • 觸發功能：趨勢分析發現 OOS 增加、穩定性失敗、製程漂移 → 產生變更需求 → 觸發 PAC
  • 驗證功能：變更實施後，監測數據確認變更沒有帶來非預期後果

沒有完善監測計畫的公司，等於既無法提前發現問題，也無法在申報時提交有力的數據支持。

比喻：心電圖 vs. 只在胸痛時才量血壓

連續心電圖監測可以在心臟病發前就發現異常心律；只有在胸痛時才去量血壓，等到症狀出現時往往已經是嚴重問題。

製程監測就是品質系統的「連續心電圖」——越早發現趨勢偏離，越能以低成本的預防性 PAC 介入，而不是等到批次失敗才做緊急矯正。

重點：變更前後的監測密度

這段文字有一個非常實用的操作指引：變更實施後，監測頻率應該提高（increased monitoring frequency），直到確認製程仍在預期範圍內。

在驗證方案（Validation Protocol）和 PAC 申報文件中，應該明確說明：

  • 變更前基線數據（Pre-change baseline）
  • 變更後強化監測計畫（Post-change enhanced monitoring plan）
  • 回復正常監測的判斷標準（Criteria to return to normal monitoring）

思考練習

你們的 APR/PQR 報告有沒有「PAC 觸發分析」這一節？也就是：本年度 APR 發現了哪些趨勢，哪些趨勢已轉化為正式的變更評估？如果 APR 只是例行性的數據彙整，沒有連接到變更管理系統，它對 PAC 的價值就大打折扣。

4.5 Quality Risk Management and Knowledge Management — QRM 與知識管理

4.5 Quality Risk Management and Knowledge Management

Quality risk management (QRM) and knowledge management (KM) are key enablers of an effective PQS. An organization that strives to have a strong, quality-oriented culture will use QRM and KM to guide the identification and proactive management of PACs. PDA Technical Report No. 54: Implementation of Quality Risk Management for Pharmaceutical and Biotechnology Manufacturing Operations provides a comprehensive description of the QRM elements and tools aligned with ICH Q9.

An effective QRM system provides a sound framework that ensures systematic and proactive risk-based and data-driven decision-making is being employed for all PACs. QRM should enable innovation by allowing faster implementation of changes that reduce the risk of quality failures and manufacturing problems and improve process capability. An effective QRM system can also separate changes that require regulatory reporting or notification from changes that can be managed completely within the PQS. Effective QRM enables a firm to demonstrate an understanding of the existing production process and of the risks, associated impacts, and mitigation of those risks in that process.

An effective KM system leverages historical and recently identified product and process knowledge, including innovation and technology advancements. It also considers knowledge gained from process monitoring, deviations, trends, complaints, recalls, annual product or product quality review, audits, inspections, and management reviews. Effective KM ensures that the right knowledge is used by the right people at the right time, allowing for effective risk-based decision-making and formal product and process risk assessments. A systematic, transparent, and well-understood KM system ensures that changes are based on scientific rationale and can facilitate implementation of identified changes accordingly.

Several FDA, Health Canada, and WHO guidance documents on CMC changes in a post-approval situation demonstrate how product and process knowledge and effective QRM can be leveraged to establish lesser reporting categories for changes. FDA consistently states in guidances its intent to apply the principles of quality risk assessment and risk management in accordance with ICH Q9 during their review. The Agency recognizes that, in order to keep pace with the many advances in quality management practices in manufacturing and enable it to more effectively allocate its limited regulatory resources, implementing a risk-based approach for regulating pharmaceutical manufacturing is necessary. Accordingly, one FDA guidance allows that changes demonstrated to have a minimal impact to a product can be filed as an annual reportable item. Likewise, the Health Canada and WHO guidances, which provide conditions to be fulfilled for specific change types to help guide firms on how to assess risk and categorize changes accordingly, include the option of annual notification for minimal impact. In light of this, the principles of KM and QRM can be equally applied in both ICH and non-ICH regions and should be recognized by global regulatory authorities.

解說：QRM + KM 是 PAC 決策的雙引擎

核心概念：QRM 決定「做不做」，KM 決定「怎麼證明」

QRM（ICH Q9） 在 PAC 中的作用：

  • 用 FMEA、風險矩陣等工具評估變更的潛在影響
  • 決定這個變更是「高風險需要事前核准」還是「低風險可以 PQS 自管」
  • 讓申報決策有可追溯的科學依據

KM（ICH Q10） 在 PAC 中的作用：

  • 彙整歷史製程知識、先前驗證數據、研發階段的理解
  • 讓風險評估不是憑感覺，而是基於「我們真的了解這個製程」
  • 當知識充分時，可以支持更低的申報類別（因為「我們了解，所以風險可控」）

比喻：QRM 是 GPS 導航，KM 是地圖資料庫

GPS（QRM）告訴你現在應該走哪條路、風險在哪裡；但如果地圖資料庫（KM）是空的或過時的，GPS 再強也會導錯路。

同樣地，QRM 工具再精良，如果公司沒有完整保存製程知識（驗證報告、DOE 數據、歷史趨勢），風險評估就只是紙上談兵。KM 是 QRM 的燃料。

重點：知識充分 = 申報類別降低

這是本節最重要的實務啟示：FDA、Health Canada、WHO 的指引都明確表示，當一個變更被充分論證為「對產品影響極小」時，可以降低申報類別（甚至只需年度申報）。

這個「充分論證」就需要 KM + QRM 共同支撐：

  • KM 提供：我們了解這個製程對產品品質的影響機理
  • QRM 提供：風險評估顯示這個變更不會影響 CQA
  • 結論：申報類別可以從 PAS 降為 CBE-30 或 Annual Report

對 CDMO 而言，這也意味著：越早建立完整的製程知識庫，越能在未來的 PAC 中幫助客戶節省申報時間和成本。

思考練習

你們的製程知識目前存放在哪裡？是分散在各個驗證報告、研發筆記、個別部門的 Excel 裡，還是有一個可搜尋、可引用的結構化知識庫？當你需要做一個 PAC 的風險評估時，能多快找到支持性的歷史數據？

如果你是 CDMO，你的製程知識庫是否已足夠支持未來客戶要求的「降級申報論證」？這是一個值得現在就開始投資的能力。

Section 2 重點總結 — PQS 五要素與全球 PAC 框架

核心架構回顧

  • ICH Q12（2019） 是 PAC 監管彈性的使能框架，但全球協調仍有缺口（分類標準、申報時程、非 ICH 地區）。
  • 三層申報架構：高風險（事前核准）→ 中低風險（通知）→ 低風險（PQS 自管）— 目標是讓更多變更落在後兩層。
  • 強大的 PQS 是獲得監管信任的關鍵，是讓更多 PAC 在公司內部管理的前提。
  • CDMO 有特別責任：須與客戶合約明定 PAC 責任歸屬，且自身 PQS 必須有效。

PQS 五要素與其 PAC 功能

  • 管理審查（4.1）：定義責任、投入資源、建立品質文化 — PAC 是公司層級的品質承諾，不只是法規部門的事。
  • CAPA（4.2）：PAC 最常見的起點；也是變更後非預期後果的偵測機制。CAPA → 變更請求 → PAC。
  • 變更管理系統（4.3）：評估 CQA/CPP/EC 影響、決定申報類別 — 分類準確是降低申報負擔的關鍵。
  • PPPQ 監測（4.4）：APR/PQR 是 PAC 觸發器；變更前後的監測數據是科學論證的基礎。
  • QRM + KM（4.5）：QRM 評估風險、KM 提供製程知識 — 兩者充分則可支持申報類別降級。

5.0 PAC Process Tools to Enhance Regulatory Flexibility

5.0 PAC Process Tools to Enhance Regulatory Flexibility

Maintaining GMP, combined with continuous learning, improvement, and in-depth knowledge about the production process and its CPPs and/or ECs, contributes to ongoing optimization. This optimization often results in changes to the product files submitted to and approved by the authorities, aiming for a new desired state. Regulatory tools should be used to enhance the management of these PACs in a way that encourages innovation and continuous improvement. Doing so may help a firm avoid time-consuming, iterative, and expensive processes and, instead, encourage smooth, straightforward interaction and transparent communication with regulatory authorities.

When deciding to implement a PAC, thereby aiming for a new desired state, the tools suggested in Table 5.0-9🔍 may prove helpful when writing the documentation and scientific justification. The use of tools and points for consideration can aid in identifying and presenting, at the outset, the most convincing and thorough documentation required for the regulatory approval process. However, while the use of a PACMP as discussed in ICH Q12 continues to gain acceptance, its use is not currently accepted in all countries.

The decision about change implementation should focus on potential product impact and maintenance or improvement of quality and compliance. The development of a business case can be a helpful tool that serves solely as internal documentation and initial justification of the change. The business case should clearly lay out pros and cons regarding cost versus benefit and impact on product quality. Other areas could potentially be impacted as well and should be considered, such as other manufacturing processes, submitted or drafted regulatory applications, quality or business procedures, functions or departments, collaborating partners, and stakeholders.

Subject matter experts are key stakeholders, and employee knowledge and insight are crucial to a successful PAC process. By obtaining increased process and product understanding, including science- and risk-based approaches for stability studies to support the evaluation of the changes, critical sources of variability can be identified and appropriately managed. By assessing potential impacts of the change, relevant mitigation strategies can be developed.

Written rationales justifying the changes should be based on the assessment of different parameters, for instance, impacts to CQAs, ECs (if applicable), and CPPs. When relevant, the process risk assessment should link process parameters and critical material attributes to CQAs, thereby defining the CPPs and linking them to an acceptable, scientifically justified range for the different process parameters.

The type of change and the type of operational flexibility required should be considered; some changes may benefit from establishing a predefined design space. Design space is defined in ICH Q8(R2) as "the multidimensional combination and interaction of input variables (e.g., material attributes) and process parameters that have been demonstrated to provide assurance of quality." Working within an established design space offers the option of implementing changes and handling the PAC process within the PQS, thereby providing increased flexibility for the implementation of changes.

The control strategy should be designed and implemented based on quality risk management, which is a valuable tool to reflect and evaluate the different steps, by linking the process to the established CPPs and understanding impacts and/or changes to CQAs. The control strategy should be reassessed as part of the PAC process.

5.1 Established Conditions (ECs) — 已建立條件

5.1.1 Background

5.1.1 Background

ICH Q12 defines ECs as legally binding information considered necessary to assure product quality. Consequently, any change to an EC necessitates a submission to the regulatory authority. ICH Q12, which the FDA officially adopted in May 2021, further considers ECs to be the elements of the pharmaceutical development activities that result in an appropriate control strategy.

Previously, FDA defined ECs as the description of the product, manufacturing process, facilities and equipment, and elements of the associated control strategy, as defined in an application, that assure process performance and quality of a product. Japan has a similar concept, called "approved matters," that refers to an item of information described in a dossier for an application that was approved by the regulatory agency. Health Canada also leverages a similar concept, requesting that market authorization holders file and maintain a Certified Product Information Document. That document constitutes part of the approval package and provides an accurate record of key quality information for the product proposed for marketing at the time the approval is issued. Thereafter, it serves as an official reference document during post-approval inspections and PAC evaluations performed by Health Canada.

Hence, the expectation of regulators to assess changes for the potential impact on product quality, safety, and efficacy has always existed. Changes that could potentially have a major impact on product quality usually have the highest reporting category.

As a term and a concept, EC may guide both the firm and the regulators in predefining the parameters that are more likely to impact the product. ICH Q10 describes the control strategy as "a planned set of controls, derived from current process and product understanding, that assures process performance and product quality," which may include CPPs and CQAs. Some elements of the control strategy may be managed in the applicant's PQS and not necessarily detailed in the dossier or application. Others may be detailed in the dossier but considered to be supportive information rather than ECs.

In the ICH Common Technical Document (CTD), ECs are already defined in Module 3: Quality. As per ICH Q12, all regulatory dossiers contain a combination of ECs and supportive information. Although identification of ECs with appropriate justifications might allow for increased flexibility in reporting PACs, explicitly identifying ECs for a given product is not necessarily mandatory. If the market authorization holder chooses to identify ECs, they should clearly identify the elements of Module 3 that they consider to be ECs and those they consider to be supportive information. The rationale for the ECs should remain in the appropriate CTD section. Maintaining a tabular summary of the ECs by section and providing those in the dossier, explicitly naming them as such, is recommended.

Another tool for defining and tracking ECs is the use of a PLCM strategy document; the PLCM document should be maintained in the firm's change management system and in CTD Module 3.2.R, as per ICH Q12.

In addition to explicitly identifying ECs, the firm should propose the type of filing that will be provided should a future change impact an EC. Once a submission is approved and the ECs are agreed upon by the firm and the regulatory agency, they are considered legally binding, and any changes to the EC must be reported to the regulatory agency. The reporting mechanism or filing type may vary, however, and the supporting data for a change may be part of the subsequent filing or may be maintained in the firm's PQS.

解說 Commentary

核心概念：EC 是什麼？

已建立條件（Established Conditions, ECs）是 ICH Q12 最重要的概念之一。簡單說：

EC = 法規機關認為必須維持、以確保產品品質的資訊。

一旦某個製程參數、設備描述、或控制策略元素被認定為 EC，對它的任何變更都必須向主管機關申報（submission required）。這就是「法律拘束力（legally binding）」的意義。

比喻：承重牆 vs. 室內裝潢

把你的藥品製程想成一棟建築：

  • EC = 承重牆：拆掉或移動需要結構工程師核准、申請建築許可，因為它直接關係到建築安全。
  • 非 EC = 室內裝潢：換個壁紙顏色、移動沙發位置，不需申請，自己處理就好。

這個分類決定了你面對的法規負擔。辨別清楚哪些是承重牆，才能在室內裝潢上自由發揮。

重點提示：各國 EC 的類似概念

EC 不是 ICH Q12 才有的新發明——各國法規其實早有類似概念：

  • 美國 FDA：自 2021 年正式採用 ICH Q12 的 EC 概念
  • 日本：稱為「approved matters（承認事項）」
  • 加拿大 Health Canada：要求維護 Certified Product Information Document

對 CDMO 而言，客戶產品可能需要在多個市場上市，每個市場的 EC 等效概念略有差異，必須在申報策略中一一考量。

做到這一點的好處：你和客戶、你和法規機關之間，對「哪些變更需要申報」有明確共識，減少日後爭議與來回溝通的時間成本。

5.1.2 Benefits of Defining Established Conditions

5.1.2 Benefits of Defining Established Conditions

ECs can be defined as part of the initial product development or through a review of product knowledge obtained over the product's lifecycle. They should be limited to the elements found essential to controlling the quality of a drug product based on scientific and risk-based justification. Careful evaluation of the product and manufacturing process will assist in focusing on the parameters critical to quality control, which will reduce the number of non-value-added PACs that require prior approval by regulatory agencies. ECs will depend on the complexity of the product and the capabilities inherent to the manufacturing process and manufacturing site. ECs may vary by dosage form and drug substance but, fundamentally, they should not vary by region or country. Regulatory convergence across jurisdictions or regulatory reliance under mutual agreement is required to fully realize the benefit.

Clearly identified and justified ECs in an application ensure that the firm and the regulatory agency are aligned on the criticality of a process parameter or quality attribute. As a result, there should be agreement on the necessity for subsequent submissions and the filing category for changes to any given EC. While a change to an EC will require a filing with a regulatory agency, the type of filing can depend on the risk assessment and the process and product knowledge available. The risk may be product- and/or process-dependent and, importantly, how the risk is assessed is dependent on the effectiveness of the PQS in place. Ultimately, the same type of change may require different filing strategies for different products.

This further highlights the importance of product and process knowledge and an effective PQS to fully realize the benefits of identifying ECs. For example, if the risk is considered low because the change will have little or no impact on product quality, the change may be made through a notification; if the risk is considered high due to a potentially high impact on product quality, prior approval of the change may be required. The documentation of these assessments and supporting data must live in the firm's PQS. When a prior approval supplement or variation is required, a PACMP may be leveraged to align the planned supportive studies and expected outcomes. This would lower the filing category and reduce the review scrutiny for the submission, which allows for implementation of the change more rapidly and efficiently.

ICH Quality Guideline Q12 Annexes: Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management illustrate how development approaches can impact product understanding and, ultimately, the identification of ECs.

In line with ICH Q10, if a firm can leverage the utility of a robust PQS system—one that incorporates KM and sound quality risk and change management strategies—the firm can gain increased flexibility to change ECs as more process and product knowledge is developed. A robust PQS will also enhance the firm's understanding of the product and its manufacturing process which, over time, will allow the firm to critically analyze ECs and reevaluate their potential impact on product quality. That critical evaluation will afford the firm the potential to remove ECs and negotiate lower filings for changes associated with existing ECs. This will then facilitate process and facility improvements.

In the current environment, firms and regulatory agencies frequently experience a lengthy submission and approval process. By predefining ECs and gaining regulatory agency agreement, firms can reduce the number of PACs that require regulatory agency submission or approval. The filing categories will be reduced for those changes that do require submission. This would both enable faster implementation of changes to the drug product and mitigate drug shortages related to lifecycle management. As they support continual improvement, PACs can result in decreased product variability, reduced supply shortages, and increased manufacturing efficiency, as well as facilitate the introduction of innovation.

5.1.3 ECs versus Non-ECs and Change Management

5.1.3 ECs versus Non-ECs and Change Management

All changes are managed through the firm's change management system, that is, within the PQS, as illustrated in ICH Q12. This guidance suggests that any parameter that needs to be controlled to ensure product quality (i.e., a CPP or process parameter where impact on quality cannot be reasonably excluded) should be considered an EC. ECs are intended to provide understanding and agreement between the firm and the regulatory agency on the parameters necessary to assure product quality; these parameters can change with knowledge gained throughout the lifecycle of the product.

The focus should be on understanding and identifying ECs and appropriately linking them to an agreed-upon post-marketing plan and regulatory oversight. As such, process parameters, which should be assigned based on criticality to the process and its control strategy, can be considered ECs and can be defined with regulatory agencies based on process and product knowledge. Yet, not all data and/or parameters described in a filing will necessarily be ECs; they may be detailed in the dossier but only as supportive information.

To define the changes that may require a regulatory agency filing, it is critical both to have explicitly defined ECs and to understand what elements are not. Ultimately, appropriately identifying ECs (and therefore non-ECs) can be a useful tool for harmonization in regions not participating in ICH when assessing the impact of PACs.

5.2 Post-Approval Change Management Protocols (PACMP)

5.2 Post-Approval Change Management Protocols

The PACMP tool described in ICH Q12 enables planning and implementation of changes to ECs and/or CPPs, and thereby to regulatory filings, in an efficient and structured manner. The concept of the PACMP is not new. The FDA and EMA have accepted comparability protocols for several years, and WHO guidances discuss the use of comparability protocols as a tool to help expedite the distribution of product post-change.

The PACMP (or comparability protocol) approved by the relevant health authority provides an agreement between the firm and the regulatory agency and describes changes with a level of detail complying to the complexity of the change, including risk assessment, control strategy, and other pertinent elements. Where possible, a description in the original protocol of the types of changes that might be considered allowable, and/or how they will be communicated to the agency post-approval, should be included. Once a PACMP is approved, when the proposed study design or acceptance criteria changes, an update of the protocol should be submitted for implementation as agreed upon in the original protocol. If changes are needed due to unanticipated or unforeseen changes in plans, prior approval of a new or revised PACMP by the regulatory agency may be required.

A firm's processes may include internal guidance for filing initial market registrations, including templates for PACMPs. In general, PACMPs can include a description of the product's current state, the change to be made, the ECs and/or the product quality attributes that will be affected, the defined testing and acceptance criteria to establish comparability of the changed product, and the proposed category for filing the change.

Typically, a PACMP describes a single change or a group of related changes that may be made at the same time, for example, a scale-up of a purification process that involves larger equipment and associated changes to process parameters. In addition, PACMPs can be used to describe the same change or changes being implemented across multiple products, such as a new drug product container–closure system being implemented across a portfolio of products. In this case, the same PACMP could be filed for multiple applications, and subsequent submissions to provide the final data could be filed for each individual application.

Required Elements of a PACMP

Contents for PACMPs may vary from one firm to another and from one type of change to another, but as a best practice, all PACMPs should contain the following elements:

  • Description of the current state and the proposed change as well as a justification or scientific rationale for the change
  • Proposed submission category or submission type for the subsequent filing that includes the data, clearly indicating which sections of the registration will be impacted
  • Detailed list of change(s)
  • Risk assessment (i.e., risk-based approach) to explain the potential impact of changes to product quality
  • Study protocol describing how the potential impact(s) would be assessed to adequately demonstrate comparability of the product quality before and after the change
  • List of predefined acceptance criteria that must be met before implementing the change
  • Validation or qualification of the changed method/process/equipment to demonstrate that all acceptance criteria are met, as required, based on the risk level of change to the product quality
  • Additional process characterization and validation studies at small-scale, as required, based on the specific change or, minimally, an assessment to confirm that the existing process characterization and validation studies adequately support the new process conditions
  • List of post-change commitments, if any, including any stability studies to demonstrate accelerated and/or long-term stability of the product with the proposed change
  • Anticipated timeline for implementation of the change
  • Description of how the approved PACMP will be modified, if needed, due to additional knowledge gained during the actual execution

PACMPs may only be accepted by a limited number of regulatory agencies; however, harmonization of these best practices across ICH regions and some non-ICH regions would greatly improve the potential for using these tools to facilitate the efficient implementation of PACs.

PACMPs may save firms considerable time and resources to effectively manage changes and provide regulators greater insight into planned changes, which would facilitate the review of post-change filings. This could alleviate constraints in the global supply chain, enabling a continuing supply of drug products to patients in a timely manner. Global acceptance by regulators, with a defined mechanism in local jurisdictions for submission of PACMPs, is needed to fully realize the benefits that PACMPs can offer.

解說 Commentary

核心概念：PACMP 是什麼？

上市後變更管理規程（PACMP）是一份預先與法規機關商定的變更計畫書。它的運作邏輯是：

  1. 你告訴法規機關：「我未來可能要做 X 變更，我計畫用這些研究、這些驗收標準來驗證。」
  2. 法規機關審查後核准：「可以，只要你的數據符合這些預定標準，變更就生效，不需要再來問我。」
  3. 變更執行後，你只需提交結果報告（notification / report），而非重新申請核准。

這就是從「事前核准（prior approval）」降級為「事後通知（notification）」的機制。

比喻：建築裝修的「預先核可施工藍圖」

一般裝修：你每改一個設計，都要重新送審，來回數個月。

有了預核施工藍圖（PACMP）：你一開始就和建管局說「我可能做 A、B、C 三種修改，每種修改的工法和驗收標準如下……」建管局預先審查核可後，後續只要按圖施工、最後驗收報告即可。

PACMP 就是你和法規機關之間的「預核施工藍圖」。

重點提示：PACMP vs. 比較性規程（Comparability Protocol）

美國 FDA 和歐盟 EMA 使用「比較性規程（Comparability Protocol）」這個術語多年，ICH Q12 的 PACMP 概念在本質上是相同的，只是在全球層面的統一用語。

核心精神一致：雙方事先就「做什麼研究、用什麼標準」達成共識，用數據說話，而非每次都重新談判。

但需注意：目前並非所有國家都接受 PACMP。在非 ICH 地區（如部分亞太市場），仍可能需要傳統的完整申報流程。

CDMO 應用：跨產品組合的 PACMP

TR91 特別提到一個對 CDMO 非常有價值的用法：一份 PACMP 可以涵蓋多個產品的相同變更。

例如：如果你要為客戶組合中的 5 個產品更換同一款容器封蓋系統（CCS），可以提交一份共通的 PACMP，描述通用的評估方法和驗收標準，再分別為每個產品提交最終數據報告。

這大幅降低了每個產品的個別申報負擔，是 CDMO 規模效益的體現。

PACMP 必要元素速記

用「現改驗準計後程」七字口訣記憶：

  • 現：當前狀態描述
  • 改：擬變更內容清單 + 科學理由
  • 驗：驗證/確效計畫（方法、設備、製程）
  • 準：預定驗收標準（可比性標準）
  • 計：研究方案（如何評估可比性）
  • 後：上市後承諾（如穩定性研究）
  • 程：實施時程與申報類別

5.3 Product Lifecycle Management (PLCM)

5.3 Product Lifecycle Management

The PLCM can be described in a strategy document that describes how the firm will:

  • Monitor the process and product attributes to ensure that both remain within the validated state in accordance with the product control strategy (e.g., continued process verification)
  • Manage and report changes to ECs
  • Execute PAC filings that are planned as part of the product lifecycle

A PLCM strategy document may include the following sections:

  • Summary of the product control strategy, e.g., a description of the CQAs for drug substance and drug product and the controls in place for raw materials, excipients, process steps, and final drug substance and drug product that ensure CQAs are maintained within acceptable ranges
  • A table describing the CTD sections that contain ECs, the ECs in each of these sections, and the reporting categories that will be used if those ECs change
  • PACMPs that may be used for future changes
  • Post-approval commitments from the initial registration or subsequent supplements and the timelines for completion

A PLCM strategy document may provide an effective tool to help manage the commercial lifecycle of a product. It can be filed with the initial registration, or it may be filed as a post-approval variation. The document would also prove helpful in the firm's records as a repository of the commitments to the regulatory agency that must be maintained throughout the commercial lifecycle. The PLCM strategy document can also be used to communicate to the regulatory agency the elements of the registration that will be maintained during the commercial lifecycle, as well as the intended reporting categories and timing estimates.

As post-approval variations are submitted, an updated PLCM should be included that describes the updated ECs, control strategy, reference to PACMPs, post-approval CMC commitments, and any other pertinent information about the change.

核心概念：PLCM 策略文件是什麼？

產品生命週期管理（PLCM）策略文件是一份活的文件（living document），它把一個產品從上市到退市的整個旅程連結成一個連續的故事：

  • 哪些 EC 需要監控？
  • 哪些變更計畫在未來 3–5 年執行？
  • 已承諾給法規機關但尚未完成的研究有哪些？
  • 每次申報後，這些答案如何更新？

PLCM 文件可以在初次上市申請時一起提交，也可以在上市後以 post-approval variation 形式提交。

比喻：PLCM 就像藥品的「人生規劃書」

每個人都有一份隱形的「人生規劃」——當前狀況、未來計畫、已承諾的義務（如房貸、教育金）、以及隨時間更新的目標。

PLCM 策略文件就是你的藥品產品的「人生規劃書」：

  • 當前狀況 = 控制策略摘要、EC 清單
  • 未來計畫 = 預定的 PACMP、製程改善藍圖
  • 已承諾義務 = Post-approval commitments（穩定性研究、驗證報告等）
  • 隨時間更新 = 每次申報後更新 PLCM，反映最新知識與 EC 狀態

重點提示：上市前 → 上市後的知識連續體

TR91 這一節的深層訊息是：上市前開發（Phase 2/3 設計空間定義）與上市後管理不應該是斷裂的兩個世界。

  • 上市前：設計空間、EC 識別、PACMP 草稿
  • 上市時：PLCM 文件隨 dossier 提交
  • 上市後：每次 PAC 申報後更新 PLCM，累積知識→精煉 EC→降低申報層級

這個循環就是 ICH Q8/Q9/Q10/Q12 整合精神的實踐。

  • 更高的法規可信度：法規機關見到有完整 PLCM 文件的申請，對 MAH 和製造商的信任感更高
  • 更快的變更執行速度：PLCM 中預先規劃的 PACMP 可以在需要時快速啟動，縮短 time-to-market

6.0 Global Lifecycle and PAC Management

6.1 ICH Regions

6.1 ICH Regions

The impact of ICH in harmonizing guidelines and aligning ICH member regions has provided significant advantages in both the PLCM and the management of PACs. The continuing challenge is the wide diversity of expectations of non-ICH regions in both the content and timing of PAC submission approvals. Some common characteristics leveraged in ICH regions that enable efficient PACs include:

  • Clear guidance for filing changes, including reporting categories based on risk
  • Acceptance of science- and risk-based approaches for developing data to support PACs, such as bracketing and/or matrixing of process validation and stability batches
  • Potential acceptance of limited stability data when justified, such as three or six months of real-time data and three months of accelerated data, to support major changes, and less or no stability data for minor changes
  • Well-defined timelines for review and approval of PACs, in the range of six months for a major change and shorter periods for minor to moderate changes
  • Relatively fewer requests for additional documentation not included in CTD Module 3, such as declarations or GMP certificates, although the latter may be needed for addition of a new facility

核心概念：ICH 成員區域的共同優勢

ICH（國際人用藥品技術要求協調委員會）成員目前涵蓋美國（FDA）、歐盟（EMA）、日本（PMDA）、加拿大（Health Canada）及瑞士（Swissmedic）等主要市場。

ICH 成員就像承認國際駕照的國家——你在一個地方考過了，其他地方認可你的資格、審查你的資料時也用同樣的標準。非 ICH 地區則要求你重新從頭考試，且規則各地不同。

重點提示：CDMO 的多地區申報挑戰

即使在 ICH 成員區域之間，同一個變更的申報類別仍可能不同：美國是 PAS（Prior Approval Supplement）、歐盟是 Type II、加拿大是 SNDS（Supplement to New Drug Submission）。CDMO 必須同步管理這些並行申報，時程、資料要求、審查回應各有差異。

6.2 Non-ICH Regions

6.2 Non-ICH Regions

Regions not participating in ICH frequently observe fewer of the characteristics that enable PACs to be managed consistently across multiple regulatory authorities. Examples seen in some non-ICH regions include:

  • Lack of available post-approval guidance
  • No or very limited provision for a differentiation in filing level, such as allowing for providing notifications rather than submissions for lower-risk changes or recognizing risk-based scientifically valid strategies, such as bracketing or matrixing of process validation and stability data
  • Requirements for extensive amounts of drug product stability data to support even minor changes or drug substance changes, for example, up to the full shelf life
  • Requirements for extensive ancillary documentation to support PACs, such as declarations, certificates of various types, and legalizations

In 2020, PDA completed a survey of industry on this topic. According to survey results, half of the respondents indicated that the authorities that take the most time to approve prior-approval supplements require more than a year to grant such approval. Some markets were reported as having extremely long review times for changes, for example, five years. Some markets add extra requirements, such as implementing product changes immediately upon that country's approval, which is not always feasible given the manufacturing schedule or lag in approval by other jurisdictions. The survey also indicated some jurisdictions may not allow for the simultaneous review of multiple changes, instead requiring each change to be submitted for agency review individually, in sequence (26). These types of requirements make it extremely difficult to file global PACs, which can make it very challenging to initiate or even propose process improvements. In some cases, this can impact product supply.

When considering the supply chain, extended approval times for products with limited expiry dating can create significant challenges, as it may not be possible to build up sufficient inventory levels. For example, a process or facility change that requires modifications that, when made, no longer allows for the product to be manufactured using the original process. Once the change is made, the inventory of product using the original process is set and, if approval takes an extended time (in some cases years), the chance the inventory will be depleted or will expire before the change is fully approved increases significantly, impacting product availability and affecting the patient.

The WHO guidances on biotherapeutics and vaccines provide good examples of changes and apply a risk-based approach to PACs, similar in format to the Health Canada Post-Notice of Compliance (NOC) Changes: Quality Document (20). The WHO publications are intended to serve as a guide for establishing national requirements for the regulation of PACs to biotherapeutic products and vaccines. In jurisdictions that do not offer their own guidance, these guidelines can be helpful in applying a risk-based approach to the PAC requirements and approval timing. In these cases, the guidance(s) should be referenced during the PAC process. Also, the WHO PAC guidelines strongly encourage national regulatory authorities to establish requirements commensurate with their own regulatory capacity, experience, and resources and to work toward minimizing any negative impact on access to approved products (4,5). National regulatory authorities are encouraged to consider establishing procedures for the expedited approval of changes based on previous expert review and approval of the same changes by the national authorities of the other countries where those products are licensed or, alternatively, based on the decision of a recognized regional regulatory agency.

Where there is a potential for the timing of change approval in non-ICH regions to impact product supply, it is highly recommended that the firm contact those regulatory agencies early to alert them to the potential issues and request their assistance.

7.0 Legacy Marketed Products and ICH Q12

7.0 Legacy Marketed Products and ICH Q12

One of the challenges with the changing global regulatory landscape relates to marketed products, that is, products that have been approved and are in distribution. The full benefits of the ICH Q12 PAC processes may initially only apply to products that are newly submitted in jurisdictions that apply ICH guidelines. Consequently, for many older products already on the market, there may be a prolonged period to implement ICH Q12 PAC principles, which may be impacted by the inclusion of specific information in the approved filing. For example, identifying which items should be called out as ECs could present a challenge and could require considerable time and resources to accomplish.

To take full advantage of ICH Q12, some type of modernization of the regulatory filing around the CMC sections may be needed.

Regardless of how they embrace ICH Q12 concepts, in ICH regions, firms will have the option to develop and submit a list of ECs to regulatory agencies in a prior-approval submission. This would allow the firm to conduct a full or partial assessment and possibly remove parameters currently in the filing that, based on product knowledge, do not require a submission to change. This could reduce the filing category of some of the parameters, for example, moving from prior approval to notification. The firm could also use a prior-approval submission to change from a parameter-based EC to performance-based conditions. This could be done for the entirety of Modules 3.2.S and 3.2.P or only for specific sections.

The potential advantage to adding ECs to a marketed product is that more historical data and a better understanding of the product exists for marketed products than would have been available when the initial marketing authorization was submitted. That product history and knowledge can be used to demonstrate that the changes made to certain parameters and quality attributes are low risk and, therefore, should be allowed notification-only status or could be managed solely within the PQS. To reduce the regulatory burden of PACs, the historic manufacturing and characterization data would have to be submitted to justify the risk assessment scientifically. This data should be submitted in the development sections of the filing (e.g., Modules 3.2.S.2.6 and 3.2.P.2) to support the proposed ECs and reduced filing category. If these data are submitted in sufficient detail, once the ECs are approved, future PACs could be implemented more quickly.

In most cases, prospectively filing a prior-approval submission to add ECs would not be efficient unless it is submitted along with a planned major change. Otherwise, the ECs would have to be approved before a change could be made without needing a submission, which would further increase the regulatory burden. Additionally, regulatory agencies in different jurisdictions may not agree to the same ECs, which would add to the complexity of the maintenance of ECs by the firm.

新建案可以一開始就符合最新建築法規；但一棟 1980 年代的老建築若想享有新規範的某些彈性（如免申報小幅改建），必須先補做結構安全評估、更新竣工圖——這就是 Legacy Product 導入 EC 的過程。工程浩大，但一旦完成，未來的改裝就輕鬆多了。

重點提示：什麼時候值得補做 EC？

TR91 明確指出：單獨為了加 EC 而提交先前核准申請，通常效益不高。最佳時機是「搭便車」——當你已經要提交一個重大變更（Major Change）的 PAC 時，順便在同一份申請中加入 EC 清單。這樣既能利用現有審查程序，又能立即享受 EC 帶來的未來申報簡化。

Parameter-Based vs. Performance-Based EC

Parameter-based EC（參數型）：直接在 filing 中列出具體製程參數值（如攪拌速度 X rpm）——改了就要申報。

Performance-based EC（效能型）：改以產品效能或功能性指標為 EC（如「最終產品無菌性符合 USP <71>」）——製程參數調整只要效能達標，可在 PQS 內管理，無需申報。後者是 ICH Q12 的理想狀態。

你的廠區承接一個已上市 8 年的生物製劑客戶產品。客戶希望利用這次製程優化的機會，同步導入 ICH Q12 EC 框架。你如何協助客戶評估哪些 CTD 章節需要補充歷史數據？這項工作應納入哪個合約里程碑？

8.0 Recommendations for Harmonization (Categorization of Changes)

8.0 Recommendations for Harmonization

In the current regulatory environment, industry benchmarking shows that it can take up to five years to have a single change approved across all affected countries. Current guidances from regulatory agencies, including the FDA, EMA, WHO, and Health Canada, attempt to clarify how changes are to be evaluated, when they should be reported, and if prior approval from the agency is required to implement the change (4, 5, 11, 13, 20). Based on the severity of the effect of the change to the ECs or product quality and, hence, to the safety and efficacy for patients, the changes are generally classified in three categories:

  • Do and Record (Lowest Risk) — manage within the PQS (e.g., Health Canada Level IV)
  • Do and Tell (Minor to Moderate) — notification (e.g., Type IA or Annual Reportable)
    — Tell, Wait and Do (some Moderate) — (e.g., Type IB or CBE-30)
  • Tell, Wait and Do (Major) — prior approval (e.g., Type II or Prior Approval Supplement)

The EU's variation regulations and post-authorization guidance and FDA's scale-up and post-approval change (SUPAC) guidance documents provide specific details on the scientific data that should be submitted and how it should be presented for specific PACs (27–31). The requirements are consistent with the potential impact of the change on the quality, safety, and efficacy of the product similar to the underlying concepts adopted by the FDA in its Guidance for Industry: Changes to an Approved NDA or ANDA (32).

Specific scientific documentation requirements and approval timelines can vary greatly among jurisdictions, however.

To allow the industry to continue to modernize and make the needed improvements in both quality and productivity in a timely fashion, action is needed in many of the non-ICH regions. Establishing new regulations or modifying existing regulations to introduce the three mechanisms for change—PQS management, notification, and prior approval—that align with ICH Q12 and other global regulatory expectations is recommended. To fully realize the benefits of ICH Q12, a certain level of regulatory convergence and harmonization is needed.

8.2 Mutual Recognition and Consistent Review Times of PACs in ICH Regions

Most often, product applications are filed across multiple regions. 11.1 Background — 背景說明

11.1 Background

Changes to adopt new and improved technologies for test methods can pose a challenge to the supply chain as this type of change typically requires regulatory agency filings and, sometimes, prior approval.

The use of testing methods to detect adventitious agents in biologics manufacturing is a necessity and is required by regulators globally. During the manufacturing process for biologics, manufacturers may use eukaryotic expression systems, which can be prone to microbial contamination. Consequently, these processes require strict microbial control strategies, including testing for such adventitious agents as mycoplasma, Leptospira, and viruses.

Current adventitious agent test methods, however, pose numerous limitations. Assays based on polymerase chain reactions (PCR) offer the potential to mitigate these limitations and offer even better overall detection of adventitious agents. Biologics manufacturers are actively exploring this area, and current research indicates that PCR-based testing is not only scientifically acceptable, but also accepted by regulators.

PCR-based test methods for Mycoplasma sp. present certain advantages for microbiological control — better sensitivity, faster turnaround of results, reduced sample handling, and fewer test failure issues (e.g., due to sample matrix effects) — and timely batch release for biologic drug substances.

核心概念解析

什麼是「Adventitious Agents」？

字面意思是「外來的偶發性微生物」——即在製造過程中不應存在、但可能意外污染的微生物，包括黴漿菌（Mycoplasma）、螺旋體（Leptospira）及各類病毒。生物製劑因為使用活細胞表達系統（如 CHO 細胞），特別容易受到這些污染物的威脅。

比喻：換一台更靈敏的火災警報器

想像原本工廠用的是傳統煙霧感測器（需要28天才能確認「沒有火災」），現在要換成紅外線熱感儀（5-8小時就能確認）。警報器更準、更快，但因為原始監管核准的是舊型號，換掉就必須去申報——這就是 PAC 的核心邏輯。

科學上更好，監管上卻必須走完變更程序。這是生技製藥監管的典型張力。

為什麼這對 CDMO 特別重要？

批次放行時間是 CDMO 的核心競爭力之一。傳統方法需等待 14-28 天才能確認無黴漿菌污染，期間產品無法出貨。PCR 方法縮短至 5-8 小時，可以讓批次放行提前 2 週以上。這直接影響庫存週轉、倉儲成本和客戶滿意度。

思考練習

11.1.2 Present State of Testing — 現行測試方法現況

11.1.2 Present State of Testing

Current detection methods for traditional adventitious agents require the following:

  • Significant manual handling of the specimens
  • Test period of up to 28 days for results, during which time Mycoplasma, Leptospira, or viruses could contaminate the downstream processing equipment, resulting in a possible supply disruption
  • Well-trained analysts with a high level of expertise to interpret results
  • Specialized equipment and laboratories

Additional drawbacks of traditional cell- and growth-based detection methods for adventitious agents are:

  • Some cytotoxic NMEs interfere with in vitro cell culture assays
  • Low sensitivity due to dilution needs (see Table 11.1.2-1🔍)
  • Limited specificity
  • Different assay platforms
  • Complex sample workflow
  • Safety, health, and environmental issues
  • Risk of secondary contamination, as some adventitious agents require the use of viable positive controls

Increasing concern has been raised in recent years regarding the sensitivity and/or specificity of traditional detection assays for adventitious agents. Validation studies have demonstrated the superiority of PCR-based rodent parvovirus detection assays over the traditional 324K-cell-based detection assays.

NME 的特殊挑戰：藥物自己干擾測試

這是一個非常現實的問題：某些新型抗癌藥（NMEs）本身具有細胞毒性，會殺死用來做指示細胞培養測試的細胞株。為了避免藥物殺死指示細胞，樣品必須稀釋到 1:10,000，靈敏度因此大幅降低。

這等於是說：越先進的藥物，傳統測試法越難用。PCR 不依賴活細胞，完全迴避這個問題。未來生物製劑管線中越來越多細胞毒性 NMEs，這個問題只會越來越嚴重。

FDA 已明確建議 PCR 偵測螺旋體

FDA 在 cGMP Q&A 文件中明確建議使用「validated PCR methods」偵測 Leptospira（螺旋體細菌）。這是監管機關主動背書 PCR 方法的公開記錄，是申報文件中很重要的引用依據。

記住：監管機關的「建議」通常是未來「要求」的前兆。先行部署 PCR 方法，等於在監管趨勢前面一步。

美國 FDA：

  • PAS（Prior Approval Supplement）：需要等待 FDA 核准後才能實施。最嚴格，但適用於重大方法變更。

比喻：「先問准再動工」vs.「動工後告知」

PAS 就像申請建築許可——蓋房子前必須等政府核准，可能等幾個月到一年。CBE-30 就像「告知鄰居後就動工」——先通知，30 天後開始，但鄰居（FDA）可能事後提出抗議。PACMP 則是「先去跟都發局談好建築標準規格，照著蓋，蓋完只需要驗收通知」。

PACMP 的策略優勢：「先談好裁判標準」

PACMP（Post-Approval Change Management Protocol） 是這份 TR 的核心策略工具。概念是：在執行驗證研究之前，先與監管機關協商好「什麼樣的數據算通過」，一旦驗收標準達成，後續只需提交通知（notification），不需重新等待批准。

這對 CDMO 最大的意義是：可以為多個客戶統一建立一套 PCR 驗證框架，只要個別產品的 product-specific validation 通過，客戶 MAH 就可以快速提交 post-change notification，不用每次重走完整審查流程。

CDMO 特別注意：誰要提交申請？

這是 CDMO 與直接商業化藥廠最大的不同點。

CDMO 本身不持有 Marketing Authorization（MA）。客戶 MAH 才是申請人。這意味著：即使 CDMO 已完成所有 PCR 驗證工作，仍需要每個客戶 MAH 各自向其所在市場的監管機關提交 PAC 申請。

CDMO 的角色是：提供技術數據支持、協調驗證時程、幫客戶準備申報文件——但最終簽名遞交的是客戶。

11.3 Comparability Protocol Strategy Overview — 可比較性協議策略概覽

11.3 Comparability Protocol Strategy Overview for the Replacement of the Traditional Mycoplasma Detection Methods with a PCR-based Method

According to Ph. Eur. Mycoplasma 2.6.7 and Ph. Eur. Nucleic Acid Amplification Techniques 2.6.21, the following parameters should be evaluated for a qualitative assay by the validation program:

  • Specificity — ability to unequivocally assess target nucleic acid in the presence of other (potentially interfering) components
  • Detection Limit/Sensitivity — lowest amount of target nucleic acid in a sample that can be detected
  • Robustness — measure of its capacity to remain unaffected by small, but deliberate, variations in method parameters
  • Comparability Study — comparison of the detection limits of the alternative method and official methods; specificity should also be considered

The validation program could be split into two sections:

  • Generic Method Validation — evaluation of all the parameters listed above
  • Product-Specific Method Validation — use of different lots of the specimen to be tested with a limited number of sensitivity experiments to evaluate whether the PCR-based detection method is unaffected by the specific sample matrix or lot-to-lot variability

The experimental data generated during the generic method validation program, as well as the data of the product-specific method validation(s) method suitability test(s), would be included in the submission package.

驗證策略的兩層架構

Generic vs. Product-Specific：平台化思維

Generic Method Validation（通用方法驗證）：這是針對 PCR 平台本身的驗證，不針對特定產品。驗證的是：這個 PCR 方法是否能可靠地偵測各類黴漿菌菌株？做一次，對所有使用該平台的產品都有效。

Product-Specific Method Validation（產品特定驗證）：這是針對特定產品基質（product matrix）的驗證。主要問題是：你的產品（例如特定單抗）會不會抑制或干擾 PCR 反應？每個產品都要做，但工作量比完整驗證小得多。

這個架構對 CDMO 非常有利：通用驗證只需做一次，後續每個新客戶產品只需補充 product-specific 部分。

比喻：通用廚師培訓 vs. 菜系適應

Generic Validation 就像培訓一位廚師掌握所有基本刀法、火候和食材處理——通用技能，一次培訓終身有效。Product-Specific Validation 就像讓這位廚師試做每位顧客的私房食譜，確認沒有特殊食材衝突。

四個驗證參數的實務意義

  • Specificity（特異性）：PCR 引子（primer）設計可能同時擴增非黴漿菌的 DNA，必須記錄這些「交叉偵測」並提出因應策略（如二次確認測試）。
  • Sensitivity/Detection Limit（靈敏度/偵測下限）：必須達到 ≤10 CFU/mL，這是法規要求的最低門檻。
  • Robustness（耐用性）：換批次試劑、換操作員、換儀器，結果是否一致？這是日常品質保證的核心。
  • Comparability Study（可比較性研究）：新方法 vs. 現行 compendial 法的並行比對，是取得監管認可的關鍵數據。

11.3.1 Acceptance Criteria — 驗收標準

11.3.1 Conditions that Need to be Met Prior to Implementation of the Change: Acceptance Criteria for the PCR-Based Mycoplasma Detection Method

11.3.1.1 Specificity

The ability of the PCR-based mycoplasma detection method to detect a large panel of mycoplasma species should be demonstrated using characterized reference panels. As the primers will likely detect other bacterial species, the potential cross-detection should be documented in the validation study.

Based on the results of this validation of specificity, if a gap in the specificity of the method is identified (such as detection of nonmycoplasmal bacteria species), an appropriate strategy must be proposed — for example, a second test may be performed using an alternative method without this specificity gap or by using an official method.

11.3.1.2 Detection Limit/Sensitivity

A positive cut-off point should be determined for the PCR-based detection method. The positive cut-off point, as defined in Ph. Eur. 2.6.21, is the minimum number of target sequence copies per volume of sample that can be detected in 95% of test runs.

Acceptance criteria for the detection limit/sensitivity are defined as follows:

  • If the alternative method is proposed to replace the culture method, the PCR-based mycoplasma detection system must be shown to detect 10 CFU/mL for each relevant mycoplasma test species chosen.
  • If the alternative method is proposed to replace the indicator-cell culture method, the NAT system must be shown to detect 100 CFU/mL for each mycoplasma test used.

11.3.1.3 Robustness

To determine whether the assay remains unaffected by deliberate variations caused by different lots of reagents and solutions, three lots of selected critical PCR assay ingredients are used.

Using any of these combinations, the PCR-based mycoplasma detection assay must be able to detect at least 10 CFU/mL of any of the mycoplasma reference preparations (e.g., M. orale).

Both thermocycler models will be included in the robustness study. The detection limit determined for all three test runs must be comparable (difference between the test runs of not more than 1 log step).

11.3.1.4 Comparability Study

In the comparability study, the PCR-based alternative method should be performed in parallel with the official methods to evaluate simultaneously the detection limit/sensitivity of both methods using CHO cultures inoculated with different spike levels of mycoplasma reference strains.

The detection limit/sensitivity, as well as the specificity of the PCR-based mycoplasma detection method, should be comparable or superior to the traditional mycoplasma detection methods.

11.3.1.5 Acceptance Criteria for Product-Specific Method Validation

To evaluate whether the test specimen possesses inhibitory or interfering properties on the PCR-based mycoplasma detection method, the parameter detection limit/sensitivity should be examined with three different lots of the test specimen (method suitability test).

The detection limit/sensitivity of the PCR-based assay determined in the generic method validation must be confirmed using a defined set of mycoplasma species for different lots of the test specimen.

驗收標準解讀

「10 CFU/mL」是法規紅線，不是建議值

Ph. Eur. 2.6.21 明確規定，替代方法替換培養法時，每個測試菌株都必須達到 ≤10 CFU/mL 的偵測下限。這個數字不是目標，是最低要求。

如果替換的是指示細胞法，則門檻放寬到 ≤100 CFU/mL，因為指示細胞法本身的靈敏度就比培養法低。

實務上，設計驗證研究時，應以 10 CFU/mL 為目標，留有安全邊際。

「95% cutoff」的統計意義

Cut-off point 定義為：在 24 次重複測試中，必須有至少 23 次（≥95%）能偵測到目標菌。這是一個統計意義上的「可靠偵測」標準，而非要求 100% 完美。

設計時要注意：三批次（三天）× 8 次重複 = 24 次總測試。少於 23 次陽性 = 不符合 95% 門檻。

比喻：耐用性測試就像「換班長」實驗

Robustness 驗證就是故意製造「真實世界的變異」來看方法是否撐得住：換一批試劑、換一台熱循環機、換一個操作員。如果換了以上任何一個條件，結果的偵測下限差距超過 1 個對數值（1 log step），代表方法太脆弱，不適合日常生產環境。

Specificity Gap 的因應策略

PCR 引子設計上，可能會對非黴漿菌的革蘭氏陽性菌（如 Streptococcus、Lactobacillus）產生交叉反應。這不一定是問題，只要：

  1. 在驗證研究中記錄哪些菌種會交叉偵測；
  2. 提出「陽性結果判讀策略」——例如，若 PCR 陽性，進行二次確認（sequencing 或另一種方法）。

監管機關不要求 PCR 方法完美無缺，但要求你誠實記錄限制並有合理的因應程序。

思考練習

Product-Specific Validation 需要用三個不同批次的產品樣品做 method suitability test。若你的廠正在驗證一個新客戶的單抗產品，但目前只有一個商業批次可用，另外兩批還在生產中，你要如何安排驗證時程？這個限制是否會影響申報時間表？

Example: Post-Approval Change Management Protocol for Change in Analytical Method

PURPOSE

The intent of this validation is to demonstrate that the mycoplasma PCR assay is suitable for the detection of mycoplasma species in preharvest cell-culture fluid (PHCCF) samples. It fulfills the requirements given in Ph. Eur. 2.6.7 for an alternative NAT-based method proposed as an alternative to the indicator-cell culture and the culture methods.

The presence of mycoplasma DNA in the sample is determined by a 400-500 bp target sequence that is amplified; this amplicon is detected finally by polyacrylamide gel electrophoresis or by real-time PCR (qPCR).

This protocol provides guidance on how to validate the PCR-based mycoplasma detection method for:

  • Generic validation of the mycoplasma PCR assay (per Ph. Eur. 2.6.21 and ICH Q2(R1) for a qualitative test)
  • Comparability study between the mycoplasma PCR assay and the two compendial mycoplasma detection methods
  • Study on inhibiting substances of the PHCCF

BACKGROUND — Test Microorganisms

  • M. hominis ATCC 23114
  • M. orale ATCC 23714
  • M. arginini ATCC 23838
  • Acholeplasma laidlawii (EDQM reference strain)
  • M. hyorhinis (EDQM reference strain)
  • M. hyorhinis ATCC 29052 (fastidious strain, used in comparability study)
  • M. pneumoniae NCTC 10119
  • M. salivarium ATCC 23064
  • M. fermentans ATCC 19989
  • Spiroplasma citri ATCC 27556

Nonmycoplasmal Strains (for specificity experiments):

  • Streptococcus bovis
  • Lactobacillus acidophilus
  • Clostridium sporogenes

CHO Cells: CHOK1 cell line (DSMZ No.: ACC 110) used for generic method validation and test positive controls (GAPDH PCR). PHCCF samples of three different fermentation runs evaluated in the study on PCR-inhibiting or PCR-interfering substances.

DESCRIPTION OF PLANNED CHANGES — Generic Validation

Specificity: The specificity of the assay will be tested with a large panel of the mycoplasma strains, as well as with other bacterial species that might be cross-detected. CHO cells will be spiked with mycoplasma strains. In addition, CHO cells will be spiked with a serial dilution of Gram-positive bacteria to concentrations of 106–102 CFU/mL.

Acceptance Criteria: An amplicon of 400-500 bp must be detectable for all mycoplasma strains tested. No acceptance criterion is defined for the cross-detection of Gram-positive bacteria.

Sensitivity/Detection Limit: To determine the detection limit for each mycoplasma strain, three independent 10-fold dilution series will be tested. The five strains M. hyorhinis, M. orale, M. arginini, M. fermentans, and A. laidlawii account for more than 95% of all isolates found in mammalian cell cultures.

In each dilution series, CHO cells will be spiked with 10, 5, 1, 0.5, and 0.1 CFU/mL. The spiked CHO cells will be analyzed as samples in the mycoplasma PCR assay. Three independent runs (dilution series) will be prepared on different days with eight test replicates for each dilution, giving a total number of 24 test results.

Acceptance Criteria: For each of the strains tested for the detection limit, a band of 400-500 bp must be present in no fewer than 23 of 24 test results (= 95% cutoff) up to a concentration of at least 10 CFU/mL.

Robustness: CHO cells will be spiked with M. orale master stock at concentrations of 1000, 100, 10, 1, 0.1, and 0.01 CFU/mL. Tests will be carried out independently by one analyst on three different days. Two lots of the commercial kit and two thermocycler models will be used.

Acceptance Criteria: A band of 400–500 bp must be present up to a concentration of 10 CFU/mL in all three test runs. The detection limit determined for all three test runs must be comparable (difference of not more than 1 log step).

Comparability Study

15 mL of CHO-K1 culture will be spiked with 10 mycoplasma strain stocks to a concentration of 100 CFU/mL. Based on this initially spiked sample, a serial 10-fold dilution will be prepared (100, 10, 1, 0.1, 0.01, and 0.001 CFU/mL) in triplicate. These samples will be subjected to all three different mycoplasma detection methods in parallel without freezing:

  • 10 mL — culture method
  • 1 mL — indicator-cell culture method
  • 900 µL — mycoplasma PCR

Criterion: The detection limit of the three mycoplasma detection methods should be comparable.

Study on PCR-Inhibiting or PCR-Interfering Substances of PHCCF

PHCCF samples of three different fermentation runs will be spiked with M. hyorhinis and A. laidlawii to titers close to the detection limit. Three independent dilution series will be prepared with eight test replicates for each dilution (24 test results total).

Acceptance Criteria:

  • A band of 400-500 bp must be present in 23 of 24 test results up to a concentration of at least 10 CFU/mL for the detection of M. hyorhinis
  • A band of 400-500 bp must be present in 23 of 24 test results up to a concentration of at least 0.1 CFU/mL for the detection of A. laidlawii

COMMITMENT AND IMPLEMENTATION PLAN

The firm's PQS will be followed to implement the change, and the relevant regulatory authorities will be notified. Post-change monitoring will occur to ensure no unintended consequences result; all documentation relevant to the change will be available for review by regulatory authorities during inspection.

The firm will outline the global implementation plan for this change (e.g., timing of the introduction).

PACMP 範例解讀：一份真實的驗證藍圖

這份 PACMP 的結構邏輯

這份範例 PACMP 就是你遞交給監管機關「先談驗收標準」的那份文件。它的核心是在實際執行驗證之前，就明確列出：

  1. 要驗什麼（Purpose：偵測 PHCCF 中的黴漿菌）
  2. 用什麼材料驗（Test Microorganisms：10 種菌株 + 3 種非黴漿菌）
  3. 怎麼設計實驗（Planned Changes：三批次 × 8 重複 × 5 稀釋度）
  4. 怎樣算過關（Acceptance Criteria：24 次中至少 23 次正確 = 95%）

一旦監管機關認可這份 PACMP，後續只需要提交「數據已達標」的通知，不需要重走完整審查。

比喻：像簽約前協商 KPI

PACMP 就像是在僱用前先跟員工談好「一年後的績效考核標準是什麼」。達標了，自動晉升（= 方法變更實施），不需要再重新面試（= 不需要重新全面審查）。這樣雙方都有安全感。

「三批次、八重複」的設計邏輯

為什麼是三批次？代表三天（不同操作日），確保結果不受單一操作日的特殊情況影響。

為什麼是八重複？8 次 × 3 天 = 24 次總測試，符合 Ph. Eur. 2.6.21 的要求，讓 95% cutoff（23/24）的統計基礎夠穩固。

這個設計本身就是「最小化數據量同時滿足監管要求」的優化結果。

PHCCF 干擾測試：為什麼只測兩株？

Product-Specific Validation 的 PHCCF 干擾研究只選用 M. hyorhinis 和 A. laidlawii，原因是：

  • M. hyorhinis：代表靈敏度較低的菌株（Generic Validation 中偵測下限 = 5 CFU/mL），是最難偵測的代表。
  • A. laidlawii：引子序列匹配程度最差的菌株（最多錯配），是最容易被 PCR 漏掉的。

用「最難偵測」和「最容易漏掉」的兩株菌做測試，若這兩株通過，其他菌株基本上也會通過。這是科學上最有效率的 worst-case 設計思路。

Commitment and Implementation Plan 的監管意涵

這份 PACMP 的最後部分承諾：依照 PQS 實施、變更後持續監控、文件備查。這不只是客套話，而是監管機關評估你是否具備「自我管理能力」的依據。

對 CDMO 來說，這份承諾意味著需要建立跨客戶的 PCR 方法變更管理 SOP，確保每個客戶產品的 post-change monitoring 都有對應的文件追蹤系統。

思考練習

若你是 CDMO 的 QA Director，你的廠已完成 PCR 方法的 Generic Method Validation，現在要幫第一個客戶做 Product-Specific Validation。你需要準備哪些文件？哪些人需要被通知和簽核？客戶那邊需要提供什麼材料和授權？試著列出一個 5 步驟的執行清單。

12.2 Current Regulatory Filing Considerations — 各地區申報分類

12.2.1 U.S. Filing Considerations

With respect to certain biologics, the FDA Guidance for Industry: Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls Changes to an Approved Application: Certain Biological Products suggests that changes impacting the transfer of manufacturing of an aseptically processed sterile drug substance or drug product into a new or renovated aseptic processing area within the currently licensed building would be considered a prior approval supplement (PAS). Likewise, the addition or modification to an approved filling line that affects sterility assurance would be considered a PAS.

In the 1997 FDA Guidance for Industry: Changes to an Approved Application: Biological Products, "Section II. Changes under §601.12(b) – Changes requiring supplement submission and approval prior to distribution of the product made using the change (major changes)," item #11 discusses potentially related changes:

Major construction, or changes in location, involving or affecting environmentally controlled manufacturing or related support areas, such as:

  • aseptic processing areas;
  • modifications to support systems with significant potential to affect air, water, or steam quality;
  • installation of a new HVAC system involving or affecting environmentally controlled manufacturing or related support areas; or
  • modifications to an existing HVAC system that supplies aseptic processing areas.

In general, the process impact of changing the drug product filling process to replace the filling unit and introduce an isolator system would be considered a major change and would require a PAS (four-month review). Both the footprint of the existing filling suite and the HVAC system will be impacted.

The needed space may be reduced, as an isolator would have a Grade A chamber that can be placed in a Grade C or D space, making a Grade B background no longer necessary. In addition, other adaptations would be needed to accommodate material and airflow accessibility.

FDA offers general information about aseptic processing in Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing – Current Good Manufacturing Practice and sets high-level expectations in Guideline for the Submission of Documentation for Sterilization Process Validation in Applications for Human and Veterinary Drug Products. Both summarize the types of data the FDA looks for in aseptic filling processes; however, neither provides specific guidelines about isolators or types of equipment.

美國申報解析

核心概念：PAS 的本質

Prior Approval Supplement（PAS）是美國最嚴格的申報類別——「先申請、獲批後才能使用」。四個月審查期意味著你的資本投資要等監管機關點頭才能產生商業價值。

為什麼隔離器導入觸發 PAS？因為它同時影響了兩個 FDA 最敏感的 CMC 要素：無菌保證系統（整個 Grade A 環境重新定義）和 HVAC 系統（空氣分類、壓差設計、氣流模式全部改變）。

重點提示：Grade A in Grade C/D

這是隔離器最重要的設施優勢之一：ISO-5（Grade A）腔體可以安置在 ISO-7（Grade C）或 ISO-8（Grade D）背景環境中，不再需要 Grade B 背景區域。這大幅降低廠房面積要求與潔淨室建設成本——也是 CDMO 升級隔離器的財務計算之一。

類比說明

想像你在倉庫裡放了一個密封的無菌手術艙。艙外是一般倉庫，艙內是 Grade A。只要艙體完整，裡面就是無菌的——這就是隔離器改變設施設計邏輯的地方。

思考題

如果你的廠目前有一間 Grade B 背景房，導入隔離器後可以降級為 Grade C。這個空間可以重新利用嗎？這個「省下來的潔淨室」如何在商業計劃書中量化為隔離器投資的間接收益？

12.2.2 Canadian Filing Considerations

The Health Canada Guidance Document: Post-Notice of Compliance (NOC) Changes: Quality Document, "Appendix III, Biologics, 3.2.P.3 Manufacture," places the following changes in the Notifiable Change reporting category:

  • 40. Change involving a drug product manufacturer/manufacturing facility, such as: *** b. replacement or addition of an equivalent formulation/filling suite
  • 42. Change in the drug product manufacturing process, such as: *** b. addition or replacement of equipment [e.g., formulation tank, filter housing, filling line and head, and lyophilizer] within the existing filling areas

A notifiable change involves a seven-day screening and logging period, plus a 90-day target review.

The same guidance places the following changes in the supplemental new drug submission (SNDS) reporting category:

  • Appendix III, Biologics, 3.2.P.3 Manufacture — 40c. addition of a non-equivalent formulation/filling suite to an approved formulation/filling facility
  • Appendix I, Drugs, 3.2.P.3 Manufacture — 25. Change in the drug product manufacturing process

An SNDS (CMC only) involves a 45-day screening and logging period, plus a 180-day target review.

A separate filing is required for each product impacted; however, data from one filing may be leveraged for other products if, for example, a range of vials and fill volume is considered.

加拿大申報解析

核心概念：等效 vs. 非等效

加拿大申報的關鍵判斷點是「新充填套間是否等效（equivalent）於現有套間」。這直接決定是走 Notifiable Change（90天）還是 SNDS（180天）。

隔離器導入通常屬於「非等效」變更——因為環境分類、設備配置、消毒方式都根本改變。因此較可能走 SNDS 路徑。

重點提示：多產品 CDMO 的申報負擔

「每個受影響產品需單獨申報」是 CDMO 在多客戶場景下的主要挑戰。如果你的充填線服務 8 個產品，理論上要提 8 份申請。

但 TR91 明確指出：如果充填範圍（vial size、fill volume）涵蓋所有產品，可以在一份申報中槓桿其他產品的數據——這正是 PACMP 設計範圍定義的重要性所在。

類比說明

把多產品 CDMO 的申報想像成「一把鑰匙開多把鎖」的策略。如果 PACMP 的驗證範圍定義夠廣（覆蓋所有產品的充填規格），監管機關接受後，後續每個產品的申報就簡化為「通知」而非「重新審查」。

12.2.3 EU Filing Considerations

According to Commission Regulation (EC) No 1234/2008 — Guideline on the details of the various categories of variations to the terms of marketing authorizations for medicinal products for human use and veterinary medicinal products [B.II.b.3, b], variations related to "substantial changes to the manufacturing process, … which may have a significant impact on the quality, safety or efficacy of the medicinal product" are classified as a major variation of Type II.

That includes variations to the manufacturing process, formulation, specifications, or impurity profile of an active substance or finished medicinal product.

Such major variations require approval of the relevant regulatory agency before implementation.

歐盟申報解析

核心概念：Type II Major Variation

歐盟的 Type II Variation 等同於美國的 PAS——都是「先批准才能使用」。關鍵條件是「可能對品質、安全性或有效性有重大影響」。

充填技術從 LAF 改為隔離器，無疑符合「substantial change to manufacturing process」的定義，因此分類為 Type II 幾乎是確定的。

重點提示：多區域同步申報的複雜度

如果一個產品在美國、歐盟、加拿大都有上市許可，同一個充填線變更需要同步提交 PAS（美國）+ Type II（歐盟）+ SNDS/Notifiable（加拿大）。三個地區的審查時程不一致，意味著你可能在某個市場獲批後，另一個市場還在審查中。

PACMP 策略的核心價值之一，就是用一份預先同意的協議，讓後續申報在所有地區都走「快速通道」。

12.2.4 WHO Considerations

According to WHO Annex 4 – Guidelines on Procedures and Data Requirements for Changes to Approved Vaccines [TRS No. 993], changes in the manufacturing process of a final product, such as the addition or replacement of equipment, including filling lines, are indicated as a "moderate quality change". Implementation of changes in the moderate (or major) category requires reporting to the national regulatory agency and must be reviewed and approved by the regulatory authority prior to implementation.

This aligns with WHO Annex 3 – Guidelines on Procedures and Data Requirements for Changes to Approved Biotherapeutic Products [TRS No. 1011], which proposes that a change in a filling line would be a moderate quality change that should be reviewed and approved by the national regulatory authority prior to implementation.

WHO 申報解析

核心概念：WHO 的重要性

WHO 指引對許多新興市場國家（東南亞、非洲、拉丁美洲）的監管機關具有直接參考效力。如果你的 CDMO 服務有 WHO 預認證（PQ）產品（如疫苗、基本藥物），就必須遵循 WHO 程序。

「中度品質變更」（moderate quality change）在 WHO 框架中需要事前批准，這與美國 PAS、歐盟 Type II 的邏輯一致——所有主要法規體系都要求「先批准，後使用」。

PURPOSE

This post-approval change management protocol (PACMP) describes the strategy and approach for demonstrating the introduction of an isolator as an improvement to an aseptic filling suite for Ourfavoritebiologic (OFB).

This protocol outlines the proposed studies, test methods, and acceptance criteria that will be used to assess the impact on OFB drug product when manufactured using the new isolator. The data from the proposed studies will demonstrate that the quality of the OFB drug product is at least comparable to the historical OFB drug product and that the new isolator is acceptable for manufacturing this product.

SCOPE OF CHANGE

Sterile drug product manufacturing is accomplished in various ways; traditionally, ISO-5 laminar air flow (LAF) units with manual operations have been widely used. As the sterility of the product is of the greatest concern, manual operations always have disadvantages over isolated systems. The overall objective in the aseptic filling process is to attain the highest sterility assurance level by using an isolated system.

Currently, this site uses ISO-5 LAF conventional filling technology, an open restricted access barrier system (RABS). To switch from LAF to any other isolated system for the filling of protein therapeutics as sterile solutions may require an extensive overhaul of the manufacturing process; however, the increased control over the following critical areas (in order of importance) plays a major role in maintaining the quality of the product:

  • Microbial contamination
  • Particulate contamination
  • Chemical contamination

Isolated systems available in the industry:

  • Closed RABS (access by glove ports)
  • Isolators (access by glove ports with automated decontamination)
  • Aseptic work cell with robot (gloveless systems)

A thorough assessment was completed with the three options (RABS, isolator, aseptic work cell) weighted against product impact, production needs, facility capabilities, and business needs. Moving forward with the isolator technology was deemed best suited for meeting the needs of the business and ensuring product quality.

PACMP 目的與範圍解析

核心概念：PACMP 的「Purpose Statement」要寫什麼

一份好的 PACMP Purpose Statement 要明確包含三個要素：

  1. Who：針對哪個藥品（OFB = Ourfavoritebiologic，在真實案例中就是產品名稱）
  2. What：什麼變更（導入隔離器替代 LAF）
  3. How：如何證明可接受性（比較性研究 + 預定義驗收標準）

這三個要素缺一不可。Purpose Statement 決定了監管機關如何理解你的申報意圖。

重點提示：三種隔離技術的選擇矩陣

本 PACMP 選擇隔離器作為最平衡的解決方案——比 Closed RABS 更高的無菌保證，比機器人工作艙更成熟的技術與驗證基礎。

類比說明：為什麼是隔離器而非機器人工作艙？

機器人工作艙（gloveless）在技術上是最優的，但對已核准產品進行這麼大的設備變更，監管風險也最高。隔離器在業界已有充足的驗證文獻和監管接受度，是「最佳可行技術」而非「最理想理論技術」。

隔離器導入帶來兩個新的 E&L 風險來源：

  2. H₂O₂ 殘留：VHP 消毒循環後，隔離器腔體內可能有微量 H₂O₂ 殘留，這是隔離器特有的化學污染風險，在傳統 LAF 中不存在。

本 PACMP 已明確說明：實驗室研究（pump shear study）已完成，確認新泵不會負面影響產品品質。

蠕動泵 vs. 隔膜泵：對蛋白質的潛在剪切風險

蠕動泵透過擠壓矽膠管來驅動液體，這個「擠壓-釋放」循環在理論上會產生剪切力（shear force），對分子量大、結構敏感的蛋白質療法（如單克隆抗體）可能造成聚集（aggregation）。

這就是為什麼 PACMP 要求做「pump laboratory study」——在工程批次前先驗證新泵的操作參數不會觸發蛋白質降解。這是監管機關最可能質疑的技術問題之一。

重點提示：CCI 測試的重要性升級

隔離器導入改變了封裝工序的機械動作（stoppered、capped 方式可能改變），因此 Container Closure Integrity (CCI) 測試是必做的。

TR91 明確指出，對於製程改變可能影響容器密封完整性的情況，必須進行 CCI 評估。可接受的方法包括：氣泡測試、壓力/真空衰減、示蹤氣體滲漏、染料穿透，或微生物挑戰測試。

重點提示：Polysorbate 80 的監控意義

CQA 表中列出了 Polysorbate 80 (PS80) 的 assay——這個非活性成分的監控在蛋白質製劑中非常關鍵。PS80 保護蛋白質在充填過程中不受介面剪切應力破壞。

泵型改變（隔膜泵 → 蠕動泵）改變了剪切力模式，理論上可能影響 PS80 的消耗速率，進而影響蛋白質穩定性。這是工程批次研究中必須監控的 CQA。

DESCRIPTION OF PLANNED CHANGES — Isolator & New Filling Line

A new isolator will be introduced into the manufacturing suite used for the manufacture of OFB. When the new isolator is introduced, the filling line will also be completely replaced using a new pump that has greater throughput and accuracy.

Isolator Technical Description

The isolator is a "Separative Device" that will provide assured protection by using physical and dynamic barriers to create separation between operation and operator; it comes supplied with HEPA-filtered air and will be decontaminated in a quantifiable and highly reproducible manner. When the system is closed, it uses only decontaminated interfaces.

Key technical features of the new isolator:

  • Stainless steel-glass construction with positive pressurization
  • Facilitates both aseptic and containment applications
  • HEPA-filtered air supply with vertical, unidirectional air flow (open) and pressure differentials of no more than 100 Pa
  • Pressure differentials between exterior and interior monitored continuously and integrated into existing automated process control system
  • Sterilization-in-place (SIP) for contact parts / fluid pathways
  • Automated vapor phase hydrogen peroxide (H₂O₂) decontamination cycle between campaigns
  • Both viable and nonviable particle monitoring conducted inside the isolator
  • New pump: peristaltic pump (replacing current diaphragm pump) — capable of increased output and greater filling accuracy
  • Scope: sterilization tunnel, filling machine, and capping machine — capable of processing 3 mL, 6 mL, 10 mL vials

The new filling isolator will require modifications to the facility, including the HVAC system and personnel and material flow, and will improve product isolation systems. Modifications will minimize human intervention and provide the highest sterility assurance for the filling operations.

No change to change-over procedures, visual inspection procedures, release specification, or cleaning procedures is proposed. The PPQ will be designed according to USP <1208>.

Validation studies to be completed: Factory acceptance testing (FAT), site acceptance testing (SAT), installation qualification (IQ), operational qualification (OQ), and performance qualification (PQ/PPQ).

核心概念：H₂O₂ VHP 消毒循環的驗證

Vapor Phase Hydrogen Peroxide (VHP) 是隔離器腔體的標準消毒方法。每次換批（campaign changeover）前，系統自動執行一個 VHP 循環：

  1. 注入（Injection）：氣化 H₂O₂ 充滿腔體
  2. 保持（Dwell）：達到目標濃度並維持足夠時間（log reduction 驗證）
  3. 通風（Aeration）：排除 H₂O₂ 殘留，確保殘留低於安全限值

這個循環本身需要驗證（VHP cycle validation），並且必須在 PACMP / PPQ 中描述。每次消毒循環的 H₂O₂ 濃度、接觸時間、殘留水平都是 CPP。

The risk of introducing the new filling system was evaluated; two aspects were considered in the risk assessments:

風險評估解析

核心概念：兩層風險評估架構

這份 PACMP 的風險評估分兩個維度：廠房設施層面（人員/物料流向、差壓、更衣室設計）和產品/製程層面（CQA、CPP 影響）。兩者缺一不可。

廠房評估確保 GMP 基礎設施沒有盲點；產品評估確保藥品品質不受影響。監管機關審查時，這兩份評估都會被仔細檢視。

核心概念：FMEA 在 ICH Q9 框架下的應用

Failure Mode and Effects Analysis（FMEA）是 ICH Q9 推薦的風險評估工具之一。它的邏輯是：列出所有可能的失效模式 → 評估每個模式的嚴重度（Severity）、發生率（Occurrence）、偵測率（Detection）→ 計算 RPN → 依 RPN 優先排序緩解措施。

RPN = S × O × D，最高分 125（5×5×5），最低分 1。

RPN 公式

RPN = Severity × Occurrence × Detection

Low:    RPN 1–20   → 標準監控即可
Medium: RPN 24–45  → 需納入比較性研究設計
High:   RPN 48–125 → 需額外緩解措施

類比說明

FMEA 就像飛機起飛前的系統查核清單（checklist）。每個潛在問題都被打分數，分數越高代表越需要立即處理。PACMP 的設計邏輯是：把分數高的項目轉化為「驗證計畫的必測項目」，讓監管機關看到你的風險有被系統性管理，而不是事後亡羊補牢。

核心邏輯：風險等級由「什麼沒有改變」決定

這份評估表每一列的 Rationale 都有一個共同的關鍵句：「The filling process is not changing. Primary container closure components are not changing.」

這句話是整個風險論證的支柱——配方沒變、容器密封系統沒變、輸入與輸出沒變。唯一改變的是充填「設備」。因此對純度、填充量、CCI 的影響只是「可能」，而非「必然」。

重點提示：為何純度/無菌性是 M 而非 L？

純度/無菌性（Purity/Sterility）評為中等風險的原因有兩點：

  • 新隔離器引入了 H₂O₂ 去污染循環，殘留過氧化氫對蛋白質穩定性可能有影響（需額外評估）。
  • 新的蠕動泵（peristaltic pump）取代隔膜泵（diaphragm pump），泵管剪切力對蛋白質完整性有潛在影響（這正是幫浦研究的由來）。

因此「緩解措施」欄位特別提到：評估殘留 H₂O₂ 影響 + 預過濾生物負擔 + 過濾器完整性測試。
