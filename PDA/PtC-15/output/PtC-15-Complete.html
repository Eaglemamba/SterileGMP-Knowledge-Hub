Document Overview — PDA PtC-15

PDA Points to Consider No. 15 was published in 2025 by the Parenteral Drug Association. It addresses one of the most consequential shifts in pharmaceutical manufacturing: the move from permanently fixed, site-built facilities to deployable, relocatable manufacturing units.

The document is structured as a series of questions and considerations across three topics:

  • Topic 1 — Technology Considerations: Facility build options, MMU advantages and challenges, implementation, validation, and technology transfer
  • Topic 2 — Regulatory Considerations: Impact of current regulations; actions regulators can take to facilitate adoption
  • Topic 3 — Implementation: How to validate an MMU at a new location

Authors represent major industry stakeholders: G-CON Manufacturing, Lupin Pharmaceuticals, Genentech, On Demand Pharmaceuticals, Parexel, NIIMBL (Ex-FDA), Pfizer, Emergent BioSolutions — ensuring the document reflects both industry practice and regulatory perspectives.

"Mobile manufacturing is the ability to relocate manufacturing while maintaining an environment suitable for good manufacturing practice (GMP) production."
— PDA PtC-15, Introduction, p.1

PDA「Points to Consider」系列文件屬於指引性文件（非強制標準），提供產業界在特定技術議題上的思考框架。PtC-15 聚焦的是「行動式製造」——一個在 COVID-19 疫情後被認真重新審視的製造典範。

  • CDMO 業務模式本質上需要靈活的製造能力，無論是服務不同客戶、不同產品，還是快速響應緊急需求

Introduction — Historical Context

The manufacturing of drugs has historically been fixed in the building in which it is processed and therefore, manufacturing was conceptualized as fixed in nature. Specifically, the conventional construction of such manufacturing facilities has been executed on the site and viewed as immobile. Such construction has often been termed stick-built or been called brick-and-mortar facilities.

This type of construction has been the dominant method in our industry throughout the past. However, the COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the need for new thinking and a more flexible, modern, and scientifically sound approach to the platform design of the manufacturing process, thereby ensuring the essential manufacture, distribution, and availability of medicinal products.

While some concepts like modular facility construction have been implemented, only recently has a significant advancement in technologies allowed alternative methods, such as mobile manufacturing facilities, to be used in its place.

Mobile manufacturing is the ability to relocate manufacturing while maintaining an environment suitable for good manufacturing practice (GMP) production. This paper focuses exclusively on biotech and pharmaceutical manufacturing.

歷史脈絡解讀

類比：餐廳 vs. 餐車

傳統製藥廠就像一間固定餐廳——廚房設備永遠安裝在同一棟建築裡，所有的基礎設施（水電、通風、儲存）都與建築結構融為一體。

行動式製造就像一輛餐車（Food Truck）——廚房設備是可移動的，能夠開到有需求的地方，但仍需要符合相同的食品安全標準。

更專業的類比：MASH 野戰醫院（Mobile Army Surgical Hospital）——在需要的地點快速部署完整的手術能力。製藥版的 MASH 就是 MMU。

Stick-built 廠房（也稱 brick-and-mortar）：指在廠區現場由各種工班（木工、電工、泥水匠等）直接施工建造的傳統廠房。這種廠房一旦建成，無法搬遷。

COVID-19 是關鍵的歷史轉折點——疫苗需求在數週內從零爆升至數十億劑，但固定廠房的建設週期根本無法跟上，迫使產業界認真思考「如何讓製造能力像軟體一樣可部署」。

Introduction — Technology Evolution

The use of self-contained, mobile manufacturing units enables the relocation or replication of a manufacturing facility between different geographic locations, which allows manufacturers to rapidly supply and scale up product manufacturing.

Examples of manufacturing strategic improvements over time include the adoption of cleanrooms (first introduced in 1962), which are now a requirement for GMP manufacturing. Prior to that time, it was difficult to control the manufacturing environment, and risks from particulates and adventitious agents were common. Since then, cleanrooms have become the status quo and are a requirement for GMP manufacturing.

The development of prefabricated elements presaged mobile manufacturing. The preassembled, prefabricated solutions which altered how manufacturing facilities were built on-site also seeded the concept of manufacturing mobility as it became common to ship these semi-completed facilities across the world.

Within the past 20 years, prefabricated technologies have been introduced that can be easily prequalified, disassembled, and relocated elsewhere. These are the technologies evaluated within this document.

The process equipment used to manufacture pharmaceuticals is prefabricated and prequalified (via factory acceptance testing) off-site prior to delivery at the facility, which lends support to the overall concept of mobile manufacturing.

FAT/SAT 在行動式製造中的核心角色

工廠驗收測試（FAT, Factory Acceptance Test）：設備在製造商場所完成的初始確效測試。

現場驗收測試（SAT, Site Acceptance Test）：設備到達最終安裝地點後的確認測試。

傳統觀念認為確效必須在「最終廠房」進行。MMU 的核心概念之一，就是將 FAT 的結果可攜帶地移轉到新地點，避免重複完整的驗證活動。這直接挑戰了現有法規框架，也是 PtC-15 後續章節的核心問題。

Introduction — Key Drivers

Another major advancement in the evolution of mobile manufacturing feasibility was the introduction in the 1970s of single-use components, which have matured dramatically since that time and provided greater flexibility in facility design.

Accompanying the advances in technology, the size of manufacturing facilities and equipment has decreased over time, driven both by:

  • Process intensification — achieving the same or greater output with smaller equipment footprints
  • Personalized medicines — targeting smaller patient populations, reducing the need for large-scale batch production

Geopolitical concerns have also been a driver of distributed manufacturing, as many countries look for in-country manufacturing. Similarly, pharmaceutical and biotechnical companies want to mitigate the risk to their assets by having the ability to move their production capabilities in the event of wars, natural disasters, or other crises.

Lastly, regulatory barriers within the drug production landscape have also lessened over time to accommodate the concept of manufacturing mobility.

三大驅動力深度解析

驅動力一：一次性技術（Single-Use Technology, SUT）

一次性技術的革命性在於：它將清潔驗證（Cleaning Validation）從製造流程中移除。傳統不鏽鋼設備需要大量清洗、滅菌、再確效的工作；一次性設備則是用完即棄，大幅簡化跨批次、跨地點的交叉污染管控。

對 MMU 而言，這意味著：每次部署不需要重新執行清潔驗證，降低了行動式製造的法規複雜度。

驅動力二：地緣政治風險

COVID-19 之後，各國政府意識到關鍵藥品的生產不能高度集中在單一國家或地區。「在地製造（In-Country Manufacturing）」需求急速上升。

對 CDMO 業者而言，能夠提供「可在客戶國境內快速部署的 MMU」，將成為重要的競爭差異化優勢。

驅動力三：製程強化與個人化醫療

傳統大批量生產（如年產百億劑疫苗）需要巨型廠房。但基因療法、細胞療法、個人化腫瘤疫苗的目標患者群往往只有數十至數千人——小批量、高彈性、多樣化成為新的生產需求，恰好符合 MMU 的能力特性。

Scope of PDA PtC-15

This document aims to assess the feasibility and requirements for designing and qualifying a self-contained manufacturing process that, once validated, can be relocated and operated in a different location for the same process, without the requirement for regulatory preapproval or revalidation.

The scope includes:

  • Exploring the technology supporting mobile manufacturing in greater detail
  • The regulatory requirements and risks associated with moving a manufacturing process from one location to another

While mobile manufacturing can be considered an enabling technology for distributed manufacturing or point-of-care manufacturing, the scope of this paper excludes identifying or elaborating on those requirements.

"This document aims to assess the feasibility and requirements for designing and qualifying a self-contained manufacturing process that, once validated, can be relocated and operated in a different location for the same process, without the requirement for regulatory preapproval or revalidation."
— PDA PtC-15, Scope, p.2

範圍界定的重要性

PtC-15 的範圍聲明中有一個關鍵詞值得反覆咀嚼：

核心目標：「無需重新預先核准或再驗證」

傳統法規要求：當一個已驗證的製程移至新地點，通常需要向監管機構重新申請核准，並在新地點執行完整的再驗證。

PtC-15 探索的問題是：能否設計出一套系統，使得已在甲地驗證的製造能力，能夠在無需重新核准的情況下移至乙地繼續生產？

這是一個非常大膽的目標，直接涉及現行 GMP 法規的根本假設——而這也是 Topic 2（法規考量）整章要處理的核心問題。

比喻：ISBU（海運貨櫃）標準化

國際標準貨櫃（ISO Shipping Container Unit, ISBU）的革命性不在於「能裝很多貨」，而在於標準化——無論在哪個港口，碼頭設備都能處理相同規格的貨櫃。

MMU 的理想狀態類似：設計一個標準化的製造單元，使其在任何符合條件的地點都能如預期運作，而不需要針對每個地點重新「客製化」確效工作。

排除範圍說明：「分散式製造（Distributed Manufacturing）」和「即地醫療製造（Point-of-Care Manufacturing）」是更廣義的概念，涉及更複雜的法規框架。PtC-15 刻意將範圍限縮在「可移動的、已完整確效的製造單元」，以保持討論的聚焦性。

Decision Hierarchy for Mobile Manufacturing

Although not stated explicitly as a numbered hierarchy in PtC-15, the document's structure and emphasis make clear that decisions in mobile manufacturing must follow a priority order. Any business case for mobility must be subordinate to product quality and regulatory compliance:

  1. Product Quality & Patient Safety — GMP compliance must be maintained regardless of whether the manufacturing environment is fixed or mobile. The cleanroom, utilities, process controls, and environmental monitoring must all perform identically whether the unit is in Site A or Site B.
  2. Regulatory Compliance — Validation portability, multi-jurisdiction requirements, and the ability to satisfy different national competent authorities are non-negotiable prerequisites before deploying to a new location.
  3. Business Flexibility — Speed-to-market, pandemic response capability, CDMO capacity management, and new business models are legitimate and important drivers — but they are pursued within the constraints established by levels 1 and 2.

決策優先順序框架

Level 1: 產品品質與病患安全
GMP 合規性在任何地點均不妥協
↓
Level 2: 法規合規性
驗證可攜帶性、多管轄區要求
↓
Level 3: 商業靈活性
速度、成本、CDMO 業務模型

這個優先順序框架在實務中的意義：

  • 不能因為「商業需求緊迫」而跳過地點確效步驟
  • 不能因為「成本考量」而降低移動後的環境監控標準
  • 法規層面的不確定性，必須在技術設計階段就納入考慮（而非事後補救）

對 CDMO 的啟示

CDMO 在推銷 MMU 服務時，常見的錯誤是先強調「速度快、成本低」（Level 3），而忽略客戶最關心的問題：「移動後我的產品品質會一樣嗎？」（Level 1）和「監管機構會接受嗎？」（Level 2）。

正確的溝通順序應該是：先建立品質保證框架，再談商業價值。

Q1.1

What are the available options to build a facility?

建廠有哪些選擇？ | PDA PtC-15, p. 9

Original Text — PDA PtC-15 (pp. 5, 9)

The means to fabricate or build a GMP-compliant facility can be categorized into two main groups: on-site construction and prefabricated solutions.

One method for on-site construction involves conventional stick-built or brick-and-mortar facilities. This involves various trades (e.g., carpenters, electricians, masons) being on-site and using stock materials cut or manipulated on-site based on build plans. Another approach to on-site construction is modular components, where materials are built off-site but assembled on-site. Both options provide a fixed facility which cannot be moved to an alternate location if needed.

Prefabricated solutions allow for the build-out of completed components assembled off-site. Being built off-site entails certain aspects of mobility, and the level of completeness or percentage of work done off-site typically lends itself to the ability to redeploy the facility elsewhere. However, certain prefabricated solutions, once installed, become fixed in place and are not easily relocated.

To be considered a mobile manufacturing platform, the ease of redeployment becomes a prerequisite. The two prefabricated units that are inherently mobile will be referred to as mobile manufacturing units (MMUs).

"Mobile manufacturing is the ability to relocate manufacturing while maintaining an environment suitable for good manufacturing practice (GMP) production."

— PDA PtC-15 (2025), Introduction

Manufacturing has historically been conceptualized as fixed in nature — stick-built or brick-and-mortar was the dominant model. The COVID-19 pandemic highlighted the need for a more flexible, modern, and scientifically sound approach to facility design, ensuring the manufacture, distribution, and availability of medicinal products under crisis conditions.

Key enabling factors for mobile manufacturing include:

  • Prefabricated elements — shipped semi-complete across the world
  • Single-use components (introduced in the 1970s, matured significantly since)
  • Process intensification — smaller equipment footprints
  • Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) — qualification off-site prior to delivery
  • Reduced geopolitical risk through distributed manufacturing

中文解說 — 建廠選擇概覽

三種建廠型態 — 一句話比較

Stick-built（傳統磚造）：工人在現場用原料一磚一瓦建造。最常見，但建造最慢，無法移動。

Modular（模組化）：模組在工廠預製，運到現場組裝。快一點，但一旦固定就無法搬移。

MMU（行動製造單元）：整個設施可以拆解、搬運、重新組裝，維持 GMP 等級。這才是本文件的主角。

生活比喻：建廠就像蓋房子？

Stick-built = 傳統磚造透天厝：一旦蓋好就永遠在那裡。

Modular = 組合屋：預製好的板材到現場拼裝，但地基打下去就固定了。

MMU = 豪華貨櫃屋：內部設備齊全、可拆解，今天在台灣，明年搬到新加坡，功能一模一樣。

MMU 的概念就來自於此 — 標準化海運貨櫃可以在全球任何港口被處理，製藥 MMU 也應該能在任何 GMP 符合的場地被部署。

關鍵判斷標準：是否具備「易於重新部署」的能力

PDA 文件明確指出，成為「行動製造平台」的先決條件是 ease of redeployment（易於重新部署）。

實務意義：如果你的預製模組安裝完成後需要大型土木工程才能移除，它就不是 MMU，只是比較快的傳統廠。

COVID-19 作為催化劑

疫情清楚暴露了固定式製造的脆弱性：單一工廠停產就可能導致全球供應中斷。

MMU 的設計理念之一就是「供應鏈的分散化韌性」：當一個地點出現危機，可以迅速在另一個地點部署相同的 GMP 製造能力，不需要重新建廠。

Q1.2

What are the advantages and challenges associated with building a mobile manufacturing unit?

MMU 的優勢與挑戰 | PDA PtC-15, pp. 5–8

Advantages of Mobile Manufacturing Units

The PDA PtC-15 document identifies multiple strategic advantages driven by technological evolution:

1. Faster Construction Timeline
Because MMUs are fabricated off-site in parallel with site preparation activities, overall project timelines are compressed significantly compared to conventional construction. Equipment qualification via Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) occurs before delivery, reducing on-site commissioning burden.

2. Pre-Qualification Off-Site (FAT)
Process equipment is prefabricated and prequalified off-site prior to delivery at the facility. This is described as a major lend-support to the overall concept of mobile manufacturing. FAT at the vendor's facility reduces uncertainty at the deployment site.

3. Relocatability
The core value proposition: a qualified MMU can be disassembled and reassembled into its functional state without incorporating any major rework. Geopolitical concerns — in-country manufacturing requirements, natural disasters, wars — are directly addressed by this feature.

4. Scalability
Additional MMU modules can be added to increase manufacturing capacity without constructing a new facility. The distributed manufacturing (DM) model networks multiple units operating under a central remote Pharmaceutical Quality System (PQS).

5. Lower Capital Risk
Compared to a full stick-built facility investment, MMU capital expenditure is more modular and can be phased. For personalized medicines targeting smaller patient populations — a growing segment — smaller footprints are appropriate.

6. Single-Use Technology Integration
The maturation of single-use components (introduced in the 1970s) enables MMUs to avoid the complexity of permanent stainless steel CIP/SIP infrastructure, reducing the footprint and turnaround time between products.

Challenges of Mobile Manufacturing Units

1. Limited Space Constraints
MMUs are inherently compact — typically shipping-container-based configurations. Equipment integration complexity increases when fitting full GMP-grade operations (cleanroom, HVAC, utilities, personnel flow) into a confined volume. Worker ergonomics in confined space is a distinct challenge versus traditional facilities.

2. Utility Hook-Up Complexity
At each deployment site, the MMU must connect to site utilities: water for injection (WFI), clean steam, compressed air, electrical power, chilled water, nitrogen. The quality and reliability of these supplies varies by site and must be independently assessed each time.

3. Multi-Site Validation and Regulatory Submissions
While the goal is to avoid revalidation, each new location creates regulatory change events. The strategy for whether a move triggers a site change supplement or can be managed under a prior approval supplement must be defined in advance and agreed with regulatory agencies.

4. Environmental Control Variability
External temperature and humidity fluctuations at each new deployment site affect the performance of the MMU's HVAC systems. A unit qualified in a temperate climate may require HVAC requalification when moved to a tropical or arctic environment.

5. Worker Ergonomics
The confined working environment of an MMU poses challenges for personnel performing manual operations. GMP-compliant gowning, material flow, and waste removal all become more complex in a compressed physical footprint.

中文解說 — MMU 優劣勢分析

核心優勢：為什麼 MMU 現在開始被認真討論？

這不只是技術突破，而是三個長期趨勢同時成熟的結果：

  • 單次使用技術（1970s 起步，2010s 成熟）消除了大型不鏽鋼基礎設施的必要性
  • 製程強化讓同樣生產量所需的設備體積縮小
  • 個人化醫療讓小批量、多地點製造在商業上可行

三個趨勢合力讓「把整個 GMP 工廠裝進貨櫃」從科幻變成工程問題。

比喻：MASH 野戰醫院 vs. 固定醫院

韓戰時期的 MASH（流動外科醫院）是軍方的「行動製造」：整套手術室可以在 24 小時內在任何地點架設，提供接近固定醫院的醫療品質。

它的挑戰也和 MMU 完全對應：

  • 空間有限 → 設備和流程必須高度精簡
  • 當地供電/用水不穩定 → 必須自備或評估當地公用設施
  • 環境條件變化（沙漠、寒帶） → 需要重新評估溫控能力

製藥 MMU 面對的是一樣的問題，只是把「無菌手術環境」換成「GMP 無菌製造環境」。

最大挑戰：法規申報策略 — 提前規劃不可省

MMU 最複雜的問題不是工程，而是法規。每次移動 MMU 到新地點，都可能觸發「地點變更」（site change），而地點變更在 FDA 21 CFR 314/601 架構下，可能需要：

  • Prior Approval Supplement (PAS)：移動前需要 FDA 核准，可能等待 6-18 個月
  • Changes Being Effected (CBE-30)：通知後 30 天可執行
  • Annual Report：最低影響的變更，事後報告即可

理想情況下，MMU 設計讓每次移動可以落在 CBE-30 甚至 Annual Report 等級。這需要在 ANDA/BLA 申請時就預先設計驗證策略，而不是事後補救。

比喻：Pop-Up Restaurant（快閃餐廳）的挑戰

一家米其林三星主廚開設快閃餐廳：同一批廚師、同一套設備、同一份食譜，在不同城市提供完全相同品質的料理。

挑戰清單幾乎和 MMU 一模一樣：

  • 廚房空間比固定餐廳小 → 動線和人員配置必須重新設計
  • 當地水質不同 → 需要額外過濾或處理
  • 當地電壓、燃氣壓力不同 → 設備需要調整
  • 當地衛生主管機關要求不同 → 需要額外申請

最優秀的快閃餐廳廚師會在每次移動前做「新地點評估」，MMU 也需要一套標準化的 Site Readiness Assessment。

Q1.3

How does one implement and validate a mobile manufacturing unit?

如何實施與確效 MMU？ | PDA PtC-15, pp. 6–8

Validation Strategy for MMUs

The MMU validation framework builds on established pharmaceutical qualification principles while addressing the unique challenge of portability. The PDA PtC-15 document articulates a risk-based approach to validation that distinguishes between elements that are stable across relocations and elements that must be re-assessed at each new site.

Factory Acceptance Testing (FAT) — Primary Qualification Strategy

FAT is described as the primary off-site qualification strategy for MMUs. The vendor performs FAT at their facility before delivering the MMU to the initially qualified site. Key activities at FAT include:

  • Verification of equipment installation against design specifications (IQ scope)
  • Functional testing of all systems under representative conditions (OQ scope)
  • HVAC performance testing: air change rates, pressure differentials, temperature/humidity uniformity
  • Utility systems: WFI, clean steam, compressed air, clean-in-place (CIP) and steam-in-place (SIP) where applicable
  • Documentation completeness review: calibration records, instrument qualification, SOPs

The MMU vendor performs initial qualification of major facility systems (water, air), but not product- or process-specific aspects of validation. Those remain the responsibility of the pharmaceutical manufacturer.

Site Acceptance Testing (SAT) — At Each New Location

Upon arrival at each new deployment site, a SAT verifies that the MMU has survived transport without damage and that site utilities are correctly connected and performing to specification. SAT scope is typically a subset of the original FAT, focused on:

  • Physical integrity check after transport
  • Utility connection verification (correct connections, correct supply quality)
  • Critical system re-verification: HVAC, WFI, clean steam
  • Environmental monitoring baseline at the new location
  • Personnel qualification for site-specific procedures

Risk-Based Re-Qualification: What Re-Validates at Each New Site?

The central question for MMU deployment is: what must be repeated, and what carries over? A risk-based approach evaluates each element based on whether relocation plausibly affects it:

  • Carries over (no re-qualification needed): Equipment-level IQ/OQ for systems with no site-dependent parameters; process validation data for parameters not affected by environmental conditions
  • Must re-qualify: HVAC performance in new climate; environmental monitoring data (microbial and particulate baseline); site utility quality (water, steam); personnel gowning qualification for new facility layout

Family Approach to Equipment Qualification

The family approach concept applies when multiple identical or very similar MMUs are operated. After the first unit is fully qualified, subsequent units of the same design can undergo reduced qualification, demonstrating functional equivalence rather than repeating all protocols in full.

Continuous Process Verification and Regulatory Filing

After initial process validation at the on-site (initially qualified) location, continued process verification (CPV) provides the ongoing data stream to demonstrate the process remains in control across sites. The regulatory filing strategy must be established before first deployment — specifically, whether a move to a new site constitutes a site change supplement requiring prior approval, or can be filed as a lower-impact change.

中文解說 — MMU 確效策略

MMU 確效的核心問題：「哪些東西搬家後還有效？」

傳統製藥確效的邏輯是：在固定地點，一次完整驗證，維持狀態。

MMU 顛覆了這個假設：設施本身會移動。確效策略必須回答一個根本問題：

「這個驗證結論，在新地點還成立嗎？」

能回答「是」的 → 攜帶過去（validation portability）
需要重新確認的 → 現場重跑（SAT scope）

比喻：「手機出廠測試」vs.「SIM 卡換卡」

想像 MMU 是一支高規格智慧型手機：

  • FAT = 工廠出廠測試：每個功能都在工廠完整驗測，確保符合規格
  • SAT = 換了一個新地點開機：你要確認 SIM 卡插對了、WiFi 連到正確的網路、電壓穩定、通話沒有雜訊

你不需要重新測試手機的每顆晶片，但你必須確認在這個新環境下，「整體功能正常」。FAT 的結果可以攜帶，但 SAT 確認「接上新環境後還能正常運作」。

Family Approach — 相同型號 MMU 的確效優惠

如果你的公司部署了 3 台相同規格的 MMU，第 1 台完整確效後，第 2 台和第 3 台可以執行「縮減確效」（reduced qualification）：

  • 只需要確認功能等效性（functional equivalence）
  • 不需要重複所有完整的確效協議
  • 前提：設計、建造、材質、配置必須真正相同

這個概念直接來自於設備確效的 Family Approach，PDA PtC-15 明確將它應用到 MMU 層級。對於大規模部署多台 MMU 的公司，這是顯著的時間和資源節省。

風險評估框架：「地點依賴性」判斷準則

判斷某個確效項目是否需要在新地點重跑，可以問這個問題：

「這個參數的表現，會受到地理位置、氣候、當地公用設施、或人員影響嗎？」

  • 答案是「否」 → 驗證結論可攜帶
  • 答案是「是，但影響可預測且在已驗設計範圍內」 → 需要現場確認，但預期通過
  • 答案是「是，且影響超出原始設計邊界」 → 需要完整重跑，可能觸發法規申報

HVAC 是最常見的重跑項目 — 為什麼？

HVAC 系統的性能與外部環境條件高度相關：

  • 台灣夏天 35°C 濕熱 vs. 德國冬天 -5°C 乾冷：相同的 HVAC 系統，在兩個環境下的除濕/加熱負荷完全不同
  • 若原始設計未考慮這個溫濕度範圍，HVAC 可能無法維持無塵室規格
  • 這意味著「跟著分子走」的 MMU 在設計階段就必須考慮最惡劣的部署環境，並以此作為 HVAC sizing 的依據

Q1.4

How can mobile manufacturing facilitate technology transfer?

行動製造如何促進技術移轉？ | PDA PtC-15, pp. 7–8

MMU as a Technology Transfer Vehicle

One of the most compelling applications of mobile manufacturing is its use as a technology transfer vehicle. In conventional pharmaceutical development, technology transfer from a developer's facility to a CDMO or commercial manufacturing site involves extensive documentation, equipment qualification, and process re-demonstration — all conducted on equipment at the receiving site that may differ from the originator's.

The MMU model fundamentally changes this dynamic: the manufacturing equipment travels with the process. The same hardware, the same SOPs, the same validated parameters are physically moved to the new location, eliminating the equipment-related sources of variation that conventionally complicate technology transfers.

CDMO Application: Run the Process at the Client Site

A CDMO operating an MMU can deploy its qualified manufacturing unit to the client's site, producing product under the CDMO's validated process within the client's facility boundary. This model offers:

  • Reduced intellectual property (IP) risk for the innovator — the CDMO's process "comes to them" rather than requiring disclosure of proprietary formulations to a third-party facility
  • Client oversight of manufacturing without requiring the client to build or license their own facility
  • The CDMO maintains control of its quality system and equipment, reducing the risk of process deviation during transfer

Phase I to Phase II to Commercial: Same MMU Follows the Molecule

Perhaps the most transformative application is following a drug candidate through its development lifecycle with the same MMU:

  • Phase I: MMU deployed at clinical trial site or investigator facility; small batch sizes; emphasis on process characterization
  • Phase II: MMU relocated to larger clinical center or CDMO hub; same equipment, expanded batch sizes, continued process understanding building
  • Phase III / Commercial: MMU moved to commercial manufacturing location; process validation executed; no equipment-change-driven re-validation of process parameters already established

The key regulatory advantage: because the equipment itself has not changed, the data generated in Phase I and Phase II remains directly relevant to the commercial filing. There is no need to re-demonstrate that the process works on new equipment — only that the new site conditions do not perturb the established process.

Knowledge Management and Documentation Portability

Technology transfer success depends heavily on knowledge transfer. The MMU model supports this through:

  • Documentation portability: Batch records, SOPs, deviation history, and qualification packages travel with the unit — there is no "receiving site SOP translation" exercise
  • Personnel continuity: Operators trained on the MMU at the originating site can travel with the unit to the receiving site, at least for the initial production campaigns
  • Process history integrity: Since the same equipment is used throughout development, the process history is continuous and unbroken — a significant advantage in regulatory submissions that require a coherent process narrative
"The self-contained, mobile manufacturing unit enables the relocation or replication of a manufacturing facility between different geographic locations, which allows manufacturers to rapidly supply and scale up product manufacturing."

— PDA PtC-15 (2025), p. 5

Distributed Manufacturing and Point-of-Care Considerations

While PDA PtC-15 explicitly excludes point-of-care (POC) manufacturing from its scope, it acknowledges MMU as an enabling technology for POC and distributed manufacturing (DM). In a DM model, multiple MMUs are networked under a central remote PQS, potentially allowing a product to be manufactured simultaneously or sequentially at multiple geographic locations — a direct response to geopolitical supply chain fragility.

中文解說 — MMU 作為技術移轉載具

傳統技術移轉的核心痛點

傳統技術移轉（Tech Transfer）失敗的最主要原因之一：「在新設備上重建製程」。

即使你把配方、製程參數、SOP 全部完整交出去，接收方的設備和原始開發用的設備仍然有：

  • 不同的混合槽幾何形狀 → 流場不同 → 混合效果可能不同
  • 不同的過濾系統 → 需要重新做相容性測試
  • 不同的灌裝機型號 → 需要重新確效灌裝參數

MMU 的解法很直接：把設備一起帶走，消除設備差異這個變數。

比喻：廚師帶著自己的刀去客戶廚房

頂尖主廚在換廚房工作時，通常會帶著自己的刀具套組。原因是：他的所有刀法和出力習慣都是針對這套刀具校準的，換了不熟悉的刀具，即使食譜一模一樣，刀工精準度也會下降。

技術移轉也是如此：製程開發工程師的所有製程知識都是在特定設備上建立的。讓那套設備跟著製程走，技術移轉的成功率最高。

CDMO 的 MMU 就是這套「隨身刀具組」：不是把食譜交給客戶，而是把廚師和廚具一起帶到客戶廚房。

「跟著分子走」的開發優勢

這是 Q1.4 最關鍵的洞察，值得深入理解：

傳統開發路徑（設備切換）：

  • Phase I：小型實驗室設備（Lab scale）
  • Phase II：中試設備（Pilot scale），需要 Scale-up 橋接研究
  • Phase III：商業設備，需要再次橋接，常常引發新的法規問題

MMU 開發路徑（設備不變）：

  • Phase I：MMU 在臨床試驗中心旁邊生產，小批次
  • Phase II：同一台 MMU 搬到更大的臨床中心，擴大批次
  • Phase III：同一台 MMU 移到商業製造地點，不需要重新橋接設備

法規優勢：Phase I 和 Phase II 的數據直接支持商業申請，因為設備沒有變更。

IP 保護：「設備到客戶那邊」vs.「技術給客戶看」

對於許多新藥開發商（innovator），最大的顧慮之一是：當我把製程交給 CDMO，我的核心 IP（配方、關鍵製程參數）是否有外洩風險？

MMU 的 CDMO 模式提供了一個有趣的解法：

  • CDMO 把整個 MMU 帶到客戶廠房，在客戶的監督下生產
  • 客戶可以全程參與，確保製程忠實度
  • CDMO 維護自己的品質系統和設備，不需要把所有細節交給客戶方設施
  • 創新藥廠不需要自建廠，也不需要向第三方完全公開配方

這個模式在孤兒藥和個人化醫療領域特別有吸引力，因為批次小、地點分散。

比喻：手機備份還原 vs. 換新手機設定

傳統技術移轉就像換了一支新手機，然後手動把所有 App 和設定重新設置：理論上可以做到完全一樣，但過程耗時、容易出錯、常常有遺漏。

MMU 技術移轉就像用 iCloud 從舊手機完整備份還原到新地點：所有東西都在，只需要確認新地點的網路和電源連接正常。

但要注意：就像換了新的 SIM 卡號碼需要重新設定，MMU 到新地點仍然需要 SAT 確認「當地連接」。完全免除現場工作是不現實的目標。

知識管理：文件和人員同步移動

MMU 技術移轉的知識管理有三個層面：

1. 顯性知識（Explicit Knowledge） — 批次記錄、SOP、確效報告 → 隨 MMU 硬碟/系統完整移動，無需翻譯或重寫

2. 隱性知識（Tacit Knowledge） — 操作員的手感、設備異常的早期警覺 → 通過人員隨行解決，初期生產由原廠人員帶領

3. 製程歷史（Process History） — 所有批次、偏差、CAPA 記錄都在同一套系統中，法規審查時可以提供完整、連貫的製程演進敘事

Key Terms & Glossary — 關鍵術語詞彙表

Official Definitions (PDA PtC-15)

Mobile Manufacturing
The manufacturing of pharmaceutical products at off-site locations via qualified manufacturing units.
Mobile Manufacturing Unit (MMU)
A deployable manufacturing unit that has been qualified to make a specific set of pharmaceutical products, regardless of location. It is designed so that it can be easily disassembled and reassembled into its functional state without incorporating any major rework.
Distributed Manufacturing (DM)
A decentralized manufacturing strategy consisting of a manufacturing platform comprising manufacturing units deployed to multiple locations.
Initially Qualified Site
The first site where a mobile unit is qualified for producing a specific pharmaceutical product or set of products prior to deployment to other locations. Usually a fixed facility of the pharma company that developed the product.
MMU Vendor
The company that assembles the mobile manufacturing units and provides them to the initially qualified site. Performs initial qualification of major facility systems (water and air), but not product- or process-specific aspects of validation.
Family Approach
A concept applied to equipment qualification where a set of identical or very similar pieces of equipment are subjected to reduced qualification after an initial member of the set is fully qualified.
On-site
The site where the MMU is fully qualified (initially qualified site) for manufacture of a pharmaceutical product. Usually a fixed site at a pharma company.
Off-site
The site where the MMU is deployed for product manufacture after initial qualification.

術語解說 — 繁體中文

行動製造 Mobile Manufacturing

在廠外地點透過合格製造單元進行藥品製造的能力。重點是「在廠外」且「維持 GMP 合規環境」。

行動製造單元 MMU

可部署的製造單元，已通過資格確認，能在任何地點製造特定藥品。關鍵設計要求：可拆解並在不進行重大改裝的情況下重新組裝到功能狀態。

分散式製造 Distributed Manufacturing

去中心化的製造策略，將多個製造單元部署到不同地點，通常由中央遠端 PQS 統一管理。

On-site vs. Off-site — 容易混淆的術語

PDA 文件中，On-site 指的是 MMU 最初完成完整確效的地點（通常是藥廠的固定廠房），而 Off-site 是 MMU 後續被部署去生產的地點。

請注意：這裡的「off-site」不是日常用語的「廠外」，而是相對於「最初確效地點」的「其他地點」。每個新部署地點都是 off-site，即使它也是一個完整的製藥廠。

MMU Vendor 的責任邊界

供應商（MMU Vendor）負責：

  • 組裝 MMU
  • 水系統（WFI、純水）初始確效
  • 空調系統初始確效

供應商不負責（這是藥廠的責任）：

  • 產品相關的確效（PQ、製程確效）
  • 特定藥品的製程開發和驗證

這個責任切分非常重要，關係到誰需要對產品品質負最終責任。

Q 2.1

How do current regulations impact the adoption of mobile manufacturing?

Original Text — PDA PtC-15 (pp. 14–15)

Current regulatory expectations with respect to facilities used in the manufacturing of drugs and biologics is that they are required to be designed in compliance with cGMPs. At a high level, processing should permit a smooth flow of materials through the facility. Space must be provided for receiving, quarantine, sampling, and storage of all manufacturing materials, including intermediate and finished bulk drug substance.

More specifically, facility builds or modifications need to ensure compliance with a host of regulations, such as the U.S. FDA Title 21, Code of Federal Regulations, the EMA Commission Decision 2021/1240 of 13 July 2021, and the equivalent requirements of other global regulators. Appendix A provides examples of applicable compliance expectations.

As a requirement of licensure, marketing authorization holders (MAHs) must provide information on the manufacturing locations, such as the address (and potentially GPS coordinates), that will allow the relevant regulatory agencies to identify the manufacturing location. The location needs to hold a manufacturing license and a GMP certificate. In some jurisdictions, the facility locations and specific GMP activities (e.g., analytical testing, storage, manufacturing) must be registered and assigned specific identification and status by the regulators.

For example, the FDA assigns a Firm Establishment Identifier (FEI) number to all facilities, and the identification of the facility or site is linked to an address and specific business entity (e.g., DUN & Bradstreet number). If multiple locations are listed in the dossier (i.e., the application), then this is required for each location. The requirements to file and register facility-specific information, including addresses and site contacts, are in large part linked to the regulators, enabling them to triage inspections.

In an ideal case of mobile manufacturing, the MMU could move from one physical location to another, and the license and certificate would only be required once. However, there are no provisions in current regulations for such a scenario. Moreover, in the United States, many state boards of pharmacy require registration for in-state drug manufacturers, presenting additional complexity of rapidly moving across state lines.

In addition, the movement of the MMU could create logistical challenges for regulators when making decisions about where and when they would need to deploy resources to conduct GMP inspections. Also, the full manufacturing process may be segmented in different locations (e.g., warehousing and the quality control of raw materials may be centrally located for stocking of one or a set of MMUs).

Finally, some countries have multiple agencies that would first need to agree to accept the license and certificate from another agency within their country. Typically, countries will not have provisions to accept such licenses from other agencies. The situation becomes even more convoluted and complicated if the MMU is expected to cross country borders.

重點解析 — 繁體中文導讀

核心障礙：法規是為「固定設施」而設計

現行 cGMP（現行良好製造規範）的基礎假設是：製造設施是固定在某個地址的永久建築物。從 FDA 的 21 CFR 210/211，到 EMA 的 EU GMP 指令，都圍繞著「地址綁定」的許可模式建立。

行動製造單元（MMU）的本質是「位置可變動」，這與現行法規的根本假設直接衝突，構成制度性障礙。

關鍵法規概念

  • MAH（行銷授權持有人）：持有藥品上市許可的公司，對產品品質負最終責任。
  • FEI 號碼：FDA 對每個製造設施賦予的唯一識別碼，與實體地址及企業 D&B 號碼掛鉤。MMU 每次搬移就需要新的 FEI，在實務上極為複雜。
  • 製造許可 + GMP 證書：雙重認證要求。在歐盟，GMP 證書由查核後頒發，每個地點各需一份。
  • 州藥局（State Boards of Pharmacy）：美國特有的州級監管層，MMU 跨州移動需面對 50 個不同的州法規要求。

比喻：駕照 vs. 汽車牌照

想像一個外籍廚師帶著整套廚房設備在不同城市開設快閃餐廳。他的廚師資格（技術能力）雖已獲認可，但每到一個城市都需要重新申請「餐廳營業執照」——因為執照是綁定地址的，不是綁定廚師或廚房設備的。

MMU 面臨的法規問題完全相同：GMP 認證是頒發給「這棟建築物在這個地址」，而非頒發給「這套製造系統」。

多層法規障礙清單

  • 聯邦層：FDA FEI 號碼重新申請（每次搬遷）
  • 州層：50 個州各有不同的藥局法規
  • 跨國層：EMA、PMDA、MHRA 等各自的 GMP 認證
  • 多機構問題：部分國家有多個主管機關需要協調一致才能承認許可
  • 查核資源問題：主管機關難以安排 GMP 查核，因地點不固定

這五層障礙疊加，解釋了為何 MMU 在實務中的法規路徑至今仍不清晰。

思考問題

若您的公司計畫在台灣部署一個 MMU，從台北移到台南再到高雄，您需要應對哪些法規步驟？TFDA（食藥署）目前是否有相應的行動製造框架？

Site Registration Mechanics — 設施登記機制詳解

FDA Dossier Filing Requirements (US)

  • NDA / BLA / ANDA: All marketing applications must list each manufacturing site with its physical address. Any site change requires a Prior Approval Supplement (PAS), Changes Being Effected (CBE-30), or annual report, depending on risk level.
  • Drug Establishment Registration: Required annually under 21 CFR 207. Each domestic establishment must register before manufacturing.
  • Foreign Establishment Registration: Foreign manufacturers must also register and pay fees under the PDUFA/GDUFA framework.
  • FEI Number Assignment: Linked to a specific physical address. Relocation triggers a new FEI application process.

EMA Site Change Variation Procedures (EU)

  • Type IA / IB / II Variations: Manufacturing site changes are classified as variations. A new primary manufacturing site is typically a Type II Major Variation, requiring advance approval before implementation.
  • Manufacturing Authorisation: Each EU member state issues a Manufacturing Authorisation (MA) for each site. Cross-border MMU movement requires new MAs in each country.
  • GMP Certificate: Issued after successful inspection by national competent authority (e.g., MHRA, BfArM, ANSM). A new site requires a new inspection and new certificate.
  • Annex 1 (2023): Updated sterile manufacturing guidance does not contemplate MMU scenarios.

ICH Q10 — Pharmaceutical Quality System

ICH Q10 establishes the Pharmaceutical Quality System (PQS) framework. Its site independence requirements mean:

  • Each site must have its own fully functional QMS.
  • Quality oversight, batch release authority, and deviation management must be site-specific.
  • For an MMU, replicating a complete QMS in a compact mobile unit and maintaining site independence at each new location is operationally challenging.

  • 在資源有限的移動單元內維持「可查核」的完整 QMS，是技術與組織上的重大挑戰。

Q 2.2

What actions can global regulators take to help facilitate mobile manufacturing?

Original Text — PDA PtC-15 (pp. 15–16)

Regulators can facilitate mobile manufacturing both by adjusting regulatory/administrative procedures and modifying validation and qualification expectations.

A prime example of where regulatory requirements could be revisited is the FDA approach to the FEI number. It would not be practical to give each MMU an FEI number: there would be too many units in circulation and the MMU manufacturer would need to reapply for a new one each time an MMU relocated, potentially every month. Not only would it be burdensome for the manufacturer but for FDA, with little public health benefit.

Alternatively, an approach could be to have an FEI number for the site where the MMUs are produced and then have that site's Quality Unit track the unit numbers from there. This would ensure the traceability expected by regulators, and regulators could revisit the concept of 100% "facility" inspection. If the MMU was viewed as analogous with a "facility," as set forth in 21 CFR 201.1, it would be impractical — a strict interpretation may require that each and every mobile unit be inspected after they are produced.

Novel validation and qualification approaches would also ease otherwise insurmountable regulatory burdens. For example, implementing a "family approach" towards validation and qualification of MMUs would streamline mobile unit qualification, without lowering quality standards. In this approach, once one unit is qualified and validated, identical follow-on units would be subject to reduced testing.

As another streamlining concept, the bulk release of raw materials or water could take place at one mobile manufacturing site, with portions of the pre-released material distributed to each unit. This would avoid unit-specific testing each time an MMU is made and stocked with material.

Recent draft guidances issued by the FDA on the Platform Technology Designation Program and the Advanced Manufacturing Technologies Designation Program potentially offer a new opportunity for MMUs. Specifically, these draft guidances propose that once a platform technology has been designated as such, it may be leveraged for other new or future products from the same sponsor or if a full right of reference for the same platform technology is provided to a third party.

As currently written, these draft guidances are limited to technology incorporated into a drug or biological product which is essential to its structure or function. Expansion of the guidances, or additional guidance to include the methods of manufacturing and the production platforms themselves, could prove useful to accelerate this paradigm.

The designation of a platform manufacturing technology should be considered early in development during the clinical phase. Other regulators, including Health Canada and the MHRA, are creating frameworks for advanced therapeutic products that take into account scientific and technological advances leading to distributed and point-of-care manufacturing, both of which could take advantage of mobile manufacturing facilities.

重點解析 — 繁體中文導讀

主管機關可採取的兩大行動方向

PDA 明確指出，監管機關可以從兩個維度推進：

  • 行政/程序面：修改 FEI 號碼制度、允許設施許可「跟著系統走」而非「綁定地址」
  • 技術/確效面：引入「家族法」（Family Approach）減少重複確效，允許原料/水系統集中批次放行

FEI 號碼改革提案解析

現行問題：FEI = 地址綁定 → MMU 搬移 = 新 FEI 申請

PDA 提議的替代方案：

  • FEI 號碼僅頒發給「MMU 製造商（生產 MMU 的工廠）」
  • 製造商的品質部門負責追蹤各個 MMU 的機台序號
  • FDA 可以查核「製造 MMU 的設施」而非每一台 MMU 本身

這個提案的本質是：將查核焦點從「地點」轉移到「系統設計與製造品質」。

比喻：汽車型式認證 vs. 逐輛查核

一輛特定型號的汽車（例如 Toyota Corolla 2026），不需要每一輛都重新通過碰撞測試——只需要「該型號」通過認證，之後每輛出廠車只需進行標準出廠檢查即可。

「Family Approach」的邏輯完全相同：MMU 型號認證一次，後續同型機台只需減量確效（Reduced Testing），而非從頭開始。

FDA 平台技術認定計畫

FDA 的兩個新方向對 MMU 業者有潛在利益：

  • Platform Technology Designation：平台技術一旦被認定，可跨產品複用，減少重複審查
  • Advanced Manufacturing Technologies (AMT) Designation：新興製造技術可申請專案認定，取得 FDA 的主動技術支援

目前這兩個計畫仍聚焦在「藥物本身的核心技術」，尚未擴展至「製造平台本身」。PDA 建議 FDA 擴大適用範圍，讓 MMU 製造平台本身也能被認定為「平台技術」。

業界倡議管道

PDA 及 ISPE 等行業協會在推動 MMU 法規框架方面扮演關鍵角色：

  • Pre-submission Meeting（預提交會議）：MMU 首創性專案應主動與 FDA 召開會議，提前釐清申報策略
  • Emerging Technology Program (ETP)：FDA 設有新興技術計畫，鼓勵創新製造技術業者提前溝通
  • MHRA / Health Canada：英國和加拿大已針對先進治療產品（ATMPs）建立更靈活的框架，可作為 MMU 的法規突破口

CDMO 法規策略建議 — 行動製造模組化布局

對於計畫導入 MMU 的 CDMO 而言，現階段最可行的法規策略包括：

  • 提前接觸監管機關：在 MMU 設計階段即安排 Pre-Sub 或 INTERACT 會議（FDA），明確申報路徑。
  • 選擇法規友善的先行市場：英國（MHRA）和加拿大（Health Canada）目前法規框架較有彈性，可作為 MMU 首次部署的試點市場。
  • 建立平台認定策略：若 MMU 採用連續製造或其他新興技術，應積極申請 FDA AMT Designation，累積法規資本。
  • Family Approach 文件架構：從第一台 MMU 起就建立「標準機台確效包（Validation Master Package）」，為後續同型機台鋪路。
  • 集中 QMS 架構：設計雲端化、可移動的 QMS 基礎架構，確保每個地點都能即時符合 ICH Q10 要求。

法規行動摘要 — 重點解析

兩軌並行的法規推進策略

從業者角度看，法規推進需要同時在兩個軌道上運作：

  • 短期（現行框架內）：利用 FDA Emerging Technology Program、Pre-Sub 會議取得個案指引；選擇已有彈性框架的轄區（英國、加拿大）作為先行部署點。
  • 中長期（框架改革）：透過 PDA、ISPE 等組織的政策倡議，推動 FEI 改革、Family Approach 官方指引、ICH 跨區確效指南的制定。

法規監管風險警示

在任何一個 MMU 項目中，必須注意以下申報策略陷阱：

  • 不要假設「一國批準 = 跨國通用」——每個監管轄區必須獨立評估。
  • 不要在未取得主管機關同意前即進行跨州/跨國搬遷——可能觸發 Consent Decree 或停產令。
  • 不要低估 Type II Variation（EMA）的審查時間——平均 60–90 天，部分案例超過 12 個月。

TOPIC 3

Implementation — 實施

Q 3.1

How would one validate a mobile manufacturing unit at a new location?

Original Text — PDA PtC-15 (p. 16)

Assuming the initial validation was fully conducted at the initially qualified site, validation of an MMU should be done in stages, with as many elements completed at the initially qualified site as possible (see Question 1.3). The initial stage occurs at the manufacturing site of the MMU (vendor), where critical systems like water and air are qualified via installation qualification/operational qualification (IQ/OQ). Mobility over various terrains would be part of this exercise as well.

Some systems could be tested at the initially qualified site of the receiving company (i.e., the drug manufacturer). For example, testing and qualification of raw materials should occur at the receiving company's fixed site, as could the initial process validation. These systems would need to be further qualified in field testing, through performance qualification (PQ) or confirmatory runs and, potentially, process validation.

This is analogous to "static" and "dynamic" testing of air systems where use in the field would be analogous to "dynamic" testing. In the case of process validation (PV), if the MMU is intended for multiproduct use, it may be challenging to perform PV for every potential product. The choice of the model process for PV should consider its complexity and robustness in order to cover a range of product types.

Key Validation Elements from Q1.3

Building on Q1.3, elements to address for MMU validation include:

  • Identify the need for an MMU and the initially qualified site
  • Determine what unit operations will be performed
  • List systems requiring qualification: utilities, manufacturing systems, computer systems, servers, access control, unit operation validation, method validation, cleaning validation, shipping validation
  • Describe procurement strategy and identify training requirements
  • Define quality oversight structure

重點解析 — 繁體中文導讀

分段確效的核心邏輯

MMU 確效的基本原則是：盡可能在控制環境中提前完成確效，最小化在新地點現場重做的範圍。

這個原則反映了風險管理思維：越早完成確效，越能在受控條件下取得高品質數據；現場確效（Field Testing）應聚焦在「地點特定的不確定因素」。

比喻：靜態測試 vs. 動態測試

PDA 使用空調系統的靜態/動態測試作為比喻，這是製藥人熟悉的概念：

  • 靜態測試（At-rest）：在無人員、無操作活動的條件下測試潔淨室性能——類比於在 Vendor 廠或初始合格地點進行的 IQ/OQ。
  • 動態測試（In-operation）：在模擬生產條件下測試——類比於在實際部署地點進行的 PQ 和確認性批次。

這個比喻非常實用：靜態確效建立「基準性能」，動態確效驗證「實際操作條件下的穩健性」。

多產品 MMU 的製程驗證挑戰

若 MMU 設計為多產品使用（Multiproduct），則面臨一個實務困境：

  • 理論上，每個產品都需要完整的製程驗證（PV）。
  • 在行動單元中對所有潛在產品進行 PV 是不現實的。
  • PDA 的建議：選擇一個「最具代表性的模型製程（Model Process）」進行 PV，該製程應具備足夠的複雜性和穩健性，能夠涵蓋計畫生產的各類產品範圍。

這個策略的法規依據需要與主管機關提前討論，確認「模型製程」的選擇邏輯被接受。

Relocation Validation: Risk-Based Scope Matrix

Relocation Validation Scope Decision Matrix

FOR EACH SYSTEM AT NEW LOCATION:

Step 1: Has the system changed since last qualification?
  YES → Full requalification required
  NO  → Proceed to Step 2

Step 2: Has the environment changed?
  (new HVAC, utilities, vibration profile, humidity zone)
  YES → IQ/OQ/PQ at new site
  NO  → Proceed to Step 3

Step 3: Is this a critical system directly impacting product quality?
  YES → Minimum PQ + confirmatory batch required
  NO  → Review / Risk Assessment + reduced testing

Step 4: Are regulatory filings required for this relocation?
  YES → Coordinate with RA team before MMU moves
  NO  → Document in Change Control record

Output: Validation scope = systems in Steps 1+2+3
        Minimum document: Change Control + Risk Assessment
        Recommended: Site Validation Protocol (SVP)

Change Control for Each Relocation

Every relocation of an MMU must be captured in the Change Control system. Key documentation includes:

  • Change Control Form: Record relocation trigger, new address/site details, planned validation scope
  • Risk Assessment: Evaluate impact on product quality, utility performance, environmental conditions
  • Validation Protocol (SVP): Define scope, acceptance criteria, responsibilities
  • Regulatory Impact Assessment: Determine if regulatory filings are triggered (PAS, CBE-30, Variation)
  • Commissioning Checklist: Utility hook-ups, HVAC performance, power quality, water system flushing
  • Environmental Monitoring Plan: EM program specific to new location
  • Validation Summary Report: Post-relocation report before resuming production

搬遷確效架構 — 繁體中文解析

確效範圍決定矩陣（中文版）

對新地點的每個系統依序判斷：

問題1：系統本身是否有變動？
  是 → 需完整重新確效（Full Requalification）
  否 → 進入問題2

問題2：環境條件是否改變？
  （新HVAC、新公用設施、振動輪廓、濕度帶）
  是 → 需在新地點執行 IQ/OQ/PQ
  否 → 進入問題3

問題3：是否為直接影響產品品質的關鍵系統？
  是 → 最低限度：PQ + 確認性批次
  否 → 風險評估 + 減量測試

問題4：此次搬遷是否觸發法規申報？
  是 → 搬遷前先與法規部門協調
  否 → 納入變更管制紀錄

輸出：確效範圍 = 問題1+2+3 觸發的系統

每次搬遷的變更管制文件清單

  • 變更管制單（CCF）：記錄搬遷觸發原因、新地點資訊、計畫確效範圍
  • 風險評估（RA）：評估對產品品質、公用設施、環境的影響
  • 地點確效協議（SVP）：定義確效範圍、驗收標準、責任分工
  • 法規影響評估：判斷是否觸發 PAS / CBE-30 / Type II Variation
  • 啟動前檢查清單：公用設施接駁、HVAC 性能確認、電力品質、WFI 系統沖洗
  • 環境監測計畫（EM Plan）：針對新地點的環境監測方案
  • 確效摘要報告：恢復生產前的總結報告

持續製程確認（CPV）的角色

ICH Q10 要求在商業化製造期間進行持續製程確認（Continued Process Verification, CPV）。對 MMU 而言，每次搬遷後的 CPV 計畫應特別注意：

  • 重新建立製程控制圖的基準（Baseline）——因地點改變可能引入新的變異來源
  • 增加搬遷後前幾批的 CPV 取樣頻率
  • 確保 CPV 數據與搬遷前數據有可比較的橋接批次（Bridging Batches）

思考問題

假設您正在管理一個生產單克隆抗體原液（mAb DS）的 MMU，計畫從台灣北部移到南部的一個新廠房。以下哪些系統您認為需要完整重做 PQ？哪些只需要風險評估？

  • WFI 純水系統（全套內置於 MMU）
  • 一次性生物反應器（與批次產品接觸）
  • HVAC 系統（MMU 內建）
  • 冷凍鏈（使用廠商提供的公用設施）

Commissioning at New Site — Practical Checklist

Site Commissioning Before Qualification

Before formal qualification activities begin at the new location, a comprehensive commissioning check must confirm:

  • Utility Hook-Ups: Electrical (voltage, frequency, phase), compressed air quality, nitrogen supply, steam or hot water, drainage systems
  • Water System: If Purified Water or WFI is generated onboard the MMU, flush and sanitize the system before use; test water quality per Ph. Eur. / USP specifications
  • HVAC Performance: Verify differential pressure cascades, air change rates, temperature/humidity setpoints at the new location (climate may differ significantly)
  • Environmental Monitoring Baseline: Run EM program for a defined period before production to establish baseline particulate and microbial counts at the new site
  • Vibration / Shock Assessment: Inspect critical equipment for damage sustained during transport (especially precision instruments, filling needles, sensors)
  • Computer System Revalidation: Confirm software, network connectivity, and data integrity systems function correctly at new site

Shipping Validation for MMU Relocation

Shipping the MMU itself is a validation exercise. Key elements:

  • Define transport conditions (temperature, humidity, vibration, shock limits)
  • Use data loggers to record actual transport conditions
  • Compare actual conditions against validated transport profiles
  • Inspect all critical equipment upon arrival per SOP
  • Document any excursions and evaluate impact on system qualification status

現場啟動 — 繁體中文解析

公用設施接駁的實務重點

MMU 的公用設施接駁是搬遷後最容易出問題的環節，尤其是：

  • 電力品質：不同國家電壓（110V/220V/380V）、頻率（50Hz/60Hz）差異。即使在台灣境內，工業區電網品質也可能影響精密儀器。
  • 壓縮空氣/氮氣：需確認純度等級符合藥品製造要求（ISO 8573）。
  • 排水/廢水：製藥廢水處理要求可能因地點而異，需預先確認當地環保規定。

環境監測基準建立

每次搬遷到新地點後，必須重新建立 EM 基準：

  • 在開始生產前，執行至少 2–4 週的環境監測數據收集
  • 評估新地點的背景微生物譜（Microbiological Profile）——不同地理位置的常見污染菌種可能不同
  • 根據新地點的 EM 數據，更新警戒限度（Alert Level）和行動限度（Action Level）
  • 此步驟是 Annex 1 (2023) 的明確要求，不可省略

運輸驗證——最容易被忽略的環節

MMU 的搬遷本身就是一個「製造設施的運輸」，需要完整的運輸驗證文件：

  • 運輸期間的溫度、濕度、振動和衝擊數據（使用資料記錄器）
  • 到達後的設備目視檢查 SOP（特別是填充針、精密感測器、閥件）
  • 任何運輸條件偏差均需進行影響評估（Impact Assessment），決定是否需要額外的確效活動

CDMO 確效策略建議

為了最大化 MMU 的商業靈活性，CDMO 應在初始確效階段即建立「可攜帶的確效包（Portable Validation Package）」：

  • 標準確效主計畫（VMP）涵蓋所有預期搬遷場景
  • 預定義的「觸發條件清單（Trigger Criteria List）」——什麼情況下需要完整 PQ，什麼情況下只需橋接確效
  • 預格式化的「地點特定確效協議模板（Site-Specific VP Template）」，減少每次搬遷的準備時間
  • 電子化變更管制和確效記錄系統，確保文件可隨 MMU 移動

多轄區法規比較的核心發現

橫跨所有主要監管轄區，Appendix A 的核心發現可以歸納為一個共同主題：

所有現行 GMP 法規體系都假設製造設施是固定在某個地址的，沒有任何一個主要監管轄區有專門針對 MMU 的法規條文。

這個共識性缺口，正是 PDA PtC-15 存在的核心理由。

多轄區複雜性警示

在規劃 MMU 跨境部署時，以下幾點是最常被低估的複雜性：

  • 雙向認可問題：即使兩國之間有 MRA（相互承認協議），這通常只適用於查核報告的共享，不等同於自動接受許可。
  • 語言與文件要求：日本要求日文申請文件；中國要求中文 CTD；巴西要求葡萄牙文——語言本身就是一個時間成本。
  • 本地代理人要求：日本、中國等市場要求有本地持有人（MAH）才能申請，CDMO 單獨行動可能受限。
  • 查核時機不可預測：各國主管機關對「何時查核」有自主決定權，MMU 搬遷後可能面臨即時查核要求。

比喻：國際駕照 vs. 各國本地駕照

您持有台灣駕照，到日本旅遊時可以使用「國際駕照」在有限時間內合法駕車。但如果您要在日本長期居住並以此為業，您必須通過日本駕照考試，取得日本本地駕照。

這正是 MMU 跨境運作的現實：短期緊急部署可能獲得「臨時豁免」或緊急授權（EUL），但長期商業運作仍需在每個市場完成完整的本地授權程序。

CDMO 多轄區申請策略建議

對計畫在多個市場部署 MMU 的 CDMO，建議採用以下策略框架：

  • 分波段策略（Phased Market Entry）：第一波選擇 MHRA 和 Health Canada（法規靈活），建立 MMU 法規先例；第二波進入 FDA 轄區（利用先例數據）；第三波考慮其他地區。
  • 法規平行申請（Parallel Submissions）：與 FDA、EMA 同步進行 Pre-Sub / Scientific Advice，雖需大量資源，但可縮短 12–18 個月的時程差。
  • WHO 預認證作為橋接：在 LMIC（低中收入國家）市場，WHO 預認證可以替代大多數本地申請要求。
  • 建立法規數據庫：系統性追蹤各轄區 MMU 相關法規動態，設立季度法規情報彙報機制。

Key Takeaways for CDMO Regulatory Strategy — CDMO 法規策略核心要點

The fundamental challenge of MMU regulation is structural, not technical: every major regulatory framework globally was designed for fixed-address facilities, and there are currently zero jurisdictions with dedicated MMU regulatory pathways. The short-term strategy for any CDMO is to use existing innovation dialogue channels (FDA ETP, MHRA Innovation Office, Health Canada Pre-submission) to negotiate individual project pathways. The medium-term opportunity lies in building a "portable validation package" and using the family approach to dramatically reduce requalification burden per relocation. The long-term play is advocacy through PDA and ISPE to establish ICH-level guidance for multi-site MMU validation and a global consensus on address-independent manufacturing authorization.

MMU 法規監管的核心挑戰是結構性的，而非技術性的：全球所有主要法規框架都是為固定地址設施設計的，目前沒有任何一個轄區有 MMU 專屬法規路徑。CDMO 的短期策略是利用現有的創新對話機制（FDA ETP、MHRA 創新辦公室、Health Canada 預申請）為個別專案協商路徑。中期機會在於建立「可攜帶確效包」並運用 Family Approach 大幅降低每次搬遷的重新確效負擔。長期方向是透過 PDA、ISPE 等組織的政策倡議，推動 ICH 層級的多地點 MMU 確效指南，以及全球對「地址無關製造授權」的共識建立。
