核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Holistic Approach (整體性方法)：TR90 在此強調設備管控不是一個個獨立的檢查點，而是「設計 → 確效 → 日常管控」三者如同鏈條般環環相扣。任何一個環節的疏忽，都可能讓其他環節的努力功虧一簣。

CCS 中的設備角色：設備是 CCS 架構中「硬體防線」的核心。與人員管控 (Section 8.0)、環境管控 (Section 7.0) 並列為三大支柱。設備設計得好，可以大幅降低對人員操作和環境控制的依賴。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像蓋一棟醫院：「非整體性方法」就像只關心手術室的無菌，卻忽略了從大門到手術室的走廊、電梯、空調是否也做好了感染管控。「整體性方法」則是從建築設計階段就規劃好動線、空氣流向、清潔區域劃分，讓每個環節都互相支援，不留死角。

重點提示 Key Notes

Table 9.0-1 的核心訊息：「清潔已確效」不代表萬事大吉。確效只是在特定條件下驗證了清潔程序的有效性，但實際操作中的 hold time 超標、殘留水分等情境，都可能讓已確效的清潔程序失效。Biofilm (生物膜) 一旦形成，移除難度將大幅增加。

這正是 EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 的精神：不能僅依賴驗證結果，必須以風險為導向持續監控。

9.1 Equipment Design 9.1 設備設計

原文內容 Original Text

Process contamination risks that are not controlled by equipment design may result in additional risks that need to be mitigated by other controls, such as creation of procedures, use of sterile tools, segregation, or shorter hold times. Not putting these other controls in place could lead to higher operational costs and higher risk for the life of the product.

For example, if the decision is made during process development to leave an in-process step as manual and open, all the surrounding control elements, including the personnel controls and all environmental controls (e.g., cleaning, gowning, flow), should be intensively built up to prevent contamination. In addition, more quality control testing/monitoring will be required at that step, and EM will need to be done every time that step is performed. The CCS should be reevaluated for risk mitigation from development to commercial phase to improve the controls as manufacturing scales up.

Items to be considered during the equipment design phase of CCS include:

  • Equipment is a closed system wherever possible
  • Equipment is for sanitary use (e.g., 316SS versus a lower grade of stainless steel, avoid any parts that may generate particles)
  • Fixed probes (e.g., for active viable air and total airborne particle sampling in an isolator) locations determined by an EM risk assessment and airflow visualization studies
  • Surfaces are smooth and easy to clean
  • Equipment is designed to minimize routine human intervention in critical areas
  • Barrier equipment designed with interlocks to prevent operator mistakes and/or alert operator about component malfunction
  • Ease of access and compatibility with operator access (in isolator or RABS) during aseptic assembly, as well as when performing interventions, to prevent operators leaning over open product or components
  • Parts are designed to be sterilized as a unit to minimize connections during setup
  • Sterile pathways covered during setup, removing covers only at the end
  • Mechanical and electrical adjustments designed to be made outside the aseptic processing area
  • Are plans for pipes, ducts, and other utilities to be installed designed to not create recesses or have unsealed openings and/or surfaces that are difficult to clean?
  • Through-the-wall passages for pipes and tubing sealed or gasketed, with no openings that expose wall interiors
  • All designs result in slopes for drainage
  • Exhaust filters allow exhaust to meet area classification where it is being discharged (e.g., Grade A or B)
  • Source air for air-break filters on autoclaves or lyophilizers drawn from the cleanroom rather than from the plant area
  • Electronics are covered or provided with a wipeable surface that is resistant to disinfectants (e.g., equipment computers or keyboards)
  • Equipment can be easily protected during storage
  • Ways to measure alert/action alarm conditions for all critical operating parameters

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

設計決定成本 (Design Drives Cost)：TR90 開宗明義指出，如果設備設計階段未能有效控制污染風險，後續就必須用更多的 SOP、無菌工具、區隔措施和更短的 hold time 來彌補。這些補救措施不僅增加營運成本，也增加了產品生命週期內的風險。

Closed System (封閉系統)：設備盡可能設計為封閉系統是首要原則。封閉系統意味著產品不會暴露於外部環境，從源頭消除了微生物和粒子污染的風險。

Interlock (互鎖機制)：屏障設備上的互鎖裝置可以防止操作員犯錯（如在不當時機開門）或警告設備元件故障。這是「設備防呆」的具體體現。

比喻說明 Analogy

設備設計就像蓋房子的地基和結構。如果地基沒打好（設備設計不佳），後續就得不斷加裝警報系統、安排更多保全（更多 SOP、更多監控），但房子還是不穩。反之，地基穩固的房子（良好的設備設計），後續維護簡單、住起來安心。在製藥業，這個差異可能意味著每批次數十萬到數百萬的成本差異。

重點提示 Key Notes

Manual and Open 的代價：如果某個製程步驟選擇了「手動且開放」的設計，TR90 明確指出必須「全面強化」周圍的所有管控措施。這包括：

  • 加強人員管控（更嚴格的更衣、行為規範）
  • 加強環境管控（更高等級的清潔、氣流管理）
  • 增加品質測試/監控頻率
  • 每次執行該步驟都必須進行 EM (Environmental Monitoring，環境監測)

從開發階段到商業化階段，CCS 都應持續重新評估以改善管控。

設計查核清單重點歸納

TR90 列出的 18 項設計考量可歸納為四大原則：

  • 隔離原則：封閉系統、屏障設備、互鎖機制、減少人員介入
  • 可清潔原則：光滑表面、衛生級材料 (316SS)、排水坡度、密封貫穿口
  • 可監控原則：固定探頭位置、Alert/Action 警報條件、電子設備可擦拭
  • 可維護原則：機械/電氣調整在無菌區外進行、設備可輕鬆保護儲存

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：你的 CDMO 正在評估引進新的充填線。客戶要求的產品需要一個開放式手動加料步驟。根據 TR90 的指導，你應該：

  1. 首先評估是否可以改為封閉系統設計來消除此步驟
  2. 如果無法避免，應計算「全面強化管控」的額外成本（人員、EM、QC 測試）
  3. 將此成本回饋給客戶的報價，或建議客戶投資封閉系統以降低長期營運成本

9.2 Equipment Cleaning Validation 9.2 設備清潔驗證

原文內容 Original Text

Cleaning validation is essential, but it is also limited in its value to predict microbial control over the lifespan of equipment due to the timing and amount of validation testing and the limitations of traditional microbiological testing. Historically, cleaning validation has primarily focused on the removal of product residue and less on prevention of biofilms. Additionally, sampling techniques and culture-based microbiological testing are known to have limited detection power for a range of microorganisms.

Equipment validation is often performed on new equipment that has not been exposed to the broad range of factors that can reduce the effectiveness of cleaning. Existing equipment is revalidated on a defined schedule and/or if the validated conditions are no longer relevant (e.g., new product introduction, new equipment configuration). Factors that reduce the effectiveness of a cleaning procedure include biofilm-producing organisms, years of residue build-up, normal wear, and surface damage.

Some cleaning regimens can be effective for years until a worst-case biofilm-forming organism is introduced, for example, Arthrobacter russicus, which can develop a biofilm that is difficult to remove in a low-nutrient environment in as little as three days. PDA Technical Report 69 provides a thorough guideline on biofilm prevention, including scenarios of contamination from equipment. Practical considerations for equipment cleaning are presented in Section 15.0, Table 8. For further information on cleaning validation, refer to PDA Technical Report 29 and PDA Technical Report 49.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Cleaning Validation (清潔確效) 的侷限性：TR90 在此提出了一個很重要但常被忽視的觀點 — 清潔確效是必要的，但其預測設備整個使用壽命內微生物控制效果的能力是有限的。原因包括：

  • 確效時機和測試量有限（通常只做 3 批）
  • 傳統微生物測試方法的偵測能力有限
  • 確效通常在新設備上進行，未接觸到長期使用的各種劣化因素

Biofilm (生物膜)：微生物在設備表面形成的保護性群落結構。一旦形成，其對清潔劑和消毒劑的抵抗力會大幅增強，使常規清潔程序失效。

比喻說明 Analogy

清潔確效就像汽車的新車出廠檢測 — 新車時一切指標都完美，但你不能因此就認為這輛車永遠不需要保養。隨著使用年限增加，引擎積碳 (= residue build-up)、橡膠老化 (= surface damage)、甚至遇到特殊路況 (= worst-case biofilm organism) 都可能讓原本有效的保養程序不再足夠。Biofilm 就像水管裡的水垢 — 一旦形成就很難徹底清除，而且會越積越厚。

重點提示 Key Notes

Arthrobacter russicus 案例：TR90 特別點名這個微生物，它能在低營養環境中僅三天就形成難以去除的 biofilm。這個案例說明：

  • 你的清潔程序可能有效運行多年，直到遇到一個 worst-case 的 biofilm 形成菌
  • 不能因為「從未出過問題」就認為清潔程序永遠足夠
  • Clean hold time 的管控至關重要 — 任何殘留水分都可能成為 biofilm 的溫床

交叉參考：PDA TR69 (biofilm 預防)、PDA TR29 和 TR49 (清潔確效) 提供了更詳細的指引。

影響清潔有效性的因素矩陣

清潔有效性下降因素：
  1. Biofilm-producing organisms (生物膜生成菌)
  2. Years of residue build-up (多年殘留物累積)
  3. Normal wear (正常磨損)
  4. Surface damage (表面損傷)

風險公式概念：
  實際清潔風險 = f(設備使用年限, 產品殘留特性,
                    微生物種類, 表面狀態, hold time 管控)

  確效結論有效性 ∝ 1 / (設備年齡 + 累積使用次數)

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠是否有針對設備表面狀態進行定期視覺檢查的 SOP？如果設備使用超過 5 年，你會如何調整清潔確效策略？
  2. 假設你的環境監測發現了 Arthrobacter 屬微生物，你應該啟動哪些調查和因應措施？
  3. 傳統清潔確效主要聚焦於產品殘留物去除，請說明為什麼這不足以預防 biofilm 形成。
9.3 Equipment Ongoing Control 9.3 設備持續管制

原文內容 Original Text

While equipment can be well designed and validated, routine and ongoing control of equipment during use is also an important consideration in the CCS strategy. Equipment that is not controlled effectively can contaminate the product, harbor biofilms of microorganisms and act as a reservoir for microbial contamination, which can be directly introduced into the process stream.

Contamination control considerations for the control phase include:

  • Prior to use, are processing vessels and components cleaned and sanitized? How is the clean status of equipment identified and verified? Is the cleaning/sanitization status readily identified with labels/tags on each piece of equipment? How do operators know the clean hold time of the equipment?
  • Are sterilized holding tanks and any contained liquids held under positive pressure or appropriately sealed to prevent microbial contamination?
  • Is sterilized equipment discarded or removed from the area when the packaging or cover is not integral, as evidenced by holes, rips, or tears or when unexpected condensate is present? Do site procedures for aseptic filling-line setup include a check for the presence of wet components and state that these should not be used?
  • Is the equipment included in the preventive maintenance program? Does the program include elastomer management and replacement?
  • Has the time interval between the washing, drying, and sterilization of components, containers, and equipment been qualified, as well as the time interval between their sterilization and use?
  • Is the equipment visually confirmed to be dry prior to wrapping or covering?
  • Are appropriate protective coverings applied to items for sterilization/depyrogenation so that sterility is maintained post-sterilization?
  • Is sterilized equipment clearly differentiated from nonsterilized items (e.g., color-change indicator)?
  • Are presterilized materials that cannot be sterilized via passage into the Grade A space (e.g., via double-door autoclave, dry heat oven) double-wrapped, at a minimum, allowing layer removal when transferred into the clean areas? Are sterile surfaces exposed only in the critical area?
  • Is dirty equipment removed as soon as possible and cleaned within the dirty equipment hold time?
  • Are doors to RABS open only when necessary and for the shortest duration? Is there a validated recovery time set for airflows?
  • Is there a transfer plan in place to ensure equipment storage does not result in the introduction of contamination when returning it to the manufacturing area?

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Reservoir for Microbial Contamination (微生物污染儲存庫)：設備如果管控不善，不僅會成為一次性的污染源，更會成為持續性的微生物「巢穴」。Biofilm 一旦在設備內壁或管路中形成，就會持續釋放微生物進入製程流中，成為反覆出現的污染根源。

Clean Hold Time vs. Dirty Hold Time：

  • Clean Hold Time (潔淨保持時間)：設備清潔/滅菌後到使用前的最大允許放置時間
  • Dirty Hold Time (髒污保持時間)：設備使用完畢後到開始清潔前的最大允許放置時間

兩者都必須經過確效，因為超過任何一個時間限制都可能導致微生物增殖或 biofilm 形成。

比喻說明 Analogy

設備的持續管控就像家裡的冰箱管理：

  • Clean hold time = 食物的保存期限標籤（過期就不能用）
  • Dirty hold time = 碗盤不能放在水槽太久不洗（會長黴菌）
  • Status label = 冰箱裡食物的日期標籤（清楚標示狀態）
  • Unexpected condensate = 食物包裝出現水滴（可能已經變質）
  • Color-change indicator = 雞蛋的新鮮度檢測器（一眼就知道狀態）

重點提示 Key Notes

12 項查核要點可歸納為五大管控領域：

  • 狀態管控：清潔/滅菌狀態標示、有色變色指示劑、目視確認乾燥
  • 時間管控：Clean hold time、Dirty hold time、滅菌到使用的時間間隔
  • 完整性管控：包裝破損即棄、預滅菌物料雙層包裝、RABS 門開啟控制
  • 正壓管控：已滅菌儲槽維持正壓或密封
  • 轉運管控：設備儲存與返回製造區的轉運計畫

RABS 門開啟管控

TR90 特別提到 RABS 門只能在必要時開啟，且持續時間應盡可能短，並且需要有經過確效的氣流 Recovery Time (恢復時間)。這意味著每次開門後，都需要等待氣流恢復到合格狀態。這是 EU GMP Annex 1 對 RABS 操作的核心要求之一。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：你的工廠剛完成一批充填，下一批需要等待 16 小時後才開始。操作員在收工時清潔了設備並貼上「已清潔」標籤。根據 TR90 的管控要求，你應確保：

  1. 「已清潔」標籤上包含清潔日期/時間，操作員可以對照 Clean hold time
  2. 設備在包裝/覆蓋前已視覺確認乾燥
  3. 如果 Clean hold time 在下一批開始前會超標，應重新清潔/滅菌
  4. 設備儲存區域有防止污染引入的管控措施

9.4 Special Equipment Considerations 9.4 特殊設備考量

原文內容 Original Text

Section 15.0, Table 1 provides a concise overview of the items discussed in Section 9.4.

9.4.1 Use of Single-Use Equipment

Single-use bags and product flow paths are increasingly being incorporated into manufacturing processes because they can provide a closed-system manufacturing environment. For example, they can be used for raw materials and sterile drug substance and as part of the sterile filtration process to drug product. These systems may be irradiated and designed to allow processing to occur without exposure to the external environment. Checking the sterilization cycles for this equipment upon receipt is very important (10). Observations for leaks or holes before and during use is very important. These systems are often linked with tube welders to connect different single-use systems (SUS) without breaching the sterile product pathway. It is essential the welders are used properly with the correct technique and maintenance (correct blades, wafers, etc. changed at appropriate intervals) and welds inspected prior to use. The risk to the process from this equipment is that leak/integrity testing often cannot be performed prior to and after use. Therefore, vendors for this equipment work to develop such safeguards to reduce these risks. More information on SUS can be found in PDA Technical Report No. 66: Application of Single-Use Systems in Pharmaceutical Manufacturing.

Single-use rapid-transfer ports can be used to dock isolators for the transfer of sterile materials. For example, stoppers may be sterilized directly into these single-use containers and subsequently transferred into a Grade A isolator.

9.4.2 Use of Barrier Technology

Barrier separation technology is preferred for use in a CCS for operations that are not fully closed, as it is increasingly important to separate the operator from the process. The use of isolators and RABS units support this practice, as interventions occur using special RABS or isolator gloves integrated within the system. The design of such equipment is important; for instance, the gloves should be placed appropriately where the interventions to be performed do not require the operator to reach over sterile product or components. A glove test strategy, combining visual and physical leak tests, should be defined for isolators and RABS. Where RABS are used, the timing, frequency, and duration of door-openings are restricted to limit risk of contamination.

9.4.3 Product Pathways

The product pathway is the environment in which the product is exposed within the system; it may include piping, vessels, and other equipment. Assessment of the control of contamination includes a closure risk analysis of the product pathway. Closed systems offer the lowest risk. Any open steps in the product pathway should be evaluated for improvements that may reduce or eliminate the risk. This may involve the use of SUS or rapid transfer ports, sterile-to-sterile connections, or equipment that is designed for closed-system dispensing and sampling.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Single-Use Systems, SUS (一次性使用系統)：SUS 的最大優勢在於提供封閉式製造環境，消除了 CIP/SIP 的需求以及交叉污染的風險。但 TR90 也指出其核心風險 — 通常無法在使用前和使用後進行 leak/integrity testing。

Tube Welder (管路焊接機)：用於在不破壞無菌產品路徑的前提下連接不同的 SUS。焊接品質直接影響產品路徑的完整性，因此刀片/晶片的定期更換和焊接接頭的使用前檢查至關重要。

Barrier Separation Technology (屏障隔離技術)：對於無法完全封閉的操作，isolator (隔離器) 和 RABS (限制進出屏障系統) 是 CCS 的首選方案。核心目的是將操作員與製程分離。

Product Pathway (產品路徑)：產品在系統中暴露的環境。進行 closure risk analysis (封閉性風險分析) 是評估產品路徑污染風險的關鍵方法。

比喻說明 Analogy

SUS = 免洗餐具：就像餐廳使用免洗餐具可以消除洗碗不乾淨的風險，但你必須確保包裝是完整的（沒有破洞），而且要在使用前檢查。SUS 的挑戰在於 — 有些「包裝」你無法在使用前完全測試其密封性。

Barrier Technology = 太空站的操作手套：太空人透過太空站的專用手套箱操作危險材料，手套的位置必須設計在合理的操作角度，避免太空人必須「彎腰越過」操作對象。同樣，isolator/RABS 的手套位置設計必須避免操作員需要跨越無菌產品或零件。

重點提示 Key Notes

SUS 的關鍵管控點：

  • 接收時確認滅菌週期資料
  • 使用前和使用中觀察是否有洩漏或破洞
  • Tube welder 的正確使用技術和維護
  • 焊接接頭在使用前必須檢查

Glove Test Strategy (手套測試策略)：對於 isolator 和 RABS，必須定義結合目視檢查和物理洩漏測試的手套測試策略。手套是屏障系統中最脆弱的環節，任何針孔洩漏都可能破壞無菌環境。

Product Pathway 風險層級：Closed system (最低風險) > Sterile-to-sterile connection > SUS/RTP > Open step (最高風險)。任何開放步驟都應評估是否可以改善為更低風險的選項。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：你的工廠使用 SUS 進行藥液配製，並透過 tube welder 連接到無菌過濾系統。最近發生了一次微生物偏差，調查顯示焊接接頭處有微小滲漏。你應該：

  1. 立即確認 tube welder 的維護紀錄（刀片/晶片是否按時更換）
  2. 檢討焊接操作 SOP 和操作員訓練紀錄
  3. 評估是否增加焊接接頭的使用前目視/物理檢查頻率
  4. 考慮與供應商合作開發使用前完整性測試方法

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠使用 isolator 還是 RABS？請比較兩者在操作彈性和污染風險方面的差異。
  2. 在產品路徑的 closure risk analysis 中，你會如何評估一個「每批需要手動取樣」的步驟？提出至少兩個降低風險的改善方案。
  3. 為什麼 TR90 強調 glove 的位置設計不應要求操作員跨越無菌產品？這與 Section 8.0 的人員管控有什麼關聯？
9.5 Maintenance 9.5 維護保養

原文內容 Original Text

Regular equipment maintenance is the key to preventing failures of the cleanroom environmental control systems (HVAC) and production equipment and ensuring the cleanrooms and equipment are operating in their validated state. Maintenance is a risk management practice used to maximize production and minimize loss and waste (see Section 15.0, Table 5). Section 17.0: Appendix III contains case studies of contamination related to the lack of adequate maintenance.

Maintenance activities can also pose contamination risks if not done correctly, starting with making appropriate design choices that reduce the amount of, or increase the ease of, maintenance based on failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA). The best design practice is to build a gray space adjacent to the classified areas that allows access to the equipment to minimize maintenance and calibration personnel working in the classified areas. For example, autoclave, dry heat tunnels, or ovens could be installed in a manner that provides remote access to the equipment spaces. These gray spaces may comprise adjacent corridor access spaces or an interstitial space above the equipment and systems.

Maintenance sometimes involves opening areas that are not cleaned routinely (e.g., behind HEPA filters, electrical panels). If performed during operations, maintenance activities may increase the risk of product, environment, and equipment contamination. Routine maintenance reduces unexpected failures which, in turn, reduces the risk of contamination. Developing a control strategy for preventive and corrective maintenance is important to ensure that the validated state, product sterility, or contamination control is not compromised. The design of the equipment (single-use disposable versus multiuse) and the validated cleaning, disinfection, and sterilization processes, in conjunction with a comprehensive preventive maintenance plan, are critical components of the microbial control strategy.

If certain sections of the cleanroom need to be segregated for maintenance, temporary gray space enclosures (TGSE) can be considered instead of shutting down the entire cleanroom. TGSE requires careful planning and specific procedures to ensure controls are in place and being followed by maintenance personnel and contractors. Planning needs to define traffic patterns into and out of the TGSE area, include monitoring for pressure differential to ensure the enclosure is maintained at a pressure lower than the surrounding classified areas, and provide specific instructions to personnel for gowning and degowning as they enter and exit the TGSE area. The frequency and type of cleaning and sanitization activities performed around the TGSE could be increased to control contamination that may be associated with the TGSE and activities occurring there. Section 16.3 discusses a related case study.

Minimizing traffic in and around the TGSE area during operations is also important. Heightened EM around this area should be considered to provide information about whether the containment process is working as planned. Environmental data, such as airborne monitoring, that shows increased levels in the adjacent classified areas would indicate that the containment, personnel pathways, and gowning enhancements should be reassessed to improve the contamination control. Prior to setting up a maintenance TGSE, a process should be established to recover the area upon completion of those activities. This would typically include cleaning to remove any construction or gross contamination that may have occurred, followed by a comprehensive disinfection process to reestablish the environmental control. Depending on the specific maintenance or contamination activity, a specific cleaning and disinfection plan should be considered before releasing the areas for routine operation. For standard shut-down situations, it is common practice to perform a triple-cleaning and disinfection cycle followed by EM before releasing the areas for routine operation.

Devising a comprehensive preventive maintenance plan for parts that are reused, including elastomeric components (e.g., hoses, diaphragms, gaskets, O-rings), probes, agitators, valves, and filters, is crucial. Routine visual inspection of surfaces, including product contact surfaces, should be performed; such a program enables the early detection of rouging or any other deterioration of process equipment, which can prevent particulate contamination in the product and avoid the loss of cleaning effectiveness. The design of piping and valves should prevent steam condensate from collecting and possible backflow that might lead to contamination. Continuous assessments of change control, work orders, and other process improvements should be conducted to ensure that their individual or cumulative effects do not impact the CCS.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Maintenance (維護) 的雙面性：TR90 揭示了維護活動的矛盾本質 — 定期維護可以預防設備故障和污染，但維護活動本身如果管控不當，也可能引入污染。這就是為什麼維護策略必須與 CCS 整合。

Gray Space (灰色空間)：建在潔淨區旁邊的非分級區域，讓維護人員可以從後方接觸設備，而不需要進入潔淨區。這是「設計消除風險」的最佳實踐。

TGSE — Temporary Gray Space Enclosure (臨時灰色空間圍封)：當潔淨室的部分區域需要進行維護時，可以建立臨時圍封而不是關閉整個潔淨室。這是在「維護需求」和「生產連續性」之間的平衡方案。

Rouging (紅鏽)：不鏽鋼設備表面因長期使用而產生的鐵氧化物沉積。會導致粒子污染並降低清潔效果。

比喻說明 Analogy

TGSE = 餐廳裝修時的臨時隔板：想像一家仍在營業的餐廳需要翻修廚房的一角。餐廳不會整間關門，而是用臨時隔板將施工區域與用餐區和其他廚房區域隔開。但這需要：

  • 確保隔板密封良好（= 壓差監測）
  • 施工人員有專用進出通道（= 定義交通動線）
  • 施工人員穿戴防護裝備（= 更衣程序）
  • 隔板周圍加強清潔（= 增加清潔消毒頻率）
  • 施工完成後徹底清潔消毒才能恢復使用（= triple-cleaning cycle + EM）

重點提示 Key Notes

TGSE 管控要點清單：

  • 定義人員進出動線
  • 監測壓差（TGSE 區域壓力必須低於周圍分級區域）
  • 專用更衣/脫衣程序
  • 增加周圍清潔消毒頻率
  • 加強 EM 監測
  • 最小化 TGSE 周圍的人員流動
  • 建立區域恢復程序

Triple-Cleaning and Disinfection Cycle (三次清潔消毒循環)：標準停機恢復做法。這不是一般的「清潔三次」，而是完整的「清潔 + 消毒」循環重複三次，之後還需要 EM 確認環境恢復合格才能放行。

彈性體零件管理

TR90 特別強調 Elastomeric Components (彈性體零件)（墊圈、O-ring、隔膜、軟管等）的預防性維護計畫。這些零件是設備中最容易老化、劣化的部分，一旦失去密封功能就會成為污染入口。定期目視檢查產品接觸面可以早期偵測 rouging 或其他劣化，避免粒子污染和清潔效果下降。

維護策略的風險平衡

維護策略考量：

  不做維護的風險：
    設備故障 → 非計畫停機 → 污染風險↑ → 產品損失

  維護本身的風險：
    開啟非常規區域 → 粒子/微生物釋放 → 環境污染

  最佳平衡點：
    預防性維護 (PM) > 矯正性維護 (CM)
    Gray space 設計 > 潔淨區內維護
    TGSE 區域隔離 > 整間潔淨室停機

  恢復標準：
    Triple clean/disinfect → EM 確認 → 放行

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：你的潔淨室 B 級區的一台 HVAC 風機需要緊急維修，但目前有兩條充填線正在進行商業批次生產。你決定使用 TGSE 方案。根據 TR90，你的行動計畫應包括：

  1. 劃定 TGSE 範圍並安裝臨時圍封，確保壓差低於周圍 B 級區
  2. 制定維護人員專用進出動線和更衣程序
  3. 在 TGSE 周圍增加 EM 取樣點和取樣頻率
  4. 增加 TGSE 周圍的清潔消毒頻率
  5. 維修完成後執行 triple-cleaning cycle + EM 確認合格後才能放行該區域
  6. 所有 EM 數據納入批次紀錄審查

  2. 你的彈性體零件（O-ring、墊圈等）的更換頻率是如何決定的？是基於時間、使用次數還是狀態檢查？
  3. 如果 TGSE 周圍的 EM 數據顯示微生物水準上升，你應該啟動哪些因應措施？
  4. 請解釋為什麼 TR90 要求「持續評估變更管控、工單和其他製程改善的個別或累積效果」對 CCS 的影響。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 整體性方法 (Holistic Approach)：設備的「設計 → 確效 → 持續管控」必須作為一個完整的鏈條來規劃，三者環環相扣、缺一不可。「已確效」不等於「永遠有效」。
  • 設計決定成本：設備設計階段的投資可以大幅降低後續營運成本和風險。封閉系統、衛生級設計、互鎖機制、Gray space 等設計決策對 CCS 的長期成效影響深遠。
  • 清潔確效的侷限性：清潔確效的預測能力受限於測試時機、數量和方法。Biofilm（如 Arthrobacter russicus 可在 3 天內形成）是長期威脅。Clean hold time 管控是關鍵防線。
  • 持續管控五大領域：狀態管控、時間管控、完整性管控、正壓管控、轉運管控。每一項都有具體的查核要點。
  • SUS 與屏障技術：SUS 提供封閉系統優勢但 integrity testing 受限；Barrier technology (Isolator/RABS) 是非封閉操作的首選；Product pathway 需進行 closure risk analysis。
  • 維護的雙面性：維護既是預防污染的手段，也是潛在的污染來源。TGSE 是平衡維護需求與生產連續性的有效工具，但需要嚴格的壓差、動線、EM 管控，以及 triple-cleaning cycle 恢復程序。
  • 彈性體管理：O-ring、墊圈等彈性體零件是設備密封的薄弱環節，需要納入預防性維護計畫。Rouging 等表面劣化的早期偵測可避免粒子污染。
