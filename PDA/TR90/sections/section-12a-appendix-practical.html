導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Contamination Control Strategy, CCS (污染控制策略)：這是 EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 所強調的核心文件，要求製造商以全面性、系統性的方式識別和管控所有污染來源。本附錄將 CCS 的各項要素以實務表格呈現，是建構 CCS 文件的最佳參考範本。

重點提示

本附錄共包含 Tables 15.0-1 至 15.0-8（原文提及至 15.0-10，此處涵蓋前八張表），每張表針對 CCS 的一個特定面向提供實務指引，並在最後列出「預防污染的效益」(Benefit to Preventing Contamination)。閱讀時建議先看效益說明，再回頭理解各項實務要求的邏輯。

Table 15.0-1: Structure of the Manufacturing Areas

原文內容 Original Text

Table 15.0-1 — Structure of the Manufacturing Areas
Closed Processes
  • Closed systems are a physical barrier between the environment and materials in a manufacturing process
  • Closed process provides protection from the introduction of contamination from the environment because introduction of materials into the equipment is restricted to closed connectors
  • A closed system prevents release of inherent contaminants into the manufacturing environment
  • Inherent bioburden of the process is contained
  • Prevent interaction of humans with the product or direct product contact surfaces
  • Isolator surfaces can be decontaminated with a validated process
  • Understand sequence operation to prevent downstream contamination
Open Processes
  • Open processes rely significantly on appropriate gowning and operator training/aseptic practices/hygienic behavior to protect process from human originating contaminates
  • Multiple barriers are required to protect against contamination in open manufacturing systems
  • Design of facilities should consider flows of people, product, materials, equipment, and waste to prevent contamination and cross-contamination
  • For open processes that directly impact purity of materials from contaminant or adventitious agents, use of barrier technology is preferred over a unidirectional flow unit or biosafety cabinet (BSC)
  • Appropriate use of a unidirectional flow unit or BSC is for reduced bioburden processing (or low or controlled); the purpose of the equipment is to provide Grade A quality air for critical processing steps
  • For products of biological origin, the matrix for microbial seeds or cell stocks should be sterile except for the microbe that produces the active substance or the mammalian cell line for cell stocks. It is not acceptable for contaminants or adventitious agents to be present in master seeds, working seeds, seed inoculums, or cell stocks. For sterile products, it is not acceptable for the test to be conducted in an environment at risk for environmental contamination. Thus, for manipulation of seeds/cell stocks and the conduct of sterility tests, isolator technology is the equipment of choice.
Single-Use Equipment
  • Single-use equipment simplifies cleaning validation
  • Single-use equipment ensures there is no introduction of cross-contamination of bioburden or viruses from incorrectly cleaned or sterilized equipment; cleaning would still require validation
  • Integrity of single-use disposable bag seams and connections is validated and tested
  • A widely recognized test for integrity is microbial ingress having a sensitivity of detection of 1 μm to 3 μm
  • Manufacturers can validate assembly of a disposable bag that can be released by a recognized test for integrity
Reusable Equipment
  • Reusable equipment may be economical, but it may allow introduction of bioburden or endotoxins if not correctly cleaned and sterilized or depyrogenated
  • Reusable equipment may need to be dedicated to a process; otherwise, the practice of reusing equipment should be supported by cleaning validation
  • Risk of contamination for reusable equipment is increased by equipment design (e.g., multiple openings) and manual assembly
  • Equipment should be appropriately designed to meet process needs, but also to reduce the opportunity for introduction of microorganisms into the process
  • No convenient test exists to guarantee the integrity of the final assembled apparatus; pressure-hold tests that are typically used at the point of assembly do not affirm that the system is integral
  • Requires detailed procedures and well-trained operators to reduce the risk for contamination in the absence of physical barriers
Benefit to Preventing Contamination:
  • A closed process provides containment and an engineering control against contamination in the smallest area in which the barrier of containment is in direct contact with product and requires temporal or other procedural controls.
  • In closed systems, humans, the most common source of contamination, are eliminated from direct product/process interaction.
  • Single-use equipment simplifies contamination control of bioburden and viruses or adventitious agents
  • Closed processing and/or single-use equipment are very powerful contamination control measures; an ideal end-to-end process design would include a closed system using single-use equipment from the step that removed contaminants, perhaps as early as DS, until the formulated DP is dispensed into primary packaging

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Closed Process (封閉製程)：指製程中產品與外部環境之間存在完整的物理屏障。物料進出僅透過封閉連接器 (closed connectors)，人員不直接接觸產品或產品接觸表面。這是最強大的污染控制工程措施。

Open Process (開放製程)：製程中產品暴露於周圍環境，必須依賴多重屏障（更衣、無菌操作訓練、氣流控制等）來防護。開放製程的污染風險顯著高於封閉製程。

Single-Use Equipment (一次性設備)：使用後即丟棄，消除了因清洗不徹底而導致的交叉污染風險。其完整性可透過微生物侵入測試 (microbial ingress test) 來驗證，偵測靈敏度達 1-3 μm。

比喻說明

想像封閉製程就像太空站：所有物資透過密封艙門傳入，內部與外部完全隔絕，太空人不需要擔心外面的真空環境。而開放製程則像在露天市場準備食物：必須靠帳篷、防風罩、手套、口罩等多重防護來減少灰塵和污染。

一次性設備就像免洗餐具：用完即丟，不必擔心洗碗是否洗乾淨。可重複使用設備則像餐廳瓷器：經濟實惠但必須確保每次清洗都達到衛生標準。

重點提示

本表的核心訊息是：封閉製程 + 一次性設備 = 最理想的端到端 (end-to-end) 污染控制設計。

特別注意對於生物製品：種子庫 (seed stocks) 和細胞庫 (cell stocks) 的操作，以及無菌試驗 (sterility test) 的執行，報告明確指出隔離器技術 (isolator technology) 是首選設備。這反映了 EU GMP Annex 1 對無菌測試環境的嚴格要求。

可重複使用設備的一個關鍵弱點：組裝後無法方便地驗證系統完整性。壓力保持測試 (pressure-hold test) 只是常用方法，但不能確認系統完全密封。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 設施設計時，應優先考慮從原料藥 (DS) 下游純化步驟開始，到成品 (DP) 充填封裝為止，全程採用封閉系統搭配一次性設備。這不僅降低污染風險，也大幅簡化清潔確效 (cleaning validation) 的負擔，縮短批次切換時間。

  2. 在什麼情況下，開放製程仍然是不可避免的？此時應如何建構多重屏障？

Table 15.0-2: Multiuse or Single-Product Facility

原文內容 Original Text

Table 15.0-2 — Multiuse or Single-Product Facility
Multiuse Facility
  • Facility design should consider features that prevent introduction of harmful contaminants into products at every stage of the manufacturing process
  • Segregation of areas during manufacture of biologics where adventitious and viral control of downstream material should be maintained
Single-Product Facility
  • Facility design should consider features that prevent introduction of harmful contaminants into products at every stage of the manufacturing process
  • Equipment may require segregation by process
Multiuse and Single-Product Facilities

The difference between contamination in a single-use facility versus a multiuse facility is quite subtle. It is unacceptable in either circumstance to introduce impurities inadvertently from an earlier upstream unit of operation. In both circumstances, if the intermediate or product is of an inferior nature, it is adulterated irrespective of the source of the contaminant.
Benefit to Preventing Contamination:
  • Single-product facilities minimize cross-contamination risk

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Multiuse Facility (多產品設施)：在同一設施中製造多種產品。需要特別注意交叉污染防護，尤其是生物製品中外來因子 (adventitious agents) 和病毒的控制。

Adulterated (摻雜/劣化)：當產品因為非預期的雜質引入而品質受損時，無論污染來源為何，該產品即被視為「摻雜」，在法規上屬於嚴重違規。

比喻說明

多產品設施就像一間供應多種料理的中央廚房：即使廚房很大，如果不嚴格區隔工作區域，海鮮的過敏原可能污染到素食料理。單一產品設施則像只做一種甜點的專賣店：交叉污染風險天然較低，但仍需注意不同批次間的品質控制。

重點提示

報告特別強調：無論是多產品還是單一產品設施，污染防護的基本要求是一樣的。不能因為是單一產品設施就放鬆警覺。從上游操作單元不慎引入的雜質，在任何情況下都是不可接受的。

對於生物製品的多產品設施，區域隔離 (segregation) 是確保下游物料的外來因子和病毒控制的關鍵措施。

Table 15.0-3: Storage of Materials and Equipment

原文內容 Original Text

Table 15.0-3 — Storage of Materials and Equipment
Control of Materials
  • Stored in closed/sealed protective containers
  • Dispensed in controlled/monitored areas
  • Stored in temperature- and humidity-controlled areas
  • Storage conditions prevent microbial proliferation
Control of Equipment
  • Equipment stored clean and dry and protected from the environment
  • Cleaning process validated and monitored
  • Sterilization processes validated
  • Sterile hold times defined and validated
Benefit to Preventing Contamination:
  • Materials and equipment are stored in a manner that prevents or minimizes introduction or proliferation of bioburden.
  • Sampling and dispensing areas do not add bioburden to raw materials.
  • Storage of clean equipment is qualified to ensure that bioburden or endotoxin levels do not increase.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Sterile Hold Time (無菌保持時間)：指設備經滅菌後，在使用前可以存放的最長時間。超過此時間，設備需要重新滅菌。這個時間必須經過確效 (validation) 才能確立。

Microbial Proliferation (微生物增殖)：當儲存條件（溫度、濕度、營養）適合微生物生長時，即使初始微生物負荷 (bioburden) 很低，也可能在存放期間大量增殖。

比喻說明

物料儲存管理就像家庭冰箱管理：食材必須密封保存、控制溫度、定期檢查，否則細菌會繁殖導致食物腐敗。設備的無菌保持時間就像便當的保鮮期限：過了期限就不能再食用，必須重新製作。

重點提示

取樣和分裝區域「不應增加原料的微生物負荷」，這看似簡單的要求，實際上需要嚴格的環境控制和操作程序。許多污染事件正是發生在取樣或分裝環節。

已清潔設備的儲存也需要確效：確認在儲存期間微生物負荷和內毒素水平不會增加。這通常涉及定期監測和定義最長儲存時間。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼儲存條件需要同時控制溫度和濕度？高濕度環境會帶來哪些微生物風險？
  2. 如果一台已滅菌的設備超過了驗證的無菌保持時間，應該採取什麼措施？

Table 15.0-4: Path of Materials, Equipment, or Waste

原文內容 Original Text

Table 15.0-4 — Path of Materials, Equipment, or Waste
Materials and/or Equipment
  • Material air locks separate from personnel air locks
  • Qualified disinfection practices (including use of sporicides where appropriate) for transfer into controlled/classified areas
  • Shedding layers of packaging as items are transferred into controlled/classified areas
  • Unidirectional flow
  • Prohibited entry of packaging materials that cannot be disinfected and can shed particles and microbes into the facility (e.g., cardboard, wood, open-cell foam)
  • Material never contacts the floor
  • During transfer across any classification border, carts, including the wheels, should be disinfected. Where possible, carts should be dedicated to one classification zone to further minimize the potential of contamination.
Path of Waste
  • Dedicated air locks for removal of waste materials
  • Use of pass-through autoclaves from decontamination of waste, placed in locations that minimize unnecessary exposure to the waste
  • Protection of the environment from waste contents
  • Waste path that does not overlap with incoming personnel or materials
  • Unidirectional flow
  • Removal of waste that is exiting the classified areas as soon as possible and without it contacting clean items
Benefit to Preventing Contamination:
  • Understanding the movement of incoming materials and waste facilitates prevents the introduction of bioburden, viruses, or adventitious agents.
  • Separate air locks for personnel and equipment prevents shedding of bioburden onto materials or equipment moved into the facility.
  • These concepts are control measures that prevent introduction of adventitious and viral agents into downstream processes.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Unidirectional Flow (單向流)：物料、人員和廢棄物均依照單一方向移動，避免清潔物品與污染物品的路徑交叉。這是設施設計的基本原則。

Sporicide (芽孢殺滅劑)：能夠殺滅細菌芽孢的消毒劑。一般消毒劑可能無法有效殺滅芽孢，因此在需要高度消毒的場合必須使用芽孢殺滅劑。

Pass-through Autoclave (穿越式高壓滅菌鍋)：安裝在潔淨區與非潔淨區之間牆壁上的滅菌設備，廢棄物從潔淨側裝入、滅菌後從非潔淨側取出，避免污染路徑逆行。

比喻說明

物料動線管理就像機場安檢流程：旅客和行李各有專屬通道；進入管制區前必須層層檢查（脫去外包裝 = 脫鞋過安檢）；垃圾有專門的出口，絕不能逆行回到候機大廳。紙箱、木材等不能消毒的包裝材料就像禁止攜帶的危險物品，絕對不允許進入潔淨區。

重點提示

幾個容易被忽略的實務要點：

  • 物料絕不接觸地面：地面是微生物負荷最高的表面
  • 推車的輪子也要消毒：輪子接觸地面，是污染傳播的重要途徑
  • 推車最好專用於特定潔淨度等級區域：進一步降低跨區域污染風險
  • 人員氣鎖與物料氣鎖分開：人是最大的污染源，必須與物料路徑隔離

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 設施設計審查 (design review) 中，應繪製完整的物料流、人員流和廢棄物流平面圖，確認三者路徑不交叉。每個分級邊界 (classification border) 的傳遞程序都應書面化並包含消毒步驟。

Table 15.0-5: Maintenance

原文內容 Original Text

Table 15.0-5 — Maintenance
Training of Maintenance Personnel Conduct contamination control training, including behavior in the cleanroom, to ensure that those who have an impact are thoroughly familiar with and able to follow all the processes and procedures of a successful contamination control program while performing activities. This program should include employees and contractors who may need to enter controlled classified areas.
Movement and Storage of Materials
  • Store commonly used maintenance parts and tools within controlled classified environment to prevent contamination risks from frequently transferring these items from unclassified areas to classified areas
  • Stage maintenance parts in a cleanroom covered cart or on shelves
  • Clean the cart, the shelves, and the parts, including the wheels, periodically
  • Cover the parts during storage to help prevent accumulation of dust
  • Clean, dry, cap, and store all hoses appropriately
  • Bring only the items needed into the controlled, classified environment and disinfect each item; large cases of maintenance equipment are difficult to disinfect properly
Protection of Environment and Equipment during Maintenance Cover all equipment with approved cleanroom materials during system invasion repair or any parts removal or repair
Clean-up and Return to Service
  • Clean up any spills immediately during maintenance using a cleanroom-appropriate cleaning agent and a qualified disinfectant
  • Create procedures that ensure containment of maintenance activities and return to service; this program will likely include cleaning, disinfection, and EM before return to service that includes containment and return-to-service requirements commensurate with the risk.
  • (Section 17.0—Appendix III, Case Study #4 presents an example program.)
  • Select the chemical agents based on the surface and frequency on which they will be applied; for example, oxidizing sporicides lead to corrosion of certain metal surfaces and degradation of walls and floors if used frequently without any rinsing.
  • Perform a physical walk-through inspection of the area before releasing it after maintenance
Benefit to Preventing Contamination:
  • Maintenance activities are inherently risky events when nonroutine equipment and materials enter the clean areas, which can bring in contamination that overchallenges the normal control measures. Also, maintenance personnel may be inexperienced with cleanroom behavior and may be unaware of basic microbiology and how their work can interfere with contamination controls. (Section 17.0—Appendix III presents example cases of contamination after maintenance events, "Clean-up and Return to Service" item #2.)
  • Understanding the potential risks from maintenance activities is important in order to build standard procedures that address how to perform all maintenance activities in a way that reduces risk.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

EM (Environmental Monitoring，環境監測)：對潔淨區的空氣微粒、浮游菌和沉降菌進行定期取樣和監測的活動。維護完成後的環境監測是確認區域可安全恢復使用的關鍵步驟。

Return to Service (恢復使用)：維護活動完成後，需要經過清潔、消毒、環境監測等一系列驗收程序，確認環境恢復到符合要求的狀態後，才能重新投入生產使用。

比喻說明

維護作業就像在醫院手術室進行管線維修：維修工人不熟悉無菌規範，攜帶的工具可能沾有灰塵和微生物，維修過程可能產生碎屑。因此，維修前要覆蓋設備（就像手術時鋪無菌布），維修後要徹底清潔消毒並檢查環境（就像手術室終末消毒後需要環境採樣確認）。

重點提示

維護作業是污染控制中最常被低估的風險來源之一。報告明確指出：

  • 維護帶入的非常規設備和材料可能超越正常控制措施的承受能力 (overchallenge)
  • 維護人員可能對潔淨室行為規範和基礎微生物學缺乏認知
  • 承包商也必須納入培訓計畫
  • 氧化性芽孢殺滅劑會腐蝕金屬表面並損壞牆壁和地面，不能不沖洗就頻繁使用

報告參考 Section 17.0 附錄三中的案例研究 #4，建議讀者深入了解維護後污染的實際案例。

實務應用 Practical Application

建議將常用維護零件和工具預先存放在潔淨區內的專用推車上，減少頻繁從非潔淨區轉入的風險。每次維護作業前擬定作業計畫 (maintenance plan)，載明：需要的工具和零件清單、預期影響的區域、覆蓋/保護措施、清潔消毒程序、恢復使用的驗收標準（包括 EM 合格標準）。

原文內容 Original Text

Table 15.0-6 — Movement and Gowning of Personnel
Path of Incoming and Exiting Personnel
  • Personnel air locks are separate from air locks for equipment and materials
  • Air locks provide a staging area to systematically replace outer garments and gloves
  • Unidirectional flow
  • Personnel are trained in effective aseptic practices for areas in which they work and instructed to understand the impact of personnel flow on bioburden introduction
Personnel Gowning
  • Use of multiple layers of gowning, where appropriate, to protect environmental or product quality in controlled or classified areas
  • Preventing the presence of personnel in the same area with different gowning requirements, such as material air locks
  • Qualification of gowning practices where required
Benefit to Preventing Contamination:
  • Understanding the movement of personnel entering and exiting the facility will help prevent introduction of bioburden or viruses in shared areas.
  • Understanding that humans are the most common source of contamination during aseptic processing and that gowning, unidirectional flows, air locks, and aseptic practices are effective barriers between controlled areas and parking lots, common spaces, rest rooms, and other sources of contaminants will raise awareness.
  • Use of air locks to replace gowns along the path to a clean room is an important terminating point for potentially contaminated garments and gloves.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Aseptic Practices (無菌操作規範)：一套訓練有素的行為準則，確保操作人員在無菌環境中的每個動作都不會引入污染。包括正確的手部動作、物品傳遞方式、身體姿態等。

Gowning Qualification (更衣資格確認)：操作人員必須通過更衣考核，證明其穿著無菌服裝後，身體表面的微生物負荷符合規定標準。這是進入潔淨區的先決條件。

比喻說明

人員更衣流程就像進入醫院手術室的更衣程序：從停車場出發，經過公共區域，到達更衣室，逐層更換衣物（去除外套 → 換手術衣 → 戴口罩手套），每一層氣鎖就是一個「換裝站」。報告特別提到停車場、公共空間和洗手間都是污染源，就像提醒外科醫生：你身上帶著外面世界的所有微生物。

重點提示

人類是無菌製程中最常見的污染來源——這是本表最核心的訊息。所有關於更衣、氣鎖、單向流和無菌操作的要求，本質上都是為了在人員與產品之間建立有效屏障。

一個容易忽略的細節：不同更衣要求的人員不應同時出現在同一區域（例如物料氣鎖內）。這是因為更衣等級較低的人員可能會將較高級別的污染傳播到較潔淨的區域。

  2. 如何設計人員動線，確保從停車場到潔淨室的路徑中，污染風險逐步降低？

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Water Activity (水活性, aw)：衡量物料中可供微生物利用的水分量。水活性越高（接近 1.0），越適合微生物生長。乾燥粉末的水活性低，微生物不易生長；而緩衝液和培養基的水活性高，是微生物的天然培養基。

Enveloped Viruses (包膜病毒)：具有脂質外膜的病毒，對低 pH 和界面活性劑 (detergent) 處理較為敏感。常見的病毒滅活步驟（如 low pH hold 或 solvent/detergent treatment）主要針對這類病毒。

Nanofiltration (奈米過濾)：使用孔徑約 20 nm 的濾膜，可有效去除包括小型非包膜病毒在內的病毒顆粒。這是生物製品製程中重要的病毒去除步驟。

比喻說明

理解產品組成對微生物風險的影響，就像評估不同食物的變質風險：牛奶（高營養、高水活性）放在室溫下幾小時就會變質，而乾燥餅乾（低水活性）可以放很久。生物製品製程中，富含營養的培養基和緩衝液就是微生物的「美食」，必須特別注意保持時間 (hold time) 的管控。

重點提示

本表的核心理念是「了解製程中的脆弱點」(vulnerable points)。不是所有製程步驟的微生物風險都一樣高。通過分析中間體和緩衝液的組成（營養含量、pH、水活性），可以識別出哪些步驟特別容易受到微生物污染，從而針對性地建構控制措施。

特別需要注意：引入促進微生物生長的物料後，下游應立即安排微生物負荷/內毒素去除步驟（過濾、滅菌等）作為安全保障。

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析

Manual Cleaning (人工清洗)：依賴操作人員手動進行的清洗程序。與自動化 CIP (Clean-In-Place，就地清洗) 相比，人工清洗的再現性 (reproducibility) 較差，因為清洗效果取決於個人操作，很難確保每次都達到相同標準。

One-way Autoclave (單向高壓滅菌鍋)：安裝在不同潔淨度等級區域之間，物品從低潔淨側裝入、滅菌後從高潔淨側取出。這確保已滅菌物品不會與未滅菌物品交叉接觸。

比喻說明

人工清洗設備就像手洗碗盤 vs. 使用洗碗機：手洗的清潔程度因人而異，可能有些角落沒洗到；洗碗機（CIP）則每次都用相同的溫度、水壓和清潔劑，確保一致的清洗品質。這就是為什麼報告強調人工清洗「很難確保再現性」。

重點提示

清潔和滅菌流程的設計不僅是操作層面的問題，也是設施設計的問題。報告指出，設施設計時就應考慮：

  • 單向高壓滅菌鍋 (one-way autoclave) 的位置安排
  • 去熱原隧道 (depyrogenation tunnel) 的動線
  • 已滅菌設備的儲存環境等級
  • 設備的維護、校準、確效和再確認需求

這些都應在設施規劃階段就納入考量，而非事後補救。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CCS 文件中，應清楚記錄每項設備的清洗方式（人工/CIP）、清洗確效狀態、滅菌方式（高壓蒸氣/乾熱/化學）、滅菌確效參數，以及相關設備（滅菌鍋、隧道等）的維護校準計畫。人工清洗的設備應特別加強確效監測，考慮增加目視檢查和化學殘留測試的頻率。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 封閉製程 + 一次性設備是最強大的污染控制組合，理想的端到端設計應從 DS 純化步驟一直延伸到 DP 充填封裝。
  • 人類是最常見的污染源：所有關於更衣、氣鎖、人員動線的設計要求，本質上都是在建立人與產品之間的有效屏障。
  • 維護作業是高風險事件：非常規設備和材料的引入可能超越正常控制措施的承受能力，需要完善的培訓、程序和恢復使用驗收標準。
  • 物料動線管理遵循單向流原則，物料氣鎖與人員氣鎖分離，紙箱/木材等不可消毒材料禁止進入潔淨區。
  • 產品組成影響微生物風險：應分析中間體和緩衝液的營養含量、pH 和水活性，識別製程中的脆弱點，針對性配置微生物負荷去除和病毒去除步驟。
  • 清潔與滅菌不僅是操作問題，也是設施設計問題。人工清洗的再現性較差，應在設計階段就規劃單向滅菌鍋和適當的儲存環境。
  • 每張實務考量表的最後都列出「預防污染的效益」，這些效益說明為什麼需要實施各項控制措施，是撰寫 CCS 文件時的重要參考。
