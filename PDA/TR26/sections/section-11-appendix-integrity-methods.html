11.1 Bubble Point Test

原文內容 Original Text

The bubble point test is based on the premise that membrane pores can be represented as a multiplicity of capillaries, and the knowledge that liquid is held in a capillary tube due to surface tension and other factors. The minimum gas pressure required to force liquid out of the pores is a direct function of pore diameter. The bubble point test is used to determine the pressure at which there is bulk flow of the test gas through the largest pores in the membrane that are the first from which liquid is displaced. Since the observed bubble point is a function of the wetting-liquid surface tension, the contact angle, the pore shape, and the pore size, results are specific to each membrane polymer/surface chemistry, structure, wetting fluid combination, methods, equipment and surface area. While bubble point is an indication of the largest pores, it cannot be used directly to calculate their diameter. The bubble point test also does not indicate the number of largest pores, which can affect the probability of bacterial retention (if the organisms are smaller than the pores).

The test can be performed manually by subjecting a wetted filter membrane to gas pressure on the upstream side. Tubing on the membrane's downstream side is immersed in a liquid. The differential pressure at which a steady stream of bubbles, or in some cases the displacement of liquid, is observed is the bubble point. The typical test equipment for performing a manual bubble point test is illustrated in Figure 11.0-1.

The manual bubble point test is a sensitive, visual technique for disc filters and other small surface-area membrane filters; it may be performed as one of the quality assurance tests for membranes during the casting process. The bubble point test detects major and minor defects and out-of-size pores and can be correlated with the bacterial retention study. It is also a reliable integrity test of larger surface-area filters until the diffusional flow across the membrane masks the flow rate from the pores from which the wetting fluid has been displaced.

The following are points to consider when performing the bubble point test manually:

  • Use a pressure gauge with easy-to-read incremental steps and increase slowly in a step-wise manner.
  • Allow pressure to stabilize at each step in the pressure increase. The true bubble point value may be overshot if pressure is increased too rapidly. The test cannot be repeated without rewetting the membrane.
  • Minimize downstream connections and avoid kinking the downstream tubing.
  • Check for leaks in the system.
  • Clearly define the bubble point as bulk air flow. The most common error is misidentifying bubbles that are not the result of bulk flow. Some bubbles will appear because of diffusion across the wetted membrane. Free flowing air is the indication, not the first bubble.
  • Manually performed, bubble point-test determination can be subjectively different from one operator to another; therefore, operators should be properly trained to conduct the test and to interpret test results.
  • Keep upstream volume to a minimum. With extremely high upstream volumes, longer stabilization between pressure increases may be required.
  • Maintain the temperature within the specified range.

Test attributes:

  • Relatively fast, with a short stabilization time
  • Can be directly related to the membrane pore size
  • Relatively easy to perform for small-to-medium area filters
  • Easy to establish correlation between bacterial retention and physical integrity test values
  • Sensitivity may be limited for large-membrane-area systems if diffusive air flow below the bubble point is significant
  • Manual testing requires downstream (i.e., sterile side) manipulations

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Bubble Point (泡點): 在濕潤的濾膜上游加壓，當壓力足以將最大孔隙中的潤濕液推出時，氣體會以「整體流」(Bulk Flow) 方式穿過，在下游液面產生持續的氣泡流。這個壓力值就是泡點。

關鍵限制：

  • 泡點反映的是最大孔隙的大小，但無法告訴你有多少個這樣的孔隙
  • 泡點值與特定的濾膜/潤濕液組合有關，不同組合不能互相比較
  • 對大面積濾膜，擴散流的背景「噪音」可能掩蓋泡點信號

比喻說明 Analogy

泡點測試就像吹氣球的反向操作。想像你有一片海綿（濾膜），上面有許多大小不同的孔，全部灌滿了水。你從一側慢慢加壓吹氣：

  • 低壓時： 空氣透過水「滲透」過去（擴散流），只看到偶爾的小氣泡 — 這不是泡點！
  • 到達泡點： 壓力大到把最大孔中的水「吹」出來，空氣直接流過，看到持續穩定的氣泡流 — 這才是真正的泡點

最常見的操作錯誤就是把擴散產生的偶發氣泡誤認為泡點。就像分辨「水龍頭在滴水」和「水龍頭開了」的差別。

重點提示 Key Notes

「不是第一個氣泡，而是自由流動的空氣」(Free flowing air is the indication, not the first bubble)： 這是泡點測試中最重要的操作判斷。許多完整性測試失敗的調查中，都發現是操作人員過早判定泡點。

不可重複性： 一旦泡點被超過（潤濕液被排出），必須重新潤濕濾膜才能再測。這與擴散流測試不同，增加了操作的緊張性。

人員主觀性： 手動泡點測試的結果因操作員而異，所以操作員培訓和資格確認至關重要。自動化儀器可以消除這種主觀性。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景： 在 CDMO 的無菌充填 (Aseptic Filling) 產線上，泡點測試最常用於：

  • 小面積濾器驗證： 實驗室中 47mm 圓盤濾膜的品質檢測
  • PUPSIT (使用前滅菌後完整性測試)： 部分場景下作為快速過/不過判斷
  • 來料檢驗： 濾膜供應商出廠時的品質保證測試

但對於生產規模的大面積濾芯（如 10 吋 cartridge），通常優先使用擴散流或壓力衰減測試，因為大面積下擴散流的背景流量會干擾泡點判讀。

11.2 Diffusive Flow Test

原文內容 Original Text

In a wetted membrane filter under pressure, gas molecules migrate through the liquid-filled pores at differential pressures below the bubble point via a diffusion process following Fick's Law of Diffusion. The overall rate of diffusion for a filter is proportional to the applied test pressure, its membrane surface area and wetted thickness, and the solubility and diffusivity of the test gas in the wetting liquid. In small surface-area filters, such as flat-disc filters, this gas flow may be too low to be measured accurately. In large surface-area filter systems, it is significant and can be measured to perform an integrity test. To accurately measure the test limit, one must use the correct limit based on the gas type. This only applies to Diffusion/Pressure Hold test; the bubble point test is not influenced by gas type.

The diffusive flow test quantitatively measures the sum of diffusive flow through the membrane and flow through any open pores. The filter user should use the manufacturer's recommended testing method for each filter, ensuring that the method is appropriate and has been validated for the specific case. Diffusive flow measurements may be performed downstream of the wetted membrane under constant test pressure, or on the upstream side by measuring the air flow required to maintain constant test pressure. These tests are performed on membrane filters by first wetting out the filter with an appropriate liquid and draining excess fluid. The upstream side of the filter is pressurized, and the diffusive flow is measured. Test equipment for a diffusive-flow integrity test with flow measurement on the downstream side is illustrated in Figure 11.0-2. The inverted burette or measuring cylinder filled with liquid for the collection of diffused gas can be replaced with an appropriate gas flowmeter.

The following are points to consider when performing the diffusive flow test manually:

  • Minimize downstream connections and downstream volume.
  • Avoid kinking downstream tubing.
  • Check for leaks in the system.
  • Use a volume-collection vessel with the proper resolution.
  • Use downstream tubing with the smallest practical diameter and shortest practical length to ensure proper flow resolution.
  • Keep upstream volume to a minimum; with extremely high upstream volumes, proper stabilization time must be allowed.
  • Maintain a stable testing temperature throughout the testing period.
  • Allow sufficient equilibration time after pressurizing the system.
  • The test should be performed at the filter manufacturer's specified pressure.

Test attributes:

  • Developed to provide increased sensitivity for large membrane surface-area systems
  • Provides an objective, quantitative measure of gas flow
  • Can be correlated to bacterial retention (as discussed in Section 7.1)
  • May be sensitive to temperature fluctuations
  • Manual testing requires downstream (i.e., sterile side) manipulations

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Diffusive Flow Test (擴散流測試): 在泡點壓力以下的固定測試壓力下，定量測量氣體通過濕潤濾膜的總流量。這個總流量包含兩部分：

  • 擴散流 (Diffusive Flow)： 氣體溶解於潤濕液中再擴散到下游 — 正常現象
  • 開放孔隙流 (Open Pore Flow)： 如果有未被潤濕的孔隙，氣體直接穿過 — 異常，表示可能有缺陷

測量方式有兩種：

  • 下游測量 (Downstream)： 在下游收集擴散出來的氣體體積（如倒置量筒法）
  • 上游測量 (Upstream)： 測量維持恆定上游壓力所需補充的氣體流量（如質量流量計法）

比喻說明 Analogy

擴散流測試就像檢查潛水衣是否漏水。你穿著潛水衣跳入水中：

  • 正常情況： 水分子會緩慢地「滲透」過潛水衣材料 — 這就像擴散流，是材料本身的特性，量很少且可預測
  • 有破洞時： 水會快速地直接灌入 — 這就像開放孔隙的整體流，量會明顯超出正常值

擴散流測試的價值在於：它能區分「正常的材料滲透」和「異常的破洞漏水」，透過量化測量給出明確的過/不過判定。

重點提示 Key Notes

測試氣體的影響： 不同氣體（如空氣 vs 氮氣 vs CO2）在潤濕液中的溶解度和擴散係數不同，因此擴散流限值必須針對特定氣體類型設定。這一點泡點測試不受影響，因為泡點取決於表面張力而非氣體溶解性。

溫度敏感性： 溫度變化會同時影響氣體的溶解度、擴散係數和潤濕液的表面張力。即使 1-2°C 的溫度波動也可能導致測量值偏差，尤其在大面積濾膜系統中效果更明顯。

下游操作的無菌風險： 手動擴散流測試需要在下游（無菌側）進行管路連接和氣體收集。這在 PUPSIT 場景中會帶來無菌性破壞的風險，是促使業界採用自動化測試儀器的重要原因之一。

公式與計算 Formula — 擴散流與面積的關係

擴散流限值通常以 ml/min 表示

對於相同材質的濾膜：
  擴散流 ∝ 面積 (A)

例如：
  單支 10" 濾芯面積 ≈ 0.7 m²
  → 擴散流限值 ≈ X ml/min

  三支 10" 並聯
  → 總面積 = 2.1 m²
  → 擴散流限值 ≈ 3X ml/min

重要：使用者必須根據實際安裝的
濾芯數量計算正確的擴散流限值

  2. 手動測試中，為什麼要「最小化下游管路連接和體積」？過大的下游體積會如何影響測量準確性？

11.3 Pressure Hold/Decay Test

原文內容 Original Text

The pressure hold (or pressure decay) test is an indirect method of upstream diffusive flow-testing. In this method, the filter-housing is pressurized to a predetermined setting and then isolated from the pressure source. Gas flow across the membrane is quantitatively measured as a decay in pressure over a specific period of time. This test has been indirectly correlated to bacterial retention through its relationship to the diffusive flow test.

The allowable pressure drop is not published by filter manufacturers because it is unique to a given filter assembly and piping system, and it is calculated by taking into account the diffusive-flow specification at a given pressure and constant temperature, the upstream system volume, and the duration of the test. Therefore, this test requires a determination of the upstream volume of the system and a calculation of the allowable pressure drop.

The pressure hold/decay test measures the diffusive gas (typically air or nitrogen) flow through all the wetted pores, and the bulk gas flow through larger non-wetted pores, when applying a predetermined gas pressure to a wetted filter in a system that has no upstream leaks. Pressure decay on the upstream side is then measured, after isolating the incoming gas supply. Upstream integrity tests are particularly useful in critical fluid applications since they can be performed without compromising the sterility of the downstream system.

The pressure decay is a function of the gas flow through the filter and the upstream volume of the filter system. The pressure hold/decay test will confirm the integrity of the entire assembly, including housing seals. Therefore, failure analysis for lack of seal integrity is required prior to reaching the conclusion that the filter element itself is not integral.

If the pressure decay is below the maximum allowable value, the filter passes the physical integrity test. If the decay and concomitant pressure drop exceed the correlated maximum-allowable value, the filter fails the test, and an investigation should be performed (see Section 7.6).

In performing the pressure hold/decay test, the physical conditions must be held constant, as the test is affected by interactions of temperature, system volume and filter area. The pressure hold/decay test equipment is illustrated in Figure 11.0-3. This test commonly is performed preprocess, post-sterilization of the filter assembly, and again after sterilizing filtration.

The following are points to consider when performing the pressure hold/decay test:

  • There should be no leaks in the upstream side of the system.
  • Upstream volume should be determined to ensure a proper maximum-allowable-pressure drop calculation. It is recommended that upstream volume be kept to a minimum.
  • The downstream side of the filter should be vented to the atmosphere.
  • Sufficient stabilization time should be allowed before beginning the test; stabilization time increases with larger upstream volumes and increased filter surface-areas.
  • A pressure gauge or transducer with the proper degree of accuracy for determining the pressure drop during testing should be used.
  • Test time may need to be extended if the pressure-drop rate is extremely small relative to the sensitivity of the gauge or transducer.
  • The specified temperature should be maintained to ensure that the temperature does not change during the integrity test. Note that lower upstream volumes will result in increased sensitivity to temperature changes.

Test attributes:

  • Relatively easy to perform
  • No downstream manipulation is required
  • Can be correlated to bacterial retention (as discussed in Section 7.1)
  • Sensitivity of the test decreases as the upstream volume of the system is increased
  • Resolution of the test is limited by the resolution of the pressure sensor used
  • Process conditions for the test, including temperature and gas used for pressurization, should be defined for the issuance of integrity test values.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Pressure Hold/Decay Test (壓力保持/衰減測試): 這是一種間接的擴散流測試方法。核心原理很簡單：將濾膜上游加壓到指定壓力後，關閉氣源，然後觀察壓力下降的速率。

  • 為什麼壓力會下降？ 因為氣體持續通過濕潤濾膜擴散到下游（加上任何缺陷孔隙的洩漏）
  • 為什麼是「間接」？ 因為它測量的是壓力變化，而非直接測量氣體流量。需要知道上游體積才能換算成等效流量
  • 最大優勢： 不需要在下游（無菌側）進行任何操作，不會破壞下游的無菌環境

比喻說明 Analogy

壓力衰減測試就像檢查腳踏車輪胎是否漏氣：

  • 打飽氣（加壓）： 你把輪胎充到指定壓力，然後拔掉氣嘴（關閉氣源）
  • 觀察壓力表（衰減測量）： 如果 10 分鐘後壓力只掉了一點點，輪胎沒問題（通過測試）；如果壓力快速下降，表示有洩漏（測試失敗）
  • 輪胎大小的影響（上游體積）： 大卡車輪胎和小腳踏車輪胎即使漏洞一樣大，壓力下降速率完全不同 — 體積越大，同樣的洩漏造成的壓力變化越小

這就是為什麼壓力衰減的「允許值」不是一個固定數字，而必須根據每個系統的上游體積單獨計算。

重點提示 Key Notes

上游體積的雙重效應：

  • 體積越大 → 測試敏感度越低： 同樣的氣體洩漏量，在大體積系統中造成的壓力變化更小，更難被偵測到
  • 體積越大 → 穩定時間越長： 加壓後需要更長時間讓溫度和壓力達到平衡
  • 體積越小 → 溫度敏感度越高： 小體積系統中，微小的溫度變化就會造成明顯的壓力波動，可能被誤判為洩漏

整體組件測試： 壓力衰減測試檢驗的不只是濾膜本身，而是包含外殼密封、O-ring、管路接頭在內的整個過濾組件。如果測試失敗，在判定「濾膜不完整」之前，必須先排除密封洩漏的可能性。

公式與計算 Formula — 壓力衰減允許值計算

壓力衰減的基本概念：

  允許壓降 = f(擴散流規格, 上游體積, 測試時間)

概念公式：
  ΔP_允許 ≈ (F_limit × t_test × P_atm) / V_up

其中：
  F_limit = 擴散流規格限值 (ml/min)
  t_test = 測試時間 (min)
  P_atm = 大氣壓力 (mbar)
  V_up = 上游體積 (ml)

實例：
  F_limit = 15 ml/min
  t_test = 10 min
  V_up = 5000 ml
  P_atm = 1013 mbar

  ΔP_允許 ≈ (15 × 10 × 1013) / 5000
           ≈ 30.4 mbar

注意：這是簡化概念。實際計算需使用
Equation 14 的精確指數衰減模型。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景 — PUPSIT 的首選方法：

壓力衰減測試是執行 PUPSIT (使用前滅菌後完整性測試) 時最受青睞的方法，因為：

  • 完全在上游操作，不需要觸碰下游無菌側 — 維護了無菌保證 (Sterility Assurance)
  • 可以在滅菌後、過濾前執行，確認整個過濾系統（濾膜+外殼+密封）的完整性
  • 配合自動化儀器，可以消除人為判斷的主觀性

但必須注意：上游體積的準確測定是此方法的先決條件。在 CDMO 中，不同產品線可能使用不同配置的管路系統，每次變更都需要重新測定上游體積。

11.4 Automated Integrity-Test Instruments

原文內容 Original Text

Numerous automated integrity-test instruments are available for use in the pharmaceutical industry to determine a bubble-point pressure value using incremental pressure hold/decay tests. The pressure hold/decay tests are performed at increasing pressures, with the bubble point identified where the pressure decay become nonlinear, based on an algorithm specific to each instrument manufacturer. Automated integrity-test units determine diffusive-flow values, either by measuring pressure hold/decay at a specified test pressure and calculating the diffusive-flow value using a predetermined measurement of the upstream volume of the filter assembly or by direct-flow measurement using an upstream mass flow meter. Another method to determine diffusive-flow values is a system with additional small volumes to maintain constant test pressure during the test, which eliminates the need to measure upstream volume.

Irrespective of the technique used to determine filter bubble point or diffusive flow, the automated integrity-test instrument must be qualified against an independent reference method.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Automated Integrity-Test Instruments (自動化完整性測試儀器): 這些儀器將手動測試的步驟自動化，主要有三種測量方法：

  • 增量壓力衰減法： 在逐步增加的壓力下執行壓力衰減，用演算法偵測壓力衰減從線性變為非線性的轉折點，自動判定泡點
  • 計算擴散流法： 在指定壓力下測量壓力衰減，利用預先測量的上游體積計算等效擴散流值
  • 直接流量測量法 (Direct Flow)： 使用上游質量流量計直接測量維持恆壓所需的氣體流量，無需知道上游體積

比喻說明 Analogy

手動 vs 自動化完整性測試，就像用秤重天平 vs 電子秤來稱重：

  • 手動（天平）： 需要操作員技巧、判斷和經驗，結果可能因人而異
  • 自動化（電子秤）： 一鍵測量，數字直接顯示，結果客觀可重複。但電子秤也需要定期校準（qualification against reference method）

重點提示 Key Notes

儀器確認 (Qualification) 是必要的： 不論使用哪種自動化儀器，都必須經過獨立參考方法的確認。這通常包括：

  • IQ (安裝確認)： 儀器正確安裝，硬體完好
  • OQ (操作確認)： 在標準條件下使用參考標準品測試，結果符合規格
  • PQ (性能確認)： 在實際生產條件下驗證儀器性能

不同廠牌的自動化儀器使用不同的演算法，因此不能假設不同儀器的測試結果完全等效。跨儀器的比較需要額外的驗證工作。

11.5 Correlation Between Pressure Hold/Decay and Diffusive Flow

原文內容 Original Text

Diffusive flow prior to the initiation of any bulk flow through the first opened pore can be expressed by Equation 10, derived from Fick's Law and Henry's Law.

Equation 10
Vs = (M / ρS) · AF · D · (p2 - p1) · k / (Hc · L)

The measured flow resulting from a specified differential pressure can be related to the microbial retentive capability of a filter membrane.

The relationship between this flow, measured by the nondestructive pressure hold/decay test and the destructive microbial challenge test, is the objective of the validation for each filter medium and each filter configuration.

Equation 10 shows that diffusive flow is proportional to the difference between the test pressure and atmospheric pressure. Since the pressure in the upstream volume decreases, due to the effluent diffusive flow, the flow also decreases.

From the ideal gas equation,

Equation 11
p2 · Vup = N · R · T

Equation 11a — the change of state per time, dt, can be derived:
(dp2/dt) · Vup = dN/dt
Equation 12
-V · ρS = dN/dt

Equations 10 and 11a can be combined to yield:

Equation 13
-(dp2/dt) · Vup / (R · T) = M · AF · D · (p2 - p1) · k / (Hc · L)

Equation 13a
[1 / (p2 - p1)] · (-dp2) = (R · T / Vup) · (M · AF · D · k) / (Hc · L) · dt

Equation 13b — carrying out the integration between limits and simplifying:
p2I - p2II = (p2I - p1) · [1 - exp(-(R · T / Vup) · (M · AF · D · k) / (Hc · L) · (tII - tI))]

To obtain an equation for the pressure decay (as a function of volume flow in the standard state), introducing Equation 10 in Equation 13b and using pS = ρSRT/M yields:

Equation 14
p2I - p2II = (p2I - p1) · [1 - exp(-(tII - tI) / Vup · Vs · ρS / (p2I - p1))]

Measurements of pressure and time are necessary for the determination of Vs because the retentive capability of the filter membrane is related to the flow Vs, as shown in Equation 14. Additionally, the upstream volume must be known and the temperature in the upstream volume must be constant.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

壓力衰減與擴散流的數學關聯： 本節的數學推導建立了一個關鍵橋樑：如何從測量到的壓力衰減值 (p2I - p2II) 計算出等效的標準體積流量 (Vs)。

推導的邏輯鏈：

  • Equation 10： Fick 定律 + Henry 定律 → 擴散流的物理模型
  • Equation 11/11a： 理想氣體定律 → 壓力變化與莫耳數變化的關係
  • Equation 12-13： 結合上述 → 壓力衰減率的微分方程
  • Equation 13b/14： 積分求解 → 壓力衰減的指數衰減模型

最終結果（Equation 14）告訴我們：壓力衰減遵循指數衰減模式，而非簡單的線性下降。

公式與計算 Formula — Equation 14 的物理解讀

Equation 14:
ΔP = (p₂ᵢ - p₁) × [1 - exp(-t/τ)]

其中時間常數 τ 與以下因素有關：
  τ ∝ V_up / V_s

物理意義：
  - 壓力衰減遵循指數衰減，不是線性下降
  - 初期衰減較快，逐漸趨緩
  - 上游體積 (V_up) 越大，衰減越慢
  - 擴散流 (V_s) 越大，衰減越快

實務簡化：
  當衰減量遠小於初始壓差時
  (即 ΔP << p₂ᵢ - p₁)
  指數函數可近似為線性：
  ΔP ≈ V_s × ρ_S × t / V_up

這就是為什麼短時間測試中，
壓力衰減看起來幾乎是線性的。

比喻說明 Analogy

壓力衰減的指數衰減就像一杯熱咖啡的冷卻過程（牛頓冷卻定律）：

  • 剛沖好的咖啡（高壓差）→ 熱散失最快（壓力衰減率最高）
  • 隨著溫度接近室溫（壓差減小）→ 冷卻速率逐漸降低
  • 杯子越大（上游體積越大）→ 冷卻越慢（壓力衰減越慢）

在短時間內觀察，冷卻幾乎是線性的 — 這就是為什麼在實際測試中（通常只有幾分鐘），線性近似通常足夠準確。

重點提示 Key Notes

驗證的核心目標： 建立非破壞性物理測試（壓力衰減/擴散流）與破壞性微生物挑戰測試之間的相關性。這意味著：

  • 物理測試的限值必須能夠可靠地預測濾膜的微生物截留能力
  • 每種濾膜材質和配置都需要獨立建立這種相關性
  • 相關性的建立是濾膜製造商的責任，使用者需要驗證其適用性

三個必要條件： 使用 Equation 14 需要：(1) 精確的壓力和時間測量、(2) 已知的上游體積、(3) 恆定的溫度。任何一項不確定都會影響計算結果的可靠性。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景 — 驗證策略：

在 CDMO 中同時生產多種產品，過濾系統配置經常變動。理解壓力衰減與擴散流的數學關聯至關重要：

  • 新產線安裝： 需要精確測定上游體積，這通常透過已知流量的氣體源和壓力響應來計算
  • 配置變更管理： 任何管路或組件的變更都可能改變上游體積，需要重新測定和更新壓力衰減允許值
  • 跨方法等效性： 如果需要在擴散流測試和壓力衰減測試之間切換，必須透過 Equation 14 建立換算關係

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 泡點測試 (Bubble Point)： 偵測最大孔隙開啟的壓力閾值，適合小面積濾膜；手動操作具主觀性，需注意區分擴散氣泡和真正的整體流
  • 擴散流測試 (Diffusive Flow)： 定量測量氣體通過濕潤濾膜的流量，為大面積濾膜系統設計；受測試氣體類型和溫度影響
  • 壓力衰減測試 (Pressure Hold/Decay)： 間接的上游擴散流測試，不需下游操作，是 PUPSIT 的首選方法；需要精確的上游體積測定
  • 自動化儀器： 消除人為主觀性，可執行泡點和擴散流測試，但必須經過獨立參考方法的確認 (qualification)
  • 方法間的數學關聯 (Equations 10-14)： 壓力衰減遵循指數衰減模型，透過 Equation 14 可將壓力衰減值換算為等效擴散流值，前提是已知上游體積和恆定溫度
  • 驗證的終極目標： 建立非破壞性物理測試與微生物截留能力之間的可靠相關性
