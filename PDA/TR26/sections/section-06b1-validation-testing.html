細菌截留研究 — 驗證除菌過濾效能的核心
PDA Technical Report No. 26 (Revised 2025) — Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids | p44 - p48
6.6 Bacterial Retention Study 6.6 細菌截留研究

原文內容 Original Text

Bacterial retention studies are used to validate a filter to perform the sterilizing function (i.e., render the filtrate sterile) by showing complete microbial removal from the challenge fluid, product, or product family, using a representative challenge microorganism (1, 35, 36). Two primary elements are taken into consideration for bacterial retention study validation:

  1. Classification of the filter membranes as sterilizing-grade by means of bacterial retention studies, using standardized test methods (6). This is the responsibility of the filter supplier as part of the filter qualification to demonstrate that the filter retains bacteria using standardized methodology (i.e., proof that the filter retains B. diminuta when challenged at a level of ≥ 1 x 107 CFU/cm2 and can be classified as a sterilizing-grade filter in accordance with the principles of ASTM F838).
  2. Demonstration by the filter user or designated test facility (e.g., filter manufacturer or contract laboratory) of complete retention of a microbial challenge from each product or product family in the actual process conditions. Scientific rationale for each product group should be developed and may be reviewed with the appropriate regulatory agency before validation testing. It is the responsibility of the end user to confirm that the selected sterilizing-grade filter performs as expected for process-specific applications (i.e., under simulated worst-case processing conditions with the intended drug product).
"These two filter-testing concepts are not interchangeable and should be validated independently."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Bacterial Retention Study (細菌截留研究)：這是驗證除菌過濾最核心的試驗。目的是用活的微生物來挑戰濾膜，證明濾膜確實能把細菌完全擋住，讓濾液達到無菌狀態。

TR26 將細菌截留驗證清楚劃分為兩個獨立的層次：

  • 第一層：濾膜供應商的資格確認 — 使用 ASTM F838 標準方法，以 B. diminuta（短小單胞菌）在 ≥ 107 CFU/cm2 的挑戰水準下，證明該濾膜可以被歸類為「除菌級」。這就像濾膜的「畢業證書」。
  • 第二層：使用者的製程驗證 — 針對實際的藥品或產品系列，在模擬最差條件下進行測試，確認該濾膜在你的特定製程中也能有效除菌。這就像「到職實習」，要證明在你的工廠條件下也能勝任。

這兩層不可互相取代，必須獨立驗證。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你要雇一位保全人員（濾膜）。第一層驗證就像他的保全證照考試（ASTM F838）— 在標準考試條件下，證明他有基本攔截能力。第二層驗證則是到你公司實地試用期 — 在你工廠的實際環境中（特定產品、特定條件），驗證他確實能擋住所有不應該進入的東西。證照合格不代表一定適合你的工廠；反之，試用合格也不能替代正式證照。兩者缺一不可。

重點提示 Key Notes

責任歸屬非常清楚：

  • 第一層（ASTM F838 標準測試）= 濾膜供應商的責任
  • 第二層（製程特定驗證）= 最終使用者的責任

對 CDMO 而言，你的客戶（藥廠 MAH）也會要求你提供第二層驗證的資料。如果你的製程條件改變了（例如更換藥品配方、改變過濾溫度或壓力），可能需要重新進行製程特定的細菌截留驗證。

  2. 如果你的 CDMO 同時生產三種不同配方的注射劑，你需要做幾次製程特定的細菌截留驗證？可以用「產品家族」的概念簡化嗎？
6.6.1 Factors Influencing Bacterial Retention 6.6.1 影響細菌截留的因素

原文內容 Original Text

Factors potentially affecting bacterial retention include, but are not limited to (2, 3):

  • Filter type (e.g., asymmetric, isometric)
    • Base polymer, surface modification chemistry, pore size distribution, and thickness
  • Fluid components
    • Formulation, surfactants, and additives
  • Fluid properties
    • pH, viscosity, osmolarity, ionic strength, and surface tension
  • Process conditions
    • Filter reuse, temperature, pressure differential, flow rate, filtration time, total filter/product contact time, and volume throughput.* The potential impact of intermittency should be considered.
  • Potential bioburden in the product (quantitative and qualitative)

*Note: As sterilization of the filter is part of the supplier qualification performed during development, exact simulation of the end user's sterilization method (e.g., irradiation, autoclave, SIP temperature, number of cycles) is not generally required for process-specific bacterial retention study.

In addition to these factors, process bioburden should be evaluated in order to establish B. diminuta as a relevant organism. Evaluation should be based on bioburden identification and a risk assessment to ensure the absence of potentially more penetrative organisms in the prefiltration bioburden (see Section 6.6.2). Consideration should be given to potential product formulations or process conditions affecting cell size or other physiological or morphological microorganism attributes that might allow organism passage.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

影響細菌截留（Bacterial Retention）的因素可以分為四大類：

  • 濾膜本身：非對稱或等距結構、基材聚合物、表面改質化學、孔徑分佈、厚度。這些決定了濾膜的「基礎攔截能力」。
  • 流體特性：pH、黏度、滲透壓、離子強度、表面張力等。這些因素可能影響微生物的大小、形態、甚至濾膜表面的吸附能力。
  • 製程條件：壓差、流速、過濾時間、總接觸時間、體積通量、間歇性操作（Intermittency，即開開停停的過濾模式）。
  • 生物負荷：產品中可能存在的微生物種類和數量。

Intermittency（間歇性操作）特別值得注意 — 如果過濾過程中停停走走，微生物可能在靜止狀態下有更多時間與濾膜互動，甚至可能被推進更深的孔道。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像一個海綿迷宮（濾膜結構），你要把小球（細菌）擋在外面。能否成功攔截取決於：迷宮本身有多密（濾膜類型）、小球滾過來的液體是什麼（流體特性，例如油滑的液體可能讓球更容易滑過）、推力多大及推多久（製程條件），以及有多少顆球同時衝過來（生物負荷）。如果推力忽大忽小（間歇性操作），有些球可能趁你不注意就被擠進迷宮深處。

重點提示 Key Notes

關於濾膜滅菌方法：原文特別標註，過濾器的滅菌方式（如輻照、高壓滅菌、SIP）是供應商在開發階段的資格確認範圍，使用者的製程特定細菌截留研究中通常不需要精確模擬滅菌條件。這是一個常見的誤解 — 很多人以為製程驗證要模擬一切條件，但滅菌部分由供應商負責即可。

生物負荷風險評估：不能只看數量，還要做定性鑑定（qualitative assessment），確認有沒有比 B. diminuta 更容易穿透的小型微生物。如果有，那 B. diminuta 可能不是最差條件的代表菌株（參見 Section 6.6.2）。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 實務情境：假設你的工廠生產一款含有界面活性劑（surfactant）的眼用製劑。界面活性劑會降低液體的表面張力，可能影響濾膜的吸附截留（Adsorptive Sequestration）能力。這時你的製程特定驗證就不能只用純水或簡單的緩衝液，必須使用實際含有界面活性劑的配方（或代用品）來做挑戰測試，才能真實反映你的製程風險。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 如果你的過濾製程是間歇性的（例如每批過濾中間有30分鐘停機），這對細菌截留驗證的設計有什麼影響？
  2. 為什麼流體的 pH 和離子強度可能影響細菌截留？提示：思考吸附截留機制（Adsorptive Sequestration）。
  3. 原文說滅菌方法「通常不需要」在使用者驗證中模擬，什麼情況下可能例外？
6.6.2 Challenge Organism Selection Criteria 6.6.2 挑戰菌株選擇標準

原文內容 Original Text

Historically, Pseudomonas diminuta, reclassified as B. diminuta, has been the microorganism of choice for bacterial retention studies. Although a product may be bactericidal to B. diminuta under normal processing conditions, other microorganisms may survive under the same conditions. Another bacterial retention study method for bactericidal products is the use of "indigenous bioburden." Indigenous bioburden consists of bacterial isolates from the manufacturing environment or the product formulation that have demonstrated the ability to survive within the product formulation under actual production processing conditions.

In process bacteria should be propagated or equilibrated in the product in order to ensure that their morphological and physiological characteristics are representative of process isolates. The smallest, or worst-case, indigenous organism should be used based on a risk assessment against use of B. diminuta as the challenge organism. For additional discussion of preparation and use of challenge organisms, see Section 6.6.3.

If diminutive microorganisms are known to exist in the product, they or an appropriate model organism, may be used to challenge test filters. Typically, these challenges are conducted in addition to B. diminuta testing, and it is strongly recommended that appropriate measures be taken to remove these organisms as a potential contaminant.

If other bacteria are used, they should be small enough to challenge the retentivity of the sterilizing-grade filter and should simulate the smallest microorganism found in production (4). A risk assessment should determine the likelihood of any prefiltration bacteria potentially being able to penetrate a 0.2 μm sterilizing-grade filter (37).

The size of the challenge microorganism should be confirmed by demonstrating passage through a 0.45 μm-rated membrane as a positive control for each challenge performed. This confirms the challenge organism is appropriately sized and monodispersed. B. diminuta grown under standard culture conditions penetrates 0.45 μm-rated membranes in small numbers at high challenge levels, typically ≥ 107 CFU/cm2 (see Section 6.6.3). In some cases, B. diminuta may not be a representative worst-case model. Documented rationale should be provided if a different challenge organism is chosen.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

B. diminuta（短小單胞菌）是細菌截留測試的「標準對手」，但它不一定永遠是最差條件。以下是挑戰菌株選擇的關鍵邏輯：

  • Bactericidal Product（殺菌性產品）：如果產品本身會殺死 B. diminuta，但製程中可能有其他「不怕」這個產品的微生物存在，那就需要使用 Indigenous Bioburden（原生生物負荷） — 也就是從你的製造環境或產品配方中分離出來、確認能在產品中存活的菌株。
  • Diminutive Microorganisms（極小微生物）：如果已知產品中存在比 B. diminuta 還小的微生物，必須額外用這些小菌或適當的模式菌來挑戰濾膜。
  • Positive Control（陽性對照）：每次挑戰測試都要用 0.45 μm 濾膜做陽性對照 — 挑戰菌必須能穿過 0.45 μm 濾膜，證明菌體確實夠小且均勻分散（monodispersed），沒有聚團。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像考試出題的邏輯。B. diminuta 是標準考題（駕照考試的路考路線）。但如果你的工廠實際遇到的「考生」更刁鑽（比標準考題更小、更會鑽縫），你就不能只用標準考題來評估。你需要找出你的環境中最「會鑽洞」的那個微生物，用它來挑戰。而 0.45 μm 的陽性對照，就像確認考題難度是否適當 — 確保你的挑戰菌確實「夠小」，不是因為菌體聚團成大球而假裝被攔住。

重點提示 Key Notes

陽性對照的必要性：0.45 μm 陽性對照是每次挑戰測試的必備項目，不是選做項目。它確認的是：

  • 挑戰菌的大小確實在 0.2-0.45 μm 範圍內（能穿過 0.45 μm 但被 0.2 μm 攔住）
  • 菌體是 Monodispersed（單一分散）的，沒有聚集成團

如果菌體聚團了，即使單個菌體很小，聚團後的「等效粒徑」會變大，被 0.2 μm 濾膜攔住是因為聚團而非濾膜的真正截留能力，這樣的結果就不可靠。

如果選擇 B. diminuta 以外的挑戰菌株，必須提供書面的科學理由說明。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：假設你的工廠生產一款含有苯酚防腐劑的注射劑。環境監測發現製程中偶爾出現一種對苯酚有抗性的小型革蘭氏陰性菌。在這種情況下：

  1. 你仍然要用 B. diminuta 做標準挑戰測試
  2. 但還需要額外用這個環境分離株（Indigenous Bioburden）做挑戰測試
  3. 這個分離株需要在產品中培養/平衡後使用，確保其形態代表實際製程條件
  4. 每次測試都要有 0.45 μm 陽性對照

  1. 為什麼即使產品會殺死 B. diminuta，細菌截留驗證仍然重要？什麼替代策略可以使用？
  2. 0.45 μm 陽性對照測試失敗（菌體無法穿過 0.45 μm 濾膜）代表什麼？你應該怎麼處理？
  3. 如果你的環境監測從未發現比 B. diminuta 更小的微生物，你是否還需要考慮使用 Indigenous Bioburden？為什麼？
6.6.3 Culture Maintenance and Challenge Preparation 6.6.3 菌株維護與挑戰液製備

原文內容 Original Text

B. diminuta ATCC® 19146TM can be obtained in lyophilized form from bacterial culture collections such as the American Type Culture Collection (ATCC). After reconstituting the microorganism according to the manufacturer's instructions, stocks can be maintained (either refrigerated or frozen) on appropriate media per standard microbiological practice. Maintenance conditions for process isolates to be used for challenge studies should be established.

Two standard techniques have been recognized as suitable for preparation and maintenance of B. diminuta for bacterial retention studies. These are the SLB and the frozen cell paste (FCP) methods. Both methods have been found to be effective in producing suitable suspensions of B. diminuta of approximately 0.3 – 0.4 μm in diameter by 0.6 – 1.0 μm in length.

Alternate media and culture methods may be equally valid to prepare B. diminuta provided they produce single, monodispersed cells suitably sized to penetrate 0.45 μm-rated membrane filters. Alternate culture methods should be validated. Bacterial retention study stock cultures can be screened for aggregation by optical microscopy. In cases where aggregation is observed, immersion of the stock culture in an ultrasonic cleaning bath filled with cold water for a qualified duration is one means of dispersing the aggregate. The cavitation action of the bath is effective in disaggregating bacterial cells without the loss of viability. This effect should be confirmed by optical microscopy, viable count, and recovery downstream of the 0.45 μm-rated size control filter (38).

The bacterial challenge concentration should provide a uniform challenge over the duration in which viability of B. diminuta is assured to yield a final challenge level of at least 107 CFU/cm2 of filter surface area. Operational parameters (e.g., flow rate, time, and pressure) should be taken into consideration when calculating the bacterial retention concentration.

A bacterial challenge level of ≥ 1 x 107 CFU/cm2 is, therefore, the requirement for a sterilizing-grade filter (historically a filter rated at 0.2 μm). This level was derived from Bowman's observations that B. diminuta could penetrate a 0.45 μm-rated membrane at > 104–106 CFU/cm2 bacterial challenge level, and her suggestion for qualifying 0.2 μm-rated membranes as "sterilizing-grade" with B. diminuta at 107 CFU/cm2 to assure minimally sufficient sensitivity to detect oversized pores (39). Bacterial challenge concentration (CFU/mL) should not be confused with bacterial challenge level (CFU/cm2).

The viability and titer of the B. diminuta suspension should be confirmed using a suitable recovery medium such as soybean-casein digest agar (also known as tryptic soy agar), Mueller Hinton Agar, or other qualified growth medium. When performing filter challenges, the viable titer of the bacterial retention suspension should be determined immediately prior to and after the challenge; the bacterial titer should be determined using an accepted microbiological testing method. For situations when this is not possible (e.g., extremely short-duration bacterial retention studies), a rationale should be provided to justify the use of either a pre- or post-input viability sample. The same culture medium should also be used to determine any recovery of B. diminuta downstream of the test and control filters.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

B. diminuta ATCC 19146 — 這是全球公認的標準菌株來源。關鍵的培養與製備知識：

  • 兩種標準製備方法：SLB 法和 Frozen Cell Paste (FCP，凍結細胞糊) 法。兩者都能產生尺寸約 0.3-0.4 μm（直徑） x 0.6-1.0 μm（長度）的適當菌體。
  • Monodispersed（單一分散）：菌體必須一個一個分開，不能聚團（Aggregation）。聚團的菌體會讓測試結果不可靠 — 實際上是被「假攔住」的。
  • 超音波分散：如果發現聚團，可以用超音波清洗浴（冷水）來打散菌團，注意不能損害菌體活性。處理效果需要用光學顯微鏡、活菌計數和 0.45 μm 濾膜通過率三重確認。

比喻說明 Analogy

培養挑戰菌就像準備考試的「出題材料」。你需要確保每顆球（菌體）的大小一致（0.3-0.4 μm），而且一個個獨立滾動（monodispersed），不能像黏在一起的飯糰（aggregation）那樣成團 — 否則就像用籃球而非乒乓球來測試紗窗的孔洞，測試結果毫無意義。超音波分散就像用振動把黏在一起的飯粒抖散開，但要注意不能抖碎（保持活性）。

公式與計算 Formula

Challenge Concentration vs. Challenge Level — 一定要分清！

Challenge Concentration (挑戰濃度) = CFU/mL
  → 菌液中每毫升的菌落形成單位數

Challenge Level (挑戰水準) = CFU/cm²
  → 每平方公分濾膜面積接受的總菌數

兩者的換算關係：
  Challenge Level = (Challenge Concentration x Total Volume) / Filter Area

範例：
  若濾膜面積 = 13.4 cm²（47mm disc）
  所需最低挑戰水準 = 1 x 10⁷ CFU/cm²
  總挑戰菌數 = 1 x 10⁷ x 13.4 = 1.34 x 10⁸ CFU
  若挑戰液體積 = 1,000 mL
  所需最低濃度 = 1.34 x 10⁸ / 1,000 = 1.34 x 10⁵ CFU/mL

重點提示 Key Notes

107 CFU/cm2 的歷史由來：這個數值來自 Bowman 博士的研究發現 — B. diminuta 在 104-106 CFU/cm2 的挑戰水準下可以穿透 0.45 μm 濾膜。她因此建議以 107 CFU/cm2 作為 0.2 μm「除菌級」濾膜的資格確認標準，以確保有足夠的靈敏度來偵測過大的孔洞。

活性確認時機：挑戰菌液的活菌效價（viable titer）必須在挑戰測試開始前和結束後都進行確認。如果是超短時間的測試無法做到，需要提供科學理由說明只取其中一個時間點的合理性。

6.6.4 Viability/Filter Flush Qualification Testing Procedure and Protocol Development 6.6.4 活菌性／過濾器沖洗確效測試程序與方案制定

原文內容 Original Text

Prior to completing the bacterial retention study, the test organism's viability should be verified by direct inoculation into the carrier fluid (ideally, product or surrogate fluid, if justified) in order to determine the appropriate challenge test methodology (see Figure 6.9-1). Scientific justification should be provided if viability testing is not performed before finalizing the bacterial retention study design (e.g., nonaqueous process fluids). The microorganism should be grown in the same manner as that used for challenge-testing to preserve its morphological and physiological characteristics. The test exposure time chosen for the viability studies should equal or exceed the maximum filter/product contact time, unless viability of the test organism is demonstrated to decline by ≥ 1 log before the actual process filtration time is reached.

Note: It is not necessary to determine the exact point in time at which viability of the test organism declines by ≥1 log; however, the viability test time should be sufficient to allow meaningful measurements to be taken during the bacterial retention study.

The organism should be added to the process fluid (or surrogate solution) such that the inoculation volume does not cause significant dilution of the drug product and viability should be monitored by taking appropriate samples over the intended process duration to determine cell concentration in accordance with standard methods. A control (e.g., water, buffer) should be spiked at the same ratio and viability of the organism monitored over the same duration. If the temperature may be inhibitory to the viability of the challenge organism (typically, over 40°C for B. diminuta), consideration should be given to assessing viability at ambient temperature so the bacterial retention study could be conducted by inoculating the organism directly into the process fluid (40). In addition, for broad processing temperature ranges, consideration may be given to the impact of the extremes of the temperature range on viability of the challenge organism. If there is less than an approximately 1 log difference between the test and control over at least 30 minutes, direct inoculation of the organism into the process fluid may be an option for the bacterial retention study. Additional consideration should be given for situations where the viability of the challenge organism in the process fluid is reduced by close to 1 log at the desired time point. For such cases, it may be necessary to reduce the total exposure time of the challenge organism in the process fluid to reduce the likelihood of die-off, which could prevent the minimum challenge level being met during the bacterial retention study. A decision should be made between ensuring the minimum challenge level of ≥ 1 x 107 CFU/cm2 is met and ensuring the bacterial retention study duration is sufficiently long to allow adequate measurements of differential pressure, flow rate, volume throughput and temperature.

Filter flush schemes should also be considered prior to performing a process-specific bacterial retention study. If the drug product is bactericidal to the challenge organism, then adequate removal/neutralization of bactericidal effects resulting from residual process fluid should be ensured prior to introduction of the bacteria to the test system. If the process fluid is nonbactericidal and used as the challenge vehicle, the lack of potential inhibitory effects to the challenge organism should be ensured during the incubation period.

After the test organism's viability within the product has been established, the challenge methodology and protocol should be developed.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Viability Testing（活性測試）是在正式的細菌截留研究之前必須完成的預備研究，目的是回答一個關鍵問題：挑戰菌在你的產品中能存活多久？

  • Direct Inoculation（直接接種）：將挑戰菌直接接種到產品（或經過科學論證的代用品）中
  • 1 Log 衰減閾值：如果菌在產品中的存活率與對照組（水或緩衝液）的差異小於約 1 log（在至少 30 分鐘內），則可以採用直接接種法進行挑戰
  • Filter Flush（濾膜沖洗）：如果產品具有殺菌性，在引入挑戰菌之前必須先沖洗掉殘留的殺菌產品成分，否則菌還沒到達濾膜就被殺死了

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像在體能測驗前先做「場地適應」。你不能直接把一個考生（B. diminuta）丟到酷暑沙漠（殺菌性產品）裡考試 — 他可能在開始考試前就中暑倒下了（die-off）。所以你先做一個「場地適應測試」（Viability Testing）：把考生放在考場環境中觀察，如果他在整場考試時間內都還精神奕奕（< 1 log 衰減），那場地沒問題。如果他很快就不行了，你就需要調整考試策略 — 要嘛縮短考試時間，要嘛先清理掉場地上的「毒物」（Filter Flush）。

重點提示 Key Notes

挑戰水準 vs. 挑戰時間的兩難：

當產品接近殺菌邊界（接近 1 log 衰減）時，會面臨一個矛盾：

  • 延長挑戰時間 = 更好地模擬實際製程，但菌可能死太多，達不到 ≥ 107 CFU/cm2
  • 縮短挑戰時間 = 保持足夠活菌數，但可能無法充分收集壓差、流速、通量等關鍵數據

這需要在兩者之間做出平衡的科學決策，不存在完美答案 — 但你的決策理由必須有書面記錄。

溫度注意：B. diminuta 在超過 40°C 時活性可能受抑制。如果你的製程溫度較高，可考慮在室溫下進行活性評估，以設計可行的挑戰策略。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 決策流程：

假設你的活性測試結果顯示 B. diminuta 在產品中 15 分鐘後衰減了 0.8 log（接近 1 log），而你的實際過濾製程需要 45 分鐘。此時你有幾個選項：

  1. 縮短挑戰時間至 15 分鐘以內，確保菌數夠，但需證明這仍能代表你的製程
  2. 改用 Filter Flush 法，先用產品預潤濕濾膜，沖洗掉殘留產品後再引入挑戰菌
  3. 提高初始接種濃度，讓即使有衰減也能維持 ≥ 107 CFU/cm2

無論選哪個方案，都需要科學理由和風險評估的書面記錄。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 什麼是「1 log 差異」規則？它如何決定你能否採用直接接種法？
  2. 如果你的產品是強酸性（pH 3.0），B. diminuta 在其中會迅速死亡，你應該如何設計挑戰方案？
  3. 為什麼 Filter Flush 不只是「沖洗」那麼簡單？需要驗證哪些事項？
6.6.5 Bacterial Retention Validation Studies 6.6.5 細菌截留驗證研究

原文內容 Original Text

The goal of bacterial retention validation studies is to have documented evidence demonstrating that the filtration process will consistently remove a high level of a standard bacterium, or relevant bioburden isolate, suspended within the product or surrogate fluid under simulated process conditions.

The decision to test membrane discs or a full-size process filter depends upon the study goal. Ideally, the test filter should mimic the process filter unit as closely as possible, including the membrane type and pore size. If the study is to validate the bacterial retention efficiency of a particular membrane material, the use of a small test membrane disc is generally regarded as sufficient, but the filter selected should be justified. The test methodology used for determining physical integrity of process filters should yield results that are meaningful in terms of bacterial retention studies, and correlation data should be available from the filter supplier. If different test methods are used, a relationship between the two should be demonstrated.

The following should be considered when conducting process-specific bacterial retention validation studies on membrane filters:

  • A thorough assessment of the filtration process should be conducted, including the nature of the process fluid (e.g., aqueous, acid, base, organic), active filtration volume, total product/filter contact time, process pressure, process flow rate, process temperature, number and/or frequency of intermittent cycles ("on/off"), and process-filter design specifications.
  • Multiple filter membrane lots (typically three) should be included in process-specific bacterial retention validation studies. In cases where the product may be considered aggressive to the membrane, the exact number of filters and the test design will depend on the process.
  • At least one of the three filter membrane lots used for the process-specific bacterial retention validation study should have a pre-study or pre-use physical integrity-test value as close as possible to the filter manufacturer's test specification. The filter manufacturer is responsible for defining the criteria for the minimum specification filter. Due to improved process control during membrane manufacturing, it may not be possible to obtain membranes at or near the filter manufacturer's production limit. In this case, test membranes should be selected from a manufactured lot whose pre-use integrity test values are as low as practical but representative of the standard filter-manufacturing process.
  • Membrane physical-integrity test values from bacterial retention validation studies should be included in test reports. The physical integrity should be determined prior to and after challenge-testing using water, product, or other wetting fluid for which specifications exist. Use of a low-surface-tension wetting fluid for post-use integrity-testing may be required to remove residual product.
  • An investigation should be conducted if the test organism is recovered downstream of any filter after the product bacterial retention study. If the investigation confirms penetration of the filter by the test organism and the filter meets its integrity-test specification, then the applicability of this filter under these process conditions should be reconsidered.
  • Families of products with the same ingredients, varying only in concentration, may be validated by challenging the concentration extremes and accepting the intermediate concentrations by bracketing. If a single product is determined to be a worst-case representative, then rationale and data should accompany the model. A placebo (without the API but belonging to the same product family) may be considered for product bracketing with appropriate justification.
  • During bacterial retention studies, incorporation of pressure increases to simulate integrity-testing (e.g., PUPSIT) should be based on risk assessment performed by the end-user (e.g., if process fluid is used for PUPSIT that could potentially pose a risk to bacterial penetration). Examples of factors to consider for risk assessment include the potential bioburden in the wetting fluid (based on a qualitative and quantitative assessment), type of integrity-test performed, and maximum number of integrity-tests that the filter could be exposed to. Filter suppliers qualify the filter to be able to withstand the elevated pressures resulting from integrity-testing. Therefore, the risk assessment should be focused on the potential for any bioburden in the wetting fluid to penetrate the filter as a result of PUPSIT.
  • Under typical manufacturing conditions with controlled conditions, the risk of bacterial penetration due to PUPSIT is considered low. If PUPSIT simulation is deemed necessary, the test design should be accompanied by a technical rationale.
  • Filter reuse is typically not practical or recommended for pharmaceutical purposes. However, if a sterilizing-grade filter is reused, justification should be provided, and reuse parameters should be validated. Scale-down laboratory studies are unlikely to be able to simulate the conditions of filter reuse by an end user (e.g., multiple sterilization cycles, clean in place, excessive handling). Therefore, consideration should be given to testing process filters that have been exposed to the entire cycle of reuse by the end-user to supplement the small-scale study.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Bacterial Retention Validation Studies（細菌截留驗證研究）是把所有前面章節的知識整合起來的最終實戰。核心設計要點：

  • 測試規模：可以用小型 Membrane Disc（膜片）或全尺寸 Process Filter（製程濾器）。膜片適合驗證膜材本身的截留效率；但必須有供應商提供的 Correlation Data（關聯數據）來橋接小型與全尺寸結果。
  • 多批次濾膜：通常需要三個不同批號的濾膜，其中至少一個批號的完整性測試值應盡可能接近供應商規格的最低限（Minimum Specification Filter），模擬最差條件。
  • 完整性測試前後都要做：挑戰前和挑戰後都要對濾膜進行物理完整性測試（Physical Integrity Test），並記錄在報告中。
  • Bracketing（括號法）：同一產品家族（相同成分、不同濃度）可以測試濃度極端值，中間濃度用括號法接受。

比喻說明 Analogy

整個驗證研究就像對保全系統做全面壓力測試。你不能只測一次、只測一道門（一個濾膜批號）：

  • 三個批號 = 測三道不同的門，確認不是運氣好遇到品質特別好的那道門
  • 最低規格濾膜 = 刻意找那道「最薄弱」的門來測，確保連最差的門也能擋住
  • 前後完整性測試 = 測試前先檢查門沒壞，測試後再檢查門有沒有被撞壞
  • Bracketing = 如果你的保全系統要擋住「1公斤」到「100公斤」的包裹，只要測試 1 公斤和 100 公斤通過，中間的就可以接受

重點提示 Key Notes

下游回收的嚴重性：如果在挑戰測試後，在濾膜下游回收到挑戰菌，這是一個重大事件。需要進行調查：

  • 如果調查確認菌體確實穿透了濾膜，而且濾膜的完整性測試是合格的 — 這代表該濾膜在這些製程條件下可能不適用，需要重新評估
  • 這是比「濾膜破損導致洩漏」更嚴重的情況 — 因為它意味著即使完整性測試合格的濾膜也可能失敗

PUPSIT 模擬：在受控的典型製造條件下，PUPSIT 導致細菌穿透的風險被認為是低的。但如果使用者決定需要模擬 PUPSIT，必須基於風險評估，重點關注潤濕液中的潛在生物負荷是否可能因 PUPSIT 壓力而穿透濾膜。

重點提示 Key Notes — Filter Reuse

Filter Reuse（濾膜重複使用）在製藥行業中通常不建議。如果確實需要重複使用：

  • 必須提供充分的科學理由
  • 重複使用的參數必須經過驗證
  • 小規模實驗室研究可能無法充分模擬實際重複使用的條件（如多次滅菌循環、CIP、過度處理等）
  • 應考慮使用在實際重複使用循環後的製程濾器來補充小規模研究

這呼應了 TR26 的決策層級：無菌濾液保證永遠是第一優先。省錢重複使用濾膜的效益，永遠不應凌駕於無菌保證的風險之上。
