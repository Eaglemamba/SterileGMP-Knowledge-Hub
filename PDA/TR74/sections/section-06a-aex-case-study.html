6.0 Appendix I: Hypothetical Case Study (Reprocessing Framework) — Anion Exchange Chromatography 附錄一：假設性案例研究（再處理框架）— 陰離子交換層析

原文內容 Original Text

Section 6.0 (Appendix I) focuses on the design and implementation considerations for a reprocessing step. Adapted from the published monoclonal antibody manufacturing (mAb) process, A-Mab: A Case Study in Bioprocess Development, for this case study, a reprocessing framework has been created using an anion exchange chromatography (AEX) step (19). This reprocessing framework is intentionally designed to require a more comprehensive analysis than a simpler reprocessing step, such as refiltration described in Section 7.0.

Reprocessing of a major unit operation, such as an AEX chromatography column, requires a detailed assessment, significant resources, planning, and commitment. Simpler unit operations require a subset of the planning and activities outlined here. Regardless, quality management systems, regulatory requirements, and a comprehensive process understanding should drive the overall design of the reprocessing procedure.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Reprocessing（再處理）：將不符合中間品 (intermediate) 規格的批次，在相同製程步驟重新處理一次，以達到可接受的品質標準。與 Reworking（重新加工，改用不同方法）不同。

AEX（Anion Exchange Chromatography，陰離子交換層析）：利用蛋白質表面負電荷與陽性樹脂之間的靜電作用進行分離。mAb 在 flow-through 模式下直接通過，而 HCP（Host Cell Proteins，宿主細胞蛋白）、DNA、內毒素、病毒等帶負電雜質則吸附於樹脂上被清除。

A-Mab Case Study：業界標竿性的假設性 mAb 生產案例，由多家公司聯合撰寫，廣泛用於 CMC 開發框架示範。TR74 借用此框架設計 AEX 再處理情境。

比喻說明 Analogy

AEX 再處理就像工廠品管員在出貨前發現某批零件表面殘留油污超標（相當於 HCP 超標），不是直接廢棄這批零件，而是把整批零件再送一次清洗線重新清洗。但這麼做必須先確認：清洗設備還在規格範圍內嗎？清洗次數有沒有超過驗證上限？清洗後零件品質不會變差？——這就是 6.2 Points to Consider 要回答的問題。

重點提示 Key Notes

TR74 刻意選擇 AEX 作為示範案例，因為它比「重新過濾」（Section 7.0 refiltration）更複雜：涉及樹脂壽命、柱床完整性、病毒清除驗證等多個面向。熟悉本節後，處理較簡單的再處理步驟時只需套用其子集即可。

決策層級提醒：品質管理系統與法規要求永遠優先於商業考量。

6.1 Unit Operation Characteristics 單元操作特性

原文內容 Original Text

Chromatography is used to purify a product (e.g., mAb, target proteins, compounds) considering the properties of the target solute, feed stream, required purity level, productivity, costs, process sequence, process parameters, and implementation of the designed purification process. Biomolecules are purified using chromatography techniques that separate them according to the difference in their specific properties as summarized in Table 6.1-1. In ion exchange chromatography (IEX), biomolecules are separated according to the difference in their net surface charges.

See Table 6.1-1 (Chromatography Techniques) and Table 6.1-2 (Quality Attribute Risk Assessment Topics) below.

The overall goal of a chromatographic step is to remove impurities, such as host-cell-derived proteins (HCP), nucleic acids, carbohydrates, and other undesired compounds.

In general, a full-scale chromatography system will consist of the following components:

  • Buffer and Product Reservoirs: Containers (hard-piped or single use disposables) used to supply the buffers, product feed, and for elution collection required during the chromatography operation.
  • Pumps: Peristaltic pumps used to feed the buffers into chromatography columns. Depending on scale, other types of pumps may be utilized considering process requirements and equipment design.
  • Column: The chamber where the chromatographic media is loaded. The size (diameter and height) of the column will be defined based on the process requirements. The material of construction (e.g., glass, polycarbonate, steel) is selected based on process conditions (e.g., pressure and flow rates).
  • Resin: Chromatography media intended for the purification process. The packaging of the columns is commonly conducted by creating a slurry where the particles are suspended, that is, poured into the column.
  • Valves: Sanitary systems used to control the flow of materials during the operation of the chromatography system. These may be manual or pneumatic units.
  • Sensors: Applications intended to monitor process parameters required for the chromatography process. These may include pH, air, temperature, flow, pressure, and UV monitor.
  • Controller/Computer: Used to control and monitor the operation of the system.

The purification strategy involving a chromatography step is based on the characteristics (e.g., properties and stability) of the product under different conditions, final specifications, feed properties (e.g., concentration of product and impurities), nature of the process (e.g., continuous or discontinuous), and nature of the substances (e.g., hazardous). When designing the reprocessing step for the AEX chromatography case study, consideration was given to the quality attributes for mAbs listed in Table 6.1-2 (19).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

四大層析原理（見 Table 6.1-1）：

  • 電荷（Charge）→ IEX：AEX（陰離子交換）、CEX（陽離子交換）
  • 大小（Size）→ SEC：Size Exclusion Chromatography，用於聚集體分析
  • 疏水性（Hydrophobicity）→ HIC / RPC：Hydrophobic Interaction / Reversed Phase Chromatography
  • 生物辨識（Biorecognition）→ Affinity：Protein A 親和層析為 mAb 第一步純化主力

Flow-Through Mode（流穿模式）：AEX 通常以 flow-through 操作。mAb 在目標 pH/conductivity 下帶正電或中性，不與陰離子樹脂結合，直接流過；而 HCP、DNA、內毒素帶負電，吸附於樹脂，藉此清除雜質。這與 Protein A（bind-and-elute 模式）截然不同。

重點提示 Key Notes

Table 6.1-2 關鍵品質屬性（CQA）標記（*）：Aggregation、Galactose content、Afucosylation、High mannose content、Nonglycosylated heavy chain、Sialic acid content、Host-cell proteins (HCP)、DNA 均被標記為 CQA。再處理設計必須證明對這些 CQA 無不良影響，這是監管機關的核心關注點。

DS（Drug Substance，原料藥）層次：本案例為 DS/上游純化流程，與 DP（Drug Product，製劑）的無菌充填不同，但相同的品質風險管理邏輯適用。CDMO 在與 Sponsor 或 DS CMO 討論時，此框架提供共同語言。

比喻說明 Analogy

把層析柱想像成濾水器的濾芯，不同濾芯設計攔截不同種類的雜質（依大小、電荷、疏水性或特異性識別）。AEX 的 flow-through 模式就像用帶負電的濾網：帶負電的雜質（HCP、DNA）會被吸住，而目標蛋白（mAb）帶正電，直接通過濾網，達到純化目的。

實務應用 Practical Application

對於 Fill-Finish CDMO 而言，雖然 AEX 屬於 DS 端製程，但在 Tech Transfer 審查 DS CMO 的 Manufacturing Process Description（Section 3.2.S.2.2）時，了解 AEX 的 flow-through 原理與 HCP 清除機制，有助於評估 Sponsor 提供的 CQA 合理性及批次放行規格的風險等級。

6.2 Points to Consider 考量要點

原文內容 Original Text

The elements in Section 6.2 should be considered as part of reprocessing a chromatography column. These elements are further discussed in PDA Technical Report No. 14 (Revised 2008): Validation of Column-Based Chromatography Processes for the Purification of Proteins.

6.2.1 Column Packing 柱填充

The resin utilized for chromatography is loaded into the column in a controlled manner to ensure a uniform distribution while preventing the formation of channels that will affect the performance of the unit operation. Commonly, a slurry containing resin in a process buffer is poured into the column. Once the resin is settled, the integrity of the packaged column is confirmed. In the case of reprocessing, the integrity of the column bed should be verified and confirmed prior to its use. Any issue affecting the bed should be solved prior to reprocessing, considering resuspension and repacking of the resin if applicable.

6.2.2 Cleaning and Sanitization 清洗與消毒

Full cleaning and sanitization of the column must be conducted in preparation for reprocessing. Cleaning and sanitization will follow existing procedures and practices, including the regeneration of the resin. (These elements are discussed further in (68, 69).)

6.2.3 Performance 操作性能

Performance of the chromatography step must be monitored according to the routine established procedures and must satisfy predefined acceptance criteria. The criteria should include, but not be limited to, the operational conditions (e.g., asymmetry, height equivalent to the theoretical plate, and/or transition analysis) and quality attributes (65).

6.2.4 Regeneration and Lifecycle 再生與生命週期

The resin must be regenerated consistent with established procedures and must satisfy the acceptance criteria defined and validated. The use of the resin for a reprocessing activity will be within the validated number of reuses if the reprocessing activity is not associated with a malfunction of the chromatography column and resin.

6.2.5 Validation and Filings 驗證與法規申報

Validation of reprocessing will be performed under a prospective protocol. Detailed guidance for validation activities of chromatography processes for the purification of proteins is provided in PDA TR 14 (68). In the event that changes are required to an existing filing, guidance is provided in PDA Technical Report No. 38: Manufacturing Chromatography Systems Post-Approval Changes (ChromPAC).

In this example, a protocol for prospective validation of reprocessing at the manufacturing scale was written and approved; however, the potential of a concurrent validation could be considered if the frequency for reprocessing did not justify a prospective protocol. The protocol required a single GMP run at manufacturing scale, including all routine in-process and release testing as well as additional nonroutine testing, for example, analysis of HCP, acidic variants, aggregate levels, fragmentation, oxidation, and potency in reprocessed material. The protocol also required placing the resulting drug substance in the stability program. The protocol acceptance criteria for nonroutine testing were based on the comparability criteria established in Section 6.4.3 and demonstrated at small-scale. (See Section 6.5.6.)

Yet, a full prospective validation approach may not be feasible or practical for reprocessing in all cases. Instead, a concurrent validation strategy may be more appropriate, allowing the manufacture and testing of products and only reprocessing when and if necessary. In the case of concurrent validation, the acceptance criteria shall be aligned with the approved specifications for the process.

The initial BLA to the FDA and MAA to the EMA for this new biologic included a complete manufacturing process development section for the AEX unit operation within Section 3.2.S.2.6 of the CTD. A comparability protocol was provided post-licensure to gain agreement with FDA on the implementation strategy and to request a reduction in reporting requirements from a PAS to a CBE-30 (i.e., documentation of changes being effected, 30-day response from FDA required). In the EU MAA, a Change Management Protocol was included that also outlined the implementation strategy to seek a reduction in reporting requirements from a Type II Variation to a Type IB Variation upon successful completion of the protocol.

Table 6.2.5-1 details the content required to support this type of regulatory strategy. The approach described is primarily focused on the U.S. and EU regulatory authorities that have defined regulatory pathways for this type of submission; however, the information described is also useful for regulatory filings in other countries.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

五大考量要點（6.2.1–6.2.5）決定再處理技術可行性：

  • 6.2.1 Column Packing（柱填充）：再處理前必須重新確認柱床（column bed）完整性。常用指標：HETP（Height Equivalent to Theoretical Plate，理論板高當量）、Asymmetry（不對稱因子）、transition analysis（轉換分析）。柱床若出現裂縫或通道，再處理毫無意義。
  • 6.2.2 Cleaning/Sanitization（清洗/消毒）：NaOH（sodium hydroxide，氫氧化鈉）是最常用的樹脂 sanitization 試劑；清洗 SOP 必須完整執行，不能因「趕時間再處理」而縮短。
  • 6.2.3 Performance（性能）：柱效測試不只看外觀，必須符合預定接受標準。
  • 6.2.4 Regeneration/Lifecycle（再生/生命週期）：再處理使用次數必須計入已驗證的樹脂壽命（resin lifetime）。若再處理導致超過驗證使用次數，需額外驗證延壽。
  • 6.2.5 Validation/Filings（驗證/申報）：再處理本身是一個製程變更，需要驗證並依 CTD 結構提交。FDA（美國）對應 CBE-30/PAS；EMA（歐盟）對應 Type IB/Type II Variation。

公式與計算 Formula

HETP（理論板高當量）= L / N
  L = 柱長（cm）
  N = 理論板數（由峰寬計算）

Asymmetry（不對稱因子）= b / a
  a = 從峰頂垂線到前峰半峰寬
  b = 從峰頂垂線到後峰半峰寬
  接受標準通常：0.8 ≤ Asymmetry ≤ 1.5

再生使用計數規則：
  Total use count = Normal runs + Reprocessing runs
  必須 ≤ Validated lifetime（已驗證生命週期）

重點提示 Key Notes

Prospective vs Concurrent Validation（前瞻性 vs 同步驗證）：

  • Prospective（前瞻性）：事先設計、執行、取得數據後才放行。再處理頻率高時適用。本案例採用 1 批 GMP 規模確認批。
  • Concurrent（同步）：允許在驗證執行期間放行批次，適用於再處理頻率極低、難以累積足夠批次的情境。接受標準必須對應已核准規格。

監管申報等級：FDA PAS（Prior Approval Supplement，事前核准補充申請）> CBE-30（Changes Being Effected，30 天通知）。透過 Comparability Protocol 預先取得 FDA 同意，可在驗證完成後改用 CBE-30，大幅縮短商業化時程。

比喻說明 Analogy

6.2.5 的監管策略就像先向主管機關「預告」一個工程變更計畫：「我打算在特定條件下執行再處理，以下是我的驗證方案和接受標準，請提前審核並同意未來改用簡化申報程序。」這樣當再處理真正發生時，只需遞交 CBE-30（30 天通知），而不必等待 PAS 的 6–12 個月審查，避免產品斷貨。

6.3.1 Determining the Need for Reprocessing 確定再處理的必要性

Table 6.3.1-1 describes the scoring criteria for prospectively assessing the potential need for reprocessing in the hypothetical A-Mab process using an FMEA approach. The FMEA assesses the severity and detection of CQAs and how readily they are detected during unit operations. Scores are determined for Sections 6.5.2– 6.5.5 and Sections 6.4.1–6.4.3 according to the criteria below.

See Table 6.3.1-1 (FMEA Scoring Criteria) and Table 6.3.1-2 (FMEA Application Example 1) below.

The AEX chromatography step was operated in the flow-through mode, binding impurities such as DNA, HCP, endotoxin, and viruses to the resin while the antibody passed through. In addition, this step was designed to reduce levels of aggregate and acidic variants. Within the operating parameters of the AEX step, in this example, high levels of HCP in the column effluent are possible on rare occasions, and therefore, HCP is the quality attribute most likely to trigger reprocessing.

The risk assessment identified the AEX column (Table 6.3.1-2) as the step with the highest RPN. Development data indicated that the risk of high HCP levels was an inherent process risk and a rare event attributable to multiple, interacting sources of variability in the unit operation. Thus, the conclusion was that the AEX unit operation was a candidate for reprocessing.

The assigned scores are multiplied together (S × O × D = RPN) to determine the risk priority number (RPN). For example, for the "anion exchange chromatography" reprocessing step in Table 6.3.1-2, S × O × D = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125.

"Note: For HCP limits in Table 6.3.1-2: an appropriate IPC limit for DS HCP would typically be set at 70–80% of the action limit (approximately 70–80 ng/mg; a practical choice is 80 ng/mg). This provides a buffer to account for assay variability and process drift, enabling corrective actions before reaching the <100 ng/mg action limit."

6.3.2 Business Case and Project Charter 商業案例與專案章程

Three options were considered in assessing the business case for reprocessing at the AEX step:

  1. Discarding the batch upon failure to meet the HCP in-process control requirement of ≤80 ng/mg
  2. Redesigning the AEX step or adding additional steps
  3. Reprocessing during the AEX step

From a business perspective, it was confirmed that all three options could be managed to ensure acceptable product quality at release, but Option 3 was the most cost-effective and could be designed to meet quality and regulatory requirements. A project charter was created, and a cross-functional team was formed to develop a reprocessing step.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

FMEA（Failure Mode and Effects Analysis，失效模式與影響分析）：一種前瞻性的結構化風險評估工具，ICH Q9(R1) 推薦方法之一。三個評分維度：

  • S — Severity（嚴重性）：評估失效對 CQA 的潛在影響程度（1/3/5 三級）
  • O — Occurrence（發生頻率）：評估失效模式的發生概率（1/3/5 三級）
  • D — Detection（可偵測性）：評估在步驟完成前能否及時偵測到失效（1/3/5 三級）

RPN = S × O × D，範圍 1–125（本案例三個維度均為最高分 5）。

IPC（In-Process Control，製程中管控）：本案例中 AEX eluate HCP 的 IPC 限度設定為 ≤80 ng/mg（action limit ≤100 ng/mg 的 80%），作為再處理觸發點的警戒值。

比喻說明 Analogy

FMEA 評分就像汽車定期保養的風險檢查表：技師會為每個零件評估「壞了有多嚴重（S）」× 「多容易壞（O）」× 「壞了能不能及時發現（D）」= 整體風險分數。剎車片壞了很嚴重（S=5）、長期不換容易磨損（O=5）、但有警示燈可以偵測（D=1），RPN = 25；而 AEX 步驟的 HCP 問題：嚴重（S=5）× 偶爾發生（O=5）× 無法在步驟完成前即時偵測（D=5）= RPN 125，是所有步驟中最高的，因此被選為再處理候選步驟。

公式與計算 Formula

FMEA 評分邏輯（本案例）：

AEX 步驟 HCP 問題：
  S = 5（HCP 超標 → 直接影響 CQA）
  O = 5（每 <100 批中發生 1 次）
  D = 5（AEX eluate 無法在步驟完成前偵測 HCP）
  RPN = 5 × 5 × 5 = 125  ← 最高分，確認為候選步驟

對比：Protein A 步驟：
  S = 3, O = 1, D = 1 → RPN = 3（低風險）

對比：CEX 步驟：
  S = 5, O = 3, D = 1 → RPN = 15（中等風險）

IPC 觸發點設計：
  Action limit：HCP <100 ng/mg
  IPC alert limit：≤80 ng/mg（= action limit × 80%）
  → 80 ng/mg 超標 → 觸發再處理調查

重點提示 Key Notes

D = 5（無法偵測）是 AEX 選為再處理步驟的關鍵：HCP 需要專門的分析方法（ELISA 或 LC-MS），無法在製程中即時監控，只能在步驟完成後採樣分析。這就是為什麼 D = 5，也是為什麼需要預先設計再處理方案，而不是等到問題發生再臨時應對。

三選項決策框架：品質（CQA 合規性）是所有選項的前提，在三個選項都能確保產品品質的情況下，Option 3（再處理）因成本最低且不需要製程重新開發，成為首選。決策層級：CQA → Business。

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

6.4.1 第二輪 FMEA（Example 2）— 評估再處理本身對 CQA 的影響：與 6.3.1 的 FMEA（評估哪個步驟需要再處理）不同，6.4.1 的 FMEA 專門評估「如果執行 AEX 再處理，每個品質屬性會受到什麼影響」。由於假設公司已有完整分析方法，D = 1（均可偵測）。

RPN ≥ 15 的屬性需要進一步評估：包括 Protein concentration（蛋白質濃度）、Aggregate（聚集體）、Acidic variants（酸性異構體）、Fragmentation（碎片）、Potency（效力）、Oxidation（氧化）、HCP（宿主細胞蛋白）。這些屬性在 6.4.2 的分析方法中均需有對應檢測方法。

重點提示 Key Notes

6.4.2 分析方法矩陣的深度：Table 6.4.2-1 涵蓋從聚集體（SEC、RP-HPLC、Fluorimetry）到糖基化（LC-MS、HPLC）的完整分析工具箱。非常規測試（nonroutine testing）需要在 AEX eluate 基質（matrix）中驗證（validated）或確認（qualified），不能直接借用 DS release 方法。

6.4.3 穩定性計畫：再處理的第一批 DS 必須納入已建立的實時穩定性（real-time stability）和加速穩定性（accelerated stability）方案。穩定性數據是後續 CBE-30/Type IB 提交的重要組成部分（見 Table 6.2.5-1，3.2.S.7）。

6.4.5 調查義務（CAPA）：每次觸發再處理都必須進行正式調查，記錄根本原因（root cause）、對產品品質的影響評估（quality impact assessment）和糾正預防措施（CAPA）。這是 GMP 合規的基本要求，不能省略。
