5.6 Post-Approval Change Tools 上市後變更決策工具

原文內容 Original Text

When deciding to implement a post-approval change (PAC), thereby aiming for a new desired state, the tools suggested in Table 5.6-1 can be beneficial while drafting the documentation and scientific justifications. The use of tools and points for consideration noted in Table 5.6-1 can aid in identifying and presenting, at the outset, the most convincing and thorough documentation required for the approval process of Reworking. (See PDA Technical Report No. 91: Post-Approval Change Management and Implementation for Biologics and Pharmaceutical Drug Products–A User's Guide.)

A regulatory toolbox was jointly agreed upon by the EMA and the FDA on 26 November 2018. The agencies sought to identify scientific and regulatory solutions to challenges commonly experienced by applicants of early-access applications in completing Module 3 data requirements in time for the marketing authorization applications (MAA).

"The use of tools and points for consideration noted in Table 5.6-1 can aid in identifying and presenting, at the outset, the most convincing and thorough documentation required for the approval process of Reworking." — PDA TR74 §5.6

See Table 5.6-1 (Tools and Considerations for Decision-Making for Post-Approval Change) below — placed full-width for readability.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Post-Approval Change (PAC，上市後變更): 任何在上市核准後對已核准製程、規格、設施或其他 CMC 要素的更動，均屬 PAC。返工本質上就是一種非預期的 PAC——它並非計畫性變更，而是因 OOS 觸發的緊急製程調整。

Table 5.6-1 六大決策維度:

  • Defining the Scope：明確變更背景與範圍
  • Understanding Risk Assessment：CQA/CPP/EC 是否受影響
  • Regulatory Assessment：風險高低決定申報類別
  • Definition / Approval Strategy：Design space 與 PACMP
  • Implementation Process：驗證、送件、報告
  • Tools：商業案例、QRM、核准策略

比喻說明 Analogy

Table 5.6-1 就像一份「建築工程變更申請書」的標準格式——建築師不能只說「我想改樓梯位置」，必須說明為什麼改、結構強度評估、主管機關許可條件、施工計畫、竣工驗收。少任何一欄，申請就退件。PAC 也是相同邏輯。

重點提示 Key Notes

FDA + EMA 聯合工具箱 (2018): EMA 與 FDA 在 2018 年 11 月正式認可共同監管工具框架，代表跨轄區申報策略可以趨同——對台灣 CDMO 而言，此框架提供了可依循的雙邊對齊基礎。然而 TFDA 尚未正式採納 ICH Q12，需確認本地規範的對應要求。

TR91 交叉參考: PDA TR74 §5.6 明確指向 PDA TR91 (Post-Approval Change Management and Implementation for Biologics)——完整 PAC 實務請參照 TR91。

5.7 Established Conditions 既定條件

原文內容 Original Text

The implementation of ICH Quality Guideline Q12: Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management by different regulatory agencies will also enable the recognition of Established Conditions (ECs) that require regulatory notification as well as those non-ECs that have been recognized as not necessary to ensure product quality and, therefore, can be managed prior to implementation notification. The amount of flexibility in a control strategy is based on the totality of product and process understanding (e.g., prior knowledge, development studies) in the context of QRM principles described in ICH Q9(R1).

A control strategy is designed to ensure that a product of required quality will be produced consistently. This planned set of controls, derived from current product and process understanding, assures process performance and product quality. The controls can include parameters and attributes related to drug substance and drug product materials and components, capability of the verified mathematical models, facility and equipment operating conditions, in-process controls, finished product specifications, and the associated methods and frequency of monitoring and control. The control strategy is based on the development of the CQAs which, in turn, have been based on thorough scientific work.

Pharmaceutical development activities result in an appropriate control strategy of the ECs, which are critical elements identified and proposed by the MAH to assure product quality and, therefore, would require a regulatory submission if changes impacted any EC. ECs can include CPPs, critical material attributes (CMAs), and CQAs as defined in ICH Q8(R2).

In the event of reworking of a drug substance or drug product, CMC changes can occur. Thus, the ECs for the manufacturing process should be defined based on enhanced product and process understanding, considering all the relevant elements of the control strategy. To identify the ECs, an enhanced approach is recommended with an increased understanding of interaction between the changed key and critical input parameters along with the outputs, and the impact on quality attributes together with a corresponding control strategy. Once ECs are identified for the material to be reworked, a detailed initial risk assessment must be developed from the prior knowledge gathered to identify the potential risk to product quality associated with changing the ECs and considering the overall control strategy.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Established Conditions (EC，既定條件): ICH Q12 定義的 EC 是「MAH 向主管機關申報、且被核准後必須維持不變的 CMC 要素清單」。只有寫進 EC 清單的參數或屬性，才需在變更時走正式監管申報。

Non-EC vs EC:

  • EC（需申報）：例如最終滅菌過濾器孔徑、某關鍵色譜管柱品牌/型號
  • Non-EC（可 PQS 內管理）：例如非關鍵緩衝液配製順序、輔助性清洗步驟

EC 的三大組成： CPP（關鍵製程參數）、CMA（關鍵物料屬性）、CQA（關鍵品質屬性）——均源自 ICH Q8(R2) 的 QbD 架構。

比喻說明 Analogy

EC 就像一棟建築的「結構圖說」——已向政府主管機關申報備案的樑柱位置、承重牆位置都是 EC。想要移動這些元素，必須重新申請建照（監管申報）。但室內裝潢、油漆顏色就是 Non-EC，屋主自行決定即可，只需留存紀錄。

返工就像發現建築有結構偏差——要確認修繕方案是否動到備案的結構圖（EC），動到就必須向主管機關報告。

重點提示 Key Notes

ICH Q12 的革命性意義： 在 Q12 之前，幾乎所有重大製程變更都需要 PAS（Prior Approval Supplement），等待時間 6–12 個月。Q12 的 EC 框架讓「不影響 EC 的變更」可以走 CBE-30 甚至 PQS 內管理，大幅縮短時程。

台灣 CDMO 的現實挑戰： TFDA 在 2024 年以前尚未正式採納 ICH Q12。若 BLA 是美國 FDA 核准的，美國 Sponsor 期待 PACMP/EC 框架；但若同時在台灣生產供台灣市場，TFDA 可能仍要求走傳統 Type II variation 等效流程。建議與 Sponsor 的 RA 部門建立雙軌申報策略，而非單純複製美國申請。

5.8 Post-Approval Change Management Protocols (PACMP) 上市後變更管理協議

原文內容 Original Text

The PACMP tools described in ICH Q12 enable the planning and implementation of changes to ECs and/or CPPs, and thereby to regulatory filings, in an efficient structured manner. The PACMP (or comparability protocol) approved by the relevant health authority provides an agreement between the MAH and the regulatory agency and describes the changes and level of detail complying to the complexity, major variation, risk assessment, control strategy, and other pertinent elements. Once a PACMP is approved, when the proposed study design or acceptance criteria changes, an update of the protocol should be submitted for implementation as agreed upon in the original protocol. If changes are needed due to unanticipated or unforeseen changes in plans, prior approval of a new or revised PACMP by the regulatory agency may be required.

In the event of a reworking, the proposed comparability protocol (CP) for a modified manufacturing process should sufficiently cover the intended change and provide an increased control when compared to the existing process. A CP can be appropriately designed and/or drafted using a risk-based approach, with an in-depth or a high-level knowledge of process and product understanding. A risk assessment should be conducted on the potential drug substance and/or intermediate CQAs to be affected by parameter changes, including unspecified parameters.

Knowledge from previous developmental studies, small-scale studies, and/or pilot studies can be critical to identify potential risks to quality and provide support in the development of a suitable evaluation plan. The risk of adverse effects as a result of cumulative changes during a reworking should be carefully identified, justified, and included in the supporting information for the CP.

The reworked material should be assessed with suitable structural characterization, analytical and procedures. It should meet the criteria to demonstrate the lack of adverse effect, including immunogenicity on the reworked product quality considering the impact of structural changes in drug substance and/or drug product. Depending on the type and complexity of the drug substance, functional characterization and additional studies should also be included.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

PACMP (Post-Approval Change Management Protocol，上市後變更管理協議): MAH 與監管機構預先商定的「變更框架協議」——等同於一份「預先批准的變更藍圖」。一旦 PACMP 獲批，未來按此藍圖執行的變更可享受較快的審查路徑。

Comparability Protocol (CP，可比性協議): PACMP 的具體技術版本，描述變更後如何證明產品品質與原製程相當。必須涵蓋：分析測試計畫、接受標準、穩定性策略、免疫原性評估。

累積變更效應 (Cumulative Changes): 返工可能涉及多個製程步驟的同步調整。每一步驟單獨的風險可能低，但累積起來可能對蛋白質結構產生意外影響。CP 必須明確討論此風險。

比喻說明 Analogy

PACMP 就像預先向政府申請的「施工範圍許可令」——先核定哪些類型的修繕可以在一定範圍內自行決定，哪些需要每次個別申請。拿到許可令後，在許可範圍內施工只需備案；超出範圍才需重新申請。返工的 PACMP 意義相同：預先取得框架同意，加快事件發生時的回應速度。

重點提示 Key Notes

免疫原性是底線： 蛋白質返工最嚴格的要求之一是免疫原性評估。任何可能導致蛋白質結構改變的步驟（pH 改變、額外過濾、熱應力）都可能影響患者抗體反應，屬於患者安全考量，優先於一切商業考量。

CDMO 無 PACMP 時的實務對策：

  1. 與 Sponsor 確認現有 BLA 中是否已有核准的 PACMP 或 CP——如有，CDMO 僅需在框架內執行。
  2. 如無，CDMO 需協助 Sponsor 起草新的 CP，並預期 4–8 週的機構審查時間（CBE-30 路徑），或更長的 PAS 路徑。
  3. 此準備工作應納入 Technology Transfer 計畫的 RA 里程碑。

Amaran 作為台灣 CDMO，若承接一個 US FDA 核准的單株抗體 DS 製造，且 Sponsor 在 BLA 中已建立 PACMP，你的工廠執行返工時應優先確認：①返工步驟是否在 PACMP 涵蓋的 EC 範圍內；②若不在範圍內，誰負責向 FDA 提交修訂版 PACMP（通常是 MAH/Sponsor，CDMO 提供技術數據支持）；③台灣本地批次若供應台灣市場，需同步向 TFDA 評估是否需要額外申報。

5.9 Instabilities and Quality Concerns 不穩定性與品質疑慮

原文內容 Original Text

Protein degradation, aggregation, poses significant challenges in the manufacturing and quality control of biologicals, particularly during reworking processes. Given that, generally, these proteins are only marginally stable in solution, they are susceptible to various forms of instability. Chemical instabilities like deamidation, oxidation, and hydrolysis, alongside such physical instabilities as denaturation and noncovalent aggregation, can compromise the quality and efficacy of the drug substances.

During an event of reworking, proteins will be exposed to stress conditions, such as elevated temperatures, low pH, or hydrophobic surfaces, which might trigger conformational instabilities. Formulation of drug substances at high concentrations can further encourage protein aggregation, impacting long-term stability.

To mitigate these risks, purification steps are essential for removing impurities and aggregates. It is crucial to implement appropriate process controls that manage pH, buffer components, and denaturants to prevent protein unfolding, which can lead to aggregation. These controls are vital to ensuring the drug substance meet specifications, maintains good appearance without visible particulates, retains stability during holding and storage, preserves efficacy, and minimizes immunogenicity concerns.

Protein manufacturing and formulation are complex processes with several factors that can impact the quality and stability of therapeutic proteins, such as:

  • Protein Concentration and Viscosity: During reworking, increased protein concentration can lead to higher viscosity, which is problematic for formulations intended for subcutaneous injections. Techniques such as rheology and dynamic light-scattering can be employed to evaluate viscosity impacts.
  • Thermal Stress: Elevated temperatures, particularly during viral inactivation steps, can lead to protein denaturation and aggregation.
  • High Ionic Strength: The use of high-ionic-strength eluents in ion-exchange and hydrophobic interaction chromatography may promote aggregation.
  • pH Considerations: Eluents with pH values near the protein's isoelectric point, or far from it, can lead to aggregation.
  • Filtration Processes: Membrane contact, presence of air bubbles, and shear stress during filtration can induce aggregation. Ultrafiltration at high protein concentrations requires careful management.
  • Formulation Challenges: Bulk storage, freeze-thaw cycles, and agitation during formulation processes can stress proteins and cause aggregation.
  • Air-Water Interfaces: Exposure to air-water interfaces during formulation can promote aggregate formation by lowering the energy barrier for protein unfolding.
  • Excipients in Formulation: Certain excipients can stress proteins and accelerate aggregation. For instance, magnesium ions (Mg²⁺) have been noted for their potential to increase aggregation rates in some formulations.
  • Visible Particulates: It is critical to effectively monitor visible particulates after any reworking.
  • Leachables and Extractables: Detected in various sterilizing-grade filters (PVDF, PES, mixed cellulose ester). These demonstrate complex patterns of destabilizing and stabilizing effects on the physical and chemical stability of mAbs, including formation of visible and subvisible particles.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

蛋白質不穩定性的兩大類型：

化學性不穩定 (Chemical Instability):

  • 去醯胺化 (Deamidation)：Asn/Gln 側鏈水解，影響電荷分佈
  • 氧化 (Oxidation)：Met/Trp/Cys 被氧化，影響結合活性
  • 水解 (Hydrolysis)：肽鍵斷裂，產生碎片

物理性不穩定 (Physical Instability):

  • 變性 (Denaturation)：三維結構展開，喪失活性
  • 非共價聚集 (Noncovalent Aggregation)：可逆或不可逆聚集體，觸發免疫反應風險
  • 纖維化 (Fibrillation)：有序聚集結構，高免疫原性

比喻說明 Analogy

蛋白質分子就像精密折紙藝術品——正確折疊的三維結構才有功能。返工中的額外製程步驟像是把折紙重新處理：pH 偏移是用水泡折紙（紙張膨脹變形）、高溫是烘乾（結構收縮），震盪是反覆折疊（疲勞斷裂）。每一步都有可能讓折紙永久變形而無法還原。

重點提示 Key Notes

區分真正的不穩定 vs 分析方法干擾： 返工後若 SEC-HPLC 顯示聚集體增加，應先確認：①樣品前處理是否引入了人工聚集？②分析方法是否已驗證在此返工樣品基質中的適用性？③是否同步做了強制降解（forced degradation）對照？排除分析干擾後，才能將結果解讀為真實品質偏差。

Leachables & Extractables 的新興警示： PVDF 和 PES 等滅菌級過濾膜的可溶出物可能對 mAb 產生「界面活性劑效應」，加速蛋白質聚集。返工中若引入新的過濾器接觸材料，必須進行 L&E 評估——這是 §5.11 時程中「Analytical Testing (4–8 週)」的重要項目。

決策層級警示： 免疫原性風險 = 患者安全直接威脅 → 永遠優先於生產時程或成本考量（決策層級第一位）。

5.10 Roles and Communication 角色分工與溝通

原文內容 Original Text

People drive reworking projects and ensure timely delivery of the biological drug product to the patient. Top management plays a critical role by setting the strategic direction and ensuring that all processes adhere to regulatory standards. Meanwhile, other team members can focus on operational aspects, ensuring each function, whether it is research, development, manufacturing, quality, logistics, or support, is executed efficiently.

5.10.1 Roles

Roles can be defined individually or integrated into teams, which allows for flexibility in management and execution, ultimately contributing to successful outcomes for the project and timely delivery to the patient.

Their responsibilities include:

  • Providing Strategic Direction and Oversight: Approving the rework plan, scope, and objectives; ensuring alignment with organizational goals, regulatory requirements, and quality standards.
  • Ensuring Regulatory Compliance and Risk Management: Supporting adherence to GMP, regulatory guidelines, and internal SOPs; overseeing risk assessments and mitigation strategies.
  • Allocating Resources and Budget: Approving necessary budgets, personnel, facilities, and equipment; ensuring sufficient resources for effective execution.
  • Facilitating Cross-Functional Collaboration: Promoting communication among manufacturing, QA, regulatory, and other departments. Departments should explicitly include contract manufacturers responsible for intermediates and supply chain/demand planning teams, and manufacturing resilience given their impact on production scheduling, inventory, and communication with commercial functions.
  • Monitoring Progress and Performance: Receiving regular updates on status, challenges, and outcomes; making high-level decisions to address issues or deviations promptly.
  • Ensuring Regulatory Submissions and Documentation: Approving documentation for regulatory filings, deviations, or change controls associated with reworking; ensuring thorough documentation for compliance and traceability.
  • Managing Ethical and Safety Considerations: Prioritizing patient safety and product quality; supporting a culture of transparency and accountability.
  • Post-Rework Evaluation and Continuous Improvement: Reviewing rework outcomes including quality, stability, and regulatory feedback; implementing lessons learned to improve future processes.

5.10.2 Communication

Effective communication is essential to ensure that all stakeholders are informed, coordinated, and aligned throughout the reworking process. The best approach involves a structured, transparent, and timely communication strategy. Key principles and steps:

  • Develop a Communication Plan: Define objectives, identify stakeholders (internal: Quality, Manufacturing, R&D, RA, Management; external: regulators, partners, suppliers), establish channels, set frequency.
  • Ensure Transparency and Clarity: Articulate reasons for rework, potential impacts, and actions being taken. Communicate known risks and mitigation strategies upfront.
  • Maintain Documentation and Traceability: Record all communications, decisions, and approvals. Use controlled documents and logs.
  • Facilitate Cross-Functional Collaboration: Hold regular cross-disciplinary meetings. Encourage open dialogue to surface concerns early.
  • Implement Regulatory Communication Protocols: If rework impacts product quality or regulatory filings, prepare necessary documentation for regulators.
  • Designate Clear Roles and Responsibilities: Assign communication responsibilities to specific personnel (e.g., project manager, Quality head, process science/MSAT head).
  • Use Clear, Concise, and Consistent Messaging: Avoid technical jargon when communicating with nonexperts. Ensure messages are consistent across all channels.
  • Prepare for Crisis or Unexpected Events: Develop contingency communication plans for contamination, delays, or regulatory findings.

The best approach to communication management during reworking is a proactive, transparent, and well-structured strategy that involves developing a detailed communication plan, ensuring cross-functional collaboration, maintaining thorough documentation, and engaging regulatory authorities when needed.

重點提示 Key Notes — CDMO 跨組織溝通挑戰

  • 保密協議：返工涉及 Sponsor 的 IP（製程機密），所有溝通紀錄需符合 CDA/MSA 規定。

5.11 Submission & Approval Timelines 送件與核准時程

原文內容 Original Text

Reworking a biological drug substance involves carefully revisiting and potentially modifying production processes to ensure product quality, safety, and efficacy. The timeline for reworking such a drug substance and/or drug product can vary significantly depending on several factors, including the complexity of the product, extent of modifications needed, regulatory requirements, and manufacturing facility's capabilities.

Typical phases and approximate timelines:

  1. Assessment and Planning (2–4 weeks): Evaluation of the current process and product; identifying reasons for rework; developing a detailed rework plan and risk assessment.
  2. Process Development and Optimization (4–12 weeks): Adjusting upstream or downstream processes; small-scale laboratory or pilot studies; analytical method development or validation if needed.
  3. Analytical and Stability Testing (4–8 weeks): Confirming product integrity, potency, and purity post-rework; stability testing to ensure product remains within specifications.
  4. Scale-up and Validation (8–16 weeks): Manufacturing at larger scale; process validation batches to confirm reproducibility.
  5. Regulatory Documentation and Submission (4–12 weeks): Preparing amendments or supplemental dossiers; addressing agency queries and obtaining prior approval.
  6. Manufacturing and Release (2–6 weeks, variable): Final production run; quality release-testing and documentation.
"Total Estimated Timeline: Typically, reworking a drug substance can take approximately 6 to 12 months, but in some cases, especially with complex modifications or regulatory hurdles, it may extend longer." — PDA TR74 §5.11

Factors Influencing Reworking Timeline:

  • Regulatory Pathways: The need for prior approval or additional filings can significantly affect timelines. Approval processes differ by region.
  • Extent of Process Changes: More extensive changes require deeper assessments and validation efforts.
  • Availability of Raw Materials and Manufacturing Capacity: Supply chain considerations and manufacturing capacity can cause delays.
  • Quality and Analytical Testing Turnaround Times: Time for quality and analytical testing can impact timelines, especially if retesting is required.
  • Single-use Systems: If new single-use systems with greater process contact-surface area or new polymers are introduced, a detailed leachable and extractable impact assessment will need to be performed before execution.
  • Regulatory Agency Review Times: Variability in how long agencies take to review submissions and respond can introduce uncertainties.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts — 申報路徑對照

美國 FDA 三種申報路徑（生物製品 BLA）：

  • Prior Approval Supplement (PAS)：上市前需獲核准，FDA 目標審查時限 6 個月（一般）或 2 個月（急件）。適用：高風險製程變更、CQA 可能受影響。
  • Changes Being Effected in 30 Days (CBE-30)：提交後 30 天可實施（FDA 如無反對）。適用：中度風險變更。
  • Annual Report (AR)：年度彙整申報，實施前不需等待。適用：低風險、輕微變更。

歐盟 EMA 主要路徑：

  • Type II Variation（主要變更）：審查時限 60 天（有效時限），對應 FDA PAS，高風險製程變更。
  • Type IB Variation（中度變更）：30 天通知後可實施，對應 FDA CBE-30。
  • Type IA (IAIN, 立即通知) / 年度評估：對應 FDA Annual Report。

比喻說明 Analogy

返工時程就像飛機緊急迫降後的「適航性恢復計畫」——工程師評估損傷（第 1 階段）→ 設計修復方案並小規模測試（第 2 階段）→ 完整結構測試（第 3 階段）→ 試飛驗證（第 4 階段）→ 向民航局申請恢復飛行許可（第 5 階段）→ 正式投入服務（第 6 階段）。跳過任何一步，民航局就不會發許可。製藥返工的監管邏輯完全相同。

重點提示 Key Notes

CDMO 時程規劃的三大陷阱：

  1. 低估監管等待時間：若返工需 PAS（FDA）+ Type II Variation（EMA），兩個機構同時送件，平行審查仍可能需要 6–12 個月。不能假設所有市場可同步獲批。
  2. Leachables & Extractables 測試時間：若引入新的一次性系統（Single-Use）接觸材料，L&E 研究本身可能需要 8–16 週，必須提前納入計畫。
  3. 穩定性數據缺口：返工批次需要至少初期穩定性數據（通常 3 個月）才能支持申報文件。穩定性研究必須在最終放大驗證批完成後立即啟動。

5.12 Quality Unit Preparedness 品質部門的預備狀態

原文內容 Original Text

The Quality Unit requires comprehensive training documents to ensure compliance and efficiency during unexpected events of reworking. Typical documents that might be needed:

  • Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs): SOPs for rework processes with step-by-step instructions; SOPs for identifying and segregating nonconforming materials.
  • Regulatory Guidelines: Relevant regulatory guidelines from FDA, EMA, or other local/national standards related to rework.
  • Quality Control Procedures: Documents detailing quality control checks required before, during, and after rework.
  • Safety Training Documents: Training materials on safety procedures specific to rework operations.
  • Documentation and Recordkeeping Training: Instructions for accurate documentation and recordkeeping to ensure traceability and compliance.
  • Risk Assessment and Management: Guidelines for conducting risk assessments related to reworking processes.
  • Material Handling Procedures: Proper procedures for handling materials safely during the rework process.
  • Equipment Handling and Maintenance: Training on specific equipment used for reworking, including maintenance and calibration.
  • Continuous Improvement Practices: Documents that facilitate identifying areas for improvement in rework processes.

Steps to effectively prepare for a reworking event:

  1. Risk Assessment and Planning: Conduct thorough risk assessment; prepare detailed rework plan with objectives, resources, timelines, and personnel responsibilities.
  2. Documentation and SOPs: Ensure all SOPs related to rework are up to date; document every step clearly and maintain traceability.
  3. Training: Provide training for personnel involved in reworking activities on new procedures, equipment use, and quality requirements.
  4. Material Management: Review and inspect raw materials, components, and consumables; establish segregation system for nonconforming materials.
  5. Monitoring, Review, and Control: Set up monitoring and control mechanisms; implement checks and balances to detect deviations promptly.
  6. Communication: Maintain clear communication channels within the team and with other departments.
  7. Quality Assurance: Conduct QA checks before, during, and after the rework. The quality unit is ultimately responsible for ensuring product quality and providing quality oversight throughout any reworking activity.
  8. Feedback and Improvement: After completing the rework, gather feedback and conduct thorough process review to identify improvement areas; update policies and procedures to reflect lessons learned.

It is crucial for the quality unit, comprising both quality assurance and quality control, to be well-prepared to manage the situations effectively and ensure that product quality and compliance are maintained. Ultimately, preparedness involves a combination of detailed planning, ongoing training, and resource management and ensuring that the team is proactive in adapting to any new challenges that arise during the reworking process.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

品質部門的雙重角色：

① 事前把關 (Pre-Approval Gating)： 在返工方案提交監管機構前，QU 是最終技術與法規審核的把關者——未獲 QU 批准的返工協議不得送件，更不得執行。

② 執行期間監督 (During-Execution Oversight)： 返工批次的每一個關鍵步驟（pH 調整、溫度控制、過濾操作）都需 QA 現場或遠端即時監督，QC 須執行即時分析以提供決策數據。

QA vs QC 分工：

  • QA：系統合規性、偏差管理、批次放行決策
  • QC：分析測試執行、OOS 調查、方法適用性確認

比喻說明 Analogy

品質部門的返工預備工作就像消防站的「災害應變演練」——消防員不能等到火災發生才開始學習操作消防車。返工 SOP、不符合物料隔離程序、緊急分析方法——都必須在平時演練好、文件備妥。當返工事件真正發生時，QU 的工作是「執行已訓練的程序」而非「臨時學習新技能」。

重點提示 Key Notes

「事前準備」的五個關鍵文件清單（CDMO 具體版）：

  1. 返工決策矩陣 SOP：定義哪些偏差可考慮返工、哪些應直接拒絕——防止返工成為「逃避拒批的捷徑」。
  2. 不符合物料隔離程序：返工中間物必須與正常批次完全實體隔離，並有明確標識。
  3. 額外分析方法清單：預先識別哪些 ECLs（延伸特性測試）可能需要用到——例如 SEC-HPLC 聚集體分析、icIEF 電荷異質性。
  4. 穩定性研究啟動標準作業：返工批次完成後，穩定性研究必須即時啟動，不得延遲。
  5. Sponsor 通知程序：符合 QAA 要求的通知時限與格式，需預先建立模板。

5.13 Conclusions 結論

原文內容 Original Text

In urgent situations, such as a global pandemic (e.g., COVID-19), wars, or natural disasters (e.g., Category 5 hurricanes, catastrophic earthquakes, major floods, meteor catastrophe), certain regulatory flexibilities or deviations may be considered by the MAH to address critical supply issues, like immediate drug shortages. However, reworking an OOS drug substance generally involves significant quality and regulatory considerations, such as:

  • Regulatory Frameworks and Guidance: Regulatory agencies such as the U.S. FDA, EMA, and other agencies offer guidance on managing deviations, OOS results, and manufacturing challenges. In urgent situations, these agencies may provide tailored guidance or emergency procedures. However, deviations from approved specifications generally require proper justification and thorough documentation.
  • Reworking of OOS Drug Substance: Reworking involves processing material that initially failed to meet specifications to bring it into compliance. This process must be validated and scientifically justified, demonstrating that reworking does not compromise product quality, safety, stability and efficacy.
  • Regulatory Approval and Documentation: Any decision to rework should be thoroughly documented, including risk assessments and validation data. For licensed products, regulatory authorities often require that deviations or exceptions from established processes be reported. Depending on the jurisdiction and the specific nature and impact of the change, prior approval may be necessary.
  • Risk Management and Justification: In emergencies or shortages, regulators may offer some flexibility, but the company must ensure reworking does not compromise drug quality. A comprehensive risk assessment, incorporating knowledge management, should be conducted to evaluate its impact on safety and efficacy.
  • Good Manufacturing Practice Compliance: Reworking must adhere to GMP standards, and the process should be validated. Proper change control procedures should be followed.
"Reworking an OOS drug substance should never be treated as routine. However, in urgent situations such as a pandemic or critical shortage, biotechnology companies may consider reworking if it is properly justified, validated, and documented. They should proactively engage with regulatory authorities, possibly under emergency use or contingency provisions, to ensure compliance and maintain product integrity." — PDA TR74 §5.13

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

緊急情況下的監管彈性 (Regulatory Flexibility in Emergencies): COVID-19 期間，FDA 和 EMA 均發布了緊急指南，允許在特定條件下對製程進行例外偏差——但這並非「無需申報」的免責，而是「加速審查 + 後續補件」的程序調整。監管彈性的前提仍是患者安全不受損。

「絕不視為例行程序」的核心意涵： 返工從本質上是一個例外事件 (exception event)。若某工廠每批次都在考慮返工，這已不是返工問題，而是根本製程能力不足的系統性問題——必須走 CAPA，從根因改善，而非重複申請返工核准。

比喻說明 Analogy

返工就像外科手術中的「緊急處置方案」——有時必要，但絕不應是計劃外手術的常態。若一個醫院每週都要對同一類手術採取「緊急處置」，問題不在緊急處置程序，而在術前評估和手術規範本身。製藥返工的哲學完全相同：準備好應對，但以避免為目標。

重點提示 Key Notes — 積極主動的心態 (Proactive Mindset)

TR74 §5.13 傳遞的核心訊息——預防勝於應對：

  • 提前建立 PACMP：不要等到 OOS 發生才起草——這就像只有在火災發生時才去申請消防許可。
  • 定期 EC 審查：每年與 Sponsor 和 RA 確認現有 EC 清單是否反映當前製程實際狀況。
  • 主動接觸主管機關：TR74 明確建議「proactively engage with regulatory authorities」——在問題擴大前主動諮詢，比被動應對更有效。對台灣 CDMO 而言，這意味著與 Sponsor 共同主動尋求 FDA/EMA 的非正式溝通 (Type C meeting, 科學諮詢)。
  • CAPA 文化優先：每一次返工事件結束後，根本原因分析和 CAPA 計畫應視為強制性輸出，而非可選項。防止重複發生是最有效的長期成本控制。

Section 5 Part B 總結 — 決策層級最終確認

回顧 Section 5 (Part B) 整章，每一個主題都指向相同的核心決策框架：

無菌保證 (Sterility) > CQA 保護 (Patient Safety) > 商業彈性 (Business)

  • §5.6–5.8：即使有 PACMP 和 EC 彈性，絕不以降低 CQA 標準換取申報速度。
  • §5.9：蛋白質不穩定和免疫原性風險是患者安全的直接威脅，優先於一切。
  • §5.10：溝通透明是維護患者安全的信任基礎，不得因商業壓力壓縮。
  • §5.11–5.12：時程再緊，也不能跳過驗證和 QU 把關。
  • §5.13：緊急彈性是工具，不是漏洞——患者安全永遠不妥協。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的工廠在過去 12 個月內對同一個 DS 製程發生了 3 次返工。Top Management 認為這是正常的「製程調整」。你作為 QA Manager 應如何回應，並採取哪些具體行動？
  2. 台灣發生重大地震，某 mAb DS 批次因停電導致冷鏈中斷，品質狀況不確定。Sponsor 要求 CDMO 立即評估是否可以「緊急返工」以避免藥品短缺。第一步應如何處理，而不是直接開始返工？

Section 5 Part B 重點回顧

  • PAC 六大決策維度（§5.6）：Table 5.6-1 提供了從定義範圍到執行實施的完整框架——每一個返工案例都應能在此框架中找到對應位置。
  • ICH Q12 的 EC 革命（§5.7）：Established Conditions 的建立是降低監管申報負擔的關鍵前提。若 BLA 尚未建立 EC，所有重大返工預設走 PAS 路徑。
  • PACMP = 預先取得的授權（§5.8）：一份事前獲批的 PACMP 在返工事件發生時可節省數月時間——這是最有價值的 RA 預防性投資。Comparability Protocol 必須覆蓋免疫原性評估。
  • 蛋白質不穩定十大風險因子（§5.9）：從濃度/黏度到 Leachables & Extractables，每一個返工新步驟都可能引入新的應力——必須用適當的分析工具預先評估。
  • CDMO 的跨組織溝通挑戰（§5.10）：返工不是 CDMO 的內部事務——Sponsor、監管機構、供應鏈都是必要利害關係人。溝通矩陣和 RACI 應寫入 Quality Agreement。
  • 6–12 個月的現實時程（§5.11）：PAS 等待期可能長達 12 個月；L&E 研究可能額外增加 8–16 週。早期規劃、早期送件是縮短總時程的唯一方法。
  • 品質部門雙重角色（§5.12）：QU 既是事前把關者（不批准就不送件），也是執行期間的監督者。返工 SOP、隔離程序、額外分析方法都需在平時準備好。
  • 積極主動的心態（§5.13）：返工應對是最後一道防線；預防——通過健全的製程驗證、EC 建立和 PACMP——才是正道。每一次返工事件必須以 CAPA 收尾，防止重複發生。
