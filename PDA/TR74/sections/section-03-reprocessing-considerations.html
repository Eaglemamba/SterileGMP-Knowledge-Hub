3.0 Reprocessing Considerations — Introduction 再處理考量：導論

原文內容 Original Text

Reprocessing (3, 9, 11–15), or repetition of an existing biopharmaceutical unit operation, requires a well-designed process, supportive data, and validation to augment the existing manufacturing process. Importantly, the supportive data must provide the manufacturer with a high degree of confidence (assurance) that the drug substance or drug product produced by reprocessing meets the predefined quality standards for the drug substance or drug product and is comparable to a nonreprocessed batch. The reprocessed and reworked biological drug substances and sterile drug products should adhere to all CQAs associated (including bioburden and endotoxins) to normal batch disposition and release. Enhanced testing protocols are often required to assess the quality attributes of the reprocessed material, ensuring that it remains consistent with the established manufacturing process.

Reprocessing in pharmaceutical manufacturing can occur under various scenarios that can be broadly categorized as proactive and reactive approaches.

In GMP, a risk-based approach (6), to reprocessing is incorporated by systematically assessing and managing potential risks associated with reactive reprocessing activities. This begins with identifying and evaluating the nature of deviations and their potential impact on product quality and patient safety. By prioritizing areas of greatest concern, manufacturers can focus resources and controls where they are most needed. Decisions regarding reprocessing are made based on these risk assessments, ensuring that only safe and effective practices are implemented.

For any event that requires reprocessing, the manufacturer must perform a formal root-cause investigation of the unit operation excursion if the excursion is not within the design space. Additional development studies may be required to supplement the root-cause investigation. Consideration of any proposed reprocessing by regulators will likely be based on the root-cause analysis and the impact of reprocessing on product quality.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Reprocessing（再處理）：重複執行現有製程中的一個單元操作（Unit Operation），目的是補救不合規的中間產品或原料藥，同時仍維持在已驗證的設計空間內。

與 Reworking（重工）的根本差別：再處理是「多做一次相同的步驟」；重工是「引入原製程設計以外的新步驟」。TR74 將兩者分開處理，法規負擔也截然不同。

CQAs（Critical Quality Attributes，關鍵品質屬性）：包括純度、效力、聚集體、HCP/DNA 殘留、生物負荷（Bioburden）及內毒素（Endotoxin）。再處理後所有 CQA 必須與正常批次可比較（Comparable）。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像廚師在米其林餐廳的後廚。若一道主菜的鹽分稍微超標（偏差），廚師可以再稀釋一次——這就是「再處理」：仍是相同的烹飪步驟，只是多做一遍。但若需要加入全新食材或改變烹飪方式，就成了「重工」，必須重新評估整道菜的品質。

法規的期望也一樣：再處理的風險邊界更窄，但程序也更清楚；重工則需要更多資料支撐和更嚴格的核准。

重點提示 Key Notes

決策層級提示：再處理的最終目的始終是保護患者安全（Patient Safety）。TR74 明確要求：再處理批次與未再處理批次必須具備「高度信心的可比性（high degree of confidence of comparability）」——這不是簡單說「結果看起來差不多」，而需要有系統性的資料包和驗證。

TFDA 與 PIC/S 框架下，若再處理未事先納入 CMC 申請，即使 QA 認為品質可接受，亦不得逕行批次放行。

3.1 Proactive Approach 主動預防式再處理

原文內容 Original Text

In a proactive scenario, the potential need for reprocessing is identified in advance through predetermined criteria. The execution of the reprocessing activities occurs through a predefined concurrent validation protocol with clear acceptance criteria or through previously validated procedures may be reviewed and approved by applicable regulatory agencies. This approach includes:

  1. Predefined Criteria: Manufacturers outline specific conditions or triggers that would necessitate reprocessing, allowing for a structured response to an event that may require reprocessing. Post reprocessing, there should be sufficient data to support a conclusion that any given reprocessed batch of a drug substance or drug product is uniform and meets the defined acceptance criteria.
  2. Validated Procedures: Reprocessing is executed according to a validated procedure that has undergone detailed review by the manufacturer's quality unit. This concurrent validation ensures that the process is capable of consistently producing a product that meets established quality standards. The validation protocol should include the scope of the manufacturing process that is being reprocessed, number of batches required, tests to be performed, and acceptance criteria. The proposed acceptance criteria for all tests should be appropriately justified. The concurrent validation approach should be described within the Validation Master Plan. An example of reprocessing that might be specifically covered by a Biological License Application (BLA) would be repeating viral filtration following a failed filter-integrity test. Because viral filters occasionally fail, manufacturers may be approved for refiltration of the protein drug substance or drug product (following successful validation). The approach to validation should be consistent with a lifecycle approach to validation, as discussed in the U.S. Food and Drug Administration Guidance for Industry: Process Validation: General Principles and Practices and PDA Technical Report No. 60 (Revised 2026) Process Validation: A Lifecycle Approach (10).

When reprocessing has been prospectively validated, it is appropriate to provide the full validation protocol and report in a regulatory submission and to follow the validated process where indicated. When reprocessing is anticipated to undergo concurrent validation, the protocol—inclusive of initial starting conditions and appropriate acceptance criteria to determine whether successful completion of a reprocessing step has been achieved—should be provided.

  1. Regulatory Guidance derived from Established Protocols: An essential prerequisite for the acceptance of a reprocessing step is a clear identification of the root cause. For biological products, these situations are usually restricted to some refiltration, reconcentration steps upon technical failure of equipment, or mechanical breakdown of a chromatography column. These steps should be appropriately described, validated, and included in the regulatory submission. Such documentation should include the description of conditions for which reprocessing could be applied and a demonstration that the reprocessing step(s) does not impact the quality of the drug substance and/or drug product.

The sequence of steps detailed in Section 3.6 provides a roadmap for implementing proactive reprocessing, ensuring that all necessary evaluations and approvals are addressed before action is taken.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Proactive Approach（主動預防式方法）的核心定義：在產品上市申請（BLA/MAA/NDA）時，就將再處理步驟的觸發條件（Trigger）、驗證方案和接受標準全部預先寫入 CMC 資料包。未來真的發生時，依已批准程序執行即可。

三個關鍵要素：

  • Predefined Criteria（預定觸發條件）：哪些製程失敗會啟動再處理？（例：病毒過濾器完整性測試失敗）
  • Validated Procedures（已驗證程序）：再處理步驟本身必須經過前瞻性驗證（Prospective Validation）或同步驗證（Concurrent Validation）。
  • Regulatory Approval（法規預核准）：再處理方案必須載於 Module 3.2.S.2.2 / 3.2.S.2.5 / 3.2.S.2.6，獲主管機關同意後方可執行。

比喻說明 Analogy

Proactive 就像飛機的標準緊急程序手冊（Emergency Checklist）：飛行員在起飛前就知道「如果引擎在 V1 前熄火，執行步驟 A-B-C」。因為事先演練過、預先批准過，遇到事件時只需按清單執行，不需要臨場發明解決方案。

相較之下，Reactive 就是引擎真的熄火了、但手冊裡沒有這個情境——需要即時評估風險、再向主管機關申報取得批准才能處置。

重點提示 Key Notes

CDMO 實務關鍵：對於 clinical-phase CDMO，Proactive 方案意味著在 IND/CTA 階段就必須討論再處理的可能性。這需要 Process Development 和 Regulatory Affairs 的早期介入，通常在 Phase 2/3 轉換時最為關鍵。

BLA 中的病毒再過濾範例（FDA 已接受）：病毒過濾器因偶發完整性失敗需要更換時，若事先驗證並申報再過濾方案，法規上屬於 Proactive 再處理，不需要逐批次報告。

Concurrent Validation（同步驗證）的使用條件：若尚未完成前瞻性驗證，允許在商業化過程中同步收集驗證資料，但前提是驗證方案必須事先獲得批准、且接受標準必須預先定義。

實務應用 Practical Application — CDMO 情境

某 CDMO 客戶正在準備一款 mAb 的 BLA 申請。在 UF/DF 步驟中，歷史資料顯示約 3% 的批次出現跨膜壓（TMP）不穩定、導致濃縮終點未能達到目標值。

Proactive 策略：在 BLA 的 Module 3.2.S.2.5 中寫入「若 TMP 偏低且未達目標濃度，允許延長再循環時間不超過 X 小時，並執行額外的 DF cycles，驗證批次數 ≥ 2 批，接受標準為純度 ≥ Y%、聚集體 ≤ Z%。」

核准後，若再發生類似事件，可直接按已批准程序執行，無需偏差申報或補充申請——大幅縮短批次放行時間，避免供應中斷。

3.2 Reactive Approach 被動應對式再處理

原文內容 Original Text

Reactive reprocessing occurs in response to unforeseen circumstances during the manufacturing process. The same categories of data and decisions required for a proactive reprocessing approach apply to this approach, except that less time and information may be available for performing assessments. As a result, reactive reprocessing is better suited for steps that are simple (such as microbial or virus filtration after failure of filter-integrity testing) and have a low likelihood of affecting product quality. The sequence of steps discussed in Section 3.6 may be used to guide the approach to reactive reprocessing, starting with the design of a reprocessing unit operation (see Section 3.6, Step 6).

Two distinct reactive reprocessing scenarios are described as follows:

  1. Nonprospectively Validated Reprocessing at an In-Process Hold Point
    1. In this scenario, a need arises for a nonprospectively validated reprocessing unit operation while a product is held at an in-process stage. This may involve scenarios such as freezing the product for an extended period.
    2. During the hold period, manufacturers can develop and execute a plan to validate the reprocessing step. This provides assurance that the product meets all the required quality attributes subsequent to the product concurrent release.
  2. Immediate Reprocessing During a Manufacturing Run
    1. This scenario occurs when a reprocessing need emerges unexpectedly during a manufacturing run. In such cases, the reprocessing step may need to be implemented before its validation.
    2. If a rapid design and implementation of the reprocessing option is feasible, it may be executed "at risk." This means that the reprocessing can occur with the understanding that supportive data must be generated and reviewed to ensure product quality before the batch is released to the market.

Of the two reactive reprocessing scenarios described above, the second scenario (#2) carries the highest degree of risk and is likely to be implemented only with relatively simple unit operations that affect a limited number of CQAs, such as dead-end filtration for drug substance or drug product. Release of the reprocessed batch using a reactive scenario should only be performed upon receiving regulatory approval. (See Section 4.0 for strategies on regulatory submissions).

A proactive process design approach that accounts for reprocessing could apply to more complex (such as ultrafiltration, diafiltration, concentration, chromatography, viral removal steps, and polishing) steps with significant benefit to the manufacturer.

In principle, almost any unit operation may be considered for potential reprocessing as long as it is demonstrated to consistently deliver a drug substance or drug product that meets the same specified quality commitments of the standard manufacturing process. Reprocessing simple unit operations is relatively well accepted and understood by regulators and the industry. Reprocessing a sterile drug substance and reprocessing and reworking of sterile-biological batches requires critical attention to maintaining sterility, endotoxin, and particulate controls. Reprocessing of nonsterile drug substances manufactured in low bioburden facilities also requires special attention to be paid to microbial and particulate controls.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Reactive Reprocessing（被動應對式再處理）：在未預期的製程偏差發生後，才決定是否、如何進行再處理。關鍵限制在於時間壓力大、資訊不足。

兩種 Reactive 情境的風險差異：

  • 情境 1（Hold Point 暫停保存）：產品可凍存或保存，利用此期間補充驗證資料後再放行——風險相對可控。
  • 情境 2（製程中即時再處理）：最高風險。執行時驗證尚未完成，屬於「at risk（承擔風險）」操作。放行前必須獲得主管機關核准。

"At Risk" 執行的意涵：CDMO 需了解，「at risk」不代表可以隨意操作——它意味著企業承擔風險先執行，但必須事後補齊驗證資料，且批次放行需等待法規核准。

比喻說明 Analogy

Reactive 就像深夜餐廳廚房突然缺少某關鍵食材，廚師必須即興調整食譜。情境 1 是「先暫停出餐、去採購新食材並試菜後再上桌」（Hold Point 策略）；情境 2 是「現在就要出菜、廚師即興更替食材、餐廳老闆在旁監督並通知食安局」（At Risk 策略）。

顯然情境 2 的風險遠高於情境 1，因此 TR74 建議對複雜步驟（如 UF/DF、層析）優先採用 Proactive 方案。

重點提示 Key Notes

複雜操作不適合 Reactive：UF/DF、層析（Chromatography）、病毒去除（Viral Removal）、拋光（Polishing）步驟，因影響的 CQA 多且複雜，不應依賴 Reactive 方案。這些步驟的再處理設計，應在產品開發階段就規劃為 Proactive。

無菌批次的額外要求：無論 Proactive 或 Reactive，一旦涉及無菌（Sterile）原料藥或藥品的再處理，無菌保證（Sterility Assurance）、內毒素控制、微粒控制必須得到明確評估——這是 Section 3.8 的主題。

TFDA/PIC/S 框架重點：Reactive 再處理批次的放行，須先取得主管機關核准（無論是 TFDA、EMA 或 FDA），不得單靠廠內 QA 決定放行。

3.3 Reprocessing Frequency 再處理頻率

原文內容 Original Text

A manufacturing process that requires frequent reprocessing is considered to be poorly controlled and not in a validated state. While there is no formal definition of "frequent," it would be reasonable to question the state-of-control for a unit operation that requires reprocessing as often as 10% of the time (16). (Ten percent is given here as an illustrative example and is not intended to be a prescriptive limit.)

It is important to distinguish between two types of reprocessing frequency:

  • Number of reprocessed unit operations within a batch
  • Number of batches that require reprocessing over a defined time period

Given that the Annual Product Review/Product Quality Review provides a one-year performance summary, it may be appropriate to evaluate reprocessing trends within that annual timeframe. Under circumstances where reprocessing becomes recurring or trends upward, a reassessment of the process design should be strongly considered. If reprocessing is carried out frequently or reaches a specified percentage of manufactured batches, it must be considered part of the normal process, described as such in the application, and receives approval.

3.3.1 Reprocessing Frequency per Batch

The frequency of reprocessing for the same batch at commercial-scale reprocessing should be restricted to a maximum number of reprocessing events according to process development data for small-scale reprocessing.

Validation of the reprocessing step should be supported by a scientifically justified number of demonstration runs. While no fixed minimum is mandated by regulatory agencies, performing at least two independent runs is common practice to demonstrate reproducibility. The exact number should be based on process complexity, risk assessment, and historical data to ensure the process consistently achieves predefined acceptance criteria.

The reprocessing frequency is established by the manufacturer based on appropriate studies at either small scale or same scale. Small-scale validation studies are commonly used to establish frequency for a reprocessing event for a particular unit operation. These studies may be conducted before implementation of a reprocessing approach at manufacturing scale. In a reprocessing event, product quality can change after repeated run exposures during filtration or chromatography processing steps and may impact the stability of long-term storage. There is no rule-of-thumb for frequency determination; the manufacturer must establish it for each project based on its impact on quality attributes. For example, a simple dead-end filtration, typically a maximum of three reprocessings, can be included in a small-scale reprocessing study. Some protein molecules may not be stable for a second reprocessing; in that case, maximum reprocessing may be restricted to only one reprocessing event.

These studies are used to obtain data on product quality attributes (e.g., aggregation) resulting from exposure to physical/shear stress during repeated unit operations. Small-scale validation-study design should be predictive of the commercial process. This will ensure that the data generated is relevant to large-scale. Small-scale reprocessing studies must use the same protein solution for the repeated number of runs and the samples to be withdrawn after each run. Product quality attributes of each run should be compared against the initial and intermediate feed-material to understand the impact of multiple exposures and to determine the frequency for the large-scale reprocessing event. All reprocessing events, including repeated runs, need to be completed within the allowed chemical and microbial hold time for intermediate pools or scientifically justified if there is any excursion from validated hold time.

When developing a proactive reprocessing step, a review of the development data and history may identify process steps with inherent variability that may, on rare occasions, lead to a need for reprocessing (e.g., failing in-process control (IPC) assay results, filter integrity failure, difficult separation step). The variability must be scientifically understood.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

兩個頻率維度需同時監控：

  • 維度 A：單批次內重複次數（per batch）——同一步驟在同一批次內最多允許再做幾次？需靠小規模驗證研究確立上限。
  • 維度 B：批次間頻率（across batches）——一年內有幾%的批次需要再處理？若趨勢上升，代表製程失控，須重新評估設計。

10% 為例示性門檻，非法規強制紅線：TR74 特別說明 10% 僅為舉例，實際門檻應依產品風險和製程特性自行定義，並納入 APR/PQR 趨勢分析。

公式與計算 Formula

再處理頻率監控建議指標：

批次頻率 =
  需再處理批次數 / 同期製造總批次數 × 100%

建議觸發評估門檻（範例）：
  > 5% → 啟動製程設計審查
  > 10% → 視為製程未受控，須提交 CAPA 及法規通報評估

單批次再處理次數上限（範例）：
  Dead-end filtration  → max 3 次
  UF/DF               → 依驗證研究定義（通常 max 1-2 次）
  Chromatography      → 依 resin 性能數據定義

比喻說明 Analogy

再處理頻率就像汽車的發動失敗率。一輛車偶爾需要「再試一次」才能發動（偶發性失敗），屬於可接受的正常變異。但若每十次就有一次無法一次發動成功，汽車廠商需要重新設計引擎——不能只靠「再試一次」的手動補救。法規邏輯完全相同。

重點提示 Key Notes

Hold Time 是關鍵限制：所有再處理操作（包括重複執行次數）必須在允許的中間產品保留時間（Hold Time）內完成。若超出已驗證的 Hold Time，需要額外的科學論證，而非默默延長。

APR/PQR 是趨勢監控的主要工具：TFDA 與 PIC/S 均要求年度產品品質回顧（Annual Product Review）須包含再處理趨勢評估。若頻率上升，應觸發製程設計重審，而非單純記錄。

小規模研究必須有預測性（Predictive）：小規模研究設計必須能代表商業規模，包括相同的剪切應力（Shear Stress）、相同的蛋白質濃度範圍，否則資料不能外推到商業化製程。

3.4 Continuation vs. Reprocessing (Repetition) of a Unit Operation 製程延續 vs. 再處理的判定

原文內容 Original Text

Most unit operations include some sort of in-process control measurement to determine whether the step meets the objective (e.g., a concentration, pH). An adjustment to bring a product within acceptable limits is not considered reprocessing and, instead, should be designed as a process "continuation." Procedures should account for continuation to perform an adjustment within defined limits. Without appropriate instructions, continuations in processing may be erroneously considered as reprocessing and lead to procedural deviations.

For example, during a step in the ultrafiltration-diafiltration operation, concentration is measured at different volumes to check for the desired target concentration. If the concentration does not meet the target specification, the process can be continued until the target specification is met, and it is considered a continuation of the process, not reprocessing. Similarly, in the titration process, the addition of titrant to achieve the desired pH or conductivity set point may be considered a continuation of the process. Likewise, adding a buffer dilution to achieve the target product concentration is considered a continuation of the process, not a reprocessing activity.

In the case of drug product manufacturing, when large-volume containers are thawed and only a partial amount is used for filling, the remaining formulated drug substance may need to be refrozen for subsequent use. This practice may be considered reprocessing if not included in an approved application. Supporting freeze-thaw stability data from reduced- or at-scale studies can help justify the impact on drug product quality.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Continuation（製程延續）vs. Reprocessing（再處理）的判定原則：

  • Continuation：在製程批記錄（Batch Record）已預先設計的框架內，為達到目標值而延長或調整操作。屬於正常製程的一部分，不需要偏差申報。
  • Reprocessing：完成一個製程步驟後，因結果不符合規格而需要「重新來過」，屬於偏差觸發的補救行動。

關鍵問題：「這個調整動作有沒有事先寫在 SOP 或批記錄裡？」若有，且在定義的限度內——Continuation；若無，或超出設計範圍——Reprocessing。

比喻說明 Analogy

烘焙蛋糕時，食譜說「烘烤 25-35 分鐘直到牙籤插入不沾黏」——在這個時間範圍內繼續烘烤是 Continuation（延續）。但若蛋糕已出爐冷卻、發現中心未熟、需要重新放回烤箱，那就是 Reprocessing（再處理），因為這超出了原始設計流程。

差異點就在「是否在原製程設計的邊界內發生」。

重點提示 Key Notes

不當分類的代價：若將 Continuation 誤分類為 Reprocessing，會觸發不必要的偏差調查和法規申報；反之，若將 Reprocessing 誤分類為 Continuation 並未申報，則屬於重大 GMP 違規。

凍融循環的特殊風險：大容量容器（Large-Volume Container）部分使用後需要重新冷凍，這在法規上屬於「灰色地帶」——需要事先在申請案中包含凍融穩定性資料（Freeze-Thaw Stability Data）並獲批准，方可視為已核准流程而非 Reprocessing。

CDMO 實務提醒：製程批記錄（Batch Record）的設計，應明確標示哪些 IPC 結果不合格可觸發 Continuation，哪些須啟動偏差程序。這是避免操作人員現場誤判的關鍵文件設計。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若 UF/DF 步驟的目標濃度為 20 mg/mL（範圍 18-22 mg/mL），批記錄設計允許「持續超濾直到達到 20 mg/mL」，但已超過驗證的最大過濾時間限制——此時是 Continuation 還是 Reprocessing？
  2. 一個 DS 批次在除菌過濾（0.22 µm）後暫存，後來 QA 決定額外增加一道 0.22 µm 再過濾做為額外保障——這屬於哪種情況？為什麼？
3.5 Quality Concerns During the Reprocessing of Biologicals 生物藥品再處理的品質考量

原文內容 Original Text

Protein aggregation is a key concern during manufacturing and quality assessment, particularly in the context of reprocessing. Most therapeutic proteins are only marginally stable in solutions and are susceptible to chemical instabilities (e.g., deamidation, oxidation, hydrolysis) as well as physical instabilities (e.g., denaturation and noncovalent aggregation).

During reprocessing, proteins may be exposed to various stress conditions. Process controls play a critical role in mitigating the effects of destabilizing factors, such as pH, buffer components, and denaturants, that can induce protein unfolding and subsequent aggregation.

Several stress factors during reprocessing can contribute to protein aggregation and instability. Key considerations include:

  • Increased Viscosity During Reprocessing: Higher protein concentration can increase viscosity, affecting subcutaneous formulation. Evaluate using rheology or dynamic light-scattering.
  • Thermal Stress: Elevated temperatures (e.g., during viral inactivation) can promote protein denaturation and aggregation. Effects are difficult to quantify and should be controlled.
  • High Ionic Strength: Use of high ionic strength eluents (e.g., in ion exchange or hydrophobic interaction chromatography) may induce aggregation.
  • pH Sensitivity: Eluents with pH near or far from the protein's isoelectric point can promote aggregation.
  • Filtration Stress: Filtration processes may lead to aggregation due to membrane contact, air bubbles, or shear stress. Ultrafiltration at high protein concentrations adds risk, especially before filling or during buffer mixing.
  • Formulation Handling: Aggregation can be triggered by agitation, freeze-thaw cycles, and prolonged storage of bulk solutions before formulation.
  • Air-Water Interfaces: Exposure to air-water interfaces during formulation can reduce protein stability and initiate aggregation pathways. Visual inspection may be required.

Note: For factors affecting reworking, refer to Section 5.9.

Failure to manage these factors may result in out-of-specification (OOS) drug substance or drug product, visible particulates, reduced storage stability, loss of efficacy, and potentially increased immunogenicity risk (17, 18). Therefore, before performing any reprocessing, evaluating and understanding the potential impact on product quality is essential. If a reactive reprocessing strategy is used, the sampling plan should include specific analytical tests to demonstrate that reprocessing does not adversely affect the quality, safety, or efficacy of the drug substance or drug product.

"Note: For reprocessing, product release may occur in parallel with ongoing stability studies, since the process remains within the validated manufacturing design space. For reworking, stability data must be available prior to release to define retesting intervals, as the introduction of new steps is equivalent to a modified synthesis with potentially higher risk (see Section 5.0)."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

蛋白質聚集（Protein Aggregation）是生物藥品再處理的首要品質風險：治療性蛋白質（mAb、融合蛋白、酵素等）本質上處於亞穩定狀態（Marginally Stable）。每一次額外的製程步驟，都是一次潛在的蛋白質壓力暴露事件。

兩類不穩定機制：

  • 化學不穩定性（Chemical Instability）：去醯胺化（Deamidation）、氧化（Oxidation）、水解（Hydrolysis）——通常在分析方法可偵測到的階段才顯現。
  • 物理不穩定性（Physical Instability）：蛋白質展折（Denaturation）、非共價聚集（Noncovalent Aggregation）——與加工操作直接相關，受剪切力、溫度、界面等影響。

免疫原性（Immunogenicity）的患者安全連結：聚集體是最主要的免疫原性誘因之一。這直接連結到患者安全（Patient Safety）——FDA/EMA 對治療性蛋白質聚集體控制有明確的法規要求。

比喻說明 Analogy

把治療性蛋白質想像成精密的折紙藝術品（Origami）。每次折紙（製程步驟）都有機會讓摺痕更清晰（品質提升），但也有機會讓紙張略微撕裂或變形（品質損傷）。在正常製程中，每一步都精確控制，損傷最小。但再處理意味著「再折一次」——若蛋白質分子在結構上已有輕微疲勞，第二次折疊可能讓損傷更顯著。

重點提示 Key Notes

再處理 vs. 重工的穩定性資料要求差異（關鍵！）：

  • Reprocessing（再處理）：可與進行中的穩定性研究「同步」放行（Product release in parallel with ongoing stability studies）。因為步驟仍在設計空間內，風險相對已知。
  • Reworking（重工）：穩定性資料必須在放行前備妥，不得平行進行。因為引入新步驟等同於修改合成路徑，需要前置數據確認。

這個差異對 CDMO 的批次放行時程有直接影響——Reworking 批次的放行時間必然更長。

Air-Water Interface 的隱性風險：許多操作者低估了氣液界面（如管路充填/排空、泡沫形成）對蛋白質穩定性的影響。再處理時更應避免劇烈攪拌或氣泡的引入。

實務應用 Practical Application

一款高濃度 mAb（150 mg/mL）在 UF 濃縮步驟後進行再處理，在第二次 UF 循環時發現黏度顯著上升，並有輕微的可見微粒出現（Visual Inspection 發現）。

應對步驟：

  1. 立即停止操作並通知 QA
  2. 取樣進行次可見微粒（Subvisible Particles）、聚集體（SE-HPLC/DLS）分析
  3. 評估是否已超出批記錄設定的再處理上限
  4. 若聚集體超標，觸發 OOS 調查並評估批次棄置
  5. 記錄事件，作為小規模再處理研究設計的輸入（需在研究中涵蓋此高黏度情境）

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一款 mAb 在再處理後的 SEC-HPLC 顯示聚集體從 0.8% 上升至 1.5%（規格 ≤ 2%）。雖然仍在規格內，你是否應該放行這批次？需要考慮哪些額外評估？
  2. 免疫原性風險與聚集體的關聯，如何影響臨床試驗用藥品的再處理決策？CDMO 應如何向 Sponsor 溝通此風險？
3.6 General Steps to Consider for a Proactive Design and Validation of a Reprocessing Step 前瞻性再處理設計與驗證的通用步驟

原文內容 Original Text

A general outline for designing, validating, and filing a reprocessing procedure with the regulators using a proactive approach is provided in this section. Figure 3.6-1 provides an example of a generic sequence of activities that could support a proactive reprocessing approach. Steps 1–20 provide an illustration of reprocessing a biopharmaceutical product.

Section 6.0 (Appendix I) provides a comprehensive example of an approach to reprocessing using a hypothetical chromatography step that was created for TR 74 based on the publication A-Mab: A Case Study in Bioprocess Development (19).

In the ideal situation, a manufacturer would plan for reprocessing during development prior to licensure of the product. However, it may also be possible to prospectively define a new step using supplementary submissions created for reprocessing in manufacturing.

The following 20 steps are presented as recommended considerations for evaluating and implementing a reprocessing strategy for a biopharmaceutical product. While the steps are written predicated on a product that has already received marketing approval, many of the key concepts are equally applicable during the development of reprocessing step(s) for a new biopharmaceutical product. Importantly, these steps are not intended to prescribe a fixed sequence but may be adapted to align with the company's quality system, regulatory commitments, and the stage of the product lifecycle.

[Figure 3.6-1 — Sequence of Events: Proactive Reprocessing]

Figure 3.6-1: Sequence of Events – Proactive Reprocessing (Steps 1–20)

Step 1: Identifying and Evaluating Reprocessing Need
The need to reprocess may arise when a biological intermediate/drug substance/product does not conform to specifications or acceptable ranges. Perform an evaluation to narrow the field of feasible and practical reprocessing steps. Consider that any reprocessing step candidate should be based on simplicity of the step to operate in manufacturing, and past failures. Conduct a technical risk assessment (e.g., FMEA) that includes review of related past manufacturing or development issues that may be helpful in identifying a potential need for reprocessing. Also, consider the effect on drug supply and whether reprocessing is needed to prevent a drug shortage, especially if it is a breakthrough product or an orphan drug. Ensure that further development of a prospective reprocessing step would result in an acceptably low regulatory or quality risk.

Step 2: Establish a Project Charter (Optional)
Establish a project team and team charter if the evaluation determines the risks to further develop the prospective reprocessing step(s) are acceptable. Development of a reprocessing step involves many stakeholders and may require numerous, varying resources and skillsets including those from Research and Development, Process/Analytical Development, Manufacturing, MSAT/Process Science, Engineering, Quality Control, Quality Assurance, Regulatory Affairs, and Project Management groups.

Step 3: Review Development History
Review the design basis and development history for the existing unit operation to understand the designed purpose of the proposed reprocessing steps, especially the control of CQAs. Leverage knowledge obtained during the original development of the unit operation to ensure that reprocessing will address the designed purpose of that step to consistently control specific product quality aspects (CQAs) (8, 9). Perform a systematic assessment to identify which development studies (e.g., viral/impurity clearance, process characterization, resin/membrane cleaning) should be repeated or augmented to support reprocessing. Evaluate the assessment for any potential impact of reprocessing to subsequent processing steps.

Step 4: Identify Reprocessing Triggers
Identify the in-process testing or monitoring triggers that would indicate a need for reprocessing at a given unit operation. It may be necessary to introduce a new in-process test(s) to detect failure modes identified in the initial reprocessing risk assessment. The detection method for a failure mode that leads to reprocessing is critical to the reliability of the overall process and to the success of a reprocessing step. The method must be rapid enough to allow for timely decision-making before the opportunity to reprocess has passed. For example, integrity testing of a 0.2 µm filter would be a better detection method to support reprocessing because it can be completed in a few minutes or hours compared to microbial testing, which can take several days to complete.

Step 5: Design Reprocessing Procedure
Begin the initial design with the proposed manufacturing-scale reprocessing procedure including all equipment, process parameters, linkages to adjacent unit operations, pre-use/post-use procedures (e.g., equilibration/cleaning), and process controls. Give special attention to any procedure required to return the process intermediate and equipment to an appropriate starting condition so that the reprocessing step can be performed. This restoration step is designed to replicate the intermediate input feed-stream conditions (e.g., concentration, volume, pH, conductivity) and is not considered to be a rework step.

Step 6: Design Assessment
Consider the broader effect of the reprocessing design outside the scope of its specific unit operation; for example, facility design, pre-/post-viral segregation, linkages to adjacent unit operations, draw on utilities, and scheduling.

Step 7: Perform Quality Attribute Impact Assessment
Perform a risk assessment (6), to identify all quality attributes that may be affected by the proposed reprocessing step. In particular, consider such issues as modes of degradation, CPPs, exposure to product-contact surfaces, leachables from filters/single-use tubing, and adsorptive losses that may be impacted by an additional processing step, intermediate hold point, or freeze-thaw cycle. It is not expected that any new CQAs will be identified, but CQAs may take on a higher or lower overall risk following reprocessing (8, 9, 20, 21). Consider whether the stability of the reprocessed batch should be formally evaluated, especially upon the first instance of reprocessing.

Step 8: Reassess Criticality
Review existing criticality assessments conducted for process parameters, raw materials, equipment, in-process controls, and other factors based on the standard process to determine whether any changes or additions are required. The criticality may be influenced by the addition of a reprocessing step.

Step 9: Identify or Develop Analytical Methods
Identify or develop the analytical methods to monitor all relevant CQAs in intermediate feed-streams and/or drug substance/product. Even though no new CQAs are likely to be identified, additional analytical methods capable of monitoring all potentially impacted CQAs in the relevant matrix may be needed. Additional development and qualification may be needed to adapt methods to the reprocessed product. The risk assessment (Step 7) should determine whether additional characterization methods are required to establish comparability and acceptable product quality.

Step 10: Establish Comparability Criteria
Establish appropriate comparability criteria for all CQAs. Once CQAs and their corresponding analytical methods are identified, it may be possible to develop the key comparability criteria to assess product quality during the development of the reprocessing unit operation. All of the original drug substance or drug product specifications must continue to be met following reprocessing. Verify that product quality at intermediate points in the process meets original expectations or consider new criteria as appropriate.

Step 11: Design Scaled-Down Model
Design and qualify a scaled-down model, if applicable. To generate additional data, a scaled-down unit operation model can be adapted from the previously qualified model of the standard process. The requirements for a laboratory-scale model of a reprocessing step are the same as those used in the development of a standard manufacturing process (i.e., design a scaled-down model that is representative of the manufacturing-scale reprocessing unit operation).

Step 12: Obtain Representative Materials
Obtain representative materials for performing reprocessing at the reduced scale. If representative intermediate feed-streams from manufacturing cannot be obtained, then it may be necessary to generate the materials using a qualified scaled-down model of all, or a section of, the manufacturing-scale process. It should also be possible to recreate the failure mode in the feed-stream in a representative manner (e.g., spiking with impurities). If the failure mode is based on operational checks, such as integrity tests, rather than a product-quality excursion, then use of a standard intermediate feed-stream may be justified.

Step 13: Execute Developmental Studies
Plan and execute reduced-scale development studies, if required once the process design elements described above in Steps 4–12 are completed. The design of reprocessing development studies (e.g., using a design-of-experiments – methodology or worst-case conditions) should result in an understanding of robustness across appropriate critical and noncritical process parameter ranges. Evaluate any effects of the reprocessing step on quality attributes using the selected analytical methods. The design and scope of these studies should take into consideration all elements of the original development studies related to the unit operation, including intermediate and drug substance/product stability. Determine which prior development studies should be repeated and what additional studies (e.g., testing for new degradants) are needed.

Step 14: Assess Development Data
Upon completion of the development studies, review the generated data to evaluate whether the scaled-down process studies indicate the following:

  • All predetermined comparability criteria are met by the reprocessing procedure.
  • Process parameter operating ranges are confirmed.
  • Process intermediate, drug substance, and product stability are potentially impacted.

Step 15: Finalize Designs
Adapt GMP manufacturing-scale reprocessing procedures and equipment based upon the results of scaled-down studies. For example, if the development studies described in Step 13 indicate that process parameter ranges must be narrowed or shifted to ensure that all quality and comparability criteria are met, then it may be necessary to modify the manufacturing-scale process design and control strategy to reflect this new process understanding.

Step 16: Transfer Technology to Manufacturing
Execute technology transfer according to company policies and procedures. Transfer of the reprocessing step to manufacturing should consider scale-up issues and implementation of associated GMP procedures. For more complex reprocessing activities, an intermediate pilot-scale process or non-GMP "engineering run" may also be beneficial to ensure a successful transfer into the GMP manufacturing environment (see Section 3.8 for more information on contamination control strategies).

Step 17: Validation, Comparability and Stability Protocols
Write formal prospective process validation, comparability, and stability protocols for manufacturing-scale reprocessing. Validation considerations include the number of lots of resin or other raw materials required, the number of runs needed, and the additional nonroutine sampling and testing, beginning with consideration of the original validation of the standard process. Additional validation and comparability run(s) at manufacturing scale may be supported by the reduced-scale data generated during development. Real-time stability studies are considered to be a holistic test of recombinant proteins and, therefore, are often used to document that no changes to the protein have occurred. After validation of the reprocessed unit operation and subsequent license approval, it may not be necessary for reprocessed lots to undergo further stability testing.

Step 18: Regulatory Submission
For approval of the reprocessing step, include the supportive scaled-down development data and prospective validation/comparability/stability protocols in the submission (3, 8, 9). If using the proactive approach, file these elements in the initial marketing application:

  • Output of the quality attribute impact assessment (Step 7), criticality reassessment (Step 8), and results of development studies (Step 13) in Module 3.2.S.2.6
  • Manufacturing-scale reprocessing protocols in Module 3.2.S.2.5, including triggers, tests/acceptance criteria, and stability commitments
  • Any other agency commitments or strategies for implementation
  • Description of the validated reprocessing step in Module 3.2.S.2.2

Step 19: Monitor and Investigate Triggering Event
Monitor failure modes for signs that the manufacturing process might need reprocessing or, alternatively, decide to proactively carry out reprocessing to collect data for a submission. Regardless of whether the reprocessing is prospectively designed, it is essential to thoroughly investigate the root cause of any triggering event and its impact on product quality following GMP procedures and the principles of ICH Q9(R1): Quality Risk Management (6). The investigation outcome, along with CAPAs, should be considered in relation to process validation, regulatory commitments (Step 20), and reprocessing design (Steps 5 and 6) (23).

Step 20: Validate and Release
For each reprocessing batch, follow established GMP procedures for executing validation, comparability, and stability protocols, and ensuring all acceptance criteria, including release-testing, are met. Document validation and comparability results in a final report, completing change control and regulatory commitments before releasing the reprocessed batch.

Context

  • No evidence of product damage, contamination, or membrane integrity failure. 無產品損傷、污染或薄膜完整性失敗跡象。
  • Deviation recorded; immediate assessment indicates issue is correctable without impacting CQAs. 偏差記錄，即時評估顯示問題可矯正且不影響 CQAs。

  • Document deviation, batch status, and hold conditions. 記錄偏差、批次狀態及保存條件。
  • Perform risk assessment focusing on CQAs (purity, aggregates, potency, HCP/DNA, residuals). 執行針對 CQAs 的風險評估。
  • Verify equipment status (e.g., UF module integrity test) and confirm reprocessing is covered by a pre-approved procedure or requires QA authorization. 確認設備狀態，確認再處理是否有預核准程序或需 QA 授權。
Reprocessing Plan — Objective
再處理計畫目標		 Complete buffer exchange and/or achieve final concentration while staying within validated ranges and maximum process exposure (e.g., total shear/recirculation time).

再處理行動
  • Resume controlled recirculation to reach target concentration. 恢復受控再循環以達目標濃度。
  • Perform additional DF cycles with qualified buffer to meet conductivity/pH endpoints. 執行額外 DF 循環以達電導率/pH 終點。
  • If flux decline/fouling is suspected, consider membrane change under validated procedure (with integrity test and line clearance). 若懷疑通量下降/阻塞，考慮按已驗證程序更換薄膜。

  • Acceptable ranges for concentration, conductivity, pH. 濃度、電導率、pH 的接受範圍。
  • Stability/exposure limits (e.g., temperature, shear). 穩定性/暴露限制（溫度、剪切力）。

  • Maintain traceability of buffers and materials used during reprocessing. 維持緩衝液及材料的可追溯性。
  • Ensure reprocessing stays within validated process boundaries; if not, escalate to QA/QC and follow rework or rejection pathways. 確保再處理維持在驗證製程邊界內；否則升報 QA/QC 並依重工或棄置程序處置。

再處理後驗證		 Perform required in-process and release testing to confirm CQAs/specifications (e.g., concentration, purity profile, aggregates, bioburden/endotoxin, residuals). Document outcomes, deviations, investigations, and CAPA as applicable.
執行必要的製程中及放行測試，確認 CQAs/規格。記錄結果、偏差、調查及 CAPA。

  • If acceptance criteria are met and reprocessing adhered to approved procedure/validation limits, proceed to next step or release. 若接受標準達標且遵循已核准程序，繼續下一步或放行。
  • If criteria are not met, follow defined pathways for additional investigation, potential rework (if allowed), or batch rejection. 若未達標，依程序進行額外調查、潛在重工（若允許）或批次棄置。
Regulatory and Quality Notes
法規與品質備注
  • Reprocessing in UF/DF is permissible if pre-defined, validated, and does not adversely affect product quality. UF/DF 再處理在預先定義、已驗證且不影響品質的前提下可被允許。
  • Expect QA oversight and documentation of rationale, risk assessment, and verification data. 需有 QA 監督及理由、風險評估、驗證資料的文件化。
  • Establish validation limits (e.g., max additional DF cycles, cumulative processing time, membrane changes). 建立驗證限制（如最大額外 DF 循環次數、累積處理時間、薄膜更換次數）。
3.7 Regulatory Submission for Reactive Approach 被動應對式再處理的法規申報

原文內容 Original Text

Similar to the proactive approach, consider the following elements in the supplemental marketing application for a reactive reprocess:

  1. Output of the quality attribute impact assessment (Step 7), the criticality reassessment (Step 8), and the results of the development studies (Step 13) in Module 3.2.S.2.6
  2. Manufacturing-scale reprocessing execution results, including:
    • The trigger (process failure mode) resulting in reprocessing
    • Results of any additional full-scale testing with comparison to acceptance criteria
    • Stability protocols and any interim stability data
  3. Updated Module 3.2.S.2.2, including a description of the validated reprocessing step

In parallel to filing a supplemental filing for the process change to include an additional reprocessing step, the manufacturer needs to work directly with FDA to obtain approval for batch use.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Reactive 與 Proactive 申報的核心差異：

  • Proactive：在初始上市申請（Initial Marketing Application）中預先納入再處理方案，事前核准。
  • Reactive：事後提交補充申請（Supplemental Application），且必須同步與 FDA/主管機關直接溝通以取得「特定批次使用許可」。

Reactive 申報的雙軌制：補充申請（修改 CMC 資料包）與批次使用核准（Batch Use Approval）是兩個獨立但同步進行的程序，不能只做其中一項。

重點提示 Key Notes

CDMO 實務風險提示：Reactive 再處理批次的放行時程無法預測，因為必須等待主管機關回應。對於供應鏈緊張的產品（Orphan Drug、Breakthrough Therapy），應優先建立 Proactive 方案，避免因法規時程導致供應中斷。

TFDA 框架下的注意事項：TFDA 對補充申請的審查時程可能與 FDA 不同。對在台灣製造、同步供應多個市場的 CDMO，需事先了解各市場的 Supplemental Application 類型（如 CBE-30、PAS 等）及審查時程，進行供應計畫整體協調。

Interim Stability Data 的角色：Reactive 申報中，若正式穩定性資料尚未完整，可先提交中期穩定性資料（Interim Stability Data），但必須承諾提交長期穩定性結果。

3.8 Impact of Contamination Control Strategy during Reprocessing 再處理過程中的污染控制策略影響

原文內容 Original Text

As discussed above, it is critical to ensure microbial controls are considered and protected during reprocessing events. The reprocessed biological drug substances and sterile drug products should adhere to all CQAs associated with bioburden and endotoxins.

During the reprocessing of a sterile biological drug substance and drug product, European Union (EU) Annex 1 (20), provides general guidance that should be used in the design and control of facilities, equipment, systems, and procedures used for the manufacture of all sterile products. The principles of quality risk management (QRM) should be applied to ensure that microbial, particulate, and endotoxin/pyrogen contamination is prevented in the final drug substance and drug products. QRM is also applicable to low-bioburden biological intermediates and drug substances (20).

Critical aspects considered in QRM include, but are not limited to:

  • Process step where reprocessing occurs
  • Time of product exposure (open operations)
  • Air classification of the reprocessing work zone (where the product is exposed in absence of closed system)
  • Particulate counts and bioburden trends
  • Training records of personnel carrying out reprocessing
  • Sanitization and disinfection procedures
  • Material movement and area segregation controls (e.g. CCS impact, pre/post viral areas)
  • Hold times during reprocessing
  • Line clearance before and after reprocessing
  • Cleaning and/or change over limits, in cases of multiproduct facilities
  • Filter validation studies (critical on operating ranges while handling concentrated protein solutions)
  • Sampling techniques and number of samples taken for studies

The Quality Control Unit should establish a more comprehensive monitoring program for operators involved in reprocessing operations, which are especially labor-intensive and require complex manipulations and time criticality.

Processes, equipment, facilities, and manufacturing activities should be managed in accordance with QRM principles to provide a proactive means of identifying, scientifically evaluating, and controlling potential risks to quality. Where alternative approaches are used, these should be supported by appropriate rationale, risk assessment, and mitigation and should adhere to principles of CCS. A combination of QRM and a contamination control strategy (CCS) should be established for a robust assurance of contamination prevention during reprocessing. The CCS should be actively reviewed and, where appropriate, updated, and it should drive continual improvement of the manufacturing and control methods. (See PDA Technical Report No. 90: Contamination Control Strategy Development in Pharmaceutical Manufacturing for further details (21, 24), Contamination Control Strategies (CCS) in Low Bioburden Biologic Drug Substance Manufacturing (25), and Contamination Control Strategy Guidance (26).)

Common microbiological attributes during reprocessing for biological drug substances and drug products are given in Table 3.8-1. See full-width table below.

With respect to bioburden, endotoxin, sterility, particulates, and container closure integrity, all the reprocessing procedures should be evaluated, reviewed, and approved by the Quality Control Unit, and the impact on validation and stability should be thoroughly evaluated.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

CCS（Contamination Control Strategy，污染控制策略）是一個主動管理框架，而非被動清單。EU Annex 1 2022 版本正式要求所有無菌藥品製造商建立書面 CCS，且必須定期審查更新。

再處理使 CCS 面臨額外挑戰：相比常規批次，再處理通常涉及：

  • 更長的產品暴露時間（Extended Exposure Time）
  • 更多次設備開啟/拆裝操作
  • 非計畫性的操作人員干預
  • 時間壓力下的複雜手動操作

每個因素都增加了微生物、內毒素、微粒污染的風險。

Pre/Post Viral 區域隔離的重要性：對生物藥品尤其關鍵。病毒去除/滅活步驟前後的區域隔離（Area Segregation）是 GMP 強制要求，再處理設計時必須確保不違反此隔離原則。

比喻說明 Analogy

再處理中的 CCS 就像醫院緊急手術時的感染控制（Hospital Infection Control for Emergency Surgery）。常規手術有標準的術前準備流程；但緊急手術（類比 Reactive 再處理）在時間壓力下更容易出現感染控制疏失——因此反而需要更嚴格的監控方案，確保基本防護不因緊急性而被犧牲。

重點提示 Key Notes

EU Annex 1 2022 的直接要求：雖然 TR74 引用的是 EU Annex 1，對於 TFDA/PIC/S 管轄的 CDMO 而言，PIC/S Annex 1 (2022) 內容幾乎相同。PIC/S Annex 1 (2022) Section 4 要求所有無菌製造商建立 CCS，並明確涵蓋再處理相關的污染風險。（PIC/S Annex 1 2022, Section 4）

QC 監控方案的強化要求：TR74 明確指出，執行再處理的操作人員需要「更全面的監控方案（more comprehensive monitoring program）」。這包括：加強更衣驗證頻率、手套完整性測試、環境監控取樣增加、人員資格再確認等。

多產品設施的額外考量：CDMO 通常是多產品設施（Multiproduct Facility），再處理結束後的清潔驗證和線路清場（Line Clearance）標準必須更嚴格，防止交叉污染。

決策層級提示：在 CCS 設計中，無菌保證（Sterility Assurance）永遠優先於時程和成本考量——若再處理無法在維護無菌保證的前提下執行，批次應予以棄置。

實務應用 Practical Application — CDMO CCS 設計

一個 Fill-Finish CDMO 在最終除菌過濾步驟後，發現過濾器完整性測試失敗（Post-Use Integrity Failure）。需要在 Grade B 環境下更換過濾器並重新過濾。

CCS 強化措施（再處理情境）：

  • 確認過濾器更換在 Grade A 局部保護下進行
  • 額外的環境監控：更換前後各進行一次主動空氣採樣
  • 操作人員資格：確認已完成最近一次更衣驗證（Gowning Qualification）
  • 更換後新過濾器的前完整性測試（Pre-Use Integrity Test, PUPSIT）
  • 記錄所有時間點、操作人員、設備批號
  • 最終批次的無菌測試樣本增加（Enhanced Sterility Sampling）

本節重點回顧 Section Summary — Section 3

  • Proactive vs. Reactive 的根本差異：Proactive 在上市申請時即預先核准再處理方案，觸發時直接執行；Reactive 是事後因應，需要補充申請及額外的主管機關溝通，複雜步驟風險高，應盡量以 Proactive 設計取代。
  • Continuation ≠ Reprocessing：在批記錄預設範圍內的製程調整屬於「延續」，不需偏差申報；超出設計範圍才是「再處理」。正確分類是 GMP 合規的基礎，文件設計應消除灰色地帶。
  • 頻率管控雙維度：需同時監控「單批次再處理次數上限」（由驗證研究定義，例如 Dead-End Filtration 通常 max 3 次）及「批次間頻率」（APR/PQR 趨勢分析，示例性門檻 10%），兩者均超標代表製程失控。
  • 蛋白質聚集是首要品質風險：每次再處理都是額外的蛋白質壓力暴露，需評估黏度增加、熱應力、剪切力、氣液界面等因素。聚集體增加直接關聯免疫原性風險，危害患者安全。
  • 20 步驟框架是實作路線圖：從 FMEA 評估到法規申報，框架提供完整的前瞻性設計路徑，適用於商業化後及開發階段。關鍵節點：Step 4 觸發偵測時效性、Step 10 可比性標準建立、Step 18 CTD Module 對應（3.2.S.2.2/2.5/2.6）。
  • CCS 在再處理中的強化要求：再處理增加了微生物、內毒素、微粒污染的風險，需結合 QRM 和 CCS（PIC/S Annex 1 2022 要求），並對執行再處理的操作人員建立更嚴格的監控方案。無菌保證永遠是最高優先級（決策層級 #1）。
  • CDMO 核心提醒：在承接新產品時，應主動與 Sponsor 討論哪些步驟具有再處理需求，並在 IND/CTA 階段就規劃 Proactive 方案，避免商業化後的 Reactive 困境。TFDA 要求再處理批次放行前需取得主管機關核准，與 FDA 框架一致。
