5.0 Introduction to Reworking 返工概論導言

原文內容 Original Text

It is the primary responsibility of the Manufacturing Authorization Holder (MAH) of a biological drug substance and/or drug product to consistently meet all quality, safety, purity, and identity requirements. This authoring team has strict recommendations on how and when such a reworking action should be considered by the MAH. The ultimate approval of a reworked drug substance and/or drug product for human use is solely at the discretion of the approving regulatory agency.

ICH and EU define reworking as:

"Subjecting an intermediate or API that does not conform to standards or specifications to one or more processing steps that are different from the established manufacturing process to obtain acceptable quality intermediate or API." (ICH Q7; EU GMP)

During reworking, the nonconforming material is subjected to a different manufacturing process to get a product that meets all the predefined acceptance criteria and specifications for quality, safety, purity, and identity (QSIP).

The scope of reworking for biologicals includes a diverse array of therapeutic proteins, each with unique challenges and considerations for manufacturing and quality control including, among others:

  • Interferons
  • Cytokines
  • Growth factors
  • Interleukins
  • Coagulation factors
  • Blood factors
  • Monoclonal antibodies (IgM, IgD, IgG, IgE, IgA)
  • Bispecific antibodies (IgG-based bsAbs, fragment-based bsAbs)
  • Antibody drug conjugates targeting cancer therapy
  • Fusion proteins
  • Glycoprotein hormones (e.g., rhCG, rFSH)
  • Insulins
  • Vaccines

Each of these therapeutic categories requires specific attention to factors like protein stability, aggregation prevention, and purification processes to ensure product purity, quality, efficacy, and safety.

Due to the intricate sourcing, including the nature of their manufacturing processes and stability concerns, cell and gene therapies are excluded from the scope for reworking. The complexity of these treatments often involves different considerations that require specialized processes and controls distinct from those applicable to the protein-based biologicals listed above.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

MAH（Marketing Authorization Holder，上市許可持有人）: 對藥品在整個生命週期中的品質、安全性及有效性負有最終法規責任的組織。在 CDMO 模型下，MAH 通常是委託方（Sponsor），而非 CMO 本身。

QSIP: Quality（品質）、Safety（安全性）、Purity（純度）、Identity（鑑別）—— 四位一體的藥品品質核心判斷軸。

Reworking（返工）核心定義: 將不符規格的中間體或 API，送入「與既定製程不同的」一個或多個步驟。這個「不同」是返工與再加工的分水嶺，詳見 5.2 節。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像一批精品威士忌桶在陳年過程中因溫控失誤，導致酒液品質不符標準。

  • 再加工（Reprocessing）= 重新裝回原型桶，按原始陳年程序重來一次 → 同樣的步驟、已知的結果。
  • 返工（Reworking）= 決定改用過桶技術（wine cask finish），用一個原始工藝計畫以外的新步驟來修正 → 不同的步驟、未知的風險 → 必須預先向監管機關申報核准。

對生物製劑而言，這瓶「威士忌」是活蛋白分子，容錯率遠低於烈酒，每一個額外步驟都可能影響免疫原性（immunogenicity）。

重點提示 Key Notes

細胞與基因治療（CGT）不在返工範圍內。TR74 明確將 CGT 排除，原因是其製程複雜性、活細胞/病毒載體的特殊性，以及返工操作本身可能對產品造成無法回復的改變。在 CDMO 如遇 CGT 批次 OOS，應直接棄批，不應嘗試返工。

決策框架: 無菌保證（Sterility）> 產品 CQA > 商業考量。返工永遠不能只因為批次成本而觸發——必須有明確的病患供應需求作為驅動力。

5.1 Prioritization Tool for Reworking 返工優先順序工具

原文內容 Original Text

Using the MoSCoW (Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, and Won't-have, or will not have right now) Prioritization Tool to evaluate reworking of a biological drug substance involves systematically categorizing tasks or issues based on their criticality and impact.

This step-by-step guide outlines how to apply the MoSCoW method in this context, and Figure 5.1-1 illustrates the Patient-Centric Reworking Prioritization tool.

1. Gather Relevant Data and Stakeholders

  • Collect all information related to the reworking process, including technical assessments, quality data, regulatory considerations, and risk analyses.
  • Assemble a cross-functional team comprising manufacturing, quality assurance, regulatory affairs, and process development experts.

2. Define the Scope of Reworking

  • Clearly specify which aspects of the biological drug substance are being considered for rework (e.g., purification, formulation, in-process controls, final product adjustments).

3. Identify Rework Tasks or Issues

  • List all potential rework activities, corrections, or modifications needed to meet quality and regulatory standards.

4. Apply the MoSCoW Prioritization Criteria
Categorize each task or issue into one of the following groups:

  • Must have (M): Critical for human survival, compliance, safety, efficacy, or regulatory approval. These are nonnegotiable and should be completed before proceeding.
  • Should have (S): Immediate medical supply need; can be scheduled after Must-Have items.
  • Could have (C): Desirable to support patient needs, especially those suffering with genetic disorders, cancer, glaucoma, blood issues, and wherever critical to support health condition of work force.
  • Won't have (W): Tasks that are not necessary in the current scope or can be deferred.

5. Prioritize Reworking Activities

  • Focus first on Must-Have tasks to ensure the reworked product meets all quality and regulatory standards.
  • Schedule Should-Have activities shortly afterwards, resources permitting.
  • Consider Could-Have tasks, if time and resources allow, to enhance overall process robustness.
  • Leave Won't-Have tasks for future considerations or continuous improvement.

6. Document and Communicate

  • Record the prioritization outcomes clearly, including rationales for each category.
  • Communicate the priorities to all relevant teams and stakeholders to ensure aligned execution.

7. Implement and Monitor

  • Proceed with rework activities according to the established priorities.
  • Monitor progress and adjust priorities if new information or issues arise.

8. Additional Measures

  • Ensure compliance with GMP and regulatory requirements throughout the rework process.
  • Use risk assessments to support the categorization, especially for CQAs.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

MoSCoW 法最初源自敏捷軟體開發，TR74 將其引入 GMP 返工決策框架，強調以病患為核心的優先排序，而非以商業壓力或成本回收為導向。

四個等級的 GMP 含義：

  • Must-have: 攸關病患存活、法規合規、產品安全有效性 → 不做等同無法放行
  • Should-have: 關鍵供應需求，但稍有時間餘裕 → 必須做，但可在 M 之後安排
  • Could-have: 強化製程韌性，支援特定病患群（罕見病、癌症）
  • Won't-have: 本次範圍外 → 納入 CAPA 或 CI（持續改善）計畫

比喻說明 Analogy

想像一座城市發生地震後的緊急應變指揮中心，如何分派搶救隊伍：

  • M：醫院、托嬰中心（有人命 → 立即動員所有資源）
  • S：幹道橋樑（影響整體救援，盡快修復）
  • C：公園圍牆（改善生活品質，資源允許時處理）
  • W：商業廣告招牌（本次不列優先）

返工亦然 — 在資源有限的 CDMO 環境，MoSCoW 幫助 QA 與管理層對齊同一語言，避免「商業壓力」偷偷把 W 級別的項目晉升為 M。

重點提示 Key Notes

TR74 強調：MoSCoW 只是示例工具，組織必須使用自身 QMS 中已核准的優先順序工具。這意味著若工廠 SOP 已有類似的批次棄留決策矩陣（例如基於 FMEA 或 RPN 的優先框架），可以整合取代，但核心病患中心原則不變。

CDMO 關鍵點：在臨床期 CDMO，每一批臨床試驗用藥（IMP）背後都有等待用藥的病患。MoSCoW 的 M 等級不是「我們想不想救這批藥」，而是「病患在等待這批藥嗎、而且替代品目前不存在嗎？」

圖表解讀 Figure Analysis — Figure 5.1-1

MoSCoW 四象限優先矩陣視覺化應包含以下要素：

  • 以同心圓或四象限呈現四個 MoSCoW 等級，由內（M）到外（W）代表緊迫度遞減
  • 病患圖示置於中心，強化「以病患為核心」的評估起點
  • 各象限列出對應的生物製劑特定考量點（如 M 區：CQA 衝擊、法規合規；S 區：供應鏈缺口；C 區：製程優化）

閱讀此圖時，應先問：「這個返工決策是從內圈（病患安全）向外擴展，還是被外圈（商業考量）向內擠壓？」任何由商業壓力驅動的返工評估，都是 GMP 合規的危險訊號。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：某臨床 II 期 mAb 批次純化後，蛋白質聚合體（aggregates）超標（CQA 不符）。Sponsor 要求緊急評估是否可以返工以維持臨床試驗時程。

請用 MoSCoW 框架評估以下項目：

  • M: 確認聚合體的免疫原性風險評估、確定病毒安全性不受返工影響
  • S: 評估額外 SEC-HPLC 監測計畫、起草返工協議草案
  • C: 小規模（bench scale）確認研究設計
  • W: 本次不考慮更換純化樹脂（屬於製程改善，非緊急返工範疇）

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若同一批藥同時出現兩個問題：①聚合體超標（影響 CQA）、②標籤印刷錯誤（GDocP 問題），用 MoSCoW 如何排序？理由為何？
  2. TR74 說 MoSCoW 只是「示例工具」。若貴公司 QMS 中沒有類似工具，這句話對你意味著什麼行動？

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

這是 TR74 最核心的一組定義對比。掌握這兩個表格，等於掌握了 90% 的 GMP 返工法規邏輯。

最簡記憶法：

  • Reprocessing（再加工）= 原有製程中的重複步驟 → 法規提前授權可能已涵蓋
  • Reworking（返工）= 原有製程以外的新步驟 → 必須先提交法規申報、取得核准，才能執行

比喻說明 Analogy

把製造流程比喻成廚師的標準食譜（SOP）：

  • Reprocessing：湯太淡，把整鍋湯再熬一次（食譜裡本來就有這步驟，只是重做）
  • Reworking：湯太濃，加入食譜裡沒有的食材去中和（新步驟 → 食譜已變更 → 需要主廚（監管機關）簽字確認）

重點提示 Key Notes — Drug Product 的特殊性

Table 5.2-2 點出一個常被忽略的重要規則：

DP（藥品製劑）通常無法被「返工」，只能被「再加工」。

例如無菌充填過程中因機械故障而中斷，允許重新滅菌過濾後再充填（reprocessing），但無法在已充填的藥品上疊加一個「原始製程外的新步驟」——那等同於改變藥品本身。

對於無菌充填 CDMO 而言，DP 層級的 OOS 絕大多數情況下的結論是棄批，而非返工。

關鍵對比速記

Reprocessing → 重複既有步驟 → 可預先授權於 License
Reworking    → 引入新步驟   → 必須事先送審取得 PAS / Type II

Reprocessing → 包含機械故障導致的不合格
Reworking    → 僅限非機械故障的品質不合格批次

Reprocessing → 若大多數批次都用到，應納入標準製程
Reworking    → 每次事件唯一，不應常態化

5.3 Background to Manufacturing Changes 製造變更背景說明

原文內容 Original Text

Reworking is an unexpected occurrence and is not preapproved as part of the marketing authorization. The MAH's Quality Unit should oversee investigations into a nonconforming (OOS) in-process drug, a bulk process intermediate (final biological bulk intermediate), a drug substance of a single batch or lot to an alternate manufacturing process during reworking decisions, prior to release to ensure that the details of rework in question has been submitted to the regulatory agency and formal approval has been obtained.

Changes that have a substantial potential to have an adverse effect on product quality (i.e., major variations) require the manufacturer (applicant) to report the change to the FDA in a supplement to the approved BLA. A PAS (Prior Approval Supplement) must be approved by the FDA prior to distribution of the product manufactured using the change to protect the public from products whose identity, strength, quality, purity, potency, safety, or effectiveness may be compromised (10, 33, 46).

If biological drug products are reworked, they must be reported in a supplement to FDA prior to distribution, unless the reworking was done according to a procedure previously approved by the Agency. The type of notification is based on the potential risk of the change having an adverse effect on the identity, strength, quality, purity, or potency of the product as it may relate to the safety or effectiveness of the product (24).

The Variation Regulation is supplemented by the European Commission's Guidelines on the Details of the Various Categories of Variations, on the Operation of the Procedures Laid Down in Chapters II, IIa, III and IV of Commission Regulation No 1234/2008 of 24 November 2008. The EU guidelines define a major variation of Type II as one that is not an extension of the Marketing Authorization and that may have a significant impact on the quality, safety, or efficacy of a medicinal product (37).

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

PAS（Prior Approval Supplement，事前核准補件）: FDA BLA 框架下，對已核准生物製劑的重大製程變更，必須提交 PAS 並取得 FDA 核准後，方可使用變更後製程生產並銷售產品。這是美國法規的「事前核准」機制。

Type II Variation（EMA）: 歐盟對上市許可的重大變更類別，定義為「對品質、安全性或有效性可能有顯著影響」的變更。等同於 FDA PAS 的歐盟版本。

BLA（Biologics License Application）: 美國生物製劑的上市許可申請書，相當於 NDA 之於化學藥品。

    • 高風險 → PAS（FDA）/ Type II（EMA）→ 事前核准
    • 中風險 → CBE-30（FDA）/ Type IB（EMA）→ 事前通知
    • 低風險 → Annual Report（FDA）/ Type IA（EMA）→ 年度報告

返工（Reworking）因其引入「製程外新步驟」，原則上屬於高風險類別，幾乎必然落入 PAS / Type II 的管轄。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像製造授權（Marketing Authorization）是一份「政府核准的食品加工許可證」，上面列明允許使用的原料、加工步驟、設備規格。

  • 若你臨時換了一個「許可證上沒有列」的新加工步驟（返工）→ 等同於在食品工廠用了政府未審核的新加工程序 → 必須先重新申請許可（PAS / Type II），取得核准後才能出貨。
  • 出貨後才申報 = 違規 = 在台灣 TFDA 管轄下可能面臨批次召回。

重點提示 Key Notes — CDMO 責任邊界

誰提交 PAS / Type II Variation？

是 MAH（通常是 Sponsor），不是 CDMO。CDMO 的角色是：

  1. 發現不合格 → 立即通知 Sponsor QU
  2. 提供技術數據與評估支援
  3. 起草 reworking protocol（由 Sponsor QU 審核）
  4. 在取得監管核准後執行返工
  5. 不能在 Sponsor 未授權的情況下自行啟動或執行返工

若 CDMO 自行啟動返工而未取得 Sponsor 或監管機關核准，法規後果由 MAH（Sponsor）承擔，但商業合約的違約責任則指向 CDMO。

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Proactive Reworking Agreement（PRA，主動返工協議）: 在啟動返工操作之前，MAH 主動與監管機關協商、提交詳細的返工協議（PACMP），取得預先核准，再執行返工的整體流程。

圖表解讀 Figure Analysis — Figure 5.4-1

主動返工協議（PRA）流程圖呈現九個步驟的線性決策路徑，關鍵閘口（Gate）包含：

  • Step 1-2 閘口：QU 調查根因 → 決定棄批或啟動返工評估
  • Step 3-4 閘口：QU 詳細評估 → 起草 reworking protocol（PACMP 格式）
  • Step 5 閘口：正式送審（PAS/Type II）→ 等待監管核准（此步驟前不得執行任何返工操作）
  • Step 6 閘口：監管機關審查問答 → MAH 回覆 → 取得預先核准
  • Step 7-9 閘口：執行 → 評估 → 最終送審放行

整個 PRA 流程的核心精神：先問，後做。在 GMP 世界中，「先做後報」對返工而言是不被接受的。

比喻說明 Analogy

PRA 就像外科手術前的術前討論與家屬同意書流程：

  • 醫師（MAH/CMO）不能因為病患情況緊急，就跳過術前評估直接開刀
  • 必須先向病患家屬（監管機關）說明為什麼要手術、如何操作、預期結果是什麼
  • 取得知情同意（核准）後，才能進行手術（執行返工）
  • 手術後仍需完整記錄手術報告（最終送審）

重點提示 Key Notes — CDMO 的執行痛點

PRA 流程中最容易出問題的環節在 Step 1-3 之間：

生物分子在 OOS 發現後的「調查等待期」，若保存條件未達最優（非驗證保存時間、溫度偏差），蛋白質結構可能已發生不可逆變化。TR74 明確指出：若 QU 判斷「在調查等待期間蛋白質完整性已受損」，整個返工申請可能直接被 QU 否決（步驟 3 的 Note）。

實務結論：CDMO 的 GMP 日常管理重點之一，是確保 OOS 調查期間的保存條件記錄完整、可追溯，這是返工可行性的前提條件。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 場景：週五下午 4 點，一批臨床 III 期 mAb 純化後發現宿主細胞蛋白（HCP）超標。QC 確認測試無誤，QU 啟動調查。此時應按 PRA 流程做什麼？

  1. 立即隔離批次，確認保存條件符合 DS 驗證儲存規範（不可超出 hold time）
  2. 通知 Sponsor QU（MAH），同步啟動聯合調查
  3. 確認根因：是層析樹脂性能下降？緩衝液配制錯誤？還是分析方法問題？
  4. 若根因確認後評估可能通過額外純化步驟降低 HCP → 啟動 PRA Step 3（詳細評估）
  5. 在獲得 Sponsor 授權且完成 PAS 提交並取得 FDA 核准前，禁止執行任何返工操作

5.5 Application of Knowledge and Risk Assessments 知識與風險評估的應用

原文內容 Original Text

ICH Quality Guideline Q10: Pharmaceutical Quality System was the first official global guideline known to firmly position QRM (Quality Risk Management) and knowledge management (KM) as important enablers of an effective pharmaceutical quality system (PQS). Specifically, QRM and KM jointly enable (i) achieving product realization, (ii) establishing and maintaining a state of control, (iii) facilitating continual improvement, and (iv) critical decision-making during the reworking strategy (57).

ICH Q9(R1) emphasizes the relationship between risk and knowledge, and it highlights the importance of KM in managing risks. It places knowledge and KM repeatedly and explicitly at the core of all QRM activities, further reinforcing the connection of scientific knowledge and risk in several other regulatory guidance documents. Specifically, the definition of risk-based decision-making reads:

"An approach to, or a process of, making decisions that considers knowledge about risks relevant to the decision and whether risks are at an acceptable level." (ICH Q9(R1))

The Risk Knowledge Infinity Cycle (RKI Cycle) establishes and promotes the following concepts:

  • Knowledge is both an input to and an output from QRM
  • Knowledge has an inverse relationship with risk
  • Knowledge should flow effortlessly and on demand to inform risk and risk-based decisions
  • The Cycle is continuous and perpetual

The RKI Cycle can be applied to support the goals of the PQS as defined in ICH Q10. A set of six principles corresponding with six "nodes" along the Cycle has been developed to better guide the tangible connection between QRM and KM practices as illustrated in Figure 5.5-1 (58).

During the reworking of biologicals, rigorous risk management ensures that any changes or corrective actions do not compromise critical quality attributes, safety, or regulatory compliance. Structured assessments (e.g., FMEA within a quality risk management framework) prioritize controls and justify decisions, while robust change control, deviation tracking, and CAPA link risks to measurable outcomes. Effective knowledge management captures batch histories, tacit operator knowhow, prior rework learnings, and real-time analytics in a shared repository. This enables cross-functional review and consistent, evidence-based decisions that reduce variability and prevent repeat issues.

Knowledge
Prior knowledge is a critical tool to identify and justify quality during the critical planning, decision-making, and execution of the OOS of the biological drug substance or a drug product to be reworked. Three forms of knowledge are critical:

  1. Internal knowledge of the manufacturer gained throughout the product lifecycle (including development and characterization of biological drug substance and drug product) is the basis for identifying the elements of CMC that are established conditions (EC) (52), and those elements which are supportive information, historical development of products and/or similar processes, proprietary technologies, modelling data, previous regulatory filings, regulatory inspections, pre-approval manufacturing changes, compatibility assessments, CQAs and any signals associated with QSIP (4).
  2. External knowledge comes from public databases, literature surveys, journals, conferences, peer-reviewed publications and monographs.
  3. Knowledge application of scientific principles from biophysical sciences, pharmaceutical sciences, biochemical engineering, material science, and application of principles of engineering for evaluation of the structure-activity relationship of the molecule under investigation.

ICH Q9(R1) defines two primary principles of QRM:

  1. The evaluation of the risk to quality should be based on scientific knowledge and ultimately linked to the protection of the patient.
  2. The level of effort, formality, and documentation of the QRM process should be commensurate with the level of risk.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

RKI Cycle（Risk Knowledge Infinity Cycle，風險知識無限循環）: ICH Q9(R1) 所呼應的核心框架。以「無窮符號（∞）」形象呈現知識與風險的持續互動：知識越多 → 風險評估越精確 → 決策品質越高 → 產生更多知識，形成正向循環。

知識的三種形式（TR74 §5.5）：

  • 內部知識（Internal）: 製造商整個產品生命週期中積累的 CMC 數據、歷史批次資料、法規文件
  • 外部知識（External）: 文獻、期刊、研討會、公共數據庫
  • 知識應用（Application）: 將生物物理、藥學、生化工程等科學原理整合應用於特定分子的結構-活性關係分析

圖表解讀 Figure Analysis — Figure 5.5-1 (RKI Cycle)

RKI Cycle 六節點框架圖的核心結構：

  1. Knowledge Acquisition（知識獲取）: 彙整批次歷史、開發數據、外部文獻
  2. Knowledge Management（知識管理）: 結構化儲存、跨功能共享
  3. Risk Identification（風險識別）: 基於現有知識辨識潛在失效模式
  4. Risk Assessment（風險評估）: FMEA、FMECA、HACCP 等工具量化風險
  5. Risk Control（風險管控）: 設計管控措施並實施
  6. Risk Review（風險回顧）: 執行後反饋 → 更新知識庫 → 進入下一輪循環

在返工情境中，此循環的速度直接決定返工協議的科學說服力：知識越充足，QU 和監管機關越容易批准返工申請。

比喻說明 Analogy

RKI Cycle 就像一個有機農場的永續耕作循環：

  • 土壤分析（知識獲取）→ 選種施肥（知識應用）→ 病蟲害風險識別 → 有機防治策略（風險管控）→ 收成後土壤回復評估（風險回顧）→ 下一季種植（知識更新）
  • 「知識」就是土壤肥力——越豐富，下一季的「風險」越低，「決策品質」越高。

若一個 CDMO 對某 mAb 已積累了 20 批的 CMC 知識庫，其返工協議的科學說服力將遠大於只有 3 批歷史的廠商。

重點提示 Key Notes — ICH Q9(R1) 兩大 QRM 原則在返工中的應用

原則一：以科學知識為基礎，最終保護病患。
在返工中，這意味著每一個「允許返工」的決定，都必須有科學數據支撐（ICH Q9(R1) §3.1）：分析數據、穩定性數據、免疫原性評估，而不能只是「我們覺得應該沒問題」。

原則二：QRM 的投入程度與風險等級相稱。
這給了 CDMO 一個靈活空間：若根因很清楚、風險評估顯示影響極低，可以用較簡化的 QRM 工具（如 What-If）；若不確定性高，則必須用 FMEA 全套方法論（ICH Q9(R1) §3.2）。

CDMO 場景：一個新承接的客戶 mAb 批次出現 OOS，但該產品在貴公司只有 2 批歷史。在 Step 2（詳細評估）中，應如何彌補「內部知識不足」的缺口？

  • 客戶知識轉移：要求 Sponsor 提供其自身的 CMC 研究數據、分子穩定性檔案、之前 FDA 審查意見
  • 知識應用：委託外部專家（蛋白質生物物理學家）進行結構-活性分析，確認 OOS 對免疫原性的潛在影響
  • 結論文件化：所有知識蒐集過程與結論，納入 change control 作為返工協議草案的科學依據

  3. FMEA（失效模式與影響分析）在返工 Step 2 評估中，應針對哪些製程節點進行 RPN（風險優先數）排序？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary (5.0–5.5)

  • 5.0 定義基礎：Reworking（返工）= 將不符規格物料送入「製程外新步驟」。MAH 主責，監管機關最終核准。細胞與基因治療排除在外。(TR74 §5.0)
  • 5.1 優先工具：MoSCoW（M/S/C/W）以病患為核心評估返工優先順序，但必須使用組織 QMS 已核准的工具。(TR74 §5.1)
  • 5.2 核心區別：Reprocessing = 重複既有步驟（可預先授權）；Reworking = 新步驟（必須事先法規申報核准）。藥品製劑（DP）基本上只能再加工，不能返工。(TR74 §5.2)
  • 5.3 法規框架：返工必須提交 FDA PAS 或 EMA Type II Variation，在核准前不得出貨；Table 5.3-1 列出下游製程及 DP 製造中可能觸發返工的重大變更清單。(TR74 §5.3)
  • 5.4 PRA 九步驟：從 OOS 發現 → 複驗確認 → 根因調查 → PACMP 協議起草 → 送審核准 → 執行 → 評估 → 最終送審，每個閘口均需 QU 把關。(TR74 §5.4)
  • 5.5 知識與風險：RKI Cycle 建立知識↑→ 風險↓的正向循環。三種知識形式（內部、外部、應用）是 Step 2 評估的科學基礎；ICH Q9(R1) 兩大 QRM 原則要求科學依據與比例原則。(TR74 §5.5; ICH Q9(R1) §3.1–3.2)
  • CDMO 核心原則：在 CDMO 模型下，MAH（Sponsor）提交法規申報；CDMO 提供技術數據、執行製程。「先問後做」是 PRA 的核心精神，商業壓力不得凌駕無菌保證與 CQA 保護。
