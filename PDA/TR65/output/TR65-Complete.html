1.0

Introduction

Why technology transfer matters in the modern pharma landscape

1.0 Introduction

Technology transfers play an increasingly important role in the regulated pharmaceutical and biopharmaceutical industries. The expanded use of contract development and manufacturing organizations, advances in manufacturing flexibility, increase in corporate mergers and acquisitions, and enhanced regulatory complexity all contribute to the need for technology transfers to maintain the continuity of drug supply. In order to replicate systems safely, maintain high-quality standards, and meet regulatory requirements, manufacturers have established approaches to successfully transfer manufacturing and analytical technology and knowledge from one site to another.

Pharmaceutical technology transfer (TT) uses a systematic approach to ensure the knowledge required to execute the manufacturing process and product testing performed at one site can be incorporated at a new site, while maintaining understanding and product characteristics. This results in the same safe, effective, and consistent pharmaceutical product being manufactured at both locations. TTs can occur at all stages of the product lifecycle, from drug development through clinical trials to commercial release.

The information in this technical report can be used to guide TT of both small- and large-molecule drug substances (biologics) as well as sterile and nonsterile drug and biological products. This document describes the steps for a single TT event. For large, complex TTs, these tools can be implemented in separate parallel processes, with a TT team established and a procedure executed for each discrete aspect being transferred. For instance, separate TT teams could be established for the transfer of drug substance manufacturing, drug product manufacturing, and packaging, with a management oversight committee ensuring the interconnectivity of the overall TT program across the supply chain.

The product realization requirements outlined in this technical report follow the guidelines of the International Council for Harmonization of Technical Requirements for Pharmaceuticals for Human Use (ICH) and should be documented in the chemistry, manufacturing, and controls (CMC) section of the regulatory filing.

核心概念：為什麼技術移轉越來越重要？

TR65 開篇點明四大驅動力：

  • CDMO 使用擴展：藥廠越來越多外包製造，產品必須跨廠移轉

CDMO 實務場景

1.1

Purpose

What this technical report aims to provide

1.1 Purpose

This technical report aims to provide a standardized approach to the TT process by supplying a matrixed "Reference Guide to Technology Transfer Activities and Deliverables," which can be used to coordinate cross-functional TT activities that help achieve operational readiness and culminate in regulatory approval. As specific details around each TT differ greatly, based on the type and complexity of the manufacturing process or analytical method being transferred, only a certain level of detail could be covered in a single technical report. This technical report is intended to provide a level of detail and approaches that can be applied across TT types.

1.2

Scope

What is and is not covered in TR65

1.2 Scope

This technical report covers the full range of requirements needed for TTs, such as new product introductions and the transfer of products that are being manufactured for phase III clinical trials or commercial release, which are the most comprehensive in nature. In transfers of products at an earlier stage of development, when process and product knowledge is less understood and therefore CMC requirements are less detailed, certain steps described in this technical report will not be necessary. This report does not detail those differences.

While this information was not developed specifically for medical devices, combination products, and advanced therapy medicinal products due to their unique requirements, many of the fundamentals expressed here can be applied to those products.

For this technical report, it is assumed that all involved parties have established systems for commercial production. Thus, standard requirements such as equipment and facility maintenance, annual product reports, lifecycle validation, complaint handling, and continued process verification (CPV) programs are not addressed. For purposes of this technical report, the TT ends with the handover to operations upon regulatory approval.

解說 Commentary

適用範圍明確：最聚焦在 Phase III 到商業化

TR65 的「甜蜜點」是臨床第三期到商業生產的移轉，以及商業廠之間的移轉。這兩種情境 CMC 要求最完整、法規要求最嚴格。早期臨床（Phase I/II）的 TT 要求相對寬鬆，TR65 不詳述差異。

範圍外的重要提示

  • 不涵蓋：醫療器材、組合產品、ATMP 的特殊要求
  • 不涵蓋：商業生產後的例行作業（CPV、年度產品報告）
  • TT 的終點：監管批准後的移交（Handover），之後由 Operations 接手

釐清終點很重要——TT 專案有開始、有結束，不是永久性職責。

CDMO 實務場景

對於無菌充填 CDMO，範圍界定尤其關鍵。客戶常將 TT 後的持續支援需求混入 TT 合約。TR65 提供依據：TT 在監管批准後的 Handover 即告終止，後續 CPV 和年度驗證是另一個服務合約的範疇。

2.0

Glossary and Abbreviations

Key terms and definitions used throughout TR65

2.0 Glossary and Abbreviations

Definitions have been provided to help clarify the concepts discussed in this technical report. While some of the definitions vary among companies and regulatory agencies, the definitions described below are consistent for use within this document. Where a definition is taken verbatim from another source, the source is cited.

Consumables
Items (e.g., storage bags, tubing, filters, diaphragms, flasks) that form or are a part of process equipment and are replaced regularly.
Critical Process Parameter (CPP)
A process parameter whose variability has an impact on a critical quality attribute and therefore should be monitored or controlled to ensure the process produces the desired quality.
Engineering Batch
A manufacturing run performed to demonstrate the transfer of a manufacturing process and confirm the suitability of qualified equipment, controls, and analytical methods that are appropriate to the lifecycle phase of the product. This may also be referred to as an engineering run, demonstration batch, or demonstration run.
Facility Fit Assessment
An assessment performed on the manufacturing capabilities of the receiving unit during a TT. This assessment will consider equipment, design, capacity, and risks associated with manufacturing the product being transferred to the receiving unit's facility.
Handover
The stage of a TT in which the TT team transitions the product's manufacturing process to the post-transfer operations team who will be responsible for the ongoing manufacturing of the product at the receiving unit. This stage may also be referred to as commercial or project turnover or hand-off.
Knowledge Transfer (KT)
A practical, systematic approach to transfer the body of knowledge available for a specific product and process, including critical-to-quality product attributes, process parameters and control strategy, process capability, testing, and manufacturing within a quality system's infrastructure between two locations.
Knowledge Management (KM)
A systematic approach to acquiring, analyzing, storing, and disseminating information related to products, manufacturing processes, and components.
Quality Target Product Profile (QTPP)
A prospective summary of the quality characteristics of a drug product that ideally will be achieved to ensure the desired quality, taking into account safety and efficacy of the drug product.
Stage Gate
A review meeting conducted during the process of TT to verify that the requirements for the current stage have been completed and that the team is ready to move into the next stage of the TT project. The meeting typically is led by the TT team and TT project manager, who present findings, results, and potential and identified risks with proposed mitigation strategies, decisions, and next steps to the steering committee for the TT. If risks are not acceptable or certain requirements have not been met, the steering committee may identify additional actions that must be performed before proceeding to the next stage. A stage gate also may include a review of lessons learned. This may also be referred to as a milestone review meeting.
User Requirements Specification (URS)
The set of owner, user and engineering requirements necessary and sufficient to create a feasible design meeting the intended purpose of the system.

解說 Commentary

三組容易混淆的術語

1. Knowledge Transfer vs. Technology Transfer
KT（知識移轉）是 TT 的一個子集或核心活動——它專注於「知識」的傳遞（製程數據、秘訣、控制策略）。TT 則更廣，包含設備評估、驗證、法規申報等整個工程。

2. Engineering Batch vs. PPQ Batch
Engineering Batch（工程批）用於確認移轉的可行性，是試跑性質；PPQ Batch（製程效能資格確認批）是正式驗證，數據用於法規申報。兩者不可混用。

3. Stage Gate vs. Milestone
Stage Gate 是正式的決策關卡，需要 Steering Committee 批准才能進入下一階段；Milestone 是時間節點，未必有決策功能。TR65 的六個 Stage Gate 是「有否決權的」。

比喻：Stage Gate = 移民入境審查

每個 Stage Gate 就像出入境審查：你可以準備好所有行李（活動完成），但必須通過審查官（Steering Committee）批准，才能登上下一班飛機（進入下一階段）。審查官有權要你補件或暫停行程。

Facility Fit Assessment — 最容易被忽略的起點

許多 TT 失敗案例都源於 Facility Fit Assessment 做得不夠深入。這個評估在 Initiation 階段就要啟動，涵蓋：設備能力、廠房設計、容量規劃，以及與該產品相關的特定風險（例如高活性、冷鏈、特殊 CCI 需求）。

CDMO 應用：QTPP 是契約的基礎

對 CDMO 而言，QTPP（品質目標產品特性）是最重要的入場文件。它定義了客戶對藥品品質的期望（效能、安全性、穩定性等），是 TT 合約範疇確認與成功標準設定的起點。若客戶在 TT 開始時無法提供完整的 QTPP，是風險訊號。

3.0

Types of Technology Transfers

ICH Q10 framework and the spectrum of TT scenarios

3.0 Types of Technology Transfers

ICH Quality Guideline Q10: Pharmaceutical Quality Systems describes TTs as new product transfers during development through manufacturing and transfers within or between manufacturing and testing sites for marketed products. This broadly encompasses all transfers, including analytical method transfers to an alternate laboratory, transfers to scale up a development product, and transfers from the laboratory to a current good manufacturing practices (GMP) production environment. When establishing a TT program, it is important to understand the impact of the product lifecycle phase, the type of sites involved, and the associated CMC requirements.

Although this section describes all types of TTs, this technical report focuses on the fundamental activities necessary for introduction of a new product to a manufacturing site when the product is moving from clinical phase III to commercial manufacturing or from one commercial manufacturing site to another.

解說 Commentary

TT 分類的兩個維度

TR65 用兩個獨立維度來分類 TT：

  • 維度一：產品生命週期（3.1）— 從哪個開發階段移轉到哪個階段
  • 維度二：廠址類型（3.2）— 內部廠到內部廠？還是涉及外部 CMO/CDMO？

這兩個維度組合，決定了 TT 的複雜度與 CMC 要求的深度。

注意：分析方法移轉（Analytical Method Transfer）也是 TT 的一部分！許多人只想到製程，但分析方法（如 HPLC 效價、無菌測試、粒徑分析）的移轉同樣需要驗證、比對，也需要跟著 TR65 的框架走。

3.1.1 Clinical-Clinical Technology Transfers

While this technical report does not describe the requirements for clinical-clinical TTs, TTs at clinical stages should be developed, reviewed, and executed with thoughtful consideration and should be phase-appropriate from a CMC perspective. TTs during these phases should address clinical patient safety and begin building the foundation for future commercial production including the development of the TPP. During early clinical phases, the involved sites should constantly consider future needs related to scaling up and validating the manufacturing process, determining the type of facility required, and defining the product, process, and analytical characteristics necessary to ensure the quality, safety, and efficacy of the product being transferred. Clinical phase TTs often are complicated by small material demands and by extended pauses for site inspections and regulatory approvals as clinical data is gathered, making knowledge management throughout the early clinical phases especially important.

3.1.2 Development-Commercial Technology Transfers

Development-commercial TTs, which often begin when the product is in clinical phase III, involve the transfer of the full scope of CMC requirements. By clinical phase III, the product, process, and analytical development should progress in parallel with the TT program to define the final CMC requirements for commercial readiness and process and analytical validation. For instance, the final dosage form and dosage should be known; the drug substance manufacturing process should be established such that there are minimal changes in the impurity profile from clinical phase III to process validation; process parameters and critical quality attributes (CQA) should be defined, including characterization of critical components and materials; and initial stability for both drug substance and drug product should have been evaluated. Risk assessments should be performed throughout final development to determine the impact areas for commercial manufacture.

The objective of the TT activity is to establish comparability between the SU and RU processes or methods and, as a result, provide the regulatory authorities the assurance needed to allow the use of the RU to supply clinical material or commercial inventory into their market.

3.1.3 Commercial-Commercial Technology Transfers

In commercial-commercial TTs, the product is already being marketed and produced at an existing facility, yet business may dictate the need to move or expand production of the drug substance or drug product to another facility. This may require an operational scale-up or scale-down. The addition of a new market may require the business to use alternate packaging facilities, which would require TT of the packaging operations. Alternate laboratories also may be used, requiring transfer of the analytical methods to the new location.

In TTs involving a marketed product, the original or current manufacturing location should provide the full CMC requirements, with historical product and process knowledge, that demonstrate manufacturing robustness. These types of TTs may be an opportunity to implement process improvements at the receiving unit, provided that those improvements align with the business and regulatory strategies for the TT.

解說 Commentary

Development-Commercial 的關鍵時間壓力

Phase III 到商業化的 TT 是「發展開發與 TT 並行」的賽跑。常見陷阱：

  • Phase III 結束才啟動 TT → 時間壓力極大
  • CMC 要求在 TT 過程中仍在演變 → 計畫不斷更改
  • 雜質特性在 Phase III 到驗證批之間改變 → 重新比對

最佳實務：Phase III 開始時同步啟動 TT 規劃。

比喻：Commercial-Commercial 是「搬家」，不是「建新家」

Commercial-Commercial TT 就像帶著已經裝潢好的家具搬到新公寓。你知道這些家具能用（有歷史數據），但新空間的格局不同，需要重新排列（設備適配），甚至趁機換一些舊家具（製程改良）。但改太多，法規機關可能要你重新申報。

CDMO 應用：最常見的是哪一種？

對 TWi 而言，最常見的情境是 Development-Commercial（新客戶的 Phase III 產品導入商業生產）和 Commercial-Commercial（客戶因產能或法規考量，將已上市產品從其他廠移轉過來）。前者挑戰最大，因為 CMC 要求尚在發展中；後者相對有歷史數據支撐，但需小心「製程改良機會」的法規影響。

3.2 Site Types

Typically, two types of sites are involved in a TT: the sending unit (SU) and the receiving unit (RU). The SU is the site with the current knowledge of the product, process, and analytical methods, while the RU is the location where the product will be manufactured or tested in the future. The use of contract manufacturing organizations (CMOs) and contract development and manufacturing organizations (CDMOs), the advent of the virtual company business model, and the rise of global manufacturing supply chains have expanded the variations of SU and RU sites involved in TTs.

Both the SU and RU sites can be classified as internal or external. Internal sites include any facility for which the existing or future marketing authorization holder (MAH) has ownership or complete control, while external sites are separate from the MAH and include CMOs, CDMOs, business partners, and external laboratories. The type of site and its relationship to the MAH impacts the effectiveness of knowledge transfer as well as the overall TT team and governance framework.

3.2.1 Internal-Internal Technology Transfers

A TT performed between locations of the same company or facility is considered the most manageable in terms of knowledge transfer between the SU and RU sites for several reasons. Most likely, these sites are governed by the same quality management system (QMS), have integrated document management systems, and employ a standardized TT process. If the company is transferring technology for an existing commercial product, historical data on process or test performance also should be readily available.

Even with use of the same site, however, a complex process or an analytical method that draws on unique knowledge held by the SU can still present challenges.

If the internal SU and RU sites are in different geographical locations, an initial evaluation will be needed to determine the potential impact of language, local implementation of the global QMS, import and export requirements, and regulatory requirements. These TTs typically will require additional budget for site-to-site interactions that allow the SU to share knowledge with the RU and participate in initial manufacturing at the new location.

3.2.2 Internal-External or External-Internal Technology Transfers

The MAH may choose to work with an external company in many different scenarios, such as:

  • Product developed internally will be manufactured at commercial scale at an external company, such as a CMO or CDMO, for cost or time-to-market purposes or due to limited capability in internal facilities.
  • Product developed at a CDMO will be manufactured at an internal site.
  • Commercialization was performed internally and transferred to an external company after receiving regulatory approval, or vice versa.
  • Use of multiple manufacturing facilities will better meet demand and/or provide risk mitigation for supply.
  • Expansion of a product to a new market requires alternate global manufacturing capability.
  • A corporate acquisition or merger results in manufacturing consolidation.

In these types of scenarios, the TT project manager (PM) has the additional challenge of managing the TT activities across multiple sites, organizations, and cultures.

Unlike internal-internal transfers, any transfer to or from an external site may involve differences in organization, quality systems, and specific procedures. In some cases, the external organization may have competing priorities, which can be difficult to discern. Defining the differences in organizational structure, quality systems, and business priorities early can facilitate alignment on the TT boundaries and success factors. Agreements on the presence of SU subject matter experts (SMEs) at the external site during the TT process to ensure a successful knowledge transfer is an important point that cannot be overlooked and should be documented in the appropriate agreements.

3.2.3 External-External Technology Transfers

Often, the MAH must manage a transfer between two external sites, which may also involve two different companies. This type of TT can present the greatest challenge, particularly if the two external parties are not known to each other (e.g., the result of a business strategy). If the external parties cannot interact, the MAH will need to act as the SU for the TT and facilitate the knowledge transfer to the RU. Agreements on the presence of MAH and SU SMEs at the external site during the TT process to ensure a successful knowledge transfer is an important point that cannot be overlooked and should be documented in the appropriate agreements.

External TT 的合約保護重點

TR65 特別強調：SU 的 SME 要到外部廠現場支援，必須在合約中明文規定。常見的合約漏洞：

  • 沒有規定 SU 技術人員出差天數和費用分擔
  • 沒有界定 SU 在 TT 後仍需支援的期限
  • 沒有處理兩廠在 QMS 差異上的責任歸屬

比喻：External-External = 婚姻媒合介紹人

在 External-External TT 中，MAH 就像婚姻介紹所。兩家外部廠商（SU 和 RU）不認識彼此，MAH 要負責「撮合」——介紹背景、促成見面、甚至在兩方鬧別扭時出面調解。如果介紹所（MAH）不積極介入，婚事（TT）很可能告吹。

CDMO 的現實：我們最常是哪種角色？

對 TWi 而言：

  • 最常見：Internal-External（客戶是 MAH/SU，TWi 是 External RU）
  • 偶見：External-External（客戶的原廠 CDMO 是 SU，TWi 是新 RU，MAH 居中協調）
  • 挑戰最大：External-External，尤其當原廠 CDMO 不願配合知識轉移時

理解自己在哪種廠址類型情境中，有助於在合約談判中預判風險。

4.0 Technology Transfer Process and Oversight

Regulatory authorities expect companies to have a documented TT process that describes the steps to be taken and the compliance requirements for each step.

Figure 4.0-1 illustrates a TT process using six stages and stage gates. These six stages and stage gates, outlined briefly in this section, are discussed in more detail in Section 5.0, and their use is described in the "Reference Guide to Technology Transfer Activities and Deliverables" (Figure 5.0-1).

Each stage of a TT serves a purpose and has identifiable outcomes. At each stage, stage gates allow a steering committee to determine whether those outcomes have been met and to decide if the TT should proceed.

In the Initiation stage, potential RUs are evaluated to identify a site with a risk profile that meets the business objectives. Once a suitable RU is selected, a TT Team is formed at the RU site. In the Planning stage, the TT Team assesses the TT risks and identifies elements critical to the knowledge transfer and overall timing. In the Knowledge Transfer stage, the formal transfer of the detailed requirements specific to the TT begins. Once the knowledge transfer elements are obtained, the TT Team works in the Readiness stage to perform the activities and achieve the deliverables required to achieve full operational readiness. In the Execution stage, the RU demonstrates and qualifies the process at scale, culminating in a regulatory submission. The final Handover stage closes the TT with regulatory approvals and allows normal operations to begin.

Each TT involves unique technical, business, and regulatory requirements that should be considered in structuring the project. Each company may choose to add or consolidate stage gates and TT process steps to fit the type of TT being undertaken, the company's organizational structure, and its level of risk tolerance.

In every TT, assessing and managing risks is essential. A mature quality risk management program, like that described in PDA TR 54, should be in place prior to starting a TT project.

解說 Commentary

法規觀點：「有文件」才算有制度

TR65 開宗明義：監管機關期望的是「有文件的 TT 流程」，而不只是靠慣例行事。這意味著你的 TT SOP 必須明確描述每個步驟的要求，並且每次 TT 都要留下執行記錄。FDA/EMA 查廠時，這是必查項目。

比喻：六個階段像火箭發射倒數

每個 Stage Gate 就像火箭發射前的倒數確認（T-10, T-5...）。任何一個子系統未就緒，發射指揮官（Steering Committee）就暫停倒數。不是懲罰，是確保安全。Stage Gate 的精神是「不帶著問題進入下一階段」。

CDMO 實務：階段可以並行，但 Gate 不能跳過

TR65 明確指出，TT 活動常因商業壓力而跨階段並行（例如，知識移轉還沒完成，就開始準備設備確認）。這是現實，可以接受。但 Stage Gate 的正式批准不能省略——即使活動並行，每個 Gate 的文件審核仍必須完整。

4.1 Cross-Functional Roles and the Project Manager

A successful TT requires the engagement of experts from cross-functional areas as well as strong project management. Table 4.1-1🔍 outlines the basic functional roles that maintain responsibilities throughout a TT and should be represented on a TT team. Because each company is organized differently, the labels for these functions may differ from one organization to the next.

Once the decision has been made to proceed with the TT, all involved sites should document expectations, roles, and responsibilities in a formal TT agreement. The TT agreement also should specify the measuring units to be used during the TT (e.g., for mass, temperature, and volume). If the transfer is internal-internal, a shared procedure may be developed in place of a TT agreement. This TT agreement or shared procedure provides information about the level of involvement of each functional role at the SU and RU that will allow each site to resource a TT team.

Each site involved in the TT should develop a cross-functional team to execute the TT. Each team should include a lead person who is an SME for each functional role described in Table 4.1-1. A single person may serve as lead for more than one functional area. While each site should have a team, references to the "TT Team" in this document mean the team at the RU unless otherwise indicated.

Team composition may vary slightly for each TT and at each involved site. In some cases, a single TT Team may be formed at the RU, with the MAH or SU also assigning staff for each functional area to provide oversight and facilitate accurate and efficient knowledge transfer. In other cases, parallel teams may be established at each site. In an external-external TT, each external location should have its own cross-functional TT team.

The TT team at each site should be involved at nearly every stage throughout the process. The TT team at the MAH and/or SU will play a significant role during the Initiation, Planning, and Knowledge Transfer stages, but should be engaged through to the Handover stage.

The TT Team at the RU will have primary responsibility for implementing the TT. This team should be involved from the Planning stage, although their role will become more significant as the TT progresses.

Each site involved in the TT should assign an individual to serve as team lead or PM for the site. This role requires basic technical knowledge across the functional areas as well as knowledge of CMC requirements. The person serving as team lead may or may not have a "project manager" job title but serves as a primary point of contact and facilitates communication, knowledge transfer, and task completion. In internal-internal TTs, where the SU and RU are located in the same facility, a single team lead may be adequate.

One PM must be the primary coordinator for the project. This technical report refers to this primary coordinator as the TT PM. Often, the PM at the RU will serve as the TT PM. In an external-external TT, however, an individual from the MAH may act as the TT PM to ensure a smooth and continuous flow of knowledge transfer. Wherever the TT PM is located, this role is critical in facilitating constant communication and should be the primary contact point for changes, decisions, and team alignment.

解說 Commentary

九個功能部門：誰最容易被遺漏？

TT 中最常被輕忽的三個部門：

  • Business（商業）：許多 TT 只有技術團隊參與，沒有財務/法務/銷售代表。但目標市場、預算、智財權問題都需要在 Initiation 階段就釐清。
  • Sourcing/Supply Chain（採購/供鏈）：原料來源和包材在新廠是否可及？物流規劃是否納入？常常到 Readiness 階段才發現原料採購有問題。
  • Regulatory（法規）：特別在 External TT 中，兩廠可能鎖定不同市場，法規申報策略需早期對齊，否則 PPQ 批次數量都可能選錯。

TT PM 的特殊要求

TR65 對 TT PM 的描述值得注意：需要「跨功能的基本技術知識 + CMC 知識」。這不是普通的專案管理師，而是需要懂技術的人。

在 External-External TT 中，TT PM 可能由 MAH 出任，而非 RU（CDMO）。這對 CDMO 意味著：你的 PM 在某些情況下不是「最後的仲裁者」，MAH 的 TT PM 才是主導決策的人。

比喻：TT 團隊像奧運轉播團隊

奧運轉播需要導演（TT PM）、攝影師（Process/Engineering）、解說員（Regulatory/QA）、技術支援（Analytical）、行政後勤（Supply Chain）全部到位。缺任何一個，轉播就會出問題。TT 團隊也是如此——缺一個功能代表，那個環節的問題就沒有人負責。

CDMO 實務：TT 協議書的必要條款

TR65 強調「TT agreement」必須列明各廠各功能的責任分工。對 CDMO 而言，這份協議書（通常是 Technical Transfer Agreement 的一部分）至少應明確：

  • SU 需提供哪些技術文件（和時限）
  • SU SME 到 RU 現場支援的天數和費用
  • 測量單位標準（避免公制/英制混用造成錯誤）
  • 每個功能部門在每個階段的決策角色

4.2 TT Management Oversight

The organizational structure supporting a TT requires clarity and definition to ensure the TT Team can maintain focus, manage resourcing, and resolve issues. A defined structure for the TT allows for improved communication and clear definition of priorities among team members and management.

A TT is usually considered a special project with a fixed scope and defined goals. As such, members of the TT Team may not be permanently assigned to the TT and may continue to report functionally outside of the team. TT Team members typically are assigned to the team under the direction of the TT PM for the duration of the project.

As the key stakeholder accountable for the product with regulatory authorities, the MAH determines the goals for the TT and is the contract giver.

Once the decision has been made to proceed with the TT, a steering committee is formed. Each site involved in the TT should assign steering committee members who will be accountable for achieving the TT goals. Typically, steering committee members are one management level above the TT team for each of the functional areas working on the TT and have decision-making authority. The steering committee is empowered to remove roadblocks, assume risk, provide resources, and determine whether the requirements of each stage have been fulfilled.

Upper management may identify a Project Sponsor to oversee the TT. The person in this role typically is the single point of contact between the TT and upper management and has overall accountability to ensure the TT is delivered as required by the business. The Project Sponsor may be part of the steering committee, but the steering committee is accountable to the Project Sponsor.

Stage gates at each of the six stages allow the steering committee to determine whether a TT is ready to proceed to the next stage. Often TT activities will be performed across stages in parallel rather than sequentially due to business needs. Each stage gate, however, allows the steering committee to ensure that all necessary activities and deliverables for that stage have been satisfactorily completed and that the TT charter, as described in Section 4.3, can be accomplished.

When a TT Team finds information during a stage that may change the TT boundaries and/or assumptions, it should be communicated immediately to the steering committee rather than waiting for the stage gate review.

解說 Commentary

重要原則：重大變化不等 Gate，立即上報

TR65 特別強調：如果 TT 團隊在某個階段發現任何可能改變 TT 邊界或假設的資訊，不要等到 Stage Gate 才報告，應立即上報 Steering Committee。這是避免「問題雪球滾到 Gate 才爆發」的關鍵原則。

實務警示：許多 TT 失敗案例，都源於團隊知道問題但選擇「看看之後能不能解決再說」，最終延誤了整個計畫。

比喻：Steering Committee = 飛機駕駛艙

TT 團隊是機艙服務員（執行任務），TT PM 是副駕駛（管理協調），Steering Committee 是機長（決策），Project Sponsor 是航空公司 CEO（最終責任）。如果機身異常震動（TT 發現重大問題），副駕駛要立刻告知機長，而不是等到「下降高度前的例行報告」。

CDMO 的雙重身份挑戰

在 Internal-External TT 中，CDMO 通常是 RU，派員加入 Steering Committee 代表 RU 的利益。但 CDMO 同時也要對客戶（MAH/SU）負責。這種「服務提供者 + Steering Committee 成員」的雙重身份，有時會產生衝突：當客戶要求跳過某個 Gate，CDMO 的 Steering Committee 代表應該如何回應？答案是：以 GMP 合規為底線，不得讓步。

The governing document of every TT project

4.3 TT Charter and Success Factors

The overall framework of a TT should be documented in a charter. The charter should be developed by the TT Team and must be approved by the steering committee and, if applicable, the Project Sponsor. Approval by the steering committee establishes that the sites have reached the alignment necessary to proceed.

The charter typically includes the following information:

  • Definition of TT completion and success factors for the TT
  • Business assumptions, major risks, and decisions
  • Target markets and market volumes
  • Regulatory strategy
  • Steering committee members
  • Relevant stakeholders and how they are involved and connected to the project
  • PMs and functional points of contact at SU and RU, if needed
  • Communication plan
  • Timeline with critical path objectives
  • Lead SMEs for each cross-functional area
  • Estimate of resource requirements

The success factors defined in the charter will serve as the boundaries within which the TT Teams will work (see Section 5.1.2 for more details). These success factors should be developed by the TT PM with agreement and input from the steering committee. Often based on business and regulatory strategic objectives, some typical success factors that would be documented in the TT charter might include:

  • Right-first-time execution of process validation batches
  • Demonstration of comparability
  • Regulatory filing submitted
  • Preapproval inspection (PAI) authorized
  • Budget goals achieved
  • Time-to-market requirements met
  • Handover to the RU post-transfer operations team
  • Process improvements impacting production efficiency or cost

Any changes to the requirements for TT success or the business assumptions defined in the charter should prompt the steering committee to revisit the Initiation and Planning stages to assess the potential impact of the changes.

Any changes to the charter after the Planning stage should be approved by the steering committee and documented.

解說 Commentary

TT Charter：TT 的「憲法」

Charter 是 TT 的基本法——所有決策都要回到 Charter 的框架內。它的功能不只是計畫書，更是：

  • 對齊工具：確保 SU、RU、MAH 對「完成標準」有共識
  • 防爭議文件：當各方對範圍有分歧時，回到 Charter 解決
  • 變更管理基線：Planning 後的任何 Charter 變更，必須 Steering Committee 批准

成功因素 vs. KPI：不要混淆

成功因素（Success Factors）是 TT 的「完成標準」，是 TT 是否可以宣告成功的判準。它不等於 KPI（績效指標）。例如：

  • 成功因素：「PPQ 三批次一次通過」
  • KPI：「每批次 OEE 達到 80%」

成功因素要在 Charter 中明文定義，並且雙方（SU 和 RU）都要同意。如果沒有事先定義，TT 結束時就會各說各話。

比喻：Charter 是「婚前協議書」

Charter 就像婚前協議：在關係正式開始前，雙方白紙黑字同意各自的責任、期望和完成標準。感情好的時候不需要，但一旦有爭議，就是最重要的法律文件。TT 中的 Charter 也是如此——業務順利時沒人看，但出問題時每個字都很重要。

CDMO 常見缺漏：Charter 沒有定義「結束點」

許多 CDMO 的 TT 專案因為 Charter 沒有清楚定義「完成即結束」，導致：

  • 客戶要求 TT 團隊繼續支援第 4、5 批商業生產
  • TT 與正式商業生產的費用邊界不清
  • 哪些「製程調整」算 TT 範圍、哪些算 post-TT 變更管理，無從界定

解決方案：Charter 中明確寫明「Handover 後，TT 正式結束，後續事宜移交 Commercial Operations 團隊處理」。

解說 Commentary

Reference Guide 的實際用法

這個矩陣是 TR65 最具操作性的工具。正確的使用方式：

  1. 啟動 TT 時：以矩陣為藍圖，識別所有活動和交付物
  2. Stage Gate 前：對照矩陣確認該功能的所有交付物是否完成
  3. 資源規劃時：以矩陣每格的工作量估算各部門需要的人力
  4. 問責追蹤時：每格對應一個主責部門，避免「空格沒人管」

關鍵句：所有部門必須「交叉並行」

TR65 強調「all functional areas must intersect and work in parallel」——這一句話否定了很多公司的「線性 TT 模式」（Process 部門先完成，Analytical 才開始，Engineering 再跟進）。正確做法是並行啟動，並且各部門之間要有交叉協作（例如，Process 和 Analytical 要一起評估在制控制的樣品計畫）。

比喻：矩陣像電影劇本分場表

電影劇本分場表列出每個場景需要哪些演員、道具、場地。Reference Guide 就是 TT 的「拍攝計畫表」——每個階段（場景）需要哪個部門（演員）做什麼事（台詞/動作）。導演（TT PM）依此排程，確保每個人在對的時間點到位。

CDMO 應用：矩陣幫助報價更準確

對 CDMO 而言，Reference Guide 矩陣還有一個重要用途：協助 TT 報價。每一格代表一個工作量，可以換算成人天數。透過估算矩陣中每格的人天，可以得出更準確的 TT 費用估算，避免低報或高估。

5.1 Initiation Stage — Overview

The Initiation stage covers the process of determining whether the TT of CMC activities for a pharmaceutical or biopharmaceutical product should be performed. In this stage, the team evaluating the need for the TT often has limited information on the product characteristics, process requirements, and analytical testing from the SU, particularly if the product is still under development. While the Initiation stage is primarily driven by the Business function of the MAH, technical experts also should conduct high-level risk assessments.

A PM should be assigned to facilitate RU site selection and to develop the initial charter. This stage results in a decision on whether to perform the TT, the choice of an RU site, the formation of a steering committee, and the development of an initial TT charter as well as an overall product strategy, including business and regulatory considerations. Table 5.1-1🔍, excerpted from the Reference Guide (Figure 5.0-1), shows the activities and deliverables for the Initiation stage.

啟動階段 — 總覽

核心概念：啟動階段是什麼？

啟動階段是技術轉移（TT）的第一個正式階段。此階段的核心問題只有一個：「我們應該轉移這個產品嗎？轉到哪裡？」

在此階段，資訊通常不完整（特別是產品仍在開發中時），因此決策依賴高層次風險評估與業務判斷，而非詳細技術數據。

比喻：搬家前的評估

啟動階段就像決定要不要搬家、以及搬到哪裡。你不需要知道每一件家具如何搬運，但你需要評估：新家的空間夠嗎？交通方便嗎？費用在預算內嗎？一旦決定了，才開始詳細規劃搬家流程。

重點：啟動階段的四大輸出

  • 決定是否執行 TT
  • 選定接收單位（RU）廠址
  • 成立指導委員會（Steering Committee）
  • 建立初始 TT 章程（Charter）與產品策略

5.1.1 Business

The Business function at the MAH takes the lead role in the Initiation stage, developing the business case for initiating a TT activity. It facilitates obtaining key information from the SU, such as the quality target product profile (QTPP) and/or specifications to identify the product characteristics and high-level process requirements. Using a high-level risk assessment, the Business function develops the product strategy to guide the TT decision. Legal counsel also is engaged for intellectual property review to ensure the freedom to operate, if needed.

At this point, the RU site selection process can begin. If the product will be sourced externally, the Business function secures nondisclosure agreements or confidentiality agreements with potential RUs. The criteria laid out in the QTPP or product specification, the product strategy, the intellectual property review, and the information required for product lifecycle phase (see Section 3.1) are used in the site selection process. Typically, potential RUs are assessed for capability, age of facility, location, capacity, resourcing, costs, tax implications, compliance history, regulatory strategy, and other logistical considerations. Once a RU site is selected, the initial TT charter is approved, and the steering committee is formed. Section 8.1 presents a case study of best practices and benefits of establishing a steering committee for a project.

The stage gate for Initiation results in a decision to proceed with the TT; selection of the RU, often with a signed contract; and approval of the initial charter. Two case studies, Section 8.4 on using a regulatory strategy for decision-making and Section 8.8 on TT of an aseptic drug product, illustrate factors to consider when selecting a RU or supplier.

5.1.1 業務功能

業務功能在啟動階段的三大任務

  1. 建立商業案例：開發 TT 活動的商業論證，包含市場需求分析與財務可行性
  2. 取得產品資訊：從 SU 獲取 QTPP 和/或產品規格，理解產品特性與製程需求
  3. 主導 RU 廠址選擇：帶領評估與選擇接收單位，完成法律文件（NDA/CDA）

比喻：RU 廠址選擇就像選外包商

選 RU 就像企業評估外包廠商：不只看報價，更要評估能力（capability）、合規歷史（compliance history）、稅務考量、地理位置、產能彈性。最終選定後才簽合約，組成指導委員會監督整個專案。

RU 選擇評估標準（典型清單）

  • 製造能力（Capability）
  • 廠房設施年齡（Age of facility）
  • 地理位置（Location）
  • 產能（Capacity）
  • 人力資源（Resourcing）
  • 成本與稅務影響（Costs, tax implications）
  • 合規歷史（Compliance history）
  • 法規策略一致性（Regulatory strategy）
  • 其他後勤考量（Logistical considerations）

階段閘門（Stage Gate）輸出

啟動階段的 Stage Gate 有三個明確輸出：

  • 決定繼續執行 TT
  • 選定 RU（通常已簽合約）
  • 初始章程獲批准

5.1.2 Initiation Stage Project Manager

Involvement of a PM during the Initiation stage of a TT is critical to provide overall leadership and accountability for the project. The PM needs to be knowledgeable across all relevant functional areas and able to communicate across organizational levels. Additionally, this role requires such soft skills as facilitation, negotiation, decision-making, and motivation to effectively lead a cross-functional team to achieve business goals. Once the RU has been selected and the TT moves into the Planning stage, a TT PM will be assigned. In many TTs, the RU PM will serve as TT PM for the remainder of the project.

The Initiation Stage PM coordinates an initial high-level risk assessment using cross-functional expertise and drives site selection by ensuring the completion of proper due diligence of the RUs under consideration. These assessments must be documented to support both the TT decision and site selection. One methodology for these assessments is risk-ranking and filtering.

One key deliverable the Initiation Stage PM must fulfill during the Initiation stage is the establishment of a charter with the steering committee. While the initial charter at this stage may not yet include all the details, it should document the boundaries, major risks, and assumptions used to determine whether the TT will proceed as a part of the product strategy, and it should specify the RU site that is selected. Boundaries and/or assumptions that should be considered during the Initiation stage to determine the scope of the TT include:

  • Required time frame for completion
  • Regulatory strategy options
  • Estimated capital required
  • Resource needs and constraints
  • Scope of process to be transferred
  • Target markets
  • Annual production volume
  • Site selection decision and criteria used

Finally, the PM creates an initial, high-level project plan estimating timing, resource needs, and budget.

5.1.2 啟動階段專案經理

TT PM 的核心職責

啟動階段 PM 是整個 TT 的早期架構師，需具備：

  • 跨功能知識：了解業務、製程、法規、品質各面向
  • 跨層級溝通：能與高管及操作層人員有效溝通
  • 軟技能：促進（facilitation）、談判（negotiation）、決策（decision-making）、激勵（motivation）

比喻：PM 是 TT 的「建設指揮官」

啟動階段的 PM 就像建案的總指揮：他不需要親自砌磚，但必須確保地基穩固（章程正確）、各工班準時到位（跨部門協調）、圖紙與實際需求一致（風險評估文件化）。建案一旦動工（進入規劃階段），通常會移交給 RU 的 PM 接手。

初始章程（Initial Charter）必須涵蓋的邊界條件

  • 完成所需時間框架
  • 法規策略選項
  • 預估所需資本
  • 人力資源需求與限制
  • 待轉移製程範圍
  • 目標市場
  • 年產量
  • 廠址選擇決定及使用的標準

思考練習：誰是你的啟動階段 PM？

在你的組織中，當一個新產品 TT 案開始時，第一個被指派的 PM 是誰？來自 MAH 還是 RU？他在章程建立之前就介入了嗎？這對 TT 決策品質有什麼影響？

5.1.3 Process

The Process function first determines the subject matter expertise required on the TT team for the product under evaluation by modality or stage of manufacture. For instance, a small-molecule drug substance would need a process expert who has worked with organic chemistry processing, while a filled-vial drug product would need an expert in sterile manufacturing. Second, the MAH Process function develops a preliminary process flow and works with the SU to develop or obtain the QTPP and/or specification for the product. Third, using the assumptions provided by the Business function, Process develops batch size options and assesses scale-up or scale-down requirements against the current process at the SU. Finally, working with the Engineering, Manufacturing, and Analytical functions, Process assesses the process fit and evaluates the facilities, equipment, and operations group of the potential RU site to determine its capability to manufacture the product, noting gaps and investments required.

5.1.3 製程功能

製程功能的四個步驟

  1. 確定 SME 需求：依產品類型決定所需專業知識（小分子藥物 vs 無菌製劑 vs 生物製品）
  2. 建立初步製程流程：與 SU 合作取得 QTPP 和/或產品規格
  3. 評估批量與規模需求：比較 SU 現有製程與 RU 目標批量，評估放大或縮小需求
  4. 製程適配性評估（Process Fit Assessment）：確定 RU 是否有能力製造該產品

關鍵交付物

  • Process Flow/Description：初步製程流程圖
  • Process Fit/Gap Assessment：製程適配與差距評估報告

差距評估的輸出將直接影響 RU 廠址選擇決定，並決定啟動後的資本投入需求。

無菌填充場景的應用

對於無菌填充藥品（如我廠的業務），製程 SME 需評估：RU 是否具備適合的隔離系統（isolator/RABS）、凍乾設備（lyophilizer）、自動化視覺檢測（AVI）、以及相應的無菌工藝控制能力，這些差距將形成資本估算的基礎。

5.1.4 Analytical

The Analytical function uses the SU's QTPP and/or specification to determine product testing requirements and to evaluate potential testing of raw materials, single-use and other components, and packaging. Test methods are categorized either as compendial or non-compendial for the target markets to define the complexity of method transfer from the SU. With this information, the Analytical function assesses the potential RU's laboratory capabilities and capacity and provides a high-level estimate of what would be required for the TT. This evaluation covers instrumentation needs, glassware, reference standards, personnel, external laboratory services, and timing. Evaluation of site analytical capabilities of potential RUs is an essential element of due diligence. At this stage, the Analytical function documents the laboratory and method evaluations.

5.1.4 分析功能

分析功能的核心工作

分析功能從 QTPP/產品規格 出發，評估：

  • 產品所需的測試項目（放行測試、原料測試、包裝材測試）
  • 方法類型：法典方法（compendial）vs 非法典方法（non-compendial）
  • RU 實驗室的儀器、人員、外部委外實驗室能力

比喻：Compendial vs Non-compendial 方法

法典方法（如 USP、Ph. Eur. 規定的無菌試驗）就像「公定食譜」，任何廠都可直接使用；非法典方法則是「獨門秘方」，必須從 SU 完整轉移，驗證難度更高、耗時更長。

關鍵交付物

  • Laboratory Evaluation Report：RU 實驗室能力評估報告
  • Methods Evaluation Report：方法評估報告

這兩份文件也是 RU due diligence 的重要組成部分，直接影響廠址選擇。

5.1.5 Engineering

Evaluation of site manufacturing capabilities is another essential element of due diligence in selecting the most appropriate RU and deciding to proceed with the TT. This evaluation, also referred to as a facility fit assessment, includes such items as site utilities and infrastructure, processing equipment, and automation capabilities. The Engineering function, working closely with Process, Analytical, and Manufacturing functions, performs the facility fit assessment to form an initial rough estimate of capital that would be required. The case study in Section 8.6 provides more detail on the facility fit assessment.

5.1.5 工程功能

廠房適配性評估（Facility Fit Assessment）

工程功能執行廠房適配性評估，涵蓋：

  • 廠區公用設施與基礎設施（site utilities and infrastructure）
  • 製造設備（processing equipment）
  • 自動化能力（automation capabilities）

評估結果形成初步資本需求估算（rough order of magnitude capital estimate）。

關鍵交付物

  • Facility Fit Assessment：廠房適配性評估報告
  • Capital Estimate：資本估算
  • Permit Requirements：建照/許可需求清單

比喻：廠房適配就像房屋驗屋

移入新家前，你會請驗屋師評估電路（utilities）、格局空間（equipment fit）、智慧家電相容性（automation capabilities）。如果需要大改裝（資本投入），就會影響你的搬家決策與預算。

5.1.6 Manufacturing

To perform site selection, the Business function needs to understand the potential RU's ability to implement the process. Capability extends beyond capital requirements. The Manufacturing function assesses the potential RU's resource flexibility, operational training program, previous and current manufacturing activities in the facility, organizational structure, current operating shifts, and process data management systems.

Environment, health, and safety (EHS) issues at the potential RU should be evaluated as well. An employee health and safety viability report can be used in evaluating potential issues. The Manufacturing function should assess the environmental and occupational permits or certifications that will be needed, along with the RU's ability to obtain them and their potential impact on the TT timeline.

5.1.6 製造功能

能力不只是設備 — 製造功能的評估範圍

製造功能提醒業務方：能力（capability）遠不止設備。評估項目包含：

  • 人力資源彈性（resource flexibility）
  • 操作培訓計畫（operational training program）
  • 現有及歷史製造活動（previous and current activities）
  • 組織架構（organizational structure）
  • 班次安排（operating shifts）
  • 製程數據管理系統（process data management systems）

EHS 評估不可忽視

環境、健康、安全（EHS）評估是廠址選擇的必要項目。相關交付物：

  • EHS Viability Report：EHS 可行性報告
  • Permit Requirements：環保/職安許可需求

EHS 許可的取得時間可能很長，必須在啟動階段即識別，否則可能成為 TT 時程的瓶頸。

5.1.7 Regulatory

Involving the MAH Regulatory function as soon as possible is critical to a TT project. A regulatory strategy should be developed to align the product strategy with the Business function and to establish boundaries and assumptions for the TT charter. Key elements of the initial regulatory strategy are filing category, regulatory assumptions, likely data requirements and source documents required, potential need for a health authority meeting, projected timing of regulatory submission to each health authority, and regulations applicable to the product in the region(s) in which it is to be manufactured and marketed.

The regulatory strategy is a living document that will evolve throughout the project. It should be reviewed at each stage for required updates and further refinement based on the knowledge gained at that stage. Therefore, the ultimate regulatory strategy may look significantly different than this initial draft as knowledge is transferred and more information becomes available.

The regulatory strategy typically would address the following:

  • List of markets and the planned timing of submission for each market
  • Proposed filing category for the TT and the justification for this determination
  • Specific regulations applicable to the product and region(s) where it will be marketed
  • Specific data requirements and source documents to support the change related to the TT that the governing regulatory agency would expect (e.g., sterility and aseptic processing, stability, qualification and validation considerations, clinical/bioequivalence requirements, and potential required permits)
  • Key regulatory assumptions and risks that could impact the overall TT success and possible mitigation strategies
  • Documents the RU will need to prepare for authoring, review, approval, and submission of the regulatory filing
  • Product approval timeline
  • GMP inspection history (lack of)
  • Current GMP certificates

The Regulatory function also will perform due diligence of potential RUs for outstanding actions taken by regulatory authorities that would make the RU unsuitable for use.

5.1.7 法規功能

法規功能必須盡早介入

TR65 明確指出：「Involving the MAH Regulatory function as soon as possible is critical。」法規策略不是製造完成後再考慮的事，而是啟動階段就要建立的邊界條件。

初始法規策略的核心要素

  • 申請類別（Filing category）：此次 TT 對應的申請類型（如 Prior Approval Supplement、Annual Report 等）
  • 數據需求（Data requirements）：各法規機構預期的支持數據
  • 申請時程（Submission timing）：各市場的預計申請時間
  • 健康主管機關會議（HA meeting）：是否需要事先諮詢
  • GMP 稽查歷史（GMP inspection history）

比喻：法規策略是「活文件」

法規策略就像導航 App 的路線規劃：出發前設好方向（初始策略），但沿途遇到施工（新法規）或改道（技術變更），會即時更新重新規劃。啟動階段的版本與最終提交版本可能大相徑庭，這是正常的。

CDMO 觀點：RU Due Diligence 的法規面

對 CDMO 作為 RU 而言，法規 due diligence 包含：確認 RU 有無未解決的法規行動（483、Warning Letter、進口禁令），以及評估 RU 在目標市場的 GMP 認可狀態。這直接影響客戶的申請策略與時程。

5.1.8 Quality

The Quality function at the MAH performs the quality assessment as part of due diligence on the potential RUs. In preparing the quality assessment for each RU, which includes the RU site assessment, the Quality function typically asks the potential RU to complete a questionnaire and conducts a search for compliance history records. For external RUs, the compliance history records may be public, while the assessment of an internal site can involve records internal to the company. The purpose of the quality due diligence is to determine the maturity of the RU quality system, the RU's organizational mindset regarding GMPs, and its adherence to applicable regulatory requirements. Quality will provide a compliance risk evaluation of the potential RUs.

5.1.8 品質功能

品質 Due Diligence 的三個目的

  1. 評估 QMS 成熟度（maturity of the RU quality system）
  2. 了解 GMP 組織文化（organizational mindset regarding GMPs）
  3. 確認法規符合性（adherence to applicable regulatory requirements）

評估方法

  • 要求 RU 填寫問卷（questionnaire）
  • 查閱公開合規歷史記錄（Warning Letters、483s、EMA 報告）
  • 內部廠址則查閱內部記錄

輸出：Quality Assessment(s) — 合規風險評估報告，作為廠址選擇的重要依據。

比喻：品質評估就像信用調查

選擇 RU 前的品質 due diligence，就像銀行放款前的信用調查：過去有多少不良記錄（Warning Letter）？現在的還款能力（現行 QMS）如何？組織文化上是否重視誠信（GMP mindset）？這決定了這個「借款方」（RU）的風險等級。

5.1.9 Sourcing/Supply Chain

The Sourcing/Supply Chain function develops initial capacity-planning models using estimated yield and cycle times. These models are used to evaluate potential RUs for batch size, manufacturing cycle time, and on-site storage needs as part of the facility fit assessment. The capacity model should incorporate product volumes for development and validation activities as well as commercial scale. The Sourcing/Supply Chain function evaluates sourcing of the required materials, consumables, and components to determine any potential long-lead items and specially regulated materials (e.g., materials subject to national quotas), and it works with the Regulatory or appropriate function to identify special import/export requirements where permits would be needed. The Sourcing/Supply Chain function also prepares a preliminary supply chain strategy to assess the impact of each potential RU on the product value chain.

5.1.9 採購 / 供應鏈功能

供應鏈功能的三大任務

  1. 初步產能規劃模型：利用估算的良率與週期時間，評估 RU 的批量、製造週期、倉儲需求
  2. 原物料來源評估：識別長前置時間（long-lead）物料與特殊管制物料（如國家配額物質）
  3. 初步供應鏈策略：評估各 RU 候選廠對產品價值鏈的影響

關鍵交付物

  • Preliminary Product Supply Chain Strategy, Including Map：初步供應鏈策略與地圖
  • Critical Material List：關鍵物料清單
  • Perform RU Capacity Analysis：RU 產能分析

長前置時間物料的重要性

識別長前置時間物料（如特定濾芯、隔離袋、特殊玻璃容器）對於 TT 時程規劃至關重要。若在規劃階段才發現某物料需要 18 個月採購期，可能導致整個 TT 時程大幅延誤。啟動階段即識別此類風險，才能提前採取行動。

What is the Initiation Stage? (啟動階段是什麼？)

The Initiation Stage answers the fundamental question: Should we perform this TT, and where? It runs before detailed planning begins, when information about the product and process is still limited. Decision-making relies on high-level risk assessments rather than detailed technical data.

啟動階段回答一個基本問題：我們應該執行這次 TT 嗎？轉到哪裡？此階段在詳細規劃開始之前進行，產品與製程資訊仍然有限，決策依賴高層次風險評估。

Key Outputs (關鍵輸出)

  • Decision to proceed with TT / 決定執行 TT
  • RU site selected (often with signed contract) / 選定 RU 廠址（通常已簽合約）
  • Steering Committee formed / 指導委員會成立
  • Initial TT Charter approved / 初始 TT 章程獲批
  • Preliminary regulatory strategy / 初步法規策略
  • High-level project plan / 高層次專案計畫

Key Takeaways for CDMO Operations (CDMO 操作重點)

  • 當你的公司（TWi）作為 RU，要通過客戶的 Business、Quality、Regulatory、Engineering due diligence，必須在各面向有充分的文件準備（Facility Fit Assessment、Quality Assessment responses、Regulatory track record）。
  • 法規功能必須在最早期就介入，因為申請策略（PAC vs. Prior Approval）直接影響 TT 時程與資源分配。
  • 長前置時間物料（如隔離袋、特殊濾芯）必須在啟動階段識別，不能等到規劃階段才處理。

5.2 Planning Stage — Overview

The Planning stage begins after the site selection decision has been made and the initial charter has been approved; it is the start of the knowledge transfer from the SU. At this point, the TT PM is assigned, and the core TT Team at the RU is resourced. The members of the TT Team at the SU or MAH may or may not be the same personnel who performed the initial assessments in the Initiation stage.

Building on the risk assessments developed in the Initiation stage, the TT Team collaborates with the SU and/or MAH to perform detailed risk assessments to define all CMC and operational readiness requirements for the TT. This risk assessment becomes a living document that results in a revised and more detailed version of the project plan, which covers project timing, capital required, resource needs, and critical path items. Other key outputs of the Planning stage are the final TT charter and a TT agreement between the SU, RU, and MAH (see Section 5.2.2).

Once the Planning stage activities and deliverables have been completed, the TT team should hold a stage gate meeting with the steering committee to obtain agreement for moving to the Knowledge Transfer stage. At this stage gate, the steering committee will approve the final charter and the project plan, which signals acceptance of the risks defined for the TT. 規劃階段 — 核心解說

核心概念：規劃階段的本質

規劃階段（Planning Stage）是整個技術移轉（TT）中正式知識傳遞的起點。它在地點選定、初始章程獲批准後才啟動，意味著此時「做不做」已決定，進入的是「怎麼做」的細部規劃。

  • TT 專案經理（TT PM）正式指派
  • 接收單位（RU）核心團隊完成組建
  • 詳細風險評估取代高層次評估
  • 專案計畫升級為具體、可執行版本

比喻：從「選地建廠」到「施工藍圖」

啟動階段（Initiation）如同選定建廠地點，規劃階段則是繪製詳細施工藍圖——每一根鋼筋、每條水電管線都要定位。少了這份藍圖，施工必然混亂且高成本。

關鍵輸出（Key Outputs）

  • 最終 TT 章程（Final TT Charter）
  • SU / RU / MAH 三方 TT 協議
  • 詳細專案計畫（含關鍵路徑）
  • Stage Gate 批准——向 Knowledge Transfer 推進

5.2.1 Business

The MAH Business function coordinates with the Sourcing/Supply Chain function to execute supply agreements (e.g., Master Service Agreement) with the RU and external laboratories. Critical to the outcome of the TT, supply agreements should be reviewed by the TT PM to ensure they contain the same boundaries and assumptions as the TT charter, align with the RU roles and responsibilities in the TT agreement, and support the requirements of the quality agreement. Many RUs ask that the supply agreements guarantee a fixed number of batches for both the initial qualification and the annual commercial production, as this allows for better resource planning.

Jointly, the Business functions and steering committee provide oversight of the project by approving resource and capital needs. A resource plan should be developed to understand staffing requirements both for the TT and for the long-term commercial needs, based on the following key inputs:

  • Personnel required and organization of shifts to be used
  • New equipment to be qualified and degree of automated vs. manual operational requirements
  • Type of expertise needed
  • Complexity of the technical requirements, including risk mitigation requirements

Section 8.3 provides a case study of the importance of incorporating the learnings from previous projects into the resource plan during the Planning stage.

The Business function should provide input on the regulatory strategy, including volume requirements, required timing for market release, and the salability of the validation batches.

5.2.1 商務功能 — 解說

核心概念：供應協議與資源計畫

商務功能在規劃階段的核心任務有二：

  • 執行供應協議（Master Service Agreement, MSA）— 確保協議邊界與 TT 章程一致
  • 批准資源與資本計畫— 與指導委員會共同監督

許多 RU 要求供應協議承諾固定批次數，涵蓋初始確認批次及年度商業生產，以利資源規劃。

比喻：婚前財產協議

供應協議就像婚前財產協議——在合作開始前把「誰負責什麼、做多少批、給多少錢」全部寫清楚，避免日後合作爭議。TT PM 必須確認協議內容與 TT 章程一致，否則「說一套、做另一套」的矛盾將在執行時爆發。

資源計畫四要素

資源計畫 =
  ① 人員配置（人數 + 班次安排）
+ ② 設備資格（自動化 vs. 手動作業比例）
+ ③ 所需專業類型（製程、分析、法規、品保⋯）
+ ④ 技術複雜度（含風險緩解需求）

同時須涵蓋 TT 期間需求 AND 長期商業生產需求，兩者不可偏廢。

重點提示：驗證批次可否銷售？

商務功能必須向法規策略提供「驗證批次（validation batches）是否可商業銷售」的資訊。在某些市場，驗證批次可作商業出貨，大幅降低成本；但需法規批准時間配合，必須提前規劃。

5.2.2 Technology Transfer Project Manager

Once the Business function determines that a TT will take place and has reached agreement with the RU, the TT PM is assigned. The TT PM provides overall leadership and accountability for the remainder of the project. Therefore, the same qualities are important: knowledge across all relevant functional areas, ability to communicate across organizational levels, and skill in facilitation, negotiation, decision-making, and motivation. Section 4.1 discusses the selection and role of the TT PM.

The TT PM should put in place a team representing the relevant cross-functional areas to begin planning and assessing the requirements for knowledge transfer. This team is established in collaboration with each site's functional managers. The case study in Section 8.2 discusses the importance of establishing a cross-functional project team.

The TT PM works with the SU and RU to define the roles and responsibilities for all knowledge transfer requirements. If both the SU and RU are external, then the MAH should be included as a third party. A responsibility assignment matrix denoting those to be responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed (RACI) can prove valuable in documenting key responsibilities to ensure alignment and clarity throughout the project. Once negotiated, these roles and responsibilities should be documented in a TT agreement, or in a shared procedure for internal-internal TTs, and personnel can be assigned. The TT agreement can aid significantly in defining resource needs and in facilitating communication.

The TT PM plays the lead role during the Planning stage by coordinating the final risk assessments and developing the project plan. Using the detailed risk assessments, the TT PM will work with the TT Team to ensure that the project plan includes the schedule, critical path activities, and resource requirements. The TT PM also will ensure that the project requirements and timeline meet the needs of the business and stakeholders, taking into account the capabilities of the functions and systems supporting the project. The project plan typically covers:

  • Capital procurement, installation, and qualification timing
  • Procurement and release of all materials and consumables
  • Process development and/or demonstration
  • Analytical method validations, transfers, or qualifications
  • Operational readiness requirements
  • Plant-scale process validation
  • Key milestones and critical path activities

Once the project plan has been developed, the TT charter can be expanded to include the communication plan. A communication plan and a system for sharing documents between the parties is necessary to facilitate knowledge transfer. The communication plan should define modes of escalation to ensure the TT Team can resolve any roadblocks that may occur.

At this time, the TT PM should hold a kick-off meeting with the final cross-functional TT team members (including both the SU and RU) and project sponsor.

5.2.2 TT 專案經理 — 解說

核心概念：TT PM 的角色轉換

TT PM 在規劃階段從「協調者」升格為「主導者」。他/她必須具備：

  • 跨功能領域的知識（製程、分析、法規、品保⋯）
  • 跨組織層級的溝通能力
  • 談判、決策、激勵等領導技能

比喻：交響樂團指揮

TT PM 如同交響樂團指揮——不必親自演奏每件樂器，但必須知道每個樂手何時進場、如何配合，並在混亂中維持節奏。RACI 矩陣就是那份樂譜，確保每個人清楚自己的音符。

RACI 矩陣的實用價值

Responsible（執行）、Accountable（當責）、Consulted（諮詢）、Informed（知會）——在 SU、RU、MAH 三方同時涉及時，若無 RACI，責任重疊與空白幾乎無可避免。

專案計畫七大覆蓋項目

專案計畫必須涵蓋：
① 資本採購、安裝、資格認定時程
② 物料與耗材採購及放行
③ 製程開發 / 展示
④ 分析方法驗證、移轉或確認
⑤ 操作就緒需求
⑥ 廠規模製程驗證（PV）
⑦ 關鍵里程碑與關鍵路徑活動

思考練習：您廠的 Kick-Off 會議夠完整嗎？

PDA TR65 明確要求 TT PM 在規劃階段末召開 Kick-Off 會議，邀集 SU、RU 雙方跨功能成員及專案贊助人。

請評估：您上一次或下一次技術移轉的 Kick-Off 會議，是否確實涵蓋 SU 代表？是否有正式溝通計畫（含升級機制）？

5.2.3 Process

During the Planning stage, the Process function begins to specify the key elements of the commercial manufacturing process for the TT, including batch scale, unit operations needed, cycle time, and material handling requirements, which will result in an initial bill of materials. These knowledge elements are used by the Sourcing/Supply Chain function for production planning and plant logistics and by the Engineering function to design and order the required capital. The Process function aligns QTPP and/or specifications with the RU. Often, Process will develop a comparability plan to identify the information necessary to show that the product made at the RU is comparable to that of the SU.

The Process function also performs a more detailed risk assessment to define the changes that will be required at the RU based on its equipment, scale, and capabilities. The risk assessment should include evaluation of the properties and requirements of the product being transferred. This risk assessment not only defines the knowledge transfer requirements from the SU, but also is used by Engineering to estimate the capital requirements. If the TT involves a new product or technology, the process needs to be designed such that it will run reproducibly at the RU to consistently meet the requirements of the QTPP and/or specifications.

The Process function, working with Quality and Regulatory, will define strategies for both process and cleaning validation at the Planning stage and ensure that the SU and RU align their change control processes. The Process function will issue the change request for the TT in the change management system as well.

5.2.3 製程功能 — 解說

核心概念：商業製程的規格化

製程功能在此階段將 SU 的製程知識「翻譯」成 RU 可執行的規格：

  • 批量（batch scale）確定
  • 單元操作（unit operations）盤點
  • 週期時間（cycle time）估算
  • 物料處理需求 → 初始物料清單（Bill of Materials）

比喻：食譜的「在地化」改版

將法式料理食譜改成台灣廚房可以執行的版本——食材換了、爐具不同、份量放大——比較計畫（comparability plan）就是確保「改版後的菜餚口感與原版一致」的驗收標準。

重點：變更控制必須現在啟動

製程功能在規劃階段就要在變更管理系統中正式發出「TT 變更申請」，並確保 SU 與 RU 的變更控制流程相互對齊。延遲啟動變更控制常是後期稽核缺失的根源。

實務應用：新產品 vs. 既有產品

若 TT 涉及的是新產品或新技術，製程必須設計到能在 RU 穩定重現，且持續符合 QTPP 要求。這與移轉既有成熟產品有本質差異——設計自由度更大，但不確定性也更高，風險評估必須更深入。

5.2.4 Analytical

The Analytical function will perform a more detailed risk assessment to determine final analytical equipment requirements that will help determine analytical testing sites. Testing sites may be located at the SU, RU, MAH, or an external laboratory. The analytical risk assessment needs to include raw materials, packaging, in-process controls, and product, stability, and cleaning test requirements, specifically noting which methods are compendial.

Adequate quantities of source materials, reference standards, and critical analytical reagents must be estimated and made available for analytical method validation, transfer, and future manufacturing. As the amount of sample source material can impact yield from validation batches, Analytical will work with the Process and Regulatory functions to develop an initial sampling plan that covers the number of samples taken, amount of sample required, tests performed, sample location, and whether the sample should be assessed only during process validation. This initial assessment of the sampling plan allows for planning of adequate analytical resources during qualification.

5.2.4 分析功能 — 解說

核心概念：分析測試地點的決策

分析功能必須決定每一項測試方法在哪裡執行：

  • SU 實驗室（已有資格認定）
  • RU 實驗室（需方法移轉）
  • MAH 中央實驗室
  • 外部合約實驗室

此決策直接影響資本投入與時程安排。

比喻：血液檢驗的分流

就像醫院決定哪些血液項目自行檢驗、哪些外送中心實驗室——常規項目留院做，罕見或高精度項目外包。分析功能的測試地點決策邏輯相同：依設備能力、成本、時效三角平衡。

重點：參考標準品與試劑的前置採購

分析方法驗證、移轉所需的參考標準品（reference standards）及關鍵試劑（critical analytical reagents）必須在規劃階段就估算用量並預排採購。這類物質往往有長交期或特殊儲存需求，拖到執行階段才處理會成為關鍵路徑瓶頸。

取樣計畫六要素

取樣計畫 =
① 取樣數量（number of samples）
② 每次取樣量（amount per sample）
③ 執行測試項目（tests performed）
④ 取樣位置（sample location）
⑤ 是否僅在製程驗證期間評估
⑥ 對驗證批次產率的衝擊評估

5.2.5 Engineering

Working closely with the Process function, Engineering will develop the capital requirements for the chosen RU, which are then documented in a capital appropriation request (CAR). Even before the TT user requirements specifications (URS) or CAR are complete, it may be necessary to decide to order long-lead equipment to meet TT timing requirements. Engineering also should be involved in change control for facility retrofits as well as laboratory modifications.

If the RU is a CMO or CDMO, the responsibilities of the Engineering function may reside at the contract site with strong oversight from the TT Team. The TT PM works closely with Engineering to coordinate the timing for equipment and facility installation, qualification, and training to align with the overall project plan.

5.2.5 工程功能 — 解說

核心概念：CAR 與長交期設備

工程功能的核心任務是將製程需求轉換成資本採購申請（CAR）。關鍵挑戰：

  • 長交期設備（long-lead equipment）往往需要在 URS / CAR 完成前就先下訂單
  • 設備安裝、資格認定（IQ/OQ/PQ）必須與整體專案時程對齊
  • 廠房改建與實驗室改造均需納入變更控制

比喻：裝潢前先訂義大利進口磁磚

裝潢設計圖還沒畫完，但義大利磁磚交期18週——先訂。工程功能面對的是同樣邏輯：凍乾機、隔離器、自動化灌裝設備的交期常達12-18個月，等 URS 完成再訂，移轉時程必然推遲。

CDMO / CMO 特殊考量

若 RU 為外部 CDMO/CMO，工程功能的執行責任可能移交至合約廠，但 TT Team 必須保持強力監督（strong oversight）。TT PM 需特別協調設備安裝、資格認定與培訓的時程，確保與整體計畫同步。

思考練習：您的 TT 有哪些長交期設備？

在您目前或即將進行的技術移轉中，列出三個可能成為時程瓶頸的長交期設備或基礎設施項目，並估算其交期。這些項目是否已納入 CAR 或提前採購規劃？

5.2.6 Manufacturing

In the Planning stage, Manufacturing coordinates with the TT PM to ensure that the project timeline considers all necessary steps for full operational readiness of the RU site. Operational requirements of the technology being transferred should be considered relative to the staff capabilities at the RU, permit requirements, and operational training that will be needed for the new process and modified facility. Manufacturing also will work with the Engineering and Process units on plant design to define URS and CAR requirements.

EHS considerations must be assessed against the RU's procedures and experience so necessary permits can be prepared. The EHS assessment includes protective equipment, product biosafety, aseptic practices, hazardous materials, and potential vent and waste streams. A report on the operational requirements and an EHS assessment report will focus attention on the areas that need to be addressed in the project plan.

5.2.6 製造功能 — 解說

核心概念：操作就緒（Operational Readiness）

製造功能的核心目標是確保 RU 在技術層面就緒之外，操作層面也同步就緒：

  • 員工能力 vs. 新製程/新設備要求的差距分析
  • 環境、健康、安全（EHS）法規許可
  • 操作培訓計畫（新製程 + 改建廠房）
  • URS / CAR 需求定義（與工程、製程協作）

比喻：新機型上線前的機師訓練

航空公司引進新機型時，設備本身再好，沒有受訓合格的機師就無法起飛。製造功能的任務就是確保「人員就緒」與「設備就緒」同步——在執行階段正式開始前完成差距訓練。

EHS 評估不可拖延

EHS 評估範圍涵蓋：個人防護設備（PPE）、產品生物安全、無菌操作規範、危險物質處理、排放廢氣及廢液處理。許可證（permits）申請往往需要數月時間，若規劃階段未啟動，將成為執行階段的阻礙。

5.2.7 Regulatory

The Regulatory function will further define the regulatory strategy in the Planning stage, identifying any items that could impact the Business function's product strategy (see Section 5.1.1). The regulatory strategy should consider the documents that will be needed and include a projected timeline for key milestones such as drafting, review, and approval. These will later be expanded into a detailed submission tracker.

The regulatory strategy should include the regulatory risks, with mitigation options, and the regulatory assumptions identified by both the MAH and the RU. Collaboration between the MAH and RU Regulatory and Quality functions at this stage is important in understanding the risk tolerance and concerns of each, both in general and specific to the product. Establishing clear understandings at this stage can help avoid delays and frustration later in the process.

The Regulatory function communicates the submission requirements to the Process, Analytical, Engineering, and Manufacturing functions, which will perform detailed risk assessments. Based on the level of risk the MAH is comfortable with, Regulatory may be able to define opportunities to reduce complexity and accelerate regulatory submission and approval. Sources of risk that often are considered in the regulatory strategy include:

  • Stability strategy
  • Analytical method transfers
  • Number and timing of validation batches
  • Process changes or improvements
  • Amount of data and activities the RU can leverage from the SU

The MAH Regulatory function may meet with regulatory authorities to review the proposed regulatory strategy to ensure success.

The RU Regulatory function works with the MAH Regulatory unit to obtain current CMC information from the registered dossiers and to evaluate any compendial or compliance standards that have changed. It also is useful to obtain information about any changes implemented across the product's lifecycle to better understand challenges in manufacturing or testing of the product. Additionally, the outputs in the submission (e.g., CQAs and critical process parameters (CPPs)) should be provided to the RU so the RU can understand any critical challenges with the product and determine the inputs necessary to meet those requirements. If the TT is for a new product, the MAH Regulatory function will define the requirements based on the target markets and manufacturing locations.

5.2.7 法規功能 — 解說

核心概念：法規策略從文件變成行動計畫

啟動階段的法規策略是方向性文件；進入規劃階段，法規功能必須將其深化為：

  • 含草稿、審查、批准時程的詳細提交追蹤表
  • MAH 與 RU 雙方的風險耐受度對齊
  • 各職能詳細風險評估的需求輸入

比喻：上市路線圖的精細化

啟動階段畫的是「大陸地圖」，規劃階段要畫「縣市街道圖」——知道每一條路的走法、哪些路段可能塞車（監管機構審查），並決定要走哪條最短路線（加速申請的機會點）。

法規風險五大來源

  • 穩定性策略（stability strategy）
  • 分析方法移轉（analytical method transfers）
  • 驗證批次數量與時程
  • 製程變更或改進
  • RU 可從 SU 借用的數據與活動範圍

實務要點：讓 RU 了解 CQA / CPP

法規功能必須確保提交文件中的 CQA（關鍵品質屬性）與 CPP（關鍵製程參數）資訊傳遞給 RU。RU 了解這些關鍵挑戰，才能確認自身輸入條件能否滿足要求。這是避免執行階段「做了才發現做不到」的關鍵防線。

5.2.8 Quality

The MAH Quality function will work with the RU Process function to document in the QTPP and product specification the requirements that will ensure the quality, safety, and efficacy of the product. In this stage, quality agreements must be developed concurrently and executed, as required, between the MAH and RU, and between the RU or MAH and additional GMP providers and external laboratories.

Based on the target market(s), current stability requirements, and commitments made to regulatory authorities, Quality will work with the Process, Analytical, and Regulatory functions to prepare the stability requirements. Regulatory uses the stability plan to further develop the overall regulatory timeline to ensure that all conditions of the current commitments can be met.

Using the regulatory strategy, Quality also begins GMP audit-readiness activities at the RU to ensure full inspection readiness when needed in the Execution stage. First, the MAH Quality function performs an audit of the chosen RU so that all compliance gaps impacting the TT can be addressed prior to physical implementation of the cross-functional requirements. When significant quality changes are required, a quality improvement plan will be developed; however, the quality assessment performed during the Initiation stage should have identified if a site has a significant compliance gap.

Quality also performs a gap assessment of the SU, RU, and MAH quality systems and negotiates an aligned QMS for the TT. This alignment should be performed even when the SU and RU are both internal sites, as interpretation of the global QMS may differ across regions.

5.2.8 品保功能 — 解說

核心概念：品質協議與 QMS 對齊

品保功能在規劃階段的三大任務：

  • 品質協議：MAH↔RU、RU/MAH↔外部實驗室，須同步執行
  • RU 稽核：找出並在執行前消弭合規缺口
  • QMS 對齊：即使 SU 與 RU 同屬內部，不同地區對全球 QMS 的解讀可能不同

比喻：婚前健康檢查

在正式合作（執行階段）開始前，MAH 對 RU 進行品質稽核，就像婚前健康檢查——提前發現問題，有時間處理，而不是在蜜月旅行（生產驗證）時才發現雙方體質不合。

重點：穩定性計畫是法規時程的骨架

品保功能協助制定的穩定性計畫（stability plan）直接影響法規申請時程——加速穩定性研究可縮短申請等待期，但必須確保所有現有承諾條件均能滿足。穩定性計畫是 Quality、Process、Analytical、Regulatory 四功能協作的輸出。

重點：內部移轉也需 QMS 對齊

許多公司誤以為「同公司內部廠間移轉」不需要正式的 QMS 對齊。PDA TR65 明確指出：即使 SU 與 RU 同屬內部，區域間對全球 QMS 的解讀差異真實存在，必須正式協商並記錄對齊結果。

5.2.9 Sourcing/Supply Chain

Once the decision to perform the TT is made, production planning begins. The Sourcing/Supply Chain function works closely with the TT PM to evaluate the time that will be needed for the TT, based on the time needed for Engineering installation and qualification, the number of engineering batches to be performed, the number of qualification batches needed, and the time needed for stability studies and regulatory approvals. Planned downtime in production, such as for maintenance (reactive or preventive) and cleaning, also should be considered.

During the Planning stage, the target market volume also is used to determine future RU capacity and resource needs for commercial supply. These requirements typically are part of the supply agreement negotiated with the RU during the Planning stage.

During this stage, the sourcing of raw materials, consumables, and packaging components should begin with identification of sources for the items with the longest lead times and sole-sourced items. Any changes to the supply of critical materials identified as part of the TT risk assessment also should be addressed.

The Sourcing/Supply Chain function also evaluates overall product logistics to further develop the product supply chain strategy. At this stage, the product supply chain strategy should show import/export considerations, distribution, and interim points involved with both the product and materials. A detailed supply chain map showing distributors and manufacturers for key components is a key output to identify supply chain risks, consider second sources of critical materials, and facilitate future troubleshooting.

5.2.9 採購 / 供應鏈功能 — 解說

核心概念：生產計畫的四維度評估

供應鏈功能評估 TT 所需時間，必須納入四個維度：

  • 工程安裝與資格認定時間
  • 工程批次數量
  • 確認批次數量
  • 穩定性研究及法規批准時間

計畫性停機（預防保養、清潔）也必須納入，不可忽略。

比喻：餐廳開幕前的食材備料

新餐廳開幕前，廚師要先確認：哪些食材只有一家供應商（sole-source）？哪些需要提前3個月訂購（long-lead）？一旦開幕後才發現關鍵食材斷貨，代價極高。供應鏈功能在規劃階段做的正是這件事——為技術移轉備好「食材」。

供應鏈地圖：預防未來麻煩的地雷圖

規劃階段的關鍵輸出之一是詳細的供應鏈地圖，顯示：

  • 進出口考量（import/export）
  • 配銷商與關鍵組件製造商
  • 二次供應來源（second sources）機會

這張地圖是未來供應鏈風險識別和故障排除的基礎工具。

實務應用：Sole-Source 物料的雙重風險

單一來源（sole-sourced）物料面臨兩重風險：
① 供應中斷風險（廠商停產、品質問題）
② 法規風險（更換供應商需重新驗證、申請變更）
在規劃階段盡早識別這類物料，並評估是否需要開發第二供應來源，是供應鏈韌性的核心工作。

5.3 Knowledge Transfer Stage — Overview

The Knowledge Transfer (KT) stage significantly overlaps with the prior Planning stage and the following Readiness stage. During Planning, key pieces of the KT are needed to finalize the TT charter and perform risk assessments. KT often is still in progress while key activities in the Readiness stage are being developed. Throughout the TT, and especially in the KT stage, any events or changes that occur at the SU that impact the product being transferred require immediate communication to the TT PM. KT can be a highly iterative and challenging process. At the KT stage, all cross-functional roles involved in performing the TT are fully engaged.

The TT agreement, quality agreement, TT charter, and project plan that were completed in the prior stage allow the SU and RU to execute the full transfer of information, systems, data, technical requirements, and practical experience necessary to deliver the CMC and operational requirements to the RU. The TT Team members will work very closely during the KT stage, employing the communication plan and document-sharing platform developed during the Planning stage.

KT extends beyond the direct TT team members. All supporting personnel across the SU and RU need to understand the TT requirements, product and regulatory strategies, and success factors as defined in the TT charter so workloads can be prioritized across the organization and timelines will be met. In addition, supporting personnel across the SU and RU may hold key information and expertise based on experience with the intended equipment, a similar process, or a previous project. This additional expertise can assist in KT.

During the KT stage, a proactive knowledge management (KM) approach can improve KT, create efficiencies between the SU and RU, and facilitate more effective transfer of both explicit (documented) and tacit information held by personnel. The transfer of tacit information may offer the greatest opportunities for improvement, as this type of information often may not be uncovered unless a failure result leads to its discovery. A robust KM approach that includes review of TT performance also can provide more detail that can be used to refine the control strategy after the TT is complete and to improve future transfers. Every opportunity should be taken to leverage KM to preserve, transfer, and increase knowledge about the product and process.

The TT package developed in the KT stage will include all the details needed to reproducibly manufacture, test, and release the product being transferred. During the stage gate at the end of the KT stage, the steering committee ensures that the TT package is complete and assesses the risk factors, with mitigation strategies, that remain after the KT.

中文解說 — KT 階段總覽

知識管理的兩大類型

顯性知識（Explicit Knowledge）：可以文字記錄的內容，例如 SOP、批次記錄、驗證報告、規格書。這類知識容易轉移，用文件包（TT Package）即可傳遞。

隱性知識（Tacit Knowledge）：存在於人腦中的操作經驗，例如「這個設備起動時要先熱機 10 分鐘才穩定」、「過濾時壓力突然升高通常是進料黏度問題而非濾膜阻塞」。這類知識很難靠文件傳遞，必須透過現場觀察、人員訓練、face-to-face 互動才能捕捉。

TR65 特別指出：隱性知識的轉移往往是最有價值但最難執行的部分。

比喻：食譜 vs. 廚藝傳承

想像你要把一道米其林餐廳的招牌菜教給另一家廚房：

  • 食譜 = 顯性知識（SOP、批次記錄）：寫出材料與步驟
  • 主廚親自到場示範 = 隱性知識轉移：「這個醬汁要炒到『聞起來像焦糖』才是對的顏色」
  • 學徒做給主廚看 = 工程批（Engineering Batch）：驗證接收端真的學會了

TT Package 只是食譜。光有食譜而沒有主廚示範，往往做不出一樣的味道。

重點：KT 階段的三大輸出

  • SU Technical Package：送出端提供的完整技術文件包
  • RU Technical Package：接收端消化後建立的自有技術文件
  • Stage Gate Approval：指導委員會確認 TT Package 完整性後的放行

KT 階段結束時，指導委員會（Steering Committee）必須確認 TT package 完整，並評估殘餘風險因子及其緩解策略。

CDMO 現場情境

對於接收產品的 CDMO（RU），KT 階段的核心挑戰是：你不知道你不知道什麼。客戶（SU/MAH）提供的文件包可能遺漏了他們認為「理所當然」的操作細節。

實務建議：在 KT kick-off 之前，CDMO 應先進行內部的 Knowledge Gap Assessment，列出：我們目前缺乏哪些關於這個製程/產品的資訊？再帶著問題清單去跟 SU 溝通，而不是被動接收文件。

5.3.1 Business

With the required contracts and nondisclosure agreements in place, the Business function focuses on refining and executing the resource plan developed in the Planning stage. The Business function monitors progress of the interactions between the SU and RU and removes any contractual roadblocks that may limit KT activities.

5.3.2 Technology Transfer Project Manager

Using the TT agreement developed during the Planning stage, the TT PM, with the TT Team, facilitates KT across key functions in the SU and RU (and third parties, as applicable) to ensure timely and complete knowledge-sharing. The KT is typically documented by the SU in a TT package containing all the information needed to manufacture the product robustly.

The TT PM should be sure to include the sharing of practical experience, or "know-how," which may or may not be documented. To capture tacit knowledge, the TT PM should coordinate on-site interactions between SU and RU personnel, allowing the SU to demonstrate activities specific to the product or process being transferred. This knowledge also should be captured in the KM system being used for the product.

KT is key to a successful handover from the project team. During KT, the TT team should both leverage learnings from previous projects and capture lessons that are learned as part of the current TT.

The TT PM establishes project controls, including lifecycle document management, and now actively manages the project scope, timing, budget, and risk factors according to the TT charter requirements. Regular TT Team meetings are used not only for KT but also for timely execution of the deliverables, identification of roadblocks, and tracking of additional action items that may be specific to the ongoing TT.

5.3.3 Process

If the product is already established commercially at the SU, the SU shares process performance qualification (PPQ) information for the process with the RU Process function. This information will include CPPs and their impact on CQAs, in-process tests, and proven acceptable ranges for the manufacturing process. The SU should share learnings from the implementation of commercial or pilot-scale manufacturing processes, along with process improvements that have been made. To ensure full understanding of the process history, lessons learned, challenges, and early development of the process, the SU and/or MAH also should share with the RU historical performance and development data wherever possible.

For new products, the RU will work with the SU to define the PPQ requirements and ensure their feasibility at the RU facility. These PPQ requirements will include a list of CPPs that are derived from quality risk assessments and laboratory and/or pilot-scale data.

In this stage, the Process function writes the process and cleaning validation master plans (VMP) as key deliverables. The process VMP outlines the PPQ requirements covering:

  • Process description, including control strategy and CPPs
  • Batch size and expected yield
  • Raw material qualification plan
  • Number of PPQ production runs, detailing any matrix or bracketing assumptions
  • Hold-time strategy
  • Rework or reprocess steps allowed
  • Stability strategy
  • Success criteria

Using the risk assessment from the Planning stage, the Process function performs activities needed to define the control strategy, including CPPs that impact CQAs and their allowed ranges, in-process testing, and control systems. When possible, the RU should demonstrate the process in the laboratory to ensure full understanding of the knowledge being transferred from the SU.

5.3.4 Analytical

In the KT stage, the Analytical function at the RU develops the full method-implementation strategy, known as a validation plan, using the sampling plan, actual non-compendial methods used at the SU, and compendial methods for the markets included in the business and regulatory strategies. The analytical validation plan must align with the QTPP and/or specification requirements provided by the SU or documented in the marketing authorization file. When a marketed product is being transferred to a different site, validation may be performed through the analytical method transfer plan.

The Analytical function determines the requirements for reference standards to allow for the ordering of reference standards for method qualification and testing. During the KT stage, potential opportunities for harmonization of analytical methods are identified and an implementation plan for use of these methods is discussed with the Regulatory function. The Analytical function documents the agreed-upon approach to implementation of all analytical TT requirements, including the use of any contract testing sites.

中文解說 — Business / PM / Process / Analytical

Business 功能在 KT 階段的角色

Business 在 KT 階段的核心任務是移除合約障礙（remove contractual roadblocks）。實務上，這包括：確保 NDA/CDA 覆蓋範圍足夠廣、解決知識產權（IP）分歸問題、確保技術文件的使用授權不受限制。

若 TT 是 CDMO 接案，Business 還需確保 Quality Agreement 與 Technical Agreement 中對知識轉移的責任分工已明確定義。

TT PM 在 KT 的核心職責

  • 知識搬運工：確保 SU 的 know-how 完整傳遞給 RU，不只是文件，還包括現場示範
  • 隱性知識捕手：安排 SU/RU 人員面對面互動，記錄到 KM 系統
  • 進度控管者：定期 TT Team 會議追蹤交付物、阻礙、行動事項
  • 過去教訓整合者：引入前次 TT 的 lessons learned，避免重蹈覆轍

Process VMP — 關鍵交付物

製程驗證主計畫（Process VMP）是 KT 階段 Process 功能的核心文件，它規劃了後續 PPQ 的所有要求，是 Readiness 和 Execution 階段的路線圖。

特別注意：Process VMP 中的成功標準（Success Criteria）必須在 Readiness 之前完成定義，這是 stage gate 的必要條件之一。

比喻：分析方法轉移像「廚師的刀工認證」

Analytical 在 KT 階段制定的驗證計畫，就像把 SU 廚師用的切菜技術「認證」給 RU 廚師：

  • 非藥典法（Non-compendial）：SU 自己開發的方法，需要在 RU 做 method transfer（含系統適用性確認）
  • 藥典法（Compendial）：USP/EP 方法，RU 需要確認自己的設備符合要求（method verification）
  • 參考標準品（Reference Standards）：KT 階段就要確認來源與訂購，因為等待時間可能是關鍵路徑

思考練習 — KT 階段

  • 你的公司在接收一個新產品時，是否有系統化的 Knowledge Gap Assessment 流程？還是只是等待 SU 寄文件？
  • 當 SU 的操作人員來 RU 示範時，你們如何確保隱性知識被記錄下來，而不只是「看了就忘」？
  • Process VMP 的 success criteria 是誰在定義？SU、RU、還是共同協商？這對後續 PPQ 爭議有什麼影響？

5.3.5 Engineering

The Engineering function performs a detailed facility-fit assessment in the KT stage. In doing so, the Engineering function uses the knowledge being transferred from the SU to compare the existing (for approved product) or proposed manufacturing process with the RU's capabilities. Process steps can be modified as part of the final facility fit to facilitate execution of the manufacturing process at the RU. However, any process modification should be proven to work prior to the Readiness stage.

Engineering also should design and implement an appropriate waste management system covering all steps from waste identification to disposal. The Engineering function writes the change controls for the facility, often as part of the overall TT change control.

Working closely with the Process, Analytical, Manufacturing, and Quality functions, Engineering will define all facility, equipment, automation, and laboratory requirements and document these in the URS (User Requirements Specification). Single-use components (sometimes known as disposables) also should be part of the URS development. A layout should be used to confirm single-use component design and to ensure all qualification requirements (i.e., extractables/leachables) are identified.

In the KT stage, vendors are identified, engineering drawings are completed, and all required capital equipment is ordered. The timeline for Engineering function activities can be highly influenced by how the URS is defined, which vendors are selected, whether on-site vendor support is used, and how well standard vendor activities can be leveraged for the capital qualification plan. Engineering works with the Quality and Regulatory functions to develop a capital VMP that describes the strategy for qualification or requalification of the facility.

5.3.6 Manufacturing

The RU Manufacturing function works with the Engineering and Process functions to ensure that the RU plant has adequate capability to complete the TT. Manufacturing will review the SU's master batch record, if applicable, and define the operational documentation requirements for the TT (e.g., batch records, SOPs, work instructions, training). Throughout the KT stage, all cross-functional areas should be interacting with Manufacturing to ensure plant design and training result in a process that can be operated in a reproducible manner to make a product of consistent quality.

SU personnel or experts should meet with RU personnel to convey practical experience in making and/or developing the product, including lessons learned from process deviations, if applicable.

In the KT stage, Manufacturing should define personal protective equipment requirements to protect both operator and product so that the Sourcing/Supply Chain function can order the needed equipment.

5.3.7 Regulatory

During the KT stage, the Regulatory function supports the RU's ability to receive the process, refines the regulatory strategy, aligns with validation approaches, continues planning activities, and focuses on building key relationships. KT from the SU or MAH Regulatory unit should include relevant health authority interactions relating to the regulatory process, historical audits, and licensure. Regulatory should communicate project-specific risks to the TT Team to ensure alignment and understanding.

The regulatory strategy developed during the Initiation and Planning stages should be further developed during the KT stage, considering the finalized stability plan. Regulatory also should consider the need for any changes to the primary and secondary labeling.

The revised regulatory strategy also should address the timing and content of planned interactions with regulatory authorities, including meetings, scientific advice, protocols, and/or correspondence. Important considerations for the regulatory strategy include definition of the matrixing approaches for qualification and validation as well as any approach to be taken to prove comparability. The MAH may consider submitting correspondence or requesting a meeting with the regulatory authority if the acceptability of the comparability strategy is uncertain. The MAH also should consider submitting a post-approval change management protocol to gain concurrence on the approach being used for this TT or to potentially reduce the reporting category for future submissions addressed in the protocol.

5.3.8 Quality

At the KT stage, the RU Quality function performs a quality risk assessment to evaluate the ability of the RU's quality systems to make a safe and efficacious product. In conducting this risk assessment, the Quality function will use the knowledge and information that has been shared by the SU. The quality risk assessment will rely on findings from audits, outcomes from the quality improvement plan (if applicable), SU quality metrics, and market actions. For a product already being manufactured under a marketing authorization, the quality risk assessment also can examine deviations and corrective actions at the SU as well as process trends, product complaint history, and annual product reports as available. The Quality function should perform compliance training and address any gaps identified by the quality risk assessment.

Quality also finalizes the stability plan based on the requirements in the QTPP and/or specifications and ensures its alignment with the regulatory strategy for the TT.

The RU Quality function works with the Sourcing/Supply Chain function to identify, schedule, and perform supplier qualification audits for all materials being sourced for the TT. These functions will document the findings and recommendations in a supplier qualification report. They also will define expiry/retest dating requirements for both materials and product.

Quality also plays a key role in assessing the compliance of the analytical validation plan, change controls, project plan, and process, cleaning, and capital VMPs.

5.3.9 Sourcing/Supply Chain

The RU Sourcing/Supply Chain function, with the Process function, defines the RU bill of materials using the material requirements that are shared during knowledge transfer. Key considerations include the SU bill of materials, storage requirements, shipping regulations, regulatory classification, and specifications. Lead time for material delivery is often a critical path item for the project plan.

The RU should consider obtaining samples of materials from suppliers for testing and performing either laboratory runs or a future engineering batch. Storage and handling requirements may also drive capital investment, so the capacity plan developed in the Initiation stage should be reevaluated.

Capacity-planning also impacts the recruitment and training of personnel required to perform the TT. The supply chain map lays out the projected flows from materials-in to product-out, including the testing needed to support production. Therefore, it serves as a key input for resource-planning in the Operations and Quality Affairs/Quality Control units, and other critical functions.

Facility-Fit Assessment — 設施適用性評估

這是 Engineering 在 KT 階段最關鍵的任務：將 SU 的製程要求與 RU 的現有設施能力逐項比對。

評估涵蓋面向：

  • 設備規格：容量、材質、控制系統是否相符
  • 廠房佈局：物料流向、人員動線、潔淨區等級
  • 公用設施：純水、壓縮空氣、HVAC 能否滿足製程需求
  • 一次性系統（SUS）：管路、容器的設計與 E&L 要求
  • 廢棄物管理：從產生到處置的完整系統

重要原則：任何為了 facility-fit 而做的製程修改，必須在 Readiness 階段之前就驗證可行。不能把「不確定這樣改是否有效」的問題帶入 Readiness。

URS — 使用者需求規格書是設備採購的起點

Engineering 在 KT 階段寫 URS，是後續所有設備採購、設計圖、Capital VMP 的基礎文件。URS 的品質直接影響：

  • 報價準確性（CAR/資本申請）
  • 廠商選擇與合約
  • 設備資格確認計畫（IQ/OQ/PQ）的範疇
  • FAT/SAT 的查驗項目

URS 如果定義不清，後續一路都會付出代價：設備不符需求、重做資格確認、延誤時程。

Regulatory：可比性策略（Comparability Strategy）

TT 的核心監管問題是：RU 生產的產品與 SU 的產品是同一個嗎？

Regulatory 在 KT 階段必須定義可比性策略（Comparability Strategy），說明：

  • 用什麼數據證明產品相同（理化、生物、穩定性）
  • 矩陣法（Matrixing）如何應用在驗證批次設計上
  • 是否需要事先與主管機關溝通，取得同意
  • Post-Approval Change Management Protocol 是否適用（可降低後續申報類別）

比喻：Quality 的角色像「獨立驗收員」

在 KT 階段，Quality 不只是負責審查文件，更像一個獨立的品管驗收員：

  • 對 SU 做「品質盡職調查」：偏差記錄、CAPA、市場行動、年度產品報告
  • 對 RU 的供應商做資格稽核：確認關鍵原料的供應商符合品質要求
  • 對所有 VMP 和驗證計畫做符合性審查

Quality 的品質風險評估結果，直接決定了 KT 階段的 stage gate 能否通過。

CDMO 情境：Bill of Materials 是供應鏈的 DNA

Supply Chain 在 KT 階段建立的 BOM（Bill of Materials），是 CDMO 接案後最重要的商業文件之一，因為它決定了：

  • 哪些原料需要客戶指定供應商（Approved Supplier List）
  • 哪些可以 CDMO 自行採購（有議價空間）
  • 哪些原料的交期是專案關鍵路徑（最長前置時間 = 最早需要下單）
  • 是否有需要從 SU 取得樣品用於工程批的物料

BOM 建立不完整，往往是 Readiness 階段材料不到位、延誤 PPQ 的主因之一。

5.4 Readiness Stage — Overview

In the Readiness stage, the TT Team has all documentation, equipment, and analytical methods in place, and the RU site is prepared to safely perform process qualification in a compliant manner. Preliminary KT has occurred, so the RU can finalize the control strategy, qualify the analytical methods, and complete the facility modifications. Activities in this stage often are performed in parallel with the KT stage, which requires an iterative process in document development and approval. Because the risk assessment is a living document, risk identification and risk mitigation strategies should be constantly reviewed throughout the TT project. A practical plant capability assessment should be conducted prior to PPQ. During this assessment, preliminary water or engineering runs may be performed as part of the overall project plan.

As the name implies, Readiness sets the stage for a successful PPQ at the RU. In the stage gate for Readiness, the steering committee determines whether all requirements are in place for batch execution and gives approval to proceed with the engineering batch.

中文解說 — Readiness 階段總覽

Readiness 的本質：「上場前的最後整備」

Readiness 階段的英文字面意義就是「準備好了」。在這個階段，TT 團隊需要完成的任務是：確保 RU 在進入 Execution（PPQ）之前，所有條件都已備齊且經過確認。

三大備齊要素：

  • 文件備齊：所有 SOP、批記、驗證協議都已撰寫並核准
  • 設備備齊：所有設備已安裝且完成資格確認（IQ/OQ/PQ）
  • 人員備齊：所有操作人員已完成訓練，記錄在案

任何一項未完成，都不得進入 Execution 階段。

Stage Gate — Readiness 關卡的意義

Readiness 的 Stage Gate 是由指導委員會（Steering Committee）主持的正式審查。這不是一個形式性的行政蓋章，而是一個技術與商業雙重審查：

  • 技術面：所有驗證文件是否準備好？設備是否已完成資格確認？
  • 商業面：資源、預算、時程是否仍在 TT charter 的範圍內？
  • 風險面：殘餘風險是否已有緩解計畫？

TR65 Section 8.11 的案例研究特別強調：跳過 Stage Gate 直接進入 Execution，是 TT 失敗最常見的原因之一。

比喻：開刀前的手術室核對清單

Readiness Stage Gate 就像外科手術前的 WHO Surgical Safety Checklist：

  • 「病人確認了嗎？」= 產品規格確認了嗎？
  • 「器械都消毒完成了嗎？」= 設備資格確認通過了嗎？
  • 「所有人員都就位了嗎？」= 人員訓練完成了嗎？
  • 「麻醉師確認可以開始了嗎？」= Steering Committee 批准了嗎？

這個清單的存在，不是為了懷疑手術團隊的能力，而是系統性防止遺漏。

5.4.1 Business

At this point of the TT, the RU Business function should be focused on completing all resourcing requirements so personnel can be trained in preparation for PPQ. The Business function also actively monitors the TT Team's ability to achieve the business objectives defined in the TT charter, including budget, launch date, and projected volume.

As the MAH Business function actively engages customers for the product being transferred, it must effectively communicate any changes to the business strategy when they occur. This allows the TT PM to assess the impact of the changes to the overall project assumptions and risk.

5.4.2 Technology Transfer Project Manager

During the Readiness stage, the TT PM monitors all workstreams and team members executing the requirements for plant readiness documented in the project plan. With many critical activities coming together, in order to proceed to the next stage the TT PM's thorough execution and coordination of the communication plan is critical in monitoring progress, challenges, and obstacles. The TT PM must continually evaluate the impact of changes on the TT charter, facilitate decision-making, and actively manage risk.

The TT PM also documents the PPQ requirements governing the TT in a TT protocol, summarizing the process and cleaning VMPs, the analytical validation plan, and the stability and sampling plans. If multiple TTs are taking place across the supply chain for drug substance production, drug product manufacture, and packaging and labeling, the project plan may include details of the product value stream.

5.4.3 Process

The Process function in the Readiness stage focuses on having all data necessary to finalize the product control strategy. The control strategy defines the criteria to ensure a robust and repeatable process for making a product that meets the QTPP and includes hold points, in-process sampling, CPPs, process ranges, and critical unit operations. Close collaboration with Process, Engineering, Analytical, and Manufacturing is needed to ensure deployment of an effective control strategy.

In accordance with ICH Q10, the classification of process parameters and their ranges typically is determined by the results of laboratory batches and the increasing knowledge gained during the clinical phases of development. For commercial products, the RU can leverage a control strategy already in use by the SU by using its historical data and practical experience. For new products, process characterization studies at laboratory- or pilot-manufacturing-scale will provide understanding of the design space and the edges of failure for product quality and process efficiency.

Final engineering batches that demonstrate the control strategy should be performed prior to PPQ. To complete the Readiness stage, the entire process should be tested to demonstrate operational readiness before moving into PPQ. Such readiness includes any final modifications, document or procedural readiness, and third-party resources. The TT Team should avoid introducing any new conditions or variables to the process once the Readiness stage has closed.

In the Readiness stage, Process writes the process validation protocols capturing all process parameters, the sampling plan, and the analytical testing strategy. Shipping and cleaning validation protocols also are written and approved during this stage.

5.4.4 Analytical

The RU Analytical function executes the validation plan developed during the Planning stage. In Readiness, all method validation and/or transfer protocols covering raw materials, product, packaging, cleaning, microbiological testing, and in-process tests are written, approved, and executed. Compendial methods should be performed at the RU to verify suitability. The Analytical function also writes the stability protocols prior to the Qualification stage. New analytical equipment will need to be installed and qualified before the method validation activities begin, and analytical personnel will need to be trained.

In the Readiness stage, the Analytical function also supports the Process function by analyzing required samples from laboratory- or pilot-scale batches.

TT Protocol — 整合型驗證計畫書

TT PM 在 Readiness 階段建立的 TT Protocol 是一份整合性文件，把各功能分頭寫的計畫匯整成一份：

  • Process VMP → PPQ 的執行要求
  • Cleaning VMP → 清潔驗證的執行要求
  • Analytical Validation Plan → 方法執行清單
  • Stability Plan → 穩定性採樣時間點
  • Sampling Plan → 各批次採樣位置與頻率

TT Protocol 是 Steering Committee 在 Readiness Stage Gate 審查的核心文件之一。

Control Strategy 終版：Readiness 的核心任務

Control Strategy（管制策略）在 Readiness 階段必須定版（Finalized）。它定義了：

  • Hold Points：哪些步驟需要暫停等待檢測結果？
  • In-Process Sampling：在製程哪個位置、用什麼方法、取多少樣品？
  • CPPs 與 Ranges：每個關鍵製程參數的可接受範圍
  • Critical Unit Operations：哪些步驟被認定為關鍵操作？

黃金法則：一旦 Readiness 階段關閉（Stage Gate 通過），就不能再對製程引入新的條件或變數。PPQ 批次必須在「鎖定」的控制策略下執行。

製程驗證協議的必要元素（PV Protocol Checklist）

Process Validation Protocol 必須包含：
□ 製程描述（Process Description）
□ 製程流程圖（Process Flow Diagram）
□ 批次大小與預期收率（Batch Size & Expected Yield）
□ 所有 CPPs 及其可接受範圍（PAR/NOR）
□ 採樣計畫（Sampling Plan）——位置、時點、頻率
□ 分析測試清單（Analytical Testing Strategy）
□ Hold Points 定義
□ 成功標準（Acceptance Criteria）——需預先設定
□ 偏差處理規則（Deviation Handling Plan）
□ 數據蒐集與完整性要求（Data Integrity Plan）
□ 統計方法（如適用）
□ 核准人員清單（Approval Matrix）

Analytical — Readiness 的關鍵：方法必須已執行

注意 KT 與 Readiness 的差別：

  • KT 階段：制定驗證計畫（Plan）
  • Readiness 階段：執行並完成驗證（Execute & Complete）

在進入 Execution（PPQ）之前，所有的分析方法必須是已驗證（Validated）或已轉移（Transferred）的狀態。使用未驗證方法的 PPQ 批次，其放行數據的法規合規性將受到質疑。

此外，穩定性協議（Stability Protocols）也必須在 Readiness 完成，因為 PPQ 第一批就要啟動穩定性放置。

  • 你的 TT Protocol 是由誰負責撰寫？TT PM 還是 Process？兩者如何分工？
  • 如果 Analytical 的某個方法轉移在 Readiness 結束前未能完成，TT PM 應如何評估是否影響 Stage Gate 通過？
  • Engineering Batch 與 PPQ Batch 的差別是什麼？Engineering Batch 可以作為商業批次放行嗎？

5.4.5 Engineering

In the Readiness stage, the Engineering function installs and qualifies all facility, utility, process, and analytical equipment, including control systems. Engineering works with vendors to facilitate FATs (Factory Acceptance Tests) at the vendor sites to confirm compliance with specifications derived from the URS, and with subsequent SATs (Site Acceptance Tests) upon delivery to the RU. The capital VMP often leverages the FATs and/or SATs to reduce the number of qualification protocol deviations or to use FAT/SAT data to meet initial qualification requirements.

While some developmental studies and testing may begin with equipment or at facilities that have not completed qualification, qualification of all new equipment and facility modifications must be complete and must pass all requirements prior to proceeding to the Execution stage.

The Engineering function writes and executes all qualification protocols for facility and utility modifications, as they must be completed prior to performing PPQ batches. In addition, Engineering will provide training on specific unit operations and facility and/or utility modifications for maintenance, manufacturing, and technical staff. Whenever possible, SU and vendor personnel should participate in this training.

By the end of the Readiness stage and using input from Manufacturing, Engineering incorporates a preventive maintenance plan and spare parts requirements into the RU systems.

5.4.6 Manufacturing

The Manufacturing function writes all SOPs, work instructions, and batch records required for the RU to execute the process. These documents are used to train all personnel involved in the TT at the RU and are used as part of the EHS risk assessment, which is completed during this stage.

Manufacturing cleans the plant facilities and equipment following the Engineering qualification activities, supports the TT Team during execution of various Readiness activities, and conducts testing to determine whether the plant is ready for use. Manufacturing also should finalize documentation and training requirements prior to proceeding to the next stage to ensure the Manufacturing function is fully ready to support execution of the PPQ.

5.4.7 Regulatory

During the Readiness stage, the Regulatory function begins to obtain and author documents necessary for the regulatory submission. Some common activities include:

  • Assessing any new information that may influence the regulatory filing, including presubmission advice, as well as feedback from the health authority on submission of a post-approval change management protocol
  • Assessing change controls
  • Assessing data from any preliminary engineering runs to provide input to the Process function in finalizing the comparability strategy
  • Finalizing the regulatory strategy based on any new information discovered
  • Finalizing the scope of work for the authoring and review of the regulatory submission, which includes:
    • Creating and/or finalizing a detailed submission tracker with a matrix denoting who will be responsible, accountable, consulted, and informed (RACI) for providing materials, as well as the projected timing
    • Confirming the availability of report authors and report and submission reviewers
    • Ensuring the submission tracker timeline aligns with the availability of the source information
  • Reviewing validation documents supporting PPQ
  • Participating in development of PAI (Pre-Approval Inspection) readiness plan

The Regulatory and Quality functions will discuss the expected timing of the completion of validation documents and stability data in support of the TT. Regulatory will also begin to finalize the product labels during the Readiness stage.

5.4.8 Quality

During the Readiness stage, the Quality function is extensively involved with all cross-functional areas, performing compliance reviews and approving documentation involved in preparing for PPQ, including quality elements of the control strategy. Quality ensures closure of all deviations, CAPAs, and change controls prior to PPQ. The Quality function should develop a procedure for PPQ batch release with input from the SU. Collaborating with the SU on the PPQ batch-release procedure ensures alignment on the data to be reviewed and ensures clarity for any unique aspects associated with the TT process and RU site, such as use tests or electronic batch records.

In this stage, the Quality function also should establish role requirements for product release to determine if a qualified person or a responsible person with a specific knowledge base will be needed.

Working with Regulatory, the Quality function also develops a PAI readiness plan, which may be revised as additional relevant information becomes available during the Execution stage. This should include a strategy to respond to any observations found during mock inspections.

The Quality function will qualify new vendors and audit high-risk and critical material suppliers in preparation for PPQ.

5.4.9 Sourcing/Supply Chain

The Sourcing/Supply Chain function orders all materials needed for the TT that have not yet been purchased. Materials, storage, and testing requirements are set up in the RU systems to ensure release by Quality for use when needed. Equipment and material needs should align with the overall project plan and the lead times and risks established during the Planning stage.

中文解說 — Engineering 至 Supply Chain (Readiness)

FAT / SAT / IQ / OQ / PQ 的關係

設備資格確認的完整流程：

  • FAT（工廠驗收測試）：在廠商工廠進行，確認設備符合 URS 規格，在出廠前確認問題
  • SAT（場址驗收測試）：設備運抵 RU 後，確認運輸和安裝未損壞，基本功能正常
  • IQ（安裝資格確認）：確認設備按照設計和製造商規格正確安裝
  • OQ（操作資格確認）：確認設備在規定操作範圍內正常運行
  • PQ（性能資格確認）：確認設備在實際生產條件下持續符合要求

Capital VMP 的技巧：把 FAT/SAT 的測試數據直接作為 IQ/OQ 的依據，可以減少 Readiness 階段的文件工作量，加快資格確認進度。

Engineering 的兩個「最後動作」

在 Readiness 階段結束前，Engineering 必須完成兩件事，之後才能離開「主角舞台」轉為支援角色：

  1. 預防性保養計畫（Preventive Maintenance Plan）：設備上線後如何維護？每隔多久要做什麼保養？由 Manufacturing 執行，但 Engineering 設計。
  2. 備件清單（Spare Parts Requirements）：哪些零件是關鍵備件，應常備？交期多長的零件需要先採購？

這兩件事如果遺漏，在商業生產階段往往會因設備故障或停機而付出高昂代價。

Quality 的 Readiness 核查清單

Quality 在 Readiness 階段承擔「最終守門員」角色，進入 PPQ 前必須確認：

  • 所有偏差（Deviation）已關閉
  • 所有 CAPA 已完成並有效性確認
  • 所有變更管制（Change Controls）已關閉
  • PPQ 批次放行程序（Batch Release Procedure）已建立並與 SU 對齊
  • 合格人員（QP/RP）的角色已定義
  • PAI 準備計畫已制定
  • 高風險供應商稽核已完成

重點：帶著未關閉的偏差或 CAPA 進入 PPQ，是監管機關在 PAI 中最容易發現的缺陷之一。

比喻：Regulatory 的 Submission Tracker 像「出版編輯時間表」

Submission Tracker 是 Regulatory 在 Readiness 階段建立的申報文件管理工具，其邏輯就像書籍出版的編輯時間表：

  • 每份文件有負責撰寫的作者（Author）
  • 有審查者（Reviewer）和核准者（Approver）
  • 有各節點的截止日期
  • 有 RACI 矩陣標示責任

所有時間點都要與「數據何時能取得」的時間軸對齊——若穩定性數據預計 T+6 個月才有，申報文件的撰寫時程就不能排在 T+4。

CDMO 情境：PAI 準備的提前量

對於在 Readiness 階段已開始規劃 PAI（Pre-Approval Inspection）準備的 CDMO，有幾個實務重點：

  • PAI 的時間是由監管機關決定的，CDMO 應假設可能在 PPQ 批次完成後 6-12 個月內到廠
  • Mock Inspection（模擬查廠）應在 PAI 前執行，找出並在正式查廠前改善缺口
  • Electronic Batch Records、Use Tests 等 RU 特有的系統，需要提前向 SU/MAH 說明，確保雙方的放行程序（Release Procedure）是一致的
  • PAI Readiness Plan 應該是一份活文件，隨 Execution 階段的數據不斷更新

思考練習 — Readiness 後半段

  • 你們公司的 Quality 在 PPQ 前的 CAPA 關閉要求是什麼？有無「重要程度分級」——高風險 CAPA 必須全部關閉，低風險允許有計畫地關閉？
  • 如果一台關鍵設備的 OQ 在 Stage Gate 前一週才能完成，你會如何與 Steering Committee 溝通這個風險？
  • Supply Chain 的材料交期已知是關鍵路徑，如果某個原料供應商突然通知延誤 4 週，你的應急計畫是什麼？

Best Practice：三個「早確認、留緩衝」原則

Readiness Best Practices 反覆強調一個模式：

  1. 早確認交期（材料/設備）：Lead time 長的項目，在 Planning 階段就要下單，不能等到 Readiness 才開始問廠商

  3. 定期查核（不要假設廠商會自動告訴你問題）：Supply chain 風險中最常見的是廠商延誤了幾週才告知 CDMO，這時候已沒有足夠時間應對

Section Summary — Key Points

Knowledge Transfer Stage (5.3)

  • KT overlaps with both the Planning stage (before) and Readiness stage (after); it is highly iterative
  • A proactive Knowledge Management (KM) approach is essential for transferring both explicit (documented) and tacit (experiential) knowledge
  • The TT Package must contain all information needed to reproducibly manufacture, test, and release the product
  • All 9 cross-functional areas are fully engaged: Business, TT PM, Process, Analytical, Engineering, Manufacturing, Regulatory, Quality, and Sourcing/Supply Chain
  • Key deliverables include: SU Technical Package, RU Technical Package, Process VMP, Cleaning VMP, Analytical Validation Plan, QRM Report, URS, Bill of Materials, Regulatory Strategy
  • The KT stage gate requires the steering committee to confirm completeness of the TT package and assess remaining risk factors
  • Best practices emphasize: well-organized TT package, face-to-face interaction for tacit knowledge, historical data sharing, comparability assessment, QbD approach, and risk-based gap identification

Readiness Stage (5.4)

  • In Readiness, all documentation, equipment, and analytical methods must be in place before PPQ can begin
  • The TT PM creates the TT Protocol — an integrated document summarizing all VMPs, validation plans, and sampling plans
  • The Control Strategy must be finalized during Readiness; no new variables may be introduced once Readiness closes
  • Engineering must complete all IQ/OQ/PQ for new equipment and facility modifications before Execution
  • Analytical must complete all method validations and transfers during Readiness
  • Quality must ensure closure of all deviations, CAPAs, and change controls prior to PPQ
  • Regulatory develops the Submission Tracker and PAI Readiness Plan during this stage
  • Best practices: risk register, readiness checklist, adequate document approval timelines, early lead time identification with sufficient buffer, regular vendor check-ins

5.5 Execution Stage

PPQ is performed in the Execution stage, during which all operational readiness and compliance requirements are completed to allow for manufacture at the RU. Materials are tested and released to perform the engineering batch and PPQ batches, as defined in the project plan and process VMP. With multiple execution activities taking place in this stage, the team may decide to perform several stage gates, rather than just one, to continually assess the results from Execution stage activities (see Section 5.5.1).

Table 5.5-1🔍 shows the activities and deliverables for the Execution stage, as excerpted from the Reference Guide (Figure 5.0-1). One major deliverable of the Execution stage is the submission of the regulatory filing. After the PPQ batches are complete, the TT Team usually will provide support for both the regulatory filing and the expected regulatory inspections.

5.5.1

Business — 商業職能

Launch strategy, stage gates, and steering committee approval

5.5.1 Business

The MAH Business function is actively engaged in defining the launch strategy, anticipating completion of PPQ batches, regulatory submission, and PAI. Business will work closely with the Sourcing/Supply Chain function to define the use of the PPQ batches and estimated market demand.

For the Execution stage, multiple stage gates may be used. A stage gate may be performed prior to the engineering run to verify readiness. Another may be conducted before PPQ to verify results of all engineering and process readiness activities. Stage gates also may be executed after the engineering batch and PPQ if any changes to the process occur, risks are identified that must be mitigated, or additional studies are required. After the steering committee approves the regulatory filing, the Business function can proceed to the Handover stage.

商業部門的執行階段角色

商業部門在本階段的核心任務是確保 上市策略（Launch Strategy） 與 PPQ 進度、法規提交時程同步對齊。與供應鏈部門協同，確認 PPQ 批次用途（臨床供應 vs. 商業用途）及預估市場需求量。

5.5.2

Technology Transfer Project Manager

Operational readiness, compliance, person-in-plant program, TT report

5.5.2 Technology Transfer Project Manager

During the Execution stage, the TT PM supports the team to remove any remaining obstacles for the engineering and PPQ batches. The TT PM ensures that all documentation is approved and complies with all operational readiness requirements. All relevant change controls and deviations should be closed, and training should be completed.

As part of Execution, the TT PM ensures that all compliance and operational requirements have been met to perform PPQ with the highest probability of success. Continual communication across all organizational levels is essential. The TT PM also guides the SU and RU teams to define and execute a person-in-plant program in which SU personnel are present to support RU execution activities and associated training.

A key deliverable for the TT PM in the Execution stage is approval of the TT report documenting completion of the TT requirements defined in the TT protocol.

TT PM 執行階段三大任務

  • 清除障礙：確保所有文件核准、變更控制關閉、培訓完成
  • 駐廠計畫（Person-in-Plant Program）：安排 SU 人員駐在 RU 現場，提供技術支援與培訓
  • TT 報告：核准技術轉移報告，記錄 TT 協議中所有要求已完成

SU 技術人員駐在 RU 現場，就像球隊教練坐在替補席——不是親自上場踢球，但隨時提供指導、即時解決技術問題，大幅降低執行風險。

5.5.3

Process — 製程職能

PPQ oversight, deviation investigation, validation reports

5.5.3 Process

The RU Process function oversees qualification exercises including shipping validation and PPQ runs. Their responsibilities include investigation of process-related deviations, execution of supporting studies as defined in the process VMP, and troubleshooting. During this stage, the Process function will provide process expertise and technical support for equipment to ensure the process is performing according to planned expectations, evaluating the effectiveness of the control strategy. Throughout the PPQ, having at least one Process staff person present for each shift of operation is recommended. Prior to starting the PPQ, the Process function ensures that the knowledge gained during the Readiness stage is incorporated clearly into the production batch records, procedures, and personnel training.

During this stage, the SU and RU Process functions should analyze data obtained during PPQ to confirm that the outcomes meet expectations. Any significant differences, as defined in the acceptance criteria and plan, should be investigated.

If possible, investigations of process deviations should include members of both the SU and RU across multiple functional areas. For example, Analytical may be needed to determine whether a deviation of an in-process sample result is due to analytical performance or an issue relating to the Manufacturing function. Where genuine process failures are identified, Process and Quality should consult with Regulatory to evaluate the impact of the failure on the status of the PPQ, including parameters under which the PPQ can proceed. The TT PM should immediately communicate any failures to the steering committee.

Any necessary improvements to the process following the Execution stage should be identified before the handover to the RU post-transfer operations team.

After completion of PPQ, the Process function closes all deviations, gathers required data, and issues the process, shipping, and cleaning validation reports. Process also may be a lead author of the CMC sections of the regulatory filing.

製程職能在執行階段的核心責任

  • 監督 PPQ 及出貨驗證執行
  • 每班至少一名製程人員在場（建議）
  • SU × RU 跨職能偏差調查

Analytical — 分析職能

5.5.4 Analytical

In the Execution stage, the Analytical function is in full operation, performing testing on all materials for release, in-process samples, finished products, and cleaning samples. Utilities and environmental testing may be required as well. Typically, PPQ requires more testing than is necessary during routine commercial production where a reduced testing program may be implemented provided all PPQ requirements defined in the VMPs have been met.

The Analytical function also has oversight of the packaging of stability samples as defined in the stability protocol.

5.5.5

Engineering — 工程職能

Supporting role, deviation investigation, validation sampling

5.5.5 Engineering

Having completed all qualification requirements for the facility and equipment, the Engineering function acts in a supporting role in addressing any issues that occur during the engineering and PPQ runs. Engineering will investigate deviations and issue change controls related to the equipment or facilities. Engineering may support validation-related sampling, data analysis, and reporting to satisfy all requirements of the PPQ and VMP and to ensure all requirements of the PPQ have been met.

中文解析

工程職能角色轉換

進入執行階段，工程部門從前期的「主導設備確認」轉為支援角色——重點是處理 PPQ 過程中設備/廠房相關的偏差、變更控制，並協助驗證取樣與數據分析。

5.5.6

Manufacturing — 製造職能

Engineering batch and PPQ batch execution, lessons learned

5.5.6 Manufacturing

A major deliverable of the Execution stage for the Manufacturing function is execution of the engineering and PPQ batches. At a minimum, the SU team should participate with the RU team during the engineering batch if possible. Knowledge gained from the engineering batch commonly leads to process adjustments and improvements, prompting updates to SOPs and the master batch record. The engineering run should use materials from the suppliers that will be used for PPQ.

The engineering batch may be produced under GMP or non-GMP conditions. If conducted under GMP requirements, the engineering batch may be used to produce product for continued clinical supply. If allowed by both the process VMP and the PPQ protocol, both of which have been approved in advance, an engineering batch run under GMP conditions also may serve as a PPQ batch, particularly when the raw material supply is limited.

The Manufacturing function is a major contributor of feedback on operations, training effectiveness, and batch records following completion of the qualification activities. This feedback should be incorporated into lessons learned prior to handover to the RU post-transfer operations team.

中文解析

工程批的雙重功能

工程批（Engineering Batch）在特定條件下可兼具兩種用途：

  • 臨床供應：GMP 條件下執行，產品可供臨床使用
  • PPQ 批次：若 VMP 及 PPQ 協議事先核准，可同時計入 PPQ 批次（原料供應受限時特別有用）

SU 參與工程批的重要性

建議 SU 團隊在工程批期間到場參與，確保知識在 SU → RU 之間有效轉移，並在正式 PPQ 前即時修正 SOP 與批次記錄。

5.5.7

Regulatory — 法規職能

Regulatory submission, PAI preparation, cross-functional filing review

5.5.7 Regulatory

During the Execution stage, the Regulatory function is actively drafting the regulatory submission and, based on the submission tracker, actively managing the authoring, reviewing, and finalizing of documents. The submission timing and content should be aligned with the regulatory strategy that was finalized in the Readiness stage. The RU should provide frequent updates to the MAH Regulatory function to ensure that new and evolving information discovered during Execution can be incorporated into the submission. The RU should inform the MAH Regulatory function of the assumptions being made and of their evaluation of regulatory risks.

At the end of the Execution stage, after ensuring that the CMC content has been extensively reviewed cross-functionally, the steering committee will authorize filing of the submission. The steering committee should be apprised of risks and assumptions before authorizing filing.

The Regulatory function also works with Quality on PAI preparation. This may include suggesting high-risk items to include in storyboard development as well as ensuring alignment of the key claims made in the dossier, plant-level documentation, and storyboards. High-risk items and topics likely to arise during PAI include activities associated with deviations during PPQ and subjects that the inspectorate has historically identified as focus areas for the site, manufacturing process, product class, or company.

5.5.8

Quality — 品質職能

Material release, batch disposition, PAI readiness, mock inspection

5.5.8 Quality

The Quality function is integral throughout the Execution stage as it releases materials for use. Quality is responsible for product release, including batch record review and overall batch disposition, in accordance with site quality procedures. Quality also is responsible for supporting the review and approval of all documentation for the PPQ, including protocols, reports, batch records, forms, results, change controls, and deviation and investigation close-outs. The Quality function would be expected to close out the quality risk assessment associated with the TT at the end of the PPQ.

At this point in the TT, Quality performs the final inspection readiness activities in anticipation of the regulatory filing which, depending on TT type, may trigger a PAI. A mock inspection (in-depth audit) of the RU performed in anticipation of a PAI may be used to identify issues that should be addressed prior to filing a submission.

Completion of the PAI may be an endpoint to the Execution stage depending on the overall business and regulatory strategies.

Sourcing / Supply Chain — 採購與供應鏈

5.5.9 Sourcing/Supply Chain

The Sourcing/Supply Chain function also will work with the Manufacturing and Engineering functions on PPQ requirements, including scheduling, material, and storage needs as well as logistics. As outputs of the Execution stage, Sourcing/Supply Chain will have established suppliers, a clear supply chain, and defined standards for production planning in order to move forward to commercial manufacture.

Handover Stage — 交接階段

Transition from TT Team to RU post-transfer operations team | p46–p50

5.6 Handover Stage

The Handover stage comprises the transition of the product from the TT Team to the RU post-transfer operations team, covering routine production of marketed products. Because the risk assessment is a living document, risk identification and risk mitigation strategies should be constantly reviewed throughout the TT project. The Handover stage includes multiple activities across several functions at the RU site to ensure it is ready to work independently for commercial production or for the next stage of product development. Requirements for a smooth transition will vary depending on the type of TT and the endpoints established in the TT charter. For instance, the extent of Sourcing/Supply Chain requirements will vary based on how quickly the Business function is able to establish the customer base and volume requirements.

Ensuring that all lessons learned from the TT are incorporated into the existing documentation for improved commercial production is a key element of the Handover stage. (This technical report does not address such standard post-transfer requirements as equipment/facility maintenance, APR, lifecycle validation, complaint handling, or CPV programs.)

5.6.6 Manufacturing

Section Summary — 本節重點總結

Execution & Handover Stages key takeaways

Key Takeaways: Execution & Handover Stages

Execution Stage (5.5): The Execution stage is where all readiness work is tested in practice through engineering and PPQ batches. Success requires cross-functional collaboration, adequate training, proper documentation, and continual communication. Major outputs include the process validation report, TT report, and regulatory filing submission.

Handover Stage (5.6): The Handover stage formalizes the transition from the TT Team to the RU's routine operations team. Effective handover requires comprehensive knowledge transfer, a lessons-learned meeting, formal training plans, and verification that all commercial production capabilities are in place — from supply chain to quality management systems.

Critical success factors across both stages:

  • SME presence during qualification activities
  • Hands-on operator training (not just read-and-sign)
  • Stage gates with cross-functional alignment before proceeding
  • Proactive risk management and lessons-learned documentation
  • Commercial readiness verified before project closure

8.1

Technology Transfer Charter, Governance, and Communication Plan

TT章程、治理架構與溝通計畫

Commercial-Commercial Stage: Planning Internal-External / External-External

To reduce costs, Company A made a business decision to consolidate outsourcing of drug product packaging, expecting to achieve savings quickly. Company A awarded a multiproduct bundle to a contract manufacturing organization (CMO), Company B, transferring packaging for 15 drug products from other contract providers. These products were new to the facility of Company B, the receiving unit (RU). This external-external TT was performed over a two-year period. The TT goal was to complete the transfer of the first product in seven months, with a new product in place each month thereafter, requiring all TTs to occur in parallel.

As Company B worked to achieve the TT goals, Company A continuously changed knowledge requirements, including labels, component design, preferred vendors and suppliers, and the qualification of backup sources. In addition, Company A asked the TT team to change the planned order of products being transferred. These changes prevented Company B's TT Team from meeting project timelines, a situation Company A escalated to senior management in both companies.

With senior leadership from both companies involved, a formal process for the TT was implemented. This process included steering committee and governance oversight, a communication plan, additional staffing, and improved TT project manager (PM) tools to allow greater visibility of the project's progress and risks. The changes specifically included the following:

  • Additional resources were provided to enable key functions to focus on their specific workstream for a cluster of products based on process similarities and timeline.
  • Following an all-day workshop, workstream meetings were held weekly to allow each function from both Company A and Company B to maintain alignment of their activities across the entire portfolio.
  • The TT PM implemented a weekly progress meeting across workstreams during which the project timeline, needs, and risks were discussed and reported to Company A. The use of improved visual tools for these updates allowed for better alignment with project goals.
  • A steering committee of one-level-higher management from both companies was formed. A weekly steering committee meeting allowed timely feedback, risk management, and escalation support from both Company A and Company B.
  • Using input from the steering committee, the senior management of both companies met monthly and held face-to-face meetings on a quarterly basis throughout the project to provide overall governance.

Company B learned the value of establishing a TT charter and of ensuring that Planning stage activities were completed before moving to the next stage. After implementing the governance structure and a communication plan, Company B encountered significantly fewer change requests from Company A, allowing the TT Team to complete knowledge transfer (KT).

Two events jeopardized the TT timeline, but the new timeline risks were quickly communicated to the steering committee for risk mitigation and the information relayed to the governance team. First, a vendor delay was resolved by negotiating a payment to the vendor to expedite the material. Second, a competing priority arose with another commercial activity using shared equipment. With the involvement of the steering committee, the TT Team was able to reach alignment on managing changes to the project scope and ensure this project received priority in order to meet launch needs.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：治理架構是TT的護欄

本案例的核心問題是：委託方（Company A）不斷改變需求，而接收方（Company B）缺乏正式機制來管控這些變更的影響。治理架構的缺失讓TT陷入被動應變的循環。

解決方案的三個層次：

  • 操作層：工作流每週會議 + 視覺化追蹤工具
  • 管理層：跨職能每週進度會議 + TT PM協調
  • 決策層：指導委員會每週會議 + 高階管理月會 + 季度面對面

比喻：建案與甲方管理

就像建商承接15棟同時施工的房子，甲方卻不斷改圖。沒有正式的變更管理流程，工人只能跑來跑去應付臨時指令，最後什麼都蓋不完。TT Charter就是「施工合約」——明確定義什麼可以變、如何變、誰來批准。

成功/失敗的關鍵轉折點

失敗前：無TT章程、無治理結構、委託方隨意更改需求 → 無法達成時程

成功後：建立三層治理架構、制定溝通計畫 → 變更請求大幅減少、KT得以完成

兩個風險事件（供應商延遲、共用設備衝突）因有指導委員會快速介入而化解，說明治理架構在危機管理中的實際價值。

CDMO承接端應用

在TT啟動（Initiation）階段，要求委託方共同簽署TT Charter，明確：

  • 變更管理流程：任何知識需求變更必須經過正式評估與批准
  • 指導委員會的組成與會議頻率
  • 溝通計畫：誰對誰報告、多久一次、用什麼工具
  • 範圍鎖定（Scope Freeze）的里程碑節點

承接端關鍵學習

不要在沒有TT章程的情況下承接多產品組合移轉。「先做再說」的代價是：無止境的變更請求吞噬資源，最終誰都無法達標。將治理架構的建立視為TT的第一個可交付成果。

8.2

Selecting the Right Team to Ensure Success of the Technology Transfer Project

選擇正確的團隊以確保技術移轉成功

Development-Commercial Stages: Initiation, Planning Internal-External

Company A decided to transfer a new sterile drug product (Product X) to a CMO (Company B). Company A's limited TT experience and limited internal expertise to address the cross-functional requirements, comprising only two executive company leaders from the Quality and Supply functions, had caused problems with a previous TT.

Company B assigned dedicated personnel from cross-functional disciplines including Project Management, Account Management, Quality, Validation, Process Engineering, Planning, and Analytical. Company A realized it was understaffed for this new project and could not support these functions adequately. In the early stages of the project, Company A relied heavily on Company B's expertise to manage the project and make all decisions.

After experiencing challenges due to its understaffing in the early stages of the TT of Product X, Company A took action during the Readiness stage. With the experience gained from both in-house and external manufacturing, Company A added staff to its TT Team. It added expertise from key functions that would be required to support the new process, with a TT Team at both the marketing authorization holder (MAH) and the CMO.

As a result of Company A restructuring its team with a subject matter expert (SME) from each key function, matching each function of Company B, the project was executed more efficiently, knowledge was transferred more effectively, and communications were improved. With the enhanced internal involvement, Company A's executive team was able to provide much-needed governance and empower its TT Team to fully manage the project efficiently.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：MAH與CMO的功能對稱原則

TT成功的人員配置原則：委託方（SU/MAH）每個職能，都應有接收方（RU/CMO）對應的職能鏡像。當委託方只有2人（QA + Supply），而CMO有7個職能的專職人員，資訊不對稱導致CMO必須代為決策——這是TT的重大風險。

對等職能配置：PM ↔ PM、QA ↔ QA、驗證 ↔ 驗證、製程工程 ↔ 製程工程、分析 ↔ 分析

比喻：足球比賽缺少後衛

就像一隊只派前鋒上場，另一隊卻有完整的11人陣容。前者贏了球（CMO做了決定），但輸了比賽的控制權（委託方喪失對產品的掌握）。TT中，委託方缺乏功能性SME，等於把自己的產品知識完全外包給CMO。

成功的關鍵轉折

Company A在Readiness階段才補齊人員——雖然比理想時間晚，但依然有效。說明：即使TT已經啟動，補強人員永遠不嫌晚。最糟糕的做法是發現問題卻繼續硬撐，直到PQ失敗才補救。

CDMO承接端應用

在Initiation階段的Kick-off Meeting前，要求委託方提供組織架構圖，確認其TT Team涵蓋以下職能：

  • Project Manager（TT決策窗口）
  • Quality（批准文件、偏差）
  • Regulatory（法規策略與申報）
  • Process/CMC（製程知識持有者）
  • Analytical（方法移轉）
  • Supply Chain（物料來源）

若委託方人員不足，在TT Charter中明確記錄此風險，並協商補足的時程。

承接端關鍵學習

CDMO不應承擔委託方應負責的決策。若委託方持續缺席決策，這是一個早期警訊，預示TT後期的知識移轉缺口與監管風險。主動要求委託方補齊對應人員，比事後補救更省資源。

8.3

Importance of the Initiation and Planning Stages to Technology Transfer

啟動與規劃階段對技術移轉的重要性

Commercial-Commercial Stages: Initiation, Planning Internal-Internal (Regional)

Company A needed to increase capacity for a drug substance (Product X). Since its sending unit (SU) site was unable to meet the full volume forecast of Product X, Company A made a business decision to transfer the process to another facility within its company network to serve as a second source of supply. Implementing a cloned process at the RU facility with minimal changes was critical to its regulatory strategy. To do this, the RU was instructed to purchase identical equipment (where possible) and replicate the existing process from the SU site. The SU shared full details of the equipment model and specifications, process documentation, and learnings with the RU to facilitate setup of the process.

Because of the process equivalency required and the ability to easily share knowledge between the sites, Company A assessed this TT to be of low risk and rapid execution, with minimal development and technical requirements. As a result, Company A underestimated both the budget and the amount of time needed for completion. Additionally, the project was staffed with only part-time internal support, primarily personnel from the RU site, that did not include the cross-functional expertise needed for the TT.

Despite having almost identical equipment and process-operating history, Company A experienced multiple technical challenges. For one, Company A could not purchase the exact models from the same vendors and had to select newer models in their place, yet no risk assessment was performed to identify the impact of what seemed to be minor differences in the new equipment. As the process would be identical, a full gap assessment for the process impact at the RU was not performed. Also, a lack of resourcing for the TT at the SU site prevented a thorough KT from the SU to the RU. Subsequently, the RU could not leverage shared learnings, historical data, or expertise the SU site had gained over the years or from the original development and qualification. As a result, the process validation and operational robustness depended on dedicated SU resources to continue assistance with their historical knowledge of the process.

Internal TT teams often fail to perform adequate due diligence of a TT in the Initiation and Planning stages of the process. This case study demonstrates the importance of following a TT process, resourcing appropriately, and maintaining effective KT even for internal TTs.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：「簡單的TT」是最危險的假設

本案例的根本錯誤是低估風險。「製程幾乎一樣」的判斷導致：

  • 預算與時程嚴重低估
  • 人員配置不足（兼職 vs. 全職專責）
  • 未執行風險評估（設備型號差異被忽略）
  • 未執行差距評估（gap assessment）
  • SU端資源不足，KT不完整

結果：RU完全依賴SU的持續支援，製程驗證無法獨立進行。

比喻：「複製貼上」的程式碼

就像程式設計師認為「複製一份程式碼就能在新環境跑」，卻忘了作業系統版本不同、程式庫版本不同。表面上程式碼一樣，但底層環境的差異讓它無法正常運作。TT中，「設備幾乎一樣」不等於「製程會一樣運作」。

三個被跳過的必要步驟

  • 風險評估：即使是「微小」的設備差異，也必須進行正式風險評估
  • 差距評估（Gap Assessment）：任何TT都應評估SU製程在RU環境的適配性
  • SU資源配置：送出方（SU）同樣需要專職資源支援KT，不能只靠接收方

CDMO承接端應用

無論委託方說這個TT「有多簡單」，承接端必須堅持執行以下評估：

  • 設備等同性評估（Equipment Equivalence Assessment）
  • 製程差距評估（Process Gap Assessment）
  • 法規影響評估（Regulatory Impact Assessment）
  • 完整的TT風險矩陣

在TT Charter中明確寫入這些評估的執行義務，並要求SU提供專職的技術支援資源。

承接端關鍵學習

內部TT（同公司不同廠區）的失敗率不低於外部TT。承接端應警惕：當委託方以「內部移轉」為由簡化流程時，這往往是最容易被忽視的風險來源。堅持完整的TT流程，不因「關係熟」而妥協。

8.4

Use of Regulatory Strategy for Initiation Stage Decisions

在啟動階段運用法規策略做出決策

Commercial-Commercial Stage: Initiation External-External

Company A used CMO C for manufacture of a veterinary drug product (Product Y) marketed only in the United States. When CMO C notified Company A that it no longer wanted to be a CMO, but would be manufacturing only for itself, Company A had to find a new CMO within a short time frame. Product Y is a sterile, lyophilized product with an approved indication only for the veterinary market. The TT was to include complete product and process transfer as well as analytical testing (release and stability) and stability storage.

Finding a company that can manufacture a lyophilized product can be a challenge, but this case presented additional barriers. Many companies with lyophilization capabilities manufacture lyophilized products for the Brazilian market. At the time, the Brazilian agency, ANVISA, specified that if a drug substance had no approved human indication, the veterinary product must be manufactured on a separate production line in a separate building from products for human use. Therefore, Company A needed to find a CMO that could manufacture Product Y on a separate production line from human products. As many CMOs had only one or two lyophilization production lines, the available pool of CMOs for transfer was limited.

Company A formed an initial TT Team comprising a PM and representatives from the Quality, Regulatory, and Supply Chain functions. The team reviewed the capabilities of multiple CMOs and found three companies (CMOs D, E, and F) that were able to manufacture Product Y in the U.S. in a manner that would be compliant with the current ANVISA requirements. The TT Team evaluated the CMOs based on their quality system and documentation, capability and expertise in lyophilized product manufacturing, equipment equivalence, analytical capabilities, costs, and projected timelines to commercial launch. Site evaluations revealed that CMO D was not interested due to the small volume that is common for veterinary products. The remaining two sites estimated similar costs, but the projected timelines to launch differed by six months, with CMO F having the longer timeline.

The TT Team further investigated the drastic difference and determined that the longer timeline was more realistic. CMO F had more experience and better understood the complexities of transferring a lyophilized product. The TT Team chose CMO F, despite the longer timeline, because it had considered in more detail what needed to be done and it employed a strong PM to help guide the process.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：法規複雜性決定CMO選擇空間

本案例展示了法規策略如何在Initiation階段就決定TT的走向：

  • 產品類型（獸藥，無人用適應症）觸發ANVISA特殊要求
  • ANVISA要求獨立生產線 → 大幅縮小可選CMO範圍
  • 最終選擇比競爭對手多6個月的CMO F → 因為其時程估算更真實

關鍵原則：較長但真實的時程，優於較短但不切實際的時程。

比喻：選承包商的智慧

找人裝修時，報價最低、工期最短的通常不是最好的選擇。有經驗的師傅會列出所有需要做的細項，解釋為什麼需要那麼多時間——這反而是信任的象徵。CMO E的6個月時程差距，不是效率優勢，而是對複雜性的低估。

CMO評選的多維度框架

本案例展示了完整的CMO評選標準：

  • 品質體系與文件化能力
  • 凍乾製造的專業能力與經驗
  • 設備等同性
  • 分析能力（放行與穩定性）
  • 成本
  • 預估商業化時程（真實性比長短更重要）
  • 法規合規性（多市場的特殊要求）

CDMO承接端應用

在提供TT時程估算時：

  • 寧願給出真實的較長時程，也不要用「樂觀估算」贏得合同
  • 主動識別所有法規複雜性（多市場要求、特殊法規限制），並在時程中體現
  • 建立詳細的時程說明文件，讓委託方理解每個里程碑背後的工作量

真實的時程是建立信任的基礎，也是避免後期衝突的最佳保障。

承接端關鍵學習

在評估TT可行性時，必須將目標市場的所有法規要求納入Initiation階段的考量。特別是多市場產品，不同監管機構的要求可能直接影響廠房佈局、設備配置、人員要求——這些都是TT時程與成本的重要驅動因素。

8.5

Knowledge Transfer to Ensure Process Qualification Success

知識移轉以確保製程確效成功

Development-Commercial Stage: Knowledge Transfer Internal-External

Company A decided to outsource production of a new vaccine drug product. The selected CMO, Company B, had significant experience performing TTs of new processes, working with dozens of customers each year at their facility. Company A was relatively new and had limited experience in the pharmaceutical industry; in fact, this would be its first experience taking a product in its pipeline through to commercialization.

Company B had implemented lessons learned from a previous project with a different company, Company C. Company B determined that multiple delays during the project with Company C had resulted from added equipment requirements, lack of clearly established goals and boundaries, and lack of transparency during KT. Additionally, complex differences in operations had required alignment between Company B and Company C to ship and receive materials from each other, resolve unique labeling requirements, and enhance material inspection requirements.

With these learnings in mind, Company B implemented significant improvements in its TT process to ensure a successful and proactive KT with Company A. Company B developed several tools and checklists to help with the KT upon project award, including:

  • Use of a handover form and checklist to establish all product requirements, TT boundaries, and goals.
  • KT checklist and a prepared playbook to be shared with Company A immediately upon award, ahead of the kick-off, detailing the customer's expectations, key next steps, and key data the RU would request. The materials included a questionnaire and checklist to help both companies ask the right questions and share key documents early in the process.
  • Break-out meetings for each function, held virtually prior to the TT kick-off, to ensure the kick-off meeting focused on the most impactful topics.
  • A face-to-face kick-off to formally start the project, align on requirements for each phase of the project, and align on any differences already discovered through the playbook, checklist, and questionnaire.

In addition:

  • The team performed an in-depth technical risk assessment of the TT to aid in effective KT for implementation at Company B, using a tracking tool and risk management document during the Execution stage to capture all knowledge and risk mitigation.
  • The team leveraged on-the-floor support from key experts at Company A to capture tacit knowledge transfer between functions as the TT progressed to development personnel and again to commercial production personnel.

Use of a detailed knowledge transfer process with transparent communications allowed the TT project to be completed on time with no delays.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：成熟的CMO從失敗中學習並系統化

Company B的優勢在於：將過去的失敗教訓轉化為可複製的TT工具與流程。這是CMO成熟度的標誌。其KT工具組包含：

  • 移交表格與核查清單（範疇、邊界、目標）
  • KT Playbook（客戶期望、下一步驟、所需數據）
  • Pre-kickoff職能分組虛擬會議
  • Face-to-face正式Kick-off
  • 技術風險評估 + 追蹤工具
  • 現場隱性知識捕捉（on-the-floor tacit KT）

比喻：Playbook的力量

頂尖廚師在新廚師加入時，不是說「跟著我做」，而是給一本詳細的食譜手冊，包含每個步驟的注意事項與常見錯誤。Company B的KT Playbook就是這個角色——讓即使第一次商業化的客戶（Company A），也能快速理解CMO需要什麼，並提前準備好。

隱性知識的捕捉是關鍵

本案例特別強調「on-the-floor support」——讓Company A的關鍵專家在製程進行時到場，直接捕捉隱性知識（tacit knowledge）。這種知識無法只靠文件傳遞：操作手感、判斷時機、異常識別——都需要在現場、面對面地傳承。

特別是在開發人員交棒給商業生產人員的環節，這個現場知識捕捉尤為重要。

CDMO承接端應用

建立並持續優化你的KT工具組：

  • 為新客戶準備標準化的KT Questionnaire（涵蓋製程、分析、法規、物料）
  • 設計Pre-kickoff的職能分組會議流程
  • 建立TT Risk Tracking工具（追蹤所有KT風險與緩解措施）
  • 在執行階段安排委託方SME到場（on-floor），捕捉隱性知識
  • 在開發→商業生產的交棒節點，重複執行KT確認

承接端關鍵學習

TT的按時完成，不是靠運氣，而是靠系統性的KT工具與透明溝通。投資建立可複製的KT Playbook，是CDMO提升TT成功率的最高槓桿點之一。每次TT後的教訓都應被整合回Playbook，讓組織不斷進化。

8.6

Performing Process Fit Assessment to Facilitate Technology Transfer

執行製程適配評估以促進技術移轉

Development-Commercial Stages: Initiation, Planning Internal-Internal

Company X developed a multivalent vaccine (Product Alpha) and produced phase I and II clinical drug substance materials at pilot scale. Company X determined that it required a commercial facility to produce drug substance for phase III clinical trials and commercial supply. A review of the market requirements pointed to the need for a change in scale, and company policy encouraged an increase in the level of automation to improve digitization of process data, reduce reliance on paper records, and reduce potential for human error.

In order to guide equipment specifications, process engineers assigned to the TT Team at Company X (the SU) developed scaling factors for the pilot-to-commercial volumes covering each unit operation. The fact that Product Alpha was multivalent presented complications, as some serotypes had different volume requirements for the same unit operation. To avoid purchasing a piece of equipment for each serotype, process engineers grouped the serotypes according to maximum and minimum volume requirements and determined the minimum amount of process equipment required to cover all cases.

An initial process fit assessment was conducted in the Initiation stage with a general idea of requirements, followed by a detailed assessment during the Knowledge Transfer stage. With yield information from the SU, and the previously mentioned scaling factor, the detailed process fit assessment was more accurate and complete. In it, ingredient additions, flowrates, maximum and minimum vessel volumes, and similar mass balance parameters were assessed to check the fit of the process in both new and existing equipment.

It was also necessary to determine what process development work, if any, would be required to support the process transfer to the new facility, especially since a change in scale was necessary. Residence and mixing times were altered in equipment and piping in the larger facility. Such changes were considered as part of the TT risk assessment. In this case, the mixing for a key reaction step was studied using computational flow dynamics modeling and a model chemical reaction to ensure adequate mixing at the larger scale.

The process fit assessment begun during the Initiation stage and revised during the Knowledge Transfer stage was vital to ensuring that the right equipment was specified and procured and that the right process development work was conducted to ensure a successful TT of Product Alpha.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：製程適配評估是兩階段迭代過程

本案例示範了Process Fit Assessment的最佳實踐：

  • 第一次（Initiation階段）：基於初步需求的概略評估 → 指導設備規格方向
  • 第二次（Knowledge Transfer階段）：基於完整產率數據的詳細評估 → 精確設備採購與製程開發計畫

評估內容涵蓋：成分添加量、流速、容器最大/最小容積、質量平衡參數、停留時間、混合時間。

比喻：房屋改建的結構評估

把一個小廚房擴建成商業廚房，不能只看平面圖就決定買什麼設備。第一步是概略評估空間和電力是否足夠；第二步是請結構工程師詳細計算承重、水電配置。少了第二步，買來的設備可能根本裝不進去，或者運作時發現電力不足。Process Fit Assessment就是這個結構評估。

多價疫苗的特殊複雜性

Product Alpha是多價疫苗，不同血清型（serotype）對同一單元操作的體積需求不同。工程師的解決方案是「最大最小體積分組」——以最少設備涵蓋所有情況。這個思路展示了process fit assessment在多產品共用設備場景下的實際應用。

CDMO承接端應用

在Initiation階段，要求委託方提供足夠的製程數據以執行初步Fit Assessment：

  • 批次大小範圍（最大/最小/典型）
  • 關鍵單元操作的體積/流量需求
  • 放大比例（pilot-to-commercial scale-up factor）
  • 混合、停留時間等關鍵製程參數

在KT階段，用實際產率數據更新Fit Assessment，並識別需要額外製程開發的領域。放大過程中的混合效率（如CFD模擬）可能需要額外的開發工作，應在KT Risk Assessment中體現。

承接端關鍵學習

Process Fit Assessment不是一次性活動，而是隨TT進展持續更新的動態文件。Initiation階段的初步評估決定設備採購方向；KT階段的詳細評估決定製程開發需求。兩次評估之間的差距，往往是TT風險的主要來源。

8.7

Planning Ahead for On Time Commercial Launch

提前規劃以實現準時商業上市

Commercial-Commercial Stages: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Handover Internal-External

Business considerations are usually the driving factor for performing a TT, with identification of the RU being the first key deliverable. In this case study, Company A (the SU) wanted to consider transferring a sterile parenteral drug product to another facility.

A small team of experts from Company A's project management, business, quality, and manufacturing functional areas performed due diligence in the choice of the RU (Company B). Once the RU was identified and a nondisclosure agreement was executed, the TT PM at the SU expanded the SU TT Team to include the Analytical, Regulatory, and Engineering functions. The RU also formed a TT Team of corresponding functional areas. These two TT teams established regular meetings to facilitate KT and communication. Working together, the teams developed the project plan, defined the regulatory strategy for approval of the drug product, and executed a risk assessment of the knowledge elements needed to adapt the manufacturing process to the equipment at the RU in order to produce product to the quality target product profile and specifications required.

While completing the regulatory filing submission and health authority inspections, preparations began to hand over the product to manufacturing. The RU TT Team held meetings of increasing frequency closer to the start date for commercial production. During these meetings, the RU TT Team shared all lessons learned from product performance, equipment and process knowledge, and supplier management, including component and material suppliers with lead times. Formal introduction to key contacts were made, and key commercial systems such as material and batch management, supply management, and equipment were set up and ready for the new product. The RU TT Team recommended essential elements for the operator training, including lessons learned, that needed to be incorporated into the batch record. The RU TT Team also recommended changes to the production schedule to take into consideration learnings from run performance and resource needs. In addition, the RU TT Team ensured that spare parts and component needs were set up and ready for the anticipated forecast and ensured the production schedule maintained an up-to-date, 18-month forecast.

Changeover (cleaning validation) and validation of the aseptic manufacturing process at the vendor facility was on a critical timeline for manufacturing of regulatory batches, regulatory approval, and launch of commercial product from the vendor site. The TT was closed by the handover from the TT Team to a post-transfer operations team with complementary expertise and responsibilities for control of commercial production.

As a result of this successful planning and management of Handover activities, the site was ready to begin commercial production at the scheduled start without any delays.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：商業化準備從TT第一天就開始

本案例展示了全程TT（Initiation到Handover）的整合規劃。關鍵成功要素：

  • 以「NDA簽署後立即啟動TT Team擴充」作為觸發點
  • RU TT Team持續更新18個月商業化預測
  • Handover會議頻率隨商業生產日期接近而升高
  • 備件與物料需求在Handover前完成設置
  • 正式移交給Post-transfer Operations Team

比喻：飛機著陸的進場程序

飛機不是到了跑道上方才開始降落準備。進場程序在20海里外就開始：速度控制、高度調整、與塔台通訊、放下起落架……商業上市的準備也是如此：供應商Lead Time管理、操作員訓練、ERP系統設定——都必須在上市前數個月完成，而不是在TT結束後才啟動。

Handover清單的關鍵要素

  • 所有來自TT的Lessons Learned已整合進Batch Record
  • 關鍵供應商聯絡人已正式介紹給商業生產團隊
  • 物料與批次管理系統（ERP/SAP）已完成設定
  • 備件庫存已按18個月預測備齊
  • 操作員訓練已完成並記錄
  • 生產排程已整合資源需求與設備能力

CDMO承接端應用

在TT項目計畫中，將以下商業化準備活動明確設為里程碑：

  • T-6個月：完成供應商資格確認，Lock住主要物料來源
  • T-4個月：ERP/SAP物料主檔建立完成
  • T-3個月：操作員訓練計畫啟動
  • T-2個月：備件庫存到位
  • T-1個月：Handover會議由週一次升為每週多次
  • T-0：正式移交Post-transfer Operations Team

承接端關鍵學習

TT成功不等於商業化成功。TT Team的最終任務是確保接手的Operations Team具備足夠的知識、工具與資源，能夠在沒有TT Team支援的情況下獨立運作。Handover是TT的最後也是最重要的品質關卡。

8.8

Navigating Process, Capital, & Ownership Changes During a Drug Product Transfer

在藥品移轉過程中應對製程、資本與所有權變更

Development-Commercial Stages: All (Initiation through Handover) External-External

In today's manufacturing, it is common when transferring technology from one manufacturing site to another manufacturing site for both sites to be external to the MAH. So-called "virtual companies" may start with one external supplier, then later add suppliers or switch suppliers for quality or pricing reasons. This case study, which involved the decision to switch manufacturers upon completion of clinical phase III, discusses the basics that should be considered for a seamless transfer.

Initiation: Following a capacity analysis aligned to the business strategy, the MAH decided to continue with external manufacturing. With regulatory considerations the driving factor, the MAH decided to transfer production from the external party manufacturing the clinical phase III supplies (Company A) to a different external party for commercial production. The MAH's existing cross-functional TT Team remained with the product to facilitate this new TT, and a TT proposal was drafted. Led by the TT PM, the TT Team visited the prospective commercial site (Company B) to evaluate the potential fit and identify the process, equipment, and analytical method gaps that would need to be addressed for a successful transfer. A material supply agreement and related quality agreement were reached. Because the MAH did not want Company A to know about the pending transfer to Company B, the material supply agreement remained confidential. In this case, the MAH acted as the responsible SU and no communication was allowed between Company A and Company B. With the approval of the TT proposal, the steering committee made the decision to launch with Company B.

Planning: A detailed project plan that spelled out clear deliverables by phase and stage gate and detailed the project governance that was created and approved by the SU and the RU (Company B). The same raw material vendors would be used, though all vendors needed to be requalified. For qualification, each vendor was required to supply three lots of raw materials used in manufacturing, followed by reissuing of all specifications, methods, and other supporting chemistry documents. The batch record was transferred, and the equipment was purchased to match as closely as possible the current process. In this case, the scale was to be increased, which resulted in a new registration batch followed by three validation batches. Based on process knowledge and guided by the process failure mode effects analysis, a process validation master plan was approved that defined the necessary protocols to be executed. The MAH discussed scalable processes, engineering batches, and process sampling and controls. The decision was made to separate the batch record into two—one batch record for mixing and one for filling—as there was a significant hold time after mixing and a check was needed on the product before filling. The steering committee approved the stage gate, authorizing the TT Team to proceed to full-scale manufacturing.

Execution: Once new equipment was installed and validated, dynamic smoke studies were conducted, and Company B decided to perform media fills using their specific vial and equipment to demonstrate that aseptic conditions were maintained under the slightly changed conditions. Microbiological testing of all personnel and the environment was performed throughout the process using touch plates, and a swab study was performed after a risk assessment to determine the appropriate environmental monitoring program. According to the stability plan, stability studies were performed in both the upright and inverted positions for all batches. All the RU's cleaning procedures, cleaning swab-testing, protocols, reports, training, and risk assessments were determined to be adequate as is and were used for the transfer. Serialization, packaging, and labeling were agreed upon. Methods were successfully transferred and were qualified or validated per requirements.

Project Closure/Lessons Learned: The TT was successful but, in hindsight, the process could have been smoother had the MAH and Company B identified early on which party would be responsible for the purchase of the additional capital. In the end, the MAH and the RU agreed to share these costs, with the exception that the Company B agreed to purchase a couple of the more expensive items. An additional difficulty was that the MAH had not negotiated or retained the rights to the methods, batch records, and all documents necessary for the transfer that Company A was performing for them. Had the MAH preemptively ensured it owned those rights, the TT would have been easier to execute.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：虛擬公司的TT特殊挑戰

本案例涵蓋兩個在CDMO業界高度相關的教訓：

  • 教訓1（資本責任）：追加設備採購的費用分攤必須在TT啟動時談妥，而非等到問題發生後才協商
  • 教訓2（文件所有權）：MAH必須確保自己擁有方法、批次記錄等關鍵文件的所有權——在與Company A的合約中就應明確，否則從A移轉至B時會遭遇重大阻礙

比喻：租房搬家前確認財產歸屬

租客搬出時，房東才說「你裝修加的那面牆是我的，不能拆走」。同理，MAH在結束與Company A的合約時，才發現批次記錄的所有權沒有事先約定——這是合約管理的重大失誤。TT就像一場搬家：在搬之前，必須確認哪些東西是你的，哪些是房東的，哪些需要特別授權才能帶走。

保密性帶來的複雜性

本案例另一個值得關注的面向：MAH不希望Company A知道正在轉移到Company B，因此禁止兩家CMO直接溝通。這意味著MAH必須作為中間層傳遞所有技術信息——增加了溝通複雜性與KT的時間成本。

現實中，CDMO常遇到這類「保密TT」情境，需要提前在KT計畫中建立對應的溝通機制。

CDMO承接端應用

在TT合約談判時，主動確認以下條款：

  • 文件所有權：明確列明哪些文件（分析方法、批次記錄、驗證報告）的所有權屬於委託方，且可在TT結束後轉移使用
  • 追加資本責任：明確約定因製程變更或設備升級產生的追加資本投入由誰負責
  • 放大策略：若TT涉及規模放大，必須在Planning階段確認驗證批次數量與策略

承接端關鍵學習

TT合約的細節決定TT的成敗。文件所有權、追加資本分攤、機密條款的溝通限制——這些看似法務問題，實際上直接影響技術移轉的執行效率。在Initiation階段投入足夠的法律與商務資源審視合約，是CDMO自我保護的第一道防線。

8.9

Failed Process Qualification Due to Incomplete Process Readiness & Knowledge Transfer

因製程就緒度與知識移轉不完整導致製程確效失敗

Commercial-Commercial Stages: Readiness, Execution External-External

Company A made a business decision to outsource its manufacturing and packaging process for Product X, a new vaccine. At the time, Company A outsourced Product X with a CMO, Company B, but was unhappy with their services. Based on cost savings and strategy, Company A decided to discontinue business with Company B by the end of the year, and it awarded a TT project to a new CMO (Company C) with an aggressive timeline. Company A informed Company C that the product must be successfully launched within 12 months to ensure that commercial supply was not interrupted. Company A stressed this criticality, as they could not go back to Company B for additional commercial business.

Company A and Company C kicked off the project smoothly and progressed to Planning and Readiness activities. Upon execution of the engineering runs, however, results were suboptimal. Company C had several ideas to improve the process to better control the results, ensure higher yield, improve confidence in right-first-time process qualification and, from their experience, avoid longer-term out-of-specification results during commercial production. These improvements would require additional, unbudgeted spending by Company A to perform additional batches, however, as well as additional time, which would push the overall timeline beyond the 12 months specified and make the target launch impossible.

Company A and Company C were misaligned and escalated the issues to their managements, losing several weeks in the process. Company C felt strongly that it should not proceed with process qualification with the current suboptimal results, and Company A wanted to proceed, at risk, to meet supply needs. Finally, a compromise was reached: Company C's TT Team agreed to make minor improvements to the current process with current equipment, capabilities, batch records, and trainings, and to conduct one additional engineering run to improve process understanding and verify improvements. If those steps went well, the team would move into process qualification. Assuming those batches passed all requirements, they would be commercially saleable. Following release of these initial launch batches, the Company C TT Team would revisit the proposed improvements. Company A accepted liability should the strategy of moving ahead with the qualification without implementing all identified improvements fail.

Unfortunately, the process qualification batch did not pass. Three months were lost executing the initial improvements and engineering run, analyzing data and investigating the root cause of the failure, finalizing a recommended path forward to improve the process, and obtaining alignment on additional costs and an adjusted timeline. The team agreed on a path forward and Company C successfully launched the product by the middle of the following year, almost six months late. Company A was forced to consider extending business relations with Company B to supply their product in the interim or otherwise impact market supply.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：供應壓力不能凌駕製程就緒度

本案例是TR65最重要的警示故事之一。失敗的根本原因：

  • Engineering run結果次優（suboptimal），但供應壓力迫使提前進入PQ
  • 委託方與CMO的目標嚴重不一致：供應連續性 vs. 製程品質
  • 妥協方案（最小化改善後進行PQ）未能解決根本的製程就緒度缺口
  • PQ失敗 → 損失3個月 → 最終延遲6個月，遠超過事先做好改善所需的時間

比喻：趕時間的外科手術

病患堅持要在血壓異常時立即進行選擇性手術，因為不想再等了。醫生提出風險，病患自行簽署免責聲明，手術台上出了問題。最後花的時間比等到血壓穩定後再手術多了三倍。TT中，在製程就緒度不足時強行進入PQ，是一樣的邏輯陷阱。

CMO的正確立場

Company C的立場是正確的：製程就緒度不足時，不應進入PQ。儘管最終做出了妥協，但Company C已明確表達其專業判斷，並要求委託方承擔風險責任。這是CDMO在面對來自委託方的時程壓力時，必須堅守的專業底線。

重要：PQ失敗的3個月損失，加上調整後的計畫執行時間，遠超過事先正確執行製程改善所需的時間。「冒險快跑」反而更慢。

CDMO承接端應用

建立明確的PQ就緒度標準（Readiness Criteria），在TT Charter中約定：

  • Engineering run的通過標準必須達到X%以上才能進入PQ
  • 若Engineering run結果次優，有權暫停並要求進行根本原因調查
  • 時程壓力不得作為跳過就緒度評估的理由
  • 委託方若堅持在就緒度不足情況下推進，必須以書面承擔相應風險責任

承接端關鍵學習

面對委託方的時程壓力，CDMO最有力的論點是：「現在多花2個月做好，勝過PQ失敗後損失6個月。」用數字說話，而非只是說「我覺得還沒準備好」。建立一套製程就緒度評分體系，讓Go/No-Go決策有客觀依據，不是主觀判斷。

8.10

Effective Use of Knowledge Transfer

有效運用知識移轉

Commercial-Commercial Stages: Initiation, Planning, Execution External-Internal

In this case study, the technology of a commercialized sterile drug product (mAb-X) approved for commercial drug product manufacturing at an external site (Site A) was transferred to Site B, the RU. Site B was an internal site of the MAH for mAb-X (Company Y) and was new to commercial drug manufacturing. Site A and Site B did not communicate directly during the TT. The Manufacturing Science and Technology group of Company Y served as the SU and was responsible for the KT from Site A to the Site B.

Company Y had experience with TT for product mAb-X having previously transferred the product to Site A in a development-to-commercial phase TT. Therefore, Company Y already possessed information important to the TT, such as the commercial manufacturing process, required equipment, process parameters classification, risk assessment for the commercial process, and analytical methods for product quality testing. This information was used as a reference for the TT of the same product to Site B.

The goal of the TT was to minimize the number of changes to the existing manufacturing process used by Site A while designing a process for commercial manufacture that fit within the constraints of Site B. To achieve this objective, a facility-fit and gap assessment was performed for Site B, which became the key to the success of the TT. The existing risk assessment information for mAb-X at Site A was used as the starting point for the risk assessment for site B. Also, since the RU was a new commercial drug product manufacturing site, the TT team worked with the on-site Validation group at Site B to map out the equipment validation activities required to support this TT (installation qualification, operational qualification, and performance qualification). An integrated project plan was drafted during the Planning stage that included an overview of all TT activities needed for the successful TT and their interdependencies.

The SU shared the information on analytical methods from the prior mAb-X TT with the RU. An evaluation for implementation of existing analytical methods for Site B was completed, and resources were allocated for the method transfer, training, and execution. Change controls and documentation procedures for manufacturing mAb-X were established before the TT activities to Site B were executed. All changes related to process, raw material specifications, equipment, and process parameters for the TT were captured under the central change control management system.

As part of the Readiness stage, the sourcing of raw materials, components, and equipment was reviewed, and relevant information was included in the validated electronic system (in this case, SAP). The information for product, packaging, and component specifications was reviewed and approved by both the RU and the SU. In addition, upon request, vendors/suppliers supplied drawings and other required information. Suppliers also provided certificate of analysis information for all raw materials and components, which was reviewed. Inspection and acceptance criteria for receiving raw materials and components in a GMP facility were agreed upon by the RU and SU teams with input from the Quality function.

As part of process performance qualification (PPQ) readiness activities, the SU Engineering group, in collaboration with their RU counterparts, prepared process description documents capturing the commercial manufacturing process for mAb-X at Site B. Information from the process description document for mAb-X at Site A was used as a template, and appropriate changes were made to capture the differences in the process at Site B. Process characterization studies were carried out to support the changes in the commercial process for Site B based on the facility-fit and gap assessment exercise, which was critical for the success of this TT. Also, prior knowledge from the TT of mAb-X to Site A was leveraged for process characterization studies. Engineering runs were performed using surrogate and using mAb-X to understand the design space for the operation of process parameters and to determine the normal operating range and proven acceptable range for various unit operations. The required number of PPQ batches were manufactured using a matrix approach to bracket the batch size (minimum and maximum), and the rationale for the PPQ strategy was documented prior to execution. Post-PPQ, all deviations were closed and documented, and required CAPAs were identified and implemented. At this point, PPQ summary reports were written that captured the process parameter data for all PPQ batches, hold-time data for various process steps, product quality attribute results (in-process and lot-release testing results), and deviations for the PPQ batches.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：先前TT的知識是下一次TT的資本

本案例最重要的洞察：Company Y因為有mAb-X到Site A的TT經驗，在mAb-X到Site B的TT中取得了顯著優勢：

  • 製程參數分類（已完成）→ 直接作為新TT起點
  • 風險評估（已完成）→ 在Site A基礎上更新，而非從零開始
  • 分析方法（已移轉）→ Site B的方法移轉以Site A為模板
  • 製程描述文件（已建立）→ Site B版本以Site A版本為模板修改

這說明：每次TT都是下一次TT的知識投資。

比喻：連鎖店的開店SOP

第一家門市開業很痛苦，所有流程都要摸索。第二家、第三家就快多了——因為有了標準化的開店手冊。Company Y的第二次TT之所以順利，正是因為它把第一次TT的痛苦轉化成了可複用的制度性知識。

PPQ矩陣策略的實際應用

本案例描述了PPQ的矩陣策略（matrix approach）：用最小和最大批次大小來bracket，而非每個尺寸都跑完整PPQ。這是ICH Q10和FDA製程驗證指引認可的基於風險的策略，在生物製劑TT中尤為常見。關鍵是策略的事前記錄與批准（documented prior to execution）。

CDMO承接端應用

建立「TT知識庫」機制：

  • 每次TT結束後，將風險評估、分析方法評估、設備等同性評估歸檔至產品知識庫
  • 下次接收同類產品或同一委託方的TT時，優先查閱既有評估，在既有基礎上更新
  • 用「差距評估 vs. 既有知識」取代「從零開始的評估」，大幅縮短KT時間

同時，要求委託方提供所有既有的TT相關文件（前一個CMO的驗證報告、製程描述等），作為本次TT的起點。

承接端關鍵學習

Change Control Management System的建立應在TT執行前完成——所有製程、規格、設備、參數的變更都應納入中央變更管控。這是防止「知識碎片化」的制度保障，也是後續監管檢查的核心文件基礎。

8.11

Use of Stage Gates to Address Process Readiness Challenges

運用階段關卡應對製程就緒度挑戰

Development-Commercial Stage: Readiness Internal-Internal (Global)

Company A transferred a very complex process involving a new drug product from development (Product X) in this case study. The new molecular entity required new technologies for which limited industry expertise existed. The TT Team encountered significant delays, and they spent more than two years troubleshooting various problems with the new equipment and repeating developmental studies on the new equipment to finalize the process parameters and develop a robust process. The TT Team did not feel it was possible to meet the company leadership's goals of both advancing Product X to PPQ to meet launch requirements and maintaining the project within the adjusted budget, which was well over the original budget. This misalignment prompted the TT Team to implement a steering committee and stage gates.

The team designed the formal TT process to allow both functional management and senior leadership to review key project data, risks, accomplishments, and proposed options for each phase of the project for risk mitigation. By engaging senior leaders from elsewhere within the company, senior management was able to provide additional expertise on the new technology and similar processes. These leaders also offered different perspectives on the suboptimal data. Following review of the data, and with support from management to improve the process, the TT team agreed on the next steps, tried the recommended strategies, and leveraged on-site support from proposed SMEs within the company.

The improvements to the processes resulted in a product that met requirements. Before proceeding, the TT Team facilitated another stage gate meeting to review recent data and ensure alignment on next steps. The process was much improved, but given the unique process and requirements, new technologies, and limited expertise, the steering committee decided the TT Team should execute one more study to facilitate the learnings and gain additional experience. That study would be followed by a mock run (water batch) to improve the training and experience of the production personnel prior to proceeding to PPQ. Ultimately, the team successfully executed their qualification batches right first time. Although the added developmental studies and mock run took additional time, the TT Team agreed that by managing the previous misalignment, they had saved time by preventing a failed qualification or Handover.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：Stage Gate是決策升級的結構化機制

本案例與8.9的關鍵差異在於：當TT Team發現目標與現實的落差時，主動建立Stage Gate機制，而非在壓力下做出妥協。Stage Gate的三個功能：

  • 資訊透明：關鍵數據、風險、成就、選項 → 呈現給更高層管理
  • 專業導入：引入公司其他部門的SME，獲得對新技術的外部視角
  • 決策對齊：在推進前獲得所有相關方的明確授權

比喻：登山的審慎回頭

登山隊在接近頂峰時遭遇惡劣天氣。沒有規紀的隊伍繼續前進，往往以悲劇告終。有紀律的隊伍在Stage Gate（預設的高度點）重新評估：繼續、等待還是下撤？加一次Mock Run（水批次訓練）就像先模擬登頂再真正出發——多花時間，但確保首次嘗試就成功。

Mock Run（Water Batch）的價值

在進入PPQ前執行Mock Run（水批次）是一個低風險的訓練工具，讓操作人員在真正使用產品批次前，充分練習新設備與新製程的操作。本案例明確說明這個額外步驟帶來了「首次成功」（right first time）的結果。對於複雜的新技術，Mock Run是降低PPQ失敗風險的有效投資。

CDMO承接端應用

在TT計畫中預設Stage Gate決策節點：

  • Engineering Run完成後（Readiness Stage Gate）：Go/No-Go for PQ
  • 複雜製程考慮加入Mock Run作為強制前置步驟
  • Stage Gate會議應包含：數據回顧、風險清單更新、外部SME意見、明確的下一步授權

特別是面對新技術或公司內部expertise有限的製程，主動提議引入外部SME，而非依靠TT Team獨自解決。

承接端關鍵學習

Stage Gate不是阻礙TT進度的官僚流程，而是在複雜或高風險TT中保護所有相關方的決策工具。案例最後的結論很有力：「雖然多花了時間，但Stage Gate機制避免了PQ失敗或Handover失敗，實際上節省了時間。」這個邏輯值得在與委託方的時程討論中直接引用。

8.12

Importance of Handover Stage for a Successful Commercial Start

移交階段對成功商業化啟動的重要性

Development-Commercial Stage: Handover Internal-Internal

Company A had little experience with TT; however, with a new building and equipment, it had gained the technological capability to support a new sterile drug product transfer being planned (Product X). In addition, Company A hired personnel, bringing new functions into the facility including TT PMs, scientists, regulatory affairs staff, and process engineers. Company A also brought in several experienced consultants to support the new equipment and its validation at the facility, who stayed on through the Product X launch. The TT Team was well supported with project staffing, regular stage gates, a steering committee, and engaged leadership. Due to their limited experience in TT and the risks associated with the new process, equipment, and technology, the company management also ensured that SMEs would be closely involved to monitor and support each step of the project.

During development and process qualification, the TT Team used an "all hands on deck" approach, expecting the entire team to participate on the floor and oversee the process during execution of the studies and commercial batches. The team managed the checklists, oversaw the batch records, and led each step to ensure the production operators and area personnel were correctly executing the process requirements. Production personnel also incorporated interventions, adjustments, and recommendations based on learnings from the TT Team's experience. The team wanted to make sure that the expertise and learnings from their history with the project and equipment were leveraged to ensure the commercial batches would be completed right first time. As a result, the project was successful: the commercial batches were executed flawlessly and met all requirements for release.

Following project closure, the TT Team personnel were repurposed to other positions within the company and the consultant contracts were terminated.

As the facility entered routine commercial operations for this product, however, the production personnel found the new process and product requirements very challenging. Without the expertise on the floor to oversee and guide them every step of the way, the batch records, SOPs, and training proved insufficient. Due to the production personnel's lack of understanding on how to set up and operate the new equipment, the facility experienced significant downtime and eventually had to obtain on-site vendor support to retrain and create training guides for the team. Because of the lost time, failed batches due to human error, investigations, and CAPAs, the manufacturing team decided to update the SOPs and batch records associated with the new process and retrain the personnel. After several months—and significant product losses and costs—the production personnel had obtained the necessary knowledge on the process and had received adequate training through the updated SOPs and batch records to successfully and repeatably execute the processes without additional support.

In hindsight, if the TT Reference Guide approach had been used, critical knowledge elements could have been identified in the KT stage, and risk mitigation strategies could have been developed to ensure the successful deployment of commercial operations.

解讀與應用

核心教訓：TT成功 ≠ 商業化成功，知識轉移必須到達操作員層級

本案例是TR65最具警示意義的案例：TT在所有技術指標上都成功了——PQ通過、首批商業批次完美——但商業化啟動後立即陷入危機。根本原因：

  • TT Team的知識留在TT Team身上，沒有真正轉移到常規生產操作員
  • TT Team被分散（人員調職、顧問合約終止），知識隨之消散
  • SOP、批次記錄和訓練在TT Team監督下足夠用，但在無監督情況下不足以支撐獨立操作

比喻：拆鷹架的時機

建築完工後急著拆鷹架，但工人還沒學會如何自行維護這棟建築。TT Team就是鷹架——在施工期間不可或缺，但必須在操作員真正具備獨立能力後才能撤離。「批次通過」不等於「操作員已具備獨立能力」。

Handover Checklist：知識是否真正轉移到操作員？

應在Handover前評估以下問題：

  • 操作員是否能在沒有TT Team監督下，獨立設定和操作新設備？
  • SOP和批次記錄是否充分詳細，足以在沒有「現場教練」時指引操作？
  • 操作員訓練的評估方式（考試？實操？模擬批次？）是否能驗證真正的能力？
  • 對於新技術，是否有Vendor的訓練材料被整合進內部SOP？

CDMO承接端應用

在Handover Stage設計「操作員獨立能力驗證」機制：

  • 在TT Team全面撤出前，安排2-3個「無監督批次」觀察操作員的獨立執行能力
  • 操作員訓練應包含設備異常處理（故障排除）的情境訓練，而不只是正常操作流程
  • 建立「知識留存計畫」：TT Team成員在轉任其他職位前，必須完成知識文件化清單
  • 對新設備，要求設備供應商提供或協助建立操作訓練教材（Training Guide）

承接端關鍵學習

Handover是TR65最容易被低估的階段。「TT成功」的定義應包含：常規生產操作員能夠在無TT Team監督下，穩定、重複地執行製程。如果操作員還需要TT Team「在旁邊」才能順利生產，Handover就尚未完成。用「TR65 Reference Guide approach」在KT階段識別所有關鍵知識要素，並確保這些要素都被轉移到SOP和訓練材料中。

Cross-Case Summary & Comparison Matrix — 跨案例總結與比較矩陣

跨案例關鍵洞察 — 繁體中文總結

五個反覆出現的成功因素

  1. 治理架構（8.1、8.11）：正式的指導委員會、Stage Gate、溝通計畫是TT在複雜條件下保持方向的關鍵
  2. 功能對稱的團隊（8.2）：委託方與接收方的TT Team職能必須對應，缺任何一方的職能都會造成知識移轉的盲區
  3. 製程就緒度不可妥協（8.9 vs. 8.11）：在Engineering run次優時強行推進PQ，最終損失的時間遠多於事先做好準備
  4. 知識必須到達最終使用者（8.12）：TT成功的標準是操作員的獨立能力，而非TT Team的技術成就
  5. 既有知識的槓桿（8.5、8.10）：成熟CMO和有TT經驗的MAH，能將過去的失敗和成功轉化為可複用的工具、模板和評估框架

三個最常被忽略的風險

  • 內部TT的低估（8.3）：「同公司、同製程」的假設導致Due Diligence被跳過；內部TT的失敗率不低於外部TT
  • 文件所有權（8.8）：在合約談判時未確認方法、批次記錄的所有權，導致後續TT執行困難
  • Handover的表面成功（8.12）：PQ通過、PPQ完美，不等於商業化已準備就緒；操作員的真實能力才是Handover的最終判斷標準

Pattern Analysis: What Made TTs Succeed or Fail

CDMO Receiving Site Master Lessons — 承接端綜合行動清單

Initiation：

  • 要求共同建立TT Charter（含變更管理流程）
  • 確認委託方TT Team的職能完整性
  • 執行初步Process Fit Assessment
  • 識別所有目標市場的法規複雜性
  • 確認文件所有權與資本投入責任

Planning：

  • 執行詳細差距評估（Gap & Risk Assessment）
  • 建立詳細、真實的時程（以法規複雜性為依據）
  • 設定Stage Gate標準（含PQ就緒度判斷準則）
  • 建立TT Risk Tracking工具

Knowledge Transfer：

  • 使用KT Playbook、Questionnaire、Checklist
  • 安排委託方SME到場捕捉隱性知識
  • 更新Process Fit Assessment（含實際產率數據）
  • 建立中央變更管控系統

Readiness & Execution：

  • 堅持PQ就緒度標準，不因供應壓力妥協
  • 對新技術考慮加入Mock Run
  • Stage Gate決策需記錄並獲得明確授權

Handover：

  • 驗證操作員的獨立執行能力（非監督狀態下）
  • 更新SOP和批次記錄至足以自主操作的詳細程度
  • 執行TT知識文件化清單後，TT Team方可解散
  • 建立TT Lessons Learned，歸入知識庫

  • 商業化準備活動（ERP設定、備件、操作員訓練）是否已排入計畫？（8.7）

  • 我們是否充分利用了既有的TT知識（過去的風險評估、方法、文件）？（8.10）
