6.0 Additional Considerations for Package Integrity Profiling | 包裝完整性概況分析的額外考量

Lifecycle stressors from manufacturing through distribution | 從製造到配送的生命週期壓力因子

Original Text — Section 6.0

A robust package integrity profile for a given product-package system considers factors experienced throughout the product's lifecycle as required by USP <1207>. As such, a package integrity profile must consider anticipated or experienced stresses, including those resulting from transportation and distribution. Such stresses may include shock, vibration, compression, temperature, pressure, and general package storage environment.

Key Concept: Package Integrity Profile

A package integrity profile is the comprehensive, lifecycle-spanning record of a product-package system's integrity status. It encompasses results from development, manufacturing, stability, and distribution phases. USP <1207> requires that this profile account for all stresses the package will experience throughout its life.

  • 震動（Shock/Vibration）：運輸途中物理撞擊
  • 壓縮（Compression）：堆疊儲存造成的壓力
  • 溫度（Temperature）：冷鏈與溫度偏差
  • 氣壓（Pressure）：航空運輸中的低壓環境
  • 儲存環境（Storage Environment）：濕度、紫外線等

生活比喻

「包裝完整性概況」就像一本護照，記錄這個產品-包裝系統從「出生」（開發）到「退休」（使用後）的每段旅程及考驗。監管機構要求你能說清楚：這個包裝在人生每個階段是否仍然完整？

6.1 Transportation and Distribution | 運輸與配送

Distribution chain stressors unique to each product-package system | 每個產品-包裝系統獨特的配送鏈壓力

Original Text — Section 6.1

A package integrity profile is unique to each product-package system, a concept that also applies to storage and distribution considerations. The types of stresses experienced in these lifecycle phases vary widely with dosage form, pack-out configuration, product requirements, and distribution chain. Additionally, the impact of any single stress factor, such as a temperature excursion, can vary with respect to product. An excursion critical to one product may be insubstantial to the quality of another.

Given the potential impact to product quality and sterility, appropriate challenges and assessments should be made to ensure that product quality is not affected during the transportation and distribution circuit. The types of challenge conditions and subsequent assessments performed, however, reflect the properly assessed risks for the system in question.

As the intent of this report is to focus on package integrity, only those distribution challenges impacting integrity are discussed here. Transportation and distribution challenges are not limited solely to integrity of the package, however. A fully integral package containing a proteinaceous product may experience vibration and shock that does not lead to integrity failure but, for example, may cause aggregation of protein. Thus, in addition to post-challenge CCIT, other assessments may be necessary to evaluate the impact of distribution on overall product quality.

中文解說 — 第6.1節

配送壓力因子會因劑型、包裝形式、產品特性和配送鏈不同而有極大差異。以溫度偏差為例，對某些蛋白質製劑可能是致命的，但對另一類產品卻幾乎無影響。

重要提醒：完整性≠產品品質全部

TR86的重點是包裝完整性，但務必記得：一個在物理上完整（沒有洩漏）的包裝，仍可能因震動導致蛋白質聚集（aggregation）等品質問題。因此除了CCIT之外，還需要進行其他品質評估（如SE-HPLC測定蛋白質聚集）。

配送圈（Distribution Circuit）概念

「配送圈」包含從製造廠出發，經過倉儲、批發商、物流中心、零售藥局，一直到患者手中的整個旅程。每個環節的壓力條件都應被評估並納入完整性概況中。

6.1.1 Physical Stressors | 物理壓力因子

Original Text — Section 6.1.1

Perhaps the most obvious challenges experienced during distribution are physical stresses such as shock, vibration, and compression. Often, these types of events result in gross leakage, for instance, a vial shattering. This type of gross defect is easily detected and generally prevents final use of the product. The formation of cracks, checks, chips, and other small defects, however, may result in leakage that negatively impacts product quality but is not readily detectable by the end user. Thus, these types of defects present a more latent and use-specific risk.

Furthermore, such types of defects may result in more serious integrity concerns further along in the distribution chain, as a check may develop into a crack, or a crack may widen and permit liquid loss or microbial ingress.

Consequently, to evaluate and reduce risk associated with a shipment, not only the primary package, but the secondary and tertiary packaging should be considered. Numerous standards are available from such organizations as ASTM and the International Safe Transit Association (ISTA) to reliably and reproducibly subject package systems to physical stresses that replicate a variety of distribution chains, including via truck, rail, air, sea, or others.

The effects of temperature on packaging and distribution is also discussed in Section 4.4 on Cryogenic Conditions.

物理壓力因子是配送中最直觀的風險，但危害程度差異很大：

「潛伏性風險」的概念

細裂紋（check）的危險在於它是一個「時間炸彈」：在製造時可能僅是微小缺陷，但在配送過程中可能擴大成裂縫，最終導致微生物侵入（microbial ingress）或藥液滲漏。這就是為何必須評估整個包裝系統（一次、二次、三次包裝）。

ISTA 標準

ISTA（International Safe Transit Association）提供一系列標準化的配送模擬測試，其中ISTA 2A是常用的運輸模擬標準，涵蓋振動、衝擊、堆疊壓縮等測試條件，可重現陸運、航空等不同配送鏈。

6.1.2 Pressure | 氣壓變化

Original Text — Section 6.1.2

In cases where a low-pressure environment could present challenges to the package and product it contains, evaluation of the pressure differentials in a distribution chain could be critical. This is particularly relevant in cases of air shipment, where product may be stored in cargo areas with partial to nonexistent pressurization that, if left unevaluated, may result in unintended consequences. The ability of a sealed flexible container to resist creep or burst during air shipment is an example of risk that should be characterized.

Another example, one discussed with increasing frequency as the prevalence of prefilled syringes and prefilled syringe-based devices expands, is the concept of plunger movement. Although the degree to which plunger movement occurs is impacted by fill level, headspace volume, general package design, and pressure differentials experienced, how a low-pressure environment may impact product sterility and quality in these types of systems must also be characterized. As a plunger moves backward, the seal, and possibly the product, may move into an unsterile area of the container. In extreme cases, the plunger may completely exit the barrel.

6.1.3 Testing Approaches | 測試方法

Original Text — Section 6.1.3

Numerous documents from internationally recognized organizations (e.g., ASTM, ISTA, ICH, ISO) are available for reference and provide reproducible ways of evaluating some of these stressors, including physical shock events, thermal cycling, and pressure cycling. However, the simulated distribution chain should reflect the anticipated stressors. Standards are meant to employ reproducible simulations but may not account for the variations and intricacies of specific distribution networks, such as iterative air shipments or a combination of different modes of transport. As with the application of many test methods and techniques, a proper assessment of the risks should inform decisions on evaluating such risks.

6.2 100% Online Testing | 100%在線測試

In-line CCIT at fill/finish for every container | 充填封口線上對每個容器進行CCIT

Original Text — Section 6.2

A holistic approach to controlling CCI should be taken to ensure package integrity at the time of manufacture as well as over the shelf life of the product. This holistic approach includes consideration of:

  • CCIT method (in-line, near-line, off-line)
  • Quality of primary package components
  • Primary package design
  • Manufacturing process qualification
  • Product manufacturing process
  • Change control process
  • Shelf-life assessment

The CCIT method is a critical part of the control strategy, and an in-line test can make the overall approach more robust. However, the in-line test should not be the sole point of control.

In-line testing is defined as 100% testing of all filled and sealed primary packages for CCI during the production process, after the final sealing operation.

Currently, some regulatory requirements state that all container types fused and filled on the filling line (e.g., ampules, blow-fill-seal containers) must be 100% inspected. For other products, no current regulatory requirement exists for 100% in-line testing. The decision to conduct 100% in-line testing for other products should be based on risk assessment, as part of a holistic approach.

A package that is accepted at the in-line test station is not guaranteed to maintain its CCI during the shelf life and use of the product. Conversely, a package that is rejected by the in-line test station is guaranteed not to meet the product's CCI requirements. Therefore, the in-line test station acts as a filter for rejected packages, reducing the risk of CCI failures due to random process failure modes.

Consistent failure modes that are due to equipment malfunctioning or setup can be detected by other means, such as off-line quality control testing. A particular advantage of in-line CCIT compared to off-line quality control testing is the elimination (and cost savings) of handling suspect lots of products due to sample rejection at the off-line test station.

For high-speed lines, current technologies cannot test for the small-sized defects that off-line test methods can. However, since most process failure modes result in medium to large defects in the package, widening the reject limit for in-line testing is a reasonable and low-risk decision.

While it can add value in some situations, technology is not currently available to enable 100% in-line testing for all CCS and product types. Where feasible, regulations and quality risk management principles identified in ICH Quality Guideline Q9: Quality Risk Management should be used to determine if 100% in-line testing should be implemented.

Integrating 100% in-line testing into the manufacturing process requires a strong commitment. The in-line test station requires capital investment and the operational cost of the equipment, which adds to the cost of the product. Multiple test stations may be required on each filling line to overcome differences between line speeds and test station throughput.

No one test method fits all products. In-line test methods must be nondestructive for the specific product to be tested.

中文解說 — 第6.2節

100%在線測試（100% In-Line Testing）指在充填封口後，對生產線上每一個容器進行CCIT，不遺漏任何一個。這是「整體性CCI控制策略」的核心環節之一。

In-line測試的雙向邏輯

通過在線測試 ≠ 保證整個保存期限都維持CCI（後續仍可能因儲存、配送等因素失效）

未通過在線測試 = 確定不符合CCI要求（100%確定淘汰）

因此，在線測試的功能是「過濾器」，將確定缺陷品剔除，但無法保證通過者的長期完整性。

In-line vs Off-line：成本效益關鍵差異

  • Off-line測試失敗：整批產品被標記為「疑似批次」，需封存、重測、調查，成本高昂
  • In-line測試失敗：僅剔除該單一容器，不影響整批；即時反饋，生產繼續
  • 因此在線測試的最大優勢是消除疑似批次處理成本

比喻：機場安全檢查

100%在線CCIT就像機場安全門——每位旅客都必須通過，不抽樣。通過的人不代表一定沒問題（例如隱藏得很好的違禁品），但沒通過的人確定有問題。離線QC測試則像隨機抽查——效率高但存在漏網之魚的風險。

法規要求

目前歐盟GMP附件1（Annex 1, 2008/2022版）及EudraLex Volume 4明確要求：安瓿（Ampoules）及吹-充填-封（BFS）容器必須進行100%密封完整性檢驗。其他容器類型則依風險評估決定是否需要100%在線測試。

6.3 Building a Quality by Design (QbD) Approach into the CCIT Program | 將QbD導入CCIT計畫

Systematic, science-based, risk-driven framework for package integrity | 系統化、科學導向、風險驅動的包裝完整性框架

Original Text — Section 6.3

Pharmaceutical QbD is a systematic approach to development that begins with predefined objectives and emphasizes product and process understanding and control based on sound science and risk management. A QbD concept can be built into a CCIT program, including such elements as:

  • Quality target product profile that identifies the critical quality attributes (CQAs) of the product-package system
  • Package design and understanding, including identification of critical material attributes that can affect package integrity
  • Process design and understanding, including identification of CPPs, linking the material attributes and process parameters to the CQAs
  • Control strategy that includes specifications for the product-package configuration with respect to CCI as well as controls during each step of the manufacturing process
  • Regular quality checks on process capability and continual improvement initiatives

One of the ways users can build QbD into a CCIT program is to employ a risk-based approach. A risk-based CCIT program is built on science-based decisions; it offers an ongoing database of product lifecycle CCIT results (also called a package integrity profile) and a risk management tool for package integrity assurance throughout the lifecycle of the product. The program should demonstrate CCI as a function of ongoing, operative variations that takes into consideration:

  • Design and material of the package
  • Package assembly
  • Processing conditions
  • Storage, distribution, and stability conditions

Key aspects for building a risk-based CCIT program using QbD principles:

  • CCI over the entire lifecycle of product: The CCIT tool kit needs to demonstrate CCI over the entire lifecycle of the product. More than one test may be employed during a given product's lifecycle based on the critical parameters to be controlled at that stage.
  • Choice of deterministic versus probabilistic methods: Deterministic methods are those where the leakage event is based on phenomena that follow a predictable chain of events. Probabilistic methods offer qualitative outcomes subject to the probability of a series of events.
  • Choice of off-line versus in-line, on-line or at-line test methods
  • Sampling: Scientifically valid sampling plans should be based on risk assessment in association with appropriate statistical criteria.
  • Defining the MALL: CCI is established when a package meets its maximum allowable leak limit. Product quality requirements define the MALL.
  • Designing a CCIT tool kit: The CCIT tool kit should encompass different test methods that can be employed to detect defect types critical to product quality and sterility.
  • Ensuring continuous improvement: In the event of a change to package design, material, or manufacturing processing conditions, the risk-based strategy should trigger a review of the CCIT tool kit.

6.3.1 Risk Assessment | 風險評估

Original Text — Section 6.3.1

A risk-based approach using the quality tools mentioned in ICH Q9 can be a very effective way to assess sterile package integrity and testing applications. Following the lifecycle phases, relevant package integrity parameters are considered to determine product-package quality intent and how to evaluate the critical parameters that effectively impact the integrity of the CCS.

A lifecycle assessment process provides ample time and opportunity to develop improved learning, better understanding, and ongoing generation of compelling data to support the risk of package integrity failure. This QbD approach consists of learning, evaluation, and control strategy determination. The impact or risk of an integrity failure is relevant to both patient and product.

A first step in risk assessment (e.g., using the Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points (HACCP) quality tool) is to identify each incremental process step and the appropriate materials relevant to that step. Next, a verification of the potential risk of failure (i.e., a leak) at the process step is made and a list drawn up of causal factors that might lead to each failure type. Another list is developed that indicates what controls may exist that could mitigate the risks. Subsequently, the risk tool can be used to determine what types of testing (monitoring) are valuable, how frequently testing should be performed, and where or what types of samples should be taken for evaluation.

Similar to Six Sigma thinking (define-measure-analyze-improve-control), the risk assessment process is an ongoing methodology for quality improvement.

6.4 Bulk Container Lifecycle Approach | 大量容器生命週期方法

Single-use systems: from supplier manufacturing through user deployment | 單次使用系統：從供應商製造到使用者部署

Original Text — Section 6.4

Bulk containers go through two distinct phases of lifecycle: First, the development phase that encompasses material selection, design, and qualification and, second, the product phase, from manufacturing steps through supply chain to point of use. QbD and QRM principles can be applied throughout both phases, during which specific risks are identified and evaluated using appropriate methods.

Increasingly, according to the criticality of their application, users are asking suppliers to conduct testing, or choose to do it themselves to mitigate the risk of leakage or contamination. The materials used for these applications are engineered polymers that must be specified and controlled appropriately up the supply chain to ensure consistency of product quality.

For the flexible container manufacturer, test methods may differ between the phases of the lifecycle. At the supplier site, specific test methods are used during the design phase to establish the capability for integrity assurance. The right materials in a laminate structure are selected and the welds and seals are designed for the intended use. Other tests are applied during the manufacturing phase, such as those used for in-process controls.

  • 用戶端使用前、使用中、使用後測試
  • 最終產品無菌性的支持性佐證

使用前vs.使用後測試的監管意義

  • 使用前洩漏測試（Pre-use Leak Testing）：驗證接收後處理/搬運沒有損害完整性，補充供應商放行測試的保障
  • 使用後洩漏測試（Post-use Leak Testing）：支持最終產品無菌性聲明（Sterility Claim）——若SUS使用後仍完整，表示整個製程中無外部污染機會進入

比喻：從食材到餐桌的冷鏈管理

SUS的生命週期管理，就像高級餐廳從農場到餐桌的整個供應鏈管理。食材（SUS袋）從農場（供應商）出發，歷經冷藏運輸（入廠前）、廚房儲存（組裝存放）、使用前檢查（目視、漏水測試）、烹飪過程（生物製程使用），以及最後確認（使用後測試）。每個環節都有檢查點，任何一環失效都可能影響最終品質。

Optimization aimed to achieve maximum defect detection sensitivity while minimizing false rejection of good units.   • 陰性對照（Negative Controls）：已知無洩漏的包裝；用於驗證「方法不誤報好品（Accuracy）」

中文解說 — LOD結果分析

方法A的兩難困境與解法

方法A的LOD最佳（6 µm），但存在少量誤拒好品（False Positive）。PharmaCo-A提出兩個方向：

  • 方向一：降低靈敏度，減少誤拒率。LOD可能從6 µm變為接近6 µm，對於MALL評估影響有限
  • 方向二：維持原始設定，接受一定誤拒率，但為QA穩定性樣品建立「重複測試確認」協議，以避免虛假調查

方法C的調查教訓

第2天，3個20 µm缺陷未被偵測，原本會使方法C的LOD確認失敗。但後續調查發現這3個包裝的缺陷是堵塞的（Clogged），因此這些數據被合理排除。這展示了：數據異常應調查根本原因，而非直接廢棄數據。調查後，方法C的LOD可確認為10 µm，但日間結果差異仍引發可靠性疑慮。

LOD定義的統計邏輯（β-Content概念）

PharmaCo-A的LOD標準：
  - 在所有測試日
  - 最小尺寸子集中 ≥95% 的陽性對照被偵測
  - 且所有更大尺寸缺陷 100% 被偵測

這等同於統計容忍區間（Tolerance Interval）的概念：
β-Content（例如 95%）= 期望偵測比例
信心水準（Confidence Level）= 研究跨日數據的統計可信度

實際應用：
  LOD ≤ MALL → 方法被認可
  LOD > MALL → 方法靈敏度不足，需更換或優化

Final Outcome — Section 6.5

Method A: Final Evaluation

Method A was notably best at culling out defective packages. Two paths proposed: (1) adjust test method parameters slightly to reduce false reject risk with minimal impact on 6 µm LOD; (2) use as-is with replicate testing protocols for QA stability samples. PharmaCo-A continued to pursue Method A.

Method B: On Hold

No false rejections of good product, but LOD at ~20 µm — too large. Smaller defects capable of putting product quality at risk would likely be missed. Further work placed on hold given availability of more sensitive option.

Method C: On Hold

LOD confirmed at 10 µm after investigation. However, wide discrepancies in detection across test days caused questions about overall reliability. Further work placed on hold.

Method D: Eliminated

No detection limit could be defined. Method D was considered too insensitive and eliminated from further consideration.

Leak test Method A was finalized and implemented for routine manufacturing and QA stability study sample verification. PharmaCo-A obtained commercial product market approval. Ongoing Method A data and package component quality trends remained part of the package integrity profile database.

Best practices in pharmaceutical packaging suggest that all activities must be evaluated for risk to the product quality attributes and, by extension, to the patient. Step-by-step framework integrating MALL, risk assessment, method selection and lifecycle control | 整合MALL、風險評估、方法選擇與生命週期控制的逐步框架

Decision Framework: From Product to Validated CCIT Program

Building a complete CCIT program under QbD principles requires a structured decision process that begins with product quality needs and ends with a validated, lifecycle-spanning control strategy. The following framework integrates the concepts from sections 6.2, 6.3, and 6.5.

Step 1 — Define the QTPP and CQAs

Identify the product's Quality Target Product Profile. For a sterile injectable, key CQAs include: sterility, particulate matter absence, potency, pH stability. The CCS must support all CQAs throughout shelf life. This defines the starting point for integrity requirements.

↓

Step 2 — Determine the MALL

Based on the CQAs, determine the Maximum Allowable Leakage Limit (MALL). Common approaches: (a) microbial ingress studies to establish the leak size at which microbial contamination risk becomes unacceptable; (b) moisture vapor or oxygen transmission studies for moisture- or oxygen-sensitive products; (c) headspace pressure requirements for lyophilized products. The MALL is a specific physical parameter (e.g., equivalent helium leak rate, nominal defect diameter) that defines the integrity threshold.

↓

Step 3 — Identify Process Failure Modes (Risk Assessment)

Apply HACCP or FMEA to map each step of the CCS assembly, filling, and sealing process. For each step, identify: potential failure modes (e.g., under-crimped vial, cracked barrel, misaligned stopper), potential causes (worn tooling, operator error, component variability), and existing controls. Rank by risk priority number (RPN) or risk level. High-risk failure modes drive the choice of testing approach (100% vs. statistical sampling).

↓

Step 4 — Select CCIT Methods (Tool Kit Design)

Select methods whose LOD ≤ MALL. Criteria include: deterministic vs. probabilistic, destructive vs. nondestructive, in-line vs. off-line applicability, testing speed and throughput, compatibility with product and package materials, and regulatory acceptance. More than one method may be needed across different lifecycle stages (development, manufacturing, stability, post-distribution).

↓

Step 5 — Develop and Validate the Selected Method(s)

Follow the PharmaCo-A model: feasibility → optimization → LOD/accuracy determination → full validation. Establish LOD using positive controls (laser-drilled or characterized defects) and confirm accuracy using negative controls. Document predefined acceptance criteria. For in-line methods, qualify the equipment and establish alert/action limits.

↓

Step 6 — Define the Control Strategy and Sampling Plan

Determine: testing frequency, sample size (based on statistical confidence and risk level), acceptance criteria, and response to failures. Where feasible, consider 100% in-line testing for high-risk or regulatory-mandated container types. For other containers, design statistically valid sampling plans referencing applicable standards.

↓

Step 7 — Build the Package Integrity Profile Database and Continuous Improvement Loop

Collect and trend CCIT results over time. Include data from manufacturing, stability studies, post-distribution testing, and complaint investigations. Use this database to trigger reviews when process changes occur, to detect trends before failures become OOS events, and to continuously improve the control strategy as knowledge accumulates over the product lifecycle.

中文解說 — QbD決策流程逐步說明

將QbD整合到CCIT計畫，意味著將「測試品質（Test Quality In）」的被動思維，轉換為「設計品質（Design Quality In）」的主動思維。以下是七步決策框架的中文詳解：

步驟1 — 定義QTPP與CQAs

「品質目標產品概況（QTPP）」是QbD的起點。它定義了「這個產品最終要達到什麼標準」，而CQAs則是具體的品質屬性清單。在包裝完整性的語境下，CQAs包括：無菌性維持、防氧化保護、頂空氣體完整性等。

步驟2 — 確定MALL

MALL（最大允許洩漏限值）的設定方法取決於產品特性：

  • 注射劑（無菌要求）：透過微生物侵入研究，確定哪個孔徑尺寸允許微生物通過
  • 凍乾製品：依據殘留水分對效期的影響，設定水蒸氣滲透率限值
  • 氧敏感製品：依據氧化速率，設定氧氣滲透率限值
  • CO₂充填製品：依據頂空CO₂濃度需求，設定氣體洩漏率限值

MALL設定的直觀理解

MALL就像一個「紅線」：一旦包裝的洩漏程度超過這條線，產品品質就會受到不可接受的影響。你的測試方法LOD必須比這條線更靈敏——只有能「看到」紅線之前的問題，才算是有效的監控工具。

步驟3 — 失效模式分析的實務重點

在FMEA/HACCP分析中，包裝完整性最常見的高風險失效模式包括：

  • 注射劑瓶：壓蓋力不足（Residual Seal Force不達標）→ 滾邊/壓蓋製程為CPP
  • 預充填注射器：柱塞安裝不當→ 密封失效或劑量不準確
  • 單次使用袋：焊縫強度不足→ 蠕變或破裂
  • 卡式瓶：橡膠塞刺穿後復原性不佳→ 多次使用後洩漏

步驟7 — 持續改進的觸發機制

以下任何事件應觸發對CCIT工具組的審查：

  • 包裝組件或材料變更（新供應商、新批號規格）
  • 製程參數變更（壓蓋機調整、充填量變更、滅菌條件變更）
  • 穩定性結果出現趨勢（CCIT失敗率上升）
  • 客戶投訴或市場返回品的完整性問題
  • 法規更新（如EU GMP Annex 1 2022年修訂）

Deep Dive: Statistical Concepts in LOD — β-Content and Tolerance Intervals | 深度解析：LOD的統計學基礎

How to establish defensible detection limits using statistical tolerance intervals | 如何使用統計容忍區間建立可辯護的偵測極限

The Statistical Basis for LOD in CCIT

The detection limit (LOD) in leak testing is not a single-point measurement. It is a statistical statement about the probability of detecting a defined type of defect with defined confidence. Two key statistical concepts underpin the LOD determination as used in TR86's PharmaCo-A example:

Concept 1: β-Content Tolerance Interval

A tolerance interval specifies a range within which at least a proportion β (the "content") of the population falls, with a specified confidence level (1-α).

In CCIT LOD context:
  β-Content = proportion of defective packages
              of a given size that will be detected
              (e.g., 95% = at least 95% detected)

  Confidence Level = statistical confidence that
                     the β-content requirement is met
                     across repeated studies

PharmaCo-A's definition:
  LOD = smallest defect size where ≥95%
        of that size subset is detected
        on ALL test days (across operators,
        days, instruments)

This provides a conservative, reproducible
definition of method capability.

Concept 2: False Positive Rate (Method Accuracy)

False Positive = a good package (no defect)
                 incorrectly identified as leaking

In PharmaCo-A's study:
  Negative controls (n=300 per method)
  were tested across all days

  Method A: 0.3%, 0%, 0.7% false positives
  Methods B, C, D: 0% false positives

Implication:
  False positive rate affects:
  - Yield loss (good product rejected)
  - Investigation burden for QA samples
  - Confidence in positive findings

Acceptance criterion:
  All negative controls pass = 0 false
  positives → confirmed method accuracy

Concept 3: Sample Size Considerations

For LOD studies, sample size per defect
size subset affects statistical power.

PharmaCo-A used n=40 per defect size subset.

To detect 95% defect detection with
95% confidence, the minimum sample
size can be estimated using:

  n ≥ log(1 - CL) / log(β)
  where CL = confidence level (0.95)
        β = proportion to be detected (0.95)

  n ≥ log(0.05) / log(0.95) ≈ 58.4 → 59

Note: PharmaCo-A used n=40, which is
a commonly applied practical number.
Consult a statistician for formal sample
size justification in validation studies.

The number of test days and operators also contributes to the statistical robustness. PharmaCo-A used 3 days and 2+ operators, providing a preliminary estimate of intermediate precision (between-day, between-operator variability).

中文解說 — LOD的統計學基礎

LOD（偵測極限）在洩漏測試中是一個統計性聲明，而非單一測量值。理解其統計基礎對於正確設計和詮釋LOD研究至關重要。

β-Content 容忍區間的直觀理解

當我們說「方法的LOD為6 µm」，真正的意思是：

  • 如果你拿100個6 µm孔徑缺陷的包裝去測，
  • 在正常操作條件下（不同天、不同操作員），
  • 至少95個會被正確識別為洩漏。

這個「95%」就是β-Content。更嚴格的定義還會要求這個95%在統計上以95%的信心水準達成（雙重95%標準）。

比喻：金屬探測器的靈敏度設定

機場金屬探測器的LOD類比：「能100%偵測到10克以上金屬，對5克金屬偵測率達95%，對1克金屬偵測率約30%」。洩漏測試的LOD定義與此完全相同——它描述的是方法在不同缺陷「大小」下的偵測概率曲線，而LOD是在這條曲線上選擇一個可接受的操作點。

誤報（False Positive）的實務影響

方法A的誤報率雖然很低（<1%），但對於QA穩定性研究的影響不可忽視：

  • 穩定性研究通常樣本量少（每時間點可能只有3-6個），誤報一個就可能觸發OOS調查
  • OOS調查費時費力，且可能錯誤地質疑產品批次
  • 解決方案：要求陽性結果進行複測（Replicate Testing），並在每次疑似失敗後用已知好品（Master）驗證儀器狀態

LOD vs MALL：關鍵不等式

基本要求：
  LOD ≤ MALL

含義：
  方法必須能可靠地偵測到等於或
  小於MALL的缺陷

實例：
  若 MALL = 10 µm（等效名義孔徑）
  則所選方法的 LOD ≤ 10 µm

  方法A（LOD=6 µm）：合格 ✓
  方法B（LOD=20 µm）：不合格 ✗
  方法C（LOD=10 µm）：邊界合格，
    但需考慮日間穩定性 ⚠

安全裕度建議：
  實務上建議 LOD ≤ 0.5 × MALL
  以提供足夠的方法安全裕度

實際驗證研究設計建議

根據TR86的PharmaCo-A範例，設計LOD驗證研究的建議：

  • 陽性對照選擇：優先使用尺寸可驗證的人工缺陷（雷射鑽孔），範圍從預期LOD的0.5倍至2-3倍
  • 陰性對照規模：n≥200（TR86用n=300），確保對誤報率有足夠的統計靈敏度
  • 測試天數：至少3天（評估日間變異），每天使用新的陽性對照子集
  • 操作員數量：至少2名操作員（評估操作員間變異）
  • 預先定義標準：在研究開始前，以書面形式固定LOD接受標準

Distribution Simulation Standards Overview

The following outlines the major standards and their application to pharmaceutical packaging integrity testing during transportation and distribution:

Air Transport Pressure — Technical Detail

Commercial cargo aircraft cargo holds are typically pressurized to approximately 6,000–8,000 feet equivalent altitude, corresponding to an atmospheric pressure of approximately 0.75–0.80 atm (75–80 kPa). Some older or smaller aircraft may have unpressurized or partially pressurized cargo holds, resulting in pressures as low as 0.50–0.60 atm at cruising altitude.

Key considerations:

  • Pressure differential from sea-level manufacturing to cargo hold: approximately 20–25% reduction
  • Duration of exposure: may extend 6–12 hours for intercontinental flights
  • Combined effect with temperature changes in cargo holds (can drop below 0°C on some routes)
  • Pressure cycles: repeated pressurization/depressurization on multi-leg journeys create fatigue stress on closures

Prefilled Syringe Plunger Retraction — Calculation Example

For a prefilled syringe with:

  • Barrel inner diameter: 6.35 mm
  • Headspace volume: 0.1 mL (at sea level, 1 atm)
  • Fill volume: 1.0 mL

At 0.75 atm (aircraft cargo), the headspace volume expands per Boyle's Law:

V₂ = V₁ × (P₁/P₂) = 0.1 mL × (1.00/0.75) = 0.133 mL

Volume increase = 0.033 mL, corresponding to a plunger retraction of approximately 1.0 mm — potentially moving the plunger stopper into an unsterile zone of the barrel if the stopper travel clearance is insufficient.

中文解說 — 配送測試與航空氣壓詳解

選擇正確的配送模擬標準，是確保測試「反映真實壓力」的第一步。以下是各標準的中文實務解說：

ISTA vs ASTM：如何選擇？

  • ISTA 2A/3A：藥廠最常使用，特別是向美國FDA/EMA提交的包裝設計驗證。ISTA 3A更全面，含溫度調整條件，適合國際路線
  • ASTM D4169：美國工業界廣泛接受，依「危害概況（Assurance Level）」設計測試強度（Level I/II/III），彈性更高
  • ISO 4180：國際醫藥法規（如EU MDR/IVDR）較傾向引用，有助於歐盟申報

建議：視主要目標市場選擇對應標準，或同時執行ISTA和ASTM以涵蓋最廣監管範圍。

航空氣壓數字的直觀感受

0.75 atm的壓力相當於站在約2,500公尺（約8,200英尺）的高山上。您是否曾在登高時感到耳鳴（氣壓降低導致耳腔壓力失衡）？預充填注射器的柱塞，面對的就是類似的「壓力失衡」——只不過對象是頂空氣體，而後果可能影響藥品無菌性。

柱塞後退（Plunger Retraction）評估方法

評估預充填注射器在航空運輸壓差下的柱塞穩定性，常用以下方法：

  • 壓力腔（Altitude Chamber）測試：將PFS置於可控壓力腔，模擬從1 atm降至0.75 atm（或更低）的壓力循環，測量前後柱塞位移
  • X光或CT掃描：非破壞性地量化柱塞位置變化
  • CCIT前後對比：壓力循環前後進行CCIT，確認密封完整性未受影響
  • 外觀檢查：確認柱塞未完全脫出筒身

多模式運輸的累積壓力效應

對於一個從台灣運往美國再轉運歐洲的製藥產品，其配送路線可能涵蓋：

  1. 工廠→機場：陸運（振動、衝擊）
  2. 台灣→美國：空運（氣壓循環×1次）
  3. 美國倉儲：溫度波動
  4. 美國→歐洲：空運（氣壓循環×1次）
  5. 歐洲→配送中心：陸運（振動）

標準化測試只涵蓋其中部分場景，因此必須根據實際配送路線設計定制化測試方案。

思考練習

您的公司準備將一批預充填注射器（1 mL，液體充填）通過空運從台灣運往德國（途經香港）。請思考：

  1. 您會選擇哪個/哪些配送模擬標準？為什麼？
  2. 航空運輸部分，您最擔心的風險是什麼？如何設計評估方案？

  4. 溫度條件（冷藏2-8°C產品）如何影響整個測試設計？
