Topic A: Glove Monitoring — 手套監測

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: When and under what conditions should single-use operator gloves be monitored or changed?

Recommendation

The monitoring and changing of sterilized, single-use gloves should be based on the specific process and on the evaluation of the risk to product, product components, and product-contact surfaces.

There are several situations where monitoring (microbiological sampling) should be conducted:

  • After an intervention where inappropriate contact with product-contact surfaces may have occurred, such as a failure to adhere to first-air requirements or contact with surfaces instead of using an approved tool or device.
  • After critical interventions, for example after assembly of filling parts and line, open door intervention in RABS, and conventional fill line interventions.
  • Periodically, as defined by internal procedures (at minimum upon every exit from the critical zone or the aseptic area).
  • As deemed necessary based upon a specific process risk.
Monitoring of gloves should not occur immediately after sanitization, glove disinfection, and surface disinfection, because of the interference of disinfecting agent with the recovery of microorganisms.

Sterile gloves should be changed:

  • When there are indications of tears, rips, or gaps.
  • After an intervention or contact that may compromise the cleanliness of a glove and sanitization might not return the glove to the proper conditions.
  • After all finger-impression plating.
  • As deemed necessary based upon a specific process risk.

NOTE: Torn and ripped gloves during cleanroom operations should be recorded in the batch record; it may require a quality notification for product impact assessment if the damage occurred during critical manual operations.

Care should be taken when changing out gloves to ensure that required monitoring is occurring after use and before the change out.

Rationale

The primary source of microbial contamination of aseptic processing areas is personnel. Sterile gloves are used to prevent the transfer of contamination. Glove-changing and sanitization are key components of the contamination-control program. The purpose of monitoring is to assess the condition of the gloves after use. Sanitization of gloves directly before monitoring may compromise the integrity of this test by inhibiting microorganisms that may have been present. Therefore, procedures should specify the acceptable timing to allow a meaningful evaluation of glove contamination.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Glove Monitoring（手套微生物監測）：在操作員離開無菌區時，對其手套（尤其是指尖）進行接觸碟（contact plate）採樣，用以評估人員活動是否可能將微生物帶入 Grade A/B 區域。這是污染控制策略（Contamination Control Strategy, CCS）中人員管控的核心工具之一。

First-Air（第一道潔淨氣流）原則：在 HEPA 氣流的第一道通路上，不應有任何物體阻擋氣流到達產品或產品接觸表面。違反此原則即需立即執行手套監測。

為何不能在消毒後立即採樣？消毒劑（如酒精）會抑制培養基上的微生物生長，造成假陰性結果。因此 PDA 明確要求：採樣時機必須讓消毒劑充分揮發或由程序定義合理等待時間。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像一位廚師在處理生食後戴著廚房手套繼續備料——衛生稽核員要在廚師「實際操作後」採樣手套表面，而不是在廚師剛剛噴了酒精消毒之後馬上採樣。道理相同：消毒後採樣看到的是「消毒的效果」，而不是「真正的操作風險」。這正是 PDA 強調「採樣時機」的邏輯所在。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 最低頻率底線：每次離開無菌區（critical zone 或 aseptic area）時，至少進行一次手套採樣——這是不可低於的基準線。
  • 手套破損需記錄批次紀錄：若在關鍵手動操作期間發生手套破損，須出具品質通知（quality notification）並執行產品影響評估（product impact assessment）。
  • EU Annex 1 Section 9.25：明確要求在關鍵手動介入（critical manual interventions）後進行人員監測，並須納入 CCS 的依據文件中。
  • 換手套順序：先採樣（monitoring）→ 再換手套（change out），順序不可顛倒，否則失去監測意義。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 常見情境：一位操作員在充填線執行開門介入（open door RABS intervention）後，立即使用酒精噴手，然後在等待酒精揮發期間繼續下一個步驟。此時 SOP 必須明確規定：介入後採樣的時間點（例如：酒精噴灑後至少等待 X 分鐘方可採樣，或採用程序定義的其他可行方法）。若 SOP 未定義，查驗員將以此作為缺失開立。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一名操作員在執行充填零件組裝後，使用酒精消毒了手套，請問此時是否應立即進行手套採樣？為什麼？
  2. 若某操作員的手套在關鍵操作中被撕裂，最少應採取哪些後續行動？（請列出三項）
  3. 「每次離開無菌區時至少採樣一次」是 PDA 的最低要求，公司可以做得更頻繁嗎？依據是什麼？
Topic B: Aseptic Personnel Qualification Program — 無菌人員資格認定計劃

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: What is the process to qualify personnel to work in or access the aseptic processing area (Grade A and Grade B area)?

Recommendation — Overview

Companies should not rely solely on personnel meeting the Aseptic Personnel Qualification Program criteria or place an overreliance on the results of the APS to provide confidence on the efficacy of the aseptic process control strategy. Instead, companies should take steps to ensure that aseptic processes are designed to provide conditions that assist and permit personnel to perform aseptic manufacturing activities proficiently and that this demonstrated proficiency is maintained continuously. Hence, all relevant personnel should receive regular training and assessments.

The Aseptic Personnel Qualification Program should confirm that aseptic processing personnel have acquired general knowledge in background topics relevant to their job function and demonstrated their ability to reliably perform aseptic processing activities in a specified manner designed to prevent microbiological contamination of product.

Qualification Prerequisites

The Aseptic Personnel Qualification Program should specify the prerequisite criteria for qualification acceptance, which should incorporate the successful completion of:

  1. Basic GMP training
  2. Aseptic gowning procedure training, followed by a proficiency-based assessment that demonstrates competency, leading to gowning qualification or certification
  3. Aseptic technique and cleanroom behavior training
  4. Process understanding
  5. Awareness of the impact of their performance and relevant hygiene on product quality and patient safety
  6. Basic applied microbiology and contamination-control training
  7. Specific job functions, intervention procedures and practical training, with consideration of participation in, or review of, airflow visualization studies to reinforce effect and understanding of first-air principles
  8. Training in those aspects of the pharmaceutical quality system that affect their job function

NOTE: Watching video recordings of airflow visualization studies is very effective for increasing awareness of the impact of personnel movement and activities on the surrounding environment and the risk of contamination.

Initial Aseptic Processing Personnel Qualification

Personnel successfully meeting initial aseptic processing qualification criteria should be permitted to participate in defined aseptic processing activities under direct supervision. Personnel successfully completing initial qualification should be qualified to perform activities that are less complex and assessed to be of lower risk but should always occur under direct supervision of a person who has attained advanced qualification.

Initial Criteria — To be considered initially qualified, personnel should have no observed lapses in the following:

  1. Prerequisites-related criteria, including demonstration of gowning proficiency, aseptic technique, and job functions
  2. Demonstrated proficiency in aseptic technique by successfully completing a test involving exposure to nutrient media (can be associated with an APS or performed in a simulated aseptic environment)
  3. Continuous, successful performance of aseptic processing activities for a period of time deemed appropriate by the company

Advanced Aseptic Processing Personnel Qualification

Personnel successfully completing advanced aseptic processing qualification criteria should be permitted to participate in aseptic processing activities with a lower level of supervision and/or perform activities that do not require direct supervision, depending on assessed risk.

NOTE: High-risk interventions must always be supervised. Where a high-risk intervention is performed, the advanced personnel performing the intervention should be observed by another advanced operator to confirm that the intervention is performed correctly.

Advanced Criteria:

  1. Successful completion of initial qualification criteria, including prerequisites
  2. Successful completion of job training specific to advanced activities (e.g., activities assessed to be of a medium to high risk of contamination)
  3. Successful performance of simulated medium to high contamination-risk aseptic processes/activities outside of the APS for a period of time deemed appropriate by the company
  4. Successful participation in a process APS run in which the personnel perform the same function(s) to the same extent as they will perform during actual production

Ongoing Aseptic Processing Personnel Assessment

The ongoing personnel assessment program should be documented and should include:

  1. Specifying the frequency of and requirements for periodic requalification, or requalification after a prolonged absence
  2. Successful participation in APS runs at least once per year to maintain qualification status
  3. Consideration of personnel-monitoring results, personnel-related deviations and failure investigations, job performance reviews, observations, absence from aseptic process job functions, and other information in the determination of qualification status

Disqualification of Aseptic Processing Personnel

The Aseptic Personnel Qualification Program should include criteria by which personnel lose their qualification status, which may include:

  1. Failure to qualify or meet periodic qualification requirements
  2. Participation in an unsuccessful or failed APS, where their performance was identified as a cause or contribution to the failure
  3. Repeated aseptic processing performance in a manner deemed unacceptable in relation to cleanroom or aseptic-process operations or functions
  4. Failure to maintain gowning qualification
  5. Failure to maintain training requirements
  6. Repeated personnel monitoring excursions
  7. Extended periods of inactivity or absence from the aseptic processing area
  8. Other aspects of performance as identified by a supervisor (e.g., behavioral)

Requalification following Disqualification

Requalification criteria should depend on the reason and relative risk associated with the disqualification and may include:

  1. Completion of specific CAPA actions
  2. Retraining in certain related topics
  3. Repeat of gowning qualification
  4. Successful personnel monitoring
  5. Participation in an aseptic-processing demonstration or successful APS runs

Requalification after Prolonged Absence

Companies should establish and define the time period of a prolonged or extended absence that may affect the personnel's ability or proficiency to the degree it would require requalification.

Access without Prior Qualification

When situations arise in which nonqualified personnel are needed to enter into an aseptic processing area, a procedure should be in place to address the controls required. At minimum, the procedure controls should include:

  1. Types of situations where nonqualified personnel are permitted to enter the aseptic area
  2. Activities, including transit, these nonqualified personnel are permitted to perform
  3. Level of prior notice and approval required
  4. Level of initial training required
  5. Level of direct supervision required
  6. Level of observation, monitoring, and reporting required
  7. Level of documentation and recording required
  8. Any subsequent actions required

Rationale

Personnel qualification can be an effective means to establish personnel competency and exhibit skills that demonstrate the proficiency needed to reliably perform required activities. It is important that personnel qualifications are not merely a demonstration of performance in an APS run. The APS is not sensitive enough to determine if the person is proficient and capable of reliably performing aseptic processing activities. A phased approach can allow for personnel access under specified levels of supervision commensurate with the relative risk of the activities to be performed.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Aseptic Personnel Qualification Program（無菌人員資格認定計劃）：一套風險導向的分階段資格管理制度，確保進入 Grade A/B 區域的每位人員具備相應的知識、技能與操作紀律。

APS（Aseptic Process Simulation，無菌製程模擬）：俗稱「培養基灌裝」（media fill），是人員資格認定的重要工具之一，但 PDA 強調：APS 的靈敏度不足以單獨確認人員能力，必須搭配整體計劃。

Advanced Aseptic Processing Personnel (AAPP)：完成進階資格認定的人員，可在較低監督下執行中至高風險操作，但高風險介入仍須另一名 AAPP 現場觀察確認。

比喻說明 Analogy

初始資格 ≈ 飛行學員進入模擬艙：在正式獨立飛行前，學員必須先通過地面學科、模擬機訓練、低風險飛行任務——這與無菌操作員「先具備先決條件 → 在監督下執行低風險活動」的邏輯完全對應。

進階資格（AAPP）≈ 外科醫師的次專科認證：一般外科醫師（初始資格）能執行常規手術，但心臟外科（高風險）需要額外的訓練、認證，且在學習曲線上仍需資深醫師在旁觀察。PDA 的 AAPP 也是同樣的邏輯。

取消資格 ≈ 飛行員的航醫定期體檢：未通過體檢或出現重大違規，飛行員資格即被暫停，且復飛需要完整的重新評估程序——與 PDA 的 disqualification 和 requalification 流程對應。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 別過度依賴 APS：PDA 明確指出，APS 的靈敏度不足以單獨判斷人員是否真正具備無菌操作能力。APS 失敗才能「發現問題」，但 APS 成功不等於「人員一定合格」。
  • 每年至少一次 APS 參與：維持資格的最低頻率要求，錯過即可能導致資格狀態需重新評估。
  • 高風險介入雙重確認原則：即使是 AAPP，執行高風險介入時仍須另一名 AAPP 觀察確認——這是 PDA 的明確立場，與 EU Annex 1 的雙人確認精神一致。
  • 未資格人員進入 ≠ 禁止：PDA 提供了風險導向的例外程序框架（8 項最低控制要求），允許未資格人員在有充分管控下進入，但必須有書面程序與紀錄。

資格層級架構 Qualification Tier Structure

      ↓

  └─ 允許：低風險活動
  └─ 監督：全程直接監督（advanced person）

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 在承接新客戶產品時，常需要讓原本負責其他產品線的操作員轉至新充填線。此時應注意：若新充填線的操作風險等級（如引入 RABS 開門介入）高於該操作員過往認定範疇，則需重新評估其資格層級，並在轉換期間安排 AAPP 全程監督。此要求直接影響人力排班計劃與產能導入時程。

Topic C: Aseptic Processing Area Access Control — 無菌處理區進出管控

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: How should manufacturers control access of personnel into the aseptic preparation and processing area?

(This section primarily addresses Grade A and B critical zones but should also address access to certain Grade C areas. A risk assessment could be utilized to determine the risk for Grade C areas.)

Recommendation

Control of access to the aseptic processing area should be in place through both procedures and physical controls. Preferably, this could be achieved during operations via electromechanical controls, such as access-card readers or biometric readers. Procedural controls may be appropriate in some operations, but justification for this practice should be documented.

A dated and timed record should be maintained of all persons entering and leaving the aseptic processing area, subject to routine review by Quality Assurance personnel.

Rationale

Each person entering the aseptic processing area has the potential to introduce microbiological contamination. For this reason, controls should be in place to ensure that only designated persons access the aseptic processing area and a record of entrances and exits should be maintained for traceability and investigation, if necessary. The recording of time and date access is also important for data integrity considerations as it provides for accuracy and contemporaneousness of information.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

進出管控的雙重層次：PDA 要求同時具備「程序性管控（Procedural Controls）」與「實體/機電管控（Physical/Electromechanical Controls）」。前者是 SOP，後者是門禁卡或生物辨識系統。兩者缺一不可，若僅依賴 SOP 而無實體管控，需有文件化的風險評估理由。

進出紀錄的雙重用途：① 偏差調查時追溯「當時誰在場」；② 數據完整性（Data Integrity）要求——時間與日期的準確性確保紀錄的「同步性（contemporaneousness）」。

比喻說明 Analogy

無菌處理區的門禁系統，就如同手術室的刷手進出管制——不是任何人拿了一件無菌衣就能進去的，必須是指定的、訓練過的人員，且每次進出都有紀錄。當術後發生感染事件時，這份進出紀錄就是追溯調查的關鍵依據。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 電子門禁優先：PDA 建議「優先（preferably）」使用電子門禁，若使用程序性管控代替，需有書面正當理由——查驗員會特別注意這點。
  • QA 定期審查：進出紀錄不只是存檔，須由品保（QA）人員定期審查。若某批次出現無菌偏差，QA 需能快速調取完整的人員進出歷史記錄。
  • Grade C 亦適用（風險評估為基礎）：雖本節主要針對 Grade A/B，但 PDA 明確說明某些 Grade C 區域也應考慮進出管控，視風險評估結果而定。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一家公司的 Grade B 走廊目前採用「人工簽到簿」管控進出，這樣是否符合 PDA 要求？需要做什麼補充說明？
  2. 為何進出紀錄的「時間與日期」對數據完整性（Data Integrity）如此重要？如果時鐘不準確會有什麼問題？
Topic D: Performance of Environmental Monitoring — EM 由誰執行

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: Who should perform EM?

Recommendation

Trained and qualified quality or production personnel can conduct EM (Environmental Monitoring).

The personnel performing the monitoring should receive training in good sampling practices, employing internal audits, unannounced monitoring, or random verification sampling by an independent quality unit to assess adherence to sampling practices.

The Quality Unit is accountable for the effectiveness of the design and the performance of the EM system and is responsible for reviewing the data and ensuring the adequacy of the monitoring, including periodic observation according to approved procedures.

Rationale

As with any procedure, individuals performing monitoring should be trained in the specific procedures. The goal is also to minimize the number of persons in the aseptic area and the concurrent risk of contamination. As long as there are appropriate safeguards (i.e., effective training and periodic sampling oversight) in place, individuals from the production or Quality Unit may perform the sampling activity. However, it is the Quality Unit that is responsible for the oversight of the EM.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

EM 執行者 vs. EM 監督者：PDA 的立場是：「執行（perform）EM」可以是生產人員或品保人員；但「對 EM 系統設計與成效負責（accountable）」的永遠是 QA。這是職責分工的重要區分。

為何允許生產人員執行 EM？主要考量是「減少進入無菌區的人員數量」——每多一個人進入 Grade B，就多一個污染風險源。若生產操作員已在場，讓他們順帶採樣（在訓練合格前提下）可降低整體人員佔用數。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像醫院的感染監測——護理師（生產人員）可以執行環境採樣，但感染管控委員會（QA）負責審查整體監測計劃的有效性，並對異常數據進行調查。護理師採樣不等於護理師「自我監管」——QA 的獨立審查確保了客觀性。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 「利益衝突」疑慮的 PDA 回應：常有人質疑「操作員自己採樣是否有利益衝突？」PDA 的回應是：只要有適當的防護措施（訓練 + QA 不定期抽查），生產人員執行 EM 是可接受的。
  • 不定期採樣（Unannounced Monitoring）：PDA 特別提到應納入不定期採樣，以防止操作員在「知道今天要被採樣」時臨時改善行為，確保採樣結果代表真實操作狀態。
  • QA 仍需定期現場觀察：根據程序，QA 須定期到現場觀察採樣執行情況——文件審查不能取代現場確認。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若一家公司的 EM 完全由生產部門執行，QA 僅審查最終報告，這樣符合 PDA 要求嗎？哪個環節可能被查驗員質疑？
  2. 「不定期採樣」的目的是什麼？如果所有採樣時間都事先告知操作員，可能帶來什麼風險？
Topic E: Supervision in the Aseptic Processing Area — 無菌區監督

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: What is the degree of oversight necessary to effectively monitor an aseptic processing area?

Recommendation

Oversight of aseptic processing should be performed by individuals who are trained, qualified, and experienced in the aseptic procedures for the areas being reviewed. These persons should have a thorough understanding of the process and the potential contamination risks. This oversight is best performed by physical presence in these areas with consideration on limiting microbial contamination risks (e.g., by monitoring the areas through windows or cameras when possible) as a result of additional supervisory personnel present in the aseptic processing area.

Aseptic processing areas that contain viewing windows may allow observation of some aspects of aseptic processing from outside the aseptic processing area.

The degree of oversight needed may depend on the level of physical separation of the operators from the exposed products and product-contact surfaces and the level of process automation.

Proper oversight is required but not limited to cleaning, maintenance, production on all shifts, or any activity that can negatively impact the aseptic conditions within the aseptic processing area.

The Quality Unit is also responsible for reviewing the oversight program for the aseptic area.

Rationale

Maintaining a successful aseptic operation is dependent on operational discipline and on the conduct of personnel in the way that they have been trained to perform, regardless of internal or external factors that may negatively influence their performance (e.g., pressure to complete a manufacturing run in a short time). Manufacturers should be able to demonstrate that operational discipline is maintained, and oversight is a key methodology to achieve this.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Supervision（監督）≠ 單純的旁觀：PDA 強調監督者必須是「訓練過且合格的」——不是任何一個主管都能擔任無菌區的監督角色。監督者必須能識別操作中的風險行為並即時糾正。

物理存在 vs. 遠端監看：PDA 的立場是「以現場存在為優先」，但也認可透過觀察窗或攝影機進行遠端監看，以減少額外人員進入 Grade B 帶來的污染風險。這是一個平衡：監督品質 vs. 人員數量控制。

自動化程度影響監督需求：高自動化生產線（如隔離器系統）人員介入少，監督需求也相對不同；傳統手動充填線則需要更密集的監督覆蓋。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像航空的飛航安全督察（Flight Safety Inspector）——不能只看飛行紀錄表（文件），必須定期上機觀察飛行員的實際操作行為。同樣地，無菌區的監督必須能覆蓋「所有班次（all shifts）」，包括夜班與週末，因為生產壓力往往在非標準時段最大，此時操作規範最容易鬆弛。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 監督需涵蓋所有活動，包括清潔與維修：PDA 特別點名 cleaning 和 maintenance——這些活動往往由承包商執行，卻常被忽視監督。查驗員會特別詢問「非生產時段的無菌區活動如何被監督」。
  • 「生產壓力」是操作規範的隱性殺手：PDA 在 Rationale 中點出——外部壓力（如要趕上交貨時間）是操作人員偏離標準的主因。監督的存在本身就是一種「正強化」，提醒每個人規範的重要性。
  • QA 負責審查整體監督計劃：QA 不只是審查 EM 數據，更要確保監督計劃本身的完整性與執行情況。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一家公司在白班有合格主管監督，但夜班僅由「資深操作員」自主監控——這是否符合 PDA 的監督要求？
  2. 隔離器（Isolator）系統是否仍需要人員在場監督？為什麼自動化程度會影響監督密度？
Topic F: Personnel Monitoring Frequency and Location — 人員監測頻率與位置

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: What should be the frequency and sampling points of aseptically gowned personnel monitoring?

Recommendation

Monitoring of personnel in aseptic processing areas should be based on the evaluation of risk to products, product components, and product-contact surfaces and to the specific process and systems (i.e., conventional, RABS, and isolator aseptic processing lines).

The sampling plans should be dynamic with monitoring frequencies and sampling locations and may be adjusted based on trend analysis. It is appropriate to increase or decrease sampling frequency based on this performance as long as a documented rationale is provided. Consideration should be given to the inclusion of periodic, unannounced personnel monitoring.

Sampling locations should be determined based on a risk evaluation and should be adequate to assess the contamination potential. For example, monitoring of gloves, forearms, and chests may be considered in filling lines and after such activities as assembly or open-door interventions of RABS, because these areas might be in the proximity of the exposed products or product-contact surfaces during interventions.

Minimally, gloves should be monitored each time an employee leaves an aseptic area (as noted in Section IV, Topic A: Glove Monitoring), and gown integrity should be checked upon entry and exit.

If a gown breach is identified, the following should be performed:

  • A quality notification/root cause analysis
  • Product impact assessment
  • Additional monitoring at the site of the breach for investigational purposes, if considered relevant

Gown-sampling points may require monitoring as applicable to the process, and monitoring is required after critical manual interventions as justified in the CCS (EU Annex 1, Section 9.25).

Rationale

Monitoring of gowning serves as a tool to demonstrate that personnel are adequately trained and are following procedures. Therefore, it is not necessary to monitor personnel to the same extent as they would be monitored during gowning certification every time they exit the aseptic processing area. While gowning certification locations are geared towards detecting whether personnel are able to appropriately don the garment, routine monitoring is geared toward understanding the aseptic behaviors in the area and if there were any risks to the aseptic process or product.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

動態採樣計劃（Dynamic Sampling Plan）：人員監測的頻率與位置不是一成不變的，必須根據趨勢分析（trend analysis）定期回顧與調整。歷史監測結果良好可為降低頻率提供依據；出現異常則應升高頻率。

日常監測 vs. 穿衣認定：穿衣認定（gowning certification）在乎的是「能否正確穿戴」，因此採樣點廣泛而全面；日常監測關注的是「操作期間是否維持無菌行為」，因此重點放在與產品接觸風險最高的部位（如手套、前臂）。

比喻說明 Analogy

駕駛執照考試（穿衣認定）考的是「你會不會開車」；日常交通違規取締（日常監測）是看你平常是否真的遵守交通規則。通過考試不代表你以後不會超速——監測是確保行為持續合規的工具，而非重複測試能力。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 手套是最低監測底線：每次離開無菌區至少採手套——無論採樣計劃如何設計，這是不可低於的基準。
  • EU Annex 1 Section 9.25 明確要求：關鍵手動介入後必須進行人員監測，且必須在 CCS 中有依據。CDMO 在撰寫 CCS 時必須納入此要求。
  • 穿衣破損（Gown Breach）的三步驟回應：品質通知 + 產品影響評估 + 破損位置的補充監測（視情況）——三步缺一都可能在查驗時被開立缺失。
  • 前臂與胸部的採樣邏輯：在執行充填零件組裝或 RABS 開門介入時，前臂和胸部最可能接近暴露的產品或接觸表面，因此這些位置在高風險操作後應納入採樣範圍。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 某 CDMO 的人員監測計劃目前只要求採手套指尖，不採前臂或胸部。這樣是否符合 PDA 要求？在什麼情況下需要擴大採樣位置？
  2. 趨勢分析顯示某操作員過去 12 個月的手套監測均為 0 CFU，公司可以降低其採樣頻率嗎？需要做什麼文件支撐？
Topic G: Sterile Gown Usage — 無菌衣的使用規定

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: Must a sterile gown be used for each entry into the aseptic processing area?

Recommendation

A sterile gown must be used for each entry into the aseptic processing area. A sterile gown is defined as a gown from a sealed package that has not been previously worn since sterilization. A sterile gown should be changed whenever the integrity of the gown might be compromised.

The maximum time usage during a single entry of the sterile gown and maximum number of times a single gown can be washed and sterilized should be determined and defined.

Rationale

The major source of microbial contamination of aseptic processing areas is personnel. Gowning is the most direct and significant environmental control measure that can be employed to control contamination derived from personnel. During the time the sterile gown is worn for each usage, the efficiency of the sterile gown decreases, therefore, the length of time the gown is worn must be controlled. In addition, repeated washing and sterilization of the gown causes degradation, therefore, the number of sterile gown processing cycles must be controlled.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

「每次進入必須使用新的無菌衣」：這是 PDA 的明確立場——即使只是短暫離開後再返回，也需要更換無菌衣。「新的」的定義是：來自密封包裝、自滅菌後未曾穿用過的無菌衣。

無菌衣的三個使用限制：

  • 單次穿用最長時間（maximum time per entry）
  • 最大洗滌與滅菌循環次數（maximum wash/sterilization cycles）
  • 完整性受損時立即更換

這三項限制都必須由驗證數據支撐，並在 SOP 中明確規定。

比喻說明 Analogy

無菌衣就像一次性外科手術巾（surgical drape）——每次手術都用新的，不會因為上台時間短就重複使用。反覆洗滌會使布料纖維鬆弛、阻隔效果下降，就像反覆洗滌的口罩過濾效率會逐漸降低一樣。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 可重複使用 vs. 一次性無菌衣：PDA 並未規定必須使用一次性無菌衣，但對可重複使用的無菌衣，必須驗證其洗滌與滅菌程序，並定義最大循環次數。滅菌方式（高壓蒸汽滅菌、環氧乙烷、伽瑪射線）均需驗證效果。
  • 「密封包裝」是關鍵定義：從洗衣房取回的無菌衣若包裝已被打開或破損，不得視為無菌衣使用，需重新滅菌。
  • 穿用時間的驗證：必須有數據支撐「最長穿用時間」的設定——例如粒子脫落測試或微生物監測數據，顯示在規定時間內無菌衣的屏障效果仍然充分。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一名操作員因更換手套需要短暫離開 Grade B 區域，返回時是否需要換上全新的無菌衣？根據是什麼？
  2. 如果一件無菌衣的「最大洗滌次數」被定義為 50 次，但公司只進行了洗滌效果驗證而未驗證滅菌效果，這樣是否合規？
Topic H: Occupancy in the Aseptic Processing Area — 無菌區人員佔用數量

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: What is the proper number of people to be present in an aseptic processing area?

Recommendation

The number of personnel required to safely and effectively perform and supervise aseptic processing activities should be determined and defined. The number of required personnel will depend on the type of activity being performed. This number may represent a maximum or a minimum number of people required.

Only the number of personnel required to perform and supervise the ongoing aseptic processing activities should be present in the respective aseptic processing areas. This also applies to ancillary areas such as airlocks and corridors.

The maximum number of qualified personnel allowed in a given room should depend on the classification of the area and should be verified through EM and process simulations.

Rationale

Personnel constitute the greatest risk to an aseptic manufacturing process from a microbiological perspective; however, the number of people must be adequate to perform the process with a proper level of attention and activity consistent with good aseptic technique. In general, the minimal number of personnel should be present to carry out such aseptic manufacturing processes.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

最大人數 vs. 最小人數：PDA 提出了一個重要的雙向思考——不只是「不超過最大人數」，也要確保「至少有足夠的人數安全執行操作」。人太多增加污染風險；人太少可能導致操作失誤或監督不足。

最大人數的驗證方式：必須透過環境監測（EM）和製程模擬（APS/Process Simulation）來驗證特定人數下無菌區的環境條件是否仍然符合標準——這不是靠估算，而是靠數據。

EU Annex 1 的「最少人員原則」：EU Annex 1 明確提出「minimum personnel（最少人員）」的要求，與 PDA 的立場完全一致——應在確保操作安全的前提下，將無菌區人員數量降到最低。

比喻說明 Analogy

手術室的手術人員配置：太少——主刀醫師缺乏支援，手術風險增加；太多——室內人員走動頻繁，感染風險上升。標準手術的最佳人員配置是通過臨床實踐與感染管控數據共同決定的，而非隨意估算。無菌充填線的最大人員數量也是同理——需要透過數據驗證，而非經驗直覺。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 走廊和氣閘也適用：PDA 特別說明，人員數量管控不只適用於充填間，也適用於「輔助區域如氣閘和走廊（airlocks and corridors）」。
  • 最大人數需以 EM 和 APS 驗證：這代表在生產驗證階段（PPQ），應包含在最大允許人數下進行的 APS，以確認無菌條件仍然成立。
  • CDMO 換產影響：不同產品的充填操作複雜度不同，所需人員數量可能不同。CDMO 在切換產品時，若人員配置有重大改變，需評估是否需要更新 APS 驗證範疇。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一家公司的充填間 SOP 規定「最多 6 人」，但從未說明「最少幾人」——這樣是否有潛在的法規缺失？
  2. 若公司在 APS 驗證時以 4 人入場，但實際生產有時需要 6 人，這樣是否合規？如何處理？
Topic I: Personnel Practices for Hygiene and Hand Washing — 人員衛生與洗手規定

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement: What are the allowable personnel-related conditions for gowning and entering a cleanroom?

Recommendation

Personnel entering cleanrooms during periods of operation should be free of conditions that pose a high risk of compromise to the purity and safety (i.e., sterility) of the product. Personnel should demonstrate a high degree of personal hygiene, cleanliness, and precautions, including but not limited to the following:

  • Personnel should not wear jewelry (i.e., rings, bracelets, piercings, or wristwatches) or other items that could tear gloves, gowns, boots, hoods, or masks or could otherwise pose a safety hazard or process contamination risk.
  • Personnel garments worn under the gown should not contribute fiber or microbiological contamination beyond the ability of the gown or boots to contain it.
  • Personnel should wear garments under gowns that minimize perspiration yet are sufficient for personal comfort and warmth.
  • Personnel should wear dedicated shoes or footwear that are not worn outside of the cleanroom area.
  • Dedicated cleanroom socks need to be worn. If worn, personnel should remove personal socks prior to donning dedicated cleanroom socks.
  • Personnel should not wear boots or shoes under gown boots that could tear the gown or contribute contamination.
  • Personnel should not wear perfume, makeup, volatile chemicals, or flaking substances that could contaminate products.
  • Personnel who require visual aids and work routinely in the cleanroom should be allocated prescription glasses that can be sanitized without degradation. Transient visitors should water-and-soap wash their glasses at the hand sanitation station and not approach the critical zone.
  • Personnel should wash and sanitize their hands prior to gowning but not use types of soap or sanitizing agents that dry the skin or cause skin to flake.
  • Personnel should have open cuts, abrasions, or freshly applied tattoos on their skin covered with bandages or other means and should not enter the aseptic processing area if it is determined that the extent of those conditions could still pose risks of environmental or product contamination.
  • Personnel with respiratory illnesses, open lesions, or other potentially infectious conditions that could pose a higher risk of environmental or product microbiological contamination and the risk of spreading contagious pathogens should not be permitted in the aseptic area.

Rationale

Personnel represent a potential source of microbiological and chemical contamination. Therefore, precautions should be employed to minimize the risk of contaminating the environment or the product. These precautions should be commensurate with the level of risk of contamination. These precautions should take into consideration the risk to product quality with personnel safety and the ability of people to perform their work functions.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

人員是微生物與化學雙重污染源：人體會持續脫落皮屑（skin shedding）、呼出微粒、產生汗液——這些都是 Grade A/B 環境的潛在污染源。化學污染（香水、彩妝、揮發性物質）可能影響產品品質，尤其對蛋白質藥物或高活性製劑更為敏感。

手部衛生的雙重考量：洗手並消毒是防止微生物污染的關鍵步驟；但 PDA 特別提醒，肥皂或消毒劑若會使皮膚乾燥或脫皮，反而會製造更多粒子——選用適當的手部護理產品本身也是污染管控的一部分。

比喻說明 Analogy

無菌區進入前的身體狀況要求，就像手術前外科醫師的刷手規定——不只是洗手，而是一整套系統：不能戴戒指（可能藏匿細菌且可能刺破手套）、不能有傷口（感染風險）、有傳染性疾病不得上台（呼吸道飛沫污染）。這些規定的邏輯是一樣的：人體本身就是潛在的污染媒介，必須在進入高度受控環境前做好完整的屏障準備。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 珠寶、穿孔（piercings）：不只是微生物風險，更可能撕裂手套或無菌衣，導致屏障失效。SOP 需明確禁止所有首飾，包括耳環與臍環等不易察覺的穿孔。
  • 皮膚脫落（Skin Shedding）的控制邏輯：避免使皮膚乾燥的洗手劑，是為了減少「皮膚屑」的產生。這是一個常被忽略的微粒污染來源，在粒子計數不合格的調查中值得納入考量。
  • 新鮮刺青（Freshly Applied Tattoos）：PDA 特別提到新鮮刺青——這是一個現代化的細節，顯示標準必須跟上實際操作環境中人員的真實情況。
  • 呼吸道疾病人員不得入場：這不是建議，而是禁止（should not be permitted）——感冒、流感期間的操作員不應進入無菌區，公司的 SOP 必須明確規定此點，並有執行記錄。
  • 眼鏡的衛生要求：長期在無菌區工作的眼鏡族應分配可以消毒的專用眼鏡；一次性訪客應在洗手站清潔眼鏡，且不應接近關鍵區域。

人員衛生管控清單 Hygiene Control Checklist

進入無菌區前的人員狀況核查：

禁止項目：
  ✗ 所有珠寶（戒指、手鐲、穿孔、手錶）
  ✗ 香水、彩妝、指甲油、揮發性化學物質
  ✗ 個人鞋（需更換潔淨室專用鞋）
  ✗ 個人鞋襪（需更換專用潔淨室機能襪）
  ✗ 可能刮破無菌衣的外衣或鞋款

要求項目：
  ✓ 穿衣前洗手 + 消毒（使用不致皮膚乾燥的製品）
  ✓ 開放性傷口、新鮮刺青：覆蓋後評估風險
  ✓ 呼吸道疾病、開放性病灶：禁止入場
  ✓ 眼鏡：使用可消毒的專用眼鏡
  ✓ 貼身衣物：最小化纖維與汗水產生

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 常見問題：冬季感冒高峰期，如何確保操作員不帶病進入無菌區？實務建議包括：在更衣室設置體溫量測站、建立「自我申報」文化（操作員知道請假不會影響績效評分）、確保備用人員資格充足避免人力壓力導致帶病上班。這些「軟性管理」措施是衛生規定真正落地的關鍵。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一名操作員在進更衣室前發現手上有一個未完全癒合的小切傷，並用 OK 繃覆蓋後繼續穿衣準備入場——這樣是否符合 PDA 要求？需要做什麼評估？
  2. 為什麼使用「會使皮膚乾燥的洗手液」是個問題？這與粒子污染有什麼關聯？
  3. 一名操作員有輕微流鼻水（可能是過敏）卻堅持要進入無菌區執行緊急充填任務，作為主管，你應該如何處理？你的判斷依據是什麼？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary — IV. Personnel (p77–p93)

  • Topic A 手套監測：每次離開無菌區至少採一次手套；消毒後不得立即採樣；手套破損須記錄批次紀錄並進行產品影響評估。
  • Topic B 人員資格計劃：分階段管理（初始→進階），APS 是工具而非唯一判斷依據；每年至少一次 APS 維持資格；高風險介入永遠需要雙人確認。
  • Topic C 進出管控：電子門禁（卡片/生物辨識）優先；所有人員進出須留有時間戳記紀錄；QA 定期審查。
  • Topic D EM 執行者：生產或 QA 人員均可執行採樣，但 QA 對 EM 系統設計與有效性負最終責任；需含不定期監測。
  • Topic E 監督：監督者必須是合格且有經驗的人員；需涵蓋所有班次與所有活動（包括清潔與維修）；可使用觀察窗或攝影機輔助遠端監看。
  • Topic F 監測頻率與位置：手套為最低基準；採樣計劃應動態調整；關鍵介入後需採前臂、胸部等高風險位置；EU Annex 1 Section 9.25 明確要求。
  • Topic G 無菌衣：每次進入必須使用新的無菌衣；最大穿用時間與最大洗滌滅菌循環次數須有數據支撐並定義在 SOP 中。
  • Topic H 人員佔用：「最少人員原則」是核心；最大人數需以 EM 和 APS 驗證；氣閘與走廊同樣適用人數管控。
  • Topic I 衛生與洗手：禁止珠寶、彩妝、香水；洗手劑選擇影響皮膚脫皮風險；呼吸道疾病人員不得入場；傷口覆蓋後仍需風險評估。
