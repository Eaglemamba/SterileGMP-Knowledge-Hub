§ V. Material Transfer — 物料傳遞
Topic A: Entry of Equipment and Material into an Aseptic Processing Area

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
What must be done when materials and/or equipment are being transferred into a Grade A and Grade B aseptic processing area?

Recommendation
Sterilized materials and equipment should be transferred into the Grade A and Grade B aseptic processing area using the following methods and taking these precautions:

  • The preferred method for transferring sterilized materials and/or equipment into an aseptic processing area is by using a unidirectional sterilization process (e.g., through an attached double-door autoclave or a depyrogenation tunnel) where materials or equipment cannot be sterilized directly into Grade A, they must be transferred into and through the Grade B area while being protected from particulate and microbiological contamination e.g., multiple wrapping.
  • Pre-sterilized items that are transferred into the Grade A and Grade B area should be contained in sealed packaging and disinfected prior to transfer into the Grade B area and subsequent transfer into the Grade A area. The integrity of the packaging should be qualified and visually verified prior to use. The packaging material should be compatible with the disinfecting method and agent. Multiple layers of protective wrapping material should be sequentially removed as the items are transferred from areas of lesser to greater control (e.g., Grade C to Grade B, Grade B to Grade A). Where possible, the use of rapid transfer port (RTP) technology should also be considered.
  • Items that have been packaged with multiple sterile packaging layers need not be stored in a cleanroom if the integrity and configuration of the packaging allows the items to be disinfected during transfer into grade A (e.g., by use of multiple layers that can be removed at each transfer from areas of lesser to greater control).
  • Where materials and/or equipment are sterilized in sealed packaging and then transferred into the area, this must be done, for example, via airlocks or pass-through hatches with accompanying disinfection of the exterior of the transfer packaging.
  • For materials and/or equipment that are necessary for aseptic processing but are not amenable to sterilization (e.g., lubricant containers or electronic instruments), an effective and validated disinfection process must be in place. If possible, equipment should remain in the aseptic processing area to minimize the risk of repeated transfers.
  • Only items that are on an approved list should be allowed to be transferred into the Grade A and Grade B area via the air lock. Any unapproved items that require transfer should be covered by a deviation that includes a specific sanitization and monitoring regime derived by consultation with quality assurance and microbiology personnel.

Rationale
Materials and equipment entering a Grade A and Grade B area may pose a risk of introducing microbiological contamination. Therefore, precautions must be taken to reduce the risk of contamination of the sterilized materials and equipment and of the Grade A and Grade B environment.

References
1. European Commission. Annex 1: Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products, EudraLex – Volume 4 (2022).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：物料進場的三大方式

單向滅菌通道 (Unidirectional Sterilization Process)：最理想的方式。雙門高壓滅菌釜（Double-door Autoclave）或去熱原隧道（Depyrogenation Tunnel）將滅菌區與無菌區直接連通，物料「進去就是無菌的」，無需穿越暴露環境。

多層包裝脫層法 (Sequential Wrapper Removal)：每進入一個更高級別的區域，就剝去一層外包裝——Grade C→B 脫第一層，Grade B→A 脫第二層。每一層都是一道屏障，也代表一次消毒機會。

快速傳遞口 (RTP, Rapid Transfer Port)：隔離器（Isolator）與 RABS 常用的技術，採用 alpha/beta 法蘭系統，在不破壞無菌環境的前提下傳遞物料。EU Annex 1 2022 鼓勵使用。

比喻：手術室的刷手流程

這就像外科醫師進手術室的程序：換鞋進更衣室→刷手消毒→穿無菌手術衣。每一步都有對應的「脫去一層汙染屏障」的動作。Grade C→B→A 的多層包裝脫除，正是同樣的邏輯——用空間梯度來替代時間梯度，確保每一步驟都在受控條件下完成。

RTP 則像「手套箱傳遞窗」——外面的手不能進來，但物品可以安全通過。

重點提示：「核准清單」是查核重點

稽查人員特別注意：只有「核准清單」上的物品才能進入 Grade A/B 氣鎖。清單之外的物品必須走偏差程序，並由 QA 與微生物學人員共同制定特定的消毒與監測方案。這一要求表示，CDMO 必須維護一份即時更新的「核准物料清單」，且清單變更需有版本控管。

EU Annex 1 2022 Section 4.26–4.30 對物料傳遞有詳細要求，特別強調 VHP（氣化過氧化氫）去汙對進入隔離器的物料的重要性。

實務應用：CDMO 物料管理 SOP 設計

在 CDMO 環境中，每個客戶產品可能用到不同的耗材與設備。建議在物料管理 SOP 中明確：

  • 核准物料清單（附核准消毒方法）
  • 非常規物料的偏差觸發條件與快速審批流程
  • 包裝完整性目視檢查的培訓記錄要求
  • 電子儀器（無法高壓滅菌）的驗證消毒替代方案（如酒精擦拭程序確效）

Topic B: Sterile Hold Times for Materials

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
How should sterilized material (e.g., sterilized equipment, utensils, components) hold times be developed and implemented?

Recommendation
A sterilized material hold-time period should be developed by an assessment of the conditions under which the sterilized material and its packaging will be held and of the anticipated use requirements of the process.

A material that has been appropriately sterilized can be expected to remain so indefinitely within the Grade A and Grade B environments until and unless the sterile protective barrier is compromised. For material going into an isolator, controlled storage (e.g., storage which prevents damage to the wrapping) within a Grade C environment is necessary and appropriate.

Because the demands of barrier methods, materials, storage conditions, and processes vary, acceptable hold times, even for similar materials, can vary widely.

The integrity of the sterile protective barrier should be qualified in APS (Aseptic Process Simulation) for the maximum hold time, and the process should include inspection of each sterile item prior to its use to ensure that the sterile protective measures have remained intact. In the case of wrapping that is heat-sealed, the heat-sealing process should be qualified.

For materials that are sterilized within a rapid transfer port (RTP) canister (material for use with RABS or an isolator), a vent filter must be employed to allow vapor transfer in and out of the canister during autoclaving. In these cases, integrity testing of the filter post-use must be performed. In addition, a visual inspection of the integrity of the canister should also be performed.

Rationale
Sterilized materials will remain sterile indefinitely until they are exposed to contaminants. The means by which sterile materials become contaminated is degradation, damage, or failure of the sterile barrier system. The ability to maintain the barrier and to detect barrier failure is the critical factor, not time.

Because the main critical factor in packaging integrity is not time but rather handling and storage, which is visually inspected at every run, a single APS is appropriate to support hold time. However, the aging of wrapping materials may be a contributing factor to barrier deterioration.

References
1. EU Annex 1 (2022). 2. ISO 11607-1:2019. 3. PDA TR No. 61: Steam in Place (2013).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：無菌保存期的真正決定因素

這是 PDA PtC-1 最具洞察力的觀點之一：「已滅菌材料只要屏障（Barrier）完整，理論上可以永久保持無菌。」

關鍵不是「時間流逝」，而是「屏障是否被破壞」。影響屏障完整性的因素：

  • 搬運方式：撞擊、穿刺、摩擦
  • 儲存條件：溫濕度、光照、堆疊壓力
  • 包裝材質老化：長時間後可能降解（這才是設定最大保存期限的原因）

因此，目視檢查（Visual Inspection）是每次使用前最重要的控制點，而非依賴有效期標籤。

比喻：真空保鮮袋的道理

把食物放入真空保鮮袋密封——只要袋子沒有破損，食物就受到保護。保存「時間」當然有其意義（材料會老化），但真正的風險是「袋子被戳破」。無菌包裝的邏輯完全相同。

RTP 罐的通氣過濾器（Vent Filter）就像壓力鍋的洩壓閥——高壓滅菌時必須讓蒸氣進出，但又要確保通道不成為汙染入口，所以高壓滅菌後必須做過濾器完整性測試（Post-use Integrity Test）。

重點提示：APS 在保存期確效中的角色

PDA 建議用一次 APS（無菌製程模擬，Media Fill）來支持最大保存期限的確效——前提是在 APS 中使用已達最大保存期限的包裝物料。

這與熱封（Heat-seal）確效相輔相成：熱封工藝確效確保密封強度，APS 則確認在模擬最惡劣使用條件下（達最大保存期限＋實際操作搬運）包裝仍能維持無菌屏障。

EU Annex 1 2022 Section 8.123 要求對滅菌後包裝完整性進行確效，並在 APS 中使用達最長保存期限的物料。

實務應用：CDMO 物料保存期管理

CDMO 同時運行多個客戶項目，物料保存期管理容易出現混亂。建議：

  • 建立物料保存期矩陣，依包裝方式（折疊＋膠帶 vs 熱封預製袋）、儲存環境（Grade B vs Grade C）分別設定最大期限
  • 每批次使用前強制執行目視檢查並記錄，作為批次記錄的一部分
  • RTP 罐使用後必須立即記錄通氣過濾器完整性測試結果，失敗即觸發偏差

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 如果一批滅菌包裝元件在 Grade B 室溫下已存放 14 個月，超過公司規定的 12 個月最大保存期限，但目視檢查包裝完整無損——根據 PtC-1 原則，您如何評估其使用的可接受性？
  2. 為什麼「RTP 罐高壓滅菌後必須做通氣過濾器完整性測試」？如果跳過此步驟，可能的 GMP 後果是什麼？
§ VI. Cleaning, Disinfection, and Sterilization — 清潔、消毒與滅菌
Topic A: Disinfection Program

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
Is a disinfectant rotation program necessary?

Recommendation
A disinfectant rotation program is not necessary; however, the use of disinfectants and sporicidal agents, where the agents are alternated on a defined schedule, may be an effective approach to the cleanroom disinfection program.

The disinfection program should be shown to be effective against anticipated levels of surface contaminants, bacteria, and fungi.

The disinfection program should be based on assessed risk to sterile product. More than one type of disinfecting agent should be used, including the periodic usage of a sporicidal agent. The selection and frequency of cleanroom disinfectants, including sporicidal agents, should be based on EM data (quantitative/qualitative), trends, cleanroom materials, and the efficacy of the disinfectant procedures.

A typical program would include the frequent use of a disinfectant agent with the less frequent use of a sporicidal agent; however, the unique filling operation must be taken into consideration to best determine which agents to select for the facility.

Each manufacturer should have a formal program governing the qualification, use, and disposal of disinfectants. This program should include the rationale for disinfectant or sporicidal agent use.

The disinfectant program should be periodically evaluated and modified or maintained based on the use of area classification, review of EM data, and risk assessment.

For isolators, the bio-decontamination process should include a sporicidal agent in a gaseous or vaporized form. For RABS, the disinfection should include the routine application of a sporicidal agent. The sporicidal agents and frequency of application should take into consideration adverse effects on equipment and material surfaces. Disinfectant supplier safety recommendations and precautions should also be followed to protect personnel from harmful effects of exposure to sporicidal agents.

Rationale
Cleanroom disinfectants are used to maintain control over the contamination level of the cleanroom environment. To be effective, cleanroom disinfectants should be matched to the anticipated level of contamination.

While it is not proven that microorganisms develop resistance to particular agents, the use of different agents and the rotation between disinfectants and sporicides results in the destruction of a broader range of potential environmental contaminants, including bacterial spore-formers. The exclusive use of sporicidal agents may be an effective microbiological approach in the short term, however, the corrosion that might result from this practice could present additional risk of cleanroom and equipment damage.

References
1. EU Annex 1 (2022). 2. PDA PtC-Isolators (2020). 3. PDA TR13 (Revised 2022). 4. PDA TR70 (2015). 5–8. USP <1072>, USP <1116>, and published literature.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：消毒程序設計的四個關鍵要素

1. 消毒劑種類（Biocide Types）：

  • 季銨鹽（QAC）— 廣效，對細菌繁殖體有效，不殺孢子
  • 酒精類（Isopropanol/Ethanol）— 快速，對細菌繁殖體有效，不殺孢子
  • 次氯酸鈉（Sodium Hypochlorite）— 殺孢子，但腐蝕性高
  • 過氧化氫（H₂O₂）— 殺孢子，液態或氣態（VHP）皆可
  • 過氧乙酸（Peracetic Acid）— 殺孢子，廣效

2. 輪替策略（Rotation Strategy）：不強制輪替，但使用多種消毒劑（包括定期使用殺孢子劑）是最佳實務。

3. 頻率由 EM 數據驅動：環境監測（EM）趨勢決定計畫的調整，而不是固定公式。

4. 隔離器必須用氣態/霧化殺孢子劑（VHP/H₂O₂）：液態消毒無法穿透複雜幾何空間。

比喻：抗生素輪替原則（抗藥性管理）

PDA 特別指出「尚未有科學證據證明微生物會對消毒劑產生抵抗性」——但這不代表可以只用一種。就像醫院的感染管控：不是怕微生物「學壞」，而是確保廣譜殺滅。

只用殺孢子劑的比喻：就像餐廳只用最強的洗碗精——短期很有效，但長期會腐蝕餐具和水槽。消毒劑的選擇必須平衡微生物效力與材料相容性。

重點提示：Grade A vs Grade B 的差異化要求

Grade A 區域（充填區）：建議使用單次用量（Single-use）的無菌消毒劑包裝，避免噴瓶回吸汙染。

Grade B 區（背景環境）：可使用可重複使用噴瓶，但噴瓶本身必須能重新滅菌，且有防止回吸汙染的設計。

EU Annex 1 2022 Section 4.36 明確要求：Grade A/B 使用的消毒劑必須是無菌的，並需定期評估消毒程序的有效性，特別是對孢子形成菌（Sporicide）的殺滅能力。

稽查重點：殺孢子劑的使用頻率和濃度是否有文件支持？接觸時間（Contact Time）是否確效？

實務應用：消毒程序驗證文件要求

一份符合 GMP 的消毒程序必須包含：

  • 消毒劑選擇的科學依據（efficacy data，含殺孢子劑效力數據）
  • 使用頻率矩陣（Grade A/B 分開說明）
  • 接觸時間（Contact Time）規格與確效數據
  • 消毒劑稀釋程序及保存期限
  • EM 趨勢審查週期與計畫調整觸發條件
  • 腐蝕性風險評估（設備材質相容性報告）

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 某廠每日使用 70% IPA 擦拭 Grade B，每月用一次次氯酸鈉。稽查員問：「你的殺孢子劑使用頻率是基於什麼依據？」您如何回答才能符合 PtC-1 的建議？

Topic B: Sterilization of Sanitizers, Disinfectants, and Cleaning Agents

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
How do you ensure that sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agents (detergents) used in aseptic processing areas (Grade A and Grade B) are free from contaminants?

Recommendation
Sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agents used in Grade A and Grade B areas should be sterile. These agents used in Grade C and Grade D may also be required to be sterile where determined in the CCS (Contamination Control Strategy). Where sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agents are diluted, prepared, and sterilized by the sterile product manufacturer, this should be done in a validated manner to prevent contamination and they should be monitored for microbial contamination. The sterilization of these prepared agents is typically performed using 0.2 μm or smaller porosity.

Sterile sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agents should be maintained in an environment that maintains this condition. The filtered agents should be stored in sterilized closed containers until use. The period of time from the filtration to the use, and duration of use should be qualified.

For Grade A, single-use, sterile sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agents are recommended for use, either within spray-flasks, pressurized spray cans, wipes, or other applicators and containers. When using reusable spray flasks, steps should be taken to ensure that the spray flasks can be re-sterilized and have not been compromised by air drawn back into the container during use.

For purchased sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agent manufacturer's instructions should be followed and certificates of analysis reviewed and documented.

NOTE: It is not necessary to perform a sterility test of purchased, ready-to-use and sterile sanitizers, disinfectants, and cleaning agents. This is generally based on an audit of the supplier, knowledge of the supplier's processes and testing. Without that confidence, there might need to be some confirmatory testing even if on skip lot.

Unless there is evidence to the contrary, the sterilization of sporicides and sporicidal agents may not be necessary because they are considered to be "self-sterilizing agents."

Rationale
The sterilization procedure by the supplier, or the sterile filtration process and holding procedure at the manufacturers should be adequate to render the disinfectants and cleaning agents free of contamination, since these solutions are inherently low-bioburden. Once a qualified process has been put in place, the sterility test would not add any further level of assurance for ready-for-use or purchased sanitizers, disinfectants, or cleaning agents.

References
1. EU Annex 1 (2022). 2. PDA TR70 (2015). 3. FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為什麼消毒劑本身也需要無菌？

這個問題初聽起來像個謎語——「用來殺菌的東西，自己也有菌？」

答案是：是的，可能有。 許多消毒劑（如 QAC、酒精）對細菌繁殖體有效，但不殺孢子。如果消毒劑本身含有孢子，你不僅沒有去汙，反而把孢子帶進了最乾淨的地方。

Grade A/B 強制無菌的原因：這兩個等級直接與開放的無菌產品接觸，任何汙染都是不可接受的。

殺孢子劑例外：PDA 接受殺孢子劑（如次氯酸鈉、VHP）本身具有「自我滅菌」特性，通常不需要另行滅菌——但需有科學依據。

比喻：用骯髒的抹布擦桌子

想像你用一塊含有細菌孢子的「消毒抹布」去擦手術台——你以為在消毒，實際上在播種汙染。

0.2 µm 過濾滅菌就像「先把抹布高壓消毒，再用來擦」。這一步看似多餘，但在 Grade A/B 環境中是無菌保證（Sterility Assurance）的基礎。

重點提示：購買成品消毒劑 vs 自製稀釋

購買即用型（Ready-to-Use）無菌消毒劑：

  • 不需要做無菌測試（Sterility Test）——供應商稽核＋CoA 即可
  • 若對供應商信心不足，可做「skip lot」確認測試

自製稀釋消毒劑：

  • 必須用 0.2 µm（或更小孔徑）過濾滅菌
  • 儲存在滅菌過的密閉容器中
  • 過濾到使用的時間間隔，以及使用中的保存期限，都必須確效
  • 必須定期做微生物監測

EU Annex 1 2022 Section 4.36：明確要求 Grade A/B 消毒劑必須無菌，且存放期限須確效。

練習思考 Practice Questions

Topic C: Hold Times for Sterilized Wrapped Parts, Components, and Other Material

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
How should hold times be determined for sterilized wrapped parts, components, and other material?

Recommendation
Preference should be given to the use of packages that are preformed and sealed over wrapping that is folded and taped.

The date of sterilization and the expiry date should be identified on each package. A maximum hold time should be set consistent with the needs of the process and supported based on the qualification of the wrapping method.

The qualification is based on:

  • Barrier property of the wrapping material
  • Qualified wrapping, handling and storage procedure
  • Quality of the handling and storage environment

Using wrapped materials in one or more media fills that have been held for the maximum hold time might be an additional step to ensure package integrity in initial qualification; however, media fills alone will not be sufficient or may not be necessary to qualify the holding time of wrapped material.

Rationale
The variables in sterilized material hold times are wrapping method, closing and sealing method, handling conditions, storage conditions and microbial barrier capability and integrity. If the barrier materials remain dry post-sterilization, the risk of contamination is low. If the procedure is sound, then time should not be a factor unless the wrapping materials degrade or are damaged over time, and as such maximum hold times do not require revalidation unless changes are made to the materials or sterilization cycle/method used. However, a maximum hold time is recommended because open-ended times may introduce unforeseen variables.

1. ISO 11607-1:2019 Packaging for Terminally Sterilized Medical Devices.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Topic B vs Topic C 的微妙差異

Topic B（Section V-B）談的是「進入無菌區後的物料保存期」，Topic C 談的是「滅菌包裝部件的保存期確效方法」——兩者本質相同，但 Topic C 更聚焦在如何確效。

三個確效基礎：

  • 屏障特性：包材的 MVTR（水蒸氣透過率）、孔隙度測試
  • 操作程序：折疊/熱封工藝確效，搬運 SOP
  • 儲存環境品質：Grade C 儲存 vs 受控一般室溫

PDA 的立場：熱封預製袋優於折疊+膠帶——因為熱封可以量化確效（剝離強度測試），而折疊主要依賴人員操作一致性。

比喻：保鮮膜 vs 真空密封袋

折疊包裝就像用保鮮膜包食物——技術依賴人，結果難以標準化。熱封預製袋就像真空密封機——有數據支持（密封溫度、壓力、時間），品質可重複，可量化確效。

這就是為什麼 PDA 說「優先選擇預製密封包裝」——不是技術迷信，而是可確效性更強。

重點提示：Media Fill 的角色與限制

Media Fill（無菌製程模擬，APS）可作為包裝保存期確效的「額外步驟」，但 PDA 明確說：Media Fill 單獨不足以，也不一定必要，來確效保存期限。

原因：Media Fill 評估的是整體無菌製程，而非單獨的包裝屏障能力。包裝確效應以物理/化學方法（ISO 11607-1：破壞性測試、染料滲透、氦氣洩漏）為主，Media Fill 為輔。

一旦確效完成，若無包材或滅菌工藝的變更，不需要定期重新確效保存期限——這降低了維護成本。

Topic D: Frequency of Requalification of Sterilization Processes

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
How frequently should sterilization processes be requalified?
NOTE: Radiation sterilization is not in scope for this topic.

Recommendation
A successful sterilization process validation program is one that is initiated early in the product lifecycle and is ongoing until the process or product reaches the end of that lifecycle. Comprehensive written procedures that define the expectations and commitment to process validation lifecycle principles is the foundation of a successful validation program.

Any change in equipment or process should be evaluated for its impact on process qualification and, if necessary, requalified. Requalification should be performed on a regular basis (typically every 12 months) to ensure there has not been an undetected change in product or process. Requalification should be performed using the same operational parameters and acceptance criteria as the original qualification runs.

For terminal sterilization, verification of acceptable steam quality for porous/hard goods load sterilization should also be performed at this time.1 Results of the requalification study should demonstrate that the sterilizer's performance has not changed since the original effort. All sterilizer load(s) should be periodically included in requalification runs. Where equivalence has been established between sterilizers, a risk-based reduction in requalification activity may be considered.

Requalification should also include review of performance data from various monitoring sources (e.g., engineering, maintenance, calibration data) of sterilizers and supporting equipment to verify that there have been no adverse trends or drifts away from the baseline performance established during validation. A review of change-control documentation should be conducted as part of the requalification.

Rationale
A qualified process is one where there is a high degree of assurance that a process will be repeatable unless a change is made. Any changes to the process or equipment should be assessed and requalified prior to implementation of changes.

The purpose of periodic requalification is to identify undetected changes and variables to the process that have the potential to negatively impact efficacy. Since the sterilization process is complex and the impact of a failure is severe, periodic assessment and revalidation is required. The basis for the revalidation frequency should emphasize process performance and control with focus on the level of variability in the delivery of key and critical parameters.

1 The quality of the sterilizing agent should also be confirmed on a regular basis.

References
1. EU Annex 1 (2022). 2. ISO 17665-1:2006. 3. PDA TR1 (Revised 2007). 4. PDA TR60 (2013). 5. FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：「每12個月」重新確效的法規依據

PDA PtC-1 推薦每 12 個月進行一次定期重新確效（Periodic Requalification）——這是業界共識，也與多個監管機構的期望一致。重新確效的三個觸發條件：

  • 設備或製程變更（Change-triggered）：任何可能影響確效狀態的變更都需評估是否重新確效
  • 定期計畫（Scheduled，通常每年）：即使沒有變更，也要確認「靜悄悄的漂移（Drift）」
  • 偏差或績效下降（Event-triggered）：如 BI 失效、儀器漂移

等效性（Equivalence）建立後，多台同型滅菌器可以減少重新確效頻率——但需有文件支持等效性的科學基礎。

比喻：汽車年度定期保養

即使您的車「感覺開起來沒問題」，每年還是要做定期保養——檢查剎車片磨損、機油老化、輪胎氣壓。滅菌製程的年度重新確效正是同樣的邏輯：不是因為「已知有問題」，而是要確認「沒有未知的問題」。

「漂移（Drift）」就像慢慢消耗的剎車片——沒有明顯故障，但長期下來會失效。年度確效就是要抓到這種緩慢的偏移。

重點提示：EU vs FDA 對重新確效頻率的立場差異

EU Annex 1 2022：Section 8.48 明確要求蒸氣滅菌製程定期重新確效，並要求在重新確效中包含蒸氣品質驗證（乾燥飽和度、不凝結氣體、過熱蒸氣測試）。

FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004)：強調基於績效的方法（Performance-based），每年回顧製程數據，必要時進行重新確效。FDA 沒有強制規定「12個月」，但 12 個月已成為業界預設標準。

PDA TR1 (Revised 2007)：最詳細的蒸氣滅菌確效指南，是本 Topic 的技術基礎文件。CDMO 在設計重新確效計畫時應同時參考 PDA TR1 和 EU Annex 1 2022。

實務應用：年度重新確效計畫的文件架構

一份完整的年度重新確效計畫應包含：

  • 所有受影響滅菌負載（Loads）的清單
  • 變更控制文件回顧記錄
  • 前一年度 BI 數據、熱分佈數據趨勢分析
  • 維護與校正記錄回顧
  • 蒸氣品質測試（若涉及多孔/硬質物品）
  • 重新確效執行記錄與結論報告

Topic E: Porous/Hard Goods Sterilization Process Qualification

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
Should discrete loading patterns be established for all moist-heat sterilization processes?

Recommendation
The loading pattern should be established for all moist-heat sterilization processes from the knowledge of the materials and equipment and verified as part of process qualification. In some cases, a minimum-maximum bracketing approach may be acceptable where studies (e.g., cycle development studies) indicate that the positioning and inclusion of materials in loads that fall between the minimum and maximum does not present more of a sterilization challenge than the qualified minimum-maximum load.

Considerations should be given to the positioning and location of materials within the load. Discrete loading patterns may not be necessary if it can be demonstrated that cycle efficacy is not affected by variable loading patterns.

Rationale
Reproducibility in moist-heat sterilization is dependent upon the capability of the sterilization process to consistently deliver process efficacy for the stated loading pattern(s).

The ability of the cycle to sterilize materials is dependent on the positioning and location of those materials in the sterilizer and may not necessarily be affected by the volume of materials in the sterilizer. The materials of the load should be oriented to ensure proper air removal, steam penetration and condensate removal.

A more detailed discussion on establishing loading patterns can be found in Technical Report No. 1 (Revised 2007): Validation of Moist Heat Sterilization Processes: Cycle Design, Development, Qualification and Ongoing Control.

References
1. EU Annex 1 (2022). 2. ISO 14937:2009. 3. ISO 17665-1:2006. 4. PDA TR1 (Revised 2007).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：裝載模式確效的三個關鍵參數

多孔/硬質物品（Porous/Hard Goods）蒸氣滅菌的挑戰遠比液體滅菌複雜，因為蒸氣必須穿透物理屏障。三個關鍵因素：

  • 空氣排除（Air Removal）：殘留空氣會阻礙蒸氣接觸，是最大的失效風險。Bowie-Dick 測試就是用來驗證空氣排除效果。
  • 蒸氣穿透（Steam Penetration）：蒸氣必須到達物品的最難穿透點（Cold Spot），通常用生物指示劑（BI）放在此處。
  • 冷凝液排除（Condensate Removal）：積水會阻礙後續蒸氣接觸，也影響乾燥。

最小-最大括弧法（Bracketing）：確效最重（最多物品）和最輕（最少物品）的裝載——若兩端都通過，中間的裝載也被接受。前提是有數據支持「中間負載的挑戰性不超過極端值」。

比喻：烤箱均勻受熱的擺放藝術

在烤箱烤東西時，食物的擺放位置決定了是否均勻受熱——太密集空氣流通不好，某些部位可能不熟。高壓滅菌器裡的蒸氣穿透問題完全相同：物品的方向和位置決定了蒸氣是否能均勻接觸每個表面。

這就是為什麼「裝載模式」需要確效，而不能隨意堆放——確效時的擺放方式就是生產時的標準。

重點提示：固定裝載 vs 彈性裝載

需要固定裝載的情況：複雜形狀、多孔材料（如摺疊布類）、有空腔的設備——蒸氣穿透對位置高度敏感。

可用彈性裝載的情況：若能證明不同裝載量/位置對滅菌效果無實質影響（如不鏽鋼小零件裝入網籃）。

Bowie-Dick 測試：是多孔負載滅菌器的標準功能測試（EN 285 要求），用一個標準化的多孔測試包來確認空氣排除效率——不是滅菌確效，而是每日功能性確認。PDA TR1 提供了更適合製藥工業的 BI 研究方法。

Topic F: Integrity Testing of 0.2 Micron Filters

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
Should 0.2 µm filters used for purposes other than sterilization (e.g., prefiltration, bioload/bioburden reduction, redundant filters backup) be integrity-tested?

Recommendation
The 0.2 µm filters used for purposes other than the sterilization of fluids can be and are used to reduce bioburden and/or protect other process equipment, for example, chromatography columns. The necessity to test the integrity of such filters should be determined using appropriate risk assessments, which include testing the function of the filter, the position, and the criticality within the process.

Rationale
The decision to test the integrity of a bioburden-reducing 0.2 µm-rated filter should be left to the discretion of the filter user. In some instances, the filter is used to protect another process step or as a prefilter in front of a virus-removal or ultrafiltration step. The criticality may not call for an integrity test being needed, as the next process step is the focal point to determine the quality of the product. In other cases, the 0.2 µm filter may be seen at a higher criticality level or might be specified to achieve a defined filtrate quality; at that point the risk assessment performed will guide the end-user to potentially integrity test such filter.

Footnote: For redundant sterilizing-filter systems, where the process has been validated as only requiring one filter, the redundant (typically first/upstream) filter does not have to be integrity-tested unless the primary sterilizing (typically second/downstream) filter fails its integrity test. However, if the redundant filtration system has been validated for the use of both filters to sterilize the product, then both filters must be integrity-tested.

1. EU Annex 1 (2022). 2. PDA TR26 (2008): Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids. 3. FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004).

核心概念：過濾器完整性測試的決策邏輯

這個 Topic 的核心問題是：「不是所有 0.2 µm 過濾器都需要完整性測試。」 關鍵是它在製程中的用途與位置。

PUPSIT 的爭議焦點：如何在不汙染無菌批次的情況下，在滅菌後、使用前進行完整性測試？EU Annex 1 2022 Section 8.85–8.87 提出了具體要求。

Topic G: Sterilization — Steam Sterilizers (EN 285 & ISO 17665-1)

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
Are the European Standard EN 285:2015+A1:2021 and International Standard ISO 17665-1:2006 applicable to pharmaceutical autoclaves and automated-equipment steam-in-place (SIP)?

Recommendation
Both EN 285 and ISO 17665-1 are valuable references that have been developed to support the sterilization of medical devices used in healthcare but may be used to assess the construction, performance testing, operation, and/or validation of steam sterilizers and SIP systems in the pharmaceutical industry. However, neither standard has been officially adopted by regulatory authorities as GMP, nor has either standard been included in an FDA Guidance for Industry or similar guidelines as recommended best practice related to GMPs for pharmaceuticals.

Rationale
EN 285 Sterilization – Steam sterilizers – Large sterilizers, is a European Standard for adoption by member nations. It was revised and approved in 2015, with amendment A1 added in 2021. It applies specifically to the design, construction, and performance testing of large-steam sterilizers. Based upon the following statement contained within the Introduction, EN 285 is applicable to medical devices:

"This document specifies test procedures and acceptance criteria to confirm whether the sterilizer is safe and can deliver an operating cycle for sterilizing the range of medical devices and loading configurations used in healthcare."

ISO 17665-1:2006 Sterilization of Health Care Products—Moist Heat—Part 1: Requirements for the development, validation, and routine control of a sterilization process for medical devices was published in 2006 and reaffirmed in 2013. ISO 17665-1 includes EN 285 as a reference in the bibliography but does not include this document in its list of Normative References.

ISO 17665-1 constitutes a consensus international standard and, if followed, may be considered to provide acceptable practice for the operation and validation of steam sterilizers for medical devices used in the healthcare industry. However, because this ISO standard has not been adopted and formally referenced within a published GMP regulation (such as ISO 14644, which is referenced in the 2022 revision of EU Annex 1), it does not constitute the sole means of compliance.

ISO/TS 17665-2:2009 Annex B: Evaluation of a sterilization process primarily based on biological inactivation and an accompanying mechanical air removal procedure incorporates biological indicator studies and is generally more aligned with pharmaceutical industry practices, as described in PDA TR-1. Most pharmaceutical practitioners would not accept the strictly physical measurements outlined in Annex A without biological-indicator data for confirmation.

NOTE: Based on the titles of EN 285 and ISO 17665–1, neither standard is directly applicable to SIP systems, as SIP systems cannot be considered large sterilizers, nor are they used to directly sterilize medical devices.

References
1. EN 285:2015+A1:2021. 2. ISO 13408-5:2006 (SIP). 3. ISO 17665-1:2006. 4. ISO/TS 17665-2:2009. 5. PDA TR1 (Revised 2007). 6–9. AAMI/ISO standards and BSI publications.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：標準的法規地位層級

這個 Topic 回答了一個實務中常見的困惑：「我必須遵守 EN 285 和 ISO 17665-1 嗎？」

答案：不是「強制」，而是「可用作參考」。製藥滅菌的法規要求來自 EU GMP Annex 1 和 FDA Guidance，而這兩份文件並未直接引用 EN 285 或 ISO 17665-1 作為強制要求（對比：EU Annex 1 2022 確實引用了 ISO 14644 作為潔淨室分級依據）。

▼ 滅菌標準層級示意
EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) — 法規強制
▼
FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (2004) — 強烈建議
▼
PDA TR1 (Revised 2007) — 最佳實務（製藥專用）
▼
ISO 17665-1 / EN 285 — 參考工具（醫療器械標準）

比喻：烹飪指南 vs 健康食品法規

EN 285 和 ISO 17665-1 就像「米其林烹飪指南」——非常有價值，但不是「食品安全法」。您可以用它們來提升製程品質，但監管機構稽查的基準是 EU GMP Annex 1 和 FDA Guidance，而非這些標準。

相對地，ISO 14644（潔淨室標準）已被 EU Annex 1 2022 直接引用，就像「食品安全法中規定的具體操作規範」——這才算強制。

重點提示：物理法 vs 生物法的爭議

ISO 17665-2 Annex A（物理量測法，EN 285 導向）vs Annex B（生物指示劑法，PDA TR1 導向）代表了製藥與醫療器械兩個行業的不同偏好：

  • 製藥行業：堅持使用生物指示劑（BI）確認滅菌效力——物理數據是必要條件，但不充分
  • 醫療器械/醫院：EN 285 的物理測試（如 Bowie-Dick）通常被認為足夠

PDA PtC-1 明確支持「大多數製藥業者不接受純物理測量，沒有 BI 數據的確認是不夠的」——這是業界共識，也是稽查員的期望。

SIP 注意事項：EN 285 和 ISO 17665-1 都不直接適用於 SIP 系統——SIP 的最佳實務參考應為 ISO 13408-5 和 PDA TR61。

Topic H: Lyophilizer Leak Qualification

原文內容 Original Text

Problem Statement
What is the frequency and the specification for the vacuum/leak integrity-testing of a lyophilizer?

Recommendation
The vacuum/leak integrity test should be performed after sterilization and prior to loading product for each batch. A leak rate may be conducted to assess the level of air infiltration caused by leaks. It may also be used to evaluate the integrity of the lyophilizer after steam sterilization. Such a test is referred to as a vacuum/leak integrity test to distinguish it from a true leak test. It is imperative to distinguish the difference in the tests and reported results.

A true leak rate—assessing the increase in the internal pressure of a lyophilizer starting from a low pressure (vacuum)—reflects the integrity of the system and the level of infiltration of air through connections to the chamber, condenser, and the chamber door. The measure value may be influenced by desorption of volatile components from the interior surfaces of the lyophilizer; this is referred to as outgassing.

Though suitable values have been provided by equipment vendors, often as part of the equipment specifications, there has been no strong scientific evidence to substantiate what an acceptable value should be for vacuum/leak integrity test. The most important aspect for comparing results of multiple tests is reproducing the same conditions of temperature, pressure, and time, as they influence the results of the test.

Rationale
The leak rate is useful to include it as part of the routine preventive maintenance and a test when changes or repairs are made. The in-process vacuum/leak integrity test is effective in ensuring that no leaks have developed from the expansion and contraction of the vessels during the steam sterilization and subsequent cooling.

To assess the level of infiltration of the atmosphere through leaks without any contribution of outgassing, the lyophilizer should be clean, dry, and empty and prepared to remove any volatile components. Results under these conditions more closely reflect a true loss of integrity of the sealing surfaces and elastomeric gaskets of the chamber, condenser connections, and the door.

The value measured after steam sterilization is strongly influenced by the evolution of absorbed volatile components from the interior surfaces of the lyophilizer, yielding much different results than those of a true leak rate. The major component and contributor to a pressure increase is desorption of residual water upon completion of steam sterilization.

Values may be reported as a pressure increase over time, such as microns (millitorr) per minute, or µmHg per minute, or as microns (millitorr) per volume–time. Including the volume factor is useful for comparing the results for different sizes of lyophilizers.

Assuring the integrity of the lyophilizer, such that the infiltration of air that is of unknown and uncontrolled microbiological and chemical quality, leads to a higher level of confidence in the product quality. As the product in the lyophilizer is unsealed and no longer protected by unidirectional air that has been HEPA-filtered, it is the integrity of the lyophilizer that protects the product from potential contamination.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：凍乾機洩漏測試的兩種模式

PDA PtC-1 特別強調必須區分兩種測試，因為它們的目的和條件完全不同：

項目 批次前真空/洩漏完整性測試 真實洩漏率測試（True Leak Rate）
時機 每批，滅菌後、裝料前 預防性維護、維修後
儀器狀態 剛完成蒸氣滅菌，有殘留水分 乾淨、乾燥、空腔，排除揮發物
結果影響 受脫氣（Outgassing）影響大 更接近真實密封完整性
主要目的 確認蒸氣滅菌後無新發展的洩漏 評估整體系統完整性

關鍵結論：批次前測試不等於真實洩漏率——但它是每批次產品保護的重要控制點。

比喻：太空艙密封性測試

凍乾機在冷凍乾燥過程中就像一個「太空艙」——產品（未封口的小瓶）完全依賴艙體的密封性來防止外界汙染空氣進入。HEPA 過濾的單向流不再保護它，因為產品在密閉腔體內。

脫氣（Outgassing）就像艙壁上的水分蒸發——即使密封完好，壓力計也會顯示壓力上升，因為這是物理現象而非洩漏。這就是為什麼必須在相同條件下比較測試結果，而不是看絕對值。

重點提示：EU Annex 1 2022 對凍乾機洩漏的要求

EU Annex 1 2022 Section 9.40–9.43 對凍乾機的要求包括：

  • 每次使用前（裝料前）必須對凍乾機進行洩漏測試
  • 洩漏測試規格應在確效過程中建立，而不是單純依賴設備廠商的規格
  • 凍乾機的艙門密封、冷凝器連接等關鍵密封點必須定期檢查
  • CCS（污染控制策略）應涵蓋凍乾機的洩漏風險

接受標準的挑戰：PDA PtC-1 誠實指出「目前尚無強科學依據支持真空/洩漏測試的可接受值應該是多少」——接受標準的建立主要依靠設備廠商規格和廠內歷史趨勢數據。

公式：洩漏率的表達方式

洩漏率單位（Leak Rate Units）：
─────────────────────────────────────
方式 1（絕對壓力上升率）：
  洩漏率 = ΔP / Δt
  單位：µmHg/min 或 mTorr/min

方式 2（體積標準化，適合比較不同尺寸設備）：
  洩漏率 = ΔP × V / Δt
  單位：µmHg·L/min

  其中：
  ΔP = 測試期間壓力上升值（µmHg）
  Δt = 測試時間（min）
  V  = 腔體體積（L）
─────────────────────────────────────
注意：後者更適合比較不同規格凍乾機。
脫氣干擾：蒸氣滅菌後測得值 >> 乾燥狀態真實洩漏率，
兩者不可直接比較。

實務應用：凍乾機洩漏程序建立建議

CDMO 在建立凍乾機洩漏測試程序時，建議：

  • 建立雙重規格：一套用於「乾淨乾燥狀態（預防性維護用）」，一套用於「滅菌後批次前（常規 in-process）」，兩者分開記錄與趨勢追蹤
  • 條件標準化：固定測試溫度、測試時間、初始抽真空壓力——結果只在相同條件下比較才有意義
  • 建立趨勢基線：收集 20–30 次歷史數據，建立控制圖，設定警戒限（Alert）與行動限（Action）
  • 供應商規格作為參考下限，廠內確效數據建立上限接受標準

  2. 為什麼蒸氣滅菌後立即進行的洩漏測試結果會比乾燥狀態下高很多？主要的化學/物理原因是什麼？這對接受標準設定有什麼含義？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • V-A 物料進場：最佳方式為單向滅菌通道（雙門高壓滅菌釜/去熱原隧道）；多層包裝脫層法是次佳選項；RTP 適用於隔離器/RABS；進場物料必須在核准清單上，否則走偏差程序
  • V-B 無菌保存期：決定因素是「屏障完整性」，不是時間；滅菌後在 Grade A/B 中可無限期保持無菌；必須設置最大期限並在 APS 中確效；RTP 罐通氣過濾器需做使用後完整性測試
  • VI-A 消毒程序：不強制輪替，但應使用多種消毒劑包括定期殺孢子劑；計畫應由 EM 數據驅動；隔離器必須用氣態/霧化殺孢子劑；EU Annex 1 2022 強制 Grade A/B 使用無菌消毒劑
  • VI-B 滅菌消毒劑：Grade A/B 使用的消毒劑本身必須無菌；自製稀釋液需 0.2 µm 過濾並確效保存期；購買成品無需另做無菌測試（供應商稽核＋CoA 即可）；殺孢子劑因具自我滅菌特性可例外
  • VI-C 包裝件保存期：優先選用熱封預製袋（可量化確效）；最大期限基於屏障特性、操作程序、儲存環境；Media Fill 是補充依據，非主要確效工具；確效後無需定期重新確效（除非有變更）
  • VI-D 重新確效頻率：建議每 12 個月定期重新確效；任何設備/製程變更須評估後重新確效；多台等效滅菌器可減少工作量；重新確效應包含維護/校正數據回顧和變更控制文件審查
  • VI-E 多孔/硬質物品確效：裝載模式必須確效；可用最小-最大括弧法；關鍵是確保空氣排除、蒸氣穿透、冷凝液排除；Bowie-Dick 是日常功能確認，BI 是確效核心
  • VI-F 0.2µm 過濾器完整性測試：非滅菌用途的 0.2µm 過濾器是否測試，由風險評估決定；備援滅菌過濾器：若主過濾器通過則不需測；若確效為雙過濾器缺一不可，則兩者都必須測
  • VI-G 蒸氣滅菌器標準：EN 285 和 ISO 17665-1 對製藥業是「參考工具」，非強制要求；製藥確效必須包含 BI 數據，不接受純物理測量；SIP 不適用 EN 285 和 ISO 17665-1；首選 PDA TR1 作為製藥蒸氣滅菌確效指南
  • VI-H 凍乾機洩漏確效：每批次滅菌後、裝料前必須測試；批次前測試不等於真實洩漏率（受 Outgassing 影響）；接受標準依廠內趨勢建立，非單純依賴廠商規格；EU Annex 1 2022 Section 9.40–9.43 有明確要求
