8.0 Introduction to Glass Types | 玻璃類型概論

8.0 Appendix I: Fundamentals of Glass Science

To know how to properly handle glass and the various rationales behind it, the fundamental chemical and physical properties of glass need to be understood. The standard types of glass used for medical purposes are soda-lime silicate and borosilicate. Borosilicate glass is a Type I glass recommended for use in parenteral packaging. Soda-lime silicate glass is a Type III glass recommended for packaging nonparenteral drugs.

Typical compositional ranges for pharmaceutical glasses are outlined in various national standards, such as ISO 12775 and ASTM E438. Both these standards describe two types of borosilicate glass based on their coefficients of mean linear thermal expansion.

核心概念解析

Type I vs. Type III 的關鍵差異

硼矽玻璃（Borosilicate glass）即 Type I 玻璃，是注射劑（Parenteral）包裝的標準選擇。其化學惰性強、熱膨脹係數低，不易與藥液反應，也耐受高低溫衝擊。

鈉鈣矽玻璃（Soda-lime silicate glass）即 Type III，化學穩定性較低，僅適用於非注射劑（Non-parenteral）包裝，如口服液劑容器。

對 CDMO 而言，Type I 不可降級使用 — 若在壓力下改用 Type III，不僅違反 USP/EP 規範，更直接危及注射劑安全性。

生活類比

Type I 硼矽玻璃就像耐熱實驗室燒杯（Pyrex），Type III 鈉鈣矽玻璃則像一般飲料玻璃瓶。兩者都是玻璃，但面對高溫、化學接觸或急速冷卻時，差距非常大。

表格解讀重點

CTE 數值的含義

CTE（熱膨脹係數，Coefficient of Thermal Expansion）數值越低，代表玻璃在溫度變化時膨脹/收縮幅度越小，熱衝擊耐受性越高。

  • Borosilicate 3.3：CTE ~3.3，最穩定，適合嚴苛溫度環境
  • Borosilicate 5.0：CTE ~5，藥用最常見的 tubular vial 材質
  • Soda-lime silicate：CTE ~7–9，熱穩定性最差，只適合 Type III
  • Quartz：CTE <1，極端穩定但成本極高，非一般藥用

實務提醒：退火點（Annealing Point）

退火點是製造工廠控制冷卻速率的關鍵溫度參數。不同玻璃類型的退火點差距不大（525–575°C），但退火冷卻速率的精確控制攸關最終容器的殘留應力水準。

Quartz 的退火點超過 1150°C，說明其製造難度與成本遠高於其他材質。

類比：CTE 好比彈性係數

CTE 越高的玻璃，就像彈性越差的橡皮筋：快速拉伸或壓縮時，更容易斷裂。硼矽玻璃是「彈性較好」的材質，但仍是脆性材料（Brittle material），只是它的「安全邊際」更寬。

8.1 Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) | 熱膨脹係數

8.1 Coefficient of Thermal Expansion

Most materials expand and contract upon heating and cooling, respectively. The extent of expansion is dependent on the specific glass chemical composition. This expansion can be measured using techniques such as push-rod dilatometry and is expressed as the change in length per temperature unit, otherwise known as the coefficient of linear thermal expansion (CTE). The CTE for a glass is generally reported as mean value over a specific temperature range. The higher the CTE, the more the material will expand when heated and, conversely, the more it will shrink when cooled. ASTM E228 and ISO 7991 provide guidance on measuring the CTE of glass.

The CTE is also a main factor in determining the resistance of glass in a flawless, nonstrengthened state to sudden temperature changes. Increasing resistance to thermal shock is correlated with decreasing CTE.

Soda-lime silicate glass with a wall thickness of 0.5 mm can withstand a maximum rapid temperature difference of approximately 110 °C without cracking. Standard borosilicate glass of the same thickness can endure greater temperature differences. Borosilicate glasses having a CTE of 5.0 × 10⁻⁶ K⁻¹ and 3.3 × 10⁻⁶ K⁻¹ with a wall thickness of 0.5 mm can withstand approximately 220 °C and 340 °C rapid temperature differences, respectively, and are generally more resistant to thermal shock than soda-lime silicate glass.

Due to the fact that glass has poor thermal conductivity, a thinner wall thickness is recommended for thermal resistance while a thicker wall will withstand greater mechanical loads. These calculations are theoretical, however, and refer to glass in a flawless state. In practice, glass is never in a flawless state; therefore, the presence of surface flaws may be more significant than the CTE alone in determining thermal shock resistance.

概念解析與實務意義

CTE 與熱衝擊耐受力的直接對比

以 0.5 mm 壁厚為例（理論值，無缺陷狀態）：

  • Soda-lime silicate（Type III）：耐 ~110°C 溫差
  • Borosilicate 5.0（Type I）：耐 ~220°C 溫差
  • Borosilicate 3.3（Type I）：耐 ~340°C 溫差

這正是注射劑選用 Type I 的物理依據：凍乾（Lyophilization）、除熱原（Depyrogenation, 350°C）等製程中的溫差遠超 Type III 的安全範圍。

壁厚的雙重效應（重要！）

玻璃導熱性差（Poor thermal conductivity）是理解壁厚影響的關鍵：

  • 薄壁：內外溫差小 → 熱衝擊風險低，但機械強度較弱
  • 厚壁：內外溫差大 → 熱衝擊風險高，但可承受更大機械負荷

設計選型時需在「熱抗性」與「機械強度」之間權衡，這也是 vial 規格設計的核心考量。

理論 vs. 實際的差距

報告明確指出：上述數據僅適用於「完美無瑕」（Flawless）的玻璃。現實中，玻璃表面必然存在微細缺陷（Micro-flaws）。因此，表面缺陷的影響往往比 CTE 更能決定玻璃的實際熱衝擊破裂風險。

換句話說：一支有嚴重刮痕的 Borosilicate 3.3 vial，可能比一支完好的 Borosilicate 5.0 vial 更脆弱。這個認知貫穿整份 TR87 的操作建議。

8.2 Stress Inside the Glass | 玻璃內部應力

8.2.1 Melting and Solidification Behavior of Glass

The transition of a solid crystal to molten liquid occurs instantaneously at fixed melting temperatures, Tm. In contrast, solid glass does not possess a melting temperature. Instead, the viscosity of glass changes continuously as a function of temperature when going from the solid state to the liquid state. This behavior can be illustrated using the Volume-Temperature (V-T) diagram.

In order to form a typical glass, a molten liquid must be cooled below Tm without crystallizing, thereby becoming a supercooled liquid. The point at which the slope of the V-T curve deviates from the supercooled liquid line marks one boundary of the "glass transformation range." The intersection point of lines extrapolated from the solid and liquid sections of the glass V-T curve is one definition of the glass transition temperature, Tg.

The Tg of a glass is dependent on chemical composition and is of practical importance in glassmaking. The Tg lies between the annealing point and the strain point. The annealing point is approximately located at the upper temperature boundary of the glass transformation range and can be broadly defined as the temperature at which a majority of residual stress within a glass is dissipated within several minutes. The strain point is approximately located at the lower temperature boundary of the glass transformation and can be broadly defined as the temperature at which a majority of residual stress within a glass is dissipated within several hours. In addition, the strain point marks a limit for structural rearrangement of the glass. Knowledge of these temperature points are used to design annealing schedules that remove a majority of forming-induced stress within a glass article.

玻璃固化行為解析

玻璃沒有固定熔點

結晶固體（如冰、金屬）在固定的熔點（Tm）瞬間轉換固液態；玻璃不同，其黏度（Viscosity）隨溫度連續變化，是一種「過冷液體（Supercooled liquid）」的凍結狀態。

這個特性使玻璃的成型與退火成為「速率控制」而非「溫度開關」的製程。

三個關鍵溫度點（從高到低）

  • 退火點（Annealing Point）：殘留應力在「數分鐘」內消散。為退火製程上限溫度，硼矽玻璃約 550–575°C。
  • 玻璃轉換溫度（Tg）：結構從液態行為轉換為固態行為的過渡區間中點，介於退火點與應變點之間。
  • 應變點（Strain Point）：殘留應力在「數小時」內消散。為退火製程下限溫度，低於此點結構不再重排。

類比：退火製程如同「慢慢放鬆」

想像一條被過度扭緊的橡皮筋：若快速鬆手，它會彈開（裂開）；若緩慢、有控制地釋放，它就能回到自然狀態而不斷裂。退火正是讓玻璃原子有足夠時間重新排列，釋放成型時累積的應力。

8.2.2 Physical Definition of Stress

Physical stress (σ) can be defined as the force (F) per unit area of a material element (A). There are two important types of stress in the pharmaceutical packaging handling process: tensile stress and compressive stress. If the atomic positions are changed by an external force (tensile or compressive force), the atoms will attempt to return to their original, optimal distance by counteracting the external force.

應力的物理定義

兩種核心應力類型

  • 拉伸應力（Tensile stress）：力使材料被拉開，原子間距離被拉大。玻璃「永遠在拉伸應力點破裂」。
  • 壓縮應力（Compressive stress）：力使材料被壓縮，原子間距離縮小。玻璃對壓縮應力有較高耐受性；這也是化學強化的核心原理。

重要觀念：施加壓縮力的一側，在容器其他位置往往同時產生拉伸應力，而這些拉伸應力區才是真正的危險點。

8.2.2.1 Thermal Stress During Container Manufacturing

During the formation of a container, the raw material is melted, and a tube is drawn and cut into lengths. The glass has a low, even stress distribution at this point in the process. Heat must be applied again to the glass tube when forming a container. Forming the container employs temperatures of 1000 °C or greater, which causes stress at the bottom and at the opening of the container while the rest of the tubing remains cool.

While heating up one part of a glass tube, the atoms being heated increase their oscillation. As the heated part is cooled down quickly after the forming process, the atoms do not have sufficient time to rearrange to their former structure. Rather, they are stuck in the position they occupied at that time, and both tensile and compressive stress is created.

In order to restore the glass to a low-stress state, the entire vial is annealed by heating to about 20 °C above Tg for some period of time and then slowly cooled in a strictly controlled process in an annealing lehr (oven). After the annealing process is complete, a majority of forming-induced stress in the glass should be reduced to an acceptable level. In this manner, introducing thermal stress into the glass and releasing it again does not lead to an accumulation of stress and weakening of the glass. However, if this stress is not released properly, the risk of the glass breaking at the zone of tensile stress is greater.

The amount of residual stress can be determined by polariscopic measurement devices.

Except for the sealing of ampoules, residual thermal stress cannot be introduced in the filling process as the temperature never exceeds the Tg of the glass.

Like the tubular process, stress is introduced into molded vials or bottles during the forming process. A gob of molten glass is dropped into a mold and formed via a press-and-blow process or blow-and-blow process. The vials must undergo an annealing process with controlled cooling to relieve the stress.

If the temperature difference at a certain spot on the glass exceeds the particular thermal shock parameter of that glass, however, micro-cracks can result. Thermal shock occurs when a thermal gradient causes different parts of an object to expand by different amounts. This phenomenon can occur where cold metal meets hot glass. For example, residual water from the washing process can cool the bottom of the vial in the depyrogenation oven while the glass is heated from the outside.

During depyrogenation, the containers are typically heated to about 350 °C in order to reduce the risk of possible pyrogen contamination. As this temperature range is below the typical Tg temperatures of glasses used in pharmaceutical packaging, this process poses no risk in terms of thermal stresses. It does, however, facilitate the introduction of scratches that decrease the mechanical strength.

製造熱應力與退火機制

Tubular Vial 成型應力的來源

玻璃管成型時，底部與開口受熱（>1000°C），中段管身保持較低溫。局部加熱 → 原子振動加劇 → 快速冷卻 → 原子來不及重排 → 「凍結」在非理想位置 → 產生拉伸與壓縮應力。

退火爐（Annealing Lehr）的功能：將整支 vial 加熱至 Tg + 20°C，讓原子有時間重新排列，再以精確控制的速率緩冷，使應力均勻消散。

退火不完全的後果

若退火製程控制不當（溫度不夠、冷卻太快、爐內溫度不均）：

  • 殘留應力留存於 vial 瓶底或瓶口轉角處
  • 在後續處理（灌裝、壓蓋、輸送）時遇到外部衝擊，更易在拉伸應力區域破裂
  • 可用偏振光儀（Polariscope）量測殘留應力分布

這也說明為何 vendor qualification 應包含殘留應力檢測，而非僅依賴尺寸規格。

除熱原（Depyrogenation）的雙重影響

除熱原加熱至 ~350°C，低於玻璃 Tg（~550°C），因此不會引入新的熱應力。但這個製程存在另一種風險：

  • 洗瓶後殘留水分可能導致瓶底局部冷卻，與外部熱空氣形成溫差 → 熱衝擊微裂縫
  • 高溫下玻璃表面保護性吸附水層蒸發 → 表面暴露，金屬導軌接觸易產生刮痕 → 機械強度下降

詳見第 8.4 節的吸附層討論。

8.2.2.1.1 Exposure to Cold Temperatures

Glass will be exposed to temperatures of approximately -40 °C to -80 °C during lyophilization and of -150 °C to -196 °C for cold storage, including cryopreservation. The lower the CTE, the more stable the glass is in terms of thermal shock resistance, but the rate of change must be extremely high for thermal shock breakage.

Use of glass with lower CTEs may reduce the breakage risk due to rapid rate of change; however, surface defects still play the major role in glass breakage. Breakage during exposure to cold temperatures are primarily due to flaws present in the glass and the outward pressure of the internal materials, not thermal stress. The fewer defects there are on the bottom area and area of drug contact, the less likely the glass will break. Vial breakage during the lyophilization process is usually due to the mechanical stress from the expanding solution rather than thermal stress.

低溫環境下的破裂機制

凍乾破裂的真正原因

凍乾（Lyophilization）時 vial 暴露在 -40 至 -80°C，長期儲存（Cryopreservation）甚至到 -196°C（液氮）。但破裂的主因並非熱衝擊，而是：

  • 內容物膨脹的機械應力：溶液結冰體積增大，向外施加壓力
  • 既有表面缺陷：瓶底與藥液接觸區域的微裂縫在應力下擴展

結論：降低 CTE（選更好的玻璃）有幫助，但減少表面缺陷才是降低凍乾破裂率的根本策略。

CDMO 實務啟示

凍乾破裂風險管理重點：

  • 進廠檢驗包含瓶底外觀及缺陷目視/自動檢查
  • 選用缺陷率較低的 vendor（表面品質要求應列入規格書）
  • 灌裝後至進凍乾機前的輸送過程，最小化 vial 碰撞與刮傷
  • 凍乾配方開發時考慮結冰膨脹率，調整充填量

8.3 Mechanical Strength of Glass | 玻璃機械強度

8.3.1 Ductile versus Brittle Materials

Along with thermal stress, physical load can introduce a great amount of stress onto glass. Breakage will occur if the physical load is greater than the practical strength of the container.

Materials are generally differentiated into two failure mode categories: ductile and brittle. Ductile materials, like metals (and some polymer materials), deform under tensile stress; that is, they undergo a plastic deformation (changing their shape) rather than breaking immediately. During plastic deformation, the molecules in the material are able to move. This provides the material's ability to bend before breaking.

Brittle materials break before the molecules in the material can move sufficiently to support bending. The molecules are literally torn apart in place. Brittle materials, such as glasses, are unable to undergo plastic deformation and therefore, break abruptly.

延性 vs. 脆性材料

玻璃是脆性材料（Brittle material）

金屬等延性材料（Ductile material）在斷裂前會先塑性變形（Plastic deformation）：可以彎曲、拉伸，給操作者「警告訊號」。

玻璃是脆性材料：原子來不及移動，就已在缺陷尖端被撕開。這意味著：

  • 破裂是突發性的，無預兆
  • 任何缺陷都是潛在的破裂起點
  • 強調「預防」勝於「偵測」

類比：鉛筆 vs. 橡皮筋

橡皮筋（延性）：拉到一半會變細、警告你快斷了。鉛筆（脆性）：用力一折，毫無預警地「啪」一聲斷掉。玻璃更像鉛筆，而且斷裂後碎片可能汙染無菌藥液。

8.3.2 Practical Strength

For brittle materials like glass, practical strength depends on such parameters as geometric aspects of the glass piece, flaws inside and on the surface of the glass, residual stress inside the glass, and the interior and exterior mechanical loads on the glass. An equation to determine practical strength for brittle materials, developed by A. A. Griffith:

Griffith Fracture Criterion 
K = σ × Y × √c

Where:
  K  = Stress Intensity Factor
       (amplification of tensile stress at flaw tip)
  σ  = Applied stress
       (from external force or internal pressure)
  Y  = Geometric Factor
       (location and shape of the defect)
  √c = Critical Dimension
       (square root of defect depth/size)

Breakage occurs when K exceeds the fracture toughness (KIC) of the glass.

The stress (σ) applied to the glass can result from an impact with a material harder or equally as hard as glass (e.g., metal). Stress can also come from inside the container, such as when internal pressure is built up during lyophilization, from vertical pressure (e.g., during crimping), or from side pressure (e.g., a jammed screw feed).

The geometric factor (Y) takes into consideration the flaw geometries, including the relative location of the flaw (through thickness crack, edge/surface flaw, corner flaw, surface flaw shapes).

The critical dimension (c) describes the geometric aspects of the defect — the larger the radius of the flaw tip, the more the glass is weakened. A very deep flaw will lower the glass strength much more than a surface scratch.

Ultimately, breakage only occurs if both the stress and the flaw or stress riser are present in tandem, and the combined phenomena exceed the practical strength of the particular piece of glass.

Griffith 方程式的實務意義

方程式解讀：三個可干預的變數

  • σ（應力）：可透過製程設計降低。例如：優化輸送線速度、避免 vial 積壓、控制壓蓋力、使用彈性導軌。
  • Y（幾何因子）：缺陷位置影響應力集中程度。瓶底角落（Corner flaw）比側面表面缺陷（Surface flaw）更危險，因為邊角的幾何放大效應更強。
  • c（缺陷深度）：深度缺陷（Deep flaw）遠比淺層刮痕危險。這是為什麼碰撞痕（Chipping）比細線刮痕（Scratch）對強度的影響更大。

核心洞見：應力 + 缺陷必須同時存在

這是 TR87 Section 8 最重要的結論之一：

「Breakage only occurs if both the stress and the flaw are present in tandem.」

實務含義：

  • 沒有缺陷的玻璃，即使施加相當應力也不會輕易破裂
  • 有缺陷但零應力，也不會破裂
  • 兩者同時存在 → 風險急遽上升

因此，風險控制策略需同時攻防兩個方向：減少缺陷（表面品質管理）+ 降低應力（製程與設備設計）。

常見應力來源對應 TR87 操作建議

  • 垂直力（壓蓋）：在瓶頸外表面和瓶跟（Heel）產生拉伸應力 → 需控制壓蓋機扭矩/力道
  • 側向擠壓（螺旋推進器、輸送積壓）：在受力點對側的外表面產生拉伸應力 → 需防止 vial 在輸送線上卡住
  • 內部壓力（凍乾）：從容器內部向外施加應力 → 結冰膨脹是最主要來源

8.3.3 Tensile and Compressive Stress

Glass always breaks at the point of tensile stress location. When compressive stress is exerted from one side, it always induces tensile stress in certain other places, but these areas of tensile stress are not always clearly detectable.

(a) A downward force (e.g., capper) creates tensile stress in the outer surface of the neck and heel and compressive stress in the side wall.

(b) A side or squeeze-force (e.g., a backed-up conveyor or screw feed) creates tensile stress on the interior surface at the exterior load location and exterior surface 90 degrees from load.

應力分布的實際模式

玻璃永遠在拉伸應力點破裂

這個原則是操作風險評估的基礎。施力方向不等於破裂方向：

  • 壓蓋機（向下力）：壓縮力作用於側壁，但在瓶頸（Neck）外表面和瓶跟（Heel）產生拉伸應力 → 破裂常見於瓶頸或瓶底轉角
  • 輸送線擠壓（側向力）：受力點的容器內表面及受力方向垂直 90° 的外表面產生拉伸應力 → 破裂可能發生在遠離接觸點的位置

這解釋了為何生產線上的破裂位置，有時看起來與施力方向「不合邏輯」。

8.3.4 Surface Condition and its Impact on Practical Strength

During the lifecycle of a glass container, it passes through a number of handling steps. Any of these steps can create varying degrees of surface discontinuities, like scratches or even microfissures in the glass, that may decrease the functional strength of that particular glass container. Only in combination with a specific stress on the glass, however, does breakage occur.

Break force, strength, or failure probability measurements can be used to determine the extent that surface discontinuities and physical impact result in decreased practical strength. In certain cases, the effects of different handling processes can be determined by these measurements.

表面狀態與強度的動態關係

容器生命週期中的累積損傷

每個接觸 vial 的步驟都可能累積損傷：製造 → 包裝 → 倉儲 → 進料 → 洗瓶 → 除熱原 → 灌裝 → 壓蓋 → 出料 → 目視檢查。

單一輕微損傷可能不足以造成破裂，但「累積效應」（Cumulative damage）加上製程應力就可能超過強度閾值。這是 TR87 強調全程監控（End-to-end monitoring）的原因。

量測方法

破裂力（Break force）、強度（Strength）、及失效機率（Failure probability）量測可用於：

  • 比較不同 handling 步驟對強度的相對影響
  • 評估新製程或新設備對 vial 完整性的衝擊
  • 建立各製程步驟的「損傷基準線（Damage baseline）」

8.4 Adsorption Layer on the Glass | 玻璃表面吸附層

8.4 Adsorption Layer on the Glass

When glass is freshly heated or cleaned, the surface reacts with adsorbates from ambient air (e.g., water, hydrocarbons) that then saturate the surface. Initially, the water is only adsorbed, and a monolayer is developed. After a few weeks, however, the water diffuses deeper into the surface. This adsorption layer acts like a cover on the glass and protects it from scratches, and the more developed layer can better protect the container from scratches. This protective layer can be removed by heating the glass (e.g., depyrogenation oven); a monolayer will evaporate more quickly under dry air/heat conditions. As the kinetics of reestablishing the protective layer are very slow; submerging the glass in water will not accelerate the reaction.

This phenomenon of building up and evaporating the protective layer is equally valid for all glasses. This also infers that scratches and sticking behavior is a matter of glass handling rather than the composition of glass itself.

The water layer not only protects the glass from scratches, but it also plays a significant role as a lubricant, decreasing the friction between glass containers or between glass and metal. This property becomes especially important during the filling process as it can limit the number of scratches that the containers may receive, potentially allowing an increase in the speed of the filling line.

吸附水層的保護與潤滑機制

吸附層的三個階段

  • 第一階段（初始）：清潔或加熱後的玻璃表面迅速從空氣中吸附水分子，形成單分子層（Monolayer）
  • 第二階段（數週後）：水分子逐漸擴散滲入表面更深層，形成更厚的保護層，防護能力增強
  • 第三階段（加熱後）：除熱原或高溫處理使保護層蒸發，表面重新「裸露」，且重建速度非常慢

關鍵洞見：刮傷是 Handling 問題，不是材質問題

TR87 明確指出：「Scratches and sticking behavior is a matter of glass handling rather than the composition of glass itself.」

實務含義：

  • 除熱原後 vial 剛出爐時，表面保護層已蒸發 → 這是整個製程中玻璃最脆弱的時刻
  • 此時進行高速輸送或與金屬表面接觸，刮傷風險最高
  • 浸水無法加速保護層重建（反應動力學（Kinetics）太慢）
  • 兩種不同成分的玻璃，若 handling 方式一致，刮傷傾向相近

類比：防曬乳的比喻

吸附水層就像皮膚上的天然油脂層：洗澡（清潔 = 除熱原）之後，皮膚最乾燥、最脆弱。需要一段時間才能重新分泌足夠的保護油脂。玻璃的吸附層機制與此非常相似。

灌裝線速度優化的科學依據

吸附水層也是 lubricant（潤滑劑），降低 vial 與 vial 之間、vial 與金屬導軌之間的摩擦係數（COF）。

在吸附層完整的條件下，可適度提高灌裝線速度而不顯著增加刮傷率。但除熱原後剛進入灌裝線的 vial，應特別注意輸送速度控制，或使用外部潤滑措施（如 Section 8.5 的化學強化塗層）。

8.5 Chemical Strengthening | 化學強化

8.5 Chemical Strengthening

Chemical strengthening by ion exchange creates a residual compression layer on the inner and outer glass vial surfaces that resists the applied tensile stresses required to propagate flaws. It can also hinder minor to moderate surface damage, blocking the routes along which cracks could otherwise propagate and, thereby, prevent breakage. Chemical strengthening can be performed on aluminosilicate, borosilicate, or soda-lime silicate glass containers with sufficient available sodium ions to increase their mechanical strength. Because the glass tubing is heated in the container formation process, any chemical process must be performed on the final container, not the glass tubing.

To avoid thermal shock, the empty glass containers are gradually heated to the bath temperature before immersion in a potassium melt (e.g., KNO₃) where an ion exchange reaction takes place. In this reaction, sodium ions (Na⁺) from the near-surface regions are exchanged for potassium ions (K⁺). Potassium ions are larger in diameter than sodium ions (K⁺'s radius is roughly 0.38 Å larger than that of Na⁺), leading to compressive forces that let the glass withstand higher external loads.

At the end of the designated immersion time, the containers are removed from the bath, allowed to cool, undergo thorough salt-removal process, and are then dried.

This surface compressive stress creates an additional barrier to prevent the formation and propagation of checks as well as suppressing the creation of visible glass particles. Chemical strengthening also improves the overall robustness of the vial, reducing the probability of breakage from high-stress loading and particulate generation from fragmentation.

Applying an additional protective coating to the outer surface of the glass container will lower its COF and minimize surface damage during frictive sliding contact.

離子交換強化機制解析

化學強化的核心原理

離子交換（Ion exchange）製程將玻璃表面層的小離子換成大離子，迫使表面受到壓縮應力（Compressive stress）：

  • Na⁺（較小）→ K⁺（較大，半徑差約 0.38 Å）
  • K⁺ 嵌入後「撐開」周圍空間 → 表面持續受到壓縮力
  • 要讓裂縫擴展，外部拉伸應力必須先克服這層壓縮預力 → 大幅提高破裂閾值

這與鋼化玻璃（Tempered glass）的原理類似，但化學方法可處理複雜形狀（如 vial）而不需要快速風冷。

化學強化的多重效益

  • 防止裂縫擴展（Crack propagation）：表面壓縮層物理性封閉裂縫路徑
  • 抑制玻璃微粒生成（Particulate generation）：減少碎裂風險，對注射劑安全性有直接意義
  • 提升整體機械強度：高應力負載（如壓蓋）下的破裂機率下降
  • 適用所有玻璃類型：前提是含有足夠的 Na⁺（鋁矽玻璃、硼矽玻璃、鈉鈣矽玻璃均可）

製程限制：必須在最終容器上處理

由於玻璃管在成型（Forming）過程中會受熱，化學強化產生的壓縮層會被破壞。因此，化學強化製程必須在容器成型完成後才能進行，不能在玻璃管階段預先處理。

這也意味著：化學強化是下游（Downstream）工序，需整合到容器供應鏈中，而非原材料規格問題。

類比：預壓混凝土

化學強化就像預力混凝土（Pre-stressed concrete）：在鋼筋上先施加預壓力，讓混凝土在受外部拉力前必須先克服這個預壓。化學強化的壓縮層就是玻璃表面的「預壓鋼筋」。

保護塗層（Protective Coating）的附加效益

在化學強化的基礎上，外表面施加保護性塗層（如 SiO₂ 或有機薄膜）可進一步降低摩擦係數（COF），減少灌裝線上的滑動接觸損傷。這兩種措施是相輔相成的，可組合使用於高風險/高價值產品。

Section 8.0 Summary | 本節重點總結（繁體中文）

  • 玻璃類型選擇的物理依據：Type I 硼矽玻璃（Borosilicate）的低 CTE（3.3–5.0 × 10⁻⁶ K⁻¹）使其熱衝擊耐受力遠超 Type III 鈉鈣矽玻璃（7.1–9.3 × 10⁻⁶ K⁻¹），這是注射劑必須使用 Type I 的物理基礎，而非單純的法規要求。
  • 退火完整性決定殘留應力水準：玻璃成型後必須在退火爐（Annealing Lehr）中以精確速率冷卻，使應力在退火點至應變點範圍內充分消散。退火不完全的 vial，在後續製程的外部應力下更易在拉伸應力集中區域破裂。
  • 破裂的雙重條件：根據 Griffith 方程式 K = σ × Y × √c，破裂只有在「足夠應力」與「既有缺陷」同時存在且超過玻璃斷裂韌性時才會發生。這意味著風險控制必須雙管齊下：減少表面缺陷（精細 handling）+ 降低操作應力（設備與製程設計）。
  • 吸附水層保護機制：玻璃表面的吸附水層（Adsorption water layer）兼具保護與潤滑功能。除熱原（350°C）後此層蒸發，且無法透過水浸快速重建。因此，剛完成除熱原的 vial 是整個製程中最易受刮傷的時間點，需特別控制輸送速度與接觸條件。
  • 化學強化是現代損傷防護技術：以 KNO₃ 熔浴進行離子交換（Na⁺ → K⁺），在 vial 表面建立壓縮應力層，需在最終容器上進行（不能預先處理玻璃管）。化學強化能抑制裂縫擴展、減少玻璃微粒生成，並提升對高應力負載的整體耐受性，是高價值注射劑產品的重要風險緩解措施。
