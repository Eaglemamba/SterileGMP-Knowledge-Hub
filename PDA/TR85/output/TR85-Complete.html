1.0 Introduction

原文內容 Original Text

Visual inspection of filled injectable products is an important part of the control of the manufacturing process. It offers a means to improve quality by removing noncompliant units and provides information to drive continuous process improvement.

Visual Inspection (VI) may be accomplished in several ways: manually (MVI) with the unaided eye under controlled conditions of lighting and time; semi-automated visual inspection (SAVI), which combines 1) automated handling of the containers to be inspected with human vision, and 2) a means to determine whether an individual unit should be accepted or rejected; and thirdly, automated visual inspection (AVI), which offers a validatable method using digital cameras to acquire images of the product and computational tools to analyze those images against preset criteria to determine if the unit is acceptable. While MVI is the reference method described in all the pharmacopoeias, all three categories are acceptable with appropriate qualification or validation.

For MVI, the visible range for particles in many filled injectable products is generally agreed to begin at 100 µm based on studies in clear glass vials (1). However, not all particles of this size can be reliably detected. Nominal probability of detection (PoD) for 100 µm particles is often 40-50% with PoD increasing with increasing particle size. Single particles greater than 200 µm in clear solution and in 10 mL clear vials typically achieve a PoD >95% (2).

To reliably meet visible particle acceptance criterion for filled products, careful control of the components used in the process is required. Criteria must generally be tighter than that of the finished product to compensate for the cumulative effects of multiple components (e.g., container and elastomeric seal), potential sources from the formulation and filling equipment, and the manufacturing environment. Today, most components are not subjected to tighter requirements as part of their incoming inspection and acceptance for use. However, specifications that meet stricter requirements are most critical for ready-to-sterilize (RTS) and ready-to-use (RTU) components since they do not undergo further particle removal steps.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Visual Inspection, VI (目視檢查): 注射劑成品製程中不可或缺的品質管控手段。不只是「挑出壞品」，更是持續改善製程的資訊來源。

三種檢查方式：

  • MVI (人工目視檢查): 以裸眼在受控光源和時間條件下檢查，是各國藥典的參考方法
  • SAVI (半自動目視檢查): 自動化處理容器 + 人眼判斷 + 接受/拒絕決策
  • AVI (自動目視檢查): 使用數位相機擷取影像，以計算工具分析是否符合預設標準，可被驗證

Probability of Detection, PoD (偵測機率): 特定粒子在特定檢查方法與條件下被看見並移除的可能性。100 µm 粒子的 PoD 僅約 40-50%，而 200 µm 粒子可達 >95%。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你在餐廳出餐前檢查每道菜是否有異物。MVI 就像主廚用肉眼一盤盤看；SAVI 就像有輸送帶自動送菜到主廚面前，但仍靠人眼判斷；AVI 則像裝了高解析度攝影機和 AI 辨識系統，自動偵測異常。

重點在於：即使主廚眼力再好，100 µm 的微小異物（比一根頭髮還細）也只有一半機率被發現。這就是為什麼我們不能只依賴最終檢查，而必須從源頭管控原料和元件的清潔度。

重點提示 Key Notes

RTS/RTU 元件為何特別重要？

Ready-to-Sterilize (RTS，即用型滅菌) 和 Ready-to-Use (RTU，即用型) 元件在使用前不會再經過額外的清洗或微粒去除步驟。這意味著供應商交付時的微粒水準就是最終水準。

對 CDMO 而言，這代表：

  • 進料檢驗規格必須比成品規格更嚴格
  • 與元件供應商的品質協議 (Quality Agreement) 中必須明確納入微粒管控要求
  • 累積效應 (cumulative effect)：容器 + 膠塞 + 充填設備 + 環境，每個來源的微粒都會加總

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 情境：你的客戶要求注射劑成品的可見微粒拒絕率 (rejection rate) 不超過 0.1%。如果你使用 RTU 膠塞和 RTU 玻璃瓶，而這兩者各自就可能貢獻微粒，再加上充填設備和環境的微粒來源 -- 每個環節的規格都必須遠嚴於最終成品的 0.1% 目標。這就是「累積效應」的實際商業意義。

1.1 Purpose

原文內容 Original Text

This technical report addresses test and inspection methods for visible particles in primary packaging components for injectable products specifically glass vials and syringes, and the elastomeric closures used to seal them. This document is most relevant for RTS and RTU components since these are not subjected to a further cleaning process immediately before use. These studies and methods were developed as part of a larger initiative to eliminate visible particles in injectable products. An overview of this initiative was published (3) previously, and a detailed analysis of particle entry points to the manufacturing process was subsequently published (4).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Primary Packaging Components (初級包材元件): 直接接觸藥品的容器和密封件，包括玻璃瓶 (glass vials)、注射器 (syringes) 和彈性膠塞 (elastomeric closures)。

TR85 的定位：本報告聚焦於「元件端」的微粒檢測與檢驗方法 -- 也就是在充填之前，針對包材本身建立可靠的測試方法。這是整個「消除注射劑可見微粒」大型倡議的一環。

比喻說明 Analogy

如果把注射劑製造比喻為做一道料理，TR85 關注的不是最終成品的品質檢查（那是上桌前試吃），而是食材進廚房前的品質篩選 -- 確保玻璃碗沒有裂痕碎屑、矽膠蓋沒有脫落碎片。食材本身如果帶進異物，不管廚師手藝再好，成品品質都無法保證。

重點提示 Key Notes

TR85 的產業背景：本報告是 PDA 推動的一系列消除可見微粒倡議的具體成果。先前已發表概覽 (reference 3) 及微粒進入製程的詳細分析 (reference 4)。TR85 補足了測試方法的缺口 -- 沒有可靠且標準化的測試方法，就無法設定有意義的規格限度。

對 CDMO 業者而言，這意味著你現在可以對供應商說：「我們依據 PDA TR85 的方法來評估你的元件品質。」這提供了一個產業公認的技術基準。

1.2 Scope

原文內容 Original Text

The use of particle, particulate, and particulate matter all have the same meaning when used within this report. They are used in various global standards and compendia with equivalent meaning. In general, this term applies to foreign material which is unintended and either removable from the surface of a primary packaging component or freely moving within a filled and sealed pharmaceutical dosage form.

The test and inspection methods discussed within this report are the first step in a standard setting process. Once test and inspection methods are available, accepted, and implemented, they can be used to assess process capability and establish meaningful process control limits. Without reliable measurements, reliable control limits are not possible.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Particle / Particulate / Particulate Matter (微粒/粒狀物質): 在本報告中三者等義互換。指的是非預期的外來物質，可能附著在初級包材表面或在密封的藥品劑型內自由移動。

Standard Setting Process (標準制定流程): TR85 的方法是這個流程的第一步。邏輯鏈為：

  • Step 1: 建立可靠的測試方法 (本報告的核心)
  • Step 2: 使用這些方法評估製程能力 (process capability)
  • Step 3: 基於製程能力數據建立有意義的管控限度 (control limits)

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像要管控工廠的室內空氣品質。你不能說「空氣要乾淨」就算設好標準了。你必須先有可靠的空氣品質檢測儀器（對應 TR85 的測試方法），用它去量測現有狀況（對應製程能力評估），然後才能設定合理的 PM2.5 限值（對應管控限度）。沒有好的量測，一切限值都是空談。

重點提示 Key Notes

「Without reliable measurements, reliable control limits are not possible.」

這句話是整份報告的核心哲學。在製藥業，我們常常看到規格限度是基於傳統或經驗而非數據。TR85 推動的是以數據驅動的品質管控（data-driven quality control）-- 先有方法，再收數據，最後用數據說話。

這也與 ICH Q10 的精神一致：持續的品質改善需要建立在客觀量測的基礎上。

2.0 Glossary and Abbreviations

原文內容 Original Text

Definitions have been provided to help clarify the concepts discussed in this technical report. While some of these definitions vary among companies, the definitions described below are consistent for use within this document.

Glossary Terms

Area
Measured surface area of a flat projection of a particle.

Aspect Ratio
The length of a particle or fiber divided by its width.

Extrinsic Particle or Fiber
Foreign material that comes from outside the primary process. Often these are from the manufacturing environment or incomplete cleaning of components. They are uncontrolled.

Feret Min and Max
Feret Min is the minimum distance between parallel tangents at opposing particle borders. Feret Max is the maximum distance between parallel tangents at opposing particle borders.

Intrinsic Particle
A particle that comes from within the primary process. These are qualified product contact materials and are often associated with the primary packaging components. They are unplanned but not unexpected.

Length
The measure of the distance along its longest axis.

Light obscuration (LO)
Analytical technique measuring the size and quantity of subvisible particles.

Membrane/microscopy (MM)
Analytical technique to collect particles from a liquid sample on a membrane filter followed by manual examination (sizing and counting) with a microscope.

Particle
Foreign material which is unintended and either removable from the surface of a primary packaging component or freely moving within a filled and sealed pharmaceutical dosage form. The term particle is used interchangeably with "particulate" and "particulate matter" without any intended difference in meaning.

Particulate
See Particle.

Perimeter
Measured length of the outer edges of a particle.

Probability of detection (PoD)
The likelihood that a given particle will be seen and removed with a specific inspection method and under specific conditions. Usually expressed as a percentage (0-100%) but may also be expressed as a probability (0-1).

Rect A
The longest side length when a rectangle is constructed around the particle.

Ready-to-sterilize (RTS)
Washed components supplied in a package suitable for sterilization prior to use with sterile injectable products.

Ready-to-use (RTU)
Washed and sterilized components supplied in a package suitable for transfer into an aseptic processing area and used with sterile injectable products without further processing.

Shape factor (SF)/Circularity
Provides information on the roundness of a particle. The SF value is between 0 and 1, where 1 represents a circle. A is the area of the particle and p is the perimeter.

Subvisible particle
A particle with a size <100 µm in diameter.

Visible fiber
A fiber-like particle with an aspect ratio ≥5 and a length >300 µm.

Visible particle
A particle with an aspect ratio of <5 and a length >100 µm.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

微粒來源分類 -- Extrinsic vs Intrinsic：

  • Extrinsic Particle (外來微粒): 來自製程外部，例如環境空氣中的纖維、清洗不完全殘留。這些是「不可控的」(uncontrolled)。
  • Intrinsic Particle (內在微粒): 來自製程內部的合格接觸材料，例如膠塞碎片、玻璃微粒。「非計劃但非意外」(unplanned but not unexpected)。

這個分類非常重要，因為不同來源的微粒需要不同的管控策略。外來微粒要靠環境和清潔管控；內在微粒要靠元件品質和製程參數管控。

核心概念解析 -- 微粒形態參數

為什麼需要這麼多形態參數？ 因為微粒不是規則的球體，每種量測方式捕捉到的資訊不同：

  • Aspect Ratio (長寬比): 區分纖維和顆粒的關鍵 -- 長寬比 ≥5 為纖維，<5 為顆粒
  • Feret Min/Max: 以平行切線量測的最小/最大距離，常用於自動影像分析
  • Shape Factor/Circularity (形狀因子): 0 到 1 之間，1 代表完美圓形。可幫助判斷微粒來源
  • Area、Perimeter、Length、Rect A: 不同的尺寸量測方式，各有適用場景

比喻說明 Analogy

把微粒分類想像成偵探辦案。你在犯罪現場發現一個可疑物體：

  • Extrinsic: 就像窗戶被打破後飛進來的外部碎片 -- 來源在場外
  • Intrinsic: 就像房間內家具自然磨損掉落的碎屑 -- 來源在場內

而各種形態參數就像偵探的量測工具：有的量長度、有的量面積、有的分析形狀 -- 綜合起來才能判斷「這個異物是什麼、從哪來的」。

重點提示 Key Notes

可見微粒 vs 不可見微粒的界線：

  • Visible particle (可見微粒): 長寬比 <5 且長度 >100 µm
  • Visible fiber (可見纖維): 長寬比 ≥5 且長度 >300 µm
  • Subvisible particle (不可見微粒): 尺寸 <100 µm

注意這裡有一個灰色地帶：100-300 µm 之間、長寬比 ≥5 的纖維既不是「可見纖維」也不是「不可見微粒」。在實務中，這類邊界情況需要特別注意分類標準的應用。

重點提示 -- RTS vs RTU

關鍵差異：

  • RTS (即用型滅菌): 已清洗，需自行滅菌後使用
  • RTU (即用型): 已清洗且已滅菌，可直接轉入無菌區使用

兩者的共同點是：使用前都不會再清洗。因此供應商交付時的微粒水準就是你最終面對的水準。這是為什麼 TR85 對這兩類元件特別重要。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 情境：你收到一批 RTU 膠塞的微粒檢測報告，報告中使用了 Feret Max 作為尺寸量測依據。但你們內部的 AVI 系統是使用 Length（最長軸）來判定。由於同一個不規則形狀的微粒，Feret Max 和 Length 可能給出不同數值，因此你必須確認量測定義的一致性，否則同一批膠塞可能在供應商端「合格」，在你的系統中「不合格」。

這就是為什麼 TR85 花大量篇幅統一術語定義。

  • 目視檢查 (VI) 的三種模式：MVI (人工)、SAVI (半自動)、AVI (全自動) -- 皆為藥典認可方法，但各有偵測極限

  • 形態參數統一：Aspect Ratio、Feret、Shape Factor 等定義的一致性是跨組織溝通和品質協議的基礎

3.1 Patient Risk Assessment

原文內容 Original Text

All parenteral drug preparations contain particulate matter. Particles ultimately administered to a patient may originate from the primary packaging components or may enter through the manufacturing process and during administration from the syringe or infusion set apparatus. Therefore, the potential patient particulate exposure burden derives from the sum of these sources.

The clinical implications of visible particulate matter in injectables have been reviewed in several recent publications (7,8). Historically the presence of unintended visible particulate matter has been regarded as a quality defect which can impact safety resulting in additional batch-related actions, including batch reinspection, rejection, or recall. Visible product defects such as cracked or leaking containers or product discoloration often point to a product quality and possible safety issue. While the presence of a rare or isolated visible particle induces a similar instinctive response, the potential safety implication requires a comprehensive and contextually-based evaluation.

Patient exposure to particles occurs across a size continuum from submicron to millimeters, while risk assessments are generally conducted on particles reported as visible or subvisible components and not the aggregated risk of both subgroups. Assessing the potential risk that patient exposure to particles might present requires identifying the source of the particle; determining particle-specific attributes such as size, number, and composition; and ascertaining the intended use including the route and frequency of administration and relevant patient population characteristics (9). Any impact to the final product's benefit-risk profile that might result from the presence of particulate matter arises from the combination of these elements and calculated once the reference product and characteristics of the intended recipient are known.

In all instances of injections, the size of the particle is a restricting attribute. The particle must be small enough (or nonrigid) to pass through the bore of the needle (i.e., the particle must be less than the internal diameter of the needle in at least two dimensions). Detection probability increases with size such that larger particles are more likely to be identified with a detection probability (under ideal conditions) typically exceeding 95% for particles that are 200 µm and larger (2). Consequently, the product is either rejected during manufacture or found during dose preparation and discarded. There have been no reports identifying patient harm from randomly administered visible particulate matter in pharmaceutical preparations (8). Therefore, particles that are ultimately injected will be at or below the limit of detection of the qualified manufacturing environment with most of those particles being subvisible.

In most instances, the greatest risk to patients receiving injectable medicines is related to a lack of product sterility. For this reason alone, a robust inspection program is not sufficient; but rather, a broad range of other visible attributes is assessed along with laboratory testing of microbiological, chemical, and physical parameters. These are based on statistical sampling plans to assess the risk of elevated bioburden or other related harms in the batch. The risks to product sterility may be mitigated through processing decisions such as qualified component and product terminal sterilization processes (e.g., moist or dry heat, irradiation, or gaseous or vapor treatments). While the presence of a single particle is unlikely to provide a sufficient substrate to support microbiologic proliferation, multiple units identified with extrinsic particles should raise an alarm since the source is likely from a nonsterile environment. Appropriately qualified depyrogenation and autoclaving processes preclude the introduction of bioburden through primary packaging components.

Inert particles such as those from elastomers or glass generally pose little concern when introduced into a patient's subcutaneous tissue or muscle because they are typically sterile or sanitized. However, the particles could potentially stimulate a local immunologic reaction consisting of macrophages and foreign body giant cells at the interface of the particulate material (e.g., a granuloma), but it is likely that such a reaction would remain asymptomatic (10). More commonly, complications from subcutaneous and intramuscular medications arise from the irritating properties of the drug product and are often drug-specific but generally have a minimal impact (11).

Intravenous (IV) infusion of particles may induce phlebitis due to the particles causing direct traumatic irritation to the vein or from chemical damage by undissolved particles (12). Recent observational studies have also described instances in select patient cohorts where morbidity has been reduced through the use of infusion filters, suggesting that particles in the infusate were responsible for the difference in outcomes (13). Other studies have failed to corroborate these findings in additional patient populations (14).

A particle introduced intravenously that exceeds the internal diameter of a pulmonary capillary (7–12 µm) will lodge in that capillary or a more proximal pulmonary arteriole. While clinical outcomes are generally benign, such matter introduced into the systemic circulation might stimulate a local immunologic reaction (e.g., a granuloma) in the lung (15). These isolated, small parenchymal lesions generally remain asymptomatic. In addition, as demonstrated in animal studies, the massive infusion of particulates has been accompanied by histologic evidence of injury to pulmonary capillary endothelial cells (16), microscopic thrombi in the pulmonary capillaries (17), pulmonary microscopic granulomata (18), and hepatic inflammatory effects (19). An important distinction to note is that most of the studies above infused massive amounts of subvisible particles. While an all-inclusive review of the clinical implications of particulate infusions is beyond the intended scope of this technical report, the potential exists for the intravascular infusion of particulate matter to cause pathophysiologic injury depending upon the size and quantity of the particles, and, therefore, should be included as an element in any clinical risk assessment.

"In summary, particulate matter will be present in every injectable dose. The risk of visible particles in injections to a patient is low from products manufactured and inspected under appropriately qualified current Good Manufacturing Practices conditions (20,21). Their origin, composition, size, numbers, and characteristics of the intended patient population are all important factors to weigh when assessing the clinical import from exposure."

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Particulate Exposure Burden (微粒暴露負擔)：患者最終接收到的微粒總量，來源包括包裝元件（玻璃瓶、橡膠塞）、製造過程，以及給藥時的注射器或輸液裝置。風險評估必須考慮所有這些來源的累積效應。

3.2 Existing Test Methods and Limitations

原文內容 Original Text

Visual inspection of primary packaging components prior to use is hindered by four factors: the lack of a standardized industry inspection method for particle detection; the diversity of potential particle defects introduced through the manufacturing process; decreased visual detection capabilities on isolated or empty components; and the probabilistic nature of particle detection (3). Visual inspection of the packaging components is generally conducted by sampling and not inspection of every piece (100% inspection), thereby limiting the information necessary to construct an adequate library of the type and number of particles the process produces. Detection of particles by MVI, SAVI, or AVI is a cumulative function of the particles' visible attributes including particle size, shape, color, density, and reflectivity. Particle detection can also be more difficult in the empty components because the particles cannot be put in motion as with particles in a liquid product (22).

The absence of standardized methods or visible particle acceptance criteria leaves it to each supplier and pharmaceutical manufacturer to establish these requirements for each product supplied. While regulatory guidance documents and industry publications may provide useful perspectives, the absence of a clearly articulated standard results in manufacturers subsequently adopting a hybrid approach using various portions of the published pharmacopeial (6,23) and International Standards Organization (ISO) methods (24). Identifying the limitations of test methods adapted for the evaluation of primary packaging components provides the opportunity to develop component-specific methods that would serve as a common standard for supplier-customer quality consideration.

Understanding the particle attributes is essential for each manufacturer when developing and adopting appropriate test methods, characterizing particle defect attributes, defining achievable detection capabilities, and assessing the potential impact of particles to the product's benefit-risk profile. The use of standard forensic methods of particle characterization (22) can be beneficial to inform the clinical risk assessment and direct allocation of resources to critical visual inspection and process control strategies. Additionally, the forensic datasets are important components to identify potential sources of particle ingress or generation, variability in raw material components, and monitor the stability of manufacturing processes (4).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

MVI / SAVI / AVI (目視檢測方法三大類)：

  • MVI (Manual Visual Inspection，人工目視檢測)：由訓練合格的檢驗員在標準化條件下以肉眼檢查
  • SAVI (Semi-Automated Visual Inspection，半自動目視檢測)：機器輔助旋轉/照明，但最終由人員判定
  • AVI (Automated Visual Inspection，自動目視檢測)：完全由機器視覺系統完成檢測和判定

Forensic Particle Characterization (微粒鑑識分析)：使用顯微鏡、光譜分析等方法，鑑定微粒的來源、成分和形態。這是建立微粒知識庫和追溯污染根因的基礎工具。

Probabilistic Nature of Detection (檢測的機率特性)：微粒檢測不是「有或無」的確定性結果，而是受微粒大小、形狀、顏色、密度、反射率等多重屬性影響的機率事件。同一顆微粒在不同條件下可能被檢出或遺漏。

比喻說明 Analogy

包裝元件的微粒檢測就像在空房間裡找灰塵 -- 比在一杯水裡找懸浮物困難得多。水中的微粒可以搖晃後觀察運動軌跡，但空的玻璃瓶或橡膠塞上的微粒是靜止的、可能黏附在表面，檢測難度大幅增加。這就是為什麼現有的藥典方法（設計給充填後的液體產品）在應用於空的包裝元件時效果有限。

重點提示 Key Notes

包裝元件檢測的四大障礙：

  1. 缺乏標準化方法 -- 沒有業界統一的包裝元件微粒檢測標準
  2. 缺陷多樣性 -- 製造過程可能產生各種不同類型的微粒缺陷
  3. 空元件檢測困難 -- 微粒無法像液體中那樣被驅動運動
  4. 檢測本身的機率性 -- 無法保證100%檢出率

這四個障礙構成了 TR85 存在的核心理由 -- 正是因為現有方法不足，才需要開發增強型測試方法。

3.2.1 ISO Test Method 8871-3

原文內容 Original Text

ISO 8871-3 Elastomeric parts for parenterals and for devices for pharmaceutical use — Part 3: Determination of released-particle count (24) provides analytical methods for the extraction of loose particulates from the surface of elastomeric components used in pharmaceutical packaging applications. The ISO standard is split into subvisible particle extraction using light extinction (or light obscuration (LO)) and visible particle extraction followed by membrane filtration and microscopic examination (MM) for particle detection. Once particle extraction is complete, quantification is performed with an optical microscope and a calibrated reticle at approximately 50x magnification. Particle counts can be conducted either manually or via a validated automated microscopic method. Both the particle extraction and the counting technique are considered optimized in evaluating elastomer-associated visible particles. However, the method would require some level of enhancement for visible particles (i.e., particles >100 µm and fibers >300 µm as defined in this report) through the design of a reticle capable of differentiating particles from fibers and identifying a shaker for use in Europe that is comparable to that specified in ISO 8871-3. Additionally, further concern exists regarding the sensitivity of the method based on the surface area/number of stoppers sampled. Currently, there is no similar ISO method for particle extraction or characterization for parenteral container inspection.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

ISO 8871-3：專門針對注射劑用橡膠元件（如瓶塞、柱塞）表面釋放微粒的定量分析方法。包含兩個主要分析路徑：

  • LO (Light Obscuration，光阻法)：用於亞可見微粒（通常 ≥10 µm），透過雷射感測微粒對光的遮擋來計數
  • MM (Microscopic Method，顯微鏡法)：用於可見微粒，通過膜過濾收集後在50倍光學顯微鏡下使用校準標尺計數

Calibrated Reticle (校準標尺)：置入顯微鏡目鏡中的刻度尺，用於測量微粒尺寸。TR85 指出需要改良設計，使其能區分微粒（particles）和纖維（fibers）。

比喻說明 Analogy

ISO 8871-3 的方法就像「洗衣服後檢查洗衣水」-- 先把橡膠塞放入溶液中搖洗（萃取），把表面鬆散的微粒沖下來，然後用濾紙過濾（膜過濾），最後在放大鏡下數濾紙上有多少顆粒（顯微鏡計數）。這個方法對橡膠元件效果不錯，但如果要檢查玻璃瓶，目前還沒有類似的「標準洗滌檢查法」。

重點提示 Key Notes

ISO 8871-3 的三個關鍵侷限：

  1. 可見微粒的增強需求：對於 >100 µm 的微粒和 >300 µm 的纖維，需要改良標尺設計才能有效區分
  2. 地區性設備差異：歐洲需要找到與 ISO 8871-3 指定震盪器等效的設備
  3. 取樣靈敏度：對於不同表面積/數量的橡膠塞，取樣策略的靈敏度是否足夠仍有疑慮

最大缺口：目前完全沒有類似的 ISO 方法可用於玻璃容器的微粒萃取和鑑定。這是 TR85 要解決的核心問題之一。

3.2.2 U.S. Pharmacopeia Methods

原文內容 Original Text

USP Chapters <788> and <790> are generally used to provide analytical method guidance for packaging component particle assessment. USP <788> Particulate Matter in Injections (23) provides for two test methods for the detection of particles ≥ 10 µm in size: LO and MM. The methods do not appear suitable for elastomeric component particles since surface particle extraction methods are not outlined, and the sample preparation and counting techniques do not appear applicable for particles >100 µm. Likewise, the applicability of these methods in the evaluation of glass-related components hold limited utility for visible particles. While nonfixed particles in glass components might be identified using these methods, the diversity of particle morphology and container design significantly impact the sensitivity and applicability of these methods to glass components.

USP <790> Visible Particulates in Injections (6) became official in 2014. Chapter <790>, along with Chapter <1790> Visual Inspection of Injections (1) established requirements and provides supporting information to be used along with 100% inspection during the manufacturing process to demonstrate that the process has produced a batch that is "essentially free" of visible particulates.

Table 3.2.2-1🔍 provides a comparison of current inspection conditions and acceptance criteria for visible particles contained in the USP, EP, and JP. The chapters describe the manual inspection environment (lighting, background, and handling methods) that many component manufacturers have adapted for the inspection of empty containers and loose components. However, particle detection can also be more difficult in these components because the particles cannot be put in motion as with particles in a liquid product and the broad range of potential manufacturing defects generated by glass primary packaging suppliers. Additionally, these chapters are intended for filled-finished product and do not include defined test methods with clear requirements and specifications directly applicable to the individual packaging components used to manufacture injectable products. Therefore, the need for packaging component visual inspection guidance, which is currently lacking in the pharmacopeias, is critical to help characterize risk associated with these components and their impact on overall product quality assurance.

USP <788> (注射劑中微粒物質)：針對亞可見微粒（≥10 µm）的定量測試方法，使用 LO 和 MM 兩種技術。但此方法未包含從包裝元件表面萃取微粒的步驟，且對 >100 µm 的可見微粒不適用。

USP <790> + <1790> (可見微粒檢測體系)：<790> 是強制性章節，規定了「essentially free（基本無可見微粒）」的要求及 AQL=0.65% 的統計接受標準；<1790> 是參考性章節，提供100%目視檢測的支援資訊。兩者結合構成了美國藥典的可見微粒管控框架。

"Essentially Free" (基本無可見微粒)：USP <790> 使用的關鍵術語，承認完全零微粒是不現實的，但要求透過合格的檢測程序證明批次達到可接受的品質水準。

比喻說明 Analogy

USP <790> 的「essentially free」概念，就像餐廳宣稱「本廚房基本無過敏原污染」-- 不是說絕對零污染（在現實中不可能），而是說已經採取了所有合理措施來最小化風險，並且能用統計數據證明。AQL=0.65% 就是那個統計證據 -- 相當於說「每檢查150個產品，允許不超過1個有缺陷」。

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

這個邏輯鏈展示了 TR85 從「技術方法」到「商業價值」再到「患者安全」的完整影響路徑。對 CDMO 而言，採用 TR85 方法不僅是技術升級，更是提升供應鏈管理能力和客戶信任的戰略投資。

4.0 Elastomeric Closures Test Method

原文內容 Original Text

To standardized a method that will allow both elastomer suppliers and pharmaceutical companies to have meaningful data where it is reasonable to make comparisons, a new method was developed leveraging accepted aspects of the existing USP <788> Method 2 (23) and the ISO 8871-3 methods (24). The new method described in Section 4.1 (the Enhanced Closures Testing Method) is specific to characterizing the visible particle load present in elastomeric closures and establishes a test to perform specifically on elastomeric closures after they are collected in their final packaging to ensure test results are representative of the product as used by pharmaceutical manufacturers.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Enhanced Closures Testing Method (增強型封蓋檢測方法): 這是 TR85 的核心貢獻 -- 一套全新的標準化方法，專門用於量化彈性體封蓋上的可見微粒負載。它融合了 USP <788> Method 2（美國藥典的微粒檢測方法）與 ISO 8871-3（國際標準化組織針對彈性體封蓋的規範）的優勢。

4.1 Enhanced Closures Testing Method Development

原文內容 Original Text

Currently, specific but different guidance requirements exist for visible and subvisible particulates in parenterals. For final product inspection, the distinction between visible and subvisible is established by what can be seen by the unaided human eye when performing the compendial method inspection of approximately 10 sec per container in front of black and white backgrounds under controlled lighting. If a particle is observed in a final-filled container by the unaided eye of a qualified inspector, it is classified as a visible particulate defect and considered against the visible particle requirements such as those defined in USP <790> (6).

In the case of packaging components for injectable products, suppliers of these components often perform an inspection for particulate matter at the time of release. Likewise, pharmaceutical manufacturing companies often perform a particulate test at incoming receipt. The testing of these components is intended to demonstrate that they meet specification and are appropriate for their intended use. As with final product testing, component testing should be capable of classifying any particulate on the components as either visible or subvisible. In cases where the packaging components are inspected by the unaided human eye for particulate, the same principles for classification apply. Namely, any particulate observed by the unaided human eye as part of packaging component testing will be considered visible.

In some cases, testing for particulate in packaging components will use analytical methods that exceed the ability of the unaided human eye. The most common example of such testing is a method that involves rinsing packaging components, filtering this rinsate, and then examining the filter under a microscope to measure and count any observed particulates. Microscopic examination will detect a wide range of particles including some which are not visible to the unaided human eye (i.e., subvisible) in addition to particles that are visible to the unaided eye. For this reason, the microscope confounds the ability of the test method to make the important distinction of visible versus subvisible. This challenge is common to any analytical or inspection method that exceeds human detection capability. Examples include current AVI systems, artificial intelligence applications, and numerous chemical and microbial assays.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Visible vs. Subvisible Particles (可見微粒 vs. 亞可見微粒): 這是注射劑品質管控的核心分類。法規上的「可見」定義非常具體：在控制照明條件下，合格檢查員肉眼在約 10 秒內於黑白背景前能看到的就是「可見」。

USP <790> (美國藥典第 790 章): 專門規範成品注射劑中可見微粒的標準，要求產品「基本上無可見微粒」(essentially free)。

AVI Systems (自動目視檢查系統): Automated Visual Inspection 的縮寫，使用機器視覺來檢測容器中的微粒，其檢測能力通常超越人眼。

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像安檢場景的差異：肉眼安檢就是保安目視檢查你的行李，而用顯微鏡就像過 X 光機 -- X 光機能看到人眼看不到的東西（如隱藏在衣物夾層裡的小物件）。問題是，X 光機「太靈敏了」，連不需要擔心的東西也標記出來。所以我們需要一個規則來判斷：X 光機發現的東西，哪些算「真正的問題」，哪些只是「無關緊要的小東西」。

重點提示 Key Notes

核心挑戰：當分析方法的靈敏度超過人眼時，它會同時偵測到可見和亞可見微粒，無法區分兩者。這不僅是顯微鏡的問題，也是所有高靈敏度方法（AVI、AI 應用等）共有的挑戰。TR85 引入尺寸閾值來解決這個分類困境。

供應商 vs. 藥廠雙重測試：封蓋供應商在出貨時測試，藥廠在進貨時也測試。兩邊必須用一致的方法和標準，否則會出現數據矛盾。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 日常運營中，進料品質管控 (Incoming QC) 經常會遇到供應商數據與自己實驗室數據不一致的情況。如果雙方都採用 TR85 的增強型方法作為共同標準，可以在供應商品質協議 (Quality Agreement) 中明確約定方法細節，大幅減少爭議和退貨。

4.1.1 Size Threshold

原文內容 Original Text

To allow for differences between visible and subvisible particles when using instrumental methods with capability that exceeds the unaided human eye, a size threshold is required to determine a visible particulate. The particulate size which is visible during inspection in filled product containers is the basis for the size thresholds. This approach ensures that particulate classification (i.e., visible and subvisible) in packaging components will more clearly connect to the regulations for visible and subvisible particulates in filled product containers.

Multiple studies (2) have been performed on the detectability of particulate matter of various sizes in the final containers of parenteral products when inspected by qualified inspectors following the compendial methods (6,26,28). These studies have demonstrated that under controlled conditions, the smallest size standard sphere that could be reliably detected (i.e., average PoD >70%) is 150 μm (2). The same studies demonstrated negligible detection of 50 μm particulates and variable detection for 100 μm particulates.

Based on this understanding of detection as a function of particle size in parenterals, the value of 100 μm was selected as the threshold above which particles would be classified as visible when using an analytical method that exceeds the capability of the unaided human eye. The 100 μm threshold was conservatively selected to ensure that most particulate matter, even those with a moderate chance of being seen by the unaided human eye in a filled container, would be classified as "visible" by the analytical method using this threshold value. The 100 μm threshold is considered conservative because it is derived from the best case inspection conditions (i.e., clear solution, clear 10 mL vial, compendial lighting, background, and inspection time) and because it is lower than the 150 μm value established as the lower limit where detection in final containers is considered reliable (i.e., average PoD >70%).

When establishing a threshold defining a particle as visible, the particle geometry must also be considered. The 100 μm threshold was derived from studies of the detectability of standard spheres in liquid filled containers. Therefore, the 100 μm value is most applicable for an equant particulate which has an approximate 1:1:1 aspect ratio (length:width:thickness). This is similar to the ratio of a circle when considering the profile of the particulate in two dimensions. However, fibrous particles can have much higher aspect ratios. Such fiber-like particles need to be much longer in length as compared to an equivalent spherical diameter to be reliably detected in final product containers (1). The lower ability to detect a fiber-like particle versus that of a spherical particle is related to the two-dimensional profile of the two shapes and how this profile presents to the inspector. For example, a single 100 μm sphere has nearly eight times the two-dimensional surface area as a 100 μm fiber-like particle with an aspect ratio of 10:1 (length:width).

Based on the reduced detectability of fiber-like particles, a second threshold size to classify a particulate as visible was established for particulate with fiber-like geometries (i.e., those with a length to width aspect ratios >5). The size threshold to consider a fiber-like particulate visible was derived by calculating the length of a fiber with an equivalent two-dimensional surface area as the 150 μm spheres which were established as the lower limit where detection in final containers is considered reliable. For this calculation, an aspect ratio of 5 was used as a conservative limit such that higher aspect ratios would result in the classification of even longer fiber lengths as subvisible. Based on this approach, a particulate with fiber-like geometry would need to be greater than 300 μm to be considered visible when observed using analytical methods more capable than the unaided human eye. The threshold values shown in Table 4.1.1-1 apply to the testing of packaging components for particulates when an analytical method is used that exceeds the capability of the unaided human eye. These thresholds should not be applied to drug product final-filled container inspection, where the unaided human eye is the standard method used to assess whether visible particle levels meet the "essentially free" requirement and inspection performance may vary from the thresholds established here. Specifically, finished product inspection performance may be affected by the container color and design and the product type (e.g., solution color, opacity, density). These confounding characteristics are removed from the test described herein. Additionally, the size thresholds should not be applied to any particulate that is detected by the unaided human eye during testing of the packaging components, as the act of detecting the particulate would be definitive proof that the particulate is visible. That classification should not be overridden by subsequent analytical characterization.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Size Threshold (尺寸閾值): 當使用超越人眼能力的分析方法時，用來界定「可見」與「亞可見」的數值界線。

比喻說明 Analogy

公式與計算 Formula

纖維閾值的推導邏輯：

150 μm 球體的二維面積 = π × (75)² ≈ 17,671 μm²

纖維（長寬比 5:1）：面積 = 長度 × 寬度 = L × (L/5)

球體面積 = π × (50)² ≈ 7,854 μm²
纖維面積 = 100 × 10 = 1,000 μm²
比值 ≈ 7.85 倍 → 文中說「nearly eight times」

  • 如果肉眼已經看到微粒，就直接歸類為「可見」，不需要再用閾值判斷

4.1.2 Novel Particle Size Measurement Tool

原文內容 Original Text

The assessment of particles in elastomer components traditionally involves rinsing the components, then filtering the rinsate, and finally examining the filter under a microscope. No thresholds to differentiate visible from subvisible, as discussed above, have been established. To consistently apply the principles of the visible particle size thresholds defined in Section 4.1, a novel measurement tool was developed for use in elastomer particle testing involving microscopic analysis of the filtrate.

Figure 4.1.2-1 depicts a novel reticle design that enables an analyst to readily determine whether particles observed microscopically on a filter should be considered visible or subvisible. The reticle has two shapes in the view field: a circle, and a rectangle. The circle is to be sized such that for a given microscope under the intended magnification, the circle is equivalent to a diameter of 100 μm. Similarly, the rectangle is 300 μm × 60 μm to address both size and aspect ratio thresholds for fiber-like particles.

The reticle design shown in Figure 4.1.2-1 allows for rapid and consistent application of the particle size and aspect ratio thresholds established for this test. When an analyst examines the filter through a microscope, they simply compare any particle observed against the rectangle and circle templates in the reticle. Any particle that fits entirely within either of the two shapes is considered subvisible and is not to be counted as visible. Those that extend beyond the boundaries of either template are counted as visible, and depending on the aspect ratio as a particle or fiber.

Reticle (標線片/十字絲): 安裝在顯微鏡目鏡中的光學輔助工具，上面刻有特定形狀和尺寸的參考圖案。分析人員透過目鏡觀察時，可以直接將微粒與標線片上的形狀做比較，不需要額外測量。

TR85 標線片設計的兩個形狀：

  • 圓形 (Circle): 直徑等於 100 μm -- 用於判定等軸微粒

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像手機螢幕上的「護照照片裁切框」-- 你把臉對準框，如果臉完全在框內就合格，如果超出框就不合格。TR85 的標線片用同樣的概念：把微粒「對準」圓形或長方形的模板，看它是否超出邊界。這個設計的巧妙之處在於，分析人員不需要精確測量每個微粒的尺寸，只需做「比大小」的直覺判斷，大幅提高了效率和一致性。

  • 可訓練性：新手只需學會「比大小」的操作，降低了技能門檻

4.2 Summary for Performing the Enhanced Closures Testing Method

原文內容 Original Text

A summary of the Enhanced Closure Testing Method for counting visible particles on elastomeric closures follows. Refer to 8.1 Appendix A for a detailed discussion on the development of this method.

A number of parts (normalized for surface area) are placed in an Erlenmeyer Flask, and 50 ml of surfactant solution is dispensed and added to the flask. The flask is then placed on an orbital shaker to agitate the parts and remove visible particles from the surface. After 20 seconds of shaking the flask is removed from the shaker and the surfactant solution is immediately filtered through a membrane filter (a gray filter was used to enhance contrast for both light and dark colored particles and fibers). The rinsing process is repeated and the filter is allowed to dry. Once the filter is dry, any visible particles present are counted by using a specialized reticle and an optical stereomicroscope.

A thorough discussion of the sample preparation process and the counting methodology used to evaluate within and between operator replicate samples can also be found in 8.2 Appendix B.

方法步驟摘要：

  1. 取樣：按表面積標準化數量的封蓋放入錐形瓶 (Erlenmeyer Flask)

  • 表面積標準化：不同尺寸的封蓋測試數量不同，以確保比較的公平性
  • 灰色膜過濾器：刻意選擇灰色而非白色或黑色，因為灰色背景能同時提升淺色和深色微粒的對比度

  • 重複沖洗：做兩次確保充分回收

4.3 Method Qualification Strategy and Lessons Learned

原文內容 Original Text

Preliminary studies for attribute agreement revealed that rigorous training and easy-to-follow instructions reduces the subjectivity of the analyst's counting technique. Detailed instructions and the use of the unique counting reticle described in Section 4.1 are key to this training. Repeatability of the particle and fiber count data was proven when multiple analysts inspected each other's filter samples. The material found to be best suited for training analysts and qualification of the method was to select stopper populations prior to their pharmaceutical washing step. These stoppers provided particle counts that were sufficiently high to yield meaningful statistical evaluation.

In attempting to measure repeatability when performing multiple sample extractions from a single stopper bag or across multiple stopper bags, significant variability was observed. Specific factors contributing to this variability in the particle counts were identified through the multiple design of experiments (DOE) based investigations and subsequent review of the findings with the operators involved.

The inherent lack of homogeneity of particle and fiber deposition on the surface of elastomeric components that are packaged within a bag or collectively transported in multiple cartons from the same batch was one of the underlying root causes for the inconsistent initial results observed early in the development of this test method. Repetitive handling, sampling, and shipping greatly increases variability and localization of particles.

Another challenge identified during development and qualification of this method was to determine the number of stoppers needed for training and qualification. Because it is common for commercial stopper batches to have particle counts near zero, these batches are not ideal to train analysts or qualify the method. There were several attempts made to purposely seed particles into stopper batches, but these were not successful. Generating appropriate particulate loading through various attempts at artificially seeding with shredded fragments of rubber and aliquots of a cellulose emulsion proved to be ineffective due to inconsistent distribution within the bag, which in turn resulted in highly variable recovery.

Additional detail on the development of this method and the evaluation of contributions to method variability can be found in 8.1 Appendix A.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Attribute Agreement (屬性一致性): 一種統計方法，用於評估不同分析人員對同一樣本的判定結果是否一致。在微粒計數中，就是確認不同人用同樣的方法數出來的結果是否可比。

Repeatability (重複性): 同一分析人員在相同條件下重複測試同一樣本，結果的一致程度。

Homogeneity (均勻性): 微粒在封蓋表面分佈的均勻程度。這是本方法面臨的最大挑戰之一 -- 微粒在包裝袋內的分佈天生就不均勻。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你要訓練一組品酒師判斷紅酒的品質。理想情況下，你需要各種品質等級的酒讓他們練習。但如果你手上的酒幾乎都是頂級（微粒計數接近零），品酒師就沒辦法學會辨別差異。你嘗試自己「製造」劣質酒（人工接種微粒），但每瓶的品質都不一致。最後你發現：最好的訓練材料是「還沒經過最後精煉步驟」的酒（洗滌前的膠塞） -- 因為它們天然含有足夠的雜質讓品酒師練習辨別。

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Shape Factor, SF（形狀因子）：用來將不同形狀粒子的「關鍵尺寸」轉換為「視覺等效面積」的係數。圓形粒子的 SF 接近 1，而細長纖維的 SF 遠小於 1（因為寬度遠小於長度）。

Normalized Particle Size（歸一化粒子尺寸）：= Key Dimension x Shape Factor。這個轉換使得粒子和纖維能在同一基準線上比較偵測率，消除了形狀差異的影響。

Apparent Area（視覺表觀面積）：檢查員肉眼看到的粒子「有效面積」。這才是決定能否被看到的真正因素，而非單一維度的長度或直徑。

公式與計算 Formula

歸一化粒子尺寸公式：

Normalized Particle Size = Key Dimension x Shape Factor (SF)

舉例：
- 圓形粒子（SF ≈ 1.0）：300 μm x 1.0 = 300 μm（歸一化後不變）
- 細長纖維（SF ≈ 0.1）：300 μm x 0.1 = 30 μm（歸一化後大幅縮小）

歸一化後的 PoD 基準：
- 100 μm（歸一化）→ 80% PoD
- 200 μm（歸一化）→ 95% PoD

對比未歸一化的數據：
- 粒子：125 μm → 70% PoD；300 μm → 95% PoD
- 纖維：300 μm → 70% PoD；800-1,000 μm → 90% PoD

比喻說明 Analogy

歸一化就像貨幣匯率換算 -- 一根 300 μm 的纖維和一顆 300 μm 的粒子雖然「標價」相同，但在人眼中的「購買力」（可見度）完全不同。通過形狀因子的換算，我們把它們都換成同一種「貨幣」（視覺表觀面積），這樣才能公平比較。換算後發現，纖維和粒子在相同的「視覺面積」下，偵測率其實差不多 -- 問題只是出在纖維的「匯率」低（面積小）。

6.0 Conclusion 結論

原文內容 Original Text

Visual inspection continues to be an important step in the manufacture and delivery of safe and effective injectable medicines. Current inspection methods, manual and machine assisted, can provide reliable monitoring and control of the visual characteristics of injectable products, including visible particles. The work described in this technical report is intended to further the development of methods to increase reliable supply and reduced rejection of injectable drug products. Reliable detection and control of visible particles in and on the components used to contain these products is essential to meeting this goal.

This technical report provides an improved method to assess visible particle burden on elastomeric closures. This method uses much that is known and available but provides standardization of key parameters including extraction, agitation, and size thresholds (100 μm for particles and 300 μm for fibers) to aid in consistent enumeration. This is expected to assist both the manufacturers of such closures and the pharmaceutical companies who use them in packaging their drug products. A standard method to assess visible particles will hopefully lead to a practical working standard which incorporates risk-based limits suitable for the intended application.

This report also describes a study to better assess the ability to inspect empty glass containers. While several studies have looked at PoD in finished, filled containers, little has been done with empty containers to-date. This study shows similar inspection performance between empty and filled containers and supports the use of standard compendial visual inspection conditions to perform inspection of empty glass containers. An average PoD of 70% was achieved for 125 μm particles and 300 μm fibers. These agree with and support the thresholds chosen for the Enhanced Closure Testing Method. Further analysis of the study data shows that PoD of particles and fibers can be related to each other through assessment of the apparent visible area.

Continued advancement in visual inspection and particle control will require broad industry support. Ongoing engagement with additional companies and regulatory organizations would further the work that was described herein and lead to acceptance of common methods and acceptance limits for visible particles on or in primary packaging components.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Enhanced Closure Testing Method (增強膠塞測試方法): TR85 的核心貢獻。這不是全新的發明，而是將既有的萃取、攪拌、計數技術加以「標準化」，讓不同實驗室、不同公司能用同一把尺來衡量膠塞上的可見微粒負擔。

Size Thresholds (粒徑閾值): 微粒 ≥ 100 μm、纖維 ≥ 300 μm。這些數字不是隨意設定，而是與空瓶檢測研究中的 PoD (Probability of Detection, 檢測機率) 數據相互驗證得出的。

Probability of Detection, PoD (檢測機率): 在特定檢測條件下成功發現某尺寸微粒的機率。本報告中空瓶研究達到 125 μm 微粒和 300 μm 纖維平均 70% PoD，這為閾值設定提供了科學依據。

Apparent Visible Area (表觀可見面積): 將微粒和纖維的 PoD 統一到同一維度的關鍵概念。不同形狀的污染物（顆粒 vs 纖維）可以透過投影面積來比較檢測難易度。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你經營一家連鎖餐廳，每家分店都需要檢查餐具是否乾淨。過去每家店用自己的標準 -- 有的用目視、有的用放大鏡、搖晃水杯的力道也不同。TR85 的做法就像制定一份統一的「餐具清潔度檢測 SOP」：規定用多少水、搖多久、多大的殘留物算不合格。這樣總部才能真正比較各分店的清潔水準，也才能跟餐具供應商談出有意義的品質規格。

重點提示 Key Notes

標準化的價值不僅在技術，更在商業： 當膠塞製造商與藥廠使用相同的測試方法和閾值時，雙方能基於共同語言來制定規格、評估偏差、比較供應商。這直接降低了供應鏈中的溝通成本和爭議風險。

風險為本 (Risk-based) 的限度設定： 報告明確指出理想目標是「practical working standard which incorporates risk-based limits」。這與 ICH Q9 品質風險管理精神一致 -- 不是一刀切的零容忍，而是依產品特性和臨床風險設定合理的可見微粒允收限度。

Method Development

Test methods that yield variable data with great certainty, such as dimensional measurements or potency, can be simply qualified or validated. However, human performance, such as visual inspection, is not directly measurable or validated. As such, the strategy for qualifying a methodology based on attribute data required an initial verification of the sample preparation process and the counting method that would ultimately be used to evaluate the comparability of within operator replicates or between operators and cohorts of samples. To accomplish this, the evaluation and testing strategy was separated into two phases, pilot (method development) and qualification. These are outlined in Figure A1.

[Figure A1: Process Map of the Pilot (method development) and Qualification Strategy]

The process map shows the following flow:

  • Attribute Agreement Analysis initiated → Execute Dry Run → Sufficient comparability between SME's? (If No: Provide Clarification) → Method Qualification (Pilot)
  • Qualification: Sample sets shipped to each Supplier → Prepare seeded samples → Sufficient Comparability? (If No: Determine cause for lack of comparability, Define actions required to correct/address identified root cause(s)) → Test method released for publication

The results from the pilot phase highlighted the necessity of seeding samples prior to distribution and evaluation by the three participating suppliers. Therefore, the formal qualification study was stratified as outlined in Figure A2. Three seeding groups were created by adding particles of elastomeric material and dissolved fiber. Although the fibers were added using a defined volume of a prepared aqueous mixture, the particle generation process employed a novel technique of creating fragments of elastomeric material and manual deposition onto the surface of each stopper sample contained in the sample bags. The resulting groups of sample bags were then provided to all three suppliers with the goal of minimizing the impact of variability of the three seeding groups.

[Figure A2: Method Qualification Sample Generation Stratification]

Stopper Samples split into 3 Seeding Groups → each group: Particles generated and fiber aliquot prepared → Seeding Group-1/2/3 distributed to Supplier-1/2/3 Sample Bags.

Statistical analysis of the resulting particulate count data revealed that the use of a nonstandard sample extraction and transfer process (due to the inherent nature of the elastic particulate matter being extracted) of the seeded particles and fibers may have contributed to unintended consequences for the counting process. Inconsistencies in the seeding process and the possible impact of forces exerted upon the sample bags during transit seemed to be contributing factors that ultimately yielded the failing results observed. Significantly, counting results from all operators were repeatable when cross comparisons were performed between operators.

An extensive investigation was performed to quantitatively evaluate the impact of the multiple sources of variability that were contributing to the initial results and define viable means of mitigation. Based on learnings from the previous studies, the team chose to discontinue the use of artificially seeded particles and opted, instead, to use stoppers that were not processed in a pharmaceutical washer in which a higher native particle burden was exhibited. 核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Variable Data (變量數據) vs. Attribute Data (屬性數據):

  • Variable Data：可以在連續量尺上測量的數據，如尺寸（mm）、效價（potency）等。這類數據的驗證相對直接，因為可以用精確的數字來衡量。
  • Attribute Data：基於分類判定的數據，如「合格/不合格」、「有微粒/無微粒」。目視檢查（visual inspection）產生的就是屬性數據——依賴人的主觀判斷。

Pilot Phase (先導階段) vs. Qualification Phase (資格確認階段): 方法開發分為兩個階段。先導階段用於驗證樣品準備流程和計數方法是否可行；資格確認階段則正式評估方法在多個供應商之間的可比較性。

Seeding (植入/加標): 在樣品中人為添加已知數量的微粒和纖維，作為挑戰測試，以評估檢測方法的靈敏度和一致性。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你在訓練三家不同餐廳的廚師品嚐同一道湯的鹹度。

  • Variable data 就像用鹽度計測量——精確到小數點，誰來測結果都一樣。
  • Attribute data 就像用嘴巴品嚐後說「偏鹹」或「適中」——不同廚師可能給出不同判斷。

所以，要讓三家餐廳的品嚐結果一致，你需要：(1) 先統一標準——什麼叫「偏鹹」（先導階段），(2) 然後正式測試所有人的判斷是否一致（資格確認階段）。

Repeatability (重複性) vs. Reproducibility (再現性):

  • Repeatability：同一操作員在相同條件下重複測試同一樣品，結果是否一致。本報告中用 paired t-test 來評估——不同操作員檢查同一張濾膜的計數結果是否一致。
  • Reproducibility：不同操作員或不同實驗室對不同萃取樣品的測試結果是否一致。本報告中用 ANOVA 來評估——不同操作員各自萃取後的微粒計數是否可比較。

ANOVA (變異數分析): 比較三組或以上數據平均值是否有統計顯著差異。p-value > 0.05 表示各組之間無顯著差異（即方法結果具有可比較性）。

Paired t-test (配對t檢定): 比較成對觀測值的差異是否顯著。適用於「同一張濾膜由兩個不同操作員計數」的情境。

比喻說明 Analogy

把「萃取+計數」想像成「煮咖啡+品嚐」：

  • Repeatability（重複性）：三個品茶師品嚐同一杯咖啡，給出的評分是否一致？→ 用 paired t-test 檢驗。
  • Reproducibility（再現性）：三個咖啡師各自沖泡，然後各自品嚐自己那杯，結果是否一致？→ 用 ANOVA 檢驗。

Supplier 1 的結果顯示：品嚐能力（計數重複性）很好（p > 0.05），但沖泡過程（萃取再現性）在纖維方面存在差異（p = 0.022 < 0.05）。這表明問題出在「萃取」而非「計數」。

重點提示 Key Notes

  • 微粒 >100 μm：萃取再現性通過（ANOVA p = 0.590），計數重複性通過（paired t-test p = 0.078-0.761）。
  • 纖維 >300 μm：萃取再現性未通過（ANOVA p = 0.022），但計數重複性仍然通過（paired t-test p = 0.391-1.000）。
  • 信賴區間延伸到負值 → 表示數據量不足或變異太大，計數本身不可能為負數，這是統計模型的局限性表現。

關鍵訊息：計數方法本身是可靠的，變異主要來自萃取過程中微粒從膠塞表面釋放的不均勻性。

原文內容 Original Text

Conclusion

The original strategy for confirming the robustness of the test method for extracting and counting the particulate present on the resulting membrane filter was intended to demonstrate quantitatively the repeatability and reproducibility of both components of the test method. Preliminary studies for attribute agreement revealed that rigorous training and easy to follow instructions are key to reducing the subjectivity of the analyst's counting techniques. Once this foundation of objectivity was formed for operators, the validity of their results was not in question. Repeatability of the particle and fiber count data was confirmed not only when extracted samples were independently reinspected, but also when all operators inspected each other's filter samples. This is evident in the hypothesis test results (well above a p-value of 0.05) that were obtained throughout the studies and the multiple qualification cohorts. On the other hand, reproducibility of output data with replicate extractions or between each operator's sample sets was not easily demonstrated.

Through multiple investigatory design-of-experiments and review of the findings with the operators involved, specific factors that contributed to providing what was initially thought to be inexplicable variability in the particle counts were identified. The inherent lack of homogeneity of particle and fiber deposition on the surface of elastomeric components that are packaged within a bag or collectively transported in multiple cartons from the same batch was one of the underlying root causes for the inconsistencies of the initial data gathered. As a result, managing sample bag to sample bag variability proved to be one of the largest factors in obtaining sufficiently low CV values. Repetitive handling, sampling, and shipping greatly increases variability and localization of particles within the bags.

Results from the final transportation and qualification study demonstrated that seeding particles was not necessary to generate counts that are sufficiently high to yield meaningful statistical evaluation if unwashed samples are used instead of RTS product.

Although several studies revealed relatively good comparability within replicate extractions for individual operators within a supplier's testing team, consistent agreement within the range of values for particles or fibers was not demonstrated across all organizations. Counting technique for particles and fibers applied to sets of replicate extractions yielded good comparability between local operators for all three suppliers. This comparability between local operators for all three suppliers demonstrates that the newly developed method is a robust and repeatable procedure.

8.2 Appendix B. Method: Determination of Visible Particles and Fibers on Elastomeric Components by Membrane Filtration and Microscopic Examination

原文內容 Original Text

Introduction

The purpose of this method is to provide a procedure to quantify the number of visible particles present on elastomeric closures used for injectable pharmaceutical products. The procedure describes how the elastomeric closures are to be sampled from the fully assembled package, then rinsed to release any particles present, and subsequently sized and counted using a stereomicroscope.

Scope

This method is applicable to all RTU and RTS elastomeric closures but only applies to visible particles. The method requires that such closures be tested from fully assembled final packaging to reflect how they are received and used by the pharmaceutical manufacturer.

Fiber-like particles (i.e., length to width aspect ratio of ≥5) have significantly less surface area than a sphere of equivalent diameter to the fiber length. This results in significantly less detection capability for a 100 μm long fiber as compared to 100 μm diameter sphere. To ensure this analytical method is detecting visible particles only, fiber-like particles have been given a larger visible size threshold of 300 μm.

Grey Membrane Filter (灰色膜過濾器): 特別選用灰色背景的格線膜，是因為大多數微粒（透明、白色或深色）在灰色背景上都能形成良好的對比，便於目視計數。格線則幫助操作員系統性地掃描整張濾膜，避免重複計數或遺漏。

Preparation for the test

Ensure that the environment in which the test is to be carried out is free from extraneous particles which could cause interference. 比喻說明 Analogy

The calibration of the stereomicroscope should be verified daily prior to use using a micrometer that is calibrated at the operational magnification. Use the following equation to calculate the number of visible particles per 10 cm² for both particles and fibers.

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Reticle (目鏡測微尺): 安裝在顯微鏡目鏡中的標定模板，包含圓形和矩形範本。操作員將觀察到的微粒與範本比較：

結果報告公式解析：

可見微粒/10cm² = (濾膜上計數的可見微粒數 × 10) ÷ (使用的零件數 × 每個零件的表面積)

  假設：使用 20mm 膠塞，每個表面積約 5 cm²
  取樣：100 cm² ÷ 5 cm²/個 = 20 個膠塞
  濾膜上計數：15 個可見微粒

  可見微粒/10cm² = (15 × 10) ÷ (20 × 5)
                  = 150 ÷ 100
                  = 1.5 個/10cm²

比喻說明 Analogy

Reticle 的功能就像海關人員手上的「尺寸對照卡」——行李（微粒）必須大於卡上的框框才會被「攔下」（計數）。比框框小的就放行（不計入可見微粒）。圓形框對應一般微粒，矩形框對應纖維，各有不同的「攔截門檻」。

重點提示 Key Notes

結果報告的標準化意義：

  • 以「每 10 cm²」為報告單位，使不同尺寸的膠塞（13mm、20mm、28mm 等）的結果可以直接比較。

The EP (26), USP (6), and JP (28) each specify a reference time of inspection per container of ten 10 sec; five sec each against black and white backgrounds. Before executing the study, an estimate was made for inspection of the complete test of 60 containers using the minimum inspection time defined by the compendia. The lower limit was set at 9 min while the upper limit was calculated as the median plus 1.66 standard deviations, resulting in an upper limit of 31 min. Results obtained outside of this range were not considered valid and were not used in the analysis.

Probability of Detection, PoD (檢測機率): 在特定條件下，檢查員正確識別並報告某個已知粒子的機率。PoD = 1.0 表示 100% 檢出率，PoD = 0 表示完全未檢出。PoD 受粒子大小、材質對比度、容器類型、檢查員經驗等多重因素影響。

比喻說明 Analogy

Main Effects — Statistical Analysis

原文內容 Original Text

Main Effects. Using the Boruta method (33) the data were analyzed for the significance that each variable has on the resulting PoD. This method is based on a machine learning technique called Random Forest. Random Forest computes the importance of predictor variables vs a response variable. It repeats this process and generates a distribution of importance for each predictor variable. It then sorts the predictor variables based on their importance and statistically compares them to random (shadow) variables to categorize them as:

  • Rejected: variable has significantly less predictive importance than random variables.
  • Confirmed: variable has significantly more predictive importance.
  • Tentative: variable cannot be categorized as either rejected or confirmed when computation stops.

Figure C10 Boruta Plot of inspection study results demonstrating factors significant to reliable inspection performance.

As a result, important variables are those that can play a significant role in predicting the response variable. This is similar to significant predictors in Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) but fails to satisfy usual assumptions of parametric models.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Boruta Method (Boruta 方法): 一種基於隨機森林（Random Forest）的特徵選擇演算法。它的獨特之處在於引入「影子變數」（shadow variables）作為基準 -- 將每個真實變數打亂順序創造隨機版本，然後比較真實變數與隨機變數的預測重要性。如果某個變數的重要性持續高於隨機變數，就被確認（Confirmed）為有意義的預測因子。

Random Forest (隨機森林): 一種機器學習方法，透過建立大量決策樹並取其平均來進行預測。它不需要資料符合常態分佈等假設（非參數方法），因此比傳統 ANOVA 更適合處理複雜的多因子交互作用。

比喻說明 Analogy

Boruta 方法就像選秀節目中的盲選機制。每位參賽者（變數）不僅與其他參賽者比較，還要與一群「隨機表演者」（影子變數）比較。只有表現持續優於隨機表演者的參賽者才能晉級（Confirmed）。表現不如隨機者的被淘汰（Rejected），而介於兩者之間的則是「待定」（Tentative）。這確保了最終選出的因子確實具有預測能力，而不是統計上的假象。

重點提示 Key Notes

為什麼不用傳統 ANOVA？ANOVA 假設數據符合常態分佈、變異數齊性等條件。但目視檢查數據通常是二元的（檢出/未檢出），且存在複雜的交互作用。Boruta/Random Forest 作為非參數方法，不受這些假設限制，能更準確地識別真正有影響力的因子。

這也是 PDA 技術報告中較為先進的統計應用，顯示業界正在將機器學習工具引入傳統的品質分析領域。

Conclusion

Based on statistical analysis of the valid data from the empty container inspection study, the main variables influencing the PoD were determined to be particle size, inspector, container, and particulate type. Further, particle size is a significant factor as well, with lower PoD observed for smaller particles.

Material providing poorer contrast against the backgrounds produced lower PoD values. 8.4 Appendix D. Method: Visual Inspection of Empty Glass Containers

原文內容 Original Text

Introduction. The purpose of this method is to provide a procedure to identify and quantify the number of visible particles present on empty glass containers used for injectable pharmaceutical products. The procedure describes the inspection conditions and how the containers should be presented to the inspector.

Scope. This method is applicable to all empty glass containers. Inspection times may need to be increased for larger (>100 mL) containers.

Equipment and materials.

  1. Inspection cabinet with matte black and white backgrounds with diffuse lighting of 2,000-3,750 lux at the point of inspection.

Glass container inspection procedure.

  1. Remove a container from the uninspected tray.
  2. Hold the sample approximately 10 cm directly below the lamp at a distance approximately 30 cm from the inspector's eyes.
  3. Inspect the sample for 5-10 sec against the black background. Rotate and invert the container to ensure all areas are examined adjusting your grip on the container as needed.
  4. Inspect the sample for 5-10 sec against the white background. Rotate and invert the container to ensure all areas are examined adjusting your grip on the container as needed.
  5. Place each piece examined into the either the reject tray or passing tray as required.
  6. Complete any necessary records documenting the inspection of each piece before moving to the next inspection.

The time spent per container may be controlled using a pacing device. Recording the time spent inspecting each batch and then calculating the nominal inspection rate is a reliable method to confirm that the rate of inspection was maintained within the defined limits.

Information recorded during or after inspection may include details such as the type and location of any particles observed, and any other defects observed. On completion of the inspection, the data should be evaluated against the established acceptance criteria to determine the disposition of the batch. During inspection, the operator should avoid direct viewing of the light source and take regular eye breaks to avoid eye strain which may adversely influence the results of the inspection.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Diffuse Lighting (漫射照明): 光線均勻散佈、不產生刺眼反射或陰影的照明方式。2,000-3,750 lux 的範圍是經過驗證的最佳檢查照度 -- 太暗看不清粒子，太亮則會造成眩光和眼睛疲勞。

Matte Black and White Backgrounds (霧面黑白背景): 黑色背景用於觀察淺色或透明粒子（如玻璃碎片、白色纖維），白色背景用於觀察深色粒子（如黑色橡膠、金屬碎片）。霧面（matte）表面避免反光干擾。

Pacing Device (節拍裝置): 控制檢查速率的工具，確保每個容器獲得足夠的檢查時間。這可以是簡單的計時器、節拍器、或整合在檢查台上的指示燈。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你是珠寶鑑定師：你有一個專用的鑑定台（檢查箱），上面有穩定的燈光（漫射照明），你會把寶石放在黑色絨布和白色紙上各看一次（黑白背景），保持寶石在特定距離和角度（10 cm 燈下、30 cm 眼距），還要不斷旋轉翻轉它（rotate and invert）。每顆寶石至少看 10-20 秒，並且記錄所有發現。目視檢查的操作本質上就是這樣的精密觀察工作。

重點提示 Key Notes

關鍵操作細節：

  • 距離標準化：10 cm 燈距 + 30 cm 眼距，這確保了一致的照度和視角
  • 雙背景檢查：每個容器必須在黑色和白色背景各檢查一次，因為不同顏色的粒子在不同背景下的可見度差異極大
  • 旋轉與翻轉：容器的每個表面都必須被檢查到，包括底部和口部
  • 眼睛休息：避免直視光源，定期休息以防眼睛疲勞影響判斷力。這是人因工程（Human Factors）的重要考量

大容器注意事項：對於 >100 mL 的容器，需要延長檢查時間。這與 Appendix C 的研究發現完全吻合 -- 更大的表面積需要更多時間。

實務應用 Practical Application

建立 SOP 的實務建議：

  • 照度驗證：定期（至少每月一次）用照度計驗證檢查點的照度在 2,000-3,750 lux 範圍內
  • 操作員資格：所有檢查員需要通過視力測試（如 Jaeger 或 Snellen 測試），並定期複檢
  • 節拍控制：使用計時器或輸送帶速度控制每個容器的檢查時間，防止因趕進度而降低品質
  • 環境控制：檢查區域應避免外部光源干擾、減少噪音分心
  • 輪換制度：建議每 30-60 分鐘輪換一次，或至少進行眼睛休息
  • 數據追蹤：記錄每批次的檢查時間、不合格率、粒子類型分佈，作為趨勢分析的基礎

想像你在量身高：如果一個人站在門框下完全不碰到門框頂部（contained inside），就代表他比門框矮（不計數）。但如果頭碰到或超過門框（touching the border），就代表他達到或超過該高度（需要計數）。Reticle 的紅色圓圈和方框就是這個「門框」，用來篩選哪些粒子大到需要被記錄。

流程圖中「Fiber-Like」的判斷是第一步關鍵決策。纖維和顆粒使用不同形狀的測量工具（方框 vs 圓圈），因為它們的幾何形態完全不同。錯誤分類會導致使用錯誤的測量標準，直接影響計數結果。

  • 測試套件設計：使用已知大小、類型的粒子固定於容器中，製作三套等效套件以消除偏差。異常值在使用前即被移除。
  • 研究規模：11 家公司、55 位檢查員、59,400 個數據點（其中 49,360 有效），為業界最大規模的空容器目視檢查能力研究之一。
  • 四大影響因子：粒子大小 > 檢查員差異 > 容器類型/大小 > 微粒材質類型，均經 Boruta/Random Forest 統計驗證。
  • 光學對比度是關鍵：透明玻璃碎片在玻璃容器中的 PoD 最低，白色纖維素纖維也明顯難以偵測。高對比度材質（黑色/橘色橡膠）相對容易被發現。
  • 容器大小效應：30 mL 瓶子的表面積約為 1 mL 注射器的三倍，導致單位面積檢查時間減少，PoD 下降。大容器需要延長檢查時間。
