Regulatory Issues | 法規議題

Key guidance documents and regulatory expectations for biotechnology cleaning validation

12.0 Regulatory Issues

Most regulatory documents dealing with cleaning validation do not make any explicit comments about biotechnology manufacturing or about how cleaning validation might be different for biotechnology as compared to other pharmaceutical manufacturing. The general principles laid out in regulatory documents, i.e., limits should be "practical, achievable, and verifiable," apply equally to biotechnology manufacturing and small molecule pharmaceutical manufacturing.

Below are specific regulatory comments relevant to biotechnology cleaning validation:

1. WHO Working Document QS/03.055/Rev.1

Includes a statement about the use of ELISA as an analytical technique for biopharmaceuticals. However, that statement is not of much help, since most biopharmaceuticals degrade in the cleaning process.

2. U.S. FDA Q&A on CGMP (updated 2005)

Provides a rationale for allowing the use of TOC for cleaning validation purposes. While it does not specifically mention use for biotechnology, the biotechnology industry is among the biggest users of TOC for cleaning validation.

3. U.S. FDA Biotechnology Inspection Guide

"Validation of the cleaning procedures for the processing of equipment, including columns, should be carried out. This is especially critical for a multi-product facility. The manufacturer should have determined the degree of effectiveness of the cleaning procedure for each BDP [Biotech-derived product] or intermediate used in that particular piece of equipment."
"Validation data should verify that the cleaning process will reduce the specific residues to an acceptable level. However, it may not be possible to remove absolutely every trace of material, even with a reasonable number of cleaning cycles. The permissible residue level, generally expressed in parts per million (ppm), should be justified by the manufacturer. Cleaning should remove endotoxins, bacteria, toxic elements, and contaminating proteins, while not adversely affecting the performance of the column."

Other than the explicit comment about including residues of bacteria and endotoxin, there is little that is specific to biotechnology manufacturing beyond these statements.

4. U.S. FDA Guidance for Lyophilization of Parenterals

"One could conclude that if contamination is found on a chamber surface after lyophilization, then dosage units in the chamber could also be contaminated. It is a good practice as part of the validation of cleaning of the lyophilization chamber to sample the surfaces both before and after cleaning."

This means that contamination of shelves from external sources (such as hydraulic fluid) found after lyophilization is likely present in vials. However, this is primarily a maintenance issue rather than a cleaning validation issue. Note that this statement carries an implicit assumption that cleaning validation is performed for vial lyophilization.

5. Health Canada Cleaning Validation Guidelines

"Relevant process equipment cleaning validation methods are required for biological drugs because of their inherent characteristics (proteins are sticky by nature), parenteral product purity requirements, the complexity of equipment and the broad spectrum of materials which need to be cleaned."

Health Canada also addresses bracketing (grouping) for biotechnology manufacturing:

"For biological drugs, including vaccines, bracketing may be considered acceptable for similar products and/or equipment provided appropriate justification, based on sound, scientific rationale is given. Some examples are cleaning of fermentors of the same design but with different vessel capacity used for the same type of recombinant proteins expressed in the same rodent cell line and cultivated in closely related growth media; a multi-antigen vaccine used to represent the individual antigen or other combinations of them when validating the same or similar equipment that is used at stages of formulation (adsorption) and/or holding. Validation of cleaning of fermentors should be done upon individual pathogen basis."

6. ICH Q7 — GMP Guide for Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients

Section 18.0: "Specific guidance for APIs manufactured by cell culture/fermentation." Key statements include:

On cell culture equipment:

"Cell culture equipment should be cleaned and sterilized after use. As appropriate, fermentation equipment should be cleaned, sanitized, or sterilized."
"Shared (multi-product) equipment may warrant additional testing after cleaning between product campaigns, as appropriate, to minimize the risk of cross-contamination."

On harvesting, isolation and purification:

"All equipment should be properly cleaned and, as appropriate, sanitized after use. Multiple successive batching without cleaning can be used if intermediate or API quality is not compromised."

On viral removal/inactivation steps:

"The same equipment is not normally used for different purification steps. However, if the same equipment is to be used, the equipment should be appropriately cleaned and sanitized before reuse. Appropriate precautions should be taken to prevent potential virus carryover (e.g., through equipment or environment) from previous steps."

7. ICH Q5A — Viral Safety Evaluation of Biotechnology Products

"Assurance should be provided that any virus potentially retained by the production system would be adequately destroyed or removed prior to reuse of the system. For example, such evidence may be provided by demonstrating that the cleaning and regeneration procedures do inactivate or remove virus."

While these are specific comments from guidance documents, it should be recognized that regulatory inspectors may (and should) have additional expectations for cleaning validation in biotechnology based on current industry practices and on their past experience with similar companies.

中文解說 | Commentary

核心原則：法規共同要求

無論生技或小分子藥品，法規的底線都相同：清潔限度必須是「實際可達、可驗證」的。這來自 FDA 1993 年清潔驗證指南，是整個行業的基石。

生技特殊性在於：蛋白質殘留難以單用 ELISA 偵測（因清潔過程中蛋白會降解），TOC 成為業界主流替代方法。

重點提示：各法規文件摘要

  • WHO QS/03.055：提及 ELISA 但實用性有限（蛋白清潔後降解）
  • FDA CGMP Q&A (2005)：支持 TOC 用於清潔驗證
  • FDA 生技查廠指南：要求色層管柱清潔驗證；明確要求去除內毒素、細菌
  • FDA 凍乾指南：建議清潔前後都要表面取樣
  • Health Canada：允許括弧法但需充分科學依據
  • ICH Q7：共用設備需額外測試；批次間可連續使用但品質不得受損
  • ICH Q5A：病毒清除/去活化需確認清潔程序有效

比喻說明：為何蛋白質「難清」

蛋白質就像生雞蛋白：正常時是透明黏稠，加熱或接觸強酸鹼後會變性凝固，黏附在表面非常難去除。生技產品的清潔挑戰遠大於小分子藥品，這正是 Health Canada 特別強調「蛋白質本質上有黏性」的原因。

括弧法（Bracketing）在生技的應用

Health Canada 允許對相似設備/產品採用括弧法，例如：

  • 相同設計但不同容量的發酵槽（同細胞株、同培養基）
  • 多抗原疫苗代表個別抗原

但前提：每種病原體的發酵槽清潔驗證必須分別進行，不能一括了之。

ICH Q7 對共用設備的要求

ICH Q7 明確說明共用（多產品）設備在產品切換後需進行額外測試。這是針對交叉污染風險的重要要求，在實際執行時需在驗證方案中預先說明測試範圍與頻率。

病毒清除 vs 清潔驗證

ICH Q5A 的要求超出一般清潔驗證範疇——不只是去除化學殘留，而是要確保病毒被滅活或去除。這對色層純化設備（如 Protein A 管柱）尤其重要，因為管柱可能多批次重複使用。

查廠現實面

文件最後一句話是關鍵：法規查廠員的期望往往超過白紙黑字的法規要求，他們會依據當前業界最佳實踐和過往查廠經驗進行評估。這意味著企業應主動追蹤業界動態，而非只符合最低法規標準。

Appendix – Carryover Calculations | 附錄：殘留攜帶計算

Three worked examples for MAC, surface area limits, and analytical limits

15.0 Appendix – Carryover Calculations

Important Note on Sampling Recovery

In the calculation examples that follow, the recovery based on sampling (percent recovery) is not included. For companies that utilize the sampling recovery to "correct" the limit, that factor should be included in their calculations.

計算框架說明

殘留攜帶計算的邏輯流程

所有三個計算範例都遵循相同的四步驟邏輯：

  1. Step 1：計算下一個產品中的允許濃度限度（μg/mL 或 μg/g）
  2. Step 2：計算最大允許攜帶量 MAC（μg），= 限度 × 最小批量
  3. Step 3：計算每單位表面積限度（μg/cm²），= MAC ÷ 共用表面積
  4. Step 4：計算棉棒取樣限度（μg/棉棒）和分析液濃度（μg/mL 或 ppm）

注意：若公司採用取樣回收率校正，應將回收因子納入計算。

Example 1: Dose-Based Limit for Formulation/Fill Manufacturing

This example is based on the dose of the active for formulation/fill manufacturing. It is based on a 1/1000 minimum therapeutic daily dose of the cleaned active.

Variables

VariableDefinition
MinTDMinimum Therapeutic Daily Dose of the active of cleaned product
MBSMinimum Batch Size of next drug product made in the same equipment
MaxDDMaximum Daily Dosage of next drug product made in the same equipment
SFSafety Factor

Given Values

  • MinTD = 25 mg (= 25,000 µg)
  • MBS = 1,000 L (Minimum Batch Size of drug product B)
  • MaxDD = 10 mL (Maximum Daily Dosage units of drug product B)
  • SF = 1,000
  • Shared surface area = 120,000 cm²
  • Area swabbed = 100 cm²
  • Solvent volume for swab desorption = 20 mL

Step 1: Limit in Next Product

Limit in next product = MinTD / (SF × MaxDD)
= 25,000 µg / (1,000 × 10 mL)
= 2.5 µg/mL

Since 2.5 µg/mL is more stringent than the 10 ppm criterion (approx. 10 µg/mL), 2.5 µg/mL is used.

Step 2: Maximum Allowable Carryover (MAC)

MAC = Limit in next product × MBS
= 2.5 µg/mL × 1,000,000 mL
= 2,500,000 µg

Step 3: Limit per Surface Area

Limit per surface area = MAC / Shared surface area
= 2,500,000 µg / 120,000 cm²
= 20.8 µg/cm²

Step 4a: Limit per Swab

Limit per swab = Limit per area × Area swabbed
= 20.8 µg/cm² × 100 cm²
= 2,080 µg

Step 4b: Limit in Desorbed Swab Sample

Limit in desorbed swab sample = Limit per swab / Desorption volume
= 2,080 µg / 20 mL
= 104 µg/mL (104 ppm)

If the active were a protein containing 50% carbon, the TOC limit (net of blank) would be 52 ppm TOC.

範例一：逐步解說（成品製造）

範例一情境

產品 A 清潔後，設備要用來製造產品 B（注射液劑型）。問：設備表面的產品 A 殘留量最多允許多少？

計算基準：千分之一劑量標準（1/1000 Dose Criterion）

Step 1 解說：下一產品中的濃度限度

邏輯：若患者每天最多服用 10 mL 的產品 B，而允許的最大污染量是產品 A 最低治療劑量（MinTD）的千分之一（SF=1000），則：

限度 = 25,000 µg ÷ (1,000 × 10 mL) = 2.5 µg/mL

比較：10 ppm 標準 ≈ 10 µg/mL，而 2.5 µg/mL 更嚴格，故以 2.5 µg/mL 為準。

原則：劑量基準和 10 ppm 基準兩者取較嚴格者。

Step 2 解說：最大允許攜帶量 MAC

MAC 是整批產品中允許的最大殘留總量：

MAC = 2.5 µg/mL × 1,000,000 mL = 2,500,000 µg = 2.5 g

注意：1,000 L = 1,000,000 mL（單位轉換很重要）。

Step 3 解說：每平方公分限度

= 2,500,000 µg ÷ 120,000 cm² = 20.8 µg/cm²

這是設備表面每平方公分的允許殘留量，是棉棒取樣計算的基礎。

Steps 4a & 4b 解說：棉棒限度

每支棉棒 = 20.8 × 100 = 2,080 µg
分析濃度 = 2,080 ÷ 20 = 104 µg/mL (104 ppm)

若產品 A 含 50% 碳的蛋白質：

TOC 限度 = 104 × 50% = 52 ppm TOC

52 ppm TOC 是儀器可偵測的合理範圍（TOC 儀器通常可測 <1 ppm，但這個範例的限度寬鬆許多）。

計算結果摘要（範例一）

計算步驟結果
下一產品濃度限度2.5 µg/mL
MAC（最大允許攜帶量）2,500,000 µg
表面積限度20.8 µg/cm²
每支棉棒限度2,080 µg
分析液濃度限度104 ppm
TOC 限度（50% 碳蛋白）52 ppm TOC

Example 2: Dose-Based Limit for Bulk Drug Manufacturing

This example is based on the dose of the active for bulk drug manufacturing, assuming the entire equipment train is shared surface area. It is based on 1/1000 minimum therapeutic daily dose of the cleaned active.

Note: The purpose of this calculation is to illustrate the low TOC levels likely if the carryover calculation utilized the entire bulk active equipment train (excluding dedicated items).

Variables

VariableDefinition
MinTDMinimum Therapeutic Daily Dose of the active of cleaned product
MBSMinimum Batch Size of next drug active made in the same equipment
MaxDDMaximum Daily Dosage of next drug active made in the same equipment
SFSafety Factor

Given Values

  • MinTD = 25 mg (= 25,000 µg)
  • MBS = 200 g (Minimum Batch Size of drug active B)
  • MaxDD = 100 mg = 0.100 g (Maximum mass of daily dosage unit of active B)
  • SF = 1,000
  • Shared surface area = 1,000,000 cm²
  • Area swabbed = 100 cm²
  • Solvent volume for swab desorption = 20 mL
  • Default limit for bulk active manufacturing = 50 ppm

Step 1: Limit in Next Drug Active

Limit in next product = MinTD / (SF × MaxDD)
= 25,000 µg / (1,000 × 0.100 g)
= 250 µg/g

Since 250 µg/g is above the 50 ppm (50 µg/g) default limit for bulk active manufacturing, the 50 ppm default is used for subsequent calculations.

Step 2: Maximum Allowable Carryover (MAC)

MAC = Limit in next product × MBS
= 50 µg/g × 200 g
= 10,000 µg

Step 3: Limit per Surface Area

Limit per area = MAC / Shared surface area
= 10,000 µg / 1,000,000 cm²
= 0.010 µg/cm²

Step 4a: Limit per Swab

Limit per swab = 0.010 µg/cm² × 100 cm² = 1.0 µg

Step 4b: Limit in Desorbed Swab Sample

Limit in desorbed swab sample = 1.0 µg / 20 mL = 0.050 µg/mL (0.050 ppm)

If the active were a protein containing 50% carbon, the TOC limit (net of blank) would be 0.025 ppm TOC (25 ppb). This concentration is not measurable by TOC in cleaning validation samples.

範例二：逐步解說（原料藥製造）

範例二情境 & 目的

與範例一不同，這個計算是針對原料藥（Bulk API）製程，且假設整個設備系統的表面都是共用面積（1,000,000 cm² vs 範例一的 120,000 cm²）。

核心目的：說明當共用表面積非常大時，TOC 限度會低到無法用儀器偵測。

Step 1 解說：取較不嚴格的限度

計算值 = 250 µg/g（高於 50 ppm 預設）
→ 使用 50 ppm 預設值

注意：此例與範例一不同——計算出的限度比預設值更寬鬆，因此改用 50 ppm 預設值。原則：取計算值和預設值中較嚴格者。

Steps 2–4 解說

MAC = 50 µg/g × 200 g = 10,000 µg
表面積限度 = 10,000 ÷ 1,000,000 = 0.010 µg/cm²
棉棒限度 = 0.010 × 100 = 1.0 µg
分析液 = 1.0 ÷ 20 = 0.050 ppm
TOC 限度 = 0.025 ppm = 25 ppb

關鍵結論：25 ppb TOC 無法量測

標準 TOC 儀器的偵測下限（LOD）通常約為 50–100 ppb。25 ppb 已超出量測能力範圍。

這說明了一個重要的產業實踐問題：若將整個原料藥設備系統視為共用面積，計算出的限度可能不切實際（無法驗證）。因此，生技 API 製程通常採用分段計算或採用較小的共用表面積範圍。

計算結果摘要（範例二）

計算步驟結果
計算限度250 µg/g（不用）
使用限度（50 ppm 預設）50 µg/g
MAC10,000 µg
表面積限度0.010 µg/cm²
每支棉棒限度1.0 µg
分析液濃度限度0.050 ppm
TOC 限度（50% 碳蛋白）0.025 ppm = 25 ppb（無法量測）

比較範例一 vs 範例二

兩個範例使用相同的 MinTD 和 SF，但結果天差地別：

  • 棉棒限度：範例一 = 2,080 µg；範例二 = 1.0 µg（差 2,080 倍）
  • 原因：①共用表面積相差 8.3 倍；②MBS 單位（液體 vs 固體）和 MaxDD 的差異

這說明在批量藥物製造中，共用表面積的範圍界定對計算結果影響極大。

Example 3: Cleaning Agent Toxicity-Based Limit for Formulation/Fill Manufacturing

This example is based on the toxicity of a cleaning agent for formulation/fill manufacturing. It is based on allowing no more than 1/100,000 of the LD50 (mg/kg of body weight in an animal model) of the cleaning agent by an intravenous route in the maximum therapeutic daily dose of the next drug product.

Variables

VariableDefinition
LD50Lethal Dose for Cleaning Agent (mg/kg body weight)
BWBody Weight of patient taking product B
MBSMinimum Batch Size of next drug product made in the same equipment
MaxDDMaximum Daily Dosage of next drug product made in the same equipment
CFConversion Factor (= 100,000)

Given Values

  • LD50 = 100 mg/kg
  • BW = 60 kg
  • MBS = 1,000 L (Minimum Batch Size of drug product B)
  • MaxDD = 10 mL (Maximum Daily Dosage of drug product B)
  • CF = 100,000
  • Shared surface area = 120,000 cm²
  • Area swabbed = 100 cm²
  • Solvent volume for swab desorption = 20 mL

Note on ADI

The product of the LD50 and the BW, which is then divided by the CF, is sometimes called the ADI (Acceptable Daily Intake). Some companies may calculate the ADI by first converting the LD50 to a NOEL (No Observable Effective Level), and then converting the NOEL to an ADI. Either formulation is acceptable and should result in the same ADI value.

Step 1: Limit in Next Product

Limit in next product = (LD50 × BW) / (CF × MaxDD)
= (100 mg/kg × 60 kg) / (100,000 × 10 mL)
= 6,000 mg / 1,000,000 mL
= 0.006 mg/mL (= 6 µg/mL)

Since 6 µg/mL is more stringent than the 10 ppm criterion (approx. 10 µg/mL cleaning agent solids), 6 µg/mL is used.

Step 2: Maximum Allowable Carryover (MAC)

MAC = Limit in next product × MBS
= 6 µg/mL × 1,000,000 mL
= 6,000,000 µg

Step 3: Limit per Surface Area

Limit per area = MAC / Shared surface area
= 6,000,000 µg / 120,000 cm²
= 50 µg/cm²

Step 4a: Limit per Swab

Limit per swab = 50 µg/cm² × 100 cm² = 5,000 µg

Step 4b: Limit in Desorbed Swab Sample

Limit in desorbed swab sample = 5,000 µg / 20 mL = 250 µg/mL (250 ppm)

It is likely in this situation that the manufacturer would utilize a more conservative value for measuring the cleaning agent. For example, utilizing a conductivity value of 5 µS/cm would result in a concentration significantly below 250 ppm for most cleaning agents.

範例三：逐步解說（清潔劑毒性基準）

範例三情境：從清潔劑角度出發

前兩個範例計算的是藥品活性成分的殘留限度；此範例計算的是清潔劑本身的殘留限度，基準從「治療劑量」改為「毒性（LD50）」。

關鍵差異：安全係數從 1,000（1/1000 劑量）改為 100,000（1/100,000 LD50）。

Step 1 解說：ADI 的概念

LD50 × BW = 動物實驗中使 50% 致死的絕對劑量
除以 CF（100,000）= 可接受每日攝入量（ADI）

ADI = (100 mg/kg × 60 kg) ÷ 100,000 = 0.060 mg/day
限度 = ADI ÷ MaxDD = 0.060 mg ÷ 10 mL = 0.006 mg/mL = 6 µg/mL

注意：NOEL（無可見效應劑量）換算路徑不同，但最終 ADI 相同。

Steps 2–4 解說

MAC = 6 µg/mL × 1,000,000 mL = 6,000,000 µg
表面積限度 = 6,000,000 ÷ 120,000 = 50 µg/cm²
棉棒限度 = 50 × 100 = 5,000 µg
分析液 = 5,000 ÷ 20 = 250 ppm

清潔劑的替代檢測方法

250 ppm 的限度非常寬鬆，製造商通常不會用 HPLC 或 TOC 檢測清潔劑，而是直接用電導度計（Conductivity meter）：

  • 5 µS/cm 的電導度值對多數清潔劑來說遠低於 250 ppm
  • 電導度測量快速、廉價、可在線監測（PAT 應用）
  • 這也是為何清潔劑殘留通常用電導度而非化學分析方法驗證

計算結果摘要（範例三）

計算步驟結果
清潔劑濃度限度6 µg/mL
MAC6,000,000 µg
表面積限度50 µg/cm²
每支棉棒限度5,000 µg
分析液濃度限度250 ppm
建議替代方法電導度 ≤ 5 µS/cm
