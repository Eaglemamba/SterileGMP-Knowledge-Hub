1.0 Introduction

1.1 Purpose/Scope

The scope of this document is to provide guidance specific to cleaning validation in biotechnology manufacturing. This guidance is supplemental to PDA Technical Report No. 29 (Revised 2012), Points to Consider for Cleaning Validation (TR29), which provides a broad foundation for cleaning validation. For this document, biotechnology manufacturing refers to processes for production of biological products from recombinant organisms that may include mammalian cell culture, microbial fermentation, and downstream processing.

The intended audience is quality and regulatory professionals as well as manufacturing scientists and engineers who are responsible for cleaning validation in biotechnology manufacturing. Regulatory agencies increasingly expect these professionals to scientifically justify their cleaning validation approaches and to keep their cleaning validation programs in a state of continuous improvement. The content in this document is based on current industry practices and should be referenced in conjunction with applicable regulations. Although this report is focused on processes that manufacture biological/biotech products, many of the principles may be applied to other manufacturing modalities.

This document was written to address the following questions:

  • What are the unique aspects of cleaning validation in biotechnology manufacturing?
  • What are the key scientific principles relevant to cleaning validation?
  • What are the typical regulatory expectations for cleaning validation in biotech manufacturing?

解讀與應用

TR49 的定位：TR29 的生技專屬補充

TR29 是清潔驗證的「通用教科書」，適用於所有製藥製造；TR49 則是針對生物技術藥品的專屬補充指引。兩者必須配合使用，不能互相取代。

在 CDMO 環境中，若廠內同時生產小分子（small molecule）與生技藥品（biotech），清潔驗證程序需分別參照 TR29 與 TR49 的不同要求。

監管機構的期望升高

原文明確指出：監管機構「increasingly expect（日益期望）」業者能科學論證清潔驗證方法，並維持持續改善。這意味著：

  • 不能只說「我們一直這樣做」——需提供科學依據
  • 靜態的清潔驗證程序已不夠——需建立持續監測與回顧機制
  • 稽核時，稽核員會問「你如何知道這個方法是有效的？」

比喻：廚房清潔 vs. 手術室清潔

TR29 就像一般廚房清潔指南——適用於大多數情境。TR49 則像是專門針對手術室（生技藥品製造）設計的超高標準清潔規範，因為生技產品的殘留（蛋白質、核酸、活性生物體）比小分子化合物複雜得多，潛在風險也不同。

三類生技製造製程

  • 哺乳動物細胞培養：如 CHO 細胞生產單株抗體（mAb）——殘留含有複雜蛋白質、培養基成分、病毒風險
  • 微生物發酵：如大腸桿菌生產重組蛋白——殘留含有內毒素（endotoxin）問題更突出
  • 下游純化：層析管柱、超濾膜——清潔困難度高，殘留分析方法需特別設計

2.0 Glossary of Terms

Key terms as defined in TR49. These definitions establish the precise language used throughout the report.

Term Definition
Acceptance Criteria
(Limits)		 The standards (numerical limits, ranges, or other suitable measures) against which the results of a process or its controls are assessed.
Active The ingredient in the drug product that produces a pharmacological effect. In the context of this document, "active" refers to the residue of concern from a biological product or process.
Bioburden The number of viable microorganisms on or in a product, component, or the environment.
Biotechnology Technology used to manipulate living organisms or their components to develop useful products, particularly the molecular biological techniques of recombinant DNA technology.
Carryover Residues of a previously manufactured product or cleaning agent that remain on equipment surfaces after cleaning and are subsequently transferred to the next manufactured product.
CIP
(Clean-In-Place)		 A method of cleaning the interior surfaces of pipes, vessels, process equipment, and associated fittings, without disassembling the equipment.
COP
(Clean-Out-of-Place)		 A method of cleaning equipment by disassembling the equipment and cleaning it in a designated area.
Cleaning Agent A substance used to remove soils from equipment surfaces. Also referred to as detergent.
Cleaning Effectiveness The ability of a cleaning process to reduce residues to acceptable levels.
Cleaning Process The combination of actions, materials, and conditions that result in effective cleaning.
Cleaning Validation Documented evidence with a high degree of assurance that a cleaning process will consistently result in equipment that is suitable for the manufacture of pharmaceutical products.
Critical Surface A surface that can come into direct contact with the product and that represents the most difficult to clean location in the equipment.
Design of Experiments
(DOE)		 A systematic method to determine the relationship between factors affecting a process and the output of that process. Also known as designed experiments.
Dirty Hold The time period between the end of manufacturing and the start of cleaning.
Equipment Train A series of equipment items that are cleaned together as part of the same cleaning cycle.
Excipient Any substance other than the active drug substance that may be included in a pharmaceutical product formulation.
Final Rinse The last rinse of the cleaning cycle, usually with purified water or water for injection (WFI).
HAMA
(Human Anti-Mouse Antibody)		 Antibodies generated by the patient's immune system in response to the administration of murine-derived antibody-based products.
HACA
(Human Anti-Chimeric Antibody)		 Antibodies generated by the patient's immune system against chimeric antibody-based products.
HAHA
(Human Anti-Human Antibody)		 Antibodies generated by the patient's immune system against humanized antibody-based products.
Immunogenicity The ability of a substance to provoke an immune response in an organism.
Limit of Detection
(LOD)		 The lowest quantity of a substance that can be distinguished from the absence of that substance with a stated confidence level.
Limit of Quantitation
(LOQ)		 The minimum amount of analyte that can be quantitatively determined with acceptable precision and accuracy.
Matrix Effect The combined effect of all components of the sample other than the analyte on the measurement of the analyte.
Maximum Allowable Carryover
(MAC)		 The maximum amount of residue that is allowed to carry over from one batch to the next while still complying with established acceptance limits.
Maximum Safe Surface Residue
(MSSR)		 The maximum amount of product or cleaning agent residue that may be present on equipment surfaces after cleaning without causing harm to patients.
Product Contact Surface Any surface that comes into contact with the drug substance or drug product at any point during manufacturing.
Recovery The proportion of a known amount of residue that is found by the analytical method after sampling.
Removal Factor The ratio of the initial amount of a residue to the amount remaining after cleaning.
Rinse The use of solvent (typically water) to flush the interior surfaces of equipment.
Soil The material to be removed from the equipment surface during cleaning. May include drug substance, excipients, cleaning agents, bioburden, endotoxins, and particulates.
Soil Matrix The combination of all soils present on equipment surfaces after manufacturing.
Swab An applicator consisting of an absorbent pad on a handle, used to collect samples from equipment surfaces.
Total Organic Carbon
(TOC)		 A measure of the total amount of organic carbon in a sample, typically used as a non-specific analytical method for cleaning validation.
Worst Case The set of conditions encompassing upper and lower processing limits and circumstances, including those within standard operating procedures, which pose the greatest chance of process or product failure when compared to ideal conditions.

術語深度解析

最重要的三組術語配對

以下三組術語在清潔驗證中最常被混淆，務必釐清：

  • LOD vs. LOQ：LOD（偵測下限）是「能不能偵測到」；LOQ（定量下限）是「能不能精確測量」。清潔驗證中，LOQ 更重要——因為我們需要準確知道殘留量是多少。
  • MAC vs. MSSR：MAC（最大允許攜帶量）是基於下一批產品的劑量計算；MSSR（最大安全表面殘留量）是基於病患安全計算的設備表面限值。兩者都要計算，取較嚴格者。
  • CIP vs. COP：CIP（就地清洗）設備不拆開直接清洗，效率高但驗證難；COP（移出清洗）拆卸後清洗，可徹底但人力需求大。

生技製造中「Soil（污染物）」的特殊性

在生技製造中，Soil 遠比小分子藥廠複雜：

  • 蛋白質殘留：可能引發免疫原性（immunogenicity）——這正是 HAMA/HACA/HAHA 術語的來源
  • 核酸殘留：宿主細胞 DNA 殘留有其安全限值（如 WHO 建議 <10 ng/劑）
  • 內毒素（Endotoxin）：革蘭氏陰性菌發酵製程尤其需要關注
  • 培養基成分：胺基酸、血清、促進劑等複雜混合物

比喻：Recovery（回收率）就像擦桌子後的驗收

想像你用棉棒擦一張桌子，桌上原本有 100 個單位的殘留物，但你的棉棒只收集到 70 個單位——Recovery = 70%。

在清潔驗證中，如果你的採樣方法 Recovery 只有 50%，而你設定的限值是 10 ppm，你必須確保實際偵測到的是 ≤5 ppm 才夠安全。回收率校正是計算真實殘留量的關鍵步驟。

免疫原性相關術語：HAMA / HACA / HAHA

這三個術語都描述「患者對藥物中非人源成分產生的抗體反應」，是生技清潔驗證中殘留攜帶（Carryover）風險評估的重要依據：

  • HAMA：針對鼠源（murine）成分——早期 mAb 藥物的主要問題
  • HACA：針對嵌合體（chimeric）成分
  • HAHA：針對人源化（humanized）成分——即便是「人源化」抗體也可能引發免疫反應

這些術語說明：即使是微量的蛋白質殘留攜帶，也可能因免疫原性而對患者造成嚴重影響。

Dirty Hold（髒放置時間）的實務意義

Dirty Hold 是設備用完後到開始清洗的等待時間。在生技製造中，這個時間段特別關鍵：

  • 蛋白質殘留在設備表面乾燥後，變性形成難以去除的薄膜
  • 微生物在培養基殘留中大量增殖，增加 Bioburden 風險
  • 因此，生技廠的 SOP 通常設定嚴格的 Dirty Hold 時間上限（如 ≤24 小時）

練習思考 1：術語應用

情境：你的 CHO 細胞培養槽剛完成一批單株抗體生產。清洗後，你用棉棒採樣，TOC 分析顯示表面殘留為 3.2 µg/cm²，而你的 MSSR 計算結果為 5.0 µg/cm²。

問題：這次清洗是否符合標準？如果採樣的 Recovery 只有 80%，你的判斷是否改變？

提示：校正後實際殘留 = 偵測值 ÷ Recovery = 3.2 ÷ 0.8 = 4.0 µg/cm²，仍在 MSSR 5.0 以內，符合標準。

練習思考 2：術語辨別

以下哪種情境更需要優先考慮 Immunogenicity 風險？

  1. 小分子化學藥廠，相鄰批次為不同劑量的同一 API
  2. 生技廠，同一設備先後生產鼠源 mAb（Product A）和人源化 mAb（Product B）
  3. 生技廠，同一設備連續生產相同的重組蛋白

答案：B。鼠源蛋白的 HAMA 殘留攜帶至 Product B 批次中，可能引發患者的免疫反應，且因 Product A/B 之蛋白質結構不同，無法以相同 HAMA 抗體相互抵消。

Section 0 Summary | 本節重點總結

Key Takeaways — 核心要點

  • TR49 補充 TR29，專為生技製造清潔驗證設計
  • 生技製造涵蓋細胞培養、發酵、下游純化三大類型
  • MAC 與 MSSR 是清潔驗證限值計算的核心工具
  • Recovery 校正是確保採樣結果有效性的必要步驟
  • Dirty Hold 時間需在 SOP 中明確管控

Key Terms to Remember — 必記術語

  • 清潔驗證 (Cleaning Validation) — 有文件佐證的高保證度清潔程序
  • 最大允許攜帶量 (MAC) — 批次間殘留攜帶的最高允許量
  • 就地清洗 (CIP) vs. 移出清洗 (COP)
  • 總有機碳 (TOC) — 非專屬性分析方法
  • 最差情況 (Worst Case) — 挑戰性最高的清潔驗證條件
