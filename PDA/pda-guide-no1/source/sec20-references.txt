21.0 Appendix 1: Liquid Filling Case Studies
When considering what type of filler would be most appropriate for an application, several considerations
are important:
• Ability to set up and operate in an aseptic manner for the delivery of a sterile product
• Product characteristics
• Container/closure to be filled
• Mechanical capabilities of the possible filling technologies
• Hygienic design requirements for the equipment and for the environment of the cleanroom
(especially decisions on location of components inside or outside of any barrier)
• Good manufacturing practice (GMP) and regulatory requirements for the filling operation
• Business needs and objectives
In addition, the filling system (and fluid pathway) should be easy to set up, operate and adjust, thereby
limiting contact with critical surfaces to the absolute minimum or best yet to no required contact at all.
When interventions are required, placement of filling system elements should enable installation and
adjustment while not compromising the quality of the environment and surrounding equipment.
The prior chapters in this Manufacturing Technology Guide outline the considerations for appropriate
design and selection of a filling technology, including key considerations in filtration, transfer of
components, environment, and utilities. This chapter presents two case studies, one for a small volume
parenteral (SVP) and one for a large volume parenteral (LVP) and steps through key decision-making in the
selection of the appropriate filling technology.
The examples are for illustrative purposes only and should not be taken as prescriptive requirements or
exclusive approaches. The products that are presented in the case studies are not intended to represent any
specific company’s product. Instead, the examples have been selected to provide the greatest possible
contrasts in decision-making. In each example, the pertinent sections of this Manufacturing Technology
Guide were converted to a decision-matrix that was used to identify the key design attributes for the filling
system that would be required for each product. When a specific attribute of the product or its
container/closure influenced the design decision, a brief explanation of how that attribute influenced
decision-making was provided.
The extensive list of requirements for the fictional products in the case studies precedes a decision matrix
and conclusion at the end of each case study that highlights the key factors that drove the technology
selection.
