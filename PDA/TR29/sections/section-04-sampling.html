6.0

Sampling | 取樣概述

Introduction to sampling strategy and method selection

6.0 Sampling

This section covers the key sampling methods used in cleaning validation: visual inspection, Rinse Sampling, Swab Sampling, and Placebo Sampling. In addition, sampling considerations for microbial and endotoxin analysis are discussed, along with Recovery Studies and the training and qualification of samplers.

The selection of a sampling method should be based on the nature of the residue, the equipment design, and the intended analytical method. No single method is universally superior; the best approach is often a combination of methods.

Visual inspection is the most basic form of sampling and represents the minimum regulatory expectation: the equipment must be visually clean when viewed with the unaided eye. Use of magnification aids is considered more stringent.

Instrumental surface methods (e.g., ATR-FTIR probe via fiber-optic cable) allow in-situ analysis without removing residue from the surface. These techniques do not require a separate Sampling Recovery study but are limited by probe length and surface geometry — many Worst-Case Locations cannot be reached.

6.1.2

Rinse Sampling | 沖洗取樣

Grab samples, separate sampling rinses, and special cases

6.1.2 Rinse Sampling

Rinse Sampling involves flowing solvent (water, aqueous solution, organic solvent, or mixture) over all relevant equipment surfaces to remove residues, which are then measured in the rinse solvent. Key considerations include solubility, location, timing, and volume.

Two main approaches exist: (1) a "grab" sample taken from the final portion of the rinse solvent during the final rinse of the cleaning process; (2) a separate sampling rinse performed after completion of the process rinse, which may involve filling the equipment with solvent, agitating for homogeneity, and then analyzing a sample.

Rationales for Rinse Sampling

  • Equipment not accessible for other types of sampling
  • The residue is volatile, so measuring it on dried surfaces is not appropriate
  • Rinse sampling adequately measures residues on surfaces

6.1.2.1 Extraction Rinse Sampling for Small Parts

Small parts may be sampled by placing them in extraction solvent and agitating or sonicating for a fixed time. For items with an orifice (e.g., filling needles), a fixed volume of sampling solution is passed through the lumen and analyzed. Because the surface area and sampling volume are precisely known, limits can be accurately calculated.

6.1.2.2 Solvent Reflux Sampling

In organic solvent reflux sampling, volatile organic solvent is heated to vaporize it. The vapors condense on upper parts of the manufacturing equipment, dissolve soluble residues, and carry them back to the reactor. Although the distribution mechanism differs, the principles of rinse sampling still apply.

6.1.3

Swab & Wipe Sampling | 棉棒／擦拭取樣

Direct surface sampling for worst-case locations

6.1.3 Swab and Wipe Sampling

Swab Sampling and wipe sampling involve wiping a surface with a fibrous material to transfer residue, which is then extracted into a solvent for analysis. Swabs consist of textile (knitted, woven, or nonwoven) attached to a plastic handle. Wipes are larger fibrous materials applied by hand. Cotton balls or pads moved across a surface with forceps are a special case.

The selection of swab or wipe requires evaluation of extractables and shedding properties. Recovery of residues depends on the size and shape of the swab head or wipe, as well as the properties of the swab handle (flexibility, length).

In most cases, swabs and wipes are wetted with a solvent prior to sampling. The solvent should assist in dissolving the residue and be compatible with the analytical method (e.g., mobile phase for HPLC; water for TOC and conductivity). Multiple swabs or wipes may be used on the same site to increase recovery; additional swabs may be dry or wetted.

6.2

Placebo Sampling | 安慰劑取樣

Demonstrating absence of carryover to the next product

6.2 Placebo Sampling

Placebo Sampling can be used to detect residues on equipment through the processing of a placebo batch subsequent to the cleaning process. It is used primarily to demonstrate the lack of carryover to the next product. The placebo should mimic product attributes.

Placebo sampling may also be called "mock runs" or "blank runs". In biotechnology, this generally involves processing only with water — which is different from rinse sampling in that the water is processed through the equipment much as the product would be.

In the sampling process, the equipment is first cleaned. Following cleaning, a manufacturing process is performed (to the extent feasible) using only a placebo product. The placebo product is then evaluated for residues as for any other cleaning validation sample.

Placebo runs can demonstrate actual carryover to the processed material, but if done, are typically used to complement swab/wipe and/or rinse sampling rather than replace them. The equipment characteristics also impact the choice of the placebo batch size. Placebo sampling may present analytical challenges for measuring residues in a true placebo (excipients may interfere with the analytical method).

中文重點解析

安慰劑取樣的本質與定位

安慰劑取樣（Placebo Sampling）是清洗驗證中最接近「真實產品生產」情境的取樣方式，用於直接評估前一批產品殘留物是否會轉移到下一批次的安慰劑中。

  • 主要目的：證明無攜帶污染（No Carryover）
  • 安慰劑要求：應模擬真實產品的物理化學特性（黏度、pH、與設備的相互作用）
  • 生技業變形：「Mock Run」或「Blank Run」，有時僅以注射用水（WFI）代替產品進行操作

安慰劑取樣的限制 — 不可忽視的挑戰

  • 分析干擾：安慰劑中的賦形劑可能干擾目標殘留物的分析（如非特異性 TOC 訊號）
  • 批次大小限制：設備容積可能限制安慰劑批次的合理規模
  • 均勻性假設：殘留物在安慰劑中的分布不一定均勻，取樣點選擇至關重要
  • 補充而非替代：應與棉棒或沖洗取樣互補使用，而非單獨作為驗證手段

比喻：安慰劑取樣就像「試飲」

想像用洗碗後的杯子倒一杯白開水喝看看——若嚐出了前一杯咖啡的味道，表示清洗不合格。安慰劑取樣的邏輯與此相同：用「空白產品」跑過清洗後的設備，看最終產品是否帶有前批殘留。

6.3

Microbial & Endotoxin Sampling | 微生物與內毒素取樣

Special considerations for bioburden and LAL testing

6.3 Sampling for Microbial and Endotoxin Analysis

Sampling for bioburden may involve rinse-water sampling and/or swabbing, and may also involve contact plates. For swabbing, a sterile solution such as phosphate-buffered saline (PBS) should be used. For rinse sampling of large equipment, use of sterile water is generally not practical; purified water or WFI is used, and results may be compared to a blank taken from the same use point.

Rinse-water sampling for bioburden should use sterile sample containers. "Aseptic" sampling technique — much like that used for cleanroom bioburden sampling — is required for any microbial method to avoid external contamination of the sample.

Sampling for endotoxin is almost always a rinse water sample, preferably with low endotoxin water.

中文重點解析

無菌取樣技術要求

微生物取樣必須採用無菌操作技術（類同潔淨室環境監測的操作規範），以防止外部微生物污染樣品，造成假陽性結果。這是微生物取樣與化學殘留取樣最大的差異點。

收集沖洗水樣品時，容器本身必須預先滅菌，且需在無菌容器中密封保存，於規定時間內送達實驗室檢測。

6.4

Additional Considerations | 補充考量

SOPs, material compatibility, and sample order

6.4 Additional Considerations

It is preferred to have a separate sampling SOP (apart from any special instructions in a cleaning validation protocol). This helps prevent "procedure drift" — which might occur if the swabbing procedure text is repeated in every protocol. A separate SOP also ensures that the same sampling procedure is used in recovery studies as in protocol execution, simplifying training.

The rinse sampling procedure may be the same procedure used for sampling water systems, appropriately modified to cover sampling of process equipment.

In selecting sampling techniques, considerations should include:

  • Compatibility of sampling materials (vials, swabs, sampling solutions) with each other, with the residue, and with the analytical method.
  • Any requirement for cleaning or removing sampling materials from the sampled surface after sampling must be addressed in the design and selection of sampling methods.

Finally, in taking samples in a protocol, consideration should be given to the impact of a given sample on subsequent samples. This includes the order in which samples are taken — considering both the type of sampling method (visual, rinse, swab) and the type of residue (active, cleaning agent, bioburden, endotoxin).

中文重點解析

獨立取樣 SOP 的重要性

將取樣程序從清洗驗證方案（Protocol）中獨立出來，有三大好處：

  • 防止程序漂移（Procedure Drift）：若每個 Protocol 都自行描述取樣步驟，不同版本間容易產生細微差異，導致取樣不一致。
  • 統一訓練基礎：取樣員只需熟悉一份 SOP，回收率研究與正式執行使用完全相同的程序。
  • 易於版本管控：SOP 更新後，所有 Protocol 自動受益，無需逐一修訂。

取樣順序的影響 — 不可忽視

正確的取樣順序原則：

  • 先目視，再取樣：目視檢查不會汙染設備，應最先進行
  • 先化學，後微生物：化學取樣（棉棒/沖洗）後，設備表面條件已改變，微生物取樣應在其前或以獨立方式進行
  • 先活性成分，後清洗劑：活性成分通常是主要的殘留關注點，應優先取樣
  • 先難清洗位置，後一般位置：避免前一個取樣點對後續點造成交叉污染

材料相容性 — 實務陷阱

取樣材料選擇若不當，可能導致假陰性或假陽性：

  • 使用含有機物的棉棒萃取液 + TOC 分析 → 棉棒本身的有機物干擾結果
  • 使用異丙醇清潔取樣口 + TOC 分析 → IPA 殘留干擾 TOC 數值
  • 玻璃樣品瓶 + 矽膠墊片 → 矽膠浸出物可能干擾 HPLC 分析

解決方案：預先評估所有取樣材料的空白（Blank）值。

6.5

Sampling Recovery Studies | 取樣回收率研究

Swab, rinse, visual, bioburden, and endotoxin recovery

6.5 Sampling Recovery Studies

Recovery Studies are generally required to demonstrate that a residue, if present on equipment surfaces, can be adequately measured by the combination of the analytical method and the sampling procedure. These studies provide a scientific basis for utilizing sampling and analytical methods to measure residues. The objective is to establish a reproducible level of recovery from equipment surfaces.

Recovery studies may be performed as part of analytical method validation or as separate studies. They are laboratory studies involving coupons of sampled equipment of different materials of construction (stainless steel, glass, PTFE, EPDM) spiked with residues to be measured.

6.5.1 General Considerations

Recovery studies may not be required for residues that are known to be readily soluble (as defined in USP or Merck Index, used well below the solubility limit, such as NaOH or H₃PO₄ used as cleaning agents), provided they are not reactive with or absorbed into the surface.

The amount of material spiked onto coupons should represent an amount equal to what could be present at the residue limit. Spiked levels at extremely low levels may give lower recovery percentages due to analytical method variability at those levels.

The spiked residue should represent the same residue present at the end of the cleaning process. In practice, it is common to only spike the active ingredient for active recovery studies, and only spike the cleaning agent for cleaning agent recovery studies. Drying and/or holding times of spiked coupons should be appropriate for the nature of the residue.

If the active degrades during cleaning, recovery studies are typically performed by spiking with the active ingredient itself, unless degradation products have significantly different recovery levels or unusual safety/solubility concerns.

A grouping or family approach may be used for recovery studies — performing one residue study on a surface documented to be equivalent (in terms of percent recovery) to other surfaces. Surfaces constituting less than 1–2% of total equipment surface area may be excluded from formal recovery studies, using the lowest recovery of any other surface type, or the company's minimum acceptable recovery percentage.

6.5.2 Swab/Wipe Recovery

Coupons are spiked in a controlled manner, allowed to dry, and sampled with the swabbing procedure. The amount recovered is compared to the amount spiked and expressed as percent recovery.

Because swabbing is a manual procedure, each person typically performs a recovery study with three replicates. It is preferable to have at least two people perform swabbing recovery studies for each combination of residue and surface type.

Acceptable recovery criteria:

  • ≥70% — required when recovery is used to qualify the sampling method without correcting a limit or analytical result
  • ≥50% — required when the recovery percentage is used to correct a residue limit or analytical result
  • <50% — requires a written rationale for why that percentage is appropriate
  • An upper limit for percent recovery should be established (to address recoveries >100%)
  • Acceptable variation within one operator: typically 15–30% RSD

Recovery studies are typically performed on a nominal coupon surface area of 25 cm² or 100 cm². A range of acceptable surface area (e.g., 25% to 150% of nominal) should be established. If the sampled area varies from nominal, the residue limit should be adjusted based on the actual surface area swabbed.

6.5.3 Rinse Recovery

Rinse recovery studies demonstrate that residues on a surface would be effectively removed and could be analyzed in the rinse solution. They address the FDA "dirty pot" and "baby/bath water" analogies. Studies are performed on spiked coupons; exact rinsing conditions cannot be fully duplicated in the laboratory, but should be simulated.

The ratio of solvent to sampled surface area should be the same or lower in the recovery study as compared to the equipment rinsing situation (worst case). Since laboratory rinse sampling studies are generally not operator dependent, three replicates by one operator may be adequate.

6.5.4 "Recovery" in Visual Inspection

This is actually the determination of a quantitative visual detection limit. Coupons are spiked at different levels (in μg/cm²) and a panel of trained observers determines the lowest level at which residues are clearly visible. Typical values reported in the literature for a visual detection limit are 1–4 μg/cm². A percent recovery is not established; the purpose is to establish the value below which any visually clean surface clearly lies.

6.5.5 Recovery for Bioburden and Endotoxin Sampling

Percentage recovery studies are not normally performed for microbiological sampling. Four reasons:

  1. Microbiological tests count colony forming units (CFU), not individual organisms — enumeration is inherently different from chemical quantification.
  2. Vegetative organisms will die or lose viability when dried on a coupon in a standard recovery procedure.
  3. It is unclear which species should be used for a recovery study.
  4. Bioburden limits are typically set significantly below levels that could cause product quality issues, so even low recovery does not impact safety conclusions.

Endotoxin recovery studies from surfaces are also not ordinarily performed. Standard endotoxin from LAL test kit suppliers may not be indicative of endogenic endotoxins from a manufacturing process. The cleaning process itself is very effective at physically removing endotoxin along with other manufacturing soils.

回收率研究可接受標準 — 快速判斷樹

  • ≥70%：直接用於判斷，無需修正限度或分析結果
  • 50–70%：可接受，但需將限度或結果除以回收率係數進行修正

分組法（Grouping / Family Approach）

當設備含多種接觸材質時，可使用分組策略減少回收率研究工作量：

  • 選擇一種「代表性材質」（documented equivalent）執行完整回收率研究

  • 佔總表面積 <1–2% 的材質，可直接套用最低回收率值
  • 此策略需有文獻或內部資料支持

為何微生物不做回收率研究？

化學回收率研究的前提是「待測物在乾燥後仍保持穩定」。然而活菌在 coupon 乾燥過程中會死亡，根本無法模擬「表面存在的活菌」被棉棒採集的情境。此外，CFU 計數本身已有固有變異性，回收率概念在此不適用。因此，微生物取樣的品質保證依賴無菌操作技術的驗證，而非數值回收率。

6.6

Training & Qualification of Samplers | 取樣員訓練與認證

Swab, rinse, and visual inspection training requirements

6.6 Training and Qualification of Samplers

Training involves the steps taken to assist the prospective sampler in learning the technique. Qualification involves the process of "certifying" that the prospective sampler can appropriately sample. For this section, "sampling" and "sampler" also include "inspection" and "inspector" for visual evaluation.

Training always precedes qualification. At a minimum, training involves:

  1. Reading of the sampling procedure
  2. Demonstration of the correct procedure by a trained sampler (including rationale for certain practices)
  3. Demonstration of correct procedure by the prospective sampler

Demonstration of technique may also utilize a visual indicator on the swabbed surface to help the trainee see the consequences of poor technique.

Qualification processes will depend on the type of sampling performed. Qualification may involve:

  • Demonstration of correct technique (last step of training), or
  • A "test" that challenges the trainee's ability to perform correctly (e.g., swab sampling for a known soil residue level on coupons; visual inspection using an array of coupons where some are soiled and others are not)

Qualification may be repeated on a regular basis or upon any retraining. Retraining may be conducted based on suspected operator error, or because an operator has not performed a swabbing event over a certain time frame.

6.6.1 Key Issues for Training for Swab Sampling

Four keys to consistency in swab sampling training:

  1. Consistency of wetting the swab head
  2. Consistency of the swabbing motion (including overlapping strokes)
  3. Consistency in applied pressure
  4. Consistency in swabbing of the correct surface area

A fifth factor for some types (e.g., TOC analysis): preventing external contamination of the swab from volatile organics in the atmosphere around the sampling location.

Because swab sampling depends heavily on operator consistency, consideration should be given to have swab samplers retrained and/or requalified on an established basis. Retraining and requalification should also be addressed as part of change control for the swabbing procedure, and when swab sampling "operator error" is suspected in the investigation of a nonconforming result.

6.6.2 Key Issues for Training for Rinse Sampling

The major concern for accuracy in rinse samples is to prevent contamination of the rinse sample. Sources of contamination include the sampling port, the environment around the port, and the operator. Prevention steps include:

  • Adequately flushing or cleaning the port prior to taking a sample
  • Avoiding isopropanol on gloves or for cleaning the port when TOC is the analytical procedure

Timing is critical for grab sampling from the final CIP rinse. Once process rinsing is complete, there is no way to go back and collect a rinse sample. The last portion of the rinse is typically sampled, but it may be acceptable to sample earlier if it represents a worst case.

Since rinse sampling consistency is less operator dependent, there may be no need for routine retraining and/or requalification; however, the need should be addressed in change control and when operator error is suspected.

6.6.3 Training for Visual Inspection

Visual inspection training depends on whether the inspection is part of protocol execution, routine monitoring, or laboratory "limit of detection" determination. A visual inspection SOP is preferred. Visual acuity of inspectors must be addressed.

For protocol execution: key issues are access to sites, appropriate lighting, and the ability to discern residues from surface imperfections. Inspectors must know when to call for further analysis.

For laboratory visual detection limit determination: inspectors must distinguish among a visually clean surface, a partially soiled surface, and a "fully" soiled surface. Viewing conditions (lighting, distance, angle) should be similar to or a worst case of actual equipment inspection conditions.

棉棒取樣 4 大一致性要素 — 記憶口訣

使用「濕動壓面」幫助記憶：

  • 濕：棉頭潤濕程度的一致性
  • 動：擦拭動作的一致性（含重疊筆畫，S 型或 Z 型路徑）

  • 面：擦拭面積的一致性（不多也不少）

額外第 5 項（TOC 分析時）：防止大氣中揮發性有機物污染棉棒。

目視檢查 SOP 的核心要素

  • 視力要求：需定期評估取樣員視力（含色覺），類同無菌檢查員的視力管理
  • 照明條件：規定照明強度、角度（oblique lighting 斜光可增強對比）
  • 觀察距離：通常規定在固定距離內觀察
  • 殘留 vs. 表面缺陷判別：取樣員必須知道何時須進一步分析確認
  • 記錄要求：即使目視合格，也需記錄觀察結論

為什麼棉棒取樣需要定期再認證？

棉棒取樣的結果有很高的操作者依賴性（operator dependent）——就像手術縫合技術，若長時間未練習就會退步。即使同一名操作者，若數月未執行棉棒取樣，技術（特別是施力均勻性和重疊筆畫）可能退步，導致回收率降低而產生假陰性結果。因此，建議建立再認證頻率（如每年一次）並在 SOP 中明確規定。

Key takeaways from Sections 6.0–6.6

Core Principles Summary

  • No single sampling method is universally superior; choose based on residue nature, equipment design, and analytical method.
  • Visual inspection is the minimum regulatory expectation; unaided eye is the baseline.
  • Rinse sampling covers the entire flow path but only detects soluble residues; swab sampling targets worst-case locations but is technique dependent.
  • Placebo sampling complements swab/rinse; it does not replace them.
  • Microbial sampling requires aseptic technique; recovery studies are not applicable to microbiological testing.
  • A separate sampling SOP prevents procedure drift and simplifies training.
  • Recovery study acceptance: ≥70% without correction; ≥50% with correction; <50% requires written rationale.
  • Swab sampler qualification must address the 4 consistency factors; routine requalification is recommended.

關鍵術語快速查閱

  • 棉棒取樣（Swab Sampling）：直接擦拭表面特定位置
  • 沖洗取樣（Rinse Sampling）：沖洗液代表整體流路殘留
  • 安慰劑取樣（Placebo Sampling）：模擬產品批次評估攜帶污染
  • 回收率研究（Recovery Study）：以 Coupon 驗證取樣+分析方法組合的有效性
  • 取樣回收率（Sampling Recovery）：回收量/Spike量 × 100%
  • 關鍵表面（Critical Surface）：直接接觸產品的表面
  • 最差情況位置（Worst-Case Location）：最難清洗、最可能有殘留的位置（死角、焊縫、閥座等）
