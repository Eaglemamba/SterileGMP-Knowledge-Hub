6.0 Environmental Monitoring by Manufacturing Application

The levels and types of monitoring conducted may vary based on the type of product being manufactured, the attributes of the product, and the manufacturing areas and processes used.

6.1 Terminal Sterilization

Environmental monitoring in support of terminal sterilization monitors microflora from all sources — manufacturing environment, process gases, water used in product formulations, sterilizer feed and cooling water — that have the potential to contribute to product bioburden or endotoxin. This includes testing of air, surfaces, and water for pharmaceutical use.

Microbial levels of incoming containers and closures may also be monitored periodically (e.g., if the product is not terminally sterilized using an overkill cycle). While control of the environment in which the products are prepared is important, the most critical aspect of the program is the bioburden of the product and components to be sterilized, including both the number and type of microorganisms found.

中文解析：製造應用類別決定監控深度

TR13 Section 6 依製程類型分門別類地說明 EM 要求，這是本報告最具實務導向的章節。核心邏輯：產品風險等級愈高，EM 範圍愈廣、頻率愈高。

終端滅菌 vs. 無菌加工的根本差異

終端滅菌（Terminal Sterilization）：產品於最終容器中接受滅菌處理。EM 的目的是監控對 bioburden（生物負載）及 endotoxin（內毒素）有貢獻的所有來源。

最關鍵的指標不是環境本身，而是待滅菌產品與組件的生物負載總量與種類——因為這直接決定滅菌製程是否能達到要求的無菌保證水準（SAL）。

若採用 overkill cycle（過殺滅菌），對容器/密封件的定期微生物監測可彈性處理；若非 overkill 製程，則應定期監測容器/密封件的菌量。

比喻：廚房食材 vs. 外科手術室

終端滅菌猶如把食材放入高壓鍋蒸煮滅菌——食材本身的污染程度（bioburden）才是能否煮熟的關鍵，廚房環境的清潔程度是次要的。無菌加工則像外科手術，每一個步驟都必須在絕對無菌的環境中進行，因為沒有最後的「煮沸」步驟。

6.2 Aseptic Processing

Environmental monitoring in support of aseptic processing is specifically designed to determine the number and type of microorganisms associated with the direct assembly or preparation of product prior to sealing the filled containers. The number of sample sites and frequency of monitoring are considerably greater than those for established terminal sterilization programs.

Air, water, personnel, compressed gases, machinery, and other surfaces within the filling room and associated support areas are routinely monitored. Microbial levels may be periodically checked on incoming containers and closures. Adequate environmental control is an integral part of the aseptic manufacturing process and is critical to sterility assurance.

EM data must be reviewed prior to product batch release and investigation of excursions closed. EM should also be conducted when operator interventions are required during aseptic processing.

6.2.1 Isolation Technology

The EM program for isolators differs from conventional aseptic filling operations and is based on the risks associated with isolator technology — especially the physical integrity of half-suits (if applicable) and gloves. Monitoring of air, surfaces, and personnel outside of the isolator environment is not required after each filling operation.

However, monitoring of isolator gloves and half-suits is required at the end of each filling operation or campaign (as applicable) to detect potential contamination. Product contact surfaces that are sterilized or changed out between batches in a campaign should be monitored at the end of the batch specific activities, prior to sterilization / change out.

中文解析：無菌加工的全面監控要求

無菌加工的 EM 範圍涵蓋：空氣、水、人員、壓縮氣體、機械設備及所有表面。這是因為產品在填充與密封之間的每一個接觸點都有污染風險。

重要法規要求：批次放行前必須審查 EM 數據

TR13 明確指出：EM 資料必須在產品批次放行前完成審查，且所有超標調查必須結案。這意味著 EM 不只是事後統計工具，而是批次放行（batch release）的前提條件之一。

此外，操作員進行干預（interventions）時，也應同步進行 EM，以捕捉干預動作可能帶來的污染風險。

6.2.1 隔離器技術的特殊監控邏輯

隔離器（Isolator）透過物理屏障將操作人員與產品完全隔離，因此：

  • 隔離器外部的空氣、表面、人員監控，不需在每次填充操作後進行
  • 隔離器手套與半身防護服（half-suits）每次填充或每個 campaign 結束時必須監控——這是最關鍵的污染入侵路徑
  • 產品接觸面在批次間進行滅菌或更換前，應在批次特定作業結束後監控

這種差異反映了 EM 的風險導向原則：監控重心隨著物理屏障的存在而轉移。

6.3 Air Monitoring

Effectively designed air-handling units and use of HEPA filters or ultralow particulate air (ULPA) filters bring air of appropriate quality to cleanrooms. People are considered a major contributor of particulates, especially microorganisms, to the environment in aseptic areas.

Air monitoring determines if there are viable and/or total airborne particulates in locations that would allow them to settle on product-contact surfaces and thereby find their way into process intermediates or final product. The analysis of trends and frequencies is also vital to the understanding of the environmental impact of personnel behavior and process activities. Air-sampling methods and frequencies of monitoring will depend on manufacturing process needs and should be risk-based.

6.3.1 Total Airborne Particulate Monitoring

Monitoring of total airborne particulates is a necessary component of an EM program. It demonstrates control of potential contaminants in the environment to which the product is exposed during the manufacturing process. Classified areas must consistently meet particulate levels (see Section 3.0).

ISO Standard 14644-1 describes the classification of air cleanliness for cleanrooms based on specified concentrations of airborne particulates per volume of air sampled. The sample volume for routine monitoring does not need to be the same as that used for formal classification.

For the United States, FDA requires measurements of particles ≥0.5 μm. EU and PIC/S Annex 1 also include measurements for particles ≥5 μm for both classification and monitoring purposes.

For remote particle counter systems, the distance from the sample collection port to the detection technology should be considered for potential particle loss. The length of tubing should typically not exceed one meter and the radius should be greater than 15 cm, aligned with ISO 14644.

中文解析：空氣監控的雙重任務

空氣監控同時追蹤兩件事：總微粒（非活性 + 活性）和活性微粒（viable particulates）。兩者搭配才能完整評估環境品質。

人員是最大污染源

即使 HEPA 過濾系統效率高達 99.97%，人員本身仍是潔淨室最主要的微粒與微生物來源。每個人每分鐘可釋放數千至數百萬個微粒，穿著不符規定的潔淨服更會大幅增加此數量。

這也是為何人員行為監控（Personnel Behavior Monitoring）和 EM 趨勢分析必須相互搭配——當 EM 出現上升趨勢，第一個檢查對象往往是人員。

重要：Grade A (ISO 5) 必須連續監控微粒

法規要求：Grade A（ISO 5）無菌加工區域在操作期間必須進行連續微粒監控（continuous monitoring）。

例外情況：若操作本身會產生大量產品微粒（如粉末填充），且這些微粒本身不構成污染風險，可在暴露前後分別監控，而非持續監控——因為此時無法區分外來污染與製程固有微粒。連續監控還可能損壞量測設備。

遠端計數系統（remote counting systems）的管路長度不得超過 1 公尺、半徑須大於 15 cm（依 ISO 14644）。

美國 vs. 歐盟/PIC/S 粒徑要求差異

  • 美國 FDA：≥0.5 μm 粒徑（分類 + 例行監控）
  • EU Annex 1 / PIC/S：≥0.5 μm 及 ≥5 μm（兩者均需監控）

這是市場差異化設計點，在台灣以外銷為主的廠商需特別注意歐盟要求。

6.3.2 Viable Particulates

Viable particulate determinations are critical in monitoring air quality in controlled environments. Traditional growth-based techniques for monitoring viable particulates in air are limited by:

  • Type of equipment
  • Growth media
  • Incubation time
  • Operator contamination of the capture media

6.3.2.2 Methods

Regulatory agencies currently require active air sampling of environments on a routine basis to demonstrate control of viable airborne particulates. Some agencies require that passive sampling methods such as settling plates should be performed, with justification provided if a company does not use passive sampling for select operations.

Active sampling methods have the benefit of sampling large homogeneous volumes of air in short amounts of time, but these methods may cause disruptions in the airflow of the environment being sampled. Therefore, these sampling methods should be included in airflow visualization studies.

Passive sampling methods can be used for prolonged periods (up to four hours) in close proximity to critical processes without affecting the airflow. An additional advantage of passive sampling is that it mimics the potential pathway of contamination to a critical process.

6.3.2.3 Equipment

Microbial air samplers are typically based on two principles — inertial or centrifugal impaction — to capture and deposit organisms onto the agar or filter surface. No microbial air samplers are 100% efficient at detection or collection of all viable particles present.

The physical efficiency of an air sampler is referred to as the "cutoff size" or "d50 value" — the size of a particle in microns that the sampler has a 50% chance of recovering — which is supplied by the equipment manufacturer.

Equipment user requirements include: calibration of the instrument and verification of volume accuracy.

中文解析：活性微粒採樣方法比較

d50 值的意義

d50 值是空氣採樣器的物理效率指標：代表採樣器有 50% 機率捕獲的粒徑大小（單位：μm）。d50 值愈小，代表採樣器能捕獲更小粒徑的微粒，效率愈高。

此值由設備製造商提供，用於評估設備是否適合特定監控場景。沒有任何空氣採樣器能達到 100% 收集效率——這是傳統生長法採樣固有的限制之一。

主動採樣器須經設備確效（qualification），包括：儀器校準（calibration）及體積準確性驗證（volume accuracy verification）。

主動採樣（Active Sampling）vs. 被動採樣（Passive Sampling）

實務建議：兩者搭配使用，主動採樣提供基準定量數據，被動採樣揭示操作期間的動態落菌風險。

6.4 Surface Monitoring

Surface monitoring is conducted to determine the bioburden of surfaces — walls, floors, ceilings, non-product-contact and product-contact surfaces of equipment — in the manufacturing area.

6.4.1 Monitoring Considerations

When periodic surface monitoring is performed, it should be done after the filling operation or a campaign has been completed so no extraneous contamination or residual growth media are introduced during filling activities. The basic methods provide surface monitoring data (qualitative or quantitative) that can be used to assess environmental quality.

Neutralizers are typically added to media to inactivate chemical disinfectant residues on surfaces. Recovery levels of surface-monitoring methods are typically low due to variability of surfaces, sampling procedures, and growth-based techniques.

6.4.1.1 Contact Plates

Contact plates are easy to use and can provide quantitative results (CFU per surface area). The plate is pressed against a flat surface by gently rolling from front to back. Prior to use, media and incubation conditions must be qualified.

The use of irradiated plates in ISO 5 areas is required and is recommended in ISO 7 and ISO 8 areas. Contact plates must be allowed to acclimate to room temperature before use. Contact plates are not suitable for irregular surfaces; swabs are recommended for that situation. Media residue must be removed after sampling.

6.4.1.2 Flexible Films

A flexible film containing media can be used similarly to a contact plate or to provide a defined sampling area. The film is pressed against a flat surface in a rolling motion. Like contact plates, flexible films leave a media residue that must be removed post-sampling.

6.4.1.3 Swabs

Swabs are employed for equipment and irregular surfaces not suitable for contact plates or flexible films. Swabbing may also be performed on flat surfaces using a template of approximately 2 in × 2 in (~25 cm²). Swabs are composed of Dacron® or nylon in flocked or spun format. Prior to plating, each swab is vortexed in an appropriate diluent (e.g., sodium chloride, phosphate buffer, or peptone solutions with neutralizers). Swabs may provide qualitative or quantitative results depending on how they are processed.

6.4.1.4 Surface Rinse Method

The surface rinse method is best used when a large surface area sample is required (e.g., kettles, equipment, trains, tanks). A sterile diluent is used as the rinsing fluid, collected from the interior surface, and tested by membrane filtration because of the large sample volume. The holding time of rinsing samples prior to plating should be qualified.

中文解析：四種表面監控方法比較

表面監控的共同原則

  • 監控時機：必須在填充操作結束後進行，避免生長培養基污染產品
  • 培養基內須加入中和劑（neutralizers）以抵銷消毒劑殘留的抑制效應
  • 回收率普遍偏低（受表面材質、操作手法、分析方法影響），但一致的採樣技術能產生可趨勢分析的數據
  • 趨勢分析揭示清潔消毒程序的有效性及製造活動對環境的衝擊

ISO 5 區域特別要求

在 ISO 5（Grade A）區域使用接觸盤時，必須使用輻照（irradiated）接觸盤，ISO 7 和 ISO 8 區域亦建議使用。原因：輻照盤本身已滅菌，降低因接觸盤引入外來污染的風險。

6.5 Personnel Considerations

Personnel are the primary source of contamination, which is why all cleanroom personnel (including maintenance workers and others who enter periodically) must be carefully trained and qualified before entering an aseptic environment.

The qualification training should include:

  • Attention to personal hygiene
  • Basic microbiology education or background
  • Aseptic techniques
  • Appropriate cleanroom behavior
  • Patient safety hazards posed by a contaminated product
  • Gowning certification
  • Participation in aseptic processing simulation (APS or media fill) activities, where applicable

After initial training, personnel should participate regularly in an ongoing training and monitoring program. Routine microbiological monitoring of personnel garments and gloves should also be completed. Supervisory personnel should routinely evaluate each operator's conformance to written procedures. The quality unit should provide regular oversight.

6.5.1 Training and Certification of Personnel for Aseptic Manufacturing Areas

All training and certification activities should be documented and kept as part of the employee file.

6.5.2 Causes for Requalification or Retraining

Companies should have a policy stating when requalification or retraining is necessary, for example:

  • Retraining on general aseptic practices and techniques should be provided at least annually
  • Recertification should be required after extended absences (e.g., for illness or family leave)
  • Recertification and/or retraining should be considered as corrective action for trends in personnel monitoring excursions

中文解析：人員管控是 EM 的核心

人員是潔淨室最大的「污染製造機」

TR13 明確指出人員是主要污染來源（primary source of contamination）。這不是一個理論假設，而是無數污染事件的共同根因。

想像潔淨室就像醫院開刀房：不論設備多先進，只要有一位手術人員疏忽了無菌操作，感染風險就立即升高。因此，所有進入潔淨室的人員——包括偶爾進入的維護人員——都必須先完成訓練與認證。

人員訓練的七大要素

  1. 個人衛生習慣
  2. 基礎微生物學教育
  3. 無菌操作技術
  4. 適當的潔淨室行為規範
  5. 污染產品對病患安全的危害認知
  6. 潔淨服穿著認證（Gowning Certification）
  7. 參與無菌製程模擬（APS / Media Fill）

所有訓練與認證記錄必須存入員工個人檔案。

再認證觸發條件

公司必須制定明確政策，規定以下情況需重新認證或再訓練：

  • 至少每年一次：無菌操作技術複訓
  • 長期缺勤後（病假、育嬰假等）：返崗前重新認證
  • 人員監控超標趨勢：作為糾正措施（CAPA）的一部分

第三點特別重要：人員監控數據若呈現上升趨勢，再訓練是最直接的糾正行動之一。

6.6 Environmental Monitoring During Product Sterility Testing

Sterility-testing facilities should be monitored to demonstrate the absence of microbial contamination. Routine types of monitoring conducted are:

  • Viable air monitoring
  • Surface monitoring (including isolator gloves if applicable)
  • Personnel monitoring

Total airborne particulate monitoring is not mandatory in sterility testing environments; however, it may provide useful information regarding the HEPA filters in the ISO 5 testing environment or the integrity of isolators. Lab materials may generate particles that are not representative of actual manufacturing conditions, and there is no risk to marketed product in the testing environment.

中文解析：無菌試驗環境的監控特殊性

為何無菌試驗區微粒監控非強制？

無菌試驗實驗室的目的是確認產品是否無菌，而非製造產品，因此：

  • 實驗室使用的試劑、培養基等材料本身可能產生大量微粒，但這些微粒不代表實際製造污染
  • 即使微粒計數偏高，也不會對市售產品造成直接風險
  • 因此，總微粒監控雖然非強制，但仍有助於評估 HEPA 過濾器效能或隔離器完整性

活性微粒監控（viable air）、表面監控（含隔離器手套）及人員監控仍為必要項目，確保試驗結果的有效性不受環境污染影響。

6.7 Environmental Monitoring in Grade A Air Supply

The term "Grade A air supply" is introduced in Annex 1 and is specifically used to support the capping of fully stoppered vials until the cap has been crimped — providing additional protection without the requirement to meet all criteria related to a Grade A area.

Grade A air supply provides air passed through a filter qualified as capable of producing Grade A total particulate quality air, but where there is no requirement to perform continuous total particulate monitoring or meet Grade A viable monitoring limits, and the area itself is not classified.

As Grade A Air Supply is not a clean room category (e.g., according to ISO 14644), no guidelines for viable and total particulate limits exist. In order to confirm Grade A total particulate quality air is supplied, testing at the point of supply should be performed as a minimum during qualification and requalification.

At-rest viable (air and surfaces) and total particulate monitoring may be performed based upon a risk assessment. Routine monitoring, limits, and positioning should reflect the requirements for this "clean process" and be aligned with the contamination control strategy, meeting criteria applied for the background of the Grade A Air supply (usually Grade C, but minimum Grade D).

中文解析：Annex 1 新概念——Grade A Air Supply

什麼是 Grade A Air Supply？

EU Annex 1（2022年修訂版）引入了「Grade A Air Supply」概念，這是一個功能性概念而非分類等級。其核心用途：在已完全加塞（fully stoppered）的小瓶上軋蓋（crimping）期間提供額外的空氣品質保護。

關鍵區別：

  • Grade A Area（區域）：需連續微粒監控 + 活性微粒限值 + 空間分類
  • Grade A Air Supply（氣源）：空氣品質達 Grade A 微粒標準，但不需連續監控、不設活性限值、空間本身不分類

背景環境通常為 Grade C，最低需達 Grade D。

監控要求最低標準

Grade A Air Supply 的最低確認要求：在資格確認（qualification）及再確認（requalification）期間，於空氣供應點進行測試，確認能達到 Grade A 總微粒品質。

靜態（at-rest）活性微粒與總微粒監控可基於風險評估執行，但不強制作為常規監控項目。這使製造商在合規設計上有更大彈性，同時不降低污染控制的有效性。

6.8 Water

This document addresses only the process monitoring of water for microbial attributes. Most countries have compendial requirements for evaluation of water quality. The recently published EMA Guideline for the Quality of Water for Pharmaceutical Use should be consulted as to the type of pharmaceutical-grade water used in manufacturing.

The type of water selected for manufacturing is based on the type of product and process. Water quality should be evaluated for microbial, bacterial endotoxin, and chemical attributes in accordance with applicable compendial or regulatory requirements. Water quality is critical for the manufacturing of parenteral drugs and selected devices because it is used in product formulation, component and equipment washing, and final rinsing.

The quality of laboratory water should meet the needs of analytical methods and technologies, which may require compendial-quality laboratory water.

製藥用水的三個層次

  • 注射用水（WFI, Water for Injection）：最高品質要求，用於注射劑配方、設備最終沖洗。細菌內毒素 < 0.25 EU/mL，微生物限值 NMT 10 CFU/100 mL。

  • USP：允許蒸餾法或同等/更優製程（如膜過濾）製備 WFI；EP 傳統上僅允許蒸餾法（2017年後 EP 10 也開放膜過濾）
  • EP 和 ChP 設有 pH 限制（5.0–7.0），USP 不設 pH 要求
  • EP 設有硝酸鹽（0.2 ppm）、亞硝酸鹽（0.02 ppm）、氨（0.2 ppm）、重金屬（0.1 ppm）限值；USP 均為 N/A
  • ChP 硝酸鹽限值（0.06 ppm）較 EP 更嚴格

台灣廠商若同時出口美國（FDA）與歐洲（EMA），需滿足兩者要求中較嚴格的一方。

6.8.1 Sample Site Selection and Frequency of Monitoring

The sampling and frequency of monitoring for water systems should be sufficient to ensure that water quality meets its intended use specifications and appropriate regulatory requirements. The frequency depends on the type of water, manufacturing process, system configuration, and facility design.

High-purity water systems should be validated to demonstrate that design requirements are consistently met and maintain a state of control. Site selection and frequency determination should be justified by thorough review of water system validation and risk-based approaches.

Once a water system is validated in a state of control, appropriate samples should be taken from:

  • Water source or generation system
  • Holding and storage tanks
  • Distribution system (all point-of-use sites for high-purity water systems)

Initial testing of water systems typically includes sampling after each process step.

6.8.2 Sample Collection and Testing

Water samples should be collected in a manner consistent with manufacturing practices. If manufacturing flushes use points prior to use, samples should be collected with the same flush cycle. All sample sites should be in a good state of repair and fully functional.

It is recommended to not use IPA on gloves or sample port prior to TOC sampling to avoid generating false positives. Microbiological examination of water should be initiated as soon as possible after sample collection. If immediate processing is not possible, samples should be refrigerated at 2–8°C. The time elapsed between collection and examination should be justified.

中文解析：用水取樣策略與實驗室操作

取樣策略的三個核心原則

  1. 代表性：取樣點需涵蓋水源、儲槽、分配系統全路徑。高純度水系統（WFI）需涵蓋所有使用點（point-of-use）。
  2. 與製造實務一致：若製造操作前會沖洗水管，取樣也應按相同沖洗步驟進行。反之亦然。
  3. 風險導向頻率設定：頻率需足以偵測化學屬性、微生物菌相及內毒素的趨勢變化。

取樣操作重要細節

  • TOC 取樣前不得使用 IPA：手套或取樣口的酒精殘留會造成 TOC 假陽性，導致不必要的調查。
  • 儘快處理樣品：若無法立即檢測，應冷藏於 2–8°C，並對從採樣到檢測的時間間隔進行驗證。
  • 潔淨蒸汽不做微生物測試：高溫蒸汽環境不支持微生物生長，僅做化學與 LAL 測試即可。

各藥典培養基與培養條件差異

R2A 培養基（Reasoner's 2A）是低營養培養基，特別適合生長緩慢、對高溫敏感的水中貧營養菌（oligotrophs），更能反映實際水質微生物組成。

6.9 Compressed Gas Monitoring

The use of compressed air and compressed gas in the aseptic ISO 5 environment may adversely affect environmental conditions if appropriate precautions, routine testing, and critical assurances are not developed into a system.

Using a 0.2 μm filter on the compressed gas line prior to entry into the cleanroom is common practice. Integrity testing of the filter and sanitization of the line post-filtration may reduce the need for sampling.

The sampling equipment must be compatible for use with compressed gases. A pre-filter may be installed in the system to reduce the bioburden to the point-of-use filter, extending its life and reducing the possibility of higher contamination reaching the area.

The particulate or bioburden level of any gas or air expelled into the environment should be equal to or better than the classification level of the room.

中文解析：壓縮氣體監控的關鍵控制點

壓縮氣體的污染風險

壓縮空氣與壓縮氣體（如氮氣、CO₂）直接噴入 ISO 5 無菌區域，若未適當過濾，可能成為微粒與微生物的引入途徑。

標準防護措施：

  • 進入潔淨室前安裝 0.2 μm 過濾器
  • 定期進行過濾器完整性測試（integrity testing）
  • 過濾後管路進行消毒（sanitization）
  • 安裝前置過濾器（pre-filter）降低最終過濾器的生物負載，延長使用壽命

核心法規要求

噴入環境的氣體或空氣，其微粒或生物負載水準必須等於或優於該房間的分類等級。例如在 ISO 5 區域使用的壓縮氣體，排放品質需達 ISO 5 或更優。

採樣設備必須與壓縮氣體相容——某些活性空氣採樣器可透過轉接頭同時適用於壓縮氣體取樣。
