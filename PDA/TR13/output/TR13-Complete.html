1.0 Introduction

The design and implementation of an environmental monitoring (EM) program allows the confirmation that controls to minimize the risk associated with processes, people, equipment, utilities, and facilities are designed correctly and continue to be effective in the manufacture of sterile pharmaceutical products. As such, it forms an important part of the contamination control strategy (CCS).

PDA first published Technical Report No. 13 in 1990 and revised it two previous times, 2001 and 2014. This third revision intends to align the content of the document with current industry trends and regulatory expectations. Specifically, it will provide additional guidance and focus on the:

  • Use of quality risk management (QRM) principles to establish a robust EM program by suggesting practical step-by-step approaches
  • Increased expectations concerning data management and data integrity
  • Prerequisites to an effective EM program
  • Qualification of controlled environments and how to maintain them in a qualified state
  • Rapid microbiological methods (RMM) or automated monitoring systems, since becoming more and more convenient, performant and accepted, or even recommended, by regulatory bodies

Members of the task force who developed this technical report represent or provide expertise to global companies to ensure that the methods, terminology, and practices reflect not only U.S., but also international procedures. Technical peer reviews were completed by prominent EM scientists.

Although this TR has been updated to ensure its alignment with existing current regulations and guidelines, current and/or newly updated regulations or guidelines should be consulted when implementing or changing an environmental monitoring program. This TR should be viewed as technical guidance; it is not intended to replace existing or future regulations, nor should it be considered an all-inclusive document.

1.1 Purpose

This document was created to aid in the establishment of an environmental monitoring program that is robust, meaningful, and practical whilst embracing innovation and the principles of quality risk management. This revision updates microbiological and total airborne particulate control concepts and principles as they relate to facilities involved in the manufacture of sterile pharmaceutical products and other designated cleanroom environments.

1.2 Scope

TR-13 serves as a resource applicable for controlled environments in general; however, its primary focus is environmental monitoring for sterile product manufacturing.

This document discusses international standards and regulatory guidances, elements of an EM program, and EM application. Current (at the time of publication) guidelines for typical EM levels and frequencies are presented in Section 3.0 and levels for pharmaceutical water are covered in Section 6.8.

A comprehensive environmental monitoring program should demonstrate the effectiveness of a solid CCS focusing on:

  • Sound facility design, including barrier systems (e.g., isolators and RABS), operation and maintenance
  • Established documentation systems
  • Qualified sanitization, disinfection, and decontamination procedures
  • Reliable process controls
  • Good housekeeping practices
  • Effective area access controls
  • Consistent sample collection and analysis
  • Effective training, certification or qualification, and personnel evaluation programs
  • Quality assurance and control of materials, facilities, and equipment
  • Continuous improvement and quality risk management programs

Bulk and intermediate product bioburden monitoring (except for pharmaceutical water) is not considered part of all EM programs and is therefore outside of the scope of this technical report. For more specifics on this topic, refer to PDA Technical Report No. 69.

解讀與應用

TR13 的核心定位

TR13 是 PDA 最經典、最廣泛被引用的環境監測指引。自 1990 年首版以來歷經三次改版（2001、2014、2022），是全球無菌製藥產業在建立 EM 程序時的重要參考文件。

2022 年修訂版特別強調與 EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 的對齊，Annex 1 首次要求企業建立書面的 CCS（污染控制策略），而 TR13 明確指出 EM 是 CCS 的重要組成部分。

2022 修訂的五大重點

相較於 2014 版，本次修訂新增或強化的方向：

  • QRM 實務化：不再只是「應用風險管理」的概念，而是提供 step-by-step 方法
  • 數據完整性：回應 FDA/EMA 近年對 Data Integrity 的高度關注
  • EM 先決條件：先確認設施、設備、人員合格，才能啟動 EM
  • 環境確效維持：不只做初始 qualification，還要維持 qualified state
  • RMM 快速方法：承認快速微生物方法已被法規接受，鼓勵採用

比喻：EM 就像工廠的「健康體檢」

環境監測就像定期為製造環境做「健康體檢」——量血壓（粒子數）、驗血（微生物採樣）、照 X 光（趨勢分析）。體檢本身不能治病，但能及早發現異常、預防「疾病」（產品污染）。

CCS 則是整套「預防醫學計畫」，而 EM 是其中的「定期檢查」環節。

Scope 的十大 CCS 聚焦要素

TR13 Section 1.2 列出 EM 程序需展現有效性的十個面向，這些要素構成了整份報告的骨架：

  • 設施設計 → Section 4（環境控制基礎）
  • 消毒程序 → 搭配 TR70 閱讀
  • 採樣與分析 → Section 5-6（採樣方法）
  • 趨勢分析 → Section 8（數據管理）
  • 人員訓練 → Section 4（人員行為要求）

範圍排除：Bioburden 監控

TR13 明確排除原料藥中間體的 bioburden 監控（製藥用水除外）。該主題由 PDA TR69（Bioburden and Biofilm Management）專門處理。在建立 EM 程序時需清楚界定邊界。

2.0 Glossary and Abbreviations

All key terms defined in TR13 Section 2.0. These definitions form the vocabulary foundation for the entire report.

Term Definition
Action Limit A microbial or total airborne particulate limit that, when exceeded, indicates a process is outside of its normal operating range. A response should involve a documented investigation and corrective actions based on the results of that investigation.
Airborne Particulate Count
(Total Airborne Particulate Count)		 The total number of particles of a specific size range per unit volume of air.
Airborne Viable Particulate Count
(Total Airborne Aerobic Microbial Count)		 The recovered number of colony-forming units or related signal per unit volume of air.
Alert Level An established value that when exceeded, provides an early warning of potential drift from normal operating conditions and validated state, which does not necessarily give grounds for corrective action but triggers appropriate scrutiny and follow-up to address the potential problem.
Aseptic Filling Part of aseptic processing in which a pre-sterilized or aseptically manufactured product is filled and sealed into sterile containers in a cleanroom, RABS, or isolator.
Aseptic Processing Handling of product, packaging materials, and/or processing equipment in a controlled environment in which the air supply, materials, equipment, and personnel are controlled to maintain (product) sterility.
Bioburden Viable microorganisms associated with personnel, manufacturing environments (air and surfaces), equipment, product packaging, raw materials (including water), in-process materials, or finished products.
Campaign A series of consecutive production batches manufactured without or with reduced intervening cleaning, disinfection, and/or sterilization.
Cleaning Chemical or physical means used to remove soil and/or microorganisms from surfaces.
Cleanroom A room designed, maintained, and controlled to prevent particle and microbiological contamination of a drug product or medical device. A cleanroom is assigned and reproducibly meets an appropriate air cleanliness classification.
Colony-Forming Unit
(CFU)		 A single macroscopic colony formed after the growth of one or more microorganisms on a solid microbiological growth medium.
Contamination Control Strategy
(CCS)		 A holistic, risk-based, planned, and documented approach based on product and process understanding, which explains all the elements that need to be controlled and monitored so microbial, particulate, and pyrogen contamination is minimized to ensure process performance and product quality. The CCS should allow for continuous improvement measures to be taken.
Continuous Monitoring A process of data collection in which conditions are monitored continuously throughout the operation. In most applications, this definition implies "during production."
Controlled Non-Classified Area
(CNC)		 An area that does not meet any of the formal classified grades of cleanliness (i.e., grades A to D), but where a manufacturer-defined level of microbial control is still maintained. (May also be referred to as Pharmaceutically Controlled Clean Area (PCCA).)
Corrective Action Action to eliminate the cause of a nonconformity and to prevent recurrence.
Critical Area / Critical Zone An area designed to maintain sterility of materials where sterilized product, containers, closures, and equipment may be exposed to the manufacturing environment.
Critical Surface A surface within a critical area that may come in direct or indirect contact with sterilized or sterile filtered products, containers, or closures.
Decontamination The overall process of removal or reduction of any contaminants (chemical residue, or microorganisms) from an area, processing equipment, or person. The method of decontamination used (e.g., cleaning, disinfection, sterilization) should be chosen and validated to achieve a level of cleanliness appropriate to the intended use.
Disinfection The chemical or physical inactivation of a bioburden on inanimate surfaces.
Grade A Air Supply Air which is passed through a filter qualified as capable of producing Grade A total particulate quality air, but where there is no requirement to perform continuous total particulate monitoring or meet Grade A viable monitoring limits and the area itself is not classified.
Grid Profiling A process of dividing areas of equivalent classifications into grids for the purpose of uniformly assessing contamination characteristics in that area.
Isolator A contained, decontaminated environment meeting Grade A (ISO 5) conditions used for aseptic process manufacturing that provides uncompromised, continuous isolation of its interior from the external environment including personnel. Subtypes: Closed Isolator (sealed throughout operations) and Open Isolator (allows continuous or semi-continuous ingress/egress of materials through engineered openings).
Manufacturing Control Parameters Conditions and corresponding measurements associated with facilities and equipment used in the control of a manufacturing process and/or area that may impact safety, quality, identity, potency, or purity of a product. Includes airflow rates, pressure differentials, temperature, humidity, total airborne particulate, viable particulates, bioburden, process rate, weight, volume, and pressure.
Microbial Characterization The description of microorganisms based on their cellular morphology, Gram reaction, and key diagnostic tests (e.g., gram-positive coagulase-negative cocci).
Microbial Classification The arrangement of microorganisms into taxonomic groups based on their similarities and relationships.
Microbial Identification The determination of the genus, and species when possible, to which a laboratory or manufacturing isolate belongs.
Operational State
(At Rest / In Operation)		 The status of a classified room used as an indicator of the presence or absence of personnel and/or active manufacturing processes. At Rest: equipment installed, standing by, no personnel present (synonymous with "static"). In Operation: usual equipment operating, specified number of personnel present, process is ongoing (synonymous with "dynamic").
Parametric Release A sterility release program based on effective control, monitoring, and documentation of a validated sterile-product manufacturing process where sterility release is based on demonstrated achievement of critical operational parameters in lieu of end-product sterility testing.
Preventive Action Action to eliminate the cause of a potential nonconformity or other potential undesirable situation.
RABS
(Restricted Access Barrier System)		 Aseptic processing systems (typically ISO 5) intended to substantially reduce human-borne contamination within the aseptic environment via separative devices and defined mechanical features. Subtypes: Open RABS (vents allow air to move from RABS to surrounding area) and Closed RABS (air vented in return ducts within the cabinet).
Risk Analysis The estimation of the risk associated with the identified hazards.
Sanitization Process of reducing the number of all forms of microbial contamination including fungi, viruses, and bacteria.
Spore A bacterial dormant form that is highly resistant to adverse conditions. Fungal spores are not highly resistant; their susceptibilities are closer to vegetative microorganisms.
Sterilization Validated process used to render product, equipment, or primary packaging materials free from viable microorganisms.
Terminal Sterilization A process where the material is sterilized in its primary packaging/container configuration.
Total Airborne Particulates A term used in reference to the total number of particulates suspended in the air. Airborne particulates may be a complex mixture of viable and nonviable particulates, characterized by their physical attributes (e.g., concentration and size distribution).
Trend Analysis A review performed in response to an alert or action condition or as part of periodic data review. This review provides an analysis of specific EM data to identify adverse trends (e.g., increase in counts, repetitive organism recoveries, or multiple counts in the same room).
Type Strain A specific isolate of a species maintained in pure culture, serotypically, genotypically, or chemotaxonomically characterized to differentiate it from other strains of the same species. Representative of the species and provides a reference based on its historic isolation, characterization, and deposition in recognized culture collections.
Vegetative Cell Cells in an actively growing state. Some microorganisms can only be vegetative, while others are spore-formers and can be in a vegetative or spore (dormant) state.

關鍵術語解析

Action Limit vs. Alert Level — 最常被問的區別

這是稽核時幾乎必問的題目，也是 EM 數據管理的基石：

  • Alert Level（警戒水準）：早期預警，超過時需要「關注與追蹤」，但不一定需要矯正措施。類似儀表板上的黃燈。
  • Action Limit（行動限度）：超過代表製程已偏離正常範圍，必須啟動調查與矯正措施。類似紅燈。

兩者都由歷史數據統計設定，而非憑經驗決定。EU GMP Annex 1 Section 9.27-9.30 對此有明確要求。

比喻：血壓的「警戒值」與「行動值」

把 Alert Level 想成健檢報告中「偏高，建議追蹤」的血壓值（如 130/85 mmHg）——醫生會記錄但不一定開藥。Action Limit 則像「高血壓確診」（如 140/90 mmHg）——必須用藥並回診追蹤。

EM 也是同樣邏輯：Alert 觸發關注，Action 觸發行動。

CCS（污染控制策略）— 2022 版最重要的新概念

EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 首次將 CCS 列為強制要求（Section 2.4-2.7）。TR13 將 EM 定位為 CCS 的關鍵組成部分：

  • CCS = 全面性的污染控制規劃文件
  • EM = CCS 中「驗證控制措施持續有效」的工具
  • CCS 涵蓋設施設計、人員控制、消毒、製程控制等
  • EM 數據提供 CCS 有效性的客觀證據

延伸閱讀：PDA TR90（CCS Development in Pharmaceutical Manufacturing）提供更完整的 CCS 建立指引。

Isolator vs. RABS — 屏障技術的選擇

TR13 定義了兩種主要屏障系統，兩者都是 Grade A/ISO 5 環境：

  • Isolator：完全封閉式隔離，人員無法直接接觸內部。提供最高等級的無菌保證。進一步分為 Closed（全密封）與 Open（有工程化開口）。
  • RABS：限制進入式屏障，有物理隔離但不如 Isolator 完全封閉。分為 Open RABS（有通風口連接背景環境）與 Closed RABS（空氣在機櫃內循環）。

選擇哪種技術直接影響 EM 的監測策略、採樣點配置和限度設定。

微生物鑑定的三個層次

TR13 區分了三個不同深度的微生物分析：

  • Characterization（特徵描述）：形態學 + 革蘭氏染色 + 關鍵診斷測試。例：「革蘭氏陽性凝固酶陰性球菌」
  • Classification（分類學分類）：根據相似性歸入分類群
  • Identification（鑑定）：確定屬名與種名（如 Staphylococcus epidermidis）

不同環境等級對鑑定深度的要求不同——Grade A/B 通常需要完整 Identification，Grade C/D 可能 Characterization 即可。

At Rest vs. In Operation — 為什麼要分兩種狀態？

「At Rest」是環境本身的基線能力——設備裝好但沒人沒生產，像是餐廳開門前的廚房清潔度。「In Operation」是實際運作時的表現——有人走動、有物料流動，像是午餐尖峰時段的廚房。

EU GMP Annex 1 Table 3-4 對兩種狀態分別設定了不同的粒子限度。At Rest 限度更嚴格，用於驗證 HVAC 系統能力；In Operation 限度反映實際製造條件。

縮寫速查與補充

培養基縮寫 — 微生物實驗室必備知識

  • TSA / SCDA：大豆胰蛋白酶瓊脂培養基，用於總好氧菌計數（細菌），培養溫度通常 30-35°C
  • SDA：沙布羅葡萄糖瓊脂培養基，專門用於真菌（黴菌和酵母菌）計數，培養溫度通常 20-25°C

環境監測時通常需要同時使用 TSA 和 SDA 兩種培養基，分別計數細菌和真菌。

VBNC — 被忽略的微生物風險

Viable But Not Culturable（可存活但不可培養）是近年微生物學的熱門議題。部分微生物在壓力條件下（如消毒劑、低營養）會進入 VBNC 狀態——它們仍然活著但無法在傳統培養基上生長。

這意味著傳統的 CFU 計數可能低估實際微生物數量。TR13 提到 VBNC，暗示未來 RMM（快速微生物方法）可能成為更可靠的替代方案。

風險管理工具：FMEA vs. HACCP

  • FMEA（失效模式與效應分析）：系統性評估每個製程步驟可能的失效模式、原因與影響，並以 RPN（風險優先數）排序。常用於 EM 採樣點選擇。
  • HACCP（危害分析關鍵控制點）：源自食品工業，識別製程中的關鍵控制點。在 EM 中用於決定哪些位置和時間點是最高風險的監測點。

兩者都是 ICH Q9 品質風險管理中推薦的工具，TR13 鼓勵在 EM 程序設計中活用。

CRR（污染回收率）— 為何重要？

CRR 表示採樣方法實際能回收到的微生物比例。如果一個採樣方法的 CRR 只有 50%，意味著表面有 100 CFU 時你只能偵測到 50 CFU。

了解 CRR 的限制，才能正確解讀 EM 數據——低 CFU 讀數不一定代表環境乾淨，也可能是回收效率低。

Section 2 總結 Summary

核心概念 Key Concepts

  • TR13 (2022) 是 PDA 環境監測最權威的實務指引，歷經四個版本演進
  • EM 是 CCS（污染控制策略）的關鍵組成部分，用於驗證控制措施的持續有效性
  • Action Limit 與 Alert Level 構成 EM 數據回應的兩層機制
  • CCS 概念由 EU GMP Annex 1 (2022) 強制要求，TR13 與之緊密對齊
  • Isolator 與 RABS 是兩種主要屏障技術，影響整體 EM 監測策略

術語重點 Key Terms

  • CCS：全面性污染控制規劃文件，EM 為其中一環
  • Alert vs. Action：黃燈（關注追蹤）vs. 紅燈（調查矯正）
  • At Rest vs. In Operation：靜態基線能力 vs. 動態實際表現
  • Characterization → Classification → Identification：微生物鑑定三層次
  • VBNC：傳統培養法的盲點，RMM 可彌補
  • TSA/SCDA（細菌）vs. SDA（真菌）：兩類核心培養基

思考練習 Practice Questions

  1. 您的工廠尚未建立正式的 CCS 文件。根據 TR13 Section 2.0 的 CCS 定義，請列出一份有效 CCS 文件應涵蓋的核心要素。
  2. 假設 Grade B 背景環境某採樣點連續三次超過 Alert Level 但未達 Action Limit。根據 TR13 的定義，您應該採取什麼行動？與超過 Action Limit 時的回應有何不同？
  3. 您的實驗室目前使用 TSA 進行所有環境監測。一位微生物學家建議同時使用 SDA。根據 TR13 的術語定義，請解釋為什麼這個建議合理。
  4. 比較 Open RABS 與 Closed Isolator 的定義差異。在 EM 採樣策略上，這兩種屏障系統可能帶來哪些不同的監測需求？
  5. TR13 提到 VBNC（Viable But Not Culturable）微生物。這對傳統以 CFU 為基礎的 EM 程序有什麼潛在影響？RMM（快速微生物方法）如何幫助解決這個問題？

3.0 Environmental Classifications: Regulatory Expectations

Overview — Regulatory Bodies Covered

An EM program should be designed and implemented in conformance with the requirements of the government agencies regulating the manufacturing site as well as requirements of international standard-setting organizations. If the intent is to serve multinational markets, the most stringent requirements should be evaluated as the basis of an EM program.

This section compares regulations and guidance instituted for EM by these authorities:

  • International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
  • U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA)
  • U.S. Pharmacopeia (USP)
  • European Pharmacopoeia (Ph. Eur.)
  • European Medicines Agency (EMA)
  • Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare of Japan (MHLW)
  • Japanese Pharmacopoeia (JP)
  • Chinese Pharmacopeia (ChP)
  • Pharmaceutical Inspection Convention (PIC) and Pharmaceutical Inspection Co-operation Scheme (PIC/S)
  • World Health Organization (WHO)

Although the regulations and guidelines of these organizations are similar in many respects, there are important differences among them in terms of the information each provides, particularly with respect to cleanroom classifications.

中文導讀：法規總覽

本節是 TR13 最核心的法規比較章節。對於在台灣或亞洲從事無菌製造的藥廠，通常需要同時符合多個法規體系的要求（例如：出口歐洲需符合 EU Annex 1，出口美國需符合 FDA Guidance，出口日本需符合 MHLW 指引）。

關鍵原則：若產品銷售至多國市場，EM 計畫應以「最嚴格的法規要求」為設計基礎。這意味著在建立監測計畫時，必須交叉比對所有目標市場的法規。

TR13 將十個主要法規/標準機構的要求濃縮於兩張比較表中，這是全球藥廠 EM 人員的實務工具。

實務提醒

中國（ChP）對非無菌產品亦要求 ISO 8 潔淨室（靜態），這比多數其他法規更為嚴格。EU GMP 僅對吸入劑（inhalants）之非無菌製造要求 ISO 8。在設計 EM 計畫時，應特別注意這類差異。

ISO 14644-1 Classifications

The most commonly accepted international cleanroom standard related to total airborne particulates is ISO 14644-1:2015 Cleanrooms and Associated Controlled Environments — Part 1: Classification of Air Cleanliness by Particle Concentration.

ISO class designations are based on the number of particles greater than a specified size (0.1–5 μm) per cubic meter of air sampled. ISO 14644-1 defines classes from 1 to 9, with ISO 1 being the cleanest. ISO classes 5 through 8 are used in the pharmaceutical industry for sterile-product manufacture.

ISO does not specify:

  • The operating state (as built, at rest, or in operation)
  • The particle-size thresholds to be employed
  • Distinction between viable and nonviable particles

The following activities are generally expected in each ISO class:

  • ISO 5: Aseptic processing zone; sterile product and/or packaging component exposed; unidirectional airflow required
  • ISO 7: Area immediately surrounding the aseptic processing zone
  • ISO 8: Non-sterile formulation, materials, and component preparation; filling area for terminally sterilized product

中文導讀：ISO 分級與實務應用

ISO 14644-1 是全球潔淨室分級的「共通語言」，但它只是一個框架 — 它告訴你「怎麼量」和「數字上限是多少」，卻不告訴你：

  • 要在哪種狀態（靜態/動態）下測量
  • 要測哪個粒徑（0.5 μm 還是 5 μm）
  • 粒子是否有活性（viable vs. nonviable）

這些細節由各國法規自行補充。這就是為什麼同為 ISO 5，FDA 和 EU 的具體要求可以不同。

ISO vs. EU Grade 對照關係

EU/PIC/S/WHO 使用 Grade A–D 字母分級，每個 Grade 對應靜態（at rest）與動態（in operation）兩種 ISO 等級：

  • Grade A = ISO 5 at rest & in operation（以 ≥5 μm 計為 ISO 4.8）
  • Grade B = ISO 5 at rest, ISO 7 in operation
  • Grade C = ISO 7 at rest, ISO 8 in operation
  • Grade D = ISO 8 at rest, 動態未定義

這意味著 Grade B 的潔淨室，在沒有操作人員時必須達到 ISO 5（和 Grade A 一樣乾淨！），但在有人操作時只需維持 ISO 7。

EU, PIC/S, WHO & Japan Classifications

The EU, PIC/S members, and WHO use an alphabetic classification of Grades A–D. For each grade, a ≥0.5 μm and ≥5 μm particle-count limit is specified. These limits are set for both at-rest and in-operation states.

Japan has adopted similar classifications in its Guidance on the Manufacture of Sterile Pharmaceutical Products by Aseptic Processing. One minor difference: Japan refers to the Grade A classification as ISO 5 rather than ISO 4.8, although the limit for particles ≥5 μm technically corresponds to ISO 4.8.

For the United States, the FDA’s 2004 Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing and USP <1116> both discuss the application and environmental requirements for cleanrooms. These include ISO classes 5–8 and their corresponding Federal Standard 209E classes (e.g., ISO 5/Class 100). Limits are set only for particles ≥0.5 μm. Both are silent on the state of the area to be tested but clearly imply areas to be "in operation."

Grade A = ISO 5 還是 ISO 4.8？

EU Annex 1 對 Grade A 的 ≥5 μm 粒子限值為 20 個/m³（表中為 29 particles/m³ at rest for Grade B），這個限值嚴格來說對應 ISO 4.8 而非 ISO 5。但日本 MHLW 直接稱之為 ISO 5。此差異在實務上不影響設備設計，但在法規文件撰寫時需注意用語。

美國 FDA/USP 的特殊之處：

  • 只規定 ≥0.5 μm 的粒子限值，不要求測量 ≥5 μm
  • 未明確定義測試狀態（at rest / in operation），但暗示為動態
  • 仍保留舊制 Federal Standard 209E 的對照（如 Class 100 = ISO 5）

EU/PIC/S 的特殊之處：

  • 同時規定 ≥0.5 μm 和 ≥5 μm 兩個粒徑
  • 對每個 Grade 分別定義靜態和動態的限值
  • Grade D 的動態限值未被定義（由操作決定）

USP <1116> 的獨特方法：污染回收率 (CRR)

所有其他法規都使用「每次取樣的 CFU 上限」作為行動限值，但 USP <1116> 採用統計方法：

  • ISO 5：少於 1% 的樣品有微生物污染
  • ISO 7：少於 5% 的樣品有微生物污染
  • ISO 8：少於 10% 的樣品有微生物污染

這種方法的優勢在於：它反映了整體趨勢而非單一事件。但由於其他法規仍使用 CFU 限值，多數藥廠在實務中同時採用兩種評估方式。

監測頻率的法規差異：

  • Grade A/ISO 5：所有法規一致要求每班次（each shift）監測
  • Grade C, D：EU 採用風險基礎方法（risk-based），日本 MHLW 則給出明確頻率（2 次/週或 1 次/週），JP XVI 的要求與 MHLW 相同
  • 隔離器（Isolator）：USP <1116> 對隔離器有獨立的頻率建議（關鍵區域每天 1 次，周圍區域每月 1 次）

表面與人員監測的空白區：

  • FDA 不規定表面限值 — 這是一個明顯的差距
  • 所有法規對 Gown（防護衣）的限值都未明確規定數值
  • Glove（手套）限值僅針對 Grade A 和 B，Grade C/D 不要求

Airflow & Pressure Differentials

Common factors among the various guidelines include the requirement that ISO 5 areas, where aseptic conditions must be maintained, typically require unidirectional airflow.

ISO 14644-1 describes unidirectional flow as 0.45 meters per second (90 ft/min) ±20% measured 150–300 mm from the supply filter face. EU Annex 1, though, recommends this measurement be taken as close to the work surface as practical.

All regulatory authorities recommend an air pressurization scheme to ensure airflow from the cleaner zone to the less-clean zone. The broadly acceptable guidance value is a differential pressure (ΔP) of 10–15 Pa (0.04–0.06 in H2O) between zones of differing classes.

Several guidance documents describe the use of air locks to maintain differential pressures while doors are used between different Grades. Where an open ISO 5 unidirectional zone is placed within an ISO 7 background, this pressure differential is not required.

  • ISO 5 區域必須使用單向氣流

  • 各級別潔淨室之間需維持 10–15 Pa 壓差

工廠實務情境

想像一間無菌充填工廠：

  • 充填線上方的 LAF（ISO 5 / Grade A）：風速 0.45 m/s 的單向氣流如同「空氣簾幕」，將潔淨空氣直吹到產品暴露區，阻隔外部微粒進入。

Section 3.0 Summary — 本節重點回顧

Key Regulatory Differences

  • ISO 14644-1 provides the classification framework; each regulator adds its own requirements for operating state, particle sizes, and viable limits.
  • EU/PIC/S/WHO use Grade A–D with both at-rest and in-operation limits; FDA/USP use ISO classes with implied in-operation testing only.
  • FDA/USP specify only ≥0.5 μm particles; EU/Japan also specify ≥5.0 μm.
  • USP <1116> uniquely uses Contamination Recovery Rate instead of CFU limits.

實務要點

  • 多國市場銷售應以最嚴格法規為 EM 設計基礎。
  • Grade A = ISO 5（at rest & in operation），Grade B = ISO 5 at rest / ISO 7 in operation。
  • ISO 5 區域：單向氣流 0.45 m/s ±20%，各級別間壓差 10–15 Pa。
  • FDA 不規定表面限值與人員限值的具體數字，但 EU/Japan 有明確數值。
  • 中國 ChP 對非無菌產品亦要求 ISO 8（靜態），需特別注意。

4.0 Environmental Monitoring Qualification of Cleanrooms

Cleanroom Classification & Initial Qualification

Clean areas for the manufacture of sterile products are classified according to the required characteristics of the environment. Each manufacturing operation requires an appropriate environmental cleanliness level in the operational state to minimize the risks of particulate or microbial contamination of the product or materials being handled.

For new areas, the initial qualification should be performed in a prospective mode. For existing facilities that require a repeat of the initial qualification, the requalification can be performed in concurrence with routine production activities if historical data of existing rooms are shown to be in a good state of control. Release of product should await final release of the cleanroom qualification exercise.

To meet the in-operation conditions, these areas should be designed to reach certain specified air-cleanliness levels in the "at rest" occupancy state. Section 9.0 (Appendix 1) provides additional guidance to ensure and verify that critical system components meet user requirements and are properly identified, installed, and calibrated (typically covered within the installation qualification (IQ) and operational qualification (OQ) phases of cleanroom qualification).

To prepare the cleanroom for classification, air filters should be evaluated for cleanliness and replaced, if necessary, especially in new or modified facilities where excess construction debris or dust may cause clogging. Cleaning and disinfection protocols should be defined and performed prior to conducting qualification.

IQ / OQ Phases & At-Rest vs In-Operation

At-Rest & In-Operation Testing

Performance of the total airborne particulate cleanroom classification described in ISO 14644-1 would confirm the air-cleanliness levels in the at-rest occupancy state. This initial exercise should be supplemented by an at-rest qualification using viable tests.

Viable at-rest locations might focus on more specific aspects related to the actual design of the cleanroom and can be documented as part of the EM risk assessment (see Section 5.2). Although human activity and material transfers (the primary sources of microbial contamination) are not evaluated in the at-rest state, the associated sampling locations will provide valuable information about the intrinsic microbial state-of-control of the cleanroom prior to starting the qualification under in-operation conditions.

Specifications applied for viable qualification at rest might be more stringent than those applicable for in-operation conditions. All necessary measures should be taken to minimize the impact of personnel during at-rest monitoring (e.g., surface monitoring at the start of the run, only EM technician present, delay timers on automated samplers).

Subsequently, it should be confirmed that the cleanroom can maintain operational conditions when executing the activities foreseen within the area. The method detailed in ISO 14644-1 can be used to confirm the total airborne particulate cleanroom classification for the in-operation status. This initial qualification is normally performed by simulating manufacturing activities, ensuring that at a certain stage the maximum number of personnel and the worst-case level of activities are covered.

The runs described in this section may form part of the OQ of the cleanroom. Data obtained during this stage should be used as part of the EM risk assessment to define routine sampling locations. For both total airborne particulate runs (in the at-rest and in-operational conditions), one run conducted according to ISO 14644-1 is considered sufficient.

Performance Qualification (PQ) & Extended PQ

PQ Execution & Requirements

A performance qualification (PQ) should follow the successful completion of the OQ. However, it may commence before the full OQ results are available, based on preliminary results (business risk approach). If this approach is chosen, the PQ protocol should list the results which should be available before the PQ start.

PQ typically consists of three consecutive runs during routine (in-operation) conditions. In batch-related production rooms, a run would typically comprise a batch covering all applicable shifts and activities. In supporting areas, a run should cover all relevant activities to be performed within that area. Simulating activities during performance qualification might reduce the total runtime (i.e., by executing them in a condensed period of time).

The sampling plan should be based on the viable and total airborne particulate sample locations defined as part of the routine monitoring (EM risk assessment) augmented with additional EM locations to cover remote parts of the room in order to confirm and document that the complete cleanroom environment is in control. This PQ can be performed during APS or activities can be simulated.

There is a business risk, however, performing PQ during APS since the facility is not operating in a fully qualified environment and deficiencies could be detected that first require corrective measures. Consequently, at a certain stage, the maximum number of personnel and the worst-case level of activities need to be covered during one or more PQ runs. The maximum limits for microbial and total airborne particulate contamination during qualification should correspond to the action limits applied during routine monitoring.

Extended PQ

Following the initial PQ, an extended PQ may be conducted for a defined time period based on the routine sampling program or continuation of extended sampling. The intent of this process would be to account for conditions which may not have been covered in the initial PQ (e.g., seasonal variation of microbes, establishment of appropriate alert levels, selection of microorganisms for disinfectant efficacy tests, additional evaluation of the periodic cleaning program, etc.).

Production or room configuration changes with a possible impact on EM (e.g., gowning procedures, cleaning set-up, layout, material or personnel flow, or other areas) should include a new assessment on the qualified status of the cleanroom and may require requalification as part of the change.

PQ 的核心要求

  • 3 次連續運行（Three Consecutive Runs）——證明環境不是「偶爾」達標，而是「穩定」達標。
  • 批次相關房間：每次運行 = 一個完整批次，涵蓋所有班次和活動。
  • 輔助區域：每次運行須涵蓋該區所有相關活動。
  • 取樣點 = 例行監控點 + 額外點位（涵蓋房間偏遠角落）。

5.0 Environmental Monitoring Program

EM Program Fundamentals

An EM program (including EMPQ) should be in conformance with current GMP, which includes appropriate training and authority of personnel involved in implementing the EM program. Equipment used should be qualified and calibrated, and microbiological growth media should be properly qualified, prepared, and tested. In addition, all operational procedures should be written, approved, and followed with appropriate controls to support their use. The methods selected should be justified and qualified for use as appropriate.

Cleaning and disinfection of the manufacturing area, and material handling and gowning processes, as well as appropriate aseptic behavior of the personnel, are key components of an effective contamination control strategy.

Selection of sampling methods (see Section 6.0), sampling sites, and sampling frequencies should be based on the principles of risk management according to ICH Q9: Quality Risk Management to ensure that the respective production area is under control and meets regulatory requirements.

Establishment of alert levels and action limits may be based on individual sample sites or groups of related sample sites with a similar design or activities. Data obtained are subject to continual review, and alert and action decisions are made by qualified personnel. A documentation system should be in place for identifying and investigating excursions and adverse trends and the effectiveness of the corrective actions taken. All data should be documented and periodically trended.

EM 計畫總覽 / 中文解讀

  • 人員——經過適當培訓且有授權
  • 設備——已確認（qualified）且校正
  • 培養基——已確認、製備、測試
  • 程序——書面化、經核准、受控執行

5.1 Cleaning, Sanitization or Disinfection and Decontamination

Cleaning & Disinfection in the EM Context

EM data demonstrates the effectiveness of the cleaning and disinfection program. The qualification of cleaning and disinfection procedures should demonstrate microbial reduction and maintenance of a microbiological state of control and should provide confidence in the effectiveness of the procedure.

This typically includes laboratory surface challenge or carrier studies for contact time and bioburden reduction, supplemented by in-situ studies.

Both USP <1072> and PDA Technical Report No. 70: Fundamentals of Cleaning and Disinfection Programs for Aseptic Manufacturing Facilities provide sufficient details of the requirements and in-vitro surface challenge methodology for qualification of the efficacy of the agent used.

清潔消毒與 EM 的關係 / 解讀

Section 5.1 的核心訊息很簡單但深刻：EM 數據是驗證清潔消毒效果的最直接證據。

清潔消毒確認的雙重驗證

  • 實驗室挑戰測試（Laboratory Challenge）：在實驗室用已知菌種接種於表面，測試消毒劑的殺菌效力與接觸時間。
  • 現場驗證（In-situ Studies）：在實際製造環境中採樣，驗證清潔消毒程序在真實條件下的效果。

5.2 EM Sample Site Selection Using Risk Management Principles

Overview of Risk-Based Site Selection

Sample site selection, selection of the appropriate sampling methods, sampling volumes, and monitoring frequencies are critical components of contamination control within a facility. However, EM risk assessments are only intended to define the EM program as a part of the overall contamination control strategy.

This section addresses how to create and how to maintain an EM program applying a lifecycle approach (Section 5.2.1) and the use of quality risk management principles to identify and select sites for EM of classified environments including ISO 5-8/Grades A-D as well as personnel monitoring (Section 5.2.2). Sections 6.8 and 6.9 discuss utilities (WFI/Compressed Air, Nitrogen, etc.).

5.2.1 Lifecycle Approach to an Environmental Monitoring Program

Lifecycle Model for EM Programs

The creation and maintenance of EM programs are best achieved using a lifecycle approach. This lifecycle approach encompasses the evaluation and definition of suitable sample sites, sample types, sampling volumes, and sample frequency for microbiological monitoring that are required before starting up a new facility, area, or room.

These sample sites are used to perform an EM performance qualification (PQ). Based on the PQ results, adaptation (mainly reduction or addition) of sample sites may be necessary before setting up the sample plan for routine and, if applicable, batch-related EM.

The impact of any major change (e.g., reconstruction, process change, or equipment relocation) needs to be evaluated and the affected area may need to be requalified.

A final EM evaluation may be necessary for decommissioning a room, isolator, or facility to demonstrate environmental control through to the end.

The model presented in Figure 5.2.1-1 outlines the general flow of the lifecycle approach of an EM program. Each step defined is designed to ensure that once an EM program is developed, it is carefully reviewed during the lifecycle of the area, product, or process to affirm that a state of control is maintained. A periodic reevaluation of the EM program (e.g., in response to an adverse trend report) may be necessary.

5.2.2 Initiation of Quality Risk Management Process

Setting Up the Risk Assessment

Prior to executing a risk assessment, several elements must be defined: scope and objective, risk question or problem statement, risk management tool to be applied, and risk-ranking criteria.

To conduct an EM risk assessment, a risk facilitator should be assigned and a multidisciplinary team should be assembled representing the areas of microbiology, contamination control and hygiene, quality engineering, facilities and utilities, quality control, quality assurance, and manufacturing.

The scope and objective of the risk assessment is defined as the area of the facility or process under assessment. The scope can involve just one room, an isolator, or a larger manufacturing area. Independent of the scope, however, the manufacturing process associated with the area in scope is key to performing the risk assessment.

Thorough process understanding and process mapping is necessary to evaluate the associated risk of contamination at a specific position within a cleanroom. Prior to initiating the risk assessment, process mapping should be conducted on the shop/manufacturing floor using room layouts and indicating equipment locations, people flow, material flow, and waste flow in the facility.

The assessment team will select the most appropriate risk management tool among the different methods applicable, e.g., Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA), Hazard Analysis Critical Control Point (HACCP), among others. One risk assessment tool, developed by a collaborative of pharmaceutical industry representatives through BioPhorum, addresses the wide range of practices within the industry, allowing harmonization as well as compliance with regulatory guidelines. This toolkit provides an easy to use, step-by-step guidance for establishing EM programs.

5.2.2.1 General Points to Consider & Grid Profiling

Risk Factors & Grid Profiling Method

For process mapping, grid profiling can be useful to describe single process steps and consider activities specific to that grid. After gridding the room layout, the process steps in each grid are evaluated and the risk ranking is determined based on six risk factors:

  1. Cleaning and sanitization access
  2. Personnel presence and flow
  3. Material flow
  4. Proximity to open product or product contact material
  5. Interventions/operations by personnel and their complexity
  6. Frequency of interventions

Based on the risk scoring, sampling locations are determined for performing the EMPQ and, afterwards, for conducting the routine monitoring. A recommendation of the grid size for the respective grades is made (e.g., 0.5 m² for Grade A). In addition, grouping grids into functional sections (e.g., filling area) enables meaningful sample points to be defined.

Additional Points to Consider

Sample locations should be chosen based on:

  • Criticality of the process being performed in the room
  • Product exposure to the environment and susceptibility to microbial and particulate contamination
  • Locations of potential ingress from supporting areas or adjacent non-classified areas (gowning areas, airlocks, corridors)
  • Sites shown to be appropriately placed to detect a problem in the area

The following factors should be considered during the facility walk-through:

  1. Adherence to industry and regulatory guidelines (CFR, ISO 14644, USP, FDA, EU)
  2. Sites where contamination would most likely have an adverse effect on product quality:
    • Proximity to open product or product contact surfaces and critical sites
    • Activities linked with interventions
    • Areas most inaccessible or difficult to clean and disinfect
    • Locations with high frequency and/or complexity of activities
    • Areas with large number of personnel and high personnel flow
    • Areas with high material flow
  3. Uniform geometric pattern or grid-profiling within the cleanrooms
  4. Assessment and justification for locations not part of the EM program
  5. Historical data and/or data obtained during qualification
  6. Anaerobic organisms (periodic/investigational monitoring for Cutibacterium acnes)
  7. Airflow visualization studies (smoke studies)

5.2.2.2–5.2.2.4 Risk Initiation, Assessment, Control & Review

5.2.2.2 Risk Initiation: Development of EM Risk Assessments

Typical approaches to EM risk assessments include the following activities:

  • Map the layout of the room(s), overlay with grids, and combine them into functional sections
  • Walk the process with the risk assessment team, detailing process activities grid-by-grid
  • Assess each grid against the risk factors and score them to determine the relative probability of contamination
  • Evaluate the results by functional sections to select sampling locations and methods
  • Create an EMPQ plan
  • Evaluate the EMPQ results
  • Determine routine EM sampling locations, methods, and frequencies
  • Review the risk assessment on a defined, periodic basis

For each grid, risk is scored based on the process used, and then ranked based on the six factors. Strategies regarding risk scoring and risk ranking can be found in the BioPhorum EM Risk Assessment Toolkit. Each risk factor is ranked as low (1), medium (2) or high risk (4), with exception of "proximity to product," which is ranked as low (1), medium (4) or high risk (8). The calculated product for each grid results in the risk scoring as low risk (1–16), medium risk (32–265) or high risk (512–8192).

5.2.2.3 Risk Assessment Phase

Once the elements of the EM risk assessment are defined, the team will begin the risk assessment phase. Hazards identified during the facility walk-through will be documented, and the hazards will be evaluated for risk factors given the current controls in place. During this phase, consider:

  • Timing of sampling and sample volume measured against the company standard
  • Frequency of sampling measured against the company standard
  • Additional criteria (e.g., height of sampling location)
  • Risk of sampling procedure for product contamination
  • Locations and areas where there is no monitoring
  • Historical data
  • Air flow studies

The outcome of the risk assessment includes a documented plan for monitoring and the rationale for the EM program (sampling locations, sampling volume, sampling frequency, methods).

If there is an existing EM program without a risk-based rationale, the risk team must be careful to ensure they are not performing an assessment to justify the current sampling program. The team must evaluate the facility/process (e.g., via a Gemba walk), taking into consideration historical data to prevent removal of sampling locations which have historically demonstrated adverse recovery trends.

5.2.2.4 Risk Control & Risk Review

Where an existing EM program was created using the principles of risk management, the team will evaluate the existing assessment to ensure that all conditions assumed in the original assessment are still in place. If there are discrepancies between the initial and current conditions, the assessment is modified, and the hazards are re-ranked to determine if the risks are below the required threshold. Any major deviations to the relevant criteria should be considered, and mitigation actions may be defined to control the risks.

5.3 Alert Levels and Action Limits

5.3 Alert Levels and Action Limits

EM programs require action limits to be established based on the applicable regulatory guidelines or requirements. These guidelines frequently recommend alert levels also be established. Some companies also choose to set levels for individual cleanrooms or sample sites. The alert level will be established based on statistical analysis of the historic EM data.

Alert levels and action limits based on CFU count excursions have been eliminated from USP <1116>, which indicates a paradigm shift to contamination recovery rate (CRR). CRR is the percentage of environmental samples found to have microbial contamination (e.g., a CRR of 1% would mean that only 1% of the samples taken showed contamination, regardless of colony numbers). The CRR approach may lend itself to wider applicability considering emerging EM technologies, such as those not reliant on CFU measurement. However, current official regulatory guidances from both the EU and the US still retain GMP requirements for alert levels and action limits. Consequently, companies may want to voluntarily monitor CRR as an EM trending tool in addition to the required alert levels and action limits.

The response to alert levels or action limits should be written and employed consistently. To create consistency, investigatory and corrective action steps should be specified in advance. Records should show that the excursion was recognized, appropriate follow-up occurred, and appropriate corrective and preventive actions (CAPA) were taken. Established alert levels should be reviewed periodically as part of routine trend analysis. They may be revised to reflect improvements, advances in technology, changes in use patterns, or other changes.

When no regulatory or industry guidelines are provided, alert levels and action limits may be derived statistically from historical data. Statistically derived alert levels, based on performance of the area, might be low; however, one should be using those values to detect atypical recurrences. Other considerations in adjusting levels include improving process capability, consistency of alert levels and action limits for similar room classifications, level of gowning, and product contamination risk. Infrequent excursions from these levels are expected at frequencies characteristic for the specific mathematical model used in their derivation. The critical requirement is recognizing an adverse trend that may represent a loss of environmental control.

The alert levels and action limits do not define product attributes, such as sterility, and should not be considered as product specifications. Rather, alert levels are intended to indicate changes or drifts in environmental control so that corrective action may be taken before product quality is adversely affected. However, where an action limit is exceeded where critical aseptic process steps are conducted, the investigation should be completed prior to release of the batches impacted by the excursion, which should include an evaluation of the product impact, based on the most probable root cause identified.

Since there is no one best method for setting alert levels, the approaches described in Sections 5.3.1–5.3.3 may be taken within the pharmaceutical industry. Where compendial requirements exist, as with action limits, they supersede these examples.

在環境監測中，Alert 是「早期預警」，Action 是「強制處理」。

USP <1116> 的典範轉移 (Paradigm Shift)：傳統上，環境監測關注每個樣品的 CFU 計數是否超過特定數值。但 USP <1116> 現在推動使用汙染回收率 (Contamination Recovery Rate, CRR)，也就是「在所有採樣中，有多少比例檢出微生物」。這個改變的意義在於：

  • CRR 不依賴於 CFU 計數，因此適用於不基於 CFU 的新興快速微生物檢測技術
  • CRR 提供了環境整體汙染「頻率」的宏觀視角，而非單一事件的「嚴重程度」
  • 目前 EU GMP Annex 1 和 FDA 仍要求設定 Alert/Action 限度，因此 CRR 可作為額外的趨勢分析工具併用

重點提示：EM 限度 vs 產品規格

TR13 明確指出：Alert/Action 限度不是產品規格 (product specifications)，不定義無菌性 (sterility)。它們是環境控制的「體溫計」——用來偵測環境漂移，以便在影響產品品質之前採取預防措施。

但當行動限度在關鍵無菌操作步驟區域被超過時，調查必須在批次放行前完成，並評估對產品的潛在影響。

Warning: EM Data Is NOT Normally Distributed / 警告：環境監測數據並非常態分佈

Using standard statistical tools for setting alert levels and action limits can be misleading because environmental monitoring data is not normally distributed. No pharmaceutical industry or regulatory consensus exists on the best statistical model to use for setting these levels. Therefore, it may be appropriate to determine the model that best fits the data and use that model to set the levels. When data does not fall within a normal distribution and exhibits high levels of skewness towards lower counts and/or zero counts, a non-parametric approach to setting alert levels and action limits is suitable when large numbers of historical observations exist.

實務含義：環境監測數據大量集中在 0 或低 CFU，呈現高度右偏態分佈。傳統 Mean ± 2SD / 3SD 的方法假設常態分佈，套用在 EM 數據上會產生誤導性的限度。因此 TR13 推薦以下三種非參數方法。

5.3.1 Nonparametric Approach

5.3.1 Nonparametric Approach

A nonparametric evaluation is based on 99.9th and 99.99th percentiles and can be chosen as the alert and action levels. These levels correspond to about mean plus 3 and 4 sigma ranges, respectively. These levels are commonly selected as process alert and action levels. When the requirement of proper sample size is met (N≈1000), the alert levels analysis establishes confidence that during actual process monitoring 99.9% of the population will lie below the stated value. When these limits are exceeded, it will trigger investigation that will prevent contamination or an adverse trend condition.

Action levels can be selected at 99.99th percentile. The action level analysis establishes confidence that during actual process monitoring 99.99% of the population will lie below the stated value. Therefore, the derived action levels serve to identify an excursion at the selected percentile level. By definition, it is very unlikely that a validated system, operating in a state of control, will deliver results that will deviate significantly from the norm especially when multiple alert/action excursions in the same room on the same day are detected. This condition is likely indicative of a significant drift in the process control(s) or a systemic failure of the validated system has occurred.

中文解析：非參數百分位數法

Nonparametric Percentile Method / 非參數百分位數法

Alert Level  = 99.9th percentile  (approx. Mean + 3 sigma)
Action Limit = 99.99th percentile (approx. Mean + 4 sigma)

Required sample size: N ≈ 1,000

Interpretation:
  - 99.9% of all future results should fall BELOW the Alert Level
  - 99.99% of all future results should fall BELOW the Action Limit
  - Exceedance of these limits is statistically RARE in a controlled system

方法解析：此方法將所有歷史數據由小到大排列，直接取第 99.9 和 99.99 百分位數的值作為 Alert 和 Action 限度。由於不假設任何特定分佈，因此叫「非參數」。

實務舉例：假設某 ISO 7 房間收集了 1,200 筆主動空氣採樣數據，將數據從小到大排列：

  • 第 99.9 百分位數 = 第 1,199 個值 = 8 CFU → Alert Level = 8 CFU
  • 第 99.99 百分位數 = 最大值附近 = 12 CFU → Action Limit = 12 CFU

關鍵注意事項：此方法需要大量數據 (N≈1000)。若數據量不足，極端值（可能來自失控事件的異常值）會被選為限度，導致限度被不當抬高。

5.3.2 Nonparametric Tolerance Limit Approach

5.3.2 Nonparametric Tolerance Limit Approach

As environmental monitoring data, especially in cleanrooms, is typically not normally distributed (i.e., exhibits skew heavily toward lower counts or zero counts), a nonparametric tolerance limit approach to setting alert levels and action limits is recommended. These limits allow the user to assert with at least 95% (K=0.95) confidence that 100(P) or 99% of a population lies below the value, as determined by the stated action limits, for the respective data. For distribution-free tolerance limits, minimum sample sizes are N=60 for 95/95 (alert level) and N=300 for 95/99 (action limits).

中文解析：非參數容忍限法

Tolerance Limit Notation / 容忍限符號說明

Format: Confidence / Coverage  (K / P)

Alert Level:  95/95
  → 95% confidence that 95% of population is below this value
  → Minimum sample size: N = 60

Action Limit: 95/99
  → 95% confidence that 99% of population is below this value
  → Minimum sample size: N = 300

與百分位數法的差異：百分位數法只給出數據的某個位置值，而容忍限法多了一層「信心水準」(confidence level)。95/95 表示「我有 95% 的信心保證，95% 的未來數據都會低於這個值」。

優勢：

  • 所需數據量遠低於百分位數法（60 vs 1000）
  • 不假設特定分佈（distribution-free）
  • 提供明確的統計信心保證

適用情境：新建潔淨室或新導入 EM 程序、尚未累積大量歷史數據時，此方法比百分位數法更實用。

5.3.3 Cutoff Value Approach

5.3.3 Cutoff Value Approach

All the test data for a particular site, or group of similar sites, are arranged in a histogram, and the alert levels and action limits are set at values whose monitoring results are respectively 5% and 1% higher than the level selected (i.e., 95th and 99th percentile, respectively). Other percentiles may be used in establishing levels. Alert levels are typically determined per test type (active air, passive air, and surface monitoring).

The alert definition should be based on a timeframe allowing the accumulation of enough data to be representative of a population. A general text on statistics recommends a statistically significant value of at least 20 data points. Alert levels are typically set at about 50–60% of action until enough data is generated. An example is to take the last 100 monitoring results or data collected during one full year and use the 95th and 99th percentile values as the alert levels and action limits.

Areas that have the same activities may be grouped to provide more data points; for example, material airlocks of the same Grade, or Grade D hallways of same function can be grouped.

The calculated alert level should be challenged for its appropriateness, however, avoid modifying or increasing the alert as such it loses any correlation with your actual cleanroom performance. In addition, performance-based alert levels that seem to be well below the applied action limits should be considered as a confirmation of a solid microbial control of the environment and does not per definition to be considered as inappropriate. One should be aware that above alerts will occur and that this is inherent with the use of a cut-off approach. One should be more focused on the level of occurrence.

If the outcome of the periodic reassessment of the alert assessment results in a level increase, this should be interpreted as a warning that the facility may be slowly drifting out of control or might be a result of modifications of the activities within the area. It is recommended not to increase alert levels without a good understanding of the reasons behind it.

The frequency-based excursion rates and trends can be used to determine the state of the EM control and gowning process. The cumulative conforming count chart (CCC) is easy to use and more sensitive to short-term drifts in process parameter, enabling its user to judge the state of control. The idea behind the CCC is the fact that the number of conforming items between two consecutive nonconforming items changes when the fraction nonconforming shifts is observed. CCC plots the number of inspectional monitoring data sets between excursions. The method is suitable when large number of data exists.

At a specified excursion rate, a likely number of excursions per given number of samples can be estimated at a 95% confidence level. The CCC can be used to identify the average and the minimum number of continuous successful (within alert levels) facility- or gown-monitoring examinations before the data shifts. Such approaches can be used to evaluate limits, define trends, and assess training effectiveness, for example, gowning process and operator or analyst disqualification and requalification.

Other models based on negative binomial, Poisson, Weibull, or exponential distributions are possible. It may be appropriate to determine the model that best fits the data and use that model to set the levels. As noted, contamination in strictly controlled environments does not typically fall within a normal distribution. Environmental monitoring data may be evaluated to determine the suitability of the approaches to level setting.

The monitoring group should review the data for trends at an appropriate frequency. The quality unit should review quarterly and yearly monitoring reports.

5.3.4 Quantitative and Qualitative Trending Rules

5.3.4 Quantitative and Qualitative Trending Rules

Trend rules should supplement alert levels and action limits and focus on two important axes:

  • To rapidly detect gradual bioburden changes before it leads to real excursions with potential impact for production and/or the cleanroom environment (immediate term trending)
  • It should be able to periodically evaluate the general state of control of the cleanroom environment (short- and long-term trending)

A useful trending rule is to look at alert level excursions over time within the same room identifying correlation between/among locations, or frequent sustained recoveries above alert. When trend rules are exceeded, an investigation is recommended.

Immediate — short term trending rules typically look at an increase in the proportion of above alerts over a specified time period. Examples:

  • A specified number of above alerts at a single location/type of test
  • A specified number of alerts within one room (same and different types of tests)

Some statistical rules can be used as well in order to identify when the proportion of above alerts is moving to an atypical level.

Short Term — monthly or quarterly: The monitoring group should perform a comprehensive review of the data at an appropriate frequency as a function of the amount and criticality of the data generated. The quality unit should review and approve the periodic monitoring reports. Typically, a thorough analysis with a statement if data indicate that the facility is operating in a state of control or if data indicate some issues, how it was discovered, where and what corrective action was taken and what impact the issue had on the facility. The integration of graphs is highly recommended to visualize the state of control. One can assess:

  • Same sample type from same location exceeding the action limit multiple times
  • Increases of the number of alert level excursions (e.g., same monitoring type: active, passive or surface monitoring)
  • Recovery rate increases for the same location, or the same room or area

Long Term — annual: Annual trending should report the changes in a year's worth of data. Since it incorporates more data, it should be able to give a better holistic picture of the cleanroom environment. It might include:

  • Number of EM Deviations per grade with indication of concerned rooms and test type and corrective actions
  • Occurrence of atypical microorganism per grade and explanation of potential root cause identified
  • Microbial flora analysis and confirmation of the disinfection program efficiency and validated state
  • Global analysis of the site performance covered by the monthly/quarterly reports and comparison with the previous period
  • Key findings and highlights on any change in SOP or modification during the year
  • Year graphical representation and global analysis of the data from your cleanrooms and conclusion with a statement such as data indicate that the facility is operating in a state of control or if data indicate some issues and any related CAPAs
  • Analysis of potential correlations between observed product contamination (e.g., bioburden) and the micro-organisms recovered within the cleanroom environment

5.4 Data Management

Routine review and analysis of EM data for trends at an appropriate frequency is essential to aid in the interpretation of process stability and assess overall environmental control performance. Management must be kept abreast of trends and the subsequent state of operations within facilities with review of quarterly and yearly monitoring reports.

Based on the large number of samples tested by a given facility, a computer-based data-tracking system is essential. Before implementation, all database applications used should be validated or qualified for specific software applications.

5.4.1 Data Integrity (ALCOA)

5.4.1 Data Integrity

The PDA Elements of a Code of Conduct for Data Integrity in the Pharmaceutical Industry and PDA Technical Report No. 80: Data Integrity Management Systems for Pharmaceutical Laboratories provide comprehensive information on this topic.

Collecting, analyzing, reporting and retention of EM data should be done under conditions that are designed to guarantee reliability and integrity of the data. The data are required to be attributable, legible, contemporaneous, original, and accurate (ALCOA):

  • Attributable: Information is captured that identifies where the data came from
  • Legible: Data are readable and understandable by humans
  • Contemporaneous: Data are recorded at the time they are generated or observed
  • Original: Data are recorded for the first time
  • Accurate: Data are correct

Important elements to consider with respect to environmental monitoring are:

  • Integrity of the sampling site data by including a formal mechanism to enable full reconciliation of sample collection, incubation, and evaluation
  • EM data must be documented on controlled worksheets for paper records or entered into a validated computer system with appropriate security, audit trails, validation, and oversight
  • Employees must adhere to the established site documentation requirement and will not record raw data on unofficial forms, writing pads, or other uncontrolled media
  • Employees will not discard or destroy raw data or original records, other than at the end of the prescribed retention period

The deployment of a second-person verification should be considered to confirm the enumeration of microbial recovery. The main focus, however, should be to have in place a robust plate-reading process, reducing the risk for variability to the minimum, for instance, by applying appropriate lighting and/or magnification and a robust training of the technicians performing the readout. The utilization of QRM principles can aid in the determination of whether or not a secondary verification is required (e.g., Grade A EM). Automated systems for EM test readouts and data storage can eliminate bias and the need to perform a second verification.

Routine data are aligned into a source in a consistent record format. At a minimum, the record format should include the following:

  • Identification of the operator and analyst
  • Monitoring date and time
  • Equipment used
  • Specific sampling locations
  • Sampling methods including media used
  • Incubation conditions
  • Colony-forming units (CFU) or total airborne particulate count results
  • Identifications performed
  • Batch information (if applicable, e.g., for batch-related monitoring)
  • Current alert levels or action limits

Records must be signed and verified by the appropriate person, depending on the type of system used. Some alternative microbiological methods can use measurements other than CFUs, provided they have been properly validated before use, for example, relative light units, cells, etc.

5.4.2 Data Trending Analysis

5.4.2 Data Trending Analysis

Histograms or tables, characterized by a number of data points that fall within a common frequency, are valuable tools to conduct the trending expected by regulatory agencies. Different room classifications with defined requirements will produce different histograms. For example, the CFU spread obtained across an ISO 8 data set will not be observed in a data set from an ISO 5 area. Therefore, each area, or area type, and its accompanying data set must be viewed as distinct. Data trends in counts, occurrences, and organism types can provide a holistic view of the cleanroom environment. They can also be used to evaluate if there is ingress from lower to higher grades. Cleanroom maps indicating sampling excursions are useful as well to show connectivity of various grades and potential ingress through equipment and personnel flows. While this does not require statistical tools, mathematical models could be applied. Examples of statistical methods and control charts can be found in PDA Technical Report 59: Utilization of Statistical Methods for Production Monitoring.

5.4.3 Interpretation & Contamination Recovery Rates

5.4.3 Interpretation

Routine environmental microbial monitoring data should demonstrate that the classified area is operating under an adequate microbial control for the needs of the operations conducted in that area. Individual recoveries in critical areas (i.e., Grade A) must be adequately investigated. Compliant EM results do not guarantee the sterility assurance of the process, however, as EM incorporates multiple limitations (e.g., recovery efficiency of the applied methods, selected sample locations etc.). EM can only confirm to some extent the robustness of the design and processes.

Data generated should be summarized and evaluated to determine whether the EM process is in a state of control and documented in periodic reports. A variety of methods are available to perform this analysis, for example, the use of frequency distribution and control charts or statistical analysis.

Measurements for viable contamination, such as contamination excursion rates and/or recovery rates, can be used to determine the level of control in a given area. The excursion rate is related to the number of samples exceeding the defined alert levels and action limits, whereas the contamination recovery rate (expressed as a percentage) is related to the number of samples that are positive for microbial growth when compared to the total number of samples taken within the same classified area(s). Therefore, recovery rates differ from excursion rates. Given that microorganisms are not homogeneously distributed in the same environments, and the sensitivity and variability associated with microbial sampling methods, these rates can be useful approaches to trending results in controlled areas.

The combined excursion rates are calculated by determining the number of samples with excursions outside of established levels, dividing by the total number of samples collected, and converting to a percentage. For an ISO 5 environment, an excursion rate of not more than 1% is achievable due to stringent controls, and the expectation is that the results show no viable recoveries.

In addition to individual excursions, CRRs may be used for trending and control of the overall microbial load in the classified environment, equipment surfaces, material, and personnel. Companies can utilize the criteria from USP <1116> to develop their recovery rates for cleanroom environments depending on the activities and processes conducted in those areas. They should also establish a CRR for each monitoring attribute, such as surfaces, air, and garments, for each classified area. Any changes from the established calculated contamination rates should be investigated and corrected. However, any contamination in Grade A/ISO 5 areas must be investigated, since there should not be any microbial recovery within those areas. The CRR provides an alternative measurement of the effectiveness of the overall contamination control strategy.

Excursion Rate vs Recovery Rate (CRR)

超標率 (Excursion Rate)：

  • = 超過 Alert/Action 限度的樣品數 ÷ 總採樣數 × 100%
  • 關注的是「超過設定限度」的比例

汙染回收率 (Contamination Recovery Rate, CRR)：

  • = 檢出任何微生物的樣品數 ÷ 總採樣數 × 100%
  • 關注的是「有微生物生長」的比例，不論是否超過限度

兩者不同！一個樣品可能有 1 CFU（陽性回收但未超標），則計入 CRR 但不計入 Excursion Rate。

EM 的固有局限性：TR13 特別提醒，合規的 EM 結果不保證製程的無菌保證 (sterility assurance)。EM 有多重限制：

  • 採樣方法的回收效率有限
  • 採樣位置只涵蓋空間的極小部分
  • EM 只能在一定程度上確認設計和流程的穩健性

5.5 Rapid Microbiological Methods (RMM) in Environmental Monitoring

The industry should look toward utilizing innovative approaches for the detection and quantification of microorganisms as part of a holistic EM program. From a QRM perspective, the benefits of implementing rapid microbiological testing strategies can help to:

  • Design robust processes that prevent contamination
  • Ensure that a state of microbial control is maintained
  • Evaluate the impact of human interventions during aseptic filling
  • Establish recovery time after power shortages
  • Develop more effective strategies to correct a contamination problem
  • Continually improve processes and products
  • Quickly and efficiently assess the potential impact of failing results on the patient

Key applications of RMM within EM programs include:

  • Confirmation of microbial control in controlled areas prior to use (e.g., manufacturing suites, gowning rooms)
  • Rapid assessment of controlled areas during facility qualification activities (e.g., EM PQ)
  • Evaluation of contamination remediation efforts and root cause analysis

Additional detailed guidance may be found in PDA Technical Report No. 33: Evaluation, Validation and Implementation of Alternative and Rapid Microbiological Methods.

中文解說：RMM 導入的戰略意義

TR13 第 5.5 節正式將快速微生物方法（RMM, Rapid Microbiological Methods）納入環境監測（EM）程序的核心討論，這標誌著業界從「傳統培養皿計數」向「即時感測」的典範轉移。

為什麼 RMM 如此重要？傳統培養基法的最大弱點是結果滯後：一般培養基需 3–7 天才出結果，等到發現超標，批次早已前處理甚至出貨。RMM 的核心價值在於縮短「問題發生→偵測→干預」的時間窗口。

RMM 適用的四大 EM 情境

  • 使用前確認：生產前快速確認潔淨室微生物負荷符合要求
  • 廠房確效：EM PQ 期間的快速評估，加速確效時程
  • 汙染溯源：根本原因調查（RCA）中，快速鎖定汙染熱點
  • 消毒效果驗證：清潔/消毒後立即評估處理效果

從品質風險管理（QRM）視角，RMM 提升了微生物控制的即時性，直接保護病患安全，這也是監管機構積極鼓勵創新方法的原因。

5.5.1 Rapid Microbiological Method Scientific Principles Applied to Environmental Monitoring

Rapid methods appropriate for EM are based on a wide variety of scientific principles. Some methods still require growth of microorganisms using conventional microbiological media; however, detection and enumeration can be faster using novel detection techniques:

Growth-dependent RMM (faster detection via novel readout):

  • Digital imaging and autofluorescence of microcolonies
  • Fluorescent staining and laser excitation of microcolonies
  • Rapid detection of carbon dioxide (CO2)
  • Detection of adenosine triphosphate (ATP) after a brief microbial enrichment period

Growth-independent RMM (instantaneous or near real-time):

  • Flow and solid-phase cytometry using viability stains and laser detection systems
  • ATP bioluminescence (when enrichment is not required due to concentration of microorganisms in the sample)
  • Intrinsic fluorescence and optical spectroscopy detection of microorganisms in air or water
  • RAMAN spectroscopy coupled with viability staining
  • Nucleic acid amplification techniques (when enrichment is not required)

Technology selection factors:

  • Sample type and size required for analysis
  • Sample compatibility with RMM workflow and instrumentation
  • Desired time to result
  • Level of sensitivity required
  • Need to neutralize antimicrobial activity (for growth-based methods)
  • Ability to allow identification of recoveries (destructive vs. non-destructive assay)
  • Potential for false positives or negatives due to cleaning or disinfectant residues

Automated and high-throughput methods using conventional media are also available for faster results with better automation, data management, and data integrity.

類型一：仍需微生物生長（Growth-Dependent）

直接偵測樣品中的微生物，結果可接近即時。

  • 流式細胞術（Flow Cytometry）：逐一計數活性細胞，速度極快
  • ATP 生物發光：無需富集時可直接偵測，常用於水系統
  • 內源螢光光譜：利用微生物固有螢光特性辨識
  • RAMAN 光譜：結合活性染色，提供分子指紋
  • 核酸擴增（NAT）：PCR 等方法偵測特定基因序列

實務選擇關鍵：沒有萬能的 RMM。選擇時需考慮樣品基質（空氣、表面、水）、是否需要後續菌種鑑定（破壞性 vs. 非破壞性）、以及消毒劑殘留可能造成的假陽性/假陰性干擾。

5.5.2 Benefits of Using Rapid Microbiological Methods

The implementation of rapid methods for EM may present operational enhancements related to product quality, process knowledge, and regulatory acceptance, including:

  • Potential for reduced in-process microbiology testing and finished product release cycle times
  • Reduction in risks associated with forward processing
  • Elimination or reduction of off-line microbiology assays
  • Increases in laboratory automation and reductions in manual testing, sample handling, and data management
  • Reduced overhead and/or headcount for sampling and/or testing
  • Ability to make immediate quality decisions on the state of microbial control
  • Faster response to contamination events and initiation of investigations
  • Reduced repeat testing, lot rejection, reprocessing, and rework
  • Reduction in room or facility downtime and investigations
  • Faster confirmation of contamination remediation efforts

中文解說：RMM 十大效益解析

TR13 列舉的效益，可從三個維度理解：

維度一：時間效益（Speed）

  • 縮短批次放行週期（Release Cycle Time）
  • 加快汙染調查啟動速度
  • 更快確認消毒補救措施效果

維度二：風險效益（Risk Reduction）

  • 降低「前處理風險」（Forward Processing Risk）：傳統法等待結果期間，後續批次可能已進入無菌灌裝，若超標則連帶受影響
  • 減少重複測試、批次拒收、再加工
  • 縮短設施停機時間

維度三：營運效益（Operational）

  • 提升實驗室自動化程度
  • 降低人力成本（採樣與測試人員）
  • 改善數據完整性（Data Integrity）

稽核提示：若公司尚未導入 RMM，稽核員可能詢問是否評估過 RMM 的可行性，以及不導入的科學理由。TR13 的這份效益清單也是業務案例（Business Case）的有力依據。

5.5.3 Validation of Rapid Microbiology Methods and Equipment

Rapid microbiology methods vary in complexity and in the associated equipment required:

  • Equipment/software validation follows the model found in USP <1058> (Analytical Instrument Qualification)
  • Method validation is discussed in compendial chapters: USP <1116> and Ph. Eur. 5.1.6
  • PDA TR No. 33 also provides industry-proven validation strategies

The most recent EU Annex 1 recommends that when a technology presents a result in a manner different from the CFU, the manufacturer should:

  • Scientifically justify the limits applied
  • Where possible, correlate them to the CFU
  • This applies to Grade A areas where "no growth" is expected

Key challenge — non-CFU signal correlation:

If the data signal is a CFU (growth-based RMM), results can be compared with existing alert levels and action limits directly. If the data signal is not a CFU, the quantitative result may not align with existing limits, even though a CFU correlation can be made. In this case, the end-user may:

  • Generate sufficient data to modify existing alert and action levels based on historical data
  • Rationalize and apply new levels using a risk-based analysis

Alternatively, trending EM values may be more appropriate than looking at individual counts — especially in well-controlled environments (e.g., Grade A / ISO 5 areas) where zero cell counts are expected.

Results using growth-independent RMM may be numerically higher than traditional methods, as viable but non-culturable (VBNC), physically stressed, damaged, or dormant organisms may be detected that would not grow on conventional media.

中文解說：RMM 驗證的三層框架與最大挑戰

RMM 驗證的複雜性遠超傳統方法驗證，TR13 清楚指出了三個層次：

驗證層次架構

  • 儀器/設備確效（USP <1058>）：AIQ (IQ/OQ/PQ)
  • 方法驗證（USP <1116>, Ph. Eur. 5.1.6）：靈敏度、專一性、線性、精確度
  • 業界策略指引（PDA TR33）：完整的替代方法評估/驗證/實施框架

最大挑戰：非 CFU 訊號的限值設定問題

傳統 EM 的警戒限（Alert Level）與行動限（Action Limit）都是以 CFU 為單位制定的（參照 EU Annex 1 Table 1）。當 RMM 產生的訊號不是 CFU（例如流式細胞術輸出的「事件數/mL」、ATP 輸出的「RLU」），就無法直接套用現行限值。

兩種解決路徑：

  • 建立 CFU 相關性曲線 → 轉換為等效 CFU 後比較（數學上可行，但統計挑戰大）
  • 用足夠的歷史數據重新制定該 RMM 的專屬警戒/行動限（較實際，需風險評估支持）

VBNC 議題：不需培養的 RMM 可偵測到「有活性但不可培養」的微生物，因此計數往往高於傳統法，這不代表環境變差，而是偵測靈敏度提升的結果。

Grade A 的特殊考量：在預期「零生長」的環境中，趨勢分析（Trending）比單一計數更有意義，因為任何正值都應觸發調查，而不必等到超過 action limit。

5.6 Identification of Isolates

Identifying and strain-typing of microorganisms is not only important as part of cleanroom qualification or when related to EM excursions, but should also form an integral part of routine surveillance monitoring programs.

The identification program selected by the laboratory should be defined in writing, including:

  • The frequency of identification
  • Standard procedures for the methods used
  • Consistency with regulatory expectations for identifications

Companies relying on contract laboratories for microbial identification should qualify them per supplier qualification procedures and have a quality agreement in place.

The identification of microorganisms forms an important element to assess microbial state of control, allowing:

  • Detection of any microbial changes and potential seasonal variations of the flora
  • Definition of potential sources of isolates related to a detected contamination (e.g., as part of an investigation)
  • Assessment of the level of contamination control
  • Response upon recovery of undesirable organisms that might pose particular risks

Grade A/B considerations: The Grade A environment is expected to be completely free of viable organisms. In the surrounding Grade B environment, spore-forming organisms (mold, Bacillus spp.) and water-borne Gram-negative organisms are of particular concern. Gram-negative organisms may indicate water intrusion, increasing the risk of mold proliferation.

Grade C/D: Levels of undesirable organisms should be controlled to limit microbial stress on surrounding aseptic cleanrooms.

中文解說：微生物鑑定的分層策略與溯源應用

微生物鑑定是 EM 程序中最具科學深度的環節之一。Table 5.6-1 提供了一個清晰的三層策略框架：

三層鑑定策略

  • 第一層：全面鑑定至種（Species Level）
    適用：Grade A/B 所有回收菌；Grade C/D 超標時；定期監測採樣
    方法：MALDI-TOF MS、16S rRNA 定序、生化鑑定
  • 第二層：週期性鑑定
    適用：Grade C/D 例行監測（即使未超標也需定期執行）
    目的：建立環境微菌相資料庫，偵測微菌相漂移（Microflora Shift）
  • 第三層：菌株分型（Strain Typing）
    適用：重大產品失敗（Media Fill、無菌測試失敗、重大不良趨勢）
    方法：MLST（多位點序列分型）、PFGE（脈衝場凝膠電泳）、全基因組定序（WGS）

比喻：鑑定就是微生物的「身分證+家族DNA」

鑑定到種（species）就像確認一個人的「身分」；菌株分型（strain typing）則像做「家族DNA比對」，可以確定兩個地方分離的菌是否為同一株（即同一汙染源）。當 Media Fill 失敗，我們需要的不只是「這是葡萄球菌」，而是「它與操作員手套上的菌是同一株嗎？」

汙染來源溯源（Source Attribution）的實際應用：

  • 分離到 Pseudomonas spp. → 立即懷疑水系統（WFI、純水）或清潔用水
  • 分離到 Staphylococcus epidermidis → 聚焦人員更衣、手套、口罩
  • 分離到 Aspergillus spp. (黴菌) → 檢查空氣過濾、外包材料帶入
  • 微菌相季節性變化 → 可能反映戶外空氣品質、植物花粉季節影響

委外鑑定的監管要求：若使用合約實驗室，必須完成供應商資格評估（Supplier Qualification）並簽訂品質協議（Quality Agreement），確保鑑定方法與精確度符合自身 SOP 要求。

5.7 Investigations and Corrective Actions

Investigations are needed in response to an action-limit excursion and to address an adverse trend in order to determine a cause-and-effect relationship or most probable cause (i.e., sources of contamination). Investigative and corrective action steps should be specified in a written procedure.

The procedure should define the level of investigation required if there are multiple or sequential excursions. The investigation should include:

  • A product impact assessment
  • Evaluation of the risk to other batches of the same or related products manufactured in the same time frame
  • Critical investigations should be closed prior to product release

中文解說：調查流程與根本原因分析策略

Section 5.7 是 EM 程序中最具實務操作性的部分。調查（Investigation）不是「找人負責」，而是「找出系統問題」。

比喻：EM 調查就像刑事偵查

偵探（QA 調查員）發現案發現場（Action Limit 超標），必須系統性地排查所有嫌疑人（汙染來源）：是空調系統（HVAC）、是水管、是人員操作疏失、還是設施本身的老化問題？不能靠直覺，必須有書面的調查 SOP，按步驟執行，最終找到「最可能的根本原因（Most Probable Cause）」。

五大系統的調查重點解析：

壓縮氣體系統（Compressed Gas）

調查最簡單直接：立即重測確認真偽 → 做過濾器完整性測試 → 若確認超標即更換過濾器。重點在「迅速行動」防止產品受影響。

空調/HVAC 系統

調查範圍最廣，需多面向檢視：人員活動（最大微生物負荷來源）、HEPA 完整性、氣流型態（靜/動態可視化測試）、壓差記錄、清潔/消毒記錄。HVAC 超標往往反映多因素交互作用。

設施表面（Surface）

需現場勘查：目視檢查牆壁/天花板/地板是否有裂縫、脫漆（可能是黴菌隱藏處）。CCTV 影片往往是還原「操作疏失」的關鍵證據。

高純水系統（WFI/PW）

需區分「點位汙染」（Port Contamination）vs「系統汙染」（System Contamination）。同步查驗內毒素數據（Gram-negative 菌的代謝產物），死角（Dead-legs）、斜度不足、軟管管理是常見的根本原因。

人員更衣（Personnel Gowning）

涉及人員個資與訓練記錄，需要訪談操作員、調閱更衣監控記錄、核對同日其他操作員的更衣數據，判斷是個人技能問題還是系統性訓練缺失。

產品影響評估（Product Impact Assessment）必須在調查中完成，且關鍵調查應在批次放行前關閉。這是監管機構稽核時的高頻查核點。

5.8 Documentation

The types of records that should be maintained as part of a routine EM program include:

  1. Date and time of sampling
  2. Product identification
  3. Identification of the individual performing test
  4. Test method or procedure reference
  5. Activity level at site during test
  6. Sampling equipment identification
  7. Physical parameters like temperature, relative humidity, and differential pressurization
  8. Sample site
  9. Area classification
  10. Schematics of areas showing sample site locations
  11. Sample site criticality
  12. Sampling frequency
  13. Test results with units (e.g., CFU/plate/hour)
  14. Identification of the analyst recording results
  15. Date results read
  16. Alert level and/or action limit
  17. Temperature and duration of incubation
  18. Traceability of the certification date, release date, lot number, and expiration date of media used
  19. Microbial characterization of contaminants
  20. Name of reviewer
  21. Approval of data
  22. Review of historical data
  23. Calibration due date for sampling instrumentation and equipment
  24. Methodology and analysis used to specify action and/or alert levels
  25. System for documenting investigative and corrective action:
    • Description of deficiency
    • Possible causes of problem
    • Identification of persons responsible for relevant corrective action
    • Description of action steps and their schedule for implementation
    • Evaluation of effectiveness of corrective and preventative action steps

中文解說：25 項文件記錄要求的分組解析

TR13 Section 5.8 的 25 項文件清單看似繁瑣，但邏輯分明，可分為五大組別：

組一：採樣事件識別（第 1–6 項）

組二：環境條件（第 7–12 項）

記錄採樣當下的環境狀態：溫溼度、壓差（#7）、採樣點位置（#8）、潔淨等級（#9）、採樣點示意圖（#10）、點位關鍵性（#11）、採樣頻率（#12）。這些數據支持趨勢分析與異常解讀。

組四：審核與核准（第 20–24 項）

最後一項「調查與矯正行動系統」是最複雜的，包含五個子要素：缺失描述、可能原因、負責人、行動步驟與時程、CAPA 有效性評估。這直接對應 FDA 21 CFR Part 211 及 EU GMP Annex 11 的要求。

稽核提示：稽核員常要求「拿一份 EM 記錄，現場核對是否包含這 25 項要素」。建立勾選式記錄表（Checklist-based Logbook）是最有效的合規工具。

6.0 Environmental Monitoring by Manufacturing Application

The levels and types of monitoring conducted may vary based on the type of product being manufactured, the attributes of the product, and the manufacturing areas and processes used.

6.1 Terminal Sterilization

Environmental monitoring in support of terminal sterilization monitors microflora from all sources — manufacturing environment, process gases, water used in product formulations, sterilizer feed and cooling water — that have the potential to contribute to product bioburden or endotoxin. This includes testing of air, surfaces, and water for pharmaceutical use.

Microbial levels of incoming containers and closures may also be monitored periodically (e.g., if the product is not terminally sterilized using an overkill cycle). While control of the environment in which the products are prepared is important, the most critical aspect of the program is the bioburden of the product and components to be sterilized, including both the number and type of microorganisms found.

中文解析：製造應用類別決定監控深度

TR13 Section 6 依製程類型分門別類地說明 EM 要求，這是本報告最具實務導向的章節。核心邏輯：產品風險等級愈高，EM 範圍愈廣、頻率愈高。

終端滅菌 vs. 無菌加工的根本差異

終端滅菌（Terminal Sterilization）：產品於最終容器中接受滅菌處理。EM 的目的是監控對 bioburden（生物負載）及 endotoxin（內毒素）有貢獻的所有來源。

最關鍵的指標不是環境本身，而是待滅菌產品與組件的生物負載總量與種類——因為這直接決定滅菌製程是否能達到要求的無菌保證水準（SAL）。

若採用 overkill cycle（過殺滅菌），對容器/密封件的定期微生物監測可彈性處理；若非 overkill 製程，則應定期監測容器/密封件的菌量。

比喻：廚房食材 vs. 外科手術室

終端滅菌猶如把食材放入高壓鍋蒸煮滅菌——食材本身的污染程度（bioburden）才是能否煮熟的關鍵，廚房環境的清潔程度是次要的。無菌加工則像外科手術，每一個步驟都必須在絕對無菌的環境中進行，因為沒有最後的「煮沸」步驟。

6.2 Aseptic Processing

Environmental monitoring in support of aseptic processing is specifically designed to determine the number and type of microorganisms associated with the direct assembly or preparation of product prior to sealing the filled containers. The number of sample sites and frequency of monitoring are considerably greater than those for established terminal sterilization programs.

Air, water, personnel, compressed gases, machinery, and other surfaces within the filling room and associated support areas are routinely monitored. Microbial levels may be periodically checked on incoming containers and closures. Adequate environmental control is an integral part of the aseptic manufacturing process and is critical to sterility assurance.

EM data must be reviewed prior to product batch release and investigation of excursions closed. EM should also be conducted when operator interventions are required during aseptic processing.

6.2.1 Isolation Technology

The EM program for isolators differs from conventional aseptic filling operations and is based on the risks associated with isolator technology — especially the physical integrity of half-suits (if applicable) and gloves. Monitoring of air, surfaces, and personnel outside of the isolator environment is not required after each filling operation.

However, monitoring of isolator gloves and half-suits is required at the end of each filling operation or campaign (as applicable) to detect potential contamination. Product contact surfaces that are sterilized or changed out between batches in a campaign should be monitored at the end of the batch specific activities, prior to sterilization / change out.

中文解析：無菌加工的全面監控要求

無菌加工的 EM 範圍涵蓋：空氣、水、人員、壓縮氣體、機械設備及所有表面。這是因為產品在填充與密封之間的每一個接觸點都有污染風險。

重要法規要求：批次放行前必須審查 EM 數據

TR13 明確指出：EM 資料必須在產品批次放行前完成審查，且所有超標調查必須結案。這意味著 EM 不只是事後統計工具，而是批次放行（batch release）的前提條件之一。

此外，操作員進行干預（interventions）時，也應同步進行 EM，以捕捉干預動作可能帶來的污染風險。

6.2.1 隔離器技術的特殊監控邏輯

隔離器（Isolator）透過物理屏障將操作人員與產品完全隔離，因此：

  • 隔離器外部的空氣、表面、人員監控，不需在每次填充操作後進行
  • 隔離器手套與半身防護服（half-suits）每次填充或每個 campaign 結束時必須監控——這是最關鍵的污染入侵路徑
  • 產品接觸面在批次間進行滅菌或更換前，應在批次特定作業結束後監控

這種差異反映了 EM 的風險導向原則：監控重心隨著物理屏障的存在而轉移。

6.3 Air Monitoring

Effectively designed air-handling units and use of HEPA filters or ultralow particulate air (ULPA) filters bring air of appropriate quality to cleanrooms. People are considered a major contributor of particulates, especially microorganisms, to the environment in aseptic areas.

Air monitoring determines if there are viable and/or total airborne particulates in locations that would allow them to settle on product-contact surfaces and thereby find their way into process intermediates or final product. The analysis of trends and frequencies is also vital to the understanding of the environmental impact of personnel behavior and process activities. Air-sampling methods and frequencies of monitoring will depend on manufacturing process needs and should be risk-based.

6.3.1 Total Airborne Particulate Monitoring

Monitoring of total airborne particulates is a necessary component of an EM program. It demonstrates control of potential contaminants in the environment to which the product is exposed during the manufacturing process. Classified areas must consistently meet particulate levels (see Section 3.0).

ISO Standard 14644-1 describes the classification of air cleanliness for cleanrooms based on specified concentrations of airborne particulates per volume of air sampled. The sample volume for routine monitoring does not need to be the same as that used for formal classification.

For the United States, FDA requires measurements of particles ≥0.5 μm. EU and PIC/S Annex 1 also include measurements for particles ≥5 μm for both classification and monitoring purposes.

For remote particle counter systems, the distance from the sample collection port to the detection technology should be considered for potential particle loss. The length of tubing should typically not exceed one meter and the radius should be greater than 15 cm, aligned with ISO 14644.

例外情況：若操作本身會產生大量產品微粒（如粉末填充），且這些微粒本身不構成污染風險，可在暴露前後分別監控，而非持續監控——因為此時無法區分外來污染與製程固有微粒。連續監控還可能損壞量測設備。

遠端計數系統（remote counting systems）的管路長度不得超過 1 公尺、半徑須大於 15 cm（依 ISO 14644）。

6.3.2 Viable Particulates

Viable particulate determinations are critical in monitoring air quality in controlled environments. Traditional growth-based techniques for monitoring viable particulates in air are limited by:

  • Type of equipment
  • Growth media
  • Incubation time
  • Operator contamination of the capture media

6.3.2.2 Methods

Regulatory agencies currently require active air sampling of environments on a routine basis to demonstrate control of viable airborne particulates. Some agencies require that passive sampling methods such as settling plates should be performed, with justification provided if a company does not use passive sampling for select operations.

Active sampling methods have the benefit of sampling large homogeneous volumes of air in short amounts of time, but these methods may cause disruptions in the airflow of the environment being sampled. Therefore, these sampling methods should be included in airflow visualization studies.

Passive sampling methods can be used for prolonged periods (up to four hours) in close proximity to critical processes without affecting the airflow. An additional advantage of passive sampling is that it mimics the potential pathway of contamination to a critical process.

6.3.2.3 Equipment

Microbial air samplers are typically based on two principles — inertial or centrifugal impaction — to capture and deposit organisms onto the agar or filter surface. No microbial air samplers are 100% efficient at detection or collection of all viable particles present.

The physical efficiency of an air sampler is referred to as the "cutoff size" or "d50 value" — the size of a particle in microns that the sampler has a 50% chance of recovering — which is supplied by the equipment manufacturer.

Equipment user requirements include: calibration of the instrument and verification of volume accuracy.

6.4 Surface Monitoring

Surface monitoring is conducted to determine the bioburden of surfaces — walls, floors, ceilings, non-product-contact and product-contact surfaces of equipment — in the manufacturing area.

6.4.1 Monitoring Considerations

When periodic surface monitoring is performed, it should be done after the filling operation or a campaign has been completed so no extraneous contamination or residual growth media are introduced during filling activities. The basic methods provide surface monitoring data (qualitative or quantitative) that can be used to assess environmental quality.

Neutralizers are typically added to media to inactivate chemical disinfectant residues on surfaces. Recovery levels of surface-monitoring methods are typically low due to variability of surfaces, sampling procedures, and growth-based techniques.

6.4.1.1 Contact Plates

Contact plates are easy to use and can provide quantitative results (CFU per surface area). The plate is pressed against a flat surface by gently rolling from front to back. Prior to use, media and incubation conditions must be qualified.

The use of irradiated plates in ISO 5 areas is required and is recommended in ISO 7 and ISO 8 areas. Contact plates must be allowed to acclimate to room temperature before use. Contact plates are not suitable for irregular surfaces; swabs are recommended for that situation. Media residue must be removed after sampling.

6.4.1.2 Flexible Films

A flexible film containing media can be used similarly to a contact plate or to provide a defined sampling area. The film is pressed against a flat surface in a rolling motion. Like contact plates, flexible films leave a media residue that must be removed post-sampling.

6.4.1.3 Swabs

Swabs are employed for equipment and irregular surfaces not suitable for contact plates or flexible films. Swabbing may also be performed on flat surfaces using a template of approximately 2 in × 2 in (~25 cm²). Swabs are composed of Dacron® or nylon in flocked or spun format. Prior to plating, each swab is vortexed in an appropriate diluent (e.g., sodium chloride, phosphate buffer, or peptone solutions with neutralizers). Swabs may provide qualitative or quantitative results depending on how they are processed.

6.4.1.4 Surface Rinse Method

The surface rinse method is best used when a large surface area sample is required (e.g., kettles, equipment, trains, tanks). A sterile diluent is used as the rinsing fluid, collected from the interior surface, and tested by membrane filtration because of the large sample volume. The holding time of rinsing samples prior to plating should be qualified.

6.5 Personnel Considerations

Personnel are the primary source of contamination, which is why all cleanroom personnel (including maintenance workers and others who enter periodically) must be carefully trained and qualified before entering an aseptic environment.

The qualification training should include:

  • Attention to personal hygiene
  • Basic microbiology education or background
  • Aseptic techniques
  • Appropriate cleanroom behavior
  • Patient safety hazards posed by a contaminated product
  • Gowning certification
  • Participation in aseptic processing simulation (APS or media fill) activities, where applicable

After initial training, personnel should participate regularly in an ongoing training and monitoring program. Routine microbiological monitoring of personnel garments and gloves should also be completed. Supervisory personnel should routinely evaluate each operator's conformance to written procedures. The quality unit should provide regular oversight.

6.5.1 Training and Certification of Personnel for Aseptic Manufacturing Areas

All training and certification activities should be documented and kept as part of the employee file.

6.5.2 Causes for Requalification or Retraining

Companies should have a policy stating when requalification or retraining is necessary, for example:

  • Retraining on general aseptic practices and techniques should be provided at least annually
  • Recertification should be required after extended absences (e.g., for illness or family leave)
  • Recertification and/or retraining should be considered as corrective action for trends in personnel monitoring excursions

6.6 Environmental Monitoring During Product Sterility Testing

Sterility-testing facilities should be monitored to demonstrate the absence of microbial contamination. Routine types of monitoring conducted are:

  • Viable air monitoring
  • Surface monitoring (including isolator gloves if applicable)
  • Personnel monitoring

Total airborne particulate monitoring is not mandatory in sterility testing environments; however, it may provide useful information regarding the HEPA filters in the ISO 5 testing environment or the integrity of isolators. Lab materials may generate particles that are not representative of actual manufacturing conditions, and there is no risk to marketed product in the testing environment.

  • 即使微粒計數偏高，也不會對市售產品造成直接風險

6.7 Environmental Monitoring in Grade A Air Supply

The term "Grade A air supply" is introduced in Annex 1 and is specifically used to support the capping of fully stoppered vials until the cap has been crimped — providing additional protection without the requirement to meet all criteria related to a Grade A area.

Grade A air supply provides air passed through a filter qualified as capable of producing Grade A total particulate quality air, but where there is no requirement to perform continuous total particulate monitoring or meet Grade A viable monitoring limits, and the area itself is not classified.

As Grade A Air Supply is not a clean room category (e.g., according to ISO 14644), no guidelines for viable and total particulate limits exist. In order to confirm Grade A total particulate quality air is supplied, testing at the point of supply should be performed as a minimum during qualification and requalification.

At-rest viable (air and surfaces) and total particulate monitoring may be performed based upon a risk assessment. Routine monitoring, limits, and positioning should reflect the requirements for this "clean process" and be aligned with the contamination control strategy, meeting criteria applied for the background of the Grade A Air supply (usually Grade C, but minimum Grade D).

6.8 Water

This document addresses only the process monitoring of water for microbial attributes. Most countries have compendial requirements for evaluation of water quality. The recently published EMA Guideline for the Quality of Water for Pharmaceutical Use should be consulted as to the type of pharmaceutical-grade water used in manufacturing.

The type of water selected for manufacturing is based on the type of product and process. Water quality should be evaluated for microbial, bacterial endotoxin, and chemical attributes in accordance with applicable compendial or regulatory requirements. Water quality is critical for the manufacturing of parenteral drugs and selected devices because it is used in product formulation, component and equipment washing, and final rinsing.

The quality of laboratory water should meet the needs of analytical methods and technologies, which may require compendial-quality laboratory water.

中文解析：製藥用水的監控策略

製藥用水的三個層次

  • 注射用水（WFI, Water for Injection）：最高品質要求，用於注射劑配方、設備最終沖洗。細菌內毒素 < 0.25 EU/mL，微生物限值 NMT 10 CFU/100 mL。
  • 純化水（Purified Water）：用於非注射產品製造、API、固體劑型。需控制微生物品質以防止工廠污染蔓延。
  • 潔淨蒸汽（Clean Steam）：用於滅菌設備與管路；通過化學與 LAL 測試，不建議微生物測試。

各藥典 WFI 規格差異重點

USP vs. EP/JP 主要差異：

  • USP：允許蒸餾法或同等/更優製程（如膜過濾）製備 WFI；EP 傳統上僅允許蒸餾法（2017年後 EP 10 也開放膜過濾）
  • EP 和 ChP 設有 pH 限制（5.0–7.0），USP 不設 pH 要求
  • EP 設有硝酸鹽（0.2 ppm）、亞硝酸鹽（0.02 ppm）、氨（0.2 ppm）、重金屬（0.1 ppm）限值；USP 均為 N/A
  • ChP 硝酸鹽限值（0.06 ppm）較 EP 更嚴格

台灣廠商若同時出口美國（FDA）與歐洲（EMA），需滿足兩者要求中較嚴格的一方。

6.8.1 Sample Site Selection and Frequency of Monitoring

The sampling and frequency of monitoring for water systems should be sufficient to ensure that water quality meets its intended use specifications and appropriate regulatory requirements. The frequency depends on the type of water, manufacturing process, system configuration, and facility design.

High-purity water systems should be validated to demonstrate that design requirements are consistently met and maintain a state of control. Site selection and frequency determination should be justified by thorough review of water system validation and risk-based approaches.

Once a water system is validated in a state of control, appropriate samples should be taken from:

  • Water source or generation system
  • Holding and storage tanks
  • Distribution system (all point-of-use sites for high-purity water systems)

Initial testing of water systems typically includes sampling after each process step.

6.8.2 Sample Collection and Testing

Water samples should be collected in a manner consistent with manufacturing practices. If manufacturing flushes use points prior to use, samples should be collected with the same flush cycle. All sample sites should be in a good state of repair and fully functional.

It is recommended to not use IPA on gloves or sample port prior to TOC sampling to avoid generating false positives. Microbiological examination of water should be initiated as soon as possible after sample collection. If immediate processing is not possible, samples should be refrigerated at 2–8°C. The time elapsed between collection and examination should be justified.

中文解析：用水取樣策略與實驗室操作

取樣策略的三個核心原則

  1. 代表性：取樣點需涵蓋水源、儲槽、分配系統全路徑。高純度水系統（WFI）需涵蓋所有使用點（point-of-use）。

  3. 風險導向頻率設定：頻率需足以偵測化學屬性、微生物菌相及內毒素的趨勢變化。

取樣操作重要細節

  • TOC 取樣前不得使用 IPA：手套或取樣口的酒精殘留會造成 TOC 假陽性，導致不必要的調查。
  • 儘快處理樣品：若無法立即檢測，應冷藏於 2–8°C，並對從採樣到檢測的時間間隔進行驗證。

6.9 Compressed Gas Monitoring

The use of compressed air and compressed gas in the aseptic ISO 5 environment may adversely affect environmental conditions if appropriate precautions, routine testing, and critical assurances are not developed into a system.

Using a 0.2 μm filter on the compressed gas line prior to entry into the cleanroom is common practice. Integrity testing of the filter and sanitization of the line post-filtration may reduce the need for sampling.

The sampling equipment must be compatible for use with compressed gases. A pre-filter may be installed in the system to reduce the bioburden to the point-of-use filter, extending its life and reducing the possibility of higher contamination reaching the area.

The particulate or bioburden level of any gas or air expelled into the environment should be equal to or better than the classification level of the room.

中文解析：壓縮氣體監控的關鍵控制點

壓縮氣體的污染風險

壓縮空氣與壓縮氣體（如氮氣、CO₂）直接噴入 ISO 5 無菌區域，若未適當過濾，可能成為微粒與微生物的引入途徑。

標準防護措施：

  • 進入潔淨室前安裝 0.2 μm 過濾器
  • 定期進行過濾器完整性測試（integrity testing）
  • 過濾後管路進行消毒（sanitization）
  • 安裝前置過濾器（pre-filter）降低最終過濾器的生物負載，延長使用壽命

核心法規要求

噴入環境的氣體或空氣，其微粒或生物負載水準必須等於或優於該房間的分類等級。例如在 ISO 5 區域使用的壓縮氣體，排放品質需達 ISO 5 或更優。

採樣設備必須與壓縮氣體相容——某些活性空氣採樣器可透過轉接頭同時適用於壓縮氣體取樣。

解讀與應用

為什麼先決條件如此重要？

環境監控不是「裝好設備就開始採樣」這麼簡單。TR13 明確列出 16 項前提作業，強調 EM 程序必須建立在完整的設施驗證、培養基資格確認、人員訓練等基礎之上，方能確保監控數據具有科學意義。

比喻：蓋房子的地基

16 項先決條件就如同蓋房子的地基工程。若地基不穩（如培養基未通過 GPT、採樣設備未校正、分析員訓練不足），後續的 EM 數據猶如建在沙上的建物——表面上有數據，但無法代表真實的環境狀況，更無法通過查廠審查。

色碼分類解讀

16 項先決條件可依性質分為四類：

  • 設施基礎（紅）：廠房設計、HVAC IQ/OQ、HEPA 驗證、氣流量測、PQ（第 1、10、11、14、15、16 項）
  • 培養基與菌株（橘）：供應商稽核、培養基選擇/放行/保存期、菌種選擇與維護（第 2–7 項）
  • 採樣作業流程（綠）：樣品保存運輸、培育策略驗證（第 8–9 項）
  • 設備與人員（藍）：設備資格確認、分析員訓練（第 12–13 項）

查廠常見缺失

FDA 483 觀察項目中，EM 先決條件相關缺失最常見的包括：

  • 培養基 GPT 未使用環境分離株（僅用 ATCC 標準菌株）
  • 樣品運輸時間/溫度未經保存研究驗證
  • 採樣設備（如撞擊式空浮採樣器）未定期校正流量
  • 分析員資格確認記錄不完整或未定期重新訓練

7.1 Facility Design

Separate or defined areas of operation in an aseptic processing facility should be appropriately controlled to attain different degrees of air quality depending on the nature of the operation. Design of a given area involves satisfying microbiological and particle criteria as defined by:

  • The equipment, components, and products exposed
  • The operational activities conducted in the area

Facility design is a crucial component of good environmental control.

ISPE Baseline Guide, Vol. 3: Sterile Product Manufacturing Facilities

Describes the design, construction, commissioning, and qualification of sterile manufacturing facilities. Similar baseline guides are available for other types of product manufacturing.

ISO 14698 (superseded by BS EN 17141:2020)

Two-part series providing extensive guidance on contamination control. ISO 14644-1 provides methods to assess control of air, surfaces, and liquid contamination.

解讀與應用

7.1 廠房設計：EM 的物理基礎

廠房設計決定了潔淨室能達到的環境等級上限。再好的 EM 程序，也無法補救設計缺陷造成的結構性污染風險。

重要標準更新

ISO 14698（潔淨室生物污染控制）已被 BS EN 17141:2020 取代。新舊版本的核心監控原則一致，但 17141:2020 更強調風險評估驅動的採樣計畫設計，與 TR13 整體精神相符。

廠房設計的 EM 相關要素

  • 壓差梯度：由高等級往低等級方向正壓，防止外部污染侵入
  • HEPA 過濾效率：ISO 5 / Grade A 區域須達 H14 等級（≥99.995%）
  • 換氣次數（ACH）：決定了空氣污染的稀釋與移除速率
  • 氣流型態：層流（laminar flow）vs. 亂流（turbulent flow）直接影響 EM 採樣點的代表性
  • 表面材質：可清潔性（cleanability）影響表面 EM 回收率

實務提示

新廠啟用前，ISPE Baseline Guide Vol. 3 的 commissioning & qualification (C&Q) 流程與 TR13 的 16 項先決條件形成完整閉環：廠房設計文件 → 建造驗收 → IQ/OQ/PQ → 基線採樣 → 正式 EM 程序啟動。

7.2 Selection of Microbiological Culture Media for EM Testing

Soybean-casein digest medium (SCDM) or trypticase soy agar (TSA) is the standard choice for EM, supporting growth of a wide range of bacteria, yeast, and molds.

Media Selection Decision Tree

  • SCDM/TSA — universal first choice; supports bacteria, yeast, and mold
  • + Neutralizing agents — add when surface monitoring is performed in areas exposed to sanitizing agents or antibiotics
  • Sabouraud Dextrose Agar (SDA) — use when fungal contamination is specifically suspected; note that SCDM already supports yeast/mold growth
  • Anaerobic media — consider only in special circumstances: risk assessment justification, prior recovery of anaerobes, or sterility / APS failure attributed to anaerobes
  • Tryptic soy broth (TSB) — used for swabs in Grade A environments (qualitative results only)

Initial Media Qualification Requirements

  • Validate fertility is not affected by desiccation at end of maximum allowed exposure period
  • Where neutralizers are required, confirm suitability for the applied disinfectants/agents
  • Confirm suitability over the supplier's full shelf life
  • Perform growth promotion testing (GPT) using low levels of microorganisms from the manufacturing environment and recognized culture collections
  • Document use of environmental isolates in an approved rationale

Routine Media Testing Requirements

  • Justify and document the QC strategy for EM media, including sterility checks and negative controls
  • Perform GPT on each delivery of a given batch
  • Consider reduced testing based on risk assessment, including supplier control confidence and supplier quality agreement (SQA)

解讀與應用

7.2 培養基選擇：EM 數據品質的核心

培養基的選擇與資格確認，直接決定了 EM 能偵測到的微生物種類與回收率。使用不適當的培養基，等同於用有盲點的探測器監測環境。

SCDM vs. SDA — 何時需要兩者並用？

TR13 指出 SCDM 本身已能支持酵母菌與黴菌生長。因此 SDA 僅作為補充選項，適用於懷疑特定真菌污染時。若藥廠常規上同時使用 SCDM 和 SDA，應在 SOP 中提供科學依據，並說明哪些情況下啟用 SDA。

若無特殊理由，避免在所有 EM 採樣點都使用雙培養基，因為這會大幅增加培養基成本與培育作業量。

中和劑（Neutralizers）的必要性

表面採樣（接觸碟或棉棒）時，消毒劑殘留（如 IPA、quaternary ammonium、過氧化氫等）可能抑制微生物生長，導致假陰性結果。因此：

  • 表面監控培養基應添加適當中和劑（如 lecithin + polysorbate 80 組合）
  • 中和劑本身需驗證：不抑制目標微生物、不殘留毒性
  • 中和劑的選擇應與現場實際使用的消毒劑品項相對應

培養基 GPT 的實務要求

TR13 強調 GPT 應使用「低接種量」（low levels）——通常為 <100 CFU，以模擬實際環境中微生物數量極低的情境。若以高接種量（>1000 CFU）進行 GPT，即使培養基效能不足，也可能得到表面上通過的結果。

GPT 菌株組合應包含：

  • 藥典要求的標準參考菌株（ATCC/NCTC）
  • 該廠環境分離株（environmental isolates）——需定期更新，反映當前菌相

7.3 Selection of Microorganisms for Growth Promotion

Selection of microorganisms for growth promotion should be established according to relevant pharmacopeial guidance, with consideration of USP <61>, based on the media to be evaluated.

Microorganisms selected should include:

  1. Those required by the appropriate compendial chapter (e.g., USP <61>)
  2. Representative environmental isolates — selected based on microbial profiling assessment using data from routine trending reports

For a new facility, organisms initially recovered from cleanroom qualification activities can be used as the environmental isolate component.

USP <1117> Microbiological Best Laboratory Practices

A microbiological culture maintenance program should be established that includes a procedure for testing and release with appropriate documentation of used passages.

解讀與應用

7.3 促生長微生物的選擇

GPT 菌株清單並非一次設定後永久不變。TR13 明確要求依據 趨勢報告（trending reports） 定期重新評估環境分離株的代表性。

GPT 菌株管理的生命週期

  • 建立期：使用藥典標準菌株 + 潔淨室資格確認時分離的菌株
  • 穩定期：依據例行 EM 趨勢，每年或每季評估是否需要更新環境分離株
  • 更新觸發點：出現新型菌種、環境分離株菌相改變、或超出 Action Level 的調查結果
  • 傳代管理：依 USP <1117> 記錄每次傳代次數，避免菌株特性漂移（strain drift）

常見缺失：只用藥典菌株

部分藥廠的 GPT SOP 僅列出 ATCC 標準菌株（如 Staphylococcus aureus ATCC 6538、Aspergillus brasiliensis ATCC 16404），而未納入環境分離株。

這不符合 TR13 和 USP <61> 的要求，在 FDA 查廠中屬於常見 483 觀察項目。原因是標準菌株不能代表廠房中實際存在的環境微生物對培養基的敏感性。

7.4 Sample Holding Studies and Transport

EM samples are sensitive to handling and storage conditions, which are deemed critical parameters. These conditions are affected by:

  • Time of sampling
  • Temperature of storage during transport

Sample hold studies should be performed to validate the sample handling from end of sampling through initial incubation.

Care should be used if transferring EM media using sanitizing airlocks (e.g., through sanitizing airlocks equipped with UV lights).

解讀與應用

7.4 樣品保存研究與運輸

採樣結束到培育開始的這段時間，是 EM 數據完整性的潛在漏洞。若未進行保存研究驗證，無法確保採樣時捕獲的微生物，在培育前仍保持存活且可計數。

保存研究的設計要點

  • 研究範圍：從「採樣結束」到「進入培育箱」的完整鏈路，包括在無菌室內的等待時間、通過氣閘時間、運輸至實驗室的時間
  • 溫度條件：應模擬最差情境（worst case），如夏季室溫或特殊生產區的高溫環境
  • 最長持有時間：應等同或超過實際作業中可能發生的最長間隔
  • 接受標準：樣品持有後的計數不應顯著低於立即培育的對照組

UV 氣閘的潛在風險

部分廠房的物料傳遞氣閘配備 UV 燈，用以對進出物料表面滅菌。TR13 特別警示：EM 培養基通過此類氣閘時，UV 輻射可能殺滅已捕獲的微生物，導致假陰性結果。應評估是否設計替代通道或在傳遞時關閉 UV 燈。

7.5 Environmental Monitoring Sample Incubation Strategy and Validation

Guidance documents and industry practices differ regarding incubation conditions. Information about appropriate incubation times and optimum temperature conditions is limited or sometimes conflicting.

Standard Incubation Conditions

Target Organisms Temperature Duration
Total aerobic bacterial count 30–35°C 48–72 hours
Total combined yeast and mold count 20–25°C 5–7 days
Slow-growing contaminants As applicable Extended (justified)

Industry Survey Finding

Industry surveys have shown a majority of companies using a two-temperature incubation with the lower temperature first (i.e., incubate at 20–25°C first, then transfer to 30–35°C).

Multiple Incubation Schemes

Multiple incubation schemes can be employed if scientifically justified and validated. Validation studies typically use:

  • Laboratory-based in vitro studies with cultured microorganisms
  • ATCC reference strains combined with in-house environmental isolates
Studies such as those published by Gordon et al. demonstrate that there may be a difference between in vitro and in situ testing (Reference 37).

解讀與應用

7.5 培育策略：業界最爭議的 EM 議題之一

培育溫度與時間的設計，直接影響 EM 偵測的靈敏度。業界目前存在多種不同做法，TR13 並不強制規定單一方案，而是要求「科學驗證」。

雙溫培育策略：為何先低溫後高溫？

行業調查顯示，多數公司採用「先 20–25°C（5–7 天）後 30–35°C（48–72 小時）」的順序。這樣做的科學依據：

  • 潔淨室環境中，酵母菌與黴菌通常比細菌更難偵測（生長較慢、菌落較小）
  • 先在低溫讓真菌充分生長，再升溫促進細菌生長，可降低細菌菌落覆蓋真菌的風險
  • 若先高溫後低溫，快速生長的細菌可能在培育盤上形成優勢，掩蓋真菌菌落

In Vitro vs. In Situ 的重要差異

Gordon et al. (Ref. 37) 的研究提醒我們：實驗室培育條件下的最佳化結果，不一定能直接預測現場環境樣品的表現。原因包括：

  • 環境微生物可能處於受損或亞致死（sublethally injured）狀態
  • 環境條件（消毒劑殘留、乾燥效應）影響菌株活力
  • 實驗室菌株（ATCC）活力通常優於實際環境分離株

因此，培育驗證研究建議同時納入「受壓力條件下的環境分離株」作為挑戰。

培育策略驗證的文件要求

無論採用何種培育方案，均需以驗證研究報告支持，並在 EM SOP 或方法驗證文件中記錄：

  • 所選溫度/時間組合的科學依據
  • 與替代方案的比較數據
  • 敏感度評估（能偵測到的最低菌量）
  • 變更管理：任何培育條件的修改都需重新驗證

7.6 Environmental Monitoring Equipment Qualification

Assessment may be required to determine gaps in equipment qualification. The following items should be considered as prerequisite:

  • Equipment and instrument listing — comprehensive inventory of all EM equipment
  • Documentation requirements — qualification protocols, certificates, maintenance records
  • Maintenance and calibration — including air volume accuracy requirements for active air samplers
  • Tube length requirement — for total airborne particulate monitoring equipment (particle counters)
  • Data collection, transmission and storage — alarm conditions and audit trail requirements
  • Installation and Operational Qualification (IQ/OQ) of EM equipment

解讀與應用

7.6 EM 設備資格確認

EM 設備資格確認（qualification）確保採集到的數據是可信的、可重現的，且可溯源至已知標準。

關鍵設備資格確認重點

主動式空浮採樣器（Active Air Samplers）

  • 採樣流量（通常 100 L/min）需定期以校正過的流量計（NIST 溯源）校正
  • 校正頻率應依使用頻率與製造商建議設定（通常每 6–12 個月）
  • 流量不準確 = 實際採樣體積不正確 = CFU/m³ 數值失真

粒子計數器（Particle Counters）

  • 採樣管（isokinetic probe）長度影響粒子計數準確性
  • 過長或彎曲過多的管路會造成大粒子沉積，導致計數偏低
  • ISO 21501-4 規定了粒子計數器的校正要求

數據完整性要求

現代 EM 系統越來越多採用電子數據採集與自動傳輸。TR13 特別提及 data collection, transmission, storage 和 alarm conditions，呼應 FDA 數據完整性指引（2018）的要求：

  • 電子記錄不可刪除或覆蓋（audit trail 必須啟用）
  • 警報條件（Alert/Action Level 觸發）應有自動記錄
  • 資料備份策略應列入 EM 系統驗證範圍

7.7 Microbiology Analyst or Operator Training and Qualification

Appropriate training curricula and qualification requirements should be established for EM analysts or operators. This entails:

  • Relevant, cohesive standard operating procedures (SOPs), including gowning and gowning qualification for aseptic areas
  • A good understanding of the procedures and their importance in aseptic manufacturing operations
  • Understanding of the impact or risk to quality for not adhering to these procedures
Analyst qualification is not merely procedural compliance — it reflects the analyst's understanding of why each step matters for product quality and patient safety.

解讀與應用

7.7 分析員訓練與資格確認

人員（analyst/operator）是 EM 最重要、也最容易被忽略的變數。再完善的培養基、設備和培育策略，都無法彌補採樣技術不當所引入的人為誤差。

EM 分析員資格確認程序

  • 初始訓練：EM 原理 → 各採樣方法 SOP → 設備操作 → 無菌技術
  • 更衣資格確認（Gowning Qualification）：在無菌區域採樣前，必須通過無菌更衣技術評估（包含 fingertip / glove impression plates）
  • 採樣技術確認：實際觀察採樣操作，確認技術符合 SOP（例如接觸碟的施力、棉棒採樣區域、設備放置位置）
  • 定期再訓練：建議每年重新評估，或在程序變更後觸發再訓練
  • 記錄保存：訓練矩陣（training matrix）需完整記錄，並可供查廠稽核

微生物學家的「風險意識」

TR13 特別強調分析員需理解「不遵守程序的品質風險與影響」。這不只是被動執行 SOP，而是主動理解每個步驟的 GMP 背景——例如：

  • 為何採樣後不能讓培養基在室溫下放置超過規定時間？
  • 接觸碟打開的瞬間，無菌技術不當可能引入什麼污染？
  • 超出 Action Level 時，第一時間應採取什麼行動？

9.0 Appendix 1: Qualification of Cleanrooms (Classified Areas)

Purpose, Scope & Procedure Overview — p43–p44

9.1 Purpose & 9.2 Scope

This appendix provides a consistent methodology for qualification of cleanrooms (classified areas) used for the manufacture, processing, packaging, holding, and testing of sterile drug products, biologics, and medical devices for human use.

EU and WHO designations for cleanroom classifications are used throughout (Grades A–D), with ISO equivalents added where appropriate.

Scope focus: Activities necessary to provide a high degree of assurance that cleanrooms meet requirements for product quality. Formal qualification (GMP) should center on:

  • Final HEPA filtration (including ductwork beyond the final filter)
  • Monitoring of area parameters to ensure cleanroom requirements are met

HVAC systems (air handlers, pre-filters, controls) have an indirect impact on product quality and should be commissioned rather than formally qualified under GMP protocols.

In scope: Isolators and unidirectional (laminar) airflow units providing Grade A air within a larger cleanroom.

Out of scope: Freezers, refrigerators, stability chambers, and sterilization tunnels (though test methods for airflow, filter testing, and air classification may be directly applied).

中文解說 Commentary

潔淨室資格鑑定的三段式架構

PDA TR13 附錄 1 將潔淨室的資格鑑定區分為三個遞進階段，這與製藥設備驗證的通用框架一致：

  • IQ（安裝資格確認）：確認設備已按規格安裝、零件正確、儀器校正完畢。
  • OQ（運行資格確認）：在「靜態」（at rest）條件下測試環境參數是否達標（如粒子數、差壓、溫濕度）。
  • PQ（性能資格確認）：在「動態」（in operation，有人員、設備、常規作業）條件下確認環境持續符合要求。

比喻：潔淨室資格鑑定如同新建大樓驗屋

IQ 如同確認建材規格與施工圖一致（鋼筋用對了嗎？管路位置正確嗎？）

OQ 如同空屋狀態下測試水電空調是否正常運作（空屋開冷氣，溫度能降到設定值嗎？）

PQ 如同實際入住後確認日常使用無問題（住了人、開了電器，冷氣還夠用嗎？）

GMP 的核心在於：不能光靠設計圖保證品質，必須透過實測數據來確認。

重點：HVAC 委託調試 vs. GMP 正式驗證

TR13 明確區分「commissioning（委託調試）」與「qualification（資格鑑定）」：

  • 風機轉速、預過濾器、線圈性能 → commissioning 即可
  • 末端 HEPA 過濾器、粒子監控 → 需要正式 GMP qualification

此區分反映「直接影響（direct impact）vs. 間接影響（indirect impact）」對產品品質的風險差異。

9.3.1

9.3.1 Installation Qualification (IQ)

Critical Component Checklist — p44–p45

IQ Requirements Checklist

IQ verifies that critical system components meet user requirements and have been properly identified, installed, and calibrated. IQ requirements may be confirmed through inspection or by referencing commissioning/FAT documentation.

Items to address during IQ include:

  • Description, location, and use of cleanrooms/classified areas to be qualified
  • Verification of type, range, and placement of sensors and alarms for environmental parameters having a direct effect on product quality
  • Verification of location of final system filters for areas with airborne viable and/or total particulate limitations (Grades A–D)
  • Verification of material and proper installation of ductwork downstream of the final HEPA filter
  • Completion of a commissioning plan, including a test and balance report, approved by the system user and responsible engineering group
  • Verification of critical system components: terminal air filters, instruments, and alarms — identifying manufacturer, model number, size/range/efficiency, and materials
  • Verification of instrument calibration certifications in compliance with site calibration procedures
  • Software/firmware title, version, and installation verification (may be in separate RMS protocol)
  • Transfer of equipment/instrument information to computerized maintenance management system (CMMS)
  • Confirmation that maintenance procedures and/or manuals are in place

  • 更換 HEPA 過濾器：只需測氣流速度/分布、過濾器差壓、洩漏測試、房間分類 — 不需重測溫濕度或差壓。

「At rest（靜態）」指：HVAC 設備安裝並已調試完成、其他設備就位並運作、但無操作人員（除必要測試人員外）、無產品或物料。

這確保 OQ 測試結果反映 HVAC 系統本身的能力，排除人員活動的干擾（人員影響留給 PQ 評估）。

FMEA 的核心思維：預測哪裡會失效

FMEA（失效模式與效應分析）的問題是：「如果這個要求沒有做到，會發生什麼？多嚴重？有多可能？我們能偵測到嗎？」

TR13 的 EM FMEA 特別設計了三個評分維度：

  • SEV（嚴重性）：偏差對無菌性和法規合規的衝擊
  • OCC（發生率）：該位置實際上被微生物污染的可能性
  • DET（可偵測性）：現有 EM 計畫能否偵測到潛在污染

注意：DET 分數越高代表越難偵測（風險越高），這與某些 FMEA 框架中 DET 越低越危險的定義相反，讀者需注意各組織的定義。

RPN 解讀範例

Item 1（Stopper bowl 缺乏監控）:
  SEV=3 × OCC=2 × DET=2 = RPN 12 → MAJOR

Item 4（採樣時機符合要求）:
  SEV=3 × OCC=1 × DET=1 = RPN  3 → MINOR

差異解析:
- SEV 都是 3（一旦出事都很嚴重）
- OCC: Stopper bowl 有多次介入 → 污染機率較高 (2)
         採樣程序完善 → 污染機率低 (1)
- DET: 無監控 → 難偵測 (2)
         有監控 → 易偵測 (1)

重要觀察：RABS Stopper Bowl 是常見缺口

Items 1 和 8 都指向同一個問題：RABS stopper bowl（停塞碗）缺乏監控。這是一個 MAJOR 風險，因為：

  • Stopper bowl 是間接產品接觸面（stopper 會進入瓶內）
  • 操作人員在附近進行多次介入動作
  • 若無主動空氣採樣和表面監控，污染事件可能被遺漏

這提醒我們：即使是「關閉式」RABS，也不代表所有位置都已納入 EM 計畫——必須系統性地用 FMEA 審查每個高風險位置。

BioPhorum 模型的創新之處在於將填充線分解成一個 4×4 網格（16 個區域），讓風險評估從定性的描述轉化為可量化的地圖（heat map）。

為什麼「鄰近開放產品（O）」權重要×2？

六個風險因子中，唯有「鄰近開放產品（Proximity to open product, O）」被賦予雙倍權重。理由：

  • 其他因子（清潔難度、人員流動等）是間接風險
  • 空氣中的微粒/微生物只有在直接鄰近開放產品或產品接觸面時，才有立即污染無菌產品的機會
  • 因此，即使一個區域介入次數少，只要它緊鄰開放產品，風險分數就會顯著提高

  • 清潔困難（C=4，難以到達的死角）
  • 距開放產品 <12 英寸（O=8，雙倍權重）
  • 需要多次手動介入排除瓶塞卡頓（CI=2, FI=4）

這表明此區域需要最嚴密的 EM 監控，且可能需要重新設計以降低介入頻率。

HACCP Definition & Seven Principles (Figure 10.1-1)

HACCP (Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points) is a formal qualitative risk assessment technique designed to identify critical steps in a process that require control and monitoring to ensure hazards are not realized. For EM purposes, the hazards analyzed are:

  • Biological: viable monitoring (microbial contamination)
  • Physical: total airborne particulates

HACCP Seven Principles — Applied to EM Program Design

  1. Conduct a Hazard Analysis
    Identify all potential sources of microbial and particulate contamination in the manufacturing area; assess their likelihood and severity.
  2. Determine Critical Control Points (CCPs)
    Identify locations where monitoring is critical to prevent or detect contamination events. Not every location is a CCP — use a structured decision tree (Q1–Q4).
  3. Establish Critical Limits
    Define the EM alert levels and action limits (viable & non-viable) for each CCP location.
  4. Establish Monitoring Procedures
    Define what will be monitored, how often, and by whom at each CCP.
  5. Establish Corrective Actions
    Define the investigation and CAPA response when critical limits are exceeded (i.e., when alert or action levels are exceeded).
  6. Establish Verification Procedures
    Confirm that the HACCP system is working effectively — e.g., trend analysis, periodic HACCP review, and EM data review.
  7. Establish Record-Keeping and Documentation
    Maintain records of HACCP decisions, monitoring results, and CAPAs.

HACCP 的核心問題：「哪裡是關鍵控制點？」

HACCP 的精髓在於用「決策樹」篩選出關鍵控制點（CCP）。並非每個潛在危害點都是 CCP——HACCP 透過四個問題（Q1–Q4）進行篩選：

  • Q1：此位置的採樣是否為 cGMP 要求？
  • Q2：此位置的採樣是否對保護患者安全或產品品質至關重要？
  • Q3：此位置的監控數據是否能支持穩健的偏差調查？
  • Q4：綜合以上，此位置是否為 CCP？

在 Table 10.1-1 的 Grade C 細胞培養室範例中，所有分析的危害點（Q4）最終都被判定為 CCP，但 HACCP 的價值在於能夠區分出哪些位置可以降低監控頻率（低風險 + 無歷史問題）。

HACCP vs. FMEA：方法論差異

TR13 同時介紹 FMEA 和 HACCP，兩者目標相似但邏輯不同：

面向 FMEA HACCP
起點 潛在失效模式 危害識別
量化方式 RPN = SEV×OCC×DET 定性：可能性 × 嚴重性 → 風險等級
輸出重點 需要 CAPA 的風險項目清單 需要嚴格監控的關鍵控制點（CCP）
食品安全起源 汽車/航太工程起源 食品安全（NASA/Pillsbury）起源
優勢 能比較不同失效模式的相對風險 能明確識別「必須嚴格管控」的節點

TR13 的立場是：兩種方法都可用於 EM 計畫設計，企業可根據自身文化選擇，關鍵是要有系統化的風險評估文件。

比喻：HACCP 如同機場安全流程設計

機場安全不是在每個角落都放安全人員，而是識別「關鍵控制點」（登機門、安全檢查站）並在那裡集中管控。HACCP 的邏輯相同：不是到處採樣，而是在真正關鍵的地方做最嚴格的監控。

反之，FMEA 更像是對整個機場系統做「如果......會怎樣？」的故障排查，找出所有可能的漏洞並優先修補最嚴重的。
