  • 理解 COVID-19 大流行對製藥製造業造成的多層次衝擊，包括設施停工、供應鏈中斷與品質系統挑戰。

Introduction & Scope — 導論與範疇
Original Text — English

Introduction

As a result of the COVID-19 pandemic, many industries–the pharmaceutical industry included–went through a substantial disruption. Some of these disruptions included facility shutdowns, unprecedented supply chain challenges, worker availability, and quality system limitations (e.g., physical signatures, investigations, document originals, risk assessments, audits). For many pharmaceutical companies, challenges increased as they raced to provide a vaccine or a therapeutic treatment for COVID-19. Their manufacturing operations were changed over to accommodate the creation of the vaccine and, in order to get the vaccine to patients quickly, many logistical challenges arose.

As the pandemic has progressed, our understanding of the virus, its control, and treatment options have changed and advanced. When reading this document, it is important to remember that its intent is to describe the lessons learned for future benefit, especially at times when the understanding of the virus and treatment options were extremely limited. Even today, as we enter what is believed to be the last phase of the pandemic, moving into an endemic state, it is difficult to comprehend the amount of anxiety and risk that existed during the initial 12 to 16 months. During that time, some types of government-imposed state of emergency were mandated in many countries that restricted the movement of individuals and closed nonessential businesses. Hospitals operated at capacity, a significant number of Covid-related deaths occurred, and a limited amount of personal protective equipment (e.g., mask, gloves) was available.

The specifics of the pandemic or the COVID-19 virus are not detailed here, as that information is widely available. One point that does need to be made is that this pandemic, like the pandemic of 1918, went through waves, and the exact timing of each wave and the number of waves experienced varied by country. These waves were not unexpected but how the operation of manufacturing facilities should react to these first waves was not immediately clear; that developed over time. This document intends to convey the understanding and evolution of thought in response to the new data and observations presented as we worked our way through 2019, 2020, 2021, and 2022.

Scope

This document applies lessons learned from the COVID-19 pandemic to pharmaceutical manufacturing. Areas covered include manufacturing, people, quality, regulatory, and supply chain.

Q1-1: Are GMP controls sufficient to protect the product? — GMP 管控是否足以保護產品？
Original Text — English

Background

Due to the novelty of the coronavirus, at the start of the pandemic, the potential impact to the safety and quality of the drugs produced as a result of infected manufacturing employees was uncertain. The virus was found to spread very efficiently, which resulted in a much higher percentage of the population being infected than with common or seasonal viral infections. As a result, the risk of a manufacturing process being exposed to the coronavirus was considered to be higher than usual, depending on the process.

Therefore, most drug manufacturers moved quickly to perform risk assessments to evaluate the established good manufacturing practices (GMP) controls in their manufacturing facilities with respect to the new potential contaminant. The expectation of risk mitigation was also later reflected in a COVID-19-specific guidance published by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) that stated manufacturers were expected to perform these risk assessments to ensure that product quality and safety, including employees' safety, are not impacted due to the pandemic.

Fortunately for the industry, careful implementation of GMP principles and practices are likely to protect product against the SARS-CoV-2 virus that causes COVID-19, as well as mitigate the risk caused by asymptomatic SARS-CoV-2-infected personnel. Some of these GMP controls include:

  • General hygiene measures and procedures in case of illness, feeling sick, open lesions, or sneezing and coughing
  • Procedures for thorough handwashing and subsequent hand sanitization before entering production areas, and regular glove disinfection during production
  • Use of environmentally controlled rooms within GMP-classified areas with specific gowning requirements (e.g., cleanroom gowns, overalls, lab coats, gloves, mask, gowning procedures) based on the operation performed
  • Restriction of access to production areas to authorized personnel only
  • Frequent cleaning cycles of the manufacturing areas and process equipment
  • Design of general process equipment to ensure manufacturing areas are as closed as possible to prevent the potential entry of contamination
  • Use of closed systems and, where product is exposed, use of isolators, restricted access barrier systems (RABS), and HEPA-filtered laminar airflow to protect the product
  • Frequent cleaning cycles of the area and of the processing equipment to further prevent contamination from spreading
  • Environmental and personnel monitoring to ensure strict adherence to GMP processes
  • Use of air cascades and pressure differentials to allow air to flow from critical to less-critical areas
  • Production flow in facilities designed to allow efficient movement and control of materials, personnel, products, equipment, and waste to prevent contamination
  • Use of gamma-irradiated, single-use process equipment interconnected with aseptic connections and typically designated as functionally closed systems
  • Stainless-steel equipment processes typically being cleaned and connected, followed by an in-situ steam sterilization (SIP) cycle
  • Process venting points outfitted with hydrophobic-membrane filters that act as barriers to potential ingress into the process

Additional considerations when working with mammalian cell lines:

  • Industry is expected to conduct adventitious-agent testing of a typical panel at the end of production cell bank. Additional risk assessment may need to be considered for banks manufactured after 2019.
  • Measures to prevent and avoid viral cross-contamination during processing are well established, including deactivation and removal of viruses. The large size of the COVID-19 virus makes it a prime candidate for removal by a 20-nm viral filter. Reports of the stability of COVID-19 over a wide range of pH values creates some potential concern around the effectiveness of a low-pH step for viral deactivation; thus, relying fully on this step for clearance is not recommended. Alternatively, detergent or solvent deactivation is effective.

Learnings

The emergence of a pandemic or similar disaster must mobilize industry to assess the continued ability of their processes and quality systems to protect product quality and patient safety. Industry must be prepared to rapidly perform such assessments in the future when a similar situation emerges. In the case of SARS-CoV-2, manufacturing processes employing strict GMP adherence in their design, personnel controls, and the environments in which they are performed likely present an extremely low risk for possible SARS-CoV-2 product contamination. In most cases, when conforming to existing GMP, no additional measures are necessary to protect product and patient. In the event of an employee operating within the GMP manufacturing environment testing positive, a specific risk assessment should be performed.

References: FDA Guidance for Industry: GMP Considerations for Responding to COVID-19 Infection in Employees in Drug and Biological Products Manufacturing (2020). Chin et al., Lancet Microbe 2020; Michen & Graule, J Appl Microbiol 2010; Miriam et al., Transfusion 2006.

核心結論：GMP 的設計本身就是最好的防護

這個問題的答案讓業界鬆了一口氣：現有的優良製造規範 (Good Manufacturing Practice，GMP) 管控措施，對 SARS-CoV-2 具有充分的防護效果。

這意味著大多數製藥廠無需因疫情而大規模改造設施——只需確實執行既有的 GMP 要求即可。

類比：GMP 潔淨室就像「防毒護盾」

GMP 製造環境本來是為了防止細菌、黴菌等微生物污染藥品而設計。COVID-19 病毒（直徑約 100 奈米）遠比細菌大，因此現有的潔淨室設計——包括 HEPA 過濾層流、密閉系統、壓差管理——對其有更強的屏蔽效果。

就像一個專門防蟑螂的密封容器，同樣能防止更大的老鼠進入。

CDMO 實務重點：七大防護層

  • 人員控制：感染症狀者禁入生產區、強制洗手消毒
  • 環境管控：ISO 分級潔淨室 + 穿著規定
  • 氣流設計：正壓梯度從關鍵區到非關鍵區（空氣僅能往外流）
  • 封閉系統：隔離器 (Isolator) / RABS + 伽瑪射線滅菌一次性設備
  • 清潔週期：頻繁清潔製造區與製程設備
  • 環境與人員監測：確保 GMP 流程嚴格遵從
  • 病毒過濾：20 奈米病毒過濾器可有效截留 COVID-19（約 100 奈米）

哺乳類細胞株製程的額外考量

外來因子測試 (adventitious-agent testing)：2019 年後建立的細胞庫需重新評估風險。

低 pH 病毒去活化的限制：COVID-19 在廣泛 pH 範圍下具穩定性，因此不應單純依賴低 pH 步驟作為病毒清除的唯一屏障。建議改用清潔劑或溶劑去活化作為替代或補充方案。

對於生物藥 CDMO（如單株抗體製造），這一點在開發病毒清除驗證策略時尤為重要。

思考練習

如果您的工廠有員工確診 COVID-19，依照本文件建議，下一步應執行什麼？

答：針對該員工的具體工作情境進行個案風險評估 (risk assessment)，分析、辨識並評估可能的危害——並非全廠立即停工。這體現了基於風險的 GMP 思維。

Q1-2: How do you develop a mRNA/gene therapy drug product rapidly (Part 1)? — 如何快速開發 mRNA／基因療法產品（上）？
Original Text — English

Background

Using messenger RNA (mRNA) to encode proteins for vaccination or protein replacement received its first in-vivo validation in 1990, when Wolff et al. demonstrated the production of a target protein after intramuscular injection in mice. The exact advantages of using mRNA products were discovered later, including:

  • Allowing human post-translational modification of protein products with the potential for less immunogenicity and full functionality
  • Permitting multimeric proteins that are not amendable to production in a bioreactor to be translated, folded, and assembled correctly in the patient's cells
  • Producing transmembrane and intracellular proteins and trafficking them to their appropriate site in the cellular environment

Learnings

The preparation of plasmid DNA (pDNA) is the first step in the production of mRNA, where functional synthetic mRNA is obtained by the in-vitro transcription of a pDNA template. The pDNA template is transcribed into the mRNA mixture containing RNA polymerase and nucleoside triphosphates. Then, a cap may be performed during the reaction, or it can be added enzymatically post-transcription. Capping improves translation initiation by recruiting translation-initiation factors, thereby protecting the synthetic mRNA against degradation. Post-transcription, the pDNA template and bacterial DNA is digested by DNase. To be used as a drug substance, mRNA has to be purified to remove contaminating transcripts, which can be done by using a single chromatographic step that will separate the mRNA based on size and result in a pure, single mRNA product.

Two forms of mRNA are being studied for use in vaccines: nonreplicating (traditional) mRNA and self-amplifying mRNA. The traditional mRNA form presents two advantages: the molecule is quite simple and small, and the possibility of an unwanted immune response is lowered because there are no other proteins encoded. However, the traditional mRNA form is limited by its transient nature. The advantage of self-amplifying mRNA is that it performs self-replication and creates high levels of the antigen not found with traditional mRNA. The self-amplifying RNA vaccine was found to show a 64-fold lower dose required for an immunologic response compared to its mRNA counterpart.

References: Wolff et al., Science 1990; Barbier et al., Nat Biotechnol 2022.

Additional Reading: Rosa et al., Vaccine 2021; Fang et al., Signal Transduction 2022; CDC Understanding COVID-19 Vaccines; Schlake et al., RNA Biol 2012; FDA Gene Therapy Products Guidance 2022.

mRNA 疫苗的歷史脈絡

mRNA 技術並非「新科技」——它的基礎研究可追溯至 1990 年。COVID-19 疫苗的快速問世，是三十年基礎研究積累的成果，而非一夕之間的奇蹟。

這一點對 CDMO 策略規劃至關重要：投資於尖端技術的平台能力，在緊急需求出現時，可以迅速轉化為商業機會。

mRNA 製造流程類比：從「設計圖」到「說明書」

想像藥廠是一家出版社：

  • 質體 DNA (pDNA)：原始設計藍圖（模板）
  • 體外轉錄 (in-vitro transcription)：複印設計圖成為工作手冊（mRNA）
  • 加帽 (capping)：在手冊封面加上「防偽標誌」，防止手冊被銷毀
  • DNase 消化：銷毀原始設計圖，只保留手冊
  • 層析純化：只挑出「合格」的手冊，丟棄破損版本

最終交給細胞的「手冊」就是純化後的 mRNA 原料藥 (drug substance)。

CDMO 業務思考

mRNA 製造包含高度專業化步驟（體外轉錄、加帽、層析純化），且對 RNase 污染極為敏感——這對廠房設計、人員訓練與設備清潔均有特殊要求。貴廠目前是否具備這些能力？建立 mRNA 平台的優先順序如何？

傳統 mRNA vs. 自我增殖 mRNA：製造決策的關鍵差異

對 CDMO 而言，自我增殖 mRNA 的「64 倍低劑量」代表：相同產能可服務更多患者，但製造難度與法規複雜度也相應提升。

Q1-3: How do you develop a mRNA/gene therapy drug product rapidly (Part 2)? — 如何快速開發 mRNA／基因療法產品（下）？
Original Text — English

Background

Gene therapy products seek to insert genetic material into a patient's cells to treat or prevent a specific disease state. There are several types of gene-therapy products, which includes plasmid DNA, viral vectors, bacterial vectors, human gene-editing technology, and patient-derived cellular gene therapy. In much the same way, mRNA therapy products seek to insert mRNA material into a patient's cells to induce the production of a specific protein to treat or prevent a specific disease state.

The COVID-19 mRNA vaccine is made of mRNA and, to allow introduction into the cell, is encompassed by lipid nanoparticles (LNP). Once in the cells, it works by instructing the cells to make a protein that will trigger an immune response to COVID-19. While there is no specific formula for the creation of a successful mRNA LNP vaccine, the mRNA LNP design is characterized by three main elements:

  • mRNA sequence design and nucleotide modification choice
  • Optimization of a LNP formulation to encapsulate and deliver mRNA
  • Long-term storage

The first step in developing a mRNA therapy product is focusing on the API (active pharmaceutical ingredient). Considerations for designing or choosing the mRNA sequence should include the expression of encoded mRNA, immunogenicity due to recognition of mRNA as a foreign entity by intracellular RNA sensors, and mRNA stability. Nucleotide modification is equally important, as nonmodified mRNA molecules are recognized by the cellular RNA sensors, which nulls the effect of their expression. The translation efficiency and intracellular-mRNA stability can be increased substantially by mRNA capping. Additionally, the decay and translation efficiency are greatly affected by the untranslated region (UTR) selection as well as the open-reading-frames (ORF) sequence design.

The purpose of the LNP formulation is to design a nanoparticle that will shield the mRNA from degradation and enable efficient delivery into targeted cells. These formulations typically comprise a neutral structural lipid, cholesterol, a PEG-lipid, and an ionizable lipid. The ionizable lipid is incredibly important for enhancing the expression and provides the most-optimal immune response. The PEG-lipid component is helpful in increasing the shelf life of the solution by preventing aggregation. Considerations for PEG-lipids used in these formulations should take into account that, at high concentrations, they can prevent delivery of the mRNA.

Throughout the process of formulation, long-term storage and stability should be major considerations in design. The ultra-low storage requirement currently approved for mRNA vaccines due to instability at room temperature is a system constraint. To freeze LNPs, a cryoprotectant such as sucrose or trehalose should be added to the formulation.

Lessons Learned

In the case of a future pandemic, the availability of global and private funding to advance the development of research for a potential vaccine will again be critical. Governmental agencies globally should develop or revise plans related to the emergency funding of such research should the need arise.

While the most desired mRNA vaccine would be one that could be provided in a lyophilized or a liquid formulation stored under refrigerated or room-temperature conditions, such formulations can take significant periods of time to develop, if even possible. Under pandemic conditions, when a vaccine is needed quickly, the initial use of an ultra-low storage condition should be considered as it removes many of the formulation challenges that would otherwise need to be overcome.

References: ASGCT Staff 2020; CDC Understanding mRNA COVID-19 Vaccines; Kon et al., Curr Opin Biotechnol 2022.

LNP 的四種成分：各司其職的「快遞包裝」

脂質奈米顆粒 (lipid nanoparticle，LNP) 是 mRNA 疫苗的關鍵遞送系統，缺少任一成分都會導致失敗：

  • 可離子化脂質 (ionizable lipid)：核心功能——在細胞內的酸性環境中帶正電，吸附帶負電的 mRNA，並幫助 mRNA 從核內體 (endosome) 中釋放。
  • 中性結構脂質 (neutral structural lipid)：維持顆粒結構穩定性。
  • 膽固醇 (cholesterol)：協助細胞膜融合，增強遞送效率。
  • PEG 脂質 (PEG-lipid)：防止顆粒聚集，延長保存期限——但濃度過高會阻礙 mRNA 遞送，需精確調控。

類比：LNP 就像「防水快遞盒」

mRNA 分子非常脆弱，接觸環境中的 RNase 酶就會迅速降解。LNP 的作用就像是：

  • 防水外殼（避免 RNase 破壞）
  • 地址標籤（引導到目標細胞）
  • 開鎖鑰匙（在細胞內釋放 mRNA）

沒有 LNP，mRNA 疫苗根本無法到達細胞核執行任務。

超低溫儲存的製造影響

mRNA 疫苗的超低溫儲存需求（-70°C 至 -80°C）不是理想選擇，而是現實妥協——因為在緊急情況下，「快速上市」的優先順序高於「方便儲存」。

對 CDMO 的製造影響：

  • 需要超低溫充填線與儲存設施
  • 冷凍保護劑 (cryoprotectant)（如蔗糖、海藻糖）必須加入配方
  • 解凍程序需標準化，否則 LNP 聚集會影響效力
  • 凍乾 (lyophilization) 配方雖是長期目標，但開發時間長，不適合緊急應對

核苷酸修飾：讓細胞「認可」mRNA

未修飾的 mRNA 會被細胞內的 RNA 感測器識別為「外來威脅」，觸發免疫反應而降解——這正是早期 mRNA 療法失敗的主因之一。

Karikó 和 Weissman（2023 年諾貝爾生理醫學獎得主）的關鍵突破，就是發現用修飾核苷酸（如假尿苷）取代天然鹼基，可以避開細胞的免疫防禦，讓 mRNA 正常表現蛋白質。

配方開發的策略思考

本文建議：在緊急疫情下，優先採用超低溫液態配方快速上市，不要為了追求室溫配方而延誤時間。這一原則是否可以應用於您公司的新藥開發決策中？什麼情況下可以接受較嚴苛的儲存條件以換取更快上市？

Q1-4: How did the route of administration impact manufacturing operations? — 給藥途徑如何影響製造作業？
Original Text — English

Background

The COVID-19 vaccine is considered a biologic parenteral drug formulated as a solution with the route of administration being sterile injections. Therefore, manufacturing of a parental drug is much more complex than the production of solid oral dosage forms such as tablets for oral ingestion. Apart from the significant differences in manufacturing a solution versus a tablet, there are many more controls required to ensure the product remains entirely sterile, including the control and use of sterile components such as vials and stoppers.

A vital step for these types of parenteral drugs that cannot be terminally sterilized is the aseptic fill–finish process in which solution is filled into containers, such as vials, and sealed with an aseptic closure, such as a stopper, as the final step before the product is packaged and shipped for patients. For several of the vaccine products developed, rapid freezing and long-term, ultra-low storage was also required.

The formulation of a drug product can have a significant impact on the sterile fill–finish capabilities of the system. For instance, a product may require different sizes and composition of filters (membranes) to achieve sterilization of the product. Different characteristics of the drug product will also affect the parameters of the system used to fill the vials, such as flow rate and pressure. In many cases, the filling process requires specialized equipment due to specific product characteristics, such as solubility, viscosity, and the tendency of a product formulation to foam.

The fill–finish of millions of doses of vaccines is no small task; it requires significant manufacturing capacity, technical capabilities, and rigorous quality-control measures to ensure product quality. To achieve the volume of production needed, smaller pharmaceutical companies developing the vaccine teamed up with larger companies that had the capacity and technical expertise needed to produce it. Moderna, for example, utilized the sterile fill–finish capabilities of the contract manufacturer Thermo Fisher Scientific to aid in this incredibly important part of the manufacturing process. BioNTech partnered with Pfizer, an expert in the manufacture of sterile drug products.

Lessons Learned

One very valuable learning point during the pandemic was the importance and willingness of companies to partner with one another to rapidly develop the manufacturing process needed for vaccine production. In the event of a future pandemic, this will again be a crucial factor in the speed with which a novel vaccine can be commercialized.

References: Higgins-Dunn, Fierce Pharma; Etherington, TechCrunch 2020.

核心概念：注射劑 vs. 口服錠劑的製造複雜度

COVID-19 疫苗是非腸道注射用生物製劑 (biologic parenteral drug)——這一給藥途徑 (route of administration) 的選擇，直接決定了製造作業的複雜程度：

  • 口服錠劑：可在非無菌環境製造，生產速度快
  • 注射用溶液：必須全程無菌，無法終端滅菌，需要無菌充填封裝 (aseptic fill-finish)

這意味著每一支疫苗的充填、封蓋，都必須在 ISO 5 等級（等同 Class 100）的潔淨環境下完成。

類比：無菌充填就像「在真空中裝配精密手錶」

製造口服錠劑就像在一般廚房烤蛋糕——對環境要求相對寬鬆，最後還可以「烤熟」（終端滅菌）。

但無菌充填就完全不同了：你必須在一個密封的無菌手套箱（隔離器）中，用機器人手臂，將每一滴液體精準灌入已滅菌的玻璃瓶，蓋上已滅菌的橡皮塞——全程不能有任何污染機會，而且無法事後再「滅菌補救」。

CDMO 的策略機遇：疫情揭示的市場空缺

Moderna（mRNA 開發商）沒有自己的無菌充填設施，必須委託 Thermo Fisher Scientific。BioNTech 同樣需要借助 Pfizer 的製造能力。

這一現象揭示了一個重要的 CDMO 策略洞察：具備高品質無菌充填封裝能力的 CDMO，是下一次大流行的關鍵基礎設施。

充填參數（流速、壓力）與配方特性（黏度、起泡性、溶解度）高度相關，這要求 CDMO 不只有硬體，還需要深厚的製程開發 (process development) 能力。

製造參數的配方依賴性

LNP 配方的充填有其特殊挑戰：

  • 過濾器選擇：LNP 顆粒大小（約 80–100 nm）影響過濾膜孔徑選擇
  • 黏度：影響充填泵的流速設定
  • 起泡性：LNP 界面活性劑成分易起泡，需特殊充填頭設計
  • 超低溫儲存：充填後需立即快速冷凍，需專用冷凍設備

合作夥伴策略思考

疫情最重要的教訓之一是：企業必須願意跨越競爭壁壘，快速建立製造夥伴關係。您的 CDMO 目前是否已建立可以迅速激活的合作網絡（上游 API 廠商、下游充填廠、冷鏈物流夥伴）？在下一次緊急需求出現前，這些關係能多快轉化為實際產能？

Q1-5: Can vaccine manufacturing capacities be scaled up rapidly? — 疫苗製造產能能否快速擴充？
Original Text — English

Background

Technology is rapidly evolving. Since 1990, when mRNA was first used in vivo, decades of research have gone into understanding mRNA–LNPs, so much was already known about mRNA before the start of the 2020 pandemic. Consequently, when the need for a COVID-19 vaccine arose, it did not take nearly as long as it would have if researchers were starting from scratch. Moderna's vaccine took only two days to finalize the sequence, 25 days to manufacture the first dose, and 63 days to be made available for the first patient to be dosed in a Phase 1 Clinical Study. Although the vaccine was made quickly, it was held to rigorous standards by the FDA.

To be effective, scaling up manufacturing of the novel drug product required a platform technology approach and available facilities in which to perform the operations.

Without considering process validation and regulatory approval timelines, the design, construction, and installation of a typical manufacturing facility requires a minimum of two to three years. In the case of COVID-19, however, the capacities needed to be scaled up much more rapidly. In 2010, the U.S. government started an initiative to fund sites that could be activated either for other vaccine purposes or to fulfill the needs of a pandemic-vaccine requirement. Unfortunately, when these sites were activated, they were not able to fulfill the mission for which they had been created.

Based on the need to respond quickly, approaches were leveraged to bring new facilities online faster:

  • The use of standard designs for cleanroom infrastructure by suppliers significantly abbreviated design timelines
  • A repeat build and qualification strategy reduced normal facility construction timelines considerably
  • Use of off-site, prefabricated, modular cleanroom infrastructures — as was the case in the Pfizer Kalamazoo site — halved the construction timeline and allowed prequalification within a high-beam shell building, like a warehouse

Some companies, such as Thermo Fisher Scientific, scaled up their manufacturing efforts quickly to meet the unprecedented demand. Initially, Thermo Fisher scaled up the manufacture of raw materials, relying on their current working supply of nucleotides and enzymes for early therapeutics development, which required no infrastructure changes. The company also built a 57,000-sq. ft. facility to manufacture the needed enzymes and nucleotides at industrial-scale levels using a platform approach.

Lessons Learned

The pandemic situation emphasized the need for pharmaceutical companies and regulatory authorities to be prepared to encourage facility design, construction, and process installations that leverage and expedite innovation and the adoption of new technology with the goal of bringing the manufacturing facilities online rapidly.

For example, the use of modular facilities that allows manufacturers to continuously customize and reconfigure equipment to accommodate new products or processes could be encouraged. This use of portable modular manufacturing suites also provides new options for geographic diversification and facility reconfiguration.

In addition, global governments should think about how to incentivize companies to design facilities in the future that are flexible enough to be repurposed during a pandemic. The benefit of building flexibility into an operating facility is that the processes surrounding those manufacturing operations are upgraded over time. Building plants that are not routinely used and maintaining them in a hibernated state creates many unforeseen issues (e.g., equipment reliability, availability of trained staff, up-to-date quality systems).

A recent McKinsey report addressed the issue of being always prepared, with upfront investments over two years, followed by maintenance investments into the preparedness level. The analysis showed that the minimum economic loss globally would be 40 times higher if preparedness investments were not made.

References: Kon et al., Curr Opin Biotechnol 2022; Rosa et al., Vaccine 2021; FDA Guidance for Industry: Development and Licensure of Vaccines to Prevent COVID-19 (2020); Sealy, CNN Health 2021.

中文解說 — Commentary

核心數字：63 天的奇蹟背後

Moderna 疫苗從序列最終確認到首位受試者接種僅花了 63 天，這背後有三個關鍵因素：

  1. 三十年技術積累：mRNA-LNP 平台早已成熟，不需從零開始
  2. 平台技術 (platform technology)：同一套製程可快速替換不同 mRNA 序列
  3. 監管快速通道：FDA 緊急使用授權 (EUA) 機制加速審查

這三者缺一不可——技術、設施、法規三軌並行。

類比：「冬眠工廠」vs.「靈活工廠」

美國政府 2010 年資助建立的備用疫苗設施，本意是「緊急備用倉庫」。但現實是，長期不使用的設備（冬眠狀態）在激活時面臨重重問題：

  • 設備老化、可靠性下降
  • 訓練有素的人員已流失
  • 品質系統未能跟上法規更新

就像一輛長期停在車庫的備用跑車，緊急時刻才發現電瓶沒電、輪胎洩氣。
相較之下，「靈活工廠」策略是：讓現有正常運作的設施保持彈性，一旦需要可快速重新配置用途。

模組化廠房：速度與彈性的解法

Pfizer Kalamazoo 廠採用的預製模組化潔淨室，具體優勢：

  • 縮短建設時間達 50%：工廠外預製，現場安裝
  • 可預先資格確認 (pre-qualified)：模組有標準設計，資格確認文件可複用
  • 放入現有廠房：無需新建建築，只需調整公用設施（水、電、HVAC）
  • 可重新配置：疫情後可調整用於其他產品

這對 CDMO 的廠房策略有直接啟示：在新建或擴建設施時，納入模組化設計的比例，可以大幅提升未來應急能力。

McKinsey 報告的商業邏輯

「全球最低經濟損失若不投資備戰，將是投資成本的 40 倍」——這不只是公共衛生論據，也是製藥業資本投資的商業論據：

具備快速響應能力的 CDMO，在下一次緊急需求出現時，將能取得優先合約、溢價收費，並建立長期戰略夥伴關係。彈性產能是護城河，不只是成本中心。

策略規劃思考

依照本節的教訓，貴廠若要提升應急製造能力，可以從哪三個面向著手？

建議方向：(1) 評估現有設施中可快速重新配置用於 mRNA 或基因療法的潔淨室空間；(2) 建立與設備供應商的模組化廠房標準設計協議；(3) 維持訓練有素的人員與最新的品質系統——確保「戰備」狀態而非「冬眠」狀態。

CDMO 策略啟示 — Strategic Takeaways

對 CDMO 的五大啟示

  • GMP 本身就是防護：嚴格執行現有 GMP 是應對未知病原體的第一道防線，不需要恐慌性改造設施。

  • 彈性設計是護城河：模組化廠房、可快速重新配置的生產線，是下一次緊急需求的最佳備戰。「冬眠工廠」在緊急時刻往往無法啟動。
