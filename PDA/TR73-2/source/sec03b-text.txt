5.2	
Chapter I — Risk Management 
Chapter I of the MDR GSPR list relates primarily to risk management (RM). The pharmaceutical 
company is required to have an internal RM process that assesses risks associated with their device part 
in the context of their DDC and its intended use. This may be part of a device RM plan or combined 
product (i.e., including drug) RM plan. In that case, either the RM standard operating procedure or a 
description of the process can be shared, for example, as part of an RM file. ICH Quality Guideline Q9: 
Quality Risk Management provides guidance, as does ISO 14971:2019 Medical Devices — Application 
of Risk Management to Medical Devices which states:
The risk management file need not physically contain all the records and other documents. 
However, it needs to contain at least references or pointers to all required documentation, so 
that the manufacturer can assemble the information referenced in the risk management file in 
a timely manner. […] The risk management file can be in any form or type of medium. […] 
See ISO/TR 24971 for guidance on establishing a risk management file for components and 
devices that were designed without using ISO 14971” (11).
Suppliers of components or device parts can support the pharmaceutical company by providing ap-
propriate documentation, which demonstrates a RM process that communicates risks associated with 
the respective component(s)/part(s). In the case of an existing RM plan, the supplier may also share 
this with the pharmaceutical company.
As always, the responsibility of deciding if and how the supportive documents will be used remains 
with the pharmaceutical company.
