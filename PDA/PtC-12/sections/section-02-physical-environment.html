Q 2.1 Room Classification Surrounding RABS

原文內容 Original Text

Q 2.1. What should be the classification of the room surrounding the restricted access barrier system (RABS)?

Recommendation

The minimum classification of the room immediately surrounding the RABS must be Grade B. However, a manufacturer may also choose to maintain these areas at more stringent levels of control when assessed for risk, especially in the area immediately surrounding the RABS doors, which may require execution of open-door interventions (i.e., during setup or filling) (1, 4).

Rationale

A RABS provides an enclosed and separated environment designed to reduce the risk of contamination to product, components, and product-contact surfaces when compared to conventional "curtained" aseptic processing operations. However, RABS are not fully sealed from the immediate surrounding environment and, in some designs, allow for open-door interventions by aseptic operators. As such, the adjacent background cleanroom environment must be appropriately classified and maintained to protect the critical processing zone(s) within the RABS; therefore, the cleanroom environment should not be lower than Grade B.

Companies should provide the maximum assurance possible and practicable so that the controlled RABS environment is not impacted by the surrounding environmental conditions since the RABS is not completely isolated from the surroundings, unlike closed isolators, for example.

"Note: The minimum regulatory expectations (e.g., FDA, EMA) for the classification of manufacturing spaces immediately adjacent (i.e., background) to the Grade A critical processing zone is Grade B."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Grade B (B 級潔淨區)：這是 RABS 周圍環境的最低分級要求。Grade B 區域在靜態（at rest）條件下需達到接近 Grade A 的微粒標準，在動態（in operation）條件下則有較寬鬆但仍嚴格的限值。

RABS vs. Isolator (隔離器) 的關鍵差異：RABS 並非完全密封的系統——它與周圍環境之間存在空氣交換路徑。這正是為何背景環境必須維持高等級的根本原因。隔離器則是完全密封的，因此其背景環境可降至 Grade C 甚至 Grade D。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像 RABS 就像一間有紗窗的手術室——紗窗（屏障）能擋住大部分污染物，但並非完全密封。因此，手術室外面的走廊（背景環境）也必須保持相當的清潔度。如果走廊髒亂不堪，即使有紗窗，灰塵和微生物仍會透過縫隙進入手術區域。而隔離器則像太空站——完全密封，外面是真空也無所謂。

重點提示 Key Notes

法規底線：FDA 和 EMA 均明確要求 Grade A 關鍵製程區域的背景環境最低為 Grade B。這不是建議，而是強制性要求。

風險加嚴原則：文件特別提到，在 RABS 門附近可能需要執行開門操作的區域，廠商可能需要採用比 Grade B 更嚴格的管控。這體現了品質風險管理 (QRM) 的精神——根據實際風險調整管控等級。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 營運中，客戶常問：「我們的 RABS 產線背景環境是 Grade B 還是更高？」這會直接影響客戶對產品無菌保證的信心。如果 CDMO 能在門區域提供加嚴的環境控制（如額外的 UDAF 保護），將成為重要的競爭優勢，尤其是面對高價值生物製劑客戶時。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼 RABS 的背景環境不能像隔離器一樣降至 Grade C 或 Grade D？請從系統密封性的角度解釋。
  2. 如果某間 CDMO 想在 RABS 門區域實施比 Grade B 更嚴格的控制，可以採取哪些具體措施？

Q 2.2 Environmental Monitoring in Extended UDAF

原文內容 Original Text

Q 2.2. Which environmental monitoring criteria are required in extended unidirectional airflow surrounding restricted access barrier system (RABS)?

Recommendation

Due to the presence of the UDAF around the open-door portion of the RABS, total particulates and viables should always reflect Grade A limits at the working position, at rest. Locations that are found to be critical based on the risk assessment, should have tighter limits than Grade B under the UDAF when the RABS door is in the open position, while in operation.

The remainder of the room should be subjected to sampling frequencies and limits (e.g., total particulates, viable air samples (active and passive)) and surface samples at a frequency appropriate for Grade B and adhering to Grade B limits.

Rationale

In the extended UDF surrounding the RABS, EM should be conducted to demonstrate the maintenance of a protective environment during the times when the doors are opened. The function of the extended UDF is to provide enhanced protection around critical zones where there is a break in the integrity of the Grade A RABS zone (i.e., during open-door interventions). During times when the doors are closed, this environment can be monitored as a more traditional Grade B environment, whereas during open-door interventions, it should be treated as an enhanced Grade B environment with limits (alert and action) defined based on a risk assessment.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Extended UDAF / UDF (延伸單向氣流)：這是安裝在 RABS 門外側的額外單向氣流系統。當 RABS 門開啟時，此氣流形成一道「空氣帷幕」，防止背景環境的污染物進入 Grade A 區域。

Enhanced Grade B (加嚴 B 級)：這是一個介於 Grade A 和 Grade B 之間的監測概念。在開門操作期間，延伸 UDAF 區域不再適用普通的 Grade B 限值，而需要採用更嚴格的警戒與行動限值。

比喻說明 Analogy

延伸 UDAF 就像機場安檢區的「空氣門」(air curtain)——當商店大門打開時，空氣門會產生一道向下的氣流，阻止外面的熱空氣（或冷空氣）進入室內。同樣地，當 RABS 門開啟進行操作時，延伸 UDAF 就是那道阻擋外部污染的「空氣門」。

重點提示 Key Notes

雙重監測標準：本文提出了一個重要的操作概念——同一個區域在不同操作狀態下需要不同的監測標準：

  • 門關閉時：按標準 Grade B 監測，使用 Grade B 限值
  • 門開啟時：按加嚴 Grade B 監測，使用基於風險評估定義的更嚴格的警戒與行動限值

這種動態監測策略反映了 EU Annex 1 (2022) 對 Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) 的要求——監測計畫必須與實際風險相匹配。

實務應用 Practical Application

在實際 CDMO 營運中，建立「門開/門關」不同監測標準需要在 EM 程序文件中明確定義切換規則。建議在 SCADA/BMS 系統中整合門感測器，自動記錄門開啟時間，以便與 EM 數據進行關聯分析。這不僅是法規要求，也是數據完整性 (Data Integrity) 的體現。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼延伸 UDAF 區域在門開啟時不能直接套用 Grade A 限值？提示：考慮操作本身對環境的影響。
  2. 如果在開門操作期間，延伸 UDAF 區域的微粒監測數據超出了「加嚴 Grade B」的行動限值，應該採取什麼措施？

Q 2.3 Airflow Visualization Studies (AVS)

原文內容 Original Text

Q 2.3. How should airflow pattern visualization/demonstration be performed in a restricted access barrier system (RABS) for airflow visualization studies (AVS)?

Recommendation

The purpose of airflow visualization studies (AVS) (also known as "smoke testing") is the verification of unidirectional airflow maintained during all required equipment functions and manual interventions. This is aligned to the principle of assuring First Air in contact with product and direct and indirect product-contact equipment. The intention is to demonstrate that all permitted interventions are appropriately designed and can be executed according to the approved methodology or procedure.

AVS should verify protective airflow is maintained at RABS entry 'mouseholes' without compromise to the process environment that protective airflow is applied to.

An additional reason to perform an AVS is to have a visual record of process operations conducted per requirements to allow the following:

  • Used as training material to demonstrate correct process execution. An important aspect about AVS is that they should be exactly representatives of operational conditions, so people should have the right gowning, validated loads and packages should be used.
  • Presenting to internal or external inspectors who may require visualization of a process that may not be (fully) running at the point of inspection.
  • Assess EM sampling site locations and design of interventions or intervention discard strategies.
"Note: It is important to know what the air velocity is at the working position during the airflow visualization testing so that a correlation can be established in routine requalification studies to demonstrate the continued applicability of the AVS."

Airflow Pattern Visualization Process Activity Considerations

It must be recognized that the act of performing airflow visualization disturbs the environment that it is designed to visualize due to the purposeful injection of a smoke or fogging spray into the critical zone that would self-evidently not occur during routine operations.

To minimize the impact of this disturbance in the air supplied to the RABS, the following should be considered:

  1. Develop a Qualification Protocol — Work with affected departments (Manufacturing, Quality Assurance, Engineering, Microbiology) to determine and document the setup, operation, and intervention steps necessary for the process. The test objective is to show unidirectional airflow sweeping over critical surfaces.
  2. Select Smoke-Generation Equipment Type (e.g., Fogger, Cartridge, Dry Ice) — There are multiple acceptable equipment options for the generation of "smoke" for airflow pattern visualization testing, including foggers that use dry ice, vaporization of water for injection (WFI) or deionized water, or liquid nitrogen. The use of foggers that generate glycerin-based vapor is not recommended because they can leave an oily residue on the test area, personnel, and/or floor areas.
  3. Select Water Vapor-Generating Equipment — Water vapor-generating equipment has a variety of generation mechanisms and design styles, including cartridge-based (handheld) and continuous supply via external devices. Depending on the specifics of the application and the RABS design, one of these styles may be the better choice as there would be no residue formation.
  4. Amount of Smoke — The amount of smoke generated should be sufficient to visualize the airflow (and possible turbulence), but not too much smoke to obscure the operators' actions or machine operations.
  5. Smoke Speed — The smoke should be generated at slow speed in the opposite direction to the airflow, at approximately 45° against the direction of a vertical airflow and at a speed that minimizes disturbance. It should be noted that if the smoke flow was in the same direction as the normal airflow, that can reinforce the airflow directionality.
  6. Smoke Generator and Smoke Diffuser (i.e., Manifold) — Movement of the smoke generator and smoke diffuser should be reduced to be as slow as possible, as recommended by good aseptic practices. To limit movements, the use of a smoke diffuser of sufficient size is suggested such that it becomes unnecessary to move it during the AVS.
  7. Smoke Generation Location — The smoke is to be generated as high as possible above the working position, according to the design of the equipment, but not too close to the HEPA filter to avoid filter-clogging due to residue accumulation.
  8. Air Movement at Interfaces — To check air movement at interfaces to the Grade B surrounding environment (e.g., when doors are open), the smoke is injected inside and outside the RABS. This is done at different locations to demonstrate the flush of the air from the Grade A RABS and its demonstrated unidirectionality (when injected inside) and the absence of air ingress (when injected from outside) into the RABS zone.
  9. Wall and Vertical Panel Considerations — Injecting smoke near walls or vertical panels should be avoided, as this may create additional disturbances in airflow that would not occur in routine operations. Note: Air will flow slower along the RABS walls, and there will be minor eddies along the walls. This is to be expected by the application of physics.
  10. Airflow Visualization Study Frequency — The frequency of re-executing an AVS is not timebound in the first instance; it should be based primarily on events (equipment or process change control). As periodic validation review is regarded as a qualification study, the study may need to be repeated for other reasons (e.g., improvements in film-capture quality over time).
  11. Open-door Intervention Considerations — In the case of open-door interventions, airflow visualization qualification should be done to ensure that there is a unidirectional airflow flush away and out of the RABS during the intervention.

Selection of Video Recording Equipment and Cinematography

The record that exists after the execution of airflow visualization testing is a GMP record that assures the data integrity, a video record that may need to exist for many years. As such, careful thought must go into both the selection of the recording equipment and the modalities concerning film cinematography to maximize value of what is shown to the reviewer in the video footage. The following points and recommendations should be followed:

  1. Equipment and Physical Setup — The equipment used and the physical setup to video-capture the performance of the airflow visualization testing must be considered to ensure that the record contains sufficient detail to demonstrate that unidirectional flow is maintained in all required areas. A good strategy to ensure this is to set up multiple cameras at different angles with significantly overlapping fields of view. Where applicable, built-in cameras and recording devices can be used to capture video of smoke studies; however, complete and detailed coverage of the study area must be accomplished.
  2. Visibility of Vapor on the Video Recording — The rear glass doors of the RABS may need to be covered by an opaque backing or a contrasting color in order to facilitate visibility in the final video footage.
  3. Cinematography Considerations — Contextualizing the cinematography by filming a timepiece that indicates the time and date before panning the camera to visualize the smoke directionality is recommended. Filming a schematic drawing of the RABS system and then the equipment section can also assist the viewer in this regard. Ensure that the differential pressure across the filter and velocity were within standard operating limits during the AVS. Each camera shot should be carefully prepared to ensure that no unnecessary activity is performed in the background. The video should be taken using two to four (2-4) different camera angles to facilitate, as much as possible, a three-dimensional view of the unidirectionality of airflow over the critical zones. Best practice is to synchronize the timing of all filmed scenes, so that direct comparison of the airflow visualization can be made from all filmed angles while viewing in a single pane. When footages are edited to be combined into one stream, the original video file, if considered to contain raw data, is to be retained.

Practical Considerations

Similarly, the logistics of the study execution require careful planning so that the captured film footage is integral and does not allow for any misinterpretation or gaps to the record. The following should be considered for implementation:

  • Simulate a walk-through of interventions (dress rehearsal) to assist with seamless professional execution that minimizes the need for extensive editing. Consider the floor communication method (i.e., radios, hand signals) during execution if participating individuals are on opposite sides of a RABS.
  • Simulate all manipulations strictly following good aseptic behavior, including glove decontamination, and limit the personnel in the space to the smoke-equipment operator, aseptic processing operators, video operator, and reviewer/approver.
  • Filling line operation at maximum speed will require airflow visualization studies because of potential impact on protective airflows, including 'First air'.
  • Consider bringing a cleanroom mobile device (e.g., tablet) to the film location so the video footage can be downloaded and reviewed by approvers on the floor immediately after the test study has been performed (subject to room classification).
  • Verify that all support systems (e.g., room HVAC, unidirectional airflow) are in a controlled and qualified state and operational. Verify all personnel are present and gowned per routine procedure.
  • Verify that the materials needed (i.e., smoke vapor source) are available and in sufficient quantities.
  • Verify any smoke-detection equipment that could trigger an alarm and/or evacuation are switched off for the zone being filmed, in collaboration with the site security personnel. Particle and viable counters should be in operation to mimic the routine, but additional precautions may need to be taken to avoid damage to sensor heads.
  • Verify that the room is in an acceptable state (i.e., normal housekeeping, no work in progress in the background of the video, no materials linked to shutdown activity stored in area being studied).

Rationale

Focus of the video reviewer may be diverted from the smoke study evaluation if simulated operations are not done with the expected level of control for the operation to be simulated (e.g., untidy areas, personnel not wearing required gowning). Additionally, abnormal conditions may not be considered representative of the routine when performing AVS.

The integrity of the airflow visualization film footage requires assurance (e.g., date, time, continuity, accuracy) for both inspectional and training purposes. The firm should assess periodically the need to repeat smoke testing to provide assurance that they reflect current operational characteristics.

This assessment should consider the following:

  • Cumulative effect of process and/or facility changes
  • Quality of the video footage being improved over time
  • Improvements in the methodology of smoke generation and insertion techniques

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Airflow Visualization Studies / AVS (氣流可視化研究)：俗稱「煙霧測試」(smoke testing)，透過在關鍵區域注入可見煙霧或霧氣，以視覺化方式驗證單向氣流 (UDAF) 是否在所有設備操作和人工介入期間維持正確方向。這是確認 First Air (初始空氣) 原則得到保障的核心驗證手段。

First Air (初始空氣)：直接從 HEPA 過濾器流出、未受任何阻擋或擾動的潔淨空氣。First Air 必須首先接觸產品和產品接觸面，這是無菌保證的基本原則。

Mousehole (鼠洞口)：RABS 上供容器或物料通過的小開口。AVS 需驗證這些開口處仍維持保護性氣流。

比喻說明 Analogy

AVS 就像用一面「空氣的鏡子」來看平時看不見的東西。正如我們在冬天呵氣可以「看見」氣流方向一樣，煙霧測試讓我們「看見」無塵室中原本不可見的空氣流動方向。但要注意一個矛盾：呵氣本身會改變周圍的氣流——同樣地，注入煙霧本身也會擾動我們想要觀察的環境。因此，如何「輕柔地呵氣」而不干擾原本的氣流，正是 AVS 的技術核心。

重點提示 Key Notes

煙霧產生的關鍵要求：

  • 不可使用甘油基霧化器——會留下油性殘留物，影響設備性能與人員安全
  • 推薦使用：乾冰、WFI 蒸汽化、去離子水霧化、或液氮
  • 注入角度：約 45° 逆向氣流方向，速度要慢，避免因注入動作本身造成紊流
  • 注入位置：盡量靠近工作高度上方，但不要太靠近 HEPA 過濾器以避免堵塞

攝影記錄的 GMP 要求

AVS 影片是正式的 GMP 記錄，必須符合數據完整性 (Data Integrity) 要求：

  • 影片開頭應拍攝時鐘顯示日期時間
  • 使用 2-4 個攝影角度，提供近似三維的氣流觀察
  • 多角度影片應同步時間軸，便於並排比對
  • 如後製剪輯，原始影片檔案必須保留（視為原始數據）
  • 背景不應有不相關的活動（維修、清潔等）

執行頻率的事件驅動原則

AVS 的重新執行頻率不是基於時間，而是基於事件——當設備或製程發生變更時才需重新執行。這與傳統「每年重做一次」的思維不同。但公司仍應定期評估是否需要重做，考量因素包括：

  • 製程/設施變更的累積效應
  • 影片拍攝品質是否可以提升
  • 煙霧產生技術是否已改進

實務應用 Practical Application

「彩排」的重要性：文件特別建議在正式 AVS 前進行 dress rehearsal（彩排）。在 CDMO 環境中，這一步至關重要——因為 AVS 涉及多個部門協作（生產、QA、工程、微生物），任何溝通不良都可能導致影片需要重拍，浪費寶貴的潔淨室時間和資源。

最大線速要求：文件指出充填線以最大速度運行時需要進行 AVS，因為高速運轉的設備可能影響保護性氣流。CDMO 在進行產能擴充時，務必將此納入驗證規劃。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼煙霧的注入方向建議逆向（約 45°）於氣流方向？如果順向注入會發生什麼問題？
  2. 文件建議在 RABS 內外分別注入煙霧。從 RABS 內部注入時，要觀察什麼？從外部注入時又要觀察什麼？
  3. 為什麼 AVS 影片被視為 GMP 記錄？如果影片中出現操作人員未正確著裝的畫面，會有什麼影響？

Q 2.4 Personnel Passage Through Grade A RABS

原文內容 Original Text

Q 2.4. What are the considerations for facility layout design regarding acceptable personnel passage through the Grade A restricted access barrier system (RABS)?

Recommendation

Using a conveyor swing arm to lift the process line and doors that close into both sides to protect the line can allow the movement of operators from one side of the line to the other in cases where it is not possible to have two entrances into the room from the Grade B corridor. This movement should always be performed only after the impacted section of the line has been cleared of vials and is preferable in a section of the line where the units have already been filled and are fully stoppered. After personnel passage through the swing conveyor, a disinfection of the RABS interior/swing conveyor is required.

The environment immediately adjacent to the RABS may be designed to provide unidirectional airflow. This will provide protection to the glove ports and the interior of the doors when opened to performed inherent interventions (i.e., sterile equipment installation) or rare open-door corrective interventions. Computational Fluid Design (CFD) modeling may determine the need to have small rigid polycarbonate sheets extending from the ceiling to the top of the door height to ensure the airflow is unidirectional providing the desired protection.

Rationale

The movement of the swing arm might create turbulence in the surrounding air. Every effort must be made to prevent air external to the RABS from entering the RABS to protect units that are not yet integral from contamination.

Using smaller HEPA curtains that reach down to door height would provide some directionality and flush airflow without introducing risk to operators or materials.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Conveyor Swing Arm (輸送帶擺臂)：一種工程解決方案，允許將製程線路的一段抬起，讓操作人員從產線一側通行到另一側。這是在設施佈局無法提供兩個獨立入口時的替代方案。

Computational Fluid Dynamics / CFD (計算流體力學)：利用電腦模擬來分析空氣流動模式。在 RABS 設計中，CFD 可用於評估是否需要在天花板到門頂之間安裝聚碳酸酯導流板，以確保氣流維持單向性。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像 RABS 充填線就像一條河流上的橋——當人員需要從河的一側到另一側時，不能直接涉水而過（那會攪動河水、帶入污染）。Conveyor swing arm 就像一座可升降的吊橋，先把「河流」（產品輸送帶）抬起來，清空河道中的「船隻」（瓶子），然後讓人員快速通過。通過後，還需要「清理橋面」（消毒 RABS 內部）才能恢復通行。

重點提示 Key Notes

人員通行的三大前提條件：

  • 清線：受影響區段的瓶子必須全部清除
  • 優先選擇已密封區段：最好在產品已充填且已加塞（fully stoppered）的區段通過
  • 通行後消毒：必須對 RABS 內部和擺臂進行消毒

設施設計的根本原則：理想的設施佈局應提供從 Grade B 走廊進入房間的兩個獨立入口，使操作人員無需穿越產線。只有在現有設施無法實現此佈局時，才應考慮 swing arm 方案。這再次體現了「設計優先」的 Quality by Design (QbD) 精神。

CFD 模擬的價值

CFD 建模不僅用於設計階段，還可以：

  • 評估聚碳酸酯導流板的安裝位置和尺寸
  • 模擬 swing arm 移動時產生的紊流範圍
  • 驗證開門操作期間 UDAF 的保護效果
  • 減少實體 mock-up 測試的次數，降低驗證成本

對 CDMO 而言，投資 CFD 建模可以在設施改造前預測潛在問題，避免昂貴的實體修改。

實務應用 Practical Application

在 CDMO 的設施規劃中，人員動線設計是初期就必須解決的問題。如果佈局設計導致操作人員經常需要穿越 Grade A 區域，每次通行都會產生額外的消毒時間和清線操作，直接影響產能利用率 (OEE)。因此，在新設施設計或舊設施改造的資本預算評估中，應將「雙走廊/雙入口」的投資與「swing arm + 每次通行損失的產能」進行總擁有成本 (TCO) 分析。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 房間分級：RABS 周圍環境的最低分級為 Grade B，這是 FDA/EMA 的強制要求。在 RABS 門區域可能需要更嚴格的管控，因為 RABS 不像隔離器那樣完全密封。
  • 延伸 UDAF 監測：RABS 門外的延伸 UDAF 區域需要動態監測策略——門關閉時按 Grade B 監測，門開啟時按「加嚴 Grade B」監測，限值應基於風險評估定義。
  • 氣流可視化研究 (AVS)：這是驗證 First Air 和 UDAF 保護的核心工具。關鍵要點包括：避免甘油基煙霧、逆向 45° 注入、多角度攝影、事件驅動的重新執行頻率，以及影片作為 GMP 記錄的數據完整性要求。
  • 人員通道設計：理想設計應避免人員穿越 Grade A 區域。必要時可使用 conveyor swing arm 方案，但必須遵守清線、選擇已密封區段、通行後消毒等前提條件。CFD 建模是優化設施氣流保護的重要工具。
