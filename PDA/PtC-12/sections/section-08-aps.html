Q 8.1 — APS in RABS: Special Considerations

Original Text

Q 8.1. What are the special considerations for aseptic process simulation (APS) in a restricted access barrier system (RABS)?

Recommendation

A risk-based analysis and approach taken for APS are recommended for all aseptic processing technologies, including RABS systems. By design, the APS should imitate routine manufacturing conditions and should ensure that all critical steps are challenged. A RABS relies on the surrounding cleanroom environment to maintain Grade A conditions for sterile processing. To maintain Grade A, the RABS routinely includes cleaning and disinfection processes, in addition to sterilization processes for processing accessories and equipment. By design, the RABS should only provide for open-door intervention during setup and, by exception, during operations. The cleanroom qualification, operator training, cleaning, and disinfection programs should all be qualified.

There are several key considerations that need to be established in support of APS, and they should include, but are not limited to:

  • Cleanroom and clean air equipment, such as UDAF units, must be qualified according to the required characteristics of the environment.
  • The RABS must be qualified to meet all requirements for aseptic processing including HEPA-filter integrity-testing, pressure differential across the filters, air velocity testing, and AVS. AVS should confirm unidirectional airflow that sweeps over and away from exposed products during processing, without increased risk of contamination through turbulent airflow. Airflow visualization qualification and monitoring should be applied at open-door interventions as a prerequisite to all APS designs to ensure environmental-control conditions are qualified in readiness for an APS.
    Note: APS cannot be used to justify poor design of equipment and processes.
  • Operator training and qualification extending to Grade A and Grade B aseptic gowning and aseptic behavior training must be complete. Operators undertaking RABS cleaning and disinfection programs should be qualified and operations should be implemented procedurally.
  • A list of authorized inherent and corrective interventions should be designed, defined, and appropriately assessed and documented in the CCS to minimize the risk of contamination. Open-door interventions should be a rare and exceptional occurrence. When required, the need to take representative sterility samples should be considered, and the intervention should be effectively demonstrated in an APS.
  • Qualified methods are in place for the transfer of materials that enter the cleanroom, and subsequently the RABS, that do not compromise the cleanroom-surrounding environment or the RABS Grade A processing zone.
  • Risk assessments support the establishment of a comprehensive EM program, which should be based on detailed knowledge of the process, facility, and equipment. Sampling locations, sampling frequency and type of EM monitoring (e.g., active air, passive, contact plate) should be defined based on risk assessment output.

The use of a "piggyback" APS can be considered as part of the overall APS design. A piggyback APS is where an APS run is undertaken at the end of a routine single or campaign filling process and can be used in support of validating an extended run or campaign length/duration. There are specific limitations associated with a piggyback APS which, by design, does not provide for an appropriate simulation of activities and variables that are only performed for the first lot of a campaign, for example, setup activities. Consideration also needs to be given to potential interferences of residual product with the growth-promoting properties of the media used in the APS. Consequently, a piggyback APS alone will not fulfill the validation requirements to demonstrate an effective APS program. Rather, its use can be considered as part of an overall effective APS program, which also must include an APS strategy that covers setup and other potential worst-case scenarios.

The scheduling of piggyback APS in conjunction with traditional APS should be supported by a formal risk assessment, for which the limitations of the piggyback approach and its use are considered.

Rationale

The planning of open- and closed-door interventions and simulation of worst-case criteria should be based on risk management principles. The same criteria for the design of an APS as stated in PDA Technical Report No. 22 (Revised 2011): Process Simulation for Aseptically Filled Products are also appropriate for all RABS.

After establishing Grade A conditions inside a RABS, the barrier technology provides a physical and aerodynamic barrier to contamination ingress to Grade A when the barrier is closed. The barrier is open at setup where presterilized materials are transferred and are installed. At this time, both Grade A conditions and the sterilized parts are at an increased risk for contamination and, thus, can compromise sterility.

If rare open-door interventions take place during aseptic manufacturing, there is also a risk escalation that needs mitigation by design, qualifications, and procedural controls, and is subject to additional qualification in an APS. Such in-process significant interventions require consideration for sterility samples to be taken and should be included in the APS.

Simulation of the production activities is a necessity for designing the APS studies.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Aseptic Process Simulation, APS (無菌製程模擬)：以培養基取代實際藥品，模擬整個無菌充填過程，驗證系統在正常與最差條件下，能否維持無菌保證 (Sterility Assurance)。APS 本質上是對「整個無菌製造系統」的一次綜合性能力測試。

Risk-Based Analysis (基於風險的分析)：APS 的設計不是隨意的，而是以品質風險管理 (QRM) 為驅動。需先識別哪些步驟具有最高的污染風險，再據此設計模擬方案。這也呼應了 EU Annex 1 對 Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) 的核心要求。

Piggyback APS (搭便車式 APS)：在常規生產批次結束後，緊接著執行一次 APS。優點是可驗證延長生產時間或 campaign 長度的能力。但其重大限制在於：它無法模擬 setup 階段的活動（因為 setup 只發生在 campaign 的第一批），所以不能單獨使用，必須搭配傳統 APS 一起構成完整的驗證方案。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像你是一位飛行員，APS 就像是飛行模擬器測試。你必須在模擬器中重現所有可能遇到的情境：正常起降、惡劣天氣、引擎故障等。RABS 的 APS 也是如此 -- 你不僅要模擬正常充填，還要模擬 open-door intervention（相當於緊急降落程序）、setup（相當於起飛前檢查），以及所有可能的worst-case 情境。

而 Piggyback APS 就像是在一次正常飛行結束後，順便進行額外的模擬測試 -- 你可以測試「長時間飛行」的耐力，但你無法從中驗證「起飛前完整檢查程序」是否正確，因為你已經飛在空中了。

重點提示 Key Notes

APS 的六大先決條件：在執行 APS 之前，以下所有項目都必須已完成確效/確認：

  1. 潔淨室確效 -- UDAF 設備符合環境要求
  2. RABS 確效 -- HEPA 完整性、壓差、風速、AVS 全部合格
  3. 人員訓練 -- Grade A/B 無菌更衣與無菌行為訓練完成
  4. 介入清單 -- 固有/矯正介入已定義並納入 CCS
  5. 物料傳遞方法 -- 已確效且不會危及 Grade A 環境
  6. EM 計畫 -- 基於風險評估建立完整的環境監控方案

特別注意原文的警語："APS cannot be used to justify poor design of equipment and processes." -- APS 是用來驗證良好設計的有效性，而不是用來為不良設計找藉口。

法規連結 Regulatory Link

本節參考了 PDA TR No. 22 (Revised 2011)：Process Simulation for Aseptically Filled Products，這是 APS 設計的核心指引文件。此外，EU Annex 1 (2022) 第 9 章對 APS 有詳細要求，包括最少充填量、頻率、以及對 worst-case 條件的模擬要求。

實務應用 Practical Application

假設你是一家 CDMO 的驗證主管，客戶要求使用 RABS 充填線進行 3 天 campaign 生產。你的 APS 方案設計必須考慮：

  • 傳統 APS 至少一次，涵蓋完整 setup 與 closed-door intervention 模擬
  • 可考慮增加 Piggyback APS，在實際生產結束後接續執行，以驗證延長生產時間的能力
  • 兩種 APS 的搭配使用需有正式的風險評估文件支持

  2. 如果一家公司只執行 Piggyback APS 而沒有傳統 APS，在稽核時可能會被提出什麼缺失？
  3. AVS 為什麼被列為 APS 的先決條件之一？試從氣流與污染控制的角度說明。
Q 8.2 — APS for Multiple Shifts and Campaign Production

Original Text

Q 8.2. What are the special considerations of the aseptic process simulation (APS) for multiple shifts and multiple-day campaign production in the restricted access barrier system (RABS)?

Recommendation

The duration of the APS should be based on risk assessment and sufficient to adequately challenge the aseptic process. The duration should be long enough to allow simulation of the predetermined interventions and activities and to fill the required units.

Where there are no risk-based time-related effects and where longer duration does not pose a risk to the routine production process, there is no requirement that the APS should be equal to or longer than the maximum production duration.

The following points need to be considered when creating an APS for campaign manufacturing:

  • Setup
  • Number and duration of interventions based on risk management principles to ensure that the APS is representative of the routine manufacturing process and qualified interventions (type, frequency, and duration)
  • Indirect product-contact parts remain installed for the duration for requalification of the campaign and are subject to an aseptic hold time (the duration may need to be shortened based on the risk assessment evaluation)
  • Replacement or leaving in place of direct product-contact parts (i.e., product filling needles), should be representative of the routine manufacturing process exposure time
  • RABS gloves remain installed for the duration of the campaign
  • Cleaning/disinfection practices occurring inside the RABS during routine campaign manufacturing should be simulated
  • Certain circumstances when performing an APS related to a production hold and/or interruptions
  • Taking of sterility samples after significant critical interventions, if justified

The approach to APS with a "piggyback" qualification is not considered a best practice as setup is missing in the final APS and not representative of the full campaign.

Rationale

Contamination of an aseptic process is primarily an outcome of events rather than time. Therefore, the duration of the APS should be sufficient to assess the performance of those activities identified in the risk assessment as having the potential to introduce contamination.

The APS is not sensitive enough to uncover the flaws, weaknesses, or failures in the integrity of the RABS, the environmental quality, or the ability of the personnel to perform activities over time.

The APS should challenge and confirm the effectiveness of these contamination control measures. This means that the maintenance of environmental conditions should be assured and assessed by the RABS system design and EM. Good process design and well-controlled activities required between the batches within a campaign (e.g., fill needles or glove replacement), RABS gloves ergonomics, training and qualification of personnel, assessment of human factors, and scheduling of breaks and rest periods for operators are better tools for controlling the complexity of RABS campaign operations than extended APS runs.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Campaign Manufacturing (連續生產/戰役式生產)：指在不進行完整清潔和重新 setup 的情況下，連續生產多個批次的模式。在 RABS 中，這意味著某些零件（如間接接觸產品的零件）在整個 campaign 期間保持安裝狀態。

"Events Rather Than Time" (事件而非時間)：這是本節最重要的觀念 -- 污染主要是由「事件」（如介入、物料傳遞、人員操作）造成的，而不是單純因為「時間長」就會污染。因此，APS 的時長不需要等於或超過最長生產時間，關鍵是要充分模擬所有可能引入污染的「事件」。

Direct vs. Indirect Product-Contact Parts：

  • Direct (直接接觸產品零件)：如充填針頭 -- 在 campaign 中可能需要更換，APS 應模擬這種更換操作
  • Indirect (間接接觸產品零件)：如導軌、stopper bowl -- 在整個 campaign 期間保持安裝，受 aseptic hold time 限制

比喻說明 Analogy

Campaign 生產的 APS 設計，就像規劃一次長途公路旅行的安全測試。你不需要實際開完全程 3,000 公里才能證明車子安全，但你需要模擬旅途中所有關鍵事件：加油停靠（相當於 batch 間的操作）、換輪胎（相當於更換充填針頭）、長時間駕駛後的疲勞管理（相當於操作員輪班和休息安排）。重點不是開了多少公里，而是有沒有把所有可能出問題的「事件」都測試到。

重點提示 Key Notes

Campaign APS 的必模擬項目清單：

  1. Setup 階段 -- 包含初始 open-door 活動
  2. 介入操作 -- 類型、頻率、時長均須代表性
  3. 間接接觸零件 -- 模擬完整 campaign 期間的安裝狀態與 hold time
  4. 直接接觸零件 -- 模擬是否更換或保留（如充填針頭）
  5. RABS 手套 -- 整個 campaign 期間保持安裝
  6. 清潔/消毒 -- 模擬批次間的常規清潔消毒操作
  7. 生產暫停/中斷 -- 模擬計畫性或非計畫性停頓
  8. 無菌取樣 -- 重大介入後的取樣程序

常見誤區 Common Misconception

許多公司誤以為 APS 必須跑得和實際生產一樣長。原文明確指出：「there is no requirement that the APS should be equal to or longer than the maximum production duration」。APS 的目的是驗證「事件管控能力」，而非「時間耐受力」。時間相關的控制（如環境維持、手套完整性、人員疲勞）應由其他工具（如 EM、設備確效、人因評估）來保障。

實務應用 Practical Application

一家 CDMO 的 RABS 充填線進行 5 天 campaign（每天 2 班），共生產 10 個批次。APS 設計考量：

  • APS 時長不需要 5 天，但需模擬所有關鍵事件
  • 包含完整 setup + 至少模擬「批次間」的清潔消毒轉換
  • 充填針頭若每 2 天更換一次，APS 需包含此更換操作
  • 模擬操作員輪班交接的程序
  • 在適當時機取 sterility sample（如重大介入後）

  2. 為什麼 Piggyback APS 在 campaign 模式下「不被視為最佳實務」？它具體缺少了什麼？
  3. 如果 campaign 期間 RABS 手套破損需更換，這個事件應該如何在 APS 中處理？
Q 8.3 — Worst-Case Conditions in RABS APS

Original Text

Q 8.3. What are the special considerations for determining worst-case conditions in a restricted access barrier system (RABS) aseptic process simulation (APS)?

Recommendation

Based on the features of the operation, the worst-case scenarios chosen for inclusion in an APS should be predefined. The best way to determine the suitable worst-case scenarios is to evaluate the APS, taking into account the pertinent elements of the process and the aseptic process risk assessment. The worst-case condition should be considered within the expected operational boundary. Worst-case does not mean creation of artificial conditions or environments that exceed allowed operating conditions and can force a system failure.

"Note: APS is not used to validate bad practices."

A few examples of worst-case conditions include, but are not limited to:

  • Open-door interventions (if required) in addition to the setup
  • Closed-door interventions (inherent/corrective)

The following parameters generally have a lower impact on the product-contamination risk, since the Grade A RABS interior environment with exposed product/critical surfaces are separated from the lower-classified environment and operators:

  • Hold time of sterile materials (e.g., filtration and filling equipment, filtered media solution, sterile garments/goggles)
  • Filling duration
  • Simulation of the maximum or minimum number of personnel
  • Speed of the filling machine (i.e., slowest speed for largest container considering the maximum-duration vial remains open online and the highest speed for the smallest container considering difficulties in handling)

Any intervention required via opening the door shall be considered along with the frequency and duration for the simulation in the APS.

Interventions that do not require handling via an open door but need manual adjustment via RABS gloves and tools shall be simulated in a similar way.

A risk-based approach shall be followed in order to determine the frequency, duration, and criticality of the intervention.

Rationale

Open-door interventions represent the highest risk for product contamination and therefore should be the focus of the predefined worst-case conditions in the APS. In addition, the design, barrier properties, and the closed-door interventions of RABS might provide other worst-case conditions, which should be considered in the design of APS. Companies should conduct thoughtful risk assessments to help determine which conditions and configurations represent the most useful challenge to the process and how to most effectively include them in the APS.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Worst-Case Conditions (最差條件)：在 APS 中模擬的「最具挑戰性」的操作條件。但有一個非常重要的界限：worst-case 必須在允許的操作範圍內，不能人為創造超出正常操作邊界的條件來強制系統失敗。

RABS 的 Worst-Case 分層：

風險等級類型說明
最高風險Open-door Interventions屏障門開啟，Grade A 區域直接暴露於 Grade B 環境
中等風險Closed-door Interventions透過 RABS 手套操作，屏障保持關閉
較低影響時間/速度/人員數量因 RABS 屏障將產品與操作員分離，這些參數的影響較低

比喻說明 Analogy

定義 worst-case 就像消防演習的情境設計。你會模擬「最困難但合理的逃生情境」，例如：電梯故障、某條走廊被煙霧阻擋。但你不會模擬「整棟大樓瞬間倒塌」，因為那超出了合理預期的範圍。

同樣地，RABS APS 的 worst-case 應該模擬「open-door intervention」（最高風險事件），但不應該人為創造一個不可能在正常操作中出現的極端條件（例如同時打開所有門並關閉所有氣流）。

重點提示 Key Notes

RABS 獨特優勢 -- 某些傳統 worst-case 參數影響較低：

與傳統開放式無菌充填不同，RABS 的物理屏障將產品/關鍵表面與操作員和低等級環境隔開。因此，以下參數在 RABS 中的影響顯著降低：

  • Hold time (保留時間) -- 滅菌物料在屏障內受保護
  • 充填時長 -- 屏障關閉時環境控制穩定
  • 人員數量 -- 操作員在屏障外，不直接暴露產品
  • 充填速度 -- 雖仍需考慮（最慢/最快），但影響較傳統系統低

這是 RABS 相較於傳統無菌充填的一個重要風險降低優勢，也是選擇 RABS 技術的商業價值所在。

兩句警語 Two Warnings

原文重複強調了兩個關鍵原則：

  1. "APS is not used to validate bad practices" -- APS 不是為不良操作背書的工具
  2. "Worst-case does not mean creation of artificial conditions...that can force a system failure" -- worst-case 不等於人為製造系統崩潰

這兩句話為 APS 設計劃定了明確的邊界：APS 驗證的是「在合理操作範圍內，系統能否維持無菌保證」。

Q 8.4 — Setup and Assembly Best Practices

Original Text

Q 8.4. What is the best practice when performing the setup and assembly of a restricted access barrier system (RABS) filling line?

Recommendation

There are two setup conditions for RABS: Initially, with an open barrier door, and then, readiness for production operations with the barrier door closed where full EM is implemented and critical tasks involving sterilized parts are completed, for example, positioning of filling needles (if single-use). As much as possible, set-up should be performed with the doors closed so careful design and understanding of the equipment is essential.

The open-door set-up process presents the highest risk of contamination when transferring in or assembling sterilized parts or components.

Based on risk knowledge and the role First Air takes in preventing contamination ingress into the Grade A zone, operators need to position themselves so protective airflow passes over body parts inserted into the RABS (such as hands and sleeves) and into the surrounding cleanroom. Positioning of materials ready for transfer should also consider protective airflow exiting the RABS Grade A zone and from the overhead or over-open-door UDAF. AVS should demonstrate the appropriate airflow from the interior of the RABS to the surrounding area.

Materials and parts should be presented for transfer at working position, typically on carts.

A secondary operator could support positioning parts and materials provided they do not compromise the protective airflow.

Good aseptic technique (e.g., transfer of inner bag at the RABS interface by double-bag methods) is required on open-barrier-door entry, and the operator's reach should be limited to the required task without touching other surfaces, critical or otherwise. Practices of hand disinfection prior to open-door interventions should be based on the contact time recommended and qualified practices. (See PDA TR-62 for additional information.)

During the intervention, EM sampling locations in the proximity of the intervention should be subject to continuous EM (i.e., viable and nonviable). The surrounding cleanroom should also be monitored at the door opening/operator interface. (See Topic 5.)

Installation of sterilized RABS gloves after open-door setup is commonly done as the final procedure. (See Topic 9.)

Before and at the time barrier doors are closed, the inside of the barrier door should be disinfected. The RABS gloves should be drawn back so only the RABS glove port entry interface should require disinfection (in some cases, covers may be inserted to prevent RABS glove contamination at open-door interventions).

With the barrier door closed, full EM should be implemented and should be ready for monitoring throughout the production-readiness setup.

Installation setup of all critical parts requires good aseptic technique, possibly with the use of tools (e.g., tweezers, tongs) and throughout the procedure. First Air protection should not be compromised.

Rationale

As interventions are such a high-risk activity, best practice is needed in all cases to mitigate risks to an acceptable level.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Two Setup Conditions (兩階段 Setup)：RABS 的 setup 分為兩個截然不同的階段，風險等級完全不同：

階段屏障狀態風險等級關鍵活動
Phase 1門開啟最高傳遞滅菌零件、組裝大型部件、安裝手套
Phase 2門關閉中等完整 EM 啟動、關鍵零件定位（如充填針頭）、生產準備

First Air (第一氣流)：指從 HEPA 過濾器出來後，尚未接觸任何物體或人員的潔淨氣流。在 open-door setup 時，操作員必須確保 First Air 從 RABS 內部流向外部，經過操作員的手和袖子後排出。這是防止外部污染進入 Grade A 區域的第一道防線。

Double-Bag Method (雙袋法)：將滅菌物品的外層包裝在 RABS 介面處移除，僅將內層（無菌）包裝傳入 Grade A 區域的技術。這是維持無菌傳遞的標準做法。

比喻說明 Analogy

RABS 的 setup 就像手術室的術前準備：

  • Phase 1 (門開啟) = 將滅菌器械從運送車搬入手術室。此時手術室門開著，風險最高，必須快速且有紀律地完成。護理人員的位置不能阻擋層流氣流。
  • Phase 2 (門關閉) = 手術室門關閉後，進行器械的精細擺放與最終確認。此時環境受控，但仍需穿戴無菌手套操作。
  • First Air = 就像手術室天花板的層流罩，潔淨空氣必須從上方往下流過器械台，再向外排出，操作人員不能站在氣流的「上游」。

重點提示 Key Notes

Open-Door Setup 的最佳實務檢查清單：

  1. 操作員定位 -- 保護性氣流經過手/袖後流向潔淨室（不可阻擋 First Air）
  2. 物料擺放 -- 待傳遞物料放在工作高度的推車上
  3. 副操作員 -- 可協助定位，但不得影響保護性氣流
  4. 無菌技術 -- 使用雙袋法傳遞；操作範圍限於必要任務
  5. 手部消毒 -- 開門介入前，依合格程序進行手部消毒（參考 PDA TR-62）
  6. 持續 EM -- 介入期間鄰近位置持續進行環境監控（viable + nonviable）
  7. 手套安裝 -- 開門 setup 的最後步驟才安裝滅菌 RABS 手套
  8. 門內側消毒 -- 關門前/關門時對門內側進行消毒

設計原則 Design Principle

原文強調：「As much as possible, set-up should be performed with the doors closed」。這意味著 RABS 的設備設計應盡量讓操作人員能透過手套口或 RTP 完成大部分 setup 工作，將必須開門的操作降到最低。這是設備採購規格（URS）中應明確要求的設計理念。

對 CDMO 而言，選購 RABS 設備時，應評估「多少 setup 操作可在關門狀態下完成」作為重要的設備評選標準。能在關門狀態下完成更多 setup 的設備，代表更低的污染風險和更高的無菌保證水準。

實務應用 Practical Application

你正在為一條新的 RABS 充填線撰寫 Setup SOP，以下是需要定義的關鍵項目：

  • 開門操作清單：列出所有必須在開門狀態下完成的操作，並為每一項定義最大允許時間
  • 操作員站位圖：標示主操作員與副操作員的位置，確保不阻擋保護性氣流方向
  • EM 取樣點：在開門介入區域周邊設置連續監控點
  • 關門檢查表：門內側消毒完成、手套安裝確認、完整 EM 啟動
  • AVS 結果參考：附上 AVS 確效報告中的氣流方向示意圖，作為操作員訓練教材

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼 RABS 手套的安裝是 open-door setup 的「最後步驟」？如果先安裝手套再傳入其他物料，會有什麼問題？
  2. 原文提到 AVS 應展示「RABS 內部到周圍區域的適當氣流」。在 open-door setup 時，理想的氣流方向應該是什麼？為什麼？
  3. 如果你是設備供應商，你會如何設計 RABS 來最大化「關門狀態下可完成的 setup 操作」比例？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • APS 先決條件：潔淨室確效、RABS 確效（HEPA/壓差/AVS）、人員訓練、介入清單、物料傳遞方法、EM 計畫 -- 六項缺一不可
  • 事件驅動原則：污染是「事件」的結果而非「時間」的結果 -- APS 時長不需等於最長生產時間，關鍵是模擬所有可能引入污染的事件
  • Worst-Case 邊界：最差條件必須在合理操作範圍內；APS 不能用來驗證不良設計或操作實務
  • RABS 獨特優勢：因物理屏障的存在，hold time、充填時長、人員數量、充填速度等傳統 worst-case 參數對 RABS 的影響較低
  • Open-Door 最高風險：open-door intervention（包含 setup）是 RABS 中風險最高的操作，應是 APS worst-case 設計的焦點
  • 兩階段 Setup：開門階段（最高風險）與關門階段（受控環境）的操作要求截然不同，設備設計應盡量讓 setup 在關門狀態下完成
  • Piggyback APS 的限制：可作為整體 APS 方案的補充，但不能單獨使用，因為它無法模擬 setup 階段
