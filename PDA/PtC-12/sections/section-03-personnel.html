Q 3.1 Operator Glove Disinfection | 操作手套消毒

原文內容 Original Text

Q 3.1. Should operator gloves be disinfected prior to entering the restricted access barrier system (RABS) gloves?

Recommendation

Disinfection of operator gloves prior to entry into the RABS gloves is an important component of the CCS and should be undertaken prior to each entry into the RABS gloves.

Gloved hands should be disinfected with an approved disinfectant (e.g., sterile isopropanol) prior to donning the RABS gloves (1, 4). The disinfectant can be applied by spraying. It is considered a basic and vital element of hygiene and cleanroom practices to minimize contamination risk or the transfer of contaminants within the clean room in general. It is also important that the risk of transfer of contamination from one operator to another is also considered and mitigated as part of the overall CCS.

Since RABS gloves are typically shared by more than one operator, the risk of contamination transfer from one operator to another through the shared use of the RABS gloves should be minimized by sanitizing the operator's own gloves before they enter into the glove ports or after exiting from the glove ports. The risk identified above (multiple operators using same gloves) is with reference to mitigating risk of contamination transfer between operators. Of course, the risk of product contamination requires equal consideration.

Rationale

RABS gloves are an integral design element and form part of the physical barrier that enables product protection by separating operators from the process.

Disinfection of gloves worn underneath RABS gloves will reduce the microbial load within the RABS gloves, in contrast to nondisinfected gloves. In the case of a pinhole in a RABS glove, a reduced microbial load on the inside of the RABS glove will reduce the chance of microbial ingress into the RABS Grade A environment (14).

RABS gloves are not infallible and, with continued use, may become worn, torn, or perforated and, hence, may potentially compromise the integrity of the system. While controls, including visual inspection (pre- and post-interventions of use) of gloves, provide for some degree of detection of holes or leaks, the identification of a leak through glove integrity-testing is usually undertaken post-batch or post-campaign manufacturing. This requires a retrospective assessment to determine the impact on the batch in the event of a confirmed glove-integrity breach.

For closed-door interventions that require the use of the RABS gloves, the risk profile associated with a potential breach of the Grade A RABS environment owing to a RABS glove integrity failure is high. This can be further compounded in the absence of operator glove disinfection, since the physical barrier between the operator hand (normally afforded through integral RABS gloves) and the Grade A environment is breached. A vector for microbiological contamination is thereby created, with risk profile reduced by ensuring adequate glove disinfection.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

RABS Glove Disinfection (RABS 手套消毒)：這是 RABS 操作中最基本但也最容易被忽視的步驟。操作人員在將手伸入 RABS 手套之前，必須先用核准的消毒劑（如無菌異丙醇）對自己的無塵室手套進行消毒。

為什麼「每次」都要做？因為 RABS 手套通常是多人共用的。操作員 A 使用後，操作員 B 接著使用同一副 RABS 手套 —— 如果 A 的手套帶有微生物，這些微生物會留在 RABS 手套內表面，等 B 進入時就可能造成交叉污染。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像共用的 RABS 手套就像醫院裡共用的手術手套架。即使手術室（Grade A 區域）本身是無菌的，如果每位外科醫生在戴上手術手套前不先洗手消毒，那手套內部就會成為細菌的「中繼站」，從一位醫生傳給下一位。

更危險的是，如果手套上有一個肉眼看不見的針孔（pinhole），未消毒的手上的細菌就會直接滲入無菌區域 —— 就像防水手套破洞後，手就會被水浸濕一樣。

重點提示 Key Notes

深層防禦 (Defense in Depth) 思維：手套完整性測試通常是在批次或批次活動結束後才進行的。這意味著如果手套在生產過程中破損，你只能在事後才發現。此時，事前的手套消毒就成為了關鍵的「最後一道防線」。

  • 手套消毒 = 降低手套內部微生物負載
  • 手套完整性測試 = 事後確認手套是否有破損
  • 兩者結合 = 即使手套破損，因為內部微生物量低，對產品的風險也被最小化

這正是 Contamination Control Strategy (CCS) 的精髓：不依賴單一控制措施，而是建立多層保護。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 的挑戰：在 CDMO 環境中，不同客戶的產品可能使用同一條 RABS 充填線，操作人員在換班或產品切換時更需要嚴格執行手套消毒。建議在 SOP 中明確規定：

  • 每次進入 RABS 手套前必須噴灑無菌 IPA
  • 操作員交接時，接手者必須在進入前消毒
  • 在 CCS 文件中記錄手套消毒的頻率與方法

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 如果一位操作員在未消毒手套的情況下進行了 RABS 關門操作，事後發現該 RABS 手套有針孔破損，你會如何評估對批次的影響？
  2. 為什麼文件強調消毒劑可以用「噴灑」方式施用？與浸泡相比有什麼實務優勢？
Q 3.2 Personnel Qualification | 人員資格認證

原文內容 Original Text

Q 3.2. How are personnel qualified for executing interventions?

Recommendation

For qualification of personnel for open-door and/or closed-door interventions, an intervention management program should be established. Typically, a phase-wise approach is followed to increase operator proficiency.

Level 1 – Training

A well-rounded personnel qualification training program should include, but not be limited to, the following:

  • Basic elements of microbiology and hygiene with specific focus on cleanroom practices (slow, deliberate movements of body and arms/gloves), principles about contamination control, First Air concept, good aseptic working techniques (i.e., in particular during RABS open-door interventions), protection of sterile products and critical surfaces, and the potential safety implications to the patient if a product is not sterile.
  • Visual airflow studies should be used in training to support the understanding of operator interventions in the aseptic space. Virtual reality-based training modules may be used as a best practice in training to assess operators' skills and mitigate risks by providing immersive, interactive simulations that help them practice real-life situations in a controlled environment.
  • Aseptic gowning and cleanroom behavior training is to be provided. The trainee should perform dry runs of gowning and degowning under the supervision of an experienced trainer, showing that the trainee is able to perform the gowning process appropriately and consistently. Those dry runs can be performed in suitable office spaces or otherwise easy-to-access rooms. Gowning qualification can be initiated in conjunction with a trainer after successful completion of the basic elements of microbiology and cleanroom behavior aspects outlined above.

Level 2 – Gowning Qualification Followed by Demonstration

The aseptic-gowning procedure should be confirmed by a visual and microbial (plating with contact plates) assessment, and the trainee's capability to don the complete sterile gown in an aseptic manner to allow entry in the aseptic area should be evaluated.

Demonstration of Assembly/Machine Setup, Interventions, and Filling Operation

Training is required on site-specific standard procedures, on machine parts setup and interventions, with the help of applicable dynamic airflow visualization studies. Training can be staggered based on intervention complexity, wherein operators first demonstrate competency to perform less complex interventions prior to moving to more complex interventions. Alternatively, the training may be certified as one block, where proficiency in all relevant interventions (irrespective of complexity) must be demonstrated for the operator to be certified.

Personnel should observe the production activities (setup and interventions) during routine commercial batch-manufacturing from the view windows or by viewing remote cameras without entering into the aseptic area. This can be done in parallel with Level 2 training, with supervisory monitoring by experienced and qualified personnel.

Personnel should simulate machine setup and interventions in the aseptic core, with the supervision of qualified personnel, by performing interventions in Grade A when there is no commercial batch-manufacturing (e.g., after completion of the routine commercial batch operations, prior to cleaning of the filling line). Assessment of nonviable and viable particle-monitoring during those simulations may be considered. The number of dry runs to be considered should be based on personnel skill set and prior experience before allowing participation in an APS run.

Level 3 – Practical Assessment in Smaller-Batch and Regular APS

After completing Levels 1 and 2, the personnel should perform setup and interventions with participation in an APS (1, 4, 7, 15). The run can be independent of routine APS, or it can be a routine APS run. The APS run may be smaller or of routine size depending on the size of the operation, the number of personnel to be qualified, and the process complexity oRABS/cRABS and open- or closed-interventions.

A bracketing approach can be applied for setup operations in the case of similarities in manipulations and interventions during the media manipulation run for qualifying more than one operator within the same run, based on the risk-based approach. However, attention should be paid to the number of units filled by an individual operator to be representative of the actual commercial operation.

Choosing a setup process that is more challenging to perform or more complex in execution and grouping interventions to perform with different sets of tools (e.g., using short or long forceps, stopper/seal track adjustments, needle adjustment) is recommended.

For open-door versus closed-door interventions, all open-door interventions and closed-door interventions that are classified as high risk within site-risk assessment documents should be chosen to be performed. When certifying the personnel to perform the aseptic processing activities in commercial batches, the same procedures that were qualified in the APS run should be chosen. Upon the successful result of APS, the data compliance monitoring (continuous viable and total particle), assessment of aseptic techniques, and the behavior of the operator(s) to be qualified for performing aseptic operations, the operator should be qualified.

Within lifecycle management, when any new open-door interventions are added to the system at later stages, quality management tools should ensure that the personnel are qualified to perform the additional interventions and the interventions have been simulated in APS runs, unless it has been otherwise documented in risk assessments with scientific rationale that the new intervention is like the qualified one.

Level 4 – Ongoing Training and Assessment

Upon successful completion of personnel qualification, the operator should be certified to perform aseptic operations, and their aseptic behavior should be monitored on a periodic/continuous basis until a sufficient level of experience is achieved. The assessment should evaluate if supervision of the newly qualified person can be reduced or if further training and additional assessment is required. It is recommended to qualify personnel annually, for both gowning and participation in APS runs.

Disqualification of Aseptic Processing Personnel

In the event when operators are considered disqualified, access to the aseptic area should be removed/prohibited until demonstration of successful requalification. The disqualification criteria may include:

  1. Failure to qualify or meet periodic qualification requirements
  2. Participation in an unsuccessful or failed APS, where their performance was identified as a cause or contribution to the failure
  3. Repeated aseptic processing technique in a manner deemed unacceptable in relation to cleanroom or aseptic-process operations or functions
  4. Failure to maintain gowning qualification
  5. Failure to maintain training requirements
  6. Repeated personnel monitoring excursions
  7. Extended periods of inactivity or absence from the aseptic processing area
  8. Other aspects of performance as identified by a supervisor (e.g., behavioral)

The requalification should be defined on the reason and relative risk associated with the disqualification.

Rationale

The performance of personnel-related activities, manipulations, and interventions in the RABS during aseptic operations can pose a risk to the sterility of the product or adversely affect it if it is not performed in an aseptic manner. Therefore, proper training and qualification of personnel with APS experience is a must.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Intervention Management Program (介入操作管理計畫)：這不只是一份訓練清單，而是一套完整的人員能力發展體系。PDA 建議採用四階段漸進式方法，從基礎知識到實際操作，逐步建立操作人員的無菌操作能力。

四階段認證體系：

  • Level 1 - 訓練 (Training)：微生物學基礎、無塵室行為規範、First Air 概念、穿衣練習
  • Level 2 - 穿衣認證 + 操作示範 (Gowning Qualification + Demonstration)：通過微生物接觸板評估的穿衣認證，加上在非生產時段的模擬操作
  • Level 3 - APS 實務評估 (Practical Assessment in APS)：參與無菌製程模擬 (APS)，在模擬條件下執行完整操作
  • Level 4 - 持續訓練與評估 (Ongoing Training)：年度再認證，包含穿衣和 APS 參與

比喻說明 Analogy

這個四階段認證體系就像飛行員的培訓過程：

  • Level 1 (地面學校)：學習航空理論、氣象學、飛行規則 —— 對應微生物學和無塵室基礎知識
  • Level 2 (模擬器訓練)：在飛行模擬器中練習各種情境 —— 對應穿衣認證和在非生產時段的操作演練
  • Level 3 (教練隨行飛行)：在真正的飛機上飛行，但有教練在旁 —— 對應在 APS 中的實際操作
  • Level 4 (定期複訓)：即使是經驗豐富的機長也要定期通過飛行檢定 —— 對應年度再認證

就像飛行員不能跳過模擬器直接上飛機一樣，無菌操作人員也不能跳過任何階段。

重點提示 Key Notes

VR 訓練的前瞻性建議：PDA 在此首次明確提到 Virtual Reality (VR) 訓練模組可作為最佳實踐。這是一個重要的產業趨勢信號 —— 未來法規檢查可能會將 VR 訓練視為先進的訓練工具。

觀察學習的重要性：Level 2 中特別提到人員應透過觀察窗或遠端攝影機觀察實際生產操作，而非直接進入無菌區域。這個「看但不碰」的階段非常重要，讓操作員在安全的環境中理解真實操作的節奏和複雜性。

取消資格條件 Disqualification Criteria

文件列出了八項可能導致操作人員被取消資格的情況，這些條件涵蓋了三個維度：

  • 技能衰退：未通過定期認證、穿衣認證失效、長時間未進入無菌區
  • 操作失誤：APS 失敗且其表現被認定為原因、重複不當操作技術
  • 監控異常：重複出現人員監控偏差

特別注意第 7 項：「長時間不活動或缺席」。這意味著即使操作員之前完全合格，長時間未執行操作後也必須重新認證。許多 CDMO 在這一點上容易疏忽。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 的 Bracketing 策略：文件提到可以對具有相似性的操作採用 Bracketing Approach（括弧法），在同一次 APS 中認證多位操作員。這對 CDMO 特別有價值：

  • 選擇最具挑戰性的設備設定流程進行模擬
  • 組合不同工具的操作（長鑷子、短鑷子、膠塞軌道調整等）
  • 確保每位操作員充填的單位數量具有代表性

這樣可以減少 APS 執行次數，同時確保認證的嚴謹性 —— 對 CDMO 而言是成本效益的最佳平衡。

  2. Level 2 訓練中提到可以在「辦公室空間」練習穿脫衣。這樣的練習環境是否足夠？與在實際無塵室中練習相比，各有什麼優缺點？
  3. 文件建議年度再認證包含穿衣和 APS 參與。如果你的工廠一年只執行兩次常規 APS，但有 20 位操作員需要再認證，你會如何安排？
Q 3.3 Open-Door vs Closed-Door Qualification | 開門與關門操作資格區分

原文內容 Original Text

Q 3.3. Is there a clear distinction between the operator qualification for closed- and open-door operator interventions, and are operators qualified for closed-door interventions allowed to perform open-door interventions (For operators qualified for specific tasks, e.g., primary operator, secondary operator, based on criticality)?

Recommendation

After setup, open-door interventions should be rare events. The personnel authorized to conduct open-door line setup and permitted open-door interventions should be trained and qualified specifically for open-door interventions after they have been permitted to undertake closed-door interventions.

Requirements for qualification of open- and closed-door interventions should be clearly distinguished.

An operator qualified for closed-door intervention is not allowed to perform open-door intervention due to the risk factors associated with aseptic techniques and intervention type. But a person qualified for open-door interventions can be allowed to perform closed-door interventions due to the low risk and considering the similarities in the intervention process. However, this should be clearly documented in the site's quality risk assessment and CCS documents (1).

A qualified-operator list should clearly specify the personnel authorized to perform interventions into the Grade A environment, with open-, closed-, or both interventions and, within that category grouping, can be done based on the similarities (e.g., loading of stoppers and/or seals if the latter is performed in the RABS).

Rationale

Interventions should be designed and performed using proper aseptic techniques to minimize the risk of product contamination. Operators should be first performing closed-door interventions and gaining a skillset at a later stage to perform open-door interventions. (See Question 3.2 for additional information.)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

比喻說明 Analogy

這就像駕駛執照的等級制度：持有大客車駕照的人可以開小客車，但只有小客車駕照的人不能開大客車。

開門操作就像開大客車 —— 需要更高的技能、更嚴格的訓練、更多的經驗。關門操作是小客車 —— 仍然需要基本資格，但風險等級較低。

重點提示 Key Notes

Qualified-Operator List (合格操作員清單) 的重要性：

文件要求建立一份明確的「合格操作員清單」，這份清單必須：

  • 清楚標示每位操作員被授權執行的操作類型（開門/關門/兩者）
  • 在同一類別內，可根據操作的相似性進行分組（如：膠塞和密封件的裝載如果操作相似，可歸為一組）
  • 必須記錄在品質風險評估和 CCS 文件中

在法規檢查時，檢查官經常會要求查看這份清單，並交叉比對當天在 RABS 內執行開門操作的人員是否確實在授權名單上。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 的排班策略：在 CDMO 的多產品環境中，確保每個班次至少有一位經認證的開門操作人員在場是非常重要的。建議：

  • 建立分級操作員制度：Primary Operator（主操作員，具開門資格）和 Secondary Operator（副操作員，僅關門資格）
  • 排班系統中加入資格檢查邏輯，確保開門操作人員的覆蓋率
  • 設備設定（setup）階段需要開門操作，此時必須安排合格人員

  1. 在夜班期間，唯一具備開門操作資格的人員因突發狀況離開。此時發生了需要開門介入才能解決的設備故障。你會如何處理？
  2. 文件提到開門操作資格的操作員「可以」執行關門操作，但需要記錄在 CCS 文件中。為什麼這個「向下相容」還需要正式文件記錄？

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

資格不對稱性原則：Q3.3 建立了一個非常重要的規則 —— 操作資格的「單向相容性」：

邏輯很清楚：開門操作的風險遠高於關門操作，因為開門時 Grade A 環境直接暴露在 Grade B 背景中。能執行高風險操作的人，自然也能執行低風險操作，但反過來則不行。

Q 3.4 Qualified vs Unqualified Interventions | 已認證與未認證介入操作

原文內容 Original Text

Q 3.4. What are the recommendations for qualified and unqualified interventions?

Recommendation

Best Practices for Qualified Interventions

Interventions should be conducted by trained and qualified personnel. (See Question 3.2 on how to qualify.)

An approved standard operating procedure (SOP) containing all defined interventions with step-by-step instructions, mitigation actions (if any), and images (where applicable) should exist (1, 4, 7, 15, 16). A dynamic AVS should be performed simulating all the listed interventions, and these studies should be repeated with a defined frequency as described in Question 2.3.

Simulation of all qualified interventions in routine APS should be performed on a rotational basis; its maximum frequency of occurrence in routine batch-manufacturing should be covered in an initial qualification run and semiannual qualification runs. A rotation policy can be defined in an internal-site risk management process but should occur at least annually.

Management of Unqualified Interventions

When an unqualified intervention occurs, a real-time risk-based impact assessment shall be performed (See Question 1.7 on how to risk assess). If the assessment determines the unqualified intervention can be performed, only authorized personnel (See Question 3.2 on authorized personnel) may execute the unqualified intervention. If the risk assessment does not support the intervention as safe, then the intervention should not proceed and the appropriate steps to end the batch should be taken. As part of batch release, the impact of the unqualified intervention must be assessed for quality and compliance on the batch.

Unqualified interventions identified to become qualified interventions shall have AVS to demonstrate unidirectional airflow and preservation of First Air are not impacted. The unqualified intervention will then be included in the ongoing APS program and upon successful completion the intervention can be added to the local procedure. Unqualified interventions that are similar to qualified interventions may not require additional AVS execution or inclusion in the APS program, to become a qualified intervention, if proven equivalent with supporting technical and scientific rationale. In certain situations, unqualified interventions may not be recommended to become a qualified intervention, based upon special events, and will not require simulation in AVS and APS programs.

Rationale

Interventions should be designed and performed using proper aseptic techniques to minimize impact to the production environment and the risk of product contamination. An unqualified intervention does not have sufficient data to demonstrate the capability of control, hence, simulating the unqualified intervention in APS testing to demonstrate the process capability is recommended.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Qualified Intervention (已認證介入操作)：指已經過完整驗證流程的操作 —— 有 SOP 文件、已做過 AVS（氣流可視化研究）證明不影響 First Air、已在 APS（無菌製程模擬）中模擬並成功完成。這些操作是「預期中會發生的」，系統已證明可以安全執行。

Unqualified Intervention (未認證介入操作)：指尚未經過上述完整驗證流程的操作。這是「意料之外的」操作，例如設備出現從未遇過的故障，需要一種新的處理方式。

關鍵區別：「未認證」不等於「禁止」。文件建立了一套完整的決策流程來處理未認證操作。

比喻說明 Analogy

把這想像成一家航空公司的飛行手冊：

  • Qualified Interventions（標準操作程序）：就像飛行手冊中已列出的所有標準和緊急程序 —— 引擎失效怎麼辦、液壓系統故障怎麼辦。這些都已經過模擬器訓練和反覆演練。
  • Unqualified Interventions（未知情況）：就像遇到飛行手冊中沒有記載的全新狀況。機長必須即時評估風險（real-time risk assessment），決定是否能安全處理，還是應該返航（停止批次）。

而且，如果某個「未知情況」開始頻繁出現，航空公司就應該把它寫入手冊（將 unqualified 升級為 qualified），進行模擬器訓練（AVS + APS），讓所有飛行員都學會處理。

重點提示 Key Notes

未認證介入操作的決策樹：

  1. 即時風險評估：發生未認證操作時，立即執行風險評估
  2. 決策分叉：
    • 風險可接受 → 由授權人員執行，但必須在批次放行時評估其影響
    • 風險不可接受 → 不執行操作，採取適當步驟結束批次
  3. 後續處理：決定該未認證操作是否應升級為已認證操作
    • 需升級 → 執行 AVS 驗證 + 納入 APS 計畫
    • 與現有已認證操作相似 → 可透過技術合理性證明等效，不一定需要額外 AVS/APS
    • 屬於特殊事件 → 不建議升級為已認證操作

重點提示 Rotation Policy

已認證操作的輪替模擬策略：文件要求所有已認證操作都必須在常規 APS 中進行模擬，但採用「輪替」方式：

  • 初始認證 APS：涵蓋操作在常規生產中的最大發生頻率
  • 半年度認證 APS：輪替模擬不同的操作
  • 輪替週期：每個操作至少每年被模擬一次

這是一個務實的做法 —— 如果有 30 種已認證操作，不可能每次 APS 都模擬全部。透過輪替，確保所有操作在一個合理週期內都被驗證過。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 的介入操作管理挑戰：

CDMO 因為處理多種不同客戶的產品，面臨的介入操作種類可能比專一產品工廠多得多。建議建立以下系統：

  • 介入操作資料庫：統一管理所有已認證操作，標記適用的產品/設備組合
  • APS 輪替矩陣：規劃每次 APS 模擬哪些操作，確保年度覆蓋率 100%
  • 未認證操作追蹤系統：記錄每次未認證操作的發生、風險評估結果、後續處理決定
  • 升級審查會議：定期（如每季）審查未認證操作記錄，決定哪些應升級為已認證

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 一次充填批次中發生了從未見過的膠塞卡料方式，操作員用一種創新方法解決了問題（未認證操作）。批次順利完成。你的後續行動步驟是什麼？
  2. 文件提到某些「與已認證操作相似」的未認證操作，可以透過技術合理性證明等效而免除額外 AVS/APS。你認為判定「相似性」的標準應該包含哪些因素？
  3. 如果風險評估結論是「不支持該操作為安全的」，文件說應「採取適當步驟結束批次」。在 CDMO 環境中，終止批次的商業影響可能非常巨大。你認為如何平衡產品安全與商業壓力？

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 手套消毒是 CCS 的基本要素：每次進入 RABS 手套前必須消毒操作員手套，這是降低手套完整性破損風險的關鍵防線，尤其因為手套完整性測試通常是事後進行的
  • 四階段漸進式認證體系：從 Level 1（基礎訓練）到 Level 4（持續評估），操作人員必須循序漸進地取得資格，不可跳級。VR 訓練被提及為最佳實踐工具
  • 開門/關門操作的單向相容性：開門操作資格可向下相容執行關門操作，但關門操作資格不可執行開門操作。每個班次必須確保有開門操作合格人員在場
  • 已認證 vs 未認證操作的管理：已認證操作透過 APS 輪替進行持續驗證；未認證操作需即時風險評估，並根據結果決定是否升級為已認證操作
  • 取消資格機制：八項明確的取消資格條件確保人員能力的持續性，長時間缺席也是取消資格的理由之一
