6.0   Risk Triage Model  ｜  風險分流模型概述

6.0 Risk Triage Model

A preventive approach to managing the risk of drug shortages caused by manufacturing or quality issues includes a risk assessment method called triage. A triage approach allows a quick and easy assessment of the shortage risks of a product and prioritizes actions for proactive drug shortage management (risk control). Not all products require proactive drug shortage management. Only medically necessary and critical products and those with the greatest impact to patients require commensurate levels of effort and planning to ensure that the supply is adequate, and the availability component of the risk calculus is assessed and appropriately managed. The method and tools proposed in Section 6.0 are intended to help determine whether a proactive Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan is required for a product.

Companies should have robust QRM and knowledge management systems to both prevent (proactive) and respond (reactive) to drug shortages (see Section 5.0). Proactive QRM for drug shortage prevention should involve the following:

  • Categorizing each product by criticality based on its indication, marketplace importance, profile, current supply situation, and patients' needs
  • Establishing and periodically reviewing a Drug Shortage Risk Register—a single source of information on hazards and the related risks that can result in drug shortages—by proactively identifying and assessing risks to quality, compliance, and supply that could lead to a shortage. A Drug Shortage Risk Register (see Section 11.0: Appendix 1 for a sample risk register template) should provide the following information, which should be updated and reviewed on a regular basis:
    • Description of potential drug shortage risks for a product, including API, excipients, and sources of risk
    • Countries or regions that could be affected
    • Description of the current controls that are or are not in place, with respect to the prevention of shortages, and the known effectiveness of such controls
    • Risk level for a potential drug shortage, considering the current controls, new risk control actions that are required, owners, and due-date status
    • Key leading and lagging indicators that would trigger early and timely responses to potential shortages, including near-misses on a batch release or the ability to meet product demand
  • Ensuring timely reduction and management of risks by completing risk control actions defined in the risk control plan based on criticality of product availability. Issues to address in the risk control plan could include:
    • Inventory and safety stock planning
    • Demand and supply planning for all relevant markets using a sourcing strategy (e.g., dual sourcing)
    • Capacity planning for manufacturing and distribution (capacity reserve)
    • Availability of alternative therapies
    • Acceleration of health authority actions (e.g., approval of post-approval changes, inspections)
  • Establishing a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan, at least for medically necessary products, which could include early warning systems for potential shortages of certain products (see Section 12.0: Appendix 2 for a sample prevention and response plan template).

Responding to and disseminating information about a potential drug shortage in a timely and effective manner is especially important for medically necessary, life supporting, or life sustaining therapies. Since some shortages need to be addressed quickly, a preestablished and well-structured drug shortage response plan can be very valuable in laying out a clear path of actions to take to minimize the impact of a shortage, end the shortage, and quickly return product supply to its normal state.

解說與重點 Commentary

比喻：急診室分流（Triage）

「Triage（分流）」源自急診醫學。急診室每天湧入大量病患，護理師必須快速判斷——哪些人需要立刻處置，哪些人可以等候？這個優先排序的過程就叫「triage」。

TR68 將同樣的概念搬進藥品供應鏈管理：面對成百上千個品項，企業必須先快速評估「哪些產品一旦短缺，對病患的傷害最大？」，再集中資源在這些高風險品項上。

核心邏輯：並非每個產品都需要同等資源，資源應流向最需要保護的地方。

核心概念：QRM 的雙向角色

TR68 強調 QRM（Quality Risk Management）必須同時具備兩個面向：

  • 主動預防（Proactive）：短缺發生前，建立防線。例如識別高風險產品、建立庫存緩衝、評估供應商穩定性。
  • 被動應變（Reactive）：短缺已發生時，啟動應變計畫，最小化損害。

本節（6.0）聚焦主動預防端，而應變計畫的細節則在 Section 7.0 說明。

重點提示：Drug Shortage Risk Register 的五大內容

藥品短缺風險登記表（Risk Register）是整個 QRM 體系的「單一資訊來源（Single Source of Truth）」，必須涵蓋：

  • 潛在短缺風險的描述（含 API、賦形劑及其來源）
  • 可能受影響的國家/地區
  • 現行控制措施及其有效性評估
  • 風險等級（考量現行控制後的殘餘風險）
  • 早期預警指標（leading indicators）與落後指標（lagging indicators）

此登記表應定期更新與審查，不是「一次性文件」。

重點提示：Risk Control Plan 的六大要素

風控計畫（Risk Control Plan）中應處理的議題包括：

  • 庫存與安全庫存規劃
  • 需求與供應規劃（含雙重採購策略 dual sourcing）
  • 製造與配送的產能規劃（產能儲備）
  • 替代療法的可及性
  • 加速法規機關行動（例如批准批准後變更）
6.1   Description of the Risk Triage Model  ｜  風險分流模型描述

6.1 Description of the Risk Triage Model

The risk triage method uses a preliminary hazards analysis (PHA) approach, which simplifies the process after information is collected on the potential or actual drug shortage. A four-step PHA triage approach is used to evaluate the relationship of three risk elements—therapeutic use of a product, availability of alternatives, and likelihood of occurrence—to determine the risk of a drug shortage. The steps in the triage process depicted in Figure 6.1-1 are as follows:

  1. Identify impact level (impact on a patient) based on therapeutic use and availability of alternatives.
  2. Determine the likelihood of a drug shortage for the product, taking into account any drug shortage prevention controls that are in place and their likely effectiveness.
  3. Determine the risk priority level based on a combination of impact on the patient and the likelihood of a drug shortage for the product.
  4. Plan and implement the suggested risk controls for the assessed product based on the risk priority level.

The details of the triage model displayed in Figure 6.1.1-1 are described step by step in Sections 6.1.1–6.1.4.

The triage method incorporates key words used to create a simple risk assessment design approach. Rather than using very general, subjective terms to describe priority levels for each of the three elements used in the triage model (e.g., high, medium, and low severity), using discrete information allows the assessment to focus on product information rather than defining "high," "medium," and "low." Table 6.1-1 shows how the key words are used to assign a priority level for each of the three elements in the risk triage model. The current Shortage Prevention Controls will be listed in the drug shortage prevention plan.

解說與重點 Commentary

核心概念：PHA（初步危害分析）

Preliminary Hazards Analysis（PHA）是一種來自系統安全工程的風險評估方法，適用於在詳細資訊不足時進行快速、早期的危害識別與排序。

TR68 採用 PHA 的精神：在短缺發生之前，僅需三項關鍵資訊（治療用途、替代品可及性、短缺可能性）即可快速評估產品風險，不需要耗費大量資源進行深入分析。

核心概念：三個風險要素

風險分流模型的核心是三個維度的交集評估：

  • 治療用途（Therapeutic Use）：這個藥品是為了什麼疾病使用的？後果有多嚴重？
  • 替代品可及性（Availability of Alternatives）：如果這個產品缺貨，有沒有其他可替代的治療選項？
  • 短缺可能性（Likelihood of Occurrence）：考量現行控制措施後，這個產品實際發生短缺的機率有多高？

前兩者合併決定「衝擊等級（Risk Level A/B/C）」，再與第三者結合產生「優先等級（Priority 1/2/3）」。

重點提示：為何使用關鍵字而非「高/中/低」評分？

傳統風險評估常使用主觀的「高/中/低」評分，導致不同評估者之間結果不一致。TR68 改用具體的描述性關鍵字（如「無替代品可用」、「有類似療法」），讓評估聚焦在產品的實際特性，減少主觀判斷的空間，提高評估的一致性與可重複性。

思考練習 1：識別關鍵字

請根據 Table 6.1-1，為以下場景選擇正確的關鍵字描述：

  1. 抗癌標靶藥物 Trastuzumab（賀癌平），目前市面上僅此一種，無可替代的治療方案。
    → 治療用途：＿＿＿ / 替代品：＿＿＿
  2. 普通感冒咳嗽糖漿，藥局貨架上有多種品牌可選。
    → 治療用途：＿＿＿ / 替代品：＿＿＿
  3. 帕金森氏症口服藥（慢性用藥），同類藥物尚有其他廠牌可選。
    → 治療用途：＿＿＿ / 替代品：＿＿＿

參考答案：1. High / No alternatives available；2. Low / Same product available；3. Medium / Alternative products available

6.1.1   Step 1: Define Impact to the Patient  ｜  步驟一：定義病患衝擊

6.1.1 Step 1: Define Impact to the Patient

Companies can benefit from categorizing their products by therapeutic use, which makes it possible to apply a risk-based approach for managing drug shortage risks for their portfolio of products. A risk level indicating the severity of the impact of a shortage on the patient is assigned based on two factors: therapeutic use of the product and availability of alternatives, as shown in Table 6.1.1-1.

Table 6.1.1-1   Step 1: Define Impact on Patient

Therapeutic Use & Consequences if Product not Available Availability of Alternatives
No Alternatives Available Alternative Products / API Available Same Product / API Available
Medically necessary product, life supporting or life sustaining
Fatal or severe irreversible harm if the patient is not treated with the product		 Risk Level A Risk Level A Risk Level B
Acute short term or chronic long term
Severe harm but reversible if patient is not treated with the product		 Risk Level A Risk Level B Risk Level C
Other indications
Inconvenience if patient is not treated with the product		 Risk Level B Risk Level C Risk Level C

"Medically necessary products," "critical medicines," and "life supporting or life sustaining products" have the highest criticality based on patient need; therefore, their shortages present the greatest risk. (Definitions of terms are provided in Section 3.0.) Ensuring an uninterrupted, reliable supply of these products at all times to all patients who need them is key; therefore, robust controls must be in place to proactively manage the risk of shortages of these drugs.

Products used to treat acute short-term conditions or manage chronic long-term diseases (e.g., Parkinson's disease, diabetes, multiple sclerosis) constitute the next level of product indication-based criticality. Although the criticality of these products is not at the same level as that of life supporting or life sustaining therapies, the unavailability of these products over an extended period could harm patients; therefore, shortages of these products cannot be tolerated for long periods.

The lowest level of product indication-based criticality is assigned to medicinal products used for other minor indications (e.g., self-care therapies for minor ailments such as cough and cold). If a product is registered for more than one indication with different levels of indication-based criticality, the more conservative or higher level of criticality should generally be applied for this assessment. For example, if one of the product indications classifies a product as medically necessary, and the second indication classifies it for treatment of a chronic long-term condition, risk planning should be done assuming the higher level of product indication-based criticality, that is, for its use as a medically necessary product.

In addition to the therapeutic use of the product, the second dimension in ranking the impact of a shortage to patients is access to and availability of alternative therapies. Relevant health authorities may need to be consulted to determine where alternatives may exist for a product and for what markets they are available. Where no alternatives are available, especially for products that are medically necessary, life supporting, life sustaining, or used to treat acute short-term or chronic long-term conditions, the impact to the patient is the highest (Risk Level A – red). Therefore, the margin for tolerance of a drug shortage with these products is minimal, and controls for these risks should ensure that:

  • Focus is on proactive prevention of a shortage
  • Time to remedy and return supply conditions toward normal is as short as possible in the event of a shortage

Where an alternative therapy is available in the form of a similar product or other presentations of the same product, the impact to the patient is lower (Risk Level B – yellow). In the event of a shortage, it is important to ensure that these alternatives are accessible to patients in a timely manner to minimize impact (e.g., by preventing interruptions in treatment).

The lowest level of impact (Risk Level C – green) is assigned to medicinal products used for other indications (e.g., self-care therapies for minor ailments such as cough and cold) for which alternatives are readily available or for products used to treat acute short-term or chronic-long terms conditions that have the same product readily available in other presentations.

解說與重點 Commentary

核心概念：衝擊矩陣的邏輯

Table 6.1.1-1 是一個 3×3 的矩陣，縱軸（列）是治療用途嚴重性，橫軸（欄）是替代品可及性。交叉點即為衝擊等級。

矩陣的設計邏輯很直觀：

  • 治療越關鍵（救命用途）+ 替代品越少 → 衝擊越高 → Risk Level A（紅）
  • 治療次重要 + 有相似替代品可選 → Risk Level B（黃）
  • 輕微用途 + 替代品充足 → Risk Level C（綠）

比喻：用三個藥品理解 A/B/C

Risk Level A — 胰島素（Insulin）
第一型糖尿病患者每天需要注射胰島素，若缺貨且沒有完全等效的替代品，患者可能在數日內陷入酮酸中毒危及生命。衝擊是即時且不可逆的。→ Risk Level A。

Risk Level B — 地高辛（Digoxin）錠劑
心衰竭用藥。假設 0.25mg 錠劑缺貨，但市面上仍有 0.125mg 錠劑（需調整劑量）或注射劑型可轉換，衝擊降低但仍需謹慎管理。→ Risk Level B。

Risk Level C — OTC 咳嗽糖漿
感冒咳嗽用，症狀輕微，藥局有多種品牌可選，缺一不礙事。→ Risk Level C。

重點提示：多用途產品取最高等級

若一個產品同時有多種適應症，且不同適應症對應不同風險等級，應採用最高的等級（保守原則）。

例如：某抗生素在甲適應症屬「醫療必需品（Risk A）」，在乙適應症則屬「慢性病管理（Risk B）」，整體應按 Risk Level A 規劃。

重點提示：替代品評估須諮詢法規機關

替代品的可及性並非僅靠藥品本身判斷，還需考慮：

  • 替代品在各市場的上市許可狀態
  • 療效與安全性的等效程度（是否有相似的副作用或禁忌症）
  • 特殊族群（小兒、老年、腎衰竭患者）的劑型適用性
  • 需要諮詢相關主管機關，確認替代品在當地市場的可及性

思考練習 2：衝擊等級分類

請查閱 Table 6.1.1-1，判斷以下產品的 Risk Level（並說明原因）：

  1. Trastuzumab（賀癌平）：用於 HER2 陽性乳癌，目前無任何等效生物相似藥上市。
  2. 血漿蛋白療法：急/慢性用途，但市面上有不同廠牌的相同規格產品可取代。
  3. Metformin（二甲雙胍）：第二型糖尿病一線用藥（慢性），同藥理機轉之替代藥物（如 SGLT-2 抑制劑）有限但存在。

參考答案：1. Risk Level A（救命用途 + 無替代品）；2. Risk Level C（急慢性用途 + 相同產品可取得）；3. Risk Level B（急慢性用途 + 有替代品但非完全等效）

6.1.2   Step 2: Assess the Likelihood of Shortage  ｜  步驟二：評估短缺可能性

6.1.2 Step 2: Assess the Likelihood of Shortage

In the second step of the triage, the likelihood of shortage for a product is assessed. Hazards that could increase the likelihood of a shortage should be assessed and current controls should be assessed to identify the hazards. Where good, robust controls exist for a hazard, the likelihood of occurrence would be low; if there are limited to no controls for a hazard, the likelihood of occurrence increases. Historical data may be available for certain hazards, and this historical data should be leveraged to assess the relative likelihood of risks.

For example, product sourcing may be one of the controls assessed to determine the likelihood of a shortage. Single-sourcing of a product or key material supplier increases the likelihood of occurrence of a shortage in the event of an issue at that sole-sourced facility or supplier. Other controls may be related to:

  • Infrastructure maintenance and improvements
  • Technology updates
  • Business continuity planning and capacity assessment
  • Process capability
  • Inventory levels
  • Supplier diversification

This assessment of likelihood of occurrence may not be quantitative, which is considered acceptable because it is more important to assess the controls and their likely effectiveness and to understand the relative likelihood of the occurrence of a shortage for identified hazards. In this risk triage model, a simple three-level (high, medium, low) scale for assessing the likelihood of occurrence has been proposed.

解說與重點 Commentary

比喻：單引擎 vs 雙引擎飛機

想像兩架飛機橫越太平洋：

  • 單引擎飛機：只要那一具引擎故障，飛機立刻面臨危機，沒有後備。→ 單一來源（Single-source）供應，短缺可能性高（High）。
  • 雙引擎飛機 + 備用燃料：一具引擎故障，另一具可繼續飛行，加上有備用燃料，危機耐受度高。→ 多元來源（Multi-source）+ 充足安全庫存，短缺可能性低（Low）。

藥品供應鏈的邏輯相同：現行控制措施越完善，短缺可能性越低。

核心概念：短缺可能性的影響因子

評估短缺可能性時，需考量以下控制措施是否到位及其有效性：

控制面向高可能性（控制不足）低可能性（控制充足）
供應來源單一來源（Single-source）多元來源（Multi-source）
庫存水準庫存不足，無緩衝充足安全庫存（Safety Stock）
製造能力無備用廠房，切換時間長（>6個月）備用廠房已就緒（<4個月）
流程穩定性製程能力不足，良率低CPV 監控完善，流程穩定
基礎設施設備老舊，維護不足定期維護，現代化設備

重點提示：定性評估即可，不須量化

TR68 明確指出，短缺可能性的評估不一定需要量化（例如給出 30% 概率），重要的是：

  • 理解現有控制措施是否有效
  • 能夠相對比較各危害的可能性高低

使用三級尺度（高/中/低）即可滿足分流模型的需求。這與傳統精細的 FMEA 計分方式不同，強調的是實用性與效率。

  2. At each risk level, consider the likelihood of drug shortage (Step 2)

A single-sourced product with no backup or a product with insufficient safety stock presents the highest risk and, in combination with Risk Levels A and B (from Step 1 of the triage), would result in the highest risk priority level (Risk Priority Level 1). A multisourced product with a low capacity reserve (e.g., preparation of a backup manufacturing facility to produce the product will take at least six months) or with sufficient short-term inventory has a medium likelihood of occurrence and, in combination with Risk Levels A and B (from Step 1 of the triage), would result in a medium risk priority level (Risk Priority Level 2). A multisourced product with an adequate capacity reserve (e.g., an active backup manufacturing facility or will require less than four months to prepare to produce the product) and a sufficient inventory level represents the lowest risk and, in combination with Risk Levels B and C, would result in the lowest risk priority level (Risk Priority Level 3).

In this example, no alternative therapies are currently marketed or are in clinical trials for a biological drug product used to treat late-stage cancer. The product is dual-sourced but does not have enough inventory reserves to cover a major manufacturing or quality issue.

These risk ratings are used in the two-step triage risk analysis shown in Figure 6.1.3-1. The high risk categorization for therapeutic use and availability of potential alternatives results in designating the risk level as high (Risk Level A) from Step 1 of the triage. This likelihood of a shortage is then assessed as medium in Step 2 of the triage. In Step 3, Risk Level A combined with the medium likelihood of a drug shortage results in the overall Risk Priority Level 1. Given this risk priority level, a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan needs to be developed for this product (see Section 7.0).

6.1.4   Step 4: Develop and Implement Preventive Risk Control Strategies  ｜  步驟四：制定與執行預防性風控策略

6.1.4 Step 4: Develop and Implement Preventive Risk Control Strategies

After the risk priority levels are assigned to the product in Steps 1–3 of the triage, appropriate risk control strategies (may be predetermined in some instances) should be developed and implemented. Some actions to consider based on the risk priority level are:

Risk Priority Level 1

  • Develop a value stream map (VSM) (starting materials through final drug product packaging and distribution) to identify and reduce inventory and manufacturing risk stress points. Identify appropriate inventory levels for each manufacturing stage based on the current state of controls, probability of drug shortage, and how long a shortage can be tolerated without adverse effects on patients. In addition, the need for a multisourcing strategy should be assessed, taking business considerations into account with senior management involvement.
  • Review supplier management controls for high risk products and high risk suppliers of the components of these high risk products. Implement controls for these products where gaps are identified in supplier management controls.
  • Proactively create and maintain a plan to communicate shortage risks with health authorities, distribution channels, and users. The guide Quality and Manufacturing Driven Supply Disruptions: Industry Communication to Authorities, developed by the European industry collaboration of EFPIA, EGA, AESGP, and PTTA, provides principles and a framework to harmonize reporting and communications between MAHs and health authorities about drug shortages due to quality and/or manufacturing disruptions.
  • Implement an effective Stage 3 continuous process verification (CPV) or ongoing process-verification monitoring program for CPV data that could help identify manufacturing issues before they occur.

Risk Priority Level 2

  • Consider additional proactive inventory management controls and a multisourcing cost–benefit analysis.
  • Consider VSM for specific manufacturing processes to identify inventory stress points and potential manufacturing problems.
  • At a minimum, ensure that CPV plans for these products to undergo additional review as part of the company's drug shortage management activities.

Risk Priority Level 3

  • A Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan is not necessary, but these risks should be reviewed regularly.

High and medium risks, along with the risk control actions and level of residual risk in the Drug Shortage Risk Register, should be tracked, periodically reviewed, and managed.

Examples of risk control strategies are provided in Table 6.1.4. The level of effort and formality required for each product assessment depends on the level of risk identified. These examples are not all-inclusive. Organizations should evaluate and determine which strategies for drug shortage control will be the most effective for the risks identified for their specific product and its use. Where appropriate, these strategies should be documented in procedures, integrated into the quality system and/or business processes, implemented in a consistent manner across the company, and actively managed using a Drug Shortage Risk Register. Section 11.0: Appendix 1 provides a template for creating a Drug Shortage Risk Register.

In general, risk control strategies vary, based on product specifics or its node in the supply chain. The listed factors and stakeholders in the root causes for shortages should also be considered.

解說與重點 Commentary

核心概念：三個優先等級的應對策略摘要

優先等級 必要行動 強制程度
Level 1 制定預防措施 + 應變計畫（Required） 強制必做
Level 2 建議制定措施；可選擇接受殘餘風險（Recommended） 強烈建議
Level 3 一般可接受風險等級，定期審查即可 持續監控

核心概念：三大關鍵工具

1. VSM（Value Stream Mapping，價值流程圖）

VSM 是精實製造（Lean Manufacturing）的工具，用於繪製從起始原料到成品配送的完整供應流，以視覺化方式識別庫存與製造的風險壓力點（stress points）。對 Priority 1 產品為強制執行，Priority 2 建議用於特定製程節點。

2. CPV（Continuous Process Verification，持續製程確效）

ICH Q10 所定義的 Stage 3 製程確效。CPV 透過持續收集與分析製程數據，在問題真正導致批次失敗之前，提前偵測製造異常趨勢。對 Priority 1 產品必須實施完整 CPV 計畫；Priority 2 至少要將 CPV 計畫納入藥品短缺管理活動的額外審查。

3. 多元供應源（Multisourcing）

建立備用 API 供應商、備用製造廠，降低單點故障（single point of failure）風險。Priority 1 產品須進行多元供應源需求評估，並納入高階管理層決策；Priority 2 產品進行成本效益分析。

重點提示：風控策略需嵌入 QMS

TR68 強調，風控策略不能只是紙上談兵——應：

  • 以書面程序（SOP）文件化
  • 整合進公司品質系統（QMS）及業務流程
  • 以一致方式在全公司推行
  • 透過 Drug Shortage Risk Register 主動管理

Risk Register（Section 11.0 附錄一有範本）是所有風控措施的追蹤中心，應定期審查。

比喻：Priority 1 = 飛行前預檢 vs Priority 3 = 一般例行保養

航空業有嚴格分級：

  • 長程國際客機（高影響、高可見度）：每次飛行前都要做詳細安全檢查（CPV 類比），飛行員和工程師都要確認備用系統就緒（VSM + 多元來源類比）。
  • 地面服務車輛（低影響）：按月保養，不用每次出車前做航前檢查。

TR68 的 Priority 1/2/3 分層，就是讓企業像航空業一樣：把最嚴格的控制，放在最關鍵的地方。

Section 3A 章節總結 Summary

本節涵蓋 TR68 Section 6.0–6.1.4，介紹風險分流模型（Risk Triage Model）的理論基礎與四步驟操作框架。

  • 6.0 Risk Triage Model：採用「分流（Triage）」概念，讓企業快速識別哪些產品需要主動的藥品短缺預防管理。並非所有產品都需要同等資源，QRM 體系應同時具備主動預防（Drug Shortage Risk Register）與被動應變（Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan）兩個面向。
  • 6.1 四步驟 PHA 分流框架：使用初步危害分析（PHA）方法，評估三個風險要素（治療用途、替代品可及性、短缺可能性），並以關鍵字描述代替主觀評分，提高評估一致性。
  • 6.1.1 Step 1 — 衝擊等級（Risk Level A/B/C）：透過 3×3 矩陣交叉評估治療用途嚴重性與替代品可及性。Risk Level A（紅）= 最高衝擊；C（綠）= 最低衝擊。多用途產品取最高等級。
  • 6.1.2 Step 2 — 短缺可能性（High/Medium/Low）：評估現行控制措施（供應來源、庫存、製造備援、流程穩定性）。單一來源 + 庫存不足 → 高可能性；多元來源 + 充足備援 → 低可能性。定性三級評估即可，不需量化。
  • 6.1.3 Step 3 — 風險優先等級（Priority 1/2/3）：查對矩陣 Table 6.1.3-1，Risk Level 與 Likelihood 的交叉點決定優先等級。Priority 1 = 必須制定預防措施；Priority 3 = 一般可接受風險，定期審查即可。
  • 6.1.4 Step 4 — 風控策略：Priority 1 需要 VSM + 多元供應源評估 + CPV + 應變計畫 + 法規溝通機制；Priority 2 考量 VSM 及成本效益分析；Priority 3 定期審查。所有策略應嵌入 QMS 並透過 Drug Shortage Risk Register 主動管理。
