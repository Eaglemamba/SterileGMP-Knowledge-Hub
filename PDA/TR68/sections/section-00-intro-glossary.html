1.0Introduction  |  導論

1.0 Introduction

PDA Technical Report No. 68: Risk-Based Approach for Prevention and Management of Drug Shortages was originally developed in 2014 as part of the interassociation collaborative response to the European Medicines Agency's (EMA) initiative on medicinal product shortages caused by manufacturing, quality, and/or good manufacturing practice (GMP) issues. This revision updates the 2014 version to recognize emerging regulatory expectations and current terminology. The risk-based approach and concepts in the original technical report are broadly recognized and remain unchanged.

This revision recognizes the advances made in pharmaceutical manufacturing and aligns the text of TR-68 with the current regulatory environment, for example, the International Council for Harmonisation (ICH) Quality Guideline Q9(R1): Quality Risk Management, ICH Quality Guideline Q12: Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management, regional regulations and guidelines, and terminologies used for drug shortages that have since been published. Examples for preventive measures to avoid drug shortages consider lessons learned since TR-68 was first published in 2014. Elements in the templates and forms included in this revision have been updated to reflect current regulatory expectations and terminology used in the European Union and its Member States and in the United States, while it also supports compliance with global expectations on managing and preventing drug shortages.

Sustainable access to safe, efficacious, and high-quality products every time a patient needs them is just as important as the therapeutic advances that have transformed the lives and survival of patients worldwide. The ICH Q9(R1) defines harm as "damage to health, including damage that can occur from loss of product quality or availability." Ensuring uninterrupted availability of and access to products, especially medically necessary, lifesaving or life sustaining critical medicines that do not have effective alternatives, is essential.

核心概念：TR-68 是什麼？

PDA Technical Report No. 68（TR-68）是由製藥業領導協會 PDA（Parenteral Drug Association）所發布的技術指引，聚焦於「藥品短缺的預防與管理」。

  • 首版：2014 年，回應 EMA（歐洲藥品管理局）對製造/品質導致藥品短缺的議題。
  • 2024 修訂版：納入 ICH Q9(R1)（品質風險管理）與 ICH Q12（產品生命週期管理）的最新監管框架。
  • 核心方法不變：風險基礎（risk-based）的預防與緩解方法仍是主軸。

生活比喻：預防勝於治療

想像藥品短缺就像醫院的「急診室爆滿」——平時若有良好的預防性健康管理（風險控制），急診需求自然降低。TR-68 的精神正是：在問題發生之前，就建立系統性的風險識別與預防機制，而非等短缺發生後才緊急應變。

重點：ICH Q9(R1) 對「危害」的新定義

ICH Q9(R1) 將「危害（harm）」定義為：「對健康的損害，包括因產品品質或可用性喪失所導致的損害。」

這是重大轉變——過去「harm」主要指品質缺陷，新定義明確將「缺貨/供應中斷」也納入危害範疇，直接將藥品短缺問題提升為品質風險管理的核心議題。

1.1Background  |  背景

1.1 Background

Drug shortages have become more frequent, more severe, and longer-lasting globally since the early 2000s for a variety of reasons. As globalization increases, the complexity of supply chains grows, global and regional regulatory expectations evolve, and economic and business motivators change. The drivers behind drug shortages in different geographic areas and countries vary, and those causes are usually multifactorial. The range of causes includes financial, economic, and business concerns, increased or unanticipated market demand (e.g., pandemic), evolving regulatory expectations, manufacturing and quality issues, capacity constraints, lack of redundancy, and supply chain issues. Recognition has evolved that the multifactorial root causes for shortages extend beyond manufacturing and quality issues, as has the need for multistakeholder interaction and collaboration to address the local issue of a shortage in a multiregional environment.

The emerging global challenge of supply disruptions is drawing greater focus from the pharmaceutical industry, regulators, legislators, healthcare providers, patient organizations, and patients, and drug shortages are now covered in the media to a much larger extent. ICH Q9(R1) places greater emphasis on product availability risks—a noteworthy change from the original guideline—in the context of quality and manufacturing issues, including GMP noncompliance, that can be a "significant cause of product availability issues (e.g., product shortages)." Furthermore, while "manufacturing and supply chain diversity can be enablers of product availability, increasingly complex supply chains lead to interdependencies that can introduce systemic quality/manufacturing risks impacting supply chain robustness."

The legislative and regulatory processes for shortage-related issues continue to evolve. In 2012, EMA published a reflection paper and implementation plan for drug shortages that affect patients. EMA followed up by holding workshops with stakeholders in October 2013 and again in October 2015. In 2016, the European Heads of Medicines Agencies (HMA) and EMA established a joint task force to develop and coordinate actions for better prevention, identification, management, and communication of issues affecting the availability of medicines to improve the continuity of medicines supply across Europe. In 2020, the European Commission's Pharmaceutical Strategy further elaborated upon the need for proactive measures to address the issue of drug shortages, and in March 2023, the HMA/EMA Task Force held a third workshop to inform and update stakeholders on their deliverables and activities.

The focus on drug shortages has also increased in the United States. In 2013, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) established a Drug Shortage Task Force, which published a strategic plan for preventing and reducing drug shortages. The task force continues to identify the root causes of drug shortages and advance long-term solutions, reporting annually to Congress. As in many countries, notification of a potential drug shortage is a legal requirement in the United States, and early and timely notifications has reduced the number of shortages by enabling manufacturers and health authorities to jointly take steps to minimize supply disruption. The annual reports to Congress for calendar years 2021 and 2022 included the impact of the increased risk of shortages during the COVID-19 pandemic and the actions taken to address them.

A 2023 Majority Staff Report of the U.S. Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs, Short Supply: The Health and National Security Risks of Drug Shortages, concluded that active drug shortages had risen by 30% between 2021 and 2022, lasted one-and-a-half years longer on average, and impacted patient care. According to the report, overreliance on foreign resources was deemed a national security risk, FDA lacked critical information to mitigate shortages, and both industry and regulators lacked end-to-end supply chain visibility.

It is essential for the senior management of a firm that is the Marketing Authorization Holder (MAH) to have control of and be accountable for ensuring the uninterrupted supply of their products as long as it is within their control. Senior Management decisions impact not only a firm's ability to cause shortages but also to prevent and recover from shortages in an expedient manner. As stated in ICH Q9(R1), the principles of quality risk management (QRM) that enable risk-based decision-making by both companies and health authorities are fundamental for the proactive prevention of drug shortages.

"Addressing lifecycle risks to manufacturing reliability and quality assurance is the foundation for supply predictability." — ICH Concept Paper, November 2020

核心概念：藥品短缺的多因素根源

自 2000 年代初期起，全球藥品短缺問題逐漸惡化，原因是「多因素（multifactorial）」的，主要包括：

  • 財務與商業因素：低利潤藥品被廠商放棄生產
  • 供應鏈全球化：API（活性藥品成分）高度集中在少數國家製造
  • 法規要求演進：GMP 合規標準日益嚴格，導致廠商停產或召回
  • 需求突增：如 COVID-19 疫情期間的非預期需求
  • 產能不足與缺乏備援：單一廠址、單一來源的脆弱架構

生活比喻：供應鏈就像水管網絡

全球製藥供應鏈就像一個錯綜複雜的城市水管網絡。全球化使管線越鋪越長、越交錯越複雜，任何一段管線的破裂（製造問題、法規查廠）都可能影響下游整個城市（患者）的供水。

ICH Q9(R1) 特別指出：供應鏈多樣性雖可提升可用性，但過度複雜的供應鏈反而會引入系統性風險，這是一個值得警惕的悖論。

重點：MAH 高層管理的責任

TR-68 明確指出：行銷授權持有人（MAH，Marketing Authorization Holder）的高層管理對確保產品不間斷供應負有直接責任，其決策不僅影響短缺發生的可能性，也決定了從短缺中恢復的速度。

這不只是 QA 部門的問題，而是企業治理層級的核心義務。

關鍵統計數據（2023 美國參院報告）

  • 2021–2022 年間，活躍藥品短缺案例增加 30%
  • 短缺持續時間平均延長 1.5 年
  • 過度依賴境外資源被認定為國家安全風險
  • FDA 與業界均缺乏端對端供應鏈可視性

1.2Purpose and Scope  |  目的與範圍

1.2 Purpose and Scope

This revision of TR-68 addresses the following aspects for proactive prevention of drug shortages and provides useful tools to assist in preparation:

  • A holistic, risk-based framework at a product level for the prevention and management of drug shortages caused by manufacturing and quality issues
  • A risk triage model that can be used to assess drug shortage risks and implement appropriate controls in the end-to-end value chain for the manufacturing and distribution of a product (Section 11.0, Appendix 1)
  • Templates for developing a Drug Shortage Risk Register and a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan at a product level (Section 12.0, Appendix 2)

The concepts and approach presented in this report may be adopted and applied by other stakeholders in the entire supply chain, such as wholesale distributors, as well as suppliers and vendors of materials used in processing, for example, filters, single-use bags, flow paths, resins, and solvents.

TR-68 focuses on addressing shortages caused by manufacturing and quality issues toward batch release. The next step in the supply chain after manufacturing and release is usually called the "handover, to the first economic customer," after which the MAH often has limited-to-no visibility (e.g., shortages related to issues that arise in distribution channels, pharmacies, or hospitals). Shortages after the "handover, to the first economic customer" are out of scope of this technical report. Nevertheless, there may be situations where the MAH is responsible for distribution as well and, therefore, is responsible for mitigating shortages in these instances. TR-68 does apply in these situations.

The framework provided in TR-68 also supports and enables MAHs and manufacturers to meet the requirements of Section 506C of the U.S. Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act and Article 13 of EU Commission Directive 2003/94/EC to notify relevant health authorities in the event of a drug shortage.

Development of PDA's risk-based approach for prevention and management of drug shortages at a product level began in 2012 as part of the Paradigm Change in Manufacturing Operations (PCMO®) project and incorporated input from the EMA-sanctioned industry interassociation task force and multistakeholder events. PDA also participated in various HMA/EMA multistakeholder workshops, including the one held in 2023; key outcomes from the workshop discussions have been incorporated into this revision of TR-68.

Additionally, PDA Technical Report No. 54: Implementation of Quality Risk Management for Pharmaceutical and Biotechnology Manufacturing Operations and its series of best-practice and companion case-study documents provide detailed guidance and practical application on how to implement QRM for the management of product quality risks in manufacturing operations, supply chains, and operations of third parties (e.g., suppliers, contract manufacturers). PDA Technical Report No. 59: Utilization of Statistical Methods for Production Monitoring describes additional risk-based activities that use statistical methodologies for quality control and manufacturing.

中文術語解說 Glossary Commentary

核心概念：TR-68 術語體系概覽

本節 18 個術語構成 TR-68 的語意基礎，可按功能分為四大群組：

  • 藥品分類：Critical Medicine、Medically Necessary Drug、Life Supporting/Sustaining Drug
  • 短缺類型：Drug Shortage、Critical Shortage、Potential Drug Shortage、Meaningful Disruption
  • 風險管理工具：QRM、Risk、Residual Risk、Risk Triage、Risk Priority Level
  • 管理文件：Risk Register、Prevention & Response Plan、Value Stream Map

生活比喻：三種藥品重要性層次

想像一個醫院的藥品清單：

  • Critical Medicine（關鍵藥品）：考量治療適應症、替代治療可及性、供應鏈脆弱性。例如：抗生素、化療藥物。
  • Medically Necessary Drug（醫療必要藥品）：治療或預防嚴重疾病，且無合適替代品。例如：某些罕見病用藥。
  • Life Supporting/Sustaining Drug（維生藥品）：缺少此藥，患者的基本生理功能將中斷。例如：胰島素、呼吸器用藥。

越往後，短缺的緊迫性越高，監管通報與緩解措施的優先順序也越高。

重點辨析：「藥品短缺」的四個關鍵元素

TR-68 對「Drug Shortage（藥品短缺）」的定義包含四個必須理解的子元素：

  • Shortage（短缺本身）：識別目前、即將發生或預期的供應中斷
  • Supply（供應）：MAH 投放市場的總存量
  • Demand（需求）：醫療專業人員/患者基於臨床需要的請求；需在正確時間、足夠數量下獲得
  • National level（國家層級）：在全國層面供不應求；地區性物流問題（如配送困難）不算藥品短缺

重點辨析：Meaningful Disruption vs. 一般停產

重大供應中斷（Meaningful Disruption） 需滿足：

  • 生產變更合理地可能導致供應明顯減少
  • 影響廠商的訂單履行能力或預期需求的供應

不屬於 Meaningful Disruption：

  • 例行維修保養（routine maintenance）
  • 預計在短期內可恢復運作的細微生產調整

這一定義直接影響廠商是否需要向 FDA 提交「早期通報」的義務觸發點。

風險定義公式

TR-68 沿用 ICH Q9(R1) 的風險定義：

Risk = Probability of Harm × Severity of Harm

其中「危害（Harm）」= 健康損害，包括因產品品質或可用性喪失所致的損害

「剩餘風險（Residual Risk）」= 實施風險控制措施後仍殘存的短缺風險——這是評估現有控制措施是否足夠的重要指標。

核心概念：Risk Priority Level 的三個維度

風險優先等級（Risk Priority Level）由以下三個維度的組合決定：

  1. 治療用途與患者衝擊：產品不可及對患者的嚴重程度
  2. 替代品可及性：是否有其他有效替代治療
  3. 短缺可能性：供應中斷發生的概率評估

這三個維度對應到 Section 11.0 的風險分流模型（Risk Triage Model），是整個 TR-68 方法論的核心評分機制。

請判斷以下情境是否構成 TR-68 定義下的「Meaningful Disruption」，並說明理由：

縮寫重要性提示：MAH 的核心地位

MAH（行銷授權持有人） 是 TR-68 中最核心的角色。在歐盟法規架構下，MAH 是藥品上市許可的持有者，對藥品的品質、安全性及供應連續性負有最終責任。

在台灣，相當於「藥品許可證持有人」，即藥廠或藥商持有衛福部食藥署核發的藥品許可證者。

關鍵縮寫：ANDA vs. NDA 對供應鏈的意涵

  • NDA（新藥申請）：原廠藥（innovator drug）的上市申請，通常有更強的市場地位，但若停產則無替代品
  • ANDA（簡化新藥申請）：學名藥（generic drug）的上市申請，市場上可能有多家競爭者，短缺衝擊相對較小

這一區別在評估「替代品可及性」時至關重要，直接影響 Risk Priority Level 的評分。

PAC（核准後變更）與藥品短缺的關聯

PAC（Post-Approval Change，核准後變更） 是供應鏈風險的重要觸發點。廠商在進行製造場址變更、供應商變更或製程變更時，需依法向主管機關申請 PAC，處理時間可能長達數月至數年，期間若未有適當備貨，即可能引發藥品短缺。

TR-68 的風險框架將 PAC 管理納入預防措施的重要考量之一。

  • TR-68 的定位：PDA 於 2014 年發布、2024 年修訂的技術指引，聚焦於藥品短缺的「預防性風險管理」，並與 ICH Q9(R1)、ICH Q12 全面對齊。
  • 問題嚴重性：自 2000 年代以來，全球藥品短缺日趨頻繁、嚴重且持久，根本原因是多因素的（製造品質、供應鏈全球化、法規演進、經濟因素、需求突增），需要多方利害關係人協作解決。
  • 監管趨勢：EMA、FDA 均已建立藥品短缺工作組與通報機制；美國法規（FD&C Act §506C）與歐盟指令（2003/94/EC 第 13 條）均要求 MAH 通報潛在短缺；早期通報已被證實有效降低短缺件數。
  • TR-68 的工具箱：提供整體風險框架、風險分流模型（Appendix 1）及藥品短缺風險登錄冊與預防應變計畫範本（Appendix 2），適用範圍從製造到「移交第一經濟客戶」。
  • 關鍵術語：熟記 Drug Shortage、Potential Drug Shortage、Critical Shortage、Meaningful Disruption、Risk Priority Level 及 Residual Risk 的精確定義，是正確應用 TR-68 方法論的前提。
  • MAH 責任：高層管理須對產品不間斷供應負責；風險基礎決策（QRM 原則）是主動預防藥品短缺的根本。
