1.0Introduction  |  導論

1.0 Introduction

PDA Technical Report No. 68: Risk-Based Approach for Prevention and Management of Drug Shortages was originally developed in 2014 as part of the interassociation collaborative response to the European Medicines Agency's (EMA) initiative on medicinal product shortages caused by manufacturing, quality, and/or good manufacturing practice (GMP) issues. This revision updates the 2014 version to recognize emerging regulatory expectations and current terminology. The risk-based approach and concepts in the original technical report are broadly recognized and remain unchanged.

This revision recognizes the advances made in pharmaceutical manufacturing and aligns the text of TR-68 with the current regulatory environment, for example, the International Council for Harmonisation (ICH) Quality Guideline Q9(R1): Quality Risk Management, ICH Quality Guideline Q12: Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management, regional regulations and guidelines, and terminologies used for drug shortages that have since been published. Examples for preventive measures to avoid drug shortages consider lessons learned since TR-68 was first published in 2014. Elements in the templates and forms included in this revision have been updated to reflect current regulatory expectations and terminology used in the European Union and its Member States and in the United States, while it also supports compliance with global expectations on managing and preventing drug shortages.

Sustainable access to safe, efficacious, and high-quality products every time a patient needs them is just as important as the therapeutic advances that have transformed the lives and survival of patients worldwide. The ICH Q9(R1) defines harm as "damage to health, including damage that can occur from loss of product quality or availability." Ensuring uninterrupted availability of and access to products, especially medically necessary, lifesaving or life sustaining critical medicines that do not have effective alternatives, is essential.

1.1Background  |  背景

1.1 Background

Drug shortages have become more frequent, more severe, and longer-lasting globally since the early 2000s for a variety of reasons. As globalization increases, the complexity of supply chains grows, global and regional regulatory expectations evolve, and economic and business motivators change. The drivers behind drug shortages in different geographic areas and countries vary, and those causes are usually multifactorial. The range of causes includes financial, economic, and business concerns, increased or unanticipated market demand (e.g., pandemic), evolving regulatory expectations, manufacturing and quality issues, capacity constraints, lack of redundancy, and supply chain issues. Recognition has evolved that the multifactorial root causes for shortages extend beyond manufacturing and quality issues, as has the need for multistakeholder interaction and collaboration to address the local issue of a shortage in a multiregional environment.

The emerging global challenge of supply disruptions is drawing greater focus from the pharmaceutical industry, regulators, legislators, healthcare providers, patient organizations, and patients, and drug shortages are now covered in the media to a much larger extent. ICH Q9(R1) places greater emphasis on product availability risks—a noteworthy change from the original guideline—in the context of quality and manufacturing issues, including GMP noncompliance, that can be a "significant cause of product availability issues (e.g., product shortages)." Furthermore, while "manufacturing and supply chain diversity can be enablers of product availability, increasingly complex supply chains lead to interdependencies that can introduce systemic quality/manufacturing risks impacting supply chain robustness."

The legislative and regulatory processes for shortage-related issues continue to evolve. In 2012, EMA published a reflection paper and implementation plan for drug shortages that affect patients. EMA followed up by holding workshops with stakeholders in October 2013 and again in October 2015. In 2016, the European Heads of Medicines Agencies (HMA) and EMA established a joint task force to develop and coordinate actions for better prevention, identification, management, and communication of issues affecting the availability of medicines to improve the continuity of medicines supply across Europe. In 2020, the European Commission's Pharmaceutical Strategy further elaborated upon the need for proactive measures to address the issue of drug shortages, and in March 2023, the HMA/EMA Task Force held a third workshop to inform and update stakeholders on their deliverables and activities.

The focus on drug shortages has also increased in the United States. In 2013, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) established a Drug Shortage Task Force, which published a strategic plan for preventing and reducing drug shortages. The task force continues to identify the root causes of drug shortages and advance long-term solutions, reporting annually to Congress. As in many countries, notification of a potential drug shortage is a legal requirement in the United States, and early and timely notifications has reduced the number of shortages by enabling manufacturers and health authorities to jointly take steps to minimize supply disruption. The annual reports to Congress for calendar years 2021 and 2022 included the impact of the increased risk of shortages during the COVID-19 pandemic and the actions taken to address them.

A 2023 Majority Staff Report of the U.S. Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs, Short Supply: The Health and National Security Risks of Drug Shortages, concluded that active drug shortages had risen by 30% between 2021 and 2022, lasted one-and-a-half years longer on average, and impacted patient care. According to the report, overreliance on foreign resources was deemed a national security risk, FDA lacked critical information to mitigate shortages, and both industry and regulators lacked end-to-end supply chain visibility.

It is essential for the senior management of a firm that is the Marketing Authorization Holder (MAH) to have control of and be accountable for ensuring the uninterrupted supply of their products as long as it is within their control. Senior Management decisions impact not only a firm's ability to cause shortages but also to prevent and recover from shortages in an expedient manner. As stated in ICH Q9(R1), the principles of quality risk management (QRM) that enable risk-based decision-making by both companies and health authorities are fundamental for the proactive prevention of drug shortages.

"Addressing lifecycle risks to manufacturing reliability and quality assurance is the foundation for supply predictability." — ICH Concept Paper, November 2020

1.2Purpose and Scope  |  目的與範圍

1.2 Purpose and Scope

This revision of TR-68 addresses the following aspects for proactive prevention of drug shortages and provides useful tools to assist in preparation:

  • A holistic, risk-based framework at a product level for the prevention and management of drug shortages caused by manufacturing and quality issues
  • A risk triage model that can be used to assess drug shortage risks and implement appropriate controls in the end-to-end value chain for the manufacturing and distribution of a product (Section 11.0, Appendix 1)
  • Templates for developing a Drug Shortage Risk Register and a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan at a product level (Section 12.0, Appendix 2)

The concepts and approach presented in this report may be adopted and applied by other stakeholders in the entire supply chain, such as wholesale distributors, as well as suppliers and vendors of materials used in processing, for example, filters, single-use bags, flow paths, resins, and solvents.

TR-68 focuses on addressing shortages caused by manufacturing and quality issues toward batch release. The next step in the supply chain after manufacturing and release is usually called the "handover, to the first economic customer," after which the MAH often has limited-to-no visibility (e.g., shortages related to issues that arise in distribution channels, pharmacies, or hospitals). Shortages after the "handover, to the first economic customer" are out of scope of this technical report. Nevertheless, there may be situations where the MAH is responsible for distribution as well and, therefore, is responsible for mitigating shortages in these instances. TR-68 does apply in these situations.

The framework provided in TR-68 also supports and enables MAHs and manufacturers to meet the requirements of Section 506C of the U.S. Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act and Article 13 of EU Commission Directive 2003/94/EC to notify relevant health authorities in the event of a drug shortage.

Development of PDA's risk-based approach for prevention and management of drug shortages at a product level began in 2012 as part of the Paradigm Change in Manufacturing Operations (PCMO®) project and incorporated input from the EMA-sanctioned industry interassociation task force and multistakeholder events. PDA also participated in various HMA/EMA multistakeholder workshops, including the one held in 2023; key outcomes from the workshop discussions have been incorporated into this revision of TR-68.

Additionally, PDA Technical Report No. 54: Implementation of Quality Risk Management for Pharmaceutical and Biotechnology Manufacturing Operations and its series of best-practice and companion case-study documents provide detailed guidance and practical application on how to implement QRM for the management of product quality risks in manufacturing operations, supply chains, and operations of third parties (e.g., suppliers, contract manufacturers). PDA Technical Report No. 59: Utilization of Statistical Methods for Production Monitoring describes additional risk-based activities that use statistical methodologies for quality control and manufacturing.

2.0 Glossary and Abbreviations

The following key terms are defined for the purposes of this Technical Report.

Term Definition
Critical Medicine Critical medicines, sometimes called "essential medicines" or "medically necessary drugs," are considered critical for public health, taking into consideration the therapeutic indication of the medicines, the availability of alternative treatments, and the vulnerability of the supply chain.
Corrective Action and Preventative Action (CAPA) Action to eliminate the cause of a detected nonconformity or other undesirable situation.
Critical Shortage A shortage where no alternative treatment is available.
Drug Shortage or Shortage A shortage of a drug or medicinal product that occurs when supply does not meet demand at a national level, where a:
  • Shortage allows for identification of current, impeding, or anticipated disruption of supply of a medicinal product.
  • Supply refers to the total volume of stock of the individual medicinal product that is placed on the market by the MAH.
  • Demand relates to the request for a medicinal product by a healthcare professional, veterinarian, or patient in response to a clinical need. For demand to be satisfactorily met, the medicinal product will need to be acquired in time and in sufficient quantity to allow continuity of best care of patients or animals.
  • National level refers to the situation in a specific country, that is, if there is an insufficient supply of medicine to meet the demands of the country overall. Logistics-related issues leading to regional supply disruption (e.g., delivery difficulties, national redistribution of stock) are a short-term and localized problem and should not be taken into account.
Drug Shortage Prevention & Response Plan A document that provides a structured action plan to proactively prevent drug shortages and also respond to a shortage in the event that one occurs. This also may also be known as a "Drug Shortage Prevention and Mitigation Plan."
Drug Shortage Risk Register A single source of information on risks that can result in drug shortages, associated risk levels, risk control actions with owners, status, due dates, and residual risk after appropriate risk control actions have been taken.
Harm Damage to health, including the damage that can occur from loss of product quality or availability.
Knowledge Management Systematic approach to acquiring, analyzing, storing, and disseminating information related to products, manufacturing processes, and components.
Life Supporting or Life Sustaining Drug A product that is essential to, or that yields information that is essential to, the restoration or continuation of a bodily function that is important to the continuation of human life.
Meaningful Disruption A meaningful disruption is a change in production that is reasonably likely to lead to a reduction in the supply of a drug by a manufacturer that is more than negligible and affects the ability of the manufacturer to fill orders or meet expected demand for its product. A meaningful disruption is not an interruption in manufacturing due to matters such as routine maintenance and does not include insignificant changes in manufacturing so long as the manufacturer expects to resume operations in a short period of time.
Medically Necessary Drug Any drug product used to treat or prevent a serious disease or medical condition for which there is no other adequately available drug product that is judged by medical staff to be an appropriate substitute.
Potential Drug Shortage A potential drug shortage is described as the occurrence of internal or external situations (single or in a combination of both), which could result in an interruption of supplies of a medicinal product, if not properly addressed and controlled.
Quality Risk Management A systematic process for the assessment, control, communication, and review of risks to the quality of the drug (medicinal) product across the product lifecycle.
Residual Risk Risk remaining after risk control measures have been implemented. In the context of drug shortages, this is considered to be the risk of a shortage after preventive and other risk control measures have been implemented.
Risk The combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm, where harm is defined as "damage to health, including the damage that can occur from loss of product quality or availability."
Risk-based Triage for Drug Shortages A process of assessing and assigning priorities for managing drug shortage risks based on criticality and impact to patients.
Risk Priority Level A relative priority ranking assigned to a risk based on a combination of (a) therapeutic use of a product and patient impact due to product unavailability, (b) availability of alternatives, and (c) likelihood of a shortage.
Value Stream Map A tool used to document, analyze, understand, and improve the flow of information or materials required to produce a product or service for a customer as it makes its way through the value stream (a set of actions that take place to add value for customers from the initial request through realization of value by the customer).

核心概念：TR-68 術語體系概覽

本節 18 個術語構成 TR-68 的語意基礎，可按功能分為四大群組：

  • 藥品分類：Critical Medicine、Medically Necessary Drug、Life Supporting/Sustaining Drug
  • 短缺類型：Drug Shortage、Critical Shortage、Potential Drug Shortage、Meaningful Disruption
  • 風險管理工具：QRM、Risk、Residual Risk、Risk Triage、Risk Priority Level
  • 管理文件：Risk Register、Prevention & Response Plan、Value Stream Map

生活比喻：三種藥品重要性層次

想像一個醫院的藥品清單：

  • Critical Medicine（關鍵藥品）：考量治療適應症、替代治療可及性、供應鏈脆弱性。例如：抗生素、化療藥物。
  • Medically Necessary Drug（醫療必要藥品）：治療或預防嚴重疾病，且無合適替代品。例如：某些罕見病用藥。
  • Life Supporting/Sustaining Drug（維生藥品）：缺少此藥，患者的基本生理功能將中斷。例如：胰島素、呼吸器用藥。

越往後，短缺的緊迫性越高，監管通報與緩解措施的優先順序也越高。

重點辨析：「藥品短缺」的四個關鍵元素

TR-68 對「Drug Shortage（藥品短缺）」的定義包含四個必須理解的子元素：

  • Shortage（短缺本身）：識別目前、即將發生或預期的供應中斷
  • Supply（供應）：MAH 投放市場的總存量
  • Demand（需求）：醫療專業人員/患者基於臨床需要的請求；需在正確時間、足夠數量下獲得
  • National level（國家層級）：在全國層面供不應求；地區性物流問題（如配送困難）不算藥品短缺

重點辨析：Meaningful Disruption vs. 一般停產

重大供應中斷（Meaningful Disruption） 需滿足：

  • 生產變更合理地可能導致供應明顯減少
  • 影響廠商的訂單履行能力或預期需求的供應

不屬於 Meaningful Disruption：

  • 例行維修保養（routine maintenance）
  • 預計在短期內可恢復運作的細微生產調整

這一定義直接影響廠商是否需要向 FDA 提交「早期通報」的義務觸發點。

風險定義公式

TR-68 沿用 ICH Q9(R1) 的風險定義：

Risk = Probability of Harm × Severity of Harm

其中「危害（Harm）」= 健康損害，包括因產品品質或可用性喪失所致的損害

「剩餘風險（Residual Risk）」= 實施風險控制措施後仍殘存的短缺風險——這是評估現有控制措施是否足夠的重要指標。

核心概念：Risk Priority Level 的三個維度

風險優先等級（Risk Priority Level）由以下三個維度的組合決定：

  1. 治療用途與患者衝擊：產品不可及對患者的嚴重程度
  2. 替代品可及性：是否有其他有效替代治療
  3. 短缺可能性：供應中斷發生的概率評估

這三個維度對應到 Section 11.0 的風險分流模型（Risk Triage Model），是整個 TR-68 方法論的核心評分機制。

關鍵縮寫：ANDA vs. NDA 對供應鏈的意涵

  • NDA（新藥申請）：原廠藥（innovator drug）的上市申請，通常有更強的市場地位，但若停產則無替代品

Recognizing Drug Shortages as a Global Problem

將藥品短缺認識為全球性問題  |  p6–p8

Original Text (English)

The primary objective in the management of drug shortages is preventing harm to patients. In any situation, it is important to maintain the continuity of supply such that all patients receive the medicines they need every time they need them to manage their medical condition. The risk of potential drug shortages should be considered when making decisions related to the supply and/or manufacture of drug products and their corresponding active substances. This technical report addresses how drug shortages caused by manufacturing and quality issues can be anticipated and avoided using a risk-based approach at a product level.

A good example of a shortage that had global impact and needed to be managed by multiple health authorities was "The Great Acetonitrile Shortage" in 2008–2009. Acetonitrile is used as a solvent in many analytical procedures that are included in submissions for the approval of drug products and in chemical reactions for the manufacture of active pharmaceutical ingredients (API). The two main manufacturers of this solvent, both located in the same area in China, were required to stop manufacturing acetonitrile to reduce air pollution for the 2008 Summer Olympics. A few months later, in September of 2008, acetonitrile production in Texas was interrupted due to hurricanes in the Gulf of Mexico. The combination of these two events caused shortages of several APIs and drug products. Only a few companies were aware that the product would not be manufactured for a year and, subsequently, had built up their inventories of the product in advance. Most regulatory agencies accepted alternative analytical methods on a short-term basis. The FDA also allowed changes to existing validated test methods within an approved application, either a new drug application (NDA) or an abbreviated new drug application (ANDA), to accommodate using less acetonitrile or an alternative solvent to acetonitrile. The Agency allowed companies to process this as a minor change in the Annual Report for the given drug application as long as the change met the criteria stated in the FDA Guidance for Industry: Changes to an Approved NDA or ANDA and Code of Federal Regulations [21 CFR 314.70(d)(2)(vii)].

This example illustrates that, in some cases, drug shortages can occur very suddenly and for reasons that are difficult to predict. Common antibiotics such as amoxicillin have also experienced shortages globally due to supply-chain disruptions. Hence, it is important for companies and regulators alike to do their best to anticipate potential drug shortages and prevent them from happening. Improved surveillance and communications through forums and consortiums, such as the International Supply Chain Consortium (Rx-360), can help in managing widespread impact in such instances.

The 2013 Birgli® report identified three principal types of causes for drug shortages—economic, business, and manufacturing supply chain issues—that are still highly relevant today. Since then, an awareness of the diversity of possible root causes for shortages has continued to evolve. 核心概念：為何藥品短缺是全球問題？

TR-68 的第一個重要聲明——藥品短缺管理的首要目標是防止病患受害（preventing harm to patients）——確立了本文件的核心倫理立場：供應連續性不僅是商業考量，更是病患安全義務。

本節以製造與品質問題為主軸，區別於商業或經濟因素，為後續各節的風險評估框架奠定範疇。

案例解析：乙腈大短缺（2008–2009）

乙腈（Acetonitrile）是 HPLC（高效液相層析法）最常用的有機溶劑，廣泛用於藥品分析方法中。2008 年的「乙腈大短缺」完美示範了供應鏈單點依賴（single-point dependency）的脆弱性：

  • 事件一：中國奧運前停產 → 生產端衝擊
  • 事件二：德州颶風打斷生產 → 地理集中風險
  • 合力結果：多個 API 和藥品出現全球性短缺

把這個故事想像成「城市只有兩家麵包廠，都在同一個低窪地帶——一旦淹水，全城人無麵包可吃」。對 CDMO 而言，這意味著關鍵輔料（溶劑、賦形劑）的供應商多元化是風險管理的必要措施，而非可選項。

Evolution of Terminologies

術語定義的演進  |  p8

Original Text (English)

Though single, internationally harmonized definitions do not currently exist for "drug shortage," "meaningful disruption," and "medically necessary product," various definitions of these terms have been published.

The FDA defines drug shortage as "a period of time when the demand or projected demand for the drug in the United States exceeds the drug's supply." In general, the FDA Drug Shortage Staff focuses on shortages of medically necessary products that have a significant effect on public health.

The EMA earlier described drug shortages as "disruptions to the manufacturing supply chain" and updated the definition of shortages in 2019 to be more specific in terms of the gap between supply and demand. In addition, individual EU member states have local definitions of drug shortages. The EU's current definition of "critical medicines" also includes the criteria of therapeutic use, availability of alternative(s), and supply-chain vulnerabilities.

The World Health Organization (WHO) refers to a "shortage" as a situation where the supply of medicines, health products or vaccines identified as essential by the health system is considered to be insufficient to meet public health and patient needs. WHO indicates that:

On the demand side: a "shortage" will occur when demand exceeds supply at any point in the supply chain and may ultimately create a "stockout" at the point of appropriate service delivery to the patient if the cause of the shortage cannot be resolved in a timely manner relative to the clinical needs of the patient.

The understanding of these terms may also vary in some respects by the different stakeholders in the supply chain (MAHs, regulatory authorities, distributors, wholesalers, providers, and patients). However, there is alignment on the concept that these definitions are based on the fundamental importance of ensuring an uninterrupted supply of medicines to prevent harm to patients. They are also aligned with public-service obligations held by MAHs and wholesale distributors in relation to ensuring the supply of medicinal products in the markets in which they operate.

目前全球沒有統一的「藥品短缺」國際定義——這本身就是一個合規挑戰。以下為三大主要機構的定義核心：

機構 定義核心 關鍵特點

Reporting Requirements and Current Regulatory Landscapes

通報要求與現行法規環境  |  p8–p10

Original Text (English)

Reporting potential shortages to relevant health authorities is a legal requirement. In the United States, Section 506C of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act establishes notification requirements to manufacturers of product discontinuance. Title X of the Food and Drug Administration Safety and Innovation Act, enacted in July 2012 to address drug shortages, also led to Section 506C being amended to require annual reporting of drug shortages to the U.S. Congress. However, it has been noted that manufacturers are not required by law to report increased demand or export restrictions for life supporting or life sustaining products.

In the European Union, Article 13 of EU GMP Directive 2017/1572 defines the legal requirement for a manufacturer "to inform the competent authority and, if applicable, the MAH of any defect that could result in a recall or an abnormal restriction on supply." Local legislation implementing this directive in the member states has demonstrated individual approaches being followed by each country. Additionally, in August 2014, Chapter 5: Production of EudraLex – Volume 4 was updated to introduce guidance in relation to supply restrictions and their notification to the MAH (Sec. 5.71). It specified that such notifications "should be done in a timely manner to facilitate reporting of the restriction in supply by the MAH, to the relevant competent authorities." Other health authorities (e.g., individual EU member states, Health Canada, Swissmedic, Therapeutic Goods Administration) are also implementing regulatory requirements in this regard. ICH Q9(R1) further highlights the need for appropriate and timely management of health authority notification.

While reporting requirements and mechanisms for drug shortages vary across different health authorities and are subject to varying legal requirements, the expectation of visibility to shortage information has increased over the last several years. Other associations, such as the American Society of Health System Pharmacists, also may provide information on drug shortages and their management through their websites. Health Canada has provided a multistakeholder toolkit that provides strategies and tools for key players in the supply chain. The differences in reporting requirements and approaches in different countries continue to make it challenging to draw meaningful comparisons of data on drug shortages or to quantify the problem at a global or regional level.

The European Federation of Pharmaceutical Industries and Associations (EFPIA), European Generic and Biosimilar Medicines Association (EGA), Association of the European Self-Care Industry (AESGP), and Plasma Protein Therapeutics Association (PPTA) jointly developed a harmonized communication framework for potential drug shortages, which includes harmonized trigger points for notifying competent authorities of drug shortages that are due to quality and manufacturing problems.

In the United States, Congress legislates the FDA's authority to act in response to drug shortages. In October 2013, FDA published its Strategic Plan for Preventing and Reducing Drug Shortages. It addresses mitigation strategies for manufacturers, such as notifying the FDA of potential shortages promptly so that manufacturers and the FDA can jointly take steps to reduce supply disruption. Another element of the FDA's drug shortage strategic plan focuses on preventive strategies, such as providing positive incentives for manufacturing and quality improvements and using proactive risk-based approaches for early identification and prevention of manufacturing and quality problems.

In 2012, the EMA published a Reflection Paper on Medicinal Product Supply Shortages Caused by Manufacturing/Good Manufacturing Practice Compliance Problems and a supporting Implementation Plan 2012–2015. In 2019, HMA and EMA published a Guidance on Detection and Notification of Shortages of Medicinal Products for MAHs in the Union (EEA), which also recommends that MAHs utilize PDA TR-68 as a reference.

The increased transparency of shortages, coupled with the mandatory requirements for early notification to health authorities of potential shortages, has allowed authorities and MAHs to work together more proactively to control and prevent potential drug shortage risks before they result in a supply disruption.

Although not all shortages result in patient harm, health authorities try to determine the causes of shortages that do affect patients and "near misses" that could have resulted in patient harm. The objective of doing so is to be prepared to proactively implement actions that can prevent recurrences. 3.3

Framework for Managing Drug Shortages due to Manufacturing and Quality Problems

製造與品質問題引起之藥品短缺管理框架  |  p10–p11

Original Text (English)

As illustrated in Figure 3.0-1, the potential causes of drug shortages are often multifactorial and complex. Business, environmental, and economic policies are also important in controlling the risks to drug availability across the end-to-end product value chain, manufacturing being a key step. Therefore, efforts must be concentrated on maintaining uninterrupted manufacturing and distribution and minimizing the risks that packaged drugs will be unavailable.

In the context of product value or supply chain, a drug shortage starts whenever an API, excipient, API starting material, reagent, solvent, component, or even packaging material cannot be made available for the manufacturing process. This can occur due to several reasons, including:

  • Manufacturing, quality, and GMP compliance issues
  • Inadequate manufacturing capacity to meet increased product demand
  • Lack of redundancy
  • Complexity of the supply chain
  • Evolving global regulatory requirements and the complexity associated with implementing them
  • Dynamic market conditions

Country- or region-specific import testing requirements can add complexity to supply chains and restrict companies from recovering quickly, potentially turning "near misses" into actual drug shortages.

Manufacturing and quality issues are often listed as a cause of drug shortages in many regions. In this context, shortages might occur as a result of issues related to such factors as the supply of materials, maintenance and technical operation of facilities and equipment, unavailable equipment, or variable manufacturing processes. Any of these factors may result in the generation of nonconforming batches or in GMP- or regulatory-compliance issues being identified by the company, health authorities, or others.

As part of the EMA interassociation drug shortages project, PDA and the International Society for Pharmaceutical Engineering (ISPE) coordinated efforts at both the product and systems levels to address the prevention of drug shortages caused by manufacturing and quality problems. They advised that companies need to focus on operating effectively in an environment of increasing global complexity while continuing to supply products.

TR-68 provides a holistic, risk-based framework at a product level for the prevention and management of drug shortages caused by manufacturing and quality issues. Risk-based approaches and risk-based decision-making are more broadly expected, accepted, and integrated into regulations and regulatory oversight mechanisms. However, the industry and regulators are still on a learning curve around the consistent application and dynamic use of risk-based approaches and risk-based decision-making in this area.

核心概念：供應鏈的「短缺起點」遠比想像的早

TR-68 明確指出，藥品短缺並非從藥房架上缺貨才開始，而是從供應鏈的任一環節的材料無法取得就已開始：

  • 原料藥（API）或其起始原料（starting material）
  • 賦形劑（excipient）、試劑（reagent）、溶劑（solvent）
  • 包裝材料（packaging material）

這對 CDMO 的意涵是：風險管理必須從原料採購端就開始，而非等到製造失敗才應對。

比喻說明：供應鏈如同多米諾骨牌

想像一張骨牌長鏈：API 供應商 → 原料測試 → 製造 → 包裝 → 倉儲 → 配送 → 醫院。任何一張骨牌倒下，後面的都跟著倒——差別在於骨牌之間的「緩衝距離」（亦即庫存與冗餘設計）決定了衝擊能否被吸收。

「Country-specific import testing requirements（各國特定進口檢測要求）」是一塊特別難預測的骨牌：本可快速恢復的供應中斷，卻因各國進口重新檢測程序，拖延數週而演變成真正的短缺。

重點提示：TR-68 在 PDA-ISPE-EMA 協作框架中的定位

TR-68 的制訂是 PDA 與 ISPE 在 EMA 跨協會藥品短缺專案下的協同成果，這賦予了 TR-68 超越一般行業建議書的地位：

  • 產品層面（product level）：TR-68 的風險評估框架
  • 系統層面（systems level）：ISPE 的供應鏈整體設計

TR-68 強調的「risk-based framework at a product level」，與 ICH Q9(R1)、ICH Q10 的品質系統框架相互呼應——製造合規不是終點，而是預防短缺的必要基礎。

「industry and regulators are still on a learning curve」這句話是對整個行業的誠實評估——接受監管機構的稽查意見和 CAPA（corrective and preventive action，矯正與預防措施）作為學習機會，而非視之為威脅。

4.0

Management Responsibilities

管理責任  |  p11–p12

Original Text (English)

A drug manufacturing company's interest is in developing, producing, and making pharmaceutical or biopharmaceutical medicines available to patients in a financially sustainable manner. Once approved by relevant health authorities, a drug should be available with no interruptions to supply; the manufacturer is accountable for producing and making the drug available while the wholesale distributors have a general service obligation to bring the drug to the patients. Senior management of the MAH is accountable for decisions that may impact the company's ability to cause, prevent, and/or recover from drug shortages. It is also accountable for ensuring the availability of their medicinal products in the amount needed and of the right level of quality.

To ensure continued supply, it is important for management to ensure regulatory compliance, as well as compliance to GMP and good distribution practices (GDP), and a robust quality culture that is maintained and enhanced over time. Elements of this quality culture include:

  • Competent employees
  • Desired behaviors
  • Learning environment
  • Decision-making (in areas that can impact the availability of drugs, such as decisions related to new supplier approvals, quality defects, deviations, and change controls)
  • Proactive QRM (i.e., ability to foresee and prevent drug shortages)
  • Environments where people can speak up freely about issues
  • Opportunities for improvement and interest in continuous improvements (e.g., technologies, systems, working methods)

Maintaining compliance with GMP requirements across all sites and functions is a prerequisite to ensure uninterrupted supply. Senior management of the MAH is ultimately accountable for maintaining manufacturing sites and other areas covered by pharmaceutical regulations, at a minimum, in compliance with GMPs and relevant regulatory requirements and/or commitments. This includes the MAH accepting accountability for oversight and the mitigation of any product quality and/or supply risks at third-party suppliers or service providers (e.g., contract development and manufacturing organizations (CDMO), contract laboratory organizations). Contracts and third-party relationships should be established with clear expectations and obligations related to quality and managing capacity constraints that might impact product supply or result in shortages.

Where noncompliance is identified, either by company employees via internal audits or other means or through health authority inspections, all efforts should be made to correct it and ensure compliance.

When a drug shortage is anticipated, senior management is responsible for initiating a dialog with the pertinent regulatory authorities. The objective of the dialog should be to reach a viable solution that considers the elements of patient safety, GMP/GDP and regulatory compliance, drug availability, technical abilities, and sustainable business operations.

Applied quality principles aimed at continually improving quality performance are also expected to be fully integrated into business operations and to go beyond compliance with the minimum GMP/GDP requirements.

There are many ways in which the MAH can ensure uninterrupted supply and receive early warning of potential supply issues; this technical report provides a structured approach to achieve such an overview using a risk triage model. Companies can consider assigning a customer service representative, a role analogous to an internal auditor or risk manager, to monitor issues that may lead to drug shortages. Those assessments would provide management with the timely information necessary to understand the risks to patients and the business because of drug shortages. Such a responsibility would be valuable because of the diversity of causes of drug shortages.

Once management obtains information about shortages, it must develop an action plan for addressing the drug shortage risks and make risk-based decisions on how resources will be managed and, potentially, how capital is invested to ensure appropriate capacity and redundancy.

5.0 Risk-Based Approaches for Prevention of Drug Shortages 藥品短缺預防之風險導向方法

5.0 Risk-Based Approaches for Prevention of Drug Shortages

A holistic approach to the prevention and management of drug shortages focused on patient safety is important and complex. There are multiple elements, both at the system and product levels, that work individually or in combination to prevent a shortage:

  • At the product level, an end-to-end understanding of the value chain for manufacturing and distribution provides the ability to understand and implement end-to-end controls.
  • At the system level, appropriate oversight and a robust pharmaceutical quality system (PQS) ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations.

Both approaches should also establish mechanisms that provide early warning signals of potential shortages, including near-misses, assess potential root causes for the shortage considering multiple aspects, drive targeted manufacturing and product quality improvements (e.g., introduction of new technologies, continual improvement, innovation), and enable timely communications with key stakeholders and health authorities. Other foundational elements should:

  • Ensure strong management accountability
  • Foster a sustainable quality culture that enables employees to speak up
  • Reward behaviors that drive joint ownership between operations and quality units for timely issue resolution
  • Build people skills and capabilities that drive change leadership and excellence in pharmaceutical operations
  • Establish a governance model that tracks operational and quality performance with appropriate metrics

As part of a robust quality system, companies should implement QRM principles and knowledge management capabilities to accomplish the following:

  • Proactively identify what controls are, and are not, in place for shortage prevention (for critical products at least)
  • Proactively identify and control risks of interruption in manufacturing and product supply
  • Link to clinical and safety risk assessments for their products
  • Facilitate risk-based decision-making by companies and health authorities in response to shortages in order to control and reduce harm to patients

Opportunities for informed and timely decision-making based on QRM principles, knowledge management, and a good understanding of patient impact in the event of a shortage allow companies to proactively manage and prevent drug shortages. When drugs become unexpectedly unavailable in the manufacturing supply chain, risk-based decision-making enables companies to provide key risk-indicating information to health authorities in a timely manner before the shortage becomes severe.

In addition to internal risk and knowledge management, it is helpful to have systems that generate reliable and up-to-date information about the marketplace for a company's products (e.g., trends in demand, usage volumes, market-share data). This combined body of information can also be very useful in decision-making for both the company and health authorities in order to respond to a shortage. It can help both parties understand what the risks of a shortage are to patients, what advice to communicate to healthcare providers, and what measures to take in order to respond quickly to a drug shortage. Information about the supply position for competitor products and alternative therapies is also important for assessing the risks of shortage-related issues.

中文解析：整體性預防框架

核心概念：雙層架構

TR68 的預防框架同時運作於兩個層面，缺一不可：

  • 產品層級 (Product Level)：對製造與配送端對端價值鏈 (end-to-end value chain) 有深入理解，從原物料到病患均納入管控視野。
  • 系統層級 (System Level)：建立健全的藥品品質系統 (Pharmaceutical Quality System, PQS)，確保符合適用法規，並形成組織文化支柱。

兩者並非替代關係，而是相輔相成——產品層級的控制依賴系統層級的制度支撐，系統層級的有效性則透過產品層級的實際表現來驗證。

比喻：消防系統 vs. 建築安全文化

想像一棟建築的防火管理：

  • 產品層級就像每條走廊都安裝了偵煙器、灑水頭、逃生指示燈——針對每個「節點」設置具體控制。
  • 系統層級則像整棟建築的安全文化、定期演練、管理階層的問責機制——確保所有控制措施持續有效運作。

藥品短缺預防亦同：光有產品控制點卻缺乏系統性支撐，危機發生時依然會手足無措。

圖 5.0-1 解讀：端對端價值鏈

重點提示：近未遂事件 (Near-Miss) 的價值

框架要求納入「近未遂」(near-miss)的早期預警訊號，而不只是等到實際短缺才行動。這呼應了航空安全管理的「事件報告文化」：大部分重大事故前，都有多次被忽視的近未遂訊號。

對 CDMO 而言，這意味著：每一次原物料延遲、設備停機、偏差發生，都應評估其潛在供應影響，而非僅從 GMP 合規角度審查。

重點提示：市場情報的重要性

TR68 特別強調，除了內部風險管理，還需建立市場資訊系統，包含：需求趨勢、使用量、市占率數據，以及競爭者與替代療法的供應狀況。這些資訊對公司與衛生主管機關在短缺時共同決策至關重要。

  • 當原物料廠商通知交貨延誤 2 週時，您的 SOP 中是否有觸發「供應風險評估」的機制？
  • 您的組織是否有跨部門（採購、生產、品保、業務）的短缺預防委員會？
5.1 Concepts of Risk and Knowledge Management 風險管理與知識管理的核心概念

5.1 Concepts of Risk and Knowledge Management

According to ICH Quality Guideline Q10: Pharmaceutical Quality System, QRM and knowledge management are enablers of the PQS. As such, QRM and knowledge management are intended to facilitate the practical integration of all elements of the PQS "to enhance the quality and availability of medicines around the world in the interest of public health."

A good understanding of the manufacturing process and end-to-end supply chain, up to the transfer of product from the MAH to the first economic customer (i.e., to wholesale distributors), is necessary for appropriate QRM to identify, assess, control, and review the risks of potential drug shortages. Once the hazards are identified and the risks are assessed, appropriate risk control measures can be implemented to reduce (control, mitigate, or eliminate) the risks of potential drug shortages before they lead to actual drug shortages. Of course, not all hazards can be anticipated; in such instances, as soon as new information on a hazard becomes available, risk-review activities can ensure timely assessment and implementation of risk control actions.

Knowledge management supports the PQS by facilitating easier access to product and process knowledge throughout the product's lifecycle. This availability of knowledge regarding process-performance and product-quality monitoring systems can enhance process understanding. In addition, ICH Q10 calls for process and product monitoring throughout product development and commercial manufacturing, so the information can be used to establish and actively manage a timely control strategy for manufacturing. Finally, management review of process performance and product quality should identify appropriate actions for continual improvement, such as the capture and dissemination of knowledge and the identification of post-approval changes (PAC).

Most companies have mechanisms to respond to drug shortages when they occur; however, these mechanisms might not be comprehensive or fully integrated into company procedures. A comprehensive approach for managing potential drug shortages should involve:

  • Ability to respond quickly to minimize the impact of a shortage
  • Framework for anticipating drug shortage triggers and risks and for establishing mechanisms to control risks and prevent shortages
  • Mechanisms and processes for timely communications

The EFPIA Good Practice Guide: Reducing Risk for Drug Product Shortages describes a "good practice" model that a company can adopt to prevent and/or manage potential drug shortage situations.

A company's QRM policy and related procedures should address risks to patient safety in the context of product quality and availability. Typically, risk ranking levels in a company's QRM policy include ranking for GMP and regulatory compliance risks and patient safety risks based on product quality. Table 5.1-1 provides an example of a risk ranking scale that incorporates considerations for both product quality and product availability in the ranking of patient safety risks.

While manufacturing and quality issues could impact product quality or patient safety, there may be instances when product quality or patient safety are not impacted. Still, there may be issues with regard to compliance with GMP or GDP or regulatory commitments provided in the product dossier. When handling GMP and regulatory compliance issues or deviations that may negatively impact the release of a product, the company must quickly identify the potential issue and work on a solution(s) to mitigate the risk of a drug shortage. Hence, it is important that the education and qualification of employees who manage deviations include aspects associated with the assessment of potential supply impact in case an issue evolves further. It is also important to establish mechanisms for escalating these GMP, GDP, and regulatory compliance issues to the appropriate level of management, as solutions might often require the allocation of resources and identification of alternatives. Companies often handle such issues internally, but they should involve the relevant health authorities early on in case there is a potential risk of a drug shortage, and a solution is unachievable in a short timeframe.

  • Shortage of a medically necessary (life supporting or life sustaining) product

  • Withdrawal of GMP certificate or manufacturing authorization
  • Critical health authority observations or repeat inspection observations
  • Systemic breakdown of GMP systems

The process of knowledge management follows the QRM process according to ICH Q9(R1). Understanding and managing risks provides knowledge that can then be used to appropriately prevent and manage risks on an ongoing basis. Thus, the QRM process explained in PDA TR-54 is a foundation for the understanding and implementation of appropriate knowledge management. It is important in this context to understand which questions are answered by the knowledge available and who needs the knowledge to enable the prevention and management of risks, including the risks of drug shortages. Therefore, an understanding of quality risks based on the available knowledge about the product, manufacturing process, value chain, and overall effectiveness of the quality system forms the foundation for all efforts performed and resources spent. Without this fundamental understanding, activities related to risk and knowledge management may not be fruitful.

中文解析：QRM 與知識管理雙支柱

核心概念：ICH Q10 的兩大賦能要素

根據 ICH Q10 藥品品質系統，有兩項要素被定義為 PQS 的「賦能要素 (Enablers)」：

  • 品質風險管理 (Quality Risk Management, QRM)：識別、評估、控制與審查潛在風險的系統性方法，依據 ICH Q9(R1) 執行。
  • 知識管理 (Knowledge Management)：確保產品/製程知識在整個生命週期中可被存取、傳遞並用於決策。

兩者都是達成 PQS 目標的工具，而非目的本身——終極目標是「提升全球藥品品質與可及性，保障公眾健康」。

比喻：PDCA 循環與藥品短缺預防

QRM 流程與知識管理的關係，可用 PDCA 持續改善循環來理解：

  • Plan（計畫）：根據現有知識識別危害、評估風險 → 制訂風險控制措施。
  • Do（執行）：實施控制措施，例如建立備用供應商、增加安全庫存。
  • Check（查核）：風險審查活動，確認控制措施有效；監控製程表現與產品品質。
  • Act（行動）：管理審查識別改善機會，擷取並傳播新知識，識別上市後變更 (PAC)。

「知識管理」就是這個循環的「記憶體」——確保每一輪 PDCA 的學習成果不流失，能被下一輪利用。

QRM 核心公式框架

根據 ICH Q9(R1) 與 PDA TR-54，QRM 的標準流程包含四個步驟：

QRM Process =
  ① Identify Hazards（識別危害）
+ ② Assess Risks（評估風險：嚴重性 × 發生率 × 可偵測性）
+ ③ Control Risks（控制風險：消除 / 降低 / 接受）
+ ④ Review Risks（持續審查）

Risk Priority = Severity × Probability × Detectability
（風險優先數 = 嚴重程度 × 發生機率 × 可偵測性）

供應中斷的「嚴重程度」評估，必須納入該品項的醫療必要性——生命支持類產品一律歸入最高嚴重等級。

表 5.1-1 解讀：三級風險對 CDMO 的意涵

高風險 (High) — 需即時啟動緊急應變

  • 產品面：涉及死亡/永久傷害，或生命支持/維持產品短缺。CDMO 必須在最短時間內通知客戶 MAH，並共同評估是否需要向衛生機關通報。
  • GMP 面：警告函 (Warning Letter)、召回、GMP 證書撤銷、重複查驗缺失——任何一項都可能直接導致停產，進而觸發高風險短缺。

中風險 (Medium) — 需加速處理，密切監控升級風險

  • 產品面：可逆性傷害，或用於急性/慢性適應症產品的短缺。這類短缺雖非立即危及生命，但時間拉長後影響仍可能很大。
  • GMP 面：主要查驗缺失 (Major observation)、高抱怨率——需在 CAPA 計畫中評估潛在供應衝擊。

低風險 (Low) — 常規管理，但仍需記錄

  • 非醫療必需品且有多個替代來源的短缺，或次要 GMP 偏差。即使列為低風險，仍應追蹤，避免累積效應升級。

重點提示：偏差評估必須涵蓋供應影響

TR68 明確指出：負責處理偏差的員工教育訓練中必須包含供應影響評估。這是一個常被忽視的缺口——傳統偏差訓練著重於 GMP 合規面，而非患者層面的影響分析。

實務建議：在偏差調查表單中，加入「是否可能影響產品放行時程或供應連續性？」的評估欄位，並設定觸發升級的門檻。

實務應用：知識管理 ≠ 文件管理

許多公司誤將「知識管理」等同於「文件管理」。TR68 的定義更廣：

  • 顯性知識：SOP、驗證報告、批次記錄——傳統文件管理的範疇。
  • 隱性知識：老員工對設備特性的經驗、歷年製程變異的模式認知——需要透過跨部門分享、導師制度、製程分析技術 (PAT) 來捕捉。
  • 動態知識：市場需求趨勢、競爭者供應狀況——需要建立即時資訊蒐集系統。

ICH Q10 要求三種知識都需管理，才能形成有效的短缺預防能力。

5.2 The Importance of Interacting with Health Authorities 與衛生主管機關互動的重要性

5.2 The Importance of Interacting with Health Authorities

There is a legal requirement for MAHs to notify relevant health authorities of any potential drug shortages in the European Union and in the United States. Therefore, early interactions should take place between a pharmaceutical company and relevant health authorities when a meaningful disruption in supply is expected. Laws and regulations related to drug shortages are continuing to evolve and are defining or clarifying responsibilities for stakeholders, such as industry, patient, and healthcare professional organizations, with respect to ensuring uninterrupted drug supplies to patients.

Health authorities generally prefer to be involved in several aspects of drug shortage management and response. For example, they might want to:

  • Oversee how the company or MAH is managing the shortage
  • Engage other manufacturers of the same product or alternatives to increase their production
  • Coordinate actions, communications, and responses to the shortage among various stakeholders, including other MAHs and manufacturers, as well as patients, healthcare professionals, and government agencies that purchase medicines
  • Ensure that adequate corrective actions are taken to prevent recurrences

At a higher level, health authorities may wish to contribute to an environment in which supply chains become more robust and shortages can generally be prevented. This can support the primary goal of health authorities, which is generally the protection of public health and the availability of medicinal products.

Health authorities usually take a risk-based approach to shortages. For example, they participate in the management of and response to shortages that may have an impact on patients now or in the near future. Health authorities, however, generally do not have the resources to become closely involved in all aspects of shortages. Risk assessments can help identify shortages that require their attention. Health authorities and industry have a mutual interest in understanding the causes of shortages, even where there is little to no impact on the patient population, to prevent them from recurring.

Knowing which shortages may or may not impact patients requires careful analysis as well as effective multistakeholder and media communications management, and the situation may vary from country to country. Robust risk assessment based on robust knowledge management of the manufacturing and supply processes and active, timely dialog with health authorities and other relevant stakeholders can facilitate this determination.

中文解析：法規義務與監管互動實務

核心概念：通報短缺是法律義務，非選項

TR68 明確指出，在歐盟 (EU) 與美國 (US)，MAH 向衛生主管機關通報藥品短缺是法律要求，而非自願性最佳實務。

  • 美國 FDA：依據 21 CFR 314.81(b)(3)(iii) 及 FDA Safety and Innovation Act (FDASIA) Section 506C，製造商必須在預期產品短缺前 6 個月（或盡早）通知 FDA；仿製藥則依 GDUFA 相關規範執行。
  • 歐盟 EMA/各國主管機關：依據歐盟藥品短缺架構 (EU Regulatory Framework on Shortages)，MAH 必須向相關成員國主管機關通報可能造成供應中斷的情況；部分國家另有特定通報時限。

「及早通報」的重要性不只在合規——越早通報，衛生機關越有時間協調替代方案，對患者的衝擊越小。

比喻：班機延誤通報

想像一架飛機面臨機械故障，可能延誤起飛：

  • 不好的做法：讓旅客在登機門等到出發前 10 分鐘才宣布延誤，旅客措手不及、轉機全泡湯。
  • 好的做法：航空公司一發現潛在問題就通報地勤，提前 2 小時告知旅客，並協助安排轉機選項。

藥品短缺的通報邏輯完全相同：衛生主管機關是「協調中心」，越早掌握資訊，越能有效調度資源（如協調其他製造商增產、發布臨床替代建議）。

衛生主管機關希望介入的面向

TR68 列出衛生機關在短缺管理中的典型角色：

  • 監督 MAH 的應對措施：確認公司的 CAPA 計畫是否充分且可執行。
  • 協調其他製造商增產：如有相同品項的其他製造商，衛生機關可扮演協調者角色，鼓勵或要求增加產能。
  • 多方溝通協調：統一對患者、醫療人員、採購機關的訊息發布，避免資訊混亂。
  • 確保根本原因改善：要求公司採取足夠的 CAPA 防止再發。

從長期來看，衛生機關也希望透過這些互動，推動整體供應鏈韌性的提升。

重點提示：衛生機關的資源有限，需要風險分級

TR68 坦率指出：衛生主管機關的資源有限，無法深度介入所有短缺事件。因此，公司提交的風險評估報告品質至關重要——它是衛生機關決定介入程度的依據。

一份優質的短缺通報應包含：

  • 受影響產品的醫療必要性評估
  • 預估短缺期間與受影響患者數量
  • 現有替代療法/替代供應來源評估
  • 公司已採取或規劃的緩解措施
  • 解決時程的最佳/最差預估

重點提示：各國情況有所差異

TR68 提醒，短缺對患者的實際影響「因國而異」。在某國已有充足替代品的情況，可能在另一國根本無替代選擇。這意味著：

  • 多國上市的 MAH 必須逐一評估每個市場的影響程度。
  • 通報義務的時限與格式在各國法規下可能不同，須建立各市場通報矩陣。
  • 媒體溝通管理在各國文化背景下也需因地制宜。

實務應用：台灣 TFDA 的對應規範

相較於歐美法規，台灣食品藥物管理署 (TFDA) 亦有藥品短缺通報機制。持有藥品許可證之製造商或進口商，若預見供應中斷，應依相關辦法向 TFDA 通報。重要對應點：

  • TFDA 藥品供應短缺通報系統（藥物食品安全週報資訊）
  • 醫療必需藥品短缺認定標準
  • 緊急輸入或暫時通融措施的申請流程

思考練習：何時通報？

以下哪些情境應主動通報衛生主管機關（美國 FDA / 歐盟相關機關）？

  • A：生產線計畫性維護，預計停機 1 週，現有庫存足以涵蓋空窗期。
  • B：主要原料供應商遭 FDA 警告函，需緊急尋找替代供應商，預估缺貨 3 個月，品項為化療用針劑。
  • C：某非處方保健食品因包裝材料短缺，延誤出貨 2 週。
  • D：廠房擴建工程導致某處方慢性病用藥預計缺貨 4 個月，但競爭廠商尚有供應。

建議答案：B 一定須通報（High Risk）；D 建議諮詢後通報（Medium Risk，確認替代供應充足程度）；A 與 C 通常不需通報，但應留存評估記錄。

6.0   Risk Triage Model  ｜  風險分流模型概述

6.0 Risk Triage Model

A preventive approach to managing the risk of drug shortages caused by manufacturing or quality issues includes a risk assessment method called triage. A triage approach allows a quick and easy assessment of the shortage risks of a product and prioritizes actions for proactive drug shortage management (risk control). Not all products require proactive drug shortage management. Only medically necessary and critical products and those with the greatest impact to patients require commensurate levels of effort and planning to ensure that the supply is adequate, and the availability component of the risk calculus is assessed and appropriately managed. The method and tools proposed in Section 6.0 are intended to help determine whether a proactive Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan is required for a product.

Companies should have robust QRM and knowledge management systems to both prevent (proactive) and respond (reactive) to drug shortages (see Section 5.0). Proactive QRM for drug shortage prevention should involve the following:

  • Categorizing each product by criticality based on its indication, marketplace importance, profile, current supply situation, and patients' needs
  • Establishing and periodically reviewing a Drug Shortage Risk Register—a single source of information on hazards and the related risks that can result in drug shortages—by proactively identifying and assessing risks to quality, compliance, and supply that could lead to a shortage. A Drug Shortage Risk Register (see Section 11.0: Appendix 1 for a sample risk register template) should provide the following information, which should be updated and reviewed on a regular basis:
    • Description of potential drug shortage risks for a product, including API, excipients, and sources of risk
    • Countries or regions that could be affected
    • Description of the current controls that are or are not in place, with respect to the prevention of shortages, and the known effectiveness of such controls
    • Risk level for a potential drug shortage, considering the current controls, new risk control actions that are required, owners, and due-date status
    • Key leading and lagging indicators that would trigger early and timely responses to potential shortages, including near-misses on a batch release or the ability to meet product demand
  • Ensuring timely reduction and management of risks by completing risk control actions defined in the risk control plan based on criticality of product availability. Issues to address in the risk control plan could include:
    • Inventory and safety stock planning
    • Demand and supply planning for all relevant markets using a sourcing strategy (e.g., dual sourcing)
    • Capacity planning for manufacturing and distribution (capacity reserve)
    • Availability of alternative therapies
    • Acceleration of health authority actions (e.g., approval of post-approval changes, inspections)
  • Establishing a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan, at least for medically necessary products, which could include early warning systems for potential shortages of certain products (see Section 12.0: Appendix 2 for a sample prevention and response plan template).

Responding to and disseminating information about a potential drug shortage in a timely and effective manner is especially important for medically necessary, life supporting, or life sustaining therapies. Since some shortages need to be addressed quickly, a preestablished and well-structured drug shortage response plan can be very valuable in laying out a clear path of actions to take to minimize the impact of a shortage, end the shortage, and quickly return product supply to its normal state.

解說與重點 Commentary

比喻：急診室分流（Triage）

「Triage（分流）」源自急診醫學。急診室每天湧入大量病患，護理師必須快速判斷——哪些人需要立刻處置，哪些人可以等候？這個優先排序的過程就叫「triage」。

TR68 將同樣的概念搬進藥品供應鏈管理：面對成百上千個品項，企業必須先快速評估「哪些產品一旦短缺，對病患的傷害最大？」，再集中資源在這些高風險品項上。

核心邏輯：並非每個產品都需要同等資源，資源應流向最需要保護的地方。

核心概念：QRM 的雙向角色

TR68 強調 QRM（Quality Risk Management）必須同時具備兩個面向：

  • 主動預防（Proactive）：短缺發生前，建立防線。例如識別高風險產品、建立庫存緩衝、評估供應商穩定性。
  • 被動應變（Reactive）：短缺已發生時，啟動應變計畫，最小化損害。

本節（6.0）聚焦主動預防端，而應變計畫的細節則在 Section 7.0 說明。

  • 現行控制措施及其有效性評估

6.1   Description of the Risk Triage Model  ｜  風險分流模型描述

6.1 Description of the Risk Triage Model

The risk triage method uses a preliminary hazards analysis (PHA) approach, which simplifies the process after information is collected on the potential or actual drug shortage. A four-step PHA triage approach is used to evaluate the relationship of three risk elements—therapeutic use of a product, availability of alternatives, and likelihood of occurrence—to determine the risk of a drug shortage. The steps in the triage process depicted in Figure 6.1-1 are as follows:

  1. Identify impact level (impact on a patient) based on therapeutic use and availability of alternatives.
  2. Determine the likelihood of a drug shortage for the product, taking into account any drug shortage prevention controls that are in place and their likely effectiveness.
  3. Determine the risk priority level based on a combination of impact on the patient and the likelihood of a drug shortage for the product.
  4. Plan and implement the suggested risk controls for the assessed product based on the risk priority level.

The details of the triage model displayed in Figure 6.1.1-1 are described step by step in Sections 6.1.1–6.1.4.

The triage method incorporates key words used to create a simple risk assessment design approach. Rather than using very general, subjective terms to describe priority levels for each of the three elements used in the triage model (e.g., high, medium, and low severity), using discrete information allows the assessment to focus on product information rather than defining "high," "medium," and "low." Table 6.1-1 shows how the key words are used to assign a priority level for each of the three elements in the risk triage model. The current Shortage Prevention Controls will be listed in the drug shortage prevention plan.

解說與重點 Commentary

核心概念：PHA（初步危害分析）

Preliminary Hazards Analysis（PHA）是一種來自系統安全工程的風險評估方法，適用於在詳細資訊不足時進行快速、早期的危害識別與排序。

TR68 採用 PHA 的精神：在短缺發生之前，僅需三項關鍵資訊（治療用途、替代品可及性、短缺可能性）即可快速評估產品風險，不需要耗費大量資源進行深入分析。

核心概念：三個風險要素

風險分流模型的核心是三個維度的交集評估：

  • 治療用途（Therapeutic Use）：這個藥品是為了什麼疾病使用的？後果有多嚴重？
  • 替代品可及性（Availability of Alternatives）：如果這個產品缺貨，有沒有其他可替代的治療選項？
  • 短缺可能性（Likelihood of Occurrence）：考量現行控制措施後，這個產品實際發生短缺的機率有多高？

前兩者合併決定「衝擊等級（Risk Level A/B/C）」，再與第三者結合產生「優先等級（Priority 1/2/3）」。

重點提示：為何使用關鍵字而非「高/中/低」評分？

傳統風險評估常使用主觀的「高/中/低」評分，導致不同評估者之間結果不一致。TR68 改用具體的描述性關鍵字（如「無替代品可用」、「有類似療法」），讓評估聚焦在產品的實際特性，減少主觀判斷的空間，提高評估的一致性與可重複性。

Companies can benefit from categorizing their products by therapeutic use, which makes it possible to apply a risk-based approach for managing drug shortage risks for their portfolio of products. "Medically necessary products," "critical medicines," and "life supporting or life sustaining products" have the highest criticality based on patient need; therefore, their shortages present the greatest risk. (Definitions of terms are provided in Section 3.0.) Ensuring an uninterrupted, reliable supply of these products at all times to all patients who need them is key; therefore, robust controls must be in place to proactively manage the risk of shortages of these drugs.

Products used to treat acute short-term conditions or manage chronic long-term diseases (e.g., Parkinson's disease, diabetes, multiple sclerosis) constitute the next level of product indication-based criticality. Although the criticality of these products is not at the same level as that of life supporting or life sustaining therapies, the unavailability of these products over an extended period could harm patients; therefore, shortages of these products cannot be tolerated for long periods.

The lowest level of product indication-based criticality is assigned to medicinal products used for other minor indications (e.g., self-care therapies for minor ailments such as cough and cold). If a product is registered for more than one indication with different levels of indication-based criticality, the more conservative or higher level of criticality should generally be applied for this assessment. For example, if one of the product indications classifies a product as medically necessary, and the second indication classifies it for treatment of a chronic long-term condition, risk planning should be done assuming the higher level of product indication-based criticality, that is, for its use as a medically necessary product.

In addition to the therapeutic use of the product, the second dimension in ranking the impact of a shortage to patients is access to and availability of alternative therapies. Relevant health authorities may need to be consulted to determine where alternatives may exist for a product and for what markets they are available. Where no alternatives are available, especially for products that are medically necessary, life supporting, life sustaining, or used to treat acute short-term or chronic long-term conditions, the impact to the patient is the highest (Risk Level A – red). Therefore, the margin for tolerance of a drug shortage with these products is minimal, and controls for these risks should ensure that:

  • Focus is on proactive prevention of a shortage
  • Time to remedy and return supply conditions toward normal is as short as possible in the event of a shortage

Where an alternative therapy is available in the form of a similar product or other presentations of the same product, the impact to the patient is lower (Risk Level B – yellow). In the event of a shortage, it is important to ensure that these alternatives are accessible to patients in a timely manner to minimize impact (e.g., by preventing interruptions in treatment).

The lowest level of impact (Risk Level C – green) is assigned to medicinal products used for other indications (e.g., self-care therapies for minor ailments such as cough and cold) for which alternatives are readily available or for products used to treat acute short-term or chronic-long terms conditions that have the same product readily available in other presentations.

6.1.2   Step 2: Assess the Likelihood of Shortage  ｜  步驟二：評估短缺可能性

6.1.2 Step 2: Assess the Likelihood of Shortage

In the second step of the triage, the likelihood of shortage for a product is assessed. Hazards that could increase the likelihood of a shortage should be assessed and current controls should be assessed to identify the hazards. Where good, robust controls exist for a hazard, the likelihood of occurrence would be low; if there are limited to no controls for a hazard, the likelihood of occurrence increases. Historical data may be available for certain hazards, and this historical data should be leveraged to assess the relative likelihood of risks.

For example, product sourcing may be one of the controls assessed to determine the likelihood of a shortage. Single-sourcing of a product or key material supplier increases the likelihood of occurrence of a shortage in the event of an issue at that sole-sourced facility or supplier. Other controls may be related to:

  • Infrastructure maintenance and improvements
  • Technology updates
  • Business continuity planning and capacity assessment
  • Process capability
  • Inventory levels
  • Supplier diversification

This assessment of likelihood of occurrence may not be quantitative, which is considered acceptable because it is more important to assess the controls and their likely effectiveness and to understand the relative likelihood of the occurrence of a shortage for identified hazards. In this risk triage model, a simple three-level (high, medium, low) scale for assessing the likelihood of occurrence has been proposed.

解說與重點 Commentary

比喻：單引擎 vs 雙引擎飛機

想像兩架飛機橫越太平洋：

  • 單引擎飛機：只要那一具引擎故障，飛機立刻面臨危機，沒有後備。→ 單一來源（Single-source）供應，短缺可能性高（High）。
  • 雙引擎飛機 + 備用燃料：一具引擎故障，另一具可繼續飛行，加上有備用燃料，危機耐受度高。→ 多元來源（Multi-source）+ 充足安全庫存，短缺可能性低（Low）。

藥品供應鏈的邏輯相同：現行控制措施越完善，短缺可能性越低。

核心概念：短缺可能性的影響因子

評估短缺可能性時，需考量以下控制措施是否到位及其有效性：

控制面向高可能性（控制不足）低可能性（控制充足）
供應來源單一來源（Single-source）多元來源（Multi-source）

6.1.4 Step 4: Develop and Implement Preventive Risk Control Strategies

After the risk priority levels are assigned to the product in Steps 1–3 of the triage, appropriate risk control strategies (may be predetermined in some instances) should be developed and implemented. Some actions to consider based on the risk priority level are:

Risk Priority Level 1

  • Develop a value stream map (VSM) (starting materials through final drug product packaging and distribution) to identify and reduce inventory and manufacturing risk stress points. Identify appropriate inventory levels for each manufacturing stage based on the current state of controls, probability of drug shortage, and how long a shortage can be tolerated without adverse effects on patients. In addition, the need for a multisourcing strategy should be assessed, taking business considerations into account with senior management involvement.
  • Review supplier management controls for high risk products and high risk suppliers of the components of these high risk products. Implement controls for these products where gaps are identified in supplier management controls.
  • Proactively create and maintain a plan to communicate shortage risks with health authorities, distribution channels, and users. The guide Quality and Manufacturing Driven Supply Disruptions: Industry Communication to Authorities, developed by the European industry collaboration of EFPIA, EGA, AESGP, and PTTA, provides principles and a framework to harmonize reporting and communications between MAHs and health authorities about drug shortages due to quality and/or manufacturing disruptions.
  • Implement an effective Stage 3 continuous process verification (CPV) or ongoing process-verification monitoring program for CPV data that could help identify manufacturing issues before they occur.

Risk Priority Level 2

  • Consider additional proactive inventory management controls and a multisourcing cost–benefit analysis.
  • Consider VSM for specific manufacturing processes to identify inventory stress points and potential manufacturing problems.
  • At a minimum, ensure that CPV plans for these products to undergo additional review as part of the company's drug shortage management activities.

Risk Priority Level 3

  • A Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan is not necessary, but these risks should be reviewed regularly.

High and medium risks, along with the risk control actions and level of residual risk in the Drug Shortage Risk Register, should be tracked, periodically reviewed, and managed.

6.2 Supply Chain and Value Stream Mapping — 供應鏈與價值流圖

VSM as a proactive and reactive drug shortage management tool

6.2 Supply Chain and Value Stream Mapping

The MAH is accountable for ensuring that risks for patient safety, product quality, and product availability are adequately managed in the end-to-end product supply chain. In addition to assessing the risks with various suppliers and external service providers (e.g., CDMOs, CLOs) and addressing them through their supplier management controls, the MAH should also review the QRM processes used, especially by their key suppliers and service providers. This can help ensure that these suppliers and service providers have an adequate framework in place, in accordance with the principles of ICH Q9(R1), to assess and manage their quality and compliance risks.

Process mapping, in general, can play a key role in managing the risk of drug shortages particularly as supply chains continue to become increasingly complex. Mapping of the end-to-end product supply processes is one of the most important tools that can be leveraged for managing the risk of drug shortages due to manufacturing and supply problems. VSM (also known as material and information flow mapping), a particularly powerful tool, graphically displays the flow of the manufacturing and transportation processes for a product and highlights potential manufacturing, inventory, and supply stress points. Although VSM is often used to reduce amounts of inventory and inefficiencies across the supply chain, it is also used to maintain proper levels of in-process inventory to ensure predefined final product inventories.

While VSM is a great tool for mapping products and information flow, it is not the only approach available. Other tools that could be used are DRIVE (Define, Review, Identify, Verify, Execute), ICOR (Inputs, Outputs, Controls, and Resources), Cause-and-Effect Diagrams, and Process Flowcharting.

The following elements constitute an effective mapping tool:

  • Team Makeup: The mapping team should be cross-functional and include individuals who are intimately familiar with manufacturing and supply processes and equipment. The most successful mapping exercises involve laboratory technicians and operators. Mapping requires good facilitation, preferably by an objective, neutral individual.
  • Planning and Preparation: The scope of the mapping (local to a site or region or broader in the supply chain) should be identified in advance. The team should establish timelines and goals for the mapping (in this case, specific to drug shortages). The exercise will likely become part of a larger initiative for developing a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan.
  • Evaluation: The team should perform all steps of the mapping process and capture data and information on how the process works. The team should also have access to existing procedures as well as quality events to capture all stress points.
  • Map: After a map of the current state is drawn, listing all the steps and handoffs in the process, a future-state map can be drawn using the same steps to identify risk reducing actions that will meet the needs of the product.
  • Action List: To develop and maintain an effective Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan, an action list needs to be developed to address the gaps and risks based on the future-state map actions.

核心概念：MAH 的端對端責任

藥品上市許可持有人（MAH）對整個供應鏈負有最終責任，不能因委託給 CDMO 就免除風險管理義務。MAH 不僅要評估自身風險，還必須審核 CDMO、CLO 等關鍵服務商的 QRM（品質風險管理）框架是否符合 ICH Q9(R1) 原則。

比喻：VSM 就像製造現場的「生產看板」

想像您走進一間工廠，牆上掛著一張大型流程圖，顯示每個工序站、物料在各站停留的時間，以及瓶頸位置——這就是 VSM 的精髓。VSM 源自豐田生產系統（TPS）的「物料與資訊流圖」，製藥業將其應用於識別：

  • 哪個環節庫存積壓最多？（隱藏的資金與風險）
  • 哪個工序是真正的瓶頸？（生產速度的天花板）
  • 資訊流（訂單、排程）是否與物料流同步？

VSM 讓供應鏈從「黑盒子」變成「透明玻璃箱」，是藥品短缺的預防性與反應性雙重工具。

重點提示：五大要素缺一不可

有效 VSM 的五大要素（Team → Planning → Evaluation → Map → Action List）必須按序完成。常見錯誤：跳過「評估」步驟，直接畫圖；或畫完圖後沒有建立「行動清單」。沒有行動清單的 VSM 只是好看的裝飾品，無法真正降低短缺風險。

實務應用：其他映射工具的選擇

當 VSM 範圍過大或複雜度超出單一流程時，可考慮替代工具：

  • DRIVE：適合需要系統性問題解決的情境
  • ICOR：強調輸入/輸出/控制/資源的四維分析
  • 魚骨圖：適合根本原因分析，識別短缺的多層原因

在 CDMO 實務中，建議結合 VSM + 魚骨圖，先用 VSM 找出瓶頸，再用魚骨圖深挖根因。

比喻：每個「庫存小時數」代表什麼？

VSM 中的「庫存（hrs）」數字代表物料在兩個工序之間等待的平均時數。這些不是「生產時間」，而是「停滯時間」。以下是關鍵數字的實務意涵：

  • Receiving: 120 hrs = 5 個工作天
    到料後需等 5 天才進入進廠檢驗。這是原物料緩衝庫存，確保即使供應商延遲交貨仍有備料。但過高也代表積壓資金與潛在品質風險（尤其對溫度敏感原料）。
  • Incoming Inspection: 33 hrs ≈ 1.4 天
    進廠檢驗等待時間，反映 QC 實驗室的排程與檢驗週期。可透過快速微生物檢驗（RMM）或預認證原料（pre-approved）縮短。
  • Inventory: 0.5 hrs
    幾乎即時流入下一工序，代表此緩衝幾乎不存在——高效率，但也缺乏彈性。
  • QC & Final Release: 95 hrs = 約 4 天
    這是整個流程最大的瓶頸！成品等待 QC 放行的時間佔總等待時間的 26%。這是最值得投資改善的環節。
  • Warehousing/Ship: 28.8 hrs ≈ 1.2 天
    出貨前的倉儲等待，與全球配送排程有關。

重點提示：QC 瓶頸是藥品短缺的隱形殺手

在這張 VSM 中，QC & Final Release 的 95 hrs 是最大的庫存堆積點。這意味著：

  • 若 QC 發生 OOS（超規格結果）調查，這 95 hrs 可能膨脹至 500+ hrs
  • 每批次進入 QC Hold 都可能延誤出貨，累積成短缺
  • CDMO 應優先投資：更快的無菌檢驗替代方法（如快速 SAL 方法）、更充足的 QC 人力排程、以及提前啟動的穩定性計畫

核心概念：VSM 的雙重用途

製藥 VSM 與傳統精實製造 VSM 最大的差異在於目的：

  • 傳統 VSM：最小化庫存 → 降低成本
  • 製藥 VSM：維持適當庫存 → 確保不斷貨

以 Receiving 的 120 hrs 為例——在傳統精實觀點下這是「浪費」，但在製藥供應韌性觀點下，這是因應 API 供應商（印度/中國）3-4 週前置時間的必要安全緩衝。合理性取決於風險評估，而非純粹效率追求。

思考練習 1：找出您廠的瓶頸

如果您要為自己廠的一個代表產品繪製 VSM，請回答：

  • 您的 QC Final Release 平均等待幾小時？（與圖中 95 hrs 相比如何？）
  • 哪個工序的庫存等待時間最長？原因是什麼？
  • 若這個瓶頸工序突然額外延遲 48 小時，最快何時會影響出貨？

提示：這個練習也是藥品短缺防範計畫（Drug Shortage Prevention Plan）的起點。

6.3

6.3 Managing Drug Shortage Risks Due to Aging Manufacturing Facilities and Technologies

Causes, continual improvement, and lifecycle management of analytical technologies

6.3 Overview & 6.3.1 Causes and Effects of Aging

Manufacturing and quality risks introduced by aging or legacy facilities and technologies (e.g., out-of-date manufacturing facilities, equipment, analytical technologies) that are not fully up to current standards or expectations can contribute to an increased likelihood of issues that could potentially result in drug shortages. These risks should therefore be proactively identified and managed during the lifecycle of the manufacturing facility, process, equipment, or analytical methods. In order to accomplish the objectives of ICH Q10, continual improvement is an important element of product realization and of maintaining a state of control.

"Product realization is achieved by designing, planning, implementing, maintaining, and continuously improving a system that allows the consistent delivery of products with appropriate quality attributes. Continual improvement is facilitated through the implementation of quality improvements appropriate to the current level of process and product knowledge."
— EU GMP Guidelines on Pharmaceutical Quality Systems (ICH Q10 context)

6.3.1 Causes and Effects of Aging Facilities, Processes, and Analytical Technologies

Some of the main reasons why facilities, processes, or analytical technologies become obsolete are:

  • Lack of long-term planning and poor understanding of the importance of reinvestment and recapitalization of facilities and processes
  • Concern about the unanticipated negative impact of improvements due to a lack of robust process knowledge or of knowledge and experience with the new technology
  • Short-term focus on maintaining or reducing costs, either cost of goods sold or capital expenses
  • Negative financial return on investment due to low drug prices (e.g., generics)
  • Challenges, long lead times, and a lack of harmonized processes for global regulatory approvals across multiple health authorities
  • Absence of positive mindset, attitude, and culture for change and continuous improvement

These factors can produce the decline that occurs over time, for example:

  • As a cost-saving effort, a company may decide to move to a limited maintenance schedule on equipment or reduce the availability of critical spare parts and stock of intermediates. The lowered or partial maintenance is believed to save costs; however, breakdowns due to the lack of maintenance or a negative trend in the manufacturing environment may stop production or lead to product rejections in the future, at a far higher cost.
  • A conscious decision to drastically reduce or eliminate facility, process, and/or equipment optimization and improvement activities, over the long run, can result in antiquated and noncompliant facilities and processes with high levels of variability and risk. Additionally, aging facilities may limit the ability to increase capacity to meet the product demand.
  • Maintaining a false assumption that equipment will not become obsolete in the future and will continue to be supported by the equipment manufacturer is imprudent. Equipment is designed with an intended life span. When the manufacturer fails to plan for equipment obsolescence, it often results in a quick upgrade done at a high cost and with some loss of production time.
  • In some cases, a process or analytical technique was designed for one product or output, and a change in a product or output might be incompatible with the old process or product.

Quality and GMP compliance gaps associated with such aging facilities, processes, and technologies should be addressed even though the existing equipment or methods may be functional. Well-trained personnel with a process ownership mentality may make such facilities run, but when such personnel retire or move to other tasks, the facility, processes, or analytical methods see their first excursions.

The continual maintenance and improvement of facilities, processes, and analytical methods is essential to maintain specified product quality and to assure supplies of the product.

中文解析：老化設施的根因與後果

核心概念：「老化」的定義不只是年齡

設施、製程、分析技術的「老化」不等於使用年限長。TR68 定義的老化是指：不再符合現行標準或監管期望的狀態。一個建造於 2005 年、但從未更新的設施，可能在 GMP 符合性、製程能力、或設備支援上已「老化」。老化的真正觸發因素往往是管理決策，而非時間本身。

比喻：延遲維護陷阱（Deferred Maintenance Trap）

想像一部汽車的車主決定「省錢」，跳過定期保養。最初幾個月看起來沒問題，但發動機磨損在悄悄累積。等到引擎徹底故障，修理費用是正常保養費的十倍，而且車子還停駛了兩週。

製藥設施完全相同：

  • 短期「節省」：減少維護預算、延後設備升級
  • 長期「代價」：設備故障停產、FDA Warning Letter、產品召回、甚至工廠關閉

FDA 483 和 Warning Letter 中，大量案例源自「Inadequate maintenance program」或「Equipment not maintained in a state of control」——這正是延遲維護陷阱的法規後果。

重點提示：「關鍵人員依賴」是隱性老化風險

TR68 特別指出一個常被忽略的老化風險：設施/製程的運作高度依賴少數資深員工的個人知識。這些人知道設備的「脾氣」、非正式的操作技巧，但這些知識從未被系統化記錄。當他們退休或離職，繼任者依照 SOP 操作卻頻繁出現偏差——這不是新問題，而是老化系統的第一次「誠實呈現」。

對策：定期進行「知識風險評估」，識別高度個人依賴的製程，強制進行知識轉移與 SOP 更新。

實務應用：老化設施風險評分矩陣

建議建立設施設備老化風險評估，涵蓋：

  • 設備製造商支援狀態（是否已停產、備件可取得性）
  • 與現行 GMP 法規的符合性差距
  • 過去 3 年的維護事件頻率趨勢
  • 設計是否容易出現人為錯誤（human error vulnerability）
  • 製程能力指數（Cpk）趨勢——是否在下降？

結果應納入廠區風險登記冊（Site Risk Register），並與資本支出計畫（CapEx Plan）直接掛鉤。

6.3.2

6.3.2 Continual Improvement of Aging Facilities and Processes

Technology innovation as a driver for quality and supply reliability

6.3.2 Continual Improvement of Aging Facilities and Processes

Processing technology and facility designs are forever evolving. The motivation of suppliers to develop and distribute new technologies is mainly driven by industry seeking new solutions for current processing problems. However, implementation of these solutions becomes challenging in spite of the value they can bring.

Given this, implementation of new technology has resulted not just in financial viability, but an overall improvement in quality and process efficiency. For example, single-use process technology has many advantages over traditional stainless steel equipment, such as faster equipment turnaround, which enhances production capacities. Also, single-use components or unit operations can be connected via aseptic connector or tube welders, which creates a closed system that enhances the safety of both the product and the operator. The technological improvements of single-use technologies have been widely embraced, not only for new, but existing processes, as the advantages of using this technology outweigh any challenges change commonly bring.

Facility and process improvements due to technology innovation are not always as dramatic as single-use process technology or new modular cleanroom designs; they can be gradual improvements of existing equipment to the next generation. Subtle, but continual improvements demonstrate the right quality attitude and ownership. Such continual improvement activities inevitably lower the risk of process or facility failure, leading to operations that run more reliably and efficiently and resulting in cost reduction.

[More information about single-use systems can be found in PDA Technical Report No. 66: Application of Single-Use Systems in Pharmaceutical Manufacturing.]

中文解析：持續改善的商業案例

核心概念：持續改善 = ICH Q10 的核心要求

ICH Q10「製藥品質系統」將持續改善（Continual Improvement）列為製藥品質系統的四大核心要素之一（與知識管理、PQS、CAPA 並列）。這不是「加分項目」，而是監管期望的基本要求。TR68 進一步強調：持續改善是預防設施老化導致藥品短缺的主動防線。

比喻：單次使用技術的「換湯不換藥」革命

傳統不鏽鋼設備就像一個需要每次清洗消毒的大型廚房器具——費時費力，且每次清洗都有交叉污染風險。

單次使用技術（SUS）就像用拋棄式容器：用完即丟，下次立即換新，省去清潔驗證、縮短周轉時間，還降低了批次間交叉污染風險。這不只是成本考量，更是供應可靠性的提升——更快的批次周轉 = 更多的年度批次產量。

重點提示：漸進式改善 vs. 破壞性創新

TR68 特別強調「漸進式持續改善」（gradual improvements）與「破壞性技術創新」同樣重要：

  • 更新老舊 HMI 介面到更直觀的觸控系統
  • 將手動記錄替換為電子批次記錄（eBR）
  • 導入 PAT（製程分析技術）的即時監測功能

這些「小步驟」積累的文化，才是真正避免設施老化的長期保障。表現出正確的品質態度與所有權意識，正是監管機構在 GMP 檢查中最想看到的。

實務應用：建立技術更新路線圖

建議每個 CDMO 廠區建立 3-5 年「技術更新路線圖（Technology Roadmap）」：

  • 盤點所有關鍵設備的製造商支援結束日期（End-of-Life）
  • 評估下一代設備的技術優勢與 PAC 申請需求
  • 將 PAC 申請時程（2-5 年）納入更新計畫的前置作業
  • 與客戶 MAH 協調設備變更的法規申報策略

此路線圖應每年更新，並納入廠區管理審查（Management Review）的討論議程。

6.3.3 Lifecycle Management and Continual Improvement of Analytical Technologies

PAC timelines, periodic assessments, and control system updates

6.3.3 Lifecycle Management and Continual Improvement of Analytical Technologies

During the commercial lifecycle of a product, analytical technologies continue to improve. However, commercial product analytical control systems—in-process, release, and stability test methods, specifications and acceptance criteria, and reference-standard controls—tend to improve minimally, if at all. This slow pace of change can be attributed to such factors as high cost, resource requirements, and the complexity of the global regulatory environment for making PACs. Revisions to control systems can take two to five years, depending on their extent and the number of countries that need to approve the PAC.

A proactive approach to control system lifecycle management is important to ensure alignment with contemporary product knowledge and industry standards. Lifecycle management of analytical control systems should be reviewed periodically (e.g., every five years), not only in response to control system issues identified (e.g., through investigations), during routine commercial use.

Periodic assessments of an analytical control system should determine whether the system continues to control drug substance and product quality at the product's release and at the end of its shelf life. Such determinations should be based on comprehensive reviews of current information about the molecule, including:

  • Adequate monitoring of relevant product quality attributes
  • Current manufacturing process capability
  • Alignment of acceptance criteria with process capability
  • Compliance with evolving regulatory requirements and expectations
  • The ability to fulfill its intended purpose
  • Current analytical technologies

Periodic assessments of the control system should include evaluations of gaps or improvement opportunities, for example, when a new critical quality attribute is identified because of new information on a molecule (such as antibody-dependent cell-mediated cytotoxicity), when superior technology is available, or when new regulatory requirements are implemented.

Some examples of when an update to the control system may be needed:

  • The current method has a high failure rate, low throughput rate, or analyst ergonomic or safety limitations
  • The method is no longer suitable for the control system
  • The current method validation is no longer applicable
  • Superior technology is available
  • New or revised specifications or acceptance criteria need to be implemented

Acceptance criteria for current control system test methods should be evaluated against manufacturing and process capability (via analysis of lot-release data) and stability data. Release acceptance criteria can be evaluated by comparing 95 or 99 tolerance intervals of historical batches to current acceptance criteria.

Revisions to current analytical control systems require submission of an application to the relevant health authorities. Therefore, they should be coordinated as much as possible with manufacturing process improvements, transfers, and other activities requiring regulatory approval. Early discussions with relevant health authorities regarding reporting level and documentation requirements for anticipated PACs will allow both the health authorities and the company to plan for the changes in a manner that will help reduce the risk of drug shortages.

中文解析：分析技術生命週期管理

核心概念：「技術時差」問題

TR68 揭示了一個結構性矛盾：分析技術本身在快速進步（每 3-5 年就有新一代技術），但藥品的分析控制系統（放行方法、穩定性方法、規格標準）幾乎一成不變。原因在於每次更新都需要提交 PAC，而 PAC 核准需要 2-5 年。

這個「技術時差」（Technology Lag）導致：市面上已有更準確、更快速的分析方法，但廠房仍在使用二十年前的舊方法——不是不想改，是改不起或改不完。

比喻：PAC 時程就像翻修老宅的「施工許可證」

想翻修一棟老房子（更新分析方法）：您有新設計圖（新技術），但要動工前必須向每個縣市申請施工許可（各國 PAC）。如果您的房子在 5 個國家都有，光跑完所有許可就要 3-5 年——而在等待過程中，房子的問題繼續存在。

這就是為何 TR68 強調要「主動提前規劃」分析控制系統的更新：在當前方法還沒出問題前，就要啟動 PAC 流程。等到出了 OOS 或失效率升高再申請，已經是亡羊補牢，藥品短缺難以避免。

重點提示：每五年定期評估是監管期望

TR68 明確建議分析控制系統應「每五年定期審查」（every five years）。這不只是最佳實踐，而是符合 ICH Q10 持續改善精神的監管期望。CDMO 應建立正式的「分析方法生命週期審查程序（Analytical Method Lifecycle Review SOP）」，包含：

  • 定期（至少每 5 年）的控制系統整體評估觸發點
  • 觸發即時審查的事件清單（高失敗率、新 CQA 識別、監管要求更新）
  • 評估結果與 PAC 計畫的連結機制
  • 製程能力指數（Cpk）與規格標準的一致性驗證

實務應用：容忍區間與規格評估

TR68 提供了一個具體的統計工具：使用歷史批次的 95% 或 99% 容忍區間與現有規格標準比較。

  • 若容忍區間與規格上/下限的距離太小（Cpk 偏低）→ 規格可能需要收緊或製程需要改善
  • 若實際製程能力遠優於規格（Cpk >> 1.33）→ 可能有機會放寬放行規格以降低 OOS 風險

參考：PDA TR59 (Revised 2012) — Utilization of Statistical Methods for Production Monitoring，提供詳細的 Cpk 計算與應用指引。

思考練習 2：CDMO 的分析方法更新策略

假設您的 CDMO 正在準備承接一個新的生物製劑代工案，客戶的分析方法是 2010 年開發的 HPLC 方法。請思考：

  • 這個方法是否符合現行 ICH Q2(R2) 的驗證要求？
  • 如果需要在方法轉移後進行更新，PAC 申請時程如何與商業供貨時程整合？
  • 客戶 MAH 是否已啟動分析控制系統的定期審查？CDMO 應如何在合約中約定這個責任？
  • 如果發現舊方法的失效率偏高（> 2%），建議的行動優先順序是什麼？

提示：CDMO 的品質協議應明確約定分析方法生命週期管理的責任歸屬。

6.4 Reducing Drug Shortages Through Expedited Post-Approval Changes

ICH Q12, global Change Protocols (gCP), and regulatory flexibility

6.4 Reducing Drug Shortages Through Expedited Post-Approval Changes

When companies face a potential drug shortage, they can mitigate the risk by expediting a change that may have already been submitted to a national health authority for approval. Current regulatory PAC processes vary among countries in the levels and terminology of change categories, requirements for documentation of changes, the need for preapproval facility inspections, and timelines for approval. For these reasons, companies usually need to run multiple versions of a manufacturing process in parallel for the same product, depending on when each national health authority is likely to approve a change.

In such cases, the company should communicate with the health authorities responsible for trade where a potential drug shortage might occur and request an expedited review of pending PACs or offer a previous version of the product if available in the inventory. Health authorities assess PACs using science and risk-based approaches. However, it would be beneficial if the regulatory approval processes for PACs could be harmonized.

An example of an effort towards harmonization is The International Coalition of Medicines Regulatory Authorities' (ICMRA) Pharmaceutical Quality Knowledge Management System Initiative (PQKMS). According to ICH Q10, Annex 1, companies that demonstrate an effective PQS and process and product understanding, including the use of QRM principles, may be eligible for enhanced regulatory approaches. This is sometimes referred to as "regulatory flexibility," where there may be an opportunity to "optimize science and risk-based post-approval change processes to maximize benefits from innovation and continual improvement."

Additionally, ICH Q12: Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management provides a framework via PQS to facilitate the management of PACs in a more predictable and efficient manner throughout the product lifecycle. Such opportunities may include managing more PACs in the PQS only, rather than as a prior-approval PAC. The intent of the guideline is to promote continual improvement and innovation, while strengthening quality assurance and reliable product supply.

中文解析：PAC 加速審查與法規彈性

核心概念：ICH Q12「法規彈性」

法規彈性（Regulatory Flexibility）是 ICH Q10 提出的一個激勵機制：企業若能證明其 PQS（製藥品質系統）完善、具備充分的製程與產品理解、並有效運用 QRM 原則，監管機構可允許該公司以「事後通報」代替「事前核准」管理部分 PAC——大幅縮短變更實施週期。

簡而言之：品質文化越好的公司，法規監管越寬鬆。這是監管機構給予「信任」企業的具體回報。

核心概念：ICH Q12 的核心工具

ICH Q12 引入了兩個關鍵概念：

  • PLCM（Product Lifecycle Management Document）：在申請時預先界定哪些未來變更可以更低的報告層級管理，讓監管機構與企業都能預期。
  • EFPIA/PAC 分類：將 PAC 依科學風險分層，高風險仍需事前核准，低風險可事後通報或僅在 PQS 內管理。

對 CDMO 而言，理解 ICH Q12 框架能幫助在合約談判時更準確地評估法規時程風險。

比喻：法規彈性就像「優良駕駛折扣」

保險公司給長期無肇事紀錄的駕駛更低的保費——因為信任他們的安全記錄。ICH Q12 的法規彈性邏輯相同：有強健 PQS 的企業，監管機構「信任」其內部品質管理能力，因此給予更寬鬆的報告類別。

相反地，歷史上有 Warning Letter、重大偏差，或 PQS 薄弱的企業，即使是微小的製程變更，監管機構也可能要求先審核才能實施。

重點提示：多國並行 PAC 的現實挑戰

TR68 點出一個實務困境：各國 PAC 分類名稱不同（美國：Major/Moderate/Minor；歐盟：Type IA/IB/II；日本：Major/Minor），核准時程各異，導致同一個製程變更在不同國家的核准狀態不同步。

結果是：公司被迫同時維持多個版本的製造流程——一個版本給已核准的國家，另一個給尚未核准的國家。這不只是邏輯複雜，更是庫存管理的噩夢，直接增加藥品短缺的風險。

Global Change Protocols (gCP) and Streamlined Changes

The industry should work with health authorities to develop predefined global change protocols (gCP) that can be available and used as a standard (non-product-specific) template for the categorization, predefined data requirements, and implementation of the different types of changes. Common gCPs that are developed in partnership with health authorities for the different types of changes can help standardize activities and requirements across the industry for a particular change type.

Each company could then use the gCP for the relevant type of change as a starting point in developing the submission for each of the changes in the PAC Management Protocol for their product. The company should update the predefined gCP with the specific aspects of the change for their particular product.

Streamlined Changes — Categories Eligible for Expedited Review

Companies and health authorities could work together to enable, where appropriate, streamlined review and approval of the following changes, provided that agreed-upon acceptance criteria from health authorities are met and that these changes do not introduce increased risks to patient safety or product quality or efficacy:

Enhanced Redundancy:

  • Additional manufacturing site or line already approved in at least one significant market or country that can serve as a reference
  • Additional raw material or API suppliers
  • Additional suppliers of container closure or other processing components
  • Additional dosage forms already approved in at least one reference country

Improved Process and Product Controls:

  • New or improved equipment and technology
  • Manufacturing changes that lead to improved process capability
  • New analytical technologies that enhance product controls, including adventitious agent controls

Other Types of Changes (provided that validated and preset criteria are met):

  • Change in reference standards
  • Use of new working cell banks
  • Addition of a product to a multiproduct facility
  • Scale-up of a manufacturing process
  • Extension of shelf life with supporting documentation
  • Change in utilities (e.g., HVAC, clean steam) or their operation with no impact on product quality
  • Change in equipment (design and/or operating principle) with no impact on product quality
  • Change in steam-in-place or clean-in-place process
  • Change in environmental monitoring program (e.g., sample location and frequency)
  • Change in cleaning methods

In all cases, the company concerned should be able to demonstrate that it has an effective PQS in place. The risk assessment for the change(s) should be included in the submission for the change to demonstrate that appropriate risk control measures are in place and provide assurance that patient safety, product quality, and product efficacy are not impacted.

中文解析：gCP 與加速審查機制

核心概念：什麼是 gCP（全球變更協議）？

全球變更協議（global Change Protocol, gCP）是由產業界與各國衛生主管機關共同預先制定的「標準變更模板」。對於特定類型的常見變更（例如：新增製造場址、更換設備），gCP 預先定義了：

  • 所需的驗證活動（validation activities）
  • 數據要求（data requirements）
  • 可接受標準（acceptance criteria）
  • 報告類別建議（reporting category）

企業只需以 gCP 為起點，加入產品特有的具體資訊，即可完成 PAC 申請——大幅縮短準備時間，提高審查效率。

CDMO 客戶注意：新增製造場址 PAC 直接受益

TR68 第 6.4 節特別列出「Additional manufacturing site or line already approved in at least one significant market」作為可加速審查的首要變更類型。

對 CDMO 客戶（MAH）而言，這意味著：

  • 若您的藥品已在 ICH 核心市場（美國、歐盟、日本）有核准的製造場址（例如原廠），且 CDMO 新廠址的 GMP 水平相當，可申請加速 PAC 審查
  • gCP 框架可讓新廠址核准時間從 3-5 年縮短至 1-2 年（在理想情境下）
  • CDMO 應協助 MAH 準備標準化的場址資格文件（Site Master File），使其符合 gCP 的預設數據要求

比喻：gCP 就像「通用插座轉接頭」

以往每個國家的 PAC 要求就像各國不同規格的插座——同樣的設備，到了不同國家就需要不同的轉接頭（不同的申請文件格式與數據要求）。

gCP 就像設計出一個「通用轉接頭」——預先包含各國主要要求的最大公約數，企業只需在通用框架上加入少量本地化內容，就能適用於多個市場的同步申請（Simultaneous Submission）。這是邁向真正法規全球化的一步。

實務應用：PQS 必須涵蓋 PAC 管理

TR68 明確要求：企業的 PQS 必須擴展以涵蓋 PAC 的規劃與評估，特別包括：

  • 如何對變更進行風險評估
  • 如何展示風險控制措施
  • PAC 管理協議（PAC Management Protocol）的建立

對 CDMO 而言，這意味著您的變更控制程序（Change Control SOP）必須有明確的 PAC 分類決策樹，並與客戶 MAH 的全球申報策略對接。PIC/S 文件《如何評估製藥品質系統在風險型變更管理中的有效性》提供了具體的評估框架，建議作為程序設計的參考。

核心概念：監管機構鼓勵創新的激勵機制

TR68 提出一個前瞻性建議：衛生主管機關應對採用創新技術（以降低風險並可量測地預防短缺）的企業提供法規激勵（regulatory incentives）。被點名的創新技術包括：

  • 隔離器技術（Isolator Technology）取代傳統開放製程
  • 封閉式製造系統（Closed Manufacturing Systems）
  • 快速微生物方法（Rapid Microbiology Methods）
  • 縮短製造週期時間的技術
  • 提升製程能力（Process Capability）的技術

這個激勵框架尚在倡議階段，但代表了監管科學的重要發展方向——值得 CDMO 在技術投資決策中納入考量。

7.0

Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan

藥品短缺預防與應對計劃 — 概述

7.0 Overview — Plan Purpose and Scope

The risk assessment triage results in an assignment of a drug shortage risk priority level to each product based on the product's indication, availability of alternative therapies, and probability of a shortage. Section 7.0 describes how a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan should be developed based on the outcomes of the risk triage. While agencies are working on new guidances, such a plan might also be known as a Shortage Prevention Plan or Mitigation Plan in the future.

A Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan can provide a holistic framework at a product level to address both the proactive and reactive management of a drug shortage. This plan should be proactively developed for medically necessary, life supporting, or life sustaining products and shared with health authorities during inspections. Medium risk products used for short-term acute conditions or long-term chronic conditions can also benefit from a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan, especially if the risk triage has identified potential drug shortage risks at Risk Priority Level 1.

A Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan can be an effective mechanism for managing a product's continuously changing drug shortage risk profile. Drug shortages may present potential opportunities for product diversion and counterfeiting. Therefore, a company's surveillance program can leverage the Drug Shortage Risk Register as useful input into its process for managing product diversion and counterfeiting risks that may arise due to shortages.

中文解析：為什麼需要這份計劃？

核心定義：Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan

這是一份以產品為單位的整合性框架文件，同時涵蓋：

  • 主動預防（Proactive）：提前識別風險、建立韌性
  • 被動應對（Reactive）：發生短缺時的應急流程

未來可能以「Shortage Prevention Plan」或「Mitigation Plan」命名，實質內容相同。

重點提示：哪些產品「必須」有此計劃？

  • 必須（Should proactively develop）：醫療必需品、維生用藥、生命支持用藥
  • 建議（Can also benefit）：中等風險、急性或慢性病用藥 — 尤其當 Risk Triage 顯示 Risk Priority Level 1

在衛生主管機關稽查時，此計劃應主動揭露，展示廠商的風險管理成熟度。

比喻：藥品短缺計劃如同「颱風應變計劃」

平時（非颱風季）就要備妥疏散路線、物資清單、聯絡電話；颱風來臨時才能有條不紊應對。Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan 就是製藥廠的「供應鏈颱風應變計劃」——不是危機發生後才臨時抱佛腳，而是提前建立、定期演練、隨時更新。

CDMO 的特殊角色

CDMO（合約研發製造機構）在此計劃中是關鍵供應鏈節點。當 MAH（上市許可持有人）制定本計劃時，CDMO 應主動提供以下資訊：

  • 自身產能狀況與瓶頸（Capacity bottlenecks）
  • 原料及關鍵物料的供應風險
  • 備用製程或供應商切換方案

CDMO 若被動等待指令，可能造成計劃中存在「盲點」，導致短缺風險被低估。

The 5-Component Structure of a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan

預防與應對計劃的五大組成要素

Plan Components — English Source Text (p34–35)

A Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan consists of the following information:

  1. Identification of drug shortage hazards: An end-to-end risk assessment of the product's value chain until the first economic customer — its manufacturing operations, storage, and transportation — should identify potential sources of drug shortages. The Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan should address the management of the drug shortage risks listed in the Drug Shortage Risk Register. Typical visibility for a MAH ends after shipment to wholesale distributors.
  2. Assessment of the impact of a potential drug shortage: Based on the product indications, an assessment should be made and documented of which patient population would be affected by a drug shortage and what this effect would be. Application of the risk triage model would identify the risk priority levels described in Section 6.2. In addition, clinical risk assessments would be helpful in understanding the clinical impact of a drug shortage.
  3. Risk control plan (short-term and long-term): This section should describe the controls identified from the end-to-end risk assessments of the product, how the risk control strategy will proactively control drug shortage risks, what measures can be taken to ensure product supply resilience, and a plan to respond to a shortage and ensure a prompt return to normal operations in the event of a shortage.
  4. Communication plan: The Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan should include a communication plan for drug shortages. The AESGP, EFPIA, EGA, and PPTA collaborative document provides guidance on communications related to product shortages, including possible triggers for communication strategies.
  5. Risk review and monitoring: The Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan should be a living document that is reviewed and updated whenever a product's risk profile changes. Potential causes include approval of a new product or dosage form, a new indication, a new market, shutdown of a facility, increase in product usage, or new quality or manufacturing issues.

中文解析：五大組成要素詳解

要素 1：識別危害 — Drug Shortage Risk Register

這是整個計劃的起點。透過端對端（E2E）風險評估，涵蓋價值鏈中的製造、儲存、運輸等環節，系統性列出所有潛在短缺風險來源，形成「藥品短缺風險登記冊（Risk Register）」。

MAH 的可視範圍通常在貨物出售給批發商後即終止，這代表零售藥局到病患的最後一哩路資訊，往往需要倚賴其他通路夥伴提供。

要素 2：評估病患衝擊 — Patient Impact Assessment

根據藥品適應症，評估「哪些病患族群」會受到短缺影響，以及「影響程度為何」。此評估須書面化，並與 Section 6.2 的風險優先等級（Risk Priority Level）掛鉤。

臨床風險評估（Clinical Risk Assessment）在此尤為重要，尤其是針對危重症病患或無替代療法的情境。

要素 3：風險控制計劃 — Short-term & Long-term

分為短期應急（例如：緊急庫存調配、優先排程）與長期韌性建設（例如：雙供應商策略、備用產線認證）兩個層次。

此要素需回答：「萬一短缺，如何最快恢復正常供應（Return to Normal Operations）？」

要素 4：溝通計劃 — Communication Plan

明確定義「What（通報什麼）、Who（通報給誰）、When（何時通報）、How（如何通報）」，以及觸發溝通的條件（Triggers）。參考組織：AESGP、EFPIA、EGA、PPTA 聯合文件提供了溝通框架指引。

要素 5：風險審查與監控 — Living Document

此計劃是「活文件」，須在產品風險輪廓（Risk Profile）發生變化時隨時更新。觸發審查的事件包括：新適應症核准、新市場進入、設施關閉、GMP 認證撤銷、庫存低於安全水位、使用量異常等。

重點提示：CDMO 如何融入這五個要素？

  • 要素 1：CDMO 須主動揭露自身的製程瓶頸、設備單點失效（SPOF）風險
  • 要素 3：CDMO 與 MAH 共同協商備用產能或替代製程的認證計劃
  • 要素 5：CDMO 發生任何影響供應的事件（如設備故障、原料缺貨）時，須立即觸發計劃更新

8.0

Stakeholder Management and Communications related to Drug Shortages

利害關係人管理與藥品短缺溝通 — 法規義務與協作對話

8.0 Legal Obligations and Collaborative Dialog

Multiple stakeholders are affected by, and need to be involved in, addressing a drug shortage — manufacturers, health authorities, distributors, pharmacies, healthcare providers and, sometimes, patients, depending on the cause(s) and level of the shortage.

The legal obligations for MAHs to notify the relevant health authorities of a potential drug shortage are described in:

  • Article 13 of Commission Directive 2003/94/EC — for the European Union
  • Section 506C of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act — for the United States

In addition, Part 1, Section 5.71 of the EU GMP Guidelines has been updated to provide guidance on notification of restrictions in supply.

A collaborative dialog between the company and the relevant health authorities is key to facilitating the control and/or prevention of a shortage and, ultimately, ensuring a continued supply of medicinal products for patients.

Companies need to consider many things when reacting to a current or impending shortage of one of their medicines in a given country or region. Developing a harmonized risk communication mechanism to alert health authorities of a potential or existing shortage is important to creating a shared understanding of the shortage.

The guidance paper Quality and Manufacturing Driven Supply Disruptions: Industry Communication Principles to Authorities provides communication principles and a framework to harmonize reporting and communications between MAHs and health authorities regarding drug shortages arising from quality and/or manufacturing disruptions. The paper proposes a harmonized trigger point, time point, and recipient(s) for communicating potential drug shortage events for nationally and centrally approved products in Europe.

Relevant health authorities, those where the product is marketed and where the shortage is expected to occur, must be notified when a meaningful disruption could occur in the supply of medicines due to manufacturing and quality issues, that is, when normal volumes of medicines cannot be delivered for further distribution.

A safety or clinical risk assessment should inform the risk communication strategy so that appropriate risk mitigation actions can be taken at the healthcare provider and/or patient level. For defective products (e.g., those with a potential lack of sterility assurance), a comparison might be needed of the clinical benefits and risks for patients of continued product use, especially when no alternative product or treatment is available.

傳統觀念認為「通報衛生主管機關」是單向的合規動作。TR68 強調的是「協作對話（Collaborative Dialog）」，更像工廠 ERT 啟動後，消防隊、廠務、安全人員共同指揮的場景——每個角色有各自專業，但共同目標是將危害最小化。

與衛生主管機關建立這種「夥伴關係」，遠比事後被動應對來得更有效率。

重點提示：何時必須通報？

觸發通報的條件是：「因製造或品質問題，可能發生有意義的供應中斷（meaningful disruption），即無法正常配送正常量」時。

風險矩陣（Table 6.1.1-1）按照療效分類與替代療法可得性，分層決定通報的緊迫程度（Risk Level A / B / C）。

缺陷產品的特殊情境：臨床利益 vs. 短缺風險

當一個產品存在潛在品質缺陷（例如：無菌保證不足），但完全沒有替代療法時，TR68 要求進行「臨床利益與風險比較」（Clinical Benefit vs. Risk Comparison）：

  • 繼續使用：可能有品質風險，但病患至少有藥可用
  • 停止供應：確保無品質風險，但病患可能面臨更嚴重的治療中斷

此決定需由公司與衛生主管機關共同協商，而非單方面決定。

8.1

Key Risk-Indicating Information to Compile for Health Authorities

向衛生主管機關提交的關鍵風險指示資訊

8.1 Information Framework

When anticipating a shortage may occur, companies should compile key pieces of information for health authorities when their involvement is needed. Knowing the reasons for the stock shortage or depletion not only facilitates the implementation of corrective and preventive actions (CAPA), but it can also help develop practical marketplace solutions for the shortage.

Based on product ownership, it is important to openly clarify communication accountabilities between the health authority and the (1) manufacturer or first distributor; (2) wholesaler, agent, trader, distributor, repackager, or relabeler; and (3) suppliers of the product to the patient (e.g., retail pharmacies, hospital pharmacies, physicians).

Patient-Related Information

  • Types of patients (e.g., by disease, demographics, age, genetics, origin) using the medicine. A good level of clinical understanding is important to assess whether any patient groups may be particularly affected (e.g., neonates, patients with cancer or diabetes).
  • Scale of the problem:
    • Likely number of patients treated with the product each month and the percentage of patients that may be affected
    • Current market share, usage profile, and monthly volume of prescriptions
    • Monthly sales volume of the product and the proportion affected
    • Possible local, regional, or global impact on product supplies

Distribution Information in the Country or Region

  • Name and address of the primary distributor of the product, if any
  • If other distributors (i.e., secondary wholesalers) supply the product
  • If the product is supplied only to hospitals or to both hospital and retail pharmacies
  • If the product is supplied through other channels, such as clinics and nursing homes
  • Opportunities for parallel import, repackaging, and relabeling, depending on regulations

Current Stock in Each Country or Region

  • Whether the product is currently out of stock in the country or region, or if another version of the product or process is available during the stock-out period
  • Whether the product stock is expected to be depleted in the near future (e.g., within the next few weeks or months) because of the supply issue
  • Whether the product is in stock but available quantities are insufficient to meet patient needs
  • Whether stockpiling is requested by the government, legislation, or guidance or as result of the Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan

通報責任分層（Communication Accountability）

TR68 明確要求釐清通報責任鏈：

  1. 製造商/首個配銷商（即 CDMO 或 MAH 自有工廠）
  2. 批發商、代理商、再包裝商
  3. 末端供應者（零售藥局、醫院藥局、醫師）

CDMO 屬於第 1 層，須確保第 1 層的資訊完整、正確，作為 MAH 向衛生主管機關通報的基礎。

CDMO 應維護的三類資訊清單

A. 病患相關資訊（Patient-Related）

  • 本產品的主要適應症與目標病患族群說明
  • 月處方量（Monthly Prescription Volume）估計
  • 特殊脆弱族群識別（如新生兒、癌症病患、糖尿病病患）

B. 配銷資訊（Distribution Information）

  • 主要批發商名稱與地址
  • 次要批發商清單（若有）
  • 供應通路說明（醫院 vs. 零售 vs. 診所）
  • 平行進口可能性評估（各市場法規不同）

C. 庫存資訊（Current Stock）

  • 目前庫存量（各市場/倉庫）
  • 預計庫存耗盡日期
  • 現有庫存是否足以滿足病患需求
  • 是否有政府要求的戰備儲量

公式：庫存天數計算（Days of Supply）

庫存天數（Days of Supply）
= 現有庫存量（Current Inventory）
  ÷ 每日消耗率（Daily Consumption Rate）

其中：
  每日消耗率 = 月銷售量（Monthly Sales Volume） ÷ 30

範例：
  現有庫存 = 150,000 單位
  月銷售量 = 30,000 單位
  每日消耗率 = 30,000 ÷ 30 = 1,000 單位/天
  庫存天數 = 150,000 ÷ 1,000 = 150 天

CDMO 應在系統中定期計算各產品的庫存天數，當天數低於警戒水位（例如：30 天或 60 天）時，自動觸發通報流程。

思考練習 2：庫存天數警報設計

8.2

Risk-Based Decision-Making by the Company and Health Authorities

公司與衛生主管機關的風險決策框架

8.2 Four HA Decision Points

In the event of a shortage, having a product risk assessment developed proactively using the triage method and the risk matrices in Section 6.1, and the key risk-indicating information compiled in Section 8.1 becomes very useful in determining the appropriate actions to take. In addition to the manufacturer or MAHs, CAPAs to address the risks may need to be owned by other stakeholders in the supply chain. Therefore, communication between the stakeholders — including affiliates of the MAH in a country or region, distribution centers, or pharmacies as well as the press — is most important to detect unintended consequences of a corrective or preventive measure early in the system.

Several decisions may need to be made by health authorities in response to reports of actual or pending product shortages:

HA Decision Point 1: Formal Involvement

Whether to become formally involved in managing the shortage or in coordinating a response. The risk triage method will be useful in determining:

  • Whether health authorities need to become directly involved (e.g., by taking direct measures, coordinating responses, developing work plans and action items)
  • Whether becoming directly involved is not necessary at that time
  • Whether health authorities need to monitor the situation closely without becoming directly involved

HA Decision Point 2: Alternative Supply Sourcing

Whether to facilitate the sourcing of alternative supplies of the product, involving determining:

  • Whether the MAH should be allowed to import and supply a version of the product intended for a different market that may not be authorized in the country
  • Whether this product could be batch packaged for the other market and supplied in that country's packaging and delivery
  • Whether the imported product needs to be repackaged and, if so, the level of regulatory approval and oversight required
  • Whether healthcare professionals should be advised that an unlicensed version of the product or a clinically equivalent product is available

HA Decision Point 3: Expedite Regulatory Approvals

Whether to expedite approval of a request to use an alternate unlicensed facility or a post-approval change submitted by the MAH. A related decision is whether the approval should be conditional, temporary, or permanent.

HA Decision Point 4: Communication Strategy

What communication strategies will be required and how those strategies will ensure that companies, health authorities, healthcare professionals, and/or patients can take appropriate risk mitigation actions. After the clinical risk assessment, the health authority may decide to advise healthcare professionals to:

  • Give priority to certain groups of patients (e.g., patients already treated) for continued treatment
  • Use a different medicine or treatment for new (or all) patients
  • If a product is defective (e.g., loss of sterility assurance), weigh the clinical benefits and risks for patients of continued use when no alternative product or treatment is available

Any time a shortage situation is encountered it should be viewed as an opportunity for the organization to review their risk register and, where relevant, assess suitable updates to their risk control program to improve prevention practices.

中文解析：四大 HA 決策節點

CAPA 所有權的擴展

TR68 特別指出：CAPA 的所有者不應只是製造商或 MAH，可能還需要包含供應鏈中其他利害關係人。例如：

  • 地區附屬機構（Affiliates）負責當地庫存調配的 CAPA
  • 配銷中心負責物流優化的 CAPA
  • 藥局負責藥物替換諮詢的 CAPA

CDMO 也可能需要承擔部分 CAPA 所有權，尤其是當短缺根因在於 CDMO 端的製程問題時。

比喻：「缺陷產品 vs. 無產品」的艱難抉擇

想像急診室只剩下一批「標示可能有誤」的 Epinephrine（腎上腺素）注射劑，但沒有任何替代品。醫師面臨的選擇是：用這批有標示風險的藥救治過敏性休克病患，還是什麼都不做？

TR68 的 HA Decision Point 4 正是處理這種「使用缺陷產品的臨床利益是否大於短缺帶來的傷害」情境。這個決定不應由製造商單獨做出，必須是公司與衛生主管機關共同協商的結果。

HA Decision Point 3 的 CDMO 實務影響

當 MAH 向衛生主管機關申請「加速使用備用未授權設施（Alternate Unlicensed Facility）」時，CDMO 可能就是那個「備用設施」。這意味著：

  • CDMO 須預先準備好接受衛生主管機關的緊急查廠或文件審查
  • CDMO 與 MAH 的合約中，應明確規範緊急狀況下的快速授權流程
  • 批准可能是「有條件（Conditional）」或「臨時（Temporary）」的，CDMO 須了解相應義務

短缺事件是改善機會（Continuous Improvement）

TR68 結語特別強調：每次短缺事件都應被視為組織審查風險登記冊、更新風險控制計劃的機會。具體行動包括：

  • 深入調查短缺的真正根本原因（True Root Cause）
  • 修訂 CAPA，防止相似產品再度發生短缺
  • 識別並修補本次短缺事件所揭露的系統缺口（Gaps）

這一原則與 ICH Q10 中持續改善（Continual Improvement）的精神完全吻合。

  • 7.0 五步驟循環：Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan 由「識別危害 → 評估病患衝擊 → 風險控制計劃（短期+長期）→ 溝通計劃 → 審查與監控」五個步驟構成，是持續循環的活文件，應在衛生主管機關稽查時主動分享。

  • 8.1 三類關鍵資訊：向衛生主管機關通報時需備妥（1）病患相關資訊（族群、規模、影響百分比）、（2）配銷資訊（通路、批發商、替代進口可能性）、（3）庫存資訊（現有庫存量、耗盡日期）。CDMO 應建立系統化維護機制，庫存天數 = 現有庫存 ÷ 每日消耗率。

  • CDMO 核心責任：積極貢獻各要素的 CDMO 端資訊、維護庫存透明度、預先準備緊急稽查就緒狀態，並在合約中明確規範緊急授權流程。

9.0 Conclusions | 結論

TR-68 整體框架的核心論點總結 • p38–p39

9.0 Conclusions

The objective of ensuring an uninterrupted supply of safe, efficacious products to patients is shared by regulators, legislators, healthcare providers, and industry bringing them together to communicate for jointly developing solutions that serve the best interests of patients. As the risk to the patient population from a drug shortage rises, the level of rigor, effort, and cross-functional collaboration within an organization to address the risk should also increase. This effort should be coupled with effective communication among the organization, health authorities, and other relevant stakeholders to proactively manage drug shortage risks.

Although challenging, the industry should address the concerns associated with drug shortages, given the objective of protecting patients from harm. Proactively identifying relevant drug shortage hazards and robustly managing drug shortage risks in the end-to-end product value chain, as well as effectively and promptly communicating potential shortages to all stakeholders, is essential to reduce the risk of drug shortages.

TR-68 provides a practical framework that companies can use, a triage model to assess drug shortage risks, and templates for developing a Drug Shortage Risk Register and a Drug Shortage Prevention and Response Plan at a product level. It also describes ways that industry can engage more proactively with health authorities to request expedited review and approval of certain PACs by multiple health authorities to reduce the risks of drug shortages. This could be achieved by using agreed-upon PAC management plans and harmonized gCPs. Such protocols would be helpful for companies to address the risk of drug shortages due to aging facilities, processes, and analytical technologies by modernizing their facilities and implementing new technologies. This technical report also leverages the guidance developed by the AESPG, EFPIA, EGA, and PPTA collaboration for communications between MAHs and health authorities.

中文解析與學習重點

三大核心主軸

TR-68 結論呈現三個互相強化的主軸：

  • 患者保護：確保不中斷供應是監管機構、立法者、醫療提供者和產業界的共同使命。
  • 風險驅動的努力強度：風險越高 → 嚴謹度與跨職能協作程度也應相應提升。
  • 主動溝通：與衛生當局及所有利害關係人的有效即時溝通，是防止短缺的核心機制。

TR-68 提供的三個工具

  • 風險登記表模板（Risk Register）— 產品層級的系統化記錄

風險登記表的三個關鍵設計邏輯

  • 「報紙標題式」風險描述：逼使填寫者思清楚「如果明天頭版是這個產品的短缺新聞，標題會是什麼？」這種表達方式確保風險描述具體、有緊迫感。
  • 先行與落後指標並列：先行指標（leading indicators）用於早期預警（庫存水位、供應商稽核結果）；落後指標（lagging indicators）用於驗證管控效果（批次拒絕率）。兩者並用才能形成完整監控機制。
  • 前後風險等級對比：記錄「Initial Risk Level」與「Risk Level After Control」，直接量化管控措施的有效性，也為管理層審查提供清晰的決策依據。

Risk Level 判斷邏輯

Part B 的核心是患者影響的二維矩陣：治療用途（嚴重性） × 替代品可用性。

三個 Risk Level 的實務含義

  • Risk Level A：最高患者風險 → 需要最嚴格的管控，任何短跡象都需立即上報衛生當局。典型範例：新生兒腸外營養液、胰島素、抗癌化療藥。
  • Risk Level B：中等患者風險 → 需要強化管控但可有一定緩衝期。典型範例：具有可替代品的慢性病用藥（有其他廠牌可用的降壓藥）。
  • Risk Level C：較低患者風險 → 標準管控即可。典型範例：非處方止痛藥、便秘藥等，有多種替代選項。

注意：即使是 Risk Level C，仍需完成 Part D 的預防計畫，只是控制措施的嚴格程度較低。

優先等級的管理意涵

Part C 是資源分配決策矩陣

Risk Priority Level 決定了公司需要投入多少資源進行管控。這個邏輯與 ICH Q9(R1) 的「風險決策原則」一致：風險等級越高，需要投入越多的驗證、監控和緩解措施。

  • Priority 1：最高優先 — 需要主動的預防措施（雙重來源、高安全庫存、PAC 備案）+ 主動與衛生當局溝通
  • Priority 2：中等優先 — 需要監控機制和應急計畫，定期複審
  • Priority 3：一般優先 — 標準品質系統管控，納入年度風險審查

關鍵洞察：即使是 Risk Level A 的產品，如果短缺可能性低（有多重預防措施），Priority 也可以是 2 而非 1 — 這鼓勵公司主動建立防護措施以降低優先等級。

三級批准（負責人 + QA 決策者 + 高級管理層）確保短缺防範計畫獲得組織最高層級的正式背書。這符合 ICH Q10 對管理責任的要求，也是稽核時展示管理承諾的重要文件。

建議：將藥品短缺防範計畫的年度複審納入管理層審查（Management Review）議程，與品質系統其他關鍵指標一同審視。
