1.0 Introduction

1.0 Introduction

Microbial data deviations are microbial test results that fall outside the product specification or acceptance criterion established in a drug application, drug master file, official compendia, good manufacturing practice regulations, or internally by the manufacturer. This includes, but is not limited to, results generated during environmental and water monitoring as well as pharmaceutical ingredient, in-process, and finished product testing. The validity of the test results is supported by a documented laboratory and manufacturing investigation, where applicable.

The information presented in this technical report was compiled by a team of experts representing both industry microbiologists and regulators and reflects current industry best practices. The membership of personnel from the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) on the task force does not imply Agency endorsement. Feedback from industry and regulatory professionals was evaluated and incorporated into the technical report to ensure that the suggested methods, terminology, and practices for conducting microbial data deviation investigations reflect sound science and current technological approaches. This technical report is not intended to establish any mandatory or implied standards. Published references supporting the recommendations presented are cited in the text and listed in Section 7.0 References.

Definitions for the terms used throughout this report are provided in the Glossary. When available, the same or similar technical definitions have been used as those included in the FDA or International Conference on Harmonization (ICH) guidelines. To avoid ambiguity, the task force members believe that use and definitions of these terms should also be consistent and standardized when incorporated into company-specific standard operating procedures, validation protocols, and regulatory submissions.

導讀解析

核心概念：什麼是微生物數據偏差？

凡微生物測試結果超出以下任一基準，即構成偏差：

  • 藥品申請文件（Drug Application）或藥品主文件（DMF）中的規格
  • 官方藥典（USP、EP 等）
  • GMP 法規要求
  • 製造商內部制定的標準

涵蓋範圍：環境監控（EM）、水系統、原料成分、中間體及成品測試——所有微生物數據都在管轄之內。

比喻：偏差就像汽車儀表板的警示燈

警示燈亮起不代表車子壞了，但你必須調查原因——是真的引擎故障（真實製程問題），還是感應器誤報（實驗室誤差）？TR88 給的就是這份「診斷手冊」，告訴你每種警示燈的診斷流程。

監管重點：FDA 參與不等於背書

FDA 人員參與 TR88 撰寫任務小組，但本報告不構成強制標準，也不代表 FDA 正式背書。這是業界 best practice 指引，非法規命令。

CDMO 實務意義：可援引 TR88 作為內部 SOP 的科學依據，但客戶合約中若有更嚴格要求，以合約為準。

思考練習

你的廠區上週發生一筆環境監控（EM）數值超過 Action Limit 的結果。這是「microbial data deviation」嗎？測試結果的效力（validity）如何確認？

提示：看偏差定義中「環境及水監控」是否明確被納入範圍，以及「documented laboratory and manufacturing investigation」如何支撐結果效力。

1.1 Purpose

1.1 Purpose

The FDA issued a Guidance for Industry: Investigating Out of Specification (OOS) Test Results for Pharmaceutical Production in 2006 in response to the U.S. versus Barr Laboratories decision, which is applicable to laboratory testing results for products regulated by the Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER) (1, 2). The FDA guidance does not extend to microbiological failure investigations, however. Consequently, PDA developed this technical report to provide approaches, specific to the pharmaceutical industry, for investigating various types of microbial data deviations based on current scientific knowledge and modern testing technologies.

The term "microbial data deviation" is preferred to "out-of-specification" or "out-of-limit result" in this technical report, as most microbiological tests, especially in a sterile product manufacturing facility, are in-process tests, not finished-product tests that must meet a regulatory-approved specification. For simplicity and consistency, the term "deviation" will be used throughout this document.

Technical Report No. 88 presents a holistic approach for performing a microbiological investigation. It provides a framework to assist with focusing on the investigational areas that may contain or contribute to the root cause of data deviations, such as sampling, test methodology, and suitability of the test, as opposed to providing methods for analysis of batch acceptability. (Batch acceptability determinations and release decisions are made by the quality control unit as required by FDA good manufacturing practice (GMP) regulations, not by deviation investigation teams.)

導讀解析

為什麼不叫 OOS，要叫「Deviation」？

FDA 2006 年的 OOS 指引源自 U.S. v. Barr Laboratories 判決，只適用於 CDER 監管的成品測試，明確不適用於微生物學失敗調查。

微生物學的特殊性：大多數微生物測試（尤其無菌充填環境中）是過程中測試（in-process tests），而非有法規規格的成品測試。用「OOS」這個詞會造成監管上的混淆，因此 TR88 統一用「deviation（偏差）」。

比喻：OOS 是出口海關，偏差是生產線品管站

OOS 像海關——只在最終出口（成品）把關，有明確的法規規格線。微生物偏差更像生產線上的品管站——每個環節（EM、水系統、中間體）都可能需要介入，但沒有統一的「合格放行線」。TR88 是為這些中間站制定的診斷框架。

重要邊界：調查 ≠ 批次放行決策

TR88 明確劃定：偏差調查團隊負責找根本原因（root cause），但批次放行（batch disposition）決策屬於 QCU 的職責，依 FDA GMP 法規執行。兩者不可混淆。

CDMO 紅線：即便調查結論是「實驗室誤差，結果無效」，批次是否放行仍需 QCU 正式核准——不能由調查團隊自行決定。

客戶要求在偏差調查報告完成前先放行批次。你會如何回應？引用 TR88 中哪個原則？合約品質協議（Quality Agreement）應如何預先規範這個情境？

1.2 Scope

1.2 Scope

Each of the three sections of this document focuses on distinct areas where microbial data is collected and inspected for deviation investigations and with the following designated responsibilities:

Roles and Responsibilities—Focuses on personnel, especially microbiologists, in the laboratory investigations and cross-functional teams responsible for manufacturing investigations.

Conducting Laboratory Investigations (Phase I)—Provides additional details on how an investigation may be conducted to determine that the microbial test was valid and what is the root cause of a failure when the result is deemed invalid. Subsections focus on different microbiological tests conducted, with points for consideration in the final determination of the investigation.

Conducting Manufacturing Investigations (Phase II)—Illustrates where a microbiologist, acting as a subject matter expert (SME), can contribute to the evaluation of personnel activities, environmental changes, utilities, materials, and process-related events and can assist in determining the probable cause of the deviation and the most appropriate corrective and preventive actions (CAPA).

If needed and when appropriate, Phase I and Phase II investigation activities can be conducted in parallel.

The roles of other contributors to deviation investigations required for effective root cause analysis, such as representatives from manufacturing, process engineering, and quality assurance, will also be briefly described. The composition of the investigation team will depend on the nature of the investigation.

This technical report promotes a lifecycle approach, but greater emphasis is placed on laboratory and manufacturing investigations of marketed drug products. The approaches described may be suitable during research and product development and the manufacture of clinical supplies, as well as the manufacture and testing of regulatory-approved products. The microbial tests discussed in this technical report include microbial attribute testing of nonsterile products, components, sterility testing, bacterial endotoxin assay, mycoplasma testing, environmental and personnel monitoring, and microbial monitoring of utilities including pharmaceutical-grade water and compressed gases.

Microbial assays of antibiotics and vitamins and the detection and enumeration of protozoans, viruses, and prions are not included in the scope of this report. Additionally, batch disposition is separate from the outcome of a microbial investigation report. This document is directed specifically to pharmaceutical manufacturing; however, the industry practices may be suitable for deviation investigations in the biological, consumer health, medical devices, and cosmetic industries. In addition, these can be extended to contract manufacturing and testing organizations supporting pharmaceutical companies.

While this document provides a framework illustrating how deviations and the resulting investigations might be conducted, each company is responsible for developing their internal policies and procedures suitable for its specific needs that also meets current GMPs.

導讀解析

核心架構：Phase I + Phase II 雙階段調查

Phase I — 實驗室調查

  • 核心問題：這次測試結果有效嗎？
  • 調查主體：微生物實驗室（分析員 + 管理層）
  • 目標：確認結果有效性；若無效，確定實驗室誤差的根因

Phase II — 製造調查

  • 核心問題：若測試有效，製造端出了什麼問題？
  • 調查主體：跨職能團隊（製造、工程、QA + 微生物 SME）
  • 目標：確定最可能根因（probable cause），提出 CAPA

關鍵：兩個 Phase 可以並行——不必等 Phase I 完全結束才啟動 Phase II。時間敏感性要求如此。

比喻：醫院的雙軌急診診斷

病患送急診，醫生同步進行：(1) 確認檢驗數據是否正確（Phase I）；(2) 調查可能病因（Phase II：感染源、用藥史、環境接觸）。兩條線並行才能快速找到真相。

CDMO 關鍵洞察：品質協議必須劃分 Phase I vs. Phase II 責任

TR88 明確指出框架可延伸至「contract manufacturing and testing organizations」。對無菌充填 CDMO：

  • Phase I（實驗室）：通常由 CDMO 的微生物實驗室主導
  • Phase II（製造）：CDMO 主導，但客戶可能要求參與或索取報告
  • 合約品質協議（Quality Agreement）必須清楚規定：誰負責哪個 Phase、時間線要求、報告格式與核准機制

未明確規定的後果：客訴糾紛、查廠發現、批次被客戶拒絕放行。

思考練習

你的廠發生一筆無菌測試（Sterility Test）陽性結果。請畫出 Phase I 和 Phase II 的調查啟動時序：哪些事項必須在 Phase I 結論前完成？哪些可以並行？誰有權核准並行啟動？

2.0 Glossary of Terms

術語解析

三層警戒結構：Alert Level → Action Limit → Adverse Trend

理解這三個術語的層次關係是 EM 管理的基礎：

  • Alert Level（警示水準）：早期預警，超標不一定要矯正行動，但必須加強監控與追蹤。
  • Action Limit（行動限度）：超標即表示製程已偏離正常操作範圍，必須啟動書面調查並採取矯正行動。
  • Adverse Trend（不利趨勢）：不是單一超標事件，而是超標頻率上升、或連續低濃度微生物回收的模式——代表製程控制正在流失，需要系統性評估。

比喻：三色交通燈

Alert Level = 黃燈（放慢、觀察）；Action Limit = 紅燈（停車、採取行動）；Adverse Trend = 連續黃燈後的模式警示（這個路口設計有問題，需要根本改善）。

Invalid Result vs. Invalid Test：微妙但重要的區別

  • Invalid Result（無效結果）：測試做完了，但經過 Phase I 調查確認結果無效。
  • Invalid Test（無效測試）：測試本身未能符合方法要求；包括測試被中止的情況（如隔離器在無菌測試途中故障）。

這個區別影響調查記錄的方式和後續動作的觸發條件——兩者在 Phase I 報告中須清楚區分。

CAPA 的核心地位：不只是一張表格

TR88 將 CAPA 定義為「收集分析資訊、識別並調查品質問題、採取有效措施防止再發」的子系統（subsystem）——它是一套完整的問責機制，不是填個表就結束。

CAPA 的有效性（Effectiveness）是任何偏差調查的最終考驗。若 CAPA 執行後同樣偏差再次發生，監管機構會追問為什麼根因找錯了。

Correction vs. Corrective Action：治標 vs. 治本

  • Correction（矯正）：處理已發生的偏差（修補、重工）——治標。
  • Corrective Action（矯正措施）：消除偏差的原因，防止再發——治本。

稽查常見問題：企業只做了 Correction（例如重測）卻沒有真正的 Corrective Action（消除根因），導致偏差反覆發生。

術語識別練習

請分類以下情境屬於哪個術語：

  1. A 無菌室連續三個月 Alert Level 超標率從 2% 升至 8%。
  2. 無菌測試途中 RABS 故障，測試中止。
  3. 水系統微生物超過 Action Limit，啟動書面調查。
  4. 發現測試員用錯培養基，結果不可信。

答案：1. Adverse Trend；2. Invalid Test；3. Environmental Monitoring Excursion（+ 觸發 OOL 調查）；4. Invalid Result（涉及 Laboratory Error）

以下句子中哪個縮寫用錯了？

3.0 Microbiologist Roles and Responsibilities — 3.1 Laboratory Microbiologist Responsibilities

3.1 Laboratory Microbiologist Responsibilities

The principal responsibility for achieving accurate laboratory testing results resides with the analyst performing the microbiological test. Recognition of potential practices that could create invalid results should be an integral part of the laboratory analyst's training. The analyst is responsible for performing all testing according to standard operating procedures (SOP) and in-house documentation practices. The analyst should only use qualified, calibrated, and well-maintained laboratory instruments and equipment and approved growth media, diluents, and reagents.

The analyst should check the product test results for compliance with their specifications before discarding test preparations, whenever practical. If any potential or suspected laboratory errors occurred during testing, the analyst must discontinue testing immediately and notify their laboratory management. The analyst, with guidance and instruction from the laboratory management, must collect and retain all test samples, reagents, consumables, paperwork, and any other relevant materials related to the affected assay, even if the test is deemed invalid, to aid the laboratory investigation.

If errors are clear and indisputable, such as atypical appearance of a test sample (e.g., unexpected particulate matter present), damaged test container, test sample spillage, use of wrong pipetting volumes, missing dilution steps, aseptic handling missteps, or use of the wrong testing medium, the analyst should immediately document the event. When in doubt, the analyst may consult with the laboratory management to confirm that the test should be discontinued.

The analyst should not knowingly continue a test that they expect to invalidate at a later time for an assignable cause (i.e., analyses should not be completed for the sole purpose of seeing that a passing result can be obtained when obvious errors are known). In addition, the analyst is responsible for assisting management with the laboratory investigation.

導讀解析

分析員的核心職責：數據品質的第一道防線

實驗室分析員（Laboratory Analyst）的職責包含：

  • 嚴格按 SOP 執行所有測試
  • 只使用合格、校正過的儀器與核准的培養基 / 稀釋液 / 試劑
  • 棄置測試製備前先確認結果是否符合規格
  • 察覺潛在誤差時立即停止測試並通報管理層
  • 保留所有相關樣品、試劑、耗材和紀錄以協助調查
  • 協助管理層進行實驗室調查

比喻：分析員是實驗室的「誠實目擊者」

外科醫生在手術中發現器械污染，第一個責任不是繼續手術期待不出事，而是立即暫停、通報、保留證據。實驗室分析員的角色完全相同。發現問題就停下來，是專業素養，不是失職。

監管紅線：不得為了得到合格結果而繼續有問題的測試

TR88 明確聲明："analyses should not be completed for the sole purpose of seeing that a passing result can be obtained when obvious errors are known."

這不只是品質要求，也是 FDA Data Integrity 的監管底線。違反此原則可能導致 Warning Letter、Form 483 觀察，甚至刑事指控。

CDMO 管理意義：分析員需要心理安全感（Psychological Safety）——在發現問題時能立即停止並通報，而不是擔心影響生產進度而硬撐。這是管理層和組織文化的責任。

情境判斷練習

分析員在無菌測試第 3 天觀察到某一培養基外觀顏色偏差，但尚未出現明顯渾濁。請問：

  1. 應立即停止測試，還是先觀察至第 7 天？
  2. 測試器材應保留還是棄置？
  3. 應記錄什麼？何時記錄？

提示：「when in doubt」原則和「即使測試被宣告無效，相關材料仍須保留」的規定如何共同指導行動？

3.2 Microbiologist Role in a Manufacturing Investigation

3.2 Microbiologist Role in a Manufacturing Investigation

The key to successful manufacturing investigations of microbial testing failures with pharmaceutical ingredients and finished drug products is the inclusion of an experienced microbiologist as an SME on the manufacturing investigation team. This microbiologist may work in the quality control (QC) microbiology testing laboratory at the manufacturing site, the quality control unit (QCU), product development, or a corporate microbiology support group. Ideally, the microbiologist should have a comprehensive knowledge of product formulation and specific manufacturing operations, obtained from frequent shop-floor visits or surveillance of the overall process. For smaller organizations, without internal microbiological expertise, the microbiologist may be an outside consultant. To be truly effective, the SME must be a trained microbiologist with broad experience in microbiological investigations. Furthermore, it would be advantageous if the SME is familiar with the actual product implicated in the failure. Without this expertise on the investigation team, it is less likely that the root cause of the failure can be determined, and the investigation may be hampered or severely limited.

The microbiologist should be engaged in the investigation as soon as is practical, as microbiological investigations are always time-sensitive.

The microbiologist should be part of the first step in an investigation, such as performing a walk-through of the manufacturing area, to become oriented and identify situations and events that are nonroutine and may be directly linked to the deviation. To be successful, the microbiologist must have access to all related testing results, have the cooperation of manufacturing managers, supervisors, operators, and support personnel, and have the authority to interview staff, inspect processing equipment, take investigatory microbial samples, and review change control and batch records. In some instances, access to customer complaint records may be necessary. For failures related to purchased pharmaceutical ingredients, it is highly desirable that a microbiologist join any audit team visiting the ingredient supplier to conduct an investigational review.

Systematic, proactive risk assessments for assessing the overall aseptic manufacturing process and controls have been proven helpful in identifying potential weaknesses in advance, and may mitigate the occurrence of a deviation, for example, sterility failures and endotoxin or environmental monitoring (EM) excursions (12).

導讀解析

製造調查中的微生物學家：技術橋樑，缺不可

TR88 強調的理想 SME 微生物學家特質：

  • 受過正式微生物學訓練
  • 有廣泛的微生物調查實戰經驗
  • 熟悉產品配方和具體製造操作（最好是現場走訪所累積的知識）
  • 理想情況下熟悉該次失敗涉及的具體產品

TR88 直接說：若調查團隊中沒有這樣的人，「the investigation may be hampered or severely limited」。

比喻：微生物學家是犯罪現場的法醫

警察（製造/工程人員）可以描述現場狀況，但要判斷「這個污染源在 24 小時內留下的嗎」，你需要法醫（微生物 SME）。法醫必須在現場及早介入，因為微生物證據（菌株、環境條件）會隨時間消失——「time-sensitive」不是比喻，是微生物學的物理現實。

實務關鍵：微生物學家必須有正式調查授權

TR88 列出微生物 SME 在調查中必須具備的授權：

  • 取得所有相關測試結果
  • 訪談製造部門員工（操作員、主管、支援人員）
  • 檢查加工設備
  • 採集調查性微生物樣品（Investigatory Microbial Samples）
  • 審閱變更控制紀錄和批次紀錄
  • （必要時）查閱客訴記錄

若微生物學家在調查中被製造部門「擋門」，或沒有採集額外樣品的正式授權，調查品質將嚴重受損。這是管理層需要在制度層面解決的問題。

CDMO 應用：主動預防勝於被動調查

TR88 指出：系統性、主動式風險評估有助於提前識別製程弱點，可降低偏差（包括無菌測試失敗、內毒素 / EM 超標）發生率。

對 Operations Director 的啟示：不要只在偏差發生後才找微生物 SME。讓微生物學家定期參與製程風險評估（FMEA、PHA），才是真正的主動品質管理。

3.3 Microbiology Laboratory Management Responsibilities

3.3 Microbiology Laboratory Management Responsibilities

Upon notification by a laboratory analyst of a suspected laboratory error, the laboratory management must conduct an objective assessment to determine if an error has occurred. Data should be reviewed promptly to ensure the contemporaneous memory of events and to determine if the results might be attributed to an isolated or systematic laboratory error. In the absence of laboratory error, a confirmed valid test could indicate problems in the manufacturing operations; however, all facts need to be evaluated before predetermining a cause. The correct compliance assumption is that the results are valid until proven otherwise.

Laboratory management should ensure that the probable cause is supported by evidence, using root cause analysis tools when appropriate (e.g., 5 Whys, Ishikawa/fishbone diagram). Laboratory management should promptly notify the QCU and any impacted departments of the suspected deviation and inform them that a laboratory investigation has been initiated and when it might be completed.

If testing is conducted by a contract testing laboratory, the quality agreement between the contract testing laboratory and the manufacturer should clearly assign responsibilities, include a timeline, and specify the requirements of the laboratory investigation.

導讀解析

實驗室管理層的行動框架

收到分析員通報後，管理層的標準反應流程：

  1. 客觀評估：是否確實發生誤差？避免「先入為主」
  2. 分類誤差：孤立性（Isolated）vs. 系統性（Systematic）實驗室誤差
  3. 前提假設：「結果有效，直到被證明為無效」——不能因為結果不好看就預設是測試問題
  4. 使用根因工具：5 Whys、魚骨圖（Ishikawa / Fishbone Diagram）
  5. 主動通報 QCU 和受影響部門：及時、附上預估調查完成時間

比喻：法官的「無罪推定」原則

「結果有效直到被證明無效」就是微生物學版本的「無罪推定」。如果默認所有不好看的結果都是「實驗室誤差」，整個品質體系就失去意義——這也是 FDA 在 Data Integrity 方面最在乎的核心原則之一。

「最可能原因」原則：偵查需要證據，不需要確定性

TR88 用的是 probable cause（最可能原因），不是 definitive root cause（確定根因）。現實中，微生物調查很難達到 100% 確定——菌株可能無法溯源，環境條件可能已改變。

監管要求的是：結論有證據支持、根因工具被適當使用、CAPA 針對 probable cause 設計。「找不到確定根因」不是可接受的結論，但「最可能原因是 X，依據是 Y 和 Z，CAPA 是 A」是可接受的。

CDMO 合約實驗室的特殊規定

若由合約測試實驗室執行測試，品質協議（Quality Agreement）必須：

  • 清楚分配調查職責（誰負責哪個部分）
  • 包含調查時間線（Timeline）
  • 規定實驗室調查的具體要求

對 CDMO 的雙重意涵：你既可能是「受委方」（對客戶負責），也可能是「委託方」（對分包實驗室有要求）。兩個方向的品質協議都必須覆蓋偏差調查責任分工。

  3. 先等 14 天觀察期結束再決定

3.4 Subject Matter Expert Responsibilities

3.4 Subject Matter Expert Responsibilities

SMEs can support the investigation by providing technical assistance related to the assay methods and technologies utilized, designing any investigational testing, and providing general scientific guidance, as needed. This expert could have greater experience and scientific background related to the assay or manufacturing process from within the company. Hiring a qualified outside consultant may also be an option.

導讀解析

SME 的角色定位：技術顧問，非調查主導者

SME（Subject Matter Expert，主題專家）在 TR88 中的定位是支援性的：

  • 提供測試方法和技術的專業協助
  • 設計調查性測試（Investigational Testing）
  • 提供一般科學指引

注意：SME 的定義比「製造調查微生物學家（3.2 節）」更廣——可以是任何領域的專家（設備工程師、統計學家等），且可以來自公司外部。

比喻：SME 是調查的「外腦」或「會診醫師」

重大醫療案例會邀請院外專家會診（Second Opinion）。偏差調查邀請 SME 是為了引入超出日常運營知識的深度專業——在 CDMO 特別有用，因為不常見的測試方法或設備問題可能超出內部能力。

何時需要外部 SME？

  • 涉及少見生物體（如孢子形成菌的無菌測試失敗）
  • 需要特殊鑑定技術（如全基因組定序 WGS）
  • 重大客訴或監管調查，需要獨立第三方意見
  • 內部調查陷入瓶頸，根因遲遲無法確定

最佳實踐：建立外部 SME 名單，包含聯絡方式和資格資訊，在偏差發生前就準備好。

3.5 Quality Control Unit Responsibilities

3.5 Quality Control Unit Responsibilities

The QCU oversees all aspects of the microbial deviation investigation. A QCU representative or other approved personnel would be responsible for coordinating with the cross-functional team involved in supporting the investigation, including personnel from manufacturing, facilities, calibration, validation; approving any laboratory retesting plans; and approving the laboratory and manufacturing investigations. The QCU also would be responsible for overseeing quality agreements made with contract testing laboratories and for ensuring that microbial deviation investigations are adequately addressed.

導讀解析

QCU：調查的最終監管者與核准機構

QCU 在偏差調查中扮演監督（Oversee）與核准（Approve）角色，而非直接執行：

  • 協調跨職能調查團隊（製造、設施、校正、驗證等）
  • 核准實驗室重測計劃（Laboratory Retesting Plans）
  • 核准 Phase I（實驗室）和 Phase II（製造）調查報告及結論
  • 監督對合約測試實驗室的品質協議執行
  • 確保微生物偏差調查被充分且妥善處理

比喻：QCU 是法院的法官，不是偵探

偵探（實驗室管理層、微生物 SME、跨職能團隊）蒐集證據和提出結論。法官（QCU）審核程序是否合規、判決是否有依據，並正式核准最終結論。沒有法官的核准，任何結論都不具效力。

CDMO Operations Director 的制度建置清單

確保以下制度已到位，才能在偏差發生時有效運作：

  • 分析員層面：訓練計劃包含「何時停止測試」的判斷準則；建立心理安全文化，讓人敢於通報問題而不是隱瞞。
  • 實驗室管理層：書面程序規定接到通報後的標準反應時序（例如：1小時內完成初步評估）；根因分析工具使用培訓。
  • 微生物 SME：SOP 中明確列出 SME 的調查通行授權；建立外部 SME 名單（含緊急聯絡）。
  • QCU：核准流程有明確 SLA；對客戶的品質協議已覆蓋 Phase I vs. Phase II 的責任分工。

本節重點回顧

  1. 偏差定義：微生物數據偏差涵蓋 EM、水系統、原料、中間體、成品等所有微生物測試，超出任何已建立的基準（法規、藥典、內部標準）即構成偏差。測試結果的效力需有書面調查支撐。
  2. 術語精確性：用「deviation」而非「OOS」，因為大多數微生物測試是 in-process tests；Alert Level（警示）、Action Limit（行動限度）、Adverse Trend（不利趨勢）三層結構必須清楚區分；Invalid Result 與 Invalid Test 有不同的調查含義。
  3. 雙階段框架：Phase I（實驗室，確認測試有效性）和 Phase II（製造，確認製程根因）可並行；批次放行決策始終屬於 QCU，不是調查團隊的工作。
  4. 調查的黃金原則：結果有效直到被證明無效；找 probable cause 而非要求 definitive root cause；絕不為了合格結果繼續有問題的測試；微生物調查 time-sensitive，越早介入越好。
  5. CDMO 核心行動項目：品質協議必須明確 Phase I vs. Phase II 的 CDMO / 客戶責任分工；微生物 SME 必須有正式調查授權；分析員文化須支持「發現問題立即停止通報」；CAPA 有效性是最終考驗。

4.0 Phase I: Conducting the Microbiology Laboratory Investigation

Original Text (English)

The first phase of a microbial data deviation (MDD) investigation is an initial assessment of the results reported by the microbiology laboratory. The purpose of the laboratory investigation is to establish the validity of test results, thereby confirming that a deviation has occurred. If a laboratory error has occurred, which would invalidate the test, the QCU (Quality Control Unit) would approve retesting and/or resampling of the pharmaceutical ingredient or drug product/drug substance. Some compendial microbiology testing chapters have limited information to address how to handle sample deviations and investigations. This technical report will discuss additional factors for microbiological evaluation of sterility, bacterial endotoxin, and other microbiological testing conditions for invalidating test results or allowing an initial test to be repeated.

Pharmaceutical QC microbiology laboratories vary in size and complexity. These factors may impact the level of experience with dealing with deviations in terms of the training, experience, and academic background of the analysts and laboratory management.

Contrary to the belief that microbial failure investigations are usually inconclusive, experience has demonstrated that the causes of the microbial contamination are determinable through robust investigations conducted by teams that include an experienced microbiologist. In the absence of a definitive cause, the most probable cause(s) can be determined based on the available information and follow-up observations, additional testing, and batch-record review. To avoid a confirmation bias, the team must be willing to reject their original hypothesis about the probable cause in light of the evidence subsequently collected. If possible, as many questions should be asked as to why the probable cause is not correct as are asked about why it is correct. Hindsight, where the successes of past microbial failure investigations often are exaggerated and may incorrectly influence the direction of the current investigation, is another bias that must be recognized and avoided. However, a history of conducting investigations will enhance the success with current investigations.

When no obvious laboratory errors are noted, the laboratory management must initiate a laboratory investigation promptly and must complete it as soon as is practically possible in accordance with regulatory expectations and company policies and procedures. Accurately recording the sequence of activities may prove helpful in determining root cause during an investigation. Some tests, such as microbial identification, may require more time and may delay completion of the laboratory investigation. If a laboratory error (Phase I investigation) cannot be found that invalidates the results, the original test results are presumed to be correct. The next step, then, is to conduct a Phase II investigation, including review of manufacturing batch records and other facility documents available to find the possible cause of the deviation or trend that may have occurred during manufacturing. Specific points to consider might include:

  • If an analyst thinks a mistake has been made in any part of the testing, including sample preparation, the assay should be terminated, and the justification documented.
  • Analysts should be instructed to neither destroy nor discard the sample, sample-preparation dilution tubes, or reagents, when possible, until the final results are known, confirmed, and released. Comparing them with the product specification may be an important part of the laboratory investigation.
  • Caution should be used in conducting investigational assays. They are not considered retests, but a response to the deviation and should not be used to release material. A laboratory SOP should precisely describe the procedure(s) that must be followed if a deviation is observed. Such a document eliminates unplanned decisions regarding the appropriateness of investigational testing, retesting, or resampling. When appropriate, retesting can be conducted according to established compendial directives.
  • Investigational testing provides the laboratory an opportunity to assess the hypotheses regarding possible reagent or analytical failures in testing; it frequently aims to identify and change a variable (e.g., analyst) from the first failed test. Investigational testing should be well-defined and justified, preapproved, and well-documented. Values generated from the investigational test may provide a clue as to the actual or potential product contamination or a mishandling of a positive control or a tube mix-up. The purpose of performing investigational tests is not to reverse the original deviation, but to gain information regarding a potential failure, so a more effective effort can be made to pinpoint the root cause and implement any necessary CAPAs.

Fully documenting each step in the lab investigation is critical. Laboratory management should determine not only the reliability of the individual test result obtained, but also the significance of the deviation. If the deviation is verified to be correct, they must determine what that represents about the laboratory quality assurance program and what impact the final report may have on other product lots. Laboratory management should continue to monitor for developing trends.

General points to consider during the investigation phase include:

  • Having all QC challenge microorganisms genotypically and phenotypically profiled may be advantageous so a library is available to compare product isolates in the event of cross-contamination during testing.
  • If growth is detected on the negative control media, these isolates should be identified for investigative purposes.
  • The two-fold variance described in USP <61> and <1111> as the maximum acceptable count applies only to approved specifications in cfu/g or mL, expressed as a power of 10.
  • Nonsterile products tested at one or two product dilutions for bioburden (e.g., 1:10, 1:100) may need to be retested at higher product dilutions to obtain a countable number of colonies, if the highest product dilution tested yields a result too numerous to count.
  • Due to the variability of microbiological results, the investigation should not be limited to the specific batch but extended to multiple batches, excipients, or APIs over a selected period of time.
  • Include historical trend data for the assay, controls, and any additional factors that may support the investigation hypothesis and/or investigational results.

核心概念：Phase I 的根本目的

先問「這個檢測結果可信嗎？」，再問「產品有問題嗎？」

Phase I 的邏輯起點非常清楚：在宣布偏差（deviation）成立之前，必須先確認檢測本身是有效的。如果實驗室自己出了錯，那個「陽性結果」可能根本不代表產品污染。

這個邏輯在法規上有直接依據：USP <71> 允許在特定條件下「作廢」（invalidate）一次無菌試驗，並進行重測。但前提是你能夠提出有力的實驗室錯誤證據。

比喻：醫療診斷的類比

想像病人血液報告顯示異常數值。在告知病人「你生病了」之前，醫生會先問：這支試管有沒有被污染？離心機有沒有校正？試劑有沒有過期？

Phase I 就是這個「先確認儀器沒問題」的步驟。如果試管被污染了，那個數值不代表任何疾病。

在無菌填充的情境中：培養基出現濁度（turbidity）不等於產品非無菌。它只是一個信號，需要調查才能確認信號的來源。

重要警示：確認偏誤（Confirmation Bias）

原文特別提到兩種危險心理：

  • 確認偏誤（Confirmation Bias）：一旦你認定「是產品問題」，你可能只收集支持這個假設的證據，而忽視反向證據。
  • 事後偏誤（Hindsight Bias）：用過去調查的成功經驗來套用在當前案例，可能讓你走錯方向。

對 CDMO 而言，這兩種偏誤都特別危險——因為你的客戶往往急著知道「是不是他們的產品有問題」，而你的實驗室承受著壓力，可能在調查尚未完成前就傾向某個結論。

CDMO 應用：合約實驗室的特殊挑戰

TR88 的調查框架對合約實驗室（contract lab）與品牌商自有實驗室同等適用。在 CDMO 環境下，有幾個額外考量：

  • 品質協議（Quality Agreement）必須明確規定：誰負責 Phase I 調查？誰審閱 raw data？客戶是否有權要求額外的鑑定確認？
  • 調查時效：CDMO 的批次排程緊湊，但調查不能因商業壓力而倉促結束。
  • 記錄可追溯性：客戶有權在稽核時查閱完整調查記錄，因此每個步驟的時序記錄格外重要。

  • Retest（重測）：在調查確認 invalidation 之後，依法規要求正式重複的試驗，結果可用於批次放行判斷。

混淆這兩者是常見的法規問題。調查性試驗的數據不能用來「推翻」原始偏差。

4.1 Microbial Identification

Original Text (English)

Identifying the microorganism(s) implicated in product failure is a critical step in a laboratory investigation and, subsequently, in the manufacturing investigation. Knowledge of the source of the microorganism may lead to a possible route to determine which microorganism contaminated the product.

Microbiological identification systems are based on different analytical methodologies, with their inherent limitations, and the databases related to the system. Identification is accomplished by matching characteristics (genotypic, phenotypic, or proteotypic) to an established standard (reference) microorganism, such as a strain-type in the database. If a microorganism is not included in the library or database of the system, it will not be identified. Therefore, pharmaceutical manufacturers should review the breadth of the database of the identification system they plan to use and its applicability to their use requirements.

As stated in USP <1113> Microbial Characterization, Identification and Strain Typing, the hierarchy of microbial identification errors of impact, in descending order, is (1) misidentification to genera, (2) misidentification to species, and (3) no identification. Incorrect identifications could lead to a faulty investigation, inappropriate CAPAs, and ill-advised product disposition. For example, a misidentification may result in a nonsterile drug product being released when it contains an objectionable microorganism. If the in-house identification system cannot identify the isolate or if there is doubt regarding the identification, it should be sent to a contract testing laboratory for confirmation; otherwise, a misidentified microorganism will always be carried in the records.

The need for microbial identification is specifically cited in USP <62> Microbiological Examination of Nonsterile Products: Tests for Specified Microorganisms. This chapter presents a requirement for confirmatory identification tests for organisms that grow on selective or diagnostic media and demonstrate defined morphological characteristics. As a related aspect, USP <71> Sterility Tests allows for invalidation of the test if, after identification of the microorganism(s) isolated from the test, the contamination may be unequivocally ascribed to faults with respect to the material and/or the technique used in conducting the sterility test procedure.

Taking a polyphasic identification approach is recommended, that is, one that assembles and assimilates many levels of information from molecular, physiological, morphological, serological, or ecological sources to classify a microorganism. Some vendors of identification instruments encourage microbiologists to forgo the preliminary characterization steps, such as colony description, Gram reaction, cellular morphology description, and/or diagnostic screening. Forgoing these steps is not recommended as it is often necessary to assess the microorganism identification against all available information to determine if the identification makes sense. For example, Salmonella typhi is unlikely to be isolated from a cleanroom, but this pathogen could be present in an animal-derived ingredient.

Another consideration is that many phenotypic identification systems require the preliminary characterization of the isolate for the selection of the appropriate identification kit. An incorrect Gram stain may result in a misidentification and flawed investigation. Criticality of this routine test should not be overlooked.

4.2.1 Sterility Testing Investigations

Original Text (English)

If a laboratory sterility test (USP <71>) result used for release testing of a pharmaceutical or biological drug product or a medical device indicates microbial growth in the microbiological growth medium (i.e., turbidity, pellicle formation, precipitation, or flocculent growth) during the incubation period and is subsequently confirmed via subculture and/or Gram reaction, it raises the question: Was the observed growth a result of a sampling error, laboratory error, or a true product contamination? This scenario with a finished product is commonly referred to as an 'out of specification (OOS) test result.' Due to the unique nature of viable microorganisms, unlike chemical analytes, microbiologists also describe these aberrant results as a 'microbiological data deviation (MDD).' The response to this presumptive failure to meet USP <71> requirements is to investigate the source of the microbial contamination in order to confirm the validity of the test results. The purpose of the investigation is to confirm that the test results were obtained while utilizing good laboratory practices, including proper aseptic technique, appropriate equipment and reagents, and sterile assay media/components within an appropriate Grade A/ISO 5 environment.

Deviations in equipment, procedures, or personnel performance that require corrective action should be discovered during the investigation. If the positive sterility test is determined to be valid, an investigation will be conducted to determine the root cause of the manufacturing practice that caused the tested product lot to become contaminated. If no definitive root cause is determined, then the manufacturing corrective actions might be based on the probable causes identified and the measures defined to prevent recurrence of such an event. The decision on the disposition of the batch needs to consider that, as no analytical cause was identified, the batch might be nonsterile. If a laboratory investigation concludes that errors or events caused the test contamination to come from the laboratory environment or met other compendial invalidation criteria, the sterility test result would be invalidated, and the sterility test would be repeated with the same number of units and quantity of product.

USP <71> requires that the sterility test media be visually examined for evidence of microbial growth at intervals during the incubation and at its conclusion. This evidence may present itself as turbidity throughout the media, a pellicle growing on the surface of the soybean-casein digest medium (SCDM), growth at the interface between the aerobic and anaerobic portions of the fluid thioglycollate medium (FTM), as floccular growth within the media, or as precipitation at the bottom of the media. The mode of growth should be documented within the sterility test report.

Although not specifically recommended in USP <71>, microscopic examination of suspected microbial growth and subculturing the contents of test media container/enclosed membrane canisters are common industry practice. The sampling of the media must be done aseptically to avoid additional microbial contamination. Incubation of the original container must complete the full 14-day period described in USP <71> in order to ensure discovery of slower growing microorganisms. Aliquot removal from the canisters (or sterility test vessel used for direct inoculation) can be challenging when using a sterile Pasteur pipette (or sterile syringe) as it is difficult to assure the same subsequent culture growth conditions that may be needed in the event of an investigation. For example, the proximity of the growth within the media may be diagnostic as to the type of microorganism(s) that had been recovered. Secondly, it is not advisable to disperse the macroscopic growth within the media, especially for FTM because the aeration of the media may be fatal for the presence of strict anaerobes.

Subculturing to isolate the suspected microbial contaminant may include:

  • Transferring up to 10 mL of the test media into fresh SCDM and FTM and incubating the media for at least 14 days at 20–25 °C and 30–35 °C, respectively. The subculture time of incubation should mimic the time period that was necessary for the original time of incubation in order to optimize microbial recovery required by the targeted species (for example, some environmental fungi require the lower temperature range and are inhibited by the higher temperature range).
  • Streaking out from the test media onto soybean-casein digest agar (SCDA) and Sabouraud dextrose agar (SDA) and incubating aerobically at 30–35 °C for SCDA and 20–25 °C for SDA, until microbial growth is obtained and/or,
  • Alternative enrichment medium (i.e., Columbia blood agar) may also be used for subculturing from the primary SCDM and FTM to support slow growing fastidious microorganism.

The medium and mode of growth within the medium will provide insight as to whether the microorganism is a strict anaerobe, facultative anaerobe, or strict aerobe and what subculturing method would be most appropriate. Any isolates should be identified at least to species and, if available, strain-typed to aid the follow-up investigations with isolates recovered from the manufacturing or material bioburden. If subculturing fails to recover the microorganism, then a direct Gram stain on an aliquot of the suspect microbial growth should be performed. If no microbial presence is detected with the direct Gram stain, the turbidity may be a result of product-media precipitation or an artifact of the media after prolonged incubation (for example, the low percent of agar in the FTM can precipitate out of solution and causes medium turbidity).

Careful review and documentation of the microbial appearance within the SCDM and the FTM test tubes or canisters before any inadvertent jostling or shaking. This is especially true when the product may potentially make the test media turbid or cause a precipitation (e.g., suspensions or liposome-based drug products). These observations should be recorded in the sterility test report or, if available, photomicrographs can be taken with a camera mounted on the microscope.

In the absence of the isolation of a microorganism on subculture of the test media and/or no microscopic evidence of the presence of microorganisms, the deviation sterility test failure is not confirmed and, therefore, the product passes the test.

USP <71>, which has been harmonized with the European Pharmacopoeia and Japanese Pharmacopoeia, is frequently used as the method of choice for finished product release and is cited in many regulatory applications. The section entitled "Observation and Interpretation of Results" describes the four acceptable conditions that may be used to invalidate the initial test result and allow for retesting:

  1. The data of the microbiological monitoring of the sterility testing facility show a fault (Table 4.2.1-1🔍)
  2. A review of the testing procedure used during the test in question reveals a fault (Table 4.2.1-2🔍)
  3. Microbial growth is found in the negative controls (Section 4.2.1.1)
  4. After determination of the identity of the microorganisms isolated from the test, the growth of this species (or these species) may be ascribed unequivocally to faults with respect to the material and/or the technique used in conducting the sterility test procedure (Section 4.2.1.2)

If evidence of microbial growth is found, the product to be examined does not comply with the test for sterility unless it can be clearly demonstrated that the test was invalid for causes unrelated to the product to be examined. If the test is declared to be invalid, it is repeated with the same number of units as in the original test. If no evidence of microbial growth is found in the repeat test, the product examined complies with the test for sterility. If microbial growth is found in the repeat test, the product examined does not comply with the test for sterility.

核心概念：四項 Invalidation 條件

USP <71> 定義了四個可以使無菌試驗「作廢」並重測的條件：

  1. 環境監控（EM）數據顯示實驗室設施有缺陷（Table 4.2.1-1🔍）
  2. 操作程序審查發現缺失（Table 4.2.1-2🔍）
  3. 陰性對照出現微生物生長（Section 4.2.1.1）
  4. 鑑定結果顯示污染明確來自實驗室環境或操作技術（Section 4.2.1.2）

這四個條件不是「任選一個就可以重測」，而是每一個都需要有充分的客觀證據支持。

重要釐清：Invalid ≠ Pass

這是最常見的認知誤區。作廢（invalidation）的意思是：

  • 原始試驗結果因確認有實驗室錯誤，不能代表產品的真實無菌狀態
  • 允許以相同單數和數量進行重測（repeat test）
  • 重測結果才是正式用於批次放行的依據

不代表產品已通過無菌試驗。如果重測仍出現微生物生長，批次不符合無菌要求。

法規稽查員對這個邏輯非常敏感——如果廠商以「原始試驗已 invalidate」為由直接放行，而沒有完整的重測記錄，將面臨嚴重的觀察事項。

比喻：裁判宣判無效球

在足球比賽中，裁判宣判進球無效（例如越位），不代表比賽分數是 0-0 且勝負已定。它只代表那一球不算，比賽繼續。

無菌試驗 invalidation 同理：那一次試驗「不算」，但產品的無菌狀態仍未被確認。你必須重新踢這一球（重測），才能決定結果。

FTM 的特殊注意事項

流體硫乙醇酸鹽培養基（Fluid Thioglycollate Medium, FTM）用於厭氧菌培養，有幾個特殊處理要點：

  • 不能搖動或散開可見生長物，因為這可能會讓介質充氧，殺死嚴格厭氧菌（strict anaerobes）
  • FTM 中的低濃度寒天（agar）在長時間培育後可能沉澱，造成假性濁度——這是假陽性的常見來源
  • 生長位置本身就有診斷意義：液面附近 = 嗜氧菌；底部 = 厭氧菌；均勻濁度 = 兼性厭氧菌

4.2.1.1 Microbial Growth Found in Negative Controls

Original Text (English)

If the appearance of microbial growth is found in the negative or manipulation control (i.e., system control), they should be carefully reviewed for the presence of contamination. Not all microbial contamination causes uniform media turbidity (e.g., surface film layer on top of the media for strict aerobes or small budding fungal growth attached to membrane filter or walls of glass containers). Any purchased media, diluent, and test equipment should be checked for manufacturing recalls. Alternatively, in-house-prepared media records should be reviewed for contamination trends.

中文解說與思考引導

核心概念：陰性對照的意義

陰性對照（negative control），在無菌試驗中也稱為操作對照（manipulation control）或系統對照（system control），是一個未加入測試樣品的培養基容器，與試驗樣品在相同環境條件下操作和培育。

其目的是：監測操作本身是否引入了污染。

如果陰性對照出現生長，說明環境或操作本身有問題，不論試驗樣品如何，整次試驗的結果都受到質疑。這直接滿足 USP <71> 第三項 invalidation 條件。

比喻：實驗室的「空白試卷」

想像一場考試，監考官把空白試卷也提交上去，結果空白試卷上出現了答案——這說明考卷印刷本身就有問題，而不是某個學生作弊。

陰性對照就是那張空白試卷。它「不應該」有任何生長。如果有，問題在系統本身。

重點：污染的外觀不一定是均勻濁度

原文特別提醒：並非所有污染都會造成均勻的培養基混濁。實際上可能出現：

  • 液面薄膜（surface film）：嚴格需氧菌在液面形成薄膜，看起來像油漬，不是濁度
  • 膜濾器或玻璃壁上的芽生真菌（budding fungal growth）：可能是微小的白點，容易被忽視
  • 底部沉澱：需與 FTM 寒天沉澱的假陽性區分

因此，陰性對照的審查需要在良好光線下仔細觀察，而非只靠目測整體混濁度。

中文解說與思考引導

核心概念：由「種相同」到「株相同」的鑑定升級

這一節描述了最嚴格的 invalidation 條件的操作邏輯：

  2. 菌株分型確認：若種名相同，進行菌株分型（strain typing），確認是否為同一株菌

  • 所有 QC 挑戰菌的基因型紀錄

Potential corrective action steps that could be implemented or strengthened for remediation of a problem associated within each of the four conditions outlined in USP <71> include:

1. Data of the microbiological monitoring of the sterility testing facility show a fault

  • Establish a documented QA/QC program to ensure the sterility and growth promotion capabilities of media, diluents, materials, and equipment used
  • Employ a sporicidal agent for the sanitization/disinfection of the area concerned, and continue to use the sporicidal agent periodically
  • Retrain personnel in the GMP of aseptic technique and the performance of the operations
  • Establish or strengthen a scheduled preventive maintenance and certification program for cleanroom equipment, and increase the microbial monitoring of the personnel and critical areas to assure the environmental controls
  • Validate and monitor (using data from each sterilization cycle) the autoclave processes used to sterilize cleanroom equipment

2. A review of the testing procedure used during the test in question reveals a fault

  • Establish appropriate SOPs for media preparation, storage, inventory control, and destruction of expired media
  • Employ a requirement for two operators to conduct sterility testing to minimize fatigue while optimizing aseptic handling and for action verification
  • Establish a separate designated area for all subculturing activity, and prepare an SOP for cleaning and monitoring this laboratory area

3. Microbial growth is found in the negative controls

  • When a negative control is found positive, require a second person, e.g., peer or laboratory manager, to observe the contamination
  • Check the container-closure integrity of the test container as it may have been damaged during manufacturing, storage, or transportation to the microbiology laboratory
  • Review component sterilization, material transfer, environmental controls, and aseptic technique used during testing.

4. After determination of the identity of the microorganisms isolated from the test, the growth of this species (or these species) may be ascribed unequivocally to faults with respect to the material and/or the technique used in conducting the sterility test procedure.

核心概念：CAPA 必須與 Invalidation 條件對應

TR88 的架構非常清晰：四個 invalidation 條件，各自對應一組 CAPA。這不是偶然——法規稽查員在審查 CAPA 時，會確認：

  • CAPA 是否對應到確認的根本原因？
  • CAPA 是否具體可執行，還是泛泛而談？
  • CAPA 的有效性如何驗證？

一個好的 CAPA 必須做到「case-specific（案例具體）」——原文特別強調這個詞。

比喻：對症下藥

如果你發現發燒原因是細菌感染，CAPA 是使用抗生素。如果你的 CAPA 是「叫病人多喝水」，那只是對症，不是對因。

同樣地，如果 invalidation 條件 1（設施缺陷）是因為 HEPA 過濾器逾期認證，那 CAPA 必須包含「建立 HEPA 認證排程管理系統」，而不只是「加強清潔」。

條件 2 的重要 CAPA：雙人操作制度

TR88 建議在無菌試驗中採用雙人操作（two-operator）要求。這個做法的理由：

  • 減少疲勞：無菌試驗操作時間長，單人容易疲勞導致操作疏失
  • 行動確認（action verification）：每個關鍵步驟都有第二人確認，降低漏步的風險
  • 即時記錄：一人操作、一人記錄，提高原始數據的即時性與完整性

在人力有限的 CDMO 環境中，如何落實這個要求需要在排班和 SOPs 中明確規定。

CDMO 應用：Sporicide 週期性使用的策略

條件 1 的 CAPA 特別提到孢子殺滅劑（sporicidal agent）的週期性使用。在 CDMO 無菌測試設施中：

  • VHP（氣態過氧化氫）是隔離器最常見的孢子殺滅方式，需定期執行
  • 對於層流罩（LAF），孢子殺滅劑的使用需與日常清潔劑的輪替計畫整合
  • 孢子殺滅劑的驗證（validation）必須定期重確認，特別是在設施改變後

發生 invalidation 後導入孢子殺滅劑，必須有記錄顯示這是在 CAPA 框架下的決定，而非例行操作的更改。

條件 4 的 CAPA：鑑定技術一致性的法規要求

這個 CAPA 看起來簡單，但有深層含義：

如果你用 MALDI-TOF 鑑定試驗分離株，你也必須用 MALDI-TOF（或等效技術）重新確認 EM 歷史分離株——不能拿 Vitek 2 的結果來比對。

這個要求的背後邏輯：只有在「等效技術、等效資料庫」下，兩個鑑定結果的比較才有科學意義。如果技術不一致，即使結果相同，法規審查員也可能質疑比對的有效性。

思考練習：你的 CAPA 系統夠「具體」嗎？

以下是一個自我檢查的框架：

  • 你的 CAPA 能回答「誰、做什麼、在哪裡、什麼時候」嗎？
  • 有沒有可測量的有效性確認指標（effectiveness check）？例如：「在接下來三個月的 EM 中，目標菌株不再出現」
  • 當類似的 invalidation 在同一設施第二次發生時，你能說明第一次 CAPA 為何未能防止再次發生嗎？

4.2.2 Bacterial Endotoxins Testing (BET) Investigations — 細菌內毒素試驗調查

4.2.2 Bacterial Endotoxins Testing Investigations

Compendial bacterial endotoxins testing (BET) detect and/or quantify lipopolysaccharide cell membrane components derived from the outer-cell membrane of Gram-negative bacteria that are most commonly responsible for human pyrogenic responses. This method is more comparable to chemical assay than to the traditional methods used for the detection and enumeration of microorganisms in pharmaceuticals, biopharmaceuticals, and medical devices. For this reason, the enzyme cascade-based Limulus amoebocyte lysate (LAL) BET has unique analytical requirements that may affect the reliability of the test results. USP <85> Bacterial Endotoxin Tests describes the procedures for performing both the gel clot and spectrophotometric methods for detecting and quantifying bacterial endotoxins. Because the methods are based on enzymatic components and principles, incubation conditions (e.g., temperature and time) are important, and laboratory analysts should have a technical understanding of the potential inhibition and enhancement of the product matrix on the product/lysate mixture. Another critical factor is the measurement and, if necessary, the adjustment of the product–lysate mixture to fall within the required pH range as stated in the lysate instructions for use. If the pH is not within the specified range, the endotoxin test results can be significantly altered from the true results (usually lower). Therefore, it is important that analysts understand the details of the compendial test and are trained to recognize how changes in the procedures may cause erroneous results is imperative (14). Section 4.2.2 describes general approaches for investigating BET deviations and provides guidance and references that contain detailed protocols on how to conduct investigations.

A deviation may be due to one of the following from a partial list of reasons:

  • Error during product dilution (excessive volume transfer during serial dilution preparation), incorrect volume (too little or too much) during reagent rehydration, misplacement of handled test tubes causing a control standard endotoxin (CSE) serial dilution for positive control preparation and inhibition and enhancement results to be incorrect, mislabeling of test tubes, or a test tube mix-up between product and CSE serial dilutions
  • CSE cross-contamination caused by pipette tips (or pipets) used repeatedly
  • Automated pipettors or bulbs contaminated with aspirated endotoxin solution because of improper use or not being cleaned routinely
  • Working on a lab bench with a nearby splash sink that creates water aerosols (tap water commonly contaminated with Gram-negative bacteria)
  • Analyst not performing the test according to the prescribed method
  • Reagent(s) or equipment not adequately depyrogenated or not handled aseptically with pyrogen-free water
  • Unexpected occurrence during the analysis that interrupted the completion of the gel clot (e.g., table vibration from draw closure or fan vibration in air-circulating stand-up incubators)
  • Glucans, at high enough concentrations, present in the test material may cause false positives
  • Contaminated water baths used for incubation of gel clot test tubes

Section 4.2.2.1 compiles a number of factors important to BET that, if not performed correctly, may culminate in a faulty analysis.

核心概念：BET 是化學分析，不是微生物生長試驗

BET 的本質是偵測內毒素這種化學物質（脂多醣，LPS），而非偵測活菌。LAL 反應是一套酵素串聯反應（enzyme cascade）：內毒素活化 Factor C，後者活化 Factor B，最終裂解凝固蛋白（coagulogen）形成凝膠或改變光吸收值。

這意味著試驗的失效模式與培養法完全不同——不是「菌沒長出來」的問題，而是「酵素反應被干擾了」的問題。

  • pH 敏感性：LAL 反應要求 pH 6.0–8.0；偏酸偏鹼均會使結果偏低（假陰性）
  • 抑制效應：產品基質中的蛋白質、界面活性劑、螯合劑（EDTA）可能抑制 LAL 酵素
  • 增強效應：某些成分反而放大訊號，造成假陽性
  • Glucan 干擾：葡聚醣（真菌細胞壁成分）能活化 Factor G 通路，造成假陽性

生活比喻：妊娠試劑 vs. 血液培養

把 BET 想像成妊娠試劑：它偵測的是一個特定化學物質（hCG / 內毒素）的存在，而不是培養「有沒有細菌長出來」。妊娠試劑也怕 pH 偏差、也怕試劑過期——道理完全相同。

這就是為什麼 BET OOS 的調查方向，第一步是「試劑與操作條件是否正確」，而不是「無菌作業有沒有破壞」。

重點提示：LAL 試驗最常見的假失效原因

  • 系列稀釋操作錯誤（移量誤差、試管對換）
  • 吸管頭重複使用造成 CSE 交叉污染
  • 附近水槽噴濺——自來水本身含有革蘭氏陰性菌
  • pH 沒有確認或調整
  • 培養期間震動或干擾（凝膠法）

4.2.2.1 Compendial Bacterial Endotoxins Test — 法定藥典內毒素試驗要求

4.2.2.1 Compendial Bacterial Endotoxins Test

When faced with an endotoxin LAL OOS result it would be best to start with a review of the mandatory requirements and controls in the harmonized compendial chapter, USP <85>. As with any testing used to release products, performing these key analytical steps and documenting them accurately and contemporaneously as required by company SOPs and the lysate manufacturer's instructions for use is essential. These records will prove critical in the event the laboratory management or auditors review or question the validity of the results. The harmonized compendial chapters include specific considerations for addressing BET analytical anomalies:

  • In the section for INTERPRETATION of the harmonized gel-clot method, when a positive result is found for only one duplicate of Solution A (product only), the test should be repeated. In the repeat test, the preparation under test complies with the test if a negative result is found for both replicates of Solution A. The preparation does not comply with the test if a positive result is found for one or both replicates of Solution A. However, if the preparation does not comply with the test at a dilution less than the maximum valid dilution (MVD), the test may be repeated using a greater product dilution, not exceeding the MVD.
  • Along with each gel-clot Limit and Quantitative Test, a 2λ positive product control, a 2λ positive control in BET water, and a negative control with just BET water should be included.
  • For the Photometric Quantitative Techniques, the absolute value of the correlation coefficient, r, must be 0.980 for the range of endotoxin concentrations used.
  • For the Photometric Quantitative Techniques, to be considered free of factors that interfere with the assay under the conditions of the test, the measured concentration of the endotoxin added to the sample solution must be within 50%–200% of the known added endotoxin concentration after subtraction of any endotoxin detected in the solution without added endotoxin. Note: Knowing how to interpret the value of the positive product control (PPC) is very important when reading either under or over the allowed range. Even an assay within the allowable PPC range may still give false test results, which may be caused by the natural endotoxin in the test sample and the spiked CSE combining to give an acceptable value for this control.
  • Additional suggestions on how to investigate bacterial endotoxin deviation can be found in USP <1085> Guidelines on the Endotoxins Test, in the section entitled “Out-of-Specification Results and Retesting Considerations” (21).
  • For those laboratories responsible for performing BET on sterile implantable devices or combination products (one that includes a medical device), specific guidance is provided in USP <161> Medical Devices–Bacterial Endotoxin and Pyrogen Tests (22). For medical device testing, careful attention to the compendial requirements should be given to performing the bacterial endotoxin extraction step from the device products.

4.2.2.2 FDA Guidance — FDA 指引補充

4.2.2.2 FDA Guidance

When performing a deviation investigation for results associated with BET, the 2012 FDA Guidance for Industry: Pyrogen and Endotoxin Testing: Questions and Answers may offer valuable insights as to some regulatory expectations (23). In general, the guidance suggests that the sampling plan used by firms should consider the potential presence of bacterial endotoxin contamination in raw materials, in-process materials, and the finished product. Specifically, they should consider aspects of the manufacturing design, including consistency of a manufacturing process, impact of in-process hold-times, endotoxin-removal steps, and finished-product endotoxin specifications. Sections of this FDA guidance document may be particularly helpful during deviation investigations, for example:

When is retesting appropriate? When conflicting results occur within a test run, firms should consult USP Chapter <85>, Gel Clot Limits Test, Interpretation, for guidance on repeat testing. As specified in Chapter <85>, if the test failure occurred at less than the maximum valid dilution (MVD), the test should be repeated using a greater (product) dilution not exceeding the MVD… All testing procedures, including those for retesting within the above limits, should be specified in advance in the standard operating procedures approved by the firm’s quality control unit.

Because of the unique nature of endotoxins to clump or bind to container surfaces, the issue of sample storage and handling becomes important. The FDA guidance recommends firms “establish procedures for storing and handling (which includes product-mixing) samples for bacterial endotoxin analysis…”

FDA recommends that pooled samples be a composite of aseptically removed aliquots (after at least 30 seconds of vigorous mixing) from each of the product containers (24). In this way, the original, individual containers will be available for possible retesting in the event the pooled sample displays an OOS result.

…For purposes of evaluating the relative risk of product contamination, quantitative testing may be preferable to limit testing to facilitate product quality trending and to identify and correct excursions before they exceed the specification and cause product failure.

Therefore, when an endotoxin is detected, quantitating the actual amount present is preferred to depending on the guesswork of using a limit test approach.

Two other documents that may be helpful during the investigation of either a laboratory deviation or the manufacturing areas are: FDA's Inspection Technical Guides: No. 40 Bacterial Endotoxins/Pyrogens and standards document ANSI/AAMI ST72:2019 Bacterial Endotoxins—Test Methodologies, Routine Monitoring, and Alternatives to Batch Testing (25, 26). The AAMI standard is acknowledged as an acceptable standard for performing BET and has been referenced in the 2012 FDA Guidance document excerpted above. Both of these documents may be used by FDA and other international government investigators during their inspectional preparation of both parenteral and medical device manufacturers.

Limit test（限度試驗）只告訴你「超過/未超過規格」，就像尿糖試紙只有陰陽兩色。Quantitative test（定量試驗）則給你實際數值，就像血糖機讓你知道是 6.5 mmol/L 還是 12 mmol/L。FDA 建議優先做定量試驗，因為趨勢管理需要數值，而不只是合格/不合格的判斷。

重點提示：重測的前提條件

FDA 明確指出：重測程序必須在 SOP 中事先規定（pre-specified）。不能在 OOS 發生後才決定要不要重測、重測幾次。這是稽核時最容易被質疑的灰色地帶。

另外，保留原始個別容器而不混合為單一 pooled sample，是為了 OOS 時有「退路」可以個別重測。

實務行動

檢查你的 BET SOP：是否已明確規定 pooled sample 的振搖秒數（至少 30 秒）？重測的條件（何時允許 / 最多幾次）是否事先定義清楚？如果還沒有，這是下次 SOP 審查的優先改善項目。

4.2.3 Antimicrobial Effectiveness Testing (AET) Investigations — 抗菌效能試驗調查

4.2.3 Antimicrobial Effectiveness Testing Investigations

The test methods and acceptance criteria for different dosage forms are defined in USP <51> Antimicrobial Effectiveness Testing. In general, the test is only applicable to aqueous, multiple-use sterile and nonsterile drug products that may be subject to microbial contamination during use. USP <51> defines aqueous as a water activity (Aw) of more than 0.6. Products with Aw < 0.6 will not support the growth of microorganisms and may be demonstrably self-preserving. The test confirms the effectiveness of the antimicrobial preservative system and/or other supporting physicochemical attributes to meet product category criteria. Although a drug product would be required to meet the test criteria during development and to be demonstrated as effective in a long-term stability study, the test is a shelf-life specification, but not a release requirement. In contrast, the preservative concentration required within the formulation to meet the product category criteria at release and at expiry is a product specification included in the regulatory submission. Typically, USP <51> is looked upon as a stability-indicating pass/fail test, where the results in terms of log reduction of the challenge organisms at specified time intervals are evaluated against the log reduction requirement for the product category. Given the inherent variability of the test method, marginally preserved products have a potential to fail at one or more time intervals in long-term stability studies supporting marketed products (27).

4.2.4 Mycoplasma Testing Investigations

4.2.5 Biological Indicators (BI) Investigations — 生物指示劑調查

4.2.5 Biological Indicators Investigations

Biological indicators (BI) are used during the validation of moist heat, dry heat, and gaseous sterilization processes and during vapor-phase hydrogen peroxide decontamination cycles. BIs may also be used for monitoring routine cycles to support the release of products that are used by the pharmaceutical industry. Additionally, a BI can be used as a load monitor to support parametric release of moist-heat sterilized products in the U.S. Typically, they consist of high numbers of resistant bacterial spores with characterized calibrated D-values that are included in sterilization loads, retrieved, and cultured to demonstrate the lethality of their destruction during the sterilization cycle.

It is an expectation that a company will conduct confirmatory testing of the population and purity stated in the certificate of analysis of the incoming BI lot and growth of the cultured spore strip. Low counts may reflect the incomplete recovery from the matrix supporting the bacterial spores, whereas the growth of a heat-resistant spore-forming bacteria like Geobacillus stearothermophilus from a BI is most likely caused by contamination of the laminar flow hood and/or test supplies from positive controls in the testing environment (30).

Probable causes of failure would include gross mishandling, insufficient exposure to the sterilization parameters, and higher BI D-values that result in a lower probability of destruction in the sterilization cycle. Other points to consider include:

  • Were the BIs stored and handled prior to use, as recommended by the manufacturer?
  • Were the BIs incubated in accordance with regulatory expectations and manufacturer instructions?
  • Were the BIs used in the manner prescribed by the manufacturer?
  • Was the appropriate BI used for the product being tested (e.g., ampoule BI used for liquid products)?
  • In cases where the BI is directly inoculated into a solution or onto a substrate, what is the D-value as used in that form?

PDA Technical Report 51 Biological Indicators for Gas and Vapor-Phase Decontamination Processes: Specification, Manufacture, Control and Use provides further guidance on this topic (31).

4.2.6 Aseptic Process Simulation Failures (Media Fills) — 無菌製程模擬失敗調查

4.2.6 Aseptic Process Simulation Failures (Media Fills)

Proper design and execution of aseptic process simulations can be found in PDA Technical Report 22: Process Simulation for Aseptically Filled Products, FDA Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing—Current Good Manufacturing Practice, PIC/S Annex 1: Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products, Guide to GMP for Medicinal Products, and ISO 13408-5 (3, 5, 32, 33). Any media-filled containers that, upon inspection, score as “turbid” during the incubation period should be examined by a microbiologist and transferred to the microbiology laboratory for investigation. The growth of microorganisms in culture medium may include any slight haze, pellicle formation, floccular amassment, or precipitation. The first task, prior to opening the container to subculture the media, is to establish if the container is flawed in any manner that would compromise the sterility of the medium. Damage may take the form of inadequate crimps to a stoppered vial, leakage around the container closure, or chips or cracks in the container that could threaten integrity. Any damage should be adequately recorded and, if possible, supplemented with photographs. Involving packaging experts in the laboratory investigation who can evaluate the extent of damage may be helpful, for instance, in performing physicochemical integrity testing to confirm that the damage would result in microbial ingress. The media fill may be inspected for damaged/nonintegral units, and those units should be discarded. The rationale for excluding these units from the final run tally should be fully documented and the deviation should be explained in the media fill report. (3, 34).

In the absence of obvious damage, the contents of a turbid container would be aseptically sampled and subcultured with appropriate solid media and incubated at 20–25 °C and 30–35 °C for three to seven days. The contents of the media fill containers may be examined microscopically for the presence of microorganisms. Any isolates should be identified at least to species and, preferably, strain-typed to aid the follow-up investigations. Guidance may be found in USP <1113> (16).

4.2.8 Utilities Monitoring — 公用設施監控

4.2.8 Utilities Monitoring

4.2.8.1 Pharmaceutical-Grade Water Systems

The purpose of the laboratory investigation is to determine whether a definitive source of sampling and/or laboratory error contributed to the root cause of the deviation. This section addresses the initial and extended laboratory investigations associated with samples collected from pharmaceutical-grade water systems, such as purified water and water for injection (WFI).

Microbiological testing of pharmaceutical water systems, including the source water (i.e., potable water) and purification steps, typically consists of total viable microbial count, BET, and/or a test for the absence of coliforms, depending on the grade of water and any local regulatory requirements. After a pharmaceutical water system is successfully validated, both the system and the source are subject to periodic microbial monitoring using standard methods. Action limits and alert levels should be established to recognize adverse trends in the water system. These alert levels or action limits should be based on historical data, the intended purpose of the sampling plan, as well as the recommended compendial or regulatory limits. In general, specifications are not set for water systems.

In a well-designed, well-operated pharmaceutical water system, sanitized by maintaining a minimum turbulence level and elevated temperature (> 65 °C) or periodic heat or chemical sanitization, test results above the action limit should be very rare.

Several events can signal that a laboratory investigation should be conducted:

  • A single test result that exceeds an action limit
  • Multiple test results that exceed an alert level, thus indicating an adverse trend
  • Isolation of microorganisms of concern based on the dosage forms manufactured in the facility

The cross-functional team conducting the laboratory investigation should review the collection and microbiological testing of the subject water sample to determine any potential sampling and laboratory errors. The data collected should include the sampling location, sample containers, sampling technique, and any alert level or action limit excursions associated with the sampling location, loop, or water system, and the operators collecting the water samples.

If this investigation does not identify a definitive source of laboratory error to explain the deviation result, the laboratory should immediately notify the owner of the utility system, operational users, and the QCU.

4.2.8.1.1 Evaluation of Sample Handling

The methods used for preparing, storing, and attaching hoses and sampling apparatus to the sample ports are critical. Regulatory guidance recommends that each point of use should be flushed using the same flushing procedure used in the manufacturing process (35).

The potential for changes in the microbial population in samples should be limited by restricting the holding time before testing and controlling the storage temperature. If the samples cannot be tested within two hours, they should be transported and stored at refrigeration temperature for a maximum of 24 hours. In situations where even 24 hours is not possible (such as when using off-site contract laboratories), it is particularly important to qualify the microbiological sample hold times and storage conditions. (See APHA/AWWA/WEF Standard Methods, USP <1231>, EMA 496873, and Japan: G8 for guidance (28, 36-38). Prior to removing the test aliquot, all samples should be adequately mixed to ensure a homogeneous mixture is achieved. Any parameters for storage and transportation of water samples that are different from standard methods should be qualified to ensure the samples are not negatively affected, resulting in potentially invalid results.

4.2.8.1.2 Evaluation of Test Methods

Review of all materials associated with the testing and their appropriateness for the grade of water tested is required in a laboratory investigation. Sterility of the membrane filters, sample containers, pipettes, and utensils used to perform testing must be suitable for their intended use and must conform within sterilization dating. Also, all diluents and reagents used must be suitable (such as sterile and/or endotoxin-free) and within their expiry dating.

Any deviation from these requirements must be evaluated and investigated. Confluent microbial growth, in contrast to discrete colonies on the membranes or pour plates, may be indicative of equipment contamination. The position of the analyst’s head, hands, and arms in the path of the airflow (preferably in a laminar flow hood with horizontal air flow) over the test materials may also be a source of microbial contamination (personnel-related considerations are discussed further in Section 5.2.1).

The identity of the microorganism(s) associated with the investigation may also reveal information about the source of contamination. The most common water isolates are Gram-negative, nonfermenting, rod-shaped bacteria historically described as pseudomonads, which may be indicators for a biofilm formation within the water system. The recovery of organisms that do not thrive or survive under the low ionic strength/low carbon conditions found in a pharmaceutical water system may suggest contamination of the sample or insufficient control or flushing of the sampling apparatus and/or hoses attached at the port. Gram-positive cocci commonly found on the human skin, such as Staphylococcus epidermidis, can be indicative of potential sampling contamination, while Gram-positive spore-formers, such as Bacillus species or the fungus Cladosporium species, can be indicative of potential environmental contamination.

4.2.8.1.3 Evaluation of Trending Data

Reviewing the historical test data will help determine if an adverse trend is associated with individual points-of-use, loops, and water systems. This information will be also useful for the manufacturing investigation.

4.3 Phase I: Conclusion and Next Steps — 第一階段結論與後續步驟

4.3 Phase I: Conclusion and Next Steps

Evaluation and questions related to the Phase I deviation investigation must use strong, evidence-based data. The investigation may not be definitive but may suggest probable cause(s). The Phase I investigation commonly concludes with one of three possible outcomes:

  1. Events uncovered during the investigation may be responsible for the deviation and present a clear root cause that is directly responsible for the deviation. Examples could show contamination due to failure to follow SOPs, use of poor aseptic techniques during either sampling or testing, a recognized laboratory error, or the use of nonsterile testing materials. There may be a direct link between isolate identification and the same contamination from the testing environment. In these cases, the test may be considered invalid.

    In the case of invalid tests, the Phase I investigation would conclude without advancing to the Phase II manufacturing investigation, or where Phase I and Phase II investigations were undertaken concurrently, the cancellation of the Phase II investigation would be warranted. Depending on the sample type and source of contamination, resampling or retesting may also be considered as long as the QCU agrees. CAPAs must be implemented (preferably prior to any retesting or resampling), however, in order to remediate the original cause of the invalidation.

  2. While the investigation might have revealed issues, there may be insufficient evidence to directly correlate the investigative findings to the deviation. For example, if the EM conducted during the testing showed microorganisms present but the organisms were a different genus and species than the contamination within the sample, the environmental results may cast doubt on the sample, but they would not invalidate the original result. Thus, the Phase I laboratory investigation would be found inconclusive, not providing sufficient evidence to invalidate the result, and the result is deemed to be valid. The investigation would then proceed to a Phase II manufacturing investigation.

  3. The results of the investigation did not show any evidence of testing failure. In other words, it can be clearly demonstrated that all procedures were followed, analysts were properly qualified to perform the testing, and all materials, equipment, and testing environment were considered appropriate for the test. The calculations for reporting data and all final results were correct and verified. Therefore, the deviation is confirmed and deemed valid, so the investigation must proceed to Phase II in order to determine the source of contamination from the processes used during manufacturing.

5.0 Phase II: Manufacturing Investigation — 製造調查概覽

5.0 Phase II: Manufacturing Investigation

Once laboratory error has been eliminated or found inconclusive through the Phase I investigative process, the investigation focuses on the manufacturing facility, personnel, processes, and materials. A manufacturing investigation includes, but is not limited to, several principal elements:

  • The cross-functional investigation team must represent all departments responsible for handling the deviated sample whether it is a pharmaceutical excipient, in-process intermediate, drug substance, or drug product. This component is essential to conducting an effective investigation. The QCU or another suitable individual may lead the team, which should include representatives from manufacturing, plant engineering, process validation, maintenance, and quality control. In a microbiological-related investigation, the contribution of a microbiologist on the team will be critical.
  • The QCU should have overall responsibility for managing or approving the outcome of the manufacturing investigation and for notifying upper management of the investigation findings and batch disposition.
  • Each investigation should be thoroughly documented and include a time for completion, as specified by company policies and procedures. Microbiological-related investigations are complex and may not be able to be completed within the specified time period; in these cases, an SOP-governed mechanism should be in place for extending the completion date. This extension must be justified and supported by the QCU.

While additional assessments can be performed if required by company policy or procedure, every manufacturing investigation should include the following actions:

  • Determine all batches in scope. Does the initial scope of the investigation need to be expanded based on laboratory findings? Batches under investigation should not be released.
  • Determine the definitive or probable cause (root cause analysis). The investigation must be comprehensive, especially if the probable cause may not be identified. When conducting the root cause analysis, all aspects must be considered, and a thoroughly objective approach must be taken to evaluate the cause of the deviation. Identifying the probable cause is critical. It determines the type and degree of CAPAs implemented, which affects the likelihood of the deviation recurring.
  • Determine if this is a recurrence of a previous deviation or a first-time incident. Is there a pattern of microbial contamination? Were previous CAPAs effective?
  • Determine the potential impact. What impact might this incident have on other batches manufactured in the facility? This impact assessment may include pharmaceutical ingredients, manufacturing equipment, or other processing lines in the same facility, other sites within the company that are manufacturing the same or related products, and distributed product.
  • Determine the level of risk. What risk might be posed to a person taking the product? What effect could there be on the stability, safety, and efficacy of the product over the shelf life of the product?
  • Identify CAPAs required and develop a plan for implementation. Create a list of all the possible solutions that would prevent the deviation from recurring and select the most effective changes for implementation.
  • Clearly identify the deviation. State what the deviation is and how it exceeded the limits. Be as specific, simple, and concise as possible.
  • Decide what, if any, manufacturing should cease during the investigation. Depending on the nature of the deviation (e.g., high microbial counts, damage to facility, microorganisms identified), the team should consider recommending that manufacturing in the affected area should cease until remediation (e.g., extra cleaning and sanitization, more environmental sampling) has been completed and before the investigation is closed. All actions taken to demonstrate containment of the deviation should be documented.
  • Maintain clear communications. Interview personnel involved and revise the investigation as needed. Provide routine updates to management and ensure that members of the team are well informed and kept up to date on the progress of the investigation.
  • Inspect the area where the deviation occurred. Locate the deviation on a plan of the facility and look at surrounding areas that might contribute to the microbial contamination. Consider personnel and material flows, and nonroutine events, as well as temperature, humidity, and space pressurization excursions.
  • Look for trends. Review prior related investigations to assess. Is there an adverse trend—same location, common personnel involvement, recurring microorganisms, root cause? If an adverse trend is identified, act promptly to remediate the problem.
  • Evaluate data. A thorough review of production or batch records should be conducted to determine if falsification or incorrect recording of data from either intentional or unintentional human error during the time of recording results occurred.

While SOPs are crucial to effectively manage an investigation, other tools can provide valuable assistance in identifying possible causes of the deviation, such as brainstorming, fishbone diagram, FMEA/HACCP, cause-and-effect analysis, and the 5-why technique.

5.1 Root Cause Analysis — 根本原因分析

5.1 Root Cause Analysis

When writing a manufacturing investigation, use of root cause analysis tools are recommended. One common approach is the use of a fishbone diagram (Figure 5.1-1); it can be used to ensure that all inputs that may have contributed to the deviation are adequately considered and assessed as a potential root cause.

Thinking through the entire scope of factors that may have influenced the deviation is very important, as is recording each potential root cause identified in the investigation, even if it is determined not likely to have been a contributing factor. For example, if pharmaceutical ingredients and packaging components were assessed and were determined to not have caused the deviation, the investigation should reflect that information. This demonstrates that the investigator performed due diligence in examining the potential impact of these materials as a source of contamination and subsequently ruled them out as a potential root cause.

The categories of contributing factors to be considered in a root cause analysis may vary based on the process involved. They can also be modified depending on the situation, for example, the dosage form, facility design, or nature of the deviation itself, location within the facility, sample type, classified area level, or involvement of personnel. The investigating team is responsible for ensuring that all contributing factors have been assessed so that no potential root cause of the deviation is overlooked.

四種調查結論等級

TR88 明確區分四個等級，這在監管溝通中非常重要：

  • Root Cause（根本原因）：高確定性，有直接證據支持。是最理想的結論，但在微生物偏差中不易達到。
  • Most Probable Cause（最可能原因）：最可能的原因，但無法 100% 確認。這是監管機構在實務上最常接受的結論等級。
  • Possible Cause（可能原因）：有可能，但證據不足以確認，需要更多資料。
  • Unknown Cause（未知原因）：資料不足以得出任何結論。這是最難對監管機構交代的結論，必須伴隨強而有力的 CAPA。

比喻：「最可能原因」是法律上的合理懷疑標準

「Most Probable Cause」就像刑事訴訟中的「合理懷疑排除」（beyond reasonable doubt）——不需要百分之百確定，但你的論據必須足夠有說服力，讓審查員認為這個解釋是合理的。關鍵在於：你的 CAPA 必須直接針對這個最可能原因，而不是泛泛而論的「加強培訓」。

重點：病患風險評估的層次

Impact Assessment 要求從多個維度評估病患風險：

  • 給藥途徑：注射劑（靜脈、肌肉）風險遠高於口服或外用。
  • 目標族群：免疫功能受損（immunocompromised）、兒科、術後患者屬高風險族群。
  • 微生物毒力：即使不直接致病，也可能產毒素、降解 API、改變黏度或變色。
  • 抗藥性：如果產品本身含抗生素，分離到的微生物是否有抗藥性？這直接影響療效評估。

對 CDMO 而言，最終風險評估通常需要客戶（MAH）參與，因為他們掌握目標族群的臨床資料。

CAPA 的三個核心要素

一個有效的 CAPA 計畫必須具備：

  • 針對性（Targeted）：CAPA 必須直接消除已確認的根本原因或最可能原因，而非泛泛的「加強員工意識」。
  • 可追蹤（Trackable）：每個行動項目必須有明確的到期日、責任人，並在電子化偏差管理系統中追蹤。
  • 有效性可驗證（Verifiable）：CAPA 關閉後，必須有一個時間點進行 Effectiveness Check，確認偏差未復發。

比喻：CAPA 就像外科手術後的追蹤門診

5.2.1 Sterility-Positive Investigations（無菌陽性調查）

5.2.1 Sterility-Positive Investigations

Should the laboratory confirm that a positive sterility result is valid, and no fault was attributed to the laboratory and actual testing of samples submitted for analysis, the lot of product or material should be considered nonsterile, and a comprehensive investigation into the source of contamination is required.

Commonly, this is a collaborative effort between the QCU, laboratory, and production along with any additional SMEs relevant to the manufacturing facilities and ancillary equipment. In order to guide this step of the investigation, both scientific and regulatory considerations are provided to incorporate into your investigative strategy.

All sterility test isolates should be identified to the species level, and the data should be reviewed for any potential linkages to organisms isolated from the production environments, personnel who participate in the manufacturing process directly or indirectly by handling of the sampling containers throughout the process, and any in-process bioburden testing. If the same species of microorganism was recovered from EM or supporting activities during batch manufacturing or testing, these colonies should be identified to the strain-type level. Using the same microbial identification platform for both the sterility test isolate(s) and any isolates recovered from the production areas is advisable to eliminate potential differences in software databases if using two different microbial identification platforms.

Useful regulatory guidance for conducting a sterility test failure investigation for an aseptically filled product are the 2004 FDA guidance on sterile drug products and some insightful chapters in the PDA book Microbial Risk and Investigations. Experience has shown that the investigational approach for aseptically filled sterile products will be significantly more complex than investigating a sterility test failure associated with terminal modalities.

解說：5.2.2 細菌內毒素來源調查

核心概念：內毒素 = 革蘭氏陰性菌的「殘骸」

細菌內毒素（Bacterial Endotoxin）是革蘭氏陰性菌細胞壁上的脂多醣（LPS）。細菌死亡後 LPS 仍然留存，且極耐高溫——一般滅菌條件無法去除，必須靠 250°C 以上的乾熱滅菌（去熱原處理）才能降解。

因此調查方向與無菌陽性不同：不是找「活菌從哪裡來」，而是找「革蘭氏陰性菌在哪個環節增生並留下 LPS 污染」。

比喻：內毒素就像「犯罪現場的 DNA」

即使凶手（細菌）已經被殺死，現場仍留有 DNA（LPS）。你必須追溯：這些 DNA 是在哪個環節留下的？水系統？原料？設備清洗不夠徹底？還是純水被誤用取代 WFI？

關鍵查核點

  • 水系統是否有革蘭氏陰性菌歷史紀錄或超標事件
  • 天然來源的賦形劑（植物/動物來源）是否做過內毒素篩查
  • 去熱原烤箱/隧道的驗證狀態是否有效
  • 壓縮空氣管線是否含水（積水是革蘭氏陰性菌孳生溫床）
  • CMO 是否有製程變更通知
5.2.3 Pharmaceutical Ingredient-Related Investigations（藥品成分相關調查）

5.2.3 Pharmaceutical Ingredient-Related Investigations

Pharmaceutical ingredients contaminated by Gram-negative bacteria may result in endotoxin contamination. Mold contamination could potentially lead to mycotoxin contamination. GMP requirements, including those identified in 21 CFR 210, 21 CFR 211, and ICH Quality Guideline Q7, include specific definitions as well as requirements for procedures for sampling and testing.

Representative samples for microbiological testing of a pharmaceutical ingredient are taken from individual drums of the incoming shipment but the ingredient lots may not be well mixed. Creating composite samples from the top, middle, and bottom of individual drums, or taking samples from each individual drum may mask pockets of microbial contamination. Unless the warehouse sampling occurs in a sampling booth, under controlled conditions, using trained personnel with the appropriate PPE and sample containers, there is a potential for sample contamination.

With respect to the manufacturing investigation, a pharmaceutical company will probably not be able to gain full access to details of the ingredient supplier's manufacturing process or visit the manufacturing site to conduct an investigation, unless it is a requirement in the quality agreement. Many pharmaceutical excipients are viewed as commodities that are supplied to other industries without GMP compliance. Many excipient suppliers set their specifications based solely on the relevant USP monograph requirements, which may not include microbial requirements.

5.2.4 Evaluation of Confirmed Mycoplasma Contamination（黴漿菌污染調查）

5.2.4 Evaluation of Confirmed Mycoplasma Contamination

Investigation should be conducted in a timely manner as mycoplasma contamination can interrupt production and prove costly to the firm.

Common sources of mycoplasma contamination are master and secondary cell lines, raw materials, and growth medium that has not been rendered sterile using a 0.1 µm filtration step. Attention should be given to investigating these materials. The authentication, acquisition, banking, passage, and cultivation history of the master cell bank and working cell lines should be reviewed, with particular emphasis on certificates of analysis, certificates of origin, and the reported mycoplasma test results. Testing additional samples from the working cell bank may be advisable. Human contact or contaminated cell culture media and reagents may contaminate cell banks, and a new working cell bank would need to be established in order to continue production.

Cell culture media is usually made mycoplasma-free by heat treatment, irradiation, antibiotic use, or 0.1 µm retention filtration. That these procedures are fully validated and documented should be verified. The media, equipment sterilization, and handling of inoculum production must be as well controlled as large-scale cell culture. The manufacturing batch records should be reviewed thoroughly, especially noting manufacturing deviations and staffing changes. If available, retained production materials could be tested using a rapid method to trace the potential source of the mycoplasma contamination.

When the mycoplasma are of human origin (Mycoplasma orale, M. fermentans, and M. hominis), human contamination may likely play a role in the failure. Additional consideration should be given to species of mycoplasma that are of animal or nonanimal origin (Acholeplasma laidlawii, M. hyorhinis, M. synoviae, and Spiroplasma) as they may be indicative of contamination of specific growth media or additive growth components.

黴漿菌（Mycoplasma）是沒有細胞壁的最小細菌，因此：

  • 標準 0.22 µm 過濾器無法截留——需要 0.1 µm
  • 在一般微生物培養基上不易生長，常規 EM 偵測不到
  • 感染細胞培養物後，細胞可能外觀正常但功能受損

這使它成為生物製藥（疫苗、抗體、細胞療法）生產中最難察覺也最具威脅的污染源。

比喻：黴漿菌就像「沒有護照的偷渡者」

一般的微生物篩查系統（護照查驗）抓不到它——它沒有細胞壁（護照），不會在常規培養基上顯形，也不會被 0.22 µm 濾膜攔截。必須用 PCR 或指定的黴漿菌測試才能找到它。

一旦確認，行動必須快：已受污染的細胞庫通常無法「清洗」，只能啟用備份細胞庫重新建立生產。

菌種與來源的對應關係

  • M. orale / M. fermentans / M. hominis：人類來源 → 懷疑人員操作污染
  • M. hyorhinis / M. synoviae：動物來源（豬/雞）→ 懷疑培養基成分（如胎牛血清）
  • Acholeplasma laidlawii：廣泛存在 → 培養基或添加物污染

菌種鑑定直接指引你去哪個環節追根溯源，節省大量調查時間。

5.2.5 Component (Container Closure) Investigations（容器密封完整性調查）

5.2.5 Component (Container Closure) Investigations

Some of the major causal factors of microbial contamination in primary packaging components that threaten the sterility assurance of sterile drug products include the container (e.g., vial, cartridge, flexible container, or syringe) and closure (e.g., syringe cap and plunger, stopper, seal).

Specific microbial tests have the potential to fail as a result of a defective container or closure system, such as the bioburden test, sterility test, process simulation media fill (turbid vial), container closure/seal integrity test, and BET. Test failures can occur during initial component validation or revalidation testing, component release testing, in-process monitoring, finished-product release testing, or stability testing.

In every case, a thorough review of the components, filling, and closure operations should be performed. Involving the supplier early in the investigation is the best plan, as they can help provide data from the manufacturer. A complete history will be needed that covers manufacture supply, storage, preparation, sterilization, use, and final finished presentation in order to successfully complete the investigation.

5.2.5.1 Pharmaceutical Containers

Vials, cartridges, and syringes used for sterile products are regarded as primary containers. Upon container receipt, a 100% inspection is not possible, both because of the destructive nature of functional testing, the size of the incoming shipments, and the general reluctance to return quality samples to inventory. Thus, acceptable quality-level attribute inspection plans are widely used for packaging components.

After the initial testing of packaging components for bioburden, endotoxin, and physical characteristics, a defect inspection and certificate of analysis review is performed. Dimensional tests are important because the necks of containers and closures form the barrier or seal that prevents microbiological and other contamination ingress. Containers that are out of round, have seam or form defects, or have cracks can present paths of ingress. Molded glass can be traced to a specific mold at the factory to identify recurring defects.

Shipping cartons that are received infested, wet, crushed, or torn should be rejected. Containers used during production of a product with a failed microbial test should be reviewed.

5.2.5.2 Pharmaceutical Closures

The most common closure for a sterile injectable product filled in a glass vial is an elastomeric stopper and crimped aluminum seal. Special consideration should be given to the semipermeable nature of elastomers. A review of the sterilization process validation also may be useful. Containers and closures can be purchased raw and unprocessed, ready-to-sterilize, and ready-to-use. These different preparations should be considered during an investigation.

5.2.5.3 Component Contamination

Post-manufacture microbiological and environmental contamination can include nonviable particles, microorganisms, and bacterial endotoxins. Viruses are generally not a concern with nonanimal raw material used to manufacture packaging components. In most cases, the high-heat extrusion process associated with glass and elastomer manufacture is microbiologically unfavorable; however, environmental controls in the manufacturing plant are often inadequate to maintain these conditions during sorting and packaging for shipment. Downstream processing is required and must include rigorous processing controls.

5.2.5.4 Container–Closure Integrity

Physical deformation, damage, and out-of-tolerance dimensional specifications of both containers and closures can result in sterile products becoming nonsterile through microbial ingress. Emphasis should be given to manufacturing set-up, in-process controls, and container–closure testing at release and in stability programs. Failure of the container–closure system and the inability to maintain microbial integrity should be fully investigated.

5.2.6 Environmental Monitoring（環境監測調查）

5.2.6 Environmental Monitoring

The pharmaceutical industry has many controlled and/or classified environments in which products are manufactured, and the key to the success of an EM investigation is understanding these requirements and the possible sources of contamination. Out-of-level monitoring results can come from the following types of environments:

  • Classified cleanrooms for sterile product manufacturing
  • Controlled or classified rooms for nonsterile product manufacturing
  • Controlled or classified rooms for low bioburden product manufacturing (e.g., biologics)
  • Laminar flow hoods, restrictive access barrier systems (RABS), and isolator systems used for product manufacturing or finished-product release testing

Measures for control can be implemented based on the following key concepts:

  • Minimize the entry of contaminants by incorporating good facility design, proper layout, efficient material and personnel flow, and sound supplier controls
  • Reduce contamination during the manufacturing process using well-developed process and equipment controls
  • Establish and maintain proper hygiene and PPE practices
  • Institute appropriate aseptic techniques and cleanroom behaviors
  • Assure a state of control through routine EM and analysis

5.2.6.1 Historical Data Evaluation

Reviewing historical data for adverse trends can be helpful to the investigation. Trending will consider sample type, organism type, test type, classified area, operational shift, or seasonal pattern. Trending can be both quantitative (i.e., count per unit surface area or air volume) or qualitative (i.e., frequency of exceeding an action limit), as illustrated by these specific examples:

  • Predefined number of consecutive alerts, actions, or contamination rates exceeded
  • Same microorganism or related microorganisms isolated across multiple locations, monitoring types, or rooms
  • Mold or Gram-negative or Gram-positive spore-forming organisms recovered in the critical locations associated with a specific manufacturing process

5.2.6.2 Manufacturing Process Evaluation

The purpose of an EM program is to determine the adequacy of the environmental controls and the aseptic technique of personnel in the manufacturing area. A well-maintained and well-operated facility requires good facility design, operation, and maintenance; functional HVAC systems; temperature and humidity control; supply of HEPA-filtered, laminar flow air; proper movement of materials, equipment, and personnel; and a sound facility cleaning and sanitization program.

Loss of environmental control in the cleanroom should be considered a potential source of microbial contamination. Excursions in temperature, differential pressure gradients, humidity, nonviable particulate levels, and appropriate cleaning and disinfection activities can contribute to microbial contamination in the cleanroom environment.

Microbial contamination can enter the manufacturing facility, including cleanrooms, in a variety of ways; in fact, the most common cause of fungal spore and bacterial endospore contamination in cleanrooms are items that enter the cleanroom. Cardboard and other cellulose-based materials known to shed particles should never enter GMP-compliant manufacturing areas. Items entering critical aseptic processing areas should first be double-wrapped, unwrapped, and wiped down with a sporicidal agent.

The age and maintenance level of facilities can affect contamination rates in the cleanroom. Ceiling tiles, HEPA-filter units, cleanroom flooring, materials of construction, drainage systems, and duct work are areas of concern in aging facilities that may need to be kept on a routine schedule for repairs. Drains in manufacturing facilities require routine disinfection with a sporicidal agent to prevent microbial proliferation.

5.2.6.3 Resampling or Investigational Monitoring

The need for additional monitoring to determine root cause should be considered early in the investigation to avoid losing time and to ensure that microbiological results will be representative of the conditions.

Based on the type of manufacturing facility, the frequency for routine environmental monitoring should be considered and a decision should be made as to whether resampling or investigational monitoring is warranted. Scenarios in which resampling may be useful include low frequency monitoring locations and locations found as important during facility walk-throughs. At the discretion of the QC microbiologist and in conjunction with approval from the QCU, additional locations may be sampled, or specialized microbiological media may be utilized. For example, recovery of mold from the wall, but not the floor, may suggest that sampling be conducted of nearby ceiling locations, adjacent walls, and additional portions of the same wall. 5.2.8 Utilities Monitoring（公用設施監測調查）

5.2.8 Utilities Monitoring

If the initial laboratory investigation (Section 4.0) does not find a conclusive source of a laboratory error that explains the deviation, deviations related to pharmaceutical water systems should be examined. Water is used in many ways in pharmaceutical manufacturing, and the required grade of water varies depending upon how it is used.

Pharmaceutical water systems consist of several components, including a water supply, a robust purification system that should consistently produce water of the intended quality under a range of conditions, and the storage and distribution systems that assure the water continues to meet all required quality attributes until it is delivered to the process.

The cross-functional team charged with investigating any deviation related to a water system should be familiar with the potential risks associated with its design, installation, operation, use, and maintenance. This team also needs to understand both the value and the limitations of data derived from system qualification and monitoring.

A monitoring plan should define the locations and frequency of sampling and the attributes for testing. The plan should encompass the entire system, from the water supply to the points of use, and relevant points in between on the generation, storage, and distribution systems. The sampling plan should reflect the results of intensive sampling during qualification, recent historical data, risk factors, and mitigations.

Routine monitoring of the water generation, storage, and distribution systems verifies that they continue to operate in a state of control, while monitoring at the points-of-use evaluates the quality of water delivered to the process.

Microbiological monitoring typically involves manual sample collection from a storage tank, a site on the loop, or at a point of use. Each step should be carefully designed to minimize potential errors and to ensure the reported result accurately reflects the condition of the material sampled.

The approach to investigating the root cause of a deviation on a pharmaceutical water system should be guided by the location and timing of the sample and by the test involved. If multiple deviations of the same type and level are observed, a cause that affects the entire system is more likely. If the deviation is not consistent with results from other contemporaneous samples, causes related to the sample point or to sampling or testing may be suspected.

In the case of a deviation for bioburden, the identity of the organism recovered is essential information. Recovery of organisms that thrive in water and form biofilms may suggest one set of causes, whereas recovery of organisms that are not compatible with the water system may suggest another set of causes, possibly related to environmental contamination.

Water Quality Standards:

  • Potable water: free of coliforms; ≤ 500 CFU/mL (per USP <1231>)
  • Purified water: ≤ 100 CFU/mL; meets conductivity and TOC limits
  • WFI: < 10 CFU/100 mL; bacterial endotoxin < 0.25 EU/mL

Potable water quality may be affected by seasonal variations due to runoff of melting snow or periods of low precipitation. Agricultural or industrial runoff may enter surface water sources or aquifers. An aging infrastructure may increase the chance of contamination entering the water supply via a damaged water main.

Risks related to the design and installation of the water system should be identified and remediated prior to qualification testing. Dead legs, as well as poor welds and other irregular surfaces, increase the risk of biofilm development. Recent design standards specify that pipes branching off the main recirculation loop should not exceed 1.5 times the pipe diameter, and the use of valves with no dead legs is now common.

Operational controls for storage and distribution systems typically include parameters such as temperature, flow rate, and pressure. The rate of water usage affects the stagnant time of water in the system and may be related to the root cause.

The investigation of a deviation may include a review of routine and corrective maintenance records. Invasive maintenance adds contamination risks, so measures to mitigate these risks and return the system to service should be prepared in advance. Measures should include periodic replacement of key parts, such as elastomers and vent filters on holding tanks.

Practices for operation of the sample or use port should be considered, especially if the deviation is not consistent with other results. Flushing prior to sampling (and use) is important to physically remove the opportunistic organisms found on hoses, sampling apparatus, or the sample port. Hoses should be removed when not in use and should be stored in a free-draining position.

5.2.8.1 Compressed Gases

Compressed gases used during the manufacturing and testing of pharmaceutical products are air, nitrogen, oxygen, and carbon dioxide. Inappropriate design and piping for distribution of these gaseous materials can introduce unwanted microorganisms. Pharmaceutical-grade compressed air is treated to remove moisture, and chemical residue like lubricants and point-of-use sterilizing-grade filters are employed.

The introduction of uncontrolled water moisture or inadequately filtered compressed gas, coupled with poorly designed pretreatment and sanitization processes of the gas delivery systems, can adversely affect the system and lead to product contamination.

Even though these utility systems have been qualified to perform as designed, the materials used during their construction may contain unexpected component defects (i.e., poor welding or incomplete passivation), as well as presenting potential ingress of grease and oils used for equipment lubrication. In addition, the metals and plastics may deteriorate over time, causing breaches or leaks in the plumbing materials or in components connected to the pipes (e.g., gauges, shut-off valves).

解說：5.2.8 公用設施調查

核心概念：水系統調查 = 沿著流程圖由上到下排查

製藥水系統的偏差調查需要系統性思維，因為任何一個環節（水源 → 預處理 → 製備 → 儲存 → 配送管路 → 使用點）都可能是根本原因。

關鍵判斷邏輯：

  • 多點同時超標 → 系統性問題（可能是整條管路生物膜或配備故障）
  • 單點超標，其他正常 → 採樣點問題、局部管路問題或操作失誤
  • 水中特有菌（水生菌） → 生物膜形成，Dead leg、低流速區
  • 非水中典型菌 → 外部環境污染進入

比喻：水系統就像城市自來水網——Dead leg 是最危險的死水區

想像一條城市供水管網，主管線持續循環流動（WFI 循環管路），但某些分支管（Dead leg）如果截止閥關閉，水就滯留在那裡變成死水。微生物在靜止水中迅速繁殖，形成生物膜——那層生物膜就像水管內壁的「永久污染源」，定期排放一點點菌到下游。

因此設計標準要求：分支管長度不超過主管直徑的 1.5 倍。這是最直接有效的結構性防範。

三種水的限度差異——調查前要確認使用的是哪種水

若調查發現用了純化水替代 WFI 沖洗設備，這本身就是一個應記錄的製程偏差。

5.2.8.1 壓縮氣體：看不見的污染媒介

壓縮空氣、氮氣、氧氣、CO₂ 都可能成為微生物污染的載體，尤其是當：

  • 系統含有未被去除的水分（condensate）
  • 過濾器未達滅菌級（sterilizing-grade, 0.22 µm）或已失效
  • 潤滑油脂或焊接缺陷造成管路內部污染
  • 管材老化導致滲漏

  3. 5.2.3 藥品成分：賦形劑不一定符合 GMP；複合取樣稀釋風險；CoA 年度驗證
  4. 5.2.4 黴漿菌：細胞庫、培養基、0.1 µm 過濾；菌種指向污染來源（人 vs. 動物）
  5. 5.2.5 容器密封：瓶、塞、蓋三者；CCI 在放行與穩定性中的角色；橡皮塞半透性
  6. 5.2.6 環境監測：歷史趨勢 → 製程評估 → 再採樣；紙箱、排水孔、老舊設施為慢性風險
  7. 5.2.7 培養基灌裝：一支濁瓶即需調查；菌種對應來源表；時間位置指引介入點
  8. 5.2.8 公用設施：水系統設計風險（Dead leg）；生物膜；壓縮氣體水分與過濾

CDMO 操作總結

Phase II 特定調查的核心邏輯是：菌種 = 線索，位置 = 佐證，趨勢 = 脈絡。一旦確認是有效偏差（非實驗室失誤），立即啟動跨職能調查組，依據偏差類型選擇對應的 5.2.x 框架。

對無菌製藥 CDMO 而言，以下四類偏差是最高警戒等級，需動員最完整的調查資源：

  • 無菌測試陽性（5.2.1）
  • 培養基灌裝陽性（5.2.7）
  • 黴漿菌確認（5.2.4）
  • WFI 連續超標（5.2.8）

調查不是為了關案——是為了確保下一批產品安全。根本原因不找到，CAPA 就沒有意義。

整體比喻：Phase II 是「系統性病因診斷」

Phase I 像是急診分診（排除假陽性、確認真實偏差）；Phase II 則是「全身系統性診斷」——從污染的「症狀」（菌種、位置、時間）推回「病因」（水系統、原料、設備、人員、包材）。這八個 5.2.x 小節，就是那份系統性問診清單。

6.0 Conclusions — 結論總覽

6.0 Conclusions

The investigation of microbial monitoring and testing deviations is a critical activity in the pharmaceutical industry that is not adequately covered by the FDA Guidance for Industry: Investigating Out of Specification (OOS) Test Results for Pharmaceutical Production (1). This technical report provides industry best practices related to microbial investigations to eliminate that gap in guidance.

These investigations are operationally divided into two phases—Phase I laboratory investigations and Phase II manufacturing investigations—with different teams responsible for each activity. Because of the importance of timeliness in microbial investigations, the manufacturing investigation may be initiated prior to completion of the laboratory investigation.

The objective of the laboratory investigation is to confirm that the sampling and testing was conducted correctly, and the reported result was valid. The investigational team is usually limited to the microbiologist conducting the test, the laboratory management, an SME with knowledge and experience with the test, and a representative of the QCU.

The major points to consider in a laboratory investigation are:

  • Integrity of the test sample used in the microbial test
  • Educational background, skill, and experience of the microbiologist conducting the test
  • Testing and environmental and personnel monitoring history associated with the microbiologist
  • Suitability of the equipment, supplies, media, diluents, and reagents used for the test
  • Environmental controls, including microbial testing facilities, laminar flow hoods, isolators, and PPE associated with the test
  • Reading of plates, scoring of microbiological broth, recording of data, accuracy of calculations, and review and reporting of the results
  • Microbial identification of the isolate implicated in the deviation

Once the validity of the microbial test is confirmed, a multifunctional team is assembled to conduct the manufacturing investigation related to the failure. Representatives of the microbiology laboratory, manufacturing, engineering, maintenance, and process validation may be members of the manufacturing investigation team usually led by the QCU. To increase the chances of a successful investigation, an experienced pharmaceutical microbiologist familiar with manufacturing operations is usually added as an SME.

The major points to consider in a manufacturing investigation are:

  • Unit operation and location within the facility directly related to the failure
  • Possible contribution of surrounding support areas
  • Possible contribution to the failure of deviations in environmental controls, i.e., space pressurization, airflow patterns, temperature, humidity, power outages, material flow, facility cleaning, and sanitization
  • Possible contribution of pharmaceutical ingredients, ingredient water, in-process materials, packaging components, and utilities
  • Deviation in critical manufacturing steps, as documented in the batch record
  • Recent change controls
  • Related in-process bioburden, environmental, and personnel monitoring deviations
  • Interviews with operators, laboratory management, and production managers as to the possible cause of the failure
  • Follow-up microbial monitoring and testing

The investigation reports must adequately document the team findings. If the root cause of the out-of-limit or out-of-specification microbial test result cannot be definitively established, the most probable cause or causes must be presented. Investigating out-of-trend results may help identify laboratory and/or manufacturing issues before they become out-of-specification results. To prevent the recurrence of these microbial failures, a CAPA plan must be implemented, with responsible individuals reporting their progress on the plan to the QCU and their management.

中文解說與 CDMO 實務視角

TR88 的核心貢獻：填補法規空白

FDA OOS 指引（Ref. 1）是所有藥廠偏差調查的起點，但它是為化學測試設計的。微生物測試的本質——活的有機體、環境污染、人員因素——讓它遠比化學 OOS 複雜。TR88 的存在，就是為了填補這個空白，提供微生物偏差調查的行業最佳實踐。

對 CDMO 而言，這份報告不只是參考文件，而是客戶稽核時的答辯依據。當 QP 問「你們的微生物 OOS 調查程序有何依據」，TR88 就是那個答案。

雙階段框架：一個整合系統，不是兩個獨立流程

TR88 最重要的架構貢獻，是把調查分為兩個有序階段：

  • Phase I（實驗室調查）：確認測試本身是否有效。由小團隊執行，焦點在分析師、設備、培養基、操作程序。
  • Phase II（製造調查）：在實驗室結果確認有效後，轉向製造環境。跨部門團隊，由 QCU 主導。

關鍵邏輯：如果 Phase I 發現測試本身有問題（例如分析師污染了培養基），就不需要進入 Phase II——這是資源效率的設計。但兩個階段可以「並行啟動」，因為微生物調查的時效性（批次放行壓力）不容等待。

「最可能原因」：一個重要的監管概念

TR88 明確允許：當無法確定明確根因時，「最可能原因（most probable cause）」是可被接受的調查結論。

這對日常工作非常重要。微生物學的現實是：污染事件往往無法100%重現或追溯。如果你的 SOP 要求每份 OOS 報告都必須有「definitive root cause」，那你會陷入兩難——要麼寫出站不住腳的根因，要麼讓批次無限期擱置。

正確做法是：用充分的科學推理支持「最可能原因」，並提出合理的 CAPA 預防再發。監管機構接受這樣的邏輯。

CDMO 特殊層次：合約品質協議的責任切割

在 CDMO 環境中，Phase I 和 Phase II 的責任歸屬比品牌廠更複雜。典型問題：

  • 客戶的原料在 CDMO 廠區測試失敗 → Phase I 誰做？CDMO 的微生物實驗室，還是客戶的 QC？
  • Phase II 發現的根因涉及客戶原料的規格設計 → 誰負責 CAPA？
  • 調查報告對外（客戶）、對監管機構各有不同層次的資訊揭露需求

行動建議：在合約品質協議（CQA）中，明確寫入「微生物 OOS 調查的 Phase I 由 CDMO 實驗室主導，Phase II 由雙方共同參與，最終報告由 CDMO QU 核准後發送客戶」。含糊的協議在真實偏差發生時會造成關係破裂。

CAPA 有效性確認：調查的真正終點

TR88 強調 CAPA 必須被「實施」並由負責人向 QCU 回報進度。但更深一層的含義是：調查在 CAPA 有效性確認之前都不算真正完成。

常見的缺失：CAPA 關閉了，但沒有後續的環境監控趨勢分析或重複偵測機制。下次稽核時，稽核員會問「上次那個 Pseudomonas 事件的 CAPA，你們怎麼確認它有效？」如果答案是「我們訓練了分析師」，那是不夠的——你需要數據佐證。

最重要的單一成功因素

TR88 在結論中兩次提到 SME（Subject Matter Expert），且都強調「有製造操作經驗的資深藥廠微生物學家」。

這不是偶然。在所有提升調查成功率的因素中，有一位真正懂得把微生物科學與製造現實連結起來的 SME，是最關鍵的單一變數。這個人不是只會看數字的 QC 人員，而是能走進無菌填充區、理解氣流設計、能與工程師對話的複合型專家。

對 CDMO Operations Director 的啟示：培養這樣的內部人才，或建立外部 SME 顧問網絡，應列入組織能力建設的優先項目。

CDMO 操作主管必讀文獻指南

50 篇文獻中，以下是對 CDMO 無菌充填日常運營影響最大的核心文件，建議優先熟悉：

Ref. 1 — FDA OOS 指引 (2006)

所有藥廠偏差調查的法規基礎。TR88 的存在是因為這份指引不足以涵蓋微生物測試的特殊性，但 OOS 指引本身的兩階段邏輯（Phase I 實驗室 / Phase II 製造）仍是 TR88 的骨幹。FDA 稽查時，你的微生物 OOS SOP 必須能與此指引對齊。

Ref. 3 — FDA 無菌製程指引 (2004)

無菌充填廠的核心法規文件，定義了潔淨室分級、環境監控要求、培養基灌裝（媒體填充）接受標準等。TR88 所有的微生物 OOS 調查，都必須在此框架下進行。對 CDMO 而言，這是客戶選廠稽核的首要審查文件。

Ref. 4 — PDA TR13 環境監控 (2014)

環境監控計畫（EMP）設計的行業標準，包括取樣點選擇、頻率設定、警戒線與行動線計算、趨勢分析方法。Phase II 製造調查中，環境監控歷史數據分析是核心工作之一，TR13 是你的工具書。建議與 TR88 搭配閱讀。

Ref. 13 — USP <71> 無菌試驗

最終產品無菌試驗的標準方法。無菌試驗失敗是 TR88 最嚴重的偏差情境。USP <71> 定義了陰性對照、陽性對照、培養基選擇、培養條件，以及什麼情況下可以進行「重新測試」（retesting）——這與 TR88 Phase I 調查直接連結。

Ref. 14 — USP <85> 細菌內毒素試驗（BET）

注射劑內毒素控制的核心 USP 章節。BET 結果 OOS 的調查邏輯（試驗干擾 vs. 真實污染）是 Phase I 分析的典型案例。對於包含注射劑產品的 CDMO，內毒素調查能力是基本功。

Ref. 32 — PDA TR22 製程模擬（媒體填充）(2011)

無菌充填製程驗證的行業標準。媒體填充（APS/LVS）失敗是 TR88 Phase II 調查中情節最嚴重的情境，通常直接觸發批次回收評估。TR22 定義了 APS 的設計、執行、接受標準、失敗調查步驟，是 CDMO 製程驗證主管的必讀文件。

Ref. 39 — PDA TR80 數據完整性 (2018)

微生物實驗室數據完整性管理。在 FDA 近年稽查中，數據完整性缺失是 483 warning letter 的首要項目之一。Phase I 實驗室調查必然涉及原始數據的完整性審查，TR80 提供了系統性的實驗室 DI 管理框架。

Ref. 33 — PIC/S GMP Annex 1 (2018)

歐盟/PIC/S 無菌製藥 GMP 的核心技術附錄（注：2022年版本已更新，本文引用2018版）。在全球 CDMO 服務歐洲客戶時，Annex 1 的要求往往比 FDA 更嚴格，尤其在污染控制策略（CCS）的文件化方面。建議確認你的工廠已更新至2022版的要求。

作為 CDMO Operations Director，如果你的無菌充填廠每年需要審查更新的法規文件，建議的優先排序是：

  1. FDA Aseptic Processing Guidance (Ref. 3) — 每次 FDA 更新時必讀
  2. PIC/S Annex 1 (Ref. 33) — 歐洲客戶必備，已更新至2022版
  3. USP <71>, <85> (Ref. 13, 14) — 每版 USP 更新時確認
  4. PDA TR13, TR22, TR88 (Ref. 4, 32) — 行業最佳實踐，5年週期更新

TR88 綜合總結 — TR88 Master Synthesis

從 CDMO Operations Director 的視角，看 TR88 的整體價值

  • TR88 的核心貢獻是提供一個完整的微生物偏差調查框架，填補 FDA OOS 指引無法涵蓋的空白。它不是法規要求，但在稽查中它是你最好的辯護依據。
  • 雙階段框架是操作骨幹：Phase I（實驗室有效性）先行確認，Phase II（製造調查）隨後展開——兩者可並行，但邏輯上有順序。在 CDMO 環境中，這個框架還有第三個維度：客戶與 CDMO 之間的責任分工，必須在合約品質協議中明確規定。
  • 「最可能原因」是監管智慧：不要因為追求「完美根因」而讓調查無止境延伸。充分的科學推理 + 合理的 CAPA = 可接受的調查報告。這是監管機構認可的現實主義。
  • CAPA 有效性是真正的終點：很多組織的 CAPA 管理停留在「關閉」，而不是「驗證有效」。建立以趨勢數據為基礎的 CAPA effectiveness check，是走向主動品質管理的關鍵一步。
