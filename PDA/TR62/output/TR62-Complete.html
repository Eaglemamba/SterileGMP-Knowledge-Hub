1.0 Introduction

The purpose of this technical report is to outline methods and approaches for control and evaluation of aseptic processing operations for drug products/medicinal products which use all or partially manual procedures. The goal of aseptic processing is to prevent the contamination of sterile materials during their processing. The goal of evaluating any aseptic process is to demonstrate that aseptic processing can be achieved and maintained successfully under the specified operational configuration, activities, and conditions. These goals are the same for manual or automated aseptic operations, and for small-scale or large-scale operations.

Manual Aseptic Processing (MAP) operations differ from automated operations in several ways. These differences pose unique operational and evaluation challenges not generally encountered with automated operations. These challenges must be considered thoroughly when designing the evaluation procedure or protocol for the MAP operation. MAP involves a human operator performing, at a minimum, the container and/or closure movements. Some semi-automated equipment may be used, but the process is not fully automated. For this reason MAP relies heavily on individual operator's basic understanding of microbiology proficiency where personnel qualification must be individually achieved.

The greatest source of microbial contamination during MAP is generally recognized to be the operational personnel and their activities. Aseptic processes that rely on manual operations are inherently subject to performance drift over time. Furthermore, reproducible human performance cannot be assumed, especially where there are significant time gaps between aseptic processing events. The likelihood of human performance deviations or failures is linked to:

  • Complex aseptic processing tasks
  • The continuous span of time during which an operator carries out repetitive aseptic activities
  • The expected rate of activity
  • Change in personnel

This technical report has value for hospital and formulation pharmacies where pharmacy compounding may occur. The guidance provided in this report may be applicable to various operations, including: vaccine preparation, cell culture, gene therapy, IND/IMP manufacturing, clinical and commercial manufacturing, and pharmacy formulation and dispensing. When manual aseptic processing of sterile dosage forms is required, special consideration must be given to sterility and verification of processing accuracy, as the administration of these products into the vascular and nervous system of human subjects poses the greatest risk of harm. As applicable, the 2004 FDA guidance on aseptic processing, EU GMP–Annex 1, Ph Eur 5.1.1, and USP <797> and <823> provide procedures and the requirements for:

  • Training of personnel involved in sterile preparation processes
  • Environmental control and monitoring requirements
  • Specifications for sterile and non-sterile ingredients and components
  • Release criteria for sterility and pyrogen testing

Compliance with these requirements is paramount to ensure the safety of human recipients.

Some of these processes require aseptic steps subsequent to sterilization but prior to filling, such as volume or pH adjustments, which present their own contamination risks.

This report is not intended to address the brief, relatively infrequent, human interventions into an otherwise automated filling process. Examples include reach-ins to remove a toppled vial from the filling line or to obtain a container for a fill-weight check, aseptic connections made during set-up, corrective activities during line stoppages, and so on. Operator activities and interventions of this nature should be integrated into the aseptic process simulation (APS) of the respective automated processes and they will not be addressed further in this report.

The guidance provided in this report builds upon, and is intended to supplement, published guidance which is generally more focused on automated large-scale operations. The list of references at the end of this report includes sources upon which this report is based.

In conclusion, for adequate evaluation of the Manual Aseptic Process it is important that human factors, such as those described above, are fully accounted for in both the design of an MAP and also in the design of the APS program. The sections that follow include points to consider in that evaluation.

第一節解析：導論

核心概念：何謂 Manual Aseptic Processing (MAP)？

MAP（手動無菌製程）是指由人工操作員執行全部或部分無菌製程步驟的製造作業，包含至少容器或封蓋的移動。部分半自動化設備可能參與其中，但整體製程並非完全自動化。

與自動化製程的核心差異：

  • 自動化製程：機器執行關鍵操作，人員干預較少，製程重現性高
  • MAP：人員是製程的主要執行者，績效高度依賴個人技術與訓練
  • MAP 的污染主要來源是操作人員本身及其行為

TR62 第 1.1 節的精神：MAP 不只是「自動化製程的簡化版」，而是具有完全不同的風險特徵與評估需求的獨立類別。

比喻：手術刀 vs 手術機器人

將 MAP 與自動化無菌製程的差異，比擬為心臟外科手術中「外科醫師親手操刀」與「達文西機器人輔助手術」的區別：

  • 機器人手術（自動化製程）：動作精準一致、不受疲勞影響、每次重現性高
  • 親手操刀（MAP）：需要更高超的個人技術、更嚴格的訓練認證、結果受操作者當日狀態影響
  • 即使是同一位外科醫師，在連續長時間手術後與剛開始時的精準度也可能不同——這正是 TR62 所指的 performance drift（績效漂移）

這也是為什麼 MAP 需要比自動化製程更嚴謹的個別人員資格認證（individual operator qualification）。

重點提示：MAP 獨有的三大風險

  1. 操作人員績效變異性（Operator Performance Variability）：不同操作員的無菌技術水準各異，無法假設其績效一致。TR62 要求每位操作員必須個別完成資格認證（individually qualified）。
  2. 績效漂移（Performance Drift）：即使是合格操作員，若兩次無菌操作之間的間隔時間過長，其技術熟練度可能下滑。這在低頻率生產（如 IND 臨床試驗批次）中尤為顯著。
  3. 複雜性與疲勞效應：任務複雜度越高、操作持續時間越長、操作速率越快，操作失誤風險越高。

這三點決定了 APS（Aseptic Process Simulation，無菌製程模擬）的設計必須充分涵蓋這些人為因素。

應用情境：CDMO 提供 MAP 服務時，TR62 要求建立什麼？

當一家 CDMO 為客戶提供手動無菌灌裝臨床批次或小量特殊產品時，在啟動生產前，TR62 要求其建立：

  • 個別操作員資格認證計畫：每位可執行 MAP 的操作員須完成初始 APS（media fill）認證，取得書面資格紀錄
  • 再認證頻率計畫：定義操作間隔時間上限，超過則需重新認證以避免 performance drift
  • 環境監控計畫：符合 ISO 5 的作業環境，具備微粒與微生物監控程序
  • 人員訓練紀錄：無菌技術訓練（aseptic technique）、更衣資格（gowning qualification）
  • 最差情況 APS 設計：模擬實際生產中最困難的 MAP 操作條件（worst case）

範圍排除：TR62 不涵蓋哪些情況？

TR62 明確排除以下情境——即在自動化灌裝線上偶發的、短暫的人工介入：

  • 伸手取出倒下的玻璃瓶（reach-in to remove toppled vial）
  • 取容器進行灌裝重量確認（fill-weight check）
  • 設備設定時的無菌連接（aseptic connections during set-up）
  • 線路停機時的修正操作

上述操作應納入自動化製程本身的 APS（如 TR22 所述）。TR62 的對象是「製程本身就是手動的」情況，而非「自動化製程中偶爾需要人工介入」的情況。

2.0 Glossary of Terms

Term Definition
Aerobic Microorganism A microorganism that utilizes oxygen as the final electron acceptor during metabolism; a microorganism that will grow primarily in the presence of oxygen. For the purpose of this report, this definition encompasses facultative anaerobes.
Anaerobic Microorganism A microorganism that does not utilize oxygen as the final electron acceptor during metabolism; microorganism that will grow only in the absence of oxygen.
Aseptic Filling The part of aseptic processing where a pre-sterilized product is filled and/or packaged into sterile containers and closed.
Aseptic Processing Handling sterile materials in a controlled environment, in which the air supply, facility, materials, equipment and personnel are regulated to control microbial and particulate contamination to acceptable levels.
Aseptic Processing Area (APA) Controlled environment, consisting of several zones, in which the air supply, facility, materials, equipment and personnel are regulated to control microbial and particulate contamination to acceptable levels.
Aseptic Processing Simulation (APS) A means for establishing the capability of an aseptic process as performed using a growth medium. Note: Aseptic processing simulations are understood to be synonymous with media fills, simulated product fills, broth trials, broth fills, etc.
Barrier System A system of physical partitions that affords ISO 5 protection by partially separating its interior from the surrounding environment utilizing airflow.
Bioburden Total number of viable microorganisms on or in a health care product prior to sterilization.
Biological Safety Cabinet (BSC) An enclosed, ventilated workspace with engineering controls designed to remove or minimize exposure to hazardous biological materials. A BSC is a principle device to provide containment of infectious splashes or aerosols generated by many microbiological procedures. BSCs are designed to provide personnel, environmental and product protection when appropriate practices and procedures are followed.
Campaign A series of consecutive production batches manufactured without intervening cleaning and sterilization.
Colony Forming Unit (CFU) One or more microorganisms that produce a visible, discrete growth entity on a semi-solid, agar-based microbiological medium.
Compounding A process wherein bulk drug substance is combined with one or more excipients and/or another bulk drug substance to produce a drug product.
Critical Area An area designed to maintain sterility of sterile materials. Sterilized product, containers, closures, and equipment may be exposed in critical areas.
Environmental Monitoring Program Defined documented program that describes the routine particulate and microbiological monitoring of processing and manufacturing areas. Note: The program should reference a corrective action plan in cases where action levels are exceeded.
First Air (First Work Location) The work location first in the path of HEPA filtered air.
HEPA filter High efficiency particulate air filter with minimum 0.3 μm particle retaining efficiency of 99.97 percent.
Human Factors A science discipline that examines human psychological, social, physical, and biological characteristics to evaluate the design, operation, or use of products or systems for optimizing human performance, health, safety, and/or habitability. [Synonym: Ergonomics]
Intervention An aseptic manipulation or activity that occurs within the critical area. This technical report regards interventions as either corrective or inherent.
Intervention, Corrective An intervention that is performed to correct or adjust an aseptic process during its execution. Examples include such activities as: clearing component misfeed, adjusting sensors, and replacing equipment components.
Intervention, Inherent An intervention that is an integral part of the aseptic process and is required for set-up or routine operation and/or monitoring, e.g., aseptic assembly, container replenishment, environmental sampling, etc. Inherent interventions are required by batch record, procedure, or work instruction for the proper conduct of the aseptic process.
ISO 5 Environmental operating conditions defined in ISO 14644-1. Note: For total particulates, ISO 5 approximates the Class 100 description from the now obsolete U.S. Federal Standard 209, and is roughly comparable to Grade A as defined in European GMP Annex 1.
ISO 7 Environmental operating conditions defined in ISO 14644-1. Note: For total particulates, ISO 7 approximates Class 10,000 from the now obsolete Federal Standard 209.
ISO 8 Environmental operating conditions defined in ISO 14644-1. Note: For total particulates, ISO 8 approximates Class 100,000 from the now obsolete Federal Standard 209.
Isolator, Closed A decontaminated unit meeting ISO 5 conditions that provides uncompromised, continuous, isolation of its interior from the surrounding environment. Any air exchange with the surrounding environment takes place only through microbially retentive filters.
Isolator, Open A decontaminated unit meeting ISO 5 conditions that provides uncompromised, continuous isolation of its interior from the surrounding environment. It may transfer air directly to the surrounding environment through openings (e.g., "mouseholes") that preclude the ingress of microbial contamination.
Positive Unit Unit filled in an aseptic processing simulation that exhibits detectable microbial growth after incubation.
Restricted Access Barrier System (RABS) RABS are aseptic processing systems (ISO 5) intended to substantially reduce human borne contamination within the aseptic environment where sterile product, containers, closures and equipment are exposed by the use of separative devices and defined mechanical features and operating procedures.
Shift Scheduled periods of work or production, usually less than 12 hours in length, staffed by alternating groups of workers.
Sterile Absence of life; usually refers to absence of viable microorganisms. Note: In practice, no such absolute statement regarding the absence of microorganisms can be proven.
Sterility Test Test performed to determine if viable microorganisms are present.
Sterilization Validated process used to render a product free of viable microorganisms. Note: In a sterilization process, the nature of microbiological death or reduction is described by an exponential function. Therefore, the number of microorganisms which survive a sterilization process can be expressed in terms of probability. While the probability may be reduced to a very low number, it can never be reduced to zero.
Validation Establishing documented evidence that provides a high degree of assurance that a specific process will consistently produce a product meeting its predetermined specifications and quality attributes. Note: There has been wide-spread evolution in the concept and definition of validation in recent years. Readers should refer to definitions established recently by the U.S. FDA, European regulators and other bodies in guidances and regulations.
Unidirectional Air Flow Hood (UAFH) A cabinet designed to protect materials from operator and environmental contamination. Also referred to as laminar air flow hood. All air supplied to the UAFH is HEPA filtered. In operation, all air flows from the UAFH, through the critical manufacturing areas, and exits to the surrounding environment.
Worst Case A set of conditions encompassing upper and lower processing limits and circumstances, including those within standard operating procedures, that pose the greatest chance of process or product failure (when compared to ideal conditions). Such conditions do not necessarily induce product or process failure.

重點提示：ISO 潔淨室分級對照

術語表列出了三個 ISO 分級，與 MAP 作業直接相關：

  • ISO 5（≈ Grade A / Class 100）：MAP 關鍵操作的最低要求環境，需提供單向氣流（UDAF）保護。所有暴露於無菌物料的操作均須在此執行。
  • ISO 7（≈ Grade B / Class 10,000）：通常作為 ISO 5 區域的支援背景環境（background environment），人員更衣室常設計為此等級。
  • ISO 8（≈ Grade C/D / Class 100,000）：一般用於準備區或較低風險的非關鍵操作區域。

比喻：APS 如同外科醫師的執照考試

MAP 操作員需個別通過 APS（media fill）認證，才能從事無菌製程操作——這類似於外科醫師必須通過執照考試才能獨立執刀。

  • 初次認證（Initial Qualification）：如同取得醫師執照——操作員首次完成 APS，證明具備基本無菌技術能力
  • 再認證（Requalification）：如同醫師的繼續教育學分——確保操作員維持技術水準，避免 performance drift
  • 最差情況設計（Worst Case APS）：如同考試中包含最難的臨床情境——確保操作員能應對實際生產中最具挑戰性的操作

3.0 Buildings and Facilities

The selection of an appropriate clean environment in which to perform the required manufacturing activities is critical to MAP operations. In general, the minimum requirement is an ISO 5 environment in which unidirectional air flow protection is provided to critical materials during the manufacturing procedures. In practice, many different locations and equipment for MAP operations are possible, including:

  • A portion of a larger environment of the same class
  • A localized UDAF (Unidirectional Air Flow Hood) protecting a specific area within a lower-classified environment
  • A table-mounted unidirectional air flow hood (horizontal or vertical flow)
  • An isolator (open or closed)

HEPA Filtration and UDAF Requirements

All air supplied to the UDAF is HEPA-filtered (minimum 99.97% efficiency at ≥0.3 µm). In operation, all air flows from the UDAF, through the critical manufacturing areas, and exits to the surrounding environment. Aseptic manipulations must be performed in "First Air" — the work location first in the path of HEPA-filtered air — not having passed over any other components or blocked by the operator's hands.

Supporting Environment and Pressure Differentials

The supporting clean environment outside the critical zone is typically ISO 7 when a UDAF or BSC is utilized. Use of an isolator under appropriate risk-management procedures may relax the requirements for the surrounding area; most new manufacturing installations employ an ISO 8 background environment in accordance with the 2004 FDA aseptic processing guidance or European GMP Annex 1.

Open isolators are typically installed in ISO 8 environments in order to maintain ISO 5 conditions for material transfers and operations. Appropriate gowning facilities are required consistent with the background environment classification.

Biological Safety Cabinets (BSC)

BSCs are designed to protect operators and the external environment from infected or dangerous materials. All air to and from the BSC is HEPA-filtered. Because air flows into the BSC from the surrounding environment, BSCs should be used only when worker safety is a meaningful concern. They are not equivalent to a UDAF for product protection purposes.

概念解析：ISO 14644 等級與 EU GMP Grade 對應

ISO 等級 ↔ EU GMP Grade 對應系統

ISO 14644 與 EU GMP Annex 1 各自使用不同命名系統，但彼此有明確對應：

  • ISO 5 = Grade A（靜態下等同 Grade B）— 最關鍵區域，所有 MAP 無菌操作均須在此等級執行
  • ISO 7 = Grade C（靜態）/ Grade B（動態）— 背景支援區，Grade A 區的外圍環境
  • ISO 8 = Grade D（靜態）/ Grade C（動態）— 最低要求的潔淨區，通常用於隔離器外圍或更衣區

為何 MAP 需要 Grade A / ISO 5？ MAP 操作中產品、容器、密封件直接暴露於環境，人員就是最主要的污染源。ISO 5 的 3,520 顆/m³（≥0.5 µm）極限配合 UDAF 單向氣流，確保任何從 HEPA 濾器吹出的「First Air」在接觸產品前未先通過人員或設備，最大程度降低微生物落下污染風險。

Table 3.0-1 解讀：靜態 vs. 動態粒子限度

靜態（at-rest）：空室或設備已就位但無人員作業時的基準值，反映潔淨室固有的過濾能力。

動態（in-operation）：正式製造操作期間量測，人員在場時產生的粒子量可比靜態高出數十倍。

Grade A（ISO 5）要求動態下仍維持 3,520 顆/m³，這在有人員操作的 MAP 環境中極具挑戰性。正是因為人體每分鐘可散發數萬顆粒子，UDAF 的單向氣流設計必須將人員隔離在「First Air」路徑之外，否則幾乎不可能達標。

ISO 8 / Grade D 動態限度則未被任何指引明確定義，因為其風險等級允許較大彈性。

為什麼選擇隔離器可以放寬背景要求？

封閉式或開放式隔離器（Isolator）透過物理屏障 + HEPA 過濾系統，將 ISO 5 的無菌環境與外部人員完全隔離。一旦隔離器本身提供足夠的 ISO 5 屏障，外部背景環境的要求即可從 ISO 7 放寬至 ISO 8，因為人員本身不再直接接觸 ISO 5 區域內的產品。

這也是新建廠房越來越傾向採用隔離器配合 ISO 8 背景的原因——總建設成本與維護成本較傳統 ISO 7 潔淨室更低，同時無菌保障水準更高。

Section 4.0 — Operational Personnel Training and Qualification

操作人員培訓與確效 | PDA TR62 p8–p9

4.0 Operational Personnel Training and Qualification

People are the most critical operational variable and represent the highest source of contamination in manual aseptic processing. Operational personnel must be highly proficient at their assigned tasks. Therefore, personnel training and qualification become critical to success.

4.1 Personnel Training and Qualification

The training requirements for operational personnel typically include the usual elements of cGMP:

  • Microbiological principles
  • Sterility assurance
  • Sterilization
  • Gowning practices
  • Good aseptic practices

Theoretical knowledge alone is insufficient. Operators must be able to adapt classroom learning to the real-world manufacturing environment. They must excel in execution of tasks that directly impact sterility assurance: aseptic gowning, aseptic assembly, and aseptic technique. They must consistently perform precision tasks without introducing contamination.

4.2 Gowning Qualification

The most basic qualification task is gowning qualification. This involves:

  • Repetitive gowning in full aseptic garb under observation of a fully qualified individual
  • Monitoring of gown surfaces — typically gloves, forearms, and chest area (body locations closest to aseptic manipulations)
  • The operator must successfully demonstrate ability to meet defined monitoring levels after each exercise

Gowning certifications should be conducted on a periodic basis to confirm consistent gowning practices. Once operators pass initial certification they are granted access to the aseptic core for continued instruction.

Note: The need for operator gowning qualification is based on the nature of the isolator process and should be evaluated by a risk assessment. Following a long-term absence, adverse trend, or out-of-limits gowning results (and based on investigation), operators should be requalified for gowning.

4.3 Risk Management

Quality Risk Management (per PDA TR54 and TR44) is highly recommended for MAP. As stated in PDA TR44:

Process failures that can result in elevated endotoxin levels and lack of sterility assurance pose a significant risk to patient safety. The ability to detect a process failure is low given the current methods for sterility testing. The probability of a process failure that could adversely affect sterility in aseptic processing is higher and less predictable than in terminal sterilization, given the inherent exposure to environmental contaminants. Quality risk management can be an effective method of identifying and reducing aseptic processing risk.

MAP involves greater risks than automated aseptic processes. A risk-based quality management approach is essential. There must be a thorough and complete understanding of the process, including critical steps and risks, so that any risk assessment is appropriately informed.

4.4 Aseptic Handling Challenges

The conventional means for establishing personnel proficiency is through participation in an APS (Aseptic Processing Simulation, also known as a media fill). The APS requires operators to perform aseptic interventions during the normal course of simulation that are typically conducted during manufacturing.

MAP is particularly challenging because:

  • MAP is more susceptible to human contamination than automated aseptic processing
  • Interventions on manual aseptic systems are frequent and may be complicated by suboptimal equipment design

An individual who has demonstrated proficiency at large-scale industrial aseptic processing must still demonstrate proficiency in the more rigorous requirements of MAP. Challenge tests require the operator to directly handle sterile equipment and materials (usually with media) to demonstrate aseptic technique. These tasks must be representative of actual process steps.

The likelihood of human performance drift deviations or failures is linked to:

  • Complex aseptic processing tasks
  • The continuous span of time during which an operator carries out repetitive aseptic activities
  • The expected rate of activity
  • Change in personnel

解析：人員確效的核心邏輯

比喻：飛行員機型換照（Type Rating）

MAP 操作員的確效流程，就像航空業的機型換照制度——

一位資深機長即使已持有 A320 機型執照，若要駕駛 B737，仍必須完成完整的 B737 機型訓練與評估，取得該機型執照後才能獨立飛行。光靠多年的飛行經驗與通用航空知識是不夠的。

同理，MAP 操作員必須針對每一種特定製程個別確效，而不能僅憑一般無菌操作經驗就被允許執行新製程。一位在大規模自動化灌裝線上經確效的操作員，仍必須為其所執行的每項 MAP 製程步驟單獨通過確效。

這個類比的核心概念是：確效對象是「人員 × 製程」的組合，而非僅針對個人資格或設備性能。

關鍵提醒：MAP 中，確效的是「人」，不是「機器」

這是 MAP 與自動化製程最根本的差異：

  • 自動化製程 → 設備確效（IQ / OQ / PQ）：只要設備保持在確效狀態，製程即視為受控
  • MAP → 人員確效：每位操作員必須個別確效；設備確效並不能取代人員確效

因此，若某操作員六個月以上未執行該製程（長期缺席），或近期更衣監測出現不良趨勢（adverse trend），TR62 要求必須重新確效，而非僅憑個人資歷推定其能力仍然維持。

這也意味著 CDMO 在人員排班規劃上必須考量：持有確效資格的操作員人數，直接決定了可安排批次的彈性。

Section 4.3 與 ICH Q9 的連結：風險管理在 MAP 中的應用

ICH Q9 Quality Risk Management 要求系統性識別、評估、控制製程中的風險。將其應用於 MAP，風險評估應特別關注：

  • 最高風險操作：管路連接（aseptic connections）、手動加入粉末（powder addition）、手工壓蓋（stopper insertion by hand）
  • 風險因素評估維度：操作複雜度、持續操作時間、人員更換頻率、疲勞累積
  • 控制措施：透過 APS 中的最壞情況模擬（worst-case simulation）驗證控制措施是否足夠

PDA TR54（Quality Risk Management Implementation）提供了以風險矩陣識別 MAP 關鍵步驟的具體案例，建議與 TR62 搭配參照。

應用情境：CDMO 新操作員確效流程（TR62 建議路徑）

假設情境：一家 CDMO 需為臨床批次手動灌裝線確效 3 位新操作員。

  1. 第一步 — cGMP 基礎訓練（Section 4.1）
    完成微生物學原則、無菌保障、滅菌原理、更衣規範的課堂訓練，並通過書面測驗
  2. 第二步 — 更衣確效（Section 4.2）
    每位操作員在受訓指導員監督下完成多次全套無菌更衣；採樣手套、前臂、胸部，結果需符合限度。通過後方可進入無菌核心區
  3. 第三步 — 製程專屬技術培訓（Section 4.4）
    在無菌核心區接受該製程步驟的操作訓練（使用模擬物料），掌握 First Air 原則、無菌組裝、採樣技術
  4. 第四步 — APS（媒體灌填）確效（Section 4.4）
    3 位操作員在模擬實際製程的條件下執行完整 APS，包含所有干預措施。APS 結果符合接受標準（通常 0 陽性）方完成確效，可正式上線

確效後需維持定期更衣再確效（periodic re-qualification），以確認操作員技術水準未因時間而衰退（performance drift）。

Section 5.0 — Equipment, Components and Container/Closure

設備、元件與容器密封系統 | PDA TR62 p10

5.0 Equipment, Components and Container/Closure

The equipment, raw material components, containers, closures and other items required for MAP vary with the requirements of the process. These items are typically reduced in size or quantity, which allows them to be supplied to the processing environment in a sealed package after depyrogenation/sterilization.

Preparation and Sterilization Methods

The preparation methods for such sealed packages prior to depyrogenation/sterilization are similar to those associated with automated aseptic processing, though on a smaller scale. The depyrogenation/sterilization methods for all items are required to be validated.

  • Glass vials and containers: Must be depyrogenated (typically by dry heat oven at ≥250°C for validated time and temperature cycles) to achieve the required reduction in endotoxin load (≥3-log reduction)
  • Rubber stoppers and closures: Typically steam-autoclaved in validated cycles; may also be purchased pre-sterilized from qualified suppliers
  • Tubing and plastic components: Sterilized by steam, gamma irradiation, or EO depending on material compatibility; validated for each component type
  • Stainless steel tools and utensils: Sterilized by steam autoclave or dry heat; sterile tools must be used wherever possible to handle sterile materials rather than direct glove contact

Commercially Purchased Sterile Components

When sterile containers/closures are purchased from a commercial source, procedures should be in place and implemented to ensure the sterility of these items is maintained when introduced into an aseptic environment and used in production.

Non-Product Contact Surfaces

Where sterilization or depyrogenation of non-product contact surfaces cannot be utilized, a validated sanitization process is required. All product contact surfaces shall be sterilized. Sterility should be maintained with protective layers that can be removed as materials are transitioned to cleaner environments.

Sterile Solution Filtration

Sterile filtration of solutions through validated 0.22 µm (or smaller) sterilizing-grade filters is required prior to aseptic dispensing. Filter integrity testing (pre- and/or post-use) must be performed per validated procedures. Aseptic connections between tubing, bags, and filter housings must be made using validated aseptic connection techniques in the ISO 5 zone.

概念解析：MAP 中的滅菌與除熱原要求

重點提示：每一個接觸產品的元件都必須有無菌保障

在 MAP 中，沒有任何「後補救」的機會——產品一旦灌裝完成，終端滅菌通常不可行（否則就不需要 MAP 了）。因此，所有接觸產品的表面、元件、工具必須在引入無菌區前完成滅菌或除熱原確效。

各類物料的常見處理方式：

  • 玻璃瓶（vials）：乾熱隧道除熱原（≥250°C），達到 ≥3-log 內毒素降低；滅菌與除熱原同步完成
  • 橡膠塞（stoppers）：蒸汽高壓滅菌（autoclave），條件需經驗證；部分廠商提供已滅菌商業化產品，須建立供應商確效程序
  • 矽膠管路（tubing）：蒸汽滅菌或 gamma 輻射滅菌（視材質相容性）；每批使用前確認滅菌包裝完整性
  • 不鏽鋼工具：高壓蒸汽滅菌；在 ISO 5 區域內應使用無菌工具操作無菌物料，避免直接手套接觸

所有方法均需通過驗證，並納入批次製造記錄（batch record）作為放行依據。

滅菌 vs. 除熱原：為什麼需要區分？

這是一個常見的混淆點：

  • 滅菌（Sterilization）：殺滅微生物（包括細菌芽孢）→ 高壓蒸汽、乾熱、gamma 輻射均可
  • 除熱原（Depyrogenation）：破壞內毒素（endotoxin，即革蘭氏陰性菌的細胞壁碎片）→ 必須使用更高能量的乾熱（≥250°C），普通滅菌條件（121°C 蒸汽）無法有效破壞內毒素

玻璃瓶既需要滅菌也需要除熱原，因此乾熱除熱原是首選，可一步到位。橡膠塞的除熱原通常由製造商在原料生產階段控制，使用者需要確認原料供應商的內毒素控制標準。

Section 6.0 — Process Time Limitations

製程時間限制 | PDA TR62 p11

6.0 Process Time Limitations

Personnel time limitations with MAP are usually more important than with automated aseptic processes. In very short or non-repetitive processes (such as an aseptic connection), time may be of little relevance except as it relates to material stability. However, where operators must perform repetitive tasks such as container filling/stoppering, egg harvesting (as in vaccine manufacturing), or similar tasks, the effect of fatigue must be considered in both routine operation and process simulation.

Operator Fatigue and Performance Drift

The operator's aseptic technique may deteriorate with the passage of time (performance drift). A worst-case evaluation of fatigue should include a process simulation equal to or greater in time duration than the longest period an operator might perform the task without interruption. Similarly, a process simulation conducted at the end of a normal day's production can evaluate the effect of fatigue on aseptic technique.

Equipment and Material Time Limits

Time limits for holding dirty/clean/sterile equipment and sterile/depyrogenated components shall also be validated. It is also necessary to validate:

  • The maximum time allowed between formulation (addition of the first ingredient, including water) to the point of sterilization by filtration — considering filter grow-through risk
  • The holding time for sterile bulk prior to and during aseptic dispensing/filling
  • The maximum time for the aseptic operation itself (from opening sterile packs to completion of filling and stoppering)

These topics are discussed further in PDA Technical Report No. 22.

Note (TR62 Section 6.0): As part of the process risk assessment, personnel fatigue factors need to be considered, and where possible minimized/eliminated through improvement in ergonomics, an understanding of human factors, etc.

Bioburden and Process Time Relationship

Bioburden in a product solution can increase over time if temperature and time are not controlled. A higher bioburden level entering sterile filtration increases the risk that some organisms may pass through the filter (filter grow-through) or that filter plugging occurs before filtration is complete. The process time limit between compounding (first ingredient addition) and filtration/filling must therefore be defined and validated based on:

  • The microbiological growth characteristics of the product formulation
  • The holding temperature during compounding and pre-filtration steps
  • The initial bioburden level of raw materials and water
  • The designed safety margin relative to filter grow-through risk

概念解析：製程時間限制的邏輯基礎

製程時間限制的核心邏輯：微生物負載風險模型

微生物負載風險 = f（初始微生物負載量，時間，溫度）

具體而言：

  微生物數量(t) = 初始數量 × 2^(t / 倍增時間)

  其中：
    t = 製程持續時間（小時）
    倍增時間 = 微生物在該配方、溫度下的代時

風險臨界點：
  若 微生物數量(t) 超過過濾器承受上限
  → 發生 filter grow-through（微生物穿透無菌過濾器）
  → 灌裝產品無法保證無菌

因此，TR62 要求在 IND/NDA/MAA 申請中明確定義並驗證：

  • 從第一個原料加入（含注射用水）到開始無菌過濾的最長允許時間（通常稱為 compounding hold time）
  • 無菌過濾後到灌裝完成的最長允許時間（sterile bulk hold time）
  • 無菌操作本身（開啟包裝到壓蓋完成）的最長允許持續時間

這些時限通常通過微生物挑戰研究（bioburden simulation study）+ 分析穩定性數據共同支撐。

MAP 特殊考量：疲勞因素是「製程時間限制」的一部分

在自動化製程中，設備不會「疲勞」——只要機器維持確效狀態，任何時間點的性能都一致。但在 MAP 中，隨著時間增加：

  • 操作員專注力下降 → 無菌技術偏差機率上升
  • 重複動作造成肌肉疲勞 → 動作精準度降低
  • 更衣汗水累積 → 更衣屏障效能可能衰退

因此，APS（媒體灌填）必須在「最壞情況時間」下執行——也就是模擬操作員在一個工作日末段、連續操作最長時間後的狀態，以評估疲勞對無菌技術的實際影響。

人因工程（Human Factors / Ergonomics）的改善，例如：更符合人體工學的工作台高度、適當的休息間隔設計，都是降低疲勞風險的有效手段，應在製程設計階段（Section 7.0）一併考慮。

製程時間限制與 PDA TR22 的關係

TR62 Section 6.0 刻意保持簡短，並明確指向 PDA Technical Report No. 22（Process Simulation for Aseptically Filled Products）作為詳細指引。TR22 包含：

  • 各類時間限制的驗證方法學（包括 bioburden 研究設計）
  • APS 持續時間的決定原則（應涵蓋實際製程最長操作時間）
  • 持有時間（hold time）驗證的最差情況設定

在閱讀 TR62 第 6 節後，建議同步參閱 TR22 的對應章節，以獲得完整的方法論支撐。

7.1 Manual Aseptic Process Design in Unidirectional Air Flow (UDAF) Hoods

TR62 Section 7.1 establishes detailed design principles for conducting MAP inside UDAF hoods (also called laminar flow hoods or laminar flow workstations). These principles address workspace layout, operator roles, material handling, and environmental monitoring strategy.

First Air and Hand Positioning

The foundational concept is First Air — all exposed product and product-contacting components must continuously remain in the critical zone that receives HEPA-filtered air before it contacts any other surface. Aseptic manipulations must be performed in First Air, meaning airflow must not have passed over other components or been blocked by the operator's hands before reaching the product.

The primary operator must wear sterile gloves and sleeves and must never contact a non-sanitized or non-sterilized item. The hands of the primary operator should remain in the ISO 5 environment at all times during critical manipulations.

Primary and Secondary Operator Roles

TR62 specifies a team-based approach:

  • The primary operator performs all critical aseptic manipulations within the ISO 5 environment. Their sterile-gloved hands must remain in ISO 5 at all times.
  • The secondary operator introduces and removes items from the ISO 5 environment, assists with less critical tasks, and must don sterile gloves/sleeves before any contact with items being transferred to the primary operator.
  • A support operator handles activities exclusively in the surrounding (non-ISO 5) environment — e.g., batch record documentation, equipment adjustment.

Whenever the primary operator must leave the ISO 5 environment, gloves and sleeves must be changed or gloves re-sanitized prior to re-entry.

Introduction of Sterile Items

Sterilized items must be introduced into the ISO 5 area by aseptically removing the final outer wrap as the item enters the zone. Extra sub-assemblies and utensils should be pre-sterilized and available for immediate replacement. Sterile tools should be used wherever possible to handle sterile materials — direct glove contact with product surfaces should be minimized. Sterile supports or hangers for tools must be available inside the ISO 5 environment to prevent tools from contacting the workspace floor.

Materials and Process Flow — Clean to Dirty

  • Wherever possible, materials being introduced should be pre-measured into tightly sealed containers prior to sterilization.
  • Electrical equipment and controls must be located outside the ISO 5 environment if possible; if not, a secondary (not primary) operator adjusts settings.
  • Liquid transfers should use peristaltic pumps located outside the aseptic environment rather than automatic pipettes, due to exhausted air concerns. Containers can be pre-marked to indicate transfer amounts.
  • Perform as much of the process as possible inside ISO 5 to minimize the removal and re-entry of in-process materials.
  • When containers must be temporarily removed from ISO 5, they should be aseptically wrapped in pre-sterilized covering before removal; that covering is removed and discarded prior to re-entry. Alternatively, container exteriors can be re-sanitized.

Sanitization Discipline

Sanitize the operating environment when empty. Sanitize each non-sterilizable item or piece of equipment as it is introduced and transitions to each cleaner environmental level. Do not introduce large items mid-process. If the primary operator performs sanitization, sterile gloves/sleeves must be changed before resuming aseptic manipulation of product.

Product-contact surfaces shall be sterilized, with sterility maintained via protective layers removed progressively as materials transition to cleaner environments.

Smoke Study Requirements

Once the process design has been established, it must be rehearsed several times and documented in smoke study (air flow visualization) studies using all required items and placebo materials. This confirms that the process design is practical and reduces contamination risk to a minimum. Engineering runs are strongly encouraged to develop and refine the process before qualification.

Documentation and Environmental Monitoring

The manufacturing process must be documented in sufficient detail for operators to understand and conform to desired practices. The secondary or support operator should complete the batch record to keep the primary operator's hands in ISO 5.

Environmental monitoring must be non-intrusive. Active air sampling during processing may use specially designed equipment that does not compromise the ISO 5 environment, including settling plates. Surface monitoring (contact plates or swabs) should be performed after processing is complete.

核心概念：First Air（第一手空氣）

First Air 指的是：HEPA濾網過濾後，最先接觸到的那一層空氣流。產品與產品接觸面，必須始終處於這道「第一手空氣」的保護之下。

操作者的雙手若擋在 HEPA 與產品之間，就會破壞 First Air 的路徑——過濾後的乾淨空氣先經過手的表面，再到達產品，此時它已不再是「第一手」，而是潛在的污染媒介。

規則：手的位置必須在產品的下游（downstream），永遠不能阻斷 First Air 的路徑。

比喻：外科手術洗手的邏輯

想像外科醫師洗完手後，雙手高舉、掌心朝外——這不是姿勢問題，而是防污染邏輯：

  • 雙手保持在最高位置 → 不讓水往上流到「乾淨的前臂」
  • 方向是從乾淨到不乾淨（高→低、上游→下游）
  • 雙手絕不碰觸非無菌表面

UDAF 工作站的 MAP 邏輯完全相同：

  • 雙手必須在氣流下游（靠近操作者一側），不能遮擋產品
  • 工作流方向是乾淨→不乾淨（clean-to-dirty），即從最靠近 HEPA 的位置開始，向外側進行
  • 主操作員的手永遠不離開 ISO 5 區域

三種操作員角色分工

主操作員（Primary Operator）
  └─ 在 ISO 5 區域內執行所有關鍵無菌操作
  └─ 手不離 ISO 5，戴無菌手套+袖套

輔助操作員（Secondary Operator）
  └─ 負責將物料送入/取出 ISO 5 環境
  └─ 協助較不關鍵的任務
  └─ 接觸物料前須戴無菌手套/袖套

支援操作員（Support Operator）
  └─ 在 ISO 5 外部環境工作
  └─ 填寫批次記錄、調整設備設定
  └─ 讓主操作員的手保持在 ISO 5 內

重點提示：Smoke Study（煙霧研究）的意義

Smoke study（煙霧可視化研究）是 TR62 明確要求的步驟——在製程設計確立後，必須使用全套實際物料與安慰劑進行多次練習，並以煙霧記錄空氣流動模式。目的是：

  • 驗證產品位置確實處於 First Air 下游
  • 發現氣流被手、設備或容器阻斷的位置
  • 確認 ISO 5 的有效工作範圍
  • 在 APS（無菌製程模擬）之前建立操作流程的可靠性

工程運行（engineering runs）在正式確認前強烈建議執行，以精進物料位置與操作步驟。

7.2 Manual Aseptic Process Design in Isolators and RABS

Isolator Definition and Absolute Barrier

TR62 Section 7.2 defines an Isolator as a sealed enclosure (or one supplied with air through a microbially retentive — minimum HEPA — filtration system) that can be reproducibly decontaminated. When closed, it uses only decontaminated interfaces or RTPs (Rapid Transfer Ports) for all materials transfer. When open, it uses defined openings ("mouse holes") that have been designed and validated to prevent contamination ingress.

Isolators can be used for aseptic processing, containment of potent compounds, or simultaneously for both. The absolute physical barrier between the operator and the product is the defining advantage of isolator MAP — the operator never contacts the product environment directly; all interaction is through glove ports.

First Air in Isolators

Regardless of isolator or RABS type, these are aseptic, not sterile, process enclosures — therefore good aseptic technique must be applied at all aseptic steps. Where unidirectional airflow is provided inside the isolator, First Air principles must be followed just as in open UDAF hoods. Where unidirectional flow is absent, aseptic technique must still be consistent with the enclosure design.

Glove Port Technique and Sterile Gloves

Operators must don fresh sterile gloves prior to entering the enclosure gloves (i.e., sterile gloves are worn inside the enclosure glove port gauntlets). Sterile tools and utensils shall be used rather than direct enclosure-glove contact with product or product-contact surfaces. Sterile supports for tools inside the enclosure minimize contact between tools and horizontal workspace surfaces.

Materials Transfer: Alpha/Beta Ports and RTP

All items and equipment must be introduced into the enclosure using validated decontamination/sterilization methods and/or RTPs (Rapid Transfer Ports). The alpha/beta port system — also called a "split butterfly valve" — consists of a port on the isolator wall (alpha half) that mates with a container port (beta half). The two halves can only be joined and opened together, maintaining a sterile-to-sterile interface and preventing any exposure of the interior to the external environment during transfer.

When containers of in-process materials must leave and re-enter the enclosure, they must be re-introduced using validated decontamination/sterilization methods and/or RTPs. Alternatively, containers can be aseptically wrapped in pre-sterilized covering (e.g., sealable packages) for temporary removal, with the covering removed and discarded prior to re-entry.

VHP Decontamination

The enclosure must be decontaminated according to a validated procedure — typically using VHP (Vaporized Hydrogen Peroxide) for isolators. VHP decontamination is required before each manufacturing campaign and after any activity that could compromise the internal environment. This is what gives isolators their decisive contamination-control advantage over UDAF hoods: the starting baseline of the internal environment is a validated decontaminated state, not simply an ISO 5 environment maintained by airflow alone.

RABS: Restricted Access Barrier Systems

RABS vary significantly by design. Where items are introduced using an RTP, operation resembles an isolator. RABS lacking RTPs operate more like a manned cleanroom and follow the UDAF-hood design principles described in Section 7.1.

Closed RABS maintain a physical barrier throughout the process; any interventions requiring the opening of doors must follow defined procedures including sporicidal disinfection and re-establishment of the controlled environment. Open RABS may allow panel or door opening during the process under strictly controlled conditions, making them less of an absolute barrier than closed RABS or isolators.

Common Design Principles for Isolators and RABS

  • Perform as much of the process as possible inside the enclosure to minimize removal and re-entry.
  • Electrical equipment should be located outside the enclosure where possible; if not, the secondary operator adjusts settings.
  • Use secondary/support operators to supply/remove items from the enclosure.
  • Pre-measure materials into sealed containers before sterilization and introduction.
  • Use peristaltic pumps outside the enclosure for liquid transfers (not automatic pipettes).
  • Perform environmental monitoring in a non-intrusive manner; surface monitoring by contact plates/swabs after processing.
  • Significant aseptic assembly should be avoided by using pre-assembled, pre-sterilized items.
  • Process design must be rehearsed with all required items and placebo materials; engineering runs are strongly recommended.
  • The manufacturing process must be fully documented; secondary/support operators complete the batch record.

中文解說：7.2 隔離器與RABS的MAP設計

重點提示：為何高風險產品推薦使用隔離器？

TR62 強調的核心原則：MAP 中最大的污染向量（contamination vector）是操作員本身。無論再嚴格的訓練、再完整的更衣程序，人體持續釋放微生物。

Isolator 的絕對屏障設計，讓操作員完全無法直接接觸產品環境——所有互動都通過 glove port 進行。這意味著：

  • 操作員的皮膚、毛髮、呼吸、服裝微粒，全部被物理隔絕於產品區域之外
  • 配合 VHP 消毒，隔離器內部環境從設計上就排除了操作員作為污染來源的可能性
  • 對於生物製劑、腫瘤藥物（oncology）、基因治療產品等高風險品類，這是商業規模 MAP 的最高標準

核心概念：VHP 消毒循環（四相循環）

VHP（汽化過氧化氫）消毒是隔離器在每次製造活動前的必要程序。標準的四相循環如下：

  • Phase 1 — 調節（Conditioning）：降低隔離器內部的相對濕度，使 VHP 能以氣態均勻分布，避免提前冷凝
  • Phase 2 — 消毒（Decontamination）：將 VHP 注入隔離器至目標濃度，開始對表面發揮殺孢子效力
  • Phase 3 — 駐留（Dwell）：在目標濃度下維持足夠時間（依驗證結果），達到所需的對數殺滅（log reduction）
  • Phase 4 — 曝氣（Aeration）：排除殘留 VHP，將隔離器內 H₂O₂ 濃度降至安全的操作水準（通常 <1 ppm）

VHP 循環須經過驗證，並在每次生產活動前執行——這是隔離器能提供可重現的消毒起始狀態的關鍵。

比喻：Alpha/Beta Port 如同太空站氣閘

想像太空站的氣閘（airlock）：

  • 太空人要進入太空站，必須先進入氣閘，關上外門，才能開啟內門
  • 外門與內門絕對不能同時開啟，否則太空站的氣壓環境立即暴露於外太空

Alpha/Beta port（快速傳遞口）的邏輯相同：

  • Beta half（外部）：安裝在容器或物料載具上，由外部操作員對接
  • Alpha half（隔離器壁）：隔離器本體上的接口
  • 兩半合體後才能同時開啟，形成無菌對無菌的傳遞界面——隔離器內部永遠不會暴露於外部環境

這正是 RTP（Rapid Transfer Port）技術的核心原理。

  ├─ 干預方式：開門進行，需嚴格程序

  • 操作員再培訓與 APS（無菌製程模擬）：所有操作員必須在新 RABS 環境中完成培訓並通過 APS 資格確認，TR62 第8章要求每半年一次維持資格
  • 干預程序重新定義：RABS 環境中任何干預行為（interventions）的觸發條件、執行方式及 CAPA 判定標準，均需重新定義和驗證
  • 環境監測計畫更新：RABS 的採樣點、採樣方法、警戒值/行動值均需針對新環境重新設定

  • First Air is the foundational principle — product and product-contact surfaces must always be in the first path of HEPA-filtered air, never downstream of the operator's hands or other components.
  • In UDAF hoods, contamination control depends entirely on operator discipline: clean-to-dirty workflow, team roles (primary/secondary/support), aseptic introduction of materials, smoke study validation, and non-intrusive environmental monitoring.
  • Isolators provide an absolute barrier by physically excluding the operator from the product zone. VHP decontamination establishes a validated clean baseline before every campaign. All material transfers use RTP / alpha/beta port systems.
  • RABS exist on a spectrum: closed RABS provide a near-isolator level of barrier during the process, while open RABS offer partial barrier with greater flexibility. Both require defined sporicidal procedures and glove port discipline.
  • All three environment types require: First Air adherence where UDAF is present, operator team structures, pre-sterilized/pre-assembled materials, peristaltic pumps for liquid transfer, and rigorous process documentation.
  • The choice of MAP environment must match the product's risk profile, manufacturing scale, and contamination control requirements — TR62 does not mandate a single environment but provides principles for each.

核心重點回顧

  • First Air 原則是所有 MAP 設計的起點——無論使用哪種環境，產品必須始終位於「第一手空氣」之下，操作員的手不得阻斷氣流路徑。
  • UDAF 工作站的 MAP 完全依賴操作員紀律：工作流方向（乾淨→不乾淨）、三種操作員角色分工、無菌物料引入程序、smoke study 驗證氣流。
  • Isolator 以絕對屏障排除操作員作為污染源，結合 VHP 四相消毒循環和 RTP/alpha/beta port 無菌傳遞系統，是高風險商業規模 MAP 的最高標準。
  • RABS 介於 UDAF 和 Isolator 之間：Closed RABS 屏障完整，Open RABS 屏障部分。兩者均須進行殺孢子消毒，並透過 glove port 執行所有操作。
  • 設備選型決策需平衡：產品風險等級、批次週轉時間需求、操作員人體工學限制、資本投入與維運成本。
  • 無論選擇何種 MAP 環境，TR62 的製程設計要求均須滿足：預組裝無菌物料、管外蠕動泵液體傳輸、二次操作員支援、非侵入性環境監測，以及充分的工程運行練習。

8.0

Evaluation of Manual Aseptic Processing — Process Simulation

手動無菌製程評估——製程模擬

8.0 Overview

The following are the elements of a MAP simulation that involve extensive participation of personnel in the aseptic process. Details of study design provided in the APS reports on aseptic processing for either filling or sterile bulk production should be consulted for those activities that are essentially unchanged when manual procedures are employed (Reference 15).

Unlike automated filling operations where the process itself is qualified and validated, in manual aseptic processing the operator is the primary variable. The qualification of the process does not automatically confer qualification on the individuals performing it. Each person who handles sterile materials must individually demonstrate — through a structured APS — that they can perform the required operations without introducing microbial contamination.

The smaller scale typical of MAP lends itself well to simulation adaptations, and in many instances only minimal changes to the production process are required to execute a valid simulation. In the event of a failure investigation, maintaining the sequence in which samples were filled during the APS can be beneficial in determining at what point contamination was introduced.

The simulation rationale should be kept current with changes to process, products, components, or equipment that could impact the acceptability of the process. MAP can encompass a variety of activities divided into 4 major categories, each addressed in a different manner.

核心概念：為何 MAP 需要專屬 APS？

自動化 vs. 手動：資格驗證的對象不同

在自動化灌裝中，驗證的對象是製程——機器、環境、速度、參數。只要製程經過驗證且在管控範圍內運行，不需要每位操作機器的人員個別接受 APS。

但在 MAP 中，操作者本身就是製程。每個人的手部動作、無菌技術、對污染的認識都不同。因此，資格驗證必須以個人為單位進行，而非以製程為單位。

製程 APS = 驗證製程能力
MAP APS = 驗證操作者能力

8.1

Simulation Design (8.1–8.1.4)

模擬設計

8.1 Simulation Design

The development of a supporting rationale for the design of the manual aseptic process simulation is essential. The rationale must define the adaptations to the production process necessary for the execution of the simulation. The smaller scale of the manual process lends itself to these adaptations and in many instances only minimal changes to the process are required.

8.1.1 Compositing/Assembly Activities

Compositing/assembly activities involve repetitive actions in which sterile materials in smaller amounts are pooled. Such practices are common in vaccine manufacturing in which the contents of incubated eggs are composited early in the formulation process.

Evaluation: For this category, adaptation of the validation methods for sterile filling and bulk materials may be appropriate for the simulation rationale.

8.1.2 Formulation/Compounding Activities

Formulation/compounding activities in a manual setting might use different equipment than automated processes due to the smaller scale of the operation. For example, laboratory glassware and utensils may be used to produce a sterile bulk formulation. The smaller scale of the operation may also mandate alternative transfer methods than those utilized in larger, automated operations.

Evaluation: The methods utilized for sterile bulk materials may be appropriate in these processes (References 1, 4, 15).

8.1.3 Filling/Subdivision Activities (Including Lyophilization if Needed)

Filling/subdivision activities involve repetitive actions in which sterile materials are transferred from a bulk container into smaller containers, sealed and closed (or partially closed when lyophilization is planned). This practice is common in early development and early clinical stage manufacturing of sterile products, and in the manufacture of extremely small lot sizes.

Evaluation: These activities may use methods defined for automated filling (References 1, 6).

8.1.4 Manual Manipulation Steps Performed in Conjunction with Other Processes

Manual activities, such as sampling, aseptic connection, etc., are usually an integral part of other aseptic processes. As such there is no need to address them independently in the rationale for the study design.

Evaluation: Manual activities performed in conjunction with automated steps are evaluated as part of the overall process simulation, not as standalone studies.

Additional details on process-specific evaluation methods are to be provided in conjunction with each of the elements addressed within the overall process evaluation protocol/procedure.

比喻：APS 設計如同消防演習

消防演習比喻

想像一場消防演習：如果只模擬「從一樓走出去」這個最簡單的情境，你永遠不知道消防員面對「十樓火災、電梯故障、走廊充煙」時能否應對。

MAP APS 的設計邏輯完全相同：模擬必須包含最複雜、最高風險的手動步驟。若只模擬簡單操作，無法確認操作者在最具挑戰性的情況下是否能維持無菌技術。

最差情境（Worst-case Simulation）原則：APS 應代表生產過程中最有可能引入污染的步驟，包括最長的操作時間、最多的人工介入次數、最複雜的容器組裝操作。

8.2–8.6

Media and Run Parameters

培養基與執行參數

8.2 Media Sterilization

The preparation of media for use in a MAP simulation is normally managed with few problems. If the media bulk container is small enough it can be sterilized in an autoclave prior to introduction into the process. In other cases non-sterile bulk media may be filtered into the process in a manner similar to the drug product.

Where possible, sterilization methods, such as autoclaving or irradiating, should be combined with filtration to remove mycoplasma contamination from media components (for a more detailed discussion, see PDA Technical Report No. 22). For compositing/assembly processes, suitable sterile materials to use in the process simulation may be unavailable. In such cases the choice may be to use the production materials themselves suitably sterilized/adapted (if possible and necessary) in the simulation, if they do not inhibit the growth of microorganisms. For the APS each of the liquid containers containing sterile materials for the process should have their contents replaced with media.

8.3 Frequency and Number of APS Runs

The recommendation for frequency and number of simulation studies (runs) for manual processing is that each operator (primary and secondary) should be qualified semi-annually (or in accordance with the applicable regulatory requirements). Typically, three replicate APS studies are performed to initially qualify an operator and process. The combined duration and size of the APS runs will support both operator and process qualification.

Note: Operator qualification is critical since the operators are the primary means of production. More frequent studies may be required for infrequent manufacturing operations.

8.3.1 Duration of Runs

Simulation studies should meet or exceed the expected maximum duration of a single working session by a single operator.

8.3.2 Size of Runs

The size of the process simulation is largely dictated by the time period that a single operator would remain performing the same activity. The actual numbers of units produced in that time period should meet or exceed the production quantity that the operator(s) would normally handle in that time period. As the operator is an integral part of the process and a key variable in MAP, it may be that each operator will have to perform the whole process simulation for the process to be considered valid especially where only small numbers are filled.

8.4 Observation of the Process Simulation

Considerations for observation of MAP simulations are consistent with observations of automated simulations, as described in PDA Technical Report No. 22:

The process simulation should be observed to assure that all planned activities are properly executed and represent an appropriate challenge to the process capability. Observation may also be used to augment aseptic conduct and technique training. Observation should commence upon the initiation of the process simulation, including equipment set-up, and continue until the process simulation has completed. Observation of the simulation should be performed by individuals having the knowledge and competency to assess if operators have used proper aseptic conduct in their activities. The process simulation observation should be documented and/or video recorded.

8.5 Media Fill Volume

In aseptic filling simulations the amount of media transferred during the simulation should be sufficient to wet the product contact surfaces of the container and be sufficient to detect growth. In compositing simulations, the amount of media transferred should be identical to that normally handled in production to simulate the process duration more accurately. In manual manufacturing simulations, the volumes of media and other fluids (which should all be replaced with media) should be identical to, or greater than that in the process to be simulated. The goal is to assure that the sterile product components (e.g., containers, closures, and media) are exposed for the maximum length seen during routine manufacturing, thus assuring the simulation represents a comparable risk as routine processing.

8.6 Anaerobes/Inert Gassing

The methods utilized for automated, machine-based aseptic processing can be adopted without change. Air should be substituted for inert gases in all systems, except in those rare instances where an isolator providing true anaerobic conditions is utilized for the production process environment. In such situations, the usual inert gas would be utilized and the appropriate media would generally be fluid thioglycollate media (FTM) or alternative FTM (if filtration of the media is necessary).

8.5 — 培養基填充量計算原則

核心要求：培養基用量必須足以完全潤濕容器所有內部接觸面，並能夠偵測微生物生長。

最低填充量標準：

對於灌裝模擬：
  V_fill ≥ 潤濕所有內表面所需的最低量
  （通常為最大填充量的 1–3%，
   或最低絕對體積，例如小瓶 ≥ 3 mL）

對於調配/合池模擬：
  V_media = V_production
  （與實際生產操作體積相同，以確保
   模擬製程時間的準確性）

對於完整製程模擬：
  所有液體均應以培養基替代
  V_media ≥ V_process（等於或大於實際生產量）

目的：確保容器、封口件和培養基在 APS 中承受與實際生產相同的最長暴露時間，使模擬代表相應的污染風險。

8.6 — 厭氧菌與惰性氣體

大多數 MAP 情況下，可直接以空氣代替惰性氣體（如氮氣、氬氣）進行 APS，因為：

  • 一般環境微生物（需要偵測的主要污染源）大多為需氧菌
  • 空氣環境不影響主要污染風險的評估

例外情況：當生產製程在真正的厭氧隔離器中進行時，APS 應使用惰性氣體環境，並選用流體硫乙醇酸鹽培養基（FTM），以同時支持需氧菌和厭氧菌的生長。

8.7–8.11

Execution and Inspection

執行與檢查

8.7 Environmental Monitoring

Environmental monitoring for MAP uses methods generally the same as those for other highly controlled (ISO 5/7) environments. The same cautions exercised with monitoring in other systems apply to MAP as well. Sampling activities must not introduce contamination into the environment or into the sample.

Since the environmental systems utilized for MAP are of smaller size, the monitoring methods must be chosen for their lack of impact on the environment. A thorough understanding of the process should be used to ensure that areas of greatest risk are monitored at the appropriate frequency using the appropriate monitor (References 1, 2, 4, 6, 14).

8.8 Execution of the Simulation

The process simulation should be performed in a manner that properly documents the activities. A batch record designed specifically for the simulation should be used. The presence of an observer who documents the simulation is recommended. The methods and principles defined for automated filling or sterile bulk chemical production can be utilized with relatively minor modifications.

8.9 Pre-Incubation Inspection

The pre-incubation inspection methods utilized for automated, machine-based aseptic processing can be adopted without change. As the containers in MAP are more likely to be hand stoppered and/or sealed, and subject to more variability, pre-incubation inspection must be performed with the same rigor as product inspection. The same caution should be applied to inspection of hand-sealed production units for the same reasons.

Units with defective seals should be removed from the materials sent for incubation. Units with cosmetic defects should be treated as integral and included in the incubation.

Note: Generally, the number and type(s) of defective units should not exceed those generated during routine manufacturing.

8.10 Incubation Time/Temperature

The same incubation methods used for automated machine-based aseptic processing are appropriate for MAP and can be found in PDA Technical Report No. 22:

Prior to incubation, units should be inverted, and swirled or manipulated to ensure contact of the medium with all internal surfaces of the closure system before they are incubated. Process simulation units should be incubated for a minimum of 14 days unless supported by another qualified duration/method. The temperature chosen should be based upon its ability to recover microorganisms normally found environmentally or in the product bioburden. A single incubation temperature in the range of 20–35°C may be used. Data should be available to show the suitability of the selected incubation temperature to support growth. The selected temperature should be controlled and monitored continuously throughout the incubation period.

8.11 Post-Incubation Inspection

The incubation methods utilized for machine-based aseptic processing are appropriate for MAP and can be found in PDA Technical Report No. 22:

At the end of the incubation period, visual inspection of all units for growth is performed to determine the final results of the aseptic process simulation. The inspection process should be performed by trained inspectors who have demonstrated the ability to detect both low and high-level microbial growth patterns. Firms may choose to inspect units partway through the incubation period.

As the pre-incubation inspection is expected to remove any units with container/closure defects, if a positive unit is detected during the post-incubation inspection it must be appropriately investigated for cause and corrective action (Reference 6).

執行與檢查的關鍵概念

8.7 — MAP 環境監測的挑戰

MAP 環境監測的核心矛盾：監測本身不應成為污染源。在小型 UDAF 工作站或隔離器中，傳統的主動式空氣取樣設備可能因：

  • 設備體積佔用有限操作空間
  • 氣流擾動影響 ISO 5 環境
  • 操作員移動設備時破壞無菌屏障

因此，MAP 的 EM 方法需特別評估其對環境的影響。沉降皿（settling plates）常作為被動式取樣方法，可最小化對工作區氣流的干擾。

8.9 — 培養前檢查：關鍵決策點

培養前檢查（Pre-Incubation Inspection）的目的是：

  • 移除容器/封口缺陷的單位（密封不完整 → 排除在外）
  • 保留外觀缺陷的單位（凹陷、刮痕等 → 視為完整，送入培養）

為什麼重要？一旦缺陷單位被排除，培養後若出現陽性結果，就必然代表 APS 期間有污染發生，必須展開根本原因調查（CAPA）。培養前檢查為後續結果判讀設立了明確的基線。

MAP 容器通常為手動壓塞，密封一致性低於機器封口，因此培養前檢查的嚴格程度必須與正式產品放行檢查相當。

8.10 — 雙階段培養的理由

為何建議 14 天培養，且許多廠商採用「7+7 天雙溫度」方案？

不同微生物的最適生長溫度：

  • 20–25°C：環境腐生菌（saprophytes）、黴菌、酵母菌 — 來自環境的主要污染菌群
  • 30–35°C：人體共生菌（human flora）、葡萄球菌屬 — 來自操作者的主要污染菌群

7+7 雙階段培養方案：
第 1–7 天：20–25°C（偵測環境腐生菌）
第 8–14 天：30–35°C（偵測人體菌群）

TR62 允許單一溫度（20–35°C 範圍內），但需有數據支持所選溫度能充分支持微生物生長。雙階段方案能最大化偵測靈敏度。

培養後檢查流程

培養後視覺檢查（Post-Incubation Inspection）是 APS 最終結果的判定步驟：

  • 由受訓且具資格的檢查員執行
  • 需能辨識低濃度和高濃度的微生物生長模式
  • 混濁（turbidity）= 陽性（positive） = 污染事件
  • 可選擇在培養期中途進行中期檢查

任何陽性單位必須啟動正式 CAPA 調查，不得以任何理由直接排除陽性結果。

8.12–8.13

Microbiology: Growth Promotion and Interpretation

微生物學：生長促進與結果判讀

8.12 Growth Promotion

The methods and considerations relevant to automated machine-based aseptic processing are adopted without change as in PDA Technical Report No. 22:

The medium's growth properties should be evaluated using pharmacopeial methods, and the inclusion of environmental organisms or those isolated from sterility test positives may be beneficial. Growth promotion should be performed after 14 days of incubation.

Suggested pharmacopeial methods include USP <71> (Reference 1) and Ph. Eur. 2.6.1 (Reference 5).

8.13 Interpretation of Test Results

Production lots produced by MAP are typically smaller than the current standard minimum simulation size of 5,000 units. Thus MAP simulations conducted in support of container filling must have a contamination frequency of zero (0) filled units. In compositing or formulation simulations, the simulated bulk material container(s) should be sterile. Consistent with PDA Technical Report No. 22:

Regardless of the number of units filled during a process simulation or the number of positives allowed, the ultimate goal for the number of positives in any process simulation should be zero. A sterile product is, after all, one which contains no viable organisms.

There are, however, numerous technical problems in achieving this goal. Media and simulated product do not match real products perfectly in terms of their processing characteristics and microbiological growth-support properties. Media differ in many respects from the products they are intended to simulate; for example, there are differences in solubility, pH, filtration rates and filterability and viscosity. With powdered products, the process simulation involves reconstituting powdered media or simulated product, introducing extra processing equipment or manipulation, with the inherent risk of contamination. Since a microbiological medium is designed specifically to support or stimulate the growth of microorganisms, it is a more rigorous challenge than processed products, which often provide neutral and sometimes hostile microbial growth environments (Reference 6).

Although Technical Report No. 22 makes provision for some low number other than zero, in the case of manual aseptic processing this criterion must be set to zero due to the manual nature of the operation. The assumptions used for low numerical acceptance are not applicable for manual aseptic processing operations.

8.12 — 生長促進試驗：為什麼必須做？

生長促進試驗（Growth Promotion Testing）是 APS 有效性的前提條件：

邏輯鏈：

  • 如果培養基沒有陽性結果，有兩種可能：
  • (A) 操作者的無菌技術完美，沒有引入污染
  • (B) 培養基根本無法支持微生物生長，即使有污染也偵測不到

生長促進試驗排除可能性 (B)，確認培養基確實能支持微生物生長，從而使「無陽性」的結果具有真正的科學意義。

方法：培養 14 天後，向部分 APS 單位中接種已知微生物，確認培養基能夠偵測該微生物的生長。

8.13 — 零污染標準：MAP 與自動化的關鍵差異

自動化 APS（TR22）：
最低 5,000 單位，允許統計學接受標準（例如：5,000 單位中允許 0 個陽性，即污染率 <0.1%）。這基於大量填充的統計推斷邏輯。

MAP APS（TR62）：
必須為零（0）污染單位，不論填充的單位數量多少。

為什麼標準更嚴格？

  • MAP 批量通常遠小於 5,000 單位（常見為數十至數百單位）
  • 統計學接受標準的前提（大樣本量）不適用
  • 任何污染對小批量產品的影響比例更高
  • 人工操作本質上要求更高的個人無菌技術標準

直覺理解：為何 MAP 不能用統計容忍？

想像一個 5,000 支注射劑的批次，其中 1 支受污染（0.02%）：統計上很低，可接受。

但如果是一個只有 50 支的 MAP 批次，其中 1 支受污染（2%）：這意味著幾乎每 50 位接受治療的患者中，就有 1 位可能接受到受污染的藥品。對臨床患者而言，這是完全不可接受的風險。

TR62 的零容忍標準反映了 MAP 通常用於臨床試驗、罕見病或個人化治療產品的現實——這些患者往往已非常脆弱，感染的後果可能是致命的。

9.0

Conclusion

結論

9.0 Conclusion

This technical report is one of the first attempts to address the subject of manual aseptic processing in a comprehensive manner. As such, it includes recommendations that may establish recommended practices in the absence of guidance from regulators or industry organizations.

Manufacturers that use manual aseptic processing to produce products used in patients must be aware of the uncertainties associated with a manufacturing process so heavily reliant on personnel performance.

In new installations, we strongly encourage the use of isolation technology to minimize the risk of microbial contamination from personnel involved in the manufacturing operations.

結論解析

TR62 結論的三個層次

第一層（現實認知）：TR62 是業界第一份全面性處理 MAP 主題的文件。在許多監管指導原則中，MAP 仍是模糊地帶——TR62 的建議在缺乏明確法規指導的情況下，本身就成為業界實踐標準。

第二層（風險認知）：廠商必須清醒認識到，高度依賴人員表現的製程（MAP）本質上存在不確定性。這種不確定性無法完全消除，只能透過嚴格的訓練、資格驗證和 APS 計畫來管理。

第三層（技術方向）：隔離技術（Isolation Technology，即隔離器 Isolator）代表了最優先的技術發展方向，因為它能最大程度地將人員因素從無菌環境中移除。這是 TR62 對整個行業的明確建議。

監管定位

TR62 的獨特地位：它不是法規（regulation），也不是指導文件（guidance），而是業界共識（industry consensus）。然而在 FDA 查廠或歐盟 GMP 檢查中，TR62 的建議常被援引作為判斷 MAP 實踐是否符合「current best practices」的參考依據。

這意味著：在缺乏明確法規要求的情況下，不遵循 TR62 的建議可能需要廠商提供充分的科學理由，否則在查廠時面臨觀察意見的風險。

Application Scenario: CDMO Clinical MAP Qualification

應用情境：CDMO 臨床 MAP 資格驗證案例

Scenario Context

A CDMO is contracted to support a Phase I clinical trial for a novel peptide drug product supplied as a lyophilized powder in vials. The clinical pharmacist at the hospital site will perform manual reconstitution of the lyophilized cake using sterile water for injection (WFI) inside a laminar airflow workstation (ISO 5 UDAF hood) located in the pharmacy cleanroom (ISO 7 background). The CDMO must design and qualify this manual operation in accordance with TR62 before the first patient dose.

TR62-Required APS Design Elements

  • Activity Category (8.1): This falls under 8.1.3 (Filling/Subdivision — reconstitution from powdered product) and 8.1.2 (Formulation/Compounding — pH check, volume adjustment)
  • Simulation Medium (8.2): TSB (tryptic soy broth) used instead of WFI; lyophilized media cake reconstituted with TSB to mirror the production process
  • Initial Qualification (8.3): The clinical pharmacist must complete 3 consecutive successful APS runs before any patient dose is prepared
  • Run Duration (8.3.1): Each APS must cover the full expected reconstitution session duration (e.g., if a patient session involves 3 vials over 45 minutes, the APS must cover ≥45 minutes)
  • Fill Volume (8.5): TSB volume must wet all internal vial surfaces; typically 1–3 mL per vial minimum
  • Observation (8.4): A qualified observer (e.g., QA or pharmacy supervisor) must observe and document the full APS, ideally with video recording
  • Incubation (8.10): Minimum 14 days; dual-phase 7+7 days at 20–25°C / 30–35°C recommended to capture both environmental saprophytes and human flora
  • Acceptance Criterion (8.13): Zero contaminated vials — any positive result invalidates the APS and requires CAPA before patient dosing
  • Re-qualification (8.3): Semi-annually, or if the pharmacist is absent for >6 months, or if any process parameter changes

CDMO 案例：臨床凍乾藥品手動再溶解的 APS 設計

案例背景

CDMO 承接一個 Phase I 臨床試驗的支援任務。醫院臨床藥師需在 ISO 5 UDAF 工作站內，手動對凍乾藥品小瓶進行再溶解（reconstitution），然後準備患者注射劑量。

在首位患者接受治療前，CDMO 必須依照 TR62 完成藥師的 MAP APS 資格驗證。

TR62 APS 設計要點摘要

  • 活動類別：8.1.3（分裝/再溶解）+ 8.1.2（調配確認）
  • 模擬培養基：以 TSB 替代 WFI 重新溶解凍乾培養基餅
  • 初始驗證：藥師需完成 3 次連續成功的 APS
  • 執行時間：每次 APS 時間 ≥ 實際最長操作時段（例如 ≥45 分鐘）
  • 填充量：TSB 足以潤濕所有小瓶內壁（≥1–3 mL/瓶）
  • 觀察記錄：合格觀察員全程記錄，建議錄影
  • 培養條件：14 天，建議雙階段 7+7 天（20–25°C / 30–35°C）
  • 接受標準：零污染小瓶（任何陽性均需 CAPA）
  • 定期再驗證：每半年一次，或有重大變更/長期缺席後

CDMO 的關鍵監管考量

在臨床試驗環境中，MAP APS 的文件記錄具有雙重意義：

  • GMP 合規：向 FDA/EMA 證明臨床藥品的無菌性保障
  • 患者安全：Phase I 患者往往是首次接受此新藥的人體試驗受試者，任何污染事件的後果無法容忍

CDMO 應在臨床試驗協議（Clinical Trial Agreement）中明確 MAP APS 的責任歸屬，確保醫院藥局的操作符合 TR62 的要求，並保存所有 APS 記錄供監管查閱。

Q&A

Practice Questions / 練習題

自我評量

練習題 1：APS 設計（Section 8.1）

一家 CDMO 需要對一個新操作進行 MAP APS 設計，該操作包含以下步驟：（a）將三種無菌原料藥液分別從獨立容器抽取並混合進入主容器；（b）手動調整 pH 值；（c）以手動注射器將混合液灌裝至 10 支西林瓶；（d）手動壓塞。

問題：根據 TR62 Section 8.1，這些步驟應歸類為哪些 MAP 活動類別？APS 設計應如何確保涵蓋「最差情境」操作？

參考答案方向：（a）屬 8.1.1 複合/組裝；（b）屬 8.1.2 調配；（c）（d）屬 8.1.3 灌裝/分裝。最差情境應包含最長操作時間、最多的手部介入次數、在三種原料的最大批量規模下執行。

練習題 2：執行頻率要求（Section 8.3）

某家醫院藥局有 4 名藥師輪流執行 MAP（手動無菌調配）。其中藥師 A 已完成初始資格驗證（3 次成功 APS），上次 APS 執行日期為去年 5 月。藥師 B 是新加入的人員，尚未完成任何 APS。藥師 C 上次 APS 為 4 個月前，近期無重大變更。藥師 D 上次 APS 為 8 個月前。

問題：根據 TR62，今天（11 月）哪些藥師可以立即執行 MAP？哪些需要先完成 APS 再執行？各需要幾次 APS？

參考答案方向：藥師 A：距上次 APS 已達 6 個月（5 月至 11 月），需進行 1 次再資格驗證 APS 才能繼續執行。藥師 B：需完成初始 3 次連續成功 APS。藥師 C：距 4 個月，尚在半年期限內，可繼續執行。藥師 D：距 8 個月，已超過半年，需進行再資格驗證 APS。

練習題 3：接受標準（Section 8.13）

一次 MAP APS 共灌裝了 150 支小瓶（TSB）。培養 14 天後，後培養檢查發現 1 支小瓶呈現混濁（turbidity）。查詢記錄發現，培養前檢查時已確認該小瓶密封完整（無缺陷），混濁在第 7 天中期檢查時即已可見。

問題：（1）根據 TR62 Section 8.13，此次 APS 結果應如何判定？（2）如果這是自動化灌裝的 APS（150 單位），結果判定是否相同？（3）需要採取哪些後續行動？

參考答案方向：（1）MAP APS：結果失敗（fail）。TR62 要求零污染，1 支陽性即構成失敗，因為混濁單位的密封已在培養前驗證完整，排除了容器問題，污染必來自操作。（2）自動化 APS：150 單位遠小於 TR22 的最低 5,000 單位標準；在 5,000 單位的情況下，理論上存在統計學接受窗口，但 150 單位不足以應用統計學接受標準。（3）需立即啟動 CAPA：偏差調查、根本原因分析、操作員再培訓、重新執行 3 次成功 APS 後方可恢復 MAP 操作。

Key Takeaways from TR62 Sections 8–11

  • Operator-centric qualification: Unlike automated APS where the process is qualified, MAP APS qualifies the individual operator. Each person performing MAP must have their own APS record.
  • Worst-case simulation design: APS must include the most complex, highest-risk manual operations across all four activity categories (8.1.1–8.1.4).
  • Run frequency: Initial qualification requires 3 consecutive successful runs per operator; re-qualification is required at least semi-annually or upon triggering events.
  • Incubation: Minimum 14 days; 20–35°C; dual-phase (7+7 days) recommended for maximum recovery of both environmental and human-source organisms.
  • Zero contamination: TR62 mandates a zero contamination acceptance criterion for MAP APS due to the small batch sizes typical of manual operations — statistical tolerance models do not apply.
  • Growth promotion: Must be performed after 14 days of incubation using pharmacopeial methods (USP <71> or Ph. Eur. 2.6.1) to validate medium performance.
  • Conclusion: TR62 strongly encourages the use of isolation technology for new MAP installations to minimize the inherent risk of personnel-dependent sterility assurance.
