Introduction — 導論

原文內容 Original Text

Cell and gene therapies are classes of advanced therapy medicinal products (ATMP) that include numerous different types of products including nucleic acids, viruses, genetically modified microorganisms, and others. Gene therapies are relatively novel technologies that tend to have extremely complex and not well-understood mechanisms of action but hold great potential for treating a variety of serious diseases, unmet medical needs, or simplification of administration and treatment (i.e., "one shot" gene therapy treatments versus a lifetime of chronic treatment for many diseases). The potential applications are diverse and numerous and may reach far beyond any current forms of therapies in terms of disease-state targets, as illustrated by the impact of the handful of currently approved successes. Notable, as well, are the abundant early- to late-stage products currently in clinical development and the anticipation of many more to come.

One technology that has shown immense promise within the ATMP space is the use of viral vectors for delivery of a therapeutic gene. Viral vectors have been shown to be useful both in the direct administration of in vivo treatment to patients and in the ex vivo genetic modification of cells that are then administered to the patient. Different viral-vector types have proven effective as gene delivery systems including, but not limited to, adeno-associated viruses (AAV) and lentiviruses, both of which have successfully commercialized products. AAV-based drug products, in particular, have been commercialized successfully in the United States, Europe, and other regions of the world.

Plasmid DNA (pDNA) are used as critical materials (i.e., the "building blocks") in the production of viral vectors and may also be delivered as naked DNA or encapsulated (e.g., in a liposome) for delivery purposes at various steps of a gene-therapy manufacturing process. Thus, ensuring consistent control of the quality in the production and use of plasmids is of great importance in the manufacture of many gene therapies, including cell-based, gene-modified therapies.

The ex vivo modification of cells was not within the primary scope of PDA Technical Report No. 81: Cell-Based Therapy Control Strategy (TR 81), therefore, some points to consider on viral vectors used in gene therapy were deemed to be of value and are addressed herein. In developing this points to consider document, the intent was to not repeat information already available in TR 81, but rather to supplement it. Likewise, when topics such as process validation, sterile filtration, filtration for viral contaminants, analytical method validation, and phase-appropriate GMPs are addressed in this document, these topics should be deemed supplemental to guidance that can be found in existing PDA technical reports.

A team of industry representatives from multiple CMC disciplines comprising product and process development, analytical development, manufacturing, quality assurance, and regulatory affairs was assembled by the PDA ATMP Advisory Board to author this document. This team was tasked not only with identifying the current major problems in the development, qualification, and manufacture of viral vectors that are not adequately addressed in current regulatory guidance, but also with recommending solutions to these problems based on current industry perspectives and best practices with consideration to compliance within a dynamic, global regulatory framework.

This document presents the views of the Parenteral Drug Association on the development, classification, manufacture, control, and testing of plasmids and vectors used in ATMP production. It is designed to encourage further dialog with industry, health authorities, and suppliers of technology and materials while taking into consideration the changes and needs of the modern, global, sterile healthcare-product manufacturing industry. It does not represent a standard or a regulatory guidance.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 什麼是 ATMP？

先進療法醫藥品 (Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products, ATMP) 是歐盟法規框架（EC Regulation 1394/2007）所定義的三大類特殊治療品的總稱：

  • 基因治療藥品 (Gene Therapy Medicinal Products, GTMP)：透過引入治療性基因序列，修正、替換、或抑制異常基因功能。
  • 體細胞治療藥品 (Somatic Cell Therapy Medicinal Products, SCTMP)：使用在體外經過操控的活細胞或組織作為治療劑。
  • 組織工程製品 (Tissue Engineered Products, TEP)：含有或由細胞/組織構成，具再生、修復或替換人體組織功能。

PtC-11 聚焦於 GTMP 中使用病毒載體 (viral vectors) 和質體 DNA (plasmid DNA, pDNA) 的部分。

病毒載體與質體 DNA 的關係：質體 DNA 是製造病毒載體的「原料」（building blocks），就像零件之於成品機器。pDNA 先在細菌細胞中大量擴增，再被引入哺乳動物細胞中，幫助組裝出完整的病毒載體顆粒。因此，pDNA 的品質直接影響最終病毒載體（即藥物活性成分）的品質。

比喻說明 — 病毒載體：治療基因的「快遞員」

想像你需要把一封重要的信（治療性基因）送進城堡（患者的細胞）裡。城堡的城牆（細胞膜）非常堅固，普通方法根本進不去。這時候你需要一位特殊的「信使」——他不僅能穿過城牆，還能確保信件被正確交到收件人手中。

病毒載體就是這個「信使快遞員」。大自然演化出的病毒，天生就有感染細胞的能力。科學家將病毒改造後，移除其致病的危險基因，保留其「進入細胞」的能力，再裝入治療性基因，就形成了病毒載體。

質體 DNA 則像是快遞公司的「生產指令手冊」——用來製造這些快遞員的藍圖。品質差的手冊，會導致製造出瑕疵品快遞員，無法準確送達信件。

AAV（腺相關病毒）擅長進入神經、肌肉等非分裂細胞；Lentivirus（慢病毒）則能整合入分裂中的免疫細胞（如 T 細胞），特別適合製造 CAR-T 細胞療法。

實務應用 — CDMO 視角：為何 ATMP 製造能力攸關策略地位？

ATMP 市場正在快速成長。截至 2025 年，全球已有超過 30 個 ATMP 產品獲批，臨床管線中有逾千個候選品。對於 CDMO 而言，搶先建立 ATMP 製造能力具有顯著的商業意義：

  • 高門檻即護城河：病毒載體製造技術複雜（需 BSL-2 生物安全等級廠房、懸浮細胞培養、超速離心純化），並非所有 CDMO 都有能力承接，先進入者享有稀缺溢價。
  • 黏性高：ATMP 客戶一旦選定 CDMO，往往難以輕易轉換（技術轉移成本高、法規申請繁複），長期訂單比率高於小分子藥品。
  • 監管風險需主動管理：pDNA 和病毒載體的分類（raw material vs. drug substance）在不同法規轄區有差異（後續 Topic 1 深度討論），CDMO 若未能理解這些差異，可能為客戶帶來不必要的法規風險。
  • TR 81 ＋ PtC-11 是雙軌必讀：熟悉兩份文件的 CMC 人員，能為客戶提供更完整的策略建議，是差異化競爭的優勢來源。
Scope — 適用範圍

原文內容 Original Text

This Points to Consider document is focused on the use of viral vectors for the purpose of the delivery of a therapeutic gene both in vivo and for ex vivo modification within the overall classification of ATMPs. The topics addressed are diverse and include plasmid and viral vector categorization and control, quality by design (QbD), control strategies, filtration, comparability, platform technologies, and potency assays. Separately and together, these topics address a broad range of development, manufacture, and control issues that may be encountered in industry. The topics are organized in a question-and-answer format, presenting a specific problem statement and an accompanying recommendation, inclusive of the recommended points to be considered with the goal to assist the reader in formulating a strategy to address the topic.

One concept that emerges throughout these discussions is the need for applying a science-driven and risk-based approach to providing solutions for current problems associated with the development and manufacture of viral vectors. That approach is embraced by regulatory agencies for the development of gene therapies, including those using viral vectors, as presented in the EU GMP Annex 1: Manufacture of Sterile Medicinal Products and the Guideline on the Risk-based Approach according to Annex I, Part IV of Directive 2001/83/EC (1). Annex I, Part IV specifically refers to a risk-based approach as the recommended methodology to design the organizational, technical, and structural measures to comply with GMP regulations. PIC/S Annex 2A: Manufacture of Advanced Therapy Medicinal Products for Human Use echoes the EU guidelines, noting that quality risk management principles are particularly important for this class of materials (2).

"In summary, the topics presented in this Points to Consider reflect the current thinking regarding some of the major challenges that have been identified by the PDA community regarding the development, control, and manufacture of gene therapy products using viral vectors. Nonetheless, this team acknowledges that there are many more topics that the industry faces for this diverse class of therapies."

比喻說明 — In Vivo vs. Ex Vivo：兩種「投遞策略」

基因治療的投遞方式，可類比為兩種截然不同的物流模型：

In Vivo（體內基因治療）——「直接快遞到戶」：病毒載體直接注射進患者體內，如同包裹直送到收件人家門口。載體在患者體內自行尋找目標細胞並完成基因轉送。AAV 是最常見的體內基因治療載體（例如治療脊髓性肌肉萎縮症的 Zolgensma）。

Ex Vivo（體外基因修飾）——「工廠加工後再出貨」：先從患者身上抽取細胞（如 T 細胞），在體外實驗室中用病毒載體修改細胞的基因，確認加工品質後，再回輸到患者體內。CAR-T 細胞療法（如 Kymriah、Yescarta）就是典型的 ex vivo 模型。慢病毒 (Lentivirus) 是最常用的 ex vivo 改造載體，因為它能將目標基因整合進分裂中的 T 細胞基因組。

PtC-11 的範疇同時涵蓋這兩種模型，因此內容對不同 ATMP 業務類型的 CDMO 皆具參考價值。

重點提示 — 科學驅動、風險為本：貫穿全文的核心哲學

「Science-driven and risk-based approach（科學驅動、風險為本的方法）」是 PtC-11 反覆強調的核心思路，也是當前所有先進療法法規指引的共同基調：

  • EU GMP Annex 1（無菌藥品製造）和其附屬的風險為本方法指引，要求廠商從科學依據設計組織性、技術性和結構性的 GMP 合規措施。
  • PIC/S Annex 2A（ATMP 製造）特別指出，品質風險管理 (Quality Risk Management, QRM) 原則對這類材料尤為重要，直接呼應 ICH Q9。
  • 對 CDMO 的實務意義：當監管指引無法提供明確答案時（ATMP 領域常有此情況），風險評估文件（risk assessment）本身就是合規的基礎。能夠清楚記錄「為什麼這樣做是科學合理的」，比單純遵循固定程序更為重要。

Q 1.1 — Classification Framework for Plasmids and Viral Vectors

原文內容 Original Text

Plasmid and viral vectors (i.e., vectors that consist of or are derived from biological material designed to deliver genetic material) express the desired gene product that is introduced into the ATMP manufacturing process at different stages of production. In some cases, the purified genetic material (i.e., plasmid or vector) is administered directly to a patient whereas in other cases, the genetic material, most commonly a viral vector, is used to genetically modify the cells ex vivo as part of the cell-therapy manufacturing process. This leads to a situation wherein similar material can be classified as a raw material, starting material, critical starting material, or an active substance (drug substance or active pharmaceutical ingredient) with each governed by different GMP requirements within different regulatory frameworks. The regional regulatory jurisdiction(s) will dictate the classification requirement, and therefore, the governing GMPs.

A starting material may be deemed a critical starting material depending on an assessment of risk and the particular regulatory jurisdiction. A critical classification may drive tighter controls within the quality system. For example, in the European Economic Area, a viral vector that is used to modify the cells ex vivo will be considered a critical starting material and follow EU EudraLex - Volume 4 - Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) Guidelines, while in the United States, it may be considered a drug substance (3). In some jurisdictions, such as Australia, the difference is even more significant: if the vector is administered in vivo to the patient, it will be considered a medicine and follow the PIC/S code of GMP PE-009 (which is largely aligned with EU Volume IV with part 4 being a notable exception) (4). However, if the same viral vector is used to modify the cells ex vivo, it will be considered a critical starting material and is regulated by a completely different code of GMPs (refer to the Australian Code of Good Manufacturing Practice for Human Blood and Blood Components, Human Tissues and Human Cellular Therapy Products) (5).

Considering that various regulatory agencies have different GMP and CMC requirements, it is extremely important to be aware of these nuances to determine the correct classification for each material used during manufacturing in order to develop the appropriate control strategy.

While it is not possible to provide a comprehensive framework for categorizing vectors and plasmids, as there are too many jurisdictions with varying requirements, it is possible to show approaches that would facilitate classification of the material, establish the CMC and GMP requirements, and build an appropriate control strategy. That approach can then be adapted for other jurisdictions.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Starting Material（起始物料）：製程中所使用的原料，在進一步加工後成為藥物物質或最終產品的一部分，本身不直接給予患者。

Critical Starting Material（關鍵起始物料）：起始物料中，因其對最終產品安全性、有效性或品質有重大影響，而被界定為需要加強管控的特殊類別。

Drug Substance / Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient（藥物物質 / 活性醫藥成分）：即直接構成藥物活性部分的物質，必須符合最高等級 GMP 要求，並作為獨立的 CMC 申報章節提交。

In vivo gene therapy（活體基因治療）：將病毒載體直接注射至患者體內，載體本身即為藥物物質。

Ex vivo genetic modification（體外基因修飾）：先在體外用病毒載體修飾患者細胞，再將修飾後的細胞回輸患者。此情境下，FDA 視載體為藥物物質；EMA 視之為關鍵起始物料。

比喻說明 Analogy

想像一間米其林餐廳。同樣的松露（病毒載體），若直接擺盤上桌供客人食用，它就是「主菜」（藥物物質，須符合最嚴格的食品安全規範）；但若廚師先用松露來烹調醬汁，再將醬汁淋在主菜上——松露就只是「食材」（起始物料）。

更麻煩的是：同一顆松露，在巴黎（EU）的廚房裡算「特級食材」（關鍵起始物料），但在紐約（FDA）的廚房裡直接算「主菜」（藥物物質）。廚師（CDMO）必須根據餐廳所在城市的衛生法規，決定這顆松露需要哪些處理程序和文件。

  • 美國客戶：載體必須按 Drug Substance GMP 生產，需要完整的批次記錄、藥物物質規格書與 DS 章節 IND 申報

  • 澳洲客戶：in vivo 與 ex vivo 用途的同一載體，適用完全不同的法規框架

For mRNA- or DNA-based in vivo gene therapies, which are directly administered to patients, the mRNA or DNA used for injection is the active drug substance(s), and the formulated, filled product in the primary container is the drug product. In these situations, the starting materials include the plasmid used at the start of the drug-substance manufacturing process and the bacterial master or working cell bank that is used to expand the plasmid prior to routine drug-substance manufacturing.

GMP requirements for ATMP active substances (drug substances) and ATMP finished products are described in Part IV of the GMP Guide. Some requirements of Part IV can also be relevant for the starting materials.

However, the main differences with regard to the starting materials are:
• Not all GMP aspects described in Part IV of the GMP Guide are required.
• According to the current legal framework, neither recurring inspections nor GMP certifications by the responsible authorities are required.

實務應用 — 同時服務美國與歐盟客戶的 CDMO 策略

原則：永遠將病毒載體視為藥物物質（Drug Substance）來製造。

具體做法：

  • 按 FDA CGMP（21 CFR Parts 210/211）規格建立並維護所有生產記錄
  • 為每個載體建立獨立的藥物物質規格書（DS Specification），包含 identity、purity、strength、quality 的完整測試清單
  • 在 IND 申請中提交獨立的 DS CMC 章節（符合 FDA 要求）
  • 同一套文件將自動滿足 EMA 對「關鍵起始物料」的要求（EMA 要求僅為 FDA 要求的子集）
  • 儘早建立 Master Virus Seed Stock 和 Master Cell Bank，以符合歐盟日益嚴格的早期臨床要求

這個策略節省的不只是合規成本，更重要的是避免了因重新製造種子庫而導致的臨床試驗延遲（可能耗時 6–18 個月）。

重點提示 — EMA 的「責任轉移」邏輯

EMA 的設計邏輯值得深入理解：由於歐盟法規目前不要求對起始物料製造商（如 CDMO）進行定期官方稽核或頒發 GMP 認證，因此監管機關將這個責任轉移給 ATMP 製造商（即 CDMO 的客戶）：

  • 客戶（持有許可證的 ATMP 製造商）必須對 CDMO 進行供應商資格審核
  • 客戶必須透過 Quality Agreement 界定雙方責任
  • 客戶的稽核結果和起始物料品質，將在 CTA（臨床試驗申請）和 GMP 稽查中被審查

這表示：即使 CDMO 自認 GMP 合規，若客戶未建立適當的供應商管理程序，兩者都可能在 EMA 稽查中被標記為缺失。

Q 1.4 — GMP and Regulatory Requirements for Vectors as Drug Substances and Starting Materials

原文內容 Original Text

The FDA CMC guidance states that:

"You should describe whether the vector DS will be formulated into the DP for administration of the genetic material (section V.B.3.b., 'Batch Formula (3.2.P.3.2),' of this guidance) or whether it will be formulated as a bulk DS for ex vivo genetic modification of cells (as outlined in section IV.B., 'Drug Substance and Drug Product,' of this guidance). As an active pharmaceutical ingredient, an appropriate level of control should be applied to each DS, and each DS should be manufactured under current good manufacturing practice (CGMP) conditions that are appropriate for the stage of development (Ref. 11) (7)."

Therefore, clearly the FDA considers the vector an active pharmaceutical ingredient, equivalent to a drug substance, for which phase-appropriate GMP are required.

The EMA provides a more explicit list of factors to include in the risk-based analysis of the starting-material manufacturing which may be useful to consider for this question. These considerations apply to any vector manufacturing process, whether performed by the sponsor or by a contract manufacturing organization. Question 5 of the EMA Q&A directs that consideration should be given to (6):

  • Cross-contamination with other vectors or genetic material
  • Adventitious agents, for example:
    • Transmissible spongiform encephalopathy (TSE) such as Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease from human donors, or bovine spongiform encephalopathy from bovine serum raw materials
    • Viral contamination or recombination into replication-competent virus
  • Microbiological controls for sterility, mycoplasma, or endotoxin
  • Impurities:
    • Originating from raw materials added to the process (e.g., antibiotics)
    • Generated as part of the process (e.g., addition of transfection enhancers)
    • Carried over into the process (e.g., residual plasmids that are carried over from the vector production into the cell-therapy process)
  • Environmental controls
  • Storage and transportation conditions, including supply-chain complexity
  • Stability

The overall process must be taken into consideration when evaluating the starting materials for any active drug substance that is similar in size to, or larger than, a small virus. This is equally true for a directly administered gene therapy as it is for a viral vector used in ex vivo genetic modification.

For these active substances, removing all risk from adventitious viruses (e.g., parvovirus) from the manufacturing process is not possible and therefore, the adventitious-agent control strategy for the starting material is paramount. While both the FDA and EMA accept animal- and human-derived raw materials (e.g., bovine or human albumin serum), they should be avoided where possible or be gamma-irradiated, unless that process impacts the product quality.

In another example, human-derived raw materials used to manufacture starting materials may have different donor-screening requirements depending on jurisdiction. Sponsors and the ATMP manufacturer should pay special attention to the donor location, screening, testing, and pool size, as well as viral removal and inactivation steps during processing. For example, additional testing may be required for the raw material itself or a negative test may be required from the donor. The control strategy should again address the most stringent requirements from the intended jurisdictions.

As stated in the FDA CMC guidance, "As the sponsor of the IND, you are ultimately responsible for the safety of subjects in the clinical investigation (21 CFR 312.3); therefore, we recommend that you and the [Contract Manufacturing Organization] CMO understand and document your respective responsibilities for ensuring product quality" (7).

To ensure the safety of subjects, it is imperative that the sponsor receive all necessary information and documentation from the CMO required to complete the IND sections relevant to the manufacturing process to properly assess and control product quality and safety. The responsibilities for this knowledge transfer should be delineated within the context of a Quality Agreement to ensure that each organization involved in manufacturing is aware of their activities and how these activities will be conducted in compliance with GMPs. The 2016 FDA Guidance for Industry: Contract Manufacturing Arrangements for Drugs: Quality Agreements provides a framework for establishing a Quality Agreement and includes examples of how quality management principles can be used to aid in the management of a contract manufacturing relationship (8).

核心概念解析 — EMA Q&A Question 5 風險分析七大因素

EMA Q&A 第 5 問要求製造商針對起始物料製造進行風險評估，涵蓋以下七大面向：

  • 交叉污染（Cross-contamination）：與其他載體或基因物質的交叉污染，多產品設施尤需嚴控
  • 外源性因子（Adventitious agents）：包括 TSE（如牛海綿狀腦病 BSE、庫賈氏症 CJD）和病毒污染（包括形成複製能力病毒 RCV 的風險）
  • 微生物管控（Microbiological controls）：無菌性、黴漿菌、內毒素的測試與管控
  • 雜質（Impurities）：來自原料（如抗生素殘留）、製程（如轉染增強劑）或攜帶進入的雜質（如殘留質體）
  • 環境管控（Environmental controls）：潔淨室等級、人員管控、製程環境監測
  • 儲存與運輸（Storage and transportation）：包括供應鏈複雜度，冷鏈管理尤為關鍵
  • 穩定性（Stability）：起始物料的穩定性數據支持有效期設定

重點提示 — 外源性因子：無法完全消除的風險

病毒載體製程面臨的最獨特挑戰是：外源性病毒風險（adventitious agents）無法被完全消除。

原因如下：

  • 病毒載體本身就是生物性大分子，無法採用終端滅菌（terminal sterilization）——高溫或輻射會破壞載體功能
  • 製程中常用的動物來源原料（牛血清白蛋白、人類白蛋白）可能攜帶潛在病毒或 TSE 病原
  • Parvovirus（細小病毒）等小型病毒對常見的病毒清除步驟（如奈米過濾）具有高度抗性
  • 病毒重組可能在生產過程中產生 Replication-Competent Virus（RCV，具複製能力病毒），這是基因治療的重大安全風險

因此，外源性因子管控策略（Adventitious Agent Control Strategy）必須採取多層次防禦：原料篩選 → 供應商資格確認 → 製程內測試 → 最終產品測試。任何單一層次的失敗都可能造成嚴重的患者安全問題。

實務應用 — Quality Agreement 委外生產必備要素

當 CDMO 為 ATMP 製造商生產病毒載體時，Quality Agreement（品質協議）是法規要求與商業關係的核心文件。以下是必須涵蓋的關鍵條款：

  • 責任分工：明確界定誰負責原料采購、批次放行、異常調查、變更管控、穩定性研究
  • 文件提供義務：CDMO 必須提供 IND CMC 章節所需的所有批次記錄、測試結果、設備確認報告
  • 外源性因子管控：明確外源性因子測試項目、方法、結果判定標準與不合格品處理流程
  • 交叉污染防控：CDMO 的清潔驗證、換產程序必須對客戶開放稽核
  • 變更通知：CDMO 任何影響產品品質的製程或原料變更，必須在實施前通知客戶（通知期通常為 90 天以上）
  • 稽核權：客戶有權對 CDMO 進行定期 GMP 稽核（通常每年一次）
  • 多國法規合規聲明：CDMO 應聲明其製程符合客戶所列目標市場的監管要求

未簽訂完善 Quality Agreement 即開始委外生產，是 FDA 483 觀察項和 EMA GMP 稽查最常見的缺失類型之一。

比喻說明 — Phase-Appropriate GMP

Phase-appropriate GMP（分階段適用的 GMP）就像一個學生的成長歷程：

  • 臨床一期（Phase I）：就像剛升大學——老師對學生的報告格式相對寬鬆，但基本學術誠信（無菌性、安全性、身份確認）不能打折扣
  • 臨床二/三期（Phase II/III）：就像研究所——要求更嚴格的論文格式、更詳細的方法論（製程驗證、系統性管控）
  • 上市後（Commercial）：就像進入職場——每份「報告」（批次）都必須符合最完整的專業標準，任何偏差都必須書面說明

但注意：無論哪個階段，「真實性」（Identity）和「患者安全」（Safety）是不可妥協的底線，就像學術誠信貫穿整個求學生涯。

Q 1.5 — Special Considerations for Classification of Plasmids

原文內容 Original Text

Plasmids are critical raw materials for most gene-therapy-based ATMPs, as shown in Figure 1.3-1. It is imperative that the plasmid contains the desired transgene and associated genetic elements (e.g., promoters) which have the correct nucleotide sequence since any mutation, deletion, or insertion could significantly impact the expressed protein sequence, function, or expression level. This is true for both DNA-containing therapies and mRNA therapies, where the plasmid is reverse-transcribed into the mRNA.

Assessing the classification of plasmid DNA is also important in the context of viral vectors (either for direct injection as a gene therapy or for ex vivo genetic modification of cells) as the viral-vector manufacturing process may use three or more plasmids to mitigate the risk of recombination that could form a replication-competent virus. As examples, AAV- and lentivirus-based vectors have a common manufacturing approach that entails the co-transfection of host cells using multiple plasmids to separately encode the gene of interest, the viral structural proteins, and the viral helper genes required to assemble the viral vectors. As a result of the multiple plasmids, there are multiple sources of error or cross-contamination that could impact patient safety and product efficacy.

Many manufacturers outsource plasmid DNA manufacturing to contract development and manufacturing organizations (CDMOs) which, in turn, manufacture plasmids for different clients with different virus types and sub-types. Potential cross-contamination could occur due to shared equipment and materials, personnel flow, or insufficient cleaning during changeover. Indeed, clinical trials have been put on hold due to contamination of research-grade plasmid used in producing clinical materials. The sponsor needs to ensure that risks are assessed, preventive actions are taken to avoid such potential cross-contaminations, and that appropriate methods are established to confirm and characterize the identity and quality of the plasmids being used in the sponsor's manufacturing process.

The FDA Guidance for Industry: Human Gene Therapy for Neurodegenerative Diseases includes the expectation that plasmids manufactured in a multiproduct manufacturing facility should be tested for the presence of other contaminating plasmids which may have been co-purified (9). Implementation of such testing may pose logistical challenges to the sponsors as CDMOs typically do not share information regarding other clients' production schedules, let alone proprietary information (e.g., plasmid sequence) that might be needed to detect contamination.

As part of the Quality Agreement expectations, sponsors should require their CDMOs to demonstrate robust changeover procedures from product to product, validate cleaning and sanitization processes, and ensure other procedural controls are in place to prevent cross-contamination. Identity and/or purity assays should include criteria that can detect the occurrence of cross-contamination. Sponsors may also consider sequencing the CDMO product periodically, whether a plasmid or viral vector, as part of raw-material receipt by the sponsor's ATMP drug product manufacturer to support a holistic control strategy established to ensure product identity.

理解為什麼 AAV 和慢病毒（Lentivirus）需要多個質體，需要先瞭解病毒載體的製造邏輯：

為什麼需要 3 個以上質體？

如果把所有病毒基因都放在同一個質體中，共轉染後的宿主細胞可能自行組裝出具備複製能力的野生型病毒（RCV）——這是極大的安全危害。因此業界的做法是將關鍵基因「分拆」到三個以上的質體中：

  • 轉殖基因質體（Transfer Plasmid）：攜帶目標治療基因（transgene）及其調控元件（啟動子等）
  • 包膜質體（Envelope Plasmid）：編碼病毒外膜蛋白（決定載體的組織趨向性，即靶向哪種細胞）

重點提示 — CDMO 質體交叉污染：已有真實臨床擱置案例

PtC-11 明確指出：「臨床試驗已因使用受污染的研究等級質體生產臨床材料而被暫停」。

這不是假設性風險，而是已發生的真實事件。典型的交叉污染發生場景：

  • 共用設備：CDMO 的發酵槽、管路、過濾系統用於多個客戶的質體生產，清潔不徹底導致上一批次質體攜帶至下一批次
  • 共用耗材：試劑、緩衝液、填充料可能在不同批次間交叉使用
  • 人員流動：操作員在不同產品間流動，可能攜帶微量污染物
  • 換產不充分：清潔驗證不嚴謹，無法保證完全去除前一批次殘留質體

對患者的潛在影響極為嚴重：若另一客戶的質體混入，患者可能接受到含有未知基因元件的製品，導致無法預測的基因表現或免疫反應。

FDA 的期望（FDA Neurodegenerative Disease Gene Therapy Guidance）：多產品設施生產的質體，必須測試是否含有共純化的其他污染質體。這項要求在實務上極具挑戰性，因為 CDMO 通常不會透露其他客戶的質體序列資訊。

實務應用 — 質體身份確認的定序策略

PtC-11 建議 ATMP 製造商在接收 CDMO 供應的質體或病毒載體時，定期進行序列定序，以支持整體身份管控策略。以下是可行的定序策略框架：

  • 每批次接收時（Receipt Testing）：
    • 限制酶切分析（Restriction Enzyme Digest，RED）：快速確認質體構型是否一致，成本低，可作為常規批次放行測試
    • 關鍵區域定序（Partial Sequencing）：針對啟動子、轉殖基因編碼序列、調控元件等關鍵區域進行 Sanger 定序，確認無突變
  • 定期全長定序（Periodic Full-Length Sequencing）：
    • 採用 Next-Generation Sequencing（NGS）進行全質體定序，頻率建議每季度或每 N 批次一次
    • NGS 可同時檢測：序列突變、插入/缺失、混雜序列（即交叉污染的外源 DNA）
    • 建立質體參考序列資料庫（Reference Database），以每次定序結果與之比對
  • 換產後首批增強測試：
    • CDMO 換產後第一批質體，要求全長 NGS 作為常規批次放行的額外條件
    • 要求 CDMO 提供換產清潔驗證報告（Cleaning Validation Report）
  • Quality Agreement 條款支撐：
    • 明確要求 CDMO 揭露其在同一設施生產的其他產品的類別（無需揭露客戶身份，但須揭露病毒類型，以評估交叉污染風險）
    • 要求 CDMO 的換產程序在稽核中可審閱

比喻說明 — 質體 DNA 的序列完整性

把質體 DNA 想像成一份精確的樂譜。基因治療的「表演」（病毒載體組裝、基因表現）完全依賴這份樂譜的準確性：

  • 一個音符的錯誤（單鹼基突變）可能讓整首曲子走調（蛋白質功能喪失）
  • 一小節被刪除（序列缺失）可能讓演出中途崩潰（治療失效）
  • 混入另一位作曲家的段落（交叉污染）可能讓觀眾聽到完全無法預測的音樂（患者接受非預期基因表現）

定序（Sequencing）就是演出前仔細校對每一頁樂譜，確認每個音符都正確無誤。對於基因治療而言，「校對樂譜」不是可選項，而是患者安全的基本保障。

Q 2.1 — What is QbD and how does it apply to viral vectors and plasmids?

原文內容 Original Text

Quality-by-design (QbD), first developed by Dr. Joseph M. Juran, has evolved greatly since its inception and today it is supported by such widely recognized standards as the International Council for Harmonisation's Quality Guidelines ICH Q8(R2): Pharmaceutical Development, ICH Q9: Quality Risk Management, and ICH Q10: Pharmaceutical Quality System. The guiding principle of QbD asserts that quality should be designed into a product and into its manufacturing process. By designing the product and process properly, understanding is increased, thereby simplifying the troubleshooting of process and product problems in the future. This perspective is encouraged by the FDA and by other global regulators. This enhanced process knowledge, in turn, simplifies risk-based quality decision-making, as the risk-based decisions are made within the framework of the effective boundaries of the process.

The major deliverables of QbD include:

  • Quality target product profile (QTPP) that identifies the critical quality attributes (CQA)
  • Product design that includes identification of critical material attributes (CMA)
  • Process design that includes identification of critical process parameters (CPP)
  • Control strategy that links CPPs and CMAs to the delivery of CQAs
  • Process capability
  • Continuous quality improvement

Like large-molecule biotechnology products and small-molecule conventional therapies, viral vectors and plasmids benefit from a structured QbD approach to development and continuous improvement. Product development teams make use of prior platform experience and scientific knowledge (e.g., industry standards, published examples) as well as perform studies as needed (e.g., design of experiment) to create the body of knowledge (subject to rugged data analysis) which supports the product throughout its lifecycle. That data is then subject to knowledge management principles and is updated and enhanced by production experience and stability results to support commercialization.

At the end of each phase of pharmaceutical development, stage gates should be defined to encourage revision of the QTPP, control strategy, and control ranges. Final revision of the overall initial control strategy should be performed at the conclusion of the process performance qualification (PPQ). Lifecycle maintenance of the control strategy will then be required until product retirement.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — QbD 交付成果體系

QbD 並不是一個單一工具，而是一套環環相扣的知識建構體系。理解每個交付成果之間的因果關係，是掌握整個品質體系的關鍵：

  • QTPP（品質目標產品特性）：由 MAH/Sponsor 定義，描述「這個產品最終要達到什麼樣的品質標準才能安全有效地用於病人」。它是所有後續工作的起點。
  • CQA（關鍵品質屬性）：從 QTPP 推導出來，代表那些若超出範圍就會顯著影響產品安全性、效力或純度的物化、生物特性。
  • CMA（關鍵物料屬性）：原料（例如輔料、起始原料）的哪些特性會影響 CQA？這是產品設計的核心問題。
  • CPP（關鍵製程參數）：製程中哪些操作參數（溫度、pH、感染複數 MOI 等）直接影響 CQA？這是製程設計的核心問題。
  • 控制策略（Control Strategy）：把上述所有連結寫成可執行的管控框架，確保每一批產品的 CQA 都在可接受範圍內。

整個鏈條：QTPP → CQA → CMA / CPP → 控制策略 → 製程能力 → 持續改善，是一個循環而非線性的系統。

比喻說明 — 建築藍圖比喻

傳統的品質管控方式就像「先蓋好房子，再叫驗屋員來挑毛病」——品質是在最後才被「檢查」出來的。這種方式在 ATMP 的世界中代價極高：一批病毒載體可能造價數百萬美元，若驗收失敗，損失無法挽回。

QbD 的思維則是：「在畫設計藍圖的時候，就把所有的安全標準、結構要求、電氣規範全部設計進去。」這樣一來，施工過程中每一個步驟都有明確的品質邊界，出問題時也能快速定位根本原因，而不是在出貨前才驚慌失措。

對於病毒載體而言，QTPP 就是「建築許可書」，CQA 是「結構安全要求」，CPP 是「施工規範」，控制策略是「驗收標準清單」。

重點提示 — Stage Gates（階段關卡）

PtC-11 特別強調：在每個臨床開發階段結束時（Phase I → II → III → 商業化），都應設立 stage gate 來重新審查 QTPP、控制策略與管控範圍。這並不是繁文縟節，而是因為：

  • 早期開發時的知識是有限的，許多 CQA 和 CPP 是基於假設或少量數據所定義的。
  • 隨著更多批次累積，對製程的理解加深，控制範圍應該被精煉（而非放寬）。
  • 最終的控制策略應在 PPQ（製程性能確效）完成後完成最後一次修訂，然後進入持續改善（lifecycle maintenance）階段。

若跳過 stage gate，可能導致後期發現 CQA 定義不足，需要進行昂貴的比較性研究才能向監管機關說明。

Q 2.2 — MAH responsibilities when working with a CRO or CMO

原文內容 Original Text

The determination of responsibility for QbD is commonly part of the business agreement and Quality Agreement between the (future) registration holder and the contract research organization (CRO) or contract manufacturing organization (CMO). However, the latitude to shift responsibility from the marketing authorization holder (MAH) or the sponsor to the CRO or CMO is limited as some responsibilities cannot be delegated and must stay with the MAH or sponsor (e.g., disposition decisions). The EMA Reflection Paper on Good Manufacturing Practice and Marketing Authorisation Holders can help in understanding the guiding principles. This reference applies to the European Economic Area (EEA), and compliance with it has brought demonstrated success with other jurisdictions.

When making use of a CRO for the development of the product, the QTPP should be prepared by the Sponsor (i.e., future MAH) and reviewed with the CRO. Since the QTPP represents the development target from a quality perspective, alignment and mutual understanding on its content are essential. Typically, it is then the responsibility of the CRO to conduct the necessary studies to determine the preliminary CQAs, CMAs, and CPPs. The (future) MAH will be required to periodically assess and verify that the recordkeeping associated with the development activities are sufficient to support the development, technology transfer, product registration, and future product disposition.

Ideally, the Quality Agreement should specify how the documentation is developed, recorded, and archived as appropriate to the phase of development. In particular, a written agreement should be struck that identifies the work (both manufacturing and analytics) to be performed under the GxP principles pertaining to the appropriate phase of development/clinical-batch production. Additional updates to the control strategy may be made as the specifics of the equipment, automation, and controls are explored during transfer and subsequent validation. Document control processes will ensure that appropriate change control and version control are maintained for the controlled documents that define the specifications and control strategy.

Comparability will be required for any new site or significant change in product or process. (See Topic 5.) Process capability will then be established with the technology transfer and process validation, becoming the initial benchmark for future continued process validation and annual product review. 重點提示 — IP 所有權：最容易被忽視的法律風險

PtC-11 的這段警告非常直白：「若未事先明訂 IP 協議，成功的產品可能在法律上難以繼續推進。」

在 ATMP 領域，這個風險特別真實，原因是：

  • 許多 Sponsor 在早期臨床階段依賴外部 CRO 開發分析方法（例如病毒滴定法、效價測試），這些方法往往是 CRO 的核心技術資產。
  • 若 Quality Agreement 沒有明確規定 MAH 有權存取、使用或取得這些方法，當 MAH 想轉換供應商或自行生產時，就可能面臨法律訴訟或被 CRO 要挾。
  • 監管機關（如 EMA、FDA）在審查上市申請時，會要求提交完整的分析方法驗證報告——若 MAH 沒有這些文件的存取權，申請可能無法完成。

實務建議：在 Quality Agreement 中明確列明：資料所有權、方法使用授權、原始數據存取權、以及供應商倒閉或終止合作時的數據移交條款。

實務應用 — CDMO 視角：技術承接前的核查清單

身為 CDMO 的接收方，在簽署技術轉移協議之前，應確認以下事項已從 MAH 取得：

  • QTPP 文件：確認已明確定義產品的目標品質特性（劑型、效力、純度標準等）。
  • CQA 清單及風險評估依據：每一個 CQA 應有對應的科學理由說明。
  • 所有 CPP 及其可接受範圍（proven acceptable range, PAR）：這些是製程開發研究的核心輸出。
  • 分析方法轉移計畫：包括方法的 SOP、驗證報告、以及可接受標準。
  • 前期批次歷史（batch history）：用於建立初始製程能力基準（benchmark）。

若以上任何一項缺失，CDMO 應在合約簽署前要求補充，否則將承擔不必要的開發風險與法規風險。

Q 2.3 — QbD when sourcing (not manufacturing) viral vectors or plasmids

原文內容 Original Text

Before determining which aspects of QbD apply, it is important to understand whether the material will be considered a starting material or an active substance (e.g., drug substance), and the jurisdiction in which it will be regulated. If regulated as an active substance, in specific jurisdictions, the requirements of a CMO as described in Q 2.2 may apply.

However, if regulated as a critical starting material, the requirements may differ. Critical starting materials are required to have an incoming specification and suitable test methods to determine, among other things, the identity, purity, and whatever attributes of potency or functionality can be assessed at this stage in production. There may be requirements for viral and microbiological safety of the material as well. However, risk assessments may be appropriate to evaluate the criticality of the starting material and whether all these requirements should apply. As with all supplied materials, it may be possible to receive some or all of these test results based on the supplier's Certificate of Analysis (CoA) and Certificate of Conformance, provided the supplier has been qualified (and/or audited) specific to their manufacture, control, and testing and subjected to an appropriate risk assessment.

Identity-testing, however, is a phase-appropriate GMP requirement and the nature and extent of the identity-testing performed should be defined and justified. For example, in some cases, partial sequencing may provide sufficient discrimination of identity without having to perform testing of the full-length sequence.

In cases where the manufacturing platform or active substance has not been developed on behalf of the (future) MAH for a specific clinical trial or application, the supplier (i.e., the CMO who supplies the viral vector or plasmid) may hold the intellectual property rights. Global regulatory health authorities may not have a final standardized guidance to address how such a product will be filed and reviewed: therefore, it is important to actively communicate with the CMO and the regulatory health authority in question. In some cases, the supplier may not be willing to provide the same level of detail about the process to the (future) MAH as a true CMO, which may be appropriate given the phase of the product's development. In these cases, the basic details should be made available by the supplier to support a clinical trial dossier. In other cases, regulators may communicate that those details of a manufacturing platform process should be shared to support a marketing authorization. In any case, onsite auditing will be required to inspect the original source data that supports those technical details.

Viral vectors and plasmids are considered critical to the manufacturing process. Even in a jurisdiction that regulates these as starting materials, compliance to the manufacturing process and manufacturing conditions of the supplier are essential quality elements. Therefore, the quality management program of the (future) MAH should appropriately classify the supplier as critical and require a stringent onsite audit to ensure material quality and adherence to GMPs in a phase-appropriate manner. In addition, due to the assigned criticality, some jurisdictions may reserve the power to conduct risk-based inspections of the facility to ensure GMPs are followed.

It is advantageous for these materials to be "banked", which means that characterization of the material, stability during storage, and effective inventory management principles to reduce the risk of mix-up are all essential elements of the supplier- and self-audits. In addition, qualification of the analytical methods and equipment that established the master/working bank(s) must be completed. All these controls should be implemented in a phase-appropriate manner. (For more information, see PDA TR 56 (Revised 2016).)

Whether the viral vector or the plasmid is considered an active substance or a starting material, change control management must be established by the supplier and, as part of the Quality Agreement, details of product, process, or analytical changes must be made known to the MAH or Sponsor prior to change implementation so any impact to the registration or clinical trial application (or its equivalent) may be assessed. When the product, process, or analytics are not the intellectual property of the MAH or Sponsor, sufficient details should be provided to appropriately assess impact and to inform regulators when deemed appropriate.

核心概念解析 — 身份測試（Identity Testing）是最低 GMP 要求

無論起始原料的監管分類為何，身份測試（identity testing）是所有 GMP 環境下不可豁免的最低要求。這背後的邏輯很直觀：你必須能夠確認「這個東西確實是我訂購的東西」，否則一切品質管控都是空談。

對於病毒載體與質體，身份測試的方法需要視開發階段（phase-appropriate GMP）來決定其深度：

  • 早期臨床（Phase I/II）：部分序列分析（partial sequencing）通常就足以確認身份，不需要全長序列測定。
  • 晚期臨床至商業化（Phase III+）：可能需要更完整的序列確認與功能性測試，以支持上市申請。

純度、效價、功能性、病毒安全性等測試，雖然也是重要要求，但可以透過風險評估來決定是否採用供應商 CoA 代替自行測試——前提是供應商已通過完整的資格認定稽核。

比喻說明 — 食材採購比喻

想像一家頂級餐廳向外部農場購買食材，而不是自己種植。即使農場負責種植，餐廳仍然必須：

  • 在收貨時確認「這確實是我訂的有機番茄，不是其他品種」（身份測試）。
  • 要求農場提供農藥殘留報告、產地證明（CoA / CoC）。
  • 定期親自去農場視察（onsite audit），而不只是看報告。
  • 在農場改變種植方式之前，要提前通知餐廳（change control）。

若農場擁有獨家種植配方（IP），餐廳或許無法得知全部細節，但至少要知道足夠的資訊來確保食品安全——監管機關（衛生局）也會直接去農場稽查，不只是稽查餐廳。

重點提示 — 供應商持有 IP 時，現場稽核仍是強制要求

PtC-11 明確指出：即使供應商以 IP 保護為由，不願意揭露完整的製程細節，現場稽核（onsite auditing）仍然是必要的。這是因為：

  • 監管機關（如 EMA、FDA）可能要求檢視原始源數據（original source data），這些數據只能在現場才能核查。
  • 品質協議（Quality Agreement）中應明訂 MAH 有權進行現場稽核的條款，即使供應商持有 IP。
  • 部分監管機關保留對供應商設施進行基於風險之稽查的權力——MAH 必須確保供應商有此準備。

若供應商拒絕接受現場稽核，這本身就是一個重大的供應商資格危險訊號（red flag），MAH 應重新評估是否繼續合作。

實務應用 — Quality Agreement 中的變更通知條款

PtC-11 要求：無論向量或質體是被分類為藥物活性成份還是起始原料，Quality Agreement 中都必須建立供應商的變更控制通知義務。

在 CDMO 環境中，建議在 Quality Agreement 中明確涵蓋以下變更通知情境：

  • 產品層面：序列修改、轉殖策略變更、新細胞株的引入。
  • 製程層面：培養基成分變更、純化步驟的調整、設備更換。
  • 分析層面：任何用於放行測試之方法的修改或取代。
  • 設施層面：生產地點變更、GMP 分類降級或升級。

通知時限的建議：重大變更應在實施前至少 60 天通知 MAH；緊急變更應立即通知並在事後補充文件說明。

Q 3.1 — Control Strategy Requirement and Timing

原文內容 Original Text

Q 3.1. Are control strategies required for viral vectors and plasmid manufacture? If so, at what stage should the control strategy be available?

Viral vector and plasmid manufacture, whether defined as a critical starting material or as a drug substance, requires a robust, holistic control strategy to drive product quality. The control strategy should link the CPPs and CMAs directly to the CQAs. By understanding which process parameters have a direct impact on the CQAs, appropriate process monitoring and controls can ensure reproducible and predictable delivery of the viral vector or plasmid. PDA Technical Report No. 81 Cell-Based Therapy Control Strategy (TR 81) describes the approach for cell-based therapy control strategies; the strategies and concepts described in PDA Technical Report No. 60 Process Validation: A Lifecycle Approach (TR 60) apply to all pharmaceutical products. The principles discussed in TR 81 and TR 60 can broadly be applied to viral vector and plasmid control strategies as well.

In addition to the principles mentioned above, phase-appropriate implementation may be warranted. For example, the control strategy may consist of preliminary factors to be monitored and assessed in a phase-appropriate manner. As experience with the production of Phase 1 and Phase 2 material is gained, the control strategy may mature while regional differences may dictate how early the control strategy must be formalized. As additional product and process knowledge is gained through clinical results and through continued small-scale and lifetime studies, process ranges must be established and CPPs defined, so that by the time pivotal clinical batches are produced, product and process consistency are assured. When creating a holistic control strategy for plasmid and vector production, inclusion of microbiological and viral control aspects, in addition to the core manufacturing process elements, is important. The microbiological and viral control strategies can either be prepared as separate documents or they may be written within the overall process control strategy. Either approach is acceptable, provided that all stages of the process and all material handling steps are included in the approaches.

Viral and microbiological controls should be in place for the first human clinical trial as they have a direct impact on patient safety. Therefore, these attributes of the control strategy may need to mature earlier than some of the other parameter definitions. In the EU, sterilization and sterility methods are required to be validated at the time of Phase 1 for an Investigational Medicinal Product Dossier. For example, if a vector is defined as a sterile critical starting material, safety-indicating methods (such as sterility and endotoxin) must be qualified. Requirements for a mycoplasma safety-indicating method may vary based on regional requirements. A risk assessment may be needed which takes into consideration the intended market and existing controls. Additionally, the initial control strategy will need to be refined and revised as additional product knowledge and process understanding are gained.

Analytical methods and their associated reference standards should mature along with the control strategy. As CPPs are defined for each associated CQA, the analytical methods that confirm CQA achievement must be demonstrated to be reproducible, accurate, and precise. In this manner, should late-stage process changes or process parameter changes be introduced (within or between sites), comparability and bridging can be accomplished with a high degree of assurance. (See Topic 5.)

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 管控策略的成熟度軌跡

Control Strategy（管控策略）是一份活的文件，描述製程如何確保每批產品的 CQA（Critical Quality Attributes，關鍵品質屬性）符合規格。它的核心邏輯是：

  • CMA（Critical Material Attributes，關鍵物料屬性） → 原料的哪些特性會影響最終品質？
  • CPP（Critical Process Parameters，關鍵製程參數） → 製程中哪些參數一旦偏差就會衝擊 CQA？
  • CQA（Critical Quality Attributes） → 最終必須達到的品質標準（如效價、純度、無菌性）

三者形成一條因果鏈：物料特性與製程參數共同決定產品品質。管控策略就是把這條鏈子的每個節點明確化、並建立監控與控制措施。

Phase-appropriate（相位適當）指的是：管控策略不必在 Phase 1 就達到商業化等級的嚴謹度。它應隨著臨床進展逐步成熟——從初步定性的監測，到明確量化的 CPP 範圍，再到完整的製程驗證。

比喻說明 — 醫院感染管控計畫的成長

想像一家正在建設中的醫院。

  • Phase 1（門診部開幕）：基本手洗規範、員工健康監測就夠了——感染管控計畫是初稿，重點放在最高風險項目（如患者接觸傳染病原的直接安全）。
  • Phase 2（住院病房啟用）：需要增加環境微生物監測、清潔程序驗證，計畫更加正式與完整。
  • Phase 3（手術室與 ICU 開幕）：整套感染管控計畫必須完全到位，包含空氣管理、無菌技術驗證、環境認證。

病毒載體的管控策略也是如此：隨著試驗進入更晚期（Phase 1 → Phase 2 → 樞紐試驗），策略的深度與嚴謹度必須逐步升級。關鍵在於：微生物與病毒管控，從 Phase 1 就必須到位——因為這直接關乎受試者安全，不允許「未來再補強」。

重點提示 — EU vs US Phase 1 期望差異（CDMO 雙市場必知）

這是針對同時服務歐美市場的 CDMO 最關鍵的監管差異之一：

  • EU Phase 1（IMPD 要求）：滅菌方法與無菌性方法必須在 Phase 1 時完成驗證 (validated)。若產品定義為「無菌關鍵起始原料」，sterility 與 endotoxin 等安全指示方法必須完成資格確認。這是強制性要求。
  • US Phase 1（FDA 期望）：展示能力 (demonstrate capability) 即可，不要求正式完成模擬挑戰試驗。完整驗證通常在 Phase 2 及以後完成。

實務影響：若客戶先申請 EU IND/IMPD，CDMO 必須在 Phase 1 前完成更嚴格的驗證工作（包括 APS/APV）。若僅針對 FDA IND，初期可以建立能力展示，降低啟動成本。雙市場策略的 CDMO 應在專案初期即釐清客戶目標市場，以避免後期補做驗證的高昂代價。

此外，mycoplasma（黴漿菌）檢測方法的資格確認要求因地區而異，需依客戶目標市場進行風險評估。

實務應用 — 分析方法與管控策略同步成熟

文中強調「Analytical methods should mature along with the control strategy（分析方法應與管控策略同步成熟）」，這對 CDMO 有深遠的商業意義：

  • 若 CPP 已定義但對應的 CQA 分析方法仍不具備可重現性，那麼製程轉移（site transfer）或批次比較（comparability）時將缺乏數據支撐，可能引發監管機關的質疑。
  • 早期建立具可重現性的參考標準品 (reference standard) 與分析平台，是後期 comparability bridging（可比性橋接研究）的基礎——對於多個客戶的同類產品（如 AAV），平台化分析方法更能大幅降低成本。
  • 請參閱 PDA TR81（Cell-Based Therapy Control Strategy）與 TR60（Process Validation Lifecycle）以獲取更詳細的策略框架。

Q 3.2. What aspects of microbiological control apply to vector and plasmid manufacture?

Phase-appropriate microbiological- and viral-control principles should be applied regardless of whether the product is considered a starting material, active substance, or final product. During the definition of the QTPP (Quality Target Product Profile), it should be established whether the viral vector or plasmid is intended to be sterile or low bioburden. This definition will guide which aspects of microbial control to apply to the process.

After classification as sterile or low bioburden, the microbiological control strategy should then be established based on a systematic assessment of each process step. The assessment should consider the intrinsic and extrinsic sources of contamination with a focus on any open processing. The control strategy must identify appropriate active controls (e.g., incoming testing or in-process treatment, such as filtration) to limit microbiological and viral contamination. In addition, monitoring requirements (e.g., environmental monitoring, bioburden sampling) should be defined and implemented as passive measures to ensure that the state of control is maintained. The intent is to establish both active and passive measures that provide confidence in the assurance of the claim of either sterility or low bioburden.

Aseptic controls are critical requirements for operation. For low-bioburden processes, control strategy development can focus on which aseptic controls are essential to support a compliant low-bioburden material. Open processing and manual manipulation steps should be a particular focus in the microbiological control strategy. Certain factors in functionally closed processes, as described in EU Annex 1, should also be considered.

Whether the end product is considered sterile or low bioburden, all basic elements of a contamination control strategy (CCS) should exist, including:

  • Environmental monitoring program (see PDA Technical Report No. 13 (Revised 2022) Fundamentals of an Environmental Monitoring Program)
  • Cleaning and sanitization of the environment (see PDA Technical Report No. 70 Fundamentals of Cleaning and Disinfection Programs for Aseptic Manufacturing Facilities)
  • Specified personnel and material flow that prevent cross-contamination
  • Gowning
  • Access control
  • Environmental controls (e.g., temperature/humidity monitoring, pressure cascades, routine certification of HEPA filters)
  • Control of raw materials
  • In-process and final-product testing
  • Cross-contamination controls, including viral material

Within a given control strategy, there may be different approaches to achieve the same level of quality. For example, some firms include screening patient materials for bioburden and endotoxin in their control strategy while other firms set aside retention samples to be used in the case of a manufacturing investigation. The utility of the latter approach is that it speeds any possible investigation without consuming day-to-day resources for low-risk, single-use equipment with closed processing. The former approach demonstrates an active surveillance program to detect potential contamination which could be introduced to the facility.

In addition to supporting multiple approaches to the same issue, control strategies may mature or change as development progresses or as experience is gained. For example, during clinical-trial eligibility screening, patients are tested for multiple viruses, serving as a beneficial control. Once commercial production begins, some facilities consider adding adventitious-virus testing, thereby enhancing the surveillance and control. In both cases, the regulatory requirements form the minimum basis of the control strategy.

Many viral vectors and plasmids cannot withstand terminal sterilization or sterile filtration, which puts them in a high-risk category due to the reliance on the aseptic processing to safeguard the sterility of the final product. For sterile processes, aseptic process simulation (APS)/aseptic process validation (APV) is expected to demonstrate appropriate control over aseptic operations. This is the case for both drug substance and drug product, although the power of the study may be less with drug substance. In some jurisdictions, such as the United States, the expectation at Phase I is that the control strategy demonstrates the capability of the aseptic process but does not strictly require a simulation run with a challenge fluid. As the product progresses to Phase II and beyond, the first full validation will be conducted. For other jurisdictions, such as the EU, it is expected in Phase I that the process is fully validated at this early time-point.

Whether water trial or media simulation, the expectation is that the process simulation is risk-based, with an emphasis on all open and manual manipulations as well as sterile holds. Where unit operations or manufacturing steps are considered "functionally closed" (e.g., steps that are closed during processing but not fully closed during support activities such as sampling), simulating these steps is recommended so data can be generated that demonstrates the necessary level of control over the process. As with all aseptic simulation activities, it should be remembered that it is not possible to validate poor aseptic technique, and that the methodology is probabilistic. The APV/APS provides confidence that the process is capable, while the routine monitoring provides the assurance of the day-to-day controls.

重點提示 — 無菌 vs 低生物負荷：一個決定，影響全局

文中強調：在 QTPP 定義階段就必須確定產品是「sterile（無菌）」還是「low bioburden（低生物負荷）」。這不是文件上的一個欄位那麼簡單——它是整個管控策略的分叉點：

  • 低生物負荷路徑：管控策略相對靈活，要求的嚴謹度與驗證工作量較低（如允許廠商 CoA 放行，穿著要求較寬鬆）。
  • 無菌/無菌操作路徑：要求 Grade A/ISO 5 環境、APS/APV、無菌穿著資格確認、每批次持續環境監測等全套措施，成本與複雜度大幅上升。

一旦分類確定，後續所有 SOP、設施設計、EM 計畫、驗證計畫都必須與之匹配。若在開發中途改變分類（例如從低生物負荷改為無菌），將牽連大量補做驗證工作，延誤時程且增加成本。因此，此決策必須在 QTPP 定義時做出，並有充分的科學依據支撐。

核心概念解析 — CCS 的九大基礎要素

無論無菌或低生物負荷，Contamination Control Strategy (CCS，污染管控策略) 都必須涵蓋以下九大要素，這是 PDA TR90 的基礎架構，也是 EU Annex 1 所要求的全廠系統性方法：

  1. EM Program（環境監測計畫）：依 PDA TR13（Revised 2022），設定警戒限、行動限與調查程序。
  2. 清潔與消毒（Cleaning & Sanitization）：依 PDA TR70，確保環境微生物負荷受控。
  3. 人員與物料動線（Personnel & Material Flow）：防止交叉污染的物理與程序性措施。
  4. 無菌穿著（Gowning）：人員是無菌操作中最大的污染風險來源之一。
  5. 門禁管制（Access Control）：限制非授權人員進入潔淨區。
  6. 環境管控（Environmental Controls）：溫溼度、壓差級聯、HEPA 定期認證。
  7. 原料管控（Raw Material Control）：進料檢測或廠商 CoA 接受標準。
  8. 製程中與最終產品檢測（In-Process & Final Testing）：主動監測以驗證管控狀態。
  9. 交叉污染管控（Cross-Contamination Controls）：特別包含病毒物料的隔離管控。

實務應用 — 如何在 QTPP 中記錄無菌分類決策

監管機關在審查 IND/IMPD 時，會特別關注「為何此產品被定義為 sterile 而非 low bioburden」。CDMO 應協助客戶準備以下文件支撐：

  • 產品特性依據：說明為何無法進行 terminal sterilization 或 sterile filtration（例如病毒顆粒會因高溫或過濾剪切力而失活）。
  • 投藥途徑分析：靜脈注射、眼內注射等高風險投藥途徑通常驅動無菌要求；肌肉注射或皮下注射的低生物負荷路徑需更強的科學論據。
  • 風險評估：引用 ICH Q9 方法論，評估若非無菌品質時對患者的危害程度。
  • 地區監管差異：同一產品在 EU 可能因 IMPD 要求而必須定義為無菌，在 US IND 則可接受低生物負荷分類，需在 QTPP 中加以說明。

比喻說明 — APS 是外科手術前的全套彩排

很多人誤解 Aseptic Process Simulation（APS，無菌製程模擬），以為只是走一遍流程、把培養液換成培養基就好。但其精神更像是：

正式外科手術前，外科團隊在手術室做的全套彩排（Dress Rehearsal）——不只是背台詞，而是：

  • 所有人員必須真實穿著無菌手術衣進行操作
  • 所有開放性步驟、手動操作都必須模擬（包括抽樣、換袋等）
  • 「功能性密封」的步驟（如取樣口的開關），也應納入模擬——因為雖然平時是密封的，但每次打開取樣時都是潛在污染點
  • 結果是概率性的（probabilistic）——即使通過一次 APS，也不代表每批都完全無菌，只是代表製程有足夠的能力控制污染風險

記住：APS 無法驗證糟糕的無菌技術。若人員操作習慣本身有問題，再多次 APS 通過，也掩蓋不了根本的風險。

重點提示 — 「無法終端滅菌」= 最高風險等級

文中明確指出：many viral vectors and plasmids cannot withstand terminal sterilization or sterile filtration，這使它們在無菌保證領域屬於高風險類別。

這個高風險地位的含義：

  • 無安全網：傳統藥品在充填後可用高壓蒸氣（121°C）滅菌，即使充填時有少量污染，最終滅菌能提供額外保障。病毒載體沒有這個「第二道防線」。
  • 無菌操作是唯一保障：每一個開放操作步驟、每一次手動操作，都是唯一決定產品無菌性的時機——一旦失誤，無法補救。
  • 監管期望更高：EU Annex 1、FDA 無菌指引均對「無法終端滅菌」的產品有更嚴格的無菌操作管控要求。
  • AAV 是例外：AAV 通常可以進行過濾除菌（sterile filtration），相對處於較低風險類別。但 lentiviral vector、rHSV、retroviral vector 等則因顆粒較大或對剪切力敏感，通常無法過濾。

對 CDMO 的實務意義：在評估新客戶產品時，首要確認其是否能進行 sterile filtration，以決定是否需要完整無菌製程設施與 APS/APV 的全套支援。

Q4.1 — Should filtration be considered as a part of the initial strategy of development and control?

原文內容 Original Text

For contamination control of proteins and biologically active materials, the use of filtration technologies is commonly preferred over other technologies (e.g., steam sterilization, gamma sterilization, X-ray) because it tends to reduce the impact on the product's quality attributes.

Microbial and viral filtration should be included in the design of the manufacturing process from the earliest conceivable timepoint so that its introduction later in development does not introduce any unintended impact on product quality attributes. Establishing filtration for a process may be challenging, as lentiviral, rHSV, and gamma-retroviral vectors may be damaged by filtration or result in such low potency that a lot may be unusable. While AAV vectors can typically be filter-sterilized, potential product changes due to filtration, such as aggregation, should be characterized.

PDA Technical Report No. 26 Sterilizing Filtration of Liquids (TR 26) and Technical Report No. 41 (Revised 2022) Virus Retentive Filtration (TR 41) provide in-depth discussions of the theoretical and technical elements associated with the filtration of fluids with the goal of removing microbial and viral contamination. EMA Annexes 1 and PIC/S Annex 2A state compliance expectations when the drug product cannot be filter-sterilized, as in the case in cell therapies. Where filtration is not possible, aseptic processing is expected. In addition, the sterile filtration or aseptic processing of starting materials is also expected (e.g., media used for producer cells, transfection agents, other additives).

The decision to aseptically produce without filtration must be accompanied by a risk assessment, including results of the filter development study from the specific product or from platform experience, that demonstrate the product was irreparably harmed by the process of filtration. For more detailed information, see FDA Guidance for Industry: Sterile Drug Products Produced by Aseptic Processing — Current Good Manufacturing Practice and EU Annex 1.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 為何過濾是生物製品的首選？

過濾 (Filtration) 是生物活性材料（如病毒載體、質體）的優先污染控制方式，原因在於它是一種物理分離過程，不需要加熱、輻射或化學反應，因此對產品分子結構的破壞最小。

相比之下：

  • 蒸氣滅菌（steam sterilization）：高溫高壓，蛋白質和病毒載體幾乎全部失活
  • 伽瑪射線（gamma sterilization）：輻射會造成核酸鏈斷裂，破壞載體的遺傳物質
  • X 光滅菌（X-ray sterilization）：與伽瑪類似，對生物活性材料損傷嚴重

過濾的核心優勢：在不破壞產品關鍵品質屬性（CQA）的前提下，物理性排除微生物與病毒污染。

比喻說明 — 過濾就像「濾湯」，而非「煮沸」

想像你熬了一鍋精心調配的高湯，裡面有細緻的香氣與營養成分（代表病毒載體的活性）。現在你發現湯裡可能有雜質（代表微生物污染）。

你有兩個選擇：

  • 選項 A：煮沸再煮沸（蒸氣滅菌）— 雜質確實被殺死了，但湯的香氣和許多營養成分也一起消失了
  • 選項 B：用細目篩網過濾（無菌過濾）— 雜質被截留在篩網上，湯的風味和營養幾乎完整保留

對生物活性材料而言，「過濾」就是那張保護風味的細目篩網。但對於像 LV（慢病毒）這種「顆粒太大容易卡在篩網上」的載體，即使是過濾本身也可能造成損失——這就是本題的挑戰所在。

重點提示 — LV / rHSV 是最具挑戰性的載體

慢病毒載體（lentiviral vector, LV）、重組單純疱疹病毒（rHSV）和伽瑪-逆轉錄病毒（gamma-retroviral vector）是 ATMP 開發中過濾難度最高的三類載體，原因如下：

  • 顆粒尺寸大：LV 約 80–120 nm，接近 0.22 µm 過濾膜的截留範圍，極易被截留或受到剪切力損傷
  • 結構脆弱：包膜病毒（enveloped virus）的脂質雙層膜在高壓差下容易破裂
  • 效價損失嚴重：過濾後往往造成活性單位（transducing units, TU）大幅下降，甚至整批報廢

相比之下，AAV（腺相關病毒） 為非包膜病毒，結構更為穩固，通常可以過濾，但仍需評估聚集（aggregation）風險。

合規提示：若決定不過濾，必須有風險評估報告及過濾研究數據（或平台經驗數據）支撐，並說明產品確實不可挽回地受到過濾損害。

Q4.2 — What are the key considerations for the control strategy when filtration is considered and when filtration cannot be performed?

原文內容 Original Text

The following are key elements to deliberate when filtration is considered:

  • Intended purpose of the filtration step (e.g., sterilization, bioburden reduction, viral removal)
  • Type of filter and its material of construction (e.g., depth filter, casted membrane, hollow fiber, cartridge)
  • Process conditions (e.g., temperature, pressure differential, flow rate, nature of the fluid, flush volume)
  • Nature and level of microbial and/or viral contamination
  • Quality impact on the product (e.g., yield, adsorption, viability/activity, inactivation, matrix reactivity, aggregates, extractables/leachables)

These elements must be evaluated as part of the control strategy when filtration is identified as the mechanism to remove adventitious agents (e.g., microbial and viral contamination). These elements should also be considered as part of the demonstration that filtration was not possible if a risk assessment is planned for that purpose. (See PDA TR 26 for additional information.)

Whether the plasmid or vector will be filtered or aseptically manipulated without filtration, the control strategy is expected to minimize opportunities for adventitious agent contamination. To ensure that the control strategy is sufficiently robust, a thorough evaluation of the process from feed to final product is required to ensure the proper controls are in place to prevent adventitious agents from being introduced into the product stream. Each step should be evaluated to identify aseptic manipulations and specify the requirements for either reduced bioburden or sterile/virus-free components.

In early development laboratories, aseptic processing may seem to be impractical, even though GLP and/or GxP quality systems are in place. If vector is produced in an R&D, academic, or process-development lab and later used in first-in-human trials, however, careful consideration through a risk assessment and an appropriate quality assurance review is warranted to evaluate the potential for microbial and viral contamination and patient risks.

PIC/S Annex 2A provides guidance on the design and separation of ventilation systems that should be considered when multiple replication-competent vectors are being developed in parallel with other processes.

核心概念解析 — 「不過濾」決策的風險評估要求

當產品無法進行無菌過濾時，控制策略必須填補這個空缺。PDA PtC-11 明確列出在考慮過濾或評估「無法過濾」時，必須系統性評估的五大要素：

  • 過濾目的（Purpose）：是無菌（sterilization）、降低生物負荷（bioburden reduction），還是病毒去除（viral removal）？目的不同，所選用的膜孔徑和驗證策略也不同
  • 過濾器類型與材質（Filter type/material）：深層濾器（depth filter）、鑄膜（cast membrane）、中空纖維（hollow fiber）、濾筒（cartridge）各有其截留機制與相容性差異
  • 製程條件（Process conditions）：溫度、壓差、流速、液體性質、沖洗體積都會影響過濾效率和產品回收率
  • 污染性質與程度（Contamination level）：已知的微生物和病毒背景污染程度決定了所需的安全邊際
  • 對產品的品質影響（Quality impact）：特別關注萃取物/浸出物（extractables and leachables, E&L）、聚集體（aggregates）、活性/效價損失

重點提示 — 早期 R&D 級材料用於 FIH 試驗是高風險合規區域

這是法規檢查中的已知高風險區域（known compliance risk area）。許多 CDMO 和學術機構的向量在早期開發時是在 R&D 或 process development 實驗室生產的，這些場所往往：

  • 缺乏正式的無菌製造設施
  • 使用 R&D 等級（非 GMP 等級）的原物料
  • 未進行完整的微生物控制驗證

然而，一旦這批材料計劃用於首次人體試驗（first-in-human, FIH），GMP 要求立即適用。此時需要：

  • 正式的風險評估（risk assessment），評估微生物和病毒污染的患者風險
  • 品質保證（QA）審查，確認生產過程的適當性

法規警示：FDA 和 EMA 均有案例顯示，因早期載體生產過程不合規而導致 IND/CTA 審查延誤，甚至臨床試驗暫停。

比喻說明 — 控制策略就像「食安管理體系」

想像一家餐廳的食材管理：無論食材最後有沒有「高溫烹飪（=過濾/滅菌）」，每一個從進貨到上桌的環節都必須有對應的污染防控措施。

如果今天廚師說「這道菜不能加熱（=載體無法過濾）」，那麼食材供應鏈、廚房衛生、餐具消毒等每一個環節都需要更嚴格的管理——這正是控制策略從「進料到最終產品」全流程評估的精神。

Q4.3 — What is the impact of sterile filtration on plasmids and vectors?

原文內容 Original Text

Removal of microbial contamination is commonly attained by means of 0.1 µm to 0.22 µm membranes. The structure of plasmids and vectors make them susceptible to aggregation during certain processing conditions. Close attention must be paid to steps prior to the filtration that could result in the formation of aggregates as the solutions approach the membrane. If aggregates are formed, they may be retained on the filter resulting in reduced yield and potency.

Working with membrane vendors during development may enable the reduction of harm to the product (e.g., preventing product loss, the formation of aggregates, loss of potency). Fine-tuning the product chemistry and the membrane characteristics may yield improved results. For example, with lentiviral vectors, performing scaled-down studies with 43 mm disc membranes from several different vendors may be beneficial to study the differences in impact that different membranes impart. The use of scaled-down studies economizes on the amount of vector used during development when it is in short supply.

Often dual-layer, asymmetric polyethersulfone (PES) membranes are successful candidates for developing a lentiviral control strategy which includes final filtration.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

實務應用 — CDMO 的 LV 過濾縮小規模研究策略

對 CDMO 而言，慢病毒載體（LV）的過濾開發研究是一項重要的服務能力。PDA PtC-11 提供了一個具體且經濟的實驗設計框架：

為什麼用 43 mm disc 膜？

  • 43 mm 圓盤型膜片的有效過濾面積約 14.6 cm²，遠小於正式生產用的濾筒（cartridge）
  • 相同比例的縮小實驗只需消耗少量珍貴的載體，大幅降低開發成本
  • 可同時對多個廠商的膜材進行平行比較，快速篩選出最佳候選膜

為什麼 PES 雙層非對稱膜是首選？

  • 聚醚碸（Polyethersulfone, PES）：低蛋白吸附性，適合病毒載體這類高價值生物製品
  • 雙層（dual-layer）：前層截留大顆粒（aggregates），後層完成精細過濾，降低堵塞風險
  • 非對稱（asymmetric）：孔徑由進液面向出液面逐漸縮小，增加截留效率並延長膜片壽命

CDMO 商業意涵：若客戶載體無法過濾，整個批次可能無法使用，損失極大。事先投入縮小規模研究（scaled-down study），是降低最終規模生產失敗風險的最佳投資。

重點提示 — 過濾前步驟同樣關鍵

PDA PtC-11 特別強調：導致聚集（aggregation）的原因往往不在過濾膜本身，而在過濾前的製程條件。需要特別監控：

  • 緩衝液置換（buffer exchange）條件
  • 濃縮步驟（concentration step）的剪切力
  • 儲存溫度和時間（凍融循環）
  • 液體 pH 和離子強度的變化

若聚集體（aggregates）已在過濾前形成，它們將被截留在膜上，直接導致產率下降和效價損失——而過濾膜本身無需為此「負責」。

比喻說明 — 膜篩選就像「為特定選手選跑道」

不同的過濾膜就像不同材質的跑道——有的對長跑選手（大顆粒 LV）友善，有的則容易讓他們絆倒（被截留）。縮小規模研究就是在正式比賽前，讓選手試跑每一條跑道，找到最適合的那條。43 mm disc 的經濟性就是讓你用最少的「試跑成本」完成賽道評選。

Q4.4 — Why is the use of nanofiltration to mitigate viral contamination relevant?

原文內容 Original Text

Virus-retentive filters are designed to remove potential viruses by means of size exclusion; the nominal pore size in these filters is 0.1 µm. These filters are used in ATMPs to process culture media or buffers used to culture or expand cells where viral contamination will be detrimental to the product, and they should be considered as part of the overall adventitious virus control strategy.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 奈米過濾在 ATMP 的角色

奈米過濾（Nanofiltration），更精確地稱為病毒保留性過濾（Virus-retentive filtration），在 ATMP 製造中的應用場景與一般無菌過濾不同：

應用對象：不是直接過濾含有病毒載體的產品，而是過濾培養基（culture media）和緩衝液（buffers）——這些材料一旦受到病毒污染，會嚴重危害細胞培養或病毒擴增過程。

作用原理：標稱孔徑（nominal pore size）0.1 µm，透過尺寸排阻（size exclusion）截留病毒顆粒，去除培養基中可能存在的外源性病毒（adventitious viruses）。

在控制策略中的定位：作為整體外源性病毒控制策略（adventitious virus control strategy）的一環，與來源材料測試、供應商資格確認等措施共同構成多層次防護（layered defense）。

重點提示 — 奈米過濾 vs. 無菌過濾的區別

容易混淆的兩個概念：

  • 無菌過濾（Sterile filtration）：使用 0.22 µm（或 0.1 µm）膜，目的是去除微生物（細菌、真菌），確保產品無菌。0.22 µm membrane 是最常見的無菌過濾標準。
  • 病毒保留性過濾（Virus-retentive filtration / Nanofiltration）：使用 0.1 µm 或更小孔徑的特殊膜，目的是去除病毒顆粒，通常用於培養基/緩衝液的病毒清除，而非終產品的無菌化。

在 ATMP 製造中，兩者可能同時出現在製程中——但應用於不同的流體和不同的防護目的。

比喻說明 — 奈米過濾是「食材供應鏈的病毒安全網」

延續前面的餐廳比喻：奈米過濾就像在食材進入廚房之前，對水源和醬汁進行的病毒檢測和過濾。即使主廚（最終產品過濾）技術再好，如果供應鏈（培養基/緩衝液）本身帶有病毒，整個批次的細胞培養都可能受到污染，造成無法挽回的損失。

Q4.5 — Does the sterile filter have to be the last step in the process before filling?

原文內容 Original Text

Ideally, the point of filtration should come after all significant additions and manipulations of the product, which naturally places the filtration step as the last activity prior to filling. If a different position within the process is warranted (e.g., based on harm to the product or excessive product loss during filtration), a risk assessment may be possible to support an earlier position for filtration in the process.

The final position for the filtration and its justification should be part of the formal control strategy.

"The final position for the filtration and its justification should be part of the formal control strategy."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 過濾位置的設計原則

無菌過濾的理想位置（ideal position）是製程的最後一個步驟，緊接於充填（filling）之前。這樣設計的邏輯是：

  • 過濾後的所有操作步驟都是潛在的再污染風險點
  • 若過濾後還有添加（additions）或操作（manipulations），代表產品已在沒有過濾保護的情況下暴露於潛在污染環境
  • 「最後過濾」相當於在最靠近患者的那一道關卡確認無菌性

重點提示 — 非最後步驟過濾必須在控制策略中明確記錄

若基於產品損害（product harm）或過度產品損失（excessive product loss）的原因，過濾步驟必須提前（earlier position），則：

  • 必須進行風險評估（risk assessment），量化提前過濾帶來的污染風險增量
  • 風險評估結果和替代控制措施（補償性控制）必須明確記錄
  • 過濾位置的選擇及其理由（justification）必須納入正式控制策略（formal control strategy）文件中

法規啟示：在 FDA 和 EMA 的 GMP 查廠中，過濾步驟的位置及其理由是必查項目之一。沒有充分文件化的提前過濾決策，是常見的 483 觀察項目（FDA 483 observation）來源。

比喻說明 — 最後過濾是「出門前最後一道門鎖」

在無菌製造中，過濾就像出門前的最後一把門鎖。理想情況下，你在踏出門（充填）的瞬間才鎖門（過濾），確保沒有任何闖入者留在屋內。

如果你因為某些原因（比如門鎖太重，門框會因此損壞——代表產品損害）必須在更早的時候鎖門，那麼你就需要在門關上後到真正出門之前的這段時間，有其他的安全措施（監控攝影機、警報器——代表補償性控制），並且要白紙黑字記錄下來為什麼要這樣做。

Q4.6 — In early vector development, what is the timing for the decision on whether to aseptically process or sterile filter?

原文內容 Original Text

The decision to filter or to aseptically process should be made as early as practicable in the design of the product. The earlier the decision is made, the easier it will be to ensure that the appropriate control elements are included in the CCS and that the necessary unit operations are protected as required to fulfill the design requirements. However, the decision to not include a sterile filtration step requires justification, appropriate risk assessment, and data to support the risk assessment, therefore, the timing may depend on the collection of data and justification of the approach.

Ultimately, the final approach should be codified within the QTPP, the control strategy for the process, the microbiological and virological CCS, and the facility controls.

Early vector development commonly occurs in shared facilities that may have more than one standard for processing: therefore, careful procedural controls should be in place for each material developed that reflect the appropriate use of those materials (e.g., animal studies, tox studies).

Different regulatory authorities have varying stances on sterilization process validation and the timing relative to the development of clinical materials. For instance, in Europe there is an expectation that sterilization validation will be complete and available prior to Phase I (see Q 4.1). The regulatory requirements for the jurisdictions selected by the MAH may therefore influence the timing of the decision on aseptic processing or filtration.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 盡早決策的理由

「越早決定，越容易實施」——這是本題的核心邏輯。過濾或無菌操作的選擇，直接決定了：

  • 設施需求：是否需要 RABS / 隔離器等高等級無菌設施
  • 設備配置：是否需要安裝過濾系統及完整性測試設備（integrity tester）
  • CCS 架構：污染控制策略的整體設計（從微生物學和病毒學角度）
  • 驗證計畫：過濾效能驗證（filter validation）或無菌模擬試驗（APS）的時間線

若在開發後期才決定，可能需要大幅修改設施、設備和製程，代價極高。

重點提示 — EU vs. US 的時機差異（Phase 1 驗證期望）

這是一個重要的法規差異點，類似 Topic 3（Q3.1）的模式：

法規轄區 Phase I 前的滅菌驗證期望
歐盟（EU）/ EMA 在 Phase I 臨床材料使用之前，滅菌驗證（sterilization validation）必須完成並可供審查
美國（US）/ FDA Phase I 期間主要是展示無菌製程能力（capability），不嚴格要求在 Phase I 前完成正式模擬驗證（simulation run）

實務意涵：若 MAH（上市許可持有人）計劃在歐盟申請 CTA（臨床試驗授權），則過濾/無菌製程的決策和驗證必須在更早的時間點完成——這會直接影響早期開發的時程規劃。

對 CDMO 而言，了解客戶的目標申請市場（EU vs. US vs. 雙軌）對於正確規劃驗證時間線至關重要。

比喻說明 — 早期決策就像「蓋房子先決定用木造還是鋼筋水泥」

過濾 vs. 無菌操作的選擇，就像蓋房子時最先要決定的結構體系——是木造（輕盈但限制較多）還是鋼筋混凝土（重但靈活度高）。

這個決定一旦確定，所有後續的設計（配管、設備、驗證計畫）都必須圍繞它展開。如果在快蓋完了才改變主意，前面做的很多工作都得拆掉重來——時間和金錢的損失都是巨大的。

Q4.7 — When sterile filtration is not an option, at what point in the process is aseptic processing needed?

原文內容 Original Text

In an aseptic process, the materials used in the process are sterilized separately (e.g., filtration, heat, irradiation) and then brought together in an aseptic manner. The point where aseptic manipulations must be conducted should be as far downstream in the process as practicable. In this manner, there is a high degree of sterility assurance for the individual components and the aseptic processing steps (the aseptic boundary) are kept to the absolute minimum.

"The point where aseptic manipulations must be conducted should be as far downstream in the process as practicable... the aseptic processing steps (the aseptic boundary) are kept to the absolute minimum."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 — 無菌製程的結構原理

無菌製程（Aseptic processing） 的核心邏輯是「分別滅菌，無菌合併」：

  1. 每一個個別組件（容器、緩衝液、活性成分）分別透過適當的方法滅菌（過濾、濕熱、輻射等）
  2. 已滅菌的組件在受控的無菌環境中組合在一起
  3. 從組合操作開始，到最終密封完成，這整個區段就是無菌邊界（aseptic boundary）的範圍

關鍵設計原則：無菌邊界越小越好。因為無菌邊界內的每一個操作步驟都是潛在的污染風險點——邊界越大，需要管控的風險點越多，無菌保證（sterility assurance）的難度也越高。

因此，無菌操作應盡可能推遲到製程最下游（as far downstream as practicable），讓上游製程在非無菌條件下完成，只在最後幾個關鍵步驟才進入無菌環境。

比喻說明 — 無菌邊界就像「手術室的開放區域」

想像一場外科手術：手術室（無菌邊界）就是那個極度嚴格管控污染的空間。手術室的範圍越小、手術時間越短，污染的機會就越少。

在設計無菌製程時，目標就是把「手術室（無菌邊界）」縮到最小：讓術前準備（upstream 製程）盡量在更外圍的空間完成，只有「開刀本身（最後幾個無菌操作步驟）」才進入最嚴格的無菌區。

換句話說：不要把整個醫院都變成手術室——只把手術台周圍那幾平方公尺維持在最高的無菌標準。這正是「盡量推遲無菌邊界啟動點」的精髓。

重點提示 — 無菌邊界最小化的實務意義

對於無法進行無菌過濾的 ATMP（如 LV 載體），製程設計的優化目標是：

  • 在合理可行的範圍內，盡量讓最多的製程步驟（細胞培養、病毒生產、初步純化）在無菌邊界以外完成
  • 讓進入無菌邊界的時機盡量靠近最終充填步驟
  • 對於進入無菌邊界的每一個個別組件，確保它們各自已達到滅菌要求（個別過濾、高壓滅菌、輻射滅菌等），再帶入無菌邊界合併

合規提示：無菌邊界的定義和範圍必須在污染控制策略（CCS）中明確記錄，並透過無菌製程模擬（Aseptic Process Simulation, APS）進行驗證。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 若一家 CDMO 正在開發慢病毒載體（LV）的充填製程，且經過 43 mm disc 縮小規模研究後確認多個廠商的 PES 膜均會造成超過 50% 的效價損失，此時應如何設計控制策略？請說明至少三項必須包含在書面風險評估中的關鍵要素。
  2. 歐盟申請 CTA 的客戶計劃在 6 個月後啟動 Phase I 試驗。目前尚未決定是否對其 AAV 載體進行無菌過濾。請說明這個時間點的法規風險，以及 CDMO 應如何建議客戶優先採取哪些行動。
  3. 「無菌邊界（aseptic boundary）應盡量推遲到製程最下游」——請用具體製程步驟（例如：培養基製備 → 病毒生產 → 純化 → 配製 → 充填）舉例說明如何應用這個原則，以及哪個步驟是無菌邊界的合理起始點。

Q 5.1 — How are bridging studies different from comparability studies?

原文內容 Original Text

Bridging and comparability are terms commonly used during the development and registration of a new product when comparing and assessing differences between processes or materials. They are used separately (e.g., bridging of reference standards, comparability of lots from different sites) and, more confusingly, can also be used together such as in ICH Quality Guideline Q5E: Comparability of Biotechnological/Biological Products Subject to Changes in their Manufacturing Process where there is a reference to a "comparability bridging study." Therefore, it is important to first define these terms in order to offer clarity about their objectives and execution.

Bridging is the process of relating earlier data to later data in a way that the ability to extrapolate earlier results to later results is maintained. Common usage includes bridging of clinical studies, bridging of reference standards, or bridging from one test method to a new version of that test method.

Comparability is the process of comparing technical data from a reference population to a new population for the purpose of ensuring equivalence in quality, efficacy, and/or safety. Common usage includes comparability of product manufactured by different manufacturing sites, particularly when launching at a new site. It may also include comparability (comparing) product quality attributes and patient safety data after a significant process change.

In many situations, bridging is achieved when analytical comparability of product quality or analytical method outputs before and after a change is demonstrated. When comparability is not demonstrated by only analytical testing, additional nonclinical and clinical bridging studies are needed.

"A study performed to provide nonclinical or clinical data that allows extrapolation of the existing data from the drug product produced by the current process to the drug product from the changed process." — ICH Q5E on comparability bridging studies

In early phases of development, establishing in-house or interim reference standards that are useful for the development work, but are not intended for commercial use, is common. When establishing a primary reference standard and subsequent working reference standard, conducting bridging studies of the reference standards to be able to relate lot test results from early safety and clinical work to the testing from batches that represent the commercial process is essential.

Similarly, test methods might evolve throughout the development and commercialization program as they are refined or improved (e.g., enhanced method controls or automation, improved precision, greater limits of quantification or detection). Bridging of methods ensures that results from earlier generations of test methods can be interpreted in the context of results generated with the new versions of the test methods.

Nowhere is bridging more critical than for stability studies. Data generated with reference standards or test methods at earlier timepoints must be preserved through an understanding of the bridging to the current methods and, therefore, the results at the current and future timepoints. Often stability studies can be performed with both the old and new methods (and old and new reference standards) in parallel from the point of introduction of the new method through the endpoint of the study.

Comparability is performed to assess the quality, efficacy, and/or safety attributes of a lot(s) against other lot(s) that are differentiated by process, site of manufacture, raw material, or other potentially significant difference.

Some important points to consider for bridging and comparability include the following:

  • Ensure that bridges and comparability are always maintained to clinical materials, especially the pivotal lots that will demonstrate efficacy, safety, and/or immunogenicity
  • Keep careful track of which lots were tested with which version of the assay or reference standard and know what those differences might mean in terms of bias or offset
  • Establish written guidelines for when CoAs will or will not be updated based on the introduction of new test methods or new reference standards, and ensure that there is a clear signifier on the CoA that traces to its version that permits ready traceability to the testing that was used to generate the values
  • Ensure that with each intended change there is a planned strategy for bridging stability data to new methods and reference standards to preserve the integrity of earlier timepoints, before the change is implemented
  • Use bridging and comparability protocols to define the criteria by which comparisons and conclusions are to be made
  • Ensure that an inventory of clinical lots, reference standard lots, and other representative lots are set aside and maintained for the purpose of establishing the essential bridges required for the program

Establishing standard operating procedures that include these requirements is one way to ensure success in demonstrating that product, processes, reference standards, and/or analytical results are consistent and traceable.

比喻說明 Analogy

Bridging（銜接）= 時代翻譯辭典

想像你用1980年代的老字典查了一個詞，但現在這個詞的定義已經更新了。「Bridging」就像是把舊版辭典和新版辭典的定義對照起來，確認當年查到的意思在今天仍然成立——過去的穩定性數據、參考標準測試結果，都需要這樣的「翻譯」才能在今日仍被接受。

Comparability（可比較性）= 兩款葡萄酒的盲測對比

同一個酒莊，在兩個不同年份（或不同國家的分廠）生產的葡萄酒，拿來並排品嚐，看看是不是同樣的品質與風味。這就是 Comparability——同時比對兩批來自不同條件的產品，確認等效性。

重點提示：穩定性銜接計畫必須「事前」制定

原文特別強調："before the change is implemented"（在變更實施之前）就必須規劃好穩定性數據的銜接策略。

這是業界最常犯的錯誤之一：等到分析方法已經升級、新版參考標準已經啟用，才意識到過去三年的穩定性時間點數據無法與新方法連貫。此時已無法補救——舊批次可能已經超出保存期限或樣本耗盡，銜接研究根本無從進行。

實務結論：方法變更或參考標準更新前，變更控制文件中必須包含「穩定性銜接計畫」章節。

實務應用：CDMO 保留樣本庫管理策略

對於 CDMO 而言，管理客戶的保留樣本（retention samples）庫存是一項高價值、高風險的業務。以下是建議的管理策略：

  • 臨床批次清單（Clinical Lot Inventory）：建立專用台帳，記錄每個臨床批次的樣本位置、剩餘量、有效期限、已進行的測試版本
  • 參考標準版本追蹤：每個批次的 CoA 應標記當時使用的參考標準版本號，並在 LIMS 系統中建立版本-批次對照關係
  • 分析方法版本對應表：維護一份「哪些批次用哪個版本的方法測試」的矩陣表，確保方法升級後可以快速識別哪些批次需要補做銜接研究
  • SOP 明文規定：什麼情況下 CoA 需要更新、什麼情況下加注版本說明即可，避免不同 QA 人員對此有不同解讀
  • 保留樣本使用審批：每次動用保留樣本庫必須有書面協議，說明使用目的、剩餘量預測、補充計畫
Q 5.2 — How can a change control strategy compare or utilize test results from two sites, two batches, or clinical to PPQ material?

原文內容 Original Text

Demonstration of comparability of product quality attributes and patient safety data from product manufactured under different conditions (e.g., different development stages, different site of manufacture, different analytical methods) is critical to prove that previous preclinical toxicology, nonclinical, and clinical study results are still relevant. Further, demonstration of comparability will prove that the same control strategy, specifications, and shelf life can still apply to the post-change products. Therefore, ICH Q5E is also applicable to ATMPs.

For analytical comparability, typically three levels are involved:

The first level is lot-release testing, where comparability criteria should be established based on historical data generated from materials manufactured before the change. In many cases, the comparability criteria might need to be tighter than the release specifications. To meet the predefined comparability criteria, not only should all lots made from the new process meet release specifications, but they also should be within the comparability criteria.

The second level is characterization testing, wherein additional characterization assays should be performed in addition to the lot-release testing to demonstrate there are no other quality attribute changes that are not detected by lot-release testing. A significant change in any quality attribute (critical or noncritical) may suggest there may be other underlying changes that could pose risks to product safety and efficacy.

The third level is stability comparability, which could include stability studies at recommended storage conditions and/or accelerated/stress stability studies. Comparability of stability (both degradation rate and profiles) should justify that the previously established shelf life can still be applied to the post-change product. In addition, accelerated/stress stability studies are considered a "pressure test" to reveal any differences that may not be detected under long-term storage conditions during routine analysis. Such forced degradation studies are commonly done for therapeutic protein products during a comparability assessment.

The stringency of the comparability exercise increases as the product progresses into later stages of development. Comparability of early preclinical/toxicology to first clinical materials enables the first-in-human clinical trial, while comparability between pivotal clinical materials and validation (process performance qualification (PPQ)) is essential to a smooth Biologics License Application (BLA) review and approval process.

Many ATMPs are for rare disease indications, so the number of lots manufactured at early stages may be limited and it could be difficult to establish meaningful comparability acceptance criteria with such a limited data set. Increasing the number of replicate tests for the same material could help set criteria that take assay variability into consideration, which is especially important for critical, yet highly variable, biological assays (e.g., titer and potency).

Unique challenges exist for ATMP comparability studies. The quantity of ATMP samples is typically limited and may not be sufficient for a comprehensive comparability testing plan. However, new analytical technologies (e.g., charge detection mass spectrometry, small-volume subvisible particle method) that consume only small sample volumes are advantageous as their use will minimize the amount of product used from the manufactured lot.

As the focus of the process/product comparability exercise is to demonstrate product comparability, certain practices can be adopted during analytical testing:

  1. Test pre- and post-change products side-by-side within one laboratory and within one run/plate/sequence, if possible, to minimize assay variability. Assay setup can be adjusted to accommodate such needs (e.g., splitting two samples equally between two plates instead of using one plate for each sample).
  2. Use reagents (especially critical reagents) from the same vendor or even use the same lots where possible.
  3. When side-by-side testing is not possible due to material scarcity and product stability, it is beneficial to include a historical sample(s) (e.g., reference standard or assay control) in the testing of post-change products to monitor potential assay drift and justify the apparent difference it causes.

比喻說明 Analogy

三層次框架 = 汽車出廠安全驗證的三道關卡

Level 1（批次放行）= 出廠前的標準安全檢查，確認轉向、煞車等基本功能合格——但這只是最低門檻。Level 2（特性測試）= 更全面的工程檢測，包含震動測試、材質疲勞分析，找出標準檢查看不到的潛在問題。Level 3（穩定性）= 長期道路模擬測試，確認這輛車五年後的可靠度和新車一樣——對應的是「加速穩定性」的「壓力測試」概念。三道關卡都通過，才算真正可比較。

重點提示：ATMP 樣本稀缺的獨特挑戰

ATMP（先進治療製藥）的可比較性研究面臨一個傳統生物製藥沒有的根本性挑戰：樣本量極為有限。

對於一個罕見疾病基因治療產品，早期 Phase I 可能只生產了 2–3 批，每批只有幾瓶。這些有限的樣本要同時支撐：分析方法開發、參考標準建立、穩定性研究、臨床前毒理研究，再加上可比較性測試——根本不夠用。

業界的應對策略包括：

  • 採用 charge detection mass spectrometry（電荷偵測質譜儀） 等微量消耗技術
  • 增加同一樣本的重複測試次數，用統計方法彌補批次數不足
  • 早期建立 assay control（分析方法管控品），作為跨時間點比較的橋樑

實務應用：側邊並行測試的執行細節

「同板、同試劑、同時段」是可比較性測試的黃金準則，具體落實方法：

  • 同一個 run/plate/sequence：將變更前後的樣本放在同一塊 ELISA 板或同一次質譜分析序列中，消除批間試劑差異和儀器漂移
  • 樣本分板技巧：若一塊板放不下所有樣本，將兩個樣本「各取一半」分放在兩塊板上，而非一個樣本佔一整塊板——這樣即使有板間差異，兩個樣本所受的影響是對稱的
  • 關鍵試劑批次管控：尤其是抗體、細胞株等關鍵試劑，提前預留足夠的同批次量用於整個可比較性研究周期，避免途中試劑批次更換引入額外變因
  • 無法同板時的替代方案：在新批次測試中，加入歷史參考標準或 assay control，監測 assay drift（分析漂移），並定量計算漂移對測試結果的影響

重點提示：BLA 審查風險——樞紐性比較失敗的後果

原文特別提醒：即使是最完善的可比較性評估，也無法完全排除監管機構在後期要求體內評估（in vivo assessment）的風險。

更嚴重的情況是：如果 pivotal clinical（樞紐性臨床）批次與 PPQ（製程效能確認）批次之間的可比較性未能充分建立，BLA 審查過程中監管機構可能要求：

  • 補充橋接臨床試驗（bridging clinical study）——可能延誤上市一到兩年
  • 修訂規格（release specification）——需要重新提交技術文件
  • 在核准條件附加上市後承諾（post-approval commitment）

結論：可比較性研究的投資回報率（ROI）極高——前期投入充分的可比較性計畫，遠比事後補救節省時間和成本。

Q 5.3 — What approaches can be used when it is necessary to change test methods during a comparability protocol?

原文內容 Original Text

Changing test methods during product development is common. This is especially true for ATMPs as these therapies are relatively new and the analytical methods and associated controls necessary to fully monitor and assure product quality are still lagging behind other biological products, such as monoclonal antibodies.

Test methods should be defined and understood prior to assessing comparability. Therefore, it is undesirable to change both the methods and the attribute to be studied for comparability (e.g., site of manufacture); however, it is understood that ongoing development may necessitate changing or updating test methods at times. When updates are necessary, method bridging can be used as one approach to understand changes in test methods. Method bridging studies can be accomplished by testing the same set of samples by both methods. The method bridging is important to demonstrate method comparability. Including all relevant sample types (especially the stability sample) is essential to ensure thorough understanding of the method differences.

While less than ideal, it is acceptable to have bias between methods as long as the impact of the bias to the control strategy—in-process controls, release and stability specifications, and comparability criteria—is adequately assessed. Well-characterized method bias should be factored in when setting up comparability acceptance criteria if historical data generated using the old method will be used to compare against new data generated with the new method.

Both process comparability exercises and method bridging studies highlight the importance of keeping retention samples. This is extremely challenging for ATMPs since, during early development, the limited material available is needed for various purposes by multiple teams (e.g., analytical development, reference standard, stability, preclinical studies). For some of the assays, a platform approach or a product independent sample/control can be adopted. For a host protein or a DNA assay, for example, a sample from a different product using the same or similar manufacturing platform process could be used in the method bridging study.

Sample stability after long-term storage needs to be taken into consideration when side-by-side retesting is performed on historical samples together with recently generated samples. Differences may arise from the stability storage of the historical sample retained. In such situations, historical results or profiles at, or close to, time-zero samples should be leveraged to demonstrate comparability.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：方法銜接（Method Bridging）的運作機制

方法銜接的本質是：用一組「共同樣本集」作為橋樑，連接舊方法與新方法的結果空間。執行步驟如下：

  1. 選擇橋接樣本集：應包含代表性的多種樣本類型——高、中、低值樣本；不同批次；穩定性樣本（尤其重要）
  2. 同期雙軌測試：同一組樣本，同時用舊方法和新方法測試，取得配對數據
  3. 計算方法偏差（Method Bias）：統計分析兩種方法的系統性偏差（bias）和精度差異
  4. 評估影響範圍：評估該 bias 對放行規格、穩定性規格、可比較性判定標準的影響
  5. 更新控制策略：如需要，調整相應的驗收標準，並在文件中記錄 bias 的大小和方向

關鍵原則：方法之間存在 bias 是可以接受的，但必須是已充分定性（well-characterized）的 bias，而非未知的不確定性。

比喻說明 Analogy

歷史穩定性樣本 = 在酒瓶中慢慢變化的老酒

想像你封存了一瓶葡萄酒，打算五年後再品嚐，看它是否還和五年前一樣。但問題是：即使你什麼都沒改變，酒本身在瓶中也會繼續演化——單寧軟化、香氣改變。當五年後你用新的品酒技術重新分析它，差異到底來自「品酒技術改變」，還是「酒本身在瓶中的自然演化」？

ATMP 的歷史穩定性樣本面臨同樣的問題：長期冷凍保存也會造成輕微的樣本降解。因此在用歷史樣本進行方法銜接時，必須先扣除「儲存效應（storage effect）」，才能正確歸因差異的來源。

重點提示：平台方法作為保留樣本匱乏的替代方案

當 ATMP 保留樣本完全不足以支撐方法銜接研究時，平台方法（platform approach）提供了一個實用的替代策略：

核心思路：對於「產品非依賴性」的分析項目，可以用「不同產品但相同平台」的樣本作為銜接研究的代理材料。

適用例子：

  • 宿主細胞蛋白質（Host Cell Protein, HCP）檢測：如果兩個不同的 AAV 產品都使用 HEK293 細胞作為生產系統，其中一個產品的 HCP 標準品可以為另一個產品的 HCP 方法銜接提供代理樣本
  • 宿主細胞 DNA（Residual Host Cell DNA）檢測：使用相同細胞株來源的 DNA 樣本，可以作為不同產品的共用方法銜接材料

限制與注意事項：這個替代方案僅適用於「方法性能驗證」目的，不能用於替代針對具體產品特性的可比較性結論。監管機構的接受度需要個案評估。

重點提示：黃金法則——不要同時改變方法與製程

原文明確提出：「it is undesirable to change both the methods and the attribute to be studied for comparability」

這是一條黃金法則，其背後邏輯是：

如果在可比較性研究進行中，你同時改變了「被比較的製程/廠址」以及「用於比較的分析方法」，那麼觀察到的任何差異就無法明確歸因——是製程差異造成的？還是方法差異造成的？兩者交絡（confounded），可比較性結論就失去說服力。

實務建議：如果製程變更與方法升級不得不同時發生，先完成方法銜接（method bridging），建立舊/新方法的等效性關係，再開始製程可比較性評估。這樣即使方法不同，也能通過已知的 bias 關係將結果換算到同一基準。

Q 6.1 — How can platform technologies be used to improve quality and efficiency in the development of ATMP products?

原文內容 Original Text

A platform is a model process (or model analytics) from which variations are developed. The term "platform" was adopted as the model represents the jumping-off point for any new development efforts. For example, a model process would have identical unit operations for the majority of the process steps with the same process controls (the process backbone) with only targeted changes, such as an adjustment to an individual process parameter or setpoint, to adapt to the needed conditions specific to the biological entity (e.g., strain).

Within the industry, the term platform may also be applied to specific unit operation equipment technology. Although used in this context, the terminology may be imprecise in some cases, as the use of a particular piece of equipment or group of equipment does not ensure that the same process or analytics are maintained. While these equipment "platforms" can help to standardize and streamline qualification, validation, and overall control strategies, they may not provide the same level of regulatory efficiency that are being described in this section.

The development of multiple products based on one or more common technologies, or platforms, offers an approach to effectively use prior knowledge from existing products as new products are developed. The interest in pursuing platform technologies has risen significantly in the last several years, particularly for ATMPs, as these products are typically developed under aggressive timelines due to the nature and promise of the therapeutic effect. The use of platforms can allow for:

  • Standardization during manufacturing process development
  • Standardization during development of analytical methods
  • Establishment of harmonized CQAs and the associated risk-based assessments
  • Establishment of consistent process and analytical control strategies
  • Harmonization of regulatory submission content and technical support documents
  • Reduced costs and timelines associated with development activities
  • Reduced time for review and approval by regulators (when prior art has already been approved)
  • Efficient process qualification approaches (e.g., the inserted coding region in a common viral-vector or plasmid drug substance is produced and purified using the same overall production and control processes)

The U.S. government has pilot programs promoting the development of platform technology (e.g., the "Paving the Way for Rare Disease Gene Therapies" project) as it is understood that the benefits to patients from the therapies, and the possible shortening of the time to market (which for traditional development pathways has been more than 7–10 years), is in the best interest of the public.

The use of a technology platform also contributes to enhanced process knowledge on an ongoing basis as data from additional molecules become available and can be used to further characterize and refine a platform.

The use of platforms is consistent with QbD (see Topic 2) and robust control strategies (see Topic 3) as there is a heavy emphasis on prior knowledge and a reliance on effective knowledge management to collect and organize the critical data that ensures appropriate conclusions can be drawn to strengthen the knowledge of the design space (i.e., the combination of critical material attributes and critical process parameter ranges that define the successful product manufacture) and the performance of the process.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析：製程平台 vs. 設備平台

製程平台 (Process Platform)：指一套標準化的模型製程，包含相同的 unit operations（單元操作）、製程控制邏輯（process backbone），以及一致的分析方法。每個新產品只需針對特定生物實體（例如不同血清型 serotype 或不同基因 gene of interest, GOI）進行最小化的參數調整，而不必從頭設計製程。這是 PtC-11 在本節中所強調的「真正的平台」。

設備平台 (Equipment Platform)：僅指特定設備或設備組合的標準化（例如相同品牌的生物反應器或層析系統）。文件明確指出：設備相同不等於製程相同。設備平台可協助簡化確效（validation）與控制策略，但其帶來的監管效率遠不及製程平台。

一句話區分：設備平台是「廚房一樣」，製程平台是「連食譜、步驟、品管都一樣，只換食材」。

平台經濟學：成本與時程的量化優勢

傳統 ATMP 新藥開發從 IND 到上市通常需要 7–10 年，而平台技術可從多個維度壓縮時間與成本：

  • 研發成本：共用的 CQA 定義、風險評估框架、分析方法開發，大幅減少重複工作。
  • 驗證/確效成本：矩陣式 PPQ（matrix approach）允許以更少批次完成製程效能確效（Process Performance Qualification）。
  • 法規時程：已核准的先例（prior art）讓審查員熟悉平台架構，理論上可縮短審查週期。
  • 知識複利效應：每個新分子的數據都回饋到平台，強化整體設計空間的認識，形成正向循環。

對 CDMO 而言，這不只是科學問題，更是商業競爭力：能夠向客戶提供「平台服務」的 CDMO，在招募新客戶時具有顯著優勢。

比喻說明：平台如同標準化的餐廳廚房

想像一家連鎖餐廳集團——每家分店的廚房設備、擺設、工作流程都完全相同（這是「process backbone」），主廚只需根據不同菜單換用不同食材或調整少數調味步驟（這是「targeted adjustments」）。

新分店開幕時，不需要重新設計廚房、重新培訓所有流程——只需學習新菜單的差異即可。這就是平台的價值：大量重複使用，最小化差異。

反觀非平台開發：每個新產品都要從頭設計製程、從頭做研究，等同於每次開餐廳都重新翻修廚房——費時、費錢，而且難以保證一致的品質。

重點提示：Prior Art（先例技術）的監管戰略意義

「先發者優勢」在法規申請中尤為重要。當一家公司的 AAV 平台首次被 FDA 或 EMA 核准後，後續基於同一平台的產品申請，監管機構對平台架構已具備熟悉度，審查員的問題會聚焦在「差異」而非「基礎」，從而縮短審查時間。

這正是文件中提到的「reduced time for review and approval by regulators (when prior art has already been approved)」的核心意涵。對 CDMO 而言，若能代表行業標杆客戶首先建立平台 prior art，則後續其他客戶使用相同平台時，監管路徑將更加順暢。

美國政府的 PaVe-GT（Paving the Way for Rare Disease Gene Therapies）計畫設立於 NCATS（National Center for Advancing Translational Sciences），正是政策層面對此方向的具體支持。

實務應用：CDMO 如何向客戶推銷平台價值

當 CDMO 與新的基因治療客戶洽談時，若客戶的 AAV 血清型與 CDMO 既有平台匹配，可以這樣呈現平台優勢：

  • 時程縮短：核心製程設計無需從頭驗證，僅需確認特定於 GOI 的工藝調整。
  • 成本降低：法規文件大部分可重用，PPQ 可能採用 matrix approach 以減少批次數。
  • 知識保證：平台已積累多個分子的製程數據，設計空間更完善，風險更低。
  • 監管可預期性：平台元素已有先例，減少不確定性。
Q 6.2 — What types of ATMPs are most likely to benefit from the application of platform technologies?

原文內容 Original Text

For different ATMPs, the decision to use one or more platforms is driven by different factors including stage of development (proof of concept versus licensed product), cost, target disease, and intellectual property.

ATMPs are a highly diverse group of products and, consequently, there is a range in the extent to which platform technologies can be applied. AAV vectors are an example of a product class that makes extensive use of platform technologies, where the manufacture of an AAV vector may use the same production methods and controls as the manufacture of a different product with the same vector serotype and different gene-of-interest (GOI) sequence. Different technologies for manufacturing AAVs have been used to develop a range of products, making it possible to use this prior knowledge to develop an understanding of how the process needs to be controlled. In contrast, an ex-vivo genetically modified cell product relies upon lot-specific starting materials and a number of different technologies are used to create a wide range of custom product types that are classified as ATMPs.

For pDNA used as a drug substance, FDA and EMA have accepted efficient PPQ approaches when multiple pDNA expressing different antigens are a) produced from the same backbone plasmid vector, b) produced with identical unit operations with the same master batch-record (MBR) and testing, and c) are combined in a single drug product. In such examples, regulatory agencies have agreed to fewer than three consecutive runs for each plasmid individually, based on a matrix approach for PPQ of the platform. In another example, in the vaccine industry, the production of drug substances with different serotypes or strains are not independently validated, but specific parameter targets are adjusted and confirmed via initial production batches.

Similarly, for CAR-T products/lentiviral vector (LV) platforms, four or more different vectors that share a common backbone and yet differ in the cell-surface-receptor-coding region and potency testing, have been filed in an IND as a platform using QbD principles, avoiding repetitive CMC sections in each Biologics License Application (BLA).

Platform technologies are used extensively both in the production and testing of ATMPs. For some products, individual manufacturing or testing activities may be based on the platform and, in other cases, platform technologies may account for the majority of all activities. For AAV vectors, for example, the platforms may differ based on such elements as cell lines, AAV serotypes, and/or construction methods.

實務應用：向 FDA/EMA 論證 Matrix PPQ（pDNA CDMO 場景）

假設 CDMO 同時生產客戶 A 的三種 mRNA 疫苗所用的 pDNA，三種質粒共享相同骨架，僅插入片段（抗原序列）不同。如何向監管機構爭取 matrix PPQ（每個質粒少於 3 個連續批次）？

需同時滿足三個條件（FDA/EMA 核准先例）：

  1. 相同骨架：三種 pDNA 均使用同一 backbone plasmid vector，書面確認載體骨架序列相同。
  2. 相同 unit operations + 同一 MBR：生產流程完全一致（發酵、細胞裂解、純化），使用同一套 master batch record 僅填入不同 pDNA 識別資訊。
  3. 組合於同一藥品：三種 pDNA 最終組合成單一藥品（combined in a single drug product）。

滿足上述條件後，可向 FDA/EMA 申請：以平台 PPQ 矩陣設計替代逐一驗證，用較少批次（例如每種 pDNA 各 1 批，加上 matrix 交叉批次）來代替每種 9 個批次的傳統要求，大幅節省時間與成本。

重點提示：CAR-T IND 平台申報——減少 BLA 重複的策略

對於 CAR-T/LV 平台，PtC-11 描述了一個策略：在 IND 階段將 4 個以上共用骨架的載體以「平台」方式申報，其 CMC 部分（製程描述、分析方法、對照品等）在 IND 中統一撰寫，後續各個 BLA 只需引用此平台 CMC 資料，針對各自的臨床數據及特定差異（例如不同細胞表面受體編碼區、效力測試）補充即可。

這樣的策略可避免在每一份 BLA 中重複撰寫相同的 CMC 內容，節省大量申報資源，同時使監管機構能夠快速定位差異所在，提升審查效率。

關鍵前提：平台 CMC 的共用元素必須在 IND 階段即建立清楚的邊界與引用框架，後期才能順利延伸至 BLA。

Q 6.3 — What are the considerations for developing and using platforms for ATMP manufacturing?

原文內容 Original Text

Any platform-leveraging approach should make use of strong risk-based rationales for all variations in processes and process control. The magnitude of the risk should drive decisions regarding when supporting studies may be required.

There is a need to carefully evaluate all changes from the baseline platform during development to determine which foundational studies may need to be repeated, in whole or in part, due to platform changes. For example:

  • Changes to upstream processing may result in the need to recharacterize cell-expansion curves, feed regimes, end of production cells, or limit of in-vitro cell age
  • Changes to downstream processing or the purification process parameters might require reevaluation of baseline studies such as small-scale viral clearance and small-scale resin lifetime studies
  • Changes at any point in the process may result in an incremental change to the analytical methods, so it is important to ensure that appropriate bridging and comparability are performed (see Topic 5)

While a platform may be able to be filed as a common control strategy, it is important to remember that individual products will require separate BLAs related to the individual product's clinical-trial results and control-strategy-specific variations.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：偏離平台的風險評估清單

任何對基準平台（baseline platform）的偏離，都必須經過正式的風險評估流程。以下是評估框架的關鍵問題：

  • 偏離的性質：是上游（細胞培養）、下游（純化）、還是分析方法的變更？
  • 影響的 CQA：此偏離可能影響哪些 CQA？影響的機制是什麼？
  • 風險等級：依據影響患者安全的程度，定義是否需要重複進行基礎研究（foundational studies）。
  • 所需補充研究：
    • 上游變更 → 細胞擴增曲線、饋料策略、生產末期細胞特性、體外細胞年齡上限
    • 下游/純化變更 → 小規模病毒清除研究 (small-scale viral clearance)、樹脂壽命研究 (resin lifetime)
    • 任何製程變更 → 分析方法橋接與可比性研究（參見 Topic 5）
  • 文件要求：所有偏離決策均需有書面的風險評估支持，不可口頭決策。

重點提示：病毒清除研究——即使是平台也不能豁免

許多人誤以為使用既有平台就可以免除所有驗證。這是危險的誤解。

病毒清除研究（viral clearance studies）和樹脂壽命研究（resin lifetime studies）是純化平台中最關鍵的安全性研究。當下游製程（purification process parameters）發生任何變更時，這些研究必須重新評估——即便只是微小的操作條件調整（例如洗脫 pH 值、鹽濃度），也可能影響病毒清除的對數降低值（log reduction value, LRV）。

監管機構（FDA、EMA）對這類安全性研究高度敏感，申報資料若無法提供下游變更對病毒清除影響的評估，很可能收到 deficiency letter。

建議：在 CDMO 的平台治理文件中，預先定義哪些下游參數變更屬於「觸發重做」的閾值，避免臨上市前才發現需補做昂貴的病毒清除研究。

實務應用：CDMO 平台治理框架（何時用核心平台、何時偏離、如何記錄）

對於提供 AAV 或 pDNA 平台服務的 CDMO，建議建立三層治理架構：

第一層：核心平台（Core Platform）
定義所有客戶共用的 unit operations、MBR 框架、CQA 清單、基礎研究（病毒清除、樹脂壽命等）的既有數據包。新客戶直接調用，無需重做基礎研究。

第二層：允許偏離（Permitted Deviations）
預先評估哪些參數可在指定範圍內調整（例如 MOI、培養溫度±1°C），並定義每種偏離所需的最少驗證工作（例如 1 批 engineering run 即可）。此層需有書面的風險評估模板供客戶使用。

第三層：重大變更（Major Deviations）
超出允許範圍的變更（例如更換細胞株、改變純化策略），需啟動完整的製程開發與驗證流程。此類情況不再屬於「平台服務」，應與客戶重新協商合約範圍與費用。

清楚的治理框架不僅有助於監管一致性，也能在商務層面保護 CDMO 免於因客戶過度客製化而承擔未預算的研究成本。

重點提示：平台控制策略 vs. 個別 BLA——兩者不可混淆

PtC-11 在 Q6.3 結尾清楚指出：即使多個產品共用同一平台控制策略，每個產品仍需提交獨立的 BLA。原因在於：

  • 各產品的臨床試驗結果（安全性、有效性數據）不可共用。
  • 控制策略的特定差異（例如不同 GOI 帶來的不同釋放規格）需在各自 BLA 中明確說明。

換言之，平台讓你在 CMC 層面「做一次，用多次」，但臨床數據包永遠是產品專屬的。這條界線在 BLA 申報策略規劃時必須清楚認識，避免誤將平台 CMC 引用關係與個別產品審核義務混淆。

Q7.1 — Key Attributes of an Effective Product-Specific Potency Assay

原文內容 Original Text

Potency is an expression of the activity of a drug substance or drug product in terms of the amount or concentration required to produce a defined effect. Clinical efficacy, on the other hand, is a measure of the therapeutic effectiveness of the drug in humans. Regulators desire a product-specific potency assay that provides a demonstrable link between potency and clinical efficacy. As such, the development team should first identify the relevant biological property linked to the product's in vivo mechanism of action (MOA) and then establish a method to provide a quantitative measure of that biological property. The assay must demonstrate that its performance remains unaffected by expected variability in product purity or matrix effects.

Other than safety, potency is the most critical quality attribute of a product. The ideal potency assay should reflect the MOA of the drug and demonstrate the product has the expected ultimate "functionality" that correlates with clinical efficacy. For an in-vivo gene therapy product, the ultimate functionality means the action of the expressed (or transcribed) gene of interest (GOI). If the product is a protein, then demonstration of the expected bioactivity (e.g., enzymatic activity) of the expressed protein is the ultimate goal of potency assay development. For an activated cell therapy product, for example, this broadly means that there must be an in vitro analytical method that correlates to in vivo reduction of tumor size or number or reducing the progression to metastasis.

Potency is a CQA for stability, not just release; therefore, an effective potency assay must also demonstrate that it is stability-indicating. To do this, forced degradation studies should be performed which demonstrate the primary degradation pathways of the product. With the samples showing between 10% and 20% degradation for each condition (where possible), as confirmed by orthogonal methods, the potency assay must be able to accurately detect the associated loss in potency.

If the product will be produced across multiple sites, it should be recognized that small differences in process and materials may exist site to site. Ideally, the potency assay should be robust enough to account for these small differences when they do not represent a difference in clinical effect.

To effectively quantitate potency, it is necessary to establish a reference standard against which the response from each lot will be quantitated. Initially, the reference standard may be a well-characterized development lot as an interim reference standard. However, as product development advances, the company must qualify a primary reference standard that will be used for commercial production. Bridging will likely be required to link the quantitation of early lots (and their stability studies) to any new reference standard that is introduced (see Topic 5). It is especially important to maintain a quantitative reference to clinical lots.

"Since development of an appropriate potency assay is complex, it is recommended to collect a significant amount of bioanalytical characterization data on early product lots until it is demonstrated that the developed potency assay is robust to the product and process and is predictive of MOA."

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析：效價、藥效與臨床療效的三角關係

Potency（效價）：產生特定生物效果所需的藥物劑量或濃度。這是一個體外可量測的生物活性數值，例如 AAV 載體感染 HEK293 細胞後，GOI 表現之酵素活性 (enzymatic activity) IU/mL。

Clinical Efficacy（臨床療效）：藥物在真實病患身上的治療有效性，僅能在臨床試驗中驗證。舉例而言，罕見溶酶體儲積症 (lysosomal storage disease) 基因治療的臨床療效，是指病患器官功能改善、生化指標恢復正常。

兩者的關鍵連結：法規機構要求效價分析必須能可驗證地連結（demonstrable link）到臨床療效。這意味著：

  • AAV 基因治療（蛋白替代）：體外酵素活性測定值需與動物模型中酵素替代療效有統計相關性
  • CAR-T 細胞治療：體外腫瘤殺傷能力（in vitro tumor killing assay）需與動物或臨床腫瘤縮小的 in vivo 結果相關

簡言之：效價是臨床療效的體外代理指標，必須有充分數據證明兩者的關聯。沒有此連結，效價測定就只是一個數字，對法規審查毫無意義。

比喻說明：引擎測功機 vs. 上路試駕

把效價分析想成在測功機（dynamometer）上測量汽車引擎馬力——不是把車開上公路看跑多快，而是在受控的實驗室條件下精確測量引擎輸出功率。

測功機數值 = Potency（效價）：量化、可重現、可控制條件下測得的生物活性數值。

上路實際表現 = Clinical Efficacy（臨床療效）：受道路條件、天氣、駕駛習慣影響，難以在開發階段精確預測。

法規要求的「demonstrable link」就是要你證明：測功機測出 500 馬力的引擎，上路後確實比 300 馬力的引擎快——這個相關性必須用數據建立，不能只假設。

重點提示：穩定性指示分析 (Stability-Indicating Assay) 設計要點

許多團隊誤以為效價只是放行用途。PtC-11 明確強調：效價是穩定性 CQA，因此效價分析必須能偵測到因儲存降解造成的活性下降。

強制降解研究（Forced Degradation Study）設計原則：

  • 每個降解條件目標：達到 10–20% 的降解幅度（過高降解會破壞產品結構，偵測不到真實降解路徑）
  • 常見條件：熱降解（elevated temperature）、氧化（H₂O₂ 處理）、光照（ICH Q1B）、機械剪切（freeze-thaw cycles）
  • 必須以正交方法（orthogonal methods）確認降解程度，例如 SEC-HPLC 確認蛋白聚集、流式細胞儀確認細胞活性下降
  • 確認降解後，效價分析必須能定量偵測到對應的活性損失

若效價分析無法偵測到降解樣品的活性下降，它就不是穩定性指示性分析，不能用於穩定性研究，法規審查時將面臨重大挑戰。

實務應用：CDMO 參考標準橋接策略

在 ATMP CDMO 的實際作業中，參考標準的演進通常經歷三個階段：

  • Phase 1 IND 申請前：使用開發批次作為臨時參考標準（interim RS）。此時需詳細記錄批次特性（GMP 批次還是研究批次、製程版本、效價測定方法版本）。
  • Phase 2/3 過渡期：製備並資格確認初級參考標準（primary RS）——需以 GMP 批次、充足的穩定性數據、全套特性分析支持。
  • 橋接研究（Bridging Study）：新 RS 引入時，必須以平行測試方式建立新舊 RS 的定量關聯，確保早期臨床批次的效價數據與新 RS 體系一致可追溯。

關鍵實務建議：在臨時 RS 階段就開始建立大量生物分析特性數據（bioanalytical characterization data）。這些數據一旦發現效價分析需要調整，就是橋接的救命稻草。文件記錄要做到有人能在 5 年後重建每一批次的效價歷史。

Q7.2 — Acceptable Approaches for Phasing in a Product-Specific Potency Assay

原文內容 Original Text

Due to the complex nature of ATMP products, developing a potency assay that is truly reflective of the MOA of the product is challenging, especially in the early development stage. Some of the GOIs encode structural proteins that lack clearly indicated bioactivity, while the functions of other GOIs are multifaceted, while some are not yet fully understood. Regulatory agencies are aware of such challenges and typically allow for flexibility in determining the most appropriate potency assay for each product at each development stage. The expectations for the potency assay increase proportionally as the drug development progresses.

A widely accepted approach for potency development is the matrix approach. A combination of various assays is used to assess product potency holistically. It starts with the measurement of a DNA copy number, vector particle number, cell counts, and cell-viability physical attributes that are necessary (but may not be sufficient) for potency. As product development progresses, the matrix evolves, and certain methods may drop out due to complexity or robustness concerns. Other assays developed in parallel that are more reflective of MOA and demonstrate functionality of the product may become more central to the testing scheme. The matrix can comprise both quantitative and qualitative assays. For example, a binding assay can provide quantitative results compared to the reference standard while a cell-based assay can confirm the expected functionality (pass or fail). The correlation between the quantitative and qualitative assays can be established during assay development to justify the use of the combination of quantitative and qualitative assays in routine release.

In specific cases where there is no ability to directly measure MOA, it may be possible to use more than one assay to represent potency, providing regulatory agreement is granted. For example, in the specific case of pDNA or mRNA as the drug product, there is no specific in vitro assay indicating MOA because transcription and translation of the drug product for presentation to the immune system is required in vivo. In these cases, FDA has accepted direct measurement of nucleic acid coupled with integrity of the molecule (e.g., supercoil measurement) as surrogates for potency assay.

The ideal lot-release assay for routine quality control release, needs to balance its capability and reliability. A complicated, research-grade assay that can only be conducted on a sophisticated instrument by a highly trained scientist is not feasible for routine quality control testing of the product. A simple, robust, and reproducible assay is preferred. A robust data package is expected to justify using a surrogate assay for functionality potency assay. A correlation study using a regular drug substance or drug product, an in-process degraded sample, and/or specially constructed products with reduced or elevated potency would be valuable to support the assay choice. Open and timely communication with regulatory agencies on a potency assay strategy is of critical importance for the success of a project. Many clinical trial applications and licensure approvals have been delayed due to potency assay concerns in recent years.

Recently, FDA proposed a multifaceted approach combining manufacturing process design, process control, material control, in-process testing, and potency lot release assays as a systematic potency assurance strategy (Ref 16). Sponsors need to prepare for a holistic overview of potency assurance in addition to a robust, MOA reflective potency assay.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：矩陣式方法的臨床分期演進

矩陣式方法（matrix approach）不是一個固定的測試組合，而是一個隨臨床分期動態演進的策略：

開發階段 典型矩陣組成 監管期望
Phase 1
IND 申請		 DNA copy number + 載體顆粒數 + 細胞活性（基本物理屬性）+ 早期功能性替代指標 彈性接受；需說明 MOA 連結開發計畫
Phase 2/3
BLA 準備期		 定量結合分析（binding assay）+ 細胞活性分析（cell-based assay）；逐步退出複雜度高或重現性差的方法 期望有明確的 MOA 連結；定量與定性分析的相關性數據
商業化
BLA 申請		 簡化、穩健、可轉移的 QC 放行組合；完整驗證（validation） 必須是穩定性指示性分析；多地點適用性；完整 RS 體系

關鍵概念：矩陣中定量分析（quantitative，如 binding assay 對比 RS）與定性分析（qualitative，如 cell-based 通過/失敗）的相關性需在開發期間建立，才能在放行時合理使用兩者的組合。

比喻說明：健康檢查套餐 vs. 單一指標

矩陣式效價方法就像全套健康檢查，不是只看一項指標決定你健不健康：

  • 血糖（fasting glucose）= DNA copy number → 必要但不充分
  • 肝功能指數（ALT/AST）= 載體顆粒數 → 結構完整性指標
  • 心臟壓力測試 = Cell-based 腫瘤殺傷測試 → 功能性最終驗證
  • 心電圖定量 = Binding assay 對比 RS → 定量功能性基準

單一指標偏高或偏低可能只是雜訊，但多項指標的綜合圖像才能真正反映健康狀態。同理，矩陣中沒有任何單一分析能獨立定義效價——是各項分析共同構成完整的效價畫面。

而且，隨著年齡增長（=開發階段進展），健康檢查的項目和標準也會升級，這正是矩陣動態演進的本質。

重點提示：pDNA/mRNA 的特殊效價策略

pDNA 和 mRNA 作為藥品時，面臨一個根本性的挑戰：它們的 MOA 必須在體內轉錄/翻譯後才能發揮作用，因此沒有直接的體外 MOA 分析可用。

FDA 接受的替代指標（Surrogates）組合：

  • 直接核酸定量（Direct nucleic acid quantitation）：確認 pDNA 或 mRNA 的含量與規格一致
  • 超螺旋完整性測定（Supercoil measurement，適用 pDNA）：確認 pDNA 的超螺旋比例，因超螺旋形式轉染效率最高；降解或開環形式效力顯著下降
  • 完整性分析（Integrity，適用 mRNA）：確認 mRNA 無降解截斷片段，通常以毛細管電泳或 Bioanalyzer 測定

重要提醒：這是 FDA 在特定情境下接受的折衷方案，不是通則。使用替代指標必須提交強健的數據包，證明替代指標與效力的相關性（例如超螺旋比例與體外轉染效率、或與動物 in vivo 蛋白表現量的相關性）。

實務應用：CDMO 的 QC 放行分析設計原則

身為 CDMO，當客戶帶著複雜的研究級效價分析方法要求放行使用時，需要協助客戶重新思考以下問題：

  • 可執行性：分析是否可在 QC 實驗室環境（非研究環境）中由標準訓練水準的分析師執行？
  • 重現性：批次間、分析師間、設備間的重現性數據（%CV）是否符合驗證接受標準？
  • 週轉時間：細胞培養基礎的功能分析通常需要 3–7 天，是否符合產品放行時間要求？
  • 替代分析的數據包：若提議使用更簡單的替代指標，需準備：
    (1) 正常樣品 vs. 降解樣品的相關性研究
    (2) 人工低效價樣品（reduced potency）和高效價樣品（elevated potency）的驗證數據
    (3) 多地點轉移（technology transfer）可行性評估

CDMO 策略建議：在合約簽訂前的技術評估（technical feasibility assessment）階段，即主動與客戶討論效價分析的可行性，避免在 BLA 申請前夕才發現無法執行的分析方法。

重點提示：效價分析問題是 IND/BLA 延誤的首要原因

PtC-11 明確指出：近年來許多 IND 申請和 BLA 許可核准均因效價分析問題而延誤。這是法規實務上不容忽視的警示。

常見的延誤場景：

  • Phase 3 試驗中途才發現效價分析不是穩定性指示性的，需重新設計並橋接
  • BLA 申請後收到 FDA 資訊要求（Information Request），要求補充 MOA 連結數據
  • 商業化生產前夕，效價分析無法成功轉移到商業化地點
  • 參考標準體系未建立，早期臨床批次的效價數據無法追溯

解決方案：早期與法規機構溝通。FDA 鼓勵申請人在 Pre-IND Meeting 或 Type B Meeting 中主動討論效價測定策略，提前取得 HA 的認可，比事後補救節省數倍時間與成本。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 效價 vs. 臨床療效：效價是定義效果所需濃度/劑量的活性測量；臨床療效是人體治療有效性。法規要求兩者有可驗證的科學連結，缺一不可。
  • 效價是安全性以外最重要的 CQA，且必須同時服務於放行和穩定性兩個目的——穩定性指示分析設計必須與強制降解研究同步規劃。
  • 矩陣式方法是 ATMP 效價開發的業界共識，從基本物理屬性出發，隨臨床進展演進為 MOA 反映型分析，最終於 BLA 前整合為簡化、穩健的商業放行方案。
  • pDNA/mRNA 特例：FDA 接受核酸定量＋超螺旋完整性（pDNA）或完整性分析（mRNA）作為效價替代指標，但需要強健的相關性數據包支持。
  • 參考標準橋接：從臨時 RS 到初級 RS 的過渡必須嚴謹規劃，早期積累的生物分析特性數據是未來橋接的基礎。
  • FDA 2023 效價保證策略（Ref 16）提出五層次整合框架，CDMO 在品質協議中應明確各方效價保證責任分工。
  • 早期 HA 溝通是成功的關鍵——效價分析問題是近年 IND/BLA 延誤的首要原因，Pre-IND Meeting 即應主動提出效價策略。
