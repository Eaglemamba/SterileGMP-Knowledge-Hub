management plan.

9.10	 Commissioning and Qualification
When the SEC finds it beneficial, an all-day meeting will be called in order to approve documents 
and/or carry out reviews. This will be scheduled no less than one week in advance and with duration 
from one day to several days.
When the SEC finds it beneficial, an all-day commissioning day will be called in order to correct er-
rors and carry through cleanroom documents. This will be arranged no less than one week ahead and 
with duration from one day to several days.
The SEC is responsible for carrying out or supervising and reporting all qualification activities up to 
and including OQ.
Company X will participate in the test activities.
Company X provides QA resources during the OQ test period.
Company X is responsible for the calibration and maintenance after OQ.
9.11	 Handover and Operation Support
The project is handed over to the customer in three steps: 1) ready to run, 2) handover, and 3) final 
handover. Final handover is the last part of the project.
The project is handed over to the customer with the participation of the SEC project manager, relevant SEC 
and/or contract project engineers, and representatives from the customer of his or her own choosing.
If the customer wishes to put the area into use before the handover has taken place, the SEC project 
manager shall ensure that a physical examination of the area takes place together with the customer 
or the user and that an agreement on operation and maintenance responsibilities is made.
Prior to final handover, deviation lists will be resolved by a cleanup team.
Warranties from third-party vendors, suppliers, and subcontractors are handed over to Company X.
PQ and PV activities are not part of this project. The SEC recommends the client to plan on a PQ 
support team. SEC will supply resources for support in PQ/PV phase if Company X so wishes. The 
SEC resources will work under Company X supervision and management during PQ/PV.
