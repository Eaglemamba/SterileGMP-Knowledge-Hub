7.7.6	 Waste
The SUS strategy and PEP address waste management as an important part of SUS implementation 
because the amount of solid waste increases significantly for an SUS compared to an MUS. Some of 
the topics the PEP covers include:
• Handling and elimination of process fluids before disposal (118)
• Collection and storage of waste in the facility and avoiding cross contamination
• Inactivation of waste before it leaves the facility or site
• Dual-stream handling of waste where necessary (i.e., split infectious and noninfectious waste)
• Feasibility of recycling SUS components, including the infrastructure for collection and 
disassembly (119)
• Collection and storage of waste outside of the SUS facility
• Transport routes
• Frequency of pickup
• Training
• Safety
• The impact of local, national or regional regulation 
Section 5 also addresses the issue of waste for SUS.
An example of when regional or national regulations would be important would include an SUS facil-
ity that must eliminate waste such as biologics (i.e., naturally occurring pathogens, including trans-
missible agents, or GMOs) or highly active, potent, or sensitizing compounds. The use of landfills for 
disposing of SUS waste is legal in the United States but is not permitted in the European Union, where 
waste must be disinfected and incinerated prior to disposal.
Other end-of-lifecycle processes (e.g., the retirement or decommissioning process, as per GEP) can 
also be addressed at this stage. In addition, consider non-GxP issues (e.g., refurbishing and reusing 
an SUS, sale of an SUS, the effect of applicable national tax credits). The end user must be aware of 
international hazard guidance for biologics and chemical entities (120–122). A BPSA guide to waste 
disposal for SUS is available for reference (65).

8.4.5.6	Noise
A risk assessment regarding noise levels shall be performed. Both internal and external noise shall be 
analyzed.
8.4.6	 Maintenance and Service
A maintenance program for the whole suite shall be developed before start of production. The pro-
gram shall include the facility and technical equipment within, the ventilation system and equipment, 
the production equipment, and all laboratory equipment. The maintenance system shall produce, in 
advance, weekly reports of equipment that needs service.
All equipment in the area shall be assigned an item number, which shall be fed to the maintenance 
system. The maintenance personnel are responsible for service of all equipment. Two times a year 
(summer and winter) there shall be production stops for maintenance of the production equipment.
Spare parts for process equipment for two years of continuous production shall be stored.
