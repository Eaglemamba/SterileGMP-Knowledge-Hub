EM is an important element in bioburden control strategies for environments used for manufactur-

4.4.1	 Executive Summary

4.4.2	 Discussion

The output of the EM risk assessment is the level of risk at the EM sampling location expressed as a

were assigned in areas that either posed a very low risk of product contamination or sites that may 
have had higher risk classes, but the risk was mitigated by high-detection capabilities. The priority 
may have equated to a frequency or need to sample based on the potential to contribute to overall 
environmental contamination risk.

Risk Assessment Design: Example 2
This report summarizes results of the risk analysis conducted to evaluate the EM sample plan for a 
media supplementation (via aliquoting) process for a cell therapy manufacturing process. 
Knowledgeable personnel from the microbiology, manufacturing, quality assurance, compliance, and 
manufacturing science and technology departments participated in the risk analysis.
In cell therapy manufacturing, media supplementation is performed by adding sterile supplements 
into sterile media using a sterile pipette or other sterile transfer method. The process occurs inside 
an ISO 5-classified UDF hood. Routine EM is performed based on the results of a risk assessment. 
Personnel are appropriately trained on the process and on the proper aseptic techniques. Personnel 
also wear sterile gloves and sterile sleeves while performing the process. 
The media, supplements, and containers used during the process are sterile. In addition, an absence-
of-contamination test is performed post-process using a rapid microbiology method.
4.5.1	 Executive Summary
The EM program is designed to monitor manufacturing areas and ensure that the levels of environ-
mental controls are maintained as specified in site SOPs and in the laboratory information manage-
ment system. Using FMEA and following the risk assessment guidance methodology, an EM risk 
analysis was conducted to identify and quantify risks. The effort was intended to optimize the EM 
sample plans for media supplementation as well as to determine the appropriate ISO/Grade level 
needed. (See Table 4.3.1-1 for recommended classifications and types of activities.) Other risk tool 
methodology may be used depending on the specific process. 
4.5.2	 Discussion
A risk tool was executed using appropriate methodology to evaluate contamination risks during 
media supplementation. EM sample site locations, including personnel monitoring, and the types 
of monitoring were determined based on the nature of the process. The nature of the materials (i.e., 
sterile), duration of the process, personnel, material flow, and post-processing testing were considered 
during the analysis. The risk analysis evaluated each EM sample location for hazards including the
