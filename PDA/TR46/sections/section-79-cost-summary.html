7.0 Cost Management and Sustainability

To ensure product availability with the right quality, effectiveness, and safety, cost-effective measures are applicable across the supply chain, from the manufacturer to the points of dispensing and administration. These measures, which include but are not limited to, inventory management, sustainability programs, and temperature control, should help prevent product shortages and have a positive impact on patient health.

7.1 Inventory Management

7.1.1 Rotating versus Stockpiling

An important criterion for the cost of stockpiles is if they are rotating or static.

Emergency stockpiles for crisis situations: Product shelf life varies by the type of product. Stockpiles can be partially dynamic, but also require holding stocks that do not rotate if they are dedicated to a specific emergency or to crisis situations and therefore need to be held as part of a static stockpiling approach (e.g., stockpiling of masks prior to the COVID-19 pandemic or, more recently, iodine tablets). If stocks are not managed on a rotating basis with "normal" inventory, there is a high risk of expiration. This necessitates their disposal, generating unnecessary additional costs, creating a complete loss of the initial investment, and triggering a negative environmental impact. A static stockpile, with no stock rotation, might therefore result in a large volume of expired stock that needs to be replenished, causing additional costs for their creation, maintenance, and the turnover of the stockpile. Who covers the cost of expired products and who pays for their disposal should be defined by contract.

Increasing buffer stocks for critical or essential medicines: This could be integrated via rotation into pharmaceutical full-line wholesaler/full-service healthcare distributor rolling stocks thereby reducing the risk of products expiring and could be carried out on a FEFO basis, i.e., dynamic stockpiling. Naturally, the shelf life of products differs according to the type of product; however, buffer stocks can be organized in a way that the products are continuously used and replenished. In that case, the length of storage, expiry date of products, and potential safe disposal of unwanted medicines need to be considered. Increased buffer stocks held at the wholesale level need to be purchased from the pharmaceutical industry, which increases the capital costs. This is one of the main reasons, other than available warehouse space, why the establishment of larger stockpiles of more than two weeks at the level of pharmaceutical full-line wholesalers and full-service healthcare distributors is highly challenging. This can be attributed to insufficient margins, while also holding a prefinancing function for the healthcare supply chain.

7.1.2 Organizational and Administrative Reflections

Additional administrative and logistics costs may apply to storage, based on the conditions and to whom the safety stocks should be delivered. Costs are higher the more frequently the list of products to be stocked changes because it requires a greater effort to administer the stocks. A decision should also be made as to what condition the emergency stocks will be supplied or released to the market, how they should be rotated (e.g., FEFO), and how the rationalization or allocation system would be organized with respect to market deliveries to ensure equitable access.

7.1 庫存管理 — 解析

FIFO vs. FEFO：藥品應選哪一個？

FIFO（First In First Out，先進先出）：按入庫時間順序出庫，適用於保質期均一的商品（如一般食品）。

FEFO（First Expired First Out，先效期先出）：按最早到期日順序出庫，無論入庫時間先後。

藥品為什麼必須用 FEFO？同一批次到貨的藥品，剩餘效期可能因補貨批次不同而有差異。FEFO 確保近效期產品優先出庫，最大限度減少過期浪費。GDP 要求倉儲流程採用 FEFO 原則（TR46 Section 7.1.1）。

囤積（Stockpiling）的風險：COVID-19 教訓

COVID-19 疫情初期，各國政府和醫院大量囤積口罩、消毒用品及部分藥品（如碘化鉀片）。靜態囤積（Static Stockpiling）的後果：

  • 效期浪費：囤積品若未與正常庫存輪換，大量到期銷毀
  • 資金凍結：採購成本全額損失，無殘值回收
  • 供應鏈扭曲：搶購造成正常配送斷鏈，患者無法取得所需藥品
  • 環境負擔：大批次銷毀帶來額外廢棄物處理成本

動態輪換（Dynamic Stockpiling / FEFO）則可將緩衝庫存融入日常供應流，有效避免上述問題。

安全庫存量（Safety Stock）計算思考

基本公式：

Safety Stock = Z × σ(Lead Time) × 日均消耗量

其中 Z 為安全係數（服務水準對應的標準差倍數，如 95% 服務水準 → Z = 1.65）。

最後一哩特別考量：最後一哩配送因天氣、交通、海關等變數，Lead Time 波動比一般批發配送更大。因此安全係數需適度提高，並定期以歷史配送資料重新校正。

思考題：若某冷藏藥品平均日銷量 50 單位，Lead Time 標準差 2 天，目標服務水準 98%（Z≈2.05），安全庫存應設多少？

7.2 Warehouse Storage and Temperature Control

The inventory level, buffer-stock determination, and size of individual packs should be considered when establishing the right size of warehouse. Choosing the right size of the warehouse is key to minimizing the cost of energy that will be required for temperature control. The cost of energy is one of the highest fixed costs of an organization. The use of engineering solutions can be helpful in saving energy. For example, mineral wool batting, polyurethane (PUR), polyisocyanurate (PIR) and polystyrene (EPS), used as insulation to reduce energy consumption. Also, the application of solar panels may be used to generate power and reduce energy costs.

7.2 倉儲溫控成本 — 深度解析

台灣 GMP/GDP 倉儲要求對照

台灣藥品倉儲需符合 GMP（衛福部食藥署）及 GDP 要求，主要包括：

  • 溫濕度持續監控（須保存至少效期後一年的記錄）
  • 溫度分佈測試（每 3 年或設備變更後重新執行）
  • 防蟲防鼠措施（定期蟲害防治合約）
  • 隔離區設置：合格品 / 不合格品 / 退貨品 / 待驗品需分區或加鎖
  • 緊急用備用電源（停電後的持溫時間需依產品要求設計）

台灣對比歐盟 GDP：歐盟要求更嚴格的 QRM（品質風險管理）文件，台灣目前主要依藥廠 SOP 執行，尚無統一 GDP 單一法規文件。

7.3 Waste Stream for Packaging Components and Drug Product

Sections 7.3–7.5 provide a non-exhaustive overview of various global directives and waste stream management for packaging components used to distribute temperature-sensitive products in the last mile. Packaging components, including ice bricks, boxes, and isolated containers, may generate waste for each container shipped (e.g., 20 kg per container). Depending on the time period, thousands of waste materials can be generated, hence the importance of studying the reuse or recycling for this business.

7.3.1 Recycling and Regulations

Most national and supranational entities have legislative guidelines in effect governing the proper post-transit disposal of pharmaceutical products and devices. Some also cover the packaging materials used during transport. Currently, the "extended producer responsibility" or Extended Product Responsibility (EPR) program, has been instituted in several countries. The concept behind EPR implies that everyone along the product chain shares responsibility for the environmental impact of packaging. That includes the upstream considerations of material selection and manufacturing processes to the downstream effects of use, waste management, and disposal. However, this approach largely sidesteps the issue of who is directly responsible for the waste and disposal of post-consumer packaging. Some corporations have instituted their own EPR programs, but those are generally confined to the return of specific product components (e.g., printer or copier ink cartridges).

European Union—Directive 94/62/EC on packaging and packaging waste forms the basis of the EU's position toward packaging issues; this directive was amended by Directive 2004/12/EC to include states newly admitted to the EU. The EU GDP does not directly address the impact of packaging on the environment, however, the EU has adopted "extended producer responsibility" as its public policy. This requires packaging producers to be fiscally and physically responsible for their products at the post-consumer stage of their lifecycle. Packaging waste can be "taken back" or managed through reuse, recycling, or remanufacturing. Optionally, the responsibility may be delegated to a third party to act as a producer-responsibility organization. Producers pay certain fees and taxes to governmental and nonprofit entities to cover the reclamation and disposal costs. For example, the Green Dot Program operates in 20 EU member countries where a green dot on a product's packaging indicates it complies with packaging regulations. Manufacturers purchase licenses to participate in the Green Dot program, which collects and recycles or otherwise disposes of the packaging on behalf of the license-holders. The EU's EPR program has recently expanded to automobiles and electronics in many member states.

United States—In the United States, the sole federal EPR program involves the take-back of rechargeable (nickel-cadmium) batteries, in which the producers of the batteries instituted a national take-back program to avoid variable individual state legislation. Most shipments of pharmaceuticals to the consumer, particularly biologicals, use EPS/foam packaging, which usually winds up in landfills. The Alliance of Foam Packaging Recyclers has posted a resource document on their website listing available recycling/reclamation sites in the United States for EPS packaging. At the present, a "true" national EPR public policy and program remains an unmet need.

Japan—Japan's packaging directives are part of the environmental Japanese Industrial Standards, which take their cue from JIS Q 0064: Guide for the Inclusion of Environmental Aspects in Product Standards.

Brazil—Resolution SMA 45/2015 establishes the Terms of Commitment for the Reverse Logistics of Household Medicines for Human Use, Expired or Disused, and their Packaging. Key obligations include: (1) consumers must dispose expired/disused medicines at designated pharmacy collection points; (2) pharmacies as collection points must maintain approved dispensing containers at their own expense; (3) distributors must collect from primary storage points (pharmacies) to secondary storage points using the same delivery vehicles; and (4) manufacturers/importers must arrange transport from secondary storage points to final treatment and environmentally appropriate disposal sites at their own expense.

7.3.2 Reverse Logistics

Reverse logistics encompasses all operations related to the upstream movement of products and materials. It is the process of moving goods from their typical destination for the purpose of capturing value or for proper disposal. Remanufacturing and refurbishing activities also may be included in the definition of reverse logistics. Two types of reverse logistics can be considered in the pharmaceutical environment:

Closed-Loop Transit: Many wholesalers in Europe and the United States use this system. Most of these companies use their own trucks to deliver to the end users, where they can then pick up old packaging and put it back into the distribution channel or recycle the components.

Return of Packaging: Some manufacturers and distributors employ this method, wherein a prepaid return freight sticker is attached or included with the original shipment to the end user. Once the end user removes the medicinal product, the package can be sent back to the distributor or manufacturer. This method is also used to return expired or mishandled medicinal products.

7.3 包裝廢棄物與逆向物流 — 解析

藥品回收計畫比較

美國 DEA 授權回收（Drug Take-Back）：依 2010 年 Secure and Responsible Drug Disposal Act，DEA 授權藥局、醫院、執法機關設置回收點，用於管制藥品及一般處方藥的安全銷毀，避免環境污染及藥物濫用。

台灣藥局回收機制：衛福部食藥署推動「廢棄藥品回收」，藥局作為回收點，收集後由各縣市環保局或合法處理業者焚化銷毀。尚無統一全國性帶回計畫，各地執行方式不一。

逆向物流成本：為何比正向貴 1.5–3 倍？

逆向物流（Reverse Logistics）的成本結構遠比正向配送複雜：

  • 隔離儲存：回收品需獨立儲存區，避免與合格品混淆
  • 品質評估：每批回收需檢查完整性、溫控記錄、有效期
  • 文件記錄：GMP/GDP 要求完整追蹤，包括回收原因、數量、處置方式
  • 低規模效益：回收量少且不穩定，無法享有正向配送的規模優勢
  • 拆解人力：冷鏈包材（PCM 冰袋、VIP 隔熱板）需人工分類才能回收

PCM 冰袋回收 vs. 一次性銷毀：永續性辯證

製造商回收再用方案：

  • 優點：降低每次使用成本、減少材料廢棄
  • 挑戰：需建立回收物流網絡、清潔 SOP、再驗證程序；回收率需達 60–70% 以上才划算

一次性銷毀方案：

  • 優點：操作簡單、無回收追蹤成本、避免交叉污染風險
  • 缺點：大量 EPS/PCM 廢棄物；不符合 EU Green Deal 精神；長期成本高

結論：高價值、高使用頻率的配送路線（如固定航線的生物製劑）更適合回收再用；低頻次、長途最後一哩配送則一次性方案更具現實性。

7.4 Establishing Lowest-Cost Temperature-Control Solution for Shipment

Freight is an important cost factor to be considered when establishing a solution for temperature-controlled shipment. Some examples of other factors that play a role in the decision are the mode of transportation, the type and weight of effective thermal packaging, the amount of time spent in transport, and the environmental impact of the packaging.

There are different kinds of isolated passive containers available, which may be made of EPS, extruded polystyrene (XPS), polyurethane (PUR), or vacuum insulated panels (VIP), and refrigerants made of water and/or phase-change material (PCM). Passive thermal solutions composed of VIPs and PCM are typically more expensive than more traditional solutions of EPS boxes and water-based refrigerants. Traditional solutions are usually physically larger and heavier for the same quantity of product to be shipped, which results in higher freight costs. The freight costs are generally greater than the temperature-control solution costs, but this solution can significantly impact the total cost of shipment.

Air freight is generally more expensive than ocean freight or road freight. Transport by road in refrigerated trucks is typically less expensive than shipping passive-thermal solutions by air, but the time it takes to reach the destination and the ability to plug the truck into an electrical power supply when the active cooling unit on the truck is turned off (e.g., on a ferry) are necessary considerations.

Even though air freight is the most expensive mode, sometimes it is the best solution or may be the only solution when considering the time available for shipment or accessibility of the destination, for instance, island locations or other difficult-to-reach areas without passable roads. Passive-thermal containers are generally less expensive than the active containers shipped by air, but their performance time is limited, and they may not provide sufficient protection before reaching the destination.

Both passive and active transport equipment is designed for a defined amount of time under specific anticipated external temperature conditions as used in the qualification studies. It is important to consider these factors when evaluating the suitability of such packaging for its intended use, as well as knowing the routing specifics, risks associated with critical control points in the process, and the overall transit time from origin to destination.

Reuse is another way to reduce the cost of passive-thermal solutions. The reuse process should be qualified and have a documented quality control process for the returned solutions, including cleaning and inspections to ensure the used system will provide acceptable performance for its intended use.

7.4 最低成本溫控方案 — 決策工具

溫控包裝全生命週期總擁有成本（TCO）公式

TCO = 採購成本（Purchase Cost）
    + 驗證成本（Qualification: IQ/OQ/PQ）
    + 操作成本（充電 PCM / 充填冰袋 / 人工預冷）
    + 廢棄成本（Disposal: 銷毀費用 + 環保稅）
    - 回收殘值（Salvage Value: 若回收再用）

補充：
  運費 = 重量 × 運費率（被動方案因重量大，運費可能高於包材本身）
  總配送成本 = TCO（包材）+ 運費 + 失敗成本概率 × 損失金額

實務上，被動式 VIP+PCM 方案雖採購成本高，但因體積小、重量輕，運費節省往往能覆蓋價差，反而比傳統 EPS+水冰盒更具總成本優勢。

被動 vs. 主動：關鍵選擇因素

  • 被動（Passive）：低操作複雜性、無電力依賴；但保護時間有限，超過設計窗口即失效。驗證需針對最惡劣溫度情境（Summer/Winter Profile）。
  • 主動（Active）：持續維持溫度、適合長途；但依賴電力（需備用電源計畫）、設備重、維護複雜。
  • 黃金法則：包材性能窗口必須覆蓋「最長預期運輸時間 + 緩衝時間（通常 +12h 或 +25%）」。

7.5 Cost of Failure

Establishing a robust shipment process with a qualified solution is an investment made to avoid losses due to quality issues. The cost associated with damage, recalls, returned goods, and rejection due to temperature-control failure is high. The cost can be even higher if the damage is to patient safety and health because of a failure in temperature-monitoring and appropriate quality controls. Any of these circumstances can damage the company brand and image.

Mistakes in purchase orders, invoices, selecting materials picking and separation, address and customers should be avoided. The result of mistakes impacts products and may lead to rejection due to limited or absence of supporting stability data. Insuring high-value shipments because of the high risks associated is common.

7.5.1 Business Contingency Plan

The goal of risk-based planning is to focus on preparing the organization for a multitude of risks, including man-made and natural disasters. Some points to consider when developing an emergency plan are:

  • Prioritize the operations and responsibilities to guide a disaster response in advance
  • Consider alternative locations for operations such as call centers, offices, warehouses, product processing, transportation operations, or equipment
  • Back up data and IT infrastructure in a remote location outside the anticipated disaster impact zone
  • Consider ways to reroute transport operations and protect or remove a product that is in an impact area
  • Prioritize key activities and responsibilities and designate personnel who will be responsible for these activities if regular personnel are unavailable
  • Plan for the cascading effects of shifting responsibilities if multiple persons are unavailable, and reorganize remaining personnel in response to the disaster
  • Establish local-level, site-specific business continuity plans tailored to individual business centers and individual customers and products, as needed
  • Prevent man-made disasters to the greatest extent practical based on the situation (the Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism guidelines produced by the U.S. Customs and Border Protection provides an example of resources to guide this effort)
  • Implement reporting systems and tools, alarms, and other automated monitoring systems to speed notification of an incident and enable a timely response
  • Establish a response plan and designate a responsible person to coordinate appropriate intervention activities
  • Apply the principles of quality assurance to monitor, measure, and continuously improve disaster-intervention results

7.5 失敗成本與業務持續計畫 — 解析

業務持續計畫（BCP）三要素框架

預防（Prevent） — 事前消除風險來源
↓
偵測（Detect） — 即時監控快速發現偏差
↓
回應（Respond） — 標準化應急程序最小化損失

BCP 文件設計思考題

假設你是一家台灣生技公司的 QA 主管，負責設計最後一哩配送的 BCP。請思考：

  • 台灣颱風季（7–9 月）對冷鏈最後一哩的最大風險是什麼？（提示：停電、道路中斷、機場關閉）
  • 你的 BCP 需要定義哪些「觸發點」（Trigger Points）來啟動應急程序？（溫度偏差門檻？停電持續時間？）
  • 演練頻率應設為多少？（建議：每年至少一次桌上演練 + 每三年一次實際演習）

8.0 Summary

The intent of this technical report is to convey the importance of proper information, training, and communication between handoffs as vital to maintaining medicinal product integrity in the pharmaceutical supply chain, especially in the last mile. Knowledge of the challenges encountered along the last mile allows for improvements in future guidance documents, products, and services to the temperature-controlled supply chain community.

The last mile of delivering temperature-controlled pharmaceutical products has many challenges to manage including, but not limited to, the complexity of pharmaceutical supply chain networks, various pharmaceutical product characteristics and temperature sensitivities, weather conditions, sourcing of services and temperature-controlled solutions, use of new technologies and communication platforms, developments in new regulations, execution of cost-effective and sustainable measures, and education in product handling. Sharing information about conditions and practices for the storage and transport of product across all supply chain partners is critical to maintaining product quality, applying appropriate measures, and preventing temperature excursions in the supply chain, including the last mile. When all supply-chain partners involved collaborate closely, the last-mile partners can deliver products on time, to the right place, with the right quantity and quality to the end user.

8.0 TR46 核心信息結構化回顧

TR46 最後一哩 GDP：5 個關鍵控制點

  1. 產品知識與穩定性預算（Sections 2–3）：在設計配送方案前，必須確認產品的溫度敏感性、效期穩定性預算（Stability Budget），並據此選擇合適的包材和運輸方式。
  2. 包裝與設備驗證（Section 4）：被動/主動溫控方案需通過基於最惡劣條件的 IQ/OQ/PQ 驗證，並定期複驗。資格認證記錄是法規查核的關鍵文件。
  3. 監控、偏差管理與追蹤（Sections 5–6）：溫度監控器選型、資料完整性（ALCOA+）、偏差調查流程（根本原因分析 + CAPA）、及供應鏈各環節的移交文件是維持合規的核心。
  4. 教育訓練與利益相關方協作（Section 6.8–6.10）：從製造商到患者的完整教育矩陣，確保每個移交點（Handoff Point）的人員都了解自身責任，是最後一哩溫鏈完整性的最後防線。
  5. 成本管理與永續性（Section 7）：FEFO 庫存輪換、TCO 導向的包材決策、EPR 法規合規、BCP 設計——成本優化必須在不損害產品品質的前提下進行。

TR46 的核心訊息（一句話）

"When all supply-chain partners involved collaborate closely, the last-mile partners can deliver products on time, to the right place, with the right quantity and quality to the end user."

最後一哩的成功，不依靠單一環節的完美執行，而依靠整條供應鏈的協同合作——從製造商、批發商、物流服務提供者、藥師到患者，每個角色都是溫鏈的守護者。

TR46 與其他 PDA TR 的關係

  • PDA TR39（溫控運輸）：TR46 引用 TR39 作為溫控藥品運輸的基礎指引；TR46 聚焦最後一哩的特殊挑戰
  • PDA TR52（GDP）：TR46 為 TR52 GDP 原則在最後一哩的具體應用
  • PDA TR58（風險管理）：TR46 的風險評估框架參照 TR58，需結合 ICH Q9(R1)
  • PDA TR64/TR72（主動/被動系統驗證）：TR46 Section 4 的包材驗證直接引用這兩份 TR 的方法論
