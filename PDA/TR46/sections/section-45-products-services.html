Supply Chains in Canada — 加拿大藥品供應鏈

聯邦 Health Canada + 省級藥學院雙層監管；極端地理與溫度挑戰

3.7 Supply Chains in Canada

Canada has two main levels of regulating bodies for medicines: Health Canada is the federal regulator, and the Colleges of Pharmacy are the provincial regulators for pharmacies. Health Canada oversees the activities of manufacturing, importation, testing, packaging, labeling, distribution, and wholesaling and, thus, regularly audits and licenses wholesalers.

Each of the ten provinces and three territories in Canada have their respective College of Pharmacy. Hospitals fall under the College of Pharmacy, and they are funded by their respective provincial government. Pharmacies and clinics, private or public, obtain their licenses from the College of Pharmacy of their respective province.

The Canadian Food and Drug Act (CFDA) and Food and Drug Regulations (FDR) are the two main legal documents that govern the drug, medical device, natural health product, and cosmetics industries. The FDR and the Guidelines for Environmental Control of Drugs during Storage and Transportation are the Canadian equivalent of the EU GDP. Health Canada refers to these documents when auditing any licensed owner of a drug establishment.

Canada is the second largest country in the world, having an area of almost 10 million km², with a relatively small population of 38 million people. Almost 80% of the population is situated in the south of the country, close to the USA–Canada border, which occupies only about 30% of the total area of the country.

Wholesalers package, pick up, store, transport, and deliver medicines to more than 11,000 pharmacies and hospitals across the country. Transportation of pharmaceutical products can be challenging due to long distances and seasonal temperatures that range from -40 °C to 50 °C, especially when products travel from the south to the north. Products need robust environmental control and protection. In some areas, like in the far north, only one carrier operates — making it imperative to assess the route, understand carrier capabilities, and account for the handoff between carriers.

Each building used to store drugs must be indicated on the drug establishment license (DEL) warehouse annex. When using contracted third parties (3PL), it is the wholesaler's responsibility to ensure that the 3PL buildings are indicated on the DEL warehouse annex and that drugs are stored according to label conditions.

Packaging and packaging configurations are the responsibility of the DEL owner, who is responsible for designing and validating the packaging used to ship products and for qualifying each route according to a protocol. The executed protocol and documentation may be presented to Health Canada during an audit.

Considering the difficult Canadian geography, long distances, and extremes in weather, manufacturers are encouraged to share allowable temperature excursions with the wholesaler. Unfortunately, this information is difficult to obtain even when it is part of the quality agreement signed between the wholesaler and the product manufacturer.

加拿大地理極端挑戰

加拿大面積近1,000萬 km²（台灣的約 280 倍），但人口僅 3,800 萬，約 80% 聚集在南部邊境 30% 的土地上。

溫度範圍：-40°C（北部冬季）至 +50°C（夏季汽車後車廂）。一條從多倫多到育空地區的配送路線可能跨越溫帶與寒帶，需要季節性包裝切換（夏季用相變材料隔熱、冬季用保溫防凍）。

某些北部地區僅有單一運輸商服務，質量協議必須涵蓋從 DC 到最後一哩的全程責任鏈。

實務困境：穩定性資訊分享障礙

TR46 指出：即使質量協議已簽訂，製造商仍不願向批發商分享可接受溫度偏差範圍（allowable temperature excursions），原因是競爭機密疑慮——若批發商知道藥品可耐受 25°C 最多 48 小時，可能重複使用此容忍度而不告知製造商，造成累積超標。

解決方向：在標示上加入運輸聲明，例如「冷藏 2–8°C；運輸期間短暫偏差至 25°C 不超過 24 小時」，此聲明需要穩定性數據支撐並經主管機關核准。

比喻：DEL 倉庫附件 = 藥品執照的「地址變更登記」

台灣類比：藥商許可執照上必須登載所有倉儲地址，若委外 3PL，倉庫地址同樣須納入執照附件，就像公司變更營業地址需向主管機關申報變更。未登載地址擅自儲藥 = 違規。

Supply Chains in China — 中國藥品供應鏈

134,000+ 配送商；醫院主導（>60%）；三步驟配送結構；eCommerce 崛起

3.8 Supply Chains in China

China had approximately 134,000 pharmaceutical distributors in 2021 with a market size of 2 trillion renminbi (RMB), equivalent to approximately 300 billion USD. These distributors sell most pharmaceuticals to China hospitals. The Central Pharmacy Department in a hospital is responsible for the purchase and dispensing of pharmaceutical products including inpatient, outpatient, and emergency pharmacies. Hospital sales still account for more than 60% of drug sales.

A typical manufacturer uses provincial distributors to cover hospital channels, a central purchase department to provide for chain pharmacies, and an ecommerce platform to supply directly to the patient. Transaction partners are required to exchange "first transaction documents" — business licenses, drug transaction licenses, and drug sales permits — before a transaction takes place.

A distributor is not necessarily bound to limit distribution to within a certain region, but the local administration that issues the distribution license will be held responsible for any conduct contrary to the regulations. Therefore, it is almost impossible for a distributor to open a distribution center in another region, since no authority would issue a license for a distributor not registered in that region.

The pharmaceutical supply chain in China can be divided into the following three steps:

  1. National distribution from manufacturers to wholesalers
  2. Local dispatch from wholesalers to hospitals/pharmacies
  3. Last-mile distribution from pharmacies to patients

Long-haul distribution is usually carried out with the support of 3PL companies, while the dispatch to hospitals is usually handled by the wholesalers themselves, using their own transportation teams who also serve (partly) as the stocking staff of hospital pharmacies. The complexity of such a delivery system makes it almost impossible for manufacturers to replace wholesalers with 3PL companies to provide direct sales to hospitals.

Retail pharmacies can dispatch prescription drug (Rx) products to homes with their own third-party courier services "if the Rx product is to treat chronic diseases and the prescription has been verified by certified pharmacists." Specialty pharmacies, or direct-to-patient pharmacies, serve as the main channel to patients with home delivery services for specialty drugs.

Two critical factors impact the last mile in China: National Health Insurance and the fast-growing eCommerce channel. Online sales for drugs grew by 37.6% in 2022 over 2021, with a seven-year compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 48.4%, accounting for 2.8% of the total drug sales.

The Online-to-Offline (O2O) platform model allows patients to select a product from listed retail pharmacies and have it delivered within 30 minutes. In 2021, the top 10 wholesalers accounted for 65% of the market with total revenue of 1.3 trillion RMB (~194 billion USD).

In summary, the supply chain in China is shifting from hospitals to retail pharmacies, and online pharmacies are growing at a faster rate than offline retail pharmacies.

Pharmaceutical Products — 藥品產品特性與穩定性

4.1 產品特性與穩定性 | 4.2 穩定性預算與MKT計算

4.0 Pharmaceutical Products

The pharmaceutical supply chain distributes different kinds of products to the patient via the last mile. Product characteristics and stability impact how these products should be handled along the chain; understanding these product properties is therefore important.

4.1 Product Characteristics and Stability

The chemical and physical properties of a drug substance and the drug product formulation determine its product stability and shelf life. During this shelf life, pharmaceutical products like vaccines, tablets, creams, and injectables are exposed to hazards that may impact their quality, efficacy, safety, and aesthetics. These hazards include:

  • Intrinsic factors: molecular structure of the drug itself
  • Environmental factors: temperature, pH, buffer species, ionic strength, light, oxygen, moisture, additives, excipients

Temperature is one of the primary hazards that affects drug stability and shelf life. Storage and shipping temperatures should stay within the allowed and filed conditions established during stability-testing programs that are typically run at the beginning of the product lifecycle. These studies determine the impact of temperature, moisture (relative humidity), light, shock, vibration, drop, and pressure on the product in its primary marketed packaging.

The formation of degradation products and decrease in label claim or potency are measured using stability-indicating methods to the end of the product shelf life. During these stability studies, the product is stored under strictly controlled conditions in climate chambers at, for example, 5 ± 3 °C and 25 ± 2 °C. Samples are drawn from the controlled temperature chambers for testing at regular intervals. From these stability studies, the product shelf life and temperature conditions during manufacture, packaging, distribution, and storage are established.

4.2 Application of Product Stability Budget and Mean Kinetic Temperature

Products requiring storage at defined temperatures may experience exposure to temperatures outside of the defined range during filling, labeling, inspection, packaging, shipping, and distribution including to the end user. Some examples of real-world scenarios:

  • Refrigerated truck: Even though a truck has reached the correct set temperature, the temperature may rise temporarily in warm environments or fall in cool environments as the doors are opened for loading and unloading.
  • Hospital/pharmacy refrigerator failure: The refrigeration unit used to store temperature-sensitive pharmaceuticals accidentally stops cooling overnight or during the weekend.
  • Pharmacy dispensing: A product is removed from the refrigerator to place a patient label and check suitability for dispensing.
  • Mail-order delivery: A controlled room temperature product is mailed to a patient's home and remains in a mailbox for several hours, exposed to ambient summer or winter temperatures.

USP <1079> states that deviations or variances in exposure to temperatures outside of the product's label range should be addressed with the manufacturer or MAH to determine if the exposure is acceptable and if the product may be used.

The total allowable time outside the storage condition, based on stability studies, will be defined as the product's Time Out of Refrigeration (TOR) or Time Out of Storage (TOS) and will be used to support excursion temperatures outside the storage conditions. The total TOR/TOS found to be acceptable is divided by site departments to cover the total time required for each stage in manufacturing, shipping, distribution, and use.

Manufacturers are reluctant to provide expanded standard stability information to other parties, since the same allowable excursions could be used multiple times by those possessing the product without the knowledge of the manufacturer. A better approach would be to use transportation statements in labeling, for example:

"Store at 2–8 ºC; excursions to 25 ºC during transport not to exceed 24 hours"
"Store at 15–25 °C; excursions to 2–32 °C during transport not to exceed 24 hours"

Such statements must be supported by stability data and approved by competent health authorities.

Drug manufacturers can facilitate the use of their temperature-controlled products by: providing a wider range of label storage temperatures (e.g., 2–15 °C rather than 2–8 °C), providing separate long-term storage and transport temperatures, and/or using Mean Kinetic Temperature (MKT).

Using the calculated MKT for a drug product is appropriate to approximate the effects of storage-temperature variations that may occur during product transport in the absence of product-specific kinetic data. MKT calculations for each portion of the distribution process may be used to estimate the impact of temperature excursions on the quality of the drug product.

  • Brief segments (e.g., transport): use MKT in conjunction with upper and lower temperature limits from the manufacturer.
  • Longer segments (e.g., storage): calculating the MKT of only the excursion is preferable to using the MKT of the entire segment.

Each successive owner of the material — manufacturer, wholesaler, pharmacy, and patient — can, at best, be responsible only for their particular segment, including the storage and transport temperatures and the interpretation of the resulting partial MKT values.

The manufacturer of a drug product has a unique responsibility, since it is the manufacturer who performs the stability studies to determine the expiration dating of the drug. For this reason, when questions arise during the distribution process about temperature excursions, they are best raised with the manufacturer.

The misalignment between logistics needs and stability data can be resolved if the logistics group openly communicates the stability needs to the research and development team that executes the stability studies. Comprehensive stability data is essential for managing temperature excursions that are likely to occur during transit.

MKT 公式 — Mean Kinetic Temperature

MKT 的計算基於 Arrhenius 方程式，將一段時間內不均勻的溫度暴露換算為「等效恆溫」：

TMKT = (ΔH / R) / −ln[ Σ(e−ΔH/RTi) / n ]

參數說明：

  • ΔH = 活化能 = 83,144 J/mol（USP 預設值，對應約 20 kcal/mol）
  • R = 氣體常數 = 8.314 J/(mol·K)
  • Ti = 第 i 個時間段的絕對溫度（Kelvin = °C + 273.15）
  • n = 時間段數量

重要：Ti 必須換算為 Kelvin（K），切勿直接使用°C！

範例計算（簡化）：
若產品在以下溫度各暴露 1 小時：
  T1 = 8°C = 281.15 K
  T2 = 15°C = 288.15 K
  T3 = 25°C = 298.15 K

步驟 1：計算各溫度的 e^(-ΔH/RT)
  ΔH/R = 83,144 / 8.314 = 10,000 K

步驟 2：取平均值後取 ln，再除負號
步驟 3：得出 T_MKT（K）後換算回 °C

Stability Budget（穩定性預算）概念

想像藥品的穩定性壽命是一個「銀行帳戶」，從製造到患者使用的每一次溫度偏差，都是在「提款」：

供應鏈環節 建議預算分配 說明
製造 / 充填10%標示、充填、包裝過程中的短暫暴露
製造商倉儲20%出貨前在製造商端的儲存時間
運輸（幹線）30%長途運輸，溫度最難控制
分銷/批發倉庫20%批發商端的儲存與分揀
零售/家庭使用20%藥局冷藏、患者家中存放

一旦某環節「超支」，後續環節的容忍空間就縮小，最終可能影響藥品效期內品質。

TOR / TOS 分配的實務挑戰

TOR（Time Out of Refrigeration）是製造商基於穩定性研究設定的總累積時限，必須在整個供應鏈各節點共享分配，但：

  • 製造商擔心下游濫用，不願公開具體數值
  • 批發商無法科學評估一次偏差是否「用掉」多少預算
  • 患者更不知道自家冰箱溫度是否符合要求

USP <1079> 的建議：任何偏差都應向 MAH（上市許可持有人）詢問，但實務上無法每次都電話確認。標示加入運輸聲明是最可行的系統性解法。

比喻：MKT 如同「溫度加權平均分」

MKT 不是普通算術平均溫度，而是考量高溫對反應速率的指數加速效果後的「等效傷害溫度」。例如：產品在 25°C 暴露 1 小時，對穩定性的傷害遠大於在 8°C 暴露 1 小時——MKT 反映的正是這種非線性累積效應。

台灣類比：就像基測/學測不是每科分數直接加總，而是依科目難度加權計算——MKT 依溫度對降解的加速程度加權計算。

5.0

Services and Temperature Controlled Systems — 服務與溫控系統

5.1 合約管理 | 5.2 儲存設施與環境控制

5.0 Services and Temperature Controlled Systems

Within supply chain management, suppliers and receivers of goods may use services and temperature-controlled systems from other entities to support the storage and transport of pharmaceutical products. When temperature-sensitive products need to be shipped, additional services may be needed to protect and monitor the goods.

5.1 Contract Management

When services or outsource activities are applied in the supply chain, contract management should be applied according to the applicable country's rules. The quality system should extend to the control and review of any outsourced activities related to the procurement, holding, supply, or export of medicinal products, and all of these processes should incorporate quality risk management.

There must be a written contract between the contract-giver and the contract-acceptor that clearly establishes the duties of each party. From a regulatory perspective, a technical or quality agreement is expected that includes additional details for deviation management and change control. The contract-givers are responsible for auditing the contract-acceptor to verify the agreed-upon activities.

The contract-acceptor should not pass any of the work entrusted to them to a third party without the contract-giver's prior evaluation, approval, and an audit of the third party. The contract-acceptor must forward any information that can influence the quality of the product(s) to the contract-giver in accordance with the requirements of the contract.

5.2 Storage

5.2.1 Storage Facilities

Storage facilities should be secure and structurally sound to allow safe storage and handling of medicinal products. Management and operation of the facility should include but is not limited to:

  • Access to the storage facilities should be restricted to authorized staff.
  • Premises should be provided with adequate lighting to enable all operations to be carried out accurately and safely.
  • Appropriate cleaning equipment and cleaning products should be chosen so as not to present a source of contamination.
  • Any products withdrawn, recalled, expired, or suspected of falsification should be segregated from saleable stock (physically or through an equivalent electronic system).
  • Medicinal products should be handled and stored to prevent spillage, breakage, contamination, and mix-ups.
  • Stock should be rotated according to the "First Expired, First Out" (FEFO) principle. Regular stock inventories should be performed, and stock irregularities should be investigated.

5.2.2 Environmental Control

The storage of pharmaceutical products is critical for the last mile. Temperature and humidity requirements must be maintained during storage, regardless of the type of storage unit used (e.g., deep freezer, freezer, refrigerator, walk-in cooler or freezer, storage rooms).

Three essential conditions must be met: temperature and humidity maintenance, distribution, and monitoring. Temperature and humidity must be maintained during regular operation and should consider traffic movement (door opening) and defrost cycles where applicable.

Storage units should be mapped (thermal mapping) to ensure that all locations inside do not deviate from the required temperature range. Temperature- and humidity-mapping should be conducted when the unit is empty. Additional studies might be required based on geographic location, atmosphere, and local regulations in combination with partial or full loads, as product placement may affect air movement.

Once temperature-mapping is executed, the next step is analyzing the thermal mapping data to identify the locations with the lowest and highest values where temperature and humidity sensors can be positioned for permanent monitoring.

In some cases, temperature probes are placed inside a container with liquid to provide a more accurate product temperature rather than air temperature, which can fluctuate and trigger an alarm unnecessarily. A contingency plan must be in place in the event of a power outage or mechanical failure. Standup freezers and refrigerators can only maintain the proper temperature for a few hours during failures and are not typically designed with significant insulation properties.

The medication provider (e.g., pharmacist) must communicate to end users the importance of proper storage conditions. Refrigerators used at home often cool the interior space with cold air from the freezer section. Placing products near the vents supplying cold air may expose products to freezing temperatures.

關鍵原則：委外不委責 — 委託方（contract-giver）對最終品質仍負責，必須定期稽核受委方。受委方未經批准不得再轉委第三方。

FEFO vs. FIFO — 為什麼藥品用 FEFO？

FIFO（First In, First Out）：先進先出，以進庫時間排序，適合非到期品管理。

FEFO（First Expired, First Out）：先到期先出，以到期日排序。藥品採用 FEFO 的原因：同批產品可能有不同到期日，或補貨時有更短效期的產品進入。FEFO 確保近效期產品優先出貨，避免過期損耗。

台灣藥事法規亦要求藥品庫存管理採行 FEFO 原則。

Thermal Mapping（溫度圖譜）三步驟

  1. 空載量測：空倉庫/冰箱時進行，找出溫度最高點與最低點（通常靠近門口、頂部、出風口）
  2. 滿載/半載量測：產品擺放影響氣流，需驗證不同載量下的溫度分布
  3. 探頭定位：將永久監控感測器安裝在最高溫與最低溫位置（worst-case positions）

家用冰箱特別注意：靠近出風口的位置溫度可能低於 0°C，導致原本要求 2–8°C 儲存的藥品結凍失效。藥師有義務告知患者正確儲放位置。

患者端儲存指導 — 藥師的最後一哩責任

TR46 明確指出：藥師必須主動告知患者：

  • 家用冰箱靠近冷凍庫出風口可能結凍
  • 冰箱過滿時空氣無法循環，可能出現局部過熱
  • 光線、濕度對部分藥品（如硝酸甘油、某些胰島素）影響顯著

這是「最後一哩」概念在個人層次的體現——專業指導是冷鏈的最後一道防線。

5.3

Packaging — 溫控包裝：被動式、主動式與永續性

5.3.1 被動式熱防護 | 5.3.2 主動式熱防護 | 5.3.3 永續包裝

5.3 Packaging

The diversity of environmental conditions encountered in the last mile makes this segment the most challenging of the whole distribution chain. For this reason, the qualification of packaging requires a deep analysis of potential environmental and handling conditions that will be experienced until the product is delivered to end users. PDA Technical Reports No. 64, No. 72, and TR 39 detail all the processes for qualifying a thermal solution.

The most crucial step is to design proper testing conditions before starting the qualification process. Any scenarios that the products will encounter must be taken into consideration. If the product will be delivered by car or truck, with or without active heating and cooling, or with couriers or parcels, the conditions inside the packaging, the number of stops, and how the products are tendered to end users need to be known in the development of testing temperature profiles and handling procedures.

Data collection is crucial in the establishment of qualification procedures. Using dummy boxes to collect outside temperatures and measure handling conditions (e.g., vibration, orientation) during shipments is a very effective method for understanding the shipping environment. In the case of parcel shipments, thermal shipping containers can be flipped multiple times.

Earlier in the supply chain, pharmaceutical products are transported in larger quantities (pallet size, multiple-counts packages). For the last mile, products are shipped in much smaller quantities for final deliveries to pharmacies, clinics, nursing homes, and directly to patients. These shipments will experience more diversified conditions than in their homogeneous distribution.

5.3.1 Packaging with Passive Thermal Functionality

Packaging with passive thermal functionality uses a combination of insulation and refrigerants, such as water-based gel packs, phase-change materials (PCM), dry ice, or liquid nitrogen to ensure that medicinal products are distributed under defined temperature conditions. Containers must be qualified.

A prequalified passive-thermal solution can be acceptable as long as the ambient temperature for which it was qualified and the payload used for the qualification is similar to the product to be shipped. Universal pack-outs do not require seasonal or regional adjustments and maximize operational efficiency but usually are more expensive and weigh more.

Factors related to home deliveries that include additional transport hours and time packages are left outside or in mailboxes must be considered in the final design of the thermal-packaging solution.

5.3.2 Packaging with Active Thermal Functionality

Several different systems with active thermal packaging are available, equipped with active refrigeration or heating capabilities using calibrated and temperature-control devices. The refrigeration or heating used to keep the products under proper temperature conditions can be mechanical, chemical, or electric and typically is designed to be reused multiple times.

Contrary to previous segments of the supply chain, active-thermal containers used in last-mile delivery are much smaller and accommodate a small number of units. They require a power source to maintain temperature and offer the advantage of being resettable — the temperature set point can be adjusted as needed.

5.3.3 Sustainability

Sustainability considerations in thermal packaging include:

  • Single use versus reuse of packaging components
  • PCM recovery rates and recycling logistics
  • Growing trends of expanded polystyrene (EPS) bans in various jurisdictions
  • Development of biodegradable packaging alternatives (e.g., mushroom foam, recycled paper insulation)
  • Carbon footprint of packaging materials vs. temperature performance trade-offs
  • Theft deterrence and damage prevention as part of holistic packaging design

PCM（相變材料）選型關鍵

相變材料（Phase-Change Material）在相變（固→液或液→固）時吸收或釋放大量潛熱，從而維持穩定溫度。常見規格：

  • +5°C PCM：適用 2–8°C 冷藏藥品（防止過熱）
  • 0°C PCM（水/冰）：成本最低，但需注意結凍風險
  • -21°C PCM：適用凍存藥品

選型時需考量：外部環境溫度、包裝尺寸、預期運輸時間、以及PCM 回收是否可行（若回收率低，每次使用後丟棄 PCM 成本可觀）。

永續包裝的監管趨勢

全球多個司法管轄區已啟動或通過保麗龍（EPS）禁令，包括部分歐盟成員國、加拿大、中國、及美國部分州。藥品冷鏈包裝面臨的轉型壓力：

  • 紙基隔熱材（如蜂窩紙板）熱性能通常不如 EPS，需要更厚材料
  • 生物可降解包裝（如菌菇泡棉）目前確效資料仍不足
  • PCM 回收需要建立逆向物流（reverse logistics）體系

TR46 提醒：永續性、防盜（theft）、防損（damage）都需納入包裝設計考量，不能只看熱性能。

最後一哩包裝確效的特殊挑戰

最後一哩配送的包裝確效比幹線運輸更複雜，因為：

  • 包裝倒置：快遞包裹可能被翻轉多次，PCM 位置改變影響溫控
  • 等待時間不確定：包裹可能在門口或信箱等候數小時
  • 環境溫度多變：同一條路線，夏季 38°C 與冬季 -10°C 的確效條件截然不同

推薦做法：使用 dummy box（擬真包裝）進行 live shipment testing，並在最惡劣季節條件下驗證。

5.4

Transportation — 運輸方式：公路、無人機、郵購藥局

5.4.1 公路運輸 | 5.4.2 無人機 | 5.4.3 郵購藥局

5.4 Transportation

5.4.1 Road Transportation

Road transportation is the most common mode of pharmaceutical last-mile delivery globally. Vehicles used for pharmaceutical transport — ranging from large refrigerated trucks to smaller vans and motorcycles — must be qualified for the intended purpose. Key considerations include:

  • Vehicle temperature qualification: the vehicle compartment must be mapped to confirm uniform temperature distribution, accounting for door openings, defrost cycles, and ambient extremes.
  • Handoff points: each transfer from one vehicle or carrier to another represents a temperature risk event that must be managed with defined procedures and documentation.
  • Route qualification: the entire route, including expected transit time, seasonal temperature profiles, and number of delivery stops, must be incorporated into the packaging and vehicle qualification.
  • Driver training: personnel handling pharmaceutical products must understand storage requirements, how to handle temperature excursions, and how to operate temperature-monitoring equipment.

5.4.2 Drones

Drone delivery is an emerging last-mile transportation mode with potential advantages for remote and underserved areas. Key aspects include:

  • Payload limitations: most commercial delivery drones can carry only 1–5 kg, restricting them to small parcel deliveries (e.g., insulin pens, EpiPens, blood products for point-of-care).
  • Flight range: current technology supports approximately 10–30 km per flight, making drones suitable for localized distribution from a hub to a defined catchment area.
  • Weather window: precipitation, high winds, and extreme temperatures restrict safe drone operation, creating delivery reliability challenges.
  • Regulatory status: in the United States, the FAA regulates drone operations; in Europe, EASA. Beyond-visual-line-of-sight (BVLOS) operations — necessary for practical pharmaceutical delivery — require special waivers or approvals that are still being developed in most jurisdictions.
  • Temperature control during flight: airborne packages are exposed to altitude-related temperature changes, wind chill, and solar radiation. Passive packaging must be specifically qualified for these conditions.

Pilot programs exist in Ghana (Zipline for blood products and vaccines), Rwanda, the USA (Wing, Amazon), and Switzerland. Regulatory frameworks continue to evolve.

5.4.3 Mail Service Pharmacy

Mail service pharmacy (MSP), also known as mail-order pharmacy, has grown substantially, particularly for chronic disease medications. Key last-mile challenges include:

  • Uncontrolled delivery environment: packages may sit in an unheated or unventilated mailbox or on a doorstep for hours after delivery.
  • Seasonal extremes: summer temperatures inside a residential mailbox can exceed 60°C; winter temperatures in northern climates can reach -30°C or lower, posing freezing risks for biologics.
  • Carrier variability: national postal services, private couriers, and regional carriers apply different handling standards; no single GDP standard applies uniformly.
  • Regulatory requirements: in the USA, state pharmacy boards regulate MSP operations; product labeling must be appropriate for the expected conditions of shipment and home storage. Pharmacists retain professional responsibility for the integrity of dispensed products.
  • Patient education: patients must be instructed to retrieve packages promptly and inspect for visible damage or evidence of temperature excursion before use.

5.5

Temperature Monitoring — 溫度監控：記錄器、指示劑與風險方法

5.5.1 數據記錄器 | 5.5.2 溫度指示劑（TTI）| 5.5.3 風險導向方法

5.5 Temperature Monitoring

5.5.1 Data Loggers

Data loggers are electronic devices that continuously record temperature (and in some models, humidity, light, and shock) at programmable intervals throughout a shipment. Key features include:

  • Downloadable history: data can be downloaded via USB, Bluetooth, or NFC after shipment completion, providing a full time-temperature record for regulatory documentation.
  • Programmable alarm thresholds: loggers can be set to alarm upon exceeding defined upper or lower temperature limits or upon accumulating a defined time out of range.
  • Calibration requirements: data loggers must be calibrated to a traceable standard at defined intervals to ensure measurement accuracy.
  • GxP compliance: for regulated shipments, data loggers must meet 21 CFR Part 11 or equivalent electronic records requirements (audit trail, data integrity).
  • Placement: loggers should be placed at the product level inside the shipping container — not attached to the outside of the container where temperatures differ from the product microenvironment.

Real-time data loggers with cellular or satellite connectivity allow remote temperature monitoring, enabling intervention before a shipment reaches its destination if an excursion is detected.

5.5.2 Temperature Indicators (TTI)

Time-temperature indicators (TTI) are low-cost, disposable devices that provide a visual indication of whether a product has been exposed to unacceptable temperature conditions. Types include:

  • Threshold indicators: change color or display a symbol if temperature exceeds a defined threshold for any duration (e.g., above 8°C indicator).
  • Cumulative TTI: integrate time and temperature exposure, changing irreversibly once a defined cumulative thermal dose (expressed as equivalent time at a reference temperature) is exceeded.

TTIs are widely used as:

  • Point-of-use consumer indicators on vaccine vials (the WHO vaccine vial monitor, or VVM, is a well-known example)
  • Supplementary indicators on outer packaging for visual triage at receiving
  • Cost-effective monitoring tools for high-volume, low-cost shipments where full data loggers are not economically justified

Limitations of TTI: they do not provide downloadable historical data, and threshold indicators do not account for cumulative exposure. They are not sufficient for regulatory documentation requiring a full time-temperature record.

5.5.3 Risk-Based Approach to Temperature Monitoring

A risk-based approach to temperature monitoring recognizes that not all shipments require the same level of monitoring. Factors to consider when selecting monitoring tools include:

  • Product sensitivity: biologics and vaccines are typically more sensitive than small-molecule tablets; higher sensitivity warrants more rigorous monitoring.
  • Shipment value: high-value specialty products justify the cost of real-time data loggers; low-cost generic drugs may be adequately monitored with TTIs.
  • Route characteristics: well-characterized, validated routes with historical excursion data may require less intensive monitoring than novel routes.
  • Regulatory requirements: some regions or product types mandate specific monitoring technologies.
  • Chain of custody complexity: shipments passing through multiple handoffs or carriers benefit from continuous monitoring to assign responsibility for any excursion.

5.6

Security — 藥品安全與防偽

最後一哩的藥品安全風險管理與防盜措施

5.6 Security

Security considerations in last-mile pharmaceutical delivery encompass multiple dimensions, including physical security, product authenticity, and chain-of-custody integrity. The last mile presents unique security vulnerabilities because:

  • Products move from large, secured distribution centers into smaller vehicles, courier bags, and ultimately to individual home addresses — environments with far less controlled access.
  • The diversity of delivery personnel (professional couriers, gig-economy drivers, postal workers) varies in awareness of pharmaceutical handling requirements.
  • High-value specialty drugs (e.g., oncology biologics, controlled substances) are particularly attractive targets for theft.
  • Counterfeit or falsified medicines can enter the supply chain at distribution and dispensing points, especially in markets with fragmented distribution structures.

Security measures applicable to last-mile pharmaceutical logistics include:

  • Serialization and track-and-trace: regulatory requirements in the EU (FMD — Falsified Medicines Directive), the USA (DSCSA — Drug Supply Chain Security Act), and China mandate unique product identifiers and electronic verification at the dispensing point, preventing the dispensing of falsified or recalled products.
  • Tamper-evident packaging: outer cartons and secondary packaging should include tamper-evident features (seals, labels, or destructible closure systems) so that patients can visually confirm the product has not been opened.
  • Chain-of-custody documentation: each transfer of pharmaceutical product must be documented, with signatures, timestamps, and temperature data, to enable retrospective investigation in the event of a security incident or quality concern.
  • GPS tracking: high-value shipments are increasingly tracked using GPS devices embedded in the shipping container, enabling real-time location monitoring and geofence-based alerts.
  • Controlled substance handling: additional regulatory requirements (e.g., DEA schedules in the USA, Narcotic Drugs Act in Taiwan) impose stricter chain-of-custody, storage, and documentation requirements for controlled substances delivered via mail or courier.
  • Patient identity verification: for specialty pharmacy and controlled substance deliveries, recipient identity verification (e.g., signature, ID check) is required at the point of delivery.

In summary, security is an integral component of pharmaceutical supply chain design, requiring both physical controls and information system safeguards. As pharmaceutical e-commerce and direct-to-patient delivery models grow, the security framework must evolve proportionally.

隨著電商藥品銷售增長，假冒藥品（counterfeit）和摻偽藥品（falsified）更容易混入供應鏈。最後一哩的防偽薄弱點：

  • 消費者缺乏驗證工具（無法掃碼驗證的家庭用戶）
  • 郵購包裹外觀難以與正品區分

本章節總結：最後一哩的五大關鍵要素

  1. 監管合規：了解並遵守每個節點的當地法規（加拿大 CFDA/FDR、中國 GSP/NMPA、美國 DSCSA 等）
  2. 穩定性管理：以 MKT 與 TOR/TOS 科學評估溫度偏差，建立穩定性預算分配
  3. 包裝確效：依據被動/主動模式選擇並確效溫控包裝，考量最惡劣場景（信箱滯留、包裹翻轉）
  4. 溫度監控：以風險導向方法選擇 Data Logger 或 TTI，確保完整可追溯的時間-溫度記錄
  5. 安全防偽：序列化 + 防竄改封緘 + 患者端驗證，形成完整的藥品供應鏈安全防護網
