I. Introduction

This guidance describes and explains the final rule on current good manufacturing practice (CGMP) requirements for combination products that FDA issued on January 22, 2013 (final rule). (21 CFR part 4). Prior to issuance of the final rule, although CGMP regulations were in place to establish requirements for drugs, devices, biological products, and Human Cells, Tissues, and Cellular and Tissue-Based Products (HCT/Ps), there were no regulations to clarify and explain the application of these CGMP requirements to combination products.

The final rule did not establish any new requirements; it was intended to clarify which CGMP requirements apply when drugs, devices, and biological products are combined to create combination products, and to set forth a transparent and streamlined regulatory framework for firms to use when demonstrating compliance with applicable CGMP requirements.

Section II provides definition of "combination product," overview of the final rule, and describes the role of the lead center and other Agency components with respect to combination product CGMP issues. Section III addresses certain general considerations for CGMP compliance for combination products. Section IV presents the purpose and content of specific CGMP requirements addressed in the final rule. Finally, Section V analyzes hypothetical scenarios that illustrate how to comply with certain CGMP requirements for specific types of combination products.

FDA's guidance documents do not establish legally enforceable responsibilities. Instead, this guidance describes the Agency's current thinking on a topic that should be viewed only as recommendations, unless specific regulatory or statutory requirements are cited.

FDA 於 2013 年 1 月 22 日正式發布 21 CFR Part 4（最終法規），這份 2017 年的指引文件是對該法規的說明書。重點在於：

  • 在 Part 4 之前，業界面臨的困境是：不知道對一個「藥器合一」產品，應該遵循哪套 CGMP
  • Part 4 的核心貢獻是建立了一個透明且精簡的法規遵循框架

II. Background

A. Definition of a Combination Product

As set forth in 21 CFR part 3, a combination product is a product composed of two or more different types of medical products (i.e., a combination of a drug, device, and/or biological product with one another). The drugs, devices, and biological products included in combination products are referred to as constituent parts of the combination product.

Under 21 CFR 3.2(e), a combination product includes:

  • Single-entity combination product — A product comprised of two or more regulated components (drug/device, biologic/device, drug/biologic, or drug/device/biologic) that are physically, chemically, or otherwise combined or mixed and produced as a single entity. Examples: a prefilled syringe, a drug-eluting stent.
  • Co-packaged combination product — Two or more separate products packaged together in a single package or as a unit. Examples: a surgical kit, a first-aid kit containing a drug.
  • Cross-labeled combination product — A drug, device, or biological product packaged separately that according to its investigational plan or proposed labeling is intended for use only with an approved, individually specified drug, device, or biological product. Example: a light-emitting device intended for use only with a specific light-activated drug.
  • Investigational cross-labeled combination product — Any investigational drug, device, or biological product packaged separately intended for use only with another individually specified investigational product.

「構成部分」的法律意義

Constituent parts（構成部分）這個術語很重要：它指的是組合進組合產品的各個藥品、器械或生物製品本身。它們各自保留原有的法規地位（drug、device 或 biologic），不因被組合而改變。這直接影響後續哪些 CGMP 適用。

B. Quality and Current Good Manufacturing Practice

One of FDA's goals is to assure the availability of quality drugs, biologics, devices, and combination products that consistently meet applicable requirements and specifications. The drug CGMP and device QS regulations, as well as the CGMPs for biologics and current good tissue practices for HCT/Ps, provide a framework of minimum requirements to help assure product quality.

The core requirements provide for systems that assure proper design, monitoring, and control of manufacturing processes and facilities. This includes:

  • Establishing a strong quality management system
  • Using appropriate quality raw materials
  • Establishing robust manufacturing and control procedures based on sound design principles
  • Detecting and investigating product quality deviations

比喻：CGMP 是「最低標準的建築法規」

CGMP 就像建築法規：它規定了最低安全標準，但不阻止你蓋得更好。它不是品質的保證，而是進入市場的「門檻」。對組合產品來說，難題在於：藥品建築法規和器械建築法規有不同的要求，Part 4 告訴你怎麼同時達標。

QS vs. QMSR — 2026 年重要更新

本指引發布時（2017年），器械 CGMP 稱為「Quality System（QS）Regulation」（21 CFR Part 820）。自 2026 年 2 月 2 日起，Part 820 更新為 Quality Management System Regulation（QMSR），與 ISO 13485:2016 高度一致化。

閱讀本指引時，凡提到「Part 820」或「QS regulation」，均應理解為現行的 QMSR。

C. Overview of the Final Rule

1. Summary of the Final Rule

The constituent parts of a combination product retain their regulatory status (as a drug or device, for example) after they are combined. The final rule clarifies that the CGMP requirements that apply to each of the constituent parts apply to the combination product they constitute.

This guidance refers to a "CGMP operating system" to mean the operating system within an establishment that is designed and implemented to address and meet the current good manufacturing practice requirements applicable to the manufacture of a combination product.

For single-entity and co-packaged combination products, part 4 identifies two ways to demonstrate compliance:

II.C 法規概覽  |  中文解說

核心原則：構成部分保留其法規地位

這是 Part 4 最根本的邏輯：把藥品和器械組合在一起，不會讓藥品「變成」器械，也不會讓器械「變成」藥品。每個構成部分保留原有的法規身分，因此其各自的 CGMP 要求都必須被滿足。

問題是：如何同時滿足兩套 CGMP？這就是 Part 4 要解決的問題。

「CGMP Operating System」是什麼？

這是本指引引入的操作性概念，指工廠內部為滿足組合產品 CGMP 要求而設計和實施的整套品質運作體系。它包括：

  • SOP、記錄、訓練體系
  • QMS 架構（CAPA、設計管控、採購管控等）
  • 廠房、設備、人員的管控系統

工廠的品質文件體系必須清楚識別出這套 Operating System。

比喻：Option 1 vs Option 2

Option 1 就像同時取得兩張駕照（汽車 + 機車），每張駕照的所有要求都要全部符合，不省略任何一項。工作量最大，但也最沒有爭議。

Option 2（精簡路徑）則像「整合考照」——以一張駕照為主體，再補考另一種車的關鍵科目。大幅減少重複性要求，但需要清楚知道補哪些科目。

Option 2: The Two Streamlined Approaches

A manufacturer may choose either streamlined approach regardless of which constituent part provides the primary mode of action (PMOA).

Drug CGMP-based 21 CFR parts 210/211 (full Drug CGMP) + 6 device QS provisions (see below)
Device QS-based 21 CFR part 820 / QMSR (full Device QS) + 8 drug CGMP provisions (see below)

Drug CGMP-based pathway — the 6 added device QS provisions:

  • 820.20 — Management responsibility

  • 820.170 — Installation

  • 211.165 — Testing and release for distribution

兩種精簡路徑的邏輯結構

路徑 A：藥品 CGMP 為基礎

為什麼這 6 條 / 8 條被選中？

FDA 選擇這些條款是因為它們各自代表對方框架中「最無法被忽視的核心要素」：

  • 給藥品廠商加的 6 條器械條款：主要針對器械特有的設計管控（820.30）和 CAPA（820.100），這兩項在傳統藥品 CGMP 中沒有明確對應要求
  • 給器械廠商加的 8 條藥品條款：主要針對藥品特有的效期管理（211.137）、穩定性測試（211.166）、組件測試（211.84），這些在器械 QS 中沒有直接對應

PMOA 決定主管中心，但不決定路徑選擇

這是本節最容易混淆的概念。Primary Mode of Action（PMOA） 只決定由哪個 FDA Center（CDER/CDRH/CBER）擔任主管中心，但不限制廠商選擇哪條精簡路徑。

舉例：一個 PFS（PMOA 是藥品），由 CDER 擔任主管中心，但廠商可以選擇「器械 QS 為基礎 + 8 條藥品條款」，而不一定要選「藥品 CGMP 為基礎」路徑。

Cross-labeled 的例外

如果兩個構成部分在完全分開的設施製造，則各設施只需遵循自己那部分（藥品或器械）的法規，不需要用精簡路徑。精簡路徑主要適用於單一設施同時製造或組裝整個組合產品的情況。

2. Documentation of CGMP Approach

Combination product manufacturers should be able to identify all documentation needed to demonstrate compliance with part 4, and make it readily accessible for FDA inspection. The facility's quality system documentation should identify the CGMP operating system for the combination product(s) manufactured at that facility.

  • NDAs, BLAs, ANDAs: CGMP approach described in the Common Technical Document (CTD).
  • PMAs: Documented in the manufacturing section of the PMA.

D. The Role of the Lead Center and Other Agency Components

A combination product is assigned to an Agency center (lead center) based on the primary mode of action (PMOA) — which constituent part provides the most important therapeutic action. The lead center has primary jurisdiction for:

  • Premarket review
  • Ensuring compliance with CGMP requirements

Manufacturers may contact the Office of Combination Products (OCP) for assistance with PMOA determinations and jurisdictional questions.

Note on QMSR (Important for 2026): The Quality System (QS) regulation (21 CFR Part 820) was revised effective February 2, 2026 to become the Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR), harmonizing with ISO 13485:2016. This guidance was issued prior to that effective date; manufacturers should review the current QMSR when applying the device QS-based streamlined approach.

2026 年重大更新：QS Reg → QMSR

生效日：2026 年 2 月 2 日

21 CFR Part 820（Quality System Regulation）正式更名並修訂為 QMSR（Quality Management System Regulation），與 ISO 13485:2016 高度一致化。

對組合產品廠商的影響：

  • 選擇「器械 QS 為基礎」精簡路徑的廠商，現在要對照 QMSR 而非舊的 Part 820
  • QMSR 引入了 ISO 13485 的條款結構，與過去 Part 820 的條款編號有所不同
  • 本指引文件（2017）中所有引用「Part 820」或「820.xx」的條款，在 QMSR 架構下需要重新映射
  • 廠商應更新 QMS 文件，確保引用正確的現行法規版本

CDMO 實務觀點：選哪條精簡路徑？

對於以無菌充填為主業的 CDMO，這個策略決策至關重要：

  • 藥品 CGMP 為基礎（路徑 A）：適合已建立完整 21 CFR 210/211 合規體系的藥品 CDMO。補加的 6 條器械條款中，820.30（設計管控）和 820.100（CAPA）對無菌充填 CDMO 影響最大，需要專門建立設計管控文件體系。
  • 器械 QS 為基礎（路徑 B）：適合以器械製造為主的 CDMO 或已導入 ISO 13485 的廠商。補加的 8 條藥品條款中，211.137（效期）、211.166（穩定性）是關鍵新增要求。

實務建議： 大多數無菌充填 CDMO 以藥品 CGMP 為核心，選路徑 A 最自然，但必須認真建立器械設計管控體系，不能僅以「這是 client 的設計」為由略過 820.30。

Section Summary

Sections I and II establish the regulatory architecture for combination product CGMP compliance. The key takeaways are:

  • Part 4 clarifies, does not create — existing CGMPs apply to constituent parts; Part 4 explains how.
  • Four types of combination products exist under 21 CFR 3.2(e): single-entity, co-packaged, cross-labeled, investigational cross-labeled.
  • Two compliance options: full compliance with all applicable CGMPs (Option 1), or a streamlined approach (Option 2).
  • Two streamlined pathways: drug CGMP-based (+ 6 device provisions) or device QS/QMSR-based (+ 8 drug provisions).
  • PMOA determines lead center, not which streamlined pathway to use.
  • QMSR in effect Feb 2, 2026 — replaces Part 820 for device QS.

Section III — Introduction

This section addresses some general considerations for CGMP compliance for combination products. Note that this guidance addresses only CGMP requirements. Additional requirements may apply including the need for premarket submissions as a result of changes to product design, intended use, or manufacture.

導論說明

III.A Demonstrating Compliance — 合規展示

How manufacturers must demonstrate compliance under the streamlined approach

What "Demonstrate" Means

The final rule identifies two ways for co-packaged and single-entity combination product manufacturers to demonstrate compliance with applicable CGMP requirements. The Agency intends for the term "demonstrate" to apply for part 4 as it would be for purposes of fulfilling the underlying CGMP regulations listed in 21 CFR 4.3. Manufacturers must demonstrate compliance with each applicable CGMP requirement for constituent parts and combination products.

If a streamlined approach is used, the manufacturer must demonstrate compliance with all of the relevant provisions of either the drug CGMPs or device QS regulation, and the provisions specified in 21 CFR 4.4(b) for the other set(s) of CGMP requirements applicable to the product.

A manufacturer who implements a streamlined approach is not expected to demonstrate compliance with the specific provisions of the non-base set of requirements other than those specified in 21 CFR 4.4(b) (e.g., for a drug CGMP-based streamlined approach, the manufacturer must demonstrate compliance with applicable drug CGMPs plus the six specified provisions from part 820, as applicable, but the manufacturer does not need to demonstrate compliance with the other specific provisions in part 820).

中文解析

「Demonstrate」的標準

「demonstrate」一詞在Part 4的意義，與在底層CGMP法規（Parts 210/211、820等）中完全相同的標準——即製造商必須能夠向FDA稽查員證明其實際符合規定，而不僅是書面聲稱符合。

精簡方式的要求結構

藥品CGMP為基礎的精簡方式：

  • 必須示範：全部適用的藥品CGMP（Parts 210/211）
  • 額外示範：21 CFR 4.4(b)(2)指定的Part 820六項條款
  • 不需示範：Part 820其他條款

器材QS法規為基礎的精簡方式：

  • 必須示範：全部適用的Part 820規定
  • 額外示範：21 CFR 4.4(b)(1)指定的Parts 210/211條款
  • 不需示範：Parts 210/211其他條款

衝突解決原則（21 CFR 4.4(e)）

若不同法規間發生衝突，對特定成分最具針對性的規定優先適用（特別法優於一般法）。此條款目的是解決法規衝突，而非授權FDA要求製造商遵守Part 4以外額外規定。

Cross-Referenced Provisions

Some of the provisions specified in part 4 from the drug CGMPs and device QS regulation cross-reference other provisions of parts 211 and 820, respectively. This raises the question of whether manufacturers must specifically demonstrate compliance with these cross-referenced provisions. FDA believes that manufacturers can adequately address the expectations of these cross-referenced provisions through implementation of either a drug CGMP-based or device QS regulation-based CGMP streamlined approach.

For example, 21 CFR 211.170 (Reserve samples) references 21 CFR 211.192 (Production record review). However, 21 CFR 211.192 is not a specified provision in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2) because part 820 encompasses the requirements of that section. In particular, under the CAPA requirements in 21 CFR 820.100, a manufacturer operating under a device QS-based streamlined approach should have the systems in place that would satisfy the requirements of 21 CFR 211.192.

Similarly, 21 CFR 211.103 (Calculation of yield) references 21 CFR 211.68 (Automatic, mechanical, and electronic equipment). Corresponding provisions in 21 CFR 820.70(i), applicable under a QS-regulation based streamlined approach, require that automated data processing systems be validated for their intended uses.

To take another example, the definition of design output in 21 CFR 820.3 makes reference to "device master record." Both part 211 and part 820 have requirements for "master records" (21 CFR 211.186 and 21 CFR 820.181). FDA believes the substantive expectations of these master record provisions are aligned and needed to control product development and manufacture.

比喻：法規的「引路石」

III.B Investigational Products — 研究用產品

Phase-dependent CGMP exemptions for drugs and devices in combination products

Scope and Phase Exemptions

Part 4 does not alter the scope of the underlying CGMP regulations for drugs, devices, biological products, and HCT/Ps. In particular, part 4 does not alter the applicability of these CGMP regulations to investigational products.

An investigational drug for use in a phase 1 study is subject to the statutory requirements set forth in 21 U.S.C. 351(a)(2)(B). The production of such a drug is generally exempt from compliance with the regulations in parts 210 and 211 and, therefore, so is an investigational combination product that includes a drug constituent part for use in a phase 1 study. This exemption does not apply to an investigational combination product or drug constituent part, however, once it has been made available for use by or for the sponsor in phase 2 or phase 3 studies, nor does the exemption apply to a drug product that has been lawfully marketed.

Under 21 CFR 812.1, investigational devices are exempt from part 820 except for design control requirements under 21 CFR 820.30. This exemption also applies to the manufacturing of investigational combination products that include device constituent parts. Investigational combination products that include a device constituent part are subject to design controls (except when the device constituent part is exempt from design controls), but not to the other provisions of part 820.

Design controls should start as early as possible, once a company has determined that a product appears to have clinical utility and management has committed to further development.

III.C.1 "Manufacture" and "Manufacturer" — 製造與製造商

Broad definition — designing is manufacturing too

Broad Definition of Manufacture

The definition of the term "manufacture" in part 4 is intended to include all of the activities considered within the scope of manufacturing for drugs, devices, biological products, and HCT/Ps. Accordingly, the definition of "manufacture" in 21 CFR 4.2 includes, but is not limited to, designing, fabricating, assembling, filling, processing, testing, labeling, packaging, repackaging, holding, and storage.

For example, if a company designs a drug-device combination product, that activity constitutes manufacture of that product, and the facility at which the design work occurs is a manufacturing facility. That company is a manufacturer subject to part 4 for the design activities it performs, even if all other aspects of the combination product's manufacture (e.g., fabricating, labeling, or packaging) are performed by another entity.

Similarly, a facility responsible for the holding and storage of a combination product would be subject to related CGMP requirements such as 21 CFR 211.142 (Warehousing procedures) and/or 21 CFR 820.150 (Storage), depending upon whether the facility applies a streamlined approach and if so, which approach it applies.

「製造」的廣義定義

21 CFR 4.2「Manufacture」定義範圍

Part 4的「製造」包含但不限於：

  • 設計（Designing） — 最重要的擴展，見右側說明
  • 製作（Fabricating）、組裝（Assembling）
  • 填充（Filling）、加工（Processing）
  • 測試（Testing）、貼標（Labeling）
  • 包裝（Packaging）、再包裝（Repackaging）
  • 持有（Holding）與儲存（Storage）

設計即製造 — 監管意涵

這是高度重要的監管原則：即使你只負責設計，未實際生產任何東西，你仍然是Part 4下的「製造商」，必須遵守相應的CGMP要求。

例如：一家專門開發藥物-器材組合產品設計的公司，其設計辦公室即為「製造設施」，即便所有實體製造都外包給其他廠商。

比喻：建築師也是「建造者」

建築師只畫圖、不蓋樓，但法律上仍須對建築設計的安全性負責。Part 4下的「設計即製造」同理——設計決策決定了產品的安全有效性，因此設計活動受CGMP約束。

CDMO關鍵影響

若CDMO為客戶提供設計服務（如協助設計prefilled syringe的填充與裝配參數，或協助客戶開發藥物與遞送器材的相容性規格），則該CDMO即成為Part 4下的製造商，須承擔相應的CGMP合規責任，不得以「我只是服務提供者，不是製造商」為由規避。

III.C.2 "Constituent Part" vs. "Component" — 成分部分 vs 元件

Critical terminology distinction — different regulatory purposes

Different Terms, Different Purposes

The term "constituent part" is used by the Agency as a succinct way to identify a drug, device, or biological product included in a combination product.

Under the drug CGMPs, "component" is defined as "any ingredient intended for use in the manufacture of a drug product, including those that may not appear in such drug product" (21 CFR 210.3). Under the device QS regulation, the term "component" is defined as "any raw material, substance, piece, part, software, firmware, labeling, or assembly which is intended to be included as part of the finished, packaged, and labeled device" (21 CFR 820.3(c)). Components can include sub-assemblies that are further processed to make a finished device.

In contrast, a finished device is "suitable for use or capable of functioning, whether or not it is packaged, labeled, or sterilized," as would be the case for a device constituent part as incorporated into a combination product.

A facility that manufactures only device components, including device components used in a combination product, is not made subject to the device QS regulation by part 4. Similarly, a facility that manufactures solely active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) is not subject to part 211 (though it is subject to statutory CGMP requirements under Section 501 of the FD&C Act). Part 4 does not make such a drug component manufacturing facility subject to part 211 if those components are for use in a combination product.

However, a facility that manufactures a drug-device combination product formed from components is subject to parts 211 and 820, and must comply with these two sets of regulations as provided in part 4.

「Component manufacturer」≠「Constituent part manufacturer」——前者只負責下游原料，後者負責整個藥品或器材成品。

CDMO常見混淆情境

答案：注射器活塞是器材元件（device component），不是finished device。若你只製造活塞而不組裝成品器材，你的設施不受Part 820約束（但須受其他適用規範，如供應商資格要求等）。

III.C.3 Drug Containers & Closures vs. Delivery Devices — 容器封蓋 vs 遞送器材

The critical hold-vs-deliver distinction — prefilled syringes are always combination products

The Hold vs. Deliver Distinction

The Agency draws a distinction between mere drug containers and closures and containers and closures that are also devices. The essential difference is generally whether the article is designed to deliver the drug it contains or merely to hold it.

If the article merely holds the drug, it is only subject to drug CGMPs as a container or closure. An article that holds or contains a drug, but also delivers it, may also be a device subject to the device QS regulation in addition to the requirements relating to drug containers and closures.

A container closure system is the sum of packaging components that together contain and protect the drug product. Elements of container closure systems that are device constituent parts include piston syringes, metered dose inhalers (MDIs), and containers for intravenously-administered (IV) fluids (such as IV bags containing saline or anti-coagulants), which both hold and deliver the drugs or biological products they contain. In the case of an IV container, the drug is delivered to the patient via the IV line.

The Agency has exempted some devices from all or certain provisions of the device QS regulations. For example, 21 CFR 880.6430 exempts liquid medication dispensers (cups, droppers, etc.) from all provisions of the device QS regulation with the exception of 820.180 and 820.198. Such exemptions may extend to device constituent parts of combination products.

If the exemptions for a device constituent part cover all of the part 820 provisions included in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1), then the Agency will consider the combination product manufacturer CGMP compliant so long as the CGMP operating system is compliant with part 211 (no demonstration of compliance with part 820 will be necessary).

However, if a device that would ordinarily be exempt from all or certain provisions in part 820 is incorporated into a container closure system (e.g., if a dropper is incorporated into the cap of a bottle), this may be a new use of the device such that the exemptions from part 820 may not be applicable.

Manufacturers should document their evaluation if they believe a container closure system that has delivery attributes is not a device, or if they believe a device constituent part is exempt from any or all provisions under the device QS regulation.

III.C.4 Convenience Kits — 便利套組

Narrow definition — any modification removes "convenience kit" status

Definition and Conditions

A kit that includes two or more different types of medical products (e.g., a device and a drug) is a co-packaged combination product and, therefore, part 4 applies to the manufacture of the kit. The CGMP requirements applicable to a kit manufacturer depend on what products are included in the kit.

If the kit includes only products that are 1) legally marketed independently and 2) packaged in the kit as they are when independently marketed (including any labeling used for independent marketing), it is a "convenience kit," and the only manufacturing steps for the combination product would be the assembly, packaging, labeling, any sterilization, and further processing of the kit itself. Accordingly, the kit manufacturer would only have to demonstrate compliance with CGMP requirements with respect to those manufacturing activities.

If a kit includes any products that are repackaged, relabeled, or otherwise modified from the independently marketed product, then the kit is not a convenience kit. Likewise, if the kit manufacturer includes labeling for the kit that modifies the intended use for a constituent part, then the kit is not a convenience kit.

Co-packaged combination product manufacturers, including manufacturers of convenience kits, should carefully consider the impact of any sterilization process on the items in the co-package. Some sterilization methods suitable for devices, such as irradiation, are not suitable for many drugs. Additional verification to confirm no degradation in safety and effectiveness after any re-sterilization may be necessary. Manufacturers should consider whether re-sterilization affects stability, material properties, and expiration dating/shelf-life.

Manufacturers should document their evaluation if they believe a kit qualifies as a convenience kit.

便利套組的嚴格條件

Convenience Kit的兩個充分必要條件

  1. 獨立合法上市：套組中每個產品必須是已在市場上獨立販售的合法上市產品。
  2. 原樣包裝：每個產品必須與其獨立上市時完全相同（包含標示內容），不得有任何再包裝、再標示或修改。

只要這兩個條件同時成立，套組製造商只需對「組裝、包裝、貼標、滅菌等套組製造活動本身」負CGMP責任，不需承擔各成分的完整CGMP義務。

失去Convenience Kit資格的情況

  • 任何成分被再包裝（repackaged）
  • 任何成分被再標示（relabeled）
  • 任何成分有其他形式的修改
  • 套組標示改變了任何成分的預期用途
  • 套組引入了新的滅菌過程，改變了產品的使用環境

一旦失去Convenience Kit資格，製造商須承擔全套成分CGMP責任。

比喻：超市禮盒 vs 特製禮盒

超市把三瓶已上市的現成醬油用一個籃子裝起來送禮（原樣），這是Convenience Kit——你只須確保籃子品質，不須重新驗證每瓶醬油。

但若你把醬油重新分裝成小瓶，或加印客製化標籤，就不再是Convenience Kit——你現在負責了那瓶醬油的全部。

滅菌相容性考量（套組製造商必知）

即使是Convenience Kit，若對整組進行滅菌（如環氧乙烷、輻射），必須評估：

  • 某些器材可承受輻射滅菌，但許多藥品不行
  • 再滅菌（re-sterilization）對穩定性、效期、材料特性的影響
  • 滅菌過程對各成分的交叉影響（如包裝材料的揮發物影響藥品）
  • 滅菌確效（validation）必須涵蓋套組中的每個成分

CDMO場景應用

客戶請你為其醫院手術套組（surgical kit）做最終包裝並滅菌。套組包含：一瓶已上市消毒藥液 + 一個已上市手術刀盤。

判斷：若兩者都維持原包裝且不改標示，你可以主張Convenience Kit，但仍須確認：(1) 滅菌製程對兩者均安全；(2) 滅菌確效涵蓋整組；(3) 整個判斷過程有書面記錄。

III.D What CGMP Requirements Apply? — 哪些CGMP適用？

Product-specific applicability, "where appropriate" language, multi-facility coordination

D.1 Applicability of CGMP Requirements to a Product

The preamble to the proposed rule noted that not all CGMP provisions apply to all combination products. For example, only an over-the-counter (OTC) combination product must comply with the tamper-evident packaging requirements in the drug CGMPs and only combination products that include a type of device constituent part that is installed or serviced must comply with installation and servicing requirements in the device QS regulation.

Many of the requirements for biological products are applicable only to certain types of biological products. For example, blood and blood components are subject to the CGMP requirements for such products under part 606. In addition, a vaccine manufactured using a spore-forming microorganism would be subject to 21 CFR 600.11(e)(3).

Similarly, not all CGMP requirements may apply at a facility that performs only certain aspects of the manufacture of a combination product. As 21 CFR 210.2(b) and 820.1(a)(1) describe, an entity that engages in only some operations subject to the regulations in parts 210, 211, 600 through 680, 820, and 1271, need only comply with the regulations applicable to those operations. For example, a facility that manufactures a co-packaged combination product that includes a finished, already-packaged drug manufactured under the drug CGMPs and received from another facility need not calculate yield for that drug constituent part.

按產品與設施活動判斷適用性

「適用性」的兩個維度

產品層次（Product-level）：某些CGMP規定只適用於特定類型的產品。例如：

  • 防止篡改包裝要求 → 只適用於OTC組合產品
  • 安裝與服務要求（820.170/820.200） → 只適用於需安裝/維修的器材
  • 血液CGMP（Part 606） → 只適用於血液製品

設施層次（Facility-level）：某個設施只執行部分製造活動，只須符合與該活動相關的CGMP規定。

實務推論

若你的CDMO設施從客戶處接收「已完成並包裝」的藥品成分，只負責將其組裝進套組：

  • 你不須為該藥品重新計算產率（yield calculation）
  • 你只須符合組裝、包裝、貼標等你實際執行的活動相關CGMP

「我做什麼，就符合那部分的CGMP」——活動範圍決定合規範圍。

D.2 The Meaning of "Where Appropriate"

Firms must demonstrate compliance with all regulations applicable to their product and facility under part 4. However, the drug CGMPs, device QS regulation, and CGTPs for HCT/Ps use language such as "where appropriate" or "as necessary" to acknowledge that certain measures may not be necessary or applicable under certain circumstances and that manufacturers must pursue such measures if they are needed.

Such language indicates that firms have the opportunity to document justifications for determining that such a measure or approach is not appropriate or necessary for a particular product or the specific manufacturing activity they are undertaking. Such justifications should generally be maintained within the quality system documentation and may warrant Agency review prior to implementation depending on the product type and relevant premarket submission obligations.

Under both 21 CFR 820.1(a)(3) and 1271.150(e), a requirement that is qualified by "where appropriate," is "appropriate" if non-implementation could reasonably be expected to result in the product not meeting its specified requirements. Under both provisions, a requirement is deemed to be appropriate unless the manufacturer can document justification that it is not.

For example, 21 CFR 820.30(i) stipulates procedures for "the identification, documentation, validation or where appropriate verification, review, and approval of design changes before their implementation." Changes to design, such as changes to properties of the material or dimensional specifications, may not need to be validated if they can be verified through appropriate measurement and test methods. However, changes that could impact user needs, such as changes to the user interface, may require validation.

「Where Appropriate」的正確解讀

預設立場：適用，除非能舉證

「Where appropriate」不是免死金牌。FDA的解讀是：

  • 凡有「where appropriate」字眼的規定，預設為適用
  • 製造商可以不執行，但必須有書面記錄的正當理由
  • 理由必須說明：「為何不執行不會影響產品符合規格要求」

換言之：你可以不做，但你必須能解釋為什麼不做不會有問題。

比喻：「在必要時使用安全帽」

工地規定「在必要時配戴安全帽」。如果你不戴，必須能說明「這個區域無墜物風險，因此不必要」，而不能只說「規定說『必要時』，所以我覺得不必要」。

CGMP中的「where appropriate」同理——負舉證責任的是你，不是FDA。

設計變更確認 vs 驗證（820.30(i)範例）

材料規格的尺寸公差變更 → 通常可透過量測驗收（verification）而無需完整確效（validation）。

使用者介面的操作流程變更 → 影響使用者需求，通常需要使用者研究或可用性驗證（validation）。

關鍵問題：這個變更是否影響使用者需求或安全有效性？影響則需確效；不影響則可用驗收代替，但仍需記錄理由。

D.3 CGMP Responsibilities Across Facilities

A facility that manufacturers a constituent part of a combination product or a complete combination product must be compliant with the CGMP requirements applicable to each manufacturing process that occurs at that specific facility. In addition, the combination product owner (the holder of the marketing authorization for the product) retains overall responsibility for the product, even if the owner is not directly engaged in its manufacture.

A facility that manufactures only a finished device intended to be a constituent part must comply only with the device QS regulation. Similarly, a facility that manufactures only a drug constituent part must comply only with the drug CGMPs. Even if a facility is manufacturing only one type of constituent part, the CGMP operating system should take into account considerations for the combination product as a whole as appropriate.

Before changes are made to the manufacturing process of a constituent part, the CGMP operating system should ensure consideration of whether such changes could affect performance and/or interaction with the other constituent part(s) and, if so, whether the safety and effectiveness of the combination product could be impacted. Quality agreements with constituent part manufacturer(s) are one way to ensure that changes to a constituent part are transparent to a combination product manufacturer or owner.

Some CGMP requirements concern the product as a whole, such as design controls, and some concern overarching considerations for the manufacturing process as a whole, such as CAPA requirements. Take, for example, a manufacturing facility collecting nonconformance data that are trended as an input to the CAPA system for a combination product. If a problem is detected that requires a product or process design change, the change may require modification of manufacturing activities at another facility or the expertise to develop and implement the change may reside at a different facility.

A CAPA system that is shared between facilities, or facility-specific CAPA systems with established links between them, may facilitate handling of issues requiring multi-facility collaboration. Regardless of the approach, the CAPA system(s) should allow for adequate flow of information between facilities and manufacturers for the combination product and appropriate investigation and resolution of identified CAPA issues.

Manufacturing activities that occur at multiple facilities and associated CGMP operating systems should be coordinated appropriately. Each manufacturing facility for a combination product should have documentation specifying its respective responsibilities, and the manufacturer of the finished combination product should have access to this documentation.

Measures that might be taken to ensure CGMP compliance at all manufacturing facilities include auditing and other oversight activities. When multiple facilities participate in the manufacturing process, the owner may facilitate CGMP compliance by coordinating interactions among the facilities, including by entering into comprehensive quality agreements with the various facilities and suppliers.

III.E Control of Changes to a Combination Product — 組合產品的變更控制

Cross-constituent impact assessment — a drug change may trigger a device change

Coordinating Changes Across Manufacturers

While not an issue unique to combination products, coordination of changes among manufacturers participating in the manufacture of a combination product is an important CGMP issue. Appropriate consideration should be given to any implications for the safety or effectiveness of the combination product that might arise from changes to the combination product or its constituent parts.

Single-entity and co-packaged combination product manufacturers must establish arrangements with their suppliers, contractors, and consultants to receive notice of changes in the product or service, where possible, in accordance with 21 CFR 820.50. This notice should address changes the supplier makes to its process or product—including to an API, other drug components, or a container-closure system—that it manufactures, if the change might affect the downstream manufacturing process or the quality of the combination product itself.

These combination product manufacturers should also establish procedures for acceptance of components, containers/closures, and constituent parts to ensure both detection and evaluation of any changes that are critical to the safety or effectiveness of the combination product prior to incorporating them into the finished combination product.

Similarly, if one entity manufactures one constituent part of a cross-labeled combination product and another entity manufactures the other constituent part, both should have procedures in place to inform one another of changes that may affect the safety or effectiveness of the combination product, and to confirm that the specifications for the respective constituent parts remain appropriate or are updated as needed.

For example, a change to the drug constituent part of a cross-labeled combination product might require a design change to the device constituent part for the combination product to remain safe and effective. Accordingly, awareness and assessment of drug changes to determine whether they require a change to the device constituent part(s) may be important to compliance with design control requirements for the device under 21 CFR 820.30.

Similarly, a change to a device constituent part of a cross-labeled combination product may necessitate a change to a drug constituent part, which may require a change in its chemistry, manufacturing, and controls (CMC).

跨成分變更控制：CDMO的關鍵風險

組合產品變更控制的核心邏輯

組合產品的特殊之處在於：一個成分的變更，可能對另一個成分的安全有效性造成影響。

這意味著變更評估必須是「跨成分的」，而不僅是「本成分內部的」。光是自己的品質體系通過了變更評估還不夠——必須確認另一個成分也沒有問題。

比喻：相互咬合的齒輪

藥品成分與器材成分就像兩個咬合的齒輪。你改變其中一個齒輪的大小或齒距，另一個齒輪也必須相應調整，否則整個系統就會卡住或空轉。

組合產品的變更控制，就是確保「齒輪咬合系統」在任何一方改變後，整體仍能安全運作。

供應商變更通知機制（21 CFR 820.50）

製造商必須與供應商、承包商、顧問建立「變更通知」安排。關鍵是：

  • 供應商改變其API配方、輔料、封蓋系統材料時，必須事先通知
  • 進料查驗程序必須能夠偵測並評估這些變更
  • 在納入成品前，必須先評估變更對組合產品安全有效性的影響

核心監管原則

  • Demonstrate（示範）：與底層CGMP法規相同的標準；精簡方式下只須示範特定條款，但「示範」的品質標準不降低。
  • 研究階段豁免：藥品Phase 1豁免Parts 210/211；器材豁免Part 820但設計控制（820.30）永遠不豁免。
  • Constituent part ≠ Component：元件製造商（component manufacturer）不受Part 4約束；只有finished constituent part的製造商才受約束。
  • Hold vs. Deliver：僅持有藥品 = container/closure only；持有並遞送 = combination product，須符合Part 820。
  • Convenience Kit：嚴格條件（獨立上市＋原樣包裝）；任何修改均失去資格。
  • Owner的整體責任：上市許可持有人即使外包全部製造，仍負最終CGMP責任。
  • 跨成分變更評估：一個成分的變更必須評估對其他成分的影響，品質協議是管理機制。

CDMO最高風險清單

五大CDMO風險點

  1. 設計服務即製造：若提供設計諮詢，即使不實際生產，也是Part 4下的製造商。
  2. PFS永遠是組合產品：承接PFS灌裝，必須有雙軌CGMP體系（藥品＋器材）。
  3. Convenience Kit資格誤判：再包裝或改標示後以為仍是Convenience Kit，可能面臨重大合規缺失。
  4. CAPA資訊孤島：各設施CAPA系統未連結，導致跨設施問題無法有效處理。
  5. 供應商變更未通知：API或封蓋材料供應商變更未及時評估對器材端的影響。

Section IV.A — Overview: The Six Device QS Provisions

This section provides summary descriptions of the provisions from the device QS regulation with which manufacturers of single-entity and co-packaged combination products that include a device constituent part must demonstrate compliance when using the drug CGMP-based streamlined approach established under 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1).

This discussion is not meant to provide a comprehensive analysis, but rather to help manufacturers — particularly drug and biological product manufacturers who may be less familiar with the device QS regulation — understand the purpose and basic elements of the device QS regulation provisions specified in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1).

The six required device QS provisions under 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1) are:

  • 820.20 — Management Responsibility
  • 820.30 — Design Controls
  • 820.50 — Purchasing Controls
  • 820.100 — Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA)
  • 820.170 — Installation
  • 820.200 — Servicing
Note: This section does not provide detailed discussion of 820.170 (installation) and 820.200 (servicing) because the Agency anticipates that installed and serviced devices will rarely be constituent parts of single-entity or co-packaged combination products.

背景解讀：為何是這六項？

兩條路徑的邏輯

21 CFR Part 4 對組合產品提供兩種CGMP遵循途徑：

  • 藥品CGMP基礎途徑（4.4(b)(1)）：以 21 CFR Part 210/211 為主體，附加這六項器材QS規定。這是藥廠 / CDMO 的自然路徑。
  • 器材QS基礎途徑（4.4(b)(2)）：以 21 CFR Part 820 為主體，附加藥品CGMP特定要求。這是器材廠的路徑。

本節討論的是前者——藥品CDMO最常走的路。

比喻：母語加選修

想像你的公司已熟悉藥品CGMP，它是你的「母語」。加入組合產品後，你不需要改說器材QS的「外語」，但需要學會六個關鍵「器材詞彙」：820.20、820.30、820.50、820.100、820.170、820.200。

FDA 選擇這六項，是因為它們補足了藥品CGMP在設計管控、供應鏈、閉環CAPA、管理承諾等面向的系統性缺口。

  1. 820.30 設計管控 — 全新概念，衝擊最大
  2. 820.100 CAPA — 需將偏差系統升級為閉環結構
  3. 820.20 管理責任 — 正式化管理審查
  4. 820.50 採購管控 — 擴展供應商資格範圍
  5. 820.170 / 820.200 — 多數無菌充填CDMO不適用

1. Management Responsibility — 21 CFR 820.20

Requirements under 21 CFR 820.20 ensure that management with executive responsibility (i.e., senior employees of a manufacturer who have the authority to establish or make changes to the quality policy and quality system) are actively engaged in oversight of the quality system, and reflect the expectation that management with executive responsibility demonstrate an active, ongoing commitment to quality system development and implementation.

Statutory CGMP provisions and regulations for drugs establish requirements related to management responsibility, and the Agency has also issued guidance on this topic. However, there are specific requirements in 21 CFR 820.20 that are not explicitly addressed in drug CGMP requirements, and a manufacturer of a combination product that includes a device constituent part must ensure that the elements required under 21 CFR 820.20 are satisfied.

A combination product manufacturer must establish and maintain an adequate organizational structure to ensure that the product is designed and produced in accordance with CGMP requirements. This structure must:

  • Establish the appropriate responsibility, authority, and interrelation of all personnel who manage, perform, and assess work affecting quality;
  • Provide adequate resources for management, performance of work, and assessment activities; and
  • Include an appointed management member who is responsible for ensuring that quality system requirements are effectively established and maintained, and for reporting on the performance of the quality system to management with executive responsibility.

Management with executive responsibility must establish the quality policy and objectives for and commitment to quality. These policies and objectives are reflected in the quality plan and quality system procedures.

The quality plan must define the quality practices, resources, and activities relevant to the combination product that is being designed and manufactured and must establish how the quality requirements will be met. The plan can either be an independent document, or it can reference elements of the manufacturer's quality system.

The quality system procedures (21 CFR 820.20(e)) ensure compliance with each aspect of the CGMPs applicable to the combination product. Drug manufacturers may already have some such procedures in place as part of the requirements for a quality control unit under 21 CFR 211.22 and can reference these procedures and augment them as needed to meet the requirements of 21 CFR 820.20.

Management with executive responsibility must periodically conduct management review of the suitability and effectiveness of the quality system, including to ensure that the quality system satisfies the established quality policy and objectives. These reviews must be conducted at defined intervals and with sufficient frequency. Management review procedures should ensure that management has adequate access to relevant information from facilities contracted to perform manufacturing activities for the combination product.

820.20 管理責任 — 解讀與應用

核心概念：管理責任的法定化

藥品CGMP（21 CFR 211.22）雖然要求設置品質管制部門，但對「高階管理層的具體義務」描述較模糊。820.20 則明確規定：

  • 品質政策（Quality Policy）：由最高管理層親自制定，不可委託
  • 管理代表（Management Representative）：指定一名管理層成員負責QMS，並向高層報告績效
  • 管理審查（Management Review）：定期、有紀錄的正式審查，是法定義務而非選項
  • 品質計畫（Quality Plan）：針對特定組合產品的資源與活動規劃

比喻：從「默契管理」到「契約管理」

在純藥品工廠，管理層對品質的承諾往往是文化性的——大家「懂」高層重視品質，但未必白紙黑字。820.20 要求把這份承諾寫成正式法律義務：管理審查要有日期、有議程、有結論、有後續行動紀錄。

就像公司治理從「家族默契」升級為「董事會議事規範」。

藥廠與CGMP的差距分析

藥廠現有的品質管制單位（21 CFR 211.22）通常已具備：品質政策、SOP體系、品質審查機制。

典型缺口：

  • 管理審查未正式化（無固定頻率、無會議紀錄）
  • 未明確指定「管理代表」角色及其職責
  • 品質計畫（Quality Plan）未針對組合產品制定

建議做法：在既有 QMS 中增加組合產品專屬的 Quality Plan 附錄，並設立每季度管理審查機制。

CDMO 實務場景

假設你的無菌充填CDMO接受一個 PFS（預填充注射器）組合產品委託案。820.20 要求你必須：

  • 為此產品制定組合產品專屬的 Quality Plan
  • 指定管理代表（可以是現有 QA Director）並正式記錄其職責
  • 在每次管理審查中納入此組合產品的品質指標（如OOS率、CAPA完成率、設計變更記錄）
  • 確保合約製造設施的品質資訊可被你的管理審查取得

2. Design Controls — 21 CFR 820.30

Design control activities confirm that there are no negative interactions between constituent parts, and ensure that their combined use results in a combination product that is safe and effective and performs as expected.

Design control requirements apply to activities during product development as well as to postmarket changes to the design or manufacturing process.

The extent and complexity of the design controls process and associated documentation will vary based on the product. For example, consider a drug product that is already legally marketed independently that will have the same formulation, route of administration and intended use when marketed as part of a combination product that also incorporates a delivery device. Design controls would begin when the delivery device configuration is judged to be feasible and appropriate to develop.

While pharmaceutical development focuses on drug considerations, if broadened to take into account the other constituent parts of combination products and how they interrelate, many pharmaceutical development practices (for example, Quality by Design principles) can be leveraged and built upon when demonstrating compliance with design controls for a combination product.

Design and Development Plan

As part of the design controls, a design and development plan must be established to describe or reference the design and development activities and define responsibility for implementation. The plans must identify and describe the interfaces with different groups or activities that provide, or result in, input to the design and development process.

Design Input

Design input requirements for the combination product should include considerations such as performance characteristics, safety and reliability requirements, and expected needs of product users and patients. Design inputs should be considered early in product development to ensure that development efforts are consistent with the product's intended use, including the needs of users, including patients.

The Quality Target Product Profile (QTPP) and Critical Quality Attributes (CQAs) in pharmaceutical development may be applied for combination products in a manner consistent with design input and output requirements.

Design Output

Once the design inputs have been established, the design outputs (e.g., specifications and engineering drawings) must be developed based on those inputs.

Design Verification and Validation

Verification means confirmation by examination and provision of objective evidence that specified requirements have been fulfilled. Design verification confirms that the product developed is consistent with the assumptions made by the design team when developing design inputs.

Design validation means establishing by objective evidence that product specifications conform to user needs and intended use(s). Design validation includes testing of production units or their equivalents under actual or simulated use conditions.

Some design verification (e.g., bench and/or pre-clinical testing) and validation (e.g., human factors testing, when appropriate) will typically be completed prior to the initiation of clinical studies of safety and efficacy for the combination product.

Design Review

Design review is required to ensure that formal and documented reviews of the design are planned and conducted at appropriate stages of the product's design development, and all of these activities must be documented in the design history file.

Risk Analysis

Manufacturers must perform risk analysis where appropriate, which should begin early in the design process and continue throughout the lifecycle for the product. Risk analysis should enable identification of unacceptable risks so that they can be mitigated.

Design Transfer

Manufacturers must establish and maintain design transfer procedures to ensure effective translation of design specifications into production methods and procedures. Design transfer is the bridge between the design of the product and the manufacturing process to make the commercial product.

Design Changes

Manufacturers are required to have procedures to ensure that any changes to design requirements are identified, documented, validated or verified where appropriate, reviewed, and approved prior to implementation. Change controls apply once initial design inputs have been approved by the manufacturer.

Design History File (DHF)

The Design History File (DHF) for a combination product captures all design issues relating to the combined use of the constituent parts. The DHF may not need to document design and development planning for established characteristics of the individual constituent parts if that constituent part was previously approved for the same indication.

If a finished device, drug, or biological product is purchased, the combination product manufacturer is not required to retrospectively "design" that constituent part with respect to such previously reviewed characteristics. Rather, the combination product manufacturer should understand the constituent part's existing design specifications thoroughly in order to perform design controls properly for its use in the combination product.

820.30 設計管控 — 藥廠最陌生的要求

為何這是最大衝擊？

純藥品工廠從不需要「設計管控」，因為藥物配方開發走的是 ICH Q8 製藥開發的框架，重點在化學穩定性、製程工藝，而不是「硬體設計」。

但當你製造含有器材組成元件的組合產品（如 PFS、自動注射器、吸入器），那個「器材」部分是有物理設計的，FDA 要求你對它執行完整的設計管控週期。

比喻：建築師 vs. 廚師

藥品開發像廚師：你試驗配方，找到對的比例，然後把食譜寫下來（批次記錄）。你的關注點是「成品好不好吃」（藥效、安全性）。

器材設計管控像建築師：你先定義用戶需求（設計輸入），畫出藍圖（設計輸出），確認藍圖符合需求（驗證），蓋完後確認使用者真的能住進去（確效），並把整個設計演變過程建檔（DHF）。

組合產品需要你同時扮演廚師和建築師。

關鍵時間點：Phase 2 之前就要開始

FDA 指引明確：設計驗證（Verification）和部分確效活動（如人因工程測試）應在臨床試驗啟動前完成。

實務意義：設計管控文件體系必須在早期開發階段就建立，而不是上市前才補。很多藥廠的錯誤在於：把設計管控當作「上市前的行政整理工作」，但 FDA 要求的是從可行性評估開始就有紀錄。

DHF vs. DMR vs. DHR — 三個縮寫的區別

  • DHF（Design History File）：記錄「如何設計出這個產品」的所有過程文件
  • DMR（Device Master Record）：記錄「如何製造這個產品」的所有現行規格文件
  • DHR（Device History Record）：記錄「每批產品如何被製造出來」的生產記錄

類比到藥品世界：DHF ≈ 開發報告；DMR ≈ Master Batch Record；DHR ≈ Batch Record。

CDMO 實務場景：現有藥品升級為組合產品

你的客戶原本委託你灌裝一個已上市的單株抗體（mAb），現在要改用 PFS 自動注射器組合銷售。

設計管控的起點是「評估 PFS 器材可行性」的那一刻，而不是申請 BLA supplement 的時候。你需要：

  • 以既有藥品規格（配方、劑量、給藥途徑）作為設計輸入的基礎
  • 評估 PFS 材質（矽化劑、橡膠柱塞）對藥品穩定性的影響 → 這是組合產品的新設計輸入
  • 建立 DHF，收納所有相容性測試、人因工程研究、臨床橋接數據
  • 即使藥品本身無需重新設計，器材-藥品介面的設計管控仍是必要的

QTPP / CQA 如何對應設計管控？

FDA 指引認可這個橋接：藥廠熟悉的 QTPP（目標品質概況）和 CQA（關鍵品質屬性）概念，可以作為組合產品設計輸入的藥品端基礎。

例如：CQA「注射劑量精確性 ±5%」可以直接轉化為 PFS 器材端的設計輸入規格。這讓藥廠可以用熟悉的語言切入設計管控框架。

思考練習

一個藥廠將一個已在 vial 劑型上市的 mAb，改為 autoinjector 組合產品。請判斷：哪些 DHF 文件必須新建？哪些可以從既有開發報告中引用？什麼情況下需要進行額外的設計驗證？

3. Purchasing Controls — 21 CFR 820.50

Manufacturers of single-entity and co-packaged combination products that include a device constituent part must control purchased products and services as described in 21 CFR 820.50. They must establish such purchasing controls for products received at their facility for use in the manufacture of the combination product, for all suppliers of these products, and for suppliers of services obtained (such as terminal sterilization conducted by an outside entity).

Facilities that have previously manufactured only drugs, rather than devices or combination products, will likely have relevant procedures in place in accordance with part 211, subpart E. However, if these procedures do not demonstrate compliance with the specific requirements of 21 CFR 820.50, they must be augmented to do so.

Manufacturers must evaluate potential suppliers and define the type and extent of control to be exercised over them based on the evaluation results. Manufacturers may design and conduct such evaluations based on factors such as the risks associated with the supplied product or service and complexity of the specifications for it.

Manufacturers must establish and maintain records of acceptable suppliers for purchased products and services, and establish and maintain data that clearly describe or reference the specified requirements for products and services received (e.g., contracts with relevant terms).

One way to facilitate purchasing control is the careful structuring of purchasing agreements with suppliers. Where possible, such agreements are used to ensure that the combination product manufacturer is notified of changes to the products, the manufacturing process, or the services being provided. Such notice of changes facilitates compliance not only with purchasing control duties, but also, potentially, with other regulatory requirements, including design control obligations to complete additional design verification testing to address the change.

If it is not possible to obtain such notice, the combination product manufacturer should implement additional controls to ensure that changes are identified and appropriate measures are taken.

Note: Regardless of the nature of purchasing controls, combination product manufacturers must comply with the testing requirements under 21 CFR 211.84 with respect to drug components, and product containers and closures.

820.50 採購管控 — 超越 211.84 的器材面向

820.50 vs. 211.84 — 兩套系統的互補關係

藥品CGMP下，21 CFR 211.84 已要求對藥品組成成分（原料、容器、封口件）進行取樣、測試、驗收。那為何還需要 820.50？

  • 211.84：聚焦在藥品端的進廠檢驗（化學、微生物測試）
  • 820.50：聚焦在整個供應鏈的風險評估與管控，包括器材零組件供應商的資格審核與持續監控

關鍵：即使你已有 211.84 的進廠檢驗程序，針對器材組成元件的供應商管控部分仍需依據 820.50 的架構進行補強。

820.50 供應商管控的三個層次

  • 層次一：供應商評估
    基於風險（供應品的關鍵性、規格複雜度）決定審核深度：書面審計 → 問卷 → 現場稽核
  • 層次二：核可供應商清單（ASL）
    建立並維護正式的 Approved Supplier List，記錄評估結論與核可條件
  • 層次三：採購合約條款
    合約中明訂供應商須主動通知材料/製程變更（Change Notification Clause）

變更通知條款 — 為何至關重要？

組合產品的挑戰在於：器材零件供應商自行更改其材料（如 PFS 玻璃管壁厚度、橡膠柱塞配方），可能直接影響藥品穩定性，但他們未必主動告知你。

FDA 明確建議：在採購合約中加入供應商變更通知義務條款，一旦供應商的製程或材料有異動，必須提前告知，讓你能執行補充的設計驗證測試。

這個條款同時服務於 820.50（採購管控）和 820.30（設計管控），是組合產品供應鏈管理的核心工具。

比喻：汽車零件 vs. 藥瓶

買普通藥品原料，像買食材——你只要確認品質規格符合即可。但買 PFS 自動注射器的器材零件，像買汽車零件——你不只要確認零件規格，還要確認供應商的製程穩定性、品管能力、變更管理程序，因為零件的製程改變可能影響整個系統的安全性。

CDMO 實務場景

你的CDMO為一個 PFS 組合產品進行充填。供應鏈涉及：

  • 玻璃管供應商（器材零件）
  • 橡膠柱塞供應商（器材零件，與藥品直接接觸）
  • 外包滅菌服務（ETO或gamma滅菌廠）

820.50 要求你必須對上述三類供應商都進行正式的資格審核，並在採購合約中加入變更通知條款。即使這些供應商在你的 211.84 程序中已被覆蓋，器材端的風險評估面向仍需補齊。

思考練習

你的CDMO目前有完整的 211.84 進廠檢驗程序和供應商資格系統。請列舉三項在轉型為組合產品製造商後，需要針對 820.50 進行補充的具體差距。

4. Corrective and Preventive Action — 21 CFR 820.100

Manufacturers of co-packaged or single-entity combination products that have a device constituent part must establish and maintain procedures for implementing CAPA in accordance with 21 CFR 820.100. Relevant requirements in the drug CGMPs include 21 CFR 211.192 and 21 CFR 211.180(e).

Manufacturers of the combination product must undertake CAPA measures, when required, for issues arising at their facilities. All relevant manufacturers should participate in cross-facility efforts, as appropriate, to determine the root-cause of problems and the appropriate measures to correct such problems and prevent recurrence. Manufacturers of combination products should document these activities.

The CAPA process for combination products also should consider implications of corrective and preventive actions for all constituent parts and for the combination product as a whole.

While combination product manufacturers have flexibility in coordinating CAPA systems across facilities, they maintain responsibility for ensuring that the 21 CFR 820.100 requirements are met. Manufacturers should ensure that an appropriately comprehensive review of activities is undertaken at relevant facilities to determine the cause of existing or potential problems, which could include manufacturing problems, deviations, or nonconformities for a constituent part or the combination product as a whole.

The manufacturer should have appropriate mechanisms in place to ensure that:

  • Issues are identified;
  • Action(s) needed to correct and prevent recurrence are taken; and
  • Necessary changes are implemented.

The manufacturer should take appropriate measures, which may include CAPAs, with regard to all relevant manufacturing steps at all relevant facilities to correct problems and to prevent or mitigate them going forward.

820.100 CAPA — 閉環品質管理的核心

什麼是「閉環CAPA」？

藥品CGMP（21 CFR 211.192）要求偏差調查，但重點在找出原因。820.100 更進一步，要求建立完整的閉環系統：

  1. 識別（Identify）：系統化收集品質數據，識別問題或潛在問題
  2. 調查（Investigate）：確定根本原因（Root Cause Analysis）
  3. 糾正行動（Corrective Action）：消除已發生問題的原因
  4. 預防行動（Preventive Action）：消除潛在問題的原因，防止首次發生
  5. 驗證有效性（Verify Effectiveness）：確認CAPA真正解決了問題
  6. 傳遞資訊（Disseminate Information）：將CAPA結果回饋給管理層和相關部門

藥品偏差調查 vs. 820.100 CAPA — 系統差距

面向 211.192 偏差調查 820.100 CAPA
觸發點 已發生的偏差/OOS 已發生 + 趨勢分析觸發的潛在問題
根因分析 要求，但未規範方法 要求系統化RCA，記錄方法論
預防面向 隱含，未強制 明確區分CA（已發生）與PA（潛在）
有效性確認 通常無正式要求 明確要求驗證CAPA有效
跨設施協調 較少涉及 明確要求跨廠協調

最常見的 FDA 483 觀察項目

對組合產品廠商而言，CAPA 相關的 FDA 483 最常見於：

  • CAPA 程序缺乏有效性確認步驟（Effectiveness Check）
  • 預防行動（PA）流於形式，與根本原因脫鉤
  • 多設施/合約商的 CAPA 資訊未有效整合
  • CAPA 資訊未定期回饋管理審查（820.20）

比喻：醫院感控 vs. 防火

藥品偏差調查像「醫院感控」——有病人感染了，找出病因，治療它。820.100 CAPA 則加上「防火」功能——不只治療感染，還要找出醫院中所有可能導致感染的潛在風險，在它發生前就修正。

這就是「糾正行動」與「預防行動」的本質差異。

CDMO 實務場景：跨廠 CAPA 協調

你的CDMO負責充填，器材零件由另一家供應商提供，最終組裝由第三家合約商進行。當市場上發現自動注射器漏液問題時，820.100 要求：

  • 你（CDMO）仍對 CAPA 的整體執行和有效性負責
  • 必須協調三家設施共同進行根因調查
  • CAPA 措施必須覆蓋所有相關的製造步驟
  • 建立機制確保合約商的 CAPA 資訊能流回你的 QMS

這意味著你的品質協議（Quality Agreement）中，必須明訂 CAPA 資訊分享和協調的義務。

思考練習

你的CDMO目前有「偏差調查SOP」和「OOS處理SOP」。要滿足 820.100 的要求，你需要新增或修改哪些程序？「Effectiveness Check」具體應如何設計？

5. Installation (820.170) and Servicing (820.200)

21 CFR 4.4(b)(1)(v) and (vi) require manufacturers to demonstrate compliance with installation requirements under 21 CFR 820.170 and servicing requirements under 21 CFR 820.200, respectively. These requirements are included in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1) to ensure manufacturers comply with them when applicable to a single-entity or co-packaged combination product.

However, the Agency anticipates that installed and serviced devices will rarely be constituent parts of such combination products. If they are constituent parts of a combination product, they are more likely to be separately manufactured and marketed as constituent parts of cross-labeled combination products. In such cases, each constituent part manufacturer would be subject to the CGMP requirements associated with the constituent part it manufactures. Accordingly, the device constituent part of such a cross-labeled combination product would be subject to installation and servicing requirements.

820.170 — Installation

820.170 applies when the combination product must be installed at the end-user site and proper installation is necessary to ensure safety and effectiveness. The manufacturer must establish and maintain adequate installation instructions and, where appropriate, inspection procedures. The manufacturer or its representative must perform installation checks and document them.

820.200 — Servicing

820.200 applies when servicing (i.e., maintenance, repair, or modification at the user site) is a specified requirement for the combination product. The manufacturer must establish and maintain instructions and procedures for performing and verifying that the servicing meets specified requirements. Service reports must be documented and analyzed to determine whether they should be addressed as complaints or signals.

820.170 & 820.200 — 多數無菌充填CDMO不適用

適用性判斷的關鍵問題

你需要問的第一個問題是：你的組合產品是否需要在終端使用者處安裝或維修保養？

  • 如果是單次使用的 PFS、自動注射器、吸入器：通常不適用 820.170 和 820.200。這類產品在使用後即丟棄，不需要安裝或服務。
  • 如果是需植入並定期維修的遞藥系統（如植入式胰島素幫浦）：適用。
  • 如果是需在醫院安裝的大型遞藥設備（如靜脈注射泵）：適用。

820.170 / 820.200 適用性矩陣

組合產品類型 820.170 安裝 820.200 服務
預填充注射器（PFS） 通常不適用 通常不適用
自動注射器（單次使用） 通常不適用 通常不適用

如何正式記錄「不適用」的判斷？

即使你判斷 820.170 和 820.200 不適用，FDA 仍期望你有書面的適用性評估記錄。

建議做法：在品質計畫（Quality Plan）中加入一節「CGMP 適用性評估」，針對每一項 4.4(b)(1) 規定，說明其是否適用及理由。

這讓稽查員能快速理解你的判斷邏輯，而不是讓他質疑你「忘記」了這兩項要求。

比喻：建築法規中的電梯條款

蓋一間兩層樓的小屋，建築法規中有電梯相關條款，但你評估後判定不需要安裝電梯——你仍需要在建築計畫書中寫明「本建築物層數低於法規強制安裝電梯的標準，故本條款不適用」。

820.170 / 820.200 的處理邏輯相同：明確記錄不適用的原因，而非沉默跳過。

思考練習

Context: The Device QS-Based Streamlined Approach / 背景：器材QS基礎簡化途徑

IV.B — Overview: Drug CGMP Provisions Under the Device QS-Based Streamlined Approach

This section provides a brief description of the provisions from the drug CGMPs with which single-entity and co-packaged combination product manufacturers must demonstrate compliance when using the device QS regulation-based streamlined approach established under 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2).

This discussion is not meant to be a comprehensive analysis, but rather to help manufacturers — particularly device manufacturers, who may be less familiar with drug CGMPs — understand the purpose and basic elements of the provisions of the drug CGMPs specified in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2), as well as to direct such manufacturers to additional guidance.

The specified provisions of the drug CGMPs include requirements for testing and other verification procedures for batches or lots, whether of drug components, drug containers and closures, drug constituent parts, or the whole combination product.

Combination product manufacturers should establish procedures defining a "batch" or "lot" in all phases of production and describe all batch and lot numbering systems used for incoming material, in-process material, and finished products. These procedures allow manufacturers to connect specific batches or lots of constituent parts, components, and in-process material to the specific batches or lots of the combination product for which they are used.

These procedures enable traceability of sampling and testing, packaging, and labeling activities, and can be important when assessing and responding to issues including complaints and adverse events. Master production and control records should be designed to enable this traceability.

解說：什麼是「器材QS簡化途徑」？

核心概念：兩種簡化途徑

21 CFR Part 4 為組合產品提供兩種簡化途徑：

  • 藥品CGMP基礎（4.4(b)(1)）：主要以21 CFR 210/211為框架，附加特定器材QS要求
  • 器材QS基礎（4.4(b)(2)）：主要以21 CFR Part 820為框架，附加8項特定藥品CGMP要求

本節（IV.B）專門解說第二種途徑：以器材QS為主體，附加8項藥品CGMP規定。

比喻：「基礎套餐 + 必選加點」

想像器材QS（21 CFR 820）是一套完整的品質餐廳套餐。使用這條路徑的廠商必須再「加點」8道菜——這8道菜都是純藥品製造獨有的要求，器材QS本身沒有。

這8道「必點菜」是：211.84、211.103、211.132、211.137、211.165、211.166、211.167、211.170。

CDMO場景：何時選擇器材QS途徑？

這條路徑較常見於：器材公司（如藥物塗層支架、預填充注射器製造商）新增藥品組成部分，已有完善的21 CFR 820體系，不想重建整套藥品GMP，因此選擇以820為主體，僅補齊8項藥品特有要求。

對於傳統無菌藥品CDMO而言，更常見的是反過來——以211為主體（IV.A途徑）。但隨著CDMO承接更多藥械組合產品，理解這8項要求變得日益重要。

批次定義的重要性

無論選哪條途徑，批次（Batch/Lot）的定義在組合產品中特別複雜——藥品組成部分、器材組成部分、最終組合產品各自可能有不同的批次編號。必須建立清晰的追溯程序，確保從原料到成品的批次連結。

IV.B.1 — 21 CFR 211.84: Component Testing and Approval/Rejection / 組件測試與核准/拒絕

1. Testing and Approval or Rejection of Drug Product Components, Containers, and Closures (21 CFR 211.84)

Drug product components, containers, and closures must be tested in accordance with 21 CFR 211.84. A drug component is any ingredient intended for use in the manufacture of a drug product, including those that may not appear in the drug product. A container closure system is the sum of packaging components that together contain and protect the drug product. This includes primary packaging components and also secondary packaging components if the latter are intended to provide additional protection to the drug product. Examples of packaging components are ampules, vials, screw caps, stoppers, and stopper overseals.

Combination product manufacturers do not need to demonstrate compliance with this provision for device constituent parts or materials used in the manufacture of a device constituent part, unless the device constituent part is also the drug container or closure or a part thereof.

21 CFR 211.84 details how to sample, test, examine, and accept or reject drug product components, containers, and closures. In lieu of such testing, 21 CFR 211.84 allows for some reliance on a supplier's report of analysis (COA), provided that certain identity testing is conducted and that the reliability of the supplier's analysis is established by the manufacturer through appropriate validation of the testing results at appropriate intervals.

Each lot of drug components, containers, and closures must be withheld from use until it has been sampled, tested, or examined, as appropriate, and released for use by the quality control unit. The samples collected for each lot must be representative of the entire lot. The number of containers to sample and the amount of material to be taken from each container must be based on appropriate criteria (e.g., component variability, confidence levels, degree of precision desired, past quality history of the supplier, and the quantity needed for analysis).

解說：211.84 — 器材廠的關鍵補強點

211.84 要求什麼？

  • 身份確認（Identity Testing）：每批藥品組件都必須進行身份測試，不得完全依賴供應商COA
  • 代表性取樣：取樣計畫必須有統計依據（樣本數、信心水準）
  • 放行前隔離：每批物料在QCU放行前必須隔離，不得投入生產
  • 容器密封系統：初級包裝（安瓿、瓶、塞、蓋）都納入管控

器材廠的典型落差

21 CFR 820.80（接收驗收）只要求廠商確保收到的產品符合規格——但沒有規定必須進行身份測試，也沒有COA可靠性驗證要求。

器材廠通常依賴供應商COA或功能性檢測，缺乏藥品規格的鑑別測試（identity test）。這是最常見的合規缺口之一。

比喻：器材廠收料 vs. 藥廠收料

器材廠做法：收到鋼製螺絲 → 目視檢查 + 尺寸量測 → OK，放行。

藥廠211.84做法：收到API → 身份鑑別測試（HPLC、IR）+ COA審查 + 樣本符合統計抽樣計畫 → QCU正式放行。

器材廠導入藥品組成部分後，必須把前者升級到後者的嚴謹度。

CDMO實務：容器密封系統的雙重身份

對於預填充注射器，注射器筒體既是器材（器材組成部分）也是藥品的容器密封系統（藥品主要包裝）。此時211.84的要求就適用於注射器筒體，包括：矽化量測、提取物測試、相容性研究等，遠超一般器材的入廠驗收範疇。

211.84 合規路徑比較

路徑A：完整自測
自行進行所有鑑別、純度、強度測試 → 符合211.84(d)

路徑B：COA依賴（有條件）
接受供應商COA + 自行進行身份鑑別測試 + 定期驗證COA可靠性 → 符合211.84(d)(3)

注意：路徑B仍必須做身份測試，不可完全略過。

思考練習

一家藥物塗層球囊（Drug-Coated Balloon）廠商，以器材QS簡化途徑運作。請判斷以下哪些物料需要符合211.84：

  1. 紫杉醇（藥品組成部分的活性成分）
  2. 球囊導管材料（PEBAX，僅用於器材組成部分）
  3. 最終產品的滅菌袋（保護性外包裝）
  4. 包含藥品的球囊本身（器材也是藥品容器）

答案：1和4必須符合211.84；2不需要；3視乎是否提供藥品的額外保護。

IV.B.2 — 21 CFR 211.103: Calculation of Yield / 產量計算

2. Calculation of Yield (21 CFR 211.103)

Actual yields and percentages of theoretical yield for the drug constituent part(s) of a combination product must be determined as described in 21 CFR 211.103. Excess or low yields may suggest problems in the production process, including equipment failure, that may affect product quality and that may not be discovered, or not be discovered as quickly, using sample-based release testing alone. Actual yield discrepancies outside of established and documented allowable variation from theoretical yield should be investigated.

Yield calculation requirements under 21 CFR 211.103 apply to the drug constituent parts of combination products. Data on the number of device constituent parts and components used and lost during the manufacture of a combination product may be necessary to ensure appropriate control of the manufacturing process, but yield calculation in accordance with 21 CFR 211.103 is not required for device constituent parts.

Yield determinations must be made at the conclusion of each appropriate phase of manufacturing, processing, packaging, and holding for the drug constituent part(s) and for the combination product as a whole. Accordingly, calculation of yield should be determined at each phase at which component, in-process material, or product loss may occur, including during the formulation of the drug prior to incorporation into the combination product, during incorporation into the combination product (e.g., filling or coating), and, possibly, during the packaging process.

These calculations must be performed by one person and independently verified by a second person, unless the yield is calculated by automated equipment in accordance with 21 CFR 211.68, in which case it must be independently verified by one person.

For each appropriate phase of the manufacturing process performed, the formula used and the data generated for the yield calculation for the phase should be documented. For manufacturers operating under a device QS regulation-based system, documentation of yield calculations may be incorporated into existing part 820 documentation (for example, as part of the device history record, 21 CFR 820.184).

If a third party is manufacturing the drug for the combination product, that manufacturer is responsible for complying with the calculation of yield requirement at the appropriate phases of the drug manufacturing process it performs, but the combination product manufacturer is responsible for ensuring that the supplier satisfies these requirements, as a part of the combination product manufacturer's purchasing controls for its product in accordance with 21 CFR 820.50.

解說：211.103 — 器材廠的全新概念

什麼是「理論產量」vs.「實際產量」？

理論產量（Theoretical Yield）：若製造過程完全沒有損失，預期可以生產多少單位（以藥品投入量計算）。

實際產量（Actual Yield）：實際得到的合格單位數量（或重量）。

產量百分比 = 實際產量 / 理論產量 × 100%

每個製造階段都必須計算，且必須有預設的可接受範圍（上下限）。超出範圍即啟動調查。

比喻：做麵包 vs. 做注射器

藥廠觀點：100 kg API → 理論上可製作 10,000 支注射劑 → 實際製出 9,850 支 → 產量98.5% → 記錄、確認在可接受範圍內（假設95-102%）。

器材廠傳統觀點：製作10,000個導管 → 9,850個通過測試 → 記錄良率（yield rate），但沒有「理論產量」概念，也沒有強制調查觸發點。

這兩種觀點都關心損失，但藥品GMP的產量計算有更嚴格的文件與調查要求。

重要：為什麼產量可以發現問題？

產量偏低可能意味著：設備故障（填充量不足）、原料密度異常、操作失誤、未記錄的廢棄物。這些問題在樣本放行測試中可能完全看不出來（因為測試的是好的產品），但產量計算可以捕捉到整體批次的異常。

CDMO實務：預填充注射器產量計算

製造預填充注射器（PFS）時，產量計算的關鍵節點包括：

  • 配液階段：投入API量 → 配製藥液量（% 理論值）
  • 過濾階段：藥液過濾後回收率（過濾器保留量需記錄）
  • 灌裝階段：填充藥液總量 vs. 灌裝後實際產品數 × 標示量
  • 組裝/包裝階段：成品通過率

每個節點都需有預定的可接受範圍，超出需調查。

文件整合建議：DHR中的產量計算

器材廠已有DHR（Device History Record，21 CFR 820.184）。FDA建議將藥品的產量計算記錄整合到DHR中，不需要建立全新的文件體系——但DHR必須擴展以容納：

  • 各製造階段的理論產量計算公式
  • 實際產量記錄（雙人複核簽名）
  • 可接受範圍的預定標準
  • 超出範圍的調查記錄連結
IV.B.3 — 21 CFR 211.132: Tamper-Evident Packaging (OTC Only) / 防篡改包裝（僅限OTC）

3. Tamper-Evident Packaging Requirements for Over-the-Counter Human Drug Products (21 CFR 211.132)

Manufacturers of OTC combination products must comply with the requirements of 21 CFR 211.132. The required controls include tamper-evident packaging and labeling alerting customers to these protective features of the product's packaging. These controls are important to help improve the security of OTC combination product packaging and help ensure the safety and effectiveness of OTC combination products.

For single-entity combination products, tamper-evident packaging requirements apply to the packaging for the combination product as a whole. For co-packaged combination products, these requirements can be met through appropriate packaging of the drug constituent part(s) within the larger co-package so long as such an approach is otherwise permissible under the packaging and labeling requirements applicable to that combination product.

Certain combination products may be exempt from OTC tamper-evident packaging requirements (e.g., toothpaste co-packaged with a toothbrush) or from bearing a statement of tamper-evident features on the package (e.g., aerosol saline nasal spray).

An exemption from tamper-evident packaging and labeling requirements for an OTC combination product may be requested in accordance with 21 CFR 211.132(d). If the manufacturer makes changes to packaging and labeling of an approved OTC product to comply with the requirements of 21 CFR 211.132, it must notify the lead center for the combination product before distributing it.

解說：211.132 — 只適用OTC的規定

關鍵：處方產品不適用！

211.132的防篡改包裝要求只適用於OTC（非處方）組合產品。絕大多數無菌注射型組合產品（預填充注射器、藥物塗層支架）都是處方產品（Rx），不受此條款約束。

這是8項附加要求中條件限制最明確的一項——許多器材廠可能完全不適用。

OTC組合產品：典型例子

  • 牙膏 + 牙刷（共同包裝）— 可能有豁免
  • OTC鼻噴劑 + 沖洗瓶
  • OTC局部藥膏 + 敷料
  • 戒煙貼片（單一實體組合產品）

需要確認產品的OTC狀態，以及是否符合任何豁免條件。

211.132 合規要求概覽

必要措施：

  • 採用防篡改包裝機制（如封膜、縮套、安全貼）
  • 外包裝標籤必須標示防篡改特徵說明
  • 若更改已核准OTC產品的包裝，須在配送前通知主責中心

可能的豁免（須向FDA申請）：

  • 特定形式（如氣霧劑）— 可豁免標示說明
  • 特定產品類別 — 可申請豁免包裝要求

比喻：安全封條的邏輯

OTC產品在開架零售，任何人都能拿起來翻看，潛在惡意篡改的機會更高。防篡改包裝的目的是讓消費者能確認「這個產品從出廠到現在沒有被打開過」。處方藥通常由藥師控管，配送鏈受到更多管控，因此這項要求的迫切性不同。

IV.B.4 — 21 CFR 211.137: Expiration Dating / 效期標示

4. Expiration Dating (21 CFR 211.137)

21 CFR 211.137 helps ensure that drug products (drug constituent parts in the case of combination products) meet applicable standards of identity, strength, quality, and purity at the time of use, by requiring that the product labeling bear an expiration date. This date must take into account any storage conditions stated in the labeling and be based on appropriate stability testing as described in 21 CFR 211.166 (see IV.B.6). The expiration dating also should take into account any other applicable shelf-life considerations (e.g., for a product that is to be provided sterile, the length of time that its packaging material can be ensured to retain its integrity and, thereby, maintain a sterile barrier).

When expiration dating is required, the constituent parts of combination products may have individual expiration dates or an expiration date for the entire combination product may be appropriate. Generally, when constituent parts of a co-packaged combination product can be used independently, expiration dating should be addressed separately for each constituent part.

If a single expiration date is listed for a co-packaged combination product, this date should be the earliest expiration date/shortest shelf-life for any constituent part. The expiration date for a combination product may be shorter than the expiration date or shelf life for its constituent part(s) if marketed independently. Reasons for a shorter expiration period could include interactions between the constituent parts when combined, the effects of additional manufacturing steps, or other differences arising from the combination of the constituent parts.

解說：211.137 — 器材廠的核心轉變

器材QS vs. 藥品GMP：效期要求的差距

21 CFR Part 820（器材QS）並沒有等同於211.137的效期標示要求。器材可以有「使用壽命（Shelf Life）」的設計驗證，但這與藥品的效期標示（必須基於穩定性數據、標示在標籤上）是不同概念。

引入藥品組成部分後，效期標示變成強制義務，且必須有穩定性數據支持。

「最短效期」原則

組合產品如果標示單一效期，必須採用任何組成部分中最短的效期。這意味著：

  • 器材組成部分效期：5年
  • 藥品組成部分效期：2年（基於穩定性數據）
  • 組合產品效期：2年（取最短）

這可能對商業策略有重大影響——藥品穩定性往往成為組合產品商業壽命的決定因素。

CDMO實務：藥物塗層支架的效期挑戰

藥物塗層支架（Drug-Eluting Stent）的效期涉及：

  • 藥品穩定性：藥物（如依維莫司）在支架塗層上的降解速率
  • 器材力學性能：支架金屬骨架的耐久性（通常更長）
  • 包裝完整性：滅菌屏障維持時間
  • 組合互動：器材-藥品界面的相容性隨時間變化

最終效期由最短的因素決定，必須有穩定性研究支持。

比喻：組合便當的保存期限

一個包含「飯糰（藥品，1天）」和「保鮮袋（器材，1年）」的組合包裝，整體保存期限是1天——受最短壽命的組件決定。就算袋子可以用1年，便當不能賣超過1天。

思考練習

一個co-packaged組合產品包含：(A) 藥物玻璃瓶（效期24個月）和 (B) 一次性給藥器械。若兩者可以獨立使用，效期應如何標示？若兩者不能獨立使用，效期又應如何決定？

答案：若可獨立使用，分別標示各自效期；若不可獨立使用（single-entity），以最短效期為準（24個月），同時需考慮組合後的交互作用。

IV.B.5 — 21 CFR 211.165: Testing and Release for Distribution / 測試與放行

5. Testing and Release for Distribution (21 CFR 211.165)

Testing and release for distribution are critical in drug product manufacture and quality control. 21 CFR 211.165 requires that an appropriate laboratory determination of satisfactory conformance to final specifications (including the identity and strength of each active ingredient) is made for each batch of drug product prior to release. 21 CFR 211.165 also requires appropriate laboratory testing, as necessary, of each batch of a drug product required to be free of objectionable microorganisms. In addition, sampling and testing plans must be described by written procedures.

Accordingly, manufacturers must test each batch of their combination product to determine satisfactory conformance to final written specifications for the drug constituent part. For single-entity combination products, laboratory testing must be performed on every batch of the combination product; for co-packaged combination products, laboratory testing must be performed on every batch of the drug constituent part(s). This testing ensures that all batches meet appropriate specifications for their approval and release. A detailed listing of all the tests performed and the acceptance criteria used should be maintained and be available for inspection.

In some cases, for single-entity combination products, in lieu of using actual units from a finished combination product batch for testing, manufacturers may wish to use samples that are not finished combination products (but that are representative of the finished combination product with respect to the characteristics and attributes being tested). The Agency does not intend to object to such an approach where the manufacturer can establish, including where appropriate through bridging studies and other quantitative means, that any differences in the manufacturing process for the samples and the finished combination product do not affect the drug constituent part. Such approaches should be supported by appropriate justification and data and discussed with the lead Center, and OCP as needed.

If one facility is manufacturing a drug product as a constituent part to be supplied to another facility for inclusion in a co-packaged combination product, appropriate and adequate laboratory testing should be conducted by the drug product manufacturing facility prior to release in accordance with 21 CFR 211.165. The combination product manufacturer is responsible for ensuring through purchasing controls in accordance with 21 CFR 820.50 that the drug constituent part conforms to specified requirements.

解說：211.165 — 「放行測試」的藥品邏輯

211.165 要求的放行測試核心

  • 鑑別（Identity）：確認活性成分是正確的
  • 強度（Strength）：確認活性成分含量符合規格
  • 純度（Purity）：確認雜質在可接受範圍
  • 品質（Quality）：其他理化及微生物特性
  • 每批都必須測試：不能跳批
  • 書面程序：取樣計畫與測試方法必須有SOP

器材廠的最大落差：功能測試 vs. 藥品放行測試

器材廠通常有完善的功能性放行測試（爆裂壓力、拉伸強度、密封完整性等）。但藥品放行測試完全不同——重點是活性成分的化學分析。

對以820為主體的廠商而言，211.165是最需要新建能力的一項要求，因為它通常需要：分析化學儀器（HPLC等）、有資格的化學分析人員、已驗證的分析方法。

CDMO實務：代理測試樣品的可接受性

FDA明確允許，在某些情況下，可以使用「非完整組合產品」的代表性樣品進行藥品放行測試，前提是：

  • 必須有橋接研究（Bridging Studies）證明代理樣品與最終產品在測試屬性上等效
  • 製造過程的差異不影響藥品組成部分
  • 需獲得主責中心（Lead Center）和OCP的認可

例如：預填充注射器廠商可能以充填相同藥液的測試瓶進行放行測試，前提是有數據支持等效性。

放行測試責任矩陣

產品類型 由誰測試 測試對象
Single-entity 組合產品製造商 每批最終組合產品
Co-packaged（自製藥品） 組合產品製造商 每批藥品組成部分
Co-packaged（外購藥品） 藥品製造商（放行前）+ 組合產品製造商（採購控制） 藥品組成部分 + 持續符合規格
IV.B.6 — 21 CFR 211.166: Stability Testing / 穩定性測試

6. Stability Testing (21 CFR 211.166)

21 CFR 211.166 requires a testing program designed to assess the stability characteristics of drug products. Furthermore, 21 CFR 211.166 sets forth required elements of the testing program, including elements concerning sample size, storage conditions for samples retained for testing, and other elements related to testing methodology and frequency.

Stability testing is performed to determine appropriate storage conditions and expiration dates (see IV.B.4). Among other considerations, this testing must enable evaluation of any effects on the stability of the drug due to storage in its marketed container closure system, which may be a device constituent part (or component of a device constituent part). For a single-entity combination product, testing must be performed on the drug constituent part as incorporated into the finished combination product.

The Agency does not intend to object to the use of bracketing and matrix approaches for stability studies where the approach has been adequately justified and, where applicable, reviewed by FDA. The Agency also does not intend to object to leveraging stability data for an already marketed combination product during new product development, with adequate justification and, where applicable, review by FDA.

The need for new stability studies should be evaluated when there are changes to any constituent part of a combination product. For example, changes to the manufacturing process, including changes based on the size or shape of the device constituent part that holds the drug or that is coated with the drug, or changes in material of construction of such a device constituent part, may impact drug stability.

If a combination product manufacturer purchases a drug product from another manufacturer for inclusion in its co-packaged combination product, the combination product manufacturer is responsible for ensuring the stability of the drug product as marketed in the co-packaged product through appropriate mechanisms, such as by implementing purchasing controls to ensure the adequacy of the drug product manufacturer's stability testing or by conducting additional stability testing (see 21 CFR 820.50).

If a drug constituent part of a co-packaged combination product has an expiration date, the combination product manufacturer may be able to rely on that labeled expiration date in lieu of conducting new stability studies, if it can be documented that any additional manufacturing operations conducted on the co-packaged product would not be expected to impact the constituent part, including its container-closure system.

解說：211.166 — 穩定性是組合產品的核心科學

211.166 穩定性計畫的必要元素

  • 書面穩定性計畫：每個產品都必須有
  • 樣本數量：足以在各測試時間點進行完整測試
  • 儲存條件：與標籤聲稱的條件一致
  • 測試頻率：各時間點（如0、3、6、12、24個月）
  • 進行中穩定性（On-going Stability）：持續監測上市批次
  • 最終容器測試：單一實體組合產品必須在最終組合狀態下測試

器材變更 = 穩定性重新評估

這是最常被忽視的要求之一。器材組成部分的任何變更（材質、尺寸、形狀、製造工藝）都可能影響藥品穩定性，需要重新評估是否需要新的穩定性研究。例如：

  • 藥物塗層球囊改變球囊直徑 → 塗層面積/厚度改變 → 藥品穩定性可能受影響
  • DES的聚合物材料更換 → 藥物釋放動力學改變 → 需新穩定性數據
  • 預填充注射器橡膠塞供應商更換 → 相容性/提取物改變 → 需評估穩定性影響

CDMO實務：穩定性研究策略

括弧法（Bracketing）：如果產品家族中最小和最大規格的穩定性可以代表中間規格，可以只測試端點規格，不需全部規格都做完整穩定性研究。

矩陣法（Matrixing）：減少測試時間點的策略設計，在維持統計力的前提下降低測試負擔。

借用已上市產品數據：新產品若是已上市組合產品的微小改動（如改變導管長度，但藥品塗層不變），可能可以借用現有穩定性數據，加速開發時程。

比喻：藥品穩定性是組合產品的「限速步驟」

就像汽車的發動機（藥品）和車身（器材）——就算車身設計可以用50年，發動機的使用壽命才決定整台車多久需要「過期」。器材組成部分的壽命通常遠超藥品組成部分的穩定性，因此藥品穩定性往往是組合產品效期的決定因素。

是否需要新穩定性研究：決策流程

情境1：全新組合產品 → 必須進行完整穩定性研究

情境2：現有產品的輕微改動（低風險） → 可能可採用縮短的穩定性研究，或借用矩陣法，需聯繫Lead Center

情境3：Co-packaged，藥品已有標示效期 → 若組合步驟不影響藥品，可能可依賴現有效期，但需文件化證明

情境4：任何可能影響藥品的變更 → 必須重新評估，可能需要新研究

IV.B.7 — 21 CFR 211.167: Special Testing Requirements / 特殊測試要求

7. Special Testing Requirements (21 CFR 211.167)

21 CFR 211.167 establishes requirements for batch testing applicable to drug products having particular characteristics. Specifically:

  • 21 CFR 211.167(a): Requires appropriate laboratory testing of drug products purporting to be sterile and/or pyrogen-free to determine conformance with such requirements.
  • 21 CFR 211.167(b): Requires appropriate testing of ophthalmic ointments to determine conformance to specifications regarding the presence of foreign particles and harsh or abrasive substances.
  • 21 CFR 211.167(c): Requires appropriate laboratory testing of controlled-release dosage forms to determine conformance to the specifications for the rate of release of each active ingredient.

A special testing requirement specified in 21 CFR 211.167 applies to a combination product only if the combination product or a drug constituent part of it falls into one or more of the three categories described above. Special testing may be required for the drug constituent part, or the combination product as a whole, depending on the product.

With respect to 21 CFR 211.167(a), batch testing requirements would apply both to the drug constituent part and to the finished combination product for a single-entity combination product (such as a prefilled syringe) to ensure the combination product is sterile and pyrogen-free when distributed. In contrast, if a vial of a vaccine were co-packaged with an empty syringe, the requirements in 21 CFR 211.167(a) would apply only to the vial of vaccine.

For purposes of conducting pyrogen and endotoxin testing, it may be permissible in some circumstances to define a "batch" based on the finished drug product rather than the finished combination product. For example, it may be acceptable to define a batch as a subcomponent of the combination product that incorporates the drug constituent part. However, the viability of such an approach would depend on many factors including: appropriate upstream controls to reduce/limit pyrogens and endotoxins; the chemical or materials-mediated pyrogenicity of the constituent parts; and the risks of pyrogen and endotoxin introduction to the combination product by manufacturing operations that occur after the step at which the batch is defined.

21 CFR 211.167(c) would apply both to a controlled-release drug constituent part of a co-packaged combination product and also to a controlled release single-entity combination product, to confirm rate of release of the active ingredient. For example, a transdermal drug patch or drug-eluting disc or stent would be subject to 21 CFR 211.167(c).

As with drugs and devices, parametric release may be acceptable for some combination products when supported by appropriate data. Such approaches should be discussed with the lead Center, or OCP as needed, prior to implementation and included in premarket submissions.

解說：211.167 — 無菌組合產品的關鍵測試

211.167三個子條款適用範圍

條款 適用情況 常見組合產品例子
211.167(a) 聲稱無菌和/或無熱原 預填充注射器、藥物塗層支架、眼科製劑
211.167(b) 眼用軟膏 含眼用軟膏的組合包裝
211.167(c) 控釋劑型 藥物塗層支架、經皮貼片、藥物洗脫盤

無菌組合產品：雙重測試要求

對於單一實體無菌組合產品（如預填充注射器）：

  • 藥品組成部分（藥液）必須通過無菌/熱原測試
  • 最終組合產品整體也必須通過無菌/熱原測試

對於共同包裝產品（如裝滿藥液的小瓶 + 空注射器）：只需測試含藥液的小瓶，空注射器的無菌性由其他法規要求管控（設計控制、製程控制）。

內毒素測試的批次定義彈性

FDA在此提供了重要的操作靈活性：對於熱原/內毒素測試，可以將「批次」定義為含藥液的中間組件（而非最終組合產品），前提是：

  • 有充分的上游控制措施減少熱原
  • 已評估器材材質引起熱原的風險
  • 在「批次定義點」之後的製造步驟不會引入新的熱原風險

這在實務上非常重要：測試整個組合產品（如DES）可能技術上很困難，允許在更早的製造步驟定義批次進行測試，大大簡化了操作。

CDMO實務：藥物洗脫支架的溶出試驗

依維莫司洗脫支架（Everolimus-Eluting Stent）必須符合211.167(c)——每批都需進行藥物釋放速率測試（In Vitro Drug Release / IVDR），確認藥物依照設計的動力學釋放。

這比一般藥品的溶出試驗更複雜，因為：支架幾何形狀影響釋放；需要模擬生理條件的測試介質；測試本身可能是破壞性的（無法保留支架繼續使用）。這些都需要納入放行測試計畫中。

比喻：參數放行的保險邏輯

傳統滅菌放行：每批都做無菌測試（費時2週）→ 才放行。
參數放行：滅菌製程經過完整驗證，每批監控所有製程參數（溫度、時間、F₀值），若參數全部合格，可省略無菌測試直接放行。

組合產品同樣可申請參數放行，但需要事前與FDA溝通，並納入上市前申請。

思考練習

一家CDMO生產以下產品，請判斷各適用哪個211.167子條款：

  1. 預填充胰島素注射器（PFS）
  2. 藥物洗脫腦部植入物（含緩釋化療藥）
  3. OTC眼用抗生素軟膏 + 眼罩（co-packaged）
  4. 乾粉吸入器（含抗生素粉末）

答案：1→(a)；2→(a)+(c)（無菌且控釋）；3→(a)+(b)；4→(a)（注意：DPI若非聲稱無菌則(a)可能不適用）。

IV.B.8 — 21 CFR 211.170: Reserve Samples / 留樣

8. Reserve Samples (21 CFR 211.170)

Reserve samples are needed to help ensure the safety and effectiveness of combination products in distribution. They are used, for example, to address certain product complaints, evaluate stability concerns, and assess the causes of adverse events. The reserve sampling requirements of 21 CFR 211.170 must be met for the drug constituent parts of combination products.

Under 21 CFR 211.170, reserve samples must be kept that are representative of both:

  • each lot of the active ingredient (21 CFR 211.170(a)), and
  • each lot or batch of the drug product (21 CFR 211.170(b)).

All reserve samples must consist of at least twice the quantity necessary to perform all required tests, except those for sterility and pyrogenicity. Furthermore, reserve samples of drug products must be retained and stored under conditions consistent with product labeling and stored in the same immediate container-closure system in which the drug product is marketed or in one that has essentially the same characteristics.

For co-packaged combination products, the requirement to keep reserve samples of drug products can be met by maintaining samples of the drug constituent part in its immediate container-closure system, without the need to retain any samples of the device or portions of it. For single-entity combination products, the drug product reserve samples should be of the drug constituent part in or upon the device constituent part or components thereof that come into contact with the drug product as packaged for distribution.

Drug product reserve samples generally must be maintained for 1 year after the expiration date for the drug product; different time periods apply to certain radioactive and OTC products. Active ingredient samples generally must be kept for 1 year after the expiration date for the last lot of the combination product containing the active ingredient.

Manufacturers must examine drug product samples for deterioration, investigate evidence of deterioration, and record and maintain the results of such examination.

Combination product manufacturers may choose to have their reserve samples of the API, drug/biologic constituent part, or finished combination product at a location other than the combination product manufacturing facility. In these cases, the combination product manufacturer should have appropriate controls in place to ensure that requirements under 21 CFR 211.170 continue to be met.

Manufacturers considering use of novel approaches to comply with reserve sampling requirements (such as bracketing and matrixing, or use of surrogate samples for some testing) are advised to discuss them with the lead center for their combination product, or OCP, as needed.

解說：211.170 — 器材廠沒有等效要求的全新義務

器材QS沒有留樣要求

21 CFR Part 820中完全沒有等同於211.170的留樣規定。器材製造商通常保留設計驗證樣品、工程樣品，但沒有「每批保留2倍測試量、在效期後再保留1年」的法規義務。

這是器材廠導入藥品組成部分後最需要建立的新體系之一。

留樣要求關鍵數字

藥品（製劑） 2× 所有必要測試所需量（無菌/熱原測試除外） 效期後1年（OTC/放射性藥品另有規定）

Single-Entity vs. Co-packaged：留樣範圍不同

Co-packaged（如小瓶 + 注射器）：只需保留藥品組成部分（小瓶），不需保留器材組成部分（注射器），但需有注射器可用以進行留樣測試時的使用。

Single-entity（如預填充注射器、DES）：必須保留整個組合產品，因為器材（注射器筒體/支架）就是藥品的容器/載體，無法分離留樣。

CDMO實務：留樣管理的實際考量

儲存空間：預填充注射器批量數萬支，2倍測試量仍是相當大的冷藏空間需求，且需維持到效期後1年（共可能3-4年以上）。

異地儲存：FDA允許留樣存放在非製造廠的地點（如合約倉庫、供應商設施），但必須有書面協議確保：適當儲存條件、製造商可隨時取用、有足夠測試能力、批次數量充足。

定期目視檢查：藥品留樣必須至少每年目視檢查一次，評估外觀劣化跡象，並記錄結果。

比喻：留樣是產品上市後的「證人」

想像每批產品出貨後，保留部分樣品在倉庫「作證」——如果未來發生投訴（如藥效不足、不良反應），可以取出留樣重新測試，確認問題是產品本身的問題還是儲存/運輸問題，或確認問題不存在（防止不合理投訴）。

沒有留樣，就沒有辦法進行這種事後調查。這就是為什麼FDA要求保留到效期後再加1年——確保整個產品生命週期內都有可調查的樣品。

思考練習

一批DES（藥物洗脫支架，效期18個月）的留樣計畫應包含什麼？保留到什麼時候？可以只留支架上的藥品部分嗎？

答案：必須保留完整支架（single-entity），數量為所有必要測試（如IVDR、無菌測試）所需量的2倍（無菌/熱原測試除外）。保留期限：18個月效期 + 1年 = 效期後1年（即30個月後才可處置）。不可只留藥品部分，因為支架是藥品的容器/載體。

IV.C — Combination Products Including Biological Products and HCT/Ps / 含生物製劑與HCT/P的組合產品

C. Combination Products That Include Biological Products and HCT/Ps

In addition to complying with drug CGMP and device QS regulation requirements as applicable in accordance with part 4, a manufacturer of a combination product that contains a biological product or an HCT/P must comply with the requirements that would apply to the biological product or HCT/P if it were not part of a combination product.

1. Complying with CGMP Requirements for Biological Products

It is important to remember that a biological product regulated under section 351 of the PHS Act is also, by definition, a drug or a device. Accordingly, in addition to the requirements in parts 600 through 680, a biological product is always either subject to the drug CGMPs or subject to the device QS regulation, regardless of whether the biological product is a constituent part of a combination product.

The CGMP requirements for biological products in parts 600 through 680 augment the drug CGMPs and device QS regulation to ensure adequate consideration of issues for biological products. The CGMP requirements for biological products in parts 600 through 680 address the particular challenges biological products pose, including challenges arising from their relative complexity. For biological products, consistency of manufacturing procedures can, in fact, be a primary means by which to ensure the safety, purity, and potency of the product.

The CGMP requirements for biological products in parts 600 through 680 must be satisfied if a combination product includes a biological product constituent part. However, many requirements in parts 600 through 680 are applicable only to certain types of biological products. For example, while part 600 facially addresses biological products in general, only products manufactured using a spore-forming microorganism would be subject to 21 CFR 600.11(e)(3). Similarly, only blood and blood components are subject to the CGMP requirements for such products under part 606.

In short, the specific requirements in parts 600 through 680 that must be met will depend on the type of biological product it includes. The Agency welcomes the opportunity to discuss these requirements with manufacturers. If manufacturers have questions regarding these requirements, they may contact the lead center for the combination product or OCP, as needed, for assistance.

2. Complying with CGMPs for HCT/Ps

An HCT/P (human cell, tissue, and cellular and tissue-based product) that is not regulated solely under section 361 of the PHS Act and Part 1271 is also regulated as a drug, device, or biological product. The drug CGMPs, device QS regulation, and the requirements in parts 600 through 680 may apply to an HCT/P depending on whether the product is regulated as a drug, device, or biological product.

CGMPs and the CGTPs (Current Good Tissue Practice) for HCT/Ps supplement one another and do not supersede each other unless the regulation specifically provides otherwise. In the event that a regulation in part 1271 is in conflict with a requirement in parts 210, 211, 600 through 680, or 820, the regulations more specifically applicable to the product in question will supersede the more general.

The CGTPs, including donor eligibility requirements for the manufacture of HCT/Ps under part 1271, are designed to prevent the introduction, transmission, and spread of communicable diseases, and thereby are essential to protecting the public health. Accordingly, the current CGTPs apply to combination products that include an HCT/P. However, requirements under some sections of part 1271 overlap with the requirements under the drug CGMPs and the device QS regulation. These overlaps are addressed in part 1271 and in the Guidance for Industry on Current Good Tissue Practice (CGTP).

解說：生物製劑與HCT/P組合產品的額外義務

關鍵原則：「疊加」而非「替代」

21 CFR Part 4的簡化途徑（無論是藥品CGMP基礎或器材QS基礎）不能取代生物製劑CGMP（21 CFR 600-680）或HCT/P的CGTP（21 CFR 1271）。

包含生物製劑的組合產品 = Part 4簡化途徑 + 21 CFR 600-680

包含HCT/P的組合產品 = Part 4簡化途徑 + 21 CFR 1271（+可能的600-680）

什麼是「生物製劑」？（21 CFR Part 600-680）

依據PHS Act Section 351，生物製劑包括：

  • 血液及血液成分製品（Part 606）
  • 疫苗（Part 610+特定要求）
  • 治療用蛋白質（如單株抗體、酵素替代療法）
  • 細胞與基因治療產品
  • 過敏原製品

每類生物製劑都有其特定的Part 600-680要求，並非所有條款適用於所有生物製劑。

什麼是HCT/P？（21 CFR Part 1271）

HCT/P = Human Cells, Tissues, and Cellular and Tissue-Based Products（人類細胞、組織及細胞/組織基產品）

僅受Section 361管控的HCT/P（最低限度操作、同質性使用等）：只需遵守Part 1271。

也被監管為藥品/器材/生物製劑的HCT/P：需同時遵守Part 1271（CGTP）+ Part 4的適用途徑 + 可能的600-680。

CDMO實務：生物製劑組合產品的典型例子

  • 預填充單抗注射器（如貝伐珠單抗PFS）：Part 4 + Part 211 + Part 600-680（生物製劑CGMP）
  • CAR-T細胞療法 + 輸液器材：Part 4 + Part 211（或820）+ Part 600-680 + Part 1271
  • 同種異體骨移植 + 固定器材：Part 4 + Part 820 + Part 1271（如為Section 351產品）
  • 血小板濃縮液 + 傷口護理器材：Part 4 + Part 606 + Part 1271

生物製劑為何需要額外CGMP？

FDA明確指出，對於生物製劑，製程一致性（Consistency of Manufacturing）本身就是保障安全性、純度和效力的核心手段。生物製劑的複雜性意味著：

  • 終產品測試無法完全表徵產品品質（批次間差異難以由最終測試捕捉）
  • 必須確保每批製造過程高度一致，才能保障患者安全
  • Part 600-680涵蓋了這些生物製劑特有的挑戰（如病毒清除、效力測試、批次放行特殊要求）

CGTP（組織規範）與CGMP的交互關係

Part 1271（CGTP）與Part 210/211或Part 820（CGMP）之間：

  • 互補而非取代：兩者共同存在，各有其適用範疇
  • 特殊規定優先：若有衝突，針對特定產品更具體的法規優先適用
  • 捐贈者資格（Donor Eligibility）：這是CGTP獨有的要求，CGMP中沒有等效規定，目的是防止傳染性疾病傳播

比喻：法規層次像「洋蔥」

生物製劑組合產品的法規要求像一個多層洋蔥：

  • 最外層：Part 4（組合產品框架）
  • 中間層：Part 211 或 Part 820（藥品CGMP或器材QS）
  • 內層：Part 600-680（生物製劑CGMP）
  • 最核心（若有HCT/P）：Part 1271（CGTP）

每一層都不能省略，且往往相互疊加。複雜程度遠高於單純的藥品或器材。

思考練習

一個CAR-T細胞療法（基因改造T細胞）被裝入一次性輸注器材中作為組合產品，以器材QS簡化途徑運作。請列出所有必須遵守的主要法規框架。

答案：(1) 21 CFR Part 820（器材QS，主體）+ (2) 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2)附加的8項藥品CGMP規定 + (3) 21 CFR 600-680（生物製劑CGMP，因為CAR-T是生物製劑）+ (4) 21 CFR 1271（CGTP，因為CAR-T是HCT/P，且非僅受Section 361管控）。這是最複雜的組合產品法規場景之一。

Section Summary / 本節總結

Key Takeaways — IV.B and IV.C

Section IV.B describes the 8 drug CGMP provisions that must be added to the device QS regulation-based streamlined approach. These provisions address areas where the device QS regulation does not have equivalent requirements: incoming component testing with identity verification, pharmaceutical yield calculation, expiration dating based on stability data, drug product release testing, a formal stability program, special testing for sterile/controlled-release products, and reserve sample retention. Tamper-evident packaging is an additional requirement for OTC products only.

Section IV.C establishes that the streamlined approach under Part 4 does not replace the biological product CGMPs (Parts 600-680) or HCT/P current good tissue practices (Part 1271). Manufacturers of combination products containing biological product or HCT/P constituent parts must comply with all applicable requirements across Part 4, the applicable base regulation (211 or 820), and the biologics/tissue-specific regulations. The specific requirements vary depending on the type of biological product or HCT/P involved, and manufacturers are encouraged to contact the lead center or OCP for product-specific guidance.

Section V.A — 預填充注射器 (Prefilled Syringe)

V.A.1 — Prefilled Syringe: Scenario Description

A drug manufacturer (Manufacturer A) plans to sell a drug in a prefilled syringe presentation. Manufacturer A already has marketing approval for the drug product and intends to apply for marketing approval for the prefilled syringe presentation. No changes to the drug formulation will be made.

Manufacturer A will buy off-the-shelf syringe components from a supplier (Manufacturer B) who also manufactures finished syringes using the same components. Manufacturer A will assemble the syringe components, prefill the syringe at a facility it operates, and then package, label, and distribute the prefilled syringe from this facility.

Manufacturer A's facility has an existing drug CGMP operating system. As the prefilled syringe is a single-entity combination product under 21 CFR 3.2(e)(1), Manufacturer A must demonstrate compliance with both the drug CGMPs and device QS regulation.

To do so, Manufacturer A opts to establish a CGMP operating system using the drug CGMP-based streamlined approach in accordance with 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1). This example focuses on considerations for demonstrating compliance with the provisions from the device QS regulation specified in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(1).

V.A.2(a) — 820.20: Management Responsibility

While section 501 of the FD&C Act (21 U.S.C. 351) and 21 CFR 211.22, 211.25, and 211.180 establish requirements relevant to management responsibility, Manufacturer A must ensure that its CGMP operating system demonstrates compliance with the specific requirements in 21 CFR 820.20.

Manufacturer A should, for example, review its existing CGMP operating system to assess whether changes are needed to comply with this provision. As part of this review, management with executive responsibility for Manufacturer A should review the facility's quality policy and develop and implement appropriate oversight procedures, if such procedures are not already in place, to ensure that both the policy and oversight are adequate.

The oversight procedures should include clear delineation of the personnel to whom management with executive responsibility is delegating responsibility for implementing the quality policy (including translation into methods and procedures) and the CGMP operating system for this product at the facility.

  • 指定管理代表（Management Representative）

V.A.2(b) — 820.30: Design Controls

Manufacturer A is responsible for establishing and maintaining procedures for design control activities for the combination product. In this scenario, Manufacturer A is the owner and is manufacturing the prefilled syringe. Accordingly, Manufacturer A is responsible for the design control activities for the syringe as part of the combination product, as well as having overall responsibility for the combination product.

However, Manufacturer A is buying the syringe components from Manufacturer B, another manufacturer, which uses the same components to manufacture finished syringes. As a result, Manufacturer A may be able to leverage syringe-specific design control documentation from the design controls Manufacturer B uses for its finished syringes.

Because no changes are to be made to the drug other than being put into the syringe, the drug comes into the design control process as an input to the design of the syringe to ensure that the syringe's design reflects adequate consideration of the drug's characteristics.

i. Design Inputs and Outputs

Design Input / User Needs Design Output
Required minimum/maximum dose delivery for drug Drawing/specification for syringe dimensions, markings, etc.
Drug viscosity and desired/required delivery rate Drawing/specification for needle bore, glide force, etc.
Expected use condition (e.g., expected user experience/education level) Content and reading level for the prefilled syringe's labeling
Maximum and minimum allowable temperature for prefilled syringe Packaging/labeling specifications for the prefilled syringe
No degradation of drug or syringe over the expected shelf-life as a result of contact with one another Specifications for drug-contacting syringe materials
Expected shipping method and appropriate storage conditions Design drawings/specifications for primary and secondary packaging, labeling for acceptable storage conditions
Drug delivery method (e.g., needle or needleless delivery) Drawing/specification for needle and/or other associated syringe components

中文解析 — 820.30 設計控制

PFS 設計控制的核心邏輯

對藥廠（Manufacturer A）而言，「設計」不是藥物配方（已核准），而是注射器如何與該藥物相容並安全遞送。

藥物 = 設計輸入（Input）→ 注射器設計必須配合藥物特性

比喻 — 槓桿供應商設計資料

廠商 B（注射器製造商）已有注射器的設計控制文件（DHF）。廠商 A 可以槓桿（leverage）這些資料，但需額外評估：

  • 加了該藥物後，有沒有新的設計問題？
  • 容器相容性（drug-syringe compatibility）
  • 滲出物/萃取物（leachables/extractables）

若廠商 B 沒有設計控制資料，廠商 A 就必須自己做更廣泛的設計驗證（如注射力測試 glide force、柱塞力 plunger force）。

設計輸入輸出解讀 — 與無菌生產相關

藥物黏度 → 針頭孔徑/注射力：高黏度蛋白質藥物需要大孔徑針頭或特殊針頭設計，這影響滅菌工藝選擇（針頭已附著或另外組裝）。

藥物/注射器接觸材質規格：矽化（siliconization）量、橡皮活塞材質、玻璃塗層 → 直接影響穩定性測試設計。

V.A.2(b) — Design Verification, Validation & Risk Analysis

ii. Design Verification and Validation

Once Manufacturer A has established design outputs for all design inputs, it must perform design verification and validation activities to ensure that the combination product meets design input requirements, including user needs and intended uses.

Examples of appropriate testing of the prefilled syringe include:

  • Bench testing of the delivery of the drug from the syringe to ensure repeatable and accurate drug delivery
  • Shock and vibration testing of the packaged prefilled syringe to ensure no damage or loss of integrity in shipping
  • Validation that expected users can adequately follow the instructions for use; other human factors studies
  • Biocompatibility testing (ISO 10993-1)
  • Drug and syringe compatibility studies
  • Leachables and extractables testing
  • Verification that the prefilled syringe works with all expected delivery methods (needle, needleless)

These activities would be documented in the DHF (Design History File) pursuant to 21 CFR 820.30(j).

iii. Risk Analysis

Manufacturer A should identify risks associated with the prefilled syringe design, its manufacturing processes, and intended uses, and also reduce or mitigate any unacceptable risks.

Risk Mitigation
Syringe filled with incorrect drug dose In-process acceptance testing, process validation
Loss of sterility Container-closure integrity testing, packaging validation/testing
Drug contamination from materials of syringe construction Purchasing controls (including receiving acceptance activities for components received from syringe component manufacturer), in-process and finished product testing to ensure no introduction of contaminants during manufacture and over the product shelf-life
Syringe failure during use Design verification testing on syringe, purchasing controls over syringe component manufacturer(s)

中文解析 — 設計驗證、確認與風險分析

Verification vs Validation — 關鍵區別

Verification（驗證）：設計輸出是否滿足設計輸入的規格？→「Do we build the product right?」

Validation（確認）：產品是否滿足最終用戶的需求和預期用途？→「Do we build the right product?」

例：針頭孔徑規格測試 = Verification；臨床使用者能否正確自注射 = Validation（人因工程研究）

無菌 PFS 必做的關鍵測試

  • 滲出物/萃取物（L&E）：玻璃注射器的矽化油、橡皮活塞的添加劑可能滲入藥液 → 必須在穩定性條件下測試
  • 容器密封完整性（CCI）：針頭保護蓋、活塞密封 → 影響無菌保障
  • 相容性研究：藥物不能改變注射器性能（如腐蝕玻璃）；注射器不能改變藥物特性（如蛋白質吸附）
  • 人因工程：患者自注射 → 用力、握持設計、針頭保護器操作

風險分析實務 — 無菌 PFS 高風險項目

失去無菌性（Loss of Sterility）是最嚴重的風險，需要：

  • 包裝完整性驗證（Package Integrity Validation）
  • 容器密封完整性測試（CCI Test）— 21 CFR 211.165 要求
  • 運輸驗證（ISTA/ASTM 標準）

注射器材料汙染藥物：購入控制 + 入廠檢驗（21 CFR 211.84）是雙重保障。

CDMO 操作啟示 — DHF 建立策略

CDMO 作為接受委託廠商，需釐清「設計控制責任歸屬」：

  • 若客戶（品牌持有人）是 Owner → 設計控制由客戶主導
  • CDMO 負責製程驗證文件（可成為 DHF 的一部分）
  • 合約需明確規定設計資料的存取權限（for FDA inspection）

V.A.2(b) — Design Changes & Design History File (DHF)

iv. Design Changes

Manufacturer A must also have procedures in place to ensure that any changes to design requirements are identified, documented, validated and/or verified, reviewed, and approved prior to implementation.

Activities should include review of the original risk analysis, review and approval of the revised design inputs and outputs, and review and approval of the design change.

For example, before changing a material used in the syringe that comes into contact with the drug, Manufacturer A should conduct verification activities to ensure that no degradation of performance characteristics will occur before the expiration date for the combination product as a result of the change of materials. Likewise, any change to the formulation of the drug should include design control activities such as verification to confirm that the new formulation does not degrade performance of the syringe.

v. Design History File

The DHF may include references or point to information residing elsewhere so long as the reference or pointer is precise enough to allow the necessary information to be readily accessed as needed, including for inspections.

For example, elements of the syringe component manufacturer's design documentation may be relied upon to support the combination product DHF. Such information on the syringe may reside at Manufacturer B's (syringe component manufacturer) facility, so long as the necessary documentation can be accessed in a reasonable time during inspection of Manufacturer A.

Manufacturer A should ensure access to such documentation through supplier agreements with Manufacturer B under its purchasing controls (21 CFR 820.50). Documentation of development of the drug product that preceded the design effort to incorporate the drug product into a prefilled syringe may be referenced in the DHF and become an input to the combination product design effort (though such drug-only development is not itself subject to design controls).

中文解析 — 設計變更與 DHF

設計變更的雙向影響

PFS 的設計變更需要同時考量兩個方向：

  • 注射器材料變更 → 藥物：新材料是否與藥物相容？穩定性是否受影響？（觸發 21 CFR 211.137 和 211.167 穩定性要求）
  • 藥物配方變更 → 注射器：新配方是否影響注射器性能（如腐蝕性、黏度改變導致注射力不足）？

材料或配方變更時，FDA 允許廠商槓桿 211 穩定性數據來協助確定 820.30 驗證活動的範圍。

DHF — 可以「指向」他處的設計文件

DHF 不需要把所有文件集中在一處，但必須能在查廠期間合理時間內取得。實務上：

  • 廠商 B 的注射器設計資料可存放在廠商 B，只要合約確保查廠時能調閱
  • 藥品開發報告（NDA/ANDA 資料）可以交叉引用為 DHF 輸入
  • 但「藥品研發本身」不受設計控制約束（因為是藥物開發，不是器械設計）

CDMO 風險提示 — 供應商協議條款

CDMO 需在注射器組件供應商合約中明確規定：

  • 材料變更的事前通知義務（Change Notification）
  • 設計文件的查廠調閱權
  • 變更評估的協作流程

缺乏這些條款，FDA 查廠時 DHF 不完整，將構成 483 觀察項。

V.A.2(c) — 820.50: Purchasing Controls

Manufacturer A is required to control its purchasing activities, including for the syringe components, in accordance with 21 CFR 820.50.

For example, if the syringe barrel and plunger material is critical to ensuring that there is no adverse reaction with the drug, Manufacturer A should structure purchasing agreements with Manufacturer B to ensure that Manufacturer A is notified of any changes to this material prior to implementation of the change.

Similarly, if Manufacturer A uses an outside facility for terminal sterilization of the prefilled syringe, Manufacturer A must also have appropriate controls over that sterilization service provider.

Note: Because the device constituent part (syringe) is also a container/closure for the drug, Manufacturer A must perform acceptance testing of the syringe components in accordance with 21 CFR 211.84.

中文解析 — 820.50 採購控制

PFS 採購控制的特殊性 — 雙重身份

注射器組件在 PFS 中扮演雙重角色：

  1. 器械組成部分（Device constituent part）→ 820.50 採購控制
  2. 藥物容器/密封（Container/closure for drug）→ 211.84 入廠檢驗

這意味著：注射器桶（barrel）、活塞（plunger）、針頭保護蓋必須同時滿足器械採購控制和藥物組件入廠檢驗要求。

Terminal Sterilization 外包的控制要求

若委外進行最終滅菌（如 EtO、伽瑪射線、電子束），該滅菌服務供應商也受 820.50 管控，需要：

  • 供應商評估與批准程序
  • 品質協議（Quality Agreement）
  • 定期供應商稽核
  • 滅菌批記錄的審查與核可

211.84 vs 820.50 — CDMO 入廠管控雙軌

法規 對象 要求重點
211.84 藥物組件/容器/密封 取樣、測試或審查；每批驗收
820.50 供應商評估與管控 供應商評估、採購協議、驗收程序

PFS 的注射器組件兩者皆適用。

V.A.2(d) — 820.100: CAPA

Manufacturer A is required to establish and maintain CAPA procedures for the combination product. Following are two examples of issues that might arise and exemplary steps for addressing them:

Example 1 — Fill Volume Nonconformity

Manufacturer A has implemented in-process manufacturing verification procedures to confirm that the syringe is being filled with the correct amount of the drug, and the data from this verification are analyzed for potential nonconformities. Manufacturer A notes an increase in nonconformities relating to the volume of drug being put in the syringe and in turn opens a CAPA to investigate the problem. Upon investigation of the cause of the improper fill volume, Manufacturer A determines that maintenance procedures on the filling equipment are the cause of the incorrect fill volume. Manufacturer A updates the maintenance procedures and performs verification/validation testing to confirm that the changes correct the problem and do not cause new ones.

Example 2 — Sterile Package Damage

Manufacturer A begins receiving an increased number of customer complaints related to holes or other damage to the syringe's sterile package and opens a CAPA to investigate the issue. The CAPA reveals that Manufacturer B has made changes to a syringe component such that there are sharp edges that can damage the sterile pouch during shipping.

Manufacturer A works with Manufacturer B to eliminate the sharp edge or finds a new supplier. Manufacturer A also augments purchasing specifications and acceptance test steps to perform visual inspection of the syringe components. Manufacturer A repeats related design verification testing to ensure that the new syringe meets all design requirements and does not result in pouch damage during shipping.

中文解析 — CAPA 與 PFS 組合產品

CAPA 在組合產品的特殊要求

820.100 CAPA 和 211 已有的變更管控（如 211.100 書面程序）邏輯相似，但 CAPA 的器械版本更強調：

  • 系統性問題根因分析（Root cause analysis）
  • CAPA 有效性驗證（Effectiveness verification）
  • 跨產品/跨系統的預防措施

案例 1 解析 — 填充量偏差

根因：填充設備維護程序問題 → 非設備本身故障，而是維護 SOP 設計不足。

CAPA 動作：更新維護 SOP + 驗證/確認設備改善後的填充準確性。

這個案例強調：CAPA 不只是「修設備」，而是要追根到系統性原因並預防再發。

案例 2 解析 — 無菌包裝破損

客訴觸發 CAPA → 調查發現是供應商（廠商 B）的未通知變更導致銳邊問題。

CAPA 的多層動作：

  • 短期：與廠商 B 解決銳邊問題或更換供應商
  • 中期：強化入廠目視檢驗標準
  • 長期：重新進行包裝設計驗證測試（設計控制的迴圈）

這展示了 CAPA → 採購控制 → 設計控制的跨系統連結。

無菌包裝破損 — 監管重點

無菌包裝完整性是無菌屏障系統（Sterile Barrier System）的關鍵。破損等同於潛在的無菌保障喪失（Breach of Sterility Assurance），可能觸發市場召回。預防的最佳手段是強健的 CAPA + 供應商變更管控。

V.A.2(e) — 820.170 Installation & 820.200 Servicing

Installation and servicing requirements would not apply to the prefilled syringe because the product does not require installation or servicing activities.

中文解析 — 820.170 & 820.200 不適用

為何 Installation 和 Servicing 不適用於 PFS？

820.170 Installation（安裝）：適用於需要在使用現場安裝的設備類器械（如透析機、影像設備）。PFS 是一次性使用產品，由使用者直接取出注射，無需安裝程序。

820.200 Servicing（服務/維修）：適用於需要售後維修保養的可重複使用器械。PFS 為單次使用（single-use）產品，使用後丟棄，無需維修服務。

這兩項在 Drug CGMP-based 方法中雖然必須「列示」，但廠商需在文件中說明不適用的理由（N/A with justification），不能留白。

Section V.B — 藥物塗層外科網片 (Drug-Coated Mesh)

V.B.1 — Drug-Coated Mesh: Scenario Description

Manufacturer A plans to sell a synthetic surgical mesh coated with a drug. Manufacturer A has a marketing authorization to sell the uncoated mesh. Manufacturer A wants to coat the mesh with a drug to treat infection at the site of the product's implantation.

Manufacturer B has an approval to market a drug for local administration to treat infection at the site of implantation of this class of device, but not in a formulation suitable for coating onto the mesh. Manufacturer A has established a business relationship with Manufacturer B to use the drug and develop the data needed to support Manufacturer A's marketing authorization for the coated mesh.

Manufacturer B will manufacture the drug formulation for spraying onto the mesh, and Manufacturer A will manufacture the finished drug-coated mesh combination product.

V.B.2 — Compliance with QS Regulation Requirements

The coated mesh product is a single-entity combination product under 21 CFR 3.2(e)(1). Therefore, Manufacturer A is subject both to the drug CGMPs and the device QS regulation for this combination product.

Because Manufacturer A is already marketing the uncoated mesh, it has an existing device QS regulation-based operating system, and chooses to operate under a device QS regulation-based streamlined approach for the combination product.

This discussion focuses on design control and purchasing control considerations arising from inclusion of the drug constituent part in the combination product.

中文解析 — 藥物塗層網片情境

情境 B 的法規結構

廠商 A = 外科網片製造商（器械主導）→ 已有 Part 820 系統 → 選擇 Device QS-based Streamlined Approach

廠商 B = 藥物供應商（提供塗布用藥物配方）

情境 B 的重點不是「選哪種方法」，而是 Device QS-based 方法如何處理「藥物組成部分加入後」的設計控制與採購控制。

比喻 — 廚師在現有菜單上加藥膳

  • 設計控制要加入「藥物劑量和藥效」的輸入要求
  • 採購控制要管控藥物供應商（廠商 B）

在本情境中，廠商 A 採用 Device QS-based，需補充：

  • 211.103 — 收率計算
  • 211.132 — 防拆包裝（OTC 適用，本案 N/A）
  • 211.137 — 有效期
  • 211.165 — 放行測試
  • 211.166 — 穩定性測試
  • 211.167 — 特殊測試要求
  • 211.170 — 保留樣品

V.B — 820.30: Design Controls for Drug-Coated Mesh

A focus of the design control process for the drug constituent part of the combination product is to ensure that the drug-coated mesh will be safe and effective for treating infection at the site of implantation.

Accordingly, if the necessary dose of the drug for effective treatment of infection is already known, it would be a design input; design outputs and validation and verification would need to be established to ensure that the required dose is provided when the drug elutes from the mesh.

Risk analysis must be conducted to identify any risks associated with the design, manufacturing, and use of the mesh. Design reviews should cover these and other related design considerations for the product, and personnel with expertise in both the drug- and device-specific issues should be included in these reviews.

i. Design Inputs and Outputs

Design Input / User Needs Design Output
Required delivery dose and delivery rate for the drug Drug formulation and concentration, coating thickness, uniformity of coating, manufacturing process requirements, allowable storage conditions
Expected use condition (e.g., anatomical location of use, surgical technique) Labeling (instructions for use), material/drug composition to ensure no damage to mesh or coating during surgical placement
Maximum allowable temperature during transportation, handling, and storage for the combination product Packaging/labeling specifications for the combination product
No unacceptable degradation of the drug over the expected shelf-life Specifications for the drug formulation drug-contacting materials and packaging, shelf-life labeling
No degradation of the surgical mesh over the expected shelf-life Specifications for mesh material, drug formulation and packaging, shelf-life labeling

ii. Design Verification and Validation

  • Clinical data must be developed to ensure that the drug, when combined with the mesh, is effective in treating infection and does not raise safety concerns (design validation)
  • Manufacturer A would have to verify that the mechanical properties of the coated mesh are such that the product can withstand the stresses anticipated during the surgical procedure and still perform as intended
  • Stability studies to ensure that the properties of the drug are not degraded over the expected shelf life. Design outputs include the labeled expiration date and storage conditions for the combination product

iii. Risk Analysis and Mitigation

Risk Mitigation
Drug concentration in coating not sufficient to treat infection (e.g., due to insufficient drug coating per surface area of the mesh or non-uniformity of coating), inappropriate delivery rate Clinical testing of the combination product, in-process acceptance testing, manufacturing process validation
Mesh erodes or drug degrades during use or storage Design verification testing (bench), clinical testing, labeling (instructions for use, expiration dating), purchasing controls over drug supplier, specifications and other process controls

中文解析 — 藥物塗層網片的設計控制

Drug-device 複合設計的關鍵挑戰

藥物洗脫速率（Elution Rate）是核心設計參數：

  • 過快 → 藥物濃度超標、毒性風險
  • 過慢 → 藥物濃度不足、無法治療感染
  • 塗布均勻性（Coating uniformity）= 洗脫速率一致性的前提

這個設計輸入/輸出的關係：藥效需求 → 塗布厚度和均勻度規格 → 生產製程參數。

設計審查人員的跨領域要求

FDA 明確要求：設計審查必須包含同時具備藥物和器械專業知識的人員（及獨立於審查階段之外的人員）。

這意味著器械廠商不能用純器械工程師團隊進行審查 — 必須引入藥物科學家或聘請顧問。對 CDMO 的啟示：跨功能設計審查委員會（Design Review Board）的組成必須包含臨床/藥理/藥物化學代表。

穩定性研究的雙重考量

外科網片的穩定性不只是「藥物效期」，還包括：

  • 網片材料穩定性：藥物塗層是否改變網片的機械強度？
  • 藥物穩定性：儲存條件下藥物含量是否維持？降解產物是否安全？

這是典型的「交互作用穩定性研究」— 不能只測藥物，也不能只測網片，要測組合後的整體。

DHF 與上市申請資料的關係

藥物塗層網片的設計控制活動（驗證/確認資料）大多也需要提交給 FDA 進行上市前審查。廠商 A 可以：

  • 將上市申請文件（premarket submission）以交叉引用方式納入 DHF
  • 利用藥物 B 的既有批准數據作為設計輸入的參考（槓桿既有藥物資料）

V.B — 820.50: Purchasing Controls for Drug-Coated Mesh

Manufacturer A already has established procedures for controlling purchasing/supplier activities pursuant to 21 CFR 820.50. Under this provision, Manufacturer A must ensure that appropriate purchasing controls for Manufacturer B are established and maintained.

Purchasing controls (and acceptance activities under 21 CFR 211.84) with respect to the drug constituent part should focus on ensuring that Manufacturer B can supply the drug that meets the specifications that Manufacturer A has established during the design control process.

Manufacturer A should establish purchasing agreements with Manufacturer B to ensure that Manufacturer A is notified of any changes that may affect the performance of the combination product prior to Manufacturer B's implementation of the changes. The notifications should address issues including:

  • Changes in the drug specification
  • Changes in its components
  • Composition of drug coating materials
  • The drug manufacturing process or facility

Such proposed changes may require that Manufacturer A complete additional design verification and/or validation. For example, verification testing may be necessary to confirm that the purity and stability of the drug is maintained, pursuant to the requirements of 21 CFR 820.30(i).

中文解析 — 採購控制 & 藥物供應商管理

器械廠管控藥物供應商的挑戰

對器械廠（廠商 A）而言，管控藥物供應商（廠商 B）需要跨越傳統器械供應鏈管理的舒適區：

  • 需了解藥物規格（API purity、strength）和藥物製造的 CGMP 要求
  • 採購協議需包含藥物特有的變更通知觸發條件（配方、製程、設施）
  • 211.84 入廠測試要求（包含身份確認、純度、效價）需建立在 Part 820 系統中

變更通知義務 — 防止「隱形」組合產品問題

藥物塗層網片的品質不只取決於自己的製程，也取決於廠商 B 的藥物品質。若廠商 B 未提前通知改變藥物濃度或製程，組合產品的洗脫速率可能悄悄改變，但廠商 A 毫不知情。

這就是合約中「變更前通知義務（Prior Change Notification）」的重要性。

Section V.C — 藥物洗脫支架 (Drug Eluting Stent, DES)

V.C.1 — DES: Scenario Description

In this scenario, Manufacturer A is the owner for a drug-eluting stent (DES) composed of a stent coated with a drug.

  • Manufacturer B — manufactures the active pharmaceutical ingredient (API or bulk drug substance)
  • Manufacturer C — manufactures a polymer with which the bulk drug substance will be combined for coating onto the stent
  • Manufacturer D — manufactures the materials used for the product's primary packaging

Manufacturer A purchases the bulk drug substance from Manufacturer B and the polymer from Manufacturer C, then formulates the drug coating solution and uses it to coat the stent in its own facility. At the same facility, Manufacturer A packages the DES using the primary packaging materials it purchases from Manufacturer D.

V.C.2 — Compliance with Drug CGMP Requirements

The DES is a single-entity combination product as defined in 21 CFR 3.2(e)(1) and, therefore, is subject to both the drug CGMPs and device QS regulation. As a device manufacturer, Manufacturer A already has a CGMP operating system designed to comply with the device QS regulation and has elected to establish a device QS regulation-based streamlined approach for the DES in accordance with 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2).

This example focuses on considerations for complying with the drug CGMP provisions specified in 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2).

中文解析 — DES 情境結構

DES 的供應鏈複雜性

DES 是最複雜的組合產品之一，涉及 4 個主要參與者：

廠商角色供應物料

BAPI 製造商原料藥（如 sirolimus, paclitaxel）

  • 211.103 — 收率計算
  • 211.132 — 防拆包裝（本案 N/A — 非 OTC）
  • 211.137 — 有效期

CDMO 類比 — 器械 CDMO 承接 DES 的挑戰

如果一家器械 CDMO 要承接 DES 代工，需要在既有 Part 820 系統基礎上建立 8 個藥物 CGMP 補充條款的執行系統 — 這是對器械廠合規體系的重大擴充，需要引進藥物 QA 人才。

V.C — 211.84: Testing and Approval/Rejection of Components

Manufacturer A has controls in place to evaluate suppliers, contractors, and consultants based on their ability to meet quality and other specified requirements and to control incoming materials in accordance with 21 CFR 820.50 and 21 CFR 820.80. Under 21 CFR 4.4(b)(2), Manufacturer A must augment its existing CGMP operating system as needed to satisfy the requirements of 21 CFR 211.84 for the DES.

In accordance with 21 CFR 211.84, the bulk drug substance, polymer, and primary packaging materials supplied by manufacturers B, C, and D must be sampled, tested, or examined, as appropriate, by Manufacturer A to determine whether they should be approved or rejected for use.

  • The primary packaging materials should be visually inspected to ensure that the correct, specified materials are received from Manufacturer D
  • Each lot of bulk drug substance and polymer must be tested to verify conformity with written specifications for purity, quality, and strength
  • Due to the nature and intended use of sterile drug eluting stents, testing must include microbiological testing of the bulk drug substance and polymer

Under certain circumstances, Manufacturer A may rely on a report of analysis from a supplier in lieu of conducting some of its own testing, subject to conditions including: Manufacturer A is still responsible for the performance of at least one specific identity test upon receipt of each lot, and reliance on reports of analyses is contingent on Manufacturer A establishing the reliability of the supplier's analyses through appropriate validation of the test results at appropriate intervals.

V.C — 211.103: Calculation of Yield

Calculation of yield should be conducted during all appropriate steps in the manufacturing process prior to and after application of the coating to the stent, including the formulation of the coating. Manufacturer A is responsible for the calculation of yield at appropriate phases of manufacture at its facility, including application of the coating to the DES, and the packaging of the DES.

Drug-coated stents that are rejected due only to defects in the device constituent part (stent) will result in corresponding loss of drug constituent part from the batch/lot. This loss should be captured as part of the yield calculations for the drug constituent part and investigation of the cause of that loss should identify the manufacturing problem that is leading to these device constituent part nonconformities.

中文解析 — 211.84 入廠測試 & 211.103 收率

211.84 的三層入廠測試架構

物料測試要求特殊要求
原料藥（廠商 B） 純度、效價、品質規格；微生物測試 每批必做身份確認測試（即使有供應商 CoA）
聚合物（廠商 C） 純度、品質；微生物測試 供應商分析報告須經驗證才可替代部分測試
初級包裝（廠商 D） 目視檢查確認物料正確性 視外觀符合規格即可

供應商 CoA 替代自行測試的條件

不可替代的：身份確認測試（Identity test）— 每批必須自行執行，無例外。

可替代的（滿足以下條件）：

  • 供應商在出貨前進行了符合性測試
  • 廠商 A 已驗證供應商測試方法的可靠性
  • 定期再驗證供應商分析結果（at appropriate intervals）

211.103 收率計算 — DES 的獨特挑戰

DES 的收率計算特別複雜，因為：

  • 藥物塗布工序：需要計算塗布前/後的藥物含量，確認收率
  • 支架不良品淘汰：若支架本身有缺陷被剔除，附著的藥物也一併損失 → 這個藥物損耗必須計入收率，並調查支架不良率的根因
  • 多工序收率：配方製備 → 塗布 → 固化 → 包裝，每個關鍵步驟都需要記錄

這個概念在純器械製造中沒有對應要求，是器械廠引入藥物 CGMP 後最陌生的項目之一。

V.C — 211.137, 211.165, 211.166, 211.167, 211.170

211.132 — Tamper-Evident Packaging (OTC)

This regulatory requirement is not applicable to drug-eluting stents, as they are not OTC products.

211.137 — Expiration Dating

As the manufacturer of the combination product, Manufacturer A is responsible for establishing the expiration date on the labeling of the finished combination product. The expiration date must be established based on the data from the stability studies for the finished packaged DES and should also take into account other shelf-life considerations as required under design control. These considerations include the functionality of the stent and polymer and the integrity of the coating and the packaging.

211.165 — Testing and Release for Distribution

Manufacturer A must test each batch of the finished combination product to determine conformance with the final written specifications for the product. A general list of tests for drug-eluting stents:

  • Appearance & Identification
  • Assay & Content Uniformity
  • Impurities and Degradation Products
  • Drug Release Rate (immediate and/or extended release rates)
  • Package Integrity and Sterility Assurance
  • Endotoxins & Particulate Matter
  • Additional Testing (polymer molecular weight, residual monomers, catalysts, and other additives)

211.166 — Stability Testing

Stability testing for the final DES product should address: appearance, assay/drug content, impurities/degradation products, rate of drug release, particulate matter, sterility, and package integrity. Analytical procedures for stability testing should be fully validated as suitable to demonstrate stability.

211.167 — Special Testing Requirements

Manufacturer A must conduct or contract to conduct testing of the DES in accordance with 21 CFR 211.167(a) because this class of product is purported to be sterile and non-pyrogenic. In addition, testing in accordance with 21 CFR 211.167(c) must be conducted because the DES constitutes a controlled-release dosage form.

211.170 — Reserve Samples

Manufacturer A must maintain reserve samples representative of each lot of the active ingredient used in the combination product and of each lot or batch of the finished, packaged DES. Reserve samples must consist of at least twice the quantity necessary to perform all tests required for the active ingredient and the finished DES, excluding sterility and pyrogen testing.

  • Samples of the active ingredient must be kept for 1 year past the expiration date of the last lot of finished DES to use that lot of the active ingredient
  • Samples for each lot of the finished DES must be kept for 1 year after the expiration date for the combination product
  • If Manufacturer A maintains reserve samples in a third-party storage facility, Manufacturer A should ensure, via purchasing controls (21 CFR 820.50), that the facility can meet applicable CGMP requirements

中文解析 — DES 藥物 CGMP 條款解析

211.137 有效期 — DES 的多維度穩定性

DES 的有效期設定需要考量所有影響有效期的因素，不只是藥物含量：

  • 藥物含量衰減（Drug assay over time）
  • 藥物洗脫速率的穩定性（Release rate stability）
  • 聚合物完整性（Polymer integrity）
  • 支架塗層的附著力（Coating adhesion）
  • 初級包裝的密封完整性（Package seal integrity）

若不同尺寸 DES 具有相似設計，FDA 允許槓桿既有穩定性數據，前提是有充分科學依據且已獲 FDA 審查。

211.165 DES 放行測試清單解析

藥物洗脫速率（Drug Release Rate）是最關鍵的 DES 專屬測試：

  • 即釋型（Immediate release）vs 緩釋型（Extended release）— 設計不同，測試方法不同
  • 體外溶出測試（In vitro dissolution）作為批放行代理指標
  • 聚合物殘留單體、催化劑測試 — 確保聚合物加工殘留物不超標

211.167 特殊測試 — 無菌 + 控制釋放雙重要求

211.167(a) — 無菌/非致熱原：每批 DES 必須進行無菌試驗和內毒素/致熱原測試（或同等無菌保障程序）。

211.167(c) — 控制釋放劑型：藥物洗脫支架屬於控制釋放劑型（controlled-release），必須進行額外的溶出/釋放速率測試，確認藥物按預期速率釋放。

211.170 保留樣品 — 雙層保留義務

DES 的保留樣品義務分兩個層次：

物料保留期限
原料藥（Active Ingredient） 用於最後一批 DES 的該批原料藥到期日後 1 年
成品 DES（含包裝） 每批成品有效期後 1 年

委外保留樣品的第三方儲存設施也受 820.50 管控，需要品質協議。

中文解析 — 核心詞彙學習重點

三種組合產品的分類記憶法

依包裝/結合方式分類：

  • Single-entity（單一實體）：已物理/化學結合 → 1個NDC/PMA/510(k)
  • Co-packaged（共同包裝）：各自獨立但裝在同一盒 → 各自有獨立的上市批准
  • Cross-labeled（交叉標示）：分開包裝分開賣，但標示規定必須搭配 → 各自有批准但標示連結

三種類型的 CGMP 要求邏輯相同，但 cross-labeled 情況下，各家廠商只對自己負責製造的 constituent part 負 CGMP 責任。

兩種 Streamlined Approach 比較

方法 主體框架 補充條款數量 適合對象
Drug CGMP-based 21 CFR 210/211（全部） 6 項 Device QS 藥廠、無菌 CDMO
Device QS-based 21 CFR Part 820（全部） 8 項 Drug CGMP 器械廠商

選擇依據：現有 CGMP 系統的主體是什麼。不要把補充條款少誤認為「更容易」— 兩套各有其深度要求。

Owner — 委外不免責

Owner（上市授權持有人）對整個組合產品的 CGMP 合規負最終責任，即使委外製造（如委託 CDMO 填充）。

這意味著：CDMO 的合規狀況直接影響品牌持有人的法規責任。品牌持有人必須透過品質協議（Quality Agreement）和供應商稽核確保 CDMO 符合要求。

Section Summary — Key Takeaways

PFS (Section V.A) — Drug CGMP-based 方法核心要點

  • PFS 是 Single-entity combination product — 必須同時符合 Drug CGMP + Device QS
  • 藥廠/CDMO 選 Drug CGMP-based → 補充 6 項 Device QS：820.20, 820.30, 820.50, 820.100, 820.170 (N/A), 820.200 (N/A)
  • 注射器組件 = 器械組成部分 + 藥物容器/密封 → 820.50 + 211.84 雙重要求
  • 設計控制重點：L&E、CCI、藥物-注射器相容性、人因工程
  • 820.170 和 820.200 對單次使用 PFS 不適用，但需書面說明理由

藥物塗層網片 (Section V.B) — Device QS-based 方法

  • 器械廠以 Part 820 為主體，補充 8 項 Drug CGMP 條款
  • 設計控制必須引入藥物科學專業（跨領域設計審查）
  • 藥物洗脫速率、塗布均勻性是核心設計輸出規格
  • 採購控制需管控藥物供應商，包含變更前通知義務

DES (Section V.C) — Device QS-based + 8 項 Drug CGMP

  • 最複雜的組合產品 — 4 家供應商、多工序製程
  • 211.84：API + 聚合物 + 初級包裝分別測試；身份確認不可用 CoA 替代
  • 211.103 收率：支架不良品淘汰的藥物損耗也要計入收率
  • 211.165 放行測試必含藥物洗脫速率測試
  • 211.170 保留樣品：原料藥（最後一批 DES 有效期後 1 年）+ 成品（有效期後 1 年）

CDMO 實戰決策框架

無菌填充 CDMO — PFS 合規策略選擇

結論：選擇 Drug CGMP-based Streamlined Approach。

理由：

  • CDMO 核心能力在藥物製造（21 CFR 211），現有系統是 Drug CGMP
  • 補充 6 項 Device QS 條款（主要是 820.20/820.30/820.50/820.100）遠比重建整套 Part 820 系統更務實
  • 820.170 和 820.200 對 PFS 不適用，實際補充工作量更少
  • 大多數客戶（品牌持有人）的 CMC 體系也是 Drug CGMP 主導

CDMO 承接 PFS 業務的合規清單

  • 820.20：確認品質政策、管理代表指定、管理審查程序
  • 820.30：建立 PFS 的 DHF，包含設計輸入/輸出、V&V 計畫、風險分析
  • 820.50：注射器組件供應商評估 + 採購協議（變更通知條款）
  • 211.84：注射器組件的入廠測試程序（身份、純度、外觀）
  • 820.100：CAPA 程序涵蓋組合產品特有場景
  • 820.170/820.200：書面確認不適用，並說明理由
  • 品質協議：與客戶（品牌持有人 / Owner）明確劃分設計控制責任
