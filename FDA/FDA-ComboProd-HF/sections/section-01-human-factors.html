III.A — Glossary and Concepts　詞彙與核心概念

原文內容 Original Text

For purposes of this document, the following definitions and concepts apply to HF studies, the final finished combination product, and the major clinical study. For additional information on these terms see the sections that follow the glossary. For related definitions see Agency guidance Applying Human Factors and Usability Engineering to Optimize Medical Device Design.

1. Human Factors Study (or HF Study)

A study conducted with representative users to assess the adequacy of the combination product user interface design to eliminate or mitigate potential use-related hazards. Typically, HF studies are part of an iterative design process that is driven by the complexity of the combination product and the nature of the safety considerations. The HF study evaluates:

  1. The ability of the user to perform critical tasks, and
  2. The ability of the user to understand the information in the packaging and labeling — such as product labels or instructions for use — that inform the user's actions and that are critical to the safe and effective use of the combination product (e.g., product preparation, administration, maintenance and disposal, or what actions to take if an adverse reaction occurs).

Both types of evaluations may be part of the HF Formative and HF Validation studies described below.

a. HF Formative Study

A study conducted on a combination product prototype user interface at one or more stages during the iterative product development process to assess user interaction with the product and identify potential use errors. HF Formative studies are iterative and inform the need for user interface changes (e.g., product design or labeling changes) and inform the content of the HF Validation study.

b. HF Validation Study

A study conducted to demonstrate that the final finished combination product user interface can be used by intended users without serious use errors or problems, for the product's intended uses and under the expected use conditions. The study should demonstrate that use-related hazards for the final finished combination product have been eliminated or that the mitigation for residual risks is acceptable; i.e., the benefit of product use outweigh the residual risk of the product. The study participants are representative of the intended users and the study conditions are representative of expected use conditions.

2. Final Finished Combination Product

The final finished combination product is the product intended for market and submitted in the marketing application. This term applies to the combined final device, drug, and/or biological product configuration including all product user interfaces (e.g., proposed packaging, labels and labeling, including training programs).

3. Major Clinical Study (or Major Clinical Trial)

As opposed to a HF study, a major clinical study is a larger scale clinical study that occurs during a later phase of combination product development. Major clinical studies provide the primary support for the safety and effectiveness of a product for a proposed indication (e.g., adequate and well-controlled studies). In addition to adequate and well-controlled studies, other types of later phase larger scale clinical studies may also be considered major clinical studies; e.g., a long-term extension study.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：什麼是「人因工程研究」？

HF Study（人因工程研究）的本質是：找代表性使用者來操作產品，觀察他們能不能安全地完成關鍵步驟，以及他們能不能理解說明書。

它評估兩件事：

  • 身體操作能力：能否正確執行關鍵任務（Critical Tasks）
  • 資訊理解能力：能否看懂標籤、說明書上的指示

這不是臨床試驗，不是在測藥效——它是在測人和裝置的介面設計是否安全可用。

比喻：HF Study 好比飛行員模擬器考試

飛行員在取得執照前，必須在模擬器上操作各種緊急情況。考官觀察他們的每一個動作：有沒有遺漏任何步驟？有沒有做錯順序？

HF Study 類似：讓真實的使用者（患者、護士、照護者）在接近真實的情境下操作組合產品，研究人員在旁觀察和記錄使用錯誤，而不是給予指導。這才是真實的「人機介面壓力測試」。

重要：「Final Finished Combination Product」的法規意義

這個詞在法規上有精確含義：它是你要上市、要送審的那個版本，包含裝置、藥品/生物製劑、包裝、標籤、說明書，甚至訓練計畫。

HF Validation 必須用這個最終版本進行——不能用修改前的原型，不能用不同批次的裝置，不能用不同版本的說明書。這對 CDMO 的影響很大（詳見後面的應用說明）。

思考練習

  1. 一家公司想在 HF Validation Study 之後，針對說明書的字體大小做微調。FDA 會怎麼看這個改動？需要重新做 Validation 嗎？
  2. HF Formative Study 的結果可以直接用來支持上市申請嗎？

三個詞的差別要搞清楚

Formative 是「設計優化工具」；Validation 是「法規門檻」；Major Clinical 是「藥效門檻」。三者目的不同，不能混淆。

III.B — Evaluation of Use-Related Risk　使用相關風險評估

原文內容 Original Text

Consistent with a risk-based design and development paradigm, the foundation for HF study designs, testing and evaluation should be a use-related risk analysis of a combination product. A use-related risk analysis is a crucial step to help identify use-related hazards associated with the combination product, as well as to characterize high-risk hazards so they can be mitigated or eliminated through improved product interface design.

The use-related risk analysis will help identify critical tasks that should be evaluated in a HF study, inform the priority of testing the tasks in a HF study, and determine if there are specific use scenarios to include in testing. A variety of methods can be used to develop and analyze use-related hazards. Two methods frequently used are Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) and Fault Tree Analysis (FTA).

The use-related risk analysis should take into account: all the intended uses, users, and use environments; therapeutic or diagnostic procedures associated with the use of the combination product; similar products used within the environments; and any associated medical factors that may affect the safe use of the combination product. In addition, if previous models of the same or similar combination products exist, the risk analysis should incorporate information on known use-related problems with those products. This information can be obtained from the applicant's own experience as well as from public sources such as literature, adverse event reports, and product safety communications.

III.B.1 — Critical Tasks

The use-related risk analysis should identify critical tasks. Critical tasks are user tasks that, if performed incorrectly or not performed at all, would or could cause harm to the patient or user, where harm is defined to include compromised medical care. Thus, categorizing a task as critical is dependent on the unique considerations for each combination product.

The Agency expects the risk analysis for the combination product to include: an identification of all the critical tasks required for using the combination product, the consequences for failing to perform each critical task correctly, and the strategies that have been applied in the design of the user interface to eliminate or reduce risks to acceptable levels. Such an assessment should include considerations of the indication, the users, the environment and other conditions that might influence the importance of a particular task.

Some examples of critical tasks include:

  • The patient being able to successfully self-administer a drug at the prescribed dose identified in the labeling. Failure could harm the patient due to mis-dosing, under-dosing, overdosing, or inability to deliver a dose.
  • The user being able to safely dispose of a used syringe. Failure could result in needle sticks.
  • The patient being able to appropriately navigate the user interface for a patient-controlled analgesia (PCA) delivery system. Failure could result in missed doses, inappropriate repeat doses, or overdoses.
  • The user being able to understand instructions for inserting a capsule into an inhaler to release the drug, and being able to insert the capsule. Failure could result in the patient swallowing the capsule instead of inhaling the contents, lack of treatment effect, or medication-related adverse events.
  • The user being able to distinguish a product from others of similar appearance. Failure could result in delivery of the wrong drug.
  • The user being able to complete a series of critical tasks required to prepare and administer a reconstituted drug from a combination product kit containing a prefilled diluent syringe, drug vial, empty syringe, needle, transfer device and infusion pump — including preparing the drug under sterile conditions, connecting the system, and introducing the reconstituted drug solution into an infusion pump. Failure could result in medication errors and/or use-related infection.

Appendix A identifies task failures that may occur with general categories of combination products such as injectors and inhalers. The information can be used to guide the applicant when conducting a risk analysis.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：「使用相關風險」與「固有風險」的根本區別

固有風險（Inherent Risk）：來自產品本身的化學或生物特性。例如：某生物製劑有免疫原性，可能引起過敏反應。

使用相關風險（Use-Related Risk）：來自人與裝置介面互動的方式。例如：患者忘記移除針蓋 → 無法給藥；患者誤以為按壓一次不夠而重複施打 → 過量。

Use-Related Risk 分析的對象是「人的行為」，而不是「藥的毒性」。這是一種針對人機介面的 FMEA。

比喻：用 FMEA 思維分析「人」

在製程 FMEA 裡，我們問：「如果這個步驟出錯，會有什麼後果？發生機率多高？能被偵測到嗎？」

Use-Related Risk 分析做的完全一樣，只是把「製程步驟」換成「使用者操作步驟」。例如：

  • 步驟：移除針蓋
  • 失效模式：忘記移除 / 移除不完整
  • 後果：無法給藥（under-dose / no dose）
  • 設計控制：加上明顯的視覺 / 觸覺提示，或加入防呆機制

核心概念：什麼是「Critical Task」（關鍵任務）？

定義很直接：如果這個任務做錯了、或根本沒做，會造成傷害（包括醫療照護受損），那它就是 Critical Task。

「傷害」的定義是廣義的，包含：

  • 直接身體傷害（針刺、感染、過量、無效劑量）
  • 醫療照護受損（病情惡化因為劑量不足）
  • 給藥錯誤（Wrong patient / wrong drug）

每個 Critical Task 都必須在 HF study 裡被評估。

監管重點：風險分析必須涵蓋現有問題報告

FDA 明確要求，如果市面上有相似產品，風險分析必須納入：

  • 不良事件報告（FDA MAUDE 資料庫）
  • 產品安全通報
  • 文獻中記錄的使用錯誤
  • 申請者自身的使用者回饋資料

這意味著你不能從零開始做風險分析——你必須先研究前車之鑑。

思考練習

  1. 某患者用自動注射器治療類風濕性關節炎，每週皮下注射一次。哪些使用場景最可能導致 Critical Task 失效？居家使用 vs. 診所使用的風險矩陣有何不同？
  2. 一個需要重組 (reconstitution) 的凍乾粉末組合產品，比 PFS 多出哪些 Critical Tasks？它們的風險等級如何？

III.B.2 — Intended Users and Use Environments　預期使用者與使用環境

原文內容 Original Text

Prior to performing a risk analysis, it is important to identify all intended users and use environments for the combination product. Intended users may be categorized into distinct user groups by their different characteristics (e.g., use responsibilities, tasks performed, age ranges, skills, or experience levels).

For combination products, distinct user groups generally are health care professionals (HCPs) and lay users (non-health care professionals). Within these two groups there are likely further subgroups based on different tasks, roles, abilities and education.

Subgroups of the HCP user population can include those with significantly different roles (e.g., nurses, pharmacists, physicians, emergency medical technicians, home health care providers). Also, within the HCP user population there may be individuals that have experience with the use of similar products and individuals that do not (e.g., injector-experienced vs naïve) or that do or do not have experience with similar appearing products with different instructions for use or different hazards.

Lay users (non-health care professionals) are those who use the product for self-administration (the patient) or those who administer the product to others as a caregiver (e.g., a family member, sports coach). Within this population, experience of individual users with similar products or products under development may vary widely. For example, when considering a drug-autoinjector combination product, some lay users may be naïve to the use of any autoinjector or may be naïve to certain types of autoinjectors. Also, lay users may have experience with a different product that might influence their interaction.

Environments of use can have diverse characteristics that affect the users' interactions with the product. Combination products may be used in various professional health care / clinical settings that include emergency departments, intensive care units, inpatient bedsides, procedure suites, outpatient clinics, mobile units, and stocking and storage locations. Likewise, they can also be used in non-clinical settings including homes, schools, offices, and various modes of transportation (e.g., ambulances, airplanes).

These environments may vary with respect to temperature, lighting and noise levels, ambient activity levels, number of people in the vicinity, and the availability of associated/accessory medication or devices. Also, a combination product that is intended for home use may be confused with other family member or pet medications stored in the same location. Such environmental conditions may lead to use errors. These environmental factors should be considered in the use-related risk analysis, and included within the design of the HF study as appropriate when they present a use hazard.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：使用者不只是醫護人員

這是 HF 研究中最常被低估的一點。組合產品的真實使用者往往是：

  • 患者本身（居家自行注射生物製劑：阿達木單抗、依那西普、胰島素）
  • 家屬/照護者（幫孩子打 EpiPen 的父母、協助注射的配偶）
  • 非專業照護者（體育教練使用 EpiPen、學校護理師使用應急裝置）

這些人沒有受過醫療訓練，對裝置可能完全陌生，而且在壓力或緊急情況下操作——這與醫院裡的受訓護士完全不同。

比喻：HF 研究的使用者選擇好比臨床試驗的受試者入選

就像臨床試驗不能只用健康年輕人來代表所有患者，HF 研究也不能只找方便招募的大學生或護理系學生來代表「所有使用者」。

你需要找真正有代表性的人群：關節炎患者（握力差）、老年患者（視力下降、精細動作退化）、初次使用者（認知負荷高）、其他產品的使用者（可能帶有錯誤習慣）。

使用環境的現實：居家環境有多糟糕

FDA 明確列出居家使用的環境挑戰：

  • 光線不足：浴室、臥室可能照明昏暗
  • 噪音分心：電視聲、孩子哭聲
  • 溫度變化：未正確保存的藥品（從冰箱取出後需回溫）
  • 混藥風險：儲藏在同一個地方的家人藥品、寵物藥品
  • 焦慮與壓力：過敏休克急救情境下的高壓操作

HF 研究必須模擬這些真實條件——在完美的實驗室環境下測試出來的成功率，無法代表真實世界。

思考練習

  1. 一個新的 adalimumab（阿達木單抗）自動注射器要上市。現有市場上已有 Humira Pen。這些「有相似產品使用經驗」的患者，在 HF 研究中應該被分為獨立族群嗎？為什麼？
  2. EpiPen 是緊急過敏救治用裝置，使用者可能是恐慌狀態下的家長。HF 研究的使用環境模擬應該如何設計？能完全在實驗室裡重現嗎？

III.B.3 — Training　訓練的角色與限制

原文內容 Original Text

Training is often proposed as a way to mitigate or control risks. However, before determining if training is appropriate for the combination product, first it is important to eliminate risks that are inherent to the product design. If there are residual risks, the next step is to determine if training is needed.

For example, for a new product that is developed as similar to or an alternative to a currently marketed product with use techniques that are well understood by the users, then training may not be necessary. Such an example might be a prefilled syringe with a staked needle for use by a health care professional. On the other hand, if there are residual risks for which training may be appropriate, the next step is to consider whether there is an opportunity for training, and if so, whether there is an expectation that training will routinely and consistently occur, before the first use of the combination product. In cases where training would be appropriate but is not expected to routinely or consistently occur, the HF study should evaluate the user interface in the absence of training.

For combination products when training is expected or needed to control or mitigate residual use-related hazards, it is important to determine what the training is likely to encompass and how it will be performed, who is responsible for conducting the training, and how to ensure consistency in the training method.

Consider, for example, a combination product being developed for a hospital-based surgical procedure. A risk analysis might determine that HCP training is required prior to the first use of the product to minimize the risk of errors related to assembling all the combination product constituent parts, preparing the treatment area and the surgical device constituent part before beginning the procedure, administering the drug constituent part(s), monitoring patient responses after using the product, and/or managing interactions across multiple users during the procedure. Due to the nature of the product and its use environment, all users would be expected to receive training before using the product. The HF study would evaluate the adequacy of the training in minimizing these potential risks.

In addition, when considering training to mitigate residual risks, it is important to consider how frequently the training will occur, as well as the length of time between the training session(s) and product use. For some combination products, training and first product use is separated by days, weeks, or months. Retention of information from the first, and possibly the only, training a user receives can decrease over time (i.e., training decay). For example, for a combination product designed for once a week self-injection, post-training information retention one week later can be anticipated to be lower than it would one hour later. If the risk analysis shows that training decay is a source of use-related error, then the HF study design should evaluate the effect of training decay. The HF Validation study should simulate the effect training decay may have on the users.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

FDA 的核心立場：訓練不能取代設計

這是 FDA 在 HF 領域最重要的政策立場之一：

風險控制的優先順序（類似工業安全的層次控制）：

  1. 設計消除（Eliminate）：讓問題在設計上根本不可能發生（防呆設計）
  2. 設計緩解（Mitigate via design）：讓設計本身降低錯誤的嚴重性或可能性
  3. 訓練 / 標籤：只有在設計無法完全解決後，才能用訓練作為補充緩解措施

FDA 不接受的說法：「這個設計問題，我們會用訓練來教使用者如何避免。」如果設計讓人容易出錯，訓練就不是有效的緩解手段。

比喻：設計 vs. 訓練的哲學差異

想像一個插座設計得讓孩子很容易把手指插進去。廠商說：「我們會訓練孩子不要插。」這顯然不夠——正確的做法是改設計（加裝安全蓋，或改變插孔形狀）。

同理，一個自動注射器如果針蓋設計得不夠直覺，患者在緊張時很容易移除失敗——廠商不能只靠說明書教。設計本身就必須讓移除動作清晰明確、不易出錯。

核心概念：「訓練衰退（Training Decay）」是法規考量點

這個概念在台灣 QA 實務中比較少被提及，但 FDA 明確要求納入考量：

場景：患者接受自動注射器使用訓練 → 一週後第一次在家自行注射。這一週內訓練記憶會衰退，尤其是步驟多、操作不常見的任務。

HF 研究應對措施：在 HF Validation Study 設計中，刻意在訓練與模擬使用之間設置時間間隔（例如數小時到數天），以模擬真實世界的訓練衰退效應。

這對 CDMO 提供的訓練材料設計也有影響——如果客戶的訓練材料設計不當，CDMO 有責任反映這個問題。

CDMO 實務場景

客戶委託 CDMO 灌製一款生物相似藥自動注射器，並要求 CDMO 提供使用訓練影片製作支援。

CDMO QA 應該向客戶確認：

  • 訓練影片是否被列為「User Interface」的一部分（若是，Validation Study 需涵蓋）
  • 訓練是否預期在每次使用前都會執行，還是只有首次使用時執行一次？
  • 患者群體是否包含無法可靠執行訓練計畫的族群（如認知功能受損的老年患者）？
  • 如果後續修改訓練影片（即使是微小改動），是否需要重新評估 HF Validation？

III.C — Human Factors Formative Studies　人因工程形成性研究

原文內容 Original Text

HF Formative studies are designed to evaluate early combination product prototypes, taking into consideration the identified use-related hazards. HF Formative study results guide prototype design changes to eliminate or mitigate use-related hazards identified during the product development process. The use of iterative HF Formative studies optimizes the design of the combination product user interface for safety, and minimizes the risk of first discovering use problems during late stages of development (e.g., during an HF Validation study, during a major clinical study, or after finalizing commercial plans).

Iterative HF Formative studies and related design modifications are performed until the user interface design appears to be sufficiently optimized for safety and ready for HF Validation testing. Iterative modifications to the user interface may include changes to the physical design attributes, changes to the packaging and labeling (including instructions for use) and changes to a training program.

The results of HF Formative studies should inform the design of the final finished combination product. None of the individual subjects in the HF Formative studies should participate in the HF Validation studies to avoid the potential for bias.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Formative Study 是「設計工具」，不是「法規文件」

很多人誤解 Formative Study 的用途。它的功能是：

  • 在產品設計早期就找出使用問題
  • 驗證設計修改是否有效解決問題
  • 積累對使用者行為的理解，以設計更好的 Validation 研究

Formative Study 的資料不是用來通過法規審查的——它是內部設計迭代的工具。但 FDA 可能在審查期間要求查閱這些資料，用以評估廠商是否盡職進行了充分的設計優化。

比喻：Formative Study 是「設計草稿」，Validation 是「最終答卷」

就像建築師在設計建築時會畫無數張草圖、做縮尺模型、請客戶試用——這些都是 Formative 階段。最終交出去的設計圖才是要審批的正式文件。

同樣，廠商可能做 3～5 輪 Formative Study（每輪 5～15 個使用者），每輪發現問題、修改設計、再測試。只有確認設計成熟後，才做 Validation（通常 15～30 個使用者，依族群而定）。

重要規則：Formative 和 Validation 的受試者不能重疊

FDA 明確要求：參加過 Formative Study 的受試者不能再參加 Validation Study。

原因很直接：做過 Formative 的人已經有「學習效應」——他們知道裝置設計的一些細節，可能在 Validation 時表現得比真實使用者好，造成偏差（bias）。

這對 CDMO 的啟示：如果客戶在開發過程中用了某些受試者群體，這些人必須被追蹤排除在後續的 Validation 招募之外。這是研究設計中的嚴格要求。

思考練習

  1. 一個 Formative Study 發現：25% 的患者無法在 10 秒內正確移除針蓋。設計團隊應該採取什麼行動？僅靠改說明書可以解決嗎？
  2. Formative Study 的結果報告應該保存多久？如果 FDA 在 NDA 審查期間要求查閱，廠商需要提供什麼？

III.D — Human Factors Validation Studies　人因工程驗證研究

原文內容 Original Text

HF Validation studies demonstrate that the final finished combination product user interface would maximize the likelihood that the product will be safely and effectively used by intended users, for the intended uses in the intended use environments. There are two types of HF Validation studies: HF Simulated-Use and HF Actual-Use Validation. For most combination products, FDA expects that a HF Simulated-Use Validation study will be sufficient to assess the adequacy of the user interface.

III.D.1 — HF Simulated-Use Validation Studies

The HF Simulated-Use Validation study focuses on confirming that the design of the final finished combination product (i.e., after iterative prototype design changes) user interface adequately mitigates or eliminates the identified use-related risks. Simulation methods for these studies vary and may include the use of a manikin, injection pads, placebo, and other elements intended to simulate the patient, the procedure, or the environment of use.

The conditions of the HF Simulated-Use Validation study should be sufficiently realistic so that the results represent relevant aspects of actual use of the product once introduced into the market. Tasks to be performed in the HF Simulated-Use Validation study should include those critical tasks identified in a use-related risk analysis that may be associated with user interface problems. The study design should provide for the identification of any unanticipated hazards or unexpected use behaviors that were not previously identified.

III.D.2 — HF Actual-Use Validation Studies

As noted above, FDA expects that for most combination products, a HF Simulated-Use Validation study will be sufficient to assess the adequacy of the user interface design. However, there are rare circumstances when it is difficult to simulate the conditions of use, physical characteristics of the product, or environment of use. Thus, a HF Actual-Use Validation study may be needed to confirm the adequacy of the user interface design.

HF Actual-Use Validation studies either (1) use the final finished combination product (including the drug, not a placebo) in a simulated use setting or (2) use the final finished combination product in a real (not simulated) environment of use.

  • A HF Actual-Use Validation study of the combination product that includes the actual drug in a simulated use setting may be necessary when the drug can affect the user's ability to perform a critical task. For example, for a drug that causes coughing on inhalation which could result in incomplete dosing, inhaler designs to minimize the risk of not completing an inhalation could not be evaluated without use of the actual drug.
  • The other type of HF Actual-Use Validation study is in a real environment of use. For example, based on the hazard analysis and results of an HF Simulated-Use Validation, it may be appropriate to evaluate in a real environment use-related risks associated with a complex combination product intended for use in crisis/emergency settings or with a combination product that has a complex operating procedure. In these instances, the user's tasks could be influenced by the presence of noise, rapidly changing circumstances, distractions, etc.

FDA recommends that applicants for combination products discuss with FDA the availability of simulation techniques and whether HF Simulated-Use Validation and HF Actual-Use Validation studies are needed to evaluate the user interface.

Regardless of the type of HF Validation study, if use errors or problems (e.g., failures, "close calls," use difficulties, and/or new findings) are identified in an HF Validation study, these should be evaluated to (1) identify the root cause(s), (2) determine the potential for harm (including the clinical significance of such errors or problems and the potential for compromised medical treatment), and (3) determine whether additional measures to eliminate or mitigate hazards are necessary.

If the HF Validation study shows that additional measures are necessary to address the risk of failures that are deemed clinically significant, then the HF Validation study will be considered failed. Changes to the user interface may be needed to eliminate or mitigate hazards and a new HF Validation study should be performed to evaluate the changes.

Also, if the product design changes or the user population changes, then the completed HF Validation study may or may not be applicable to the design change. A use-related risk analysis should be completed and, dependent upon the findings of the risk analysis, a new HF Validation study may be advisable. If the product design remains unchanged but the applicant seeks to add a new user population, then as applicable, a new use-related risk analysis and new HF Validation study should be performed.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Validation Study 是法規里程碑

HF Validation Study 是整個 HF 流程的最終法規門檻。它需要滿足四個關鍵條件：

  • 代表性使用者：招募的受試者必須真正代表預期的使用族群（包括最困難的次族群）
  • 代表性使用條件：模擬真實使用環境（居家、診所、緊急情境）
  • 生產等效裝置：必須使用等同於最終上市版本的裝置（或非常接近的版本）
  • 無指導原則：研究過程中不能給受試者提示或糾正——觀察自然行為

如果 Validation Study 失敗，不是簡單的「補救措施」就能過——需要修改設計，然後重新做整個 Validation。

Validation 失敗的後果：必須重做，不能只靠 CAPA

如果 Validation Study 發現臨床顯著的使用錯誤，FDA 的立場是：

  1. Validation Study 判定為「失敗」
  2. 必須修改 User Interface（設計、標籤、說明書）
  3. 必須做新的 Validation Study

這意味著：在 Validation 階段才發現問題，代價非常高——時間延誤、成本倍增。這是為什麼 Formative Study 的迭代如此重要：問題越早發現，越容易修正。

設計變更後的 Validation 有效性問題

FDA 明確說明：如果產品設計改變或使用族群改變，現有的 Validation Study 可能不再有效。以下情況需要重新評估：

  • 產品設計改變（即使是看起來微小的改動）→ 需要重新做 Use-Related Risk Analysis，再決定是否需要新的 Validation
  • 增加新的使用族群（如從 HCP 擴展到 Lay Users）→ 需要新的 Risk Analysis 和 Validation

對 CDMO 而言：如果 CDMO 更改了填充量、柱塞推力、針頭保護機制——即使客戶認為「差不多」——也可能讓已完成的 HF Validation 失效。這必須在合約中明確釐清責任。

CDMO 實務場景：生產等效裝置的責任

客戶在 HF Validation Study 中需要使用「生產等效裝置（Production-Equivalent Device）」。CDMO 作為組合產品的填充製造商，可能需要提供這批裝置。

CDMO 必須確保提供的裝置符合：

  • 填充量：與最終上市規格完全一致
  • 填充精度：在規格範圍內，不能有明顯偏差
  • 柱塞推力 (Glide Force)：直接影響使用者能否順利按壓注射
  • 針頭保護機制：安全針蓋的啟動力道和設計
  • 外觀與手感：與最終上市品相同（不能用不同顏色、不同材質的測試品）

如果 CDMO 後來因為工藝最佳化而改變了上述任何參數（例如調整配方導致黏度變化 → 柱塞力改變），需要立即通知客戶並評估對 HF Validation 有效性的影響。

思考練習

  1. 一個 Simulated-Use Validation Study 進行了 25 名受試者測試，其中 3 人未能成功完成「確認給藥完成」這個 Critical Task。Root Cause 分析顯示是裝置上的觀察窗太小，老年患者看不清楚。下一步應該怎麼做？
  2. 某藥廠完成了針對 HCP 族群的 Validation Study，現在想要讓 Lay Users 也能使用同一裝置在家自行注射。這份 Validation Study 還有效嗎？需要什麼額外步驟？

III.E — Human Factors Knowledge Task Studies　知識任務研究

原文內容 Original Text

In situations when the understanding of the information provided in a combination product's labels or labeling is a critical task to using a product safely and effectively, a study to assess the user's understanding of such information (Knowledge Task study) is appropriate. Knowledge Task studies may occur as part of the HF formative, or HF validation process.

However, in comparison to other types of HF studies in which critical task performance is assessed by observing user interaction with the product, Knowledge Task studies focus on the understanding and interpretation of important information in the user interface that will be applied to make use-related decisions. The users' understanding of the labeling is evaluated by questioning test participants and assessing whether the information has been understood.

Knowledge Task studies may focus on particular aspects of labeling. For example, a Knowledge Task study could evaluate:

  • HCP's understanding of their roles and responsibilities when introducing a combination product as part of a new procedure, or associated with complex medical/surgical procedures that involve many different HCPs;
  • The user's ability to select the appropriate task from a lengthy set of instructions that include different options;
  • The user's understanding of how to identify defective or expired product;
  • The user's awareness and understanding of the combination product's pertinent safety information provided in the instructions for use;
  • The user's ability to recognize clinical signs, identified in the instructions for use, that would prompt medical attention — e.g., shortness of breath, allergic reaction, weakness, signs of disease progression; or
  • The user's understanding of the diagrams provided in the labeling.

Certain types of Knowledge Task studies are also used in the development of non-prescription products. Generally, these are quantitative studies that evaluate whether results are statistically significant.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Knowledge Task Study 測的是「理解」，不是「操作」

這是 HF 研究體系中一個容易被忽略的組成部分。普通的 HF 研究測試「使用者能不能正確做」——觀察行為。

Knowledge Task Study 測試「使用者是否理解」——通過問卷和訪談評估認知。

典型的 Knowledge Task 問題：

  • 「在使用這個自動注射器後，您應該等多久才能移除裝置？」
  • 「如果您在注射後發現皮膚出現紅腫，您應該怎麼做？」
  • 「請找出說明書中關於儲存溫度的說明。」
  • 「這張圖示顯示的步驟是什麼意思？」

比喻：操作考試 vs. 筆試

駕駛執照考試包含兩個部分：路考（實際操作）和筆試（知識理解）。

HF 研究也類似：

  • Simulated-Use Study = 路考（觀察你能否正確開車）
  • Knowledge Task Study = 筆試（測試你是否理解交通規則、危險情境辨識）

兩者可以分開進行，也可以整合在同一個 HF 研究中。

何時特別需要 Knowledge Task Study？

以下情況下，Knowledge Task Study 的重要性大幅提升：

  • 複雜的多步驟說明書：如需要重組的凍乾粉末組合套件，步驟多且容易混淆
  • 條件式指示：「如果出現 X 症狀，執行步驟 Y；否則執行步驟 Z」
  • 安全警示理解：使用者需要能辨識過敏反應、注射部位感染等需要就醫的徵兆
  • 多個 HCP 分工合作的程序：如手術室中藥師、護士、外科醫師各自負責不同步驟
  • 圖示說明的理解：特別是在多語言市場，圖示理解的文化差異可能導致錯誤

CDMO 實務場景：標籤設計的 HF 影響

CDMO 通常負責（或協助）組合產品的標籤和外包裝設計。以下標籤設計問題可能在 Knowledge Task Study 中被發現：

  • 使用說明的步驟圖示不夠清晰（老年患者在昏暗光線下看不清楚編號）
  • 儲存條件說明（「2-8°C 冷藏；使用前回溫 30 分鐘」）患者理解度不足
  • 用藥後的注意事項（「注射後 24 小時不可接種疫苗」）患者看漏了
  • 批號/有效期標示的位置讓患者混淆（特別是多劑型的注射筆）

如果客戶在 Knowledge Task Study 中發現標籤問題，CDMO 可能需要協助修改標籤設計並重新印刷——這會影響生產時程和成本。因此，CDMO 應在標籤設計初稿階段就提供 HF 角度的審查意見。

綜合思考練習

  1. 一個用於類風濕性關節炎的生物製劑 PFS 正在進行 HF 研究計畫。請設計一個包含 Formative、Knowledge Task、和 Simulated-Use Validation 三個階段的完整 HF 計畫框架，說明各階段的目的、樣本數量、使用族群、以及成功標準。
  2. 某款自動注射器的 HF Validation Study 的結果顯示：所有 Critical Tasks 的通過率都超過 90%，但有 2 名患者在「識別何時需要就醫」的 Knowledge Task 上失敗。FDA 會如何評估這個結果？
  3. 作為 CDMO QA 經理，客戶告知你：他們計畫在 HF Validation Study 使用你們工廠生產的第一批樣品，而這批樣品使用了與最終商業批不同的注射器主體供應商（另一家供應商的玻璃注射器）。你應該建議客戶怎麼做？
