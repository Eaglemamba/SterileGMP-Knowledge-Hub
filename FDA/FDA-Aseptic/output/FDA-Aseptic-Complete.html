Section I — Introduction 導論

原文內容 Original Text

This guidance is intended to help manufacturers meet current good manufacturing practice (CGMP) regulations under 21 CFR parts 210 and 211 when manufacturing sterile drug and biological products using aseptic processing. This document replaces the 1987 Aseptic Processing Guideline.

For NDA, ANDA, and BLA products, this guidance should be read in conjunction with the Guideline for the Submission of Documentation for Sterilization Process Validation.

FDA's guidance documents do not establish legally enforceable responsibilities. Instead, guidances describe the Agency's current thinking on a topic and should be viewed only as recommendations, unless specific regulatory or statutory requirements are cited. The use of the word "should" in Agency guidances means that something is suggested or recommended, but not required.

CFR text boxes are included in this guidance to aid readers in identifying applicable regulatory requirements.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：這份指引是什麼？

本文件是 FDA 在 2004 年發布的正式指引，取代了舊版的 1987 年無菌製程指引。 它的核心目的是協助廠商理解 FDA 對 無菌製程 (Aseptic Processing) 的現行期望，並對應到 CGMP (現行良好製造規範) 法規條文（21 CFR parts 210 和 211）的具體要求。

對 CDMO 而言，這是接受客戶委託生產無菌製劑時，必須熟讀的基礎文件。 無論客戶的品牌藥（NDA）、學名藥（ANDA）、或生物製劑（BLA）， 本指引都是稽核與技術評估的共同語言。

類比：「Should」vs 強制要求——紅綠燈 vs 建議路線

想像你開車出門：紅燈是法律強制規定，違反就會被罰； 而導航 app 給你的「建議路線」雖然不強制，但若你偏離，出了事情就得自己解釋。

FDA 指引中的「should」正是建議路線的概念—— 技術上不強制，但若你選擇不遵循，稽核時你必須能提出同等效果的替代做法， 否則 FDA 查廠時很可能將其列為缺失。 換句話說，對有經驗的 CDMO 而言，「should」在實務上幾乎等於「must」。

注意：法規文字箱 (CFR Text Boxes) 的意義

本指引內嵌的 CFR 文字箱（法規原文方框）是刻意設計的提醒裝置。 當你看到 CFR 文字箱時，代表接下來的內容已從「建議」升格為 21 CFR 強制規定，具法律效力。 閱讀本指引時，務必區分一般段落（建議性）與 CFR 文字箱（強制性）的不同層級。

Section II-A — Background: Regulatory Framework 背景：法規框架

原文內容 Original Text

A. Regulatory Framework

This guidance pertains to CGMP regulations under 21 CFR parts 210 and 211 for the manufacture of sterile drug and biological products using aseptic processing.

Supplementary requirements for biological products are found in 21 CFR parts 600–680.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：法規層次架構

美國對無菌藥品的監管採取「雙軌並行」的法規架構：

  • 21 CFR parts 210 & 211：適用於所有藥品的 CGMP 基礎規定，包含廠房設施、設備確效、製程控制、品管等通用要求。
  • 21 CFR parts 600–680：專門針對生物製劑（如疫苗、血液製品、基因治療）的額外要求，由 CBER 監管。

一般小分子無菌針劑主要適用 210/211；生物製劑（如 mAb 注射劑）則需同時符合兩套法規。 身為 CDMO，接不同類型客戶委託時，要確認適用的法規範疇，才能正確規劃 GMP 策略。

類比：法規層次如同建築法規

21 CFR 210/211 就像城市通用的「建築基準法」，所有建築物都要遵守； 而 21 CFR 600–680 就像針對醫院或核電廠的「特殊建築附加規範」， 在一般標準之上額外要求更高規格。 若你的 CDMO 同時承接一般注射劑與生物製劑，就等於在同一棟廠房裡同時遵守兩套建築法規， 設計與管理的複雜度自然更高。

注意：CDER vs CBER 的監管分工

FDA 內部，CDER（藥品評估研究中心）負責監管大多數藥品（含生物類似藥）， CBER（生物製劑評估研究中心）則監管疫苗、血液製品及部分細胞/基因治療產品。 CDMO 在規劃稽核應對與文件策略時，需確認客戶產品的主管機關是哪一個中心。

Section II-B — Background: Technical Framework 背景：技術框架

原文內容 Original Text

B. Technical Framework

Terminal sterilization involves filling and sealing product containers under high-quality environmental conditions. Products are not initially sterile, but the final sealed container undergoes a sterilization process (e.g., heat or irradiation). High assurance of sterility is achieved by thoroughly validating the sterilization process parameters and the absence of significant variation in the manufacturing process. This approach offers a high level of sterility assurance, limiting the possibility of error.

Aseptic processing involves separate sterilization of the drug product, container, and closure, followed by their aseptic assembly. Because the product cannot be terminally sterilized in its final container, each of the components and the dosage form must be individually sterilized and protected from contamination throughout the entire filling process.

For example, the glass containers may be subjected to dry heat; the rubber closures to moist heat (autoclaving); and liquid dosage forms to filtration through a sterilizing-grade filter. Each of these processes requires validation and stringent control to provide an extremely high-quality environment.

Any manual or mechanical manipulation of sterilized items prior to or during aseptic assembly poses a contamination risk. Unlike terminal sterilization, aseptic processing involves considerably more variables that must be controlled.

A significant proportion of all recalls attributable to lack of sterility assurance during the period 1980–2000 involved products produced by aseptic processing.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

類比：兩種滅菌策略的直覺理解

最終滅菌 (Terminal Sterilization) 就像外科醫師使用完手術器械後，將整包器械送進高壓蒸汽鍋滅菌—— 操作過程不需要無塵室，因為最終整包一起高溫殺菌，風險由滅菌步驟統一消除。

無菌製程 (Aseptic Processing) 則像外科醫師在手術前先對每一件器械個別滅菌， 然後在超潔淨的手術室內小心組裝—— 每一個動作、每一個人員、每一次開關艙門都是潛在的污染風險， 全程需要極致的環境控制與操作紀律。

哪個方式更安全？當然是最終滅菌。因此 FDA 明確表示： 只有當最終滅菌不可行時，才使用無菌製程。

核心概念：無菌製程的三個分流滅菌路徑

由於最終容器無法進行滅菌，每個組成要素必須走自己的滅菌路徑：

  • 玻璃容器 (Glass Containers)：乾熱滅菌 (Dry Heat) — 高溫烘箱，同時去除熱原 (Depyrogenation)
  • 橡膠塞 (Rubber Closures)：濕熱滅菌 (Moist Heat / Autoclaving) — 蒸汽高壓，確保高度無菌保證水準
  • 液體藥品 (Liquid Dosage Forms)：過濾滅菌 (Filtration) — 通過 0.22 µm 除菌過濾器去除微生物

三條路徑匯流於無菌填充間 (Aseptic Fill Room)，在 ISO 5（A/B 級）環境下完成最終組裝。 每一條路徑都必須獨立驗證，任何一個環節失效，無菌保證即受威脅。

重要警示：1980–2000 年的召回教訓

FDA 特別指出，1980–2000 年間因無菌保證不足而召回的產品中， 絕大多數均為無菌製程生產。這個歷史數據不是偶然—— 它深刻反映了無菌製程固有的高複雜度與高風險性。

對 CDMO 而言，這個數據意味著：無菌製程的 GMP 系統必須建立在 預防性設計而非事後補救的基礎上。 一旦召回，不僅是客戶的損失，更是 CDMO 商譽的重大打擊。

CDMO 業務應用場景

當 CDMO 業務團隊向客戶介紹無菌製造能力時，最常被問到的問題是： 「你們能做最終滅菌嗎？」這個問題背後有深刻的商業邏輯—— 客戶知道最終滅菌的無菌保證更高、召回風險更低、法規稽核壓力更小。

若 CDMO 只有無菌製程能力，必須能清楚說明： (1) 為何該品項不適合最終滅菌（如蛋白質熱穩定性問題）； (2) 你的無菌製程如何透過環境控制、工藝設計、媒體填充模擬試驗 (Media Fill) 等手段補償這個固有風險。 這是技術銷售的關鍵說服點。

Section III — Scope 範圍

原文內容 Original Text

This guidance covers selected issues in aseptic processing, but does not address every aspect of the subject. It focuses primarily on finished drug product CGMP issues. This guidance updates the 1987 guideline with respect to:

  • Personnel qualification
  • Cleanroom design
  • Process design
  • Quality control
  • Environmental monitoring
  • Production records review

The use of isolators for aseptic processing is also discussed. This guidance does not address terminal sterilization of drug products.

A key principle underlying this guidance is that sterile drugs should be manufactured using aseptic processing only when terminal sterilization is not feasible.

Some final packaging may afford unique advantages not achievable with terminal sterilization (e.g., some dual-chamber syringes); in such cases, the manufacturer can explore adjunct processing steps that may augment sterility assurance of the product. A list of references is included.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：本指引的六大更新主題

相較於 1987 年舊版，本指引針對以下六大領域進行了系統性更新， 每一項都對應到 CDMO 日常 GMP 管理的重要模組：

  • 人員資格確認 (Personnel Qualification)：gowning 評估、無菌技術訓練、行為控制
  • 潔淨室設計 (Cleanroom Design)：HVAC 系統、氣流型式、壓差梯度、潔淨室分級
  • 製程設計 (Process Design)：填充工藝設計、人員動線、設備配置風險評估
  • 品質控制 (Quality Control)：無菌測試、培養基模擬填充 (Media Fill)、過濾完整性
  • 環境監控 (Environmental Monitoring)：微粒計數、微生物取樣、趨勢分析
  • 生產記錄審查 (Production Records Review)：批次記錄完整性、異常事件調查

這六大主題構成了本指引後續各章節的核心架構。後面的學習都圍繞這些主題展開。

重要原則：無菌製程是「最後手段」

FDA 明確強調：當最終滅菌不可行時，才使用無菌製程。 這不只是技術建議，而是貫穿整份指引的設計哲學。

實際上，對許多蛋白質藥物、脂質體、mRNA 製劑而言， 高溫滅菌會破壞活性成分，最終滅菌確實不可行。 但 FDA 要求廠商必須事先評估並記錄「為何無法採用最終滅菌」， 不能未經評估就直接採用無菌製程。這個評估是 CDMO 技術方案文件的必要組成。

類比：雙腔室注射器的特殊地位

雙腔室注射器 (Dual-Chamber Syringes) 是一個有趣的特例—— 就像「夾心餅乾」一樣，兩種成分分開存放，使用前才混合。 這種設計從包裝結構上就使最終滅菌不可行，因為兩腔的成分可能對滅菌條件有不同承受能力。

對這類特殊包裝，FDA 並非完全不允許，而是要求廠商探索 輔助加工步驟 (Adjunct Processing Steps)—— 例如對局部可耐受滅菌的組件仍盡可能進行最終滅菌， 以在整體上增強無菌保證水準。這體現了 FDA 風險導向的監管思維。

隔離器 (Isolator) 的出現：新時代的無菌屏障技術

1987 年的舊版指引中，隔離器技術尚未成熟。 2004 年版本特別將隔離器 (Isolator)納入討論， 因為它提供了比傳統潔淨室更高的人員-環境隔離水準—— 操作者完全在隔離屏障之外，透過手套箱 (Glove Box) 操作， 大幅降低人員帶入污染的風險。

隔離器技術現已成為業界提升無菌保證的重要工具， 後續章節將有專節討論其設計與確效要求。

CDMO 業務應用場景

當 CDMO 接受新客戶技術評估時，了解本指引的「範圍聲明」有重要的業務含義：

(1) 能力評估對應：本指引的六大更新主題，正好是 FDA 稽核時最關注的六個領域， 也是 CDMO 能力展示的核心清單。業務開發時，這六個面向的能力証明文件是贏得客戶信任的關鍵。

本節重點回顧 Section Summary

  • 法規地位：本指引取代 1987 年版，幫助廠商理解 FDA 對無菌製程的現行期望；指引中的「should」表示建議而非強制，但實務上等同業界標準。
  • 法規框架：無菌製程 CGMP 的主要法規依據為 21 CFR 210 & 211；生物製劑另需符合 21 CFR 600–680；CFR 文字箱代表強制性規定。
  • 最終滅菌 vs 無菌製程：最終滅菌的無菌保證更高、變數更少；無菌製程需對藥品、容器、塞子分別滅菌，再於高潔淨度環境下組裝，召回風險歷史上較高。
  • 三條滅菌路徑：玻璃容器（乾熱）、橡膠塞（濕熱/高壓蒸汽）、液態製劑（過濾滅菌），每條路徑均需獨立驗證。
  • 核心原則：無菌製程是「最後手段」——只有當最終滅菌不可行時才使用；廠商需記錄此評估依據。
  • 本指引涵蓋：人員資格確認、潔淨室設計、製程設計、品質控制、環境監控、生產記錄審查六大主題，以及隔離器技術；不涵蓋最終滅菌。
  • CDMO 含義：了解以上框架有助於技術能力展示、新品項可行性評估、設施升級決策，以及稽核風險管理。

Section IV — Buildings and Facilities (Overview)

Separate or defined areas in an aseptic processing facility should be appropriately controlled to attain different degrees of air quality. IV.A — Critical Area: Class 100 (ISO 5) / 關鍵區域

IV.A — Critical Area (Class 100 / ISO 5)

Definition and Regulatory Basis

A critical area is one in which the sterilized drug product, containers, and closures are exposed. The primary objective of this environment is to maintain the sterility of components as required by 21 CFR 211.42(c)(10).

Activities in critical areas include:

  • Aseptic connections
  • Sterile ingredient additions
  • Filling operations
  • Closing/stoppering operations

The exposed product is vulnerable and will not be subsequently sterilized in its immediate container. Any contamination introduced at this stage cannot be remediated.

Particle Quality Requirements

No more than 3,520 particles ≥ 0.5 µm per cubic meter at sites not more than 1 foot from the work site, within the airflow, during filling and closing operations. Measurements should be taken at the highest risk sites. Regular monitoring shall occur during each production shift.

A remote counting system is recommended — this approach is less invasive and more comprehensive than portable equipment that requires an operator in proximity to the work site.

Some operations (e.g., powder filling) generate product-intrinsic particles that do not pose a contamination risk. In such cases, air can be sampled to characterize extrinsic particle contamination separately from product particles.

Airflow: Unidirectional Flow

HEPA-filtered air should be supplied at a velocity sufficient to sweep particles away from the filling/closing area, forming a unidirectional airflow pattern (also called laminar flow). Typical velocity:

  • 0.45 m/s (90 ft/min) ± 20%

Proper design prevents turbulence and stagnant air zones. Turbulence is a key mechanism by which lower-quality ambient air can be entrained into the critical zone.

In Situ Air Pattern Analysis

In situ air pattern analysis (smoke studies / airflow visualization) should be conducted under dynamic conditions — with personnel present, equipment operating, and production activities simulated. Videotape is useful for assessing and documenting airflow patterns. This analysis confirms that unidirectional flow is maintained and that no stagnant zones exist near exposed product.

"Air monitoring samples of critical areas should normally yield no microbiological contaminants."

中文導讀 — 關鍵區域（Class 100 / ISO 5）

Critical Area是整個無菌製造廠房中最重要的區域——藥品、容器、密封件在此暴露，且後續不會再次滅菌。一旦此處受到污染，批次就必須拒絕。FDA在查廠時，對關鍵區域的審查最為嚴格。

關鍵區域的三個核心要求

  • 粒子要求：≥0.5 µm 粒子不超過 3,520 /m³，等同ISO 5標準，量測位置距離工作點不超過1英尺
  • 微生物要求：正常情況下不得偵測到微生物（行動水準 1 cfu/m³，但實務目標為零）
  • 氣流要求：單向流（Unidirectional Flow），流速 0.45 m/s（90 ft/min）±20%

比喻：無塵工廠的精密晶片生產

Class 100潔淨區就像半導體廠的晶片曝光室——晶圓在此被刻印電路，任何一粒灰塵都可能毀掉整片晶圓。廠商投入數億元打造超潔淨環境，且操作人員在進入前必須完整更衣。同理，無菌填充區投入的工程成本，正是為了保護那瓶無法「重來」的藥品。

單向流（Unidirectional Flow）的物理原理

HEPA過濾後的空氣以均勻速度（0.45 m/s）向下「掃除」粒子，形成一道「空氣簾」保護暴露的藥品。關鍵點：

  • 速度太低（<0.36 m/s）→ 無法有效掃除，粒子可能滯留
  • 速度太高（>0.54 m/s）→ 氣流擾動過大，反而可能產生渦流
  • 煙霧試驗（Smoke Study）在動態條件下執行，確認沒有「死角」或渦流

遠端計數系統 vs. 手持式計數器

FDA明確推薦使用遠端採樣系統（Remote Counting System），原因是：手持式計數器需要操作人員靠近工作區，操作員本身就是粒子和微生物的來源，會干擾量測結果的代表性。遠端系統可在操作員不在場的情況下持續監測，數據更客觀、更全面。

粉末灌裝（如凍乾粉針）產生的「產品本身粒子」不代表污染風險，可設計分開的採樣策略來區分外源性粒子。

查廠焦點：Smoke Study的常見缺失

FDA 483觀察報告中，Smoke Study（氣流可視化研究）的常見缺失包括：只在靜態（無人員）條件下執行、未覆蓋所有填充位置、影像記錄不完整或解析度不足。建議在驗證計畫中明確定義動態條件的定義（人員數量、設備狀態、模擬操作內容）。

IV.B — Supporting Clean Areas / 支撐潔淨區

IV.B — Supporting Clean Areas

Various supporting areas exist within an aseptic processing facility, each with different classifications and functions. Many support areas are used to prepare, hold, or transfer:

  • Nonsterile components and formulated products
  • In-process materials
  • Equipment
  • Containers and closures

Classification depends on the activities performed and the degree of exposure risk. FDA recommendations by zone:

Adjacent to Aseptic Processing Line

FDA recommends that the area immediately adjacent to the aseptic processing line meet at minimum Class 10,000 (ISO 7) under dynamic conditions. This area may alternatively be classified as Class 1,000 (ISO 6), or the entire aseptic filling room may be maintained at Class 100 (ISO 5).

Less Critical Activities

Class 100,000 (ISO 8) is appropriate for less critical support activities, such as:

  • Equipment cleaning and washing
  • Preparation of non-exposed components
  • Personnel gowning anteroom (in some designs)

The classification of each area must reflect the worst-case activities performed within it. Higher-risk activities in a room drive the classification requirement upward.

中文導讀 — 支撐潔淨區

無菌製造廠房不只有一個「最乾淨」的核心區，圍繞在核心區周圍的「支撐區」同樣需要依照風險程度進行分級管理。支撐區的主要功能是為關鍵區提供已清潔、已準備好的物料與設備。

支撐區分級邏輯

FDA的分級建議具有彈性，企業可以選擇更嚴格的標準：

  • 最低要求：緊鄰無菌填充線的區域，至少Class 10,000（ISO 7）
  • 升級選項A：緊鄰區升級為Class 1,000（ISO 6）
  • 升級選項B：整個無菌填充室維持Class 100（ISO 5）
  • 較低分級區：設備清洗等活動，Class 100,000（ISO 8）即可

選擇升級選項可降低操作風險，但需要更高的建設與運營成本。

比喻：手術室的緩衝走廊

手術室（Class 100）的外面通常有一條「潔淨走廊」（Class 10,000），再外面才是「半清潔走廊」（Class 100,000）。這種設計讓污染從外到內逐步被過濾與阻擋，而非直接從外部環境進入手術室。無菌製造廠房的「支撐潔淨區」設計理念完全相同。

分級決策的風險驅動原則

每個房間的分級必須反映其中風險最高的活動。如果一個Class 100,000的清洗間偶爾被用來暫存已滅菌但未密封的容器，那麼這個房間就需要重新評估其分級。FDA查廠時會核對房間用途與實際監測數據的一致性。

CDMO廠房規劃考量

對CDMO而言，支撐區的分級選擇直接影響廠房設計的彈性：整個填充室維持Class 100（選項B）可以簡化操作程序，但建設成本大幅提高；最低要求（ISO 7緊鄰區）雖然節省成本，但需要更嚴格的物料進出管制程序。多產品共用廠房（Multi-product Facility）的分級設計尤為複雜，必須考慮最嚴格產品的需求。

IV.C — Clean Area Separation / 潔淨區隔離

IV.C — Clean Area Separation

Pressure Differential Requirements

Adequate separation between areas of different cleanliness levels prevents contamination migration. Airflow moves from higher to lower cleanliness areas. A positive pressure differential of at least 10–15 Pascals (Pa) should be maintained between adjacent rooms of differing classification (with doors closed).

When doors are opened, outward airflow should be sufficient to minimize ingress of lower-quality air. Door open time must be strictly controlled.

For same-classification adjacent rooms, maintaining a pressure differential still provides beneficial directional separation. For an unclassified adjacent room, the aseptic processing room should maintain at least 12.5 Pa overpressure at all times.

If pressure drops below the minimum threshold, the environmental quality must be restored and confirmed before production continues.

Continuous Monitoring and Documentation

Pressure differentials shall be:

  • Monitored continuously throughout each shift
  • Frequently recorded
  • All alarms must be documented
  • All deviations must be investigated
Air Change Rate Requirements

Minimum air change rates by classification:

  • Class 100,000 (ISO 8): at least 20 air changes per hour
  • Class 10,000 (ISO 7) and Class 100 (ISO 5): higher rates are required (specific values determined by validation and design)

The facility monitoring system should be capable of rapidly detecting atypical changes in environmental conditions.

中文導讀 — 潔淨區隔離與壓差管理

壓差管理是無菌廠房防污染的「第一道防線」。只要不同分級的區域之間維持正確的壓差梯度，空氣就會自然從「乾淨」流向「較不乾淨」，而不是反向。

壓差要求的三個層次

  • 相鄰不同分級房間：至少 10–15 Pa（門關閉狀態）
  • 無菌處理室 vs. 非分級區域：至少 12.5 Pa 正壓（任何時候）
  • 開門時：向外氣流應能有效阻擋低品質空氣流入，必須嚴格控制開門時間

壓差一旦低於最低值，必須先恢復並確認環境品質後才能繼續生產——這是強制要求，不是建議。

比喻：飛機客艙的氣壓艙原理

飛機的機艙維持正壓（相對於外部大氣）防止外部冷空氣入侵；高鐵的月台門系統同樣利用壓差防止隧道風直接衝擊候車月台。無菌廠房的正壓差設計邏輯相同：潔淨室相對於走廊維持正壓，即使門縫有微小洩漏，氣流也是「向外推」，而非「向內吸」污染空氣。10–15 Pa 的壓差看起來微小，卻足以在門縫處形成穩定的「空氣牆」。

FDA查廠重點：壓差記錄的連續性

FDA查驗員在查廠時，必定會要求調閱壓差監測記錄（Pressure Differential Logs），包括：

  • 每班次的連續記錄圖表（不只是每小時一筆點位數據）
  • 所有警報（Alarm）記錄及其調查報告
  • 壓差偏低事件的影響評估（對相關批次的影響）

警報記錄不完整或偏差未調查，是最常見的483觀察報告項目之一。

換氣次數（Air Change Rate）快速參考

Class 100,000（ISO 8）最低要求：20 ACH（Air Changes per Hour）

ACH = 每小時總送風量（m³/hr）÷ 房間體積（m³）

Class 10,000（ISO 7）和Class 100（ISO 5）需要更高的換氣次數，具體值由驗證決定，通常ISO 7 ≥ 40–60 ACH，ISO 5層流工作站則由設計速度（0.45 m/s）決定，而非ACH。

CDMO運營視角：壓差監控系統選擇

連續壓差監測需要安裝差壓傳感器（Differential Pressure Transducers）並整合至樓宇管理系統（BMS）或環境監測系統（EMS）。傳感器的維護（校驗週期）和警報閾值設定直接影響合規性。部分CDMO會選擇與FDA 21 CFR Part 11合規的EMS，確保所有數據都有電子稽核軌跡（Audit Trail）。

IV.D — Air Filtration

D.1 — Membrane Filters (Compressed Gas)

Compressed gas used in cleanroom operations (e.g., purging, overlaying) must be of appropriate purity — free from oil. After filtration, the microbiological and particle quality of compressed gas must be equal to or better than the environment into which it is introduced.

Common gases used: air, nitrogen, CO₂.

FDA recommendations for membrane filters:

  • Sterile membrane filters required for compressed gas used in aseptic areas
  • Recommended for autoclave air lines
  • Recommended for lyophilizer vacuum breaks
  • Recommended for tanks containing sterilized materials
  • Sterilized holding tanks should be kept under positive pressure or sealed

Condensate control: Gas filters must be dry — condensate causes filter blockage or microbial growth. Hydrophobic filters are recommended to prevent moisture ingress.

Integrity testing: Recommend integrity testing upon installation and periodically thereafter. Filters should be replaced at defined intervals.

D.2 — HEPA Filters

HEPA filter integrity must be maintained at all times. Specific requirements:

Leak Testing Frequency:

  • At installation (initial qualification)
  • Thereafter at suitable intervals — e.g., twice per year for aseptic processing rooms
  • Additionally required when:
    • Air quality monitoring shows unacceptable results
    • Facility renovations or modifications
    • Media fill (APS) failure investigation
    • Sterility failure investigation
  • Dry heat depyrogenation tunnels and ovens also require HEPA leak testing

Challenge Aerosol: Must meet viscosity and other specifications. DOP (dioctyl phthalate) and PAO (poly-alpha-olefin) are appropriate aerosols. The aerosol must not promote microbial growth.

Efficiency Test vs. Leak Test distinction:

  • Efficiency test: uses monodispersed 0.3 µm particles; assesses overall filter media performance
  • Leak test: uses polydispersed aerosol; scans entire filter face and frame with photometer probe at 1 inch from the filter face, at ≥ 1 cubic foot per minute flow rate

Leak Acceptance Criterion: A single probe reading equivalent to 0.01% of the upstream challenge concentration constitutes a significant leak → the filter must be replaced or repaired, followed by confirmatory retesting.

Velocity Uniformity: HEPA filter leak testing alone is insufficient. Velocity uniformity must also be monitored — velocities are measured at 6 inches from the filter face and at defined distances from the work surface. HEPA filters shall be replaced when non-uniformity is detected or when airflow patterns are adversely affected.

Drug manufacturers are responsible for ensuring that contractor activities meet specifications, even when testing is outsourced.

中文導讀 — 空氣過濾（膜過濾器與HEPA）

空氣過濾是無菌製造的技術骨幹。本節涵蓋兩類過濾系統：用於壓縮氣體的膜過濾器，以及用於潔淨室空調的HEPA過濾器。兩者都有嚴格的完整性測試要求。

D.1 膜過濾器：壓縮氣體的「看不見的污染源」

壓縮氣體（氮氣、CO₂、壓縮空氣）廣泛用於推送物料、覆蓋保護藥品，但如果氣體本身含有粒子、油霧或微生物，就直接把污染「注入」產品中。

  • 膜過濾後的氣體品質必須≥所在潔淨室的等級
  • 疏水性過濾器（Hydrophobic Filters）防止水氣——因為濕濾膜是微生物生長的溫床
  • 高壓釜（Autoclave）進氣管線、凍乾機（Lyophilizer）破真空管線，也都必須安裝膜過濾器

比喻：潛水氣瓶的氣體純度要求

潛水員使用的壓縮空氣氣瓶，必須確保氣體中沒有油霧（油霧在高壓下會引起中毒）。製藥廠的壓縮氣體要求完全類似——吹入藥品容器的氮氣，必須像「手術室空氣」一樣潔淨。任何油分、水分、或微生物都可能成為污染媒介。

D.2 HEPA過濾器：效率測試 vs. 洩漏測試

很多人混淆這兩種測試，但它們的目的完全不同：

  • 效率測試（Efficiency Test）：
    • 使用單分散0.3 µm粒子
    • 評估整片濾材的整體過濾效率
    • 確認濾材達到HEPA規格（≥99.97% @ 0.3 µm）
  • 洩漏測試（Leak Test）：
    • 使用多分散氣膠（DOP或PAO）
    • 用光度計（Photometer）探頭距濾面1英寸掃描全部表面及邊框
    • 流量≥1 ft³/min
    • 目的是找出濾材針孔或邊框密封洩漏點

比喻：橋樑安全檢測——HEPA 0.01%洩漏標準

橋樑工程師不只測試鋼材整體強度（= 效率測試），還要逐英寸掃描焊接點和螺栓連接處（= 洩漏測試）。HEPA的0.01%上游濃度標準就像橋樑的「裂縫容忍極限」——任何單點讀數超過此值，整片濾材就必須維修或更換，而不是平均合格就繼續使用。這個標準非常嚴格：即使整體效率達到99.99%，一個局部洩漏點仍然是不可接受的。

HEPA測試頻率的觸發條件

除了每年兩次的例行測試外，以下任何一個事件都是額外測試的觸發條件：

  • 環境監測數據不合格（粒子或微生物超標）
  • 廠房裝修或設備更動（可能震動損壞濾材或密封）
  • 媒體灌裝（APS / Media Fill）失敗調查
  • 無菌性測試（Sterility Test）失敗調查

注意：乾熱去熱原隧道（Depyrogenation Tunnel）的HEPA也需要定期洩漏測試！這一點常被忽略。

速度均勻性：洩漏測試的必要補充

通過洩漏測試的HEPA不代表氣流均勻。如果某些濾面區域的速度明顯偏低，就會形成「低流速死角」，粒子在此沉積而非被帶走。FDA明確要求速度量測應在距濾面6英寸處及工作面設定距離處進行，發現不均勻時必須更換HEPA。

委外測試的責任歸屬

許多CDMO或藥廠將HEPA洩漏測試委外給認證公司執行。FDA明確聲明：即使委外，藥廠仍然對測試結果完全負責。這意味著藥廠必須：審查委外廠商的SOP、確認其使用的氣膠符合規格、審核測試記錄原始數據，並保存完整的委外稽核記錄。

IV.E — Facility and Equipment Design

Core Design Principles

Aseptic processes are designed to minimize exposure of sterile articles to contamination hazards. Essential design considerations:

  • Limiting the duration of exposure of sterile items
  • Providing the highest possible environmental control
  • Optimizing process flow
  • Designing equipment to prevent entrainment of lower-quality air into Class 100 (ISO 5)
Personnel and Material Flow

Personnel and material flow should be optimized to minimize unnecessary activities. Equipment layout should provide good ergonomics for operators. Minimize the number of personnel present in the aseptic processing room and limit frequency of entries/exits to the critical area.

Any intervention or unplanned stoppage increases contamination risk. Therefore:

  • On-line weight checks eliminate repeated manual weighing activities
  • SIP (Sterilize-in-Place) eliminates aseptic connections requiring manual setup
  • Robotics and automation further reduce human intervention risk
Product Transfer and Protection

Products must be transferred under appropriate cleanroom conditions. For lyophilization:

  • Partially sealed containers must be transferred in critical area conditions only
  • Class 100 (ISO 5) must be maintained between the filling line and the lyophilizer
  • Curtains and rigid plastic shields may serve as barriers

Isolator systems significantly enhance product protection and are increasingly recommended for high-risk operations.

Double-door or integrated sterilizers support direct product flow without exposure to lower-classified environments. Airlocks and interlocking doors provide better air balance control and prevent simultaneous opening of adjacent doors.

Materials must be disinfected or sterilized when entering higher-classified areas.

Stoppered vials exiting the aseptic zone prior to capping (crimping) require local protection until the crimp seal is applied.

Cleanroom Construction and Surface Requirements

Cleanrooms should function as integrated, seamless units. Requirements:

  • Seamless, rounded floor-to-wall junctions (coved corners) to prevent particle accumulation
  • Smooth, hard surfaces throughout
  • Minimal unnecessary equipment and fixtures
  • Sanitary fittings and valves
  • Drains are inappropriate for classified areas except Class 100,000 (ISO 8)
  • No horizontal surfaces or ledges that could accumulate particles
  • Equipment must not obstruct airflow patterns
Equipment Sterilization and Aseptic Setup

Equipment shall be designed for:

  • Ease of sterilization
  • Ease of aseptic setup and assembly
  • Minimal complexity during aseptic interventions
Shutdown and Restart Procedures

Deviation and change control systems shall address planned or unplanned shutdowns of air handling or other utilities. Written procedures must specify the steps required to return the facility to operating conditions after any shutdown, and the environmental qualification activities required before production may resume.

中文導讀 — 廠房與設備設計

IV.E是全節中實務操作密度最高的部分。這裡的設計原則直接影響無菌操作的成功率、環境監測的結果，以及FDA查廠的合規評分。

設計哲學：「防禦性設計」

FDA的設計原則可以歸納為四個「最小化」：

  • 最小化暴露時間：無菌物品在開放環境中的時間越短越好
  • 最小化人員介入：每一次人員介入都是污染機會
  • 最小化低品質空氣侵入：設備設計不能阻礙單向流或造成渦流
  • 最小化不必要設施：多餘的架子、管線、設備都是積塵和清潔死角

比喻：醫療手術室的「人員最小化」原則

頂級外科手術室有一條不成文規定：「非必要人員不得入室」。每增加一個人，就增加一個產生微粒和微生物的來源。無菌製造室完全相同。FDA明確要求降低填充室的人員數量，並限制進出關鍵區的頻率。「人員即污染源」是無菌製造設計的核心認知。

自動化與隔離技術的層次

FDA對減少人員介入的推薦技術，由低到高：

  • 在線重量檢測：消除手動稱重介入（簡單但有效）
  • SIP（原位滅菌）：消除無菌管路連接——連接點本身就是污染風險
  • 機器人/自動化：完全排除人員在關鍵區的操作（高投資，最高保護）
  • 隔離器（Isolator）：在物理隔離的正壓或負壓環境中操作，為最高等級的保護

凍乾製程（Lyophilization）的設計要求

半加塞的凍乾製品從灌裝線到凍乾機的轉移，是無菌製造中最高風險的步驟之一：

  • 轉移路徑全程必須維持Class 100（ISO 5）
  • 若使用隔離器（Isolator）從灌裝線直接連接凍乾機，可大幅降低此風險
  • 壓克力簾或硬式隔板可作為局部保護屏障
  • 完成軋蓋（Crimping）前，已加塞但未軋蓋的小瓶仍需局部保護

潔淨室建設細節：為什麼角落必須弧形？

直角角落（90°）是微粒和微生物的藏身之處——傳統清潔方式無法徹底清潔直角接縫。FDA要求「地板與牆壁交接處採用弧形圓角（Coved Corners）」，目的是：

  • 消除清潔死角，確保消毒液能完整覆蓋所有表面
  • 減少微生物的物理庇護空間
  • 便於目視檢查（沒有暗角積塵）

同理，排水口（Drains）在ISO 5/6/7的潔淨室中是不被允許的，因為排水管是微生物的通道——只有ISO 8（Class 100,000）區域（如清洗間）才允許設置排水口。

停機後的恢復程序：常被忽略的合規要求

空調系統非計畫性停機（如電力故障）後，不能在恢復供電後立即重啟生產。必須有書面SOP規定：

  • 恢復空調後的「回沖（Purge）」時間
  • 必須執行的環境確認監測（粒子+微生物）
  • 確認壓差已恢復至合格範圍
  • 記錄停機事件及恢復確認結果

FDA在查廠時如果發現缺少此類書面程序，會直接開具483觀察報告。

CDMO廠房設計的商業決策矩陣

CDMO在為新客戶評估產品接受度時，廠房設計決策影響深遠：

  • 隔離器 vs. 傳統RABS：隔離器初期成本高，但環境監測數據通常更好，有助於提高APS（無菌工藝模擬）合格率
  • SIP管路設計：前期工程成本更高，但減少無菌連接程序，長期運營風險更低
  • 機器人填充系統：高資本投入，但可承接高風險高價值產品（如細胞治療、基因治療），溢價收費

每一個設計升級都是「風險降低」與「成本增加」的平衡——CDMO的競爭力往往體現在這個取捨的智慧上。

Critical Numerical Values to Remember

  • Class 100 (ISO 5): ≤ 3,520 particles/m³; microbial action level 1 cfu/m³ (normally zero)
  • Class 100,000 (ISO 8): ≤ 3,520,000 particles/m³; 100 cfu/m³; ≥ 20 ACH
  • Unidirectional flow velocity: 0.45 m/s (90 ft/min) ± 20%

  • HEPA leak test frequency: minimum 2× per year for aseptic processing rooms
  • HEPA leak test significant threshold: 0.01% of upstream challenge concentration
  • HEPA velocity measurement point: 6 inches from filter face

V.A Personnel — Training & Aseptic Technique

Key CFR Citations: 21 CFR 211.25(a) — each person shall have education, training, and experience; training shall be continuing and with sufficient frequency. 21 CFR 211.22(a) — QC unit has authority to approve/reject. 21 CFR 211.28(b) — personnel practice good sanitation and health habits.

A well-designed, maintained, and operated aseptic process minimizes personnel intervention. As operator activities increase, risk to product sterility also increases. It is critical for operators to use aseptic technique at all times.

Training topics required before entering the aseptic manufacturing area:

  • Aseptic technique
  • Cleanroom behavior
  • Microbiology fundamentals
  • Hygiene and health practices
  • Gowning procedure
  • Patient safety hazards
  • Specific written procedures (SOPs)

After initial training, an ongoing training program is maintained. Supervisory personnel routinely evaluate each operator's conformance. The QC unit provides regular oversight.

Key aseptic techniques operators must follow:

  • Contact sterile materials only with sterile instruments held under Class 100 (ISO 5) conditions between uses in sterilized containers. Replace as necessary throughout operation.
  • Sterile gloves should be regularly sanitized or changed. Personnel should not directly contact sterile products, containers, closures, or critical surfaces.
  • Move slowly and deliberately. Rapid movements create unacceptable turbulence and disrupt unidirectional airflow.
  • Keep entire body out of the path of unidirectional airflow. Disruption poses risk to product sterility.
  • Approach manipulation from the side, not above (in vertical unidirectional flow). Refrain from speaking when in direct proximity to the critical area.
  • Maintain proper gown control. No activity posing unreasonable contamination risk to the gown is permitted.

V.A 人員訓練與無菌技術（中文解說）

這一節的核心訊息：人是無菌製程中最大的污染風險源。每位操作員每天脫落數百萬片皮屑；每次說話、每次快速移動，都可能破壞精心設計的單向氣流保護。FDA 的回應是：用訓練、規範與持續監管來控制這個風險。

核心概念：訓練的三個層次

  • 入職前訓練（Initial Training）：七大必修主題，缺一不可，且必須在進入無菌區之前完成。
  • 持續訓練（Ongoing Training）：FDA 要求頻率「足夠」（sufficient frequency），不能僅訓練一次就結束。
  • 主管評核（Supervisory Evaluation）：主管須定期在現場觀察操作員的合規情形；QC 單位提供常規監管。

三個層次缺一不可。任何一層失效，都可能在 FDA 查廠時形成 483 觀察項目。

生活比喻：無菌操作員 = 外科醫師上手術台

外科醫師在進行心臟手術前，每一個動作都有規範——如何刷手、如何讓護士協助戴上手套而不碰觸外表面、進入手術室後手臂必須保持在腰部以上……。無菌製造操作員的每一個動作，要求的嚴謹程度完全相同：「移動要緩慢而有目的性」、「不要站在氣流路徑上」、「靠近關鍵區時不能說話」——這些都是從手術室借鑒來的規範，因為保護病患的邏輯是一樣的。

CDMO 稽查重點：訓練記錄常見缺失

  • 訓練記錄缺少日期、簽名或講師資訊
  • 無法證明訓練「持續進行」（缺乏再訓練紀錄）
  • 操作員執行了未受過訓練的工作
  • 主管現場評核記錄不存在或流於形式

客戶在技術協議（Technical Agreement）審查時，通常也會確認訓練體系的完整性。

Section V.A — Gowning Qualification Program | 著裝資格鑑定計畫

V.A Gowning — Requirements & Qualification

CFR Citations: 21 CFR 211.28(a) — personnel wear clean clothing appropriate for duties; protective apparel as necessary. 21 CFR 211.28(c) — only authorized personnel enter limited-access areas.

Only qualified and appropriately gowned personnel are permitted in the aseptic manufacturing area. The gown provides a barrier between the body and sterile materials.

The Agency recommends gowns that are sterilized and nonshedding, covering all skin and hair. Required components include:

  • Facemasks
  • Hoods
  • Beard and moustache covers (where applicable)
  • Protective goggles
  • Elastic gloves

Written procedures detail donning in an aseptic manner. Adequate barrier is provided by overlapping of gown components (e.g., gloves overlapping sleeves). Any torn or defective element must be changed immediately. Gloves must be sanitized frequently.

Gowning Qualification Program — assesses the ability of cleanroom operators to maintain gown quality. The Agency recommends microbiological surface sampling of several locations:

  • Glove fingers
  • Facemask
  • Forearm
  • Chest area

Periodic requalification is required. Annual requalification is normally sufficient for automated operations. Additional or more frequent requalification is required if adverse conditions occur.

Written procedures should address circumstances for retraining, requalification, or reassignment. Sanitizing gloves just prior to sampling is inappropriate, as it can prevent recovery of microorganisms present during manipulation.

V.A 著裝資格鑑定計畫（中文解說）

著裝（Gowning）不只是「穿上無菌衣服」。它是一道需要數據支撐的屏障系統（Barrier System），且每位操作員必須個別通過資格鑑定，才能進入無菌製造區域。

核心概念：著裝資格鑑定的三個關鍵要素

  • 取樣位置標準化：FDA 建議對手指、口罩、前臂、胸部進行微生物表面取樣——這些是最容易累積微生物的部位。
  • 定期再鑑定（Requalification）：通過一次不夠，需要定期重做。若發生不良事件（如微生物超標），必須增加頻率。
  • 取樣前不得消毒手套：FDA 特別指出這是不當行為——取樣前消毒手套，會把操作過程中真實存在的微生物殺死，導致結果失真，掩蓋真正的風險。

生活比喻：著裝程序 = 外科醫師穿手術服的程序

外科醫師穿手術袍有嚴格順序——由護士協助繫帶（自己不能碰外表面），最後由護士協助戴上無菌手套（確保手套外面未被污染）。無菌室著裝的邏輯完全相同：SOP 規定的「套疊順序」（例如手套要蓋過袖口）確保沒有皮膚裸露在外。如果任何一個步驟走錯，屏障效果就可能失效——就像外科醫師不小心碰到手術台外側，手套就必須立即更換一樣。

CDMO 483 高風險項目：著裝資格鑑定常見缺失

  • 無書面 SOP 規定著裝順序與取樣計畫
  • 超標後未進行調查與再鑑定
  • 取樣前消毒手套（影響數據真實性）
  • 著裝鑑定記錄不完整或未與批次生產掛鉤
  • 臨時人員（合約工）未完成鑑定即進入無菌區

著裝鑑定不合格的操作員若曾進入客戶產品的充填間，可能觸發批次拒絕與客戶通知義務。

Section V.B & V.C — Laboratory Personnel & Monitoring Program | 實驗室人員與監測計畫

V.B & V.C — Lab Personnel & Personnel Monitoring

V.B — Laboratory Personnel: The basic principles of training, aseptic technique, and personnel qualification also apply to those performing aseptic sampling and microbiological laboratory analyses. Systems cannot be considered in control if the validity of laboratory data is in question.

V.C — Monitoring Program: Personnel can significantly affect environment quality. A vigilant and responsive personnel monitoring program should be established.

Surface samples of each operator's gloves should be taken on a daily basis or in association with each lot. This is accompanied by sampling of other selected gown locations. The QC unit establishes more comprehensive monitoring for especially labor-intensive operations.

Maintaining contamination-free gloves and gowns throughout operations is the ongoing goal.

Sanitizing gloves just prior to sampling is inappropriate, as it can prevent recovery of microorganisms present during manipulation.

When operators exceed established levels or show an adverse trend, the following actions are required:

  • Investigation
  • Increased sampling
  • Increased observation
  • Retraining
  • Gowning requalification
  • Reassignment (if necessary)

V.B & V.C 實驗室人員與人員監測計畫（中文解說）

人員監測計畫是無菌製程的「免疫系統」——它的功能不是懲罰人，而是早期偵測趨勢，在問題演變為批次失敗之前介入。

核心概念：監測計畫的兩個維度

  • 常規監測：每位操作員每天或每批次一次手套表面取樣，加上部分著裝表面取樣。建立個人基線數據。
  • 加強監測觸發：超出警戒限（Alert Level）或行動限（Action Level）時，必須啟動調查、增加取樣、觀察、再訓練、再鑑定，必要時重新分配崗位。

V.B 提醒：實驗室人員（執行無菌取樣、微生物分析）同樣適用這些要求。若實驗室數據的有效性受到質疑，整個品質系統的可信度都會崩潰。

生活比喻：監測計畫 = 飛行員定期體檢

民航飛行員必須定期接受體檢；若健康狀況不符標準，必須暫停飛行直到恢復。這不是不信任飛行員，而是因為乘客安全需要系統性保障，不能依賴個人自我評估。無菌室操作員的每日手套取樣邏輯相同——建立一套客觀的、數據驅動的持續監控機制，確保任何問題都能及早被發現。

實務警示：趨勢分析比單點超標更重要

FDA 不只看單次超標，更關注趨勢（Trend）。若某操作員連續 5 次菌落數接近警戒限但未超行動限，部分公司可能不採取行動——但這在 FDA 眼中是「不良趨勢未被識別與回應」的缺失。健全的監測計畫必須在 SOP 中定義「不良趨勢」的判定標準與回應程序。

  2. 某操作員連續三週手套取樣結果為 1 CFU（警戒限 3 CFU，行動限 5 CFU）。公司應採取什麼行動？

Section VI.A — Components | 原料成分管控

VI.A Components — Sterilization & Endotoxin Control

CFR Citations: 21 CFR 210.3(b)(3) — component includes any ingredient intended for use in manufacture. 21 CFR 211.80(a) — written procedures for receipt, identification, storage, handling, sampling, testing, approval/rejection. 21 CFR 211.84(d)(6) — each lot liable to microbiological contamination shall be subjected to microbiological tests before use. 21 CFR 211.113(b) — validation of sterilization processes.

A drug product can be contaminated through contaminated components. Examples include active ingredients, Water for Injection (WFI), and other excipients. It is important to characterize the microbial content (bioburden and endotoxin) of each component and establish acceptance limits.

Endotoxin load data is particularly significant because parenteral products must be nonpyrogenic. Written procedures and specifications for acceptance/rejection of each lot with respect to endotoxins must be established. Any component failing defined endotoxin limits should be rejected.

In aseptic processing, each component is individually sterilized, or components are combined and the mixture is sterilized. Knowledge of bioburden is important for assessing sterilization adequacy.

Methods for sterilizing components:

  • Filtration (widely used): Solution passed through a sterilizing membrane or cartridge filter. Used where the component is soluble and heat-labile. Variation: aseptic crystallization/precipitation or lyophilization as a sterile powder — but more handling equals higher contamination potential.
  • Dry heat sterilization: Suitable for heat-stable and insoluble components. Heat penetration/distribution studies especially significant for powders due to insulating effects.
  • Irradiation: Suitable for some components. Studies must demonstrate appropriateness.

VI.A 原料成分管控（中文解說）

無菌藥品的污染路徑不只來自人員和環境——原料本身就可能是污染的起點。即使後續滅菌做得再好，若投入物料的內毒素負荷過高，最終產品仍可能造成病患發燒或敗血性休克。

核心概念：物料管控的兩個關鍵指標

  • 生物負荷（Bioburden）：物料中存活的微生物數量。對滅菌製程設計至關重要——滅菌前生物負荷愈高，需要更強的滅菌條件才能保證無菌保證水準（SAL）。
  • 內毒素（Endotoxin）：革蘭氏陰性菌細胞壁的脂多醣（LPS）片段。即使細菌已被殺死，內毒素仍存在且具有高熱原活性，注射到人體後引起發熱，嚴重時致命。

每批物料都必須有明確的生物負荷與內毒素接受標準；不符標準的批次必須拒絕（Reject）——不可妥協。

生活比喻：內毒素 = 外觀完好但已含毒素的食物

想像一鍋煮熟的白米飯放在室溫 12 小時。外觀、氣味看起來都好，但蠟樣芽孢桿菌已在冷卻過程中大量繁殖，並產生了耐熱毒素。就算之後重新加熱到 100°C 殺死細菌，那些毒素依然存在，吃了還是會食物中毒。內毒素的道理完全相同：普通加熱滅菌可以殺死細菌，但殺死細菌不等於去除內毒素。這就是為什麼注射劑對每批原料都需要獨立的內毒素檢驗，而不能只依賴滅菌製程。

CDMO 業務風險：物料內毒素 OOS 的連鎖後果

  • 整批製品可能需要拒絕（即使已完成後續所有製程）
  • 必須向客戶通知（Customer Notification），影響交期與信任關係
  • 若已放行，可能觸發市場召回（Recall）
  • 必須進行根本原因分析（RCA）並提交 CAPA

紮實的進料管控（Incoming Material Testing）與供應商資格審核（Supplier Qualification）是避免這些代價高昂場景的第一道防線。

Section VI.B — Containers & Closures Preparation | 容器與封閉件之製備

VI.B Containers & Closures — Preparation

CFR Citations: 21 CFR 211.94(c) — containers and closures shall be clean and, where indicated, sterilized and processed to remove pyrogenic properties. 21 CFR 211.94(d) — standards, specifications, methods of testing written and followed.

Containers and closures must be sterile and nonpyrogenic for parenteral products. A validation study must demonstrate the ability to render them sterile and nonpyrogenic. Written procedures must specify revalidation frequency and time limits for holding sterile depyrogenated containers/closures.

Glass Containers:

  • A series of wash and rinse cycles to remove foreign matter. High purity rinse water is recommended. Final rinse water must meet WFI, USP specifications.
  • Depyrogenation adequacy validated by spiking containers with known quantities of endotoxin, measuring content after depyrogenation. Endotoxin solution allowed to air dry on surfaces. Positive controls measure percentage recovery.
  • Validation data must demonstrate at least 99.9% (3-log) endotoxin reduction.
  • Dry heat generally accomplishes both sterilization and depyrogenation for glass. Validation includes heat distribution/penetration studies, worst-case cycles, container characteristics (mass), and specific loading configurations.

Plastic Containers:

  • Nonpyrogenic by nature; WFI rinses can remove residual pyrogens.
  • Can be sterilized with EtO (ethylene oxide), irradiation, or other means.
  • For EtO: temperature, pressure, humidity, gas concentration, exposure time, degassing, aeration, and residuals must be specified and monitored. EtO is an effective surface sterilant. Biological indicators (BI) are important for EtO validation. Residuals (EtO and byproducts) must be within specified limits.

Rubber Closures (stoppers, syringe plungers):

  • Multiple wash/rinse cycles, then steam or irradiation sterilization.
  • Initial rinses: at least Purified Water, USP; final rinses with WFI.
  • Depyrogenation by multiple hot WFI rinses.
  • Minimize time between washing, drying, and sterilizing (residual moisture supports microbial growth and endotoxin generation).
  • Rubber is a poor heat conductor — extra attention to heat penetration validation.
  • Validation data must demonstrate successful endotoxin removal.
  • Siliconization of rubber stoppers: silicone must meet QC criteria; must not adversely affect product safety, quality, or purity.

Contract Sterilization Facilities: Same CGMP requirements apply. The finished dosage form manufacturer must review and assess the contractor's validation protocol and final validation report.

VI.B 容器與封閉件製備（中文解說）

容器和封閉件必須同時達到無菌且不含熱原（Sterile & Nonpyrogenic）兩個標準，缺一不可，且兩者都需要驗證數據支撐。

3-Log 去熱原驗證：1000 倍的毒素削減

FDA 要求玻璃容器去熱原驗證至少達到 3 log（99.9%）的內毒素削減。

具體計算：若驗證時在玻璃瓶內壁接種 1,000 EU 的內毒素標準品，乾熱處理後殘留必須不超過 1 EU。

1,000 EU → 不超過 1 EU = 削減 99.9% = 3 log 削減。

陽性對照（Positive Control）確認回收率，確保 LAL 測定方法本身有效。這個要求是最低標準（Minimum Requirement），不是建議。

生活比喻：3-Log 削減 = 讓毒素減少 1000 倍

把 3 log 換算成日常語言：如果起始有 1,000 單位的內毒素，完成去熱原後只能剩下不到 1 單位——這相當於把一個足球場大小的空間，壓縮到一張 A4 紙的面積。這樣的削減倍率才能確保注射劑進入人體後不會引發熱原反應。在客戶審計和監管說明中，「1000 倍削減」是最直觀的解釋方式。

CDMO 稽查要點：委外滅菌設施管理

若將容器封閉件的滅菌/去熱原委外，FDA 要求：

  • 藥品製造商必須審查並評估委外廠的驗證協議（Validation Protocol）和最終驗證報告（Final Validation Report）
  • 委外廠同樣受 CGMP 規範，不因委外而降低要求
  • 品質協議（Quality Agreement）必須明確責任歸屬

客戶稽查時通常會要求查閱容器封閉件供應商的去熱原驗證報告，以及公司對這些報告的審查記錄。

Section VI.B — Container Closure Integrity | 容器密封完整性

VI.B Container Closure Integrity (CCI)

The container closure system must not permit penetration of microorganisms. Damaged or defective units must be detected and removed during final sealed product inspection.

Safeguards must preclude shipment of products lacking container closure integrity (CCI). Equipment suitability problems or incoming container/closure deficiencies can cause loss of integrity.

Functional defects in delivery devices — such as syringe device defects and delivery volume errors — should be monitored by in-process testing.

Defects outside specifications must be investigated per 21 CFR 211.192.

CFR Citations: 21 CFR 211.94(c); 21 CFR 211.94(d); 21 CFR 211.192 — investigation of unexplained discrepancies.

VI.B 容器密封完整性（中文解說）

容器密封系統是藥品無菌性的最後一道實體屏障。即使藥品在充填時完美無菌，若密封出現微孔或缺陷，外部微生物依然可能隨時間滲入。

核心概念：CCI 的兩個保護面向

  • 阻擋微生物入侵：密封必須在整個貨架期內阻止微生物穿透，這是最核心的功能。
  • 傳遞裝置功能性：預充填注射器等含傳遞裝置的產品，還需監控容量準確性、活塞滑動力等功能性指標。

生活比喻：CCI = 真空保鮮袋的密封性

真空保鮮袋的目的是隔絕空氣和微生物。但如果袋子有肉眼看不到的微孔，它外觀仍像「密封的」，食物卻已在你不知情的情況下慢慢變質。藥品容器密封失效的邏輯完全相同——這就是為什麼 CCI 驗證必須使用靈敏的儀器方法（如染料滲透、真空衰減、高壓放電），而不能僅憑視覺檢查。

CDMO 品質風險：CCI 失效的連鎖反應

  • 若在市場上發現：可能觸發第一類召回（Class I Recall）
  • 若在廠內發現：整批產品面臨銷毀風險
  • 客戶必須被通知，影響後續合約
  • FDA 要求根本原因調查與 CAPA，可能要求重新驗證充填封蓋設備

Section VII — Endotoxin Control | 內毒素控制

VII Endotoxin Control — Equipment & Processing

CFR Citations: 21 CFR 211.63, 211.65(a), 211.67(a) — equipment design, construction, cleaning. 21 CFR 211.167(a) — appropriate laboratory testing for each batch purporting to be sterile and/or pyrogen-free.

Endotoxin contamination of injectable products can occur as a result of poor CGMP controls. Certain patient populations are at greater risk:

  • Neonates
  • Those receiving other injections concomitantly
  • Those administered parenterals in atypically large volumes or doses

Areas to address in establishing endotoxin control:

  • Drug product components
  • Containers and closures
  • Storage time limitations
  • Manufacturing equipment

Adequate cleaning, drying, and storage of equipment will control bioburden and prevent endotoxin accumulation. Equipment should be easily assembled/disassembled, cleaned, sanitized, and sterilized. Endotoxins can be contributed by both upstream and downstream processing equipment.

Critical point: Sterilizing-grade filters and moist heat sterilization (autoclaving) have NOT been shown to be effective in removing endotoxin.

Endotoxin on equipment surfaces can be:

  • Inactivated by high-temperature dry heat
  • Removed by cleaning procedures

CIP (Clean-In-Place) procedures: initial rinses with high purity water and/or cleaning agents (acid, base, surfactant), followed by final rinses with heated WFI. Equipment should be dried following cleaning, unless proceeding immediately to a sterilization step.

VII 內毒素控制（中文解說）

Section VII 的核心訊息是：內毒素控制是一個系統性任務，貫穿整個製程，從原料、容器、設備到儲存時間，每一個環節都可能是內毒素引入的節點。

核心概念：內毒素控制的四個節點

  • 物料（Components）：每批次獨立內毒素檢驗，有接受標準。
  • 容器與封閉件（Containers & Closures）：去熱原驗證，3-log 削減要求。
  • 時間限制（Storage Time Limits）：存放時間愈長，微生物繁殖愈多，產生的內毒素也愈多；時間限制由數據支撐。
  • 設備（Equipment）：清洗（CIP）、乾燥、儲存條件決定設備表面的內毒素水準；上下游設備都要管控。

生活比喻：設備上的內毒素 = 廚具上的細菌毒素

想像一個砧板，每次用完若不徹底清洗、乾燥，殘留的食物碎屑和水分就會讓細菌大量繁殖，細菌死後留下的毒素仍然殘留在砧板上——即使你之後把砧板放進沸水裡煮，這些毒素也不一定會被破壞。製藥設備的內毒素管控邏輯完全相同：清洗 + 乾燥 + 適當儲存，才能從根本上控制內毒素的累積。

關鍵警告：高壓蒸汽滅菌（Autoclave）無法去除內毒素

FDA 明確聲明：滅菌級過濾器和濕熱滅菌（高壓蒸汽）均無法有效去除內毒素。這是許多人的常見誤解。

  • 高壓蒸汽（121°C, 15 min）殺死細菌 — 但內毒素（LPS）耐熱，在此條件下不被破壞。
  • 去除內毒素的方法：乾熱（180°C 以上高溫，用於玻璃器具）或化學清洗（酸、鹼、介面活性劑加 WFI 沖洗）。
  • 對設備而言，CIP 程序（鹼洗 + WFI 終沖洗）是主要的去熱原手段。

在 CDMO 的客戶審計中，若廠方宣稱「設備高壓滅菌後即無熱原」，將是嚴重的技術錯誤，直接質疑廠方的技術能力。

思考練習 — 內毒素控制

  1. 為什麼「殺死細菌」並不等於「去除內毒素」？請從內毒素的化學本質（LPS 結構）解釋。
  2. 某設備在 CIP 後未充分乾燥，直接封存儲存了 3 天。請分析這可能帶來的內毒素風險。
  3. 製程中上游設備（如配製槽）和下游設備（如充填針頭）對內毒素的貢獻方式有何不同？
  4. 若某公司宣稱「設備經 121°C 高壓蒸汽滅菌，因此無熱原」，這個說法有什麼問題？
Section VIII — Time Limitations | 時間限制

VIII Time Limitations — Aseptic Processing Phases

CFR Citation: 21 CFR 211.111 — time limits for completion of each phase of production shall be established; deviation acceptable if not compromising quality, must be justified and documented.

Time limits must be established for each phase of aseptic processing. Examples include:

  • Period between start of bulk product compounding and sterilization
  • Filtration processes
  • Product exposure while on processing line
  • Storage of sterilized equipment
  • Storage of sterilized containers/closures

Time limits must be supported by data. Bioburden and endotoxin load must be assessed when establishing time limits for the formulation processing stage.

Total time for product filtration must be limited to:

  • Prevent microorganisms from penetrating the filter (filter integrity)
  • Prevent significant increase in upstream bioburden
  • Prevent significant increase in upstream endotoxin load

Filters used upstream for clarification or particle removal also have maximum use times that must be established and followed.

Any deviation from established time limits must be documented and justified; it is acceptable only if product quality is not compromised.

VIII 時間限制（中文解說）

時間限制的核心邏輯：微生物在任何未受控的潮濕環境中都會繁殖，而繁殖不僅增加生物負荷，還會產生更多內毒素。FDA 要求每一個製程階段都必須有數據支撐的時間上限，超過該時限的任何偏差都必須有文件記錄與科學合理性說明。

核心概念：需要建立時間限制的六個製程節點

  • 配製開始 → 滅菌完成：此段時間愈長，生物負荷與內毒素累積愈多。
  • 過濾時間：時間過長，微生物可能穿透過濾膜（Filter Integrity Risk）。
  • 充填線上的產品曝露時間：充填線暴露於環境中的累積時間須有上限。
  • 滅菌後設備的儲存時間：設備滅菌後存放過久，可能因環境因素重新污染。
  • 滅菌後容器封閉件的儲存時間：同上，需有書面時間限制。
  • 上游澄清/去粒子過濾器的使用時間：並非只有最終滅菌過濾器需要時間限制。

生活比喻：時間限制 = 食材保存期限與食品安全時間限制

生鮮雞肉放在 4°C 冰箱裡可以存放 2 天，放在室溫下只能存放 2 小時——這不是隨意規定的，而是根據細菌繁殖速率（倍增時間）計算出的安全時間上限。超過這個時間，食物不一定有明顯異味，但細菌數量已超過安全標準。藥品製程中的時間限制邏輯完全相同：每個「保存期限」都必須有微生物生長數據支撐，而不是憑經驗拍腦袋決定。

CDMO 稽查重點：時間限制的文件管理

FDA 及客戶稽查時，時間限制相關的常見缺失包括：

  • 批次記錄（Batch Record）中未記錄各階段的實際開始/結束時間
  • 時間限制無數據支撐，僅為歷史慣例（"We've always done it this way"）
  • 超時偏差（Time Limit Deviation）未依 21 CFR 211.111 進行文件化說明
  • 過濾時間限制只針對最終滅菌過濾器，而忽略了上游澄清過濾器
  • 滅菌後容器/設備的儲存時間未在 SOP 中明確規定

對 CDMO 而言，時間限制的科學依據（驗證研究報告）必須隨時可供稽查人員查閱。

思考練習 — 時間限制

  1. 某批次在配製完成後，因設備故障延遲了 2 小時才開始過濾滅菌，超出了 SOP 規定的時間限制。公司應如何處理這個偏差？依 21 CFR 211.111，什麼條件下偏差可以被接受？
  2. 為什麼過濾時間過長會帶來雙重風險（過濾膜完整性 + 生物負荷/內毒素）？這兩個風險各自如何影響最終產品品質？
  3. 某公司只對最終滅菌過濾器設定了使用時間限制，但對上游澄清過濾器沒有規定。FDA 會如何看待這個做法？
  4. 建立「配製 → 滅菌」時間限制時，需要收集哪些數據？請描述一個合理的驗證研究設計。

Section VIII — Time Limitations 時間限制 (pp. 20–21)

原文內容 Original Text

21 CFR 211.111 states: "When appropriate, time limits for the completion of each phase of production shall be established to assure the quality of the drug product. Deviation from established time limits may be acceptable if such deviation does not compromise the quality of the drug product. Such deviation shall be justified and documented."

When appropriate, time limits must be established for each phase of aseptic processing (§ 211.111). Time limits should include, for example, the period between the start of bulk product compounding and its sterilization, filtration processes, product exposure while on the processing line, and storage of sterilized equipment, containers and closures. The time limits established for the various production phases should be supported by data. Bioburden and endotoxin load should be assessed when establishing time limits for stages such as the formulation processing stage.

The total time for product filtration should be limited to an established maximum to prevent microorganisms from penetrating the filter. Such a time limit should also prevent a significant increase in upstream bioburden and endotoxin load. Because they can provide a substrate for microbial attachment, maximum use times for those filters used upstream for solution clarification or particle removal should also be established and justified.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為何需要時間限制？

無菌製程的每一個階段都涉及微生物污染的動態風險。時間越長，潛在的生物負荷累積、 內毒素增長、或過濾膜穿透風險就越大。21 CFR 211.111 要求廠商對每個生產階段 建立有數據支持的時間限制，而不是憑經驗猜測。

需要建立時間限制的關鍵階段包括：

  • 配製（Compounding）開始 → 過濾滅菌：防止生物負荷過度累積
  • 過濾滅菌 → 填充完成：防止已滅菌液體再污染
  • 滅菌設備/容器/塞子 → 使用：防止滅菌後再污染或生物膜形成
  • 生產線上的產品暴露時間：控制環境暴露風險

注意：時間限制需數據支持，不是「查 SOP 上面寫的」

FDA 要求時間限制由生物負荷 (bioburden) 和內毒素負荷 (endotoxin load) 的實際數據來支持。常見的稽核缺失是：廠商的時間限制來自「業界慣例」或「以前一直這樣做」， 而無法出示時程研究 (hold time study) 數據。

如果超出建立的時間限制，必須文件化記錄 + 科學論證不影響品質， 不能只是填寫偏差報告了事。

類比：食材備料 vs 上菜時限

想像高級餐廳的廚房：海鮮從處理好到烹調完成送上桌，有嚴格的時間窗口。 這不是任意規定，而是基於食品安全數據——細菌在某個溫度下每幾小時翻倍。 無菌製程也是同樣邏輯：配製好的藥液、滅菌好的容器，都有其「保鮮窗口」， 超過就需要有科學證明它依然安全。

過濾時間限制：兩個層面的保護

過濾時間限制有雙重功能：

  • 防止微生物穿透 (filter penetration)：長時間過濾下， 濾膜上積累的微生物可能因為壓力、生長等原因穿透至下游，破壞無菌性。
  • 控制上游生物負荷與內毒素：即使是用於澄清或除粒子的預過濾器， 其表面也是微生物附著的基質，需要限制使用時間，防止內毒素在膜上累積並在壓力下釋放。

這說明即使是「非滅菌級」的上游過濾器，也需要時間使用限制，且需書面合理化說明。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

在 CDMO 技術轉移過程中，建立時間限制是確效計畫的一部分， 需在工藝特性研究 (process characterization) 階段，設計並執行保留時間研究 (hold time study)。 典型的研究設計是：在最差條件下（最高配製溫度、最長預計保留時間）取樣， 監測生物負荷與內毒素，以科學數據支撐最終 SOP 中的時間限制數值。 沒有支持數據的時間限制，是 FDA 483 觀察項目的常見來源。

Section IX — Validation Overview 確效總覽 (p. 21)

原文內容 Original Text

21 CFR 211.113(b): "Appropriate written procedures, designed to prevent microbiological contamination of drug products purporting to be sterile, shall be established and followed. Such procedures shall include validation of any sterilization process."

This section primarily discusses routine qualification and validation study recommendations. Change control procedures are addressed only briefly, but are an important part of the quality systems established by a firm. A change in facility, equipment, process, or test method should be evaluated through the written change control program, triggering an evaluation of the need for revalidation or requalification.

To ensure the sterility of products purporting to be sterile, sterilization, aseptic filling and closing operations must be adequately validated (§ 211.113). The goal of even the most effective sterilization processes can be defeated if the sterilized elements of a product (the drug formulation, the container, and the closure) are brought together under conditions that contaminate any of those elements.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：確效的完整框架

Section IX 的確效體系涵蓋三大支柱，分別對應無菌製程的三個關鍵環節：

  • A. 製程模擬 (Process Simulations / Media Fill)：驗證無菌填充操作本身
  • B. 過濾效能確效 (Filtration Efficacy)：驗證過濾滅菌的有效性
  • C. 設備/容器/塞子滅菌確效：驗證所有與產品接觸元件的無菌性

這三個支柱缺一不可，因為「連鎖破口」理論告訴我們：即使每個元件都分別通過了 最嚴格的滅菌，一旦組合過程中發生污染，整個無菌保證體系就失效了。

注意：變更控制是確效的延伸，不是例外

許多廠商誤以為確效是「做一次就夠」的事。FDA 明確指出：設施、設備、製程、 測試方法的任何變更，都必須透過書面變更控制系統評估是否需要 再確效 (revalidation) 或再資格確認 (requalification)。 這意味著確效計畫是「活的文件」，需要持續維護和響應變更。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

對 CDMO 而言，每次接受新委託方的技術轉移，或者原有產品更換設備/廠房， 都構成需要評估再確效的觸發事件。建立完善的觸發事件清單 (trigger list) 並納入 變更控制 SOP，可以有效降低因「沒有意識到需要再確效」而在 FDA 查廠時 被出具 483 觀察項目的風險。

Media fill studies should closely simulate aseptic manufacturing operations incorporating, as appropriate, worst-case activities and conditions. FDA recommends that the media fill program address applicable issues such as:

  • Factors associated with the longest permitted run (e.g., operator fatigue)
  • Representative number, type, and complexity of normal interventions, as well as nonroutine interventions (e.g., maintenance, stoppages, equipment adjustments)
  • Lyophilization, when applicable
  • Aseptic assembly of equipment (e.g., at start-up, during processing)
  • Number of personnel and their activities
  • Representative number of aseptic additions (e.g., charging containers and closures as well as sterile ingredients) or transfers
  • Shift changes, breaks, and gown changes (when applicable)
  • Type of aseptic equipment disconnections/connections
  • Aseptic sample collections
  • Line speed and configuration
  • Weight checks
  • Container closure systems (e.g., sizes, type, compatibility with equipment)
  • Specific provisions in written procedures relating to aseptic processing (e.g., conditions permitted before line clearance is mandated)

A written batch record, documenting production conditions and simulated activities, should be prepared for each media fill run. Media fills should not be used to justify practices that pose unnecessary contamination risks.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Media Fill 的設計哲學

培養基模擬填充 (Media Fill) 的根本邏輯是：用微生物培養基取代真正的藥品， 「假裝」走一遍完整的無菌填充流程。如果任何步驟引入了污染，培養基就會長菌， 提供明確的可偵測訊號。

關鍵設計原則是「最差情境 (worst-case)」——Media Fill 不是要讓程序看起來很完美，而是要刻意納入所有高風險活動， 以確認即使在最具挑戰性的條件下，無菌保證仍能維持。

類比：消防演習不應選最好的天氣

消防演習若只在白天、好天氣、全員在場時舉行，就無法真實反映深夜緊急事故的應對能力。 同理，Media Fill 如果只在環境最好、人員最少、操作最簡單的條件下進行， 就失去了驗證意義。FDA 要求納入「最差操作條件」，正是要確保演習的有效性。

注意：13 項設計要素——每一項都是稽核重點

FDA 列出的 13 項 Media Fill 設計要素，是查廠時的逐項核查清單。 常見缺失包括：

  • 換班/休息沒有模擬 → 只做單班，漏掉換班污染風險
  • 非例行性干預沒有納入 → 設備調整、維修未模擬
  • 冷凍乾燥產品未模擬部分真空抽取步驟
  • 批次記錄 (batch record) 填寫不完整或流於形式

CDMO/COO 操作應用

在 CDMO 環境中，同一條生產線可能服務多個客戶的不同產品， Media Fill 的設計必須能代表所有這些產品的最差操作情境。 實務上建議建立一份最差情境矩陣 (worst-case matrix)， 列出每個品項的特殊操作步驟（如無菌添加、手動充填、容器尺寸差異）， 確保設計一次 Media Fill 能覆蓋多個產品共用的確效需求， 同時不犧牲代表性。這也是在提案階段向客戶展示專業能力的好機會。

Section IX.A.2 — Frequency and Number of Runs 頻率與執行次數 (pp. 22–23)

原文內容 Original Text

When a processing line is initially qualified, individual media fills should be repeated enough times to ensure that results are consistent and meaningful. We recommend that at least three consecutive separate successful runs be performed during initial line qualification. Subsequently, routine semi-annual qualification conducted for each processing line will evaluate the state of control of the aseptic process.

All personnel who are authorized to enter the aseptic processing room during manufacturing, including technicians and maintenance personnel, should participate in a media fill at least once a year.

Each change to a product or line change should be evaluated using a written change control system. Any changes or events that have the potential to affect the ability of the aseptic process to exclude contamination should be assessed through additional media fills. For example, facility and equipment modifications, line configuration changes, significant changes in personnel, anomalies in environmental testing results, container closure system changes, extended shutdowns, or end product sterility testing showing contaminated products may be cause for revalidation of the system.

When data from a media fill indicate the process may not be in control, an investigation should be conducted. Once corrections are instituted, process simulation run(s) should confirm that deficiencies have been corrected. When an investigation fails to reach well-supported, substantive conclusions, three consecutive successful runs in tandem with increased scrutiny of the production process may be warranted.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：「三次連續成功」的統計意義

要求三次連續成功，而不是一次，是有統計學依據的：單一成功可能是偶然的好運， 而三次連續成功才能建立對製程穩定性 (process consistency) 的信心。 反過來說，若三次結果發散（例如一次成功、一次失敗），則更清楚地暴露出製程 尚未受控 (out of control)。

注意：觸發額外 Media Fill 的事件清單

以下事件可能觸發需要額外 Media Fill 或再確效：

  • 廠房或設備重大修改
  • 生產線配置變更
  • 人員重大異動（關鍵操作人員更換）
  • 環境監控異常趨勢
  • 容器/塞子系統變更
  • 長期停產後復產
  • 成品無菌測試出現污染結果

這張清單應納入廠商的變更控制觸發事件矩陣，確保不遺漏任何觸發點。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

對 CDMO 而言，「每人每年至少參與一次」的要求意味著需要精心安排 Media Fill 的排程， 確保所有技術員、QC 人員、維修工程師都能在計畫中的 Media Fill 執行期間出現並參與。 若 CDMO 同時服務多條生產線，建議建立人員參與追蹤矩陣， 防止某些人員「過期」而需要臨時安排補做。 這也是 CDMO 在合約談判時，需要向客戶解釋的固定年度稽核成本之一。

Section IX.A.3 — Duration of Runs 執行持續時間 (p. 23)

原文內容 Original Text

The duration of aseptic processing operations is a major consideration in media fill design. Although the most accurate simulation model would be the full batch size and duration, other appropriate models can be justified. The duration of the media fill run should be determined by the time it takes to incorporate manipulations and interventions, as well as appropriate consideration of the duration of the actual aseptic processing operation. Interventions that commonly occur should be routinely simulated, while those occurring rarely can be simulated periodically.

When aseptic processing employs manual filling or closing, or extensive manual manipulations, the duration of the process simulation should generally be no less than the length of the actual manufacturing process to best simulate contamination risks posed by operators.

For lyophilization operations, FDA recommends that unsealed containers be exposed to partial evacuation of the chamber in a manner that simulates the process. Vials should not be frozen, and precautions should be taken that ensure that the medium remains in an aerobic state to avoid potentially inhibiting the growth of microorganisms.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：持續時間影響哪些風險？

Media Fill 的持續時間直接影響能否捕捉到「時間依賴性」的污染風險，包括：

  • 操作人員疲勞效應：長時間操作後，操作人員的無菌意識和動作準確性下降
  • 環境狀態劣化：長時間開啟 RABS/Isolator 後，環境微粒或微生物可能累積
  • 干預頻率累積：長時間運行，非例行干預（設備調整、堵塞清除等）發生機率增加

因此，「全程模擬」雖理想，但需根據製程特性合理設計。手動操作為主的製程 （如手動充填、手工蓋塞）必須做全程模擬，不能縮短。

注意：凍乾 (Lyophilization) Media Fill 的特殊要求

凍乾製程的 Media Fill 有兩個關鍵注意點：

  • 培養基不能冷凍：低溫會抑制微生物生長，使潛在污染無法被偵測
  • 必須維持有氧狀態：若進行真空抽取步驟，需確保培養基仍處於有氧環境， 否則好氧微生物無法生長，造成假陰性結果

實務上通常是模擬凍乾機的裝載過程（開放小瓶在凍乾機內暴露）， 然後恢復大氣壓、塞塞子，而不是真的進行凍乾程序。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

許多 CDMO 為了節省培養基成本或人力，傾向縮短 Media Fill 持續時間。 這在 FDA 查廠時是風險點——當生產批次是 16 小時連續操作， 而 Media Fill 只做了 4 小時，FDA 會質疑後 12 小時的風險是否被充分評估。 建議在 Media Fill 方案 (protocol) 中明確記錄持續時間的科學依據， 包括「如何覆蓋最差情境干預」的論述，而不僅僅是寫一個時間數字。

Section IX.A.4 — Size of Runs 模擬規模 (p. 23)

原文內容 Original Text

The simulation run sizes should be adequate to mimic commercial production conditions and accurately assess the potential for commercial batch contamination. The number of units filled during the process simulation should be based on contamination risk for a given process. A generally acceptable starting point for run size is in the range of 5,000 to 10,000 units. For operations with production sizes under 5,000, the number of media filled units should at least equal the maximum batch size made on the processing line.

When the possibility of contamination is higher based on the process design (e.g., manually intensive filling lines), a larger number of units, generally at or approaching the full production batch size, should be used. In contrast, a process conducted in an isolator can have a low risk of contamination and can be simulated with a lower number of units as a proportion of the overall operation.

Media fill size is an especially important consideration because some batches are produced over multiple shifts or yield an unusually large number of units. These factors should be carefully evaluated when designing the simulation.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：規模影響統計偵測靈敏度

Media Fill 的規模直接影響其統計靈敏度。規模越大， 能夠偵測到的低水平污染率越低。例如：

  • 10,000 支小瓶中出現 1 個陽性 = 0.01% 污染率
  • 1,000 支小瓶中出現 1 個陽性 = 0.1% 污染率

後者的靈敏度低 10 倍。FDA 對隔離器生產線允許較小規模， 是因為其固有的低污染風險使較低的統計靈敏度是可接受的， 但前提是需要科學論證。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

多班次生產是 CDMO 常見的商業模式，但 FDA 明確指出多班次批次需要特別考量。 若一批產品橫跨 3 個生產班次，Media Fill 的設計必須涵蓋所有班次的換班時刻， 否則 FDA 會認為換班帶來的污染風險未被評估。這也是為什麼每班次需獨立設計 半年度 Media Fill 的原因之一——確保每個班次的獨特操作習慣和人員行為都被驗證。

Section IX.A.5–6 — Line Speed & Environmental Conditions 線速度與環境條件 (pp. 23–24)

原文內容 Original Text

The media fill program should adequately address the range of line speeds employed during production. Each media fill run should evaluate a single line speed, and the speed chosen should be justified. For example, use of high line speed is often most appropriate in the evaluation of manufacturing processes characterized by frequent interventions or a significant degree of manual manipulation. Use of slow line speed is generally appropriate for evaluating manufacturing processes with prolonged exposure of the sterile drug product and containers/closures in the aseptic area.

Media fills should be adequately representative of the conditions under which actual manufacturing operations are conducted. An inaccurate assessment (making the process appear cleaner than it actually is) can result from conducting a media fill under extraordinary air particulate and microbial quality, or under production controls and precautions taken in preparation for the media fill. To the extent standard operating procedures permit stressful conditions (e.g., maximum number of personnel present and elevated activity level), it is important that media fills include analogous challenges. Stressful conditions do not include artificially created environmental extremes, such as reconfiguration of HVAC systems to operate at worst-case limits.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：高速 vs 低速——哪個是最差情境？

這是一個反直覺的重要觀念：

  • 高線速 (High Speed) 最差情境：頻繁干預的製程（如頻繁塞子卡住需手動清除）， 高速下干預頻率更高，每次干預都是污染機會，因此高速是最差情境。
  • 低線速 (Slow Speed) 最差情境：產品在無菌區暴露時間長的製程， 低速意味著每支小瓶在填充線上停留時間更長，環境暴露更多。

媒體填充方案需要根據製程特性選擇對應的「最差情境」線速，並書面說明選擇理由。

注意：「不能讓 Media Fill 看起來比日常生產更乾淨」

FDA 明確指出一個常見問題：廠商為了確保 Media Fill 通過， 在執行前進行特別清潔、減少人員、加強環境控制等措施， 使 Media Fill 環境優於正常生產環境， 這導致 Media Fill 結果無法真實反映商業生產的污染風險。

正確做法是：Media Fill 的環境條件應等同於正常商業生產的條件， 包括人員數量、活動水平、設備狀態。但不包括刻意惡化環境（如關閉部分 HVAC）， 那屬於人工創造最差情境，不合理。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

CDMO 常有客戶要求「在 Media Fill 執行日加強清潔」， 以確保結果漂亮。這是需要婉拒並解釋的請求——FDA 明確反對此做法， 而且若 FDA 發現 Media Fill 日的環境監控數據明顯優於日常生產日， 會直接質疑 Media Fill 的代表性，甚至要求重做。 保持 Media Fill 日與正常生產日一致的環境條件， 才是保護雙方的正確做法。

Section IX.A.7 — Media 培養基選擇 (pp. 24–25)

原文內容 Original Text

In general, a microbiological growth medium, such as soybean casein digest medium, should be used. Use of anaerobic growth media (e.g., fluid thioglycollate medium) should be considered in special circumstances. The media selected should be demonstrated to promote growth of gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria, and yeast and mold (e.g., USP indicator organisms).

Growth promotion units should be inoculated with a <100 CFU challenge. If the growth promotion testing fails, the origin of any contamination found during the simulation should nonetheless be investigated and the media fill promptly repeated.

The production process should be accurately simulated using media and conditions that optimize detection of any microbiological contamination. Each unit should be filled with an appropriate quantity and type of microbial growth medium to contact the inner container closure surfaces (when the unit is inverted or thoroughly swirled) and permit visual detection of microbial growth.

If the medium is handled properly and is promptly followed by the cleaning, sanitizing, and sterilization of equipment, subsequently processed products are not likely to be compromised.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：培養基選擇的科學依據

大豆酪蛋白消化培養基 (Soybean Casein Digest Medium, SCDM)， 又稱 Tryptic Soy Broth (TSB)，是 FDA 首選，因為它能促進廣泛微生物生長：

  • 革蘭氏陽性菌（如 Staphylococcus aureus）
  • 革蘭氏陰性菌（如 Escherichia coli）
  • 酵母菌（如 Candida albicans）
  • 黴菌（如 Aspergillus brasiliensis）

厭氧培養基（Fluid Thioglycollate Medium, FTM）只在特殊情況（如厭氧製程、 使用橡膠零件較多的複雜容器）時才需額外考慮。

注意：生長促進試驗 (Growth Promotion Test) 是 Media Fill 的品質把關

FDA 要求每批 Media Fill 培養基都需通過生長促進試驗，使用 <100 CFU 的低濃度挑戰， 確認培養基確實能促進微生物生長。這樣才能確保：

  • 陰性結果（無菌長出）是真正的無污染，而非培養基失效
  • 如果生長促進試驗失敗，即使 Media Fill 結果看似無污染， 也無法作為製程受控的佐證，需要重做

類比：培養基就像「污染偵探的放大鏡」

如果偵探的放大鏡本身有瑕疵（培養基無法促進微生物生長）， 那麼「沒有發現污染」就不是真的清白，只是工具失效。 生長促進試驗就是定期確認「偵探的放大鏡」工作正常的必要步驟。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

使用培養基代替藥液，最令廠商擔心的是「培養基會不會污染設備、影響下一批生產」。 FDA 的答案很明確：只要執行適當的清潔、消毒、必要時滅菌， 後續生產不太可能受影響。這說明 Media Fill 後的設備清潔驗證 (cleaning validation) 也是不可缺少的環節——若清潔 SOP 已被確效，則無需額外擔心。 這提醒 CDMO 確保 Media Fill SOP 與 Cleaning Validation 程序的無縫銜接。

Section IX.A.8 — Incubation and Examination 培養與檢查 (pp. 25–26)

原文內容 Original Text

Media units should be incubated under conditions adequate to detect microorganisms that might otherwise be difficult to culture. Incubation conditions should be established in accord with the following general guidelines:

  • Incubation temperature should be suitable for recovery of bioburden and environmental isolates and should at no time be outside the range of 20–35°C. Incubation temperature should be maintained within ±2.5°C of the target temperature.
  • Incubation time should not be less than 14 days. If two temperatures are used, the units should be incubated for at least 7 days at each temperature (starting with the lower temperature).

Each media-filled unit should be examined by personnel with appropriate education, training, and experience. All suspect units should be brought to the immediate attention of the QC microbiologist. To allow for visual detection, we recommend substituting clear containers for amber or other opaque containers.

Written procedures regarding aseptic interventions should be clear and specific (e.g., intervention type; quantity of units removed), providing for consistent production practices. If written procedures and batch documentation are adequate to describe an associated clearance, the intervention units removed during media fills do not need to be incubated. In no case should more units be removed during a media fill intervention than would be cleared during a production run.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：干預單位的處理——一個法規灰色地帶

「干預移除的單位是否需要培養」是 Media Fill 管理的重要細節：

  • 不需要培養的條件：書面 SOP 明確規定了干預後需移除的單位數量， 且批次記錄確認了遵守情況。
  • 必須培養的情況：SOP 缺乏具體說明，或移除數量超出 SOP 規定。
  • 嚴格限制：Media Fill 中干預移除的單位數， 不能超過實際生產批次中移除的數量。

FDA 擔心的是：廠商透過移除大量「干預單位」來刻意避開潛在的污染單位進入培養計數。

注意：培養後發現損壞單位的處理

培養期間若發現損壞（non-integral）單位，不能直接排除在計數外。 任何排除必須充分說明理由，並在 Media Fill 報告中解釋偏差原因。 若損壞與微生物污染之間存在相關性，必須進行深入調查。 這一要求防止了廠商以「損壞」為由移除疑似陽性單位。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

建立完善的 Media Fill 干預記錄表是 CDMO 的必要工具， 記錄每次干預的類型、時間點、移除單位數，並在後續 Media Fill 報告中逐一核對。 這不只是 FDA 要求，也是 CDMO 向客戶展示製程受控程度的有力文件。 當 FDA 查廠稽核師翻閱 Media Fill 批次記錄時， 清晰的干預記錄可以大幅降低進一步質疑的可能性。

Section IX.A.9 — Interpretation of Test Results 結果判讀 (pp. 26–27)

原文內容 Original Text

Any contaminated unit should be considered objectionable and investigated. The microorganisms should be identified to species level. The investigation should survey the possible causes of contamination. Any failure investigation should assess the impact on commercial drugs produced on the line since the last media fill.

Whenever contamination exists in a media fill run, it should be considered indicative of a potential sterility assurance problem, regardless of run size. Modern aseptic processing operations in suitably designed facilities have demonstrated a capability of meeting contamination levels approaching zero. Recommended criteria for assessing state of aseptic line control are as follows:

  • When filling fewer than 5,000 units: no contaminated units should be detected. One (1) contaminated unit is considered cause for revalidation, following an investigation.
  • When filling from 5,000 to 10,000 units: One (1) contaminated unit should result in an investigation, including consideration of a repeat media fill. Two (2) contaminated units are considered cause for revalidation.
  • When filling more than 10,000 units: One (1) contaminated unit should result in an investigation. Two (2) contaminated units are considered cause for revalidation.

A firm's use of media fill acceptance criteria allowing infrequent contamination does not mean that a distributed lot of drug product may contain a nonsterile unit. Invalidation of a media fill run should be a rare occurrence.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：「接近零污染」是現代無菌製程的標準

FDA 明確指出：現代設計良好的無菌製程設施，其 Media Fill 污染率應接近零。 接受準則中的「允許 1 個污染單位進行調查」，並不是廠商的正常操作目標， 而是監管容忍的最低門檻。

更重要的原則是：即使污染率未達到觸發再確效的門檻， 若反覆出現零星污染，也是製程失控的警示訊號， 需要啟動趨勢調查，而不是依靠「接受準則仍在範圍內」自我安慰。

注意：Media Fill 無效化 (Invalidation) 必須謹慎

FDA 明確表示，Media Fill 無效化應屬罕見事件。 僅在商業批次也需要以同樣方式處理的情況下，才能中止 Media Fill。 這防止了廠商以各種理由「廢掉」不利的 Media Fill 結果而不計入記錄。 任何無效化都需要完整的文件化支持和理由說明。

類比：Media Fill 接受準則 ≠ 商業批次放行標準

Media Fill 允許「有條件接受」1 個污染單位，這並不意味著 商業批次出貨時可以含有一個非無菌單位。兩者是完全不同的概念： Media Fill 是製程驗證工具，其接受準則反映的是製程能力評估的統計容忍度； 而商業批次必須做到每一支都無菌。 FDA 在這裡明確說明了這一重要區別，避免廠商混淆。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

CDMO 對於 Media Fill 陽性結果的調查深度，往往是 FDA 查廠時的重點審查對象。 調查報告至少需包含：污染菌的種別鑑定（到種的層級）、 可能污染來源的系統性分析（人員、設備、環境、材料）、 對前次 Media Fill 以來所有商業批次的影響評估（是否需要啟動商業批次的額外測試或召回評估）， 以及矯正預防措施 (CAPA) 的具體內容和驗證計畫。 不完整的調查報告是 FDA 查廠最常提出的 483 觀察項目之一。

Section IX.B — Filtration Efficacy 過濾效能確效 (pp. 27–28)

原文內容 Original Text

Filtration is a common method of sterilizing drug product solutions. A sterilizing grade filter should be validated to reproducibly remove viable microorganisms from the process stream, producing a sterile effluent. Currently, such filters usually have a rated pore size of 0.2 µm or smaller. Use of redundant sterilizing filters should be considered in many cases.

Validation should include microbiological challenges to simulate worst-case production conditions. Product bioburden should be evaluated when selecting a suitable challenge microorganism. The microorganism Brevundimonas diminuta (ATCC 19146) when properly grown, harvested and used, is a common challenge microorganism for 0.2 µm rated filters because of its small size (0.3 µm mean diameter). A challenge concentration of at least 107 organisms per cm2 of effective filtration area should generally be used.

Direct inoculation into the drug formulation is the preferred method because it provides an assessment of the effect of drug product on the filter matrix and on the challenge organism.

Factors that can affect filter performance include: (1) viscosity and surface tension, (2) pH, (3) compatibility of formulation components with the filter, (4) pressures, (5) flow rates, (6) maximum use time, (7) temperature, (8) osmolality, and (9) effects of hydraulic shock. Filter validation should be conducted using the worst-case conditions.

Integrity testing of the filter(s) should be routinely performed post-use. Forward flow and bubble point tests are two integrity tests that can be used.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為何選擇 Brevundimonas diminuta 作為挑戰菌？

Brevundimonas diminuta（舊名 Pseudomonas diminuta，ATCC 19146） 的選擇有其明確的科學邏輯：

  • 尺寸小：平均直徑僅 0.3 µm，比 0.2 µm 濾膜孔徑稍大， 是能被 0.2 µm 濾膜保留的最小常見細菌之一，代表最具挑戰性的穿透風險
  • 生長穩定性：在標準條件下能重複性地生長到均勻尺寸分佈， 確保挑戰的重現性
  • 非病原性：實驗室操作安全

挑戰濃度 ≥ 10⁷ 個/cm² 過濾面積的設計，提供了遠超生產中預期生物負荷的安全邊際。

注意：完整性測試 (Integrity Test) 必須在使用後執行

許多廠商只在使用前進行完整性測試（如泡點測試 Bubble Point Test 或前向流測試 Forward Flow Test），卻忽略了使用後測試的重要性。 FDA 明確要求：過濾後必須進行完整性測試，因為使用前測試無法偵測到 在過濾過程中才發生的濾膜損傷（如過濾壓差過大造成的微孔破裂）。 若使用後完整性測試失敗，則該批過濾液的無菌性受到質疑， 需要啟動偏差調查和風險評估。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

過濾效能確效往往是 CDMO 接受客戶委託時，最容易被忽視的確效項目。 常見問題包括：使用客戶提供的確效數據，但未確認數據適用於 CDMO 的特定製程條件（如壓力、溫度、濾器批號）； 或使用了與確效研究不同批號/供應商的濾膜。 FDA 明確指出：確效數據必須適用於使用者的特定產品和使用條件， 不同條件下的過濾性能可能顯著不同。 CDMO 需要建立完整的濾膜身份確認 (filter identity verification) 程序， 確保生產用濾膜與確效用濾膜一致（相同聚合物、相同孔徑規格）。

Section IX.C — Sterilization of Equipment, Containers, and Closures 設備、容器及塞子滅菌 (pp. 28–30)

原文內容 Original Text

Equipment surfaces that contact sterilized drug product or its sterilized containers or closures must be sterile (§ 211.67 and 211.113). Sterility of aseptic processing equipment should normally be maintained by sterilization between each batch.

Validation studies should be conducted to demonstrate the efficacy of the sterilization cycle. The specific load configurations, as well as biological indicator and temperature sensor locations, should be documented in validation records.

It is important to remove air from the autoclave chamber as part of a steam sterilization cycle. The insulating properties of air interfere with the ability of steam to transfer its energy to the load.

Empty chamber studies evaluate numerous locations throughout a sterilizing unit to confirm uniformity of conditions (e.g., temperature, pressure). Heat penetration studies should be performed using the established sterilizer loads.

A sterility assurance level (SAL) of 10-6 or better should be demonstrated for a sterilization process. Cycle specifications should be based on the delivery of adequate lethality to the slowest to heat locations.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：SAL 10⁻⁶ 是什麼意思？

無菌保證水準 (Sterility Assurance Level, SAL) 10⁻⁶ 意味著： 每 100 萬個滅菌後的物品中，最多允許有一個倖存的微生物。 這是衛生標準中極高的要求，相當於「百萬分之一的不良率」。

為達到 SAL 10⁻⁶，滅菌周期的設計必須基於最難被殺滅的位置（冷點）， 確保即使是最難傳熱的位置，也能接收足夠的致死熱量。這也是為何 熱穿透研究 (heat penetration study) 和生物指示劑 (biological indicator) 測試必不可少——單靠溫度傳感器的數據是不夠的。

注意：蒸汽中的空氣 (Air) 是致命的確效殺手

高壓蒸汽滅菌的工作原理是飽和蒸汽傳遞能量。 空氣是熱的絕緣體，如果高壓釜腔體內殘留空氣， 被空氣包圍的物品（如密閉的套管、折疊的布包）實際溫度可能比腔體溫度低好幾度， 導致表面上看起來達到 121°C，但冷點實際上可能只有 115°C 甚至更低， SAL 目標根本無法達成。 因此排氣步驟（如預真空程序）是蒸汽滅菌周期設計的關鍵， 需要在確效中驗證其有效性。

核心概念：SIP 系統的特殊挑戰

就地滅菌 (Sterilize-in-Place, SIP) 系統（如大型配製罐的管路滅菌） 面臨特殊的確效挑戰：管路中安裝的過濾器會在其兩端產生顯著壓差， 導致過濾器下游溫度明顯低於上游。 FDA 建議將生物指示劑放置在過濾器的下游位置， 確保確效數據反映了最難殺滅的位置。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

CDMO 在滅菌確效方面最常見的稽核問題包括：

  • 裝載構型變更未再確效：客戶更換了不同尺寸的容器或更改了裝載密度， 但未評估是否需要重新進行熱穿透研究
  • 生物指示劑擺放位置不代表最差情境：習慣性放置於「以前一直放的位置」， 而非根據最新的空腔製圖結果重新評估冷點
  • 儀器校正記錄不完整：確效用溫度傳感器在研究前後的校正記錄缺失或過期

建立以裝載構型為索引的滅菌確效矩陣， 記錄每種構型的確效狀態、冷點位置和生物指示劑佈置， 是應對查廠的最有效備查工具。

Section IX.C.2 — Equipment Controls and Instrument Calibration 設備控制與儀器校正 (pp. 30–31)

原文內容 Original Text

For both validation and routine process control, the reliability of the data generated by sterilization cycle monitoring devices should be considered to be of the utmost importance. Devices that measure cycle parameters should be routinely calibrated. Written procedures should be established to ensure these devices are maintained in a calibrated state.

  • Temperature and pressure monitoring devices for heat sterilization should be calibrated at suitable intervals. The sensing devices used for validation studies should be calibrated before and after validation runs.
  • Dwell time monitoring devices should be periodically calibrated.
  • The microbial count of a biological indicator should be confirmed. If the reliability of a vendor's Certificate of Analysis is established through an appropriate qualification program, the D-value can be accepted in lieu of confirmatory testing of each lot.
  • For dry heat depyrogenation tunnels, devices used to measure belt speed should be routinely calibrated. Bacterial endotoxin challenges should be appropriately prepared and measured.

Equipment control should be ensured through placement of measuring devices at those control points that are most likely to rapidly detect unexpected process variability. Routine evaluation of sterilizer performance-indicating attributes, such as equilibrium (come up) time is important.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：D 值 (D-value) 是什麼？

D 值 (D-value) 是滅菌科學的核心參數：指在特定溫度下， 將微生物群體減少一個 log（即 90% 的微生物被殺滅）所需的時間（分鐘）。

例如：D₁₂₁ = 1.5 分鐘意味著在 121°C 下，每 1.5 分鐘微生物數量減少 10 倍。 若初始生物負荷為 10⁶，達到 SAL 10⁻⁶ 需要額外殺滅 12 個 log 量級， 所需時間 = 12 × D 值。這就是滅菌周期時間計算的科學依據。

注意：確效用傳感器需在研究前後各校正一次

這是一個常被忽視但 FDA 明確要求的規定：用於確效研究的溫度傳感器， 必須在確效開始前和確效完成後都進行校正。 原因是：如果只有研究前校正，無法排除傳感器在研究過程中發生偏移； 研究後校正可以確認整個確效過程中的溫度數據是可靠的。 若研究後校正發現偏差超過規格，則整個確效結果的有效性都需要重新評估。

類比：「均衡時間 (Equilibrium/Come Up Time)」就像烤箱預熱

當你把麵糰放入烤箱，不是一插上電源就開始計時——你要等到烤箱 真正達到設定溫度並穩定後，才開始計算烘烤時間。 高壓蒸汽滅菌的「均衡時間 (come up time)」是同樣概念： 蒸汽進入腔體後，需要一段時間才能使腔體內所有位置的溫度均勻達到目標值， 這段「爬升穩定時間」必須不計入有效殺菌時間內。 若均衡時間異常延長，通常代表設備老化、閥門問題或其他需要調查的工程異常。

CDMO/COO 操作應用

對 CDMO 而言，維護滅菌儀器校正系統（溫度、壓力、時間、皮帶速度） 是 FDA 查廠時的標準審查項目。建議建立儀器校正主計畫 (Instrument Calibration Master Plan)， 並確保所有確效用儀器都有獨立的、高於生產用儀器標準的校正頻率。 此外，若客戶提供了特殊的生物指示劑（自行製備或非商業來源）， 必須確保其 D 值已透過適當的資格確認程序驗證， 不能直接以供應商 CoA 作為替代，除非已完成供應商確認計畫。

A. Environmental Monitoring — 1. General Written Program 環境監測：書面計畫總則

原文內容 Original Text

In aseptic processing, one of the most important laboratory controls is the environmental monitoring program. This program provides meaningful information on the quality of the aseptic processing environment (e.g., when a given batch is being manufactured) as well as environmental trends of ancillary clean areas. Environmental monitoring should promptly identify potential routes of contamination, allowing for implementation of corrections before product contamination occurs (211.42 and 211.113).

Evaluating the quality of air and surfaces in the cleanroom environment should start with a well-defined written program and scientifically sound methods. The monitoring program should cover all production shifts and include air, floors, walls, and equipment surfaces, including the critical surfaces that come in contact with the product, container, and closures.

It is important that locations posing the most microbiological risk to the product be a key part of the program. It is especially important to monitor the microbiological quality of the critical area to determine whether or not aseptic conditions are maintained during filling and closing activities. Air and surface samples should be taken at the locations where significant activity or product exposure occurs during production.

Critical surface sampling should be performed at the conclusion of the aseptic processing operation to avoid direct contact with sterile surfaces during processing. Detection of microbial contamination on a critical site would not necessarily result in batch rejection. The contaminated critical site sample should prompt an investigation of operational information and data that includes an awareness of the potential for a low incidence of false positives.

Environmental monitoring methods do not always recover microorganisms present in the sampled area. Because false negatives can occur, consecutive growth results are only one type of adverse trend. Increased incidence of contamination over a given period is an equal or more significant trend to be tracked. In the absence of any adverse trend, a single result above an action level should trigger an evaluation and a determination about whether remedial measures may be appropriate.

All environmental monitoring locations should be described in SOPs with sufficient detail to allow for reproducible sampling. Written SOPs should also address:

  • (1) frequency of sampling
  • (2) when the samples are taken (during or at the conclusion of operations)
  • (3) duration of sampling
  • (4) sample size (e.g., surface area, air volume)
  • (5) specific sampling equipment and techniques
  • (6) alert and action levels
  • (7) appropriate response to deviations from alert or action levels

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：環境監測計畫 (EM Program) 的目的

EM 計畫不只是合規形式，它是無菌製程的「即時健康追蹤系統」。 FDA 明確指出，EM 計畫的兩個核心功能是：

  • 即時資訊：在特定批次生產時，確認環境符合無菌條件
  • 趨勢資料：長期追蹤輔助潔淨區（走廊、更衣室）的微生物狀況

最重要的是「先發制人」——在污染發生前，透過趨勢識別潛在風險路徑。 這與事後的產品無菌試驗 (Sterility Test) 性質完全不同：EM 是製程監控工具， 不是批次放行的替代品。

類比：EM 計畫就像醫院的感染控制系統

想像一間醫院手術室：不是等病人感染了才消毒，而是持續監測環境菌落數、 對空氣品質做週期性採樣，建立「正常基準值」，一旦偏離就立即調查原因。 無菌製程的 EM 計畫邏輯完全相同——預防導向，趨勢驅動。

關鍵要點：關鍵表面取樣時機

FDA 明確要求：關鍵表面 (Critical Surface) 的取樣必須在無菌操作「結束後」進行， 而非操作中途，以避免取樣行為本身污染無菌接觸面。 這在 CDMO 操作中常被誤解——有些廠商在無菌灌裝進行中途插入 contact plate 取樣， 此舉反而構成污染風險，且 FDA 稽核員非常熟悉這個錯誤。

關鍵要點：陰性結果不代表安全

EM 方法的偵測效率本來就不完美——假陰性 (False Negative) 是結構性問題， 不是特例。因此，FDA 要求不只追蹤「連續陽性」，更要追蹤：

  • 同一地點的污染頻率增加（即使每次都低於行動水準）
  • 跨班次、跨房間的空間擴散趨勢
  • 菌種組成的質變（從常見皮膚菌轉為環境菌或產孢菌）

CDMO 操作落地：SOP 七要素

FDA 明確列出 EM SOP 必須涵蓋的七個要素。稽核員在查廠時通常會逐條核對。 以下是 CDMO 常見缺失：

  • 取樣位置圖不夠詳細，無法重複再現 → 補充含坐標的平面圖
  • 只寫「操作後取樣」但未定義「操作後多久內」→ 加入具體時限
  • 警示/行動水準設定無科學依據文件 → 補充設定依據報告
  • 偏差回應程序未區分單點超標 vs 趨勢超標 → 分級處置流程
A.2 Establishing Levels and a Trending Program 建立水準值與趨勢計畫

原文內容 Original Text

Microbiological monitoring levels should be established based on the relationship of the sampled location to the operation. The levels should be based on the need to maintain adequate microbiological control throughout the entire sterile manufacturing facility. One should also consider environmental monitoring data from historical databases, media fills, cleanroom qualification, and sanitization studies, in developing monitoring levels. Data from similar operations can also be helpful in setting action and alert levels, especially for a new operation.

Environmental monitoring data will provide information on the quality of the manufacturing environment. Each individual sample result should be evaluated for its significance by comparison to the alert or action levels. Averaging of results can mask unacceptable localized conditions. A result at the alert level urges attention to the approaching action conditions. A result at the action level should prompt a more thorough investigation.

Trend reports should include data generated by location, shift, room, operator, or other parameters. The quality control unit should be responsible for producing specialized data reports (e.g., a search on a particular isolate over a year period) with the goal of investigating results beyond established levels and identifying any appropriate follow-up actions. Significant changes in microbial flora should be considered in the review of the ongoing environmental monitoring data.

Written procedures should define the system whereby the most responsible managers are regularly informed and updated on trends and investigations.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：Alert Level vs Action Level 的本質差異

這是 FDA 查廠中最常被考核的觀念之一，必須徹底理解：

重要警告：不可對EM結果取平均值

FDA 明確警告：不得對不同位置的EM數據取平均值（Averaging of results can mask unacceptable localized conditions）。 這是稽核中常見的 483 觀察項目——某些廠商將同一房間多個取樣點的 CFU 數平均後評估， 導致某個局部嚴重污染點被「稀釋」掩蓋。
每一個取樣位置的每一次結果都應獨立評估。

類比：水準值就像汽車的儀表板警示燈

Alert Level 就像「油表快到 1/4 格」的橘燈——還可以繼續開，但應該找加油站了。 Action Level 就像「油表亮紅燈」——必須立刻停車加油，繼續開可能會拋錨。 而「取平均值」就像把駕駛員的油表讀數平均後才決定加不加油——完全失去即時告警的意義。

CDMO 趨勢報告實務建議

FDA 要求趨勢報告涵蓋：位置、班次、房間、操作員等多個維度。 CDMO 的 EM 資料庫應能支援以下查詢類型：

  • 單一操作員在過去一年的個人採樣結果趨勢
  • 特定菌種（如 Bacillus cereus）的時間分佈
  • 班次間差異分析（夜班 vs 白班的污染頻率）
  • 媒填批次前後的環境數據對比

此外，FDA 強調管理層需定期收到趨勢資訊更新， 這意味著 EM 數據不只是 QC 的工作，而是廠務管理決策的依據。

A.3 Disinfection Efficacy 消毒劑有效性

原文內容 Original Text

The suitability, efficacy, and limitations of disinfecting agents and procedures should be assessed. The effectiveness of these disinfectants and procedures should be measured by their ability to ensure that potential contaminants are adequately removed from surfaces.

To prevent introduction of contamination, disinfectants should be sterile, appropriately handled in suitable (e.g., sterile) containers and used for no longer than the predefined period specified by written procedures. Routinely used disinfectants should be effective against the normal microbial vegetative flora recovered from the facility.

Many common disinfectants are ineffective against spores. For example, 70 percent isopropyl alcohol is ineffective against Bacillus spp. spores. Therefore, a sound disinfectant program also includes a sporicidal agent, used according to a written schedule and when environmental data suggest the presence of sporeforming organisms.

Disinfection procedures should be described in sufficient detail (e.g., preparation, work sequence, contact time) to enable reproducibility. Once the procedures are established, their adequacy should be evaluated using a routine environmental monitoring program. If indicated, microorganisms associated with adverse trends can be investigated as to their sensitivity to the disinfectants employed in the cleanroom in which the organisms were isolated.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：消毒劑計畫的三層架構

一個符合 FDA 期望的消毒劑計畫至少需要三層保護：

  • 第一層：日常消毒劑——針對常見菌的基礎消毒（如 70% IPA）
  • 第二層：輪換消毒劑——定期更換作用機制，防止菌株適應（如換用季銨鹽）
  • 第三層：殺孢劑 (Sporicide)——定期或依 EM 數據觸發使用（如過氧化氫蒸汽、二氧化氯）

重要警示：70% IPA 無法殺死 Bacillus 芽孢

這是 FDA 在指引中罕見地以具體例子指出的要點，代表 FDA 在稽核中非常重視此事。 Bacillus 屬（如 B. cereus、B. subtilis）能形成高度耐受環境的芽孢， 70% IPA 對其完全無效。

若 EM 數據出現 Bacillus spp.，這是立即觸發殺孢劑的信號。 若廠房的消毒計畫中沒有殺孢劑輪換程序，FDA 幾乎必然列出 483 觀察項目。

類比：消毒劑輪換就像抗生素療程設計

長期只用同一種消毒劑，就像一直給同一種抗生素——細菌會「學習」並產生耐受性。 消毒劑的輪換計畫 (Rotation Program) 就是用不同作用機制打擊微生物， 防止特定菌株在潔淨室中建立生態優勢地位。 而殺孢劑的使用則是「當普通藥效果不夠時的強效療程」。

CDMO 消毒劑計畫稽核要點

查廠員在審核消毒劑計畫時通常會核查：

  • 消毒劑的無菌性確認（是否有 COA 或自行過濾滅菌記錄？）
  • 消毒劑的有效期限與配製記錄（配製日期、有效至日期、配製人員）
  • 接觸時間 (Contact Time) 是否有書面規定並可驗證
  • 殺孢劑使用觸發條件是否明確定義（如每季固定執行 + EM 陽性觸發）
  • 消毒劑有效性驗證報告（Disinfectant Efficacy Study）是否涵蓋代表性菌株
  • 當 EM 趨勢出現特定菌株時，是否評估其對現行消毒劑的敏感性

A.4b Monitoring Methods — Active Air Monitoring 主動空氣監測

原文內容 Original Text

b. Active Air Monitoring

Assessing microbial quality of air should involve the use of active devices including but not limited to impaction, centrifugal, and membrane (or gelatin) samplers. Each device has certain advantages and disadvantages, although all allow testing of the number of organisms per volume of air sampled. We recommend that such devices be used during each production shift to evaluate aseptic processing areas at carefully chosen locations.

Manufacturers should be aware of a device's air monitoring capabilities, and the air sampler should be evaluated for its suitability for use in an aseptic environment based on:

  • collection efficiency
  • cleanability
  • ability to be sterilized
  • disruption of unidirectional airflow

Manufacturers should ensure that such devices are calibrated and used according to appropriate procedures. The volume of air sampled should be sufficient to yield meaningful measurements of air quality in a given environment.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

四項設備評估標準的實務意涵

FDA 要求從四個維度評估主動式採樣設備，CDMO 常遇到的問題包括：

  • Collection Efficiency（採集效率）：不同設備在低濃度環境的偵測敏感度差異大，ISO Class 5 的低濃度環境尤其重要
  • Cleanability（可清潔性）：設備必須能在潔淨室內清潔且不留死角，設計複雜的設備往往難以確保清潔驗證
  • Ability to be Sterilized（可滅菌性）：進入 Grade A/ISO 5 區域的設備或其接觸部件必須能被滅菌
  • Disruption of Unidirectional Airflow（氣流干擾）：設備體積、形狀不得破壞 UDAF（單向流），否則反而創造渦流污染點

CDMO 主動採樣操作規範

每個生產班次都應執行主動空氣取樣，FDA 強調的是「每班次」而非每天或每批次。 這對 24 小時連續生產的 CDMO 而言意味著：夜班也需要受訓的操作員執行 EM 取樣， 且夜班數據必須被同等審核，不能因人力不足而降低夜班監測頻率。

設備校驗記錄（流量校驗、計時器校驗）必須完整，且校驗週期需有書面規定。 稽核員查到設備校驗逾期而仍在使用，是即時開 483 的情況之一。

A.4c Monitoring Methods — Passive Air Monitoring (Settling Plates) 被動空氣監測（沉降碟）

原文內容 Original Text

c. Passive Air Monitoring (Settling Plates)

Another method is the use of passive air samplers, such as settling plates (petri dishes containing nutrient growth medium exposed to the environment). Because only microorganisms that settle onto the agar surface are detected, settling plates can be used as qualitative, or semi-quantitative, air monitors.

Their value in critical areas will be enhanced by ensuring that plates are positioned in locations posing the greatest risk of product contamination. As part of methods validation, the quality control laboratory should evaluate what media exposure conditions optimize recovery of low levels of environmental isolates. Exposure conditions should preclude desiccation (e.g., caused by lengthy sampling periods and/or high airflows), which inhibits recovery of microorganisms.

The data generated by passive air sampling can be useful when considered in combination with results from other types of air samples.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

沉降碟 vs 主動採樣的關係

沉降碟是「只抓得到會自然落下的微生物」——依靠重力沉降， 不能主動截獲懸浮微粒，因此 FDA 明確定性為定性或半定量工具， 不能替代主動採樣成為主要監測方法。

但沉降碟有一個主動採樣器無法提供的優勢：它可以連續暴露整個操作期間， 捕捉整個批次生產過程中任何時間點的落菌事件，而主動採樣通常只採幾分鐘。

乾燥化 (Desiccation) 問題——常被忽略的陷阱

FDA 特別警告乾燥化問題：在 Grade A 區域，HEPA 過濾的高速氣流 會在數小時內讓沉降碟的培養基表面快速乾燥， 導致即使有微生物落下也無法生長，形成假陰性。

解決方案：在方法驗證時確定每個位置的最大安全暴露時間， 超過此時限的沉降碟結果可能失去意義。 部分廠商使用帶蓋、半開的設計或選用含中和劑的培養基來延緩乾燥。

沉降碟放置策略

FDA 強調沉降碟應放置在「產品污染風險最高的位置」。 在灌裝作業中，這通常意味著：

  • 灌裝針頭正下方區域（但需確保不干擾操作）
  • 瓶蓋上蓋區域附近
  • 操作員雙手活動最頻繁的區域兩側
  • 機器人幹預動線的起點與終點

沉降碟的位置圖必須歸入 EM SOP，且實際放置位置需與圖紙一致， 偏差需記錄說明。

B. Microbiological Media and Identification 微生物培養基與菌種鑑定

原文內容 Original Text

Characterization of recovered microorganisms provides vital information for the environmental monitoring program. Environmental isolates often correlate with the contaminants found in a media fill or product sterility testing failure, and the overall environmental picture provides valuable information for an investigation.

Monitoring critical and immediately surrounding clean areas as well as personnel should include routine identification of microorganisms to the species (or, where appropriate, genus) level. In some cases, environmental trending data have revealed migration of microorganisms into the aseptic processing room from either uncontrolled or lesser controlled areas.

Genotypic methods have been shown to be more accurate and precise than traditional biochemical and phenotypic techniques. These methods are especially valuable for investigations into failures (e.g., sterility test; media fill contamination).

The microbiological culture media used in environmental monitoring should be validated as capable of detecting fungi (i.e., yeasts and molds) as well as bacteria and incubated at appropriate conditions of time and temperature:

  • Total aerobic bacterial count: 30–35°C for 48–72 hours
  • Total combined yeast and mold count: 20–25°C for 5–7 days

Incoming lots of environmental monitoring media should be tested for their ability to reliably recover microorganisms. Growth promotion testing should be performed on all lots of prepared media. Where appropriate, inactivating agents should be used to prevent inhibition of growth by cleanroom disinfectants or product residuals (e.g., antibiotics).

核心概念：菌種鑑定為何重要？

只記錄「CFU 數量」是不夠的——知道是什麼菌才能判斷：

  • 污染來自何處（皮膚菌？環境菌？外來汙染源？）
  • 是否與媒填或無菌試驗失敗中發現的菌相同（關鍵鑑識工具）
  • 是否有菌種從低管制區遷移進入無菌處理區
  • 現行消毒劑是否對此菌有效

FDA 要求常規鑑定至種 (Species) 層級，不只是屬 (Genus) 層級， 因為即使是同屬的不同種菌，其致病性、消毒劑耐受性和溯源意義可能差異很大。

培養條件速查表

基因型鑑定 vs 表現型鑑定的選擇

常規 EM 監測：傳統生化/表現型方法（如 API strips、VITEK）可接受， 成本低、速度快，適合日常大量鑑定。

調查失敗事件時：FDA 建議使用基因型鑑定 （如 16S rRNA 定序、MALDI-TOF）——準確度更高，可提供菌株層級的比對， 確認媒填污染菌與 EM 分離菌是否為同一來源，這是調查報告的關鍵證據。

培養基收貨管制：Growth Promotion Test 的 CDMO 實務

每批收到的 EM 培養基（如 TSA plates、SDA plates）必須執行 促生長試驗 (GPT)，以確認培養基的回收能力。 常見的 CDMO 問題：

  • GPT 菌株未包含環境中常見的菌種（只用 USP 標準菌株，未加入環境菌株）
  • 含抗生素產品殘留的取樣環境中未加入中和劑 (Neutralizing Agent) （如 lecithin + polysorbate 80 中和季銨鹽），導致假陰性
  • 培養基有效期內未重新驗證，開封後未限定使用期限

特別注意：若 CDMO 同時生產含抗生素的產品，抗生素殘留極可能抑制 EM 培養基上的菌落生長。培養基中必須加入合適的中和劑， 且中和效果需經過驗證。

C. Prefiltration Bioburden 過濾前生物負載

原文內容 Original Text

Manufacturing process controls should be designed to minimize the bioburden in the unfiltered product. In addition to increasing the challenge to the sterilizing filter, bioburden can contribute impurities (e.g., endotoxin) to, and lead to degradation of, the drug product. A prefiltration bioburden limit should be established.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為什麼要控制前過濾生物負載？

無菌過濾（滅菌過濾）的設計假設是：進入過濾器的微生物量（bioburden） 在一個可合理管控的低水準。若 bioburden 過高，會有三個後果：

  • 過濾器挑戰量增加：超過過濾器的設計容量，可能導致穿透失效
  • 內毒素 (Endotoxin) 升高：即使微生物被過濾掉， 革蘭氏陰性菌的細胞壁碎片（內毒素）可通過 0.22μm 過濾器進入產品
  • 產品降解：高 bioburden 代表高酶活性， 某些菌可分泌蛋白酶、核酸酶，在過濾前就降解藥物原料

類比：前過濾管制就像淨水前的沉澱池

自來水廠不會把污水直接推入精密過濾器——先要沉澱、前處理， 降低懸浮物和微生物量，再讓精密膜過濾發揮最終把關作用。 無菌過濾的道理完全相同：前過濾 bioburden 管制是過濾器壽命和效能的保護措施， 也是產品品質的第一道防線。

Prefiltration Bioburden Limit 設定依據

前過濾生物負載限值通常設定在 10 CFU/100 mL（依產品特性可能更嚴）， 且應基於過濾器驗證時所使用的挑戰菌量。 FDA 要求此限值必須書面建立，且日常製程必須有相應的監測計畫。

若 bioburden 結果超過設定限值，需立即調查原因： 原料 bioburden 偏高？配製過程中暴露時間過長？配製用水 (WFI/PW) 微生物偏高？ 這些都是需要在偏差報告中系統性排查的項目。

CDMO 前過濾 Bioburden 監測計畫

在技術轉移或工藝開發階段，CDMO 應與客戶確認前過濾 bioburden 的：

  • 取樣時機（過濾前立即取樣，避免暴露時間延長）
  • 取樣體積（通常取 100 mL 以確保足夠靈敏度）
  • 限值設定依據（需有書面文件）
  • 偏差回應流程（超標時是否需要重新評估過濾驗證的有效性？）

特別注意：生物製劑（蛋白質、抗體）配製液比化學藥品更容易受微生物影響， 因為培養基成分豐富，bioburden 增長速度更快，需要更嚴格的配製時限管制。

D. Alternate Microbiological Test Methods 替代微生物試驗方法

原文內容 Original Text

Other suitable microbiological test methods (e.g., rapid test methods) can be considered for environmental monitoring, in-process control testing, and finished product release testing after it is demonstrated that the methods are equivalent or better than traditional methods (e.g., USP).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：快速微生物方法 (Rapid Microbiological Methods, RMM)

傳統無菌試驗需要 14 天；傳統 EM 計數需要 3–7 天。 快速微生物方法 (RMM) 旨在大幅縮短這個時間——從數天縮短到數小時甚至即時結果。 FDA 在 2004 年指引中已開放 RMM 用於：

  • 環境監測 (EM)
  • 製程中管制測試 (In-Process Control Testing)
  • 成品放行測試 (Finished Product Release Testing)

採用 RMM 的前提：等效或更優

FDA 的允許條件非常明確：必須證明新方法等效於或優於傳統方法（如 USP 標準方法）。 這意味著需要完整的方法等效性驗證 (Method Equivalency Validation)，包括：

  • 準確度 (Accuracy)、精密度 (Precision)
  • 靈敏度 (Sensitivity) 下限不低於傳統方法
  • 特異性 (Specificity)——不誤判
  • 與傳統方法的比對數據（至少 30 組平行樣本）

若用於成品放行，通常需要提交 NDA/ANDA/BLA 補充申請， 在美國需經 FDA 核准後方可替代 USP 法定方法。

CDMO 導入 RMM 的商業考量

快速微生物方法的潛在優勢對 CDMO 極具商業價值：

  • 縮短批次放行時間：從傳統無菌試驗 14 天縮至數小時（如 BacT/ALERT, BACTEC）
  • 更快的製程偏差響應：EM 數據次日即可取得，利於即時糾正
  • 競爭優勢：能提供更快速放行服務的 CDMO 對客戶更具吸引力

挑戰：初期驗證成本高、設備投資大、法規審查程序複雜。 因此，CDMO 通常優先在 EM 用途導入 RMM（監管要求較寬鬆）， 再逐步擴展至成品放行測試。

Section XI — Sterility Testing 無菌性檢驗（概覽）

原文內容 Original Text

21 CFR 211.167(a): For each batch of drug product purporting to be sterile and/or pyrogen-free, there shall be appropriate laboratory testing to determine conformance to such requirements. The test procedures shall be in writing and shall be followed.
21 CFR 211.165(e): The accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, and reproducibility of test methods employed by the firm shall be established and documented.
21 CFR 211.160(b): Laboratory controls shall include the establishment of scientifically sound and appropriate specifications, standards, sampling plans, and test procedures designed to assure that components, drug product containers, closures, in-process materials, labeling, and drug products conform to appropriate standards of identity, strength, quality, and purity.

Certain aspects of sterility testing are of particular importance, including control of the testing environment, understanding the test limitations, and investigating manufacturing systems following a positive test.

The testing laboratory environment should employ facilities and controls comparable to those used for aseptic filling operations. Poor or deficient sterility test facilities or controls can result in test failure. If production facilities and controls are significantly better than those for sterility testing, the danger exists of mistakenly attributing a positive sterility test result to a faulty laboratory even when the product tested could have, in fact, been nonsterile. Therefore, a manufacturing deficiency may go undetected.

The use of isolators for sterility testing minimizes the chance of a false positive test result.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：無菌性檢驗的三大關鍵面向

FDA 指出，無菌性檢驗有三個特別重要的面向，缺一不可：

  • 測試環境的管控——實驗室本身要有達到無菌操作等級的環境控制
  • 理解測試的侷限性——統計上，這個測試並不是高靈敏度的篩選工具
  • 陽性結果後的系統調查——不能只看實驗室，必須追溯製程

這三點對 CDMO 的 QC 實驗室和 QA 部門都有直接影響，在客戶稽核時也常被問及。

警示：實驗室品質不能低於生產環境

FDA 的邏輯非常清楚：如果生產區的環控比實驗室更嚴格，當陽性結果出現時， 廠商可能錯誤地把責任推給實驗室，卻對真正不無菌的產品放行， 造成製程缺失被掩蓋。

實務建議：QC 微生物實驗室應維持至少 ISO 5（A/B 級）的操作條件， 並定期進行環境監測，且數據須可追溯。

CDMO COO 應用：隔離器 (Isolator) 投資的業務邏輯

FDA 明確推薦使用隔離器進行無菌性檢驗以降低假陽性機率。 對 CDMO 而言，採用隔離器不只是技術升級，更是商業風險管理： 減少因假陽性導致的批次報廢、重測成本與交期延誤， 同時在客戶稽核時展現先進的實驗室管控能力，提升競爭差異化。

Section XI-A — Microbiological Laboratory Controls 微生物實驗室管控

原文內容 Original Text

A Sterility testing methods are required to be accurate and reproducible, in accordance with 211.194 and 211.165. USP <71> "Sterility Tests" is the principal source used for sterility testing methods, including information on test procedures and media.

As a part of methods validation, appropriate microbiological challenge testing will demonstrate reproducibility of the method to reliably recover representative microorganisms. If growth is inhibited, modifications (e.g., increased dilution, additional membrane filter washes, addition of inactivating agents) to the test method should be implemented to optimize recovery.

Ultimately, methods validation studies should demonstrate that the method does not provide an opportunity for false negatives.

It is essential that the media used to perform sterility testing be rendered sterile and demonstrated as growth promoting. Personnel performing sterility testing should be qualified and trained for the task. A written program should be in place to maintain updated training of personnel and confirm acceptable sterility testing practices.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：方法驗證的三個關鍵證明

無菌性檢驗的方法驗證必須能夠回答三個問題：

  • 促生長性 (Growth Promotion)：培養基本身能支持目標微生物生長嗎？（培養基每批次都需確認）
  • 抑菌效果排除 (Bacteriostasis/Fungistasis Test)：產品本身是否抑制微生物生長？若有，修改方法（稀釋、沖洗、加中和劑）
  • 無假陰性 (No False Negatives)：方法不能因產品抑制或操作問題錯過真正的污染

類比：方法驗證就像「偵探訓練」

想像你要訓練一位偵探去抓壞人（微生物）。你必須先確認： 這位偵探的工具（培養基）能正常運作嗎？ 現場有沒有干擾偵探辦案的阻礙（產品的抑制效果）？ 訓練測試中，偵探是否每次都能找到藏起來的壞人（挑戰微生物）？ 如果偵探在訓練中也找不到，就不能派他去執行真正的任務。

CDMO 實驗室合規要點

常見稽核缺失：培養基促生長記錄不完整、人員訓練記錄缺少驗證環節、 或方法驗證報告未涵蓋抑菌性測試。 對於含抗菌成分的產品（如含防腐劑的多劑量製劑）， 抑菌性測試尤其重要，是客戶技術轉移清單中必須確認的項目。

Section XI-B — Sampling and Incubation 取樣與培育

原文內容 Original Text

B Sterility tests are limited in their ability to detect contamination because of the small sample size typically used. For example, as described by USP, statistical evaluations indicate that the sterility test sampling plan "only enables the detection of contamination in a lot in which 10% of the units are contaminated about nine times out of ten in making the test."

Statistical Illustration: If a 10,000-unit lot with a 0.1% contamination level was sterility tested using 20 units, there is a 98% chance that the batch would pass the test. This means a lot with 10 contaminated units out of 10,000 would almost certainly be released.

It is important that the samples represent the entire batch and processing conditions. Samples should be taken:

  • at the beginning, middle, and end of the aseptic processing operation
  • in conjunction with processing interventions or excursions

Because of the limited sensitivity of the test, any positive result is considered a serious CGMP issue that should be thoroughly investigated.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

重要警示：無菌性檢驗不是高靈敏度的測試

這個統計數字令人震驚，但卻是業界必須正視的事實： 一批有 10 瓶污染的 10,000 瓶批次， 用 20 個樣品進行無菌性檢驗，有 98% 的機率會通過。

這正是為什麼 FDA 強調：無菌保證不能只靠最終的無菌性檢驗， 而必須建立在整個製程的系統管控上——亦即 「Sterility Assurance by Process」 而非 「Sterility Assurance by Testing」。

代表性取樣的策略意義

FDA 要求樣品必須代表整批製程條件，特別是：

  • 批次頭、中、尾：涵蓋不同時間點的環境暴露與設備狀態
  • 介入操作後：人員介入是最高污染風險時段，應特別標記追蹤

對 CDMO 而言，取樣計畫必須在批次製造規程 (BPR) 中明確記錄， 包含取樣時機的 rationale，以應對客戶及 FDA 的文件審查。

CDMO COO 啟示：陽性即嚴肅事件

任何無菌陽性結果都是嚴重的 CGMP 問題，不能以「可能是實驗室污染」 為理由輕易帶過。即使最後判定為實驗室錯誤， 整個調查過程與決策邏輯都必須有文件支持。 對 CDMO 而言，此類事件若處理不當，可能影響客戶對整個委託生產平台的信心。

C Care should be taken in the performance of the sterility test to preclude any activity that allows for possible sample contamination. When microbial growth is observed, the lot should be considered nonsterile and an investigation conducted. 導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

黃金原則：有生長就是陽性，不能「自動無效化」

FDA 的立場非常強硬：只要測試中出現微生物生長， 該批次即被視為不無菌，調查必須立即啟動。 唯一可以使初次測試無效的情況，是有明確且有文件支持的 實驗室錯誤證據。

常見錯誤做法（稽核紅旗）：調查結論寫「可能是實驗室污染」但缺乏具體證據， 然後直接進行重測。FDA 視此為程序不符合，可能導致 Warning Letter。

七大調查要素的邏輯架構

這七個要素不是隨機列舉，而是系統性地涵蓋了污染可能的所有來源：

  • 要素 1（菌種鑑定）：確認是哪種菌，才能比對環境資料庫找出來源
  • 要素 2（實驗室偏差）：排除或確認實驗室為污染源
  • 要素 3（生產環境監測）：比對生產日的環境數據趨勢
  • 要素 4（人員監測）：操作人員的微生物監測資料
  • 要素 5（滅菌前生物負荷）：原料或中間體的微生物起始量
  • 要素 6（批次記錄）：製程中是否有異常事件或偏差
  • 要素 7（製造歷程）：歷史數據、過去偏差、製程變更記錄
調查七大要素詳解 Investigation Elements — Detail

要素 1 & 2 原文 Elements 1 & 2

1. Identification (speciation) of the organism

Sterility test isolates should be identified to the species level. Microbiological monitoring data should be reviewed to determine if the organism is also found in laboratory and production environments, personnel, or product bioburden. Advanced identification methods (e.g., nucleic-acid based) are valuable for investigational purposes. When comparing results from environmental monitoring and sterility positives, both identifications should be performed using the same methodology.

2. Record of laboratory tests and deviations

Review of laboratory deviation and investigation findings can help to eliminate or implicate the laboratory as the source of contamination. For example, if the organism is seldom found in the laboratory environment, product contamination is more likely than laboratory error. If the organism is found in laboratory and production environments, it can still indicate product contamination.

The proper handling of deviations is an essential aspect of laboratory control. When a deviation occurs during sterility testing, it should be documented, investigated, and remedied. If any deviation is considered to have compromised the integrity of the sterility test, the test should be invalidated immediately without incubation.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

要素 1：菌種鑑定的技術深度

僅報告「革蘭氏陽性球菌」是不夠的，FDA 要求鑑定至種 (Species) 層級。 原因在於：唯有到種層級，才能與環境監測資料庫進行比對， 確認同一菌種是否存在於生產環境、實驗室或特定人員身上。

現代分子生物學方法（如 16S rRNA 定序）比傳統生化鑑定更精確， FDA 特別提到核酸定序在調查中的價值。 重要：環監資料與無菌陽性的鑑定方法必須一致，否則比對無效。

要素 2：偏差記錄的「立即無效化」規則

若測試過程中的偏差已危害測試的完整性（例如：隔離器洩漏、 培養基受污染），必須立即無效化，不可繼續培育。 這代表無效化的時間點是「發現偏差當下」，而非事後討論。

稽核紅旗：若廠商在培育結束後才討論是否無效化， FDA 會質疑該決定是受測試結果影響（如果無生長就不會提無效化）， 而非基於真正的偏差理由——此模式本身即為 CGMP 違規。

CDMO COO 應用：環境監測資料庫的戰略價值

維護一個完整的、可查詢菌種歷史記錄的微生物資料庫， 是無菌陽性調查的核心競爭力。 CDMO 在服務多個客戶時，若能快速從資料庫中找出特定菌種的歷史出現點， 調查效率與說服力都會大幅提升，也更能保護客戶批次免於不必要的拒絕。

要素 3 & 4 原文 Elements 3 & 4

3. Monitoring of production area environment

Trend analysis of microorganisms in the critical and immediately adjacent areas is especially helpful in determining the source of contamination in a sterility failure investigation. Consideration of environmental microbial data should not be limited to results of monitoring the production environment for the lot, day, or shift associated with the suspect lot. For example, results showing little or no recovery of microorganisms can be misleading, especially when preceded or followed by a finding of an adverse trend or atypically high microbial counts. It is therefore important to look at both short- and long-term environmental trend analyses.

4. Monitoring Personnel

The review of data and associated trends from daily monitoring of personnel can provide important information indicating a route of contamination. The adequacy of personnel practices and training also merit significant review and consideration.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

要素 3：趨勢分析的時間維度

FDA 特別警告：僅看「批次生產當天」的環境監測結果是不夠的， 甚至可能誤導調查方向。正確做法是同時檢視：

  • 短期趨勢：生產前後數天的環監數據
  • 長期趨勢：該填充線過去數月的環監走向，是否有上升趨勢

「當天數據正常」並不代表製程無問題——若前後幾天有超標記錄， 這個「正常」數據反而可能是異常中的偶然。

要素 4：人員是最高風險污染源

無菌製程中，人員是最主要的污染來源（人體每分鐘會散發數千個微粒）。 FDA 要求調查時必須審查：人員的每日手套、更衣監測數據， 以及人員訓練記錄是否符合要求。

特別注意：若調查批次的填充日有臨時或新人員參與操作， 這一點必須在調查報告中明確評估。

要素 5–7 原文 Elements 5–7

5. Product Presterilization Bioburden

We recommend review of trends in product bioburden and consideration of whether adverse bioburden trends have occurred.

6. Production record review

Complete batch and production control records should be reviewed to detect any signs of failures or anomalies that could have a bearing on product sterility. For example, the investigation should include elements such as:

  • Events that could have impacted on the critical zone
  • Batch and trending data that indicate whether utility and/or support systems are functioning properly — e.g., records of air quality monitoring for filling lines could show a time at which there was improper air balance or an unusually high particle count
  • Whether construction or maintenance activities could have had an adverse impact

7. Manufacturing history

The manufacturing history of a product or similar products should be reviewed as part of the investigation. Past deviations, problems, or changes (e.g., process, components, equipment) are among the factors that can provide an indication of the origin of the problem.

Additional FDA Guidance on Lab Trends

A sterility positive result can be viewed as indicative of production or laboratory problems, and the entire manufacturing process should be comprehensively investigated since such problems often can extend beyond a single batch. Where a laboratory has a good track record with respect to errors, this history can lower suspicion of the lab. However, where a laboratory has a poor track record, firms should not assume that the contamination is automatically more attributable to the laboratory and consequently overlook a genuine production problem. Accordingly, it is essential that all sterility positives be thoroughly investigated.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

要素 5：滅菌前生物負荷的趨勢意義

無菌製程是「無法透過最終滅菌挽救」的製程， 因此滅菌前的微生物負荷控制至關重要。 若調查發現生物負荷有上升趨勢，代表原料或製程中間體的管控可能已出現問題， 需追溯原物料供應商、水系統、或製程設備的清潔狀態。

要素 6：批次記錄中的「隱藏線索」

FDA 點名三類特別重要的記錄：

  • 關鍵區域事件：靠近暴露產品的任何活動（包含機器調整、容器掉落等）
  • 支援系統記錄：HVAC 氣流、微粒計數、WFI TOC 等——這些「周邊」數據往往是污染的早期預警信號
  • 施工與維修：即使在鄰近區域進行的工程也可能影響無菌區的微粒或氣流

要素 7 類比：製造歷程是「犯罪現場時間軸」

警察調查謀殺案時不只看案發當天——他們會建立完整的時間軸， 找出所有可能相關的事件和關係人。 製造歷程審查的邏輯完全一樣：近期設備變更、製程偏差、 或原物料供應商改變，都可能是問題的根源。 FDA 特別強調「相似產品」也要納入調查範圍——因為同一填充線、 同一操作人員、同一環境可能影響多個品項。

CDMO COO 總結：調查報告的商業意涵

一份高品質的無菌陽性調查報告，不只是法規合規的要求， 更是 CDMO 對客戶的信任合約。 當客戶的產品在你的廠房出現無菌陽性，他們首先要看的是： 調查是否完整、客觀、有科學支撐，且 CAPA 是否實際有效。 調查報告的品質直接反映 CDMO 的品質文化與技術能力。

Section XII — Batch Record Review: Process Control Documentation 批次記錄審查：製程管控文件

原文內容 Original Text

21 CFR 211.100(b): Written production and process control procedures shall be followed in the execution of the various production and process control functions and shall be documented at the time of performance. Any deviation from the written procedures shall be recorded and justified.
21 CFR 211.192: All drug product production and control records, including those for packaging and labeling, shall be reviewed and approved by the quality control unit to determine compliance with all established, approved written procedures before a batch is released or distributed. Any unexplained discrepancy or the failure of a batch or any of its components to meet any of its specifications shall be thoroughly investigated, whether or not the batch has already been distributed. The investigation shall extend to other batches of the same drug product and other drug products that may have been associated with the specific failure or discrepancy.

Manufacturers should build process and environmental control activities into their aseptic processing operation. It is critical that these activities be maintained and strictly implemented on a daily basis.

The requirement for review of all batch records and data for conformance with written procedures, operating parameters, and product specifications prior to arriving at the final release decision calls for an overall review of process and system performance for that given cycle of manufacture. 核心概念：批次放行 = 整個製造生態系統的評估

FDA 對批次放行的定義不只是「產品符合規格」， 而是對整個製造循環（Cycle of Manufacture）的全面評估。 放行審查必須涵蓋：

  • 環境監測數據：生產期間環控是否維持在可接受範圍
  • 人員監測數據：操作人員的更衣及動態微粒監測
  • 支援系統記錄：HVAC、WFI、蒸汽產生器的運行數據
  • 設備功能確認：批次警報記錄、過濾器完整性測試結果
  • 實驗室管控結果：所有中間體及最終產品檢驗數據

「每日嚴格執行」的合規文化意涵

FDA 使用「daily basis（每日）」這個字眼具有深意。 製程管控不是稽核前才執行的活動，而是每一個生產班次的基本要求。 這對 CDMO 的班組長 (Shift Supervisor) 和 QA 現場代表 (QA Floor Rep) 有直接的行為要求：每天完成、每天確認、每天記錄。

CDMO COO 應用：批次記錄的數位化策略

傳統紙本批次記錄難以支持快速的多品項切換。 導入電子批次記錄系統 (eBRS/EBR) 可以： 自動整合環監、支援系統、實驗室數據， 減少人工匯整錯誤，並讓 QA 放行審查更高效。 這也是客戶在技術轉移評估中越來越常要求的 CDMO 能力指標。

Section XII — Interventions & Line Stoppages 介入操作與停線記錄

原文內容 Original Text

While interventions and/or stoppages are normally recorded in the batch record, the manner of documenting these occurrences varies. In particular, line stoppages and any unplanned interventions should be sufficiently documented in batch records with the associated time and duration of the event.

In addition to lengthened dwell time of sterile product elements in the critical area, an extensive intervention can increase contamination risk. Sterility failures have often been attributed to atypical or extensive interventions that have occurred as a response to an undesirable event during the aseptic process.

Written procedures describing the need for line clearances in the event of certain interventions, such as machine adjustments and any repairs, should be established. Such interventions should be documented with more detail than minor events. Interventions that result in substantial activity near exposed product or container closures or that last beyond a reasonable exposure time should, where appropriate, result in a local or full line clearance.

Any disruption in power supply, however momentary, that could affect product quality is a manufacturing deviation and must be included in batch records (211.100, 211.192).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

高風險事件：非計畫性介入是最常見的無菌失敗根因

FDA 明確指出：許多無菌失敗案例的根本原因， 是非計畫性、長時間的介入操作（例如機器故障時的人工調整）。 這類操作往往在緊急狀況下倉促執行，無菌技術更容易出現漏洞。

批次記錄的記載要求：

  • 介入類型（計畫性 vs. 非計畫性）
  • 發生時間與持續時間
  • 操作人員身份
  • 是否觸發停線清場 (Line Clearance)

停線清場 (Line Clearance) 的觸發標準

FDA 要求廠商建立書面程序，明確定義哪些介入事件需要觸發局部或全線清場。 觸發情境應包含（但不限於）：

  • 暴露產品附近的大型機械維修
  • 超過合理暴露時間的連續介入
  • 任何危及關鍵區域無菌性的事件

對 CDMO 而言，這項程序的設計品質，在技術轉移審查時會被客戶直接評估。

電力中斷：零容忍的批次記錄要求

任何可能影響產品品質的電力中斷——即使只是瞬間—— 都必須記載於批次記錄並作為製造偏差處理。 影響範圍可能包含：HVAC 氣流中斷、填充泵停止、 層流罩風扇停轉、環境監測設備暫停等。 這些都需要在偏差評估中逐一確認影響程度。

CDMO COO 實務建議：介入分級管理制度

建議建立介入操作分級制度（例如 Level 1 / 2 / 3）， 依嚴重程度規定不同的記錄詳細度、QA 通報要求及清場判斷標準。 此制度能幫助操作人員在現場快速判斷， 同時確保批次記錄的完整性能通過 FDA 及客戶稽核的檢視， 也是多品項 CDMO 環境下實現標準化管控的有效工具。

Appendix 1 — Aseptic Processing Isolators: Overview 無菌製程隔離系統：概述

原文內容 Original Text

Aseptic processing using isolation systems separates the external cleanroom environment from the aseptic processing line and minimizes its exposure to personnel. A well-designed positive pressure isolator, supported by adequate procedures for its maintenance, monitoring, and control, offers tangible advantages over traditional aseptic processing, including fewer opportunities for microbial contamination during processing.

However, users should remain vigilant to potential sources of operational risk. Manufacturers should also be aware of the need to establish new procedures addressing issues unique to isolators.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：隔離系統的本質優勢

隔離系統 (Isolator) 是無菌製程技術的重大進化。其核心邏輯是： 將製程區域與外部潔淨室（和人員）實體分離，從根源上消除最大的污染來源——人員。

  • 傳統無菌製程：人員在 ISO 5 環境中穿著無菌服操作，人員是最大污染源
  • 隔離系統：人員在隔離器外操作，內部維持持續的 ISO 5 環境
  • 正壓設計：隔離器內部維持正壓，防止外部空氣滲入

FDA 明確肯定此優勢，但同時提醒：新技術帶來新風險點，需要建立專屬的 SOP。

注意：隔離器不是萬靈丹

FDA 特別強調使用者需對「潛在操作風險保持警覺」—— 隔離器本身的設計缺陷、手套破損、除污不完全等問題， 都可能造成比傳統製程更難察覺的污染事件。 引入隔離系統後，必須建立一套新的 SOP 體系。

Appendix 1-A — Maintenance: General & Glove Integrity 維護：一般維護與手套完整性

原文內容 Original Text

A. Maintenance

1. General

Maintenance of isolator systems differs in some significant respects from the traditional, non-isolated aseptic processing operations. Although no isolator forms an absolute seal, very high integrity can be achieved in a well-designed unit. However, a leak in certain components of the system can constitute a significant breach of integrity.

The integrity of gloves, half-suits, and seams should receive daily attention and be addressed by a comprehensive preventative maintenance program. Replacement frequencies should be established in written procedures that ensure parts will be changed before they breakdown or degrade. Transfer systems, gaskets, and seals are among the other parts that should be covered by the maintenance program.

2. Glove Integrity

A faulty glove or sleeve (gauntlet) assembly represents a route of contamination and a critical breach of isolator integrity. A preventative maintenance program should be established. The choice of durable glove materials, coupled with a well-justified replacement frequency, are key aspects of GMP to be addressed.

With every use, gloves should be visually evaluated for any macroscopic physical defect. Physical integrity tests should also be performed routinely. A breach in glove integrity can be of serious consequence.

Due to the potential for microbial migration through microscopic holes in gloves and the lack of a highly sensitive glove integrity test, FDA recommends affording attention to the sanitary quality of the inner surface of the installed glove and to integrating the use of a second pair of thin gloves.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：維護管理的關鍵差異

隔離系統的維護與傳統無菌製程有本質差異。關鍵的維護對象包含：

  • 手套/半身防護服 (Gloves/Half-suits)：每日目視檢查 + 定期物理完整性測試
  • 接縫 (Seams)：可能因頻繁使用或化學除污而劣化
  • 傳輸系統、墊圈、密封件：均需列入預防性維護計畫

書面程序必須規定「在零件降解或損壞之前」完成更換——這意味著更換頻率要基於 材料耐用性研究和實際使用數據，而不是等到發現問題才處理。

注意：手套完整性是頭號風險點

FDA 對手套完整性給予了格外高的重視，原因在於：

  • 微觀孔洞（肉眼不可見）可能允許微生物遷移
  • 目前尚無高靈敏度的手套完整性測試方法
  • 一旦破損，污染後果嚴重

FDA 的具體建議：在安裝手套的內表面保持衛生品質，並使用 雙層薄手套作為額外屏障。這是一個務實的風險緩解策略。

類比：外科手套的兩層防護

外科醫師在高風險手術中常規使用雙層手套。第一層如有微小破損， 第二層仍可提供防護，同時雙層之間的液體染色可作為完整性破損的視覺警示。 FDA 對隔離器手套的建議背後是同樣的邏輯——縱深防禦 (Defense in Depth)。

Appendix 1-B — Design: Airflow, Pressure & Clean Area Classification 設計：氣流、壓差與潔淨分級

原文內容 Original Text

B. Design

1. Airflow

There are two types of aseptic processing isolators: open and closed. Closed isolators employ connections with auxiliary equipment for material transfer. Open isolators have openings to the surrounding environment that are carefully engineered to segregate the inner isolator environment from the surrounding room via overpressure.

Turbulent flow can be acceptable within closed isolators, which are normally compact in size and do not house processing lines. Other aseptic processing isolators employ unidirectional airflow that sweeps over and away from exposed sterile materials, avoiding any turbulence or stagnant airflow in the area of exposed sterilized materials, product, and container closures.

2. Materials of Construction

Suitable materials should be chosen based on durability, as well as ease of cleaning and decontamination. Rigid wall construction incorporating stainless steel and glass materials is widely used.

3. Pressure Differential

A positive air pressure differential adequate to achieve physical separation should be employed and supported by qualification studies. Positive air pressure differentials from the isolator to the surrounding environment have largely ranged from approximately 17.5 to 50 Pascals (0.07" to 0.20" water gauge).

The positive pressure differential should be coupled with an appropriately designed opening to the external environment to prevent potential ingress of surrounding room air by induction. Local Class 100 (ISO 5) protection at an opening is an example of a design provision that can provide a further barrier.

4. Clean Area Classifications

The interior of the isolator should meet Class 100 (ISO 5) standards. The classification of the environment surrounding the isolator should be based on the design of its interfaces (e.g., transfer ports), as well as the number of transfers into and out of the isolator. A Class 100,000 (ISO 8) background is commonly used. An aseptic processing isolator should not be located in an unclassified room.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

注意：「感應氣流」(Induction) 的危害

Appendix 1-C — Transfer of Materials/Supplies 物料傳輸系統

原文內容 Original Text

C. Transfer of Materials/Supplies

The ability to maintain integrity of a decontaminated isolator can be affected by the design of transfer ports. Various adaptations, of differing capabilities, allow for the transfer of supplies into and out of the isolator.

Multiple material transfers are generally made during the processing of a batch. Frequently, transfers are performed via direct interface with manufacturing equipment. Properly maintained and operated rapid transfer ports (RTPs) are an effective transfer mechanism for aseptic transfer of materials into and out of isolators.

Some transfer ports might have significant limitations, including marginal decontaminating capability (e.g., ultraviolet) or a design that has the potential to compromise isolation by allowing ingress of air from the surrounding room. In the latter case, localized HEPA-filtered unidirectional airflow cover in the area of such a port should be implemented.

Isolators often include a mousehole or other exit port through which product is discharged, opening the isolator to the outside environment. Sufficient overpressure should be supplied and monitored on a continuous basis at this location to ensure that isolation is maintained.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：傳輸系統是完整性的薄弱環節

傳輸系統是隔離器維持完整性最困難的挑戰之一。物料需要進出， 但每次傳輸都是一次潛在的污染機會。FDA 認可的主要傳輸機制：

  • 快速傳輸埠 (RTP, Rapid Transfer Port)：設計良好、維護良好的 RTP 是推薦的標準方法
  • 直接介面：與製造設備（如乾熱隧道）直接對接
  • 滑鼠洞 (Mousehole)：產品出口，需持續監控正壓差

注意：UV 除污埠的侷限性

FDA 明確點出部分傳輸埠的除污能力有限，特別是使用紫外線（UV）的設計。 UV 對表面殺菌有效，但穿透力差、陰影效應明顯， 不能視為等同於汽化過氧化氫 (VHP) 的系統性除污。 若使用此類傳輸埠，應在其區域加裝局部 HEPA 單向流保護。

類比：機場安全閘道

隔離器的傳輸埠就像機場的安全閘道——人員和物品需要通過， 但每次通過都必須保持安全控制。RTP 的設計原理類似雙門氣鎖： 在同一時間只有一道門開啟，確保內部環境不被外部環境污染。 持續監控 Mousehole 的正壓差，就像在出口閘道持續維持安全氣簾。

Appendix 1-D — Decontamination 除污

原文內容 Original Text

D. Decontamination

1. Surface Exposure

Decontamination procedures should ensure full exposure of all isolator surfaces to the chemical agent. The capability of a decontaminant to penetrate obstructed or covered surfaces is limited. For example, to facilitate contact with the decontaminant, the glove apparatus should be fully extended with glove fingers separated during the decontamination cycle. It is also important to clean the interior of the isolator per appropriate procedures to allow for a robust decontamination process.

2. Efficacy

The decontamination method should render the inner surfaces of the isolator free of viable microorganisms. An appropriate, quantified Biological Indicator (BI) challenge should be placed on various materials and in many locations throughout the isolator, including difficult to reach areas.

Cycles should be developed with an appropriate margin of extra kill to provide confidence in robustness. Normally, a four- to six-log reduction can be justified depending on the application. The uniform distribution of a defined concentration of decontaminating agent should also be evaluated.

3. Frequency

When an isolator is used for multiple days between decontamination cycles, the frequency adopted should be justified. A breach of isolator integrity should normally lead to a decontamination cycle. Breaches of integrity should be investigated. If it is determined that the environment may have been compromised, any product potentially impacted by the breach should be rejected.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：三大除污驗證要素

  • 表面暴露 (Surface Exposure)：物理上確保所有表面都能接觸到除污劑，包括手套內部、難以到達的角落
  • 效力 (Efficacy)：用生物指示劑 (BI) 驗證對數殺滅效果（4–6 log reduction）
  • 頻率 (Frequency)：必須有書面依據；完整性破損後必須重新除污

注意：統計方法（Fraction Negative）的限制

FDA 指出：因除污劑（如 VHP）的特性，一般用於蒸汽滅菌的統計方法 （fraction negative 法）通常不適用於確認除污製程的致死能力。 應使用具有明確孢子計數的 BI 進行定量挑戰測試。

CDMO 應用：完整性破損的處置程序

完整性破損的觸發條件包括：停電、閥門故障、正壓不足、手套/接縫破孔。 程序應規定：(1) 即時隔離可能受影響的產品；(2) 啟動調查； (3) 執行除污循環；(4) 根據調查結果決定產品的後續處置（拒批）。 此程序需在 CAPA 系統中追蹤。

Appendix 1-E/F/G — Filling Line Sterilization, Environmental Monitoring & Personnel 充填線滅菌、環境監控與人員

原文內容 Original Text

E. Filling Line Sterilization

To ensure sterility of product contact surfaces from the start of each operation, the entire path of the sterile processing stream should be sterilized. Aseptic processing equipment or ancillary supplies to be used within the isolator should be chosen based on their ability to withstand steam sterilization (or equivalent method).

Where decontamination methods are used to render certain product contact surfaces free of viable organisms, a minimum of a six-log reduction should be demonstrated using a suitable biological indicator.

F. Environmental Monitoring

An environmental monitoring program should be established that routinely ensures acceptable microbiological quality of air, surfaces, and gloves (or half-suits) as well as particle levels, within the isolator. Nutrient media should be cleaned off of surfaces following a contact plate sample.

Air quality should be monitored periodically during each shift. FDA recommends monitoring the exit port for particles to detect any unusual results. Media used for environmental monitoring should not be exposed to decontamination cycle residues, as recovery of microorganisms would be inhibited.

G. Personnel

Although cleanroom apparel considerations are generally reduced in an isolator operation, the contamination risk contributed by manual factors can not be overlooked. Traditional aseptic processing vigilance remains critical, with an understanding that contaminated isolator gloves can lead to product nonsterility. Meticulous aseptic technique standards must be observed (21 CFR 211.113), including appropriate use of sterile tools for manipulations.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：充填線滅菌的兩條路徑

隔離器內產品接觸表面的滅菌可透過兩種途徑：

  • 蒸汽滅菌 (SIP)：首選方法，需達到至少 6-log 殺滅效果；設備材料必須能耐受高溫高壓
  • 化學除污：當設備無法承受 SIP 時使用；同樣需要 6-log BI 驗證；不能只靠除污循環取代滅菌

關鍵原則：從每次操作開始，整個無菌製程路徑必須已滅菌完畢。

注意：環境監控的特殊考量

隔離系統的 EM 有幾個容易忽略的細節：

  • 接觸碟採樣後，培養基必須清除——殘留培養基會促進微生物生長
  • 環境監控培養基不可暴露於除污循環殘留物（如 VHP 殘留），否則微生物回收率會被抑制，造成假陰性
  • 建議監控出口埠 (exit port) 的顆粒水準，以早期發現異常

人員風險：「鋼化玻璃外殼」的盲點

許多人誤以為隔離系統使人員完全脫離製程。實際上，手套操作仍是頻繁的介入行為。 FDA 特別提醒：隔離系統內的無菌操作技術標準 與傳統無菌室完全相同—— 「換了環境，不代表可以降低無菌操作的警覺性」。 污染的隔離手套同樣會導致產品不合格。

Appendix 2 — Blow-Fill-Seal Technology: Equipment Design & Air Quality 吹灌封技術：設備設計與空氣品質

原文內容 Original Text

Blow-fill-seal (BFS) technology is an automated process by which containers are formed, filled, and sealed in a continuous operation. This manufacturing technology includes economies in container closure processing and reduced human intervention and is often used for filling and packaging ophthalmics, respiratory care products, and, less frequently, injectables.

A. Equipment Design and Air Quality

Most BFS machines operate using the following steps:

  • Heat a plastic polymer resin
  • Extrude it to form a parison (a tubular form of the hot resin)
  • Cut the parison with a high-temperature knife
  • Move the parison under the blow-fill needle (mandrel)
  • Inflate it to the shape of the mold walls
  • Fill the formed container with the liquid product
  • Remove the mandrel
  • Seal

The three steps with the greatest potential for exposure to particle contamination and/or surrounding air are those in which (1) the parison is cut, (2) the parison is moved under the blow-fill mandrel, and (3) the mandrel is removed (just prior to sealing).

The classified environment surrounding BFS machinery should generally meet Class 100,000 (ISO 8), or better, standards. HEPA-filtered or sterile air provided by membrane filters should be used during the steps when sterile products or materials are exposed. Air in the critical area should meet Class 100 (ISO 5) microbiological standards during operations.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：BFS 的競爭優勢與適用範圍

BFS 技術的三大優勢：

  • 連續整合操作：容器成型、充填、密封在同一設備完成，減少物料暴露
  • 降低人員介入：高度自動化，操作員接觸無菌環境的機會大幅減少
  • 成本效益：容器成型與充填整合，省去獨立容器採購、清洗、滅菌等步驟

典型應用：眼科用藥（eye drops）、呼吸道吸入液、部分注射劑。

注意：三個最高風險步驟

FDA 精確識別了 BFS 製程中三個最容易發生顆粒污染的關鍵時刻：

  1. Parison 切割（高溫刀切割塑料管，產生顆粒）
  2. Parison 移動至充填針頭下方（暴露於環境）
  3. 心軸 (Mandrel) 移除（充填後、密封前的短暫暴露）

這三個步驟是工程控制和 EM 設計的重點。

Appendix 2-B/C — BFS Validation/Qualification & Batch Monitoring 吹灌封驗證/確效與批次監控

原文內容 Original Text

B. Validation/Qualification

Advantages of BFS processing include rapid container closure processing and minimized aseptic interventions. However, only a properly functioning process can realize these advantages. FDA recommends affording special attention to setup, troubleshooting of equipment, and related aseptic personnel procedures.

Equipment sterilization, media fills, polymer extrusion/sterilization, product-plastic compatibility, forming and sealing integrity, and unit weight variation are among the key issues to address in validation and qualification studies.

Data gathered during such studies should ensure that BFS containers are sterile and, if used for parenteral drugs, nonpyrogenic. This can generally be achieved by validating that time temperature conditions of the extrusion process are effective against endotoxin or spore challenges in the polymeric material.

The choice of appropriate polymer material includes assessing if a material is pharmaceutical grade, safe, pure, and passes appropriate criteria for plastics. Polymer suppliers should be qualified and monitored for raw material quality.

C. Batch Monitoring and Control

Various in-process control parameters (e.g., container weight variation, fill weight, leakers, air pressure) provide information to monitor and facilitate ongoing process control. It is essential to monitor the microbial air quality. Samples should be taken according to a comprehensive sampling plan that provides data representative of the entire filling operation.

Container closure defects can be a major problem. It is critical that the operation be designed and set-up to uniformly manufacture integral units. Significant defects due to heat or mechanical problems should be investigated in accordance with §§ 211.100 and 211.192.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：BFS 驗證的特殊重點

BFS 的驗證除了標準的設備確效外，還有幾個獨特要素：

  • 擠出製程的熱力學驗證：塑料在高溫擠出過程中，時間-溫度條件需驗證能有效去除內毒素和孢子
  • 聚合物材料相容性：藥品-塑料相容性研究（藥物不得被塑化劑污染或被容器吸附）
  • 密封完整性：容器/密封介面的一致性是批次放行的關鍵
  • 重量變異：充填重量的一致性反映製程穩定性

注意：Parenteral 用途的去熱原要求

如果 BFS 用於生產注射劑（Parenteral）， 容器必須是無熱原的——這意味著擠出製程必須驗證其去除內毒素的能力。 FDA 指出：驗證塑料擠出的時間-溫度條件對內毒素和孢子的殺滅效果， 是實現這一要求的有效路徑。

Appendix 3-A — Aseptic Processing from Early Manufacturing Steps 前處理：早期製造步驟的無菌操作

原文內容 Original Text

The purpose of this appendix is to supplement the guidance with information on products regulated by CBER or CDER that are subject to aseptic processing at points early in the manufacturing process, or that require aseptic processing through the entire manufacturing process because it is impossible to sterile filter the final drug product.

A. Aseptic processing from early manufacturing steps

Some products undergo aseptic processing at some or all manufacturing steps preceding the final product closing step. In other instances, the final drug product cannot be sterile-filtered and, therefore, each component in the formulation would be rendered sterile and mixed aseptically. For example, products containing aluminum adjuvant are formulated aseptically because once they are alum adsorbed, they cannot be sterile-filtered.

Procedures (e.g., aseptic connection) that expose a product or product contact surfaces should be performed under unidirectional airflow in a Class 100 (ISO 5) environment. The environment of the room surrounding the Class 100 (ISO 5) environment should be Class 10,000 (ISO 7) or better.

Process simulation studies covering the steps preceding filling and sealing should be designed to incorporate all conditions, product manipulations, and interventions that could impact on the sterility of the product. The studies should include worst-case conditions such as maximum duration of open operations and maximum number of participating operators.

Process simulation studies for the formulation stage should be performed at least twice per year.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：為何前處理需要無菌操作？

標準的無菌製程邏輯是：製程最後用 0.22 µm 過濾器滅菌過濾，然後充填。 但有些產品根本無法過濾——這時必須從更早的步驟開始全程維持無菌：

  • 鋁佐劑疫苗：吸附到鋁鹽後，顆粒太大無法通過滅菌過濾膜
  • 大分子蛋白質：某些生物製劑在過濾後可能失活或損失
  • 細胞治療產品：活細胞本身就是無法過濾的「產品」

注意：製程模擬的範圍要求

前處理階段的製程模擬（媒體充填 / Media Fill）有特殊要求：

  • 必須涵蓋充填前的所有無菌操作步驟，不只是充填步驟本身
  • 必須包含最壞情況：最長開放時間、最多操作人員數
  • 必須模擬散裝桶/容器的儲存和運輸
  • 配方階段的製程模擬至少每年兩次（頻率高於標準充填線要求）
Appendix 3-B — Cellular Therapy Products and Cell-Derived Products 細胞治療與細胞衍生產品

原文內容 Original Text

B. Aseptic processing of cellular therapy products and cell-derived products

Cellular therapy and some cell-derived products (e.g., lysates, semi-purified extracts) represent a subset of the products that cannot be filter-sterilized and therefore undergo aseptic manipulations throughout the manufacturing process. Where possible, closed systems should be used during manufacturing.

Cellular therapy products often have short processing times at each manufacturing stage, particularly between the harvest, formulation of the final product, and product release. These products are frequently released from the manufacturing facility and administered to patients before final product sterility testing results are available.

In situations where results of final sterility testing are not available before the product is administered, additional controls and testing should be considered. For example, additional sterility tests can be performed at intermediate stages of manufacture, such as after the last manipulation of the product prior to harvest.

Other tests that may indicate microbial contamination, such as microscopic examination, Gram stain (or other bacterial and fungal stain), and endotoxin testing should be performed and meet acceptance criteria prior to product release.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念：細胞治療的獨特挑戰

細胞治療產品面臨傳統滅菌製程所沒有的根本性挑戰：

  • 無法過濾滅菌：活細胞是產品本身，不能被過濾掉
  • 極短保存期限：從採集到施用可能只有數小時至數天
  • 無菌測試滯後：傳統 14 天無菌測試在產品施用前根本無法完成
  • 個人化生產：許多細胞治療是「自體」的，一個患者一批，規模化 QC 策略不適用

注意：「先施用、後出結果」的替代控制策略

當最終無菌測試結果在施用前無法取得時，FDA 期望有額外的微生物學控制措施：

  • 中間階段無菌測試：在最後一次操作後（如採集前）進行
  • 顯微鏡檢查：快速的形態學檢查
  • 革蘭氏染色：快速細菌/真菌鑑別
  • 內毒素測試：快速 LAL 測試（可在數小時內完成）

所有替代測試必須在放行前達到預設的接受標準。

細胞治療 CDMO 的品質系統必須設計「快速放行路徑 (Expedited Release Pathway)」： 整合過程中測試、快速 EM 數據、即時微生物監控， 以在極短的窗口期內完成品質決策。 此領域的法規要求仍在快速演進中，需密切追蹤 FDA CBER 的最新指引。

Alert Level vs Action Level：兩個不同的觸發邏輯

這兩個詞彙經常被混淆，但在環境監控 (EM) 程序中有截然不同的管理意涵：

D 值的直觀理解

D 值是微生物抵抗力的「計時指標」。若某 BI 的 D 值為 1.5 分鐘， 意味著每 1.5 分鐘殺死 90% 的孢子。 要達到 6-log 殺滅（無菌保證），需要 6 × 1.5 = 9 分鐘的暴露時間 （再加上安全係數）。D 值越高的 BI，代表挑戰越嚴格。

Decontamination vs Disinfection vs Sterilization：三個層次

  • Disinfection（消毒）：降低活性生物負荷，可能不殺孢子；適用於表面清潔
  • Decontamination（除污）：使用殺孢子劑消除活性生物負荷；適用於隔離器表面
  • Sterilization（滅菌）：消滅所有形式的微生物（包括孢子）達到 SAL ≤ 10⁻⁶；適用於產品接觸表面和最終容器

三者要求依次遞增，選用時需根據接觸風險等級決定。

隔離器定義中的兩大類型

FDA 在詞彙表中對隔離器給出了完整的分類定義：

  • 封閉式隔離系統 (Closed Isolator)：透過無菌連接輔助設備完成物料傳輸， 而非使用對周圍環境的開口。整個操作過程保持密封狀態。
  • 開放式隔離系統 (Open Isolator)：設計為允許物料在操作過程中 持續或半持續地進出，開口工程設計（如持續正壓）防止外部污染物進入。

Overkill vs Standard Sterilization：保守度比較

Overkill 方法是最保守的蒸汽滅菌設計策略： 不管初始生物負荷是多少，直接設計出能提供至少 12-log 殺滅效果的循環。 這相當於「就算裝滿了最頑強的微生物，也能殺光」的保障水準。

代價是較長的滅菌循環時間，可能對熱敏感材料造成額外壓力。 因此不是所有產品都適用——必須與產品的熱穩定性平衡。

Unidirectional Flow vs Laminar Flow：FDA 的用字選擇

FDA 在詞彙表中對這兩個術語給出了相似但不完全相同的定義： Laminar Flow（層流）強調流動的物理特性（單一方向、平行層、恆定速度）； 而 Unidirectional Flow（單向流）則強調其在無菌製程中的 功能性目的——「足以將顆粒從關鍵區域可重複性地掃除」。 FDA 在正文中更傾向使用「Unidirectional Airflow」，因其強調了效能要求， 而非僅描述流動形態。
