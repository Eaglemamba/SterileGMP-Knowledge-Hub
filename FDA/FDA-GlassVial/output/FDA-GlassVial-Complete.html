I. Introduction 導論

原文內容 Original Text

This guidance represents the current thinking of the Food and Drug Administration (FDA or Agency) on this topic. It does not establish any rights for any person and is not binding on FDA or the public. You can use an alternative approach if it satisfies the requirements of the applicable statutes and regulations. To discuss an alternative approach, contact the FDA office responsible for this guidance as listed on the title page.

FDA is issuing this guidance to collate recommendations for appropriate reporting categories and the content of postapproval change submissions across numerous FDA guidance documents. This guidance conveys recommendations to holders of approved new drug applications (NDAs), biologics license applications (BLAs), and abbreviated new drug applications (ANDAs) regarding the reporting and implementation of some common changes to container closure system (CCS) components consisting of glass vials and stoppers for approved sterile drug products, including biological products, administered parenterally. This guidance also discusses pathways available to application holders to obtain Agency feedback. Additionally, this guidance discusses risk-based tools available to facilitate the implementation of changes to CCSs consisting of glass vials and stoppers. This guidance does not apply to CCS types other than glass vials and stoppers.

"This guidance does not apply to CCS types other than glass vials and stoppers."

Footnote 1: This guidance has been prepared by the Office of Pharmaceutical Quality in the Center for Drug Evaluation and Research in cooperation with the Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research at the Food and Drug Administration.

Footnote 2: In March 2021, FDA published the guidance for industry COVID-19 Container Closure System and Component Changes: Glass Vials and Stoppers, which provided recommendations regarding the reporting and implementation of some common changes to container closure system (CCS) components consisting of glass vials and stoppers for approved sterile drugs. That guidance was intended to remain in effect only for the duration of the public health emergency related to COVID-19 declared by the Secretary of Health and Human Services under section 319 of the Public Health Service Act (section 319 public health emergency), which has now expired. FDA is issuing this final guidance because many of the recommendations set forth in the 2021 guidance are applicable outside the context of the section 319 public health emergency.

Footnote 3: Although this guidance does not principally address data recommendations for original applications pending approval, the risk-based regulatory approaches discussed in this guidance, other guidance documents referenced in References, and the data recommendations provided in the tables in Appendix may be useful during product development and for original application submission. Should an applicant need to change CCS components before product approval, the applicant should proactively seek feedback from the appropriate FDA review division to mitigate the effect of the change on the review timeline of the pending application.

In general, FDA's guidance documents do not establish legally enforceable responsibilities. Instead, guidances describe the Agency's current thinking on a topic and should be viewed only as recommendations, unless specific regulatory or statutory requirements are cited. The use of the word should in Agency guidances means that something is suggested or recommended, but not required.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Container Closure System (CCS, 容器密封系統)：CCS 是藥品與外界之間的最後一道屏障，由玻璃瓶 (glass vial)、瓶塞 (stopper)、鋁蓋 (aluminum seal) 等組成。CCS 的功能包括：(1) 維持無菌性 (sterility)、(2) 保護藥品免受光、氧氣及水分影響、(3) 與藥品配方不產生有害交互作用。

Reporting Categories (通報類別)：FDA 對核准後變更的通報分為三級：

  • PAS (Prior Approval Supplement)：需 FDA 核准後才能實施，用於高風險變更
  • CBE-30 (Changes Being Effected in 30 Days)：提交後 30 天即可實施
  • Annual Report (年度報告)：實施後於年度報告中通知，用於低風險變更

比喻說明 Analogy

把 CCS 想像成餐廳的餐具選擇 (restaurant dinnerware selection)。高級餐廳（注射劑藥廠）對餐具（CCS）的要求極為嚴格——不僅要乾淨，還要確保不會與食物產生化學反應、不會釋出有害物質。如果餐廳要換一套新餐具（變更 CCS 供應商），你不能只看外觀一樣就直接用，必須先測試新餐具在各種溫度、酸鹼度下是否安全。這就是為什麼 FDA 要求系統性的變更評估。

重點提示 Key Notes

COVID-19 的催化效應：這份 2024 年正式版 Guidance 起源於 2021 年 COVID-19 緊急期間的臨時指引。疫情期間全球玻璃瓶短缺（特別是 Type I borosilicate 玻璃），迫使藥廠緊急尋找替代供應商。FDA 因此發布臨時指引加速 CCS 變更審查。疫情結束後，FDA 認為這些建議具有長期適用性，因此正式化為這份 Guidance。

適用範圍限定：本 Guidance 僅適用於玻璃瓶和瓶塞。不包含 pre-filled syringes、cartridges、plastic containers 等其他 CCS 類型。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 情境：作為臨床期無菌充填代工廠 (CDMO)，你的客戶持有 NDA/BLA，而你是充填製造場地。當客戶的玻璃瓶供應商要求變更（如更換製造廠址），客戶必須評估此變更的通報類別並提交 supplement。你作為 CDMO 的角色是提供製程驗證數據（如 CCIT、media fill 數據）來支持客戶的申請。

Dual Sourcing 策略：疫情教訓使得許多公司開始建立 CCS 的雙供應商策略。本 Guidance 提供了清晰的法規路徑，讓藥廠可以預先規劃第二供應商的核准流程。

II. Regulatory Approaches to CMC Changes 法規變更途徑

原文內容 Original Text

As described in section 506A of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (21 U.S.C. 356a), 21 CFR 314.70, 21 CFR 314.97, and 21 CFR 601.12, an applicant must notify FDA about changes in each condition (e.g., the product, production process, quality controls, equipment, facilities, or labeling) established in an approved application beyond the variations already provided for in the application. The reporting category for such changes depends on the potential to have an adverse effect on the identity, strength, quality, purity, or potency of the product as these factors may relate to the safety or effectiveness of the product. Therefore, changes can range from those that require approval before implementation (prior approval supplement (PAS)), those that require notification 30 days before (changes being effected in 30 days (CBE-30)), or upon implementation (changes being effected), and those where notification can be provided after implementation (annual report). Some of these identified changes, depending on the circumstances, may be more appropriately submitted as an original application instead of as supplements.

FDA has conveyed additional clarification regarding reporting of CMC changes to an NDA, BLA, and ANDA in guidance documents specific to various application types and product dosage forms (see References). The content in section IV. Common Changes Related to Glass Vials and Stoppers related to changes to glass vials and stoppers for finished drug and biological products includes content derived from the referenced guidance documents.

FDA applies a risk-based approach to the evaluation of proposed CMC changes to a CCS (e.g., changes in components, composition, container type, suppliers, and manufacturers), taking into consideration the characteristics of the specific product. The suitability and compatibility of a CCS depends not only on the properties of a container, but also on the properties of other CCS components and their interactions with the drug product formulation over its intended shelf life. Applicants must validate the effects of the change prior to distribution of the drug, and, as appropriate, conduct additional qualification tests or submit information to address product-specific risks as part of that assessment. For example, this additional information to be submitted to FDA could include studies to assess: the effect of formulation attributes such as high pH; the effect of factors that increase the potential for glass delamination, particulate matter, leachables, or interactions of labile molecules with leachables (such as susceptibility of some proteins to interact with metal ions); or the effect of the lyophilization process on product quality. Finally, applicable tests and studies as outlined in United States Pharmacopeia (USP) chapters should be conducted to demonstrate the suitability of the glass vial following a change; such tests must be conducted where needed to ensure continued compliance with compendial standards for strength, quality, or purity of the drug.

FDA may consider available risk evaluations and product quality risk mitigation strategies to support a lower reporting category for specific changes. FDA recommends applicants use the risk management principles described in the ICH guidance for industry Q9(R1) Quality Risk Management (May 2023), and tools described in ICH guidance for industry Q12 Technical and Regulatory Considerations for Pharmaceutical Product Lifecycle Management (May 2021), where appropriate. Whether or not a change is required to be reported to FDA, in accordance with applicable regulations, FDA expects all changes to be appropriately managed by an establishment's/facility's pharmaceutical quality system under the applicable current good manufacturing practice regulations in 21 CFR parts 210, 211, and 600. Applicants should also refer to the recommendations in the ICH guidance for industry Q10 Pharmaceutical Quality System (April 2009).

A supplement for a CCS-related change should include information to support the proposed change and can incorporate certain information by reference to a drug master file (DMF), where appropriate. However, when incorporating information by reference to a DMF, the supplement should also include sufficient product-specific information to support the change.

Applicants are ultimately responsible for assessing the effects of the proposed change(s) on product quality, and ensuring that information to support the change is provided for a complete submission of a supplement or annual report. DMF holders must notify affected authorized parties of any DMF changes, additions, or deletions. DMF holders also should provide application holders with sufficient information to determine the appropriate reporting category for their applications based on the potential to have an adverse effect on the identity, strength, quality, purity, or potency of the product as these factors may relate to the safety or effectiveness of the product. This notification should occur well before making any changes to permit authorized parties to submit application changes within an appropriate time frame. For additional information on DMF submission guidelines, refer to 21 CFR 314.420, 21 CFR 601.51(a), and the draft guidance for industry Drug Master Files (October 2019).

This guidance is not intended to alter existing submission processes; applicants should follow the recommendations in current guidance documents for reporting CMC changes to an approved application (see References).

三級通報機制 (Tiered Reporting System)：

Risk-Based Approach (風險導向方法)：FDA 評估 CCS 變更的核心原則是「對產品 identity、strength、quality、purity、potency 的潛在不良影響」。風險越高，通報類別越嚴格。

Drug Master File (DMF, 藥物主檔案)：DMF 是 CCS 供應商（如玻璃瓶製造商）向 FDA 提交的機密技術檔案，包含組成、製程、品質控制等資訊。藥廠可在 supplement 中引用 DMF，但仍需提供足夠的產品專屬資訊 (product-specific information)。

比喻說明 Analogy

三級通報機制就像房屋裝修的審批程序：

  • PAS = 結構變更（如拆牆）：必須先取得建築許可才能開工，因為可能影響結構安全
  • CBE-30 = 水電變更（如移動插座）：先通知主管機關，30 天內沒有異議就可以動工
  • Annual Report = 油漆換色：不影響安全，做完之後在年度維護報告中記錄即可

從藥品角度：換一種全新材質的玻璃瓶（PAS）vs 同供應商不同廠址（AR），風險截然不同。

重點提示 Key Notes

ICH Q9(R1) + Q12 的核心角色：FDA 明確建議使用 ICH Q9(R1) 的品質風險管理 (Quality Risk Management) 原則和 ICH Q12 的產品生命週期管理工具（如 Established Conditions、PACMPs）。這意味著，如果藥廠能展示充分的風險評估和緩解策略，FDA 可能同意降低通報類別（例如從 PAS 降到 CBE-30）。

CCS 相容性的多維度評估：CCS 的適用性不只看容器本身，還要考慮：

  • Glass delamination（玻璃脫層）——特別是高 pH 配方
  • Leachables（可溶出物）——與藥品的交互作用
  • Particulate matter（微粒物質）——來源可能是玻璃或瓶塞
  • 蛋白質與金屬離子的交互作用——生物製品尤須注意
  • 凍乾 (lyophilization) 製程對產品品質的影響

USP 標準的強制性：文中使用 "must be conducted" 而非 "should"，表示 USP 合規測試是強制性要求，不可省略。關鍵的 USP 章節包括 <381>（彈性體瓶塞）、<660>（玻璃容器）、<1207>（包裝完整性）、<1660>（玻璃內壁耐久性）。

重點提示 DMF 責任鏈

DMF Holder 的通知義務：DMF 持有者（通常是 CCS 組件供應商）在做任何 DMF 變更時，必須通知所有受影響的授權方 (authorized parties)。而且這個通知要在變更之前足夠提前發出，讓藥廠有時間提交相應的 supplement。

對 CDMO 的啟示：作為 CDMO，你不直接持有 NDA/BLA，但你的客戶依賴你的資訊來判斷通報類別。如果你的玻璃瓶供應商通知你變更了製程，你需要及時將此資訊傳達給客戶，讓他們評估是否需要向 FDA 提交 supplement。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 供應商變更管理流程：

  1. 接收通知：玻璃瓶供應商通知你他們的製造廠址將從 A 廠移至 B 廠
  2. 初步風險評估：評估變更範圍——僅廠址？還是連製程也變了？
  3. 查表決定通報類別：參考本 Guidance Appendix 表格（Section 3 將詳述）
  4. 通知客戶 (Application Holder)：提供變更詳情和建議的通報類別
  5. 準備支持數據：CCIT、穩定性數據、media fill 結果等
  6. 客戶提交 Supplement：客戶向 FDA 提交相應等級的 supplement

這個流程與 PIC/S Annex 1 (2022) 對 CCS 完整性的要求一致——變更管理是污染控制策略 (Contamination Control Strategy) 的一部分。

原文內容 Original Text

The tables in the Appendix of this guidance include information derived from existing FDA guidance documents that contain recommendations for reporting category and submission content related to common CMC changes to CCS components (glass vials and stoppers) for finished drug and biological products. The tables include the type of data recommended to support a specific change (as discussed in the guidance for industry Container Closure Systems for Packaging Human Drugs and Biologics; Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls Documentation (May 1999)), as well as the recommended reporting category. Some recommendations regarding data submission and reporting categories may differ based on the nature of the product and the potential risk to product quality as it may relate to safety and effectiveness of the product. The quantity of data that should be included in the submission depends on the application type and available body of knowledge.

"The quantity of data that should be included in the submission depends on the application type and available body of knowledge."

Footnote 12: The quantity of data recommended in the Appendix tables for NDAs, ANDAs, and BLAs is derived from related guidance documents. For example, the number of batches is derived from principles outlined in scale-up and postapproval changes guidance documents (e.g., 1 to 3 batches for ANDAs and NDAs depending on the body of knowledge). Where guidance documents do not provide specific information on the quantity of data expected to support the change, the applicant may contact FDA to obtain feedback.

Changes should be supported with confirmatory batch data. To assess the change, FDA may request that the applicant provide additional information in the supplement or referenced DMF.

Footnote 13: For the purposes of this guidance, confirmatory data means comparative product batch data (on product manufactured prior to and after the proposed change) that is sufficient to demonstrate no adverse effect on product quality due to the proposed CCS change.

An applicant seeking to make more than one related change concurrently should submit a supplement with data appropriate to address the potential risk from the cumulative effect of multiple changes. Where the recommended reporting categories for the individual changes differ, the submission category selected should be the one associated with the highest risk change (e.g., PAS) of the categories recommended for the individual changes.

When the CCS changes described in the Appendix tables necessitate a corresponding change in finished product specifications, the applicant should provide all studies and data appropriate to support the affected specification change(s). The reporting category depends on the potential to have an adverse effect on the identity, strength, quality, purity, or potency of the product as these factors may relate to the safety or effectiveness of the product. For example, tightening the acceptance criteria for a quality attribute may warrant a lower reporting category than widening the acceptance criteria.

Footnote 14: The ICH guidances for industry Q6A Specifications: Test Procedures and Acceptance Criteria for New Drug Substances and New Drug Products: Chemical Substances (December 2000) and Q6B Specifications: Test Procedures and Acceptance Criteria for Biotechnological/Biological Products (August 1999) define specifications as "a list of tests, references to analytical procedures, and appropriate acceptance criteria that are numerical limits, ranges, or other criteria for the tests described."

Before introducing a new CCS component that differs from the specified CCS component type/grade in product labeling (e.g., "packaged in type 1 glass"), a supplement to change product labeling may be appropriate. When the CCS new component differs from that specified in the applicable USP monograph, appropriate engagement with USP regarding an update to the product monograph may be necessary to ensure continued compliance with the monograph.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Confirmatory Data (確認性數據)：這是 FDA 在 CCS 變更評估中要求的核心證據。確認性數據指的是比較性的批次數據——比較變更前後生產的產品，證明變更未對產品品質造成不良影響。這不是簡單的「通過測試」，而是要展示變更前後的一致性 (comparability)。

Body of Knowledge (知識基礎)：FDA 允許根據現有知識基礎來調整提交數據量。如果申請者已經累積了豐富的產品和 CCS 相容性數據（例如通過長期穩定性研究、多批次生產經驗），則可能需要較少的額外數據。這是 ICH Q12 中 Established Conditions 概念的延伸。

Cumulative Effect (累積效應)：當同時進行多個相關變更時，不能分別評估每個變更的風險，而要評估變更的累積影響。通報類別以最高風險的個別變更為準。

比喻說明 Analogy

同時變更的「最高風險」原則就像機場安檢升級：如果你同時攜帶了筆電（一般安檢）和一瓶液體超過 100ml（加強安檢），你不能分開兩個通道處理——你必須走最嚴格的安檢通道。同理，如果你同時變更玻璃瓶供應商（可能是 AR）和瓶塞材料（PAS），整包 supplement 必須以 PAS 規格提交。

重點提示 Key Notes

FDA CCS Guidance (1999) 的基礎角色：本 Guidance 的 Appendix 表格中推薦的數據類型，源自 1999 年的 Container Closure Systems for Packaging Human Drugs and Biologics 指引。該 1999 年指引至今仍是 FDA CCS 評估的基石文件。理解 1999 年指引中對 protection、safety、compatibility、performance 四個面向的評估框架，是正確使用本 2024 年 Guidance 的前提。

Tightening vs Widening Specifications：這是一個常被忽略的重要原則。收緊 (tightening) acceptance criteria（例如將微粒限度從 NMT 600 降至 NMT 300 個/container）通常被視為低風險變更，因為代表更嚴格的品質要求；而放寬 (widening)（例如從 NMT 300 升至 NMT 600 個/container）則是高風險，因為可能降低產品品質。

重點提示 與 SUPAC 指引的連結

SUPAC 系列：Appendix 表格中推薦的批次數量（如 NDA/ANDA 1-3 批）源自 SUPAC (Scale-Up and Postapproval Changes) 系列指引的原則。SUPAC-IR（即釋製劑）和 SUPAC-SS（半固體製劑）雖然主要針對口服製劑，但其中的 CCS 變更通報原則也被本 Guidance 引用。對於注射劑，本 Guidance 的建議更為具體和嚴格。

NDA/ANDA vs BLA 的差異：生物製品（BLA）的 CCS 變更通常比化學藥品（NDA/ANDA）要求更高的通報類別。這是因為生物製品（如蛋白質、抗體）對 CCS 組件的交互作用更敏感——例如，玻璃瓶溶出的金屬離子可能導致蛋白質聚集 (aggregation)。

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 情境 — Confirmatory Data 的準備：

假設客戶的 NDA 產品需要從供應商 A 的 Type I 玻璃瓶切換到供應商 B。作為 CDMO，你需要準備的 confirmatory data 包括：

  1. 變更前數據 (Pre-change)：最近 3 批使用供應商 A 玻璃瓶的 release 和穩定性數據
  2. 變更後數據 (Post-change)：至少 1-3 批使用供應商 B 玻璃瓶的 release 和穩定性數據（加速 + 長期）
  3. 比較分析：展示變更前後的 CQA（如 pH、微粒、可見異物、含量、效價）無顯著差異
  4. CCIT 數據：Container Closure Integrity Testing 確認密封完整性

時程規劃提示：加速穩定性數據至少需要 3 個月，長期數據需要 12-24 個月。因此，CCS 供應商變更計畫應提前 1-2 年啟動。

實務應用 Labeling 合規性

標示變更的連鎖效應：如果你的產品標示寫 "packaged in Type I glass"，而新的 CCS 組件屬於不同 type/grade，除了 CCS 變更的 supplement 外，還需要另一個 labeling supplement。同時，如果新組件與 USP monograph 中規定的不同，可能需要與 USP 協商更新 monograph。

這對 CDMO 的啟示：在協助客戶評估 CCS 變更時，務必同步檢查產品標示和 USP monograph 的合規性，避免遺漏附加的法規提交。

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 你的客戶同時要變更玻璃瓶供應商（無組成變化，建議 AR）和瓶塞尺寸（建議 CBE-30）。正確的提交方式是什麼？為什麼不能分開提交兩個不同類別的 supplement？
  2. "Body of knowledge" 如何實際運用？舉例說明一個擁有豐富知識基礎的產品和一個新核准產品，在 CCS 變更時需要的數據量差異。
  3. 為什麼 confirmatory data 必須是「比較性」數據？只提交變更後的批次數據（顯示符合規格）是否足夠？
  4. Tightening vs widening specifications 的原則如何應用到 CCS 變更後的微粒限度 (particulate matter limits)？請以具體數字舉例。

IV.A Risk-Based Considerations for Supplement Reporting Categories 通報類別的風險考量

原文內容 Original Text

For CCS and component changes that are the subject of this guidance, FDA intends to consider risk-based approaches using existing tools and capabilities to facilitate providing timely feedback, guidance, and assessment of proposed changes as discussed below.

FDA may expedite the assessment of a supplement or determine that a different reporting category is appropriate, taking into account public health priorities and the applicant's rationale and associated risk assessment and mitigation strategy for the proposed change. Should a different reporting category be determined to be appropriate, FDA will notify the applicant and convey its rationale for the determination.

Consistent with applicable regulations and existing guidance documents related to postapproval CMC changes for NDAs, ANDAs, and BLAs, FDA may consider available information and approaches to mitigate the risk to product quality when determining whether a lower reporting category for supplements is appropriate. Applicants should consider using tools in ICH Q12 (e.g., established conditions, postapproval change management protocols (PACMPs)) to support proposals for reduced reporting categories based on an increased understanding of the risk to product quality when making a change related to the glass vial or stopper.

Before submitting a supplement with a lower reporting category than what is required in existing regulations and recommended in existing guidance, applicants should contact FDA for feedback and concurrence. For Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER)-regulated products, applicants should contact the regulatory project management staff (regulatory project manager or regulatory business process manager) within the appropriate CDER review office responsible for the subject product application. For Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research (CBER)-regulated products, applicants should contact the appropriate CBER review office. Additionally, if the CDER- or CBER-regulated product could enter, or is currently in, drug shortage, then the respective applicant should include drugshortages@fda.hhs.gov for products regulated by CDER and cbershortage@fda.hhs.gov for products regulated by CBER on those communications.

Footnote 17: For additional information, see 21 CFR 314.70(b)(4). For FDA policies describing expedited review requests related to prior approval supplements to NDAs and BLAs, see MAPP 5310.3 Rev 1 Requests for Expedited Review of New Drug Application and Biologics License Application Prior Approval Supplements Submitted for Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls Changes; for FDA policies describing prioritization of the review related to original ANDAs, amendments, and supplements thereof, see MAPP 5240.3 Rev. 5 Prioritization of the Review of Original ANDAs, Amendments, and Supplements; for FDA policies on drug shortage management, see MAPP 4190.1 Rev. 3 Drug Shortage Management.

Footnote 18: Depending on the regulatory jurisdiction of the product, the applicant can contact the responsible review division within CDER or CBER (as applicable) to gain additional feedback on specific changes, the information needed to support the change, and the filing approach, as needed.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

ICH Q12 工具在 CCS 變更中的應用：

  • Established Conditions (ECs, 既定條件)：ICH Q12 中定義的、對產品品質有直接影響且需要法規通報的製程條件。明確區分 EC 與非 EC，有助於集中資源管理真正關鍵的變更。
  • PACMPs (Post-Approval Change Management Protocols, 核准後變更管理計畫書)：預先核准的變更計畫，定義了未來變更的評估方法和接受標準。與 Comparability Protocol (CP) 概念相似，但更廣泛。

Drug Shortage 觸發加速審查：如果產品面臨或可能面臨藥品短缺 (drug shortage)，FDA 會考慮加速審查 supplement 或接受更低的通報類別。這是一個重要的策略杠桿——CDMO 的客戶在論證降低通報類別時，可以將供應鏈風險和潛在短缺作為論據之一。

比喻說明 Analogy

Risk-based 通報類別降級就像信用卡額度調整：銀行（FDA）根據你的信用歷史（body of knowledge + risk assessment）決定你的信用額度（通報類別的嚴格程度）。如果你一直按時還款、信用紀錄良好（有完善的品質系統、豐富的產品知識、充分的風險評估），銀行可能願意給你更高的額度（更低的通報類別）。但你必須先與銀行溝通（contact FDA for feedback and concurrence），不能自行決定降級。

重點提示 Key Notes

"Contact FDA first" 的強制性：FDA 明確要求，在提交低於法規要求/指引建議的通報類別之前，必須先聯繫 FDA 取得反饋和同意 (feedback and concurrence)。這不是可選步驟——未經 FDA 同意就提交較低類別的 supplement，可能導致拒絕受理 (refuse to file) 或要求重新提交。

CDER vs CBER 的聯繫管道：

  • CDER 產品：聯繫 Regulatory Project Manager (RPM) 或 Regulatory Business Process Manager
  • CBER 產品：聯繫相關的 CBER review office
  • 藥品短缺：額外抄送 drugshortages@fda.hhs.gov（CDER）或 cbershortage@fda.hhs.gov（CBER）

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 策略 — 利用 ICH Q12 降低通報負擔：

假設你的 CDMO 為多個客戶充填同一規格的 2R 玻璃瓶。如果你需要切換到新的玻璃瓶供應商（例如從 Schott 切換到 Nipro），以下是利用 ICH Q12 工具的策略：

  1. 建立 Established Conditions：明確定義哪些玻璃瓶特性是 EC（如 composition、dimensions、surface treatment），哪些不是（如 packaging configuration）
  2. 準備 Risk Assessment：使用 ICH Q9(R1) 工具（如 FMEA）評估供應商切換的風險
  3. 聯繫 FDA：向 RPM 提出降低通報類別的提案，附上風險評估和緩解策略
  4. 如有藥品短缺風險：同步通知 Drug Shortage Program，爭取加速審查

這個策略特別適用於 dual sourcing 場景——預先建立好框架，未來供應商切換可以更快速實施。

IV.B Comparability Protocols 比較性協議書

原文內容 Original Text

As explained in the guidance for industry Comparability Protocols for Postapproval Changes to the Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls Information in an NDA, ANDA, or BLA (October 2022), a comparability protocol (CP) is a comprehensive, prospectively written plan for assessing the effect of a proposed CMC postapproval change(s) on the identity, strength, quality, purity, and potency of a drug product or a biological product. It is a tool for applicants to obtain feedback from FDA on prospective scientific approaches, submitted as a part of an original application or PAS, to facilitate expeditious implementation of postapproval changes. CPs allow FDA to review a description of one or more proposed CMC postapproval changes, supporting information (including any analysis and risk assessment activities), the plan for implementing the change(s), and, if appropriate, the proposed reduced reporting category for the change(s). By delineating the specific approach to be used to evaluate one or more future changes and the rationale for that approach, the applicant can gain the Agency's approval of the plan well in advance of the need to implement the change(s). This process can facilitate a more efficient submission process for the applicant and review process for FDA.

"A CP can then be used for a one-time change(s) or be used repeatedly for a specified type of change over the life cycle of a product."

If the original application or PAS containing the CP is approved, the change usually can be implemented under a lower reporting category than would normally be expected based on recommendations in guidance (e.g., changes otherwise requiring a prior approval supplement could be implemented under a CBE-30). A CP can then be used for a one-time change(s) or be used repeatedly for a specified type of change over the life cycle of a product. A CP can also be submitted to cover an identical change(s) that affects multiple applications (grouped supplements, trans-BLA submissions). A CP is a well-suited tool for making changes to glass vials and stoppers, because a change to one component can be implemented to many products sharing that container or closure, and the protocol could be used multiple times as the need arises for additional changes to accommodate the dynamic supply chain evolution.

Footnote 19: See the guidance for industry Chemistry, Manufacturing, and Controls Changes to an Approved Application: Certain Biological Products (June 2021).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Comparability Protocol (CP, 比較性協議書)：CP 是一份預先撰寫的、經 FDA 核准的計畫書，描述未來 CCS 變更時將如何評估、測試和判定變更可接受。CP 的核心價值在於「先核准計畫，後快速執行」。

CP 的三個關鍵特性：

  • 一次性或可重複使用：CP 可以設計為單次變更（如從供應商 A 切換到供應商 B），也可以設計為可反覆使用（如「未來任何玻璃瓶供應商切換均適用本 CP」）
  • 跨產品適用：CP 可以覆蓋共享同一 CCS 組件的多個產品，透過 grouped supplements 或 trans-BLA submissions 實現
  • 降低通報類別：一旦 CP 被核准，原本需要 PAS 的變更可以降級為 CBE-30 實施

Grouped Supplements / Trans-BLA Submissions：當一個 CCS 變更影響多個 NDA/BLA 產品時，可以用 grouped supplement 同時覆蓋多個申請。這大幅降低了法規提交的重複工作。

比喻說明 Analogy

Comparability Protocol 就像預先核准的旅行清單 (pre-approved travel checklist)。假設你是國際業務人員，每次出差都需要公司批准。你可以和主管（FDA）事先制定一份通用出差計畫：定義出差條件、費用上限、報告格式。主管核准後，未來每次符合條件的出差，你只需填寫清單、附上證明，就可以快速出發，不需要每次都重新申請完整的出差審批。

對 CCS 變更來說：你預先定義了「什麼樣的供應商切換是可接受的」「需要做哪些測試」「判定標準是什麼」，FDA 核准後，未來每次供應商切換只需按照 CP 執行，提交 CBE-30 而非 PAS。

重點提示 Key Notes

CP 是 CDMO 最強大的法規工具之一：

FDA 在本 Guidance 中明確指出 CP 特別適合 CCS 變更，原因是：

  1. 一對多 (One-to-Many)：一個玻璃瓶變更可能影響使用同一瓶型的所有產品。CP 可以一次覆蓋所有產品。
  2. 供應鏈動態性 (Dynamic Supply Chain)：玻璃瓶供應商的廠址變更、製程升級是持續發生的。可重複使用的 CP 讓每次變更都能快速實施。
  3. 時間效益：PAS 的審查時間可能需要 6-12 個月。透過 CP，變更可以降級為 CBE-30（30 天），或甚至 Annual Report。

與 ICH Q12 PACMP 的關係：CP 是 FDA 的具體機制，PACMP 是 ICH Q12 的概念性框架。二者目標一致——透過預先計畫降低核准後變更的法規負擔。FDA 在其 ICH Q12 實施指引中說明了兩者的對應關係。

公式與計算 CP 投資回報分析

CP 開發成本 ≈ 50-100 萬台幣（撰寫 + 內部審查 + FDA 提交）
PAS 每次提交成本 ≈ 30-50 萬台幣（數據準備 + 審查費 + 等待成本）
PAS 審查等待時間 ≈ 6-12 個月（期間可能無法使用新供應商）

假設 CP 覆蓋 5 個產品、未來 5 年 3 次供應商變更：
  不使用 CP：5 產品 × 3 次 × 40 萬 = 600 萬台幣（PAS 成本）
  使用 CP：100 萬（CP 開發）+ 5 × 3 × 5 萬（CBE-30 成本）= 175 萬台幣

節省：600 - 175 = 425 萬台幣
時間節省：每次變更從 6-12 月縮短至 30 天

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 的 CP 策略規劃：

作為臨床期 CDMO，建議客戶在以下時機啟動 CP 規劃：

  • NDA/BLA 提交階段：在原始申請中包含 CP，這是最理想的時機——FDA 審查申請時一併核准 CP，無需額外提交 PAS
  • 商業化初期：當產品開始商業生產後，預見到供應鏈需求（如 dual sourcing），提交含 CP 的 PAS
  • 供應鏈危機前：不要等到供應商出問題才行動。主動建立 CP 是供應鏈韌性 (supply chain resilience) 策略的一部分

CP 內容建議（for CCS 變更）：

  1. 定義變更範圍：哪些類型的 CCS 變更由本 CP 覆蓋
  2. 測試計畫：CCIT、extractables/leachables、穩定性研究的規格和方法
  3. 判定標準：什麼條件下變更可接受
  4. 通報類別論證：為什麼降級是合理的
  5. 適用的產品清單（grouped supplements）

練習思考 Practice Questions

  1. 為什麼 FDA 特別指出 CP 「well-suited」 for glass vial and stopper changes？相較於其他 CMC 變更（如配方變更、製程變更），CCS 變更有什麼特殊性使 CP 特別有效？

The following tables include information derived from existing FDA guidance documents containing FDA recommendations for reporting categories and submission content for common chemistry, manufacturing, and controls changes to glass vials and stoppers, the container closure system (CCS) components of finished pharmaceuticals and biological products. These tables provide baseline recommendations for the information to be submitted and the reporting category for specific changes, and do not consider the cumulative effect of concurrent changes. As stated in sections III and IV of the guidance, FDA may request more information to support the change, and determine that a different reporting category is appropriate based on risk assessment information related to the specific change proposed.

These tables do not include all possible CCS changes that would necessitate a submission as described in applicable regulations. Application holders should consult existing guidance documents for further information on CCS change recommendations. Depending on regulatory jurisdiction of the product application, the applicant can contact the responsible review division within the Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER) or the Center for Biologics Evaluation and Research (CBER) (as applicable) to gain additional feedback on specific changes, the information needed to support the change, and the filing approach, as needed.

For the following tables, the term confirmatory data means comparative data that demonstrate no adverse impact on product quality due to the proposed CCS change.

重點提示 凍乾產品的特殊升級

核心概念解析 Method vs Parameters

Method 變更 vs Parameter 變更的分界線：

Table E 清楚展示了一個關鍵的法規區分：

  • Method 變更（方法變更）= PAS：例如滅菌方法從 moist heat 改為 dry heat，或從濕熱改為照射 (irradiation)。這是根本性的製程變更。
  • Parameter 變更（參數變更）= CBE-30：例如在同一滅菌方法（乾熱）下調整溫度或時間。這是同一方法框架內的微調。

這個「方法 vs 參數」的區分邏輯貫穿整個 Table E 和 Table F。

Stopper Siliconization 為何需要 PAS：矽化 (siliconization) 改變了瓶塞表面的化學性質，直接影響 extractables/leachables profile 和 CCS 完整性。這等同於「材料性質變更」而非單純的「製程參數調整」。

比喻說明 Analogy

Method vs Parameter 的差別就像烹飪中的「換烹調方式」vs「調整火候」：

  • Method 變更 (PAS)：從水煮改為油炸——完全不同的烹調方式，成品口感和營養成分都會不同
  • Parameter 變更 (CBE-30)：水煮溫度從 100°C 調到 95°C——同樣是水煮，只是微調條件

同樣地，stopper siliconization process 變更之所以是 PAS，是因為它相當於「給菜加了一層新的調味醬」——改變了表面化學性質，而不只是調整製程參數。

重點提示 Key Notes

CDMO 直接相關的變更：Table E 中的變更類型多數發生在 CDMO 環境中：

  • Vial sterilization/depyrogenation：如果 CDMO 自行做 vial 的 depyrogenation（如 dry heat tunnel），任何方法變更需要 PAS，參數變更需要 CBE-30
  • Stopper washing：CDMO 自行洗瓶塞的流程變更只需 CBE-30
  • Stopper sterilization：方法變更（如從 autoclave 改為 gamma irradiation）需要 PAS；同一方法下的參數調整需要 CBE-30

這些都是 CDMO 日常操作中可能遇到的變更，尤其在設備升級或製程優化時。
