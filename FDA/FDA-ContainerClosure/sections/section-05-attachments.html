Attachment A: Regulatory Requirements 法規要求

原文內容 Original Text

1. The Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act

a. Section 501

A drug or device shall be deemed to be adulterated "if its container is composed, in whole or in part, of any poisonous or deleterious substance which may render the contents injurious to health" (section 501(a)(3)); or "if it is a drug and the methods used in, or the facilities or controls used for, its manufacture, processing, packing, or holding do not conform to or are not operated or administered in conformity with current good manufacturing practice to assure that such drug meets the requirements of this Act as to safety and has the identity and strength, and meets the quality and purity characteristics, which it purports or is represented to possess" (section 501(a)(2)(B)).

b. Section 502

A drug or device shall be deemed to be misbranded:

  • "[i]f it purports to be a drug the name of which is recognized in an official compendium, unless it is packaged and labeled as prescribed therein" (section 502(g))
  • "[i]f it is a drug and its container is so made, formed, or filled as to be misleading" (section 502(i)(1))
  • "[i]f it is a drug and its packaging or labeling is in violation of an applicable regulation issued pursuant to section 3 or 4 of the Poison Prevention Packaging Act of 1970" (section 502(p))

c. Section 505

"No person shall introduce or deliver for introduction into interstate commerce any new drug, unless an approval of an application filed pursuant to subsection (b) or (j) is effective with respect to such drug" (section 505(a)).

Section 505(b)(1)(D) requires "a full description of the methods used in, and the facilities and controls used for, the manufacture, processing, and packing of such drug."

2. The Code of Federal Regulations

a. 21 CFR 211 — Current Good Manufacturing Practice for Finished Pharmaceuticals

21 CFR 211.94 — Drug Product Containers and Closures:
(a) Drug product containers and closures shall not be reactive, additive, or absorptive so as to alter the safety, identity, strength, quality, or purity of the drug beyond the official or established requirements.
(b) Container closure systems shall provide adequate protection against foreseeable external factors in storage and use that can cause deterioration or contamination of the drug product.
(c) Drug product containers and closures shall be clean and, where indicated by the nature of the drug, sterilized and processed to remove pyrogenic properties to ensure that they are suitable for their intended use.
(d) Standards or acceptance criteria, test methods, and, where indicated, methods of cleaning, sterilizing, and processing to remove pyrogenic properties shall be written and followed for drug product containers and closures.

Related sections include:

  • Subpart E: Control of Components and Drug Product Containers and Closures (21 CFR 211.80–211.94)
  • Subpart F: Production and Process Controls (21 CFR 211.100–211.115)
  • Subpart G: Packaging and Labeling Control (21 CFR 211.122–211.137), including tamper-resistant packaging for OTC products (21 CFR 211.132)

b. 16 CFR 1700-1702 — Special Packaging (CPSC)

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) is responsible for enforcing the Poison Prevention Packaging Act of 1970 (PPPA). The PPPA requires special packaging of hazardous household substances to protect children from serious personal injury or serious illness from handling, using, or ingesting the substances.

Drug products containing controlled substances, most human oral prescription drug products (including oral investigational drugs used in outpatient trials), and OTC drug preparations containing aspirin, acetaminophen, diphenhydramine, liquid methyl salicylate, ibuprofen, loperamide, lidocaine, dibucaine, naproxen, iron, or ketoprofen, require special packaging (16 CFR 1700.14).

Several situations where child-resistant packaging is not required:

  • Bulk-packaged prescription drugs intended for pharmacist repackaging
  • Prescribed drugs dispensed within institutions (hospitals, nursing homes)
  • Prescription drug samples distributed to physicians (physician samples)

For OTC preparations, manufacturers may market one size in non-child-resistant packaging as long as child-resistant packages are also supplied (requires special labeling per 16 CFR 1700.5).

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

21 CFR 211.94 四項核心要求:

(a) Non-reactive / Non-additive / Non-absorptive: 不改變 SISPQ

(b) Adequate protection: 抵抗外部因素導致的劣化或污染

(c) Clean / Sterilized / Depyrogenated: 適合其預定用途

(d) Written standards: 必須有書面的標準、接受標準和測試方法

這四點構成了 CGMP 對包裝材料管控的法規基礎，也是 FDA 查廠時的主要檢查項目。

比喻說明 Analogy

21 CFR 211.94 就像餐廳對餐具的四大要求：(a) 餐具不能與食物起化學反應（不沾鍋塗層不脫落）；(b) 餐具要能保護食物不被灰塵、蟲害污染（有蓋的容器）；(c) 餐具要乾淨、必要時消毒（高溫殺菌的餐具）；(d) 清洗和消毒程序要有書面 SOP。

重點提示 Key Notes

Indirect Food Additive Regulations (21 CFR 174-186) 的限制: 這些法規原本是針對食品包裝的，並不直接適用於藥品包裝。但 FDA 接受對固體口服劑型的包裝材料引用這些法規作為安全性證據。注意：對液體口服劑型（尤其是長期使用者）和注射劑，僅引用 food additive 法規通常不夠——需要額外的 extraction/toxicological evaluation。

Child-resistant packaging 豁免: 機構內使用 (hospitals/nursing homes) 和 physician samples 可豁免。但出院帶藥處方仍需遵守 PPPA 要求。

圖表解讀 Table Analysis

USP General Chapters 對照表: 此表是包裝測試方法的「路線圖」。對無菌 CDMO 最重要的章節：

  • USP <381>: elastomeric stopper 的生物和物化測試
  • USP <87>/<88>: 塑膠和彈性體的生物反應性測試
  • USP <661>: 玻璃容器化學阻抗、塑膠物化測試、光透過率
  • USP <71>: 無菌測試（驗證容器完整性）

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 的品保部門應建立一份「法規對照清單」，將每個包裝組件對應到相關的 CFR 條文、USP 章節和測試方法。例如：注射劑 vial → 21 CFR 211.94 + USP <661> (Glass Containers) + USP <1> (Injections)；elastomeric stopper → USP <381> + USP <87>/<88>。這份清單是 FDA 查廠時展示法規遵從的最佳工具。

Attachment C: Extraction Studies 萃取研究

原文內容 Original Text

An extraction study of a packaging component typically involves exposing a sample of the component, often subdivided into small pieces to increase surface area, to an appropriate solvent system at elevated temperatures, followed by chemical analysis. The purpose of elevated temperature is to increase the rate of extraction, so that a short experimental time may simulate a longer exposure time at room temperature, or to maximize the amount of extractables obtained from a sample.

The methods employed to analyze the resulting extracts vary, depending on the purpose of the extraction study and the nature of the packaging component. The extraction solvent may be evaporated to concentrate the extracts or to determine the total weight of nonvolatile extractables. Appropriate methods, such as HPLC or gas chromatography, may be used to obtain qualitative or quantitative extraction profiles of volatile or nonvolatile extractables.

Extraction studies may be conducted during the qualification of packaging components for any of the following purposes:

  • To perform USP characterization tests on plastics (USP <661>) or elastomers (USP <381>)
  • To perform USP Biological Reactivity Tests (USP <87> and <88>) on plastics or elastomers
  • To obtain qualitative extraction profiles of plastics or elastomers
  • To obtain quantitative extraction profiles of plastics or elastomers
  • To evaluate whether the FDA indirect food additive regulations provide an adequate indicator of safety

Extraction studies may also be conducted on a routine basis as a quality control measure to monitor the chemical compositions of elastomeric or other packaging components.

The solvent that should be used in an extraction study depends on the purpose of the study. The ideal situation is for the extracting solvent to have the same propensity to extract substances as the dosage form, thus obtaining the same quantitative extraction profile. For this study, the preferred solvent would be the drug product or placebo vehicle. When feasible, the dosage form itself would be used. A stronger extracting solvent than the drug product would be used to obtain a qualitative extraction profile that would be used to establish quality control criteria.

導師解析 Tutorial Commentary

核心概念解析 Key Concepts

Extraction Study (萃取研究): 將包裝組件樣品（通常切碎以增加表面積）暴露在適當溶劑中，於升溫條件下進行萃取，然後分析萃取物。這是 Extractables & Leachables (E&L) 研究的核心實驗方法。

Extractables (可萃取物): 在加速條件（高溫、強溶劑）下從包裝組件中萃取出的化學物質——代表「最壞情況」下可能遷移到藥品中的所有物質。

Leachables (浸出物): 在正常使用條件下實際遷移到藥品中的物質——是 extractables 的子集。

比喻說明 Analogy

Extraction study 就像用熱水泡茶——升溫加速萃取，就像用滾水泡茶比冷泡快得多。茶葉中所有能溶出的物質（extractables）就像用沸水強力萃取得到的一切；而日常飲用溫度下實際溶出的物質（leachables）是其中一小部分。FDA 要求先用「沸水」找出所有可能的溶出物，再評估正常條件下病人會接觸到多少。

重點提示 Key Notes

溶劑選擇邏輯（極為重要）：

  • Quantitative profile（定量）: 用藥品本身或 placebo vehicle 作為溶劑——最能反映實際浸出情況
  • Qualitative profile（定性）: 用比藥品更強的萃取溶劑——找出所有可能的 extractables，用於建立 QC 標準

這個溶劑選擇邏輯直接影響 E&L 研究的設計，也是 FDA 審查的重點。

Extraction study 不僅用於初始 qualification，也可作為常規 QC 手段來監控包裝組件的化學組成一致性。這對 elastomeric stoppers 尤其重要。

公式與計算 Formula

Extraction Study 五大目的：
1. USP <661> plastics 特性測試
2. USP <381> elastomers 特性測試
3. USP <87>/<88> 生物反應性測試
4. Qualitative extraction profile → QC criteria
5. Quantitative extraction profile → Safety evaluation

實務應用 Practical Application

CDMO 在驗證新的包裝組件時，extraction study 的設計應考慮：(1) 產品為水性還是含共溶劑（如 polysorbate 80、乙醇），因為共溶劑會增強萃取能力；(2) 樣品的表面積/體積比要模擬實際使用條件；(3) 升溫條件要合理加速但不能改變材料的化學性質。建議同時進行定性（強溶劑）和定量（藥品 vehicle）兩套研究。結果可與 PQRI 的 Safety Concern Threshold (SCT) 和 Qualification Threshold (QT) 比對以評估安全性。

  • FD&C Act Sections 501/502/505 提供包裝法規的法律基礎——adulterated (有毒容器)、misbranded (包裝遺漏)、NDA 要求 (全面描述)
