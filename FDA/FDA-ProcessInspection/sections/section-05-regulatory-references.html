Part V: Regulatory/Administrative Strategy ── 法規行政策略

Part V: Regulatory/Administrative Strategy

The following types of CGMP deficiencies may warrant regulatory or administrative action. This list is intended as guidance for investigators and is not exhaustive. The severity and pattern of deficiencies, along with the firm's compliance history, will inform the type and scope of action taken.

16 Categories of CGMP Deficiencies Warranting Action

  1. Contamination: Evidence of microbial contamination of sterile drug product, including positive sterility tests (with valid reason for batch distribution) or positive media fills
  2. Specification Failure: Release of product that fails to meet established specifications (identity, strength, quality, or purity)
  3. Distribution of Non-Conforming Product: Distribution of product known to fail specifications or otherwise not conforming to CGMP
  4. Sterilization Validation Failure: Failure to validate terminal sterilization cycles using parametric methods (e.g., F₀ calculation), or use of sterilization cycles without adequate validation
  5. Media Fill Validation Failure: Inadequate aseptic process simulation (media fill) program — insufficient frequency, inadequate design, failure to simulate worst-case conditions, or unresolved positive units
  6. Investigation Failures: Failure to adequately investigate out-of-specification (OOS) results, including positive sterility tests, media fill positives, or EM exceedances; or failure to implement effective corrective actions
  7. Facility/Equipment Inadequacy: Facilities or equipment inadequate to ensure sterility — including HVAC failures, inadequate cleanroom design, or equipment that cannot be adequately cleaned and sanitized
  8. Disinfection Program Failure: Inadequate disinfection/sanitization program, including failure to validate disinfectant efficacy or failure to use sporicides in cleanroom environments
  9. WFI System Failure: Water for Injection system non-compliance — including action level exceedances, inadequate trending, inadequate maintenance, or failure to demonstrate endotoxin removal efficacy
  10. Poor Employee Practices: Documented aseptic technique failures by personnel — including poor gowning practices, improper interventions in aseptic areas, or failure to maintain aseptic conditions
  11. Inadequate Training: Failure to adequately train personnel in aseptic technique, CGMP, or specific job functions; including failure to document training or assess competency
  12. 100% Inspection Failure: Failure to perform adequate 100% visual inspection of sterile products for visible particulates, container defects, or other quality attributes
  13. Batch Record Incompleteness: Incomplete or inaccurate batch production records; failure to document critical in-process controls; or records that cannot be used to reconstruct the manufacturing process
  14. Inadequate Test Methodology: Use of test methods that are not validated, not suitable for their intended purpose, or not in compliance with compendial requirements (e.g., USP <71>, USP <85>)
  15. Inadequate Environmental Monitoring Program: EM program that does not cover all critical areas, all shifts, does not have defined alert/action levels, or fails to trend data over time
  16. Inadequate Personnel Monitoring: Personnel monitoring program that is insufficient in frequency, does not cover critical area personnel, or does not include both glove and gown monitoring

⚖️ 法規行政策略的查廠觀點

🚨 16 類重大缺失的分類邏輯

FDA 列出這 16 類缺失，是幫助查廠員判斷「哪些問題嚴重到需要啟動法規行政行動」。可以分成幾大群組理解：

缺失 1–3：產品直接品質問題
污染、規格失敗、分銷不合格品 ── 消費者已或可能已接觸到問題產品
缺失 4–5：關鍵驗證失敗
末端滅菌驗證、Media Fill 驗證 ── 製程的「無菌保證基礎」無法建立
缺失 6：調查系統崩壞
OOS / 陽性測試結果無有效調查或 CAPA ── 問題沒被發現或被掩蓋
缺失 7–9：基礎設施問題
廠房設備不足、消毒程序失效、WFI 系統問題 ── 製造環境本身有問題
缺失 10–11：人員問題
無菌操作不良、訓練不足 ── 人為因素是無菌失敗的主要來源
缺失 12–13：操作與記錄問題
目視檢驗不足、批次記錄不完整 ── 無法保證每批品質
缺失 14–16：監測與測試系統問題
測試方法不適當、EM 程序不足、人員監測不足 ── 偵測系統失靈

💡 Warning Letter 的行政本質

CPGM 7356.002A 明確指出：Warning Letter 是一種行政建議行動（advisory action），而非最終懲處。它傳遞的訊息是：「FDA 已識別問題，請改正，否則將採取更強力措施。」

對於嚴重的違規（如已有汙染產品流出市場），FDA 可以跳過 Warning Letter 直接採取法律行動（Recall、Seizure、Injunction）。

💡 為什麼 FDA Guidance 等同法規？

FDA Guidance Document 雖然法律上是「建議」，但在實務中被視為標準。原因是：若廠商不依循 FDA Guidance，FDA 在查廠時會要求廠商解釋「為什麼採用不同做法」以及「如何證明替代方案同等有效」。這個舉證責任極重，等同於強制遵守。

Part VII: Center Responsibilities ── 中心職責

Part VII: Center Responsibilities

CDER Oversight Role

The Center for Drug Evaluation and Research (CDER) provides oversight of sterile drug process inspections through two primary offices:

  • Office of Pharmaceutical Quality (OPQ): Provides technical expertise for complex manufacturing processes; reviews inspection findings for significant pharmaceutical quality issues; participates in regulatory action decisions for significant CGMP violations
  • Office of Surveillance (OS): Coordinates surveillance inspection planning; reviews and acts on inspection reports for potential regulatory action; liaises with ORA on CGMP enforcement

Warning Letter as Advisory Action

The Warning Letter is explicitly characterized in this CPGM as an advisory action — it advises the firm of significant violations and requests corrective action within a specified timeframe (typically 15 working days). Key points:

  • Warning Letters are posted publicly on the FDA website
  • Firms must respond with specific corrective action plans and timelines
  • Failure to respond adequately may trigger escalated regulatory action
  • For serious violations (e.g., contaminated product already in commerce), FDA may proceed directly to Seizure, Injunction, or Recall without first issuing a Warning Letter

Drug Shortage Notification and Balance

CDER explicitly acknowledges the tension between CGMP enforcement and drug availability. The CPGM states that CDER will:

  • Consider drug availability implications when making regulatory action decisions for products in shortage or with limited alternative sources
  • Work with firms to identify ways to maintain supply while addressing CGMP violations
  • This does not mean CGMP violations are overlooked — rather, CDER coordinates the scope and timing of actions
  • Manufacturers are encouraged to proactively notify FDA of supply disruptions at: DrugShortages@CDER.fda.gov

CDER's Role in Inspection Support

  • Provides science-based technical guidance to ORA investigators upon request
  • Reviews inspection reports (EIR — Establishment Inspection Report)
  • Issues NAI / VAI / OAI classifications in coordination with ORA
  • Supports foreign inspection program for sterile drug manufacturers

🏛️ CDER 中心職責的管理架構

⚠️ Warning Letter 的本質：行政建議，非最終懲處

很多人誤解 Warning Letter 是 FDA 最嚴厲的行動，實際上它只是行政建議的起點：

  • Warning Letter = FDA 通知廠商「我們發現重大問題，請在 15 個工作日內回應」
  • 公開於 FDA 網站，具相當大的市場/聲譽壓力
  • 若問題嚴重（如已有汙染品上市），可直接進行查扣（Seizure）、禁制令（Injunction）或召回（Recall）

⚖️ 品質合規 vs. 藥品可及性的平衡

CPGM 罕見地承認 FDA 在執法上面臨「雙重目標」：

  • 確保 CGMP 合規：維護公眾用藥安全
  • 維持藥品供應：避免因強制行動造成藥品短缺，尤其是無替代品的必要用藥

這不意味著違規會被放過，而是 CDER 會協調行動的範圍與時機，讓廠商有機會在持續供應下進行改正。

💡 ORA vs. CDER 的分工

簡單類比：ORA 是警察（執行查廠、收集證據）；CDER 是檢察官+法官（評估證據、決定行動）。兩者密切合作，但職責分工明確。最終法規行動的決定通常需要 CDER（特別是 OPQ/OS）的參與。

Attachment A: Inspection Questions by Topic ── 查廠問題清單（依主題）

Attachment A Overview

Attachment A provides structured inspection question sets organized by 8 major topics. These questions serve as a guide for investigators during on-site inspections of sterile drug manufacturing facilities. They are not exhaustive but represent key areas of inquiry.

Topic 1: Moist Heat Sterilization

  • Sterilizer validation (heat distribution, heat penetration studies)
  • F₀ calculation methodology and target values
  • Biological indicator (BI) placement and challenge
  • Load configuration control (maximum and minimum load validated)
  • Cycle monitoring (temperature, pressure, time recording)
  • Preventive maintenance and calibration of sterilizer
  • Steam quality (superheat, non-condensable gases, Bowie-Dick test for porous loads)

Topic 2: Dry Heat Sterilization / Depyrogenation

  • Oven validation (heat distribution, penetration, uniformity)
  • Depyrogenation validation: ≥3-log reduction of endotoxin using Geobacillus stearothermophilus or equivalent endotoxin challenge
  • Temperature mapping: all positions in oven at minimum and maximum load
  • Cycle parameters: typical 250°C / ≥30 min for depyrogenation
  • Belt conveyor speed validation for tunnel depyrogenation ovens

Topic 3: Aseptic Filling

  • HVAC system qualification (air flow patterns, smoke studies, pressure differentials)
  • Personnel gowning qualification and re-qualification
  • Aseptic technique during interventions
  • Media fill program: frequency, design, number of units, incubation, positives
  • Environmental monitoring during filling: settle plates, active air, personnel
  • Line clearance procedures

Topic 4: Lyophilization

  • Lyophilizer qualification and sterilization-in-place (SIP) validation
  • Cycle development: primary drying, secondary drying, collapse temperature
  • Chamber load configuration consistency
  • Stopper seating/crimping during and after lyophilization
  • Residual moisture testing (Karl Fischer or loss on drying)
  • Headspace gas analysis for oxygen-sensitive products

Topic 5: Isolator Barrier Technology

  • Isolator design: unidirectional airflow in Grade A zone, pressure differentials
  • Decontamination cycle validation: ≥6-log reduction of resistant spore BIs (Geobacillus stearothermophilus or equivalent)
  • Cycle development: VHP/H₂O₂ concentration, contact time, aeration
  • Glove integrity testing: routine leak testing of gloves and half-suits
  • Transfer port control: RTP (Rapid Transfer Port) design and use
  • EM requirements: reduced but still required; focus on isolator exit points

Topic 6: Environmental Monitoring — Non-viable (Particles)

  • Particle counter placement and qualification
  • Alert and action levels per ISO grade (ISO 5/7/8 equivalents)
  • At-rest vs. in-operation classification
  • Frequency and locations of non-viable particle monitoring
  • Actions taken when limits are exceeded

Topic 7: Environmental Monitoring — Viable (Microbiology)

  • Viable EM program design: sampling locations, frequencies, methods
  • All shifts monitored: day, evening, weekend
  • Alert and action levels: separate for each ISO grade and monitoring method
  • Personnel monitoring: gloves (every session), gown (periodic)
  • Microorganism identification: all critical area isolates and exceedances
  • Trending and investigation of adverse trends
  • Disinfectant efficacy validation and rotation program

Topic 8: Biological Indicators

  • BI specifications: species, population, D-value, Z-value
  • BI storage conditions and shelf life management
  • D-value verification: incoming testing and periodic re-testing
  • Positive control use in sterilization validation runs
  • Incubation conditions for BI recovery: temperature, duration, reading
  • Contamination prevention when handling positive BIs

📝 Attachment A 八大主題的查廠要點

🔥 主題1：濕熱滅菌（Moist Heat Sterilization）
  • F₀ 計算方法與目標值是否有驗證基礎
  • BI 放置位置是否代表最冷點（cold spot）
  • 最大與最小裝載量均需驗證
  • 蒸汽品質：過熱蒸汽、不凝性氣體的控制
🌡️ 主題2：乾熱滅菌/去熱原（Dry Heat / Depyrogenation）
  • 關鍵驗證指標：≥3-log 內毒素減少（通常 250°C / ≥30 分鐘）
  • 溫度映射：所有位置均需達標
  • 隧道型去熱原爐：傳送帶速度需驗證
💉 主題3：無菌灌裝（Aseptic Filling）
  • HVAC 確認（氣流型態、煙霧測試、壓差）
  • Media Fill：頻率（每半年/每班次）、設計、陽性單位處理
  • 人員無菌技術：干預操作最關鍵
❄️ 主題4：凍乾（Lyophilization）
  • 凍乾機 SIP（就地滅菌）驗證
  • 凍乾循環開發：一次乾燥、二次乾燥、崩解溫度
  • 殘留水分測試（Karl Fischer）
  • 塞子就位與軋蓋完整性確認
🛡️ 主題5：隔離艙技術（Isolator）
  • 去汙驗證：≥6-log BI 減少（G. stearothermophilus 或同等）
  • VHP 濃度、接觸時間、通風（aeration）
  • 手套完整性：日常洩漏測試（routine leak test）
  • 快速轉移口（RTP）使用管制
🌫️ 主題6：非活性粒子環境監測
  • 粒子計數器擺放位置與確認
  • Alert/Action Level 依 ISO 等級分別定義
  • 靜態（at-rest）vs 動態（in-operation）分類結果
🦠 主題7：活性微生物環境監測與人員監測
  • 全班次監測（含假日班）
  • 各等級各方法分別設定 Alert/Action Level
  • 關鍵區域所有菌株需鑑定至菌種
  • 趨勢分析：未超標但趨勢惡化 = 需調查
  • 消毒劑輪換計劃與效力驗證
🧫 主題8：生物指示劑（Biological Indicators）
  • BI 規格：菌種、菌數、D 值、Z 值
  • D 值驗證：到貨時及定期複驗
  • 陽性對照（positive control）必須包含在每次驗證運行中
  • 孵育條件：溫度、時間、判讀標準

📊 關鍵驗證數值速查
