5.0 Glass Vial Handling Process — 玻璃瓶處理製程概覽

5.0 Glass Vial Handling Process

The glass-handling process begins at the glass vial manufacturer and continues through the final packaging at the pharmaceutical company and shipment to their customer. Numerous transfers take place within that process. Considerations for glass handling must be taken into account for the transfers between process steps.

Some points to consider in transfers to and from the warehouse include ensuring the hand truck or lift wheels are in good condition, the operators are properly trained, the facility floor is in good condition with no potholes or deep cracks, and the floor of the transition from room to room is level.

Once vials are in the filling process, many types of transfers occur such as tray loading (flipping) or unloading onto the washer loader. Ensuring the proper fit for off-loading trays and the proper alignment of the transfer trays will mitigate the risk of damage to the vials. During transfer, "pushing" on glassware during the transfer can create excessive back pressure, which should be avoided. Any damaged vials should be discarded promptly throughout the process.

Caution should be taken to ensure suitable materials of construction are used for transfers on the filling line for belts, guide rails, dead plates, screw feeds, and star wheels.

5.0 解說：製程概覽與轉移風險

核心概念：「搬運即風險」

玻璃瓶從玻璃製造商出廠，到製藥公司完成二次包裝出貨，整條鏈上每一次轉移（transfer）都是潛在損傷點。TR87 的核心訊息是：損傷往往不在充填當下，而是在上游某個不起眼的搬運環節累積的。

類比：像在搬一箱雞蛋

你不會把雞蛋放在顛簸不平的手推車上快速推行，也不會讓雞蛋彼此用力擠壓。玻璃瓶的邏輯完全一樣——地板、輪子、作業員訓練，全都是風險因子。一顆有微裂紋的瓶，在充填加壓或凍乾熱脹冷縮時就是未來爆瓶的定時炸彈。

關鍵設備術語（首次出現）

  • Back pressure（背壓）：推壓瓶群時，後方的瓶對前方瓶造成的壓力，過大易造成玻璃應力集中。
  • Dead plate（靜止轉移板）：兩段輸送帶之間的靜止承接平板，是玻璃瓶轉移的常見磨損點。
  • Star wheel（星輪）：間歇轉動的撥瓶輪，用於定位與間距控制，也是衝擊風險最高的元件之一。
  • Screw feed（螺旋進料）：利用螺旋槽定間距的進料裝置，間距不當易夾傷瓶身。

5.1 Packaging for Transport and Storage — 運輸與儲存包裝

5.1 Packaging for Transport and Storage

The glass-handling process begins at the glass vial manufacturer, where the pack-out configuration must be specified to minimize the risk of damage or breakage during transit or shipment from a supplier to a pharmaceutical firm. These configurations should be standardized for each container type and size: shrink-wrapped module type with glass-to-glass contact or a tray with or without cell partitions or separators. The number of containers that are held in a module or tray and their arrangement is typically driven by the infeed of the pharmaceutical processing equipment. The packaging format is also sized to maximize pallet space. Where modules are used, ensure proper packing and shrink-wrapping of the module (e.g., vial alignment, tightness of shrink-wrap) and avoid flexing and module-to-module impact during handling which may cause glass-to-glass abrasive damage.

The remaining aspect of the pack-out configuration should include a review of such factors as: the set-back of the load from the edge of the pallet; module-stacking configuration; need for corner protection and straps; need for slip sheets between layers; method of securing the load to the pallet; and total height of the pallet. Double-stacking of pallets, especially during transit, should be avoided.

5.1 解說：包裝設計的學問

Pack-out Configuration（包材配置）的兩種主要形式

  • Shrink-wrapped module（熱縮膜模組）：瓶與瓶緊密接觸，依靠收縮薄膜固定整體；優點是空間利用率高，缺點是一旦熱縮膜鬆弛，玻璃間摩擦立即加劇。
  • Tray with/without cell partitions（托盤式，含或不含隔間）：有隔間的設計能避免玻璃直接接觸；選擇取決於瓶型尺寸與設備進料格式是否匹配。

類比：就像裝運精密儀器

航運高級相機鏡頭時，廠商會指定發泡材質、固定位置、棧板高度。玻璃瓶雖然是消耗品，但一批幾十萬支瓶若在海運途中因棧板雙層堆疊而壓碎，損失的不只是玻璃，而是整批製程可信度。

棧板設計要點

  • 貨物不可超出棧板邊緣（set-back from edge）
  • 層間需有 slip sheet（防滑墊片）
  • 需有角落保護條（corner protection）與束帶（straps）
  • 棧板總高度需符合倉儲與運輸限制
  • 嚴禁雙層棧板堆疊（double-stacking），特別在運輸中

實務操作：QA 稽查 Checklist

向玻璃供應商索取時，應確認以下文件：

  • Pack-out specification（包材規格書），載明模組/托盤尺寸與排列方式
  • 運輸測試報告（vibration / drop test），驗證包材足以承受實際運輸應力
  • 棧板配置圖（pallet layout drawing），確認與倉儲設備兼容
5.2 Integrated Line with Multiple Transfer Points — 整合產線與多重轉移點

5.2 Integrated Line with Multiple Transfer Points

To ensure the pharmaceutical processing equipment is designed appropriately, collaboration with machine manufacturers is recommended. Where equipment from different machine manufacturers is used, ensure that each machine is properly integrated into the line. An appropriate risk assessment should be conducted during the machine design and line integration. Typical transfer points at the pharmaceutical company that should be evaluated include belt transfers, guide rails, dead plates, screw feeds, star wheels, and pick-and-place devices.

Vials can be automatically or manually pushed from a tray onto an accumulation table, where they are transferred to an infeed screw. To ensure the proper feeding of vials into the screw, proper spacing between vials, which is determined by belt speed before the screw, is important. The number of vials that back up prior to the screw should be kept to a minimum to reduce glass-to-glass contact between vials that could potentially cause damage.

Machine designers endeavor to remove potential negative impacts to the vial; however, not all risks can be removed. With the transport of vials, abrasion to the body is always a risk; therefore, proper materials should be used to reduce friction on the bottom, heel, and body of the vial. The greatest risk in vial damage comes during the transition from infeed screw to star wheel or from star wheel to star wheel (or inspection turret). Proper timing of the star wheels is critical, as is precision machining of size-change parts and low-backlash gearing or clutches. If the vial is not handled smoothly, with care taken to reduce impact forces, the vial body may experience excessive stress, which can cause breakage, cracks, or other damage.

A risk assessment should be conducted to properly design the process and materials for collecting downed vials that are ejected from the line if they may be reintroduced into the line. The assessment should determine the appropriate drop height, the amount of cushion or padding required, the frequency of emptying the eject bin, and the level of inspection required prior to reintroducing the vials to the line. The edges of the eject bin should be rounded to avoid sharp edges that could damage the vials.

5.2 解說：整合產線的風險管理

多供應商整合的挑戰

現實中，一條充填線的洗瓶機、去熱原隧道、充填機、凍乾機可能來自 3–4 家不同設備商。每個介面的：速度配合、高度差、材質相容性，都需要在設備資格確認（IQ/OQ/PQ）前做好風險評估。TR87 明確要求要在機器設計與產線整合期間就執行 risk assessment，而非等到 validation 才發現問題。

最高風險轉移點：Screw → Star Wheel

螺旋進料（infeed screw）將瓶子定間距送入，星輪（star wheel）則以間歇動作接收。兩者的時序（timing）若有誤差，瓶子在交接點被「夾到」，產生的衝擊力遠超過玻璃的容許應力。這是產線上碎瓶事故的首要來源。

修正方向：精密加工的尺寸更換零件（size-change parts）＋低背隙齒輪或離合器（low-backlash gearing/clutches）＋定期時序校正。

類比：高速公路匝道合流

汽車從匝道合流主幹道，若時機不對就會側撞。星輪接瓶的原理相同——速度差、角度差、時間差，三個變數同時出錯就碎瓶。

Eject Bin（退瓶箱）重新投入的風險評估要素

風險評估應涵蓋：
1. 落下高度（drop height）
2. 緩衝墊規格（cushion/padding）
3. 清空頻率（frequency of emptying）
4. 重新投入前的目視檢驗等級（inspection level）
5. 退瓶箱邊緣設計（rounded edges，避免銳邊二次割傷）
5.2.1 Accumulators — 積瓶轉台

5.2.1 Accumulators

Accumulators are used between segments of a filling line where the throughput speeds of the specific units of equipment may vary or be intermittent, or where one unit may be more likely to experience periods of down time (e.g., after depyrogenation and before filling, or after stoppering but before capping). Accumulators are also used for loading and off-loading trays at the beginning, during transfers, or at the end of line.

Accumulators may be of several types, including rotary tables, push (back pressure), and bi-flow.

5.2.1.1 Key Parts of the Process and Potential Impact on the Containers

The accumulation of vials often leads to abrasion damage to the glass surface. This happens especially where large numbers of vials are tightly gathered and the vials continue to revolve and rotate against one another, or where one or more vials become trapped in an eddy of the flow and remain for a long period of time. This type of damage is typically evidenced by circumferential scuff or scratch rings. These rings are usually observed at contact areas just below the shoulder and above the heel of the vial. While every surface discontinuity affects glass strength, the significance of these, relative to the potential for container failure, should be evaluated through the process described in Section 5.4.

The type of damage may occur wherever rotary tables are used, but it is most prevalent when these tables are used immediately after depyrogenation. Depyrogenation dehydrates the glass surface, eliminating its natural lubricity and exacerbating friction damage. In addition to damaging the glass surface, visible and subvisible glass particulates may be generated in the process.

5.2.1.2 Points to Consider

As good general practice, the table should be properly sized for the planned line speeds and large enough so that it is not loaded to more than one-third of its capacity. Additionally, directional flow control devices should be engineered to prevent eddies (opposite circular flows) in the flow of vials.

Guides and rails should be constructed of suitable, nonabrasive plastic materials. Transfers onto the table should prevent any impact, either glass-to-glass or glass-to-metal, especially at the heel of the vial. To facilitate this, the turntable itself should be slightly lower than the dead plate or belt from which the vials are being received. This difference, however, must be small enough that it does not cause vials to tip over while still allowing for a smooth transfer.

Monitoring and controlling the vial accumulators can mitigate the risk of damage to the glass surface, and limiting the number of vials allowed to accumulate on the table to a practical minimum is recommended.

Throughput speeds should be as consistent as possible for each segment of the line, reducing the need for accumulation. When this is not possible, the line should be regulated to minimize accumulation. Accordingly, the movement of vials on the table should also stop if the line itself is stopped. This will preclude excessive accumulation of vials onto the table.

If turntables or accumulation tables must be used, they should be designed to minimize the momentum of the containers (e.g., low speed settings) in order to reduce the level of forces between containers, thus reducing the occurrence of abrasions in the glass and generation of the glass particles.

5.2.1 解說：積瓶轉台的損傷機制

為什麼需要 Accumulator？

充填線各段設備速度不一致（如去熱原隧道連續出瓶，但充填機可能因更換規格或故障而短暫停機），accumulator 在中間起緩衝作用。常見類型：

  • Rotary table（旋轉轉台）：最常見，瓶子在旋轉台上做圓周運動排隊
  • Push / back pressure（推壓式）：靠後方持續推力保持前進，易產生過大背壓
  • Bi-flow（雙向流）：可雙向輸送，靈活度高

最常被忽視的損傷：Circumferential Scuff Rings（周向磨損環）

旋轉台上，瓶子與瓶子持續對轉，如果某支瓶被困在迴流（eddy）中無法前進，就會在同一個高度被反覆磨擦，形成環狀磨痕。

位置：肩部下方（below shoulder）與瓶踵上方（above heel）——恰好是玻璃應力最集中的兩個幾何突變區。

後果：雖然外觀看起來「不嚴重」，但這些微裂紋在凍乾或高壓滅菌的熱應力下可能成為裂紋起點，造成 container failure。

類比：砂紙打磨原理

兩支玻璃瓶在旋轉台上相互研磨，與用砂紙打磨是同一個物理原理。去熱原後的玻璃更像「乾燥的砂岩」——失去水膜潤滑，COF 升高，同樣的力道造成的磨損深度倍增。

旋轉台設計的關鍵規則

最佳實務規則：
  裝載量 ≤ 轉台容量 × 1/3

轉台高度：
  轉台面 < 送入 dead plate 高度
  高度差需小到不讓瓶子傾倒

線速管控：
  各段速度應盡量一致 → 減少積瓶需求
  線體停止 → 轉台也應立即停止
  轉台速度設定 → 盡量低，以減少容器動量

實務：旋轉台操作 Checklist

  • 確認導軌材質為非磨蝕性塑膠（非金屬）
  • 確認轉台有流向控制裝置（防止 eddy 形成）
  • 設定最大積瓶量警報，不超過容量 1/3
  • 線體停止時，確認轉台連鎖停止（interlock）有效
  • 去熱原後的第一個旋轉台須特別監控，此時玻璃 COF 最高
5.3 Vial Washing — 洗瓶

5.3 Vial Washing

In parenteral packaging, glass vials are typically washed prior to being sterilized for filling. Vial washing is conducted in order to ensure removal of such materials as bacterial toxins, chemical substances, and particulates so that the product meets expectations of identity, purity, quality, and safety.

5.3.1 Washing Machines

Washing machines are available in various designs, the most common being linear tunnel and rotary. Vials are washed with either a diving spray nozzle or a stationary, nonpenetrating spray nozzle. When using diving spray nozzles, where a needle enters the vial and sprays the vial from the bottom up, there is a risk of needle strikes on the bottom of the vial, the inside wall, or the finish of the vial that could result in a bent needle, glass damage, and particulate generation (glass chips). The risk of vial strikes increases when multiple vials must be aligned with an array of needles for simultaneous washing. The process parameters, including needle depth and centering, must be adapted for varying vial sizes or designs. Needles should be routinely inspected for straightness to prevent needle strikes. Some newer washing machines can be equipped with needle strike alarms to aid in risk management.

For washers that use buckets to hold the vials, inverted or double-stacked vials (two vials within the same bucket) are vulnerable to breakage due to needle strikes. Automated systems that detect and eject upside-down vials or detect double-stacked vials can be incorporated to mitigate this risk. Manual intervention procedures to address vial orientation issues may be needed for some washing systems.

For rotary washers, water jet pressure should be moderated to ensure vials are not forcibly ejected from the grippers by an excessive jet stream.

In limited applications, batch washers are used to wash vials one tray or module at a time. Loading plates and corresponding washing needles must be matched to the respective tray configuration and vial size to avoid glass damage or breakage during loading. A visual check and preventive maintenance plan should be in place to identify bent or damaged needles. Care should be taken during loading and unloading to prevent glass damage or breakage.

5.3 解說：洗瓶機的損傷風險

洗瓶的目的

注射劑包材的洗瓶目標是去除：細菌毒素（bacterial toxins）、化學污染物（chemical substances）、微粒（particulates）。這直接關係到最終藥品的安全性與 FDA/EMA 的合規要求。

Needle Strike（針頭撞擊）——洗瓶最大風險

成因：使用 diving spray nozzle 時，針頭必須準確插入瓶口。若對位不良（misalignment），針頭會撞到瓶底、內壁或瓶口（finish），導致：

  • 針頭彎曲 → 後續批次持續 needle strike 直到被發現
  • 玻璃碎片（glass chips）產生 → 不可見微粒污染
  • 瓶口損傷 → 封塞密封性下降

預防：定期目視或自動檢查針頭直度；換規格時重新確認針頭深度與對位；新款洗瓶機可配置 needle strike alarm。

倒置瓶與雙層瓶（Inverted / Double-stacked Vials）

在 bucket 式洗瓶機中，若有倒置或兩支瓶卡在同一 bucket，針頭撞擊的風險劇增，且因倒置瓶阻擋噴水，清洗效果也大打折扣。自動偵測剔除（automated detection and eject）系統是最佳解，無此功能的設備需建立手動程序。

類比：注射針對準血管

醫護人員打針時若針頭未對準，會造成血管壁損傷。洗瓶機的洗針（washing needle）也是同樣道理——對位精度決定了是「乾淨清洗」還是「製造新的微粒污染」。

實務：洗瓶機 PM 要點

  • 換規格前：確認 needle depth、centering 設定值已調整
  • 定期：目視 100% 針頭直度檢查（建議每批次換規格後）
  • 旋轉式洗瓶機：確認水壓設定不超出 gripper 夾持力
  • 批次洗瓶機：loading plate 與托盤規格需一一對應，建立文件管控
5.4 Dry Heat Sterilization and Depyrogenation — 乾熱滅菌與去熱原

5.4 Dry Heat Sterilization and Depyrogenation

Dry heat sterilization and depyrogenation of the vials occurs immediately after washing and is usually accomplished using dry heat. Dry heat ovens may take the form of tunnels or batch ovens. The ovens often serve as the transfer portals from the preparation area into the aseptic area. Vials are usually sterilized at ≥ 160 °C for up to two hours and depyrogenated at approximately 325 °C for at least thirty minutes, but the times and temperatures can vary depending on the specific process requirements.

These two processes may increase the coefficient of friction (COF) of the glass surface and make the vials more susceptible to abrasion. This is of particular concern if the vials will be subjected to glass-to-glass or glass-to-metal contact, such as on a rotary accumulation table. This type of friction damage is typically evidenced by scuff rings or scratches on the vials. This damage is usually observed just below the shoulder and above the heel of the vials. Though small and relatively minor in appearance, scratches and scuffs can potentially contribute to breakage under sufficient mechanical or thermal stresses further downstream. In addition to damaging the glass surface, visible and subvisible glass particulates may also be generated in the process. The longer the time the vials are subjected to high temperatures, the more profound the increase of the COF. In a case where vials are in contact with one another, one vial may bond with the surface of another vial, causing them to stick together. When these vials come apart, distinct vertical marks (stuck marks) will be apparent. These marks may act as stress concentrators and fracture origins as the vials are subjected to subsequent loads during downstream processing or handling.

5.4.1 Continuous Convection Tunnel

After the air-drying process, the glass vials (either in single file or in rows) pass onto a conveyor belt over a dead plate. Back pressure from the continuous loading of the belt pushes the material onto the conveyor.

Ideally, the conveyer belt should be operated in a "pull mode" and run at a slightly higher speed than the feed rate from the washer in order to maintain a small space (e.g., ~1 mm) between the rows of vials. This transition is sometimes supported by a pushing device to complete the transfer from the washer to depyrogenation tunnel.

Maintaining a minimal downward transition of the vials is recommended to reduce the probability of damaging the heel region.

Vials are then moved through the convection tunnel on the conveyor at a predetermined rate. Tunnels typically include three heat zones: a preheat zone, a hot zone (where the glassware is exposed to sufficient temperature for sufficient time to effect sterilization/depyrogenation), and a cooling zone. Depyrogenation guidelines are presented in PDA Technical Report No. 3.

The vials are then pushed out of the tunnel, using slight back pressure, across a dead plate, and onto the next stage of the process by an accumulator.

5.4.2 Points to Consider

As best practice, containers should be dry prior to exposure to depyrogenation temperatures (Zone 2), as residual moisture may attack the interior surface of the glass causing etching or glass corrosion.

In the event downstream processes come to a stop, it should also halt additional vials from entering the tunnel to prevent overloading, while allowing the in-process batch to complete the validated depyrogenation cycle.

Viewing windows are recommended so the entire depyrogenation area can be observed for evidence of overcrowding, fallen or broken containers.

Although the glass structure is not impacted directly by the temperatures used for depyrogenation, leaving the containers in the tunnel beyond the validated depyrogenation process time is not recommended.

The temperatures achieved during depyrogenation remove the protective water skin from the containers, leaving them more vulnerable to surface damage, such as scratches.

5.4.3 Batch Depyrogenation Oven

Another method for dry heat sterilization and depyrogenation is the use of a depyrogenation oven, which allows for processing of smaller rather than continuous processing. Trays of vials are loaded into the oven on racks and depyrogenated according to a validated cycle. The trays are typically unloaded under a Grade A (ISO 5) unidirectional airflow and then transported to the vial-filling line. Due to the absence of the protective water skin, consideration should be given during loading, unloading, and transport to limit movement of the glass vials within the stainless-steel trays to prevent damage or breakage.

5.4 解說：去熱原後玻璃的脆弱性

乾熱滅菌 vs. 去熱原：溫度與時間比較

乾熱滅菌（Dry Heat Sterilization）：
  溫度：≥ 160 °C
  時間：最長 2 小時
  目的：殺滅微生物（達 SAL 10⁻⁶）

去熱原（Depyrogenation）：
  溫度：約 325 °C
  時間：至少 30 分鐘
  目的：使熱原（pyrogens/endotoxins）降解
  法規依據：PDA Technical Report No. 3

關鍵記憶：
  去熱原溫度 >> 滅菌溫度
  → 對玻璃表面的影響更大

COF（摩擦係數）升高的後果

去熱原過程中，玻璃表面的「保護水膜（water skin）」被蒸發殆盡。這層水膜是天然潤滑劑，失去它之後：

  • 玻璃表面 COF 顯著上升
  • 任何玻璃對玻璃、玻璃對金屬的接觸，磨損效果倍增
  • 高溫時間越長，COF 升高幅度越大

Stuck marks（黏結垂直痕）：高溫下兩支接觸的玻璃瓶可能發生表面黏合（bonding），分開時留下垂直條痕，成為下游破瓶的應力集中點（stress concentrator）。

類比：剛出爐的陶瓷

陶瓷器從窯中取出時表面乾燥、脆性最高，此時若相互碰撞，比常溫下更容易崩缺。去熱原後的玻璃瓶類似這個狀態——它在結構上完整，但表面保護層已不在，任何機械摩擦都留下傷痕。

連續隧道式的三段溫區

  • 預熱區（Preheat zone）：避免玻璃因急遽升溫產生熱應力
  • 熱區（Hot zone）：達成滅菌/去熱原的有效暴露時間與溫度
  • 冷卻區（Cooling zone）：緩慢降溫，避免玻璃因溫差破裂，並確保進入無菌區前已降至安全溫度

「Pull mode」輸送帶設計：帶速略快於洗瓶機出瓶速，使瓶間保留 ~1 mm 間隙，避免背壓堆積。

5.4.2 操作注意事項彙整

  • 進隧道前瓶子必須完全乾燥——殘留水分在高溫下侵蝕玻璃內表面（etching/corrosion）
  • 下游停線時必須連鎖停止進料（interlock），但隧道內已進入的瓶應繼續完成 validated cycle
  • 設置觀察窗（viewing windows）監控隧道內積瓶、倒瓶狀況
  • 不建議超出已驗證的去熱原時間，雖然玻璃結構不直接受影響，但水膜持續缺失時間越長越不利

批次式去熱原烤箱（5.4.3）

適用於小批量或研發階段。關鍵點：

  • 出烤箱後必須在 Grade A（ISO 5） 單向流下卸載，維持無菌條件
  • 不鏽鋼托盤中的玻璃瓶無水膜保護，搬運時需盡量減少晃動以防磨損或破裂
  • 批次式的風險：相較連續隧道，出爐後需要更多人工轉移操作，暴露在非受控環境的機率更高

實務：去熱原後的特別管控 SOP 建議

  • 去熱原後第一個旋轉台：轉速降至最低（low speed setting）
  • 所有導軌材質確認為非磨蝕塑膠，禁用金屬導軌
  • 隧道出口至充填機之間的 dead plate 高度差不超過 1 mm
  • 批次烤箱出爐：由受訓人員在 Grade A 罩下操作，避免敲碰托盤
5.5 Vial Filling — 充填

5.5 Vial Filling

Drug product is added to vials at a filling station. Vials are transferred to the filling station after washing and depyrogenation by a conveyor belt, with a star wheel or by a gripper that picks up the vial by the neck. Vials are filled with a filling needle by either a fixed nozzle or a diving nozzle apparatus. Once filled, the vials move along the line to the stoppering station where they are stoppered either by direct placement (where the stopper is picked up by vacuum) or by a rotating wheel.

5.5.1 Impacts

Vials transported by means of a conveyor belt experience glass-to-glass contact as they move along the line, which can result in abrasions to the glass. Vials that are transported by a star wheel or by gripper have glass-to-glass contact only at the vial gate, where they accumulate, and where damage can occur. For filling lines that use grippers (i.e., pick and place) to move vials, the gripper configuration and the amount of pressure exerted on the vial are critical and should be compatible with the vial neck size. Ideally, grippers should be constructed of plastic rather than metal.

Needle installation and alignment should be verified at set-up. A needle strike may occur when a filling needle is misaligned with the vial being filled; the needle strikes the top of the vial and can chip the vial and/or bend the needle. Needles can also become bent when striking vials that have offset finishes. Damage in the lip region of the vial can go unnoticed through inspection as the stopper and seal can mask the damage. Regular inspection during the production batch should assess misalignment of and damage to filling needles.

During weight checks, vials can be damaged or broken when transferred from the filling line to the weigh station. Vacuum-assisted vial grippers can reduce the incidence of damage but can also be impacted by particulate matter if the vials are not adequately clean.

5.5.2 Points to Consider

The filling process can be improved to further reduce damage to the vials by replacing the metal with plastic throughout the line. Damage to the vial due to needle strikes can be reduced by changing the stainless-steel filling needles to disposable plastic. In contrast to the impact from a metal needle, a plastic filling needle would fail or bend before the vial would break.

5.5 解說：充填站的損傷模式

三種充填前傳瓶方式的比較

傳瓶方式玻璃接觸風險主要關注點
輸送帶（conveyor belt）高（全程玻璃對玻璃）速度、背壓、導軌材質
星輪（star wheel）低（僅在 vial gate 積瓶時）時序精度、star wheel 材質
夾爪（gripper）低（個別搬運）夾力與頸部尺寸相容性、材質（塑膠優先）

充填針頭撞擊（Needle Strike）——充填站版本

與洗瓶機的 needle strike 類似，但充填站的版本有一個特殊危險：瓶口（lip region）的損傷被封塞和鋁蓋遮蓋，目視檢查幾乎不可能發現。

這代表一個已有瓶口微裂紋的藥瓶，可能順利通過出廠 AQL，直到消費者開蓋時才破裂——安全風險極高。

特別風險：Offset finish（偏心瓶口）——瓶口幾何偏差的瓶子，更容易導致充填針偏斜。

類比：塑膠針頭的「犧牲設計」

一次性塑膠充填針（disposable plastic needle）的原理是工程設計的「安全保險絲」概念：寧可針頭自己彎折（便於偵測），也不要玻璃瓶受到損傷。金屬針頭則剛好相反——金屬夠硬，撞到瓶時是玻璃先碎。

稱重取樣時的損傷風險

充填後定期取樣稱重（IPC weight check）時，若搬運方式不當（如直接夾瓶身金屬夾），很容易在此步驟造成損傷。建議：

  • 使用 vacuum-assisted gripper 真空吸附取瓶
  • 確保瓶子已清潔，避免微粒堵塞真空吸附孔
  • 建立標準化稱重取樣 SOP，納入人員培訓

實務：充填線材質替換建議

  • 導軌全線改用非磨蝕性塑膠（替換金屬件）
  • gripper 改為塑膠材質，確認與瓶頸尺寸相容
  • 評估採用一次性塑膠充填針（減少 needle strike 破玻璃的風險）
  • 每班次定期目視檢查充填針對位與直度（建議記錄於批次紀錄）
5.6 Stopper Placement — 封塞

5.6 Stopper Placement

At the stoppering station, the stoppers can be placed on the vials either directly (pick-and-place) or by a rotating wheel. The risk of incurring damage during the stoppering process is very low as there is no glass-to-glass contact during stoppering. Any breakage observed during stoppering is most likely due to damage that occurred upstream.

5.6 解說：封塞的品質意義

封塞站：損傷的「揭露點」而非「製造點」

TR87 的重要結論：封塞過程本身無玻璃對玻璃接觸，玻璃損傷風險極低。但若在此站出現破瓶，幾乎可確定是上游某個步驟已累積損傷（最可能是 star wheel 交接點、旋轉台磨損、或充填針撞擊）。

因此，若在封塞站觀察到破瓶或碎玻璃：

  • 應立即啟動root cause investigation，往上游溯源
  • 評估同批次已封塞的瓶子是否有潛在隱性損傷
  • 重新審視去熱原後的旋轉台操作條件

類比：最後一根稻草

一塊石板若有裂縫，輕輕一壓就斷了——但是你不會認為「是那個輕輕一壓的力量太大」，而是要追問「裂縫從何而來」。封塞站破瓶的邏輯相同，不要只修封塞機，要找那個讓玻璃先有裂縫的上游步驟。

兩種封塞方式

  • Pick-and-place（直接放置）：真空吸附塞子後直接放置，對準精度要求高，但封塞力可精確控制
  • Rotating wheel（旋轉輪）：塞子在旋轉盤上被依序推入瓶口，速度快，適合高產能產線

兩種方式均不涉及玻璃接觸，因此封塞站的重點管控是塞子的完整性與定位精度，而非玻璃損傷。

Section 5 (Part A) 章節總結 — 玻璃瓶處理製程（5.0–5.6）

  • 整體框架：玻璃瓶的損傷是一個累積過程，從供應商包材配置開始，每個轉移步驟都可能留下微損傷，後果在下游才顯現（尤其在凍乾或二次包裝）。
  • 包裝與運輸（5.1）：Pack-out configuration 必須標準化；禁止雙層棧板堆疊；shrink-wrap 模組需保持緊實，避免模組間碰撞造成玻璃對玻璃磨損。
  • 整合產線（5.2）：最高風險點在 infeed screw → star wheel 的交接；旋轉積瓶台裝載量不超過 1/3；去熱原後的旋轉台是最易發生磨損的節點。
  • 洗瓶（5.3）：Needle strike 是首要風險，需定期檢查針頭直度；自動偵測倒置/雙層瓶可有效降低風險。
  • 去熱原（5.4）：325 °C 去除水膜後玻璃 COF 升高，磨損效應加劇；pull mode 輸送帶維持瓶間 ~1 mm 間距；批次烤箱出爐後在 Grade A 下操作並最小化晃動。
  • 充填（5.5）：充填針撞擊瓶口的損傷被封塞遮蓋，是最難被發現的損傷類型；建議以塑膠充填針替代不鏽鋼，以「針彎」代替「瓶碎」作為偵測機制。
  • 封塞（5.6）：封塞本身損傷風險最低；此站的破瓶幾乎必然是上游損傷的結果，應啟動溯源調查而非僅調整封塞機參數。
