Glass Containers — Definitions & Classification

Glass containers for pharmaceutical use are glass articles intended to come into direct contact with pharmaceutical preparations.

Colourless glass is highly transparent in the visible spectrum. Coloured glass is obtained by the addition of small amounts of metal oxides, chosen according to the desired spectral absorbance.

Neutral glass is a borosilicate glass containing significant amounts of boric oxide, aluminium oxide, alkali metal oxides and/or alkaline earth oxides in the glass network. Due to its composition, neutral glass has a high hydrolytic resistance and a high thermal shock resistance.

Soda-lime-silica glass is a silica glass containing alkali metal oxides, mainly sodium oxide, and alkaline earth oxides, mainly calcium oxide, in the glass network. Due to its composition, soda-lime-silica glass has only a moderate hydrolytic resistance.

The hydrolytic stability of glass containers is expressed by the resistance to the release of soluble mineral substances into water under the prescribed conditions of contact. The hydrolytic resistance is evaluated by titrating released alkali reacting ions. According to their hydrolytic resistance, glass containers are classified as follows:

  • Type I glass containers: neutral glass, with a high hydrolytic resistance due to the chemical composition of the glass itself;
  • Type II glass containers: usually of soda-lime-silica glass with a high hydrolytic resistance resulting from suitable treatment of the inner surface;
  • Type III glass containers: usually of soda-lime-silica glass with only moderate hydrolytic resistance.

Suitability for Pharmaceutical Preparations

Type I glass containers are suitable for most preparations whether or not for parenteral administration.

Type II glass containers are suitable for most acidic and neutral, aqueous preparations whether or not for parenteral administration.

Type III glass containers are in general suitable for non-aqueous preparations for parenteral administration, for powders for parenteral administration (except for freeze-dried preparations) and for preparations not for parenteral administration.

Glass containers with a hydrolytic resistance higher than that recommended above for a particular type of preparation may generally also be used.

The container chosen for a given preparation shall be such that the glass material does not release substances in quantities sufficient to affect the stability of the preparation or to present a risk of toxicity. In justified cases, further detailed information may be necessary to assess the impact on chronic use and for vulnerable patient groups.

Preparations for parenteral administration are normally presented in colourless glass, but coloured glass may be used for substances known to be light-sensitive. It is recommended that all glass containers for liquid preparations and for powders for parenteral administration permit the visual inspection of the contents.

The inner surface of glass containers may be specially treated to improve hydrolytic resistance, to confer water-repellancy, etc. Except for type I glass containers, glass containers for pharmaceutical preparations are not to be re-used. Containers for human blood and blood components must not be re-used.

Production — Delamination Risk

When glass containers for pharmaceutical use are manufactured under stressed conditions (e.g. temperature-time profile) and/or are placed in contact with particularly aggressive pharmaceutical preparations, they may undergo delamination, i.e. the separation of the inner glass surface into thin layers called lamellae or flakes.

Glass delamination may be the result of a chemical attack that occurs according to well-known glass corrosion mechanisms, such as dissolution by hydrolysis and ion exchange (leaching) as a function of the pH. The process of interaction between the glass surface and the pharmaceutical preparation requires incubation time, and flaking may only become visible a number of months after filling.

Several risk factors are known to increase the propensity of a glass to delaminate. The chemical composition of the pharmaceutical preparation, the presence of buffers like citrate or phosphate, which are known to corrode glass, and the ionic strength of the liquid medium may all strongly favour delamination. The manufacturing process of the container, chemical treatments of the inner surface, and terminal sterilisation and processing at the pharmaceutical filling lines are other important risk factors.

It is recommended that the user of the container assesses the compatibility of the glass container and the pharmaceutical preparation on a case-by-case basis, considering for example the dosage form, properties of the formulation and glass quality.

The propensity to delamination of glass containers from different sources can be assessed and ranked by exposing the container to accelerated degradation testing, carried out at specified temperatures for a short time and using the solutions associated with the actual pharmaceutical preparation as extractants. The presence of particles in the extraction solution, the occurrence of phase separation on the inner surface, and the steep increase of silica concentration in the extraction solution are all indicators of a potential propensity for delamination.

玻璃脫層：重要安全風險

脫層（Delamination）是玻璃內表面剝落成薄片（lamellae/flakes）的現象，是注射劑中可見異物和次可見微粒的重要來源之一。FDA於2011年曾發出多起因玻璃脫層造成的藥品召回（recall），引發業界高度重視。

脫層高風險因素
  • 緩衝劑：檸檬酸鹽（citrate）和磷酸鹽（phosphate）緩衝液對玻璃腐蝕性強，顯著增加脫層風險。
  • 高離子強度：高鹽製劑加速玻璃表面侵蝕。
  • 終端滅菌（Terminal sterilization）：高溫（121°C）蒸汽滅菌會促進玻璃-藥液反應，增加脫層風險。
  • 玻璃管製造工藝：玻璃管成型時的溫度-時間曲線影響內表面結構。
  • 延遲顯現：脫層通常在充填後數月才肉眼可見——IPC檢查時可能不被發現。
脫層傾向評估方法
  • 加速降解試驗（Accelerated degradation testing）：在規定溫度下短時間暴露，以實際製劑溶液為萃取液，檢測：粒子出現、內表面相分離、矽濃度急劇升高。
  • 正式相容性穩定性測試：加速試驗只作為篩選工具（預測性），最終需在正常條件下進行完整穩定性試驗確認玻璃與藥液的相容性。
CDMO應對措施
  • 新產品容器選型時進行玻璃相容性研究，特別是pH >7或含磷酸鹽/檸檬酸鹽緩衝液的製劑。
  • 終端滅菌產品：評估玻璃脫層風險，考慮優先選用Type I（耐性最高）。
  • 參考USP <1660>（Evaluation of the Inner Surface Durability of Glass Containers）和PDA TR87中的相容性評估框架。

Tests Overview & Hydrolytic Resistance

Glass containers for pharmaceutical use comply with the relevant test or tests for hydrolytic resistance. Tests for hydrolytic resistance are carried out to define the type of glass (I, II or III) and to control its hydrolytic resistance. In addition, containers for aqueous parenteral preparations are tested for arsenic release and coloured glass containers are tested for spectral transmission.

Hydrolytic Resistance — Table 3.2.1.-1. Types of Glass

Test C is performed if there are uncertainties whether the container is type I or type II.

Equipment (for Tests A, B, C)

  • An autoclave or steam steriliser capable of withstanding 2.5 × 10⁵ N/m² (≥0.25 MPa = 2.5 bar), with capability to maintain temperature at 121 ± 1 °C. Equipped with heating device, thermometer, pressure gauge, vent cock, and tray above water level.
  • Calibrated resistance thermometer or calibrated thermocouple with external measuring device.
  • Burettes, 1000 mL one-mark volumetric flasks, pipettes, beakers, conical flasks (100 mL and 250 mL), water-bath, metal foil (aluminium/stainless steel).

Flasks and beakers must already have been used for the test or have been filled with water R and kept in an autoclave at 121 °C for at least 1 h before being used.

Determination of the Filling Volume

The filling volume is the volume of water to be introduced into the container for the purpose of the test. For vials and bottles the filling volume is 90 per cent of the brimful capacity. For ampoules it is the volume up to the height of the shoulder.

Vials and Bottles

Select, at random, 6 containers (or 3 if their capacity exceeds 100 mL). Weigh empty containers (±0.1 g). Fill with water R to the brimful line. Weigh filled containers. Calculate mean brimful capacity and multiply by 0.9. This volume (to 1 decimal place) is the filling volume.

Precision: expressed to 2 decimal places for containers ≤ 30 mL nominal; to 1 decimal place for containers > 30 mL nominal.

Ampoules

Place at least 6 dry ampoules on a flat surface and fill with water R from a burette until the water reaches point A, where the body declines to the shoulder. Read capacities (to 2 decimal places) and calculate the mean. Expressed to 1 decimal place, this is the filling volume. May also be determined by weighing.

Syringes and Cartridges

Select 6 syringes or cartridges. Close the small opening (needle/Luer cone for syringes; mouth for cartridges) using an inert material (e.g. a tip cap). Determine mean brimful volume as per vials and bottles and multiply by 0.9.

充填容積（Filling Volume）測定

充填容積的定義和計算

充填容積是試驗中注入水的體積——代表「實際使用狀態下的液體量」。規定如下：

小瓶/瓶：Filling volume = 滿裝容積（brimful capacity） × 0.9
安瓿：Filling volume = 充填至「肩部起始點（point A）」的體積
注射器/卡匣：Filling volume = 滿裝容積 × 0.9

採用90%而非100%的原因：模擬實際使用時的充填狀態（通常不充填至溢出），同時確保蒸壓時液體不溢出。

充填容積的重要性

充填容積直接決定Test A的試驗液體積（Table 3.2.1-2，見Section 2）和所需的滴定次數。充填容積越大，接觸面積越大，萃取的鹼性離子越多，但稀釋體積也更大。正確測定充填容積是確保試驗結果可重現的前提。

安瓿的「肩部（Shoulder）」

安瓿的充填容積特別規定至「肩部」，即玻璃體開始收縮變細之處（Figure 3.2.1-1所示的point A）。這是因為安瓿的頸部較窄，充填至頸部會影響試驗操作；填至肩部是最能代表實際藥液充填量的位置。

6個容器取樣的統計意義

選取6個容器（大容量≥100 mL選3個），計算平均充填容積——這是為了考慮同一批次容器的容積均勻性。若單取1個容器可能因製造偏差而不具代表性，6個平均值能更準確反映批次實際充填容積。

Cleaning

Remove any debris or dust. Shortly before the test, fill each container to the brim with water R and allow to stand for 20 ± 5 min. Empty the containers, carefully rinse twice with water R and once with water R1 and allow to drain. Closed ampoules are not rinsed before testing.

Test A — Autoclaving Process

Reference Thermal Cycle

The autoclave is run in such a way that the temperature in the containers follows this thermal cycle:

  • Temperature raised from room temperature to 100 °C within 20–30 min;
  • Temperature maintained at 100 ± 1 °C for 10 ± 1 min;
  • Temperature raised from 100 °C to 121 °C within 20–22 min;
  • Temperature maintained at 121 ± 1 °C for 60 ± 1 min;
  • Temperature cooled to 100 °C within 40–44 min.

Autoclave Calibration

Before being used for the first time, the autoclave and the temperature measuring system are calibrated. NOTE: significant differences may be observed between the temperature measured in the autoclave chamber and inside the containers. The calibrated thermocouple is inserted into one of the containers through a hole in the closure.

For manually operated autoclaves, leave the vent cock open; heat at a regular rate so that steam issues vigorously from the vent cock after 20–30 min and maintain vigorous steam for a further 10 min. When cooling down, vent to prevent the formation of a vacuum. Do not open the autoclave before the water in the containers has reached 95 °C. Remove hot samples and cool cautiously to room temperature within 30 min.

Routine Autoclave Runs

Use autoclave settings established during calibration. Container sets of different capacity can be tested during the same run. Keep the glass load very similar to the calibration load. The calibrated thermocouple is no longer necessary once calibration is validated.

這個熱循環設計是為了確保容器內部達到121°C，而非只是高壓滅菌器腔室的溫度——容器和腔室之間可能有顯著溫差，因此必須在容器內部放置熱電偶進行校正。

10分鐘維持100°C的目的

蒸汽滅菌器的初始階段需要先排除腔室內的不凝性氣體（主要是空氣）。若空氣未排除，蒸汽無法直接接觸容器，導致容器內部溫度低於指定值。在100°C維持10分鐘的目的就是蒸汽置換（Steam purge）——徹底排除不凝性氣體後再加壓升溫。這也是為什麼手動高壓滅菌器要將排氣閥（vent cock）打開讓蒸汽持續排出20–30分鐘。

「容器內部」溫度校正的重要性

Ph.Eur.特別強調：高壓滅菌器腔室溫度讀數與容器內部水溫之間可能存在顯著差異。CDMO在建立此試驗方法時，必須先完成儀器校正——用熱電偶插入實際容器內部測量，確認腔室設定能使容器內部達到121 ± 1°C，然後記錄校正後的設定值作為常規使用參數。

試驗 vs 藥品滅菌：同一台設備，不同目的

Ph.Eur. 3.2.1的高壓滅菌是「化學萃取試驗」——目的是讓水與玻璃內表面充分反應，萃取鹼性離子；而藥品的終端滅菌（terminal sterilization）目的是殺滅微生物。兩者使用的溫度相同（121°C），但試驗不需要生物指示劑（BI），只需嚴格控制熱循環曲線確保萃取條件一致。

Test B — Hydrolytic Resistance of Glass Grains (Glass Grains Test)

Check that the articles as received have been annealed to a commercially acceptable quality. The test may be performed on the canes used for the manufacture of tubing glass containers or on the containers.

Equipment

  • Mortar, pestle and hammer (tempered magnetic steel) or a ball mill (agate/zirconia/stainless steel, 250 mL volume, 2×40 mm or 3×30 mm balls);
  • 3 stainless steel square-mesh sieves: (a) no. 710, (b) no. 425, (c) no. 300;
  • Permanent magnet, metal foil, hot-air oven (140 ± 5 °C), balance (±0.005 g), desiccator, ultrasonic bath.

Method — Crushing & Sieving

Rinse containers with water R, dry. Wrap ≥3 glass articles in clean paper and crush to produce 2 samples of ~100 g. Crush pieces in mortar (30–40 g at a time, one heavy hammer strike) and sieve through set (a)–(c). Collect grains that pass through sieve (b) and are retained on sieve (c) — target particle size range 300–425 µm. Continue until ~10 g of glass on sieve (a) (reject this portion and the portion passing through (c)).

Remove iron particles with magnet. Clean grains: 3 × 30 mL acetone R washes + ultrasonic bath (1 min × 2), then dry at 140 °C for 20 min, cool in desiccator. Weigh 10.00 g of cleaned dried grains into 2 separate conical flasks.

Autoclaving & Titration

Add 50 mL of water R1 into each flask. Use the autoclaving procedure as in Test A, but maintain 121 ± 1 °C for only 30 ± 1 min (not 60 min). Add 0.05 mL methyl red solution R. Titrate blank with 0.02 M HCl, then titrate test solutions to match blank colour. Subtract blank from test titration. Express as mL of 0.02 M HCl per gram of sample.

1 mL of 0.02 M HCl is equivalent to 620 µg of sodium oxide.

Repeat the test if the highest and lowest observed values differ by more than 20 per cent.

Limits

  • Type I: not more than 0.1 mL of 0.02 M HCl per gram of glass
  • Type II and III: not more than 0.85 mL of 0.02 M HCl per gram of glass

Test C — To Determine Whether Containers Have Been Surface-Treated (Etching Test)

If there are uncertainties whether a container has been surface-treated, and/or to distinguish between type I and type II glass containers, test C is used in addition to test A. Alternatively, tests A and B may be used. Test C may be carried out either on unused samples or on samples previously used in test A.

Vials and Bottles

Rinse the containers twice with water R, fill to the brimful point with a mixture of 1 volume of hydrofluoric acid R and 9 volumes of hydrochloric acid R and allow to stand for 10 min. Empty the containers and rinse carefully 5 times with water R. Immediately before the test, rinse once again with water R. Submit the containers thus prepared to the same autoclaving and determination procedure as described in Test A.

If the results are considerably higher than those obtained from the original surfaces (by about a factor of 5 to 10), the samples have been surface-treated.

Ampoules, Cartridges and Syringes

NOTE: ampoules, cartridges and syringes made from glass tubing are not normally subjected to internal surface treatment because their high chemical resistance is dependent upon the chemical composition of the glass as a material. Apply the test method as described above. If the ampoules are not surface-treated, the new values are slightly lower than those obtained in previous tests.

Test C的設計原理

Type II玻璃的高耐水解性來自「內表面處理層」（通常是硫磺化處理，形成薄薄的SiO₂富集層）。Test C先用HF/HCl混酸（1:9比例）蝕刻10分鐘，破壞這個表面處理層，然後重新做Test A。如果蝕刻後Test A結果大幅升高（5–10倍）→ 說明原本的高耐水解性依賴表面處理 → Type II。如果蝕刻後Test A結果變化不大 → 說明耐水解性來自玻璃本身組成 → Type I。

Arsenic Test

The test applies to glass containers for aqueous parenteral preparations.

Method: Hydride generation atomic absorption spectrometry (2.2.23, Method I).

Test solution: Use the extraction solution obtained from containers of types I and II, after autoclaving at 121 °C for 1 h as described under Test A. Transfer 10.0 mL to a 100 mL volumetric flask. Add 10 mL of hydrochloric acid R and 5 mL of a 200 g/L solution of potassium iodide R. Heat on a water-bath at 80 °C for 20 min, allow to cool and dilute to 100.0 mL with water R.

Reference solutions: arsenic standard solution (1 ppm As) R. Apply same acid/KI treatment. Concentration range: typically 0.005–0.015 ppm of As.

Wavelength: 193.7 nm. Atomisation device: air-acetylene flame. Source: hollow-cathode lamp.

Limit: maximum 0.1 ppm of As.

砷（Arsenic）試驗

為何需要砷試驗？

鈉鈣矽酸鹽玻璃在高溫熔製過程中，可能引入微量砷作為澄清劑（fining agent）——砷氧化物（As₂O₃）在高溫下可加速玻璃氣泡的消除。雖然現代製造已大量減少此用途，但仍有潛在風險。注射劑直接進入血液循環，砷的毒性（類金屬，致癌物）需要嚴格控制。

砷試驗參數彙整

適用對象：含水注射用玻璃容器（Type I 和 II）
萃取液來源：Test A已高壓滅菌萃取液（121°C × 1 h）
分析方法：氫化物生成原子吸收光譜法（HG-AAS）

參考液範圍：0.005–0.015 ppm As

氫化物生成AAS原理

標準AAS對砷的靈敏度有限。「氫化物生成法（Hydride Generation）」先用KI將As(V)還原為As(III)，再以硼氫化鈉（NaBH₄）反應生成砷化氫氣體（AsH₃），導入AAS石英管中原子化偵測。此法可將偵測靈敏度提升1000倍以上，達到ppb（µg/L）級別，因此能有效偵測微量砷釋出。

萃取液複用的設計

砷試驗的試驗液直接來自Test A的萃取液——同一次高壓滅菌萃取，既可做鹼性離子滴定（耐水解性），又可做砷分析（取10.0 mL），有效節省時間和容器用量，也確保兩個試驗的萃取條件一致性。

Spectral Transmission for Coloured Glass Containers

Equipment: A UV-Vis spectrophotometer equipped with a photodiode detector or photomultiplier tube coupled with an integrating sphere.

Preparation of Specimen

Break the glass container or cut it with a circular saw fitted with a wet abrasive wheel. Select sections representative of the wall thickness. If the specimen is too small, mask the uncovered portion with opaque paper or tape. Wash, dry and wipe with a lens tissue. Mount with wax, avoiding fingerprints.

Method

Place the specimen with its cylindrical axis parallel to the slit and the light beam perpendicular to the surface (minimise reflection losses). Measure the transmission of the specimen with reference to air in the spectral region of 290–450 nm, continuously or at intervals of 20 nm.

Annex — Test for Surface Hydrolytic Resistance by Flame Spectrometry

The surface hydrolytic resistance of glass of types I and II may be determined by analysis of the leaching solution by flame spectrometry. A number of elements that, when present as oxides in glass, contribute to the alkalinity of the solution, are determined and used to express an alkali equivalent.

The spectrometric method has the advantage of allowing the use of a much smaller sample of extract so that it can be applied to small individual containers. This enables an evaluation of the uniformity of the containers in a given batch where this is critical.

The results of this measurement are not equivalent to those of titrimetry and the 2 methods cannot be considered interchangeable. The titrimetric method is the reference method of the Pharmacopoeia; the spectrometric method may be used in justified and authorised cases.

Elements determined: sodium oxide (Na₂O), potassium oxide (K₂O), calcium oxide (CaO). Spectrochemical buffer: caesium chloride solution (80 g/L CsCl in 0.01 M HCl). Concentrations reported as alkali equivalent in µg/mL.

Cs緩衝液（Spectrochemical buffer）的作用

火焰光譜法中，鉀（K）和鈣（Ca）的測量容易受其他元素（如Na）的「電離干擾（ionization interference）」影響。加入大量Cs⁺（鉮離子）可飽和火焰中的電離空間，使K和Ca保持原子態（不被電離）而能被準確量測。這是火焰AAS/AES中「釋放劑（releasing agent）」的標準作法。
