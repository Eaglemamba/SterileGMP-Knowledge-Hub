PART I: CONTINUOUS MANUFACTURING OF DRUG SUBSTANCES AND DRUG PRODUCTS

1. INTRODUCTION

1.1. Objective

This guideline describes scientific and regulatory considerations for the development, implementation, operation, and lifecycle management of continuous manufacturing (CM). Building on existing ICH Quality guidelines, this guideline provides clarification on CM concepts and describes scientific approaches and regulatory considerations specific to CM of drug substances and drug products.

1.2. Scope

This guideline applies to CM of drug substances and drug products for chemical entities and therapeutic proteins. It is applicable to CM for new products (e.g., new drugs, generic drugs, biosimilars) and the conversion of batch manufacturing to CM for existing products. The principles described in this guideline may also apply to other biological/biotechnological entities.

CM involves the continuous feeding of input materials into, the transformation of in-process materials within, and the concomitant removal of output materials from a manufacturing process. While this description may apply to an individual unit operation (e.g., process chromatography, tableting, perfusion cell culture), this guideline focuses on the integrated aspects of a CM system in which two or more unit operations are directly connected. In this context, any changes made in a unit operation of a CM system may have impact on downstream and upstream unit operations (e.g., back pressure resulting in forward mixing) and output material quality.

Fundamental aspects of CM that are generally not specific to technology, dosage form, or molecule type are described within the main body of this guideline. Annexes are provided to augment the main guideline by providing illustrative examples and considerations specific to certain modalities (e.g., chemical entities, therapeutic proteins), technologies, and production methods (e.g., integration of drug substance and drug product manufacturing). The examples and approaches described in these annexes are illustrative, and alternative approaches can be used. Topics that are broadly applicable to both CM and batch manufacturing are not in the scope of this guideline, and other existing ICH guidelines should be used as appropriate.

核心概念：Q13 在 ICH 架構中的定位

Q13（2022）是 ICH Q 系列的最新成員，專門針對連續製造（CM）的科學與法規框架。其在 ICH 架構中的關係：

ICH 指南與 Q13 的關係
Q7（API GMP）Q13 採用 Q7 的批次定義；DS 的 CM 在 Q7 基礎上擴展
Q8（製劑開發）設計空間、PAT、RTRT 的科學基礎——CM 大量應用這些概念
Q9（風險管理）CM 控制策略開發的風險評估方法論
Q10（PQS）CM 的 Pharmaceutical Quality System 框架；「受控狀態（State of Control）」概念
Q11（DS 開發）DS 的 CM 在 Q11 的 DS 製程開發基礎上擴展
Q13上述指南在 CM 整合系統中的具體應用；提供 CM 專屬的法規申報框架

重點提示｜Q13 聚焦「整合系統」而非單一單元操作

Q13 的範疇是「兩個或以上單元操作直接連接的 CM 系統」——這個看似技術性的定義有深刻的法規意涵：

  • 單一連續單元操作（如灌流細胞培養、連續層析）不是 Q13 的主要焦點
  • 真正的 CM 是「整合系統」——各單元操作的上下游之間存在直接的材料流動和相互影響
  • 這種整合性帶來獨特的挑戰：一個單元操作的擾動（disturbance）可能通過物料流影響整個系統的輸出品質

因此，Q13 的控制策略必須從「系統整合」的視角設計，而不是把各單元操作的控制策略簡單加總。

比喻說明｜CM vs. 批次製造，就像「流水線」vs.「訂製工坊」

傳統批次製造就像「訂製工坊」——每個「批次」是一個獨立的產品，做完一批再做下一批，每次都是全新的開始。連續製造就像「汽車流水線」——原材料不斷投入，產品不斷產出，整個系統是一個持續運作的整體。在流水線上，若某個工位（單元操作）出了問題，不只影響那個工位的產品，還會影響整條流水線的狀態——上游的材料積壓、下游的供料中斷。這種「牽一髮動全身」的特性，正是 Q13 需要特別規範的原因。

2. CM CONCEPTS

2.1. Different Modes of CM

CM can be applied to some or all unit operations in a manufacturing process. Examples of CM modes include:

  • A manufacturing approach in which some unit operations operate in a batch mode while others are integrated and operate in a continuous mode
  • A manufacturing approach in which all unit operations of a drug substance or drug product manufacturing process are integrated and operate in a continuous mode
  • A manufacturing approach in which drug substance and drug product unit operations are integrated across the boundary between drug substance and drug product to form a single CM process (i.e., the drug substance is continuously formed and processed into the drug product through integrated unit operations)

A manufacturing approach may incorporate surge lines or tanks to maintain a constant flow of material inputs and outputs in any mode of CM described above.

2.2. Batch definition

The ICH Q7 definition of a batch is applicable to all modes of CM, for both drug substances and drug products. Based on this definition, the size of a batch produced by CM can be defined in terms of one of the following:

  • Quantity of output material
  • Quantity of input material
  • Run time at a defined mass flow rate

Other approaches to define batch size are possible, if scientifically justified based on the characteristics of the CM process and Good Manufacturing Practice.

A batch size can also be defined as a range. For example, a batch size range can be established by defining a minimum and maximum run time.

CM 的三種模式詳解

模式描述典型應用
混合模式（部分連續） 部分單元操作批次，部分連續，之間有緩衝儲存 固體口服劑：連續混合 + 批次壓片；或連續發酵 + 批次純化
全程連續 所有 DS 或 DP 的單元操作均整合為連續模式 全連續固體劑型線（進料→混合→壓片→包衣一氣呵成）
跨 DS/DP 整合 原料藥合成與製劑成型整合為單一連續製程 化學合成 API 直接連續製備成片劑，無獨立的 DS 隔離步驟

Surge tanks/lines（緩衝槽/管線）：在各模式中，緩衝槽用於平衡上下游質量流速差異，防止因短暫流量不匹配導致系統中斷。這是 CM 系統設計的重要元素，直接影響系統的整合穩定性。

CM 批次大小定義的三種方式及其含義

方式 1：輸出物料量（Output Quantity）
  例：每次運行生產 500 kg 的製劑
  優點：直觀，與傳統批次概念最接近
  挑戰：CM 系統輸出量可能變動（運行時間、良率波動）

方式 2：輸入物料量（Input Quantity）
  例：每次運行投入 10 kg API + 90 kg 賦形劑
  優點：成本控制明確
  挑戰：輸入量 ≠ 輸出量（損耗、物料分流）

方式 3：定質量流速下的運行時間（Run Time）
  例：在 1 kg/min 的質量流速下運行 8 小時 = 一個批次
  優點：最直接反映 CM 的「時間-空間」特性
  常見：多以此方式定義最小/最大批次大小（Run Time Range）

批次大小定義為範圍：
  最小運行時間（min run time）→ 最小批次
  最大運行時間（max run time）→ 最大批次
  → 靈活應對市場需求波動，無需每次提交新的批次大小變更申請

重點提示｜批次大小「範圍」的法規優勢

將批次大小定義為範圍（如「最小 4 小時，最大 12 小時的運行時間」）是 CM 的重要法規優勢：

  • 傳統批次製造若需要改變批量（如放量生產），通常需要提交 Prior Approval 或 Notification
  • CM 系統若事先在申請中定義了批次大小範圍，在範圍內調整不構成 EC 變更
  • 這允許製造商根據市場需求彈性調整每次運行的時長，同時維持同一套已驗證的製程和控制策略

這是 CM 相對於批次製造的重大監管效率優勢之一。

比喻說明｜CM 批次定義就像「排班制服裝廠」

一條 24 小時運轉的服裝流水線，可以按不同方式定義「一個生產批次」：

  • 按輸出量：「生產完 1000 件」就是一批——但每批耗時可能不同
  • 按輸入量：「投入 50 匹布」就是一批——但可能裁剪出多少件不確定
  • 按運行時間（最常見）：「一個班次（8小時）」就是一批——最清楚、可追蹤、GMP 記錄最完整
  • 時間範圍：「4-12 小時都算一批，視訂單量調整」——最靈活，允許生產計畫彈性

實務應用｜CDMO 導入 CM 的策略起點

對 CDMO 而言，從批次製造轉向 CM 的策略應考慮：

  • 選擇混合模式作為入門點：先在高週轉、相對簡單的單元操作（如混合、制粒）導入連續模式，積累 CM 操作經驗
  • 批次大小定義策略：建議採用「運行時間範圍」定義，在 MAA 申請中預先設定最小/最大運行時間，以最大化未來的生產靈活性
  • 投資 PAT 基礎設施：CM 系統有效運作的前提是即時監控，PAT 工具的投資是 CM 轉型的必要前提，也是申請文件的重要支撐元素

3. SCIENTIFIC APPROACHES

3.1. Control Strategy

The development of a control strategy for CM is enabled by a holistic approach, considering aspects specific to CM (discussed below), principles outlined in ICH Q7, Q8, Q10 and Q11, and quality risk management described in ICH Q9.

3.1.1. State of Control

A state of control (ICH Q10) is a condition that provides assurance of continued process performance and product quality. The condition may vary, depending on the mode of CM and the specific process steps. For example, a state of control can be demonstrated for CM processes when a set of parameters (e.g., process parameters, quality attributes) are changing within specified ranges, but the processes are not necessarily in a steady state. Elements of the control strategy monitor the state of control and, when necessary, take appropriate actions to maintain control of the process. It is important to have mechanisms in place to evaluate the consistency of unit operations and the system, and to identify when parameters drift or trend within the specified range.

3.1.2. Process Dynamics

Knowledge of process dynamics is important to maintain a state of control in CM. Understanding the impact of transient events helps to identify risks to material quality and to develop an appropriate control strategy. Transient events that occur during CM operation may be planned (e.g., process start-up, shutdown and pause) or unplanned (e.g., disturbances) and can be described through time, process parameter and quality attribute values.

Process dynamics should be characterised to understand how output material quality is impacted by transient events. This characterisation could be done by determining properties such as residence time distribution (RTD). RTD characterises the time available for material transport and transformation, and is specific to the process, composition/formulation, material properties, equipment design and configuration, etc.

Process dynamics should be characterised over the planned operating ranges and anticipated input material variability using scientifically justified approaches. Appropriate methodologies (e.g., RTD studies, in silico modeling with experimental confirmation) should be used. Step testing by making changes to the quantitative composition of the process stream is another useful technique to determine the RTD.

3.1.3. Material Characterisation and Control

Material attributes can impact various aspects of CM operation and performance, such as material feeding, process dynamics, and output material quality. Input materials may require evaluation and control of attributes beyond those typically considered for a material specification used in batch manufacturing. For example:

  • In a solid dosage form process, particle size, cohesiveness, adhesiveness, hygroscopicity, static charge, or specific surface area may impact powder feeding and material flow.
  • In a chemically synthesised drug substance process, viscosity, concentration, or the multiphase nature of the feed may impact flow properties or conversion.
  • In a therapeutic protein process, lot-to-lot variability of cell culture media or feed components may impact cell culture performance or process consistency.

3.1.4. Equipment Design and System Integration

The design of equipment and their integration to form a CM system affect process dynamics, material transport and transformation, output material quality, etc. When developing a CM process and its control strategy, it is important to consider the characteristics of the integrated systems. These include the system's ability to maintain an integrated flow, manage potential disruption to CM operations, and complete the intended transformation of the material stream within the respective planned operational ranges.

  • Design and configuration of equipment (e.g., compatibility and integrity for maximum run time; geometry to promote desired transformation; spatial arrangement to facilitate material flow)
  • Physical connections and digital control interfaces between equipment (e.g., use of a surge tank between two unit operations to mitigate temporary differences in mass flow rates)
  • Locations of material diversion and sampling points

3.1.5. Process Monitoring and Control

Process monitoring and control support the maintenance of a state of control during production. Process analytical technology (PAT) (ICH Q8) is well-suited for CM. Example applications include in-line UV flow cells to monitor therapeutic protein concentration, in-line near-infrared spectroscopy to assess blend uniformity or water content, and on-line HPLC to monitor conversion of a chemical reaction.

An appropriate sampling strategy is an important aspect of process monitoring and control. The variables monitored, monitoring method and frequency, amount of material sampled, sampling location, statistical method, and acceptance criteria depend on the intended use of the data and process dynamics. The intended use of data may include detection of rapid changes such as disturbances, assessment of quality of a batch when RTRT is used, or analysis of process trends or drifts.

3.1.6. Material Traceability and Diversion

CM processes may include periods when non-conforming materials are produced, for example, during system start-up and shutdown or when disturbances are not appropriately managed. The ability to detect and divert potential non-conforming material from the product stream during production is an important characteristic of CM.

Understanding the RTD and process dynamics of individual unit operations and integrated systems over planned operating conditions enables tracking of the distribution of materials over time. Material traceability, understanding how disturbances affect material quality, and the use of appropriate measurements (e.g., PAT) allow for real-time determination of when to start and stop material collection or diversion. Criteria should be established to trigger the start and end of the diversion period and restart of product collection.

3.1.7. Process Models

Process models can be used for development of a CM process or as part of a control strategy for commercial production, including the diversion strategy. Process models may also be used to predict quality attributes in real time, enabling timely process adjustments to maintain a state of control. During development, process models can support the establishment of a design space by explaining how inputs and outputs are related.

  • A process model is specific to system design and configuration and relevant material properties.
  • Model development requires understanding of underlying model assumptions (e.g., plug flow versus mixed flow systems).
  • Model performance depends on factors such as mathematical constructs and the quality of model inputs.
  • Model validation assesses the fitness of the model for its intended use based on predetermined acceptance criteria.
  • During commercial manufacture, model maintenance and monitoring of model performance should occur on a routine and ongoing basis.

核心概念：CM 的受控狀態 ≠ 穩態

這是 CM 與批次製造最根本的概念差異之一。在批次製造中，「穩態（Steady State）」通常是品質保證的基礎——製程要保持穩定。但 CM 的「受控狀態（State of Control）」允許參數在指定範圍內動態變化，只要整體過程和輸出品質持續受控。

舉例：在連續混合-壓片線上，饋料器的流量可能隨著物料粉末流動特性的輕微批間差異而波動，但只要關鍵 CQA（如含量均一度）持續符合規格，系統就處於「受控狀態」——即使它不在「穩態」。

這個「動態但受控」的概念，是 PAT 和即時放行測試（RTRT）在 CM 中特別重要的原因：需要即時測量才能確認系統始終保持受控狀態。

RTD（停留時間分佈）——CM 的核心科學概念

RTD（Residence Time Distribution，停留時間分佈）是描述物料在 CM 系統中停留時間分佈的函數。它是 CM 科學的核心工具：

RTD 的用途說明
材料追蹤若某時刻投入了一個擾動，RTD 告訴你這個擾動在何時、以何種濃度出現在下游
分流策略設計根據 RTD，確定何時開始和停止分流，以隔離受影響的物料
採樣策略採樣頻率必須與 RTD 匹配——採樣間隔不能大於 RTD 的時間尺度
批次邊界定義在整合式 DS/DP CM 中，RTD 幫助定義一個批次的「起點」和「終點」
製程模型輸入RTD 是製程模型（如 feedforward control）的關鍵輸入參數

RTD 表徵方法：示蹤劑研究（tracer studies，需選擇與物料流動特性相似但惰性的示蹤劑）、步驟測試（step testing）、in silico 模擬配合實驗確認。

重點提示｜CM 物料規格可能需要超出藥典標準

§3.1.3 的一個重要提示：CM 製程中，輸入物料可能需要控制「超出傳統批次製造規格」的屬性。例如：

  • 固體製劑連續製造中，粒徑分佈、吸濕性、靜電荷等影響粉體流動性的屬性，在批次製程中通常不列入規格，但在 CM 中可能是 EC
  • 若藥典有標準，可能需要在藥典標準之外增加 CM 專屬的控制屬性

對 CDMO 而言，這意味著在 CM 製程轉換時，必須重新評估所有輸入物料規格，確認是否需要增加 CM 專屬的物料屬性控制要求。若原有供應商的物料不符合 CM 的新要求，可能需要更換供應商或要求供應商調整製程。

  → 觸發調查 + 預防性分流啟動

Step 2: 分流範圍確定
  → 基於 RTD：擾動訊號在系統中傳播的時間範圍
  → 加入安全裕度（safety margin）考慮 RTD 不確定性
  → 若多個 PAT 測量點都顯示正常 → 可縮小分流範圍

Step 3: 分流結束與重新收集
  → PAT 數據恢復正常且穩定
  → 達到預設的「重新收集」標準
  → 記錄分流起始/結束時間和分流量

Step 4: 分流物料處置
  → 分流物料的命運：隔離 → 離線測試 → 調查 → 棄置/回收
  → PQS 中維護分流物料的處置程序

比喻說明｜RTD 就像「河流中的汙染物擴散模型」

假設在一條河流的上游某處倒入了一桶染料（擾動），RTD 告訴你：這些染料在何時開始出現在下游的水質監測站？何時濃度最高？何時稀釋到可忽略的程度？掌握 RTD，就可以精確計算「從上游監測到染料投入到下游水質開始受影響」之間的時間窗口——這正是 CM 中分流策略的科學基礎。不知道 RTD 的系統，就像不知道河流流速和擴散模式的工程師，無法設計有效的汙染管控措施。

原文內容 Original Text

3.2. Changes in Production Output

Several approaches to changing production output are discussed below. For already approved products manufactured using a CM process, it is important to justify the selected approach, assess risk and understand its impact on the overall control strategy and process performance, and as needed, update the control strategy to ensure risk control.

  • Change in run time with no change to mass flow rates and equipment: Issues not observed over shorter run times may become visible as run time increases. Additional risks and constraints should be considered, including process drift, increased equipment temperatures, material build-up, exceeding the performance limit of components (e.g., validated in vitro cell age, resin cycle number), material degradation, membrane or sensor fouling, and microbial contamination. Decreasing production output generally would not imply additional risks.
  • Increase mass flow rates with no change to overall run time and equipment: The risks associated with this approach may impact output material quality and are related to changes in process dynamics and system capability to handle increased mass flow rates. This approach may require re-evaluation and modification of the control strategy, including process parameters and controls, material traceability, RTD, sampling, and diversion strategies.
  • Increase output through duplication of equipment (scale-out):
    • Replication of production lines (like-for-like): Replicating the integrated CM production line can increase production output. The replicate production lines follow the same control strategy.
    • Parallel unit operations on the same production line: Risks include maintaining control across parallel unit operations, maintenance of uniform flow distribution, synchronisation and re-integration of parallel flow streams, and material traceability.
  • Scale-up by increasing equipment size/capacity: General principles of equipment scale-up apply. As elements such as RTD, process dynamics, and system integration may change, various aspects of the control strategy may be impacted. The risks of scale-up and applicability of the original control strategy should be assessed at the new scale.

3.3. Continuous Process Verification

In CM, frequent process monitoring and control can be achieved through use of process parameters, PAT tools, soft sensors, and process models. These tools allow real-time data collection for parameters relevant to process dynamics and material quality, and hence ensure the state of control for every batch. Additionally, since CM can facilitate changes to production output without increasing equipment size, there is an opportunity to generate development knowledge at the same scale intended for commercial manufacturing. These tools, together with the system design and the control strategy, facilitate early execution of process validation activities and the adoption of continuous process verification (CPV) (ICH Q8) as an alternative approach to process validation.

比喻說明｜CPV 就像「持續健康監測的智慧型設備」vs.「定期體檢」

傳統製程確效就像「每年做一次完整體檢」——在規定的時間點集中評估健康狀況。連續製程確效就像「配戴智慧型健康手錶 24 小時監測」——心率、血壓、睡眠品質持續記錄，任何異常立即提醒。CM 製程天然搭配 PAT 和即時監控，就像是一個「自帶健康監測功能的製造系統」，使得 CPV 成為邏輯上最自然的確效方式——不需要特別安排「確效批次」，因為每一批生產都在持續確效。

實務應用｜CDMO 選擇 CPV vs. 傳統 PV 的策略考量

對 CDMO 而言，CPV 的導入需要具備以下前提：

  • 充分的 PAT 基礎設施：在線/近線 NIR、UV 等測量系統已安裝並驗證
  • 統計製程控制（SPC）能力：能夠即時分析 CM 數據並識別製程漂移
  • 製程模型支撐：用於即時品質評估的軟感測器或分析模型已建立並驗證
  • 監管機構早期溝通：建議在申請前與 FDA/EMA 進行 Type B/C 諮詢，確認 CPV 方案的可接受性

若上述條件尚未成熟，建議先採用傳統 PV 路徑，同時建設 CPV 基礎設施，在獲批後的生命週期管理中逐步轉換。

第4節 法規考量：概述

核心原則

ICH Q13 的法規考量章節核心：連續製造的申請資料檔案（dossier）仍須遵循 ICH M4Q（CTD 的品質部分格式要求），但需額外補充 CM 特定的資訊。

§4.1 Description of Manufacturing Process and Process Controls p17

In line with ICH M4Q, a sequential narrative description of the manufacturing process and process controls should be included in sections 3.2.S.2.2 and 3.2.P.3.3 of the CTD and supported by pharmaceutical development data in 3.2.S.2.6 or 3.2.P.2.

For CM, sections 3.2.S.2.2 and 3.2.P.3.3 should be supplemented by the following, when applicable:

  • A summary of start-up, shutdown, pause and restart procedures, to describe how the integrated CM process functions
  • The strategy for material collection and diversion including criteria for product collection during routine manufacturing
  • Process parameters related to continuous flow (e.g., mass flow rate(s) or flow rate range)
  • When appropriate (e.g., solid materials), a description of how material is transported from one piece of equipment to another
  • A flow diagram with the following aspects identified, when applicable:
    • Points of material entry and exit (including diversion and collection points)
    • Sequence of unit operations, including any surge lines or tanks
    • Direction of material movement through each process step
    • Clear indication of continuous and batch process steps
    • Critical steps and location points at which process controls, intermediate tests, or final product controls are conducted (e.g., PAT measurement, feedforward or feedback process control)
  • A suitably detailed description of aspects of equipment design, configuration and system integration shown to be critical to process control or to impact product quality

§4.2 Control Strategy p17–19

The control strategy of a CM process is designed to ensure that output materials made over time are of the desired quality. The control strategy should consider the elements discussed in Section 3. The dossier should describe the relevant controls and operational aspects used during manufacturing.

Input Material Attributes

Impact of input material attributes and their variability (e.g., inter-batch, different suppliers) on continuous processing and product quality should be assessed. Proposed material attribute acceptable ranges should be justified when establishing the material specification. For input materials for which pharmacopoeial requirements exist, characterisation and control may extend beyond those requirements.

Process Monitoring and Control

An appropriate description should show a robust approach to process monitoring and maintaining a state of control. The dossier should describe how the control system uses process parameters and in-process material attribute measurements to make process- and quality-related decisions (e.g., to pause the process or divert material).

Other important aspects should be defined: sampling strategy (location, sample size, frequency, statistical approach and criteria), summary of models if used, and use of data in making in-process control decisions.

The data analysis method used should appropriately detect disturbances or variability. When data averaging is used, averaging across appropriate time intervals should be considered rather than across the entire CM run time. The time intervals should consider the relevancy of PAT measurement frequency to the RTD and process dynamics.

System Operation

Procedures should be established and maintained on site for managing system start-up, shutdown, and pauses and for handling disturbances (see Annex V). Relevant approaches should also be described at adequate level of detail in the dossier. The disposition of material impacted by transient and pause events should be justified, considering potential risks to output material quality.

Material Diversion and Collection

The material diversion and collection strategy should be summarised and justified in the dossier. The strategy should include:

  • Criteria for triggering material diversion
  • Basis for determining the extent of diverted materials
  • Conditions for resuming material collection

Factors such as sampling frequency, RTD, and amplitude/duration/propagation of disturbances should be considered. The extent of diverted material should incorporate justified safety margins, considering uncertainty of RTD and other measurements.

Procedures for managing material collection, diversion, and disposition (quarantine, offline testing, investigations) do not need to be in the dossier but should be maintained within the PQS (ICH Q10).

RTRT

RTRT as described in ICH Q8 can be applied to output material quality attributes. RTRT is not a regulatory requirement for CM implementation. When RTRT is proposed, the associated reference test method should be described.

Development of the data collection approach for RTRT should include a risk assessment of how any lapses in data collection (e.g., recalibrating a NIR probe) may affect quality decisions. The proposed control strategy should include alternative or additional quality controls to mitigate any risks posed by data lapses.

Equipment and System Integration

Aspects of equipment design and system integration that are shown to be critical to output material quality and its control should be described and justified in the context of the overall control strategy.

A summary of the control strategy should be provided in CTD section 3.2.S.2.6 or 3.2.P.2.3 with links or references to CTD sections that contain the detailed information.

資料平均化的關鍵警示

Q13 明確指出：不應對整個 CM 運行時間的資料進行平均。應依「時間區間」平均，且該區間必須考量 PAT 量測頻率與 RTD 的相關性。

原因：CM 過程中干擾事件（disturbance）可能只影響特定時段的物料，若對全程平均，將稀釋局部異常信號，掩蓋品質問題。

物料轉向策略設計要素

設計安全的轉向策略需考量：

  • RTD 的不確定性：轉向範圍需包含 RTD 預測的上下限加上安全邊際
  • 干擾傳播：干擾信號可能沿系統下游傳播，需追蹤振幅與持續時間
  • 取樣頻率：決定「最早何時可確認品質恢復正常」
比喻：轉向策略如同交通管制

想像高速公路上發生事故（干擾），不只關閉事故當下的車道，還需管制前後一段距離（RTD範圍＋安全邊際）的交通流量。「恢復通車」需等確認事故完全排除後，不能只看即時路況。

RTRT的重要澄清

RTRT 不是 CM 的法規要求，而是可選工具。但若申請採用 RTRT，必須：

  • 說明參考測試方法（reference test method）
  • 評估資料收集中斷（如重新校正 NIR 探頭）對品質決策的影響
  • 提出替代或額外的品質控制措施作為備援
CTD 控制策略摘要位置

完整的控制策略必須在以下章節提供摘要，並交叉引用詳細資訊：

  • 3.2.S.2.6（藥物物質製造開發）
  • 3.2.P.2.3（藥品開發，製造開發部分）

摘要需提供「連結或引用」其他 CTD 章節中的詳細資訊，以便審查員追蹤驗證。

§4.3 Batch Description and Batch Size p19–20

The approach to define batch size (see Section 2.2 examples) and the proposed commercial batch size or range should be described in the dossier.

If a range is proposed, it should be justified, and the approach for achieving the range should be described (see Section 2.2). Changes in batch size within the approved batch size range can be managed within the PQS. Any post-approval change beyond the approved range should be supported by data (Section 3.2) and appropriately managed (prior approval or notification).

A suitable quantitative metric with acceptance criteria should be defined within the PQS to establish batch-to-batch consistency and system robustness. For example, when batch size is defined by amount of collected material, the amount of diverted materials relative to collected materials for each batch should be considered.

The actual intended size of a given batch should be defined before manufacturing begins.

§4.5 Drug Substance and Drug Product Stability p20–21

Regulatory expectations for the stability data package generally do not differ between CM and batch manufacturing modes (refer to ICH Q1A, ICH Q5C). Batches used to generate primary stability data should be manufactured using a manufacturing process and equipment representative of the commercial process. Primary stability batches should incorporate the variability described in the ICH stability guidelines (e.g., different drug substance batches).

Additionally, for chemical drug substances or drug products:

  • Stability batches could be obtained with a single start-up/shutdown sequence provided the aforementioned variability is incorporated into the batches (e.g., by introducing different batches of drug substances sequentially).
  • Stability batches may be produced from shorter manufacturing runs, provided that:
    • The control strategy, mass flow rate(s), and equipment are representative of the commercial process
    • It is demonstrated that a state of control is established and maintained when the process operates over the longer commercial run times

    The concept of pilot scale batch (e.g., at minimum one-tenth of full production scale) as defined in other guidelines (e.g., ICH Q1A) may not be necessarily applicable in this scenario.

  • If modes of increasing production output (other than increasing run time) are being used (e.g., increasing equipment size), applicants should justify their approach for defining primary stability batches. Applicants are encouraged to discuss their scale-up and primary stability batches approach with regulatory authorities.

思考問題

為何 Q13 允許用「較短製造時間的批次」做穩定性試驗，但要求「必須另行證明較長商業運行時間下仍維持受控狀態」？兩者之間是否矛盾？

不矛盾。穩定性試驗評估的是「產品本身的化學/物理/生物穩定性」，這可由較短批次反映；而「較長運行時間下的受控狀態」評估的是「製造穩健性」，需透過製程驗證或 CPV 數據另行證明。兩者評估目的不同，分開要求符合科學邏輯。

§4.6 Conversion of a Batch Process to CM p27

Changing the manufacturing mode from batch to continuous necessitates the development of an appropriate control strategy, considering factors identified in Section 3. The output materials from the batch and continuous processes should have comparable quality.

A science and risk-based approach should be used for establishing product comparability and assessing the need for additional bioequivalence, non-clinical and/or clinical studies, and stability data. Additional details regarding how to establish product comparability for therapeutic proteins can be found in ICH Q5E.

Manufacturers should seek regulatory approval before implementing a conversion of an approved batch process to a CM process. Manufacturers can seek advice from the regulatory authority to gain clarification on the regulatory expectations and acceptability of their strategy and data package for the proposed changes (e.g., when potential changes in formulation are necessary to enable conversion to CM).

§4.7 Process Validation p27–28

The requirements for process validation as established by region are similar for CM and batch manufacturing. Approaches such as traditional process validation (fixed number of batches) or continuous process verification can be used.

When continuous process verification is used, the CM system performance and output material quality is continuously monitored, such that real-time data collected demonstrate the maintenance of a state of control and production of output material with the desired quality for the run time duration.

The use of continuous process verification should be justified based on:

  • Product and process understanding
  • System design
  • Overall control strategy

This justification should be provided in the dossier (see Table 1).

When a continuous process verification approach is used to support initial product launch, applicants should justify the appropriateness of the validation activities to provide confidence in the commercial manufacturing process.

§4.8 Pharmaceutical Quality System p28

PQS expectations are the same for batch and CM processes and should follow pertinent ICH guidelines. One important operational aspect of CM is that non-conforming materials can be diverted from the rest of the batch when material traceability, process monitoring, and material diversion strategies are well established.

Procedures for material diversion, when required, should be established under the PQS (see Section 4.2). An overarching plan or decision tree that describes how disturbances are managed for various types of material diversion should be maintained under the PQS.

Diverted materials resulting from planned events (e.g., system start-up and shutdown) generally do not require investigation when the events meet established process performance criteria.

When unexpected and/or frequent disturbances occur, appropriate investigation, root cause analysis, and CAPA should be instituted (see Annex V).

Annex III: CM of Drug Substances (Therapeutic Proteins) p34–36

Annex III provides additional regulatory and scientific considerations for CM of therapeutic protein drug substances and recombinant proteins used as intermediates for conjugation.

Unique Considerations

  • Adventitious agent control (§2.1): Extended cell culture duration requires design measures to demonstrate acceptability of all cell culture material. Rapid testing may enable real-time decision making during contamination events.

Bioreactors for CM may operate significantly longer than batch. Limit of in vitro cell age established for batch mode may not apply to CM under different culture conditions. The limit should be based on data from pilot or commercial scale expanded under proposed conditions (per ICH Q5A, Q5B, Q5D).

ICH Q5A recommendations remain applicable. Where not applicable to a CM system, scientifically justified alternatives may be proposed.

Annex IV: Integrated Drug Substance and Drug Product CM p37–42

Annex IV addresses CM processes where drug substance and drug product manufacturing are fully integrated — drug substance is not isolated between the two steps.

Drug Substance Specification (§3.1)

Even though the drug substance is not isolated, a drug substance specification should be defined and justified (per ICH Q6A). Drug substance testing may not be required on a routine basis when the integrated process is appropriately controlled. Conformance to specification should be verified on a periodic and event-driven basis:

  • Periodic: Pre-selected batches or at predetermined intervals (e.g., every 5th batch or annually)
  • Event-based: Triggered by changes in supplier, starting material, synthesis conditions, or other risk factors

Attributes typically associated with drug substance quality are generally included in the drug product specification (e.g., related substances, residual solvents, elemental impurities) unless justified per ICH Q6A.

  • Drug substance stability: Re-test period not applicable (not isolated/stored). Data may support in-house reference standards, stability profiles, and hold times during production interruptions.

附錄四：藥物物質與藥品整合型連續製造

整合型 CM 的定義特徵

附錄四討論「藥物物質 → 藥品全程連續整合」：藥物物質不進行分離、乾燥、儲存，直接以中間體形式（如晶漿）流入藥品製造步驟。

範例流程：化學反應 → 連續結晶 → 交叉流過濾 → 濕式造粒 → 乾燥 → 研磨 → 壓片 → 包衣

「未分離藥物物質」的規格管理

雖然藥物物質不分離，仍必須建立藥物物質規格。日常生產測試頻率可降低：

  • 常規生產：製程符合預設績效標準時，視為「若測試則符合規格」，不必每批離線測試
  • 定期驗證：每 N 批或每年，實際取樣測試確認規格符合性
  • 事件驅動：供應商更換、原料改變等高風險事件觸發測試
藥品規格的特殊要求

整合型製程的藥品規格通常需包含傳統上屬於「藥物物質規格」的測試項目（有關物質、殘留溶劑、元素雜質），因為藥物物質從未被單獨測試放行，其品質控制只能透過最終藥品測試確認。

思考問題

整合型 CM 製程中，若例行監控發現取樣點 T1 的雜質濃度有升高趨勢（仍在規格內），應採取哪些行動？

解答

趨勢雖在規格內，仍應：(1) 啟動趨勢調查（OOT 程序），評估是否有系統性根本原因；(2) 評估是否達到「事件驅動」定期驗證的觸發條件；(3) 趨勢持續時，考慮收緊內部警戒限度或實施 CAPA；(4) 評估是否影響後續藥品規格中的有關物質測試結果。整合型製程的「連鎖性」使藥物物質趨勢需與藥品品質指標一起評估。

Annex V: Perspectives on Managing Disturbances p43–46

This annex describes examples of approaches for managing transient disturbances during CM. The discussion is not exhaustive; alternative approaches can be used.

Background — Funnel Plot Tool

Disturbances may result in variation in material quality. The impact is affected by the amplitude, duration, and frequency of the disturbance. Funnel plot predictions based on an RTD model help understand the qualitative and quantitative impact of a disturbance's amplitude and duration on material quality.

Example: Figure 5 shows that a disturbance of ±20% lasting less than 90 seconds would not cause drug concentration in the blend to exceed 90–110% LC.

Three Disturbance Scenarios

  • Example 1 (within criteria, infrequent): +20% LIWF flow spike, 40 s — within predefined acceptance criteria. Blend concentration stays within 90–110% due to back mixing. Action: No diversion, no investigation needed.
  • Example 2 (outside criteria, unexpected): +20% spike, 300 s — exceeds predefined criteria. NIR confirms blend exceeds 110%. Action: Non-conforming material diverted per pre-established procedure; process continues; concurrent investigation initiated.
  • Example 3 (within criteria but frequent): Multiple +20% spikes, 40 s each — each individually meets criteria, but system cannot dampen cumulative effect, resulting in non-conforming material. Action: Monitor closely; divert when control criteria no longer met; pause if disturbances persist; investigation required to determine root cause.

Diverted amount depends on RTD confidence intervals (safety margin), sampling strategy, and ability to trace and remove materials.

Setting acceptance criteria for frequency of disturbances is also recommended to aid management.

附錄五：干擾管理的實務案例

漏斗圖（Funnel Plot）的設計功能

漏斗圖以「干擾振幅」為 y 軸、「干擾持續時間」為 x 軸，繪製出對應物料品質（如含量均勻性 90-110% LC）的「可接受區域」邊界。

實用意義：幫助確定哪些干擾需要轉向物料、哪些不需要，從而設定合理的振幅/持續時間驗收標準。是設計「按振幅/持續時間觸發轉向」控制策略的核心工具。

三種干擾情境的決策比較
情境干擾特徵品質影響處置調查？
Example 1 +20% / 40秒，偶發，符合標準 含量在 90-110% 內（back mixing 吸收） 不轉向，繼續收集 否
Example 2 +20% / 300秒，非預期，超出標準 含量超過 110%（NIR 確認） 轉向，製程繼續 是（可並行）
Example 3 +20% / 40秒 × 多次，頻繁 累積後系統無法吸收 → 不符合規格 監控→轉向→持續則暫停 是（必須調查）
Example 3 的重要啟示

「每次符合標準的干擾，多次累積後可能超標」——CM 控制策略不能只設定單次干擾的驗收標準，還需要設定干擾頻率的驗收標準。Q13 明確建議：「設定干擾頻率的驗收標準，可輔助干擾管理」。

綜合練習問題

為何 Example 2 的「轉向」可以與「調查」並行，但 Example 3 需要考慮「暫停製造」？

解答

Example 2：干擾已結束，製程已恢復正常。非符合物料的範圍確定（依 RTD 安全邊際），轉向執行後製程可繼續正常運行，調查不需要中斷生產。

Example 3：干擾仍在持續且頻繁發生，說明問題根本原因尚未解決。若在調查期間繼續運行，可能持續產生非符合物料，且累積干擾可能超出系統的吸收能力。「暫停」確保在問題解決前不生產可能需廢棄的物料，符合風險管理原則。

§5 Glossary — Official Definitions p37–39

Active Process Controls
A system consisting of hardware and software architecture, mechanisms, and algorithms that automatically adjust a process to maintain the process output within a desired range. Examples include feedforward and feedback process controls.
Disturbances
Unplanned changes to process inputs beyond normal operating range or conditions (e.g., process parameter, material property, equipment condition, or environment) that are introduced into a system.
Diversion
Procedure in which materials are isolated and separated from the product stream in the manufacturing process.
Material Traceability
The ability to track materials throughout the manufacturing process.
Model Maintenance
A set of planned activities over the product lifecycle to monitor and sustain the model's performance to continually ensure its suitability for the intended and approved purpose.
Multivariate Statistical Process Control
The application of multivariate statistical techniques to analyse complex process data with potentially correlated variables. (Ph. Eur.)
Process Dynamics
The response of a manufacturing process to changing inputs or conditions or transient events.
Residence Time Distribution (RTD)
A measure of the range of residence times experienced by material passing through a specific process environment/vessel/unit operation.
Run Time
The time interval used to produce a quantity of output material.
Soft Sensors
A model that is used in lieu of physical measurement to estimate a variable or attribute (e.g., a quality attribute of material) based on measured data (e.g., process data). The model development, including selection of such data variables, is driven by comprehensive product and process understanding.
Steady State
A stable condition that does not change over time.
System
A manufacturing architecture that, in the context of CM, consists of individual pieces of equipment, their connections to one another, monitoring and control systems, and spatial layout.
Transient Events
A temporary condition in which a process goes through a dynamic change. This change may be due to a disturbance or an intentional alteration in the selected operating conditions (e.g., start-up, shutdown, changes from one operating condition to another).
Unit Operation
A basic step in a process. Unit operations involve a physical, chemical or biological transformation such as: reaction, crystallisation, filtration, blending, granulation, tableting, cell culture, purification or virus inactivation.
